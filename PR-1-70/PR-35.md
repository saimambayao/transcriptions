Proposed Resolution No. 35

***

**A RESOLUTION DIRECTING THE MINISTRY OF INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT (MILG) TO DEVELOP A POLICY ON THE PREVENTION OF VIOLENT EXTREMISIM BASED ON THE REINTEGRATION PROGRAM CONVEYED TO THE PROVINCIAL LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNIT OF LANAO DEL SUR BY THE UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM (UNDP)**

**WHEREAS,** with the intention of strengthening the national and local resilience to risks of violent extremism, the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) in partnership with the Provincial Local Government Unit of Lanao del Sur and relevant stakeholders organized the *Kalilintad Roadmap Discussions* which involved a series of public discussions and consultations with various sectors such as security enforcers, Ulama, education, traditional leaders, non-government organizations, and the twelve (12) local government units affected by the Marawi Siege;

**WHEREAS,** the *Kalilintad Roadmap Discussions* identified as a solution and caused the creation of the Peace and Management Office (PMO) which implemented a reintegration program from October 2019 to March 2021 to former violent extremists;

**WHEREAS,** the functions of the PMO were carried out by the *Balay Mindanao Foundation Inc. (BMFI)* which is a non-government organization funded by the UNDP;

**WHEREAS,** PMO provided different services to municipalities with high number of returnees including *psycho-social awareness program, distributions of starter kits, outreach programs for the communities, leadership and advocacy trainings for the youth in the communities and Madrasah & Masjids renovations;*

**WHEREAS,** after the completion of the contract between the UNDP and PMO-Balay Mindanaw, the PMO was conveyed to the Provincial Local Government Unit of Lanao del Sur and was renamed as Peace Development Office (PDO) which will continue the reintegration program and other programs for peace and order in the province of Lanao del Sur;

**WHEREAS,** all the documents and supplies including the office have been conveyed to PDO as a guide in the campaign and continuation of the reintegration program and other peace and order programs;

**WHEREAS,** the reintegration program included the establishment of an information system that will enable planning, monitoring and evaluation of programs on socio-economic inclusion, empowerment and social cohesion in the province of Lanao del Sur and contribute towards the development of local government approaches on recovery and rehabilitation;

**WHEREAS,** the data gathered using the information system established by the UNDP can serve as a guide or material in developing a comprehensive plan or region-wide approach in addressing violent extremism;

**WHEREAS,** to strengthen this reintegration program, the Bangsamoro Government is encouraged to develop a policy aligned with the 1987 Constitution, especially the Constitutionally-protected right of the people to freedom of speech and religion that would sustain this program;

**NOW THEREFORE,** be it resolved as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, **TO DIRECT THE MINISTRY OF INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT (MILG) TO DEVELOP A POLICY ON PREVENTION OF VIOLENT EXTREMISIM BASED ON THE REINTEGRATION PROGRAM CONVEYED TO THE PROVINCIAL LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNIT OF LANAO DEL SUR BY THE UNITED NATIONS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM (UNDP).**
