**A RESOLUTION**
**DIRECTING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH (MOH) TO FAST-TRACK THE IMPLEMENTATION OF COMMUNITY-INTENDED PROJECTS IN ORDER TO LESSEN THE BURDEN OF THE BANGSAMORO CONSTITUENTS DURING THE COVID-19 PANDEMIC**

**WHEREAS,** Presidential Proclamation No. 922, s. 2020, was issued, declaring a state of public health emergency throughout the Philippines due to the Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19);

**WHEREAS,** on 11 September 2020, the President signed into law Republic Act No. 11494, known as the “*Bayanihan to Recover as One Act*”¹;

**WHEREAS,** under the Section 3 of Republic Act No. 11494, it is the policy of the State to promote a just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the nation and free the people from poverty, particularly in the aftermath of natural and man-made disasters, through policies that provide adequate social services, and promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality of life for all;

**WHEREAS,** the continuous increase of confirmed cases of COVID-19 in the region poses a serious threat to the health, safety, security, and lives of the Bangsamoro people. The disruption to livelihood and all other productive activities were seen in the region’s economic reduction during the pandemic;

**WHEREAS,** a number of community-intended projects such as medical outreach program, vaccination program, maternal and child health care program, among others, are programmed for implementation;

**WHEREAS,** the community projects aim to enhance quality of life of the vulnerable sectors in the Bangsamoro region. It safeguards the needs of the community to be considered and met;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT HEREBY RESOLVED** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution directing the Ministry of Health to fast-track the implementation of community-intended projects in order to lessen the burden of the Bangsamoro constituents during the COVID-19 pandemic.

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this resolution be furnished to the Ministry of Health.
