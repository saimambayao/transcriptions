### Critical Findings

1. **FAIL -- VERBATIM (Q14):** The LROD quote from BAA 13, Sec. 17(3)(d) is truncated. The omitted portion -- "and provide written Shari'ah opinions to the Chief Minister and the Cabinet" -- is a materially distinct function (advisory vs. policy review). Must be completed.

2. **Art. X, Sec. 1 quote in BOL Provisions table (not a Q&A but the reference table):** The document presents Art. X, Sec. 1 as a single combined quote: *"The Bangsamoro justice system shall be administered in accordance with the unique cultural and historical heritage of the Bangsamoro. The dispensation of justice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be in consonance with the Constitution, Shari'ah, traditional or tribal laws, and other relevant laws."* However, the actual BOL text (02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:384-394) contains additional paragraphs between these sentences covering: (a) Shari'ah as part of Islamic tradition, (b) Shari'ah applying exclusively to Muslims, (c) tribal laws for IP disputes, (d) non-Muslim/non-IP rights protection. The document's quote splices non-adjacent text into a single block quote. **Severity: IMPROVE** -- the individual sentences are verbatim but their concatenation is misleading.

3. **Art. X, Sec. 2 quote in BOL Provisions table:** Similarly combines text from Sec. 2 with language about Congress creating courts. The actual BOL text also mentions creating Shari'ah courts "outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in areas where a considerable number of Muslims reside" which is omitted. **Severity: IMPROVE** -- not material to BARMM scope but should be noted.

### Missing Questions

1. **What is the role of the Darul Ifta' in the justice system?** -- BAA 13 Sec. 52 creates the Darul Ifta' which has a justice-adjacent function (Islamic legal opinions). This is mentioned in File 2 but not explored in File 1 despite its direct relevance to Shari'ah justice administration.

2. **How does BAA 89 (Transitional Justice) interact with the regular justice system?** -- BAA 89 is listed in the reference table but no Q&A explores its mechanism, the BTJRC's relationship with courts, or how transitional justice proceedings relate to regular/Shari'ah court jurisdiction.

3. **What is the relationship between Katarungang Pambarangay and traditional/tribal justice?** -- Both are barangay-level justice mechanisms but serve different communities. A Q&A distinguishing their jurisdictions and integration potential would be valuable.
