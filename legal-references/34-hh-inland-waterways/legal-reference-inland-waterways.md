# (hh) Inland Waterways for Navigation

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(hh)** — "Inland waterways for navigation;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(hh) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes — Art. XIII, Sec. 22 (last para.): "The Bangsamoro Government shall coordinate and cooperate with the Philippine Coast Guard concerning inland waterways for navigation." |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Transportation and Communications (MTC) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE); Philippine Coast Guard (national coordination); Maritime Industry Authority (MARINA) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 11 — Infrastructure Development |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Inland Waterways Navigation Act enacted; inland waterway navigation functions exercised through MTC under BAA 13 | Enact a Bangsamoro Inland Waterways Act establishing navigation standards, vessel registration, route designation, and safety protocols |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 22 requires coordination and cooperation with the Philippine Coast Guard; no formal coordination protocol established | Establish a MOA between MTC and PCG defining roles, safety standards, and enforcement jurisdiction for inland waterway navigation |
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No inland waterway transport development plan despite Res. 251 urging a feasibility study | Commission and complete the feasibility study on inland waterway transport; incorporate results into a Bangsamoro Inland Waterways Development Plan |
