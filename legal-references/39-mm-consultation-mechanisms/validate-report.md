## 4. 5% GAD Budget Claim

### Claim: "minimum 5% of each MOA's budget for gender-responsive programs, plus 5%-30% of ODA" (line 47)

**SOURCED** -- This is a direct BOL provision (Art. XIII, Sec. 5, para. 2), not an author inference. Verified verbatim. The document correctly attributes this to the BOL.

### Claim: BWC monitors "5% GAD budget requirement" (line 596)

**AUTHOR'S OBSERVATION** -- The document states BWC monitors compliance with the 5% GAD budget. BAA 13 Sec. 7(2)(a) authorizes BWC to "Monitor, assess and ensure compliance" and to "require all ministries, agencies and offices of the Bangsamoro Government to submit their GAD plan and budget." The inference that this includes the 5% requirement is reasonable, though BAA 13 does not explicitly name the "5% GAD budget" -- it references compliance with "issued guidelines and implementation of their Gender and Development (GAD) plans." Minor gap but reasonable inference.

## 7. Additional Findings (Outside Focus Areas)

### Art. IX, Sec. 3 -- FPIC cross-reference (line 77)

> "Their right to free, prior and informed consent (FPIC) is also protected under Art. XIII, Sec. 12."

**MISLEADING** -- Art. XIII, Sec. 12 of the BOL is titled "Rights of Indigenous Peoples to Natural Resources" and the FPIC it references is specifically about "development initiatives and the exploration, development, and utilization of the natural resources within ancestral domains covered by Certificate of Ancestral Domain Title." This is a narrower FPIC than what line 77 implies (a general FPIC right for all governance decisions). The broader FPIC reference would be Art. XIII, Sec. 10 (protected areas within ancestral domains). The document should clarify that Art. XIII, Sec. 12 FPIC is natural-resource-specific.
