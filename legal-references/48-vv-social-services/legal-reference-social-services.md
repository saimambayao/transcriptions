# (vv) Social Services, Social Welfare, and Charities

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(vv)** — "Social services, social welfare, and charities;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(vv) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No — BOL does not require national law consistency for social services; Bangsamoro Government has authority to design and deliver social services within its territorial jurisdiction |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Bangsamoro Nutrition Commission (BAA 83); Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC); Bangsamoro Youth Commission (BYC); Special Development Fund (BAA 85, Ch. XXXII) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 9 — Social Services (Education, Health) |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## BDP 2023-2028 Alignment

| BDP Chapter | Relevant Goals/Strategies | Connection to Power |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Chapter 9 — Social Services | Strengthen social protection systems; expand social safety nets; support vulnerable populations (PWDs, senior citizens, orphans, widows) | Social services power is the primary authority for delivering BDP Chapter 9 social protection targets |
| Chapter 10 — Peace and Security | Normalization; reintegration support for former combatants and conflict-affected populations | BAA 57 directly implements social welfare for mujahideen as part of normalization |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No comprehensive social services code | No Bangsamoro Social Welfare Code has been enacted. MSSD exercises the mandate through BAA 13 (Administrative Code); BAA 57 and BAA 83 are specific interventions, not comprehensive frameworks | Enact a Bangsamoro Social Welfare Code defining social protection standards, charity regulation, welfare program eligibility, and institutional framework for MSSD |
| **Type A** — No PWD legislation | No standalone Bangsamoro legislation for persons with disabilities despite Art. IX, Sec. 23 mandate to create "a special agency, support facilities and livelihood or skills training for persons with special needs." MSSD exercises through BAA 13 | Enact a Bangsamoro Persons with Disabilities Act creating the special agency mandated by Art. IX, Sec. 23 |
| **Type A** — No senior citizen legislation | No standalone Bangsamoro legislation for senior citizens despite Art. IX, Sec. 23 mandate to "ensure the welfare of senior citizens." MSSD exercises through BAA 13 | Enact a Bangsamoro Senior Citizens Welfare Act tailored to regional needs |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | BAA 57 covers senior/disabled mujahideen but broader social protection for all conflict-affected populations (IDPs, orphans, widows, poverty-stricken communities) mentioned in Art. XIV, Sec. 1 lacks comprehensive coverage | Expand social welfare legislation to cover all populations enumerated in Art. XIV, Sec. 1 |
