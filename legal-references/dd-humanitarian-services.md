# (dd) Humanitarian Services and Institutions

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(dd)** — "Humanitarian services and institutions;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(dd) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No — BOL does not require national law consistency for humanitarian services; however, Art. XIV establishes a normalization/rehabilitation framework jointly with the National Government |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Special Development Fund (BAA 85, Ch. XXXII); Office of the Chief Minister (normalization programs) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 9 — Social Services (Education, Health) |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## BDP 2023-2028 Alignment

| BDP Chapter | Relevant Goals/Strategies | Connection to Power |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Chapter 9 — Social Services | Strengthen social protection systems; provide services for conflict-affected populations, IDPs, widows, and orphans | BAA 57 (mujahideen assistance) and BAA 62 (IDP rights) directly implement BDP humanitarian targets |
| Chapter 10 — Peace and Security | Normalization process; reintegration of former combatants | Art. XIV rehabilitation mandate for MILF/MNLF members is central to normalization |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No comprehensive humanitarian code | No overarching Bangsamoro Humanitarian Services Act exists; BAA 57 covers mujahideen assistance and BAA 62 covers IDP rights, but no comprehensive framework for all humanitarian services and institutions. MSSD exercises the broader mandate through BAA 13 (Administrative Code) | Enact a Bangsamoro Humanitarian Services and Institutions Act establishing a unified framework for disaster relief, conflict-affected population services, and humanitarian institution standards |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | BAA 62 protects IDP rights but does not create a dedicated IDP assistance agency or define permanent resettlement procedures, especially for Marawi siege IDPs | Amend BAA 62 or enact supplementary legislation for permanent IDP resettlement programs |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | BAA 57 covers senior/disabled mujahideen but does not extend to families of deceased combatants or decommissioned women auxiliary forces mentioned in Art. XIV, Sec. 1 | Expand BAA 57 or enact separate legislation for families of martyred combatants and decommissioned women |
