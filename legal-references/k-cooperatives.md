# (k) Cooperatives and Social Entrepreneurship

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(k)** — "Cooperatives and social entrepreneurship"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(k) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No explicit national law consistency clause — BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 27 grants Parliament authority to "pass laws to strengthen the growth and development of social enterprises" without referencing national law; however, Art. XIII, Sec. 24 references a "Bangsamoro cooperative code" suggesting autonomous legislative authority |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Cooperatives and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Ministry of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT), Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 7 (Economic Development) |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## Legislative Gaps

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A — No BAA** | BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 24 explicitly mandates enactment of a "Bangsamoro cooperative code" — none has been enacted | Prioritize passage of Bill 210 (Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code) or equivalent |
| **Type A — No BAA** | No dedicated social enterprise legislation despite Art. XIII, Sec. 27 mandate to "pass laws to strengthen the growth and development of social enterprises" | Include social enterprise provisions in the cooperative code or enact standalone legislation |
| **Type B — Partial** | CSEA exists institutionally under BAA 85 budget allocation but lacks an enabling organic act defining its powers, functions, and regulatory authority | Enact CSEA charter legislation or include CSEA mandate in the cooperative code |
