### Critical Findings

1. **FAIL -- VERBATIM (Q10):** The citation "Art. XII, Sec. 19" for DBM's organization/staffing authority is incorrect. Art. XII covers Fiscal Autonomy and does not contain a Sec. 19 provision about organization/staffing standards. The correct provision is **Art. XVI, Sec. 10** (05-articles-XVI-to-XVIII.md:114): "The creation of the different offices and their respective staffing complement shall be consistent with existing budgeting, organization, staffing, position classification and compensation policies, guidelines, and standards of the National Government." This must be corrected.

2. **Missing limitation (Q4):** Art. VII, Sec. 39 establishes that national civil service laws prevail in case of conflict. This is a direct constraint on administrative organization that is not listed among the three limitations.

3. **Missing mandatory mandate (Q3):** The civil service law mandate (Art. VII, Sec. 39) is a second mandatory legislative requirement directly relevant to administrative organization. While the Administrative Code is the priority code, the civil service law is also mandated.

### Missing Questions

1. **How does the Bangsamoro civil service law interact with CSC authority?** -- Art. VII, Sec. 39 creates a dual-authority structure (Bangsamoro civil service law + CSC) that deserves analysis. What happens when a Bangsamoro personnel action is challenged at CSC? What is the scope of "disciplinary authority over its own officials and employees"?

2. **What is the role of SAMLMG (BAA 85, Ch. XXXIII)?** -- Listed as a supporting agency in the Power Overview but never explained in any Q&A. What does it do and how does it support administrative organization?

3. **How does BAA 49 (Local Governance Code) divide authority between MILG and DILG?** -- The overlapping jurisdiction Q&A mentions this briefly but does not explain the division. Since LGU supervision is a core administrative organization function, this deserves a dedicated question.

4. **What is the status of the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code?** -- Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a) authorizes enactment of a civil service code. Has it been enacted? If not, what gap does this create?
