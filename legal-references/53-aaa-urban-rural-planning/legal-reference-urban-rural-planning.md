# (aaa) Urban and Rural Planning Development

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(aaa)** — "Urban and rural planning development;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(aaa) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No — BOL does not require national law consistency for urban and rural planning; the Bangsamoro Government has authority to plan development within its territorial jurisdiction |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA); Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board (BAA 31); Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (Art. XIII, Sec. 4) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 3 — Spatial Development Strategy; Chapter 9 — Social Services |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## BDP 2023-2028 Alignment

| BDP Chapter | Relevant Goals/Strategies | Connection to Power |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Chapter 3 — Spatial Development Strategy | Spatial framework for balanced regional development; urban growth management; rural development corridors; settlement hierarchy | Urban and rural planning power is the primary authority for implementing BDP Chapter 3's spatial strategy |
| Chapter 11 — Infrastructure Development | Infrastructure master plan aligned with spatial development strategy | Planning determines infrastructure location and prioritization |
| Chapter 4 — Development Framework | Equitable development across subregions; balanced urban-rural growth | Planning power enables implementation of development framework across the territory |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No comprehensive planning code | No Bangsamoro Urban and Rural Planning Act has been enacted. MHSD and BPDA exercise planning functions through BAA 13 (Administrative Code). BAA 31 creates the Sustainable Development Board but does not establish a comprehensive planning framework | Enact a Bangsamoro Urban and Rural Planning Act defining spatial planning standards, zoning authority, comprehensive land use planning procedures, and the role of MHSD and BPDA in regional planning |
| **Type A** — No zoning legislation | No Bangsamoro zoning ordinance framework exists; LGUs rely on national RA 7160 provisions for zoning | Enact Bangsamoro zoning legislation that aligns with the BDP spatial development strategy |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | BAA 31 creates the Sustainable Development Board but its scope covers environment/sustainability rather than comprehensive urban-rural planning | Expand BAA 31 or enact complementary legislation to integrate sustainable development with spatial planning authority |
