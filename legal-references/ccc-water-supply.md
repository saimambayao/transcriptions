# (ccc) Water Supply and Services, Flood Control, and Irrigation Systems

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(ccc)** — "Water supply and services, flood control, and irrigation systems."

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(ccc) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes — Art. XIII, Sec. 37 ("The National Government shall fund and implement the construction and maintenance of ... water supply and services, and flood control and irrigation systems"; for systems connecting outside BARMM, "there shall be cooperation and coordination between the Bangsamoro Government and the appropriate national or local government bodies") |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Public Works (MOPW) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | IMPACT Fund (BAA 85, Ch. XXXVI); Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) for irrigation; Intergovernmental Infrastructure Development Board (Art. VI, Sec. 6) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 11 — Infrastructure Development |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Water Supply Act, Flood Control Act, or Irrigation Systems Act has been enacted despite the enumerated power. MOPW exercises water supply and flood control functions through BAA 13 (Administrative Code); MAFAR coordinates irrigation through its BAA 13 mandate | Enact a Bangsamoro Water Supply, Flood Control, and Irrigation Systems Act defining water service standards, flood mitigation protocols, and irrigation development framework |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | Art. XIII, Sec. 37 provides that the National Government "shall fund and implement" water supply, flood control, and irrigation; this means nationally-funded projects are implemented by the National Government, creating a dual-authority situation for water infrastructure | Clarify the boundary between nationally-funded and Bangsamoro-funded water projects through the Intergovernmental Infrastructure Development Board; enact enabling legislation for Bangsamoro-funded water infrastructure |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | Cross-boundary water supply and flood control systems require "cooperation and coordination" per Art. XIII, Sec. 37, but no formal coordination mechanism has been legislated | Formalize intergovernmental coordination through the Intergovernmental Infrastructure Development Board or a dedicated water resources coordination agreement |
| **Type A** — No water district regulation | No Bangsamoro legislation governs the creation, operation, or regulation of water districts within BARMM; national PD 198 (Provincial Water Utilities Act) applies by default. MOPW exercises through BAA 13 | Enact Bangsamoro water district regulation or incorporate water utility provisions in a comprehensive water supply act |
