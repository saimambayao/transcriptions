# (bb) Health

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(bb)** — "Health;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(bb) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes — Art. IX, Sec. 22, last para. (regulation of food, drinks, drugs, and tobacco "in accordance with the policies, rules and regulations of the concerned national government agencies"); quarantine services require cooperation with National Government |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Health (MOH) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Bangsamoro Nutrition Commission (BAA 83); Quick Response Fund (BAA 85, Ch. XXXV) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 9 — Social Services (Education, Health) |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## BDP 2023-2028 Alignment

| BDP Chapter | Relevant Goals/Strategies | Connection to Power |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Chapter 9 — Social Services | Improve health service delivery; expand hospital network; reduce maternal and infant mortality; strengthen disease prevention | 17 health-related BAAs (hospital establishment/upgrades) directly implement BDP Chapter 9 health targets |
| Chapter 4 — Development Framework | Goal 2: Human Capital and Social Development | Health is a key pillar for human capital development |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No comprehensive health code | No standalone Bangsamoro Health Code has been enacted; the 17 BAAs are individual hospital establishment/upgrade laws. MOH exercises the health mandate through BAA 13 (Administrative Code) | Enact a comprehensive Bangsamoro Health Code covering service delivery standards, primary care, hospital system classification, health worker regulation, and health financing |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | Hospital BAAs address infrastructure but do not cover primary health care delivery, community health programs, or preventive health services at the barangay level | Enact legislation for community-based primary health care delivery and barangay health station standards |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | BAA 83 creates the Nutrition Commission but no comprehensive nutrition or food safety code exists | Enact Bangsamoro Nutrition and Food Safety Act |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | Art. IX, Sec. 22 requires food/drug regulation "in accordance with" national government agency rules; Res. 409 requested DOH to establish an FDA regional office in BARMM — mechanism for coordination remains informal | Formalize MOH-DOH-FDA coordination through an intergovernmental agreement or memorandum of understanding |
