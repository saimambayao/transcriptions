# (uu) Science and Technology, Research Councils and Scholarship Programs

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(uu)** — ***"Science and technology, research councils and scholarship programs;"***

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(uu) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No — the BOL does not require consistency with national law for this power. Art. IX, Sec. 21 is a self-contained grant with no "consistent with" or "in accordance with" qualifier. National law applies suppletorily where no BAA exists. |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | **Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST)** — BAA 13, Book VI, Title XII, Sec. 1-12; BAA 85, Chapter XV |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE — science education standards); Bangsamoro Science High School System (BAA 40 — under MOST supervision); Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA — STI planning coordination) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 8: Harnessing Technology and Innovations to Increase Socioeconomic Opportunities and Improve Government Services |
| **Named Legislative Mandate** | No express "Parliament shall enact" mandate for a standalone science and technology code. Art. IX, Sec. 21 states priorities and principles but does not direct Parliament to enact specific legislation by name. |

## Legal Briefer

A granular Q&A analysis. Answers stay as close as possible to the language of the law. All verbatim statutory text in ***bold italic***. Key terms in **bold**. Themes follow the legal hierarchy and analytical flow: BOL grant → constitutional floor → national law → implementation → Shari'ah dimension → divergence → gaps → development.

### A. BOL Grant and Scope

**Q. What power does the BOL grant the Bangsamoro Government in this area?**

A. Art. V, Sec. 2(uu) grants the Bangsamoro Government authority over ***"Science and technology, research councils and scholarship programs."***[^1] This is a tripartite grant covering: (1) **science and technology** — policy formulation, program implementation, R&D, technology transfer, and application; (2) **research councils** — creation and management of bodies that direct, fund, and coordinate research; and (3) **scholarship programs** — administration of grants, scholarships, and financial assistance for science and technology education. The scope is broad and self-contained — the BOL imposes no "consistent with national law" qualifier on this power.

**Q. What specific BOL provisions elaborate on this power beyond Art. V, Sec. 2?**

A. Art. IX, Sec. 21 is the primary elaborating provision. It establishes four directives: (1) the Bangsamoro Government ***"recognizes science and technology as essential to national and regional progress"***; (2) it ***"shall give priority to science, research, inventions, technology education, and their development"***; (3) it shall ***"ensure the full and effective participation of all sectors in the planning, programming, coordination, and implementation of scientific and technological researches"***; and (4) it shall ***"prioritize the acquisition, adoption, innovation, and application of science and technology for development."***[^2] Additionally, Art. V, Sec. 3 references the duty to ***"encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities"***[^3] — reinforcing the self-reliance mandate. Art. XVI, Sec. 8(m) lists ***"Science and Technology"*** among the fifteen primary ministries of the interim Bangsamoro Government.[^4]

**Q. Does the BOL mandate the Parliament to enact specific legislation for this power?**

A. No. Art. IX, Sec. 21 states priorities and principles but does not contain a "Parliament shall enact" directive for a specific S&T code or act. Compare this with Art. IX, Sec. 22 (health) which expressly mandates Parliament to ***"establish by law a general hospital system"*** — no comparable directive exists for science and technology. However, the broad language of Art. IX, Sec. 21 implicitly authorizes comprehensive legislation.

**Q. Does the BOL create any specific body, office, or institution for this power?**

A. Art. XVI, Sec. 8(m) designates the **Ministry of Science and Technology** as one of the fifteen ministries.[^4] The BOL does not name specific subsidiary bodies (no named research council, no named scholarship program). BAA 13 and BAA 40 subsequently created the detailed institutional framework.

**Q. What conditions, limitations, or qualifications does the BOL place on this power?**

A. The BOL places no direct national law consistency requirement on the S&T power. Two indirect constraints apply: (1) Art. IX, Sec. 16 requires the Bangsamoro education system to ***"conform to the minimum standards set by the National Government"***[^5] — this affects MOST-supervised science schools (BSHS must be ***"on par with the standards of the Philippine Science High School"*** per BAA 40, Sec. 4); (2) Art. XII, Sec. 28 requires ***"review and approval of the National Government"*** for international technical cooperation agreements[^6] — constraining MOST's ability to independently enter foreign research partnerships.

### B. Constitutional Considerations

**Q. What constitutional provisions apply to this subject area?**

A. The **universal constraint** under Art. X, Sec. 15 of the Constitution requires autonomous regions to operate ***"within the framework of this Constitution."***[^7] Subject-specific provisions include:

- **Art. II, Sec. 17** (State Policy): ***"The State shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture, and sports to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development."***[^8] This is a declaration of state policy — the highest-level constitutional endorsement of S&T as a national priority.
- **Art. XIV, Sec. 10** (Science and Technology): ***"Science and technology are essential for national development and progress. The State shall give priority to research and development, invention, innovation, and their utilization; and to science and technology education, training, and services. It shall support indigenous, appropriate, and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, and their application to the country's productive systems and national life."***[^9] This is the primary constitutional mandate — BOL Art. IX, Sec. 21 closely mirrors this language.
- **Art. XIV, Sec. 11**: ***"The Congress may provide for incentives, including tax deductions, to encourage private participation in programs of basic and applied scientific research. Scholarships, grants-in-aid, or other forms of incentives shall be provided to deserving science students, researchers, scientists, inventors, technologists, and specially gifted citizens."***[^10]
- **Art. XIV, Sec. 12**: ***"The State shall regulate the transfer and promote the adaptation of technology from all sources for the national benefit. It shall encourage the widest participation of private groups, local governments, and community-based organizations in the generation and utilization of science and technology."***[^11]
- **Art. XIV, Sec. 13**: ***"The State shall protect and secure the exclusive rights of scientists, inventors, artists, and other gifted citizens to their intellectual property and creations, particularly when beneficial to the people, for such period as may be provided by law."***[^12]

**Q. What rights and privileges does the Constitution guarantee in this area?**

A. Art. XIV, Sec. 11 guarantees that **scholarships, grants-in-aid, and incentives** shall be provided to deserving science students and researchers.[^10] Art. XIV, Sec. 13 guarantees **intellectual property rights** for scientists and inventors.[^12] Art. XIV, Sec. 5(2) guarantees **academic freedom** in institutions of higher learning[^13] — protecting research autonomy in BARMM universities and research institutions. Art. XIV, Sec. 5(3) guarantees every citizen's **right to select a profession or course of study**[^14] — BARMM cannot restrict access to science education based on non-academic criteria.

**Q. What constitutional mandates exist for legislation or institutions in this area?**

A. Art. XIV, Sec. 10 directs the State to ***"support indigenous, appropriate, and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities"***[^9] — a mandate that carries constitutional weight when applied to BARMM. Art. XIV, Sec. 11 states Congress ***"may provide for incentives"*** — permissive, not mandatory. Art. XIV, Sec. 12 mandates the State to ***"regulate the transfer and promote the adaptation of technology"***[^11] — BARMM inherits this obligation within its jurisdiction.

### C. National Law Considerations

**Q. What does the current national legal framework provide for this subject area?**

A. The national S&T framework is built on several pillars: (1) **institutional** — the Department of Science and Technology (DOST), created as a department by Executive Order 128 (1987), with sectoral councils (PCIEERD, PCAARRD, PCHRD) directing research in industry, agriculture, and health; (2) **human capital** — RA 7687 and RA 8248 (S&T Scholarship Act and amendments), RA 8439 and RA 11312 (Magna Carta for S&T Personnel), RA 11035 (Balik Scientist Act); (3) **innovation** — RA 11293 (Philippine Innovation Act), RA 11337 (Innovative Startup Act), RA 10055 (Technology Transfer Act); (4) **science education** — RA 8496 and RA 9036 (PSHS System), RA 6959 (Provincial S&T Centers); (5) **intellectual property** — RA 7459 (Inventors Incentives Act), RA 8293 (IP Code of the Philippines).

**Q. What national laws currently apply in BARMM?**

A. All national S&T laws apply **suppletorily** in BARMM — no BAA expressly supersedes any of them. Key laws: RA 8439/11312 (Magna Carta for S&T Personnel) applies **directly** to all government S&T employees including MOST staff; RA 7687/8248 (S&T Scholarship) provides a parallel national program accessible to BARMM students alongside the regional BASE program; RA 10055 (Technology Transfer Act) governs IP from government-funded R&D including MOST-funded research; RA 11293 (Innovation Act) and RA 11337 (Startup Act) provide the national innovation policy framework that supplements MOST's mandate.

**Q. What national institutions exercise authority in BARMM for this area?**

A. **DOST** — through its regional office (DOST Region XII services cover parts of BARMM) and national sectoral councils — continues to operate programs in BARMM including scholarship programs, SETUP (the national DOST SETUP predates MOST's regional counterpart), and research grants. **TAPI** (Technology Application and Promotion Institute, under DOST) provides inventor support services. **PSHS** maintains a campus in the Central Mindanao area accessible to Bangsamoro students. The devolution of DOST functions to BARMM through MOST is **ongoing but incomplete** — DOST retains national-level research coordination, international cooperation, and regulatory functions (standards, metrology, testing) that MOST is progressively building capacity to absorb.

### D. Implementation

#### D.1 How the Bangsamoro Government Implements This Power

**Q. Which ministry or office is primarily responsible for exercising this power?**

A. The **Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST)** — established by BAA 13, Book VI, Title XII, Sec. 1-12[^15] and funded under BAA 85, Chapter XV.[^16]

**Q. What standalone legislation (BAAs) has the Parliament enacted for this power?**

A. One standalone BAA: **BAA No. 40** (Bangsamoro Science High School System Act of 2023), PB No. 37.[^17] BAA 40 creates the BSHS System under MOST supervision, establishes governance structures (Board of Trustees, Executive Committee, Campus Directors), and appropriates PHP 55 million for initial implementation. No comprehensive **Bangsamoro Science and Technology Code** has been enacted.

**Q. What are the lead ministry's mandate and functions under BAA 13?**

A. BAA 13, Book VI, Title XII, Sec. 1 declares the policy of ***"morally guided science and technology programs"*** aimed at ***"a resilient, progressive and economically secure Bangsamoro."***[^18] Sec. 2 mandates MOST to ***"set the direction and leadership in science, research, inventions, technology education, and their development, and ensure the full and effective participation of all sectors."***[^19]

Sec. 3 enumerates ten (10) powers and functions:[^20]

(a) Formulate and adopt a comprehensive **Bangsamoro Science and Technology Plan** — goals, policies, plans, programs, projects — and monitor and coordinate its funding and implementation;
(b) Pursue **research and development** toward informed policy formulation, respond to pressing regional priorities, push emerging technologies to maturity, and build S&T literature through a research compendium;
(c) Support **technology transfer, promotion, innovation and commercialization** for MSMEs;
(d) Enhance S&T services in **information technology and disaster risk reduction and management**;
(e) Develop and maintain an **information system and databank** on science and technology;
(f) Improve access to quality education through **S&T scholarship programs**, capacity building for science and mathematics teachers, upgrading of science laboratories, and development of Bangsamoro science schools;
(g) Administer **scientific and technological scholarships, awards and grants**;
(h) Conduct **R&D on information technology and advanced fields** and undertake venture financing;
(i) Contribute to **quality standards including *halal*** for food and non-food industry through testing, analysis and capacity-building;
(j) Perform such other functions as may be provided by law.

**Organizational structure** (Sec. 4, 9):[^21] Ministry Proper (Minister, Deputy Minister, Director-General), with supporting units: Legal and Legislative Liaison Section; Internal Audit Section; Information and Communications Section; Planning Division; Administrative Division; Finance Division; **Technical Management and Operation Services** (two divisions: Science and Technology Services Division with Technology Transfer and Technology Application/Promotion Sections; Science Education, Scholarship, Grants and Awards Division with Scholarship/Grants and Science Education Sections); and **Research and Development Services** (three divisions: R&D and Innovation; Integrated Laboratory; Advanced Science and Technology).

**Provincial presence** (Sec. 10-12):[^22] Seven Provincial Science and Technology Centers — Maguindanao, Cotabato City, Lanao del Sur and Marawi City, Special Geographic Area, Basilan and Lamitan City, Sulu, and Tawi-Tawi. Each headed by a Senior S&T Specialist. Functions: identify local S&T needs, implement programs, assist in R&D execution, and develop institutional linkages with LGUs, academe, and NGOs.

**Q. What can the lead ministry NOT do without a standalone BAA beyond BAA 13?**

A. BAA 13 provides the institutional foundation — MOST exists, has a mandate, and can run programs. However, without a comprehensive **Bangsamoro Science and Technology Code**, MOST cannot: (1) establish a **regulatory framework** for S&T standards, testing, and certification with enforcement mechanisms and penalties; (2) create a **Bangsamoro Research Council** with dedicated funding, competitive research grants, and peer review processes; (3) codify **intellectual property incentives** specific to BARMM innovators beyond what national law provides; (4) establish a **Bangsamoro Science Park** or **Technology Business Incubator** with a dedicated legal charter; (5) create **venture capital or innovation fund** with statutory basis and fiduciary governance; (6) enact **mandatory S&T budgetary allocation** (e.g., a percentage of GAAB dedicated to R&D); (7) define **jurisdictional boundaries** with DOST for regulatory functions (metrology, standards, testing).

**Q. Have any parliamentary resolutions signaled intent to legislate in this area?**

A. Res. No. 482 commends MOST's scholarship programs and Res. No. 485 commends science achievement — both signal Parliament's endorsement of S&T development. However, neither resolution directs the filing of specific legislation. PB No. 388 (Basilan Science High School) shows ongoing legislative interest in expanding science education infrastructure.

#### D.1.A Institutional Validation

**Q. Is the assigned lead MOA the correct one for this power?**

A. Yes. MOST is the correct and sole lead ministry for power (uu). Its BAA 13 mandate covers the full scope of the enumerated power — science and technology policy, research and development, technology transfer, scholarship administration, and science education support. No other ministry exercises overlapping primary authority over S&T. MBHTE has jurisdiction over education policy generally (power (v)), but BAA 40 places science high schools under **MOST administrative control** — confirming MOST's primacy for science education within the BSHS System. The assignment is clean with no mismatch.

**Q. What BARMM institutions currently exist for this power?**

A. The following institutions are operational:

- **Lead ministry**: Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) — BAA 13, Book VI, Title XII; BAA 85, Chapter XV. Operational with central office and seven Provincial Centers.
- **BSHS System**: Created by BAA 40. Board of Trustees chaired by MOST Minister. At least one campus operational.
- **Provincial S&T Centers**: Seven centers (BAA 13, Sec. 10) — extensions of MOST in each province/city of BARMM.
- **Halal Board participation**: MOST provides technical staff to the Bangsamoro Halal Board Secretariat (BAA 13, Book VI, Title XIV, Sec. 36) for *halal* testing and standards.
- **BARMM READi Office**: MOST has a representative in the Bangsamoro READi Office (BAA 13, Sec. 28 of the DRRM title) for S&T services in disaster response.

**Q. What institutions does the BOL mandate that have NOT been created yet?**

A. The BOL does not mandate specific S&T institutions beyond MOST itself (Art. XVI, Sec. 8(m)). Art. IX, Sec. 21 speaks in general terms about priorities and principles — it does not direct the creation of a named research council, science foundation, or technology authority. All institutional development beyond MOST has come from BAA 13 and BAA 40, not from BOL mandates.

**Q. Does this power require creating NEW institutions that the BOL does not specifically name?**

A. Yes. Fully exercising this power requires institutions that do not currently exist:

- A **Bangsamoro Research Council** or **Research and Development Fund** — a body with a dedicated budget to issue competitive research grants, set R&D priorities, and coordinate inter-institutional research. BDP Chapter 8 identifies the target of raising R&D spending to 1% of GRDP (from 0.23%).
- A **Bangsamoro Science Park and Technology Business Incubator** — BDP Chapter 8 specifically programs PHP 200 million for this. Would require a legal charter defining governance, tenant qualifications, and fiscal incentives.
- A **Bangsamoro Innovation Fund** — dedicated financing mechanism for venture capital, startup grants, and technology commercialization to implement MOST's BAA 13 mandate on venture financing (Sec. 3(h)).

#### D.2 How the National Government Implements This Power

**Q. How does the national government currently exercise authority in this area within BARMM?**

A. DOST operates in BARMM through its Regional Office, providing: S&T scholarship programs (national DOST scholarships), SETUP grants for MSMEs, community-based technology programs (e.g., STARBOOKS digital libraries), and metrology/testing services through its regional standards laboratory. DOST's sectoral councils (PCIEERD, PCAARRD, PCHRD) fund research in BARMM universities — particularly Mindanao State University system campuses.

**Q. What national government programs and services operate in BARMM for this area?**

A. Key national programs: (1) **DOST-SEI Scholarships** — national S&T scholarships for undergraduate and graduate students; (2) **DOST-SETUP** — technology upgrading for MSMEs (the national program predates MOST's regional counterpart); (3) **DOST-STARBOOKS** — digital science library kiosks deployed in schools and barangay halls; (4) **PSHS-Central Mindanao Campus** — serves learners from BARMM-adjacent areas; (5) **DOST-ITDI** and **TAPI** — industrial technology development and inventor support accessible to BARMM inventors and enterprises.

**Q. What is the status of devolution for this power — has authority been transferred from national agencies to BARMM?**

A. Devolution is **partial and ongoing**. MOST was created as a new ministry (not a devolved DOST regional office), which means it built institutional capacity from scratch rather than inheriting DOST staff and assets. DOST continues to operate national programs in BARMM — the two systems run in parallel. No formal MOA or transition plan has been published that delineates the complete transfer of DOST functions to MOST within BARMM.

**Q. Are there areas where both national and Bangsamoro agencies operate concurrently?**

A. Yes — three key areas of overlap: (1) **Scholarships** — both DOST-SEI and MOST-BASE offer S&T scholarships to BARMM students; (2) **SETUP for MSMEs** — both DOST and MOST run technology upgrading programs; (3) **Standards and testing** — DOST's regional standards laboratories and MOST's integrated laboratory (BAA 13, Sec. 9(8)) perform overlapping metrology and testing functions. This overlap is by default (devolution incomplete), not by BOL design. A standalone BAA could clarify jurisdictional boundaries.

#### D.3 BARMM Implementation

**Q. What has the BARMM accomplished so far in exercising this power?**

A. MOST has achieved concrete results: (1) **BASE Scholarship** — 714 scholars across four batches, with the first batch (2020) graduating in AY 2023-2024, including 18 graduates with Latin honors from universities across BARMM (Res. No. 482); (2) **BASE MERIT Scholarship** — 208 additional scholars; (3) **Mujahideen/Mujahidat Assistance for Science Education** — 444 scholars (a unique BARMM program for children of former combatants); (4) **BSHS System** — BAA 40 enacted in 2023, operational campus(es) established; (5) **Seven Provincial S&T Centers** — extending MOST's reach across all BARMM provinces; (6) **Halal testing and standards** — MOST contributes to the Bangsamoro Halal Board's certification programs; (7) **Bangsamoro S&T Laboratory** — operating in Cotabato City providing testing, calibration, and metrology services to MSMEs.

**Q. Where is the BARMM stronger than the national government in this area?**

A. BARMM's distinctive advantages: (1) **Values-integrated science education** — BAA 40's BSHS System integrates Islamic values education with the Mufti of the *Darul Ifta'* on the Board, a model the national PSHS does not offer; (2) **Morally guided S&T policy** — BAA 13's declaration frames S&T as ***"morally guided"*** — reflecting Islamic principles absent from DOST's mandate; (3) **Conflict-sensitive scholarship design** — the Mujahideen/Mujahidat Assistance for Science Education program targets children of former combatants — a unique post-conflict reintegration tool that DOST does not replicate; (4) ***Halal* standards integration** — MOST's mandate for *halal* quality testing connects S&T to the Bangsamoro halal economy in ways DOST does not pursue.

**Q. What is holding BARMM back from full implementation?**

A. The **single biggest constraint** is the absence of a comprehensive Bangsamoro Science and Technology Code. Without it, MOST operates on BAA 13's general mandate — which provides institutional existence but not: a regulatory framework, a dedicated R&D fund, IP incentive structures, or science park charters. Secondary constraints include: limited laboratory infrastructure (one operational S&T laboratory for the entire region per BDP Chapter 8), weak research capacity (0.23% of GRDP on R&D vs. the 1% target), and the absence of a technology business incubator or science park. Legislative action — specifically a standalone S&T code — would remove the primary constraint and provide the legal basis to address the secondary ones.

#### D.4 National Government Implementation

**Q. What has the national government built for this area that BARMM does not yet have?**

A. The national system has: (1) **DOST** — a department-level agency with over 30 years of institutional history, specialized sectoral councils, and a nationwide network of regional offices; (2) **PSHS System** — 16 campuses nationwide with established curricula, faculty, and facilities (BARMM has one BSHS campus); (3) **National research infrastructure** — Philippine Space Agency, Nuclear Research Institute, Advanced Science and Technology Institute, MIRDC, ITDI — none of which have BARMM counterparts; (4) **Innovation ecosystem** — Philippine Innovation Act (RA 11293) created the National Innovation Council and Philippine Innovation Fund; Innovative Startup Act (RA 11337) established a startup ecosystem framework; (5) **IP and technology transfer systems** — TAPI, IPOPHL, Technology Transfer Act (RA 10055) — mature systems for converting R&D into commercial products; (6) **S&T personnel regime** — Magna Carta for Scientists (RA 8439/11312) provides career incentives and Scientific Career System with decades of implementation.

**Q. Where does the national system fall short for BARMM?**

A. The national S&T framework has significant gaps for BARMM: (1) **No *halal* S&T integration** — DOST does not have a dedicated *halal* testing and certification mandate; MOST's *halal* quality standards function is unique; (2) **Underrepresentation** — BARMM's R&D spending (0.23% of GRDP) is far below the national average, reflecting historical underinvestment in the region's S&T capacity; (3) **No post-conflict S&T programming** — DOST has no equivalent of the Mujahideen/Mujahidat scholarship or programs designed for conflict-affected communities; (4) **Language and cultural barriers** — DOST programs are delivered in Filipino/English without accommodation for Bangsamoro languages or Islamic institutional culture; (5) **Limited physical presence** — DOST's regional office coverage in BARMM is thin compared to Luzon and Visayas, with limited laboratory and testing infrastructure.

**Q. What should BARMM replicate, adapt, or replace from the national system?**

A. **Replicate**: The Scientific Career System from RA 8439/11312 — adopt the national career ladder for MOST personnel to attract and retain scientists. Replicate DOST's competitive research grant mechanism for a Bangsamoro R&D Fund.

**Adapt**: The PSHS model (RA 8496/9036) — BAA 40 already does this, requiring the BSHS to be "on par with" PSHS standards while integrating values education. Adapt RA 10055's technology transfer framework for BARMM-specific IP governance. Adapt RA 11337's startup ecosystem model to include *halal*-certified and Shari'ah-compliant enterprise tracks.

**Replace**: DOST's secular institutional framework — MOST's ***"morally guided"*** policy orientation and *halal* standards mandate represent a fundamentally different approach to S&T governance that reflects Bangsamoro identity and Islamic values. The BARMM approach does not merely replicate DOST with a different name — it integrates S&T into a value system the national framework does not accommodate.

### E. Shari'ah Considerations

**Q. Does this power have a Shari'ah dimension?**

A. Yes — though indirect, the Shari'ah dimension is meaningful. Science and technology is not governed by specific *fiqh* rules, but Islamic principles inform how the Bangsamoro Government exercises this power. The BOL's framing and BAA 13's declaration of policy explicitly integrate moral and ethical guidance into S&T governance.

**Q. What does the BOL say about Shari'ah in relation to this power?**

A. The BOL does not reference Shari'ah in Art. IX, Sec. 21 (the S&T provision). However, MOST's BAA 13 mandate includes ***"morally guided science and technology programs"*** (Sec. 1)[^18] and a specific function for ***"halal"*** quality standards (Sec. 3(i))[^20] — both reflecting Islamic values. BAA 40 integrates Values Education into the BSHS curriculum and seats the **Mufti of the Bangsamoro *Darul Ifta'*** on the Board of Trustees[^23] — institutional Shari'ah oversight of science education.

**Q. What Shari'ah principles are relevant to legislation in this area?**

A. Three principles are relevant: (1) ***Hifz al-'aql*** (preservation of the intellect) — the pursuit and dissemination of knowledge is a fundamental objective of Shari'ah; investment in science education and R&D serves this objective directly; (2) ***Maslahah*** (public interest) — government expenditure on S&T is justified when it addresses community needs (food security, health, disaster preparedness); (3) ***Amanah*** (trust/stewardship) — S&T resources entrusted to government must be managed transparently and accountably, with public benefit as the primary objective.

**Q. What existing Shari'ah legislation or jurisprudence applies?**

A. PD 1083 (Code of Muslim Personal Laws) does not address science and technology. No Shari'ah court has jurisdiction over S&T matters specifically. The relevant Shari'ah integration is institutional — through the *Darul Ifta'* representative on the BSHS Board and MOST's *halal* testing mandate — rather than judicial.

**Q. How should Shari'ah compliance be integrated into Bangsamoro legislation for this power?**

A. A **principled** approach is appropriate — legislation guided by Islamic values (moral guidance, *halal* standards, ethical research) without mandating specific Shari'ah compliance for all S&T activities. The BAA 40 model (Values Education integration, *Darul Ifta'* Board seat) should be replicated in any standalone S&T code — ensuring institutional Shari'ah review without constraining scientific inquiry. For *halal*-specific testing and certification, **mandatory compliance** is appropriate for products seeking *halal* certification. A Shari'ah Legislative Review Board (SLRB) review of S&T legislation would ensure alignment with Islamic ethical principles.

### F. Divergence Analysis

**Q. To what extent can the BARMM diverge from national law and policy in this area?**

A. BARMM has **broad latitude** to diverge from national S&T policy. As established in the **BOL Grant and Scope** section (Section A), no national law consistency requirement constrains power (uu). The only indirect constraint is Art. IX, Sec. 16's education standards requirement (affecting BSHS) and Art. XII, Sec. 28's approval requirement for international cooperation. Within these limits, BARMM can: (1) establish its own R&D priorities distinct from DOST's national agenda; (2) create S&T institutions with no national counterpart (e.g., a *halal* science laboratory, morally guided research councils); (3) design scholarship programs tailored to Bangsamoro needs (BASE, Mujahideen/Mujahidat scholarships); (4) set regional S&T standards for *halal* compliance that go beyond national requirements; (5) integrate Islamic values into science education and research governance. The **Constitutional Considerations** section (Section B) establishes the constitutional floor — Art. XIV, Sec. 10-13 mandates state support for S&T — which BARMM satisfies and extends. The **Shari'ah Considerations** section (Section E) provides additional basis for distinctiveness — *halal* integration, values-based education, and moral guidance of S&T programs represent legitimate divergence grounded in Bangsamoro identity.

**Q. If BARMM enacts different standards, what are the implications for rights holders and cross-boundary operations?**

A. For **S&T personnel**: MOST employees are covered by RA 8439/11312 (Magna Carta for Scientists) regardless — a BARMM S&T code could enhance but not diminish these rights. For **researchers**: IP generated from MOST-funded research would need to comply with both RA 10055 (national) and any BARMM technology transfer provisions — mutual recognition is needed. For **students**: BSHS graduates must be recognized nationally — BAA 40 addresses this by requiring parity with PSHS standards. For ***halal* testing**: MOST's *halal* certification would need mutual recognition with national *halal* certification bodies to enable market access beyond BARMM.

**Q. What happens to national law when the Parliament enacts a BAA on this subject?**

A. The BAA governs S&T policy within BARMM. National S&T laws apply suppletorily where the BAA is silent. RA 8439/11312 (Magna Carta for Scientists) applies directly to all government S&T personnel regardless of BAA enactment. Constitutional provisions (Art. XIV, Sec. 10-13) always apply.

### G. Legislative Gaps and Recommended Legislation

**Q. Are there overlapping or unclear jurisdictions between Bangsamoro and national authority?**

A. Three areas of jurisdictional ambiguity: (1) **Scholarships** — both DOST-SEI and MOST-BASE operate parallel scholarship programs targeting the same student population, with no coordination framework; (2) **Standards and testing** — DOST's metrology and testing mandate overlaps with MOST's integrated laboratory function (BAA 13, Sec. 9(8)); no MOA delineates which agency's standards apply within BARMM; (3) **Technology transfer and IP** — RA 10055 governs nationally, but MOST's mandate includes technology commercialization (BAA 13, Sec. 3(c)) — unclear whether MOST or TAPI has primary jurisdiction for IP from MOST-funded research.

**Q. What pending Parliament Bills address the gaps?**

A. PB No. 388 (Basilan Science High School) addresses one narrow gap — science education infrastructure in Basilan. However, no pending bill addresses the broader gaps: no S&T code, no research council, no innovation fund, no science park charter. The major legislative gaps remain unaddressed by pending bills.

### H. Development and Policy Connection

**Q. Which BDP 2023-2028 goals and Mas Matatag na Bangsamoro Agenda pillars depend on this power?**

A. **BDP Chapter 8** is entirely dedicated to this power. Development goal: ***"Build sustainable science, technology and innovation ecosystem."*** Three sub-sector objectives: (1) Strengthen STI ecosystem and governance — target: 10 LGUs with STI-responsive plans, 5 STI-related enterprises by 2028; (2) Improve R&D systems — target: 200 completed research studies (from 20), 35 registered IPs (from 3), 15 technology transfers (from 0), R&D at 1% of GRDP (from 0.23%), 2,500 R&D personnel (from 1,100); (3) Enhance S&T laboratory access — target: 3 STI laboratories (from 1), 4 STI schools (from 2). Priority programs: Regional S&T Scholarship (PHP 108M), STI Week (PHP 45M), Science Centrum/Museum (PHP 250M), SETUP (PHP 180M), BRSHS (PHP 1.125B), S&T Park/TBI (PHP 200M), Metrology Laboratory (programmed).

**Mas Matatag na Bangsamoro Agenda**: **Pillar 2** (Mas Matatag na Kabuhayan) depends on technology and innovation for economic development — MSME technology upgrading, *halal* economy, and BIMP-EAGA integration. **Pillar 3** (Mas Matatag na Pamayanan) depends on science education for human capital development.

**Q. What development outcomes are at risk if the legislative gaps remain unaddressed?**

A. Without a standalone S&T code: (1) the **1% GRDP R&D target** is at risk because there is no statutory funding mechanism requiring a minimum R&D budget allocation; (2) the **S&T Park and TBI** cannot be established with proper legal charter, governance, and fiscal incentives; (3) **technology transfer** remains at zero (baseline) because no BARMM-specific IP and commercialization framework exists; (4) **innovation ecosystem** development stalls — RA 11337's startup provisions apply nationally but BARMM cannot customize incentives for local conditions; (5) the Bangsamoro Research Council remains uncreated, leaving R&D priorities set ad hoc rather than by a dedicated body with competitive grant mechanisms. BAA 13 alone cannot deliver these outcomes — it creates the institution but not the regulatory and fiscal architecture.

### I. Power-Specific Questions

**Q. How does the BSHS System relate to MOST's broader S&T mandate?**

A. BAA 40 places the BSHS System under ***"the administrative control and supervision of the Ministry of Science and Technology"***[^24] rather than MBHTE (which handles general education under power (v)). This is a deliberate policy choice — science high schools are instruments of S&T policy, not merely educational institutions. The MOST Minister chairs the Board of Trustees. This mirrors the national model where PSHS operates under DOST (not DepEd). However, PB No. 388 (Basilan Science High School) places the proposed school under MBHTE — a potential institutional inconsistency if enacted.

**Q. What is unique about MOST's *halal* quality standards function?**

A. MOST's mandate to ***"contribute to the development and quality standards including halal for food and non-food industry through testing, analysis and capacity-building"***[^20] is a function with no DOST counterpart. This positions MOST at the intersection of science and Shari'ah — providing the technical capacity (*halal* testing and certification) that the Bangsamoro Halal Board requires. MOST staff serve on the Halal Board Secretariat (BAA 13, Book VI, Title XIV, Sec. 36). A standalone S&T code should formalize this function with laboratory accreditation standards and *halal* certification protocols.

**Q. How does the BASE scholarship program compare to DOST's national scholarship?**

A. MOST administers three scholarship tracks: (1) **BASE** (Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education) — PHP 80,000 per scholar per annum, 714 recipients across four batches; (2) **BASE MERIT** — 208 scholars with merit-based selection; (3) **Mujahideen/Mujahidat Assistance for Science Education** — 444 scholars targeting children of former combatants. The national DOST-SEI scholarship offers similar financial support but has no conflict-sensitive track. BARMM students can access both programs — the regional BASE and the national DOST scholarship — creating a dual pipeline for S&T human capital development.

**Q. What R&D infrastructure does BARMM have and what is missing?**

A. Existing: one operational S&T laboratory (Bangsamoro Science High School Technology Laboratory / BSHTL) in Cotabato City providing testing, calibration, and metrology services. Missing: (1) a **Science Park** or **Technology Business Incubator** to convert R&D into commercial products (BDP targets PHP 200M investment); (2) additional **metrology laboratories** in island provinces (Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi) — currently, all testing requires travel to Cotabato City; (3) a **Science Centrum/Museum** for public S&T awareness (BDP targets PHP 250M); (4) specialized research facilities for agriculture, fisheries, and health aligned with BARMM's economic priorities.

**Q. Why does the BOL not require national law consistency for S&T but does for education?**

A. Art. IX, Sec. 16 requires the education system to ***"conform to the minimum standards set by the National Government"***[^5] — ensuring national educational quality benchmarks. Art. IX, Sec. 21 (S&T) contains no equivalent requirement. The distinction reflects the BOL's approach: education standards must be nationally consistent (a student's qualifications must be recognized nationwide), but S&T policy — R&D priorities, innovation strategies, technology transfer — can be tailored to regional needs without national uniformity. MOST can set its own R&D agenda, establish unique scholarship programs (like the Mujahideen/Mujahidat track), and integrate *halal* standards — none of which require national approval. The exception is international cooperation (Art. XII, Sec. 28), which requires National Government review.
