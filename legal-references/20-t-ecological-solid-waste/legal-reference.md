# (t) Ecological Solid Waste Management and Pollution Control

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(t)** — "Ecological solid waste management and pollution control;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(t) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | No specific BOL provision requires national law consistency for this power. General limitation under Art. V, Sec. 2 chapeau ("Subject to Section 20, Article X of the Constitution and this Organic Law") applies. |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG); Ministry of Health (MOH); constituent LGUs |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 12 — Environment and Natural Resources |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Ecological Solid Waste Management Act enacted; solid waste management exercised through MENRE under BAA 13 and RA 9003 applies suppletorily | Enact a Bangsamoro Ecological Solid Waste Management Code, including provisions on single-use plastics, MRF establishment, and LGU compliance |
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Pollution Control Act enacted; pollution regulation exercised through MENRE under BAA 13 | Consider a comprehensive Bangsamoro Environmental Code covering pollution control, hazardous waste, and air/water quality standards |
| **Type B** — Partial implementation | PB No. 248 (single-use plastics regulation), PB No. 378 (solid waste management mechanisms), and PB No. 389 (single-use plastics ban) have been filed in Parliament but not yet enacted as BAAs | Prioritize passage of pending solid waste and plastics bills |
