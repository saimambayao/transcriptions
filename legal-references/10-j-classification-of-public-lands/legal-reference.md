# (j) Classification of Public Lands

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(j)** — "Classification of public lands;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(j) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes — Art. XIII, Sec. 17 (para. 1): classification "shall be recommended by the Bangsamoro Government to the President"; Presidential approval required |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR); Land Management Bureau; National Mapping and Resource Information Authority (NAMRIA) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 12 — Environment and Natural Resources |
| **Priority Code** | — |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Public Land Classification Act enacted; land classification functions exercised through MENRE under BAA 13 | Enact legislation establishing a systematic classification program, criteria, and institutional process |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 17 limits Bangsamoro authority to recommendation; the President retains final approval over land classification | Establish a standing coordination mechanism with DENR and the Office of the President for expedited classification approvals |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | Constitutional Art. XII, Sec. 3 restricts land categories to four classes (agricultural, forest/timber, mineral, national parks) — Bangsamoro cannot create new categories | Bangsamoro classification recommendations must conform to constitutional land categories |
