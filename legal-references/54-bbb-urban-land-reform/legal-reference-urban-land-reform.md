# (bbb) Urban Land Reform and Land Use

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(bbb)** — "Urban land reform and land use;"

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(bbb) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes — Art. XIII, Sec. 17 (last para.): "Subject to the provisions of the Constitution, the Parliament may enact an urban land reform and land use program for the region." Constitutional limitation applies (1987 Constitution, Art. XIII, Secs. 9-10 on urban land reform and housing). |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | Ministry of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE); Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA); Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG); constituent LGUs |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 3 — Spatial Development Strategy |
| **Priority Code** | — |

| BDP Chapter | Relevant Goals/Strategies | Connection to Power |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Chapter 3 — Spatial Development Strategy | Spatial development framework; urban-rural continuum; land use zoning; urban growth centers | Direct implementation — spatial strategy depends on regional land use program |
| Chapter 12 — Environment and Natural Resources | Sustainable land management; prevention of land degradation | Land use regulation supports environmental sustainability goals |
| Chapter 9 — Social Services | Housing and human settlements; resettlement for IDPs and conflict-affected populations | Urban land reform addresses housing needs particularly for Marawi IDPs |

## Legislative Gaps

Note: most powers are exercised by MOAs through their BAA 13 mandate even without standalone legislation.

| Gap Type | Description | Recommended Action |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No Bangsamoro Urban Land Reform and Land Use Act enacted despite express BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 17 authority for Parliament to enact such program; MHSD exercises human settlements functions through BAA 13 | Enact a Bangsamoro Urban Land Reform and Land Use Act establishing the regional land use framework, zoning standards, and land reform mechanisms |
| **Type A** — No standalone legislation | No comprehensive regional land use plan or regional framework for LGU CLUPs; land use planning functions exercised through BPDA under BAA 13 | Develop a Regional Physical Framework Plan and companion legislation requiring LGU compliance with regional land use standards |
| **Type C** — National law interaction | BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 17 subjects urban land reform to "the provisions of the Constitution" — particularly Art. XIII, Secs. 9-10 (just distribution, resettlement, eviction safeguards) | Any Bangsamoro land reform program must comply with constitutional urban land reform provisions and RA 7279 standards |
