## Executive Summary

BARMM's ability to diverge from national laws and standards is governed by a **5-layer constraint framework** derived from an exhaustive scan of all 126 constraining provisions across 18 BOL articles — not the 5-area model the Legal Research Guidebook currently presents. Validation of 35 completed legal references confirmed the divergence classifications are substantively correct, with 4 minor labeling inconsistencies identified and 1 budgeting superlative requiring correction. Three Supreme Court decisions (2024-2025) provide the judicial test: BARMM legislative power "must always operate within the bounds of the Constitution and national statutes" (*Ali v. BTA*, GR-02219, 2025). Equal protection applies to all BARMM legislation but with different scrutiny levels — rational basis for regulatory standards (easy to satisfy) vs. strict scrutiny for fundamental rights. The BOL does NOT supersede all national laws — it amends only 19 specific laws and national law continues to apply for residual powers, consistency-required areas, and legislative gaps.

## The Judicial Test (2025)

The Supreme Court in *Ali et al. v. BTA Parliament* (GR-02219, September 30, 2025) stated: [S][1]

> *"While Congress may establish autonomous regions and grant them legislative authority, such authority must always operate within the bounds of the Constitution and national statutes."*

The Court described the Art. X, Sec. 20 limitation as **"clear and categorical."** This is the most current SC articulation of the divergence constraint. Where the BOL uses "subject to national laws" language, the SC's framing confirms those qualifiers are **enforceable constitutional limits** — not aspirational language. [S][1]

**Three SC decisions form the divergence jurisprudence:**

| Case | GR No. | Year | Key Holding |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Province of Sulu v. Medialdea* | 242255 | 2024 | BOL constitutional; BARMM natural resource powers upheld against challenge |
| *Sinsuat v. Ebrahim* | 271741 | 2024 | BAAs 53/54/55 partially unconstitutional — plebiscite must include parent municipality voters |
| *Ali v. BTA Parliament* | E-02219 | 2025 | BAA 77 unconstitutional — "clear and categorical" limit on BARMM legislative authority |

## The 5-Layer Constraint Framework

### Layer 1: Constitutional Rights and Mandates (Absolute)

The Constitution sets the floor that no legislation — national or Bangsamoro — can breach. [S][2]

- **Art. III** (Bill of Rights) — due process, equal protection, association, religion
- **Art. XII, Sec. 15** — promote cooperatives as instruments of social justice
- **Art. XIII, Sec. 3** — full protection to labor
- **Art. XIV, Sec. 1** — right to quality education
- **Art. II, Sec. 13** — protect children's welfare

The Constitution constrains **purposes and rights**, not specific numbers or procedures. A cooperative membership threshold of 10 or 30 is a statutory choice — the Constitution requires only that the POLICY serves the PURPOSE. [P][2]

### Layer 2: BOL "Shall Prevail" Supremacy Clauses (11 provisions)

National law overrides any conflicting BAA. [S][3]

| BOL Provision | Subject | Language |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. VII, Sec. 39** | Civil service | "national civil service laws, rules, and regulations shall prevail" |
| **Art. V, Sec. 1** | Residual powers | Powers not granted remain with National Government |
| **Art. XII, Sec. 9** | Tax limitations | 12 categories of taxes BARMM cannot impose |
| **Art. XVII, Sec. 1** | BOL amendment | Only Congress can amend the BOL |
| **Art. XIII, Sec. 39** | Telecom franchises | Congress retains national franchise power |

Civil service (Art. VII, Sec. 39) is confirmed as the **most constrained** of all 55 powers — it is the only provision with an express conflict-resolution supremacy clause covering BARMM's own legislation. [S][3][4]

### Layer 3: BOL Subject-Specific Minimum Standards (7 provisions)

BARMM must meet or exceed national minimums. Can go above, cannot go below. [S][3]

| BOL Provision | Subject | Verbatim Language | National Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Art. IX, Sec. 16** | Basic education | "shall conform to the minimum standards set by the National Government" | DepEd K-12 |
| **Art. IX, Sec. 17** | Vocational/TESD | same language | TESDA Training Regulations |
| **Art. X, Sec. 8** | Shari'ah judges | "regular member of the Philippine Bar" | SC Bar admission |
| **Art. VII, Sec. 13** | Compensation | "shall adopt the Revised Compensation and Position Classification System...RA 6758" | Salary Standardization |
| **Art. XII, Sec. 3** | Local treasurer qualifications | "guided by the standards set by DOF-BLGF" | DOF-BLGF standards |
| **Art. VI, Sec. 10** | Municipality creation criteria | "criteria laid down in RA 7160...shall be satisfied" | LGC criteria |
| **Art. XII, Sec. 20(b)** | Salary ceiling | "No official...entitled to a salary rate higher than the maximum fixed...by applicable laws" | RA 6758 grades |

### Layer 4: BOL Institutional Jurisdiction Retention (47 provisions)

National agencies keep authority within BARMM. Cannot be displaced. [S][3]

Key retentions: Supreme Court (courts, bar, personnel), COA (exclusive auditor), CSC (civil service), COMELEC (elections), BSP (Islamic banking supervision), PNP/BFP/BJMP (security), DOE (fossil fuels — joint exercise), CHED/TESDA/DepEd (education coordination), FDA (food/drug/tobacco), PSA (civil registration).

### Layer 5: BOL "Shall Not Diminish" Floors (8 provisions)

Existing rights and privileges are the floor — one-way ratchet. [S][3]

- Art. VI, Sec. 10 — LGU privileges under RA 7160
- Art. IX, Sec. 3 — IP rights under RA 8371 "shall not in any manner diminish"
- Art. XII, Sec. 8 — existing investor incentives
- Art. XII, Sec. 36 — IP revenue share rights

## When Can BARMM Diverge?

### Going Below National Statutory Standards

Permitted when ALL of these are true: [P][3][5]

1. The BOL has **no conformity requirement** (not in Layers 2-5)
2. The lower standard still serves the **constitutional purpose**
3. Art. III rights are maintained

~20 of the 55 powers fall in this category (cooperatives, housing, culture, solid waste, humanitarian services, customary laws, etc.).

### Going Above National Standards

Almost always permitted. [S][3][5]

Constraints on going above:
- Don't set a standard so high it **defeats the constitutional purpose** (500-member cooperative = prohibits formation) [P]
- Don't exceed **salary caps** (Art. XII, Sec. 20(b)) [S]
- If the higher standard **burdens a fundamental right**, strict scrutiny applies [P]

### National Laws After the BOL

The BOL does NOT supersede all national laws. [S][3]

| Scenario | New National Law Applies? |
|---|---|
| Residual national power | **Yes — fully** |
| Enumerated + BOL requires consistency | **Yes — as framework** |
| Enumerated + no consistency + no BAA | **Yes — suppletorily** |
| Enumerated + no consistency + BAA enacted | **No — BAA governs** |

Congress cannot override the BOL through ordinary legislation — Art. XVII, Sec. 1 requires deliberate amendment. Laws of general application (RA 6713, RA 10173, RA 9710) always apply. [S][3]

## Equal Protection: Scrutiny Levels

Equal protection applies to every classification but at different levels: [S][2][5]

| Classification | Scrutiny | Practical Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Suspect categories (race, religion) | **Strict** | Hard to justify |
| Fundamental rights | **Strict** | Hard to justify |
| Regulatory standards (membership thresholds, training hours) | **Rational basis** | Almost always survives |

The BOL provides the "substantial distinction" for rational basis — Congress recognized BARMM's "common and distinctive" characteristics. [S][2][3]

**30-member cooperative threshold:** Survives rational basis. Equal protection is NOT the binding constraint — only the constitutional PURPOSE test matters (does it prohibit formation?). [P][5]

## Case Study: RA 11596 vs. PD 1083 (Anti-Child Marriage)

RA 11596 (2021) applies in BARMM because: [S][6][7][8]

1. **Criminal law is residual national power** — *prision mayor* penalties exceed BARMM's *arresto menor* jurisdiction [S]
2. **Implements constitutional mandates** — Art. II Sec. 13, CEDAW, CRC; BOL Art. IX Sec. 14 itself requires child protection [S]
3. **BOL did not amend PD 1083 Art. 16** — Art. XVIII Sec. 4(d) amended 10 PD 1083 articles on court jurisdiction, not marriage age [S]
4. **No exemption** — one-year transitional period expired circa December 2022 [S]
5. **BTA Parliament accepted** — Resolution No. 259 urged forums but did not oppose [S]

## Decision Tree

```
Can a proposed BAA set a different standard from national law?

Step 1: CHECK THE BOL (Layers 2-5)
├── "Shall prevail"? → Cannot contradict. Stop.
├── "Minimum standards"? → Must meet or exceed. Stop.
├── "Consistent with"? → Must not contradict. Stop.
├── "Shall not diminish"? → Floor locked. Stop.
├── National agency retains jurisdiction? → Cannot displace. Stop.
└── BOL is SILENT? → Proceed to Step 2.

Step 2: CHECK THE CONSTITUTION (Layer 1)
├── Serves constitutional PURPOSE? → Permissible.
├── Maintains Art. III rights? → Proceed.
└── BAA can set its own standard — below, above, or different.

Step 3: EQUAL PROTECTION (if BAA classifies)
├── Fundamental right? → Strict scrutiny.
├── Suspect category? → Strict scrutiny.
└── Regulatory standard? → Rational basis (almost always survives).
```
