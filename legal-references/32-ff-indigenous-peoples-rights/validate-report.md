## Critical Issues

### CRITICAL: "MIAP" Fabrication (Lines 144, 429)
The document twice claims the ministry was "originally MIAP" or "renamed from MIAP." There is **zero evidence** for the abbreviation "MIAP" in the BOL, BAA 13, BAA 64, or BAA 85. All sources consistently use "Ministry of Indigenous Peoples' Affairs" -- which abbreviates to "MIPA." The "MIAP" reference should be removed.

### HIGH: Art. XVII, Sec. 4(c) Mischaracterization (Line 112)
The document says RA 8371 is "Listed among national law provisions **not repealed** by the BOL." In reality, Art. XVII, Sec. 4 is titled "**Amendatory Clause**" -- these provisions are **amended**, not preserved unchanged. While IPRA otherwise continues to apply (per the non-diminution clause), the specific characterization of Art. XVII, Sec. 4(c) as a "non-repeal" provision is misleading.

### MEDIUM: Unverified BAA Numbers (BAA 49, BAA 89)
BAA 49 (Bangsamoro Local Governance Code) and BAA 89 (Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Act) are cited but not verified from local source files.

### LOW: Many BAA 64 Section References Unverified
Approximately 20 BAA 64 section references (Sec. 20-21, 22-27, 28, 34-37, 56, 63-64, 66-70, 84-88, 89-91, 93, 98, 100) could not be verified because the full text was not read. The verified sections (Sec. 3, 4, 12, 54, 77-80, 96-97, 102, 105) were all accurate, suggesting the unverified ones are likely correct.
