The entire Article VIII is dedicated to the **Wali** (ceremonial head of the Bangsamoro Government):
- **Sec. 1:** Wali as ceremonial head consistent with parliamentary form
- **Sec. 2(b):** Must be able to read and write in Filipino, English, **or Arabic**
- **Sec. 3:** Ceremonial duties: opening Parliament session, administering oath, dissolving Parliament
- **Sec. 4:** First Wali appointed by BTA; succeeding Wali serves 6-year terms
- **Sec. 6:** Removal grounds include failure to dissolve Parliament after no-confidence vote
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:199-234`
- **Aspect:** Islamic governance concept (Wali) formally institutionalized; Arabic literacy recognized

### 1.17 Art. IX, Sec. 18 -- Madaris Educational System

> "...the Bangsamoro Government shall establish, maintain, and supervise *Madaris* education in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region."

> "The Parliament shall enact legislation to develop and strengthen the *Madaris* educational system..."
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:336-340`
- **Aspect:** Three-part mandate: (1) supervise madaris, (2) integrate Islamic/Arabic studies in public schools, (3) enact madaris legislation

### 1.18 Art. X, Sec. 1 -- Justice System Foundation

> "The dispensation of justice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be in consonance with the Constitution, *Shari'ah*, traditional or tribal laws, and other relevant laws."
> "*Shari'ah* or Islamic law forms part of the Islamic tradition derived from religious precepts of Islam, particularly the *Qur'an* and *Sunnah*."
> "*Shari'ah* shall apply exclusively to cases involving Muslims. Where a case involves a non-Muslim, *Shari'ah* law may apply only if the non-Muslim voluntarily submits to the jurisdiction of the *Shari'ah* court."
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md` (via `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:384-394`)
- **Aspect:** Foundational: Shari'ah is one of three justice pillars (with Constitution and tribal law); exclusive Muslim application with voluntary non-Muslim submission

### 1.19 Art. X, Sec. 2 -- Shari'ah Courts as Part of Philippine Judiciary

> "*Shari'ah* courts within the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction shall form part of the Philippine judicial system subject to the supervision of the Supreme Court."
> "The Congress of the Philippines may create additional *Shari'ah* courts in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region upon the recommendation of the Supreme Court."
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:396-398`
- **Aspect:** Shari'ah courts are national courts under SC supervision, not purely autonomous

### 1.20 Art. X, Sec. 3 -- Sources of Shari'ah

> "Principal Sources: (a) *Al-Qur'an* or the Koran; (b) *Al-Sunnah* or traditions of Prophet Muhammad SAW;"
> "Secondary Sources: (a) *Al-Ijma* or Consensus; (b) *Al-Qiyas* or Analogy."
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:400-412`
- **Aspect:** BOL codifies the four sources of Shari'ah in Philippine law

### 1.21 Art. X, Sec. 4 -- Parliament's Shari'ah Legislative Power

> "The Parliament shall have the power to enact laws on personal, family, and property law jurisdiction."
> "...laws governing commercial and other civil actions not provided for under Presidential Decree No. 1083...and criminal jurisdiction on minor offenses punishable by *arresto menor* or *ta'zir*..."
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:414-416`
- **Aspect:** Parliament can legislate on Shari'ah matters beyond PD 1083, including ta'zir criminal offenses

### 1.22 Art. X, Sec. 5 -- Shari'ah Circuit Court Jurisdiction

Full exclusive original jurisdiction over:
- (a) Offenses under PD 1083
- (b) Marriage, divorce, betrothal, mahr, property disposition on divorce, maintenance, restitution of marital rights
- (c) Communal property disputes
- (d) Ta'zir offenses under Shari'ah law enacted by Parliament
- (e) Real property actions under Shari'ah law (up to P400,000)
- (f) Civil actions where law governing relations not specified (up to P200,000)
- **Source:** `02-articles-VI-to-IX.md:418-443`
- **Aspect:** Comprehensive personal status and minor criminal jurisdiction

### 1.23 Art. X, Sec. 6 -- Shari'ah District Court Jurisdiction

Full exclusive original jurisdiction over:
- (a) Custody, guardianship, legitimacy, paternity/filiation under PD 1083
- (b) Estate settlement, probate, administration of deceased Muslims
- (c) Absence/death declarations, Muslim Registry corrections
- (d) Shari'ah-compliant contracts between Muslims
- (e) Mandamus, prohibition, injunction, certiorari, habeas corpus (appellate aid)
- (f) Family home constitution, name change, insanity commitment
- (g) All other personal/real actions between Muslims (except forcible entry/unlawful detainer)
- (h) Special civil actions (interpleader/declaratory relief) between Muslims
- (i) Real property under Shari'ah law (above P400,000)
- (j) Civil actions where governing law not specified (above P200,000)
- Plus: appellate jurisdiction over Shari'ah Circuit Courts per PD 1083, Art. 144
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md:18-41`
- **Aspect:** Broad civil jurisdiction paralleling RTC for Muslim parties

### 1.24 Art. X, Sec. 7 -- Shari'ah High Court (Created by BOL)

> "There is hereby created within the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction, as part of the Philippine judicial system, a *Shari'ah* High Court."
- Exclusive original jurisdiction: mandamus, prohibition, injunction, certiorari, habeas corpus; annulment of Shari'ah District Court judgments
- Exclusive appellate jurisdiction: over all Shari'ah District Courts (within or outside BARMM)
- Decisions final and executory except on questions of law (appeal to SC)
- Pending organization: Shari'ah District Court decisions appealable to Court of Appeals
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md:42-54`
- **Aspect:** New apex Shari'ah court -- did not exist before BOL; equivalent to Court of Appeals level

### 1.25 Art. X, Sec. 8 -- Qualifications of Shari'ah Judges

| Court Level | Age | Bar Membership | Practice | Shari'ah/Islamic Jurisprudence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Circuit Court | 30+ | Regular member | 5+ years | 2+ years |
| District Court | 35+ | Regular member | 10+ years | 2+ years |
| High Court | 40+ | Natural-born, regular member | 15+ years | 2+ years |

All must be Muslim citizens. SC may grant incumbent judges not in the Bar a reasonable period to qualify.
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md:56-66`
- **Aspect:** Dual qualification requirement: Philippine Bar + Shari'ah/Islamic Jurisprudence

### 1.26 Art. X, Sec. 9 -- Shari'ah High Court Composition

> "The *Shari'ah* High Court shall be composed of five (5) Justices, including the Presiding Justice."
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md:68`

### 1.27 Art. X, Sec. 10 -- Compensation and Rank Equivalences

- Shari'ah High Court Justices = Court of Appeals Justices
- Shari'ah District Court Judges = Regional Trial Court Judges
- Shari'ah Circuit Court Judges = Municipal Trial Court Judges
- **Source:** `03-articles-X-to-XII.md:70-74`
- **Aspect:** Shari'ah judges have full parity with regular court counterparts

### 1.28 Art. X, Sec. 11 -- Shari'ah Rules of Court

### 2.13 Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' (Secs. 52-56, lines 1406-1439)

> **Sec. 52:** "The Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' shall serve as the consultative body for policy-making through Islamic jurisprudence in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region."
- Attached to Office of the Chief Minister
- Composed of: Bangsamoro Mufti + 6 recognized ulama (one per province + SGA)
- Selection via *mashuwarah* (consultative process)

> **Sec. 53:** Mandate -- "strengthen Islamic preaching and guidance, and utilize knowledge and social capital of the Ulama for the common good"

> **Sec. 54:** Powers and Functions:
  - (a) Promulgate *fatwa* (religious edicts) on the whole breadth of Islamic jurisprudence
  - (b) Promote intra/inter-religious dialogue
  - (c) Consultant to Chief Minister on religious affairs
  - (d) Ramadan moon-sighting coordination (local and international)
  - (e) Other functions by law or Chief Minister

> **Sec. 55:** Organization:
  - Headed by Bangsamoro Mufti (appointed by CM, preferably from resident Ulama)
  - Secretariat with Executive Director
  - Divisions: Fatwa Division, Research Division, Information & Communications Division, Administration Section

> **Sec. 56:** Bangsamoro Mufti powers:
  - Call sessions with Islamic Jurists for promulgating fatawa
  - Preside over Darul-Ifta' sessions
  - Exercise overall management/supervision
  - Represent in interfaith dialogue
  - **Declare start and end of Ramadan, Eid'l Fitr, and Eid'l Adha**
  - Provide reference to Chief Minister on religious affairs and Islamic events
- **Source:** `BAA-13.md:1406-1439`
- **Aspect:** The Darul Ifta' is the highest Islamic advisory body in BARMM governance

## Task 4: PD 1083 -- Code of Muslim Personal Laws (Complete Scope)

**Source:** `/Users/saidamenmambayao/apps/transcriptions/legislation/national-laws/PD-1083.md`
**Date:** February 4, 1977 (Martial Law era)

### 4.1 Structure

PD 1083 is organized into **4 Books**:

**BOOK ONE -- GENERAL PROVISIONS**
- Title I: Title and Purposes of Code (Arts. 1-2)
- Title II: Construction and Definition of Terms (Arts. 3-7)
  - Key: Art. 3 -- PD 1083 prevails over laws of general application in case of conflict
  - Art. 6 -- Sunni schools (Hanafi, Hanbali, Maliki, Shafi'i) are the recognized schools
  - Art. 7(h) -- Muslim Law (Shari'a) = "all ordinances and regulations governing Muslims as found principally in the Qur'an and the Hadith"

**BOOK TWO -- PERSONS AND FAMILY RELATIONS**
- Title I: Civil Personality (Arts. 8-12)
- Title II: Marriage and Divorce (Arts. 13-57)
  - Chapter 1: Applicability (Art. 13) -- applies to both-Muslim marriages or Muslim male marriages
  - Chapter 2: Marriage (Nikah) -- requisites (ijab/qabul, wali, mahr), ceremony, prohibited marriages (consanguinity, affinity, fosterage), subsequent marriages, batil/fasid marriages, spousal rights/obligations, property relations
  - Chapter 3: Divorce (Talaq) -- 7 forms: talaq, ila, zihar, li'an, khul', tafwid, faskh; 'idda waiting period
- Title III: Paternity and Filiation (Arts. 58-64)
  - Legitimacy, acknowledgment (igra), adoption prohibition (no status transfer)
- Title IV: Support (Nafaqa) (Arts. 65-70)
  - Support during marriage and 'idda, ascendant/descendant obligations
- Title V: Parental Authority (Arts. 71-80)
  - Joint parental authority (father's decision prevails), custody rules (mother below 7, choice after 7), wali for marriage, guardian of property
- Title VI: Civil Registry (Arts. 81-88)
  - Shari'ah court clerks as District/Circuit Registrars for Muslim marriages, divorces, revocations, conversions

**BOOK THREE -- SUCCESSION**
- Title I: General Provisions (Arts. 89-98)
  - Order: sharers (ashab-ul-furud), residuaries (ashab-ul-mirath), distant kindred (dhaw-ul-arham), acknowledged kinsman/universal legatee/bait-ul-mal
- Title II: Testamentary Succession (Arts. 99-109)
  - Will (wasiya): max 1/3 of estate if heirs exist; testamentary waqf (wagf-bill-wasiya)
- Title III: Legal Succession (Arts. 110-128+)
  - Fixed shares (furud) per Islamic inheritance rules; detailed share tables for husband, wife, father, mother, daughters, sisters, brothers

**BOOK FOUR -- ADMINISTRATION AND ADJUDICATION** (not fully transcribed in the file, but covers):
- Shari'ah courts jurisdiction and organization
- Agama Arbitration Councils

### 4.2 Key PD 1083 Concepts Relevant to Shari'ah Legal Reference

| Concept | PD 1083 Articles | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Marriage (Nikah) | Arts. 14-22 | Islamic marriage contract with ijab/qabul, wali consent, mahr |
| Prohibited marriages | Arts. 23-26 | Consanguinity, affinity, fosterage |
| Polygyny | Art. 27 | Permitted with conditions of just treatment |
| Divorce (Talaq) | Arts. 45-55 | Seven forms including judicial (faskh) |
| 'Idda | Arts. 56-57 | Waiting period after divorce or death |

## Task 5: Constitutional Provisions

### 5.1 Art. III, Sec. 5 -- Religious Freedom

> "No law shall be made respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof. The free exercise and enjoyment of religious profession and worship, without discrimination or preference, shall forever be allowed. No religious test shall be required for the exercise of civil or political rights."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:94`
- **Aspect:** Non-establishment and free exercise clauses; basis for Shari'ah as allowed religious law system

### 5.2 Art. VI, Sec. 28(3) -- Tax Exemption for Mosques

> "Charitable institutions, churches and parsonages or convents appurtenant thereto, **mosques**, non-profit cemeteries, and all lands, buildings, and improvements, actually, directly, and exclusively used for religious, charitable, or educational purposes shall be exempt from taxation."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:276`
- **Aspect:** Mosques constitutionally exempt from taxation

### 5.3 Art. VI, Sec. 29(2) -- Non-Appropriation Clause

> "No public money or property shall be appropriated, applied, paid, or employed, directly or indirectly, for the use, benefit, or support of any sect, church, denomination, sectarian institution, or system of religion..."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:282`
- **Aspect:** Tension point: state funding of Islamic institutions (madaris, Darul Ifta') must navigate this clause

### 5.4 Art. X, Sec. 1 -- Autonomous Regions in Muslim Mindanao

> "There shall be autonomous regions in **Muslim Mindanao** and the Cordilleras as hereinafter provided."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:604`
- **Aspect:** Constitutional basis for Muslim Mindanao autonomy

### 5.5 Art. X, Sec. 15 -- Creation of Autonomous Regions

> "There shall be created autonomous regions in **Muslim Mindanao** and in the Cordilleras consisting of provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical areas sharing common and distinctive historical and cultural heritage, economic and social structures, and other relevant characteristics **within the framework of this Constitution** and the national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:634`
- **Aspect:** Key "within the framework" clause -- all Shari'ah governance must operate within constitutional limits

### 5.6 Art. X, Sec. 16 -- General Supervision

> "The President shall exercise general supervision over autonomous regions to ensure that the laws are faithfully executed."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:636`

### 5.7 Art. X, Sec. 17 -- Reserved Powers

> "All powers, functions, and responsibilities not granted by this Constitution or by law to the autonomous regions shall be vested in the National Government."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:638`

### 5.8 Art. X, Sec. 18 -- Organic Act Requirements

> "...The organic acts shall likewise provide for **special courts with personal, family, and property law jurisdiction** consistent with the provisions of this Constitution and national laws."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:640`
- **Aspect:** Constitutional mandate for special courts (= Shari'ah courts) with personal, family, property jurisdiction

### 5.9 Art. X, Sec. 20 -- Legislative Powers of Autonomous Regions

> "(4) Personal, family, and property relations;"
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:651`
- **Aspect:** Constitutional basis for Parliament's Shari'ah legislative power over personal status

### 5.10 Art. XIV, Sec. 3(3) -- Religious Instruction in Schools

> "At the option expressed in writing by the parents or guardians, religion shall be allowed to be taught to their children or wards in public elementary and high schools..."
- **Source:** `1987-The-Philippines-Contitution.md:928`
- **Aspect:** Constitutional basis for Islamic and Arabic studies in BARMM public schools

### Constitutional Foundation
1. **Art. III, Sec. 5** -- Religious freedom (non-establishment + free exercise)
2. **Art. X, Sec. 15** -- Autonomous regions "within the framework" of the Constitution
