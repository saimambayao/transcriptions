# (n) Creation of Sources of Revenues

> **BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(n)** — ***"Creation of sources of revenues;"***

## Power Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **BOL Basis** | Art. V, Sec. 2(n) — enumerated power |
| **National Law Consistency Required** | Yes. Art. XII, Sec. 14 mandates the Revenue Code be ***"in accordance with the Constitution and this Organic Law."*** Art. XII, Sec. 6 requires taxes to be ***"consistent with the principles of equalization, equity, accountability, administrative simplicity, harmonization and economic efficiency, and fiscal autonomy."*** Art. XII, Sec. 9 imposes 12 categories of taxes BARMM **cannot** impose. The Intergovernmental Fiscal Policy Board (IFPB) coordinates fiscal matters. |
| **Lead Ministry/Office** | **Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM)** — Bangsamoro Finance Office (fiscal/tax policy) and Bangsamoro Revenue Office (assessment/collection) (BAA 13, Book VII, Title IV) |
| **Supporting Agencies** | Bangsamoro Treasury Office; Bangsamoro Budget Office; Intergovernmental Fiscal Policy Board (IFPB); Bureau of Internal Revenue (national — current collector) |
| **BDP Chapter(s)** | Chapter 12 (Fiscal Autonomy and Revenue Mobilization); Chapter 3 (Governance) |
| **Priority Code** | **Bangsamoro Revenue Code** — Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a) lists it as a **mandatory** transition priority and Art. XII, Sec. 14 mandates its enactment. **NOT yet enacted as of BAA 89.** |

## Legal Briefer

A granular Q&A analysis. Answers stay as close as possible to the language of the law. All verbatim statutory text in ***bold italic***. Key terms in **bold**. Themes follow the legal hierarchy and analytical flow: BOL grant → constitutional floor → national law → implementation → Shari'ah dimension → divergence → gaps → development.

### A. BOL Grant and Scope

**Q. What power does the BOL grant the Bangsamoro Government over revenue creation?**

A. Article V, Section 2(n) enumerates ***"creation of sources of revenues"*** as an enumerated power. Art. XII, Sec. 6 elaborates: the Bangsamoro Government ***"shall have the power to create its own sources of revenues and to levy taxes, fees, and charges."*** This is a **broad fiscal power** — the authority to generate revenue through taxation and other means — but it is among the **most heavily conditioned** of all 55 powers, with 12 categories of exclusions and mandatory intergovernmental coordination.[^1][^2]

**Q. What specific BOL provisions elaborate on this power beyond Art. V, Sec. 2?**

A. Art. XII (Fiscal Autonomy) is the **most detailed article** in the entire BOL, with 41 sections governing revenue, taxation, block grants, and fiscal policy. Key provisions:

   (1) Art. XII, Sec. 6: enumerates **12 sources of revenue** — taxes, fees, block grant, natural resource shares, national tax shares, GOCC dividends, grants, loans, appropriations, and quarry taxes. LGUs continue exercising RA 7160 taxing powers.[^2]

   (2) Art. XII, Sec. 7: taxation principles — ***"uniformity and equity,"*** not ***"unjust, excessive, oppressive, confiscatory."*** Mandates a ***"progressive, responsive, and culture sensitive system of taxation."*** Requires prior public hearing.[^3]

   (3) Art. XII, Sec. 8: tax exemptions and incentives — Parliament may grant by majority vote; ***"shall not diminish national revenues."***[^4]

   (4) Art. XII, Sec. 9: **12 categories of excluded taxes** and **4 exclusive BARMM taxes** (see Section I (Power-Specific Questions) for full enumeration).[^5]

   (5) Art. XII, Sec. 10: **75-25 revenue sharing** of national taxes collected in BARMM.[^6]

   (6) Art. XII, Sec. 11: mandates creation of the **Bangsamoro Revenue Office (BRO)**.[^7]

   (7) Art. XII, Sec. 14: mandates enactment of a ***"Bangsamoro tax and revenue code."***[^8]

   (8) Art. XII, Sec. 15-19: **annual block grant** — 5% of net NIRC collections.[^9]

**Q. Does the BOL mandate the Parliament to enact specific legislation for this power?**

A. Yes — **two express mandates**:

   (1) Art. XII, Sec. 14: ***"The Parliament shall enact a Bangsamoro tax and revenue code."*** This is the Revenue Code mandate — one of the mandatory priority codes under Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a). **Not fulfilled as of BAA 89.**[^8]

   (2) Art. XII, Sec. 13: ***"The Parliament shall enact a law detailing the shares of constituent local government units"*** in the 75% national tax share. **Not fulfilled as of BAA 89.**[^10]

**Q. Does the BOL create any specific body, office, or institution for this power?**

A. Yes. Art. XII, Sec. 11 mandates the Parliament to ***"establish by law the Bangsamoro Revenue Office."*** BAA 13, Book VII, Title IV, Chapter 7 (Sec. 19-20) establishes the **BRO** under MFBM with functions including assessment and collection of all Bangsamoro taxes. Art. VI, Sec. 4 creates the **Intergovernmental Fiscal Policy Board (IFPB)** to coordinate fiscal policy between the National Government and the Bangsamoro Government.[^7][^11]

**Q. What conditions, limitations, or qualifications does the BOL place on this power?**

A. The most heavily conditioned of all 55 powers:

   (1) **12 categories of excluded taxes** (Art. XII, Sec. 9) — see Section I (Power-Specific Questions) for full enumeration.[^5]

   (2) **Constitutional consistency** (Art. XII, Sec. 14) — Revenue Code must be ***"in accordance with the Constitution and this Organic Law."***[^8]

   (3) **Uniformity and equity** (Art. XII, Sec. 7) — taxes cannot be ***"unjust, excessive, oppressive, confiscatory."***[^3]

   (4) **Non-diminution of national revenues** (Art. XII, Sec. 8) — tax exemptions cannot reduce national revenue.[^4]

   (5) **IFPB coordination** (Art. XII, Sec. 9, last para.) — disputes on the 4 exclusive taxes resolved by IFPB.[^5]

   (6) **No dual taxation** (Art. XII, Sec. 7) — National Government and BARMM cannot impose similar taxes on the same entity.[^3]

### B. Constitutional Considerations

**Q. What constitutional provisions apply to revenue creation?**

A. Four categories:

   (1) **Art. VI, Sec. 29**: ***"No money shall be paid out of the Treasury except in pursuance of an appropriation made by law."*** The fundamental constitutional fiscal discipline requirement.

   (2) **Art. VI, Sec. 28(1)**: ***"The rule of taxation shall be uniform and equitable. The Congress shall evolve a progressive system of taxation."*** The national taxation framework.

   (3) **Art. X, Sec. 20**: Autonomous regions operate within the constitutional framework — all fiscal powers are subject to this constraint.[^12]

   (4) **Art. III, Sec. 1**: Equal protection and due process apply to tax assessments, collections, and penalties.

**Q. What constitutional mandates exist for fiscal legislation or institutions?**

A. Art. VI, Sec. 29 mandates appropriation by law. Art. X, Sec. 18 guarantees autonomous regions ***"their own sources of revenue"*** and entitles them to ***"an equitable share of national revenues"*** — the constitutional basis for the block grant and revenue sharing provisions.

### C. National Law Considerations

**Q. What does the current national legal framework provide for taxation?**

A. The national framework is **comprehensive and mature**: **RA 8424** (NIRC) as amended by **RA 10963** (TRAIN), **RA 11534** (CREATE), and other laws governs all national internal revenue taxes. **RA 7160** (Local Government Code) governs LGU taxation. **BIR** administers and collects all national taxes. The framework includes income tax, VAT, excise taxes, documentary stamp taxes, donor's tax, estate tax, and other national taxes.

**Q. What national laws currently apply in BARMM?**

A. All national tax laws apply **directly** — there is no Bangsamoro Revenue Code to displace or supplement them:

   (1) **RA 8424 (NIRC)** as amended — all national tax laws govern collections in BARMM. BIR collects.[^13]
   (2) **RA 7160** — LGU taxing powers continue per Art. XII, Sec. 6.[^14]
   (3) **RA 10963 (TRAIN)**, **RA 11534 (CREATE)** — amendments apply.

**Q. What national institutions exercise authority in BARMM for this area?**

A. The **Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR)** exercises full tax administration authority within BARMM — assessment, collection, enforcement. Art. XII, Sec. 11 provides that ***"Until such time that the Bangsamoro Revenue Office is established, tax collection shall be undertaken by the Bureau of Internal Revenue."*** BIR maintains regional district offices within BARMM. When the BRO is fully operational, BIR and BRO will share collection responsibilities per IFPB rules.[^7]

### D. Implementation

#### D.1 How the Bangsamoro Government Implements This Power

**Q. Which ministry or office is primarily responsible for exercising this power?**

A. The **Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM)** is the lead ministry. BAA 13, Book VII, Title IV defines MFBM's mandate, powers, and organizational structure. Under MFBM: the **Bangsamoro Finance Office** (fiscal/tax policy — Sec. 15-16), the **Bangsamoro Budget Office** (expenditure planning — Sec. 17-18), the **Bangsamoro Revenue Office** (assessment/collection — Sec. 19-20), and the **Bangsamoro Treasury Office** (custodian of revenues — Sec. 10-14).[^15]

**Q. What standalone legislation (BAAs) has the Parliament enacted for this power?**

A. **No Revenue Code** has been enacted. **BAA 84** (Budget System Act) provides the expenditure framework. **BAA 85** (GAA FY 2026) is the annual appropriation. The fiscal power's **revenue side** — the taxing authority — remains unlegislated.

**Q. What are MFBM's mandate and functions under BAA 13?**

A. BAA 13, Book VII, Title IV:

   **Mandate** (Sec. 2): ***"The Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management is primarily mandated to evolve sound, efficient and responsive fiscal management and utilization of resources. It shall be responsible for the formulation, institutionalization and administration of fiscal policies."***[^15]

   **Powers and functions** (Sec. 3) — **15 enumerated functions** including:
   - (a) ***"Formulate medium-term expenditure and annual plans covering the Bangsamoro Government's resources mobilization efforts"***
   - (c) ***"Formulate, institutionalize and administer fiscal and tax policies"***
   - (d) ***"Supervise, direct, manage, and control the collection of Bangsamoro Government revenues"***
   - (e) ***"Act as custodian of, and manage all financial resources of Bangsamoro Government"***
   - (i) ***"Ensure the implementation of policies and measures on local revenue administration"***
   - (n) ***"Conceptualize and administer the Bangsamoro Government's compensation and position classification consistent with national standards"***[^16]

   **Bangsamoro Finance Office** (Sec. 15-16): 5 divisions — Fiscal Policy Development, Revenue Policy Development, Corporate Affairs, International and Other Finance, Strategy Economics and Results. Functions include formulating fiscal/tax policies and supervising revenue collection.[^17]

   **Bangsamoro Revenue Office** (Sec. 19-20): 3 divisions — Collections, Assessments, Document Processing. Plus Revenue District Offices. Functions: ***"Assess and collect all taxes, fees and charges; formulate preventive measures for tax evasion."***[^18]

   **Bangsamoro Treasury Office** (Sec. 10-14): custodian of financial assets; investment strategy; cash flow analysis; public debt management.[^19]

**Q. What can MFBM NOT do without a Revenue Code?**

A. Without a Revenue Code, MFBM **cannot**: (a) impose the **4 exclusive BARMM taxes** (CGT, DST, Donor's Tax, Estate Tax) — there is no enabling legislation even though the BOL grants the power; (b) establish **tax rates, bases, and exemptions** for Bangsamoro-specific taxes; (c) define **BRO's full regulatory authority** — the BRO exists under BAA 13 but has no comprehensive enabling statute; (d) create **tax incentive programs** for BARMM investors; (e) establish **penalty provisions** for tax evasion; (f) integrate ***zakat*** into the formal tax administration; (g) establish the **LGU share formula** for the 75% national tax share (Art. XII, Sec. 13 mandate).

**Q. Have any parliamentary resolutions signaled intent to legislate in this area?**

A. Yes — Res. No. 137 (Mandanas ruling impact), Res. No. 85 (tax relief for conflict-affected taxpayers), and Res. No. 493 (adherence to salary schedule updates) signal fiscal policy attention, though none directly call for the Revenue Code's enactment.

#### D.1.A Institutional Validation

**Q. Is the assigned lead MOA the correct one for this power?**

A. Yes. **MFBM** is the correct lead ministry — mirroring the national structure where the Department of Finance leads fiscal policy and the BIR (under DOF) administers tax collection. The BRO under MFBM is the BARMM counterpart to BIR.

**Q. What BARMM institutions currently exist for this power?**

A. - **MFBM** (lead ministry — BAA 13, Book VII, Title IV; BAA 85)
   - **Bangsamoro Finance Office** (fiscal/tax policy — BAA 13, Sec. 15-16)
   - **Bangsamoro Revenue Office** (assessment/collection — BAA 13, Sec. 19-20)
   - **Bangsamoro Budget Office** (expenditure planning — BAA 13, Sec. 17-18)
   - **Bangsamoro Treasury Office** (custodian — BAA 13, Sec. 10-14)
   - **IFPB** (intergovernmental fiscal coordination — BOL Art. VI, Sec. 4; Art. XII, Sec. 37-41)
   - **LGU treasurers** (local tax collection — RA 7160, BAA 49)

**Q. What institutions does the BOL mandate that have NOT been created yet?**

A. The **Bangsamoro Revenue Office** exists institutionally under BAA 13 but lacks its **enabling Revenue Code** (Art. XII, Sec. 11 and Sec. 14). Without the Revenue Code, BRO cannot independently exercise its full collection authority — BIR continues to collect.

**Q. Does this power require creating NEW institutions that the BOL does not specifically name?**

A. A **Bangsamoro Tax Appeals Board** or **Regional Tax Court equivalent** may be needed to adjudicate tax disputes within BARMM — providing an accessible forum that does not require taxpayers to litigate at the Court of Tax Appeals in Manila. The Revenue Code could establish this.

#### D.2 How the National Government Implements This Power

**Q. How does the national government currently exercise authority in this area within BARMM?**

A. **BIR** exercises full tax administration authority through its regional district offices. BIR assesses and collects all national taxes — income tax, VAT, excise taxes, documentary stamp taxes, donor's tax, estate tax — within BARMM. The 75% share of collections is remitted to the Bangsamoro Government. The **block grant** is released by DBM. The **IFPB** coordinates fiscal policy between the national and Bangsamoro governments.

**Q. What is the status of devolution for this power?**

A. Devolution is **incomplete**. The BOL grants the taxing power and mandates the BRO, but without a Revenue Code: (a) BIR continues to collect; (b) the 4 exclusive BARMM taxes have not been activated; (c) the BRO has no enabling framework for independent collection. The revenue power remains largely **unexercised** — BARMM relies on the block grant and national tax sharing rather than own-source revenues.

#### D.3 BARMM Implementation

**Q. What has the BARMM accomplished so far in exercising this power?**

A. **Institutional establishment** — MFBM and all sub-offices (Finance Office, Revenue Office, Budget Office, Treasury Office) exist under BAA 13. **BAA 84** (Budget System Act) provides expenditure discipline. Annual GAAs (BAA 3, 15, 23, 32, 56, 65, 85) demonstrate functioning appropriation processes. However, the **revenue generation side** — the actual exercise of the taxing power — is essentially untouched. BARMM has not imposed any of the 4 exclusive taxes. The BRO has not commenced independent tax collection.

**Q. Where is the BARMM stronger than the national government in this area?**

A. Two potential advantages — neither yet realized:

   (1) ***Zakat* integration** — BARMM can build a revenue system that recognizes *zakat* as a complementary wealth redistribution mechanism, potentially increasing voluntary compliance among Muslim taxpayers. The national system has no equivalent.

   (2) **Culture-sensitive tax administration** — Art. XII, Sec. 7's mandate for a ***"culture sensitive system of taxation"*** authorizes approaches tailored to BARMM's economic context — agricultural communities, conflict-affected areas, informal economies, and Islamic finance.

**Q. What is holding BARMM back from full implementation?**

A. The **single biggest constraint** is the **absence of a Bangsamoro Revenue Code**. This is the most critical unfulfilled legislative mandate in the entire BOL — it is one of the mandatory priority codes under Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a) and has its own express mandate in Art. XII, Sec. 14. Without it, BARMM cannot: impose the 4 exclusive taxes, activate the BRO's collection authority, establish LGU share formulas, or reduce dependence on the block grant. **PB No. 286** (Revenue Code) has been filed but not enacted.

#### D.4 National Government Implementation

**Q. What has the national government built for this area that BARMM does not yet have?**

A. - **Legislative depth**: The NIRC (RA 8424) as amended has hundreds of sections covering every aspect of taxation, refined over decades with TRAIN (RA 10963), CREATE (RA 11534), and other amendments.
   - **Institutional infrastructure**: BIR has 19 regional district offices, 121 revenue district offices, thousands of staff, and specialized enforcement divisions.
   - **IT systems**: BIR's **Electronic Filing and Payment System (eFPS)**, **Electronic BIR Forms (eBIRForms)**, taxpayer registration database, and audit management systems.
   - **Enforcement**: BIR has investigation, prosecution, and enforcement capabilities that the BRO does not yet possess.

**Q. Where does the national system fall short for BARMM?**

A. (1) **No *zakat* integration** — BIR does not recognize *zakat* as a tax-equivalent payment. (2) **No culture-sensitive administration** — BIR operates under a uniform national framework that does not accommodate BARMM's unique economic context (informal agricultural economies, conflict-affected areas, Islamic commercial practices). (3) **Limited presence** — BIR offices in remote BARMM areas (island provinces, mountainous Lanao del Sur) are underresourced. (4) **No Shari'ah-compliant penalty structures** — surcharges and interest on delinquent taxes follow conventional interest-based formulas.

**Q. What should BARMM replicate, adapt, or replace from the national system?**

A. - **Replicate**: tax administration processes (assessment, collection, auditing), taxpayer registration systems, compliance monitoring, and enforcement mechanisms.
   - **Adapt**: tax base definitions and rates (within BOL parameters), penalty structures (Shari'ah-compliant alternatives), tax incentive programs (tailored to BARMM investment priorities), and taxpayer services (local languages, mobile services for remote areas).
   - **Replace**: nothing in terms of excluded taxes (Art. XII, Sec. 9 defines what BARMM cannot tax). For the 4 exclusive taxes and any new Bangsamoro taxes, build from the ground up with BARMM-specific design.

### E. Shari'ah Considerations

**Q. Does this power have a Shari'ah dimension?**

A. **Yes — significant.** Two areas: (1) ***zakat*** is a **pillar of Islam** and a distinct form of wealth redistribution that predates and differs from secular taxation. A Bangsamoro Revenue Code has the opportunity to integrate *zakat* administration alongside secular taxes — potentially through PB No. 160 (zakat-income tax integration). (2) The prohibition of ***riba*** (interest) affects tax penalty structures — interest-based surcharges on delinquent taxes should have Shari'ah-compliant alternatives.

**Q. What does the BOL say about Shari'ah in relation to this power?**

A. Art. XII, Sec. 7 mandates a ***"culture sensitive system of taxation"*** — the most direct textual basis for Shari'ah-informed tax policy. The BOL does not mention *zakat* by name, but Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes Parliament to enact laws on Shari'ah matters including commercial law, which could encompass *zakat* administration.

**Q. How should Shari'ah compliance be integrated into Bangsamoro legislation for this power?**

A. The recommended approach is **optional dual-track**: a Revenue Code that provides both conventional tax administration and Shari'ah-compliant features — including *zakat* administration (potentially as an offset or credit against secular taxes), Shari'ah-compliant penalty structures, and recognition of *waqf* (endowment) properties in the tax base.

### F. Divergence Analysis

**Q. To what extent can the BARMM diverge from national tax law?**

A. **Moderate but heavily bounded.** As established in the **BOL Grant and Scope** section (Section A), BARMM has the power to create revenue sources but faces 12 exclusion categories. BARMM can diverge by: (a) imposing the **4 exclusive taxes** with BARMM-specific **administration** (collection, procedures, filing requirements) — but the tax rates and bases remain governed by the NIRC, so this divergence is **administrative, not substantive**; (b) creating **new taxes** not covered by Art. XII, Sec. 9's exclusions; (c) establishing **different tax incentive programs**; (d) integrating ***zakat*** into the revenue system; (e) creating **Shari'ah-compliant penalty structures**. BARMM **cannot** diverge by: imposing income tax (except on banks), VAT, customs duties, excise taxes, or any of the other 12 excluded categories.

**Q. If BARMM enacts different standards, what are the implications?**

A. (1) **For taxpayers within BARMM**: They would face BARMM-imposed taxes (CGT, DST, Donor's Tax, Estate Tax) administered by BRO instead of BIR — potentially with different administration but NIRC-mandated rates. (2) **For cross-boundary entities**: Art. XII, Sec. 12 establishes the situs rule — taxes paid where business is conducted. The IFPB resolves allocation disputes for entities operating in both jurisdictions. (3) **For LGUs**: Art. XII, Sec. 13 requires Parliament to determine LGU shares — affecting every municipal and provincial government in BARMM.

**Q. What happens to national tax law when the Parliament enacts the Revenue Code?**

A. National tax law continues for all taxes BARMM cannot impose (the 12 exclusions). For the 4 exclusive taxes and any new Bangsamoro taxes, the Revenue Code governs. BIR and BRO may share collection responsibilities per IFPB-promulgated rules.

### G. Legislative Gaps

**Q. Are there overlapping or unclear jurisdictions between Bangsamoro and national authority?**

A. Yes — the **most complex intergovernmental fiscal relationship** in the BOL. Key overlaps: (a) the boundary between BIR and BRO collection authority — when BRO starts collecting, which taxes does it handle vs. BIR?; (b) the 4 exclusive taxes — Art. XII, Sec. 9 provides that if BARMM imposes them, BIR ***"shall continue to levy and collect"*** until BARMM does — but the transition mechanism is undefined; (c) the IFPB's role in resolving disputes — operational capacity of the IFPB is unclear.

**Q. What pending Parliament Bills address the gaps?**

A. **PB No. 286** (Revenue Code) — if enacted, would fulfill Art. XII, Sec. 14 and Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a). **PB No. 160** (zakat-income tax integration) — addresses culture-sensitive revenue. However, even if PB No. 286 passes, the **LGU share law** (Art. XII, Sec. 13 mandate) and detailed **IFPB implementing rules** remain gaps.

### H. Development Connection

**Q. Which BDP 2023-2028 goals and Mas Matatag na Bangsamoro Agenda pillars depend on this power?**

A. **BDP 2023-2028**, Chapter 12 (Fiscal Autonomy) — the Revenue Code is the single most critical enabling legislation for fiscal self-sufficiency. **Mas Matatag na Bangsamoro Agenda**, **Pillar 1 (Good Governance)** depends on own-source revenues for genuine autonomy, and **Pillar 3 (Economic Resilience)** requires fiscal capacity to fund economic development programs.

**Q. What development outcomes are at risk if the legislative gaps remain unaddressed?**

A. Without a **Revenue Code**: BARMM remains **entirely dependent** on the block grant and national tax sharing for its revenues. The **4 exclusive taxes** (CGT, DST, Donor's Tax, Estate Tax) — worth potentially billions in revenue — cannot be activated. The **BRO** cannot exercise independent collection authority. **LGU shares** in national tax collections remain unlegislated, creating fiscal uncertainty for every municipality and province. **Own-source revenue** targets in the BDP cannot be met. The Revenue Code is the **most consequential unfulfilled legislative mandate** in the entire BOL — it directly determines whether BARMM achieves fiscal autonomy or remains fiscally dependent on Manila.

### I. Power-Specific Questions

**Q. What are the 12 categories of taxes BARMM CANNOT impose?**

A. Art. XII, Sec. 9 enumerates:

   (a) **Income tax** — except when levied on banks and other financial institutions
   (b) **Customs duties**, registration fees of vessels, wharfage, tonnage dues, and all other customs fees — except BARMM-registered vessels and BARMM-maintained wharves
   (c) **Taxes on goods** carried into or out of BARMM territorial jurisdictions — except tolls on BARMM-constructed bridges/roads
   (d) **Taxes on agricultural and aquatic products** sold by marginal farmers or fisherfolk
   (e) **Taxes on pioneer/non-pioneer enterprises** certified by BOI or Parliament — for 6 and 4 years respectively
   (f) **Excise taxes** on NIRC-enumerated articles and petroleum products
   (g) **Percentage or VAT** on sales, barters, exchanges — except as provided by national law
   (h) **Taxes on gross receipts** of transportation contractors and common carriers
   (i) **Taxes on reinsurance/retrocession premiums**
   (j) **Taxes on exported Philippine products** — except as provided by Congress
   (k) **Taxes on cooperatives** registered under RA 6810 and RA 6938 and **countryside/barangay business enterprises**
   (l) **Taxes on the National Government**, its agencies, instrumentalities, and LGUs — except GOCCs primarily organized to do business[^5]

**Q. What 4 taxes can BARMM impose exclusively?**

A. When **all taxable elements are within BARMM**, Parliament may impose to the **exclusion of BIR**: (1) **Capital Gains Tax** — on sale/disposition of capital assets (real property); (2) **Documentary Stamp Tax** — on documents, instruments, loan agreements; (3) **Donor's Tax** — on gratuitous transfers; (4) **Estate Tax** — on transfer of deceased's estate. Tax rates must follow the NIRC. If BARMM does not impose them, BIR continues to collect. Both cannot impose simultaneously.[^5]

**Q. Why is the Revenue Code the most consequential unfulfilled mandate in the BOL?**

A. Because it is: (1) one of the **5 mandatory priority codes** under Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a) — the only one still unfulfilled; (2) the **enabling legislation** for the 4 exclusive taxes and own-source revenue generation; (3) the prerequisite for **BRO operational independence** from BIR; (4) the basis for determining **LGU fiscal shares** (Art. XII, Sec. 13); and (5) the foundation for **fiscal autonomy** — the BOL's central promise. Without it, BARMM's fiscal powers exist on paper but not in practice.

**Q. What is the Intergovernmental Fiscal Policy Board (IFPB) and what role does it play?**

A. The **IFPB** is created by Art. VI, Sec. 4 as a coordination body between the National Government and the Bangsamoro Government. Art. XII, Sec. 37-41 define its functions: (a) promulgating rules on the 4 exclusive taxes' taxable elements (Sec. 9); (b) resolving disputes between BIR and BRO (Sec. 9); (c) determining GOCC dividend shares (Sec. 6(f)); (d) annual reporting on fiscal operations (Sec. 40); (e) full disclosure of financial affairs (Sec. 41). The IFPB is a **critical intergovernmental mechanism** without which the Revenue Code cannot function effectively — it resolves the cross-jurisdictional issues that inevitably arise when two governments share a tax base.[^11]

**Q. How does the block grant relate to the revenue creation power?**

A. The **block grant** (Art. XII, Sec. 15-19) and the **revenue creation power** (Art. XII, Sec. 6-14) are two **complementary pillars** of BARMM's fiscal framework. The block grant — 5% of net NIRC collections — provides the **baseline funding** that ensures BARMM can operate regardless of its own-source revenue performance. The revenue creation power provides the **growth opportunity** — the ability to generate own-source revenues above and beyond the block grant. Currently, BARMM relies almost entirely on the block grant because the revenue creation power has not been activated through a Revenue Code. The BDP's fiscal autonomy vision depends on progressively shifting the revenue mix from block grant dependence toward own-source revenue generation.[^9]
