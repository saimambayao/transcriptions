## Training Levels

### Four-Tier Framework

| Level | Target Audience | Duration | Focus |
|-------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Foundation** | Emerging leaders, supervisors | 8-16 hours | Leadership basics, self-awareness |
| **Intermediate** | Team leaders, managers | 24-40 hours | Styles, competencies, teams |
| **Advanced** | Senior leaders, executives | 40+ hours | Strategic, transformational, culture |
| **Executive** | Top leadership, executives | 16-24 hours | Vision, change, organizational |

### Level Objectives

**Foundation:**
- Understand what leadership is
- Develop self-awareness
- Learn basic leadership styles
- Build communication skills

**Intermediate:**
- Master multiple leadership styles
- Develop emotional intelligence
- Lead teams effectively
- Make better decisions

**Advanced:**
- Lead organizational change
- Develop transformational capacity
- Build leadership culture
- Coach future leaders

**Executive:**
- Set organizational vision
- Navigate complex challenges
- Lead adaptive change
- Develop leadership legacy

## Foundation Leadership Course

### Standard Duration: 8-16 hours (1-2 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. What is Leadership?** | Definition, nature, leader vs manager | 1-2 hours |
| **2. Self-Awareness** | Strengths, weaknesses, values, blind spots | 2-3 hours |
| **3. Leadership Styles Intro** | Overview of major styles, when to use | 2-3 hours |
| **4. Communication Essentials** | Active listening, feedback, influence | 2-3 hours |
| **5. Leading Others** | Motivation basics, delegation intro | 1-2 hours |
| **6. Personal Leadership Plan** | Goal setting, development action plan | 1-2 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Define leadership and distinguish from management
2. Identify personal leadership strengths and areas for growth
3. Recognize different leadership styles and their applications
4. Apply basic communication and influence skills
5. Create a personal leadership development plan

### Key Concepts

**Leader vs Manager:**
| Leader | Manager |
|--------|---------|
| Sets direction | Plans and budgets |
| Aligns people | Organizes and staffs |
| Motivates and inspires | Controls and problem-solves |
| Produces change | Produces order |

**CCL Fundamental 4:**
1. Self-Awareness
2. Communication
3. Influence
4. Learning Agility

## Intermediate Leadership Course

### Standard Duration: 24-40 hours (3-5 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Leadership Theories** | Trait, behavioral, contingency overview | 3-4 hours |
| **2. Leadership Styles Deep Dive** | Transformational, servant, situational | 4-6 hours |
| **3. Emotional Intelligence** | Self-awareness, self-management, social skills | 4-6 hours |
| **4. Team Leadership** | Building teams, motivation, delegation | 4-6 hours |
| **5. Decision-Making & Problem-Solving** | Models, adaptive challenges | 3-4 hours |
| **6. Leading Change** | Change models, resistance, communication | 3-4 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Compare and contrast leadership theories
2. Apply multiple leadership styles situationally
3. Demonstrate emotional intelligence competencies
4. Build and lead effective teams
5. Navigate change and adaptive challenges

### Module Details

**Module 2: Leadership Styles**
- Transformational Leadership (Four I's)
- Servant Leadership (10 characteristics)
- Situational Leadership (4 styles, 4 development levels)
- Goleman's Six Styles

**Module 3: Emotional Intelligence**
| Domain | Competencies |
|--------|--------------|
| Self-Awareness | Emotional awareness, accurate self-assessment |
| Self-Management | Emotional control, adaptability, achievement |
| Social Awareness | Empathy, organizational awareness |
| Relationship Management | Influence, conflict management, teamwork |

**Module 4: Team Leadership**
- Tuckman's Team Stages (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing)
- Motivation: Intrinsic vs Extrinsic
- Delegation Framework
- Psychological Safety

**Module 5: Decision-Making**
- Rational decision-making model
- Adaptive vs technical challenges (Heifetz)
- Group decision-making techniques

## Advanced Leadership Course

### Standard Duration: 40+ hours (5+ days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Strategic Leadership** | Vision, strategic thinking, alignment | 6-8 hours |
| **2. Transformational Leadership** | Deep dive, organizational transformation | 6-8 hours |
| **3. Adaptive Leadership** | Complex challenges, systems thinking | 6-8 hours |
| **4. Leading Culture** | Organizational culture, values, change | 6-8 hours |
| **5. Authentic Leadership** | Values, purpose, genuine leadership | 4-6 hours |
| **6. Developing Leaders** | Coaching, mentoring, succession | 4-6 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Set and communicate organizational vision
2. Lead transformational change
3. Navigate adaptive challenges
4. Shape organizational culture
5. Develop future leaders

### Module Details

**Module 2: Transformational Leadership**
- Four I's in depth
- Charismatic vs transformational
- Building shared vision
- Inspiring commitment

**Module 3: Adaptive Leadership**
- Technical vs Adaptive Challenges
- Dance Floor/Balcony metaphor
- Productive disequilibrium
- Giving work back to people

**Module 6: Developing Leaders**
- 70-20-10 Model
- Coaching framework
- Mentoring relationships
- Succession planning

## Assessment Methods

### Knowledge Assessment

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Pre-test | Baseline knowledge |
| Post-test | Learning gain |
| Case analysis | Application skills |
| Reflection journals | Self-awareness development |

### Skills Assessment

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Role plays | Leadership style practice |
| Simulations | Decision-making, change |
| 360-degree feedback | Behavioral assessment |
| Leadership style inventory | Style awareness |

### Practical Assessment

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Leadership project | Real-world application |
| Action learning | Group problem-solving |
| Coaching practice | Development skills |
| Personal development plan | Sustained growth |

### Certification

| Certificate | Requirements |
|-------------|--------------|
| Foundation Certificate | Attendance + post-test (70%+) + personal plan |
| Intermediate Certificate | Attendance + assessments + team project |
| Advanced Certificate | Attendance + leadership project + 360 feedback |
| Executive Certificate | Program completion + organizational impact |

## Integration with /academy

### When to Use with /academy

Invoke `/leadership` with `/academy` when:
1. Creating leadership course/workshop
2. Developing leader development programs
3. Building executive education curriculum
4. Designing leadership certification programs

### Workflow

```
1. IDENTIFY training need and audience
         ↓
2. SELECT course level (Foundation/Intermediate/Advanced/Executive)
         ↓
3. USE /leadership for content expertise
         ↓
4. USE /academy for curriculum design
         ↓
5. DEVELOP learning materials
         ↓
6. IMPLEMENT training
         ↓
7. EVALUATE and improve
```

### Information to Provide /academy

When invoking with `/academy`:

1. **Course Level**: Foundation, Intermediate, Advanced, Executive
2. **Target Audience**: Supervisors, managers, senior leaders, executives
3. **Duration**: Hours/days available
4. **Delivery Mode**: In-person, online, hybrid
5. **Specific Topics**: If not full curriculum
6. **Context**: Cooperative, Social Enterprise, Government
7. **Assessment Requirements**: Certification needs

### Sample Request Format

```
Create a [course level] leadership course for [target audience]:
- Duration: [X hours/days]
- Delivery: [mode]
- Topics: [specific or full curriculum]
- Context: [Cooperative/SE/Government]
- Certification: [Yes/No + requirements]
```

### Context-Specific Curriculum Adaptations

**For Cooperatives:**
- Emphasize servant and democratic leadership
- Include facilitation and consensus-building
- Add member engagement elements
- Focus on shared/distributed leadership

**For Social Enterprises:**
- Include mission-driven leadership
- Add impact articulation
- Address mission drift prevention
- Include stakeholder engagement

**For Government:**
- Emphasize ethical leadership
- Include adaptive leadership for policy
- Add public service orientation
- Address political navigation
