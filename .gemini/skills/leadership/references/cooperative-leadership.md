## Workflow 4: Leading in Cooperatives

Practical guidance on how leadership applies specifically to cooperative organizations.

### Leadership in Democratic Organizations

Cooperatives are unique - leaders lead among equals (member-owners). This fundamentally changes leadership dynamics.

| Aspect | Traditional Organization | Cooperative |
|--------|--------------------------|-------------|
| **Authority Source** | Hierarchical position | Member mandate (elected) |
| **Decision Making** | Top-down | Democratic, consensus-building |
| **Accountability** | To superiors | To members (one-member-one-vote) |
| **Leadership Style** | Directive possible | Facilitative required |
| **Power Base** | Position power | Influence and expertise |

**Key Principles:**

1. **Leading Among Equals**: Officers are member-owners themselves; they lead peers, not subordinates
2. **Influence Without Positional Authority**: Cannot rely on "I'm the boss"; must build consensus
3. **Building Consensus vs Directing**: Success measured by member buy-in, not compliance
4. **Shared/Distributed Leadership**: Leadership spread across BOD, committees, management
5. **Servant Leadership as Natural Fit**: "Member-first" aligns with cooperative identity

> **RA 9520, Art. 44**: "The board of directors or trustees shall be responsible for the strategic direction and policies of the cooperative."

The BOD leads through policy and direction, not operational control.

### Leadership Styles for Cooperatives

Not all leadership styles work in cooperatives. Democratic governance requires collaborative approaches.

#### Recommended Styles

| Style | Why It Works | Application |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **Servant Leadership** | Member-centric, service-oriented; aligns with cooperative purpose | BOD serving members, managers supporting staff |
| **Democratic Leadership** | Matches one-member-one-vote principle | GA facilitation, committee decisions |
| **Transformational** | Inspires shared vision and collective action | Strategic planning, change initiatives |
| **Situational** | Adapts to member/staff development levels | Member education, staff supervision |
| **Coaching** | Develops capability, builds succession | Officer mentoring, emerging leader development |

#### Styles to Avoid (or Use Sparingly)

| Style | Why It's Problematic | Exception |
|-------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Coercive** | Violates democratic principles; breeds resentment | Crisis/emergency only |
| **Pacesetting** | Creates dependency on leader; unsustainable | Expert technical situations only |
| **Authoritarian** | Conflicts with member-ownership; erodes trust | Not recommended |

**Best Practice**: Cooperative leaders should master at least 3 styles (Servant, Democratic, Situational) and switch flexibly based on context.

### Leadership Roles in Cooperatives

Different roles require different leadership competencies.

#### BOD Chairperson

| Responsibility | Leadership Competency |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Preside over BOD and GA meetings | Democratic facilitation, parliamentary procedure |
| External representation | Communication, stakeholder management |
| Board effectiveness | Team leadership, conflict resolution |
| Strategic oversight | Visionary thinking, strategic planning |
| Officer coordination | Influence, collaboration |

> **RA 9520, Art. 45**: Qualifications for directors include Filipino citizenship, membership in good standing, and training requirements per CDA.

**Primus Inter Pares** (First Among Equals): The Chairperson has no vote advantage; they facilitate, not dictate.

#### Committee Chairs

| Committee | Leadership Focus |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Audit Committee** | Integrity, attention to detail, independence |
| **Election Committee** | Fairness, transparency, democratic process |
| **Ethics Committee** | Ethical judgment, conflict resolution, confidentiality |
| **Education Committee** | Training facilitation, member development, ETF management |
| **Credit Committee** (Credit Coops) | Risk assessment, member relations, policy adherence |

**Committee Chair Competencies:**
- Functional expertise in committee area
- Meeting facilitation skills
- Report writing and presentation
- Team coordination
- Policy recommendation development

#### Manager/CEO

| Responsibility | Leadership Competency |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Day-to-day operations | Operational leadership, execution |
| Staff supervision | People management, performance management |
| BOD implementation | Upward management, reporting |
| Member services | Service orientation, member relations |
| Business development | Entrepreneurial thinking, innovation |

**Manager's Leadership Challenge**: Leading staff while serving elected officials who are often less experienced in operations.

> **RA 9520, Art. 47**: "The manager shall be appointed by the board of directors or trustees and shall have such powers and duties as may be prescribed by the by-laws."

#### Emerging Leaders

Building the leadership pipeline is critical for cooperative sustainability.

| Development Stage | Leadership Focus |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Active Member** | Understanding cooperative values, participating in GA |
| **Committee Member** | Learning functional areas, contributing to decisions |
| **Committee Chair** | Leading small groups, managing specific functions |
| **Board Director** | Strategic thinking, governance oversight |
| **Senior Officer** | Chairperson, Vice-Chair, executive committee |

### Leadership Development in Cooperatives

Cooperatives have a built-in advantage: the Education and Training Fund (ETF).

#### Using ETF for Leadership Training

> **RA 9520, Art. 59**: "Education and Training Fund - This fund shall be used for the training and development of members, officers, and employees of the cooperative."

**ETF Allocation for Leadership:**

| Leadership Training | Eligible Use | Priority |
|---------------------|--------------|----------|
| Officer orientation | New BOD/committee training | High |
| Leadership seminars | External leadership programs | Medium |
| Governance training | Democratic governance, parliamentary procedure | High |
| Management training | Manager/staff development | Medium |
| Succession preparation | Emerging leader development | Medium |

**ETF Tip**: Allocate 30-40% of ETF specifically for leadership development to build sustainable governance capacity.

#### Leadership Succession Planning

Critical for cooperative continuity - voluntary BOD positions require planned transitions.

**Succession Planning Process:**

```
Step 1: Identify Critical Roles
        +-- BOD Chairperson
        +-- Key Committee Chairs
        +-- Manager position

Step 2: Assess Current Pipeline
        +-- Who has potential?
        +-- What gaps exist?
        +-- Timeline for transitions?

Step 3: Develop Successors
        +-- Assign to committees
        +-- Provide training (use ETF)
        +-- Mentor relationships
        +-- Stretch assignments

Step 4: Transition Planning
        +-- Knowledge transfer
        +-- Shadowing period
        +-- Gradual handover
```

**BOD Succession Timeline:**

| Year | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Year 1 | Identify high-potential members; engage in committees |
| Year 2 | Committee chair assignments; formal leadership training |
| Year 3 | Board observation/orientation; governance training |
| Year 4 | Board candidacy; election; officer development |

#### Mentoring Emerging Leaders

**Mentor-Mentee Framework:**

| Mentor Role | Activity |
|-------------|----------|
| **Guide** | Share experiences, provide perspective |
| **Teacher** | Explain governance, cooperative principles |
| **Coach** | Develop specific skills, give feedback |
| **Sponsor** | Recommend for positions, create opportunities |
| **Connector** | Introduce to networks, federation contacts |

**Mentoring Topics for Cooperative Leaders:**
- Understanding cooperative governance vs corporate governance
- Navigating the BOD-Management relationship
- Building member trust and engagement
- Managing voluntary (unpaid) officer roles
- Balancing cooperative principles with business realities

#### Youth Cooperative Leadership Programs

Building the next generation of cooperative leaders.

| Program Element | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Youth Committee** | Create a youth committee to engage young members |
| **Youth Representative** | Reserve a BOD seat for youth (under 30) |
| **Junior Officer Program** | Shadow positions for leadership exposure |
| **Youth Assembly** | Separate youth GA for leadership practice |
| **Leadership Camp** | Annual youth leadership training |

> Youth engagement ensures long-term cooperative sustainability and brings fresh perspectives.

### Practical Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Developing a BOD Chair for Effective Meeting Facilitation

**Situation**: Maria was elected BOD Chair but struggles with meeting facilitation. Meetings run long, dominant members take over, and decisions aren't clear.

**Development Plan:**

| Week | Activity | Outcome |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1-2 | Parliamentary procedure training | Understand Robert's Rules basics |
| 3-4 | Observe effective facilitator (federation meeting) | Learn techniques in action |
| 5-6 | Practice with committee meeting | Apply skills in lower-stakes setting |
| 7-8 | BOD meeting with mentor observation | Execute with feedback |
| 9-10 | Self-facilitation with debrief | Independent practice |

**Key Facilitation Skills to Develop:**
- Agenda management and time control
- Balanced participation (drawing out quiet members)
- Summarizing and confirming decisions
- Managing conflict constructively
- Calling for votes and recording resolutions

**ETF Use**: Parliamentary procedure seminar (external), facilitation skills workshop

---

#### Scenario 2: Building Committee Chair Leadership Capacity

**Situation**: The Audit Committee Chair, Juan, is technically strong but struggles to lead committee members and present findings to the BOD.

**Development Plan:**

| Gap | Intervention | Timeline |
|-----|--------------|----------|
| Meeting leadership | Committee chair training module | Month 1 |
| Presentation skills | Presentation workshop + practice | Month 2 |
| Report writing | Audit report writing coaching | Month 2-3 |
| Influence skills | 1:1 coaching with experienced chair | Ongoing |

**Committee Chair Leadership Curriculum:**
1. Role of committee in cooperative governance
2. Effective committee meeting management
3. Working with committee members
4. Preparing committee reports
5. Presenting to BOD and GA
6. Coordinating with management

**Mentoring Assignment**: Pair with experienced committee chair from another cooperative (arrange through federation)

---

#### Scenario 3: Creating a Leadership Succession Pipeline

**Situation**: Current BOD is aging (average age 58); no clear successors identified. Risk of governance vacuum in 5 years.

**Strategic Succession Plan:**

**Year 1: Assessment and Identification**
```
Actions:
+-- Conduct governance risk assessment
+-- Survey membership for leadership interest
+-- Identify 8-10 potential leaders (diverse ages, genders)
+-- Create "Emerging Leaders Program"
```

**Year 2: Development Foundation**
```
Actions:
+-- Assign emerging leaders to committees
+-- Conduct Foundation Leadership Training (16 hours)
+-- Establish mentor pairs (current officer + emerging leader)
+-- Provide cooperative governance orientation
```

**Year 3: Leadership Practice**
```
Actions:
+-- Promote to committee chair positions
+-- Intermediate Leadership Training (24 hours)
+-- External exposure (federation, CDA events)
+-- Stretch assignments (lead special projects)
```

**Year 4: Transition Preparation**
```
Actions:
+-- Board observation program
+-- Advanced Governance Training (16 hours)
+-- Election preparation and candidacy
+-- Formal handover planning
```

**Year 5: New Leadership**
```
Actions:
+-- Supported transition (outgoing officers as advisors)
+-- New officer orientation
+-- Continuous development
+-- Start next succession cycle
```

**ETF Budget Allocation (Annual):**
| Activity | % of ETF |
|----------|----------|
| Foundation training | 15% |
| Intermediate training | 15% |
| External programs/seminars | 10% |
| Mentoring program support | 5% |
| Total for Leadership | 45% |
