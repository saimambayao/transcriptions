# BARMM Context Adaptation for Lean Canvas

> **Source:** Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028

## BDP 2023-2028 Vision and Mission

### Vision
"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

### Mission
"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

### Overall Goal
**An empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro**
- **Empowered**: Improved community capacity through quality basic social services and economic opportunities
- **Cohesive**: Reduced violent conflict, forged partnerships in governance and development
- **Progressive**: Sustained ability to optimize resources and realize economic/human potential

## Lean Canvas Adaptation for BARMM Context

### Problem Block - Key Problems to Address

**For Cooperatives (from BDP Analysis):**
1. Low compliance rate (only 11.66%)
2. Lack of enacted Cooperative Code
3. Inadequate LGU participation
4. No appointed Municipal Cooperative Officers
5. Limited access to credit facilities

**For MSMEs:**
1. Most operate in informal sector (0.92% of national BNRs)
2. Limited connectivity (digital and physical)
3. Skills mismatch with available resources
4. Limited access to production/processing facilities
5. High cost of utilities
6. Technical competency gaps

**For Agri-Fishery Enterprises:**
1. High production costs
2. Post-harvest losses
3. Low technology adoption
4. Weak value chain integration
5. Limited access to financing and insurance
6. Weak extension services

**For Investment Climate:**
1. Low business confidence (peace/order perception)
2. Underdeveloped halal products and services
3. High cost of utilities
4. Weak BIMP-EAGA linkages
5. Poor Ease of Doing Business implementation
6. Inadequate data on investment opportunities

### Solution Block - BDP-Aligned Solutions

**Government Program Solutions:**
- CSEA compliance assistance and capacity building
- Halal certification and industry development (HIDeP)
- BIMP-EAGA market access facilitation
- Islamic banking products development
- Negosyo Center services for MSMEs
- Shared Service Facilities (SSF)

**Value Chain Solutions:**
- Post-harvest processing facilities
- Cold storage and logistics infrastructure
- Product packaging, labeling, and standardization
- Quality assurance and certification
- Export facilitation through BIMP-EAGA

**Capacity Building Solutions:**
- Enterprise-based education and training
- Skills training aligned to industry needs
- Financial literacy programs
- Technology adoption support
- Cooperative governance training (PESO Project)

### Unique Value Proposition Contexts

**For BARMM Enterprises:**
1. **Halal Advantage**: Only region with dedicated halal industry development
2. **BIMP-EAGA Gateway**: Strategic proximity to regional markets (Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei)
3. **Commodity Leadership**: #1 in seaweed, cassava nationally
4. **Cultural Authenticity**: Rich Bangsamoro heritage for tourism/products
5. **Peace Dividend**: Transition from conflict to development creates opportunities
6. **Government Support**: Dedicated BBOI, CSEA, and development programs

**Social Impact UVP Elements:**
- Contribution to peace and stability
- Support for former combatants (Combatant Transitional Program)
- Community empowerment and poverty reduction
- Cultural preservation and promotion
- Environmental sustainability

### Customer Segments - BARMM Context

**Primary Beneficiaries:**
- 12,214 registered cooperatives (need compliance support)
- 252,758 registered fisherfolk (largest in Philippines)
- 65,379 Agrarian Reform Beneficiaries
- Informal sector MSMEs (formalization opportunity)
- Former combatants (reintegration through cooperatives)

**Secondary Markets:**
- Domestic consumers (halal products demand)
- BIMP-EAGA markets (regional trade)
- International export markets (seaweed, specialty crops)
- Tourism visitors (nature, cultural, halal tourism)

### Key Metrics - BDP-Aligned

**Economic Indicators:**
- GRDP growth rate (target: 8-9%)
- Poverty reduction (target: 20-25%)
- Business registrations (BNR growth)
- Investment generation

**Cooperative/SE Specific:**
- Compliance rate (target: 11.66% to 50%)
- Number of functional cooperatives
- Established social enterprises
- Profitability indicators

**Employment Indicators:**
- Jobs created
- Unemployment rate (target: 3-5%)
- Underemployment rate (target: 4-7%)

1. Voluntary and Open Membership
2. Democratic Member Control
3. Member Economic Participation

5. Education, Training, and Information
6. Cooperation Among Cooperatives

## Social Lean Canvas for BARMM SEs

### Modified Lean Canvas for Social Enterprises

| Standard Block | SE Adaptation |
|----------------|--------------|
| Problem | Social/environmental challenge in BARMM |
| Customer Segments | Beneficiaries + Paying customers |
| UVP | Social mission + Market differentiation + Peace contribution |
| Solution | Impact-driven intervention |
| Key Metrics | Impact metrics + Financial sustainability |
| Revenue Streams | Earned income + Grants + Donations |
| Unfair Advantage | Mission alignment + Community trust + Government support |

### Shari'ah Compliance Considerations

**Halal Business Practices:**
- Avoid haram (prohibited) products
- No usury (riba) in financial dealings
- Ethical sourcing and labor practices

**Islamic Finance Principles:**
- Zakat integration (charitable giving)
- Waqf structures (endowment)
- Qard Hassan (interest-free loans)
- Islamic banking products
