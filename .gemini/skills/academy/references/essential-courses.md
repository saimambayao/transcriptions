## Cooperative Training (CDA-Mandated)

Per CDA MC 2015-09, these trainings are **mandatory** for cooperative officers.

### 1. Fundamentals of Cooperatives

**Status**: Priority 1 - Must Develop

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Code** | COOP-101 |
| **Type** | Online Course |
| **Duration** | 8 hours (Micro Coops) / 16 hours (Larger Coops) |
| **Target Audience** | All cooperative officers (BOD, committees, CEO/GM) |
| **Compliance** | CDA MC 2015-09 Mandatory |
| **Delivery** | Self-paced with final assessment |

**Learning Objectives**:
- Explain the history and philosophy of the cooperative movement
- List and apply the 7 International Cooperative Principles
- Describe the cooperative values (self-help, democracy, equality, equity, solidarity)
- Summarize key provisions of RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code)
- Interpret the cooperative's bylaws and policies
- Contribute to the cooperative's strategic planning process

**Module Structure**:

```
Module 1: Cooperative Philosophy & History (2h)
├── Lesson 1.1: Origins of the Cooperative Movement
├── Lesson 1.2: Rochdale Pioneers and Principles
├── Lesson 1.3: Cooperative Movement in the Philippines
└── Quiz 1

Module 2: Cooperative Values & Principles (3h)
├── Lesson 2.1: Self-Help, Self-Responsibility, Democracy
├── Lesson 2.2: Equality, Equity, and Solidarity
├── Lesson 2.3: The 7 Cooperative Principles in Practice
├── Lesson 2.4: Difference from Corporations
└── Quiz 2

Module 3: Legal Framework - RA 9520 (2h)
├── Lesson 3.1: Overview of RA 9520
├── Lesson 3.2: Types of Cooperatives
├── Lesson 3.3: Registration and Compliance
├── Lesson 3.4: Rights and Privileges
└── Quiz 3

Module 4: Bylaws & Strategic Planning (1h)
├── Lesson 4.1: Understanding Your Bylaws
├── Lesson 4.2: Introduction to Strategic Planning
└── Final Assessment
```

### 2. Governance and Management of Cooperatives

**Status**: Priority 2 - Must Develop

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Code** | COOP-201 |
| **Type** | Online Course |
| **Duration** | 8 hours (Micro Coops) / 16 hours (Larger Coops) |
| **Target Audience** | Board of Directors, Committee Officers, CEO/GM |
| **Prerequisite** | COOP-101 Fundamentals of Cooperatives |
| **Compliance** | CDA MC 2015-09 Mandatory |

**Learning Objectives**:
- Apply principles of good governance in cooperative management
- Distinguish roles and responsibilities of BOD vs Management
- Implement ethical standards and conflict of interest policies
- Design performance measurement systems
- Apply basic financial, HR, and marketing management concepts

├── Lesson 2.2: Officers (Chair, Secretary, Treasurer)
├── Lesson 2.3: Committees and Their Functions
├── Lesson 2.4: CEO/General Manager Role
├── Lesson 2.5: Board-Management Relations
└── Quiz 2

## Compliance Notes

### CDA MC 2015-09 Requirements

Officers must complete mandatory training **within the first half of their term**:

| Officer Type | Required Courses | Deadline |
|--------------|------------------|----------|
| All officers (Micro Coops) | COOP-101 (8h) + COOP-201 (8h) | First half of term |
| All officers (Larger Coops) | COOP-101 (16h) + COOP-201 (16h) | First half of term |
| Officers of S&C Coops (₱5M+) | + COOP-301 + COOP-302 + COOP-303 | First half of term |

**Non-compliance consequences**:
- Disqualification from future officer positions
- Affects cooperative's Certificate of Compliance

### CSEA Requirements

For social enterprises and cooperatives under CSEA:
- FRAMES compliance training recommended
- EDP completion encouraged for new registrations
