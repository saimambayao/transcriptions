# EDP-500: Enterprise Development Program

Intensive 3-day training program for cooperative and social enterprise development.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | EDP-500 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 3 days (24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Coop/SE managers, aspiring entrepreneurs |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Max Participants** | 25-30 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (80% attendance + action plan) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this program, participants will be able to:

1. **Assess** enterprise current state using structured analysis tools
2. **Design** a viable business model using Lean Canvas
3. **Create** financial projections and pricing strategies
4. **Develop** a marketing plan aligned with target market
5. **Formulate** a 90-day action plan for implementation

## Workshop Curriculum

### DAY 1: Foundation - Understanding & Designing Your Business (8 hours)

**Theme**: Know where you are, design where you're going

- Program overview and expectations

  - Key performance indicators for coops/SEs

- Template: Value Proposition Canvas

**Session 3.2: Unique Selling Point** (30 min)

### DAY 3: Action - Simulation & Implementation (8 hours)

**Theme**: Experience, learn, and turn plans into reality

#### Module 8: Business Simulation Game (3.5 hours)

**Simulation: Aisah's Pastil Enterprise**

**Setup Phase** (30 min)
- Team formation (5-6 teams of 5 participants)
- Role assignment (Manager, Finance, Marketing, Operations, Quality)
- Initial capital and resources distribution
- Game rules explanation

**Round 1: Market Entry** (40 min)
- Decision: Product pricing
- Decision: Production quantity
- Decision: Marketing spend
- Process: Customer orders based on decisions
- Output: Round 1 financial results

**Round 2: Competition** (40 min)
- Event: Competitor enters market
- Decision: Response strategy
- Decision: Quality investment
- Process: Market share calculation
- Output: Round 2 results

**Round 3: Growth Challenge** (40 min)
- Event: Bulk order opportunity
- Decision: Capacity expansion
- Decision: Financing options
- Process: Loan calculations
- Output: Round 3 results

**Debrief Session** (50 min)
- Team presentations (3 min each)
- Lessons learned discussion
- Connection to real enterprise challenges
- Winners announcement

**Lunch** (1 hour)

---

#### Module 9: Action Planning (2.5 hours)

**Session 9.1: Goal Setting (SMART)** (45 min)
- Type: LECTURE + WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - SMART goals framework
  - Prioritizing goals
  - Setting 90-day milestones
- Output: 3-5 SMART goals

**Session 9.2: Action Plan Development** (1.5 hours)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Breaking goals into tasks
  - Timeline creation
  - Resource identification
  - Accountability partners
- Template: 90-Day Action Plan
- Output: Complete action plan

**Session 9.3: Peer Review** (15 min)
- Type: GROUP_DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Action plan pair review
  - Quick feedback exchange
- Output: Refined action plan

**Break** (15 minutes)

---

#### Final Presentations (1.5 hours)

**Pitch Presentations**
- Each participant presents (2-3 minutes):
  - Enterprise overview
  - Value proposition
  - Key goals for next 90 days
- Panel feedback (facilitators + guest evaluators)
- Best pitch awards

---

#### Closing Ceremony (45 min)

**Post-Assessment** (15 min)
- Knowledge assessment
- Program evaluation survey

**Certificate Distribution** (20 min)
- Requirements: 80% attendance
- Recognition of achievements
- Photo opportunity

**Closing Remarks** (10 min)
- Program summary
- Next steps and resources
- Alumni network invitation
- Farewell

### Key Business Model Canvas Facts

**Alexander Osterwalder (born 1974):**
- Swiss business theorist, author, and entrepreneur
- PhD in Management Information Systems from University of Lausanne (2004)
- Thesis: "The Business Model Ontology - a proposition in a design science approach"
- Co-founded Strategyzer in 2010
- Business Model Generation sold over 4 million copies

**Business Model Canvas Development:**
- Nine building blocks first proposed in 2005
- Refined through collaborative workshops starting in 2008
- Popularized in 2010 book *Business Model Generation* (co-authored with Yves Pigneur)
- Co-created by 470 practitioners from 45 countries
- Used by over 5 million people worldwide

**Lean Canvas by Ash Maurya (2010):**
- Published in book *Running Lean* (2010)
- Adapted from Business Model Canvas for startups
- Focuses on potential risks and assumptions
- Influenced by Eric Ries' Lean Startup and Steve Blank's Customer Development
- Used by over 1 million entrepreneurs worldwide

**Value Proposition Canvas (2014):**
- Created by Alexander Osterwalder, Yves Pigneur, and Alan Smith
- Launched October 20, 2014 in book *Value Proposition Design*
- Zooms in on two BMC blocks: Customer Segments and Value Propositions
- Helps achieve product-market fit systematically
