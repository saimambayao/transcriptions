**Lesson 1.4: Building a Risk Framework** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"A risk framework isn't a thick document no one reads.
It's a living system that protects your cooperative."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE RISK MANAGEMENT CYCLE:
[Animated circular diagram]

Step 1: IDENTIFY RISKS
"What could go wrong?"
- Brainstorm with board and management
- Learn from other coops' experiences
- Consider internal and external threats
- Document everything in a RISK REGISTER

Step 2: ASSESS RISKS
"How likely? How severe?"
- Likelihood: Rare, Unlikely, Possible, Likely, Almost Certain
- Impact: Minimal, Minor, Moderate, Major, Catastrophic
- Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact

[Risk matrix visual]
                    IMPACT
           Low    Med    High
LIKELY     Med    High   Critical
POSSIBLE   Low    Med    High
UNLIKELY   Low    Low    Med

Step 3: MITIGATE RISKS
"What can we do about it?"
- Accept: Small risks, cost of mitigation exceeds benefit
- Reduce: Implement controls to lower likelihood or impact
- Transfer: Insurance, guarantees
- Avoid: Don't do risky activities

Step 4: MONITOR RISKS
"Are our mitigations working?"
- Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)
- Regular reporting to board
- Update risk register
- Learn from incidents

Step 5: REVIEW & IMPROVE
"What did we learn?"
- Annual risk framework review
- Post-incident analysis
- Continuous improvement

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's see how Hadji starts building MoroCoop's risk register..."
```

**Lesson 2.2: Understanding Credit Risk** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Credit risk has three components. Miss any one,
and your analysis is incomplete."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE THREE COMPONENTS OF CREDIT LOSS:

[Visual formula]
Expected Loss = PD x EAD x LGD

PD = Probability of Default
"What's the chance this borrower won't pay?"
- Before typhoon: MoroCoop's average was 5%
- After typhoon for affected farmers: 60-80%

EAD = Exposure at Default
"How much do they owe when they default?"
- Outstanding principal + accrued interest
- MoroCoop's affected exposure: P10,800,000

LGD = Loss Given Default
"If they default, how much do we actually lose?"
- Depends on: Collateral, guarantees, recovery efforts
- For unsecured agricultural loans: Often 70-90%

CALCULATING MoroCoop'S EXPECTED LOSS:

Scenario 1: All affected loans default, 80% loss
P10,800,000 x 100% x 80% = P8,640,000 loss

Scenario 2: 50% default, 70% loss
P10,800,000 x 50% x 70% = P3,780,000 loss

Scenario 3: With restructuring, only 20% default
P10,800,000 x 20% x 60% = P1,296,000 loss

RASHID'S REALIZATION:
"If we do nothing: P8.6M loss. If we help members restructure:
P1.3M loss. The choice is clear."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"But how do we assess each borrower and decide who to help?"
```

**Lesson 2.5: Credit Risk Mitigation Strategies** (TEXT - 8 min)

```markdown
# How MoroCoop Mitigates Credit Risk

Rashid presents mitigation strategies to the board.

## Strategy 1: Loan Restructuring

For borrowers with temporary hardship:

| Original Terms | Restructured Terms |
|----------------|-------------------|
| P100,000 loan | Same principal |
| 24 months term | 36 months term |
| P4,800/month payment | P3,200/month |
| Interest: 18% annual | Interest: 12% (temporary) |

Result: Monthly burden reduced 33%, borrower can survive

## Strategy 2: Payment Moratorium

For severely affected borrowers:

- 3-6 month payment holiday
- Interest continues to accrue (or not, board decision)
- Principal payments resume after harvest recovery
- Member must provide recovery plan

## Strategy 3: Collateral Realization

For borrowers who abandon/refuse:

| Collateral Type | Recovery Rate | Timeline |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Land title | 60-80% | 6-12 months |
| Equipment | 30-50% | 3-6 months |
| Co-maker guarantee | Variable | 1-3 months |
| Crops (destroyed) | 0% | N/A |

Problem: Most MoroCoop agricultural loans have crop liens as collateral.
With crops destroyed, collateral value = 0.

## Strategy 4: Insurance (After the Fact - Too Late!)

What MoroCoop SHOULD have had:
- Philippine Crop Insurance Corporation (PCIC) coverage
- Premium: ~3% of loan amount
- Coverage: Up to 80% of principal for typhoon damage

Rashid: "If we had required crop insurance, PCIC would
pay us P8.6M x 80% = P6.9M. We'd have minimal loss."

LESSON LEARNED: Require crop insurance for all agricultural loans.

## Strategy 5: Portfolio Diversification

MoroCoop's problem: 60% concentration in agriculture, same area.

Future targets:
| Sector | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Agriculture | 60% | 40% |
| Sari-sari/trading | 15% | 25% |
| Transport/service | 10% | 15% |
| Salary loans | 10% | 15% |
| Housing | 5% | 5% |

Diversification reduces concentration risk.

## Key Lesson

Hadji: "We were exposed because we didn't PLAN for this.
       Never again."
```
