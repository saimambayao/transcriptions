## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Interpret** cooperative financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow)
2. **Calculate** and interpret basic financial ratios
3. **Explain** the statutory fund allocation process under RA 9520
4. **Prepare** a simple cooperative budget
5. **Identify** basic internal control principles
6. **Assess** basic financial health indicators of a cooperative

**Lesson 1.2: The Balance Sheet Explained** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Maria draws a simple diagram: 'Think of a weighing scale.
On one side, what we OWN. On the other, what we OWE plus what members own.
They ALWAYS balance. That's why it's called a BALANCE sheet.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
THE BALANCE SHEET = Statement of Financial Position

LEFT SIDE (or TOP): ASSETS (What we own)
+------------------------------------------+
| CURRENT ASSETS (can become cash in 1 year)|
| - Cash on hand and in bank               |
| - Receivables (loans to members)          |
| - Inventory (products for sale)           |
| - Prepaid expenses                        |
|------------------------------------------|
| NON-CURRENT ASSETS (long-term)           |
| - Land and buildings                      |
| - Equipment                               |
| - Investments                             |
+------------------------------------------+

RIGHT SIDE (or BOTTOM): LIABILITIES + EQUITY

LIABILITIES (What we owe)
+------------------------------------------+
| CURRENT LIABILITIES (due within 1 year)  |
| - Member deposits (savings)               |
| - Accounts payable                        |
| - Short-term loans                        |
|------------------------------------------|
| LONG-TERM LIABILITIES                    |
| - Bank loans (over 1 year)               |
| - Other obligations                       |
+------------------------------------------+

MEMBER EQUITY (What members own)
+------------------------------------------+
| - Share capital                          |
| - Statutory funds (Reserve, ETF, CDF)    |
| - Undivided net surplus                  |
+------------------------------------------+

THE EQUATION:
ASSETS = LIABILITIES + MEMBER EQUITY

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
P2,800,000 = P1,200,000 + P1,600,000
```

**Lesson 1.5: The Income Statement Explained** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Amir asks: 'The balance sheet shows what we have NOW.
But how do we know if we're doing WELL?'"

CONTENT (6 min):
INCOME STATEMENT = Statement of Operations

THE STORY: Revenue minus Expenses equals Surplus (or Loss)

STRUCTURE:
+------------------------------------------------+
| REVENUE (What we earned)                       |
|   Interest income from loans                   |
|   Sales income                                 |
|   Service fees                                 |
|   Other income                                 |
|-------------------------------------------------|
| Less: COST OF SALES                            |
|   Cost of products sold                        |
|   Direct costs of services                     |
|-------------------------------------------------|
| = GROSS MARGIN                                 |
|-------------------------------------------------|
| Less: OPERATING EXPENSES                       |
|   Salaries and wages                           |
|   Utilities                                    |
|   Depreciation                                 |
|   Office supplies                              |
|   Other expenses                               |
|-------------------------------------------------|
| = NET SURPLUS (or NET LOSS)                    |
+------------------------------------------------+

COOPERATIVE vs CORPORATION:
- Corporation: Net PROFIT (for shareholders)
- Cooperative: Net SURPLUS (for members - different distribution!)

THE FLOW:
Revenue -> Gross Margin -> Net Surplus -> Distribution to Members

VISUAL: Income statement flowchart

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Let's see MoroCoop's actual income statement..."

PAUSE PROMPT: "Net Surplus is for members. Net Profit is for _____?"
```

**Lesson 1.7: The Cash Flow Statement** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Amir is confused: 'We have P300,000 net surplus but only P200,000
in cash. How can we have profit but no money?'"

CONTENT (6 min):
MARIA'S ANSWER: "Cash and profit are DIFFERENT."

EXAMPLE:
- You sold rice for P10,000 on CREDIT (not paid yet)
- Income statement shows P10,000 revenue
- But cash didn't increase!

THE CASH FLOW STATEMENT tells us:
1. Where cash CAME FROM
2. Where cash WENT TO
3. Why cash position changed

THREE SECTIONS:

1. OPERATING ACTIVITIES
   - Cash from business operations
   - Collections from members
   - Payments to suppliers
   - The CORE business cash

2. INVESTING ACTIVITIES
   - Buying/selling equipment
   - Buying/selling investments
   - Cash for GROWTH

3. FINANCING ACTIVITIES
   - New loans received
   - Loan payments made
   - New share capital collected
   - Cash from FUNDING sources

THE FORMULA:
Beginning Cash + Net Cash Flow = Ending Cash

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
P100,000 + P100,000 = P200,000 (ending cash)

VISUAL: Cash flow diagram

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's see how all three statements connect..."
```

**Lesson 2.2: Liquidity Ratios** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Member deposits are P600,000. Members can withdraw anytime.
Do we have enough cash to pay them?"

CONTENT (6 min):
LIQUIDITY = Ability to pay short-term obligations

RATIO 1: CURRENT RATIO
Formula: Current Assets / Current Liabilities

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
Current Assets = P1,650,000
Current Liabilities = P700,000
Current Ratio = P1,650,000 / P700,000 = 2.36

INTERPRETATION:
- For every P1 we owe short-term, we have P2.36 in short-term assets
- Target: Between 1.5 and 2.5
- MoroCoop: 2.36 = HEALTHY!

RATIO 2: QUICK RATIO (More conservative)
Formula: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
(P1,650,000 - P100,000) / P700,000 = 2.21
Still healthy!

WHY THIS MATTERS:
- If members withdraw savings, can we pay?
- If suppliers demand payment, are we ready?
- This is SURVIVAL test #1

VISUAL: Gauge showing MoroCoop's liquidity

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Good liquidity. But are we borrowing too much?"

PAUSE PROMPT: "If Current Ratio is below 1.0, what does it mean?"
```

**Lesson 2.4: Solvency Ratios** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Hadji asks: 'We took a P500,000 loan from Land Bank.
Are we borrowing too much?'"

CONTENT (6 min):
SOLVENCY = Long-term ability to meet all obligations

RATIO 3: DEBT-TO-EQUITY RATIO
Formula: Total Liabilities / Member Equity

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
Total Liabilities = P1,200,000
Member Equity = P1,600,000
Debt-to-Equity = P1,200,000 / P1,600,000 = 0.75

INTERPRETATION:
- For every P1 of member equity, we have P0.75 in debt
- Target: Less than 2.0 (lower is safer)
- MoroCoop: 0.75 = VERY HEALTHY!

WHAT THIS MEANS:
- Members own MORE than creditors
- Cooperative is not overleveraged
- We can handle financial stress

RATIO 4: DEBT RATIO
Formula: Total Liabilities / Total Assets

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
P1,200,000 / P2,800,000 = 0.43 or 43%

INTERPRETATION:
- 43% of our assets are funded by debt
- 57% is funded by member equity
- Target: Less than 50%
- MoroCoop: 43% = HEALTHY!

VISUAL: Debt composition chart

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"We're not over-borrowed. But are we EARNING enough?"
```

**Lesson 2.5: Profitability Ratios** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Ibrahim insists: 'Okay, we can pay bills and we're not over-borrowed.
But are we actually MAKING money efficiently?'"

CONTENT (6 min):
PROFITABILITY = How well we convert revenue into surplus

RATIO 5: NET SURPLUS MARGIN
Formula: Net Surplus / Total Revenue x 100

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
Net Surplus = P300,000
Total Revenue = P800,000
Net Surplus Margin = P300,000 / P800,000 = 37.5%

INTERPRETATION:
- For every P100 in revenue, we keep P37.50 as surplus
- Target: Above 5% (varies by type)
- MoroCoop: 37.5% = EXCELLENT!

RATIO 6: RETURN ON ASSETS (ROA)
Formula: Net Surplus / Total Assets x 100

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
P300,000 / P2,800,000 = 10.7%

INTERPRETATION:
- Our assets generate 10.7% return
- Target: Above 3%
- MoroCoop: 10.7% = VERY GOOD!

RATIO 7: RETURN ON EQUITY (ROE)
Formula: Net Surplus / Member Equity x 100

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
P300,000 / P1,600,000 = 18.75%

INTERPRETATION:
- Members' investment generates 18.75% return
- Target: Above 8%
- MoroCoop: 18.75% = EXCELLENT!

VISUAL: Profitability dashboard

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Ibrahim is impressed. Let's summarize what we learned..."
```

```text

NET SURPLUS = What's left after all expenses

THE JOURNEY:
+-----------------------------------------------+
| Total Revenue (sales, interest, fees)         |
| - Cost of goods/services                      |
| = GROSS MARGIN                                |
| - Operating expenses (salaries, rent, etc.)   |

IMPORTANT DISTINCTIONS:

SURPLUS vs CASH:
- Surplus is ACCOUNTING (earned minus spent)
- Cash is ACTUAL money in hand
- You can have surplus but no cash (tied up in receivables)

SURPLUS vs PROFIT:
- Both are revenue minus expenses
- But DISTRIBUTION is different:
  - Corporate profit: Shareholders decide
  - Cooperative surplus: Law dictates allocation

WHAT HAPPENS TO SURPLUS?
1. Statutory allocations (MANDATORY)
2. Patronage refund (to members)
3. Interest on capital (to members)
4. Optional reserves
5. Undivided surplus (retained)
```

**Lesson 3.5: Patronage Refund and Interest on Capital** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Member asks: 'I transacted P100,000 with the coop this year.
How much do I get back?' Maria explains patronage refund."

CONTENT (6 min):
AFTER MANDATORY ALLOCATIONS:
What's left = ALLOCABLE SURPLUS

TWO WAYS TO RETURN TO MEMBERS:

1. PATRONAGE REFUND (Balik-patronage)
   +------------------------------------------------------+
   | Basis: Member's transactions with the cooperative    |
   | Rate: At least 30% of allocable surplus              |
   |                                                      |
   | Example:                                             |
   | Member A purchased P100,000 worth                    |
   | Total coop sales: P1,000,000                         |
   | Member A's share: 100,000 / 1,000,000 = 10%         |
   | If patronage refund pool is P60,000:                 |
   | Member A gets: P60,000 x 10% = P6,000               |
   +------------------------------------------------------+

2. INTEREST ON SHARE CAPITAL
   +------------------------------------------------------+
   | Basis: Member's share capital contribution           |
   | Rate: Limited (not exceeding market rate)            |
   |                                                      |
   | Example:                                             |
   | Member A has P10,000 share capital                   |
   | Interest rate: 8%                                    |
   | Member A gets: P10,000 x 8% = P800                  |
   +------------------------------------------------------+

WHY PATRONAGE REFUND IS UNIQUE:
- Rewards ACTIVE participation
- Not based on how much you INVESTED
- Based on how much you USED the coop
- This is the COOPERATIVE DIFFERENCE!

DISTRIBUTION METHODS:
- Cash payment
- Credit to share capital
- Applied to outstanding loans
- Combination of above
```

```text
- Positive variance: Better than planned
- Negative variance: Worse than planned

|-----------|----------|----------|---------|

- Recurring pattern
- Unexpected changes

- Were savings intentional or accidental?
- Is the budget realistic?
- What adjustments are needed?
```

**Lesson 4.5: Internal Control Fundamentals** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Hadji asks: 'How do we know the Treasurer isn't taking money?'
Maria: 'Internal controls. They protect everyone - including me.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
INTERNAL CONTROLS = Procedures that safeguard assets and ensure accuracy

WHY CONTROLS MATTER:
- Prevent theft and fraud
- Ensure accurate records
- Protect officers from suspicion
- Build member trust

FIVE KEY CONTROL PRINCIPLES:

1. SEGREGATION OF DUTIES
   +--------------------------------------------+
   | - Person who handles cash doesn't record it|
   | - Person who approves doesn't disburse     |
   | - Creates checks and balances              |
   +--------------------------------------------+

2. AUTHORIZATION PROCEDURES
   +--------------------------------------------+
   | - Spending limits by position              |
   | - BOD approval for large amounts           |
   | - Written authorization required           |
   +--------------------------------------------+

3. PHYSICAL SAFEGUARDS
   +--------------------------------------------+
   | - Cash in locked safe                      |
   | - Checkbooks secured                       |
   | - Important documents in fireproof cabinet |
   +--------------------------------------------+

4. DOCUMENTATION
   +--------------------------------------------+
   | - Official receipts for all collections    |
   | - Vouchers for all disbursements           |
   | - Supporting documents attached            |
   +--------------------------------------------+

5. RECONCILIATION
   +--------------------------------------------+
   | - Monthly bank reconciliation              |
   | - Cash counts                              |
   | - Receivables aging review                 |
   +--------------------------------------------+

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
Maria (Treasurer) collects cash.
Amir (Secretary) records it.
Fatima (Manager) reconciles.
Three people, three checks.
```

## Appendix: Financial Terms Glossary

| Term | Filipino | Definition |
|------|----------|------------|
| Balance Sheet | Talaan ng Kalagayang Pinansyal | Statement of assets, liabilities, and equity |
| Income Statement | Talaan ng Operasyon | Statement of revenues and expenses |
| Cash Flow | Daloy ng Pera | Movement of cash in and out |
| Net Surplus | Netong Sobra | Revenue minus all expenses |
| Reserve Fund | Pondo ng Reserba | Mandatory savings for stability |
| ETF | Pondo ng Edukasyon | Fund for training activities |
| CDF | Pondo ng Komunidad | Fund for community projects |
| Patronage Refund | Balik-patronage | Member share of surplus based on transactions |
| Current Ratio | Ratio ng Kasalukuyang Likido | Liquidity measure |
| Debt-to-Equity | Ratio ng Utang sa Kapital | Leverage measure |
| Budget | Badyet | Financial plan for income and expenses |
| Variance | Pagkakaiba | Difference between budget and actual |

## Appendix: Sample Ratio Benchmarks for Micro Cooperatives

| Ratio | Formula | Target Range | Danger Zone |
|-------|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 - 2.5 | < 1.0 |
| Quick Ratio | (CA - Inventory) / CL | 1.0 - 1.5 | < 0.8 |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Liabilities / Member Equity | < 2.0 | > 3.0 |
| Net Surplus Margin | Net Surplus / Total Revenue | > 5% | Negative |
| ROA | Net Surplus / Total Assets | > 3% | < 1% |
| ROE | Net Surplus / Member Equity | > 8% | < 3% |

*Note: Benchmarks vary by cooperative type and local conditions.*
