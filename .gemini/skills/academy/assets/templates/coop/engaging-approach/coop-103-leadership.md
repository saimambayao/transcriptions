# COOP-103: Leadership and Values Formation

## Engaging Approach Design

Developing cooperative leadership qualities and ethical foundations - designed using the ENGAGE methodology for maximum learner engagement and retention.

> **Series**: COOP-100 (Basic Cooperative Course for Members)
> **Regulatory Basis**: CDA MC 2025-09 Annex A (adopted by CSEA for BARMM)
> **Methodology**: ENGAGE Framework (Experience-first, Narrative-driven, Active Learning)

## Design Philosophy

This course applies the **ENGAGE Framework**:

| Letter | Principle | How Applied |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **E** | Experience First | Leadership transition creates urgent learning need |
| **N** | Narrative Thread | Hadji announces retirement, who will lead MoroCoop next? |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Define** cooperative leadership as service to members and community
2. **Identify** core values essential for cooperative members
3. **Demonstrate** commitment to ethical behavior in cooperative activities
4. **Explain** the importance of active participation in cooperative governance
5. **Apply** cooperative values in personal and community decision-making
6. **Align** leadership principles with BARMM's Moral Governance framework

**Lesson 1.2: What is Cooperative Leadership?** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"In corporations, leaders give orders. In cooperatives, leaders SERVE.
This is the fundamental difference."

CONTENT (6 min):
COOPERATIVE LEADERSHIP IS DIFFERENT:

CORPORATE BOSS:
- Goal: Maximize shareholder profit
- Power: Comes from position
- Style: Command and control
- Success: Financial returns

COOPERATIVE LEADER:
- Goal: Serve member needs
- Power: Comes from member trust
- Style: Servant leadership
- Success: Member welfare and empowerment

THE SERVANT LEADERSHIP MODEL:
"Ang servant leader ay naglilingkod muna bago mamuno.
Hindi siya ang may pinakamataas na posisyon -
siya ang pinaka-dedicated na maglingkod."

HADJI'S EXAMPLE:
- Arrived first at meetings, left last
- Visited members' farms to understand their problems
- Never took extra compensation
- Made decisions transparently
- Mentored future leaders (like Maria and Fatima)

KEY INSIGHT:
In a cooperative, EVERY MEMBER is a potential leader.
Leadership is not about titles - it's about SERVICE.

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"But what VALUES guide a cooperative leader?
Let's see what Hadji taught his team..."

PAUSE: "Do you see leadership as service? How does this change your view?"
```

**Lesson 1.5: Hadji's Leadership Legacy** (TEXT - 8 min)

```markdown
# What Hadji Taught Us About Leadership

As news of Hadji's retirement spreads, members reflect on what
they learned from him over 8 years.

## Hadji's Leadership Principles

### 1. Transparency in Everything
"Kung may desisyon, ipaliwanag. Kung may gastos, ipakita."

Hadji made every decision visible:
- Monthly financial reports shared publicly
- BOD meeting minutes posted
- Major decisions voted in GA, never behind closed doors

### 2. Decisions Through Consultation
"Hindi ako ang may-ari ng cooperative. Tayong lahat ang may-ari."

Hadji practiced Shura (consultation):
- Major decisions brought to General Assembly
- Sought input from affected members
- Changed his mind when members showed better options

### 3. Developing Future Leaders
"Ang tunay na leader ay gumagawa ng maraming leader, hindi followers."

Hadji mentored:
- Maria - from member to Treasurer to potential Chairman
- Fatima - from bookkeeper to professional Manager
- Ibrahim - from skeptical new member to engaged participant

### 4. Leading by Example
"Kung di mo kaya gawin, wag mong i-expect sa iba."

Hadji never asked others to do what he wouldn't do:
- Paid his obligations on time
- Attended every training
- Participated in community projects personally

### 5. Knowing When to Step Aside
"Ang cooperative ay mas malaki kaysa sa isang tao."

Hadji's retirement shows:
- Leadership is not about holding power
- New voices strengthen the cooperative
- Succession is part of sustainability

## Your Leadership Reflection

Which of Hadji's principles do you admire most?
Which one could you practice in YOUR role?
```

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
```

```text
CONTENT (6 min):
THE SIX ESSENTIAL VALUES:

1. INTEGRITY AND HONESTY (Katapatan)
   "Ang sinasabi mo at ang ginagawa mo ay iisa."
   - Truthfulness in all dealings
   - No hidden agendas
   - Report problems, not hide them

2. COMMITMENT (Dedikasyon)
   "Kapag nag-commit, tumutupad."
   - Dedication to cooperative success
   - Show up even when inconvenient
   - Complete what you start

3. RESPECT (Paggalang)
   "Lahat ng miyembro ay pantay-pantay."
   - Value all members equally
   - Listen before you speak
   - Accept different opinions

4. RESPONSIBILITY (Pananagutan)
   "Huwag ituro ang sisi sa iba."
   - Own your duties
   - Pay obligations on time
   - Admit mistakes and fix them

5. CONTINUOUS LEARNING (Patuloy na Pag-aaral)
   "Ang magaling ay hindi tumitigil mag-aral."
   - Complete required trainings
   - Learn from experience
   - Share knowledge with others

6. SOLIDARITY (Pagkakaisa)
   "Sama-sama tayo, hindi magkanya-kanya."
   - Support cooperative decisions
   - Help fellow members
   - Celebrate collective success

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"These values connect directly to the ICA principles AND to our Bangsamoro heritage..."
```

```text
   - Help with community projects
```

## Appendix: Hadji's Retirement Speech (Story Resource)

```text
"Mga kasama, mga kaibigan, mga kapatid sa kooperatiba...

Walong taon na akong naglilingkod bilang inyong Chairman.
Sa walong taon na iyon, kasama ko kayong lahat sa bawat tagumpay at pagsubok.

Naalala ko noong 2016, 28 lang tayo na nagsimula.
Ngayon, 85 na tayo. At patuloy pa tayong lumalago.

Pero ang cooperative ay hindi tungkol sa isang tao.
Ang cooperative ay tungkol sa LAHAT tayo.

Kaya ngayon, inaanunsyo ko na hindi ako tatakbo muli bilang Chairman.
Panahon na para sa bagong mga boses.
Panahon na para sa bagong mga ideya.
Panahon na para sa bagong pamumuno.

Ang aking payo sa magiging kapalit ko:
Una, maglingkod muna bago mamuno.
Pangalawa, makinig bago magsalita.
Pangatlo, palaging alalahanin - ang cooperative ay para sa mga miyembro,
hindi para sa mga opisyales.

Sa bawat isa sa inyo: Kayo ay may kakayahang mamuno.
Kahit hindi kayo maging Chairman, kayo ay leader sa inyong sariling paraan.
Maglingkod kayo. Mag-participate kayo. Maging halimbawa kayo.

Ang kooperatiba ay hindi mamamatay sa aking pag-alis.
Ang kooperatiba ay lalong lalakas dahil sa inyong paglahok.

Maraming salamat sa inyong tiwala sa loob ng walong taon.
Ako ay mananatiling miyembro, at ako ay patuloy na maglilingkod -
hindi bilang Chairman, kundi bilang simpleng kasama.

Salamat, at mabuhay ang Kooperatiba Mindanao Farmers!"
```
