**Lesson 1.4: The Three Fiduciary Duties** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Ibrahim just became a director. Maria whispers:
 'Congratulations. Now you can be personally sued.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
FIDUCIARY DUTIES - Your Legal Obligations:

DUTY #1: CARE (Tungkuling Mag-ingat)
- Make informed decisions
- Attend meetings prepared
- Ask questions, seek information
- Business Judgment Rule: Good faith + due diligence = protection
- MoroCoop Example: Ibrahim reads financial reports BEFORE the meeting

DUTY #2: LOYALTY (Tungkuling Maging Tapat)
- Put cooperative's interest above personal interest
- No self-dealing or personal profit
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- MoroCoop Example: Hadji's cousin wants to be a supplier—he must DISCLOSE

DUTY #3: OBEDIENCE (Tungkuling Sumunod)
- Act within cooperative's purpose
- Follow bylaws and policies
- Comply with laws (RA 9520, CDA regulations)
- MoroCoop Example: Can't use coop funds for political campaign

VISUAL: Triangle showing three duties interconnected

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Break these duties, and you can be personally liable.
 But follow them, and you're protected."

PAUSE PROMPT: "Have you seen a potential conflict of interest
in your cooperative? How was it handled?"
```

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Amina asks: 'What exactly is my authority?'
 Let's answer that clearly."

CONTENT (6 min):
AUDIT COMMITTEE MANDATE (RA 9520 Art. 46):

COMPOSITION:
- At least 3 members
- Elected by GA (not appointed by board)
- Cannot be board members or employees
- At least one should understand accounting

FUNCTIONS:
1. INTERNAL AUDIT
   - Review financial records
   - Check internal controls
   - Verify asset safeguards
   - Test compliance with policies

2. EXTERNAL AUDIT COORDINATION
   - Recommend external auditor to GA
   - Review audit findings
   - Monitor management's response

3. REPORTING TO GA
   - Annual audit report
   - Findings and recommendations
   - Management response status

AMINA'S SITUATION:
- She HAS authority to investigate expenses
- She should NOT accuse Maria publicly
- She should document, report findings, recommend action
- The BOARD (not Audit Committee) decides discipline

BEST PRACTICE: Audit Committee Charter
- Written terms of reference
- Clear scope and authority
- Approved by GA

**Lesson 2.5: Credit and Ethics Committees** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Two more committees that are critical for
 growing cooperatives: Credit and Ethics."

CONTENT (6 min):
CREDIT COMMITTEE (for Savings & Credit Coops):

COMPOSITION:
- At least 3 members
- Elected by GA or BOD (per bylaws)
- Independent from loan staff

FUNCTIONS:
- Review and approve loan applications
- Within authority limits (e.g., up to P500k)
- Monitor portfolio quality
- Recommend policy improvements

AUTHORITY LEVELS (MoroCoop Example):
- Loan Officer: Up to P50,000
- Credit Committee: P50,001 to P500,000
- BOD: Above P500,000

---

ETHICS/MEDIATION COMMITTEE (RA 9520 Art. 48):

COMPOSITION:
- At least 3 members
- Elected by GA
- Respected, impartial members
- MoroCoop: Ustadz Abdul chairs (community elder)

FUNCTIONS:
1. Receive complaints about member/officer conduct
2. Investigate fairly (hear both sides)
3. Mediate disputes
4. Recommend sanctions to BOD

PROCESS:
Complaint -> Initial Review -> Investigation -> Hearing ->
Recommendation -> BOD Decision -> Appeal to GA

THE P150K CASE:
- If Amina finds wrongdoing, she reports to BOD
- BOD may refer to Ethics Committee for investigation
- Ethics Committee investigates, recommends
- BOD decides on action
- Maria can appeal to GA

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Committees work together—not against each other."

**Lesson 2.7: Committee Coordination** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text

 How do they coordinate effectively?"

CONTENT (6 min):
INTER-COMMITTEE COMMUNICATION:

1. REGULAR REPORTING
   - All committees report to BOD (except Audit to GA)
   - Monthly written reports
   - Quarterly joint committee meetings

2. REFERRAL SYSTEM

   [Issue identified] -> [Which committee handles?]

   - Financial irregularity -> Audit Committee
   - Loan default -> Credit Committee
   - Member complaint -> Ethics Committee
   - Training need -> E&T Committee

3. ESCALATION PATH

   Committee -> BOD -> GA (for major issues)

4. SHARED CALENDAR
   - All committee meetings on one calendar
   - Avoid scheduling conflicts
   - Ensure adequate coverage

MoroCoop'S NEW PROCESS FOR THE P150K CASE:

1. Amina (Audit) documents findings
2. Reports to BOD with evidence
3. BOD reviews, refers to Ethics if needed
4. Ethics investigates, hears Maria's side
5. Ethics recommends action
6. BOD decides
7. Maria can appeal to GA

THE RESULT:
- Process followed
- Maria's rights protected
- Truth discovered (receipts found, poor filing system)
- System improvement made

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Good committees = good governance = member trust."

Hadji doesn't know how to manage the debate. Someone moves to table the motion; another member says that's out of order. The meeting descends into confusion. Why do parliamentary rules matter, and how do they actually work?

**Lesson 3.5: Motions and Voting** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"'I move that...' These three words start the
 formal decision-making process."

CONTENT (6 min):
HOW MOTIONS WORK:

STEP 1: MOTION
Member: "I move that we increase share capital to P5,000."

STEP 2: SECOND
Another Member: "I second the motion."
(Shows at least 2 people want to discuss it)

STEP 3: CHAIR STATES MOTION
Chair: "It has been moved and seconded that we increase
       share capital to P5,000. Is there any discussion?"

STEP 4: DEBATE/DISCUSSION
- Members speak for and against
- Chair recognizes speakers
- Each member may speak once (or as rules allow)
- Stay on topic

STEP 5: VOTE
Chair: "All in favor, say 'Aye'... All opposed, say 'No'..."
Or: Show of hands, standing vote, or ballot

STEP 6: ANNOUNCE RESULT
Chair: "The ayes have it. The motion is carried."
Or: "The noes have it. The motion is defeated."

VOTING METHODS:
| Method        | When Used                    |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| Voice vote    | Simple, non-controversial    |
| Show of hands | Need clear count             |
| Standing      | Very close vote              |
| Ballot        | Elections, confidential      |

**Lesson 3.7: Handling Amendments and Special Situations** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Someone wants to change P5,000 to P3,000.
 How do we handle that properly?"

CONTENT (6 min):
AMENDMENTS - Changing a Motion:

ORIGINAL MOTION: "Increase share capital to P5,000"
AMENDMENT: "I move to amend by changing P5,000 to P3,000"

PROCESS:
1. Amendment needs second
2. Debate the amendment (not original motion)
3. Vote on amendment first
4. If amendment passes: Now debating P3,000 motion
5. If amendment fails: Back to P5,000 motion
6. Vote on main motion

---

SPECIAL MOTIONS:

1. POINT OF ORDER
   - "Point of order! The member is off-topic."
   - Chair rules immediately
   - No second needed

2. MOTION TO TABLE
   - Postpone to future meeting
   - Needs second, not debatable
   - Majority vote

3. CALL FOR QUESTION
   - "I move the previous question"
   - Ends debate, goes to vote
   - Needs 2/3 majority

4. MOTION TO ADJOURN
   - End the meeting
   - Needs second, not debatable
   - Majority vote

MoroCoop'S GA FIXED:
Back to the chaotic moment...
- Member moves to amend to P3,000
- Hadji: "Is there a second?"
- Member seconds
- Hadji: "We will now debate the amendment.
         Speak only about whether P3,000 is better than P5,000."
- Orderly debate on amendment
- Vote on amendment (passes)
- Continue debate on P3,000 motion
- Final vote (passes)
- Clean, legitimate decision!

"Good procedure = good decisions = member trust."
