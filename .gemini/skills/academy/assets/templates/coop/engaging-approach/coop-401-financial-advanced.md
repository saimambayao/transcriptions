By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

4. **Monitor** capital adequacy and liquidity requirements
5. **Identify** financial statement red flags before they become crises
6. **Calculate** stress test scenarios for board reporting

**Lesson 1.2: The PEARLS Framework** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"PEARLS isn't just a fancy acronym. It's a complete health
checkup for your cooperative's finances."

CONTENT (6 min):
[Animated breakdown with MoroCoop's actual numbers]

P - PROTECTION (Allowances and Reserves)
   "If loans don't get paid, is there money to cover losses?"
   MoroCoop's provision: P540,000 for P900,000 at-risk loans = 60%
   Standard: Should be 100% of loans 1-12 months delinquent

E - EFFECTIVE FINANCIAL STRUCTURE
   "Is money going where it should?"
   Net Loans / Total Assets = P18M / P25M = 72%
   Standard: 70-80% (MoroCoop is on target)

A - ASSET QUALITY
   "How healthy is the loan portfolio?"
   Delinquency Rate = P900,000 / P18M = 5%
   Standard: <5% (MoroCoop slightly over - warning sign!)

R - RATES OF RETURN AND COST
   "Is the coop making enough to survive?"
   Net Interest Margin = 8%
   Operating Expense Ratio = 6%
   Standard: Margin > Expenses (MoroCoop is profitable)

L - LIQUIDITY
   "Can we pay members who want to withdraw?"
   Liquid Assets / Deposits = P1.5M / P12.5M = 12%
   Standard: >15% (MoroCoop is below - DANGER!)

S - SIGNS OF GROWTH
   "Is the coop moving forward?"
   Asset growth = 22%, Member growth = 18%
   Standard: Sustainable growth matching deposits

PAUSE PROMPT: "Look at the L - Liquidity. At 12%, what's the risk?"

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Amina just spotted something. MoroCoop might not have enough cash..."
```

**Lesson 1.4: Cash Flow Analysis** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text

        Why is our liquidity so low?"

CONTENT (6 min):
[Spreadsheet demonstration with MoroCoop data]

OPERATING CASH FLOW:
+ Net Surplus: P800,000
+ Depreciation (non-cash): P50,000
- Increase in Loans: P3,000,000
+ Increase in Deposits: P2,500,000
+ Increase in Payables: P100,000
= Operating Cash Flow: P450,000

"Net surplus was P800K, but actual cash generated was only P450K.

FREE CASH FLOW:
Operating Cash Flow: P450,000
- Fixed Asset Purchases: P300,000
= Free Cash Flow: P150,000

"After buying new computers and furniture, only P150K is left."

CASH FLOW ADEQUACY:

outpacing our deposits."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
```

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Every loan in your portfolio should be recorded correctly.
Here's how to do it - and what Maria was doing wrong."

CONTENT (6 min):
[Animated ledger entries with MoroCoop examples]

LOAN DISBURSEMENT:
When MoroCoop releases a P100,000 loan to a member:

Debit: Loans Receivable    P100,000
Credit: Cash               P100,000

INTEREST ACCRUAL (Monthly):
If the loan earns 1.5% monthly:

Debit: Interest Receivable P1,500
Credit: Interest Income    P1,500

"This is called ACCRUAL accounting. You record income when
EARNED, not when RECEIVED."

PAYMENT RECEIVED:
Member pays P5,000 (P1,500 interest + P3,500 principal):

Debit: Cash               P5,000
Credit: Interest Receivable  P1,500
Credit: Loans Receivable     P3,500

BUT WHAT ABOUT MR. SANTOS?

He stopped paying. Maria's sticky note said "don't record interest."

ACCRUAL REVERSAL (for Non-Performing Loans):
After 90 days of non-payment, you MUST:
1. Stop accruing interest
2. Reverse any accrued but unpaid interest

Debit: Interest Income     P4,500 (3 months accrued)
Credit: Interest Receivable P4,500

"This is why the Income Statement must match REALITY."
```

**Lesson 4.2: Capital Adequacy Explained** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Capital is your cooperative's shock absorber. The bigger
the shock you might face, the more capital you need."

CONTENT (6 min):
WHAT IS CAPITAL ADEQUACY?

[Visual: Building with capital foundation]

Capital = What's left if you pay all debts
       = Assets - Liabilities
       = Members' Equity

For MoroCoop:
Assets: P25,000,000
Liabilities: P14,000,000 (deposits + payables)
Capital: P11,000,000

BUT NOT ALL CAPITAL IS EQUAL:

NET INSTITUTIONAL CAPITAL (NIC):
= Share Capital + Reserve Fund + Undivided Net Surplus
= P3,500,000 + P1,200,000 + P800,000
= P5,500,000

This is "permanent" capital that can absorb losses.

CAPITAL ADEQUACY RATIO (CAR):
= Net Institutional Capital / Risk-Weighted Assets

What are Risk-Weighted Assets?
Different assets have different risks:

| Asset | Amount | Risk Weight | Weighted |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Cash | P1.5M | 0% | P0 |
| Bank Deposits | P500K | 20% | P100K |
| Government Bonds | P2M | 0% | P0 |
| Member Loans | P18M | 100% | P18M |
| Fixed Assets | P3M | 100% | P3M |
| Total | P25M | | P21.1M |

MoroCoop's CAR:
= P5,500,000 / P21,100,000
= 26%

CDA REQUIREMENT: Minimum 10%
MoroCoop STATUS: 26% - MORE than adequate!

"Even if 26% of MoroCoop's risky assets went bad, the capital
would absorb it without touching member deposits."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"MoroCoop's capital is strong. But the problem right NOW is liquidity..."
```

## Appendix: Key Calculations

### Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)

```
CAR = Net Institutional Capital / Risk-Weighted Assets

Net Institutional Capital = Share Capital + Reserve Fund + Undivided Surplus

Risk-Weighted Assets:
- Cash: 0%
- Government Securities: 0%
- Bank Deposits: 20%
- Member Loans: 100%
- Fixed Assets: 100%
```

### Liquidity Ratio

```
Liquidity Ratio = Liquid Assets / (Deposits + Short-term Borrowings)

Liquid Assets = Cash + Bank Deposits + Short-term Investments

Target: >15%
```
