**Lesson 1.2: The 5 Cs of Credit** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Before you approve any loan, ask five questions. Miss ONE,
and you're inviting trouble."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE 5 Cs OF CREDIT:
[Visual: Pentagon with each C]

1. CHARACTER (Ugali)
   "Will they pay? Do they WANT to pay?"

   What to check:
   - Credit history with your coop
   - Payment behavior in the past
   - References from other members
   - Reputation in the community

   Mang Ernesto's file:
   "Credit history: NOT CHECKED" - FIRST MISTAKE

2. CAPACITY (Kakayahan)
   "Can they pay? Do they have enough income?"

   What to check:
   - Income statements (verified, not verbal)
   - Other debt obligations
   - Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

   Formula: DSCR = Net Income / (Principal + Interest Payment)
   Target: At least 1.25 (25% cushion)

   Mang Ernesto's file:
   "Income: Verbal estimate P80,000/month" - SECOND MISTAKE
   No verification!

3. CAPITAL (Puhunan)
   "Do they have skin in the game?"

   What to check:
   - Net worth (assets - liabilities)
   - Equity contribution to the project
   - Savings with the cooperative

   Target: Borrower should contribute at least 20% equity

   Mang Ernesto's file:
   "Savings with MoroCoop: P5,000" - For P500K loan? THIRD MISTAKE

4. COLLATERAL (Sangla)
   "If they don't pay, what can we take?"

   What to check:
   - Collateral value (independent appraisal)
   - Loan-to-Value ratio (should be <80%)
   - Legal enforceability

   Mang Ernesto's file:
   "Chattels mortgage on inventory - unverified" - FOURTH MISTAKE
   Inventory value? Unknown. Location? His warehouse. Easy to hide.

5. CONDITIONS (Kondisyon)
   "What's the loan for? What are the market conditions?"

   What to check:
   - Purpose of loan (productive vs consumptive)
   - Industry/sector outlook
   - Economic conditions

   Mang Ernesto's file:
   "Purpose: Working capital expansion"
   But: Trading in what? Who are customers? Competitors?
   FIFTH MISTAKE - no business analysis

RASHID'S CONCLUSION:
"Mario failed ALL five Cs. This loan was approved on
friendship, not credit analysis."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's see how to PROPERLY evaluate each C..."
```

**Lesson 1.4: Deep Dive - Character & Capacity** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Character tells you if they WANT to pay. Capacity tells you
if they CAN. You need both."

CONTENT (6 min):
EVALUATING CHARACTER:

[Visual: Character assessment checklist]

1. Credit History Review
   Internal: Check all past loans with your coop
   - Payment pattern: On time? How late? How often?
   - Previous restructurings?

   External: Check with other coops, banks (with consent)
   - Is there a pattern of default elsewhere?

2. Reference Checking
   Not just the references they provide!
   - Talk to suppliers, customers if business loan
   - Talk to employer if salary loan
   - Talk to neighbors, community leaders

3. Red Flags to Watch
   - Evasive answers about previous debt
   - References who seem rehearsed
   - Unable to explain gaps in history
   - Pressure to approve quickly ("I need it urgently!")

EVALUATING CAPACITY:

[Visual: DSCR calculation]

1. Income Verification
   For business loans:
   - Tax returns (ITR) - official but often understated
   - Bank statements - shows actual cash flow
   - Sales records - verify randomly
   - On-site visit - see actual operations

   For salary loans:
   - Certificate of Employment
   - Recent pay slips
   - Company verification call

2. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
   Example: Mang Ernesto (if properly verified)

   Monthly Net Income: P60,000 (verified, not verbal)
   Existing Monthly Obligations: P15,000
   Proposed New Payment: P32,000

   DSCR = P60,000 / (P15,000 + P32,000) = P60,000 / P47,000 = 1.28

   Target: >1.25 (This passes, barely)

   But wait - the income was verbal "P80,000"
   If reality is P40,000?
   DSCR = P40,000 / P47,000 = 0.85 (CANNOT PAY!)

3. Rule of Thumb
   Total debt payments should be <40% of net income

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Even with good character and capacity, you need protection.
That's where Capital and Collateral come in..."
```

**Lesson 1.5: Deep Dive - Capital, Collateral, Conditions** (TEXT - 8 min)

```markdown
# The Safety Net: Capital, Collateral, Conditions

Even good borrowers face unexpected problems. Capital, Collateral, and
Conditions provide your safety net.

## Capital: Skin in the Game

### Why It Matters

If a borrower has significant equity in a project, they're less likely
to walk away when things get tough.

### How to Assess

| Source | What to Check | Document |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Savings | Balance with your coop | Passbook |
| Property | Owned real estate | Tax declaration |
| Vehicles | Registered vehicles | OR/CR |
| Business Equity | Owner's capital in business | Financial statements |
| Other Assets | Investments, jewelry | Declarations |

### Minimum Standards

| Loan Type | Minimum Equity Contribution |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| Business Expansion | 30% of project cost |
| Working Capital | 20% of loan amount |
| Asset Acquisition | 20% downpayment |
| Consumption/Emergency | Co-maker or collateral |

### Mang Ernesto's Failure

Loan amount: P500,000
His MoroCoop savings: P5,000 (1%)
No equity contribution documented
No asset declaration verified

**Red flag: Why does someone with a "successful trading business"
have only P5,000 in savings?**

---

## Collateral: Your Second Way Out

### Collateral Types (Best to Worst)

| Type | Loan-to-Value | Why |
|------|---------------|-----|
| Real Estate (titled) | Up to 70% | Stable value, legal clarity |
| Vehicle (registered) | Up to 60% | Depreciates, can be hidden |
| Equipment (fixed) | Up to 50% | Hard to move, verifiable |
| Inventory/Chattels | Up to 40% | Can disappear, hard to value |
| Jewelry/Personal | Up to 30% | Must hold physically |

### Valuation Rules

1. **Independent appraisal** for real estate >P200K loan
2. **Recent market prices** for vehicles (not purchase price)
3. **Physical inspection** for all collateral
4. **Documented custody** (who holds title, where stored)

### Mang Ernesto's Failure

Collateral: "Chattels mortgage on inventory"
Questions that weren't asked:
- What inventory exactly?
- Where is it stored? (His warehouse = can disappear)
- What's it worth? (No appraisal)
- Is it insured?
- Can we actually take it if he defaults?

**Answer: MoroCoop can't find the inventory. It was "sold before the typhoon."**

---

## Conditions: Context Matters

### Business Loan Analysis

| Factor | What to Assess | Mang Ernesto |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Industry | Growing, stable, declining? | Trading - vague |
| Competition | How many competitors? | Not assessed |
| Customers | Who buys? Stable? | "Various clients" |
| Suppliers | Reliable? Terms? | Not assessed |
| Experience | How long in business? | "5 years" (unverified) |

### Economic Conditions

- Interest rate trends
- Local economic outlook
- Seasonal factors
- Known risks (typhoon season for agriculture)

### Loan Purpose Analysis

Productive loans (prefer):
- Business expansion with clear ROI
- Equipment that generates income
- Inventory for resale

Consumption loans (caution):
- Personal expenses
- Medical emergencies (repayment harder)
- Education (delayed ROI)

### Mang Ernesto's Failure

Loan purpose: "Working capital expansion"
No business plan submitted
No projection of how the P500,000 would be used
No expected ROI or cash flow improvement

**Mario approved based on: "Kilala ko siya, matagal na sa negosyo."
(I know him, he's been in business a long time.)**
```

**Lesson 1.7: The Credit Decision Process** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Good credit analysis is worthless without a good decision process.
Here's how to structure it."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE CREDIT PROCESS FLOW:

[Flowchart visual]

1. APPLICATION
   Member submits:
   - Completed application form
   - Required documents (ID, income, collateral)
   - Business plan (for business loans)

   Loan officer checks:
   - Completeness
   - Eligibility (member in good standing)
   - Within policy limits

2. INVESTIGATION
   Loan officer conducts:
   - 5 Cs analysis
   - Site visit (home and business)
   - Reference checks
   - Collateral verification

   Documents produced:
   - Credit Investigation Report
   - Collateral Appraisal Report
   - Financial Analysis Worksheet

3. EVALUATION
   Credit Officer/Committee reviews:
   - All investigation findings
   - DSCR calculation
   - Risk rating assignment
   - Recommendation

   Risk Rating Scale:
   | Rating | Description | Approval Authority |
   |--------|-------------|-------------------|
   | A | Excellent | Loan Officer (up to limit) |
   | B | Good | Credit Officer |
   | C | Acceptable | Credit Committee |
   | D | Marginal | BOD review required |
   | E | Reject | No approval |

4. APPROVAL
   Approval authority depends on:
   - Loan amount
   - Risk rating
   - Borrower type (related party = BOD always)

   MoroCoop Approval Matrix:
   | Amount | Rating A-B | Rating C | Rating D |
   |--------|-----------|----------|----------|
   | <P50K | Loan Officer | Credit Officer | Credit Committee |
   | P50K-P200K | Credit Officer | Credit Committee | BOD |
   | P200K-P500K | Credit Committee | Credit Committee | BOD |
   | >P500K | Credit Committee | BOD | BOD |

5. DOCUMENTATION
   Before disbursement, ensure:
   - Promissory note signed
   - Collateral documents executed
   - Insurance (if required) in place
   - Pre-disbursement checklist complete

6. DISBURSEMENT
   - Check or bank transfer (avoid cash for large loans)
   - Proper recording in loan system
   - Payment schedule issued to borrower
   - First payment date confirmed

MANG ERNESTO'S PROCESS FAILURE:

What should have happened:
- D or E rating based on weak 5 Cs = BOD review or reject

What actually happened:
- Mario gave "B" rating (how?)
- Approved as Credit Officer (within his limit)
- No Credit Committee review
- No BOD awareness until it became NPL

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's practice evaluating a real loan application..."
```

**Lesson 2.2: Portfolio Quality Metrics** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"You can't fix what you can't measure. Here are the metrics
that tell you if your portfolio is healthy or sick."

CONTENT (6 min):
KEY PORTFOLIO METRICS:

[Dashboard visual with gauges]

1. PORTFOLIO AT RISK (PAR)
   PAR 30 = Loans 30+ days past due / Total Portfolio
   MoroCoop: P3,600,000 / P18,000,000 = 20%
   Target: <5%
   Status: CRITICAL

   Variants:
   - PAR 1: Any late payment (early warning)
   - PAR 30: Standard measure
   - PAR 60, PAR 90: Severity measures

2. NON-PERFORMING LOAN RATIO (NPL)
   NPL Ratio = Loans 90+ days past due / Total Portfolio
   MoroCoop: P1,440,000 / P18,000,000 = 8%
   Target: <3%
   Status: HIGH RISK

3. CURRENT PORTFOLIO RATE
   Current = On-time loans / Total Portfolio
   MoroCoop: P14,400,000 / P18,000,000 = 80%
   Target: >95%
   Status: POOR

4. COLLECTION RATE
   Collection Rate = Amounts collected / Amounts due
   MoroCoop last month: P1,200,000 / P1,400,000 = 86%
   Target: >97%
   Status: BELOW TARGET

5. PROVISION COVERAGE RATIO
   Provision / PAR 30
   MoroCoop: P540,000 / P3,600,000 = 15%
   Target: >100% of NPL, 35% of PAR 30
   Status: SEVERELY UNDERFUNDED

6. WRITE-OFF RATIO
```

**Lesson 3.5: Collection Techniques** (TEXT - 8 min)

```markdown
# How to Actually Collect: Scripts and Techniques

## The Psychology of Avoidance

Why does Mang Tomas avoid Rashid's calls?

1. **Shame** - He's embarrassed he can't pay
2. **Overwhelm** - The problem feels too big to face
3. **Fear** - What will the cooperative do?
4. **Hope** - Maybe if he ignores it, it will go away

**Understanding this helps you connect, not just demand.**

---

## Collection Conversation Techniques

### Opening: Build Connection, Not Confrontation

Bad opening:
"Mang Tomas, you owe us P85,000 and you're not paying.
When are you going to pay?"

Good opening:
"Mang Tomas, salamat po at nakausap kita. Alam ko mahirap
ang sitwasyon mo ngayon. Gusto ko pong malaman kung ano
ang nangyayari at kung paano namin kayo matutulungan."

(Translation: Thank you for talking to me. I know your situation
is difficult. I want to understand what's happening and how
we can help you.)

### Middle: Understand Before Solving

Ask open questions:
- "Paano po ang inyong negosyo ngayon?" (How is your business now?)
- "Ano po ang nangyari sa farm?" (What happened to the farm?)
- "May iba po ba kayong pinagkukunan ng income?" (Do you have other income sources?)
- "Ano po ang kaya ninyong bayaran ngayon?" (What can you afford to pay now?)

Listen more than you talk. Take notes.

### Addressing Avoidance Directly

"Mang Tomas, napansin ko pong hindi po kayo sumasagot sa calls ko.
Nauunawaan ko na mahirap po talaga ang sitwasyon. Pero pag hindi
po tayo nag-uusap, hindi namin kayo matutulungan. At lalaki lang
po ang problema."

(I noticed you haven't been answering my calls. I understand the
situation is really difficult. But if we don't talk, we can't help
you. And the problem will only get bigger.)

### Getting a Commitment

Don't leave without a specific agreement:

Vague: "I'll try to pay next month."
Specific: "I will pay P5,000 on April 15. I will come to the office
          at 2pm. If I can't make it, I will call Rashid by April 14."

Write it down. Have them sign. Follow up if they don't keep it.

---

## The Restructuring Conversation

For Mang Tomas (genuine hardship + jeepney income):

RASHID: "Mang Tomas, nakita ko po na wala na talaga ang farm ninyo.
        Pero may jeepney po kayo na kumikita. Tama ba?"

TOMAS: "Opo, mga P15,000 po kada buwan."

RASHID: "Ayaw po naming idagdag sa problema ninyo. Pero kailangan
        din namin protektahan ang savings ng ibang members.
        Paano kung ganito: imbes na P7,000 monthly na due ninyo,
        gawing P3,000 muna hanggang ma-recover ang farm?
        Extend namin ng 12 months ang term."

TOMAS: "Kaya ko po ba talaga yun?"

RASHID: "P15,000 income, P3,000 payment. May P12,000 po para sa
        pamilya. At hindi kayo magiging delinquent. Hindi natin
        kailangang mag-legal action. Ayaw namin nun."

TOMAS: "...Sige po. Pero ano kung hindi ko kayang mag-P3,000?"

RASHID: "Kung may buwan na hindi kaya, tawagan ninyo ako BAGO
        ang due date. Maghahanap tayo ng paraan. Ang importante,
        huwag po kayong magtatago."

Don't restructure if:
- Borrower is clearly able to pay but choosing not to
- Borrower has history of breaking restructuring agreements
- No viable repayment source exists
- Borrower is uncooperative or dishonest

For these cases: proceed to legal collection.
```

## Appendix: 5 Cs Quick Reference

| C | Question | What to Check | Red Flags |
|---|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Character** | Will they pay? | Credit history, references | Evasive, bad history |
| **Capacity** | Can they pay? | Income, DSCR | DSCR <1.25, unstable income |
| **Capital** | Do they have stake? | Net worth, equity | No savings, no contribution |
| **Collateral** | What's the security? | Appraisal, LTV | Unreliable, low value |
| **Conditions** | What's the context? | Purpose, market | Poor outlook, unclear use |
