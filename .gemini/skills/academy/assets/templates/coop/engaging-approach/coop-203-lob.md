# COOP-203: Line of Business Training (Micro)

## Engaging Approach Design

Specialized training based on cooperative type and primary line of business - designed using the ENGAGE methodology for maximum learner engagement and retention.

> **Series**: COOP-200 (Advanced Course for Micro Cooperative Officers)
> **Regulatory Basis**: CDA MC 2025-09 Annex A (adopted by CSEA for BARMM)
> **Methodology**: ENGAGE Framework (Experience-first, Narrative-driven, Active Learning)

## Design Philosophy

This course applies the **ENGAGE Framework**:

| Letter | Principle | How Applied |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **E** | Experience First | Each track opens with business scenario before explanation |
| **N** | Narrative Thread | MoroCoop considers adding new lines of business |
| **G** | Guided Practice | Watch-Pause-Try pattern with business decisions |
| **A** | Active Processing | Apply concepts to YOUR cooperative's LOB every 5-7 min |

| **E** | Extend Beyond | Create action plans for your specific business |

**Lesson A1.4: Setting Interest Rates** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Amir asks: 'Can we just offer 10% interest on savings?
That will surely attract deposits!' Maria laughs."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE INTEREST RATE PUZZLE:

Savings Rate vs Lending Rate = SPREAD (your income)

Example:
- Savings interest paid: 3%
- Lending interest earned: 12%
- Spread: 9% (gross income before expenses)

IF SAVINGS RATE IS TOO HIGH:
- Spread shrinks
- Coop may not cover expenses
- Not sustainable long-term

IF SAVINGS RATE IS TOO LOW:
- Members won't deposit
- Funds won't grow
- Can't expand lending

THE BALANCING ACT:

Step 1: Calculate your cost of funds
        (Total interest paid / Total deposits)

Step 2: Calculate your lending yield
        (Total interest earned / Total loans)

Step 3: Calculate spread
        (Yield - Cost)

Step 4: Ensure spread covers:
        - Operating expenses
        - Loan loss provisions
        - Reserve requirements
        - Desired surplus

MoroCoop CALCULATION:
- Current cost of funds: 2.5%
- Lending yield: 12%
- Spread: 9.5%
- Operating expense ratio: 6%
- Available for provisions/surplus: 3.5%

VISUAL: Spread calculation diagram

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Let's practice creating a savings product..."
```

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Five letters that can save your cooperative from bad loans."

THE 5Cs OF CREDIT:

1. CHARACTER (Ugali)
   +----------------------------------------------------+
   | - Track record with the cooperative                |
   | - Repayment history                                |
   | - Reputation in the community                      |
   | - Honesty and reliability                          |
   | Question: Is this person trustworthy?              |
   +----------------------------------------------------+

   +----------------------------------------------------+
   | - Income sources                                   |
   | - Existing obligations                             |

   +----------------------------------------------------+

   +----------------------------------------------------+
   | - Member's equity/savings                          |
   | - Other assets owned                               |
   | - Investment in the project being financed         |

   +----------------------------------------------------+

4. COLLATERAL (Garantiya)
   +----------------------------------------------------+
   | - Assets pledged as security                       |
   | - Co-makers/guarantors                             |
   | - Value vs loan amount                             |
   | Question: If they don't pay, can we recover?       |
   +----------------------------------------------------+

5. CONDITION (Kalagayan)
   +----------------------------------------------------+
   | - Economic environment                             |
   | - Purpose of loan                                  |
   | - Industry/sector conditions                       |
   | Question: Are external factors favorable?          |
   +----------------------------------------------------+
```

**Lesson A2.3: Capacity Analysis** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"The best character and collateral don't help if the member
simply CANNOT AFFORD to pay."

CONTENT (6 min):
CAPACITY = Ability to repay from regular income

THE ANALYSIS:

Step 1: IDENTIFY INCOME SOURCES
        - Salary: P15,000/month
        - Farm income: P8,000/month (average)
        - Spouse income: P10,000/month
        - TOTAL: P33,000/month

Step 2: IDENTIFY EXISTING OBLIGATIONS
        - Existing coop loan: P3,000/month
        - Store credit: P2,000/month
        - TOTAL EXISTING: P5,000/month

Step 3: CALCULATE DISPOSABLE INCOME
        Total Income: P33,000
        Less Existing Debts: (P5,000)
        Less Living Expenses: (P18,000) estimated
        DISPOSABLE: P10,000/month

Step 4: CALCULATE PROPOSED PAYMENT
        Loan: P150,000
        Term: 24 months
        Interest: 12% p.a.
        Monthly Payment: Approximately P7,000

Step 5: COMPARE
        Disposable Income: P10,000
        Proposed Payment: P7,000
        SURPLUS: P3,000 (buffer for emergencies)

DEBT SERVICE RATIO:
Total Debt Payments / Total Income = DSR
(P5,000 + P7,000) / P33,000 = 36%

TARGET: DSR should be below 40%
RASHID: 36% = ACCEPTABLE

VISUAL: Income-expense calculation

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Let's practice loan evaluation..."
```

**Lesson A4.4: Loan Loss Provisioning** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Not all loans will be repaid. Smart cooperatives EXPECT losses
and set aside money for them."

CONTENT (6 min):
LOAN LOSS PROVISION = Money set aside for expected losses

WHY PROVISION?
- Some loans WILL go bad
- Better to recognize slowly than sudden shock
- Matching principle (recognize loss when incurred)
- Regulatory requirement

PROVISIONING RATES (Typical):

+----------------------------------------------------------+
| Loan Status              | Provision Rate                |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Current                  | 1%                            |
| 1-30 days past due       | 5%                            |
| 31-60 days past due      | 25%                           |
| 61-90 days past due      | 50%                           |
| 91-180 days past due     | 75%                           |
| Over 180 days            | 100%                          |
+----------------------------------------------------------+

MoroCoop EXAMPLE:
+----------------------------------------------------------+
| Category      | Balance    | Rate  | Provision            |
|---------------|------------|-------|----------------------|
| Current       | P1,800,000 | 1%    | P18,000              |
| 1-30 days     | P100,000   | 5%    | P5,000               |
| 31-60 days    | P60,000    | 25%   | P15,000              |
| 61-90 days    | P40,000    | 50%   | P20,000              |
| TOTAL REQUIRED|            |       | P58,000              |
+----------------------------------------------------------+

Current Allowance for Loan Losses: P50,000
Additional Provision Needed: P8,000
(This reduces net surplus)

WHERE DOES PROVISION COME FROM?
- Charged against income (reduces surplus)
- Accumulated in "Allowance for Loan Losses"
- Shows as NEGATIVE on Balance Sheet (reduces receivables)
```
