**Lesson 1.2: The Accounting Equation** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"All of accounting—every report, every number—
 comes from ONE simple equation."

CONTENT (6 min):
THE FOUNDATION:

    ASSETS = LIABILITIES + EQUITY

    "What you HAVE = What you OWE + What you OWN"

BREAKING IT DOWN:

ASSETS (Mga Ari-arian):
- What the cooperative HAS or OWNS
- Cash in bank: P500,000
- Loans receivable: P5,100,000
- Equipment: P800,000
- Building: P1,200,000

LIABILITIES (Mga Utang):
- What the cooperative OWES to others
- Member deposits: P3,200,000
- Loans payable: P1,000,000
- Accounts payable: P250,000

EQUITY (Kapital ng mga Miyembro):
- What BELONGS to members
- Share capital: P1,800,000
- Retained surplus: P1,350,000

THE EQUATION IN ACTION (MoroCoop):
P8,500,000 = P4,450,000 + P4,050,000
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
 CHECK!

WHY THIS MATTERS:
- EVERY transaction affects this equation
- It must ALWAYS balance
- If it doesn't balance, something is wrong

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's see how individual transactions affect this equation..."

PAUSE PROMPT: "What are the main ASSETS of your cooperative?"
```

**Lesson 1.4: How Transactions Work** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Every time money moves, the equation changes—
 but it ALWAYS stays balanced."

CONTENT (6 min):
TRANSACTION EXAMPLES:

TRANSACTION 1: Member Ibrahim deposits P10,000 savings
BEFORE: Assets P8,500,000 = Liabilities P4,450,000 + Equity P4,050,000
CHANGE: +P10,000 Cash (Asset) | +P10,000 Member Deposits (Liability)
AFTER:  Assets P8,510,000 = Liabilities P4,460,000 + Equity P4,050,000
         STILL BALANCED!

---

TRANSACTION 2: Cooperative grants P50,000 loan to member
BEFORE: Cash P500,000 | Loans Receivable P5,100,000
CHANGE: -P50,000 Cash | +P50,000 Loans Receivable
AFTER:  Cash P450,000 | Loans Receivable P5,150,000
         Total Assets unchanged—just moved from one asset to another!

---

TRANSACTION 3: Cooperative earns P5,000 interest
BEFORE: Cash P450,000 | Equity P4,050,000
CHANGE: +P5,000 Cash (Asset) | +P5,000 Surplus (Equity)
AFTER:  Cash P455,000 | Equity P4,055,000
         Assets increased, Equity increased—BALANCED!

---

TRANSACTION 4: Cooperative pays P3,000 electricity bill
BEFORE: Cash P455,000 | Equity P4,055,000
CHANGE: -P3,000 Cash (Asset) | -P3,000 Surplus (Equity - expense)
AFTER:  Cash P452,000 | Equity P4,052,000
         Assets decreased, Equity decreased—BALANCED!

THE KEY INSIGHT:
- Income INCREASES equity
- Expenses DECREASE equity
- That's why we track them separately in the Income Statement!

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now you know why the Balance Sheet and Income Statement are connected."
```

**Lesson 1.5: The Three Financial Statements** (TEXT - 8 min)

```text
# Understanding the Three Financial Statements

## 1. Balance Sheet (Statement of Financial Position)
"A snapshot of what we HAVE, OWE, and OWN at a specific date"

Structure:
+----------------------------------+
|           ASSETS                 |
| Current Assets (cash, receivables)|
| Non-Current Assets (property)    |
+----------------------------------+
|         LIABILITIES              |
| Current (due within 1 year)      |
| Non-Current (due after 1 year)   |
+----------------------------------+
|           EQUITY                 |
| Share Capital                    |
| Statutory Funds                  |
| Retained Surplus                 |
+----------------------------------+

## 2. Income Statement (Statement of Comprehensive Income)
"A record of what we EARNED and SPENT over a period"

Structure:
+----------------------------------+
| REVENUES (interest, fees, sales) |
| - EXPENSES (salaries, utilities) |
| = NET SURPLUS (or DEFICIT)       |
+----------------------------------+

MoroCoop Example (2024):
Revenue:        P1,250,000
Expenses:       (P980,000)
Net Surplus:     P270,000

## 3. Cash Flow Statement
"A record of how CASH moved in and out"

Three Sections:
1. Operating Activities (from main business)
2. Investing Activities (buying/selling assets)
3. Financing Activities (borrowing, share capital)

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT FROM INCOME STATEMENT:
- You can be PROFITABLE but have NO CASH
- Example: You sold P100,000 but customer hasn't paid yet
  - Income Statement: +P100,000 revenue
  - Cash Flow: P0 cash received

## THE CONNECTION

Balance Sheet (Dec 31, 2023)
        |
        + Income Statement (2024) = Changes in Equity
        |
        v
Balance Sheet (Dec 31, 2024)

Cash Flow Statement explains the change in CASH
from one Balance Sheet to the next.
```

**Lesson 1.7: Cooperative-Specific Accounts** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Cooperative accounting has UNIQUE accounts that
 corporations don't have. Know these!"

CONTENT (6 min):
UNIQUE COOPERATIVE ACCOUNTS:

1. SHARE CAPITAL
   - Member contributions (not investor shares)
   - Common shares: Everyone gets the same
   - Preferred shares: Optional, with fixed dividend
   - MoroCoop: 450 members x P4,000 average = P1,800,000

2. MEMBER DEPOSITS (Liability)
   - Savings deposits: Members' savings
   - Time deposits: Fixed term savings
   - NOT the coop's money—members can withdraw
   - MoroCoop: P3,200,000 in member deposits

3. LOANS RECEIVABLE (Asset)
   - Money lent to members
   - Current: Due within 1 year
   - Non-current: Due after 1 year
   - MoroCoop: P5,100,000 in outstanding loans

4. STATUTORY RESERVES (Equity)
   - Required by law (RA 9520 Art. 86):
   - Reserve Fund: 10% of net surplus (cannot be distributed)
   - Education & Training Fund: 10%
   - Community Development Fund: 3%
   - Optional Fund: 7% (coop decides)

5. NET SURPLUS (not Profit!)
   - Cooperatives earn SURPLUS, not profit
   - Distributed as PATRONAGE REFUND
   - Based on member transactions, not shares

6. INTEREST ON SHARE CAPITAL
   - Limited dividend on shares (Art. 85)
   - Must not exceed market rate
   - Optional—coop decides

VISUAL: Chart of accounts specific to cooperatives

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's apply this to real financial statements..."
```

**Lesson 2.2: Liquidity Ratios** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Can your cooperative pay its bills TOMORROW?
 Liquidity ratios answer this question."

CONTENT (6 min):
LIQUIDITY = Ability to meet SHORT-TERM obligations

RATIO 1: CURRENT RATIO
Formula: Current Assets / Current Liabilities

MoroCoop Calculation:
Current Assets: P4,950,000
Current Liabilities: P3,550,000
Current Ratio = 4,950,000 / 3,550,000 = 1.39

Interpretation:
- 1.39 means: For every P1 due, we have P1.39
- Target: 1.5 to 2.5 is healthy
- Below 1.0: Warning! Can't pay obligations
- MoroCoop: Acceptable but could be stronger

---

RATIO 2: QUICK RATIO (Acid Test)
Formula: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

Why exclude inventory? Can't always sell it quickly.

MoroCoop Calculation:
(4,950,000 - 180,000) / 3,550,000 = 1.34

Interpretation:
- Target: 1.0 to 1.5
- MoroCoop: 1.34 is good

---

SPECIAL FOR COOPS: LIQUIDITY RATIO
Formula: (Cash + Short-term Investments) / Member Deposits

MoroCoop Calculation:
520,000 / 3,200,000 = 0.16 or 16%

Interpretation:
- Can you handle withdrawal requests?
- 15-20% is generally acceptable
- MoroCoop: 16% is adequate

VISUAL: Gauge showing healthy vs concerning ranges

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Now let's look at SOLVENCY—the long-term picture..."

PAUSE PROMPT: "Calculate your cooperative's current ratio."
```

**Lesson 2.4: Solvency Ratios** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Liquidity is about TOMORROW. Solvency is about SURVIVAL.
 How much debt is too much?"

CONTENT (6 min):
SOLVENCY = Ability to meet LONG-TERM obligations

RATIO 1: DEBT-TO-EQUITY RATIO
Formula: Total Liabilities / Total Equity

MoroCoop Calculation:
Total Liabilities: P4,450,000
Total Equity: P4,050,000
Debt-to-Equity = 4,450,000 / 4,050,000 = 1.10

Interpretation:
- 1.10 means: P1.10 in debt for every P1 in equity
- Target: Below 2.0 for most coops
- Higher = More risk, but also more leverage
- MoroCoop: Acceptable, but don't increase debt much more

---

RATIO 2: DEBT-TO-ASSETS RATIO
Formula: Total Liabilities / Total Assets

MoroCoop Calculation:
4,450,000 / 8,500,000 = 0.52 or 52%

Interpretation:
- 52% of assets are funded by debt
- 48% funded by equity (member capital)
- Target: Below 60%
- MoroCoop: Good position

---

RATIO 3: EQUITY-TO-ASSETS RATIO
Formula: Total Equity / Total Assets

MoroCoop Calculation:
4,050,000 / 8,500,000 = 0.48 or 48%

Interpretation:
- Members own 48% of assets
- Higher = More financial cushion
- Target: Above 40%
- MoroCoop: Solid

WHY IT MATTERS:
- High debt = High risk if revenue drops
- But some debt is OK—it's leverage
- Key is BALANCE

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Next: Are we making money efficiently?"
```

```text
Formula: Loans Overdue > 30 days / Total Loan Portfolio x 100

MoroCoop Calculation:
Loans overdue > 30 days: P612,000
Total portfolio: P5,100,000
PAR = 612,000 / 5,100,000 x 100 = 12%

Interpretation:
- 12% of loans have missed payments
- Target: Below 5%
- MoroCoop: CONCERNING! Need collection action

BREAKDOWN:
- PAR 30: Loans 31-60 days overdue (early warning)
- PAR 60: Loans 61-90 days overdue (serious)
- PAR 90: Loans 91+ days overdue (critical)

---

RATIO 2: LOAN LOSS PROVISION RATIO
Formula: Loan Loss Provision / Total Portfolio x 100

Why provision? Set aside money for expected losses

MoroCoop Calculation:
Provision: P255,000
255,000 / 5,100,000 x 100 = 5%

Interpretation:
- We've set aside 5% for potential losses
- Should match or exceed PAR
- MoroCoop: Provision (5%) < PAR (12%) = UNDER-PROVISIONED!

---

RATIO 3: DELINQUENCY RATE
Formula: Amount Overdue / Amount Due x 100

Different from PAR—focuses on amounts due, not total portfolio

---

WHAT MoroCoop SHOULD DO:
1. Increase loan loss provision immediately
2. Intensify collection efforts
3. Review lending policies
4. Report honestly to GA
```

## Appendix: Financial Ratios Quick Reference

| Category | Ratio | Formula | Target |
|----------|-------|---------|--------|
| **Liquidity** | Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 - 2.5 |
| **Liquidity** | Quick Ratio | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | 1.0 - 1.5 |
| **Solvency** | Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Member Equity | < 2.0 |
| **Solvency** | Debt-to-Assets | Total Debt / Total Assets | < 0.6 |
| **Profitability** | Net Surplus Margin | Net Surplus / Revenue | > 5% |
| **Profitability** | ROA | Net Surplus / Total Assets | > 3% |
| **Profitability** | ROE | Net Surplus / Member Equity | > 8% |
| **Portfolio** | PAR | Loans > 30 days / Total Portfolio | < 5% |
