# PRS: Pre-Registration Seminar

## Engaging Approach Design

CDA/CSEA-mandated orientation for prospective cooperative organizers before registration--designed using the ENGAGE methodology as the ORIGIN STORY of cooperative formation.

> **Regulatory Basis**: CDA MC 2025-11 (3h 45m curriculum, adopted by CSEA for BARMM)
> **Methodology**: ENGAGE Framework (Experience-first, Narrative-driven, Active Learning)
> **Story Arc**: The PREQUEL - How MoroCoop Was Born

## Design Philosophy

This seminar applies the **ENGAGE Framework**:

| Letter | Principle | How Applied |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **E** | Experience First | Opens with the founding problem, not lecture |
| **N** | Narrative Thread | Follow Ibrahim, Maria, Hadji, Fatima's founding journey |
| **G** | Guided Practice | Decision points: "Should they form a coop?" |
| **A** | Active Processing | Activity every 5-7 minutes |
| **G** | Group Learning | Table discussions, peer decisions |
| **E** | Extend Beyond | "Would you join this coop?" personal application |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this seminar, participants will be able to:

1. **Define** what a cooperative is and its distinguishing characteristics
2. **Explain** the 7 Cooperative Principles and core values
3. **Identify** the type of cooperative appropriate for their needs
4. **List** the requirements for cooperative registration
5. **Describe** the rights and responsibilities of cooperative members
6. **Understand** the basic organizational structure of a cooperative
7. **Decide** whether to proceed with cooperative formation

## Module 1: The Problem (30 min)

### Story Arc: "Why Are We Struggling Alone?"

> **Opening Scene**: The four community members stay after the meeting. They start talking. Ibrahim says: "Why does the trader get rich while we stay poor?" Maria adds: "And why do I keep paying the 5-6 lender forever?" Hadji responds: "Alone, we have no power. The question is--what do we do about it?"

### Lesson 1.1: Ibrahim's Frustration (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Bakit yumayaman ang trader, tayo naman patuloy na mahirap?"

Ibrahim slams his hand on the table. The others listen.

CONTENT (6 min):
IBRAHIM'S STORY:
- Harvests 100 cavans of rice per season
- Trader offers PHP 18/kilo. "Take it or leave it."
- In the market, same rice sells for PHP 45/kilo.
- "Who keeps the PHP 27 difference? Not me."

THE MATH OF POWERLESSNESS:
- Ibrahim's gross: 100 cavans x 50 kg x PHP 18 = PHP 90,000
- Market value: 100 cavans x 50 kg x PHP 45 = PHP 225,000
- Lost opportunity: PHP 135,000 per season
- "Paano magkakapera kung 60% ng dapat ko, sa iba napupunta?"

MARIA'S REALIZATION:
"Same with me and the 5-6 lender. Alone, I have no choice."

HADJI'S WISDOM:
"My grandfather used to say: Isang kahoy, madaling baliin.
Sampu, hindi mo kaya.
One stick breaks easily. Ten together? Unbreakable."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"But how do we become the 'ten sticks'?
What kind of organization gives us collective power?"

PAUSE PROMPT: "Have you felt like Ibrahim? Powerless against unfair prices?"
```

### Lesson 1.3: What is a Cooperative? (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"The barangay captain returns with a pamphlet from CSEA.
'Let me explain what a cooperative really is...'"

CONTENT (6 min):
THE OFFICIAL DEFINITION (ICA):
"An autonomous association of persons united voluntarily
to meet their common economic, social, and cultural needs
through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise."

[Break it down for Ibrahim:]

"Autonomous" - WE govern ourselves
  Ibrahim: "No outside investor tells us what to do?"
  Captain: "Correct."

"Persons united voluntarily" - We CHOOSE to join
  Maria: "No one forces me?"
  Captain: "It's your choice. In and out."

"Common needs" - We share the same problems
  Hadji: "Like all of us needing fair prices?"
  Captain: "Exactly."

"Jointly-owned" - WE own it together
  Fatima: "Not one person owns it?"
  Captain: "You ALL own it. Equally."

"Democratically-controlled" - One member, one vote
  Ibrahim: "So Hadji's vote equals mine? Even if he has more land?"
  Captain: "Exactly. Your VOICE, not your wealth."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Ibrahim is intrigued. Maria is hopeful. Hadji is thinking.
Fatima is excited. But how is this DIFFERENT from a corporation?"

VISUAL: The definition animated, each word highlighted
```

### Lesson 1.4: The Cooperative Difference (VIDEO - 5 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Ibrahim's brother-in-law runs a small corporation.
'Why not just do that?' Ibrahim asks."

CONTENT (4 min):
SIDE BY SIDE COMPARISON:

Corporation vs Cooperative

| Your Brother-in-Law's Corp | The Cooperative You'd Form |
|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| Shareholders own it       | MEMBERS own it            |
| Votes by shares owned     | One member, ONE vote      |
| Purpose: Profit for investors | Purpose: Serve member needs |
| Surplus: Dividends by shares | Surplus: Patronage by use |
| Control: Big investors decide | Control: Members decide   |

THE KEY INSIGHT:
Ibrahim: "Sa corporation, pera ang boto. Sa cooperative, tao ang boto."

Maria: "So even if Hadji invests more, his vote equals mine?"
Captain: "Yes. That's democracy."

Hadji: "This is like our Islamic value of shura--consultation.
       Every voice matters equally."
```

### Lesson 2.2: The 7 Principles (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Fatima shows her phone: 'The International Cooperative Alliance
turned these values into 7 Principles. Rules for how cooperatives
must operate.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
PRINCIPLE 1: Voluntary and Open Membership
- Anyone can join (if they meet requirements)
- No discrimination: religion, gender, status
- Ibrahim: "So my Christian neighbor can join?"
  Captain: "Yes. Cooperatives don't discriminate."

PRINCIPLE 2: Democratic Member Control
- One member, one vote
- Officers elected by members
- Maria: "Even the chairman can be voted out?"
  Captain: "Yes. By the members. That's democracy."

PRINCIPLE 3: Member Economic Participation
- Members contribute capital fairly
- Limited return on investment
- Surplus based on transactions (patronage)
- Ibrahim: "So if I sell more through the coop, I get more back?"
  Captain: "Exactly. Based on your PATRONAGE, not your investment."

PRINCIPLE 4: Autonomy and Independence
- Self-governing, even with government support
- Hadji: "Government helps but doesn't control?"
  Captain: "Correct. YOU control your cooperative."

[continued in next video]

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Four principles down. Three more to go.
And one of them explains why YOU are here today..."
```

## Module 4: The Commitment (35 min)

### Story Arc: "What Does It Take?"

> **Opening Scene**: After the break. The four farmers are excited but Hadji brings them back to reality. "Before we go further, we need to know: What will this REQUIRE from us? Rights are nice. But what about RESPONSIBILITIES?"

### Lesson 4.1: Hadji's Reality Check (VIDEO - 5 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Sandali muna," Hadji raises his hand.
"Before we get too excited, let's be realistic.
What will this REQUIRE from each of us?"

CONTENT (4 min):
HADJI'S CONCERN:
"I've seen groups start excited and fail within a year.
Why? Because nobody talked about RESPONSIBILITIES.
Only rights."

THE TWO SIDES:

RIGHTS (what you GET):
- Right to vote and be voted
- Right to patronage refund
- Right to information
- Right to participate
- Right to due process

RESPONSIBILITIES (what you GIVE):
- Patronize the cooperative (USE its services)
- Pay your share capital
```

## Facilitator Guide

### Qualifications

- CDA/CSEA-accredited Training Service Provider (TSP)
- Or CSEA Extension Office staff
- Knowledge of RA 9520 and cooperative principles
- Facilitation skills (discussion-based, not lecture-only)

### Preparation

**1 Week Before**:
- [ ] Confirm venue and participants
- [ ] Prepare all materials (handouts, certificates)
- [ ] Review video scripts for facilitation

**Day Before**:
- [ ] Set up venue with table groups (not rows)
- [ ] Test AV equipment if using videos
- [ ] Prepare registration area

### Facilitation Tips

1. **Story-first**: Start each module with the character scenario
2. **Interactive**: Ask questions, encourage discussion
3. **Table groups**: Seat participants in groups of 4-6 for activities
4. **Use local language**: Translate key terms as needed
5. **Be honest**: Cooperatives require commitment--filter out those not ready
6. **Stay neutral**: Inform, don't recruit
7. **Decision points**: Give real time for reflection, not rushed
