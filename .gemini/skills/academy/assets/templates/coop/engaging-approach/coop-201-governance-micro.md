# COOP-201: Cooperative Management and Governance (Micro)

## Engaging Approach Design

Management and governance fundamentals for officers of micro cooperatives - designed using the ENGAGE methodology for maximum learner engagement and retention.

> **Series**: COOP-200 (Advanced Course for Micro Cooperative Officers)
> **Regulatory Basis**: CDA MC 2025-09 Annex A (adopted by CSEA for BARMM)
> **Methodology**: ENGAGE Framework (Experience-first, Narrative-driven, Active Learning)

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Explain** the roles and responsibilities of BOD members, officers, and committees
2. **Apply** parliamentary procedure basics in cooperative meetings
3. **Formulate** cooperative policies aligned with RA 9520 and CDA regulations
4. **Conduct** effective General Assembly meetings
5. **Demonstrate** ethical decision-making in cooperative governance
6. **Comply** with CDA/CSEA reporting and regulatory requirements

**Lesson 1.4: The Three Duties of a Director** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Hadji tells the board: 'We have THREE sacred duties.
Break any one, and we fail our members.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
THE FIDUCIARY DUTIES:

1. DUTY OF CARE (Tungkulin ng Pag-iingat)
   - Make informed decisions
   - Attend meetings, read documents
   - Ask questions before voting
   - Amir's lesson: "I can't just raise my hand without understanding"

2. DUTY OF LOYALTY (Tungkulin ng Katapatan)
   - Put cooperative interest above personal interest
   - No self-dealing
   - Disclose conflicts of interest
   - Ibrahim's test: His brother wants a loan - what should he do?

3. DUTY OF OBEDIENCE (Tungkulin ng Pagsunod)
   - Follow the bylaws
   - Comply with RA 9520
   - Respect GA decisions
   - Maria's principle: "The GA is supreme, not us"

VISUAL: Three pillars holding up "Good Governance"

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"These duties apply to ALL directors. But what about specific ROLES?"
```

```text
THE FIVE MANDATORY COMMITTEES:

1. AUDIT COMMITTEE (Art. 46)
   - Internal financial oversight
   - Reviews financial statements
   - Not the same as external auditor

2. ELECTION COMMITTEE (Art. 47)
   - Conducts fair elections
   - Manages nominations
   - Counts votes

3. MEDIATION/CONCILIATION COMMITTEE (Art. 48)
   - Resolves member disputes
   - First step before external action
   - Maintains harmony

4. ETHICS COMMITTEE (Art. 49)
   - Enforces code of conduct
   - Handles ethics complaints
   - Recommends disciplinary action

5. EDUCATION & TRAINING COMMITTEE (Art. 50)
   - Plans training programs
   - Uses ETF properly
   - Develops members and officers

VISUAL: Five icons representing each committee
```

**Lesson 2.7: Ethics and Mediation Committees** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"The complaint about Member A and B reaches two committees.
Who handles it?"

CONTENT (6 min):
MEDIATION/CONCILIATION COMMITTEE:
- Purpose: Resolve DISPUTES between members
- Approach: Bring parties together, find agreement
- Outcome: Settlement or referral
- When: Conflicts, misunderstandings, disagreements

ETHICS COMMITTEE:
- Purpose: Enforce CODE OF CONDUCT
- Approach: Investigate, determine violation, recommend action
- Outcome: Disciplinary recommendation to BOD
- When: Policy violations, misconduct, ethical breaches

THE MEMBER A VS B CASE:
Step 1: Mediation Committee tries to reconcile
Step 2: If mediation fails AND there's a code violation,
        Ethics Committee investigates
Step 3: Ethics recommends action to BOD
Step 4: BOD decides on discipline (if any)

IMPORTANT: Due process always!
- Right to be heard
- Right to present evidence
- Right to appeal

VISUAL: Case handling flowchart

BRIDGE (30 sec):
```

```text
STEP-BY-STEP GA CONDUCT:

1. REGISTRATION (Before formal start)
   - Verify membership
   - Issue voting cards
   - Check quorum continuously

2. CALL TO ORDER
   - Chair calls meeting to order
   - National anthem (optional but traditional)
   - Invocation (optional)

   - Secretary confirms notice was given
   - Secretary announces attendance vs quorum
```

**Lesson 3.7: Parliamentary Procedure Basics** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"A member shouts: 'I MOVE to fire the manager!'
Chaos erupts. Hadji bangs the gavel. 'Order! There's a process.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
BASIC PARLIAMENTARY PROCEDURE:

THE MOTION PROCESS:
1. Member is recognized by Chair
2. Member makes a motion: "I move that..."
3. Another member seconds: "I second the motion"
4. Chair states the motion
5. Discussion (for and against)
6. Chair calls for vote
7. Chair announces result

COMMON MOTIONS:
- Main Motion: Propose action
- Amendment: Change the motion
- Table: Postpone discussion
- Call the Question: End debate, vote now
- Point of Order: Rules are being broken

VOTING METHODS:
- Voice vote (Aye/Nay) - quick matters
- Show of hands - when voice is unclear
- Standing vote - when hands are unclear
- Secret ballot - elections, sensitive matters

THE "NO QUORUM" QUESTION:
- NEVER proceed without quorum
- Option 1: Wait for more members
- Option 2: Recess and reconvene
- Option 3: Adjourn and reschedule
- Decisions without quorum = VOID

VISUAL: Motion flowchart

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"After decisions, what must be documented?"
```

**Lesson 4.2: What is a Cooperative Policy?** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"A policy is NOT a personal preference.
It's an OFFICIAL rule that everyone must follow."

CONTENT (6 min):
POLICY vs PRACTICE vs PREFERENCE:

- PREFERENCE: "I think we should limit loans to P30,000"
  (Personal opinion - not binding)

- PRACTICE: "We usually limit loans to P30,000"
  (Tradition - inconsistent, undocumented)

- POLICY: "The cooperative shall limit individual loans to P30,000
           per borrower, subject to the following conditions..."
  (Official - approved, documented, enforceable)

CHARACTERISTICS OF A GOOD POLICY:
1. Written (documented)
2. Approved (by proper authority)
3. Communicated (members know it)
4. Consistent (applied equally)
5. Legal (compliant with laws)

WHO APPROVES POLICIES?
- BOD: Most operational policies
- GA: Fundamental policies (membership, fees, bylaws)

VISUAL: Policy approval flowchart
```

**Lesson 4.4: Policy Development Process** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):

POLICY DEVELOPMENT STEPS:

STEP 1: IDENTIFY THE NEED
- What problem does this policy solve?
- Who requested it or who will benefit?
- MoroCoop Example: Members need loans but there's no clear process

STEP 2: RESEARCH AND DRAFT
- Look at laws (RA 9520, CDA MCs)
- Check similar cooperatives' policies
- Consult affected members
- Draft the policy document

STEP 3: REVIEW AND CONSULT
- Legal review (does it comply?)
- Stakeholder input (do members agree?)
- Committee recommendation

STEP 4: APPROVAL
- BOD meeting for operational policies
- GA for fundamental policies
- Document the resolution

STEP 5: COMMUNICATE
- Announce to all members
- Train staff on implementation
- Make policy accessible

STEP 6: IMPLEMENT AND MONITOR
- Apply consistently
- Track issues
- Review periodically (annually)
```

```text
5. OTHER REQUIREMENTS
   - Updated registration
   - Amended bylaws (if changes)
   - Special reports (as required)

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
- No COC = Can't do business
- Continued non-compliance = Suspension
- Repeated violations = Dissolution
```
