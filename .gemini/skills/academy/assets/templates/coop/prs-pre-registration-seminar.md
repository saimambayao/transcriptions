## Learning Objectives

By the end of this seminar, participants will be able to:

1. **Define** what a cooperative is and its distinguishing characteristics
2. **Explain** the 7 Cooperative Principles and core values
3. **Identify** the type of cooperative appropriate for their needs
4. **List** the requirements for cooperative registration
5. **Describe** the rights and responsibilities of cooperative members
6. **Understand** the basic organizational structure of a cooperative
7. **Decide** whether to proceed with cooperative formation

## Seminar Curriculum (Standard 6-8 Hour Version)

### Opening (30 minutes)

- Registration and attendance
- Welcome and introductions
- Seminar overview and ground rules
- Icebreaker: "Why are you interested in cooperatives?"

**Facilitator Notes**:
- Collect complete attendee information for certificate
- Explain that PRS certificate is required for registration
- Set expectation: this is an orientation, not a commitment

---

### Module 1: What is a Cooperative? (1-1.5 hours)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants can define a cooperative and explain its core characteristics

**Session 1.1: Definition and Characteristics** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE + DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - ICA Definition: "An autonomous association of persons united voluntarily..."
  - Key characteristics:
    - Member-owned
    - Member-controlled (democratic)
    - Member-benefiting
  - The cooperative difference from corporations
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Use local language for key terms
  - Ask: "What do you think a cooperative is?" before defining
  - Emphasize: "You own it together, you control it together, you benefit together"

**Session 1.2: Cooperative vs Corporation vs NGO** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE + GROUP_DISCUSSION
- Content:

| Aspect | Corporation | Cooperative | NGO |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Ownership | Shareholders | Members | No owners |
| Control | By shares | One member, one vote | Board |
| Profit | To shareholders | To members (patronage) | No profit distribution |
| Purpose | Profit maximization | Member benefit | Social mission |

- Discussion: "Which model fits your group's goals?"
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - This is key differentiator; spend time here
  - Many think coops are "like corporations" - clarify difference

**Session 1.3: Brief History** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Rochdale Pioneers (1844, England)
  - Cooperative movement in the Philippines
  - Cooperatives in BARMM today
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Keep brief; focus on "cooperatives have a long, successful history"
  - Mention: 12,000+ coops in BARMM

---

### Module 2: Cooperative Values and Principles (1 hour)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants can list and explain the 7 Cooperative Principles

**Session 2.1: Core Values** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Self-help, self-responsibility
  - Democracy, equality, equity
  - Solidarity
  - Ethical values: honesty, openness, social responsibility, caring for others
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Connect values to Islamic principles where appropriate
  - Values are the "soul" of cooperatives

**Session 2.2: The 7 Cooperative Principles** (45 min)
- Type: LECTURE + ACTIVITY
- Content:

| Principle | Meaning | Your Coop Application |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| 1. Voluntary & Open Membership | Anyone can join without discrimination | Who can be members? |
| 2. Democratic Member Control | One member, one vote | How will you make decisions? |
| 3. Member Economic Participation | Members contribute capital fairly | How will you fund the coop? |
| 4. Autonomy & Independence | Self-governing, independent | Who controls your coop? |
| 5. Education, Training & Information | Members must be educated | How will you train members? |
| 6. Cooperation Among Cooperatives | Work with other coops | What networks exist? |
| 7. Concern for Community | Sustainable community development | How will you help your community? |

- Activity: For each principle, discuss "How would this work in YOUR cooperative?"
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - This module is foundational; participants must understand principles
  - Use real examples for each principle
  - Principle 5 (Education) is why this seminar exists

**Break** (15 minutes)

---

### Module 3: Types of Cooperatives (1 hour)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants can identify the appropriate cooperative type for their needs

**Session 3.1: Cooperative Types by Function** (45 min)
- Type: LECTURE + DISCUSSION
- Content:

| Type | Primary Activity | Example |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **Credit** | Savings and loans | Credit union |
| **Consumer** | Buying goods for members | Palengke coop |
| **Producer** | Producing goods together | Weaving coop |
| **Marketing** | Selling members' products | Farmers marketing coop |
| **Service** | Providing services | Transport coop |
| **Multi-purpose** | Multiple activities | MPC with credit + consumer |

- Discussion: "Which type fits YOUR group?"
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Most new coops want to be multi-purpose; explain pros/cons
  - Single-purpose is often better for starting

**Session 3.2: Cooperative Categories** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Primary (individuals as members)
  - Secondary (coops as members - federation)
  - Tertiary (federations as members - union)
  - Laboratory (for learning/training)
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Focus on Primary - this is what they'll form
  - Mention federations as future opportunity

---

### Module 4: Legal Framework (1 hour)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants understand key legal requirements for cooperatives

**Session 4.1: RA 9520 Overview** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008
  - Key provisions:
    - Definition and principles (Art. 3-4)
    - Formation requirements (Art. 7-14)
    - Member rights and obligations (Art. 18-22)
    - Governance structure (Art. 38-47)
    - Capital structure (Art. 53-60)
    - Tax exemptions and privileges (Art. 90)
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Don't overwhelm with legal details
  - Focus on "what you need to know to get started"
  - Emphasize: legal protection for coops

**Session 4.2: CDA/CSEA and Regulation** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Role of CDA (national) / CSEA (BARMM)
  - Registration process overview
  - Annual compliance requirements:
    - Reports (CAPR, CAFS)
    - General Assembly
    - Officer training
  - Benefits of registration
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - In BARMM, emphasize CSEA as regulatory body
  - Registration gives legal personality, access to programs

**Lunch** (1 hour)

---

### Module 5: Member Rights and Responsibilities (45 min)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants understand what being a coop member means

**Session 5.1: Rights of Members** (20 min)
- Type: LECTURE + DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Right to vote and be voted
  - Right to participate in General Assembly
  - Right to information (financial statements, policies)
  - Right to patronage refund
  - Right to due process
- Discussion: "Which rights matter most to you?"

**Session 5.2: Responsibilities of Members** (25 min)
- Type: LECTURE + DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Patronize the cooperative (use its services)
  - Pay shares and comply with policies
  - Attend meetings and participate
  - Exercise rights responsibly
  - Elect competent leaders
- Discussion: "Are you ready for these responsibilities?"
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Be honest: membership requires commitment
  - This helps filter out those not ready

---

### Module 6: Organizational Structure (45 min)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants understand basic cooperative governance

**Session 6.1: Governance Structure** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - General Assembly (highest authority)
  - Board of Directors (policy-making)
  - Officers (CEO/GM, Treasurer, Secretary)
  - Committees:
    - Audit Committee (elected)
    - Election Committee (elected)
    - Education Committee (appointed)
    - Mediation Committee (appointed)
- Visual: Organization chart
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Emphasize: GA is the boss, not the Board
  - Democratic control means member participation

**Session 6.2: Officer Qualifications** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Basic qualifications (member in good standing, legal age)
  - Training requirements (COOP-101, COOP-201)
  - Term limits and elections
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Explain: officers must undergo training within first half of term

---

### Module 7: Capital Structure and Benefits (45 min)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants understand cooperative financial structure

**Session 7.1: Capital Structure** (30 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Share capital (membership contribution)
  - Member deposits (optional savings)
  - Statutory funds:
    - Reserve Fund (10% of surplus)
    - Education & Training Fund (up to 10%)
    - Community Development Fund (3%)
    - Optional Fund (up to 7%)
  - Net surplus distribution (patronage refund)
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - Use simple examples with numbers
  - Emphasize: surplus goes back to members based on transactions

**Session 7.2: Benefits of Membership** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE + DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Access to services (loans, products, training)
  - Patronage refund (share of surplus)
  - Voting rights and ownership
  - Community and solidarity
  - Tax privileges (for registered coops)
- Discussion: "Which benefits attract you most?"

**Break** (15 minutes)

---

### Module 8: Registration Process (45 min)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants know the steps to register a cooperative

**Session 8.1: Pre-Registration Steps** (20 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Form organizing committee
  - Gather at least 15 members
  - Conduct economic survey
  - Complete PRS (this seminar)
  - Draft Articles of Cooperation and By-laws
  - Open bank account, deposit capital

**Session 8.2: Registration Requirements** (25 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Documentary requirements:
    1. Name Reservation Slip
    2. Articles of Cooperation (4 copies, notarized)
    3. By-Laws (4 copies)
    4. Economic Survey (4 copies)
    5. Treasurer's Affidavit
    6. Surety Bond
    7. PRS Certificates (all organizers)
    8. Registration fee
  - Where to submit: CSEA Office (for BARMM)
  - Timeline: Processing within [X] days
- Provide: Registration checklist handout
- **Facilitator Notes**:
  - This is practical information they need
  - Mention: templates available from CSEA

---

### Module 9: Next Steps and Decision (30 min)

**Learning Outcome**: Participants make informed decision about proceeding

**Session 9.1: Decision Worksheet** (15 min)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Activity: Complete personal decision worksheet
  - Am I ready for the responsibilities?
  - Is my group ready to work together?
  - Do we have enough committed members (15+)?
  - Do we have initial capital?
  - What type of coop fits our needs?
- Output: Personal decision (proceed / not ready / need more info)

**Session 9.2: Resources and Support** (15 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - CSEA services and support programs
  - Training resources (MoroAcademy)
  - Federation membership benefits
  - Contact information for follow-up
- Provide: Resource directory handout

---

### Closing (30 min)

**Q&A Session** (15 min)
- Open questions from participants
- Clarify remaining concerns

**Certificate Distribution** (10 min)
- Requirements: Attendance for entire seminar
- PRS Certificate of Completion
- Photo opportunity

**Closing Remarks** (5 min)
- Recap of key points
- Encouragement for those proceeding
- Farewell

## Facilitator Guide

### Qualifications

- CDA/CSEA-accredited Training Service Provider (TSP)
- Or CSEA Extension Office staff
- Knowledge of RA 9520 and cooperative principles
- Facilitation skills

### Preparation

**1 Week Before**:
- [ ] Confirm venue and participants
- [ ] Prepare all materials
- [ ] Print certificates with participant names

**Day Before**:
- [ ] Set up venue
- [ ] Test equipment
- [ ] Prepare registration area

### Facilitation Tips

1. **Use local language** - Translate key terms to Meranao, Maguindanaon, Tausug as appropriate
2. **Be interactive** - Ask questions, encourage discussion
3. **Use real examples** - BARMM coops, success stories, even failures
4. **Be honest** - Coops require commitment; not for everyone
5. **Stay neutral** - Your job is to inform, not recruit

### Common Questions

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "How much capital do we need?" | Depends on type; minimum share capital set by by-laws |
| "Can we register immediately after PRS?" | Yes, if you have all requirements |
| "What if we're fewer than 15?" | You need at least 15 members to register |
| "Can non-Filipinos join?" | As associate members only, not voting |
| "Is it hard to register?" | Process is straightforward if documents are complete |
