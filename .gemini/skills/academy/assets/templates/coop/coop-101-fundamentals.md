## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Describe** the historical origins of the cooperative movement (Rochdale Pioneers, global spread, Philippine and BARMM context)
2. **Define** what a cooperative is according to ICA and RA 9520 definitions
3. **List** and **explain** the 7 International Cooperative Principles
4. **Identify** the 6 cooperative values and their ethical foundations
5. **Distinguish** cooperatives from corporations, NGOs, and other organizations
6. **Recognize** the role of cooperatives in national and international development
7. **Appreciate** the contribution of cooperatives to BARMM development

### Unit 1: Origin and Philosophy of Cooperatives (2 hours)

> **MC 2025-09 Topic**: Origin and Philosophy of Cooperatives

**Lesson 1.1: Before Rochdale - Early Cooperative Experiments** (VIDEO - 7 min)
- The Fenwick Weavers' Society (Scotland, 1761) - earliest documented cooperative
- Industrial Revolution: worker exploitation, low wages, poor conditions
- The search for alternatives to industrial capitalism
- Why workers sought collective solutions
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 1.2: Robert Owen and Cooperative Philosophy** (VIDEO - 10 min)
- Robert Owen (1771-1858): Welsh industrialist turned reformer
- The New Lanark experiment: proof that profit and worker welfare can coexist
- Owen's vision: "villages of unity and cooperation"
- The foundation of cooperative thought
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 1.3: The Rochdale Pioneers - Birth of Modern Cooperatives** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- The 1844 Rochdale story: 28 weavers, £1 each
- Their innovation: combining ideals with practical business principles
- The original Rochdale Principles:
  - Open membership, democratic control
  - Distribution of surplus proportional to trade (patronage refund)
  - Limited interest on capital
  - Political and religious neutrality
- From 4 items (flour, oatmeal, sugar, butter) to a global movement
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 1.4: Global Spread of Cooperatives** (VIDEO - 8 min)
- From Rochdale to Europe: credit unions (Raiffeisen, Germany 1862)
- The International Cooperative Alliance (ICA, 1895)
- Major cooperative movements worldwide
- The cooperative movement today: 3 million cooperatives, 1 billion members
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 1.5: Cooperative Movement in the Philippines** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- **Spanish Period (1896)**: Earliest cooperative societies
- **American Period (1915)**: Rural Credit Law
- **Commonwealth Era (1938)**: Cooperative Marketing Law
- **Post-War (1952)**: RA 821 - ACCFA
- **Martial Law Era (1973)**: PD 175
- **Modern Era (1990)**: RA 6938 (Cooperative Code of 1990)
- **Current (2008)**: RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008)
- **Key Fact**: Philippines has 35,000+ registered cooperatives with 14+ million members
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 1.6: Cooperatives in the Bangsamoro** (VIDEO - 10 min)
- **BARMM Cooperative Landscape**: 12,214 registered cooperatives
  - Maguindanao: 4,671 (38.24%)
  - Lanao del Sur: 4,554 (37.29%)
  - Island provinces: Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi
- **CSEA Role**: Sole regulatory agency for cooperatives in BARMM
- **Major Cooperative Types in BARMM**:
  - Producer cooperatives (37.58%)
  - Marketing cooperatives (27.54%)
  - Agricultural cooperatives (12.82%)
- **BDP 2023-2028 Goal**: "Functional cooperatives and social enterprises increased"
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Quiz 1: Origin and Philosophy** (10 questions)

### Unit 2: Statement of Cooperative Identity (2 hours)

> **MC 2025-09 Topic**: Statement of Cooperative Identity

**Lesson 2.1: What is a Cooperative? - Definitions** (VIDEO - 10 min)
- **ICA Definition**: "An autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise."
- **RA 9520 Definition**: "An autonomous and duly registered association of persons, with a common bond of interest..."
- Key characteristics:
  - Member-owned, member-controlled
  - Surplus benefits members, not outside investors
  - Democratic governance (one member, one vote)
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.2: The 6 Cooperative Values** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- **Core Values**:
  - **Self-help**: Members improving their situation through collective action
  - **Self-responsibility**: Members taking responsibility for cooperative success
  - **Democracy**: Member control through democratic processes
  - **Equality**: Equal rights and opportunities for all members
  - **Equity**: Fair treatment and distribution of benefits
  - **Solidarity**: Members supporting each other
- **Ethical Values**: Honesty, Openness, Social Responsibility, Caring for Others
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.3: The 7 Cooperative Principles - Part 1** (VIDEO - 15 min)
- **Principle 1**: Voluntary and Open Membership
  - Open to all without discrimination
  - Freedom to join and leave
- **Principle 2**: Democratic Member Control
  - One member, one vote
  - Elected representatives accountable to members
- **Principle 3**: Member Economic Participation
  - Members contribute to capital
  - Limited return on share capital
  - Patronage refund concept
- **Principle 4**: Autonomy and Independence
  - Self-help organizations controlled by members
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.4: The 7 Cooperative Principles - Part 2** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- **Principle 5**: Education, Training, and Information
  - Training for members and officers
  - Why YOU are taking this course
- **Principle 6**: Cooperation Among Cooperatives
  - Working together locally and nationally
  - Federations and unions
- **Principle 7**: Concern for Community
  - Sustainable community development
  - Community Development Fund (CDF)
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.5: Cooperative Values in BARMM - Islamic Principles Alignment** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- How ICA values align with Islamic values:

| ICA Value | Islamic Equivalent | Arabic Term |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Self-help | Mutual Assistance | *Ta'awun* |
| Self-responsibility | Trust/Stewardship | *Amanah* |
| Democracy | Consultation | *Shura* |
| Equality | Human Dignity | *Karamah* |
| Equity | Justice | *'Adl* |
| Solidarity | Brotherhood | *Ukhuwwah* |

- Shari'ah-compliant practices: Halal transactions, no riba (usury), no maysir (gambling)
- Alignment with Moral Governance (12 Pillars)
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Quiz 2: Cooperative Identity** (10 questions)

### Unit 3: Cooperative Difference (1 hour)

> **MC 2025-09 Topic**: Cooperative Difference

**Lesson 3.1: Cooperatives vs Corporations vs NGOs** (VIDEO - 12 min)
- Governance structure differences
- Profit/surplus distribution
- Purpose: profit maximization vs. member benefit vs. social mission
- Decision-making: shareholder votes by shares vs. one person one vote

| Aspect | Corporation | Cooperative | NGO |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Ownership** | Shareholders | Members | No owners |
| **Voting** | By shares | One person, one vote | Board decides |
| **Purpose** | Profit maximization | Member benefit | Social mission |
| **Surplus** | Dividends to shareholders | Patronage refund to members | Reinvested |

- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 3.2: Types of Cooperatives** (VIDEO - 10 min)
- **By Level**: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary
- **By Function**:
  - Credit Cooperative
  - Consumer Cooperative
  - Producer Cooperative
  - Marketing Cooperative
  - Service Cooperative
  - Multi-purpose Cooperative
- Choosing the right type for your needs
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 3.3: Benefits of Cooperative Membership** (TEXT - 10 min)
- Access to services (credit, marketing, inputs)
- Patronage refund - share in surplus based on transactions
- Democratic voice in decision-making
- Training and education opportunities
- Tax advantages passed to members
- Community development participation
- **Reading**: English + Filipino versions

## Script Requirements

### Video Scripts

Each video lesson requires:
1. **English Script** - Primary language for formal terms
2. **Filipino Script** - Localized version for accessibility
3. **Timing Markers** - Scene/slide change indicators
4. **Visual Cues** - On-screen text and graphics notes

### Script Pacing (~90 WPM)

All scripts use **~90 wpm** to accommodate BARMM's ESL beginner learners.

**Technical Term Pattern**: `"[English term], o [Filipino explanation]"`
- Example: "Ang **cooperative**, o kooperatiba, ay..."
- Example: "Ang **patronage refund**, o bahagi ng surplus batay sa iyong transaksyon..."
