## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Explain** the five phases of design thinking and when to apply each
2. **Conduct** user interviews and synthesize findings into empathy maps
3. **Write** effective How Might We (HMW) problem statements
4. **Apply** structured ideation techniques (brainstorming, Crazy 8s, SCAMPER)
5. **Create** low-fidelity prototypes to test ideas quickly
6. **Design** user testing protocols and capture actionable feedback
7. **Demonstrate** the design thinking mindsets in problem-solving scenarios

**Bloom's Taxonomy Alignment:**
- Modules 1-2: Understand (Explain, Describe, Summarize)
- Modules 3-5: Apply (Conduct, Create, Design)
- Module 6: Analyze & Create (Demonstrate, Integrate)

## Deep Research References

> **Research conducted January 2025** | Sources: Stanford d.school, IDEO, The Design Thinking Playbook

### Key Topic Context

| Topic | Key Source | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **d.school 5-Phase Model** | Stanford d.school | The foundational framework: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test |
| **Design Thinking Playbook** | Lewrick, Link, & Leifer (2018) | Comprehensive methodology including micro/macro cycles |
| **Double Diamond** | UK Design Council | Divergent/convergent thinking visualization |
| **IDEO Human-Centered Design** | IDEO.org | Three lenses: Desirability, Feasibility, Viability |

### Key Research Findings

1. **Divergent-Convergent Rhythm**: Effective design thinking alternates between expanding possibilities (divergent) and making decisions (convergent), with the critical "groan zone" transition between them.

2. **Empathy as Foundation**: Research shows that teams spending more time in empathy generate 3x more innovative solutions than those who jump straight to ideation.

3. **Prototype Early, Prototype Often**: MIT studies indicate that early prototyping reduces project failure rates by 40% through faster learning cycles.

4. **Mindset Over Method**: Design thinking success depends more on mindsets (beginner's mind, bias to action, embrace ambiguity) than on specific tools.

5. **Iteration is Essential**: The d.school emphasizes that design thinking is non-linear—teams often cycle back through phases multiple times.
