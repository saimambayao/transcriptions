# SE-101: Fundamentals of Social Entrepreneurship

Foundational training for social enterprise founders and staff.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | SE-101 |
| **Type** | Online Course (Self-Paced) |
| **Duration** | 8 hours |
| **Target Audience** | SE Founders, Managers, Staff |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Compliance** | CSEA recommended for new SE registrations |
| **Certificate** | Yes (80% completion + 70% assessment) |
| **Languages** | English + Filipino (bilingual scripts) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Define** social entrepreneurship and its distinction from traditional business
2. **Identify** characteristics of successful social enterprises
3. **Develop** a social mission and theory of change
4. **Apply** the social business model canvas
5. **Measure** social impact using basic frameworks

## Script Requirements

### Video Scripts

Each video lesson requires:
1. **English Script** - Primary language for formal terms
2. **Filipino Script** - Localized version for accessibility
3. **Timing Markers** - Scene/slide change indicators
4. **Visual Cues** - On-screen text and graphics notes

### Script Pacing (~90 WPM for All Languages)

All scripts (Filipino or English) use **~90 wpm** to accommodate BARMM's ESL beginner learners. Additional pacing considerations:
- Longer words with affixes (mag-, nag-, -in, -an) in Filipino
- Technical term explanation pairs (English term + Filipino meaning)
- Natural code-switching (Taglish) pauses
- Clearer enunciation for educational content

**Technical Term Pattern**: `"[English term], o [Filipino explanation]"`
- Example: "Ang **social enterprise**, o negosyong panlipunan, ay..."
- Example: "Dapat nating sukatin ang **impact**, ang epekto sa komunidad..."

**Timing Buffers**:
- Add +15 seconds per technical definition
- Add +20 seconds for example explanations
- Total buffer: 15-20% of estimated duration

### Script Template

```
LESSON: [Title]
DURATION: [X] minutes
LANGUAGE: [Filipino/English]
WORD COUNT: [~90 wpm × duration in minutes]

Visual: [Description]
```

### Key Figures in Social Enterprise

**Bill Drayton (1943-)**:
- Founded **Ashoka** in 1980 - first organization dedicated to funding social entrepreneurs
- Credited with popularizing the term "social entrepreneur"
- Ashoka now supports nearly 3,000 Fellows in 70+ countries
- Key qualities sought: creativity, entrepreneurial quality, social impact, ethical fiber

**Muhammad Yunus (1940-)**:
- Founded **Grameen Bank** in 1976 - pioneer of microfinance
- Nobel Peace Prize 2006 for microcredit work
- Defines social business as: "Non-loss, non-dividend" enterprise solving social problems
- Founded Yunus Social Business (2011) to spread the model globally

### Timeline of Social Entrepreneurship
