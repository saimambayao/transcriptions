### Key Operations Management Facts

**Toyota Production System (TPS):**
- Developed by Taiichi Ohno and Eiji Toyoda (1948-1975)
- Taiichi Ohno: Father of the Toyota Production System
- Core concepts: Just-in-time (JIT), Jidoka (automation with human touch)
- Created the Kanban system for production scheduling
- Identified 7 wastes (Muda): Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Overprocessing, Defects
- Later expanded to 8 wastes (TIMWOODS) adding Skills/Talent underutilization

**PDCA/PDSA Cycle Origins:**
- **Walter Shewhart** (1891-1967): Original concept developed at Bell Laboratories (1920s-1939)
- Published in *Economic Control of Quality of Manufactured Product* (1931)
- **W. Edwards Deming** (1900-1993): Refined and popularized the cycle
- Taught to Japanese engineers in 1950s, sparking Japan's quality revolution
- Deming later called it PDSA (Plan-Do-Study-Act) to emphasize learning
- Foundation of Total Quality Management (TQM) and Kaizen

**Kaizen (改善):**
- Japanese word meaning "change for the better" or continuous improvement
- Introduced to Japan by Deming and Juran post-WWII
- Popularized by Masaaki Imai in *Kaizen: The Key to Japan's Competitive Success* (1986)
- Philosophy: Small, incremental improvements over time yield significant results
