# GOV-301: Executive Leadership

Strategic leadership development for senior government leaders.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-301 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 3 days (24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Directors, Assistant Secretaries, Executives |
| **Prerequisites** | Management/executive appointment |
| **Max Participants** | 20 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + participation) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Apply** strategic leadership frameworks to government challenges
2. **Lead** organizational change and transformation initiatives
3. **Manage** diverse stakeholders effectively
4. **Make** sound decisions with incomplete information
5. **Build** and sustain high-performing teams

### Key Leadership Theory Facts

**James MacGregor Burns (1918-2014) - Transformational Leadership:**
- Coined "transformational leadership" in his 1978 book *Leadership*
- Distinguished between transactional (self-interest/exchange) and transformational (ethics/beliefs) leadership
- "Leaders and followers raise one another to higher levels of motivation and morality"
- Drew on Maslow's hierarchy of needs
- Based on WWII observations: effective units had leadership "within the ranks"

**Bernard Bass (1985) - Four Components of Transformational Leadership:**
1. Idealized Influence (charisma)
2. Inspirational Motivation
3. Intellectual Stimulation
4. Individualized Consideration

**SWOT Analysis Origins (1960-1970):**
- Developed at Stanford Research Institute (SRI)
- Robert Franklin Stewart credited as originator
- Originally called SOFT: Satisfactory, Opportunities, Faults, Threats
- Relabeled to SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) in 1967
- 2×2 matrix format appeared in *Systematic Corporate Planning* (Argenti, 1974)
- Research funded by Fortune 500 companies to understand why corporate planning failed
