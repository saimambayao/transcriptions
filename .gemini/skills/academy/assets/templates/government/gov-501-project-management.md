# GOV-501: Project Management for Government

Comprehensive project management training for government project managers and team leaders.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-501 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 3-5 days (24-40 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Project Managers, Program Officers, Technical Staff |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Max Participants** | 25 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + project plan output) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Apply** project management principles to government projects
2. **Develop** comprehensive project plans with WBS and schedules
3. **Manage** project resources, risks, and stakeholders
4. **Monitor** project progress and implement corrective actions
5. **Close** projects properly with documentation and lessons learned

### Key Project Management History Facts

**Early Project Management (1950s-1960s):**
- PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique) developed by US Navy for Polaris missile program
- CPM (Critical Path Method) developed by DuPont and Remington Rand
- Large-scale engineering and defense projects drove formal project management methods

**Project Management Institute (PMI):**
- Founded in 1969 in Pennsylvania, USA
- Started as professional organization to advance project management practice
- Early founders included Jim Snyder and others
- Established PMP (Project Management Professional) certification as global standard

**PMBOK Development:**
- First PMBOK published as white paper in 1987
- Became ANSI standard in 1998
- Defines 5 process groups: Initiating, Planning, Executing, Monitoring & Controlling, Closing
- Originally 9 knowledge areas, now 10 knowledge areas

**Philippine Government Procurement (RA 9184):**
- Government Procurement Reform Act
- Requires public bidding as primary mode
- Allows alternative methods under specific conditions
- PhilGEPS as central procurement portal
