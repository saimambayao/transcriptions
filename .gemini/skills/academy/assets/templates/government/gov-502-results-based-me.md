# GOV-502: Results-Based Monitoring & Evaluation

Comprehensive M&E training for government program managers and M&E officers.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-502 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 3 days (24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | M&E Officers, Program Managers, Planners |
| **Prerequisites** | GOV-401 Strategic Planning (recommended) |
| **Max Participants** | 25 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + M&E plan output) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Design** results frameworks with clear indicators
2. **Develop** comprehensive M&E plans for programs
3. **Collect** and manage monitoring data effectively
4. **Analyze** data for evidence-based decision making
5. **Conduct** program evaluations and report findings

### Key RBM/M&E History Facts

**Origins of Results-Based Management:**
- World Bank Operations Evaluation Department produced Note on RBM in 1997
- CIDA (Canadian International Development Agency) published RBM guidance in January 1999
- OECD published "Glossary of Evaluation and Results Based Management Terms" in October 2000

**Definition:**
"A broad management strategy aimed at achieving improved performance and demonstrable results" - adopted by multilateral development organizations, bilateral agencies, and public administrations worldwide.

**UNDP's RBM Framework - Four Pillars:**
1. Definition of strategic goals providing focus for action
2. Specification of expected results contributing to goals
3. Alignment of programmes, processes and resources behind expected results
4. On-going monitoring and assessment integrating lessons learned

**Key M&E Resources:**
- UNDP "Handbook on Planning, Monitoring and Evaluating Development Results" (2009)
- UNDG RBM Handbook adopted life-cycle approach to RBM
- World Bank Scorecard uses 22 indicators for expected/achieved results

**OECD-DAC Evaluation Criteria (Updated 2019):**
1. **Relevance**: Is the intervention doing the right things?
2. **Coherence**: How well does the intervention fit?
3. **Effectiveness**: Is the intervention achieving its objectives?
4. **Efficiency**: How well are resources being used?
5. **Impact**: What difference does the intervention make?
6. **Sustainability**: Will the benefits last?
