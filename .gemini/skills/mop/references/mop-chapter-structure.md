# MOP Chapter Structure -- Detailed Templates

Detailed sub-section templates for each of the 9 MOP chapters. Read this file during Phase 4 (DRAFT) to structure each chapter correctly.

---

## Chapter I: Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
- State why this MOP exists and what it aims to achieve
- Link to the entity's mandate and strategic objectives
- **Document**: 1-2 paragraphs explaining the MOP's role in standardizing operations

### 1.2 Scope
- Define which offices, divisions, and personnel are covered
- Specify geographic scope (if applicable -- provincial, municipal, regional)
- Clarify what is NOT covered (refer to other MOPs or manuals if relevant)
- **Document**: Bullet list of covered areas and explicit exclusions

### 1.3 Legal Basis
- Cite the enabling legislation: BAA 13 (Administrative Code), relevant BAAs, BOL provisions
- Include national laws that apply (RA 9485 Anti-Red Tape Act, RA 11032 EODB Act, etc.)
- Reference executive orders, office orders, and issuances that govern the entity
- **Document**: Numbered list of legal references with full citation
- **Interview**: Legal officer or administrative head for the complete list

### 1.4 Definition of Terms
- Define technical, legal, and operational terms used throughout the MOP
- Include acronyms table
- **Document**: Alphabetical glossary table (Term | Definition)

### 1.5 Amendment Procedures
- How the MOP is updated: who can propose amendments, approval process, version control
- Include amendment log template (Date | Section | Description | Approved By)
- **Document**: Step-by-step amendment procedure + log template

## Chapter II: Organizational Structure

### 2.1 Mandate and Functions
- Entity's mandate from its enabling law (BAA, executive order, BOL provision)
- Vision, mission, and core values
- Key result areas or strategic objectives
- **Document**: Mandate statement + vision/mission + objectives list
- **Interview**: Entity head or chief of staff

### 2.2 Organizational Chart
- Visual org chart showing all divisions, sections, and units
- Reporting lines and supervisory relationships
- Indicate plantilla positions vs. contractual/COS positions
- **Document**: Org chart diagram + narrative description
- **Interview**: HR officer or administrative division head

### 2.3 Departments and Divisions
- For each division/section/unit:
  - Name and designation code
  - Functions and responsibilities
  - Key outputs and deliverables
  - Staffing complement (authorized vs. filled positions)
- **Document**: Division profile table (Division | Functions | Staff | Key Outputs)
- **Interview**: Division heads

### 2.4 Roles and Responsibilities
- Position-specific duties for key roles (head, division chiefs, section heads, staff)
- Job descriptions referencing the plantilla
- Decision-making authority levels
- **Document**: Role description per position with authority matrix
- **Interview**: HR officer + division heads
- **Forms**: Existing job descriptions, plantilla, designation orders

## Chapter III: Human Resources

### 3.1 Staffing and Recruitment
- Staffing standards and complement
- Recruitment process (posting, screening, selection, appointment)

- **Interview**: HR officer

### 3.2 Performance Management

- Evaluation cycle and timeline
- Rating scale and criteria
- Performance improvement plans
- **Document**: Performance management cycle diagram + rating criteria table
- **Interview**: HR officer + division heads
- **Forms**: IPCR/OPCR templates, performance commitment form

### 3.3 Leave Management
- Types of leave and entitlements
- Application and approval process
- Leave tracking and monitoring
- **Document**: Leave types table + application SOP
- **Forms**: Leave application form, leave ledger template

### 3.4 Training and Development
- Training needs assessment process
- Capacity building programs and calendar
- Scholarship and study leave policies
- **Document**: Training plan template + needs assessment form
- **Interview**: HR officer or training focal person

### 3.5 Discipline and Grievance
- Code of conduct and ethics standards
- Administrative discipline procedures
- Grievance mechanism
- **Document**: Discipline process flowchart + grievance submission procedure
- **Forms**: Complaint form, incident report template

## Chapter IX: Annexes

### Annex A: Organizational Chart
- Full org chart with all positions

### Annex B: Job Descriptions
- Complete job descriptions by position

### Annex C: Forms Collection
- All forms referenced in the MOP, compiled with instructions
- Each form should include: form number, title, purpose, how to fill out, routing

### Annex D: Process Flowcharts
- All flowcharts referenced in the MOP, compiled in one section
- Standard symbols: oval (start/end), rectangle (process), diamond (decision), parallelogram (input/output)

### Annex E: Legal References
- Full text or key excerpts of enabling legislation
- BOL provisions relevant to the entity
- BAA 13 provisions applicable to entity type
- Office orders and issuances in effect

### Annex F: Directory
- Contact directory of key personnel
- Stakeholder contact list
- Emergency contact information

### Annex G: Glossary
- Comprehensive glossary of terms and acronyms

### Annex H: Amendment Log
- Version history table (Version | Date | Section Changed | Description | Approved By)

## Adaptation Guide

### Provincial MOP (Full 9 Chapters)
- Include all chapters with full sub-sections
- Each chapter should have detailed SOPs for every process
- Include all annexes
- Expected length: 80-150 pages
- Example: MDN Provincial MOP

### Division MOP (5-6 Chapters)
- **Always include**: I (Introduction), II (Org Structure), IX (Annexes)
- **Select from**: The 2-3 chapters most relevant to the division's core function
  - Research division: V (Planning) + VI (Service Delivery)
  - Finance division: IV (Financial Management) + VII (Procurement)
  - Admin division: III (HR) + VIII (Reporting)
- **Merge where possible**: Combine related sub-sections from omitted chapters into existing ones
- Expected length: 40-80 pages

### Office MOP (3-4 Chapters)
- **Always include**: I (Introduction, abbreviated), II (Org Structure, abbreviated)
- **Core chapter**: One detailed chapter covering the office's primary function
- **Annex**: Simplified IX with relevant forms and flowcharts only
- Expected length: 20-40 pages
- Suitable for: small offices, project management units, committee secretariats
