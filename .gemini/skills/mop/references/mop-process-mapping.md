# MOP Process Mapping -- Documentation Methodology

How to document organizational processes for inclusion in the MOP. Read this file during Phase 3 (GATHER) and Phase 4 (DRAFT).

---

## Process Mapping Methodology

### Step 1: Identify Processes to Map

Categorize processes by type and priority:

| Category | Examples | Priority |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Core/Mandate** | Service delivery, legislative support, policy development | High -- map first |
| **Support** | HR, finance, procurement, records management | Medium -- map next |
| **Management** | Planning, M&E, reporting, decision-making | Medium |
| **Compliance** | Audit, FOI, transparency, legal requirements | High if externally mandated |

### Step 2: Select Mapping Technique

| Technique | Best For | Output |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Flowchart** | Sequential processes with decision points | Process flow diagram |
| **Swim Lane Diagram** | Processes crossing multiple divisions/roles | Cross-functional flow |
| **RACI Matrix** | Clarifying who does what | Responsibility assignment table |
| **Value Stream Map** | Identifying waste and bottlenecks | Current vs. future state |

### Step 3: Map Current State ("As-Is")

For each process:

1. **Name the process** (use verb-noun format: "Process Leave Application")
2. **Identify start and end points** (trigger event and final output)
3. **List all steps** in sequence
4. **Note decision points** (where the process branches)
5. **Identify actors** at each step (who performs the action)
6. **Record inputs and outputs** for each step
7. **Measure time** (how long each step takes, total processing time)
8. **Note forms and documents** used at each step

### Step 4: Analyze for Improvements ("To-Be")

- **Bottlenecks**: Where do delays occur? Why?
- **Redundancies**: Are any steps duplicated across units?
- **Gaps**: Are there undocumented steps that staff "just know"?
- **Compliance risks**: Are there steps without proper authorization or documentation?
- **Improvement opportunities**: Can steps be combined, automated, or eliminated?

### Step 5: Document the Improved Process

Write the process in the SOP entry format (see template below) and create the visual diagram.

## Staff Interview Methodology

### Interview Preparation

1. **Identify interviewees** -- process owners, frontline staff, supervisors, clients
2. **Review existing documents** first -- come prepared with specific questions
3. **Schedule 30-60 minute sessions** -- one process area per session
4. **Use structured questions** but allow open-ended follow-up

### Interview Questions by Process Area

#### General Process Questions
- Walk me through this process from start to finish
- What triggers this process? (request, schedule, event, directive)
- What is the final output or deliverable?
- How long does this typically take from start to finish?
- What forms or documents do you use?
- Who else is involved in this process?
- What approvals are required and from whom?
- What are the most common problems or delays?
- Is there a written procedure for this? If not, how did you learn it?
- Has this process changed recently? What prompted the change?

#### HR Process Questions
- How are positions filled? Walk me through the last recruitment
- How is performance evaluated? What forms are used?
- How are leave requests processed?
- How are training needs identified?

#### Financial Process Questions
- How are budget proposals prepared? What template is used?
- Walk me through a typical disbursement from request to check release
- How are cash advances processed and liquidated?
- What pre-audit checks are done before payment?

#### Service Delivery Questions
- What services does this office provide to the public?
- What are the requirements for each service?
- How long should each service take? How long does it actually take?
- How do citizens provide feedback or file complaints?
- What is the Citizen's Charter and is it posted?

#### Procurement Questions
- How is the Annual Procurement Plan prepared?
- Walk me through a typical procurement from request to delivery
- What procurement modes are used and when?
- How is the BAC constituted?

### Interview Tips

- **Listen more than talk** -- the interviewee is the expert on their process
- **Ask for examples** -- "Can you show me a recent [form/report/request]?"
- **Clarify ambiguity** -- "When you say 'it goes up,' who specifically receives it?"
- **Note informal processes** -- undocumented workarounds reveal gaps the MOP should address
- **Validate with multiple sources** -- interview at least 2 people per process area
- **Record with permission** -- take notes or record for accuracy

## Flowchart Standards

### Standard Symbols

| Symbol | Shape | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Oval | Rounded rectangle | Start / End |
| Rectangle | Standard rectangle | Process step / Action |
| Diamond | Rotated square | Decision point (Yes/No) |
| Parallelogram | Slanted rectangle | Input / Output (document, data) |
| Arrow | Directional line | Flow direction |
| Circle | Small circle | Connector (continues elsewhere) |

### Flowchart Best Practices

- Flow top-to-bottom or left-to-right
- Label every arrow from a decision diamond (Yes/No, Approved/Returned)
- Include the responsible person/office at each step
- Number steps to match the written SOP
- Keep flowcharts to one page where possible -- split complex processes
- Include timeline indicators where relevant (e.g., "within 3 working days")

## RACI Matrix Template

| Process Step | Responsible | Accountable | Consulted | Informed |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| [Step 1] | [Who does it] | [Who owns it] | [Who advises] | [Who is notified] |
| [Step 2] | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Definitions:**
- **R (Responsible)**: Performs the work
- **A (Accountable)**: Has final authority and approval (only one per step)
- **C (Consulted)**: Provides input before the action
- **I (Informed)**: Notified after the action

Use this template for each Standard Operating Procedure documented in the MOP:

```
### [Section.Number] [Process Name]

**Purpose**: [Why this process exists -- 1-2 sentences]

**Scope**: [Who and what this SOP covers]

**Legal Basis**: [BAA, BOL provision, office order, or national law]

**Responsible Office/Personnel**: [Division/Section and specific position]

**Frequency**: [Daily / Weekly / Monthly / As needed / Per schedule]

**Prerequisites**: [What must be in place before starting]

#### Procedure

| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeline | Form/Document |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | [Action verb + what is done] | [Position] | [Duration] | [Form name or N/A] |
| 2 | [Next action] | [Position] | [Duration] | [Form name or N/A] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Decision Points:**
- If [condition], proceed to Step [N]
- If [other condition], return to Step [N] with [instruction]

**Output**: [What is produced at the end of this process]

**Performance Standard**: [Expected completion time, quality metric]

**References**: [Related SOPs, forms, or policies within this MOP]

[Flowchart reference: See Annex D, Figure [N]]
```

## Identifying Bottlenecks and Improvements

### Common Bottleneck Patterns

| Pattern | Symptom | Typical Cause | MOP Solution |
|---------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Approval bottleneck** | Documents wait days for one signature | Too many approval layers | Delegate authority, define approval thresholds |
| **Information bottleneck** | Staff cannot proceed without data from another unit | No information-sharing protocol | Define handoff procedures and timelines |
| **Capacity bottleneck** | One person handles all requests | No backup or cross-training | Document process so others can perform it |
| **Compliance bottleneck** | Requirements unclear, documents returned repeatedly | Missing checklist or unclear standards | Include complete requirements checklist in SOP |
| **Technology bottleneck** | Manual processes that could be automated | No digital systems or underused systems | Note automation opportunities for future improvement |

### Process Improvement Documentation

When the MOP introduces improvements over current practice:

1. **Document the current process** ("As-Is") -- acknowledge what exists
2. **Document the improved process** ("To-Be") -- what the MOP prescribes
3. **Note the change** -- briefly explain why the improvement was made
4. **Include transition guidance** -- how to shift from current to improved process
5. **Set review date** -- when to evaluate if the improvement is working
