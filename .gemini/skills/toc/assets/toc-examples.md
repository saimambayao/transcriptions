# Theory of Change Examples for BARMM

## Example 1: Agricultural Cooperative

### Organization: Bangsamoro Rice Farmers Cooperative
### Type: Producer Cooperative
### Date: 2024

---

### Situation Analysis

**Problem**: Rice farmers in BARMM face low productivity, limited market access, and vulnerability to climate risks, resulting in persistent poverty.

**Root Causes**:
1. Outdated farming techniques and limited access to inputs
2. Fragmented smallholder farms with no collective bargaining power
3. Dependence on middlemen who capture most value
4. Climate vulnerability and lack of irrigation

**Target Group**: 500 smallholder rice farming families in Maguindanao

## Example 2: Social Enterprise

### Organization: Maranao Heritage Crafts SE
### Type: Social Enterprise (Cultural Preservation)
### Date: 2024

---

### Situation Analysis

**Problem**: Traditional Maranao weaving and crafts are dying out as artisans age and youth migrate to cities, threatening cultural heritage and community livelihoods.

**Root Causes**:
1. Master artisans aging without transferring skills
2. Low income from traditional crafts discourages youth
3. Limited market access for authentic products
4. Competition from mass-produced imitations

**Target Groups**:
- Primary: 50 traditional artisans, 30 youth apprentices
- Secondary: Broader Maranao community, cultural tourists

## Example 3: Peace and Development Program

### Organization: BARMM Peace Building Initiative
### Type: Government/NGO Partnership
### Date: 2024

---

### Situation Analysis

**Problem**: Despite the peace agreement, some communities in BARMM experience ongoing tension, limited economic opportunities, and weak social cohesion.

**Root Causes**:
1. Historical grievances and distrust
2. Youth unemployment and lack of opportunities
3. Weak local governance capacity
4. Limited inter-community interaction

**Target Groups**:
- Primary: 20 conflict-affected barangays, 5,000 households
- Youth: 2,000 out-of-school youth
- Leaders: 200 barangay officials and traditional leaders

---

### Theory of Change Narrative

**The Change We Seek:**

We believe that sustainable peace in conflict-affected BARMM communities requires addressing both the root causes of conflict and creating positive pathways for community development. Our long-term impact is peaceful, prosperous communities where diverse groups live together productively.

**How Change Happens:**

If we (1) build capacity of local leaders for conflict resolution, (2) create economic opportunities for youth, and (3) facilitate inter-community dialogue and cooperation, then communities will develop stronger social cohesion and reduced conflict.

This works because:
- Trained leaders can address disputes before escalation
- Youth with livelihoods have stake in peace
- Positive inter-community interaction builds trust
- Economic cooperation creates mutual dependence

**Key Assumptions:**
- Political will exists at all levels
- Security situation allows program implementation
- Communities willing to engage across divides
- Economic opportunities can be created locally

## Key Takeaways for BARMM Context

### 1. Dual-Track for Social Enterprises

Always include both social impact and business sustainability pathways.

### 2. Cooperative Principles Integration

Map ICA principles to outcomes:
- Democratic control → Governance outcomes
- Member participation → Engagement outcomes
- Community concern → Social impact outcomes

### 3. Peace Sensitivity

Include assumptions about:
- Security conditions
- Community willingness to engage
- Political support

### 4. Shari'ah Alignment

For Muslim communities, consider:
- Halal compliance in activities
- Islamic social finance integration
- Religious leader endorsement

### 5. Living Document Approach

- Review ToC quarterly in first year
- Update based on learning
- Engage stakeholders in revisions
