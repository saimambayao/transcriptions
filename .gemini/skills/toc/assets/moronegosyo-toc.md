## 1. Situation Analysis

### The Problem

**What problem are we trying to solve?**

Bangsamoro cooperatives and social enterprises remain economically marginalized despite having quality products and services. They cannot access markets beyond their immediate communities, limiting their growth, member/beneficiary income, and contribution to regional economic development.

**Who is affected by this problem?**

| Affected Group | How They're Affected | Estimated Number |
|----------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Cooperative members | Limited livelihood income from coop activities | 50,000+ members |
| SE beneficiaries | Constrained impact due to limited SE revenue | 10,000+ individuals |
| Coop/SE managers | Unable to grow their organizations | 1,000+ leaders |
| Conscious consumers | Cannot find/trust authentic community products | Millions potential |
| BARMM economy | Underdeveloped cooperative sector | Region-wide |

**What are the root causes?**

1. **Geographic Isolation**: Many coops/SEs are in rural barangays with limited physical market access
2. **Digital Exclusion**: No affordable, appropriate e-commerce platform for community enterprises
3. **Trust Deficit**: Buyers cannot verify product authenticity or social impact claims
4. **Capacity Gaps**: Limited digital literacy and business management skills
5. **Infrastructure Gaps**: Poor logistics, connectivity, and payment systems in rural areas
6. **Regulatory Burden**: Compliance requirements seen as barrier rather than enabler

**What is the current situation?**

- 500+ cooperatives registered with CSEA, but most operate only locally
- Social enterprises struggle to scale impact beyond initial communities
- Online selling is fragmented (Facebook, Messenger) with no verification
- CSEA lacks real-time visibility into sector economic performance
- Diaspora and urban consumers want to support Bangsamoro but can't find trusted channels

## 10. Narrative Summary

### The Change We Seek

MoroNegosyo aims to transform the economic prospects of Bangsamoro's cooperative and social enterprise sector by removing the barriers that keep community enterprises isolated from broader markets.

Today, a weaver's cooperative in Maguindanao can only sell to neighbors and occasional visitors. A coffee social enterprise in Lanao del Sur struggles to tell its story to conscious consumers in Manila. CSEA staff spend weeks compiling reports that could be automated. The sector's potential remains locked.

We envision a future where every registered cooperative and social enterprise has a professional online presence, where buyers across the Philippines and the diaspora can confidently purchase authentic community products, and where the data generated through commerce automatically demonstrates the sector's contribution to regional development.

### How Change Happens

Our theory of change is built on a simple insight: **market access + trust + efficiency = sustainable community enterprises**.

**Stage 1: Digital Presence** - We start by giving every registered coop/SE a zero-setup storefront. This immediately solves the "no website" problem and establishes digital presence.

**Stage 2: Trust Building** - Through CSEA verification badges and impact stories, we help buyers distinguish authentic community products from commercial alternatives. Trust converts browsers to buyers.

**Stage 3: Transaction Facilitation** - Integrated order management, payment tracking, and logistics partnerships make buying and selling smooth. Each successful transaction builds confidence.

**Stage 4: Operational Efficiency** - As transactions flow, data automatically populates compliance reports, reducing burden and improving CSEA oversight. Coops spend less time on paperwork.

**Stage 5: Market Expansion** - With digital tools and buyer trust established, coops/SEs can reach diaspora, urban, and institutional buyers they never could before.

**Stage 6: Sustainable Growth** - Expanded markets and efficient operations lead to increased revenue, which flows back to members through patronage refunds and SE beneficiaries through expanded programs.

### Key Assumptions

The most critical assumptions underlying our theory:

1. **Adoption is possible**: Coops/SEs with limited digital literacy can use the platform if it's simple enough and supported by training.

2. **Trust signals work**: CSEA verification will meaningfully influence buyer behavior - not just inform, but convert.

3. **Infrastructure is adequate**: Mobile connectivity, payment systems, and logistics can support e-commerce in BARMM, even if imperfectly.

4. **Value flows to members**: Increased coop revenue will translate to member benefits through the cooperative patronage refund mechanism.

5. **CSEA commitment sustains**: CSEA will maintain ownership and support for the platform as a core part of its mandate.

We will actively test and monitor these assumptions, adjusting our approach as we learn.

## Risk Factors

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Low seller adoption | Medium | High | Intensive onboarding support, CSEA encouragement |
| Poor user experience | Medium | High | User testing, iterative improvement, mobile-first |
| Trust signals ignored | Medium | Medium | Prominent placement, buyer education, testimonials |
| Logistics failures | High | Medium | Multiple logistics partners, seller-arranged options |
| Payment challenges | Medium | Medium | Multiple payment options, COD for local |
| Security incidents | Low | High | Security best practices, regular audits |
