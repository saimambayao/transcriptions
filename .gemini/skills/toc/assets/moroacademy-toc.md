## 1. Situation Analysis

### The Problem

**What problem are we trying to solve?**

The capacity of Bangsamoro's cooperative and social enterprise sector is constrained by inadequate access to relevant, quality training. Leaders lack the skills needed for effective governance, compliance, and business growth, while aspiring entrepreneurs cannot find structured pathways to launch their ventures.

**Who is affected by this problem?**

| Affected Group | How They're Affected | Estimated Number |
|----------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Coop officers | Undertrained for governance, finance, compliance roles | 5,000+ officers |
| SE founders/staff | Lack business development and impact measurement skills | 1,000+ individuals |
| Aspiring entrepreneurs | No clear path from idea to business launch | 10,000+ potential |
| CSEA trainers | Can only reach dozens through in-person training | 20+ trainers |
| Cooperative members | Affected by poorly-managed cooperatives | 50,000+ members |

**What are the root causes?**

1. **Geographic Barriers**: In-person training requires travel that rural participants cannot afford or undertake
2. **Time Constraints**: Learners juggle farming, fishing, family, and faith - no large time blocks available
3. **Content Gaps**: Existing training is generic (Manila-centric) or unavailable in appropriate formats
4. **Scalability Limits**: Quality trainers can only reach dozens per session; expertise doesn't scale
5. **No Learning Pathways**: Fragmented courses with no progression from beginner to competent
6. **Digital Divide**: Limited digital literacy and connectivity in target population

**What is the current situation?**

- CSEA conducts in-person training limited by budget, venue, and trainer availability
- Training reaches ~500-1,000 participants per year vs. 5,000+ who need it
- No local LMS exists for cooperative/SE development in BARMM
- YouTube and generic courses are not relevant to local context (RA 9520, FRAMES)
- Certificates from in-person training are paper-based and hard to verify
- Training Service Providers have limited access to BARMM market

## 10. Narrative Summary

### The Change We Seek

MoroAcademy aims to build the human capacity of Bangsamoro's cooperative and social enterprise sector by making quality, relevant learning accessible to everyone who needs it - regardless of where they live or how busy they are.

Today, a cooperative treasurer in a remote barangay must travel hours and spend days away from family to attend CSEA training in Cotabato. An aspiring entrepreneur in Marawi watches generic YouTube videos that don't address local regulations or market realities. CSEA trainers reach hundreds when thousands need their expertise.

We envision a future where learning happens on any phone, anytime, anywhere - in 10-minute moments between farm work and family duties. Where structured paths guide learners from complete beginner to certified leader. Where CSEA trainers reach tens of thousands through courses that live forever. Where every cooperative has access to the skills they need to thrive.

### How Change Happens

Our theory of change is built on the principle that **accessible + relevant + structured learning = capable leaders and entrepreneurs**.

**Stage 1: Platform Accessibility** - We start by building a mobile-first platform that works offline, delivers learning in bite-sized pieces, and doesn't require sophisticated digital skills. If it's not accessible, nothing else matters.

**Stage 2: Content Relevance** - CSEA trainers create foundational courses using BARMM regulations, local examples, and Filipino/local language terms. Generic content fails; local content connects.

**Stage 3: Structured Pathways** - Instead of random courses, we create clear journeys: "New Coop Officer" → "Competent Treasurer" → "Senior Financial Manager". Learners know where they are and where they're going.

**Stage 4: Completion & Certification** - Microlearning, cohort accountability, and meaningful certificates keep learners progressing. Completion rates matter more than enrollment.

**Stage 5: Knowledge Application** - Practical exercises, MoroNegosyo integration (learn to sell → sell), and on-the-job support ensure skills transfer to practice.

**Stage 6: Ecosystem Growth** - As the platform proves value, TSPs join to reach the BARMM market. More providers mean more content, more specialization, and a self-sustaining learning ecosystem.

**Stage 7: Sector Capability** - Over time, a generation of capable leaders emerges - officers who manage compliant cooperatives, entrepreneurs who launch sustainable businesses, a sector that drives regional development.

### Key Assumptions

The most critical assumptions underlying our theory:

1. **Mobile learning works in BARMM**: Despite connectivity challenges, smartphone-based learning with offline capability can reach rural learners.

2. **Microlearning is effective**: Short (5-10 minute) lessons will be completed and will result in learning, even without long class sessions.

3. **Local content matters**: BARMM-specific content (RA 9520, FRAMES, local examples) will be significantly more effective than generic business courses.

4. **Credentials motivate completion**: CSEA-recognized certificates will be valued enough to drive learners through full courses and paths.

5. **Application follows learning**: Learners will actually apply what they learn, especially with MoroNegosyo integration providing immediate application opportunity.

6. **Trainers will create content**: CSEA trainers and TSPs will use no-code tools to create quality courses without extensive technical support.

We will continuously test these assumptions through pilot programs, learner feedback, and outcome tracking.

## Integration with MoroNegosyo

### Learn → Earn Pathway

MoroAcademy and MoroNegosyo form an integrated system where learning creates capacity for earning:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                         LEARN → EARN INTEGRATION                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                              │
│   MOROACADEMY (Learning)                  MORONEGOSYO (Earning)              │
│   ──────────────────────                  ─────────────────────              │
│                                                                              │
│   [Complete "Start Your SE"]  ─────────►  [Prompt: Create Storefront]        │
│                                                                              │
│   [Complete "Product Photos"] ─────────►  [Badge: "Photo Certified"]         │
│                                                                              │
│   [Low sales detected]       ◄─────────   [Suggest: Marketing Course]        │
│                                                                              │
│   [Complete "FRAMES Course"] ─────────►   [Badge: "FRAMES Certified"]        │
│                                                                              │
│   [Certificate earned]       ─────────►   [Profile: Training Credentials]    │
│                                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

This integration creates a virtuous cycle:
- Learning improves selling capability
- Selling success motivates more learning
- Credentials build buyer trust
- Data flows between platforms for insights
