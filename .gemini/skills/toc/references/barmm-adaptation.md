# BARMM Context Adaptation for Theory of Change

This document adapts Theory of Change (ToC) methodology to the Bangsamoro development context, drawing from the 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028 and related frameworks.

## BARMM Development Landscape

### Current Context

- Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) established 2019
- Extended transition period under Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) until 2028
- 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028 guides regional development
- Focus on peace, development, genuine autonomy, and self-determination
- Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law) provides legal framework
- Multiple development programs from government, UN, NGOs, and development partners

### Vision (BDP 2023-2028)

> "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

The Bangsamoro government envisions a territory devoid of all types of conflict - one that is permanently peaceful where residents enjoy a sense of security and comfort, with just, morally upright, and forward-thinking leadership. Key elements of peace, unity, and justice remain relevant as a desired state by the end of 2028.

### Mission (BDP 2023-2028)

> "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

Principles of moral governance and genuine and meaningful autonomy remain central for the Bangsamoro government in achieving the twin goals of lasting peace and sustainable socioeconomic development. The value of inclusion through multi-stakeholder participation and effective governance through services to communities remain salient features of Bangsamoro leadership.

### Overall Goal

**Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro**

| Dimension | Description | ToC Application |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Empowered** | Improved capacity of communities as a byproduct of improved access to quality basic social services and widening of economic opportunities, caused by remarkable growth in institution building and service delivery | Capacity outcomes, service delivery indicators |

| **Progressive** | Sustained ability to optimize existing and potential resources in addressing development needs and realizing the economic and human potential of the region | Development sustainability, resource optimization |

## BDP 2023-2028 Development Framework

### Development Goals

The 2nd BDP consists of six (6) development goals that provide the outcome framework for Theory of Change:

#### Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Stable** | Ability of ministries, government offices, and authorities to carry out duties effectively and efficiently, even during emergencies and political events such as elections or changes in power | Service continuity rates, institutional resilience scores |
| **Just** | Government as primary agent of economic and social justice, exercising all powers prudently to advance public interest, ensuring leaders use powers for inclusive and pervasive growth | Policy implementation rates, equity indicators |
| **Accountable** | Institutions, leaders, and staff held accountable for use of government resources and powers, with frameworks, mechanisms, systems, and processes to recognize good performance, learn lessons, and avoid bad practices | Transparency scores, audit compliance, accountability mechanism adoption |

**ToC Application**: Governance outcomes, institutional capacity indicators, public trust measures

#### Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Equitable** | Economic gains benefitted by all with special consideration for poor and marginalized; improvements in Gini coefficient, poverty gap, and poverty severity | Income distribution metrics, poverty reduction rates |
| **Competitive** | BARMM economy enjoys comparative and competitive advantage in selected commodities, optimized through improved local and international market linkages; use of technology by key players | Market access rates, export growth, technology adoption |
| **Robust** | Sustained double-digit GDP growth; diversified and stabilized economy to increase quality job opportunities and improve access to goods and services; industry and services sectors account significant share | GDP growth rates, sector diversification indices |

**ToC Application**: Economic outcomes, livelihood indicators, market access, employment quality

#### Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Peaceful** | Lasting peace through ensuring security and intensifying peace-building efforts | Conflict incident rates, peace agreement implementation |
| **Safe** | Protecting Bangsamoro people through safety measures | Crime rates, safety perception indices |
| **Resilient** | Communities able to withstand natural calamities and human-induced disasters | Disaster preparedness scores, recovery rates |

**ToC Application**: Peace and conflict indicators, community resilience outcomes, safety metrics

#### Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Inclusive** | Services for all sectors regardless of colors, tribes, and political affiliations, ensuring no one is left behind | Service coverage rates, disaggregated access data |
| **Responsive** | Services respond to needs of all people and the Bangsamoro as a whole | Service satisfaction scores, needs assessment alignment |
| **Quality** | High standard of service delivery meeting established benchmarks | Quality assurance scores, service standard compliance |

**ToC Application**: Social service outcomes, access and quality indicators, equity measures

#### Goal 5: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved and Recognized

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Rich** | Exercising powers and authority to maintain and promote cultural wealth | Cultural program investments, heritage site preservation |
| **Diverse** | Recognizing multiple cultural traditions within Bangsamoro | Cultural representation indices, minority inclusion |
| **Preserved** | Protection of heritage and identity as mandated under Section 24, Article IX of BOL as primary cause of Bangsamoro struggle | Heritage documentation rates, cultural practice continuity |

**ToC Application**: Cultural preservation outcomes, identity indicators, heritage protection

#### Goal 6: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure

| Attribute | Description | ToC Indicators |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Strategic** | Infrastructure with broad-based and inclusive effects to industries and communities | Infrastructure utilization rates, economic multiplier effects |
| **Adequate** | Ability to meet infrastructure demands of industries and communities | Infrastructure gap reduction, demand satisfaction rates |
| **Climate-Resilient** | Infrastructure that can withstand calamities induced by climate change | Climate resilience scores, disaster damage reduction |

**ToC Application**: Infrastructure outcomes, connectivity indicators, resilience measures

## Macroeconomic Targets 2023-2028

Key targets for Theory of Change impact indicators with baseline, targets, and key assumptions:

### Table: BARMM Macroeconomic Targets

| Indicator | Baseline | Target (2028) | Key Assumptions | ToC Application |
|-----------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| GRDP Annual Average Growth Rate | 7.5% (2020-2021) | 8%-9% for six years | No sustained large-scale war; recovery from pandemic effects; no large-scale external shocks | Economic impact indicator |
| GRDP Per Capita Growth Rate | 6.4% (2020-2021) | 6%-9% annually | Economic growth rate outpaces population growth rate (2%-4%); sustained economic growth | Per capita income outcomes |
| Industry and Services Growth | Industry 7.9%, Services 6.6% | 10%-12% double-digit growth | New investments in industry and services; growth in construction, mining, wholesale, retail, real estate | Sector development outcomes |

### Critical Assumptions for Economic Targets

1. **Peace and Security**: No sustained large-scale war in the next six years; significant headways in peace process
2. **Pandemic Recovery**: Region fully recovers from COVID-19 effects; no large-scale external shocks requiring long-term lockdowns
3. **Government Pump-Priming**: Government expenditure expected to stimulate economy as sectors remain in infancy
4. **Legislative Progress**: Priority codes (Investment Code) enacted during extension period
5. **Political Stability**: Political events or changes in power will not cause overall regional instability
6. **Infrastructure Development**: National government invests in large-scale public infrastructure (airports, ports, national roads, bridges, telecommunications)

## Pillars of Moral Governance

Moral Governance is the centerpiece of BARMM leadership for 2023-2028. These 12 pillars inform Theory of Change assumptions and values:

| # | Pillar | Description | ToC Application |
|---|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Integrity** | Public trust holders should not bind themselves financially or otherwise to avoid influence on official duties | Assumption: Leaders free from conflicts of interest |
| 2 | **Accountability** | Public office holders should be open and transparent about decisions and actions, documenting decisions and limiting information only when public interest requires | Indicator: Transparent decision-making and documentation |
| 3 | **Transparency and Honesty** | Declaring private interests related to public duties; resolving conflicts to protect public interest; all public transactions made public | Assumption: Open public transactions |
| 4 | **Sense of Patriotism** | Investing in way of life, achievements, rich history, and love for country; maintaining and protecting reputation and integrity | Context: Pride in Bangsamoro achievements |
| 5 | **Striving for Excellence** | Upholding legitimacy of actions; delivering highest quality prioritizing process over results for good of all | Outcome: Quality service delivery |
| 6 | **Cultural Understanding and Tolerance** | Better understanding and awareness of socioeconomics and differences between societies; appreciating diversity | Assumption: Respect for diversity |
| 7 | **Unwavering Loyalty to God** | Public officials strictly follow rules and regulations as part of devotion | Context: Faith-based governance principles |
| 8 | **Justice** | Serving public without prejudice; equity without regard for self, family, companions, or political alliances | Outcome: Equitable service without prejudice |
| 9 | **Inclusivity** | Fostering inclusive environment welcoming and free from discrimination based on religion, ethnicity, or culture | Assumption: Welcoming environment free from discrimination |
| 10 | **Objectivity** | Making only merit-based decisions in public business (appointments, contracts, rewards); deciding solely in public interest | Assumption: Merit-based decisions |
| 11 | **Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement** | Asking community opinion through dialogue to specify needs and achieve meaningful engagement and positive change | Activity: Community consultation processes |
| 12 | **Balance between Practical Life and Moral Side** | Balancing practical and moral/ethical values in government duties without deviating from moral aspect | Context: Ethical governance framework |

## Monitoring and Evaluation Framework

### Results-Based Monitoring and Evaluation (RBME)

RBME is the overall approach adopted by BARMM, with focus on outcomes because:
- Outcomes are transformational in character
- BARMM MOAs have 100% accountability for sectoral objectives
- Physical and financial accomplishments tracked monthly as supporting indicators

### Four Dimensions of M&E

| Dimension | Description | Frequency | ToC Application |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Implementation Monitoring** | Feedback on delivery of inputs and activities in relation to outputs; tracks physical and financial accomplishments | Monthly | Output tracking |
| **Outcomes Monitoring** | Tracks changes in behavior or condition (personal, relational, cultural, structural) | Periodic | Outcome measurement |
| **Context Monitoring** | Tracks external factors affecting implementation; identifies and evaluates risks; designs mitigating measures | Ongoing | Assumption validation |
| **Program/Project Evaluation** | Assesses appropriateness, relevance, efficiency, and effectiveness | Annual, midterm, terminal | Impact evaluation |

### RBME Principles for ToC

| Principle | Description | ToC Application |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Subsidiarity** | Those closest to problem or directly involved are in strategic position to identify contexts and solutions | Participatory ToC development |
| **Evidence-based** | Claims of success or failure must be supported with data and evidence | Verification of outcomes |
| **Appreciative Inquiry** | Deliberate effort to look for positive things and strengths attributable to the initiative | Success story documentation |
| **Premium on Local Contexts** | Investments in unpacking specific and widely varied contexts to reduce attribution risks | Context-sensitive design |
| **Inclusive and Participatory** | Meaningful participation of non-state actors brings legitimacy to findings | Stakeholder engagement |
| **Reform over Tradition** | Purpose is to introduce reforms; long-standing practices can be amended if they don't advance reform agenda | Adaptive management |
| **Tight Feedback Loop** | Findings should immediately inform concerned parties for appropriate action | Learning and adaptation |

### M&E System: Regional Project Monitoring and Evaluation System (RPMES)

- **Purpose**: Scheme for M&E projects, facilitates information generation, detects implementation problems, enables remedial action
- **Database**: Kobo Toolbox - field data collection platform for challenging environments, works online and offline
- **GIS Feature**: Map of all monitored and evaluated programs with geotagging
- **Evaluation Schedule**: Annual, midterm, and terminal evaluations conducted by BEDC sectoral committees, coordinated by BPDA

## Adapting ToC for Cooperatives

### ICA Cooperative Principles in ToC

Integrate the 7 cooperative principles into Theory of Change:

| Principle | ToC Application | Indicators |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Voluntary Membership | Outcome: Increased member participation | Membership growth rate, participation rates |
| Democratic Control | Assumption: Members actively govern | AGM attendance, voting participation |
| Economic Participation | Indicator: Patronage refunds, equity growth | Patronage distribution, share capital growth |
| Autonomy | Context: Enabling regulatory environment | Regulatory compliance, independence scores |
| Education & Training | Activity: Capacity building programs | Training participation, skill improvement |
| Cooperation | Outcome: Inter-coop partnerships | Partnership agreements, federation membership |
| Community Concern | Impact: Community development outcomes | Community investment, social impact metrics |

### Cooperative ToC Pathway Example

```text
IMPACT: Improved livelihoods for member-families

LONG-TERM OUTCOMES:
├── Members earn sustainable income
├── Cooperative financially viable
└── Community economic development

MEDIUM-TERM OUTCOMES:
├── Increased product sales/services
├── Improved product quality
├── Stronger market linkages
└── Enhanced member skills

SHORT-TERM OUTCOMES:
├── Members trained in production
├── Collective marketing established
├── Quality standards adopted
└── Shared facilities operational

ACTIVITIES:
├── Skills training programs
├── Market access facilitation
├── Quality improvement initiatives
└── Infrastructure development
```

### Key Assumptions for Cooperatives

| Category | Assumptions |
|----------|-------------|
| Governance | Members actively participate in governance; leadership committed to cooperative principles |
| Market | Market demand exists for products/services; buyers accessible |
| Regulatory | Regulatory environment supports cooperatives; CSEA policies enabling |

## Adapting ToC for Social Enterprises

### Social Enterprise ToC Components

Social enterprises require dual-track outcomes:

1. **Social Impact Track**: Changes for beneficiaries
2. **Business Sustainability Track**: Financial viability

### SE ToC Pathway Example

```text
IMPACT: Reduced poverty among target communities

SOCIAL TRACK:                    BUSINESS TRACK:
├── Beneficiary income up        ├── SE financially sustainable
├── Skills transferred           ├── Revenue covers costs
├── Employment created           ├── Market position secured
└── Community empowered          └── Growth capital available

OUTCOMES:
├── Products/services delivered
├── Beneficiaries engaged
├── Quality standards met
└── Customer base established

ACTIVITIES:
├── Production/service delivery
├── Beneficiary recruitment
├── Quality management
└── Marketing and sales
```

### Shari'ah Compliance Considerations

For SEs operating in BARMM's Muslim-majority context:

**Halal Business Principles:**

| Principle | Description | ToC Application |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Permissibility | Products and services permissible under Islamic law | Product/service design |
| No Usury (Riba) | No interest in financial arrangements | Financing model |
| Ethical Sourcing | Ethical labor practices and supply chain | Supply chain activities |
| Transparency | Honest dealings in all transactions | Business practices |

**Islamic Social Finance Integration:**

| Instrument | Description | ToC Application |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Zakat | Obligatory charitable giving | Outcome/activity funding |
| Waqf | Endowment structures | Sustainability mechanism |
| Sadaqah | Voluntary charity programs | Community support activities |
| Qard Hassan | Interest-free lending | Financing activities |

**ToC Assumptions for Shari'ah Compliance:**

- Business model aligns with Islamic principles
- Community acceptance of SE activities
- Shari'ah-compliant financing available
- Religious leaders support SE approach
