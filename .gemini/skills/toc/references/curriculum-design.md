# Theory of Change Curriculum Design

## Integration with /academy

This reference provides curriculum design guidance for Theory of Change training in MoroAcademy.

## Learning Levels

### Foundational Level

**Target**: Community leaders, coop officers, SE managers, NGO staff
**Duration**: 4-8 hours (half-day to full-day workshop)
**Outcome**: Understand ToC concepts, participate in ToC development

### Intermediate Level

**Target**: Program managers, M&E officers, project coordinators
**Duration**: 16-24 hours (2-3 day workshop)
**Outcome**: Facilitate ToC development, create ToC diagrams

### Advanced Level

**Target**: Strategic planners, evaluators, consultants, trainers
**Duration**: 40+ hours (week-long or multi-week program)
**Outcome**: Lead complex ToC processes, train others, evaluate ToC quality

## Standard Course Modules

### Module 1: Introduction to Theory of Change (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Explain what Theory of Change is and its purpose
- Describe the history and origins of ToC
- Distinguish ToC from Logic Models and Logframes

**Content:**

- Definition and core concepts
- Carol Weiss and Aspen Institute origins
- Why ToC matters for social change
- Comparison with other planning tools

**Activities:**

- Case study: Successful ToC example
- Discussion: When to use ToC vs other tools
- Quick assessment: Current planning practices

### Module 2: Core Components of ToC (3 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Identify the six essential components of ToC
- Explain the role of each component
- Recognize quality criteria for ToC

**Content:**

- Long-term goals and impact
- Outcomes pathway (short, medium, long-term)
- Preconditions and necessary conditions
- Assumptions and causal logic
- Interventions and activities
- Indicators and measurement

**Activities:**

- Component identification exercise
- Quality criteria checklist review
- Mapping existing program to ToC components

### Module 3: Backward Mapping (3 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Apply backward mapping methodology
- Work from goals to preconditions
- Make assumptions explicit

**Content:**

- Backward vs forward planning
- Four-stage backward mapping process
- Identifying preconditions
- Drawing logical connections
- The value of debate and discussion

**Activities:**

- Backward mapping simulation
- Small group ToC development
- Assumption surfacing exercise

### Module 4: Developing Your ToC (4 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Facilitate a ToC development process
- Create a ToC diagram
- Write a ToC narrative

**Content:**

- NPC Ten Steps framework
- Stakeholder engagement process
- Visualization techniques
- Narrative writing guidelines

**Activities:**

- Full ToC development workshop
- Diagram creation (paper or digital)
- Peer review and feedback

### Module 5: Assumptions and Risks (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Identify different types of assumptions
- Assess assumption validity
- Plan for risks and uncertainties

**Content:**

- Causal assumptions
- Contextual assumptions
- Implementation assumptions
- Risk identification and mitigation

**Activities:**

- Assumption mapping exercise
- Risk assessment matrix
- Contingency planning

### Module 6: Indicators and Measurement (3 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Develop outcome indicators
- Design measurement approaches
- Connect ToC to M&E systems

**Content:**

- Types of indicators (outcome, process, context)
- Indicator quality criteria (SMART)
- Data collection methods
- M&E framework integration

**Activities:**

- Indicator development workshop
- Measurement planning exercise
- M&E framework alignment

### Module 7: Using ToC for Learning (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Use ToC for adaptive management
- Update ToC based on evidence
- Facilitate organizational learning

**Content:**

- Living document approach
- Regular review processes
- Evidence-based adaptation
- Learning questions development

**Activities:**

- ToC review simulation
- Learning agenda development
- Case study: ToC adaptation

### Module 8: Facilitation Skills (3 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Facilitate participatory ToC processes
- Manage stakeholder dynamics
- Handle common challenges

**Content:**

- Facilitation techniques
- Stakeholder engagement strategies
- Common pitfalls and solutions
- Virtual facilitation adaptations

**Activities:**

- Facilitation practice sessions
- Role-play challenging scenarios
- Facilitation toolkit development

## Assessment Methods

### Formative Assessments

- Component identification quizzes
- Backward mapping exercises
- Peer feedback on drafts

### Summative Assessments

- Complete ToC development (individual or group)
- ToC diagram and narrative presentation
- Facilitation demonstration

### Portfolio Components

1. Initial draft ToC for own organization/program
2. Assumption and risk analysis
3. Indicator development worksheet
4. Final ToC with diagram and narrative
5. Reflection on learning journey

## BARMM-Specific Adaptations

### Alignment with BDP 2023-2028 Development Goals

All ToC training should reference the 6 BDP Development Goals as potential impact areas:

| BDP Goal | ToC Training Application |
|----------|-------------------------|
| Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government | Governance capacity outcomes |
| Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy | Economic and livelihood outcomes |
| Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Communities | Peace-building and resilience outcomes |
| Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services | Service delivery outcomes |
| Goal 5: Cultural Preservation and Recognition | Cultural identity outcomes |
| Goal 6: Strategic, Climate-Resilient Infrastructure | Infrastructure and connectivity outcomes |

### Using BDP Development Strategies in ToC

The 8 Development Strategies provide intervention categories:

1. **Governance mechanisms** - Institutional capacity building activities
2. **Enabling environment for economy** - Market development, policy advocacy
3. **Technology and innovation** - Digital transformation activities
4. **Ecological integrity and resilience** - Environmental outcomes
5. **Peace, safety, and human rights** - Conflict sensitivity, peace-building
6. **Inclusive social services** - Service delivery, human capital
7. **Cultural mainstreaming** - Identity and heritage preservation
8. **Climate-resilient infrastructure** - Physical development outcomes

### Using BDP Macroeconomic Targets as Indicators

Example impact indicators from BDP 2023-2028:

| Target | Potential ToC Indicator |
|--------|------------------------|
| GRDP growth 8%-9% | Contribution to regional economic growth |
| Poverty incidence 20%-25% | Poverty reduction among target beneficiaries |
| Unemployment 3%-5% | Employment creation outcomes |
| Underemployment 4%-7% | Quality employment indicators |

### Moral Governance Pillars in ToC

Integrate the 12 Pillars of Moral Governance as assumptions or values:

**Key Pillars for ToC Assumptions:**
- Integrity - Leaders free from conflicts of interest
- Accountability - Transparent decision-making
- Transparency and Honesty - Open public transactions
- Inclusivity - No discrimination based on religion, ethnicity, culture
- Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement - Community consultation processes
- Justice - Equitable service without prejudice

### For Cooperatives

- Use cooperative examples throughout
- Integrate ICA principles into outcomes
- Include member participation indicators
- Connect to CSEA compliance requirements
- Align with BDP Goal 2 (Equitable, Competitive Economy)

### For Social Enterprises

- Dual-track outcomes (social + business)
- Impact measurement integration
- Shari'ah compliance considerations
- Connect to SE regulatory framework
- Align with BDP Goal 2 and Goal 4 (Economy and Social Services)

### For Development Programs

- Align with 6 BDP Development Goals as impact areas
- Use 8 BDP Development Strategies as intervention categories
- Include peace and conflict sensitivity (BDP Goal 3)
- Connect to UN SDGs through BDP alignment table
- Integrate with BPDA/BEDC M&E systems

### Cultural Considerations

- Use local examples and case studies from BARMM provinces
- Incorporate Bangsamoro success stories aligned with BDP achievements
- Respect Islamic principles and Moral Governance pillars
- Support local language facilitation (Bahasa Sug, Meranaw, Maguindanaon)
- Reference Bangsamoro vision: "United, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive"

## Facilitation Guide

### Pre-Workshop Preparation

1. Gather context about participants' organizations
2. Prepare relevant local examples
3. Create large paper templates (A0/A1 size)
4. Gather sticky notes, markers, tape
5. Set up breakout areas

### Workshop Flow

1. **Opening (10%)**: Context setting, introductions, expectations
2. **Teaching (20%)**: Brief concept introduction, examples
3. **Practice (50%)**: Hands-on ToC development
4. **Share (15%)**: Presentations, peer feedback
5. **Close (5%)**: Summary, next steps, evaluation

### Post-Workshop Follow-up

1. ToC submission for review
2. Feedback and refinement support
3. Implementation check-ins
4. Refresher sessions (6-12 months)
5. Peer learning network
