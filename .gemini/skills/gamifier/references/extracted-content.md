## Workflow: Gamification in Cooperatives

This workflow provides specialized guidance for designing gamified training, simulations, and member engagement systems specifically for cooperative contexts. It integrates Philippine Cooperative Code (RA 9520), CDA regulations, and International Cooperative Alliance (ICA) principles.

### 1. Cooperative Training Games

Design engaging games for mandatory and voluntary cooperative training programs.

#### Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS) Engagement Games

Per CDA MC 2015-09, PRS is mandatory before cooperative registration. Gamify engagement:

| Game Element | Description | Learning Objective |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Coop Principle Match** | Card matching game pairing ICA principles with real examples | Understand 7 cooperative principles |
| **Registration Quest** | Journey game through registration steps | Know CDA registration process |
| **Rights & Responsibilities Bingo** | Bingo with member rights/duties | Understand membership obligations |
| **Share Capital Calculator** | Quick math challenge on share values | Grasp capital contribution concepts |
| **Coop vs Corporation Quiz Race** | Team competition on differences | Distinguish cooperatives from corporations |

**Implementation Tips**:
- Keep games 10-15 minutes (PRS has many topics to cover)
- Use physical materials that can be reused across sessions
- Award small prizes to maintain energy during full-day PRS

#### Officer Training Simulations

For BOD, committee chairs, and management staff:

| Simulation | Duration | Key Learning |
|------------|----------|--------------|
| **Board Meeting Roleplay** | 45 min | Parliamentary procedure, decision-making |
| **Conflict Resolution Scenario** | 30 min | Handling member disputes |
| **Strategic Planning Exercise** | 60 min | Setting direction, resource allocation |
| **Audit Response Drill** | 45 min | Responding to CDA/CSEA findings |
| **Crisis Management Game** | 60 min | Delinquency spike, market disruption |

#### Financial Literacy Games for Members

Essential for member education fund (RA 9520 Art. 86):

| Game | Teaches | Format |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Budget Bingo** | Personal budgeting | Bingo with expense categories |
| **Savings Race** | Value of regular savings | Board game with compound interest |
| **Loan or No Loan** | Responsible borrowing | Decision game with consequences |
| **Interest Rate Detective** | Understanding rates | Mystery-solving calculation game |
| **Net Surplus Journey** | How profits become patronage refund | Visual flow game |

#### Cooperative Principles Learning Games

Reinforce ICA 7 Principles through interactive experiences:

| Principle | Game/Activity | Key Mechanic |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| 1. Voluntary & Open Membership | "Open Door" roleplay | Inclusion scenarios |
| 2. Democratic Member Control | "One Member, One Vote" exercise | Voting simulation |
| 3. Member Economic Participation | "Capital Builder" game | Investment/return cycles |
| 4. Autonomy & Independence | "Influence Resistance" scenario | External pressure roleplay |
| 5. Education, Training, Information | "Knowledge Chain" relay | Team teaching relay race |
| 6. Cooperation Among Cooperatives | "Coop Network" trading game | Inter-coop partnerships |
| 7. Concern for Community | "Community Impact" board game | Social project planning |

#### Compliance Training Gamification

Make regulatory compliance engaging:

| Requirement | Game Format | Engagement Hook |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| FRAMES reporting | "Report Rush" timed challenge | Beat-the-clock accuracy |
| AML/KYC procedures | "Spot the Risk" detection game | Detective-style investigation |
| Annual report submission | "Deadline Dash" calendar game | Planning and prioritization |
| Statutory fund allocation | "Fund Flow" allocation puzzle | Resource optimization |
| Good standing maintenance | "Compliance Quest" progress tracker | Achievement unlocking |

### 2. Cooperative Business Simulations

Full-scale simulations teaching cooperative operations through experience.

#### Credit Cooperative Simulation

**Objectives**: Understand savings mobilization, loan portfolio management, delinquency handling, and statutory fund allocation.

```
CREDIT COOPERATIVE SIMULATION
├── Teams: 4 departments
│   ├── Savings Department (deposits, withdrawals)
│   ├── Loans Department (applications, approvals)
│   ├── Collections Department (repayments, delinquency)
│   └── Finance Department (books, fund allocation)
├── Rounds: 6 (each = 1 month)
├── Duration: 90-120 minutes
│
├── Round Structure
│   ├── DEPOSIT PHASE (5 min)
│   │   └── Members make savings deposits
│   ├── LOAN PHASE (15 min)
│   │   ├── Process loan applications
│   │   ├── Credit committee reviews
│   │   └── Approve/deny decisions
│   ├── COLLECTION PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Receive loan repayments
│   │   ├── Handle delinquent accounts
│   │   └── Apply collection strategies
│   └── REPORTING PHASE (10 min)
│       ├── Update financial statements
│       ├── Calculate delinquency ratio
│       └── Allocate to statutory funds
│
└── Victory Conditions
    ├── Highest net surplus
    ├── Lowest delinquency rate
    └── Proper fund allocation
```

**Key Metrics to Track**:
- Total savings mobilized
- Loan portfolio size
- Delinquency ratio (target: <5%)
- Net surplus margin
- Statutory fund compliance (10% Reserve, 10% CETF, 3% CTF)

#### Marketing Cooperative Simulation

**Objectives**: Collective selling power, pricing strategies, quality standards, and market access.

```
MARKETING COOPERATIVE SIMULATION
├── Teams: 4-6 farmer groups
├── Market: Facilitated by instructor
├── Rounds: 5 (each = 1 harvest cycle)
├── Duration: 90 minutes
│
├── Round Structure
│   ├── PRODUCTION PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Each farmer group produces goods
│   │   └── Quality varies by dice roll + investment
│   ├── CONSOLIDATION PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Decide: sell individually or pool?
│   │   ├── Pooling enables bulk pricing
│   │   └── Quality grading determines price tier
│   ├── MARKET PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Market prices fluctuate
│   │   ├── Bulk sellers get premium
│   │   └── Individual sellers face price volatility
│   └── SETTLEMENT PHASE (5 min)
│       ├── Calculate revenue
│       ├── Distribute to members (if pooled)
│       └── Record-keeping
│
└── Learning Outcomes
    ├── Value of collective bargaining
    ├── Quality standards impact on pricing
    └── Risk reduction through pooling
```

#### Consumer Cooperative Simulation

**Objectives**: Bulk purchasing, member discounts, inventory management, and patronage-based benefits.

```
CONSUMER COOPERATIVE SIMULATION
├── Teams: Store operations team
├── Rounds: 4 (each = 1 quarter)
├── Duration: 60 minutes
│
├── Round Structure
│   ├── PROCUREMENT PHASE (15 min)
│   │   ├── Negotiate bulk purchases
│   │   ├── Higher volume = lower unit cost
│   │   └── Storage costs apply
│   ├── SALES PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Members purchase goods
│   │   ├── Record patronage per member
│   │   └── Non-member sales (if allowed)
│   └── PROFIT DISTRIBUTION PHASE (5 min)
│       ├── Calculate net surplus
│       ├── Allocate statutory funds
│       └── Compute patronage refund
│
└── Key Decisions
    ├── How much stock to buy?
    ├── What markup for members vs non-members?
    └── How to distribute patronage refund?
```

#### Net Surplus Allocation Exercise

Interactive exercise teaching RA 9520 Article 86 mandatory allocations:

```
NET SURPLUS ALLOCATION GAME
│
├── Given: P100,000 Net Surplus from operations
│
├── Step 1: Mandatory Allocations
│   ├── Reserve Fund (10% minimum) → P10,000
│   ├── Education & Training Fund (10% minimum) → P10,000
│   ├── Optional Reserve Fund (as bylaws) → P5,000
│   └── Community Development Fund (3% minimum) → P3,000
│
├── Step 2: Distributable Surplus
│   └── P100,000 - P28,000 = P72,000
│
├── Step 3: Interest on Share Capital
│   └── As per bylaw rate (e.g., P12,000)
│
├── Step 4: Patronage Refund
│   └── P72,000 - P12,000 = P60,000
│   └── Distribute based on patronage volume
│
└── Game Mechanic: Teams compete to correctly allocate, fastest correct answer wins
```

#### Patronage Refund Calculation Game

Make patronage computation hands-on:

```
PATRONAGE REFUND CHALLENGE
│
├── Scenario: Consumer Coop with P60,000 patronage refund pool
│
├── Member Data Cards
│   ├── Member A: P50,000 purchases (25% of total)
│   ├── Member B: P30,000 purchases (15% of total)
│   ├── Member C: P80,000 purchases (40% of total)
│   └── Member D: P40,000 purchases (20% of total)
│
├── Challenge: Calculate each member's refund
│   ├── Member A: P60,000 x 25% = P15,000
│   ├── Member B: P60,000 x 15% = P9,000
│   ├── Member C: P60,000 x 40% = P24,000
│   └── Member D: P60,000 x 20% = P12,000
│
└── Game Format: Race to calculate, verify with group
```

### 4. Cooperative-Specific Game Elements

Game mechanics that reflect cooperative values and principles.

#### Democratic Voting Mechanics

Simulate "one member, one vote" principle:

| Mechanic | Application | Learning Outcome |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Ballot Box** | Each team gets ONE vote regardless of size | Equality in decision-making |
| **Quorum Check** | Must have 25% participation to proceed | Importance of member turnout |
| **Majority Rules** | 50%+1 decides, minority recorded | Democratic process |
| **Secret Ballot** | Anonymous voting for sensitive issues | Protection of member voice |
| **Proxy Voting** | Simulate limitations per RA 9520 | Understand proxy rules |

**Implementation in Simulations**:
```
DEMOCRATIC DECISION ROUND
│
├── Present: Issue for vote (e.g., new service offering)
├── Deliberate: Teams discuss pros/cons (5 min)
├── Vote: One vote per team, secret ballot
├── Announce: Majority decision
└── Debrief: Discuss minority view, consensus-building
```

#### Collective Achievement Badges

Badges earned by the cooperative as a whole, not individuals:

| Badge | Criteria | Display |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **100% Attendance** | All teams present at GA simulation | Cooperative banner |
| **Zero Delinquency Round** | No defaults in simulation round | Team celebration |
| **Community Champion** | Completed community project | Public recognition |
| **Inter-Coop Ally** | Successfully traded with other coop team | Partnership certificate |
| **Sustainable Operations** | Maintained statutory fund compliance | Compliance seal |

**Why Collective Badges Work**:
- Reinforces "together we achieve" cooperative mindset
- Reduces unhealthy individual competition
- Builds team identity and solidarity
- Mirrors real cooperative success metrics

### 5. Debriefing for Cooperative Learning

Specialized debriefing that connects game experiences to cooperative realities.

#### Connecting Game Outcomes to Cooperative Principles

Structure debrief around ICA 7 Principles:

| Principle | Debrief Questions |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Voluntary & Open | "Did everyone feel free to participate? Were there barriers?" |
| Democratic Control | "How were decisions made? Did everyone have equal voice?" |
| Economic Participation | "Was risk/reward fairly distributed? Did contribution match return?" |
| Autonomy | "Did external factors (market, competition) affect your choices? How did you maintain independence?" |
| Education | "What did you learn? How will you share this with others?" |
| Cooperation | "Did you help other teams? Could cooperation have improved outcomes?" |
| Community | "Did your decisions consider broader community impact?" |

#### Reflecting on Democratic Decision-Making

After simulations involving voting or group decisions:

```
DEMOCRATIC DEBRIEF FRAMEWORK
│
├── PROCESS REFLECTION
│   ├── "How did you reach your decision?"
│   ├── "Did everyone contribute to discussion?"
│   ├── "How was dissent handled?"
│   └── "Did the process feel fair?"
│
├── OUTCOME ANALYSIS
│   ├── "Was the decision effective?"
│   ├── "What would you change about the process?"
│   └── "How does this compare to your real coop?"
│
└── TRANSFER
    ├── "What will you bring to your next GA?"
    ├── "How can you encourage more participation?"
    └── "What governance improvements does your coop need?"
```

#### Linking to RA 9520 Requirements

Connect simulation experiences to legal requirements:

| Simulation Event | RA 9520 Connection | Debrief Point |
|------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Net surplus distribution | Art. 86 mandatory allocations | "Did you follow the legal minimums?" |
| Loan approval process | Art. 60 credit committees | "Who should approve loans in real life?" |
| GA voting | Art. 39 quorum requirements | "What if quorum wasn't met?" |
| Membership acceptance | Art. 26 qualifications | "Can you refuse a member? When?" |
| Officer election | Art. 37 BOD composition | "What qualifications matter?" |

#### Transferring Learning to Real Cooperative Operations

Ensure game lessons translate to workplace:

```
TRANSFER FRAMEWORK
│
├── IMMEDIATE APPLICATION
│   ├── "What will you do Monday morning?"

├── SYSTEMIC IMPROVEMENT

#### Scenario 2: Creating a Credit Cooperative Business Simulation

**Request**: "Design a simulation where officers experience running a credit cooperative, including handling delinquency."

**Full Simulation Design**:

```
CREDIT COOP CHALLENGE
│
├── OVERVIEW
│   ├── Participants: 16-24 (teams of 4-6)
│   ├── Teams: 4 credit cooperatives competing
│   ├── Duration: 2 hours
│   └── Objective: Highest net surplus with lowest delinquency
│
├── STARTING RESOURCES (per team)
│   ├── Share Capital: P500,000 (play money)
│   ├── Savings Deposits: P1,000,000
│   ├── Loan Portfolio: P800,000 (existing loans)
│   ├── Reserve Fund: P100,000
│   └── Delinquency: 5% (P40,000 at risk)
│
├── TEAM ROLES
│   ├── General Manager: Overall strategy, final decisions
│   ├── Loans Officer: Process applications, recommend approval
│   ├── Credit Committee: Approve/deny loans (3 members)
│   └── Treasurer: Track finances, report to facilitator
│
├── ROUND STRUCTURE (6 rounds = 6 months)
│   │
│   ├── ROUND START: Facilitator distributes
│   │   ├── New savings deposits (random: P50K-P200K)
│   │   ├── Loan applications (5-10 per round)
│   │   └── Collection reports (who paid, who didn't)
│   │
│   ├── LENDING PHASE (15 min)
│   │   ├── Review loan applications (cards with borrower profiles)
│   │   ├── Credit committee votes: Approve/Modify/Deny
│   │   ├── Set interest rate (within allowed range)
│   │   └── Record loans granted
│   │
│   ├── COLLECTION PHASE (10 min)
│   │   ├── Facilitator reveals which loans paid
│   │   ├── Delinquent accounts: Choose strategy
│   │   │   ├── Restructure (lower payment, extend term)
│   │   │   ├── Intensify collection (cost: P5,000)
│   │   │   └── Write off (reduce portfolio, impact surplus)
│   │   └── Update delinquency ratio
│   │
│   └── REPORTING PHASE (5 min)
│       ├── Calculate interest income
│       ├── Deduct operating costs (fixed: P50,000/round)
│       ├── Compute net surplus for round
│       └── Report to facilitator
│
├── RANDOM EVENTS (one per round)
│   ├── Round 2: Economic downturn (delinquency +5%)
│   ├── Round 3: New competitor coop (savings may leave)
│   ├── Round 4: CDA audit (must show proper records)
│   ├── Round 5: Loan demand spike (opportunity or risk?)
│   └── Round 6: Member emergency (large withdrawal request)
│
├── VICTORY CONDITIONS
│   ├── Primary: Highest cumulative net surplus
│   ├── Secondary: Lowest delinquency ratio
│   └── Bonus: Best CDA audit score (record-keeping)
│
└── DEBRIEF QUESTIONS
    ├── "What made loan approval decisions hard?"
    ├── "How did you balance growth vs. risk?"
    ├── "What strategies worked for collection?"
    ├── "How would you prepare for economic downturns?"
    └── "What will you change in your real coop?"
```

**Materials**:
- Play money (denominations: P1K, P5K, P10K, P50K)
- Loan application cards (30-50 borrower profiles)
- Financial tracking sheets
- Delinquency tracking board
- Random event cards
- Timer/bell for phase changes

│   │   ├── 1000 points: Extra patronage refund (1%)
│   │   └── 2000 points: Leadership program nomination
│   │
│   ├── GA-SPECIFIC PRIZES
│   │   ├── Top 3 point earners: Special recognition + gift
│   │   ├── Random draw (all participants): Major prize
│   │   └── Bingo completers: Small gift
│   │
│   └── COLLECTIVE REWARDS
│       ├── 80% attendance: Extra community fund allocation
│       ├── 90% attendance: Member appreciation event
│       └── 100% attendance: Major celebration + media coverage
│
└── IMPLEMENTATION TECHNOLOGY
    ├── Mobile app or web portal for point tracking
    ├── QR codes for check-in/out
    ├── SMS notifications for point updates
    ├── Digital leaderboard (opt-in display)
    └── Integration with member management system
```

**Key Success Factors**:
- Make rewards meaningful but not excessive
- Emphasize collective achievements (cooperative spirit)
- Ensure accessibility (not everyone has smartphones)
- Celebrate participation, not just winners
- Connect to cooperative values in all communications

## Simulation Design Framework

### Structure Template

```
SIMULATION: [Name]
├── Teams: [Number and types]
├── Rounds: [3-5 rounds typical]
├── Duration: [60-120 minutes]
│
└── Round Structure
    ├── INJECTION PHASE (5 min)
    │   └── New resources/opportunities enter
    ├── ACTION PHASE (15-30 min)
    │   ├── Trading between teams
    │   └── Production decisions
    ├── SETTLEMENT PHASE (5 min)
    │   └── Loan repayments, costs deducted
    └── RECORD-KEEPING PHASE (10 min)
        └── Update financial statements
```

### Economy Design

Define the **flow of value**:

```
[Source] ──► [Actor 1] ──► [Actor 2] ──► [Sink]
                 │              │
                 └──────────────┘
                 (circular flow)
```

**Cooperative Economy Example**:
```
Market Injection ──► Consumer Coop ──► Producer Coops ──► Service Coop
       │                                                        │
       └────────────────── Loan Repayment ◄────────────────────┘
```

### Team Role Template

| Team | Role | Starting Resources | Wins By |
|------|------|-------------------|---------|
| Agricultural Coop | Produces raw goods | Seeds, land tokens | Selling crops |
| Producers Coop | Transforms goods | Processing capacity | Value-added sales |
| Consumer Coop | Retail to market | Store location | Customer sales |
| Service Coop | Provides services | Expertise tokens | Service fees |

### Difficulty Progression (Flow)

```
Round 1: Introduction (Low Challenge)
├── Simple transactions
├── Clear instructions
└── Facilitator guidance

Round 2-3: Building (Medium Challenge)
├── Multiple trading options
├── Strategic decisions emerge
└── Less facilitator help

Round 4-5: Mastery (High Challenge)
├── Market disruptions
├── Complex multi-party deals
└── Time pressure
```
