## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal invites you to champion the people's stake in BARMM's digital governance transformation. As a civil society leader who has transitioned to parliamentary service, you understand that government exists to serve citizens—and that effective representation and rigorous oversight are the mechanisms through which government remains accountable to the people.

The proposed e-Governance Portal provides you with enhanced Representation and Oversight tools—the platform capabilities most aligned with your civil society mission. These modules ensure that constituent voices are heard and that government operations remain transparent and accountable.

With an initial partnership investment of PHP 250,000 per month over six months, you become a founding architect of a system that embeds citizen-centered accountability into BARMM's digital infrastructure.

Your voice in shaping this platform ensures it serves the real needs of constituents and strengthens the oversight mechanisms that protect public interest.

## 2. e-Governance and Its Value to the Bangsamoro Government

### 2.1 The Civil Society Vision for Governance

Civil society organizations exist to bridge the gap between government and citizens. They advocate for transparency, demand accountability, and ensure that government serves public—not private—interests. Your transition to Parliament brings this civil society perspective into the heart of governance.

Digital governance amplifies these civil society values by making government operations visible, constituent engagement trackable, and accountability enforceable through transparent systems.

### 2.2 Case Study: Estonia's Citizen-Centered Digital Government

Estonia's digital transformation placed citizens at the center:

| Achievement | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **99% of public services online** | Citizens access government services 24/7 |
| **Real-time transparency** | Citizens see how their taxes are spent |
| **Citizen service dashboards** | Every request tracked, status visible |

| **85% citizen trust in digital government** | Transparency builds public confidence |

Estonia's model demonstrates that digital governance, designed with citizen interests at the center, strengthens rather than replaces the accountability role of civil society.

### 2.4 The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254)

On September 5, 2025, President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. signed Republic Act 12254, the **E-Governance Act**, institutionalizing the Philippines' transition to digital governance. This landmark legislation provides both the mandate and framework for BARMM's digital transformation.

#### Key Provisions

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Digital-First Mandate** | All government services must transition to digital platforms |
| **Chief Information Officers** | Every agency must designate a CIO to lead digital transformation |
| **E-Government Master Plan** | DICT must formulate comprehensive digital transformation blueprint |
| **Interoperability Framework** | Agencies must enable secure data sharing through APIs |
| **Cybersecurity Standards** | Mandatory security assessments and CERT establishment |

#### Compliance Timeline

| Timeline | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **1 Year** | All cities and municipalities must establish digital portals or adopt eLGU system |
| **1 Year** | Government must complete transition to e-governance |
| **3 Years** | E-Government Master Plan reviewed and upgraded |
| **Annually** | E-Government maturity surveys and compliance reports |

#### E-Government Programs Mandated

1. **Citizen Frontline Delivery Services Platform** - Unified citizen service access
2. **Electronic Local Government Unit (eLGU) System** - Standardized LGU digital services
3. **Government Digital Payment Systems** - Integrated e-payment gateway
4. **Government Public Key Infrastructure** - Secure digital signatures
5. **Online Public Service Portal** - Single window for government services
6. **Philippine Digital Health System** - Integrated health information

#### Implications for BARMM

RA 12254 creates both **obligation and opportunity** for BARMM:

- **Compliance Requirement**: BARMM ministries and LGUs must meet digital governance standards
- **Funding Access**: Access to E-Government Interoperability Fund for implementation
- **Technical Support**: DICT cooperation for digital transformation roadmap
- **Leadership Opportunity**: Early adoption positions BARMM as compliance leader

The law addresses a critical gap—DICT now has authority to enforce digital integration across all government agencies, making digital transformation not optional but mandatory.
