# ADDRESS IT — Completed Staff Work (CSW) Process Framework

The user's proprietary 7-step CSW methodology for BARMM. This is the **canonical CSW process** for all guidebooks that cover Complete Staff Work — whether for the Bangsamoro Parliament, Ministries, Offices, or Agencies.

**Source**: CSW Training Improvement Plan by Saidamen R. Mambayao

---

## Overview: The ADDRESS IT Mnemonic

```
A  — Step 1: Analyze the Context
DD — Step 2: Define and Deliberate the Problem or Need
R  — Step 3: Research Needed Information
E  — Step 4: Explore Options
SS — Step 5: Submit Proposal or Report
     Step 6: Sustain Efforts to Implement the Solution
IT — Step 7: Iterate and Improve the Solution
```

The CSW process is cyclical — Step 7 can loop back to Step 1 when circumstances change or new problems emerge.

---

## Step 1: Analyze the Context

The staff member thoroughly examines the context surrounding the assigned issue or task. This step lays the foundation for all subsequent steps.

### 1.1 Understand the Assignment
- Clearly identify the issue or task assigned by the superior or decision-maker
- Determine the purpose, objectives, and desired outcomes
- Clarify any ambiguities or uncertainties with the superior

### 1.2 Assess the Office's Function and Staff's Role
- Examine the function, mandate, and responsibilities of the office or department
- Identify the specific role, duties, and responsibilities of the staff member undertaking the CSW
- Ensure alignment between the assignment and the office's function

### 1.3 Identify Stakeholders
- Determine all relevant stakeholders affected by or interested in the issue
- Categorize into primary (directly affected) and secondary (indirectly affected) groups
- Analyze stakeholders' interests, concerns, expectations, and perspectives
- Consider both internal (within organization) and external stakeholders

### 1.4 Examine Internal and External Factors
- **Internal factors**: organizational structure, culture, values, available resources (human, financial, technological)
- **External factors** (PESTEL):
  - Political (government policies, political climate)
  - Economic (market conditions, funding availability, budget constraints)
  - Social (demographic trends, public opinion, media attention)
  - Technological (advancements, disruptions, digital infrastructure)
  - Environmental (sustainability, ecological considerations)

### 1.5 Review Relevant Laws, Policies, and Regulations
- Identify and compile all relevant laws, policies, guidelines, and regulations:
  - External laws and regulations (BOL, BAAs, national laws)
  - Internal organizational policies, procedures, and guidelines
- Examine the implications and requirements of these legal and regulatory frameworks
- Ensure CSW process aligns with and adheres to applicable laws

### 1.6 Gather Preliminary Data and Information
- Collect initial data and information from readily available sources
- Identify key documents, reports, studies, and expert opinions
- Conduct preliminary research to establish a basic understanding
- Document and organize gathered data for easy reference in subsequent steps

## Step 2: Define and Deliberate the Problem or Need

Clearly define the problem or need based on the contextual analysis from Step 1. Deliberation involves engaging stakeholders to gain comprehensive understanding.

### 2.1 Articulate the Problem or Need
- Clearly state the problem or need based on contextual analysis
- Describe the current situation, challenges, or opportunities
- Identify the gaps between current state and desired state
- Ensure the problem statement is specific, measurable, and aligned with objectives

### 2.2 Engage Stakeholders in Deliberation
- Identify key stakeholders with valuable insights, expertise, or decision-making authority
- Conduct stakeholder consultations, interviews, or focus group discussions
- Encourage open and inclusive dialogue
- Document stakeholder inputs and incorporate feedback

### 2.3 Identify Root Causes
- Analyze the underlying factors contributing to the problem
- Use problem-solving techniques: root cause analysis, fishbone diagrams, "5 Whys" method
- Distinguish between symptoms and root causes to ensure CSW addresses fundamental issues

### 2.4 Assess Potential Consequences
- Evaluate potential consequences and impacts if the problem is left unaddressed
- Consider short-term and long-term implications on stakeholders, the organization, and broader context
- Identify risks, challenges, and opportunities associated with addressing or not addressing the problem

### 2.5 Establish Scope and Objectives
- Define the scope of the CSW effort — clearly outline what is within and outside boundaries
- Establish SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Ensure objectives align with the assignment's purpose and organizational goals

### 2.6 Document the Problem or Need Statement
- Develop a clear and concise problem or need statement
- Ensure it is well-defined, unambiguous, and easily understandable by all stakeholders
- Seek validation and consensus from relevant stakeholders
- Use as a reference point throughout the CSW process

---

## Step 3: Research Needed Information

Conduct thorough research to gather necessary information and data related to the defined problem. Findings inform the development of potential solutions.

### 3.1 Develop a Research Plan
- Identify specific information and data requirements
- Determine scope and depth of research needed
- Outline research questions, hypotheses, or areas of inquiry
- Develop structured plan with timelines, resources, and responsibilities

### 3.2 Identify Relevant Information Sources
- Government databases, reports, and publications
- Academic journals, research papers, and studies
- Industry reports, case studies, and best practices
- Expert opinions, interviews, and consultations
- Stakeholder inputs and feedback
- Assess credibility, reliability, and relevance of each source

### 3.3 Conduct Literature Review
- Systematically review existing literature, documents, and publications
- Analyze and synthesize key findings, theories, and frameworks
- Identify gaps, inconsistencies, or conflicting information
- Document literature review findings and insights

### 3.4 Gather Primary Data
- Determine if primary data collection is necessary to fill information gaps
- Select appropriate methods (surveys, interviews, focus groups, observations)
- Design collection instruments, protocols, and sampling strategies
- Conduct collection adhering to ethical guidelines and data privacy regulations

### 3.5 Analyze and Synthesize Information
- Organize and categorize gathered information and data
- Employ appropriate analysis techniques (statistical, content, thematic)
- Identify patterns, trends, and relationships
- Synthesize findings and draw meaningful insights and conclusions

### 3.6 Identify Best Practices and Potential Solutions
- Examine research findings for best practices, case studies, or successful approaches
- Analyze applicability and feasibility of adapting best practices to current context
- Brainstorm potential solutions based on research findings and insights
- Document best practices and potential solutions for further exploration

### 3.7 Document Research Findings
- Prepare comprehensive research report with key findings, insights, and conclusions
- Include section on identified best practices and potential solutions
- Use visual aids (graphs, charts, tables) to present data effectively
- Provide references and citations for all sources

### 3.8 Validate and Refine Findings
- Share findings with relevant stakeholders and subject matter experts for validation
- Incorporate stakeholder inputs and refine findings as necessary
- Identify limitations, assumptions, or uncertainties
- Document the validation process and any revisions

---

## Step 4: Explore Options

Explore and evaluate various options or solutions to address the defined problem. Options should be based on research findings from Step 3.

### 4.1 Review Research Findings
- Revisit research findings, insights, and conclusions from Step 3
- Analyze applicability and relevance to the current problem
- Identify key insights that can inform option generation

### 4.2 Generate Options
- Conduct brainstorming sessions or ideation workshops
- Encourage creative thinking and explore a wide range of possibilities
- Engage relevant stakeholders and subject matter experts
- Document all generated options with key features, benefits, and potential challenges

### 4.3 Evaluate and Refine Options
- Develop evaluation criteria based on problem statement, objectives, and stakeholder requirements
- Assess each option against criteria considering:
  - **Feasibility**: Is it practical and implementable within constraints?
  - **Effectiveness**: To what extent does it address the problem?
  - **Efficiency**: Does it make optimal use of resources (time, budget, personnel)?
  - **Sustainability**: Is it viable long-term and adaptable to future changes?
  - **Alignment**: Does it align with organizational goals, values, and priorities?
- Refine options based on evaluation — combine or modify as necessary

### 4.4 Conduct Impact Analysis
- Assess potential impact of each refined option on stakeholders, organization, and broader context
- Identify benefits, risks, and unintended consequences
- Analyze short-term and long-term implications
- Document impact analysis findings for each option

### 4.5 Perform Cost-Benefit Analysis
- Estimate costs (direct and indirect) for each option
- Identify quantifiable and non-quantifiable benefits
- Conduct cost-benefit analysis to assess value and ROI
- Consider time horizon and discount future costs/benefits as appropriate

### 4.6 Prioritize Options
- Based on evaluation, impact analysis, and cost-benefit analysis, prioritize options
- Rank by overall desirability, feasibility, and potential impact
- Consider stakeholder preferences and priorities
- Document the prioritized list with rationale for ranking

### 4.7 Validate and Refine Options
- Present prioritized options to stakeholders for validation and feedback
- Engage in discussions to gather insights, concerns, and suggestions
- Refine based on stakeholder input, addressing gaps or challenges
- Document validation process and revisions

### 4.8 Prepare for Recommendation
- Select the most promising option(s) for recommendation
- Develop clear and concise rationale for the selected option(s)
- Gather supporting evidence and arguments to justify the recommendation
- Prepare to present to decision-maker(s) in Step 5

---

## Step 5: Submit Proposal or Report

Prepare and submit a comprehensive proposal or report to the decision-maker(s), after obtaining review and clearance/endorsement from the supervisor.

### 5.1 Develop the Proposal or Report Structure
- Determine appropriate format and structure based on organizational guidelines and decision-maker preferences
- Typical structure: executive summary, introduction, problem statement, research findings, options explored, recommended solution(s), implementation plan, conclusion
- Ensure logical, coherent, and easy-to-navigate structure

### 5.2 Write the Executive Summary
- Craft concise summary capturing key points: problem, recommended solution(s), expected benefits
- Limit to one or two paragraphs as a high-level overview

### 5.3 Provide Background and Context
- Relevant background information and context for the problem
- Significance of addressing the issue and alignment with organizational goals
- Brief summary of the CSW process undertaken

### 5.4 Present the Problem Statement and Research Findings
- Clearly state the problem or need
- Summarize key findings from research including relevant data, insights, and best practices
- Use visual aids (graphs, charts, tables) effectively

### 5.5 Discuss the Options Explored
- Describe options/solutions explored with key features, benefits, and challenges
- Present evaluation criteria and results of impact and cost-benefit analyses
- Explain rationale behind prioritization and selection of recommended option(s)

### 5.6 Present the Recommended Solution(s)
- Clearly state recommended solution(s) with detailed description
- Explain how it addresses the problem and aligns with organizational goals
- Provide supporting evidence and arguments including expected benefits and outcomes

### 5.7 Develop an Implementation Plan
- Detailed implementation plan: specific actions, timelines, resources, roles, responsibilities, milestones
- Address potential risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Discuss monitoring and evaluation mechanisms

### 5.8 Conclude with a Call to Action
- Summarize key points and reiterate importance of addressing the problem
- Emphasize benefits and potential consequences of inaction
- Clear call to action urging decision-maker(s) to approve and support implementation

### 5.9 Review and Refine
- Carefully review and proofread for clarity, coherence, and accuracy
- Seek feedback from colleagues or mentors
- Refine based on feedback to meet required standards

### 5.10 Obtain Supervisor Review and Clearance/Endorsement
- Submit to supervisor for review and clearance/endorsement
- Engage in discussions to address concerns, questions, or suggestions
- Make necessary revisions and obtain formal clearance/endorsement

### 5.11 Submit to Decision-Maker(s)
- After supervisor clearance, formally submit through appropriate channels
- Be prepared to present in person and answer questions
- Follow up to ensure receipt and address additional requirements

---

## Step 6: Sustain Efforts to Implement the Solution

Once the solution is approved, maintain momentum through resource mobilization, capacity building, progress monitoring, and stakeholder engagement.

### 6.1 Keep Seeking Approval
- Continue to seek and maintain support of decision-makers and stakeholders
- Regularly update them on implementation progress
- Address concerns or questions that arise

### 6.2 Mobilize Resources
- Mobilize necessary resources: personnel, budget, and infrastructure
- Coordinate with relevant departments and teams for efficient resource allocation

### 6.3 Develop Action Plans
- Develop detailed action plans with steps, responsibilities, and timelines
- Clearly communicate to all involved parties
- Ensure everyone understands their roles and expectations

### 6.4 Provide Training and Capacity Building
- Ensure all stakeholders involved have the necessary skills and knowledge
- Offer ongoing support and guidance to facilitate effective implementation

### 6.5 Monitor Progress
- Regularly monitor implementation progress tracking key performance indicators and milestones
- Identify and address challenges or obstacles

### 6.6 Engage Stakeholders
- Engage stakeholders throughout implementation and maintain their support
- Provide regular updates, seek feedback, and address concerns

---

## Step 7: Iterate and Improve the Solution

Continuously monitor and evaluate the implemented solution, collecting feedback and refining as needed. The CSW process is cyclical — restart from Step 1 if fundamental reassessment is needed.

### 7.1 Monitor and Evaluate
- Continuously monitor and evaluate effectiveness, efficiency, and impact
- Establish clear metrics and KPIs to measure success

### 7.2 Collect Feedback
- Collect feedback from stakeholders, end-users, customers, and partners
- Conduct surveys, interviews, or focus groups for insights and suggestions

### 7.3 Analyze Performance Data
- Regularly analyze performance data against established metrics and targets
- Identify trends, patterns, and areas excelling or falling short

### 7.4 Identify Areas for Improvement
- Based on monitoring, evaluation, and feedback, identify specific areas for improvement
- Prioritize based on potential impact and alignment with organizational goals

### 7.5 Iterate and Refine
- Develop and implement targeted improvements and refinements
- Continuously iterate to enhance effectiveness, efficiency, and user experience

### 7.6 Document Lessons Learned
- Document lessons learned, best practices, and challenges encountered
- Share insights with relevant stakeholders to promote knowledge sharing

### 7.7 Foster Continuous Improvement
- Cultivate a culture of continuous improvement, encouraging ongoing learning, innovation, and adaptability
- Recognize contributions from stakeholders and team members

### 7.8 Restart CSW Process if Necessary
- If monitoring reveals significant challenges or changes in circumstances requiring fundamental reassessment, restart from Step 1
- Adapt the CSW approach as needed to address emerging problems or changing requirements
