# MOP Formulation Methodology

Authoritative guide for creating Manuals of Operations for BARMM entities.
Source: "Manual on How to Formulate and Implement a MOP for BARMM Ministries and Offices" by Saidamen R. Mambayao (©SRM)

When /guidebook-writer receives a MOP-type guidebook request, read this file to follow the formulation methodology rather than improvising the chapter structure.

---

## The 7-Phase MOP Formulation Process

### Phase I: Introduction
- Purpose: standardize MOP creation across all BARMM entities
- 6 specific objectives (uniform format, document core functions, ensure compliance, promote transparency, facilitate onboarding, support continuous improvement)
- Scope: all ministries, offices, agencies, instrumentalities of BARMM
- Compliance required — exceptions must be justified and documented

### Phase II: Planning and Preparation
A. Identifying the need (5 triggers: lack of standardization, inefficiencies, new programs, legal requirements, knowledge transfer)
B. Forming the MOP Development Team (6 roles):
   1. Team Leader — senior official with comprehensive ops understanding
   2. Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) — experienced staff from various departments
   3. Quality Assurance Specialist — ensures standards compliance
   4. Technical Writer — clear, structured documentation
   5. Legal Expert — compliance with BOL, BAAs, BARMM regulations
   6. Graphic Designer — visual aids, flowcharts, formatting
   Team selection criteria: expertise, availability, collaboration ability
C. Conducting a Needs Assessment (6 steps):
   1. Identify stakeholders
   2. Define scope
   3. Gather data (surveys, interviews, focus groups, document review)
   4. Analyze data — identify patterns and gaps
   5. Prioritize needs — by impact, compliance, resources
   6. Document findings
D. Defining Objectives and Scope
   - Objectives: standardize processes, provide clear guidelines, ensure compliance, facilitate onboarding, support continuous improvement
   - Scope: org structure, core functions, policies/guidelines, stakeholder engagement, performance M&E
E. Creating a Project Plan (7 components):
   1. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
   2. Resource Allocation
   3. Timeline with milestones
   4. Budget
   5. Risk Management
   6. Quality Assurance process
   7. Communication Plan

### Phase III: Information Gathering
A. Reviewing existing policies, procedures, processes (8 steps: collect documents, identify key processes, map current processes, analyze policies, identify gaps, evaluate effectiveness, engage stakeholders, document findings)
B. Conducting stakeholder interviews (7 steps: identify stakeholders, develop interview guide, schedule, conduct, analyze data, follow up, maintain confidentiality)
C. Observing current operations and workflows (6 steps with 3 techniques: shadowing, time/motion studies, process mapping)
D. Researching best practices and industry standards (7 steps: identify key areas, literature review, professional associations, benchmarking, expert consultation, analyze and adapt, incorporate)
E. Analyzing collected data for gaps (8 steps: organize data, triangulate sources, identify strengths, identify gaps, analyze root causes using 5 Whys/fishbone, prioritize improvements, develop recommendations, validate findings)

### Phase IV: Drafting the Manual
A. Structure and format:
   - Common sections: Introduction, Organizational Structure, Policies/Guidelines, Procedures, Forms/Templates, References
   - Table of contents with page numbers
   - Consistent numbering system (decimal or outline)
   - Headers and footers (office name, manual title, section, page, revision date)
   - Typography: clear readable font, consistent heading sizes, bold/italic for emphasis
   - White space for readability
B. Writing procedures: clear, concise, step-by-step
C. Visual aids: flowcharts, diagrams, process maps
D. Consistency in language and terminology

### Phase V: Review and Approval
A. Circulate draft for feedback
B. Incorporate feedback and revise
C. Obtain approval from relevant authorities
D. Finalize the manual

### Phase VI: Implementation and Training
A. Develop implementation plan
B. Communicate to all relevant staff
C. Conduct training sessions
D. Provide ongoing support

### Phase VII: Monitoring and Evaluation
A. Establish metrics and KPIs
B. Regularly assess effectiveness
C. Gather feedback from users
D. Update and revise as needed

## MOP-Specific Chapter Structure (Recommended)

When creating a MOP using /guidebook-writer, use this structure instead of the generic chapter template:

1. **Introduction** — Purpose, scope, legal basis, definitions
2. **Organizational Profile** — Mandate, vision/mission, organizational structure, staffing
3. **Core Functions and Processes** — Major output categories with detailed procedures
4. **Approval Hierarchies** — Decision-making chain, delegation of authority
5. **Standard Operating Procedures** — Step-by-step for each key process
6. **Forms and Templates** — Standardized documents with instructions
7. **Policies and Guidelines** — Internal rules, compliance requirements
8. **Quality Standards** — KPIs, performance metrics, review criteria
9. **Staff Development** — Onboarding plan, training requirements, competency standards
10. **Review and Continuous Improvement** — Amendment procedures, feedback mechanisms, revision schedule
