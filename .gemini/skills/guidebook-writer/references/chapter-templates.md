## Policy Recommendations Guidebook

**Composing skill**: `/policy-recommendation`
**Audience**: MOA policy units, Parliament committee staff, consultants

### Recommended Chapters (Standard: 8-10 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — Purpose, audience, how to use this guide
2. **The Policy Cycle in BARMM** — Problem identification → analysis → formulation → adoption → implementation → evaluation. Contextualizing the national policy cycle within BARMM's autonomous governance structure.
3. **Understanding the Policy Environment** — BOL framework, BDP 2023-2028 goals, MOA mandates, stakeholder mapping. How to scan the policy environment before drafting.
4. **Evidence-Based Policy Analysis** — Research methods, data sources (PSA-BARMM, DSWD, implementing agencies), needs assessment frameworks. How to build the evidence base.
5. **Structuring a Policy Recommendation** — The 4-section template: Context & Rationale, Policy Framework, Implementation Structure, Management Framework. Executive summary written last.
6. **Legal Foundations and Compliance** — Aligning recommendations with BOL, BAAs, national laws. Shari'ah considerations. Avoiding ultra vires recommendations.
7. **Budgetary and Financial Analysis** — Costing methodology, funding sources (block grant, special development fund, GAA), sustainability planning. BAA 84 budget system.
8. **Stakeholder Consultation and Validation** — Consultation protocols, community engagement (especially for indigenous communities and OBCs), interagency coordination.
9. **Quality Review and Approval** — Internal review checklist, peer review process, approval chain (varies by MOA), transmittal procedures.
10. **From Recommendation to Action** — Implementation monitoring, M&E framework, feedback loops, reporting to Parliament committees.

## Speech Writing Guidebook

**Composing skill**: `/speech-writer`
**Audience**: Parliament staff (MPs' legislative staff), executive assistants, communications officers

### Recommended Chapters (Standard: 8-10 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — Purpose, audience, the role of speech in Bangsamoro governance
2. **Understanding Your Speaker** — Profiling the MP or executive, aligning with their legislative agenda, voice and style preferences
3. **Speech Types in the Bangsamoro Parliament** — Authorship speech, privilege speech, manifestation, 1-minute vote, interpellation, opening/keynote. Structure, length, and tone for each.
4. **Speech Types for MOA Executives** — Keynote addresses, program launches, budget defense presentations, interagency forums, community dialogues
5. **The Writing Process** — Research → outline → draft → review → rehearsal. Working with the speaker's calendar and agenda.
6. **Language and Cultural Protocols** — Islamic greetings (Arabic + transliteration), Tagalog/Taglish conventions, honorifics (MP, Minister, Chief Minister), cultural sensitivity across 13 ethnolinguistic groups
7. **Rhetorical Techniques for Bangsamoro Governance** — Connecting to core themes: peace, self-determination, moral governance, inclusive development, Bangsamoro identity. Using narrative, data, and emotional appeal.
8. **Speeches for Special Occasions** — Budget deliberations, commemorative events (Bangsamoro Day, Eid celebrations), crisis communication, international engagements
9. **From Speech to Public Communication** — Social media adaptation, press releases, official messages. Multi-channel communication strategy.
10. **Quality Standards and Review** — Review checklist, fact-checking names and titles, ensuring Shari'ah compliance, speaker approval workflow

## Legislative Briefer (CSW) Guidebook

**Composing skill**: `/legislative-briefer`
**Audience**: Parliament staff (committee secretariat, PRLS), legislative researchers

### Recommended Chapters (Standard: 10-12 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — The CSW tradition in governance, purpose of legislative briefers
2. **The 13-Section CSW Format** — Overview of all sections, how they interconnect, which sections are analytical vs. speech-oriented
3. **Section I-II: Overview and Brief Description** — Summarizing the measure, identifying key dates, bill reference formatting
4. **Section III-V: Proceedings, Implementing Agencies, Implementation** — Committee history, agency identification, phased implementation planning
5. **Section VI: Budgetary and Financial Implications** — Cost analysis methodology, revenue impact, block grant considerations, BAA 84 compliance
6. **Section VII: Shari'ah Analysis** — Islamic jurisprudence framework, when Shari'ah analysis applies, sources of Shari'ah opinion
7. **Section VIII: Gender Analysis** — Gender impact assessment, GAD compliance, PCW framework adaptation for BARMM
8. **Section IX: Brief Speech** — Writing authorship and support speeches in Tagalog/Taglish for plenary delivery
9. **Section X: Parliamentary Strategy and Interpellation** — The INTRODUCTION → QUESTION → ANTICIPATED ANSWER → MANIFESTATION → AMENDMENT → JUSTIFICATION framework
10. **Section XI-XII: Proposed Amendments and 1-Minute Vote** — Drafting amendment text, constructing concise plenary declarations
11. **Section XIII: Strategic Intelligence** — Gathering and presenting information the MP needs but didn't ask for
12. **Complexity Tiers and Adaptation** — Light (commendatory), Standard (regulatory), Deep (appropriations), Focused (committee), Express (urgent)

## Legal Review Guidebook

**Composing skill**: `/legal-reviewer`
**Audience**: Parliament legal division, MOA legal units, PRLS

### Recommended Chapters (Compact: 6-8 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — Role of legal review in Bangsamoro governance, constitutional and BOL basis
2. **The Bangsamoro Legal Hierarchy** — Constitution → BOL (RA 11054) → BAAs → Executive Orders → Local Ordinances. How to navigate conflicting provisions.
3. **Reviewing Bills and Resolutions** — Constitutionality, BOL compliance, consistency with existing BAAs, Shari'ah alignment, drafting quality
4. **Reviewing MOA Legal Documents** — Administrative orders, memoranda, contracts, MOAs/MOUs. Ensuring compliance with BAA 13 (Administrative Code)
5. **Verification Methodology** — Source hierarchy (local transcriptions → BOL text → PDFs → web search), verbatim accuracy standards, citation formatting
6. **Common Legal Issues in BARMM Legislation** — Ultra vires, overlapping authority, concurrent vs. exclusive powers, reserved powers, Shari'ah conflicts
7. **Preparing Legal Opinions and Memoranda** — Structure, analysis framework, recommendation format, transmittal procedures
8. **Quality Standards** — Review checklist, peer review, approval workflow

## Complete Staff Work (CSW) Guidebook

**Composing skill**: `/legislative-briefer` (CSW format)
**Audience**: Parliament staff, MOA staff (cross-cutting)
**Canonical framework**: ADDRESS IT (user's proprietary 7-step CSW methodology — read `${GEMINI_SKILL_DIR}/references/address-it-csw-framework.md`)

### Recommended Chapters (Comprehensive: 12-14 chapters)

The chapter structure follows the **ADDRESS IT** mnemonic — each step becomes one or two chapters, plus context and application chapters.

1. **Introduction** — CSW philosophy: the decision-maker should only need to approve or disapprove. The ADDRESS IT framework overview.
2. **CSW in the Bangsamoro Context** — Adapting the CSW tradition to BARMM's parliamentary and ministerial governance. How ADDRESS IT applies to both legislative and executive staff work.
3. **Types of CSW** — Legislative briefers (13-section format), policy analysis, budget analysis, project proposals, legal opinions. How ADDRESS IT adapts to each type.
4. **Step 1: Analyze the Context** — Understanding the assignment, assessing the office's function, identifying stakeholders, examining PESTEL factors, reviewing laws/policies (BOL, BAAs), gathering preliminary data.
5. **Step 2: Define and Deliberate the Problem or Need** — Articulating the problem, engaging stakeholders in deliberation, root cause analysis (5 Whys, fishbone), assessing consequences, establishing SMART objectives, documenting the problem statement.
6. **Step 3: Research Needed Information** — Developing a research plan, identifying sources, literature review, primary data collection, analysis and synthesis, identifying best practices, documenting and validating findings.
7. **Step 4: Explore Options** — Generating options, evaluation criteria (feasibility, effectiveness, efficiency, sustainability, alignment), impact analysis, cost-benefit analysis, prioritizing and validating options, preparing for recommendation.
8. **Step 5: Submit Proposal or Report** — Report structure (executive summary, problem, findings, options, recommendation, implementation plan), writing the executive summary, presenting research, the call to action, supervisor review and clearance, submission to decision-maker.
9. **Step 6: Sustain Efforts to Implement the Solution** — Maintaining stakeholder support, resource mobilization, action plans, training and capacity building, progress monitoring, stakeholder engagement throughout implementation.
10. **Step 7: Iterate and Improve the Solution** — M&E framework, collecting feedback, analyzing performance data, identifying improvement areas, iterating and refining, documenting lessons learned, restarting the CSW cycle when needed.
11. **CSW for Parliamentary Committees** — Applying ADDRESS IT to legislative briefers: how the 13-section format maps to the 7 steps. Committee-specific CSW, committee reports, oversight CSW.
12. **CSW for MOA Decision-Making** — Applying ADDRESS IT to executive contexts: budget requests, project proposals, personnel actions, procurement justifications. Approval hierarchies and transmittal procedures.
13. **Writing Standards and Quality Assurance** — Clarity, completeness, conciseness, objectivity. The "so what" test. Peer review. Transmittal memorandum. Shari'ah and gender review where applicable.
14. **Building a CSW Culture** — Training staff in the ADDRESS IT methodology, institutionalizing CSW practices, continuous improvement as organizational habit.

## Manual of Operations Guidebook

**No composing skill** — this is a standalone manual-building workflow
**Audience**: MOAs, Parliament offices/divisions
**Reference**: PRLS-LRD and PRLS-LMLAD Manuals of Operations (improve upon)

### Recommended Chapters (Standard: 8-10 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — Purpose of a Manual of Operations, how to use this guidebook, legal basis (BAA 13)
2. **Organizational Context** — How to document the office's mandate, structure, staffing, and reporting relationships. Mapping to the broader MOA/Parliament structure.
3. **Defining Outputs and Deliverables** — Identifying the major outputs of the office, classifying by type (research, analysis, drafting, advisory, administrative). The PRLS model: 4 major output categories.
4. **Designing Workflow Procedures** — Step-by-step process design: reception → evaluation → delegation → execution → review → approval → delivery. Role assignment at each step.
5. **Approval Hierarchies and Delegation** — Designing approval chains, delegation of authority, escalation procedures. Adapting to BARMM's governance culture.
6. **Forms, Templates, and Standardization** — Creating standardized forms (referral slips, transmittal memos, action slips), output templates, tracking systems
7. **Quality Standards and Performance Metrics** — Defining output quality criteria, turnaround times, KPIs, performance monitoring
8. **Documentation and Records Management** — Filing systems, document numbering, archiving, digital systems
9. **Review and Continuous Improvement** — Periodic review schedule, feedback mechanisms, amendment procedures for the manual itself
10. **Implementation Guide** — Rolling out the manual: staff orientation, training, transition period, compliance monitoring

### Improvements Over Existing PRLS MOPs
- **Add rationale sections** explaining WHY each procedure exists (not just WHAT to do)
- **Include best practices** and tips, not just bare procedures
- **Add quality standards** per output type (existing MOPs lack explicit quality criteria)
- **Include sample outputs** as appendices (existing MOPs reference forms but don't show completed examples)
- **Add performance metrics** (turnaround times, quality benchmarks)
- **Modernize for digital workflows** where applicable

## Policy Notes Guidebook

**No composing skill** — this is a standalone format guide
**Audience**: PRLS (Parliament), MOA policy/research units
**Reference**: PRLS-LRD-Policy-Notes-003-2022 (improve upon)

### Recommended Chapters (Compact: 6-8 chapters)

1. **Introduction** — The role of policy notes in Bangsamoro governance, the PRLS Policy Notes Series (ISSN 2815-2115)
2. **Types of Policy Notes** — Issue briefs, analytical papers, position papers, situation reports. When to use each.
3. **Research Methodology** — Literature review, data analysis, stakeholder consultation, legal analysis. Building an evidence base worthy of the ISSN series.
4. **Structure and Format** — The enhanced format: Executive Summary → Background → Issue → Methodology → Analysis → Key Findings → Policy Options → Recommendation → References. (Improvement: adds Methodology and Policy Options sections missing from existing format.)
5. **Writing Standards** — Academic rigor with policy accessibility. Citation style (Chicago footnotes). Tone calibration: objective analysis with clear recommendations.
6. **Review and Publication Process** — Internal review, peer review, editorial standards, ISSN tracking, publication schedule
7. **Dissemination and Impact** — Distribution to MPs, committee referral, media engagement, tracking policy influence
8. **Building a Policy Research Agenda** — Aligning research with BDP goals, responding to legislative needs, proactive policy scanning

### Improvements Over Existing PRLS Policy Notes
- **Add methodology section** (existing format lacks explicit research methodology)
- **Add policy options** before recommendation (existing format jumps from analysis to single recommendation)
- **Expand analysis depth** from ~3 pages to 8-15 pages for substantive issues
- **Add data visualization** where quantitative evidence supports arguments
- **Standardize citation format** (existing format uses inconsistent footnoting)
- **Include impact tracking** (did the policy note influence legislation or policy?)
