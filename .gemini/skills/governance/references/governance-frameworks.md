## Five Principles of Corporate Governance

### The Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fairness** | Equal treatment of all stakeholders; access to information and participation |
| **Accountability** | Board and management answer for actions and decisions |
| **Responsibility** | Duty to act in best interest of organization; informed decisions |
| **Transparency** | Open, honest disclosure of financial and governance information |
| **Independence** | Freedom from conflicts of interest; objective judgment |

### Application
Organizations applying these principles:
- Outperform peers financially
- Attract quality investors
- Build stakeholder trust
- Reduce governance risks

## Three Fiduciary Duties

### Duty of Care

**Definition**: Act with the competence and diligence that a reasonably prudent person would exercise.

**Requirements:**
- Prepare thoroughly for meetings
- Review materials before decisions
- Stay informed about organization
- Ask critical questions
- Request additional information when needed
- Investigate irregularities

**Standard**: "Ordinarily prudent person in similar circumstances"

### Duty of Loyalty

**Definition**: Prioritize organization's interests over personal interests.

**Requirements:**
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Disclose potential conflicts
- Recuse from conflicted decisions
- Never use position for personal gain
- Protect confidential information
- Act in good faith

**Key Test**: "Undivided allegiance to the organization"

### Duty of Obedience

**Definition**: Ensure organization operates within legal boundaries and fulfills its mission.

**Requirements:**
- Follow laws and regulations
- Adhere to governing documents (bylaws, charter)
- Uphold organizational mission
- Respect limits of board authority
- Ensure compliance with reporting requirements

**Key Test**: "Decisions align with organizational purpose"

### Consequences of Breach
- Personal liability for directors
- Legal claims and lawsuits
- Reputational damage
- Financial penalties
- Removal from board

## OECD Principles of Corporate Governance

### Overview
Global standard for corporate governance, updated 2023.

### Six Principle Areas

| Area | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **I. Effective Framework** | Legal and regulatory foundations |
| **II. Shareholder Rights** | Equal treatment, voting rights |
| **III. Institutional Investors** | Stock markets, intermediaries |
| **IV. Stakeholder Role** | Stakeholder engagement |
| **V. Disclosure & Transparency** | Material information disclosure |
| **VI. Board Responsibilities** | Strategic guidance, oversight |

### 2023 Updates
- Greater emphasis on sustainability and climate-related risks
- Enhanced stakeholder considerations
- Updated risk management guidance
- Strengthened board diversity recommendations
- New provisions on digitalization and technology governance

## King Reports

### Overview
South Africa's comprehensive governance framework, known for "apply or explain" approach.

### Evolution
| Report | Year | Key Focus |
|--------|------|-----------|
| King I | 1994 | Foundation principles |
| King II | 2002 | Sustainability integration |
| King III | 2009 | Integrated reporting |
| King IV | 2016 | Outcomes-based governance |
| King V | 2025 | Latest revision |

### Philosophy
Three key elements:
1. **Leadership** - Ethical, effective leadership
2. **Sustainability** - Economic, social, environmental performance
3. **Good Corporate Citizenship** - Responsible organizational behavior

### King IV Governance Outcomes
| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ethical Culture** | Values-driven organization |
| **Good Performance** | Value creation for stakeholders |
| **Effective Control** | Risk and opportunity management |
| **Legitimacy** | Stakeholder trust and confidence |

### Integrated Reporting
- Holistic representation of financial and sustainability performance
- Replaces separate annual and sustainability reports
- Interconnects strategy, governance, and sustainability

## Business Judgment Rule

### Definition
Legal doctrine protecting board directors from personal liability for business decisions made in good faith.

### Requirements for Protection
| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Good Faith** | Honest belief decision is in best interest |
| **Informed** | Reasonable diligence in decision-making |
| **No Conflict** | Free from personal interest |
| **Rational** | Reasonable basis for decision |

### When Rule Does NOT Apply
- Fraud or self-dealing
- Gross negligence
- Conflict of interest
- Bad faith or corrupt motive
- Failure to consider material facts

### Practical Implications
- Document decision-making process
- Seek expert advice when appropriate
- Ensure adequate information before deciding
- Disclose and manage conflicts

## Board Committee Structure

### Core Committees

**Audit Committee**
| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Financial Oversight | Ensure accuracy of financial statements |
| Internal Controls | Evaluate control systems |
| External Audit | Oversee independent auditors |
| Risk Management | Monitor significant risks |
| Compliance | Ethics and regulatory compliance |

**Compensation/Remuneration Committee**
| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Executive Pay | Set CEO and executive compensation |
| Incentive Plans | Design performance-based programs |
| Policy Alignment | Align pay with performance and strategy |
| Disclosure | Compensation transparency |

**Nomination/Governance Committee**
| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Board Composition | Identify skill needs and candidates |
| Director Selection | Vet and nominate directors |
| Succession Planning | CEO and board succession |
| Board Evaluation | Assess board effectiveness |
| Governance Policies | Develop and review governance guidelines |

### Independence Requirements
- All committee members should be independent
- Audit and Compensation have stricter standards
- Chair should be non-executive

## Board Leadership Models

### Separation vs. Combined Roles

| Model | CEO/Chair | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Separated** | Different people | Clear oversight, checks and balances |
| **Combined** | Same person | Unified leadership, efficiency |
| **Lead Director** | Combined + Lead | Balance of both approaches |

### Arguments for Separation
- Independent oversight of management
- Prevents concentration of power
- Clear accountability
- Better risk management

### Arguments for Combined
- Unified leadership
- Faster decision-making
- Clear authority
- Reduces coordination costs

### Current Trend
Growing preference for separation, especially in public companies.
