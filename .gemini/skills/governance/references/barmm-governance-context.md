## Workflow 5: BARMM Governance Context

Specialized guidance for governance in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), based on the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028.

### BARMM Governance Framework

The Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL/RA 11054) provides the Bangsamoro government:
- Autonomy to exercise self-governance
- Powers to promote general welfare
- Authority to regulate constituent LGUs
- Mechanism for resolving intergovernmental relations
- Justice system in consonance with traditional laws and the Constitution
- Fiscal autonomy

**Development Goal:** Stable, Just, and Accountable Bangsamoro Government

### 12 Pillars of Moral Governance (BDP 2023-2028)

Moral Governance is the centerpiece of BARMM's leadership and governance. These 12 pillars guide all public service:

| # | Pillar | Description |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Integrity** | Public officers avoid financial or other bindings that could influence official duties |
| 2 | **Accountability** | Decision-makers are open, transparent, document decisions, and answer to the people |
| 3 | **Transparency & Honesty** | Declare private interests, resolve conflicts protecting public interest, make transactions public |
| 4 | **Sense of Patriotism** | Invest in way of life, achievements, history, love for country; maintain reputation and integrity |
| 5 | **Striving for Excellence** | Uphold legitimacy, deliver highest quality, prioritize process for the good of all |
| 6 | **Cultural Understanding & Tolerance** | Understand socioeconomic differences, value diversity across the Bangsamoro |
| 7 | **Unwavering Loyalty to God** | Public servants strictly follow rules and regulations set by authorities |
| 8 | **Justice** | Serve without prejudice; equity triumphs regardless of self, family, or political alliances |
| 9 | **Inclusivity** | Foster inclusive environment free from discrimination based on religion, ethnicity, or culture |
| 10 | **Objectivity** | Make merit-based decisions; act solely in public interest without personal gain |
| 11 | **Dialogue & Meaningful Engagement** | Consult communities, specify needs, achieve meaningful engagement and positive change |
| 12 | **Balance of Practical & Moral Life** | Balance practical duties with moral/ethical values in carrying out responsibilities |

### BARMM Governance Sub-Sectors

The Development Administration sector covers six sub-sectors:

| Sub-Sector | Key Functions |
|------------|---------------|
| **Bureaucracy** | Organizational strengthening, civil service, HR management |
| **Development Planning** | Plan formulation, M&E systems, BEDC coordination |
| **Legislation** | Priority codes, resolutions, oversight functions |
| **Fiscal Management** | Budget, revenue generation, fund utilization |
| **Multi-Stakeholder Engagement** | CSO accreditation, PSR participation, LGU coordination |
| **Bangsamoro Justice System** | Shari'ah courts, traditional justice, ADR mechanisms |

| **LGAIP** | LGU Grant Assistance for Innovative Practices |
| **SMB** | Search for Model Barangays |

**Seven Elements of LGU Functionality:**
1. Presence of government center with facilities
2. Working elected and appointed mandatory officials
3. Functional local special bodies
4. Functional local legislative body
5. Approved mandated plans and budget
6. Availability of basic services
7. Sound fiscal management

### BARMM Fiscal Management Framework

**Revenue Sources (per BOL):**

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Annual Block Grant** | 5% of net national internal revenue (BIR + BOC) - ~90% of total |
| **Special Development Fund** | Php 5B/year for 10 years for conflict-affected areas - ~7% |
| **Share in National Taxes** | 75% of taxes collected in BARMM |
| **Regional Taxes** | Regional wealth tax, contractors' tax, travel tax |
| **Regional Fees & Charges** | Fees from regulatory offices |

**Fund Allocation:**
- Regular Funds - ministry/office operations
- Special Development Funds - BOL-mandated development projects
- Special Purpose Funds - pension, MPBF, contingent fund

### Bangsamoro Justice System

**Multi-Justice System Components:**

| Component | Status | Governing Law |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| **Shari'ah High Court** | Not yet established | BOL Art. X |
| **Shari'ah District Courts (SDC)** | 5 operational | PD 1083 |
| **Shari'ah Circuit Courts (SCC)** | 33 organized, 15 not yet | JBC |
| **Shari'ah Public Assistance Office** | Not yet established | BAA 13 |
| **Bangsamoro Integrated Bar** | Not yet established | BOL Art. X |
| **Traditional Justice System** | Recognition pending | BOL Sec. 17 |
| **ADR Mechanisms** | Codification pending | BOL Sec. 20 |

**Five Pillars of Criminal Justice System:**
1. Law Enforcement - public safety, order maintenance
2. Prosecution - case preparation and filing
3. Courts - adjudication of cases
4. Correction - reformation and rehabilitation
5. Community - LGU and public participation

### BARMM Governance Challenges (2023-2028)

**Bureaucracy Challenges:**
- Overlapping functions between government levels
- Weak inter-MOA/LGU coordination
- IGR mechanisms not fully established
- Nepotism and lack of political will

**Development Planning Challenges:**
- Weak planning-budgeting linkages
- Weak data and communications management
- Non-institutionalization of M&E system

**Fiscal Management Challenges:**
- Limited technical know-how in program budgeting
- Manual budget processing (no e-NGAS, BTMS)
- Lack of Revenue and Investment Codes
- Fluctuating local revenue generation

**Justice System Challenges:**
- BJS not fully implemented
- Lack of Shari'ah judges and personnel
- Less prioritization on Shari'ah capacity development
- Weak coordination among five pillars of justice

### Applying BARMM Governance Context

**For Governance Courses/Workshops:**
- Incorporate 12 Pillars of Moral Governance as foundation
- Reference BOL provisions and BAA 13
- Include BARMM-specific case studies
- Address multi-justice system awareness

**For Cooperative/SE Governance:**
- Align with BARMM development goals
- Connect to CSEA regulatory framework
- Reference FRAMES compliance requirements
- Integrate with LGU governance mechanisms

**For Platform Features:**
- Support BARMM compliance tracking
- Enable moral governance indicators
- Integrate with BEDC reporting requirements
- Support multi-stakeholder engagement
