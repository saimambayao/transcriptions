# BARMM Governance Framework: BDP 2023-2028

Comprehensive governance reference integrating Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 with governance principles applicable to government, cooperatives, and social enterprises in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

---

## BARMM Vision & Mission

### Vision (BDP 2023-2028)
"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

### Mission
"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

### Overall Goal
**An Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro**
- Empowered: Improved community capacity and economic opportunities
- Cohesive: Strengthened relationships and reduced conflict
- Progressive: Sustained ability to optimize resources and realize potential

---

## Six Development Goals (BDP 2023-2028)

1. **Stable, Just, and Accountable Government** - Government stability with effective service delivery and accountability frameworks
2. **Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy** - Economic gains distributed to all with sustained growth and quality jobs
3. **Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Communities** - Lasting peace through security and disaster resilience
4. **Inclusive, Responsive, Quality Social Services** - Services accessible to all, leaving no one behind
5. **Preserved Cultural and Identity** - Maintained and promoted Bangsamoro culture and diversity
6. **Climate-Resilient Infrastructure** - Strategic, adequate infrastructure withstanding climate disasters

## 12 Pillars of Moral Governance (BDP 2023-2028)

Moral Governance is the centerpiece of BARMM's leadership. These 12 pillars guide all public service:

| # | Pillar | Application |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Integrity** | Public officers avoid influences affecting official duties; no conflicts of interest |
| 2 | **Accountability** | Open and transparent about decisions; documented and answerable to people |
| 3 | **Transparency & Honesty** | Private interests declared; conflicts resolved protecting public interest |
| 4 | **Sense of Patriotism** | Invested in achieving excellence and maintaining institutional reputation |
| 5 | **Striving for Excellence** | Highest quality delivered; processes prioritized over pure results |
| 6 | **Cultural Understanding** | Respect socioeconomic differences; value diversity across Bangsamoro |
| 7 | **Loyalty to God** | Ethical foundation; strict adherence to rules and regulations |
| 8 | **Justice** | Serve without prejudice; equity regardless of family/political alliances |
| 9 | **Inclusivity** | Foster inclusive environment free from religion/ethnicity discrimination |
| 10 | **Objectivity** | Merit-based decisions; act solely in public interest without personal gain |
| 11 | **Dialogue & Engagement** | Consult communities; achieve meaningful engagement and positive change |
| 12 | **Balance of Practical & Moral** | Balance practical duties with ethical values in public service |

## Cooperative Governance Context

### BARMM Cooperative Statistics (BDP 2023-2028)

**Current State:**
- Total Registered Cooperatives: **12,214** (2010-2020)
- Current Compliance Rate: **11.66%** (1,424 cooperatives)
- **Target Compliance: 50% by 2028**

**Distribution:**
- Maguindanao: 4,671 (38.24%)
- Lanao del Sur: 4,554 (37.29%)
- Producer Cooperatives: 4,590 (37.58%)
- Marketing Cooperatives: 3,364 (27.54%)
- Agricultural Cooperatives: 1,566 (12.82%)

### Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA)

**Role:** Regulatory agency for cooperatives and social enterprises in BARMM

**Transition Status:** Moving from former ARMM-CDA framework to BARMM governance

**Governance Challenges:**
- Low compliance reflects transition difficulties
- New cooperatives (many former combatants) struggle with requirements
- Policy modernization needed for BARMM context

**Priority Interventions (BDP 2023-2028):**
- PESO Project (Profitability and Equity Support Oriented)
- LAKAS SE Program (Lakbay Aral sa Social Enterprise)
- Combatant Transitional Program with technical and financial support

### Governance Principles for BARMM Cooperatives

**Democratic Foundation (per RA 9520 & ICA Principles):**
- One Member, One Vote - equal voting regardless of investment
- General Assembly (GA) - annual supreme authority meeting
- Board of Directors - elected from/by membership (5-15 members)
- Mandatory Committees: Audit, Election, Mediation/Conciliation, Ethics

**BARMM-Specific Requirements:**
- Alignment with 12 Pillars of Moral Governance
- Shari'ah-compliant governance for Muslim-majority cooperatives
- CSEA compliance and Certificate of Compliance
- FRAMES monitoring for sustainability

## BARMM Governance Structure

### Constitutional Framework

**Governing Law:** Republic Act 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law - BOL)

**Governance Type:** Parliamentary system with:
- Parliament (legislative body)
- Chief Minister (executive head)
- 15 Ministries (BAA 13)
- 5 Provinces, 3 Cities, 116 Municipalities, 2,590 Barangays

### Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (BEDC)

**Function:** Socio-economic planning, monitoring, coordination

**Composition:**
- 5 Provincial governors
- 3 City mayors
- 5 Private Sector Representatives (accredited)
- Civil Society Organizations (accredited)

**Key Committees:**
- Development Administration Committee (DACom)
- Economic Development Committee (EDCom)
- Social Development Committee (SDCom)
- Infrastructure Development Committee (IDCom)
- Peace, Public Order, Safety & Security Committee (PPOSSCom)

## Islamic Governance Principles

### Shari'ah Principles in BARMM Governance

**Amanah (Trusteeship/Sacred Trust)**
- Public officers hold positions as sacred trusts
- Fiduciary duty as Islamic concept of trusteeship
- Accountability to God for stewardship
- Application: Board members as trustees; emphasize moral obligation

**Shura (Consultation)**
- Qur'anic principle requiring consultation before decisions
- Goal of consensus decision-making
- All voices heard, including minorities
- Application: Consultative board processes; stakeholder input before major decisions

**Adl (Justice/Equity)**
- Fair treatment regardless of status
- No favoritism or discrimination
- Equal justice for all
- Application: Merit-based decisions; fair treatment of all stakeholders

**Muslihat (Community Welfare)**
- Organizational decisions benefit community
- Sustainability of community welfare
- Profits serve community good
- Application: Community benefit prioritized; long-term sustainability focus

### Integration with 12 Pillars

| Islamic Principle | Aligned Pillar(s) |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Amanah | Pillar 2 (Accountability), Pillar 4 (Patriotism) |
| Shura | Pillar 11 (Dialogue), Pillar 9 (Inclusivity) |
| Adl | Pillar 8 (Justice), Pillar 10 (Objectivity) |
| Muslihat | Pillar 12 (Practical & Moral Balance) |

## Multi-Justice System

### Bangsamoro Justice System Components

| Component | Status | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Shari'ah Courts** | Partially operational (PD 1083) | Muslim personal law matters |
| **Regular Courts** | Operational (RPC hierarchy) | General criminal and civil matters |
| **Traditional/Tribal Justice** | Recognition pending (BOL Sec. 17) | Indigenous peoples disputes |
| **ADR Mechanisms** | Codification pending (BOL Sec. 20) | Conciliation, mediation, arbitration |

### Shari'ah Court Structure

**Shari'ah High Court:** Not yet established; intended as appellate court (5 justices)

**Shari'ah District Courts (SDC):** 5 operational; jurisdiction over family law, personal status

**Shari'ah Circuit Courts (SCC):** 33 organized, 15 not organized (Tawi-Tawi, Basilan); first-level courts

**Shari'ah Public Assistance Office:** Not yet established; will provide free legal assistance to indigent litigants

### Governance Implications for Cooperatives

**Shari'ah-Compliant Governance:**
- Cooperatives with Muslim membership may apply Shari'ah principles
- Board decisions aligned with Islamic requirements
- Dispute resolution using Shari'ah-compliant ADR mechanisms
- Amanah concept central to fiduciary duties

## Accountability Mechanisms

### LGU Performance Assessment Tools

| Tool | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **SGLG** | Seal of Good Local Governance - governance integrity |
| **SEAL** | Salamat Hashim Excellence Award - LCE leadership |
| **SGFH** | Seal of Good Financial Housekeeping - financial compliance |
| **FDP** | Full Disclosure Policy - transparency |
| **PACTS** | Performance Audit & Compliance Tracking System |
| **LoGFA** | Local Government Functionality Appraisal |
| **LTIA** | Lupong Tagapamayapa Incentives Award - dispute resolution |

**Seven Elements of LGU Functionality:** Government center with facilities, working officials, functional special bodies, functional legislative body, approved plans/budget, basic services, sound fiscal management

### Cooperative Compliance Tracking

**Certificate of Compliance (CSEA):**
- Current: 11.66% achievement (1,424 of 12,214)
- Target: 50% by 2028
- Issued when cooperatives meet all requirements

**FRAMES Monitoring:**
- CSEA framework for sustainability assessment
- Monitors financial health and governance
- Identifies cooperatives needing support

### Five Pillars of Criminal Justice System

| Pillar | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Law Enforcement** | Preserve public safety and order |
| **Prosecution** | Case preparation and filing |
| **Courts** | Adjudicate cases impartially |
| **Correction** | Reformation and rehabilitation |
| **Community** | Public participation through LGUs |

**Challenge:** Weak coordination among pillars - priority for BDP 2023-2028

## Integration Framework: Applying BARMM Governance

**Step 1: Align with 12 Pillars**
- Adopt 12 Pillars in cooperative bylaws
- Train board members on moral governance
- Integrate pillars into decision-making

**Step 2: Implement Accountability**
- Establish governance documentation systems
- Regular board self-assessment
- Transparency to membership
- Compliance with CSEA framework

**Step 3: Incorporate Islamic Principles**
- Integrate Amanah, Shura, Adl, Muslihat
- Align with Shari'ah-compliant practices
- Islamic finance for financial governance

**Step 4: Engage Multi-Stakeholders**
- Community involvement in decisions
- Local government partnerships
- Participate in BEDC initiatives
- Align with regional development goals

**Step 5: Monitor and Improve**
- Track progress toward 50% compliance
- Implement FRAMES monitoring
- Participate in CSEA capacity building
- Report improvements to membership
