## Training Levels

### Four-Tier Framework

| Level | Target Audience | Duration | Focus |
|-------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Foundation** | New board members, aspiring directors | 8-16 hours | Governance basics, fiduciary duties |
| **Intermediate** | Experienced directors, committee chairs | 24-40 hours | Board effectiveness, oversight |
| **Advanced** | Board chairs, governance leaders | 40+ hours | Strategic governance, leadership |
| **Executive** | C-suite, senior leaders | 16-24 hours | Governance and strategy |

### Level Objectives

**Foundation:**
- Understand what governance is
- Know fiduciary duties
- Recognize board vs. management roles
- Participate effectively in meetings

**Intermediate:**
- Master committee functions
- Conduct effective oversight
- Manage conflicts of interest
- Evaluate organizational performance

**Advanced:**
- Lead board effectiveness
- Drive governance culture
- Navigate complex decisions
- Develop board succession

**Executive:**
- Align governance with strategy
- Interface effectively with board
- Build governance culture
- Lead organizational transformation

## Foundation Governance Course

### Standard Duration: 8-16 hours (1-2 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. What is Governance?** | Definition, purpose, governance vs. management | 1-2 hours |
| **2. Role of the Board** | Responsibilities, authority, limitations | 2-3 hours |
| **3. Fiduciary Duties** | Care, loyalty, obedience | 2-3 hours |
| **4. Board Meetings** | Preparation, participation, decision-making | 1-2 hours |
| **5. Ethics & Conflicts** | Conflict of interest, ethical conduct | 1-2 hours |
| **6. New Director Orientation** | Organizational overview, governance documents | 1-2 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Define governance and distinguish from management
2. Describe the three fiduciary duties
3. Identify board member responsibilities
4. Recognize and manage conflicts of interest
5. Participate effectively in board meetings

### Key Concepts

**Governance vs. Management:**
| Governance (Board) | Management (CEO/Staff) |
|--------------------|------------------------|
| What and why | How and when |
| Policy | Implementation |
| Oversight | Operations |
| Ends | Means |
| Strategic | Operational |

**Three Fiduciary Duties:**
1. Duty of Care - Informed, prudent decisions
2. Duty of Loyalty - Organization first
3. Duty of Obedience - Follow laws and mission

## Intermediate Governance Course

### Standard Duration: 24-40 hours (3-5 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Governance Theories** | Agency, stewardship, stakeholder theories | 3-4 hours |
| **2. Board Structure** | Committees, composition, independence | 4-6 hours |
| **3. Effective Oversight** | Financial, risk, performance oversight | 4-6 hours |
| **4. Strategy & Governance** | Strategic planning, board's role | 3-4 hours |
| **5. CEO Relationship** | Hiring, evaluation, compensation | 3-4 hours |
| **6. Governance Frameworks** | OECD, King Reports, best practices | 3-4 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Apply governance theories to decision-making
2. Evaluate board structure and composition
3. Conduct effective management oversight
4. Contribute to strategic direction
5. Manage CEO relationship appropriately

### Module Details

**Module 2: Board Structure**
- Committee types and functions (audit, compensation, nomination)
- Board composition and skills matrix
- Independence requirements
- Director recruitment and onboarding

**Module 3: Effective Oversight**
| Oversight Area | Board Focus |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Financial** | Statements, budgets, audits |
| **Risk** | Enterprise risk, compliance |
| **Performance** | KPIs, strategic goals |
| **Compliance** | Legal, regulatory, ethical |

**Module 5: CEO Relationship**
- Hiring process
- Performance evaluation
- Compensation setting
- Succession planning
- Appropriate boundaries

## Advanced Governance Course

### Standard Duration: 40+ hours (5+ days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Board Leadership** | Chair role, board culture, effectiveness | 6-8 hours |
| **2. Strategic Governance** | Long-term value creation, stakeholder management | 6-8 hours |
| **3. Crisis Governance** | Crisis management, board's role in crises | 4-6 hours |
| **4. Governance Transformation** | Improving governance, change leadership | 6-8 hours |
| **5. Complex Decisions** | M&A, major investments, restructuring | 4-6 hours |
| **6. Succession & Development** | CEO and board succession, director development | 4-6 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Lead board effectiveness initiatives
2. Navigate complex governance challenges
3. Manage board during crises
4. Drive governance improvement
5. Develop future governance leaders

### Module Details

**Module 1: Board Leadership**
- Chair responsibilities and effectiveness
- Building board culture
- Managing board dynamics
- Board evaluation and development

**Module 3: Crisis Governance**
- Board's role in crisis response
- Communication during crisis
- Reputational risk management
- Recovery and lessons learned

**Module 6: Succession & Development**
- CEO succession planning process
- Emergency succession plans
- Director recruitment pipeline
- Board refreshment strategies

## Integration with /academy

### When to Use with /academy

Invoke `/governance` with `/academy` when:
1. Creating board governance training
2. Developing director development programs
3. Building governance certification programs
4. Designing governance workshops for specific contexts

### Workflow

```
1. IDENTIFY training need and audience
         ↓
2. SELECT course level (Foundation/Intermediate/Advanced)
         ↓
3. SELECT context (Cooperative/SE/Corporate/Government)
         ↓
4. USE /governance for content expertise
         ↓
5. USE /academy for curriculum design
         ↓
6. DEVELOP learning materials
         ↓
7. IMPLEMENT training
         ↓
8. EVALUATE and improve
```

### Information to Provide /academy

When invoking with `/academy`:

1. **Course Level**: Foundation, Intermediate, Advanced, Executive
2. **Target Audience**: New directors, experienced directors, chairs, executives
3. **Duration**: Hours/days available
4. **Delivery Mode**: In-person, online, hybrid
5. **Specific Topics**: If not full curriculum
6. **Context**: Cooperative, Social Enterprise, Corporate, Government
7. **Assessment Requirements**: Certification needs

### Context-Specific Curriculum Adaptations

**For Cooperatives:**
- Emphasize democratic governance
- Include member engagement
- Add ICA principles and values
- Focus on balancing democracy and efficiency

**For Social Enterprises:**
- Include mission guardianship
- Add dual accountability concepts
- Address mission drift prevention
- Include stakeholder balancing

**For Corporations:**
- Emphasize shareholder accountability
- Include regulatory compliance
- Add ESG governance
- Focus on board independence

**For Government:**
- Emphasize public accountability
- Include transparency requirements
- Add political navigation skills
- Focus on service orientation
