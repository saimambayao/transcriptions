## Cooperative Governance

### Democratic Foundation

**ICA Principle 2: Democratic Member Control**
> "Cooperatives are democratic organisations controlled by their members, who actively participate in setting their policies and making decisions."

### Key Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **One Member, One Vote** | Equal voting power regardless of investment |
| **Elected Board** | Directors elected by and from membership |
| **Member Accountability** | Board accountable to membership |
| **Participatory** | Active member involvement in decisions |
| **Distributed Control** | No concentration of power |

### Board Structure

**Composition:**
- Elected from membership
- Often geographic or sector representation
- Term limits common
- May include professional directors

**Responsibilities:**
- Strategic direction
- Manager/CEO oversight
- Member interest protection
- Cooperative values stewardship
- Policy development

### Governance Challenges

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Democratic vs. Efficiency** | Clear delegation to management |
| **Member Apathy** | Engagement programs, communication |
| **Board Skill Gaps** | Director training, co-option |
| **Elite Capture** | Term limits, transparency |

### Best Practices
- Separation of governance (board) and management (CEO)
- Regular member communication
- Board training and development
- Transparent decision-making
- Clear conflict of interest policies
- Annual general meetings
- Member education on governance

## Social Enterprise Governance

### Dual Mission Challenge

Social enterprises face unique governance challenges from their hybrid nature:
- Social mission AND financial sustainability
- Multiple stakeholder accountability
- Risk of mission drift

### Governance Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Mission-Driven** | Social purpose at center |
| **Dual Accountability** | To beneficiaries AND funders |
| **Stakeholder Diversity** | Multiple principal stakeholders |
| **Hybrid Structure** | Blend of nonprofit and for-profit |

### Board Composition Options

| Model | Stakeholder Focus |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Beneficiary-Focused** | Target group representation (common in nonprofits) |
| **Investor-Focused** | Funder/investor representation (common in for-profits) |
| **Balanced** | Mix of stakeholders |
| **Expert-Focused** | Skill-based composition |

### Key Governance Functions

1. **Mission Guardianship** - Prevent mission drift
2. **Dual Performance Oversight** - Social AND financial metrics
3. **Stakeholder Balancing** - Navigate competing interests
4. **Sustainability Assurance** - Long-term viability

### Preventing Mission Drift

| Mechanism | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mission Lock** | Legal protections for mission |
| **Board Composition** | Mission-aligned directors |
| **Impact Metrics** | Track social outcomes |
| **Stakeholder Voice** | Beneficiary input |
| **Governance Policies** | Explicit mission priority |

### Best Practices
- Clear mission statement in governing documents
- Social impact measurement and reporting
- Beneficiary representation or voice mechanisms
- Conflict of interest policies for dual-purpose tensions
- Regular board education on hybrid governance
- Transparent communication with all stakeholders

## Corporate Governance

### Shareholder-Centric Foundation

Traditional corporate governance prioritizes shareholder interests within legal and ethical boundaries.

### Governance Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Shareholder Primacy** | Maximize shareholder value |
| **Fiduciary Duties** | Care, loyalty, obedience |
| **Independent Oversight** | Non-executive directors |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Securities laws, listing rules |
| **Transparency** | Disclosure requirements |

### Board Structure

**Typical Composition:**
- Executive directors (CEO, CFO)
- Independent non-executive directors (majority)
- Chair (often independent)

**Mandatory Committees:**
- Audit Committee (all independent)
- Compensation Committee (all independent)
- Nomination/Governance Committee

### Modern Evolution: ESG Integration

| Trend | Governance Implication |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Environmental** | Climate risk oversight |
| **Social** | Workforce, community responsibility |
| **Governance** | Board diversity, ethics, transparency |

### Best Practices
- Majority independent board
- Separation of CEO and Chair
- Regular board evaluation
- Director term limits or refreshment
- Shareholder engagement
- ESG integration into strategy
- Risk committee (especially for financial institutions)

## Public Sector Governance

### Public Accountability Foundation

Government entities and public organizations serve citizens and must maintain high accountability standards.

### Governance Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Public Accountability** | Answer to citizens and legislators |
| **Transparency** | Open records, public meetings |
| **Service Orientation** | Public interest as primary purpose |
| **Political Context** | Navigate political environment |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Public sector laws and regulations |

### Key Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Rule of Law** | Operate within legal framework |
| **Transparency** | Open decision-making, disclosure |
| **Accountability** | Clear responsibility for actions |
| **Performance** | Effective, efficient service delivery |
| **Integrity** | Ethical conduct, probity |
| **Responsiveness** | Address stakeholder needs |

### Board/Oversight Structure

**Typical Elements:**
- Appointed or elected governing body
- Clear separation from management
- Independent audit function
- Legislative oversight
- Public participation mechanisms

### Audit and Oversight Functions

| Function | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Oversight** | Ensure proper conduct, deter corruption |
| **Insight** | Independent assessment of programs |
| **Foresight** | Identify trends, emerging challenges |

### Best Practices
- Clear separation of governance and management
- Independent audit and oversight
- Performance measurement and reporting
- Public participation and consultation
- Conflict of interest disclosure
- Whistleblower protection
- Regular governance audits

## Comparative Summary

### Governance Focus by Context

| Aspect | Cooperative | Social Enterprise | Corporate | Public Sector |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary Accountability** | Members | Beneficiaries + Funders | Shareholders | Citizens |
| **Decision Authority** | Democratic vote | Board | Board/Shareholders | Governing body |
| **Key Challenge** | Balancing democracy & efficiency | Preventing mission drift | Aligning interests | Political navigation |
| **Theory Alignment** | Stakeholder + Stewardship | Stakeholder + Stewardship | Agency + Stakeholder | Stakeholder |
| **Board Selection** | Elected by members | Appointed/Mixed | Elected by shareholders | Appointed/Elected |

### Common Elements Across All Contexts

1. **Fiduciary Duties** - Care, loyalty, obedience apply universally
2. **Separation of Roles** - Governance vs. management
3. **Accountability** - Answering to stakeholders
4. **Transparency** - Disclosure of information
5. **Oversight** - Monitoring performance
6. **Ethics** - Integrity in conduct

```

□ CEO/Board relationship
```
