# Professional Mode: Concept Notes & Institutional Proposals

This reference defines the "Professional Mode" for the `/humanizer` skill. Use this mode when drafting or revising concept notes, project proposals, executive summaries, or any formal documents meant for institutional partners, government ministries, or development agencies.

## The Core Philosophy

AI tends to write proposals like marketing brochures—full of buzzwords, bulleted lists with bold headers, and grandiose but empty claims. **Professional Mode** strips all of that away. It replaces sales pitches with authoritative, logically tight, and highly specific prose.

### 1. Paragraphs Over Bullets
AI defaults to turning every concept into a bulleted list. Real professionals write cohesive paragraphs.
* **AI Slop:** "Key benefits include: **Standardization** - it provides a standard framework. **Clarity** - it offers clear guidelines."
* **Professional Mode:** Use flowing paragraphs with strong topic sentences. Group related ideas logically. If you must use a list, use it for genuine sequences or distinct items, not as a crutch for poor paragraph structure.

### 2. Direct, Assertive Tone
Remove hedging and weak verbs. State the problem clearly and present the solution as a definitive action.
* **Avoid:** "This guidebook aims to help eliminate guesswork and hopes to serve as a resource."
* **Use:** "The guidebook eliminates guesswork and replaces it with a shared, documented methodology."

### 3. Absolute Ban on Decorative Fluff
Institutional proposals do not need emotional or decorative adjectives. 
* **Kill on sight:** "rich tapestry," "valuable insights," "fosters," "catalyst," "delves," "multifaceted," "seamlessly," "transformative."
* **Instead:** Use plain, precise verbs (e.g., establishes, bridges, maps, verifies, aligns).

### 4. Institutional Precision
Never use a generic term when a specific institutional entity exists.
* **Avoid:** "Various stakeholders and government agencies will be involved."
* **Use:** "The Committees on Shari'ah and Justice, along with the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta', will validate the manuscript."
* Name the actual laws (e.g., BOL, BAA), bodies (Parliament, MOAs, BDI), and mechanisms.

### 5. Precise Logical Boundaries
AI often ignores nuance to make broad, sweeping statements (e.g., "The law did not provide a manual"). Professional mode requires rigorous logical consistency.
* **Example:** Don't say "A guidebook on how to interpret the Qur'an" if the institution's mandate prohibits non-scholars from interpreting scripture. Say "A methodology for citing religious texts and defining the bounds of legal analysis."

### 6. No Internal Jargon in External Documents
AI and internal development processes produce shorthand terms ("institutional boundary," "pipeline enforcement," "context rot," "skill triggering") that mean nothing to external readers. Professional Mode requires every term to be understandable by the intended audience without needing to have worked on the project.
* **Test:** If a Shari'ah scholar, an MP, or a ministry director would need to ask "what does that mean?", rewrite it.
* **Example:** Don't say "the institutional boundary between legal analysis and fatawa issuance." Say "the line between legal analysis — which any researcher can perform — and fatawa, which only the BDI can issue."
* Name the actual concepts in plain language. If a term only makes sense to the person who built the document, it is jargon.

### 7. No Formulaic Transitions or Conclusions
AI loves writing "In conclusion, this project represents a significant step forward in our shared journey." 
* **Professional Mode:** Drop the hollow summaries. End with a definitive "Call to Action" or "Next Steps" that details exactly what is required from the reader (e.g., formal endorsement, signing an MOU, participating in a consultation).

## Quick Checklist for Professional Mode
- [ ] Are concepts explained in paragraphs rather than bullet-point lists?
- [ ] Are all sweeping/grandiose claims ("transformative impact") replaced with concrete deliverables?
- [ ] Are all generic references ("stakeholders") replaced with specific titles/offices?
- [ ] Is the document free of Tier 1 AI slop words (delve, foster, crucial role, multifaceted)?
- [ ] Does the conclusion actually ask for something specific rather than just summarizing the document?
