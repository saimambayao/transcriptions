## What is Marketing?

### AMA Definition

"Marketing is the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large." — American Marketing Association

### Core Marketing Concept

Marketing is about creating value for customers while achieving organizational objectives.

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Needs** | Basic human requirements (food, shelter, belonging) |
| **Wants** | Needs shaped by culture and personality |
| **Demands** | Wants backed by buying power |
| **Value** | Benefits received relative to costs paid |
| **Exchange** | Obtaining something by offering something in return |

## The Marketing Mix

### The 4Ps (Traditional)

Developed by E. Jerome McCarthy, refined by Philip Kotler.

| P | Element | Key Questions |
|---|---------|---------------|
| **Product** | What you sell | What are its features, benefits, quality? |
| **Price** | What you charge | What's the pricing strategy? Discounts? |
| **Place** | Where you sell | Distribution channels? Location? Accessibility? |
| **Promotion** | How you communicate | Advertising? PR? Sales promotion? |

### The 7Ps (Extended for Services)

Added by Booms and Bitner for service marketing.

| P | Element | Application |
|---|---------|-------------|
| **Product** | Service offering | Core service + supplementary services |
| **Price** | Pricing strategy | Value-based, cost-based, competition-based |
| **Place** | Distribution | Physical location, online, delivery |
| **Promotion** | Communication | Advertising, PR, social media, word-of-mouth |
| **People** | Service providers | Staff training, customer interaction, culture |
| **Process** | Service delivery | How service is delivered, efficiency, quality |
| **Physical Evidence** | Tangible cues | Environment, branding, documentation |

### When to Use Which

| Context | Recommended |
|---------|-------------|
| Physical products | 4Ps |
| Services (coops, SEs) | 7Ps |
| Digital products | 4Ps + Digital elements |
| Government services | 7Ps (emphasis on People, Process) |

## STP Framework

Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning.

### Segmentation

Dividing the market into distinct groups with different needs.

**Segmentation Variables:**

| Type | Variables | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Geographic** | Location, climate, region | Urban/rural, BARMM provinces |
| **Demographic** | Age, gender, income, education | Youth, women, low-income |
| **Psychographic** | Lifestyle, values, personality | Health-conscious, eco-friendly |
| **Behavioral** | Usage, loyalty, benefits sought | Heavy users, brand loyalists |

### Targeting

Selecting which segments to serve.

**Targeting Strategies:**

| Strategy | Description | Best For |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Undifferentiated** | Same offer to all | Commodity products |
| **Differentiated** | Different offers per segment | Multiple segments |
| **Concentrated** | Focus on one segment | Niche markets, limited resources |
| **Micromarketing** | Individual customization | High-value customers |

### Positioning

Creating a distinct place in the customer's mind.

**Positioning Statement Formula:**
```
For [target segment], [brand] is the [category] that [key benefit] because [reason to believe].
```

**Example:**
"For Bangsamoro cooperatives, CSEA Marketplace is the platform that connects quality local products with buyers because it is backed by government certification and supports community development."

## AIDA Model

Classic customer decision-making framework.

### The Four Stages

```
AWARENESS → INTEREST → DESIRE → ACTION
    ↓           ↓          ↓         ↓
  Know       Learn      Want       Buy
```

| Stage | Goal | Tactics |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Awareness** | Get noticed | Advertising, social media, PR, SEO |
| **Interest** | Engage attention | Content marketing, demonstrations, information |
| **Desire** | Create want | Benefits, testimonials, emotional appeal |
| **Action** | Drive purchase | Clear CTA, easy purchase process, incentives |

### Extended AIDA Models

| Model | Addition | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **AIDAS** | +Satisfaction | Post-purchase experience |
| **AIDCA** | +Conviction | Building confidence before action |
| **AIDAR** | +Retention | Customer loyalty and repeat purchase |

### Applying AIDA to Cooperative Marketing

| Stage | Cooperative Application |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Awareness** | Community events, local media, word-of-mouth |
| **Interest** | Member testimonials, product quality stories |
| **Desire** | Social impact messaging, community benefit |
| **Action** | Easy membership, accessible products |

## Customer Journey

Modern understanding of customer experience across touchpoints.

### Customer Journey Stages

| Stage | Customer Action | Marketing Focus |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Awareness** | Realizes need/problem | Brand visibility, content |
| **Consideration** | Researches options | Information, comparison |
| **Decision** | Chooses solution | Conversion, trust signals |
| **Purchase** | Completes transaction | Easy process, support |
| **Retention** | Uses product/service | Satisfaction, value delivery |
| **Advocacy** | Recommends to others | Loyalty programs, referrals |

### Journey Mapping Elements

1. **Personas**: Who is the customer?
2. **Stages**: What phases do they go through?
3. **Touchpoints**: Where do they interact with us?
4. **Actions**: What do they do at each stage?
5. **Emotions**: How do they feel?
6. **Pain Points**: What frustrations arise?
7. **Opportunities**: Where can we improve?

## Kotler's Marketing Principles

Philip Kotler's foundational marketing principles.

### The 5-Step Marketing Process

```
1. Understand Marketplace & Customer Needs
           ↓
2. Design Customer-Driven Marketing Strategy
           ↓
3. Construct Marketing Program (4Ps)
           ↓
4. Build Profitable Relationships
           ↓
5. Capture Value (Revenue & Loyalty)
```

### Kotler's 6 Principles of Marketing

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Customer Value** | Create superior value for customers |
| **2. Customer Relationships** | Build long-term relationships |
| **3. Marketing Strategy** | Segment, target, position effectively |
| **4. Integrated Marketing** | Coordinate all marketing activities |
| **5. Societal Marketing** | Consider society's long-term interests |
| **6. Marketing Ethics** | Practice honest, responsible marketing |

### Marketing Orientations

| Orientation | Focus | Limitation |
|-------------|-------|------------|
| **Production** | Efficiency, availability | Ignores customer needs |
| **Product** | Quality, features | May miss market needs |
| **Selling** | Aggressive promotion | Short-term focus |
| **Marketing** | Customer needs | More complex to implement |
| **Societal** | Society + customer + company | Hardest to balance |

## Quick Reference Tables

### Marketing Framework Selection

| Situation | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| New market entry | STP + 4Ps/7Ps |
| Customer acquisition | AIDA |
| Experience improvement | Customer Journey |
| Service design | 7Ps |
| Brand development | Positioning |

### Key Marketing Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Use |
|--------|---------|-----|
| **Market Share** | Sales / Total Market Sales | Competitive position |
| **CAC** | Marketing Cost / New Customers | Acquisition efficiency |
| **CLV** | Avg. Purchase × Frequency × Lifespan | Customer value |
| **Conversion Rate** | Conversions / Visitors | Funnel efficiency |
| **NPS** | Promoters% - Detractors% | Customer loyalty |
