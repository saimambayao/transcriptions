# Supervision System Document Template

A generalized template for designing supervision systems for any BARMM entity. Based on the MDN Supervision System structure, adapted for ministries, offices, agencies, and LGUs across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

## Section I: Policy Statement and Objectives

### Policy Statement

```
[Entity Name] establishes this Supervision System to provide a structured,
developmental, and accountable framework for all supervisors and managers.
This system ensures that supervision is purposeful, consistent, and aligned
with the entity's mandate, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, and the principles
of good governance.
```

Adapt the policy statement to reflect the entity's specific mandate and values.

### Objectives

1. **Performance excellence** — Ensure staff produce high-quality outputs that advance the entity's mandate
2. **Staff development** — Build the capability of every staff member through coaching, mentoring, and structured development
3. **Accountability** — Establish clear expectations, transparent evaluation, and consistent consequences
4. **Moral governance** — Ground supervision in Islamic leadership principles and ethical public service
5. **Institutional capacity** — Strengthen the entity's ability to deliver on its BOL mandate and BDP commitments

### Islamic Governance Principles

The supervision system integrates five Islamic leadership principles:

| Principle | Arabic | Application to Supervision |
|---|---|---|
| **Integrity** | Amanah (trust/trustworthiness) | Supervisors are entrusted with the development and well-being of their staff. This trust requires competence, fairness, and responsibility. |
| **Justice** | Adl | Performance evaluation, workload distribution, recognition, and discipline must be fair and equitable — applied consistently regardless of personal relationships. |
| **Consultation** | Shura | Supervisors seek input from staff on decisions that affect them. Goal-setting, process improvement, and problem-solving benefit from collective wisdom. |
| **Compassion** | Rahmah | Supervision considers the whole person — acknowledging personal circumstances, providing support during difficulties, and leading with empathy. |
| **Truthfulness** | Sidq | Honest feedback, transparent evaluation, and truthful communication build trust and enable genuine improvement. |

These principles are not abstract values — they translate into specific supervisory behaviors throughout the system.

## Section II: Scope and Applicability

### Who This System Covers

- **Primary audience**: All personnel in supervisory or managerial positions (Director, Division Chief, Section Head, Unit Head, Team Leader)
- **Secondary audience**: All staff who report to a supervisor (as recipients of supervision)
- **Excluded**: Elected officials, political appointees with no supervisory function, consultants/contractors (unless managing staff)

### Organizational Levels

| Level | Title Examples | Primary Supervisory Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Executive** | Minister, Deputy Minister, Head of Entity, Director | Strategic direction, policy decisions, organizational performance |
| **Senior Management** | Division Chief, Department Head, Assistant Director | Division performance, staff management, cross-unit coordination |
| **Middle Management** | Section Head, Unit Head, Program Manager | Day-to-day operations, task distribution, quality assurance |
| **First-line Supervisor** | Team Leader, Senior Staff with supervisory function | Direct staff guidance, work review, mentoring |

Adapt these levels to match the entity's actual organizational structure and position titles.

## Section III: Legal and Regulatory Framework

### Applicable Laws and Regulations

1. **Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054)** — Establishes the BARMM government structure and mandates of entities
2. **Bangsamoro Administrative Code** — Governance and administrative procedures for BARMM entities
3. **Civil Service Commission (CSC) Rules** — Merit and fitness principles, SPMS, administrative discipline
4. **CSC Strategic Performance Management System (SPMS)** — Framework for IPCR/OPCR-based performance evaluation
5. **Relevant Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts (BAAs)** — Entity-specific organic legislation
6. **Entity-specific issuances** — Internal policies, office orders, memoranda on supervision and performance

### Alignment Notes

- The supervision system complements but does not replace CSC SPMS requirements
- Performance evaluation cycles align with CSC mandated timelines (January-June, July-December)
- Progressive discipline follows CSC Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service (RRACCS)
- The system adds developmental supervision layers (coaching, check-ins, feedback) on top of CSC compliance requirements

## Section IV: Supervisory Roles and Responsibilities

### By Level

#### Director / Head of Entity

| Responsibility | Frequency | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Set strategic direction for supervision | Annually | Supervision policy, strategic priorities |
| Approve division-level performance targets | Quarterly | Approved OPCRs |
| Conduct performance reviews with Division Chiefs | Quarterly | Performance review records |
| Model supervisory values and behavior | Ongoing | Visible leadership |
| Ensure resources for staff development | Annually | Training budget, IDP support |
| Review and improve the supervision system | Annually | Updated supervision system document |

#### Division Chief / Department Head

| Responsibility | Frequency | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Cascade division targets to section/individual goals | Quarterly | Section targets, staff IPCRs |
| Conduct performance check-ins with Section Heads | Monthly | Check-in notes, action items |
| Review CSW before submission to Director | Per submission | Cleared/endorsed CSW documents |
| Manage underperformance in the division | As needed | PIPs, coaching plans, documentation |
| Develop and implement staff IDPs | Annually | IDPs, training nominations |
| Coordinate cross-section work within the division | Ongoing | Work plans, coordination meetings |
| Report division performance to Director | Quarterly | Division performance reports |

#### Section Head / Unit Head

| Responsibility | Frequency | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Distribute tasks and set daily/weekly priorities | Daily/Weekly | Task assignments, work plans |
| Conduct one-on-one check-ins with staff | Weekly | Check-in notes |
| Review work quality and provide real-time feedback | Ongoing | Feedback records, quality notes |
| Monitor staff attendance and compliance | Daily | Attendance records, exception reports |
| Flag performance issues to Division Chief | As needed | Performance concern memos |
| Guide staff through the ADDRESS IT process | Per CSW assignment | Supervisor touchpoints at each step |
| Mentor new staff during onboarding | First 90 days | Onboarding checklist, mentoring notes |

### Supervisory Standards

Every supervisor, regardless of level, is expected to:

1. Know the supervision system and apply it consistently
2. Conduct regular check-ins with direct reports at the prescribed frequency
3. Give feedback that is specific, timely, and actionable
4. Document supervisory interactions (coaching notes, performance conversations, feedback given)
5. Complete performance evaluations on time and with evidence-based ratings
6. Develop at least one staff member actively through a structured IDP
7. Model the values of integrity, justice, consultation, compassion, and truthfulness

## Section V: Performance Management Cycle

### The Four Phases

```
PLANNING → MONITORING → EVALUATION → IMPROVEMENT
   ↑                                        ↓
   ←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←
```

#### Phase 1: Planning (Start of rating period)

- Cascade organizational targets to individual performance commitments
- Conduct goal-setting conversations with each staff member
- Document IPCRs with SMART targets
- Agree on competency development goals
- Establish check-in schedules and communication expectations

#### Phase 2: Monitoring (Throughout the rating period)

- Conduct regular check-ins at prescribed frequency
- Track progress against IPCR targets
- Provide real-time feedback (corrective and positive)
- Document coaching conversations and supervisory observations
- Identify issues early and intervene before they become crises
- Adjust goals if circumstances change (mid-year review)

#### Phase 3: Evaluation (End of rating period)

- Gather performance evidence from multiple sources
- Conduct formal performance appraisal conversation
- Rate performance against IPCR targets and competency standards
- Discuss strengths, areas for improvement, and career aspirations
- Complete IPCR rating and submit for review/approval
- Participate in calibration sessions with peer supervisors (if applicable)

#### Phase 4: Improvement (Post-evaluation, feeds into next Planning)

- Develop or update Individual Development Plans based on evaluation results
- Implement coaching plans for underperformers
- Recognize and reward strong performance
- Plan stretch assignments and development opportunities for high-potential staff
- Feed lessons learned into the next planning cycle

### Performance Evaluation Alignment

| Entity Cycle | CSC SPMS Cycle | Supervision System Activity |
|---|---|---|
| January | Start of first semester | IPCR goal-setting, IDP planning |
| March | Mid-semester check | Formal mid-semester check-in |
| June | End of first semester | First semester IPCR evaluation |
| July | Start of second semester | Revised IPCR goals, mid-year IDP review |
| September | Mid-semester check | Formal mid-semester check-in |
| December | End of second semester | Annual IPCR evaluation, IDP completion review |

## Section VI: Coaching and Feedback Protocols

### Check-In Cadences

| Interaction | Frequency | Duration | Format | Who Initiates |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Informal guidance | Daily | 5-10 min | In-person or chat | Either party |
| Structured one-on-one | Weekly | 15-30 min | Private meeting | Supervisor |
| Performance conversation | Monthly | 30-45 min | Private meeting with documentation | Supervisor |
| Formal review | Quarterly | 45-60 min | Scheduled, documented, aligned with SPMS | Supervisor |

### Feedback Model: SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)

1. **Situation**: Describe the specific context (when, where)
2. **Behavior**: Describe the observable behavior (what the person did or said — not what they intended)
3. **Impact**: Describe the effect of the behavior (on the work, the team, the stakeholders, or the supervisor)

Example: "During yesterday's committee meeting (S), when you presented the budget analysis without the comparative data from last fiscal year (B), the committee members could not assess whether spending had increased or decreased, which delayed their decision (I)."

### Coaching Approach by Performer Level

| Performer Level | Coaching Style | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **New / struggling** | Directive — tell, show, guide | Build basic competence and confidence |
| **Developing** | Collaborative — ask, discuss, co-create | Expand capability through guided practice |
| **Strong** | Empowering — challenge, stretch, delegate | Deepen expertise and broaden scope |
| **Star** | Mentoring — strategic exposure, leadership | Prepare for expanded leadership roles |

### Documentation

Maintain a supervision log for each direct report containing:
- Date and type of interaction (check-in, feedback, coaching, performance review)
- Key topics discussed
- Action items and commitments (by both parties)
- Follow-up from previous interactions
- Any performance concerns or commendations noted

## Section VII: Staff Development Framework

### Individual Development Plans (IDPs)

Each staff member develops an IDP with their supervisor annually, covering:

1. **Current role assessment** — Competency strengths and gaps relative to role requirements
2. **Career aspirations** — Where the staff member wants to grow (not just what the organization needs)
3. **Development goals** — 2-3 specific competencies to develop this year
4. **Interventions** — For each goal, specific actions:
   - Formal training (courses, certifications)
   - On-the-job learning (stretch assignments, job rotation, project leadership)
   - Mentoring (internal or external mentor)
   - Self-directed learning (reading, online courses, professional networks)
5. **Timeline and milestones** — When each intervention happens and how progress is measured
6. **Resources needed** — Budget, time, access, management support
7. **Success indicators** — How both parties will know the development goal was achieved

### Career Pathing

Map competency requirements for each level so staff can see what is needed for advancement:

| Level | Position Type | Required Competencies | Typical Development Path |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry | Staff/Technical Officer | Technical skills, CSW production, teamwork | Formal training, mentoring, CSW practice |
| Mid | Senior Staff/Specialist | Advanced technical, analytical, communication | Stretch assignments, cross-unit projects |
| Supervisory | Section Head/Unit Head | All 12 supervisory competencies at Proficient level | Supervisory development course, coaching practicum |
| Managerial | Division Chief | Strategic thinking, team leadership, change management at Advanced level | Leadership programs, executive coaching, job rotation |
| Executive | Director/Head of Entity | All competencies at Advanced-Expert level | Strategic assignments, inter-agency leadership, policy development |

## Section VIII: Delegation and Decision-Making

### Delegation Matrix

| Decision Type | Section Head | Division Chief | Director |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily task assignment | Decides and acts | Informed as needed | Not involved |
| CSW quality clearance | Reviews, clears for Division Chief | Reviews, endorses for Director | Approves |
| Leave approval (1-3 days) | Recommends | Approves | Informed |
| Leave approval (4+ days) | Recommends | Recommends | Approves |
| Training nomination | Recommends | Approves (within budget) | Approves (if exceeds budget) |
| Procurement (below threshold) | Initiates | Approves | Informed |
| Procurement (above threshold) | Initiates | Recommends | Approves |
| Performance rating | Rates | Reviews and calibrates | Approves final ratings |
| Disciplinary action (informal) | Initiates | Informed | Not involved |
| Disciplinary action (formal) | Documents and recommends | Reviews and endorses | Decides (with HR/Legal) |
| Policy interpretation | Escalates | Decides (routine) or escalates | Decides (complex) |
| Inter-agency commitment | Not authorized | Not authorized | Decides |

Adapt thresholds and categories to match the entity's actual authority structure and procurement rules.

### Escalation Protocol

When an issue exceeds a supervisor's authority:
1. Document the issue and the analysis done so far
2. Present options with a recommendation (not just the problem)
3. Escalate to the next level with the documentation
4. The escalating supervisor remains involved — escalation does not mean abdication

### Acting Authority

When a supervisor is absent:
- Designate an acting authority in writing before the absence
- Specify which authorities transfer and which are suspended until return
- The acting authority follows the same supervision standards
- Brief the returning supervisor on all actions taken during the absence

## Section IX: Accountability and Compliance Mechanisms

### Reporting Requirements

| Level | Report | Frequency | Submitted To |
|---|---|---|---|
| Staff | Task accomplishment report | Weekly | Section Head |
| Section Head | Section performance summary | Monthly | Division Chief |
| Division Chief | Division performance report | Quarterly | Director |
| Director | Entity performance report | Semi-annually | Ministry/Commission |

### Audit Mechanisms

- **CSW quality audits** — Random sampling of CSW outputs for completeness, accuracy, and compliance with ADDRESS IT
- **Supervision compliance checks** — Verify that check-ins, feedback, and evaluations are happening at prescribed frequencies
- **Documentation audits** — Review supervision logs, IPCRs, coaching records for completeness
- **Staff satisfaction surveys** — Annual anonymous survey on supervision quality and supervisor effectiveness

### Grievance and Appeals

- Staff may raise concerns about supervisory conduct through:
  1. Direct conversation with the supervisor (preferred first step)
  2. Written concern to the supervisor's supervisor
  3. Formal grievance to HR/Admin division
  4. Appeal to the head of entity
- All grievances are investigated within 15 working days
- Whistleblower protection applies per CSC and BARMM policies
- No retaliation for good-faith complaints

### Progressive Discipline

| Step | Action | When Used | Documentation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Coaching conversation | First occurrence, minor issue | Supervision log entry |
| 2 | Verbal warning | Repeated minor issue or moderate issue | Verbal warning memo to file |
| 3 | Written warning | Continued issue after verbal warning | Formal written warning |
| 4 | Performance Improvement Plan | Sustained underperformance | PIP document with timeline |
| 5 | Formal administrative charge | Serious misconduct or PIP failure | Per CSC RRACCS |

## Section X: M&E for the Supervision System

### System Health Indicators

| Indicator | Target | Data Source |
|---|---|---|
| Check-in compliance rate | 90%+ of scheduled check-ins conducted | Supervision logs |
| Feedback frequency | Average 2+ feedback instances per staff per month | Supervision logs |
| IPCR completion rate | 100% of staff have current IPCRs | HR records |
| IDP completion rate | 80%+ of staff have current IDPs | HR records |
| Staff satisfaction with supervision | 75%+ positive on annual survey | Anonymous survey |
| Supervisor competency score | Average 3.0+ (Proficient) on the 12-competency rubric | Pre/post assessments |
| Underperformance resolution rate | 70%+ of PIPs result in improvement | PIP outcome records |

### Review Cycle

| Review Type | Frequency | Scope | Led By |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operational review | Semi-annual | Are check-ins happening? Is feedback being given? Quick fixes. | Division Chiefs |
| Comprehensive review | Annual | Full system assessment. Policy updates. Training needs. | Director + HR |
| Triggered review | As needed | After major change (restructuring, new mandate, leadership transition) | Director |

### Amendment Procedures

1. Any supervisor may propose changes through their Division Chief
2. HR/Admin division collects and reviews proposals
3. Director approves amendments with stakeholder consultation
4. Updated system document is versioned and redistributed
5. Training is provided on significant changes

### Continuous Improvement Loop

```
Collect data → Analyze trends → Identify gaps → Design interventions → Implement → Measure impact → Repeat
```

The supervision system is a living document. Version it, date it, and review it regularly.
