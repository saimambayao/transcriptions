# Supervisory Development Course — Training Blueprint

A 5-day intensive course for supervisors and division chiefs in BARMM entities. Based on the MDN Supervisory Development Course (12 modules), designed for delivery as a standalone course or integrated into a broader capacity building program.

---

## Course Overview

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Supervisory Development Course |
| **Duration** | 5 days (40 hours) |
| **Target audience** | Newly appointed supervisors, Division Chiefs, Section Heads, Unit Heads, Team Leaders |
| **Prerequisite** | Currently in a supervisory role or designated for promotion to one |
| **Maximum participants** | 25-30 (to allow meaningful practice exercises) |
| **Delivery mode** | In-person, facilitated workshop with lectures, discussions, role-plays, case studies, and practicum |
| **Competency framework** | The 12 Supervisory Competencies (see supervisory-competencies.md) |
| **Assessment** | Pre-assessment (Day 1 morning) + Post-assessment (Day 5 afternoon) + 90-day follow-up |

---

## Daily Schedule Overview

| Day | Theme | Competencies Covered | Key Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Foundations and Assessment | 1 (Foundations), 2 (Assessment) | Supervisors understand their role and can assess staff performance |
| 2 | Feedback, Coaching, and Goals | 3 (Feedback), 4 (Coaching), 5 (Goals) | Supervisors can give effective feedback, coach, and set clear goals |
| 3 | Check-Ins, Underperformance, and Talent | 6 (Check-Ins), 7 (Underperformance), 8 (Talent) | Supervisors can manage the full performance spectrum |
| 4 | Teams, Change, and Delegation | 9 (Teams), 10 (Change), 11 (Delegation) | Supervisors can lead teams and manage organizational dynamics |
| 5 | Accountability, Practicum, and Action Planning | 12 (Accountability) + Integration | Supervisors commit to specific actions and demonstrate integrated competency |

## Day 1: Foundations and Assessment

### Morning (4 hours)

#### Opening and Pre-Assessment (1.5 hours)
- Welcome, course objectives, expectations setting
- Ice-breaker: "My best supervisor and my worst supervisor" (draw out key behaviors)
- **Pre-assessment**: Participants self-rate on all 12 competencies using the 5-level rubric (Limited through Expert)
- Facilitator collects pre-assessment data for comparison on Day 5

#### Module I: Foundations of Supervision (2.5 hours)
- **Lecture**: The supervisor's role — from doer to leader
  - What changes when you become a supervisor (responsibilities, relationships, time allocation)
  - The dual accountability: upward to leadership, downward to staff
  - The coaching mindset vs. the directing mindset
- **Discussion**: Islamic leadership principles in supervision
  - Amanah: the trust placed in supervisors
  - Adl: fairness in workload distribution, evaluation, and recognition
  - Shura: consulting staff before decisions that affect them
  - Rahmah: leading with compassion without sacrificing standards
  - Sidq: truthful feedback and transparent communication
- **Exercise**: "A Day in the Life" — participants map their typical day and identify how much time they spend doing work vs. managing people. Discussion on ideal allocation.

### Afternoon (4 hours)

#### Module II: Assessing Performance, Potential, and Readiness (4 hours)
- **Lecture**: The three dimensions — performance (what they deliver), potential (what they could become), readiness (what they are prepared for now)
- **Tool**: The 9-Box Grid — plotting staff on performance vs. potential
  - High performance + high potential: future leaders
  - High performance + low potential: solid contributors (do not neglect them)
  - Low performance + high potential: coaching investments
  - Low performance + low potential: difficult but necessary conversations
- **Lecture**: Common rating biases and how to avoid them
  - Recency bias: rating based on recent events rather than the full period
  - Halo/horn effect: one trait coloring the entire assessment
  - Leniency bias: rating everyone high to avoid conflict
  - Central tendency: rating everyone average to be "safe"
- **Exercise**: Case study assessment — given profiles of 6 hypothetical staff members, participants rate their performance, potential, and readiness. Followed by calibration discussion where participants explain and defend their ratings.
- **Exercise**: 9-Box mapping of actual team — participants plot their real direct reports (anonymized during group discussion) and discuss development strategies for each quadrant.

## Day 2: Feedback, Coaching, and Goals

### Morning (4 hours)

#### Module III: Giving Effective Feedback (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Why feedback fails — common mistakes (too vague, too late, too personal, too harsh, too soft)
- **Framework**: The SBI model
  - Situation: specific context (time, place, circumstances)
  - Behavior: observable action (not interpretation or motive)
  - Impact: consequence on work, team, or stakeholders
- **Lecture**: Preparing for feedback conversations
  - Gather evidence, identify the key message, anticipate reactions, plan the opening
- **Lecture**: Handling defensive reactions
  - Acknowledge emotion without backing down from the feedback
  - Refocus on observable behavior and impact
  - Separate the feedback from the relationship
- **Exercise**: SBI feedback practice in pairs — each participant writes 2 SBI statements (one corrective, one positive) about a real workplace situation, delivers them to a partner, and receives feedback on the feedback.
- **Exercise**: "Feedback fishbowl" — one volunteer gives feedback in the center while the group observes, then debriefs on what worked and what could improve.

#### Module IV: Coaching for Performance and Development (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Coaching vs. directing — when to use each
  - Coaching works when staff have some capability but need guidance to find their own path
  - Directing works when staff need specific instructions due to inexperience or urgency
- **Framework**: The GROW coaching model
  - **G**oal: What do you want to achieve?
  - **R**eality: Where are you now? What have you tried?
  - **O**ptions: What could you do? What else?
  - **W**ill: What will you do? By when? What support do you need?
- **Lecture**: Adapting coaching to performer level
  - New/struggling: more directive, structured, frequent
  - Developing: collaborative, guided exploration
  - Strong: empowering, challenging, delegating
  - Star: mentoring, strategic, leadership-focused
- **Exercise**: Coaching triads — groups of 3, rotating roles (coach, coachee, observer). Each person coaches for 15 minutes on a real work challenge. Observer gives feedback using a coaching effectiveness checklist.

### Afternoon (4 hours)

#### Module V: Setting Goals and Expectations (4 hours)
- **Lecture**: Goal cascade — from BDP and entity mandate to division targets to individual IPCRs
  - How organizational strategy translates into measurable individual contributions
  - The difference between outputs (what you produce) and outcomes (what changes because of your work)
- **Framework**: SMART goals — turning vague expectations into measurable commitments
  - Common mistakes: goals that are too broad, not time-bound, or not within the staff member's control
- **Lecture**: Collaborative goal-setting — involving staff in setting their own goals
  - Why imposed goals generate compliance, but collaborative goals generate commitment
  - When the supervisor must set non-negotiable goals (regulatory requirements, non-negotiable standards)
- **Lecture**: Expectation-setting conversations
  - Quality standards, communication norms, reporting expectations, professional behavior
  - Written documentation of expectations — verbal agreements are forgotten
- **Exercise**: IPCR writing workshop — participants draft 3 SMART performance targets for one of their direct reports, then peer-review each other's targets for SMART compliance.
- **Exercise**: Expectation-setting role-play — supervisor sets expectations with a new staff member, including quality standards, check-in schedule, and reporting requirements.

## Day 3: Check-Ins, Underperformance, and Talent

### Morning (4 hours)

#### Module VI: Conducting Effective Check-Ins (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Check-ins vs. status updates — the check-in is developmental, not just transactional
- **Lecture**: Preparation, agenda, and purposeful conversations
  - Supervisor prepares: reviews task status, identifies discussion points, brings data
  - Staff prepares: updates on progress, identifies blockers, comes with questions
  - Agenda: progress, blockers, priorities, support needed, development, personal well-being
- **Lecture**: Key questions for effective check-ins
  - "What progress are you most proud of this week?"
  - "What is blocking you that I can help remove?"
  - "What do you need from me that you are not getting?"
  - "Is there anything you are struggling with that we should discuss?"
- **Exercise**: Check-in simulation — pairs conduct a 15-minute check-in using the structured agenda. Partner gives feedback on listening, questioning, and follow-up quality.

#### Module VII: Managing Underperformance (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Identifying underperformance early — signals and patterns
  - Declining output quality, missed deadlines, decreased engagement, increased absences
- **Framework**: Root cause analysis before intervention
  - **Capability gap** → Training, coaching, mentoring
  - **Motivation gap** → Feedback, incentives, consequences
  - **Systems gap** → Fix the process, not the person
  - **Personal issues** → Support, accommodation, referral
- **Lecture**: Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
  - Structure: clear standards, specific actions, supervisor support, timeline, consequences
  - PIPs should be supportive instruments, not punishment — they give the person a fair chance
  - Documentation requirements for due process
- **Lecture**: Progressive discipline aligned with CSC rules
  - Coaching → verbal warning → written warning → PIP → formal charges
  - Due process requirements at each step
- **Exercise**: Root cause diagnosis — given 4 underperformance case studies, participants diagnose the root cause and design the intervention. Group discussion on whether participants agree on the diagnosis.
- **Exercise**: PIP drafting — participants draft a PIP for one of the case study employees.

### Afternoon (4 hours)

#### Module VIII: Developing Talent (4 hours)
- **Lecture**: The supervisor as talent developer — development is a core supervisory responsibility, not HR's job
- **Lecture**: Career pathing in government
  - Competency requirements at each level
  - Balancing merit-based advancement with civil service tenure rules
  - The role of stretch assignments, job rotation, and project leadership
- **Framework**: Individual Development Plans (IDPs)
  - Current assessment, career aspirations, development goals, interventions, timeline, success indicators
  - The IDP conversation — making it collaborative, not imposed
- **Lecture**: Succession planning
  - For every critical role, at least one person should be developing toward readiness
  - Succession planning in government: navigating plantilla positions, appointment processes, and merit systems
- **Lecture**: Mentoring as a development tool
  - Formal vs. informal mentoring
  - Selecting mentors, structuring the relationship, measuring impact
- **Exercise**: IDP workshop — each participant drafts an IDP for one actual direct report (bring data from the 9-Box exercise on Day 1). Peer review and discussion.
- **Exercise**: Succession mapping — participants identify critical roles in their unit and assess bench strength.

## Day 4: Teams, Change, and Delegation

### Morning (4 hours)

#### Module IX: Leading Teams (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Team dynamics — Tuckman's model (forming, storming, norming, performing)
  - How supervisor behavior needs to adapt at each stage
  - Recognizing when the team is stuck in storming
- **Lecture**: Team motivation in government
  - Public service motivation — why people join government and what sustains them
  - Intrinsic motivators: purpose, mastery, autonomy (Daniel Pink)
  - What supervisors can control vs. what they cannot (salary, promotion are constrained; recognition, assignments, growth are not)
- **Lecture**: Managing conflict within teams
  - Interest-based negotiation: separate people from problems, focus on interests not positions
  - When to mediate, when to direct, when to escalate
- **Lecture**: Cross-functional coordination
  - Working with other divisions and sections
  - Managing dependencies without authority
- **Exercise**: Team assessment — participants complete a team effectiveness survey for their actual team and identify 2-3 improvement actions.

#### Module X: Managing Change (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Supervisors as change translators — the bridge between leadership decisions and frontline implementation
- **Framework**: Kotter's 8-Step Change Model (adapted for government)
  1. Create urgency (why the change is necessary)
  2. Build a coalition (who supports it)
  3. Form a vision (what success looks like)
  4. Communicate widely (not once — repeatedly, through multiple channels)
  5. Empower action (remove barriers)
  6. Generate quick wins (build momentum)
  7. Consolidate gains (do not declare victory too early)
  8. Anchor in culture (make it "how we do things now")
- **Lecture**: Understanding resistance
  - Resistance is information, not opposition — it tells you what people fear or do not understand
  - Common sources: fear of incompetence, loss of status, increased workload, distrust of leadership
- **Lecture**: BARMM-specific change dynamics
  - BOL transition challenges
  - BTA-to-elected government transition
  - Ministerial restructuring and mandate changes
- **Exercise**: Change communication plan — given a realistic BARMM change scenario, participants draft communication messages for different audiences (staff, peers, external stakeholders).

### Afternoon (4 hours)

#### Module XI: Delegation and Empowerment (4 hours)
- **Lecture**: What to delegate, what to keep
  - Delegate: routine tasks, developmental opportunities, tasks within staff capability
  - Keep: strategic decisions, confidential matters, tasks requiring supervisor-level authority, new/critical tasks during learning phase
- **Framework**: Authority levels
  - Level 1: Investigate and report — I decide
  - Level 2: Recommend — I decide
  - Level 3: Decide and inform me before acting
  - Level 4: Decide and act — report what you did
  - Level 5: Decide and act — no report needed unless problem
- **Lecture**: Common delegation failures
  - Micromanaging: delegating the task but not the authority
  - Abdication: delegating and disappearing
  - Reverse delegation: staff pushes the task back up ("what should I do?")
  - Uneven distribution: same person always gets the important/easy work
- **Lecture**: Delegation as development
  - Stretch assignments build capability — but match the stretch to the person's readiness
  - Use authority levels to calibrate empowerment: start at Level 2, increase as trust builds
- **Exercise**: Delegation audit — participants list their current workload and categorize each item: keep, delegate (to whom, at what authority level), or eliminate. Group discussion on what is hardest to let go.
- **Exercise**: Delegation conversation role-play — practice delegating a task with clear authority level, resources, deadline, and reporting expectations.

## Day 5: Accountability, Practicum, and Action Planning

### Morning (4 hours)

#### Module XII: Building a Culture of Accountability (2 hours)
- **Lecture**: Accountability starts with clarity — people cannot be accountable for unknown expectations
- **Lecture**: The accountability cycle
  - Clear standards → Commitment → Follow-through → Consequences (positive and corrective)
- **Lecture**: Recognition practices
  - Public recognition, commendation letters, team celebrations, stretch assignment as reward
  - Recognition must be specific, timely, and genuine — not generic "good job"
- **Lecture**: Consistent consequences
  - The biggest accountability killer: inconsistency — holding some people to standards and not others
  - Favoritism, avoidance, and fear of conflict erode accountability faster than anything else
- **Lecture**: Islamic accountability principles
  - Hisab: accountability before Allah for those entrusted to your care
  - The hadith on leadership responsibility: "Every one of you is a shepherd, and every one of you is responsible for his flock"
- **Exercise**: Accountability audit — participants assess their team culture against 10 accountability indicators and identify the top 3 areas for improvement.

#### Integration Practicum (2 hours)
- **Scenario-based exercise**: Given a complex supervision scenario involving multiple competencies (e.g., underperforming team member during an organizational change, with delegation decisions and feedback needed), participants work through the scenario applying tools from all 12 competencies.
- Scenarios should be based on the entity's actual supervision challenges (prepared by the facilitator using Phase 1 assessment data).
- Small group work (4-5 people) followed by group presentation and facilitated debrief.

### Afternoon (4 hours)

#### Post-Assessment (1 hour)
- **Post-assessment**: Participants self-rate on all 12 competencies again using the same 5-level rubric
- Facilitator compares pre/post data to show growth and remaining gaps
- Individual reflection: "Which 2-3 competencies showed the most growth? Which still need work?"

#### Personal Action Planning (1.5 hours)
- Each participant develops a 90-day supervisory action plan:
  - **3 immediate actions** (start this week): e.g., schedule weekly check-ins, start a supervision log, give SBI feedback to one staff member
  - **3 short-term goals** (within 30 days): e.g., conduct a goal-setting conversation with each direct report, draft an IDP for one staff member, complete a delegation audit
  - **3 medium-term goals** (within 90 days): e.g., implement a full check-in cycle, complete one coaching engagement, conduct a mid-year performance review
- Accountability partner: pair each participant with another for mutual check-ins over the 90-day period
- Supervisor commitment: participants' own supervisors receive a copy of the action plan and commit to supporting implementation

#### Closing (1.5 hours)
- Course evaluation forms
- Certificate ceremony
- Group photo
- Facilitator summary: key takeaways, common themes, encouragement
- Announcement of post-training support: peer learning circles, monthly coaching calls, online resource access

## Post-Training Support Structure

Training without follow-up decays rapidly. Implement these support mechanisms:

### 90-Day Coaching Plan
- Month 1: Weekly 15-minute check-ins with facilitator or peer coach (focus on immediate actions)
- Month 2: Biweekly check-ins (focus on short-term goals)
- Month 3: Monthly check-in (focus on medium-term goals and sustained change)

### Peer Learning Circles
- Groups of 5-6 participants meet monthly for 1 hour
- Rotating facilitation
- Agenda: share wins, discuss challenges, practice skills, accountability check

### Supervisor Communities of Practice
- Quarterly gatherings of all supervisors in the entity
- Themed sessions on specific competencies
- Case study discussions using real (anonymized) supervision challenges
- Guest speakers from senior leadership

### 90-Day Follow-Up Assessment
- Repeat the 12-competency self-assessment
- Staff upward feedback (anonymous survey of direct reports)
- Supervisor assessment by the participant's own supervisor
- Compare with pre-training and post-training data to measure sustained change

## Adaptation Notes

### For Different Entity Sizes

| Entity Size | Adaptation |
|---|---|
| **Small** (under 50 staff, few supervisors) | Compress to 3 days. Combine Modules I-II, III-V, IX-XI. More peer discussion, less formal exercise. |
| **Medium** (50-200 staff) | Full 5-day course as designed. |
| **Large** (200+ staff, multiple offices) | Add Day 6 for entity-specific practicum. Run separate cohorts for different supervisory levels. |

### For Different Supervisory Levels

| Level | Emphasis | De-emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| **First-time supervisors** | Modules I-VI (foundations, feedback, coaching, goals, check-ins) | Modules IX-X (teams and change — introduce but do not go deep) |
| **Experienced supervisors** | Modules VII-XII (underperformance, talent, teams, change, delegation, accountability) | Module I (brief refresher only) |
| **Division Chiefs** | Modules IX-XII + strategic performance management, cross-division coordination, succession planning | Daily operations-level content (Module VI check-ins — reframe for division-level cadence) |

### For Refresher Training

| Duration | Content |
|---|---|
| **Half-day** | Review action plan progress, practice one competency in depth, set new goals |
| **1 day** | Competency assessment update, two competency deep dives based on gap data, new action plan |
| **2 days** | Full reassessment, three competency workshops, case study practicum, updated action plan |
