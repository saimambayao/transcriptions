## Ratio Analysis

### Liquidity Ratios
Measure ability to meet short-term obligations.

| Ratio | Formula | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | >1 = can cover short-term debts |
| **Quick Ratio** | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | More conservative; excludes inventory |
| **Cash Ratio** | Cash / Current Liabilities | Most stringent; cash only |

**Benchmarks:**
- Current Ratio: 1.5-2.0 generally healthy
- Quick Ratio: >1.0 preferred
- Industry-specific variations apply

### Profitability Ratios
Measure earnings and returns.

| Ratio | Formula | What It Shows |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | Markup on products/services |
| **Operating Margin** | Operating Income / Revenue | Efficiency of core operations |
| **Net Margin** | Net Income / Revenue | Bottom-line profitability |
| **ROA** | Net Income / Average Total Assets | Asset utilization for profit |
| **ROE** | Net Income / Average Equity | Return to owners |

### Efficiency Ratios
Measure operational effectiveness.

| Ratio | Formula | What It Shows |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Asset Turnover** | Revenue / Average Total Assets | Sales per dollar of assets |
| **Inventory Turnover** | COGS / Average Inventory | Times inventory sold per year |
| **Receivables Turnover** | Revenue / Average Receivables | Collection efficiency |
| **Days Sales Outstanding** | 365 / Receivables Turnover | Average collection period |
| **Days Inventory Outstanding** | 365 / Inventory Turnover | Average days to sell inventory |

### Solvency (Leverage) Ratios
Measure long-term financial stability.

| Ratio | Formula | What It Shows |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Debt-to-Assets** | Total Debt / Total Assets | Portion financed by debt |
| **Debt-to-Equity** | Total Debt / Total Equity | Leverage level |
| **Interest Coverage** | EBIT / Interest Expense | Ability to pay interest |
| **Debt Service Coverage** | Net Operating Income / Total Debt Service | Ability to service debt |

## Cash Flow Analysis

### Working Capital Management

**Cash Conversion Cycle:**
```
CCC = Days Inventory + Days Receivables - Days Payables
```

| Component | Goal |
|-----------|------|
| Days Inventory | Minimize (sell faster) |
| Days Receivables | Minimize (collect faster) |
| Days Payables | Maximize (pay slower, within terms) |

**Shorter CCC = Better cash flow**

### Cash Flow Drivers

| Improve Cash Inflows | Improve Cash Outflows |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Faster collections | Negotiate payment terms |
| Deposits on orders | Lease vs. buy decisions |
| Reduce credit terms | Inventory optimization |
| Diversify revenue | Control operating costs |

### Liquidity Management

**Liquidity Hierarchy:**
1. Cash and bank balances
2. Short-term investments
3. Accounts receivable
4. Inventory
5. Other current assets

## Variance Analysis

### Types of Variances

| Variance Type | Comparison | Purpose |
|---------------|------------|---------|
| **Budget-to-Actual** | Actual vs. Annual Budget | Performance against plan |
| **Forecast-to-Actual** | Actual vs. Recent Forecast | Prediction accuracy |
| **Period-over-Period** | Current vs. Prior Period | Trend analysis |

### Variance Classification

| Variance | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Favorable** | Better than expected | Revenue higher, costs lower |
| **Unfavorable** | Worse than expected | Revenue lower, costs higher |

### Variance Analysis Process

```
1. CALCULATE variance (Actual - Budget)
         ↓
2. IDENTIFY materiality (Is it significant?)
         ↓
3. INVESTIGATE root cause
         ↓
4. EXPLAIN the "why"
         ↓
5. RECOMMEND corrective action
         ↓
6. UPDATE forecast if needed
```

### Common Causes of Variances

| Revenue Variances | Cost Variances |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Volume changes | Price changes (materials, labor) |
| Price changes | Efficiency changes |
| Mix changes | Volume changes |
| Timing differences | Unplanned expenses |
| Market conditions | Waste/shrinkage |

## Financial Health Assessment

### Financial Health Checklist

```
Liquidity:
□ Current ratio > 1.0?
□ Positive working capital?
□ Cash reserves adequate?

Profitability:
□ Gross margin stable/improving?
□ Operating margin positive?
□ Net income positive?

Efficiency:
□ Inventory turnover improving?
□ Receivables collected timely?
□ Asset turnover reasonable?

Solvency:
□ Debt-to-equity manageable?
□ Interest coverage > 2x?
□ Debt declining or stable?

Cash Flow:
□ Operating cash flow positive?
□ CCC improving?
□ Cash reserves growing?
```

### Red Flags

| Category | Warning Signs |
|----------|---------------|
| **Liquidity** | Current ratio < 1, declining cash |
| **Profitability** | Declining margins, consistent losses |
| **Efficiency** | Rising days receivables, slow inventory |
| **Solvency** | Rising debt-to-equity, interest coverage < 1 |
| **Cash Flow** | Negative operating cash flow |

### Trend Analysis

**Horizontal Analysis:**
- Compare line items across periods
- Calculate percentage change
- Identify trends and patterns

**Vertical Analysis:**
- Express each item as % of base
- Income statement: % of revenue
- Balance sheet: % of total assets

## Analysis Best Practices

1. **Use Multiple Ratios** - No single ratio tells complete story
2. **Compare to Benchmarks** - Industry averages, peer organizations
3. **Analyze Trends** - Look at 3-5 years minimum
4. **Consider Context** - Seasonality, economic conditions
5. **Look Behind Numbers** - Understand underlying drivers
6. **Regular Monitoring** - Monthly or quarterly review
