## Three Financial Statements

### 1. Income Statement (Profit & Loss)

Shows profitability over a period of time.

**Structure:**
```
Revenue (Sales)
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
= Gross Profit
- Operating Expenses
= Operating Income (EBIT)
- Interest Expense
- Taxes
= Net Income
```

**Key Metrics:**
| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| Gross Margin | Gross Profit / Revenue |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue |
| Net Margin | Net Income / Revenue |

### 2. Balance Sheet

Snapshot of financial position at a point in time.

**Fundamental Equation:**
```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
```

**Components:**
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Current Assets** | Cash, accounts receivable, inventory |
| **Non-Current Assets** | Property, equipment, intangibles |
| **Current Liabilities** | Accounts payable, short-term debt |
| **Long-Term Liabilities** | Long-term debt, bonds payable |
| **Equity** | Share capital, retained earnings |

### 3. Cash Flow Statement

Shows cash movements over a period.

**Three Sections:**
| Section | Activities |
|---------|------------|
| **Operating** | Day-to-day business (receipts, payments) |
| **Investing** | Asset purchases/sales, investments |
| **Financing** | Debt, equity, dividends |

**Key Insight:** Net income ≠ cash flow. Accrual accounting recognizes revenue when earned, not when cash received.

## Accounting Methods

### Cash Basis
- Revenue recognized when cash received
- Expenses recognized when cash paid
- Simpler, used for day-to-day management
- Not GAAP compliant for formal reporting

### Accrual Basis
- Revenue recognized when earned
- Expenses recognized when incurred
- Required for GAAP-compliant statements
- Better matches revenues with related expenses

### Modified Cash Basis
- Cash basis with adjustments
- Includes tangible assets on balance sheet
- Common in cooperatives for internal use

## Budgeting Types

### Operating Budget
| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Time Horizon** | One fiscal year |
| **Focus** | Day-to-day expenses |
| **Components** | Salaries, rent, utilities, supplies |
| **Accounting** | Hits P&L immediately |
| **Approval** | Senior management |

### Capital Budget
| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Time Horizon** | Multi-year |
| **Focus** | Long-term investments |
| **Components** | Equipment, facilities, R&D |
| **Accounting** | Capitalized and depreciated |
| **Approval** | Board approval typically required |

### Budget Components
1. **Revenue Budget** - Expected income sources
2. **Expense Budget** - Planned expenditures
3. **Cash Budget** - Expected cash flows
4. **Capital Budget** - Long-term investments

## Cost Classification

### Fixed Costs
- Do not change with production volume
- Examples: Rent, salaries, insurance, depreciation
- Remain constant over relevant range

### Variable Costs
- Change proportionally with volume
- Examples: Raw materials, direct labor, commissions
- Per-unit cost remains constant

### Semi-Variable (Mixed) Costs
- Fixed portion plus variable component
- Examples: Utilities, phone bills
- Fixed base + usage-based charges

### Break-Even Analysis

**Formula:**
```
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)
```

**Contribution Margin:**
```
Contribution Margin = Price - Variable Cost per Unit
Contribution Margin Ratio = Contribution Margin / Price
```

**Break-Even in Sales Dollars:**
```
Break-Even Sales = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin Ratio
```

## Internal Controls

### Purpose
- Prevent errors and fraud
- Safeguard assets
- Ensure accurate financial reporting
- Promote operational efficiency

### Segregation of Duties (SoD)

**Principle:** No single person should control all phases of a transaction.

**Key Segregations:**
| Function | Should Be Separate From |
|----------|------------------------|
| Authorization | Custody of assets |
| Custody | Record-keeping |
| Record-keeping | Reconciliation |

**Example - Cash Handling:**
```
□ Person A collects cash
□ Person B records transactions
□ Person C makes bank deposits
□ Person D reconciles bank statements
```

### Control Types

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Preventive** | Stop errors before they occur | Approvals, segregation |
| **Detective** | Find errors after they occur | Reconciliations, audits |
| **Corrective** | Fix identified problems | Policy updates, training |

### Small Organization Considerations
When full segregation isn't possible:
- Implement compensating controls
- Detailed supervisory review
- External oversight (board, accountant)
- Regular audits
