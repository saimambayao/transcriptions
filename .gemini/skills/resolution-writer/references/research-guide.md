# Research Guide for Bangsamoro Parliament Resolutions

This guide provides systematic research strategies for drafting well-substantiated Bangsamoro Parliament resolutions.

## Research Philosophy

**Goal:** Gather enough factual information to write credible, specific WHEREAS clauses. The goal is **targeted efficiency** - not exhaustive research, but substantive enough to create a professional resolution.

**Key Principle:** Every WHEREAS clause should be based on verifiable facts, not assumptions or generalities.

## Research Structure

All research for Bangsamoro Parliament resolutions follows three main phases:

**A. Legal Research** - Establish legal authority and basis following Philippine legal hierarchy
**B. Information Gathering** - Collect factual research about the subject matter
**C. Other Research** - Best practices, precedents, and contextual information

The depth of research in each phase depends on the resolution category and complexity (see Three-Tiered Approach below).

## A. Legal Research

Legal research establishes the constitutional and statutory authority for parliamentary action. Follow the Philippine legal hierarchy when citing legal bases in WHEREAS clauses.

### 1. Constitutional Provisions (MANDATORY)

**When to research:** MANDATORY for all resolutions to establish constitutional basis
- Identify constitutional provisions related to the resolution topic
- Establish constitutional framework for parliamentary action
- Cite specific articles and sections granting authority

**Where to search:**
- officialgazette.gov.ph - Official text of 1987 Constitution
- lawphil.net - Annotated Constitution with jurisprudence
- sc.judiciary.gov.ph - Supreme Court interpretations

**Common constitutional provisions for BARMM resolutions:**
- Article X - Local Government (autonomy provisions)
- Article XII - National Economy and Patrimony
- Article XIII - Social Justice and Human Rights
- Article XIV - Education, Science and Technology, Arts, Culture and Sports

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, Section [X], Article [Y] of the Constitution of the Philippines provides that...
```

### 2. BOL Provisions (MANDATORY) - Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054)

**When to research:** MANDATORY for all resolutions to cite specific BOL authority for parliamentary action.

**Where to search:**
- Read tool: `legal-documents/RA-11054-Bangsamoro-Organic-Law.pdf`
- officialgazette.gov.ph
- lawphil.net

**Common BOL citations by resolution category:**

**Internal/Procedural Resolutions:**
- Article VII, Section 5 - Parliament may adopt its own rules of procedure
- Article VII, Section 7 - Quorum and voting requirements
- Article VII, Section 15 - Parliamentary immunity and privileges

**Policy/Statement Resolutions:**
- Article VII, Section 10 - Power of inquiry in aid of legislation
- Article VII, Section 11 - Question hour (requiring officials to appear)
- Article VI, Sections 2-3 - Reserved and exclusive powers

**Budget/Financial Resolutions:**
- Article IX, Section 12 - Annual appropriations
- Article IX, Section 13 - Supplemental appropriations for urgent/unforeseen expenditures
- Article IX, Section 14 - Bangsamoro budget process

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, Section [X], Article [Y] of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054) provides that...
```

### 3. National Laws

**When to research:**
- Matters involving national-regional coordination
- Issues governed by national legislation
- Resolutions urging compliance with national laws

**Where to search:**
- officialgazette.gov.ph - Official Gazette for Republic Acts
- lawphil.net - Searchable database of Philippine laws
- WebSearch for specific acts (e.g., "Local Government Code Philippines")

**Common national laws for BARMM resolutions:**
- Local Government Code (RA 7160)
- General Appropriations Act (annual)
- Indigenous Peoples Rights Act (RA 8371)
- Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Act (RA 10121)
- Anti-Corruption laws
- Other relevant Republic Acts

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, Republic Act No. [XXXX], otherwise known as "[Title of Act]", provides that...
```

### 4. Related Jurisprudence

**When to research:** When applicable and supports the resolution
- Supreme Court decisions on relevant constitutional/legal issues
- Court of Appeals decisions (if relevant)
- Legal doctrines and principles applicable to resolution subject
- Precedents that strengthen the resolution's legal foundation

**Where to search:**
- sc.judiciary.gov.ph - Supreme Court decisions
- lawphil.net - Case law database
- chanrobles.com - Philippine jurisprudence

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, in [Case Name], G.R. No. [number], the Supreme Court held that...
```

### 5. Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts

**When to research:** When resolution relates to existing regional legislation
- Implementation of existing Bangsamoro laws
- Amendments to Bangsamoro legislation
- References to established regional policies
- Coordination with existing BAAs

**Where to search:**
- Read tool: `legal-documents/BAA-13-Bangsamoro-Admin-Code.pdf` (for Administrative Code)
- bangsamoro.gov.ph - Official gazette and enacted laws
- parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph - Passed legislation

**Key Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts:**
- BAA No. 13 - Bangsamoro Administrative Code (comprehensive admin regulations)
- Other BAAs on specific topics (civil service, local governance, etc.)

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. [X], entitled "[Title]", provides that...
```

### 6. Other Legal Bases (Optional - when applicable)

**When to research:** When resolution involves specific procedural or administrative matters
- Parliamentary rules and procedures (AR 268)
- Executive orders and regulations
- International agreements relevant to BARMM
- Traditional law and customs

**AR 268 - BTA House Rules:**
- Read tool: `legal-documents/AR-268-BTA-House-Rules.pdf`
- Rule XVII, Section 5 - Official resolution types
- Other rules on parliamentary procedure, committees, voting

**How to cite:**
```
WHEREAS, Rule [X], Section [Y] of the Bangsamoro Parliament Rules of Procedure (AR 268) provides that...
```

**Presidential/Executive Issuances:**
```
WHEREAS, Executive Order No. [X] directs that...
```

## B. Information Gathering (Factual Research)

Factual research provides the specific details, statistics, and context that make WHEREAS clauses credible and compelling.

### What to Research

**Depending on resolution category, gather:**

**Commendatory Resolutions:**
- Full legal name and official title/position
- Specific achievements with exact dates
- Quantifiable impact (number of people served, funds raised, etc.)
- Official awards, recognitions, or certifications
- Organizational background and affiliations
- Direct quotes from officials or beneficiaries (when available)

**Policy/Statement Resolutions:**
- Current statistics and data on the issue
- Specific incidents or examples with dates and locations
- Government reports or studies
- Impact on Bangsamoro population (numbers affected)
- Current status of the problem
- Previous related resolutions or legislative actions
- Positions of stakeholders

**Budget/Financial Resolutions:**
- Exact amounts required
- Budget breakdown by category/program
- Source of funds (block grant, IGR, national subsidy, etc.)
- Previous budget allocations for comparison
- Expected beneficiaries (number and description)
- Timeline for expenditure
- Urgency justification with supporting facts

**Internal/Procedural Resolutions:**
- Current parliamentary structure/procedures
- Gaps or inefficiencies in existing system
- Comparison with other legislative bodies (if relevant)
- Expected improvements or outcomes
- Resource requirements

### Where to Search

**Official Bangsamoro Government Sources (highest priority):**
- bangsamoro.gov.ph - Main government portal, press releases
- parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph - Parliament website, enacted laws
- Ministry websites - Program data, reports
- Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority - Statistics, development plans

**News Sources (for current events and context):**
- mindanews.com - Local Mindanao news (most relevant)
- National news outlets - Inquirer, Rappler, Manila Bulletin, PhilStar
- Official government press releases

**Statistical Sources:**
- Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)
- Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority
- National government agency reports
- International organizations (UNDP, World Bank, etc.)

**How to Search:**

Use WebSearch for initial discovery:
```
"[topic] Bangsamoro BARMM statistics"
"[issue] Mindanao [current year]"
"[ministry/office] BARMM program data"
```

Use WebFetch for detailed information from specific sources:
- Fetch official BARMM websites for authoritative data
- Fetch news articles for specific facts and quotes
- Fetch government reports for statistics

### Research Quality Standards

**Minimum factual requirements:**
- At least 3 specific facts with dates or numbers
- At least 2 independent sources corroborating key claims
- Verification of names, titles, and official positions
- Current/recent information (within past 2-3 years unless historical)

**Avoid:**
- Vague statements without specifics
- Unverified claims from single sources
- Outdated information (unless historical context)
- Assumptions about impact without data

## C. Other Research / Best Practices

Additional research to strengthen resolutions and ensure alignment with parliamentary norms.

### 1. Parliamentary Precedents

**Research similar resolutions:**
- Search parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph for previously enacted resolutions
- Review how similar issues were addressed
- Note citation patterns and WHEREAS clause structures
- Identify common RESOLVED directives for similar topics

**Value:**
- Ensures consistency with parliamentary practice
- Provides language models for formal drafting
- Identifies successful approaches to similar issues

### 2. Best Practices from Other Legislatures

**When helpful:**
- Novel or unprecedented parliamentary actions
- Complex procedural matters
- Regional governance innovations

**Where to search:**
- Other regional legislatures in the Philippines (ARMM archives, Cordillera)
- National Congress resolutions (Senate, House of Representatives)
- International parliamentary best practices (Westminster, ASEAN parliaments)

**How to use:**
- Adapt language and structure (not copy verbatim)
- Ensure compatibility with BOL and BARMM context
- Consider cultural and legal differences

### 3. Stakeholder Positions and Consultations

**Research stakeholder views when:**
- Resolution affects specific communities or sectors
- Controversial or sensitive topics
- Traditional governance matters
- Indigenous peoples' concerns

**Sources:**
- Official statements from affected ministries
- Community organization positions
- Traditional leader consultations
- Civil society reports

### 4. Academic and Research Studies

**When valuable:**
- Complex policy issues requiring expert analysis
- Evidence-based policy recommendations
- Historical or cultural context

**Sources:**
- Institute for Autonomy and Governance
- University research centers (MSU, UP Mindanao)
- International peace-building organizations
- Policy think tanks

**How to use:**
- Cite credible studies in WHEREAS clauses
- Use data and findings to support positions
- Reference expert recommendations

### 5. Cultural and Traditional Context

**Research when resolution involves:**
- Traditional governance systems
- Indigenous peoples' rights and territories
- Islamic law and customs
- Cultural heritage and practices

**Considerations:**
- Consult BOL provisions on traditional governance
- Review customary law where applicable
- Consider traditional decision-making processes
- Respect cultural protocols and sensitivities

**Sources:**
- Traditional governance experts
- Islamic scholars and institutions (for Sharia-related matters)
- Indigenous peoples' organizations
- Cultural research centers

## Minimum Research Requirements by Resolution Category

### Commendatory Resolutions

- ✓ Full name and title/position of honoree
- ✓ At least 3 specific achievements/actions with dates
- ✓ Impact or significance of contributions
- ✓ Background context (organization, role, etc.)
- ✓ Any relevant awards, recognition, or milestones

### Policy/Statement Resolutions

- ✓ Clear problem statement with current facts
- ✓ At least 2-3 specific examples or statistics
- ✓ Relevant BOL or legal provisions (if applicable)
- ✓ Impact on Bangsamoro people/region
- ✓ Current status or recent developments

### Budget/Financial Resolutions

- ✓ Exact amount needed
- ✓ Specific purpose and justification
- ✓ Legal authority for appropriation (BOL citation)
- ✓ Urgency or timeline
- ✓ Expected beneficiaries or outcomes

### Internal/Procedural Resolutions

- ✓ Current parliamentary need or situation
- ✓ BOL provision authorizing Parliament to act
- ✓ Precedent or best practices (if available)
- ✓ Intended effect of the resolution

## Research Strategy: Three-Tiered Approach

Research depth varies by resolution complexity. Each tier applies the A-B-C structure with different levels of thoroughness.

### Tier 1: Foundation (Simple Topics)

**When to use:** Simple, straightforward topics with readily available information.

**Research Scope:**

**A. Legal Research (Tier 1):**
- Basic BOL provision citation (if applicable)
- Skip constitutional and national law research unless obviously required
- Use AR 268 for internal/procedural matters

**B. Information Gathering (Tier 1):**
- Run 2-3 WebSearch queries to understand the topic
- Identify key facts, dates, names, and numbers
- Verify the basic premise (is this real? recent? relevant?)
- Minimum 3 specific facts

**C. Other Research (Tier 1):**
- Skip unless immediately relevant
- Basic precedent check if topic is procedural

**Output:** Basic factual foundation with minimal legal citation for 3-4 WHEREAS clauses.

### Tier 2: Substantiation (Standard Resolutions)

**When to use:** Standard resolutions requiring authoritative sources and specific details. **This is the default tier.**

**Research Scope:**

**A. Legal Research (Tier 2):**
- Identify 1-2 relevant BOL provisions (almost always required)
- Check constitutional provisions if rights/autonomy involved
- Review national laws if national-regional coordination needed
- Cite AR 268 for parliamentary procedures
- Review Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts if implementing existing laws

**B. Information Gathering (Tier 2):**
- Use WebFetch on official sources for authoritative information
- Find at least 3 concrete facts with dates/numbers
- Identify 2-3 sources that corroborate key claims
- Gather specific statistics, names, titles, amounts
- Verify current status and recent developments

**C. Other Research (Tier 2):**
- Quick check for similar parliamentary precedents
- Review best practices if procedural matter
- Consider stakeholder positions if relevant

**Output:** Well-substantiated WHEREAS clauses with specific data and proper legal citations.

### Tier 3: Deep Dive (Complex/Controversial Topics)

**When to use:** Complex, controversial, or legally sensitive topics requiring comprehensive research.

**Research Scope:**

**A. Legal Research (Tier 3):**
- Comprehensive constitutional review (relevant provisions)
- Multiple BOL provisions with cross-references
- National laws and implementing regulations
- Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts and Administrative Code
- Legal precedents and Supreme Court jurisprudence (if applicable)
- International agreements or customary law (if applicable)

**B. Information Gathering (Tier 3):**
- Research multiple perspectives and sources
- Gather detailed statistics and data from official sources
- Verify facts across 3+ independent sources
- Review government reports and studies
- Document timeline of events
- Interview quotes or official statements
- Impact assessments and projections

**C. Other Research (Tier 3):**
- Review related resolutions and legislation (national and regional)
- Study parliamentary precedents in detail
- Research best practices from other legislatures
- Analyze stakeholder positions comprehensively
- Academic studies and research papers
- Cultural/traditional context (if applicable)
- Consult expert opinions

**Output:** Comprehensive research with multiple legal citations, extensive factual support, diverse sources, and nuanced understanding.

### Selecting the Right Tier

**Default to Tier 2** unless topic is:
- Very simple/straightforward (Tier 1)
- Highly complex/controversial (Tier 3)
- User specifies depth needed

**Use Tier 1 for:**
- Simple commendatory resolutions for known individuals
- Routine internal/procedural matters
- Straightforward, uncontroversial topics

**Use Tier 2 for:**
- Most policy/statement resolutions
- Budget/financial resolutions
- Commendatory resolutions requiring background research
- Standard parliamentary actions

**Use Tier 3 for:**
- Constitutional or legal controversies
- Major policy investigations
- Complex budget appropriations
- Sensitive cultural or traditional governance matters
- Resolutions with significant political implications

## Research Workflow

### Phase 1: Initial Search (2-3 WebSearch queries)

Search patterns by resolution category:

**Commendatory:**
- "[Person/Org name] Bangsamoro BARMM"
- "[Person/Org] achievements [year]"
- "[Topic/event] [location] [date]"

**Policy/Investigation:**
- "[Issue] Bangsamoro BARMM"
- "[Issue] Philippines [current year]"
- "[Issue] investigation report"

**Budget/Financial:**
- "[Ministry/Office] Bangsamoro budget"
- "[Topic] funding Philippines"
- "[Specific program/need] appropriation"

**Internal/Procedural:**
- "Bangsamoro Parliament [procedural topic]"
- "Philippine parliamentary [topic]"
- "BOL Article [X] Section [Y]"

### Phase 2: Authoritative Source Verification (WebFetch)

**Prioritized sources** (in order):

1. **Official Bangsamoro Government sources:**
   - bangsamoro.gov.ph - Main government portal
   - parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph - Parliament website
   - Ministry websites

2. **Legal sources:**
   - officialgazette.gov.ph - Official laws and executive issuances
   - lawphil.net - Philippine laws and jurisprudence
   - chanrobles.com - Legal research
   - sc.judiciary.gov.ph - Supreme Court decisions

3. **News sources (for current events):**
   - mindanews.com - Local Mindanao news
   - National news outlets (Inquirer, Rappler, Manila Bulletin)
   - Official government press releases

4. **Academic and research sources:**
   - Institute for Autonomy and Governance
   - Mindanao studies centers
   - International organizations (e.g., International Alert)

### Phase 3: Legal Citation Research (if applicable)

**When legal authority is needed**, consult included PDF references:

**Use AR 268 (House Rules)** for:
- Parliamentary procedures and rules
- Committee jurisdictions
- Voting requirements
- Internal parliamentary matters

**Use BOL (Republic Act No. 11054)** for:
- Parliamentary powers (Article VII)
- Budget and appropriations (Article IX, Sections 12-13)
- Exclusive and reserved powers (Article VI, Sections 2-3)
- Power of inquiry (Article VII, Section 10)

**Use Bangsamoro Administrative Code (BAA No. 13)** for:
- Ministry structures and mandates
- Government office functions
- Administrative procedures

See [context.md](context.md) for common BOL citations and when to use each legal document.

## Research Quality Checklist

Before moving to drafting:

- [ ] Verified at least 3 specific facts with dates/numbers
- [ ] Identified authoritative sources (not just news articles)
- [ ] Located relevant BOL or legal provisions (if applicable)
- [ ] Confirmed information is current and accurate
- [ ] Found impact/significance data (people affected, amounts, etc.)
- [ ] Identified appropriate recipients for resolution copies

## Research Red Flags

**Stop and verify further if:**
- Information comes from only one source
- Dates or numbers conflict across sources
- Cannot find official confirmation of key facts
- Topic is controversial but research shows only one perspective
- Legal citations cannot be verified in official documents

## Examples of Good vs. Poor Research

### Good Research Example (Commendatory Resolution)

**Topic:** Commending health workers for pandemic response

**Research conducted:**
- ✓ Found specific number of health workers in BARMM
- ✓ Located statistics on COVID-19 cases handled
- ✓ Identified specific programs/initiatives implemented
- ✓ Found quotes from officials about their contributions
- ✓ Verified dates of pandemic response activities

**Result:** Specific WHEREAS clauses with verifiable facts.

### Poor Research Example

**Topic:** Same as above

**Research conducted:**
- ✗ Only general statement "health workers worked hard"
- ✗ No specific numbers or dates
- ✗ No verification of actual BARMM health worker programs
- ✗ Assumptions about what happened

**Result:** Generic WHEREAS clauses lacking credibility.

## Special Research Considerations

### Cultural and Traditional Context

When researching matters involving traditional governance, indigenous peoples, or Islamic law:
- Consult sources on traditional datu systems
- Reference Islamic scholars or institutions when appropriate
- Consider cultural protocols and sensitivities
- Review BOL provisions on traditional governance (if applicable)

### Geographic Context

BARMM includes specific provinces and areas:
- Lanao del Sur (except Iligan City)
- Maguindanao del Norte and Maguindanao del Sur
- Basilan (except Isabela City)
- Sulu
- Tawi-Tawi
- Cotabato City
- 63 barangays in North Cotabato

Ensure geographic references are accurate and within BARMM jurisdiction.

### Historical Context

When referencing the peace process or BARMM establishment:
- MNLF Peace Agreement (1996) - Created ARMM
- GPH-MILF Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (2014)
- Bangsamoro Organic Law (2018)
- Plebiscite (2019) - Two-stage ratification
- BTA Period (2019-2025)
- Regular Parliament (2025 onwards)

See [context.md](context.md) for more detailed historical and cultural context.
