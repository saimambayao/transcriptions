# Bangsamoro Parliament Resolution Types

## Official Resolution Types from AR 268

**BTA Parliament Approved Resolution No. 268** (Rule XVII, Section 5) defines exactly **three (3) official types of resolutions**. These are the ONLY official classifications in the BTA House Rules:

### Type 1: Simple Resolution
**Definition (from AR 268):** A measure used by the Parliament to take action affecting its own procedures, urgent resolution requiring immediate action by the Parliament, or to express an opinion, sympathy or commendation.

**Characteristics:**
- Most common type of resolution
- Covers internal procedures, urgent matters, opinions, sympathy, and commendations
- Does not create or modify law
- Adopted by majority vote

### Type 2: Concurrent Resolution
**Definition (from AR 268):** A resolution issued concurrently by both the Bangsamoro Cabinet and the Parliament expressing their sentiments on certain matters.

**Characteristics:**
- Requires joint action by both Cabinet and Parliament
- Used for matters needing both executive and legislative endorsement
- Less commonly used than Simple Resolutions

### Type 3: Joint Committee Resolution
**Definition (from AR 268):** A resolution of two (2) or more committees expressing the will or action of the Members on certain matters. This requires the majority vote of all the Committees concerned.

**Characteristics:**
- Multiple committees acting together
- Requires majority vote of ALL committees involved
- Used when matter spans multiple committee jurisdictions

## Practical Purpose-Based Classification

**Note:** The following categories, though NOT defined in AR 268, are practical classifications based on common purposes of resolutions, particularly Simple Resolutions. These are organizational tools only, not official AR 268 categories.

Most resolutions you will draft will be **Simple Resolutions** (Type 1) that serve different purposes:

### 1. Internal/Procedural Resolutions

**Purpose:** Address matters entirely within the internal operations and procedures of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Resolution Type:** Simple Resolution (Type 1 from AR 268)

**Characteristics:**
- Affect only the Parliament itself
- Do not require executive approval
- Do not have force and effect of law
- Adopted by majority vote of members present

**Common Uses:**
- Adopting or amending parliamentary rules and procedures
- Creating internal committees
- Fixing parliamentary schedules and sessions
- Approving organizational structures
- Internal administrative matters

**Example Topics:**
- "A Resolution Adopting the Organizational Structure of the Bangsamoro Parliament"
- "A Resolution Amending the Rules of Procedure of the Bangsamoro Parliament"
- "A Resolution Creating a Special Committee on Indigenous Peoples' Affairs"

### 2. Commendatory/Recognition Resolutions

**Purpose:** Express the Parliament's recognition, appreciation, or honor to individuals, groups, or institutions.

**Resolution Type:** Simple Resolution (Type 1 from AR 268 - expressing opinion, sympathy or commendation)

**Characteristics:**
- Ceremonial and commemorative in nature
- Recognize achievements or contributions
- May be used for condolences or sympathy
- Generally non-binding

**Common Uses:**
- Commending individuals for outstanding service
- Honoring heroes and community leaders
- Recognizing organizations for contributions
- Expressing condolences
- Commemorating significant events or anniversaries

**Example Topics:**
- "A Resolution Commending Frontline Health Workers for Their Service During the Pandemic"
- "A Resolution Expressing Sympathy Over the Passing of [Notable Person]"
- "A Resolution Commemorating the Anniversary of the Bangsamoro Organic Law Ratification"

### 3. Policy/Statement Resolutions

**Purpose:** Express the Parliament's official position, urge action, or direct investigations.

**Resolution Type:** Typically Simple Resolution (Type 1 from AR 268 - expressing opinion or urgent action) or Joint Committee Resolution (Type 3) when multiple committees are involved

**Characteristics:**
- State the Parliament's stance on issues
- May direct investigations or inquiries
- Can urge executive action
- May call for legislative measures

**Common Uses:**
- Expressing position on regional or national issues
- Directing committees to conduct inquiries
- Urging government agencies to take action
- Calling for enactment of specific legislation
- Requesting reports from executive departments

**Example Topics:**
- "A Resolution Directing an Inquiry into Alleged Corruption in [Agency]"
- "A Resolution Urging the National Government to Release Bangsamoro Block Grant"
- "A Resolution Expressing Support for Peace Initiatives in Mindanao"
- "A Resolution Calling for Investigation of [Specific Issue]"

### 4. Budget/Financial Resolutions

**Purpose:** Address financial and budgetary matters of the Bangsamoro Government.

**Resolution Type:** Typically Simple Resolution (Type 1 from AR 268 - urgent resolution requiring immediate action)

**Characteristics:**
- Deal with appropriations and budget allocations
- May authorize specific expenditures
- Approve supplemental budgets
- Authorize financial transactions

**Common Uses:**
- Approving annual budgets
- Authorizing supplemental appropriations
- Approving budget realignments
- Authorizing loans or financial agreements
- Directing allocation of funds

**Example Topics:**
- "A Resolution Approving the Annual Budget of the Bangsamoro Government for FY 2025"
- "A Resolution Authorizing the Chief Minister to Enter into a Loan Agreement"
- "A Resolution Realigning Funds from [Program] to [Program]"

## Additional Guidance

### Understanding Resolution Types vs Categories

**Resolution Types** (3 official types from AR 268):
- **Type 1: Simple Resolution** - Most common; covers internal procedures, urgent matters, opinions, and commendations
- **Type 2: Concurrent Resolution** - Joint action with Bangsamoro Cabinet
- **Type 3: Joint Committee Resolution** - Multiple committees acting together

**Resolution Categories** (purpose-based classifications):
- Internal/Procedural
- Commendatory/Recognition
- Policy/Statement
- Budget/Financial

Most resolutions you'll draft will be **Simple Resolutions** (Type 1), but fall into different **categories** based on their purpose.

### Scope Classification

**Internal Resolutions:** Resolutions affecting only the Parliament's internal affairs and operations (typically Simple Resolutions, internal category).

**External Resolutions:** Resolutions expressing positions or urging action from external entities (typically Simple Resolutions in policy/statement category, or Concurrent Resolutions).

### When to Use Concurrent Resolutions (Type 2)

Per AR 268, use Concurrent Resolutions when:
- The matter requires joint action by both the Bangsamoro Cabinet AND the Parliament
- Expressing sentiments that need both executive and legislative endorsement
- Coordinating positions on matters affecting both branches

### When to Use Joint Committee Resolutions (Type 3)

Per AR 268, use Joint Committee Resolutions when:
- Two or more parliamentary committees need to express joint will or action
- The matter falls under multiple committees' jurisdictions
- Requires majority vote of ALL committees concerned

## Resolution Selection Guide

### Step 1: Choose the Resolution Type (Per AR 268)

**Use Type 1: Simple Resolution when:**
- Taking action affecting Parliament's own procedures
- Urgent resolution requiring immediate action
- Expressing an opinion, sympathy, or commendation
- Most resolutions fall into this category

**Use Type 2: Concurrent Resolution when:**
- The matter requires joint action with the Bangsamoro Cabinet
- Expressing sentiments that need both executive and legislative endorsement
- Less common; used for matters requiring dual endorsement

**Use Type 3: Joint Committee Resolution when:**
- Two or more committees need to jointly express will or action
- The matter spans multiple committee jurisdictions
- Requires majority vote of all committees concerned

### Step 2: Choose the Resolution Category (Purpose-Based)

**Note:** Categories are practical organizational tools, NOT official AR 268 classifications. They help identify the purpose and common structure for your resolution.

**Use Internal/Procedural Category when:**
- The matter concerns only internal parliamentary operations
- No external action is required
- You're organizing the Parliament's structure or procedures
- Adopting or amending rules

**Use Commendatory Category when:**
- Recognizing individuals or groups
- Expressing sympathy or support
- Commemorating events
- The purpose is ceremonial

**Use Policy/Statement Category when:**
- Expressing the Parliament's official position
- Calling for investigations
- Urging government action
- The Parliament needs to make a public statement

**Use Budget/Financial Category when:**
- Approving budgets or appropriations
- Authorizing financial transactions
- Realigning funds
- Financial matters require parliamentary approval
