# Bangsamoro Cultural Diversity

Source: 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan, Chapter 6

## Sectoral Goal

Rich and diverse Bangsamoro culture and identity preserved and recognized.

---

## 13 Ethnolinguistic Groups

The Bangsamoro is an ethnolinguistic mosaic of at least 13 Islamized, non-Islamized, and indigenous groups that share a historical identification with Islam and a common history of colonial and post-colonial state persecution. They eventually embraced the term "Moro" as a collective identifier.

### Islamized Groups (11)

1. **Maguindanaon** — People of the flooded plain (Maguindanao provinces). Retain distinct traditions and identities with shared Islamic heritage.
2. **Meranao** — People of the lake (Lanao del Sur, around Lake Lanao). Known for rich artistic traditions, metalcraft, and the Maranao epic Darangen.
3. **Iranun/Iranaun** — Maritime people historically based in Iligan Bay area. Maintained maritime power through the Iranun Confederation.
4. **Tausug** — People of the current (Sulu archipelago). Historically the Sultanate of Sulu, the oldest Islamic institution in the Philippines (established 1457).
5. **Sama** — Sea-dwelling and coastal people (Tawi-Tawi, Sulu). Includes the Sama Dilaut (Badjao), known as "Sea Nomads."
6. **Yakan** — Agricultural people of Basilan. Known for distinctive weaving traditions.
7. **Sangil** — From Sarangani Islands and southern Mindanao.
8. **Molbog** — People of Balabac island (southernmost Palawan).
9. **Jama Mapun** — People of Cagayan de Sulu (Mapun island, Tawi-Tawi).
10. **Kalagan** — From southeastern Mindanao (Davao Gulf area).
11. **Kalibugan** — Mixed-descent communities in Zamboanga Peninsula.

### Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples (4)

12. **Teduray** — Highland people of interior Maguindanao.
13. **Lambangian** — Related to Teduray, in the highlands of Maguindanao.
14. **Dulangan Manobo** — Highland group in interior Maguindanao.
15. **B'laan** — From the uplands of Maguindanao and adjacent areas.

## Traditional Governance Systems

### Sultanates
- **Sultanate of Sulu** — Established 1457, oldest Islamic institution in the Philippines
- **Sultanate of Maguindanao** — Major power in central Mindanao
- **Sultanate of Kabuntalan** — In present-day Maguindanao
- **Sultanate of Buayan** — In present-day Maguindanao

### Royal Houses
- **Royal House of Ranao** — Pat a Pangampong (Four Lake Kingdoms of Lanao)
- **Royal House of the Iranun** — Maritime confederation

These traditional governance structures represent pre-colonial political institutions that predate Spanish colonization.

## Customary Laws and Traditions

| Group | Customary System | Description |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Maguindanaon | Walay na Bityara | Traditional dispute resolution and societal regulation |
| Meranao | Taritib ago Igma | Customary law system for social order |
| Island Provinces | Baitsyara | Traditional justice and community governance |

These customs were traditionally used for regulating societal affairs but are now rarely practiced in formal settings.

## Cultural Heritage Status

**Challenges:**
- New generations gradually losing appreciation of culture and heritage
- Absence of integrated/comprehensive Bangsamoro history
- Poor promotion initiatives
- Exposure to foreign cultures and modern lifestyles reducing cultural appreciation
- Traditional handcrafted instruments now rare; authentic artists difficult to find
- Majority of new generation lacks knowledge of Bangsamoro history and traditional practices
- Cultural heritage largely informal, unprotected, and not explicitly managed

**Institutional Gaps:**
- No institution concentrated on Bangsamoro history with library of records, objects, and artifacts
- Limited number of Bangsamoro historians and cultural experts
- Limited innovative/creative platforms for cultural expression
- Historical and cultural sites under jurisdiction of national agencies (National Museum, NHCP) — to be transferred to BCPCH under BOL Section 26, Article IX

## Preservation Strategies (2nd BDP)

1. **Bangsamoro Diverse Culture and Identity Promotion Program** — IEC materials, Official Gazette "Pusaka" magazine, radio programs
2. **Bangsamoro Diverse Culture Preservation Program** — Schools of Living Tradition, cultural masters assistance, brassware/handicraft/weaving training, Tarsila (Jawi) digitalization
3. **Bangsamoro Traditional Sports Promotion Program** — Training of trainers, mainstreaming to regular sports
4. **Cultural Heritage Protection and Development Program** — Cultural mapping, profiling of heritage, documentation, infrastructure
5. **Islamic Culture Promotion Program** — Culture of peace, Madrasa strengthening, Hajj/Umrah assistance, Shari'ah justice system

---

## Legislative Agenda

**Bangsamoro Heritage Act** — Localized version of RA 10066 to create Registry of Cultural Property and preserve tangible and intangible cultural heritage in BARMM.
