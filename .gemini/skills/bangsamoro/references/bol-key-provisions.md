## Key Provisions by Topic

### Territory (Article III)
- Core territory: provinces of the former ARMM (under RA 6734/RA 9054)
- Municipalities that voted to join: Baloi, Munai, Nunungan, Pantar, Tagoloan, Tangkal (Lanao del Norte)
- 39 barangays in North Cotabato (from Carmen, Kabacan, Midsayap, Pigkawayan, Pikit)
- Cotabato City and Isabela City (Basilan) — subject to plebiscite
- Other contiguous areas may petition to join
- Constituent units: provinces, cities, municipalities, barangays, geographical areas
- **Sulu excluded**: *Province of Sulu v. Medialdea* (G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693; Sept 9, 2024; finalized Nov 26, 2024). SC upheld BOL but ruled Sulu not part of BARMM — violated Art. X, Sec. 18 (only areas voting favorably join). Sulu rejected BOL by 54.3%. EO 91 (July 30, 2025) realigned Sulu under Region IX. Doctrine of operative fact applied to protect prior acts.
- **SGA municipalities**: 63 North Cotabato barangays organized into 8 new municipalities (Pahamuddin, Kadayangan, Nabalawag, Tugunan, Malidegao, Ligawasan, Kapalawan, Old Kaabakan) — now 2 parliamentary districts under BAA 86
- **Redistricting SC ruling**: *Ali et al. v. BTA* (G.R. No. E-02219) and *Macapaar et al. v. COMELEC* (G.R. No. E-02235; Sept 30, 2025). BAA 77 and BAA 58 declared unconstitutional. BAA 86 (Jan 20, 2026) enacted: 32 districts (Lanao del Sur 9, Maguindanao del Norte 5, Maguindanao del Sur 5, Basilan 4, Tawi-Tawi 4, Cotabato City 3, SGA 2)

### Powers of Government (Article V)
- **Enumerated Bangsamoro powers** (Sec. 2): 55 subject areas from (a) administration of justice to (ccc) water supply — including administrative organization, creation of sources of revenue, ancestral domain/natural resources management, social services, education, environmental protection, labor, disaster risk reduction, culture and language, cooperatives, public works, tourism
- **Implied and incidental powers** (Sec. 3, General Welfare): Powers expressly granted, those necessarily implied, and powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for efficient governance
- **Residual clause** (Sec. 1): "All powers, functions, and responsibilities not granted by the Constitution or by national law to the Bangsamoro Government shall be vested in the National Government"
- **Overlapping authority**: Some enumerated areas overlap with national legislation (e.g., labor, education, disaster management), creating shared jurisdiction that requires harmonization through the intergovernmental relations mechanisms in Article VI

### Parliamentary System (Article VII)
- **Bangsamoro Parliament**: Legislative body with Members of Parliament
- **Chief Minister**: Head of government, elected by Parliament from among its members
- **Cabinet**: Ministers appointed by Chief Minister with Parliament consent
- **Wali**: Ceremonial head representing moral authority
- Parliamentary form of government within the framework of the Philippine Constitution

### Fiscal Autonomy (Article XII)
- **Annual Block Grant**: Equal to 5% of the net national internal revenue of the BIR and BoC
- **Special Development Fund**: PHP 5 billion per year for 10 years (PHP 50 billion total)
- **Revenue generation**: Power to create own sources of revenue (taxes, fees, charges)
- **75% tax share**: Of national taxes collected within BARMM
- **Wealth sharing**: Share in natural resource development, strategic minerals, mines
- **Bangsamoro Budget**: Autonomous budget process, appropriated by Parliament

### Basic Rights (Article IX)
- Right to life, liberty, and property
- Freedom of religion, expression, assembly
- Right to education (Section 14) — free quality education
- Right to culture and identity (Section 24) — preserve and promote Bangsamoro culture
- Women's rights and protection from violence
- Indigenous peoples' rights
- Right to adequate social services

### Shari'ah Jurisdiction (Article X)
- Shari'ah courts with jurisdiction over:
  - Personal law (marriage, divorce, inheritance, custody)
  - Property relations among Muslims
  - Cases involving Muslims within BARMM
- District and Circuit Shari'ah Courts
- Shari'ah Appellate Court as highest Shari'ah court in BARMM
- Principal sources: Qur'an and Sunnah

### Transition (Article XVI)
- **BTA**: Interim government with both legislative and executive powers
- **Transition timeline**: Originally until 2022, extended to 2025 (RA 11593), further extended to 2028 (RA 12064)
- **BTA compositions**: 80 members appointed by the President
  - First BTA (Feb 2019 – Sept 2022): appointed by President Duterte
  - Second BTA (Sept 2022 – Feb 2025): reconstituted by President Marcos under RA 11593
  - Current BTA (March 2025 – present): appointed by President Marcos under RA 12064
- **Legislative continuity**: Bills filed during one BTA composition were not archived when a new composition was appointed — they continued to be deliberated by the succeeding composition without refiling
- **Transition tasks**: Enact priority codes, establish ministries, build institutions
- **Priority Codes** (7 total, 4 enacted):
  - Enacted: Administrative Code (BAA 13, Oct 2020), Civil Service Code (Feb 2021), Education Code (May 2021), Electoral Code (BAA 35, 2023)
  - Remaining: Revenue Code, Local Government Code, Investment Code
- **Key electoral legislation**: BAA 86 (Parliamentary District Act, Jan 2026) — 32 single-member districts; BAA 87 & 88 (Electoral Code amendments, Jan 2026)
- **SC ruling (Oct 2025)**: Declared BAA 58 and BAA 77 (redistricting laws) unconstitutional, requiring fresh redistricting via BAA 86

### Economy (Article XIII)
- Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA) for economic zones
- Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI) for investment promotion
- Free enterprise economy with social justice
- Support for MSMEs, cooperatives, and social enterprises
- Tourism development aligned with cultural heritage
