# Bangsamoro Development Framework 2023-2028

Source: 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan, Chapter 4

## Overall Goal

An empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro:
- **Empowered**: Improved capacity of communities through enhanced access to quality basic social services and widened economic opportunities via institution building and service delivery
- **Cohesive**: Improved relationships between stakeholders evidenced in reduced violent conflict and forged partnerships in governance and development
- **Progressive**: Sustained ability to optimize existing and potential resources addressing community development needs and realizing economic and human potential

## Vision

"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

## Mission

"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

## Six Development Goals

### Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government
Development administration assumes stability during external and internal shocks, ensuring continuity of service delivery and effectiveness of ministries during emergencies, elections, or power changes. Just government means being the primary agent of economic and social justice, exercising powers prudently to advance public interest. Accountable government means institutions, leaders, and staff are held accountable with frameworks, mechanisms, and systems in place.

### Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy
Equitable economy implies economic gains benefit all with special consideration for poor and marginalized, with improvements in Gini coefficient, poverty gap, and poverty severity. Competitive economy means BARMM enjoys comparative advantage in selected commodities with optimized market linkages. Robust economy means sustained growth over six years with diversified, stabilized economy creating quality jobs.

### Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities
Achieve lasting peace through ensuring security and intensifying peace-building efforts. Create safe communities by protecting Bangsamoro people and building communities able to withstand natural calamities and human-induced disasters.

### Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services
Quality social services inclusive of all sectors regardless of color, tribe, or political affiliation, ensuring no one is left behind. Services respond to the needs of all people and BARMM as a whole.

### Goal 5: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved and Recognized
Exercise powers and authority to maintain and promote rich, diverse culture and identity. Mandated under Section 24, Article IX of the BOL — the primary cause of the Bangsamoro struggle.

### Goal 6: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure
Strategic infrastructure has broad-based inclusive effects to industries and communities. Adequate infrastructure meets demands of industries and communities. Climate-resilient infrastructure withstands climate change-induced calamities.

## Eight Development Strategies

1. **Improve and strengthen governance mechanisms** for a stable, just, and accountable Bangsamoro government
2. **Create an enabling environment** towards an equitable, competitive, and robust economy
3. **Harness technology and innovations** to increase socioeconomic opportunities and improve government services
4. **Improve ecological integrity and resilience** of communities and ensure a healthy and clean environment
5. **Enhance and strengthen peace, public order, safety, and security** and uphold human rights for inclusive, peaceful, progressive, and sustainable development
6. **Ensure inclusive and equitable access** to quality services for social justice and human capital development
7. **Mainstream Bangsamoro cultural diversity**, beliefs, heritage, and identity
8. **Scale up functional, strategic, climate-resilient, and quality infrastructure** to support sustainable socioeconomic development

## Twelve Pillars of Moral Governance

1. **Integrity** — Avoid conflicts of interest in executing official duties
2. **Accountability** — Open and transparent about decisions and actions
3. **Transparency and Honesty** — Declare private interests, resolve conflicts protecting public interest
4. **Sense of Patriotism** — Invest in reputation and integrity of the Bangsamoro
5. **Striving for Excellence** — Uphold legitimacy, deliver highest quality
6. **Cultural Understanding and Tolerance** — Awareness of socioeconomic differences between societies
7. **Unwavering Loyalty to God** — Strict adherence to rules as result of devotion to God
8. **Justice** — Serve without prejudice or self-interest
9. **Inclusivity** — Foster welcoming environments free from discrimination
10. **Objectivity** — Merit-based decisions in public interest
11. **Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement** — Community consultation for positive change
12. **Balance Between Practical Life and Moral Side** — Balance practical and ethical values in governance
