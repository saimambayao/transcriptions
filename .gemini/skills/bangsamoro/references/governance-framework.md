## Parliamentary System

### Shift from Unitary to Parliamentary

BARMM adopted a parliamentary system, a fundamental change from ARMM's unitary system:

**Key Features:**
- Stronger executive-legislative working relationships
- Chief Minister as head of executive branch
- Parliament holds both legislative and oversight powers
- Cabinet appointed from Parliament members

**Advantages:**
- Integrated approach to governance
- Faster policy implementation
- Accountability through parliamentary mechanisms

## Bangsamoro Transition Authority

### Composition

The BTA serves as the interim government with both legislative and executive powers:

**Leadership:**
- **Chief Minister:** Ahod B. Ebrahim (bearing executive power)
- **Speaker:** Atty. Pangalian M. Balindong (leading Parliament)
- **80 Members of Parliament** (initially appointed, elections in 2025)

### Powers and Functions

1. **Legislative Power:**
   - Enact laws for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
   - Approve annual budgets
   - Conduct oversight functions

2. **Executive Power:**
   - Execute laws and policies
   - Manage ministries and agencies
   - Deliver government services

### Transition Plan

The BTA transition plan (approved June 17, 2019) serves as basis for:
- Organizational restructuring
- Phasing out of ARMM employees
- Implementation of priority codes

## Twelve Pillars of Moral Governance

Moral Governance is the centerpiece of BARMM leadership. The 12 pillars guide all public servants:

### 1. Integrity
> Public office holders should not bind themselves in any manner that could influence their duties, whether financially or otherwise.

### 2. Accountability
> Public office holders should be as open and transparent about all decisions and actions. They must answer to the people and authority.

### 3. Transparency and Honesty
> Holders of public offices must declare private interests related to public duties and resolve conflicts protecting public interest. All public transactions should be made public.

### 4. Sense of Patriotism
> Government officials should invest heavily in their way of life, achievements, rich history, and love for country. The reputation and integrity of the place must be maintained.

### 5. Striving for Excellence
> Uphold the legitimacy of all actions. Public servants should strive to deliver the highest quality, prioritizing process over results for the good of all.

### 6. Cultural Understanding and Tolerance
> Public officials should gain better understanding of socioeconomic differences between societies, appreciating how provincial elements add variety to the Bangsamoro.

### 7. Unwavering Loyalty to God
> Public officials designated as leaders are required to strictly follow rules and regulations set by authorities, guided by devotion to God.

### 8. Justice
> A public servant should serve without prejudice. Equity should triumph without regard for oneself, family, companions, or political alliances.

### 9. Inclusivity
> Foster an inclusive environment that makes communities welcoming and free from discrimination based on religion, ethnicity, or culture.

### 10. Objectivity
> Make only merit-based decisions when conducting public business. Public officials should decide solely in the public interest.

### 11. Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement
> Public servants should seek community opinion through dialogue to specify needs and achieve meaningful engagement and positive change.

### 12. Balance Between Practical Life and Moral Side
> In carrying out duties, public servants must balance the practical and moral/ethical values, never deviating from the moral aspect.

## Intergovernmental Relations

### IGR Mechanisms under BOL (Article VI)

The BOL establishes mechanisms for coordination between national and Bangsamoro governments:

| Mechanism | Status | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| IGRB (Intergovernmental Relations Body) | Operational | Resolve IGR issues |
| PCBPF (Philippine Congress-Bangsamoro Parliament Forum) | Convened Nov 2022 | Legislative coordination |
| BSDB (Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board) | Passed June 2022 | Sustainable development |
| Joint Normalization Committee | Operational | Peace agreement implementation |

**6 of 8 IGR mechanisms are fully operationalized.**

### Key IGRB Milestones

1. **Cotabato City Turnover** (December 2020) - Historic transfer of supervision
2. **Education Sector Salaries** - Resolved for SGA and Cotabato City
3. **CSC Coordination** - Omnibus requests approved
4. **Law Enforcement** - Creation of regional NAPOLCOM, BJMP, BFP offices

## LGU Functionality

### Seven Elements of Functionality

MILG assesses LGU functionality based on:

1. Presence of government center with facilities
2. Working elected and appointed mandatory officials
3. Functional local special bodies
4. Functional local legislative body
5. Approved mandated plans and budget
6. Availability of basic services
7. Sound fiscal management

### Performance

- **2021:** 59% of 70 LGUs compliant (up from 50% in 2020)
- Island provinces register lower compliance due to geographic challenges
