## 2. The Challenge

### Context: A Historic Transition

The Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament serves as the legislative foundation for the Philippines' most significant governance experiment since the 1987 Constitution. Parliament's effectiveness directly determines whether autonomy delivers better outcomes for the Bangsamoro people.

### Current Parliamentary Operations Gaps

**Fragmented legislative tracking.** Bills move through spreadsheets without unified visibility. No systematic workflow exists from filing to enactment. Legislative items risk falling through the cracks.

**Limited committee coordination.** Scheduling conflicts between committees persist. Document management remains fragmented. Deliberation history proves difficult to track.

**Disconnected legal research.** Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts scatter across formats. National laws and jurisprudence require external searches. No AI-assisted drafting support exists.

**Ad hoc constituent services.** Requests get logged inconsistently. Systematic follow-up mechanisms are absent. Geographic service equity goes unmeasured.

**Weak oversight infrastructure.** Ministry performance data arrives periodically. No real-time visibility into executive operations exists. Budget execution monitoring relies on reports, not dashboards.

### Why This Matters

Parliament's legislative and oversight functions shape how quickly community needs become law, how effectively the executive is held to account, how constituents experience their government, and how Parliament is perceived by citizens and partners.

## 3. Our Solution: Parliament Portal

Providing Parliament with integrated digital tools for legislative management, oversight, and constituent services allows MPs and staff to operate more efficiently and transparently. This leads to faster legislation, stronger oversight, better constituent services, and improved institutional credibility—ultimately contributing to effective self-governance and demonstrating that the peace process delivers tangible benefits.

### Parliament Portal Features

#### Legislative Work Module

| Feature | Function | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Bill/Resolution Registry | Digital tracking from filing to enactment | No bills lost in process |
| Legal Databases | BAAs, resolutions, national laws, jurisprudence | Informed legislative drafting |
| AI-Assisted Workspace | Drafting support, legal research, consistency checking | Higher quality legislation |
| Legislative Calendar | Session planning, agenda management | Coordinated parliamentary schedule |
| Committee Support | Document management, hearing coordination | Efficient deliberations |
| Records Repository | Searchable legislative history | Institutional memory preserved |

#### Oversight Work Module

| Feature | Function | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Law Enforcement Tracking | Monitor implementation of enacted legislation | Accountability for executive action |
| Ministry Mandate Oversight | Track MOA compliance with BOL provisions | Mandate fulfillment visibility |
| PPA/Budget Monitoring | Real-time program and budget execution data | Evidence-based oversight |
| Service Delivery Tracking | Monitor public service performance | Citizen-focused accountability |

#### Representation Work Module

| Feature | Function | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Constituent Request Tracking | End-to-end request management | No requests forgotten |
| Service Delivery Management | Systematic service provision | Consistent constituent experience |
| Geographic Mapping | Service distribution visualization | Equity across constituencies |
| Public Information | Media affairs coordination | Transparent communication |

### Direct Users (GAAB 2026)

| User Group | Count | Primary Functions |
|------------|-------|-------------------|
| Members of Parliament | 80 | Legislative work, oversight, representation |
| Parliament Secretariat | 1,404 | Legislative support, committee services, administration |
| Non-permanent Staff | 266 | Contract/support positions |
| **Total** | **1,750** | |

**User distribution by function:** MPs include the Speaker, Deputy Speakers (12), Floor Leaders, and Committee Chairs. Legislative Affairs covers Legislative Staff Officers, Policy Research Staff, and Legal Services. Political Affairs includes MP Office Staff, Chiefs of Staff, and Political Affairs Officers. ### Indirect Beneficiaries

Bangsamoro communities benefit through faster legislative response to community needs, stronger parliamentary oversight of government programs, more responsive constituent services, and greater transparency in parliamentary proceedings.

## 5. Use of Funds

### Budget Summary

| Category | Amount (PHP) | Percentage |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| Platform Development | 2,800,000 | 58% |
| Capacity Building | 900,000 | 19% |
| Infrastructure & Hosting | 650,000 | 13% |
| Project Management | 350,000 | 7% |
| Monitoring & Evaluation | 150,000 | 3% |
| **Total** | **4,850,000** | **100%** |

### Budget Narrative

**Platform Development (PHP 2,800,000)** covers a full-stack development team of 4-6 personnel over 12 months. This includes Parliament Portal modules for Legislative, Oversight, and Representation functions, AI integration for legal research and drafting support, and security implementation and testing.

**Capacity Building (PHP 900,000)** funds training workshops for MPs, committee staff, and secretariat; training materials and user guides; administrator certification; and change management support.

**Infrastructure & Hosting (PHP 650,000)** provides cloud hosting services (Railway or equivalent), database services (PostgreSQL, Redis), file storage for legislative documents, and security services (SSL, monitoring).

**Project Management (PHP 350,000)** supports a project coordinator for stakeholder liaison, progress reporting, and risk management.

**Monitoring & Evaluation (PHP 150,000)** covers baseline assessment of current operations, progress monitoring, end-of-project evaluation, and learning documentation.
