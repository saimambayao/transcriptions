## Advancing Technology-Enabled Governance Through Digital Transformation

### For Honorable Member of Parliament Ibrahim Ibay
### Chair, Committee on Science and Technology

> **Date**: January 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Funding Request:** PHP 4,850,000 (approximately USD 87,000)

**Duration:** 12 months

**Focus Area:** Full e-Governance Platform with Technology Oversight Dashboard

### The Opportunity

As Chair of the Committee on Science and Technology, you lead Parliament's oversight of digital transformation across the Bangsamoro Government. The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254) mandates that all government agencies digitize operations—your committee oversees this transition. Using the platform yourself positions you as both champion and expert in technology-enabled governance.

### Your Role

Your committee mandate includes overseeing implementation of RA 12254 in BARMM, monitoring digital transformation across ministries, evaluating technology investments and outcomes, and championing science and technology for regional development.

### Impact at a Glance

| Outcome | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Digital transformation oversight enabled | Real-time implementation tracking |
| Technology investments monitored | Platform usage and adoption data |
| RA 12254 compliance tracked | Agency digitization progress |
| Committee expertise built | Practitioner knowledge of e-governance |

---

## 2. The Challenge

### Your Oversight Mandate

The Committee on Science and Technology oversees RA 12254 implementation (E-Governance Act compliance across BARMM), BICTO operations (Bangsamoro ICT Office performance), technology investments (government ICT spending and outcomes), and digital transformation (ministry digitization progress).

### Current Oversight Gaps

**Limited Visibility into Implementation.** Digital transformation progress arrives through periodic reports. No real-time view of ministry digitization exists. RA 12254 compliance assessment depends on documents rather than observable progress.

**No Practitioner Experience.** Committee members evaluate technology without using it. Oversight relies on vendor presentations, not hands-on experience. A gap persists between policy understanding and practice understanding.

**Technology Assessment Constraints.** Investment outcomes get measured through documents alone. User adoption data remains unavailable. Platform effectiveness stays unclear because the committee lacks direct visibility into usage patterns.

**Oversight Work Module** provides BICTO performance monitoring, ministry ICT operations, and technology investment tracking.
