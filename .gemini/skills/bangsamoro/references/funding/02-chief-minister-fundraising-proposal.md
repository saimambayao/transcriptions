## 2. The Challenge

### Context: Leading a Complex Government

The Chief Minister leads a government responsible for PHP 114.07 billion in annual budget, 15 ministries with distinct mandates, implementation of the 12-Point Priority Agenda, coordination of the Bangsamoro Development Plan, and management of parliamentary relations.

### Current Coordination Gaps

**Fragmented Visibility.** Ministry performance arrives through periodic reports. No real-time dashboard shows government operations status. Budget execution tracking relies on disconnected systems that staff must reconcile manually.

**Planning Silos.** Ministries plan independently with limited coordination. BDP alignment receives assessment annually rather than continuously. Resource allocation decisions proceed without complete information across agencies.

**Legislative-Executive Coordination.** Bill progress remains separate from executive planning. Budget authorization lacks connection to execution monitoring. Policy implementation does not receive systematic tracking after enactment.

**Accountability Gaps.** Performance data requires manual compilation for oversight bodies. COA audit preparation consumes significant staff time. No automated compliance monitoring exists to flag issues before they become problems.

### Why This Matters

The Chief Minister's effectiveness in delivering the promise of Bangsamoro autonomy depends on several factors. Coordination allows alignment of 15 ministries toward common goals. Accountability demonstrates results to Parliament, COA, and citizens. Efficiency maximizes impact of limited resources. Transparency builds trust through visible governance.

## 3. Our Solution: e-Bangsamoro Platform

### Theory of Change

The platform's logic works as follows: When government personnel have integrated digital tools for parliamentary operations, ministerial planning, and budget management, they coordinate more effectively and operate more transparently. This produces aligned ministry operations, accountable resource use, and coordinated development implementation. The ultimate outcome supports effective self-governance and demonstrates that autonomy delivers better outcomes for the Bangsamoro people.

### Platform Architecture

The e-Bangsamoro Platform provides three integrated portals:

#### Parliament Portal

Digital workspace for legislative operations:
- Legislative Work: Bill/resolution tracking, legal databases, AI-assisted drafting
- Oversight Work: Ministry performance monitoring, budget execution visibility
- Representation Work: Constituent services, geographic service mapping

#### Ministry Portal

Strategic planning and coordination platform:
- 10-Point Framework: Standardized planning across all ministries
- PPA Management: Program/Project/Activity lifecycle tracking
- Performance Monitoring: Results-Based M&E dashboards
- Coordination: Cross-ministry visibility and collaboration

#### Budget Portal

Complete BBSA lifecycle automation:
- Preparation: Budget call, submission, consolidation
- Authorization: GAB tracking, amendment management
- Execution: Allotment, obligation, disbursement monitoring
- Accountability: BFAR generation, COA audit readiness

### Chief Minister's Command Center

A unified dashboard provides government-wide performance rankings, budget execution status across all ministries, BDP progress indicators by sector, 12-Point Priority Agenda coverage mapping, and alert notifications for variances that need attention.

## 5. Use of Funds

### Budget Summary

| Category | Amount (PHP) | Percentage |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| Platform Development | 2,800,000 | 58% |
| Capacity Building | 900,000 | 19% |
| Infrastructure & Hosting | 650,000 | 13% |
| Project Management | 350,000 | 7% |
| Monitoring & Evaluation | 150,000 | 3% |
| **Total** | **4,850,000** | **100%** |

### Budget Narrative

**Platform Development (PHP 2,800,000)** covers a full-stack development team of 4-6 personnel over 12 months. Work includes all three portals (Parliament, Ministry, Budget), the Chief Minister Command Center, security implementation, and testing.

**Capacity Building (PHP 900,000)** funds training workshops across all user groups, training materials and user guides, administrator certification, and change management support.

**Infrastructure & Hosting (PHP 650,000)** provides cloud hosting services, database services (PostgreSQL, Redis), file storage, and security services.

**Project Management (PHP 350,000)** supports the project coordinator, stakeholder coordination, and progress reporting.

**Monitoring & Evaluation (PHP 150,000)** covers baseline assessment, progress monitoring, and end-of-project evaluation.
