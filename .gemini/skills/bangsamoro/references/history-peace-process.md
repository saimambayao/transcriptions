## Pre-Colonial Period

The Bangsamoro homeland existed as independent sultanates before Spanish colonization:

**Major Sultanates:**
- **Sultanate of Sulu** - Established in 1457, the oldest Islamic institution in the Philippines
- **Sultanate of Maguindanao** - Powerful sultanate that controlled much of Mindanao
- **Sultanate of Buayan** - Sister sultanate of Maguindanao
- **Sultanate of Kabuntalan** - Confederation in the Cotabato region
- **Royal House of Ranao** - Pat a Pangampong (Four Lake Kingdoms) of Lanao
- **Iranun Confederation** - Maritime power known for naval prowess

These sultanates maintained:
- Independent political systems
- Islamic legal traditions (Shariah)
- Trade relations with Southeast Asian neighbors
- Distinct cultural identities

## Colonial Era and Resistance

### Spanish Period (1521-1898)

The term "Moro" was applied by Spanish colonizers from their experience fighting "Moors" in the Iberian Peninsula. Despite aggressive anti-Muslim policies, the Bangsamoro maintained:
- Distinct political autonomy
- Islamic identity and practices
- Armed resistance for over 300 years

**Key Point:** The Bangsamoro were never fully conquered by Spain, maintaining effective self-governance throughout the colonial period.

### American Period (1898-1946)

- Mindanao was administered as the "Moro Province" separately from Luzon and Visayas
- Despite Bangsamoro protests, the region was integrated into the Philippine Commonwealth under the 1935 Constitution
- This integration occurred over the "vehement resistance of the Bangsamoro"

### Post-Independence (1946-Present)

The Bangsamoro struggle for self-determination continued through:
- Political advocacy
- Armed resistance movements
- Peace negotiations

## Modern Peace Process

### Key Milestones

**1996 Final Peace Agreement (FPA)**
- Between Government of the Philippines (GPH) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF)
- Led to creation of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM)
- Signed under the Ramos administration

**2012 Framework Agreement on the Bangsamoro (FAB)**
- Between GPH and Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF)
- Signed under the Aquino III administration
- Established the framework for a new autonomous entity

**2014 Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB)**
- Comprehensive peace agreement between GPH and MILF
- Covered:
  - Normalization process
  - Power-sharing arrangements
  - Wealth-sharing mechanisms
  - Transitional arrangements

**2019 Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) Ratification**
- Republic Act 11054 signed by President Duterte (July 27, 2018)
- Ratified through plebiscite in January-February 2019
- Created the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)

## Transition from ARMM to BARMM

### Institutional Transformation

**ARMM (1989-2019):**
- Unitary system of governance
- 25 regional departments
- Limited fiscal autonomy

**BARMM (2019-Present):**
- Parliamentary system of governance
- Restructured to 15 ministries
- Genuine fiscal autonomy under BOL

### Transition Challenges

1. **Phasing out of ARMM offices** - Gradual reorganization of bureaucracy
2. **Personnel transition** - Managing separation and absorption of employees
3. **Property transfers** - Full transfer of powers, functions, and properties
4. **Service continuity** - Maintaining government services during transition

### Key Transition Events

- **December 2020:** Turnover of Cotabato City to BARMM
- **2021:** Extended transition via RA 11593 (BTA extension to 2025)
- **Ongoing:** Establishment of regional offices for NAPOLCOM, BJMP, BFP

## Key Peace Agreements

### 1996 Final Peace Agreement (FPA)
- **Parties:** GPH and MNLF
- **Outcome:** Creation of ARMM
- **Implementation:** Special Zone of Peace and Development (SZOPAD)

### 2014 Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB)

**Four Annexes:**

1. **Annex on Transitional Arrangements and Modalities**
   - Transition mechanism from ARMM to BARMM
   - Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA)

2. **Annex on Revenue Generation and Wealth-Sharing**
   - Block grant mechanism
   - Natural resource sharing
   - Tax powers

3. **Annex on Power-Sharing**
   - Reserved (national government) powers
   - Exclusive (Bangsamoro) powers
   - Concurrent (shared) powers

4. **Annex on Normalization**
   - Decommissioning of MILF forces
   - Socioeconomic development programs
   - Transitional justice and reconciliation

## Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054)

### Overview

The Bangsamoro Organic Law replaced RA 6734 (as amended by RA 9054) creating the ARMM. It established BARMM as a new political entity with greater autonomy.

### Key Provisions

**Article VII - Bangsamoro Parliament**
- Unicameral parliament
- 80 members (initially appointed as BTA)
- Both legislative and executive oversight

**Article XII - Fiscal Autonomy**
- Annual block grant (5% of net national internal revenue)
- Special Development Fund (PHP 50 billion over 10 years)
- Power to create regional revenue sources

**Article IX - Basic Rights**
- Protection of Bangsamoro identity
- Cultural heritage preservation
- Recognition of indigenous peoples

**Article XVI - Transition**
- BTA as interim government
- Phase-out of ARMM structure
- Transfer of powers and properties

### Territorial Scope

- **Core Territory:** Provinces of Basilan (except Isabela City), Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi
- **By Plebiscite:** Cotabato City and 63 barangays of the Special Geographic Area (SGA)

## Battle Cry of the Bangsamoro

> "It is better to die than to have two identities."

This historic battle cry reflects the Bangsamoro commitment to preserving their identity as Muslims, preferring death over surrendering their cultural and religious identity. This sentiment remains central to Bangsamoro consciousness.

## Extended Transition (RA 11593)

In 2021, Republic Act 11593 extended the BTA mandate, resetting elections to 2025. This extension provides:

1. Additional time to accomplish critical transition plans
2. Opportunity to enact priority codes
3. Continued institution-building
4. Sustained implementation of peace agreements
