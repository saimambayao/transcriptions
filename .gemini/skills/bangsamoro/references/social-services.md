## Education

### Overview

Education in BARMM is governed by the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), which uniquely combines four sectors: basic, higher, technical, and Madaris educations under BAA 13 (Administrative Code of the BARMM).

### Health Outcomes (2015-2020)

**Birth and Death Rates:**

| Year | Crude Birth Rate (BARMM) | Crude Death Rate (BARMM) |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 2015 | 20.78% | 1.59% |
| 2018 | 18.18% | 1.42% |
| 2019 | 16.46% | 1.34% |
| 2020 | 16.70% | 1.20% |

**Maternal Mortality Rate:**

| Year | BARMM MMR | National MMR | Notes |
|------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| 2018 | 42.35% | - | Lowest in period |
| 2020 | 73.83% | 70.66% | Above national average |

**Key Issues:**
- Delays in seeking medical care
- Limited access to health services
- Shortage of skilled healthcare personnel
- Geographic barriers (island provinces)

### Healthcare System Challenges

1. **Low immunization rates** including COVID-19 vaccination
2. **Insufficient support** for adolescent and women's health
3. **Limited psychosocial and mental health services**
4. **Low PhilHealth coverage**
5. **Prevalence of malnutrition** and inadequate dietary intake
6. **Human resource shortages** - insufficient doctors, nurses, midwives
7. **Supply chain and data management issues**

### Nutrition (FNRI 2018)

| Indicator | BARMM Rate | National Rate | Status |
|-----------|------------|---------------|--------|
| Stunting (0-59 months) | High | Lower | BARMM highest |
| Wasting | High | Lower | BARMM highest |
| Underweight | High | Lower | BARMM highest |

**Contributing Factors:**
- Food insecurity
- Limited WASH access
- Healthcare access barriers
- Poverty

## Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH)

### Access Indicators

| Indicator | BARMM Status | National Rank |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Basic Safe Water Access | Lowest in country | 17th of 17 regions |
| Sanitation Facilities | Lowest in country | 17th of 17 regions |
| Hygiene Facilities | Lowest in country | 17th of 17 regions |

### Impact on Health

Limited WASH access directly contributes to:
- High stunting rates in children
- High morbidity rates
- Waterborne disease prevalence
- Malnutrition cycles

### Priority Interventions

1. Level I, II, III water supply systems
2. Sanitary toilet facilities construction
3. Hygiene promotion campaigns
4. School WASH programs
5. Community-led total sanitation (CLTS)
