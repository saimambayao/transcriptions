## Territorial Overview

### Total Land Area

**BARMM Total Land Area:** 12,536 sq km (approximately)

### Territorial Composition

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Core Territory | 5 provinces (excluding Isabela City in Basilan) |
| By Plebiscite | Cotabato City and 63 barangays (SGA) |
| Maritime Territory | Sulu-Celebes Seas |

### Geographic Division

**Mainland Provinces:**
- Maguindanao (to be split into Maguindanao del Norte and Maguindanao del Sur)
- Lanao del Sur

**Island Provinces:**
- Basilan (excluding Isabela City)
- Sulu
- Tawi-Tawi

**Commonly referred to as BaSulTa (Basilan-Sulu-Tawi-Tawi)**

## Provinces and Component Cities

### Maguindanao

**Capital:** Buluan

**Key Features:**
- Largest land area in BARMM
- Mindanao River Basin
- Agricultural heartland
- Home to Bangsamoro Government Center

**Municipalities:** 36

**Key Cities/Towns:**
- Cotabato City (administered separately but geographically central)
- Datu Odin Sinsuat
- Sultan Kudarat

### Lanao del Sur

**Capital:** Marawi City

**Key Features:**
- Lake Lanao (second largest lake in Philippines)
- Royal House of Ranao heritage
- Marawi rehabilitation ongoing
- Pat a Pangampong (Four Lake Kingdoms)

**Municipalities:** 39

**Key Sites:**
- Lake Lanao
- Marawi City (Islamic City)
- Torogan (traditional royal houses)

### Basilan

**Capital:** Lamitan City

**Key Features:**
- Island province north of Sulu
- Isabela City NOT part of BARMM
- Agricultural (rubber, coconut)

**Municipalities:** 11 (excluding Isabela City)

**Component City:** Lamitan City

### Sulu

**Capital:** Jolo

**Key Features:**
- Sultanate of Sulu heritage
- Archipelagic province
- Maritime trade history
- Security challenges

**Municipalities:** 19

### Tawi-Tawi

**Capital:** Bongao

**Key Features:**
- Southernmost province of the Philippines
- Gateway to BIMP-EAGA
- Marine biodiversity
- Seaweed capital

**Municipalities:** 11

### Cotabato City

**Status:** Component city, joined BARMM by plebiscite

**Key Features:**
- Bangsamoro Government Center location
- Commercial and transportation hub
- Gateway to BARMM mainland

**Barangays:** 37

### Special Geographic Area (SGA)

**63 barangays** from municipalities that voted to join BARMM:
- Primarily in North Cotabato
- Voted in the 2019 plebiscite

## Natural Hazards and Climate

### Flood Susceptibility

| Province | Risk Level |
|----------|------------|
| Maguindanao | High (Mindanao River Basin) |
| Lanao del Sur | Moderate to High |
| Basilan | Varies by location |
| Sulu | Coastal flooding |
| Tawi-Tawi | Coastal flooding |

### Landslide Susceptibility

| Region | Risk Level |
|--------|------------|
| Mainland Provinces | Higher in uplands |
| Island Provinces | Moderate |

### Fault Lines and Seismic Activity

- Fault lines and trenches present in BARMM
- Earthquake preparedness needed
- Building code compliance important

### Storm Surge

All coastal areas subject to storm surge risks:
- Island provinces highly vulnerable
- Coastal barangays at risk

### Climate Change Vulnerability

The region is increasingly vulnerable to:
- More intense typhoons
- Sea level rise
- Changing weather patterns
- Agricultural impacts

**Example:** Typhoon Paeng (October 2022)
- 61 deaths in BARMM (highest in country)
- Flooding and landslides
- Demonstrated increasing vulnerability

## Key Geographic Features

### Mindanao River Basin

- Largest river system in BARMM
- Agricultural irrigation
- Flood management critical
- Mindanao River Basin Integrated Management and Development Masterplan

### Lake Lanao

- Second largest lake in Philippines
- Cultural significance (Pat a Pangampong)
- Hydroelectric potential
- Water resource management

### Sulu-Celebes Sea

- Rich marine biodiversity
- Fisheries resources
- International maritime routes
- BIMP-EAGA connectivity

### Watersheds and Protected Areas

Multiple declared watersheds for:
- Domestic water production
- Power generation
- Irrigation
- Biodiversity protection
