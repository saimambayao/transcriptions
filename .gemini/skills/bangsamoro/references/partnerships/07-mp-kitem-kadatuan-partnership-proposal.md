## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal invites you to serve as the fiscal steward of BARMM's digital governance transformation. As Chair of the Committee on Finance, Budget and Management, and a member of the Bangsamoro Budget Coordinating Council (BBCC) under the Bangsamoro Budget System Act (BBSA), you are uniquely positioned to ensure that digital governance advances fiscal accountability across the Bangsamoro Government.

The proposed e-Governance Portal provides you with comprehensive access to the Budget Portal—the platform's fiscal management core—alongside the Parliamentary Portal for your legislative work. This fiscal-focused access, designed for BBSA compliance champions, recognizes your critical role in BARMM's budget governance.

With an initial partnership investment of PHP 250,000 per month over six months, you become a founding architect of a system that transforms how BARMM prepares, authorizes, executes, and accounts for its PHP 114.07 billion budget.

Your expertise shapes a platform that makes fiscal accountability not just a requirement, but a capability embedded in daily government operations.

## 2. e-Governance and Its Value to the Bangsamoro Government

### 2.1 The Fiscal Accountability Imperative

BARMM manages one of the largest regional budgets in the Philippines. The Bangsamoro Budget System Act establishes rigorous requirements for budget preparation, authorization, execution, and accountability. Yet traditional paper-based workflows create delays in fiscal reporting, gaps in real-time visibility, and challenges in variance detection.

Digital fiscal governance resolves these tensions by providing real-time budget visibility, automated compliance monitoring, and integrated workflows that ensure every peso is tracked from appropriation through disbursement.

### 2.2 Case Study: Estonia's Digital Fiscal Management

Estonia's digital governance transformation delivered remarkable fiscal benefits:

| Achievement | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Real-time budget visibility** | Government-wide spending tracked daily, not monthly |
| **2% of GDP saved annually** | Elimination of redundant processing and paperwork |
| **Automated compliance** | System-enforced adherence to fiscal rules |
| **Audit efficiency** | Digital trails reduced audit preparation by 80% |
| **Cabinet budget reviews reduced from 5 hours to 30 minutes** | Digital dashboards enable focused fiscal decisions |

Estonia's Finance Ministry reports that digital fiscal tools transformed budget execution from a quarterly reporting exercise to a continuous monitoring capability.

### 2.4 The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254)

On September 5, 2025, President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. signed Republic Act 12254, the **E-Governance Act**, institutionalizing the Philippines' transition to digital governance. This landmark legislation provides both the mandate and framework for BARMM's digital transformation.

#### Key Provisions

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Digital-First Mandate** | All government services must transition to digital platforms |
| **Chief Information Officers** | Every agency must designate a CIO to lead digital transformation |
| **E-Government Master Plan** | DICT must formulate comprehensive digital transformation blueprint |
| **Interoperability Framework** | Agencies must enable secure data sharing through APIs |
| **Cybersecurity Standards** | Mandatory security assessments and CERT establishment |

#### Compliance Timeline

| Timeline | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **1 Year** | All cities and municipalities must establish digital portals or adopt eLGU system |
| **1 Year** | Government must complete transition to e-governance |
| **3 Years** | E-Government Master Plan reviewed and upgraded |
| **Annually** | E-Government maturity surveys and compliance reports |

#### E-Government Programs Mandated

1. **Citizen Frontline Delivery Services Platform** - Unified citizen service access
2. **Electronic Local Government Unit (eLGU) System** - Standardized LGU digital services
3. **Government Digital Payment Systems** - Integrated e-payment gateway
4. **Government Public Key Infrastructure** - Secure digital signatures
5. **Online Public Service Portal** - Single window for government services
6. **Philippine Digital Health System** - Integrated health information

#### Implications for BARMM

RA 12254 creates both **obligation and opportunity** for BARMM:

- **Compliance Requirement**: BARMM ministries and LGUs must meet digital governance standards
- **Funding Access**: Access to E-Government Interoperability Fund for implementation
- **Technical Support**: DICT cooperation for digital transformation roadmap
- **Leadership Opportunity**: Early adoption positions BARMM as compliance leader

The law addresses a critical gap—DICT now has authority to enforce digital integration across all government agencies, making digital transformation not optional but mandatory.
