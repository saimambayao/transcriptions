## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal invites you to serve as a bridge between Parliament and the Executive in BARMM's digital governance transformation. As the Chief Minister's alter-ego in the Bangsamoro Parliament, you occupy a unique position—understanding both legislative imperatives and executive operations. Your role demands government-wide visibility and coordination capability that standard parliamentary tools cannot provide.

The proposed e-Governance Portal provides you with comprehensive access across all three platforms: Parliamentary Portal, Ministerial Portal, and Budget Portal. This government-wide visibility, typically reserved for executive leadership, recognizes your critical coordination role between the legislative and executive branches.

With an initial partnership investment of PHP 250,000 per month over six months, you become a founding architect of a system that enables seamless coordination across BARMM's governance structure.

Your voice in shaping this platform ensures it serves the real needs of parliamentary-executive coordination.

## 2. e-Governance and Its Value to the Bangsamoro Government

### 2.1 The Coordination Imperative

Effective governance requires seamless information flow between Parliament and the Executive. As the Chief Minister's legislative partner, you need real-time visibility into ministerial operations to inform parliamentary proceedings, while ministries need parliamentary context to align their programs. Traditional workflows create information gaps that slow government coordination and reduce effectiveness.

Digital governance bridges these gaps by providing integrated visibility across all government operations, enabling the coordination that effective governance requires.

### 2.2 Case Study: Estonia's e-Cabinet

Estonia's e-Cabinet system demonstrates the transformation digital tools enable for executive-legislative coordination:

| Achievement | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Cabinet meetings reduced from 5 hours to 30 minutes** | Digital briefings enable focused decision-making |
| **Real-time information sharing** | All stakeholders access same data simultaneously |
| **Cross-government coordination** | Ministries and Parliament see aligned information |
| **2.2 billion digital transactions annually** | Seamless data exchange across government |
| **1,345 working years saved annually** | Time redirected to substantive work |

Estonia's Prime Minister's Office reports that digital coordination tools transformed government from siloed operations to unified execution.

### 2.4 The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254)

On September 5, 2025, President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. signed Republic Act 12254, the **E-Governance Act**, institutionalizing the Philippines' transition to digital governance. This landmark legislation provides both the mandate and framework for BARMM's digital transformation.

#### Key Provisions

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Digital-First Mandate** | All government services must transition to digital platforms |
| **Chief Information Officers** | Every agency must designate a CIO to lead digital transformation |
| **E-Government Master Plan** | DICT must formulate comprehensive digital transformation blueprint |
| **Interoperability Framework** | Agencies must enable secure data sharing through APIs |
| **Cybersecurity Standards** | Mandatory security assessments and CERT establishment |

#### Compliance Timeline

| Timeline | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **1 Year** | All cities and municipalities must establish digital portals or adopt eLGU system |
| **1 Year** | Government must complete transition to e-governance |
| **3 Years** | E-Government Master Plan reviewed and upgraded |
| **Annually** | E-Government maturity surveys and compliance reports |

#### E-Government Programs Mandated

1. **Citizen Frontline Delivery Services Platform** - Unified citizen service access
2. **Electronic Local Government Unit (eLGU) System** - Standardized LGU digital services
3. **Government Digital Payment Systems** - Integrated e-payment gateway
4. **Government Public Key Infrastructure** - Secure digital signatures
5. **Online Public Service Portal** - Single window for government services
6. **Philippine Digital Health System** - Integrated health information

#### Implications for BARMM

RA 12254 creates both **obligation and opportunity** for BARMM:

- **Compliance Requirement**: BARMM ministries and LGUs must meet digital governance standards
- **Funding Access**: Access to E-Government Interoperability Fund for implementation
- **Technical Support**: DICT cooperation for digital transformation roadmap
- **Leadership Opportunity**: Early adoption positions BARMM as compliance leader

The law addresses a critical gap—DICT now has authority to enforce digital integration across all government agencies, making digital transformation not optional but mandatory.
