# e-Governance Partnership Proposal

## For the Honorable Speaker of the Bangsamoro Parliament

> **Date**: January 2, 2026
> **Classification**: Strategic Partnership Proposal
> **Reference**: EGOV-2026-001

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal presents a strategic partnership to establish the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) as the Philippines' first digitally-governed autonomous region. Through the deployment of an integrated e-Governance Portal, the Bangsamoro Parliament will gain unprecedented capabilities in legislative management, government oversight, and constituent representation.

The proposed platform draws from Estonia's globally-recognized digital governance model, adapted specifically for BARMM's unique parliamentary structure under the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054). With an initial partnership investment of PHP 250,000 per month over six months, Parliament can pilot transformative digital tools that position BARMM as a model of modern governance in Southeast Asia.

As Speaker, your leadership in championing this initiative establishes the foundation for institutional modernization that will serve the Bangsamoro people for generations.

## 2. e-Governance and Its Value to the Bangsamoro Government

### 2.1 The Global Shift to Digital Governance

Governments worldwide are transitioning from paper-based bureaucracies to integrated digital platforms. This transformation is not merely technological—it represents a fundamental reimagining of how governments serve citizens, manage resources, and ensure accountability. For autonomous regions like BARMM, digital governance offers an opportunity to leapfrog traditional administrative challenges and establish world-class institutions from the outset.

### 2.2 Case Study: Estonia's Digital Society

Estonia, a nation of 1.3 million people, has become the global benchmark for digital governance. Beginning in 1997, Estonia systematically digitized its government operations with remarkable results:

| Achievement | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **99% of public services online** | Citizens access government services 24/7 from any location |
| **2.2 billion digital transactions annually** | Seamless data exchange across all government agencies |
| **2% of GDP saved annually** | Elimination of redundant paperwork and processing |
| **1,345 working years saved per year** | Time redirected from administration to substantive work |
| **Cabinet meetings reduced from 5 hours to 30 minutes** | Digital briefing materials and pre-meeting coordination |

Estonia's e-Cabinet system transformed parliamentary and executive operations. Ministers receive digital briefing packages days before meetings, enabling informed discussion rather than document review. Digital signatures eliminated the need for physical presence for routine approvals, while maintaining complete audit trails for accountability.

### 2.4 The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254)

On September 5, 2025, President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. signed Republic Act 12254, the **E-Governance Act**, institutionalizing the Philippines' transition to digital governance. This landmark legislation provides both the mandate and framework for BARMM's digital transformation.

#### Key Provisions

| Provision | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Digital-First Mandate** | All government services must transition to digital platforms |
| **Chief Information Officers** | Every agency must designate a CIO to lead digital transformation |
| **E-Government Master Plan** | DICT must formulate comprehensive digital transformation blueprint |
| **Interoperability Framework** | Agencies must enable secure data sharing through APIs |
| **Cybersecurity Standards** | Mandatory security assessments and CERT establishment |

#### Compliance Timeline

| Timeline | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **1 Year** | All cities and municipalities must establish digital portals or adopt eLGU system |
| **1 Year** | Government must complete transition to e-governance |
| **3 Years** | E-Government Master Plan reviewed and upgraded |
| **Annually** | E-Government maturity surveys and compliance reports |

#### E-Government Programs Mandated

1. **Citizen Frontline Delivery Services Platform** - Unified citizen service access
2. **Electronic Local Government Unit (eLGU) System** - Standardized LGU digital services
3. **Government Digital Payment Systems** - Integrated e-payment gateway
4. **Government Public Key Infrastructure** - Secure digital signatures
5. **Online Public Service Portal** - Single window for government services
6. **Philippine Digital Health System** - Integrated health information

#### Implications for BARMM

RA 12254 creates both **obligation and opportunity** for BARMM:

- **Compliance Requirement**: BARMM ministries and LGUs must meet digital governance standards
- **Funding Access**: Access to E-Government Interoperability Fund for implementation
- **Technical Support**: DICT cooperation for digital transformation roadmap
- **Leadership Opportunity**: Early adoption positions BARMM as compliance leader

The law addresses a critical gap—DICT now has authority to enforce digital integration across all government agencies, making digital transformation not optional but mandatory.

### 3.2 Parliamentary Portal

The Parliamentary Portal serves as the digital workspace for all legislative operations. It encompasses three core modules:

**Legislative Work Module**

This module provides comprehensive support for the legislative process, from bill drafting through enactment. Key capabilities include:

- Digital bill registry with version control and amendment tracking
- AI-assisted legal research integrated with Philippine jurisprudence databases
- Committee management with automated scheduling and document distribution
- Plenary session support including voting records and attendance tracking
- Resolution tracking from filing through adoption

**Oversight Work Module**

Parliament's constitutional oversight function is enhanced through real-time visibility into executive operations:

- MOA budget variance analysis with automated anomaly detection
- Program and project monitoring aligned with the Bangsamoro Development Plan
- Investigation workflow management for oversight inquiries
- Performance scorecards enabling comparative ministry assessment

**Representation Work Module**

This module strengthens the connection between Parliament Members and their constituents:

- Constituent request management with end-to-end tracking
- Beneficiary registry documenting services provided
- Geographic service mapping across BARMM territories
- Media affairs coordination for public communications

### 3.3 Ministerial Portal

The Ministerial Portal enables strategic planning and coordination across all ministries and attached agencies. Built around the 10-Point Framework for ministerial planning, it provides:

- Unified strategic planning workspace aligned with BOL provisions
- BDP alignment tracking against development targets
- Program, Project, and Activity (PPA) management with UACS coding
- Resource planning across personnel, MOOE, and capital outlay
- Results-Based Monitoring and Evaluation (RBME) dashboards

### 3.4 Budget Portal

The Budget Portal implements the complete BBSA budget lifecycle:

- **Preparation**: Budget ceiling management, MOA proposal submission, BEP development
- **Authorization**: Legislative tracking, budget defense coordination, GAAB viewer
- **Execution**: Appropriation-to-disbursement flow, utilization monitoring, variance alerts
- **Accountability**: BFAR reporting, COA audit management, compliance tracking

### 3.5 Technical Architecture

The platform is built on modern, secure infrastructure:

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Authentication** | JWT-based with 15-minute token rotation |
| **Data Isolation** | PostgreSQL Row-Level Security for multi-tenant separation |
| **Encryption** | TLS 1.3 for transit, AES-256 for data at rest |
| **Audit Logging** | Immutable transaction records for all operations |
| **Availability** | Cloud-hosted with 99.9% uptime target |
