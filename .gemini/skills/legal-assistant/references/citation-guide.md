# Philippine Legal Citation Guide

Working guide for all Bangsamoro parliamentarian skills. Based on the **Philippine Manual of Legal Citations (PMLC)** by Myrna S. Feliciano, 10th Edition (UP Law Complex, 2019). The 10th Ed. incorporates Harvard Bluebook 20th Ed., ALWD Citation Manual 3rd Ed., and OSCOLA standards.

For full depth on any rule, read the complete PMLC transcriptions at:
- `~/Vault/Ph-Laws/philippine-manual-of-legal-citations-10th-ed.md` (10th Edition, 2019 — **authoritative**)
- `~/Vault/Ph-Laws/philippine-manual-of-legal-citations.md` (earlier edition, 1999 — for historical reference)

## Footnote vs Inline Citation

| Document Type | Philippine Law | Islamic Jurisprudence | Statistics/Data |
|---------------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Bill text** (sections) | Inline | Footnote (general in text, source in footnote) | Inline |
| **Explanatory Note** | Inline or footnote | Footnote | Footnote |
| **Resolution** (WHEREAS clauses) | Inline | Footnote | Inline |
| **Legislative Briefer** (CSW) | Inline | Footnote | Inline with source |
| **Speech** (plenary/forum) | Inline (spoken) | Oral reference (no footnote in delivery text) | Inline |
| **Policy Recommendation** | Footnote or endnote | Footnote | Footnote |
| **Legal Memo** | Footnote | Footnote | Footnote |

**Rule:** In enacted legislation (bills, resolutions), Philippine law is cited **inline** within the provision text. Islamic jurisprudence uses **footnotes** to respect the constitutional separation of church and state while honoring the BOL's Article X authorization of Shari'ah legislation.

## I. Primary Sources

### A. Court Decisions

#### 1. Case Names (PMLC Rule I.A.1)

Cite cases by the **surname** of the parties, the volume, title and page of reports, and the year of promulgation in parentheses. Abbreviate *versus* as **v.** Italicize the case title in text; place the full citation in the footnote.

**Exceptions:**
- **Islamic, Chinese, and compound names**: Cite in full (`Lim Sian Tek v. Ladislao`, `People v. De la Cruz`, `Una Kibad v. Commission on Elections`)
- **Corporations/associations**: Cite full name; may abbreviate words after the first (`Allied Workers Ass'n. of the Phil. v. Republic Trading Corp.`)
- **Government/criminal cases**: `Republic v. _____`, `People v. _____`, `U.S. v. _____`
- **Public officers**: Where the person is named in official capacity, give the person's name only (`City of Manila v. Subido`)
- **Procedural terms**: Use `In re`, `Ex parte` as they appear in the decision

#### 2. Case Reports

**(a) Supreme Court --- Philippine Reports** (official, preferred):

> *volume* Phil. *page* (*year*)

Example: `Anaga v. Javellana, 92 Phil. 330 (1952).`

**(b) Supreme Court --- Official Gazette:**

> G.R. No. _____, *date promulgated*, *volume* O.G. *page* (*Month, year of issue*)

Example: `Espiritu v. Rivera, G.R. No. 17092, September 30, 1963, 62 O.G. 7226 (Oct., 1966).`

**(c) Supreme Court --- SCRA** (unofficial but widely used):

> *volume* SCRA *page* (*year*)

Example: `46 SCRA 160 (1972).`

**(d) Supreme Court --- Unreported (G.R.):**

> G.R. No. _____, *date of promulgation*

Example: `Gutierrez v. Diansay, G.R. No. 14733, September 30, 1963.`

**(e) Court of Appeals:**

> C.A.-G.R. No. ___-R/CV/CR/SP, *date of promulgation*

Example: `Caliboso v. Bueno, C.A.-G.R. No. 20401-R, April 8, 1960.`

**(f) Sandiganbayan:**

> Sandiganbayan Crim. Case No. _____, *date*, *volume* Sandiganbayan Rep. *page* (*year*)

**(g) Regional Trial Court:**

> RTC (Place & Branch No.) Civil/Criminal Case No. _____, *date*

Example: `People v. Johnson, RTC (San Jose, Occidental Mindoro, Br. 45) Crim. Case R-1681, August 6, 1984.`

**(h) Shari'ah Courts:**

> Shari'ah Dist./Circ. Ct. (place) SP/Civil/Crim. No. _____, *date*

Example: `Al-Awadhi v. Rulona, Shari'ah Dist. Ct. (Marawi City) Special Proceeding No. 011-87, September 15, 1988.`

**(i) Administrative Decisions:**

> *Agency abbreviation* Case No. _____, *date*

Example: `In re Colocado, CSC Adm. Case No. R-27182, September 29, 1964.`

### B. Statutory Materials

#### 1. Constitution (PMLC Rule I.B.1)

Cite by article, section, and paragraph. When no longer in force, put the year in parentheses.

**Format (current):** `Const. art. [Roman], sec. [number], par. ([number]).`
**Format (former):** `Const. ([year]), art. [Roman], sec. [number].`

**Examples:**
- `Const. art. X, sec. 15.` --- current 1987 Constitution
- `Const. (1935), art. III, sec. 1, par. (3).` --- former Constitution

**In body text** (first mention): Write out "Article" and "Section" in full.
Example: "Section 15, Article X of the Constitution provides..."

**In footnotes/short form:** Use abbreviations: `Const. art. X, sec. 15.`

> **Note:** The abbreviation `Const.` without a year always refers to the 1987 Constitution. In informal bill text, `1987 Constitution, Article X, Section 15` is also acceptable for clarity.

#### 2. Republic Acts (PMLC Rule I.B.2.f)

**Formal (PMLC) format:**

> Rep. Act No. [number] ([year of effectivity]), sec. [number].

**Examples:**
- `Rep. Act No. 11054 (2018), art. VI, sec. 12.`
- `Rep. Act No. 9520 (2009), art. 4, sec. 5.`
- `Rep. Act No. 1792 (1957), sec. 4.`

**Provision references follow the main citation:**
- `Rep. Act No. 11054 (2018), art. XII, sec. 9.`
- `Rep. Act No. 9520 (2009), bk. I, tit. I, ch. 1, art. 4, sec. 5.`

> **Note on shorthand:** In internal references, bill text, and working drafts, `RA 11054` or `RA 11054, Art. VI, Sec. 12` is acceptable and widely used. For formal citations (footnotes, explanatory notes, legal memos), use the PMLC format `Rep. Act No.`

#### 3. Other Session Laws (PMLC Rule I.B.2)

| Law Type | PMLC Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Public Laws (1900--1934) | Act No. [number] ([year]) | `Act No. 1160 (1904), art. 3.` |
| Commonwealth Acts (1935--1945) | Com. Act No. [number] ([year]) | `Com. Act No. 52 (1936), sec. 2(b).` |
| Presidential Decrees (1972--1986) | Pres. Decree No. [number] ([year]) | `Pres. Decree No. 603 (1975).` |
| Batas Pambansa (1984--1986) | Batas Blg. [number] ([year]) | `Batas Blg. 80 (1981).` |

#### 4. Codes (PMLC Rule I.B.3)

Cite the name of the code and the article/section number, without date --- unless the code is no longer in force or has been revised.

**Key Philippine Code Abbreviations (PMLC):**

| Code | Cite As |
|---|---|
| Civil Code | Civil Code, art. [number] |
| Revised Penal Code | Rev. Pen. Code, art. [number] |
| Family Code | Family Code, art. [number] |
| Labor Code | Labor Code, art. [number] |
| Corporation Code | Corp. Code, sec. [number] |
| Cooperative Code | Cooperative Code, art. [number] |
| Local Government Code | Local Govt. Code, sec. [number] |
| Tax Code (NIRC) | Tax Code, sec. [number] |
| Insurance Code | Ins. Code, sec. [number] |
| Muslim Code of Personal Laws | Muslim Code, art. [number] |
| Omnibus Election Code | Elect. Code, sec. [number] |
| Revised Administrative Code | Rev. Adm. Code, sec. [number] |
| Rules of Court | Rules of Court, Rule [number], sec. [number] |

**Examples:**
- `Civil Code, art. 297.`
- `Civil Code (1889), art. 67.` --- former code
- `Muslim Code, art. 13.`
- `Rules of Court, Rule 130, sec. 2, par. (a).`

#### 5. Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL)

The BOL is Republic Act No. 11054 and follows the standard Republic Act format.

**Formal:** `Rep. Act No. 11054 (2018), art. [Roman], sec. [number].`
**Short form:** `BOL, art. VI, sec. 12.`

**In body text (first mention):** "Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), Article VI, Section 12"

#### 6. Presidential Acts (PMLC Rule I.B.6)

**(a) Executive Orders:**

> Exec. Order No. [number] ([year]), *volume* O.G. *page* (*Month, year*).

Example: `Exec. Order No. 329 (1950), 46 O.G. 2035 (July, 1950).`

If the O.G. publication is unavailable: `Exec. Order No. 329 (1950).`

**(b) Proclamations:**

> Proc. No. [number] ([year]).

Example: `Proc. No. 784 (1961), 57 O.G. 7122 (September, 1961).`

**(c) Administrative Orders:**

> Adm. Order No. [number] ([year]).

Example: `Adm. Order No. 21 (1966), 62 O.G. 7194 (October, 1966).`

**(d) Other Martial Law presidential acts:**

| Type | Format |
|---|---|
| General Orders | Gen. Order No. [number] ([year]) |
| Letters of Instructions | L.O.I. No. [number] ([year]) |
| Letters of Implementation | L.O. Impl. No. [number] ([year]) |

#### 7. Administrative Rules and Regulations (PMLC Rule I.B.8)

Cite by agency abbreviation, designation, serial number, and year.

> [Agency abbr.] [designation] No. [number] ([year]).

**Examples:**
- `DCI (Cooperatives) Adm. O. No. 1 (1963).`
- `DNR (Forestry) Adm. O. No. 25 (1976).`
- `Labor Employment Service Reg. No. 3 (1966).`

#### 8. Opinions of the Secretary of Justice

> Sec. of Justice Op. No. [number], s. [year].

Example: `Sec. of Justice Op. No. 271, s. 1982.`

#### 9. Ordinances (PMLC Rule I.B.9)

> [Municipality/City] Ordinance [number], [date].

Example: `Manila Ordinance 6120, Jan. 26, 1967.`

#### 10. Bills, Resolutions and Committee Reports (PMLC Rule I.B.4)

**Congress of the Philippines:**

| Type | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Senate Bill | S. No. [number], [Congress], [Sess.] ([year]) | `S. No. 5, 6th Cong., 1st Sess. (1966).` |
| House Bill | H. No. [number], [Congress], [Sess.] ([year]) | `H. No. 15085, 5th Cong., 4th Sess. (1965).` |
| Senate Resolution | S. Res. [number], [Congress], [Sess.] ([year]) | `S. Res. 1, 4th Cong., 1st Sess. (1958).` |

#### 11. Treaties and International Agreements (PMLC Rule I.B.5)

Cite the name of the agreement, date of signature by the Philippines, and source.

> Treaty of Friendship with India, July 11, 1952 (1953), N-2 DFATS 1, 2 P.T.S. 797, 203 U.N.T.S. 73.

**Treaty source abbreviations:** D.F.A.T.S., P.T.S., L.N.T.S., U.N.T.S., T.I.A.S., U.S.T.

### C. Islamic Jurisprudence

*This section is a **Bangsamoro extension** --- not from the PMLC. The Bangsamoro Parliament may enact Shari'ah-related legislation under BOL Article X, whose principal sources are the Qur'an and the Sunnah.*

#### In-text rule:
Express the principle in **general legislative language** that fits the document's tone. Do not copy Qur'anic verses or Hadith word-for-word into legislative text.

#### Footnote rule:
Place the specific source in a **footnote** with these fields:

**For the Qur'an:**
```
[n] Qur'an, Surah [Name] ([Number]):[Ayat]. Arabic: "[exact Arabic text]".
    Translation: "[English translation]".
```

**Example:**
```
1 Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2):282. Arabic: "يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا تَدَايَنتُم بِدَيْنٍ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُّسَمًّى فَاكْتُبُوهُ".
  Translation: "O you who believe, when you contract a debt for a specified term, write it down."
```

**For Hadith:**
```
[n] [Collection Name], Book [Number], Hadith [Number]. "[Exact text]".
    Translation: "[English translation]".
```

**Example:**
```
2 Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 34, Hadith 19. "لا ضرر ولا ضرار".
  Translation: "There should be neither harming nor reciprocating harm."
```

#### Why footnotes:
This protocol respects the constitutional "separation of church and state" (Const. art. II, sec. 6) while honoring the BOL's authorization of Shari'ah legislation. The legislative text uses neutral, general language; the footnote provides the exact religious source for scholarly verification.

#### Accuracy requirement:
The accuracy of Qur'anic verses and Hadith is **critical**. If a citation cannot be verified, mark it as `[VERIFY: Qur'an/Hadith citation]` and flag it for the user's review. Never fabricate or guess at religious text.

## II. Secondary Sources

### A. Books and Pamphlets (PMLC Rule II.A)

> *volume* (if more than one), author (initial + surname), title, page, edition, and date in parentheses.

Example: `M. Moran, Comments on the Rules of Court 195 (6th ed., 1963).`

### B. Periodical Articles (PMLC Rule II.C)

> Author, *Title*, volume, periodical abbreviation, page (year).

Example: `J. Luna, *International Law Standards and the Philippine Law on Deportation*, 1 Phil. Int'l L.J. 347 (1963).`

**Key Philippine periodical abbreviations:** Phil. L.J., Ateneo L.J., UST L. Rev., San Beda L.J., J. Integ. Bar Phil. (See PMLC for complete list.)

### C. Newspapers (PMLC Rule II.D)

> Newspaper Name, date, p. [page], col. [column].

Example: `Manila Times, Dec. 9, 1966, p. 5, col. 1.`

### D. Internet Sources (PMLC Rule II.J)

> Author, 'Title', *Journal/Site* (year) <URL> [pinpoint].

Example: `People v. Alquizales [Supreme Court] G.R. No. 128386, March 25, 1999 (unreported, Quisumbing, J.) <URL> November 15, 1999 [11-12].`

## III. Repeating Citations (PMLC Rule V)

### A. Supra (above)

Use *supra* to refer to a case or publication **previously cited on the same or preceding page**. Do not use if more than one page intervenes. Do not use for statutes or constitutions.

> `Lao Ichong v. Hernandez, *supra* at 27.`
> `N. Gonzales, *supra* at 188.`
> `See note 15, *supra*.`

### B. Infra (below)

Seldom used. Refers forward to a citation that will appear later.

### C. Ibid. (the same reference)

Use *Ibid.* when a reference is **immediately repeated** with the same volume and page. Not used for primary sources (statutes, constitutions).

### D. Id.

Use *Id.* when citing the **immediately preceding authority** --- including when the page differs.

> ^1 V. Sinco, Philippine Political Law 551 (11th ed., 1962).
> ^2 *Id.* at 555.

**Rules:** Do not use *Id.* for primary sources. If the first citation is to only part of an authority, do not use *Id.* for a subsequent citation to the entire authority.

### E. Op. cit. (work cited)

Use *op. cit.* (or *op. cit. supra*) to avoid repeating titles of books and articles. Precede with the author's name; follow with the earlier footnote reference and page.

> `F. Capistrano, *op. cit. supra* note 15 at 198.`
> `V. Sinco, *op. cit.* note 5 at 133.`

### F. Abbreviated Titles for Repeated Sources

If a source is cited several times, use the full citation once and define an abbreviation in brackets. Use that abbreviation consistently thereafter.

> `International Labour Office, Sharing in Development: A Programme of Employment, Equity and Growth for the Philippines 304 [1974] [hereinafter referred to as the Ranis Report].`

### G. Signals (PMLC Rule V.G)

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Accord* | Substantial support despite different facts |
| *Contra* | Contradicts or opposes the proposition |
| *cf.* | Parallel or supporting case with substantially different facts |
| *See* (italics) | Dictum supporting the proposition |
| *See* (roman) | Secondary authority supporting the proposition |
| *But see* (italics) | Dictum opposing the proposition |
| *But see* (roman) | Secondary authority contradicting the proposition |
| *E.g.* | Illustrative citation from numerous supporting holdings |

### Source Attribution

After every verbatim extraction, note the source:

```
Source: [Transcription file path] or [PDF file path] or [URL]
```

When sourced from the web rather than local files, add:
```
[i] Note: Sourced from web; verify against official gazette or published copy.
```

### Cross-Referencing Rules

1. **First mention**: Always use the full citation with the complete law name and number.
2. **Subsequent mentions**: May use the short form or common abbreviation.
3. **Multiple provisions from the same law**: Cite the law once, then list sections.
   - Example: `Rep. Act No. 9520 (2009), secs. 23, 24, and 25.`
4. **Amending laws**: Note the amending relationship.
   - Example: `Rep. Act No. 11364 (2019) (amending Rep. Act No. 6939), sec. 3.`

## V. Bangsamoro Extensions

*The following formats are **not in the PMLC** (which predates BARMM, established 2019). These are local conventions used in BTA Parliament work.*

### Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts (BAA)

> BAA No. [number], art. [Roman], sec. [number].

**Examples:**
- `BAA No. 13, art. I, sec. 3.`
- `BAA No. 5, sec. 12.`
- `BAA No. 11, art. III, sec. 7(b).`

**Short form:** `BAA No. 13, sec. 3.`

### Parliamentary Resolutions (PR)

> PR No. [number], sec. [number].

**Examples:**
- `PR No. 47, sec. 1.`
- `PR No. 12, Whereas Clause 3.`

### BTA Parliament Bills

> BTA Bill No. [number], [Session], [Parliament] ([year]).

### Implementing Rules and Regulations of BAAs

> IRR of BAA No. [number], Rule [Roman/number], sec. [number].

Example: `IRR of BAA No. 13, Rule V, sec. 15.`

### Memorandum Circulars

> MC [Year]-[Number], "[Full Title]"

**Examples:**
- `MC 2024-15, "Manual of Registration"`
- `MC 2025-02, "Reporting with Annexes"`

**Short form after first citation:** `MC 2024-15`

### BARMM Agency Abbreviations

For the complete BARMM agency hierarchy, see `/bangsamoro` skill's `references/moa-structure.md`. The 16 executive units are: OCM + 15 line ministries (MAFAR, MBHTE, MENRE, MFBM, MOH, MHSD, MIPA, MILG, MOLE, MPOS, MPW, MOST, MSSD, MTIT, MOTC).
