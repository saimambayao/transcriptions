# Budget Briefer Template (BB)

CSW Form: BB
Based on: Office of MP Ramos Budget Briefer Template Ver0 (November 2023)
Improved with: ADDRESS IT methodology mapping + enhanced guide notes

---

## Template Structure

Each section follows the coded pattern: CODE + SECTION + CSW Section Objective + Guide/Notes + Review Questions.

When filling this template, map each section to its ADDRESS IT step.

---

### BB01 — MINISTRY / AGENCY / OFFICE

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 1 (Analyze Context — 1.2 Assess Office's Function)

**CSW Section Objective**: Establish the institutional identity and mandate of the agency under review.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Provide the complete name and acronym of the agency
- State the agency's mandate as defined in the BOL and/or BAA 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code)
- Identify the agency's position within the BARMM organizational structure
- Note the current head of agency (name and title)

**Review Questions**:
1. Is the agency name and acronym accurately stated?
2. Does the mandate description align with the latest version of the BOL and BAA 13?

---

### BB02 — BEP REFERENCE (CODE / PAGE #)

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 1 (Analyze Context — 1.6 Gather Preliminary Data)

**CSW Section Objective**: Enable quick cross-reference to the agency's budget in the Bangsamoro Expenditure Program (BEP).

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Provide the page number(s) where the agency's budget appears in the current year's BEP
- If the BEP is organized by program/department, note the specific section
- Include the BEP document year and version

**Review Questions**:
1. Can the reviewer quickly locate the agency's budget using this reference?

---

### BB03 — MANDATE

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 1 (Analyze Context — 1.5 Review Relevant Laws)

**CSW Section Objective**: Ground the budget analysis in the agency's legal mandate.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Provide the mandate as stated in the BEP
- Attach relevant provisions from BAA 13 for the reviewer's reference
- Identify any recent legislative changes affecting the mandate (new BAAs, amended provisions)
- Note alignment or misalignment between the stated mandate and the budget allocation

**Review Questions**:
1. Is the mandate accurately quoted from the authoritative source?
2. Are there any recent legislative changes that affect this mandate?

---

### BB04 — OVERALL DEVELOPMENT GOAL/S

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 2 (Define Problem — 2.5 Establish Scope and Objectives)

**CSW Section Objective**: Connect the agency's budget to the broader development framework.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- List the development goals from the BEP
- Map each goal to the relevant BDP 2023-2028 goal (6 goals, 8 strategies)
- Identify any goals that do NOT align with the BDP — flag for scrutiny
- Note the 12-point priority agenda items this agency addresses

**Review Questions**:
1. How do the agency's stated goals align with the BDP 2023-2028?
2. Are there goals that seem disconnected from the broader development framework?

---

### BB05 — ORGANIZATIONAL OUTCOMES (CURRENT YEAR)

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 3 (Research — 3.5 Analyze and Synthesize)

**CSW Section Objective**: Analyze the agency's KPIs and outcome indicators for the current budget year.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
1. List all KPIs the agency uses to measure performance and outcomes
2. Define each indicator clearly — avoid vague metrics
3. Evaluate KPI alignment with BDP goals; flag misaligned KPIs with explanation
4. Include baseline data from previous BEPs or GAABs — must be accurate and current
5. Identify new indicators vs. previous years; explain changes
6. Note any inter-agency collaboration in measurement

**Review Questions**:
1. Are the KPI definitions clear and specific?
2. How does each outcome indicator align with BARMM development goals?
3. What is the baseline for each indicator? Is the data accurate and current?
4. What methodology measures each indicator? Is it reliable and consistent?
5. What data sources are used? Are they credible, relevant, and timely?
6. Are there new indicators compared to previous years? Why?
7. Does the agency collaborate with other agencies in measurement?
8. What risks or challenges threaten achieving these targets? What mitigation strategies exist?
9. How do indicators relate to the budget allocated? Is the budget adequate?
10. What are consequences of not meeting targets?

---

### BB06 — BUDGET COMPARISON (PS, MOOE, CO) — MULTI-YEAR

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 3 (Research — 3.5 Analyze and Synthesize) + Step 4 (Explore Options — 4.5 Cost-Benefit)

**CSW Section Objective**: Provide comprehensive multi-year budget comparison with variance analysis.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
1. Analyze budget categories: Personnel Services (PS), MOOE, Capital Outlay (CO) — for each of the past 3 years
2. Create comparison tables/charts for visual clarity
3. Calculate numerical variances (absolute and percentage) between consecutive years
4. Explain significant variances: policy shifts, inflation, specific projects
5. Flag consistent underutilization — seek explanations
6. Cite laws, EOs, or policy directives influencing allocations
7. Compare with similar agencies where relevant
8. Identify multi-year project commitments reflected in budgets
9. Check for GAD (Gender and Development) funds/programs
10. Source all data from official financial statements — verify accuracy
11. Provide preliminary recommendations for further scrutiny

**Review Questions**:
1. Can you explain significant variances in PS/MOOE/CO over the years?
2. Are there underutilized budget items? Why?
3. What laws or policy directives influenced budgetary allocations?
4. Are there multi-year project commitments? How are they reflected?
5. Are the data sources credible and official?
6. Is the budget influenced by stakeholder consultations?
7. How are budgetary gaps or shortfalls addressed? What contingency measures exist?
8. How is gender-responsive budgeting incorporated?

---

### BB07 — EXISTING PPAS (TIER 1)

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 3 (Research) + Step 4 (Explore Options)

**CSW Section Objective**: Analyze existing Programs, Projects, Activities, and Services under Tier 1 funding.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
1. Enumerate and categorize each PPAS aligned with the agency's organizational strategy
2. Indicate budget allocation per PPAS (PS, MOOE, CO breakdown)
3. Briefly describe each PPAS: objective, scope of work, anticipated outputs/outcomes/impact
4. Rank by priority level per agency strategic goals or legislative mandates
5. List performance indicators for each PPAS
6. Reference statutory or policy mandates governing each PPAS

**Review Questions**:
1. Can you share design and implementation documents for existing PPAS?
2. What is the timeline and milestones for each PPAS?
3. What is the extent of stakeholder consultation in PPAS formulation?
4. Are there interagency collaborations essential for implementation?
5. What challenges have been encountered? What are potential risks?
6. How are monitoring, review, and evaluation conducted? Who is responsible?
7. How is data accuracy and reliability ensured?

---

### BB08 — NEW PPAS (TIER 2)

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 4 (Explore Options — 4.2 Generate Options)

**CSW Section Objective**: Analyze new PPAS proposed under Tier 2 funding for the upcoming fiscal year.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
1. Enumerate and categorize new PPAS aligned with organizational strategy
2. Provide detailed budget allocation per new PPAS (PS, MOOE, CO)
3. Research the rationale for each new PPAS: what problem or opportunity does it address?
4. Describe objective, scope of work, anticipated outcomes and benefits
5. Assign priority level per agency strategic goals or legislative mandates
6. Specify performance indicators for evaluation
7. Cite enabling laws, regulations, or policies

**Review Questions**:
1. What is the rationale for introducing each new PPAS?
2. How does each new PPAS align with the agency's strategic goals and the BDP?
3. What are the expected outcomes and how will they be measured?
4. Are the proposed budgets realistic given the scope of work?
5. What risks could affect successful implementation?

---

### BB09 — RELATED LAWS AND POLICIES

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 1 (Analyze Context — 1.5 Review Relevant Laws)

**CSW Section Objective**: Map the legal and policy framework governing the agency's budget and operations.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- List all BAAs, national laws (RAs), executive orders, and administrative orders relevant to the agency
- Include the BOL provisions that define the agency's powers
- Note any pending legislation that could affect the budget
- Identify compliance requirements from each legal instrument

**Review Questions**:
1. Are all relevant laws and policies identified?
2. Are there compliance requirements the agency is not meeting?

---

### BB10 — SHARI'AH ANALYSIS (where applicable)

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 1 (Analyze Context — 1.5) + Step 2 (Define Problem)

**CSW Section Objective**: Assess alignment with Shari'ah principles where the agency's mandate intersects with Islamic governance.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Determine if Shari'ah analysis applies to this agency's budget and programs
- If applicable, assess alignment with BOL Shari'ah provisions (Art. IX)
- Consult with Dar al-Ifta or Shari'ah advisors if needed

---

### BB11 — GENDER ANALYSIS

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 3 (Research — 3.5 Analyze)

**CSW Section Objective**: Assess gender-responsiveness of the agency's budget and programs.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Identify GAD budget allocation and percentage
- Assess whether PPAS address gender-specific needs
- Check for PCW framework compliance adapted to BARMM context

---

### BB12 — RECOMMENDATIONS AND PROPOSED ACTIONS

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 4 (Explore Options — 4.8 Prepare for Recommendation) + Step 5 (Submit)

**CSW Section Objective**: Present the analyst's recommendations based on the budget review.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Summarize key findings from the analysis
- Provide specific, actionable recommendations
- Indicate which recommendations are for the MP's interpellation and which are for committee deliberation
- Prioritize by urgency and impact
- Include proposed questions for budget deliberation

---

### BB13 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**ADDRESS IT Step**: 5 (Submit — 5.2 Write Executive Summary)

**CSW Section Objective**: Provide a high-level overview of the entire budget briefer for the decision-maker.

**CSW Guide / Notes**:
- Write LAST — after all other sections are complete
- Limit to 1-2 paragraphs
- Highlight: agency identity, key budget figures, most significant findings, top recommendation
- This is what the MP reads first — make it count

---

## Output Checklist

Before transmittal, verify:
- [ ] All BB sections (BB01-BB13) are filled — no placeholders
- [ ] Data sourced from official BEP and financial statements
- [ ] Legal citations verified (BOL articles, BAA numbers)
- [ ] Names and titles of officials verified
- [ ] Multi-year budget figures are accurate and consistent
- [ ] Executive summary written last and reflects the full analysis
- [ ] Transmittal memorandum prepared
