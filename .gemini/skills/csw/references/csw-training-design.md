# CSW Training Design — ADDRESS IT Framework

Training design reference for Complete Staff Work (CSW) capacity-building programs
targeting any BARMM entity (Parliament, Ministries, Offices, Agencies, LGUs).

Originally designed for LGU of Paglat (Maguindanao del Sur, April 25-27, 2024).
Updated for universal BARMM application with the 7-step ADDRESS IT framework.

**Author**: Saidamen R. Mambayao

---

## Training Overview

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3 days (can be compressed to 2.5 days) |
| **Target Participants** | Staff, technical personnel, and mid-level managers of any BARMM entity |
| **Prerequisites** | None — designed for mixed-level audiences |
| **Maximum Class Size** | 30 participants (for effective writeshop facilitation) |
| **Key References** | MC No. 72/2019, MC No. 2/2022, ADDRESS IT Framework, ACSCCRaPATFEE Competencies |
| **Output** | Participants produce a draft CSW output using ADDRESS IT during the writeshop |

## Day 1: Foundations (7 hours)

### Session 0: Opening and Energizer (45 min)
- **Opening program** (15 min): Welcome, prayer, introduction of participants
- **"CSW Can Be Fun!" — Marshmallow Tower Challenge** (30 min)
  - Teams of 4-5 build the tallest freestanding structure using 20 sticks of spaghetti, 1 meter of tape, 1 meter of string, and 1 marshmallow (placed on top)
  - Debrief points:
    - The marshmallow represents the decision-maker's approval — you build everything to support it
    - Teams that prototype and iterate (like ADDRESS IT Step 7) outperform teams that plan a single grand design
    - Collaboration (Competency 5) and time management (Competency 9) determine success
  - Transition: "CSW is exactly like this — structured process, teamwork, iteration, and a clear deliverable at the top"

### Session 1: Introduction to CSW (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1.1 History and Concept | 30 min | What is CSW; origin (US military staff work); Philippine adoption; the "completed" standard — the superior should only need to approve or disapprove |
| 1.2 Legal Basis | 30 min | MC No. 72/2019 (CSW for national government); MC No. 2/2022 (CSW for LGUs); applicability to BARMM entities under BOL |
| 1.3 Agencies Implementing CSW | 30 min | Examples from national government, BARMM Parliament, ministries; how CSW applies to different entity types (legislative vs. executive vs. LGU) |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can explain what CSW is, why it matters, and its legal basis in BARMM governance.

### Session 2: Importance of CSW in BARMM Governance (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 2.1 CSW and Good Governance | 30 min | How CSW supports transparency, accountability, and evidence-based decision-making |
| 2.2 CSW in the BARMM Context | 30 min | Unique challenges: transition government, BOL mandates, BDP priorities, inter-governmental coordination |
| 2.3 Common CSW Failures | 30 min | Case studies of incomplete staff work — what happens when CSW is rushed, shallow, or politically compromised |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can articulate why CSW is critical for BARMM entities and identify common failure patterns.

### Session 3: Competencies of CSW Practitioners — ACSCCRaPATFEE (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 The 12 Competencies Overview | 30 min | Walk through the ACSCCRaPATFEE framework; mapping to ADDRESS IT steps |
| 3.2 Self-Assessment Exercise | 30 min | Participants rate themselves on each competency (Limited to Expert); identify top 3 strengths and top 3 gaps |
| 3.3 Competency Development Planning | 30 min | Pair discussion: What competencies does your entity need most? What can you develop in 90 days? |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can identify the 12 CSW competencies and assess their own proficiency levels.

**Reference**: `csw-competencies.md` (ACSCCRaPATFEE indicators and rubrics)

### Session 4: The ADDRESS IT Process — Steps 1-3 (1.75 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 Step 1: Analyze the Context | 35 min | Understanding the assignment; assessing the office's function; identifying stakeholders; PESTEL analysis; reviewing relevant laws |
| 4.2 Step 2: Define and Deliberate | 35 min | Articulating the problem/need clearly; deliberating with stakeholders; agreeing on scope and boundaries |
| 4.3 Step 3: Research Needed Information | 35 min | Identifying information needs; research methods; evaluating sources; organizing findings; identifying gaps |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can execute the first three ADDRESS IT steps for a real-world CSW scenario.

**Reference**: `address-it-framework.md` (Steps 1-3 detailed procedures)

## Day 2: Process and Practice (7 hours)

### Session 5: The ADDRESS IT Process — Steps 4-7 (2.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1 Step 4: Explore Options | 35 min | Generating alternatives; comparative analysis; cost-benefit analysis; risk assessment; selecting the best option |
| 5.2 Step 5: Submit Proposal or Report | 35 min | Structuring the CSW output; writing for decision-makers; the "completed" standard; quality review checklist |
| 5.3 Step 6: Sustain Efforts to Implement | 30 min | Implementation planning; monitoring and evaluation; stakeholder communication; progress reporting |
| 5.4 Step 7: Iterate and Improve | 30 min | Feedback loops; performance measurement; continuous improvement; when to loop back to Step 1 |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can execute all 7 ADDRESS IT steps and understand the cyclical nature of the process.

**Reference**: `address-it-framework.md` (Steps 4-7 detailed procedures)

### Session 6: CSW Output Types and Templates (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 6.1 Types of CSW Outputs | 30 min | Policy memos, project proposals, legal opinions, budget briefers, legislative briefers, position papers |
| 6.2 Template Walkthrough | 30 min | Review templates: general CSW, budget briefer, legal opinion, project proposal |
| 6.3 Adapting Templates to Your Entity | 30 min | How Parliament staff vs. ministry staff vs. LGU staff adapt the same framework to different output types |

**Learning Objective**: Participants can identify which CSW output type fits their assignment and adapt templates accordingly.

**References**: `general-csw-template.md`, `budget-briefer-template.md`, `legal-opinion-template.md`, `project-proposal-template.md`

### Session 7: Writeshop Preparation (1 hour)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 7.1 Selecting Real CSW Topics | 30 min | Each participant or team selects a real problem from their entity; facilitator validates topic suitability |
| 7.2 Forming Writeshop Teams | 30 min | Teams of 3-4 formed by entity or function; roles assigned (lead writer, researcher, reviewer) |

**Learning Objective**: Each team has a validated topic and clear roles for the writeshop.

### Session 8: Writeshop — ADDRESS IT Steps 1-4 (2 hours)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Step 1: Analyze Context | 30 min | Teams document the context, stakeholders, and legal/policy landscape for their chosen topic |
| Step 2: Define and Deliberate | 20 min | Teams articulate the problem statement; peer feedback from another team |
| Step 3: Research | 40 min | Teams identify and gather needed information (using materials brought, phones, facilitator guidance) |
| Step 4: Explore Options | 30 min | Teams generate at least 3 options and select the best one with rationale |

**Facilitator Role**: Circulate, provide feedback, ensure teams are following ADDRESS IT structure.

## Day 3: Writeshop and Presentation (6 hours)

### Session 9: Writeshop — ADDRESS IT Steps 5-7 (2.5 hours)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Step 5: Draft the CSW Output | 90 min | Teams draft their CSW output using the appropriate template |
| Quality Review | 30 min | Peer review using quality checklist; revisions |
| Steps 6-7: Implementation and Iteration Plan | 30 min | Teams outline how the CSW would be implemented and what iteration triggers exist |

**Facilitator Role**: Focus on quality — formatting, completeness, decision-readiness.

### Session 10: Team Presentations (2.5 hours)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Presentations | 90 min | Each team presents their CSW output (10-15 min per team) |
| Panel Feedback | 45 min | Facilitator and invited resource persons provide structured feedback using the assessment rubric |
| Revision Time | 15 min | Teams note feedback for post-training refinement |

**Assessment Criteria**: Use ACSCCRaPATFEE rubric dimensions — analytical quality, communication clarity, attention to detail, ethical grounding.

### Session 11: Synthesis and Action Planning (1 hour)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 11.1 Key Takeaways | 20 min | Facilitator-led synthesis of ADDRESS IT process, competencies, and common writeshop challenges |
| 11.2 Individual Action Plans | 20 min | Each participant writes a 90-day CSW improvement plan: 2 competencies to develop, 1 CSW output to complete |
| 11.3 Closing and Evaluation | 20 min | Training evaluation forms; certificates; closing program |

**Learning Objective**: Participants leave with a concrete personal development plan for CSW practice.

## Training Adaptation Guide

This design is entity-agnostic. Adapt it based on your target audience:

| Entity Type | Key Adaptations |
|---|---|
| **BTA Parliament** | Emphasize legislative briefers, bill analysis, committee reports; use BAA examples |
| **Ministries/Offices/Agencies (MOAs)** | Emphasize policy memos, project proposals, budget briefers; use ministry-specific cases |
| **LGUs** | Emphasize local governance challenges; use MC No. 2/2022 as primary legal basis; simpler output formats |
| **Mixed Audience** | Use generic CSW scenarios in writeshop; form cross-entity teams for knowledge exchange |

## Pre-Training Requirements

- Participants should bring 1-2 real work problems they want to address through CSW
- Entity head should endorse participation and commit to reviewing writeshop outputs post-training
- Facilitator should review the entity's mandate, recent priorities, and organizational structure before training

## Post-Training Follow-Up

- **30 days**: Participants submit their completed CSW output (the one started in the writeshop)
- **60 days**: Check-in on 90-day action plan progress
- **90 days**: Follow-up assessment using ACSCCRaPATFEE rubric; identify candidates for advanced training

## Detailed Session Plans

Expanded session-level guidance based on the CSW Session Guide v1.1. Use these plans when facilitating each session or when generating facilitator guides via `/training-assistant`.

### Session 1: Introduction to Complete Staff Work (2 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Activity Type | Details |
|---------|----------|---------------|---------|
| 1.1 Marshmallow Challenge | 30 min | Energizer / Team Activity | Teams of 4-5 build the tallest freestanding structure using spaghetti, tape, string, and a marshmallow on top. Debrief: the marshmallow = decision-maker's approval; iteration beats grand planning; collaboration and time management determine success. |
| 1.2 CSW Concept and History | 5 min | Presentation | What is CSW; origin in US military staff work; Philippine adoption; the "completed" standard — the superior should only need to approve or disapprove. |
| 1.3 Importance in Local Governance | 10 min | Presentation + Discussion | How CSW supports transparency, accountability, and evidence-based decision-making in BARMM entities and LGUs. |
| 1.4 The 5-Step CSW Process Overview | 20 min | Presentation | Walk through the ADDRESS IT framework at a high level — what each step does and why the sequence matters. Introduce the 7-step process as a structured methodology. |
| 1.5 Workshop: Identifying CSW Examples | 45 min | Group Activity | Participants work in small groups to identify real examples of CSW from their daily work. Each group presents 2-3 examples and classifies them by CSW type (policy memo, project proposal, budget briefer, etc.). Facilitator maps examples to ADDRESS IT steps. |
| 1.6 Q&A and Synthesis | 10 min | Open Discussion | Address questions; reinforce key concepts; preview next session. |

**Facilitator Notes**: The marshmallow challenge sets the tone — CSW should feel engaging, not bureaucratic. The workshop segment (1.5) is critical for grounding the concepts in participants' actual experience.

### Session 2: Importance of CSW (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Activity Type | Details |
|---------|----------|---------------|---------|
| 2.1 Video Screening | 20 min | Multimedia | Screen a video on governance challenges, decision-making failures, or CSW success stories. Suggested: case study of a policy decision made with and without proper staff work. |
| 2.2 Group Discussion: Governance Challenges | 40 min | Small Group Activity | Groups discuss: What governance challenges in your entity could be addressed by better CSW? Each group identifies their top 3 challenges and presents to plenary. |
| 2.3 CSW Failures Case Studies | 30 min | Presentation + Discussion | Facilitator presents 2-3 real (anonymized) cases of incomplete staff work — what went wrong, what the consequences were, and how ADDRESS IT would have prevented the failure. |

**Facilitator Notes**: The video screening should be emotionally engaging — show real consequences of poor decision-making. The group activity should surface actual pain points that the rest of the training will address.

### Session 3: CSW Competencies (1.5 hours)

| Segment | Duration | Activity Type | Details |
|---------|----------|---------------|---------|
| 3.1 ACSCCRaPATFEE Overview | 20 min | Presentation | Walk through the 12 competencies; explain the acronym; show the mapping to ADDRESS IT steps. |
| 3.2 Self-Assessment Exercise | 30 min | Individual Activity | Each participant completes the CSW Competency Assessment Tool (7-level scale). See `csw-assessment-tool.md` for the instrument. Participants identify their top 3 strengths and top 3 development areas. |
| 3.3 Group Discussion: Competency Priorities | 25 min | Small Group Activity | Groups discuss: Which competencies does your entity need most? What can you realistically develop in 90 days? Groups share their top priority competencies. |
| 3.4 Competency Development Planning | 15 min | Individual Activity | Each participant drafts a brief personal development plan: 2 competencies to strengthen, specific actions for each. |

**Facilitator Notes**: Collect the pre-training assessment forms after segment 3.2 — these become the baseline for post-training comparison. Emphasize that the assessment is a development tool, not an evaluation for promotion.

**Reference**: `csw-assessment-tool.md` (7-level assessment instrument), `csw-competencies.md` (competency definitions and rubrics)

### Sessions 4-8: The ADDRESS IT Process Steps (One Session Per Step)

Each session follows a consistent pattern: concept presentation, worked example, hands-on practice.

| Session | ADDRESS IT Step | Duration | Key Activities |
|---------|----------------|----------|----------------|
| **Session 4** | Step 1: Analyze the Context | 1.5 hours | Presentation on context analysis methods (PESTEL, stakeholder mapping); hands-on exercise using a provided case study; participants practice analyzing context for their own CSW topic. |
| **Session 5** | Step 2: Define and Deliberate | 1.5 hours | Problem statement writing workshop; 5 Whys exercise; role-play deliberation with stakeholders; peer feedback on problem statements. |
| **Session 6** | Step 3: Research Needed Information | 1.5 hours | Research methodology overview; source evaluation exercise; participants identify information needs for their CSW topic and develop a research plan. |
| **Session 7** | Step 4: Explore Options | 1.5 hours | Option generation techniques (brainstorming, benchmarking); comparative analysis matrices; cost-benefit analysis basics; participants generate and evaluate 3+ options for their CSW topic. |
| **Session 8** | Step 5: Submit Proposal or Report | 1.5 hours | Template walkthrough for the appropriate CSW type; writing-for-decision-makers principles; quality review checklist application; participants draft key sections of their CSW output. |

**Facilitator Notes**: Steps 6 (Sustain) and 7 (Iterate) are covered as part of the writeshop (Sessions 8-9 in the main training design above) rather than as standalone sessions, since they apply after the CSW document is produced.

### Workshop: Status, Benefits, Challenges, and Strategies of CSW Implementation

This workshop segment can be used as a standalone activity or integrated into the training. It works especially well as a Day 2 afternoon activity or as a post-training follow-up session.

**Duration**: 2 hours

| Segment | Duration | Activity |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Small Group Discussion | 60 min | Groups of 5-6 discuss the questions below; each group gets a designated facilitator and recorder. |
| Plenary Presentation | 40 min | Each group presents their key findings (5-7 min per group). |
| Synthesis and Action Items | 20 min | Facilitator synthesizes common themes; group agrees on priority actions. |

**Discussion Questions**:

1. **Current Status** — What is the current status of CSW implementation in your office or entity? Is CSW formally adopted? How consistently is it practiced?
2. **Key Benefits** — What benefits have you experienced (or would you expect) from implementing CSW? How has it improved decision-making quality?
3. **Challenges and Barriers** — What are the main challenges and barriers to effective CSW implementation? (Consider: capacity, culture, leadership buy-in, time constraints, tools.)
4. **Strategies for Effective Implementation** — What strategies would make CSW implementation more effective in your entity? What has worked elsewhere?
5. **Integration with Planning and Budgeting** — How can CSW be integrated into your entity's planning and budgeting processes? What changes are needed?
6. **Capacity Building Interventions** — What specific capacity building interventions are needed to strengthen CSW practice? (Consider: training, mentoring, templates, technology.)
7. **Monitoring and Evaluation** — What M&E mechanisms should be established to track CSW utilization and quality over time? How do you measure whether CSW is improving governance outcomes?

**Expected Outputs**:
- Entity-level CSW implementation status assessment
- Priority list of barriers and proposed solutions
- Recommendations for integrating CSW into institutional processes
- Capacity building roadmap with specific interventions and timelines
