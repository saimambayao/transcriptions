# CSW Competency Assessment Tool

A structured assessment instrument for measuring Complete Staff Work (CSW) practitioner competencies using the **ACSCCRaPATFEE** framework. Designed for pre-training baseline measurement, post-training evaluation, and ongoing professional development in any BARMM entity.

**Author**: Saidamen R. Mambayao

---

## Overview

This tool measures a practitioner's proficiency across all 12 ACSCCRaPATFEE competencies using a **7-level rating scale**. Unlike the competency descriptions in `csw-competencies.md` (which define what each competency means), this instrument is the **actual assessment form** — the questions respondents answer and the scale they use.

### Use Cases

- **Pre-training assessment** — establish baseline proficiency before a CSW training intervention
- **Post-training assessment** — measure competency gains after training (compare against pre-training scores)
- **Self-assessment** — practitioners rate themselves to identify development priorities
- **Supervisor assessment** — supervisors rate staff to validate self-assessments and identify blind spots
- **Organizational capacity mapping** — aggregate scores across an entity to identify systemic competency gaps

---

## 7-Level Rating Scale

| Level | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Novice** | New to this area; needs extensive guidance; struggles with basic concepts |
| 2 | **Beginner** | General understanding of basics; can complete simple tasks with support |
| 3 | **Emerging** | Independently handles routine tasks; becoming comfortable with the area |
| 4 | **Intermediate** | Reliable grasp; independently manages most tasks; solves common problems |
| 5 | **Proficient** | Strong understanding; confidently navigates complex tasks; shares knowledge with others |
| 6 | **Advanced** | Deep, comprehensive understanding; develops creative solutions; provides leadership to others |
| 7 | **Expert** | Leading authority; pioneers innovative approaches; sets strategic direction for the organization |

### Scale Usage Notes

- **Odd-numbered scales** (1-7) are preferred over even-numbered scales because they allow a true midpoint (4 = Intermediate), reducing forced-choice bias
- Respondents should select the level that **best describes their current, consistent behavior** — not their best day or worst day
- For supervisor assessments, ratings should be based on **observed behavior over at least 3 months**, not a single incident

### Administration Modes

| Mode | Who Rates | When | Purpose |
|------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Self-Assessment** | The practitioner rates themselves | Pre-training (Day 1) and post-training (Day 3 or 30 days after) | Identify self-perceived strengths and gaps |
| **Supervisor Assessment** | The practitioner's direct supervisor | Pre-training and 90 days post-training | Validate self-assessment; measure observed behavioral change |
| **360-Degree** | Self + supervisor + 2 peers | Annual development review | Comprehensive proficiency profile |

### Administration Instructions

1. **Explain the purpose** — This is a development tool, not a performance evaluation for promotion or discipline. Honest self-rating produces better development plans.
2. **Read each question aloud** (for group administration) or provide printed forms (for individual completion).
3. **Allow 20-30 minutes** for completion of all 12 competency sections (36-48 questions).
4. **Collect forms immediately** — do not allow respondents to take forms home and return them later (this introduces social desirability bias).
5. **For pre/post comparison** — use the exact same questions in both administrations. Do not change wording between pre and post.

## Scoring and Interpretation

### Individual Score Calculation

1. **Competency Score** = Average of all item ratings within that competency (round to 1 decimal place)
2. **Overall CSW Score** = Average of all 12 competency scores
3. **ADDRESS IT Step Readiness** = Average of competencies mapped to each step (see mapping table below)

### ADDRESS IT Step Readiness Mapping

| ADDRESS IT Step | Mapped Competencies | How to Calculate |
|-----------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Step 1: Analyze Context | Competencies 1, 3 | Average of C1 and C3 scores |
| Step 2: Define and Deliberate | Competencies 5, 7, 11 | Average of C5, C7, C11 scores |
| Step 3: Research | Competencies 1, 6 | Average of C1 and C6 scores |
| Step 4: Explore Options | Competencies 2, 3, 7 | Average of C2, C3, C7 scores |
| Step 5: Submit | Competencies 4, 8 | Average of C4 and C8 scores |
| Step 6: Sustain | Competencies 4, 5, 10, 11 | Average of C4, C5, C10, C11 scores |
| Step 7: Iterate | Competencies 3, 10 | Average of C3 and C10 scores |
| Cross-cutting | Competencies 9, 12 | Average of C9 and C12 scores |

### Interpretation Guide

| Score Range | Proficiency Band | Interpretation |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1.0 - 1.9 | **Novice** | Needs intensive mentoring and structured learning; not ready for independent CSW |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | **Beginner** | Can handle simple, well-defined tasks with close supervision |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | **Emerging** | Ready for routine CSW assignments with periodic check-ins |
| 4.0 - 4.9 | **Intermediate** | Can independently produce standard CSW outputs; ready for moderate complexity |
| 5.0 - 5.9 | **Proficient** | Handles complex CSW; can mentor junior staff; reliable for high-stakes assignments |
| 6.0 - 6.9 | **Advanced** | Can lead CSW processes; develops new approaches; serves as organizational resource |
| 7.0 | **Expert** | Thought leader; designs systems and frameworks; strategic CSW authority |

### Identifying Development Priorities

1. **Rank competencies** from lowest to highest score
2. **Flag critical gaps** — any competency scored below 3.0 needs immediate attention
3. **Identify the weakest ADDRESS IT step** — use the step readiness mapping to find which step in the CSW process is most at risk
4. **Cross-reference with job demands** — a competency gap matters more if the practitioner's role heavily depends on that competency (e.g., a researcher with low Research and Analysis scores vs. low Communication scores)
5. **Compare self vs. supervisor ratings** — gaps of 2+ levels between self-assessment and supervisor assessment indicate blind spots that need discussion

### Pre/Post Training Comparison

| Metric | How to Calculate | What It Tells You |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Competency Gain** | Post-score minus Pre-score per competency | Which competencies improved most |
| **Overall Gain** | Post-overall minus Pre-overall | Aggregate training impact |
| **Effect Size** | Mean gain / Standard deviation of pre-scores | Whether the gain is statistically meaningful (>0.5 = medium, >0.8 = large) |
| **Ceiling Effect Check** | Count of pre-scores at 6-7 | High pre-scores leave little room for measured improvement |

## Using Results to Design Targeted Training

### From Assessment to Training Design

| Assessment Finding | Training Response |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Entity-wide low scores on Competencies 1, 6 (Analytical Thinking, Research) | Prioritize ADDRESS IT Steps 1 and 3 sessions; add research methodology workshop |
| Individual low scores on Competency 8 (Attention to Detail) | Pair with quality review exercises; assign as peer reviewer to build the habit |
| Weak ADDRESS IT Step 5 readiness | Focus on CSW output formatting, template usage, and writing-for-decision-makers exercises |
| Large self-supervisor rating gaps | Include 360-degree feedback exercises; facilitate honest self-reflection activities |
| High scores across the board (5+) | Shift from basic training to advanced: case studies, mentoring roles, process design |

### Training Duration Recommendations by Score

| Average Pre-Training Score | Recommended Training Duration |
|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| 1.0 - 2.5 | 5 days (extended program with intensive practice) |
| 2.5 - 3.5 | 3 days (standard program per `csw-training-design.md`) |
| 3.5 - 5.0 | 2 days (accelerated, focus on advanced topics and writeshop) |
| 5.0+ | 1 day (refresher + advanced case studies + mentoring certification) |

### Feeding Results into /training-assistant

When passing assessment results to `/training-assistant` for training package generation:
- Include the aggregate competency scores and the weakest ADDRESS IT steps
- Specify which competencies to prioritize in session design
- Request pre/post assessment forms using this instrument's question bank
- Request that writeshop scenarios target the identified weak competencies
