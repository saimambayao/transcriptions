# Supervision System (SV) — CSW Template

A coded-section template for designing organizational supervision systems. Based on the MDN Supervision System concept, adapted for BARMM entities using the ADDRESS IT methodology.

**Type Code**: SV (Supervision System)

---

## SV01: Organization Profile

**CSW Section Objective**: Establish the organizational context — mandate, structure, and staffing — so the supervision system is grounded in the entity's actual operating environment.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- State the organization's legal mandate (BOL provision, BAA, Executive Order, or national law)
- Describe the organizational structure (divisions, sections, units) with reporting lines
- Provide staffing complement: total positions, filled vs. vacant, plantilla vs. contractual
- Identify the geographic scope (central office, regional/field offices, satellite units)
- Note any unique operating conditions (conflict-affected areas, remote locations, multi-site operations)

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Does the profile accurately reflect the current organizational structure and staffing?
- Are all reporting lines clearly identified from the head of entity down to frontline staff?
- Is the mandate statement traceable to a specific legal instrument?

---

## SV02: Supervisory Framework

**CSW Section Objective**: Define the supervisory philosophy, guiding principles, and legal/regulatory basis that govern how supervision is conducted in the organization.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- State the supervisory philosophy (e.g., developmental vs. directive, coaching-oriented, results-based)
- List guiding principles (e.g., transparency, fairness, due process, constructive feedback, moral governance)
- Cite the legal basis: Civil Service Commission rules, BARMM Administrative Code, relevant BAAs, internal policies
- Define the scope of supervision: what falls within supervisory authority and what does not
- Distinguish between administrative supervision (attendance, compliance) and technical supervision (quality of outputs)

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Is the supervisory philosophy consistent with the organization's values and mandate?
- Are the legal bases current and correctly cited?
- Does the framework clearly distinguish administrative from technical supervision?

---

## SV03: Supervisory Roles and Responsibilities

**CSW Section Objective**: Define supervisory expectations at each organizational level so every person in the chain understands their role.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- **Director / Head of Entity**: Sets strategic direction, approves major outputs, holds division chiefs accountable, conducts quarterly performance reviews
- **Division Chief / Department Head**: Manages division workload, assigns tasks, reviews CSW before submission to Director, conducts monthly performance check-ins, mentors supervisors
- **Section Supervisor / Unit Head**: Distributes daily tasks, provides technical guidance, conducts weekly check-ins with staff, reviews work quality, flags issues to Division Chief
- **Staff / Technical Officer**: Executes assigned tasks using ADDRESS IT methodology, submits CSW to supervisor for clearance, self-monitors progress against deadlines, seeks guidance when blocked
- For each level, specify: what they approve, what they review, what they escalate, and what they report

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Are responsibilities specific enough that overlaps and gaps are eliminated?
- Does each level know exactly what they approve vs. what they escalate?
- Is the CSW clearance chain clearly mapped (Staff -> Supervisor -> Division Chief -> Director)?

## SV04: CSW Integration

**CSW Section Objective**: Show how the ADDRESS IT methodology integrates into the daily supervision workflow — making CSW the standard operating procedure for staff work, not an add-on.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Map ADDRESS IT steps to supervisor touchpoints:
  - Step 1 (Analyze): Supervisor assigns task with clear parameters; staff confirms understanding
  - Step 2 (Define): Staff presents problem statement to supervisor for validation before proceeding
  - Steps 3-4 (Research/Explore): Supervisor checks in at midpoint; staff may need access or clearance
  - Step 5 (Submit): Supervisor reviews and clears/endorses before submission to decision-maker
  - Steps 6-7 (Sustain/Iterate): Supervisor monitors implementation; staff reports progress
- Define CSW quality standards the supervisor checks: completeness, accuracy, timeliness, alignment with assignment
- Specify turnaround times for supervisor review at each checkpoint
- Include the transmittal memorandum as the standard handoff document

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Are supervisor touchpoints mapped to every ADDRESS IT step?
- Are turnaround times for review realistic given the supervisor's workload?
- Does the integration make CSW a natural part of the workflow rather than an additional burden?

## SV05: Performance Management System

**CSW Section Objective**: Define how individual and team performance is measured, evaluated, and tied to organizational outcomes.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Establish Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) by role level:
  - Staff: CSW quality score, timeliness, completeness, number of rework cycles
  - Supervisor: team output quality, staff development progress, turnaround time on reviews
  - Division Chief: division-level targets, cross-unit coordination, strategic alignment
- Define evaluation cycles: quarterly check-ins, semi-annual reviews, annual appraisal (aligned with CSC SPMS)
- Specify evaluation tools: Individual Performance Commitment and Review (IPCR), Office Performance Commitment and Review (OPCR)
- Link performance to consequences: recognition, promotion eligibility, performance bonus, coaching plans, progressive discipline
- Include both quantitative metrics (deadlines met, outputs delivered) and qualitative assessments (stakeholder feedback, innovation)

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Are KPIs SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)?
- Do evaluation cycles align with CSC Strategic Performance Management System requirements?
- Is there a clear link between performance ratings and consequences (both positive and corrective)?

## SV06: Delegation of Authority

**CSW Section Objective**: Establish a decision-making matrix that clarifies who can approve what, and how issues escalate when they exceed a level's authority.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Create a Delegation Matrix (decision type vs. authority level):
  - Routine operational decisions (supervisor level)
  - Resource allocation up to threshold (division chief level)
  - Policy interpretation and exceptions (director level)
  - Inter-agency coordination and commitments (head of entity)
- Define escalation procedures: when to escalate, to whom, with what supporting documentation
- Specify acting authority protocols: who acts when a supervisor is absent, what authorities transfer
- Address special delegations: signing authority, financial approval thresholds, procurement limits
- Include redelegation rules: can delegated authority be further delegated?

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Does the matrix cover all common decision types the organization encounters?
- Are financial thresholds aligned with COA and BARMM procurement rules?
- Is the escalation path clear — does every staff member know where to go when they hit their ceiling?

## SV07: Feedback and Coaching Framework

**CSW Section Objective**: Structure regular supervisory interactions focused on staff growth, not just compliance checking.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Define feedback frequency and format:
  - Daily: informal guidance on active tasks (verbal or chat)
  - Weekly: structured one-on-one check-ins (15-30 minutes) covering task status, blockers, priorities
  - Monthly: performance conversation reviewing KPI progress, CSW quality trends, development goals
  - Quarterly: formal review aligned with SPMS cycle
- Coaching approach: use the SBI model (Situation-Behavior-Impact) for feedback delivery
- Document coaching conversations: maintain a supervision log for each staff member
- Address difficult conversations: underperformance, behavioral issues, interpersonal conflicts
- Include peer feedback mechanisms where appropriate
- Positive reinforcement: recognition practices, commendation letters, team celebrations

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Is the feedback schedule realistic given supervisory span of control?
- Are supervisors trained in coaching techniques (SBI, active listening, constructive feedback)?
- Is there a documentation system for coaching conversations that protects both parties?

## SV08: Staff Development Plan

**CSW Section Objective**: Map competency gaps to training interventions and career paths so supervision drives professional growth.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Conduct competency assessment using the ACSCCRaPATFEE framework (12 CSW competencies) where applicable
- Identify gaps by comparing current competency levels against role requirements
- Design individual development plans (IDPs) for each staff member:
  - Target competencies to develop
  - Specific interventions (training, mentoring, job rotation, stretch assignments)
  - Timeline and milestones
  - Success indicators
- Map career paths: what competencies and experience are needed for promotion to the next level
- Budget for training: allocate resources for formal training, conferences, certifications
- Include knowledge transfer mechanisms: documentation, shadowing, cross-training

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Are competency assessments conducted at least annually?
- Does every staff member have a current IDP that they helped design?
- Are career paths transparent and tied to demonstrable competency development?

## SV09: Accountability and Compliance

**CSW Section Objective**: Ensure the supervision system includes reporting requirements and audit mechanisms that keep supervisors and staff accountable.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Define reporting requirements:
  - Staff: weekly accomplishment reports, CSW status updates
  - Supervisors: monthly division reports, staff performance summaries
  - Division Chiefs: quarterly reports to head of entity
- Audit mechanisms:
  - Internal quality audits of CSW outputs (random sampling)
  - Compliance checks against CSC rules, BARMM policies, and internal procedures
  - Time and attendance monitoring
- Grievance and appeals process: how staff can raise concerns about supervisory conduct
- Whistleblower protection: mechanisms for reporting misconduct without retaliation
- Progressive discipline framework: verbal warning, written warning, suspension, dismissal (aligned with CSC Revised Rules on Administrative Cases)
- Documentation requirements: what records must be maintained and for how long

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Are reporting requirements proportionate (not creating excessive paperwork)?
- Is there a functional grievance mechanism that staff trust and use?
- Does the progressive discipline framework comply with CSC rules and due process requirements?

---

## SV10: Continuous Improvement

**CSW Section Objective**: Define how the supervision system itself is reviewed, updated, and improved over time.

**CSW Guide / Notes for this Section**:
- Establish a review schedule:
  - Semi-annual: operational review of supervision practices (what is working, what is not)
  - Annual: comprehensive review of the entire supervision system document
  - Triggered: immediate review after significant organizational changes (restructuring, new mandate, leadership change)
- Review inputs: staff satisfaction surveys, performance data trends, exit interview findings, audit results
- Amendment procedures: who can propose changes, review process, approval authority, version control
- Benchmarking: compare against CSC standards, other BARMM entities, and recognized best practices
- Feedback loop: how findings from Steps 6-7 of ADDRESS IT (Sustain and Iterate) feed back into supervision system improvements
- Document revision history: track all changes with dates, authors, and rationale

**Review Questions for this Section**:
- Is there a named owner responsible for maintaining and updating the supervision system?
- Are review cycles actually happening, or do they exist only on paper?
- Does the improvement process incorporate input from all organizational levels, not just management?

## How SV Maps to ADDRESS IT

| ADDRESS IT Step | SV Section(s) |
|---|---|
| 1. Analyze Context | SV01 (Organization Profile), SV02 (Framework) |
| 2. Define Problem | SV03 (Roles), SV06 (Delegation) |
| 3. Research | SV05 (Performance Management), SV08 (Staff Development) |
| 4. Explore Options | SV04 (CSW Integration), SV07 (Feedback/Coaching) |
| 5. Submit Report | Full SV document as the deliverable |
| 6. Sustain Implementation | SV09 (Accountability/Compliance) |
| 7. Iterate & Improve | SV10 (Continuous Improvement) |
