# Competencies of Bangsamoro Parliament Staff

Comprehensive competency framework for all staff working in an MP's office in the Bangsamoro Parliament. Created by Saidamen R. Mambayao. This framework serves as a reference model for competency development across BARMM — adaptable from Parliament to MOAs.

**Source**: `PDFs/COMPETENCIES OF BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT STAFF.pdf` (20 pages, ©SRM)

**Rubric scale**: 4 levels — Needs Improvement, Developing, Proficient, Exemplary

---

## Three-Tier Structure

### A. Core Competencies (All Staff)

Required for any role within an MP's office. These are the baseline.

| # | Competency | Definition | CSW/Supervision Mapping |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Critical, Creative, and Strategic Thinking** | Approach issues analytically, propose innovative solutions aligned with Bangsamoro values, formulate strategies | Maps to ACSCCRaPATFEE #1 (Analytical), #2 (Creativity), #3 (Strategic) |
| 2 | **Effective Communication** | Clearly convey ideas verbally and in writing, adapt style to audiences including diverse Bangsamoro communities | Maps to ACSCCRaPATFEE #4 (Communication) |
| 3 | **Synergistic Collaboration** | Work cooperatively with stakeholders inside and outside the Parliament, foster teamwork, build partnerships | Maps to ACSCCRaPATFEE #5 (Collaboration) |
| 4 | **Complete Staff Work (CSW)** | Take ownership of tasks, research thoroughly, prepare comprehensive documents and reports, offer well-thought-out recommendations | Maps directly to ADDRESS IT process; ACSCCRaPATFEE #6-8 |
| 5 | **Public Service and Cultural Competence** | Deep commitment to serving Bangsamoro people with dedication and integrity; adherence to moral governance; awareness of identity, culture, history, struggle | Maps to ACSCCRaPATFEE #12 (Ethics) + Bangsamoro-specific cultural dimension |

### B. Leadership Competencies (Supervisory Roles)

For individuals in supervisory or leading positions. Maps directly to `/supervision` skill.

| # | Competency | Definition | Supervision Mapping |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Planning for Organizational and Systems Change** | Anticipate change, assess needs, develop strategic and operational plans | Supervisory Competency #1 (Foundations) + #10 (Managing Change) |
| 2 | **Building High-Trust Organizational Culture** | Create respectful, inclusive, quality workplace; ensure accountability | Supervisory Competency #9 (Leading Teams) + #12 (Accountability) |
| 3 | **Supervising Disciplined Execution and Managing for Results** | Set clear expectations, provide regular feedback, motivate staff, monitor progress | Supervisory Competency #3 (Feedback) + #5 (Goals) + #6 (Check-Ins) |
| 4 | **Developing High-Performance Teams** | Assemble diverse teams, foster relationships, provide resources, support development | Supervisory Competency #4 (Coaching) + #8 (Talent Development) |
| 5 | **Leading and Influencing Change** | Inspire and guide others through change; communicate "why" in culturally resonant ways; nurture resilience | Supervisory Competency #10 (Managing Change) + #11 (Delegation) |

### C. Functional Competencies (Role-Specific)

Specialized knowledge per role type. Three role categories with 5 competencies each.

#### For Political Affairs Officers (PAOs)
1. Stakeholder Engagement, Community Organizing, and Constituency Building
2. Representation Skills
3. Social Marketing, Advocacy, and Lobbying
4. Project, Program, and Events Management
5. Monitoring, Reporting, and Evaluation

#### For Legislative Staff Officers (LSOs)
1. Investigation and Research Skills
2. Policy Making, Analysis, and Evaluation
3. Legal and Legislative Competence
4. Attention to Details and Quality
5. Oversight Skills

#### For Public Information and Media Affairs Officers (PIMAOs)
1. Strategic Communications
2. Documentation and Editing Skills
3. Social Media Skills
4. Print and Digital Media Skills
5. Media Handling Skills

## How to Use This Reference

- **For `/csw`**: When training Parliament staff on CSW, use Core Competency #4 (CSW) as the target. The rubric defines what "Needs Improvement" through "Exemplary" CSW looks like at the Parliament level. Cross-reference with ACSCCRaPATFEE for detailed sub-competency assessment.
- **For `/supervision`**: When designing supervision systems for Parliament offices, use Leadership Competencies B.1-B.5 as the supervisory competency standard. These are the Parliament-specific expression of the generic 12 Supervisory Competencies from MDN.
- **For `/training-assistant`**: When designing staff development programs, use the Functional Competencies (C) to tailor training by role type — PAOs need different skills from LSOs and PIMAOs.
- **For MOA adaptation**: Replace "Parliament office" with "Ministry/Office", "MP" with "Minister/Director", and adapt Functional Competencies to the MOA's specific role types.
