# CSW Practitioner Competencies — ACSCCRaPATFEE Framework

The 12 competencies of a CSW practitioner, based on the ADDRESS IT 7-step process.
Originally developed for LGU training (Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur, April 2024),
updated for universal BARMM application (Parliament, MOAs, LGUs).

**Author**: Saidamen R. Mambayao

## How to Use This Framework

- **For training**: Use indicators as learning objectives, rubrics as assessment tools
- **For CSW quality review**: Map competency gaps to specific ADDRESS IT step failures
- **For staff development**: Identify which competencies need strengthening for each staff member
- **For training design**: Feed into /training-assistant when designing CSW training packages

## 1. Analytical and Critical Thinking (A)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 1 (Analyze Context), 3 (Research)

Evaluate information and data to make informed decisions. Identify patterns, draw logical conclusions, and make evidence-based decisions within the BARMM entity's mandate and operating context.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Presents info clearly; identifies key issues; draws logical conclusions; recognizes patterns |
| **Intermediate** | Analyzes complex issues and root causes; synthesizes from multiple sources; develops evidence-based recommendations |
| **Complex** | Conducts comprehensive research on multifaceted issues; develops innovative solutions; builds analytical capacity in others |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Struggles to identify key issues; analysis is superficial; rarely proposes solutions |
| **Developing** | Identifies basic issues but struggles with complexity; recognizes obvious patterns |
| **Proficient** | Consistently analyzes complex issues, identifies root causes, presents findings clearly |
| **Advanced** | Recognized expert; conducts comprehensive research; develops innovative solutions with long-term impact |
| **Expert** | Visionary leader in analytical thinking; develops new frameworks and methodologies; thought leader |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 1 (Analyze Context)**: Core competency — examining internal/external factors, stakeholder analysis, and PESTEL assessment all require strong analytical thinking
- **Step 3 (Research)**: Evaluating source credibility, synthesizing findings, and identifying information gaps

## 2. Creativity and Innovation (C)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 4 (Explore Options)

Generate novel ideas, approaches, and solutions. Challenge conventional thinking and propose creative alternatives that serve the BARMM entity's goals.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Proposes new ideas; shows willingness to try different approaches; adapts existing solutions |
| **Intermediate** | Develops creative solutions to complex problems; introduces process improvements; applies innovative tools |
| **Complex** | Drives systemic innovation across the organization; creates new frameworks; mentors others in creative problem-solving |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Relies solely on established methods; resistant to new approaches; avoids experimentation |
| **Developing** | Shows occasional creativity but defaults to conventional solutions; open to others' innovations |
| **Proficient** | Regularly generates and implements creative solutions; balances innovation with practical constraints |
| **Advanced** | Champions innovation; develops original methodologies that improve organizational effectiveness |
| **Expert** | Recognized innovator; creates paradigm-shifting solutions; builds a culture of innovation across the entity |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 4 (Explore Options)**: Primary competency — generating alternative solutions, conducting comparative analysis, and proposing non-obvious approaches

## 3. Strategic Thinking (S)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 1 (Analyze), 4 (Explore), 7 (Iterate)

See the big picture and align CSW outputs with long-term organizational goals, the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP), and BOL mandates. Anticipate consequences and plan accordingly.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Understands organizational goals; aligns work with office mandate; considers short-term impacts |
| **Intermediate** | Connects work to BDP priorities and BOL mandates; anticipates second-order effects; plans for multiple scenarios |
| **Complex** | Shapes organizational strategy; integrates cross-cutting concerns (gender, IP, peace); drives long-term institutional reform |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Focuses only on immediate tasks; fails to connect work to broader goals or mandates |
| **Developing** | Understands organizational direction but struggles to align daily work with strategic priorities |
| **Proficient** | Consistently links CSW outputs to BDP goals and BOL mandates; anticipates downstream effects |
| **Advanced** | Shapes strategic direction; identifies emerging opportunities and threats; develops multi-year roadmaps |
| **Expert** | Visionary strategist; influences BARMM-wide policy direction; builds strategic capacity across entities |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 1 (Analyze Context)**: Strategic assessment of internal/external factors and stakeholder landscape
- **Step 4 (Explore Options)**: Evaluating options against long-term strategic goals, not just immediate needs
- **Step 7 (Iterate)**: Using feedback loops and performance data to refine strategies over time

## 4. Communication (C)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 5 (Submit), 6 (Sustain)

Convey information clearly and persuasively — written, verbal, and visual. Tailor messaging to the audience, whether Parliament members, ministry executives, LGU officials, or community stakeholders.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Writes clear memos and reports; speaks confidently in meetings; uses appropriate tone for audience |
| **Intermediate** | Drafts policy papers and briefers; presents complex findings accessibly; facilitates group discussions |
| **Complex** | Crafts strategic communications for high-level decision-makers; mediates between competing stakeholders; coaches others in communication |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Written work is unclear or disorganized; struggles to articulate ideas verbally; misjudges audience |
| **Developing** | Communicates adequately in routine situations but struggles with complex or sensitive topics |
| **Proficient** | Produces clear, well-structured documents; presents confidently; adapts style to different audiences |
| **Advanced** | Exceptional communicator; persuades at senior levels; produces publication-quality documents |
| **Expert** | Master communicator; shapes organizational voice and standards; trains others in strategic communication |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 5 (Submit)**: Core competency — the CSW output must be clear, complete, and decision-ready for the superior
- **Step 6 (Sustain)**: Communicating implementation progress, coordinating with stakeholders, reporting on outcomes

## 5. Collaboration and Teamwork (C)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 2 (Deliberate), 6 (Sustain)

Work effectively with others across offices, ministries, and external stakeholders. Build consensus, share knowledge, and contribute to collective goals within the BARMM governance ecosystem.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Works cooperatively with colleagues; shares information willingly; respects diverse viewpoints |
| **Intermediate** | Coordinates across offices and ministries; resolves team conflicts; facilitates inter-agency cooperation |
| **Complex** | Builds and leads cross-functional teams; establishes inter-entity partnerships; creates collaborative frameworks |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Works in isolation; reluctant to share information; creates friction in team settings |
| **Developing** | Cooperates when directed but does not proactively seek collaboration; limited cross-office engagement |
| **Proficient** | Actively collaborates across units; contributes to team goals; builds productive working relationships |
| **Advanced** | Facilitates complex multi-stakeholder processes; resolves inter-agency conflicts; builds lasting partnerships |
| **Expert** | Creates collaborative ecosystems across BARMM entities; institutionalizes knowledge-sharing mechanisms |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 2 (Define and Deliberate)**: Deliberating with stakeholders to define the problem correctly requires strong collaboration
- **Step 6 (Sustain)**: Implementation always involves coordination with multiple offices, agencies, and external partners

## 6. Research and Analysis (Ra)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 3 (Research)

Gather, evaluate, and synthesize information from diverse sources — legal, empirical, institutional, and community-based. Distinguish credible evidence from opinion and apply findings to CSW outputs.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Locates relevant laws, policies, and data; uses basic research tools; organizes findings logically |
| **Intermediate** | Conducts literature reviews; triangulates sources; applies research methodologies; identifies data gaps |
| **Complex** | Designs original research; conducts field studies; develops evidence-based policy recommendations; publishes findings |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Relies on single sources; does not verify information; research is incomplete or biased |
| **Developing** | Gathers information but lacks systematic approach; struggles to evaluate source credibility |
| **Proficient** | Conducts thorough, systematic research; triangulates sources; presents findings with proper attribution |
| **Advanced** | Expert researcher; designs studies; produces evidence that shapes organizational decisions |
| **Expert** | Thought leader in research methodology; builds research capacity across the entity; contributes to BARMM knowledge base |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 3 (Research)**: This is the primary step — gathering needed information, reviewing laws and policies, consulting experts, and building the evidence base for the CSW

## 7. Problem-Solving and Decision Making (P)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 2 (Define), 4 (Explore)

Identify problems accurately, generate viable solutions, evaluate trade-offs, and recommend the best course of action. Make sound decisions under uncertainty and time pressure.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Identifies straightforward problems; proposes workable solutions; follows decision-making frameworks |
| **Intermediate** | Diagnoses complex, multi-causal problems; develops decision matrices; weighs risks and benefits systematically |
| **Complex** | Resolves systemic organizational problems; makes high-stakes decisions; builds decision-making capacity in others |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Fails to identify root causes; proposes superficial fixes; avoids making decisions |
| **Developing** | Identifies problems but struggles with complex causation; needs guidance on solution selection |
| **Proficient** | Accurately diagnoses problems, generates multiple solutions, and recommends the best option with clear rationale |
| **Advanced** | Resolves complex, cross-cutting problems; makes sound decisions under uncertainty; mentors others in problem-solving |
| **Expert** | Solves unprecedented organizational challenges; develops new problem-solving methodologies; trusted advisor on critical decisions |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 2 (Define and Deliberate)**: Accurately defining the problem is the foundation — a misdiagnosed problem leads to wasted CSW effort
- **Step 4 (Explore Options)**: Generating, evaluating, and selecting from multiple alternatives

## 8. Attention to Details and Quality (A)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 5 (Submit), Phase 4 (Quality Pipeline)

Ensure accuracy, completeness, and professional quality in all CSW outputs. Catch errors before the decision-maker sees them. Uphold the "completed" standard — the superior should only need to approve or disapprove.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Checks work for errors; follows formatting standards; ensures completeness of required elements |
| **Intermediate** | Applies quality checklists; cross-references data; ensures legal citations are accurate; maintains version control |
| **Complex** | Designs quality assurance processes; establishes organizational standards; conducts peer review programs |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Submits work with frequent errors; ignores formatting standards; misses required elements |
| **Developing** | Catches obvious errors but misses subtle issues; inconsistent quality across outputs |
| **Proficient** | Produces consistently accurate, well-formatted work; uses checklists; catches errors before submission |
| **Advanced** | Sets quality benchmarks; develops QA processes; produces outputs that serve as organizational templates |
| **Expert** | Defines quality standards for the entity; builds quality culture; outputs are consistently publication-ready |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 5 (Submit)**: The CSW output must be decision-ready — every detail checked, every citation verified, every formatting standard met
- **Phase 4 (Quality Pipeline)**: The self-review, peer review, and supervisor review stages all depend on this competency

## 9. Time and Attention Management (T)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: All steps (cross-cutting)

Manage time, priorities, and attention effectively across multiple CSW assignments. Meet deadlines without sacrificing quality. Allocate appropriate effort to each ADDRESS IT step.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Meets routine deadlines; prioritizes tasks; manages daily workload; avoids unnecessary delays |
| **Intermediate** | Manages multiple concurrent CSW projects; sets milestones; delegates effectively; handles competing priorities |
| **Complex** | Designs workflow systems; optimizes organizational time allocation; manages entity-wide project portfolios |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Frequently misses deadlines; poor prioritization; overwhelmed by multiple assignments |
| **Developing** | Meets deadlines for simple tasks but struggles with complex or concurrent projects |
| **Proficient** | Consistently meets deadlines; manages multiple projects; allocates appropriate time to each ADDRESS IT step |
| **Advanced** | Optimizes team productivity; anticipates bottlenecks; designs efficient workflows for complex CSW processes |
| **Expert** | Transforms organizational time management; builds systems that ensure entity-wide CSW throughput and quality |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **All steps**: Time management is cross-cutting — every ADDRESS IT step requires appropriate time allocation. Spending too long on Step 3 (Research) can starve Step 4 (Explore) and Step 5 (Submit)

## 10. Flexibility and Adaptability (F)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 7 (Iterate), 6 (Sustain)

Adjust approaches when circumstances change, new information emerges, or initial plans prove inadequate. Embrace the iterative nature of CSW — the first draft is rarely the final product.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Accepts feedback constructively; adjusts work based on new information; handles routine changes calmly |
| **Intermediate** | Pivots strategy when evidence warrants; manages ambiguity; adapts CSW outputs to changing political or policy contexts |
| **Complex** | Leads organizational change; transforms processes in response to systemic shifts; builds adaptive capacity in teams |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Resistant to feedback; rigid in approach; struggles when plans change |
| **Developing** | Accepts change when directed but does not proactively adapt; slow to incorporate feedback |
| **Proficient** | Embraces feedback; adjusts approaches based on new information; handles ambiguity effectively |
| **Advanced** | Thrives in dynamic environments; leads teams through change; turns disruptions into opportunities |
| **Expert** | Architect of adaptive organizations; builds resilience into BARMM systems and processes |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 7 (Iterate)**: Core competency — iterating requires willingness to revisit and revise based on implementation feedback
- **Step 6 (Sustain)**: Implementation rarely goes exactly as planned; adaptability ensures sustained progress despite obstacles

## 11. Emotional Intelligence and Accountability (E)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: 2 (Deliberate), 6 (Sustain)

Manage emotions, build relationships, and take ownership of outcomes. Understand the human dynamics of governance — CSW is ultimately about people making decisions that affect communities.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Manages own emotions professionally; takes responsibility for own work; shows empathy to colleagues and stakeholders |
| **Intermediate** | Navigates interpersonal conflicts; holds self and team accountable; reads organizational dynamics perceptively |
| **Complex** | Builds emotionally intelligent teams; creates accountability systems; influences organizational culture toward ownership |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Reacts emotionally under pressure; deflects blame; lacks awareness of how behavior affects others |
| **Developing** | Generally professional but struggles under high stress; inconsistent accountability |
| **Proficient** | Manages emotions effectively; takes full ownership of work quality; builds trust through reliability |
| **Advanced** | Emotionally intelligent leader; holds teams accountable constructively; navigates sensitive political dynamics |
| **Expert** | Shapes organizational culture of accountability and emotional maturity; trusted mediator in high-stakes situations |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **Step 2 (Define and Deliberate)**: Deliberation with stakeholders requires emotional intelligence — understanding concerns, managing disagreements, building consensus
- **Step 6 (Sustain)**: Sustained implementation requires accountability — following through, reporting honestly, owning both successes and failures

## 12. Ethics and Integrity (E)

**ADDRESS IT Steps**: All steps (cross-cutting)

Uphold the highest ethical standards in all CSW work. Ensure honesty, transparency, and compliance with laws and organizational policies. CSW that lacks integrity is worse than no CSW at all.

### Indicators

| Level | Key Behaviors |
|---|---|
| **Simple** | Acts honestly; follows ethical guidelines; declares conflicts of interest; maintains confidentiality |
| **Intermediate** | Navigates ethical dilemmas; ensures compliance with BOL and BAAs; promotes transparency in processes |
| **Complex** | Shapes ethical frameworks for the organization; leads anti-corruption initiatives; serves as moral compass for the entity |

### Assessment Rubric

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Cuts ethical corners; fails to declare conflicts; compromises integrity under pressure |
| **Developing** | Generally ethical but avoids confronting ethical issues in others; passive compliance |
| **Proficient** | Consistently ethical; proactively identifies and addresses ethical issues; ensures compliance |
| **Advanced** | Ethical role model; builds integrity systems; confronts unethical behavior constructively |
| **Expert** | Defines and champions ethical standards across BARMM; shapes anti-corruption policy; trusted custodian of public trust |

### ADDRESS IT Mapping

- **All steps**: Ethics is cross-cutting — from honest context analysis (Step 1) to transparent research (Step 3) to truthful reporting (Step 5) to accountable implementation (Step 6)
