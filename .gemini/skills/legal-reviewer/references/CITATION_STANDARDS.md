# Philippine Legal Citation Standards

Standards for verifying and formatting legal citations in Bangsamoro parliamentary resolutions based on Philippine Manual of Legal Citations and the Bluebook.

## 1. Constitutional Citations

### Standard Format for Constitutional Provisions

**Text Format (in WHEREAS clauses):**
```
[Law], [Article/Section], [provides/states/provides for]: "[Exact quoted provision]"
```

**Example - CORRECT:**
```
The 1987 Philippine Constitution, Article X, Section 15, provides: "There shall be created autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and in the Cordilleras consisting of provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical areas sharing common and distinctive historical and cultural heritage..."
```

**Footnote Format:**
```
CONST. (Phil. 1987) art. [Roman #], § [number], verified against Official Gazette Constitutional Text Database ([Date])
```

**Example Footnote - CORRECT:**
```
CONST. (Phil. 1987) art. X, § 15, verified against Official Gazette Constitutional Text Database (Nov. 3, 2024)
```

### Constitutional Citation Components to Verify

When verifying a Constitutional citation:

1. **Article Number** - Verify correct Roman numeral (I, II, III, etc.)
   - Article X = Autonomous Regions (correct for BARMM matters)

2. **Section Number** - Verify section number is correct
   - Section 15 is about autonomous regions
   - Section 18 is about effectivity of autonomous region provisions

3. **Quoted Text** - Must match official Constitution word-for-word
   - Compare against Official Gazette official text
   - Check punctuation, capitalization, spacing

4. **Current Status** - Verify provision not amended or superseded
   - Most constitutional provisions are permanent
   - Some have been amended (rare)

### Constitutional Citation Errors to Watch For

| Error Type | Example (WRONG) | Correct Version |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong Article | Article VI (Legislative) | Article X (Autonomous Regions) |
| Wrong Section | Section 18 (not about creation) | Section 15 (about creation) |
| Misquoted Text | "autonomous regions shall be created" | "There shall be created autonomous regions" |
| Incomplete reference | Article X (no section) | Article X, Section 15 |

## 2. Supreme Court Case Citations

### Standard Format for Supreme Court Cases

**MANDATORY REQUIREMENT: All Supreme Court citations MUST include G.R. numbers**

**Text Format (in WHEREAS clauses):**
```
[Case Name], G.R. No./Nos. [number(s)], [Month Date, Year], [Court if not SC], [SCRA ref if available]: "[Key holding or fact]"
```

**Example - CORRECT:**
```
In Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary, G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693, Supreme Court of the Philippines (September 3, 2024), the Court upheld the validity of the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the constitutional authority of autonomous regions to exercise legislative powers within their competencies.
```

**Footnote Format:**
```
[Case Name], G.R. No. [number], [Date], [Vol.] SCRA [page], verified against Supreme Court Online Database ([Access Date])
```

**Example Footnote - CORRECT:**
```
Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary, G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693, Supreme Court of the Philippines (Sept. 3, 2024), verified against Supreme Court Online Database (Nov. 3, 2024)
```

### G.R. Number Format and Examples

**Single Case with One G.R. Number:**
```
G.R. No. L-36142 (old numbering system)
G.R. No. 123456 (modern numbering system)
```

**Single Case with Multiple G.R. Numbers (consolidated cases):**
```
G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693 (multiple consolidated cases)
```

**Verification Requirements:**
- G.R. number must exist in Supreme Court database
- Date must correspond to G.R. number in database
- Case name must match exactly (allow for "et al." if multiple parties)
- L-prefix used for pre-1971 cases, dropped for post-1971 cases

### Components of Supreme Court Citations to Verify

1. **Case Name** - Exact parties to the case
   - Format: Petitioner v. Respondent
   - Example: "Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary" (not "Sulu Province v. Secretary" - wrong formatting)

2. **G.R. Number(s)** - **MANDATORY**
   - Format: G.R. No. [number] or G.R. Nos. [number], [number], [number]
   - All Supreme Court cases MUST have G.R. number(s)
   - Must be verifiable in official SC database

3. **Decision Date** - Format: Month Date, Year
   - Example: "September 3, 2024" (not "2024-09-03")
   - Example: "March 31, 1973" (older cases use short year format)

4. **SCRA Reference** (optional but preferred if available)
   - Format: [Volume] SCRA [page]
   - Example: "50 SCRA 30" (case is in volume 50, starting at page 30)
   - Use specific page number for specific holding cited

5. **Court Reference** - Usually "Supreme Court of the Philippines"
   - May specify "Supreme Court" if obvious from context
   - Do NOT cite lower court decisions unless explicitly from relevant court

### Supreme Court Citation Errors to Watch For

| Error Type | Example (WRONG) | Correct Version |
|---|---|---|
| Missing G.R. number | Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary (2024) | G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693 (Sept. 3, 2024) |
| Wrong G.R. number | G.R. No. 242255 (incomplete) | G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693 |
| Case name variation | "Sulu Province v. Secretary" | "Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary" |
| Wrong decision date | "July 2024" | "September 3, 2024" |
| SCRA page wrong | "50 SCRA 50" (wrong page) | "50 SCRA 30" (correct page) |
| Misquoted holding | Paraphrased text in quotes | Exact quote from official case text |

## 3. Republic Act Citations

### Standard Format for Republic Acts (National Laws)

**Text Format (in WHEREAS clauses):**
```
[Law Name], [Act number with year], [Article/Section if cited], [provides/states]: "[Exact quoted provision if applicable]"
```

**Example - CORRECT:**
```
Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law), Article VII, Section 3, provides that the Parliament "shall have the authority to enact laws on matters that are within the powers and competencies of the Bangsamoro Government"
```

**Footnote Format:**
```
[Law Short Name], Rep. Act No. [number], art. [#], § [#] ([Year]) verified against Official Legislative Database ([Access Date])
```

**Example Footnote - CORRECT:**
```
Bangsamoro Organic Law, Rep. Act No. 11054, art. VII, § 3 (July 26, 2018) verified against Senate Legislative Database (Nov. 3, 2024)
```

### Republic Act Citation Components to Verify

1. **Republic Act Number**
   - Format: "Republic Act No." or "Rep. Act No." followed by number
   - Example: Rep. Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law)
   - Verify number exists in legislative database

2. **Law Name** - Popular name of the law (optional in formal citation but recommended for clarity)
   - Example: "Bangsamoro Organic Law"
   - Must match official law name

3. **Year of Enactment** - Usually placed in parentheses at end
   - Example: "(July 26, 2018)" or "(2018)"
   - Verify year matches official enactment date

4. **Article/Section Reference** - If citing specific provision
   - Format: "Article [Roman numeral], Section [number]"
   - Example: "Article VII, Section 3"
   - Verify article and section exist and are correctly numbered

5. **Quoted Text** - If law is quoted
   - Must match official legislative text exactly
   - Verify against official version on lawphil.net or Official Gazette

### Republic Act Citation Examples

**BOL Citations (Most Common in BARMM Resolutions):**

CORRECT:
- Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law), Article X, Section 1
- Rep. Act No. 11054, art. VII, § 3 (2018)
- "Bangsamoro Organic Law, Rep. Act No. 11054, Article VII, Section 3, provides..."

INCORRECT:
- "RA 11054" (abbreviation not used in formal citations)
- "Republic Act 11054" (missing "No.")
- "Article 7, Section 3" (section numbers, not spelled out - use numerals)
- "Bangsamoro Organic Law 2018" (missing "Rep. Act No. 11054")

**Extension of Transition Period Citation:**

CORRECT:
- Republic Act No. 11593 (Extension of Transition Period of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region)
- Rep. Act No. 11593 (2020)

**Corrective Measures for Bangsamoro Autonomy Act:**

CORRECT:
- Republic Act No. 11711 (Corrective Measures for the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act)

### Republic Act Citation Errors to Watch For

| Error Type | Example (WRONG) | Correct Version |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong number | Rep. Act No. 11056 | Rep. Act No. 11054 (BOL) |
| Missing "No." | Republic Act 11054 | Republic Act No. 11054 |
| Wrong section | Section 2 of Article X | Section 1 of Article X |
| Law name wrong | "Bangsamoro Autonomy Law" | "Bangsamoro Organic Law" |
| Year wrong | (2019) | (July 26, 2018) |
| Quoted text mismatch | Paraphrased text in quotes | Exact text from official law |

## 4. Bangsamoro Autonomy Act Citations

### Standard Format for Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts

**Text Format (in WHEREAS clauses):**
```
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. [number] ([Descriptive Title]), [Section/Article reference], [provides/establishes]: "[Exact text if quoted]"
```

**Example - CORRECT:**
```
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code), Section 5, establishes the internal governance structure of the Bangsamoro Parliament and its supporting institutions
```

**Footnote Format:**
```
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. [#], § [#] ([Year]) verified against Official Bangsamoro Gazette ([Access Date])
```

**Example Footnote - CORRECT:**
```
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code), § 5 (2020) verified against Official Bangsamoro Gazette (Nov. 3, 2024)
```

### Bangsamoro Autonomy Act Citation Components to Verify

1. **Autonomy Act Number**
   - Format: "Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No." followed by number
   - Example: "Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13"
   - Verify number exists and is current (not superseded)

2. **Act Name** - Descriptive title of the autonomy act
   - Example: "Bangsamoro Administrative Code"
   - Must match official name from Official Bangsamoro Gazette

3. **Section Reference** - Specific provision cited
   - Example: "Section 5"
   - Format: "Section [number]" or "§ [number]"
   - Verify section exists in the act

4. **Year of Enactment** - When autonomy act became effective
   - Usually placed in parentheses
   - Verify against Official Bangsamoro Gazette records

5. **Quoted Text** - If provision is quoted
   - Must match official autonomy act text exactly
   - Verify against Official Bangsamoro Gazette

### Key Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts for Reference

| Act # | Official Name | Year | Key Areas |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bangsamoro Executive Code | 2020 | Executive structure |
| 2 | Bangsamoro Public Financial Management Code | 2020 | Budget and finance |
| 3 | Bangsamoro Fiscal Code | 2020 | Revenue and taxation |
| 4 | Bangsamoro Agricultural Code | 2020 | Agriculture |
| 5 | Bangsamoro Aquatic Resources Code | 2020 | Fisheries |
| 6 | Bangsamoro Forestry Code | 2020 | Forests |
| 7 | Bangsamoro Public Works Code | 2020 | Infrastructure |
| 8 | Bangsamoro Commerce Code | 2020 | Business/trade |
| 13 | Bangsamoro Administrative Code | 2020 | Parliament/Admin |

### Bangsamoro Autonomy Act Verification Requirements

1. **Act Must Be Authorized by BOL** - Verify the autonomy act is within Parliament's authority per Bangsamoro Organic Law
2. **Act Must Be Current** - Verify act has not been superseded, repealed, or substantially amended
3. **Sections Must Exist** - Verify cited sections are in the official text
4. **BOL Compliance** - Confirm act does not conflict with BOL provisions
5. **Official Source** - Verify against Official Bangsamoro Gazette, not secondary sources

## 5. Common Citation Errors and How to Fix Them

### Error: Missing G.R. Numbers in Supreme Court Cases

**WRONG:**
```
Supreme Court jurisprudence on autonomous regions' legislative authority
```

**CORRECT:**
```
In Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary, G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693, Supreme Court of the Philippines (September 3, 2024), the Court upheld the validity of the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the constitutional authority of autonomous regions to exercise legislative powers within their competencies
```

### Error: Paraphrased Text Presented as Quotation

**WRONG:**
```
"grants constitutional autonomy to autonomous regions"
```

**CORRECT:**
```
"There shall be created autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and in the Cordilleras..."
```

### Error: Incomplete Statutory References

**WRONG:**
```
The Bangsamoro Organic Law provides authority for the Parliament
```

**CORRECT:**
```
Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law), Article VII, Section 3, provides that the Parliament "shall have the authority to enact laws on matters that are within the powers and competencies of the Bangsamoro Government"
```

### Error: Unverifiable Authority References

**WRONG:**
```
Bangsamoro jurisprudence supports the need for Islamic law expertise
```

**CORRECT:**
```
The Bangsamoro Justice Framework development initiative (held July 2023), convened by Bangsamoro law experts and Shari'ah scholars, identified the critical need for stronger institutional capacity in legislative research on Islamic law
```

(If referencing a specific authority, cite it with exact document details and source)

## 6. Citation Verification Examples

### Example 1: Verifying a Constitutional Citation

**Citation as Written:**
```
The 1987 Philippine Constitution, Article X, Section 15, provides: "There shall be created autonomous regions..."
```

**Verification Steps:**
1. Confirm this is from Article X (yes - about autonomous regions)
2. Confirm Section 15 (yes - specifically about autonomous region creation)
3. Extract official Constitution text from Official Gazette
4. Compare: "There shall be created autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and in the Cordilleras consisting of provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical areas sharing common and distinctive historical and cultural heritage, economic and social structures, and other relevant characteristics within the framework of this Constitution and the national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines"
5. Verify quoted text matches official text exactly
6. **STATUS: VERIFIED** if match is exact; **ERROR** if any differences

### Example 2: Verifying a Supreme Court Case Citation

**Citation as Written:**
```
Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary, G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, 243693, Supreme Court of the Philippines (September 3, 2024)
```

**Verification Steps:**
1. Access Supreme Court Online Database
2. Search for case name: "Province of Sulu v. Executive Secretary"
3. Search for G.R. numbers: 242255, 243246, 243693
4. Confirm case exists with these G.R. numbers
5. Confirm decision date: September 3, 2024
6. Confirm Court: Supreme Court of the Philippines
7. **STATUS: VERIFIED** if all components match; **ERROR** if any mismatch

### Example 3: Verifying Quoted Statutory Text

**Quotation as Written:**
```
"shall have the authority to enact laws on matters that are within the powers and competencies of the Bangsamoro Government"
```

**Verification Steps:**
1. Access official legislative text of RA 11054 (from lawphil.net or Official Gazette)
2. Locate Article VII, Section 3
3. Extract exact text: "shall have the authority to enact laws on matters that are within the powers and competencies of the Bangsamoro Government"
4. Compare character-by-character with quoted text
5. Check: spelling, capitalization, punctuation, spacing all match exactly
6. **STATUS: VERIFIED** if exact match; **ERROR** if any character differs

---

**Remember:** When in doubt about a citation's correctness, consult official authoritative sources (Official Gazette for Constitution and national laws, Supreme Court Online Database for cases, Official Bangsamoro Gazette for autonomy acts). Never rely on secondary sources or paraphrases for citation verification.
