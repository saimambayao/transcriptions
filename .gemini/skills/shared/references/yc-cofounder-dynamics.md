## YC on Co-Founders

### The Most Important Decision

> "Choosing a co-founder is one of the most important decisions you'll make. It's like a marriage." — Sam Altman

**YC Statistics:**
- Most YC companies have 2-3 co-founders
- Solo founders can succeed but face harder path
- Co-founder conflict is a top 5 startup killer

## YC Equity Split Recommendation

### The YC Position: Equal Splits

> "YC strongly recommends equal equity splits." — YC Partner Advice

**Why Equal:**
| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Trust Signal** | Shows co-founders value each other equally |
| **Simplifies Decisions** | No "I own more" power dynamics |
| **Long-term Motivation** | Everyone stays fully invested |
| **Reduces Conflict** | Nothing to argue about |

### When Unequal Might Work

| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Significant time gap** | One founder worked 2+ years before other joined |
| **Major IP contribution** | One founder brings critical technology |
| **Capital contribution** | One founder funded entire seed stage |
| **Part-time vs full-time** | Different commitment levels |

**Even Then:** YC suggests difference should be small (55/45, not 70/30)

## Vesting Standards

### YC Standard: 4-Year Vesting, 1-Year Cliff

```
Year 0-1:    Nothing vests (cliff period)
Year 1:      25% vests immediately
Year 1-4:    Remaining 75% vests monthly (1/48 per month)
```

### Why Vesting Matters

| Scenario | Without Vesting | With Vesting |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Co-founder leaves after 6 months | Keeps 50% equity | Keeps 0% equity |
| Co-founder leaves after 2 years | Keeps 50% equity | Keeps 25% equity |
| Relationship protects | Nothing | Both founders |

### Founder-Friendly Modifications

- **Single-trigger acceleration** - Vesting accelerates on acquisition
- **Double-trigger acceleration** - Vesting accelerates if acquired AND founder is fired
- **Extended cliff for late joiners** - May justify shorter cliff for proven teams

## Founder Qualities YC Evaluates

### The YC Founder Framework

| Quality | Description | How YC Evaluates |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Determination** | Won't quit when things get hard | Past evidence of perseverance |
| **Flexibility** | Willing to change based on evidence | Openness to pivoting |
| **Imagination** | Can see what others miss | Novel insights about market |
| **Naughtiness** | Healthy disregard for rules | Willingness to break conventions |
| **Friendship** | Genuine bond between co-founders | How they interact in interview |

### Red Flags YC Watches For

| Red Flag | Why It's Concerning |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Can't explain each other's roles** | Unclear partnership |
| **One person dominates interview** | Power imbalance |
| **Avoiding eye contact** | Trust issues |
| **Contradicting each other** | Misalignment |
| **Met recently** | Relationship untested |

## Co-Founder Selection

### YC Advice on Finding Co-Founders

**Best Sources:**
1. Former co-workers (you know how they work)
2. College classmates (shared formative experience)
3. Long-time friends (deep trust built over years)
4. Prior collaborators (proven working relationship)

**Risky Sources:**
1. Meetups (strangers with unknown work styles)
2. Co-founder dating sites (optimized for matching, not working)
3. Recent acquaintances (relationship untested)

### What to Look For

| Factor | Questions to Ask |
|--------|------------------|
| **Complementary Skills** | Do we cover product, sales, and engineering? |
| **Shared Values** | Do we agree on what success looks like? |
| **Work Ethic Match** | Do we have similar intensity levels? |
| **Conflict Resolution** | Have we disagreed and resolved it? |
| **Communication Style** | Can we give and receive hard feedback? |

## Founder-Market Fit

### YC's Evaluation

> "We look for founders who are genuinely passionate about their problem and have unique insights into it."

| Question | Good Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Why you? | Personal experience with the problem |
| Why now? | Change in world that makes this possible |
| Why this? | Obsession with solving this specific problem |

### Types of Founder-Market Fit

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Industry Expert** | 10 years in healthcare, now building healthtech |
| **Problem Sufferer** | Experienced the pain directly |
| **Technology Pioneer** | Built core tech at previous company |
| **Market Insider** | Deep relationships in target industry |

## The Struggle (Ben Horowitz / YC-Aligned)

### What YC Knows About Founder Psychology

> "Every great company faces The Struggle. How you handle it determines if you survive."

**The Struggle Includes:**
- Running out of money
- Key employees quitting
- Product not working
- Competitors winning
- Co-founder conflict
- Personal sacrifices

### YC's Advice for Hard Times

| Situation | Advice |
|-----------|--------|
| **Low on cash** | Cut costs, extend runway, keep building |
| **Team leaving** | Let them go, focus on who stays |
| **Product failing** | Talk to users, pivot if needed |
| **Co-founder conflict** | Address immediately, don't let it fester |
| **Personal burnout** | Take a break, come back stronger |

## Co-Founder Conflict Resolution

### YC Partner Advice

**Prevention:**
1. **Define roles clearly** - Who decides what
2. **Regular check-ins** - Weekly honest conversations
3. **Written agreements** - Equity, vesting, responsibilities
4. **External advisor** - Neutral party for disputes

**Resolution:**
1. **Address immediately** - Don't let issues compound
2. **Assume good intent** - Most conflict is miscommunication
3. **Focus on company** - What's best for the startup
4. **Get help if needed** - Mediators, advisors, therapists

### When to Part Ways

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Values misalignment** | Discuss, attempt to reconcile |
| **Work ethic mismatch** | Set clear expectations |
| **Trust broken** | Very difficult to repair |
| **Vision conflict** | One founder may need to exit |

## YC Founder Responsibilities

### Typical Co-Founder Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **CEO** | Vision, fundraising, culture, external |
| **CTO** | Technology, engineering, product |
| **COO** | Operations, sales, customer success |

### How to Divide

**YC's Heuristic:**
- Someone must be CEO (external point person)
- Divide by function, not by equity
- Overlap is okay, gaps are not
- Revisit as company grows

## Decision-Making Frameworks

### When to Involve Co-Founder

| Decision Type | Process |
|---------------|---------|
| **Strategic** | Both founders, take time |
| **Hiring** | Both founders for early hires |
| **Spending** | Set thresholds ($X+ needs both) |
| **Day-to-day** | Whoever owns that function |
| **Pivots** | Always both founders |

### Breaking Ties

**Options:**
1. **CEO decides** - One person has final call
2. **Expertise wins** - Domain expert decides
3. **Test it** - Run experiment to get data
4. **Delay** - Wait for more information

## Equity Allocation Examples

### Two Equal Founders (YC Recommended)

```
Founder A: 50%
Founder B: 50%
Total: 100%

After 4-year vest with 1-year cliff:
- Year 0-1: Both at 0% vested
- Year 1: Both at 12.5% vested
- Year 4: Both at 50% vested
```

### Unequal with Justification

```
Founder A (1 year head start, $50K capital): 60%
Founder B (joining now, no capital): 40%
Total: 100%

Note: YC would likely suggest 55/45 instead
```

### With Employee Option Pool

```
Founder A: 40%
Founder B: 40%
Option Pool: 20%
Total: 100%
```

## Communication Frameworks

### Weekly Co-Founder Check-In (YC Practice)

**Agenda:**
1. **Wins** - What went well this week
2. **Challenges** - What's not working
3. **Alignment** - Are we on the same page on priorities
4. **Personal** - How are we doing individually

### Difficult Conversations

**Framework:**
1. **State facts** - What happened, without interpretation
2. **Share impact** - How it affected you/company
3. **Request** - What you need going forward
4. **Listen** - Hear their perspective
