## Purpose {#purpose}

Standardize and systematize speech writing. Ensure all essential elements are covered from initial planning to final delivery. Guide construction of speeches that are coherent, well-researched, and aligned with the speaker's legislative and development agenda.

## General CSW Guidelines

1. **Consistency** — adhere to this template for institutional memory and performance metrics
2. **Tailoring** — each speech must be tailored to context and audience
3. **Alignment** — ensure alignment with the speaker's legislative and development priorities
4. **Legal scrutiny** — all speeches must comply with existing laws including the BOL
5. **Accuracy** — verify all statistics, quotes, and historical references
6. **Tone** — formal, respectful, appropriate to the mandate and occasion
7. **Review** — rigorous review before finalization
8. **Rehearsal** — staff should rehearse the speech for familiarity with flow and content
9. **Archive** — add final versions to centralized archive
10. **Feedback** — collect post-event feedback for future improvement
11. **Quality** — facilitate high-quality, impactful speeches that convey mission, vision, and priorities

## 1. Title of Speech {#title}

**Objective:** Create a concise, impactful title that encapsulates the speech's essence.

**Guide:**
- Directly relevant to subject matter and event
- Brief — overly long titles dilute impact
- Consider audience sensibilities
- Tone congruent with event mood (formal for legislative, casual for community)
- Unique — distinguishes from other speeches at the same event
- Searchable if published online
- Free from offensive or copyrighted language

---

## 2. Event Name & Details {#event}

**Objective:** Unambiguously identify the event for context and archival.

**Guide:**
- Full official event name (no abbreviations unless widely recognized)
- If part of a series, specify which edition
- Local, national, or international scope
- Event format (conference, seminar, workshop, ceremony)
- Primary language — the speaker generally prefers **Tagalog**

---

## 3. Date, Time, Location {#datetime}

**Objective:** Precise logistics for preparations and context.

**Guide:**
- Exact date including day of week
- Start/end times + speaker's time slot
- Time zone for virtual/international events
- Full venue address with room/hall numbers
- Indoor/outdoor, capacity, features
- Accessibility considerations
- Virtual attendance details if applicable
- Weather contingency for outdoor events
- Transportation and parking
- Security protocols

---

## 4. Organizer {#organizer}

**Objective:** Identify organizing body for context and coordination.

**Guide:**
- Full official name (list co-organizers)
- Brief background — mission, why hosting
- Key contact persons with roles
- Prior interactions or collaborations
- Credibility/objectivity assessment
- Mission alignment with BTA and speaker's agenda
- Legal status confirmation
- Logistics role (facilities, equipment)
- Audience attraction method
- Verify all information

---

## 5. VIPs / Greet List {#vips}

**Objective:** Hierarchical list of dignitaries for proper acknowledgment.

**Guide:**
- Organized by **official rank** — crucial for diplomatic norms
- Inclusive — no important individual left out (perceived as a slight)
- Correct titles, honorifics, designations
- Affiliations where relevant
- Phonetic guidance for challenging names
- Dynamic — update for last-minute changes
- Verify accuracy of all names and titles

---

## 6. Describe the Audience {#audience}

**Objective:** Comprehensive audience understanding for tailoring content, tone, delivery.

**Guide:**
- Estimated size (affects tone — large = formal, small = conversational)
- Demographics: age, gender, occupation, education
- Attitudes, values, interests
- Key stakeholders present (politicians, activists, experts, community leaders)
- Audience expectations (information, inspiration, entertainment)
- Cultural, religious, regional sensitivities
- Previous interactions with this audience

---

## 7. Brief Description of Event {#event-description}

**Objective:** Concise yet comprehensive event description for context.

**Guide:**
- Primary purpose (inform, inspire, celebrate, raise awareness)
- Specific theme
- Organizer's objectives
- Key activities/segments (attach program flow)
- History of recurring events
- Speaker's past participation
- Notable sponsorships or endorsements

---

## 8. Relevance to BTA Mandate {#bta-relevance}

**Objective:** Align speech with BTA's legal and policy frameworks.

**Guide:**
- Alignment with **Bangsamoro Organic Law** (specify Articles/Sections)
- Intersection with **BARMM institutions** — specify ministries, agencies, offices with relevant mandate
- Relevance to **priority legislation**
- Connection to **Chief Minister's 12-Point Agenda**
- Link to **Bangsamoro Development Plan** goals
- Wider scope of BARMM responsibilities (national/international commitments)
- Strategic importance for the region

---

## 9. Relevance to Speaker's Agenda {#speaker-relevance}

**Objective:** Connect event to the speaker's legislative and development priorities.

**Guide:**
- **Sustained Peace** — how event advances peace initiatives
- **Effective Governance** — alignment with governance agenda (platforms for women, youth, PWDs, vulnerable sectors)
- **Empowered Constituencies** — connection to empowerment through entrepreneurship, education, social services
- **Alignment with Filed Legislations** — specific bills/resolutions the speaker has championed
- **Upcoming Measures** — potential legislative outputs from the event
- Strategic value (immediate and long-term)
- Focus on vulnerable groups (women, youth, PWDs) alignment

---

## 10. Introduction Notes {#introduction}

**Objective:** Engage audience from the outset, establish rapport, articulate purpose.

**Guide:**
- Impactful opening (striking fact, pertinent question, relevant quote)
- Acknowledgments following VIP/Greet List rank order
- Establish context early
- State purpose clearly (inform, persuade, celebrate)
- Note speaker's role (keynote, guest of honor, participant)
- Rhetorical devices for audience engagement
- Hint at main points (audience roadmap)

---

## 11. Context and Background {#context}

**Objective:** Essential information that sets the stage for main points.

**Guide:**
- Brief history of the issue
- Recent developments with verified data
- Tie to speaker's legislative priorities and BTA mandate
- Relevant laws including BOL
- Global/national trends
- Key stakeholders and their roles
- Obstacles or challenges (candor adds credibility)
- Upcoming events/milestones for urgency
- Verifiable data with cited sources
- Transition statement to next section

---

## 12. Key Points {#key-points}

**Objective:** Core arguments, proposals, or insights — the speech's centerpiece.

**Guide:**
- Logical order (chronological, thematic, or by importance)
- Align each point with speaker's legislative/development goals
- Support with statistics, research from credible institutions
- Real-life examples, case studies, constituent stories
- Persuasive language with rhetorical devices (analogies, metaphors)
- Reference existing/upcoming legislation
- Audience-centered framing
- Anticipate and address counter-arguments
- Specific call to action per key point
- Transitional statements between points

---

## 13. Impact Statements / Quotable Quotes {#quotes}

**Objective:** Memorable takeaways for audience and media, shareable on social media.

**Guide:**
- Correspond with key points
- Clear, concise, easily remembered (effective sound bites)
- Emotionally engaging — use alliteration, metaphor, parallel structure
- Authoritative external quotes that enhance credibility
- Original quotes attributable to the speaker
- Relevant to context, audience, and legislative agenda
- Proper attribution for external sources
- Audience-resonant
- Readable aloud with natural rhythm

**Rhetorical devices:**
- **Alliteration** — repetition of initial sounds for musical effect
- **Metaphor** — comparison of unlike things for deeper meaning
- **Parallel structure** — same grammatical pattern for balance and rhythm

---

## 14. Concluding Statement {#conclusion}

**Objective:** Compelling close that encapsulates key messages and leaves lasting impression.

**Guide:**
- Summarize main points
- Call to action aligned with speaker's agenda
- Revisit impact statements for reinforcement
- Align with legislative/development goals
- Personally engage audience
- Clear, concise, impactful sentences
- Professional tone appropriate to setting
- Glimpse into future initiatives
- Thank audience and organizers
- Graceful closing opening floor for Q&A or next segment

---

## 15. Q&A Section {#qa}

**Objective:** Prepare for audience questions with informative, aligned responses.

**Guide:**
- Anticipate questions based on speech content
- Responses consistent with legislative agenda
- Concise yet comprehensive — avoid jargon
- Interactive — ask for audience input
- Cite sources for statistics
- Time-conscious responses
- Diplomatic handling of sensitive/controversial issues
- Defer when appropriate
- Summarize key Q&A points and thank audience

---

## 16. Visual Aids {#visuals}

**Objective:** Plan visual aids to enhance impact, comprehension, and retention.

**Guide:**
- Directly relevant to topic/key points
- Professional appearance — clear, legible fonts
- Simplify complex topics (charts, graphs, infographics)
- Consistent design throughout
- Technical compatibility sorted in advance
- Contingency plan (printed backups)
- Proper citation for non-original images/data
- Practice with visual aids for seamless integration
- Accessible to all attendees including visually impaired
- Timing aligned with speech sections

---

## 17. Citations {#citations}

**Objective:** Comprehensive, accurate source list reinforcing credibility.

**Guide:**
- APA style preferred, consistent throughout
- Distinguish primary vs secondary sources
- No plagiarism — proper credit always
- Reputable, reliable, verifiable sources
- In-text citations for oral delivery (speaker cites sources aloud)
- Recent and relevant data
- Respect intellectual property
- Each citation maps to specific speech section

---

## 18. Clean Copy {#clean-copy}

**Objective:** Polished final version free from errors, ready for delivery.

**Guide:**
- Multiple proofreading rounds
- Consistent formatting (fonts, headers, bullets)
- Remove all internal notes/annotations
- Aligns with all preceding sections including citations
- Page numbers for navigation
- Subtle pacing/emphasis cues for delivery
- Rehearse to ensure flow and timing
- Handle confidential content carefully
- Multiple backup copies (print + digital)
- Final review and approval from the speaker

---

## 19. AI Usage Guidelines {#ai-guidelines}

**Objective:** Responsible use of AI in speech drafting, revising, reviewing, and finalizing.

**Guidelines:**
1. AI enhances speed and quality but requires human oversight
2. AI drafts must be reviewed and customized to the speaker's voice and stances
3. Strict confidentiality and data privacy protocols
4. Double-check all AI-suggested statistics, quotes, and references
5. Use AI for grammar/style checks appropriate to legislative context
6. Manual legal review still required for BOL compliance
7. AI helps maintain tone consistency; human judgment ensures appropriateness
8. Final draft always reviewed and approved by designated authorities
9. AI can generate visual aid ideas; professionals handle design
10. Use AI for practice run structuring; human judgment for final delivery
11. Employ AI plagiarism detection
12. AI can analyze post-speech feedback; complements human analysis
