# BTA Parliament Speech Structure

## Opening Protocol

### Islamic Greetings (Arabic)
Always begin with:
```
Bismillahir Rahmanir Raheem.
Assalāmu 'alaikum wa rahmatullāhi wa barakātuho.
```

### Formal Salutations (Tagalog)
Adapt names to current officeholders and the occasion:
```
Sa ating butihing Chief Minister [Name], sa ating mga Deputy Chief Ministers,
sa ating Parliament Speaker, [Name], sa mga Deputy Speakers, Floor Leader,
at Deputy Floor Leaders, pati na rin sa iba pang mga opisyales at kasamahan
sa BTA Parliament, mga panauhin, at mga tagasubaybay ng ating live sessions,
magandang [umaga/hapon/gabi] po sa ating lahat.
```

For non-parliamentary events, adapt the salutation:
- **Forums/Summits:** Address the host, keynote speakers, distinguished guests, then "mga kababayan"
- **Community events:** More informal — "Mga minamahal naming kababayan sa [place]"
- **Executive meetings:** Address the Chief Minister, ministers, then colleagues

## Speech Body

### Introduction
- State the purpose clearly in the first 2-3 sentences
- Introduce the bill, resolution, or topic being addressed
- Provide essential context the audience needs

### Main Body
- Connect to Bangsamoro themes: peace, self-governance, identity, inclusive development
- Include personal stories, constituent testimonials, or historical references
- Cite specific data, laws, or provisions to ground arguments
- Tie everything to the speaker's legislative priorities and BARMM's mandate

### Call to Action
- Direct and specific — what do you want the audience to do?
- Frame in terms of collective Bangsamoro benefit, not individual gain
- Use inclusive language: "tayo", "atin", "sama-sama natin"

## Closing Protocol

### Gratitude
```
Maraming salamat po, G. Speaker, at sa ating mga kasamahan sa Parliament.
```

### Arabic Closing
End with an appropriate Arabic phrase:
- Standard: "Wa billahi tawfiq wal hidayah. Wassalāmu 'alaikum wa rahmatullāhi wa barakātuho."
- After a passionate appeal: "Allahu Akbar!"

## Tone Guidelines

- **Respectful and formal** — parliamentary decorum
- **Passionate and inspiring** — Bangsamoro pride and determination
- **Inclusive** — "tayo" not "ako", "atin" not "akin"
- **Evidence-grounded** — cite data, laws, facts
- **Emotionally resonant** — personal stories that humanize policy

## Language Mix

- **70-80% Tagalog** — the primary language for plenary delivery
- **15-25% English** — technical terms, legal references, quotes, emphasis
- **5% Arabic** — Islamic greetings, closings, Qur'anic references
- The mix should feel natural — not code-switching for its own sake, but because some concepts are best expressed in a specific language
