## The Challenge

### A Historic Transition

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao was established in 2019 following ratification of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054). This marked the culmination of decades of peace negotiations and the Philippines' commitment to addressing historical injustices faced by the Bangsamoro people.

The Bangsamoro Transition Authority serves as the interim government until 2028. Its mandate is straightforward but demanding: establish parliamentary governance systems, build institutional capacity, deliver development to long-underserved communities, and demonstrate that peace delivers tangible benefits.

### The Governance Gap

Despite significant progress, the BTA contends with structural challenges that constrain effective governance.

Manual, paper-based processes dominate current operations. Legislative tracking relies on spreadsheets and physical documents. Budget preparation uses disconnected systems. No unified view of government operations exists across agencies.

Institutional memory remains fragile. Staff turnover risks losing operational knowledge. Procedures lack systematic documentation. Training new personnel becomes increasingly difficult as tacit knowledge accumulates.

Coordination between agencies operates largely through informal channels. The 12 ministries function in isolation. Parliament-Executive coordination follows ad hoc patterns. Planning cycles across agencies remain unsynchronized.

Transparency mechanisms fall short of citizen expectations. Tracking bill status publicly proves difficult. Budget execution visibility remains limited. Constituent requests lack systematic follow-up.

Compliance burdens consume scarce capacity. The Bangsamoro Budget System Act requires complex adherence. The E-Governance Act of 2025 (RA 12254) mandates digital transformation. Manual compliance is both resource-intensive and error-prone.

### Why This Matters

BARMM's success or failure carries implications beyond the region.

For peace: effective governance validates the autonomy that the peace process created. For development: efficient government operations enable faster service delivery to underserved communities. For democracy: transparent, accountable institutions build public trust in self-governance. For the Philippines: BARMM's success can inform governance innovations nationally.

## Our Solution: e-Bangsamoro Platform

Providing Bangsamoro government personnel with integrated digital tools for legislative management, budget compliance, and inter-agency coordination enables them to operate more efficiently, transparently, and accountably. This leads to faster policy responses, better resource allocation, and improved service delivery to Bangsamoro communities—ultimately contributing to peace consolidation and demonstrating that self-governance delivers tangible benefits.

### Platform Architecture

e-Bangsamoro is a unified, multi-tenant platform with three integrated portals:

**Parliament Portal** digitizes legislative operations for the Bangsamoro Parliament.

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Bill Management | Draft, track, and archive Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts |
| Resolution Tracking | Manage parliamentary resolutions through approval workflow |
| Committee Work | Organize committee schedules, documents, and deliberations |
| Session Management | Track plenary sessions, voting records, attendance |
| Constituent Services | Log and track requests from constituents |
| Office Management | Task management, calendar, staff coordination |

Parliament users (GAAB 2026): 1,750 staff including 80 MPs, 1,404 permanent secretariat staff, and 266 contract positions.

**Ministry Portal** supports strategic planning and coordination across executive agencies.

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| 10-Point Framework | Align ministry plans with Chief Minister's priorities |
| PPA Management | Track Programs, Projects, and Activities |
| Performance Monitoring | Results-Based M&E dashboards |
| Inter-Ministry Coordination | Shared calendars, task coordination |
| Office Management | Task management, calendar, staff coordination |
| Document Management | Centralized policy and planning documents |

Ministry users (GAAB 2026): 40,492 MOA staff across the Office of the Chief Minister (1,130 including attached agencies) and 15 ministries (39,362 personnel).

**Budget Portal** automates BBSA compliance and fiscal management.

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Budget Preparation | Guided workflow for annual budget formulation |
| Appropriation Tracking | Monitor legislative appropriations |
| Allotment Management | Track fund releases and allocations |
| Obligation Monitoring | Record and track financial commitments |
| Expenditure Tracking | Monitor actual spending against plans |
| Compliance Automation | UACS coding, BBSA workflow enforcement |

Budget users (GAAB 2026): ~2,000 role-specific budget/finance staff across all MOAs.

**Citizen Portal** provides transparency and citizen services.

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Transparency Dashboard | Government performance, budget execution visibility |
| Legislative Tracker | Track bills, resolutions, enacted laws |
| Budget Transparency | View GAAB appropriations, utilization, projects |
| Service Request | Submit requests, track status |
| Program Discovery | Discover and apply for government programs |
| MP Directory | Find MPs, contact representatives |

Citizen users: ~5 million Bangsamoro constituents including anonymous visitors, registered citizens, barangay officials, and CSO representatives.

### Indirect Beneficiaries

Citizens do not directly use the platform but benefit through improved governance outcomes.

**Faster policy response** means bills addressing community needs move through the legislative process more efficiently. Reduced bottlenecks in deliberation accelerate lawmaking. Systematic tracking prevents bills from stalling or disappearing in the process.

**Better resource allocation** results from budget alignment with development priorities. Real-time tracking reduces fund misallocation. BBSA compliance ensures proper fiscal management across agencies.

**Improved constituent services** emerge as MP offices systematically track constituent requests. Requests no longer get lost or forgotten. Data-driven understanding of community needs informs parliamentary action.

**Enhanced transparency** allows citizens to access bill status information. Budget execution visibility builds trust. Audit trails enable accountability for government actions.

**Coordinated development** flows from ministry plans aligned with the Bangsamoro Development Plan. Inter-agency coordination reduces duplication of programs. Systematic M&E enables course correction when programs underperform.

## Use of Funds

### Budget Summary

| Category | Amount (PHP) | Percentage |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| Platform Development | 2,800,000 | 58% |
| Capacity Building | 900,000 | 19% |
| Infrastructure & Hosting | 650,000 | 13% |
| Project Management | 350,000 | 7% |
| Monitoring & Evaluation | 150,000 | 3% |
| **Total** | **4,850,000** | **100%** |

### Budget Narrative

**Platform Development (PHP 2,800,000)** covers a full-stack development team of 4-6 personnel over 12 months. The frontend uses React 19, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS. The backend runs on Django and Django REST Framework with PostgreSQL. This allocation also covers testing, quality assurance, and security review.

**Capacity Building (PHP 900,000)** funds user training workshops across multiple cohorts, training materials development, administrator certification, and change management support.

**Infrastructure & Hosting (PHP 650,000)** provides cloud hosting on Railway or equivalent, database services for PostgreSQL and Redis, file storage, and domain/SSL/security services.

**Project Management (PHP 350,000)** supports a project coordinator for stakeholder coordination, progress reporting, and risk management.

**Monitoring & Evaluation (PHP 150,000)** covers baseline assessment, progress monitoring, end-of-project evaluation, and learning documentation.

## Alignment with Donor Priorities

### Governance & Democracy Funders

e-Bangsamoro strengthens parliamentary capacity and democratic institutions. The platform builds transparency mechanisms that enable public accountability. Compliance automation supports rule of law through BBSA and RA 12254 adherence. Improved service delivery reaches marginalized communities across the region.

The platform demonstrates that autonomy delivers better governance, validating the peace dividend. Strong institutional capacity reduces grievances that fuel conflict.
