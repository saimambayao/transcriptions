## Foundation Course

### Cofounding Fundamentals (8-16 hours)

**Target Audience:** Aspiring entrepreneurs considering cofounding

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Evaluate whether to seek a cofounder
2. Assess their own cofounding readiness
3. Identify complementary skills needed
4. Screen potential cofounder candidates
5. Conduct a cofounder "dating" process
6. Recognize red flags and green flags

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Solo vs. Team Decision** | Pros and cons of each path. When cofounders add value. Self-assessment. | 2 hours |
| **2. Cofounder Self-Assessment** | Your skills, gaps, work style, values. What you bring and need. | 2 hours |
| **3. Finding Cofounder Candidates** | Sources: networks, events, platforms. The "cofounder dating" approach. | 2 hours |
| **4. Evaluating Compatibility** | Skill complementarity. Values alignment. Work style fit. Trust assessment. | 3 hours |
| **5. Red Flags and Green Flags** | Warning signs. Positive indicators. Reference checking. | 2 hours |
| **6. Next Steps** | Trial projects. Early conversations. When to formalize. | 2 hours |

### Assessments
- Self-assessment portfolio (cofounding readiness)
- Cofounder criteria definition (what you're looking for)
- Mock screening interview (role play)
- Action plan (next steps to find cofounder)

## Practitioner Course

### Cofounding Dynamics (24-40 hours)

**Target Audience:** Active cofounders or those about to formalize partnership

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Negotiate and structure equity splits fairly
2. Draft comprehensive founders agreements
3. Establish effective cofounder communication
4. Navigate cofounder conflicts productively
5. Make joint decisions efficiently
6. Plan for cofounder transitions and departures

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Equity Fundamentals** | What is equity? Why it matters. Cap table basics. Dilution. | 3 hours |
| **2. Equity Split Models** | Equal vs. contribution-based. Dynamic equity. CSEA incubation model. | 4 hours |
| **3. Vesting and Protection** | Vesting schedules. Cliffs. Acceleration. Buyback provisions. | 3 hours |
| **4. Founders Agreements** | Key terms. Legal considerations. Philippine context. Templates. | 4 hours |
| **5. Role Definition** | Dividing responsibilities. CEO vs. CTO dynamics. Overlap zones. | 3 hours |
| **6. Decision Making** | Consensus vs. divided authority. Major decisions. Deadlock resolution. | 3 hours |
| **7. Communication** | Regular cadence. Difficult conversations. Feedback. | 3 hours |
| **8. Conflict Resolution** | Common conflicts. Prevention. Resolution frameworks. When to seek help. | 4 hours |
| **9. Cofounder Transitions** | Departure scenarios. Buyouts. Maintaining relationship. | 3 hours |
| **10. Founder Wellness** | Managing stress. Support systems. Sustainable pace. | 3 hours |

### Assessments
- Equity split proposal (for their own venture)
- Founders agreement draft (using template)
- Role matrix (who does what)
- Conflict scenario response (case study)
- Communication protocol document

## Incubation Specialist Course

### Supporting Founding Teams (16-24 hours)

**Target Audience:** Incubator staff, mentors, program managers, CSEA staff

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Design cofounder matching programs
2. Assess founding team health
3. Intervene in cofounder conflicts
4. Support founding teams through transitions
5. Apply CSEA incubation model principles
6. Build sustainable incubation programs

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Incubation Models Overview** | Incubator vs. accelerator. Equity vs. non-equity. CSEA model. | 3 hours |
| **2. Cofounder Matching Programs** | Matching methodologies. Events. Platforms. Facilitation. | 3 hours |
| **3. Founding Team Assessment** | Health indicators. Red flags. Intervention triggers. | 3 hours |
| **4. Supporting Team Dynamics** | Coaching techniques. Facilitation. When to intervene. | 4 hours |
| **5. Conflict Mediation** | Mediation skills. Neutrality. Resolution frameworks. | 4 hours |
| **6. CSEA Incubation Model** | Partnership approach. No-equity support. Success metrics. | 3 hours |
| **7. Program Design** | Curriculum. Mentorship. Resources. Graduation. | 3 hours |

### Assessments
- Cofounder matching event design
- Team health assessment (case study)
- Conflict mediation simulation
- CSEA partnership proposal
- Program design document

## Assessment Methods

### Assessment Types

| Type | Purpose | Weight |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Self-Assessment** | Personal readiness evaluation | 15% |
| **Case Analysis** | Apply concepts to scenarios | 20% |
| **Document Creation** | Produce real artifacts | 25% |
| **Role Play** | Practice difficult conversations | 20% |
| **Reflection** | Learning synthesis | 10% |
| **Participation** | Engagement and collaboration | 10% |

### Rubrics

**Equity Split Proposal Rubric:**

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Factor identification** | All relevant factors considered | Most factors considered | Key factors missing |
| **Fair weighting** | Weights clearly justified | Reasonable weights | Arbitrary weights |
| **Split rationale** | Clear, compelling logic | Adequate reasoning | Weak justification |
| **Protection mechanisms** | Comprehensive vesting, buyback | Some protections | Missing protections |

**Conflict Resolution Rubric:**

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Problem identification** | Root cause clearly identified | Issue understood | Superficial understanding |
| **Communication** | Empathetic, clear, constructive | Adequate communication | Blaming or unclear |
| **Solution generation** | Multiple creative options | Some options | Limited options |
| **Relationship preservation** | Strengthens partnership | Maintains partnership | Damages partnership |
