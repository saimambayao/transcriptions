## Equity Fundamentals

### What is Founder Equity?

Founder equity represents ownership stake in the startup, typically expressed as a percentage of total shares.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Shares** | Units of ownership in the company |
| **Equity** | Percentage of total ownership |
| **Cap Table** | Document tracking all equity ownership |
| **Dilution** | Reduction in ownership % when new shares issued |
| **Vesting** | Earning equity over time based on conditions |

### Equity vs. Compensation

| Compensation Type | When to Use | Risk Level |
|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Salary** | Need income stability | Low |
| **Equity only** | Believe strongly in upside, have savings | High |
| **Hybrid** | Balance risk and reward | Medium |
| **Deferred salary + equity** | Early stage, limited funds | Medium-High |

## Equity Split Models

### Equal Split (50/50 or N-way)

**When to use:**
- Cofounders have truly equal contributions
- Similar commitment levels
- Joined at the same time
- Comparable skills and networks

**Advantages:**
- Simple, no negotiation needed
- Signals partnership of equals
- Reduces conflict

**Disadvantages:**
- May not reflect actual contributions
- Decision deadlock risk (50/50)
- May breed resentment if contributions differ

### Contribution-Based Split

Calculate equity based on weighted contributions:

```text
CONTRIBUTION-BASED CALCULATION:
│
├── FACTOR 1: IDEA (5-10%)
│   └── Who originated the concept?
│
├── FACTOR 2: BUSINESS PLAN (5-10%)
│   └── Who developed the strategy?
│
├── FACTOR 3: DOMAIN EXPERTISE (5-15%)
│   └── Industry knowledge, connections
│
├── FACTOR 4: COMMITMENT (20-30%)
│   └── Full-time? Part-time? Duration?
│
├── FACTOR 5: RESPONSIBILITIES (20-30%)
│   └── CEO, CTO, COO roles and scope
│
├── FACTOR 6: CAPITAL (10-20%)
│   └── Cash or in-kind investment
│
└── FACTOR 7: OPPORTUNITY COST (5-15%)
    └── What are they giving up?
```

### Dynamic Equity Split (Slicing Pie)

Mike Moyer's model where equity is calculated based on actual contributions over time.

**Formula:**
```text
Your Equity % = Your Slices / Total Slices

Where Slices = Time × Rate + Money × Multiplier + Other Contributions
```

**Slice Rates:**
| Contribution | Slice Rate |
|--------------|------------|
| Time (at fair market rate) | 1 slice per peso |
| Cash investment | 2-4 slices per peso |
| Significant IP | Negotiated value |
| Equipment/assets | Fair market value × multiplier |

**Advantages:**
- Fair to early contributors
- Adjusts as contributions change
- Reduces upfront negotiation

**Disadvantages:**
- Complex to track
- Can create uncertainty
- Requires ongoing administration

## Vesting Structures

### Standard 4-Year Vesting with 1-Year Cliff

The most common vesting structure:

```text
STANDARD VESTING SCHEDULE:
│
├── Month 0-12: CLIFF PERIOD
│   └── 0% vested (must stay 1 year to earn any)
│
├── Month 12: CLIFF VESTS
│   └── 25% vests at 12-month mark
│
├── Month 13-48: MONTHLY VESTING
│   └── Additional ~2.08% vests each month
│
└── Month 48: FULLY VESTED
    └── 100% of granted shares vested
```

### Vesting Variations

| Variation | Structure | When to Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **No cliff** | Monthly from day 1 | Trust established, proven track record |
| **6-month cliff** | 6 months, then monthly | Shorter trial period needed |
| **3-year vesting** | 3-year total period | Smaller companies, faster startups |
| **Back-weighted** | More equity vests later | Encourage long-term stay |
| **Milestone-based** | Vesting tied to achievements | Performance-oriented cultures |

### Acceleration Clauses

**Single Trigger Acceleration:**
- Vesting accelerates upon acquisition
- Protects founders from losing unvested shares

**Double Trigger Acceleration:**
- Requires BOTH acquisition AND termination
- More investor-friendly
- Standard in VC-backed startups

```text
DOUBLE TRIGGER EXAMPLE:
│
├── Trigger 1: Company is acquired
│   └── Vesting continues normally
│
├── Trigger 2: Founder is terminated/demoted
│   └── Acceleration kicks in
│
└── Result: Remaining shares vest immediately
```

## Equity Negotiation

### Preparation Checklist

Before negotiating:

| Preparation Step | Questions to Answer |
|------------------|---------------------|
| **Know your worth** | What skills do you uniquely bring? |
| **Understand alternatives** | What's your BATNA? |
| **Research comparables** | What do similar cofounders receive? |
| **Clarify priorities** | Equity vs. control vs. salary? |
| **Anticipate objections** | What will they argue? |

### Negotiation Framework

```text
EQUITY NEGOTIATION PROCESS:
│
├── 1. START WITH PRINCIPLES
│   ├── What values guide the split?
│   ├── How will we measure contribution?
│   └── What's fair to everyone?
│
├── 2. SHARE PERSPECTIVES
│   ├── Each founder states their view
│   ├── Listen without interrupting
│   └── Understand underlying interests
│
├── 3. IDENTIFY FACTORS
│   ├── List all relevant factors
│   ├── Weight each factor
│   └── Score each founder
│
├── 4. PROPOSE AND ITERATE
│   ├── Start with data-based proposal
│   ├── Discuss adjustments
│   └── Seek win-win modifications
│
├── 5. ADD PROTECTIONS
│   ├── Vesting schedule
│   ├── Acceleration clauses
│   └── Buyback provisions
│
└── 6. DOCUMENT
    └── Formalize in shareholders agreement
```

### Key Negotiation Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Focus on interests, not positions** | Why do they want 60%? What need does it serve? |
| **Use objective criteria** | Market rates, contribution metrics |
| **Generate options** | Different splits for different scenarios |
| **Build the relationship** | The negotiation sets the tone for partnership |
| **Put it in writing** | Verbal agreements lead to disputes |

## Legal Structures

### Essential Legal Documents

| Document | Purpose | When Needed |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Founders Agreement** | Initial relationship terms | Day 1 |
| **Shareholders Agreement** | Formal equity terms | At incorporation |
| **Stock Purchase Agreement** | Equity grant mechanics | At equity issuance |
| **IP Assignment** | Transfer of IP to company | At incorporation |
| **Vesting Agreement** | Vesting terms | At equity grant |

### Founders Agreement Key Terms

```text
FOUNDERS AGREEMENT CONTENTS:
│
├── EQUITY ALLOCATION
│   ├── Initial split percentages
│   ├── Vesting schedule
│   └── Cliff provisions
│
├── ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
│   ├── Title and position
│   ├── Key responsibilities
│   └── Time commitment
│
├── DECISION MAKING
│   ├── Day-to-day decisions
│   ├── Major decisions (require consensus)
│   └── Deadlock resolution
│
├── INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
│   ├── Prior IP disclosure
│   ├── Assignment of new IP
│   └── Confidentiality
│
├── DEPARTURE PROVISIONS
│   ├── Voluntary departure
│   ├── Termination for cause
│   ├── Buyback rights
│   └── Non-compete terms
│
└── DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    ├── Mediation first
    └── Arbitration process
```

### Buyback Provisions

| Scenario | Typical Buyback Terms |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Voluntary departure (good leaver)** | Fair market value for vested shares |
| **Termination for cause (bad leaver)** | Original price or lower for all shares |
| **Death or disability** | Fair market value, often with insurance |
| **Founder conflict** | Pre-agreed formula, mediation |

## Common Mistakes

### Equity Split Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Splitting too early** | Don't know contributions yet | Work together 3+ months first |
| **Equal split by default** | May not reflect reality | Use contribution-based factors |
| **No vesting** | Free equity for departure | Always include vesting |
| **Idea premium too high** | Ideas are cheap, execution matters | Weight execution higher |
| **Ignoring opportunity cost** | Unfair to those sacrificing more | Factor in what's given up |

### Legal Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **No written agreement** | Disputes with no resolution | Document everything |
| **Handshake deals** | Unenforceable, forgotten | Formalize agreements |
| **Skipping IP assignment** | Company doesn't own its IP | Assign all IP to company |
| **No departure provisions** | Messy breakups | Include buyback terms |
| **Ignoring dilution** | Surprised by ownership reduction | Model future rounds |

### Negotiation Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Prevention |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Avoiding the conversation** | Festering resentment | Have the hard talk early |
| **Positional bargaining** | Damaged relationships | Focus on interests |
| **Winner-take-all mindset** | Partner loses, partnership loses | Seek fair outcomes |
| **Rushing to close** | Missed important terms | Take time to get it right |
| **Not getting advice** | Missing important protections | Consult lawyer and mentors |

# Equity Model Consulting (from SKILL.md)

### Mode 2: Equity Model Consulting

For cofounder matching, team building, and partnership structuring.

#### Step 1: Assess Cofounder Situation

```text
COFOUNDER SITUATION ASSESSMENT:
│
├── Are you looking for a cofounder?
│   └── YES → Cofounder Search guidance
│
├── Have you identified a potential cofounder?
│   └── YES → Compatibility Assessment
│
├── Are you structuring a cofounder agreement?
│   └── YES → Partnership Structure guidance
│
├── Are you having cofounder conflicts?
│   └── YES → Conflict Resolution guidance
│
└── Are cofounders parting ways?
    └── YES → Separation guidance
```

#### Step 2: Apply Appropriate Framework

**Cofounder Search:**

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Self-assessment** | What do you bring? What do you need? |
| **Network mapping** | Who do you know? Who do they know? |
| **Dating period** | Work on small project first |
| **Reference checks** | Talk to people who've worked with them |

**Compatibility Assessment:**

```text
COFOUNDER COMPATIBILITY CHECKLIST:
│
├── SKILLS
│   □ Do skills complement (not duplicate)?
│   □ Are critical gaps covered?
│
├── VISION
│   □ Do you agree on the end goal?
│   □ Do you agree on the path to get there?
│
├── VALUES
│   □ Are ethical frameworks aligned?
│   □ Are work-life priorities compatible?
│
├── COMMITMENT
│   □ Is time commitment equal?
│   □ Are financial contributions fair?
│
├── COMMUNICATION
│   □ Can you disagree productively?
│   □ Can you give/receive feedback?
│
└── TRUST
    □ Do you trust their judgment?
    □ Do you trust their integrity?
```

**Partnership Structure:**

| Element | Consideration |
|---------|---------------|
| **Equity split** | Contribution-based, with vesting |
| **Vesting schedule** | 4-year with 1-year cliff typical |
| **Roles & responsibilities** | Clear, documented |
| **Decision rights** | Who decides what |
| **Exit provisions** | What happens if someone leaves |
| **IP assignment** | All work belongs to company |
