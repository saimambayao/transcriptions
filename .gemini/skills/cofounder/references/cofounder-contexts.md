## Cooperative Cofounding

### Unique Characteristics

Cofounding cooperatives differs fundamentally from conventional startups due to democratic ownership and member control.

| Aspect | Conventional Startup | Cooperative |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Founders + investors | All members equally |
| **Governance** | Board/CEO control | Democratic (1 member = 1 vote) |
| **Founder role** | Permanent owner | Founding member (equal to others) |
| **Equity split** | Negotiated percentages | No traditional equity |
| **Exit** | Acquisition, IPO | No individual exit |
| **Control** | Based on equity | Based on membership |

### Cooperative Founder Roles

Instead of equity-based roles, cooperative founders take on:

- **Incorporators**: Minimum 15 natural persons (primary coop), sign Articles of Cooperation
- **Initial Officers**: Chairperson, Vice-Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Board Members
- **Committee Heads**: Education, Election, Mediation, Audit committees
- **Special Roles**: General Manager, Technical advisors, External consultants

### Compensation for Cooperative Founders

| Compensation Type | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Patronage refund** | Share of surplus based on transactions |
| **Interest on capital** | Limited return on share capital |
| **Honoraria** | For officers (per bylaws) |
| **Salary** | If employed as manager/staff |
| **Recognition** | Founding member status |

## Social Enterprise Cofounding

### Dual Nature of SE Cofounding

Social enterprises blend mission and business, creating unique cofounding dynamics:
- **Mission Alignment**: Shared vision for social change, beneficiary priorities, impact measurement
- **Business Viability**: Complementary skills, revenue model agreement, growth vs. impact trade-offs

### SE Cofounder Roles

| Role | Focus | Key Responsibilities |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| **Impact Champion** | Mission | Beneficiary needs, ToC, impact measurement |
| **Business Driver** | Sustainability | Revenue, operations, finance |
| **Community Builder** | Stakeholders | Beneficiaries, partners, community |
| **Technical Lead** | Product/Service | Delivery, quality, innovation |

## BARMM Context

*Updated with Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028*

### BDP 2023-2028 Vision and Mission

**Vision**: "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**Mission**: "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

**Overall Goal**: Empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro
- **Empowered**: Improved capacity through quality services and economic opportunities
- **Cohesive**: Improved stakeholder relationships, reduced violent conflict
- **Progressive**: Sustained ability to optimize resources for development

## Comparative Summary

### Cofounding Approaches by Context

| Aspect | Conventional | Cooperative | Social Enterprise | BARMM |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Equity** | Standard | None | Varies | Often none |
| **Governance** | Founder control | Democratic | Mission-guided | Relationship-based |
| **Selection** | Skills-first | Values-first | Mission + skills | Trust-first |
| **Compensation** | Equity + salary | Patronage + honoraria | Varies | Often modest |
| **Exit** | Sale of shares | Withdrawal | Varies | Community continuity |

### Key Success Factors by Context

| Context | Critical Success Factors |
|---------|-------------------------|
| **Conventional** | Skill complementarity, equity agreement, vesting |
| **Cooperative** | Democratic commitment, member focus, patience |
| **Social Enterprise** | Mission alignment, impact + sustainability balance |
| **BARMM** | Trust, relationships, community standing, patience |

### Conflict Resolution by Context

| Context | Primary Resolution Method |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Conventional** | Legal agreements, mediation, buyout |
| **Cooperative** | Mediation committee, democratic process |
| **Social Enterprise** | Mission alignment check, stakeholder input |
| **BARMM** | Elder mediation, community process, Shari'ah arbitration |
