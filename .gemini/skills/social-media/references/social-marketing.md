## Social Marketing on Social Media

### Definition

Social Marketing uses marketing principles to influence behavior for social good. When applied to social media, it means using platforms strategically to promote positive behavior change.

### Social Marketing vs Social Media Marketing

| Social Marketing | Social Media Marketing |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Goal is behavior change | Goal is engagement/sales |
| May or may not use social media | Always uses social media |
| Success = behavior adoption | Success = likes, clicks, conversions |
| Audience may be resistant | Audience is interested |

### Why Social Media for Social Marketing?

| Platform Advantage | Application |
|--------------------|-------------|
| **Reach** | Scale awareness and messaging |
| **Targeting** | Segment audiences precisely |
| **Social Proof** | Show others adopting behavior |
| **Engagement** | Two-way dialogue, support |
| **Cost** | Affordable for small organizations |

## Behavior Change Content Strategies

### Stages of Change Content

| Stage | Content Type | Examples |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **Precontemplation** | Awareness, risk information | Statistics, consequences |
| **Contemplation** | Benefits, testimonials | Success stories, comparisons |
| **Preparation** | How-to, resources | Tutorials, checklists, tools |
| **Action** | Support, reinforcement | Encouragement, tips |
| **Maintenance** | Community, celebration | Milestones, peer support |

### Content Pillars for Social Marketing

| Pillar | Purpose | % of Content |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Awareness** | Educate about issue/behavior | 25% |
| **Motivation** | Inspire and encourage | 25% |
| **Enablement** | Provide tools and how-tos | 25% |
| **Community** | Social proof and support | 25% |

### Message Framing

| Frame | When to Use | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Gain Frame** | Low-risk behaviors | "Save money by switching to..." |
| **Loss Frame** | High-risk behaviors | "Don't miss out on..." |
| **Social Frame** | Normative behaviors | "Join thousands who..." |
| **Self-Efficacy** | Challenging behaviors | "You can do this..." |

### Emotional Appeals

| Emotion | Use Case | Caution |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Hope** | Aspirational change | Must be believable |
| **Fear** | Risk awareness | Can backfire if too strong |
| **Pride** | Identity behaviors | Connect to values |
| **Empathy** | Helping behaviors | Show real impact |

## Social Norms Messaging

### Types of Social Norms

| Type | Definition | Social Media Application |
|------|------------|--------------------------|
| **Descriptive** | What most people do | "92% of members participate" |
| **Injunctive** | What people approve of | "Our community values..." |
| **Dynamic** | Trends toward behavior | "More people are joining every day" |

### Social Proof Content Types

| Content Type | Example |
|--------------|---------|
| **Testimonials** | Video stories of behavior adopters |
| **User-Generated Content** | Followers sharing their experience |
| **Numbers/Stats** | "10,000+ families have joined" |
| **Endorsements** | Community leaders, influencers |
| **Challenges** | Group participation campaigns |

### Best Practices

```
DO:
✓ Use positive norms (what people DO, not DON'T)
✓ Make norms specific to audience
✓ Show trend toward desired behavior
✓ Use real people, not actors

DON'T:
✗ Highlight negative behaviors as common
✗ Use vague or unbelievable statistics
✗ Shame or criticize non-adopters
✗ Make behavior seem rare or difficult
```

### BARMM Social Norms Messaging

| Sector | Norm Messaging Approach |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Cooperatives** | "More barangays are forming cooperatives" |
| **Social Enterprises** | "Conscious consumers choose impact products" |
| **Government** | "Bangsamoro citizens are registering for programs" |

## Digital Nudges

### Nudge Theory on Social Media

Nudges are subtle design choices that encourage behavior without restricting options.

### Social Media Nudge Techniques

| Nudge | Application | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Defaults** | Pre-set positive options | Auto-follow impact accounts |
| **Simplification** | Reduce friction | One-click sign-up links |
| **Timing** | Prompt at right moment | Reminder posts at decision points |
| **Salience** | Make behavior visible | Highlight impact, outcomes |
| **Commitment** | Public pledges | "I commit to..." campaigns |
| **Feedback** | Show progress | Progress trackers, milestones |

### Platform-Specific Nudges

| Platform | Nudge Opportunity |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Facebook** | Groups for commitment, Events for action |
| **Instagram** | Stories for reminders, Highlights for how-tos |
| **TikTok** | Challenges for participation, Trends for norms |
| **Messenger** | Chatbots for support, Reminders |

## Campaign Frameworks

### Social Marketing Campaign Structure

```
1. PRE-CAMPAIGN
   - Define behavioral objective
   - Research audience barriers/motivators
   - Develop message strategy
   - Create content calendar

2. LAUNCH
   - Awareness content (problem, risk)
   - Social norms messaging
   - Call to action with easy first step

3. ENGAGEMENT
   - How-to content
   - Testimonials and success stories
   - Interactive elements (polls, Q&A)
   - Community building

4. REINFORCEMENT
   - Celebrate adopters
   - Share outcomes and impact
   - Maintain community
   - Address relapse

5. EVALUATION
   - Track behavior metrics
   - Measure engagement
   - Document outcomes
   - Iterate based on data
```

### Campaign Types

| Type | Objective | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Awareness** | Educate about issue | 2-4 weeks |
| **Action** | Drive specific behavior | 4-8 weeks |
| **Sustained** | Long-term behavior change | 6+ months |
| **Crisis** | Rapid response | As needed |

### Content Calendar for Behavior Change

| Week | Focus | Content Types |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Week 1 | Awareness | Statistics, issue explanation |
| Week 2 | Motivation | Benefits, testimonials |
| Week 3 | Enablement | How-tos, resources, tools |
| Week 4 | Community | UGC, challenges, celebration |

### BARMM Campaign Examples

**Cooperative Membership Campaign:**
```
Behavior: Join local cooperative
Audience: Unaffiliated farmers and fisherfolk
Platforms: Facebook (primary), Messenger (support)
Content Mix:
- Member success stories (40%)
- Benefits and how-to-join (30%)
- Community events and updates (20%)
- Promotional offers (10%)
```

**Social Enterprise Impact Campaign:**
```
Behavior: Purchase impact products
Audience: Conscious consumers, diaspora
Platforms: Instagram (primary), Facebook
Content Mix:
- Maker stories and behind-scenes (40%)
- Product features and quality (25%)
- Impact numbers and outcomes (25%)
- Promotions and offers (10%)
```

**Government Program Uptake Campaign:**
```
Behavior: Register for CSEA program
Audience: Eligible cooperatives and SEs
Platforms: Facebook, Local radio (cross-media)
Content Mix:
- Program benefits and eligibility (35%)
- Success stories from beneficiaries (30%)
- How to register (step-by-step) (25%)
- Deadline reminders (10%)
```

## Metrics for Social Marketing

### Behavior Change Metrics

| Level | Metrics |
|-------|---------|
| **Awareness** | Reach, impressions, video views |
| **Engagement** | Likes, comments, shares, saves |
| **Intent** | Link clicks, sign-up starts |
| **Behavior** | Completions, registrations, adoptions |
| **Maintenance** | Retention, repeat behavior, referrals |

### Attribution Challenges

| Challenge | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| Behavior happens offline | Use tracking links, surveys |
| Long behavior change timeline | Track intermediate outcomes |
| Multiple touchpoints | Use unique codes, attribution models |
| Self-reported data | Validate with actual behavior data |
