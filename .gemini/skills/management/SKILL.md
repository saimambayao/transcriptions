## Quick Reference

### Management Definition

> "To manage is to forecast and plan, to organize, to command, and to control." — Henri Fayol (1916)

Management is the process of getting things done through and with people by effectively utilizing limited resources.

### POLC Framework

| Function | Definition | Key Activities |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| **Planning** | Setting objectives, determining courses of action | Goals, strategies, forecasting |
| **Organizing** | Arranging resources, establishing structure | Departmentalization, delegation |
| **Leading** | Influencing and motivating people | Directing, communicating |
| **Controlling** | Monitoring and correcting performance | Standards, measuring, correcting |

### Levels of Management

| Level | Focus | Time Horizon | Key Skills |
|-------|-------|--------------|------------|
| **Top** | Strategic direction | 3-5 years | Conceptual |
| **Middle** | Tactical, departmental | 1-3 years | Human + Conceptual |
| **Operational** | Day-to-day operations | Daily/weekly | Technical + Human |

### Katz's Management Skills

| Skill | Description | By Level |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Technical** | Job-specific knowledge, tools | High → Low (operational to top) |
| **Human** | People skills, communication | High at all levels |
| **Conceptual** | Big picture, strategic thinking | Low → High (operational to top) |

### Mintzberg's 10 Managerial Roles

| Category | Roles |
|----------|-------|
| **Interpersonal** | Figurehead, Leader, Liaison |
| **Informational** | Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson |
| **Decisional** | Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator |

### Manager vs Leader

| Manager | Leader |
|---------|--------|
| Administers | Innovates |
| Maintains | Develops |
| Relies on control | Inspires trust |
| Short-range view | Long-range perspective |
| Does things right | Does the right thing |

*Both roles are complementary and necessary.*

## Workflow 2: Management Consulting Mode

For organizational management consulting.

### Assessment Framework

**Organizational Diagnosis:**
```
□ Management structure and levels
□ Decision-making processes
□ Communication patterns
□ Leadership style assessment
□ Performance management systems
□ Organizational culture
```

**POLC Assessment:**
```
□ Planning: Are goals clear? Strategies defined?
□ Organizing: Is structure appropriate? Clear roles?
□ Leading: Is leadership effective? Staff motivated?
□ Controlling: Are controls in place? Performance monitored?
```

### Context-Specific Considerations

**For Cooperatives** (use with `/cooperative`):
- Democratic member control (one member, one vote)
- Board elected by General Assembly
- Manager reports to Board, not owners
- Balance professional management with member participation
- See [references/management-contexts.md](references/management-contexts.md)

**For Social Enterprises** (use with `/social-enterprise`):
- Dual mission: social impact + financial sustainability
- Risk of mission drift
- Multiple stakeholder accountability
- Blended value creation
- See [references/management-contexts.md](references/management-contexts.md)

**For Government/Public Organizations:**
- Public accountability and transparency
- Rule-based, bureaucratic structures
- Service delivery focus
- POSDCORB framework

## Decision-Making Framework

### Rational Decision-Making Model

```
1. Define the problem
2. Generate alternatives
3. Evaluate alternatives
4. Select best alternative
5. Implement decision
6. Evaluate results
```

### Bounded Rationality (Herbert Simon)

- Cognitive limitations prevent perfect rationality
- **Satisficing**: Choose "good enough" rather than optimal
- Time and information constraints

### Decision Types

| Type | Characteristics | Approach |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Programmed** | Routine, repetitive | Follow procedures |
| **Non-programmed** | Novel, unstructured | Require judgment |
