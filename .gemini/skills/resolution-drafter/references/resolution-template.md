# BTA Parliament Resolution Template

## Standard Heading

```
P. P. Res. No. ____

Republic of the Philippines
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT
Bangsamoro Government Center, Cotabato City

[SESSION — e.g., FOURTH REGULAR SESSION]

PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. ____
```

For **proposed** resolutions (filed by MPs, not yet adopted), use:
```
Proposed Resolution No. ____

Principal Author(s): [Use plural when multiple, singular otherwise]

Co-Author(s): [Use plural when multiple, singular otherwise]
```

Notes:
- "P. P. Res. No." (Parliament Proposed Resolution) appears at the top of recent resolutions (AR-553, AR-565)
- "PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO." is the adopted number assigned after approval
- The address changed from "BARMM Compound, Cotabato City" (early resolutions) to "Bangsamoro Government Center, Cotabato City" (current) — use the current form

## Title

The title must be identical to the main operative clause. Format:

```
A RESOLUTION [describing what the resolution does — urging, requesting, commending, endorsing, etc.]
```

## Preambulatory (WHEREAS) Clauses

**Punctuation rule:** Each WHEREAS clause ends with a **semicolon** (`;`), except the **last** clause before "NOW, THEREFORE" which ends with a **colon** (`:`).

Build a deductive argument in this order (adapt to topic — not all tiers are needed for every resolution):

### 1. Global Context
Established facts, data, international conventions, research findings.
```
WHEREAS, the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal No. 13 calls on all
nations to take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts;
```

### 2. Islamic Principles
Express in general legislative terms. Exact source in footnotes.
```
WHEREAS, Islamic tradition emphasizes the preservation of life and well-being
as one of the five essential objectives (maqasid al-shari'ah) of Islamic law;¹

---
¹ The preservation of life (hifz al-nafs) is one of the five objectives of
Shari'ah as articulated by Imam al-Ghazali. The Qur'an states: "...whoever
saves a life, it is as if he has saved all of humanity" (Surah Al-Ma'idah, 5:32).
```

### 3. National Context
Constitutional provisions, national laws, national agency policies.
```
WHEREAS, Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution mandates
the State to protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill
health consciousness among them;
```

### 4. BARMM Context
BOL provisions, Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts, local policies, local facts.
```
WHEREAS, Section 4 of Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as
the Bangsamoro Organic Law, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall have
the authority to promote and protect the right to health of the Bangsamoro people;
```

### 5. Specific Problem/Situation
The immediate facts, events, or circumstances prompting this resolution.
```
WHEREAS, the province of Lanao del Sur experienced severe flooding in [month/year],
displacing approximately [number] families across [number] municipalities;
```

## Operative Clauses

### Main Clause
Note: The last WHEREAS clause ends with a **colon** (`:`), not a semicolon. Then:
```
NOW, THEREFORE, be it

RESOLVED, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority
Parliament, To [main operative clause — IDENTICAL to the title].
```
Note the capital **"T"** in "To" — this is consistent across adopted resolutions (AR-553, AR-565, AR-304).

### Additional Clauses (only when genuinely necessary)
```
RESOLVED FURTHER, That [additional operative clause — e.g., directing a committee
to conduct an investigation, requesting a report within a timeframe].

RESOLVED, FURTHER, That [alternative comma placement — both forms appear in practice].
```

### Final Clause (typically for transmittal)
```
RESOLVED, FINALLY, To forward this Resolution to [relevant offices],
for information and appropriate action.

RESOLVED FINALLY, That copy of this foregoing Resolution be given to
[relevant agencies, offices, or persons], for information.
```

Keep operative clauses minimal. Each additional clause adds complexity. Common patterns:
- Single operative + transmittal = most resolutions
- Operative + investigation/report + transmittal = inquiry resolutions
- Operative + timeline + funding request + transmittal = action resolutions

## Adopted Date and Signing Block

Use the Hijri-Gregorian dual date format used in real adopted resolutions:

```
ADOPTED, [Hijri Month] [Hijri Day], [Hijri Year]/[Gregorian Month] [Gregorian Day], [Gregorian Year]

Certified Correct:

[SECRETARY-GENERAL NAME]
Secretary-General

Attested:
[SPEAKER NAME]
Speaker
```

Examples of dual dates from adopted resolutions:
- `ADOPTED, Jumada Al-Akhirah 26, 1444/January 19, 2023`
- `ADOPTED, Muharram 22, 1447/July 17, 2025.`
- `ADOPTED, Rajab 30, 1447/January 19, 2026`

If the exact Hijri date is unknown at drafting time, use `[Hijri date]/[Gregorian date]` and flag as `[VERIFY: Hijri date conversion]`.

Note: The signing block for a **proposed** resolution (filed by an MP) includes the principal author's signature before the Secretary-General and Speaker attestation. For **adopted** resolutions, only the Secretary-General certification and Speaker attestation appear.

## Resolution Types by Purpose

| Type | Operative Verb | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Urging** | "urging [agency] to [action]" | PR-23 (infrastructure plan) |
| **Requesting** | "requesting [agency] to [action]" | PR-188 (PhilHealth drives) |
| **Commending** | "commending [person/group] for [achievement]" | Youth achievement |
| **Endorsing** | "endorsing [proposal/plan/program]" | Policy endorsement |
| **Directing** | "directing [committee] to investigate" | PR-359 (Hajj investigation) |
| **Expressing** | "expressing [support/condolence/gratitude]" | Condolence, solidarity |
| **Declaring** | "declaring [position/stance]" | Policy declarations |
