# Example Bill: Bangsamoro Dual Calendar Act

## User Prompt

"Draft a bill mandating the use of both Hijri and Gregorian calendars in all BARMM government offices and documents."

## Drafting Notes

- **Implementing agency**: Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' (under OCM) — selected because its mandate covers Islamic affairs and religious observances per BAA No. 13's MOA structure
- **Islamic citation**: The Hijrah is referenced in general legislative terms in the Declaration of Policy; specific Qur'anic/Hadith references would go in footnotes per the skill's Islamic citation protocol
- **Scope decision**: Covers all BARMM agencies AND LGUs AND educational institutions — broad coverage justified by the cultural identity purpose
- **IRR timeline**: 90 days — standard for administrative implementation requiring coordination across multiple agencies
- **BOL basis**: Article IX, Section 4 (cultural rights) and Article X, Section 1 (self-governance powers) of RA 11054

### EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Islamic calendar, known as the Hijri calendar, has been an integral part of Muslim life for over fourteen centuries. It determines the dates of religious observances, governs personal and family law matters, and serves as a marker of cultural identity for the predominantly Muslim population of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Despite its significance, the Hijri calendar has no formal recognition in official Bangsamoro government transactions. All government documents, correspondence, and records exclusively use the Gregorian calendar. This creates a disconnect between the administrative apparatus and the cultural reality of the communities it serves.

Republic Act No. 11054, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, guarantees the Bangsamoro people's right to preserve and develop their cultural heritage and identity. The dual calendar system advances this constitutional guarantee by embedding Islamic temporal reckoning into the daily operations of government without disrupting compatibility with national administrative systems.

This bill mandates the concurrent display and use of both Hijri and Gregorian dates in all official documents, correspondence, signage, and digital systems of BARMM agencies, local government units, and educational institutions. It designates the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' as the implementing agency, tasking it with the annual publication of an official conversion table and the provision of technical assistance to all covered entities.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

MP [Author Name]

---

### AN ACT MANDATING THE DUAL USE OF HIJRI AND GREGORIAN CALENDARS IN ALL MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS, OFFICES AND INSTITUTIONS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Dual Calendar Act."

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government that in the exercise of its right to self-governance pursuant to Republic Act No. 11054, it shall promote and preserve Bangsamoro cultural identity and heritage. Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government adopts the dual use of Hijri and Gregorian Calendars in the conduct of official business, recognizing the significance of the Islamic calendar to the predominantly Muslim population while maintaining compatibility with the national calendar system.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. — As used in this Act:

(a) "Hijri Calendar" refers to the Islamic lunar calendar, also known as the Muslim calendar, consisting of twelve months based on the lunar cycle, beginning from the year of the Hijrah of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) from Makkah to Madinah;

(b) "Gregorian Calendar" refers to the solar calendar adopted internationally and used as the official calendar of the Republic of the Philippines;

(c) "Official Documents" refers to all written or electronic records, correspondence, memoranda, orders, circulars, certificates, permits, licenses, and other instruments issued by any covered entity in the conduct of official business;

(d) "Covered Entities" refers to all ministries, agencies, offices, bureaus, and instrumentalities of the Bangsamoro Government, including local government units and public educational institutions within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

SECTION 4. Mandatory Dual Calendar Display. — All covered entities shall display both the Hijri and Gregorian dates in their official documents, correspondence, signage, digital platforms, and public-facing materials. The Gregorian date shall appear first, followed by the corresponding Hijri date in parentheses.

SECTION 5. Official Conversion Table. — The Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' shall publish an official Hijri-Gregorian conversion table for each calendar year, to be disseminated to all covered entities not later than the first day of Muharram of each Hijri year. The conversion table shall serve as the authoritative reference for date correspondence.

SECTION 6. Training and Technical Assistance. — The Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta', in coordination with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) and the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), shall provide training programs and technical assistance to all covered entities for the proper implementation of the dual calendar system.

SECTION 7. Implementing Agency. — The Office of the Chief Minister, through the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta', shall be the primary implementing agency of this Act.

SECTION 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — The Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta', in coordination with the Office of the Chief Minister, shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act within ninety (90) days from its effectivity.

SECTION 9. Appropriations. — The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current year's appropriations of the Office of the Chief Minister. Thereafter, the necessary funds shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

SECTION 10. Separability Clause. — If any provision of this Act or the application thereof to any person or circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of the Act, and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.

SECTION 11. Repealing Clause. — All laws, orders, decrees, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 12. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Approved,

## Annotations

1. **Em-dash convention**: Every section title ends with a period followed by an em-dash (—) before the content begins. This is mandatory BTA bill format, never a colon or hyphen.

2. **Continuous prose**: All section content is written as flowing paragraphs, never as bullet points or numbered lists. The only exception is the Definition of Terms section, which uses lettered subsections (a), (b), (c).

3. **Standard closing sections**: Sections 10-12 (Separability, Repealing, Effectivity) are verbatim boilerplate from the bill template. They should not be modified unless the bill has specific repealing targets.

4. **Implementing agency selection**: The Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta' was selected from the MOA structure because it operates under the Office of the Chief Minister and handles Islamic affairs. The bill routes through OCM (not a line ministry) because the Darul-Ifta' is an OCM-attached agency per BAA No. 13.

5. **Two-part structure**: The bill has an Explanatory Note (Part A) followed by the enacted provisions (Part B). Both share the same heading block. The Explanatory Note is persuasive prose explaining the rationale; the Main Body is formal legislative text.

6. **Long title format**: The long title ("AN ACT MANDATING...") is written in ALL CAPS and describes the bill's full scope. It ends with "AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES" as a standard catch-all.

7. **Enacting clause**: "Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:" — this exact formulation is required for all BTA bills.

8. **Appropriations pattern**: Initial costs charged to existing OCM budget, future costs included in the annual GAA. This two-phase pattern avoids requiring a separate appropriations measure.
