# Bangsamoro Parliament Bill Structure Guide

## Standard Bill Format

Every Bangsamoro Parliament bill follows the standard Philippine legislative drafting format with these essential components:

### 1. Header Section

```
Republic of the Philippines
BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT
Cotabato City

BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. ___

[TITLE OF THE BILL IN ALL CAPS - AN ACT...]
```

**Guidelines:**
- Title should begin with "AN ACT..." format
- Title should be descriptive and comprehensive
- Title should indicate the bill's purpose clearly
- Use ALL CAPS for the title

### 2. Authorship Section

```
Authored by: MP [Principal Author Name]
Co-authored by: MP [Co-author Name], MP [Co-author Name]
```

**Guidelines:**
- List principal author(s) first
- Include "MP" title before names (e.g., "MP Juan Dela Cruz")
- Separate multiple authors with commas
- Co-authors are optional

### 3. Enacting Clause

**Required format:**

```
Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:
```

**Guidelines:**
- This clause is MANDATORY for all bills
- Appears immediately after authorship and before Section 1
- Standard wording; do not modify
- Establishes legislative authority

### 4. Short Title (Section 1)

**Format:**

```
SECTION 1. Short Title. – This Act shall be known as "[Popular/Short Title]."
```

**Examples:**
- "This Act shall be known as the 'Bangsamoro Revenue Code of 2025'."
- "This Act shall be known as the 'Bangsamoro Local Government Code'."

**Guidelines:**
- Always Section 1
- Provides a convenient reference name
- Short title should be memorable and descriptive

### 5. Declaration of Policy (Section 2 or early section)

**Format:**

```
SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to [state the policy objectives and principles underlying the legislation].
```

**Guidelines:**
- Usually Section 2 (after Short Title)
- States the bill's fundamental purpose and objectives
- Establishes the policy framework
- Provides context for interpretation
- May cite constitutional or BOL provisions
- Should be comprehensive but concise (2-4 paragraphs typical)

**Example:**

```
SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to establish a just and equitable revenue system that promotes economic growth, social equity, and fiscal autonomy consistent with the Bangsamoro Organic Law. The Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its taxation powers to generate adequate revenues for the delivery of basic services and the sustainable development of the region, while ensuring fairness, transparency, and accountability in revenue collection and administration.
```

### 6. Definition of Terms (Early section)

**Format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Definition of Terms. – As used in this Act, the following terms shall mean:

(a) "[Term]" refers to [definition];

(b) "[Term]" refers to [definition];

(c) "[Term]" refers to [definition];

[Continue with all defined terms]
```

**Guidelines:**
- Usually Section 3 (after Declaration of Policy)
- Alphabetize definitions for easy reference
- Define all technical or ambiguous terms used in the bill
- Use consistent terminology throughout the bill
- Format: lowercase letter in parentheses, definition ending with semicolon
- Last definition ends with period

**When to include:**
- Technical or specialized legislation (always)
- Terms with specific legal meanings
- Terms used repeatedly throughout the bill
- Terms that could be ambiguous or misunderstood

**When to omit:**
- Simple bills with no technical terms
- Bills using only commonly understood terms

### 7. Substantive Provisions (Main Body)

**Organization options:**

**A. Simple Section-by-Section (for shorter bills):**

```
SECTION 4. [Title of Section]. – [Provision content].

SECTION 5. [Title of Section]. – [Provision content].

SECTION 6. [Title of Section]. – [Provision content].
```

**B. Chapter Organization (for longer/complex bills):**

```
CHAPTER I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

SECTION 4. [Title]. – [Content].

SECTION 5. [Title]. – [Content].

CHAPTER II
[SUBSTANTIVE TOPIC]

SECTION 6. [Title]. – [Content].

SECTION 7. [Title]. – [Content].
```

**C. Article and Chapter Organization (for comprehensive codes):**

```
ARTICLE I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I
[SUBTOPIC]

SECTION 1. [Title]. – [Content].

CHAPTER II
[SUBTOPIC]

SECTION 2. [Title]. – [Content].

ARTICLE II
[MAJOR TOPIC]

CHAPTER I
[SUBTOPIC]

SECTION 3. [Title]. – [Content].
```

**Guidelines for Substantive Provisions:**
- Organize logically from general to specific
- Group related provisions together
- Use clear, descriptive section titles
- Number sections consecutively throughout the bill
- Start each section with a title followed by " – "
- Use subsections for detailed provisions:
  - (a), (b), (c) for first-level subsections
  - (1), (2), (3) for second-level subsections
  - (i), (ii), (iii) for third-level subsections

**Example of subsection structure:**

```
SECTION 10. Tax Rates. – The following tax rates shall apply:

(a) For individuals:

(1) Income up to One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000): Five percent (5%);

(2) Income over One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000): Ten percent (10%);

(b) For corporations:

(1) Domestic corporations: Fifteen percent (15%);

(2) Foreign corporations: Twenty percent (20%).
```

### 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations Clause

**Standard format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Implementing Rules and Regulations. – Within [number] days from the effectivity of this Act, the [implementing agency/office] shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.
```

**Alternative formats:**

**When multiple agencies involved:**

```
SECTION [X]. Implementing Rules and Regulations. – Within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act, the [Agency A], in coordination with [Agency B] and [Agency C], shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.
```

**When Parliament approval required:**

```
SECTION [X]. Implementing Rules and Regulations. – Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the [implementing agency] shall promulgate the necessary implementing rules and regulations, subject to approval by the Bangsamoro Parliament.
```

**Guidelines:**
- Specify timeline (typically 30-90 days)
- Identify the responsible agency/office
- Use "promulgate" for IRR
- May require consultation with stakeholders
- May require Parliament approval for sensitive matters

### 9. Appropriations Clause (if applicable)

**Format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Appropriations. – The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of [source of funds]. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
```

**Alternative when specific amount is needed:**

```
SECTION [X]. Appropriations. – The amount of [Specific Amount in Words] Pesos (₱[Amount]) is hereby appropriated from [source of funds] for the implementation of this Act. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
```

**Guidelines:**
- Include only if bill requires funding
- Specify source of funds (current appropriations, special fund, etc.)
- Address both initial and continuing funding
- Link to annual budget process

### 10. Separability Clause

**Standard format (use this):**

```
SECTION [X]. Separability Clause. – If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.
```

**Alternative format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Separability Clause. – If any part or provision of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, all other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.
```

**Guidelines:**
- MANDATORY for all bills
- Standard wording; minimal variation
- Protects remainder of law if one provision is struck down
- Usually second-to-last section

### 11. Repealing Clause

**Standard format for general repeal:**

```
SECTION [X]. Repealing Clause. – All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.
```

**Format for specific repeals:**

```
SECTION [X]. Repealing Clause. – [Specific law/provision] is hereby expressly repealed. All other laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.
```

**Example with multiple specific repeals:**

```
SECTION [X]. Repealing Clause. – The following laws are hereby expressly repealed:

(a) Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. [X];

(b) Sections [Y] to [Z] of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. [XX];

(c) All other laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations inconsistent with this Act.
```

**Guidelines:**
- MANDATORY for all bills
- Use general repealing clause for most bills
- Use specific repealing clause when expressly replacing previous legislation
- Place before effectivity clause

### 12. Effectivity Clause (Final Section)

**Standard format (15 days after publication):**

```
SECTION [X]. Effectivity. – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
```

**Immediate effectivity:**

```
SECTION [X]. Effectivity. – This Act shall take effect immediately upon its approval.
```

**Delayed effectivity (specific date):**

```
SECTION [X]. Effectivity. – This Act shall take effect on [specific date] or fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation, whichever comes later.
```

**Conditional effectivity:**

```
SECTION [X]. Effectivity. – This Act shall take effect upon its approval and after the completion of [specific condition].
```

**Guidelines:**
- MANDATORY - must be final section
- Default: 15 days after publication (per Philippine legal tradition)
- Publication required for general applicability
- Use immediate effectivity only for urgent matters
- Specify "Official Gazette or newspaper of general circulation"

## Bill Numbering System

- **Proposed Bills:** "BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. ___" (blank until enacted)
- **Enacted Bills:** "BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. [number]" (assigned upon enactment)
- Sequential numbering from BAA No. 1 onwards

## Section Numbering

**Continuous numbering throughout the bill:**
- Number sections consecutively: SECTION 1, SECTION 2, SECTION 3, etc.
- Do NOT restart numbering for each chapter or article
- Even if bill has articles and chapters, sections continue sequentially

**Example:**

```
ARTICLE I
CHAPTER I
SECTION 1. [...]

CHAPTER II
SECTION 2. [...]

ARTICLE II
CHAPTER I
SECTION 3. [...]
SECTION 4. [...]
```

## Language and Style

1. **Formal Legislative Language:** Use precise, formal language appropriate for law
2. **Active Voice:** Prefer active voice ("The Ministry shall..." rather than "Shall be done by...")
3. **Present Tense:** Use present tense for ongoing obligations ("shall," "may," "must")
4. **Mandatory vs. Permissive:**
   - "Shall" = mandatory obligation
   - "May" = permissive/discretionary
   - "Must" = mandatory (stronger than shall)
5. **Precision:** Be specific; avoid ambiguity
6. **Consistency:** Use the same terms throughout; don't vary terminology
7. **Gender-Neutral:** Use gender-neutral language where possible

## Common Formatting Elements

- **Font:** Times New Roman or similar serif font (formal documents)
- **Spacing:** Single or 1.5 line spacing
- **Margins:** Standard 1-inch margins
- **Capitalization:**
  - "SECTION" always capitalized when referring to bill sections
  - "ARTICLE" and "CHAPTER" capitalized when used as organizational headings
  - Bill title in ALL CAPS
  - Proper nouns capitalized per standard rules
  - Formal offices and titles capitalized (e.g., "Chief Minister," "Parliament")

## Drafting Principles

### Clarity and Precision

- Use clear, unambiguous language
- Define all technical terms
- Avoid legal jargon unless necessary
- Use short sentences where possible

### Consistency

- Use consistent terminology throughout
- Maintain parallel structure in similar provisions
- Use same formatting for similar elements

### Completeness

- Address all necessary aspects of the subject matter
- Include all required clauses (enacting, separability, repealing, effectivity)
- Anticipate implementation issues

### Legal Soundness

- Ensure provisions are within Parliament's legislative authority per BOL
- Cite constitutional and BOL basis in Declaration of Policy
- Avoid unconstitutional provisions
- Consider relationship with existing laws

## Optional Clauses

### Transitory Provisions

**When to include:** When transitioning from old system to new system

**Format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Transitory Provisions. – All [officials/entities/rights] existing at the time of the effectivity of this Act shall continue to [hold office/operate/be recognized] until [transition completed/new appointments made/etc.].
```

### Savings Clause

**When to include:** When repealing old law but preserving certain rights/obligations

**Format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Savings Clause. – Nothing in this Act shall affect any act done, right accrued, or penalty incurred under the provisions of the law repealed hereby, which shall be governed by the provisions of the law in force at the time of such act, right, or penalty.
```

### Non-Diminution Clause

**When to include:** When amending laws affecting employment benefits

**Format:**

```
SECTION [X]. Non-Diminution of Benefits. – Nothing in this Act shall be construed to diminish benefits and privileges already being enjoyed by [affected persons] at the time of the effectivity of this Act.
```

## Bill Organization: Choosing the Right Structure

### Use Simple Section-by-Section when:
- Bill is short (10-20 sections)
- Single, focused topic
- No complex subdivisions needed
- Straightforward implementation

**Example:** A bill creating a single office or commission

### Use Chapter Organization when:
- Bill is moderate length (20-50 sections)
- Multiple related topics to organize
- Logical groupings of provisions
- Clear topical divisions

**Example:** A bill on revenue administration with chapters on assessment, collection, enforcement

### Use Article and Chapter Organization when:
- Bill is comprehensive (50+ sections)
- Creating a code or major legislation
- Multiple major topics with subdivisions
- Complex regulatory framework

**Example:** A comprehensive Bangsamoro Local Government Code or Revenue Code
