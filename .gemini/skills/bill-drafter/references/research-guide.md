# Research Guide for Bangsamoro Parliament Bills

## Research Philosophy for Bill Drafting

**Goal:** Gather comprehensive legal, factual, and comparative information to draft well-grounded, implementable legislation. Bill drafting requires more extensive research than resolution drafting due to the permanent, legally binding nature of legislation.

**Key Principle:** Every provision in a bill should be based on verifiable legal authority, factual necessity, and practical implementability.

## Research Structure for Bills

All research for Bangsamoro Parliament bills follows an expanded four-phase approach:

**A. Legal Research** - Constitutional authority, BOL provisions, existing laws, jurisprudence
**B. Factual/Problem Research** - Data, statistics, current situation, gaps, needs assessment
**C. Comparative Research** - Best practices, similar legislation, international standards
**D. Implementation Research** - Fiscal impact, administrative capacity, stakeholder readiness

---

## A. Legal Research for Bills

Legal research establishes the constitutional and statutory foundation for the bill and ensures compliance with the legal hierarchy.

### 1. Constitutional Provisions (MANDATORY)

**When to research:** MANDATORY for all bills to establish constitutional basis
- Identify constitutional provisions related to the bill topic
- Establish constitutional framework for legislative authority
- Cite specific articles and sections granting authority to Bangsamoro Parliament
- Verify no constitutional prohibitions or limitations

**Where to search:**
- officialgazette.gov.ph - Official text of 1987 Constitution
- lawphil.net - Annotated Constitution with jurisprudence
- sc.judiciary.gov.ph - Supreme Court interpretations

**How to use:**
- Cite constitutional basis in Declaration of Policy
- Ensure bill provisions don't violate constitutional rights
- Verify Bangsamoro's authority to legislate on the subject

### 2. Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) Provisions (MANDATORY)

**When to research:** MANDATORY for all bills to cite specific BOL authority for legislative action
- Primary source of Bangsamoro legislative authority
- Must verify authority to legislate on the bill's subject matter

**What to research:**
- Article VI, Sections 2-3 - Reserved and Exclusive Powers (verify authority to legislate)
- Article VII - Parliament powers and procedures
- Subject-specific BOL provisions related to bill topic
- Limitations and restrictions on Bangsamoro authority

**Where to search:**
- Read tool: BOL reference documents
- officialgazette.gov.ph
- lawphil.net

**Critical BOL Articles by Bill Type:**

**General Legislation:**
- Article VI, Sections 2-3 - Verify power to legislate on subject

**Appropriation Bills:**
- Article IX, Sections 12-14 - Budget process and appropriations authority

**Revenue Bills:**
- Article IX, Sections 3-4 - Taxation powers and limitations

**Administrative Bills:**
- Article VIII - Organization of Bangsamoro Government
- Article VI, Section 2(44) - Power to reorganize government

**Local Government Bills:**
- Article X - Local Governance
- Article VI, Section 2(35)-(37) - LGU-related powers

**How to use:**
- Quote exact BOL provisions granting authority in Declaration of Policy
- Ensure all bill provisions fall within BOL-granted powers
- Identify any BOL limitations or requirements
- Check for BOL provisions requiring specific procedures (e.g., consultation, plebiscite)

### 3. National Laws

**When to research:** When bill relates to national legislation or shared/concurrent powers
- When bill subject involves shared/concurrent powers
- When bill supplements or coordinates with national legislation
- When bill must comply with national framework laws

**What to research:**
- Existing Republic Acts on the subject matter
- National codes (Civil Code, Labor Code, LGC, etc.)
- Presidential Decrees and Executive Orders
- Administrative regulations

**Common National Laws by Bill Type:**

**General Legislation:**
- Relevant national laws governing the subject (environmental laws, education laws, etc.)

**Appropriation Bills:**
- General Appropriations Act (annual)
- National budget process laws

**Revenue Bills:**
- National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC)
- Local Government Code (RA 7160) - LGU taxation provisions
- Tax reform laws

**Administrative Bills:**
- Civil Service Law (RA 7160, PD 807)
- Government Procurement Act (RA 9184)
- Administrative Code of 1987

**Local Government Bills:**
- Local Government Code (RA 7160) - MANDATORY reference

**Where to search:**
- officialgazette.gov.ph - National laws and issuances
- lawphil.net - Searchable Philippine laws database
- chanrobles.com - Legal research

**How to use:**
- Identify national laws that bill must comply with
- Ensure bill provisions don't conflict with national laws
- Cite national framework laws in Declaration of Policy
- Determine areas where Bangsamoro can legislate vs. areas reserved to National Government

### 4. Related Jurisprudence

**When to research:** When applicable and supports the bill
- When bill involves constitutional or legal questions previously addressed by courts
- When subject matter has Supreme Court precedents
- When bill might face legal challenges
- When legal doctrines strengthen the bill's foundation

**What to research:**
- Supreme Court decisions on relevant constitutional/legal issues
- Court of Appeals decisions (if relevant)
- Legal doctrines and principles applicable to bill subject

**Where to search:**
- sc.judiciary.gov.ph - Supreme Court decisions
- lawphil.net - Case law database
- chanrobles.com - Philippine jurisprudence

**How to use:**
- Ensure bill provisions align with judicial doctrines
- Anticipate and address potential constitutional challenges
- Cite relevant rulings in Declaration of Policy when appropriate
- Draft provisions to withstand judicial scrutiny

### 5. Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts

**When to research:** When bill relates to existing regional legislation
- To avoid duplication or conflict with existing BAAs
- When amending or repealing existing BAAs
- When implementing or supplementing existing BAAs
- To ensure consistency with regional legal framework

**What to research:**
- All existing BAAs on the subject matter
- BAA No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code) - reference for administrative matters
- Related BAAs that might be affected by new bill

**Where to search:**
- bangsamoro.gov.ph - Official gazette
- parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph - Enacted legislation
- Read tool: Available BAA documents

**How to use:**
- Review existing legal framework
- Identify gaps the bill will address
- Ensure consistency with existing BAAs
- Draft repealing clause to address conflicts
- Consider amendments to existing laws vs. new legislation

### 6. Other Legal Considerations (Optional - when applicable)

**Traditional and Customary Law:**

**When to research:**
- Bills affecting traditional governance systems
- Bills involving indigenous peoples' rights
- Bills touching on customary practices
- Shari'ah-related matters

**What to research:**
- BOL Article XIII (Traditional and Indigenous Justice Systems)
- Indigenous Peoples Rights Act (RA 8371)
- Customary law principles relevant to bill subject
- Traditional governance structures and processes

**Where to search:**
- BOL provisions
- Academic studies on traditional governance
- Indigenous peoples' organizations
- Traditional leader consultations
- Shari'ah law principles (for Islamic law matters)

**How to use:**
- Ensure compatibility with customary law
- Provide for traditional leader consultation mechanisms
- Respect indigenous peoples' rights
- Integrate traditional governance where appropriate

## B. Factual/Problem Research

Factual research establishes the empirical need for the legislation and provides data for evidence-based policymaking.

### What to Research

**Problem Statement and Current Situation:**
- Current state of affairs requiring legislation
- Specific problems, gaps, or needs
- Statistical data on the issue
- Geographic scope and affected populations
- Urgency and severity of the problem

**Impact Assessment:**
- Who will be affected by the bill? (beneficiaries, regulated entities, etc.)
- How many people/entities affected?
- Economic impact (costs, benefits, revenue implications)
- Social impact (equity, access, rights)
- Environmental impact (if applicable)

**Existing Programs and Initiatives:**
- Current government programs addressing the issue
- Non-government initiatives
- What's working and what's not
- Gaps in current responses

**Stakeholder Positions:**
- Government agencies' views
- LGU positions
- Civil society perspectives
- Private sector input
- Affected communities' voices
- Traditional leaders' views (if applicable)

### Where to Search

**Official Bangsamoro Sources:**
- bangsamoro.gov.ph - Government data and reports
- Ministry websites - Program data, statistics
- Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority - Development plans, statistics
- parliament.bangsamoro.gov.ph - Legislative studies

**National Statistical Sources:**
- Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)
- National government agency reports
- National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA)

**News and Current Events:**
- mindanews.com - Local Mindanao news
- National news outlets - Context and current issues

**Academic and Research:**
- Institute for Autonomy and Governance
- University research centers (MSU, UP Mindanao)
- International organizations (UNDP, World Bank, etc.)

**How to Use:**
- Establish factual basis for Declaration of Policy
- Quantify problem magnitude (numbers, statistics)
- Identify target beneficiaries and affected populations
- Document need for legislative intervention
- Support appropriations requests with data

## C. Comparative Research

Comparative research identifies best practices and proven legislative approaches from other jurisdictions.

### What to Research

**Similar Philippine Legislation:**
- Bills enacted by other regional autonomous governments (Cordillera, former ARMM)
- Provincial and city ordinances on similar subjects
- National laws addressing similar issues
- How other jurisdictions structured similar legislation

**International Best Practices:**
- Legislation from comparable autonomous regions globally
- International standards and conventions (if applicable)
- ASEAN member states' approaches
- Federal systems and their regional legislation

**Successful Models:**
- What worked well in other jurisdictions?
- What challenges did they face?
- How were implementation issues addressed?
- What can be adapted to Bangsamoro context?

### Where to Search

**Philippine Sources:**
- National Congress bills and laws (Senate, House of Representatives)
- LGU ordinances and resolutions
- Former ARMM legislation
- Provincial government websites

**International Sources:**
- International parliamentary databases
- ASEAN legislative resources
- Academic comparative law studies
- International organizations' legislative guides

### How to Use

- Adapt proven legislative models (don't copy verbatim)
- Learn from other jurisdictions' mistakes
- Identify implementation best practices
- Reference successful models in Declaration of Policy
- Ensure cultural and legal compatibility with Bangsamoro context

## D. Implementation Research

Implementation research assesses the practical feasibility and requirements for effective implementation of the bill.

### What to Research

**Fiscal Impact:**
- Cost of implementation (initial and ongoing)
- Revenue implications (for revenue bills)
- Source of funds (block grant, IGR, national subsidy, etc.)
- Budget availability and fiscal sustainability
- Cost-benefit analysis

**Administrative Capacity:**
- Existing agencies capable of implementing?
- Need for new office/agency?
- Personnel requirements (number, qualifications)
- Training and capacity-building needs
- Infrastructure and equipment requirements

**Implementation Timeline:**
- How long to implement?
- Phased implementation needed?
- Transitory provisions required?
- IRR development timeline

**Coordination Requirements:**
- Which agencies must coordinate?
- National-regional coordination needed?
- LGU involvement required?
- Private sector/civil society partnerships?

**Monitoring and Evaluation:**
- How will implementation be monitored?
- Performance indicators and metrics
- Reporting requirements
- Oversight mechanisms

### Where to Search

**Government Sources:**
- Ministry capacity assessments
- Budget documents
- Government organizational structures
- Civil service data

**Expert Consultations:**
- Ministry/agency officials
- Budget officials
- Implementation experts
- Technical specialists

### How to Use

- Draft realistic implementation provisions
- Include adequate appropriations
- Specify responsible implementing agencies
- Provide capacity-building measures
- Include monitoring and evaluation mechanisms
- Address transitory issues

## Research Depth: Three-Tiered Approach for Bills

### Tier 1: Foundation Research (Simple Bills)

**When to use:** Simple, straightforward bills with limited scope

**Research Scope:**
- Basic BOL authority verification
- Existing laws review (quick scan)
- Basic problem statement and data (3-5 key facts)
- Simple implementation assessment
- Brief comparative check (if readily available)

**Example:** Bill creating a simple advisory body or small office

### Tier 2: Standard Research (Most Bills)

**When to use:** Standard bills requiring comprehensive foundation **[DEFAULT]**

**Research Scope:**
- Thorough BOL and constitutional review
- Comprehensive existing laws review (national and BAAs)
- Detailed factual research (10+ data points, multiple sources)
- Stakeholder positions identified
- Comparative research (2-3 similar laws from other jurisdictions)
- Detailed implementation assessment (fiscal, administrative, timeline)

**Example:** Most general legislation, administrative bills, revenue measures

### Tier 3: Comprehensive Research (Complex/Major Bills)

**When to use:** Major legislation, codes, controversial matters, high fiscal impact

**Research Scope:**
- Exhaustive legal research (Constitution, BOL, all relevant laws, jurisprudence)
- Extensive factual research (multiple data sources, impact studies)
- Broad stakeholder consultation and analysis
- Comprehensive comparative research (multiple jurisdictions, international standards)
- Detailed implementation research (full fiscal impact analysis, capacity assessment)
- Traditional/customary law integration (if applicable)
- Expert consultations

**Example:** Revenue Code, Local Government Code, major reorganization bills

## Research Workflow for Bills

### Phase 1: Initial Assessment (1-2 hours)

**Questions to answer:**
1. Does Bangsamoro have authority to legislate on this? (Check BOL Article VI)
2. Are there existing laws (national or BAA) on this subject?
3. What's the basic problem this bill addresses?
4. What research tier is appropriate?

**Tools:** Quick WebSearch, BOL review (Read tool)

### Phase 2: Legal Foundation Research (2-4 hours)

**Tasks:**
1. Research constitutional provisions related to subject
2. Identify relevant BOL articles and sections (quote exact provisions)
3. Review existing national laws on subject
4. Check existing BAAs for conflicts or gaps
5. Research relevant jurisprudence (if applicable)
6. Review traditional/customary law considerations (if applicable)

**Tools:** WebFetch (officialgazette.gov.ph, lawphil.net), Read tool (BOL, BAAs)

**Output:** Legal research memo with citations ready for Declaration of Policy

### Phase 3: Factual/Problem Research (2-6 hours depending on tier)

**Tasks:**
1. Gather statistics and data on the problem
2. Identify affected populations and stakeholders
3. Document current programs and gaps
4. Research stakeholder positions
5. Assess urgency and severity

**Tools:** WebSearch, WebFetch (bangsamoro.gov.ph, mindanews.com, PSA)

**Output:** Factual research memo supporting legislative need

### Phase 4: Comparative Research (1-3 hours depending on tier)

**Tasks:**
1. Find similar Philippine laws (national, LGU, other regions)
2. Research international best practices (if applicable)
3. Identify successful models and lessons learned
4. Adapt approaches to Bangsamoro context

**Tools:** WebSearch, WebFetch (Congress websites, international sources)

**Output:** Comparative analysis with adaptable provisions

### Phase 5: Implementation Research (2-4 hours depending on tier)

**Tasks:**
1. Assess fiscal impact and funding sources
2. Evaluate administrative capacity for implementation
3. Identify coordination requirements
4. Determine implementation timeline
5. Plan monitoring and evaluation mechanisms

**Tools:** WebFetch (government agency sites), stakeholder consultations

**Output:** Implementation plan informing bill provisions

## Research Quality Standards for Bills

**Minimum Requirements:**
- [ ] BOL authority clearly identified with exact article/section citations
- [ ] Constitutional compliance verified
- [ ] All existing relevant laws reviewed (national and BAAs)
- [ ] At least 5-10 specific facts/data points supporting need for bill
- [ ] At least 2-3 comparative examples from other jurisdictions
- [ ] Fiscal impact estimated
- [ ] Implementation agency/mechanism identified
- [ ] Stakeholder positions considered

**Higher Standards for Complex Bills:**
- [ ] Jurisprudence review completed
- [ ] Comprehensive data and impact studies
- [ ] Extensive stakeholder consultation documented
- [ ] Detailed fiscal impact analysis
- [ ] Capacity assessment of implementing agencies
- [ ] Phased implementation plan developed

## Research Documentation

**Maintain research file with:**
- Legal citations (Constitution, BOL, national laws, BAAs, cases)
- Factual data sources (statistics, reports, studies)
- Comparative examples (other laws, jurisdictions)
- Stakeholder input (positions, recommendations)
- Fiscal and implementation notes

**Why document research:**
- Supports Declaration of Policy drafting
- Provides citations for bill provisions
- Enables fact-checking and verification
- Assists in committee hearings and deliberations
- Justifies appropriations requests
- Demonstrates evidence-based policymaking

## Special Research Considerations for Bangsamoro Bills

### Traditional Governance Integration

**Research required:**
- BOL Article XIII provisions
- Traditional governance structures in affected areas
- Customary law principles
- Traditional leader roles and consultation processes

**When applicable:**
- Bills affecting indigenous peoples
- Local governance matters
- Land and resource management
- Dispute resolution systems

### Islamic Law Considerations

**Research required:**
- BOL provisions on Shari'ah
- Islamic jurisprudence principles
- Shari'ah court system
- Islamic scholars' perspectives

**When applicable:**
- Bills affecting Shari'ah matters
- Personal law (family law, inheritance, etc.)
- Islamic institutions
- Halal regulation

### Regional Context

**Consider:**
- BARMM's peace process context
- Post-conflict development needs
- Inter-ethnic and inter-religious dynamics
- Economic development priorities
- Security and stability implications
