# Bangsamoro Parliament Bill Format Guide

## Formatting Standards for Bills

This guide provides detailed formatting requirements for Bangsamoro Parliament bills. For comprehensive legal citation standards and general formatting principles, also refer to the resolution-writer's format-guide.md which contains shared formatting conventions.

## Bill-Specific Formatting Requirements

### Header Format

```
Republic of the Philippines
BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT
Cotabato City

BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. ___
```

**Rules:**
- "Republic of the Philippines" - centered
- "BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT" - all caps, centered
- "Cotabato City" - centered
- Blank line
- "BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. ___" - all caps, centered, blank until enacted

### Title Format

```
AN ACT [DESCRIBING THE MAIN PURPOSE OF THE BILL]
```

**Rules:**
- ALL CAPS
- Begins with "AN ACT"
- Descriptive and comprehensive
- May span multiple lines if long
- Centered or left-aligned depending on length

**Examples:**

```
AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO REVENUE CODE, PROVIDING FOR THE ASSESSMENT AND COLLECTION OF TAXES, FEES, AND CHARGES IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES
```

```
AN ACT CREATING THE BANGSAMORO TOURISM AUTHORITY, DEFINING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES
```

### Authorship Format

```
Authored by: MP [Full Name]
Co-authored by: MP [Full Name], MP [Full Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Same as resolution format
- Include "MP" before names
- Separate multiple authors with commas

### Enacting Clause Format

```
Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:
```

**Rules:**
- MANDATORY for all bills
- Appears immediately before Section 1
- Standard wording - do not modify
- Not numbered as a section

### Section Format

**Standard section:**

```
SECTION [number]. [Section Title]. – [Content of the section].
```

**Examples:**

```
SECTION 1. Short Title. – This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Revenue Code of 2025."

SECTION 5. Powers and Functions. – The Authority shall have the following powers and functions:

(a) [First power/function];

(b) [Second power/function];

(c) [Third power/function].
```

**Rules:**
- "SECTION" always in all caps
- Section number followed by period
- Section title followed by period
- Em dash (–) separates title from content
- Content starts with capital letter
- End with period

### Subsection Format

**Multi-level subsections:**

```
SECTION 10. Tax Rates. – The following tax rates shall apply:

(a) For individuals:

(1) Income up to One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000): Five percent (5%);

(2) Income over One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000): Ten percent (10%);

(i) With additional surcharge of two percent (2%) for late payment;

(ii) Plus interest of one percent (1%) per month;

(b) For corporations:

(1) Domestic corporations: Fifteen percent (15%);

(2) Foreign corporations: Twenty percent (20%).
```

**Subsection hierarchy:**
- First level: (a), (b), (c) - lowercase letters in parentheses
- Second level: (1), (2), (3) - numbers in parentheses
- Third level: (i), (ii), (iii) - lowercase Roman numerals in parentheses
- Fourth level: (A), (B), (C) - uppercase letters in parentheses (rarely used)

**Punctuation:**
- Each subsection item ends with semicolon (;)
- Final item in a series ends with period (.)
- Last subitem at any level ends with period

### Chapter and Article Format

**Chapter headings:**

```
CHAPTER I
GENERAL PROVISIONS
```

**Article headings:**

```
ARTICLE I
GENERAL PROVISIONS
```

**Rules:**
- ALL CAPS
- Roman numerals for numbering
- Centered or left-aligned
- No punctuation
- Blank line before and after

**Example of combined structure:**

```
ARTICLE I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I
SHORT TITLE AND DECLARATION OF POLICY

SECTION 1. Short Title. – [...]

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. – [...]

CHAPTER II
DEFINITION OF TERMS

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. – [...]

ARTICLE II
SUBSTANTIVE PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I
[TOPIC]

SECTION 4. [Title]. – [...]
```

### Number Format

**Writing numbers in bills:**

**Spell out AND use numerals in parentheses:**

```
One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000)
Fifteen percent (15%)
Ninety (90) days
Five (5) members
```

**Rules:**
- Spell out the number in words
- Follow with numeral in parentheses
- Use peso sign (₱) for currency
- Use percent sign (%) for percentages
- Capitalize first letter of spelled-out number

**Ordinal numbers:**

```
First (1st) reading
Second (2nd) reading
Third (3rd) reading
Twenty-first (21st) day
```

**Days/Timelines:**

```
Within ninety (90) days from effectivity
Not less than fifteen (15) days before
At least thirty (30) days prior to
```

### Currency Format

**Pesos:**

```
One Million Pesos (₱1,000,000)
Five Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱500,000)
Ten Thousand Pesos (₱10,000)
```

**Rules:**
- Spell out in words first
- Follow with peso sign and numerals in parentheses
- Use commas for thousands
- Always include "Pesos" in the spelled-out version

### Percentage Format

```
Five percent (5%)
Ten percent (10%)
Twenty-five percent (25%)
```

**Rules:**
- Spell out in words first
- Follow with numeral and percent sign in parentheses
- No space between numeral and %

### Date Format

**Specific dates:**

```
January 1, 2025
December 31, 2024
```

**Relative dates:**

```
Within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act
Not later than fifteen (15) days after approval
At least thirty (30) days before the end of the fiscal year
```

### Mandatory Clauses Format

**Implementing Rules and Regulations:**

```
SECTION [X]. Implementing Rules and Regulations. – Within [number] days from the effectivity of this Act, the [implementing agency] shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.
```

**Appropriations Clause (if applicable):**

```
SECTION [X]. Appropriations. – The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of [source]. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
```

**Separability Clause:**

```
SECTION [X]. Separability Clause. – If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.
```

**Repealing Clause:**

```
SECTION [X]. Repealing Clause. – All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.
```

**Effectivity Clause (FINAL SECTION):**

```
SECTION [X]. Effectivity. – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
```

## Capitalization Rules

### Always Capitalize:
- "SECTION" when referring to bill sections
- "ARTICLE" and "CHAPTER" when used as organizational headings
- Bill title (ALL CAPS)
- Formal government offices and titles: "Chief Minister," "Parliament," "Ministry"
- Proper nouns: "Bangsamoro," "Philippines"
- Beginning of sentences and subsections

### Do Not Capitalize:
- General references: "the chief minister stated," "parliamentary session"
- Generic terms: "the ministry," "the office"
- Common nouns unless part of official title

## Punctuation Rules

### Semicolons:
- End each subsection item except the last
- End each item in a series

### Periods:
- End the final item in any series
- End sections and subsections
- After "SECTION [number]" and section titles

### Commas:
- Standard grammar rules apply
- Separate items in a list (within text)
- After "WHEREAS" (in preambles, if included)

### Em Dash (–):
- Separates section title from section content
- Format: `Section Title. – Content`

### Parentheses:
- Enclose numerical equivalents: "ninety (90) days"
- Enclose subsection markers: (a), (1), (i)

## Language and Style

### Legislative Drafting Principles:

1. **Clarity:** Use clear, precise language; avoid ambiguity
2. **Brevity:** Be concise; avoid unnecessary words
3. **Consistency:** Use the same terms throughout; don't vary terminology
4. **Formality:** Use formal legislative language
5. **Precision:** Be specific; avoid vague terms

### Mandatory vs. Permissive Language:

**"Shall" = Mandatory obligation:**

```
The Ministry shall submit an annual report.
All taxpayers shall file returns by the deadline.
```

**"May" = Permissive/Discretionary:**

```
The Chief Minister may appoint additional members.
The Authority may enter into partnerships.
```

**"Must" = Mandatory (stronger than shall):**

```
Applicants must meet all qualifications.
```

### Active Voice Preferred:

**Preferred:**
```
The Ministry shall implement the program.
The Commissioner shall assess the tax.
```

**Avoid:**
```
The program shall be implemented by the Ministry.
The tax shall be assessed by the Commissioner.
```

### Gender-Neutral Language:

**Use:**
- "they/their" for singular generic references
- "person" instead of "he" or "she"
- "chairperson" instead of "chairman"
- "office holder" or "official" instead of "officeholder"

## Citation Format

**Constitutional provisions:**

```
Section 15, Article X of the 1987 Philippine Constitution
```

**BOL provisions:**

```
Article VI, Section 2 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054)
```

**National laws:**

```
Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law
Section 5, Republic Act No. 7160 (Local Government Code of 1991)
```

**Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts:**

```
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13
Section 10 of BAA No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code)
```

**Internal cross-references within the bill:**

```
As provided in Section 5 hereof
Under Section 10 of this Act
Pursuant to the provisions of Article II of this Act
```

## Common Phrases and Formulations

**Powers and Functions:**

```
The [Office/Agency] shall have the following powers and functions:
```

**Qualifications:**

```
To qualify for [position], a person must:
```

**Prohibitions:**

```
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
No person shall:
```

**Penalties:**

```
Any person who violates this Act shall be punished by [penalty].
```

**Definitions:**

```
As used in this Act, the following terms shall mean:
```

**Effectivity dates:**

```
This Act shall take effect [timeframe] after its publication in [publication venue].
```

## Document Presentation

**For formal submission/printing:**
- Font: Times New Roman, 12pt
- Line spacing: 1.5 or double
- Margins: 1 inch all sides
- Page numbers: bottom center or top right
- Header: May include bill number on subsequent pages

**For working drafts:**
- Clear formatting that's easy to read and edit
- Consistent indentation
- Clear visual hierarchy
