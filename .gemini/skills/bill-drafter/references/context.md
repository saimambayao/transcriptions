## Bill Drafting-Specific Context

### Legislative Authority Framework

**Bangsamoro Parliament's legislative powers are defined by:**

1. **Constitution (1987)** - Article X: Autonomous regions
2. **Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054)** - Primary source of authority
   - Article VI: Powers of the Bangsamoro Government
   - Article VII: The Bangsamoro Parliament
3. **AR 268 (BTA House Rules)** - Parliamentary procedures

### Key BOL Provisions for Bill Drafting

**Article VI - Powers of the Bangsamoro Government**

**Section 2: Reserved Powers** - Areas where Bangsamoro has full legislative authority:
- Examples: Education, health, social services, agriculture, tourism, trade and industry, environment

**Section 3: Exclusive Powers** - Powers shared with National Government where Bangsamoro has primary authority:
- Examples: Local government, customary law, traditional institutions

**Section 4: Concurrent Powers** - Shared with National Government:
- Examples: Energy development, scientific and technological services

**Article VII - The Bangsamoro Parliament**

**Section 1** - Legislative power vested in Parliament
**Section 5** - Rules of procedure
**Section 8** - Financial bills originate in Parliament
**Section 10** - Power of inquiry in aid of legislation

**Article IX - Fiscal Autonomy and Economic Development**

**Section 3** - Taxation powers
**Section 4** - Limitations on taxation (no national internal revenue tax duplication)
**Sections 12-14** - Budget and appropriations process

**Article X - Local Governance**

Provisions on relationship between regional government and LGUs

## Terminology Specific to Bill Drafting

**"This Act"** - Refers to the bill being drafted (used throughout the bill)

**"Herein," "hereof," "hereby"** - Formal legislative language
- "herein" = in this Act
- "hereof" = of this Act
- "hereby" = by means of this Act

**"Promulgate"** - Issue or declare officially (used for IRRs)

**"In session assembled"** - Standard phrase in enacting clause

**"Appropriated"** - Officially allocated funds

**"Charged against"** - Paid from (specific funding source)

## Cultural Sensitivity in Bill Drafting

**When drafting bills affecting:**

**Indigenous Peoples:**
- Ensure compliance with Indigenous Peoples Rights Act (RA 8371)
- Provide for Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC) where required
- Respect ancestral domains and customary law
- Consult with indigenous peoples' organizations

**Traditional Governance:**
- Integrate traditional governance structures where appropriate
- Respect customary law and practices
- Provide for traditional leader consultation
- Ensure compatibility with BOL Article XIII

**Islamic Law and Customs:**
- Ensure compatibility with Shari'ah principles (where applicable)
- Coordinate with Shari'ah justice system
- Respect Islamic institutions and practices
- Consider Halal requirements for relevant legislation

**Inter-ethnic and Inter-religious Harmony:**
- Draft inclusive legislation respecting diversity
- Avoid provisions that could marginalize any group
- Promote unity while respecting diversity

## Legal Hierarchy for Bill Drafting

When drafting bills, ensure compliance with this legal hierarchy:

1. **Philippine Constitution (1987)** - Supreme law
2. **Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054)** - Fundamental law for BARMM
3. **National Laws** - Republic Acts, Presidential Decrees (within concurrent/shared powers)
4. **Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts** - Regional legislation
5. **Executive Issuances** - BOL-authorized executive orders, administrative orders
6. **Implementing Rules and Regulations** - Agency regulations
7. **Local Ordinances** - LGU legislation (within their jurisdiction)

Bills must not conflict with higher-ranking laws but can supplement or implement them within Bangsamoro's granted authority.

## Bill Approval Process (Overview)

1. **Filing** - MPs file bills with the Secretariat
2. **First Reading** - Bill number assigned, referred to committee
3. **Committee Deliberation** - Committee study, public hearings
4. **Second Reading** - Period of amendments, debate
5. **Third Reading** - Final vote (no amendments)
6. **Submission to Chief Minister** - For approval
7. **Executive Action** - Chief Minister approves or vetoes
8. **Publication** - In Official Gazette or newspaper
9. **Effectivity** - Per effectivity clause (usually 15 days after publication)

**Bill drafting should anticipate:**
- Committee scrutiny and questions
- Need for amendments during deliberations
- Implementation requirements after enactment

## Common Legislative Challenges in BARMM

**Awareness during bill drafting:**

1. **Fiscal Constraints** - Limited budget; bills must be fiscally realistic
2. **Capacity Limitations** - New bureaucracy; implementation capacity varies
3. **Coordination Complexity** - Multiple agencies, national-regional coordination
4. **Traditional Governance Integration** - Balance formal law with customary practices
5. **Post-Conflict Context** - Peace dividends, normalization needs
6. **Diverse Population** - Multiple ethnic groups, religions; inclusive legislation needed

**Draft bills that:**
- Are implementable with existing capacity or provide capacity-building
- Are fiscally sustainable
- Coordinate effectively across agencies
- Respect traditional governance and cultural diversity
- Contribute to peace and development goals
