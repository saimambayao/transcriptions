# Bangsamoro Parliament Bill Types Guide

## Overview

All bills enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament become **Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts (BAAs)**. However, bills can be classified by their purpose and content to help guide drafting approaches.

## Bill Classification by Purpose

### 1. General Legislation / Policy Bills

**Purpose:** Establish policies, create programs, regulate activities, or address substantive governance matters within Bangsamoro's legislative authority.

**Characteristics:**
- Most common type of bill
- Broad policy-making function
- Creates rights, obligations, or regulatory frameworks
- May establish offices, commissions, or implementing agencies
- Subject to BOL exclusive and reserved powers

**Common Examples:**
- Environmental protection and natural resource management
- Social services and welfare programs
- Education and cultural preservation
- Trade and industry regulation
- Health services and public health
- Agriculture and fisheries development
- Tourism development
- Housing and urban development

**BOL Authority:** Article VI, Sections 2-3 (Reserved and Exclusive Powers)

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Must clearly articulate policy objectives in Declaration of Policy
- Define implementation mechanisms and responsible agencies
- Include enforcement and penalty provisions if regulatory
- Ensure consistency with national laws and BOL

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Establishing the Bangsamoro Environmental Protection Code"
- "An Act Creating the Bangsamoro Commission on Indigenous Peoples"
- "An Act Promoting Halal Industry Development in the BARMM"

---

### 2. Appropriation / Budget Bills

**Purpose:** Authorize government expenditures and allocate funds for the operation of government and delivery of services.

**Characteristics:**
- Authorizes spending of public funds
- Based on annual or supplemental appropriations
- Requires budget message from Chief Minister
- Subject to fiscal rules and BOL budget process
- Parliament has power of the purse

**Types of Appropriation Bills:**

**A. General Appropriations Act (Annual Budget)**
- Comprehensive annual budget
- Covers all government operations
- Submitted by Chief Minister
- Required per BOL Article IX

**B. Supplemental Appropriations**
- Additional funding during fiscal year
- For urgent or unforeseen expenditures
- Requires justification of urgency

**C. Special Appropriations**
- Funding for specific programs or projects
- Time-bound or project-specific
- May be standalone legislation

**BOL Authority:** Article IX, Sections 12-14 (Budget and Appropriations)

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Specify exact amounts and sources of funds
- Detail budget allocation by ministry/office/program
- Include fiscal responsibility provisions
- Link to revenue sources
- Address monitoring and accountability mechanisms
- Consider multi-year implications

**Key Sections to Include:**
- Appropriation amounts by office/program
- Sources of funds (block grant, IGR, grants, loans)
- Budget execution authority
- Limitations and restrictions on use
- Reporting requirements
- Validity period

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Appropriating Funds for the Operations of the Bangsamoro Government for Fiscal Year 2026"
- "An Act Providing Supplemental Appropriations for Disaster Response and Rehabilitation"

---

### 3. Revenue / Tax Bills

**Purpose:** Impose, modify, or abolish taxes, fees, charges, and other revenue-generating measures to fund government operations.

**Characteristics:**
- Exercises fiscal autonomy and taxation powers
- Must originate from Parliament (not initiative/referendum per BOL)
- Subject to limitations in BOL Article IX
- Must not conflict with national internal revenue laws
- Balanced with economic development goals

**Types of Revenue Measures:**

**A. Tax Imposition Bills**
- Create new taxes or modify existing tax rates
- Real property tax, business tax, professional tax, etc.
- Within Bangsamoro's taxation powers per BOL

**B. Revenue Code / Tax Code Bills**
- Comprehensive codification of tax laws
- Systematic organization of revenue measures
- Includes definitions, assessment, collection, enforcement

**C. Fee and Charge Bills**
- User fees for government services
- Regulatory fees and permits
- Administrative charges

**BOL Authority:**
- Article IX, Section 3 (Taxation powers)
- Article IX, Section 4 (Limitations on taxation)
- Article VI, Section 2(48) (Power to levy taxes, fees, charges)

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Define tax base, taxable persons, and exemptions clearly
- Specify tax rates and computation methods
- Include assessment and collection procedures
- Provide penalties for non-compliance
- Address enforcement and administrative remedies
- Ensure compliance with national tax laws
- Consider economic impact and equity
- Include transitory provisions for existing taxpayers

**Key Sections to Include:**
- Coverage and scope
- Definition of taxpayers and tax base
- Tax rates and computation
- Filing and payment procedures
- Exemptions and exclusions
- Assessment and collection
- Penalties and enforcement
- Administrative remedies
- Tax credit and refund procedures

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Establishing the Bangsamoro Revenue Code"
- "An Act Imposing Real Property Tax in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region"
- "An Act Providing for a Business Permit and Licensing System"

---

### 4. Administrative / Organizational Bills

**Purpose:** Establish, reorganize, or abolish government offices, agencies, and administrative structures.

**Characteristics:**
- Organizes executive branch structure
- Creates ministries, offices, commissions, authorities
- Defines mandates and functions
- Prescribes organizational structure and staffing
- May involve civil service and personnel matters

**Types of Administrative Bills:**

**A. Office/Agency Creation Bills**
- Establish new government offices
- Define mandate, powers, and functions
- Provide organizational structure
- Authorize positions and appropriations

**B. Reorganization Bills**
- Restructure existing offices
- Merge or abolish agencies
- Rationalize government structure
- May include transitory provisions for personnel

**C. Administrative Code / Governance Bills**
- Comprehensive administrative procedures
- Government-wide regulations
- Civil service rules
- Procurement and financial management

**BOL Authority:**
- Article VI, Section 2(44) (Reorganize regional government structure)
- Article VIII (Organization of Bangsamoro Government)
- Various BOL provisions on specific offices

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Clearly define mandate and powers
- Specify organizational structure (divisions, units, positions)
- Address funding sources and sustainability
- Include qualification requirements for key positions
- Provide appointment and selection processes
- Consider overlap with existing agencies
- Include transitory provisions for personnel
- Address assets, liabilities, records transfer

**Key Sections to Include:**
- Creation and mandate
- Powers and functions
- Organizational structure
- Governing body or head of office
- Qualifications and appointment process
- Personnel complement and compensation
- Funding and appropriations
- Relationship with other agencies
- Transitory provisions

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Creating the Bangsamoro Tourism Authority"
- "An Act Establishing the Office of the Bangsamoro Ombudsman"
- "An Act Reorganizing the Bangsamoro Ministry of the Interior and Local Government"

---

### 5. Local Government Bills

**Purpose:** Legislate matters concerning local government units within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Characteristics:**
- Governs provinces, cities, municipalities, barangays in BARMM
- Supplements national Local Government Code
- Addresses regional-local relationships
- Respects local autonomy while ensuring regional coordination

**Common Local Government Legislation:**

**A. Local Government Code**
- Comprehensive framework for LGUs in BARMM
- Powers, functions, and organization
- Fiscal management and local taxation
- Intergovernmental relations

**B. Municipal/City Creation or Conversion Bills**
- Create new municipalities or cities
- Convert municipality to city
- Subject to BOL requirements and plebiscite

**C. Redistricting Bills**
- Redraw provincial/parliamentary district boundaries
- Subject to constitutional and BOL requirements

**D. LGU Powers and Functions Bills**
- Devolution of specific functions to LGUs
- Shared governance arrangements
- Capacity-building measures

**BOL Authority:**
- Article VI, Section 2(35)-(37) (Local government matters)
- Article X (Local Governance)

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Respect LGU autonomy
- Define regional-local relationships clearly
- Ensure consistency with national LGC
- Address funding and fiscal decentralization
- Consider local capacity to implement
- Provide capacity-building support
- Balance regional interests with local needs

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Providing for a Bangsamoro Local Government Code"
- "An Act Creating the City of [Name] in the Province of [Province]"
- "An Act Devolving Health Services to Local Government Units in the BARMM"

---

### 6. Code / Consolidation Bills

**Purpose:** Comprehensively organize and codify all laws on a specific subject matter into a single, systematic code.

**Characteristics:**
- Consolidates scattered laws into unified code
- Organizes provisions systematically
- May include amendments and harmonization
- Creates definitive reference for a subject area
- Often very lengthy and complex

**Common Types of Codes:**

**A. Administrative Code**
- Government-wide administrative procedures
- Civil service rules
- Procurement and financial management
- Already exists: BAA No. 13

**B. Revenue/Tax Code**
- All taxation and revenue measures
- Systematic organization by tax type
- Assessment, collection, enforcement provisions

**C. Local Government Code**
- Comprehensive LGU governance framework
- Powers, functions, fiscal systems

**D. Subject-Specific Codes**
- Education Code
- Health Code
- Environmental Code
- Agriculture Code

**Drafting Considerations:**
- Extensive research on existing laws required
- Organize systematically (articles, chapters, sections)
- Harmonize conflicting provisions
- Update outdated provisions
- Include comprehensive definitions
- Provide clear repealing clause for superseded laws
- Very lengthy drafting process
- Stakeholder consultation essential

**Structure:**
- Use Article and Chapter organization
- Comprehensive definitions section
- Logical progression from general to specific
- Clear cross-references
- Detailed table of contents

**Example Topics:**
- "An Act Establishing the Bangsamoro Revenue Code"
- "An Act Providing for a Bangsamoro Education Code"

## Special Considerations for Bangsamoro Bills

### Legislative Authority Limits

**Bills must be within Bangsamoro's legislative authority per BOL:**

**Reserved Powers (Article VI, Section 2):** Powers the Bangsamoro Government has full authority over, subject only to the Constitution and BOL.

**Exclusive Powers (Article VI, Section 3):** Powers shared with the National Government but where Bangsamoro has primary authority.

**Prohibited Areas:**
- National defense and security
- Foreign affairs
- National currency and banking
- Immigration and naturalization
- Other matters reserved to National Government

**Always verify:** Does the proposed bill fall within BOL-granted powers?

### Relationship with National Laws

**Bangsamoro bills must:**
- Not conflict with the Constitution
- Not conflict with the BOL
- Respect national laws in areas of concurrent/shared jurisdiction
- Coordinate with national agencies where required

**In areas of shared jurisdiction:**
- Bangsamoro laws prevail within the region (subject to BOL provisions)
- Must coordinate with national framework laws

### Traditional Governance Integration

**When bills affect traditional governance or indigenous peoples:**
- Consult BOL Article XIII (Traditional and Indigenous Justice Systems)
- Ensure compatibility with customary law
- Provide for traditional leader consultation
- Respect indigenous peoples' rights

### Islamic Law Considerations

**When bills affect Shari'ah matters:**
- Consult BOL provisions on Shari'ah
- Ensure compatibility with Islamic principles
- Coordinate with Shari'ah justice system
- May require consultation with Islamic scholars

## Bill Selection and Drafting Approach

### Step 1: Identify Bill Purpose

What is the primary purpose of this legislation?
- Establishing policy? → General Legislation
- Funding programs? → Appropriation Bill
- Generating revenue? → Revenue Bill
- Creating offices? → Administrative Bill
- Governing LGUs? → Local Government Bill
- Codifying laws? → Code Bill

### Step 2: Verify Legislative Authority

Does the Bangsamoro Parliament have authority to legislate on this matter per BOL?
- Check Article VI (Powers of the Bangsamoro Government)
- Verify not prohibited or reserved to National Government
- Consider concurrent/shared powers with National Government

### Step 3: Choose Appropriate Structure

Based on length and complexity:
- Simple topic (10-20 sections) → Section-by-section
- Moderate topic (20-50 sections) → Chapter organization
- Complex topic (50+ sections) → Article and Chapter organization
- Comprehensive code (100+ sections) → Multi-article structure

### Step 4: Research Requirements

Based on bill type:
- All bills → BOL authority, constitutional compliance
- Revenue bills → Economic impact, existing tax laws
- Appropriation bills → Fiscal capacity, budget process
- Administrative bills → Existing structures, personnel implications
- Local government bills → LGU capacity, devolution readiness
- Codes → All existing laws on subject, harmonization needs
