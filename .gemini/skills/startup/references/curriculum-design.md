## Foundation Course

### Startup Fundamentals (8-16 hours)

**Target Audience:** Aspiring entrepreneurs, students, career changers

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Define what a startup is and isn't
2. Identify problems worth solving
3. Describe the Lean Startup approach
4. Create a basic Lean Canvas
5. Conduct problem interviews
6. Evaluate if they should pursue entrepreneurship

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Entrepreneurial Mindset** | What is entrepreneurship? Myths vs. reality. Risk and uncertainty. Self-assessment. | 2 hours |
| **2. Finding Problems** | Problem discovery techniques. Jobs-to-be-Done. Observation. | 2 hours |
| **3. Validating Problems** | Problem interviews. Customer discovery basics. Documenting insights. | 3 hours |
| **4. Introduction to Lean Startup** | Build-Measure-Learn. MVPs. Pivoting. | 2 hours |
| **5. Business Model Basics** | Lean Canvas introduction. Value proposition. Customer segments. | 3 hours |
| **6. Next Steps** | Resources. Support ecosystem. Action planning. | 2 hours |

### Assessments
- Problem statement pitch (2 minutes)
- Lean Canvas draft
- Problem interview report (5 interviews)

## Intermediate Course

### Startup Validation (24-40 hours)

**Target Audience:** Early-stage founders with an idea

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Conduct comprehensive customer discovery
2. Design and build MVPs
3. Set up startup metrics tracking
4. Make data-driven pivot decisions
5. Build and manage an early team
6. Bootstrap or raise early funding

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Customer Development Deep Dive** | Four steps. Problem interviews. Solution interviews. Iteration. | 4 hours |
| **2. Value Proposition Design** | Value prop canvas. Fit. Messaging. Positioning. | 3 hours |
| **3. MVP Development** | MVP types. Experiment design. Build strategies. No-code tools. | 6 hours |
| **4. Startup Metrics** | AARRR framework. Cohort analysis. Leading vs. lagging indicators. | 4 hours |
| **5. Pivoting & Persevering** | Pivot types. When to pivot. How to pivot. Pivot case studies. | 3 hours |
| **6. Early Team Building** | Cofounder dynamics. First hires. Culture foundations. | 3 hours |
| **7. Bootstrapping** | Lean operations. Cash management. Revenue-first strategies. | 3 hours |
| **8. Early Funding** | Funding options. Pitch basics. Investor relations. | 4 hours |
| **9. Legal & Operations** | Entity formation. Contracts. IP basics. Compliance. | 3 hours |
| **10. Action Planning** | 90-day plan. Milestones. Accountability. | 3 hours |

### Assessments
- Customer discovery report (20+ interviews)
- MVP demonstration
- Metrics dashboard setup
- Pitch deck (3 minutes)
- 90-day action plan

## Advanced Course

### Startup Growth (40+ hours)

**Target Audience:** Founders with early traction seeking PMF and scale

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:
1. Identify and achieve product-market fit
2. Design and execute growth strategies
3. Raise Series A/B funding
4. Build scalable operations
5. Lead and develop a growing team
6. Prepare for transition to enterprise operations

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Product-Market Fit** | Defining PMF. Sean Ellis test. Retention curves. PMF signals. | 4 hours |
| **2. Growth Frameworks** | Growth loops. Flywheels. Network effects. Viral mechanics. | 4 hours |
| **3. Traction Channels** | 19 channels. Bullseye framework. Channel testing. Optimization. | 6 hours |
| **4. Growth Marketing** | Acquisition. Activation. Retention. Revenue. Referral. | 6 hours |
| **5. Fundraising Strategy** | Series A/B preparation. Due diligence. Term sheets. Negotiation. | 4 hours |
| **6. Scaling Operations** | Systems thinking. Process documentation. Automation. Quality. | 4 hours |
| **7. Leadership & Culture** | Wartime/peacetime CEO. Hiring at scale. Culture preservation. | 4 hours |
| **8. Financial Management** | Unit economics. Cash runway. Financial modeling. Board reporting. | 4 hours |
| **9. Strategic Planning** | OKRs. Quarterly planning. Execution rhythms. | 3 hours |
| **10. Transition to Scale** | When to transition. /enterprise-dev handoff. Exit considerations. | 3 hours |

### Assessments
- PMF analysis with evidence
- Growth strategy with channel experiments
- Financial model (3-year projection)
- Series A pitch deck
- Scaling plan document

**Target Audience:** Cooperative officers, SE leaders, corporate intrapreneurs

**Learning Objectives:**
By end of course, participants can:

2. Lead innovation initiatives
3. Balance innovation with core operations
4. Build internal startup culture
5. Manage new venture portfolios

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Innovation in Organizations** | Why innovate? Types of innovation. Innovation barriers. | 3 hours |
| **2. Lean Startup for Intrapreneurs** | Adapting Lean. Internal MVPs. Stakeholder management. | 4 hours |
| **3. Cooperative Innovation** | Democratic innovation. Member-driven R&D. Pilot programs. | 3 hours |
| **4. SE Innovation** | Mission-aligned innovation. Impact experiments. | 3 hours |
| **5. Innovation Culture** | Psychological safety. Experimentation culture. Failure tolerance. | 3 hours |
| **6. Portfolio Management** | Innovation portfolios. Resource allocation. Kill criteria. | 3 hours |
| **7. Action Planning** | Innovation roadmap. Quick wins. Change management. | 3 hours |

### Assessments
- Innovation opportunity assessment
- Lean experiment proposal
- Innovation portfolio framework
- Change management plan

## Assessment Methods

### Assessment Types

| Type | Purpose | Weight |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Problem Statement Pitch** | Validate problem understanding | 15% |
| **Customer Discovery Report** | Demonstrate research skills | 20% |
| **MVP Demonstration** | Show building capability | 20% |
| **Pitch Deck** | Communication skills | 20% |
| **Action Plan** | Strategic thinking | 15% |
| **Participation** | Engagement and collaboration | 10% |

### Rubrics

**Problem Statement Pitch Rubric:**

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Problem clarity** | Crystal clear, specific | Clear but broad | Vague or generic |
| **Evidence** | Strong customer evidence | Some evidence | Assumptions only |
| **Significance** | Clearly important | Moderately important | Questionable importance |
| **Delivery** | Compelling, confident | Clear, adequate | Unclear, uncertain |

**MVP Demonstration Rubric:**

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Hypothesis** | Clear, testable | Somewhat clear | Unclear |
| **Implementation** | Functional, appropriate | Functional | Incomplete |
| **Learning** | Clear insights gained | Some insights | No clear learning |
| **Iteration** | Evidence of iteration | Some iteration | No iteration |

## BARMM Curriculum Customization (BDP 2023-2028)

### Context-Specific Adaptations

When delivering startup curriculum in BARMM, customize content based on the Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 priorities and regional context.

### Priority Sector Focus

| BDP Priority Sector | Curriculum Application |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Halal Industry** | Include Halal certification, supply chain compliance, Islamic finance |
| **Seaweed/Cassava** | Agricultural value chain, post-harvest processing, export opportunities |
| **Coffee/Rubber** | Cooperative models, quality standards, industry roadmap alignment |
| **Tourism** | Circuit development, cultural tourism, safety perception management |
| **Islamic Banking** | Shari'ah-compliant financing, profit-sharing models, Halal investment |

### Module Adaptations for BARMM

**Foundation Course Adaptations:**
| Standard Module | BARMM Customization |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Entrepreneurial Mindset | Include Bangsamoro entrepreneurship examples; address out-migration challenge |
| Finding Problems | Focus on BDP priority challenges; infrastructure gaps as opportunities |
| Validating Problems | Relationship-first approach; barangay-level validation; offline methods |
| Business Model Basics | Islamic finance options; cooperative vs. sole proprietor considerations |

**Intermediate Course Adaptations:**
| Standard Module | BARMM Customization |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| MVP Development | Low-tech, offline-first MVPs; SMS-based; power-independent solutions |
| Early Funding | BBOI incentives; MTIT programs; Islamic banking; cooperative share capital |
| Legal & Operations | Halal certification; BNR process; cooperative registration; CSEA requirements |

**Executive Course Adaptations:**
| Standard Module | BARMM Customization |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Cooperative Innovation | CSEA compliance; member development; FRAMES alignment |
| SE Innovation | BDP sectoral objectives alignment; social impact measurement |
| Innovation Culture | Cross-generational engagement; youth attraction to agriculture |

### BARMM-Specific Learning Objectives

Add these learning objectives for BARMM-delivered courses:

**Foundation Level:**
- Identify BDP 2023-2028 priority sectors and startup opportunities
- Understand Shari'ah-compliant business principles
- Navigate BARMM business registration (Negosyo Centers, MTIT)

**Intermediate Level:**
- Design offline-first and low-connectivity MVPs
- Access BARMM government support programs (BMSMED, SETUP, BIIP)
- Apply Islamic finance principles to startup funding

**Advanced Level:**
- Leverage BIMP-EAGA trade opportunities
- Navigate Halal certification and standards (BHSTL, Bangsamoro Halal Board)
- Scale across BARMM provinces with infrastructure constraints

**Executive Level:**
- Align innovation initiatives with BDP 2023-2028 objectives
- Lead cooperative/SE innovation within CSEA regulatory framework
- Develop youth entrepreneurship programs addressing out-migration
