## Team Composition Models

### Founder Archetypes (For Enterprise Team Design)

Understanding archetypes helps you build a balanced founding team for your enterprise:

| Archetype | Strengths | Weaknesses | Typical Role |
|-----------|-----------|------------|--------------|
| **Hustler** | Sales, networking, deals | May oversell | CEO, BD |
| **Hacker** | Building, problem-solving | May over-engineer | CTO |
| **Hipster** | Design, UX, storytelling | May over-polish | CPO, CMO |
| **Analyst** | Strategy, finance, ops | May over-analyze | COO, CFO |

### Optimal Team Combinations by Stage

| Enterprise Stage | Recommended Team Composition |
|------------------|------------------------------|
| **Pre-product** | Hacker + Hustler |
| **Pre-revenue** | Hacker + Hustler + Hipster |
| **Pre-scale** | Full founding team or key hires |
| **Scaling** | Add professional management |

### Skills Coverage Matrix

Ensure your founding team covers critical enterprise functions:

```text
ENTERPRISE SKILLS COVERAGE:
│
├── PRODUCT/TECHNICAL
│   ├── Can we build the product?
│   ├── Can we iterate quickly?
│   └── Do we have domain expertise?
│
├── COMMERCIAL
│   ├── Can we sell?
│   ├── Can we market?
│   └── Do we understand the customer?
│
├── OPERATIONS
│   ├── Can we deliver?
│   ├── Can we manage finances?
│   └── Can we scale operations?
│
└── LEADERSHIP
    ├── Can we set vision and strategy?
    ├── Can we attract talent?
    └── Can we build culture?
```

## Hiring for Startups

### When to Hire

```text
HIRING DECISION FRAMEWORK:
│
├── DO WE NEED SOMEONE?
│   ├── Is this role critical to our stage?
│   ├── Can founders cover this temporarily?
│   ├── Can we outsource instead?
│   └── Do we have runway for this hire?
│
├── WHAT KIND OF HIRE?
│   ├── Full-time: Core roles, ongoing need
│   ├── Part-time: Specialized, limited scope
│   ├── Contractor: Project-based, temporary
│   └── Advisor: Strategic guidance, equity-based
│
└── WHEN TO HIRE?
    ├── When pain is unbearable
    ├── When opportunity cost > salary cost
    └── When you can't learn the skill quickly
```

### Startup Hiring Principles

| Principle | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hire for strength, not lack of weakness** | One exceptional skill > mediocre at everything |
| **Hire missionaries, not mercenaries** | People who believe in the mission |
| **Hire for the stage you're in** | Not the stage you hope to be |
| **Hire slowly, fire quickly** | Take time to get it right |
| **Culture add, not culture fit** | Expand, don't duplicate |

### First Hires by Function

| When You Need... | What to Look For |
|------------------|------------------|
| **First engineer** | Can build AND architect, tolerates ambiguity |
| **First salesperson** | Can sell anything, not just execute process |
| **First marketer** | Full-stack marketer, can do AND measure |
| **First ops person** | Systems thinker, can build process from scratch |
| **First customer success** | Customer obsessed, can also sell and support |

### Startup Interview Questions

**For All Roles:**
1. What's the most impressive thing you've built/done?
2. What would you do in your first 30 days?
3. How do you handle ambiguity?
4. What's a time you failed and what did you learn?
5. Why this company, why now?

**For Technical Roles:**
- Walk me through a technical decision you made
- How do you balance speed vs. quality?
- How do you learn new technologies?

**For Commercial Roles:**
- Walk me through a deal you closed
- How do you handle rejection?
- How do you learn about customers?

## Role Definition

### CEO vs. CTO Division

| Domain | CEO | CTO |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Vision** | Business strategy | Technical strategy |
| **External** | Investors, partners, press | Technical community |
| **Internal** | Business team, culture | Engineering team |
| **Decisions** | Business model, positioning | Architecture, tech stack |
| **Metrics** | Revenue, growth, burn | Tech debt, velocity, uptime |

**Overlap Zones** (decide together):
- Product decisions
- Hiring senior roles
- Major pivots
- Large investments

### Defining Roles Clearly

```text
ROLE DEFINITION TEMPLATE:
│
├── TITLE AND SCOPE
│   ├── What is the title?
│   ├── What team/function does this own?
│   └── What is explicitly NOT in scope?
│
├── KEY RESPONSIBILITIES
│   ├── 3-5 primary responsibilities
│   ├── What decisions can they make alone?
│   └── What requires escalation?
│
├── SUCCESS METRICS
│   ├── What outcomes are they accountable for?
│   ├── How will we measure success?
│   └── What's the review cadence?
│
├── INTERFACES
│   ├── Who do they report to?
│   ├── Who reports to them?
│   └── Who are key collaborators?
│
└── GROWTH PATH
    ├── What does advancement look like?
    └── What skills should they develop?
```

### RACI Matrix for Startups

| Decision Area | CEO | CTO | COO | Team |
|---------------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| **Product roadmap** | A | R | C | I |
| **Technical architecture** | I | A/R | I | C |
| **Hiring** | A | R | R | C |
| **Budget allocation** | A/R | C | R | I |
| **Customer pricing** | A/R | I | C | I |

R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed

## Organizational Structure

### Startup Org Structures by Stage

**Stage 1: Founding Team (2-5 people)**
```text
┌─────────────────┐
│   Founders      │
│   (All roles)   │
└─────────────────┘
```

**Stage 2: First Hires (5-15 people)**
```text
       ┌──────────────┐
       │     CEO      │
       └──────┬───────┘
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    ▼         ▼         ▼
┌───────┐ ┌───────┐ ┌───────┐
│  CTO  │ │ Sales │ │  Ops  │
│  +    │ │   +   │ │       │
│ Eng   │ │ Mktg  │ │       │
└───────┘ └───────┘ └───────┘
```

**Stage 3: Functional Teams (15-50 people)**
```text
           ┌──────────────┐
           │     CEO      │
           └──────┬───────┘
    ┌──────┬──────┼──────┬──────┐
    ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼
┌──────┐┌──────┐┌──────┐┌──────┐┌──────┐
│ Eng  ││Product││ Sales││ Mktg ││ Ops  │
│ Team ││ Team ││ Team ││ Team ││ Team │
└──────┘└──────┘└──────┘└──────┘└──────┘
```

### Reporting Structures

| Structure | When to Use | Watch Out For |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Flat** | Early stage, high trust | Unclear accountability |
| **Functional** | Clear specialization | Silos, slow cross-function |
| **Product-based** | Multiple products | Duplication, inconsistency |
| **Matrix** | Complex orgs | Confusion, slow decisions |

## Team Scaling

### Scaling Challenges

| From-To | Key Challenges |
|---------|----------------|
| **2→5** | Founders letting go of tasks |
| **5→15** | Adding management layer |
| **15→30** | Formalizing process |
| **30→50** | Culture preservation |
| **50→100** | Specialization and silos |

### Scaling Principles

1. **Hire ahead of the curve** - Don't wait until it's painful
2. **Add management thoughtfully** - Not too early, not too late
3. **Document as you scale** - Process that lives in heads doesn't scale
4. **Preserve culture intentionally** - It dilutes by default
5. **Over-communicate** - What worked at 10 won't work at 50

### When to Add Management

```text
MANAGEMENT LAYER DECISION:
│
├── ADD MANAGEMENT WHEN:
│   ├── Team > 7-8 people per manager
│   ├── Decisions bottleneck at founders
│   ├── Quality or speed declining
│   └── Team asking for more structure
│
├── DELAY MANAGEMENT WHEN:
│   ├── Team is high-performing and self-organizing
│   ├── You don't have the right person
│   └── Adding overhead without value
│
└── MANAGEMENT ROLE:
    ├── Set clear expectations
    ├── Remove obstacles
    ├── Develop people
    └── Make decisions at right level
```

### Hiring Managers vs. Promoting

| Hire External When | Promote Internal When |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Need specific expertise | Have high-potential people |
| Scaling function rapidly | Culture fit critical |
| Major transformation | Institutional knowledge needed |
| No internal candidates | Reward and retain |
