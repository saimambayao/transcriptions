# Cooperative Integration for Design Thinking

Design thinking in cooperatives requires fundamental adaptations for member-ownership, democratic governance, and ICA Cooperative Principles.

## Member-Centric vs User-Centric Design

| Dimension | User-Centric Design | Member-Centric Design |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Role** | Customer/consumer | Owner AND user (dual role) |
| **Voice** | Feedback through surveys | Democratic input through governance |
| **Focus** | Individual preferences | Collective needs balanced with individual |
| **Relationship** | Transactional | Long-term ownership |
| **Decision Rights** | None | Voting rights through GA |

**Key Principle**: Design decisions must serve the dual identity of members as both users AND owners.

## Cooperative Member Persona Template

Capture: basic demographics, membership tenure, ownership mindset, democratic participation, patronage expectations, member type (active/passive/champion/at-risk), cooperative identity.

## Empathy Mapping for Member-Owners

Add ownership and governance dimensions to standard empathy maps:
- SAYS: What do they say about being an owner? About governance?
- THINKS: How do they think about their stake?
- DOES: Do they attend GA? Vote? Patronize?
- FEELS: Do they feel like owners? Heard? Valued?

## Democratic Problem Framing

Problems should be prioritized through democratic processes:
1. DISCOVERY: Gather from member feedback, committees, staff, BOD
2. SYNTHESIS: Cluster, frame as HMW, assess mission alignment
3. DEMOCRATIC INPUT: Committee review, focus groups, pre-GA consultations
4. DECISION: BOD approval, GA ratification for major changes
5. COMMUNICATION: Share priorities and rationale with members

## Committee-Based Ideation

| Committee | Ideation Focus |
|-----------|---------------|
| Education | Member capability solutions |
| Credit | Financial innovation |
| Audit | Governance solutions |
| Election | Engagement solutions |
| Mediation | Community solutions |

## GA as Validation Forum

Pre-GA: Share problem, collect ideas. During GA: Present concepts, small groups, dot voting. Post-GA: Compile input, refine, report back.

## Prototype & Test: Member Validation

Select pilot groups representing demographic diversity, engagement levels, and special groups (officers, new members, long-standing).

Prototypes must pass through relevant committees and BOD before wider rollout. Significant innovations require formal BOD approval with GA ratification for bylaw changes.

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Savings Product for Young Members (Credit Coop)
Empathize with 18-30 year olds -> Define: young members prefer digital wallets -> Ideate: gamified savings, mobile access -> Prototype with 30 members -> BOD approval -> GA presentation

### Scenario 2: Improving GA Participation
Survey non-attenders -> Root causes: timing, format, relevance -> Concepts: hybrid GA, shorter format, incentives -> Pilot new format -> Measure attendance and satisfaction

### Scenario 3: Digitizing Ordering (Consumer Coop)
Segment by digital literacy -> Constraint: no member worse off -> Multi-channel solution (app + SMS + counter) -> Progressive digitization with support
