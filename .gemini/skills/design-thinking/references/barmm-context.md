## BDP 2023-2028 Foundation

### Vision

The BDP 2023-2028 envisions:

> "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

This vision directly informs human-centered design approaches, emphasizing unity, peace, justice, and moral governance as guiding principles for all service design.

### Mission

> "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

**Design Thinking Alignment**: The mission explicitly emphasizes:
- **Human-centered focus**: Services suited to "systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people"
- **Multi-stakeholder participation**: Inclusive design processes
- **Partnership facilitation**: Collaborative solution development

### Overall Goal: Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro

| Goal Element | Definition | Design Thinking Implication |
|--------------|------------|----------------------------|
| **Empowered** | Improved capacity through better services and economic opportunities | Design for capability building and access |
| **Cohesive** | Improved relationships, reduced conflict, forged partnerships | Design for collaboration and inclusion |
| **Progressive** | Sustained ability to optimize resources and realize potential | Design for sustainability and scalability |

### Six Development Goals

| Goal | Description | Design Focus |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Goal 1** | Stable, just, and accountable government | Service design for transparency and efficiency |
| **Goal 2** | Equitable, competitive, and robust economy | Economic opportunity design for all |
| **Goal 3** | Peaceful, safe, and resilient communities | Community-centered safety solutions |
| **Goal 4** | Inclusive, responsive, and quality social services | Leave-no-one-behind service design |
| **Goal 5** | Rich and diverse culture and identity preserved | Culturally-sensitive design approaches |
| **Goal 6** | Strategic, adequate, and climate-resilient infrastructure | Sustainable infrastructure design |

### Eight Development Strategies

The BDP 2023-2028 identifies eight key strategies that inform design thinking applications:

1. Improve and strengthen governance mechanisms for a stable, just, and accountable Bangsamoro government
2. Create an enabling environment towards an equitable, competitive, and robust economy
3. **Harness technology and innovations to increase socioeconomic opportunities and improve government services**
4. Improve ecological integrity, resilience of communities, and ensure a healthy and clean environment
5. Enhance and strengthen peace, public order, safety, and security and uphold human rights
6. Ensure inclusive and equitable access to quality services for social justice and human capital development
7. Mainstream Bangsamoro cultural diversity, beliefs, heritage, and identity
8. Scale-up functional, strategic, climate-resilient, and quality infrastructure

### Pillars of Moral Governance (Design Principles)

The BDP 2023-2028 establishes 12 Pillars of Moral Governance that serve as design principles:

| Pillar | Design Application |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Integrity** | Design honest, trustworthy services |
| **Accountability** | Build transparency into every solution |
| **Transparency and honesty** | Make all processes visible to stakeholders |
| **Sense of patriotism** | Honor Bangsamoro history and achievements |
| **Striving for excellence** | Prioritize quality and continuous improvement |
| **Cultural understanding and tolerance** | Design for diversity and inclusion |
| **Unwavering loyalty to God** | Align solutions with ethical and religious values |
| **Justice** | Ensure equity without prejudice |
| **Inclusivity** | Create welcoming, discrimination-free experiences |
| **Objectivity** | Make merit-based, public-interest decisions |
| **Dialogue and meaningful engagement** | Involve communities in design process |
| **Balance between practical life and moral side** | Balance efficiency with ethical values |

## Design Thinking for Cooperatives

### Why Design Thinking for Coops?

Cooperatives face unique challenges that design thinking can address:
- Member-centric services (aligns with human-centered design)
- Democratic governance (requires inclusive ideation)
- Community benefit (social impact focus)
- Resource constraints (need for efficient innovation)

### Alignment with BDP Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy

Design thinking for coops supports:
- **Equitable economy**: Economic gains benefiting all, especially the poor and marginalized
- **Competitive economy**: Comparative advantage through improved market linkages
- **Robust economy**: Sustainable growth through quality job opportunities

### Macroeconomic Targets for Coop Contribution

| Target | Value | Coop Design Focus |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| GRDP Growth Rate | 8-9% annually | Design market expansion strategies |
| Poverty Reduction | To 20-25% by 2028 | Design livelihood programs for members |
| Underemployment | 4-7% target | Design skills and employment programs |
| Services/Industry Growth | 10-12% | Design value-added services |

### Adapting the 5 Phases for Coops

| Phase | Coop Application | Key Activities |
|-------|------------------|----------------|
| **Empathize** | Understand member needs | Member interviews, community mapping, service shadowing |
| **Define** | Frame member challenges | Member journey mapping, pain point synthesis |
| **Ideate** | Co-create with members | Participatory design sessions, member assemblies |
| **Prototype** | Test service improvements | Pilot programs, member feedback loops |
| **Test** | Validate with members | Member satisfaction surveys, usage metrics |

### Coop-Specific Tools

| Tool | Coop Application |
|------|------------------|
| **Member Journey Map** | Track member experience from joining to active participation |
| **Service Blueprint** | Map front-stage member interactions and back-stage operations |
| **Value Proposition Canvas** | Align coop services with member needs |

### Integration with Cooperative Principles

| ICA Principle | Design Thinking Alignment |

| Member economic participation | Value-focused prototyping |

## Design Thinking for Social Enterprises

### Why Design Thinking for SEs?

Social enterprises need design thinking for:
- Balancing social mission with sustainability
- Understanding beneficiary needs deeply
- Innovating business models for impact
- Measuring and improving outcomes

### Alignment with BDP Economic Targets

Design solutions should contribute to:
- Sustained 8-9% annual growth rate
- Poverty reduction to 20-25% by 2028
- Reduced underemployment (4-7% target)
- Growth in services and industry sectors (10-12%)
- Gross Capital Formation increase to 18-20% of GRDP

### Adapting for Social Enterprise Development

| SE Lifecycle Stage | Design Thinking Focus |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Ideation** | Empathize with target beneficiaries |
| **Birth** | Define problem, prototype solutions |
| **Nurturing** | Test and iterate business model |
| **Maturity** | Scale solutions, measure impact |

### SE-Specific Considerations

**Triple Bottom Line Design**:
- Desirability: Do beneficiaries want/need this?
- Viability: Can the SE sustain itself?
- Impact: Does it achieve social and environmental outcomes?

**Theory of Change Integration**:
Use design thinking to develop and test:
1. **Inputs** - What resources are needed?
2. **Activities** - What will we do?
3. **Outputs** - What will we produce?
4. **Outcomes** - What changes will result?
5. **Impact** - What long-term difference will we make?

## Cultural Considerations

### BDP 2023-2028 Cultural Framework

**Sectoral Goal (Goal 5)**: Rich and diverse Bangsamoro culture and identity preserved and recognized.

**Sub-sector Objectives**:
- Bangsamoro cultural heritage and cohesive identity strengthened
- Sufficient resources for comprehensive historical culture and identity ensured
- Policies on cultural and historical preservation enacted
- Impact of climate change, globalization, and migration on cultural heritage addressed

### Bangsamoro Identity and Diversity

The Bangsamoro is an ethnolinguistic mosaic of at least 13 groups:

| Category | Groups |
|----------|--------|
| **Islamized Groups** | Maguindanaon, Meranao, Iranun, Tausug, Yakan, Sama, Jama Mapun, Sangil, Molbog, Palawani, Kalagan, Kalibugan |
| **Non-Islamized/Indigenous** | Various indigenous peoples of the Bangsamoro |

**Design Implication**: Solutions must accommodate this diversity while strengthening cohesive identity.

### Historical Sultanates and Heritage

Design should honor the rich heritage of:
- Sultanate of Sulu
- Sultanate of Maguindanao
- Sultanate of Kabuntalan
- Sultanate of Buayan
- Royal House of Ranao
- Iranun traditions
- Non-Moro indigenous peoples

### Traditional Systems for Design Inspiration

| Tradition | Origin | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Walay na Bityara** | Maguindanaon | Conflict resolution, consensus building |
| **Taritib ago Igma** | Maranao | Social regulation, community standards |
| **Baitsyara** | Island provinces | Dialogue and mediation |

These traditional systems offer frameworks for participatory design and community engagement.

### Cultural Design Considerations

| Factor | Consideration | Adaptation |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| **Community Focus** | Collective decision-making valued | Group empathy sessions, community workshops |
| **Relationship-Based** | Trust builds over time | Longer empathy phase, relationship building |
| **Oral Tradition** | Stories and narratives important | Use storytelling in ideation, storyboard prototypes |
| **Respect for Elders** | Authority and wisdom valued | Include elders in validation, seek guidance |
| **Islamic Values** | Halal, ethical business | Ensure solutions align with Shari'ah principles |

### Preserving Tangible and Intangible Heritage

**Challenge**: Bangsamoro tangible and intangible cultural heritages are vanishing due to:
- Exposure to foreign cultures
- Modern lifestyle and social media
- Limited knowledge transfer to younger generations
- Natural and human-induced calamities

**Design Response**:
- Integrate cultural preservation into digital solutions
- Use design to facilitate intergenerational knowledge transfer
- Create platforms that showcase Bangsamoro arts, music, and traditions

### Traditional Sports and Arts Integration

Design solutions can help preserve and promote:

| Traditional Sports | Traditional Music |
|-------------------|-------------------|

| Kaphaso (Horse racing) | Kapangotyapi |

| And others | And others |

### Language and Communication

- Conduct empathy research in local languages (Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug)
- Use visual methods to bridge language barriers
- Include translators in synthesis sessions
- Test prototypes with diverse language groups
- Consider endangered language preservation in solutions

### Inclusive Design Principles (BDP-Aligned)

1. **Gender-Inclusive**: Ensure women's voices in all phases (cross-cutting theme in BDP)
2. **Age-Inclusive**: Include youth and elders
3. **Ability-Inclusive**: Consider persons with disabilities

## Application Examples

### Example 1: Cooperative Registration Redesign

**Challenge**: Coop registration takes too long, discourages formation

**BDP Alignment**: Goal 1 (Accountable government), Goal 2 (Equitable economy)

**Empathize**:
- Interview aspiring coop organizers in multiple languages
- Shadow registration process
- Map current journey (pain points)
- Understand community decision-making dynamics

**Define**:
- HMW: "How might we make coop registration simple enough that community groups can complete it independently while respecting collective decision-making?"

**Ideate**:
- Brainstorm simplification ideas
- Consider digital solutions accessible across language groups
- Explore guided assistance models with cultural sensitivity

**Prototype**:
- Create simplified application form in multiple languages
- Build step-by-step guide with visual aids
- Pilot with 5 new coops across different provinces

**Test**:
- Measure time reduction
- Gather organizer feedback in local languages
- Iterate based on results

### Example 2: Social Enterprise Training Program

**Challenge**: SE training doesn't lead to successful business launches

**BDP Alignment**: Goal 2 (Robust economy), Goal 4 (Quality social services)

**Empathize**:
- Interview past training graduates
- Understand barriers to launching (cultural, economic, social)
- Map post-training journey
- Include elders and community leaders in research

**Define**:
- HMW: "How might we design training that builds both confidence and practical skills for SE launch while honoring Bangsamoro values?"

**Ideate**:
- Combine classroom and field learning
- Add mentorship component with cultural masters
- Include prototype-building exercises
- Integrate Shari'ah-compliant business practices

**Prototype**:
- Design pilot curriculum
- Create mentor matching process
- Develop launch support package with community validation

**Test**:
- Run pilot with 20 participants across provinces
- Track launch success rate
- Refine based on outcomes

### Example 3: Technology Adoption for MSMEs (SETUP)

**Challenge**: Low absorptive capacity of industries, weak STI culture

**BDP Alignment**: Goal 2 (Competitive economy), Strategy 3 (Harness technology and innovations)

**Empathize**:
- Visit MSMEs in different sectors
- Understand current technology usage and barriers
- Map technology adoption journey
- Identify cultural factors affecting adoption

**Define**:
- HMW: "How might we make technology adoption accessible and beneficial for Bangsamoro MSMEs while respecting local business practices?"

**Ideate**:
- Peer learning networks
- Technology demonstration centers
- Mobile technology training units
- Incentive programs for early adopters

**Prototype**:
- Design pilot technology package for selected sector
- Create training materials in local languages
- Develop support hotline system

**Test**:
- Pilot with 10 MSMEs in target sector
- Measure productivity improvements
- Gather feedback on support services
- Iterate based on results

### Example 4: Community Health Service Delivery

**Challenge**: Limited health facility access, behavioral barriers to healthcare

**BDP Alignment**: Goal 4 (Inclusive social services), Goal 3 (Resilient communities)

**Empathize**:
- Interview community members about health-seeking behavior
- Shadow Barangay Health Workers
- Map health service journey in remote areas
- Understand cultural and religious considerations

**Define**:
- HMW: "How might we bring quality health services to remote communities in culturally appropriate ways?"

**Ideate**:
- Mobile health units
- Community health worker empowerment
- Telemedicine solutions
