## Empathy Tools

### Empathy Mapping

Four-quadrant visualization of user experience.

| Quadrant | Focus | Sample Questions |
|----------|-------|------------------|
| **Says** | Direct quotes | What do they say aloud? |
| **Thinks** | Internal thoughts | What might they be thinking? |
| **Does** | Observable actions | What behaviors do you notice? |
| **Feels** | Emotional states | What emotions might they feel? |

**When to Use**: Early in research to synthesize user insights
**Template**: See [assets/empathy-map-template.md](../assets/empathy-map-template.md)

### User Interviews (Needfinding)

**6-Phase Structure** (from The Design Thinking Playbook):

| Phase | Purpose | Key Actions |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **1. Introduction** | Create comfort | Explain purpose, emphasize no "right/wrong", ask to document |
| **2. Beginning** | Establish connection | Let them introduce themselves, start with open question |
| **3. Reference** | Anchor in experience | Find recent examples they remember well |
| **4. Grand Tour** | Explore deeply | Deepen topics, search for contradictions, find hidden insights |
| **5. Reflection** | Synthesize | Summarize, let them add/correct, ask "why" |
| **6. Wrap-up** | Capture final insights | Keep recording, thank them, let them ask questions |

**Expert Tip**: Don't turn off the recording device at wrap-up - the most intriguing things often occur at the very end.

**Template**: See [playbook-needfinding.md](playbook-needfinding.md) for complete interview protocol

### The 6 WH Questions

Systematic approach for comprehensive understanding.

| What | Who | Why |
|------|-----|-----|
| What is the problem? | Who is involved? | Why is it important? |
| What would we like to know? | Who is affected? | Why does it occur? |
| What assumptions are we making? | Who decides? | Why wasn't it solved before? |

| Where | When | How |
|-------|------|-----|
| Where does it occur? | When did it begin? | How could it be an opportunity? |
| Where was it solved before? | When do people want results? | How could it be solved? |
| Where did similar situations exist? | When can we start? | What has been tried before? |

### Observation (AEIOU Framework)

| Element | What to Observe |
|---------|-----------------|
| **A**ctivities | What are people doing? What tasks? What before/after? |
| **E**nvironment | What does the space look like? Nature and function? |
| **I**nteractions | How do systems interact? What interfaces exist? |
| **O**bjects | What objects/devices are used? By whom? Where? |
| **U**sers | Who are the users? What roles? Who influences them? |

### Lead User Method

Involve trend-leading users as innovation partners.

**Characteristics of Lead Users**:
- Have needs earlier than mass market
- Strong drive to solve problems
- Often create interim solutions themselves

**3-Step Process**:
1. **Identify trends** - Scan sources, find early needs
2. **Search for lead users** - Target market and analogous markets
3. **Co-create solutions** - Workshop with lead users, internal teams

**Warning**: Mind the gap between lead users and early majority. Too much focus on lead users can create "white elephants."

### Journey Mapping

Visual representation of user's experience over time.

**Components**:
1. **Persona**: Who is the user?
2. **Scenario**: What are they trying to achieve?
3. **Stages**: Key phases of the journey
4. **Actions**: What they do at each stage
5. **Thoughts**: What they're thinking
6. **Emotions**: How they feel (emotional curve)
7. **Pain Points**: Frustrations, obstacles
8. **Opportunities**: Where can we improve?

## Define Tools

### How Might We (HMW) Statements

Reframe problems as opportunities for design.

**Formula**: "How might we [action] for [user] so that [outcome]?"

**Principles for Strong HMWs**:

| Principle | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Ground in research | Base on real user insights |
| Avoid embedded solutions | "HMW reduce wait time?" not "HMW add more staff?" |
| Right breadth | Not too narrow, not too broad |
| Focus on outcomes | Address root causes |
| Use positive language | Frame as possibilities |

**Template**: See [assets/hmw-template.md](../assets/hmw-template.md)

### Point of View (POV) Statement

**Formula**: [User] needs [need] because [insight]

**Example**: "A busy cooperative manager needs to quickly verify member compliance status because they spend 60% of their time on manual record-checking instead of member support."

### Critical Assumptions Matrix

Validate assumptions before building solutions.

```
                    DECISIVE
                        │
     ┌──────────────────┼──────────────────┐
     │  Review Last     │   Review First   │
     │                  │   (Highest Risk) │
KNOWING ────────────────┼──────────────── IGNORANT
     │  Skip for Now    │   Review Second  │
     │                  │                  │
     └──────────────────┼──────────────────┘
                    INCIDENTAL
```

**Process**:
1. Formulate idea in one sentence
2. List need assumptions (What motivates use? What prevents use?)
3. Identify critical assumptions
4. Prioritize for validation

## Ideation Tools

### Brainstorming Rules

1. **Defer judgment** - No criticism during ideation
2. **Encourage wild ideas** - Push boundaries
3. **Build on others' ideas** - "Yes, and..."
4. **Stay focused on topic** - One conversation at a time
5. **Be visual** - Sketch, don't just write
6. **Go for quantity** - More ideas = more options
7. **One conversation at a time** - Listen actively

### Targeted Brainstorming (Playbook Methods)

| Method | Purpose | Time |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Critical Functions** | Identify essential user experiences | 1-2 hours |
| **Benchmarking** | Ideas from other industries | 90 min |
| **Dark Horse** | Remove constraints, boost creativity | 50 min |
| **Funky Prototype** | Maximize benefit, ignore budget | 1 hour |

**See**: [playbook-cycles.md](playbook-cycles.md) for detailed macro cycle process

### Crazy 8s

Rapid sketching technique for quick ideation.

**Process**:
1. Fold paper into 8 sections
2. Set timer for 8 minutes
3. Sketch one idea per section (1 minute each)
4. Share and discuss with team

### SCAMPER

| Letter | Prompt | Questions |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **S**ubstitute | What can be replaced? | Different materials? Different people? |
| **C**ombine | What can be merged? | Combine features? Merge steps? |
| **A**dapt | What can be borrowed? | Ideas from other industries? |
| **M**odify | What can be changed? | Bigger? Smaller? Different shape? |
| **P**ut to other use | Alternative applications? | Other contexts? Other users? |
| **E**liminate | What can be removed? | Simplify? Remove steps? |
| **R**earrange | Different order? | Reverse sequence? Different layout? |

## Prototyping Tools

### Prototype Types by Resolution

| Resolution | Examples | When to Use |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Low** | Sketches, paper, role-play, Pinocchio | Early exploration |
| **Medium** | Mock-ups, wireframes, storyboards, Wizard of Oz | Concept validation |
| **High** | MVP, working demos, pilots | Final validation |

**Complete catalog**: See [playbook-prototyping.md](playbook-prototyping.md)

### The Three Prototyping Principles

1. **Love it, change it, leave it** - Clear decision after each test
2. **Never fall in love with your prototype** - Stay objective
3. **Iterate, iterate, iterate** - Continuous improvement

### Prototype Progression

```
Vision → Functional (MVP) → Finished → Final → Implementation
```

| Stage | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| **Vision** | Combine best ideas from all sources |
| **Functional** | Test critical variables only |
| **Finished** | Multiple functions integrated |
| **Final** | Elegant, simple, convincing |

**Template**: See [assets/prototype-vision-canvas-template.md](../assets/prototype-vision-canvas-template.md)

### Boxing and Shelfing

**Boxing**: Create physical box summarizing product benefits (front: name/slogan, back: details, sides: WH questions)

**Shelfing**: Sort offerings into three portfolios:
- Solution portfolio
- Product portfolio
- Service portfolio

**Purpose**: Quickly detect gaps and synergies in offerings

### Storyboarding

**Structure**:
1. **Setup**: Context, user, goal
2. **Conflict**: Problem, pain point
3. **Resolution**: How solution helps
4. **Outcome**: Improved state

## Testing Tools

### 4-Step Test Sequence

```
Preparation → Conduct Test → Document → Infer Learnings
```

### User Testing Protocol

| Phase | Time | Activities |
|-------|------|------------|
| **Introduction** | 2 min | Explain purpose, encourage honesty |
| **Task scenarios** | 15-20 min | Observe user completing tasks |
| **Follow-up** | 5-10 min | Clarify observations |
| **Debrief** | 5 min | Thank, explain next steps |

**Expert Tip**: Test multiple variants, not just one. Users give more differentiated feedback when comparing.

**Motto**: "Never go hunting alone" - Test in pairs (moderator + observer)

### Think-Aloud Protocol

**Prompts**:
- "What are you thinking right now?"
- "What do you expect to happen?"
- "What are you looking for?"

**Probing Questions**:
- "Can you say more about how it feels?"
- "Why?"
- "Show us why this would (not) work for you"

### Feedback Capture Grid

```
┌─────────────────────┬─────────────────────┐
│  + LIKES            │  Δ WISHES           │
│  What works well    │  Constructive       │
│                     │  criticism          │
├─────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
│  ? QUESTIONS        │  💡 IDEAS           │
│  Uncertainties      │  New possibilities  │
└─────────────────────┴─────────────────────┘
```

### A/B Testing

**Requirements**:
- Clear success metric
- Sufficient sample size
- Single variable change
- Concurrent testing

### Experiment Grid

For structured test documentation:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hypothesis** | What we want to test |
| **Experiment** | Prototype, interview, survey |
| **Measurement** | What data to collect |
| **Success criteria** | What shows right/wrong track |
| **Results** | Photos, videos, observations |
| **Learnings** | Insights, conclusions, next steps |

## Cross-Phase Tools

### Question Maps

Non-linear interview preparation tool.

**Why Maps vs Questionnaires**:

| Questionnaire | Question Map |
|---------------|--------------|
| Linear structure | Topical islands |
| Top-to-bottom | Ad-hoc navigation |
| Fixed sequence | Conversation-guided |

**Building a Question Map**:
1. Place main topic in center
2. Create theme islands around it
3. Add specific questions per island
4. Draw connections between islands

### Mindfulness Practices

Foundation for empathy and innovation success.

**10 Steps to Empathetic Engagement**:
1. Change perspective
2. Give full attention
3. Listen actively
4. Reflect on your behavior
5. Read signals
6. Question your readiness for empathy
7. Ask open questions
8. Explore feelings and needs
9. Express your own feelings
10. Act with empathy
