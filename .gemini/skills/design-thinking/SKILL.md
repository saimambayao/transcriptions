## Core Concepts

### What is Design Thinking?

A human-centered approach to innovation that integrates:
1. **Desirability** - What do people need?
2. **Feasibility** - What is technically possible?
3. **Viability** - What is economically sustainable?

### The Stanford d.school 5-Phase Model

| Phase | Purpose | Key Activities |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| **1. Empathize** | Understand users deeply | Interviews, observation, immersion |
| **2. Define** | Frame the right problem | Insights synthesis, POV, HMW statements |
| **3. Ideate** | Generate solutions | Brainstorming, Crazy 8s, SCAMPER |
| **4. Prototype** | Build to think | Rapid prototyping, storyboards |
| **5. Test** | Learn and refine | User testing, feedback, iteration |

### Five Mindsets of Design Thinkers

1. **Empathy** - Understand from user perspective
2. **Optimism** - Believe better solutions exist
3. **Experimentalism** - Build to learn, fail fast
4. **Collaboration** - Diverse teams, multiple perspectives
5. **Bias Toward Action** - Do, don't just discuss

### Mode 4: SE Development Journey (Phase 1: Discover & Design)

**Use this mode when invoked by `/social-enterprise` for SE development.**

This mode focuses on Phase 1: DISCOVER & DESIGN Business Idea, preparing outputs for `/lean-canvas`.

#### Step 1: Empathize with Dual Focus

**For Social Enterprises, understand BOTH:**
- **Beneficiaries**: Who experiences the social problem?
- **Customers**: Who will pay for the solution?

```text
EMPATHY RESEARCH AREAS:
│
├── Beneficiary Research
│   ├── What problem do they face?
│   ├── What have they tried before?
│   ├── What barriers exist?
│   └── What does success look like for them?
│
└── Customer/Payer Research
    ├── Who would pay for this solution?
    ├── What value do they perceive?
    ├── What channels reach them?
    └── What are they currently using?
```

#### Step 2: Define the Social Problem

Create a clear Problem Statement using:

```text
[USER GROUP] needs [NEED] because [INSIGHT].
```

**SE-Specific Additions:**
- Quantify the problem scope
- Identify root causes (for ToC later)
- Note existing solutions and their gaps

#### Step 3: Develop HMW Statements

Frame opportunities as "How Might We" statements:

```text
How might we [ACTION] for [USER] so that [OUTCOME]?
```

**For SEs, consider:**
- HMW create impact AND be sustainable?
- HMW serve beneficiaries while creating value for customers?
- HMW scale the solution without losing mission?

#### Step 4: Initial Ideation

Generate solution concepts focusing on:
- Social impact delivery mechanism
- Revenue model possibilities
- Potential unfair advantages

#### Step 5: Package Outputs for /lean-canvas

```text
HANDOFF TO /lean-canvas:
│
├── Problem Statement
│   └── → Lean Canvas: Problem block
│
├── User Personas
│   ├── Beneficiary persona
│   └── Customer persona (may be different)
│   └── → Lean Canvas: Customer Segments
│
├── Key Insights
│   ├── What matters to users
│   ├── Current alternatives
│   └── Unmet needs
│   └── → Lean Canvas: UVP development
│
└── HMW Statements
    └── Top 3 opportunity statements
    └── → Lean Canvas: Solution direction
```

## Quick Reference

### Tools by Phase

| Phase | Quick Tools | Deep Tools |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Empathize** | Interviews, observation | Ethnography, journey mapping |
| **Define** | HMW, POV statements | Personas, insight clustering |
| **Ideate** | Brainstorm, Crazy 8s | SCAMPER, design charrette |
| **Prototype** | Paper prototypes | Clickable mockups, pilots |
| **Test** | Think-aloud, feedback grid | Usability testing, A/B tests |

### HMW Statement Formula

```text
How might we [ACTION] for [USER] so that [OUTCOME]?
```

**Principles:**
- Ground in research (real insights)
- Avoid embedded solutions
- Right breadth (not too narrow/broad)
- Focus on outcomes (root causes)
- Use positive language

## BARMM Application Examples

### Social Enterprise Example
**Challenge**: SE products don't communicate social impact to buyers

**Empathize**: Interview buyers about purchasing decisions
**Define**: HMW help buyers see and feel the impact of their purchase?
**Ideate**: Impact labels, storytelling, beneficiary connections
**Prototype**: Create impact story cards for 5 products
**Test**: A/B test with and without impact stories

**→ Hand off to /lean-canvas** with:
- Problem: Buyers can't see impact
- Segments: Conscious consumers, gift buyers
- Insights: Stories > statistics for impact perception
- HMW: Create emotional connection between buyer and beneficiary

### Cooperative Example
**Challenge**: Members don't attend general assemblies

**Empathize**: Interview members about barriers to attendance
**Define**: HMW make assemblies valuable enough that members prioritize them?
**Ideate**: Brainstorm format changes, incentives, accessibility options
**Prototype**: Test new format with one barangay-level coop
**Test**: Measure attendance, gather feedback, iterate
