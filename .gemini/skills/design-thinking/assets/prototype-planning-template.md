## Rapid Prototyping Principles

Remember these as you build:

1. **Build to think** - Prototyping is learning, not proving
2. **Fail fast** - Quick, cheap experiments beat slow, expensive ones
3. **Just enough detail** - Only what's needed for feedback
4. **Make it tangible** - Physical beats abstract
5. **Test with users** - Real feedback, not assumptions
6. **Embrace imperfection** - Rough prototypes invite feedback

## Example: Cooperative Registration Prototype

**What we're prototyping**: Simplified coop registration form

**Hypothesis**: If we reduce the form to 5 essential fields, then aspiring coop organizers will complete it without assistance, because the current 15-field form overwhelms them.

**Fidelity**: Low (paper prototype)

**Method**: Paper form + role-play

**Test Plan**:
- 5 aspiring coop organizers
- Give them the paper form
- Ask them to complete it
- Observe where they struggle
- Interview about their experience

**Success Criteria**:
- 4/5 complete without asking questions
- Average completion time under 10 minutes
- Participants report feeling confident
