## Legal Landscape

### Current State

**No dedicated social enterprise law exists in the Philippines.**

SEs operate under various legal forms:
- Cooperatives (RA 9520)
- Non-stock, non-profit corporations (RA 11232)
- SEC-registered businesses with social missions
- NGOs and foundations

### SE Statistics (Pre-COVID)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Number of SEs | ~164,000 |
| % of all businesses | 15% |
| % employing women | 56% |
| % women-led | 44% |
| Economic contribution (2016) | Php 1.15 billion |

### Common SE Forms

- Cooperatives
- Agricultural associations
- Microfinance institutions
- Foundations
- Social businesses

## PRESENT Bill

### Full Name
**Poverty Reduction through Social Entrepreneurship (PRESENT) Act**

### Status
Pending legislation (filed multiple times, most recent: Senate Bill 782, 19th Congress)

### Key Provisions

| Provision | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Loans** | Access to financing for SE development |
| **R&D Funds** | Research and development support |
| **Preferential Procurement** | Government preference for SE products/services |
| **Value Chain Linkage** | Market access and supply chain integration |
| **Training** | Capacity-building activities |
| **Disaster Insurance** | Protection for SEs during calamities |

### SE Definition (PRESENT Bill)

**SEMPS**: Social Enterprise with Marginalized as Primary Stakeholders

Key characteristics:
- Primary purpose: Improve lives of poor and vulnerable communities
- Promote sustainable, climate-friendly products
- Socially responsible practices

### Supporting Coalition

**PRESENT Coalition** - Network of:
- Civil society organizations
- Social entrepreneurs
- Resource institutions
- Academe
- Advocates

**Conveners**:
- Ateneo School of Government (ASoG)
- Foundation for a Sustainable Society (FSSI)

## RA 9520 Cooperative Code

### Definition of Cooperatives

An autonomous and duly registered association of persons with:
- Common bond of interest
- Voluntarily joined together
- Meet social, economic, cultural needs
- Equitable capital contributions
- Shared risks and benefits
- Cooperative principles adherence

### State Policy

Government shall:
- Foster cooperative creation and growth
- Provide technical guidance
- Provide financial assistance
- Enable cooperatives as viable economic enterprises

### Types of Cooperatives

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Credit** | Savings and lending services |
| **Consumer** | Procurement and distribution of goods |
| **Producer** | Undertake joint production |
| **Marketing** | Collective marketing of products |
| **Service** | Provide services to members |
| **Workers** | Employment for worker-members |
| **Advocacy** | Promote cooperativism and social projects |
| **Multi-Purpose** | Combine two or more types |

### Cooperative Principles

1. Voluntary and open membership
2. Democratic member control
3. Member economic participation
4. Autonomy and independence
5. Education, training, information
6. Cooperation among cooperatives
7. Concern for community

### Social Audit Requirement

Cooperatives must assess social impact and ethical performance against:
- Stated mission and vision
- Goals
- Code of social responsibility

## RA 11232 Corporation Code

### Key Provisions Relevant to SEs

#### One-Person Corporation (OPC)
- Single person can organize a corporation
- Previously required minimum 5 incorporators
- Useful for social entrepreneurs starting alone

#### Non-Stock Corporations
- No shares of stock issued
- Profits not distributed to members
- Suitable for charitable, social purposes
- Categories: Charitable, Educational, Scientific, Vocational

#### Perpetual Corporate Existence
- No 50-year limit unless specified in Articles
- Provides long-term stability for SE operations

#### Remote Communication
- Electronic filing of reports
- Virtual meetings permitted
- Enables distributed SE governance

### Relevant for SEs

Non-stock, non-profit corporations can be used for:
- Foundations with social missions
- Charitable organizations
- Educational institutions
- Community development organizations

## Government Programs

### DSWD Sustainable Livelihood Program (SLP)

**Purpose**: Community-based capacity building for marginalized households

**Two Tracks**:

| Track | Focus | Support |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Micro-enterprise Development (MD)** | Entrepreneurship | Seed Capital Fund (P15K individual, P20K association) |
| **Employment Facilitation (EF)** | Job placement | Skills training, job matching |

**Target Beneficiaries**:
- Poor, marginalized, vulnerable households
- NHTS-PR identified
- 4Ps program participants priority

### DTI Programs

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Shared Service Facilities (SSF)** | Equipment/facilities for MSMEs including SEs |
| **KMME-MME** | Kapatid Mentor Micro Enterprise program |
| **Negosyo Centers** | Business registration and development support |

### DOST Programs

**WHWise Program**: Support for Filipina social entrepreneurs in science and technology

### DTI-DSWD Collaboration

- Joint entrepreneurial capability-building training
- SLP associations receiving business development support
- Registration assistance (SEC, CDA, DTI)
