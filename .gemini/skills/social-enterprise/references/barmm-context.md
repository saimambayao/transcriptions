## CSEA Overview

### Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority

**Mandate**: Sole regulatory agency for cooperatives and social enterprises in BARMM

**Functions**:
- Registration of cooperatives and social enterprises
- Technical guidance
- Capability building
- Inspection, monitoring, validation

**Operates under**: Office of BARMM Chief Minister

**Transition Status (BDP 2023-2028)**: CSEA is in its transition phase from being the sole agency mandated to register cooperatives in the defunct ARMM - Cooperative Development Authority (CDA). The policies and guidelines for cooperatives were predominantly aligned with the previous entity, making newly registered cooperatives, which were mainly composed of former combatants, encounter difficulties in complying with requirements.

### Bangsamoro Social Enterprise Council (BSEC)

Alliance of social enterprise leaders organized by CSEA to:
- Leverage networks
- Share knowledge and experience
- Build enabling SE environment

## BTA Bill No. 210

### Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code

**Status**: First reading in Bangsamoro Parliament (July 2023)

**BDP 2023-2028 Context**: The Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise (BCSE) Bill is yet to be approved and passed by the Bangsamoro Parliament. The said bill is essential for the operationalization of the BCSE as this will serve as a legal basis for their existence and strategic planning and programming.

**Structure**: 4 Books, 70 pages

| Book | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Book 1** | General Provisions |
| **Book 2** | Cooperative Development |
| **Book 3** | Social Enterprise Development |
| **Book 4** | Miscellaneous and Final Provisions |

### SE Definition (BTA Bill 210)

> Business organizations primarily dedicated to addressing social, cultural, or environmental challenges, with profit generation for owners and stakeholders being a secondary concern.

### Key Features

#### Cooperative Classification

CSEA may classify cooperatives into types:
- Credit cooperatives
- Consumer cooperatives
- Producer cooperatives
- Marketing cooperatives
- Workers cooperatives

#### Cooperative Principles

1. Voluntary and open membership
2. Democratic member control
3. Member economic participation
4. Autonomy and independence
5. Education and training initiatives
6. Cooperation among cooperatives
7. Community concern

#### SE Operating Principles

Must follow Shari'ah-compliant principles (see below)

#### Governance

- **CSEA**: Sole registration authority
- **BSEC**: Alliance of SE leaders
- **Committee on Cooperatives and Social Enterprises**: Parliamentary oversight

## Shari'ah-Compliant Principles

### Core Requirements for BARMM SEs

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fulfilling Shari'ah Objectives** | Align with Islamic values and goals |
| **Halal Transactions** | Permissible business dealings only |
| **Legitimate Wealth** | Asset-based wealth generation |
| **Avoiding Haram** | No forbidden practices |
| **Risk Sharing** | Shared business and financial risks |

### Prohibited Practices

- **Riba** (usury/interest) - Prohibited in financial dealings
- **Gharar** (excessive uncertainty) - Avoid speculative transactions
- **Maysir** (gambling) - No gambling-based activities
- **Haram products/services** - No alcohol, pork, etc.

### Halal Business Practices

1. **Trade-based financing** - Buy-sell arrangements (Murabaha)
2. **Partnership models** - Profit-loss sharing (Musharakah, Mudarabah)
3. **Leasing** - Asset-based leasing (Ijarah)
4. **Agency** - Commission-based services (Wakalah)

### Application to SEs

SEs in BARMM should consider:
- Halal certification for products
- Shari'ah-compliant financing options
- Ethical business practices aligned with Islamic values
- Community benefit focus (maslahah)

## Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028

### BDP Vision and Mission

**Vision**: "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**Mission**: Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership.

**Overall Goal**: Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro

### Pillars of Moral Governance

The BDP 2023-2028 is grounded in 12 Pillars of Moral Governance:
1. Integrity
2. Accountability
3. Transparency and honesty
4. Sense of patriotism
5. Striving for excellence
6. Cultural understanding and tolerance
7. Unwavering loyalty to God
8. Justice
9. Inclusivity
10. Objectivity
11. Dialogue and meaningful engagement
12. Balance between practical life and the moral side

### Macroeconomic Targets 2023-2028

| Indicator | Baseline | End-of-Plan Target |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| GRDP Annual Growth Rate | 7.5% (2020-2021) | 8%-9% sustained |
| GRDP Per Capita Growth | 6.4% (2020-2021) | 6%-9% annually |
| Industry & Services Growth | Industry 7.9%, Services 6.6% | 10%-12% double-digit |
| Gross Capital Formation | 16.91% of GRDP (2021) | 18%-20% of GRDP |
| Inflation Rate | 2.40% (2021) | 2%-4% |
| Unemployment Rate | 3.80% (2021) | 3%-5% |
| Underemployment Rate | 9.0% (2020) | 4%-7% |
| **Poverty Incidence** | **29.80% (2021)** | **20%-25%** |

### Development Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy

**Sectoral Objectives for SEs:**

- **2.5 Functional Cooperatives and Social Enterprise Increased**
  - 2.5.1 Profitability of cooperatives increased
  - 2.5.2 Established social enterprises increased

**Related MSME Objectives:**

- 2.3.1 Competitive and sustainable MSMEs with value-added products and profitability increased
- 2.3.3 Access to Financial Islamic banking of MSMEs ensured
- 2.3.4 Halal-compliant enterprises and industries established and local products gained wider markets

### Priority Strategies for Cooperatives and SEs

**Strategy 21**: Ensure profitability and functionality of cooperatives through:
- Sufficient technical and financial assistance
- Institutionalization of monitoring and evaluation system and mechanism
- Community promotion
- Capacitation of human resources

**Strategy 22**: Institutionalize enabling policies for the establishment of social enterprises in the BARMM

### Priority Programs

#### Development of Cooperatives and Social Enterprise Program

Aims to strengthen the capacities of cooperatives to ensure profitability and functionality. Consists of:

1. **Profitability and Equity Support Oriented (PESO) Project** - Financial and technical support for cooperative profitability
2. **Lakbay Aral sa Social Enterprise (LAKAS SE) Program** - Learning visits and exposure to successful SEs

#### Combatant Transitional Program

Support for former combatants to organize into cooperatives:
1. Strengthening of Technical and Operational Knowledge Project
2. Cooperative Financial and Material Support Project

### Priority Challenges (BDP 2023-2028)

**Low functionality and unsustainable profitability of cooperatives** caused by:

1. **Low compliance rate** - Only 11.66% of registered cooperatives have certificate of compliance (target: 50%)
2. **Lack of enacted Code** - Pending Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code slows institutionalization
3. **Inadequate LGU participation** - Most LGUs have no appointed Municipal Cooperative Officers (MCOs); regional cooperative officers find it difficult to coordinate at the LGU level
4. **Transition difficulties** - Newly registered cooperatives, mainly composed of former combatants, encounter difficulties in complying with requirements

### Priority Sectors for SE Development

| Sector | SE Opportunities | BDP Alignment |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| **Halal Industry** | Halal food production, certification services, slaughterhouses | Strategic Investment Priority (unique to BARMM) |
| **Agriculture** | Farm cooperatives, agri-processing, value chain development | 48.42% national seaweed production, 41.7% cassava |
| **Fisheries** | Fisher cooperatives, seafood processing, aquaculture | 1,010,804 MT production (2021), largest fisherfolk population |
| **Handicrafts** | Weaving cooperatives, cultural products | Industry Roadmap for Weaving Industry |
| **Tourism** | Community-based tourism enterprises | 195,822 tourist arrivals (2021), nature-based tourism potential |
| **Coffee** | Coffee cooperatives, processing | 17.4% national share, Industry Roadmap for Coffee |

### Halal Industry Development Plan

Key elements from BDP 2023-2028:
- **Strategic Investment Priority** unique to BARMM (alongside Islamic banking and BIMP-EAGA enterprises)
- **Bangsamoro Halal Board (BHB)** as policy-making body
- **Bangsamoro Standards and Halal Testing Laboratory (BHSTL)** of MOST
- Halal production development and slaughterhouse establishment
- MSMEs training on Halal awareness, food safety, packaging and labeling
- Subsidy for Halal certification and License-To-Operate

**Challenges identified in BDP**:
- Underdeveloped Halal products and services
- Limited support for Halal and organic production program
- Limited number of Halal-certified products and services

### BIMP-EAGA Integration

Priority areas for SE development:
- Agribusiness (Greater Sulu-Sulawesi Corridor)
- Halal industry (strategic advantage)
- Islamic finance
- Barter trade (cultural trading practice with Malaysia, Indonesia)

**Greater Sulu-Sulawesi Corridor**: Maritime corridor covering North Sulawesi (Indonesia), Sabah (Malaysia), and Mindanao/Palawan (Philippines). This corridor is the nerve center of barter trade in BIMP-EAGA, particularly in agriculture and aquaculture products.

## Social Impact and Development

### Poverty Context (BDP 2023-2028)

**Historic Improvement**:
- BARMM recorded highest significant improvement: 52.6% (2018) to 29.80% (2021)
- Region remains poorest compared to other regions
- Island provinces of BaSulTa have relatively high incidence of poverty among families
- Approximately 408,951 Bangsamoro families live in poverty

**Provincial Poverty Trends (2018-2021)**:
- Lanao del Sur: 64.2% to 7.4% (highest decrease)
- Sulu: 75.3% to 51.0% (increased)
- Tawi-Tawi: 15.1% to 28.1% (increased)

**Subsistence Incidence**: 8.8% (2021) - second highest nationally

### Social Services Context

**Education Challenges**:
- Functional literacy rate: 71.6% (lowest in country vs 91.6% national average)
- Dropout rate for Grades 1-7: 8.36% (highest among regions)
- Completion rate for Grades 1-6: 63.98% (lowest among regions)
- Poverty leaves children with no choice but to help with family needs

**Child Labor**: 7.5% of working children in Philippines are found in BARMM (54,200 child laborers) - directly attributed to high poverty incidence

**Displacement**: 93,525 IDPs in BARMM (82.5% of total IDPs in Mindanao as of August 2022)

### Workforce and Employment

**Labor Force Participation Rate (2020)**: 62.3% (surpassed national average of 62.1%)

**Unemployment Rate (2020)**: 3.8% (below national average of 9%)

**Underemployment Rate (2020)**: 9.0% (below national average of 9.8%)

**Fisherfolk Demographics**: 162,167 male, 90,951 female

**Youth**: 42% of population belongs to 15-39 age bracket - large pool of highly trainable workforce

### Women in SE Development

**Priority Focus from BDP 2023-2028**:
- Strategy 25: Increase employability of Bangsamoro workers, youth, **women**, displaced workers, persons with special needs, IPs, and elderly
- Women empowerment programs under Social Welfare sector
- Women-led enterprises prioritized in CSEA programs
- SUBATRA-ECSO Project: 85% women participants

**Related Programs**:
- Women Empowerment for Community Resilience (WECoRe) Project
- Women Livelihood Acceleration Project
- Women Organizations Accreditation Project (target: 2,650 Bangsamoro Women Organizations)

### Social Protection Programs

**Key Programs Relevant to SEs**:
- Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps)
- Social Pension for Indigent Senior Citizens
- Unlad Pamilyang Bangsamoro Project
- Bangsamoro Sagip Kabuhayan (BSK) - livelihood grants and skills training
