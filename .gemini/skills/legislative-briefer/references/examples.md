# Legislative Briefer — Annotated Examples

These examples demonstrate the expected quality level for key CSW sections. Read these before drafting.

---

## Example 1: Section X — Interpellation Q&A (Good vs Weak)

**Measure:** BAA No. 18 (Bangsamoro Education Code)

### WEAK Example (do not produce this)

```
INTRODUCTION: Education is important for BARMM.
QUESTION: What is the budget for education?
ANTICIPATED ANSWER: The ministry will determine the budget.
```

**Why this fails:** No data citation, no specific section reference, no follow-up strategy. The introduction is a platitude. The question is too broad to elicit a useful answer. The anticipated answer is a guess with no grounding.

### STRONG Example (target this quality)

```
INTRODUCTION: Ang BAA No. 18 ay naglalayong itatag ang isang comprehensive education system
para sa Bangsamoro. Ngunit, ayon sa 2nd BDP Chapter 9, ang functional literacy rate ng BARMM
ay 71.6% lamang — ang pinakamababa sa buong bansa kumpara sa national average na 91.6%.

QUESTION: Ipagpawalang-galang po, G. Sponsor. Sa ilalim ng Section 12 ng panukalang batas,
tinukoy na ang MBHTE ang lead implementing agency. Ano po ang magiging mekanismo ng MBHTE
para ma-address ang 20-percentage-point gap sa functional literacy sa loob ng limang taon?

ANTICIPATED ANSWER: [Ang sponsor ay malamang na babanggitin ang implementation plan sa
Section 25 ng panukalang batas at ang coordination mechanism sa mga LGUs per Section 30.]

MANIFESTATION: Salamat, G. Sponsor. Kung ganoon, nais ko pong ipanukala na magdagdag ng
specific measurable targets sa Section 12 — halimbawa, isang minimum annual increase ng 2%
sa functional literacy rate, na may annual reporting requirement sa Parliament.

PROPOSED AMENDMENT: Amend Section 12 to include: "The MBHTE shall submit to Parliament an
annual progress report on literacy rates, with a target of reducing the literacy gap by at
least 2 percentage points per year."

JUSTIFICATION: Without measurable targets, the implementation plan lacks accountability.
The 2nd BDP itself sets specific macroeconomic targets — education legislation should do
the same.
```

**Why this works:**
- Introduction uses real data (71.6% vs 91.6%) from an authoritative source (2nd BDP Chapter 9)
- Question cites a specific section of the bill (Section 12)
- Anticipated answer references specific bill provisions (Sections 25, 30)
- Manifestation proposes a concrete, measurable amendment
- Proposed amendment is drafted in legislative language ready for insertion
- Justification ties back to the BDP's own standards

## Example 2: Section XII — 1-Minute Yes Vote

**Measure:** BAA No. 20 (Bangsamoro Dual Calendar Act)
**Language:** Tagalog-primary (~150 words)

```
G. Speaker, mga kasamahan sa Parliament —

Bumoboto po ako ng PABOR sa Bangsamoro Dual Calendar Act.

Ang panukalang batas na ito ay hindi lamang tungkol sa kalendaryo. Ito ay pagkilala sa
ating karapatan bilang Bangsamoro na ipakita ang ating pagkakakilanlan sa loob mismo ng
ating mga opisina at institusyon. Ang Hijri calendar, na nagsimula sa Hijrah ng Propeta
Muhammad (ﷺ), ay bahagi ng ating pamana bilang Muslim. Ang pagsasama nito sa Gregorian
calendar sa ating opisyal na dokumento ay nagpapakita ng pagkakaisa at paggalang sa ating
diversity.

Ang panukalang ito ay naaayon sa Article IX, Section 24 ng BOL na nag-uutos ng
pagpreserba at pagtataguyod ng Bangsamoro culture at identity. Bilang kinatawan ng ating
mga kababayan, obligasyon nating protektahan ang mga ganitong aspeto ng ating buhay.

Ipasa natin ang batas na ito — para sa ating pagkakakilanlan, para sa ating kasaysayan,
para sa Bangsamoro.

PABOR PO AKO. Maraming salamat, G. Speaker.
```

**Structure demonstrated:**
1. Opening declaration ("Bumoboto po ako ng PABOR")
2. Key benefits — identity recognition, cultural preservation
3. Link to BOL provision (Article IX, Section 24)
4. Bangsamoro impact framing
5. Memorable closing slogan
6. Call to action ("PABOR PO AKO")

**Word count:** ~150 words — within the 150-300 target range.

## Example 3: Overview (Executive Summary)

**Measure:** BAA No. 18 (Bangsamoro Education Code)
**MP:** Member of Parliament assigned to Committee on Basic Education

```
## OVERVIEW

BAA No. 18, the proposed Bangsamoro Education Code, establishes a comprehensive education
system for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. The measure covers basic education, higher
education, madaris integration, alternative learning systems, and teacher qualification
standards across 8 chapters and 120 sections. It designates the MBHTE as the lead
implementing agency with coordination mechanisms involving MIPA for indigenous peoples'
education and the private madaris sector.

Key findings from this analysis: (1) The measure aligns with 2nd BDP Goal 4 on human
capital development but lacks measurable literacy targets; (2) Budget implications are
significant — estimated PHP 2.8B annual increase over current MBHTE allocation; (3) Shari'ah
compliance is strong on madaris provisions but Section 45 on curriculum standardization may
conflict with traditional Islamic education autonomy.

**Recommended position:** SUPPORT with amendments. The measure addresses a critical gap —
BARMM's functional literacy rate of 71.6% is the lowest nationally — but needs stronger
accountability mechanisms and clearer budget appropriation pathways.

**Strategic considerations for the MP:** As Committee on Basic Education member, the MP is
well-positioned to propose amendments during committee deliberation. Priority areas: (1)
Insert measurable targets in Section 12, (2) Clarify MBHTE-LGU coordination in Section 30,
(3) Strengthen madaris integration safeguards in Chapter 5.
```

**Format demonstrated:**
- Paragraph 1: What the measure does (factual summary)
- Paragraph 2: Key findings from the analysis (numbered)
- Paragraph 3: Position recommendation with justification
- Paragraph 4: Strategic advice tailored to the specific MP
