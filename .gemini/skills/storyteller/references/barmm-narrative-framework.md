## Bangsamoro Development Vision & Mission

**Vision**: "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**Mission**: "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

**Overarching Goal**: An empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro.

### Six Development Goals (2023-2028)

When crafting stories, anchor transformation narratives to these goals:

1. **Stable, Just, and Accountable Government** - Story element: Leadership integrity and service
2. **Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy** - Story element: Shared prosperity through enterprise
3. **Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities** - Story element: Collective security and harmony
4. **Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services** - Story element: Access and dignity for all
5. **Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved** - Story element: Heritage as strength
6. **Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure** - Story element: Foundation for growth

## Twelve Pillars of Moral Governance

These principles provide narrative themes for character development and values-driven storytelling:

1. **Integrity** - Protagonist avoids conflicts of interest; acts with honesty
2. **Accountability** - Character documents decisions; answers to community
3. **Transparency and Honesty** - Character discloses conflicts; resolves publicly
4. **Sense of Patriotism** - Character protects community's reputation and history
5. **Striving for Excellence** - Character prioritizes process integrity and quality
6. **Cultural Understanding and Tolerance** - Character appreciates BARMM diversity
7. **Unwavering Loyalty to God** - Character integrates Islamic values naturally
8. **Justice** - Character serves without prejudice or favoritism
9. **Inclusivity** - Character creates welcoming, discrimination-free spaces
10. **Objectivity** - Character makes merit-based decisions
11. **Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement** - Character listens and collaborates
12. **Balance Between Practical and Moral** - Character navigates ethics while achieving goals

## Bangsamoro Ethnocultural Mosaic

Represent the region's 13+ cultural groups authentically in character development:

| Cultural Group | Key Heritage | Story Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Maguindanaon** | Walay na Bityara (customary law); agriculture | Farmer/cooperative leader with strong governance principles |
| **Meranao** | Lake Lanao culture; weaving; Taritib ago Igma customs | Weaver/craftsperson; community elder with traditional wisdom |
| **Tausug** | Maritime traditions; Sulu Sultanate heritage | Trader/fisherman; connection to regional commerce |
| **Yakan** | Weaving traditions; Basilan-based culture | Artisan preserving heritage while modernizing |
| **Iranun/Iranaun** | Coastal/maritime traditions; Iranun Bay | Coastal fisher; connection to sea-based livelihoods |
| **Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples** | Diverse highland/coastal practices | Representation of minority voices and inclusion |

### Cultural Sensitivity in Storytelling

**Do**:
- Show protagonists respecting elders and cultural masters
- Feature intergenerational knowledge transfer (parent to child, master to apprentice)
- Include Islamic values naturally in decision-making (honesty, fairness, community support)
- Reference local languages for authenticity (Maguindanaon, Meranao, Tausug, Yakan greetings)
- Honor diverse traditions within Bangsamoro identity
- Show women in leadership and business roles

**Avoid**:
- Treating Bangsamoro as monolithic; emphasize diversity
- Stereotyping any ethnic group
- Treating cultural practices as "exotic"; they are normal, meaningful life
- Using outdated or potentially offensive terminology
- Overlooking non-Moro indigenous peoples

- **Kakhoya** (marathon) = Endurance, persistence through challenges
- **Kaphaso** (horse racing) = Strategy, timing, competitive advantage

### Traditional Music (Audio/Emotional Elements)
- **Kakholintang/Kaagong** = Community celebration, gathering music
- **Kapangotyapi** = String traditions, melodic storytelling
- **Kapangobing** = Vocal heritage, emotional expression
- **Kapangintsi** = Ceremonial joy, festive moments

## Cooperative/Social Enterprise Transformation

Align enterprise stories to BARMM's cooperative data and development targets:

### Key Economic Indicators (2023-2028 Targets)
- **GRDP Growth**: 8-9% annual average
- **Poverty Reduction**: From 29.8% (2021) to 20-25% target
- **Business Registrations**: 8,427 in 2021 (161% increase); goal is formalization of informal sector
- **Cooperative Compliance**: 11.66% compliant (2020); target: 50% by 2028
- **Tourism Arrivals**: 195,822 (2021, recovering); opportunity for cultural/rural tourism

### Cooperative Success Archetypes

**1. The Founding Story** - 12 farmers organizing; trust replacing middlemen; first harvest success

**2. The Member Transformation** - From poverty/dependence → financial stability → community leadership

**3. The Generational Legacy** - Founder's vision → children's innovation → grandchildren's stewardship

**4. The Crisis & Recovery** - Typhoon/market crash → collective decision-making → stronger community

**5. The Cultural Preservation** - Traditional craft cooperative protecting heritage while creating livelihoods

### Cooperative Narrative Principles

- Frame as "We" journey, not individual hero
- Democratic participation = character development
- Shared benefits = mutual aid in action
- Community impact = ripple effects beyond members
- Leadership = service to members, not power over them

## Macroeconomic Context for Aspirational Narratives

Ground transformation stories in BARMM's economic opportunities:

**Agriculture/Fishery Potential**:
- BARMM produces 48% of national seaweed (Tawi-Tawi strength)
- Cassava, mangosteen, rubber, coffee are competitive crops
- 252,758 registered fisherfolk (highest in country)
- Low productivity = opportunity for training-driven transformation

**Services & Industry Growth**:
- Services/industry sectors targeting 10-12% annual growth
- Construction, retail, accommodation services creating jobs
- Halal industry development as regional competitive advantage
- Tourism recovering (195,822 arrivals in 2021)

**Enterprise Formalization**:
- Business registration jumped 161% in 2021
- Cooperatives need compliance support (current 11.66%)
- Informal sector offers massive opportunity for formalization stories

## Story Arc Templates with BARMM Specificity

### The Quest: From Informal to Formal
- Character: Jeepney manufacturer in Cotabato City
- Starting state: Unregistered, no access to loans or large contracts
- Journey: Learning business registration, cooperative formation, compliance
- Transformation: Registered SE, accessing government contracts, employing 12 youth
- Community ripple: Mentoring other informal manufacturers

### Rags to Riches: Agricultural Innovation
- Character: Young rice farmer from Maguindanao
- Challenge: Low yields (3.11 mt/ha vs. 4.09 national average)
- Journey: Cooperative training, improved techniques, market linkages
- Success: Yield increase; income supports family of five; builds home
- Values: Continues mentoring other farmers; preserves Islamic stewardship

### The Generational Legacy: Weaving Through Time
- Generation 1: Mother learns traditional Meranao weaving; creates family income
- Generation 2: Daughter modernizes loom; joins weaving cooperative; teaches community
- Generation 3: Granddaughter launches heritage SE; exports globally; supports cultural preservation
- Legacy: Three generations strengthening both livelihoods and cultural identity

## Key Storytelling Principles for BARMM Context

1. **Collective over Individual**: Center "we" transformations, not lone heroes
2. **Values-Driven**: Weave moral governance pillars into character choices
3. **Culturally Rooted**: Authentic references to traditions, languages, history
4. **Intergenerational**: Show knowledge transfer and legacy-building
5. **Inclusive**: Represent all cultural groups; honor women's leadership; include minorities
6. **Aspirational Yet Realistic**: BARMM poverty reduction from 29.8% to 20-25% is ambitious but achievable
7. **Community-Centered**: Impact beyond individual members; ripple effects matter
8. **Spiritually Integrated**: Islamic values as natural compass, not imposed
