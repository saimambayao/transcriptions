# Evaluation Form Guide

This guide provides standards and templates for creating effective training evaluation forms for CSEA-BARMM training programs. The evaluation form is generated as part of the training package and should be tailored to the specific training design.

---

## Document Styling

- **Font**: Inter throughout
- **Body**: 10pt, line spacing 1.0
- **Header**: Organization name, course title, course code
- **Accent color**: `#1B4F72` (dark blue) for section headers and table headers
- **Rating scales**: Use numbered boxes or circles, not stars or emojis
- **No purple colors**
- **Icons only, no emojis**

---

## Evaluation Framework

The evaluation approach is based on the Kirkpatrick Model, adapted for CSEA-BARMM training context:

| Level | What It Measures | Tool | When |
|-------|-----------------|------|------|
| Level 1: Reaction | Satisfaction, perceived relevance | Satisfaction Survey | End of training |
| Level 2: Learning | Knowledge/skill acquisition | Pre-test and Post-test | Beginning and end |
| Level 3: Behavior | Application on the job | Follow-up survey (separate) | 30-90 days after |
| Level 4: Results | Organizational impact | Impact assessment (separate) | 6-12 months after |

**This guide covers Levels 1 and 2 only** (administered during the training day). Levels 3 and 4 require separate instruments administered after the training.

---

## Decision Matrix: Which Evaluation Components to Generate

| Training Characteristic | Pre/Post Test | Satisfaction Survey | Skills Rubric |
|------------------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Knowledge-focused, Category A basics | Yes | Yes | No |
| Skill-focused, hands-on workshop | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Short training (2-4 hours) | Combined 5-item check | Brief (5 items) | No |
| Full-day (6-8 hours) | Full 10-item | Full (8-10 items) | If applicable |
| Multi-day training | Full 10-item per day | Full + daily pulse | Yes |
| Awareness/orientation only | Optional | Yes | No |

## Component 1: Pre-Test / Post-Test

### Purpose

Measure knowledge gain by comparing scores before and after the training. The same instrument (or a parallel version) is used for both pre-test and post-test.

### Design Principles

1. **Alignment to learning objectives**: Every test item must map to a specific learning objective (LO) from the training design
2. **Coverage**: At least 2 items per learning objective
3. **Item types**: Mix of True/False, Multiple Choice, and Short Answer
4. **Difficulty**: Match the target proficiency level in the training design (usually "Developing" for Category A)
5. **Accessibility**: Items must be administerable orally for participants with limited reading proficiency
6. **Language**: Clear, unambiguous phrasing; avoid double negatives; use Filipino/local language equivalents for key terms where helpful

### Item Type Guidelines

**True/False Items**
- State a clear, unambiguous proposition
- Avoid "always," "never," "all" (which make items too easy)
- Incorrect statements should reflect common misconceptions, not absurdities
- Example: "In a cooperative, each member has one vote regardless of how much capital they contributed. (True/False)"

**Multiple Choice Items**
- Stem (question) should be a complete, clear question
- 3 options (a, b, c) -- not 4; keeps it accessible for lower literacy levels
- All distractors should be plausible but clearly wrong
- Avoid "all of the above" and "none of the above"
- Example: "Who owns a cooperative? (a) The government (b) The members (c) The board of directors"

**Short Answer Items**
- Require a brief response (1-2 sentences or a single term)
- Provide clear parameters for what constitutes an acceptable answer
- Use sparingly (max 2-3 per test) as they take longer to administer orally
- Example: "Name one of the seven cooperative principles."

### Standard Pre-Test / Post-Test Structure

```
COOPERATIVE AND SOCIAL ENTERPRISE AUTHORITY
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao

{PRE-TEST / POST-TEST}
{Course Title} ({Course Code})

Name: ________________________________
Cooperative: ________________________________
Date: ________________________________

Instructions: Answer all items. For True/False, write T or F.
For Multiple Choice, circle the letter of the correct answer.
For Short Answer, write your response in the space provided.

This test has {N} items. You have {X} minutes to complete it.

1. {Item text} ({Item type})
   {Options if MC}
   LO: {hidden -- for facilitator reference only}

2. {Item text}
   ...

[Continue for all items]

Score: _____ / {N}
```

### Sourcing Test Items

**If the training design includes Section 15 (Assessment Instruments):**
- Use those items directly
- Verify alignment to learning objectives
- Add items if coverage is insufficient (less than 2 per LO)

**If the training design does NOT include assessment instruments:**
- Generate 10 items total (for 6-8 hour trainings)
- Generate 5 items total (for 2-4 hour trainings)
- Distribution: approximately 40% True/False, 40% Multiple Choice, 20% Short Answer
- Map each item to a specific learning objective

## Component 2: Satisfaction Survey (Kirkpatrick Level 1)

### Purpose

Measure participants' reaction to the training: Was it relevant? Was it well-facilitated? Was the time well-spent? This provides immediate feedback for improving future training delivery.

### Standard Satisfaction Survey Template

```
COOPERATIVE AND SOCIAL ENTERPRISE AUTHORITY
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao

TRAINING EVALUATION FORM
{Course Title} ({Course Code})
Date: ________________  Venue: ________________

Please rate the following on a scale of 1 to 5:
1 = Strongly Disagree  2 = Disagree  3 = Neutral  4 = Agree  5 = Strongly Agree

SECTION A: CONTENT AND RELEVANCE

A1. The training content was relevant to my role in the cooperative.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

A2. The learning objectives were clearly stated and achieved.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

A3. The topics covered were appropriate for my level of experience.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

A4. I can apply what I learned to my work in the cooperative.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]


SECTION B: FACILITATION

B1. The facilitator explained concepts clearly and effectively.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

B2. The facilitator encouraged participation and questions.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

B3. The facilitator was knowledgeable about the subject matter.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]


SECTION C: TRAINING DELIVERY

C1. The training materials (handouts, slides) were helpful.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

C2. The activities and exercises enhanced my understanding.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

C3. The time allocated for each topic was adequate.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

C4. The venue and logistics were satisfactory.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]


SECTION D: OVERALL ASSESSMENT

D1. Overall, I am satisfied with this training.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]

D2. I would recommend this training to other cooperative members.
    [ 1 ]  [ 2 ]  [ 3 ]  [ 4 ]  [ 5 ]


SECTION E: OPEN-ENDED FEEDBACK

E1. What was the most useful part of this training?
    _______________________________________________
    _______________________________________________

E2. What could be improved in future sessions?
    _______________________________________________
    _______________________________________________

E3. What additional topics would you like to learn about?
    _______________________________________________
    _______________________________________________

E4. Any other comments or suggestions:
    _______________________________________________
    _______________________________________________


Thank you for your feedback. Your responses will help us improve future trainings.
```

### Adapting the Satisfaction Survey

**For short trainings (2-4 hours):**
- Reduce to 5 Likert items (A1, B1, C2, C3, D1)
- Keep 2 open-ended items (E1, E2)

**For multi-day trainings:**
- Use the full survey on the final day
- Use a brief 3-item "daily pulse" at the end of each intermediate day:
  1. Today's sessions were useful (1-5)
  2. The pace was appropriate (1-5)
  3. One thing to improve for tomorrow: _______________

**For skill-focused trainings:**
- Add a self-efficacy section:
  "After this training, I am confident I can:"
  - [ ] {Skill 1 from learning objectives} (1-5 scale)
  - [ ] {Skill 2} (1-5 scale)
  - [ ] {Skill 3} (1-5 scale)

## Component 3: Skills Demonstration Rubric (When Applicable)

### When to Include

Include a skills rubric when the training design:
- Involves Tier 3 (Collaborative Production) activities
- Has learning objectives with action verbs like "develop," "formulate," "create," "demonstrate"
- Produces tangible outputs (e.g., MVO statement, Theory of Change canvas, business plan)

### Standard Rubric Structure

```
SKILLS DEMONSTRATION RUBRIC
{Course Title} ({Course Code})

Participant/Group: ________________________________
Activity: {Activity name from session plan}

| Criterion | Exceeds (4) | Meets (3) | Approaching (2) | Beginning (1) | Score |
|-----------|------------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|-------|
| {Criterion 1 from LO} | {Descriptor} | {Descriptor} | {Descriptor} | {Descriptor} | /4 |
| {Criterion 2} | ... | ... | ... | ... | /4 |
| {Criterion 3} | ... | ... | ... | ... | /4 |
| {Criterion 4} | ... | ... | ... | ... | /4 |

Total: _____ / {Max}

Facilitator Notes:
_______________________________________________
```

### Rubric Criteria Guidelines

- Derive criteria directly from learning objectives
- Use observable, measurable descriptors
- 3-5 criteria per rubric (keep it manageable for the facilitator)
- Descriptors should be specific enough that two facilitators would give similar scores
- For BARMM context: consider that output quality may vary due to literacy levels; focus on conceptual understanding over written eloquence

## Combined Evaluation Form (For Short Trainings)

For trainings of 2-4 hours, combine the knowledge check and satisfaction survey into a single page:

```
COOPERATIVE AND SOCIAL ENTERPRISE AUTHORITY
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao

TRAINING EVALUATION
{Course Title} ({Course Code})
Date: ________________

Name (optional): ________________________________

PART A: KNOWLEDGE CHECK (5 items)

1. {Item} (T/F)
2. {Item} (MC)
3. {Item} (T/F)
4. {Item} (MC)
5. {Item} (Short Answer)

PART B: YOUR FEEDBACK

Rate from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree):

1. The training was relevant to my work.         [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ]
2. The facilitator was effective.                 [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ]
3. The activities helped me learn.                [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ]
4. The time was well-spent.                       [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ]
5. I would recommend this training to others.     [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ]

What was most useful? _________________________________
What could be improved? _________________________________

Thank you!
```

## Accessibility Considerations for BARMM Context

1. **Oral administration**: All evaluation items must be readable aloud by the facilitator for participants with limited reading proficiency
2. **Simple language**: Avoid complex sentence structures; use short, direct sentences
3. **Visual scales**: Use numbered boxes rather than descriptive anchors alone (numbers are more universally understood)
4. **Multilingual support**: Facilitators should be prepared to explain items in Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug, or Sama as needed
5. **Anonymous option**: Satisfaction surveys should allow anonymous submission to encourage honest feedback (name field marked "optional")
6. **Time allocation**: Allow extra time for completion if administering orally (approximately 1.5x the standard time)

## Scoring and Analysis Notes (For Facilitator Guide)

Include these guidelines in the Facilitator Guide's assessment administration section:

**Pre/Post Test Scoring:**
- Score each item as correct (1) or incorrect (0)
- Calculate percentage score: (correct / total) x 100
- Learning gain = Post-test % minus Pre-test %
- Target: Average learning gain of at least 20 percentage points

**Satisfaction Survey Analysis:**
- Calculate mean score per item
- Calculate overall mean across all Likert items
- Flag any item with mean below 3.0 for immediate attention
- Compile open-ended responses thematically

**Reporting:**
- Submit completed pre/post tests and evaluation forms to CSEA-BARMM Training Records
- Retain copies for the training provider's records
- Include summary statistics in the post-training report
