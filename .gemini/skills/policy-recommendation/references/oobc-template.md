# OOBC Policy Recommendation Template

> **Agency-specific adaptation** of `generic-template.md` for the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities. Demonstrates how to customize the generic template for a specific BARMM agency.

## What's Different from the Generic Template

| Element | Generic Template | OOBC Adaptation |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Reference number | `[AGENCY]-PR-YYYY-XXX` | `OOBC-PR-YYYY-XXX` |
| Evidence sources | Agency-specific data | OBC-MANA assessment data + diaspora consultations |
| Target population | Varies by agency | Bangsamoro communities OUTSIDE the region |
| Strategic alignment | BDP + agency mandate | BDP + 6 OOBC goal areas |
| Stakeholders | Varies | Host LGUs, national agencies (DepEd, DSWD, DOLE), OBC community leaders |
| Key distinction | — | OOBC serves OBCs outside BARMM; OSC serves settlers inside BARMM |

## OOBC Goal Areas

When aligning with OOBC's mandate, reference one or more of these 6 goal areas:
1. **Institutional Strengthening** — building OOBC capacity and field presence
2. **Rights Protection** — legal aid, documentation, anti-discrimination
3. **Social Development** — education, health, social welfare for OBCs
4. **Economic Development** — livelihood, employment, enterprise for OBCs
5. **Cultural Preservation** — Bangsamoro identity, language, cultural programs
6. **Peace Advocacy** — peacebuilding, interreligious dialogue, social cohesion

## Reference Number Format

`OOBC-PR-[YYYY]-[XXX]`
- YYYY: Year of issuance
- XXX: Sequential number (e.g., 001, 002)

## OOBC-Specific Sections

The generic template structure applies fully. Below are OOBC-specific guidance notes for key sections:

### Section 1.2 — Situation Analysis (OOBC context)
- Use **OBC-MANA** (Mapping and Needs Assessment) data as primary evidence
- Include diaspora community consultations (FGDs, community assemblies)
- Reference geographic distribution of OBC communities (NCR, Visayas, Davao, other Mindanao)

### Section 1.4 — Strategic Alignment (OOBC mandate)
- Cite **BAA No. 13** (OOBC enabling legislation) for mandate
- Align with the relevant OOBC goal area(s) from the list above
- Reference BDP Chapter on OBCs and diaspora communities

### Section 2 — Stakeholder Analysis (OOBC network)
Typical OOBC stakeholders:
- **Primary**: OBC community members, OBC leaders and organizations
- **Secondary**: OOBC field offices, host LGUs, MILG, MBHTE, MSSD
- **Tertiary**: DepEd-Central Office, DSWD-Central, DOLE, CSOs, development partners
- **National**: Philippine Commission on Muslim Filipinos, NCMF

### Section 3.1 — Institutional Arrangements (OOBC as lead)
- **Lead**: OOBC (mandate under BAA No. 13)
- **Co-implementers**: Relevant BARMM ministry (MBHTE for education, MSSD for welfare, MAFAR for agriculture)
- **LGU coordination**: Through host LGU MOAs (OOBC provides staffing/curriculum; LGU provides facility/utilities)
- **National coordination**: Through OOBC-national agency MOAs

### Section 4.2 — Resource Requirements (OOBC funding)
- Primary source: BARMM General Appropriations Act (OOBC budget line)
- Secondary: BARMM Special Development Fund
- External: Development partner grants (UNDP, JICA, etc.)

## Full Document Structure

Follow `generic-template.md` Tier 2 structure exactly. The sections above provide OOBC-specific content guidance within that standard structure.

### APPENDICES (OOBC additions)
In addition to standard Appendices A-G, OOBC recommendations may include:
- H. OBC-MANA Summary Data
- I. Community Consultation Records (with participant lists)
- J. Map of Affected OBC Communities

## Standard BAA References for OOBC Policy Documents

> **Quick-reference checklist** — use this section to ensure every OOBC policy document cites the correct BAAs. Core BAAs should appear in ALL documents; topic-specific BAAs should be cited when the document touches their relevant policy area.

### Core BAAs (cite in ALL OOBC documents)

These two references establish the foundational mandate for OBC work and must appear in every OOBC policy recommendation:

| BAA | Title / Source | Why It's Core | Key Provision |
|-----|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| BOL (RA 11054) | Bangsamoro Organic Law | Foundational OBC mandate | **Section 12, Article VI** — establishes the right of Bangsamoro people outside the region to participate in governance |
| BAA No. 13 | Bangsamoro Administrative Code | Establishes OOBC as an agency | **Secs. 5-8, Chapter D** — defines OOBC's organizational structure, powers, and functions |

### Topic-Specific BAAs (cite when relevant)

Include these BAAs when the policy document addresses their corresponding topic areas:

| BAA | Title | Relevant Topics | Key Sections |
|-----|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| BAA 6 | Socio-Economic Development Planning | All planning-related | Development planning framework |
| BAA 8 | Bangsamoro Women's Commission | Women/Youth | OBC women on advisory council |
| BAA 10 | Bangsamoro Youth Commission | Scholarships, Women/Youth | Sec. 7(k) explicitly includes "Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM" |
| BAA 18 | Education Code | Scholarships, Madaris | Sec. 117 (Madrasah subsidy) |
| BAA 19 | Overseas Bangsamoro Workers | Social Services | OBC diaspora welfare |
| BAA 31 | Sustainable Development Board | Infrastructure, Governance, Partnerships | Sec. 3(2) ("rights of settler communities") |
| BAA 49 | Local Governance Code | Governance Participation | Local development councils |
| BAA 50 | Kulliyyah for Islamic Studies | Madaris, Scholarships | Islamic education scholarships |
| BAA 59 | Agriculture & Fisheries Training | Halal Enterprise, Tourism | Sec. 10 (Halal industry development) |
| BAA 62 | IDP Rights | Social Services, Governance | Sec. 4 (displacement outside BARMM) |
| BAA 64 | Indigenous Peoples' Act | Cultural Development | Cultural heritage framework |

### Usage Guide

When drafting an OOBC policy recommendation:
1. **Always cite** BOL Section 12, Article VI and BAA No. 13 in Section 1.4 (Strategic Alignment)
2. **Scan the topic columns** above — if your document touches any listed topic, cite the corresponding BAA
3. **Quote specific sections** (e.g., "pursuant to BAA No. 10, Sec. 7(k)") rather than just naming the BAA
4. **Cross-reference**: Many OBC policies span multiple topics — a scholarship program may need BAA 10, BAA 18, and BAA 50
