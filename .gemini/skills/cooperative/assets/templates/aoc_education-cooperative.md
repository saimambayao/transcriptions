## Article I
### Name of the Cooperative

That the name of this Cooperative shall be _____________________ ______________________________________ Education Cooperative.

---

## Article II
### Purposes

That the purposes for which this Cooperative is organized are to engage in:

1. School Operations;

2. Training Center;

3. Review Center;

4. Promoting and advancing the economic and social status of the members;

5. Coordinating and facilitating the activities of cooperatives;

6. Advocating for the cause of the cooperative movements;

7. Ensuring the viability of cooperatives through the utilization of new technologies; and

8. Encouraging and promoting self-help or self-employment as an engine for economic growth and poverty alleviation.

> **Note:** Items 1-3 specify the educational services. Additional purposes may be added as applicable (e.g., tutorial services, special education, scholarship programs, etc.)

---

## Article III
### Goals

That the goals of this Cooperative are to help improve the quality of life of its members and thereby contribute to inclusive growth, enterprise development and employment. In furtherance thereto, it shall aim:

1. To attain increased income, savings, investments, productivity, and purchasing power, and promote among themselves equitable distribution of net surplus through maximum utilization of economies of scale, cost-sharing and risk-sharing;

2. To provide optimum social and economic benefits to its members;

3. To teach members efficient ways of doing things in a Cooperative manner;

4. To propagate Cooperative practices and innovative ideas in business undertakings and management;

5. To empower through provision of access, ownership, control and opportunities to the poor, vulnerable, lower income and less privileged groups to increase their ownership in the wealth of the nation;

6. To actively support the government, other Cooperatives and people oriented organizations, both local and foreign, in promoting Cooperatives as a practical means towards sustainable socio-economic development under a truly just and democratic society;

7. To develop a dynamic savings mobilization and capital build-up schemes to sustain its developmental activities and long-term investments, thereby ensuring optimum economic benefits to the members, their families and the communities;

8. To adopt membership expansion mechanism/scheme, thereby ensuring growth of the Cooperative movement;

9. To implement policy guidelines that will ensure transparency, accountability and equitable access to its resources and services, and promote the interests of the members;

10. To adopt such other plans as may help foster the welfare of the members, their families and the community;

11. To advance the competitiveness and innovativeness of the industry;

12. To coordinate with other Cooperatives on learning exchanges, coop trade, and information exchanges in fostering sustainable development;

13. To advocate legal framework and enabling policies appropriate for the development of education Cooperatives; and

14. To be the voice and the institution of the poor and the excluded in resisting the growth-centered development aggression and instead promote people-centered development.

---

## Article IV
### Powers and Capacities

That the powers, rights and capacities of this Cooperative are those prescribed under Article 9 of Republic Act 9520.

1. To the exclusive use of its registered name;

2. To sue and be sued;

3. Of succession;

4. To amend its Articles of Cooperation in accordance with the provisions of RA 9520;

5. To adopt by-laws not contrary to law, morals or public policy, and to amend and repeal the same in accordance with RA 9520;

6. To purchase, receive, take or grant, hold, convey, sell, lease, pledge, mortgage, and otherwise deal with such real and personal property as the transaction of the lawful affairs of the Cooperative may reasonably and necessarily require, subject to the limitations prescribed by law and the Constitution;

7. To enter into division, merger, or consolidation, as provided under RA 9520;

8. To form subsidiary Cooperatives and join federations or unions, as provided in this Code;

9. To avail of loans, be entitled to credit and to accept and receive grants, donations and assistance from foreign and domestic sources subject to the conditions of said loans, credits, grants, donations or assistance that will not undermine the autonomy of the cooperative. The Authority, upon written request, shall provide necessary assistance in the documentary requirements for the loans, credit, grants, donations and other financial support;

10. To avail preferential rights granted to Cooperatives under RA 7160, otherwise known as the Local Government Code, and other laws, particularly those in the grant of franchises to establish, construct, operate and maintain ferries, wharves, markets or slaughterhouses and to lease public utilities, including access to extension and on-site research services and facilities related to agriculture and fishery activities;

11. To exercise such other powers granted under RA 9520 or necessary to carry out its purposes as stated in this Articles of Cooperation; and

12. To act as Guardian Cooperative and accept the responsibilities of supervising and monitoring the activities of the Laboratory Cooperative and act in its behalf in dealings with third parties when capacity to contract is required. *(applicable to Guardian Cooperative only)*

## About Education Cooperatives

### Definition

An Education Cooperative is a service cooperative organized to provide educational services such as school operations, training centers, review centers, tutorial services, and other educational programs. It aims to make quality education accessible and affordable to members and the community.

### Key Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Type** | Service Cooperative (Education) |
| **Common Bond** | Residential, Institutional, Associational, or Occupational |
| **Members** | Educators, parents, students (of legal age), community members |
| **Focus** | Educational services and programs |
| **Share Structure** | Common and Preferred shares |

### Types of Educational Services (Article II)

| Service Type | Description | Regulatory Agency |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **School Operations** | Basic education (K-12), Alternative Learning System | DepEd |
| **Higher Education** | College, university programs | CHED |
| **Training Center** | Skills training, livelihood programs | TESDA |
| **Review Center** | Board exam reviews, entrance exam prep | DepEd/CHED |
| **Tutorial Services** | Remedial, enrichment, academic support | DepEd |
| **Special Education** | SPED programs, inclusive education | DepEd |
| **Distance Learning** | Online, modular, blended learning | DepEd/CHED |

### Regulatory Framework

Education Cooperatives must comply with education sector regulations:

| Agency | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **CDA** | Cooperative registration and compliance |
| **DepEd** | Basic education (K-12), ALS, review centers |
| **CHED** | Higher education programs |
| **TESDA** | Technical-vocational education and training |
| **PRC** | Professional review programs |
| **LGU** | Business permits, local compliance |

### Permit Requirements by Service Type

| Service | Required Permits |
|---------|------------------|
| **Basic Education School** | DepEd Recognition/Permit, Government Recognition |
| **Higher Education** | CHED Permit to Operate, Government Recognition |
| **TESDA Training Center** | TESDA Registration, Program Accreditation |
| **Review Center** | DepEd/CHED Permit (depending on level) |
| **Tutorial Center** | LGU Business Permit, DepEd Permit (if applicable) |

### Key Benefits for Members

- Affordable tuition and educational services
- Scholarship programs for members and dependents
- Democratic governance of educational institution
- Patronage refunds based on tuition/service usage
- Access to educational loans and financing
- Member discounts on school supplies and materials
- Priority enrollment for members' dependents
- Quality education with community accountability

### Services of Education Cooperatives

| Service Category | Examples |
|------------------|----------|
| **Academic Programs** | Basic education, college courses, graduate programs |
| **Training Programs** | Skills training, livelihood courses, professional development |
| **Support Services** | Tutorial, remedial, enrichment classes |
| **Review Services** | Board exam review, entrance exam preparation |
| **Scholarship Programs** | Merit-based, need-based financial assistance |
| **Educational Loans** | Tuition loans, educational financing |
| **Learning Materials** | Bookstore, supplies, uniforms |

### Cooperative Schools in the Philippines

Notable examples of education cooperatives:

- Cooperative-owned schools providing K-12 education
- Training centers registered with TESDA
- Review centers for professional board examinations
- Community-based learning centers

### Financial Sustainability

Education Cooperatives should ensure:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Tuition Setting** | Affordable while ensuring quality and sustainability |
| **Enrollment Management** | Optimal student population |
| **Program Mix** | Balance of programs based on demand |
| **Auxiliary Services** | Canteen, bookstore, transport |
| **Scholarship Fund** | ETF allocation for member scholarships |
| **Capital Investment** | Facilities, equipment, technology |

### Education and Training Fund (ETF)

Under RA 9520, cooperatives must allocate up to 10% of net surplus to ETF. For Education Cooperatives, this fund is especially important for:

- Scholarship programs for members and dependents
- Faculty and staff development
- Curriculum enhancement
- Educational materials and resources
- Cooperative education integration in curriculum

### BARMM Context

In Bangsamoro, education cooperatives are important for:

- Providing accessible education in underserved areas
- Integrating Islamic values in education (Madrasah)
- Supporting mother tongue-based multilingual education
- Promoting peace education and cultural preservation
- Complementing government education programs

### Membership Composition

Education Cooperatives may have diverse membership:

| Member Type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Educators** | Teachers, professors, trainers |
| **Parents** | Parents of students |
| **Alumni** | Former students of the cooperative school |
| **Community Members** | Residents supporting education |
| **Students** | Of legal age (18 years old and above) |

### Guardian Cooperative Provision

Article IV, Item 12 provides that an Education Cooperative may act as a **Guardian Cooperative** for Laboratory Cooperatives. This means:

- Supervising and monitoring Laboratory Cooperative activities
- Acting on behalf of Laboratory Cooperatives in dealings with third parties
- Providing guidance during the formation period

> **Note:** This provision is applicable only when the cooperative agrees to serve as a Guardian Cooperative.
