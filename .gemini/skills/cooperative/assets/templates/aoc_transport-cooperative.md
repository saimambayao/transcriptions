## Article I
### Name of the Cooperative

That the name of this Cooperative shall be _____________________ ______________________________________ Transport Cooperative.

---

## Article II
### Purposes

That the purposes for which this Cooperative is organized are to engage in the following:

1. Provide public transport services primarily to members and the commuting public (land and sea/water transportation services, and is limited to small vessels, as defined or classified under the Philippine Maritime laws for the safe conveyance of passengers and/or cargoes);

2. Engage in allied services or businesses such as:
   - a. Importation, distribution and marketing of petroleum products in accordance with existing laws;
   - b. Operation of gasoline stations and transportation service centers;
   - c. Importation, distribution and marketing of spare parts and supplies; and
   - d. Marketing of vehicle/drivers insurance policies.

3. Promote and advance the economic and social status of the members;

4. Coordinate and facilitate the activities of cooperatives;

5. Advocate for the cause of the cooperative movement;

6. Ensure the viability of cooperatives through the utilization of new technologies; and

7. Encourage and promote self-help or self-employment as an engine for economic growth and poverty alleviation.

---

## Article III
### Goals

That the goals of this Cooperative are to help improve the quality of life of its members and thereby contribute to inclusive growth, enterprise development and employment. In furtherance thereto, it shall aim:

1. To attain increased income, savings, investments, productivity, and purchasing power, and promote among themselves equitable distribution of net surplus through maximum utilization of economies of scale, cost-sharing and risk-sharing;

2. To provide optimum social and economic benefits to its members;

3. To teach members efficient ways of doing things in a cooperative manner;

4. To propagate cooperative practices and innovative ideas in business undertakings and management;

5. To empower through the provision of access, ownership, control and opportunities to the poor, vulnerable, lower income and less privileged groups to increase their ownership in the wealth of the nation;

6. To actively support the government, other cooperatives and people oriented organizations, both local and foreign, in promoting Cooperatives as a practical means towards sustainable socio-economic development under a truly just and democratic society;

7. To develop dynamic savings mobilization and capital build-up schemes to sustain its developmental activities and long-term investments, thereby ensuring optimum economic benefits to the members, their families and the communities;

8. To adopt membership expansion mechanism/schemes, thereby ensuring the growth of the cooperative movement;

9. To implement policy guidelines that will ensure transparency, accountability and equitable access to its resources and services, and promote the interests of the members;

10. To adopt such other plans as may help foster the welfare of the members, their families and the community;

11. To advance the competitiveness and innovativeness of the industry;

12. To coordinate with other cooperatives on learning exchanges, coop trade, and information exchanges in fostering sustainable development;

13. To advocate legal frameworks and enabling policies appropriate for the development of transport cooperatives; and

14. To be the voice and the institution of the poor and the marginalized in resisting the growth-centered development aggression and instead promote people-centered development.

---

## Article IV
### Powers and Capacities

That the powers, rights and capacities of this Cooperative are those prescribed under Article 9 of Republic Act 9520, as follows:

1. To the exclusive use of its registered name;

2. To sue and be sued;

3. Of succession;

4. To amend its Articles of Cooperation in accordance with the provisions of RA 9520;

5. To adopt By-laws not contrary to law, morals or public policy, and to amend and repeal the same in accordance with RA 9520;

6. To purchase, receive, take or grant, hold, convey, sell, lease, pledge, mortgage, and otherwise deal with such real and personal property as the transaction of the lawful affairs of the Cooperative may reasonably and necessarily require, subject to the limitations prescribed by law and the Constitution;

7. To enter into division, merger, or consolidation, as provided under RA 9520;

8. To form subsidiary Cooperatives and join federations or unions, as provided in this Code;

9. To avail of loans, be entitled to credit and to accept and receive grants, donations and assistance from foreign and domestic sources subject to the conditions of said loans, credits, grants, donations or assistance that will not undermine the autonomy of the cooperative. The Authority, upon written request, shall provide necessary assistance in the documentary requirements for the loans, credit, grants, donations and other financial support;

10. To avail of preferential rights granted to cooperatives under RA 7160, otherwise known as the Local Government Code, and other laws, particularly those in the grant of franchises to establish, construct, operate and maintain ferries, wharves, markets or slaughterhouses and to lease public utilities, including access to extension and on-site research services and facilities related to agriculture and fishery activities;

11. To exercise such other powers granted under RA 9520 or necessary to carry out its purposes as stated in this Articles of Cooperation; and

12. To act as Guardian Cooperative and accept the responsibilities of supervising and monitoring the activities of the Laboratory Cooperative and act in its behalf in dealings with third parties when capacity to contract is required. *(applicable to Guardian Cooperative only)*

## About Transport Cooperatives

### Definition

A Transport Cooperative is a service cooperative organized by operators and/or drivers engaged in land, sea, or air transportation services. It provides public transport services to members and the commuting public while promoting the welfare of transport workers.

### Key Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Type** | Service Cooperative (Transport) |
| **Common Bond** | Occupational, Residential, or Associational |
| **Members** | Transport operators, drivers, conductors |
| **Focus** | Public transportation services |
| **Share Structure** | Common and Preferred shares |
| **Special Article** | Article VIII - Business Operation (franchise-based) |

### Types of Transport Services

| Mode | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Land Transport** | Jeepneys, buses, tricycles, UV Express, taxis, TNVS |
| **Sea/Water Transport** | Small vessels, ferries, motorboats (as defined under Philippine Maritime laws) |
| **Freight/Cargo** | Trucking, delivery services |

### Allied Services (Article II)

Transport cooperatives may engage in:

1. **Petroleum Products**
   - Importation and distribution
   - Gasoline station operation

2. **Transportation Service Centers**
   - Terminals and dispatch facilities
   - Maintenance and repair shops

3. **Spare Parts and Supplies**
   - Importation and distribution
   - Parts store operation

4. **Insurance Services**
   - Vehicle insurance marketing
   - Driver insurance policies

### Franchise Requirements (Article VIII)

Transport cooperatives must operate based on:

| Document | Issuing Agency | Purpose |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| Certificate of Public Convenience (CPC) | LTFRB | Land transport franchise |
| Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity (CPCN) | LTFRB/MARINA | Transport authorization |
| Provisional Authority (PA) | LTFRB | Temporary operation permit |
| Special Permit | LTFRB | Special routes/services |

### Regulatory Compliance

Transport Cooperatives must comply with:

| Requirement | Authority |
|-------------|-----------|
| Cooperative registration | CDA |
| Transport franchise | LTFRB |
| Vehicle registration | LTO |
| Driver's license compliance | LTO |
| Business permits | LGU |
| Tax compliance | BIR |
| Maritime registration (if applicable) | MARINA |

### PUV Modernization Program

Under the DOTr PUV Modernization Program:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Fleet Management** | Cooperative fleet operation |
| **Cashless Payment** | AFC/AFCS implementation |
| **Modern Vehicles** | Euro 4/Electric vehicles |
| **Route Rationalization** | Optimized route networks |
| **GPS Tracking** | Real-time vehicle monitoring |

### Key Benefits for Members

- Collective franchise application and management
- Bulk fuel purchasing at discounted rates
- Group insurance at lower premiums
- Access to vehicle financing programs
- Training and capacity building
- Legal assistance and representation
- Dispatch and terminal facilities
- Maintenance and repair services

### Membership Requirements

Typical qualifications for transport cooperative members:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Operators** | Valid franchise/CPC holder |
| **Drivers** | Valid professional driver's license |
| **Age** | Of legal age (18 years old) |
| **Citizenship** | Filipino citizen |
| **Character** | Good moral character |
| **Residence** | Within area of operation |

### Guardian Cooperative Provision

Article IV, Item 12 provides that a Transport Cooperative may act as a **Guardian Cooperative** for Laboratory Cooperatives. This means:

- Supervising and monitoring Laboratory Cooperative activities
- Acting on behalf of Laboratory Cooperatives in dealings with third parties
- Providing guidance during the formation period

> **Note:** This provision is applicable only when the cooperative agrees to serve as a Guardian Cooperative.
