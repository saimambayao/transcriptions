## BARMM Vision and Development Framework

*Source: Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028, Chapter 4*

### Vision

> "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

The Bangsamoro government envisions a territory devoid of all types of conflict - one that is permanently peaceful and where residents enjoy a sense of security and comfort as well as just, morally upright, and forward-thinking leadership.

### Mission

> "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

### Overall Goal

An **empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro**:
- **Empowered**: Improved capacity of communities through better access to quality basic social services and widening economic opportunities
- **Cohesive**: Improved relationships between stakeholders, reduced violent conflict, and forged partnerships in governance
- **Progressive**: Sustained ability to optimize resources in addressing development needs and realizing economic and human potential

### Six Development Goals (BDP 2023-2028)

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Goal 1** | Stable, just, and accountable government |
| **Goal 2** | Equitable, competitive, and robust economy |
| **Goal 3** | Peaceful, safe, and resilient Bangsamoro communities |
| **Goal 4** | Inclusive, responsive, and quality social services |
| **Goal 5** | Rich and diverse Bangsamoro culture and identity preserved and recognized |
| **Goal 6** | Strategic, adequate, and climate-resilient infrastructure |

### Development Strategies (BDP 2023-2028)

1. Improve and strengthen governance mechanisms for a stable, just, and accountable Bangsamoro government
2. Create an enabling environment towards an equitable, competitive, and robust economy
3. Harness technology and innovations to increase socioeconomic opportunities and improve government services
4. Improve ecological integrity, resilience of communities, and ensure a healthy and clean environment
5. Enhance and strengthen peace, public order, safety, security and uphold human rights for inclusive, peaceful, progressive, and sustainable development
6. Ensure inclusive and equitable access to quality services for social justice and human capital development
7. Mainstream Bangsamoro cultural diversity, beliefs, heritage, and identity
8. Scale-up functional, strategic, climate-resilient, and quality infrastructure to support sustainable socioeconomic development

### Pillars of Moral Governance

1. **Integrity** - No financial or other binding in execution of official duties
2. **Accountability** - Open and transparent decisions with documentation
3. **Transparency and honesty** - Public declaration of private interests
4. **Sense of patriotism** - Investment in heritage and reputation
5. **Striving for excellence** - Highest quality with process over results
6. **Cultural understanding and tolerance** - Awareness of socioeconomic differences
7. **Unwavering loyalty to God** - Following rules and regulations
8. **Justice** - Serving public without prejudice
9. **Inclusivity** - Welcoming environment free from discrimination
10. **Objectivity** - Merit-based decisions in public interest
11. **Dialogue and meaningful engagement** - Community consultation
12. **Balance between practical life and moral side** - Ethical governance

## CSEA Overview

### About CSEA

The **Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA)** is the Bangsamoro government agency mandated to regulate, develop, and promote cooperatives and social enterprises in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

**Full Name**: Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority

**Under**: Office of the Chief Minister, BARMM

**Previous Name**: Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) - BARMM operations

**Transition Status**: CSEA is in its transition phase from being the sole agency mandated to register cooperatives in the defunct ARMM - Cooperative Development Authority (CDA). The policies and guidelines for cooperatives were predominantly aligned with the previous entity, making the newly registered cooperatives, which were mainly composed of former combatants, encounter difficulties in complying with the requirements.

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapter 7*

### CSEA Mandate

1. Registration of cooperatives and social enterprises
2. Technical guidance and assistance
3. Capability building and training
4. Inspection, monitoring, and validation
5. Issuance of compliance certificates
6. Policy development for cooperative sector

## BTA Bill No. 210

### Overview

**BTA Bill No. 210** is the proposed **Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code** - a comprehensive legislation to govern cooperatives and social enterprises in BARMM.

**Status**: The Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise (BCSE) Bill is yet to be approved and passed by the Bangsamoro Parliament. The bill is essential for the operationalization of BCSE as it will serve as a legal basis for their existence and strategic planning and programming.

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapter 7*

**Scope**: 70 pages, 4 books

### Structure

| Book | Title | Coverage |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Book I** | General Provisions | Definitions, principles, CSEA mandate |
| **Book II** | Cooperative Development | Types, registration, governance, operations |
| **Book III** | Social Enterprise Development | SE definition, requirements, support mechanisms |
| **Book IV** | Miscellaneous & Final Provisions | Transitional, penalties, effectivity |

### Key Provisions

**Cooperative Definition**:
> Autonomous and duly registered association of individuals with a shared interest, operating according to established cooperative principles and moral governance standards.

**Social Enterprise Definition**:
> Business organizations primarily dedicated to addressing social, cultural, or environmental challenges, with profit as a secondary consideration.

### Governing Principles

The Code mandates alignment with:

1. **Universal Cooperative Principles**
   - Voluntary and open membership
   - Democratic member control
   - Member economic participation
   - Autonomy and independence
   - Education, training, and information
   - Cooperation among cooperatives
   - Concern for community

2. **Moral Governance**
   - Ethical conduct
   - Accountability
   - Transparency
   - Responsiveness to community

3. **Local Cultures and Values**
   - Respect for Bangsamoro identity
   - Integration of traditional practices
   - Community-centered approach

4. **Shari'ah Objectives**
   - Halal transactions
   - No usury (riba)
   - No gambling (maysir)
   - No speculative transactions (gharar)
   - Risk sharing principles

### Key Challenges (BDP 2023-2028)

The BDP 2023-2028 identifies the key problem as: **low functionality and unsustainable profitability of cooperatives**. This is mainly due to:

1. **Low compliance rate** - Only 11.66% of registered cooperatives able to secure compliance certificates; target is 50% compliance
2. **Lack of enacted Code** - The pending codification of the Cooperative Code and Social Enterprise Code currently slows down full operationalization and institutionalization of both Cooperatives and SE
3. **Inadequate LGU participation** - Most LGUs in the Bangsamoro Region have no appointed Municipal Cooperative Officers (MCOs). Regional cooperative officers find it difficult to coordinate at the LGU level. Establishment of provincial extension offices of CSEA is necessary.
4. **Newly registered cooperatives** - Mainly composed of former combatants encountering compliance difficulties due to policies aligned with the previous CDA entity

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapter 7*

## BDP 2023-2028 Cooperative Sector Goals

*Source: Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028, Chapter 7*

### Sectoral Goal

**Goal 2: Equitable, competitive, and robust economy**

- An equitable economy where economic gains benefit all with special consideration for the poor and marginalized
- Competitive economy with comparative and competitive advantage in selected commodities
- Robust economy with sustained growth and diversified, stabilized economy

### Sectoral Objective 2.5

**Functional Cooperatives and Social Enterprises Increased**

| Sub-sector Objective | Target |
|---------------------|--------|
| 2.5.1 | Profitability of cooperatives increased |
| 2.5.2 | Established social enterprises increased |

### Priority Challenge

**Low functionality and unsustainable profitability of cooperatives**

Root causes:
1. **Low compliance rate** - Only 11.66% compliant; target is 50% compliance
2. **Lack of enacted Code** - Pending Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code (however, extensive consultations show great potential for a uniquely Bangsamoro code)
3. **Inadequate LGU participation** - No appointed Municipal Cooperative Officers (MCOs) in most LGUs

### Priority Strategies (2023-2028)

**Strategy 21:** Ensure profitability and functionality of cooperatives through:
- Sufficient technical and financial assistance
- Institutionalization of monitoring and evaluation system and mechanism
- Community promotion
- Capacitation of human resources

**Strategy 22:** Institutionalize enabling policies for the establishment of social enterprises in the BARMM

### Priority Programs

#### 1. Development of Cooperatives and Social Enterprise Program

Aims to strengthen capacities of cooperatives to ensure profitability and functionality of business enterprises on social enterprises. Includes:

| Project | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **PESO Project** | Profitability and Equity Support Oriented - financial and technical assistance to improve cooperative profitability |
| **LAKAS SE Program** | Lakbay Aral sa Social Enterprise - learning program for social enterprise development |

#### 2. Combatant Transitional Program

Provides support to former rebels to organize themselves into cooperatives to legally conduct businesses. Provides financial and material support to cooperatives organized by former combatants. Includes:

| Project | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **STOCK Project** | Strengthening of Technical and Operational Knowledge - capacity building for former combatant cooperatives |
| **Cooperative Financial and Material Support Project** | Direct financial and material assistance to former combatant cooperatives |

### Related Agricultural Strategy

**Strategy 8:** Strengthen capacities of farmer's organizations and agri-cooperatives as the driver of agricultural development in partnership with LGUs and other development stakeholders including private sectors

### Social Enterprise Institutionalization

Several efforts are being made to institutionalize Social Enterprise (SE) in the region. Unlike cooperatives, which exist by virtue of RA 9520, there is a lack of national government legislation relating to SE. The Cooperatives and SE sub-sector are in continuing talks with the principal author of the Social Enterprise Bill as one of the steps to the enactment of the said bill.

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapter 7*

## Shari'ah Compliance

### Principles for Cooperatives in BARMM

Under BTA Bill No. 210, cooperatives must adhere to:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Halal Transactions** | Only permissible (halal) business activities |
| **Legitimate Wealth** | Earn through lawful means |
| **Asset-Based Transactions** | Real economic activity, not speculation |
| **Risk Sharing** | Equitable distribution of risks |
| **No Riba (Usury)** | No interest-based lending |
| **No Maysir (Gambling)** | No gambling or games of chance |
| **No Gharar (Uncertainty)** | Avoid excessive speculation |
| **No Haram Products** | No forbidden goods or services |

### Prohibited Activities

1. **Riba (Usury/Interest)**
   - No charging of interest on loans
   - Alternative: profit-sharing arrangements

2. **Maysir (Gambling)**
   - No games of chance
   - No lottery or raffle schemes

3. **Gharar (Excessive Uncertainty)**
   - No speculative transactions
   - Clear terms in all contracts

4. **Haram Products/Services**
   - No alcohol
   - No pork products
   - No conventional insurance
   - No activities contrary to Islamic law

### Shari'ah-Compliant Alternatives

| Conventional | Shari'ah Alternative |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Interest loans | Murabaha (cost-plus) |
| Interest savings | Wadiah (safekeeping) |
| Insurance | Takaful (mutual guarantee) |
| Joint venture | Musharakah (partnership) |
| Profit sharing | Mudarabah |

## Registration with CSEA

### Documentary Requirements

Same as CDA requirements:

1. Cooperative Name Reservation Slip
2. Articles of Cooperation (4 copies, notarized)
3. By-Laws (4 copies, signed by all)
4. Economic Survey (4 copies)
5. Treasurer's Affidavit
6. Surety Bond of Accountable Officers
7. PRS Certificate from accredited institution
8. Registration Fee

### Process

```
1. RESERVE cooperative name
          |
2. CONDUCT economic survey
          |
3. COMPLETE Pre-Registration Seminar
          |
4. PREPARE documents
          |
5. SUBMIT to CSEA Office
          |
6. CSEA REVIEWS and evaluates
          |
7. RECEIVE Certificate of Registration
```

### Annual Compliance

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| General Assembly | Within 90 days after fiscal year |
| CAPR submission | Within 120 days after fiscal year |
| CAFS submission | Within 120 days after fiscal year |
| COC renewal | Before expiration |

### Additional BARMM Considerations

When registering or operating in BARMM:

1. **Ensure Shari'ah compliance** in bylaws and operations
2. **Align with Moral Governance** principles (12 pillars)
3. **Respect local cultures** and traditions
4. **Engage with community** stakeholders
5. **Support Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028** objectives

### Alignment with BDP 2023-2028

Cooperatives should align their operations with:

| BDP Element | Cooperative Application |

| Strategy 21 | Technical assistance, M&E, community promotion, human resource capacitation |

## BARMM Macroeconomic Context (BDP 2023-2028)

*Source: Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028, Chapter 4*

### Economic Targets for 2023-2028

| Indicator | Baseline | End-of-Plan Target |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| GRDP Annual Growth Rate | 7.5% (2020-2021) | 8%-9% for six years |
| GRDP Per Capita Growth | 6.4% (2020-2021) | 6%-9% |
| Industry & Services Growth | Industry 7.9%, Services 6.6% | 10%-12% |
| Gross Capital Formation | 16.91% of GRDP (2021) | 18%-20% of GRDP |
| Inflation Rate | 2.40% (2021) | 2%-4% |

| Underemployment Rate | 9.0% (2020) | 4%-7% |
| Poverty Incidence | 29.80% (2021) | 20%-25% |

### Poverty Context

- BARMM recorded the highest significant improvement in poverty incidence: from 52.6% in 2018 to 29.80% in 2021
- Approximately 408,951 Bangsamoro families live in poverty
- The region remains the poorest compared to other regions in the country
- Island provinces of BaSulTa are among clusters with relatively high incidence of poverty

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapter 9*

### Social Development Context

- **Population**: 4,944,800 (2020 census)
- **Population growth rate**: 3.26% (highest in the country)
- **Functional literacy rate**: 71.6% (lowest among regions)
- **Poverty incidence among families**: 29.80% (2021)

*Source: BDP 2023-2028, Chapters 4 & 9*
