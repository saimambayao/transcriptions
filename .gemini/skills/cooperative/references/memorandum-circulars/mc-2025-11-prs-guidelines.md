# CDA Memorandum Circular No. 2025-11

## Revised Guidelines for Conducting Pre-Registration Seminars for Prospective Cooperatives

## Overview

This circular establishes guidelines governing the conduct of Pre-Registration Seminars (PRS), covering the primary objectives, course outline, implementation mechanics, how to avail of PRS, and documented information requirements.

## Section I. Title

This Guidelines shall be known as the **Revised Guidelines for Conducting Pre-Registration Seminars (PRS) for Prospective Cooperatives**.

---

## Section II. Legal Basis

1. **RA 11364, Section 4.c** - Register all cooperatives including amendments to the Articles of Cooperation and By-laws (ACBL), division, merger and consolidation.

2. **CDA MC 2020-20** - Guidelines Governing the Registration of Cooperatives Through the Electronic Cooperative Registration Information System (E-CoopRIS)

3. **RA 11535, Article XVII, Sec. 487, 3.ii** - In collaboration with the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA), provide assistance to prospective cooperatives in the conduct of the required pre-registration seminar and/or pre-membership education seminar and in the preparation of required documents for registration.

---

## Section III. Purpose

This Memorandum Circular is hereby issued to establish guidelines governing the conduct of the PRS. This guideline covers:
- Primary objectives of conducting a PRS
- Course outline
- Implementation mechanics
- How to avail of the PRS
- Necessary documented information

---

## Section IV. Definition of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS)** | The seminar conducted by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) for prospective cooperatives. A Certificate of Attendance to a PRS issued by the Regional Director of the CDA Regional Extension Office where the PRS was conducted is one of the documentary requirements for registering a primary cooperative. |

---

## Section V. Implementation Mechanics

### 1. Administration
The Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS) shall be administered by the Regional Extension Offices through the **Cooperative Development Specialists (CDS)**, assisted by personnel from the Registration Section and the **Cooperative Research Information and Training Section (CRITS)**.

### 2. CDS Qualifications
The Regional Extension Office Director shall ensure that the designated CDS II shall have attended the training/orientation on how to conduct PRS conducted by CRITS and Registration Section. A Certificate of Completion, documenting their participation in the training/orientation, will be issued and included in their personnel records.

### 3. Venue
PRS sessions may be conducted at the Regional Extension Offices or in venues mutually agreed upon by the CDA and the requesting parties.

### 4. External Venue Requests
Requests for conduct of PRS in venues to be provided by the requesting party may be allowed subject to the condition that the **cost of rental of the venue shall be borne by the requesting party**.

### 5. Special Schedule Requests
In cases of requests for the conduct of PRS outside of the regular schedule of the Authority, the same shall be approved subject to the availability of CDA and **prior authorization from the Regional Director is mandatory**.

### 6. Schedule Posting
Schedule of PRS shall be posted on the **official social media platforms** of the respective Regional Extension Offices and may also be physically posted in conspicuous locations for wider visibility.

### 7. Free of Charge
The Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) shall conduct Pre-Registration Seminars (PRS) **free of charge**. No fees shall be collected from participants. For face-to-face sessions, participants shall be informed in advance that they will shoulder their own meal expenses. It is encouraged that the required number of prospective members for cooperative registration attend the PRS as a group. However, in cases where individual attendance is unavoidable, the PRS shall still be conducted.

### 8. Delivery Mode
PRS sessions can be conducted through either **online or traditional face-to-face** methods.

### 9. Certificate Issuance
A coded Certificate of Completion of the PRS shall be issued by the **Regional Director** to organizations whose required number of members for the registration have successfully completed the seminar.

### 10. Certificate Validity
A Certificate of Completion shall be valid for a period of **three (3) years** from the date of issuance.

---

## Section VI. Assistance of Local Government Units (LGUs) to Cooperatives

### 1. LGU Role
The Local Government Units (LGUs), through their **Cooperative Development Officers (CDOs)**, in collaboration with the CDA, provide assistance to prospective cooperatives in the conduct of the required pre-registration seminar and/or pre-membership education seminar and in the preparation of required documents for registration.

### 2. LGU Assistance to Prospective Cooperatives
LGUs through their CDOs may provide the following assistance to groups signifying to be organized as cooperatives:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **a.** | Provide venue, meals, and other facilities during the conduct of PRS |
| **b.** | Coordinate with CDA to conduct a PRS |
| **c.** | Conduct an orientation on cooperative development programs of the LGU, including situational analysis, and social preparation |
| **d.** | Provide facilities and logistical support for the online conduct of PRS |

---

## Section VII. PRS Course Outline

The PRS shall be conducted in accordance with the following course outline:

### Complete Course Outline

| Subject Matter | Objective | Content | Time Allotment |
|----------------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Preliminaries** | | Opening Program, Introduction of Participants | 10 minutes |
| **Objectives and Description of a PRS** | Enable participants to understand the reasons for a PRS | Description of the PRS, Objectives of a PRS | 20 minutes |
| **The CDA and its Mandate (AVP)** | | Salient provisions of RA 11364, Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs | 15 minutes |
| **Introduction to Cooperatives (AVP)** | | Cooperative Identity, Types and services of cooperatives, Purpose of cooperatives, Rights/privileges, duties and responsibilities of members, Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs | 2 hours |
| **Requirements and Process of Registering a cooperative** | | E-CoopRIS, Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs | 1 hour |
| **Include what to expect after the registration (AVP)** | | Ask Questions (FAQs) | *(included above)* |
| **TOTAL** | | | **3 Hours and 45 minutes** |

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 1: Preliminaries (10 minutes)
- Opening Program
- Introduction of Participants

#### Module 2: Objectives and Description of PRS (20 minutes)
**Objective**: Enable participants to understand the reasons for a PRS
- Description of the PRS
- Objectives of a PRS

#### Module 3: The CDA and its Mandate (15 minutes)
- Salient provisions of RA 11364
- Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs
- *(Uses Audio-Visual Presentation)*

#### Module 4: Introduction to Cooperatives (2 hours)
- Cooperative Identity
- Types and services of cooperatives
- Purpose of cooperatives
- Rights/privileges, duties and responsibilities of members of cooperatives
- Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs
- *(Uses Audio-Visual Presentation)*

#### Module 5: Requirements and Process of Registration (1 hour)
- E-CoopRIS (Electronic Cooperative Registration Information System)
- Facilitation of Q/A or FAQs
- What to expect after registration
- *(Uses Audio-Visual Presentation)*

## Section VIII. Documented Information

The Regional Extension Office shall maintain the following documented information related to the PRS:

| # | Document | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Attendance Sheet** | Names of participants, organization/group, address, gender, place where PRS was conducted, names of Resource Person/s during the PRS |
| 2 | **Digital Copy of Presentation Materials** | Presentation materials used during the PRS |
| 3 | **Action Photos** | Photos of the PRS conducted |

---

## Section IX. Data Privacy and Protection

In compliance with the **Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA No. 10173)**, CDA Regional Extension Offices, and other training organizers shall ensure the proper handling, storage, and protection of personal information collected during training activities.

### Covered Information
- Attendance sheets
- Registration forms
- Digital presentations
- Participant details
- Training documentation with identifying information

### Data Use Limitations
Collected data shall be used **solely** for purposes of:
- Training documentation
- Monitoring
- Reporting as required under this Circular

Data shall **NOT be shared with third parties** without the explicit consent of the data subjects, except as required by law.

### Safeguards Required
- Proper safeguards must be in place to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse of personal data
- All personnel handling such information must be trained on data privacy protocols and responsibilities

## BARMM/CSEA Context

### Important: CSEA Conducts PRS in BARMM

For cooperatives in BARMM, the **Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA)** conducts Pre-Registration Seminars, not CDA.

| Aspect | CDA (Rest of Philippines) | CSEA (BARMM) |

| **PRS Conductor** | CDA Regional Extension Offices | CSEA |
| **Certificate Issuer** | CDA Regional Director | CSEA |
| **Jurisdiction** | Outside BARMM | Within BARMM |
