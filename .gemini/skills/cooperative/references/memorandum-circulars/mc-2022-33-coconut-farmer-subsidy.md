# CDA Memorandum Circular No. 2022-33

## Guidelines on the Release of Audit and Registration Subsidy to Coconut Farmer Cooperatives

**Series of 2022**

**Approved**: November 16, 2022 (BOD Resolution No. 957, S-2022)

**Issued**: December 21, 2022

**Received by ONAR**: January 5, 2023

**Signed by**: Usec. Joseph B. Encabo, Chairman

---

## Overview

This circular provides guidelines for the release of **audit subsidy** and **registration subsidy** to coconut farmer cooperatives under the **Coconut Farmer and Industry Development Plan (CFIDP)**, specifically Chapter 6.2 on Coconut Farmers Organization and Empowerment through Cooperatives. The program is implemented jointly by CDA and the Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA).

---

## Quick Reference

| Subsidy Type | Maximum Amount |
|--------------|----------------|

| **Registration Subsidy** | P20,000.00 |

### Qualified Beneficiaries

| Subsidy Type | Qualifications |

| **Audit Subsidy** | 1. Duly registered cooperative under RA 9520 |

## Section 1. Legal Basis

### a. RA 11524 (Coconut Farmers and Industry Trust Fund Act)

Sec.4(g), RA 11524 mandates the Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA) to prepare the Coconut Farmers and Industry Development Plan (Plan) which shall, among others, take into account the distribution of the annual allocation of the trust fund to organization and empowerment of coconut farmer organization and their cooperatives, to be implemented by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) for farmers cooperatives and LGUs for coconut farmer organizations.

### b. Executive Order No. 172 (June 2, 2022)

Approved the Plan which provides, among others, national programs:
1. Establishment of community-based enterprises
2. Provision of social protection for coconut farmers, farmworkers, and their families
3. Organization and development of coconut farmers
4. Conduct of innovative research on coconut processing, production, and distribution
5. Integrated processing of coconut and downstream products

### c. RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008)

Article 2 declares the policy of the state to foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as practical vehicles for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people power towards the attainment of economic development, equity and social justice.

### d. RA 11364 (CDA Charter of 2019)

Section 2, paragraph 4, **Declaration of Policy** - The government and all its branches, subdivisions, instrumentalities and agencies shall continue to provide technical guidance, financial assistance and other services to enable the cooperatives to develop into viable and responsive economic enterprises towards a strong cooperative movement, free from condition which infringes upon the objective and character of cooperatives.

### e. RA 11364, Section 4 (Powers, Functions and Responsibilities)

The Authority shall have the following powers and functions:

**(b)** Formulate, adopt and implement integrated and comprehensive plans and programs on cooperative development consistent with the national policy on cooperatives and establish an integrated framework on cooperative development for all government agencies;

**(m)** Administer all grants and donations exclusively intended for cooperatives coursed through the Authority for cooperative development, without prejudice to the right of cooperatives to directly receive and administer such grants and donations upon agreement with the grantors and donors thereof;

## Section 2. Qualified Beneficiaries

The beneficiaries of the program shall have the following minimum qualifications:

### For Audit Subsidy:

| # | Qualification |
|---|---------------|
| 1 | Duly registered cooperatives under RA 9520 |
| 2 | Accredited by the PCA |
| 3 | With no unliquidated financial assistance with the CDA |

### For Registration Subsidy:

Registering cooperative with the CDA which should be a:

| # | Type |
|---|------|
| 1 | Coconut farmer organization/association accredited by the PCA which opted to convert into a cooperative |
| 2 | Group of individual coconut farmers who organized themselves into a cooperative |

---

## Section 3. Purpose of the Subsidy

The subsidy shall be utilized by the cooperative for the following purposes:

### 1. Audit Subsidy

Payment of audit fee for the conduct of financial audit by the CDA accredited cooperative external auditor.

### 2. Registration Subsidy

Payment of registration fee and other incidental expenses in the registration of cooperative such as:
- Surety bond
- Notarial fees
- Transportation
- Photocopy
- Printing
- Supplies

---

## Section 4. Application for Subsidy

The following documents shall be submitted in the application for Audit or Registration Subsidy:

| # | Document |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Letter request duly signed by the Chairman and/or Chief Executive Officer/General Manager of the cooperative or by the President/Head of the association or group of individual coconut farmers addressed to the CDA Extension Office thru its Regional Director which has jurisdiction to the cooperative |
| 2 | Resolution of the Board of Directors of the Cooperative or Registering Cooperative approving the request for subsidy to the CDA |

---

## Section 5. Procedures for the Release of Subsidy

The subsidy shall be granted to the cooperative/registering cooperative on a **reimbursement basis**. As such the following shall be the procedures on the release of subsidy:

### Step 1: Verification

CDA Regional Office verifies whether the cooperative or the registering cooperative is eligible as beneficiary of the subsidy.

### Step 2: Notification and Document Submission

If the cooperative/registering cooperative is eligible, the CDA shall inform the cooperative/registering cooperative to submit the following documents for the release of the subsidy:

**For Audit Subsidy:**
| # | Document |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Official receipt issued by the Accredited Cooperative External Auditor |
| 2 | Copy of the Audit Report |

**For Registration Subsidy:**
| # | Document |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Photocopy of Certificate of Registration with the CDA |
| 2 | Official receipts of the registration fee and other incidental expenses in the registration of cooperative |

### Step 3: Processing and Release

Once completed, the CDA Regional Office shall process and release the subsidy to the cooperative.

---

## Section 6. Amount of Subsidy

The following are the **maximum amounts** to be granted to the cooperative subject to submission of documents specified in Section 5(2) of this Guidelines:

| Subsidy Type | Maximum Amount |
|--------------|----------------|
| **Audit Subsidy** | P5,000.00 |
| **Registration Subsidy** | P20,000.00 |

**Important Notes:**
- The amount to be released shall correspond to the official receipts and all other evidence to be submitted by the cooperative
- The amount released shall **not exceed** the amounts specified above

---

| Aspect | Audit Subsidy | Registration Subsidy |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Maximum Amount** | P5,000.00 | P20,000.00 |

## BARMM/CSEA Context

### Applicability to BARMM

This MC is specific to the **CFIDP program** administered by CDA and PCA nationally. For BARMM:

| Aspect | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Jurisdiction** | CDA has jurisdiction over PCA-accredited coconut farmer cooperatives |
| **CSEA Role** | CSEA may develop similar subsidy programs for BARMM coconut farmers |
| **Coordination** | Potential coordination between CSEA, PCA-BARMM, and MAFAR for coconut sector |

### Potential BARMM Application

BARMM has significant coconut farming communities. CSEA could:
1. Develop parallel subsidy guidelines for BARMM coconut farmer cooperatives
2. Coordinate with MAFAR and PCA-BARMM for similar programs
3. Use this MC as reference framework for BARMM-specific coconut cooperative support

### Key Differences for BARMM

| CDA (National) | CSEA (BARMM) |
|----------------|--------------|
| PCA accreditation required | May develop BARMM-specific accreditation |
| CDA Regional Office processes | CSEA processes |
| CFIDP fund source | BARMM agricultural development funds |
