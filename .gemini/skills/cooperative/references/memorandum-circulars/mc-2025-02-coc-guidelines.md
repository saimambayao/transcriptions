# CDA Memorandum Circular 2025-02

**COOPERATIVE DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY**
**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR 2025-02**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: REVISED GUIDELINES ON THE SUBMISSION OF REQUIRED REPORTS AND PRESCRIBING THE REQUIREMENTS FOR THE ISSUANCE OF A CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE (COC) AND THE GROUNDS FOR THE NON-ISSUANCE, SUSPENSION AND REVOCATION OF COC**

Pursuant to the rule-making power of the Authority under RA 11364, otherwise known as the Cooperative Development Authority Charter of 2019, the following Guidelines is hereby issued.

---

## SECTION 1. TITLE

This Guidelines shall be known as the "Revised Guidelines on the Submission of Required Reports and Prescribing the Requirements for the Issuance of a Certificate of Compliance (COC) and the Grounds for the Non-issuance, Suspension and Revocation of COC."

---

## SECTION 2. LEGAL BASES

- Article 53 of RA No. 9520
- Rule 8 of the Revised IRR
- Section 4(g), (h) of RA No. 11364
- DOF-DTI Joint Administrative Order 001-2023, including any amendments thereto

---

## SECTION 3. SCOPE

This Guidelines shall cover all cooperatives registered with the Authority **except CSF Cooperatives**.

---

## SECTION 4. OBJECTIVES

1. To prescribe the requirements for the issuance of a Certificates of Compliance (COC); and
2. To specify the grounds for the non-issuance, suspension or revocation of COC.

---

## SECTION 5. DEFINITION OF TERMS

As used in this Guidelines, the following terms shall be construed to mean:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Authority** | Refers to the Cooperative Development Authority |
| **Calendar Year** | The one (1) year period that begins on January 1 and ends on December 31 |
| **Certificate of Compliance (COC)** | The certificate issued to the cooperative after complying with the rules and regulations being implemented by the Authority |
| **Fiscal Year** | The operating cycle of business operations and activities of a cooperative, covering a twelve (12) month period as provided in the by-laws of the cooperative. The calendar year may be adopted as the fiscal year |
| **Legitimate Purpose** | Any purpose that is not contrary to law, morals, good customs, public order or public policy |
| **Newly Registered Cooperative** | A cooperative with less than one (1) year of operation from the date of its registration as of December 31 of the preceding year |
| **Suspension** | Temporarily invalidating the issued COC of the cooperative |
| **Revocation** | The official cancellation of the issued COC |

---

## SECTION 6. PURPOSES OF A CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE (COC)

A COC shall be issued for any of the following purposes:

1. To affirm the cooperative's compliance with the submission of required reports as prescribed by the Authority;
2. To secure a Certificate of Tax Exemption (CTE) and to avail incentives and privileges set forth under Articles 60 and 61 of RA 9520, other related laws and issuances of government Agencies;
3. To avail of local tax exemption privileges and secure permits and licenses prescribed under the provisions of RA 7160 or Local Government Code of 1991 and subsequent amendments thereto;
4. To comply with the requirements for accreditation as CDA training provider; and
5. Such other legitimate purposes it may serve.

---

## SECTION 7. REQUIREMENTS FOR THE ISSUANCE OF CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 For Primary Cooperatives, Federations, Cooperative Banks, and Insurance Cooperatives

| No. | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | **Cooperative Annual Progress Report (CAPR)** with attachments (see below) |
| 2 | Annual Tax Incentive Report (ATIR) and Annual Benefits Report (ABR) - for cooperatives issued with CTE |
| 3 | Sworn Statement of Cooperative officers affirming adherence to RA 9520 and CDA MC 2024-16 (Annex A) |
| 4 | Certificate of Compliance (COC) fee of **P100.00** |

**CAPR Attachments:**

a. **Financial Statements** audited by a CDA-accredited external auditor:
   1. Statement of Management Responsibility signed by the Chairman and Treasurer
   2. Auditor's Report/Opinion
   3. Statement of Representation
   4. Statement of Financial Condition
   5. Statement of Operation
   6. Statement of Changes in Equity
   7. Statement of Cash Flows
   8. Notes to Financial Statements

b. List of Officers and Mandatory Training Undertaken/Completed

c. Social Audit Report including its program of activities pursuant to its socio-civic goals

d. Performance Audit Report

e. Semi-annual Report on Mediation and Conciliation

---

### 7.2 For Electric Cooperatives

| No. | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | **Cooperative Annual Progress Report (CAPR)** with attachments (same as 7.1) plus: List of Member-Consumer-Owners (MCO) issued with Share Capital Certificate |
| 2 | Annual Tax Incentive Report (ATIR) and Annual Benefits Report (ABR) - for cooperatives issued with CTE only |
| 3 | Sworn Statement of Cooperative officers affirming adherence to RA 9520 and CDA MC 2024-16 (Annex A) |
| 4 | Certificate of Compliance (COC) fee of **P100.00** |

---

### 7.3 For Union of Cooperatives

| No. | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | **Cooperative Annual Progress Report (CAPR)** with attachments (see below) |
| 2 | Annual Tax Incentive Report (ATIR) and Annual Benefits Report (ABR) - for cooperatives issued with CTE |
| 3 | Sworn Statement of Cooperative officers affirming adherence to RA 9520 and CDA MC 2024-16 (Annex A) |
| 4 | Certificate of Compliance (COC) fee of **P100.00** |

**CAPR Attachments for Unions:**

a. **Financial Statements** audited by a CDA-accredited external auditor:
   1. Statement of Management Responsibility signed by the Chairman and Treasurer
   2. Auditor's Report/Opinion
   3. Statement of Representation
   4. Statement of Financial Condition
   5. **Statement of Receipts and Disbursements**
   6. **Statement of Changes in Fund Balance**
   7. Statement of Cash Flows
   8. Notes to Financial Statements

b. List of Officers and Mandatory Trainings Undertaken/Completed

c. Social Audit Report including its program of activities pursuant to its socio-civic goals

d. Performance Audit Report **(Governance and Management Audit Report only)**

e. Semi-annual Report on Mediation and Conciliation

---

### 7.4 For Newly Registered Cooperatives

**Initial COC:** Automatically issued together with the Certificate of Registration, valid until April 30 of the immediately succeeding year.

**For the immediately succeeding year, submit:**

| No. | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | **Cooperative Annual Progress Report (CAPR)** with Financial Status Reports signed by Audit Committee and Chairman: Statement of Financial Condition (Annex B), Statement of Operation (Annex C) |
| 2 | Annual Tax Incentive Report (ATIR) and Annual Benefits Report (ABR) - for cooperatives issued with CTE |
| 3 | Sworn Statement of Cooperative officers affirming adherence to RA 9520 and CDA MC 2024-16 (Annex A) |
| 4 | Certificate of Compliance (COC) fee of **P100.00** |

---

## SECTION 8. SUBMISSION BY COOPERATIVES USING FISCAL YEAR OTHER THAN CALENDAR YEAR

All cooperatives whose fiscal year ends on a date other than December 31 as stated in their By-Laws shall submit the required reports **within 120 calendar days from the end of the Calendar Year**.

For the Audited Financial Statement (AFS) requirement, the cooperative shall submit the **latest AFS as of the reporting year**.

---

## SECTION 9. ONLINE SUBMISSION OF REQUIRED REPORTS

### A. For 2024 Reports

| Cooperative Type | Submission Method |
|------------------|-------------------|
| All types and categories (except primary micro) | Through **Cooperative Assessment Information System (CAIS)** |
| Primary micro cooperatives | Prescribed Google Form in Online Services (Submission of Reports) tab in CDA Website |

Hard copies of Audited Financial Statement shall be submitted to the Authority or may be uploaded through the Google Form.

### B. For CY 2025 Reports Onwards

- All requirements shall be submitted online through the **Authority's new Reporting System**
- Audited Financial Statements (AFS) shall be uploaded by all cooperatives upon submission
- After successful submission, cooperative receives confirmation email
- In exceptional circumstances, coordinate with nearest CDA Extension Offices

---

## SECTION 10. PERIOD FOR SUBMISSION AND PENALTY FOR LATE SUBMISSION

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Submission Deadline** | Within **120 days** after close of calendar year |
| **Penalty for Late Filing** | **Php 100.00 per day of delay** imposed on accountable officers |

---

## SECTION 11. NON-SUBMISSION OF REQUIRED REPORTS

If a cooperative fails to submit the required reports, the Authority shall, within **fifteen (15) days** from the expiration of the prescribed period for submission, send a **Notice of Non-Submission of Required Reports**, stating its non-compliance and the commensurate penalties that will be imposed until such time that the cooperative has complied with the requirements.

---

## SECTION 12. REPORTS WITH DEFICIENCY

- All submitted reports shall be subject to evaluation by the Authority
- If there are findings, a **Notice of Deficiency** shall be sent to the cooperative's official email address
- The cooperative shall comply with the findings within **fifteen (15) calendar days** from receipt
- Non-compliance within said period shall mean non-submission of required reports
- Monetary penalty of **Php 100.00 per day of delay** shall be imposed after the 15-day period

---

## SECTION 13. ISSUANCE OF SCO (SHOW CAUSE ORDER)

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Until June 30 of current year | Cooperatives fail to submit required reports or complete deficient reports |
| Within month of July | SCO shall be issued thru mail, personal service or electronic means |
| Within 15 days from receipt of SCO | Cooperative files answer, submits required reports and pays penalties |
| Within 10 working days | SED/SES causes lifting of SCO and issues corresponding COC |
| If no compliance | CDA commences proceedings for revocation of Authority to Operate per Art 53 of RA 9520 |

---

## SECTION 14. WAIVER OF COMPLIANCE WITH REQUIRED REPORTS

Submission of reports may be waived in case of **total destruction/loss of records and reports due to force majeure or fortuitous events** subject to:

1. **Certification** issued by the LGU, Barangay, or local DRRMC stating occurrence of such event
2. **Letter of application for relief** signed by Chairperson and/or General Manager
3. **Damage Assessment Report**

The concerned Extension Office shall evaluate and endorse to CDA Board of Directors for approval.

---

## SECTION 15. ISSUANCE AND VALIDITY OF COC

### A. For CY 2025 COC

Upon approval and payment, COC shall be released:
1. Personally, through CDA Head Office or Extension Office; or
2. Through registered mail or courier services

### B. For CY 2026 COC Onwards

- Cooperative receives email notification that COC is available for printing
- Cooperative can print the **e-certificate of compliance**

**Validity:** Until **April 30 of the succeeding year** unless earlier suspended or revoked for cause.

---

## SECTION 16. UNSUBMITTED REPORTS FOR PREVIOUS YEAR/S

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Previous reports (except AFS) | No longer required to be submitted |
| Monetary penalties | Payment required for maximum 1-year period |
| COC for those years | Shall no longer be issued |
| Audited Financial Statement | Still required to submit comparative AFS for last **two (2) years** preceding current reporting period |

---

## SECTION 17. GROUNDS FOR NON-ISSUANCE OF COC

The cooperative will receive email notification of non-issuance on any of the following grounds:

| No. | Ground |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Non-submission of required reports |
| 2 | Significant discrepancies between encoded online reports and uploaded PDF copies of AFS |
| 3 | Non-payment of monetary penalties for late or non-submission (except micro cooperatives) |
| 4 | Non-settlement of monetary and non-monetary obligations to the Authority (e.g., unliquidated grants/loans) |
| 5 | Willful failure to comply with mandatory trainings per Rule 7, Section 5 of Revised IRR and CDA MC No. 2015-09 |
| 6 | Two or more sets of BOD claiming to be legitimate officers (subject of verified complaint) |
| 7 | Cooperative has been issued with Show Cause Order (SCO) |
| 8 | Cooperative is undergoing dissolution proceedings |
| 9 | Cooperative has been issued with Cease-and-Desist Order (CDO) |
| 10 | Previous COC suspended or revoked and grounds not yet complied with |
| 11 | Non-compliance with order or directive to comply with examination or investigation findings |
| 12 | Failure to comply with SCA, PFRF, CETF, CDF guidelines and other issuances as result of inspection |
| 13 | Officers found to have violated Articles 45, 48 and 49 of RA 9520 or CDA MC No. 2024-16 (conflict of interest) |

---

## SECTION 18. GROUNDS FOR SUSPENSION OF COC

| No. | Ground |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Two or more sets of BOD claiming legitimacy (subject of verified complaint with Authority and court) |
| 2 | Cooperative issued with Show Cause Order (SCO) |
| 3 | Cooperative issued with Order to Comply based on examination/investigation findings and failed to comply |
| 4 | Cooperative issued with Cease-and-Desist Order (CDO) |
| 5 | Non-compliance with undertaking/arrangement on settlement of obligations |
| 6 | Non-action within prescribed period to address material findings in AFS analysis |
| 7 | Failure to comply with issuances of the Authority |
| 8 | Officers found to have violated Articles 45, 48 and 49 of RA 9520 or CDA MC No. 2024-16 |

---

## SECTION 19. PROCEDURES IN SUSPENSION AND EVENTUAL REVOCATION OF COC

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Administrator (Insurance Coops) or Regional Director issues **Order of Suspension** stating grounds and directives | After due process |
| 2 | Cooperative submits response with actions and proof of compliance | Within **10 calendar days** from receipt |
| 3a | If meritorious: **Order Lifting the Suspension** issued | Upon review |
| 3b | If not satisfactory: **Order of Revocation** issued | Upon review |
| 4 | Copy of order provided to Administrator and RSES Deputy Administrator | If issued at EO level |

---

## SECTION 20. INTERVENTIONS FOR COOPERATIVES WITH SPECIAL CONCERNS

Regional Extension Offices shall provide technical assistance or interventions to cooperatives with special concerns or difficulties:
- ELCAC areas with internet issues
- PWDs
- IPs (Indigenous Peoples)
- Farmers
- Fisherfolks

---

## SECTION 21. AUTOMATIC REVOCATION OF COC

In addition to Section 19, COC is automatically revoked in any of the following instances:

1. Cooperative was issued an **Order of Dissolution**
2. **Revocation or Cancellation of Certificate of Registration**
3. Cooperative was dissolved by **order of a competent court**

---

## SECTION 22. POSTING/PUBLICATION OF REVOKED COCs

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| CDA Website | Public notification posted |
| CDA Social Media | Public notification posted |
| Extension Office | List posted in conspicuous place |
| Other Agencies | Copy furnished to government agencies, financial institutions, and other entities |

---

## SECTION 23. EFFECTS OF NON-ISSUANCE, SUSPENSION OR REVOCATION OF COC

### A. TO THE COOPERATIVE

1. No Certification of whatever nature shall be issued endorsing the cooperative to avail of privileges, benefits or programs from CDA or other agencies
2. Tax exemption privileges and application for renewal of CTE may be adversely affected

### B. TO THE ACCOUNTABLE OFFICERS

The cooperative (through BOD or General Assembly if majority of BOD are offenders) shall initiate sanctions:

| No. | Sanction |
|-----|----------|
| 1 | Barred from continuing as officers of primary cooperatives |
| 2 | Barred from continuing as officers of secondary/tertiary cooperatives |
| 3 | Disqualified to be elected or appointed in concerned cooperative for immediately succeeding **two (2) consecutive years** |
| 4 | Barred from continuing as officers of CDCs, RCOs, SAOs, NAC or other cooperative organizations |
| 5 | Shoulder payment of cooperative's fine of **Php 100.00 per day of delay** (for non-issuance due to non-submission) |
| 6 | Shoulder payment of tax and other penalties imposed by BIR and other agencies |

---

## SECTION 24. INFORMATION DISSEMINATION

All Extension Offices shall cause the widest dissemination of this Guidelines to ensure compliance.

---

## SECTION 25. MONITORING AND REPORTING

- CDA Head Office and Extension Offices shall maintain a database for proper monitoring
- Regular monitoring of strict adherence to these guidelines
- Establish systematic feedback mechanism
- Evaluate, address, and report implementation and policy issues

---
