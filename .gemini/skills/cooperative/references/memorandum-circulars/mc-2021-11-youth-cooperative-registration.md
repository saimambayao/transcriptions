# CDA Memorandum Circular No. 2021-11

## Guidelines Governing the Organization and Registration of Cooperatives to be Established by Youths

**Series of 2021**

**Approved**: July 21, 2021 (BOD Resolution No. 312, s-2021)

**Issued**: September 9, 2021

**Signed by**: USEC. Joseph B. Encabo, Chairman

**Received by National Printing Office**: September 21, 2021

---

## Overview

This circular recognizes cooperatives to be organized by youths and promulgates guidelines for their registration, pursuant to RA 11364 (CDA Charter of 2019) and RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008). It supports youth empowerment and the Youth Entrepreneurship Act (RA 10679).

## Article 1. Title

This Memorandum Circular shall be known as the **"GUIDELINES GOVERNING THE ORGANIZATION AND REGISTRATION OF COOPERATIVES TO BE ESTABLISHED BY YOUTHS."**

---

## Article 2. Legal Bases

### Article 2 of RA No. 9520

> "Art. 2. Declaration of Policy. It is the declared policy of the State to foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as a practical vehicle for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people power towards the attainment of economic development and social justice. xxx"

### Section 4 (c) of RA No. 11364

> "Section 4. Powers, Functions and Responsibilities. – The Authority shall have the following powers and functions:
> (c) Register all cooperatives including amendments to the Articles of Cooperation and Bylaws (ACBL), division, merger, and consolidation;"

---

## Article 3. Purposes

This Memorandum Circular shall have the following purposes:

| No. | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **1** | To recognize cooperatives organized by youths because of the role that cooperatives play in fostering empowerment among the youth through self-organization and development which can help solve the high unemployment rate, lack of modest work, precarious work and other challenges |
| **2** | To provide guidelines for their orderly registration in compliance with the requirements provided under RA No. 9520, its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) and other pertinent laws |
| **3** | To develop and promote youth cooperatives in the Philippines as agents of positive change for peace and sustainable human development process |
| **4** | To support RA No. 10679, or the "Youth Entrepreneurship Act," in promoting the sustainable development of young entrepreneurs and encourage the entrepreneurial spirit among the youth |

---

## Article 4. Coverage

This Guidelines shall govern the organization and registration of **all types of cooperatives** established by youth.

---

## Article 5. Definition of Terms

As used in this Guidelines, the following terms shall mean:

### a.) Area of Operation

Refers to the area where the cooperative operates and where its members come from as provided for in their Articles of Cooperation and By-laws.

> *Reference: Section 2 (3) Revised IRR of RA 9520*

### b.) Associate Member

Refers to a member of a cooperative who opts to continue to be a member upon reaching the age of 36. He/she has no right to vote nor be voted upon but is entitled to limited rights, privileges and membership duration as provided in the By-laws of the cooperative, the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008, and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.

### c.) Authority

Refers to the Cooperative Development Authority.

### d.) Authorized Share Capital

Refers to the capitalization of the cooperative as provided in the Articles of Cooperation and as approved by the Authority.

### e.) Bond of Membership

Refers to the conditions where members group themselves to attain their common goals and objectives which may either be residential, occupational, associational and institutional.

> *Reference: Section 2 (7) Revised IRR of RA 9520*

### f.) Cooperative

Refers to an autonomous and duly registered association of persons, with a common bond of interest, who have voluntarily joined together to achieve their social, economic, and cultural needs and aspirations by making equitable contributions to the capital required, patronizing their products and services and accepting a fair share of the risks and benefits of the undertaking in accordance with universally accepted cooperative principles.

> *Reference: Article 3, RA 9520*

### g.) Economic Survey

Refers to a general statement describing, among others, the structure and purposes of the proposed cooperative.

> *Reference: Article 11, RA 9520*

### h.) Registration

Refers to the operative act of the Authority in granting juridical personality to a proposed cooperative and is evidenced by a Certificate of Registration.

### i.) Regular Member

Refers to one who has complied with all the membership requirements and is entitled to all the rights and privileges of membership.

### j.) Share

Refers to a unit of capital, the par value of which may be fixed at any figure not less than One Hundred Pesos (Php100.00) but not more than One Thousand Pesos (Php 1,000.00) which is divisible by One Peso (Php1.00) and may be divided into common and preferred share capital.

> *Reference: Article IV (f) CDA MC No. 2020-24*

### k.) Share Capital

Refers to the money paid or required to be paid by the members for the conduct of the business operation of the cooperative as provided for in the By-Laws.

### l.) Youth

Refers to those persons whose ages range between **eighteen (18) to thirty-five (35) years old**.

> *Reference: Youth Network Formation, International Cooperative Alliance (ICA), 2003*

### m.) Youth Cooperative

Refers to cooperatives established by youths.

## Article 6. General Requirements

### 6.1 Membership

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Minimum Members** | 15 or more natural persons |
| **Citizenship** | Filipino citizens |
| **Age Range** | 18 to 35 years old |
| **Common Bond** | Required |
| **Residency** | Residing or working in intended area of operations |

**Membership Transition:**
- Upon reaching age **36**, regular member ceases to be a regular member and becomes an **associate member**
- Associate members may form a **Board of Elders** to provide guidance and advice to the youth cooperative

### 6.2 Capitalization

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Authorized Share Capital** | As provided in Articles of Cooperation |
| **Subscribed Capital** | At least 25% of authorized share capital |
| **Paid-up Capital** | At least 25% of subscribed share capital |
| **Minimum Paid-up** | Not less than **P15,000.00** |

---

## Article 7. Submission of Documentary Requirements

Original copies of the following documents shall be submitted to the Authority by those intending to form a youth cooperative:

| No. | Requirement | Copies |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| 7.1 | Economic Survey | 3 copies |
| 7.2 | Articles of Cooperation (signed by all cooperators on each and every page) | 3 copies |
| 7.3 | By-laws (signed by at least a majority of all members on adoption page) | 3 copies |
| 7.4 | Treasurer's Affidavit | 3 copies |
| 7.5 | Surety Bond of Accountable Officers | 1 original + 2 certified photocopies |
| 7.6 | Certificate of Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS) | 1 original + 2 certified photocopies |
| 7.7 | Favorable endorsement from other regulatory agencies | If necessary |

> **Note:** The approved system-generated registration documents shall be printed by the applicant in **8.5 x inch-size white bond paper** and forwarded to the concerned regional office.

## Article 8. Area of Operation

### 8.1 Coverage Options

The area of operation of the cooperative may either be:
- National
- Inter-regional
- Regional
- Provincial
- City/Municipal

### 8.2 Expansion to Nationwide

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Minimum Operation Period** | 2 years |
| **Proof Required** | Increasing net income |

---

## Article 9. Bond of Membership

### 9.1 Categories of Bond of Membership

There are **four (4) categories** of bond of membership:

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Residential** | Members aged 18-35, working and/or actually and physically residing in the same place |
| **Institutional** | Members aged 18-35 consisting of employees, workers and/or officers of a particular institution |
| **Associational** | Members aged 18-35 who come from a registered and/or recognized association, group, club, fraternity, religious group, cultural and other similar aggrupation. Recognized association refers to a legitimate aggrupation of persons which is validated to be publicly known in a particular community and is able to present a certification from an applicable government entity or substantial proof of visible activity in the community |
| **Occupational** | Members aged 18-35 who come from the same or allied profession or occupation |

### 9.2 Single Bond Requirement

The cooperative can adopt **only one** common bond of membership.

---

## Article 10. Name of Cooperative

### 10.1 Required "Youth" Designation

The name of the youth cooperative shall include the word **"youth"** before the type of cooperative.

**Examples:**
- Young at Heart Youth Marketing Cooperative
- Masigasig Youth Consumers Cooperative

### 10.2 Prohibited Names

No cooperative shall be allowed to use a name that is:
- Identical or deceptively or confusingly similar to that of any existing cooperative
- Contrary to public policy, morals or existing laws

### 10.3 Prohibited Words

The following words are prohibited in the name of a proposed primary cooperative unless it is part of the registered name of the association or institution where the members come from:

| Prohibited Terms |
|------------------|
| Development |
| Integrated |
| Incorporated |
| Company |
| Incorporation |
| Corporation |
| Partnership |
| Federation |
| Union |
| Philippines |
| Pilipinas |

### 10.4 Name Format Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Capital Letters** | Not allowed (except for acronyms or names of registered corporations, partnerships, associations, foundations) |
| **Special Characters** | Not allowed |
| **Maximum Characters** | 100 (including acronym, if using one) |

## Article 11. Common and Preferred Shares

### 11.1 Share Capital Composition

The share capital of a cooperative may consist of:
- Common share capital, and/or
- Preferred share capital (if provided under the cooperative's Articles of Cooperation)

If the cooperative decides to have both common and preferred shares, a statement to that effect should appear in the Articles of Cooperation, specifying the amount of shares to be offered as common shares and preferred shares.

> **Recommendation:** Only common shares be offered.

### 11.2 Preferred Share Rights

Should preferred share capital be offered, the rights and privileges of holders of preferred shares shall be provided in the By-laws of the cooperative.

### 11.3 Preferred Share Limit

The preferred share capital shall **not exceed twenty-five percent (25%)** or one-fourth (1/4) of the total authorized share capital of the cooperative.

### 11.4 Paid-up and Subscription Requirements

The paid-up and subscription of the common share capital contribution should strictly adhere to the **twenty-five percent (25%)** requirement based on the authorized share capital.

### 11.5 Maximum Individual Ownership

No member shall own more than **ten percent (10%)** of the subscribed share capital of the cooperative.

---

## Article 12. Bonding Requirements of Accountable Officers/Employees

In order to meet any contingency that may arise in the operation of a cooperative, accountable officers/employees shall submit a surety bond for the faithful performance of their functions in accordance with Articles 14(5) and (56) of RA 9520.

### Officers Required to be Bonded

| No. | Position |
|-----|----------|
| a. | Chairperson |
| b. | Treasurer and cashier |
| c. | Manager |
| d. | Warehouseman |
| e. | Loan/deposit collectors |
| f. | Signatories of checks and other financial instruments |
| g. | Such other persons as may be authorized by the cooperative to act as a custodian of funds, merchandise, inventories, securities and other assets of the cooperative |

### Bond Adequacy Determination

The Board of Directors shall determine the adequacy of such bonds. For this purpose, the Board of Directors shall be guided based on the **initial net worth** of the cooperative which shall include:
- Paid-up capital
- Membership fees
- Other assets of the cooperative at the time of registration

---

## Article 13. Jurisdiction

All applications for registration of youth cooperatives shall be filed with the **CDA regional office (RO)** having jurisdiction over the principal office of the proposed cooperative.

---

## Article 14. Period to Approve an Application for Registration and Period to Appeal

### 14.1 Processing Timeline

An application for registration must be disposed of by the Authority within the period stated in the Citizen's Charter from the time of filing of complete documentary requirements. Otherwise, the application is **deemed approved**, unless the cause of the delay is attributable to the applicant.

### 14.2 Appeal to CDA Head Office

In case of a denial by the regional office, an appeal may be filed by the applicant with the CDA Head Office within **ten (10) working days** from receipt of the Notice of Denial.

### 14.3 Appeal to Office of the President

In case of a denial by the Authority, an appeal may be filed with the Office of the President within **ninety (90) days** from receipt of the Notice of Denial.

### 14.4 Deemed Approval

The failure of the Office of the President to act on the appeal within **ninety (90) days** from the filing thereof shall mean the approval of said application.

### Appeal Timeline Summary

| Stage | Timeline | Filed With |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Regional Office Denial** | 10 working days | CDA Head Office |
| **CDA Denial** | 90 days | Office of the President |
| **OP Inaction** | 90 days → Deemed Approved | - |

---

## Article 15. Validation Requirement

### 15.1 Pre-Registration Validation

Prior to registering the cooperative, the concerned regional office shall conduct a validation to ensure that the requirements for application for registration of youth cooperatives are complied with and in order.

### 15.2 Governing Guidelines

The conduct of validation is governed by the provisions of **MC No. 2020-11, Series of 2020** (Guidelines on the Conduct of Validation) and any other succeeding issuances of the Authority modifying or repealing the said MC.
