**Section I.     Title**

This Memorandum Circular shall be known as the **Required and Recommended Trainings for Members, Officers, and Staff of Cooperatives**.

**Section II.    Legal Bases.**

1. Section 4 (o), RA 11364. *Develop and conduct management and training programs that will provide members of cooperatives with the entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, and technical skills required for efficient operation of their cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of cooperativism and provide, when necessary technical and professional assistance to ensure the viability and growth of cooperatives with special concern for agrarian reform, agriculture, fishery and the economically depressed sectors;*

2. Section 4 (ii), RA 11364. *Formulate, adopt and implement, in a manner consistent with Section 17 of this Act, a standard training for cooperative officers or members which shall focus on basic cooperative principles and values, good governance, business and entrepreneurial management, human resource management, risk management, conflict management, and technical skills required for the efficient and effective operations of the cooperatives;* and

3. Section 17, RA 11364. *The Authority, in partnership with learning and training institutions with the cooperative sector, shall formulate standards of training requirements for cooperative officers and members to ensure compliance thereof.*

4. Article 4 (5) RA 9520, The Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008. *Education, Training and Information- cooperatives shall provide education and training for their members, elected and appointed representatives, managers, and employees, so that they can contribute effectively and efficiently to the development of their cooperatives.*

5. Article 44 RA 9520 *Functions, Responsibilities and Training Requirements of Directors, Officers and Committee Members. The functions and responsibilities of directors, officers and committee members, as well as their training requirements, shall be in accordance with the rules and regulations issued by the Authority.*

6. Section 3 (D) RA 10744, *Build-up the capability of cooperatives and NGOs in the areas of credit evaluation, loan and risk management, and good governance principles;*

7. Section 5 Rule 7 of the Revised IRR of RA 9520. *Training Requirements for the Officers of the Cooperative. The following officers of the cooperative regardless of type and size, such as: Board of Directors, Secretary, Treasurer, Election Committee, Audit Committee, Ethics Committee, Mediation and Conciliation Committee, other committees created by the General/Representative Assembly and General Manager or Chief Executive Officer, shall be required to undergo a minimum of sixteen (16) hours of training on the fundamentals of cooperatives and another sixteen (16) hours on governance and management of cooperatives within the first half of their term. Provided, That, in case of micro-cooperatives, they shall only undergo sixteen (16) hours of training on the fundamentals, governance and management of cooperatives. The content of the training program shall be prescribed by the Authority in consultation with the accredited training providers and the cooperative sector.*

   *In addition to the above-mentioned trainings, the officers of cooperatives engaged in savings and credit operations shall be required to undergo an additional sixteen (16) hours of specialized training on financial management, risk management and credit management, provided that these cooperatives have at least Five Million Pesos (Php5,000,000.00) worth of deposit liabilities based on the latest Audited Financial Statement of the cooperative.*

   *Cooperative officers shall likewise endeavor to undergo additional appropriate trainings.*

**Section III.   Scope.**

This Memorandum Circular covers the required and recommended trainings for all members, officers and staff of cooperatives registered with the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA).

**Section IV.    Purposes.**

This Memorandum Circular is being issued for the following purposes:

1. Prescribe the required and recommended trainings for members, elected and appointed officers, managers, and employees of cooperatives per category including a standard course outline for the required trainings;

2. Identify the authorized providers of the required trainings prescribed in this Circular.

3. Prescribe the sanctions for non-compliance to this Circular.

**Section V.     Definition of Terms**

**Required Trainings.** Refer to courses covering the fundamentals, governance and management of cooperatives and specialized training on financial management, risk management and credit management, as stipulated in Section 5 of the Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of Republic Act 9520 and any additional training required by the CDA.

**Recommended Trainings.** These pertain to trainings and courses that are recommended for members, officers, and staff of cooperatives. These trainings are voluntary in nature and are highly recommended for those seeking to enhance their expertise, insights, and proficiencies in specific areas relevant to cooperative management, governance, or operational aspects.

**Accredited Cooperative Training Providers (CTPros).** This refers to institutions or organizations that have been accredited by the Cooperative Development Authority to conduct required and recommended trainings for cooperatives in accordance with the provisions of RA 9520 and its IRR, RA 11364 and its IRR, Memorandum Circular 2023-11, and their subsequent amendments.

**Cooperative Development Officers (CDOs).** This refers to the officer in the Municipal, City, and Provincial levels who takes charge of the office for the development of cooperatives registered with the Cooperative Development Authority, in accordance with RA No. 11535¹,

**Section VI.    Required Trainings**

**VI.1   Members.** All cooperatives are required to ensure that all their members undertake the Fundamentals of Cooperatives Course, Financial Literacy² and Leadership and Values Formation within one (1) year from the date of acceptance of their membership. For existing members, the cooperative shall prepare an action plan to comply with this requirement within one (1) year from the effectivity of this Circular.

> **VI.1.1 Continuing Education for Members.** All cooperatives shall adopt an annual continuing education program for members to be reflected in their annual development plan.

**Section VII.   Recommended Trainings**

Officers, members, and staff of cooperatives, in order to enhance their skills, knowledge, and capabilities are encouraged to undertake various training programs, including but not limited to the following:

1. Organizational Development and Management
2. Project Development and Management
3. Basic Computer Literacy
4. Digitalization
5. Succession Planning
6. Franchising
7. Cooperative Business Continuity and Resiliency
8. Conflict Management
9. Strategic Planning
10. Audit Management
11. Records Management
12. Entrepreneurial and Business Management
13. Cooperative Performance Standards
14. Risk Management and Business Continuity Management
15. Other trainings (e.g IP Rights)

**Section VIII.  Sanctions for Non-Compliance with the Required Trainings**

The following will be imposed in accordance with due process:

a. Willful failure of the cooperative to comply with the training requirements for its officers despite due notice from the Authority shall be a ground for the non-issuance of Certificate of Compliance for the succeeding year.

b. Failure of any officer/s of a cooperative to undergo the prescribed trainings shall disqualify said officer/s to hold any elective or appointive position.

**Section IX.    Report on Trainings Undertaken by Officers, Members, and Staff of Cooperatives**

All cooperatives shall submit a list of trainings undertaken by officers to the Regional Office which has jurisdiction over them in accordance with the form prescribed by the Supervision and Examination Division (SED). Insurance and CSF Cooperatives shall submit their list to the Head Office.

Records of trainings attended by members and staff shall be kept by the cooperatives and made available during the inspection.

**Section X.     Miscellaneous Provisions**

Regional Extension Offices shall prepare their respective quarterly training calendars for micro and small cooperatives to be posted on their websites and social media platforms

Trainings may be conducted through the traditional method, hybrid, and virtual or online means.

CDA personnel are not allowed to act as resource persons in trainings conducted by Accredited Cooperative Training Providers.

Certified Cooperative Development Officers are still required to undergo trainings on Financial Management, Risk Management and Credit Management in accordance with guidelines to be established by the Cooperative Development Authority as a prerequisite for conducting such trainings under VI.2.1 hereof.

All training providers shall adopt the Standard Training Curricula for the Required Trainings herein provided as Annex A.

**Section XI.    Transitory Provisions.**

Required trainings undertaken prior to the effectivity of this Circular shall be considered as compliance to the same.

**Section XII.   Repealing Clause**

All prior issuances inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed.

**Section XIII.  Separability Clause**

If for any reason, any provision of this Guidelines is declared null and void or unconstitutional, the other provisions hereof which are not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect. The Doctrine of Operative Fact shall also be applicable to provisions that were later declared to be unconstitutional.

**Section XIV.   Effectivity.**

This Circular shall be effective fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or any newspaper of general circulation and registration with the Office of the National Administrative Register (ONAR).
