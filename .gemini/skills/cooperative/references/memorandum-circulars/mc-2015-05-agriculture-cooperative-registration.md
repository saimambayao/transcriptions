# CDA Memorandum Circular No. 2015-05

## Guidelines for the Registration of Agriculture Cooperative

**Series of 2015**

**Approved**: August 18, 2015 (BOA Resolution No. 215, S-2015)

**Date**: September 16, 2015

**Signed by**: Orlando R. Ravanera, Chairman

---

## Overview

This circular provides specific guidelines for the registration of primary agriculture cooperatives, supplementing MC 2015-01 with type-specific requirements for cooperatives engaged in raising/culture of plants, animals, and related agricultural activities.

---

## Quick Reference

### Key Requirements

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Minimum Members** | 15 Filipino citizens |
| **Minimum Paid-up Capital** | ₱15,000.00 |
| **Subscribed Capital** | Min. 25% of Authorized |
| **Paid-up Capital** | Min. 25% of Subscribed |
| **Share Par Value** | ₱100.00 to ₱1,000.00 |
| **Preferred Shares Max** | 25% of Authorized Capital |
| **Individual Ownership Max** | 10% of Subscribed Capital |

### Validation Timeline

| Activity | Timeline |
|----------|----------|
| Validation conduct | Within 3 days from appointment |
| Report submission | Within 5 days from completed validation |

### Common Bond Options

| Type | Members |
|------|---------|
| **Residential** | Farmers residing in area of operation |
| **Occupational** | Those engaged in raising/culture of plants, animals |
| **Associational** | From registered farmer association, group, club |

---

## Section 1. Title

These Guidelines shall be known as the **"Guidelines For The Registration of Agriculture Cooperative"**.

---

## Section 2. Legal Bases

### RA 9520, Article 2 - Declaration of Policy

> *"It is the declared policy of the State to foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as a practical vehicle for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people power towards the attainment of economic development and social justice."*

### RA 6939, Section 3(a)

> *"Formulate, adopt and implement integrated and comprehensive plans and programs on cooperative development consistent with the national policy on cooperatives and the overall socio-economic development plans of the Government."*

### RA 9520, Article 23(1)(u)

> *"Other types of cooperative as may be determined by the Authority."*

---

## Section 3. Purpose

These Guidelines shall provide for the orderly registration of proposed primary agriculture cooperative in compliance with the requirements as provided in RA 9520, its Implementing Rules and Regulations and other pertinent laws.

---

## Section 4. Scope

These Guidelines shall cover proposed **primary agriculture cooperatives** that will engage in:
- Raising, culture of plants
- Raising, culture of animals
- For gainful objectives

---

## Section 5. Definition of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Area of Operation** | The area where the cooperative operates and where its members come from as provided in their Articles of Cooperation and By-laws |
| **Associate Member** | A member with no right to vote nor be voted upon, entitled only to limited rights, privileges and membership duration as provided in the By-Laws |
| **Authority** | Cooperative Development Authority |
| **Authorized Share Capital** | Capitalization of the cooperative as provided in the Articles of Cooperation and approved by the Authority |
| **Bond of Membership** | The condition where members group themselves to attain common goals and objectives (residential, occupational, associational, or institutional) |
| **Cooperative** | An autonomous and duly registered association of persons, with a common bond of interest, who have voluntarily joined together to achieve their social, economic, and cultural needs and aspirations |
| **Agriculture Cooperative** | A primary cooperative which or whose members are involved/engaged in raising/culture of plants, animals, fungi, and other living organisms for productive and economic purpose and in related activities that lead to reduction of cost and/or value addition of outputs |
| **Agriculture** | The art and science of raising/culture of plants, animals, fungi and other living organisms for economic purposes |
| **Registration** | The operative act of the Authority granting juridical personality to a proposed cooperative, evidenced by the Certificate of Registration |
| **Regular Member** | One who has complied with all membership requirements and is entitled to all rights and privileges of membership |
| **Share** | A unit of capital in primary cooperative with par value fixed at minimum ₱100.00 to maximum ₱1,000.00, may be divided into common and preferred share capital |
| **Share Capital** | Money paid or required to be paid by members for the conduct of cooperative operations |

---

## Section 6. Jurisdiction

Agriculture Cooperatives shall file their application for registration with the **CDA Extension Office**, which shall have jurisdiction over the said cooperatives.

---

## Section 7. Documentary Requirements

The following documents shall be submitted to the Authority in **four (4) copies** except for item (1):

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | **Cooperative Name Reservation Notice (CNRN)** | Original copy only |
| 2 | **Economic Survey** | 4 copies |
| 3 | **Articles of Cooperation and By-Laws** | 4 copies, all original |
| | | AOC signed by all cooperators on each and every page |
| | | By-Laws signed by all members on adoption page |
| 4 | **Treasurer's Affidavit** | 4 copies |
| 5 | **Surety Bonds of Accountable Officers** | 4 copies |
| 6 | **Certificate of Pre-Membership Seminar (PMES)** | Signed by Interim Chairman, validated by Authority |
| 7 | **Undertaking to change name** | 4 copies |
| 8 | **Undertaking to comply with auditing and accounting standards** | 4 copies |
| 9 | **Undertaking to comply with other regulatory requirements** | When applicable |
| 10 | **Tax Identification Number (TIN) of all cooperators** | 4 copies |
| 11 | **Registration Fee** | Per CDA schedule |

---

## Section 8. Cooperative Business Activities

### Primary Activities

The cooperative may engage in all or any of the following:

| # | Activity |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Raising, culture of plants, animals |
| 2 | Facilitating procurement of farm inputs/implements for members |
| 3 | Processing and marketing of members' products/produce |
| 4 | Any other activities that lead to reduction of cost and/or value addition of outputs |

### Allied Services

In addition to primary activities, the cooperative may also engage in:
- Storage of members' products/produce
- Transport of members' products/produce
- Other allied services

---

## Section 10. Procedures for Registration

### 10.1. Technical Assistance

The proposed agriculture cooperative may inquire from the Extension Office the list of requirements, and any CDS may assist in explaining the procedures and compliance thereto.

### 10.2. Submission

Submit required documents in accordance with **Memorandum Circular 2015-01** on Revised Guidelines Governing the Registration of Cooperatives.

### 10.3. Validation and Verification

Applications for registration shall be subject to verification and validation prior to registration.

| Step | Activity | Timeline |
|------|----------|----------|
| 10.3.1 | CDA Extension Office appoints technical personnel as validator upon receipt of documents | Upon receipt |
| 10.3.2 | Validator conducts validation | Within **3 days** from appointment |
| 10.3.3 | Validator submits report to Registration Unit | Within **5 days** from completed validation |

---

## Section 11. Number of Members Required for Registration

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Minimum Number** | 15 or more natural persons |
| **Citizenship** | Filipino citizens |
| **Common Bond** | Having a common bond of interest |
| **Residence/Work** | Residing or working in the intended area of operations |

---

## Section 12. Capital Requirements

### Basic Structure

| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| **Subscribed Capital** | At least 25% of Authorized Share Capital |
| **Paid-up Capital** | At least 25% of Subscribed Share Capital |
| **Minimum Paid-up** | ₱15,000.00 |

### Example Computation

For an Agriculture Cooperative with ₱100,000 Authorized Capital:

| Item | Computation | Amount |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Authorized Capital | Set by organizers | ₱100,000.00 |
| Minimum Subscribed | 25% × ₱100,000 | ₱25,000.00 |
| Minimum Paid-up | 25% × ₱25,000 | ₱6,250.00 |
| **Actual Minimum** | MC 2015-05 floor | **₱15,000.00** |

---

## Section 13. Cooperative Name and Prohibition

### Required Elements

The following must be included in the cooperative name:
- "Agriculture"
- "Cooperative", "Kooperatiba", or "Cooperativa"
- Specification of cooperative type

### Prohibited Names

| Prohibition | Reason |
|-------------|--------|
| Identical to existing cooperative | Confusion prevention |
| Deceptively or confusingly similar | Confusion prevention |
| Contrary to public policy, moral, and existing laws | Legal compliance |

### Discouraged Terms

| Term | Status |
|------|--------|
| "Development" | Discouraged |
| "Integrated" | Discouraged |

### Prohibited Terms

| Term | Reason |
|------|--------|
| "Incorporated" | Corporation-related |
| "Corporation" | Corporation-related |
| "Company" | Corporation-related |
| "Incorporation" | Corporation-related |
| "Partnership" | Partnership-related |
| "Federation" | Reserved for secondary cooperatives |
| "Union" | Reserved for secondary cooperatives |

> **Exception**: "Federation" or "Union" may be used if part of the registered name of an association or institution where the cooperative is organized.

---

## Section 14. Common and Preferred Share

### Share Types

| Type | Holders | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Common Shares** | Regular members | Entitled to all rights |
| **Preferred Shares** | Associate members, Regular members | Limited rights per By-Laws |

### Preferred Share Requirements

| Requirement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Maximum percentage | 25% (1/4) of Authorized Share Capital |
| When required | If cooperative accepts associate members |
| Rights and privileges | Must be provided in By-Laws |

### Ownership Limitations

| Limitation | Maximum |
|------------|---------|
| Individual ownership | 10% of subscribed share capital |
| Subscription requirement | 25% based on authorized capital |

---

## Section 15. Bond of Membership

Agriculture cooperatives may have the following common bonds:

| Bond Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Residential** | Members are farmers who are actually and physically residing within the area of operation |
| **Occupational** | Members are those engaged in raising, culture of plants, animals |
| **Associational** | Members are from a registered association, group, club, cultural and other similar aggrupation of farmers |

---

## Section 16. Dual Membership

A prospective agriculture cooperative **may include** in its by-laws a provision not allowing members of existing agriculture cooperative within the same area of operation to be a member of the proposed cooperative **unless there is consent from the latter**.

---

## Section 17. Bonding Requirements of Accountable Officers/Employees

Per Article 14(5) and Article 56 of RA 9520:

### 17.1. Persons Required to be Bonded

| Position | Required |
|----------|----------|
| Chairperson | Yes |
| Treasurer and Cashier | Yes |
| Manager | Yes |
| Warehouseman | Yes |
| Loan/Deposit Collectors | Yes |
| Signatories of checks and financial instruments | Yes |
| Other custodians of funds, merchandise, inventories, securities, and other assets | Yes |

### 17.2. Bond Adequacy Determination

The **Board of Directors** shall determine the adequacy of such bonds based on:
- Initial net worth of the cooperative
- Paid-up capital
- Membership fees
- Other assets at the time of registration

---

## Section 18. Monitoring and Inspection

The Extension Office (EO) concerned shall:
- Monitor the Agriculture Cooperative
- Conduct inspection

**Purpose**: To ensure compliance with:
- These Guidelines
- Laws, rules and regulations
- Issuances of the Authority
- Other laws/issuances of appropriate government agencies

---

## Section 19. Separability Clause

If any provision of these Guidelines is declared null and void or unconstitutional, the other provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in force and effect.

---

## Section 20. Repeal

All previous circulars and/or guidelines issued by the Authority which are inconsistent with these guidelines are repealed or modified accordingly.

---

## Section 21. Effectivity

These guidelines shall take effect:

2. Fifteen (15) days after submission with the Office of National Administrative Registry (ONAR)
