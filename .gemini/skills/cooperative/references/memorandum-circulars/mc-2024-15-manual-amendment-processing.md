# CDA Memorandum Circular No. 2024-15

## Guidelines on the Manual Processing of Applications for the Registration of Amendments to Articles of Cooperation and By-Laws of Cooperatives Under Specific Circumstances

**Series of 2024**

**Approved**: June 14, 2024 (BOD Resolution No. 452, s-2024)

**Issued**: June 27, 2024, Quezon City

**Signed by**: USEC. Joseph B. Encabo, Chairman

**Received by U.P. Law Center / National Administrative Register**: July 02, 2024

**CSAT Registration No.**: 24-0699

## Overview

This circular establishes guidelines for the **manual processing** of amendments to Articles of Cooperation and By-laws (ACBL) when proposed amendments cannot be processed through E-CoopRIS due to system-related issues. This provides an alternative pathway for cooperatives facing technical limitations with the online registration system.

## Section 1. Title

This circular shall be known as the **"GUIDELINES ON THE MANUAL PROCESSING OF APPLICATIONS FOR THE REGISTRATION OF AMENDMENTS TO ARTICLES OF COOPERATION AND BY-LAWS OF COOPERATIVES UNDER SPECIFIC CIRCUMSTANCES."**

---

## Section 2. Purpose

These guidelines are issued to establish a common understanding of the basis, coverage, requirements, and procedure for manual processing of applications for amendments to the Articles of Cooperation and By-laws (ACBL) of CoopRIS and E-CoopRIS-registered cooperatives in the event that proposed amendments cannot be processed through E-CoopRIS due to reasons or problems that are purely system-related.

---

## Section 3. Coverage

These guidelines shall cover the manual processing of applications for amendments to Articles of Cooperation and By-laws of all types and categories of cooperatives, **excluding Credit Surety Fund Cooperatives (CSFC)**.

### Specific Circumstances Covered

| No. | Circumstance | Description |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| **1** | Single-purpose to Multipurpose | Cooperatives intending to add business activity and amend ACBL from single-purpose to multipurpose |
| **2** | Adding Business Activities | Multipurpose Cooperatives adding other services, transport cooperatives, agriculture cooperatives, agrarian reform cooperatives, fishermen cooperatives |
| **3** | Large Membership Capitalization | Cooperatives with **150 or more members** intending to amend for an increase in capitalization |
| **4** | Address Changes | Cooperatives amending registered address due to changes in political subdivision of area of operation |
| **5** | System Limitations | Such conditions/circumstances which may prevent processing/evaluation through E-CoopRIS |

---

## Section 4. Definition of Terms

### 4.1 Certificate of Registration of Amendment

An official document issued by the Authority under its official seal shall serve as conclusive evidence of such registered amendment unless it is proved that the registration has been canceled or further amended.

### 4.2 CoopRIS

A web-based information system that provides an online platform for:
- Cooperative name reservation
- Evaluation and approval of applications for new registration
- Amendments
- Branch/satellite/laboratory recognition

### 4.3 E-CoopRIS

An **enhanced** web-based information system that provides an online platform for:
- Cooperative name reservation
- Evaluation and approval of applications for new registration
- Amendments
- Branch/satellite/laboratory recognition

### 4.4 Referendum

Refers to a mechanism of securing the approval of the general membership of an **Electric Cooperative** on issues affecting its registration, operation, and management.

### 4.5 Registration of Amendment

Shall mean the operative act of granting validity and effectivity to the modification of Articles of Cooperation and By-laws as evidenced by the Certificate of Registration of Amendment issued by the Authority.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Effectivity Date** | Date of issuance of the Certificate of Registration of Amendment |

### 4.6 Validation

An activity or series of activities to ensure that the requirements for registration of applications to amendments of the Articles of Cooperation and By-laws are complete, correct, and per applicable laws. This thorough validation process is designed to instill confidence and ensure the integrity of the amendments.

---

## Section 5. Documentary Requirements

The following shall be submitted in **three (3) original copies**:

### Standard Requirements

| Item | Requirement | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **a.** | General Assembly (GA) Resolution | Stating that the proposed amendment was approved by at least two-thirds (2/3) of all members with voting rights |
| **b.** | Board of Directors and Secretary's Certificate | Duly notarized, stating that the required vote approved the resolution of the members with voting rights |
| **c.** | Amended Articles of Cooperation and By-laws | Complete amended ACBL document |
| **d.** | Treasurer's Affidavit | Duly notarized; required if there is an increase/decrease in capitalization |
| **e.** | Endorsement Letter/Certificate of Authority | From another regulatory agency, *if applicable* |

### Referendum Requirements (Electric Cooperatives)

In case the amendment is made through **referendum**, the following documents shall be submitted **in lieu of (a) and (b)**:

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **1** | Approval by the concerned CDA Office of the letter request by the Chairperson, Chief Executive, or General Manager of the cooperative to hold a referendum |
| **2** | Notarized Board of Directors and Secretary's Certificate stating that: |
| | - Proper information dissemination on the proposed amendments was conducted |
| | - Cooperative members were duly informed |
| | - Any clarificatory questions were adequately addressed in lieu of deliberations in a meeting |
| | - The proposed amendment to the ACBL has been approved by not less than two-thirds (2/3) of the members with voting rights in the referendum conducted |

---

## Section 6. Process of Amendment

The following shall be the procedure for submission and processing under these guidelines:

### 6.1 Submit Amendment Documents and Encoded Data/Information in Excel Format

| Submission Type | Requirement |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Hard Copy Documents** | Submit to CDA Head Office or Regional Extension Office having jurisdiction, either personally or through courier |
| **Electronic Data** | Encoded data/information in Excel format must be submitted via email in editable soft copy to CDA Head Office or Regional Extension Office |

### 6.2 Initial Evaluation of Submitted Applications

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              INITIAL EVALUATION PROCESS                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│   Documents Received by Registration Section                │
│                        ↓                                    │
│   Check for Completeness                                    │
│         ├─── Complete ──→ Forward to Senior CDS             │
│         │                      ↓                            │
│         │                 Assign Validator                  │
│         │                      ↓                            │
│         │                 Conduct Validation                │
│         │                                                   │
│         └─── Incomplete ──→ Issue Deficiency Checklist      │
│                                  ↓                          │
│                            Notify Cooperative               │
│                            (by email if courier)            │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 6.3 Conduct of Validation

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Method** | On-site validation using validation tool |
| **Alternative Method** | Off-site validation (when on-site is not possible due to force majeure or circumstances beyond control) |
| **Report Submission** | **2 working days** from receipt of complete application documents |

### 6.4 Processing and Issuance

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Documents Complete & In Order** | Certificate of Registration of Amendment (CRA) will be issued |
| **Documents Incomplete/Not in Order** | Application returned to cooperative for compliance |

---

## Section 7. Jurisdiction

All applications for amendments shall be filed with the Authority's **Head Office** or concerned **Extension Office**, which has jurisdiction over the registration and supervision of the said cooperative.

| Cooperative Type | Filing Location |
|------------------|-----------------|
| **Regular Cooperatives** | Extension Office with jurisdiction |
| **Cooperative Banks** | CDA Head Office |
| **Insurance Cooperatives** | CDA Head Office |

---

## Section 8. Validity of Amendments

The registered amendments shall be **valid and binding** on the cooperative and its members upon the Authority's issuance of the appropriate Certificate of Registration of Amendment.

---

## Section 9. Effectivity

These guidelines shall take effect **immediately** following the completion of its publication in the Official Gazette or newspaper of general circulation.

## Comparison: E-CoopRIS vs Manual Processing

| Aspect | E-CoopRIS (MC 2024-05) | Manual (MC 2024-15) |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Application** | Online submission | Physical/courier submission |
| **Data Format** | System-generated | Excel format via email |
| **Hard Copy Deadline** | 30 working days from email | Submitted with application |
| **When to Use** | Normal circumstances | System-related issues preventing E-CoopRIS processing |
| **Exclusions** | None specified | Credit Surety Fund Cooperatives (CSFC) |
