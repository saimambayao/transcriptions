This circular provided the original guidelines for conducting Pre-Registration Seminars (PRS) exclusively by the CDA for prospective members of would-be primary cooperatives.

## Section 1. Legal Bases

### RA 9520, Article 10 - Organizing a Primary Cooperative

> *"Fifteen (15) or more natural persons who are Filipino citizens, of legal age, having a common bond of interest and are actually residing or working in their intended area of operation, may organize a primary cooperative under this Code: Provided, That a prospective member of a primary cooperative must have completed a Pre-Membership Education Seminar (PMES)."*

### RA 6939, Section 3, Paragraph (b) - Powers, Functions and Responsibilities

> *"Develop and conduct management and training programs upon request of cooperatives that will provide members of cooperatives with the entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, and technical skills required for the efficient operation of their cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of cooperativism and provide, when necessary, technical and professional assistance to ensure the viability and growth of cooperatives with special concern for agrarian reform, fishery and economically depressed sectors."*

---

## Section 2. Purpose

This Guidelines is issued to:
1. Provide and prescribe the conduct of Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS) to be given to prospective members of would-be primary cooperatives
2. Direct the creation of a Pre-Registration Seminar Team (PRS TEAM)

---

## Section 3. Conduct of PRS

The PRS shall be conducted by the following:

| Conductor | Supervision |
|-----------|-------------|
| CDA Extension Offices | CRITS supervises and monitors |
| CDA Field Office/CDS | CRITS supervises and monitors |

The Extension Office through its **CRITS** shall supervise and monitor the conduct of the PRS.

---

## Section 4. Composition of PRS Team

A PRS TEAM must be created in every region to conduct PRS.

### 4.1. CDA Extension Office Team

| Position | Role |
|----------|------|
| Senior CDS for CRITS | Team Leader |
| Technical personnel from Registration Section | Member |
| CDS II assigned in the Regional Office | Member |

### 4.2. CDA Field Office Team

| Position | Role |
|----------|------|
| Cooperative Development Specialists assigned in Province/City | Conductor |

---

## Section 5. Implementing Mechanism

### 5.1. Frequency of Conduct

| Office Level | Minimum Frequency |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **CDA Extension Office** | At least once a month |
| **CDA Field Office** | At least once per quarter |

**Location Requirements**:
- PRS shall be held regularly in all CDA Extension Offices
- Conducted at the regional level and field offices
- Location should consider the center and most accessible place for prospective cooperatives

**Coordination Requirements**:
- Extension Office Director shall ensure proper scheduling, coordination, and dissemination
- Notice/advisory shall be posted in:
  - Regional Offices
  - Provincial Offices
  - CDA Regional/Central Office websites

**Field Office Requests**:
- For requests received by CDA field offices, the CDS shall inform and seek approval of the Extension Office Director before conducting PRS

### 5.2. Resource Persons

| PRS Type | Resource Persons |
|----------|------------------|
| **Extension Office PRS** | Senior CDS for CRITS; Technical staff from Registration Section; CDS assigned in the region |
| **Field Office PRS** | CDS assigned in the province OR CDA Extension Office authorized representative |

**Special Topics**:
CDA Officials from the level of Directors to Administrators may act as resource persons in topics requiring expertise such as:
- Cooperative Banks
- Electric Cooperatives
- Labor Service Cooperatives
- Insurance Cooperatives
- Other special types of cooperatives

### 5.3. Resource Materials

The curriculum to be adopted in the conduct of PRS:
- RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008)
- RA 6939 (CDA Charter)
- Implementing Rules and Regulations
- Prescribed PRS course outline (attached)

### 5.4. Certificate

| Certificate Type | Issuer |
|------------------|--------|
| **Certificate of Completion** (all participants) | Senior CDS for CRITS (Extension) / CDA Representative (Field) |
| **Certification of Attendance** (for registration purposes) | Senior CDS for CRITS, noted by Extension Office Director |

### 5.5. Availing of PRS

| Aspect | Provision |
|--------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Free of charge |
| **Fees** | No fees shall be accepted from participants |
| **Meals** | Participants may bring their own food |

## Historical Context

### Why MC 2017-02 Was Important

1. **First Formal PRS Guidelines** - Established the framework for pre-registration education
2. **Exclusive CDA Conduct** - Centralized PRS delivery through CDA
3. **PRS Team Structure** - Created regional PRS teams
4. **Free Service** - Established PRS as free public service

### Why It Was Replaced

1. **COVID-19 Response** - Need for online delivery options
2. **E-CoopRIS Integration** - Registration process digitization
3. **Updated Legal Framework** - RA 11364 (CDA Charter of 2019)
4. **Standardization** - Need for consistent duration and curriculum
5. **Data Privacy** - Compliance with RA 10173
6. **LGU Collaboration** - Formalize local government partnership
