## Overview

**Republic Act No. 9520**, also known as the **Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008**, was signed into law on **February 17, 2009** by President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo. It amended and strengthened Republic Act No. 6938 (Cooperative Code of 1990).

### Key Statistics

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Chapters** | 19 |
| **Articles** | 146 |
| **Effectivity** | 15 days after publication |
| **Regulatory Body** | Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) |

### Declaration of Policy (Article 2)

The State shall:
- Foster creation and growth of cooperatives as practical vehicles for self-reliance
- Harness people power towards economic development and social justice
- Recognize cooperatives as instruments of equity, social justice, and economic development
- Ensure that cooperatives remain true to their goals and principles

## Chapter I: General Concepts and Principles

**Articles 1-5** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-i---general-concepts-and-principles)

### Article 1: Title

This Act shall be known as the **"Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008"**.

### Article 2: Declaration of Policy

The State recognizes cooperatives as instruments of:
- Equity
- Social justice
- Economic development

### Article 3: General Concepts

> **Definition**: A cooperative is an autonomous and duly registered association of persons, with a common bond of interest, who have voluntarily joined together to achieve their social, economic, and cultural needs and aspirations by making equitable contributions to the capital required, patronizing their products and services and accepting a fair share of the risks and benefits of the undertaking in accordance with universally accepted cooperative principles.

**Key Elements**:
1. Autonomous and duly registered
2. Common bond of interest
3. Voluntary membership
4. Equitable capital contributions
5. Patronage of products/services
6. Fair share of risks and benefits
7. Adherence to cooperative principles

### Article 4: Cooperative Principles

| # | Principle | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Voluntary and Open Membership** | Open to all without gender, social, racial, political, or religious discrimination |
| 2 | **Democratic Member Control** | One member, one vote; members actively participate in policies |
| 3 | **Member Economic Participation** | Equitable contribution to capital; limited interest on capital |
| 4 | **Autonomy and Independence** | Autonomous, self-help organizations controlled by members |
| 5 | **Education, Training, Information** | Provide education to members, officers, employees, and public |
| 6 | **Cooperation Among Cooperatives** | Serve members effectively through local, national, regional, international structures |
| 7 | **Concern for Community** | Work for sustainable development of communities |

### Article 5: Definition of Terms

**Key Definitions**:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Authority** | Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) |
| **Bond of Membership** | Common interest binding members together |
| **Cooperative Bank** | Organized primarily to provide financial and credit services to cooperatives |
| **Federation** | Union of cooperatives in same or related line of business |
| **Financial Service Cooperative** | Accepts deposits, makes loans, provides credit services (non-bank) |
| **Net Surplus** | Total income less expenses for fiscal year |
| **Patronage Refund** | Surplus distributed based on member transactions |
| **Primary Cooperative** | Members are natural persons |
| **Secondary Cooperative** | Members are primary cooperatives |
| **Tertiary Cooperative** | Members are secondary cooperatives |

## Chapter II: Organization and Registration

**Articles 6-25** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-ii---organization-and-registration)

### Article 6: Purposes of Cooperatives

Cooperatives may be organized for purposes that:
- Encourage thrift and savings
- Create funds for loans at reasonable interest
- Provide goods and services
- Engage in any business activity for mutual benefit

### Article 7: Objectives and Goals

Every cooperative shall:
- Provide goods/services to members
- Encourage savings among members
- Teach efficient operation methods
- Propagate cooperative principles

### Article 8: Not in Restraint of Trade

Cooperative activities are **not** considered in restraint of trade or illegal combinations.

### Article 9: Cooperative Powers and Capacities

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Sue and be sued | Full juridical personality |
| Own property | Acquire real and personal property |
| Enter contracts | Full contractual capacity |
| Borrow money | Issue bonds, promissory notes |
| Invest funds | Per board authorization |
| Merge/consolidate | With other cooperatives |

### Article 10: Organizing a Primary Cooperative

**Minimum Requirements**:

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Organizers | At least 15 natural persons |
| Citizenship | Filipino citizens |
| Age | Legal age (18 years) |
| Common Bond | Residing or working in intended area |
| PRS | All must complete Pre-Registration Seminar |

### Article 11: Economic Survey

Required contents:
- Area of operation
- Number of prospective members
- Economic feasibility study
- Business plan and projections

### Article 12: Liability

Members' liability is **limited** to share capital subscribed.

### Article 13: Term of Existence

- Perpetual unless otherwise stated in Articles of Cooperation
- May be limited by members' choice
- Subject to dissolution provisions

### Article 14: Articles of Cooperation

**Required Contents**:

| Section | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Name | Cooperative name (must include "Cooperative") |
| Purpose | Primary and secondary purposes |
| Principal Office | Complete address |
| Term | Duration of existence |
| Common Bond | Basis of membership bond |
| Area of Operation | Geographic scope |
| Capital Structure | Authorized, subscribed, paid-up |
| Contributions | Non-refundable contributions |
| Division/Merger | Provisions for division or merger |
| Amendments | Procedure for amending AOC |
| Officers | Names and addresses of incorporators/trustees |

### Article 15: By-Laws

**Required Contents**:

| Section | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Rights/Duties | Member rights and obligations |
| Termination | Conditions for membership termination |
| Share Capital | Transfer, forfeiture, redemption |
| Meetings | GA and board meeting procedures |
| Officers | Qualifications, powers, duties |
| Vacancies | Filling of officer vacancies |
| Order of Business | Meeting procedures |
| Fiscal Year | Accounting period |
| Amendments | Procedure for amending bylaws |
| Distribution | Net surplus distribution |
| Disputes | Settlement procedures |

### Article 16: Registration

**Documentary Requirements**:

1. Cooperative Name Reservation Slip
2. Articles of Cooperation (4 copies, notarized)
3. By-Laws (4 copies, signed by all)
4. Economic Survey (4 copies)
5. Treasurer's Affidavit (paid-up capital certification)
6. Surety Bond of Accountable Officers
7. PRS Certificates
8. Registration Fee

**Processing Timeline**:
- CDA must act within **60 days**
- Failure to act = deemed approved (if not applicant's fault)

### Article 17: Certificate of Registration

Effects of registration:
- Acquires juridical personality
- Can sue and be sued
- Can own property
- Perpetual succession

### Article 18: Amendment of AOC and By-Laws

**Amendment Process**:

| Step | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Proposal by BOD or petition by 20% of members |
| 2 | Notice to all members (30 days prior) |
| 3 | GA approval by 2/3 majority |
| 4 | Filing with CDA within 30 days |
| 5 | CDA approval and registration |

### Article 19: Pre-Registration Contracts

Contracts executed before registration:
- Valid and binding on cooperative upon registration
- Liability of organizers ceases upon registration
- Cooperative may ratify or reject

### Article 20: Division of Cooperatives

Requirements:
- Resolution by 2/3 of GA
- Approved plan of division
- Division of assets, liabilities, membership

### Article 21: Merger and Consolidation

**Merger**: One cooperative absorbs another
**Consolidation**: Two or more form new cooperative

Requirements:
- Approved by 2/3 of each GA
- Plan of merger/consolidation
- Articles of merger/consolidation filed with CDA

### Article 22: Effects of Merger/Consolidation

- Surviving/new cooperative acquires all assets
- Assumes all liabilities
- Pending cases continue against surviving entity
- Members become members of surviving/new cooperative

### Article 23: Types and Categories

**By Type (Business Activity)**:

| Category | Types |
|----------|-------|
| **Financial** | Credit, Cooperative Bank, Financial Service, Insurance |
| **Production** | Producer, Workers, Agrarian Reform, Dairy, Fishermen |
| **Consumer** | Consumer, Housing, Transport, Water Service, Electric |
| **Services** | Marketing, Service, Health Services, Education |
| **Multi-Purpose** | Combines 2+ types above |
| **Advocacy** | Promotes cooperative principles |

**By Category (Membership Level)**:

| Category | Members | Minimum |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Primary** | Natural persons | 15 |
| **Secondary (Federation)** | Primary cooperatives | 2 |
| **Tertiary (Union)** | Secondary cooperatives | 2 |

### Article 24: Functions of Federation

- Provide technical services to members
- Coordinate member activities
- Represent members in policy advocacy
- Provide training and education

### Article 25: Cooperative Unions

Functions:
- Non-business activities
- Advocacy and representation
- Education and information
- Technical assistance

## Chapter III: Membership

**Articles 26-31** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-iii---membership)

### Article 26: Kinds of Membership

| Type | Characteristics | Rights |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| **Regular Member** | Met all requirements, entitled to all rights | Full voting, all privileges |
| **Associate Member** | Has not yet qualified for regular status | Limited per bylaws, no voting |

### Article 27: Government Officers and Employees

- May organize and join cooperatives
- Cannot interfere with cooperative management
- Must follow cooperative bylaws

### Article 28: Application

- Written application required
- BOD approves/disapproves
- Must comply with bylaws

### Article 29: Liability of Members

- Limited to unpaid share capital subscription
- No personal liability beyond subscription

### Article 30: Termination of Membership

**Grounds for Termination**:

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Voluntary Withdrawal | Written notice to BOD |
| Death | Transfer to heirs or refund |
| Expulsion | For cause, with due process |
| Failure to Patronize | Per bylaws |
| Non-Payment | Of share capital or obligations |

**Due Process for Expulsion**:
1. Written notice of charges
2. Opportunity to be heard
3. BOD recommendation
4. GA decision (appealable within 30 days)

### Article 31: Refund of Interests

Upon termination:
- Entitled to refund of share capital
- Less any obligations owed
- Per schedule in bylaws (max 2 years)

## Chapter IV: Administration

**Articles 32-50** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-iv---administration)

### Article 32: General Assembly Composition

- Composed of all members entitled to vote
- Highest policy-making body
- May delegate to representative assembly

### Article 33: Powers of General Assembly

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Approve/Amend AOC | Constitutional changes |
| Approve/Amend By-Laws | Regulatory changes |
| Elect/Remove Directors | Board composition |
| Elect Audit Committee | Independent oversight |
| Elect Election Committee | Electoral oversight |
| Approve Plans | Development programs and budgets |
| Decide Major Issues | Merger, consolidation, dissolution |
| Delegate Powers | By 3/4 vote |

### Article 34: Meetings

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| **Annual GA** | Within 90 days after fiscal year | Regular business, elections |
| **Special GA** | As needed | Specific urgent matters |
| **Board Meeting** | Monthly (minimum) | Operational decisions |

**Notice Requirements**:
- Annual GA: 15 days written notice
- Special GA: 7 days written notice
- Must state purpose, time, place

### Article 35: Quorum

| Body | Quorum | Majority Required |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| General Assembly | 25% + 1 of members | Simple majority of quorum |
| Board of Directors | Majority of board | Simple majority of quorum |
| Committees | Per bylaws | Per bylaws |

### Article 36: Voting

- **One member, one vote** (regardless of capital)
- Proxy voting allowed per bylaws
- Secret ballot for elections
- Show of hands for other matters (unless contested)

### Article 37: Board of Directors

**Composition and Term**:

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Size | 5-15 members |
| Election | By General Assembly |
| Term | Maximum 2 years |
| Re-election | Per bylaws |

### Article 38: Powers of the Board

| Category | Powers |
|----------|--------|
| **Policy** | Formulate policies, strategic plans |
| **Management** | Hire/fire General Manager, set compensation |
| **Financial** | Authorize investments, approve loans |
| **Operational** | Create committees, delegate duties |
| **Reporting** | Submit reports to GA |

### Article 39: Directors' Qualifications

**Qualifications**:
- Voting member of cooperative
- Possesses qualifications in bylaws
- Not disqualified by law

**Disqualifications**:
- Engaged in business with conflict of interest
- Holds operational position in cooperative
- Removed for cause from any cooperative
- Delinquent in obligations to cooperative

### Article 40: Compensation

- Directors serve without compensation
- Per diems for meetings allowed
- Reimbursement of actual expenses

### Article 41: Officers

**Required Officers**:

| Officer | Selection | Key Duties |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Chairman** | Elected by BOD from among directors | Preside at meetings |
| **Vice Chairman** | Elected by BOD | Assume chairman duties when absent |
| **Secretary** | Elected by BOD | Records, correspondence, custody |
| **Treasurer** | Elected by BOD | Financial custody, disbursements |
| **General Manager** | Hired by BOD | Day-to-day operations |

### Article 42: Duties of Officers

**Chairman**:
- Preside at GA and BOD meetings
- Execute authorized documents
- Represent cooperative in ceremonies

**Treasurer**:
- Custody of all funds
- Deposit funds in authorized depository
- Sign checks with co-signatory
- Maintain financial records

**Secretary**:
- Record minutes of meetings
- Maintain membership registry
- Custody of corporate seal
- Give notices of meetings

### Article 43: Mandatory Committees

| Committee | Selection | Term | Key Functions |
|-----------|-----------|------|---------------|
| **Audit Committee** | Elected by GA | 2 years | Internal audit, monitor controls, review financials |
| **Election Committee** | Elected by GA | 2 years | Conduct elections, validate candidates, certify results |
| **Mediation/Conciliation** | Appointed by BOD | Per bylaws | Hear complaints, mediate disputes |
| **Ethics Committee** | Appointed by BOD | Per bylaws | Enforce code of ethics, recommend sanctions |
| **Education & Training** | Appointed by BOD | Per bylaws | Plan training programs, develop curriculum |

### Article 44: Audit Committee Functions

- Continuous internal audit
- Monitor management controls
- Review internal audit reports
- Report findings to GA
- Recommend external auditors

### Article 45: Election Committee Functions

- Prescribe election rules
- Screen candidates
- Conduct elections
- Canvass and certify results
- Resolve election protests

### Article 46: Vacancy in Board

**Filling Vacancies**:
- BOD may fill temporary vacancies
- Special GA election if majority vacant
- Appointee serves until next regular election

### Article 47: Removal of Directors/Officers

**Grounds**:
- Breach of fiduciary duty
- Conflict of interest
- Gross negligence
- Fraud or dishonesty
- Conviction of crime

**Process**:
- Written charges filed
- Opportunity to be heard
- 3/4 vote of GA for removal

### Article 48-50: Additional Provisions

- Term limits per bylaws
- Holdover until successors elected
- Transition procedures

## Chapter V: Responsibilities, Rights and Privileges

**Articles 51-62** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-v---responsibilities-rights-and-privileges-of-cooperatives)

### Article 51: Registered Address

- Must maintain registered address
- Notify CDA of any change
- Keep records at registered address

### Article 52: Books and Records

**Required Records**:

| Record | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Membership Registry | Names, addresses, capital contributions |
| Minutes Book | GA and BOD meeting minutes |
| Stock and Transfer Book | Share capital transactions |
| Financial Records | Complete accounting records |
| Annual Reports | CAPR, financial statements |

### Article 53: Reports to CDA

**Annual Reporting Requirements**:

| Report | Deadline | Contents |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **CAPR** | 120 days after fiscal year | Progress, activities, statistics |
| **Audited Financial Statements** | 120 days after fiscal year | Balance sheet, income statement |
| **List of Officers** | Within 30 days of election | Names, positions, terms |

### Article 54: Tax Treatment (Tax Exemptions)

**Fully Exempt Cooperatives** (Transactions with members only):
- All national internal revenue taxes
- All local taxes

**Conditionally Exempt** (Member + Non-member transactions):

| Condition | Tax Status |
|-----------|------------|
| Accumulated Reserves ≤ PHP 10M | Full exemption |
| Accumulated Reserves > PHP 10M | Exempt on member transactions only; taxable on non-member portion |

**Perpetual Exemptions**:
- Donations to cooperatives (donor's deduction)
- Transactions among cooperatives
- Government procurement preferential treatment

### Article 55: Non-Tax Privileges

| Privilege | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| No court fees | Exempt from court, sheriff fees |
| No appeal bond | For appeals to CDA |
| Securities exempt | If not for speculation |
| Preferential procurement | In government purchases |
| Priority allocation | Market stalls, public markets |
| Credit access | Preferred status with GFIs |

### Article 56: Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Accurate records | Maintain proper books and records |
| Report filing | Submit reports on time |
| Compliance | Follow laws, rules, regulations |
| Good governance | Apply cooperative principles |
| Member welfare | Prioritize member interests |

### Article 57-62: Additional Rights and Privileges

- Access to government programs
- Technical assistance from CDA
- Training and education support
- Export incentives (for qualified coops)
- Priority in livelihood programs
- Tax incentives for qualified activities

When cooperative cannot meet obligations:
- May apply for remedies under Insolvency Law (Act No. 1956)

**Indicators of Insolvency**:

- Ceased operations

## Chapter VII: Dissolution

**Articles 64-70** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-vii---dissolution-of-cooperatives)

### Article 64: Voluntary Dissolution (No Creditors Affected)

**Requirements**:
- Majority vote of BOD
- 3/4 vote of GA members present
- Notice published 3 consecutive weeks
- 30-day written notice to members

### Article 65: Voluntary Dissolution (Creditors Affected)

**Requirements**:
- Petition to CDA
- Notice published 30 consecutive days
- Written notice to known creditors
- CDA approval required

### Article 66: Involuntary Dissolution

**Grounds**:

| Ground | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Fraud | Fraudulent registration or operations |
| Illegality | Engaging in illegal activities |
| Willful Violation | Repeated code/bylaw violations |
| Failure to Operate | Ceased operations for 2+ years |
| Insufficient Membership | Below minimum for 1+ year |

### Article 67: Winding Up

- Cooperative continues for 3 years for settlement
- Elected liquidators wind up affairs
- Pay debts in order of preference

### Article 68: Distribution of Assets

**Order of Distribution**:
1. Costs of liquidation
2. Secured creditors
3. Employee wages (priority)
4. Government claims
5. Unsecured creditors
6. Member claims
7. Statutory fund disposition

### Article 69: Reserve Fund Upon Dissolution

- Cannot be distributed to members
- Transferred to federation/union
- If no federation, to CDA for cooperative development

### Article 70: Implementing Guidelines

CDA issues rules and regulations for liquidation.

## Chapter VIII: Capital, Property and Funds

**Articles 71-79** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-viii---capital-property-and-funds)

### Article 71: Share Capital

**Capital Structure**:

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Authorized Capital | Maximum shares issuable |
| Subscribed Capital | At least 25% of authorized |
| Paid-Up Capital | At least 25% of subscribed |
| Minimum Paid-Up | Per cooperative type |

**Minimum Paid-Up Capital by Type**:

| Cooperative Type | Minimum Capital |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Primary (General) | PHP 15,000 |
| Multi-Purpose | PHP 100,000 |
| Credit | PHP 100,000 |
| Financial Service | PHP 10,000,000 |
| Cooperative Bank | Per BSP regulations |
| Electric | PHP 5,000,000 |
| Water Service | PHP 1,000,000 |

### Article 72: Par Value

- Shares have par value stated in AOC
- All shares have equal rights
- No fractional shares

### Article 73: Transfer of Shares

**Restrictions**:
- May only transfer to qualified persons
- BOD approval required
- Subject to lien for obligations

### Article 74: Lien on Shares

Cooperative has first lien on member's shares for:
- Unpaid subscriptions
- Outstanding loans
- Other obligations

### Article 75: Decrease of Share Capital

**Requirements**:
- 2/3 vote of GA
- CDA approval
- No prejudice to creditors

### Article 76: Increase of Share Capital

**Requirements**:
- 2/3 vote of GA
- Amendment of AOC
- CDA registration

### Article 77: Deposits

- Members may make deposits with cooperative
- Interest rates per bylaws
- Subject to BSP regulations (for banks)

### Article 78: Investment of Funds

Funds may be invested in:
- Government securities
- Cooperative banks
- Other cooperatives
- Real estate for cooperative use
- Other investments per bylaws

### Article 79: Revolving Capital

- GA may authorize revolving capital
- Deferred patronage refunds
- Certificates issued with serial number
- Retirement schedule specified

## Chapter X: Allocation and Distribution of Net Surplus

**Articles 85-86** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-x---allocation-and-distribution-of-net-surplus)

### Article 85: Net Surplus

**Definition**: Total income minus total expenses for fiscal year.

**Components**:
- Operating income
- Non-operating income
- Less: Operating expenses
- Less: Non-operating expenses

### Article 86: Allocation of Net Surplus

**Mandatory Allocations**:

| Fund | Rate | Purpose | Notes |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| **Reserve Fund** | 10% minimum | Cover losses, stability | 50% for first 5 years; not distributable |
| **Education & Training Fund (ETF)** | Up to 10% | Training, coop development | May transfer to federation |
| **Community Development Fund (CDF)** | 3% minimum | Community projects | Non-refundable |
| **Optional Fund** | Up to 7% | Land, building, other | Per bylaws |

**After Statutory Allocations**:

| Distribution | Rate | Basis |
|--------------|------|-------|
| **Interest on Share Capital** | Limited rate (per bylaws) | Based on capital contribution |
| **Patronage Refund** | At least 30% of remaining | Based on member transactions |

**Distribution Mechanics**:

```
Net Surplus
├── Reserve Fund (10-50%)
├── ETF (up to 10%)
├── CDF (3% minimum)
├── Optional Fund (up to 7%)
└── Remaining
    ├── Interest on Capital (limited)
    └── Patronage Refund (at least 30%)
```

**Patronage Refund Distribution**:
- Regular members: Full entitlement
- Associate members: Per bylaws
- Non-member patrons: Set aside in general fund

Special provisions apply to cooperatives organized by agrarian reform beneficiaries.

### Article 88: Definition and Purpose

- Organized by marginal farmers (majority ARBs)
- Purpose: Land tenure, development, consolidation
- Support CARP implementation

### Article 89: Functions

- Provide production inputs
- Market agricultural products
- Provide credit and savings
- Engage in land development

### Article 90: Area of Operation

- Within agrarian reform communities
- May expand per CDA/DAR approval

### Article 91: Membership Requirements

- Agrarian reform beneficiaries
- Farmers within community
- Completes orientation seminar

### Article 92: Assistance from Government

- Technical assistance from DAR
- Credit access priority
- Training and capability building

### Article 93: Land Use

- May develop awarded lands collectively
- May engage in agribusiness ventures
- Subject to CARP restrictions

### Article 94: Implementing Rules

DAR and CDA jointly issue implementing rules.

## Chapter XII: Cooperative Banks

**Articles 95-104** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-xii---cooperative-banks)

### Article 95: Governing Law

Subject to:
- This Code
- General Banking Law (RA 8791)
- BSP regulations

### Article 96: Definition

Financial institution:
- Owned by cooperatives
- Provides banking services primarily to cooperatives
- Subject to BSP supervision

### Article 97: Organization

**Requirements**:
- At least 3 registered cooperatives as members
- BSP authority to operate
- CDA registration

### Article 98: Minimum Capital

As determined by BSP based on:
- Area of operation
- Services offered
- Risk profile

### Article 99: Powers

- Accept deposits
- Grant loans
- Issue letters of credit
- Other banking services per BSP

### Article 100: Deposits

- Insured with PDIC
- Subject to reserve requirements
- Interest rates per BSP

### Article 101: Loans

- Priority to member cooperatives
- Collateral requirements per BSP
- Interest rates within legal limits

### Article 102: Investments

Subject to BSP regulations on:
- Allowed investments
- Concentration limits
- Liquidity requirements

### Article 103: Reserves

Maintain reserves as required by:
- BSP regulations
- This Code
- Cooperative bylaws

### Article 104: Applicability of Banking Laws

General Banking Law provisions prevail for bank operations.

## Chapter XV: Credit Cooperatives

**Articles 114-118** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-xv---credit-cooperatives)

### Article 114: Coverage

Cooperatives primarily engaged in:
- Promoting thrift
- Granting loans to members

### Article 115: Membership

- Common bond requirement strict
- Residential, occupational, or associational

### Article 116: Services

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Savings | Accept member deposits |
| Loans | Grant loans for productive/provident purposes |
| Insurance | Provide loan protection |
| Education | Financial literacy programs |

### Article 117: Loan Policies

- Loans to members only
- Productive and provident purposes
- Interest within legal limits
- Collateral per bylaws

### Article 118: Conversion to FSC

Existing credit cooperatives may convert to Financial Service Cooperatives by:
- Meeting FSC requirements
- Notifying CDA
- Complying with enhanced regulations

## Chapter XVI: Financial Service Cooperatives

**Articles 119-125** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-xvi---financial-service-cooperatives)

### Article 119: Definition

Non-bank financial institution that:
- Accepts deposits
- Makes loans
- Provides credit services
- Offers enhanced financial products

### Article 120: Organization

**Requirements**:

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Minimum Capital | PHP 10,000,000 |
| Risk-Based Adequacy | Per CDA regulations |
| Governance | Enhanced requirements |
| Reporting | Quarterly reports to CDA |

### Article 121: Powers

| Power | Scope |
|-------|-------|
| Accept deposits | From members and qualified depositors |
| Grant loans | To members, subject to limits |
| Issue credit cards | Per CDA/BSP regulations |
| Foreign exchange | Limited dealer authority |
| Electronic banking | Subject to regulations |

### Article 122: Supervision

- Primary supervision by CDA
- BSP coordination for systemic issues
- Subject to examination and audit

### Article 123: Reserves and Liquidity

Maintain:
- Statutory reserves per this Code
- Liquidity reserves per CDA
- Capital adequacy per risk-based formula

### Article 124: Regulatory Framework

CDA issues regulations on:
- Capital adequacy
- Asset quality
- Management standards
- Earnings benchmarks
- Liquidity requirements

### Article 125: Exclusive Terminology

Terms "credit cooperative," "financial service cooperative" are exclusive to registered entities.

## Chapter XIX: Final Provisions

**Articles 139-146** | [Full Text](../assets/ra-9520-philippine-cooperative-code-2008.md#chapter-xix---final-provisions)

### Article 139: Implementing Rules

CDA issues IRR within 120 days of effectivity.

### Article 140: Penalties

**Violations and Penalties**:

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Minor violations | Warning, fine up to PHP 10,000 |
| Serious violations | Fine PHP 10,000 - PHP 100,000 |
| Fraud/misrepresentation | Fine + imprisonment |
| Repeat offenses | Dissolution proceedings |

### Article 141: Use of Cooperative Name

- Only registered cooperatives may use "Cooperative"
- Violations subject to penalties

### Article 142: Transitory Provisions

Existing cooperatives must:
- Comply with new requirements within 2 years
- Submit updated AOC/Bylaws
- Meet capitalization requirements

### Article 143: Separability Clause

Invalid provisions do not affect other valid provisions.

### Article 144: Repealing Clause

Repeals RA 6938 and inconsistent laws.

### Article 145: Applicability

Applies to all cooperatives registered with CDA.

### Article 146: Effectivity

Takes effect 15 days after complete publication.
