## General Assembly

### Definition

The General Assembly is the highest policy-making body of the cooperative, composed of all members entitled to vote (or their duly-elected delegates).

### Types of Meetings

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Annual General Assembly (AGA)** | Once a year, within 90 days after fiscal year end | Regular business, elections, reports |
| **Special General Assembly** | As needed | Urgent matters, special resolutions |

### Powers and Functions (Article 26)

1. **Constitutional**
   - Approve and amend Articles of Cooperation
   - Approve and amend Bylaws

2. **Electoral**
   - Elect members of Board of Directors
   - Elect Audit Committee members
   - Elect Election Committee members
   - Remove directors/officers for cause

3. **Financial**
   - Approve annual budget
   - Approve distribution of net surplus
   - Approve developmental plans

4. **Corporate**
   - Approve mergers and consolidation
   - Approve voluntary dissolution
   - Approve investment decisions

5. **Oversight**
   - Receive and approve annual reports
   - Receive audit committee report
   - Hold officers accountable

### Quorum and Voting

| Requirement | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| **Quorum** | Majority of members (unless bylaws provide otherwise) |
| **Regular votes** | Majority of members present |
| **Amendments to AC/Bylaws** | 2/3 of members present |
| **Dissolution** | 3/4 of all members |
| **Delegation of powers** | 3/4 of all members |

### Voting Rights

- **One member, one vote** (primary cooperatives)
- Proxy voting as allowed by bylaws
- No cumulative voting

## Board of Directors

### Composition

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Size** | 5-15 members |
| **Term** | Maximum 2 years (as per bylaws) |
| **Election** | By General Assembly |
| **Meetings** | At least monthly |

### Qualifications

1. Voting member of the cooperative
2. Of legal age
3. Filipino citizen
4. Possesses qualifications in bylaws
5. Completed required training

### Disqualifications

1. Engaged in business similar to cooperative (conflict of interest)
2. Holds position in daily operations/management
3. Previously removed as officer for cause
4. Convicted of offense involving moral turpitude
5. Does not meet training requirements

### Powers and Responsibilities

**Strategic Role**:
- Strategic planning and direction-setting
- Policy formulation
- Major decision-making

**Oversight Role**:
- Monitor management performance
- Ensure compliance with laws and bylaws
- Review financial reports

**Fiduciary Role**:
- Act in best interest of cooperative and members
- Exercise duty of care and loyalty
- Avoid conflicts of interest

**Administrative Role**:
- Hire and supervise General Manager
- Determine officer compensation
- Approve major contracts

### Board Meetings

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Frequency** | At least once a month |
| **Quorum** | Majority of directors |
| **Decisions** | Majority of quorum |
| **Minutes** | Must be recorded and kept |

## Mandatory Committees

### Audit Committee

**Selection**: Elected by General Assembly

**Composition**: 3 members (minimum), not from BOD

**Accountability**: Directly to General Assembly

**Functions**:
1. Continuously monitor management control systems
2. Audit cooperative performance
3. Review financial statements
4. Investigate irregularities
5. Report findings to General Assembly
6. Recommend corrective actions

**Independence**: Cannot be removed except by General Assembly

### Election Committee

**Selection**: Elected by General Assembly

**Composition**: 3 members (minimum), not from BOD

**Functions**:
1. Promulgate election rules and regulations
2. Validate candidate qualifications
3. Supervise conduct of elections
4. Canvass votes and certify results
5. Proclaim winning candidates
6. Resolve election protests

**Tenure**: Until next election is completed

### Mediation and Conciliation Committee

**Selection**: Appointed by Board of Directors

**Composition**: 3 members (minimum)

**Functions**:
1. Hear complaints and grievances against members, officers, or staff
2. Mediate disputes between parties
3. Recommend resolutions
4. Submit findings to Board
5. Maintain confidentiality

**Jurisdiction**: Internal disputes only (not external legal matters)

### Ethics Committee

**Selection**: Appointed by Board of Directors

**Composition**: 3 members (minimum)

**Functions**:
1. Develop Code of Ethics and Conduct
2. Investigate ethical violations
3. Conduct hearings on violations
4. Recommend sanctions to Board
5. Promote ethical behavior

**Authority**: Recommendatory only; Board decides on sanctions

### Education and Training Committee

**Selection**: Appointed by Board of Directors

**Composition**: 3 members (minimum)

**Functions**:
1. Plan education and training programs
2. Oversee PRS for prospective members
3. Ensure officer training compliance
4. Manage Education and Training Fund
5. Coordinate with CDA-accredited trainers

**Budget**: Utilizes Cooperative Education and Training Fund (CETF)

## Officers

### Elected Officers

| Officer | Selection | Key Responsibilities |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Chairperson** | BOD elects among themselves | Preside meetings, represent cooperative |
| **Vice-Chairperson** | BOD elects among themselves | Assist Chair, act in absence |

### Appointed Officers

| Officer | Appointed By | Key Responsibilities |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Secretary** | Board of Directors | Minutes, records, correspondence |
| **Treasurer** | Board of Directors | Funds custody, financial records |
| **General Manager** | Board of Directors | Day-to-day operations |

### Surety Bond Requirement

All officers handling funds, securities, or property must be covered by surety bond:
- Issued by registered insurance/bonding company
- Amount determined by Board of Directors
- For faithful performance of duties

## Management

### General Manager Role

**Appointment**: Hired by Board of Directors

**Term**: As determined by Board or contract

**Functions**:
1. Manage day-to-day operations
2. Implement Board policies and decisions
3. Supervise staff
4. Prepare reports for Board
5. Execute contracts (as authorized)

### Separation of Powers

| Body | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **General Assembly** | Policy and constitutional matters |
| **Board of Directors** | Strategy and oversight |
| **Management** | Operations and implementation |

**Key Principle**: Directors cannot hold positions in daily operations and management (Article 38).

## Governance Best Practices

### Transparency

- Regular member communication
- Open access to records (within limits)
- Published financial statements
- Clear reporting of fund usage

### Accountability

- Regular audits (internal and external)
- Officer performance evaluation
- Clear sanctions for violations
- Member feedback mechanisms

### Member Participation

- Encourage meeting attendance
- Facilitate member engagement
- Support member education
- Respond to member concerns

### Conflict of Interest Management

- Disclosure requirements
- Recusal from conflicted decisions
- Register of interests
- Clear policies

### Succession Planning

- Develop future leaders
- Training programs for potential officers
- Knowledge transfer systems
- Staggered terms where appropriate
