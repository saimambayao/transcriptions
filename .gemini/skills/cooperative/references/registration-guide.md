## Pre-Formation Requirements

### Minimum Requirements to Form a Cooperative

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Number of Organizers** | At least 15 natural persons |
| **Citizenship** | All Filipino citizens |
| **Age** | All of legal age (18+) |
| **Common Bond** | Residing or working in intended area of operation |
| **PRS** | All must complete Pre-Registration Seminar |

### Common Bond of Interest

The common bond may be based on:
- Geographic proximity (barangay, municipality, city)
- Occupation or profession
- Employment in same company/institution
- Membership in same organization
- Engagement in same industry/sector

### Steps to Organize

```
1. Identify potential organizers (15+)
           ↓
2. Conduct orientation/planning meetings
           ↓
3. Complete Economic Survey
           ↓
4. Attend Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS)
           ↓
5. Draft Articles of Cooperation
           ↓
6. Draft Bylaws
           ↓
7. Secure capital commitments
           ↓
8. Prepare documentary requirements
           ↓
9. Submit to CDA for registration
```

## Economic Survey

### Purpose

The economic survey establishes the viability and need for the cooperative. It must demonstrate:
- Demand for cooperative services
- Potential member base
- Available resources
- Economic feasibility

### Required Contents

1. **General Information**
   - Proposed cooperative name
   - Type of cooperative
   - Principal address
   - Area of operation

2. **Membership Profile**
   - Number of prospective members
   - Common bond of interest
   - Economic profile of members
   - Potential patronage

3. **Market Analysis**
   - Products/services to be offered
   - Target market
   - Competition analysis
   - Pricing strategy

4. **Financial Projections**
   - Initial capital requirements
   - Sources of funds
   - Projected income and expenses
   - Break-even analysis

5. **Organizational Plan**
   - Management structure
   - Staffing requirements
   - Facilities needed
   - Equipment requirements

## Pre-Registration Seminar

### What is PRS?

The **Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS)** is a mandatory training for all prospective cooperative members and organizers.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Conductor** | CDA-accredited training provider |
| **Duration** | 4-8 hours minimum |
| **Certificate** | PRS Certificate issued upon completion |
| **Validity** | Required before registration |

### PRS Curriculum Topics

1. **What is a Cooperative?**
   - Definition and characteristics
   - Difference from other organizations
   - Cooperative identity

2. **Cooperative Principles & Values**
   - ICA 7 Principles
   - Core and ethical values
   - Practical application

3. **Types of Cooperatives**
   - Classification by type and membership
   - Services offered by each type
   - Choosing the right type

4. **Member Rights & Responsibilities**
   - Member rights (Article 10)
   - Member obligations (Article 11)
   - Membership types

5. **Organizational Structure**
   - General Assembly
   - Board of Directors
   - Committees and Officers
   - Management

6. **Capital Structure & Statutory Funds**
   - Share capital
   - Reserve fund
   - Education and Training Fund
   - Community Development Fund
   - Net surplus distribution

7. **Benefits of Membership**
   - Services available
   - Patronage refunds
   - Interest on capital
   - Networking opportunities

8. **Registration Requirements**
   - Documentary requirements
   - Registration process
   - Compliance obligations

## Documentary Requirements

### Complete List of Requirements

| # | Document | Copies | Notes |
|---|----------|--------|-------|
| 1 | **Name Reservation Slip** | 1 | From CDA (valid 30/60/90 days) |
| 2 | **Articles of Cooperation** | 4 | Notarized, signed by all on every page |
| 3 | **Bylaws** | 4 | Signed by all on adoption page |
| 4 | **Economic Survey** | 4 | Signed by organizing committee |
| 5 | **Treasurer's Affidavit** | 1 | Notarized, stating capital received |
| 6 | **Surety Bond** | 1 | For accountable officers |
| 7 | **PRS Certificate** | 1 per member | From CDA-accredited provider |
| 8 | **Registration Fee** | - | Amount per CDA schedule |

### Name Reservation

**Process**:
1. Submit Cooperative Name Reservation Report Form (CNRRF)
2. Pay reservation fee

**Fees**:
| Duration | Fee |
|----------|-----|
| 30 days | PHP 100 |
| 60 days | PHP 200 |
| 90 days | PHP 300 |

**Name Requirements**:
- Must include "Cooperative" or abbreviation
- Must not be identical or confusingly similar to existing cooperatives
- Must not be misleading or contrary to law

### Articles of Cooperation

**Required Contents**:
1. Name of cooperative
2. Purpose and objectives
3. Principal office address
4. Term of existence (perpetual or fixed)
5. Area of operation
6. Names and residences of organizers
7. Authorized share capital
8. Par value of shares
9. Number of directors

### Bylaws

**Required Contents**:
1. Membership (qualifications, admission, termination)
2. Meetings (annual, special, notice, quorum)
3. Board of Directors (election, powers, meetings)
4. Officers (duties, compensation, removal)
5. Committees (composition, functions)
6. Capital (shares, transfer, withdrawal)
7. Funds allocation
8. Amendments procedure

### Treasurer's Affidavit

Must certify:
- Total amount received as share capital
- At least 25% of authorized capital subscribed
- At least 25% of subscribed capital paid up
- Minimum paid-up capital met

### Surety Bond

| Officer | Coverage |
|---------|----------|
| Treasurer | Required |
| Officers handling funds/property | Required |
| Board members (if handling funds) | As determined |

## Document Specifications

Per **MC 2015-01**, all registration documents must comply with:

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Paper Size** | 8.5" x 13" (legal) or 8.5" x 14" (long) |
| **Font** | Bookman Old Style 12 |
| **Copies** | 4 original copies per document |
| **Signatures** | Original signatures on all copies |
| **AOC Signing** | All organizers must sign on every page |
| **Bylaws Signing** | Majority must sign on adoption page |
| **Fasteners** | No metallic objects (no staple wires, paper clips) |
| **Notarization** | AOC and Treasurer's Affidavit must be notarized |

## Capital Requirements

Per **MC 2015-01 as amended by MC 2016-02**:

### Basic Capital Structure

| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| **Subscribed Capital** | 25% of Authorized Capital |
| **Paid-up Capital** | 25% of Subscribed Capital |

### Minimum Paid-up Capital by Type

| Cooperative Type | Minimum Paid-up Capital |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Primary Cooperatives** (general) | ₱15,000.00 |
| **Multipurpose Cooperatives** | ₱100,000.00 |
| **Cooperative Banks** | Per BSP regulations |
| **Secondary Cooperatives** | ₱100,000.00 |
| **Tertiary Cooperatives** | ₱100,000.00 |

### Example Capital Computation

For a Primary Credit Cooperative with ₱100,000 Authorized Capital:

| Item | Computation | Amount |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Authorized Capital | Set by organizers | ₱100,000.00 |
| Minimum Subscribed | 25% × ₱100,000 | ₱25,000.00 |
| Minimum Paid-up | 25% × ₱25,000 | ₱6,250.00 |
| **Actual Minimum** | MC 2016-02 floor | **₱15,000.00** |

> **Note**: Even if the computed paid-up is less than ₱15,000, the minimum ₱15,000 still applies per MC 2016-02.

## Registration Process

### Step-by-Step Process

```
1. SUBMIT application to CDA Extension Office
   (where principal office is located)
           ↓
2. CDA REVIEWS documents for completeness
           ↓
3. CDA EVALUATES for compliance with law
           ↓
4. CDA ISSUES Certificate of Registration
   (within 60 days if complete)
           ↓
5. COOPERATIVE acquires juridical personality
```

### Processing Timeline

| Stage | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Document review | 5-10 working days |
| Evaluation | 20-30 working days |
| Approval/Denial | Within 60 days total |

**Note**: Failure to act within 60 days = deemed approved (if delay not applicant's fault)

### Grounds for Denial

1. Incomplete documentary requirements
2. Non-compliance with minimum requirements
3. Name similar to existing cooperative
4. False statements in documents
5. Purposes contrary to law or public policy

## Post-Registration

### Immediate Obligations

| Obligation | Timeline |
|------------|----------|
| Conduct organizational meeting | Within 30 days |
| Elect first BOD and officers | At organizational meeting |
| Open bank account | Immediately after |
| Secure surety bonds | Before operations |
| Register with BIR | As required |

### Annual Compliance

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| General Assembly | Within 90 days after fiscal year end |
| CAPR submission | Within 120 days after fiscal year end |
| CAFS submission | Within 120 days after fiscal year end |
| Surety bond renewal | Before expiration |

### Amendments to Articles of Cooperation

**Process**:
1. Board resolution recommending amendment
2. 2/3 vote of General Assembly
3. File petition for amendment with CDA
4. CDA issues Certificate of Amendment

### Amendments to Bylaws

**Process**:
1. Board proposal or member petition
2. 2/3 vote of General Assembly
3. File amended bylaws with CDA
4. CDA issues acknowledgment

## BARMM/CSEA Registration

### CSEA vs CDA

| Jurisdiction | Authority |
|--------------|-----------|
| BARMM (Bangsamoro) | CSEA (Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority) |
| Rest of Philippines | CDA (Cooperative Development Authority) |

### CSEA Registration Requirements

Same documentary requirements as CDA, submitted to:

**CSEA Office**
JICA Building, BGC
Gov. Gutierrez Avenue
Brgy. RH XII, Cotabato City
Maguindanao 9600

**Contact**: (00) 552-1105

### Additional BARMM Considerations

Under BTA Bill No. 210:
- Alignment with moral governance
- Respect for local cultures and values
- Shari'ah-compliant operations (no usury, gambling, haram)
- Halal transactions only
