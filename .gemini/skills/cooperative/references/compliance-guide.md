## Reporting Requirements

### Annual Reports

| Report | Description | Deadline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **CAPR** | Cooperative Annual Progress Report | 120 days after fiscal year end |
| **CAFS** | Cooperative Audited Financial Statements | 120 days after fiscal year end |

### CAPR Contents

The Cooperative Annual Progress Report includes:

1. **General Information**
   - Cooperative profile
   - Registration details
   - Contact information

2. **Business Activities**
   - Types of operations
   - Services offered
   - Revenue sources

3. **Membership**
   - Total members (by type)
   - New members
   - Terminated members
   - Active vs inactive

4. **Officers**
   - Current BOD and officers
   - Committee members
   - Training compliance status

5. **Transactions and Finances**
   - Deposit liabilities
   - Loans outstanding
   - Taxes paid

6. **Other Information**
   - Type-specific data
   - Community activities
   - Awards/recognition

### CAFS Requirements

| Cooperative Size | Auditor Requirement |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Assets < PHP 3M | Internal audit acceptable |
| Assets PHP 3M - 15M | CPA-signed |
| Assets > PHP 15M | External audit by CDA-accredited auditor |

**CAFS Components**:
- Statement of Financial Position
- Statement of Operations
- Statement of Changes in Members' Equity
- Statement of Cash Flows
- Notes to Financial Statements

## CAIS System

### What is CAIS?

**Cooperative Assessment Information System (CAIS)** is CDA's online platform for:
- Annual report submission
- Compliance monitoring
- Cooperative data management

**Access**: https://cs.cda.gov.ph/cais/

### CAIS Modules

| Module | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| General Information | Cooperative profile |
| Business Activities | Operations data |
| Membership | Member statistics |
| Officers | Leadership data |
| Transactions | Financial transactions |
| Other Information | Type-specific data |
| Laboratory | For laboratory cooperatives |

### Submission Process

```
1. LOGIN to CAIS system
          ↓
2. COMPLETE all required modules
          ↓
3. UPLOAD financial statements
          ↓
4. VALIDATE data entry
          ↓
5. SUBMIT electronically
          ↓
6. PRINT acknowledgment receipt
          ↓
7. SUBMIT hard copy AFS to CDA Extension Office
```

### Offline Submission

If CAIS is unavailable:
- Download offline CAPR form from CDA website
- Complete and print
- Submit to CDA Extension Office

## Certificate of Compliance

### What is COC?

The **Certificate of Compliance (COC)** is CDA's certification that a cooperative has complied with all statutory requirements.

### COC Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| CAPR submission | Complete and accurate |
| CAFS submission | Audited as required |
| Surety bonds | Current and adequate |
| Training compliance | Officers trained |
| Prior deficiencies | Addressed and resolved |

### Grounds for Non-Issuance

1. Non-submission of required reports
2. Discrepancies between online and hard copy submissions
3. Outstanding deficiencies from prior years
4. Unpaid penalties
5. Non-compliance with corrective orders

### COC Validity

- Valid for one (1) year
- Must be renewed annually
- Required for various transactions

### Uses of COC

- Proof of good standing
- Required for bank transactions
- Required for government dealings
- Required for loan applications
- Required for partnerships

## Statutory Funds Compliance

### Required Allocations

| Fund | Minimum % | First 5 Years |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Reserve Fund** | 10% | 50% |
| **ETF** | Up to 10% | Up to 10% |
| **CDF** | 3% | 3% |

### Reserve Fund Rules

**Allowed Uses**:
- Cover operating losses
- Provide for stability

**Prohibited**:
- Distribution to members
- Direct charging of losses without GA approval

### Education and Training Fund (ETF)

**Allocation**:
- 50% Local (cooperative's own training)
- 50% Federation/Union contribution

**Allowed Uses**:
- Member education
- Officer training
- Employee development
- Public information campaigns

### Community Development Fund (CDF)

**Allowed Uses**:
- Community projects
- Social responsibility programs
- Local development initiatives

**Prohibited**:
- Member-exclusive benefits
- Political activities

### Monitoring

CDA inspectors verify annually:
- Proper allocation percentages
- Correct fund utilization
- Complete documentation
- Proper accounting treatment

## Training Requirements

### Mandatory Training for Officers

| Position | Required Training | Within |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| **BOD** | Basic Coop Management Course | First half of term |
| **Committee Chairs** | Specialized training per committee | First half of term |
| **Treasurer** | Financial management | First half of term |
| **Secretary** | Records management | First half of term |

### Training Providers

Only CDA-accredited training providers may conduct mandatory training.

**Accreditation Requirements**:
- Juridical personality
- At least 2 years existence
- Pool of 5+ competent trainers
- Trainers with Bachelor's degree
- Trainers with TOT completion
- 2+ years experience as resource person

### Sanctions for Non-Compliance

**For Officers**:
- Disqualification from future election/appointment
- Until training is completed

**For Cooperative**:
- Non-issuance of Certificate of Compliance
- Warning letter
- Potential sanctions

### Training Records

Cooperatives must maintain:
- Training attendance records
- Certificates of completion
- Training provider documentation
- Submitted to CDA in CAPR

## Common Violations

### Documentary Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Late report submission | Penalty + COC delay |
| Incomplete submissions | Deficiency notice |
| False information | Registration cancellation |
| Missing bonds | COC non-issuance |

### Governance Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| No General Assembly | Serious violation |
| BOD not meeting monthly | Warning |
| Elections not held | Serious violation |
| Quorum issues | Invalid resolutions |

### Financial Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| No proper books | Serious violation |
| Fund misuse | Criminal liability |
| No audit | COC non-issuance |
| Unreported transactions | Fines |

### Operational Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Operating beyond registration | Warning to cancellation |
| Non-member transactions (excessive) | Tax implications |
| Illegal activities | Dissolution |

## Penalties and Sanctions

### Administrative Sanctions

| Level | Sanctions |
|-------|-----------|
| **Minor** | Warning, compliance order |
| **Moderate** | Fines, suspension of privileges |
| **Serious** | Suspension of operations |
| **Grave** | Dissolution, registration cancellation |

### Fines

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Late submission of reports | Per CDA schedule |
| Non-compliance with orders | Per CDA schedule |
| Continued violations | Escalating fines |

### Dissolution Grounds

1. Fraudulent registration
2. Illegal operational purpose
3. Willful Code/bylaw violations
4. Failure to operate for 2 consecutive years
5. Failure to maintain minimum membership
6. Insolvency

### Appeal Process

```
1. Receive order/decision from CDA
          ↓
2. File motion for reconsideration (15 days)
          ↓
3. If denied, appeal to CDA Board (15 days)
          ↓
4. If denied, appeal to Court of Appeals (15 days)
```
