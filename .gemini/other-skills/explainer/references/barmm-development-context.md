# BARMM Development Context Reference

**Purpose**: Comprehensive reference for understanding BARMM's development framework, economic data, governance structure, and strategic context for explaining the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and Negosyo Bangsamoro Platform.

**Source**: 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028, Official Government of BARMM

## I. BARMM Vision, Mission & Framework

### Vision Statement

**"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."**

Key elements:
- **United**: Harmony across the Bangsamoro community
- **Enlightened**: Informed, educated population
- **Self-governing**: Genuine autonomy and control over own affairs
- **Peaceful**: Permanent peace and security
- **Just**: Fair and equitable treatment
- **Morally upright**: Guided by ethical leadership
- **Progressive**: Forward-thinking and development-oriented

### Mission Statement

**"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."**

Core principles underpinning mission execution:
- **Moral Governance**: Integrity, accountability, transparency, justice, excellence
- **Genuine Autonomy**: Independence in policy-making while remaining part of Philippines
- **Sustained Development**: Long-term economic and social progress
- **Community-Centered**: Responsive to actual needs and aspirations
- **Inclusive Participation**: All stakeholders in decision-making
- **Partnership Facilitation**: Collaboration with government, private, civil society

### Overall Goal: Empowered, Cohesive, Progressive Bangsamoro

| Dimension | Meaning | Indicators |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Empowered** | Improved capacity of communities through quality services and economic opportunities | Access to education, healthcare, financial services, jobs, business opportunities |
| **Cohesive** | Strong relationships, reduced conflict, forged partnerships | Lower violent conflict incidents, active stakeholder collaboration, cross-sector partnerships |
| **Progressive** | Sustained ability to optimize resources and realize economic/human potential | GDP growth, poverty reduction, skills development, technology adoption |

## II. Six Development Goals (2023-2028)

### Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government

**Components**:
- **Stability**: Continuity of service delivery despite external/internal shocks
- **Justice**: Government as agent of economic and social justice; inclusive growth
- **Accountability**: Clear frameworks for recognizing good performance and preventing bad practices

**Key Targets**: Effective and efficient government operations even during emergencies

### Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy

**Components**:
- **Equitable**: Benefits reach all, especially poor and marginalized; improved inequality indicators
- **Competitive**: BARMM economy has comparative advantages in selected commodities with optimized market linkages
- **Robust**: Sustained economic growth over six years with diversified economy

**Key Target**: Double-digit growth in services and industry sectors (10-12% annually)

### Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities

**Components**:
- **Peaceful**: Lasting peace through security and peace-building
- **Safe**: Communities protected from violence and disaster
- **Resilient**: Capacity to withstand natural calamities and human-induced disasters

**Key Target**: Reduced violent conflict incidents; improved disaster preparedness

### Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services

**Components**:
- **Inclusive**: Services for all sectors regardless of affiliation; leave no one behind
- **Responsive**: Services match actual needs of people and communities
- **Quality**: Excellence in delivery across health, education, social protection

**Key Target**: Universal access to quality education, healthcare, and social services

### Goal 5: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved and Recognized

**Components**:
- **Cultural Preservation**: Maintain and promote rich, diverse culture
- **Identity Recognition**: Acknowledge Bangsamoro identity as integral to region's character
- **Heritage Protection**: Safeguard traditional knowledge, arts, customs

**Key Target**: Mandated under BOL Section 24, Article IX

### Goal 6: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure

**Components**:
- **Strategic**: Infrastructure with broad-based inclusive effects across industries and communities
- **Adequate**: Meets infrastructure demands of industries and communities
- **Climate-Resilient**: Withstands climate-induced calamities

**Key Target**: Road network improvements, port development, power infrastructure

## IV. BARMM Economic Profile

### Key Statistics

**BARMM Economic Scale**:
- **GRDP Share**: 1.4% of national economic growth (lowest among regions)
- **Population**: ~4.3 million (2020 Census)
- **Land Area**: 11.17% of total Philippine land
- **Geographic Composition**: 6 provinces + 3 cities + 1 Special Geographic Area

### Cooperative & Social Enterprise Sector

**Critical for Negosyo Bangsamoro Context**:

| Metric | Figure |
|--------|--------|
| **Registered Cooperatives** | 12,214 coops (as of latest data) |
| **Type Distribution** | Marketing, service, credit, multi-purpose, producer coops |
| **Coverage** | All 6 provinces and 3 cities |
| **Members Served** | Thousands of cooperative members across BARMM |
| **MSME Participation** | Cooperatives are primary vehicle for MSME formalization |

**Role in Economy**:
- Primary mechanism for rural economic inclusion
- Essential for agricultural product consolidation and marketing
- Vehicle for access to credit, training, and services
- Support structure for social enterprises

### Agricultural Base (Still Significant)

**Top Commodities** (2020):
1. Coconut
2. Rice
3. Corn
4. Banana
5. Calamansi
6. Cassava
7. Sweet potato
8. Mango
9. Abaca
10. Pumpkin

**Sectors Targeted for Growth**:
- Fisheries (marine and inland)
- Livestock and poultry
- Agro-processing
- Value-added agricultural products

**Land Availability**:
- **Alienable & Disposable**: 628,401 hectares
- **Forest Land**: 665,151 hectares
- **CARP Coverage**: 340,486 hectares (highest in country)
- **Distribution Status**: 68% distributed, 32% balance pending

### Manufacturing & Industry Status

**Current State (Challenges)**:
- Only 1,340 registered manufacturing establishments (0.6% of national total)
- Weak industrial base compared to other regions
- Limited technology adoption
- Nascent STI ecosystem

**Growth Opportunity**:
- Ecozone development (planned across provinces)
- Technology business incubators
- Science parks for startup nurturing
- MSME technology upgrading programs

### Tourism Sector

**Performance** (2018-2021):
- Tourist arrivals growing but still nascent
- Island provinces (Sulu, Tawi-Tawi) have potential
- Cultural and heritage tourism opportunities
- Marine and ecological tourism prospects

## V. Governance Structure (BOL Framework)

### BARMM Governance Hierarchy

**Constitutional Authority**:
- **Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL)** - Republic Act No. 11054
- Effective: July 27, 2018
- Provides: Bangsamoro autonomous powers and governance structure

### Key Governance Bodies

#### Bangsamoro Parliament
- **Function**: Legislative body for BARMM
- **Composition**: Representatives from provinces, cities, and constituencies
- **Powers**: Enact laws on matters within BARMM jurisdiction (per BOL Art. V)
- **Role**: Approval authority for development plans, legislation

#### Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (BEDC)
- **Function**: Planning, monitoring, and coordinating agency for development
- **Composition**: Sectoral committees (Social, Economic, Infrastructure, Governance, Peace & Security)
- **Technical Secretariat**: Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA)
- **Responsibility**: Formulate and monitor development plans like 2nd BDP 2023-2028

#### Chief Minister's Office
- **Executive Leadership**: Head of executive branch
- **Powers**: Implement laws, direct ministries, issue executive orders
- **Coordination**: Ensures alignment of agency programs with development plan

### BARMM Authorities under BOL

**Article V: BARMM Legislative Powers** include:
- Agriculture, livestock, food security
- Cooperatives and social entrepreneurship (CSEA authority)
- Business name registration and trade/industry
- Economic zones, industrial centers, free ports
- Fishery, marine, aquatic resources
- Islamic banking and finance
- Labor, employment, occupation
- Tourism development

### Cooperative Regulatory Authority: CSEA

**Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA)**:
- **Mandate**: Regulation and development of cooperatives and social enterprises in BARMM
- **Authority**: Granted under BOL and BARMM Autonomy Act (BAA) provisions
- **Regulatory Framework**: Philippine Cooperative Code (RA 9520) as adapted for BARMM context
- **Key Functions**:
  - Registration of cooperatives
  - Compliance monitoring (FRAMES rating system)
  - Training and education support
  - Technical assistance
  - Advocacy for cooperative development

1. **Governance**: Improve and strengthen governance mechanisms for stable, just, accountable government
2. **Economic Environment**: Create enabling environment for equitable, competitive, robust economy
3. **Technology**: Harness technology and innovations to increase socioeconomic opportunities

| Pillar | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Integrity** | Act without undue influence; transparent in decisions |
| **Accountability** | Open about decisions; document actions; answer to people |
| **Transparency** | Declare interests; resolve conflicts; make transactions public |
| **Patriotism** | Invest in community; maintain dignity and integrity of region |
| **Excellence** | Deliver highest quality; prioritize credibility and transparency |
| **Cultural Understanding** | Recognize and respect socioeconomic differences; value diversity |
| **Divine Guidance** | Strict adherence to moral and ethical principles |
| **Justice** | Serve without prejudice; equity above personal interest |
| **Inclusivity** | Foster welcoming environment; freedom from discrimination |
| **Objectivity** | Merit-based decisions; public interest focus |
| **Dialogue** | Meaningful engagement with communities; collaborative problem-solving |
| **Balance** | Integrate practical and moral/ethical values in governance |

## VIII. Negosyo Bangsamoro Platform Context

### Platform Purpose in BARMM Development

**Alignment with Goal 2 (Equitable, Competitive, Robust Economy)**:
- Support cooperative and social enterprise formalization
- Enable market linkages for BARMM products
- Facilitate business registration and compliance
- Provide digital infrastructure for MSMEs
- Support sectoral growth (agriculture, trade, services)

### CSEA's Role in Platform

**Regulatory & Developmental Functions**:

| Function | Platform Support |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Cooperative Registration** | Digital registration process; profile management |
| **Compliance Monitoring** | FRAMES-based assessment tracking; document management |
| **Business Development** | Training resources; market linkage tools; analytics |
| **Market Facilitation** | Marketplace for cooperative products; storefronts |
| **Data & Analytics** | Sector performance tracking; cooperative statistics |

### Alignment with BDP Priorities

**Supports Multiple Development Goals**:
- **Goal 1**: Accountable government through transparent registration
- **Goal 2**: Competitive economy through market linkages and digital infrastructure
- **Goal 4**: Quality services through accessible business support
- **Goal 6**: Infrastructure through digital infrastructure investment

**Supports Economic Strategies**:
- Enabling environment for cooperatives and SEs
- Technology harness for business opportunities
- Digital infrastructure for service delivery

### Key Metrics for Platform Success

**Economic Impact Targets**:
- Cooperative registration and formalization rates
- Cooperative members engaged in platform marketplace
- Product sales through platform
- MSME technology adoption
- Job creation through cooperative growth

**Development Outcomes**:
- Poverty reduction through economic opportunity
- Increased cooperative economic activities
- Improved market linkages for cooperative products
- Enhanced competitiveness of BARMM enterprises

## IX. Technology & Innovation Context

### STI Importance in BARMM Development

**Strategic Role**:
- Key driver for economic growth and development
- Central to transition to inclusive, sustainable development
- Enabler for agricultural sector productivity
- Required for industrial sector development
- Essential for services sector competitiveness

### Current STI Ecosystem Status

**Challenges**:
- Weak institutional support for R&D
- Limited science and technology laboratory facilities
- Low scientific literacy (national avg ~3.2%)
- Weak industry-academia linkage
- Limited startup support infrastructure
- Only 1 service laboratory (BSHTL in Cotabato City)

**Opportunities**:
- BASE Scholarship for science students
- BARMM Regional Science High School development
- BARMM R&D Agenda 2020-2025
- BARMM Health Research Consortium
- Technology business incubator opportunities
- Halal research potential

### Platform Technology Context

**Negosyo Bangsamoro as STI Initiative**:
- Digital infrastructure for MSME capacity building
- Technology adoption support for cooperatives
- SETUP (Small Enterprise Technology Upgrading Program) integration
- Digital marketplace as innovation platform
- Training platform (MoroAcademy) for skills development
