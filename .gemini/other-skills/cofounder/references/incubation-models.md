## Incubation Overview

### Incubation vs. Equity Cofounding

| Aspect | Equity Cofounding | Incubation Model |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Ownership** | Equity stake in venture | No ownership stake |
| **Commitment** | Full-time, long-term | Project-based, defined scope |
| **Risk** | Share success and failure | Limited to resources invested |
| **Exit** | Acquisition, IPO, or failure | Graduation, success fee, or ongoing services |
| **Relationship** | Partner/owner | Advisor/supporter/facilitator |
| **Incentive** | Equity appreciation | Fees, impact, reputation |

### When to Use Incubation Model

**Use incubation when:**
- You want to support many ventures (portfolio approach)
- You can't commit full-time to one venture
- The venture is a cooperative or SE (no traditional equity)
- You want to build an ecosystem, not just a company
- You prefer defined engagements over open-ended commitments

**Use equity cofounding when:**
- You want to go all-in on one venture
- You have unique skills the venture can't buy
- You want full upside (and downside) participation
- You're ready for long-term commitment

#### Incubation Program Design

**Program Components:**

| Component | Purpose | Duration |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Selection** | Choose high-potential ventures | 1-2 months |
| **Onboarding** | Set expectations, baseline | 2 weeks |
| **Core program** | Intensive support | 3-6 months |
| **Demo/showcase** | Visibility, validation | 1 day |
| **Graduation** | Transition to independence | 1 month |
| **Alumni support** | Ongoing light-touch | Ongoing |

**Program Models:**

| Model | Duration | Intensity | Best For |
|-------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Accelerator** | 3 months | High (full-time) | Growth-stage |

| Indicator | Threshold |
