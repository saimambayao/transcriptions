## YC Core Philosophy

### The Motto: "Make Something People Want"

Y Combinator's entire methodology centers on one simple principle:

> "Make something people want. If you don't do that, nothing else you do will matter." — Paul Graham

This is not about what you *think* people want, but what they *actually* want, validated through real user behavior.

### Key Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **User Obsession** | Talk to users constantly, understand their problems deeply |
| **Launch Fast** | Get something in front of users in weeks, not months |
| **Iterate Rapidly** | Use feedback loops to improve continuously |
| **Founder-First** | Great founders can overcome product issues; great products can't overcome founder issues |
| **Focus on Growth** | A startup is defined by its growth rate, not its size |

## YC Program Structure

### The Batch Model

YC runs two batches per year (Winter and Summer), each lasting approximately 3 months:

```
Week 0-1:     Onboarding, goal setting
Week 2-10:   Intensive building, weekly office hours, group sessions
Week 11-12:   Demo Day preparation
Demo Day:     Pitch to 1,500+ investors
Post-batch:   Lifetime alumni network access
```

### Key Components

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Office Hours** | Weekly 1:1 sessions with YC partners for specific advice |
| **Group Partners** | Assigned group of ~20 startups for peer support |
| **Dinners** | Weekly talks from successful founders (Airbnb, Stripe, etc.) |
| **Demo Day** | Culminating pitch event to investor audience |
| **Alumni Network** | 10,000+ founders across 5,000+ companies |

## YC Investment Terms

### Standard Deal (as of 2024)

| Component | Amount | Terms |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Initial Investment** | $125,000 | For 7% equity (common stock) |
| **MFN SAFE** | $375,000 | Uncapped, Most Favored Nation |
| **Total** | $500,000 | Combined investment |

### Key Terms Explained

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**
- Not debt, not equity—converts to equity in future priced round
- No valuation cap on the MFN portion
- "Most Favored Nation" means founders get best terms if they offer better SAFEs later

**Why YC Uses SAFEs**
- Fast (minimal legal negotiation)
- Founder-friendly (no board seats, no control provisions)
- Standardized (investors understand them)

## YC Partner Key Teachings

### Paul Graham (Co-founder)

#### Core Essays & Frameworks

| Essay | Key Insight |
|-------|-------------|
| **"Do Things That Don't Scale"** | Manual, unscalable tactics are essential at the start |
| **"How to Get Startup Ideas"** | Best ideas come from problems you personally experience |
| **"Startup = Growth"** | A startup is defined by its growth rate |
| **"The 18 Mistakes That Kill Startups"** | Common failure patterns to avoid |

#### "Do Things That Don't Scale" Tactics

1. **Recruit users manually** - Go door-to-door, call people directly
2. **Delight users individually** - White-glove service, personal attention
3. **Do things by hand first** - Manual before automation
4. **Focus on narrow market** - Dominate a small niche before expanding

> "The most successful startups start by doing things that don't scale. Airbnb went door-to-door, Stripe installed their product for users, Wufoo sent handwritten notes."

#### How to Get Startup Ideas

**Good Ideas Come From:**
- Problems you personally experience
- Things that seem like toys
- Changes in the world (new technology, regulations)
- Fixing broken industries

**Bad Ideas Come From:**
- Trying to think of startup ideas
- Solutions looking for problems
- Copycat ideas without unique insight

### Michael Seibel (Managing Director, CEO)

#### Product-Market Fit (PMF)

> "98% of founders that don't get PMF also don't know the definition of product-market fit."

**PMF Definition:** When you're "drowning in demand" - can't keep up with customer requests.

**PMF Indicators:**
| Indicator | What It Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Retention** | Users keep coming back without prompting |
| **Organic Growth** | Word-of-mouth without marketing spend |
| **Pull** | Customers actively seeking you out |
| **Revenue** | Willing to pay, and pay more |

**PMF Anti-Patterns:**
- Vanity metrics (downloads, signups without engagement)
- Investor interest (doesn't equal customer interest)
- Press coverage (doesn't mean product works)

#### MVP Strategy

**The 2-Week MVP:**
> "If you can't build an MVP in 2 weeks, you're building too much."

**90/10 Solution:**
- Build 90% of the value with 10% of the effort
- Focus on the core problem, nothing else
- Ship something embarrassingly simple

**MVP Types:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Concierge** | Do it manually first | Food delivery by hand before app |
| **Wizard of Oz** | Fake automation | Human behind "AI" chatbot |
| **Landing Page** | Test demand before building | Signup page with waitlist |
| **Prototype** | Minimal functional version | Basic working app |

### Sam Altman (Former President)

#### The Startup Playbook

**Four Things That Matter:**

1. **Great Idea** - Includes market size, growth, defensibility
2. **Great Product** - Users love it, recommend it
3. **Great Team** - Can execute and adapt
4. **Great Execution** - Speed and focus

**On Ideas:**
> "The best ideas are often the ones that seem bad at first but turn out to be good."

**On Growth:**
- Focus on growth rate, not absolute numbers
- 5-7% weekly growth is good
- 10% weekly growth is exceptional

#### Execution Priorities

| Priority | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **1. Growth** | Users/revenue increasing week over week |
| **2. Momentum** | Keep the team moving fast |
| **3. Focus** | Say no to everything except the core |

### Dalton Caldwell (Managing Director, Admissions)

#### What YC Looks For

**Founder Qualities:**
| Quality | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Clarity** | Can explain idea clearly in 1-2 sentences |
| **Determination** | Won't give up, history of perseverance |
| **Domain Expertise** | Deep knowledge of the problem space |
| **Technical Ability** | Can build the product themselves |
| **Flexibility** | Willing to pivot based on evidence |

#### Application Advice

**What Works:**
- Short, clear answers
- Concrete evidence of traction
- Specific, not generic claims
- Show, don't tell

**Red Flags:**
- Overcomplicated explanations
- No users or revenue
- Attacking competitors instead of building
- Solution in search of problem

## YC Growth Metrics

### Weekly Growth Targets

| Growth Rate | Classification |
|-------------|----------------|
| **1-2%/week** | Struggling |
| **5-7%/week** | Good |
| **10%/week** | Exceptional |

### The Power of Compound Growth

```
5% weekly growth = 13x annual growth
7% weekly growth = 33x annual growth
10% weekly growth = 142x annual growth
```

### Key Metrics to Track

| Stage | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Pre-launch** | User interviews completed | Problems validated |
| **MVP** | Active users | Engagement, feedback |
| **Growth** | Weekly growth rate | Retention, NPS |
| **Scale** | Revenue | Unit economics, CAC/LTV |

## Common YC Advice

### The Essentials

1. **Launch now** - Something basic in weeks, not months
2. **Talk to users** - 10+ conversations per week minimum
3. **Do things that don't scale** - Manual first, automate later
4. **Write code and talk to users** - Only two activities that matter
5. **Focus** - Do one thing well before adding features
6. **Growth is everything** - Optimize for weekly growth rate
7. **Iterate fast** - Ship updates weekly or faster
8. **Pick a good co-founder** - Most important decision
9. **Stay alive** - Default alive > default dead

### What to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Harmful |
|---------|------------------|
| **Building without users** | No validation, wasted effort |
| **Premature scaling** | Scaling a broken model |
| **Ignoring metrics** | Flying blind |
| **Too many features** | Diluted focus |
| **Hiring too fast** | Burns cash, slows decisions |
| **Raising too much** | Complacency, unrealistic expectations |

## Quick Reference Tables

### YC Decision Framework

```
Question to ask constantly:
"Is this helping us grow faster?"

If YES → Do more of it
If NO → Stop doing it
If UNSURE → Test it for 2 weeks
```

### Founder Self-Assessment (YC Style)

| Question | Good Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Can you explain your idea in one sentence? | Clear, specific, compelling |
| How do you know people want this? | Evidence: users, revenue, engagement |
| Why are you the right team? | Domain expertise, technical ability, determination |
| What have you built so far? | Working product with users |
| What's your weekly growth rate? | 5-10% or higher |

### PMF Checklist

```
□ Users would be "very disappointed" if product went away (40%+)
□ Organic growth without paid marketing
□ Users actively recommend to others
□ Demand exceeds your ability to serve
□ Customers willing to pay (and pay more)
□ Clear path to profitable unit economics
```
