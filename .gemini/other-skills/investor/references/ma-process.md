## M&A Deal Lifecycle

### Overview

M&A transactions typically take 6-12 months from initial contact to closing, sometimes longer for complex deals.

```text
M&A TIMELINE:
│
├── PHASE 1: STRATEGY & PLANNING (Weeks 1-4)
│   ├── Define strategic rationale
│   ├── Identify target criteria
│   └── Screen potential targets
│
├── PHASE 2: INITIAL CONTACT (Weeks 4-8)
│   ├── Approach targets
│   ├── Sign NDA
│   └── Exchange preliminary information
│
├── PHASE 3: PRELIMINARY VALUATION (Weeks 8-12)
│   ├── Analyze target financials
│   ├── Develop initial valuation
│   └── Assess strategic fit
│
├── PHASE 4: LETTER OF INTENT (Weeks 12-16)
│   ├── Negotiate key terms
│   ├── Sign LOI
│   └── Establish exclusivity
│
├── PHASE 5: DUE DILIGENCE (Weeks 16-24)
│   ├── Comprehensive review
│   ├── Validate assumptions
│   └── Identify risks
│
├── PHASE 6: DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT (Weeks 24-28)
│   ├── Negotiate final terms
│   ├── Draft and review agreements
│   └── Sign definitive agreement
│
├── PHASE 7: REGULATORY & CLOSING (Weeks 28-36+)
│   ├── Regulatory approvals
│   ├── Financing completion
│   └── Close transaction
│
└── PHASE 8: POST-MERGER INTEGRATION
    ├── Day-1 execution
    ├── Integration implementation
    └── Synergy realization
```

## Sell-Side Process

### Objectives

- Maximize value for shareholders
- Run competitive process to create tension
- Control information and timing

### Process Steps

```text
SELL-SIDE M&A PROCESS:
│
├── 1. PREPARATION
│   ├── Engage advisors (IB, legal, accounting)
│   ├── Prepare confidential information memorandum (CIM)
│   ├── Build data room
│   └── Develop buyer list
│
├── 2. MARKETING
│   ├── Send teaser to potential buyers
│   ├── Execute NDAs with interested parties
│   ├── Distribute CIM
│   └── Conduct management presentations
│
├── 3. FIRST ROUND BIDS
│   ├── Receive initial indications of interest
│   ├── Evaluate bids on price, terms, certainty
│   └── Select parties for second round
│
├── 4. DUE DILIGENCE
│   ├── Open data room to selected bidders
│   ├── Conduct management meetings
│   └── Respond to diligence requests
│
├── 5. FINAL BIDS
│   ├── Receive binding offers
│   ├── Negotiate with final bidders
│   └── Select winning bid
│
└── 6. CLOSING
    ├── Sign definitive agreement
    ├── Obtain approvals
    └── Close transaction
```

### Key Documents

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Teaser** | Anonymous summary to generate interest |
| **NDA** | Protect confidential information |
| **CIM** | Detailed company description |
| **Process Letter** | Instructions for bidders |
| **Management Presentation** | Deep-dive for serious bidders |

## Buy-Side Process

### Objectives

- Acquire target at attractive price
- Minimize execution risk
- Achieve strategic objectives

### Process Steps

```text
BUY-SIDE M&A PROCESS:
│
├── 1. STRATEGY
│   ├── Define acquisition criteria
│   ├── Screen potential targets
│   └── Engage advisors
│
├── 2. INITIAL APPROACH
│   ├── Contact target (direct or via banker)
│   ├── Sign NDA
│   └── Receive initial information
│
├── 3. PRELIMINARY ANALYSIS
│   ├── Review CIM/initial materials
│   ├── Develop preliminary valuation
│   └── Assess strategic fit
│
├── 4. SUBMIT IOI
│   ├── Indication of interest with value range
│   ├── Outline key terms
│   └── Describe rationale and approach
│
├── 5. DUE DILIGENCE
│   ├── Access data room
│   ├── Conduct workstreams (financial, legal, commercial)
│   └── Meet with management
│
├── 6. SUBMIT BINDING OFFER
│   ├── Final valuation
│   ├── Detailed terms
│   └── Markup of purchase agreement
│
└── 7. NEGOTIATE & CLOSE
    ├── Final negotiations
    ├── Sign definitive agreement
    └── Complete closing conditions
```

## Due Diligence Framework

### Overview

Due diligence is the comprehensive investigation of a target company to validate information and identify risks.

### Due Diligence Workstreams

```text
DUE DILIGENCE AREAS:
│
├── FINANCIAL
│   ├── Quality of earnings analysis
│   ├── Working capital analysis
│   ├── Debt and off-balance sheet liabilities
│   ├── Tax positions and exposures
│   └── Financial projections validation
│
├── COMMERCIAL
│   ├── Market size and dynamics
│   ├── Competitive positioning
│   ├── Customer analysis and concentration
│   ├── Pricing power and margins
│   └── Growth opportunities
│
├── OPERATIONAL
│   ├── Operations and manufacturing
│   ├── Supply chain analysis
│   ├── Technology and systems
│   ├── Human resources and culture
│   └── Real estate and facilities
│
├── LEGAL
│   ├── Corporate structure
│   ├── Material contracts
│   ├── Intellectual property
│   ├── Litigation and disputes
│   └── Regulatory compliance
│
└── INTEGRATION
    ├── Synergy identification and validation
    ├── Integration complexity
    ├── Cultural compatibility
    └── Day-1 readiness
```

### Quality of Earnings Analysis

```text
QUALITY OF EARNINGS:
│
├── NORMALIZE EBITDA
│   ├── Remove one-time items
│   ├── Adjust for related party transactions
│   ├── Normalize owner compensation
│   └── Adjust for non-recurring revenue/expenses
│
├── VALIDATE REVENUE
│   ├── Revenue recognition policies
│   ├── Customer concentration
│   ├── Revenue sustainability
│   └── Backlog analysis
│
├── ANALYZE COSTS
│   ├── Cost structure breakdown
│   ├── Variable vs. fixed
│   ├── Unusual cost items
│   └── Cost trends
│
└── WORKING CAPITAL
    ├── Net working capital calculation
    ├── Seasonality impacts
    ├── Target NWC for closing
    └── Cash conversion cycle
```

### Due Diligence Red Flags

| Category | Red Flags |
|----------|-----------|
| **Financial** | Declining margins, accounting changes, unusual adjustments |
| **Commercial** | Customer concentration, market share loss, pricing pressure |
| **Operational** | Key person dependency, aging assets, supplier issues |
| **Legal** | Pending litigation, IP disputes, regulatory violations |
| **Cultural** | High turnover, management conflicts, resistance to change |

## Deal Structuring

### Consideration Types

| Type | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Cash** | Certainty, simplicity | Buyer cash outflow, no upside sharing |
| **Stock** | Preserves cash, shared upside/risk | Dilution, valuation complexity |
| **Mixed** | Balance of benefits | More complex |

### Payment Structures

| Structure | Description | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **All Upfront** | Full payment at closing | Clean, certain deals |
| **Earnout** | Contingent on future performance | Bridge valuation gaps |
| **Holdback** | Portion held for representations | Risk mitigation |
| **Escrow** | Funds held by third party | Indemnification protection |
| **Seller Note** | Deferred payment | Seller financing |

### Key Agreement Terms

```text
DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT KEY TERMS:
│
├── PURCHASE PRICE
│   ├── Base price
│   ├── Working capital adjustment mechanism
│   ├── Earnout provisions (if any)
│   └── Escrow/holdback amounts
│
├── REPRESENTATIONS & WARRANTIES
│   ├── Seller reps about the business
│   ├── Buyer reps about authority/capacity
│   └── Survival periods
│
├── INDEMNIFICATION
│   ├── Caps and baskets
│   ├── Survival periods
│   └── Special indemnities
│
├── CONDITIONS TO CLOSING
│   ├── Regulatory approvals
│   ├── Third-party consents
│   ├── MAC clause
│   └── Bring-down of reps
│
└── COVENANTS
    ├── Pre-closing operating covenants
    ├── Post-closing covenants
    └── Non-compete/non-solicit
```

### Material Adverse Change (MAC)

```text
MAC CLAUSE:
│
├── DEFINITION
│   └── Significant negative change to target business
│
├── TYPICAL CARVE-OUTS
│   ├── General economic conditions
│   ├── Industry-wide changes
│   ├── Changes in law
│   └── Announced transaction effects
│
└── PURPOSE
    └── Allows buyer to terminate if significant deterioration
```

# M&A Advisory Mode (from SKILL.md)

### Mode 3: M&A Advisory

For M&A process consulting and deal support.

#### M&A Deal Types

| Deal Type | Description | Key Considerations |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Horizontal** | Same industry merger | Synergies, market share |
| **Vertical** | Supply chain integration | Cost savings, control |
| **Conglomerate** | Unrelated businesses | Diversification, portfolio |
| **Acquisition** | Buyer acquires target | Premium, integration |
| **Merger of Equals** | Two companies combine | Governance, culture |

#### Due Diligence Framework

```text
DUE DILIGENCE AREAS:
│
├── FINANCIAL
│   ├── Quality of earnings (→ /financial-analyst)
│   ├── Working capital analysis
│   ├── Debt and liabilities
│   └── Cash flow sustainability
│
├── PRODUCT DIFFERENTIATION (MVaP Assessment)
│   ├── Has ONE clear Delighter as UVP?
│   │   └── Warning: "Maximally Undifferentiated Product" is a red flag
│   ├── Performance features meet market threshold?
│   │   └── Over-engineering indicates poor capital allocation
│   ├── Must-Haves use existing solutions?
│   │   └── Building commodities from scratch = wasted resources
│   └── Feature prioritization discipline?
│       └── Reference: ../shared/references/mvap-framework.md
│
├── COMMERCIAL
│   ├── Market position
│   ├── Customer concentration
│   ├── Competitive dynamics
│   └── Growth prospects
│
├── OPERATIONAL
│   ├── Operations efficiency
│   ├── Supply chain
│   ├── Technology and systems
│   └── Human capital
│
├── LEGAL
│   ├── Contracts and commitments
│   ├── Intellectual property
│   ├── Litigation and disputes
│   └── Regulatory compliance
│
└── INTEGRATION
    ├── Cultural fit
    ├── Synergy validation
    ├── Integration risks
    └── Day-1 readiness
```

#### Deal Structuring

| Structure Element | Options | Considerations |
|------------------|---------|----------------|
| **Consideration** | Cash, Stock, Mixed | Tax, dilution, risk sharing |
| **Payment** | Upfront, Earnout, Holdback | Risk allocation, incentives |
| **Conditions** | Regulatory, Financing, MAC | Deal certainty, timeline |
| **Governance** | Board seats, Veto rights | Control, influence |
