## Course Levels

| Level | Audience | Duration | Prerequisites |
|-------|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Foundation** | Business students, entrepreneurs | 8-16 hours | Basic finance concepts |
| **Intermediate** | Finance professionals, analysts | 24-40 hours | Foundation or financial analysis |
| **Advanced** | Investment professionals, PE/VC | 40+ hours | Intermediate course |
| **Executive** | C-suite, board members | 8-16 hours | Business experience |

## Foundation Course

### Module 1: Introduction to Investment Banking (2 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain the role of investment banks
- Distinguish between IB divisions
- Understand the investment banking industry

**Topics:**
1. What is Investment Banking?
2. Investment Banks vs. Commercial Banks
3. Key Divisions: Capital Markets, Advisory, Trading
4. Major Players (Bulge Bracket, Boutiques)
5. Career Paths in Investment Banking

### Module 2: Understanding Capital Markets (2 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain equity and debt capital markets
- Understand the IPO process
- Describe bond issuance basics

**Topics:**
1. Equity Capital Markets (ECM) Overview
2. Debt Capital Markets (DCM) Overview
3. Primary vs. Secondary Markets
4. IPO Process Overview
5. Bond Issuance Overview

### Module 3: Introduction to Valuation (3 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain why valuation matters
- Understand the four core valuation methods
- Calculate enterprise value

**Topics:**
1. Why Valuation Matters
2. Intrinsic vs. Relative Valuation
3. Enterprise Value vs. Equity Value
4. Overview: DCF, Comparables, Precedent, LBO
5. When to Use Each Method

### Module 4: Basic M&A Concepts (3 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain the M&A process
- Identify types of M&A transactions
- Understand strategic rationale for M&A

**Topics:**
1. What is M&A?
2. Types of Transactions (Merger, Acquisition, Asset Deal)
3. Strategic vs. Financial Buyers
4. The M&A Process Overview
5. Synergies and Value Creation

### Module 5: Investment Decision Making (2 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Develop basic investment thesis
- Assess risk and return
- Make simple investment decisions

**Topics:**
1. What is an Investment Thesis?
2. Risk and Return Basics
3. Simple Investment Criteria
4. Case Study: Simple Investment Decision
5. Introduction to Due Diligence

## Intermediate Course

### Module 1: Investment Banking Industry Deep Dive (3 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand industry structure and dynamics
- Explain fee structures
- Analyze industry trends

**Topics:**
1. Industry Structure and Players
2. Advisory Services and Fees
3. Capital Markets Services
4. Industry Trends and Disruption
5. Regulation and Compliance

### Module 2: DCF Valuation Methodology (6 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Build a complete DCF model
- Calculate WACC
- Determine terminal value

**Topics:**
1. DCF Theory and Framework
2. Projecting Free Cash Flow
3. WACC Calculation
   - Cost of Equity (CAPM)
   - Cost of Debt
   - Target Capital Structure
4. Terminal Value Methods
5. Sensitivity Analysis
6. DCF Model Building Exercise

### Module 3: Comparable Company Analysis (4 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Select appropriate comparables
- Calculate trading multiples
- Apply multiples to derive valuation

**Topics:**
1. Selecting Comparable Companies
2. Spreading Comparables
3. Key Multiples (EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/E)
4. Applying Multiples
5. Interpreting Results
6. Comps Exercise

### Module 4: Precedent Transaction Analysis (4 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify relevant precedent transactions
- Calculate transaction multiples
- Understand control premiums

**Topics:**
1. Finding Precedent Transactions
2. Transaction Data Sources
3. Calculating Transaction Multiples
4. Control Premium Analysis
5. Applying to Target
6. Precedent Transaction Exercise

### Module 5: M&A Process and Deal Lifecycle (4 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Navigate the full M&A process
- Understand sell-side vs. buy-side
- Prepare key M&A documents

**Topics:**
1. Sell-Side M&A Process
2. Buy-Side M&A Process
3. Key Documents (Teaser, CIM, IOI, LOI)
4. Negotiation Dynamics
5. Timeline and Milestones
6. Case Study: M&A Process

### Module 6: Due Diligence Framework (4 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Conduct financial due diligence
- Identify commercial risks
- Perform operational due diligence

**Topics:**
1. Due Diligence Overview
2. Financial Due Diligence
3. Commercial Due Diligence
4. Operational Due Diligence
5. Legal Due Diligence Basics
6. Red Flags and Risk Assessment

### Module 7: Deal Structuring Basics (3 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand consideration types
- Structure payment mechanisms
- Navigate key deal terms

**Topics:**
1. Cash vs. Stock Consideration
2. Earnouts and Contingent Payments
3. Working Capital Adjustments
4. Representations and Warranties
5. Indemnification Basics

### Module 8: Investment Thesis Development (4 hours)

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Build compelling investment thesis
- Present investment recommendations
- Defend investment positions

**Topics:**
1. Investment Thesis Framework
2. Value Driver Analysis
3. Risk Assessment
4. Return Analysis
5. Presentation and Communication
6. Case Study: Investment Memo

## Assessment Methods

| Assessment | Weight | Description |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| **Quizzes** | 20% | Module-end knowledge checks |
| **Model Building** | 25% | DCF, LBO, M&A models |
| **Case Studies** | 25% | Deal analysis and recommendations |
| **Final Project** | 30% | Comprehensive investment analysis |

### Final Project Options

1. **Full DCF Valuation**: Complete DCF analysis of public company
2. **LBO Case**: PE acquisition analysis with returns
3. **M&A Case**: Buy-side or sell-side deal analysis
4. **Investment Memo**: Full investment recommendation
