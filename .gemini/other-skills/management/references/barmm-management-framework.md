# BARMM Management Framework: BDP 2023-2028 Context

## Overview

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) presents a unique management environment shaped by the 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028. This framework guides cooperative and social enterprise management within the regional context while aligning with Islamic principles, RA 9520 (Cooperative Code), and national development priorities.

---

## BARMM Strategic Vision and Development Goals

### Regional Vision
**"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."**

### Mission
*"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people."*

### Six Development Goals (2023-2028)

**Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government**
- Foundation for reliable business environment
- Managers must understand government support systems
- Compliance with BARMM administrative requirements

**Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy**
- Emphasis on MSME formalization and growth
- Competitive advantage in selected commodities
- Cooperatives/SEs as drivers of inclusive growth

**Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities**
- Security as prerequisite for operations
- Conflict sensitivity in management practices
- Community engagement protocols

**Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services**
- Access to support services for cooperatives/SEs
- Training and development availability
- Equitable benefit distribution

**Goal 5: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved**
- Islamic management principles integration
- Cultural sensitivity in operations
- Heritage-respectful business practices

**Goal 6: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**
- Infrastructure support for business operations
- Climate adaptation strategies
- Logistics and connectivity considerations

## BARMM Economic Context for Cooperative/SE Management

### Macro-Economic Targets (2023-2028)

| Indicator | Target Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| GRDP Annual Growth | 8-9% |
| GRDP Per Capita Growth | 6-9% |
| Industry & Services Growth | 10-12% |
| Inflation Rate | 2-4% |
| Unemployment Rate | 3-5% |
| Underemployment Rate | 4-7% |
| Poverty Incidence | 20-25% |

**Management Implication**: Expect increasing market opportunities and improved business environment; prepare for moderate inflationary pressures.

### MSME and Cooperative Sector Status

**Business Registration Data (BNR)**:
- 2021: 8,427 registered MSMEs (161% increase from 2020)
- Total registrations 2020-2021: 11,650 businesses
- Jobs created (2020-2021): 31,000
- Investment generated (2020-2021): 2.5 billion PHP
- Regional investment (2017-2021 MSMEs): 9.02 billion PHP
- Employment from MSMEs (2017-2021): 62,166 jobs

**Key Challenge**: BARMM accounts for only 0.92% of national BNR, indicating massive untapped formalization potential and significant room for cooperative/SE growth.

### Priority Economic Sectors

**Agriculture, Forestry & Fisheries (36.41% of GRDP, 2021)**:
- Top commodity: Seaweed (48.42% of national production)
- Second: Cassava (41.70% of national production)
- Also strong: Mangosteen, rubber, coffee, coconut, durian
- 252,758 registered fisherfolk (highest in Philippines)
- Aquaculture represents 79.89% of fisheries production

**Cooperative/SE Opportunities**:
- Cooperative management for agricultural processing
- Fisheries product cooperatives (especially seaweed value-chain)
- Agricultural input supply cooperatives
- Agricultural marketing cooperatives

**Services Sector (growing rapidly)**:
- Construction, mining, wholesale/retail trade
- Real estate and accommodation services
- Tourism (1,000-fold increase in arrivals 2020-2021 to 195,822)
- Digital services and e-commerce potential

**Trade and Industry**:
- Limited formal business sector growth
- Barter trade still dominant in some areas
- Need for business formalization support
- Islamic banking and finance expansion

## Islamic Principles in BARMM Management

### Islamic Governance Principles

**Moral Governance Pillars** (BDP 2023-2028 Framework):

1. **Integrity** - Avoid financial/personal conflicts; execute duties impartially
2. **Accountability** - Transparent decisions; document and justify actions
3. **Transparency and Honesty** - Declare interests; public accountability
4. **Justice** - Equitable treatment without prejudice or favoritism
5. **Inclusivity** - Welcome diverse participation; prevent discrimination
6. **Unwavering Loyalty to God** - Moral foundation for all decisions
7. **Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement** - Consult communities; seek input

### Sharia-Based Business Principles

**Halal Concept Extension**:
- Product purity and ethical sourcing
- Fair dealing in transactions
- Community welfare consideration
- Environmental stewardship

**Cooperative Context**:
- Riba (interest) avoidance in Islamic cooperatives
- Takaful (mutual insurance) principles
- Musharaka (partnership) profit-sharing models
- Zakat (charitable giving) integration

**Social Enterprise Context**:
- Maqasid al-Shariah (objectives): Protection of religion, intellect, life, property, family
- Waqf (endowment) partnerships for sustainable social mission
- Community investment with spiritual dimension

## Cooperative Management in BARMM Context

### RA 9520 Cooperative Code Framework

**Applicable Principles**:
- Democratic member control
- Voluntary and open membership
- Member economic participation
- Autonomy and independence
- Education and training focus
- Cooperation among cooperatives
- Community concern integration

### BARMM-Specific Cooperative Requirements

**Registration and Licensing**:
- CSEA (Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority) oversight
- Compliance with BARMM regulations
- Provincial cooperative offices
- Ongoing compliance monitoring

**Cooperative Management Structure**:
```
General Assembly (All members)
    ↓
Board of Directors (Elected representatives)
    ↓
General Manager (Professional operations)
    ↓
Department Heads/Staff
```

### Board-Manager Dynamics in BARMM Cooperatives

**Board Responsibilities**:
- Strategic direction aligned with BDP 2023-2028 goals
- Financial oversight and sustainability
- Member representation and advocacy
- BARMM compliance monitoring
- Quarterly reporting to CSEA

**Manager Responsibilities**:
- Day-to-day operations
- BARMM regulatory compliance
- Staff management and training
- Report to Board and CSEA
- Community engagement

### Management Challenges in BARMM Context

| Challenge | BARMM Factor | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Board turnover** | Limited management experience; economic pressures | Comprehensive training programs; mentoring |
| **Member apathy** | Limited education; competing livelihood pressures | Relevant engagement; tangible benefits |
| **Formalization** | Large informal sector; low literacy | Phased formalization; simplified processes |
| **Capital access** | Limited credit availability; Islamic banking emerging | Group guarantees; Islamic microfinance |
| **Market access** | Transportation challenges; geographic dispersion | Cooperative marketing networks; e-commerce |
| **Peace/security** | Conflict sensitivity required | Clear conflict protocols; community mapping |

## Social Enterprise Management in BARMM Context

### Dual Mission in BARMM Environment

**Social Impact Objectives**:
- Poverty reduction (targeting 20-25% incidence by 2028)
- Livelihood creation (31,000 jobs from MSMEs 2020-2021)
- Agricultural productivity improvement
- Tourism development for community benefit
- Youth economic empowerment

**Financial Sustainability Objectives**:
- Generate local revenue (targeting PHP 9.02B+ MSME investment)
- Achieve cost recovery
- Build organizational reserves
- Scale operations
- Demonstrate social return on investment

### BARMM Impact Metrics

**Required Outcomes Measurement**:
1. **Beneficiary Impact**: Member income changes, skill development, asset building
2. **Economic Scale**: Job creation, revenue generation, investment attraction
3. **Community Benefits**: Food security, reduced poverty, educational access
4. **Environmental Sustainability**: Climate resilience, natural resource management
5. **Social Inclusion**: Female participation, youth involvement, geographic coverage

- Local guide services
- Opportunity: 1,000-fold tourist growth potential

**Services SEs**:

## Sectoral Management Adaptations

### Agricultural Cooperative Management

**BARMM Context**:
- 628,872.64 hectares of protected agricultural areas
- 340,486 hectares under agrarian reform
- Focus on seaweed, cassava, rubber, coffee, coconut

**Management Priorities**:
1. **Productivity Improvement**: Bridging yield gap with national average
2. **Value Chain Development**: Processing and marketing
3. **Input Coordination**: Seeds, fertilizer, equipment access
4. **Market Linkages**: Buyer relationships and quality standards
5. **Climate Adaptation**: Monsoon and drought response

**Board Focus**:
- Agricultural extension coordination
- Market research and planning
- Equipment and facilities management
- Member training programs
- Supply agreements

### Fisheries Cooperative Management

**BARMM Context**:
- 252,758 registered fisherfolk
- 1,010,804.29 mt. annual production
- 79.89% from aquaculture
- Tawi-Tawi dominant in seaweed (90%+ regional production)

**Management Priorities**:
1. **Sustainable Practices**: Resource conservation
2. **Processing Infrastructure**: Post-harvest value addition
3. **Market Access**: Export quality standards
4. **Gear and Equipment**: Fishing technology upgrades
5. **Spatial Management**: Inter-commune coordination

**Board Focus**:
- Resource monitoring protocols
- Subsector coordination (aquaculture, municipal, commercial)
- Processing facility operations
- Market standards compliance
- Government liaison

### Tourism-Based SE Management

**BARMM Opportunity**:
- Tourist arrivals grew 1,000-fold (2020-2021)
- Untapped heritage and ecological tourism
- Growing investment confidence

**Management Priorities**:
1. **Community Benefit Maximization**: Local hiring and supply
2. **Cultural Heritage Protection**: Respectful operations
3. **Environmental Conservation**: Sustainability practices
4. **Market Development**: Quality and standards
5. **Capacity Building**: Skills and training

**Focus Areas**:
- Community homestay standards
- Guide training programs
- Heritage site management
- Tourist experience design
- Benefit distribution to host communities
