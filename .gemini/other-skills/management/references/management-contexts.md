## Cooperative Management

### Democratic Governance Foundation

**ICA Principle 2: Democratic Member Control**
> "Cooperatives are democratic organisations controlled by their members, who actively participate in setting their policies and making decisions."

### Unique Management Characteristics

| Aspect | Traditional Business | Cooperative |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Shareholders | Members |
| **Voting** | By shares | One member, one vote |
| **Board** | Appointed by investors | Elected by members |
| **Manager** | Reports to Board/owners | Reports to elected Board |
| **Purpose** | Profit maximization | Member benefit |
| **Surplus** | Dividends to shareholders | Patronage refund to members |

### Governance Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│        GENERAL ASSEMBLY             │ ← Highest authority
│     (All members vote equally)      │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                  │ Elects
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────┐
│        BOARD OF DIRECTORS           │ ← Policy/oversight
│     (Elected representatives)       │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                  │ Hires/supervises
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────┐
│        GENERAL MANAGER              │ ← Operations
│     (Professional management)       │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                  │ Directs
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────┐
│            STAFF                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Board-Manager Relationship

**Board Responsibilities:**
- Strategic direction and policy
- Hire and evaluate General Manager
- Financial oversight
- Member representation

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day operations
- Implement Board policies
- Staff management
- Report to Board

**Key Principle**: Board governs, Manager manages.

### Management Challenges in Cooperatives

| Challenge | Description | Mitigation |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Democratic tension** | Balancing efficiency with participation | Clear governance protocols |
| **Board turnover** | Elected boards may lack experience | Training programs |
| **Member apathy** | Low participation in decisions | Engagement initiatives |
| **Dual accountability** | Professional vs. member interests | Clear role definitions |

### POLC in Cooperatives

| Function | Cooperative Adaptation |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Planning** | Member input required; General Assembly approves major plans |
| **Organizing** | Committee structure; balance paid staff and volunteers |
| **Leading** | Servant leadership; facilitate member participation |
| **Controlling** | Accountable to members; transparent reporting |

## Social Enterprise Management

### Dual Mission Challenge

Social enterprises pursue both **social impact** and **financial sustainability** simultaneously.

```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              SOCIAL ENTERPRISE                     │
├────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────┤
│   SOCIAL MISSION   │   FINANCIAL SUSTAINABILITY   │
│   (Impact goals)   │   (Revenue goals)            │
├────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────┤
│ • Beneficiary      │ • Customers pay              │
│   outcomes         │ • Cost recovery              │
│ • Community        │ • Surplus generation         │
│   development      │ • Growth funding             │
│ • Social change    │ • Operational viability      │
└────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────┘
         ↓                      ↓
    BLENDED VALUE CREATION
```

### Unique Management Characteristics

| Aspect | Traditional Business | Social Enterprise |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Purpose** | Profit | Social impact + financial return |
| **Success metrics** | Financial KPIs | Impact + financial KPIs |
| **Stakeholders** | Shareholders, customers | Beneficiaries, funders, community |
| **Governance** | Shareholder focus | Multi-stakeholder |
| **Strategy** | Market-driven | Mission-driven |

### Mission Drift Risk

**Warning Signs:**
- Financial goals override social goals
- Original beneficiaries underserved
- Mission language disappears from decisions
- Staff focus shifts to revenue only

**Prevention Strategies:**
- Mission-centric governance
- Impact measurement systems
- Stakeholder accountability
- Board composition balance

### Governance Models

| Model | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Stewardship** | Directors as mission guardians |
| **Stakeholder** | Multiple stakeholder representation |
| **Hybrid Board** | Mix of social and business expertise |
| **Advisory Councils** | Beneficiary input mechanisms |

### POLC in Social Enterprises

| Function | SE Adaptation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Planning** | Integrate social and financial objectives |
| **Organizing** | Structure supports dual mission |
| **Leading** | Mission-driven leadership |
| **Controlling** | Measure both impact and finances |

### Impact Measurement

**Theory of Change Framework:**
```
INPUTS → ACTIVITIES → OUTPUTS → OUTCOMES → IMPACT
(Resources)  (What we do)  (Products)  (Changes)  (Long-term)
```

**IRIS+ Dimensions:**
1. **What**: Outcomes the enterprise seeks
2. **Who**: Stakeholders affected
3. **How Much**: Scale, depth, duration
4. **Contribution**: Additionality
5. **Risk**: Impact risk factors

## Public/Government Management

### Unique Characteristics

| Aspect | Private Sector | Public Sector |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Purpose** | Profit | Public service |
| **Accountability** | Shareholders | Citizens/taxpayers |
| **Authority** | Market | Legal mandate |
| **Resources** | Revenue | Taxes, appropriations |
| **Performance** | Profit/loss | Policy outcomes |
| **Stakeholders** | Customers | All citizens |

### POSDCORB Framework (Luther Gulick)

Classic public administration framework:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **P**lanning | Defining goals and methods |
| **O**rganizing | Establishing formal authority structure |
| **S**taffing | Personnel functions |
| **D**irecting | Making decisions, giving instructions |
| **CO**ordinating | Interrelating work parts |
| **R**eporting | Keeping informed through records |
| **B**udgeting | Fiscal planning and control |

### Public Sector Management Principles

**Accountability:**
- Legal accountability (rule of law)
- Political accountability (elected officials)
- Administrative accountability (internal controls)
- Public accountability (transparency)

**Bureaucratic Structures:**
- Clear hierarchy and chain of command
- Written rules and procedures
- Formal documentation
- Merit-based employment
- Standardized processes

### Challenges in Public Management

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Political influence** | Political considerations affect decisions |
| **Red tape** | Excessive rules and procedures |
| **Risk aversion** | Fear of failure/criticism |
| **Resource constraints** | Fixed budgets, limited flexibility |
| **Multiple stakeholders** | Diverse, sometimes conflicting interests |

### POLC in Government

| Function | Public Sector Adaptation |
|----------|-------------------------|
| **Planning** | Policy-driven; legislative mandates |
| **Organizing** | Formal structures; civil service rules |
| **Leading** | Within political context; public service motivation |
| **Controlling** | Strong emphasis on compliance and accountability |

## Comparative Summary

### Management Approach by Context

| Aspect | Cooperative | Social Enterprise | Government |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Primary Goal** | Member benefit | Social impact | Public service |
| **Governance** | Democratic | Multi-stakeholder | Bureaucratic |
| **Leadership** | Servant/participative | Mission-driven | Compliance-focused |
| **Decision-making** | Participatory | Balanced | Hierarchical |
| **Accountability** | To members | To mission + finances | To public |
| **Key Challenge** | Balancing democracy/efficiency | Mission drift | Rigidity |

### Theory Application by Context

| Theory | Cooperative | Social Enterprise | Government |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Taylor** | Limited (conflicts with democracy) | Selective (efficiency needs) | Applicable (standardization) |
| **Fayol** | Adapted (democratic principles) | Applicable | Highly applicable |
| **Weber** | Selective (hierarchy vs. democracy) | Selective | Highly applicable |
| **Theory Y** | Preferred (participation) | Preferred (motivation) | Mixed (structure needs) |
| **Systems** | Important (member-org interaction) | Essential (impact systems) | Important (policy systems) |
| **Contingency** | Important | Essential (dual mission) | Important |

### Management Skills by Context

| Skill | Cooperative Priority | SE Priority | Government Priority |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Technical** | Medium | Medium | High |
| **Human** | Very High | Very High | High |
| **Conceptual** | High | Very High | Medium |
