## Training Levels

### Four-Tier Framework

| Level | Target Audience | Duration | Focus |
|-------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Foundation** | New supervisors, aspiring managers | 8-16 hours | Management basics, POLC |
| **Intermediate** | First-line and middle managers | 24-40 hours | Roles, skills, theories |
| **Advanced** | Senior managers, leaders | 40+ hours | Strategic, change, culture |
| **Executive** | Top management, executives | 16-24 hours | Strategic leadership |

### Level Objectives

**Foundation:**
- Understand what management is
- Apply POLC framework basics
- Distinguish management from leadership
- Develop foundational skills

**Intermediate:**
- Master management theories
- Apply Mintzberg's roles effectively
- Develop Katz's three skills
- Make better decisions

**Advanced:**
- Lead organizational change
- Think strategically
- Build organizational culture
- Develop future leaders

**Executive:**
- Set organizational direction
- Navigate complex environments
- Build high-performance organizations
- Lead transformation

## Foundation Management Course

### Standard Duration: 8-16 hours (1-2 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. What is Management?** | Definition, nature, levels, management vs. leadership | 1-2 hours |
| **2. The POLC Framework** | Planning, Organizing, Leading, Controlling basics | 2-3 hours |
| **3. Management vs Leadership** | Differences, complementary roles, when each applies | 1 hour |
| **4. Communication Basics** | Effective communication, listening, feedback | 1-2 hours |
| **5. Time Management** | Prioritization, delegation basics, productivity | 1-2 hours |
| **6. Problem-Solving** | Identify, analyze, solve, evaluate | 1-2 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Define management and its key functions
2. Explain the POLC framework
3. Differentiate between management and leadership
4. Apply basic communication techniques
5. Prioritize tasks effectively
6. Use structured problem-solving approaches

### Delivery Methods

- Lecture/presentation
- Case discussions
- Role plays
- Group exercises
- Self-assessment tools

## Intermediate Management Course

### Standard Duration: 24-40 hours (3-5 days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Management Theories** | Classical, Behavioral, Modern approaches | 4-6 hours |
| **2. Mintzberg's Managerial Roles** | 10 roles across 3 categories, application | 3-4 hours |
| **3. Katz's Management Skills** | Technical, Human, Conceptual skills development | 3-4 hours |
| **4. Decision-Making** | Rational model, bounded rationality, decision types | 3-4 hours |
| **5. Team Leadership** | Building teams, motivation, delegation | 4-6 hours |
| **6. Performance Management** | Goal setting, monitoring, feedback, evaluation | 3-4 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Compare and contrast management theories
2. Identify and apply appropriate managerial roles
3. Develop all three management skill types
4. Apply structured decision-making processes
5. Build and lead effective teams
6. Manage performance effectively

### Module Details

**Module 1: Management Theories**
- Frederick Taylor's Scientific Management
- Henri Fayol's Administrative Principles
- Max Weber's Bureaucracy
- Elton Mayo's Human Relations
- Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs
- McGregor's Theory X/Y
- Systems and Contingency approaches

**Module 2: Mintzberg's Roles**
- Interpersonal: Figurehead, Leader, Liaison
- Informational: Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson
- Decisional: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator
- Role balance and prioritization

**Module 3: Katz's Skills**
- Technical skills assessment and development
- Human skills: communication, motivation, conflict resolution
- Conceptual skills: strategic thinking, analysis
- Skills by management level

**Module 4: Decision-Making**
- Rational decision-making model
- Herbert Simon's bounded rationality
- Satisficing vs. maximizing
- Programmed vs. non-programmed decisions
- Group decision-making

**Module 5: Team Leadership**
- Team formation stages (Tuckman)
- Motivation theories applied
- Delegation framework
- Conflict resolution
- Building team culture

**Module 6: Performance Management**
- SMART goal setting
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Performance monitoring systems
- Constructive feedback techniques
- Performance evaluation methods

## Advanced Management Course

### Standard Duration: 40+ hours (5+ days)

### Module Outline

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **1. Strategic Management** | Strategic planning, analysis, implementation | 8 hours |
| **2. Change Management** | Leading change, overcoming resistance | 6-8 hours |
| **3. Organizational Behavior** | Culture, structure, motivation | 6-8 hours |
| **4. Advanced Leadership** | Leadership styles, situational leadership | 6-8 hours |
| **5. Systems Thinking** | Organizations as systems, interdependencies | 4-6 hours |
| **6. Developing Others** | Coaching, mentoring, succession | 4-6 hours |

### Learning Objectives

By end of course, participants can:
1. Develop and implement strategic plans
2. Lead organizational change effectively
3. Shape organizational culture
4. Apply appropriate leadership styles
5. Think systemically about organizations
6. Develop future leaders

## Integration with /academy

### When to Use with /academy

Invoke `/management` with `/academy` when:
1. Creating management course/workshop
2. Developing supervisor training programs
3. Building leadership development curriculum
4. Designing manager certification programs

### Workflow

```
1. IDENTIFY training need
         ↓
2. SELECT course level (Foundation/Intermediate/Advanced/Executive)
         ↓
3. USE /management for content expertise
         ↓
4. USE /academy for curriculum design
         ↓
5. DEVELOP learning materials
         ↓
6. IMPLEMENT training
         ↓
7. EVALUATE and improve
```

### Information to Provide /academy

When invoking with `/academy`:

1. **Course Level**: Foundation, Intermediate, Advanced, Executive
2. **Target Audience**: Supervisors, managers, senior managers, executives
3. **Duration**: Hours/days available
4. **Delivery Mode**: In-person, online, hybrid
5. **Specific Topics**: If not full curriculum
6. **Context**: Cooperative, Social Enterprise, Government
7. **Assessment Requirements**: Certification needs

### Sample Request Format

```
Create a [course level] management course for [target audience]:
- Duration: [X hours/days]
- Delivery: [mode]
- Topics: [specific or full curriculum]
- Context: [Cooperative/SE/Government]
- Certification: [Yes/No + requirements]
```

### Context-Specific Curriculum Adaptations

**For Cooperatives:**
- Emphasize democratic governance
- Include Board-Manager relationship
- Add member participation elements
- Theory Y focus (participative management)

**For Social Enterprises:**
- Include mission-driven management
- Add impact measurement
- Address dual-mission balance
- Include stakeholder management

**For Government:**
- Emphasize accountability frameworks
- Include POSDCORB framework
- Add public service ethics
- Address political context management
