## Trait Theory

### Great Man Theory (Thomas Carlyle, 1840s)

**Core Premise**: Leaders are born, not made. Great leaders possess innate traits.

**Key Leader Traits Identified:**
| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Intelligence** | Cognitive ability, problem-solving |
| **Self-confidence** | Belief in own abilities |
| **Determination** | Drive, persistence, dominance |
| **Integrity** | Honesty, trustworthiness |
| **Sociability** | Interpersonal skills, friendliness |

**Strengths:**
- Intuitive appeal
- Research-backed trait correlations
- Useful for leadership selection

**Limitations:**
- No universal trait list exists
- Overlooks situational factors
- Doesn't account for learned behaviors
- Can create elitist view of leadership

## Behavioral Theory

### Core Premise
Leadership can be learned. Focus on what leaders DO, not who they ARE.

### Ohio State Studies (1940s-50s)

Two independent dimensions:
- **Initiating Structure**: Task-oriented; defining roles, organizing work
- **Consideration**: Relationship-oriented; trust, respect, rapport

**High-High Leader**: Best outcomes when high on both dimensions.

### University of Michigan Studies

Two orientations:
- **Production-Oriented**: Technical, task completion focus
- **Employee-Oriented**: Interpersonal relationships, employee needs

**Finding**: Employee-oriented leaders achieved higher productivity and satisfaction.

### Blake-Mouton Managerial Grid

| Score | Concern for People | Concern for Production | Style |
|-------|-------------------|----------------------|-------|
| 1,9 | High | Low | Country Club |
| 9,1 | Low | High | Authority-Compliance |
| 5,5 | Medium | Medium | Middle-of-Road |
| 1,1 | Low | Low | Impoverished |
| 9,9 | High | High | Team (Ideal) |

## Contingency Theories

### Core Premise
No one-size-fits-all leadership. Effectiveness depends on situation.

### Fiedler's Contingency Model (1967)

**Leader Style** (fixed): Task-motivated vs Relationship-motivated

**Situational Favorableness** (3 factors):
1. Leader-member relations (good/poor)
2. Task structure (high/low)
3. Position power (strong/weak)

**Application:**
- Task-motivated leaders: Best in very favorable OR very unfavorable situations
- Relationship-motivated leaders: Best in moderately favorable situations

### Path-Goal Theory (House, 1971)

**Leader Behaviors:**
| Behavior | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Directive** | Clear expectations, guidance | Ambiguous tasks, low-skilled |
| **Supportive** | Friendly, concern for well-being | Stressful tasks, need satisfaction |
| **Participative** | Consults, uses input | Need commitment, complex decisions |
| **Achievement-oriented** | Sets high goals, shows confidence | Challenging tasks, high-skilled |

**Key Insight**: Leaders adapt behavior based on subordinate and task characteristics.

## Modern Approaches

### Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) Theory

- Leaders form unique relationships with each follower
- **In-group**: High trust, interaction, support
- **Out-group**: Formal, limited interaction
- Goal: Develop high-quality relationships with all

### Complexity Leadership

- Leadership as emergent, distributed phenomenon
- Adaptive, enabling, administrative leadership functions
- Suited for knowledge-based, dynamic environments

### Shared/Distributed Leadership

- Leadership distributed among team members
- Multiple leaders contribute different strengths
- Common in self-managing teams, cooperatives

## Application Guidelines

### When to Apply Each Theory

| Situation | Recommended Approach |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Leadership selection | Trait theory for baseline |
| Leadership development | Behavioral theory (skills can be learned) |
| New or changing situations | Contingency/Situational theories |
| Complex, adaptive challenges | Modern approaches |
| Cooperatives, flat organizations | Shared/Distributed leadership |

### Context Considerations

**For Cooperatives:**
- Distributed leadership aligns with democratic principles
- Servant leadership (see styles) fits member-centric focus
- Avoid trait-based elitism

**For Social Enterprises:**
- Transformational for mission inspiration
- Contingency for dual-purpose balance

**For Government:**
- Path-Goal for public service motivation
- Ethical leadership paramount
