## Transformational Leadership

### Origin
James MacGregor Burns (1978) coined the term; Bernard Bass (1985) developed the model.

### The Four I's

| Component | Description | Leader Behavior |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Idealized Influence** | Role modeling | Acts as ethical role model; earns trust and respect |
| **Inspirational Motivation** | Vision articulation | Communicates compelling vision; sets high expectations |
| **Intellectual Stimulation** | Innovation encouragement | Challenges assumptions; encourages creativity |
| **Individualized Consideration** | Personal attention | Coaches, mentors; recognizes individual needs |

### When to Use
- Organizational change and transformation
- Building new culture or vision
- Inspiring followers to exceed expectations
- Crisis requiring new direction

### Limitations
- Can be seen as manipulative if not authentic
- May overlook operational details
- Requires leader charisma and vision

## Transactional Leadership

### Core Concept
Exchange-based leadership: Rewards for performance.

### Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Contingent Reward** | Clarifies expectations; rewards achievement |
| **Management-by-Exception (Active)** | Monitors for deviations; takes corrective action |
| **Management-by-Exception (Passive)** | Intervenes only when problems become serious |

### When to Use
- Stable environments with clear goals
- Routine operations requiring consistency
- Performance management systems
- Short-term task completion

### Complementary Role
Most effective when combined with transformational leadership.

## Servant Leadership

### Origin
Robert K. Greenleaf, "The Servant as Leader" (1970)

### Core Philosophy
> "The servant-leader is servant first... It begins with the natural feeling that one wants to serve, to serve first."

### 10 Characteristics (Larry Spears)

| # | Characteristic | Description |
|---|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Listening** | Receptive listening; hearing inner voice |
| 2 | **Empathy** | Understanding others; accepting them |
| 3 | **Healing** | Helping make whole; supporting recovery |
| 4 | **Awareness** | Self-awareness and situational awareness |
| 5 | **Persuasion** | Convincing rather than coercing |
| 6 | **Conceptualization** | Thinking beyond day-to-day; dreaming |
| 7 | **Foresight** | Understanding past, present, and future |
| 8 | **Stewardship** | Holding in trust for greater good |
| 9 | **Commitment to Growth** | Developing people professionally and personally |
| 10 | **Building Community** | Creating genuine community in organizations |

### Test Questions
- Do those served grow as persons?
- Do they become healthier, wiser, freer, more autonomous?
- Are they more likely to become servants themselves?
- What is the effect on the least privileged?

### Best Fit
- Cooperatives (democratic, member-centric)
- Non-profits and social enterprises
- Values-driven organizations

## Situational Leadership

### Origin
Paul Hersey & Ken Blanchard (1969)

### Core Concept
Match leadership style to follower development level.

### Four Leadership Styles

| Style | Task Behavior | Relationship Behavior | Description |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|-------------|
| **S1: Directing** | High | Low | Leader decides; provides specific instructions |
| **S2: Coaching** | High | High | Leader explains decisions; invites questions |
| **S3: Supporting** | Low | High | Leader facilitates; shares decision-making |
| **S4: Delegating** | Low | Low | Leader empowers; turns over responsibility |

### Four Development Levels

| Level | Competence | Commitment | Needs |
|-------|------------|------------|-------|
| **D1** | Low | High | Direction, close supervision |
| **D2** | Some | Low | Coaching, support, praise |
| **D3** | Moderate-High | Variable | Support, less direction |
| **D4** | High | High | Delegation, autonomy |

### Matching Guide
- D1 → S1 (Directing)
- D2 → S2 (Coaching)
- D3 → S3 (Supporting)
- D4 → S4 (Delegating)

### Four Competencies
1. **Diagnose**: Assess development level for task
2. **Adapt**: Adjust style to match
3. **Communicate**: Present approach clearly
4. **Advance**: Move toward higher performance

## Goleman's Six Leadership Styles

### Origin
Daniel Goleman, "Leadership That Gets Results" (HBR, 2000)

### The Six Styles

| Style | Approach | Climate Impact | When to Use |
|-------|----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Coercive** | "Do what I say" | Negative | Crisis, turnaround, problem employees |
| **Authoritative** | "Come with me" | Most positive | New direction needed, vision required |
| **Affiliative** | "People come first" | Positive | Building trust, healing rifts, boosting morale |
| **Democratic** | "What do you think?" | Positive | Need buy-in, fresh ideas, input |
| **Pacesetting** | "Do as I do" | Often negative | Quick results from highly skilled team |
| **Coaching** | "Try this" | Positive | Long-term development, capability building |

### Key Finding
Best leaders master **4+ styles** and switch flexibly based on situation.

### Style Details

**Coercive (Use Sparingly)**
- Demands immediate compliance
- Best in crisis or with problem employees
- Damages motivation if overused

**Authoritative/Visionary (Most Versatile)**
- States direction but allows freedom in methods
- Builds commitment to vision
- Most positive impact on climate

**Affiliative**
- Creates emotional bonds, harmony
- Builds team cohesion
- May allow poor performance to continue

**Democratic**
- Builds consensus through participation
- Generates fresh ideas
- Can lead to endless meetings

**Pacesetting (Use Carefully)**
- Sets high standards, models excellence
- Overwhelms most employees
- Only effective with highly competent teams

**Coaching**
- Develops people for future
- Requires receptive employees
- Time-intensive but high ROI

## Authentic Leadership

### Origin
Bill George (2003); Avolio & Gardner research

### Four Dimensions

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Self-awareness** | Knowing strengths, weaknesses, values, motives |
| **Balanced processing** | Objectively analyzing data; considering all perspectives |
| **Internalized moral perspective** | Self-regulated by internal values, not external pressures |
| **Relational transparency** | Genuine, open relationships; sharing true self |

### Development
- Life experiences shape authentic leadership
- Trigger events create self-awareness
- Continuous self-reflection and growth

## Adaptive Leadership

### Origin
Ronald Heifetz, Harvard Kennedy School

### Technical vs Adaptive Challenges

| Technical | Adaptive |
|-----------|----------|
| Problem defined clearly | Problem requires discovery |
| Solution known | No known solution |
| Expert can solve | Requires collective learning |
| Implementation by authority | Requires stakeholder change |
| Addresses symptoms | Addresses root causes |

### Key Principles

1. **Get on the balcony**: Step back to see patterns
2. **Identify adaptive challenge**: Distinguish from technical
3. **Regulate distress**: Maintain productive tension
4. **Maintain disciplined attention**: Keep focus on hard work
5. **Give work back to people**: Don't rescue too quickly
6. **Protect voices of leadership**: Value diverse perspectives

### Dance Floor/Balcony Metaphor
Leaders alternate between:
- **Dance floor**: In the action, doing the work
- **Balcony**: Observing patterns, seeing the system

## Style Selection Guide

### By Context

| Context | Recommended Styles |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Cooperatives** | Servant, Democratic, Coaching |
| **Social Enterprises** | Transformational, Servant, Authentic |
| **Government** | Adaptive, Democratic, Authoritative |
| **Crisis** | Coercive (short-term), Adaptive |
| **Change/Transformation** | Transformational, Authoritative, Adaptive |
| **Development Focus** | Coaching, Servant, Situational |
| **Stable Operations** | Transactional, Situational |

### By Follower Needs

| Need | Style |
|------|-------|
| Clear direction | Directive, Coercive |
| Skill development | Coaching, Servant |
| Autonomy | Delegating, Democratic |
| Motivation | Transformational, Affiliative |
| Complex problem | Adaptive, Democratic |
