## Video Duration Guidelines

Based on research from MIT/edX (6.9M video sessions), cognitive load theory, adult learning principles, and 2024-2025 validation studies. See [video-duration-methodology.md](../../video-duration-methodology.md) for full research (last validated: December 2025).

| Duration | Purpose | Content Type | Word Count (~90 wpm) |
|----------|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| **3 min** | Quick definitions | Single term/concept introduction | ~270 words |
| **5 min** | Single concepts | One idea with brief explanation | ~450 words |
| **7 min** | Concepts + examples | Idea with 2-3 practical examples | ~630 words |
| **10 min** | Processes/workflows | Step-by-step procedures | ~900 words |
| **12 min** | Complex topics | Multi-part concepts with practice | ~1,080 words |
| **15 min** | Comprehensive | Full topic coverage with application | ~1,350 words |

**Unified ~90 WPM**: MoroAcademy uses **80-100 wpm (target ~90 wpm)** for both Filipino and English content. This slower pace is designed for BARMM's ESL beginner learners (English as L3/L4, rural conflict-affected areas, varied educational backgrounds).

**Key Research Findings (Validated 2024-2025):**
- **6 minutes is optimal** for engagement (MIT/edX study, 6.9M sessions)
- **9% higher performance** with short videos vs long-form (2024 study)
- **25-60% retention improvement** with segmented microlearning
- **Segmenting effect size: 0.79-0.98** (strong evidence for chunking)
- Break complex topics into multiple shorter videos
- One concept per video for beginners
- Include 2-3 examples in 7-minute videos

## Script Requirements

### Video Scripts

Each video lesson requires:
1. **English Script** - Primary language for formal terms
2. **Filipino Script** - Localized version for accessibility
3. **Timing Markers** - Scene/slide change indicators
4. **Visual Cues** - On-screen text and graphics notes

### Script Pacing (~90 WPM for All Languages)

All scripts (Filipino or English) use **~90 wpm** to accommodate BARMM's ESL beginner learners. Additional pacing considerations:
- Longer words with affixes (mag-, nag-, -in, -an) in Filipino
- Technical term explanation pairs (English term + Filipino meaning)
- Natural code-switching (Taglish) pauses
- Clearer enunciation for educational content

**Technical Term Pattern**: `"[English term], o [Filipino explanation]"`
- Example: "Ang **target market**, o mga gustong maging customer, ay..."
- Example: "Dapat nating alamin ang **value proposition**, kung ano ang halaga na iniaalok natin..."

**Timing Buffers**:
- Add +15 seconds per technical definition
- Add +20 seconds for example explanations
- Total buffer: 15-20% of estimated duration

### Key Marketing History Facts

**Philip Kotler's Contributions (1931-present):**
- Published *Marketing Management* in 1967, now in 15th edition
- Transformed marketing from peripheral activity to central business function
- Shifted emphasis from price/distribution to meeting customer needs
- Broadened marketing to non-commercial organizations (charities, museums, political parties)
- PhD from MIT (1956), studied under three Nobel Laureates

**E. Jerome McCarthy and the 4Ps (1928-2015):**
- Proposed 4Ps in *Basic Marketing: A Managerial Approach* (1960)
- Synthesized Neil Borden's complex "marketing mix" into practical framework
- Product, Price, Place, Promotion became universal marketing foundation
- Received AMA Trailblazer Award in 1987

**Digital Marketing Evolution:**
- 1969: ARPANET established (foundation of internet)
- 1997: Six Degrees - first social media site (Andrew Weinreich)
- 1998: Google founded, revolutionizing search
- 2000: Google AdWords launches (pay-per-click advertising)
- 2002: Friendster founded (early social network)
- 2004: Facebook founded by Mark Zuckerberg
- 2007: Facebook launches advertising platform
- 2010s: Data-driven marketing, influencer marketing, social commerce
- 2026: Social commerce projected to reach $2.9 trillion
