Practical accounting fundamentals for cooperative and SE officers without accounting background.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Record** basic business transactions using double-entry bookkeeping
2. **Prepare** simple journal entries
3. **Post** to ledger accounts
4. **Prepare** a trial balance
5. **Understand** the accounting cycle

## Video Duration Guidelines

Based on research from MIT/edX (6.9M video sessions), cognitive load theory, adult learning principles, and 2024-2025 validation studies. See [video-duration-methodology.md](../../video-duration-methodology.md) for full research (last validated: December 2025).

| Duration | Purpose | Content Type | Word Count (~90 wpm) |
|----------|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| **3 min** | Quick definitions | Single term/concept introduction | ~270 words |
| **5 min** | Single concepts | One idea with brief explanation | ~450 words |
| **7 min** | Concepts + examples | Idea with 2-3 practical examples | ~630 words |
| **10 min** | Processes/workflows | Step-by-step procedures | ~900 words |
| **12 min** | Complex topics | Multi-part concepts with practice | ~1,080 words |
| **15 min** | Comprehensive | Full topic coverage with application | ~1,350 words |

**Unified ~90 WPM**: MoroAcademy uses **80-100 wpm (target ~90 wpm)** for both Filipino and English content. This slower pace is designed for BARMM's ESL beginner learners (English as L3/L4, rural conflict-affected areas, varied educational backgrounds).

**Key Research Findings (Validated 2024-2025):**
- **6 minutes is optimal** for engagement (MIT/edX study, 6.9M sessions)
- **9% higher performance** with short videos vs long-form (2024 study)
- **25-60% retention improvement** with segmented microlearning
- **Segmenting effect size: 0.79-0.98** (strong evidence for chunking)
- Break complex topics into multiple shorter videos
- One concept per video for beginners
- Include 2-3 examples in 7-minute videos

## Script Requirements

### Video Scripts

Each video lesson requires:
1. **English Script** - Primary language for formal terms
2. **Filipino Script** - Localized version for accessibility
3. **Timing Markers** - Scene/slide change indicators
4. **Visual Cues** - On-screen text and graphics notes

### Script Pacing (~90 WPM for All Languages)

All scripts (Filipino or English) use **~90 wpm** to accommodate BARMM's ESL beginner learners. Additional pacing considerations:
- Longer words with affixes (mag-, nag-, -in, -an) in Filipino
- Technical term explanation pairs (English term + Filipino meaning)
- Natural code-switching (Taglish) pauses
- Clearer enunciation for educational content

**Technical Term Pattern**: `"[English term], o [Filipino explanation]"`
- Example: "Ang **debit**, o pagpasok ng halaga sa kaliwa, ay..."
- Example: "I-record natin ito sa **journal**, ang aklat ng mga transaksyon..."

**Timing Buffers**:
- Add +15 seconds per technical definition
- Add +20 seconds for calculation walkthroughs
- Total buffer: 15-20% of estimated duration

## Learning Tips

### For Non-Accountants
- Don't memorize - understand the logic
- Practice with real transactions
- Use T-accounts to visualize
- Start simple, build complexity
- Ask questions - no shame in not knowing

### Common Struggles
- Debit/credit confusion: Remember DEAD CLIC
  - **D**ebits: **E**xpenses, **A**ssets, **D**rawings increase
  - **C**redits: **L**iabilities, **I**ncome, **C**apital increase
- Finding errors: Check one entry at a time
- Overwhelm: Take it slow, one concept at a time

### Key Historical Facts

**Luca Pacioli (1445-1517)**:
- Franciscan friar and Renaissance mathematician
- Published *Summa de Arithmetica* in 1494 (Venice)
- First to systematically describe double-entry bookkeeping
- Collaborated with Leonardo da Vinci
- Known as the "Father of Accounting"

**Double-Entry Bookkeeping Origins**:
- Earliest records: Amatino Manucci (Florence, 1299-1300)
- Developed by Italian merchants before Pacioli
- Pacioli codified and published the system
- Benedetto Cotrugli described it 36 years before Pacioli (unpublished)

### The Accounting Equation Foundation

**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

This fundamental equation, first codified by Pacioli, remains the foundation of all modern accounting. His principles established:
- **Consistency** - Apply methods consistently across periods
- **Materiality** - Not all information is equally important
- **The accounting cycle** - Still followed today, 500+ years later

### Double-Entry Principle

> "For every debit, there must be an equal and opposite credit."

This ensures the accounting equation always balances and provides built-in error detection.

| **IFRS** | International Financial Reporting Standards (Global) |
| **CDA Standards** | Philippine cooperative accounting standards |
