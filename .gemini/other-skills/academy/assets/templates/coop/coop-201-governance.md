# COOP-201: Governance and Management of Cooperatives

CDA/CSEA-mandated governance training for cooperative officers and managers.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | COOP-201 |
| **Type** | Online Course (Self-Paced) |
| **Duration** | 8 hours (Micro Coops) / 16 hours (Larger Coops) |
| **Target Audience** | Board of Directors, Committee Officers, CEO/GM |
| **Prerequisites** | COOP-101: Fundamentals of Cooperatives |
| **Compliance** | CDA MC 2015-09 Mandatory (adopted by CSEA for BARMM) |
| **Certificate** | Yes (80% completion + 70% assessment) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Apply** principles of good governance in cooperative management
2. **Distinguish** roles and responsibilities of BOD vs Management
3. **Implement** ethical standards and conflict of interest policies
4. **Design** performance measurement systems
5. **Apply** basic financial, HR, and marketing management concepts

## Script Requirements

Each video lesson requires dual-language scripts:

1. **English Script** - Primary language for formal/technical terms
2. **Filipino Script** - Localized version for accessibility
3. **Timing Markers** - Scene/slide change indicators
4. **Visual Cues** - On-screen text and graphics notes

### Script Pacing (~90 WPM for All Languages)

All scripts (Filipino or English) use **~90 wpm** to accommodate BARMM's ESL beginner learners. Additional pacing considerations:
- Longer words with affixes (mag-, nag-, -in, -an) in Filipino
- Technical term explanation pairs (English term + Filipino meaning)
- Natural code-switching (Taglish) pauses
- Clearer enunciation for educational content

**Technical Term Pattern**: `"[English term], o [Filipino explanation]"`
- Example: "Ang **governance**, o pamamahala ng kooperatiba, ay..."
- Example: "Dapat sundin ang **fiduciary duty**, ang responsibilidad bilang pinagkakatiwalaan..."

**Timing Buffers**:
- Add +15 seconds per technical definition
- Add +20 seconds for example explanations
- Total buffer: 15-20% of estimated duration

### Governance Best Practices

**Fiduciary Duties of Cooperative Board Members:**
- **Duty of Care**: Act with reasonable care, make informed decisions, attend meetings, ask questions
- **Duty of Loyalty**: Put the cooperative's interests first; avoid conflicts of interest
- **Duty of Obedience**: Follow laws, bylaws, and cooperative policies
- **Business Judgment Rule**: Protects directors who act in good faith within their authority

**Key Governance Principle**: A director has a fiduciary obligation to the cooperative and its members as a whole, rather than a duty to a constituency or group of members.
