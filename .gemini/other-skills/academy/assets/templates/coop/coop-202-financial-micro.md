## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Interpret** cooperative financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow)
2. **Calculate** and interpret basic financial ratios
3. **Explain** the statutory fund allocation process under RA 9520
4. **Prepare** a simple cooperative budget
5. **Identify** basic internal control principles
6. **Assess** basic financial health indicators of a cooperative

  - Non-current assets (property, equipment)

- Revenue/income sources

**Lesson 1.4: The Cash Flow Statement** (VIDEO - 10 min)

- Operating activities
- Investing activities

### Unit 2: Financial Ratio Analysis (2 hours)

> **MC 2025-09 Topic**: Financial Management - Ratio Analysis

**Lesson 2.1: Introduction to Ratio Analysis** (VIDEO - 10 min)

- What are financial ratios?
- Why ratios matter for decision-making
- Categories of ratios
- Limitations of ratio analysis
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.2: Liquidity and Solvency Ratios** (VIDEO - 15 min)

- **Current Ratio**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  - Measures short-term ability to pay
  - Target: 1.5 to 2.0
- **Quick Ratio**: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
  - More conservative liquidity measure
- **Debt-to-Equity Ratio**: Total Liabilities / Member Equity
  - Measures financial leverage
- Worked examples with cooperative data
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.3: Profitability and Efficiency Ratios** (VIDEO - 12 min)

- **Net Surplus Margin**: Net Surplus / Total Revenue
  - Measures operational efficiency
- **Return on Assets (ROA)**: Net Surplus / Total Assets
  - Asset utilization effectiveness
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: Net Surplus / Member Equity
  - Return to member investment
- Cooperative-specific interpretations
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 2.4: Ratio Analysis Practice** (TEXT - 15 min)

- Sample cooperative ratios calculation
- Benchmarking against standards
- Trend analysis basics
- Action items from ratio analysis
- Practice problems with solutions
- **Reading**: English + Filipino versions

**Quiz 2: Ratio Analysis** (5 questions)

### Unit 3: Statutory Funds and Surplus Allocation (2 hours)

> **MC 2025-09 Topic**: Financial Management - Net Surplus Allocation

**Lesson 3.1: Understanding Net Surplus** (VIDEO - 12 min)

- What is net surplus in a cooperative?
- Difference from corporate profit
- Gross vs net surplus
- Sources of surplus (operations, investments)
- Pre-allocation deductions
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 3.2: Statutory Fund Requirements (RA 9520 Art. 86)** (VIDEO - 15 min)

- **Reserve Fund**: 10% minimum (50% first 5 years)
  - Purpose: Cover losses, stability cushion
  - Cannot be distributed
- **Education & Training Fund (ETF)**: Up to 10%
  - Purpose: Member/officer training, cooperative education
  - Must be used for training activities
- **Community Development Fund (CDF)**: 3% minimum
  - Purpose: Community benefit projects
  - 7th Cooperative Principle implementation
- **Optional Fund**: Up to 7%
  - Purpose: Land, building, equipment
  - Based on bylaws
- Fund computation examples
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 3.3: Patronage Refund and Interest on Capital** (VIDEO - 12 min)

- Patronage refund concept and calculation
  - Based on member transactions
  - At least 30% of allocable surplus
- Interest on share capital
  - Limited rate (not exceeding market rate)
  - Based on average capital during period
- Distribution timeline and methods
- Member communication
- **Script**: English + Filipino

**Lesson 3.4: Surplus Allocation Process** (TEXT - 12 min)

- Step-by-step allocation procedure
- GA approval requirements
- Documentation and reporting
- Sample surplus allocation statement
- Common mistakes to avoid
- **Reading**: English + Filipino versions

**Quiz 3: Statutory Funds** (5 questions)

## Appendix: Financial Terms Glossary

| Term | Filipino | Definition |
|------|----------|------------|
| Balance Sheet | Talaan ng Kalagayang Pinansyal | Statement of assets, liabilities, and equity |
| Income Statement | Talaan ng Operasyon | Statement of revenues and expenses |
| Cash Flow | Daloy ng Pera | Movement of cash in and out |
| Net Surplus | Netong Sobra | Profit after all expenses |
| Reserve Fund | Pondo ng Reserba | Mandatory savings for stability |
| ETF | Pondo ng Edukasyon | Fund for training activities |
| CDF | Pondo ng Komunidad | Fund for community projects |
| Patronage Refund | Balik-patronage | Member share of surplus based on transactions |
| Current Ratio | Ratio ng Kasalukuyang Assets | Liquidity measure |
| Budget | Badyet | Financial plan for income and expenses |

## Appendix: Sample Ratio Benchmarks for Micro Cooperatives

| Ratio | Formula | Target Range |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 - 2.5 |
| Quick Ratio | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | 1.0 - 1.5 |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Liabilities / Member Equity | < 2.0 |
| Net Surplus Margin | Net Surplus / Total Revenue | > 5% |
| ROA | Net Surplus / Total Assets | > 3% |
| ROE | Net Surplus / Member Equity | > 8% |

*Note: Benchmarks vary by cooperative type and local conditions.*
