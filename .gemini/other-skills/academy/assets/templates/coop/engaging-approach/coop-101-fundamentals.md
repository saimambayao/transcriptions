# COOP-101: Fundamentals of Cooperatives

## Engaging Approach Design

Foundation course on cooperative history, principles, values, and identity—designed using the ENGAGE methodology for maximum learner engagement and retention.

> **Series**: COOP-100 (Basic Cooperative Course for Members)
> **Regulatory Basis**: CDA MC 2025-09 Annex A (adopted by CSEA for BARMM)
> **Methodology**: ENGAGE Framework (Experience-first, Narrative-driven, Active Learning)

## Design Philosophy

This course applies the **ENGAGE Framework**:

| Letter | Principle | How Applied |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **E** | Experience First | Each unit opens with a scenario before explanation |
| **N** | Narrative Thread | Follow "MoroCoop" story throughout |
| **G** | Guided Practice | Watch-Pause-Try pattern in every lesson |
| **A** | Active Processing | Activity every 5-7 minutes |
| **G** | Group Learning | Discussion forums, peer reflection |
| **E** | Extend Beyond | Apply to your own cooperative at each unit's end |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Describe** the historical origins of the cooperative movement
2. **Define** what a cooperative is according to ICA and RA 9520
3. **List** and **explain** the 7 International Cooperative Principles
4. **Identify** the 6 cooperative values and their ethical foundations
5. **Distinguish** cooperatives from corporations and NGOs
6. **Recognize** the role of cooperatives in BARMM development
7. **Apply** cooperative principles to their own cooperative situation

# Know Your Cooperative Type

## By Level

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Primary** | Individual members | MoroCoop (85 farmer-members) |
| **Secondary** | Federation of cooperatives | BARMM Farmers Federation |
| **Tertiary** | National union | National Cooperative Union |

## By Function

### Credit Cooperative
- Primary service: Savings and loans
- Members save together, borrow from shared pool
- Example: Teachers' credit cooperative

### Consumer Cooperative
- Primary service: Retail goods at fair prices
- Members buy together for better deals
- Example: Community store cooperative

### Producer Cooperative
- Primary service: Collective production
- Members produce together, share resources
- Example: Farmers pooling equipment

### Marketing Cooperative
- Primary service: Collective selling
- Members market products together
- Example: Mango growers' marketing coop

### Service Cooperative
- Primary service: Shared services
- Members access services affordably
- Example: Transport cooperative

### Multi-Purpose Cooperative
- Multiple services combined
- MoroCoop is this type! (Agriculture + Savings)

## Appendix: Traditional vs Engaging Approach

| Aspect | Traditional (Old) | Engaging (New) |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Opening** | "Today we'll learn about..." | Scenario with relatable problem |
| **Content** | Lecture-style explanation | Story-driven with characters |
| **Videos** | Up to 15 min lectures | 5-7 min segments with variety |
| **Activities** | End-of-unit quizzes only | Activities every 5-7 minutes |
| **Reflection** | None | Pause prompts, journaling |
| **Application** | Theoretical | Apply to YOUR cooperative |
| **Assessment** | Factual recall | Scenario-based application |
| **Engagement** | Passive watching | Active participation |
| **Social** | Individual only | Forums, peer review, cohorts |
| **Output** | Certificate only | Commitment card, downloads |
