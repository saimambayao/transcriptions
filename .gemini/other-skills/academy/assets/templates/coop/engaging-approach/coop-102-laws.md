## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Explain** the purpose and scope of RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code)
2. **Identify** member rights under RA 9520 and how to exercise them
3. **Describe** member responsibilities and duties to the cooperative
4. **Outline** the basic governance structure of cooperatives (GA, BOD, Committees)
5. **Explain** the role of CDA/CSEA as regulatory authorities
6. **Summarize** RA 11364 (CDA Charter) provisions relevant to members
7. **Describe** RA 10744 (Credit Surety Fund) and its benefits to cooperatives

**Lesson 1.9: Share Capital and Your Investment** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Maria the Treasurer asks: 'Members often ask me about their share capital.
Let me explain what the law says about YOUR money.'"

CONTENT (6 min):
SHARE CAPITAL UNDER RA 9520 (Article 53-55):

WHAT IS SHARE CAPITAL?
- Your ownership stake in the cooperative
- Not a savings deposit - it's an INVESTMENT
- Required for membership

MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS:
- Varies by cooperative type and size
- Your bylaws specify the amount
- Can be paid in installments

SHARE CAPITAL vs. SAVINGS:
|                    | Share Capital    | Savings          |
|--------------------|------------------|------------------|
| Nature             | Investment       | Deposit          |
| Return             | Limited interest | Higher interest  |
| Withdrawal         | Restricted       | More flexible    |
| Risk               | Higher           | Lower            |
| Voting Rights      | Yes              | No               |

WITHDRAWAL (Art. 55):
- Generally, cannot withdraw while a member
- Exceptions: Expulsion, death, resignation
- May be restricted to protect coop stability

BRIDGE (30 sec):
```

```text
NET SURPLUS ALLOCATION UNDER ARTICLE 86:

STEP 1: Calculate Net Surplus
Revenue minus all expenses = Net Surplus

STEP 2: Mandatory Allocations FIRST
Before any distribution to members, you MUST set aside:

| Fund | Minimum Rate | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Reserve Fund | 10% | Cover losses, stability |
| Education & Training Fund (ETF) | Up to 10% | Member training (like THIS course!) |
| Community Development Fund (CDF) | 3% | Community projects |
| Optional Fund | Up to 7% | Land, building, other |

EXAMPLE WITH MoroCoop COOP:

Reserve Fund (10%):          PHP 50,000
ETF (10%):                   PHP 50,000
CDF (3%):                    PHP 15,000
Optional Fund (7%):          PHP 35,000
-----------------------------------------
Total Mandatory Allocations: PHP 150,000

Remaining for Distribution:  PHP 350,000

STEP 3: Distribute the Remainder
- Interest on Share Capital (limited)
- Patronage Refund (based on transactions)

WHY PATRONAGE REFUND IS SPECIAL:
"Ang patronage refund ay batay sa iyong TRANSAKSYON, hindi sa iyong SHARES.
```

```markdown
## Tax Exemptions Under RA 9520 (Article 61)

### What Cooperatives Are Exempt From:

1. **Income Tax** on member transactions

3. **Documentary Stamp Tax**
4. **Donor's Tax** on donations received

- File annual reports with CDA/CSEA

- Operate according to cooperative principles

1. **Preferential treatment** in government procurement
2. **Reduced court fees** in legal cases
3. **Government assistance** priority

### Why This Matters to You:

Because your cooperative doesn't pay these taxes:
- More surplus to distribute as patronage refund
- Lower fees for services
- Better rates on loans

### MoroCoop's Realization:
```

```text
4. DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS

   - Technical assistance
   - Market linkage support

FOR BARMM/CSEA:
CSEA exercises these same powers for cooperatives in BARMM,
adapted to our regional context.
```

**Lesson 3.2: What is the Credit Surety Fund?** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Imagine a group of cooperatives pooling money together to guarantee each other's loans.
That's the Credit Surety Fund."

CONTENT (6 min):
RA 10744 - CREDIT SURETY FUND ACT OF 2015:

WHAT IS CSF?
"Credit Surety Fund, o CSF, ay isang pooled guarantee fund.
Maraming kooperatiba ang naglalagak ng pera para maging garantiya
ng loan ng bawat isa."

HOW IT WORKS:

Step 1: Cooperative joins a CSF Cooperative
- Contributes to the surety fund
- Becomes a member of the guarantee pool

Step 2: Cooperative needs a loan
- Applies to partner bank (Land Bank, DBP, etc.)
- CSF provides the collateral/guarantee

Step 3: Bank approves the loan
- Lower interest rate (because risk is lower)
- No need for traditional collateral

VISUAL:
+-------------+        +-------------+        +-------------+
| MoroCoop COOP    |  --->  | CSF COOP    |  --->  | LAND BANK   |
| Needs loan  |        | Guarantees  |        | Approves    |
|             |        | the loan    |        | the loan    |
+-------------+        +-------------+        +-------------+

WHO BENEFITS:
- Cooperatives lacking collateral
- MSMEs (Micro, Small, Medium Enterprises)
- Farmers' organizations
```

**Lesson 3.4: How CSF Benefits Your Cooperative** (TEXT - 10 min)

```markdown
# Credit Surety Fund: A Member's Guide

Maria reports to the BOD about CSF and how it can help MoroCoop's expansion.

## Benefits to Cooperatives

### 1. Access to Credit Without Traditional Collateral
- No need for land titles or building deeds
- The CSF acts as your collateral
- Removes the biggest barrier to borrowing

### 2. Lower Interest Rates
- Banks see CSF guarantee as lower risk
- Risk means lower rates
- Savings on interest = more for members

### 3. Building Credit History
- Successful CSF loans build your track record
- Future loans become easier
- Graduate to higher loan amounts

### 4. Partner Banks
CSF works with:
- Land Bank of the Philippines
- Development Bank of the Philippines (DBP)
- Other partner financial institutions

## Requirements to Access CSF

### Step 1: Join a CSF Cooperative
- Must be a registered, compliant cooperative
- Pay membership contribution
- Meet CSF cooperative requirements

### Step 2: Contribute to the Fund
- Contribution based on your desired credit line
- Typically: Contribution = 10-20% of desired credit
- Example: PHP 200,000 contribution for PHP 1,000,000 credit line

### Step 3: Apply for Credit Line
- Submit loan application
- Undergo credit evaluation
- CSF approves guarantee

### Step 4: Access Loans
- Apply to partner bank
- CSF guarantee covers collateral requirement
- Bank releases funds

## CSF in BARMM Context

CSEA is working to expand CSF access in BARMM:
- Promoting CSF cooperative formation
- Linking with Land Bank Mindanao branches
- Supporting agricultural cooperatives

## MoroCoop's Decision

After learning about CSF, the MoroCoop BOD decides:
1. Join the provincial CSF cooperative
2. Contribute PHP 200,000 from reserves
3. Apply for PHP 2,000,000 credit line
4. Access funding for their rice processing facility

**The Result**: MoroCoop's expansion dream becomes possible!
```
