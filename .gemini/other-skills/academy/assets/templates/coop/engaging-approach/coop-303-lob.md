**Lesson 1.2: Why Coops Diversify** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Multi-purpose cooperatives serve members better—
 but complexity creates challenges."

CONTENT (6 min):
REASONS COOPERATIVES ADD BUSINESS LINES:

1. MEMBER NEEDS
   - Members need more than one service
   - MoroCoop: Farmers need credit AND supplies AND markets
   - One-stop-shop convenience

2. REVENUE DIVERSIFICATION
   - Don't depend on one income source
   - If credit slows, store or marketing may grow
   - Stabilizes overall performance

3. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE
   - Serve members others can't
   - Leverage existing relationships
   - Build member loyalty

4. ECONOMIES OF SCALE
   - Share fixed costs across businesses
   - Same office, same staff, multiple functions
   - Lower cost per transaction

---

BUT DIVERSIFICATION HAS RISKS:

1. MANAGEMENT COMPLEXITY
   - Each business needs different skills
   - Can't be expert in everything
   - Risk of neglecting core business

2. RESOURCE DILUTION
   - Capital spread thin
   - Staff attention divided
   - Quality may suffer

3. HIDDEN LOSSES
   - Profitable business subsidizes losing one
   - Hard to measure true performance
   - May continue bad business too long

VISUAL: Balance scale showing benefits vs risks

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"The key is STRATEGIC FIT. Let's learn how to evaluate it..."

PAUSE PROMPT: "Why did your cooperative add its business lines?"
```

**Lesson 1.4: Strategic Fit Analysis** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Not every business makes sense for every cooperative.
 How do you know if a business line FITS?"

CONTENT (6 min):
STRATEGIC FIT FRAMEWORK:

Ask these 5 questions for EACH business line:

1. MEMBER ALIGNMENT
   "Does this serve our members' actual needs?"
   - Who uses this service?
   - What problem does it solve for them?
   - MoroCoop Store: 150 members use it monthly = member need exists

2. MISSION ALIGNMENT
   "Does this fit our cooperative's purpose?"
   - Is it in our Articles of Cooperation?
   - Does it support our core business?
   - MoroCoop Store: Supports farmers = fits mission

3. CAPABILITY FIT
   "Do we have the skills to run this well?"
   - Do we have expertise?
   - Can we compete effectively?
   - MoroCoop Store: Weak retail skills = concern

4. RESOURCE REQUIREMENT
   "Can we afford to do this properly?"
   - Capital needed
   - Staff needed
   - Management attention needed
   - MoroCoop Store: P200,000 inventory tie-up = significant

5. SYNERGY POTENTIAL
   "Does this strengthen other business lines?"
   - Does it bring in members?
   - Does it reduce costs elsewhere?
   - Does it increase loyalty?
   - MoroCoop Store: Brings 150 members to credit office = HIGH synergy!

---

SCORING SYSTEM:
Rate each question 1-5
Add up scores (max 25)

| Score   | Recommendation              |
|---------|------------------------------|
| 20-25   | Strong fit, invest           |
| 15-19   | Moderate fit, maintain       |
| 10-14   | Weak fit, review carefully   |
| Below 10| Poor fit, consider exiting   |

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Let's score MoroCoop's consumer store..."
```

**Lesson 2.2: Principles of Resource Allocation** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"Every peso should work for the cooperative's goals.
 Here's how to make sure it does."

CONTENT (6 min):
RESOURCE ALLOCATION PRINCIPLES:

PRINCIPLE 1: STRATEGY FIRST
- Allocate based on strategic priorities
- Core business gets priority
- Growth investments need clear returns
- MoroCoop Priority: Credit > Processing > Marketing > Store

PRINCIPLE 2: MEMBER BENEFIT
- Which investment benefits most members?
- Which improves member services most?
- MoroCoop: Credit expansion serves all 450 members

PRINCIPLE 3: RETURN ON INVESTMENT
- What return does each peso generate?
- Compare opportunities systematically
- Example: P100,000 to Credit = P15,000 annual income
- Example: P100,000 to Store = P5,000 loss improvement

PRINCIPLE 4: RISK CONSIDERATION
- Higher return often = higher risk
- Balance proven businesses and new ventures
- Don't bet everything on one project

PRINCIPLE 5: MINIMUM VIABLE INVESTMENT
- Some things need minimum investment to work
- Half-funded projects often fail completely
- Better to fully fund fewer priorities

PRINCIPLE 6: MANDATORY ALLOCATIONS
- Some allocations are required (ETF, CDF)
- Compliance costs are non-negotiable
- Include these BEFORE discretionary allocation

VISUAL: Funnel showing allocation process

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Let's build an allocation framework for MoroCoop..."

PAUSE PROMPT: "What's your cooperative's highest strategic priority?"
```

CAPITAL INVESTMENT DECISION TOOLS:

TOOL 1: PAYBACK PERIOD
"How long until the investment pays for itself?"

Example: Cold storage for marketing
- Investment: P150,000
- Annual savings/revenue: P50,000/year
- Payback: 150,000 / 50,000 = 3 years

Target: Generally prefer payback under 5 years

---

TOOL 2: RETURN ON INVESTMENT (ROI)
"What return does each peso generate?"

ROI = (Gain - Cost) / Cost x 100

Example: Credit expansion
- Investment: P200,000
- Annual return: P30,000
- ROI = 30,000 / 200,000 x 100 = 15%

Compare projects: Higher ROI = better investment

---

TOOL 3: STRATEGIC VALUE SCORE
(When returns are hard to measure)

Criteria (1-5 each):
- Member benefit
- Strategic alignment
- Risk level (inverse)
- Synergy with other lines

Weight by importance and calculate total score

---

|-------------------|------------|-------|---------|----------|----------|

| Cold storage      | 150K       | 10%   | 3 yrs   | Medium   | DEFER    |
| Store inventory   | 100K       | 5%    | 5 yrs   | Low      | PARTIAL  |
