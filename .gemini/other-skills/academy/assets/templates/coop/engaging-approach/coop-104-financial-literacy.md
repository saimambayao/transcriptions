## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. **Apply** basic budgeting principles to personal finances
2. **Explain** how cooperative financial services work (savings, loans, patronage)
3. **Calculate** simple interest and understand loan repayment
4. **Demonstrate** safe use of digital financial tools
5. **Identify** common financial scams and how to avoid them
6. **Manage** personal finances responsibly as a cooperative member

**Lesson 2.2: Understanding Cooperative Financial Products** (VIDEO - 7 min)

```text
HOOK (30 sec):
"When you joined the cooperative, you paid 'share capital.'
But do you know what it really means?"

CONTENT (6 min):
THE 4 FINANCIAL PRODUCTS:

1. SHARE CAPITAL (Puhunan)
   "Ito ang iyong OWNERSHIP sa cooperative."

   - Required to become a member
   - Usually PHP 500 - PHP 10,000 (depending on coop)
   - Not the same as savings!
   - Cannot easily withdraw while member
   - Earns LIMITED interest (not guaranteed)
   - Gives you VOTING RIGHTS

   Ibrahim's share capital: PHP 5,000

2. SAVINGS DEPOSITS (Ipon)
   "Ito ang iyong pera na pwede mong i-withdraw."

   - Voluntary - save as much as you want
   - Earns interest (higher than banks usually)
   - More flexible withdrawal
   - No voting rights (just savings)

   Ibrahim's savings: PHP 12,000

3. LOANS (Utang)
   "Humiram mula sa collective fund ng mga miyembro."

   - Access credit through your coop
   - Usually lower interest than lending companies
   - Must be repaid on schedule
   - Affects your good standing

   Ibrahim's current loan: PHP 20,000

4. PATRONAGE REFUND (Balik-Patronage)
   "Ito ang balik sa iyo mula sa surplus."

   - Based on your TRANSACTIONS with the coop
   - Not based on share capital
   - More you use coop services = more refund

   Ibrahim's last patronage refund: PHP 2,000

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"These products work together. Let's see how COOP rates compare to others..."

VISUAL: Table showing share capital vs savings vs loan
```

```text

WHEN TO BORROW:

GOOD REASONS:
- Productive investment (farm equipment, business)
- Emergency (medical, unexpected expenses)
- Education (children's schooling)

BAD REASONS:
- Wants that can wait (new phone, celebration)
- Covering lifestyle beyond your means
- Paying other loans (debt spiral!)

UNDERSTANDING LOAN TERMS:

+----------------------+----------------------------------------+
| Term                 | Meaning                                |
+----------------------+----------------------------------------+
| Principal            | The amount you borrow                  |
| Interest Rate        | The cost of borrowing (% per month/yr)|
| Loan Term            | How long to repay                      |
| Monthly Payment      | What you pay each month                |
+----------------------+----------------------------------------+

SIMPLE INTEREST CALCULATION:

Maria's Example:
- Principal: PHP 10,000
- Interest: 1% per month
- Term: 10 months

Interest = Principal x Rate x Time
Interest = 10,000 x 0.01 x 10 = PHP 1,000

Total to Repay: PHP 10,000 + PHP 1,000 = PHP 11,000
Monthly Payment: PHP 11,000 / 10 = PHP 1,100

COMPARING COOP VS. LENDER:

| Lender              | Rate/Month | PHP 10,000 for 10 months |
|---------------------|------------|--------------------------|
| Cooperative         | 1%         | PHP 11,000 total         |
| Lending company     | 3%         | PHP 13,000 total         |
| 5-6 lender          | 20%        | PHP 30,000 total         |

AVOID THE 5-6!
"Ang 5-6 ay magpapa-dukha sa iyo. Ang coop ay magpapa-yaman."

BRIDGE (30 sec):
"Borrowing is okay when done responsibly. Let's learn how to avoid over-indebtedness..."
```

**Lesson 2.5: Avoiding Over-Indebtedness** (TEXT - 8 min)

```markdown
# Don't Let Debt Drown You

Maria shares: "I've seen members who borrowed too much.
They lost their farms, their share capital, even their membership.
Let me teach you how to borrow RESPONSIBLY."

## The Debt-to-Income Rule

### Maximum Safe Debt

Your monthly loan payments should NOT exceed 30% of your income.

Example (Ibrahim):
- Monthly income: PHP 15,000
- 30% of income: PHP 4,500
- Maximum loan payment: PHP 4,500/month

If Ibrahim's loan payment is PHP 5,000/month = OVER-INDEBTED!

### Warning Signs of Over-Indebtedness

- [ ] Borrowing to pay other loans
- [ ] Missing payment due dates
- [ ] Unable to save anything
- [ ] Stress about money constantly
- [ ] Hiding debts from family
- [ ] Maxing out all credit sources

### What Happens If You Default?

1. Late fees and penalties accumulate
2. Credit standing damaged
3. May lose share capital as payment
4. May lose cooperative membership
5. May face legal action

## Responsible Borrowing Checklist

Before taking a loan, ask yourself:

### 1. Do I really NEED this?
- Is this a need or a want?
- Can I wait and save instead?

### 2. Can I AFFORD the payments?
- Calculate the monthly payment
- Is it less than 30% of my income?
- Do I have income for the entire loan term?

### 3. What is the PURPOSE?
- Will this loan generate income?
- Is this an emergency?
- Is this productive?

### 4. What are the TERMS?
- Interest rate
- Payment schedule
- Penalties for late payment
- Total amount to repay

## Maria's Golden Rule

"Bago umutang, tanungin mo ang sarili mo:
'Kung hindi ko makukuha ang loan na ito, mamamatay ba ako?'
Kung hindi, baka hindi mo pa kailangan umutang."
```

**Lesson 3.4: Common Scams and How to Avoid Them** (TEXT - 7 min)

```markdown
# Don't Be a Victim: Know the Scams

Fatima educates the members: "Scammers are creative.
But their tricks follow patterns. Learn these patterns!"

## Common Scams Targeting Cooperative Members

### 1. Phishing (Panloloko gamit pekeng mensahe)

**How It Works:**
- Fake text/email pretending to be bank/government
- Asks you to click a link
- Link leads to fake website
- Steals your information

**Example:**
"Your BPI account is locked. Click here to unlock: http://bpi-unlock.xyz"

**How to Avoid:**
- Banks NEVER send links via text
- Check the URL carefully (real BPI is bpi.com.ph)
- When in doubt, go directly to the bank/office

### 2. Fake Investment Schemes

**How It Works:**
- Promises very high returns (e.g., "100% return in 1 month!")
- Asks for upfront investment
- Pays early investors with later investors' money
- Eventually collapses, everyone loses

**Example:**
"Invest PHP 10,000, get PHP 20,000 in 30 days! Guaranteed!"

**How to Avoid:**
- If it sounds too good to be true, IT IS
- No legitimate investment guarantees such returns
- Check if registered with SEC

### 3. Money Mule Schemes

**How It Works:**
- Someone asks to use your account to receive money
- They promise you a cut
- The money is from illegal activities
- YOU become a criminal accomplice

**Example:**
"Just receive PHP 100,000 in your GCash, keep PHP 5,000 for yourself"

**How to Avoid:**
- Never let anyone use your account
- Receiving stolen money is a crime

### 4. SIM Swap Fraud

**How It Works:**
- Scammer gets your personal info
- Contacts your mobile provider, claims to be you
- Gets a new SIM with your number
- Receives your OTPs, accesses your accounts

**How to Avoid:**
- Never share personal info (birthday, mother's maiden name)
- Enable additional security on your accounts
- Report lost SIM immediately

### 5. Fake Cooperative Officers

**How It Works:**
- Someone claims to be from your coop
- Asks for payment or personal info
- Uses it to steal from you

**How to Avoid:**
- Verify by calling your coop office directly
- Never give info to callers - call them back on official number

## Red Flags - When to Be Suspicious

- [ ] Unsolicited messages about winning/inheritance
- [ ] Urgency ("Act now or lose everything!")
- [ ] Requests for passwords, PINs, OTPs
- [ ] Pressure to keep it secret
- [ ] Offers that seem too good
- [ ] Unknown senders asking for money

## What to Do If Scammed

1. **Don't panic** - but act fast
2. **Contact your bank/e-wallet** immediately
3. **Change your passwords** right away
4. **Report to authorities** (NBI Cybercrime)
5. **Tell others** so they don't fall for it too
```

## Appendix: Financial Terms Glossary

| Term | Filipino | Definition |
|------|----------|------------|
| Budget | Badyet | Plan for income and expenses |
| Income | Kita | Money received |
| Expenses | Gastos | Money spent |
| Savings | Ipon | Money set aside for future use |
| Interest | Interes | Cost of borrowing OR earnings on savings |
| Principal | Prinsipal | Original amount borrowed or deposited |
| Share Capital | Puhunan | Member's ownership stake in the cooperative |
| Patronage Refund | Balik-patronage | Member's share of surplus based on transactions |
| Loan | Utang | Money borrowed to be repaid with interest |
| Collateral | Kolateral | Asset pledged as security for loan |
| Deficit | Depisit | When expenses exceed income |
| Surplus | Surplus | When income exceeds expenses |
| E-wallet | E-wallet | Digital money storage app |
| OTP | OTP | One-Time Password for verification |
| Phishing | Phishing | Scam using fake messages to steal information |
| Scam | Scam/Pandaraya | Fraudulent scheme to steal money |
