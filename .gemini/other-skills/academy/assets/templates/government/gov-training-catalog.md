## Completed Staff Work (CSW) Training

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Duration | 2 days (16 hours) |
| Target Audience | Government staff, analysts, technical writers |
| Max Participants | 30 |
| Certificate | Yes |
| Methodology | Lecture, Workshop, Case Study, Peer Review |

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:
1. Explain the CSW doctrine and its importance in government decision-making
2. Prepare a complete staff work following the standard format
3. Analyze problems and present alternative solutions with recommendations
4. Write clear, concise, and actionable staff papers

---

### DAY 1: CSW Fundamentals (8 hours)

**Module 1: Understanding CSW (3 hours)**

Session 1.1: What is Completed Staff Work? (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Definition and origin of CSW
  - The CSW doctrine
  - Importance in bureaucracy
  - Common misconceptions
- Quote: "A completed staff work is the study of a problem and presentation of a solution in such form that all that remains to be done on the part of the head of the agency is to indicate approval or disapproval of the completed action."

Session 1.2: Elements of Effective CSW (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE + GROUP_DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Completeness (all info needed for decision)
  - Clarity (easy to understand)
  - Conciseness (no unnecessary details)
  - Correctness (accurate facts and figures)
  - Courtesy (proper format and tone)
- Discussion: Examples of good vs poor CSW

Session 1.3: CSW vs Other Documents (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - CSW vs Memorandum
  - CSW vs Position Paper
  - CSW vs Research Report
  - When to use CSW
- Reference: Document comparison chart

**Module 2: CSW Format and Structure (3 hours)**

Session 2.1: CSW Template Walkthrough (1.5 hours)
- Type: LECTURE + WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Heading (TO, FROM, SUBJECT, DATE)
  - Problem Statement
  - Background/Discussion
  - Alternative Courses of Action (ACAs)
  - Recommendation
  - Coordination
  - Attachments
- Template: **CSW Format Template**
- Output: Format familiarity exercise

Session 2.2: Problem Statement Workshop (1.5 hours)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Writing clear problem statements
  - Scope definition
  - Single vs multiple problems
  - Practice exercises
- Output: 3 practice problem statements

**Module 3: Analysis and Recommendations (2 hours)**

Session 3.1: Developing Alternatives (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Brainstorming techniques
  - Criteria for viable alternatives
  - Pros and cons analysis
  - Status quo option
- Minimum: Present at least 2 alternatives

Session 3.2: Making Recommendations (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE + GROUP_DISCUSSION
- Content:
  - Basis for recommendation
  - Supporting evidence
  - Risk assessment
  - Implementation considerations
- Key: Recommend only ONE option

---

### DAY 2: CSW Practice (8 hours)

**Module 4: Case Study Analysis (3 hours)**

Session 4.1: Case Study 1 - Budget Request (1.5 hours)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Real-world budget scenario
  - Analyze the problem
  - Develop alternatives
  - Draft recommendation
- Output: Individual CSW draft

Session 4.2: Case Study 2 - Policy Proposal (1.5 hours)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Policy challenge scenario
  - Stakeholder considerations
  - Legal implications
  - Draft CSW with coordination
- Output: Team CSW draft

**Module 5: CSW Writing Workshop (3 hours)**

Session 5.1: Writing Your CSW (2 hours)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Participants write complete CSW
  - Based on their actual work situations
  - Facilitator coaching
- Output: Complete CSW draft

Session 5.2: Common Errors (1 hour)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Incomplete analysis
  - Biased recommendations
  - Missing coordination
  - Format violations
  - Grammar and style issues
- Template: **CSW Quality Checklist**

**Module 6: Peer Review Session (2 hours)**

Session 6.1: Peer Exchange (1 hour)
- Type: WORKSHOP
- Content:
  - Exchange CSW drafts with partner
  - Review using checklist
  - Provide constructive feedback
- Output: Reviewed drafts with feedback

Session 6.2: Revision and Presentation (1 hour)
- Type: WORKSHOP + PRESENTATION
- Content:
  - Revise based on feedback
  - Volunteer presentations
  - Facilitator feedback
- Output: Final CSW

### CSW Format Template

```
OFFICE OF THE [AGENCY NAME]
[Office Address]

COMPLETED STAFF WORK

TO          : [Decision-maker name and title]
FROM        : [Preparer name and title]
SUBJECT     : [Clear, specific subject]
DATE        : [Date of submission]

1. PROBLEM

   [One paragraph stating the problem clearly and specifically]

2. BACKGROUND/DISCUSSION

   [Relevant facts, history, and analysis. Include data, legal basis,
   and other supporting information. Keep it concise but complete.]

3. ALTERNATIVE COURSES OF ACTION

   A. [First Alternative]
      - Description: [What this option involves]
      - Advantages: [Benefits]
      - Disadvantages: [Drawbacks]
      - Resource Implications: [Cost, time, personnel]

   B. [Second Alternative]
      - Description: [What this option involves]
      - Advantages: [Benefits]
      - Disadvantages: [Drawbacks]
      - Resource Implications: [Cost, time, personnel]

   [Optional: C. Status Quo/Do Nothing option]

4. RECOMMENDATION

   [Recommend ONE specific course of action with clear rationale.
   Include implementation steps if applicable.]

5. COORDINATION

   [List offices/persons consulted and their positions on the matter]
   - [Office A] - [Concurs/Non-concurs/Comments]
   - [Office B] - [Concurs/Non-concurs/Comments]

6. ATTACHMENTS

   a. [Supporting document 1]
   b. [Supporting document 2]


                              [Preparer's Signature]
                              [Preparer's Name]
                              [Position/Designation]


APPROVED / DISAPPROVED

_______________________
[Decision-maker Signature]
[Date]
```

### CSW Quality Checklist

#### Format (10 points)
- [ ] Follows standard CSW format
- [ ] Correct heading (TO, FROM, SUBJECT, DATE)
- [ ] All sections present
- [ ] Proper pagination and margins
- [ ] Clean, professional appearance

#### Problem Statement (15 points)
- [ ] Clear and specific
- [ ] Single problem identified
- [ ] Scope defined
- [ ] Actionable (can be solved)

#### Analysis (25 points)
- [ ] Sufficient background provided
- [ ] Facts are accurate and verifiable
- [ ] Legal/regulatory basis cited
- [ ] Relevant data included
- [ ] Stakeholders considered

#### Alternatives (25 points)
- [ ] At least 2 alternatives presented
- [ ] Each alternative is viable
- [ ] Pros and cons clearly stated
- [ ] Resource implications included
- [ ] Alternatives are distinct

#### Recommendation (20 points)
- [ ] Only ONE option recommended
- [ ] Clear rationale provided
- [ ] Aligns with analysis
- [ ] Implementation steps included
- [ ] Decision-ready

#### Writing Quality (5 points)
- [ ] Clear and concise
- [ ] Professional tone
- [ ] No grammatical errors
- [ ] Proper citations

**Total: /100 points | Passing: 70 points**

### Records Retention Quick Reference

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| General correspondence | 3 years |
| Financial records | 10 years |
| Personnel records | Permanent |
| Contracts | 10 years after expiry |
| Board minutes | Permanent |
| Audit reports | Permanent |
| Project files | 5 years after completion |
| Legal documents | Permanent |
| Policies/Procedures | Until superseded + 5 years |

### Key CSW and Government Administration Facts

**Completed Staff Work Origins:**
- Originated from military staff work doctrine (US Army, early 20th century)
- Concept: Staff officer completes all analysis so commander only decides
- Famous quote: "A completed staff work is the study of a problem and presentation of a solution in such form that all that remains to be done on the part of the head of the agency is to indicate approval or disapproval of the completed action."
- Adapted for Philippine civil service through CSC guidelines

**Philippine Civil Service Commission (CSC):**
- Constitutional body (Article IX-B, 1987 Constitution)
- Central personnel agency of the government
- Issues memorandum circulars on administrative procedures
- MC 72 s. 2019 - Guidelines on CSW format and requirements

**ARTA (Republic Act No. 11032):**
- Ease of Doing Business and Efficient Government Service Delivery Act (2018)
- Establishes Anti-Red Tape Authority
- Sets service delivery standards for government transactions
- Citizen's Charter requirements

**Records Management Standards:**
- National Archives of the Philippines (NAP) - national records authority
- General Records Disposition Schedule (GRDS)
- Retention periods mandated by law
- Digital records management guidelines
