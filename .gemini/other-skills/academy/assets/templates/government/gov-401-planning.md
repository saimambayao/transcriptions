# GOV-401: Strategic Planning

Comprehensive strategic planning training for government planners and managers.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-401 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 2-3 days (16-24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Planners, Program Managers, Division Chiefs |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Max Participants** | 25 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + outputs) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Conduct** situational analysis (internal/external environment)
2. **Develop** vision, mission, and strategic goals
3. **Create** strategic objectives and performance indicators
4. **Design** programs and projects aligned to strategy
5. **Build** implementation and monitoring plans

### Key Strategic Planning Facts

**SWOT Analysis Origins (1960-1970):**
- Developed at Stanford Research Institute (SRI) Long Range Planning Service
- Robert Franklin Stewart credited as originator
- Originally called SOFT: Satisfactory, Opportunities, Faults, Threats
- Relabeled to SWOT in 1967: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
- 2×2 matrix format appeared in *Systematic Corporate Planning* (Argenti, 1974)
- Research funded by Fortune 500 companies to understand why corporate planning failed
- By 1973, the acronym was well-known enough to be called "the mnemonic, familiar to students"

**Strategic Planning Research Team (1960-1970):**
- Marion Dosher
- Dr. Otis Benepe
- Albert Humphrey
- Robert Stewart
- Birger Lie

**Corporate Planning History:**
- Corporate planning trend first appeared at Du Pont in 1949
- By 1960, every Fortune 500 company had a 'corporate planning manager'
- SWOT analysis has been used at different levels: businesses, non-profits, governmental units, and individuals
