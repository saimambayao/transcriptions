# GOV-201: Supervision Skills for Government

Core supervisory skills training for government supervisors and managers.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-201 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 2-3 days (16-24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Supervisors, Division Chiefs, Department Heads |
| **Prerequisites** | Supervisory experience or appointment |
| **Max Participants** | 25 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + 70% assessment) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Understand** the transition from staff to supervisor
2. **Apply** effective delegation and empowerment techniques
3. **Manage** day-to-day supervisory responsibilities
4. **Build** and lead high-performing teams
5. **Handle** difficult conversations and team conflicts

## Key Templates

### 1. Delegation Worksheet
```
Task: _________________
Delegate to: ___________

□ What: Clear description of the task
□ Why: Purpose and importance
□ How: Standards and constraints
□ When: Deadline and checkpoints
□ Resources: What they'll need
□ Authority: What decisions they can make
□ Follow-up: When/how to check in
```

### 2. GROW Coaching Template
```
GOAL
- What do you want to achieve?
- What would success look like?

REALITY
- Where are you now?
- What have you tried?
- What's getting in the way?

OPTIONS
- What could you do?
- What else?
- What if you had unlimited resources?

WILL
- What will you do?
- When?
- What support do you need?
```

### 3. Difficult Conversation Preparation
```
SITUATION: What happened? (facts only)
BEHAVIOR: What specific behavior?
IMPACT: What was the effect?

OPENING: How will you start?
MAIN MESSAGE: Key point to convey
EXPECTED RESPONSE: How might they react?
DESIRED OUTCOME: What agreement do you want?
FOLLOW-UP: Next steps
```

### Key Management Theory Facts

**Frederick Winslow Taylor (1856-1915) - Scientific Management:**
- Father of Scientific Management
- Developed principles at Bethlehem Steel Company
- Four principles: scientific study of tasks, scientific selection/training, detailed instruction/supervision, equal division between managers and workers
- Focus on operational efficiency at supervisory level

**Henri Fayol (1841-1925) - Administrative Management:**
- Father of Modern Management Theory
- French Mining Engineer who developed general theory of administration
- Five functions of management: Planning, Organizing, Command, Coordination, Control
- 14 principles of management including Unity of Command and Scalar Chain
- Introduced concept of "esprit de corps" (team spirit)

**Key Differences:**
| Aspect | Fayol | Taylor |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Focus | Top-level management | Production-level management |
| Basis | Personal experience | Observation and experimentation |
| Orientation | Managerial function | Production and engineering |
| Applicability | Universal | Limited to specific organizations |
