# GOV-101: Completed Staff Work (CSW)

Essential document preparation training for government staff.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | GOV-101 |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 2-3 days (16-24 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | All government staff preparing reports, memos, recommendations |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Max Participants** | 30 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + 70% assessment) |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Define** Completed Staff Work and its importance in government operations
2. **Apply** the CSW doctrine to prepare action documents
3. **Write** clear, concise, and complete staff recommendations
4. **Avoid** common CSW errors and pitfalls
5. **Present** options and recommendations effectively

#### Module 1: Understanding CSW (2.5 hours)

**Session 1.1: What is Completed Staff Work?** (45 min)
- Type: LECTURE
- Content:
  - Definition and origin of CSW
  - The CSW doctrine: "A completed staff work is the study of a problem and presentation of a solution in such form that all that remains to be done on the part of the head of the agency is to indicate approval or disapproval."
  - Importance in bureaucracy
  - Common misconceptions
- Discussion: Examples of good vs poor CSW

**Session 1.2: Elements of Effective CSW** (45 min)
- Type: LECTURE + GROUP_DISCUSSION
- The 5 Cs of CSW:
  - **Completeness**: All information needed for decision
  - **Clarity**: Easy to understand
  - **Conciseness**: No unnecessary details
  - **Correctness**: Accurate facts and figures
  - **Courtesy**: Proper format and tone
- Activity: Critique sample CSW documents

**Session 1.3: CSW vs Other Documents** (45 min)

## CSW Format Template

```
OFFICE OF THE [AGENCY NAME]
[Office Address]

COMPLETED STAFF WORK

TO          : [Decision-maker name and title]
FROM        : [Preparer name and title]
SUBJECT     : [Clear, specific subject]
DATE        : [Date of submission]

1. PROBLEM

   [One paragraph stating the problem clearly and specifically.
   Focus on the issue requiring decision, not symptoms.]

2. BACKGROUND/DISCUSSION

   [Relevant facts, history, and analysis. Include:
   - Context and circumstances
   - Data and evidence
   - Legal or regulatory basis
   - Stakeholder considerations
   Keep concise but complete.]

3. ALTERNATIVE COURSES OF ACTION

   A. [First Alternative]
      - Description: [What this option involves]
      - Advantages: [Benefits]
      - Disadvantages: [Drawbacks]
      - Resource Implications: [Cost, time, personnel]

   B. [Second Alternative]
      - Description: [What this option involves]
      - Advantages: [Benefits]
      - Disadvantages: [Drawbacks]
      - Resource Implications: [Cost, time, personnel]

   [Optional: C. Status Quo/Do Nothing option]

4. RECOMMENDATION

   [Recommend ONE specific course of action.
   Provide clear rationale based on analysis above.
   Include implementation steps if applicable.]

5. COORDINATION

   [List offices/persons consulted and their positions]
   - [Office A] - [Concurs/Non-concurs/Comments]
   - [Office B] - [Concurs/Non-concurs/Comments]

6. ATTACHMENTS

   a. [Supporting document 1]
   b. [Supporting document 2]


                              [Preparer's Signature]
                              [Preparer's Name]
                              [Position/Designation]


APPROVED / DISAPPROVED

_______________________
[Decision-maker Signature]
[Date]
```

## CSW Quality Checklist

### Format (10 points)
- [ ] Follows standard CSW format
- [ ] Correct heading (TO, FROM, SUBJECT, DATE)
- [ ] All sections present
- [ ] Proper pagination and margins
- [ ] Clean, professional appearance

### Problem Statement (15 points)
- [ ] Clear and specific
- [ ] Single problem identified
- [ ] Scope defined
- [ ] Actionable (can be solved)

### Analysis (25 points)
- [ ] Sufficient background provided
- [ ] Facts are accurate and verifiable
- [ ] Legal/regulatory basis cited
- [ ] Relevant data included
- [ ] Stakeholders considered

### Alternatives (25 points)
- [ ] At least 2 alternatives presented
- [ ] Each alternative is viable
- [ ] Pros and cons clearly stated
- [ ] Resource implications included
- [ ] Alternatives are distinct

### Recommendation (20 points)
- [ ] Only ONE option recommended
- [ ] Clear rationale provided
- [ ] Aligns with analysis
- [ ] Implementation steps included
- [ ] Decision-ready

### Writing Quality (5 points)
- [ ] Clear and concise
- [ ] Professional tone
- [ ] No grammatical errors
- [ ] Proper citations

**Total: /100 points | Passing: 70 points**

### Key CSW Facts

**Historical Background:**
- CSW was popularized by former President Fidel Valdez Ramos in the Philippines
- Institutionalized by succeeding administrations through various memorandum circulars
- MC No. 68 (s. 2004) directed all government heads to conduct CSW before submitting requests for presidential issuance
- MC No. 72 (s. 2019) strengthened CSW standards for efficient program delivery
- MC No. 2 (s. 2022) mandated strict CSW compliance across all government agencies

**CSW Doctrine:**
"A completed staff work is the study of a problem and presentation of a solution in such form that all that remains to be done on the part of the head of the agency is to indicate approval or disapproval."

**Key Requirements (per MC 72):**
- Proposals must be evidence-based, inclusive, and holistic
- Cover memorandum not exceeding three pages
- Required format: Title, Antecedent, Recommendation, Rationale, Agency Coordination, Funding
- Whole-of-government approach considering legal, political, social, economic, technological, security, cultural and environmental standpoints
- Non-compliance = sufficient basis for OP to not act on request
