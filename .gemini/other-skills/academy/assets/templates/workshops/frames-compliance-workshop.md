# FRAMES Compliance Workshop

Comprehensive governance and compliance training for cooperatives using the FRAMES framework.

---

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Code** | FRAMES-WS |
| **Type** | Live Workshop |
| **Duration** | 5 days (40 hours) or 2-day intensive (16 hours) |
| **Target Audience** | Cooperative Officers, Board Members, Committee Members |
| **Prerequisites** | COOP-101 (recommended) |
| **Max Participants** | 30 |
| **Certificate** | Yes (100% attendance + 70% assessment) |

## FRAMES Framework

| Letter | Focus Area | Key Topics |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **F** | Financial Management | Bookkeeping, statements, audit, PEARLS |
| **R** | Responsiveness | Member services, grievance handling |
| **A** | Accountability | Transparency, reporting, oversight |
| **M** | Membership | Growth, education, engagement |
| **E** | Empowerment | Capacity building, leadership development |
| **S** | Sustainability | Long-term viability, governance strength |

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Apply** the FRAMES framework to assess cooperative health
2. **Manage** financial records according to CDA standards
3. **Fulfill** reporting and accountability requirements
4. **Strengthen** membership engagement and education
5. **Develop** strategic plans for sustainability

### Key Cooperative History Facts

**Rochdale Pioneers (1844):**
- 28 artisans (weavers) in Rochdale, England
- Formed due to miserable working conditions and low wages
- Pooled £28 of starting capital over 4 months
- Opened store at Toad Lane on December 21, 1844
- Started with butter, sugar, flour, and oatmeal
- 80 members and £182 capital by end of first year
- Established the prototype of modern cooperative movement

**Original Rochdale Principles (1844-1854):**
1. Democracy (one member, one vote)
2. Open, voluntary membership
3. Limited return on equity
4. Surplus belongs to members
5. Education of members and public
6. Cooperation between cooperatives

**International Co-operative Alliance (ICA):**
- Founded August 19, 1895 in London, England
- Delegates from 13 countries at 1st Co-operative Congress
- Today: ~1 billion members worldwide
- Cooperatives employ 250 million people directly or indirectly

**Philippine Cooperative Law:**
- RA 6938: Original Cooperative Code of the Philippines
- RA 9520: Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008 (amendment)
- RA 11364: Further amendments
- CDA: Cooperative Development Authority (regulatory body)
- Revised IRR signed March 18, 2015

**State Policy (RA 9520):**
"Foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as a practical vehicle for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people power towards the attainment of economic development and social justice."
