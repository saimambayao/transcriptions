## BDP 2023-2028 Overview

### Vision & Mission

**BARMM Vision**: "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**BARMM Mission**: "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

### Overall Goal

**Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro**

- **Empowered**: Improved capacity of communities through quality social services and widened economic opportunities
- **Cohesive**: Improved relationships between stakeholders, reduced violent conflict, forged partnerships
- **Progressive**: Sustained ability to optimize resources and realize economic and human potential

## Development Framework & Alignment

### Six Development Goals

**Goal 1: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government**
- Ensures continuity of service delivery during crises
- Government as primary agent of economic and social justice
- Accountability frameworks, systems, and processes in place
- Application: Government training through MoroAcademy (CSW, leadership, M&E)

**Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy**
- Economic gains benefit all, especially poor and marginalized
- Reduced inequality indicators (Gini coefficient, poverty gap)
- Sustained economic growth, diversified sectors
- Application: Cooperative/SE training aligned to economic targets

**Goal 3: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Bangsamoro Communities**
- Lasting peace through security and peace-building
- Safe communities resilient to natural and human-induced disasters
- Application: Community-based training, disaster-aware curriculum design

**Goal 4: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services**
- Quality services for all sectors, leaving no one behind
- Services responsive to diverse community needs
- Application: Accessibility in MoroAcademy design (multiple languages, modalities)

**Goal 5: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture and Identity Preserved and Recognized**
- Exercise authority to maintain and promote diverse culture
- Mandated under BOL Section 24, Article IX
- Application: Culturally-contextualized examples, Islamic values integration

**Goal 6: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure**
- Broad-based, inclusive infrastructure effects
- Infrastructure meeting demands, withstanding climate impacts
- Application: Consideration of offline delivery, accessibility for remote areas

### Macroeconomic Targets (2023-2028)

| Target | Baseline (2021) | End-of-Plan (2028) | Implication for Training |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| GRDP Growth Rate | 7.5% | 8-9% annually | Skilled workforce needed for growth sectors |
| Industry & Services Growth | 7.9%, 6.6% | 10-12% | Skills training priority in these sectors |
| Unemployment Rate | 3.8% | 3-5% | Employment-ready skills programs |
| Underemployment Rate | 9.0% | 4-7% | Skills diversification needed |
| Poverty Incidence | 29.8% | 20-25% | Livelihood and economic empowerment training |

## Moral Governance Principles

MoroAcademy training must integrate the 12 Pillars of Moral Governance into all curricula, especially government training programs.

### 12 Pillars of Moral Governance

**1. Integrity**: Avoiding financial/other influences on public duties
- Training integration: Ethical decision-making modules, conflict of interest scenarios

**2. Accountability**: Open, transparent decisions with documentation
- Training integration: Reporting requirements, documentation standards

**3. Transparency and Honesty**: Declaring interests, public transactions
- Training integration: Conflict resolution, transparency frameworks

**4. Sense of Patriotism**: Investment in homeland reputation and integrity
- Training integration: Nation-building context for all programs

**5. Striving for Excellence**: Legitimacy, credibility, transparency in work
- Training integration: Quality assurance, continuous improvement mindset

**6. Cultural Understanding and Tolerance**: Awareness of socioeconomic differences
- Training integration: Multicultural communication, respect for diversity

**7. Unwavering Loyalty to God**: Devotion to God as characteristic of effective servant
- Training integration: Islamic values, ethical foundations, faith-informed leadership

**8. Justice**: Serving without prejudice, equitable treatment
- Training integration: Fair governance, equitable resource allocation

**9. Inclusivity**: Welcoming environment free from discrimination
- Training integration: Inclusive language, diverse examples, accessibility features

**10. Objectivity**: Merit-based decisions, public interest focus
- Training integration: Evidence-based decision-making, data literacy

**11. Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement**: Consulting community on needs and solutions
- Training integration: Participatory approaches, community feedback loops

**12. Balance Between Practical Life and Moral Side**: Balancing pragmatism with ethics
- Training integration: Real-world case studies showing ethical considerations

## Education & Skills Development Strategy

### BDP Education Priorities (Strategy 6: Human Capital Development)

**Sectoral Goal**: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services

**Key Education Sub-Sector Objectives**:

1. **Access to Quality & Equitable Education at All Levels Improved**
   - Early childhood development programs
   - Alternative Learning Systems (ALS) for out-of-school youth
   - TVET expansion (target: 89% completion rate)
   - MoroAcademy role: Accessible, affordable upskilling pathways

2. **Effective Educational Management & Administration Improved**
   - School/institution leadership training
   - Performance monitoring systems
   - MoroAcademy role: Government management courses (GOV-301, GOV-401)

3. **Madaris Education System Institutionalized**
   - Islamic Studies and Arabic Language (ISAL) programs
   - Madrasah standardization and accreditation
   - Asatidz (teacher) professional development
   - MoroAcademy role: Islamic curriculum integration, madaris-aligned training

4. **Stakeholder Investments in Education Sector Ensured**
   - ETF utilization for cooperative member education
   - Government agency training budget allocation
   - Community education partnerships
   - MoroAcademy role: ETF-eligible courses, subsidized government training

### BARMM Education Baseline Challenges

| Indicator | BARMM | National | Gap | Training Implication |
|-----------|-------|----------|-----|----------------------|
| Functional Literacy | 71.0% | 91.6% | -20.6% | Simplified language, visual/audio, Grade 8 reading level |
| Dropout Rate | 8.36% | 3.76% | +4.60% | Adult learning approaches, flexible schedules |
| Cohort Survival (Gr 1-6) | 64.75% | 83.02% | -18.27% | Mobile delivery, make-up sessions |
| Completion Rate (Gr 1-6) | 63.98% | 82.51% | -18.53% | Modular, short-course options |

**MoroAcademy Design Implications**:
- Design for lower literacy levels
- Multi-modal delivery (video, audio, visual, text)
- Consider offline/low-bandwidth options for remote areas
- Build foundational skills before advanced content
- Flexible pacing and completion timelines

### BARMM TVET Landscape

**Current TVET Data**:
- Enrolled: 28,065 learners
- Graduates: 25,002 (89% completion rate)
- Assessed for Certification: 14,932 (60% of graduates)
- Certified Competent: 14,036 (94% of assessed)

**Priority TVET Sectors** (focus curriculum development):
1. Construction
2. Tourism
3. Electrical and Electronics

**Assessment Gap**: Only 60% undergo competency assessment due to limited centers. MoroAcademy courses should prepare learners for National Competency Assessment (NCA).

## Economic Development & Workforce Needs

### BDP Economic Development Strategy (Goal 2: Equitable, Competitive, Robust Economy)

**Key Sectors for Workforce Development**:

**1. Agriculture, Forestry, Fisheries & Agrarian Reform**
- Productivity challenges: Low yields, inadequate infrastructure, pre/post-harvest losses
- Training needs: Modern farming techniques, post-harvest processing, market linkages
- Priority skills: Agricultural entrepreneurship, cooperative management, sustainable farming
- MoroAcademy courses: Agriculture-focused cooperative training, market linkage workshops

**2. Trade & Industry**
- Generated PHP 25.3 billion investments (2010-2021)
- Created 20,103 employment opportunities
- Challenge: Skill-job mismatch
- Training needs: Industrial skills, business management, quality control
- MoroAcademy courses: BIZ-101 (accounting), BIZ-201 (marketing), specialized trade courses

**3. Tourism**
- Tourism accounts for 0.8% of BARMM GDP
- Post-pandemic recovery: Arrivals grew from 7,332 (2020) to 195,822 (2021)
- Challenge: Underdeveloped tourism circuits, limited skilled workforce
- Training needs: Hospitality skills, tourism product development, destination management
- MoroAcademy courses: Tourism hospitality training, tour guide development

**4. Cooperatives & Social Enterprises**
- Key vehicle for inclusive economic development
- Challenge: Capacity gaps, governance weaknesses, compliance issues
- Training needs: Cooperative governance, financial management, FRAMES compliance
- MoroAcademy courses: COOP-101, COOP-201, COOP-301, EDP-500, FRAMES compliance workshops

**5. Labor & Employment**
- Youth unemployment needs investigation
- Challenge: High underemployment (9.0%), low average wages
- Training needs: Job-skills alignment, entrepreneurship, decent work advocacy
- MoroAcademy courses: Employability skills, business startup workshops

### Workforce Development Priorities

**1. Skills-Job Mismatch Reduction**
- Identify skill gaps between available training and sector needs
- Develop curricula aligned to industry demands
- Facilitate employer engagement in curriculum design
- Establish apprenticeship/internship pathways

**2. Youth Employability Enhancement**
- BARMM has 42% of population in 15-39 age bracket (highly trainable)
- Address youth unemployment through skills training
- Entrepreneurship and self-employment pathways
- MoroAcademy integration: Youth-focused course variants

**3. Professional Competency Development**
- Government workforce upskilling (CSW, leadership, M&E)
- Cooperative officer mandatory training (CDA MC 2015-09)
- TVET learner assessment preparation
- Madrasah teacher professional development

**4. Inclusive Economic Participation**
- Women economic empowerment
- Indigenous Peoples livelihood training
- Persons with disabilities employment support
- Vulnerable population economic integration

## Islamic Education Integration

### Madaris Education Institutionalization (BDP Priority)

**ISAL Program**: Islamic Studies and Arabic Language

**Integration Opportunities for MoroAcademy**:

1. **Islamic Principles in Governance Training**
   - Moral governance rooted in Islamic values
   - Justice and equity from Islamic perspective
   - Servant leadership (khidmah) concepts
   - Include Quranic references in government training

2. **Islamic Finance & Banking for Cooperative Training**
   - Principles of Riba (usury) prohibition
   - Profit-sharing models in cooperatives
   - Zakat and charitable giving concepts
   - Sadaqah and community investment

3. **Islamic Business Ethics**
   - Honest dealings (sidq) in business
   - Ethical marketing (no false claims)
   - Fair wages and labor practices
   - Environmental stewardship (khalifah concept)

4. **Madrasah Asatidz Professional Development**
   - Arabic language teaching methodologies
   - Islamic education pedagogy
   - Integration of Islamic education with secular curriculum
   - Student assessment in Islamic context

5. **Madrasah Student Pathways**
   - Transition programs from madaris to TVET
   - Integration of Islamic education with vocational training
   - English and Arabic language skills development
   - Digital literacy for madrasah students

### Shari'ah Compliance in Training Programs

**Considerations for Course Design**:
- Gender-appropriate teaching methodologies
- Halal principles in any business/entrepreneurship training
- Islamic work ethic emphasis
- Respect for prayer times and Islamic calendar
- Separate facilities where required by Islamic practice
- Female trainer preferences for certain topics

## Skills Gaps & Training Priorities

### Agriculture & Fisheries Productivity Gaps

**Identified Problems**:
- Low agriculture and fisheries productivity
- Inadequate infrastructure and market access
- Limited technology adoption
- Weak monitoring and evaluation
- Mismatch between available skills training and sector needs

**Training Responses**:
- Agricultural entrepreneurship training
- Cooperative-based marketing and value chains
- Technology transfer workshops
- Post-harvest processing techniques
- Market linkage facilitation

### Labor Market Skills Gaps

**Core Problem: Low Skilled & Inexperienced Workforce**
- Limited access to quality education and training
- High dropout rates, low vocational enrollment
- Low core employability skills
- Job-skills mismatch

**MoroAcademy Responses**:
- BIZ-101, BIZ-201 (foundational business skills)
- Employability skills modules in all courses
- Industry-partnership training
- TVET learner support courses

### Tourism Sector Development Gaps

**Core Problem: Underdeveloped Tourism Circuits**
- No established tourism circuits
- Limited support amenities and facilities
- Few tourism enterprises operating
- Concentrated in urban centers only

**MoroAcademy Responses**:
- Tourism hospitality training
- Destination management workshop
- Small business development for tourism MSMEs
- Integration with EDP-500 for tourism entrepreneurs

### Government Capacity Gaps

**Identified Needs**:
- Improved service delivery during emergencies
- Better decision-making and planning systems
- Results-based management capability
- Staff competency development

**MoroAcademy Responses**:
- GOV-101 (Completed Staff Work)
- GOV-301 (Executive Leadership)
- GOV-401 (Strategic Planning)
- GOV-502 (Results-Based M&E)

- Fee waivers for disadvantaged sectors

## Implementation Considerations

### ETF Utilization for Training

**RA 9520 Article 59**: Cooperatives allocate maximum 10% of net surplus to Education & Training Fund

**Eligible MoroAcademy Uses**:
- Officer training (COOP-101, 201, 301)
- Member education
- Staff professional development
- Training materials and subscriptions
- TSP coordination fees

**Documentation Requirements**:
- Annual training plan approved by BOD
- Attendance records and certificates
- Receipts and invoices
- ETF ledger tracking
- Annual ETF utilization report to CSEA

### Government Agency Training Budget

**Alignment with BDP Implementation**:
- Ministry training allocations
- Agency staff development budgets
- Leadership development programs
- Competency-based advancement

**MoroAcademy as Training Provider**:
- Subsidized government training
- Custom agency training modules
- Multi-agency cohort training
- Results-based evaluation support

### Cooperative Compliance Integration

**CSEA Requirements for Cooperatives**:
- CDA MC 2015-09 mandatory training
- FRAMES compliance framework
- Annual Certificate of Compliance
- Audit compliance tracking

**MoroAcademy Role**:
- Ensure CDA MC 2015-09 compliance
- FRAMES curriculum alignment
- Training records for compliance documentation
- Completion certificates for officer files

## Quick Reference: BDP Strategy Checklist for Curriculum Design

When designing new MoroAcademy courses, ensure alignment with:

**Strategy 1: Governance Improvement**
- [ ] Incorporates moral governance principles
- [ ] Supports accountable, just government
- [ ] Builds institutional capacity

**Strategy 2: Economic Development**
- [ ] Supports equitable, competitive economy
- [ ] Develops workforce for priority sectors
- [ ] Addresses identified skills gaps

**Strategy 3: Technology & Innovation**
- [ ] Leverages digital learning (MoroAcademy platform)
- [ ] Teaches digital skills where relevant
- [ ] Accessible in low-bandwidth contexts

**Strategy 6: Human Capital Development**
- [ ] Inclusive, accessible design
- [ ] Quality content aligned to outcomes
- [ ] Addresses BARMM-specific challenges
- [ ] Supports equitable access to services

**Strategy 7: Cultural Diversity**
- [ ] Culturally contextualized examples
- [ ] Islamic education integration
- [ ] Local language support
- [ ] Respects Bangsamoro identity

**Horizontal Alignment (12-Point Priority Agenda)**
- [ ] Contributes to one or more priorities
- [ ] Coordinated with related initiatives
- [ ] Monitored for contribution to targets
