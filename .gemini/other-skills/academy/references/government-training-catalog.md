## Course Details

### 1. Completed Staff Work (CSW)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Code** | GOV-101 |
| **Type** | Workshop |
| **Duration** | 16-24 hours |
| **Target Audience** | All government employees preparing reports, memos, recommendations |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Description**: Master the art of preparing complete, ready-for-decision documents that save executive time and improve organizational efficiency.

**Learning Objectives**:
- Define Completed Staff Work and its importance in government operations
- Apply the CSW doctrine to prepare action documents
- Write clear, concise, and complete staff recommendations
- Avoid common CSW errors and pitfalls
- Present options and recommendations effectively

**Module Structure**:

```
DAY 1: CSW Fundamentals (8h)

Module 1: Understanding CSW (2h)
├── Session: What is Completed Staff Work?
├── Session: The CSW Doctrine
├── Session: Why CSW Matters in Government
└── Activity: CSW vs Incomplete Staff Work Examples

Module 2: Elements of Effective CSW (3h)
├── Session: Problem Definition
├── Session: Research and Analysis
├── Session: Options Development
├── Session: Recommendation Formulation
└── Activity: Case Study Analysis

Module 3: CSW Document Structure (3h)
├── Session: Action Memo Format
├── Session: Position Paper Format
├── Session: Executive Summary Writing
└── Workshop: Draft Your First CSW

---

DAY 2: CSW Practice (8h)

Module 4: Common CSW Errors (2h)
├── Session: Incomplete Analysis
├── Session: Unclear Recommendations
├── Session: Missing Attachments
└── Activity: Error Identification Exercise

Module 5: Writing Workshop (4h)
├── Workshop: CSW Writing Exercise (Real Scenarios)
├── Peer Review Session
└── Facilitator Feedback

Module 6: Presentation & Sign-off (2h)
├── Session: Presenting to Decision-Makers
├── Session: Handling Questions and Revisions
├── Activity: Mock Presentation
└── Closing: Action Planning

---

DAY 3 (Optional): Advanced CSW & Certification (8h)

Module 7: Complex CSW Documents (3h)
├── Session: Multi-Option Recommendations
├── Session: Policy Proposals and Position Papers
├── Session: CSW for Sensitive/Urgent Matters
└── Workshop: Advanced CSW Drafting

Module 8: Review and Certification (3h)
├── Workshop: CSW Portfolio Completion
├── Peer Review and Refinement
├── Facilitator Review and Scoring
└── Individual Feedback

Module 9: Implementation Planning (2h)
├── Session: Embedding CSW in Your Office
├── Activity: Office CSW Standards Development
├── Certification Exam (if applicable)
└── Closing and Certificates
```

**Key Outputs**:
- Completed CSW document (real or practice scenario)
- Personal CSW checklist
- Action plan for implementation

**Assessment**:
- Pre-test (knowledge baseline)
- CSW writing exercise (rubric-scored)
- Post-test (knowledge gain)

## BARMM Training Context: BDP 2023-2028

### Development Framework Alignment

**BARMM Vision**: "A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**BARMM Mission**: "Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people."

**Overall Goal**: Empowered, Cohesive, and Progressive Bangsamoro

### Pillars of Moral Governance

All BARMM government training should integrate these 12 Pillars of Moral Governance:

| Pillar | Training Integration |
|--------|---------------------|
| 1. Integrity | Ethics modules, conflict of interest |
| 2. Accountability | Documentation, reporting, audit readiness |
| 3. Transparency and Honesty | Open records, public disclosure |
| 4. Sense of Patriotism | Bangsamoro history, regional pride |
| 5. Striving for Excellence | Quality standards, continuous improvement |
| 6. Cultural Understanding and Tolerance | Diversity training, cultural sensitivity |
| 7. Unwavering Loyalty to God | Values-based leadership |
| 8. Justice | Fair processes, equity in service delivery |
| 9. Inclusivity | Serving all constituents without discrimination |
| 10. Objectivity | Merit-based decisions, evidence-based policy |
| 11. Dialogue and Meaningful Engagement | Stakeholder consultation, participatory planning |
| 12. Balance Between Practical Life and Moral Side | Ethical decision-making frameworks |

### BDP 2023-2028 Development Goals

Training programs should contribute to these six development goals:

| Goal | Training Relevance |
|------|-------------------|
| **Goal 1**: Stable, Just, and Accountable Government | GOV-101 (CSW), GOV-201 (Supervision), GOV-202 (Performance Management), GOV-301 (Executive Leadership) |
| **Goal 2**: Equitable, Competitive, and Robust Economy | Enterprise development, livelihood, financial management |
| **Goal 3**: Peaceful, Safe, and Resilient Communities | Disaster preparedness, conflict resolution, community development |
| **Goal 4**: Inclusive, Responsive, and Quality Social Services | Service delivery, citizen engagement, social welfare |
| **Goal 5**: Rich and Diverse Bangsamoro Culture Preserved | Cultural competency, heritage preservation |
| **Goal 6**: Strategic, Adequate, and Climate-Resilient Infrastructure | Project management, infrastructure planning |

### BDP Development Strategy 6: Human Capital Development

**Strategy Statement**: "Ensure inclusive and equitable access to quality services for social justice and human capital development."

This strategy directly supports government training priorities:

**Education Strategies** (relevant to capacity building):

### GOV-001: Moral Governance in Public Service (Proposed)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Code** | GOV-001 |
| **Type** | Workshop |
| **Duration** | 8-16 hours |
| **Target Audience** | All BARMM government employees |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Description**: Foundation course on moral governance principles for all BARMM government employees, covering the 12 Pillars and their application in daily public service.

**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand the BARMM vision, mission, and moral governance framework
- Apply the 12 Pillars of Moral Governance in daily work
- Demonstrate ethical decision-making in government settings
- Integrate Bangsamoro values in public service delivery

**Module Structure**:

```
Module 1: BARMM Foundations (2h)
├── The Bangsamoro Struggle and BOL
├── BARMM Vision and Mission
├── BDP 2023-2028 Overview
└── The Meaning of Genuine Autonomy

Module 2: The 12 Pillars of Moral Governance (4h)
├── Integrity, Accountability, Transparency
├── Patriotism, Excellence, Cultural Tolerance
├── Faith, Justice, Inclusivity
├── Objectivity, Dialogue, Ethical Balance
└── Workshop: Pillar Application Scenarios

Module 3: Ethical Decision-Making (2h)
├── Ethical Frameworks for Public Service
├── Conflict of Interest Management
├── Whistleblowing and Protection
└── Case Study: Ethical Dilemmas in BARMM

Module 4: Moral Governance in Practice (2-8h)
├── Personal Moral Governance Commitment
├── Office Ethics Assessment
├── Action Planning
└── Closing: Oath of Moral Governance
```

## Training Implementation Guidelines for BARMM

### Language Considerations

- Primary: Filipino/English
- Consider local language options: Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug, Sama
- Avoid jargon; use plain language
- Provide glossaries for technical terms

### Cultural Sensitivity

- Schedule around prayer times (especially Friday Jumu'ah)
- Consider Ramadan timing for scheduling
- Gender-appropriate seating arrangements if requested
- Halal food for catered events

| Province/Area | Access Consideration |
