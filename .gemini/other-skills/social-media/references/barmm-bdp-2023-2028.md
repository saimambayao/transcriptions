# BARMM Social Media Strategy: BDP 2023-2028 Reference Guide

## Overview

This reference guide aligns social media strategies with the **2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028**, providing frameworks for government agencies, cooperatives, and social enterprises to communicate development priorities, economic goals, and cultural values to BARMM audiences.

---

## Part 1: BDP 2023-2028 Strategic Framework

### Vision, Mission & Development Goals

**BARMM Vision:**
"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."

**BARMM Mission:**
"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."

**Overall Goal:**
An empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro.

### Six Development Goals

1. **Stable, just, and accountable government** - Effective delivery, transparency, accountability
2. **Equitable, competitive, and robust economy** - Inclusive growth, market competitiveness, sustained GDP growth
3. **Peaceful, safe, and resilient Bangsamoro communities** - Security, peace-building, disaster resilience
4. **Inclusive, responsive, and quality social services** - Education, health, social protection
5. **Rich and diverse Bangsamoro culture and identity preserved and recognized** - Cultural heritage, identity
6. **Strategic, adequate, and climate-resilient infrastructure** - Transportation, utilities, climate adaptation

### Twelve Pillars of Moral Governance

Social media messaging should emphasize BARMM's moral governance framework:
1. Integrity and anti-corruption
2. Accountability and transparency
3. Honesty and conflict resolution
4. Patriotism and regional pride
5. Excellence in service delivery
6. Cultural understanding and tolerance
7. Unwavering loyalty to Islamic values
8. Justice and equity
9. Inclusivity and non-discrimination
10. Merit-based decisions
11. Dialogue and community engagement
12. Balance between practical and moral values

## Part 2: Macroeconomic Targets 2023-2028

### Key Growth Targets

| Target | Baseline (2020-2021) | Goal (2023-2028) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|
| **GRDP Annual Growth Rate** | 7.5% | 8-9% annually |
| **GRDP Per Capita Growth** | 6.4% | 6-9% annually |
| **Industry & Services Growth** | 7.9%-6.6% | 10-12% double-digit |
| **Gross Capital Formation** | 16.91% of GRDP | 18-20% of GRDP |
| **Inflation Rate** | 2.40% | 2-4% range |
| **Unemployment Rate** | 3.80% | 3-5% range |
| **Underemployment Rate** | 9.0% | 4-7% range |
| **Poverty Incidence** | 29.80% | 20-25% range |

**Social Media Implication:** Frame economic growth as shared prosperity benefiting all Bangsamoro communities, emphasizing job creation, poverty reduction, and inclusive development.

## Part 3: Priority Economic Sectors

### Sector 1: Agriculture, Fisheries & Agrarian Reform

**Current Status:**
- Agriculture remains BARMM's strongest sector
- Fisheries volume (2021): 1,010,804.29 metric tons
- Seaweed production: Highest in Philippines (720,300.94 MT)
- Major producing areas: Tawi-Tawi, Sulu, Maguindanao

**Social Media Content Opportunities:**
- Farmer success stories and productivity gains
- Seaweed farming and aquaculture innovations
- Halal meat production potential
- Agri-cooperatives and farmer organizations
- Climate-resilient farming practices
- Women farmers and agricultural entrepreneurs

**2028 Targets:**
- Increased productivity and market access
- Resilience to climate shocks
- Sustainable fishing practices
- Value-added agricultural products

### Sector 2: Trade, Industry & MSMEs

**Current Status:**
- 8,427 registered MSMEs in 2021 (161% increase from 2020)
- Only 1,340 manufacturing enterprises (0.6% of national total)
- Mostly in informal sector, facing competitiveness challenges
- Generated PHP 9.02 billion in investments (2017-2021)
- Created 62,166 jobs (2017-2021)

**Social Media Content Opportunities:**
- MSME success stories and growth trajectories
- Formalization benefits and compliance support
- Product showcases and value-added innovations
- Business registration and accreditation processes
- Credit and financing opportunities (Islamic banking)
- Technology adoption and digital transformation (SETUP Program)

**2028 Targets:**
- Increased competitiveness of MSMEs
- Value-added product development
- Better market linkages and access
- Formalization of informal sector businesses

### Sector 3: Cooperatives & Social Enterprise

**Critical Statistics:**
- **12,214 registered cooperatives** (2010-2020)
- **Only 11.66% compliance rate** (1,424 compliant)
- Maguindanao: 38.24% of cooperatives (4,671)
- Lanao del Sur: 37.29% of cooperatives (4,554)
- Producer cooperatives: 37.58% (4,590)
- Marketing cooperatives: 27.54% (3,364)
- Agricultural cooperatives: 12.82% (1,566)

**Key Challenges:**
- Low compliance with regulatory requirements
- Limited technical and financial capacity
- Difficulty adapting to new CSEA requirements
- Many organized by former combatants (transitional support needed)

**2028 Compliance Target:** 50% compliance rate (from current 11.66%)

**Social Media Content Opportunities:**
- Cooperative transformation stories
- Compliance journey narratives
- Member success and empowerment
- Impact of cooperative principles on livelihoods
- Women and youth cooperatives
- Agricultural and producer cooperative innovations
- Social enterprise mission stories
- Community-based cooperative initiatives

**Social Enterprise Focus:**
- Mission-driven business models
- Social and environmental impact
- Inclusive employment practices
- Sustainable product lines
- Indigenous and cultural product enterprises

### Sector 4: Tourism

**Current Status:**
- Significant untapped tourism potential
- Tourism contribution: Only 0.8% of GDP (2018)
- Tourist arrivals (2021): 195,822 (up from 7,332 in 2020)
- Still below pre-pandemic levels (2018-2019)
- Limited tourism circuits and developed destinations
- Growing confidence due to peace gains

**Tourism Assets:**
- Nature tourism and beaches
- Cultural and heritage sites
- Potential for halal tourism
- Island destinations (Tawi-Tawi, Sulu, Basilan)
- Growing regional connectivity

**Social Media Content Opportunities:**
- Destination showcases (beaches, mountains, cultural sites)
- Tourist testimonials and travel stories
- Tourism business development
- Cultural experience documentation
- Sustainable tourism practices
- Adventure and eco-tourism offerings
- Local tourism enterprise stories
- Infrastructure development projects

**2028 Targets:**
- Increased tourist arrivals
- Developed tourism circuits and products
- Enhanced accommodation and services
- Market competitiveness of tourism MSMEs

## Part 4: Science, Technology & Innovation Context

### STI Challenges (Chapter 8, BDP 2023-2028)

**Scientific Literacy:**
- National baseline: Only 3.20% scientific literacy
- Weak STI culture among households
- Low utilization of new technologies

**STI Ecosystem Gaps:**
- Only 2 HEIs offering science/technology courses
- No BARMM Science Park
- 1,340 registered manufacturers (0.6% of national total)
- Limited R&D support (0.23% of GRDP in 2019)

**Social Media Implication:**
Use simple, visual, relatable content. Avoid jargon. Focus on practical applications and community benefits of technology and innovation.

### Key STI Programs & Content Hooks

| Program | Social Media Content |
|---------|---------------------|
| **BASE Scholarship** | Youth science education stories; scholarship opportunities |
| **Bangsamoro Regional Science High School** | STEAME education; student achievements |
| **BARMM Science & Tech Park** | Innovation ecosystem; startup success stories |
| **SETUP Program** | MSME technology adoption; productivity gains |
| **Bangsamoro Research Info System** | Data-driven development storytelling |

### 2028 STI Targets

- 10 LGUs with STI-responsive plans
- 5 STI-related enterprises
- 2,500 R&D personnel (from 1,100 in 2019)
- 1% of GRDP allocated to R&D (from 0.23%)

## Part 5: BARMM-Specific Social Media Strategies

### Cultural & Religious Sensitivity Framework

**Islamic Values Integration:**
- Frame development as pursuit of justice and social welfare (core Islamic values)
- Emphasize moral governance and ethical leadership
- Reference Islamic principles in messaging
- Respect prayer times (avoid scheduling during salah)
- Acknowledge Islamic holidays and observances (Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha)

**Content Calendar Adjustments:**
- Ramadan: Adjust timing for pre-dawn and post-break announcements
- Friday: Consider Jumu'ah prayers timing
- Eid seasons: Special greetings and culturally-relevant celebrations
- Islamic New Year and Prophet's Birthday: Recognition content

**Language Considerations:**
- Primary: Filipino and English
- Secondary: Local Bangsamoro languages (Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug, Samal)
- Translate key development messages to local dialects
- Use simple Filipino over complex English for accessibility

### Platform Strategy (Priority Order)

1. **Facebook (92.1% reach)** - Dominant platform; primary government communication channel
2. **Messenger** - Customer service; direct inquiries; business support
3. **Instagram** - Visual storytelling; youth and urban audiences
4. **TikTok** - Youth engagement; educational short-form content
5. **YouTube** - Long-form tutorials; development videos; training content

### Content Pillars for BARMM Organizations

**Column 1: Development & Progress**
- BDP 2023-2028 goal updates
- Sector achievements and statistics
- Infrastructure projects
- Economic growth stories
- Frequency: 2-3x weekly

**Column 2: Community & Culture**
- Local traditions and heritage
- Cultural celebrations
- Community events
- Cooperative and SE stories
- Frequency: 2x weekly

**Column 3: Education & Empowerment**
- Skills development programs
- STEAME education content
- Financial literacy
- Business training
- Technology adoption guides
- Frequency: 2-3x weekly

**Column 4: Impact & Human Interest**
- Beneficiary transformation stories
- Livelihood improvements
- Family and community impact
- Success narratives
- Frequency: 2x weekly

**Column 5: Engagement & Information**
- Program announcements and deadlines
- Application processes and requirements
- Service information
- Interactive Q&A
- Community feedback opportunities
- Frequency: As needed, 1-2x weekly

### Audience-Specific Messaging

| Audience | Platform Focus | Content Tone | Key Messages |
|----------|----------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Farmers/Fisherfolk** | Facebook, Radio | Practical, solution-focused | Productivity, markets, resilience |
| **MSMEs/Entrepreneurs** | Facebook, Instagram | Inspirational, action-oriented | Growth opportunities, support, innovation |
| **Cooperative Leaders** | Facebook | Professional, collaborative | Compliance, capacity-building, success metrics |
| **Youth/Students** | TikTok, Instagram | Engaging, aspirational | Education, careers, opportunities |
| **Government Staff** | Facebook, Email | Formal, regulatory | Policies, implementation, accountability |
| **General Public** | Facebook, Instagram | Accessible, inclusive | Community benefits, cultural celebration |
| **Media/Stakeholders** | All platforms | Newsworthy, data-backed | Official announcements, statistics, impact |

## Part 6: Content Strategy Examples

### Cooperative Compliance Journey Story

**Context:** Target 50% compliance rate by 2028 (from 11.66%)

**Sample Content Series:**
- Week 1: "From Informal to Compliant: Why Cooperative Registration Matters"
- Week 2: Feature story - "Meet Maria's Cooperative: Her 3-Month Compliance Journey"
- Week 3: Infographic - "5 Steps to Cooperative Compliance"
- Week 4: Q&A - "Your Compliance Questions Answered" (direct engagement)
- Week 5: Success story - "Now Compliant: How This Cooperative Transformed"

**Platform Mix:** Facebook (post + link), Instagram (story + carousel), TikTok (short guide)

### MSME Technology Adoption Story

**Context:** SETUP Program - technology upgrading for MSMEs

**Sample Content Series:**
- Day 1: Problem - "Are your competitors using technology? You could be too."
- Day 2: Solution - "The SETUP Program: Technology Made Affordable"
- Day 3: Testimonial - "How Fatima's Bakery Increased Production by 40%"
- Day 4: How-to - "Simple Tech Tips for Your Small Business"
- Day 5: Call-to-action - "Ready to Upgrade? Here's How to Apply"

**Engagement Hooks:** Poll (have you adopted new tech?), Comments (what tech challenges do you face?), Shares (tag a business friend)

### Economic Growth & Shared Prosperity

**Context:** GRDP 8-9% growth target; poverty reduction goals

**Monthly Theme:** "BARMM Growing Together"

**Sample Posts:**
- "In 2021, 62,166 new jobs were created by MSMEs. Your success is our success."
- "8,427 entrepreneurs registered their businesses in 2021. Will you be next in 2024?"
- "12,214 cooperatives, 1 vision: A prosperous Bangsamoro where all can thrive."
- "Poverty decreased from 29.8% to... We're making progress together."

**Statistics Presentation:** Use infographics, animated graphics, video overlays with data

## Part 7: Crisis & Sensitive Topic Communication

### Framework for Regulatory/Compliance Messages

**Tone:** Supportive, solution-focused (not punitive)

**Structure:**
1. State the issue (compliance gap, low productivity)
2. Explain the why (benefits, legal requirements)
3. Provide support (programs, assistance, deadlines)
4. Show success examples (peer success stories)
5. Clear call-to-action (application process, contact info)

**Example - Cooperative Compliance:**
"We know compliance seems complex, but we're here to help. Through the PESO Project, cooperatives like yours get free technical assistance. See how Mindanao Coop increased compliance in just 6 months [link to story]."

### Conflict-Sensitive Messaging

**Historical Context:** BARMM recovering from decades of conflict

**Content Guidelines:**
- Emphasize peace dividends and new opportunities
- Celebrate reconciliation and unity
- Include diverse voices in storytelling
- Avoid divisive political messaging
- Focus on shared economic and social goals
- Recognize cultural diversity as strength

**Example Strong Message:**
"Peace is opportunity. With stability, businesses grow, farmers thrive, and families prosper. This is our BARMM story."

## Part 8: Analytics & Performance Metrics

### Content Performance KPIs

| Metric | Target Benchmark | BARMM Context |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Engagement Rate** | 3-5% | Monitor by content pillar |
| **Reach** | 10% of followers per post | Account for limited connectivity |
| **Click-through (CTAs)** | 2-3% | Track application/registration clicks |
| **Share Rate** | 1-2% | Community messaging strength |
| **Comment Sentiment** | 80%+ positive | Monitor compliance messaging reception |
| **Video Views** | 50%+ of followers | Prioritize video over text |
| **Mobile Traffic** | 90%+ | Optimize all content for mobile |

### Audience Insights to Track

- Language preference (Filipino vs. English vs. local dialects)
- Device type (mobile dominates)
- Peak engagement times (adjust for prayer times)
- Content type performance (video > carousel > text)
- Geographic reach by province
- Demographic breakdown (age, gender, occupation)

## Summary: Strategic Integration

Social media for BARMM organizations should:

1. **Communicate Clear Development Goals** - Frame all content within BDP 2023-2028 strategic framework
2. **Celebrate Economic Growth** - Highlight macroeconomic progress and shared prosperity
3. **Tell Human Stories** - Feature beneficiaries, entrepreneurs, farmers, and community leaders
4. **Respect Cultural Context** - Integrate Islamic values, local languages, and tradition-sensitive timing
5. **Build Compliance & Capacity** - Support key targets (50% cooperative compliance, MSME formalization, STI adoption)
6. **Enable Digital Inclusion** - Optimize for mobile, low-bandwidth, and geographically remote audiences
7. **Foster Dialogue** - Use social media for two-way engagement, feedback, and community participation
8. **Track Impact** - Monitor metrics that demonstrate progress toward 2028 targets

**Overall Message:** BARMM is empowered, cohesive, and progressive - building a prosperous future together through moral governance, inclusive growth, and shared opportunity.
