## Lifecycle Stages

### Four-Stage Model

```
INCUBATION → BIRTH → NURTURING → MATURITY
     ↓          ↓         ↓          ↓
   Vision     Action    Growth      Scale
```

### Stage 1: Incubation

**Focus**: Vision formation and problem identification

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Key Activities** | Problem identification, desired change conceptualization |
| **Mindset** | Visionary thinking, personal conviction |
| **Outputs** | Clear social problem statement, initial solution concept |
| **Duration** | Weeks to months |

**Key Questions**:
- What social problem needs addressing?
- What change do we want to see?
- Why are we personally motivated?

### Stage 2: Birth

**Focus**: Taking action and building foundation

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Key Activities** | Theory of change development, MVP creation, first beneficiaries |
| **Challenges** | Overcoming critics, self-doubt, resource constraints |
| **Outputs** | Working prototype, initial team, first impact |
| **Duration** | Months to first year |

**Key Questions**:
- How will we create change? (Theory of Change)
- Who are our first beneficiaries?
- What resources do we need?

### Stage 3: Nurturing

**Focus**: Growth and evolution

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Key Activities** | Ecosystem mapping, process refinement, team building |
| **Transition** | Founder shifts from doer to manager |
| **Outputs** | Refined processes, growing team, established relationships |
| **Duration** | Years 1-3 |

**Key Questions**:
- What resources are missing in our ecosystem?
- How do we refine our approach based on learning?
- How do we build the right team?

### Stage 4: Maturity

**Focus**: Consolidation and scaling

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Key Activities** | Impact measurement, stability building, replication planning |
| **Challenges** | Managing competition, maintaining mission |
| **Outputs** | Measurable outcomes, stable operations, scaling strategy |
| **Duration** | Year 3+ |

**Key Questions**:
- How do we measure and communicate impact?
- How do we maintain quality while growing?
- Should we scale up or scale across?

## Feasibility Assessment

### Six Components

| Component | Key Questions | Outputs |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Social Impact** | Who benefits? What changes? How measured? | Impact thesis, ToC, metrics |
| **Market Analysis** | Is there demand? Who are competitors? | Market size, positioning |
| **Financial Viability** | Revenue model? Cost structure? Sustainability? | Financial projections |
| **Operational Plan** | Activities? Resources? Timeline? | Operations plan |
| **Risk Assessment** | What could go wrong? How to mitigate? | Risk matrix |
| **Stakeholder Engagement** | Who are stakeholders? How to engage? | Stakeholder map |

### Social Impact Assessment

**Key Elements**:
1. Target population definition
2. Theory of Change articulation
3. Expected outcomes (short, medium, long-term)
4. Impact measurement plan
5. Baseline data collection

### Market Analysis

**Questions to Answer**:
- Is there demand for this solution?
- What are alternatives/competitors?
- What is our unique value proposition?
- What is the addressable market size?

### Financial Viability

**Revenue Model Options**:

| Model | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Earned Income** | Sales of products/services | Product sales, service fees |
| **Grants** | Foundation/government funding | Project grants, capacity building |
| **Donations** | Individual/corporate giving | Fundraising, CSR partnerships |
| **Hybrid** | Multiple revenue streams | Mix of above |

**Cost Structure Categories**:
- Program delivery costs
- Administrative overhead
- Fundraising costs
- Capital expenditures

### Risk Assessment

**Risk Categories**:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Impact Risk** | Failing to achieve intended outcomes |
| **Financial Risk** | Revenue shortfall, cost overruns |
| **Operational Risk** | Staff turnover, supply chain issues |
| **External Risk** | Policy changes, economic downturn |
| **Reputational Risk** | Negative publicity, mission drift |

## Scaling Strategies

### Two Overarching Approaches

#### 1. Organizational Growth Strategy

Directly expand to reach more beneficiaries.

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Geographic expansion** | Expand to new locations | Proven model, resources available |
| **New products/services** | Develop related offerings | Existing beneficiaries have other needs |
| **Deepening impact** | Serve same beneficiaries more intensively | Quality over quantity priority |

#### 2. Ecosystem Growth Strategy

Indirectly scale by strengthening the SE ecosystem.

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Advocacy** | Policy change for systemic impact | Regulatory barriers exist |
| **Knowledge sharing** | Training others to replicate | Limited resources for direct scaling |
| **Coalition building** | Partner with similar organizations | Collective action more effective |

### Scale Up vs Scale Across

| Approach | Description | Control Level | Speed | Cost |
|----------|-------------|---------------|-------|------|
| **Scale Up** | Direct expansion (endogenous growth) | High | Slower | Higher |
| **Scale Across** | Enable others to replicate | Lower | Faster | Lower |

### Scaling Mechanisms

1. **Franchising** - License model to independent operators
2. **Affiliation** - Loose network of similar organizations
3. **Dissemination** - Share knowledge and tools freely
4. **Branching** - Open new locations under same organization
5. **Merger/Acquisition** - Combine with similar organizations

### Key Considerations

**Before Scaling**:
- Is the model proven?
- Can it work in different contexts?
- Do we have the resources?
- Can we maintain quality?
- Will impact increase proportionally?

## Business Model Transformation

### From Traditional to Social Business

**Key Shifts**:

| From | To |
|------|-----|
| Profit maximization | Impact optimization with financial sustainability |
| Shareholder primacy | Stakeholder balance |
| Competition | Coopetition (collaboration + competition) |
| Linear value chain | Circular/regenerative models |

### Transformation Process

1. **Mission Clarification**
   - Define social purpose
   - Identify primary beneficiaries
   - Articulate theory of change

2. **Business Model Redesign**
   - Use Social Business Model Canvas
   - Balance impact and revenue
   - Identify sustainable funding mix

3. **Governance Restructuring**
   - Include stakeholder representation
   - Establish profit distribution limits
   - Create accountability mechanisms

4. **Impact Integration**
   - Embed impact measurement
   - Align incentives with outcomes
   - Report transparently

### Common Transformation Paths

| Starting Point | Transformation |
|----------------|----------------|
| NGO → SE | Add earned income streams |
| Business → SE | Prioritize social mission, limit profit distribution |
| Cooperative → SE | Expand beyond members to community impact |
| Startup → SE | Design for impact from founding |
