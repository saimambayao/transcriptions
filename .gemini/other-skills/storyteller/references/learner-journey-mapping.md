# Learner Journey Mapping for MoroAcademy

This guide provides frameworks and tools for mapping learner journeys in courses and workshops, ensuring learner-centered design that addresses real needs and creates transformative experiences.

## What is Learner Journey Mapping?

A learner journey map is a visual representation of a learner's complete experience—from awareness to completion and beyond. It forces you to shift perspective from **inside-out** (what we want to teach) to **outside-in** (what learners actually experience).

**Key Benefits**:
- Identify pain points and friction in the learning experience
- Design interventions at critical moments
- Improve retention and completion rates
- Create emotionally engaging learning paths
- Align content delivery with learner needs at each stage

## Learner Personas

Before mapping journeys, define who your learners are.

### Creating Learner Personas

A learner persona is a fictional representative of your target audience based on real observations and data.

**Essential Elements**:

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Name & Photo** | Humanize the persona | "Fatima, 35" |
| **Role** | Job title or position | Coop Treasurer |
| **Background** | Education, experience | 5 years in coop, no formal accounting |
| **Goals** | What they want to achieve | Pass mandatory training, improve reports |
| **Pain Points** | Current challenges | Time constraints, tech anxiety |
| **Motivators** | What drives them | Career growth, member service |
| **Learning Preferences** | How they prefer to learn | Visual, short sessions, mobile |
| **Barriers** | What might prevent success | Slow internet, family responsibilities |

### Mapping Template

For each stage, document:

| Stage | Learner Goals | Actions | Touchpoints | Emotions | Pain Points | Opportunities |
|-------|---------------|---------|-------------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Awareness | Find relevant training | Search, ask colleagues | Google, social media, word of mouth | Curious, uncertain | Too many options, unclear quality | Clear value proposition |
| Consider | Evaluate fit | Review course details, check reviews | Course page, testimonials | Hopeful, skeptical | Unclear outcomes, time commitment | Preview content, outcomes |
| Enroll | Sign up easily | Create account, pay | Registration form | Committed, anxious | Complex forms, payment issues | Streamlined process |
| Onboard | Get started | First login, orientation | Welcome email, platform | Excited, overwhelmed | Tech confusion, navigation | Clear first steps |
| Learn | Master content | Watch videos, do quizzes | LMS, content pages | Engaged, frustrated | Technical issues, difficult content | Progress indicators |
| Complete | Earn certificate | Pass final assessment | Certificate, portal | Accomplished, relieved | Unclear requirements | Celebration, recognition |
| Apply | Use skills | Implement at work | Job tasks | Confident, challenged | Gap between learning and reality | Follow-up support |
| Advocate | Share experience | Recommend to others | Social, conversations | Proud, grateful | No prompts to share | Easy sharing tools |

## The Transformation Framework

Design courses around learner transformation—the journey from Before State to After State.

### Before/After State Mapping

| Dimension | BEFORE State | AFTER State |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Knowledge** | Doesn't know procedures | Can explain and apply procedures |
| **Skills** | Cannot perform task | Performs task independently |
| **Confidence** | Unsure, hesitant | Confident, decisive |
| **Behavior** | Avoids responsibility | Takes initiative |
| **Identity** | "I'm not a leader" | "I am a capable leader" |
| **Status** | Non-compliant | Certified, compliant |

### Transformation Statement Template

```
TRANSFORMATION STATEMENT

Before this [course/workshop], learners [describe current state].

After completing this [course/workshop], learners will:
- KNOW: [knowledge outcomes]
- DO: [skill outcomes]
- FEEL: [emotional/confidence outcomes]
- BE: [identity/status outcomes]
```

**Example: COOP-101 Fundamentals**

> Before this course, learners have limited understanding of cooperative principles and may not recognize their role in cooperative development.
>
> After completing this course, learners will:
> - KNOW: The 7 cooperative principles and their applications
> - DO: Explain cooperative difference to members and community
> - FEEL: Proud of cooperative identity, connected to movement
> - BE: Informed cooperative advocates

## Seven-Step Transformative Learning Journey

Based on academic research on transformative education:

| Step | Focus | Activities |
|------|-------|------------|
| **1. Excavate** | Surface existing beliefs/assumptions | Reflection exercises, pre-assessments |
| **2. Absorb** | Take in new perspectives | Lectures, readings, expert input |
| **3. Experience** | Engage directly with concepts | Simulations, scenarios, practice |
| **4. Observe** | Notice patterns and reactions | Journaling, group observation |
| **5. Deepen** | Explore implications fully | Discussion, analysis, questioning |
| **6. Exchange** | Share and test with others | Peer feedback, presentations |
| **7. Consolidate** | Integrate into identity | Action planning, commitment |

## Practical Tools

### Journey Mapping Workshop (60-90 min)

**Materials**: Large paper/whiteboard, sticky notes, markers

**Steps**:
1. **Define Scope** (10 min): Which course/workshop? Which persona?
2. **Map Stages** (15 min): Identify key phases along timeline
3. **Walk Through** (30 min): For each stage, add:
   - Goals (green sticky)
   - Actions (yellow sticky)
   - Emotions (blue sticky)
   - Pain Points (red sticky)
   - Opportunities (orange sticky)
4. **Analyze** (15 min): Identify critical moments, major pain points
5. **Prioritize** (10 min): Select top 3 improvements to implement

- **UXPressia**: Online journey mapping with templates
- **Miro/FigJam**: Collaborative whiteboard for mapping
- **Notion**: Documentation and persona management

## BARMM Context: Bangsamoro Learner Journey Considerations

### Understanding the Bangsamoro Learner

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) has unique characteristics that shape learner journeys. According to the BDP 2023-2028, the region comprises at least 13 ethnocultural groups including Maguindanaon, Meranao, Tausug, Yakan, Iranun, and non-Moro indigenous peoples.

**Key Contextual Factors**:
1. **Geographic Diversity**: Island provinces (Sulu, Basilan, Tawi-Tawi) and mainland provinces (Maguindanao, Lanao del Sur) present different accessibility challenges
2. **Infrastructure Variability**: Some areas have limited electricity and internet access
3. **Language Diversity**: Multiple local dialects require translation considerations
4. **Cultural Identity**: Strong cultural and Islamic values influence learning preferences
5. **Intergenerational Dynamics**: Respect for elders and cultural masters shapes learning relationships

### BARMM Learner Personas

- **Pain Points**: Inconsistent internet, limited formal training, distance from mainland
- **Motivators**: Family livelihood, community service, Islamic values
- **Learning Preference**: Face-to-face workshops, mobile-friendly content, practical examples
- **Barriers**: Slow/expensive internet, travel constraints, competing family responsibilities
- **Cultural Element**: Maritime trading traditions, honor-based relationships

### BARMM Empathy Map Considerations

When creating empathy maps for Bangsamoro learners, consider these additional quadrants:

| Quadrant | BARMM-Specific Considerations |
|----------|------------------------------|
| **HEAR** | Community elders' guidance; Religious leaders' advice; Dialect-specific communications; Radio/TV announcements in local language |
| **SEE** | Government programs in Tagalog/English; Successful coops in other regions; Local success stories; Cultural events and gatherings |
| **THINK** | "Will this respect our traditions?"; "Is this halal/Islamic-compliant?"; "Will my community benefit?"; "Can I do this without leaving my family?" |
| **SAY/DO** | Consult with family before decisions; Seek community consensus; Prefer face-to-face over online; Use Messenger groups for coordination |
| **PAINS** | Geographic isolation; Infrastructure challenges; Language barriers; Post-conflict trauma in some areas; Limited formal education access |
| **GAINS** | Community recognition; Family pride; Islamic values alignment; Economic improvement; Cultural preservation |

### Journey Stage Adaptations for BARMM

#### Awareness Stage - BARMM Context

| Touchpoint | BARMM Adaptation |
|------------|------------------|
| **Word of Mouth** | Leverage community gatherings, mosque announcements, barangay meetings |
| **Social Media** | Focus on Facebook (most used platform); content in local dialects |
| **Radio** | Critical for remote areas; partner with local stations |
| **Government Channels** | BPDA, CSEA, provincial/municipal announcements |
| **Print Materials** | Posters, flyers translated to local dialects for areas without electricity |

#### Enrollment Stage - BARMM Context

| Challenge | Adaptation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Complex Forms** | Simplify registration; offer offline registration at LGU offices |
| **Payment Issues** | Provide GCash/Maya options; consider subsidized/free courses |
| **Tech Barriers** | Phone-based registration via SMS or call; assisted registration at CSEA offices |
| **Language** | Registration materials in Maguindanaon, Meranao, Tausug, and other dialects |

#### Learning Stage - BARMM Context

| Challenge | Adaptation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Connectivity** | Downloadable content for offline viewing; low-bandwidth video options |
| **Language** | Subtitles/dubbing in local dialects; bilingual facilitators for live sessions |
| **Scheduling** | Respect prayer times; avoid Friday noon sessions; consider agricultural seasons |
| **Content** | Local case studies; culturally familiar scenarios; Islamic business ethics integration |
| **Platform** | Mobile-first design; minimal data usage; text-based alternatives |

#### Completion Stage - BARMM Context

| Consideration | Approach |
|---------------|----------|
| **Certificate Significance** | Physical certificates valued; public recognition at community events |
| **Celebration** | Graduation ceremonies with cultural elements; family involvement |
| **Sharing** | Encourage peer teaching; create trainer-of-trainers pathway |
| **Follow-up** | WhatsApp/Messenger groups for alumni; regional meetups |

### BARMM Learner Journey Barriers and Solutions

| Barrier | Impact | Solution |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Geographic Isolation** | Cannot attend live workshops | Hybrid delivery; local satellite sessions; recorded content |
| **Connectivity Issues** | Cannot complete online courses | Offline downloads; SMS-based progress; USB content distribution |
| **Language Barriers** | Difficulty understanding content | Multi-dialect translations; local facilitators; visual-heavy content |
| **Time Constraints** | Competing livelihood demands | Flexible deadlines; micro-learning modules; evening/weekend options |
| **Family Responsibilities** | Cannot travel for training | Family-inclusive events; childcare considerations; home-based learning |
| **Post-Conflict Trauma** | Difficulty focusing; trust issues | Trauma-informed facilitation; peer support; culturally sensitive content |
| **Formal Education Gaps** | Difficulty with academic content | Practical focus; minimal jargon; visual aids; peer tutoring |

### Key Touchpoints for Bangsamoro Learners

#### High-Impact Moments

1. **Community Recommendation**: When trusted community member recommends program
2. **First Login Success**: Overcoming tech anxiety with smooth experience
3. **First "Aha" Moment**: Connecting content to real community challenge
4. **Peer Connection**: Meeting others with similar challenges in forums/groups
5. **Certificate Moment**: Public recognition of achievement
6. **First Application Success**: Using learned skill to solve real problem

#### Critical Risk Points

1. **Registration Complexity**: Drop-off due to complicated forms or payment
2. **Tech Frustration**: Abandonment due to platform difficulties
3. **Connectivity Failure**: Cannot complete due to infrastructure
4. **Content Disconnect**: Leaving because examples seem irrelevant
5. **Time Pressure**: Dropping out due to livelihood/family demands
6. **Post-Completion Gap**: Not applying learning due to lack of support
