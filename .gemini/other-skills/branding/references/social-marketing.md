## Social Marketing and Branding

### Definition

Social Marketing uses marketing principles to influence behavior for social good. For branding, this means building brand identity and equity around positive behavior change.

### Social Marketing vs Commercial Branding

| Dimension | Commercial Branding | Social Marketing Branding |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Goal** | Brand preference, purchase | Behavior adoption, social change |
| **Promise** | Product/service benefits | Personal/social benefits |
| **Competition** | Other brands | Existing behaviors, inertia |
| **Success** | Market share, loyalty | Behavior change, impact |

### Brand Role in Social Marketing

| Brand Element | Social Marketing Application |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Brand Identity** | Embody desired behavior and values |
| **Brand Positioning** | Position as enabler of positive change |
| **Brand Messaging** | Communicate benefits of behavior |
| **Brand Experience** | Make behavior easy, rewarding |

## Cause Branding

### What is Cause Branding?

Cause branding integrates a social cause into the core brand identity, not as an add-on but as a fundamental brand element.

### Cause Branding Spectrum

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Cause-Related Marketing** | Temporary campaign tie-in | "Buy one, donate one" |
| **Cause Branding** | Cause integrated into brand | TOMS, Patagonia |
| **Social Purpose Brand** | Brand exists for the cause | Charity: water, Grameen Bank |

### Cause Branding for BARMM Sectors

| Sector | Cause Branding Approach |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Cooperatives** | Brand around cooperative values, community ownership |
| **Social Enterprises** | Brand around social mission, impact story |
| **Government** | Brand around public service, citizen empowerment |

### Authenticity Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Alignment** | Cause must connect to core business |
| **Commitment** | Long-term, not opportunistic |
| **Transparency** | Clear about impact and limitations |
| **Consistency** | Reflected in all brand touchpoints |

## Brand Positioning for Behavior Change

### Positioning Statement for Social Marketing

```
For [target audience with current behavior],
[Brand] is the [category]
that helps you [desired behavior/benefit]
because [reason to believe/unique value].
```

**Example (Cooperative):**
```
For Bangsamoro farmers seeking fair prices,
[Coop Name] is the member-owned cooperative
that helps you access markets and grow together
because every member has a voice and shares in success.
```

### Points of Parity and Difference

| Type | Commercial Brand | Social Marketing Brand |
|------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Points of Parity** | Match competitor basics | Make new behavior as easy as old |
| **Points of Difference** | Unique selling proposition | Unique social/personal benefits |

### Behavior-Based Positioning

Position brand based on the behavior it enables:

| Positioning Approach | Example |
|---------------------|---------|
| **Enabler** | "We make recycling easy" |
| **Supporter** | "We're with you on this journey" |
| **Community** | "Join others who care" |
| **Aspirational** | "Be the change you want to see" |

## Social Proof and Norms

### Social Norms in Branding

Social norms messaging shows that the desired behavior is common, approved, and expected.

### Types of Social Norms

| Type | Definition | Brand Application |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Descriptive** | What most people do | "9 out of 10 members participate" |
| **Injunctive** | What people approve of | "Our community values..." |
| **Dynamic** | Trends toward behavior | "More families are joining" |

### Social Proof Elements

| Element | Brand Integration |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Testimonials** | Member stories, beneficiary voices |
| **Numbers** | "Join 10,000+ members" |
| **Endorsements** | Community leaders, influencers |
| **Certifications** | Trust marks, quality seals |
| **User-Generated Content** | Community participation, sharing |

### BARMM Social Proof Strategies

| Sector | Social Proof Approach |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Cooperatives** | Member success stories, community endorsements |
| **Social Enterprises** | Beneficiary transformations, impact numbers |
| **Government** | Citizen testimonials, program outcomes |

## Impact Messaging

### Impact Storytelling Framework

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **The Problem** | Challenge faced | "Before, families had no access to..." |
| **The Solution** | Behavior/intervention | "Through [brand], they now..." |
| **The Impact** | Measurable outcome | "Today, 500 families..." |
| **The Invitation** | Call to action | "Join us to..." |

### Impact Hierarchy

| Level | What to Communicate |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Output** | What was done (activities, products) |
| **Outcome** | What changed (behavior, knowledge) |
| **Impact** | Ultimate difference made (lives changed) |

### Emotional vs Rational Appeals

| Appeal Type | When to Use | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Emotional** | Building connection, motivation | Personal stories, imagery |
| **Rational** | Overcoming skepticism, proof | Statistics, evidence |
| **Combination** | Most effective | "Meet Maria... she's one of 500 who..." |

### Impact Messaging for BARMM

| Sector | Key Impact Messages |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Cooperatives** | Member empowerment, community prosperity, democratic participation |
| **Social Enterprises** | Lives changed, livelihoods created, traditions preserved |
| **Government** | Citizens served, communities developed, peace built |

## Brand Identity for Social Marketing

### Kapferer Prism for Social Marketing

| Facet | Commercial Brand | Social Marketing Adaptation |
|-------|------------------|----------------------------|
| **Physique** | Product appearance | Behavior visibility, tools |
| **Personality** | Brand character | Supportive, empowering |
| **Culture** | Brand values | Social values, community |
| **Relationship** | Brand-customer bond | Partnership for change |
| **Reflection** | Typical user | Positive role model |
| **Self-Image** | How user feels | Proud, responsible, empowered |

| Element | Social Marketing Approach |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Colors** | Positive, culturally appropriate |
| **Imagery** | Real people, authentic moments |
| **Typography** | Accessible, clear |
| **Symbols** | Aspirational, inclusive |
