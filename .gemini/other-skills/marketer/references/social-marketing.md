## Definition and Distinction

### What is Social Marketing?

"The use of marketing principles and techniques to influence a target audience to voluntarily accept, reject, modify or abandon a behavior for the benefit of individuals, groups or society as a whole." — Kotler, Roberto, and Lee (2002)

### Social Marketing vs Commercial Marketing

| Dimension | Commercial Marketing | Social Marketing |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Purpose** | Profit, customer satisfaction | Positive social change |
| **What is sold** | Products and services | Behaviors, ideas, attitudes |
| **Primary users** | For-profit businesses | Non-profits, government, NGOs |
| **Beneficiary** | The organization | Individuals and society |
| **Competition** | Other brands | Existing behaviors, apathy |
| **Timeframe** | Immediate monetary returns | Medium/long-term social benefits |

### Social Marketing vs Social Media Marketing

| Social Marketing | Social Media Marketing |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Behavior change for social good | Promotion using social platforms |
| May or may not use social media | Always uses social platforms |
| Rooted in public health, social policy | Rooted in digital marketing |
| Success = behavior adoption | Success = engagement, conversions |

## Key Frameworks

### Andreasen's Six Benchmark Criteria (2002)

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Behavior Objective** | Clear focus on achieving specific behavioral goals |
| **2. Audience Segmentation** | Targeting specific audience segments |
| **3. Formative Research** | Research to understand the audience |
| **4. Exchange** | Evidence of exchange concept (benefits vs costs) |
| **5. Marketing Mix** | Multiple marketing mix elements used |
| **6. Competition** | Consideration of competing behaviors |

### NSMC Eight Benchmark Criteria

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Behaviour** | Specific measurable behavioral goals |
| **2. Customer Orientation** | Focus on audience's lives and context |
| **3. Theory** | Use behavioral theories to inform approach |
| **4. Insight** | Actionable insights from research |
| **5. Exchange** | Benefits and costs of behavior change |
| **6. Competition** | Address internal and external competing factors |
| **7. Methods Mix** | Multiple methods to bring about change |
| **8. Segmentation** | Target specific audience segments |

### Kotler-Lee 10-Step Planning Model

```
1. Describe social issue, background, purpose, focus
2. Conduct SWOT analysis
3. Segment market and choose target audience
4. Select desired behavior (behavioral objective)
5. Identify barriers, benefits, motivators
6. Craft positioning statement
7. Develop strategic marketing mix (4Ps)
8. Develop evaluation plan
9. Establish budgets and funding
10. Outline implementation plan
```

## Behavior Change Models

### Stages of Change (Transtheoretical Model)

| Stage | Description | Social Marketing Approach |
|-------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Precontemplation** | Unaware behavior is problematic | Raise awareness, risk communication |
| **Contemplation** | Weighing pros and cons | Address barriers, highlight benefits |
| **Preparation** | Ready to act soon | Provide tools, remove obstacles |
| **Action** | Recently changed behavior | Support, reinforce, celebrate |
| **Maintenance** | Sustained change (6+ months) | Prevent relapse, build habits |

### Health Belief Model

| Construct | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Perceived Susceptibility** | Communicate personal risk |
| **Perceived Severity** | Show consequences of inaction |
| **Perceived Benefits** | Highlight positive outcomes |
| **Perceived Barriers** | Address obstacles to adoption |
| **Cues to Action** | Provide triggers and reminders |
| **Self-Efficacy** | Build confidence to take action |

### Theory of Planned Behavior

| Predictor | Intervention Strategy |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Attitude** | Change beliefs about behavior outcomes |
| **Subjective Norms** | Influence perceived social pressure |
| **Perceived Control** | Enhance confidence and capability |

### Nudge Theory

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Defaults** | Opt-out vs opt-in (organ donation) |
| **Social Norms** | "Most people in your community..." |
| **Simplification** | Remove steps, make easy |
| **Timing** | Intervene at decision points |
| **Feedback** | Show progress, consequences |

## Social Marketing Mix

### Product (The Behavior)

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Core** | Benefits audience wants | Peace of mind, health |
| **Actual** | The behavior itself | Getting vaccinated |
| **Augmented** | Supporting products/services | Vaccination sites, reminders |

### Price (Barriers and Costs)

| Cost Type | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| **Monetary** | Financial expenses |
| **Time** | Hours required |
| **Effort** | Physical/mental energy |
| **Psychological** | Discomfort, fear, embarrassment |
| **Social** | Disapproval, stigma |
| **Opportunity** | What must be given up |

**Strategy**: Reduce real and perceived costs; provide incentives.

### Place (Access and Convenience)

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Physical locations** | Where can behavior be performed? |
| **Timing** | When is it accessible? |
| **Channels** | How do people access it? |
| **Convenience** | How easy is it? |

### Promotion (Communications)

| Channel | Use For |
|---------|---------|
| **Advertising** | Awareness, attitudes |
| **Public relations** | Credibility, reach |
| **Personal outreach** | Persuasion, support |
| **Social media** | Engagement, norms |
| **Events** | Experience, community |

## Campaign Planning

### Social Marketing Campaign Template

```
1. DEFINE THE ISSUE
   - What social problem are we addressing?
   - What is the current behavior?
   - What is the desired behavior?

2. RESEARCH THE AUDIENCE
   - Who is the primary audience?
   - What are their barriers to change?
   - What motivates them?
   - What stage of change are they in?

3. SET BEHAVIORAL OBJECTIVES
   - Specific behavior to change
   - Measurable target
   - Timeframe

4. DEVELOP THE STRATEGY
   - Product: What benefits do we offer?
   - Price: How do we reduce costs?
   - Place: How do we make it accessible?
   - Promotion: How do we communicate?

5. IMPLEMENT AND EVALUATE
   - Process evaluation (activities)
   - Outcome evaluation (behavior change)
   - Impact evaluation (social outcomes)
```

### Evaluation Metrics

| Level | Metrics |
|-------|---------|
| **Reach** | Audience exposure, awareness |
| **Engagement** | Interaction, participation |
| **Behavior Change** | Adoption rates, compliance |
| **Sustainability** | Maintenance, relapse prevention |
| **Impact** | Health outcomes, social change |
