## Assumption Testing

### What is an Assumption?

> "An assumption is something you believe to be true but have not yet proven with evidence."

The cost of testing is small. The cost of building on a wrong assumption is enormous.

### Common Business Assumptions

| Canvas Block | Example Assumption |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **Problem** | "Customers struggle to find affordable halal food" |
| **Customer** | "Office workers aged 25-45 are my target" |
| **Value Proposition** | "Authentic, affordable, delicious food" |
| **Revenue** | "Customers will pay PHP 15 per piece" |
| **Channels** | "Office delivery is where customers will find me" |

### Extracting Assumptions from Lean Canvas

Review each block of your Lean Canvas and ask: "What am I assuming to be true?"

| Block | Question to Ask |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Problem** | Is this really a problem? How often? How painful? |
| **Customer** | Are these really my customers? Can I reach them? |
| **Solution** | Will this solution actually work? |
| **Value Proposition** | Do customers care about this benefit? |
| **Channels** | Will customers find me here? |
| **Revenue** | Will they pay this price? |
| **Cost** | Can I produce at this cost? |
| **Metrics** | Are these the right things to measure? |

## Risk Scoring

### Risk Score Formula

```
RISK SCORE = IMPORTANCE × UNCERTAINTY
```

| Factor | Scale | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Importance** | 1-3 | 1 = Nice to have, 3 = Critical (business fails if wrong) |
| **Uncertainty** | 1-3 | 1 = Confident (have evidence), 3 = Very uncertain (guessing) |

### Risk Prioritization

| Score | Risk Level | Action |
|-------|------------|--------|
| 7-9 | High | Test FIRST |
| 4-6 | Medium | Test SECOND |
| 1-3 | Low | Test LATER |

### Example: Risk Score Calculation

| Assumption | Imp | Unc | Score |
|------------|-----|-----|-------|
| Workers prefer home-style meals | 3 | 2 | 6 |
| PHP 15 is acceptable price | 3 | 3 | **9** |
| Office location is best | 3 | 2 | 6 |
| Banana leaf packaging OK | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Word of mouth works | 2 | 3 | 6 |
| Can produce 250/day | 2 | 1 | 2 |

**Priority Order**: Test price first (Score 9), then location and home-style preference (Score 6).

## The Mom Test

### The Problem with Direct Questions

> When you ask "Would you buy this?", everyone says yes. Even your mom. She loves you. Her encouragement does not predict sales.

### The Mom Test Principle

> "Talk about THEIR past behavior, not YOUR idea."

People cannot predict the future. But they can accurately describe the past.

### Bad vs. Good Questions

| Bad (Hypothetical) | Good (Past Behavior) |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| "Would you buy this?" | "What did you eat yesterday?" |
| "Do you think halal is important?" | "When you buy food, what do you look for?" |
| "Would you switch from siopao?" | "Why did you buy that specific siopao?" |
| "Would you pay PHP 15?" | "How much did you spend on lunch yesterday?" |
| "Do you like this feature?" | "When did you last use something like this?" |

### Question Design by Assumption

| Assumption | Mom Test Question |
|------------|-------------------|
| Workers prefer home-style | "When you buy food, what makes it feel like home?" |
| PHP 15 is acceptable | "How much did you spend on lunch yesterday?" |
| Location is good | "Where do you usually buy lunch?" |
| Need for halal | "What do you look for when buying food?" |

### Interview Tips

**Do:**
- Ask about past behavior ("When did you last...")
- Listen more than you talk
- Ask follow-up questions ("Tell me more about...")
- Take notes on specific facts and stories
- Look for patterns across multiple interviews

**Don't:**
- Ask hypothetical questions ("Would you...")
- Pitch your idea too early
- Lead the witness ("Don't you think...")
- Accept vague answers ("it depends")
- Interrupt or argue

## 5 Validation Methods

### Method Overview

| Method | Purpose | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Interviews** | Deep understanding of needs | Problem validation, customer insights |
| **Observation** | Seeing actual behavior | Behavior patterns, traffic counting |
| **Surveys** | Getting data from many people | Quantitative validation, preferences |
| **Competitor Analysis** | Understanding alternatives | Market positioning, gaps |
| **Small Tests** | Testing willingness to pay | Pre-orders, MVPs, landing pages |

### 1. Interviews

**When to use**: Validating problem, understanding customer needs, exploring motivations.

**Sample size**: 10-15 interviews for pattern recognition.

**Tips**:
- Schedule 20-30 minute conversations
- Use Mom Test questions
- Record (with permission) or take detailed notes
- Look for patterns across interviews

### 2. Observation

**When to use**: Validating behavior, counting traffic, understanding context.

**Examples**:
- Count foot traffic at a potential location
- Watch how customers use competitor products
- Observe decision-making in stores

**Tips**:
- Be systematic (same time, same location)
- Record quantitative data (counts, times)
- Note qualitative observations (behaviors, expressions)

### 3. Surveys

**When to use**: Validating at scale, after initial qualitative research.

**Tools**: Google Forms, Typeform, Facebook polls.

**Tips**:
- Keep it short (5-10 questions max)
- Use multiple choice where possible
- Include one or two open-ended questions
- Aim for 50+ responses for statistical relevance

### 4. Competitor Analysis

**When to use**: Understanding market, identifying gaps, setting pricing.

| Analysis Area | Questions |
|---------------|-----------|
| **Products** | What do they offer? Quality? |
| **Pricing** | How much do they charge? |
| **Place** | Where do they sell? |
| **Promotion** | How do they market? |
| **Strengths** | What do they do well? |
| **Weaknesses** | Where do they fall short? |

### 5. Small Tests (Pre-selling)

**When to use**: Validating willingness to pay, demand.

**Examples**:
- Pre-orders with deposits
- Landing page with sign-up
- Free samples → paid conversion
- Pop-up sales events

**Success metrics**:
- Conversion rate from interest to payment
- Number of pre-orders achieved
- Deposit collection rate

## Validation Planning

### Validation Plan Template

| Priority | Assumption | Method | Action | Success Metric |
|----------|------------|--------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Price is OK | Interview + Test | Ask spending + Offer pre-order | 15/20 accept |
| 2 | Need exists | Interview | Ask decision criteria | 7/10 mention |
| 3 | Location works | Observation | Count traffic | 500+/hour |

### Timeline Example

| Week | Activity | Success If |
|------|----------|------------|
| Week 1 | Interview 10 workers | 7/10 mention key problem |
| Week 2 | Observe location entrance | 500+ workers/hour |
| Week 3 | Offer 20 pre-orders | 15+ paid orders |

### Decision Framework

| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Strong validation | Proceed with confidence |
| Partial validation | Adjust assumption, retest |
| No validation | Pivot or abandon assumption |

## Cooperative Networks for Validation

### Built-in Validation Partners

Cooperatives have a unique advantage: they operate within networks where validation can happen directly.

### Who Validates Who?

| Coop Type | Validates With | Example Question |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Agricultural** | Producers Coop | "Is there demand for our corn?" |
| **Fishermen** | Producers Coop | "What seafood do you need?" |
| **Producers** | Consumer Coop | "What sells best in your store?" |
| **Consumer** | End Customers | "What products are people buying?" |

### Direct Validation Example

```
Agricultural Coop          Producers Coop
     │                           │
     │  "How much rice           │
     │   do you need?"           │
     │ ─────────────────────────►│
     │                           │
     │  "We need 15 cavans/week" │
     │ ◄─────────────────────────│
     │                           │
```

### Network Validation Advantages

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Immediate feedback** | No need for cold outreach |
| **Trust built in** | Fellow cooperative members |
| **Quantifiable demand** | Specific numbers, not guesses |
| **Reduced risk** | Validate before production |

## Quick Reference

### Validation Checklist

```
□ List 5-6 key assumptions from canvas
□ Calculate Risk Score for each (Imp × Unc)
□ Identify top 3 riskiest assumptions
□ Design Mom Test questions for each
□ Select validation method for each
□ Create validation timeline
□ Define success metrics
□ Conduct validation activities
□ Analyze results and decide
```

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Asking leading questions | Use Mom Test format |
| Too few interviews | Aim for 10-15 minimum |
| Confirming bias | Actively seek disconfirming evidence |
| No success metrics | Define before testing |
| Ignoring negative feedback | Document and analyze all feedback |
