# Bangsamoro Context and Reference Information

## Bangsamoro Autonomous Region Overview

### Key Terminology

- **Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL)** - The primary law establishing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), signed into law in 2018 and ratified in 2019.
- **Chief Minister** - The head of the Bangsamoro Government, equivalent to a regional executive.
- **Ministries** - Executive departments of the Bangsamoro Government (use "Ministry," not "Department").
- **Parliament structure** - Unicameral legislative body consisting of elected and reserved-seat members.
- **Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA)** - The transitional parliamentary body (2019-2025) that preceded the regular Bangsamoro Parliament.

### Cultural and Linguistic Context

- **Languages** - Resolutions may reference concepts in Filipino, Arabic, and English, reflecting the multilingual nature of the Bangsamoro.
- **Cultural sensitivity** - Respect Islamic and indigenous traditions when drafting resolutions. Consider religious holidays, cultural practices, and traditional governance systems.
- **Indigenous peoples** - The region includes various indigenous groups (Teduray, Lambangian, Dulangan Manobo, etc.) with distinct cultures and governance systems.
- **Traditional leaders** - Include traditional datus, sultans, and other community leaders when appropriate.

### Government Structure

**Executive Branch:**
- Office of the Chief Minister
- Various Ministries (e.g., Ministry of Health, Ministry of Education, Ministry of Public Works)
- Bangsamoro Government agencies and offices

**Legislative Branch:**
- Bangsamoro Parliament (unicameral)
- Parliamentary committees
- Office of the Parliament Secretary-General

**Judicial Branch:**
- Shari'ah courts (for personal, family, and property law among Muslims)
- Regular courts (general jurisdiction)

## Common Bangsamoro Organic Law Citations

Reference these BOL provisions when drafting resolutions:

### Parliamentary Powers and Procedures

**Article VII, Section 5 - Rules of Procedure**
- Parliament may adopt its own rules of procedure
- Use for: Simple resolutions adopting or amending parliamentary rules

**Article VII, Section 10 - Power of Inquiry**
- Parliament may conduct inquiries in aid of legislation
- Parliament may require appearance of witnesses and production of documents
- Use for: Policy/statement resolutions directing investigations

**Article VII, Section 11 - Question Hour**
- Parliament may require officials to appear and answer questions
- Use for: Resolutions summoning officials for questioning

### Budget and Finance

**Article IX, Section 12 - Appropriations**
- Annual budget must originate from the Parliament
- No money shall be paid from the Bangsamoro treasury except in pursuance of an appropriation
- Use for: Budget resolutions, appropriations, and financial authorizations

**Article IX, Section 13 - Supplemental Appropriations**
- Parliament may authorize supplemental appropriations for urgent and unforeseen expenditures
- Use for: Emergency budget resolutions

### General Powers

**Article VI, Section 2 - Reserved Powers**
- Parliament has general legislative powers not reserved to national government
- Use for: Resolutions asserting parliamentary authority on regional matters

**Article VI, Section 3 - Exclusive Powers**
- Lists specific exclusive powers (agriculture, trade, social services, etc.)
- Use for: Resolutions within Parliament's exclusive jurisdiction

### Other Relevant Provisions

**Article VII, Section 7 - Attendance and Quorum**
- Parliamentary quorum and voting requirements
- Use for: Resolutions related to parliamentary sessions

**Article VII, Section 15 - Parliamentary Immunity**
- Members' privilege from arrest and free speech protection
- Use for: Resolutions asserting parliamentary privilege

**Article XII - Transitional Provisions**
- Provisions specific to the transition period
- Use for: Resolutions during the transition phase (if still applicable)

## Legal Research Hierarchy

When researching and citing legal authorities for resolutions, follow this hierarchy:

1. **Constitution of the Philippines (1987)**
   - Supreme law of the land
   - Available at: officialgazette.gov.ph, lawphil.net

2. **Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054)**
   - Primary legal authority for all BARMM matters
   - Establishes the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
   - Available at: officialgazette.gov.ph, lawphil.net

3. **Other National Laws**
   - Republic Acts and other national legislation
   - Presidential Decrees, Executive Orders (as applicable)
   - Available at: officialgazette.gov.ph, lawphil.net

4. **Bangsamoro Administrative Code**
   - Codified administrative regulations of BARMM
   - Regional administrative framework

5. **Other Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts**
   - Legislation enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament
   - Regional laws specific to BARMM

- Cotabato City (joined BARMM in 2019 plebiscite)
- 63 villages in North Cotabato (joined BARMM in 2019 plebiscite)

## Historical Context

- **MNLF Peace Agreement (1996)** - Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) established
- **GPH-MILF Peace Process** - Led to the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) in 2014
- **Bangsamoro Organic Law (2018)** - Signed into law, creating BARMM to replace ARMM
- **Plebiscite (2019)** - Ratification of BOL in two stages
- **Bangsamoro Transition Authority (2019-2025)** - Transitional parliamentary body
- **First regular parliamentary elections** - Expected in 2025

Use this historical context when drafting resolutions that reference the peace process, transition, or establishment of BARMM.
