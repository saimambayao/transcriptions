## What is a Startup?

> "A startup is a human institution designed to create a new product or service under conditions of extreme uncertainty." — Eric Ries, The Lean Startup

Startups differ from established businesses in their:
1. **Uncertainty** - Business model not yet validated
2. **Search mode** - Looking for repeatable, scalable model
3. **Speed** - Rapid iteration and learning cycles
4. **Resource constraints** - Limited time, money, people

### Startup vs. Small Business vs. Enterprise

| Dimension | Startup | Small Business | Enterprise |
|-----------|---------|----------------|------------|
| **Goal** | Scale rapidly | Steady income | Optimize operations |
| **Model** | Searching | Known | Established |
| **Growth** | Exponential target | Linear/stable | Incremental |
| **Risk** | High uncertainty | Moderate | Low |
| **Skill** | `/startup` | N/A | `/enterprise-dev` |

## Quick Reference

### The Lean Startup Cycle

```text
       ┌─────────────┐
       │    IDEAS    │
       └──────┬──────┘
              │ Build
              ▼
       ┌─────────────┐
       │   PRODUCT   │ ◄─── MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
       └──────┬──────┘
              │ Measure
              ▼
       ┌─────────────┐
       │    DATA     │ ◄─── Validated Learning
       └──────┬──────┘
              │ Learn
              ▼
       ┌─────────────┐
       │  PIVOT or   │
       │  PERSEVERE  │
       └─────────────┘
```

### Startup Stages (0-5 Years)

| Stage | Focus | Key Question | Duration |
|-------|-------|--------------|----------|
| **1. Problem-Solution Fit** | Validate problem exists | Is this worth solving? | 0-6 months |
| **2. Product-Market Fit** | Validate solution works | Do people want this? | 6-18 months |
| **3. Scale** | Grow efficiently | Can we grow profitably? | 18-36 months |
| **4. Maturity** | Optimize operations | How do we sustain? | 36+ months |

### AARRR Pirate Metrics

| Metric | Question | Example KPIs |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Acquisition** | How do users find us? | Visits, signups, CAC |
| **Activation** | Do they have a great first experience? | Onboarding completion, first action |
| **Retention** | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, churn rate |
| **Revenue** | Do they pay? | Conversion, ARPU, LTV |
| **Referral** | Do they tell others? | NPS, viral coefficient |

## Core Frameworks

### Lean Startup (Eric Ries)

**Core Principles:**
1. **Entrepreneurs are everywhere** - Startups can exist in any organization
2. **Entrepreneurship is management** - A startup needs adaptive management
3. **Validated learning** - Progress through learning, not deliverables
4. **Build-Measure-Learn** - Iterate through the feedback loop
5. **Innovation accounting** - Measure progress with actionable metrics

See [references/startup-frameworks.md](references/startup-frameworks.md) for detailed frameworks.

### Customer Development (Steve Blank)

**Four Steps:**
1. **Customer Discovery** - Validate problem and solution hypotheses
2. **Customer Validation** - Validate sales model and product-market fit
3. **Customer Creation** - Scale customer acquisition
4. **Company Building** - Transition to execution mode

### Product-Market Fit Indicators

**Sean Ellis Test:**
> "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
> - 40%+ "Very disappointed" = PMF achieved

**Other Indicators:**
- Organic growth without marketing
- High retention and engagement
- Word-of-mouth referrals
- Customer willingness to pay
