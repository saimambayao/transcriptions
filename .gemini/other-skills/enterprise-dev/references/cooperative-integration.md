## Cooperative Enterprise Development Pathway

Cooperatives in the Philippines progress through distinct development stages:

| Category | Members | Asset Size |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Micro | <=10 | <=3M |
| Small | >10-100 | >3M-15M |
| Medium | >100-7,500 | >15M-100M |
| Large | >7,500 | >100M |

Each stage requires different enterprise development interventions:
- **Pre-Cooperative**: Community organizing, PRS, formation, registration
- **Micro**: Building membership, basic systems, initial capital, basic governance
- **Small**: Professionalizing management, financial systems, committee development, compliance
- **Medium**: Scaling operations, diversification, professional staff, strategic planning, technology
- **Large**: Federation formation, advocacy, national impact, subsidiaries, international cooperation

## Scaling Cooperatives

### Horizontal Scaling: More Members, Geographic Expansion
- Membership expansion aligned with common bond
- Branch offices, sub-offices, mobile operations
- Challenge: maintaining democratic participation at scale

### Vertical Scaling: Value Chain Integration
- Backward integration (raw materials, shared procurement)
- Forward integration (processing, marketing, direct-to-consumer)
- Full integration (farm-to-table, production-to-retail)

### Diversification: Adding Services/Products

| Type | Description | Diversification Example |
|------|-------------|------------------------|
| Credit | Savings and lending | Add insurance, remittance |
| Consumer | Retail goods | Add production, processing |
| Producer | Production services | Add marketing, credit |
| Marketing | Collective marketing | Add processing, storage |
| Service | Common services | Add credit, consumer store |
| Multi-Purpose | Two or more types | Comprehensive diversification |

### Maintaining Cooperative Identity During Growth

Preservation strategies across governance, member participation, economic participation, values/principles, and accountability. Key principle: innovate in service of cooperative values, not at their expense.

## Federation and Secondary Cooperatives

### Cooperative Categories by Level (RA 9520 Art. 5)

| Level | Category | Composition | Minimum |
|-------|----------|-------------|---------|
| 1st | Primary | Natural persons | 15 members |
| 2nd | Secondary | Primary cooperatives | 2 primary coops |
| 3rd | Tertiary | Secondary cooperatives | 2 secondary coops |
| Apex | National | All levels | Sectoral representation |

### Benefits of Federation
1. Shared services (IT, accounting, audit, legal, training)
2. Collective bargaining
3. Economies of scale
4. Unified advocacy voice
5. Risk sharing
6. Technical support
7. Market access

### Formation Process
Month 1-2: Exploration and consensus building
Month 3-4: BOD resolutions, GA ratification, PRS, economic survey
Month 5: Organizational meeting, elect BOD
Month 6: Registration with CDA/CSEA
Month 7-12: Operationalization

## Cooperative-to-Cooperative Business Development

### ICA Principle 6: Cooperation Among Cooperatives

Forms of inter-cooperation:
- **Supply Chain Partnerships**: Producer coop -> Marketing coop
- **Shared Services**: IT, training, audit, insurance pooling
- **Joint Ventures**: Co-investment in facilities
- **Consortia**: Project-based collaboration
- **Apex Organizations**: National federations

### Inter-Cooperation Agreement Elements
1. Purpose and scope
2. Terms and conditions
3. Governance and dispute resolution
4. Duration and exit
5. Risk sharing
6. Benefit sharing

## Enterprise Development Programs for Cooperatives

### EDP Curriculum (Core Modules)

| Module | Topics | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Business Planning | Vision, mission, situational analysis, strategic plan | 2 days |
| Financial Management | Bookkeeping, financial statements, budgeting | 2 days |
| Marketing | Market research, marketing mix, sales | 1-2 days |
| Operations | Production, quality, efficiency | 1-2 days |
| Human Resource | Staffing, training, performance | 1 day |
| Governance | BOD, GA, committees, policies | 1 day |
| Compliance | Regulatory requirements, reporting | 1 day |

### Financial Sustainability Planning
- Capitalization: Share capital growth, institutional funds
- Profitability: Gross margin, net surplus targets
- Liquidity: Cash reserve requirements
- Solvency: Debt-to-equity ratio management
- Statutory Funds: RF, CETF, CF, ODF compliance (RA 9520 Art. 86)

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Micro to Small Cooperative Transition
Context: 10-member agricultural coop in Maguindanao, PHP 2.5M assets
Target: 50+ members, >15M assets over 3 years
Pathway: Membership expansion (Y1) -> Operations professionalization (Y1-2) -> Business expansion (Y2-3)

### Scenario 2: Secondary Cooperative Formation
Context: 5 coffee producer coops in Lanao del Sur forming secondary coop
Expected outcomes: 20% better price, 30% cost reduction, unified quality standards

### Scenario 3: Credit-Agri Inter-Cooperation
Context: Credit coop partnering with agri coops for production loans with crop marketing
Model: Auto-deduction at source reduces default risk
