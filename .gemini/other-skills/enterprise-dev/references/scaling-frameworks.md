# Scaling Frameworks

Proven methodologies for scaling enterprises, adapted for cooperatives and social enterprises.

## Scaling Up (Verne Harnish)

### The Four Decisions

| Decision | Focus | Key Question |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| **People** | Team-of-teams structure | Do we have the right people? |
| **Strategy** | Customer-focused plan | What's our competitive advantage? |
| **Execution** | Relentless repeatability | Are we delivering consistently? |
| **Cash** | Supercharge cash flow | Do we have runway? |

### One-Page Strategic Plan (OPSP)

Single-page document covering:
- Core Values/Beliefs
- Core Purpose
- BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal)
- Profit per X (economic engine)
- Brand Promise
- Annual Priorities (3-5)
- Quarterly Rocks (3-5)
- Key Thrusts/Capabilities

### Rockefeller Habits Checklist

**10 Habits (40 Sub-habits):**

1. Executive team healthy and aligned
2. Everyone aligned with #1 thing
3. Communication rhythm established
4. Every facet has person accountable
5. Ongoing employee input collected
6. Reporting on customer feedback
7. Core values and purpose alive
8. Employees can articulate strategy
9. All employees know their #s
10. Company plans reviewed quarterly

**Implementation**: Introduce 1-2 habits per quarter over 24-36 months.

### 13-Week Execution Cadence

| Rhythm | Frequency | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Daily Huddle | Daily | Quick sync, stuck items |
| Weekly Meeting | Weekly | Accountability, metrics |
| Monthly Review | Monthly | Department deep-dives |
| Quarterly Planning | Quarterly | Rocks, priorities |
| Annual Summit | Yearly | Strategy, BHAG |

**Cooperative Adaptation:**
- "A-players" → "member development"
- Quarterly planning → include member input
- Team structure → align with committees

## Good to Great (Jim Collins)

### The Flywheel Concept

```text
No overnight transformation:
     ┌──────────────────┐
     │   Consistent     │
     │   small pushes   │
     │   in one         │
     │   direction      │
     └────────┬─────────┘
              │
              ▼
     ┌──────────────────┐
     │   Momentum       │
     │   builds         │
     └────────┬─────────┘
              │
              ▼
     ┌──────────────────┐
     │   BREAKTHROUGH   │
     └──────────────────┘
```

**Key Insight**: Breakthrough comes after sustained discipline, not dramatic transformation.

### Hedgehog Concept

Three intersecting circles:

```text
         PASSION
            ▲
           / \
          /   \
         /  ◆  \
        /       \
       /_________\
   BEST AT ◄───► ECONOMIC
                  ENGINE
```

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What are we deeply passionate about?
2. What can we be the best in the world at?
3. What drives our economic/resource engine?

**SE Adaptation:**
- "Best in the world" → "Best in our community/sector"
- Add fourth circle: "What creates social impact?"

### Level 5 Leadership

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Executive: Builds enduring greatness |
| 4 | Effective Leader: Catalyzes commitment |
| 3 | Competent Manager: Organizes people |
| 2 | Contributing Team Member: Contributes |
| 1 | Capable Individual: Productive work |

**Level 5 Characteristics:**
- Personal humility + professional will
- Credits others for success
- Takes responsibility for failures
- Aspirational for organization, not self

### First Who, Then What

**Principle**: Get the right people on the bus (and wrong people off) before deciding where to drive.

**Application for Cooperatives:**
- Member screening and education
- Board composition and training
- Staff development investment

## Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey Moore)

### Technology Adoption Lifecycle

```text
│ Innovators │ Early    │ THE    │ Early   │ Late     │ Laggards │
│   2.5%     │ Adopters │ CHASM  │ Majority│ Majority │   16%    │
│            │  13.5%   │        │  34%    │   34%    │          │
└────────────┴──────────┴────────┴─────────┴──────────┴──────────┘
```

### The Chasm Problem

Early Adopters (visionaries) have different buying motivations than Early Majority (pragmatists):

| Early Adopters | Early Majority |
|----------------|----------------|
| Willing to take risks | Want proven solutions |
| Value innovation | Value reliability |
| Self-sufficient | Need references |
| Tolerate bugs | Expect quality |

### Crossing Strategies

**Big Fish, Small Pond:**
- Focus ALL resources on one segment
- Become market leader in that segment
- Then expand to adjacent segments

**Whole Product:**
- Deliver complete solutions, not just core
- Include training, support, integration
- Remove friction from adoption

**Reference Customers:**
- Build referenceable pragmatist customers
- Same industry, similar use case
- Willing to speak publicly

### BARMM Cooperative Application

When introducing new products/services:
1. Focus on one municipality/barangay first
2. Build success stories (references)
3. Then expand to adjacent communities
4. Complete solution (not just product)

## Social Enterprise Scaling

### Scaling Strategies for SEs

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Scaling Up** | Grow organization | Strong management capacity |
| **Scaling Out** | Replicate model | Proven, replicable process |
| **Scaling Across** | Entrust to others | Network capacity exists |
| **Scaling Deep** | Intensify impact | Quality over quantity |

### Bricolage Approach

For resource-constrained enterprises:

**Core Principles:**
- Combine existing resources creatively
- Iterative: pilot → evaluate → implement
- "Zero investment improvement"
- Leverage social capital
- Network for low-cost resources

**Network Bricolage:**
- Reconfigure resources through partnerships
- Share infrastructure and capabilities
- Joint purchasing and marketing
- Knowledge exchange

### Dual-Track Scaling

**Impact Track:**
- Deepen beneficiary outcomes
- Expand beneficiary reach
- Measure and validate impact

**Sustainability Track:**
- Grow revenue streams
- Optimize cost structure
- Build financial reserves

**Balance Required:**
- Neither track should dominate
- Regular dual-track review
- Integrated planning process

## BARMM Scaling Strategies (BDP 2023-2028)

### Economic Scaling Context

The 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (2023-2028) outlines specific strategies for scaling enterprises in the region, with the overall strategy being to "Create an enabling environment towards an equitable, competitive, and robust economy."

### Scaling Targets by Sector

| Sector | Baseline | 2028 Target | Scaling Strategy |
|--------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| Industry | 7.9% growth | 10%-12% growth | Double-digit sustained growth |
| Services | 6.6% growth | 10%-12% growth | Economic corridor development |
| Capital Formation | 16.91% of GRDP | 18%-20% of GRDP | Investment code passage |
| Cooperatives | 11.66% compliant | 50% compliant | CSEA capacity building |

### MSME Scaling Framework

**Formalization Strategy:**
1. Business Name Registration (BNR) as entry point
2. Access to capacity development support
3. Enterprise skills training and digital literacy
4. Product pricing, packaging, and market access

**Scaling Metrics (2017-2021):**
- BNR Growth: 2,575 to 8,427 (227% increase)
- Investment Generated: Php 9.02 billion
- Jobs Created: 62,166

**Competitiveness Enhancement (Strategy 14):**
- Institutional building
- Value adding and product development
- Market promotion and linkages
- Business registration and accreditation services

### Cooperative Scaling Model

**BARMM Cooperative Scaling Pathway:**

```text
Stage 1: Registration
    └── 12,214 registered (2010-2020)

Stage 2: Compliance
    └── Target: 11.66% → 50% compliant

Stage 3: Profitability
    └── Technical and financial assistance

Stage 4: Sustainability
    └── M&E systems and community promotion
```

**Key Scaling Programs:**

1. **PESO Project (Profitability and Equity Support Oriented)**
   - Financial assistance for cooperative operations
   - Technical capacity building
   - Profitability improvement support

2. **LAKAS SE Program (Lakbay Aral sa Social Enterprise)**
   - Social enterprise exposure and learning
   - Best practice replication
   - Network building

3. **Combatant Transitional Program**
   - Former combatant cooperative organization
   - Financial and material support
   - Business operations assistance

### Investment Scaling Strategy

**Investment Generation Programs:**

**1. Bangsamoro Integrated Investment Program (BIIP)**
- Investor encouragement through laws and incentives
- LGU capacity building for investment promotion
- Local investment code formulation assistance

**2. Investment Generation and Development Program (IGDeP)**
- Investment leads generation
- BARMM Investment Facilitation Network (BIF-Net)
- Management Investment Data System
- Networking on Investment for Local Executives (NILE)

### Economic Corridor Development

**Scaling Through Connectivity (Sub-sector Objective 2.3.2):**

Develop economic corridors linking:
- Investments
- Freeports and ecozones
- Production centers
- Manufacturing facilities
- Marketing and services industries
- Within and outside BARMM markets

**Bangsamoro Economic Zone Development:**
- Polloc Freeport and Economic Zone (PFEZ-BARMM)
- BEZA Law institutionalization
- Ecozone investment promotion
- Infrastructure modernization

### Halal Industry Scaling

**Strategic Priority for BARMM:**

1. **Production Scaling**
   - Halal slaughter houses
   - Halal certification support
   - License-To-Operate subsidies

2. **Market Scaling**
   - BIMP-EAGA trade linkages
   - International halal market access
   - Halal tourism promotion

3. **Standards Scaling**
   - Bangsamoro Standards and Halal Testing Laboratory (BHSTL)
   - ISO/IEC 17025:2017 compliance
   - Halal accreditation guidelines

### Trade and Market Scaling

**GEARED Program (Growth Enhancement Approach):**
- Industry Roadmap for Bangsamoro Coffee
- Industry Roadmap for Bangsamoro Fishing Industry
- Industry Roadmap for Bangsamoro Weaving Industry
- Shared Service Facilities (SSF)

**ACE Program (Accelerate Competitiveness):**
- Trading Engagement on Inter-Regional and International Market (TEIIM)
- BIMP-EAGA Trading Partnership
- Chamber of Commerce strengthening

### Bricolage for BARMM Context

**Resource-Constrained Scaling:**

Given BARMM's unique challenges, the bricolage approach is highly relevant:

| Challenge | Bricolage Solution |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Limited capital | Network resource pooling |
| Informal sector dominance | Gradual formalization pathway |
| Skills mismatch | On-the-job training + apprenticeship |
| Limited infrastructure | Shared Service Facilities |
| Market access gaps | Value chain linkages |

**Barter Trade as Bricolage:**
The BDP recognizes barter trading as a culturally-rooted economic practice:
- Inter-familial transactions (Sama Badjao-Sabah)
- Social trust-based exchanges
- Safety net during crises
- BIMP-EAGA informal trade linkages

### Scaling Readiness for BARMM Enterprises

| Dimension | BARMM Context | Scaling Approach |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| Model Clarity | Low (informal sector) | Formalization support |
| Team Capacity | Limited (skills gap) | TVET and apprenticeship |
| Financial Health | Constrained (credit access) | Islamic banking access |
| Systems | Weak (low compliance) | CSEA M&E systems |
| Demand | Growing (BIMP-EAGA) | Market linkage programs |

### Priority Legislative Enablers

For enterprise scaling, the following codes are prioritized:

1. **Bangsamoro Investments Code**
   - Stable BBOI foundation
   - Uniform fiscal incentives
   - Investment-friendly climate

2. **Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code**
   - Legal basis for BCSE operations
   - Strategic planning framework
   - Regional-specific provisions

3. **BEZA Law**
   - Economic zone administration
   - Incentive granting authority
   - CREATE Act alignment

4. **Ease of Doing Business Implementation**
   - RA 11032 localization
   - Business One Stop Shop (BOSS)
   - Digital government transactions
