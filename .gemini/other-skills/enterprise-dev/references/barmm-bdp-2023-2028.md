# BARMM Enterprise Development Context: BDP 2023-2028

Reference guide for enterprise development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, aligned with the 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028.

---

## Regional Vision & Mission

### Vision Statement

**"A Bangsamoro that is united, enlightened, self-governing, peaceful, just, morally upright, and progressive."**

A territory devoid of all types of conflict with permanent peace, security, comfort, and forward-thinking leadership.

### Mission Statement

**"Guided by moral governance and in pursuit of genuine and meaningful autonomy, the Bangsamoro government ensures the necessary conditions for enduring peace and sustained socioeconomic development suitable to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of its people by providing services to communities, ensuring multi-stakeholder participation, and facilitating appropriate partnership."**

### Overall Goal

**An empowered, cohesive, and progressive Bangsamoro:**
- **Empowered**: Improved community capacity through quality basic services and expanded economic opportunities
- **Cohesive**: Reduced violent conflict, improved relationships between stakeholders, forged partnerships
- **Progressive**: Sustained regional ability to optimize resources and realize economic and human potential

## Priority Economic Sectors

### Seaweed: National & Regional Leader

**BARMM Position:**
- **National Share**: 48.42% of Philippines seaweed production
- **Primary Location**: Tawi-Tawi (90%+ of regional production)
- **Key Municipalities**: Sitangkai and Sibutu islands

**Development Priorities:**
1. Post-harvest processing plants & modernized equipment
2. Municipal-wide seaweed nursery systems
3. Seaweed research & development capacity
4. Full value-chain from production to marketing
5. Carrageenan export product development

**Enterprise Opportunities:**
- Processing cooperatives
- Export-oriented enterprises
- Research-based social enterprises
- Supply chain integration

### Cassava: High-Volume Commodity

**BARMM Position:**
- **National Share**: 41.70% of Philippines cassava production
- **Rank**: 2nd top commodity by volume
- **Primary Location**: Lanao del Sur (47% of regional production)

**Development Priorities:**
1. Value-added product development
2. Processing technology adoption
3. Certified seed production
4. Market linkage strengthening
5. Yield improvement (currently below national average)

**Enterprise Opportunities:**
- Food processing enterprises
- Starch extraction businesses
- Livestock feed production
- Biofuel development

### Halal Industry: Strategic Priority

**Global Context:**
- Rising international demand for halal products
- BARMM's unique Islamic character competitive advantage
- Strategic importance for BIMP-EAGA trade

**Development Priorities:**
1. Halal product certification expansion
2. Slaughterhouse establishment & modernization
3. MSME halal awareness training
4. Halal poultry production scaling
5. Quality assurance systems

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Bangsamoro Halal Board (BHB) - policy direction
- Bangsamoro Standards & Halal Testing Laboratory (BHSTL)
- Halal Implementing Rules & Regulations (M.C. 1 series 2021)
- Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Program

**Enterprise Opportunities:**
- Halal meat processing
- Halal food manufacturing
- Halal tourism services
- Islamic banking ventures

### Coffee & High-Value Crops

**BARMM Production:**
- **Coffee**: 17.4% of national production (3rd rank nationally)
- **Rubber**: 18.4% of national production (3rd rank)
- **Lanzones**: 15.3% of national production (3rd rank)
- **Mangosteen**: 22.4% of national production (2nd rank)
- **Durian**: 8.4% of national production (2nd rank)

**Development Strategy:**
- Specialty coffee development (Maguindanao focus)
- Rubber plantation expansion (Basilan focus)
- Organic/premium certification
- Processing facility establishment
- Geographic indication (GI) protection

**Regional Summary:**
- **Total Investment Value**: PHP 25.3 billion
- **Employment Generated**: 20,103 jobs
- **Registered Business Enterprises**: 46

### Investment by Province (PHP 25.3B Total)

|----------|-----------|-----------|---------|

| **Tawi-Tawi** | PHP 3.56B | 2,779 | 14.1% |

| **Sulu** | PHP 851M | 170 | 3.4% |

**Strategic Insights:**
- Maguindanao leads investment & employment generation
- High concentration in mainland provinces
- Island provinces (BaSulTa) lag significantly
- Opportunity for geographic diversification

### MSME Business Name Registration (BNR)

**Growth Trajectory:**
- 2017: 2,575 registrations
- 2018: 2,881 registrations
- 2019: 3,223 registrations
- 2020: 3,572 registrations
- **2021: 8,427 registrations (161% increase)**

**Key Metrics:**
- 2020-2021: 11,650 businesses registered
- Jobs Created (2020-2021): 31,000
- Investment Generated (2020-2021): PHP 2.5 billion
- National Share: Only 0.92% (indicates huge growth potential)
- MSME Investment (2017-2021): PHP 9.02 billion

**Strategic Opportunity:**
- BARMM represents massive untapped MSME formalization potential
- Shift from informal to formal economy ongoing
- Access to government support accelerating

### Registered Cooperatives

**Current Status (as of 2020):**
- **Total Registered**: 12,214 cooperatives
- **Maguindanao**: 38.24% (4,671)
- **Lanao del Sur**: 37.29% (4,554)

**Cooperative Types:**
- Producer Cooperatives: 37.58% (4,590)
- Marketing Cooperatives: 27.54% (3,364)
- Agricultural Cooperatives: 12.82% (1,566)

**Compliance Challenge:**
- **Only 11.66% Compliant** (1,424 of 12,214)
- Target: Increase to 50% compliance by 2028

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA) - transitioning from CDA
- Bangsamoro Cooperative & Social Enterprise (BCSE) Bill - pending approval
- Policy-regulatory gap from ARMM to BARMM transition

### Enterprise Development Compliance

**Business Name Registration (BNR):**
- Required for single-proprietorship MSMEs
- Portal for formalization
- Access point to government support

**Cooperative Requirements:**
- Certificate of Compliance with legal requirements
- Democratic governance structure
- Regular financial reporting
- Member education programs

**Social Enterprise Framework:**
- Emerging regulatory structure
- Focus on dual mission (profit + impact)
- BCSE Bill pending for legal clarity

**Halal Certification:**
- Mandatory for food/beverage businesses (strategic advantage)
- BHSTL testing & certification
- Training programs required
- Subsidy available for certification

## BIMP-EAGA Regional Integration

### Strategic Geographic Advantages

**Location Benefits:**
- Brunei Darussalam-Indonesia-Malaysia-Philippines East ASEAN Growth Area
- Proximity to Sabah, Malaysia & North Sulawesi, Indonesia
- Maritime corridor access (Sulu-Sulawesi Seas)
- Biodiverse Coral Triangle positioning

### Trade Patterns & Opportunities

**Historical Trade Links:**
- North Sulawesi - Mindanao strong connections
- Sabah - Mindanao established routes
- Barter trade dominance in Sulu Archipelago
- Inter-familial trading relationships

**Greater Sulu-Sulawesi Corridor:**
- Highly biodiverse, globally significant
- Agriculture & aquaculture product focus
- Port-to-port trade flows
- Shipping service networks

### Barter Trade as Economic Engine

**Characteristics:**
- Dominant trading mode in BARMM
- Sama-Bajao inter-maritime trade patterns
- Social trust-based transactions
- Essential informal economy safety net

**Enterprise Adaptation:**
- Value chain linking (not price-based)
- Community-rooted supply chains
- Social protection integration
- Informal-formal bridge mechanisms

### Investment Opportunities

**Strategic Investment Priorities (National Government Identified):**
1. **Halal Industry** - Unique to BARMM
2. **Islamic Banking & Finance** - Regulatory advantage
3. **BIMP-EAGA Trade Enterprises** - Regional positioning

**Competitive Advantages:**
- Only region with own Board of Investments (BBOI)
- Fiscal incentives for qualifying investments
- Non-fiscal support (business development)
- Islamic finance enabling environment

## Enterprise Development Challenges 2023-2028

### MSME Competitiveness Gap

**Barriers to Scale:**
1. Limited technology adoption
2. Low formal credit access
3. Informal sector dominance (lacks standards, contracts, IP)
4. Skill-enterprise mismatch
5. Limited market linkages

**Regulatory Barriers:**
- Certification costs (halal, organic, quality)
- Business registration complexity
- Land title issues (contested lands at 16%)
- Cooperative compliance burden (only 11.66% compliant)

### Geographic & Infrastructure Constraints

**Connectivity Challenges:**
- BARMM road density: 0.10 (lowest nationally, vs 0.25 national)
- Island provinces isolated (Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi)
- Power supply unreliable (especial BaSulTa)
- Water supply insufficient for industrial use

**Infrastructure Solutions Needed:**
- Port expansion (especially Lamitan for barter trade)
- Airport connectivity expansion
- Road network densification
- Utilities expansion programs

### Halal Industry Development Gaps

**Current Status:**
- Underdeveloped halal products & services
- Limited MSME halal awareness
- Insufficient slaughterhouse capacity
- Quality assurance system needs strengthening

**Development Requirements:**
1. Training & capacity building for producers
2. Certification subsidy programs
3. Processing facility development
4. Market promotion strategies
5. Export market linkage
