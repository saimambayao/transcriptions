
### How This Can Improve Your Claude Skills and Workflows

**The Bootstart Blueprint's 3-level framework should be mapped to your Claude skill invocation order.** Level 1 (Business Model Design) maps to /lean-canvas. Level 2 (Demand Validation) maps to /grill-me and /startup running Demo-Sell-Build. Level 3 (Build and Scale) maps to /write-a-prd, /tdd, and /enterprise-dev. The critical discipline is never invoking Level 3 skills until Level 2 skills have confirmed demand. If /grill-me cannot defend the demand evidence, /write-a-prd should not produce a technical spec.

**Use /startup to run the "hobby vs. business" classification on every MoroTech platform.** Feed each platform into /startup with the honest question: "Does this platform have a clear path to revenue or institutional adoption within 12 months?" /startup should produce a binary classification: business (weekday priority) or hobby (weekend only). This classification, done quarterly through /startup, prevents the pattern of giving equal attention to all 9 platforms when only 2-3 have viable business models.

**The Lean(er) Canvas concept should become a /lean-canvas variant for cooperative training workshops.** Create a simplified /lean-canvas prompt that asks only three questions: Who is it for? Where to start? What problems to tackle? This "Lean(er) Canvas for Cooperatives" template, delivered through /training-assistant, lowers the barrier for first-time entrepreneur workshops in BARMM. After cooperative leaders master the simplified version, graduate them to the full /lean-canvas exercise.

**Your /product skill should include the Customer Factory diagnostic (Activation, Retention, Referral) as a standard health check.** Every month, /product should produce a Customer Factory report for MoroMarket: activation rate (what percentage of signed-up cooperatives complete their first transaction?), retention rate (what percentage are active 30 days later?), referral rate (how many new cooperatives came from existing cooperative recommendations?). The weakest link in this chain becomes the sole focus of the next sprint -- no exceptions.

**The "Sell Before You Build" philosophy should override the default behavior of every building-oriented skill.** When you invoke /write-a-prd, /tdd, or /enterprise-dev for a platform that has not yet completed Demo-Sell-Build, these skills should warn: "This platform has not completed demand validation. Are you sure you want to proceed with building?" This built-in friction prevents the most expensive mistake a solo developer can make: building something nobody has agreed to buy.
