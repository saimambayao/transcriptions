**Assembling the Business Model Jigsaw**

**05:52**

**06:14**

**06:30**
I'll present one way that I use a lot based on this definition of a business model by Saul Kaplan.

**06:36**
A business model is a story of how a company or product creates, delivers, and captures value from its users and customers. In case you're wondering what I mean by capturing value, that's just a fancy way of saying how your business model makes money.

**Mapping Jobs to Canvas Boxes**

**06:50**
Let's see how these three jobs are mapped onto the Lean Canvas. You create value with your unique value proposition or UVP.

**06:59**
In order to craft a compelling unique value proposition, you need to deeply understand your customer segments and their problems.

**07:06**
You deliver your value through your solution and channels.

**07:10**
And finally, capturing value or getting paid lies at the intersection of your cost structure and revenue streams.

**07:16**
These three jobs also come together to help you describe a working business model. If you can create more value for your customers than you capture from them through your pricing model, then you create a compelling value proposition. This is the value equation.

**07:32**
And if you can capture more value from your customers than the cost to deliver that value, then you have a sustainable or profitable business model. This is the monetization equation.

**Measuring and Defending Value**

**07:41**

**07:52**

**07:57**
And finally, once your model starts to work, it will inevitably attract copycats and competitors. You deter those copycats and competitors using the unfair advantage box.

### **Organized Notes**

**I. The Failure of Traditional Business Plans**
*   **Timestamp: 00:00 - 01:28**
*   Traditional plans are often 60+ pages with 5-year forecasts and 18-month roadmaps.
*   **The Issues:**
    *   Most information at the early stage is "unknowable," making the plan fictional.
    *   They are too "heavyweight" and take too long to write.
    *   **Low Engagement:** Research shows only 30% of founders write them, and less than 2% of investors actually read them in full. Investors prefer 1-page summaries or short pitch decks.

**II. The Danger of "No Plan" (Zombie Startups)**
*   **Timestamp: 01:39 - 02:03**
*   Founders often skip planning for a "just do it" approach.
*   **Result:** This often leads to a **Zombie Startup**â€”a company that is stuck in a market too small for growth, neither fully alive nor dead.

**III. Introduction to the Lean Canvas**
*   **Timestamp: 02:11 - 03:15**
*   **Business Model vs. Business Plan:** A business model assumes the "perfect plan" is a myth and focuses on a dynamic, lightweight snapshot of the idea.
*   **Lean Canvas:** A 1-page model that takes ~20 minutes to create. It is designed to be quick to update and easy for others to read.

**IV. Origins and Comparison (Lean Canvas vs. BMC)**
*   **Timestamp: 03:16 - 04:30**
*   Created by Ash Maurya in 2009, derived from the **Business Model Canvas (BMC)**.
*   **Key Differences:**
    *   BMC is academic/descriptive for existing businesses.
    *   Lean Canvas is for startups; it replaces "Key Partners/Activities/Resources" with "Problem/Solution/Key Metrics" to focus on early-stage risks.

**V. The Layout and Idea Genesis**
*   **Timestamp: 04:40 - 06:14**
*   The layout is non-linear because ideas don't start logically; they start as "flashes of inspiration."
*   **Common Starting Points:**
    *   **Solution box:** Inspired by new tech (e.g., AI).
    *   **Problem box:** "Scratch your own itch" ideas based on personal pain points.
    *   **Unfair Advantage box:** Leveraging an exclusive resource like a patent.

**VI. The Core Definition of a Business Model**
*   **Timestamp: 06:36 - 08:08**
*   A business model is a story of how a company **Creates, Delivers, and Captures** value.
*   **Job Mapping on Lean Canvas:**
    *   **Create Value:** Unique Value Proposition (deeply linked to Problems/Customers).
    *   **Deliver Value:** Solution and Channels.
    *   **Capture Value:** Cost Structure and Revenue Streams (getting paid).
    *   **Sustainable Model:** If Capture Value > Deliver Value (Profit).
    *   **Bonus Jobs:** Measuring Value (Key Metrics) and Defending Value (Unfair Advantage).
