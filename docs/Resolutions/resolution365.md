**RESOLUTION NO. 365**

RESOLUTION CONDEMNING THE BURNING OF COPIES OF THE HOLY QUR’AN IN SWEDEN DURING EID AL-ADHA

WHEREAS, on June 28, 2023, a Holy Qur’an was burned outside the Stockholm Central Mosque, coinciding with the celebration of Eid al-Adha in the Muslim world;

WHEREAS, the act of desecrating the Holy Qur’an is widely regarded as a provocative act that deeply offends and undermines the faith of Muslims;

WHEREAS, the European Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms, and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights have consistently upheld the fundamental principles of freedom of religion, expression and belief;

WHEREAS, Section 5, Article III of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the free exercise and enjoyment of religious profession and worship, without discrimination or preference, shall forever be allowed;

WHEREAS, Section 4, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054 also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law states that the customs, beliefs, and traditions of the Bangsamoro people are hereby recognized, protected, and guaranteed;

WHEREAS, the same article extends that the Parliament shall adopt measures to ensure mutual respect and protection of the distinct beliefs, customs, and traditions of the Bangsamoro people and the other inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. No person in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be subjected to any form of discrimination on account of creed religion, ethnic origin, parentage, or gender;

WHEREAS, a book that is considered holy by a certain religion should be respected, and respect must also be accorded to everyone who believes in it;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) expresses its profound concern and dismay over the recent burning of the Holy Qur’an, recognizing the potential of such an act to incite hatred, extremism and violence globally;

WHEREAS, the BTA recognized and affirmed the principles of tolerance, respect, and non-discrimination as cornerstones of a just and harmonious society;

WHEREAS, the BTA calls upon the Swedish authorities to thoroughly investigate the incident, bring the perpetrators to justice, and ensure the implementation of adequate measures to prevent future occurrences of religiously motivated hate crimes;

WHEREAS, Swedish authorities should proscribe anti-Islamic actions under the pretext of freedom of expression:

NOW, THEREFORE, be it

RESOLVED, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, To strongly condemn the burning of copies of the Holy Qur’an in Sweden during Eid al-Adha.

RESOLVED, FURTHER, That this Resolution is communicated to the Swedish authorities to emphasize the urgent need for a comprehensive response to this incident and the prevention of similar acts in the future.

RESOLVED, FINALLY, That a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Swedish Embassy Manila in Taguig.

ADOPTED, Dhul Hijjah 29, 1444/July 17, 2023.
