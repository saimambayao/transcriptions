**RESOLUTION NO. 411**

RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND THANKING THE KONRAD ADENAUER STIFTUNG FOR ITS INVALUABLE CONTRIBUTION SINCE 2001 TO THE DEVELOPMENT OF AUTONOMY, PEACE, AND DEVELOPMENT IN THE BANGSAMORO

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution categorically provides, “the Philippines renounces war as an instrument of national policy, adopts the generally accepted principles of international law as part of the law of the land and adheres to the policy of peace, equality, justice, freedom, cooperation, and amity with all nations;”

WHEREAS, Section 7, Article II of the Constitution provides, “the State shall pursue an independent foreign policy. In its relation with other states, the paramount consideration shall be national sovereignty, territorial integrity, national interest, and the right to self-determination;”

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, or the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao”, provides, “the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the powers expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;”

WHEREAS, the Konrad Adenauer Stiftung (KAS) is a political foundation inspired by the ideals of Konrad Adenauer as the first Chancellor of the Federal Republic of Germany;

WHEREAS, the KAS is founded on the basic principles of freedom, justice, and solidarity, and it has made a significant contribution in supporting people to live self-determined lives in freedom and dignity;

WHEREAS, the KAS has been actively promoting democracy, the rule of law, and a social market economy in its seventy (70) offices abroad and projects in one hundred twenty (120) countries;

WHEREAS, KAS has been promoting democracy and political institutions in the Philippines since 1964;

WHEREAS, KAS has been providing grants, technical assistance, and capacity building in the Bangsamoro region, particularly in strengthening autonomy, governance, and political parties since 2001;

WHEREAS, KAS, with the European Union, has been at the forefront in promoting the parliamentary and political party system in the Bangsamoro through the programs Democratic Party Development Project (DEPAdev) and the Democratic Leadership and Active Civil Society Empowerment Bangsamoro from 2014 to 2022 that raised capacities of civil society organizations (CSOs) and emerging political parties including that of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front, the Moro National Liberation Front, women, youth, and Indigenous Peoples’ groups in developing principled political parties in the Bangsamoro, empowered stakeholders, and underrepresented groups to transform their interest groups and to operate them as functional programmatic political parties;

WHEREAS, KAS is in consortium with PARTICIP Germany in implementing the Support to Bangsamoro Transition Programme which is providing technical assistance in building the foundation for lasting peace and development in the Bangsamoro by strengthening the capacities of its institutions to establish an enabling democratic governance environment during the transition period;

WHEREAS, KAS has been consistently and sustainably providing a number of training and technical assistance to the Bangsamoro policy bodies particularly the Regional Legislative Assembly of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, the Bangsamoro Transition Commission, and the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) in fulfilling their mandates in building effective public policies and institutions in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;

WHEREAS, KAS has brought many key Bangsamoro government and nongovernmental leaders in Germany and Europe for study and benchmarking sessions that strengthened international support to the cause of the Bangsamoro people and enriched the discourse and institution building in the Bangsamoro towards effective autonomous and parliamentary system;

WHEREAS, KAS has assisted the indigenous peoples in the Bangsamoro through the program “Recognition of the Rights of the Indigenous Peoples in the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao for their Empowerment and Sustainable Development” thereby significantly contributing to the promotion of inclusive peace and governance in the Bangsamoro;

WHEREAS, KAS, through the annual Kusog Mindanaw Conference, has significantly helped promote the Bangsamoro peace process in the national and international arena, particularly in promoting autonomy and federalism as sustainable solutions to the Mindanao conflict;

WHEREAS, KAS and partners have produced numerous publications and policy papers that helped raise awareness and capacities of policymakers, academic institutions, international partner organizations, CSOs, and advocacy groups on key political and governance issues in the Bangsamoro:

NOW, THEREFORE, be it

RESOLVED, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To commend and thank the Konrad Adenauer Stiftung for its invaluable contribution since 2001 to the development of autonomy, peace, and development in the Bangsamoro.

RESOLVED, FURTHER, To extend its recognition and appreciation to the Konrad Adenauer Stiftung for its continuing support to the Bangsamoro and for its steady and consistent work for sustainable peace and development in the region, thereby approving this Resolution commending and thanking the KAS for its invaluable contribution to the development of autonomy, peace, and development in the Bangsamoro since 2001.

RESOLVED, FINALLY, To present this Resolution to Ms. Daniela Braun, the Country Director of the KAS in the Philippines on the occasion of her official visit to the BTA on November 28, 2023, for information.

ADOPTED, Rabi Al-Akhir 08, 1445/October 23, 2023.
