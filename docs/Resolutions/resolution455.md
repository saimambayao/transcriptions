**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 455**

**RESOLUTION REQUIRING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH TO PROVIDE PROGRESS REPORT ON THE IMMUNIZATION OF BABIES AND CHILDREN IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO AND TO PROVIDE AN ACTION PLAN FOR REACHING ITS TARGET IMMUNIZATION RATE**

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Government is mandated to protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

**WHEREAS,** the Ministry of Health (MOH) is primarily responsible for the formulation, planning, implementation, and coordination of policies and programs for health, and is mandated to promote, protect, preserve, and restore the health of the people through the delivery of health services through regulation and encouragement of providers of health goods and services;

**WHEREAS,** based on a 2019 Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) Household Survey by the World Bank, only thirty-four percent (34%) of children aged 12-23 months received basic vaccine coverage, while twenty-four percent (24%) had no vaccine history at all;

**WHEREAS,** based on the report mentioned above, vaccine dropout is also a significant issue in BARMM where twenty-five percent (25%) to fifty percent (50%) of children who received first dose of vaccine antigen failed to receive the second and third doses in the series;

**WHEREAS,** against the vaccination target of is ninety-five percent (95%), BARMM managed to fully vaccinate only around sixty-one percent (61%) of the eligible children based on the 2021 Field Health Services Information System of the Department of Health, which is also below the sixty-three percent (63%) national average;

**WHEREAS,** based on the abovementioned study by the World Bank, only fifty percent (50%) of the respondents heard about the importance of receiving all the recommended vaccines for their babies and children, and only eight point two percent (8.2%) knew they had to return for the next doses of routine vaccination;

**WHEREAS,** immunization is vital in protecting babies and children from diseases, which are already preventable by available vaccinations, and failure to do so may put their health at serious risk:

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To require the Ministry of Health to provide progress report on the immunization of babies and children in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao and to provide an action plan for reaching its target immunization rate.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** To call on the Ministry of Health to provide its progress report on the immunization of babies and children in BARMM, including updates on its information drive campaign.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE,** To urge the Ministry of Health to furnish the BTA Parliament of its action plans for reaching the target immunization rate of the region.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY,** To forward this Resolution to the Ministry of Health for their proper action.
