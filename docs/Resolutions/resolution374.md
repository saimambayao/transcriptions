**RESOLUTION NO. 374**

RESOLUTION URGING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY (GOTD) TO INSTITUTE PREPARATORY MEASURES FOR THE IMPENDING EL NIÑO PHENOMENON

WHEREAS, Section 16, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature;

WHEREAS, Section 8, Article XIII of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) states that the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and development of forests, coastal, and marine resources, including the adoption of programs and projects, to ensure the maintenance of ecological balance and biodiversity shall be given priority;

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article V of the same law states in part that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

WHEREAS, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) is the state weather bureau of the Philippines mandated to provide protection against natural calamities and utilize scientific knowledge as an effective instrument to ensure the safety, well-being, and economic safety of all the people, and for promotion of national progress;

WHEREAS, El Niño refers to a warming of the ocean surface, or above-average sea surface temperatures, in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean;

WHEREAS, recent developments in the tropical Pacific have led PAGASA to raise the PAGASA El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO) Alert and Warning System to El Niño Alert on May 2, 2023, where recent conditions and model forecasts indicate that El Niño may emerge in the coming season (June-July-August) at eighty (80) percent probability and may persist until the first quarter of 2024;

WHEREAS, El Niño increases the likelihood of below-normal rainfall conditions which could have negative impacts such as dry spells and droughts in some areas of the country;

WHEREAS, the El Niño phenomenon has a serious and adverse impacts on agriculture, particularly rice and corn production, which may affect the availability and distribution of food in the country;

WHEREAS, the El Niño phenomenon may have a grave impact on the peoples’ lives, environment, health, and socio-economic conditions in terms of loss of life, disruption of production, livelihood opportunities, disruption of essential services, and damage to property and national infrastructure;

WHEREAS, the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR) said that the country’s fish supply may be affected by the looming El Niño which could result in fish kills;

WHEREAS, Secretary Benhur Abalos of the Department of Interior and Local Government (DILG) has directed through Memorandum Circular No. 2023-065 that all governors, city and municipal mayors, punong barangays, including the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) Minister of Interior and Local Government, DILG regional directors and all others concerned to prepare for El Niño to mitigate its impact in their respective areas;

WHEREAS, a ranking official of the PAGASA warned that Mindanao will be severely affected by the looming El Niño phenomenon – with at least a thirty (35) percent reduction in rainwater brought by lack of typhoons – followed by Visayas and Luzon;

WHEREAS, there is a need to undertake preparatory measures in the BARMM to mitigate and if possible, prevent the impending effects of El Niño:

NOW, THEREFORE, be it

RESOLVED, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, To urge the Government of the Day (GOTD) to institute preparatory measures for the impending El Niño phenomenon.

RESOLVED, FURTHER, That this Resolution be forwarded to the Office of the Chief Minister, for information and appropriate action.

ADOPTED, Muharram 07, 1445/July 25, 2023.
