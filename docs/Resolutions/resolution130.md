BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT

**THIRD REGULAR SESSION**

**RESOLUTION NO. 130**

**RESOLUTION DIRECTING THE BANGSAMORO *DARUL-IFTA* TO ISSUE *FATWA* ON WHAT CONSTITUTE *HALAL* CONDUCT OF PUBLIC OFFICIALS AND EMPLOYEES, AS *KHALIFA*, IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS GOVERNMENT PURSUANT TO MORAL GOVERNANCE**

**WHEREAS,** the Constitution provides that “Public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees must, at all times, be accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency; act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives.”;

**WHEREAS,** our Bangsamoro Administrative Code, in view of its adherence to Moral Governance, has considered public office as “sacred public trust”;

**WHEREAS,** under the principles of Moral Governance, public officers and employees are required, at all times, not only to be accountable to the people but to the Almighty God;

**WHEREAS,** public officers and employees in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) are “*khalifa*” and, in public service, they shall act with the utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives;

**WHEREAS,** under the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, *khalifa* refers to a human as a vicegerent, trustee, steward of Allah (*Subhannahu Wa’ Taalah*) on earth as provided;

**WHEREAS,** both the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the Bangsamoro Administrative Code underscored *halal* but only in the context of regulation, development, and certification of food and non-food industry within the BARMM;

**WHEREAS,** *halal* should not be limited in that concept on food and non-food industry for purposes of development, certification, and regulation but *halal* should also encompass public officers and employees’ code of conduct and responsibility in the government in pursuant to the Bangsamoro Government affirmation that public office is a sacred public trust and that they as *khalifa*, they are trustee vicegerent, trustee, steward of Allah (*Subhannahu Wa’ Taalah*) on earth and specifically as public servant in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Autonomous Government should define the parameters on what constitute *halal* acts and conduct in the government;

**WHEREAS,** under the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, the Bangsamoro *Darul-Ifta* has the power to “promulgate *fatwa* (religious edicts) pertaining to the whole breadth of Islamic jurisprudence”;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro *Darul-Ifta* can issue a *fatwa* in a Filipino and other major languages of Bangsamoro that shall define what constitute *halal* acts and conduct of Bangsamoro officials and members, being *khalifa*, in pursuit of Moral Governance thereby affirming that public office is a sacred public trust;

**WHEREAS,** the *fatwa* that shall be issued by the Bangsamoro *Darul-Ifta* can be considered by the Bangsamoro Parliament for purposes of possible codification as Code of Conduct of Bangsamoro in the Government.

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to direct the Bangsamoro *Darul-Ifta* to issue *fatwa* on what constitute *halal* conduct of public officials and employees, in their capacities as *khalifa*, in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Government pursuant to moral governance.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** to forward this Resolution to the office of the Bangsamoro *Darul-Ifta* for information and appropriate consideration.

**ADOPTED,** June 24, 2021.
