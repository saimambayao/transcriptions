**FIRST REGULAR SESSION**

**RESOLUTION NO. 60**

**RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO TO PROVIDE A STANDBY EMERGENCY MEDICAL ASSISTANCE TEAM AT THE SHARIFF KABUNSUAN CULTURAL COMPLEX (SKCC) DURING PARLIAMENTARY SESSIONS OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government, subject to Section 20, Article X of the Constitution and Republic Act No. 11054, shall exercise authority to promote health and safety;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government is mandated to “exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare” (Sec. 3, Art. V, RA 11054);

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government shall “adopt a policy on health that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents” and “uphold the peoples' right to have access to essential goods health, and other social services that promote their well-being.” (Sec. 22, Art. IX, RA 11054);

**WHEREAS**, there was an incident during a session of the Parliament where a staff of a Member of the Parliament collapsed inside the session hall and was immediately rushed by some Parliament members and staff to a hospital, where he later transpired;

**WHEREAS**, there is a need for a standby emergency medical assistance team to provide emergency medical care and first-aid services when needed by the Parliament members, staff, guests, and the audience during sessions of the Parliament;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, To request the Ministry of Health of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to provide a standby emergency medical assistance team at the Shariff Kabunsuan Cultural Complex (SKCC) during parliamentary sessions of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, to forward this Resolution to the Honorable Minister Saffrullah M. Dipatuan, Ministry of Health-BARMM, for appropriated consideration.

**Adopted**, January 31, 2020.
