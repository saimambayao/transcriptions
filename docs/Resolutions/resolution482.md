**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 482**

**RESOLUTION COMMENDING CHIEF MINISTER AHOD B. EBRAHIM AND THE MINISTRY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY UNDER THE LEADERSHIP OF MINISTER AND MEMBER OF THE PARLIAMENT ENGINEER AIDA M. SILONGAN, MPDS FOR ITS BANGSAMORO ASSISTANCE FOR SCIENCE EDUCATION AND OTHER SIMILAR SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAMS, AND CONGRATULATING THE FIRST BATCH OF SCHOLARS (YEAR 2020) OF THE BANGSAMORO ASSISTANCE FOR SCIENCE EDUCATION FOR THE ACADEMIC YEAR 2023-2024 GRADUATES, SOME OF WHOM HAVE BROUGHT FURTHER HONOR TO THE BANGSAMORO BY EARNING PRESTIGIOUS LATIN AWARDS, INCLUDING THE HIGHEST LATIN HONORS FROM ESTEEMED INSTITUTIONS WITHIN THE REGION, THEREBY SHOWCASING THE EXCELLENCE OF BANGSAMORO STUDENTS**

**WHEREAS,** Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim and the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), under the distinguished leadership of Minister and Member of the Parliament Engr. Aida M. Silongan, MPDS, has successfully implemented the Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education (BASE) along with other similar scholarship programs to support and advance the education of Bangsamoro youth;

**WHEREAS,** the first batch of BASE scholars (year 2020) has successfully completed their studies and graduated in the academic year 2023-2024;

**WHEREAS,** several graduates from this cohort have earned prestigious Latin awards, including the highest Latin honors from esteemed institutions within the region, thereby the Bangsamoro Transition Authority recognizes their exceptional achievements for their outstanding academic accomplishments and for bringing further honor and recognition to the Bangsamoro community;

**WHEREAS,** the following are the students who have earned prestigious Latin awards:

**WHEREAS,** these achievements exemplify the dedication, perseverance, and academic excellence of Bangsamoro students, serving as a source of inspiration for future generations;

**WHEREAS,** a total of seven hundred fourteen (714) students from the 1$^{st}$ batch to 4$^{th}$ batch have been granted the BASE scholarship, two hundred eight (208) students with the BASE MERIT scholarship, four hundred forty-four (444) with the *Mujahideen/Mujahidat* Assistance for Science Education scholarship, and four hundred (400) with the BASE scholarship, reflecting the significant investment in the education of Bangsamoro children and youth as the future of the Bangsamoro;

**WHEREAS,** the MOST, as the leading agency in the implementation of programs, projects, and initiatives related to science, technology, and innovations, envisions technologically advanced and improved access to quality education through science and technology scholarship programs, capacity building for science and mathematics teachers, and upgrading of science laboratories;

**WHEREAS,** the MOST has implemented several significant programs under the Science, Education, Scholarships, and Grants division to support Bangsamoro youth in the fields of science and technology, including but not limited to the BASE with a benefit of Eighty Thousand Pesos (PHP 80,000.00) per scholar per annum:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To commend the Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim and the Ministry of Science and Technology under the leadership of Minister and Member of the Parliament Engineer Aida M. Silongan, MPDS for its Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education and other similar scholarship programs, and congratulates the first batch of scholars (year 2020) of the Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education for the academic year 2023-2024 graduates, some of whom have brought further honor to the Bangsamoro by earning prestigious Latin awards, including the highest Latin honors from esteemed institutions within the region, thereby showcasing the excellence of Bangsamoro students.

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** To furnish a copy of this Resolution to Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim, the MOST, and to all the aforementioned Bangsamoro Scholars who graduated with Latin honors as a token of recognition and appreciation for their remarkable achievements and for this Resolution’s necessary effects.
