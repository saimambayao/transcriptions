**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 525**

**RESOLUTION URGING THE BANGSAMORO MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, OFFICES, AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS TO CONDUCT ANNUAL TREE PLANTING ACTIVITY, IN ALIGNMENT WITH REPUBLIC ACT NO. 10176, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE “ARBOR DAY ACT OF 2012”, EVERY JANUARY 21 AS PART OF THE BANGSAMORO FOUNDATION DAY CELEBRATION IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, ENCOURAGING PARTICIPATION OF EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS, NON-GOVERNMENT ORGANIZATIONS, AND PRIVATE ESTABLISHMENTS WITHIN THE REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Section 16, Article II of the 1987 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines provides that:

*“The State shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature;”*

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the *“Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (OLBARMM)”* mandates the Bangsamoro Government to enhance and preserve a balanced ecology within its territorial jurisdiction;

**WHEREAS**, Republic Act No. 10176, also known as the *“Arbor Day Act of 2012”*, mandates all local government units (LGUs) to designate a day for tree planting activities and allocate a budget for such annual events;

**WHEREAS**, recurring states of calamity were declared across the Bangsamoro Region in 2022 and 2024 due to severe weather events, emphasizing the need for enhanced ecological resilience;

**WHEREAS**, on August 18, 2022, the Honorable Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim declared the Bangsamoro Region in a state of calamity from August 18 to October 31, 2022, through Proclamation No. 0003, Series of 2022. This declaration was due to widespread flooding caused by successive weather systems that affected large parts of Maguindanao, Special Geographic Area, and Cotabato City;

**WHEREAS**, on October 29, 2022, the Honorable Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim also declared a state of calamity in the Bangsamoro Region after Typhoon Paeng caused massive flooding and landslides in some parts of the region—particularly in Cotabato City, Maguindanao del Norte, and Maguindanao del Sur. The typhoon displaced thousands of families, resulted in widespread damage to properties, infrastructures, power stations, and telecommunications services, and led to numerous casualties;

**WHEREAS**, on November 02, 2022, His Excellency President Ferdinand Marcos, Jr. signed Proclamation No. 84, placing Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), Regions IV-A (Calabarzon), V (Bicol Region), and VI (Western Visayas) under the State of Calamity for six (6) months due to the devastation caused by Severe Tropical Storm *“Paeng;”*

**WHEREAS**, on April 29, 2024, the Honorable Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim signed Proclamation No. 002, Series of 2024, declaring a State of Calamity in the BARMM due to the effects of El Niño;

**WHEREAS**, the *Qur’an* and *Sunnah* designate human beings as stewards of the earth, emphasizing the importance and the rewards of caring for the environment, including activities such as planting trees;

**WHEREAS**, the article *“Harnessing the Power of Trees: Nature’s Flood Protectors”* by the Provincial Environment and Natural Resources Office declares that as climate change intensifies flooding, communities worldwide are turning to trees as a natural and cost-effective flood mitigation strategy. Trees play a vital role by intercepting, absorbing, and slowing floodwaters, thereby protecting communities from severe impacts;

**WHEREAS**, the article *“Weathering the Storm: Natural Solutions to El Niño”* from Trees4Trees, a non-profit organization, discusses how trees can help prevent disasters such as erosion, floods, drought, and water shortages associated with El Niño. It highlights the benefits of planting trees in Indonesia to retain soil moisture, provide shade, and prevent erosion during the dry El Niño season;

**WHEREAS**, during El Niño, establishing an adaptable and soil-suitable nursery is crucial for cultivating drought-resistant and heat-tolerant tree species. Planting at the right time during brief rainfalls and actively engaging the community is essential for resilience against the challenges posed by El Niño, ensuring the success of tree planting initiatives;

**WHEREAS**, the article *“5 Ways Planting Trees Helps Gender Equality”* by One Tree Planted highlights women as inherent nurturers and explains how tree-planting initiatives empower them by enhancing decision-making, food security, education, and climate resilience. It emphasizes that all members of society shall emulate women’s nurturing roles, particularly by utilizing tree planting to mitigate the effects of natural calamities;

**WHEREAS**, on January 21, 2019, the OLBARMM was ratified through a plebiscite, leading to the establishment of the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, Section 10, Chapter 2, Book I of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13, otherwise known as the *“Bangsamoro Administrative Code”*, proclaims January 21 as the Bangsamoro Foundation Day, recognizing it as a non-working holiday to celebrate the establishment of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government is committed to sustainable development, disaster risk reduction and management, and climate change adaptation, as well as environmental conservation, recognizing the vital role of trees in mitigating climate change, protecting biodiversity, and ensuring a healthy ecosystem, and thus designating January 21 as *“Bangsamoro Tree Planting Day”* aligns with the celebration of Bangsamoro Foundation Day, symbolizing the region’s commitment to environmental stewardship;

**WHEREAS**, encouraging participation from Bangsamoro ministries, agencies, and offices, as well as LGUs, educational institutions, non-government organizations (NGOs), and private establishments within the BARMM in tree planting activities on this day will foster a collective effort towards community engagement in environmental protection, preservation, and promotion:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To urge the Bangsamoro ministries, agencies, offices, and local government units to conduct annual tree planting activity, in alignment with Republic Act No. 10176, otherwise known as the *“Arbor Day Act of 2012”*, every January 21 as part of the Bangsamoro Foundation Day celebration in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, encouraging participation of educational institutions, non-government organizations, and private establishments within the region.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To furnish this Resolution to the Office of the Chief Minister, all Bangsamoro ministries, agencies, offices, LGUs, educational institutions, and networks of NGOs, and private establishments, for their information, guidance, and appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, *Rajab* 23, 1446/January 23, 2025.
