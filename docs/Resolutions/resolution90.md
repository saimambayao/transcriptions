**SECOND REGULAR SESSION**

**RESOLUTION NO. 90**

**RESOLUTION ADOPTING AND APPROVING THE INTERIM COMPOSITION OF THE RESPECTIVE RECRUITMENT, SELECTION, AND PLACEMENT BOARDS OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY’S MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES**

**WHEREAS**, Section 4 (c), Article XVI of RA 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority shall prioritize the organization of the bureaucracy of the Bangsamoro Government during transition, including the approval and implementation of a transition plan, and the institution of a placement process for hiring of personnel during transition;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority approved a Transition Plan, which includes the Guidelines on the Recruitment, Selection and Placement of Human Resource in the Bangsamoro Government (“Guidelines”);

**WHEREAS**, in the said Guidelines, each Ministry, Office, or Agency shall have its own Recruitment, Selection and Placement Board (RSPB) which shall deliberate the positions, and screen applications to vacant positions;

**WHEREAS**, in January 2020, ministries with approved Notice of Organization, Staffing, and Compensation Action (NOSCA) issued by the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management commenced their hiring process by constituting their *interim* RSPBs and publishing vacant positions, as required by law;

**WHEREAS**, Section 88, Rule IX of the Omnibus Rules on Appointments and Other Human Resource Actions (ORAOHRA, Revised 2018) prescribes the composition of an agency’s Human Resource Merit Promotion and Selection Board (HRMPSB);

**WHEREAS**, in CSC Resolution No. 2000769 issued on August 28, 2020, the Civil Service Commission recognizes that the Bangsamoro Organic Law grants the Bangsamoro Transition Authority the power to institute a placement process for hiring of personnel during the transition, which accordingly, incidentally and necessarily includes the determination of the composition of the selection board as the latter is a major component of the placement process;

**WHEREAS**, in the same resolution, the Civil Service Commission applied the requirements of the ORAOHRA for Local Government Units on the composition of the RSPBs to the BARMM by analogy;

**WHEREAS**, the Office of the Chief Minister submitted the list of *interim* composition of the respective RSPBs of the ministries, agencies, and offices to the Bangsamoro Parliament, for appropriate action;

**WHEREAS**, to facilitate the hiring processes in the BARMM, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled, deems it necessary to approve and adopt the *interim* composition of the respective RSPBs of the ministries, agencies, and offices until their regular members qualify;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to approve and adopt the *interim* composition of the respective RSPBs of the ministries, agencies, and offices until their regular members qualify.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that the respective Ministries, Offices, and Agencies of the BARMM shall continue their hiring processes through their respective RSPBs, as hereunder approved, adopted, and confirmed.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE**, that the Heads of Offices of the Ministries, Offices, and Agencies of the BARMM shall prioritize the filling up of positions which under the ORAOHRA shall be regular members of their respective RSPBs.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE**, that upon hiring of personnel who qualify to become regular members of the RSPBs of the Ministries, Offices, and Agencies of the BARMM, the concerned Head of Office shall reconstitute the said RSPB to be composed of such regular members thereof in accordance with the provisions of the ORAOHRA.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE**, that all Ministries, Offices, and Agencies of the BARMM must regularly submit the composition of their duly constituted RSPBs to the Parliament on or before January 31ˢᵗ of each year.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, Forward this Resolution to all BARMM offices for information and appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, October 28, 2020.
