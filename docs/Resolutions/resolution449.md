**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 449**

**RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING THE SIX BANGSAMORO TOPNOTCHERS AND ALL THE BANGSAMORO PASSERS IN THE RECENTLY CONCLUDED RESPIRATORY THERAPY LICENSURE EXAMINATION FOR BRINGING PRIDE AND HONOR TO THE BANGSAMORO PEOPLE**

**WHEREAS,** Section 13, Article II of the 1987 Constitution and Section 13, Article IX of the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao both recognize the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being, as well as their role to inculcate in the youth patriotism and nationalism, and encourage their involvement in public and civic affairs;

**WHEREAS,** the Respiratory Therapy Licensure Examination (RTLE) given by the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) took place on February 6-7, 2024, given by the Board of Respiratory Therapy in National Capital Region, Baguio, Cebu, Davao, and Zamboanga;

**WHEREAS,** on February 12, 2024, the PRC announced that out of the one thousand three hundred eighty-three (1,383) examinees, one thousand seventy-seven (1,077) passed the RTLE;

**WHEREAS,** among the top ten (10) performing examinees, six (6) of them were young Bangsamoro topnotchers who garnered the highest scores and dominated the RTLE, namely:

1. Top 3, Ms. Hannah Acosta Sangkay, with an exemplary average score of 91.25%, from San Pedro College — Davao City;
2. Top 6, Mr. Hafsin A. Eisa, with an exemplary average score of 90.50%, from Saint Jude College — Manila;
3. Top 8, Mr. Muhammad Alnashri D. Ismail, with an exemplary average score of 90.00% from Our Lady of Fatima University – Valenzuela;
4. Top 9, Mr. Abdul Salam S. Abdulhamid, with an exemplary average score of 89.75% from San Pedro College – Davao City;
5. Top 10, Ms. Nadjahsalam G. Dimaudtang, with an exemplary average score of 89.50% from San Pedro College – Davao City; and
6. Top 10, Ms. Norliza S. Salamun, with an exemplary average score of 89.50% from Universidad De Zamboanga (for ZAEC);

**WHEREAS,** their exceptional performance as topnotchers together with all the Bangsamoro passers serves as a guiding light for the Bangsamoro children, youth, and young professionals, resonating not only within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region but also across the entire nation;

**WHEREAS,** acknowledging their remarkable achievement encourages and inspires all the Bangsamoro youth to pursue academic and professional excellence, drawing inspiration from their exemplary performance as role models;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, in recognition of achievement and all other Bangsamoro passers of the RTLE, deems it fitting to commend their unwavering commitment to academic hard work and excellence:

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To congratulate and commend the six Bangsamoro topnotchers and all the Bangsamoro passers in the recently concluded Respiratory Therapy Licensure Examination for bringing pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** To furnish this Resolution to Ms. Hannah Acosta Sangkay, Mr. Hafsin A. Eisa, Mr. Muhammad Alnashri D. Ismail, Mr. Abdul Salam S. Abdulhamid, Ms. Nadjahsalam G. Dimaudtang, and Ms. Norliza S. Salamun as a token of our recognition and appreciation for their achievements as well as for this Resolution’s necessary effects, for information.

**ADOPTED,** *Dhu Al-Qa’dah 13, 1445/May 21, 2024.*
