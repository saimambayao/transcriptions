**AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO AGRICULTURE AND FISHERIES TRAINING INSTITUTE**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute Act”.

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous State to promote agriculture as a means to alleviate poverty and food sufficiency in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. To this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall give priority to the promotion of science and technology training and innovation for all Bangsamoro Farmers and Fisherfolks. It shall develop an extensive information campaign to actively promote and disseminate available modern agriculture and fisheries techniques, innovations and knowledge.

**SECTION 3. Establishment of Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute; Powers and Functions.** – There is hereby created Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute (BAFTI) which shall have the following powers and functions:
(a) Lead in the formulation of the regional agriculture and fisheries extension (AFE) agenda and budget;
(b) Prepare an integrated plan for publicly-funded training programs in agriculture and fisheries;
(c) Formulate and issue guidelines in planning, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating AFE programs;
(d) Assist, in coordination with State universities and colleges (SUCs), the LGU extension system by improving their effectiveness and efficiency through capability-building and complementary extension activities such as technical assistance, training of LGU personnel, improvement of physical facilities, extension cum research, and information support services.

**SECTION 4. BAFTI Offices.** – There are hereby established at least three (3) Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute Offices in key areas of the Bangsamoro territory. Each Office shall be headed by a Director I.

The Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute Offices shall be attached to the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform, all directly reporting to the Office of the Minister.

**SECTION 6. Partnership with the Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority.** – Farmers’ and fisherfolks’ cooperatives shall be the cornerstone for training development and dissemination. As such, the Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority shall be a key partner in the dissemination of information relating to training and extension services, as well as assessment of the needs of farmers and fisherfolk.

**SECTION 7. Research, Innovation, and Science and Technology.** – The BAFTI shall give priority to research and keep itself updated with modern agriculture and fisheries trends, including the use of machineries. Training modules shall be created and updated regularly in partnership with the State Universities and Colleges.

**SECTION 8. Training Needs Assessment and Information, Education and Communication Materials.** – The BAFTI, together with the MAFAR and the LGU concerned, shall conduct a Training Needs Assessment for the Bangsamoro Farmers and Fisherfolks in order to deliver the correct, culturally appropriate, and relevant training to the Farmers and Fisherfolks. The BAFTI shall also deliver culturally appropriate Information, Education and Communication (IEC) materials in the language most appropriate to the end-user.

**SECTION 9. Halal Development.** – The BAFTI shall prioritize the development of the Halal industry and shall include the relevance of promoting Halal standards and its incentives in all training modules.

**SECTION 10. Agriculture Short Courses.** – The BAFTI shall cooperate with the Technical Education of the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education for the creation of Agriculture and Fisheries short courses, which shall include short-term training on agriculture and fisheries, and related marketing and enterprise.

**SECTION 11. Private Participation.** —The BAFTI shall recognize the invaluable participation of the private sector in the development of extension services all throughout the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. To this end, public-private corporations and partnerships shall be actively sought and encouraged.

**SECTION 12. Appropriations.** — The funds necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act shall be charged against the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government.

**SECTION 13. Separability Clause.** - Should any provision of this Act be subsequently declared unconstitutional, the other provisions not so declared shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 14. Repealing Clause.** — All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations, or any part thereof, which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SECTION 15. Effectivity.** — This Act shall take effect immediately.
