**AN ACT**

**PROVIDING FOR RECOGNITION AND INCENTIVES TO BANGSAMORO TOPNOTCHERS, SPORTS ACHIEVERS, AND OTHER EXCEPTIONAL INDIVIDUALS, AND PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR**

**BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **"Bangsamoro Achievers Act of 2023."**

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – The Bangsamoro Government hereby recognizes the vital role of Bangsamoro professionals, athletes, and other exceptional individuals in contributing to nation-building and the overall growth and development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It shall therefore promote and sustain their development, providing them with proper recognition, support, and incentives. This includes acknowledging and rewarding topnotchers in professional licensure examinations, those who excel in sports competitions both at the national and international levels, and those who achieve exceptional accomplishments in various fields of endeavor such as arts, culture, or community service. These actions are seen as efforts that bring pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people and contribute to their upliftment and societal growth.

The Bangsamoro Government affirms the importance of providing a nurturing environment for these individuals to further excel in their respective fields, thereby enabling them to become role models for the Bangsamoro youth, children, and future generations. The Bangsamoro Government commits to providing these individuals with the resources they need to succeed, including but not limited to monetary incentives, scholarships for further education, career development opportunities, and access to facilities and resources.

Furthermore, the Bangsamoro Government acknowledges the role of educational institutions, training centers, and organizations in producing these outstanding individuals and commits to provide them with the necessary support to continue producing and nurturing topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals.

It is also the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to create a platform for these achievers to be recognized and applauded for their contributions. This will be facilitated through mechanisms such as the establishment of a Bangsamoro Wall of Achievers and the publication of their achievements on official websites and social media pages of the Bangsamoro Government.

Ultimately, the Bangsamoro Government endeavors to create a culture of excellence, hard work, and achievement among the Bangsamoro people, thereby encouraging every individual to strive for excellence and contribute to the development of BARMM and the nation as a whole.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.** - The following terms are defined as:

a. “Bar examinations” refers to the examinations conducted by the Supreme Court for the admission of qualified persons to practice law and membership in the Integrated Bar of the Philippines;

b. “Board examinations” refers to the examinations given Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) to qualified persons to determine whether they shall be issued with a license to practice their professions;

c. “Topnotchers” refers to bar or board examinees who are among the ten examinees who obtained the highest ratings;

d. "Sports Achievers" refers to Bangsamoro athletes who have won medals at national or international sports competitions, including e-sports, recognized by the Bangsamoro Sports Commission;

e. "Exceptional Individuals" refers to Bangsamoro individuals who have shown exceptional achievements in international competitions, including Qur’an Reading, and in other fields such as arts, culture, or community service, as recognized by the relevant Bangsamoro Government bodies;

f. “Bangsamoro Achievers” refers to Bangsamoro individuals who have excelled as topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals and achieved outstanding success and recognition in their respective fields;

g. "Natives" as used in this Act refers to individuals who are considered indigenous or belonging to the original inhabitants of the Bangsamoro region. It implies a connection to the ancestral heritage, cultural identity, and historical roots of the Bangsamoro people as provided in the Bangsamoro Organic Law.

**SECTION 4. Recognition of Bangsamoro Achievers.** - The Bangsamoro topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals, including those from communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, referred to in this Act are hereby declared to be entitled to recognition by the Bangsamoro Government under the guidelines provided for in this Act.

**SECTION 5. Forms of Recognition and Incentives.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall give the topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals the following forms of recognition and incentives:

a. Citations signed by the Chief Minister and Speaker which shall be awarded and received by the topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals in a ceremony before the Parliament and in the presence of the Wali;

b. Inscription in the Bangsamoro Book of Achievers of the names of the topnotcher, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals. The *Bangsamoro Book of Achievers* is therefore hereby established;

c. Monetary incentives under the following terms:

i. Topnotchers in bar and board examinations who attain the first rank, as well as athletes who are awarded gold medals, shall be awarded a monetary incentive of One Hundred Thousand Pesos (Php100,000.00).

ii. Those who rank from the second to the fifth position in the bar and board examinations, as well as athletes who are awarded silver medals, shall be awarded a monetary incentive of Seventy-Five Thousand Pesos (Php75,000.00).

iii. For individuals who attain a rank between the sixth and the tenth positions in the bar and board examinations, as well as athletes who are awarded bronze medals, shall be awarded a monetary incentive of Fifty Thousand Pesos (Php50,000.00).

iv. Other exceptional individuals shall also be eligible for monetary incentives. The specific amounts of these incentives will be determined by the relevant Bangsamoro Government bodies conferring awards for their exceptional achievements in fields such as arts, culture, or community service.

d. **Full scholarship grant** to the Bangsamoro Achievers if they decide to pursue undergraduate, graduate, and postgraduate degrees or other fields of specialization as provided by the implementing guidelines of the implementing agencies. *Provided, That* the implementing agency may impose reasonable terms and conditions of availment. *Provided further, That* the grantee shall contribute to the development of the Bangsamoro region by rendering return service as employee, contractual, contract of service, or job order, to the Bangsamoro Government equivalent to at least half the length of the scholarship period.

In exceptional cases where a grantee is unable to fulfill the return service obligation due to valid reasons such as severe illness, disability, or other unforeseen circumstances, the implementing agency shall review and determine appropriate alternatives or waivers, considering the individual's circumstances and ensuring fairness and compassion.

e. **Career development opportunities and access to appropriate training facilities, programs, and resources** for both topnotchers and sports achievers;

f. Specific support and recognition mechanisms for exceptional individuals, as determined by the relevant Bangsamoro Government bodies.


**SECTION 6. Requirements.** - For a topnotcher, sports achiever, or exceptional individual to be entitled to the incentives under this Act, he or she shall submit the following minimum documentary requirements:

a. For topnotchers, a certification or document by the Supreme Court Bar Confidant or the Philippine Regulatory Commission showing proof that he or she belongs to the Top Ten Examinees;

b. For sports achievers, a certificate from the Bangsamoro Sports Commission confirming their achievement;

c. For exceptional individuals, a certificate from the relevant Bangsamoro Government body confirming their achievement; and

d. Common documentary requirements for all Bangsamoro Achievers:

i. Authenticated Certificate of Live Birth issued by Philippine Statistics Authority;

ii. A valid identification (ID) card;

iii. Barangay Certificate on the residency of the topnotcher, sports achiever, or exceptional individual; and

iv. Such other documents or competent proof as the implementing agency deems acceptable and appropriate.

**SECTION 7. Publication.** – The Bangsamoro examinees who are declared topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals shall be given due recognition for bringing pride and honor to the Bangsamoro through publication and posting of their achievements in the Bangsamoro Government official websites and pages for information and dissemination.

**SECTION 8. Non-Prejudicial Clause.** - The topnotchers, sports achievers, and exceptional individuals shall not be precluded from receiving the recognition provided in this Act on account that they have received awards, recognitions, incentives, and/or scholarships from other institutions.

**SECTION 9. Implementing Agencies.** - The Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), the Bangsamoro Sports Commission (BSC), and other Bangsamoro Government bodies with recognition and incentive awards for exceptional individuals shall be the implementing agencies responsible for the effective implementation of this Act. They shall adopt their respective implementation guidelines and program proposals, in consultation with relevant stakeholders, necessary to ensure the smooth execution of the recognition and incentive programs outlined in this Act.

The MBHTE, in coordination with relevant bodies, shall oversee the recognition of Bangsamoro achievers in professional licensure examinations. It shall establish procedures to validate and verify the achievements of topnotchers and ensure the provision of monetary incentives and other forms of recognition as stipulated in this Act. The MBHTE shall also coordinate with educational institutions to provide scholarship grants and facilitate the career development of topnotchers who opt to pursue graduate or postgraduate degrees.

The BSC, on the other hand, shall be responsible for recognizing Bangsamoro athletes who have excelled in national and international sports competitions, including e-sports, as specified in this Act. It shall develop the necessary mechanisms to identify sports achievers, award them with monetary incentives, provide career development opportunities, and grant them access to high-level training facilities and resources. The Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall collaborate with relevant sports organizations to ensure the proper assessment and recognition of sports achievements.

Furthermore, other Bangsamoro Government bodies with recognition and incentive awards for exceptional individuals, including among others the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC), Bangsamoro Youth Commission (BYC), Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH), Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC), Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), and Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) shall actively participate in the implementation of this Act. These bodies shall provide recognition, incentives, and support to exceptional individuals who have demonstrated outstanding achievements in arts, culture, community service, and other relevant fields, including international competitions. They shall determine the specific amounts of monetary incentives and other forms of support for exceptional individuals based on their respective fields of achievement.

The implementing agencies shall document and assess the impact of this Act on the development and recognition of Bangsamoro achievers. They shall gather feedback and conduct evaluations to identify areas for improvement, propose necessary amendments or modifications, and enhance the effectiveness of the recognition and incentive programs. The implementing agencies shall coordinate and collaborate with the Office of the Chief Minister and other relevant bodies and organizations to ensure the effective implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of this Act.

**SECTION 10. Supervision and Coordination.** - The Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) shall supervise and coordinate the implementation of this Act. The Chief Minister shall exercise oversight, ensure the proper execution of the recognition and incentive programs, monitor the progress and impact of this Act, and provide necessary guidance and support to the implementing agencies. As such, the OCM shall issue directives and guidelines to ensure effective and efficient implementation of the provisions of this Act, as well as facilitate coordination among the implementing agencies for seamless implementation.

**SECTION 11. Coordinating Council for Recognition of Bangsamoro Achievers.** The Coordinating Council for Recognition of Bangsamoro Achievers (CCRBA) is hereby established. The CCRBA shall serve as the central coordinating body responsible for the effective implementation and coordination of the recognition and incentive programs outlined in this Act.

(a) It shall be composed of the following members:

(1) Chief Minister, as Chairperson
(2) Minister of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education, as Vice Chairperson
(3) Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Sports Commission, as Vice Chairperson
(4) Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Women Commission, as Member
(5) Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Youth Commission, as Member
(6) Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage, as Member
(7) Executive Director of the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities, as Member
(8) Minister of the Ministry of Science and Technology, as Member
(9) Minister of the Ministry of Public Order and Safety, as Member
(10) Heads of such other ministries, offices, and agencies with recognition and incentive programs for Bangsamoro Achievers, as Members

(b) It shall, among others, have the following functions and responsibilities:

(1) Develop and recommend policies, guidelines, and procedures for the implementation of the recognition and incentive programs under this Act;
(2) Coordinate and collaborate with the implementing agencies to ensure the smooth execution of the recognition and incentive programs;
(3) Review and evaluate the effectiveness of the recognition and incentive programs and recommend necessary amendments or modifications to enhance their efficiency and effectiveness; and
(4) Undertake other functions and responsibilities necessary to achieve the objectives of this Act.


**SECTION 12. Conferment of Recognition.** The implementing agency shall endorse the conferment of recognition of the Bangsamoro Achievers to the Bangsamoro Parliament.

Upon receipt of the endorsement or on its own initiative, the Bangsamoro Parliament may extend a formal invitation to the Bangsamoro Achiever to attend a plenary session. The Parliament may suspend its rules for the presentation and acknowledgment of the Achiever’s contributions.

The concerned implementing agency shall facilitate the logistics of the Achiever's participation, including accommodation and transportation. The necessary arrangements shall be made to ensure the smooth and dignified involvement of the Achiever in the plenary session.

**SECTION 13. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – Within sixty (60) days after the effectivity of this Act, the OCM, in collaboration with the implementing agencies and in consultation with relevant stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 14. Periodic Review.** - The MBHTE, Bangsamoro Sports Commission, and other relevant Bangsamoro Government bodies shall review every three (3) years or as they may deem necessary the implementation and effectiveness of this Act. Based on the findings of the review, they shall propose necessary amendments or modifications to improve the effectiveness of this Act and better serve the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people.

**SECTION 15. Appropriation.** – The amount necessary to initially carry out the provisions of this Act shall be sourced from the Contingent Fund, subject to the availability of funds as certified by the Ministry of Finance and Budget and Management (MFBM). Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the corresponding budgets of the implementing agencies in the General Appropriations Act for the Bangsamoro (GAAB).

**SECTION 16. Repealing Clause.** - All laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations or other issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 17. Separability Clause.** - If for any reason, any section or provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other sections or provisions hereof which are not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**SECTION 18. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of general or regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

*Approved.*
