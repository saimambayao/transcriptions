**RESOLUTION ESTABLISHING THE CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICS FOR MEMBERS OF THE PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, the fundamental principle that public office is a public trust is enshrined in the highest law of the land; it is therefore the solemn duty of all government officials and employees to remain at all times accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency; act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government is founded upon the principle of moral governance, which demands that all public officials and employees, especially Members of the Parliament, conduct themselves in accordance with the highest standards of ethical, spiritual, and moral values, thereby ensuring that the exercise of public authority is always guided by integrity, justice, and the common good, in faithful adherence to the mandate that public office is a public trust as enshrined in the Constitution and the Bangsamoro Organic Law;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament, as the highest governing body of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, is vested with the sacred mandate to enact laws and measures that promote, protect, and ensure the welfare and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people and all inhabitants of the region;

**WHEREAS**, Members of the Parliament hold a distinguished office and are consequently bound by a higher standard of conduct; it is therefore incumbent upon each Member to act at all times in a manner that reflects honor upon their own person, preserves the dignity of the office they hold, and safeguards the integrity of the Parliament as an institution;

**WHEREAS**, the establishment of Code of Conduct is essential to provide Members with ethical guidelines governing all their actions, decisions and dealings, both public and private, that affect the integrity of their office; the Code shall therefore serve as the foundational instrument to ensure uniform observance of the important norms of conduct by Members of the Parliament, thereby strengthening public trust and upholding the dignity and honor of the Bangsamoro Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, in order to promote, enforce, and preserve the ethics and privileges of the Parliament, it is imperative that the Committee on Ethics and Privileges be vested with the full authority to conduct thorough, impartial, and fair investigations into any alleged breaches of this Code and, upon finding sufficient cause, to recommend appropriate disciplinary actions to the Plenary against any erring Members of the Parliament;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY**

**RESOLVED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament, to adopt the following Code of Conduct and Ethics for Members of the Parliament in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

**PREAMBLE**

We, the Members of the Parliament, acknowledge our responsibility to uphold the public trust and accountability placed in us by performing our duties with honesty and integrity, in accordance with the core values of moral governance, promoting and protecting the individual rights, respecting the rule of law, the institution and conventions of the Parliament, and implementing our mandates to advance the welfare of the people in Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**RULE I**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 1. *Purpose of the Code.*** — The general purpose of this Code of Conduct and Ethics (hereinafter, “Code”) is to assist all Members in fulfilling their parliamentary duties and obligations to the Parliament, their colleagues, and the people of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. Specifically, it is designed to establish a set of rules governing the conduct and ethics of Members of the Parliament in the exercise of their rights and the discharge of their duties and responsibilities. The Code outlines the minimum ethical standards of conduct and behavior that the Members of Parliament are expected to comply with. It reflects the expectations of the people of Bangsamoro regarding the conduct of legislators and civil servants, emphasizing the importance of upholding propriety, integrity, and ethical values.

**Sec. 2. *Scope and Application.*** — This Code shall apply to all Members of the Bangsamoro Parliament, including the Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Ministers, and Members of the Cabinet who are concurrently Members of Parliament, whether elected through district, party-list, or sectoral representation, and regardless of affiliation or status. The provisions of this Code shall govern the conduct of Members of the Parliament in the performance of their legislative, administrative, and representative functions, as well as in matters where their private interests may affect or appear to affect the discharge of their public duties.

For purposes of this Code, "conduct" shall include actions, statements, and representations made by Members in person, in writing, or through any means of communication, whether physical, digital, or electronic, including social media. The standards and obligations set forth herein shall equally apply to conduct occurring in physical, virtual, or otherwise, and to all forms of public or private communication that may impact the integrity, reputation, or effective functioning of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Sec. 3. *Construction and Interpretation.*** — The provisions of this Code shall be construed and interpreted liberally in favor of upholding the highest standards of integrity, accountability, and public service among Members of the Bangsamoro Parliament. In case of doubt, the interpretation that best promotes transparency, ethical conduct, and the public interest shall prevail. This Code shall be read in harmony with the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees (Republic Act No. 6713), and other applicable national laws. Nothing in this Code shall be construed to diminish or contravene the rights, duties, and obligations of Members of the Parliament as provided by the Constitution, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, and other relevant statutes.

**Sec. 3. *Norms of Conduct.*** — Members of the Parliament shall observe the following norms of conduct in the performance of their official duties:

(a) Commitment to Public Interest. — Members shall always uphold the public interest over and above personal interest. They shall avoid conflicts of interest and shall not use their official position to secure unwarranted benefits, privileges, or exemptions for themselves or others.

(b) Professionalism and Excellence. — Members shall perform and discharge their duties with the highest degree of professionalism, competence, and excellence. They shall endeavor to improve their skills and knowledge to better serve the Bangsamoro people.

(c) Justness and Sincerity. — Members shall act with fairness, honesty, and sincerity in all their official dealings. They shall not discriminate against anyone, especially the poor and underprivileged, and shall extend prompt, adequate, and courteous service to the public.

(d) Political Neutrality. — Members shall provide service to everyone without discrimination and regardless of party affiliation or preference. They shall not allow their political beliefs or affiliations to influence the discharge of their official functions.

(e) Responsiveness to the Public. — Members shall extend prompt, adequate, and courteous service to the public. They shall provide information on their policies and procedures in clear and understandable language, and shall encourage the public to participate in policy-making.

(f) Nationalism and Patriotism. — Members shall at all times be loyal to the Republic of the Philippines and the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, promote the sovereignty and integrity of the nation, and uphold the Constitution and the Bangsamoro Organic Law.

(g) Commitment to Democracy. — Members shall commit themselves to the democratic way of life and values, maintain the principle of accountability, and uphold the rule of law.

(h) Simple Living. — Members and their families shall lead modest lives appropriate to their positions and income. They shall not indulge in extravagant or ostentatious display of wealth in any form.

**RULE II**
**RIGHTS, DUTIES, PRIVILEGES AND IMMUNITIES**

**Sec. 4. *Rights of Members of the Parliament.*** — All Members of the Parliament shall enjoy the following rights:

(a) Equal rights, privileges, and obligations. The rules, procedures, and practices of the Parliament shall be administered impartially;
(b) Right to receive all notices, schedules of sessions, committee hearings, meetings, public hearings, and consultations;
(c) Equal rights to make and present motions;
(d) Right to second motions when necessary;
(e) Right to full and free debate and discussion of motions presented before the plenary sessions, committee hearings, and meetings;
(f) Right to vote on motions, except when there is a conflict of interest or financial and pecuniary interest in the legislative measures under consideration, in which case they must inhibit from participating in the proceedings;
(g) Right to understand the meaning of the issues and questions being debated and discussed;
(h) Right to object when the Parliamentary rules and procedures are being violated;
(i) Shall not, in a debate and discussion of any proposed legislative measure, have to endure personal abuse and attacks from other Members;
(j) Right to access all the minutes and journals of the sessions, committee meetings, and hearings; and
(k) All other rights and privileges provided for under the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the Rules of the Parliament.

**Sec. 5. *Duties.*** — The primary duty of Members of the Parliament shall be legislation. To fulfill this duty, Members are bound by the following: duties to the Parliament, duties to fellow Members; and duties to constituents.

(a) Duties to the Parliament:

1. Prepare, introduce, and advocate for the passage of legislative measures addressing political, social, economic, educational, scientific, technological, cultural, environmental, and other developmental needs in the Bangsamoro;
2. Be obliged to promptly attend plenary sessions, committee hearings, and meetings where they are Members or where they are the authors and sponsors of proposed legislative measures under consideration;
3. Required to stay in the session hall and committee rooms until the end of sessions, committee hearings, and meetings.
4. Must be ready to intelligently participate in debates on legislative measures whether in the plenary, committee hearings and meetings, or public consultations; and
5. Perform other lawful and necessary functions and activities to ensure the passage of legislative measures addressing political, social, economic, educational, scientific, technological, cultural, environmental, and other developmental needs and concerns in the Bangsamoro.

(b) Duties to Fellow Members:

1. Allowed to speak candidly but must allow everyone their turn to speak;
2. Must treat everyone with respect and courtesy, refraining from using abusive and insulting language, imputing accusations of dishonest conduct, or corrupt practices against fellow Members;
3. Members of Parliament must be frank and honest in official dealings with colleagues;
4. Members must exercise particular care to consider the rights and duties of others before utilizing the unique protection available under parliamentary privilege. This privilege should not be abused or used without due regard to accuracy; and
5. Must adhere to the rules of debate as outlined in the Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Parliament.

(c) Duties to Constituents and the Public:

1. Carry out their duties responsibly, with integrity, and honesty;
2. Make decisions solely in the public interest, without being bound by any instructions that would jeopardize their ability to respect and adhere to the present Code;
3. Make utmost efforts to represent the interests of various constituents and serve as their respective voice;
4. Ensure transparency and regularly communicate to inform the public about their accomplishments, programs, and projects; and
5. Conduct regular and periodic consultations on key issues and concerns affecting their constituencies and various stakeholders.

**Sec. 6. *Privileges and Immunities.*** — Any Member of the Parliament who commits an offense within the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction that is punishable by not more than six (6) years imprisonment shall be privileged from arrest while the Parliament is in session.

No Member of the Parliament shall be questioned or be held liable in any other place for any speech or debate in the Parliament or in any of its committees.

The privileges and immunities herein provided shall not be construed to exempt any Member from liability for offenses punishable by more than six (6) years imprisonment, nor from acts committed outside the exercise of their parliamentary functions.

**RULE III**
**PROHIBITED ACTS**

**Sec. 7. *Unparliamentary Acts and Language.*** — No Member of the Parliament shall engage in unparliamentary acts or use unparliamentary language during any session, committee meeting, or official proceeding of the Parliament.

For purposes of this Code, "unparliamentary acts and language" shall include, but are not limited to:

(a) The use of offensive, abusive, obscene, or insulting words or expressions directed at any Member, official, guest, or any person present in the Parliament;
(b) Personal attacks, imputations of improper motives, or allegations of corruption, dishonesty, or other misconduct against any Member or official, unless based on substantiated evidence and raised in accordance with the rules of the Parliament;
(c) The use of language or gestures that incite disorder, violence, or hatred within the Parliament or towards any person or group;
(d) Disruption of proceedings by shouting, banging on tables, or any other disorderly conduct;
(e) Refusal to obey the lawful orders or rulings of the Presiding Officer or the Parliament;
(f) Acts of physical violence, threats, intimidation, or harassment against any Member, official, staff, or guest of the Parliament;
(g) Disclosure of confidential or executive session proceedings without proper authority;
(h) Use of language or acts that undermine the dignity, authority, or integrity of the Parliament or its proceedings;
(i) Any other act or language which, in the judgment of the Parliament or its Presiding Officer, is inconsistent with the standards of decorum, respect, and parliamentary courtesy.

Nothing in this provision shall be construed to limit the right of Members to free and responsible debate, subject to the rules of order and decorum established by the Parliament.

**Sec. 8. *Conflict of Interest.*** — Members of the Parliament shall, during their term of office, not engage, directly or indirectly, in any business or commercial enterprise within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region where such engagement may result in a conflict of interest in the exercise of their official functions.

It shall be unlawful for any Member of the Parliament to directly or indirectly:

(a) Engage in any business transaction with the Bangsamoro Government or with any of its authorized boards, officials, agents, or attorneys, whereby money is to be paid, or property or any other thing of value is to be transferred, directly or indirectly, out of the resources of the Bangsamoro Government to such person or firm;
(b) Hold interests in any cockpit or other games licensed by the Bangsamoro Government;
(c) Purchase any real estate or other property forfeited in favor of the Bangsamoro Government for unpaid costs or assessment, or by virtue of a legal process at the instance of the Bangsamoro Government;
(d) Act as surety for any person contracting or doing business with the Bangsamoro Government for which a surety is required; and
(e) Possess or use any public property of the Bangsamoro Government for private purposes.

All other prohibitions governing the conduct of national public officers relating to prohibited business and pecuniary interest, as provided for under Republic Act No. 6713 and other national laws, shall likewise apply to Members of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Sec. 9. *Unauthorized Practice of Profession.*** — (a) The Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Ministers, and Members of the Cabinet are strictly prohibited from practicing their profession or engaging in any occupation other than the exercise of their official functions as officers of the Bangsamoro Government.

(b) Members of the Parliament who do not hold Cabinet positions may practice their professions, engage in other occupations, or teach in schools, except during session hours. However, Members of the Parliament who are also members of the Philippine Bar shall not:

(1) Appear as counsel before any court in any civil case wherein the Bangsamoro Government or any of its offices, agencies, or instrumentalities is the adverse party;
(2) Appear as counsel in any criminal case wherein an officer or employee of the National Government or the Bangsamoro Government is accused of an offense committed in relation to their office;
(3) Collect any fee for appearance in administrative proceedings involving the Bangsamoro Government; and
(4) Use government property or personnel for private purposes, except when defending the interest of the government.

(c) Doctors of medicine who are Members of the Parliament may practice their profession during official hours only in cases of emergency, provided that no monetary compensation is derived therefrom.

**Sec. 10. *Solicitation or Acceptance of Gifts.*** — No Member of the Parliament shall solicit or accept, directly or indirectly, any gift, gratuity, favor, entertainment, loan, or anything of monetary value from any person in the course of their official duties or in connection with any operation being regulated by, or any transaction which may be affected by, the functions of their office.

As an exception, Members may accept and retain a gift of nominal value tendered and received as a souvenir or mark of courtesy, provided that such acceptance is appropriate or consistent with the interests of the Bangsamoro Government and is permitted by the Speaker of the Parliament.

**Sec. 11. *Non-Compliance with Reporting and Disclosure Requirements.*** — (a) All Members of the Parliament shall strictly comply with all reporting and disclosure requirements mandated by law, this Code, and the rules of the Parliament. Non-compliance, whether by omission, concealment, or misrepresentation, is strictly prohibited and shall constitute a ground for disciplinary action.

(b) The following shall be disclosed by every Member of Parliament:

(1) A complete and sworn Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN), in accordance with applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to Republic Act No. 6713 and the Bangsamoro Organic Law;
(2) Full disclosure of financial and business interests, including those of their spouse and children, upon assumption of office and as may be required by law or the Parliament;
(3) Any potential or actual conflict of interest that may arise in the performance of official duties, including but not limited to the filing, sponsorship, or authorship of bills, resolutions, or other official actions;
(4) Any other information or interest required to be disclosed under national law, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, or the rules of the Parliament; and
(5) Any disclosure required in the course of work, including recusal from deliberations or decisions where a conflict of interest exists.

**Sec. 12. *Improper Influence.*** — (a) Improper influence shall refer to any act, omission, or conduct by a Member of the Bangsamoro Parliament that seeks, directly or indirectly, to use the Member's position, authority, or influence to obtain or secure any undue advantage, benefit, or privilege for oneself or for any other person or entity, contrary to law, public policy, or ethical standards.

(b) The following acts constitute improper influence and are strictly prohibited:

(1) Acting as a paid advocate in any proceeding of Parliament or any of its committees;
(2) Knowingly and improperly promoting any matter, voting on any bill or resolution or asking any question in the Parliament or any of its committees in return for any remuneration, fee, payment, reward or benefit in kind, of a private nature, which the Member, the Member's family within the fourth civil degree of consanguinity or affinity, a business associate of the Member, or any other person or entity from whom the Member expects to receive a financial benefit, is receiving or expects to receive as a consequence;
(3) Knowingly and improperly using his or her influence as a Member to seek to affect a decision by a public official including a Minister, public sector employee, statutory officer or officer of a public body, to further, directly or indirectly, the private interests of the Member, a member of the Member's family, or a business associate of the Member.

**Sec. 13. *Improper or Illegal Use of Resources.*** — No Member of the Parliament shall, directly or indirectly, use, appropriate, or permit the use of any funds, property, equipment, personnel, or other resources of the Bangsamoro Government for any purpose other than those officially sanctioned by law or necessary for the performance of their official duties. The use of public resources for private gain, personal benefit, or for the benefit of any business, commercial enterprise, or political activity is strictly prohibited.

**Sec. 14. *Other Unlawful, Immoral or Improper Conduct.*** — Members of the Parliament shall, at all times, conduct themselves in a manner that upholds the integrity and dignity of the Parliament, both within and outside its premises. Members are strictly prohibited from engaging in any unlawful, immoral, or improper conduct in public or private life.

The followings acts are expressly prohibited:

(a) Committing any act that constitutes a criminal offense under Philippine law or the laws of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
(b) Engaging in corrupt practices, including but not limited to bribery, graft, or other forms of corruption;
(c) Participating in activities that promote or incite violence, discrimination, or hatred against any individual or group, including through posts, comments, or messages on social media or other online platforms;
(d) Engaging in conduct that brings the Parliament or the Bangsamoro Government into disrepute, including public intoxication, gambling in prohibited venues, or involvement in scandalous behavior;
(e) Abuse of authority or position for personal gain or to the prejudice of others;
(f) All forms of gender-based sexual harassment and domestic violence or abuse, including acts of violence against women and children;
(g) Discrimination against any person on the basis of creed, religion, ethnic origin, parentage, gender, age, disability, or other status;
(h) Any act that violates the Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees (Republic Act No. 6713) or other applicable laws and regulations; and
(i) Any other act or omission that is unlawful, immoral, or improper, and which seriously undermines the public’s trust in the Parliament or the Bangsamoro Government.

**RULE IV**
**THE COMMITTEE ON ETHICS AND PRIVILEGES**

**Sec. 15. *Jurisdiction of the Committee.*** — The Committee on Ethics and Privileges (hereinafter, “Committee”) shall have exclusive jurisdiction over all matters relating to the conduct, rights, privileges, safety, dignity, integrity, and reputation of the Bangsamoro Parliament and its Members. It shall investigate and recommend appropriate action on complaints or issues involving violations of this Code or any act or omission that may affect the honor and integrity of the Parliament or its Members.

**Sec. 16. *Composition of the Committee.*** — The Committee shall be composed of seventeen (17) members on the basis of proportional representation affiliated with the Majority and the Minority of the Parliament: *Provided*, however, that the Speaker, Deputy Speakers, Majority Floor Leader, Deputy Majority Floor Leaders, and Minority Floor Leader shall be *ex officio* Members.

The Committee will have one Chairperson and up to three Vice-Chairpersons. The Majority and Minority will each submit their nominees for committee membership and for the positions of Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons to the Speaker. The Majority Floor Leader will present these names to the Parliament, which will confirm them in a plenary session without a vote, based on motions from the Majority and Minority Floor Leaders.

However, the Chairperson must be elected by a majority of all Parliament members in plenary. The Vice-Chairpersons are nominated by a majority of the Committee members, and at least one Vice-Chairperson must come from the Minority.

The Chairperson, Vice-Chairpersons, and Members shall commence the exercise of their duties and functions immediately after their affirmation in the plenary session. They shall cease to perform their duties and functions when their successors shall have been duly elected and qualified.

**Sec. 17. *Powers and Functions of the Committee.*** — The Committee shall have the following powers and functions:

(a) To investigate, *motu proprio* or upon complaint, any alleged violation of this Code or any act or omission by a Member of the Bangsamoro Parliament that may affect the honor, integrity, or dignity of the Parliament or its Members;
(b) To require the attendance of any Member or employee of the Parliament, or any other person, and to compel the production of documents or other evidence necessary to the conduct of its investigations;
(c) To issue *subpoenas ad testificandum* and *duces tecum*, signed by the Chairperson of the Committee, to any person within the jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
(d) To cite in contempt any person who, without legal excuse, refuses to obey any lawful order, subpoena, or process of the Committee, or who acts in a manner that obstructs or impedes the conduct of its proceedings, subject to the rules of the Parliament and the requirements of due process;
(e) To recommend to the Parliament such disciplinary or other appropriate actions as may be warranted by its findings, which may include, but are not limited to: reprimand, censure, suspension for a period not exceeding sixty (60) days, expulsion, or such other measures as may be provided under the rules of the Parliament; and
(f) To perform such other functions as may be necessary to uphold the honor, integrity, and dignity of the Parliament and its Members.

**RULE V**
**PROCEDURES**

**Sec. 18. *Breach of the Code.*** — Any Member of the Parliament who commits a breach of this Code shall be subject to disciplinary proceedings before the Committee. A breach of this Code occurs when a Member engages in any of the prohibited acts enumerated under Rule III or violates any other provision of this Code.

**Sec. 19. *How Initiated.*** — Disciplinary proceedings before the Committee on Ethics and Privileges may be initiated by:

(a) *Motu proprio* action of the Committee, upon its own determination that there exists sufficient cause to investigate alleged misconduct by a member of the Bangsamoro Parliament;
(b) A verified written complaint filed by any member of the Bangsamoro Parliament or by any person directly affected by the alleged misconduct, addressed to the Committee; or
(c) A resolution adopted by the Bangsamoro Parliament, *motu proprio* or upon motion of any member, referring a matter to the Committee for investigation.

The complaint or resolution shall clearly and concisely state the acts or omissions constituting the alleged violation of the Code or other applicable rules, and shall be accompanied by supporting evidence, if any.

**Sec. 20. *Anonymous Complaint.*** — Disciplinary proceedings against a Member of the Parliament may also be initiated by an anonymous complaint, provided that such complaint is supported by public records or other substantial evidence.

**Sec. 21. *Appointment of Investigators.*** — To facilitate the conduct of investigations under this Code, the Committee may designate an investigator or a panel of investigators, not exceeding three (3) members, from among its own members, to conduct a preliminary evaluation and/or formal investigation. Provided, that any preliminary evaluation report or formal investigation report shall be subject to the approval of the Committee.

**Sec. 22. *Preliminary Evaluation.*** — Within ten (10) days upon receipt of a verified or anonymous complaint, the Committee shall promptly conduct a preliminary evaluation to determine whether the complaint is sufficient in form and substance. If the Committee finds the complaint insufficient, or if it *prima facie* appears to be baseless, or filed merely to harass, embarrass, or unduly delay proceedings, the Committee shall dismiss the complaint outright. However, disciplinary proceedings initiated by a resolution of the Parliament shall not be subject to preliminary evaluation under this Section.

**Sec. 23. *Answer of the Respondent.*** — Within five (5) days from receipt of the resolution of the Parliament, or upon the issuance by the Committee of a preliminary evaluation report finding the complaint sufficient in form and substance, as the case may be, the Committee shall promptly furnish the Respondent with a copy of the complaint or resolution, together with all supporting documents and annexes.

The Respondent shall be required to file an Answer, which shall be subscribed and sworn to before a person authorized to administer oaths, within ten (10) days from receipt of the complaint or resolution. The Answer shall specifically address each material allegation in the complaint or resolution and may be accompanied by supporting documents or the affidavits of witnesses, if any.

In cases where the Committee acts *motu proprio*, the Chairperson shall issue an Order, supported by a resolution adopted by the Committee, stating the acts or omissions allegedly committed by the Member and directing the latter within ten (10) days upon receipt to show cause why no disciplinary proceedings should be initiated against him or her.

Failure of the Respondent to file an answer or comply with the show cause order within the prescribed period, without justifiable cause, shall be deemed a waiver of the right to present a defense, and the proceedings shall continue *ex parte* based on the evidence on record.

Service of the complaint, resolution or show cause order and all pertinent documents shall be made personally or, if personal service is not practicable, by registered mail or other reasonable means as may be determined by the Committee to ensure receipt by the Respondent.

**Sec. 24. *Formal Investigation.*** — Upon receipt of the Respondent’s Answer or compliance with the show cause order, or upon the expiration of the period to file the same, the Committee shall promptly determine whether a formal investigation is warranted. If so, the Committee shall commence the formal investigation not earlier than five (5) days nor later than ten (10) days from such receipt or expiration, unless otherwise justified by meritorious circumstances.

The formal investigation shall be completed within thirty (30) days from its commencement, unless extended by the Committee in meritorious cases. The Committee shall ensure that both the Complainant and the Respondent are accorded due process, including the right to be informed of the charges, to present evidence, to confront and cross-examine witnesses, and to be represented by counsel.

The formal investigation may consist of, or combine, the submission of position papers, memoranda, affidavits, and other documentary evidence, as well as the conduct of summary or clarificatory hearings. The Committee may require the attendance of witnesses and the production of documents through *subpoena* or *subpoena duces tecum*.

The Committee may, at its discretion, allow the proceedings to be conducted in person or through electronic means, as may be appropriate.

**Sec. 25. *Resolution of Disciplinary Proceedings.*** — Upon the conclusion of the investigation, the Committee shall render a written decision within thirty (30) days from the termination of the proceedings or from the submission of the investigator’s report, as the case may be. The decision shall clearly state the findings of fact and the legal basis for the Committee’s conclusions, and must be signed by a majority of the Committee members.

A certified copy of the decision shall be furnished to all parties. The parties may file a motion for reconsideration within ten (10) days from receipt of the decision; otherwise, the decision shall become final and executory. Only one motion for reconsideration shall be entertained. The Committee shall resolve any motion for reconsideration within fifteen (15) days from its filing. A certified copy of the final decision shall likewise be furnished to all Members of the Parliament.

The outcome of the disciplinary proceedings shall be without prejudice to any other administrative, civil, or criminal liability under applicable laws and regulations.

**Sec. 26. *Recommendation of Penalty.*** — If the Committee finds the Respondent liable for disciplinary action, its decision shall include a recommendation as to the appropriate penalty, taking into account the gravity and nature of the offense, the frequency of commission, the amount of public funds or property involved, and other relevant circumstances. The Committee may recommend any of the following penalties: reprimand, censure, suspension for a period not exceeding sixty (60) days, or expulsion from the Parliament.

**Sec. 27. *Parliamentary Action on Disciplinary Penalties.*** — A recommendation for suspension or expulsion shall not be effective unless adopted by resolution of the Parliament in plenary session and supported by at least two-thirds (2/3) of all Members of the Parliament. In cases of reprimand or censure, approval by the Speaker shall suffice. The Speaker shall issue an Order to implement the reprimand or censure, with a warning that any repetition of the same or similar act shall be dealt with more severely.

**RULE V**
**MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS**

**Sec. 28. *Non-Applicability of Technical Rules of Procedure.*** — Proceedings under this Code shall not be strictly bound by the technical rules of procedure observed in courts of law. The Committee shall resolve matters in a summary and expeditious manner, employing all reasonable means to ascertain the facts, and guided by the principles of fairness, due process, and substantial justice. The rights of the parties shall be respected at all times.

**Sec. 29. *Suppletory Application of the Rules of Court.*** — In all matters not specifically provided for in this Code, the pertinent provisions of the Rules of Court of the Philippines shall apply in a suppletory manner, insofar as they are not inconsistent with the provisions of this Code.

**Sec. 30. *Effectivity.*** — This Code shall take effect immediately upon adoption. It shall apply to all pending and future disciplinary cases involving Members of the Parliament, provided that such retroactive application shall not be given effect if it would prejudice the rights of any party.
