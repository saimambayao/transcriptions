**A RESOLUTION URGING THE REGIONAL GOVERNMENT TO ALLOCATE FUNDS FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF A MASTERPLAN FOR THE TAWI-TAWI TOURISM CORRIDOR**

WHEREAS, the province of Tawi-Tawi is rich in natural beauty, cultural heritage, and historical significance, making it a key area for tourism development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

WHEREAS, Tawi-Tawi holds vast potential for eco-tourism, cultural tourism, and marine tourism, which can drive sustainable economic growth and provide livelihood opportunities for local communities;

WHEREAS, there is an urgent need for a comprehensive Tourism Corridor Masterplan for Tawi-Tawi to effectively plan, promote, and manage tourism activities, while ensuring environmental sustainability and cultural preservation;

WHEREAS, the proposed masterplan will address critical areas such as infrastructure development, accessibility, environmental protection, and cultural heritage conservation, while engaging local communities and stakeholders in the process;

WHEREAS, adequate funding is essential to support the development of the Tourism Corridor Masterplan, to be sourced from both the BARMM Regional Budget and national government allocations, in accordance with existing laws and development funds;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), to strongly urge the regional government to allocate the necessary funds for the development of the Tawi-Tawi Tourism Corridor Masterplan, with the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT) as the lead agency, in collaboration with the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) and the Provincial and Municipal Governments of Tawi-Tawi.

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that the MTIT, BPDA, and the Local Chief Executives of Tawi-Tawi shall ensure that the masterplan is developed in close consultation with tourism stakeholders, and the community, reflecting the unique cultural and environmental attributes of the province.

**RESOLVED FINALLY,** that copies of this resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister of BARMM, the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT), the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), the Ministry of Budget, Finance, and Management, and the Local Chief Executive of Tawi-Tawi for appropriate action.
