**A RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE NEW SHARI’AH COUNSELORS-AT-LAW WHO SUCCESSFULLY PASSED THE 2024 SHARI’AH BAR EXAMINATIONS**

**WHEREAS**, the *Shari’ah* or Islamic law forms part of the Islamic tradition derived from religious precepts of Islam, particularly the *Qur’an* and *Sunnah*, and a special field of law based on Presidential Decree (PD) No. 1083 or the Code of Muslim Personal Laws in the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, the *Shari’ah* is one of the pillars of the Bangsamoro Tri-Justice System, which upholds the unique cultural and historical heritage of the Bangsamoro in the administration of justice, per Section 1, Article X of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law;

**WHEREAS**, Section 5 (5) of Article VIII of the 1987 Constitution grants the Supreme Court of the Philippines the power to regulate the practice of law before *Shari’ah* courts and to set the standards, procedures, and regulations for the *Shari’ah* Bar Examinations;

**WHEREAS**, on 28 April 2024, and 02 May 2024, the Supreme Court conducted the first digitalized and regionalized *Shari’ah* Bar Examinations at the University of the Philippines, Diliman, Quezon City, and at the Ateneo de Davao University, Davao City;

**WHEREAS**, a total of Eight Hundred Fifty Three (853) individuals aspiring to practice law before *Shari’ah* courts took the examinations;

**WHEREAS**, on 16 July 2024, the Supreme Court, through Associate Justice Maria Filomena Singh, the 2024 *Shari’ah* bar chairperson, announced that One Hundred Eighty Three (183) examinees or Twenty One Point Forty Five percent (21.45%) of the total number of examinees successfully passed the 2024 *Shari’ah* Bar Examinations;

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Article X of RA 11054 provides that “[a] successful examinee who has qualified for special membership in the Philippine Bar shall be duly conferred the title of counselor-at-law;”

**WHEREAS**, the same was affirmed by the Supreme Court in an *en banc* resolution, B.M. No. 4248, which approves the usage of the formal title *Shari’ah* Counselor-at-law (SCL) by successful passers of the *Shari’ah* Bar Examinations;

**WHEREAS**, the new *Shari’ah* counselors-at-law hurdled a rigorous two-day examination, which tested not only their legal knowledge and stamina but also their deep commitment to upholding the rich traditions and principles of *Shari’ah* within the Philippines legal landscape;

**WHEREAS**, the new *Shari’ah* counselors-at-law hold great potentials in raising the standards of the legal profession and improving the administration of justice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, the peoples in the Bangsamoro have high hopes that our new *Shari’ah* counselors-at-law will help provide the much-needed *Shari’ah* legal services and demonstrate a strong commitment to justice, ethics, and the moral values we collectively uphold as Bangsamoro;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To commend the new Shari’ah Counselors-at-law who successfully passed the 2024 Shari’ah Bar Examinations.
