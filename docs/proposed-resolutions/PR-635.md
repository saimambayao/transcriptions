**Proposed Resolution No. 635**

**RESOLUTION**
**EXPRESSLY RECOGNIZING THE ROLE OF THE MINDANAO STATE UNIVERSITY – COLLEGE OF LAW, INCLUDING ALL ITS EXTENSIONS, AS THE MAIN SOURCE OF COST-EFFECTIVE LEGAL EDUCATION WITHIN THE BANGSAMORO REGION**

WHEREAS, the Mindanao State University – College of Law (MSU Law for brevity) was established in 1981 by the said university's Board of Regents, the body which solely and exclusively exercises the government of MSU which necessarily includes MSU Law, pursuant to Section 6, paragraph (b) of the Republic Act No. 1387, as amended, or An Act Providing for the Establishment of the Mindanao State University in Marawi City and Authorizing the Appropriation of Funds therefor;

WHEREAS, ever since the establishment of MSU Law, not only was it able to consistently produce lawyers every Bar examinations, but also managed to produce Bar topnotchers and exemplary bar passers, a conclusive evidence of the quality legal education MSU Law have been offering and imparting its students;

WHEREAS, carrying on with the foregoing subject matter, MSU Law is the top 16th producer of lawyers in the Philippines, among 119 Law Schools nationwide, as per Legal Education Board’s (LEB) gathered data reckoning from 2011 to 2020, with a total of 422 lawyers contributed or a 1.69% share in the National Population of New Philippine Lawyers; and this rising status of MSU Law sourced from LEB did not yet include the 2022 and 2023 Bar Examinations which resulted to the production of more Bangsamoro Lawyers from MSU with a sum total of 194 bar passers, to which if added to the total bar passers from 2011-2020 has a grand total of 616 lawyers presently attending the legal needs of the Bangsamoro community, all of whom are proud products of MSU Law;

WHEREAS, noteworthy also is the fact that in the Bangsamoro Region, MSU Law have contributed a total of 80.84% New Lawyers from 2011 to 2020 to serve, safeguard and advocate for the rights of marginalized and underserved communities, especially the vulnerable members of the Bangsamoro;

WHEREAS, undeniably, MSU Law is more than just an academe rather a symbolic institution which liberated the Muslim Mindanao, including other cultural minorities of *MinSuPala*, from legal illiteracy which became a solution to the lack of legal representation from among the members of its community in articulating and advocating the aspirations of the Bangsamoro for social justice, injustices, *inter alia*. In conclusion, **MSU Law is an indispensable institution of the Bangsamoro Region** as it provides hope and salvation, on top of cost-effective legal education and service for justice, to the optimistic Bangsamoro whom indubitably demands legal program to overlook and battle violence in the said region;

WHEREAS, without MSU Law, the Bangsamoro would be devoid of development, accessible justice, and chance to express and introduce its uniqueness to the harsh phenomenon of the world given the Bangsamoro’s genuine life, history, demography, condition, and reality, to which case’s certainty will definitely never see the light of day;

**NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, BY THE BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT,** to expressly recognize the role of the Mindanao State University – College of Law, including all its extensions, as the main source of cost-effective legal education within the Bangsamoro Region.

**ADOPTED,**
