**RESOLUTION**
**ENDORSING THE NOMINATION OF INTERIM CHIEF MINISTER AHOD BALAWAG EBRAHIM FOR THE NOBEL PEACE PRIZE**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro region has been plagued by decades of conflict, with various groups struggling for self-determination and autonomy;

**WHEREAS**, Ahod Balawag Ebrahim nom de guerre Al Haj Murad Ebrahim is the Chairman of Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF);

**WHEREAS**, the armed conflict between the Government of the Philippines (GPH) Armed Forces and the Bangsamoro revolutionary forces is the longest and bloodiest in South East Asia;

**WHEREAS**, in 1968 Al Haj Murad Ebrahim joined the clandestine Moro Movement and held various position as highly-trained military commander from the undivided Moro movement, the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) and as a Vice-Chairman for Military Affairs of Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF);

**Whereas**, the GPH-MILF 17 years of peace negotiations is the longest in history;

**WHEREAS**, CM Ebrahim was the Chairman of the MILF Peace Negotiating Panel from 2001-2003 while at the same time the Chief of Staff of the Bangsamoro Islamic Armed Forces (BIAF), and signed in behalf of MILF a breakthrough agreement that paved the way for the cessation of hostilities of decades-old conflict in Mindanao, the Tripoli Agreement of Peace of 2001 in Tripoli, Libya popularly known as the Ceasefire Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, he became Chairman of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front after Sheikh Salamat Hashim passed away in 2003, spearheading both the peace negotiation and the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, he witnessed the signing of the Framework Agreement on the Bangsamoro (FAB) in 2012, and the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) in 2014, both leading the way to the creation and establishment of the new political entity through the ratification of Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) in 2019, the transitory government of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao where he serves as the Interim Chief Minister;

**WHEREAS**, through his tireless efforts, he has continued to exemplify strong commitment in leading the successful implementation of the peace processes in Mindanao;

**WHEREAS**, his unwavering commitment to peaceful resolution and inclusive dialogue has brought about a significant reduction in violence and improved the lives of countless individuals in the region, by promoting security, stability, and development in the region;

**WHEREAS**, his leadership has brought a new era of hope for the Bangsamoro people, by fostering a political settlement to the long-standing conflict that has resulted in the deaths of thousands of people and displacement of millions,

**WHEREAS**, the nomination of CM Ahod B. Ebrahim for the Nobel Peace Prize recognizes their contributions to the peace process and promotes the importance of peaceful resolution in resolving conflicts in the global sphere;

**THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** that the **Bangsamoro Parliament HEREBY endorses the nomination of CM Ahod B. Ebrahim for the Nobel Peace Prize Award**, in recognition of his outstanding leadership and exceptional contributions to the peace process in the Bangsamoro region.

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED** that the Bangsamoro Parliament calls on the national government and the international community to recognize the efforts of CM Ahod B. Ebrahim in promoting peace, stability, and development in the region, and to support their ongoing efforts to bring lasting peace to the Bangsamoro region.
