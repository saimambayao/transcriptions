**A RESOLUTION TO CONDUCT A PARLIAMENTARY INQUIRY IN AID OF LEGISLATION REGARDING POWER DISTRIBUTION IN ELECTRIC COOPERATIVES IN THE BANGSAMORO REGION AND ADDRESS RELATED ISSUES**

**WHEREAS,** Article XIII, Section 36 of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Organic Law of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, it is mandated that the Bangsamoro Government shall actively encourage investments in the energy and power generation sector, encompassing both domestic and international, public and private stakeholders, within the power sector industry in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** a stable and reliable power supply is crucial for the region's growth and prosperity, impacting the socio-economic conditions and quality of life of the people;

**WHEREAS,** the BTA Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy is responsible for overseeing energy-related issues, including power distribution in the Bangsamoro region;

**WHEREAS,** reports of inconsistent power supply have been consistently received from various areas of the Bangsamoro region, negatively affecting businesses, households, and public services;

**WHEREAS,** concerns have been raised regarding the performance of electric cooperatives in the region, including poor services, frequent outages, and discrepancies in electricity provision;

**WHEREAS,** the National Grid Corporation of the Philippines (NGCP) plays a vital role in transmitting and distributing electricity across the country, including the Bangsamoro region;

**WHEREAS,** evaluating the NGCP's operations, particularly its power distribution in electric cooperatives within the Bangsamoro region, is necessary;

**WHEREAS,** the Department of Energy has issued regulations, such as DOE Circular No. DC 2018-02-003 and DOE No. 2004-01-001, requiring a transparent and fair process for power purchase agreements (PPAs) to ensure competition and affordability for consumers;

**WHEREAS,** power sector projects, including power generation and transmission infrastructure, are capital intensive, and private sector participation can provide governments with access to capital, technology, and expertise;

**WHEREAS,** there have been concerns and issues surrounding power purchase agreements in the Philippines, including allegations of unfavorable terms, lack of transparency, and potential for corruption;

**WHEREAS,** conducting a Parliamentary Inquiry in Aid of Legislation would provide a platform to address these issues, enabling a comprehensive examination of power distribution in the region, the role of the NGCP, the potential for public-private partnerships (PPPs) through power purchase agreements, and the related issues in the power purchase agreement process;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority that:

**1.** The BTA Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy is directed to conduct a Parliamentary Inquiry in Aid of Legislation to investigate power distribution in electric cooperatives in the Bangsamoro region, with a specific focus on the operations of the National Grid Corporation of the Philippines.

**2.** The inquiry aims to gather information on the current state of power distribution, including power outage frequency and duration, discrepancies in electricity provision, and the overall performance of electric cooperatives in the region. Additionally, it will explore the potential of PPPs in power purchase agreements to leverage private sector capital, technology, and expertise.

**3.** The BTA Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy is further directed to invite representatives from the National Grid Corporation of the Philippines, the Department of Energy, relevant private sector entities experienced in PPPs, and stakeholders knowledgeable about power purchase agreement issues in the Philippines to attend the inquiry. They should provide necessary information and explanations regarding their roles in power distribution within the Bangsamoro region, the potential for PPPs, and the challenges and concerns related to power purchase agreements.

**4.** The inquiry will also explore other relevant issues concerning electric cooperatives in the Bangsamoro region, including their management, governance, efficiency, potential measures to enhance power supply stability and reliability through PPPs, and the improvement of the power purchase agreement process in the Philippines.

**5.** The BTA Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy shall submit a comprehensive report containing its findings, recommendations, and proposed legislative actions, including the exploration of PPPs through power purchase agreements and measures to address power purchase agreement issues in the Philippines, to the BTA plenary for further deliberation and appropriate action.
