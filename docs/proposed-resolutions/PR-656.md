**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE MINISTRY OF PUBLIC WORKS (MPW) AND THE MINISTRY OF TRADE, INVESTMENT AND TOURISM (MTIT) TO CONDUCT AN INVESTIGATION AND SURVEILLANCE AUDIT ON THE ALLEGED SUBSTANDARD REINFORCING BARS (REBARS) SOLD TO THE PUBLIC BY MANUFACTURERS AND SUPPLIERS OF CONSTRUCTION MATERIALS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and the **promotion of general welfare** are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy;

**WHEREAS**, Section 20 of the same Article provides that the State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides incentives to needed investments;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, **promote health and safety**, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and **preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants**;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippine Iron and Steel Institute (PISI) a non-stock, non-profit organization registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and recognized as the premiere organization representing the country’s steel industry, has raised concerns over the prevalence of substandard rebars in Mindanao and Northern Luzon;

**WHEREAS**, PISI has reported the discovery of inferior rebars in these regions, highlighting the need to intensify government efforts to address this issue;

**WHEREAS**, according to PISI’s findings, the substandard rebars produced are sufficient to construct over 10,000 houses per month, potentially endangering the lives of up to 30,000 people;

**WHEREAS**, in Mindanao, PISI conducted random purchases of rebars from various hardware stores in Davao del Sur, Davao del Norte, Maguindanao, Lanao del Sur, Lanao del Norte, Zamboanga del Norte, Samal Island, Cotabato City, Pagadian City and Iligan City sometime in September 9 to 13, 2024;

**WHEREAS**, PISI’s report indicated that the rebars tested in Mindanao were deficient in weight and prone to brittleness, mirroring the findings for Northern Luzon rebars tested by the Bureau of Philippine Standards (BPS) Testing Laboratory in Cavite;

**WHEREAS**, PISI further reported that three steel manufacturers failed to meet the minimum weight requirements and six hardware shops were found selling substandard rebars;

**WHEREAS**, a rebar or reinforcing bar, is a steel bar used in concrete construction to provide strength, prevent cracking, and ensure structural integrity as mandated by Philippine law and building codes;

**WHEREAS**, rebars play a vital role in ensuring the strength, durability, and safety of concrete structure, including infrastructure, buildings, bridges, houses, and other construction;

**WHEREAS**, in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), the Ministry of Public Works (MPW) serves as the engineering and construction arm of the Bangsamoro Government, responsible for planning, designing, constructing, rehabilitating, and maintaining infrastructure such as as roads, bridges, and flood control projects;

**WHEREAS**, the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) is the primary government agency in BARMM tasked with implementing laws, programs, and projects related to trade, investments, and tourism;

**WHEREAS**, the alleged influx of substandard rebars in the Mindanao market is alarming as it poses significant risks to public safety, undermines the quality and integrity of infrastructure projects, and endangers the development efforts of the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, it is imperative for the MPW and MTIT to conduct an investigation and surveillance audit of manufacturers and suppliers of rebars in BARMM to address these allegations, safeguard public safety, and guide the Bangsamoro Parliament in formulating appropriate policies;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Parliament to urge the Ministry of Public Works (MPW) and the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) to conduct an investigation and surveillance audit on the alleged substandard reinforcing bars (rebars) sold to the public by manufacturers and suppliers of construction materials in BARMM;

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this resolution be forwarded to MPW and MTIT for their appropriate action.
