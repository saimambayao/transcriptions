**RESOLUTION**

**CALLING ALL MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, OFFICES, AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM) TO STRENGTHEN THE IMPLEMENTATION OF REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7877 AND REPUBLIC ACT NO. 11313 ACROSS BARMM TO UPHOLD THE RIGHTS, SAFETY, AND DIGNITY OF ALL**

**WHEREAS**, Section 11, Article II of the 1987 Constitution provides that: “The State values the dignity of every human person and guarantees full respect for human rights”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 14, Article II of the 1987 Constitution declares that: “The State recognizes the role of women in nation-building, and shall ensure the fundamental equality before the law of women and men”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 6, Article IX of Republic Act No. (RA) 11054 or the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” (BOL) the affirms that: “Bangsamoro Government shall fully respect human rights”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Article IX of the BOL sets forth that: “The Bangsamoro Government shall uphold and protect the fundamental rights of women, including the right to engage in lawful employment, and to be protected from exploitation, abuse, or discrimination, as embodied in the Convention on the Elimination of all Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Parliament shall enact the necessary laws for the implementation of this section”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article IX of the BOL further states that: “The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It shall inculcate patriotism and nationalism, encourage involvement in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through sports”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 14, Article IX of the BOL also declares that: “The Bangsamoro Government shall respect, protect, and promote the rights of children, especially orphans of tender age. They shall be protected from exploitation, abuse, or discrimination. Their education and development, both physical and mental, shall be fully addressed. Bangsamoro policies and programs shall take into utmost consideration the best interest of children, and promote and protect the rights of children, youth, and adolescents, including their survival and development”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 41, Article VII of the BOL further provides that: “Public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees shall at all times be accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives”;

**WHEREAS**, RA 7877, or the “Anti-Sexual Harassment Act of 1995,” which is recognized as the first national law criminalizing sexual harassment in employment, education, and training settings, took effect on March 5, 1995;

**WHEREAS**, it was enacted to value the dignity of every individual, enhance human resource development, guarantee full respect for human rights, and uphold the rights and dignity of workers, employees, applicants, students, and trainees, specifically declaring all forms of sexual harassment in employment, education, and training environments as unlawful, and providing legal redress and protection for victims;¹

**WHEREAS**, RA 7877 defines and penalizes sexual harassment committed in work, education, or training-related settings and covers acts where a person in authority, influence, or moral ascendancy (such as an employer, manager, teacher, or trainer) demands, requests, or requires any sexual favor from another, regardless of whether the request is accepted;²

**WHEREAS**, RA 7877 also imposes that employers and heads of offices in work-related, educational, or training environments adopt preventive measures against sexual harassment, promulgate rules—jointly approved with employees, students, or trainees—prescribing procedures and sanctions, establish a committee to raise awareness and investigate complaints, and ensure that policies are properly disseminated, without prejudice to criminal prosecution;³

**WHEREAS**, to strengthen and complement the provisions of RA 7877, RA 11313, otherwise known as the “Safe Spaces Act” or “Bawal Bastos Law,” was signed into law in April 2019 and took effect on August 3, 2019;⁴

**WHEREAS**, while RA 7877 remains the primary legal framework for addressing authority-based sexual harassment, RA No. 11313 expands protection to peer-level, stranger-initiated, and online harassment, and imposes heightened institutional responsibilities on government offices and local government units to adopt gender-responsive, auditable, and proactive mechanisms for preventing and addressing all forms of gender-based sexual harassment;⁵

**WHEREAS**, RA 11313 defines and penalizes gender-based sexual harassment committed in streets and public spaces, online platforms, workplaces, and educational or training institutions, and imposes higher penalties when such acts are committed under qualified circumstances such as in public transportation, by uniformed personnel, or within government offices—covering a range of acts including catcalling, wolf-whistling, misogynistic, transphobic, homophobic, or sexist slurs, unwanted sexual remarks or advances, cyberstalking, groping, and other unwelcome conduct that demeans, intimidates, or threatens a person’s dignity, safety, or personal space;⁶

**WHEREAS**, under RA 11313, Local Government Units (LGUs) are primarily responsible for enforcing Article I of the law, including enacting ordinances to localize its provisions, conducting information campaigns and seminars in educational institutions, creating an anti-sexual harassment hotline, disseminating copies of the law and ordinance, and coordinating with the Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG) to ensure its effective implementation;⁷

**WHEREAS**, it also mandates the DILG to ensure the full implementation of the law by inspecting LGUs for compliance, disseminating best practices, and providing capacity-building and training programs for local officials in coordination with relevant institutions;⁸

**WHEREAS**, it also imposes duties upon government offices, agencies, ministries, and local government units, as employers, to prevent, deter, or punish acts of gender-based sexual harassment in the workplace, including the posting of the law in visible areas, conducting anti-sexual harassment seminars and peer-to-peer public harassment, and are crucial Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI) with proper representation, and formulating a workplace policy or code of conduct—developed in consultation with employees—that reiterates the prohibition on harassment and outlines procedures and administrative penalties;⁹

**WHEREAS**, it also mandates specific responsibilities for employees and co-workers in government workplaces,¹⁰ school heads,¹¹ and the Women and Children’s Protection Desks of the Philippine National Police operating in the Bangsamoro region,¹² in the prevention, reporting, and response to gender-based sexual harassment in workplaces, schools, and public spaces;

**WHEREAS**, RA 7877 and RA 11313 are essential nationwide laws that provide comprehensive protection against sexual harassment and gender-based violence, addressing both hierarchical abuse and peer-to-peer or public harassment, and are crucial for ensuring safe and accountable institutions in BARMM and across the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, strengthening the implementation of these laws through coordinated efforts by ministries, agencies, offices, and LGUs in BARMM is necessary to address persistent challenges such as underreporting, cultural barriers, and limited access to support services;¹³

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government reaffirms its commitment to upholding the rights, safety, and dignity of every person by ensuring that public institutions are free from all forms of sexual harassment and responsive to the needs of survivors;

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it **RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority to call upon all ministries, agencies, offices, and local government units in the BARMM to take active measures to strengthen the implementation of Republic Act No. 7877 and Republic Act No. 11313, in accordance with their respective mandates and in support of a coordinated, region-wide effort to uphold the rights, safety, and dignity of all persons in BARMM;

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** to furnish a copy of this Resolution to the Office of the Chief Minister, the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government, all concerned ministries, agencies, offices, and local government units in the BARMM, and the Philippine National Police—BARMM Regional Office, including its Women and Children’s Protection Desks, for their information and appropriate action.
