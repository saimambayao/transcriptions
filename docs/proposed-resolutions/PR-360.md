**RESOLUTION EXPRESSING DEEPEST CONDOLENCES AND SYMPATHIES TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY OF GRAND MUFTI, SHEIKH ABU HURAIRA ABDULRAHMAN UDASAN AND EXTENDING PROFOUND APPRECIATION AND GRATITUDE FOR HIS CONTRIBUTIONS TO THE BANGSAMORO STRUGGLE AND TO THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Sheikh Abuhuraira Abdulrahman Udasan is the first Bangsamoro Grand Mufti when the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) was established in 2019;

**WHEREAS**, the Grand Mufti served as the leader of the Office of Bangsamoro Darul Ifta (BDI), handling issuance of fatwas (a ruling on a point of Islamic law given by a recognized authority) and providing legal opinion on matters involving Muslim personal laws and Islamic jurisprudence;

**WHEREAS**, he was born on March 3, 1942, in the village of Kitango (formerly Municipality of Dulawan, now Datu Piang) in the province of Maguindanao del Sur;

**WHEREAS**, before the age of six, he began memorizing several chapters from the Holy Qur’an under his father’s guidance;

**WHEREAS**, he studied abroad and earned an “Al-Ijazah Al-Aliah” degree from the Faculty of Da’wah and Usuluddin, specializing in comparative religion, at the Islamic University in Medina, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA), from the calendar year 1967 to 1971;

**WHEREAS**, the Grand Mufti dedicated his life to providing spiritual guidance to the Bangsamoro People, shaping their daily lives and their commitment to Jihad fi Sabilillah.

**WHEREAS**, during the revolution, he played a pivotal role in organizing the top 300 members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF);

**WHEREAS**, He was one of the co-founders of the MILF along with the late MILF Founding Chairman Salamat Hashim (*Allah Yarhamu*) and Sheikh Khalipha Usman Nando (*Allah Yarhamu*), the first Wālī of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, he played an advisory role in matters related to Islamic laws, jurisprudence (fiqh), religious guide for politicians, government employees, and professionals in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;

**WHEREAS**, other than his religious and professional achievements, he was fluent in English, Arabic, Malay, and Tagalog, and possessed proficiency in several local Moro dialects, including Maguindanaon, Iranon, Maranao, Sulug (Tausug) and Davaonon;

**WHEREAS**, at 81 years old, he returned to the Grace of Almighty Allah and breathed his last on Monday morning, July 3, at the age of 81 in his abode in Lugay-Lugay, Brgy. Bagua I, Cotabato City;

**WHEREAS**, the entire Bangsamoro people are in grief following the demise of a revered figure, the Grand Mufti of the Bangsamoro, Sheikh Abu Huraira Abdulrahman Udasan;

**WHEREAS**, as an influential religious leader and considered one of the pillars of the Bangsamoro struggle and the Bangsamoro Government, his passing is regarded as a tremendous loss for the Bangsamoro people;

**NOW THEREFORE**, be it;

**RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament expresses its deepest condolences and sympathies to the bereaved family of Grand Mufti Sheikh Abuhuraira Abdulrahman Udasan and extends profound appreciation and gratitude for his contributions to the Bangsamoro struggle and to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the bereaved family of the late Sheikh Abuhuraira Abdulrahman Udasan.
