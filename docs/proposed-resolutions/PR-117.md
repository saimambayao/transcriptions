**A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING THE SINCERE APPRECIATION AND GRATITUDE OF THE BANGSAMORO TO SENATOR CHRISTOPHER “BONG” GO FOR PROVIDING DISASTER RELIEF AND ASSISTANCE TO THE PEOPLE OF THE BANGSAMORO WHO SUFFERED CATASTROPHIC DAMAGE CAUSED BY SEVERE TROPICAL STORM PAENG**

**WHEREAS**, on 26 October 2022, Severe Tropical Storm Paeng (international name: Nalgae) made landfall in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) causing massive destruction, loss of lives, and damage to agriculture and critical infrastructures brought about by flooding, sea surge and landslides.

**WHEREAS**, based on the data released by the Bangsamoro Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (BDRRMC), at least 570,285 individuals were affected by Severe Tropical Storm Paeng including 53 casualties, 12 missing, and 38 injured across the region.

**WHEREAS**, a total of 371 houses were damaged while 480 were partially damaged. Damages to infrastructure and agriculture within the region, caused by the onslaught of Severe Tropical Storm Paeng is estimated to be around Php 585, 602,426.33;

**WHEREAS**, on 29 October 2022, Bangsamoro Chief Minister Ahod Ebrahim declared a state of calamity in the Bangsamoro Region due to the widespread damage and destruction brought about by Severe Tropical Storm Paeng;

**WHEREAS**, due to the complete devastation caused by Severe Tropical Storm Paeng, thousands are without access to basic supplies, including food, clean drinking water, and medical equipment, necessitating urgent assistance and response efforts, including procuring, transporting, and distributing emergency relief commodities for the affected population;

**WHEREAS**, on 5 November 2022 Senator Christopher “Bong” Go together with Senator Robin Padilla, personally came to the Bangsamoro Region to provide much-needed disaster relief, in the form of basic necessities and cash.

**WHEREAS**, to alleviate the suffering of the people of the Bangsamoro, the Senator expressed his support and solidarity with the calamity-stricken families by giving uplifting speeches and interacting with them;

**WHEREAS**, this proactive initiative and compassion of Senator Christopher “Bong” Go to the People of the Bangsamoro was instrumental in ensuring that basic goods and relief reached the calamity-stricken areas;

**NOW THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, that the Bangsamoro expresses its most sincere gratitude and appreciation to Senator Christopher “Bong” Go for the responsive relief and assistance in the disaster relief efforts in the Bangsamoro Region.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that a copy of this foregoing Resolution be furnished to the Office of Senator Christopher “Bong” Go., for information.
