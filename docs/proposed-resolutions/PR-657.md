**RESOLUTION**

**CREATING A PARLIAMENTARY REVIEW AND STYLING COMMITTEE, THEREBY AMENDING SECTION 4, RULE NO. XII OF APPROVED RESOLUTION NO. 268 OR THE RESOLUTION PROVIDING FOR RULES, PROCEDURES, AND PRACTICES OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Section 25, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall ensure the autonomy of local governments;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article X of the 1987 Constitution provides that territorial and political subdivisions shall enjoy local autonomy;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Book I of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 49 or the Bangsamoro Local Governance Code of 2023 (BLGC) mandates that the Bangsamoro Government’s territorial and political subdivisions shall enjoy genuine and meaningful local autonomy and self-governance to enable them to attain full development as self-reliant communities and become effective partners in achieving holistic Bangsamoro development;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article IV of R.A. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that in exercising its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in the Organic Law;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are **essential to the promotion of general welfare**. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article VII of the BOL states that the powers of government shall be vested in the Parliament which shall exercise those powers and functions expressly granted to it in this Organic Law, and those necessary for, or incidental to the proper governance and development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It shall set policies, legislate on matters within its authority, and elect a Chief Minister who shall exercise executive authority on its behalf;

**WHEREAS**, Section 25, Article VII of the BOL provides that the Parliament shall adopt its own rules for the conduct of its business;

**WHEREAS**, Section 41, Article VII of the BOL states that public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees shall at all times be accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article XVI of the BOL states:

> ***Section 3.*** *Powers and Authorities. Legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during transition shall be vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority. During the transition period, executive authority shall be exercised by the interim Chief Minister who shall be appointed by the President as such, while legislative authority shall be exercised by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority.*
>
> *All powers and functions of the Bangsamoro Government as provided in this Organic Law is vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority during the transition period. Xxx*

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament, approved in plenary session as Adopted Resolution No. 268, or the Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament which guides the conduct of all sessions, committee hearings and meetings, and other similar activities of the Bangsamoro Parliament¹;

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Rule XII of AR 268 provides the definition of a committee which is an organized body composed of Members of the Parliament, constituted to specialize in particular areas of concern and to carry out specific functions. It considers and makes recommendations and draws reports concerning disposition of bills, resolutions, and other preparatory works to be presented to the plenary sessions of the Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Rule XII of the same resolution provides that the Parliament shall have Statutory committees and Parliamentary committees. Ad hoc, joint ad hoc, special, and sub-committees may also be created and organized as the Parliament may deem necessary;

**WHEREAS**, Committees of the Bangsamoro Parliament are composed of Members of the Parliament (MPs), who are experts in recognized fields necessary for the fulfillment of the interim government’s duties specified in the BOL;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Rule XII of the same resolution provides that Statutory Committees are permanent committees provided under the Organic Law of the BARMM and Res. No. 268. The following are the Statutory Committees:

1) Committee on Finance, Budget and Management;
2) Committee on Basic, Higher and Technical Education;
3) Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy;
4) Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform;
5) Committee on Local Government;
6) Committee on Health;
7) Committee on Social Services;
8) Committee on Labor and Employment;
9) Committee on Trade, Investment and Tourism;
10) Committee on Transportation and Communications;
11) Committee on Public Works and Highways;
12) Committee on Human Settlements and Developments;
13) Committee on Public Order, Security and Safety;
14) Committee on Indigenous Peoples Affairs;
15) Committee on Science and Technology;
16) Committee on Bangsamoro Justice System; and
17) Committee on Women, Youth, Children and Persons with Disabilities.

**WHEREAS**, Section 4, Rule XII of the same resolution provides that Parliamentary committees are permanent committees which exercise continuing responsibility in the legislative activity of the Bangsamoro Parliament. The following are the Parliamentary Committees:

1) Committee on Rules;
2) Committee on Accounts;
3) Committee on Ways and Means;
4) Committee on Good Government, Accountability of Public Officers and Investigation, otherwise known as the Blue Ribbon Committee;
5) Committee on Ethics and Privileges;
6) Committee on Amendments, Revision, and Codification of Laws; and
7) Special Committee on the Marawi Recovery, Reconstruction, and Rehabilitation.

**WHEREAS**, one of the keys to having a prosperous and thriving region is a government that responds to the needs of its constituents and officials and employees who adhere to the principle of accountability;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament approved Bangsamoro Autonomy Act Nos. 53, 54 and 55 on December 20, 2023;

**WHEREAS**, on August 20, 2024, in a press briefing, Supreme Court spokesperson Camille Ting said the High Court “declared unconstitutional Section 5 in Bangsamoro Autonomy Act Nos. 53, 54, and 55”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 5 of the said laws state that “qualified voters in a plebiscite to be conducted in the barangays comprising the municipality pursuant to Section 2 hereof²”;

**WHEREAS**, the Court emphasized that when creating new municipalities, the people directly affected must be recognized and allowed to participate in a plebiscite for their ratification in accordance with Article X, Section 10 of the 1987 Constitution³;

**WHEREAS**, in addition, the plebiscite must not be limited to voters from the new municipalities, as the creation of the new towns also affects the economic and political rights of voters from the mother municipality. Thus, qualified voters from both the new and mother municipalities should be included in the plebiscite, contrary to what was written in Section 5⁴;

**WHEREAS**, The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is a newly created region that is still in the process of establishing a government suited to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, there is currently no committee dedicated to reviewing and refining the language of legislative measures filed by Members of Parliament, a critical step in ensuring the clarity, consistency, and legal soundness of proposed legislation;

**WHEREAS**, it is imperative to establish a Review and Styling Committee to meticulously examine the wording, implications, and legal aspects of legislative measures to ensure they meet the highest standards of precision and adherence to constitutional and statutory provisions;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED BY THE BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT:**

**Section 1. Constitution of a Review and Styling Committee.** – A Review and Styling Committee is hereby constituted as the Bangsamoro Parliament’s mechanism for reviewing and refining bills and resolutions adopted by the Parliament;

**Section 2. Composition.** – The Review and Styling Committee shall be composed of a Chairperson, a Vice-Chairperson, and such members as the Rules or the Bangsamoro Parliament may allow. The composition of the Committee shall be confirmed by the Bangsamoro Parliament in a plenary session;

**Section 3. Functions.** – The Review and Styling Committee shall perform the following functions:

a) Examine every legislative proposal submitted by the Parliament;
b) Closely and methodically review the language and structure of legislative measures;
c) Evaluate the implications of proposed legislation on the Bangsamoro Government;
d) Ensure compliance with the Constitution and relevant national laws, and identify potential conflicts; and
e) Perform other functions as directed by the Parliament.

**Section 4. Secretariat.** – The Committee shall be supported by a Secretariat to assist in its functions.

**Section 5. Budget.** – The Committee Chair shall request the necessary budget for its operations from the Speaker of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Section 6. Coordination and Cooperation.** – Ministries, agencies, and offices of the BARMM Government shall extend necessary assistance to the Committee for purposes relevant to its functions.

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, by the Bangsamoro Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Creating a Parliamentary Review and Styling Committee, Amending Section 4, Rule No. XII of Adopted Resolution No. 268 or the Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament.
