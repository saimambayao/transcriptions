**RESOLUTION**
**CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING MOHAMMAD NUR GUMA-OS CASIB FOR WINNING GOLD AND BEING HAILED AS "NUCLEAR AMBASSADOR" IN THE 1ST INTERNATIONAL NUCLEAR SCIENCE OLYMPIAD (INSO) HELD AT NEW CLARK CITY, PAMPANGA LAST AUGUST 1 – 6, 2024**

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that the State recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It shall inculcate in the youth patriotism and nationalism, and encourage their involvement in public and civic affairs;

**WHEREAS**, Section 17, Article II of the Constitution provides that the State shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture, and sports to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development;

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article IX of Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, and nationalism, encourage involvement in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through sports;

**WHEREAS**, Section 21, Article IX of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government recognizes science and technology as essential for national and regional progress. It shall give priority to science, research, inventions, technology education, and their development;

**WHEREAS**, the International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) is an international competition for secondary school students in nuclear science and technology (NST)¹;

**WHEREAS**, the INSO aims to increase awareness of the peaceful applications of Nuclear Science and Technology (NST), to popularize knowledge and understanding of NST to improve the nuclear scientific approach towards its uses and applications, and to enhance the interest in NST among secondary school students²;

**WHEREAS**, the youth of today are the hope for the future, and they have to be inspired and empowered to become change makers of the world's nations³;

**WHEREAS**, the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) was held at New Clark City, Pampanga on August 1 to 6, 2024, marking a significant milestone in nuclear education⁴;

**WHEREAS**, the competition aims to highlight the Philippines’ commitment to advancing nuclear education and fostering international collaboration in the peaceful use of nuclear science⁵;

**WHEREAS**, the event featured 55 top students, 27 team leaders, and 14 observers from 14 Asia-Pacific countries⁶;

**WHEREAS**, the event was organized by the Philippine Nuclear Science Institute, in collaboration with the Department of Science and Technology (DOST), Philippine Nuclear Science Foundation Incorporated, Department of Education (DepEd), and National Research Council of the Philippines⁷

**WHEREAS**, the Olympiad consisted of rigorous theoretical and practical exams, held on 2 August and 4 August, respectively. Competing students were tasked to analyze data and answer questions related to sedimentation patterns in the environment and radiation protection⁸;

**WHEREAS**, the contestants came as teams representing their respective countries, but they competed as individuals and were ranked according to the points they obtained in the two exams⁹;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippines garnered a total of three (3) medals in the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) namely: two (2) Gold Medals and one (1) Silver medal;

**WHEREAS**, the Gold medalists were Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib of Philippine Science High School – Central Mindanao Campus and Neil Kyle Olaer Maniquis of Manila Science High School, while Jeremiah E. Auza of Philippine Science High School – Central Visayas Campus won Silver medal¹⁰;

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib, a young Bangsamoro of Maranao descent and incoming Grade 12 student of the Philippine Science High School – Central Mindanao Campus (PSHS-CMC) in Balo-i, Lanao del Norte was one of the contenders from the Philippines in the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO);

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib won gold and shone the brightest, emerging as the top scorer in two examinations out of 55 contenders from 14 Asia-Pacific countries¹¹;

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib and Singaporean’s Tran Doan Duy Phuc scored the highest in the theoretical and experimental exams, respectively, but it was Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib who earned the title of “Nuclear Ambassador” for topping the overall tests¹²;

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib is a proud son of Dr. Norodin S. Casib and Dra. Sittienor M. Guma-os – Casib who are natives of Municipalities of Bayang and Ganassi, Lanao del Sur, and he has consistently excelled in science and technology competitions both locally and internationally;¹³

**WHEREAS**, being as Bangsamoro, he has been bringing honor, pride and prestige to the entire Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) by winning in competitions both international and national such as but not limited to the 55th International Chemistry Olympiad in Zurich, Switzerland, 16th International Astronomy and Astrophysics Olympiad (IOAA) in Poland, 35th Asian Pacific Mathematical Olympiad (APMO) held online, 64th International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) in Japan, and 36th International Math Olympiad held in the University of the Philippines, Quezon City¹⁴;

**WHEREAS**, his exemplary achievements did not only showcase his competitive spirit, dedication and perseverance, but they also serve as inspiration and motivation to aspiring Bangsamoro people, most especially to the Bangsamoro youth, to pursue academic excellence and excel in various fields of knowledge and applications;

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib has brought great pride and prestige to the whole Bangsamoro Region, as he showed great perseverance and dedication, and will serve as inspiration not only to the Bangsamoro people in the Philippines but to the millions of Bangsamoro people across several countries of the world;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), as the interim government of the Bangsamoro Region, has often recognized outstanding Bangsamoro, particularly the Bangsamoro youth, for their remarkable accomplishments of bringing immense pride and glory to the Bangsamoro Region by winning international and national competitions of various fields; and

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib’s remarkable achievement and exceptional contribution that brought pride, honor and prestige to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region deserves the highest commendation from the BTA;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Congratulating and Commending Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib for Winning Gold and for being hailed as “Nuclear Ambassador” in the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) held at New Clark City, Pampanga last August 1 – 6, 2024.

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be presented to Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib in recognition and expression of appreciation for his exemplary dedication and great perseverance, and for bringing pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people.
