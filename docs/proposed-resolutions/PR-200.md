**A RESOLUTION URGING THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO MANDATE THE MINISTRY OF BASIC AND HIGHER EDUCATION (MBHTE) TO ENCOURAGE ALL ITS TEACHERS AND ADMINISTRATIVE STAFF TO UNDERGO ANNUAL MEDICAL CHECK UPS.**

**WHEREAS**, under Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution, “The state shall **protect and promote the right to health** of the people and instill health consciousness among them.”

**WHEREAS**, under Section 3, Article V, Bangsamoro Organic law, “The Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, **promote health and safety**, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants.”

**WHEREAS**, under Article 20 of Bangsamoro Autonomous Act No. 17 (BAA No. 17) or the **Bangsamoro Civil Service Code**, “Each agency shall prepare a **career and personnel development plan**. Such career and personnel development plan shall include provisions on merit promotions, performance evaluation, in-service training, including overseas and local scholarship and training grants, job rotation, suggestions and incentive award systems, and such **other provisions for employees’ health**, welfare, counseling recreation, and similar services.”

**WHEREAS**, keeping a healthy workforce is one of the primary concerns of the Bangsamoro government considering that the physical well-being of its employees, especially the teachers and administrative staff, has a significant impact on the efficiency and effectiveness of public service delivery.

**WHEREAS**, in order to monitor and maintain the health and welfare of teachers and administrative employees of the Ministry of Basic and Higher Education (MBHTE), there shall be an *annual medical examination* that shall be incorporated into the ministry’s career and personnel development plan’s provisions for employees’ health and welfare, as mandated by BAA No. 17 or the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code.

**WHEREAS**, teachers and administrative employees who are below 40 years of age shall include the following: Physical examination, Chest X-ray, Complete Blood Count (CBC), Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS), Urinalysis, and Stool Examination. Meanwhile, employees whose age is 40 years and above shall be entitled to the following: Physical examination, Chest X-ray, Complete Blood Count (CBC), Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS), Urinalysis, Stool Examination, and ECG.

**WHEREAS**, AMBAG and Tulong Medical of the Members of Parliament shall allocate a reasonable percentage out of its budget allocation to cover the costs of the medical check-up of teachers and administrative staff of MBHTE.

**WHEREAS**, this resolution seeks to monitor probable health issues among teachers and administrative staff of MBHTE for a prompt medical intervention, as well as to reduce, if not totally prevent, the probability of the spread of contagious disease among teachers and their students.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in **A RESOLUTION URGING THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO MANDATE THE MINISTRY OF BASIC AND HIGHER EDUCATION (MBHTE) TO ENCOURAGE ALL ITS TEACHERS AND ADMINISTRATIVE STAFF TO UNDERGO ANNUAL MEDICAL CHECK UPS.**
