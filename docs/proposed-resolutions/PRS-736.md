**A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING FULL SUPPORT TO CHIEF MINISTER ABDULRAOF “SAMMY GAMBAR” A. MACACUA IN HIS CALL FOR THE COURTESY RESIGNATION OF ALL MINISTERS, DEPUTY MINISTERS, AND HEADS OF OFFICES AND AGENCIES OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT**

**WHEREAS**, the 1987 Philippine Constitution, under Article XI, Section 1, and that of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054), under Section 41, Article VII, both state that "*Public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees must at all times be accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives*";

**WHEREAS**, Section 19, Book 1 of the Bangsamoro Administrative Code (Bangsamoro Administrative Act No. 13) provides that “All public officers and employees shall be guided by the core values of moral governance and the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code”;

**WHEREAS**, Article 170 of the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code provides that the “Bangsamoro Government officials and employees shall uphold the set of rules, practices, and processes of governance completely devoid of all evils of graft and corruption, and shall be explicitly driven by the moral principles of utmost dedication, devotion, honesty, justice, and integrity. The application of commitment to moral governance requires a high sense of sincerity (ikhlaas), perfection (itqaan), excellence (ihsaan), and optimism (tafaa-ul)”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3 of Bangsamoro Administrative Code under Declaration of Principles and Policies states that “the Bangsamoro Government, in pursuit of moral governance, shall maintain honesty, integrity, transparency and accountability, participation in governance and take positive and effective measures against graft and corruption”;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is currently undergoing a crucial and defining phase of its transition as mandated by Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), with the first regular parliamentary elections scheduled in October 2025;

**WHEREAS**, Section 32 of Article VII of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law, provides that the Chief Minister shall exercise the following powers, duties, and functions:

(a) head of the Bangsamoro Government;
(b) Appoint heads of ministries, agencies, bureaus, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government or other officers of Bangsamoro-owned or controlled corporations or entities with original charters;
(c) Appoint other officers in the Bangsamoro Government, as may be provided by the Parliament;
xxx
(e) Issue executive orders and other policies of the Bangsamoro Government;
xxx
(h) Exercise such other powers and functions inherent to the office.

**WHEREAS**, Chief Minister Abdulraof “Sammy Gambar” A. Macacua, in the exercise of his leadership prerogatives and commitment to moral governance, issued a directive calling for the unqualified courtesy resignation of all appointed or designated Ministers, Deputy Ministers, and Heads of Offices and Agencies to be submitted on June 30, 2025, in light of serious and grave complaints brought to him, and in order to promote efficiency and integrity in public service;

**WHEREAS**, such directive is aimed at restoring public trust, enhancing institutional performance, and enabling a strategic reorganization of the Ministries, Offices and Agencies to meet the urgent needs of the Bangsamoro people at the tail end of the transition;

**WHEREAS**, the call for unqualified courtesy resignations is a legitimate and reasonable administrative tool, consistent with the principles of moral governance, and intended to ensure that only those who are competent, ethical, and committed to the vision of the Bangsamoro Government continue to serve;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, in the exercise of its legislative and oversight functions, fully recognizes and supports this act of leadership reform in the interest of the greater welfare of the Bangsamoro constituents;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby **RESOLVED**, that the **Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament expresses its full and unwavering support to Chief Minister Abdulraof A. Macacua** in his call for the unqualified courtesy resignation of all appointed or designated Ministers, Deputy Ministers, and Heads of Offices and Agencies in the BARMM;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that this Resolution affirms the collective support of the Parliament to the reform agenda of the Chief Minister and expresses confidence in his efforts to strengthen moral governance, uphold public trust, and deliver better public service for the Bangsamoro people;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the Parliament urges all concerned officials to **heed the call** of the Chief Minister in the spirit of accountability, professionalism, and service to the Bangsamoro people;

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, that copies of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister and all Ministries, Agencies, and Offices of the Bangsamoro Government for their information and appropriate action.
