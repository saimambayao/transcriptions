**RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE BANGSAMORO PILGRIMAGE AUTHORITY (BPA) TO DEVELOP STRATEGIC INITIATIVES FOR THE MANAGEMENT OF PILGRIMS FROM THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO**

**WHEREAS,** Section 17, Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution provides that the State shall recognize, respect, and protect the rights of indigenous cultural communities to preserve and develop their cultures, traditions, and institutions. It shall consider these rights in the formulation of national plans and policies;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

**WHEREAS,** Section 42, Article VII of the BOL provides that the Parliament may create other offices such as for the youth, women, settler communities, disaster risk reduction and management, and planning and development, including a Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority which shall exercise jurisdiction over Hajj and Umrah matters affecting pilgrims from within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Republic Act No. 9997 created the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos (NCMF);

**WHEREAS,** Section 4 of R.A. 9997 mandates the NCMF to preserve and develop the culture, tradition, institutions, and well-being of Muslim Filipinos, in conformity with the country’s laws and in consonance with national unity and development;

**WHEREAS,** the NCMF is also specifically mandated, among others, in paragraph (q) of Section 8, Article II of the act to administer all programs, projects and activities, formulate the necessary rules and regulations, and coordinate with pertinent offices to ensure the success of the annual Hajj (pilgrimage) to Mecca, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia;

**WHEREAS,** Section 43, Title III of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act 13, or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code (BAC), provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall have primary jurisdiction over Hajj and Umrah matters affecting pilgrims from within the Bangsamoro. The Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority (BPA) shall act in close coordination with the NCMF on Hajj and Umrah matters to ensure that the pilgrims from the Bangsamoro are provided with the same level of services as those from outside;

**WHEREAS,** Section 44, Title III of the same law states that the BPA is primarily responsible for the administration of the annual Muslim pilgrimage to Mecca, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia of pilgrims from within the Bangsamoro. It shall formulate and implement programs, projects and activities for the efficient and effective administration and supervision of the conduct of pilgrimage activities of Bangsamoro pilgrims;

**WHEREAS,** Section 45, Title III of the same law states that the BPA shall formulate necessary guidelines to ensure the timely processing of the Bangsamoro pilgrim’s travel documents and the equitable and reasonable collection of fees. It shall also accredit qualified and deserving sheikhs. It shall likewise formulate, subject to the approval of the Chief Minister, the time table of annual Hajj activities which must be posted in all areas accessible to Bangsamoro Muslims.

Pursuant to the above, collections shall be limited to *mutawiff* and passporting and/or processing fees only. Collected fees shall be deposited in a special trust fund which shall be established by the Authority, the expenses from which shall be subject to existing auditing laws, rules and regulations.

The pilgrims shall be afforded free choice of travel agencies and airlines or other means of transportation to and from the site of pilgrimage: *Provided,* That pilgrims comply with the prescribed arrival and departure schedule fixed by the Ministry of Hajj of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia: *Provided, further,* That all pilgrims shall be legitimate holders of round-trip tickets to and from the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia: *Provided, furthermore,* That the pilgrims shall be free to choose on where to purchase or acquire other logistics, materials and supplies for the pilgrimage.

In no instance shall the pilgrims be forced to purchase such logistics, materials and supplies from the Authority or its authorized agents or representatives.

The Authority shall directly engage and enter into agreements with the appropriate authorities in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, upon consultation with, and prior approval by, the Chief Minister;

**WHEREAS,** the Hajj, or the annual pilgrimage to Makkah, Saudi Arabia, is one of the five pillars of Islam, and is a religious obligation for all Muslims that must be carried out at least once in their lifetime by all who are physically and financially capable;

**WHEREAS,** the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia’s statistics authority said more than 1.8 pilgrims had joined this year's rituals, making it the largest since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic¹;

**WHEREAS,** the NCMF has reported that about 7,500 performed the Hajj this year²;

**WHEREAS,** on June 28, 2023, the Philippine Embassy in Saudi said that many Filipino pilgrims were stranded in *Muzdalifah*, which was an open area associated with Hajj³;

**WHEREAS,** Yusoph Mando, the Commissioner of the NCMF, said that the Filipino pilgrims were stuck in *Muzdalifah* due to the traffic that was preventing service vehicles from fetching the pilgrims and bringing them to other religious sites, which are *Mina* and *Jamarat*, both located less than half-an-hour drive away from *Muzdalifah⁴*;

**WHEREAS,** in a report⁵, the Philippine Embassy said more than 200 Filipino hajj pilgrims were stranded in *Muzdalifah*;

**WHEREAS,** one of the biggest challenges that the pilgrims faced during the hajj is the hot weather making it a physically demanding ritual;

**WHEREAS,** the problems and controversies encountered during hajj caused hardships to our Bangsamoro pilgrims that should be looked into by the Bangsamoro government, in order to correct the handling of pilgrims and to provide better services for the next annual hajj pilgrimage;

**WHEREAS,** it is the duty of the Bangsamoro government to protect its people by creating a plan that can strategically solve the recent problems encountered by the pilgrims related to the performance of the annual Islamic pilgrimage (hajj) in order to avoid the same mistakes from happening again; and

**WHEREAS,** the BPA, as the agency primarily responsible for the administration of the annual pilgrimage of the pilgrims from within the Bangsamoro Region, must prepare strategic initiatives for the efficient and effective administration and supervision of conducting pilgrimage activities of Bangsamoro pilgrims.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority to Develop Strategic Initiatives for the Management of Pilgrims from the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**RESOLVED FURTHER** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority, for information and appropriate action.
