**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE MINISTRY OF TRADE, INVESTMENT AND TOURISM (MTIT) TO PROMOTE AND ENCOURAGE INVESTORS OF A FLOATING MARKET ON RIO GRANDE DE MINDANAO AND TAMONTAKA RIVER**

**WHEREAS,** Section 19, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall develop a self-reliant and independent national economy effectively controlled by Filipinos;

**WHEREAS,** Section 20 of the Constitution provides that the State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides incentives to needed investments;

**WHEREAS,** Section 24, Article XIII of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government recognizes the private sector as a mover of trade, commerce, and industry;

**WHEREAS,** the same provision provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall encourage and support the building up of entrepreneurial capability in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and shall recognize, promote, protect, and support the development of cooperatives and other medium, small, and micro enterprises to achieve equity, social justice and economic development;

**WHEREAS,** Section 1, Chapter 1, Title XIV of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13, or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall promote tourism as a major socio-economic activity respecting, upholding, and maintaining diverse cultural heritage and the moral and spiritual values of the people in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) is the primary agency in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) mandated to implement laws, programs and projects on trade, investments, and tourism. Towards this end, it shall promote and develop an industrialization program effectively controlled by the inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and shall act as catalyst for intensified private sector activity in order to accelerate and sustain economic growth¹;

**WHEREAS,** the Rio Grande de Mindanao, formerly known as the Cotabato River, is the main river of Cotabato City. It is a major inland-transportation artery, mainly transporting agricultural products²;

**WHEREAS,** the population centers along the aforementioned river include Cotabato City, Datu Piang, and Midsayap³;

**WHEREAS,** the Tamontaka River is located in Cotabato City, and has a length of 70.33 kilometers⁴;

**WHEREAS,** the Rio Grande de Mindanao and its branch, the Tamontaka River, are major waterways in the Philippines which have significant potential for economic growth and development, alongside attracting tourism;

**WHEREAS,** floating markets are traditional marketplaces situated on the water, typically found on rivers or lakes, where goods are sold from boats⁵;

**WHEREAS,** floating markets are a unique form of traditional commercial business, playing an important role in waterway trade while also featuring distinctively attractive local cultural values. These markets are found to play a focal role in the socio-economic and cultural development of the country⁶ they are based in;

**WHEREAS,** floating markets affect local people and communities in economic, social, cultural, and environmental aspects. The development of these markets can lead to increased employment opportunities and higher incomes for the locals⁷;

**WHEREAS,** floating markets have proven to be successful and flourishing in neighboring Southeast Asian countries like Thailand and Vietnam, not only attracting tourists, but also significantly promoting local products and cultural heritage;

**WHEREAS,** in the Philippines, Sitangkai, Tawi-Tawi, also known as "Venice of the South," has built a floating public market with the assistance of the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG)⁸;

**WHEREAS,** every day, small boats laden with a variety of agricultural products such as fruits and vegetables, fresh fish, and shellfishes are moored along the side of the main footbridge of Sitangkai⁹;

**WHEREAS,** investors from the private sector and businesspeople are instrumental in establishing these floating markets. Funding from investors can be used for developing human resources and innovating tourism products¹⁰;

**WHEREAS,** the establishment of floating markets on the Rio Grande de Mindanao and the Tamontaka River can renew and jump-start economic activities, create more employment opportunities, and contribute to the overall socio-economic growth and development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region while also promoting our cultural heritage and revitalizing our traditions;

**WHEREAS,** promoting and encouraging potential investors to establish floating markets on the Rio Grande de Mindanao and the Tamontaka River aligns with the Bangsamoro Government's, through the MTIT, objective of promoting sustainable tourism and boosting local economies in the region;

**WHEREAS,** tourism development can be an effective strategy to reduce poverty and attain sustainable livelihoods in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Directing the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) to promote and encourage investors of a floating market on Rio Grande de Mindanao and Tamontaka River.

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) for its information and appropriate action.
