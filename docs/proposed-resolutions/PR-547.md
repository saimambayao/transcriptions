**RESOLUTION**
**REQUESTING TELECOMMUNICATION COMPANIES TO EXPAND THEIR COVERAGE IN THE SIX (6) MORO ISLAMIC LIBERATION FRONT (MILF) PREVIOUSLY ACKNOWLEDGED CAMPS TO CONTRIBUTE TO THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE NORMALIZATION TRACK OF THE COMPREHENSIVE AGREEMENT ON THE BANGSAMORO (CAB)**

**WHEREAS,** Section 24, Article II of the 1987 Constitution states that the State recognizes the vital role of communication and information in nation-building;

**WHEREAS,** Section 39, Article XIII of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law, states the Bangsamoro Government has the authority of granting regional franchises, licenses, and permits to telecommunication utilities whose frequencies are confined to and whose main offices are located within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Section 4, Article II of Republic Act No. 7925, also known as the Public Telecommunications Policy Act of the Philippines, states that Telecommunications is essential to the economic development, integrity and security of the Philippines, and as such shall be developed and administered as to safeguard, enrich and strength the economic, cultural, social and political fabric of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS,** Article 19 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights states that Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression; this right includes freedom to hold opinions without interference and to seek, receive and impart information and ideas through any media and regardless of frontiers¹;

**WHEREAS,** during the 32nd session of the United Nations Human Rights Council, which emphasizes that access to information on the Internet facilitates vast opportunities for affordable and inclusive education globally, thereby being an important tool to facilitate the promotion of the right to education, while underlining the need to address digital literacy and the digital divide, as it affects the enjoyment of the right to education²;

**WHEREAS,** the Annex on Normalization of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB), defines Normalization as a process whereby communities can achieve their desired quality of life, which includes the pursuit of sustainable livelihood and political participation within a peaceful deliberative society³;

**WHEREAS,** major Telecommunications providers such as Globe Telecom have been supportive of the Bangsamoro's initiatives⁴;

**WHEREAS,** aside from Globe, the PLDT-SMART, the DITO Telecommunications, and the Starlink are the major telecommunication companies operating in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** previous measures have been taken at expanding connectivity within the BARMM⁵;

**WHEREAS,** former Minister Dickson Hermoso of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications (MOTC-BARMM) met with representatives from DITO Telecommunications, with the purpose of improving telecommunications services in the region⁶;

**WHEREAS,** internet, and in a broader scope, telecommunications can now be considered a fundamental human right that a state should provide today;

**WHEREAS,** telecommunications can vastly enhance the quality of life in these camps, as it is a means of greater integration and connectivity with the outside world;

**WHEREAS,** the presence of telecommunication is a sign of progress and development in an area, as they exhibit modern infrastructure facilities;

**WHEREAS,** telecommunication is an excellent conduit of economic activity and development, as there is a causal relationship between the two⁷;

**WHEREAS,** to further facilitate the transformation of these camps, their inclusion in the coverage of telecommunication companies’ is a must;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, as the interim government of the Bangsamoro Region, has the power to request, prioritize, and approve the extension of the Telecommunication providers' coverage to include these six camps;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Requesting Telecommunication Companies to Expand Their Coverage in the Six (6) Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) Previously Acknowledged Camps to Contribute to the Implementation of the Normalization Track of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro.

Adopted,

**ENGR. BAINTAN ADIL-AMPATUAN, MNSA**
