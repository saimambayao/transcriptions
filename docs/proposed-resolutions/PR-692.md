**A RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE BANGSAMORO PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY (BPDA) TO PREPARE AND MAINTAIN A COMPREHENSIVE DATABASE OF BANGSAMORO SCHOLARS FROM VARIOUS SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAMS BEING IMPLEMENTED BY BANGSAMORO MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES (BMOAs), IN LINE WITH THE "ONE PROFESSIONAL PER FAMILY" ADVOCACY OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT.**

**WHEREAS,** Section 9, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution (Constitution) provides that the State shall promote a just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the nation and free the people from poverty through policies that provide adequate social services, promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality of life for all;

**WHEREAS,** Section 13 of the same Article provides that the State recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being;

**WHEREAS,** Section 17 of the same Article provides that the State shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture, and sports to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development;

**WHEREAS,** Section 26 of the same Article provides that the State shall guarantee equal access to opportunities for public service;

**WHEREAS,** Section 7, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government **shall ensure every Filipino citizen in its territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life.** Social justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS,** Section 8, Article IX of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs, quality education, appropriate services, livelihood opportunities, affordable and progressive housing projects, power and electricity, and water inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Section 16 of the same Article provides that it shall be a top priority of the Bangsamoro Government to establish, maintain, and support a complete and integrated system of quality education, which shall be a subsystem of the national education system. The Bangsamoro Government shall develop an educational framework relevant and responsive to the needs, ideas, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS,** one of the general directions or priority areas of the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) is “One Professional per Family”¹;

**WHEREAS,** according to Minister Mohagher Iqbal of MBHTE, the Bangsamoro Government “aims to produce (at least) one professional in every family in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It will not matter if our young professionals will graduate from a traditional academic institution or a technical-vocational training center. What is essential [is that] these establishments offer a curriculum that is relevant for employability – and save us from Hellfire, in the day hereafter.”²;

**WHEREAS,** the BARMM government is intensifying its efforts to provide educational opportunities for its youth through various scholarship programs³;

**WHEREAS,** Bangsamoro Spokesperson Mohd Asnin Pendatun, who also serves as Cabinet Secretary, announced that thousands of students are benefiting from the Access to Higher and Modern Education Scholarship Program (AHME-SP) and 902 from the Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education (BASE)⁴;

**WHEREAS,** the AHME-SP, which is allocated P277 million, aims to enhance the quality of life for Bangsamoro by supporting at least one professional per family. The program, implemented by MBHTE, provides P60,000 per student per academic year to qualified beneficiaries enrolled in BARMM Regional Priority Programs⁵;

**WHEREAS,** a total of 1,400 qualified students were able to benefit from the AHME-SP for Fiscal Years 2020-2021, while another 1,400 scholars have been produced in 2022 to ensure at least one professional per family in Bangsamoro⁶;

**WHEREAS,** Spokesperson Pendatun also highlighted the achievements of the AHME-SP 1st Cohort Scholars for the 2023-2024 academic year, who were honored with Latin awards from their respective alma maters. A total of 1,400 scholars graduated on April 18, 2024 in Cotabato City⁷;

**WHEREAS,** at least 7,000 scholars are targeted to be beneficiaries of the MBHTE’s scholarship program for Technical Vocational Education and Training (TVET). The said program package shall include skills training, entrepreneurship training, values transformation training, national assessment, and a training support fund of Php 100.00 per day per student⁸;

**WHEREAS,** another significant initiative, the BASE program, implemented by the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), aims to inspire and support Bangsamoro youth, especially incoming first-year students, in pursuing careers in science and technology⁹;

**WHEREAS,** eligible awardees of the BASE program will receive monthly cash assistance amounting to Php 8,000.00, or Php 40,000.00 per semester¹⁰;

**WHEREAS,** MOST also launched the **Mujahideen/Mujahidat Assistance for Science Education (MASE) Program**, which is open and applicable only to mujahideen or mujahidat, or legitimate sons and daughters of the same, from any college level who intend to pursue science and technology courses and will receive monthly cash assistance amounting to Php 8,000.00, or Php 40,000.00 per semester¹¹;

**WHEREAS,** MOST also offers the **Bangsamoro Assistance for Science Education – Merit (BASE-Merit) Program**, which is limited to highly specialized Science and Technology courses such as petroleum/oil engineering, chemistry, meteorology, geology, aeronautics, anatomy, and similar fields. Eligible awardees will receive monthly cash assistance amounting to Php 20,000.00, or Php 100,000.00 per semester¹²;

**WHEREAS,** aside from the above-mentioned scholarship and/or grant programs, MOST also offers the **Bangsamoro Special Assistance for Science Education (BSASE)**, **Professional Assistance for Science Education**, and the **Bangsamoro International Graduate Study for Science and Technology (BIGAST)**¹³;

**WHEREAS,** Bangsamoro graduates of pre-medical courses may avail themselves of free medical schooling through the Medical Scholarship Program of the Ministry of Health (MOH), which is open to Bangsamoro people residing in the region, especially health professionals serving in government health offices, children and dependents of BARMM employees, barangay health workers, traditional birth attendants, families of Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) combatants, current medical students intending to avail themselves of the program, and those who have not been granted a scholarship before¹⁴;

**WHEREAS,** the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD), through its **Angat Bangsamoro Tungo sa Karunungan (ABaKa) Program**, provides one-time financial assistance to poor students in the region to cover school-related expenses such as school supplies, books, projects, internet/data load, and other related expenses¹⁵;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) was created under Section 14 of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 06, or the **Bangsamoro Socio-Economic Development Planning Act of 2019.** It shall be the socio-economic development planning and policy coordinating authority of the Bangsamoro Government¹⁶;

**WHEREAS,** the BPDA shall serve as the planning, coordinating, and monitoring agency for all development plans, policies, programs, and projects of the Bangsamoro Government. It shall also serve as the counterpart of the National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region¹⁷;

**WHEREAS,** a database refers to information that is collectively organized for easy access, management, and updating. It is used for storing, maintaining, and accessing all sorts of data. It is a collection of information about people, places, or things. Such information is gathered in one place so that it can be observed and analyzed. Databases can be thought of as an organized collection of information¹⁸;

**WHEREAS,** a database of Bangsamoro scholars from different scholarship programs being implemented by various Bangsamoro ministries, offices, and agencies (BMOAs) shall be a systematic and organized collection of structured data involving all Bangsamoro scholars in and outside the BARMM who have benefited from and availed themselves of the said scholarship programs;

**WHEREAS,** such a database, when established and maintained by the BPDA, will guide policymakers and other relevant stakeholders in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in policy-making, planning, programming, budgeting, project implementation, and monitoring and evaluation;

**WHEREAS,** the availability of information on Bangsamoro scholars who are beneficiaries of various scholarship programs implemented by BMOAs can help policymakers and other relevant stakeholders in the Bangsamoro Government in decision-making, policy-making, and future legislation;

**WHEREAS,** establishing and maintaining a comprehensive database of Bangsamoro scholars offers benefits to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It ensures the viability and efficiency of the various scholarship programs being implemented by BMOAs;

**WHEREAS,** a comprehensive database ensures the availability of data concerning Bangsamoro scholars according to their chosen academic course, academic status, location, academic achievements, and other relevant factors. This data can help the Bangsamoro Government achieve its goal of having at least one (1) professional per family in the region;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution, urging the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) to prepare and maintain a comprehensive database of Bangsamoro scholars from various scholarship programs being implemented by Bangsamoro ministries, offices, and agencies (BMOAs), in line with the "One Professional Per Family" advocacy of the Bangsamoro Government.

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to BPDA for information and appropriate action.

Adopted,
