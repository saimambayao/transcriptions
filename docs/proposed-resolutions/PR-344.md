**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE OFFICE OF THE CHIEF MINISTER (OCM) THROUGH THE BANGSAMORO INFORMATION AND COMMUNICATIONS TECHNOLOGY OFFICE (BICTO) TO MONITOR AND FURNISH THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT OF THE TRANSPARENCY SEAL POSTING AND UPDATING COMPLIANCE OF THE MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Section 24, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that the State recognizes the vital role of communication and information in nation-building;

**WHEREAS**, Section 26, Article II of the same law guarantees equal access to opportunities for public service;

**WHEREAS**, Section 27, Article II of the same law provides that the State shall maintain honesty and integrity in the public service and take positive and effective measures against graft and corruption;

**WHEREAS**, Section 7, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure every Filipino citizen in its territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life. Social justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, Section 40, Article XII of Republic Act No. 11054 provides that the Bangsamoro Government adopts a policy of full disclosure of its budget, finances, bids and public offerings, and shall provide protocols for the guidance of local authorities in the implementation of said policy which shall include, among others, the posting of the Summary of Income and Expenditures, and the participation of representatives from civil society in the budget process. The same policy shall apply to its constituent local government units, as may be provided in a Bangsamoro local government code to be enacted by the Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, the Department of Budget and Management (DBM) issued National Budget Circular No. 542 on August 29, 2012 which reiterates compliance with Section 93 of the General Appropriations Act (GAA) of FY 2012 or the Transparency Seal provision¹;

**WHEREAS**, Section 93 or the Transparency Provision of GAA FY 2012 provides:

**Section 93**: *Transparency Seal*. To enhance transparency and enforce accountability, all national government agencies shall maintain a transparency seal on their official websites. The transparency seal shall contain the following information:

(i) the agency’s mandates and functions, names of its officials with their position and designation, and contact information;
(ii) annual reports, as required under National Budget Circular Nos. 507 and 507-A dated January 31, 2007, respectively, for the last three (3) years;
(iii) their respective approved budgets and corresponding targets immediately upon approval of this Act;
(iv) major programs and projects categorized in accordance with the five key result areas under E.O. No. 43, s. 2011;
(v) the program/projects beneficiaries as identified in the applicable special provisions;
(vi) status of implementation and program/project evaluation and/or assessment reports; and
(vii) annual procurement plan, contracts awarded and the name of contractors/suppliers/consultants.

The respective heads of the agencies shall be responsible for ensuring compliance with this section²;

**WHEREAS**, a Transparency Seal, prominently displayed on the main page of the website of a particular government agency, is a certificate that it has complied with the requirement of Section 93. This Seal links to a page within the agency’s website which contains an index of downloadable items of each of the above-mentioned documents³;

**WHEREAS**, the Transparency Seal, depicted by a pearl shining out of an open shell, is a symbol of the policy shift towards openness, through providing access to government information. On one hand, it hopes to inspire Filipinos in the civil service to be more open to citizen engagement; on the other, it invites the Filipino citizenry to exercise their right to participate in governance⁴;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office (BICTO) is mandated to be the primary policy, planning, coordinating and implementing body in BARMM for promoting, developing and regulating the use of efficient and effective information and communication technology infrastructure, systems, facilities, and services to support sound, systematic, transparent, and accountable form of governance⁵;

**WHEREAS**, the BICTO is further mandated to promote, provide technical assistance, and monitor the compliance of the BARMM ministries to existing Information and Communications Technology (ICT) laws, policies, standards, and guidelines⁶;

**WHEREAS**, official website links of ministries, agencies and offices in the Bangsamoro Government are posted in the official website of the Bangsamoro Government⁷;

**WHEREAS**, most of these ministries, agencies and offices are not consistently and or devotedly compliant of Section 93 of GAA FY 2012;

**WHEREAS**, although most of these ministries have complied with the posting of Transparency Seal in their official websites, except for few offices or agencies which do not have official website, their posting of the Transparency Seal is not compliant of Section 93 of GAA FY 2012;

**WHEREAS**, as of July 4, 2023, none of the official websites of the ministries, offices, and agencies in the BARMM has completely complied with the documents required by Section 93 of GAA FY 2012;

**WHEREAS**, to ensure and monitor strict compliance of ministries, agencies and offices with Section 93 of GAA FY 2012, these ministries, offices, and agencies should update their official websites in accordance with the said provision; and

**WHEREAS**, being the office in the BARMM which is mandated to promote and monitor the compliance of the BARMM ministries, agencies, and offices to existing ICT laws, policies, standards and guidelines, the BICTO should furnish the BTA Parliament of the compliance of Transparency Seal provision of GAA FY 2012.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the BTA Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Office of the Chief Minister Through the Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology to Monitor and Furnish the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament of the Transparency Seal Posting and Updating Compliance of the Ministries, Offices and Agencies of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the OCM and BICTO for information and appropriate action.
