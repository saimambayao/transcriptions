**RESOLUTION**
**CALLING FOR A SWIFT AND THOROUGH INVESTIGATION INTO THE FATAL SHOOTING OF MS. CHRISTHIA "THIA" ANGELA O. ARAGONCILLO, A LOGISTICS MANAGEMENT OFFICER (LMO) AT THE MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT (MSSD) – MAGUINDANAO PROVINCIAL OFFICE**

**WHEREAS,** the 1987 Philippine Constitution mandates the government to serve and protect the people (Article II, Section 4), maintain peace and order, protect life, liberty and property, and promote the general welfare (Article II, Section 5), ensure that no person is deprived of life, liberty or property without due process of law (Article III, Section 1), and uphold the dignity of every human person while guaranteeing full respect for human rights (Article II, Section 11);

**WHEREAS,** Republic Act No. 11054, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, mandates that "Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall... maintain peace and order and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants" (Article V, Section 3);

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 4 or the “Bangsamoro Human Rights Act of 2019” mandates the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission to “protect human rights and uphold international humanitarian law through conducting investigations, monitoring, fact-finding missions, and public inquiries, reporting the results of the same, and proceeding with prosecution when warranted” [Section 27(b)];

**WHEREAS,** Adopted Resolution No. 268, or the Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, includes a Committee on Bangsamoro Justice System, which has jurisdiction on “all matters relating to the plural justice systems in the BARMM, including but not limited to... criminal jurisdiction which the Parliament has the power to legislate on as provided under Republic Act No. 11054” (Rule XII, Section 3). It also provides for the Parliament to create special or ad hoc committees “to consider specific issues which necessitate immediate and prompt action by the Parliament” (Rule XII, Sections 11 and 12);

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) strongly condemns the senseless killing of Ms. Christhia “Thia” Angela O. Aragoncillo, a social service worker of the Bangsamoro Government, and the wounding of her colleague in the incident that occurred on 17 April 2024;

**WHEREAS,** this coward act of violence against government employees is a direct affront to the rule of law, human rights, and the safety and well-being of the people in the Bangsamoro region;

**WHEREAS,** it is imperative that the perpetrator(s) of this heinous crime be swiftly identified, apprehended, and brought to justice to ensure the protection and security of all Bangsamoro citizens, including those serving in the government;

**WHEREAS,** a thorough and expeditious investigation into this incident is crucial to prevent similar acts of violence from occurring in the future and to maintain peace and order within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Government, in cooperation with relevant law enforcement agencies, must exhaust all efforts to resolve this case promptly and to provide justice for Ms. Aragoncillo, her injured colleague, and their families;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to call for a swift and thorough investigation into the fatal shooting of Ms. Christhia "Thia" Angela O. Aragoncillo, a Logistics Management Officer (LMO) at the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) – Maguindanao Provincial Office;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** to urge the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission, the relevant committee(s) in the BTA Parliament, and the relevant law enforcement agencies to exert all efforts to identify, apprehend, and prosecute the perpetrator(s) of this crime to the fullest extent of the law;

**RESOLVED, FINALLY,** to direct the Bangsamoro Government to provide all necessary support and assistance to the families of Ms. Aragoncillo and her injured colleague and to ensure the safety and security of all government employees in the performance of their duties.
