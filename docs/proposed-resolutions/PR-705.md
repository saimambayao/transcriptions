**A RESOLUTION**

**URGING ALL THE MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO TO INCLUDE MANDATORY DRUG TESTING IN THEIR INTERNAL GUIDELINES ON THE GRANTING OF THE ANNUAL MEDICAL ALLOWANCE TO ENTITLED BARMM GOVERNMENT PERSONNEL**

**Whereas**, Section 7 of Executive Order (EO) No. 64, s.2024 authorizes, beginning FY 2025, the grant of a Medical Allowance to each qualified government civilian personnel in an amount not exceeding Seven Thousand Pesos (₱7,000.00) per annum, subject to the conditions and guidelines to be issued by the Department of Budget and Management (DBM) or the Governance Commission for the GOCCs (GCG), as the case may be;

**Whereas**, the Medical Allowance serves as a subsidy for the availment of health maintenance organization (HMO)-type benefits and may be granted either as an HMO-type product coverage or cash;

**Whereas**, the Bangsamoro Government has included the grant of Medical Allowance for Bangsamoro Government Personnel based on EO No. 64, s.2024 in the 2025 General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro (GAAB);

**Whereas**, the medical allowance benefit covers all positions for the Bangsamoro Government personnel regardless of appointment status, whether regular, casual, or contractual in nature, appointive or elective, full-time or part-time, now existing or hereafter created in the Bangsamoro Government, whose compensations are sourced from the GAAB;

**Whereas**, the grant of Medical Allowance is consistent with the Bangsamoro Government’s commitment to the protection and promotion of the right to health of the people and to instill health consciousness among them per Section 22, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law;

**Whereas**, the Bangsamoro Government likewise adopts the policy of the State to safeguard the well-being of its citizenry from the harmful effects of dangerous drugs on their physical and mental well-being;

**Whereas**, the use of dangerous drugs, which can have a wide range of harmful effects on individuals, families, and communities, continues to pose a serious threat to the health of the Bangsamoro people;

**Whereas**, the Bangsamoro Government has consistently pursued measures to promote morale and the highest degree of responsibility, morality, integrity, loyalty, efficiency, and professionalism in the civil service per Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 37 or the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code and to ensure that the regional bureaucracy is drug-free in keeping with the provisions of Republic Act (RA) No. 9165, or the Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002;

**Whereas**, the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management (MFBM) issued Bangsamoro Budget Circular (BBC) No. 2025-004 on 02 January 2025 to provide guidelines for the release of Medical Allowance to qualified Bangsamoro Government employees;

**Whereas**, MFBM BBC No. 2025-004, s. 2025 authorizes Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (MOAs) to establish their respective internal implementing rules, guidelines, and/or procedures for the release, use, and monitoring of Medical Allowance;

**Whereas**, the inclusion of drug testing will not only deter the use of dangerous drugs that causes impaired judgment, absenteeism, and poor performance of personnel, but it will also help promote safe and healthful working conditions for all BARMM employees;

**Whereas**, the Bangsamoro Government is obliged to ensure that the regional bureaucracy remains drug-free in line with its policies on health and the civil service, and pursuant to the provisions of RA 9165 and the policies laid down in Civil Service Commission (CSC) Memorandum Circular No. 13, series of 2017, as amended, or the Guidelines in the Mandatory Random Drug Test for Public Officials and Employees and for Other Purposes;

**Whereas**, the implementation of drug testing policies as part of workplace regulations helps ensure a drug-free environment;

**Whereas**, implementing drug testing can significantly enhance public trust in the Bangsamoro Government, fostering a safer, secure, and more efficient workforce;

**Whereas**, the inclusion of mandatory drug testing for employees, as a condition for the release of the Medical Allowance, in the respective internal implementing rules, guidelines, and/or procedures of the MOAs per MFBM BBC No. 2025-004, s. 2025 is in the best interest of the Bangsamoro civil service and consistent with the policies of the Bangsamoro Government, the provisions of RA 9165, and the Drug-Free Workplace Policy of the CSC;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it resolved as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to urge all the Ministries, Offices, and Agencies in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to include mandatory drug testing in their internal guidelines on the granting of the annual Medical Allowance to entitled BARMM government personnel.**
