**A RESOLUTION**
**INVITING A CHORAL GROUP FROM ANY SCHOOL OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNIT (LGU) IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM) TO LEAD THE SINGING OF THE NATIONAL ANTHEM AND THE BANGSAMORO HYMN EVERY FIRST MONDAY OF THE SESSION MONTH, THEREBY AMENDING RULE VIII OF ADOPTED RESOLUTION NO. 268, OR THE "RESOLUTION PROVIDING FOR RULES, PROCEDURES, AND PRACTICES OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT".**

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article II of the Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) states that the Bangsamoro Parliament shall adopt the official flag, emblem, and hymn of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. The flag of the Republic of the Philippines shall always be displayed alongside the Bangsamoro flag. Whenever the Bangsamoro hymn is sung, it shall always be preceded by the singing of the Philippine National Anthem;

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article IV of BOL states that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in this Organic Law;

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article V the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article VII of the BOL states that the powers of government shall be vested in the Parliament which shall exercise those powers and functions expressly granted to it in this Organic Law, and those necessary for, or incidental to the proper governance and development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It shall set policies, legislate on matters within its authority, and elect a Chief Minister who shall exercise executive authority on its behalf;

WHEREAS, Section 25, Article VII of the BOL provides that the Parliament shall adopt its own rules for the conduct of its business;

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article XVI of the BOL states:

> **Section 3. Powers and Authorities.** Legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during transition shall be vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA). During the transition period, executive authority shall be exercised by the interim Chief Minister who shall be appointed by the President as such, while legislative authority shall be exercised by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority.
>
> All powers and functions of the Bangsamoro Government as provided in this Organic Law is vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority during the transition period. Xxx

WHEREAS, the BTA adopted Resolution No. 268, entitled "Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament," which guides the conduct of all sessions, committee hearings and meetings, and other similar activities of the BTA¹;

WHEREAS, Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268 provides for the "Parliamentary Order of Business," wherein all matters to be addressed during a specific session are laid down;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is a newly created region that is still in the process of setting up a government suited to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people;

WHEREAS, Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 7, or the "Bangsamoro Hymn Act of 2020," was passed into law on January 30, 2020;

WHEREAS, Section 5 of the said law mandates all public and private schools, colleges and universities, public offices, government-owned or controlled corporations, and other government instrumentalities within the Bangsamoro to play and sing the Bangsamoro Hymn during flag-raising and flag lowering ceremonies;

WHEREAS, the said Section further states that whenever the Bangsamoro Hymn is sung, it shall always be preceded by the singing of the Philippine National Anthem pursuant to Article II, Section 2 of the BOL;

WHEREAS, both the National Anthem and the Bangsamoro Hymn are played through audio-visual presentations during the BTA Parliament's plenary sessions;

WHEREAS, it is preferable to invite a choral group from any school or local government unit (LGU) to sing the National Anthem and the Bangsamoro Hymn during the first Monday of each session month;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED by the BTA Parliament, to amend Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268, to read as follows:

**“Rule VIII. - Parliamentary Order of Business**

**Section 1. Order of Business.** - On any session day of the Parliament, each Member of the Parliament shall be furnished with a copy of the Order of Business.

The daily Order of Business shall be as follows:

(a) Call to Order;
(b) Invocation or Prayer;
(c) National Anthem (by Choral Group);
(d) Bangsamoro Hymn (by Choral Group);
(e) Roll Call;
(f) Reading and Approval of Journal of the Previous Session;
(g) Recognitions, Commendations, Memorials, and Messages, and Communications;
(h) Reference of Business:
(1) Bills on First Reading; and
(2) Resolutions
(i) Bills on Second Reading:
(j) Committee Reports on Bills;
(1) Period of Interpellations;
(2) Period of Amendments; and
(3) Approval of Second Reading.
(k) Resolutions Referred to the Committee:
(1) Committee Reports;
(2) Period of Interpellations;
(3) Period of Amendments; and
(4) Approval.
(l) Unfinished Business;
(m) Privilege Hour;
(n) Question Hour;
(o) Bills on Third Reading.

On the first Wednesday of every quarter, the Order of Business shall include the Chief Minister's Hour immediately after the Roll Call.

In case of Special Session, the Order of Business as communicated shall be strictly in accordance with the purpose of the request of the Chief Minister or the order of the Speaker.

**Section 2. Singing of the National Anthem and Bangsamoro Hymn by a Choral Group.** - A choral group from any school or local government unit (LGU) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) shall be invited to lead the singing of the National Anthem and the Bangsamoro Hymn on the first Monday of each session month.

The choral ensemble may have a maximum of 20 members, or as many as may be allowed by the BTA.

**Section 3. Manner of Dissemination.** - The daily Order of Business shall be posted on the websites of the Parliament and sent through electronic mail the Members. As far as practicable, such Order of Business shall be sent at least three (3) days before the commencement of session.

**Section 4. Amendment of the Order of Business.** - Any Member of the Parliament may, after the Roll Call, move for an amendment of the Order of Business. If no amendment is raised, it shall be deemed adopted.”

RESOLVED FINALLY, by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution inviting a choral group from any school or local government unit (LGU) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) to lead the singing of the National Anthem and the Bangsamoro Hymn every first Monday of the session month, thereby amending Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268, or the "Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament."
