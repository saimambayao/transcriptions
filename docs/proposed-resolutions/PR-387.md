**A RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING THE DELEGATES OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), IN THE 63RD PALARONG PAMBANSA ALSO KNOWN AS THE PALARO 2023 HELD IN MARIKINA CITY, PHILIPPINES**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, and nationalism, encourage involvement in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through sports;

**WHEREAS**, it is also stated that the Bangsamoro educational system shall encourage and support programs, league competitions, indigenous games, martial arts, and amateur sports, including training for regional, national, and international competitions;

**WHEREAS**, *Palarong Pambansa* is an annual multi-sport event involving student-athletes from different regions in the Philippines, governed by the Department of Education since 1948;

**WHEREAS**, the 63rd *Palarong Pambansa*, also known as *Palaro 2023* and Marikina 2023, was held in Marikina, Metro Manila from July 29 to August 5, 2023;

**WHEREAS**, the *Palaro 2023*, featuring the theme “*Batang Malakas, Batang Matatag*” is the first *palaro* since the pandemic following the last *palaro* held from April to May 2019 in Davao City;

**WHEREAS**, a total of 9,172 student-athletes from Kindergarten to Grade 12, representing all seventeen (17) regions of the Philippines, competed in 34 different sporting events and disciplines;

**WHEREAS**, the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) recorded a total of 186 regular athletes and 32 para games (special events) athletes representing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;

**WHEREAS**, MBHTE also provided that the latter participated in a total of seventeen (17) sporting events including three (3) events for para games;

**WHEREAS**, BARMM bagged two (2) gold and four (4) bronze medals in regular sports, two (2) bronze in demo sports, and two (2) silver and one (1) bronze in para games;
