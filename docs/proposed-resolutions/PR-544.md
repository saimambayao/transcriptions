**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE MINISTRY OF BASIC, HIGHER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION (MBHTE) TO ENHANCE ITS COLLABORATION WITH THE PHILIPPINE DRUG ENFORCEMENT AGENCY - BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (PDEA-BARMM) IN STRENGTHENING THE ANTI-ILLEGAL DRUGS INFORMATION, EDUCATION, AND COMMUNICATION (IEC) CAMPAIGN IN ALL EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)¹**

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article II of the 1987 Constitution provides that the State recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being;

**WHEREAS**, Section 15, Article II of the Constitution states that the State shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2 of R.A. No. 9165, or the Comprehensive Dangerous Act of 2002, that the State safeguards the integrity of its territory and the well-being of its citizenry particularly the youth, from the harmful effects of dangerous drugs on their physical and mental well-being, and to defend the same against acts or omissions detrimental to their development and preservation;

**WHEREAS**, Section 43, Article IV of the aforementioned law provides that instruction on drug abuse prevention and control shall be integrated in the elementary, secondary and tertiary curricula of all public and private schools, whether general, technical, vocational or agro-industrial as well as in non-formal, informal and indigenous learning systems;

**WHEREAS**, Section 45, Article IV of the same law provides that with the assistance of the Dangerous Drugs Board (DDB), the Secretary of the Department of Education (DepEd), the Chairman of the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and the Director-General of the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) shall cause the development, publication and distribution of information and support educational materials on dangerous drugs to the students, the faculty, the parents, and the community;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA), as the implementing arm of the DDB, is responsible to cause the efficient and effective implementation of the national drug control strategy formulated by DDB thereby carrying out a national drug campaign program which shall include drug law enforcement, control, and prevention campaign with the assistance of concerned government agencies²;

**WHEREAS**, in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) is the agency responsible for the formulation, planning, implementation, and coordination of the policies, plans, programs, and projects in the areas of formal and non-formal education at all levels, supervise all educational institutions, both public and private, and provide for the establishment, maintenance, and support of a complete, adequate, and integrated system of education relevant and responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro People;

**WHEREAS**, the MBHTE plays a fundamental role in shaping the values and attitudes of the Bangsamoro youth through the educational system;

**WHEREAS**, educational institutions have a central role in shaping the future of the Bangsamoro Youth and of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) as a whole;

**WHEREAS**, according to the DDB, there are approximately 1.8 million current drug users in the Philippines, and 4.8 million Filipinos report having used illegal drugs at least once in their lives³;

**WHEREAS**, a 2019 survey from the DDB extrapolated that two in every 100, or around 1.7 Filipinos aged 10-69 were using drugs⁴;

**WHEREAS**, the issue of drug abuse and addiction in educational institutions continues to be a significant concern in the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, information, education, and communication (IEC) is strategy to spread awareness through communication channels to a target audience to achieve a desired positive result. It is a strategy of sharing information through the broadcast or the print media, or interpersonal communication in a manner, appropriate to the target group’s culture and values⁵;

**WHEREAS**, a collaboration between the PDEA and the MBHTE can significantly enhance the effectiveness of anti-illegal drugs IEC campaigns in all educational institutions in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, it is imperative to strengthen the partnership between law enforcement agencies and educational institutions to prevent and address drug-related issues among Bangsamoro youth, especially the students;

**WHEREAS**, partnership between law enforcement agencies and educational institutions can promote a safer school environment, deter drug-related activities, and discourage students from engaging in drug-related activities;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) to Enhance its Collaboration with the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency - Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (PDEA-BARMM) in Strengthening the Anti-Illegal Drugs Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) Campaign in all Educational Institutions in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA) and to the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) for their information and appropriate actions.
