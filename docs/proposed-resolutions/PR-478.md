**RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES TO COME-UP WITH QUICK RESPONSE (QR) CODE FEEDBACK SURVEYS FOR THEIR SERVICES**

WHEREAS, Section 9, Article II of the 1987 Constitution states that “the State shall promote a just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the nation and free the people from poverty through policies that provide adequate social services, promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality of life for all”;

WHEREAS, Section 38, Article VII of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law, states that the Bangsamoro Government shall have the power to create offices and organize its own bureaucracy. It may establish other offices and institutions necessary for the exercise of its functions and delivery of social services in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region”;

WHEREAS, feedback surveys are defined as a process used to identify or measure the happiness and satisfaction of different business entities or consumers¹;

WHEREAS, feedback surveys can also be defined as a tool that provides an honest opinion of what their present or future customers think about them and helps them in taking an informed decision²;

¹ https://www.questionpro.com/blog/feedback-survey
² https://www.mbaskool.com/business-concepts/marketing-and-strategy-terms/15228-survey-feedback.htm

WHEREAS, in the private sector, customer experiences are valuable to gathering feedback, where understanding people’s needs and then talking action to improve their experiences³ is a common practice;

WHEREAS, in the context of Government Service, Businesses can be equated to the Ministries, and Customers are analogous to the Constituents of the Bangsamoro;

WHEREAS, the ministries, offices, and agencies have their respective approved performance targets based on the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro (GAAB);

WHEREAS, part of the performance indicators ministries, offices, and agencies are satisfaction rating of the beneficiaries;

WHEREAS, Quick Response Codes, often abbreviated to the colloquial term QR Code, are a type of “two-dimensional (2D) bar codes that are used to provide easy access to online information through the digital camera on a smartphone or tablet”⁴;

WHEREAS, QR Codes can be an efficient method of disseminating information to the public⁵;

WHEREAS, QR Codes can be utilized to distribute links to survey forms;

WHEREAS, on the onset of the COVID-19 Pandemic, the use and adoption of QR Codes skyrocketed⁶ and are now widely adopted by the public;

WHEREAS, ministries, offices and agencies soliciting direct feedback from those they serve can be an “effective way to boost satisfaction, address problems, and ensure your programs, policies, and services reflect community needs”⁷;

WHEREAS, a number of National Government Agencies have previously and continue to utilize QR Codes in service delivery⁸; and

WHEREAS, the implementation of Government Surveys can conduct a direct and modernized method of gathering feedback from the constituents of the Bangsamoro.

NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Ministries, Offices, and Agencies to Come-Up with Quick Response (QR) Code Feedback Surveys for their Services.

RESOLVED FINALLY that copy of this Resolution be furnished to all ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
