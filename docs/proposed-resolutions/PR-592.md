**A RESOLUTION ENCOURAGING EFFICIENT USE OF PAPER IN THE MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES OF BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Section 16, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides:

> “*Section 16.* The State shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature.”

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “*Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao,*” states:

> “*Section 3. General Welfare.* The Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the ... xx... enhancement of the right of the people to a balanced ecology ... xx.”

**WHEREAS**, under Section 27 of Republic Act No. 8792, or the *Electronic Commerce Act*:

> “*Section 27. Government Use of Electronic Data Messages, Electronic Documents, and Electronic Signatures.* — Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, within two (2) years from the date of the effectivity of this Act, all departments, bureaus, offices, and agencies of the government, as well as all government-owned and -controlled corporations, that pursuant to law require or accept the filing of documents, require that documents be created, or retained and/or submitted, issue permits, licenses or certificates of registration or approval, or provide for the method and manner of payment or settlement of fees and other obligations to the government, shall —
> 
> (a) accept the creation, filing, or retention of such documents in the form of electronic data messages or electronic documents;
> 
> .. xx ..
> 
> (d) transact the government business and/or perform governmental functions using electronic data messages or electronic documents .. xx.”

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes the importance of sustainable practices in the conservation of natural resources and the reduction of waste;

**WHEREAS**, the pulp and paper industries are one of the highest energy-consuming and polluting sectors and one of the largest greenhouse gases (GHGs) emitters, including carbon dioxide (CO2) among the manufacturing industries¹;

**WHEREAS**, paper consumption in government offices contributes significantly to deforestation and environmental degradation, thereby aggravating climate change and ultimately producing negative effects like the current global warming;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government, in its commitment to promoting environmental sustainability and efficient resource management, must urgently prioritize the establishment of stringent guidelines for paper usage;

**WHEREAS**, the implementation of this Rule can not only substantially reduce paper usage but also improve operational efficiency;

**NOW THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, hereby issues and promulgates the following:

**SECTION 1. Title.** This rule shall be known and cited as the Efficient Use of Paper Rule of the Bangsamoro Government.

**SECTION 2. Applicability.** This Rule shall apply to the Bangsamoro Government, its Ministries, Offices, and Agencies.

**SECTION 3. Limitation on Document Copies.** All communications, correspondence, and similar submissions, including the attachments thereof, shall be submitted in **two (2) copies only**, one for the receiving office and one for the originating office.

Moreover, additional copies that are required to be provided to other departments within the same receiving office should be **rendered only in electronic form**. Extra printed copies may be produced only when specifically requested and for reasonable purposes.

**SECTION 4. Electronic Copies for Concerned Third-Party Offices.** Concerned third-party offices, including but not limited to regulatory bodies, partner organizations, and external stakeholders, shall only receive electronic copies of documents from the Bangsamoro Government. Physical copies shall be provided only upon specific request and justification, in accordance with existing regulations and protocols.

**SECTION 5. Definition of Ordinary Correspondence.** For the purposes of this resolution, "ordinary correspondence" refers to routine communications, memos, letters, and similar documents that do not require special handling or confidentiality and are used by ministries, offices, and agencies as their means of communication for their internal affairs. Ordinary correspondence does not include documents that are classified as confidential or sensitive in nature, which shall be subject to separate guidelines and protocols for handling and dissemination.

**SECTION 6. Electronic copies for Ordinary Correspondence.** All ordinary correspondence, including, but not limited to memos, letters, and internal documents, shall **utilize electronic signatures**, which will be recognized by relevant ministries, offices, and agencies as having the same effect as wet signatures.

Hard copies of ordinary correspondence shall not be required, and electronic copies shall be deemed sufficient for all official purposes. Electronic signatures utilized in ordinary correspondence shall comply with established security protocols to ensure authenticity and integrity.

**SECTION 7. Date of Effectivity.** This Rule shall take effect upon its approval.
