**A RESOLUTION CONDEMNING IN THE STRONGEST SENSE THE SHOOTING AND KILLING OF USTADZ MOHAMMAD HESSAM A. MIDTIMBANG OF THE BANGSAMORO DARUL-IFTA’ – BARMM AND URGING THE COTABATO CITY POLICE OFFICE TO INVESTIGATE THE INCIDENT AND THE BANGSAMORO HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION (BHRC) TO MONITOR CLOSELY THE PROGRESS OF THE CASE**

WHEREAS, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy;

WHEREAS, Section 1 Article III of the 1987 Philippine Constitution also provides that no person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law, nor shall any person be denied the equal protection of the laws;

WHEREAS, Article V, Section 3 of Republic Act 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

WHEREAS, Republic Act No. 8551, otherwise known as the Philippine National Police Reform and Reorganization Act of 1998, states that the Philippine National Police (PNP) shall be a community and service oriented agency responsible for the maintenance of peace and order and public safety;

WHEREAS, it is declared as the policy of the Bangsamoro Government that the Bangsamoro Government values the dignity of every human person and guarantees full respect for human rights. It shall give the highest priority to the enactment of measures to protect and enhance the right of all people to human dignity at all stages of human life;

WHEREAS, on August 21, 2023, Ustadz Mohammad Hessam A. Midtimbang, an employee of the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ was shot and killed around 6:00pm at Governor Gutierrez Avenue, Cotabato City, a few hundred meters away from the main gate of the Bangsamoro Government Center, is strongly condemned;

WHEREAS, Ustadz Midtimbang is 34 years old, a father of 2-year-old female child and a promising and active Bangsamoro youth employee of Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ – BARMM who dedicated his life to the Bangsamoro struggle;

WHEREAS, the tragic death of Ustadz Mohammad Hessam A. Midtimbang not only deprived his family of their pride and joy, but also deprived the Bangsamoro People of a good leader;

WHEREAS, government employees play a crucial role in the delivery of essential government services to the Bangsamoro People, and their safety and security are of paramount importance to the stability and progress of the region;

WHEREAS, Cotabato City LGU and City Police force need to strengthen the safety and security measures in the City;

WHEREAS, the progress of the case should be monitored by the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC);

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by this parliament, to condemn in the strongest sense the shooting and killing of Ustadz Mohammad Hessam A. Midtimbang of the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ – BARMM and urging the Cotabato City Police Office to investigate the incident and the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC) to monitor closely the progress of the case;

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the City Mayor Hon. Bruce Matabalao, BHRC, the Cotabato City Police Director PCOL Querubin L. Manalang. Jr, and the Office of the Chief Minister, for information and appropriate action.
