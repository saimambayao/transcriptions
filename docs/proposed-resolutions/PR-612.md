**A RESOLUTION URGING ALL MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, AND OFFICES, INCLUDING THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS, IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, TO UTILIZE NEGOTIATED PROCUREMENT-COMMUNITY PARTICIPATION IN PROCUREMENT TO PROMOTE FOOD SECURITY AND SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE IN THE REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Section 9, Article II of the Philippine Constitution directs the State to “promote a just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the nation and free the people from poverty through policies that provide adequate social services, promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality of life for all;”

**WHEREAS**, Goal 1 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDG), which “provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet,”¹ is to end poverty in all its forms everywhere;

**WHEREAS**, Goal 2 of the SDG is to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippine Government wants to ensure that food will be sufficient and stable, and that Filipino consumers have access to safe, nutritious, and affordable food, and that nutrition improves for Filipinos of all ages (Subchapter 3.1 Philippine Development Plan (PDP) 2023-2028);²

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government targets to attain equitable, competitive, and robust economy through agri-fishery productivity and tenurial security improvement, and food security;

**WHEREAS**, food security remains a pressing issue in the Bangsamoro region, where poverty and malnutrition continue to affect a significant portion of the population, and empowering local farmers and fisherfolk is essential for sustainable agricultural and fishery development, which in turn can contribute significantly to food security and create inclusive economic opportunities;

**WHEREAS**, the government is implementing several food programs and projects that demonstrate an ongoing efforts to address food security, but due to strict procurement policies and the lack of market access and trade monopoly, local farmers and fishermen are unable to participate as producers or suppliers;

**WHEREAS**, the active involvement of local farmers and fisherfolk in procurement processes can provide them with better market access, fair prices, and the opportunity to supply goods and services directly eliminating unscrupulous middlemen traders in the supply chain, thereby improving their livelihoods;

**WHEREAS**, the conduct of negotiated procurement-community participation in procurement is a viable strategy to directly engage local communities of farmers and fisherfolks, ensuring that procurement processes are inclusive and offer a unique opportunity to promote equitable growth, ensuring that the benefits of development projects reach marginalized communities and alleviate food security in the region;

**WHEREAS**, the principles of moral governance, transparency, accountability and community empowerment are fundamental to the sustainable development and prosperity of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**WHEREAS**, it is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote domestic trade preference for goods produced and materials sourced from within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and adopt measures to increase their competitiveness;

**WHEREAS**, the Government Procurement Reform Act (GPRA) Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) provides mechanisms for community participation as one of the instances by which negotiated procurement can be implemented;

**WHEREAS**, Section 53.12 of the said IRR provides that “xxx in the interest of project sustainability or to achieve certain specific social objectives, it is desirable in selected projects, or its components, to call for participation of local communities through Community or Social Groups, or members thereof in the delivery of Goods and Simple Infrastructure Projects, subject to the Guidelines issued by the Government Procurement Policy Board (GPPB);

**WHEREAS**, the GPPB issued Resolution No. 18-2021 to serve as guidelines in the implementation of Community-based Projects by the Procuring Entities (PEs);

**WHEREAS**, this Guidelines has been developed to integrate an inclusive procurement process that promotes the participation of Community or Social Groups (CSGs) and local farmers and fisherfolk in Community-based Projects while incorporating the relevant provisions of such laws on community outsourcing initiatives;

**WHEREAS**, negotiated procurement through community participation encourages the community to organize themselves and participate in the procurement process and ensure that its intended social objectives are achieved and project sustainability is increased;

**WHEREAS**, under negotiated procurement through community participation, the community is given the opportunity to be involved in managing the procurement by participating in the planning, procurement, and implementation of the project, as well as acting as contractors or service providers;

**WHEREAS**, by harnessing the expertise and resources of the community, the Bangsamoro government can foster sustainable food system, enhance local economic development, and promote the overall well-being of the Bangsamoro people;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it RESOLVED** by the Bangsamoro Parliament to urge all ministries, agencies, and offices, including local government units in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, to utilize negotiated procurement-community participation in procurement to promote food security and sustainable agriculture in the region.
