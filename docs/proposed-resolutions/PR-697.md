**A RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH (MOH), LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS (LGUs), THROUGH THE MINISTRY OF INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT (MILG), IN COORDINATION WITH THE PHILIPPINE HEALTH INSURANCE CORPORATION – BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (PHILHEALTH-BARMM), TO PROMOTE, STRENGTHEN, AND INTENSIFY AWARENESS CAMPAIGNS AND INFORMATION DRIVES ON THE KONSULTASYONG SULIT AT TAMA (KONSULTA) PROGRAM**

**WHEREAS,** Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution (Constitution) provides that the State shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

**WHEREAS,** Section 11, Article XIII of the Constitution provides that the State shall adopt an integrated and comprehensive approach to health development which shall endeavor to make essential goods, health and other social services available to all the people at affordable cost;

**WHEREAS,** the same provision states that there shall priority for the needs of the under-privileged, sick, elderly, disabled, women, and children and that it shall endeavor to provide free medical care to paupers;

**WHEREAS,** Section 2, Chapter 1 of Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11223 or the Universal Health Care Act provides that the State shall adopt a health care model that provides all Filipinos access to a comprehensive set of quality and cost-effective, promotive, preventive, curative, rehabilitative and palliative health services without causing financial hardship, and prioritizes the needs of the population who cannot afford such services;

**WHEREAS,** the same section provides that the State shall adopt a people-oriented approach for the delivery of health services that is centered on people’s needs and well-being, and cognizant of the differences in culture, values, and beliefs;

**WHEREAS,** the Universal Health Care Act (UHCA) in its Section 6(b) mandates for the implementation of a comprehensive outpatient benefit, including outpatient drug benefit and emergency services;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Rule I of the Implementing Rules and Regulations of R.A. No. 11223, aims to ensure that all Filipinos are guaranteed equitable access to quality and affordable health care goods and services and protected against financial risk;

**WHEREAS,** Section 7, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure every Filipino citizen in its territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life. Social justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS,** Section 8, Article IX of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs, quality education, appropriate services, livelihood opportunities, affordable and progressive housing projects, power and electricity, and water inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Konsultasyong Sulit at Tama (Konsulta) Program of PhilHealth is a comprehensive outpatient benefits as mandated by the Universal Health Care Law which aims to protect the health of every Filipino against chronic illnesses, avoid complications through every detection and to provide affordable drugs and medicines¹;

**WHEREAS,** under the PhilHealth Konsulta, all Filipinos shall be registered with an accredited PhilHealth Konsulta Provider of their choice and be eligible to avail consultation, health risk screening and assessment, selected laboratory and diagnostic test, and selected drugs and medicines²;

**WHEREAS,** the Konsulta includes first-contact, comprehensive, continuing and coordinated primary care services that include consultation and case management of all conditions, including individual health education managed by a Primary Care Physician and team as determined by the legal scope of practice³;

**WHEREAS,** Konsulta also provides preventive health services, assisting eligible beneficiaries access to facilities, referrals to specialty care, some laboratory or diagnostic examinations needed as part of case management by the PhilHealth Konsulta primary care team⁴;

**WHEREAS,** in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), the PhilHealth-BARMM advises members to enroll⁵ in Konsulta and it also intensifies⁶ Konsulta info drive in BARMM;

**WHEREAS,** the package specifically covers 13 laboratory and diagnostic examinations namely complete blood count with platelet count, urinalysis, fecalysis, sputum microscopy, fecal occult blood, pap smear, HbA1c, lipid profile, fasting blood sugar, oral glucose tolerance test, creatinine, electrocardiogram, and chest x-ray⁷;

**WHEREAS,** the medicines that can be availed of are anti-microbial, fluid and electrolytes, anti-asthma, anti-pyretics, anti-diabetic, anti-dyslipidemia, anti-hypertensives, anti-thrombotics, and anti-histamine⁸;

**WHEREAS,** as of February 28, 2025, seventy-seven (77) Konsulta Package Providers (KPPs) have been accredited in the BARMM⁹;

**WHEREAS,** despite the availability of KPPs, many constituents in BARMM remain unaware of the Konsulta Program and how to avail of its services;

**WHEREAS,** based on PhilHealth data, in 2024, only one (1) in twenty (20) Filipinos accessed the Konsulta program¹⁰;

**WHEREAS,** out of the 112.88 million population in 2024, 90 percent or 102.75 million are registered as PhilHealth members, but only 27.8 million or 24.6 percent are registered with Konsulta providers¹¹;

**WHEREAS,** only 6.37 million (5.6 percent) had a “first-patient encounter,” wherein a primary care provider took or updated the basic health data of an eligible beneficiary¹²;

**WHEREAS,** under RA 11223, all Filipinos are automatically enrolled as PhilHealth members, but not all PhilHealth members are immediately enrolled in Konsulta, as it requires registration with their preferred providers¹³;

**WHEREAS,** according to PhilHealth data, 88 percent of the 1,642 cities and municipalities in the country already have an accredited Konsulta provider, but relatively few Local Government Units (LGUs) are “very aggressive” in helping their people to get registered¹⁴;

**WHEREAS,** according to PhilHealth President and CEO, Dr. Edwin Mercado, although PhilHealth is providing many benefits under the Konsulta program, if the Filipinos do not have access to them because there are only a few hospitals and facilities that are accredited to offer them, then the program will not succeed¹⁵;

**WHEREAS,** there is a need to promote, strengthen and intensify awareness campaigns and information drives about the PhilHealth Konsulta program to the constituents of the Bangsamoro Region in order for them to avail of these services;

**WHEREAS,** BARMM LGUs are encouraged to help in disseminating information to their constituents about the Konsulta program;

**WHEREAS,** in the BARMM, the Ministry of Health (MOH) is mandated to promote, protect, preserve, and restore people’s health through regulation and support of health-care providers and is in-charge of the development, planning, implementation, and coordination of health policies and programs;

**WHEREAS,** the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG) is mandated to regulate its constituent local governments to ensure that their affair affairs are within the scope of their powers and functions;

**WHEREAS,** coordinated efforts among MOH, MILG, and PhilHealth-BARMM are crucial to effectively disseminate information and promote widespread awareness and utilization of the Konsulta Program;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Ministry of Health (MOH), Local Government Units (LGUs), through the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG), in coordination with Philippine Health Insurance Corporation – Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (PhilHealth-BARMM), to promote, strengthen, and intensify awareness campaigns and information drives on the Konsultasyong Sulit at Tama (Konsulta) Program as mandated by Republic Act No. 11223 or the Universal Health Care Act.

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Ministry of Health (MOH), the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG), and PhilHealth-BARMM for their information and appropriate action.
