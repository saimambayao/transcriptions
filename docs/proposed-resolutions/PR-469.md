**RESOLUTION**
**CONDEMNING IN THE STRONGEST POSSIBLE TERMS THE BOMBING INCIDENT DURING A SUNDAY CATHOLIC MASS AT DIMAPORO GYMNASIUM IN MINDANAO STATE UNIVERSITY, MARAWI CITY**

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 4, Art. II, 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the "prime duty of the Government is to serve and protect the people";

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 5, Art. II, 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that "the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy";

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 11, Article II, 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that "the State values the dignity of every human person and guarantees full respect for human rights";

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 1, Art. III, 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that "no person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law";

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 5, Art. III, 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the free exercise and enjoyment of religious profession and worship, without discrimination or preference, shall forever be allowed;

**WHEREAS**, on 3 December 2023 at approximately 7:00 AM, a bombing incident occurred at Dimaporo Gymnasium, Mindanao State University (the "University"), during a Sunday Catholic mass;

**WHEREAS**, a Philippine National Police - Police Regional Office Bangsamoro Autonomous Region (PNP PRO-BAR) Spot Report indicates that the bombing resulted in the immediate death of four individuals. Three of them died instantaneously at the gymnasium, while the other one was declared dead on arrival upon reaching the APMC hospital;

**WHEREAS**, this report further details that among the casualties were at least forty-two (42) injured individuals, many being students of Mindanao State University, were immediately taken to Amai Pakpak Medical Center (APMC) for urgent medical care. Among them, four (4) required surgical intervention, and thirty-three (33) are currently under close medical supervision;

**WHEREAS**, findings by the Office of Member of Parliament Diamila Disimban Ramos-Marawi Team also noted that other individuals received medical assistance for minor injuries at MSU-Medical Services and Hospital or the University Infirmary;

**WHEREAS**, Mindanao State University issued a statement on the same day strongly condemning the lawless act and expressed heartfelt condolences to the victims and their families. The University suspended classes indefinitely, heightened security measures, and collaborated with local authorities and law enforcement in the ongoing investigation to apprehend those responsible;

**WHEREAS**, on 4 December 2023, Provincial Governor Of Lanao Del Sur Hon. Mamintal "Bombit" Alonto Adiong Jr. released a public statement confirming that the number of dead victims remained four (4), while the affected rose to at least fifty (50) individuals where thirty-five (35) of them are released from confinement;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government vehemently condemns this heinous act of violence during the Sunday mass, recognizing it as a profound breach of societal norms and values, and stands united in its vehement demand for justice and denunciation of such violent acts;

**WHEREAS**, in this time of tribulation, we stand in solidarity with the community of peace, urging adherence to safety protocols set by local authorities, and together, we reaffirm our commitment to fostering a society rooted in tolerance, respect, and lasting peace;

**WHEREAS**, we express our deepest condolences to the families of the four victims who tragically lost their lives in this atrocity, and extend our sincere sympathies to the injured and affected, assuring them of our steadfast support in their time of need.

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to condemn in the strongest possible terms the bombing incident during the Catholic Mass held at the Dimaporo Gymnasium in Mindanao State University, Marawi City.
