**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY (GOTD) TO INSTITUTE PREPARATORY MEASURES FOR THE IMPENDING EL NIÑO PHENOMENON**

**WHEREAS**, Section 16, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature;

**WHEREAS**, Section 8, Article XIII of the Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) states that the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and development of forests, coastal, and marine resources, including the adoption of programs and projects, to ensure the maintenance of ecological balance and biodiversity shall be given priority;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the same law states in part that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) is the state weather bureau of the Philippines mandated to provide protection against natural calamities and utilize scientific knowledge as an effective instrument to insure the safety, well-being, and economic safety of all the people, and for promotion of national progress¹;

**WHEREAS**, El Niño refers to a warming of the ocean surface, or above-average sea surface temperatures, in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean²;

**WHEREAS**, recent developments in the tropical Pacific has led PAGASA to raise the PAGASA El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO) Alert and Warning System to **El Niño Alert** on May 2, 2023, where recent conditions and model forecasts indicate that El Niño may emerge in the coming season (June-July-August) at 80 percent probability and may persist until the first quarter of 2024³;

**WHEREAS**, El Niño increases the likelihood of below-normal rainfall conditions, which could have negative impacts such as dry spells and droughts in some areas of the country⁵;

**WHEREAS**, the El Niño phenomenon has a serious and adverse impacts on agriculture, particularly rice and corn production, which may affect the availability and distribution of food in the country;

**WHEREAS**, the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR) said that the country’s fish supply may be affected by the looming El Niño, which could result in fish kills⁶;

**WHEREAS**, Secretary Benhur Abalos of the Department of Interior and Local Government (DILG) has directed through Memorandum Circular No. 2023-065 that “all governors, mayors, barangays, including the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) Minister of Interior and Local Government, and regional directors, to prepare for El Niño to mitigate its impact in their respective areas⁷;

**WHEREAS**, a ranking official of the PAGASA warned that Mindanao will be severely affected by the looming El Niño phenomenon – with at least a 35 percent reduction in rainwater brought by a lack of typhoons – followed by Visayas and Luzon⁸;

**WHEREAS**, there is a need to undertake preparatory measures in the BARMM to mitigate and if possible, prevent the impending effects of El Niño;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the BTA Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Government of the Day (GOTD) to Institute Preparatory Measures for the Impending El Niño Phenomenon.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), for information and appropriate action.
