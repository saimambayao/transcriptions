**RESOLUTION**

**CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING MR. MOHAMMAD NUR GUMA-OS CASIB FOR HIS EXEMPLARY ACHIEVEMENT IN WINNING A GOLD MEDAL AND BEING HAILED AS THE “NUCLEAR AMBASSADOR” IN THE 1ST INTERNATIONAL NUCLEAR SCIENCE OLYMPIAD, AND FOR GIVING PRIDE AND HONOR TO THE BANGSAMORO PEOPLE**

**WHEREAS,** the State recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being,¹ and declares further that science and technology are essential for national development and progress;²

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Government similarly recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promotes and protects their physical, moral, spiritual, and nationalism³ and likewise acknowledges that science and technology as essential to national and regional progress;⁴

**WHEREAS,** the first-ever International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) was held on August 1-6 at the New Clark City in the Philippines;⁵

**WHEREAS,** INSO is an annual international science competition for secondary school students younger than 20 years old. It will be hosted by an Asian country and launched in August 2024 under the framework of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) technical cooperation project “Supporting Nuclear Science and Technology Education at the Secondary and Tertiary Level”;⁶

**WHEREAS,** the Philippines was the host country for the first INSO. It is organized by the Philippine Nuclear Research Institute (DOST-PNRI), with support from the International Atomic Energy Agency;⁷

**WHEREAS,** the primary objective of the First International Nuclear Science Olympiad (1st INSO) in the Philippines is to showcase the country’s commitment to advancing nuclear education and fostering international cooperation in the peaceful applications of nuclear science and technology;⁸

**WHEREAS,** 37 Olympians reaped the rewards of their months-long efforts, securing 8 gold, 13 silver, and 16 bronze medals among 55 competitors from 14 Asia-Pacific countries. The medalists included both junior and senior high school students aged 20 years and under;⁹

**WHEREAS,** 2 of the gold medalists are from the Philippines, namely Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib and Neil Kyle Olaer Maniquis;¹⁰

**WHEREAS,** a special award was also conferred to Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib for obtaining the highest points in the experimental and theoretical exams. Furthermore, he held the highest points overall, earning him the title “Nuclear Ambassador”;¹¹

**WHEREAS,** Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib is an incoming Grade 12 student of the Philippine Science High School (PSHS) Central Mindanao Campus in Balo-i, Lanao del Norte;¹²

**WHEREAS,** Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib is a young Bangsamoro of Maranao descent and a proud son of Dr. Norodin S. Casib and Dra. Sittienor M. Guma-os - Casib, who are natives of the Municipalities of Bayang and Ganassi, Lanao del Sur, BARMM;

**WHEREAS,** in March 2024, Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib also won Gold in the 36th International Math Olympiad held at the University of the Philippines in Diliman, and he is a participant in another Olympiad in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, for the 17th International Olympiad on Astronomy and Astrophysics from August 17 to 26, 2024;¹³

**WHEREAS,** his remarkable achievements have not only demonstrated unparalleled dedication and excellence in his field but have also made him an inspiration for the Bangsamoro youth, serving as a powerful testament to the limitless potential within the Bangsamoro community and motivating others to strive for greatness;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Government takes immense pride in his outstanding accomplishments, recognizing them not only as personal victories but as a collective triumph that reflects the success and progress of the entire Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, further solidifying our commitment to fostering excellence and empowering our youth;

**NOW THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority to congratulate and commend Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib for his exemplary achievement in winning gold and being hailed as the “Nuclear Ambassador” in the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad, and for bringing pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people;

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** to present a copy of this resolution to Mohammad Nur Guma-os Casib in recognition and appreciation of his exemplary dedication and hard work that brought pride to the Bangsamoro people.
