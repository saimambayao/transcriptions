**A RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING THE HONORABLE DEPUTY FLOOR LEADER OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT AND MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT (MSSD) MINISTER ATTY. RAISSA HERRADURA JAJURIE FOR BEING A RECIPIENT OF THE 2024 INQUIRER WOMEN OF POWER (WOP) AWARD GIVEN BY THE PHILIPPINE DAILY INQUIRER LAST MARCH 11, 2024**

**WHEREAS,** it is enshrined in Article II, Section 14 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution that, “The State recognizes the role of women in nation-building, and shall ensure the fundamental equality before the law of women and men.”

**WHEREAS,** Article XIII, Section 5 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that “The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the role of women in governance and ensure their fundamental equality before the law. It shall guarantee full and direct participation of women in governance and development process, ensuring that women shall benefit equally in the implementation of development programs and projects.”

**WHEREAS,** the Philippine Daily Inquirer – one of the country’s most trusted newspapers with more than a million daily readers nationwide – has been giving the Women of Power (WOP) Award to honor exceptional women from diverse sectors for their remarkable achievements in their profession, business, civic mission, or creative endeavors, through which they have become a source of inspiration and made a positive impact on the community.

**WHEREAS,** on March 11, 2024, Honorable Member of Parliament and MSSD Minister Atty. Raisa H. Jajurie was chosen as one of the recipients of the 2024 Women of Power Award during a ceremony at Seda Hotel in Bonifacio Global City, Taguig by The Philippine Daily Inquirer.

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Raisa Herradura Jajurie is a Bangsamoro Lawyer who is currently serving the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) as a Deputy Floor Leader of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, the current Minister of the Ministry of Social Services and Development, the Committee on Local Government Chairperson and Committee on Rules Vice Chairperson.

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Jajurie is a Tausug who was born in the southern part of the Philippines – Jolo Sulu, a graduate of Ateneo de Manila University with a degree in Political Science. She finished her Law degree at the University of the Philippines and was admitted to the Philippine Bar in the year 1995.

**WHEREAS,** prior to her appointment as a Member of the Parliament and MSSD Minister, Atty. Jajurie held several key positions in different organizations where she displayed extraordinary leadership, service, and competence, which include the following:
*   Co-founder, Nissa Ui-Haqq fi Bangsamoro (Women for Justice in the Bangsamoro)
*   Co-founder Bangsamoro Lawyers Network (BLN)
*   Member, Moro Islamic Liberation Front Peace Implementing Panel
*   Member, Bangsamoro Transition Commission (tasked to create a draft for the Bangsamoro Organic Law)

**WHEREAS,** for over two decades, Atty. Jajurie also served in the Sentro ng Alternatibong Lingap Panlegal (SALIGAN) where she was active in human rights advocacy as a lawyer, researcher, and trainer while working with Moro Lawyers, Muslim Women, and the Moro communities.

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Jajurie’s career is indeed an exceptional and exemplary one befitting of commendation and noteworthy for taking the role of setting a benchmark for excellence.

**WHEREAS,** this resolution intends to give due credit to the efforts of our fellow Bangsamoro who have truly exhibited the prowess that a Bangsamoro Woman is capable of, in the Philippines and around the world.

**WHEREAS,** to recognize and celebrate this excellent and historic accomplishment of our very own colleague in this Honorable Parliament, it is therefore fitting and proper that we should extend our congratulations and commendation for this extraordinary woman of power.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to **CONGRATULATE AND COMMEND THE HONORABLE DEPUTY FLOOR LEADER OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT AND MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT (MSSD) MINISTER ATTY. RAISSA HERRADURA JAJURIE FOR BEING A RECIPIENT OF THE 2024 INQUIRER WOMEN OF POWER (WOP) AWARD GIVEN BY THE PHILIPPINE DAILY INQUIRER LAST MARCH 11, 2024.**
