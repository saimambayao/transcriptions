**RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, AND OFFICES OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT TO INCLUDE PROGRAMS, PROJECTS, ACTIVITIES, AND SERVICES FOR OTHER BANGSAMORO COMMUNITIES IN THEIR RESPECTIVE BUDGETS IN IMPLEMENTATION OF SECTION 12, ARTICLE VI OF THE BANGSAMORO ORGANIC LAW**

**WHEREAS,** the 1987 Philippine Constitution recognizes the autonomy of Muslim Mindanao within the framework of national sovereignty and territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines;¹

**WHEREAS,** Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, was enacted to “establish a political entity, provide for its basic structure of government in recognition of the justness and legitimacy of the cause of the Bangsamoro people and the aspirations of Muslim Filipinos and all indigenous cultural communities in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to secure their identity and posterity, allowing for meaningful self-governance within the framework of the Constitution and the national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines”;²

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Organic Law mandates that “[t]he National Government shall ensure the protection of the rights of the Bangsamoro people residing in communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and undertake programs for their rehabilitation and development. The Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with local government units where these communities are located and the appropriate national government agencies, shall provide assistance to enhance their economic, social and cultural development”;³

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 13, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, established the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC) under the Office of the Chief Minister to serve as the primary unit responsible for recommending policies and systematic programs for promoting the welfare of Bangsamoro communities outside the region, including provision of services, in coordination with local government units as well as appropriate national government agencies;⁴

**WHEREAS,** the OOBC conducted a series of consultations in Pagadian City⁵, Ipil⁶, Davao City⁷, and General Santos City⁸ to gather insights and recommendations from stakeholders on the development needs and priorities of Bangsamoro communities residing outside the BARMM;

**WHEREAS,** these consultations identified key priority areas for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OBCs) including education, economic development, social development, cultural preservation, and strengthening connections and linkages;⁹

**WHEREAS,** specific needs highlighted during these consultations include scholarship programs for both secular and Islamic education, support for Madaris and religious teachers, development of the Halal industry, support for agriculture and fisheries sectors, extension of existing BARMM social and humanitarian programs, youth-focused initiatives, cultural heritage preservation, and establishment of OOBC offices in each province;¹⁰

**WHEREAS,** the OOBC has outlined a comprehensive plan for 2025-2028 focusing on gathering information and assessing needs, policy recommendation and program development, strengthening coordination and collaboration, institutional strengthening and capacity building, monitoring, evaluation, and reporting, and strategic communication;¹¹

**WHEREAS,** to effectively implement Section 12, Article VI of the Bangsamoro Organic Law and address the identified needs of OBCs, it is crucial that all ministries, agencies, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government incorporate programs, projects, activities, and services for OBCs in their 2025 budget plans;

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, to urge the ministries, agencies, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government to include programs, projects, activities, and services for Other Bangsamoro Communities in their respective budgets in implementation of Section 12, Article VI of the Bangsamoro Organic Law;

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that the ministries, agencies, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government coordinate with the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities in identifying and designing appropriate programs, projects, activities, and services for OBCs to ensure alignment with identified needs and priorities;

**RESOLVED FINALLY,** that copies of this resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, all ministries, agencies, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government, and the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities for their information and appropriate action.
