**RESOLUTION**

**CONDEMNING THE RAID THAT LED TO THE KILLING OF SEVEN (7) MEMBERS OF THE MORO ISLAMIC LIBERATION FRONT (MILF), CALLING FOR FAIR, IMPARTIAL, AND SPEEDY INVESTIGATION, AND REQUESTING THE RETURN OF THE INTERNATIONAL MONITORING TEAM (IMT) IN THE BANGSAMORO REGION**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament is committed to upholding the rights, welfare, and security of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, it has come to the attention of the Bangsamoro Parliament that a raid conducted on June 18, 2023, in Barangay Damawato, Datu Paglas town in Maguindanao del Sur resulted in the tragic killing of seven members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF);

**WHEREAS**, the victims who are Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) members, namely: NASSER YOUSEF M. HUSAIN (34 y.o.), NORJIHAD M. HUSAIN (29 y.o.), NASRULLAH MAMAY SINGKALA (38 y.o.), IVAN PUMPUGAY (18 y.o.), IZRAEL LAGUIAB (41 y.o.), MORSID MADIDIS (50 y.o.), AND MAMA KAKIM (53 y.o.), all succumbed to fatal gunshot wounds;

**WHEREAS**, it is the duty of the Bangsamoro Parliament to ensure that the human rights of all Filipino citizens, including members of the MILF, are respected and protected;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament recognizes the need for law enforcement agencies to apprehend individuals suspected of criminal activities, but this must be done in accordance with established legal procedures and in compliance with human rights standards;

**WHEREAS**, it has been reported that the victims were hand-cuffed and killed in different areas, including a mosque, raising concerns about the use of excessive force and the violation of the victims’ human rights;

**WHEREAS**, the raid occurred in the midst of the implementation of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro, specifically the decommissioning and normalization track, where the MILF is a significant stakeholder committed to the pursuit of lasting peace;

**WHEREAS**, the victims have already filed their application for amnesty and were anticipating the grant thereof;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament acknowledges the role of the International Monitoring Team (IMT) in fostering confidence-building measures and maintaining peace and security in the region;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Parliament strongly condemns the raid that led to the killing of seven members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and expresses deep concern over the reported use of excessive force and the violation of the victims’ rights;

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Parliament calls the higher authorities for a fair, impartial, inquiry and speedy investigation into the circumstances surrounding the raid, with the aim of holding accountable those responsible for any wrongdoing or violations of the law;

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Parliament urges the relevant authorities to ensure that the investigation is conducted in a transparent manner and that any findings are made public;

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Parliament requests the immediate return of the International Monitoring Team (IMT) in the Bangsamoro Region to facilitate the ongoing peace process and provide independent monitoring in light of the current situation on the ground;

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Parliament affirms its commitment to the peace process and calls for all parties to uphold the established processes, including the Coordinating Committee on the Cessation of Hostilities (CCCH), the Ad Hoc Joint Action Group (AHJAG), and the peace panel, in apprehending MILF members suspected of involvement in criminal activities;

**BE IT FINALLY RESOLVED**, that copies of this resolution be transmitted to the Office of the Chief Minister, the Office of the Presidential Adviser on Peace, Reconciliation and Unity (OPAPRU), the Commission on Human Rights, and other relevant government agencies for their immediate attention and appropriate action.
