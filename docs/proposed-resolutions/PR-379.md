**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY (GOTD) TO PRODUCE A MORAL GOVERNANCE HANDBOOK TO GUIDE STAKEHOLDERS OF ITS IMPLEMENTATION IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Section 27, Article II of the 1987 Constitution provides that the State shall maintain honesty and integrity in the public service and take positive and effective measures against graft and corruption;

**WHEREAS**, Section 20, Article II of the same law stipulates that the State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides incentives to needed investments.

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article IV of the Republic Act No. 11054, also known as Bangsamoro Organic Law, states that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in this Organic Law;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the aforementioned law provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants.

**WHEREAS**, Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 13, or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, defines Moral Governance as the set of rules, practices, and processes completely devoid of all the evils of graft and corruption, and explicitly driven by the moral principles of utmost dedication, devotion, honesty, justice, and integrity;

**WHEREAS**, the first Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) of 2020 outlined Principles and Pillars of Moral Governance, which included: *Taqwa* (Cognizance of God), *Akhlaq* (Moral and Ethical Values), *Khalifah* (Vicegerency and Upholding Trust), and *Ihsan* (Striving for Excellence)¹;

**WHEREAS**, Chief Minister Ahod Ebrahim called on every Bangsamoro to embrace Moral Governance in their everyday lives²;

**WHEREAS**, the Government of the Day continues to inculcate values of moral governance to its workforce, to further enhance service delivery and operation in the region³;

**WHEREAS**, Moral Governance is also continuously promoted at all levels of education in the region⁴;

**WHEREAS**, lectures on Moral Governance are also often conducted in BARMM, through activities such as oath-taking⁵;

**WHEREAS**, aside from BAA No. 13 and the BDP 2020, Moral Governance is not widely defined and or discussed in public aside from governmental affairs;

**WHEREAS**, stakeholders in the public and private sectors of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region may be interested in understanding and adopting Moral Governance and its principles;

**WHEREAS**, various sectors in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region perceive Moral Governance as a key ingredient in ensuring the region’s success⁶;

**WHEREAS**, the crafting of a handbook could synthesize and standardize various lectures and definitions of Moral Governance, promoting efficiency;

**WHEREAS**, the dissemination of the proposed handbook can also further promote and spread awareness about Moral Governance to the public and private sectors;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Government of the Day to produce a Moral Governance handbook to guide the stakeholders in its implementation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Adopted,
