**RESOLUTION**

**CONDEMNING IN THE STRONGEST POSSIBLE TERMS THE HEINOUS BOMBING OF THE DIMAPORO GYMNASIUM IN THE MINDANAO STATE UNIVERSITY (MSU) – MAIN CAMPUS IN MARAWI CITY, LANAO DEL SUR DURING A SUNDAY CATHOLIC MASS ON DECEMBER 3, 2023**

**WHEREAS,** Article 18 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights states that everyone has the right to freedom of thought, conscience and religion; this right includes freedom to change his religion or belief, and freedom, either alone or in community with others and in public or private, to manifest his religion or belief in teaching, practice, worship and observance;

**WHEREAS,** Section 4, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution categorically provides that it is the prime duty of the Government **<u>to serve and protect the people;</u>**

**WHEREAS,** Section 5, Article II of the Constitution states that the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty and property, and the promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy;

**WHEREAS,** Section 5, Article III of the Constitution, under the Bill of Rights, specifically provides that the **<u>free exercise and enjoyment of religious profession and worship, without discrimination or preference, shall forever be allowed;</u>**

**WHEREAS,** Section 5, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall guarantee religious freedom and the free exercise thereof pursuant to the Constitution, national laws, and principles of international law. The Bangsamoro Government shall protect all persons from harassment or any undue pressure, coercion, and violence on account of religion;

**WHEREAS,** a tragic bombing incident took place inside the Dimaporo Gymnasium of the Mindanao State University (MSU) – Main Campus in Marawi City in the morning of December 3, 2023, which resulted to the loss of innocent lives and the infliction of injuries to the victims who were gathered for their Holy Sunday Mass;

**WHEREAS,** this horrendous and senseless act of violence has caused the instant deaths of four (4) individuals alongside wounding forty-five (45) others¹;

**WHEREAS,** the Mindanao State University (MSU) is a renowned educational institution that promotes mutual respect, diversity, inclusivity, and peaceful coexistence among its students, faculty, community members and other stakeholders, regardless of race, culture, and religion;

**WHEREAS,** the MSU-Main Campus in Marawi City has been a home to many Filipino students from across the country who wanted to pursue high quality and excellent education since time immemorial;

**WHEREAS,** this atrocious act of violence by the perpetrators goes against the principles of respect, resilience, harmony and religious freedom, values of which that are fundamental to our society;

**WHEREAS,** this bombing incident has not only caused the tragic loss of innocent lives alongside inflicting physical harm, but has also instilled thoughts of fear, trauma, and the feeling of being vulnerable inside the campus premises among students, faculty members, community members and other stakeholders of MSU;

**WHEREAS,** this needless act also hampers the development and maintenance of peace and order, and affect the overall peace initiatives in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**WHEREAS,** as the interim government of the BARMM, it is the collective responsibility of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament to condemn and denounce such acts of violence; and

**WHEREAS,** it is the BARMM’s cooperative duty to stand united in promoting peace and harmony amongst all, regardless of race, culture, and religion.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Condemning in the Strongest Possible Terms the Heinous Bombing of the Dimaporo Gymnasium in Mindanao State University (MSU) – Main Campus in Marawi City, Lanao del Sur during a Catholic Sunday Mass on December 3, 2023.

Adopted,
