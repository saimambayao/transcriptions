**A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING SYMPATHY AND CONDOLENCES TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY OF THE LATE GRAND MUFTI OF BARMM ABUHURAIRA ABDULRAHMAN UDASAN AND COMMENDING HIS INVALUABLE SERVICES TO THE BANGSAMORO.**

**WHEREAS**, on March 3, 1942, Grand Mufti Abuhuraira Abdulrahman Udasan was born in Kitango village of Dulawan, Cotabato, now Datu Piang, Maguindanao del Sur.

**WHEREAS**, growing up in a religious family, Mufti Udasan memorized many chapters of the Qur’an at the early age of six.

**WHEREAS**, in his commitment to Islamic studies, Mufti Udasan traveled abroad and earned an “Al-Ijazah Al-Aliah” degree from the Faculty of Da’wah and Usuluddin, specializing in comparative religion, at the Islamic University in Medina, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA), from 1967 to 1971, and in 1966, he pursued spiritual studies in Comparative Religion (Christian-Islam) in Jerusalem.

**WHEREAS**, concomitant with his religious and professional accomplishments, Mufti Udasan was fluent in multiple languages such as English, Arabic, Malay, and Tagalog. He was also proficient in various local Moro dialects which include Maguindanaon, Maranao, Iranon, and Sulug (Tausug).

**WHEREAS**, with his desire to share his vast experience and knowledge, Mufti Udasan diligently served in various spiritual and educational missions such as; Director of “Ma’had Al-Rahmaniah”, a high school institution focused on Islamic studies in the Arabic language located in Maganoy, Sharif Aguak, Maguindanao del Sur in 1971-1972; Director of Islamic Da’wah for the United Sabah Islamic Association in Sabah, Malaysia for 15 years; and engagement in Islamic missionary works and dialogues from the year 1974

**WHEREAS**, Mufti Udasan also authored several books during his lifetime relating to the teachings of Islam which included, “Islam In Practice”, “The Wisdom of Jihad”, “The Bible of Qur’an”, “Islamic Dah’wah”, “Allegations Against Islam and How I Refuted Them”, and “Back to Qur’an”.

**WHEREAS**, with his devotion to providing spiritual guidance to the Bangsamoro people in forming their commitment to Jihad fi Sabilillah, the Grand Mufti played an essential role in organizing the top 300 members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF).

**WHEREAS**, Mufti Udasan is also one of the co-founders of the MILF along with the late MILF Founding Chair Salamat Hashim and Sheikh Khalipha Usman Nando, the first Wāli of the Bangsamoro.

**WHEREAS**, Mufti Udasan served as a religious guide for politicians, government employees, and professionals in the Bangsamoro Region where he played an advisory role in matters pertaining to Islamic laws, jurisprudence, and ecclesiastical affairs.

**WHEREAS**, during the surge of the COVID-19 pandemic, the Grand Mufti being the figurehead of the Darul Iftah, made a very decisive and intellectual declaration about the permissibility or acceptability of the COVID-19 vaccines in Islam for being safe and are meant to protect the people from the said virus. He also made a declaration that being vaccinated does not nullify one’s fast. Such declarations eventually led to the enlightenment of many speculations among Bangsamoro people about the COVID-19 vaccination.

**WHEREAS**, the exceptional wisdom and steadfast devotion of Mufti Udasan to the principles of the Islamic faith served as the guiding light to his followers, especially to the Bangsamoro people, in taking the right path by implanting in their hearts the true meaning of Islam.

**WHEREAS**, on July 3, 2023, at the age of 81, Grand Mufti Abuhuraira Abdulrahman Udasan departed as he was summoned and called by our Almighty Creator.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament - Bangsamoro Transition Authority of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in **A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING SYMPATHY AND CONDOLENCES TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY OF THE LATE GRAND MUFTI OF BARMM ABUHURAIRA ABDULRAHMAN UDASAN AND COMMENDING HIS INVALUABLE SERVICES TO THE BANGSAMORO.**
