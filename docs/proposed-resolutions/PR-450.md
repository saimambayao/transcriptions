**A RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO DIRECT ALL THE CONCERNED MINISTRIES AND AGENCIES TO STRICTLY ENFORCE AND MONITOR COMPLIANCE TO REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7394 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE CONSUMER ACT OF THE PHILIPPINES, REPUBLIC ACT NO. 10611 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE FOOD SAFETY ACT OF 2013, AND RA 10817 OR THE PHILIPPINE HALAL EXPORT DEVELOPMENT AND PROMOTION ACT OF 2016**

**WHEREAS**, the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides for the protection and promotion of consumers, under the following articles to wit:

> “The State shall protect Filipino enterprises against unfair foreign competition and trade practices” (Article XII, Section 1);
> 
> “The State shall pursue a trade policy that serves the general welfare and utilizes all forms and arrangements of exchange on the basis of equality and reciprocity” (Article XII, Section 13);
> 
> “The State shall regulate or prohibit monopolies when the public interest so requires. No combinations in restraint of trade or unfair competition shall be allowed” (Article XII, Section 19);
> 
> “The State shall protect consumers from trade malpractices and from substandard or hazardous products” (Article XVI, Section 9);
> 
> “The advertising industry is impressed with public interest, and shall be regulated by law for the protection of consumers and the promotion of the general welfare” [Article XVI, Section 11(2)].

**WHEREAS**, in consonance with the 1987 Philippine Constitution, on September 26, 1997, former President Fidel V. Ramos signed Proclamation No. 1098 which declared the month of October and the year after as the “Consumer Welfare Month” to protect the public against unreasonable price increases or fraudulent practices in the market.

**WHEREAS**, as declared under R.A. No. 7394 or The Consumer Act of the Philippines, it is the policy of the State to protect the interests of the consumers, promote their general welfare, and establish standards of conduct for business and industry. Towards this end, the State shall implement measures to achieve the following objectives:

> i. Protection against hazards to health safety;
> ii. Protection against deceptive, unfair, and unconscionable sales acts and practices;
> iii. provision of information and education to facilitate sound choice and the proper exercise of rights by the consumer;
> iv. provision of adequate rights and means of redress; and
> v. involvement of consumer representatives in the formulation of social and economic policies.

**WHEREAS**, under Section 3 of R.A. 10611, the state adheres to the following objectives:

> i. Protect the public from food-borne and water-borne illnesses and unsanitary, unwholesome, misbranded or adulterated foods;
> ii. Enhance industry and consumer confidence in the food regulatory system; and
> iii. Achieve economic growth and development by promoting fair trade practices and sound regulatory foundation for domestic and international trade.

**WHEREAS**, under Article V, Section 2 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its authority over the following matters without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the Philippines:
> xxx
> (zz) Trade and Industry
> xxx

**WHEREAS**, the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT), Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), Ministry of Health (MOH), Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG), Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), and other agencies associated with the handling, preparation, manufacturing, labeling, and distribution of foods, drugs, cosmetics, and other products or commodities that require quality and safety inspection in relation to trade and industry play a major part in the economic recovery of the region. Its protection towards consumers against unreasonable price increases, product misrepresentation, and other fraudulent practices in the market is a matter that needs the utmost vigilance of the ministries to ensure the well-being and welfare of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, this Proposed Resolution seeks to task the abovementioned ministries to undertake programs and activities to ensure the successful observance of R.A. No. 7394 and R.A. No. 10611. To take measures to comply with the established priorities, standards, and guidelines all for the protection of the consuming public against unreasonable price increases or fraudulent practices of business enterprise and for the promotion, and development of the Philippine Halal Industry in compliance with the standards set by R.A. 10817, respectively.

**WHEREAS**, this undertaking also challenges the ministries to advance and strengthen the implementation of the halal industry, allotting equal importance to the proper labeling of food, drugs, and other consumables conveying information on the product’s identity, contents, and other important data to protect consumer’s health in terms of food safety and nutrition.

**WHEREAS**, the ministries are requested to critically inspect the online sellers and retailers within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region ensuring their capabilities to facilitate online transactions and protect the consumers to purchase in a safe and secure manner, guarding as well the safety of customer’s data against breaches.

**WHEREAS**, whenever applicable, all products available within BARMM must comply with the Philippine Standard (PS) Quality and/or Safety Mark and Import Commodity Clearance (ICC) Sticker to guide consumers and assure them that they are purchasing certified quality and safe products that conform to applicable Philippine National Standards. These products pertain, but are not limited to the following:

> i. Household Appliances
> ii. Consumer Electronics
> iii. Lighting and Wiring Devices
> iv. Steel Products
> v. Plastic Pipes and Ceramic Products
> vi. Cement and other Construction Materials
> vii. Chemical Products
> viii. Automotive Related Products, and
> ix. Other consumer products

**WHEREAS**, the ministries shall also guarantee that beauty products such as but not limited to makeup, cosmetics, skincare, fragrance, hair, and beauty products available and subject for sale within Bangsamoro Autonomous Region are free of additives specifically referred to as “haram” for consumption or usage.

**WHEREAS**, all items available and circulating within Bangsamoro Autonomous Region must be provided with English translation in labeling and packaging of the product’s functions, ingredients, and warnings for safety information.

**WHEREAS**, this proposed resolution is to enhance the confidence of the consumers, promote the well-being, and welfare of the Bangsamoro people in the marketplace, and ultimately contribute to the economic development and stability of the Bangsamoro Region;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in **A RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO DIRECT ALL THE CONCERNED MINISTRIES AND AGENCIES TO STRICTLY ENFORCE AND MONITOR COMPLIANCE TO REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7394 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE CONSUMER ACT OF THE PHILIPPINES, REPUBLIC ACT NO. 10611 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE FOOD SAFETY ACT OF 2013, AND RA 10817 OR THE PHILIPPINE HALAL EXPORT DEVELOPMENT AND PROMOTION ACT OF 2016.**
