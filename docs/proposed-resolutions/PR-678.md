**RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE CHIEF MINISTER TO CREATE AN ADDITIONAL MINISTER POSITION WITHIN THE OFFICE OF THE CHIEF MINISTER (OCM) TO CHAMPION AND OVERSEE IRRIGATION SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and the promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy;

**WHEREAS**, Section 9 of the same Article provides that the State shall promote a just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the nation and free the people from poverty through policies that provide adequate social services, promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality of life for all;

**WHEREAS**, Section 21 of the said Article states that the State shall promote comprehensive rural development and agrarian reform;

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article XIII of the Constitution provides that the State shall recognize the right of farmers, farmworkers, and landowners, as well as cooperatives and other independent farmers’ organizations to participate in planning, organization, and management of the program, and **shall provide support to agriculture** through appropriate technology and research, and adequate financial, production, marketing, and other support services;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2 of Republic Act No. 10969 or the Free Irrigation Service Act provides that the State shall promote comprehensive rural development, food self-sufficiency, equitable access to opportunities and sustained productivity as key strategies to raise the quality of life in rural areas and attain overall national development;

**WHEREAS**, the same provision states that the State shall ensure that vital support services are made available, particularly irrigation services, through the continued construction, repair, and maintenance of necessary irrigation facilities, to increase production of agricultural crops, encourage productivity, and increase the incomes of farmers;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) specifically provides that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in the BOL;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article V of the BOL provides that subject to Section 20, Article X of the Constitution, the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its authority without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the Philippines over matters including agriculture, food security, inland waters, public works, infrastructure water supply and services, flood control, and irrigation systems;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, Section 37, Article XIII of the BOL provides that with regard to water supply and services, flood control and irrigation systems that connect or from facilities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, there shall be cooperation and coordination between the Bangsamoro Government and the appropriate national or local government bodies;

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Chapter 1, Title I, Book VI of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure resiliency and productivity for food security, income and rural employment in an enabling environment for farmers, fisherfolks, and agrarian reform beneficiaries;

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Chapter 2, Title IV, Book III of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code provides that the Chief Minister shall also appoint such other ministers and/or officers as may be necessary to perform the functions of government;

**WHEREAS**, maintaining peace, supported by increasing farm profitability, diversifying income sources, enhancing access to basic social services, and upgrading infrastructure, will accelerate poverty reduction in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)¹;

**WHEREAS**, agriculture is the primary income source for the majority of BARMM’s population, and sustainable poverty reduction will require enhancing agricultural productivity and raising farmers’ incomes. Diversifying economic activity to create quality jobs is also essential to improving the region’s economic prospects²;

**WHEREAS**, an efficient and effective irrigation system in the BARMM is necessary to improve agricultural productivity, to ensure food security, and to enhance the livelihoods of the farmers in the region;

**WHEREAS**, it is vital for the Bangsamoro Government to have a focused direction, and innovative solutions to address irrigation gaps and to work directly on irrigation system development and management in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, the creation of a Minister for Irrigation System Development (MISD) within the Office of the Chief Minister would provide a focused framework for synchronizing the efforts and coordination among various stakeholders, including government agencies, non-government organizations, civil society organizations, and international partners, in irrigation system development and management in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, it is crucial to institute a clear mandate and dedicated people to strengthen the Bangsamoro Government’s capacity to address all irrigation concerns and gaps, thereby ensuring food security in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, the MISD shall serve as the overall in-charge of irrigation concerns for both nationally and BARMM-funded programs and projects, ensuring alignment with national and international frameworks and fostering collective efforts among all stakeholders;

**WHEREAS**, the Minister of ISD shall perform the following functions:

a) Lead in the development of comprehensive irrigation policies, strategies, programs and projects in the BARMM;

b) Coordinate with the National Irrigation Administration (NIA) regarding the construction and maintenance of nationally-funded irrigation systems and those that connect to or from facilities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region³;

c) Lead in the crafting, updating of, and coordinate the implementation of the Irrigation System Development and Management Plan (ISDMP) in coordination with relevant stakeholders;

d) Lead the formulation of a roadmap to further develop the irrigation systems of the BARMM⁴;

e) Coordinate with relevant local government units, government agencies and instrumentalities, and other stakeholders to improve irrigation system development and infrastructure;

f) Advocate for the allocation of funding and resources for irrigation projects from national and international sources to ensure the sustainability of irrigation programs;

g) Monitor and evaluate the performance and impact of irrigation projects to ensure they meet the set objectives and provide feedback for improvement;

h) Promote the adoption of innovative and environmentally sustainable irrigation technologies to enhance water use efficiency and productivity;

i) Foster partnerships with academic and research institutions to encourage research and development in the field of irrigation and water resource management; and

j) Perform such other functions and tasks as may be assigned by the Chief Minister.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Chief Minister to create an additional minister position within the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) to champion and oversee Irrigation System Development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister for immediate and appropriate action.
