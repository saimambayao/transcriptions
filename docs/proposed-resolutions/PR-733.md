**RESOLUTION PRESCRIBING THE ACCOUNTING AND LIQUIDATION OF THE RESPECTIVE BUDGETARY ALLOCATIONS OF EACH MEMBER OF THE PARLIAMENT AS CLASSIFIED AND CONSISTENT WITH APPLICABLE ACCOUNTING AND AUDITING RULES**

**WHEREAS**, Section 2 of Article 5 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that the Commission on Audit shall be the exclusive auditor of Bangsamoro Government and its constituent local government units. The Bangsamoro Government shall establish an auditing body which shall have internal auditing responsibility in accordance with Republic Act No. 3456, otherwise known as the "Internal Auditing Act of 1992," as amended. The Bangsamoro Government shall implement transparency and accountability mechanism consistent with open government practices and generally accepted financial management principles;

**WHEREAS**, Item 2 of the Special Provisions of the Approved Budget of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) under the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro (GAAB) appropriated for the current year shall also be effective in the coming years for Constituency Servicing Program (CSP) per Member of the Parliament and shall be released upon their written request to the Finance Services;

**WHEREAS**, the same GAAB also approved an appropriation under the BTA for Financial Assistance/Subsidy (FAS);

**WHEREAS**, there is no current guidelines on the Utilization and Liquidation of the CSP and FAS, the BTA deems it appropriate to formalize a guideline on the utilization and liquidation of the CSP and FAS of the Members of the Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, the Members of the Parliament are fully cognizant of their duty and responsibility to account to the public for the use or expenditure of public funds appropriated under their respective budgetary allocations under the GAAB and have faithfully abided by the established auditing rules and regulations applicable to the legislature;

**WHEREAS**, in recognition of the delicate and unique nature of the functions, operational and organizational structures of the BTA, and in keeping with the nature of the performance by the Members of the Parliament and their respective committees of their legislative, oversight, and constituent functions and responsibilities:

**NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the BTA to adopt the following policies in the Accounting and Liquidation of CSP and FAS per Member of the Parliament:

**1.** The liquidation of the respective budgetary allocations for CSP and FAS of each Member of the Parliament as classified and consistent with applicable accounting and auditing rules and regulations;

**2.** The liquidation of the amount received by each Member of the Parliament shall be through Liquidation by way of Certification;

**3.** The Office of the Speaker of the Parliament shall issue policy/guidelines, including the forms necessary for the liquidation of the CSP and FAS; and

**4.** Each Member of the Parliament shall, in all cases, secure relevant documentary evidence of payments/disbursement of their CSP and FAS, which must be readily available for inspection and audit of the Commission on Audit if necessity arises.

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that this measure shall be effective unless otherwise revoked by the Honorable members of the Parliament.
