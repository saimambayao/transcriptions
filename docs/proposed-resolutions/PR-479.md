**RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT (MP) ATTY. SHA ELIJAH DUMAMA-ALBA FOR BEING DESIGNATED AS THE NEW MINISTER OF THE MINISTRY OF INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT, AND OTHER MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT WHO WERE DESIGNATED AND RE-DESIGNATED AS MEMBERS OF THE CABINET**

WHEREAS, on July 26, 2018, President Rodrigo Roa Duterte signed into law a landmark legislation, Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)”;

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social and cultural development as provided for in the Organic Law;

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

WHEREAS, Section 4, Article VII of the BOL states that the executive function and authority shall be exercised by the Cabinet, which shall be headed by a Chief Minister. The Chief Minister shall be elected by majority vote of all the members of the Parliament;

WHEREAS, the same section further provides that the Chief Minister shall nominate two (2) Deputy Chief Ministers who shall be elected by the Parliament, as provided for in Section 35 of Article VII and appoint the members of the Cabinet, majority of whom shall come from the Parliament;

WHEREAS, the Chief Minister issued Memorandum Circular No. 189 s. 2023 directing all senior officials to tender their courtesy resignation in order for him to have a free hand to reorganize his cabinet to further improve the service delivery in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

WHEREAS, Chief Minister Ahod B. Ebrahim designated Member of Parliament Atty. Sha Elijah Dumama-Alba as the new minister of the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG)¹, replacing former Minister Naguib Sinarimbo;

WHEREAS, MP Atty Dumama-Alba earned her Bachelor of Arts in Public Administration at UP Diliman and finished her Law degree at San Beda Mendiola in 2007. She was thereafter admitted to the Philippine Bar in 2008²;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba is the Asia Foundation Development Fellow alumna. She placed 3rd in the Special Shariah Bar Examinations in 2018. She was also part of the 2019 Obama Leaders Class in Asia, Tatler Asia’s Gen.T List 2020 Leaders of Tomorrow, alongside being an Asia Peace Innovators Fellow 2020 and a United States International Visitors Leadership Program grantee in 2021³;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba was a member of the Coordinating Team for Transition (CT4T) representing the Moro Islamic Liberation Front, which drafted the Transition Plan from ARMM to BARMM from 2018 to 2019⁴;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba served as Attorney VI of the Civil Service Commission – ARMM before being appointed as Bangsamoro Attorney General in 2019 and Member of Parliament in 2022⁵;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba is a member of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), the interim government in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao during the transition period. She serves as the Floor Leader of the Bangsamoro Parliament. As the Floor Leader, she is an ex-officio member of all other Bangsamoro Parliament committees⁶;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba is the Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Parliament’s Committee on Rules and the Vice-Chairperson of Statutory Committee on Bangsamoro Justice System⁷;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba was the Bangsamoro Attorney General of BARMM since its creation in March 2019. As the Bangsamoro Attorney General, she had been designated as Focal for Justice Programs in BARMM. Her office has spearheaded initiatives in improving and strengthening the Shari’ah as a Justice System, as well as promoting the Tri-Justice Systems in BARMM⁸;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Dumama-Alba is also the head of the Secretariat of the Intergovernmental Relations Body (IGRB), a mechanism for cooperation and coordination between the National Government and Bangsamoro Government⁹;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Alba assumed leadership on one of the biggest ministries of the Bangsamoro Government, the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government on December 7, 2023¹⁰;

WHEREAS, the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG) is responsible in strengthening the governance capabilities of LGUs. The ministry deals with the LGUs within the jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Government and shall retain supervisory functions on police matters within LGUs;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, as the interim government of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), extends its commendations and salutations to the newly appointed Minister of the MILG;

WHEREAS, MP Atty. Anna Tarhata S. Basman was designated as the new Attorney-General replacing former Attorney-General Atty. Sha Elijah B. Dumama-Alba;

WHEREAS, MP Dr. Susana S. Anayatin was designated as the new Executive Director of the Office of the Settler Communities replacing former Executive Director Prof. Edgar Ramirez;

WHEREAS, MP Said M. Sheik was designated as the new Assistant Cabinet Secretary and Program Manager of the Marawi Rehabilitation Program replacing former Assistant Cabinet Secretary MP Atty. Anna Tarhata S. Basman;

WHEREAS, the Chief Minister also re-designated some Members of Parliament to include minister Mohagher M. Iqbal¹¹ of the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE), minister Ar. Eduard U. Guerra¹² of the Ministry of Public Works (MPW), minister Atty. Paisalin P. Tago, CPA¹³ of the Ministry of Transportation and Communication (MOTC), minister Engr. Aida T. Silongan¹⁴ of the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), and minister Hussein P. Munoz of Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS)¹⁵; and

WHEREAS, the BTA hopes for the new minister and the re-designated ministers to continue steering their respective ministry to greater heights.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution commending and congratulating MP Atty. Sha Elijah Dumama-Alba for being designated as the new minister of the MILG, and other MPs who were designated and redesignated as members of the Cabinet.

**RESOLVED FINALLY** that copy of this Resolution be furnished to the minister of MILG and other honorable MPs mentioned in this Resolution.

Adopted,

(Signed)
**ENGR. BAINTAN ADIL-AMPATUAN, MNSA**
