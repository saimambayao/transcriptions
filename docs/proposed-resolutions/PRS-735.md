**A RESOLUTION URGING ALL MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES WITHIN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION TO STRICTLY IMPLEMENT THE PRESCRIBED REVISED DRESS CODE FOR GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS AND EMPLOYEES**

**WHEREAS**, the Civil Service Commission (CSC) has prescribed a revised dress code for government officials and employees through CSC Resolution No. 24000766 promulgated on 29 August 2024;

**WHEREAS**, the same Resolution outlines specific attire requirements and prohibited items, aiming to promote a professional image and boost employee morale;

**WHEREAS**, under the Revised Dress Code, government officials and employees must wear ASEAN-inspired attire or clothing influenced by the traditional garments of ASEAN member states, including the Philippines every First Monday of the Month; Filipiniana-inspired outfits every Second to Fourth Monday, which reflects Filipino culture, heritage, and customs; and agency-prescribed office uniforms;

**WHEREAS**, the agency-prescribed uniform shall be worn from Tuesday to Friday (Weekend) which must be appropriate to the to the workplace and/or duties of the official or employee;

**WHEREAS**, on days without agency-prescribed office uniform, an official or employee must wear smart casual that is well-fitting, neat, and appropriate pieces that are slightly less formal than a business casual or business professional dress code.

**WHEREAS**, Anti-Red Tape Act (ARTA) Identification Card (ID) forms part of the agency-prescribed office uniform and so the officials and employees shall wear their IDs during office hours or official duty;

**WHEREAS**, under the same CSC Resolution, any violation of the Revised Dress Code shall be considered as ground for disciplinary action under civil service laws, rules and regulations.

**WHEREAS**, ensuring the strict and consistent implementation of this prescribed revised dress code is essential to achieve its intended objectives and to project a credible and respectable image of the Bangsamoro Government;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby **RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled, urging all Ministries, Offices, and Agencies within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to strictly implement the prescribed revised dress code for government officials and employees.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that copies of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, and all the other concerned Ministries, Offices, and Agencies within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region for their information, guidance, and immediate and appropriate action.
