**PROPOSED RESOLUTION 173**

INTRODUCED BY:

**Author: MP DR. SUSANA S. ANAYATIN**

**A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING SYMPHATY AND CONDOLENCES TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY ON THE DEMISE OF THE LATE SHEIKH KHALIPHA USMAN NANDO, RECOGNIZING AND HONORING HIS CONTRIBUTION AS FIRST WALI OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT.**

**WHEREAS,** on August 24, 1959, Sheikh Khalipha Usman Nando, at the age of 17, was one of the early Moro students sent for a 2nd batch on a diplomatic scholarship program of Al-azhar University in Cairo, Egypt.

**WHEREAS,** Sheikh Nando was one of the co-founders of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) along with Salamat Hashim and Abu Huraura.

**WHEREAS,** as part of the MILF, he served under various capacities including as Chairman of the Committee on Education of the MILF Central Committee for 16 years and Chief Justice of the MILF Supreme Shari’ah Court for 14 years. He was also a member of the Ceasefire Committee of the Eastern Cotabato Region and chaired the Bangsamoro Consultative Assembly for two years.

**WHEREAS,** Sheik Nando had been unconditionally serving the Bangsamoro cause for the right to self-determination in various capacity for sixty (60) years.

**WHEREAS,** on March 29, 2019, pursuant to Section 1, Article VIII of Republic Act No. 11054, the Interim Parliament of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) through its BTA Resolution No. 1, appointed Sheikh Nando as the first Wali of the duly constituted Bangsamoro autonomous region. He likewise opened the inaugural session of the second interim parliament on September 15, 2022.

**WHEREAS,** on February 5, 2023, Sheikh Khalipha Usman Nando joined as he was summoned and called by our Almighty Creator.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in **A RESOLUTION EXPRESSING SYMPHATY AND CONDOLENCES TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY ON THE DEMISE OF THE LATE SHEIKH KHALIPHA USMAN NANDO, RECOGNIZING AND HONORING HIS CONTRIBUTION AS FIRST WALI OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT.**

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to his bereaved family.

Adopted, February ____, 2023.
