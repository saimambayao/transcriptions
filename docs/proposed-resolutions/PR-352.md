**RESOLUTION**
**CONDEMNING THE BURNING OF COPIES OF THE HOLY QUR’AN IN SWEDEN DURING EID AL-ADHA**

**WHEREAS,** on June 28, 2023, a Holy Qur’an was burned outside the Stockholm Central Mosque, coinciding with the celebration of Eid al-Adha in the Muslim world.

**WHEREAS,** the act of desecrating the Holy Qur’an is widely regarded as a provocative act that deeply offends and undermines the faith of Muslims;

**WHEREAS,** European Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms, and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights have consistently upheld the fundamental principles of freedom of religion, expression, and belief;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) expresses its profound concern and dismay over the recent burning of the Holy Qur’an, recognizing the potential of such an act to incite hatred, extremism and violence globally;

**WHEREAS,** the BTA recognized and affirmed the principles of tolerance, respect, and non-discrimination as cornerstones of a just and harmonious society;

**WHEREAS,** BTA call upon the Swedish authorities to thoroughly investigate the incident, bring the perpetrators to justice, and ensure the implementation of adequate measures to prevent future occurrences of religiously motivated hate crimes;

**WHEREAS,** Swedish should proscribe anti-Islamic actions under the pretext of freedom of expression;

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it resolved, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to strongly condemn the burning of the Holy Qur’an in Sweden;

**RESOLVE FURTHER,** that this resolution is communicated to the Swedish authorities to emphasize the urgent need for a comprehensive response to this incident and the prevention of similar acts in the future.
