**A RESOLUTION URGING ALL MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES UNDER THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT TO APPRISE THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY ON THE MANNER OF RELEASE OF MAINTENANCE AND OTHER OPERATING EXPENSES FROM THE REGIONAL OFFICE TO THE LOWEST OPERATING UNITS, AS WELL AS TO SUBMIT A LIST OF THEIR UNPAID SUPPLIERS AND CONTRACTORS**

**WHEREAS**, Section 28, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution declares that:

> “*Section 28*. Subject to reasonable conditions prescribed by law, the State adopts and implements a policy of full public disclosure of all its transactions involving public interest,”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article XII of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), mandates that:

> “*Section 2*. X x x. The Bangsamoro Government shall implement transparency and accountability mechanism consistent with open government practices and generally accepted financial management principles.”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 28, Article VII of the BOL provides that:

> “*Section 28*. Budget. — The form, content, and manner of preparation of the Bangsamoro budget shall be prescribed by law enacted by the Parliament, consistent with existing laws, rules and regulations of the National Government. X x x”

**WHEREAS**, in line with the directive of the Chief Minister to address issues relating to the non-payment of suppliers and contractors, there is a need to ascertain the extent of outstanding obligations and ensure that ministries, offices, and agencies are complying with fiscal and contractual responsibilities;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, in the exercise of its oversight function, requires timely, accurate, and verifiable reports to assess the flow of Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses (MOOE) from the regional offices to the lowest operating units and to monitor any delays in financial obligations;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, to urge all ministries, offices, and agencies from the regional office to the lowest operating units under the Bangsamoro Government to:

1. Submit a Systematic MOOE Release and Payment Status Report, structured exactly as follows:

    a. Cover Page
        i. Name of Ministry/Office/Agency
        ii. Period Covered (from 1st Quarter of 2024 to First Quarter of 2025)
        iii. Date of Submission
        iv. Responsible Officer (Name, Position, Contact No.)

    b. Part I – MOOE Release Schedule Matrix (in tabular form):
        i. Date of Release
        ii. Amount Released
        iii. Mode of Disbursement (cash, check, bank transfer)
        iv. Receiving Unit
        v. Reference Document No.

    c. Part II – Release Justifications
        o For each entry in the Matrix, provide:
            1. Legal/Policy Basis (e.g., BOL Section, Office Circular)
            2. Operational Rationale (why funds were released on that date/amount)
            3. Any Deviations from standard schedule, with reasons

    d. Part III – Internal Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
        o A one-paragraph summary of your office’s SOP for MOOE releases, including approval levels and processing timelines.

2. Furnish a Certified List of Unpaid Suppliers and Contractors, indicating for each:
    a. Name of Payee
    b. Amount Owed
    c. Nature of Goods/Services
    d. Reason for Non-Payment
    e. Projected Date of Settlement

3. Attach supporting documents (Annexes): Scanned copies of Memoranda of Release, Disbursement Vouchers, and any relevant Office Orders or Memos cited above, each clearly bookmarked or tabbed.

4. Transmit the Entire Report Package—the Cover Page, Parts I–III, the Certified List of Unpaid Suppliers, and Annexes—in both printed (2 copies) and digital (PDF/A and editable spreadsheet for the Matrix) formats within thirty (30) days from receipt of this Resolution.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the Parliament’s Committee on Finance, Budget, and Management, in coordination with the Commission on Audit – Bangsamoro Autonomous Region (COA-BARMM), shall review the submitted reports for compliance and recommend appropriate actions for any discrepancies, omissions, or delays in financial obligations;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that non-compliance with this Resolution without valid justification shall be referred to the Bangsamoro Parliament’s Committee on Ethics and Privileges for appropriate disciplinary action in accordance with internal rules and laws;

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, that copies of this Resolution be furnished to the Chief Minister and all concerned ministries, offices, and agencies for their immediate and appropriate compliance.

ADOPTED.
