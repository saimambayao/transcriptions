**A RESOLUTION URGING THE COMMITTEE ON ENVIRONMENT AND NATURAL RESOURCES (CENRE) TO INVESTIGATE, IN AID OF LEGISLATION, COMPLAINTS REGARDING BILLING AND CHARGES IMPOSED BY COTABATO LIGHT AND POWER COMPANY ON CONSUMERS**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, as the legislative body of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), is mandated to uphold the welfare and protect the rights of its constituents through effective legislation and oversight;

**WHEREAS**, numerous reports and complaints have been brought to the attention of this Parliament by consumers of Cotabato Light and Power Company concerning alleged erroneous billing practices, irregular meter readings, unjustified charges, and a general lack of transparency in the computation and imposition of electricity bills;

**WHEREAS**, these persistent complaints have caused significant financial burden and distress to the households and businesses within the Bangsamoro, undermining public trust in essential utility services;

**WHEREAS**, Article VII, Section 5 of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), empowers the Bangsamoro Parliament to enact necessary laws to promote, protect and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, it is incumbent upon the Bangsamoro Parliament to exercise its legislative oversight function and the inherent power of inquiry to ascertain the veracity of these complaints, identify systemic issues, and formulate appropriate legislative remedies to ensure consumer protection and accountability from public utility providers operating within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, to respectfully urge the Committee on Environment and Natural Resources (CENRE) to conduct a thorough investigation, in aid of legislation, into the widespread complaints regarding the billing and charges imposed by Cotabato Light and Power Company on its consumers within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Committee on Environment and Natural Resources be tasked to determine the extent and nature of the issues, including but not limited to, meter reading accuracy, billing transparency, rate computation, and customer service responsiveness of Cotabato Light and Power Company, and to recommend immediate and long-term legislative measures, policies, or actions to address these concerns and safeguard the rights and interests of Bangsamoro consumers;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that this resolution be communicated to the Cotabato Light and Power Company, the City Government of Cotabato and other relevant agencies for their immediate and appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, this _ day of __________, 2025, in the Bangsamoro Parliament.
