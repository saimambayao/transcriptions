**A RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE INTERGOVERNMENTAL RELATIONS BODY TO ELUCIDATE THE FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS OF THE RECENT SUPREME COURT’S RULING EXCLUDING SULU PROVINCE FROM BARMM IN THE CONTEXT OF THE BANGSAMORO DEVELOPMENT PLAN**

WHEREAS, the Supreme Court of the Philippines recently ruled that Sulu Province is not part of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)¹;

WHEREAS, this ruling has significant implications for the governance and administrative framework of the BARMM, particularly regarding budgetary allocations and development planning;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) serves as a critical framework for the economic and social development of the region, including financial planning for various projects and initiatives;

WHEREAS, the exclusion of Sulu Province from BARMM raises questions about the allocation of resources and funding within the BDP, which may affect ongoing and future development projects in the region;

WHEREAS, the employees of Sulu Province may face uncertainty regarding their employment status, job security, and access to benefits that are typically provided within the BARMM framework;

WHEREAS, clarity on the financial implications of this ruling is essential for effective governance and the equitable distribution of resources among the provinces within BARMM;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, to request the Intergovernmental Relations Body to:

1. **Elucidate the financial implications of the Supreme Court's ruling regarding Sulu Province's status and how it affects the overall budget of BARMM.**

2. **Assess the impact on existing and planned projects within the Bangsamoro Development Plan and provide recommendations for addressing any potential shortfalls in funding.**

3. **Evaluate the employment status of affected employees in Sulu Province and outline potential pathways for their job security and integration into the current administrative structure.**

4. **Provide a comprehensive report detailing how the ruling may alter budget allocations, including potential impacts on local government units, social services, and infrastructure projects in BARMM.**

5. **Engage relevant stakeholders to gather insights and feedback on the ruling's implications, ensuring that the voices of affected communities, including employees, are considered in the analysis.**

**RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this resolution be furnished to the Intergovernmental Relations Body, the Office of the Chief Minister, and other relevant authorities for their information and guidance.
