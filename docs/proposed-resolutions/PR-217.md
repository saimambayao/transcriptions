**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT THROUGH THE MINISTRY OF TRANSPORTATION AND COMMUNICATION (MOTC), MINISTRY OF TRADE, INVESTMENTS AND TOURISM (MTIT) AND THE BANGSAMORO BOARD OF INVESTMENTS (BBOI) TO PROMOTE, ENCOURAGE, AND INCENTIVIZE INVESTMENTS IN THE MARITIME TRANSPORTATION AND LOGISTICS OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION ESPECIALLY IN THE ISLAND PROVINCES OF THE REGION**

WHEREAS, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution categorically states that the protection of life, liberty and property, and the promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of a democracy¹;

WHEREAS, Section 20, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that the State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides incentives to needed investments²;

WHEREAS, Section 38, Article XIII of Republic Act (RA) No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall have the authority to grant regional franchises, licenses, and permits to land, sea, and air transportation plying routes in the provinces or cities within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region; provided, that the National Government shall regulate the airside operations of all existing airports³;

WHEREAS, Section 4, Chapter II of Republic Act (RA) No. 9295 or the Domestic Shipping Development Act of 2004 enumerates investment incentives which shall be granted to qualified domestic ship operators to insure the continued viability of domestic shipping, and to encourage investments in the domestic shipping industry⁴;

WHEREAS, Section 8, Chapter II of R.A. No. 9295 provides that in order to encourage investments in the domestic shipping industry by existing domestic ship operators and attract new investment from new operators and investors, domestic ship operators are hereby authorized to establish their own domestic shipping rates: Provided, that effective competition is fostered, and public interest is served⁵;

WHEREAS, the Ministry of Transportation and Communication (MOTC) is mandated to be the primary policy, planning, programming, coordinating, implementing, regulating, and administrative entity of the Bangsamoro Government in the promotion, development, and regulation of dependable and coordinated networks of transportation and communications systems, as well as fast, safe, efficient and reliable transportation and communication services⁶;

WHEREAS, the Ministry of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT) is the primary government agency mandated to implement laws, programs, and projects on trade, investments and tourism in the Bangsamoro⁷;

WHEREAS, MTIT aims to promote and facilitate the increase of investments in trade and industries, and tourism in the region;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI) is the body which generates, approves, and promotes investments coming in the region and the incentive giving body that approves the incentives of huge entrepreneurs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region⁸;

WHEREAS, sea transport is defined as the movement of goods or people over large bodies of water. This method of transport has become the most common way to send cargo or shipment across vast distances⁹;

WHEREAS, the critical role of maritime transport in ensuring an integrated and coordinated transport network is foundational for an archipelagic country like the Philippines¹⁰;

WHEREAS, the Philippines relies on the maritime industry as a vital component in attaining inclusive growth and socio-economic progress.

WHEREAS, Shipping remains the major infrastructure by which islands are linked¹¹;

WHEREAS, ensuring the safety of our passengers is of crucial importance. Our citizens rely on the safety and efficiency of our transportation sector in their daily lives, since the country is an archipelago; and

WHEREAS, to encourage and promote investments from private sectors to the maritime transport of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, the Bangsamoro Government through MOTC, MTIT and BBOI, should come up with an incentive and coordinated program which will attract other shipping lines to operate in the ports of the region and serve the Bangsamoro people.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Bangsamoro Government through the Ministry of Transportation and Communication (MOTC); Ministry of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT) and the Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI) to promote, encourage, and incentivize investments in the maritime transportation and logistics of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region especially in the island provinces of the region, for consideration and approval of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, that copy of this Resolution be furnished to the MOTC, MTIT, and BBOI for information and appropriate action.
