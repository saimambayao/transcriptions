**A RESOLUTION CONDEMNING IN THE STRONGEST POSSIBLE SENSE THE TRAGIC DEATH OF EZELL A. TAN AND REQUESTING THE APPROPRIATE AGENCIES OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT TO EXPEDITE THE CONDUCT OF THEIR INVESTIGATION IN ORDER TO SERVE JUSTICE TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY OF THE DECEASED.**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that, “the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessing of democracy.”

**WHEREAS**, on July 20, 2023, at twilight, a fisherman came across a floating cadaver along the coast of Brgy. Molimoc, Balabagan, Lanao del Sur. The fisherman allegedly reported the matter to the Barangay officials and the latter then referred the incident to the Municipal Police Station.

**WHEREAS**, according to Balabagan, Lanao del Sur Municipal Chief of Police, Police Major (P/MAJ) Michael Yap, the lifeless body was determined to be Ezell A. Tan, after being confirmed by the latter’s family.

**WHEREAS**, P/MAJ Yap believes that the deceased Ezell has been brutally abused before his life was taken as there were marks in his head and body that were swollen and other indications of possible torture.

**WHEREAS**, according to Cotabato City Police Station 2, under the leadership of Station Commander, P/MAJ John Vincent F. Bravo, Ezell A. Tan has been reported missing since July 18, together with the one (1) multi-purpose vehicle (MPV) Toyota Avanza with license plate LGX 113 which was allegedly owned by the deceased Ezell.

**WHEREAS**, Ezell A. Tan is a male 34-year-old resident of Rosary Heights V, Cotabato City, a Philippine Navy Reservist, and is currently employed at Commission on Population and Development XII as Accountant II.

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro government is committed to protect its constituents against threat to security and safety that will hinder the progressive development and maintenance of peace and order in the region. Additionally, the act made by the perpetrators behind Ezell A. Tan’s death is unlawful and against human rights which the Philippines strongly condemns.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament – Bangsamoro Transition Authority of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao **A RESOLUTION CONDEMNING IN THE STRONGEST POSSIBLE SENSE THE TRAGIC DEATH OF EZELL A. TAN AND REQUESTING THE APPROPRIATE AGENCIES OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT TO EXPEDITE THE CONDUCT OF THEIR INVESTIGATION IN ORDER TO SERVE JUSTICE TO THE BEREAVED FAMILY OF THE DECEASED.**
