**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE CHIEF MINISTER TO CREATE AN ADDITIONAL MINISTER POSITION WITHIN THE OFFICE OF THE CHIEF MINISTER (OCM) TO CHAMPION AND OVERSEE THE HALAL INDUSTRY DEVELOPMENT IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and **promotion of the general welfare** are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2 of the Republic Act No. 10817, or the Act Instituting the Philippine Halal Export Development and Promotion Program Creating for the Purpose the Philippine Halal Export Development and Promotion Board, and for Other Purposes, declares that it is the policy of the State to **develop and promote Halal industries as a mode of achieving equity and justice** among our farmers and producers, and increasing employment opportunities for the domestic labor force;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article IV of the Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), states that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in this Organic Law;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the aforementioned law provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the **preservation and enrichment of culture**, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

**WHEREAS**, Section 30, Article XIII of the same law states that the Parliament shall enact laws to further strengthen its policy and programs on halal development;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Title XIV of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 13, or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code mandates the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT) to coordinate with the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), and other concerned agencies in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) in the development and promotion of Halal industry;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Title XII of the same code mandates the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) to contribute to the development and quality standards including Halal for food and non-food industry through testing, analysis, and capacity-building;

**WHEREAS**, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) Briefing highlights that the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) has the potential to become a Halal hub in the Philippines due to its large Muslim population and its strategic location in the southern part of the Philippines, near the significant Muslim markets of Indonesia and Malaysia;

**WHEREAS**, furthermore, the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) has intensified its efforts to strengthen the Halal food industry within the Bangsamoro Region as part of its Halal Program and Development;

**WHEREAS**, the Halal Industry Development initiative aims to position the country as a supplier of Halal products, which are items that comply with Islamic or Shariah law;

**WHEREAS**, according to the People's Budget - Bangsamoro Halal Ecosystem Program, the Development Advisory Group Philippines reports that the Halal market remains a fledgling endeavor. It has yet to develop fully into an industry, existing instead as a small niche with few participants and a vast unserved market;

**WHEREAS**, the Department of Science and Technology's Food Sector Halal Roadmap (2022-2028) highlights that the significance of the Halal industry is not yet widely understood, both locally and globally;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) has significant potential to become a global Halal industry powerhouse due to its strategic location and strong ties with neighboring countries, particularly within the Brunei Darussalam-Indonesia-Malaysia-Philippines East ASEAN Growth Area (BIMP-EAGA) sub-region;

**WHEREAS**, a significant number of Muslims remain unaware of the importance of complying with Islamic laws in the production and consumption of goods and services;

**WHEREAS**, the expansion of the Halal business is a key approach to ensuring the general well-being and future of the Bangsamoro, providing assurance to the region's Muslim residents that the food they consume is safe and compliant with Shariah law;

**WHEREAS**, implementing activities to develop the Halal food industry can also promote harmony and enhance appreciation and understanding of Muslim culture;

**WHEREAS**, it is necessary for the Bangsamoro Government to appoint a Minister to facilitate the effective and efficient development of the Halal industry within the Bangsamoro region;

**WHEREAS**, the establishment of a Minister for Halal Industry under the Office of the Chief Minister would create a more focused and targeted framework for coordinating the efforts of various government agencies, non-governmental organizations, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders, both domestically and internationally, in the development of the Halal industry;

**WHEREAS**, furthermore, appointing a Minister with a dedicated and focused role in Halal industry development will better equip the Bangsamoro Government to devise innovative strategies to effectively promote, foster, and expand the Halal industry;


**WHEREAS**, the Minister of Halal Industry will serve as the overall leader for Halal industry development, ensuring that all relevant government agencies, offices, instrumentalities, and various stakeholders, both local and international, work collectively to effectively and efficiently promote and develop the Halal industry;

**WHEREAS**, the Minister of Halal Industry shall be under the Office of the Chief Minister and shall perform the following functions:

a) Be overall responsible for the Bangsamoro region's Halal industry development;

b) In coordination with concerned ministries, offices, and agencies, lead the formulation of the Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Plan, serving as a guide to strengthen the region's comparative and competitive advantages through the utilization of abundant local resources and access to external markets;

c) Ensure that the plans, policies, and programs in the Halal Industry Development Plan are effectively implemented by relevant stakeholders;

d) Coordinate and collaborate with local and national government agencies, offices, and instrumentalities, as well as international partners, to grow the Halal industry;

e) Supervise the implementation of initiatives and plans related to the growth of the Halal industry sector by various government agencies and instrumentalities in the BARMM;

f) Actively promote awareness and understanding of the Halal industry among local businesses, consumers, and stakeholders;

g) Oversee training and capacity-building initiatives to develop local expertise in Halal certification, production, and trade;

h) Attract local and foreign investment by highlighting the regionâ€™s potential as a Halal industry hub;

i) Ensure the establishment and enforcement of Halal certification standards and regulatory frameworks aligned with international norms;

j) Conduct and oversee research and development to identify emerging trends, opportunities, and challenges in the global Halal market;

k) Foster partnerships between the public sector, private businesses, and civil society organizations to enhance the Halal industry's growth and sustainability;

l) Provide the Chief Minister with regular updates on the status of Halal industry development projects and initiatives carried out in the BARMM by the government, business sector, civil society groups, and volunteers;

m) Establish a system to monitor and evaluate the progress and effectiveness of Halal industry development programs and projects;

n) Develop contingency plans to address potential disruptions in the Halal supply chain due to natural disasters, political changes, or global economic trends; and

o) Perform such other functions and tasks as may be assigned by the Chief Minister.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Chief Minister to create an additional minister position within the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) to champion and oversee the Halal Industry Development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister for immediate and appropriate action.
