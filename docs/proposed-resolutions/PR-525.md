**A RESOLUTION ISSUING DRESS CODE GUIDELINES FOR MEMBERS OF THE PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Members of the Parliament are expected to uphold the dignity of the Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, in upholding the dignity of the Parliament, it is imperative for every Member of the Parliament to adhere to an appropriate attire standard;

**WHEREAS**, there exist a necessity to establish clear dress code guideline which best reflect with the Parliamentâ€™s dignity;

**WHEREAS**, during regular sessions, members shall wear the following:

1. **Mondays and Tuesdays:**
- For Gentlemen: *Barong Tagalog* or Coat and Tie
- For Ladies: *Filipina* Dress or Modest Business Attire

2. **Wednesdays and Thursdays:**
- Traditional Filipino dress, including modest traditional attire of the Bangsamoro.

**WHEREAS**, during special sessions, every Member may choose to wear any of the aforementioned attires in the preceding clause;

**WHEREAS**, Members shall wear their respective issued Parliament Pins during parliamentary sessions;

**WHEREAS**, the following attires are considered inappropriate at all times during sessions, public consultations, committee meetings, and hearings:

1. Tattered or distressed denim jeans;
2. Denim jeans *except* when paired with a casual or business coat during public consultations, committee meetings, and hearings;
3. Above ankle pants;
4. Sneakers;
5. Shoes or heels exceeding 3 inches in height;
6. Noisy or distracting footwears;
7. Miniskirts;
8. Outfits with plunging necklines;
9. Polo shirts *except* when paired with a casual or business coat during public consultations, committee meetings, and hearings; and
10. Sleeveless attire.

**WHEREAS**, all Members of Parliament shall observe this guideline, except when the Speaker of Parliament issues an order requiring the Members of the Parliament to wear a different dress code for a particular occasion;

**WHEREAS**, Member/s who violate or does not comply with these guidelines shall be deemed absent. Thus, any action they raise before the plenary, committee meetings, public consultations, or hearings shall produce no effect and shall not be recorded *except* when the Member disrupts the said meetings.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, that this guideline be implemented upon the commencement of the next regular session of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority for 2024 and shall remain in force perpetually unless a new set of guidelines on the same subject matter be issued.
