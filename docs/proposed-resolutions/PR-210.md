**RESOLUTION STRONGLY URGING THE MINISTRY OF TRANSPORTATION AND COMMUNICATIONS (MOTC) TO TEMPORARILY SUSPEND THE OPERATION OF ALESON SHIPPING LINES WITHIN THE TERRITORIAL WATERS OF THE BANGSAMORO REGION AND CONDUCT THOROUGH INVESTIGATION IN AID OF LEGISLATION ON THE SEA MISHAP INVOLVING M/V LADY MARY JOY 3 WHICH RESULTED TO 29 DEATHS.**

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Article III of the 1987 Constitution of the Philippines provides “*No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law, nor shall any person be denied the equal protection of the laws*”;

**WHEREAS**, under the Republic Act No. 9295, otherwise known as “Domestic Shipping Development Act of 2004”, provides that the Maritime Industry Authority (MARINA) has the power and authority to register vessel and issue certificate of operation thereof as well as modify, suspend or revoke any certificate license or accreditation it may have issued to any domestic ship operator;

**WHEREAS**, further stated, under Sec. 9 of the Revised Rules and Regulations implementing R.A. No. 9295 requires the safely standards of all vessels operated by domestic ship operators shall at all times in seaworthy condition, properly equipped with adequate life-saving, communication, safely and other equipment, operated and maintained in accordance with the standards set by MARINA;

**WHEREAS**, Sec. 38 (b), Art. XIII of R.A. No. 11054 also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) states that “*the Bangsamoro Government shall have the authority over the registration of land, water, and air transportation operating exclusively within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region*”;

**WHEREAS**, on March 29, 2023, MV Lady Mary Joy 3, owned and operated by Aleson Shipping Lines, carrying about two hundred fifty (250) passengers and crew, was enroute to Jolo, Sulu from Zamboanga City when it caught fire and ran aground in Baluk-baluk Island, Hadji Muhtamad Municipality, Province of Basilan;

**WHEREAS**, based on the initial reports, the fire incident resulted to the loss of twenty nine (29) lives, injuries to survivors, damage to their properties, and severe traumatic experiences of all the survivors and relatives of the victims;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Maritime Industry Authority (BMARINA) is an attached agency of the MOTC which is tasked, among others, to issue Certificate of Public Convenience (CPC) or Provisional Authority (PA) to vessels and to conduct safety inspection of vessels operating within the jurisdiction of the BARMM to determine its compliance with Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS) and manning requirement thereof;

**WHEREAS**, in response to the urgent need, it is imperative to conduct an immediate investigation of the said incident, in order to determine the circumstances, causes of the incident, and its complete details with the aim of protecting the rights and claims of the victims and survivors, improving safety at sea of the vessels, and preventing future accidents.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority strongly urging the Ministry of Transformation and Communications (MOTC) to temporarily suspend the operation of Aleson Shipping Lines within the Territorial Waters of the Bangsamoro Region and to conduct thorough investigation in aid of legislation on the sea mishap involving M/V Lady Mary Joy 3 which resulted 29 deaths.
