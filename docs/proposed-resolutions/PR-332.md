**RESOLUTION**

**CALLING ON THE MINISTRY OF TRANSPORTATION AND COMMUNICATION (MOTC), THE AIRPORT TRANSPORTATION SERVICES (ATS), AND THE CIVIL AVIATION AUTHORITY OF THE BANGSAMORO (CAAB) TO IMMEDIATELY AND PROPERLY IMPLEMENT THE MUSLIM MINDANAO AUTONOMY ACT NUMBER THREE HUNDRED TWENTY-FOUR (MMA ACT NO. 324)**

**WHEREAS**, under Section 38, Article XIII of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the Bangsamoro Government is expressly authorized “*to grant regional franchises, licenses, and permits to land, sea, and air transportation plying routes in the provinces or cities within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region*” and to “*Exercise of quasi-judicial powers over the operation of land and water transportation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in accordance with the rules of procedure established by the Parliament*”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the General Welfare Clause of the Bangsamoro Organic Law grants the Bangsamoro Government all the powers “*necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare*”;

**WHEREAS**, under the same provision, the Bangsamoro Government is obliged to “*enhance economic prosperity and social justice*” and “*preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants*”;

**WHEREAS**, under Section 2, Chapter 1, Title VX of the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, the MOTC is mandated to “*be the primary policy, planning, programming, implementing administrative entity of the Bangsamoro Government in the promotion, development, and regulation of dependable and coordinated networks of transportation and communications systems, as well as fast, safe, efficient and reliable transportation and communications services*”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3 thereof enumerates the functions of the Ministry to include, among others, the following:

> “a. Administer and enforce all transportation and communications laws, rules, and regulations within its jurisdiction and deputize appropriate law enforcement agencies for the purpose;
> 
> f. Manage or supervise the landside operation of ports and airports in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, except those in economic zones;”

**WHEREAS**, Section 19 thereof creates the Office of the CAAB, which is empowered to “*manage, supervise, and control government airports within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region*”;

**WHEREAS**, Article XVIII, Section 1 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that “*All subsisting laws enacted by the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao Regional Legislative Assembly by virtue of authorities provided under Republic Act No. 6734, as amended by Republic Act No. 9054, shall be deemed valid and in effect, unless inconsistent with this Organic Law or repealed by-laws passed by the Parliament*”;

**WHEREAS**, the 8th Legislative Assembly of the defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) enacted MMA Act No. 324, otherwise known as the Tawi-Tawi Airport Act of 2015, which was approved by the Regional Governor Mujiv S. Hataman on 17 December 2015;

**WHEREAS**, said Act renamed Sanga-Sanga Airport in the Province of Tawi-Tawi into Tawi-Tawi Airport or TTA;

**WHEREAS**, the said MMA Act No. 324 is neither inconsistent with the Bangsamoro Organic Law nor has it been repealed by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority acting as the Bangsamoro Parliament;

**THEREFORE, it is resolved, as it is HEREBY RESOLVED, to AFFIRM that the MMA Act No. 324 renaming Sanga-Sanga Airport to Tawi-Tawi Airport or TTA is a subsisting law and HEREBY CALLS for its immediate implementation;**
**RESOLVED FURTHER, that the MOTC, its ATS, and the CAAB, in coordination with the Department of Transportation and Communication (DOTC), the Air Navigation Services (AIS), and the Civil Authority Aviation of the Philippines (CAAP), shall cause the immediate and proper implementation of the provision of the MMA Act No. 324;**

**RESOLVED FURTHERMORE, that copy of this resolution be furnished to the DOTC, the Air Navigation Service (AIS), and the CAAP so that they can facilitate the updating of the Airport Code or location indicator of the Tawi-Tawi Airport in accordance with the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), and International Air Transport Association (IATA);**

**RESOLVED FINALLY, to advise the MOTC, its attached agencies: the ATS, and the CAAB, to inform all ministries, offices, agencies, and the public about the new name of the said airport.**
