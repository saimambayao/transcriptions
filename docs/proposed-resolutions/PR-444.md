**A RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO ALLOCATE FUNDS FOR FREE MAMMOGRAM CHECK-UP ON BOTH BANGSAMORO MEN AND WOMEN AGES FORTY (40) YEARS OLD AND ABOVE AND THOSE YOUNGER WITH SYMPTOMS FOR PREVENTION OR EARLY TREATMENT**

**WHEREAS**, under Article II, Section 15 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution, “The state shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them.”

**WHEREAS**, under Article III, Section 3. Bangsamoro Organic law, it provides that xxx “Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall xxx promote health and safety xxx of its inhabitants.”

**WHEREAS**, according to the World Health Organization, in the year 2020, there were 2.3 million women diagnosed with breast cancer and 685,000 deaths globally. As of the end of 2020, there were 7.8 million women alive who were diagnosed with breast cancer in the past 5 years, making it the world’s most prevalent cancer, highlighting the pressing need for early detection and prevention measures;

**WHEREAS**, breast cancer is the most common cancer among Filipino women with an increasing incidence in recent years. According to the Philippine Society of Medical Oncology, Breast Cancer ranks as the top cancer site for both sexes, and about 65% of cases are diagnosed at an advanced stage emphasizing the need for enhanced prevention and early detection efforts;

**WHEREAS**, as published by the Philippine Institute for Development Studies (PIDS) on April 20, 2023, previous data by the Department of Health (DOH) showed that 3 in every 100 women in the country will be diagnosed with breast cancer in their lifetime. While women have a higher risk, the Philippine Cancer Society noted that the disease does not spare men since at least one in 1,000 men will have breast cancer in their lifetime;

**WHEREAS**, ensuring equitable access to mammogram check-ups for both Bangsamoro men and women aged forty (40) years old and above, as well as those younger with symptoms, is essential to reducing the burden of breast cancer and saving lives in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, this Resolution aims to provide a monthly free mammogram consultation on at least twenty (20) individuals who are residents of the Bangsamoro Region;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government shall allocate in its Annual General Appropriations Act to be attached to the Ministry of Health (MOH) annual budget for the furtherance of this Proposed Resolution;

**WHEREAS**, in celebration of Breast Cancer Awareness Month, this resolution aims to provide free mammogram check-ups for qualified patients to promote health safety and establish health consciousness in the Bangsamoro community;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in **A RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY TO ALLOCATE FUNDS FOR FREE MAMMOGRAM CHECK-UP ON BOTH BANGSAMORO MEN AND WOMEN AGES FORTY (40) YEARS OLD AND ABOVE AND THOSE YOUNGER WITH SYMPTOMS FOR PREVENTION OR EARLY TREATMENT.**
