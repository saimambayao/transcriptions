**RESOLUTION**

**EXPRESSING SUPPORT TO HOUSE BILL NO. 6522 or "AN ACT CREATING THE PHILIPPINE CENTERS FOR DISEASE PREVENTION AND CONTROL, DEFINING THEIR POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR"**

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao is committed to safeguarding the health and well-being of its constituents and contributing to the overall health security of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS,** House Bill No. 6522 entitled **"An Act Creating the Philippine Centers for Disease Prevention and Control, Defining their Powers and Functions and Appropriating Funds Therefor"**, approved on its third reading, aims to enhance the country's capacity to effectively manage and respond to public health emergencies;

**WHEREAS,** the establishment of the Center for Disease Prevention and Control under the proposed legislation signifies a crucial step towards improving the coordination, efficiency, and effectiveness of public health emergency preparedness and response efforts;

**WHEREAS,** the modernization of public health emergency preparedness and response capabilities aligns with the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region's commitment to promoting the health and welfare of its people, particularly in light of the region's unique healthcare challenges and opportunities;

**WHEREAS,** the proposed legislation envisions a comprehensive approach to disease prevention and control that resonates with the Bangsamoro people's desire for accessible, responsive, and equitable healthcare services;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, as it hereby resolves, to extends its full support to the speedy enactment of House Bill No. 6522 or **"AN ACT CREATING THE PHILIPPINE CENTERS FOR DISEASE PREVENTION AND CONTROL, DEFINING THEIR POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR"**

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** to transmit a copy of this Resolution to the House of Representatives and the Senate, relevant government agencies, and all concerned parties as a testament to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region's steadfast support for the proposed legislation.
