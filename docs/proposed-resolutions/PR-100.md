**RESOLUTION**

**ESTABLISHING AND CONSTITUTING THE BANGSAMORO-DEVELOPMENT BUDGET COORDINATING COMMITTEE (B-DBCC) IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION**

**WHEREAS**, Section 6, Article XII of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that "[t]he Bangsamoro Government shall have the power to create its own sources of revenues and to levy taxes, fees, and charges, subject to the provisions of this Organic Law xxx. Such taxes, fees, and charges shall accrue exclusively to the Bangsamoro Government.

The sources of revenues of the Bangsamoro Government include, among others, the following:

a) Taxes, in accordance with the provisions of this Organic Law;
b) Fees and charges;
c) Annual block grant from the National Government;
d) Revenues or shares in revenues from the exploration, development, and utilization of natural resources derived from land or water areas or territories within the Bangsamoro jurisdiction;
e) Share in the National Government taxes, fees, and charges collected in the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction;
f) Dividends from Bangsamoro government-owned or controlled corporations and other corporations, and shares from the dividends of national government-owned or controlled corporations and their subsidiaries in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region as may be determined by the Intergovernmental Fiscal Policy Board;
g) Grants from economic agreements or conventions entered into by the Bangsamoro Government to which the National Government is a party;
h) Grants, endowments, donations, foreign assistance and other forms of aid;
i) Loans and official development assistance;
j) Shares and revenues generated from the operations of public utilities within the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction;
k) Appropriations and other budgetary allocations from the National Government; and
l) Tax of not more than ten percent (10%) of fair market value in the locality per cubic meter of ordinary stones, sand, gravel, earth, and other quarry resources, as defined in the National Internal Revenue Code, as amended, extracted from public lands or from the beds of seas, lakes, rivers, streams, creeks, and other public waters within its territorial jurisdiction";

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article V of the same Organic Law grants the Bangsamoro Government its budgeting authority without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the Philippines. No public money, including the block grant and other national government subsidies and support funds given in lump sum, shall be spent without an appropriations law clearly defining the purpose for which it is intended. The Parliament shall pass an annual appropriations law;

**WHEREAS**, the Organic Law prescribed that the form, content, and manner of preparation of the Bangsamoro budget shall be prescribed by a law enacted by the Parliament consistent with existing laws, rules and regulations of the National Government. For this purpose, the Parliament shall create a Bangsamoro Budget Office;

**WHEREAS**, **Title IV, Section 10, Chapter 4**, Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 13 or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code (BAC) provides for the organization of the Bangsamoro Treasury Office (BTO) within the Ministry of Finance Budget and Management (MFBM);

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament enacted Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 6, creating the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) as the socio-economic planning and policy coordinating authority of the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, the Rules, Procedures and Practices of the BTA Parliament provides the establishment of the Committee on Finance, Budget and Management (CFMB) as one of its statutory committees;

**WHEREAS**, the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) has the overall control and supervision of all ministries and offices of the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, the amount of the block grant shall be equivalent to five percent (5%) of the net national internal revenue tax collections. The annual block grant shall be automatically appropriated in the General Appropriations Act of the Congress of the Philippines to the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, the Organic Law requires further that the Parliament shall pass an annual appropriations law allocating the block grant to various agencies and programs to the power and functions of the Bangsamoro Government. Copies of the development plans of the Bangsamoro Government shall be furnished the Department of the Interior Local Government (DILG);

**WHEREAS**, the Organic Law underscores that in the allocation of the Block Grant, the national laws and the budgeting rules and regulations implemented by the Department of Budget and Management (DBM) and DILG applicable to the local government units shall apply;

**WHEREAS**, in accordance with its mandate and functions, the compositions of the members of the Bangsamoro Development Budget Coordinating Committee shall be constituted, as follows:

**Chair** : Minister, MFBM
**Members** : Deputy Senior Minister, OCM
Director General, BPDA
Chairman, CFMB, BTA
Bangsamoro Treasurer, BTO;

**WHEREAS**, a Technical Board composed of the most senior permanent officials of the Bangsamoro DBCC is also constituted, as follows:

**Chair** : Director General, MFBM
**Members** : Director, Technical Management Services, OCM
Director, Planning and Policies Bureau, BPDA
Director, Plenary and Committee Affairs Department, Bangsamoro Parliament
Division Head, Management and Reporting Division;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Development Budget Coordinating Committee shall generally review and approve macro-economic assumptions, revenue projections, borrowing level, aggregate budget level and expenditure priorities and recommend to the Parliament and the Chief Minister the consolidated public sector financial position and the Bangsamoro Government fiscal program, in close coordination with concerned national agenciesÂ¹. Specifically, the Bangsamoro DBCC shall:

a) Review and approve the budget level and expenditure priorities and recommend to the Parliament and the Chief Minister of the consolidated BARMM financial position and fiscal program;
b) Recommend for the Chief Minister executive approval, the level of the Annual Bangsamoro Government expenditure and ceiling of Bangsamoro government spending for socio-economic development, government priorities, and other services, for consideration of the Bangsamoro Parliament;
c) Recommend to the Chief Minister and the Parliament the specific guidelines in the allocation of Block Grant for Personal Services (PS) Maintenance and Other Operating Expenditures (MOOE) and Capital Outlay (CO) in accordance with budgeting rules and regulations of the DBM;
d) Recommend to the Chief Minister and the Parliament the proper allocation for each development programs and projects including special projects for conflict affected areas;
e) Recommend to the Chief Minister and the Parliament the necessary measures for revenue generation; and alternative modes of financing development programs and projects; and
f) Review and update the macro-economic targets of the Bangsamoro region in order to ensure their achievements.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Establishing and Constituting the Bangsamoro-Development Budget Coordinating Committee (B-DBCC) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
