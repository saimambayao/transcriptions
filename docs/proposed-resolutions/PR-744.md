**A RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE, BUDGET AND MANAGEMENT (MFBM), BANGSAMORO BUDGET OFFICE (BBO), MINISTRY OF THE INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT (MILG), AND OTHER RELEVANT OFFICES TO CONDUCT REGULAR AND MANDATORY TRAININGS FOR BIDS AND AWARDS COMMITTEES (BACs) IN BARMM, AND TO ALLOCATE FUNDS FOR THIS PURPOSE**

**WHEREAS**, the 1987 Philippine Constitution, Article XI, Section 1, provides that Public Office is a public trust. Public officers and employees must at all times be accountable to the people, serve them with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives. utmost responsibility, integrity, and efficiency;

**WHEREAS**, Section 41, Article VII of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law, affirms that public office is a public trust and mandates all public officers and employees to be accountable to the people, serve with utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty, and efficiency, act with patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article XII of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law, mandates the Bangsamoro Government to implement transparency and accountability mechanisms consistent with open government practices and generally accepted financial management principles, in addition to establishing internal auditing systems;

**WHEREAS**, Republic Act No. 12009, also known as the New Government Procurement Act, governs the policies and procedures in government procurement and emphasizes efficiency, transparency, competition, and accountability in the procurement process;

**WHEREAS**, recurring issues in the procurement processes of ministries, agencies, offices, and local government units within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) are, in part, linked to gaps in the technical knowledge and skills of the Bids and Awards Committee (BAC) members, resulting in procurement delays, disallowances, or procedural violations;

**WHEREAS**, mandatory capacity development and training programs will further equip Bids and Awards Committee (BAC) members with the necessary understanding and tools to effectively comply with Republic Act No. 12009 and the Bangsamoro Government’s internal procurement policies;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby **RESOLVED**, that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament urges the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM), the Bangsamoro Budget Office (BBO), the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG), and other relevant offices to conduct regular and mandatory procurement trainings for all Bids and Awards Committees (BACs) across ministries, agencies, offices, and local government units in BARMM;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the Parliament urges said agencies to allocate sufficient funds in their annual budgets for the conduct of said trainings, including capacity-building modules, training logistics, certification, and related operational support;

**RESOLVED FURTHERMORE**, that this Resolution affirms the Parliament’s commitment to promoting moral governance, transparency, and procurement integrity in line with the policy direction of the Chief Minister and the mandates of the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the New Government Procurement Act;

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, that copies of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, MFBM, BBO, MILG, COA-BARMM, and all ministries, offices, and LGUs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region for their information, guidance, and appropriate action.
