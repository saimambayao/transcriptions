**RESOLUTION**
**EXTENDING PROFOUND GRATITUDE AND APPRECIATION TO THE SENATE COMMITTEE ON WOMEN, CHILDREN, FAMILY RELATIONS AND GENDER EQUALITY FOR THE SUCCESSFUL CONDUCT OF THE BENCHMARKING ACTIVITY OF BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY COMMITTEE ON WOMEN, YOUTH, CHILDREN, AND PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES AT THE SENATE OF THE PHILIPPINES**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) established the Committee on Women, Youth, Children, and Persons with Disabilities (CWYCP) on 22 November 2022, through the adoption of Resolution No. 268, which provides for the rules, procedures, and practices of the BTA Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, the CWYCP consists of 25 members and exercises jurisdiction over all matters concerning the representation, participation, welfare, and development of women, youth, children, and persons with disabilities within the Bangsamoro region, as outlined in Paragraph 17, Section 3, Rule XII, of BTA Resolution No. 268;

**WHEREAS**, as a newly established BTA committee, CWYCP in a meeting approved the conduct of Benchmarking Activity of all its women members with their counterparts at the Congress of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, the primary purpose of the Benchmarking Activity was to facilitate sharing of knowledge, experiences, and best practices as well as exploration of collaboration and capacity-building areas between the CWYCP and its counterpart committees in Congress. It aimed to enhance the CWYCP’s ability to develop effective legislation, policy making, oversight, and representation, particularly of the women sector in BARMM. Moreover, it sought to foster stronger partnership and linkage with legislators from both houses of Congress;

**WHEREAS**, the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations and Gender Equality, composed on 11 members, is the national counterpart of CWYCP at the Senate of the Philippines. Said Senate Committee has jurisdiction over all matters relating to women, the welfare and protection of children, family relations, and equality before the law of women and men (Paragraph 40, Section 13, Rule X, Rules of The Senate);

**WHEREAS**, the CWYCP delegation, led by Chairperson Member of Parliament (MP) Diamila Disimban-Ramos, included Deputy Floor Leader MP Atty. Mary Ann Arnado, Subcommittee on Children Chairperson MP Sittie Fahanie Uy-Oyod, Subcommittee on Persons with Disabilities Chairperson MP Tarhata Maglangit, and committee members MP Baintan Ampatuan, MP Susana Anayatin, and MP NurRedha Misuari. Meanwhile, the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations, and Gender Equality was represented by Chairperson Senator Risa Hontiveros, along with her key staff members;

**WHEREAS**, the Benchmarking Activity served as an invaluable opportunity for the CWYCP to learn about effective legislative strategies, policy implementation practices, and innovative solutions for addressing women’s and children’s issues, which are essential for the progress of the Bangsamoro region;

**WHEREAS**, during the activity with the Senate Committee, key takeaways were identified, including the following:

1.	The importance of mutual learning and collaboration between the counterpart committees of the Senate and the BTA;

2.	The necessity of public consultations to ensure that legislation addresses the real needs and concerns of the communities;

3.	The critical role of legislative oversight to ensure the effectiveness of laws protecting women and children;

4.	The advocacy for an intersectional approach to legislative work, recognizing the complex interplay of gender with other social factors and lenses;

5.	The persistent challenges of sexism and misogyny within institutions and the need to confront these biases;

6.	The significant role of the women's movement in advocating for gender equality and supporting legislative efforts;

7.	The focus on key legislative areas to protect women and girls and ensure gender-responsive emergency management;

8.	The effective utilization of the Gender and Development (GAD) budget to fund complementary programs and initiatives;

9.	The encouragement for young women and men to embrace their roles as agents of positive change and to support gender and development initiatives.

**WHEREAS**, with the support of the Office of the then Senate President Juan Miguel Zubiri, through the External Affairs Services, the CWYCP delegation also made an appearance and was recognized at the Senate Plenary Session. The committee members engaged in brief networking and exchange with the good senators at the Senate Session Hall. They also visited the Senate Museum and were inspired on how the upper house has collected and preserved their memorabilia;

**WHEREAS**, the Benchmarking Activity enhanced the learning and collaboration between the CWYCP and the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations, and Gender Equality, with both committees engaging in a meaningful exchange aimed at protecting women and girls and promoting their empowerment. Senator Risa Hontiveros, the Chairperson of the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations and Gender Equality, in her remarks stressed the importance of dialogue to align legislative initiatives and be responsive to the specific needs various sectors, including those in the BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority deeply appreciates the generosity, warm welcome, and time afforded by the Senate of the Philippines and its Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations, and Gender Equality during the CWYCP’s Benchmarking Activity;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority to extend profound gratitude and appreciation to the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations and Gender Equality for the successful conduct of Benchmarking Activity of the BTA Committee on Women, Youth, Children, and Persons with Disabilities at the Senate of the Philippines on May 8-9 2024;

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, to present a copy of this resolution to the Senate Committee on Women, Children, Family Relations and Gender Equality to the Senate President as a token of our gratitude and appreciation as well as for this resolution’s necessary effects.
