**“A RESOLUTION URGING LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS (LGU) TO SUPPORT THE BARMM FOOD SUFFICIENCY ROADMAP THROUGH CREATION AND ESTABLISHMENT OF A SUSTAINABLE FOOD SECURE AND RESILIENT COMMUNITIES TO SERVE AS MODELS, EXAMPLES, OR A PILOT PROJECT FOR OTHER LGUs TO FOLLOW”**

       WHEREAS, The Philippines along with 168 nations adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development in September 2015 to usher in a new global development era with a common pledge that “no one will be left behind” and to contribute for the attainment of the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) No. 2 of Zero Hunger;

       WHEREAS, Paragraph 2, Article 11 of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights provides that States Parties recognize the fundamental right of everyone to be free from hunger, and shall take, individually and through international cooperation, measures to improve methods of production, conservation and distribution of food;

       WHEREAS, Section 11, Article XIII of the Constitution provides that the State shall adopt an integrated and comprehensive approach to health development, which shall endeavor to make essential goods, health and other social services available to all the people at affordable cost;

       WHEREAS, Consistent with Ambisyon 2040 of President Rodrigo Roa Duterte and thus complements in the attainment of the Philippine Development Plan (PDP) 2017-2022, that seeks to contribute to a comprehensive and integrated approach towards addressing food security and malnutrition;

       WHEREAS, in January 2021, the Duterte administration issued Executive Order No. 101 creating an Inter-Agency Task Force on Zero Hunger to ensure that government policies, initiatives and projects on attaining zero hunger shall be coordinated, responsive and effective. The Local Government Units are enjoined to render such necessary assistance to the Task Force in the implementation and achieve the objectives of this order;

       WHEREAS, Section 2 (c), Article V, Republic Act (RA) 11054 otherwise known as the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall, among others, exercise its authority over agriculture, livestock and food security of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the Philippines;

       WHEREAS, Chief Minister Ahod Balawag Ebrahim issued an Executive Order 005 Series 2020 as amended by the Memorandum Order No. 509 Series 2020 creating Bangsamoro Food Sufficiency Task Force (BFSTF) and its Secretariat” to facilitate innovations, convergence, reforms, and increase investments towards sustainable food system in BARMM;

       WHEREAS, Section 2 EO No. 005 S 2020, states that the BFSTF has powers and functions to formulate the Bangsamoro Food Sufficiency Plan (BFSP) which shall outline the regional priorities based on the comprehensive understanding of the problem related to access, availability and the utilization of food in the region and to address the existing challenge in the food insecurity and prevalence of malnutrition in the region;

       WHEREAS, the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) as the Chairperson of BFSTF, includes its flagship programs such as Basic Integration for Harmonized Intervention (BINHI), Integrated and Sustainable Development for Aquaculture (ISDA) and Land Distribution and Development for Upliftment of Poor ARBOs (LUPA) which provides a nutrition – sensitive lens in collaboration with partner agencies and local government units;

       WHEREAS, the Philippines in 2015-2020, showed 64% of its total population were chronically food insecure, of which 17% moderately food insecure and 8% severely food insecure. Then out of the 71 provinces it was analysed that Lanao del Sur and Sulu were among the provinces who were experiencing severe chronic food insecurity with (IPC Level 4) which is driven by poor food consumption quality, quantity and high level of chronic undernutrition;

       WHEREAS, the Food Security status in 2019 based in the Expanded National Nutrition Survey, BARMM total households recorded 34.7% suffers from moderate food insecurity and 29.1% of family households were not able to meet their basic food needs;

       WHEREAS, hence, it is imperative to face the current situation in the region through the collaborative efforts of ministries and local government in improving and managing local governance, health, nutrition, education, agriculture, environment, and other related sectors, once and for all, address the region’s decades-long challenges;

       WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro government needs champions of Food Security and Nutrition Programs utilizing various best practices recognized as appropriate, applicable, sustainable and cost-efficient.

       **NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority and the Office of the Member of Parliament Dr. Kadil M. Sinolinding Jr., DPBO, to pass and approve this Resolution in urging the Local Government Units to support the Food Security and Nutrition Roadmap through creation, establishment of local plan for sustainable food secure and resilient communities in their jurisdiction to serve as models, examples, or a pilot project for other communities to follow.

       **FURTHER RESOLVED,** that the Statutory Committee being the Secretariat of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority to furnish a copy of this resolution to Bill and Index Department and all members of the BTA a copy of this resolution.
