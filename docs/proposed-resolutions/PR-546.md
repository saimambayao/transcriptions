**RESOLUTION**
**URGING THE MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE, FISHERIES, AND AGRARIAN REFORM (MAFAR) AND THE MINISTRY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY (MOST) TO STRENGTHEN THEIR RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS IN ORDER TO ADDRESS FOOD INSECURITY IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)¹**

**WHEREAS**, Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article XIII of the Constitution categorically provides that the State shall provide support to agriculture through appropriate technology and research, and adequate financial, production, marketing, and other support services;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article V of the aforementioned law provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its authority over agriculture, livestock, and food security;

**WHEREAS**, in the 2023 State of the Nation Address (SONA) of His Excellency President Ferdinand “Bongbong” R. Marcos, Jr., he emphasized that in the Philippine’s quest for food security, the methods used are more guided by science and the balance of nature, so that production is both sustainable and responsible, benefiting both today’s and future generations²;

**WHEREAS**, the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) is mandated to promote a sustainable agri-fishery growth and development, equitable land distribution and tenurial security in the Bangsamoro region³;

**WHEREAS**, as the counterpart of the Department of Agriculture (DA) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), the MAFAR also envisions a food-secure and resilient Philippines with empowered and prosperous farmers and fishers⁴. It shall ensure the availability, adequacy, accessibility, and affordability of food supplies at all times in the Bangsamoro Region⁵;

**WHEREAS**, the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) is mandated to set direction and leadership in Science, Research, Inventions, Technology Education, their Development and ensure the full and effective participation of all sectors in the planning, programming, coordination and implementation of scientific and technological researches⁶;

**WHEREAS**, the MOST is further mandated to pursue research and development towards informed policy formulation, respond to pressing regional priorities, push to maturity emerging technologies towards productivity, and build-up science and technology literatures through development of research compendium⁷;

**WHEREAS**, the BARMM covers over 11,608 km², or 4 percent of the country’s land area, with a population of over 4.12 million⁸.

**WHEREAS**, the BARMM’s economy is heavily dependent on agriculture as one of its sources of livelihood⁹;

**WHEREAS**, according to the Philippine Statistics Authority – BARMM, about 53 percent families in the region are poor and 29.1 percent of them were not able to meet their basic food needs during the first semester of 2018¹⁰;

**WHEREAS**, according to the Expanded National Nutrition Survey (ENNS) conducted by the Department of Science and Technology – Food and Nutrition Research Institute (DOST-FNRI) in 2018, 2019, and 2021, the BARMM recorded the highest prevalence of household food insecurity at 84.1 percent among all regions¹¹;

**WHEREAS**, being a newly created autonomous region, the BARMM strives to directly resolve the alarming problem on food insecurity and malnutrition among its people especially in the rural areas¹²;

**WHEREAS**, research and development play a crucial role in identifying innovative and practical solutions to combat food scarcity, enhance agricultural practices, and promote the use of sustainable farming and fishing methods;

**WHEREAS**, strengthening research and development programs focused on agricultural practices, crop yields, and technological advancements can significantly contribute to addressing food insecurity in the BARMM;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) to Strengthen their Research and Development (R&D) Programs in order to Address Food Insecurity in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) for their information and appropriate actions.
