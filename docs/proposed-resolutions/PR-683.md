**A RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH (MOH) TO INTENSIFY DENGUE PREVENTION AND CONTROL MEASURES AMID INCREASING CASES IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)**

**WHEREAS**, Article II, Section 15 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution mandates the State to protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054), under Article IX, Section 6, mandates the Bangsamoro Government to establish and maintain an effective healthcare system that is responsive to the needs of its people, particularly in addressing public health concerns such as vector-borne diseases;

**WHEREAS**, dengue fever, caused by the dengue virus and transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, remains a significant public health threat in the Philippines, with seasonal outbreaks occurring particularly during the rainy season¹;

**WHEREAS**, data from the Ministry of Health – BARMM Regional Epidemiology and Surveillance Unit reported **11,671 dengue cases and 61 deaths from January 1 to December 31, 2024**, reflecting a **29.79% increase** compared to the previous year;

**WHEREAS**, the highest number of cases was recorded in **Lanao del Sur (3,225 cases), City of Marawi (2,368 cases), Maguindanao del Norte (2,214 cases), and Maguindanao del Sur (1,271 cases)**, with children aged **0-9 years old** being the most affected age group (47% of cases);

**WHEREAS**, the World Health Organization (WHO) recommends an integrated vector management approach, including community engagement, environmental control, and the use of insecticide-treated nets, as effective strategies in reducing dengue transmission²;

**WHEREAS**, the National Dengue Prevention and Control Program (NDPCP) of the Department of Health (DOH) outlines a strategic approach to dengue management, emphasizing the "4S Strategy": Search and destroy mosquito breeding sites, Secure self-protection, Seek early consultation, and Support fogging and spraying in hotspot areas³;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, urging the Ministry of Health (MOH) to intensify dengue prevention and control measures amid increasing cases in BARMM;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that the MOH strengthen community-based dengue surveillance and early warning systems to identify and address high-risk areas effectively;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that local government units (LGUs), in coordination with the MOH, implement intensified mosquito control activities, including regular clean-up drives, fogging operations in dengue hotspot areas, and distribution of insecticide-treated nets;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that the MOH enhance its public health education campaigns to increase community awareness and participation in dengue prevention, emphasizing the "4S Strategy" as recommended by the Department of Health;

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that partnerships be established with national and international health organizations for technical assistance, research collaboration, and procurement of dengue-preventive measures such as vaccines and diagnostic kits;

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, that copies of this resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, Ministry of Health, relevant regional agencies, local government units, and other concerned entities for their information and appropriate action.
