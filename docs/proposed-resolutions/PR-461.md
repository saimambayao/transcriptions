**A RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF BASIC HIGHER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION (MBHTE) ON IMPLEMENTATION OF MAINSTREAMING INDIGENOUS PEOPLES EDUCATION FOR INCLUSIVE AND CULTURALLY RELEVANT LEARNING TO PUBLIC ELEMENTARY AND SECONDARY HIGH SCHOOL**

**WHEREAS**, Article XIV, Section 2(4) of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that the State shall “encourage non-formal, informal, and indigenous learning systems, as well as self-learning, independent, and out-of-school study programs particularly those that respond to community needs”;

**WHEREAS**, Article IX Basic Rights, Section 8 Right of Access to Basic Services of R.A. 11054 provides that the “Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs, quality education, appropriate services, livelihood opportunities, affordable and progressive housing projects, power and electricity, and water inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region”;

**WHEREAS**, Chapter 2, Section 8 of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18 provides: (j) Establish, maintain, and supervise an indigenous education system to provide equitable access to quality education for indigenous peoples’ (IP) learners and preserve, protect, and promote IP culture and tradition. (d) Rooted in Context – Bangsamoro education shall take into consideration the diverse contexts of learners, educational institutions, and communities, including the historical narrative and aspirations of the Bangsamoro People and the Indigenous People, (q) Recognize and support the indigenous peoples’ educational system for its integration in the Bangsamoro educational system and effectively address the higher educational needs of indigenous people;

**WHEREAS**, Chapter 1, Section 64 *Bangsamoro Indigenous Peoples’ Education Framework (BIPEF)* of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18 ) provides that the Bureau of Indigenous Peoples’ Education (IPed), in consultation with concerned stakeholders, shall formulate a Bangsamoro Indigenous Peoples’ Education Framework to guide the implementation of initiatives in support of Indigenous Peoples’ Education;

**WHEREAS**, this resolution recognizes the significance of education as a fundamental right and a cornerstone of our nation's progress; upholds and enhances the provisions outlined in Article XIV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution; and through concerted efforts aim to ensure quality education, promote patriotism, and cultivate a holistic learning environment for all citizens;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament of Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao urging the MBTE to implement the mainstreaming the Indigenous Education for inclusive and relevant learning to public elementary and secondary level within the Bangsamoro region;

**RESOLVED FINALLY**, that copies of this resolution be furnished to MBHTE for information in appropriate consideration.
