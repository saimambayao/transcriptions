**A RESOLUTION**
**CREATING A COMMITTEE ON LEGISLATIVE FRANCHISES, THEREBY AMENDING SECTION 4, RULE XII OF RESOLUTION NO. 268 OR THE “RESOLUTION PROVIDING FOR RULES, PROCEDURES, AND PRACTICES OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) PARLIAMENT”**

**WHEREAS,** Section 25, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution (Constitution) provides that the State shall ensure autonomy of local governments;

**WHEREAS,** Section 2, Article X of the Constitution provides that territorial and political subdivisions shall enjoy local autonomy;

**WHEREAS,** Section 2, Article IV of Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) provides that in exercising its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in the Organic Law;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS,** Section 2, Article VII of the BOL states that the powers of government shall be vested in the Parliament which shall exercise those powers and functions expressly granted to it in this Organic Law, and those necessary for, or incidental to the proper governance and development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It shall set policies, legislate on matters within its authority, and elect a Chief Minister who shall exercise executive authority on its behalf;

**WHEREAS,** Section 38, Article XIII of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall have the authority to grant regional franchises, licenses, and permits to land, sea, and air transportation plying routes in the provinces or cities within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region: Provided, that the National Government shall regulate the airside operations of all existing airports;

**WHEREAS,** Section 39 of the same Article provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall have the authority to grant regional franchises, licenses, and permits to telecommunication utilities whose frequencies are confined to and whose main offices are located within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region; Provided, that nothing herein, shall be construed as limiting in any way the power of the Congress of the Philippines to grant national franchises in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** Section 25, Article VII of the BOL stipulates that the Parliament shall adopt its own rules for the conduct of its business;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article XVI of the BOL provides:

> **Section 3. Powers and Authorities.** *Legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during transition shall be vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority. During the transition period, executive authority shall be exercised by the interim Chief Minister who shall be appointed by the President as such, while legislative authority shall be exercised by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority.*
>
> *All powers and functions of the Bangsamoro Government as provided in this Organic Law is vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority during the transition period. xxx*

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Parliament, in plenary session, adopted Resolution No. 268, entitled “A Resolution Providing for the Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament,” which governs the conduct of all sessions, committee hearings and meetings, and other similar activities of the Bangsamoro Parliament;

**WHEREAS,** Section 1, Rule XII of Resolution No. 268 or as cited by the Bangsamoro Parliament as Rules, provides the definition of a committee which is an organized body composed of Members of the Parliament, constituted to specialize in particular areas of concern and to carry out specific functions. It considers and makes recommendations and draws reports concerning disposition of bills, resolutions, and other preparatory works to be presented to the plenary sessions of the Parliament;

**WHEREAS,** Section 2 of the same Rule provides that the Parliament shall have Statutory committees and Parliamentary committees. Ad hoc, joint ad hoc, special, and sub-committees may also be created and organized as the Parliament may deem necessary;

**WHEREAS,** the Committees of the Bangsamoro Parliament are composed of Members of Parliament (MPs) who are experts in recognized fields relevant to the fulfillment of the interim government's duties as specified in the BOL;

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Rule XII of the Rules provides that Statutory Committees are permanent committees provided under the Organic Law of the BARMM and Resolution No. 268. The following are the Statutory Committees:

1) Committee on Finance, Budget and Management;
2) Committee on Basic, Higher and Technical Education;
3) Committee on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy;
4) Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform;
5) Committee on Local Government;
6) Committee on Health;
7) Committee on Social Services;
8) Committee on Labor and Employment;
9) Committee on Trade, Investment and Tourism;
10) Committee on Transportation and Communications;
11) Committee on Public Works and Highways;
12) Committee on Human Settlements and Developments;
13) Committee on Public Order, Security and Safety;
14) Committee on Indigenous Peoples Affairs;
15) Committee on Science and Technology;
16) Committee on Bangsamoro Justice System; and
17) Committee on Women, Youth, Children and Persons with Disabilities;

**WHEREAS,** Section 4 of the same Rule provides that Parliamentary Committees are permanent committees which exercise continuing responsibility in the legislative activity of the Bangsamoro Parliament. The following are the Parliamentary Committees:

1) Committee on Rules;
2) Committee on Accounts;
3) Committee on Ways and Means;
4) Committee on Good Government, Accountability of Public Officers and Investigation, otherwise known as the Blue Ribbon Committee;
5) Committee on Ethics and Privileges;
6) Committee on Amendments, Revision, and Codification of Laws; and
7) Special Committee on the Marawi Recovery, Reconstruction, and Rehabilitation.

**WHEREAS,** one of the keys to building a prosperous and thriving region is having a government that is responsive to the needs of its constituents, and officials and employees who uphold the principle of accountability;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is a newly established region that is still in the process of building a government suited to the systems of life, needs, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS,** there is currently no committee dedicated to matters directly and principally relating to the grant, amendment, extension, or revocation of franchises in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS,** it is imperative for the Bangsamoro Parliament to establish a Committee on Legislative Franchises to oversee and regulate all matters relating to the grant, amendment, extension, or revocation of regional franchises, particularly those that impact communications, public utilities, transportation, and other essential public services;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, BY THE BANGSAMORO PARLIAMENT:**

**Section 1. Constitution of the Committee on Legislative Franchises.** – A Committee on Legislative Franchises is hereby constituted as the Bangsamoro Parliament's mechanism for reviewing, evaluating, assessing, and conducting deliberations on bills and resolutions submitted by Members of Parliament, concerning the grant, amendment, extension, or revocation of regional franchises;

**Section 2. Composition.** – The Committee on Legislative Franchises shall be composed of a Chairperson, a Vice Chairperson, and such other members as may be allowed by the Rules or by the Bangsamoro Parliament. The composition of the Committee shall be confirmed by the Bangsamoro Parliament in plenary session;

**Section 3. Functions.** – The Committee on Legislative Franchises shall perform the following functions:

a) Review and evaluate applications for regional franchises involving communications, public utilities, transportation, and other essential public services;

b) Conduct deliberations, meetings, and hearings on bills and resolutions referred to it by the Bangsamoro Parliament;

c) Conduct public consultations with relevant stakeholders in the BARMM on bills submitted to the Bangsamoro Parliament that impact communications, public utilities, transportation, and other essential public services; and

d) Perform such other functions as may be directed by the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Section 4. Secretariat.** – The Committee shall be supported by a Secretariat that shall assist in the performance of its functions.

**Section 5. Budget.** – The Committee Chairperson shall request the necessary budget for its operations from the Speaker of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Section 6. Coordination and Cooperation.** – All ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro Government shall extend assistance to the Committee in matters relevant to the performance of its functions.

**RESOLVED FINALLY,** by the Bangsamoro Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution creating a Committee on Legislative Franchises, thereby amending Section 4, Rule XII of Resolution No. 268 or the “Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament.”
