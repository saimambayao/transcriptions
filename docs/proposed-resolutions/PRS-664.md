**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE BANGSAMORO PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY (BPDA) TO CONDUCT CONTINUOUS CAPACITY-BUILDING ON RESULTS-BASED MANAGEMENT (RBM) FOR ALL BANGSAMORO MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES (BMOAs) TO ENSURE THE ALIGNMENT OF THE BANGSAMORO DEVELOPMENT PLAN (BDP) WITH AGENCY MANDATES AND THEIR ANNUAL PROPOSED BUDGETS**

**WHEREAS**, Section 27, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall **maintain honesty and integrity in the public service** and take positive and effective measures against graft and corruption;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article IX-B of the Constitution mandates the Civil Service Commission, as the central personnel agency of the Government, shall establish a career service and adopt measures to promote morale, efficiency, integrity, responsiveness, progressiveness, and courtesy in the civil service. It shall strengthen the merit and rewards system, integrate all human resources development programs for all levels and ranks, and institutionalize a management climate conducive to public accountability;

**WHEREAS**, Section 32, Chapter IX, Book I of Executive Order No. 292, or The Administrative Code of 1987, specifically provides that *"Public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees must at all times be accountable to the people, serve them with the utmost responsibility, integrity, loyalty and efficiency, act within patriotism and justice, and lead modest lives"*;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3 of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 6, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Socio-Economic Development Planning Act of 2019, states that the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council shall serve as the socio-economic planning, monitoring, and coordinating agency for all development plans, programs, and projects of the Bangsamoro Government. It shall evaluate and recommend short, medium, and long-term comprehensive development plans, programs, and projects for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, for approval by the Parliament;

**WHEREAS**, Section 4, Article XIII of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (BEDC) serves as the planning, monitoring, and coordinating agency for all development plans, programs, and projects of the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, in the pursuit of its development vision and objectives, the Bangsamoro Government through the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), as the Technical Secretariat of the BEDC formulated the second Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 and its component Bangsamoro Development Investment Program (BDIP), where major development and investment programs and projects had been identified as priorities;

**WHEREAS**, the BPDA, as the Technical Secretariat of the BEDC, has formulated the second Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 and its accompanying Bangsamoro Development Investment Program (BDIP), which defines priority development and investment programs and projects;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government is authorized under the BOL to prepare its annual budget from various sources, including taxes, fees, charges, the annual block grant from the National Government, shares in revenues from natural resource exploration and utilization within Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction, and other sources;

**WHEREAS**, a total of 33 ministries, agencies, and offices have been established by virtue of the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the Bangsamoro Administrative Code;

**WHEREAS**, the Committee on Finance, Budget, and Management (CFBM) of the Bangsamoro Parliament, with 31 members, oversees matters related to the preparation of budgets, appropriation of funds, expenditure, payment of public indebtedness, and intergovernmental revenue sharing, among others;

**WHEREAS**, the CFBM has reviewed and scrutinized the proposed budgets of all BMOAs from 2020 to 2025 and observed inconsistencies in performance indicators (PIs), revealing weak or non-aligned PIs with agency functions, the development plan, and programs and projects;

**WHEREAS**, the CFBM has recommended revisions to several BMOAsâ€™ PIs and proposed budgets over the years;

**WHEREAS**, a common understanding of Results-Based Management (RBM) is essential for both the CFBM and BMOAs; and

**WHEREAS**, continuous capacity building for planning officers, budget officers, supervisors, and agency heads, as well as CFBM members, is necessary to establish a shared understanding of the link between the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP), Annual Work and Financial Plan (WFP), and proposed annual budgets.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, by the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) to conduct continuous capacity-building on Results-Based Management (RBM) for all Bangsamoro Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (BMOAs) to ensure alignment of Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) with agency mandates and their annual proposed budgets.

**BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED** that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the BPDA for immediate and appropriate action.
