**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE INTERGOVERNMENTAL RELATIONS BODY (IGRB) THROUGH THE OFFICE OF THE CHIEF MINISTER (OCM) TO INCLUDE IN ITS AGENDA THE TRANSFER AND TURN-OVER OF THE PHILIPPINE COCONUT AUTHORITY (PCA) REGIONAL OFFICE NO. XIV TO THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5, Article XIII of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall recognize the right of farmers, farmworkers, and landowners, as well as cooperatives, and other independent farmers' organizations to participate in the planning, organization, and management of the program, and shall provide support to agriculture through appropriate technology and research, and adequate financial, production, marketing, and other support services;

**WHEREAS**, Executive Order No. 460, s. 1991 was issued by then President Corazon C. Aquino devolving to the Autonomous Regional Government (ARG) of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) the powers and functions of the Department of Agriculture, the control and supervision over its offices in the region and for other purposes;

**WHEREAS**, Section 9 of said Executive Order No. 460 provides that the functions, together with the personnel, assets and equipment, the corresponding budgets, programs and projects being implemented in the ARMM by the Philippine Coconut Authority and Philippine Fisheries Development Authority shall be transferred to the ARG;

**WHEREAS**, Section 23, Article XIII of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) categorically provides that the policies and laws of the Bangsamoro Government on agriculture, fisheries and aquatic resources shall advance agriculture as a key development strategy, promote productivity measures, and provide support for small farmers, landholders, and marginal fisherfolk;

**WHEREAS**, pursuant to Section 2, Article VI of the BOL, a National Government - Bangsamoro Government Intergovernmental Relations Body referred to as "Intergovernmental Relations Body" is created to coordinate and resolve issues on intergovernmental relations through regular consultation and continuing negotiation in a non-adversarial manner;

**WHEREAS**, in one of the cases decided upon by the Supreme Court, the coconut industry was asserted to be “one of the major industries supporting the national economy. It is, therefore, the State's concern to make it a strong and secure source not only of the livelihood of a significant segment of the population, but also of export earnings the sustained growth of which is one of the imperatives of economic stability”¹;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article I of Presidential Decree No. 1468, s. 1978, or the Revised Coconut Industry Code provides that it is hereby declared to be the policy of the State to promote the rapid integrated development and growth of the coconut and other palm oil industry in all its aspects and to ensure that the coconut farmers become direct participants in, and beneficiaries of, such development and growth;

**WHEREAS**, based on the Crops Production Survey (CrPS) released by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), the Bangsamoro Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is the 6th region with the most coconut production from July to September 2021, with 352,100 metric tons of total coconut production²;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is one of the top 10 producers of coconut in the Philippines with about over 376,000 hectares planted for coconuts³;

**WHEREAS**, being one of the major coconut producing regions in the country, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region has benefited many of its constituents such as coconut growers, owners, producers, farmers, workers and other coconut beneficiaries;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA) is the sole government agency that is tasked to develop the coconut industry of the country to its full potential in line with the new vision of a united, globally competitive and efficient coconut industry⁴;

**WHEREAS**, the PCA presently has its Regional Office XIV in the BARMM, which is responsible for the management, supervision, and administration of all related activities involving coconut industry in BARMM;

**WHEREAS**, with the formation of Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim (BARMM) after the ratification of its basic law, the Bangsamoro Government has instituted its governance structure and its own implementation mechanisms to ensure better governance of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and the proper implementation of its development policies, plans and programs for the welfare and well-being of its constituents;

**WHEREAS**, consistent with its development policies and programs, it is fundamental that the agencies and offices operating within the jurisdiction of the BARMM, particularly involving, affecting, and serving the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region’s constituents to be supervised, administered, and managed by the Bangsamoro Government including the Philippine Coconut Authority;

**WHEREAS**, it is imperative that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament, as the interim government of the Bangsamoro Government, to recommend to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) to submit a proposal to the IGRB that would allow the full transfer and turnover of the PCA Regional Office XIV in BARMM to the Bangsamoro Government;

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED** by the BTA Parliament to pass and approve this Resolution Urging the Intergovernmental Relations Body (IGRB) through the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) to include in its Agenda the transfer and tur-over of the Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA) Regional Office No. XIV to the Bangsamoro Government.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that a copy of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, for information and appropriate action.
