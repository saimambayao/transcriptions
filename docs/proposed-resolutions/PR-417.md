**RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH TO UTILIZE TELEMEDICINE TO INCREASE THE ACCESSIBILITY OF HEALTH SERVICES AND ADOPT A GUIDELINE ON THE USE OF TELEMEDICINE IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO**

WHEREAS, Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Constitution states that the State shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them;

WHEREAS, Section 8, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the 'Organic Law of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao," states that the Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

WHEREAS, DOH-DILG-PHILHEALTH Joint Administrative Order No. 0001 S. 2021 institutionalizes the adoption and implementation of telemedicine as an integral mode for the delivery of individual-based health services;

WHEREAS, DOH-DILG-PHILHEALTH Joint Administrative Order No. 0001 S. 2021 provides that the implementation of telemedicine in the delivery of individual-based health services under the integrated P/CWHS in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao shall be in accordance with Article IX, Section 22 of RA 11054 and subsequent laws and issuances. Likewise, the Ministry of Health (MOH) may, in coordination with the DOH, adopt its own guidelines for telemedicine provided that the same shall be consistent with the provisions of DOH-DILG-PHILHEALTH Joint Administrative Order No. 0001 S. 2021 and other subsequent laws and issuances;

WHEREAS, Section 22, Article IX of RA 11054 provides:

"**Section 22. Comprehensive and Integrated Health Service Delivery.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy on health that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents. It shall establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.
The Bangsamoro Government shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them. It shall also uphold the peoples' right to have access to essential goods health, and other social services that promote heir well-being. The Parliament shall enact appropriate legislation towards this end, including the regulation of the manufacture and distribution of food, drinks, drugs, and tobacco in accordance with the policies, rules and regulations of concerned national government agencies. The Bangsamoro Government shall assist and cooperate with the National Government in the provision of quarantine services for the prevention and control of epidemic and other communicable diseases."

WHEREAS, Telemedicine refers to the practice of medicine by means of electronic and telecommunications technologies such as phone call, chat or short messaging service (SMS), audio- and video-conferencing, among others, to deliver healthcare at a distance between a patient at an originating site, and a physician at a distant site;¹

WHEREAS, telemedicine was widely used to maintain access and delivery of continuous, coordinated, and integrated individual-based health services and information during the Covid-19 pandemic;

WHEREAS, despite the lifting of the public health emergency on July 21, 2023, the demand for telemedicine remains high especially those focused on mental healthcare as more people prefer telemedicine as a way to continuously engage with their physicians;

WHEREAS, telemedicine positively affects underserved populations, rural seniors, patients who suffer from chronic conditions, and patients with mobility or transportation issues;

WHEREAS, telemedicine also provides an opportunity to meet the ever-increasing demand for safe and quality health services especially in underserved communities. It also helps manage the surge and reduce the risk posed by traffic congestion in hospitals; and

WHEREAS, telemedicine shall not serve as a replacement for a regular consultation but only as a convenient alternative for patients to connect with healthcare providers for a consultation, diagnosis and treatment.

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED as it is hereby resolved** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, to urge the Ministry of Health to utilize telemedicine to increase the accessibility of health services and adopt a guideline on the use of telemedicine in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao; and

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, to send a copy of this resolution to the Ministry of Health for their appropriate action.
