**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE BANGSAMORO INFORMATION OFFICE (BIO) AND THE MINISTRY OF INTERIOR AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT (MILG) TO DEVISE AN INFORMATION, EDUCATION AND COMMUNICATION (IEC) CAMPAIGN FOR THE ELECTORATES OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION ON THE PROVISIONS OF THE BANGSAMORO ELECTORAL CODE**

WHEREAS, on July 26, 2018, President Rodrigo Roa Duterte signed into law a landmark legislation, Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)”;

WHEREAS, Section 2, Article IV of the BOL provides that in the exercise of its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social and cultural development as provided for in the Organic Law;

WHEREAS, Section 3, Article V of the BOL provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;

WHEREAS, under Section 2, Article XVI of the BOL, it is stated that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) shall be the interim government in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition period;

WHEREAS, as enshrined in the same Article, Section 4(a) of the same law mandates the BTA to ensure, among others, the enactment of priority legislations such as the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, Bangsamoro Revenue Code, Bangsamoro Electoral Code, Bangsamoro Local Government Code, Bangsamoro Civil Service Code and Bangsamoro Educational Code consistent with powers and prerogatives vested in the Bangsamoro Government by the BOL;

WHEREAS, the passing of the priority codes is necessary for the smooth operations of the Bangsamoro Government;

WHEREAS, from October 2022 to January 2023, public consultations were held in Manila, Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, Maguindanao, Lanao del Sur, Cotabato City, and the BARMM Special Geographic Area to ensure that all stakeholders were involved in the legislation process of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code¹;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Parliament unanimously approved and adopted on Wednesday, March 08, 2023, the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 35 or the Bangsamoro Electoral Code of 2023;

WHEREAS, this measure was approved with 64 affirmative votes, zero negative votes, and zero abstentions²;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Electoral Code is a landmark legislation that prescribes the structural, functional and procedural principles in the conduct of elections, plebiscites, initiatives, referenda, recall proceedings and encourage the formation of genuinely principled political parties in the Bangsamoro Region;

WHEREAS, this Code shall guide the conduct of a free, orderly, honest, peaceful, and credible elections through fair election practices, and uphold the right of the Bangsamoro people to effective and rational participation at all levels of social, political, and economic decision-making;

WHEREAS, the first regular election for the Bangsamoro Government will be held synchronously with the 2025 national and local elections;

WHEREAS, in order to achieve a successful and democratic 2025 elections within the Bangsamoro Region, the electorate must understand their rights and responsibilities, and their political system, alongside being sufficiently knowledgeable in exercising their suffrage (right to vote);

WHEREAS, a comprehensive approach in disseminating information regarding the provisions of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code is in order to raise the level of awareness of the electorate especially the disadvantaged and vulnerable sectors;

WHEREAS, institutionalizing the information, education and communication (IEC) campaign on the provisions of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code through the provision of informational materials, seminars, orientations to the public, and strengthening the education campaign can help instill knowledge and disseminate information about the Electoral Code to the general public; and

WHEREAS, the IEC campaign, as proposed in this measure, can have a significant impact on the future of the region.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED,** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution urging the Bangsamoro Information Office and the Ministry of Interior and Local Government to devise an information, education and communication campaign for the electorates of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region on the provisions of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code.

**RESOLVED FINALLY,** that copy of this Resolution be furnished to the BIO and MILG for information and appropriate action.
