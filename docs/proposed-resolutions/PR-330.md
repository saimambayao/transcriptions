**RESOLUTION**

**URGING THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, NATURAL RESOURCES, AND ENERGY (MENRE) TO ESTABLISH A TREATMENT, STORAGE, AND DISPOSAL (TSD) FACILITY FOR WASTE ELECTRICAL AND ELECTRONIC EQUIPMENT (WEEE) OR ELECTRONIC WASTE (E-WASTE) IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)**

**WHEREAS**, Section 16, Article II of the 1987 Constitution provides that “the State shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 2 of Republic Act No. 6969, otherwise known as the “Toxic Substances and Hazardous and Nuclear Wastes Control Act of 1990,” declares that it is the policy of the state to regulate the use and disposal of chemical substances and mixtures that present unreasonable risk and/or injury to health or the environment;

**WHEREAS**, Section 5 thereof provides that Hazardous wastes shall also refer to consumer discards of manufactured products;

**WHEREAS**, the Department of Energy and Natural Resources (DENR) Administrative Order (DAO) 2013-22: Revised Procedures and Standards for the Management of Hazardous Wastes classifies Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) as hazardous wastes with waste number M506 and provides the following criteria: “All waste electrical and electronic equipment that contains hazardous components such as lead, cadmium, mercury, hexavalent chromium, polybrominated biphenyls (PBBs), and polybrominated biphenyl ethers (PBDEs) that includes its peripherals, i.e., ink -- cartridges, toners, etc.”;

**WHEREAS**, Section 24 of the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of R.A. No. 6969 provides that the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) encourages proper management of hazardous wastes generated within the country by promoting, in order of preference: minimization of the generation of hazardous waste; recycling and reuse of hazardous waste; treatment of hazardous waste to render it harmless; and landfill of inert, hazardous waste residues;

**WHEREAS**, it is further provided thereof that hazardous waste shall be managed in such a manner as not to cause or potentially cause – pollution; state of danger to public health, welfare, and safety; harm to animals, birds, wildlife, fish, or other aquatic life; harm to plants and vegetation; or limitation in the beneficial use of a segment of the environment;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “Bangsamoro Organic Law,” provides that “the Bangsamoro Government shall xxx promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, xxx”;

**WHEREAS**, pursuant to Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13, otherwise known as the “Bangsamoro Administrative Code,” the MENRE is mandated to be primarily responsible for the exploration, utilization, management, conservation, protection, and sustainable development of the region’s environment, natural resources and energy sources;

**WHEREAS**, the Global Environment Report showed that the country generated approximately 3.9 kilograms of e-waste per capita in 2019. In the same year, the Philippines generated a total of 32,664.41 metric tons of Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) or E-Waste, based on a report by the Environmental Management Bureau (EMB);

**WHEREAS**, the EMB said that e-waste includes computers, television (TV) sets, refrigerators, cell phones, and other electronic devices, which are complex mixtures of materials and components because of their hazardous content such as mercury and lead;

**WHEREAS**, according to the World Health Organization (WHO), adverse health impacts linked to e-waste include changes in lung function, respiratory and respiratory effects, DNA damage, impaired thyroid function, and increased risk of some chronic diseases later in life, such as cancer and cardiovascular disease;

**WHEREAS**, with the advent of the prevalence of online distance learning and work-from-home arrangements that utilize electronic devices such as computers, laptops, tablets, mobile phones, and Wi-Fi modems, there is now a high-demand and actual usage of the said devices;

**WHEREAS**, several agencies in BARMM have already spent millions and distributed IT equipment to students, teachers, and other employees alike within the Region;

**WHEREAS**, according to Hewlett-Packard (HP), mid-range laptops usually last for only three years;

**WHEREAS**, as of January 2020, there are 135 registered Treatment, Storage, and Disposal (TSD) facilities in the country, and none of these are located within the Region, according to the Environmental Management Bureau of DENR;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it **RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to urge the MENRE to establish a Treatment, Storage, and Disposal (TSD) Facility for Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment or E-waste in BARMM;

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, that copies of this resolution be furnished to the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy for information and appropriate consideration.
