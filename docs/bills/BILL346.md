**AN ACT ESTABLISHING TRADE AND TOURISM CENTERS IN COTABATO CITY, LAMITAN CITY, MARAWI CITY, JOLO (SULU), AND BONGAO (TAWI-TAWI), AND PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

**SECTION 1. Title** - This Act shall be known as the "**BARMM Trade and Tourism Center Development Act of 2025.**"

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy** - It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote economic growth, trade development, and cultural tourism by establishing Trade and Tourism Centers (TTCs) in major economic and cultural centers across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). These centers shall serve as hubs for commerce, investment, and tourism promotion, ensuring sustainable infrastructure development and maximizing local economic benefits.

The design of each Trade and Tourism Center shall be reflective of the culture, tradition, and heritage of its respective province, incorporating elements that honor the rich history and identity of the region.

**SECTION 3. Objective** - This act aims to establish Trade and Tourism Centers (TTCs) in each of the following locations:

1. Cotabato City
2. Lamitan City (Basilan)
3. Marawi City (Lanao del Sur)
4. Jolo (Sulu)
5. Bongao (Tawi-Tawi)

The exact locations of these centers shall be determined by the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT-BARMM) in coordination with relevant agencies.

**SECTION 4. Funding Allocation** - The funds appropriated for the establishment of the TTCs shall be allocated as follows:

a) Architectural and Engineering Design – An initial funding of ₱20 million shall be allocated for the preparation of master plans, feasibility studies, and structural designs.

b) Land Acquisition – To be sourced from the annual appropriations or supplemental budget of the Bangsamoro Government, without limiting the possibility of land donations from local government units (LGUs) or private entities.

c) Construction – To be funded through annual appropriations or supplemental budget allocations.

**SECTION 5. Management and Operations** - The Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT-BARMM) shall have full management and operational control over the Trade and Tourism Centers.

The MTIT-BARMM shall be responsible for:
• Administration and governance of the centers.
• Development of programs and policies for trade and tourism promotion.
• Lease agreements, investment incentives, and business operations within the centers.
• Marketing and branding of the centers to attract investors and tourists.

• Marketing and branding of the centers to attract investors and tourists.

**SECTION 6. Construction and Infrastructure Development** - The MTIT-BARMM shall oversee the construction, ensuring compliance with safety, environmental, and architectural standards.

Each TTC shall include the following core facilities:
• Business and Investment Center
• Trade Exhibition Hall
• Tourism and Cultural Pavilion
• Training and Innovation Hub
• Marketplace and Commercial Zones

The architectural design of each center shall reflect the cultural heritage and traditional aesthetics of the region, incorporating indigenous motifs, historical elements, and environmentally sustainable features.

Additional infrastructure, such as hotels, commercial spaces, and tourism amenities, may be constructed through public-private partnerships (PPPs) and investment programs.

The Bangsamoro Government shall prioritize local contractors, laborers, and materials in the construction process to boost employment and economic activity within BARMM.

**SECTION 7. Future Funding and Private Sector Participation** - Further development, expansion, and sustainability of the TTCs shall be funded through:
• Annual appropriations from the BARMM Government
• Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) with local and foreign investors
• Grants and soft loans from national and international development agencies
• Philippine government funding programs for trade and tourism development

The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage private sector participation through investment incentives, tax breaks, and lease agreements for businesses operating within the TTCs.

**SECTION 8. Monitoring and Evaluation** - The MTIT-BARMM shall conduct an annual review to assess the progress of land acquisition, design, and construction.

The Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI) shall monitor the economic impact of the centers, including job creation, business growth, and tourism development.

**SECTION 9. Separability Clause** - If any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 10. Repealing Clause** - All laws, decrees, orders, and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SECTION 11. Effectivity** - This Act shall take effect 15 days after its publication in the Official Gazette of the Bangsamoro Government or in a newspaper of regional circulation.
