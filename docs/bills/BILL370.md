**AN ACT CREATING BANGSAMORO IDENTIFICATION SYSTEM AND PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR**

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:*

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the "*Bangsamoro Identification System Act.*"

**SEC 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) to ensure the provision and equitable access to basic services such as health, education, and social welfare. The establishment of a comprehensive and efficient Bangsamoro Identification System (BIS) will provide a reliable and secure means of identification for Bangsamoro citizens, promote good governance, and facilitate access to government services, including health, livelihood and social benefits.

**SEC 3. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Identification System.** – The Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) and the Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office (BICTO) shall jointly establish and implement the BIS, which will integrate both a physical ID card and a digital platform to facilitate service delivery and access to public services.

**SEC 4. Scope and Coverage.** – The BIS shall cover all Bangsamoro citizens and residents of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It shall be used for identification for legal, financial and government transactions.

**SEC 5. Data Collection.** – MILG shall collect the following personal information from Bangsamoro citizens:

a. Full name
b. Date and place of birth
c. Citizenship
d. Biometric data (e.g., fingerprints, facial recognition)
e. Address and contact information
f. Employment and economic status (optional)
g. Additional data required for service eligibility (e.g., senior citizens, persons with disabilities)

**SEC 6. Data Storage.** - All data shall be stored securely and managed by BICTO in compliance with relevant data privacy laws. Citizens may opt for both a physical ID card and a digital version through a mobile app.

**SEC 7. Issuance of Bangsamoro ID Cards.** – The MILG shall issue Bangsamoro ID cards to all registered citizens. The ID card shall contain the following:

a. Cardholder’s name, photo, and biometric features
b. Card number
c. Citizenship
d. Date of birth and place of birth
e. Sex
f. Date of issuance and expiry

A digital version of the ID card shall be available through a mobile platform, allowing citizens to access e-services and receive notifications.

**SEC 8. Digital Integration and Online Service Access.** – The BIS shall include a digital platform to:

a. Allow citizens to register and update personal information online
b. Access to government services information (e.g., health, education, welfare) through web portals and mobile applications
c. Receive real-time updates on available benefits, deadlines, and important notifications

**SEC 9. Security Measures.** – MILG and BICTO shall implement stringent security measures to protect the integrity of the BIS, including:

a. Encryption of sensitive data
b. Biometric verification for identity authentication
c. Secure access controls and authentication for government transactions
d. Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments to maintain system integrity

**SEC 10. Data Governance.** – MILG and BICTO shall establish a data governance framework to ensure the quality, integrity, and security of BIS data. This framework will include policies for data collection, storage, use, and sharing, ensuring compliance with privacy laws and security standards.

**SEC 11. Role of the Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office.** – The BICTO shall be responsible for the technical development, implementation, and maintenance of the BIS. Its duties shall be designing the IT infrastructure for the BIS, developing and maintaining the software applications used in the system, ensuring the security and integrity of BIS data and providing technical support and training for BIS personnel.

**SEC 12. Penalties.** – Any person who knowingly provides false or misleading information during the registration process shall be subject to administrative penalties, including fines and suspension of ID privileges. Unauthorized access, modification, or disclosure of BIS data will be subject to criminal penalties, under existing laws.

**SEC 13. Information Campaign.** - To ensure that all Bangsamoro citizens are aware of the benefits and features of the Bangsamoro Identification System (BIS), the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) and the Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office (BICTO) shall conduct an information campaign to inform and educate the public on the following:

a. The importance and benefits of the BIS, including its role in enhancing security, improving governance, and promoting economic development and social inclusion;
b. The registration process and requirements for obtaining a BIS ID card;
c. The features and functionalities of the BIS ID card;
d. The security measures in place to protect personal data and prevent unauthorized access, modification, or disclosure;
e. The penalties for providing false or misleading information during the registration process.

**SEC 14. Appropriations.** - The funds necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act shall be charged against the annual appropriation of the Bangsamoro Government under the Ministry of Local Government and BICTO.

**SEC 15. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The MILG and BICTO shall jointly promulgate the necessary implementing rules and regulations to carry out the provisions of this Act within 90 days.

**SEC 16. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SEC 17. Repealing Clause.** All regional laws, executive orders, administrative orders, or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provision of this act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SEC 18. Effectivity.** This act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the official newspaper of general circulation within the region.
