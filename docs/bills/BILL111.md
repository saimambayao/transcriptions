**“AN ACT CREATING THE RANAW DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY PRESCRIBING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR”**

*Be it enacted by the Members of the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:*

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Ranaw Development Authority Act”**.

**Section 2. Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the State to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature.

The Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BOL) also provides as a matter of policy that the Bangsamoro Government shall also protect, conserve, rehabilitate and develop inland waters and aquaculture resources of Lake Lanao, including the surrounding environments, watersheds, water tributaries, forests and agriculture land. The Bangsamoro Government also aims for the adoption of programs and projects, to ensure that the maintenance of ecological balance and biodiversity shall be given priority.

It shall also be the policy to explore, develop, and utilize the natural resources, including surface and subsurface rights, inland waters, coastal waters, and renewable and nonrenewable resources in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. The protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and development of forests, coastal, and marine resources, including the adoption of programs and projects, to ensure the maintenance of ecological balance and biodiversity shall be given priority, in line with Section 8, Article XIII, of the BOL.

The Bangsamoro Government shall have exclusive powers over inland waters, including lakes, marshes, rivers, and tributaries within its territory, except those that provide energy in line with Section 22, Article XIII, of the BOL.

In order to protect and improve the well-being and quality of life of its inhabitants, the development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) shall be carefully planned taking into consideration the ecological balance and the natural resources that are available for the use of current and future generations.

In the exercise of these powers and functions, the Authority shall anchor its development and management objectives, plans and programs on customary principles and practices, including:

a) *Khilafa wa amanah* (stewardship and trusteeship) – It is the duty of every person to protect the Lake and its resources for the benefit of the present and of future generations;

b) *Amrun bil-ma’ruf wa nahyun Anil munkar* (enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong) – On this basis, the Authority can regulate activities that may cause harm to the Lake ecosystem and the health of local communities;

c) *Ihya al-amwat* (giving life to dead lands) - Under this principle, the Authority shall pursue rehabilitation of degraded areas; and

d) *Tahsin al-‘Ardh* (beautifying the earth) - This principle enjoins the Authority to enhance and develop the area and the natural resources therein.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms.** – The words in this Act are defined as follows:

a. “Authority” – whenever cited in this Act shall mean Ranaw Development Authority;

b. “Basin” – shall refer to the lake and its watersheds;

c. “Board” – shall refer to the Board of Directors of the Ranaw Development Authority;

d. “Shoreland” also known as the “buffer zone” shall refer to the land at the shore of the lake that is part of the lakebed along the lakeshore, alternately submerged or exposed by the normal annual rising and lowering of the lake levels;

e. “Water Tributaries” – shall refer to a river or stream that enters a larger body of water especially a lake or river;

f. “Agricultural Land” – shall refer to lands devoted to or suitable for the cultivation of the soil, planting of crops, growing of trees, raising of livestock, poultry, fish or aquaculture production, including the harvesting of such farm products, and other farm activities and practices performed in conjunction with such farming operations by persons whether natural or juridical and not classified by law as mineral land, forest land, residential land, commercial land, or industrial land;

g. “Forest Lands” or forest area include the public forest, the permanent forest or forest reserves, and forest reservations;

h. “Aquaculture”- shall refer to fishery operations involving all forms of raising and culturing fish and other fishery species of fresh, brackish, and marine water areas

i. “Aquatic Resources” – shall include fish, all other aquatic flora and fauna, and all other living resources of the aquatic environment, including, but not limited to salt and corals

j. “Fisheries”- shall refer to all activities relating to the act or business of fishing, culturing, preserving, processing, marketing, development, conserving, and managing aquatic resources, and the fishery areas, including the privilege to fish or take aquatic resources thereof;

k. “Fishery species”- shall refer to all aquatic flora and fauna, including, but not limited to fish, algae, coelenterates, mollusks, crustaceans, echinoderms and cetaceans.

l. “Ecological Balance” – shall refer to a state of dynamic equilibrium within a community of organisms in which genetic, species and ecosystem diversity remain relatively stable, subject to gradual changes through natural succession;

m. “Fish Pond”- shall refer to a land-based facility enclosed with earthen or stone material to impound water for growing fish;

n. “Fish pen”- is an artificial enclosure constructed within a body of water for culturing fish and fishery/aquatic resources made up of poles closely arranged in an enclosure with wooden materials, screen or nylon netting to prevent escape of fish.

o. “Fish Cage” – shall refer to an enclosure which is either stationary or floating made up of nets or screens sewn or fastened together and installed in the water with opening at the surface or covered and held in a place by wooden/bamboo posts or various types of anchors and floats.

p. “Endangered, Rare and/or Threatened Species” – shall refer to aquatic plants, animals, including some varieties of corals and seashells in danger of extinction as provided for in existing fishery laws, rules, and regulations or in the Protected Areas and Wildlife Bureau of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) and in the Convention of the International Trade of Endangered Species of Flora and Fauna (CITES);

q. “Exotic/Introduced Species” – shall refer to exotic species, with respect to a particular ecosystem, refers to any species, including its larvae, seeds, eggs, spores, or other biological material capable of propagating the species, which is not native to that ecosystem (Beck et al., 2008). An exotic species does not necessarily cause economic or environmental harm to an ecosystem native to, and restricted to, a particular geographical region. Highly endemic species, those with very restricted natural ranges, are especially vulnerable to extinction if their natural habitat is eliminated or significantly distributed;

r. “Endemic species”- native to, and restricted to, a particular geographical region. Highly endemic species, those with very restricted natural ranges, are especially vulnerable to extinction if their natural habitat is eliminated or significantly distributed;

s. “Ranaw Region” – shall refer to the inland waters defined as Lake Lanao along with its shoreland and all other lakes within the Province of Lanao del Sur, including all city, towns and municipalities within Province of Lanao del Sur;

t. “Subsidiary Corporation” – shall refer to a corporation that is organized or a corporation already in existence wherein at least fifty-one percent of its shares of stock are owned or controlled by the organizing or subscribing Authority in this case, the Ranaw Development Authority, to carry out or accomplish its purposes.

**Section 4. Creation of the Ranaw Development Authority. –** There shall be created a regulatory body exercising quasi-judicial powers and functions to be known as the Ranaw Development Authority to manage and administer the utilization, exploration and development of the Lake Lanao and its surrounding natural resources, as well as the preservation and rehabilitation of the lake and its surrounding environment and resources. It should be an attached agency to the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE).

**Section 5. Powers and Functions of the Authority. –** The Authority, shall have the following powers and functions:

a) Conduct a consultative and comprehensive survey of the physical, natural resources, potentialities and projected ecological risks of Lake Lanao and the region, particularly its social, cultural, historical, and economic conditions, hydrologic characteristics, projected water demand and water stress level, power potentials, scenic and tourist spots and such other areas of concerns, and thereafter, craft a comprehensive and detailed Master Plan as basis for comprehensive planning and management over the Lake Lanao region, including the conduct of zoning; *Provided that*, the implementation of all plans, projects, activities and programs of the Authority pertaining to the exploration, development and utilization of Lake Lanao as potential source of energy shall comply with Section 22, Article XIII, of the BOL;

b) Make recommendation to other related agencies on financing, technical support, assistance on any agriculture, forestry, industrial and trade, fishery resources, , ecological and cultural tourism energy projects or programs impacting Ranaw Region;

c) Study, pass upon, approve or disapprove all plans, programs and projects proposed by any LGUs, ministry, offices, agencies within BARMM, public corporations, and private persons or enterprises on any agriculture, forestry, industrial and trade, fisheries and aquatic resource, energy projects or programs directly or indirectly impacting Ranaw Region. *Provided that*, prior to the approval, the proponent shall consult all stakeholders of Lake Lanao including but not limited to the representatives of local and indigenous peoples, civil society organizations and academe who are active on conservation issues, and hydrology experts;

d) Monitor the proper implementation of said plans, programs, activities and projects, as well as implementing policies consistent with the objectives and purposes of this Act;

e) Undertake various research and studies on fisheries and aquatic, waste and water quality, sedimentation, soil erosion, forestry, agricultural, and any natural resources management issue of Lake Lanao needed as basis for policy formulation or any decision making in addition to public consultation with the people;

f) Prepare a water quality management report and program on a continuing basis, which the Authority shall carry out with the assistance and support of the national and local government units involved in water quality management;

g) To exercise exclusive regulatory power and control in the preservation, protection, development and utilization of the Ranaw Region and its natural resources, provided that such development and utilization is not for energy purposes, in which case co-management principle shall apply;

h) To set standards for environmental compliance for construction, operation, rehabilitation management and conservation of the fisheries and aquatic resources, and maintenance of power plants, irrigation projects, abattoirs, dams, reservoirs, aquaculture facilities, roads, wharves, telephones, transmission lines, transportation systems, water supply, flood control, sewage work, waste disposal facilities and all other facilities and projects in order to ensure sustainability of the water resources and protection of ecological balance, without prejudice to existing national, regional and local laws;

i) Without prejudice to existing environmental laws, establish and enforce anti-pollution standards and water system quality guidelines on domestic, municipal commercial and industrial wastewater effluents being discharged in to the Lake Lanao;

j) To regulate the, utilization, management, development, conservation and allocation system of fisheries and aquatic resources particularly on the introduction of Foreign Aquatic Species and on the protection of Rare, Threatened, and Endangered Species;

k) To regulate the utilization, management, development, conservation and allocation system of fisheries and aquatic resources particularly on the remaining endemic species of cyprinids in Lake Lanao.

l) Conduct activities that promote cultural, economic, environmental, and historical importance of Lake Lanao whether nation wide or within the region;

m) Undertake awareness-raising activities in order to achieve social inclusion;

n) To issue necessary permit or license, collect fees for the use of the lake waters and its tributaries for all beneficial purposes including but not limited to fisheries, recreation, water supply for municipal, industrial and agricultural uses, navigation, irrigation, and waste disposal purpose or impose penalty for any improper or illegal use. All the fees so collected shall be used for the management and development of the Lake and its watershed areas: Provided, that the rates of the fees to be collected shall be subject to the guidelines to be approved by the Authority for the purpose.

o) Fix, impose, collect, periodically review and adjust reasonable fees and other related charges for services rendered in the performance of its mandate;

p) To require the local government units within the Ranaw Region to pass appropriate zoning ordinances and regulatory measures and to enforce the same with the assistance of the Authority;

q) To propose the zoning and reclassification of land to the local government units concerned;

r) Require residential houses, clinical and medical establishments, car and motor repair shops, private and public markets, abattoirs, private and public buildings and other human settlement within the Ranaw Region to construct individual and decentralized wastewater and sanitary treatment plants, or centralized sewerage systems and sewage treatment facilities. The Authority shall impose reasonable fees and other similar charges for the issuance of renewal of all the required permits;

s) Establish the lake water elevation system in order to ensure the safety and welfare of the surrounding residents and monitor strict compliance therewith including the imposition of appropriate sanctions in case of violations with proper coordination with the local government units;

t) Accept grants, donations, gifts, bequests, funds, and/or properties in whatever form and whatever source intended for the furtherance of the plans and programs of the Authority subject to coordination with the appropriate agency and administer the same in accordance with the terms thereof, or in the absence of any condition, in such manner consistent with the policy and powers of the Authority provided for in this Act;

u) Enter into contract/s with another government agency and/or private entities to carry out the purposes and objectives of the Authority;

v) Issue rules and regulations by a majority vote as may be necessary to effectively carry out the powers herein provided, including the plans, programs, activities and projects of the Authority. Such rules and regulations shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication thereof, in a newspaper of general circulation;

w) Adopt rules and regulations governing the approval of the sewage works and waste treatment and disposal systems and the issuances of clearances or permits in accordance with the provisions of this Act and inspect construction and maintenance of sewerage works and industrial waste treatment and disposal systems in compliance with clearance or permit conditions, with observance of due process;

x) Grant clearances and permits for projects and activities within or affecting the Lake Lanao including industrial and commercial activities, recreation and tourism, navigation, housing and other forms of land developments, construction and operation and impose necessary safeguards, management and control of wastewater and collect necessary fees for such activities and projects;

y) Issue, after due notice and hearing, orders or decisions to compel compliance with the provisions of this Act;

z) Deputize or request the assistance of appropriate government agencies or instrumentalities for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this Act and its implementing rules and regulations and the decisions of the Authority

aa) Enter, inspect or investigate at all reasonable times any public and private property devoted to commercial and industrial use within the Ranaw Region, on any matter relating to pollution and existing or imminent environmental hazard and danger;

bb) Enforce power in the Ranaw Region within the territorial jurisdiction of BARMM to carry out the functions and attain the purposes and objectives declared under this Act. In the exercise of this power, the authority shall request the assistance of the Bangsamoro Regional Police Office or other pertinent enforcement offices. Such enforcement power shall be exercised in connection with the operations of water quality surveillance, monitoring and enforcement units, and implementation of rules and regulations promulgated by the Board pursuant to the authority granted under this Act;

cc) Conduct investigations based on the procedures that the Board may prescribe in accordance with the provisions of the Rules of Court serving as supplemental guidelines;

dd) Issue a cease and desist order to immediately halt any practice found to be in violation of the provisions of this Act. Such other shall be without prejudice to the civil or criminal prosecution of persons under the existing laws; and

ee) To perform such other functions as are necessary in the discharge of its powers and functions.

**Section 6. Ranaw Development Authority Master Plan. –** A detailed and comprehensive Master Plan shall be developed in coordination with the concerned local government units and various stakeholders to carry out the plans and objectives of the Authority at least six (6) months after its establishment. The Master Plan shall contain long-term strategy and commitment to implement the same.

The Board, shall undertake a mandatory review of the Master Plan, every five (5) years.

**Section 7. Ranaw Region. –** For the purpose of this Act, the term “Ranaw Region” shall refer to the inland waters defined as Ranaw and all other lakes within the Province of Lanao del Sur, including all city, towns and municipalities within Province of Lanao del Sur.

In order to carry out the objectives of this Act, the management and protection of nature reserves and aquatic parks, forests, watershed reservations, and other protected areas in the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro that have already been defined by and under the authority of the National Government shall be transferred to the Ranaw Development Authority, including power plants operating within the Province of Lanao del Sur.

The sharing on the proceeds or revenues generated or collected from the utilization and development of all natural and other resources within the Ranaw Region shall be subject to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Revenue Code.

**Section 8. Board Composition. –** The Authority shall consist of Board of Directors, hereinafter referred to as the “Board”, which shall be headed by the Provincial Director of the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy of Lanao del Sur. The regulatory powers and functions of the Authority shall be vested in and exercised by Board of Directors, hereinafter referred to as the Board, which shall be composed of the:

(i) Provincial Director of Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), Lanao del Sur;
(ii) Provincial Director of Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), Lanao del Sur;
(iii) Permanent representative from the Ministry of Transportation and Communication (MOTC), Lanao del Sur;
(iv) Provincial Director of Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG), Lanao del Sur;
(v) Provincial Director of Ministry of Trade, Industry and Tourism (MTIT), Lanao del Sur;
(vi) Permanent representative from the Ministry of Public Works (MPW), Lanao del Sur;
(vii) Permanent representative from the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA);
(viii) Permanent Representative from National Power Corporation (NAPOCOR);
(ix) Dean of the College of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources, Mindanao State University, Marawi City; and
(x) The Executive Director II of the RDA.

The Board of Directors shall elect annually from among their members a Chair and a Vice-Chairman and there shall be a Secretary who shall be appointed by the Board. The Executive Director II shall be a member of the Board.

The officials next in rank to the abovementioned members shall serve as the permanent alternate members of the Authority and shall attend meetings of the Board in the absence of their Principals and receive their corresponding per diems.

**Section 9. Powers and Functions of the Board. -** The Board shall have the following powers and functions:

1. To formulate, prescribe, amend and repeal rules and regulations to govern the conduct of business of the Authority;
2. Provide a comprehensive policy guideline for the development of the Ranaw Region;
3. Decide the objectives, strategies and policies of the Authority in accordance with the provisions of this Act;
4. Exercise appellate powers on the decision of the Executive Director II;
5. Approve the organizational and administrative structures and the corresponding staffing pattern of the Authority; fix their reasonable compensation, allowances and other benefits in accordance with the salary standardization law; prescribe their duties and establish methods and procedures as maybe necessary to ensure the efficient, honest and economical administration of the provisions and purposes of this Act. All personnel of the Authority shall be selected only from those with civil service eligibility and shall be subject to civil service laws, rules and regulations;
6. Approve the annual and supplemental budgets of the Authority and authorize each operating and capital expenditures and disbursements as may be necessary for the effective management, operation and administration of the Authority;
7. Create and organize ad-hoc technical working groups composed of the representatives from the government and other stakeholders to assist the Board;
8. By a majority vote of all members of the Board, suspend, remove or otherwise discipline for just cause all officials appointed by the Board;
9. To render annual reports to the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro Parliament such special reports as may be requested; and
10. To do such other acts and perform such other functions as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.

**Section 10. Quorum. -** The presence of at least six (6) Members of the Board, including the Chairman shall constitute a quorum. Such vote by the majority constituting a quorum shall be sufficient for the adoption of any rule, resolution, decision or any act of the Board.

**Section 11. Board Meetings. –** The Board shall meet regularly every quarter and may hold special meetings to consider urgent matters upon the call of the Chair or upon the initiative of four (4) members. The rules and procedures in the conduct of the Board meetings shall be prescribed by the Board.

**Section 12. Per Diems. –** The members of the Board shall receive a per diem for each meeting actually attended, the amount of which shall be determined by the Board, subject to usual accounting and auditing rules and regulations. Members of the Board shall be entitled to commutable transportation and representation allowances in the performance of official functions for the Authority as authorized by the Board.

**Section 13. Executive Director II. -** The Chief Minister shall appoint an Executive Director II who shall be the chief executive officer of the Authority. The Executive Director II shall be an ex-officio member of the Board.

The Executive Director II must be:
a) Filipino citizen and a resident of Lanao del Sur;
b) At least thirty-five (35) years of age;
c) With good moral characters, unquestionable integrity, recognized competence; and
d) A degree holder with at least five (5) years of supervisory or management experience in the field of public administration, economic planning, environmental planning and natural resource management or its equivalent, in the establishment and management of large agricultural, commercial or industrial enterprises or other related fields;

**Section 14. Powers and Functions of the Executive Director II.-** The Executive Director II shall be the chief executive of the Authority, as such, he or she shall have the following powers and functions:

1. To submit, for the consideration of the Board, the policies and measures which he or she believes to be necessary to carry out the purposes and provisions of this Act;
2. Head and administer the Lake Lanao Protection Adjudication Panel as the implementing arm for the quasi-judicial powers of the authority;
3. Execute, administer and implement the policies, plans, programs and projects approved by the Board;
4. Manage, direct and supervise the operation and internal administration of the Authority. The Executive Director II may delegate some of his or her administrative responsibilities to other officers of the Authority subject to the rules and regulations of the Board;
5. Submit quarterly reports to the Board on personnel selection, placement and training;
6. Prepare the annual budget of the Authority for report to the Board and various stakeholders regarding the operation of the Authority including its latest financial statements;
7. Appoint officials and employees, in accordance with the existing regional laws. In addition, to remove, suspend or otherwise discipline them for valid cause, in accordance with the Bangsamoro laws, rules and regulations;
8. Exercise such other powers as may be vested in him by the Board.

**Section 15. Term of Office. –** The Executive Director II shall have a term of four (4) years. He shall only be removed for cause in accordance with the rules and regulations prescribed ty the Civil Service Commission. The Executive Director II shall have the salary grade of 27 and without prejudice of being reappointed every four (4) years.

**Section 16. Preferential Rights of Bona Fide Residents of the BARMM. -** Qualified citizens who are bona fide residents of the BARMM, all other conditions being equal, shall have preferential rights over the exploration, development, and utilization of natural resources of Lake Lanao.

**Section 17. Representation of the Authority. -** The Executive Director II shall be the principal representative of the Authority. He or she shall be empowered, in such capacity, and in accordance and approval of the Board, by two-thirds (2/3) vote, on the following:

a) Represent the Authority in all dealings with other offices, agencies, and instrumentalities of the Government and with all other persons and entities, whether public or private, domestic or foreign;
b) Sign contracts concluded by the Authority, annual reports, balance sheets, profit and loss statements, correspondences and other official documents of the Authority; and
c) Represent the Authority, either personally or through counsel, in any legal proceedings or actions.

**Section 18. Management Structure.-** In carrying out the activities of the Authority, the Executive Director II shall be assisted by an Executive Director I and department heads, who shall have such powers, duties and functions as the Executive Director II may prescribe or delegate. The Executive Director I shall act as the Executive Director II in the absence or during temporary incapacity of the Executive Director II or until such time as a new Executive Director II is duly appointed. The Executive Director I shall have a salary grade of 26.

The following divisions shall be created within the Authority: Administration and Finance; Legal; Planning and Research; Environmental Protection and Integrated Water Management; Social Development, Advocacy and Linkages and such other departments as may be necessary to effectively carry out the functions of the Authority. The head of the departments shall have a salary grade of 24.

**Section 19. - Consultative Assembly.-** There shall be established a consultative assembly which shall serve as venue for public consultations and participation by stakeholders and presentation of the Authority’s plans and programs or modification, amendment or revision of rules and regulations prior to its adoption or implementation within its jurisdiction. The consultative assembly shall be composed of the following:

a) Provincial Governor, Lanao del Sur;
b) City Mayor of Marawi, Lanao del Sur;
c) President League of Municipalities in Lanao del Sur;
d) Barangays’ League President in Lanao del Sur;
e) Private sectors representative of the owners, proprietors and authorized operators of all major private, commercial or industrial business establishments located in all barangays in Lanao del Sur chosen by the sector; and
f) Representatives of the homeowners associations, civil society organizations, Ulama Sector, women, youth, traditional leaders and people’s organizations in Lanao del Sur chosen by the sector;

The Authority shall convene an annual year-end general assembly to report and present its financial and fiscal status and updates on its plans, programs and projects to its stakeholders and to the public. The required logistical support, such as the food and transportation, allowances shall be provided to the members of the Board and the Consultative Assembly during the meeting at the expense of the Authority subject to government accounting and auditing procedures.

The Authority shall administer a system for audio and visual broadcasting, recording, and live streaming through the internet of its assemblies.

**Section 20. Prohibition against Conflict of Interest. –** Any member of the Board shall not be allowed to have any financial interest, directly or indirectly, in any contract entered into by the Authority or in any special privilege granted by the Authority during his or her term of office. All contracts entered into by any such member in such instance shall be voided, after the conduct of proper investigation, without prejudice to the imposition of administrative sanctions. Further, the member of the Board who violated this Section shall be disqualified from serving his or her unexpired term and shall be perpetually disqualified in any position in the Authority.

**Section 21. Location of Principal Office. -** The Authority shall maintain its principal office at a convenient place within the province of Lanao del Sur, but it may have satellite offices in **Islamic City of Marawi** or in such other places as may be deemed necessary for the proper conduct of its business.

**Section 22. Compliance to all Rules and Regulations of the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code and Bangsamoro Administrative Code.-** The Authority shall comply with all Rules and Regulations of the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code and Bangsamoro Administrative Code in the implementation of all its personnel and administrative matters.

**Section 23. Appropriations.** Initial operations for Personnel Services shall be charged against the Miscellaneous Personnel Benefit Fund, MOOE and Capital Outlay shall be charged against the Contingent Funds of the 2022 General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro. Thereafter, the sum necessary for the continued operation and maintenance of the Authority shall be included in the annual budget of the Bangsamoro Government. The Authority shall have fiscal autonomy. Thus, the budget allocated for its efficient operation, for personnel services, and for capital outlay shall be directly transferred to the Authority subject to government accounting and auditing rules and regulations.

**Section 24. Fines and Penalties. -** The Authority is hereby authorized to fix and impose administrative fines and other penalties as determined and approved by the Board for non-compliance with permit and regulatory conditions and for undertaking development activities without clearance/permit from the Authority against any person, juridical or otherwise, within or without the Lake Lanao who may be found violating, or have in fact violated, any of its rules and regulations.

**Section 25. Mandatory Review. –** The Bangsamoro Government, through the Board, shall undertake a mandatory review of the implementation of this Act, five (5) years after its implementation, and every five (5) years thereafter. The Bangsamoro Parliament shall be part of the process.

**Section 26. Repealing Clause. -** All laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations or other issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**Section 27. Separability Clause. -** If for any reason, any section or provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other sections or provisions hereof which are not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**Section 28. Effectivity. -** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of general or regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
