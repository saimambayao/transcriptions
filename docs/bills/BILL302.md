**AN ACT**
**REQUIRING THE ACCREDITATION OF HOSPITALS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION AS IBADAH FRIENDLY HOSPITAL, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**Section 1. Short Title.** - This Act shall also be known as the **Ibadah Friendly Hospital Act.**

**Section 2. Definition of Terms.** — As used in this Act—

(a) ‘Ibadah’, (Arabic: عبادة, ‘ibādah, also spelled ibada) is an Arabic word meaning service or servitude. In Islam, ibadah is usually translated as "worship", and ibadat—the plural form of ibadah—refers to Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) of Muslim religious rituals.

(b) ‘Hospital’, means a place or an institution that is built, staffed, and equipped for the diagnosis of disease; for the treatment, both medical and surgical, of the sick and the injured; and for their housing during this process. The modern hospital also often serves as a centre for investigation and for teaching.

(c) ‘Government hospital’, means a hospital operated and maintained either partially or wholly by the national, provincial, municipal or city government or other political subdivision, or by any department, division, board or other agency thereof.

(d) ‘Private hospital’, means one which is privately owned, established and operated with funds raised or contributed through donations, or by private capital or other means, by private individuals, association, corporation, religious organization, firm, company or joint stock association.

(e) ‘Clinic’, means a place in which patients avail of medical consultations or treatments on an out-patient basis. However, any clinic or dispensary where there is at least six beds or cribs or bassinets installed for twenty-four-hour use by patients shall be construed to fall within the definition of a hospital as described in this Act.

(f) ‘Accreditation’, means the action or process of officially recognizing someone as having a particular status or being qualified to perform a particular activity.

**Section 3. Accreditation Agency.** - For purposes of setting standards and accreditation as Ibadah Friendly Hospital (IFH), the Ministry of Health (MOH), in addition to its present mandate, shall act as the accreditation agency.

The Minister of Health shall organize the Ibadah Friendly Hospital Committee (IFHC) to include a representative from a public and private hospitals (hospital administrators), and such personnel from Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ BARMM, to carry out the purposes of this Act without necessarily hiring additional staff to the number of present personnel strength of the Ministry.

**Section 4. Ibadah Friendly Hospital Committee; Duties and Functions.** - The Ibadah Friendly Hospital Committee shall have the following powers and duties:

a. To prescribe Ibadah Friendly Hospital standards and Assessment Tool in consultation with the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ BARMM.
b. To conduct an ocular survey of facilities, government or private, with a view to determine their fitness for Ibadah Friendly Hospital accreditation considering their facilities and physical plant.
c. To provide guidelines for the Ibadah Friendly Hospital accreditation processes for facilities in accordance with the provisions of this Act.
d. To keep a permanent register of accredited hospitals indicating the following;
1. Name of the hospital,
2. Address or location,
3. Classification of the facility,
4. Name of the director or administrator,
5. Ownership,
6. Number of authorized beds and
7. Such other pertinent data to be provided in the form, as may be necessary.
e. To grant, cancel or revoke accreditation certificate and seal in accordance with the provisions of this Act and its IRR.
f. To make periodic inspection of all accredited facilities to check compliance with the standards and make recommendations for the correction of standard deviations noted during inspections.
g. To publish annually, a list of accredited facilities indicating the name, location, type, authorized beds, and name of the director or administrator.
h. To submit periodic reports (quarterly, semi-annual and annual) to the Minister of Health, such reports to include a list of accredited facilities indicating the name, location, bed capacity and name of the director or administrator and make recommendations on the needs or requirements for institutions that do not meet the standards for the Ibadah Friendly Hospital accreditation.

**Section 5. Accreditation Application.** - Application for accreditation shall be submitted to the office of the Ibadah Friendly Hospital Committee at the MOH, using the prescribed form.

Application for accreditation shall be filed with the Ibadah Friendly Hospital Committee on a form prescribed by it. Certification may be issued upon compliance with the provisions of Section 6 hereof and the rules and regulations prescribed by the Committee pursuant to the provisions of this Act.

**Section 6. Minimum Standards of Accreditation.** - In order that a certification can be issued the hospital must have a place of worship, wash area, providing services for a deceased patient, a person responsible for these services, a morgue, attendants and helpers.

Patient wards shall be constructed such that segregation of the sexes is observed.

**Section 6. Standards of Accreditation.** - In order that a certification can be issued, an institution must meet the standards set in the IFH Accreditation standards indicated in the Assessment Tool.

For the partially met standards, the facility will be given ninety (90) days to comply with the recommendations before the final decision can be rendered.

**Section 9. Issuance of Certificate.** - Accreditation certificate shall be issued by the Committee only after an ocular inspection has been conducted by the members of the Committee and certified that the applicant has satisfactorily complied with requisites prescribed in this Act.

The certification shall be renewed every year upon payment of the prescribed fees.

**Section 10. Revocation of Certificate.** - The Committee may cancel, suspend or revoke a certificate already issued for any of the following grounds:

1. Violation of any provision of this Act or of any other existing laws governing the operations of the accredited facility.
2. Violation of rules and regulations prescribed in the implementation of this Act.
3. Failure to make necessary corrections or adjustments within the period required by the Committee in the improvement of facilities and services.

**Section 11. Appeal.** - Any institution whose accreditation has been denied, suspended or revoked shall be entitled to an appeal with the Committee to determine the justifiability of such denial, suspension or revocation of the certification: *Provided*, That the appeal is made within thirty (30) days from the receipt of the order of such denial, suspension or revocation.

**Section 12. Accreditation not transferable.** - The certification of facilities shall not be transferable. The Committee shall be notified of any change in ownership, change of name of the hospital, and transfer of location and in the latter case, an application for a new certification should be submitted.

**Section 13. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** — Within ninety (90) days period, the Committee acting as a certifying body and subject to the approval of the Minister of Health, shall promulgate rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act.

**Section 14. Accreditation Fees.** - Each applicant for the accreditation of a facility shall pay the amount prescribed by the committee.

The amount collected shall be officially receipted by the MOH and shall constitute as a revolving fund for the use of the certifying committee.

**Section 15. Penalties.** - Any person, partnership, association, or corporation who establishes, operates, conducts, manages or maintains a hospital or hospital clinic within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region misleading the general public by presenting itself as Ibadah Friendly Hospital accredited without first obtaining a certification as provided for in this Act or violates any provision hereof shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof shall be liable to a fine of not more than fifty thousand (P50,000.00) pesos for the first offense and not more than one hundred thousand (P100,000.00) pesos for each subsequent offense.

**Section 16. Appropriation.** — The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current year’s appropriation of the MOH. Thereafter, such amount as may be necessary for the continued operation of the committee shall be included in the Annual Bangsamoro Appropriation Act.

**Section 17. Repeal.** - Any regional law or laws or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed.

**Section 18. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
