**AN ACT MANDATING COMMERCIAL AND PUBLIC ESTABLISHMENTS WITHIN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION TO INSTALL HANDHELD BIDET IN ITS RESTROOMS, IMPOSING PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS THEREFORE AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short title.** –This Act shall be known as the “Restroom Handheld Bidet Sprayer Act”.

**SEC 2. Declaration of policy.** – It is the policy of the state to promote health and sanitation allowing hygienic measures of isolating human contact with hazardous wastes generated by excreta, urine, wastewater, solid wastes, and industrial wastes which will be detrimental to human health if left uncontrolled.

The Bangsamoro government uphold the peoples' right to have access to good health, and other social services that promote their well-being.

**SEC 3. Coverage.** – There shall be an installed bidets in the public restrooms of malls, hospitals, cinemas, restaurants, cafes, inns, hotels, airport, terminals, gasoline stations and other similar establishments in every city and municipality across Bangsamoro Region which can be accessed by the general public.

**SEC 4. Lead agency.** – The Ministry of Health shall be the Lead Agency and Ministry of Internal and Local Government (MILG) in the implementation, enforcement, monitoring and evaluation of this Act. MOH shall formulate the proper guidelines and protocols that will be observed in order to ensure compliance.

**SEC 5. Monitoring and evaluation.** – It shall be the duty of the MOH and MILG to oversee the full compliance of this Act and the operation maintenance of the facilities of the covered establishments.

**SEC 6. Violation and penalties.** – For violation of this Act, the following penalties shall be imposed:
a. A stern warning and a 30-day grace period shall be given to all covered establishments for the repair of any bidet spray, upon inspection, found to be non-operative and non-compliant.
b. Upon lapse of the grace period, the following fines and penalties shall be imposed:

1. Private Establishments and Commercial Restrooms:

a. First Violation: A fine of ₱5,000 to ₱10,000.
b. Second Violation: A higher fine of ₱15,000 to ₱25,000.
c. Subsequent Violations: A fine of ₱30,000 to ₱50,000 plus suspension of operation until compliance

2. Government Facilities: - For government offices or agencies, an administrative sanction against the head of the office (warning, reprimand, or suspension for negligence in ensuring compliance) shall be implemented.

The aforementioned fines shall be imposed on the owners, operators and/or managing officers of the malls, hospitals, cinemas, restaurants, cafes, inns, hotels, airport, terminals, gasoline stations and other similar establishments who failed to comply with Section 3 of this Act.

**SEC 7. Compliance.** –All existing establishments shall comply with the provisions of this Act within ninety (90) days from its approval.

**SEC 8. Separability Clause.** –Any portion or provision of this Act that is declared unconstitutional shall not have the effect of nullifying other portions or provisions hereof as long as such remaining portions can still subsist and be given effect in their entirety.

**SEC 9. Repealing Clause.** –All regional laws, ordinances, rules, regulations, other issuances, or parts thereof, which are inconsistent with this Act, are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SEC 10. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation.
