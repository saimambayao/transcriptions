**THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY**

**AN ACT CREATING A BANGSAMORO VETERAN MUJAHIDEEN/ MUJAHIDAT AFFAIRS OFFICE, DEFINING ITS BASIC ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Act of 2022”.

**SECTION 2. Name.** – The office to be created under this Act shall be known as the Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office (BVMAO).

**SECTION 3. Purpose.** – The purpose of this Act is to establish an office that will provide financial, medical, social, and other assistance to the veterans of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) who are underprivileged and have no opportunity for employment because of their old age, lack of the required education, or other requirements for employment.

**SECTION 4. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect the interests of its people and ensure every Bangsamoro in its territorial jurisdiction be provided with the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life. It is also the declared policy of the Bangsamoro government to put in place economic mechanisms tailored to the needs of its people.

**SECTION 5. Definition of Terms.** – As used in this Act, the following terms shall be understood to mean:

(a) “Employment” refers to the state of being employed in any public or private enterprise or organization with expectation of remuneration for the services rendered.

(b) “Qualified Beneficiaries” refers to those who may avail of the benefits, privileges and other assistance granted under this Act;

(c) “Underprivileged” refers to those Veteran Mujahideen and other qualified beneficiaries who have no opportunity for employment because of their old age, lack of the required education, or other requirements for employment, and thus, needs financial, medical, social, and other assistance from the Bangsamoro Government;

(d) “Veteran Mujahideen” refers to those members of the MILF and MNLF who had actually rendered their revolutionary services in the protracted wars for self-determination between the Bangsamoro people and the National Government for at least 20 years between 1969 and 2014 and who are at least fifty-five (55) years of age from the time of the effectivity of this Act;

(e) “Revolutionary services” refers to actual contributions of either MILF or MNLF members in the protracted wars for right to self-determination whose services are categorized by the following:

Category A: refers to Combatants who had an actual engagement in military combat.

Category B: refers to First Aider who had provided medical support during combat.

Category C: refers to Support Group who had provided food and non-food supplies to support combatants.

Category D: refers to Peace Mechanism or those individual members who have contributed in the peace process either through official platform or back-door negotiations.

**SECTION 6. Creation.** – There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office (BVMAO) which shall be placed under the operational supervision and control of the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) as one of its component divisions.

**SECTION 7. Composition and Organizational Structure.** – The Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office shall be headed by a Director II, and shall be composed of the (a) Processing and Validation Division, (b) Benefit Availment Division, and (c) Monitoring Evaluation Division. Each division shall be headed by a Development Management Officer V.

**SECTION 8. Appointment.** - The Executive Director I shall be appointed by the Interim Chief Minister for a term of three (3) years without prejudice for reappointment. Thereafter, the Executive Director I shall be appointed by the Chief Minister for a term of six (6) years without reappointment.

**SECTION 9. Powers and Functions.** – The Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office shall perform the following:

(a) Create a functional and effective inter-agency working group composed of representatives from the Ministry of Social Services; the Ministry of Health; the Ministry of Trade, Investment, and Tourism; the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform; the Ministry of, Basic, Higher, and Technical Education; Ministry of Human Settlement and Development; and other relevant ministries, offices and agencies of the Bangsamoro Government as may be determined under the implementing rules of this Act;

(b) Formulate plans, programs, and policies with the inter-agency working group to accomplish the purpose and objective of this Act;

(c) Establish and maintain an effective and reliable data-gathering and evaluation mechanism for the determination and selection of the qualified beneficiaries under this Act;

(d) Extend assistance, as determined in this Act, to qualified beneficiaries as well as their spouses and minor children;

(e) Maintain an updated list or register of qualified beneficiaries;

(f) Determine other benefits, such as skills, socioeconomic and educational trainings and seminars that may be availed of by the beneficiaries of this Act; and

(g) Perform such other relevant functions as may be provided under the implementing rules of this Act or as may be assigned by the Chief Minister or the Minister of the MPOS.

**SECTION 10. Scope.** – The benefits and privileges provided under this Act shall be available to the underprivileged Veteran Mujahideen as defined in Section 5 of this Act. The monthly cash allowance as provided in paragraphs (a to d) of Section 11 hereof shall be granted to the spouse and children of Veteran Mujahideen within five (5) years from the time of his/her death.

The same benefits and privileges, however, shall not be available to those Veteran Mujahideen who receive benefits and privileges under the decommissioning process as part of the normalization.

**SECTION 11. Benefits and Privileges.** – The underprivileged Veteran Mujahideen shall be entitled to receive or enjoy the following benefits and privileges as provided in Section 5 paragraph of this Act:

(a) For Category A: Ten Thousand Pesos (PhP10,000.00) monthly cash allowance;

(b) For Category B: Eight Thousand Pesos (PhP8,000.00) monthly cash allowance;

(c) For Category C: Six Thousand Pesos (PhP6,000.00) monthly cash allowance;

(d) For Category D: Five Thousand Pesos (PhP5,000.00) monthly cash allowance;

(e) Free medical assistance from any of the BARMM-Ministry of Health supervised facilities, health centers, and hospitals;

(f) Exemption from taxes, fees, and other charges imposed by the Bangsamoro Government; and

(g) Such other benefits and privileges as may thereafter be determined by law.

**SECTION 12. Appropriation.** – The funds necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act shall be charged against the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government.

**SECTION 13. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) in coordination with the Bangsamoro Attorney General’s Office (BAGO) shall formulate the necessary implementing rules and regulations within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act in order to effectively implement the provisions of this Act.

The MPOS shall coordinate with the Central Committee of Moro Islamic Liberation Front and Moro National Liberation Front for the list of qualified and validated beneficiaries.

**SECTION 14. Separability Clause.** – Should any provision of this Act be subsequently declared unconstitutional or illegal, the other provisions not so affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 15. Repealing Clause.** – All regional laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations, or any part thereof, which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SECTION 16. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
