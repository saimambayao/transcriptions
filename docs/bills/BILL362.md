**AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE INTEGRATED BANGSAMORO GREENING PROGRAM, PROVIDING FOR ITS IMPLEMENTATION, INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:

**Section 1. Short Title.** This Act shall be known as the “Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program (IBGP) Act of 2025.”

**Section 2. Declaration of Regional Policy.** It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to pursue sustainable forest management and environmental stewardship as strategic approaches to promote ecological resilience, inclusive development, social justice, and lasting peace. The Bangsamoro Government recognizes forestlands as vital environmental and socio-political assets, particularly in areas historically affected by conflict.

The Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program shall:

(a) Align with the Bangsamoro Organic Law;
(b) Respect cultural, religious, and ancestral rights;
(c) Support the reintegration of decommissioned MILF and MNLF combatants;
(d) Foster equitable economic opportunities, biodiversity conservation, and climate adaptation; and
(e) Promote the shared moral obligation of all Bangsamoro people as caretakers of the environment.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms.**

(a) Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program (IBGP) – a comprehensive regional program for ecological restoration and sustainable land management;

(b) BARMMULA – a region-wide annual tree-growing festival derived from the Maguindanaon word "bamula," meaning "to plant";

(c) Sustainable Forest Management (SFM) – forest management practices that balance environmental, economic, social, and cultural objectives;

(d) Family-Based Greening Approach – a strategy engaging households and communities in managing greening sites;

(e) Community-Based Forest Management (CBFM) – a participatory program empowering local communities to sustainably manage forestlands with government support;

(f) Government Procurement Buy-Back Scheme – a mechanism wherein produce from IBGP sites is purchased for institutional feeding programs;

(g) Convergence Area Development Plan (CADP) – a multi-sectoral, community-driven greening plan harmonized with local government and regional development plans;

(h) Public-Private Partnership (PPP) – a transparent, ethical collaboration between the government and private entities with accountability mechanisms and environmental safeguards;

(i) Climate-Resilient Species – tree and crop varieties adapted to the projected impacts of climate change;

(j) Ecosystem-Based Adaptation (EbA) – the use of biodiversity and ecosystem services to help people adapt to climate change.

(k) Riparian Zones - refer to the interface between land and a river, stream, or other inland waterbody, which serves as a natural buffer that supports biodiversity, prevents soil erosion, enhances water quality, and mitigates the impacts of flooding. These zones are critical ecological corridors and shall be prioritized in the greening and restoration efforts under the IBGP.

**Section 4. Scope and Coverage.** The IBGP shall target lands within BARMM, prioritized as follows:

a. Denuded and degraded forestlands;

b. Production and protection forestlands for sustainable forestry;

c. Mangrove and coastal greenbelts;

d. Ancestral domains (with Free, Prior and Informed Consent);

e. Civil and military reservations, subject to formal agreements;

f. Urban and peri-urban greening sites aligned with Comprehensive Land Use Plans (CLUPs);

g. Watersheds and ecological corridors;

h. Designated Ecoparks and community-managed green spaces;

i. Zones identified in the normalization and decommissioning process.

Prior to any site development, MENRE shall coordinate with the Ministry of Indigenous Peoples’ Affairs and the Ministry of Public Order and Safety to verify land tenure and mediate any disputes arising from overlapping claims.

A phased implementation plan shall be developed within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, prioritizing areas based on ecological vulnerability, peacebuilding potential, and community readiness.

**Section 5. BARMMULA Tree Growing Festival.** The BARMMULA Tree-Growing Festival shall be held annually on the first Friday of June, coordinated by the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy in partnership with local government units and regional stakeholders. Activities shall include tree growing, cultural performances, interfaith blessings, and knowledge exchanges. The festival shall promote ecological stewardship, intergenerational responsibility, and cultural pride.

**Section 6. Governance and Institutional Structure.**

**Section 6.1. Technical Steering Committee.** A Technical Steering Committee shall be established to provide policy guidance, technical oversight, and inter-ministerial coordination for the implementation of the Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program (IBGP). The Committee shall be chaired by the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), and co-chaired by the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG).

The Committee shall be composed of representatives from the following:

*   Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA);
*   Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE);
*   Ministry of Indigenous Peoples’ Affairs (MIPA);
*   Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST);
*   Local Government Units (LGUs);
*   State Universities and Colleges (SUCs);
*   Civil society organizations (CSOs), people's organizations (POs), and faith-based groups;
*   Media and youth sectors;
*   Representatives of the decommissioned Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF), selected through a joint process with the Joint Normalization Committee (JNC) and the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS).

The specific roles, responsibilities, and decision-making processes of the Technical Steering Committee shall be defined in the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) to be developed by MENRE, in consultation with relevant ministries and stakeholders.

**Section 6.2. Greening Technical Secretariat.** A Greening Technical Secretariat shall be established within the Forest Management Services Office of the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) to support the day-to-day coordination and implementation of the Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program.

The Secretariat shall be responsible for planning, budgeting, policy development, data management, monitoring and evaluation, and ensuring public access to program-related information and reports.

**Section 6.3. Inter-Ministerial Oversight Board.** An Inter-Ministerial Oversight Board shall be constituted to ensure transparency, accountability, and performance monitoring of the Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program. The Board shall be composed of representatives from the Commission on Audit (COA), the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), and accredited civil society organizations (CSOs).

The Oversight Board shall conduct annual reviews of the Program’s implementation, including progress, budget utilization, and outcomes. It shall convene annual public hearings to present its findings and solicit feedback from stakeholders. The Board shall submit a comprehensive report of its findings and recommendations to the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Section 7. Strategic Implementation Framework**

**7.1 Institutional Track**

The IBGP shall be mainstreamed into local and regional development plans and shall include:

(a) Development of Convergence Area Development Plans (CADPs) using participatory processes and conflict-sensitive planning;

(b) Institutionalization of Community-Based Forest Management (CBFM) programs that promote tenure security;

(c) All Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) must comply with national and BARMM PPP rules, include environmental and social safeguards, and undergo open competitive bidding;

(d) Establishment of policy learning labs, encouraging adaptive management and innovation;

(e) Deployment of decommissioned Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) members as trained peace and environmental workers in nurseries, community facilitation, and forest protection—under the supervision of MENRE and MPOS and monitored within the normalization program’s monitoring and evaluation framework.

**7.2 Operational Track.** The IBGP shall also include:

(a) Family-Based Greening Approach, prioritising organised cooperatives and vulnerable communities;

(b) Implementation of the “Kayud Ka Bangsamoro” livelihood initiative, combining food security, reforestation, and green enterprise;

(c) Launch of a Buy Back Scheme for agricultural and forestry products, with fixed pricing ceilings, publicly disclosed procurement schedules, and annual audits submitted to the Commission on Audit;

(d) Access to green microfinance, community insurance, and cooperative development support;

(e) Integration of Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) community projects into economic reintegration programs, including training, contracts, and recognition mechanisms.

**Section 8. Community Engagement and Empowerment**

(a) Establish Community Greening Committees per Local Government Unit, with mandated representation of women, youth, Indigenous Peoples, civil society organizations, and decommissioned MILF and MNLF combatants (if applicable);

(b) Conduct sustained Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) campaigns and environmental education;

(c) Promote participatory environmental audits and grievance mechanisms;

(d) Provide recognition to outstanding community initiatives through awards and incentives.

(e) Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) decommissioned combatants and their communities shall be prioritized as beneficiaries of the Family Approach model. MENRE, in partnership with Office of the Presidential Adviser on Peace, Reconciliation and Unity (OPAPPRU) and MILG, shall ensure that reforestation initiatives also contribute to the normalization track of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB), including socioeconomic support and non-recurring development assistance;

(f) Dedicated modules on peacebuilding, reconciliation, and environmental stewardship shall be developed for these communities;

(g) Site development contracts shall be offered to organized groups composed of former combatants, subject to community endorsement and technical evaluation by MENRE and the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS).

**Section 9. Environmental Safeguards and Risk Reduction**

Require Environmental and Social Impact Assessments (ESIA) for major greening activities;

(a) Promotion of native and climate-resilient species;

(b) Avoid invasive species and require native or endemic planting materials;

(c) Align greening sites with Disaster Risk Reduction and Management and Climate Action Plans;

(d) Build nature-based solutions into flood control, landslide risk reduction, and water recharge interventions.

**Section 10. Monitoring, Evaluation, and Data Systems.** An integrated monitoring platform shall be developed, utilizing geotagging and geospatial technologies. Independent performance audits shall be conducted every three years. Monitoring shall include civil society and academic participation, with results reported publicly using key performance indicators aligned with the Bangsamoro Development Plan and the Global Development Goals.

**Section 11. Role of Civil Society, Faith-Based Groups, Media, and Academia**

(a) Civil society organizations and people's organizations shall be recognized as essential partners in the implementation and oversight of the Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program. Their roles shall include mobilizing communities, conducting capacity-building and training activities, participating in program monitoring, and providing independent feedback and reporting.

(b) Faith-based organizations and religious leaders shall be actively engaged in promoting environmental responsibility, drawing from spiritual teachings to reinforce ecological values and foster community-based environmental ethics.

(c) Media organizations, including local and community-based outlets, shall play a key role in raising public awareness, promoting transparency, and amplifying community voices. Special efforts shall be made to support youth-led journalism, environmental advocacy campaigns, and community radio programs that promote civic engagement and peacebuilding through environmental stewardship.

(d) The Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy shall publish an annual “State of the Forests” report detailing accomplishments, challenges, and progress under the Program. This report shall be made publicly available through online platforms and local community forums to ensure transparency and informed public participation.

(e) Academic and research institutions shall be encouraged to contribute by conducting independent assessments, documenting innovations and best practices, and developing educational materials to support environmental learning and policy development.

**Section 12. Economic Sustainability and Livelihood Development**

(a) Promote sustainable forest enterprises, such as bamboo processing, honey production, herbal medicine, and seedling nurseries;

(b) Establish access to marketing support, public procurement, and export linkages;

(c) Provide training in green entrepreneurship, value chain development, and cooperative management;

(d) Ensure economic activities support both household income and ecological restoration.

**Section 13. Education, Scholarships, and Capacity Development**

(a) The Ministry of Science and Technology shall offer scholarships to children of participants in the Integrated Bangsamoro Greening Program, prioritizing degree programs in forestry, environmental science, agroforestry, geology, geodetic engineering, mining engineering, natural resource management, and other related disciplines. The selection process shall be transparent, merit-based, and publicly announced, ensuring the inclusion of marginalized sectors such as Indigenous Peoples, women, and persons with disabilities. All scholarship recipients shall be required to render return service within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao upon graduation, in accordance with guidelines set by the Ministry;

(b) Establish training centers and demo farms for green skills and indigenous practices;

(c) Embed IBGP content into curricula, extension programs, and cultural education modules.

**Section 14. Strategic Planning.** A ten-year Strategic Plan shall be developed and reviewed every three years. A Regional Greening Observatory shall be established for research and monitoring. Annual summits to assess the alignment of the implementation for the long-term goals shall convene stakeholders for dialogue for the purpose that the program is still aligned to regional, national, and global frameworks and priorities.

**Section 15. Funding and Resource Mobilization.** Funds necessary for the implementation of the IBGP for a given year shall be charged against the available budget of MENRE, as appropriated. Funding for subsequent years shall be incorporated into the regular appropriations of the concerned Ministries. Additional sources may include Official Development Assistance (ODA), climate financing mechanisms, and partnerships with the private sector through Public-Private Partnership (PPP) and corporate social responsibility (CSR) programs.

**Section 16. Sustainability Measures.** MENRE may establish a Greening Trust Fund to finance post-planting maintenance, site sustainability, and community livelihood programs under the IBGP.

**Section 17. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, MENRE shall formulate the IRR in consultation with MAFAR, MILG, BPDA, MBHTE, and relevant stakeholders, ensuring participatory and inclusive processes.

**Section 18. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**Section 19. Repealing Clause.** All laws, rules, regulations, or issuances inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 20. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
