**AN ACT CREATING THE ENERGY DEVELOPMENT CORPORATION OF THE BANGSAMORO (EDCB), DEFINING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** — This Act shall be known as the "Charter of the Bangsamoro Energy Development Corporation."

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** — It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote economic development through the effective and efficient utilization of energy sources, for power generation to achieve sustainable development goals. In line with this policy, the establishment of an organized entity is necessary in order to supplant power and energy sources and consequently enable the unimpeded and efficient growth of the economy.

**SECTION 3. Creation of the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro.** — For the purpose of undertaking the development of energy sources utilization and for other purposes specified in this Act, there is hereby created a public corporation which shall be known as the as the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro. The words "EDCB" and "Board" appearing in this Act shall respectively refer to the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro and the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro Board, hereinafter provided. For aforesaid purpose, the EDCB shall have capacity to sue and be sued.

**SECTION 4. Supervision.** - The EDCB under the supervision of the Ministry of Energy and Natural Resources and Economy.

The Minister of Energy and Natural Resources and Economy shall, in a concurrent capacity, be the *ex officio* chairman of the EDCB Board, unless otherwise directed by the Chief Minister: *Provided, That in no case shall the Minister be the chief executive officer or chief operating officer of the EDCB, any law to the contrary notwithstanding.*

**SECTION 5. Office and Term.** - The principal office of the EDCB shall be determined by its Board of Directors. Provided that it be within the Bangsamoro Government Territory. It may establish such offices, agencies, subsidiaries, branches or correspondents in the Philippines or abroad as its business operations would require.

The EDCB shall have a term of fifty (50) years from approval of this Act, which shall be deemed renewed for an equal period unless sooner dissolved by law.

**SECTION 6. Registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission.** – The EDCB through its Board shall register with the Securities Exchange Commission within one (1) year from the constitution of the Board.

**SECTION 7. Definition of Terms.** –

"Energy projects" shall mean activities or projects relative to the exploration, extraction, production, importation-exportation, processing, transportation, marketing, distribution, utilization, conservation, stockpiling, or storage of all forms of energy products and resources.

"Energy resources utilization" shall include exploration, discovery, development, extraction, utilization, refining, processing, transport, and marketing of all forms of energy resources.

"Energy resources" means any substance, mineral or otherwise, which by itself or in combination with other substance or after processing or refining or the application to it of technology emanates, gives off, generates or causes, the emanation or generation of heat or power or energy such as, but not limited to, petroleum or oil, coal, marsh gas, methane gas, geothermal sources of heat and power, uranium and other minerals and fossils deposits.

**SECTION 8. Purposes.** — The EDCB shall have the following purposes:

a. To engage in the exploration, utilization, and development of energy resources within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro;
b. To engage in the transportation, storage, importation, exportation, refining, supply, sale and distribution of crude oil, refined petroleum and petroleum based products, whether imported or produced by local refineries;
c. To foster conditions relating to energy resources utilization conducive to a balanced and sustainable growth of the economy; and
d. To undertake, by itself or otherwise, exploration, utilization, and development of all energy resources of the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro, including surveys and activities related thereto.

**SECTION 9. Powers and Functions of the EDCB.** — The EDCB shall have the following powers and functions:

a. To adopt a code of by-laws to complement this charter;
b. To adopt and use a corporate seal which shall be judicially noticed;
c. To undertake, by itself or otherwise, exploration, utilization and development of all energy resources within the in the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro including surveys and activities related thereto;
d. To establish, maintain, control and direct in any area within the in the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro as it may deem appropriate, and construct, install or maintain therein ports adequate for the use of vessels engaged in offshore oil drilling operations, airports sufficient for direct service flights, telecommunications center and ship-to-shore communications facilities, provide electric power and fresh water supply, and perform such other acts as it may deem necessary and advantageous or convenient to such operations;
e. To lease, at reasonable rates, to private domestic entities or persons within the in the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro such portion or portions of the petroleum and energy base, including facilities necessary for warehousing, logistical centers for the storage of oil drilling and oil well supplies, fabrication of off-shore drilling components and structures, mechanical repair facilities and the like; spaces for the office, habitation and recreational requirements of personnel directly engaged in offshore oil drilling and in manning the various logistical support operations and their immediate dependents;
f. To undertake all other forms other energy resources exploitations;
g. To enter into contracts, with any domestic person or entity, National Governments agencies, or foreign entities following the conditions under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, and as allowed by the Parliament, for the undertaking of the varied aspects of energy resources exploitation including the acquisition, by way of purchase, lease or rent or other deferred payment arrangements of equipment and/or raw materials and supplies, as well as for services connected therewith under such terms and conditions as it may deem proper and reasonable;
h. To borrow money from local and foreign sources as may be necessary for its operations;
i. Any provision of law to the contrary notwithstanding, including but not limited to Section 13 of Act 1459, as amended, to invest its funds as it may deem proper and necessary in any activity related to its purposes, including in any bonds or securities issued and guaranteed by the Bangsamoro Government of the Philippines and the EDCB may organize and incorporate subsidiary corporations for the purpose. The capital stock of the corporations organized and incorporated by the EDCB may be subscribed in whole or in part by the EDCB. Where the EDCB has a controlling interest of not less than fifty-one percent (51%) of the issued and outstanding capital stock of such subsidiaries, the securities, including shares of capital stock issued by the subsidiaries and corporations owned and / or controlled by it, as well as the sale of and / or subscription to such securities and shares if capital stock shall be exempt from registration, licensing or other requirements imposed under the existing laws, decree, order or regulation, Provided, that EDCB has registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission.
j. To purchase, hold, alienate, mortgage, pledge or otherwise dispose of the shares of the capital stock of, or any bond, security of other corporations or associations of this or any other country; and while the owner of said stock, to exercise all the rights of ownership, including the right to vote thereon;
k. To hold lands and acquire rights over mineral lands in excess of the areas permitted to private corporations, associations and persons by statute;
l. To acquire assets, real or personal, or interests therein, and encumber or otherwise dispose the same as it may deem proper and necessary in the conduct of its business;
m. To determine its organizational structure, and the number and salaries of its officers and employees;
n. To establish and maintain offices, branches, agencies, subsidiaries, correspondents or other units within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro as may be needed by the EDCB and reorganize or abolish the same as it may deem proper;
o. To exercise the right of eminent domain for properties within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro, as may be necessary for the purpose for which the EDCB is created;
p. Subject to payment of the proper amount, to enter private lands for the purpose of conducting geological or geophysical studies in connection with energy resources, exploration and exploitation;
q. To acquire easement over public and private lands within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro necessary for the purpose of carrying out any work essential to its energy resources operations and exploitation, subject to payment of just compensation;
r. To establish and maintain technical educational system for the sustained development of the necessary manpower to manage and operate its affairs and business;
s. To perform such acts and exercise such functions as may be necessary for the attainment of the purposes and objectives herein specified; and
t. To perform such other functions as may be provided by law.

**SECTION 10. Governing Body.** — The EDCB shall be governed by the EDCB Board of Directors. The Board which shall be composed of nine (9) members, to be appointed by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government of the Philippines. The members of the Board shall serve for a term of three (3) years or until their successors shall have been appointed and qualified. In case of any vacancy in the Board, the same shall be filled by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government for the unexpired term.

No person shall be appointed as member of the Board unless he is a natural born citizen of the Philippines, at least thirty-five (35) years of age and of established integrity. The Chairman of the Board, who shall be the chief executive officer of the EDCB as well as the President of the EDCB, shall be appointed by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government. The other officers of the EDCB shall be appointed by the Board.

**SECTION 11. Capital Stock.** - The EDCB shall have a capital stock divided into ten million no-par shares to be subscribed, paid for the voted as follows:

a. One million shares of stock shall be originally subscribed and paid for by the Bangsamoro Government at an original issue value of P50 per share.
b. The remaining five million shares of stock may be subscribed and paid for by the Bangsamoro Government or by Bangsamoro Government financial institutions at values to be determined by the Board, but in no case less than the original issued value above stated to the Bangsamoro Government.
c. The voting power pertaining to shares of stock subscribed by the government of the Bangsamoro Government shall be vested in the Chief Minister or in such person or persons as the Parliament may designate.
d. The voting power pertaining to shares of stock subscribed by the government financial institutions shall be vested in them.

**SECTION 12. Duties and Responsibilities of the Chairman of the Board and President** — The Chairman of the Board and the President, shall exercise such powers and perform such duties as may be provided in the By-Laws or as may be vested in them by the Board.

**SECTION 13. Issuance of Bonds.** — The EDCB, upon the recommendation of the Minister of Finance and Budget and Management and with the approval of the Chief Minister, is hereby authorized to issue bonds or other securities, which may be guaranteed by the Bangsamoro Government, to finance its operations.

**SECTION 14. General Counsel.** - The EDCB shall establish a legal division which shall perform the duties of General Counsel until the creation of the office of the Solicitor General of the Bangsamoro Government. Any provision of law to the contrary notwithstanding, the General Counsel and such personnel as may be necessary to assist him in the performance of his duties and responsibilities shall receive such allowances as shall be fixed by the Board.

**SECTION 15. Auditor.** — Any provision of law to the contrary notwithstanding, the shall appoint, subject to the approval of the Board, a representative who shall be the Auditor of the EDCB and such personnel as may be necessary to assist said representative in the performance of his duties. The salaries of the Auditor and his staff shall be approved by the Board. The Auditors of corporations owned or controlled by the EDCB shall be reputable accounting and auditing firms shall be appointed by their respective boards of directors.

**SECTION 16. Appointment, Control and Discipline of Personnel** - Provision of law to the contrary notwithstanding, the "Board", upon recommendation of the President of the EDCB, shall appoint the officers and employees of the EDCB and its subsidiaries; fix their compensation, allowances and benefits, their working hours and such other conditions of employment as it may deem proper; grant them leaves of absence under such regulations as it may promulgate, discipline and or remove them for cause; and establish and maintain a recruitment and merit system for the EDCB and its affiliates and subsidiaries.

The organizational framework and staffing pattern of the EDCB shall be prescribed and approved by the Chief Minister within sixty (60) days after the approval of this Act and the authorized positions created therein shall be filled by regular appointments by the Chief Minister, subject to existing Bangsamoro Government laws, rules and regulation.

**SECTION 17. Loans.** —The Bangsamoro Government, through the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government, or his duly authorized representative, may guarantee, absolutely and unconditionally, as primary obligor, the payment of the loans, credits and indebtedness secured by the EDCB or any of its affiliate corporations, for the amount which the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government has been authorized by its Parliament subject to limitations under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, as well as the performance of all or any of the obligations undertaken by the EDCB or its affiliate corporations in the territory of the Bangsamoro Government pursuant to loan agreements entered into with foreign governments or any international financial institutions or fund sources.

**SECTION 18. Bangsamoro Government Financial Institutions Guarantees.** — The provision of any law to the contrary notwithstanding, any financial institution owned or controlled by the Bangsamoro Government other than social insurance institutions, if any, are empowered to guarantee acceptance credits, loans, transactions, undertakings, or obligations of any kind which may be incurred by the EDCB, whether directly or indirectly, in favor of any person, association or entity, whether domestic or foreign.

**SECTION 19. Privileges and Incentives.** — The EDCB shall be entitled to all the incentives and privileges granted by existing laws within the within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro, to private enterprises engaged in energy operations.

Other Bangsamoro Government offices and other government-owned-or-controlled corporations created under the Bangsamoro Government shall extend whatever assistance may be needed by the EDCB or any of its affiliates or subsidiaries, including the detail of its officials and employees to the EDCB or any of its subsidiaries on full time or part-time basis under arrangement satisfactory to the EDCB and its affiliates and subsidiaries and the other office or corporation concerned. Said officials and employees, as well as the directors of the EDCB and its affiliates and subsidiaries, may receive allowances and other emoluments notwithstanding the provision of any law to the contrary.

The employees of the EDCB shall be entitled to all the retirement and insurance benefits and leave privileges of a Bangsamoro Government employee. However, subsidiaries of the EDCB, organized to undertake purely business ventures shall not, as a matter of right, be subject to the retirement privileges, regular working hours, and other government employee benefits effective within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro.

**SECTION 20. Appropriations.** — For the initial capital requirements of the EDCB, the sum of one-hundred fifty Million Pesos is hereby set aside and appropriated from the Bangsamoro Appropriations, not otherwise appropriated.

**SECTION 21. Reports.** — The EDCB shall, submit a quarterly report to the Bangsamoro Parliament, for the first five (5) years of operations, and within three months after the end of every fiscal year, submit its annual report to the Chief Minister. It shall likewise submit such periodic or other reports as may be required of it from time to time.

**SECTION 22. Separability Clause.** — Should any provision of this Decree be held unconstitutional, no other provision hereof shall be affected from time to time.

**SECTION 23. Effectivity.** — This Act shall take effect immediately.
