## EXPLANATORY NOTE

Republic Act No. 12199, otherwise known as the "Early Childhood Care and Development Act," was signed into law on May 8, 2025, repealing Republic Act No. 10410 or the "Early Years Act (EYA) of 2013." This landmark legislation establishes a comprehensive national framework for the care and development of children from conception to eight years of age, recognizing this period as the most critical stage in human development that lays the foundation for lifelong learning, health, and well-being.

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), established under Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), possesses the constitutional authority to enact legislation on matters within its exclusive and concurrent legislative powers. Article IX of the BOL grants the Bangsamoro Parliament the power to enact laws governing the educational system in the region, while Article XII empowers the regional government to establish programs for social welfare and child development.

The unique context of the Bangsamoro region necessitates a regional ECCD law that localizes and operationalizes the national framework while incorporating culturally appropriate adaptations. The Bangsamoro people possess a distinct cultural identity rooted in Islamic faith and indigenous traditions that must be reflected in early childhood programs. Young Bangsamoro children deserve an ECCD system that nurtures their development while affirming their identity as Muslims and as members of their respective ethnolinguistic communities, whether Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug, Sama, Yakan, or other groups.

The situation of young children in the Bangsamoro region demands urgent attention. Data from various sources indicate that children in the region face significant challenges, including higher rates of malnutrition, lower access to early childhood education services, and exposure to the effects of armed conflict and displacement. Many communities, particularly in geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas, lack Child Development Centers and qualified ECCD service providers. The transition from home to formal schooling remains difficult for many children who have not had access to quality early childhood programs. The 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 (BDP), particularly Chapter 9 on Social Services, identifies early childhood development as a priority area under Goal 4 ("Inclusive, responsive, and quality social services") and targets improved access to ECCD programs across the region.

This proposed legislation seeks to establish a comprehensive Bangsamoro ECCD System that addresses these challenges while building on the strengths of Bangsamoro families and communities. The bill creates a Bangsamoro ECCD Coordinating Council to provide policy direction and coordinate the efforts of various ministries and stakeholders. It establishes the framework for a culturally responsive curriculum that integrates Islamic values, Arabic language familiarization, and Bangsamoro cultural heritage. It provides for the professionalization of ECCD service providers and the establishment of Child Development Centers in every barangay. It also includes special provisions for children with disabilities, children in conflict-affected areas, and children in geographically isolated communities.

This bill builds upon and harmonizes with existing Bangsamoro legislation, including Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18 (Bangsamoro Education Code), which assigns the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education the power to adopt educational plans for early childhood care and education, and Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 (Bangsamoro Local Government Code), which mandates the creation of Barangay Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Committees (BECCDCCs) and Municipal Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Committees (MECCDCCs).

The enactment of this bill will demonstrate the commitment of the Bangsamoro Government to investing in its youngest citizens, ensuring that every child in the region has the opportunity to reach their full potential. This is not merely a matter of education policy but a fundamental investment in the future of the Bangsamoro people and the success of the autonomous region.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of this measure is earnestly sought.

## CHAPTER I — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**SECTION 1.** *Short Title.* — This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Early Childhood Care and Development Act."

**SEC. 2.** *Declaration of Policy.* — It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to safeguard and promote the right of every child in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to holistic well-being, growth, and dedicated care, recognizing the age-appropriate stages of development from conception to eight (8) years of age. The Bangsamoro Government commits to creating nurturing environments for children that ensure a healthy and sustainable program for nutrition, age-appropriate development, and special protection with full recognition of the nature of childhood.

The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the primacy of parents and parent-substitutes in the care and development of their children, and shall support them in their roles as primary caregivers and educators. The Bangsamoro Government further recognizes the importance of family, extended kinship networks, and community in child development, consistent with Bangsamoro cultural values and Islamic principles.

The Bangsamoro Government shall institutionalize a comprehensive, integrative, and sustainable Bangsamoro Early Childhood Care and Development System that involves multisectoral and interagency collaboration among regional ministries, local government units, families, and communities, in coordination with the national ECCD Council established under Republic Act No. 12199. The ECCD System shall be anchored on strategies that include service delivery for children below five (5) years of age, educating and training parents and parent-substitutes on responsible caregiving, encouraging their active involvement in ECCD programs, and raising awareness about the importance of quality early childhood experiences for young children and families.

Pursuant to Republic Act No. 11650 or the "Instituting a Policy of Inclusion and Services for Learners with Disabilities in Support of Inclusive Education Act," the Bangsamoro ECCD System shall promote the inclusion of children with disabilities, where they shall be accorded the services stipulated under the law and provided with reasonable accommodation and accessible environments, with respect for cultural and linguistic diversity.

**SEC. 3.** *Objectives.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall pursue the following objectives:

(a) Reduce infant and child mortality rates in the Bangsamoro region, and subsequently eliminate preventable deaths, by ensuring that adequate health and nutrition programs are accessible to young children and their parents and parent-substitutes, from the prenatal period throughout the early childhood years;

(b) Enhance the physical-motor, socio-emotional, cognitive, language, and spiritual development of infants and young children through culturally responsive and developmentally appropriate programs and services;

(c) Facilitate a seamless transition to, and ensure that young children are adequately prepared for, the formal learning system that begins at kindergarten, consistent with the goals of the regional basic education system;

(d) Establish an efficient system for early identification, prevention, referral, and intervention for children below five (5) years of age with special needs, using culturally appropriate assessment tools and methods;

(e) Reinforce the role of parents and parent-substitutes as primary caregivers and educators of their children especially those below five (5) years of age, with recognition of their rights and responsibilities under Islamic law and Bangsamoro customs;

(f) Improve the quality standards of public and private ECCD programs in the Bangsamoro region through recognition, accreditation, and capacity building for service providers;

(g) Ensure that special support is provided in the delivery of ECCD programs and services for the poor, disadvantaged, children in conflict-affected areas, minority communities, and children with disabilities, accommodated through the most appropriate languages and means of communication, in environments that maximize academic and social development;

(h) Employ teachers, including teachers with disabilities, who are qualified to manage young children with developmental delays and disabilities, and train professionals and staff who work at all levels of education;

(i) Integrate Islamic values, Bangsamoro cultural heritage, and indigenous knowledge systems in ECCD programs, including the use of regional languages as media of instruction and the introduction of Arabic language familiarization; and

(j) Establish mechanisms for coordination with the national ECCD Council and relevant national government agencies in the implementation of ECCD programs in the Bangsamoro region.

**SEC. 4.** *Definition of Terms.* — As used in this Act:

(a) *Bangsamoro ECCD System* — refers to the full range of health, nutrition, early childhood education, and social services development programs in the Bangsamoro region that provide for the basic holistic needs of young children below five (5) years of age, and promote their optimum growth and development;

(b) *Child Development Center (CDC)* — refers to a facility in a barangay, municipality, city, or province where children below five (5) years of age are cared for during part of the day by ECCD service providers to deliver ECCD programs and services;

(c) *ECCD Curriculum* — refers to the developmentally appropriate educational objectives and practices, programs of activities, organized learning experiences, recommended learning materials, and appropriate assessment for children below five (5) years of age that are aimed towards the seamless transition to, and adequate preparation for, the formal learning system;

(d) *ECCD Services* — refers to the services delivered in implementing ECCD programs for the early childhood education, health, nutrition, and social development of young children below five (5) years of age;

(e) *ECCD Service Providers* — refers to the various professionals, paraprofessionals, and volunteer caregivers who are directly responsible for the care and education of young children below five (5) years of age through center-based, home-based, and other alternative modalities, including but not limited to Child Development Teachers (CDTs), Child Development Workers (CDWs), teacher aides, rural health midwives, social workers, community health workers, barangay nutrition scholars, Parent Effectiveness Service volunteers, parents, parent-substitutes, newborn to pre-school caregivers, and play facilitators;

(f) *Child Development Teacher (CDT)* — refers to a person who has completed a bachelor's degree, preferably with units in early childhood education, and has passed the Board Licensure Examination for Professional Teachers (BLEPT), responsible for implementing the ECCD curriculum and managing Child Development Centers;

(g) *Child Development Worker (CDW)* — refers to a person who has completed at least senior high school education and has passed the certification by the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), who assists the CDT in implementing ECCD programs and services;

(h) *Center-based Modalities* — refers to ECCD service delivery approaches provided in Child Development Centers, day care centers established under Republic Act No. 6972, child-minding centers, workplace-related childcare facilities, early childhood education programs in madaris, and health centers;

(i) *Home-based Modalities* — refers to ECCD service delivery approaches provided in homes and family settings, including neighborhood-based play groups, family childcare programs, parent education, and home visiting programs;

(j) *Parent Education* — refers to various formal and alternative means of providing parents and parent-substitutes with information, skills, and support systems to assist them in their roles as their children's primary caregivers and educators;

(k) *Culturally Responsive Practice* — refers to ECCD approaches that respect, value, and integrate the cultural backgrounds, languages, traditions, and beliefs of Bangsamoro children and families into all aspects of early childhood programs;

(l) *Conflict-Affected Areas* — refers to communities within the Bangsamoro region that have experienced or are currently experiencing armed conflict, displacement, or other forms of violence that affect the safety, security, and well-being of children and families; and

(m) *Geographically Isolated and Disadvantaged Areas (GIDAs)* — refers to communities in the Bangsamoro region that are physically and socio-economically separated from the mainstream society, characterized by physical inaccessibility, limited access to basic services, and vulnerability to risks.

**SEC. 5.** *Scope and Coverage.* — This Act shall apply to all ECCD programs and services within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, covering children from conception to eight (8) years of age. Without limiting the primary right and duty of parents to rear their children, the Bangsamoro ECCD Coordinating Council shall be responsible for children below five (5) years of age, while the responsibility to help develop children in the formative years between age five (5) to eight (8) years shall be with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) consistent with Republic Act No. 10533 or the "Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013." This Act shall apply to both public and private ECCD service providers operating within the Bangsamoro region.

## CHAPTER II — THE BANGSAMORO ECCD SYSTEM

**SEC. 6.** *Establishment of the Bangsamoro ECCD System.* — There is hereby established a comprehensive, integrative, and sustainable Bangsamoro Early Childhood Care and Development System that shall operate in coordination with the national ECCD System established under Republic Act No. 12199. The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall be implemented in accordance with Philippine laws, the policies of the national ECCD Council, and the regulations of the Bangsamoro ECCD Coordinating Council. The System shall include the delivery of complementary and integrated programs and services for good health, adequate nutrition, quality early childhood education, safety and security, responsive caregiving, opportunities for learning, well-being, sanitation, and cultural activities. The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall build upon and harmonize with the provisions of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18 (Bangsamoro Education Code) and Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 (Bangsamoro Local Government Code) relating to early childhood care and development.

**SEC. 7.** *Components of the Bangsamoro ECCD System.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall include the following components:

(a) *Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum* — The curriculum shall focus on children's holistic development, take into account age, individual and socio-cultural appropriateness, and utilize developmentally appropriate practice, such as play, as its core component. It shall include programs and activities designed to prepare a child for formal schooling and facilitate a seamless transition from a home and community environment to a classroom setting. The curriculum shall integrate Islamic values, Bangsamoro cultural heritage, and indigenous games. The use of the mother tongue as the primary medium of instruction shall be ensured, consistent with Republic Act No. 10533 or the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013, with Arabic language familiarization introduced at appropriate developmental stages;

(b) *Parent Education and Involvement, Advocacy, and Mobilization of Communities* — This component shall harness and develop parents' strengths as providers of ECCD programs at home, active partners of other stakeholders, advocates for community concerns that affect children, and pillars of support for local and regional ECCD programs through community organization efforts. The Bangsamoro ECCD Coordinating Council shall promote awareness of all laws on the rearing, health, and development of the child, breastfeeding, maternal and reproductive health, and other laws related thereto. The Council shall also integrate a referral mechanism for parents to relevant government agencies with programs related to the rearing, health, and development of the child, as well as maternal and reproductive health;

(c) *Integrated Nutrition and Health Component* — This component shall prioritize the utmost welfare of the child and integrate the different programs and services for the nutrition and health of children below five (5) years of age, lactating mothers, and pregnant women under various laws and policies, including Republic Act No. 11148 or the "Kalusugan at Nutrisyon ng Mag-Nanay Act," Republic Act No. 11037 or the "Masustansyang Pagkain para sa Batang Pilipino Act," Republic Act No. 10354 or "The Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Act of 2012," and Republic Act No. 10028 or the "Expanded Breastfeeding Promotion Act of 2009;"

(d) *Human Resource Development Component* — This component shall establish mechanisms for the systematic professionalization of CDTs through enrollment in educational programs, pre-service or in-service training, or continuing education programs. It shall likewise provide enabling conditions for the competency assessment, certification, reskilling, and upskilling of CDWs and other service providers, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education (B-CHED), the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), and recognized higher education institutions in the Bangsamoro region; and

(e) *ECCD Management, Monitoring, and Evaluation* — This component shall consist of a continuing process of planning, implementation, supervision, financial management, monitoring, evaluation, and reporting to persons concerned and shall encourage the active involvement and build the capabilities of service providers, parents, and local government officials to sustain the program.

**SEC. 8.** *Program Modalities.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall be implemented through the following modalities:

(a) Center-based modalities, such as day care service established under Republic Act No. 6972 or the "Barangay-Level Total Development and Protection of Children Act," community or faith-based early childhood education programs including those in madaris, NGO or people's organizations-initiated programs, workplace-related childcare and education programs, child-minding centers, and health centers and stations;

(b) Home-based modalities, such as neighborhood-based play groups, family childcare programs, parent education, and home visiting programs; and

(c) Other alternative modalities that provide innovations, including those to be employed during and after disaster and emergency situations, for the development, effective implementation, and continuous delivery of ECCD services, conforming to the needs of their respective barangays and communities.

## CHAPTER III — INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK

**SEC. 9.** *Bangsamoro ECCD Coordinating Council.* — There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Council, hereinafter referred to as the "Bangsamoro ECCD Council" or the "Council," which shall be the primary policy-making and coordinating body for ECCD in the Bangsamoro region. The Council shall be attached to the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) for administrative purposes.

**SEC. 10.** *Composition of the Council.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall be composed of the following:

(1) Minister of the Interior and Local Government, as Chairperson;
(2) Minister of Basic, Higher and Technical Education, as Vice Chairperson for ECCD Curriculum and all matters related to early childhood education;
(3) Executive Director of the Bangsamoro ECCD Council Secretariat, as ex officio Vice Chairperson;
(4) Minister of Social Services and Development, as ex officio member;
(5) Minister of Health, as ex officio member;
(6) Minister of Finance, Budget and Management, as ex officio member;
(7) Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education, as ex officio member;
(8) Director of the TESDA Regional Office, or the head of its successor agency in BARMM, as ex officio member;
(9) Executive Director of the National Nutrition Council Regional Office, as ex officio member;
(10) One (1) representative from the League of Provinces in BARMM;
(11) One (1) representative from the League of Cities and Municipalities in BARMM;
(12) One (1) early childhood education expert or practitioner from the academe; and
(13) One (1) representative from accredited civil society organizations working on children's issues in the region.

The Council shall meet quarterly or upon the call of the Chairperson or three (3) members of the Council. The members of the Council who are not ex officio members shall be appointed by the Chief Minister and shall serve for a term of three (3) years.

**SEC. 11.** *Functions of the Council.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall carry out the objectives of the Bangsamoro ECCD System by performing the following functions:

(a) Formulate regional policies and guidelines in consultation with LGUs and other stakeholders, and promulgate and implement such policies and guidelines, consistent with the objectives and system framework as defined in this Act and the national policies of the ECCD Council established under Republic Act No. 12199;

(b) Establish standards and review the Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum every two (2) years to facilitate the seamless transition of young children to, and their adequate preparation for, the formal learning system that begins at kindergarten, in accordance with relevant laws and regulations;

(c) Develop regional standards for the recruitment of qualified CDTs and CDWs, and develop programs for the continuing education, reskilling, and upskilling of CDTs, CDWs, and other service providers, supervisors, and administrators, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education, TESDA, and recognized higher education institutions in the Bangsamoro region;

(d) Issue minimum requirements for the establishment of center-based and home-based modalities, and other alternative modalities that provide innovations, and the conversion of existing day care centers into CDCs;

(e) Administer the recognition and accreditation of public and private ECCD programs within the Bangsamoro region;

(f) Provide guidelines for streamlining the delivery of ECCD services, taking into consideration the number of young children being serviced by each CDC, the physical accessibility of CDCs, and the availability of funds and resources of the LGUs;

(g) Develop a system of awards and recognition for deserving ECCD program implementers and service providers within the Bangsamoro region;

(h) Conduct regular independent audits on the delivery of services to ECCD program beneficiaries in the Bangsamoro region;

(i) Conduct research and policy studies on ECCD, evaluate and assess the impact of various ECCD programs, and report the outcome to the Bangsamoro Parliament through an effective information system;

(j) Recommend to the Bangsamoro Parliament amendatory legislation to enhance the provisions and improve the implementation of this Act;

(k) Develop a regional system for registration, early identification, screening, and monitoring of young children below five (5) years of age under ECCD programs with the aim of universal access while ensuring an interoperable network of information systems with the MBHTE;

(l) Develop various support mechanisms and sustainable financing models for scaling quality and effective ECCD programs that maximize public and private resources, giving priority to the needy and high-risk children from poor and disadvantaged communities;

(m) Promote and encourage private sector initiatives for the establishment of ECCD programs;

(n) Provide guidelines for the conduct of solicitations from local and international civic organizations, and request private foundations to supplement available resources;

(o) Promote ECCD programs to stakeholders, targeting all communities with special consideration for at-risk children and families, through information, education, and communication campaigns, with the aim of increasing the attendance of children below five (5) years of age in such programs;

(p) Coordinate with the national ECCD Council and relevant national government agencies on the implementation of ECCD programs in the Bangsamoro region;

(q) Attend meetings called by the national ECCD Council and participate in the formulation of national ECCD policies and programs; and

(r) Perform such other functions as the Council may deem necessary for the effective implementation of this Act.

**SEC. 12.** *Bangsamoro ECCD Council Secretariat.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council Secretariat, to be headed by an Executive Director, shall be organized to provide administrative and technical support services for the coordination and monitoring of the implementation of policies and plans formulated by the Council. The Executive Director shall be appointed by the Chief Minister upon recommendation of the Council and shall have a fixed term of five (5) years, unless earlier removed for cause. The Executive Director shall have the rank, privileges, and emoluments of an Undersecretary. The Council shall fix the staffing pattern of the Secretariat and determine the qualifications, functions, and compensation scheme for the positions to be created, subject to existing laws, rules and regulations.

**SEC. 13.** *Responsibilities of Bangsamoro Ministries.* — The following Bangsamoro ministries shall have the following responsibilities in the implementation of the Bangsamoro ECCD System:

(a) The Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) shall ensure that all local government units are implementing the ECCD System and complying with the provisions of this Act. It shall include capacitating the LGUs in implementing the ECCD System and delivering the ECCD programs and services;

(b) The Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) shall recognize early childhood education as the foundation of the learning continuum and shall promote it for all children below five (5) years of age. It shall serve as the lead ministry for the development and review of the Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Darul Ifta' (BDI) on matters relating to Islamic values integration;

(c) The Ministry of Health (MOH), the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD), the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education, the National Nutrition Council Regional Office, and TESDA Regional Office shall provide continuing professional development program support, supplementary learning materials, reference materials, supplemental nutrition, and healthcare services;

(d) The MBHTE, TESDA, and LGUs, through the MILG, shall coordinate on the promotion of early childhood education careers to senior high school students and graduates. They shall also facilitate the assessment of graduates for certification as CDWs, and ensure their seamless progression to employment; and

(e) The MSSD shall coordinate with LGUs in the delivery of social welfare services to young children and their families, including supplementary feeding programs, family support services, and child protection programs.

**SEC. 14.** *Responsibilities of Local Government Units.* — LGUs, through their ECCD Offices as provided under Section 15 of this Act, shall be primarily responsible for the implementation of the ECCD System, including programs and services in their respective jurisdictions. LGUs shall include allocations from their Special Education Fund (SEF) and Gender and Development (GAD) Fund in addition to other local funds to fulfill this primary responsibility. Specifically, LGUs shall:

(1) Aim to achieve universal ECCD access by identifying young children, including the poor, the vulnerable, and the marginalized, and supporting their parents or parent-substitutes within their respective jurisdictions;

(2) Submit their respective roadmaps to the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, to be updated every three (3) years, towards the achievement of the goal of universal ECCD access;

(3) Issue and implement policies based on regional policies formulated by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, and ensure compliance with the minimum requirements for the establishment of center-based and home-based modalities, and other alternative modalities that provide innovations;

(4) Provide the facilities and resources for the effective delivery of the programs and services under the ECCD System. They shall establish or convert existing day care centers to CDCs and maintain them according to standards and specifications set by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council: *Provided*, That the LGUs shall ensure that there is at least one (1) CDC in every barangay. They shall also implement home-based modalities and other alternative modalities that provide innovations, conforming to the needs of their respective barangays;

(5) Create plantilla positions for CDTs and CDWs pursuant to Section 23 of this Act;

(6) Recruit, hire, and ensure the professional development of ECCD service providers, including but not limited to CDTs and CDWs, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education, the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE), TESDA, and other relevant government agencies;

(7) Supervise the service providers and monitor the delivery of the ECCD programs and services;

(8) Administer the recognition and accreditation of private ECCD programs in accordance with guidelines issued by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council;

(9) Conduct data collection, maintenance, and harmonization of the existing databases relevant to ECCD, in accordance with the parameters or metrics given by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council. The submission of reports to the Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall be done annually;

(10) Endeavor to augment their local funds from private sources, both local and international, for the implementation of the ECCD System;

(11) Implement, in coordination with the MSSD, feeding programs for children below five (5) years of age, in accordance with Republic Act No. 11037;

(12) Formulate and implement a localized comprehensive emergency program for young children during disasters and other emergency situations, prioritizing the needs of pregnant women, and young children and their parent-substitutes, in accordance with Republic Act No. 10821 or the "Children's Emergency Relief and Protection Act";

(13) Develop innovative arrangements and partnerships with private ECCD service providers through supporting the operations of community-based, home-based, and other alternative modalities of ECCD service delivery;

(14) Engage parents, parent-substitutes, government agencies, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders in the delivery of the ECCD programs and services;

(15) Promote ECCD programs to stakeholders through information, education, and communication campaigns, with the aim of increasing the attendance of children below five (5) years of age in such programs;

(16) Provide for the training of parents and parent-substitutes, as well as barangay health workers and nutrition scholars, on early stimulation, care, growth, and development, especially for children below three (3) years of age, consistent with the programs on health and nutrition under various laws which include Republic Act No. 11148 and Republic Act No. 11037; and

(17) Attend meetings called by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council.

**SEC. 15.** *Creation of ECCD Offices in LGUs; Organizational Structure and Staffing Pattern.* — Every province, city, and municipality in the Bangsamoro region shall create an ECCD Office, and design and implement its own organizational structure and staffing pattern taking into consideration its service requirements and financial capability, subject to the standards and guidelines prescribed by the Civil Service Commission (CSC). The ECCD Office, which shall be under the administrative supervision of the local chief executive, shall manage the delivery of the programs and services under the ECCD System within its jurisdiction and its daily operations, including the supervision of CDTs and CDWs in the performance of their duties and responsibilities.

The ECCD Office shall closely collaborate with the local councils for the protection of children and local school boards to ensure the coordination and harmonization of plans, programs, and activities, and facilitate a seamless transition of children under five (5) years of age to a classroom setting, with the utmost welfare of the child as the paramount consideration.

The ECCD Office shall also require the CDTs and CDWs to coordinate with the barangay health workers and barangay nutrition scholars for the monitoring of the nutrition and health of children under five (5) years of age, and the delivery of ECCD programs and activities pertinent to nutrition and health, including the delivery of ECCD programs and services under various laws. The ECCD Office shall participate in the development of the local nutrition action plan and render technical support to the LGU and the National Nutrition Council, and shall harmonize its local nutrition database with the national nutrition information system as provided under Section 6 of Republic Act No. 11037.

The ECCD Office shall coordinate with the existing Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Committees mandated under Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 (Bangsamoro Local Government Code), including the Barangay ECCD Coordinating Committee (BECCDCC) and the Municipal ECCD Coordinating Committee (MECCDCC), to ensure harmonization and avoid duplication of functions.

**SEC. 16.** *Provincial and Municipal ECCD Committees.* — There shall be created in every province, city, and municipality a Provincial, City, or Municipal ECCD Committee, as the case may be, to serve as the local coordinating body for ECCD programs and services. Where a Municipal ECCD Coordinating Committee (MECCDCC) has already been established pursuant to Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49, the committee created under this section shall subsume the functions of the MECCDCC. The Committee shall be chaired by the local chief executive or an authorized representative, with the head of the local ECCD Office as the secretary. Members shall include the local health officer, the local social welfare and development officer, the schools division superintendent or representative, representatives from private ECCD service providers, representatives from parent organizations, and representatives from civil society organizations working on children's issues. The Committee shall meet at least quarterly to coordinate ECCD implementation, monitor program performance, and address issues and concerns in ECCD service delivery.

**SEC. 17.** *Barangay ECCD Focal Persons.* — Every barangay in the Bangsamoro region shall designate an ECCD focal person who shall coordinate ECCD programs at the barangay level. The ECCD focal person shall work with CDTs and CDWs assigned to the barangay, mobilize community support for ECCD, maintain barangay-level ECCD data, and facilitate referrals of children and families to health and social welfare services. The barangay ECCD focal person shall report to the municipal or city ECCD Office and shall coordinate with the Barangay ECCD Coordinating Committee (BECCDCC) established under Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49.

## CHAPTER IV — THE BANGSAMORO ECCD CURRICULUM

**SEC. 18.** *Development of the Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum.* — The MBHTE, in coordination with the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, shall develop a Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum that meets national standards established by the national ECCD Council while incorporating regional cultural content. The curriculum shall be developed in consultation with ECCD practitioners, parent organizations, religious scholars (*ulama*), the Bangsamoro Darul Ifta' (BDI), and cultural experts. The curriculum shall undergo review every two (2) years to ensure continued relevance and alignment with national standards and the evolving needs of Bangsamoro children.

**SEC. 19.** *Curriculum Framework.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum shall be anchored on a framework that covers all developmental domains:

(a) Physical-motor development, including gross motor skills, fine motor skills, and health and safety awareness;

(b) Cognitive development, including problem-solving, logical thinking, and early numeracy and literacy;

(c) Language development, including listening, speaking, pre-reading, and pre-writing in the mother tongue, with familiarization with Arabic and Filipino/English;

(d) Socio-emotional development, including self-awareness, self-regulation, social skills, and emotional expression; and

(e) Values and spiritual development, including the inculcation of Islamic values, Bangsamoro cultural values, respect for diversity, and appreciation of one's identity.

The curriculum framework shall emphasize developmentally appropriate practice, play-based learning, and culturally responsive pedagogy. It shall include programs and activities that prepare children for formal schooling while respecting their developmental readiness and individual differences.

**SEC. 20.** *Integration of Islamic Values and Bangsamoro Cultural Content.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Curriculum shall integrate Islamic values, Bangsamoro cultural heritage, and indigenous knowledge systems appropriate to early childhood. This shall include familiarization with basic Islamic teachings and practices suitable for young children, such as simple supplications (*du'a*), basic *akhlaq* (good character), and awareness of Islamic traditions. The curriculum shall also incorporate Bangsamoro cultural content, including traditional games (e.g., *sipa*, *tumbang preso*, indigenous Bangsamoro games), songs, stories, arts, and oral literature from various Bangsamoro ethnolinguistic groups. The integration of cultural content shall promote children's sense of identity and belonging while fostering respect for diversity.

**SEC. 21.** *Language of Instruction.* — The mother tongue shall be the primary medium of instruction in ECCD programs, consistent with Republic Act No. 10533 or the Mother Tongue-Based Multilingual Education (MTB-MLE) policy. Recognizing the linguistic diversity of the Bangsamoro region, the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, in coordination with the MBHTE, shall:

(a) Support the development of learning materials in the major languages of the Bangsamoro region, including but not limited to Maguindanaon, Maranao, Tausug, Sama, Yakan, Iranun, and other languages spoken in the region;

(b) Provide training for ECCD service providers in the use of mother tongue as medium of instruction;

(c) Allow the introduction of Arabic language familiarization, including simple vocabulary and basic scripts, at appropriate developmental stages and with appropriate instructional time allocation; and

(d) Ensure that children from minority language groups within the Bangsamoro region have access to ECCD programs that respect their linguistic rights.

## CHAPTER V — SERVICE PROVIDERS AND HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT

**SEC. 22.** *ECCD Service Providers.* — The following shall be recognized as ECCD service providers in the Bangsamoro region:

(a) Child Development Teachers (CDTs);
(b) Child Development Workers (CDWs);
(c) Teacher aides;
(d) Rural health midwives;
(e) Social workers;
(f) Community health workers;
(g) Barangay nutrition scholars;
(h) Parent Effectiveness Service volunteers;
(i) Parents and parent-substitutes serving as home-based caregivers;
(j) Newborn to pre-school caregivers;
(k) Play facilitators; and
(l) Such other service providers as may be recognized by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council.

**SEC. 23.** *Professionalization of CDTs; National Competency Assessment of CDWs; Certification, Reskilling, and Upskilling of Incumbent CDWs; Scholarships for Continuing Education.* — There shall be at least one (1) CDT and one (1) CDW for every CDC, and every province, city, and municipality shall create plantilla positions pursuant to Section 7(b)(5) of Republic Act No. 12199.

The guidelines for the salary grades (SGs), eligibility and qualifications, and duties and responsibilities, among others, shall be issued by the local chief executive in consultation with and as approved by the CSC and the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM): *Provided*, That the SGs shall not be lower than SG 11 for CDTs, SG 10 for CDWs with an associate degree, and SG 8 for the other CDWs: *Provided, further*, That a CDT shall be a holder of any bachelor's degree, preferably with units in early childhood education, and has passed the Board Licensure Examination for Professional Teachers (BLEPT): *Provided, furthermore*, That a CDW shall be a holder of at least an associate degree in early childhood education, at least a senior high school graduate and has passed the national competency assessment for CDWs, or a high school graduate who has finished two (2) years of tertiary education and has passed the same assessment by the TESDA for CDWs: *Provided, finally*, That a CDT or a CDW (a) must not have been convicted by final judgment of an offense or crime involving moral turpitude; (b) must not have a pending case in relation to Republic Act No. 11930 or the "Anti-Online Sexual Abuse or Exploitation of Children (OSAEC) and Anti-Child Sexual Abuse or Exploitation Materials (CSAEM) Act"; Republic Act No. 9208 or the "Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2003," as amended; or Republic Act No. 7610 or the "Special Protection of Children Against Abuse, Exploitation and Discrimination Act," as amended; and (c) must not be included in the child sex offender registry list.

Incumbent CDWs shall complete mandatory reskilling and upskilling training programs on ECCD or early childhood education to be provided by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, and pass a certification by the TESDA. TESDA shall provide such assessment and certification free of charge.

The Bangsamoro ECCD Council, in collaboration with the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education and CHED, shall ensure the availability of ECCD training programs across the region, the formulation of degree and associate programs in early childhood education, and the recognition of prior learning and credit transfer mechanisms.

Further, the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, in collaboration with the Bangsamoro Commission on Higher Education, shall develop a scholarship program for the continuing education of CDWs who shall complete their bachelor's degrees and be required to take the BLEPT within an agreed period of time. The scholars shall be required to fulfill an obligation to return service in the CDCs for such reasonable period as may be determined by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council.

**SEC. 24.** *Professional Development and Continuing Education.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall establish a comprehensive professional development program for ECCD service providers in the region. This shall include pre-service training, in-service training, mentoring and coaching, professional learning communities, and opportunities for advanced education. Training programs shall address both general ECCD competencies and specialized topics relevant to the Bangsamoro context, including culturally responsive practice, working with children affected by conflict, inclusive education for children with disabilities, and the integration of Islamic values in early childhood programs. The Council shall maintain a registry of accredited training providers and ensure that professional development opportunities are accessible to service providers throughout the region, including those in remote and underserved areas.

## CHAPTER VI — CHILD DEVELOPMENT CENTERS

**SEC. 25.** *Establishment of Child Development Centers.* — Every barangay in the Bangsamoro region shall have at least one (1) Child Development Center. LGUs shall establish new CDCs or convert existing day care centers to CDCs meeting the standards and specifications set by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council. Where establishing a CDC in a barangay is not feasible due to population size, geographic conditions, or other valid reasons, the LGU shall provide for alternative arrangements such as clustering of CDCs for multiple barangays, home-based modalities, or mobile ECCD units, subject to approval by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council.

**SEC. 26.** *Standards for Child Development Centers.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall establish minimum standards for CDCs in the region, consistent with national standards, which shall include:

(a) Physical infrastructure standards, including building safety, adequate space per child, proper ventilation and lighting, accessibility for children with disabilities, and provision for prayer and ablution facilities appropriate to the cultural and religious needs of the community;

(b) Learning environment standards, including age-appropriate furniture, developmentally appropriate learning materials, indoor and outdoor play areas, and provision for rest areas;

(c) Health and safety standards, including sanitation and hygiene facilities, safe water supply, first aid provisions, emergency evacuation plans, and child protection protocols;

(d) Staffing standards, including adult-child ratios appropriate to the age groups served, qualifications of service providers, and supervision requirements; and

(e) Administrative standards, including record-keeping, reporting requirements, and governance structures.

**SEC. 27.** *Registration and Accreditation of CDCs and ECCD Programs.* — All CDCs and ECCD programs, whether public or private, operating in the Bangsamoro region shall be registered with the appropriate ministry or LGU as determined by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council. Public CDCs shall be registered with the municipal or city ECCD Office, while private CDCs and ECCD programs shall be registered with the MSSD or MBHTE as appropriate. The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall establish a voluntary accreditation system to recognize CDCs and ECCD programs that meet higher quality standards beyond the minimum requirements. Accredited programs shall be entitled to incentives as may be determined by the Council. Operating an unregistered CDC or ECCD program shall be subject to administrative sanctions as provided in this Act.

## CHAPTER VII — SPECIAL PROGRAMS AND POPULATIONS

**SEC. 28.** *Children with Disabilities.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall ensure that children with disabilities have equitable access to quality ECCD programs with appropriate accommodations and support services, consistent with Republic Act No. 11650 or the "Instituting a Policy of Inclusion and Services for Learners with Disabilities in Support of Inclusive Education Act." The regional ECCD system shall include early identification, screening, and referral services for children with developmental delays and disabilities using culturally appropriate assessment tools; early intervention programs and services; inclusive classroom practices in CDCs; provision of assistive devices and technologies; specialized training for ECCD service providers on inclusive education; and parent education and support for families of children with disabilities. The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall coordinate with the MBHTE and MSSD to ensure seamless transition of children with disabilities from ECCD programs to inclusive basic education.

**SEC. 29.** *Children in Conflict-Affected Areas.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall address the unique needs of children in conflict-affected communities within the region through flexible service delivery modalities that can adapt to security conditions; psychosocial support programs for children and families affected by conflict and displacement; rapid restoration of ECCD services following displacement or disruption; coordination with humanitarian organizations and peace-building initiatives; peace education integrated into the ECCD curriculum; and trauma-informed care training for ECCD service providers. LGUs in conflict-affected areas shall develop contingency plans for the continuation of ECCD services during emergencies and shall prioritize the restoration of ECCD programs in post-conflict recovery efforts.

**SEC. 30.** *Children in Geographically Isolated and Disadvantaged Areas.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall provide for alternative ECCD modalities for children in geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDAs) where establishing conventional CDCs may not be feasible. These may include mobile ECCD units that bring services to remote communities on a scheduled basis; home-based programs with regular visits by trained ECCD workers; community-based programs in non-traditional spaces such as community halls, mosques, or homes of community members; use of technology-enabled learning supports where infrastructure permits; and intensive parent education programs to strengthen home-based early stimulation and care. The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall develop guidelines for the implementation of alternative modalities in GIDAs and shall ensure that children in these areas receive services that meet quality standards appropriate to their context.

**SEC. 31.** *Emergency ECCD Response.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD System shall establish protocols for continuing ECCD services during emergencies, consistent with Republic Act No. 10821 or the "Children's Emergency Relief and Protection Act." LGUs shall develop local ECCD emergency response plans that shall include identification of temporary learning spaces and child-friendly spaces; emergency feeding programs for young children; psychosocial first aid and ongoing support; mechanisms for tracking and reunifying children separated from families; coordination with local disaster risk reduction and management councils; and pre-positioning of emergency ECCD supplies and materials. ECCD service providers shall receive training on emergency preparedness and response, including psychological first aid for children and families.

## CHAPTER VIII — FINANCING

**SEC. 32.** *Financing ECCD Programs.* — ECCD programs in the Bangsamoro region shall be financed through a combination of public and private funds. All public ECCD program providers shall prioritize children below five (5) years of age from families who are in greatest need and who can least afford private ECCD programs. The following shall be the sources of funds for ECCD programs:

(a) *Block Grant Allocation* — The annual block grant provided to the Bangsamoro Government under the BOL shall include allocations for social services, which shall encompass ECCD programs. The Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM) shall ensure that adequate funds are allocated for ECCD in the annual regional budget;

(b) *National Government Allocations* — The MILG, MOH, MBHTE, MSSD, and TESDA shall support the implementation of the ECCD System by providing financial and technical support packages to ECCD programs through adopting a program convergence budgeting approach to promote the efficient and effective allocation and management of fiscal resources for ECCD programs and services. Allocations from the Local Government Support Fund under the annual General Appropriations Act shall include a line-item allocation to support the establishment of CDCs and the hiring of CDTs and CDWs, including addressing other human resource needs, in fourth (4th) and fifth (5th) class municipalities;

(c) *Local Government Allocations* — LGUs shall support public ECCD programs in their respective localities. Additional funds may be generated from intergovernmental donors and government financial institutions to support the public programs including the urban poor;

(d) *ECCD Program Contracting Scheme* — There shall be established a fund for an ECCD Program Contracting Scheme with accredited private service providers at the community levels;

(e) *Fees and Contributions* — Fees and contributions collected for both public and private ECCD programs shall be monitored by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council to ensure that these are affordable and within reasonable limits. Families are encouraged to contribute their time and services to support the ECCD programs; and

(f) *Islamic Endowment (Waqf)* — The Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Darul Ifta' and the MFBM, may establish or receive endowments (*waqf*) dedicated to the support of ECCD programs, consistent with the provisions of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18 (Bangsamoro Education Code) on the establishment of endowment funds for education. Waqf properties and funds dedicated to ECCD shall be administered in accordance with Islamic law and applicable regulations.

**SEC. 33.** *Bangsamoro ECCD Fund.* — There is hereby established a Bangsamoro ECCD Fund as a special fund for financing ECCD programs in the region. The Fund shall receive appropriations from the annual regional budget as may be determined by the Bangsamoro Parliament; a portion of the block grant allocated for social services as may be determined by the MFBM; contributions from LGUs; and donations from private sources, both local and international. The Fund shall be administered by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council through its Secretariat, subject to auditing by the Commission on Audit. The Council shall issue guidelines on the utilization, disbursement, and accountability requirements for the Fund.

**SEC. 34.** *Incentives for Private Sector Participation.* — Partnership between the Bangsamoro Government and the private sector catering to the diverse needs of children below five (5) years of age shall be encouraged. Any donation from the private sector in favor of public CDCs for the provision of the necessary educational assistance, facilities, materials, and other appropriate support and related services for children below five (5) years of age shall be entitled to the benefits and incentives provided under Section 5 of Republic Act No. 8525 or the "Adopt-a-School Act of 1998," allowing an additional deduction from the gross income equivalent to fifty percent (50%) of such expenses. Valuation of assistance other than money shall be based on the acquisition cost of the property taking into consideration its depreciated value in case said property has already been used. They may also be entitled to the benefits of the first paragraph of Section 6 of Republic Act No. 8525.

## CHAPTER IX — COORDINATION WITH THE NATIONAL GOVERNMENT

**SEC. 35.** *Coordination with the National ECCD Council.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall maintain a formal relationship with the national ECCD Council established under Republic Act No. 12199. The Bangsamoro region shall be represented in the meetings and activities of the national ECCD Council. The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall participate in the formulation of national ECCD policies and guidelines, ensuring that the interests and circumstances of the Bangsamoro region are considered. The annual workshop mandated under Republic Act No. 12199 shall include dedicated sessions addressing the specific needs and circumstances of the Bangsamoro region. Memoranda of agreement shall be executed between the Bangsamoro ECCD Council and the national ECCD Council to formalize coordination arrangements.

**SEC. 36.** *Role of the Intergovernmental Relations Body.* — Matters arising from the implementation of this Act that require resolution between the Bangsamoro Government and the National Government shall be referred to the Intergovernmental Relations Body (IGRB) established under Article VII, Section 4 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law. The IGRB shall address matters such as the interpretation of national standards in the regional context; allocation of resources from national ECCD programs to the Bangsamoro region; resolution of conflicts between national and regional regulations; and capacity building and technical assistance from national agencies.

**SEC. 37.** *Coordination with National Agencies.* — The Bangsamoro ministries with ECCD responsibilities shall coordinate with their national counterpart agencies to ensure harmonized implementation of ECCD programs. This coordination shall cover policy harmonization to ensure regional policies are consistent with national policies; technical assistance and capacity building from national agencies; resource sharing including access to national training programs, materials, and expertise; data sharing and reporting to national information systems; and joint monitoring and evaluation of ECCD programs. Joint implementing guidelines shall be developed between Bangsamoro ministries and national agencies to ensure seamless coordination in ECCD implementation.

## CHAPTER X — QUALITY ASSURANCE, MONITORING, AND EVALUATION

**SEC. 38.** *Quality Standards.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall establish quality standards for ECCD programs in the region that are consistent with national standards while reflecting Bangsamoro cultural values. Standards shall cover structural quality, including facilities, materials, staffing ratios, and qualifications; process quality, including curriculum implementation, teacher-child interactions, and classroom practices; and outcome quality, including child development outcomes, school readiness, and family satisfaction. The Council shall develop quality assessment tools and protocols appropriate for the Bangsamoro context and shall conduct regular quality assessments of ECCD programs in the region.

**SEC. 39.** *ECCD Indicators in the Seal of Good Local Governance.* — The MILG shall incorporate ECCD indicators into the Seal of Good Local Governance (SGLG) or its equivalent assessment criteria under the social protection and sensitivity program, health compliance and responsiveness, and programs for sustainable education, as provided under Section 7(c), (d), and (e) of Republic Act No. 11292 or "The Seal of Good Local Governance Act of 2019," to promote and recognize LGUs commitment to ECCD. The MILG, together with the Bangsamoro ECCD Council, shall jointly develop the indicators and formulate incentives and programs to support LGUs.

**SEC. 40.** *Bangsamoro ECCD Information Management System.* — There is hereby established a Bangsamoro ECCD Information Management System to collect, store, analyze, and report data on ECCD programs and child development outcomes in the region. The system shall be developed and maintained by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council Secretariat in coordination with relevant ministries and LGUs. The system shall be interoperable with the national ECCD database under the MBHTE and DepEd. Data collected shall include enrollment and attendance of children in ECCD programs; child development and school readiness assessments; service provider qualifications and professional development; CDC facilities and resources; program expenditures and financing; and such other data as may be required by the Council. Data privacy safeguards consistent with Republic Act No. 10173 or the "Data Privacy Act of 2012" shall be incorporated in the system.

**SEC. 41.** *Annual Report and Mandatory Evaluation and Review.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council, at the close of each calendar year, shall submit annual physical and financial reports to the Bangsamoro Parliament, giving a detailed account of its proceedings and accomplishments during the year. On the fifth (5th) year of the implementation of this Act and every five (5) years thereafter, the Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall conduct a mandatory review and submit a report to the Bangsamoro Parliament with recommendations for the adoption of measures that will improve the ECCD System and ensure achievement of universal access of ECCD benefits to all children below five (5) years of age. In its report, the Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall provide comprehensive information on the baseline data, targets, and the success indicators related to its implementation.

**SEC. 42.** *Parliamentary Oversight.* — The appropriate committee of the Bangsamoro Parliament shall conduct oversight of the implementation of this Act. The committee shall conduct regular hearings on ECCD programs and receive annual reports from the Bangsamoro ECCD Council. The committee may conduct investigations in aid of legislation and recommend measures to improve the implementation of the Bangsamoro ECCD System.

## CHAPTER XI — PROHIBITED ACTS AND PENALTIES

**SEC. 43.** *Prohibited Acts.* — The following acts are prohibited under this Act:

(a) Operating an unregistered Child Development Center or ECCD program;

(b) Employing unqualified persons as CDTs or CDWs;

(c) Using corporal punishment or psychological abuse against children in ECCD programs;

(d) Discriminating against children with disabilities or denying them access to ECCD programs without valid justification;

(e) Misappropriating or misusing funds intended for ECCD programs;

(f) Violating child safety and protection protocols established by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council;

(g) Operating a CDC or ECCD program that does not meet minimum standards after due notice and opportunity to comply;

(h) Collecting excessive or unauthorized fees from families of children enrolled in public ECCD programs; and

(i) Such other acts as may be determined by the Bangsamoro ECCD Council through appropriate regulations.

**SEC. 44.** *Administrative Sanctions.* — Violations of this Act and its implementing rules and regulations shall be subject to the following administrative sanctions:

(a) For first offense: Written warning and directive to correct the violation within a specified period;

(b) For second offense: Suspension of registration or accreditation for a period not exceeding six (6) months;

(c) For third offense: Revocation of registration or accreditation and permanent closure of the non-compliant facility; and

(d) For violations involving child abuse, exploitation, or endangerment: Immediate suspension and referral for criminal prosecution.

The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall promulgate rules on the investigation of violations and the imposition of administrative sanctions, ensuring due process for all parties. Appeals from administrative sanctions may be filed with the appropriate courts.

**SEC. 45.** *Penal Provisions.* — Any person who commits acts of child abuse, exploitation, or endangerment in connection with ECCD programs shall be prosecuted and penalized under Republic Act No. 7610 or the "Special Protection of Children Against Abuse, Exploitation and Discrimination Act," as amended, and other applicable laws. The imposition of administrative sanctions under this Act shall not preclude criminal prosecution for the same acts.

## CHAPTER XII — MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

**SEC. 46.** *Implementing Rules and Regulations.* — The Bangsamoro ECCD Council, in consultation with relevant ministries, LGUs, and stakeholders, shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations (IRR) of this Act within ninety (90) days from its effectivity. The IRR shall be published in a newspaper of general circulation and in the Official Gazette of the Bangsamoro. The IRR issued pursuant to this section shall take effect thirty (30) days after its publication in a newspaper of general circulation.

**SEC. 47.** *Transitory Provisions.* — The following transitory provisions shall apply:

(a) All existing day care centers established under Republic Act No. 6972 and other ECCD programs operating in the Bangsamoro region at the time of the effectivity of this Act shall register with the appropriate ministry or LGU within one (1) year from the effectivity of the IRR;

(b) Incumbent Day Care Workers who do not meet the qualification requirements for CDWs under this Act shall be given a period of three (3) years from the effectivity of this Act to comply with such requirements;

(c) The Bangsamoro ECCD Council shall be organized within six (6) months from the effectivity of this Act;

(d) Until the Bangsamoro ECCD Council is fully organized, the MILG, in coordination with the MBHTE and MSSD, shall exercise the functions of the Council on an interim basis; and

(e) In the event that the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) is reorganized, split, or renamed by subsequent legislation, all references in this Act to the MBHTE shall be deemed to refer to the successor ministry or agency that assumes responsibility for basic education in the Bangsamoro region.

**SEC. 48.** *Appropriations.* — The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current year's appropriations of the concerned ministries. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro. LGUs shall likewise allocate funds from their annual budgets for the implementation of this Act.

**SEC. 49.** *Separability Clause.* — If any provision of this Act or the application thereof to any person or circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of the Act, and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.

**SEC. 50.** *Repealing Clause.* — All laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations, and other issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly. In particular, the provisions of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 relating to the Barangay Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Committee (BECCDCC) and the Municipal Early Childhood Care and Development Coordinating Committee (MECCDCC) shall be deemed modified to the extent necessary to harmonize with the institutional framework established under this Act.

**SEC. 51.** *Effectivity.* — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro region and in the Official Gazette of the Bangsamoro.

**ADOPTED.**
