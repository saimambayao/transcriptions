# Glossary of Supervisory Terms

**Accountability** — The practice of honoring commitments, maintaining standards, and accepting consequences for actions. In the BARMM context, accountability is grounded in both organizational policy and the Islamic principle of amanah (trustworthiness). See Chapter 12.

**Adl (Justice)** — The Islamic principle of fairness and justice. Requires supervisors to apply the same standards to all staff, make decisions based on evidence rather than relationships, and ensure equitable access to opportunities. See Chapter 1.

**ADDRESS IT** — A 7-step methodology for producing Complete Staff Work: Analyze context, Define and Deliberate the problem, Research needed information, Explore options, Submit the report, Sustain implementation, Iterate and improve. See Chapter 14.

**ACSCCRaPATFEE** — The 12-competency framework for CSW practitioners: Analytical and Critical Thinking, Creativity and Innovation, Strategic Thinking, Communication, Collaboration and Teamwork, Research and Analysis, Problem-Solving and Decision Making, Attention to Details and Quality, Time and Attention Management, Flexibility and Adaptability, Emotional Intelligence and Accountability, Ethics and Integrity. See Chapter 14.

**Amanah (Trustworthiness)** — The Islamic principle of holding a trust. Supervisors hold the trust of their position, their staff, and the public. Using authority for personal benefit or failing to fulfill supervisory obligations betrays this trust. See Chapters 1 and 12.

**Authority Level** — The degree of decision-making power granted to a staff member when work is delegated. Five levels range from "research and report back" (Level 1) to "decide and act without reporting" (Level 5). See Chapter 11.

**BDP (Bangsamoro Development Plan)** — The comprehensive development plan guiding BARMM governance priorities. The current plan covers 2023-2028. Individual and team goals should cascade from BDP priorities. See Chapter 5.

**BOL (Bangsamoro Organic Law)** — Republic Act 11054, the foundational law establishing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. Defines the governance structure, powers, and mandates of the Bangsamoro government.

**BTA (Bangsamoro Transition Authority)** — The interim governing body for BARMM during the transition period, preceding the election of the regular Bangsamoro government.

**Calibration Session** — A meeting among peer supervisors to align performance rating standards. Each supervisor presents proposed ratings and supporting evidence. Peers challenge inconsistencies. Ensures a "Very Satisfactory" in one division means the same as in another. See Chapter 13.

**Career Pathing** — Helping staff see a trajectory — where they are, where they can go, and what they need to get there. Includes vertical (promotion), lateral (transfer), functional (specialization), and project-based paths. See Chapter 8.

**Check-In** — A regular, structured one-on-one conversation between supervisor and staff member covering progress, barriers, priorities, and development. Follows the 4-step protocol: Roles, Goals, Progress/Coaching, Next Steps. See Chapter 6.

**Coaching** — A structured process that builds self-awareness, develops skills, and sustains behavior change. Follows the 5-element cycle: Self-Awareness, Motivation, Ongoing Feedback and Support, Application, Accountability. Different from feedback (which identifies the gap) and mentoring (which focuses on career development). See Chapter 4.

**Complete Staff Work (CSW)** — The research, analysis, and recommendation prepared by staff so that a decision-maker can decide. The standard of quality: the decision-maker should be able to approve, modify, or reject the recommendation based solely on the submitted document. See Chapter 14.

**CSC (Civil Service Commission)** — The central personnel agency of the Philippine government responsible for civil service rules, merit-based employment, and administrative discipline.

**Delegation** — Assigning work to staff with clarity on the task, purpose, authority level, resources, and deadline. Distinct from empowerment (the broader philosophy) and from abdication (assigning without monitoring). See Chapter 11.

**Empowerment** — A leadership philosophy that creates conditions for staff to take ownership, make decisions, and drive results. Built through clear boundaries, tolerated learning mistakes, shared information, and recognized initiative. See Chapter 11.

**Feedback** — Specific, timely, behavior-focused communication about a person's actions and their impact. Uses the SBI model (Situation-Behavior-Impact). Distinct from coaching (which builds capability) and evaluation (which assigns a rating). See Chapter 3.

**Hisab** — The Islamic concept of being called to account. Applies to supervisors on two levels: organizational accountability (to the institution and public) and spiritual accountability (answering for those under your care). See Chapter 12.

**IDP (Individual Development Plan)** — A written agreement defining a staff member's development goals, activities, support, and timeline. Updated annually or after significant assessment events. See Chapter 8.

**IPCR (Individual Performance Commitment and Review)** — The individual-level performance plan under the CSC's Strategic Performance Management System (SPMS). Contains specific targets cascaded from the office OPCR. Serves as the basis for annual performance evaluation.

**Mentoring** — A long-term developmental relationship where a senior professional guides a junior professional on career development, institutional knowledge, and professional growth. Driven by the mentee's agenda. Distinct from coaching (performance-focused, supervisor-driven). See Chapter 8.

**Micromanagement** — Excessive supervisory control over how staff do their work. Reviewing every email, dictating methods, requiring excessive updates. Reduces staff initiative, autonomy, and development. See Chapter 11.

**Motivation (Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic)** — Intrinsic motivation comes from purpose, mastery, autonomy, and belonging. Extrinsic motivation comes from external rewards and consequences. Intrinsic motivation drives sustained performance for complex knowledge work. See Chapter 9.

**OPCR (Office Performance Commitment and Review)** — The office-level performance plan under the SPMS. Contains the collective targets for the division, section, or office. Individual IPCRs should cascade from the OPCR.

**PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)** — A structured agreement between supervisor and staff member defining what must improve, by when, with what support, and what consequences follow if improvement does not occur. A support tool, not a punishment. See Chapter 7.

**PPR (Performance, Potential, Readiness)** — A 3-step assessment framework. Performance: how the person performs in the current role (results + behaviors). Potential: capacity for growth into a larger role. Readiness: whether the person has the skills for a specific new role right now. See Chapter 2.

**Progressive Discipline** — A graduated system of consequences: verbal warning, written warning, formal charges, suspension or dismissal. Each stage requires documentation and due process. Follows CSC RRACCS procedures for formal stages. See Chapter 7.

**Psychological Safety** — An environment where team members feel safe to admit mistakes, ask questions, disagree with the supervisor, and take interpersonal risks without fear of punishment or humiliation. Built through the supervisor's response to vulnerability. See Chapter 9.

**Rahmah (Compassion)** — The Islamic principle of mercy and compassion. Requires supervisors to recognize the human context of performance, distinguish between patterns and incidents, and treat staff with dignity even when delivering corrective feedback. See Chapter 1.

**Reverse Delegation** — When a staff member pushes a delegated task back to the supervisor. Sounds like: "I am not sure what to do — can you just handle it?" Addressed by coaching through the obstacle rather than taking the work back. See Chapter 11.

**RRACCS (Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service)** — The CSC rules governing formal disciplinary proceedings in government. Specify due process requirements: written complaint, notice, opportunity to respond, investigation, decision, and appeal. See Chapter 7.

**SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)** — The feedback model used throughout this guidebook. Situation: when/where. Behavior: what the person did (observable). Impact: the effect of the behavior. Keeps feedback specific, factual, and actionable. See Chapter 3.

**Shura (Consultation)** — The Islamic principle of seeking counsel before making decisions. Requires supervisors to consult staff before decisions that affect them. Does not require consensus but requires genuine input. See Chapter 1.

**Sidq (Truthfulness)** — The Islamic principle of honesty. Requires supervisors to give honest feedback, report accurate progress, admit ignorance when appropriate, and refrain from inflating performance assessments. See Chapter 1.

**SMART Goals** — Goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. The standard for individual performance targets in the IPCR. See Chapter 5.

**SPMS (Strategic Performance Management System)** — The CSC's performance management framework for government agencies. Uses OPCR and IPCR instruments with a defined rating cycle and scale. See Chapter 13.

**START/CONTINUE/STOP** — A reflection framework. START: what behavior should begin. CONTINUE: what behavior should persist. STOP: what behavior should cease. Used for individual self-reflection, staff development, and team exercises. See Chapter 3.

**Stretch Assignment** — A task that pushes a staff member beyond their current capability. Designed to develop new skills through real work. Requires coaching support to prevent failure. See Chapter 8.

**Succession Planning** — Identifying critical roles and developing potential successors. For every critical position, at least one person should be developing toward readiness. See Chapter 8.

**Supervision Cycle** — The four-phase cycle that structures the supervisor's work: Plan (set goals, assign work), Monitor (check-ins, feedback), Evaluate (assess performance), Improve (coach, develop, address underperformance). See Chapter 1.

**Tuckman's Model** — A team development framework with four stages: Forming (new team, polite, cautious), Storming (conflicts emerge), Norming (roles settle, collaboration improves), Performing (team operates effectively). Each stage requires different leadership. See Chapter 9.

**4-Quadrant Coaching Guide** — A matrix plotting performance (high/low) against potential (high/low) to determine the appropriate supervisory intervention for each staff member. See Chapter 2.

**7-Element Assignment Checklist** — The standard for delegating work: What, Why, Who, When, How, Authority Level, Resources. See Chapter 11.

**70-20-10 Guideline** — A guideline for development planning: 70% learning from experience (on-the-job), 20% from relationships (mentoring, coaching), 10% from formal learning (training, courses). See Chapter 8.
