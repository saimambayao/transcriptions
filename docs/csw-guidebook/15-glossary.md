# Glossary of CSW Terms

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Complete Staff Work Guidebook for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

---

**ACSCCRaPATFEE** — The 12-competency framework for CSW practitioners: Analytical and Critical Thinking, Creativity and Innovation, Strategic Thinking, Communication, Collaboration and Teamwork, Research and Analysis, Problem-Solving and Decision Making, Attention to Details and Quality, Time and Attention Management, Flexibility and Adaptability, Emotional Intelligence and Accountability, Ethics and Integrity. Developed by the author as a companion to the ADDRESS IT methodology.

**ADDRESS IT** — The seven-step Complete Staff Work methodology: **A**nalyze the Context, **D**efine and **D**eliberate the Problem or Need, **R**esearch Needed Information, **E**xplore Options, **S**ubmit Proposal or Report, **S**ustain Efforts to Implement, **I**terate and **I**mprove. The mnemonic framework structures CSW from analysis through implementation and continuous improvement.

**Approval Chain** — The hierarchical sequence of reviewers and endorsers a CSW output must pass through before reaching the final decision-maker. Typically: analyst, section head, division chief, director, minister (or equivalent).

**BAA (Bangsamoro Autonomy Act)** — Legislation enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament. BAAs are the laws of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Referenced in CSW for legal basis and compliance requirements.

**BDP (Bangsamoro Development Plan)** — The master planning document for BARMM. The BDP 2023-2028 articulates six development goals, eight core strategies, and specific targets. CSW outputs should demonstrate alignment with BDP priorities.

**BEP (Bangsamoro Expenditure Program)** — The annual budget document submitted by the Bangsamoro Government to Parliament for appropriation. The BEP details budget allocations by agency, program, and expenditure class.

**BOL (Bangsamoro Organic Law)** — Republic Act No. 11054, the foundational law establishing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. The supreme law of BARMM, against which all CSW recommendations must be checked for consistency.

**BTA (Bangsamoro Transitional Authority)** — The interim government of BARMM, serving until the first regular elections. The BTA exercises both legislative (Parliament) and executive (Chief Minister and MOAs) functions.

**Budget Briefer (BB)** — A 13-section CSW output (BB01-BB13) used to analyze an agency's budget during Parliament's appropriation process. Covers agency mandate, development goals, organizational outcomes, budget comparison, PPAS analysis, and recommendations.

**Call to Action** — The concluding section of a CSW output that states exactly what the decision-maker is asked to approve or decide. Must be specific and actionable.

**Capital Outlay (CO)** — Budget category for acquisition of assets: land, buildings, equipment, infrastructure, and other fixed assets with useful life beyond one year.

**CBA (Cost-Benefit Analysis)** — A systematic method for comparing the total costs and total benefits of each option. Used in ADDRESS IT Step 4 (Explore Options) to assess value and return on investment.

**COA (Commission on Audit)** — The constitutional body that examines, audits, and settles all government accounts. CSW outputs must anticipate COA requirements and build audit-ready documentation.

**Complete Staff Work (CSW)** — A study of a problem that includes a recommended action by staff personnel, carried to the point where all that remains for the head of office is to approve or disapprove. The professional standard mandated by MC 72/2019 and MC 2/2022.

**CSW Type** — One of nine categories of CSW output: Legislative Analysis Briefer (LA), Budget Briefer (BB), Policy Recommendation (PR), Legal Opinion (LO), Project Proposal (PP), Personnel/Administrative Action (PA), Supervision System (SV), General CSW (GN), or Custom CSW (CU).

**Decision-Maker** — The individual with authority to approve or disapprove the CSW recommendation. May be a committee chairperson, minister, director, or head of office, depending on the institutional context.

**Evaluation Criteria** — The five standards against which CSW options are assessed: Feasibility, Effectiveness, Efficiency, Sustainability, and Alignment with organizational goals.

**Executive Summary** — A one-page overview of a CSW output capturing the problem, recommended solution, expected benefits, and action requested. Always written last, after all other sections are complete.

**Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa Diagram)** — A root cause analysis tool that organizes potential causes of a problem into categories (People, Process, Policy, Resources, Environment, Data). Used in ADDRESS IT Step 2 (Define and Deliberate).

**Five Whys (5 Whys)** — A root cause analysis technique that asks "Why?" iteratively (typically five times) to trace a problem from its surface symptoms to its root cause. Used in ADDRESS IT Step 2.

**GAAB (General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro)** — The enacted budget law for a given fiscal year. The BEP becomes the GAAB after Parliament approval.

**GAD (Gender and Development)** — A cross-cutting framework requiring government programs and budgets to address gender-specific needs. CSW outputs should include gender analysis where applicable.

**General CSW (GN)** — The universal CSW template (9 sections, GN01-GN09) for issues that do not fit a specialized type. Follows the ADDRESS IT framework directly as its section structure.

**Implementation Plan** — The section of a CSW output that specifies actions, timelines, responsible parties, resource requirements, risks, and monitoring indicators for carrying out the approved recommendation.

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** — A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively an implementation is achieving its objectives. Defined during ADDRESS IT Step 6 (Sustain) and tracked during Step 7 (Iterate).

**Legislative Analysis Briefer (LA)** — A 13-section CSW output used by Parliament staff to analyze bills, resolutions, or policy issues for committee deliberation.

**MC 2/2022 (Memorandum Circular No. 2, Series of 2022)** — Civil Service Commission circular extending the CSW requirement to local government units.

**MC 72/2019 (Memorandum Circular No. 72, Series of 2019)** — Civil Service Commission circular establishing Complete Staff Work as a standard for national government agencies.

**MOA (Ministry, Office, or Agency)** — The executive branch entities of BARMM, including Ministries (e.g., MFBM, MBHTE), Offices (e.g., OCM), and Agencies. MOA staff produce CSW for executive decision-making.

**MOOE (Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses)** — Budget category for day-to-day operating costs: supplies, travel, utilities, communications, training, and contracted services.

**Moral Governance** — A foundational principle of Bangsamoro governance emphasizing ethical decision-making, accountability, transparency, and public trust. Established in BAA 13 and grounded in Islamic governance values.

**Options Evaluation Matrix** — A structured tool for scoring and ranking CSW options against the five evaluation criteria using weighted scores. Used in ADDRESS IT Step 4.

**Peer Review** — A quality assurance step where a colleague reviews the CSW output before it is submitted to the supervisor. Checks for logic, data accuracy, completeness, clarity, and sensitivity.

**PESTEL Analysis** — A framework for examining external factors: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal. Used in ADDRESS IT Step 1 (Analyze the Context) to scan the environment surrounding a CSW issue.

**PPAS (Programs, Projects, Activities, and Services)** — The specific activities funded by an agency's budget, classified as Tier 1 (existing) or Tier 2 (new proposals).

**PRLS (Parliamentary Rules and Legislative Staff)** — The administrative support structure for parliamentary proceedings and legislative staff work.

**Problem Statement** — A clear, specific, measurable articulation of the problem or need that the CSW addresses. Includes the gap between current and desired states, consequences of inaction, and institutional alignment. Produced during ADDRESS IT Step 2.

**PS (Personnel Services)** — Budget category for salaries, wages, allowances, and benefits of government personnel.

**RA 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act)** — The national law governing procurement of goods, infrastructure, and consulting services by government agencies, including BARMM entities.

**Root Cause Analysis** — The process of identifying the underlying factors that produce a problem, distinguishing root causes from surface symptoms. Techniques include the 5 Whys and fishbone diagram.

**SMART Objectives** — Objectives that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Set during ADDRESS IT Step 2 to define what the CSW effort aims to accomplish.

**Stakeholder Analysis** — A systematic identification and assessment of individuals, groups, or institutions affected by or interested in the CSW issue. Categorizes stakeholders by interest, influence, and position.

**Stoplight Format** — A progress reporting method using color codes: Green (on track, 90%+ of target), Yellow (at risk, 70-89%), Red (off track, below 70%).

**Transmittal Memorandum** — The cover memo accompanying a CSW output to the decision-maker. Introduces the document, summarizes the recommendation, states the action requested, and carries the supervisor's endorsement signature. Serves as a quality gate.
