# Chapter 1 — The Philosophy of Complete Staff Work

Every time you submit incomplete work to your superior, you transfer your job to them. They stop leading and start fixing. The hours they spend rewriting your memo, verifying your data, or hunting for the option you failed to consider are hours stolen from governance.

Complete Staff Work exists to prevent this. It is a professional discipline with a single standard: **your superior should only need to approve or disapprove.**

## 1.1 What CSW Means in Practice

### 1.1.1 The Decision-Maker Should Only Need to Approve or Disapprove

This is the core principle. Memorize it. Test every output against it.

When you hand a document to your supervisor, ask yourself: "If I were the decision-maker, could I sign this right now?" If the answer is no, your work is not complete.

**Complete** means the document contains:

- A clear statement of the problem
- Evidence gathered through research
- Multiple options analyzed against objective criteria
- A recommended course of action with justification
- An implementation plan with timelines, resources, and responsibilities
- A call to action that tells the decision-maker exactly what to approve

**Incomplete** means any of these are missing, vague, or unsupported.

**The test is binary.** Your supervisor should be able to read your output, understand the situation, evaluate your recommendation, and make a decision. They should not need to conduct their own research, generate their own options, or rewrite your prose to make it understandable.

> **TIP:** Before submitting, read your document as if you have never seen the subject before. If any section raises a question you have not answered, the work is not complete.

### 1.1.2 CSW as Professional Discipline, Not Bureaucratic Burden

CSW is not paperwork for the sake of paperwork. It is a thinking discipline. The documents you produce are artifacts of rigorous analysis, not administrative exercises.

Consider the difference:

| Without CSW | With CSW |
|---|---|
| Staff submits a one-paragraph memo asking for a decision | Staff submits a structured analysis with problem, options, recommendation, and implementation plan |
| Supervisor asks: "What about the legal implications?" | Legal implications are addressed in Section 4.5 |
| Decision is delayed while staff goes back to research | Decision is made in the same meeting |
| Implementation fails because no one planned for resource constraints | Implementation plan includes resource requirements and risk mitigation |

CSW saves time. It saves your supervisor's time. It saves the organization's time. The upfront investment in analysis and structure pays for itself many times over.

> **CAUTION:** The most common misconception about CSW is that it slows things down. The opposite is true. Decisions made on incomplete analysis get reversed, amended, or abandoned. Complete analysis leads to decisions that stick.

## 1.2 CSW in the Philippine Public Service Tradition

### 1.2.1 MC No. 72/2019 and MC No. 2/2022

Complete Staff Work has formal legal backing in the Philippine government.

**Memorandum Circular No. 72, Series of 2019** (MC 72/2019) issued by the Civil Service Commission (CSC) establishes CSW as a standard for all national government agencies. It defines CSW as:

> The product of a study of a problem, which includes presentation of a recommended action by staff personnel, and is carried to the point where all that remains to be done on the part of the head of the office is to approve or disapprove.

The MC requires that staff work be:

- **Thorough** in research and analysis
- **Complete** in presenting all relevant information
- **Decision-ready** so the approving authority can act immediately

**Memorandum Circular No. 2, Series of 2022** (MC 2/2022) extends the CSW requirement to local government units (LGUs). It recognizes that LGUs, like national agencies, need structured staff work to improve governance quality.

Both circulars share a common vision: government staff should produce work that enables, not burdens, their superiors.

### 1.2.2 Adaptation for Bangsamoro Autonomous Governance

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region operates under its own governance structure established by Republic Act No. 11054 (the Bangsamoro Organic Law, or BOL). BARMM is neither a national agency nor a traditional LGU. It is an autonomous region with its own parliament, ministries, and administrative system.

This creates a unique CSW context:

**Parliament staff** produce legislative briefers, committee reports, and oversight analyses that support 80 Members of Parliament. Their CSW outputs directly shape legislation affecting 4.4 million Bangsamoro people.

**Ministry, Office, and Agency (MOA) staff** produce policy recommendations, budget requests, project proposals, and administrative actions that drive the executive functions of BARMM governance.

**LGU staff** within BARMM produce staff work that must align with both BOL mandates and the local governance framework.

The ADDRESS IT framework in this guidebook adapts MC 72/2019 and MC 2/2022 for these three BARMM contexts. The core principle remains the same: the decision-maker should only need to approve or disapprove. But the templates, examples, and procedures are tailored to Bangsamoro governance.

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** Unlike national government CSW, Bangsamoro CSW must account for the BOL's special provisions on Shari'ah governance (Article X), moral governance principles (BAA 13), indigenous peoples' rights, and the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) alignment requirement. Chapters 2 and 13 address these unique dimensions.

## 1.3 Why CSW Matters for the Bangsamoro People

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is young. The BOL was ratified in 2019. BARMM institutions are still being built. Every decision made today shapes the governance culture that will endure for generations.

In this context, CSW is not just an administrative tool. It is a governance value.

### CSW Strengthens Transparency

When staff work is documented — with clear problem statements, cited evidence, and analyzed options — decisions become traceable. The public can understand *why* a policy was adopted, *what* alternatives were considered, and *who* recommended the chosen course of action.

Without CSW, decisions happen in corridors and text messages. Rationale is lost. Accountability evaporates.

### CSW Builds Institutional Memory

Staff rotate. Officials change. Political transitions happen. If decisions rest in the heads of individuals, they leave when those individuals leave.

CSW creates a documented record. The next analyst who picks up a file can trace the analysis, understand the reasoning, and build on the work rather than starting from scratch.

### CSW Protects Against Audit Findings

The Commission on Audit (COA) does not just check whether money was spent correctly. It checks whether decisions were made with due diligence. A properly documented CSW — showing research, options analysis, and justification — is your best defense against audit observations.

### CSW Enables Evidence-Based Governance

BARMM faces complex challenges: post-conflict development, poverty reduction, infrastructure gaps, education access, health service delivery, and cultural preservation. These challenges demand evidence-based solutions, not guesswork.

CSW is the mechanism that forces evidence into the decision-making process. When you conduct Step 3 (Research) and Step 4 (Explore Options) rigorously, you replace assumptions with data.

### CSW Upholds Moral Governance

The Bangsamoro governance framework emphasizes **moral governance** — decision-making grounded in ethical principles, accountability, and public trust. BAA No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code) establishes this as a governing principle.

CSW supports moral governance by ensuring decisions are:

- Based on evidence, not favoritism
- Documented, not hidden
- Analyzed for impact on all stakeholders, not just the politically connected
- Reviewed for compliance with Shari'ah principles where applicable

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** The concept of **amanah** (trust) in Islamic governance aligns directly with CSW's accountability requirement. When you produce complete, transparent, evidence-based staff work, you fulfill your amanah to the Bangsamoro people. When you cut corners, you betray it.

### The Cost of Incomplete Staff Work

Consider what happens when CSW fails in BARMM:

- A **budget request** without proper justification is denied by Parliament, delaying a flagship program by a fiscal year
- A **legislative briefer** with inaccurate data leads an MP to ask the wrong questions during budget deliberation, missing a critical oversight issue
- A **project proposal** without stakeholder consultation is approved but faces community resistance during implementation, wasting public funds
- A **legal opinion** that misreads a BOL provision leads to a policy challenge in the Supreme Court
- A **personnel action** without proper documentation results in a COA disallowance, requiring officers to personally refund the government

Every one of these failures is preventable through Complete Staff Work.
