# Chapter 3 — Types of CSW in BARMM

Your first decision when you receive an assignment is: **what type of CSW output does this require?** The wrong format wastes your time and frustrates your decision-maker. A budget question needs a budget briefer, not a general memo. A legal question needs a legal opinion, not a project proposal.

This chapter describes the nine CSW types used in BARMM and provides a decision matrix to help you choose correctly every time.

## 3.1 Overview: The CSW Type Table

| Code | Type | Primary Use | Typical Decision-Maker | Sections |
|------|------|------------|----------------------|----------|
| **LA** | Legislative Analysis Briefer | Analyze bills, resolutions, or policy issues for committee deliberation | Committee Chairperson, MPs | 13 sections |
| **BB** | Budget Briefer | Analyze agency budgets for appropriation review | Committee on Finance and Budget | 13 sections (BB01-BB13) |
| **PR** | Policy Recommendation | Recommend a policy change or new initiative | Minister, Director, Chief Minister | Bardach's Eightfold Path adapted |
| **LO** | Legal Opinion | Provide legal analysis and advice | Minister, legal counsel, committee chair | 8 sections (LO01-LO08) |
| **PP** | Project Proposal | Propose a new project, program, or initiative | Minister, MFBM, Parliament | 10 sections (PP01-PP10) |
| **PA** | Personnel/Administrative Action | Justify hiring, promotion, reorganization, or administrative decisions | HR committee, Minister, head of office | Variable |
| **SV** | Supervision System | Design organizational supervision frameworks | Head of entity, management committee | 10 sections (SV01-SV10) |
| **GN** | General CSW | Universal template for issues that do not fit a specialized type | Any decision-maker | 9 sections (GN01-GN09) |
| **CU** | Custom CSW | Hybrid or entity-specific formats combining elements from other types | Variable | Variable |

Every type follows the ADDRESS IT process. The difference is how the output is structured and what content each section requires.

## 3.2 Legislative Analysis Briefer (LA) — The 13-Section Format

The Legislative Analysis Briefer is the standard CSW output for Parliament staff. It provides a comprehensive analysis of a bill, resolution, or policy issue in 13 structured sections.

**When to use:** Any time a committee needs analysis of pending legislation, oversight issues, or policy questions.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| I. Overview | Bill title, author, committee referral, status | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| II. Brief Description | Summary of the bill's key provisions | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| III. Previous Proceedings | Legislative history and related measures | Step 2 (Define) |
| IV. Implementing Agency | Agency responsible for implementation | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| V. Appropriations Needed | Estimated budget impact | Step 3 (Research) |
| VI. Objects of Expenditure | Specific budget line items | Step 3 (Research) |
| VII. Source of Funds | Where the money comes from | Step 3 (Research) |
| VIII. Sectoral Analysis | Impact on specific sectors or communities | Step 3 (Research) |
| IX. BOL Alignment | Constitutional and BOL consistency check | Step 1 + Step 3 |
| X. Parliamentary Strategy | Recommended approach for committee action | Step 4 (Explore) |
| XI. Proposed Amendments | Specific textual amendments recommended | Step 4 (Explore) |
| XII. Recommended Vote | Approval, deferral, or disapproval with rationale | Step 5 (Submit) |
| XIII. Strategic Intelligence | Broader political and policy context | Step 4 + Step 5 |

> **TIP:** Section XIII (Strategic Intelligence) is what separates a good briefer from a great one. This is where you provide the context the committee needs to make a politically informed decision — stakeholder positions, potential controversies, timing considerations, and implementation risks. Do not skip it.

**Chapter 11 provides detailed guidance on producing Legislative Analysis Briefers within the ADDRESS IT framework.**

## 3.3 Budget Briefer (BB) — The BB-Coded Template

The Budget Briefer is the standard CSW output for analyzing agency budgets during Parliament's appropriation process.

**When to use:** During budget deliberation season, when committees review the Bangsamoro Expenditure Program (BEP) and each agency's budget request.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| BB01 | Ministry/Agency/Office | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| BB02 | BEP Reference (code/page) | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| BB03 | Mandate | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| BB04 | Overall Development Goals | Step 2 (Define) |
| BB05 | Organizational Outcomes (current year) | Step 3 (Research) |
| BB06 | Budget Comparison (PS, MOOE, CO) — multi-year | Step 3 + Step 4 |
| BB07 | Existing PPAS (Tier 1) | Step 3 + Step 4 |
| BB08 | New PPAS (Tier 2) | Step 4 (Explore) |
| BB09 | Related Laws and Policies | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| BB10 | Shari'ah Analysis (where applicable) | Step 1 + Step 2 |
| BB11 | Gender Analysis | Step 3 (Research) |
| BB12 | Recommendations and Proposed Actions | Step 4 + Step 5 |
| BB13 | Executive Summary | Step 5 (Submit) |

**Key rule:** BB13 (Executive Summary) is always written last. It captures the essence of the entire analysis in one to two paragraphs. This is what the MP reads first.

> **CAUTION:** Budget briefers require verified numerical data. Every figure — appropriations, obligations, disbursements, variances — must trace to an official source (BEP, GAAB, financial statements). A single wrong number destroys the briefer's credibility. Cross-check every figure before transmittal.

## 3.4 Policy Recommendation (PR) — Bardach's Eightfold Path

The Policy Recommendation is the standard CSW output for proposing a new policy, modifying an existing one, or responding to a policy challenge.

**When to use:** Any time a minister, director, or committee needs a recommended course of action on a policy question.

**Structure (adapted from Eugene Bardach's Eightfold Path):**

| Step | Section | ADDRESS IT Mapping |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | Define the Problem | Step 2 (Define) |
| 2 | Assemble the Evidence | Step 3 (Research) |
| 3 | Construct the Alternatives | Step 4 (Explore) |
| 4 | Select the Criteria | Step 4 (Explore) |
| 5 | Project the Outcomes | Step 4 (Explore) |
| 6 | Confront the Trade-Offs | Step 4 (Explore) |
| 7 | Decide | Step 5 (Submit) |
| 8 | Tell Your Story | Step 5 (Submit) |

Bardach's framework integrates naturally with ADDRESS IT. Steps 1-3 of ADDRESS IT map directly to Bardach's Steps 1-3. ADDRESS IT's Step 4 (Explore Options) expands Bardach's Steps 3-6. ADDRESS IT then adds implementation (Step 6) and iteration (Step 7), which Bardach does not cover explicitly.

**Example BARMM policy recommendation:**

> **Problem:** BARMM scholarship recipients have a 35% dropout rate after the first year.
>
> **Evidence:** Exit interviews with 200 dropped scholars show three root causes: inadequate stipend for cost of living outside BARMM, weak academic preparation from K-12, and lack of mentoring support.
>
> **Alternatives:** (A) Increase stipend by 40%, (B) Establish mandatory pre-college bridging program, (C) Deploy regional mentors in 10 host cities.
>
> **Recommendation:** Implement Options B and C simultaneously. Option A alone treats symptoms without addressing academic readiness or isolation.

## 3.5 Legal Opinion (LO)

The Legal Opinion provides legal analysis and advice on specific questions assigned by a decision-maker.

**When to use:** When a minister, committee chair, or head of office needs a legal assessment before making a decision.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| LO01 | Query / Legal Question | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| LO02 | Executive Summary (write last) | Step 5 (Submit) |
| LO03 | Factual Background | Step 1 (Analyze) |
| LO04 | Legal Issues Defined | Step 2 (Define) |
| LO05 | Applicable Legal Framework | Steps 1 + 3 |
| LO06 | Legal Analysis | Steps 3 + 4 |
| LO07 | Legal Opinion / Recommendation | Steps 4 + 5 |
| LO08 | Proposed Next Steps | Steps 5 + 6 |

**Key principle:** A legal opinion must present both sides of an argument before stating a conclusion. Even when the law seems clear, identify the competing interpretation and explain why it fails. This demonstrates thoroughness and protects the decision-maker from surprise challenges.

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** Legal opinions in BARMM often involve BOL interpretation, the interaction between BAAs and national laws, and Shari'ah law provisions. Always check Article X of the BOL when the issue has a Shari'ah dimension. Consult the Dar al-Ifta when needed.

## 3.6 Project Proposal (PP)

The Project Proposal presents a new project, program, or initiative for approval and funding.

**When to use:** When proposing a new development project, service delivery program, infrastructure initiative, or institutional reform.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| PP01 | Project Title and Proponent | Step 1 |
| PP02 | Executive Summary (write last) | Step 5 |
| PP03 | Background and Context | Step 1 |
| PP04 | Problem/Need Statement | Step 2 |
| PP05 | Research and Evidence Base | Step 3 |
| PP06 | Project Description | Step 4 |
| PP07 | Budget and Resource Requirements | Step 4 |
| PP08 | Implementation Plan | Steps 5 + 6 |
| PP09 | Monitoring and Evaluation | Step 7 |
| PP10 | Appendices | Supporting documents |

**Key requirement:** PP07 must include a detailed budget breakdown by component (Personnel Services, MOOE, Capital Outlay) and identify the funding source (block grant, Special Development Fund, General Appropriations Act, or external funding). Vague budgets kill proposals.

## 3.7 Personnel/Administrative Action (PA)

The Personnel/Administrative Action supports decisions on hiring, promotion, reorganization, disciplinary cases, or other administrative matters.

**When to use:** Any time a personnel or administrative decision needs documented justification.

**Key elements:**

- **The action requested** — State exactly what you are asking the decision-maker to approve
- **Legal basis** — Cite the CSC rules, BAA 13 provisions, or office policies that authorize the action
- **Justification** — Explain why the action is necessary, supported by data (staffing ratios, workload analysis, performance records)
- **Options considered** — Show that alternatives were evaluated before arriving at the recommendation
- **Financial impact** — Calculate the cost implications (salary differentials, benefits, training costs)
- **Implementation steps** — Outline the administrative process after approval

> **CAUTION:** Personnel actions are among the most audit-sensitive CSW outputs. Every step must be documented. Every requirement must be met. Missing a single CSC rule can result in a disallowance. Follow the checklist meticulously.

## 3.8 Supervision System (SV)

The Supervision System template is used to design or formalize an organizational supervision framework.

**When to use:** When a head of entity or office director needs to establish a structured supervision system that integrates CSW, performance management, delegation, and staff development.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| SV01 | Organization Profile |
| SV02 | Supervisory Framework |
| SV03 | Supervisory Roles and Responsibilities |
| SV04 | CSW Integration |
| SV05 | Performance Management System |
| SV06 | Delegation of Authority |
| SV07 | Feedback and Coaching Framework |
| SV08 | Staff Development Plan |
| SV09 | Accountability and Compliance |
| SV10 | Continuous Improvement |

This is a meta-CSW document: it defines how CSW itself is managed within the organization. Section SV04 explicitly maps how ADDRESS IT fits into the supervisory workflow.

## 3.9 General CSW (GN) — The Universal Template

When your assignment does not fit any specialized type, use the General CSW template. It follows ADDRESS IT directly as the section structure.

**When to use:** Any issue that needs structured analysis but does not require a specialized format. Examples: responding to a directive from the Chief Minister, analyzing a cross-cutting governance issue, preparing a position paper.

**Structure:**

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| GN01 | Executive Summary (write last) | Step 5 |
| GN02 | Background and Context | Step 1 |
| GN03 | Problem or Need Statement | Step 2 |
| GN04 | Research Findings | Step 3 |
| GN05 | Options Analysis | Step 4 |
| GN06 | Recommended Solution | Steps 4 + 5 |
| GN07 | Implementation Plan | Steps 5 + 6 |
| GN08 | Conclusion and Call to Action | Step 5 |
| GN09 | Appendices | Supporting documents |

The GN template includes an **Options Matrix** in GN05:

| Criterion | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Feasibility | | | |
| Effectiveness | | | |
| Efficiency | | | |
| Sustainability | | | |
| Alignment | | | |
| **Overall Rank** | | | |

> **TIP:** The GN template is your fallback. When in doubt about which type to use, start with GN. You can always restructure into a specialized format later if needed.

## 3.10 When to Use Which Type

Use this decision matrix:

**Start here: What is the nature of your assignment?**

| If your assignment involves... | Use this type | Template code |
|-------------------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Analyzing a bill or resolution for committee deliberation | Legislative Analysis Briefer | LA |
| Reviewing an agency's budget for appropriation | Budget Briefer | BB |
| Recommending a policy change or new initiative | Policy Recommendation | PR |
| Answering a specific legal question | Legal Opinion | LO |
| Proposing a new project or program | Project Proposal | PP |
| Justifying a personnel or administrative decision | Personnel/Administrative Action | PA |
| Designing an organizational supervision framework | Supervision System | SV |
| Analyzing any other governance issue | General CSW | GN |
| A combination of the above or a unique requirement | Custom CSW | CU |

### Quick Decision Flowchart

1. **Is it about legislation?** Use LA.
2. **Is it about a budget?** Use BB.
3. **Is it a legal question?** Use LO.
4. **Is it a new project?** Use PP.
5. **Is it about personnel?** Use PA.
6. **Is it a policy question?** Use PR.
7. **Is it about supervision systems?** Use SV.
8. **None of the above?** Use GN.
9. **Multiple types apply?** Use CU (combine relevant sections from two or more templates).

### When to Use Custom CSW (CU)

Custom CSW is appropriate when:

- Your assignment spans multiple domains (e.g., a project proposal that requires legal analysis)
- Your entity has an established internal format that differs from the standard templates
- The decision-maker has specified a particular output structure

When building a Custom CSW, always maintain the ADDRESS IT sequence. Even if you restructure the sections, ensure every step is represented. The framework is the constant; the format is flexible.

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** Some BARMM entities have developed their own CSW formats through practice. If your office has an established template that works, you do not need to abandon it. Instead, map its sections to ADDRESS IT steps to ensure completeness. The ADDRESS IT framework is a quality standard, not a formatting mandate.

**Chapter Summary**

- Nine CSW types serve different decision-making needs: LA, BB, PR, LO, PP, PA, SV, GN, and CU
- Every type follows the ADDRESS IT process; only the output structure differs
- Use the decision matrix to select the right type for your assignment
- The General CSW (GN) template is the universal fallback
- Custom CSW (CU) combines elements from multiple types when needed
- All templates include an executive summary that is written last
