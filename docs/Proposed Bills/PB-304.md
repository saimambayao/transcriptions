**An Act Establishing the Legal and Shari’ah Scholarship and Return Service For Deserving Students in the Bangsamoro, and Appropriating Funds Therefor**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “Lawyer and Shariah Counsellor for the People of the Bangsamoro Region Act”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy-** It is hereby declared policy of the Bangsamoro government to provide every Filipino citizen within its territorial jurisdiction with basic necessities and equal opportunities in life, and to ensure that social justice is promoted throughout all phases of life and development in the region. It is committed to ensuring equal access to justice for all its residents and their right to free access to the courts and quasi-judicial bodies and ensuring that adequate legal assistance shall not be denied to any person by reason of poverty.

Towards this end, it is crucial to invest in the development of legal and Shariah scholarship and provide opportunities for deserving students to pursue their legal education and gain the necessary skills and knowledge to contribute towards strengthening the justice system in the Bangsamoro.

Recognizing the importance of giving back to society, the Bangsamoro Government shall establish a return service program of the scholarship recipients, thereby ensuring that the benefits of their legal education are shared with the Bangsamoro community.

**Section 3. Bangsamoro Legal and Shariah Scholarship and Return Service Program.** -There shall be established a Bangsamoro Legal and Shariah Scholarship and Return Service (BLSSRS) Program for deserving students who intend to pursue a Juris Doctor or Bachelor of Laws degree in state universities and colleges (SUCs) or in private higher education institutions (HEIs) areas of the Bangsamoro region where there are no SUC’s offering Juris Doctor or Bachelor of Laws.

The BLSSRS Program shall provide the following benefits to qualified legal education students:

(a) Free tuition and other university fees
(b) Monthly living allowance
(c) Monthly lodging allowance
(d) Allowance for prescribed books and supplies;
(e) Payment for the bar exam review fee
(f) Allowance for bar exam review and reading materials
(g) Payment for Philippine bar exam fee as prescribed by the Supreme Court

In the event that a scholar fails the bar examination on his first attempt, he or she will be responsible for all the expenses associated with the succeeding bar examinations.

**Section 4-Financial Assistance Program for the Shari’ah Bar Examinees-** There shall be financial assistance program for examinees who intend to take the Shari’ah Bar Exam (SBE) and they are entitled to the following benefits:

(a) Daily living allowance during the mandatory Shariah Training Seminar as administered by the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos
(b) Allowance for Book and Shari'ah Bar exam reading materials
(c) Payment for Special Shariah bar exam fee as prescribed by the Supreme Court
(d) Transportation and accommodation allowance to the Shariah Bar exam site Those who receive financial assistance to take the SBE are only eligible for two attempts at financial assistance, and if they fail, they must pay for subsequent SBEs themselves.


**Section 5- Qualifications and Requirements. –**

A. In order to qualify for the grant of scholarship under the BLSSRS Program, a prospective legal education student must satisfy the following requirements:

1) Must be a Filipino Citizen residing in the Philippines but preference shall be given to those residing within BARMM
2) Must not have completed a Bachelor of Laws or Juris Doctor degree from any SUC or private HEI offering legal education;
3) Must be a graduating student or a graduate of an undergraduate degree program; and
4) Must have passed the entrance examination and complied with other requirements for admission into the Juris Doctor or Bachelor of Laws degree program offered by the SUC or private HEI where the student intends to enroll.

B.In order to qualify for the grant of financial assistance for the Shariah Bar Exam, prospective examinees must satisfy the following requirements:

1) Must be a Filipino Citizen residing in the Philippines but preference shall be given to those residing within BARMM
2) Must participate or have completed the Shariah Training Seminar (STS) as administered by the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos
3) Must be unconditionally admitted by the Office of the Bar Confidant to take the SBE

**Sec. 5. Conditions for the Grant of Legal Scholarship.** - Qualified students accepted into the BLSSRS Program shall be subject to the following conditions:

(a) Must sign an agreement stating the terms and conditions of the scholarship which shall be prescribed by the Bangsamoro Attorney General Office

(b) Must carry full load of subjects prescribed per semester by the SUC or private HEI concerned, and shall not, under any circumstance, drop a course which would result in underloading;

(c) Must finish the Juris Doctor or Bachelor of Laws degree program within the prescribed time frame of the SUC or private HEI where the scholar is enrolled in, subject to the retention policies of the SUC or private HEI concerned: *Provided*, That a scholar who is already enrolled, may be allowed, for valid and justifiable reasons, to file a leave of absence;

(d) Must take the bar examination within a maximum period of one (1) year after completion of the Juris Doctor or Bachelor of Laws degree;

(e) Must render return service as provided under Section 8 of this Act.

**Section 6. Conditions for the Grant of Financial Assistance for the Shariah Bar Exam-**

Qualified SBE examinees accepted to the financial assistance program shall comply with the following requirements:

(a) Must sign an agreement in accordance with Section 5 par (a) of this Act

(b) Prospective examinees must possess the necessary academic qualifications as specified by the Supreme Court as the Shariah bar examination authority.

(c) Must fully participate and complete the Shariah Training Seminar as administered by the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos. *Provided*, That a trainee who is already admitted to the seminar, may be allowed, for valid and justifiable reasons, to file a leave of absence;

(d) Must take the Shariah bar examination within a maximum period of one (1) year after completion of the Shariah Training Seminar

Furthermore, those examinees who completed STS prior to the passage of this Act shall be required to sign an agreement and provide proof of their completion of the STS to qualify for the financial assistance program. Examinees who availed of the financial assistance to take the SBE shall only be given two attempts and if they fail, subsequent SBE shall be borne by the examinees

**Section 7. Disqualifications-** The scholarship grant shall be terminated, and the recipient of the legal scholarship shall repay the full cost of the scholarship and related benefits received, including all the expenses incurred during the participation in the BLSSRS program, in case of the following circumstances:

(a) If the scholar fails to meet the academic requirements or to complete the course within the prescribed period without valid cause as may be determined by the SUC or private HEI concerned;

(b) If the scholar accepts another scholarship from another government or private agency while enjoying the benefits under this Act

(b) If the scholar commits gross misconduct in a manner that would bring significant damage to the SUC or private HEI concerned or to the community as a whole.

Furthermore, Shariah Bar examinees who receive financial assistance but fail to take the SBE without any justifiable reason shall be required to refund the total amount of the assistance extended and may be subjected to legal action.

**Sec. 7. Mandatory Return Service of Scholars under BLSSRS Program.** - Within five (5) years from admission to the practice of law, a scholar shall render mandatory return service for five (5) years in government agencies, including, but not limited to: Regular or Shariah Courts, Bangsamoro Attorney General Office, Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission, Ministries and instrumentalities of Bangsamoro government, the Public Attorney’s Office, National Prosecution Service, and any other government agencies and instrumentalities that provide legal services to poor and marginalized individuals and communities, Provided, that the mandatory return service shall be separate and distinct from the Clinical Legal Education Program as provided under Rule 138-A of the Rules of Court: Provided further, that beneficiaries of BLSSRS Program appointed to plantilla positions shall receive the appropriate salaries, benefits and other emoluments for services rendered under the mandatory return service.

If the scholar fails to obtain a passing grade in the Bar Exam within five (5) years from the time the scholar has completed his or her law degree, Provided, That, instead of repaying the full cost of the scholarship and related expenses, the scholar whose scholarship status has been terminated under this Act may opt to engage in work within the BARMM government for a period prorated to the number of years of mandatory return service provided under this Act.


**Section 8. Alternative to Mandatory Return Service-** Beneficiaries of the BLSSRS Program who, after three (3) years from admission to the practice of law , fail to secure an appointment in government shall have the option to (i) pay the full cost of scholarship and related benefits received, including all the expenses incurred during the participation in the scholarship program; or (ii) undertake alternative compliance program to be formulated by the Bangsamoro Attorney General Office pursuant to the provisions of this Act and Republic Act (RA) No. 9999, otherwise known as the Free Legal Assistance Act of 2010: Provided, finally, That in the application of Republic Act No. 9999, the allowable deduction from the gross income shall be the amount that could have been collected from the actual free legal services rendered or up to twenty percent (20%) of the gross income derived from the actual performance of the legal profession, whichever is lower.

**Section 9. Number and Allocation of Students to be Admitted to the Program.** - Prior to the implementation of this Act and every three (3) years thereafter, the Bangsamoro Attorney General Office shall, in consultation with the law schools in Bangsamoro and upon the assessment of the shortage of legal practitioners in the provinces of the Bangsamoro and determine the appropriate number and geographic allocation of scholars to be admitted to the BLSSRS Program.

**Section 10. Sanctions.** Any scholarship recipients the who has availed of the benefits under the LSRS Program but fails or refuses to comply with the mandatory return service as provided under this Act shall be required to pay twice the amount of scholarship, including other benefits received by reason of participation in the LSRS Program and maybe subjected to legal action: *Provided*, That the penalty imposed herein shall not apply to scholars who fail to comply with the required return service by reason of *force majeure* or not due to their fault or negligence.

**Section 11. Appropriations.** - The amount necessary for the implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.

**Setion. 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** - Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the BAGO shall, in consultation with Law Schools in Bangsamoro and National Commission on Muslim Filipinos promulgate the rules and regulations to effectively implement the provisions of this Act.

**Sec. 13. Separability Clause.** - If any provision or part hereof is held invalid or unconstitutional, the remainder of this Act or any provision not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**Sec. 14. Repealing Clause.** - All laws, orders, circulars, rules and regulations, or parts thereof which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Sec. 15. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect immediately following its publication in the newspaper of regional circulation.
