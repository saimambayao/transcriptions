**AN ACT ENSURING SAFE AND CLEAN DRINKING WATER AND ADEQUATE SANITATION FACILITIES IN SCHOOLS WITHIN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), PROVIDING FUNDS FOR THE PURPOSE, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**Section 1. Short Title. –** This Act shall be known as the "*BARMM Water Safety in Schools Act.*"

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy. –** The Bangsamoro Government affirms the right of every student in BARMM to access safe, clean drinking water and adequate sanitation facilities, essential to a healthy, dignified, and conducive learning environment. To uphold this right and ensure the welfare of students, this Act aims to implement region-specific standards and practices for water and sanitation in BARMM educational institutions, consistent with the Bangsamoro Government's commitment to student health, well-being, and educational development.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms. –** For the purposes of this Act, the following terms are defined as follows:

a) *WASH* - Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene. This encompasses the provision and management of safe water, sanitation facilities, and hygiene education to ensure a healthy and clean environment in schools. WASH practices aim to reduce health risks by promoting handwashing, safe drinking water, and proper sanitation practices.

b) *Safe Water Supply Systems* - Infrastructure and systems necessary for providing safe drinking water, including water filtration units, potable water tanks, piped water systems, and other mechanisms to meet the safety and quality standards for potable water.

c) *Sanitation Facilities* - Facilities in schools that include functional toilets, handwashing stations with an adequate supply of water and soap, and appropriate waste disposal mechanisms. These facilities must comply with health, safety, and accessibility standards, with their maintenance and operational needs to be funded through the Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses (MOOE) of each institution as specified by BARMM.

d) *BARMM WASH Monitoring Board (BWMB)* - A regional body created under this Act to oversee, coordinate, and ensure compliance with the standards of water safety, sanitation, and hygiene in BARMM schools.

e) *Institutions* - All public and private basic and secondary educational institutions, including madrasah schools, alternative learning centers, and equivalent non-tertiary educational settings in BARMM. This Act does not cover universities, colleges, or technical-vocational institutions.

f) *Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses (MOOE)* - Refers to the allocation in each school's budget used to cover costs of maintenance, operations, and upkeep of school facilities, including the costs for regular upkeep and maintenance of water and sanitation facilities in accordance with this Act.

**Section 4. Objectives. –** The objectives of this Act are as follows:

a) To guarantee access to safe and clean drinking water and adequate sanitation facilities for all students in public and private educational institutions within BARMM.

b) To integrate WASH education into the school curriculum, fostering student knowledge of and commitment to safe water use, sanitation, hygiene, and environmental stewardship.

c) To establish a BARMM-specific monitoring body to ensure compliance with standards in schools, providing support to institutions requiring assistance to meet these standards.

d) To support the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) in implementing and advancing the Department of Education’s WASH in Schools (WinS) program in ways that address the unique needs of the region.

**Section 5. Scope and Coverage. –** This Act applies to all public and private basic and secondary educational institutions, including madrasah schools, alternative learning centers, and equivalent non-tertiary education programs within BARMM.

**Section 6. School Water Supply Infrastructure. –** All educational institutions covered under this Act must ensure the availability of safe and clean drinking water through adequate water supply systems, which may include water filtration units, potable water tanks, piped water connections, or other approved systems meeting minimum standards for safe drinking water. Schools shall maintain water supply facilities that meet safety and quality standards set by the BWMB.

Maintenance of sanitation facilities, including functioning toilets and handwashing stations with adequate water supply and soap, shall be funded under each institution's MOOE to ensure consistent upkeep in compliance with health, safety, and accessibility standards.

**Section 7. WASH Education in Schools. –** The MBHTE shall incorporate WASH education into the standard curriculum of basic and secondary education, including madrasah and alternative learning centers. The curriculum shall cover water conservation, hygiene practices, sanitation, and the importance of clean water for health and well-being.

Each school shall implement a school-based WASH program, incorporating student-led initiatives and school-wide activities to reinforce learning and WASH practices. Schools shall conduct regular awareness campaigns, clean-up drives, handwashing demonstrations, and other practical activities to foster student involvement and commitment to maintaining clean, safe, and sustainable practices in their schools and communities.

**Section 8. BARMM WASH Monitoring Board (BWMB). –** A BWMB is hereby established to ensure the effective implementation of this Act. The BWMB shall be composed of representatives from the following agencies:

1) Ministry of Health (MOH)
2) Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE)

The BWMB shall carry out the following functions:

a) Develop and implement standards and protocols for water safety, sanitation, and hygiene in BARMM educational institutions, based on regional needs and best practices.
b) Conduct regular inspections, assessments, and compliance monitoring of water supply and sanitation facilities in schools.
c) Provide technical assistance, guidance, and recommendations to schools that require support to comply with water and sanitation standards.
d) Coordinate training programs for school staff and administrators to equip them with the knowledge and skills necessary to implement effective WASH programs in their respective institutions.
e) Facilitate knowledge-sharing activities among schools, encouraging the dissemination of best practices and successful WASH initiatives that can be implemented across BARMM institutions.
f) The BWMB shall submit an annual report to the Bangsamoro Government on the status and progress of WASH compliance in BARMM schools, along with any recommendations for improving the water and sanitation conditions in educational institutions.

**Section 9. Appropriations. –** The initial funds necessary for the implementation of this Act shall be sourced from the General Appropriations Act of BARMM (GAAB) as allocated by the Ministry of Finance and Budget Management (MFBM). Subsequent funding requirements for sustaining the initiatives shall be included in the annual budgets of the Ministry of Health, Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education, and other relevant ministries as needed.

**Section 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). –** Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the MOH, in coordination with MBHTE, shall develop the implementing rules and regulations (IRR) necessary for the effective implementation of this Act. The IRR shall detail the standards, procedures, timelines, and specific roles of each agency in ensuring compliance with water and sanitation standards in schools.

**Section 11. Separability Clause. -** If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 12. Effectivity. –** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after the completion of its publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
