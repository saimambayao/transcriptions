**AN ACT ESTABLISHING A COMPREHENSIVE REGIONAL PEACE AND PUBLIC SAFETY FRAMEWORK, INSTITUTIONALIZING THE CULTURE OF SECURITY, AND MANDATING INTEGRATED SECURITY MECHANISMS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO**

**CHAPTER I**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the "BARMM Peace and Security Act of 2025."

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** - The Bangsamoro Government, consistent with its mandate under the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054), upholds the fundamental right of every person to life, liberty, and security. It affirms that peace and public safety are necessary foundations for inclusive development, justice, and good governance. In accordance with national and international obligations, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes the essential role of communities, local government units (LGUs), civil society, and state security forces in establishing and sustaining a culture of peace, security, and resilience across the region.

Pursuant to the 1987 Philippine Constitution, the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 16 (Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions), and the ASEAN Political-Security Community Blueprint 2025, the Bangsamoro Government adopts a comprehensive, multi-stakeholder, and community-based approach to peace and security, with a special emphasis on human rights, rule of law, gender-responsiveness, cultural sensitivity, and local ownership of peace and order programs.

Accordingly, the Bangsamoro Government commits to: (1) Strengthening the regional and local security architecture through institutional development; (2) Cultivating a participatory Culture of Security anchored on shared responsibility; (3) Ensuring proactive, preventive, and inclusive measures against terrorism, criminality, and other threats; (4) Integrating peace and order concerns in development planning, disaster preparedness, and governance mechanisms; and (5) Building trust and cooperation between state institutions and the communities they serve.

**Section 3. Purpose and Objectives** – This Act seeks to accomplish the following objectives:

1. Institutionalize a culture of security to mainstream a regional culture of security as a collective civic responsibility, anchored on vigilance, preparedness, participation, and mutual accountability among the government, civil society, and ordinary citizens;
2. Establish a coordinated peace and security framework to create a unified, inclusive, and accountable peace and order system in BARMM, led by the Bangsamoro Peace and Order Council (BPOC) and supported by Local Peace and Order Councils (LPOCs) at all administrative levels;
3. Empower communities and strengthen local security capacities to capacitate LGUs, barangay governments, and civil society actors through training, funding, and mechanisms to effectively address threats to public safety, prevent violent extremism, and promote peaceful dispute resolution;
4. Institutionalize the use of technology in public safety to promote the strategic and lawful use of surveillance technologies such as CCTV systems, including data governance standards, privacy safeguards, and mandatory compliance from establishments and institutions;
5. Mainstream peace and security in governance and budgeting to ensure that peace and order concerns are systematically integrated in regional and local development planning, with guaranteed budget allocations, results-based monitoring, and reporting mechanisms;
6. Promote inclusive and rights-based peacebuilding to uphold human rights and international humanitarian law, promote gender equality and child protection, and safeguard the rights of Indigenous Peoples and vulnerable communities in all peace and security interventions, and;
7. Establish a regional task force for civic security education to create and operationalize Task Force Peace and Security – BARMM (TFPS-BARMM) to conduct regular community awareness, training, and preparedness programs modeled after successful practices like Task Force Davao.

**Section 4. Coverage.** This Act shall apply to all provinces, cities, municipalities, and barangays within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), including government institutions, private entities, civil society organizations, traditional structures, and individual residents, without distinction as to ethnicity, religion, gender, political affiliation, or economic status.

It shall cover all aspects of peace, public order, and safety in the Bangsamoro region, including but not limited to:

1. **Security Governance** – application of peace and security policies, coordination among security actors, development of safety standards, and institutionalization of local peace and order councils and mechanisms.
2. **Public Order and Community Safety** – prevention, detection, response, and mitigation of crimes, threats, disturbances, and incidents affecting community life, including violent extremism, terrorism, organized crime, and other internal security concerns.
3. **Surveillance, Monitoring, and Early Warning Systems** – regulation and implementation of surveillance technologies such as closed-circuit television (CCTV) systems, early warning tools, and real-time monitoring of public areas and high-risk zones.
4. **Emergency Preparedness and Crisis Response** – community-based safety planning, disaster-related conflict prevention, incident management, and deployment of responders during emergencies and peace and order disruptions.
5. **Peacebuilding and Conflict Transformation** – promotion of dialogue, mediation, traditional dispute resolution, peace education, youth engagement, and interfaith cooperation aimed at addressing root causes of violence and sustaining long-term peace.
6. **Preventing and Countering Violent Extremism (PCVE)** – implementation of localized, culturally sensitive, and rights-based interventions to counter recruitment, radicalization, and extremist ideologies that weaken social connection and unity.
7. **Institutional Roles and Responsibilities** – mandates and powers of regional ministries, law enforcement units, LGUs, traditional leaders, security councils, and community-based actors under this Act.
8. **Sectoral Participation and Protection** – affirmative inclusion of women, youth, Indigenous Peoples (IP), senior citizens, persons with disabilities (PWDs), and other vulnerable sectors in peace and security initiatives.
9. **Budgetary, Administrative, and Legal Support** – allocation of public funds, establishment of implementing units, capacity development, and legal mandates for compliance, reporting, and monitoring mechanisms.
10. **Private Establishments and Institutions** – compliance of private entities with minimum security requirements such as installation of CCTV, personnel training, emergency response protocols, and data cooperation with authorized agencies.
11. **Public Engagement and Civic Responsibility** – encouragement of citizen involvement in promoting a culture of security, responsible incident reporting, and participation in local security initiatives and community defense programs.

**Section 5. Definition of Terms** – For the purposes of this Act, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

1. **Bangsamoro Peace and Order Council (BPOC)** – The primary regional policy and coordination body for peace and security, created under this Act.
2. **Local Peace and Order Council (LPOC)** – Refers to the peace and order body created at the provincial, city, municipal, and barangay levels to ensure localized implementation of security and peace initiatives.
3. **Culture of Security** – A way of life wherein individuals and communities are constantly alert, informed, and actively engaged in maintaining safety and order through vigilance, responsible reporting, mutual support, and civic participation.
4. **Peace and Order and Public Safety Plan (POPS Plan)** – A planning and budgeting document that contains programs and projects on peacebuilding, security, crime prevention, and safety strategies of LGUs.
5. **Task Force Peace and Security – BARMM (TFPS-BARMM)** – A mobile, inter-agency training and civic education team under the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) that facilitates security awareness and emergency preparedness.
6. **Surveillance System** – Any lawful technology, such as CCTV cameras, used to monitor, record, and analyze activities in public or semi-public spaces for security purposes.
7. **Minimum Technical Specifications (MTS)** – Prescribed hardware and software standards for CCTV and surveillance systems to ensure they are useful for crime detection and deterrence.
8. **Human Security** – A people-centered approach to security that addresses not only threats of violence or conflict but also economic, environmental, political, and health vulnerabilities affecting communities.
9. **Community Policing** – A collaborative approach between law enforcement agencies and the community to identify and solve public safety problems, enhance trust, and co-produce security.
10. **Preventing and Countering Violent Extremism (PCVE)** – Refers to strategies and programs aimed at preventing the spread of extremist ideologies and addressing their root causes through education, dialogue, and inclusive development.
11. **Multi-Stakeholder Participation** – Involvement of diverse actors, including government agencies, LGUs, CSOs, faith-based groups, traditional leaders, youth, women, and IPs, in the planning and implementation of peace and security programs.
12. **Gender-Responsive Security** – Security policies and practices that recognize and address the different needs, vulnerabilities, and contributions of all genders, especially women and girls.
13. **Early Warning and Early Response (EWER)** – A systematic approach to identifying potential conflicts or threats and intervening at the earliest possible stage to prevent escalation.
14. **Incident Reporting Mechanism (IRM)** – A standardized and confidential system for citizens to report suspicious activity, crime, or emergencies to the appropriate authorities.
15. **Internal Security Threats** – Any domestic risk or danger to public peace, including criminality, armed conflict, terrorism, or organized violence that affects communities in BARMM.
16. **Public Safety** – A condition where the general public is protected from crimes, disasters, hazards, and other threats through preventive measures and coordinated response.

**CHAPTER II**
**CULTURE OF SECURITY AND CITIZEN ENGAGEMENT**

**Section 6. Definition of the Culture of Security** – for purposes of this Act, shall refer to a sustained, values-based, and community-driven framework wherein individuals, households, and institutions across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region are vigilant, informed, and actively involved in preventing criminality, violence, and emerging threats. It fosters collective responsibility, proactive engagement, and shared ownership of public safety in accordance with international human security standards and national legal mandates.

**Section 7. Citizen Engagement** - All citizens in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region are hereby encouraged to become proactive partners in safeguarding their communities through legally recognized and culturally appropriate security measures. Engagement shall be institutionalized through localized platforms, including but not limited to barangay assemblies, peace committees, interfaith dialogues, and digital feedback mechanisms.

**Section 8. Four Pillars of the Culture of Security.** – The Four Pillars of the Culture of Security shall be promoted and mainstreamed through all levels of governance, educational institutions, religious centers, and civil society engagements:

a) **Volunteer** - Citizens are encouraged to:
1. Assist in peace and security programs;
2. Cooperate in investigations as lawful witnesses or informants;
3. Support emergency response or community mediation as trained volunteers.
b) **Report** - Citizens must report suspicious behavior, threats, or criminal activities through designated hotlines, online systems, or local authorities.
c) **Remind** - Citizens shall help enforce and raise awareness on safety protocols, ordinances, and lawful behavior within their families, workplaces, and communities.
d) **Participate** - Active participation in peace forums, drills, consultations, trainings, and safety audits is encouraged to build collective preparedness, social connection, and unity.

**CHAPTER III**
**INTEGRATED COMMUNITY DEFENSE SYSTEM (ICDS)**

**Section 9. Establishment of the ICDS** – There is hereby established an Integrated Community Defense System (ICDS) to serve as a unifying operational framework that enhances coordination among the Bangsamoro Government, security forces, local governments, and civil society in ensuring peace, order, and public safety.

**Section 10. Composition of the ICDS** – The ICDS shall be composed of the following actors:

a) **Regional Peace and Order Council (RPOC)** – The RPOC shall serve as the primary inter-agency policy-making and coordinating body on peace, order, public safety, and conflict resolution across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. It shall endorse regional Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) Plans and monitor their implementation.
b) **Ministry of the Interior and Local Government – MILG** shall provide policy oversight and technical assistance to LGUs in mainstreaming the Culture of Security in barangay governance, supervising the Barangay Peace and Order Committees (BPOCs) and institutionalizing community policing models.
c) **Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS)** – MPOS shall lead the coordination, planning, and implementation of regional peace and security programs; consolidate conflict monitoring data; and act as technical secretariat of the ICDS. It shall spearhead capacity-building initiatives and facilitate inter-agency collaboration.
d) **Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD)** – MSSD shall ensure social protection interventions for individuals and communities affected by violence, armed conflict, or disasters. It shall lead to implementing trauma-informed support services and community resilience programs.
e) **Ministry of Indigenous Peoples’ Affairs (MIPA)** – MIPA shall ensure that all peace and security interventions respect and integrate the customs, traditions, and governance systems of Indigenous Peoples (IPs) within the region, particularly in ancestral domains and IP communities.
f) **Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE)** – MBHTE shall integrate peace education, conflict prevention, human rights awareness, and disaster risk reduction in the basic and tertiary education curricula, and support the establishment of School Peace and Security Councils (SPSC) in learning institutions.
g) **Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC)** – BHRC shall monitor human rights conditions in relation to security operations, detention, law enforcement, and civil-military relations. It shall ensure that all security measures comply with national and international human rights standards.
h) **Bangsamoro Police Regional Office (BPRO)** – BPRO shall provide law enforcement, crime prevention, intelligence, and public safety functions. It shall deploy trained personnel in coordination with local BPATs and implement the region’s POPS Plan and Community and Service-Oriented Policing (CSOP) strategies.
i) **Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) units deployed in BARMM** – AFP units based in the region shall support civil authorities in maintaining peace and order, especially in conflict-affected and geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDAs), in accordance with their mandate and in full respect of civilian supremacy and human rights.
j) **Local Government Units (LGUs)** – LGUs at the provincial, city, municipal, and barangay levels shall be primarily responsible for implementing localized POPS Plans, convening peace councils, and mobilizing resources for citizen engagement and grassroots peace-building.
k) **Civil Society Organizations (CSOs), Faith-Based Institutions, and Traditional Leaders** – These stakeholders shall ensure inclusive representation in ICDS planning and monitoring, promote conflict sensitivity and community dialogue, and provide cultural and spiritual guidance in sustaining the Culture of Security.
l) **Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC)** – BWC shall ensure that all peace and security initiatives are gender-responsive, and advocate for the protection and empowerment of women in conflict, displacement, or peacebuilding roles.
m) **Bangsamoro Youth Commission (BYC)** – BYC shall lead youth peacebuilding initiatives, promote volunteerism and digital vigilance among youth, and support the formation of Youth Peace Action Teams in schools and communities.
n) **Recognized Security Task Forces** – These specialized units shall assist in the enforcement of peace and order, manning of checkpoints, emergency response, and joint operations during declared crises or states of emergency, subject to regional and national protocols.
o) **Barangay Peacekeeping Action Teams (BPATs)** – Frontline responders trained to monitor, report, and intervene in basic peacekeeping functions at the barangay level.
p) **Academe and Research Institutions** – Academic institutions shall support the ICDS through peace and security research, evidence-based policy development, and the conduct of training, monitoring, and evaluation activities to enhance program effectiveness and sustainability.

**Section 11. Functions and Responsibilities** – The ICDS shall serve as the principal convergence platform for multi-stakeholder peace and security coordination in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Its core functions and responsibilities shall include, but not be limited to, the following:

a) **Harmonization of Regional and Local Security Programs** – Align, integrate, and monitor the implementation of regional and local Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) initiatives to ensure coherence, resource optimization, and strategic direction in accordance with the Bangsamoro Development Plan, the Bangsamoro Peace and Security Framework, and relevant national and international peacebuilding commitments.
b) **Implementation of Community-Based Prevention and Resilience Strategies** – Institutionalize evidence-based, culturally sensitive, and gender-responsive programs for crime prevention, violent extremism mitigation, and community resilience. This includes early warning systems, conflict-sensitive risk mapping, and support for vulnerable populations.
c) **Information-Sharing and Interoperability** – Establish secure, interoperable, and encrypted communication protocols for real-time information exchange among ICDS member institutions, subject to strict compliance with Republic Act No. 10173 or the Data Privacy Act of 2012 and applicable regional data protection regulations.
d) **Oversight and Technical Support in POPS Planning** – Provide technical guidance, capacity development, and performance monitoring to LGUs in the formulation, implementation, and periodic updating of their Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) Plans. The ICDS shall ensure that such plans are conflict-sensitive, inclusive, and consistent with regional security standards.
e) **Monitoring, Evaluation, and Reporting** – Conduct regular performance assessments, outcome monitoring, and results-based evaluations of local and sectoral peace and safety interventions. The ICDS shall submit an Annual Peace and Security Report to the Bangsamoro Parliament, which shall include evidence-based recommendations, disaggregated data analysis, and compliance indicators.
f) **Establishment of a Grievance Redress and Feedback Mechanism** – Operationalize an accessible, transparent, and culturally appropriate grievance redress system that enables individuals and communities to report violations, raise concerns, and seek remedies in relation to peace and security services. This shall include mechanisms for dispute resolution, referral pathways, and legal aid support in coordination with the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission and other relevant institutions.
g) **Capacity Building and Training** – Lead in the design and delivery of regular capacity-building programs for ICDS stakeholders, including civil society, BPATs, LGUs, and traditional leaders, on topics such as community safety, human rights, conflict transformation, and counter-disinformation strategies.
h) **Coordination with Regional and National Security Agencies** – Serve as the official coordination body of the Bangsamoro Government with national security forces, the National Task Force to End Local Communist Armed Conflict (NTF-ELCAC), the AFP, PNP, and other relevant agencies on matters of joint operations, intelligence sharing, and peace enforcement.

**Section 12. Governance Structure** – To ensure an integrated, devolved, and participatory implementation of peace and security initiatives across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, the Integrated Community Defense System (ICDS) shall adopt a multi-tiered governance structure composed of the following bodies and mechanisms:

a) **Regional ICDS Council** – The Regional ICDS Council shall serve as the highest policymaking and coordinating body within the ICDS framework. It shall be chaired by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao or his/her duly authorized representative, and shall be composed of all primary agencies enumerated under Section 12. The Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) shall serve as the lead convenor, while the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) shall act as the technical secretariat responsible for coordination, documentation, and technical facilitation.
b) **Provincial ICDS Councils** – Each province within BARMM shall establish a Provincial ICDS Council, to be chaired by the Provincial Governor, and composed of provincial representatives of national and regional peace and security actors. These councils shall translate regional security directives into local strategies, monitor municipal and city ICDS units, and ensure the integration of culturally responsive and conflict-sensitive approaches in provincial Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) Plans.
c) **City and Municipal ICDS Units** – Every city and municipality in BARMM shall operationalize a City or Municipal ICDS Unit, to be chaired by the Local Chief Executive (Mayor), with technical support from the respective Municipal Local Government Operations Officer (MLGOO) and the Municipal Peace and Order Officer. These units shall be responsible for the implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of localized POPS Plans, ensuring community engagement, coordination with barangays, and alignment with higher-tier security frameworks.
d) **Barangay Peace and Order Committees (BPOCs) and Barangay Peacekeeping Action Teams (BPATs)** – At the grassroots level, the BPOCs and BPATs shall serve as the frontline peace and security structures of the ICDS. Chaired by the Barangay Captain, these teams shall be capacitated to carry out community surveillance, early warning, basic mediation, referral of incidents, and direct engagement with law enforcement and local government units. Their functions shall be standardized across the region and supported through capacity-building initiatives by MILG and MPOS.

**Section 13. Internal Accountability Mechanisms** - In recognition of the critical need for transparency, ethical conduct, and continuous improvement, the ICDS shall institutionalize the following internal accountability mechanisms:

a) **Independent Monitoring and Evaluation Committees (IMECs)** – Independent Monitoring and Evaluation Committees shall be established at the regional and provincial levels, composed of representatives from civil society organizations, interfaith networks, traditional leaders, academic institutions, and rights-based organizations. These committees shall regularly assess the performance, compliance, and social impact of ICDS programs and submit periodic reports with actionable recommendations to the Regional ICDS Council.
b) **Annual Public Accountability Forum** – An Annual Public Accountability Forum shall be convened by the Regional ICDS Council to publicly disclose key performance outcomes, fiscal expenditures, implementation bottlenecks, and forward strategies. This forum shall provide a platform for citizens, stakeholders, and government agencies to engage in open dialogue on peace and security issues and to strengthen public trust in the ICDS system.
c) **Audit Compliance and Oversight for Intelligence and Security Units** – All agencies and units under the Integrated Community Defense System (ICDS) shall comply with audit and financial transparency protocols as mandated by the Bangsamoro Audit Authority (BAA) and the Commission on Audit (COA), especially where operations involve classified intelligence activities. For intelligence and sensitive security operations, audit and oversight shall be conducted by designated internal audit bodies or special oversight committees with the appropriate security clearances, pursuant to guidelines ensuring both accountability and protection of sensitive information. The Bangsamoro Parliament shall promulgate implementing rules and regulations defining the scope, procedures, and confidentiality standards for auditing intelligence and classified activities.

**CHAPTER IV**
**COMMUNITY SURVEILLANCE AND PUBLIC SAFETY MEASURES**

**Section 14. Installation of Surveillance Systems** – All business establishments, public buildings, terminals, ports, and high-traffic areas within the BARMM, as determined by the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government, shall be required to install Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV) systems to aid in crime deterrence, investigation, and emergency response.

**Section 15. Minimum Technical Specifications.** – The Bangsamoro ICT Office, in coordination with the Ministry of Science and Technology, shall issue and periodically update the Minimum Technical Specifications for CCTV Systems, which shall include but not be limited to:
1) Night vision and high-resolution capability;
2) Minimum storage of 30 days of video footage;
3) Secure access to prevent tampering or unauthorized use.

**Section 16. Prohibited Acts.** – The following shall be considered violations of this Act:
a) Failure to install and maintain compliant CCTV systems after a mandatory period of six (6) months from the effectivity of implementing rules;
b) Intentional disabling, destruction, or interference with CCTV systems;
c) Refusal to grant reasonable access to authorized personnel conducting inspection, unless judicially restrained.
d) Violators shall be subject to administrative sanctions, business permit suspension, or penalties as may be provided in the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).

**Section 17. Community Monitoring Mechanisms** – Barangay-based Safety Monitoring Desks (BSMDs) shall be established to receive, log, and respond to citizen reports related to safety, violence, or emergencies. These desks shall be integrated into the ICDS communication network and supported through technical and legal training.

**Section 18. Privacy and Legal Compliance** – All surveillance and monitoring measures shall be consistent with Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012), existing jurisprudence, and applicable international human rights treaties to which the Philippines is a party.

**CHAPTER V**
**BUDGETING AND PROGRAM INTEGRATION**

**Section 19. Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) Budget** – In order to sustain effective peace and security initiatives across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), at least five percent (5%) of the annual development budget of the regional government and all local government units (LGUs) shall be allocated exclusively for Peace and Order and Public Safety (POPS) programs. These funds shall cover, but are not limited to, procurement of equipment, capacity-building and training activities, community engagement programs, and other initiatives aimed at promoting public safety and security.

**Section 20. POPS Planning and Reporting** – All LGUs within BARMM shall prepare and submit an Annual Peace and Security Plan to the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) and the Barangay Peace and Order Council (BPOC). This plan shall outline strategies, resource allocation, and implementation schedules for peace and order activities at the local level. Submission of this plan is mandatory to ensure policy coherence, operational coordination, and accountability across all levels of government in BARMM.

**CHAPTER VI**
**PUBLIC EDUCATION, TRAINING, AND TASK FORCES**

**Section 21. Security Awareness Programs** – All educational institutions, government agencies, and workplaces within BARMM shall institutionalize security awareness programs that include regular peace and safety education, emergency preparedness drills, orientations on security protocols, and community-based risk reduction measures. These programs aim to cultivate a Culture of Security by embedding safety habits and readiness among students, employees, and citizens at all societal levels.

**Section 22. Creation of Task Force Peace and Security (TFPS-BARMM)** – There is hereby created a regional task force named Task Force Peace and Security – BARMM (TFPS-BARMM) under the supervision of the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG). The task force shall be responsible for conducting mobile trainings, organizing community awareness campaigns, and supporting peace and security operations throughout BARMM. TFPS-BARMM shall draw lessons from successful models such as Task Force Davao to ensure adaptive, responsive, and community-oriented peacekeeping efforts.

**CHAPTER VII**
**PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT**

**Section 23. Violations and Penalties** – Violations of provisions under this Act shall be subject to the following penalties:
a) Failure to install mandated Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV) systems in establishments as required: a fine ranging from Twenty-Five Thousand Pesos (₱25,000) to One Hundred Thousand Pesos (₱100,000) per violation;
b) Tampering with, damaging, or disabling CCTV cameras or related security equipment: imprisonment from six (6) months to two (2) years;
c) Obstruction of authorized inspections of security systems by regulatory officers: imposition of fines, and in cases of persistent non-compliance, suspension or revocation of business permits.

**Section 24. Accountability of Public Officials** – Any public official or government employee who willfully neglects, refuses, or fails to perform duties mandated by this Act shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the provisions of the Bangsamoro Administrative Code. Such accountability measures promote ethical governance, uphold the rule of law, and reinforce the commitment to peace and security in BARMM.

**CHAPTER VIII**
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 25. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR)** – The Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG), in coordination with the Barangay Peace and Order Council (BPOC) and other relevant agencies, shall formulate the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of this Act within ninety (90) days from its effectivity. The IRR shall provide detailed guidelines and procedures necessary for the effective implementation and enforcement of the provisions herein.

**Section 26. Separability Clause** – If any provision or part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid by a competent court, such declaration shall not affect the validity or enforceability of the other provisions not affected thereby, which shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 27. Repealing Clause** – All laws, executive orders, ordinances, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof, which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Section 28. Effectivity** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the official gazette of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao or in a newspaper of general circulation within the region.
