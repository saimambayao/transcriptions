**AN ACT ESTABLISHING A CHILDREN’S PLAYGROUND IN EVERY SCHOOL, DEFINING ITS SCOPE AND COVERAGE, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREOF, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro Playground Act of 2022”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them. It shall also uphold the people’s right to have access to essential goods, health, and other social services that promote their well-being. It is then the responsibility of the Government to regulate the basic facilities for health and convenience of its people.

Moreover, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building, and it is declared the policy of the state to promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, and nationalism, encourage involvement in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through sports.

**Section 3. Objectives.** – This Act generally aims to establish a children’s playground in schools located within the Bangsamoro Region. Specifically, it has the following objectives:

a) to create a conducive learning environment for the children that will encourage them to enjoy the process of learning;
b) to help children grow crucial cognitive, physical, social, emotional, and imaginative skills;
c) to establish an atmosphere that can instill a sense of community among the students;
d) to give space to students where they can fully express themselves and an opportunity to let loose;
e) to improve the attention and decrease stress and anxiety of students;
f) to promote health awareness among the students; and
g) to have a healthy population and an environment that allows education to be more fun.

**Section 4. Establishment of Playground.** – The Bangsamoro Government shall establish in every school a playground with safe facilities and fixed gaming equipment, such as but not limited to slides, swings, monkey bars, and the like.

The playground shall be in a safe outdoor area located inside the premises of the school. It must be sufficient, accessible, safe and secure, inclusive and interactive, and well-maintained and clean;

a) *sufficient* – the playground equipment must be able to cater a good number of students;
b) *accessible* – the playground needs to be easily accessible to children and their size and should be with wide spaces to allow safe and independent movement and opportunities for children to mix freely;
c) *safe and secure* –
i. the playground and its equipment should be equipped with safety mechanisms;
ii. it should be located within school premises, in an open area where the kids can be visible to teachers, staff, and their parents or guardians;
iii. there should be at least one (1) assigned staff to look after the kids and their safety;
d) *inclusive and interactive* –
i. the playground must be open for all children, regardless of gender, race, age, religion, or ability;
ii. must cater to all, especially to children with special needs;
iii. it must encourage cooperative and collaborative play so that the kids can freely and enjoyably play together;
iv. it must be a place where the children can interact with other kids in order for them to develop their social skills;
v. there should be equipment that will require the operation of more than one person, such as a seesaw, so that children may be given more opportunities to play together, which promotes social interaction;
e) *well-maintained and clean* –
i. the playground equipment should always be checked on a daily basis to know if they need maintenance, so as to ensure their safe use;
ii. the playground in general should always be clean to reduce the build-up of bacteria in the ground and on the play equipment;
iii. trash bins should be accessible to the children and other people to reduce trash build-up in the play area.

This shall be implemented in all schools located within the Bangsamoro Region.

**Section 6. Coverage.** – This Act shall cover all the schools operating within the Bangsamoro Region.

**Section 7. Implementation.** – Within sixty (60) days from the promulgation of this Act, the necessary rules and regulations for the proper implementation of its provision shall be formulated by the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE).

**Section 8. Appropriations.** – The funds necessary for the establishment of playgrounds shall be included in the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and submitted to the Parliament for its approval.

**Section 9. Separability Clause.** – If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 10. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 11. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
