**AN ACT**
**CREATING AND ESTABLISHING THE PURA MUNICIPAL HOSPITAL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF DATU BLAH T. SINSUAT, PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO DEL NORTE, BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) interim Parliament*

**Section 1. Short Title. –** *This Act shall be known as the “The Pura Municipal Hospital Act of 2022.”*

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy. –** It is a policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, as provided under Section 22, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” to provide for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents and establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellence and affordable medical services.

**Section 3. Creation of Pura Municipal Hospital. –** To implement the above-mentioned policy, a hospital facility is hereby created and established to be known as the Pura Municipal Hospital (PMH) to be located in the municipality of Datu Blah T. Sinsuat, Maguindanao Norte, BARMM.

**Section 4. Hospital Bed Capacity. –** The PMH shall be constructed, initially, as an Infirmary with at least 10-15 bed capacity and shall start its operations under such capacity and capability as prescribed by the licensing standards personnel. The medical and office equipment and other facilities required shall likewise be provided to the hospital. The PMH may be upgraded thereafter into a higher facility with a larger capacity upon the evaluation and recommendation by the Ministry of Health (MoH).

**Section 5. Catchment Area/Coverage. –** The Pura Municipal Hospital shall serve primarily, but shall not limited to, the patients coming from the municipality of Datu Blah T. Sinsuat. It may extend its services as an augmentation and support to other existing medical facilities to patients coming from the adjacent municipalities, particularly the coastal areas of Maguindanao del Norte, when such need arises.

**Section 6. Objectives. –** The creation and establishment of Pura Municipal Hospital aims to:

(a) to provide highly accessible, affordable, comprehensive total health care and hospital services to the BARMM constituents, cited under Section 5 above; and

(b) to integrate in its system of operations all pertinent and relevant provisions under Republic Act No. 11223, otherwise known as the Universal Health Care (UHC) Act.

**Section 7. Administration, Management, and Organization. –** The Pura Municipal Hospital shall be under the administrative supervision of the Minister of Health who is hereby authorized to determine and approve the appropriate organizational structure for the management and operations of the facility.

**Section 8. Appropriation. –** The Ministry of Health shall include in its annual appropriations the amount necessary for the construction of the hospital building, procurement of equipment, personnel services, maintenance and other operating expenses, and the subsequent upgrading, as it may deem necessary.

**Section 9. Separability Clause. –** If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 10. Repealing Clause. –** All laws, orders, rules, and regulations under the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) Government, including those passed under the defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) Government, regarding the construction, establishment, and operations of the Pura Municipal Hospital, which are not consistent with this Act are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 11. Effectivity. –** This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its full publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
