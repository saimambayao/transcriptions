**AN ACT**
**TO CREATE THE LAKE LANAO REHABILITATION AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY, DEFINING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, AND PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:* 

**CHAPTER I**
**General Provisions**

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority Act of 2023”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policies.** – It is hereby declared to be the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote and accelerate the development and balanced growth of the Lake Lanao Area within the context of plans and policies for social and economic development, and to carry out the development of the Lake Lanao Area with due regard for environmental management and control, preservation of the quality of human life and ecological systems, formulation of programs that will ensure food security, promotion of eco-tourism, and the prevention of undue ecological disturbances, deterioration, and pollution.

It shall also be the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect, conserve, rehabilitate, and develop forests, coastal, and marine resources, including adoption of programs and projects to ensure the maintenance of ecological balance and biodiversity.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms.** – As used in this Act, the following terms and phrases are defined as follows:

(a) **“Authority”** whenever cited in this Act shall mean Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority;

(b) **“Board”** whenever cited in this Act shall mean Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority Board;

(c) **“Lake Lanao”** refers to the Lake situated and surrounded by the province, city, and municipalities of Lanao del Sur;

(d) **“Lake Lanao Area”** refers to the inland waters known as Lake Lanao along with its shoreland, including the City of Marawi, towns, and municipalities within Lanao del Sur and surrounding the lake;

(e) **“Biodiversity”** refers to the variety of all living things: the different plants, animals and microorganisms, the genetic information they contain, and the ecosystem they form; and

(f) **“Ecological Balance”** refers to a state of dynamic equilibrium within a community of organisms in which genetic, species, and ecosystem diversity remain relatively stable and subject to gradual changes through natural succession.

**CHAPTER II**
**THE LAKE LANAO REHABILITATION AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY**

**Section 4. Creation of Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority.** – There is hereby created the Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority (LLRDA), which shall be an attached body to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM).

**Section 5. Powers and Functions of the LLRDA.** – Generally, the LLRDA shall be responsible in the overall policy-making, planning, coordination, and management of the implementation of the Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development. Specifically, it shall have the following powers and functions:

(a) Exercise regulatory and supervisory authority in the implementation of rehabilitation and development programs and projects within the Lake Lanao Area, without diminution of the authority of the local government units concerning matters granted to them under the Local Government Code;

(b) Enter into contracts, memoranda, agreements, and other cooperative arrangements with public and private entities;

(c) Accept donations, contributions, grants, bequests, or gifts in cash or in kind from various sources, domestic or foreign, in consonance with existing laws and regulations. The cash donations shall not be used to fund the personal services requirement of the Authority;

(d) Facilitate the preparation of a 30-year Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Master Plan with feasibility studies of top 10 potential big ticket projects;

(e) Formulate a multi-year investment and budget proposal based on the 30-year Master Plan;

(f) Recommend to the OCM, through the LLRDA Board, the implementation of the identified feasible programs and projects from the Master Plan by the concerned ministries and offices towards the rehabilitation and development of Lake Lanao;

(g) Recommend to the LLRDA Board appropriate policies which may help hasten the rehabilitation and development of Lake Lanao;

(h) Coordinate the implementation of plans, programs, and projects of the ministries, offices, local government units, government owned and controlled corporations, and the private sector when such plans, programs, and/or projects have impact on Lake Lanao’s rehabilitation and development;

(i) Coordinate with local government units within Lake Lanao for the passage of zoning ordinances and other regulatory measures necessary to carry out the objectives of the LLRDA;

(j) Monitor the enforcement of national and regional laws, ordinances, rules and regulations relative to the rehabilitation and development of Lake Lanao;

(k) Report annually, or as required, to the LLRDA Board and the OCM of its activities including those in collaboration with various government and private entities engaged in the implementation of the programs and projects; and

(l) Perform other related functions as may be directed by the LLRDA Board and other competent authority.

**Section 6. Structure of the Authority.** – The LLRDA shall be composed of the LLRDA Board and the Executive Office. The LLRDA Board shall serve as the policy making-body with respect to the implementation of the Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Master Plan, while the Executive Office implements the policies, plans, and initiatives of the LLRDA.

**Section 7. LLRDA Board Composition.** - The LLRDA Board is composed of the following:

a) Chairman, Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority (LLRDA) as Chair, who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister;
b) Minister of Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy (MENRE) as Co-Chair;
c) Minister of Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) as Member;
d) Minister of Ministry of Public Works (MPW) as Member;
e) Minister of Ministry of Trade, Investment, and Tourism (MTIT) as Member;
f) Minister of Ministry of Transportation and Communication (MOTC) as Member;
g) Minister of Ministry of Finance and Budget Management (MFBM) as Member;
h) Director General, Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) Member;
i) Representative from the Provincial Government of Lanao del Sur; and
j) Two (2) Representatives from Non-Government Organizations (NGOs).

The representatives of the NGOs shall be appointed by the Chief Minister from among the list of nominated representatives.

**Section 8. Powers and Functions of the LLRDA Board.** – The LLRDA Board shall have the following power and functions:

a) Approve the Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Master Plan, its programs and projects in accordance with the Bangsamoro Development Plan;

b) Formulate, prescribe, amend, and repeal rules and regulations that govern the conduct of business of the Authority;

c) Approve plans, programs, and projects deemed necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act;

d) Determine the organizational structure and staffing pattern of LLRDA; subject to the approval of the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management;

e) Approve the annual and/or supplemental budget of the Authority, for consideration of the Office of the Chief Minister and the Parliament;

f) Determine the rate of allowances, honoraria, and other remuneration of the members of the LLRDA Board to take effect during the term of the succeeding Board, subject to the approval by the Chief Minister; and

g) Perform such other acts and perform such other functions as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.

**Section 9. Chair of LLRDA Board.** – The Chair of the Board shall be the Executive Director of the Authority and shall exercise overall administrative supervision to ensure the effective and efficient implementation of the policies laid down by the Authority. The Chairman shall have the rank and salary of a Director General pursuant to the Standard Position and Classification and Compensation System being adopted by the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM).

**Section 10. Powers and Functions of the Chairman.** The Chairman shall have the following powers and duties:

a) Submit for consideration of the LLRDA Board the policies and measures which he/she believes to be necessary to carry out the purposes and provisions of this Act;

b) Execute and administer the policies, plans, programs, and projects approved by the LLRDA Board;

c) Direct and supervise the operation and internal administration of the Authority. He/she may delegate some of his/her administrative responsibilities to other officers of the Authority subject to the rules and regulations of the LLRDA Board;

d) Appoint all subordinate officers and employees who shall enjoy security of tenure and may be removed for cause in accordance with law. He/she may engage the services of experts/consultants either on full time or part-time basis, as may be required in the performance of his/her functions and duties;

e) Submit quarterly reports to the LLRDA Board on personnel selection, placement and training, and exercise the power to discipline subordinate officials and employees under the provisions of law;

f) Submit to the Chief Minister and to the Bangsamoro Parliament an annual report and such other reports as may be required, including the details of the annual and supplemental budgets of the Authority; and

g) Perform such other functions as may be provided by law.

**Section 11. Structure of the Executive Office.** – The Executive Office shall be composed of the Office of the Executive Director, the Office of the Deputy Executive Director, and their immediate staff, and the divisions under it such as a) Research and Planning Division, b) Project Development Division, c) Operations Division, d) Regulation, Monitoring, and Evaluation Division, and e) Finance and Administrative Division.

**Section 12. Location of the Principal Office.** – The LLRDA shall maintain its principal office within the Province of Lanao del Sur where Lake Lanao is located, but it may have branch or field offices in other places necessary for the conduct of its operation.

**CHAPTER III**
**MEETINGS OF THE BOARD**

**Section 13. LLRDA Board Meetings.** – The LLRDA Board shall hold regular meeting once every three (3) months in its principal office or in any place the Board may choose to hold such meeting. The Board shall be convened by the Chair or upon written request signed by the majority of the members.

**Section 14. Quorum.** – A majority of the members of the LLRDA Board shall constitute a quorum for the conduct of business.

**Section 15. Per Diems and Allowances.** – The members of the LLRDA Board shall receive a per diem for each meeting actually attended, the amount of which shall be determined by the Board, consistent with government accounting and auditing rules and regulations.

**CHAPTER IV**
**LAKE LANAO REHABILITATION AND DEVELOPMENT MASTER PLAN**

**Section 16. The 30-year Master Plan.** - The 30-year Master shall include, among others, the assessment of physical and natural resources and potentialities of the Lake Lanao Area, particularly its social and economic conditions, hydrological characteristics, power potentials, development of scenic and tourists spots, conservation of water resources. It shall also include proposed projects towards the rehabilitation of Lake Lanao, such as but not limited to watershed protection and management, flood control projects, Lake Lanao bio-diversity revival and restoration, improvement and maintenance of the desirable water quality of Lake Lanao, and socio-economic projects utilizing the raw materials in the area. It takes into consideration the existing Bangsamoro Development Plan and other thematic and sectoral plans of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

The Master Plan shall serve as the roadmap that will guide the conduct of programs, projects, and activities for the optimal realization of the objectives of this Act in rehabilitating and developing the Lake Lanao Area that will likewise ensure food security, promotion of eco-tourism, and the prevention of undue ecological disturbances, deterioration, and pollution.

The strategic interventions, programs, and other activities in Lake Lanao from the provincial to the local levels shall be anchored on the Master Plan. It shall be regularly reviewed and evaluated to determine the accomplishment of its targets.

**Section 17. Sources of Funds and Operating Budget of LLRDA.** – To carry out the purposes of this Act, initial operations of the LLRDA shall be charged against the Contingent Fund of the 2023 General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro. Thereafter, the annual expenditures including capital outlays of the LLRDA shall be provided in the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

**CHAPTER V**
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 18. Mandatory Review on the Implementation of this Act.** – Every ten (10) years after the effectivity of this Act, an independent review panel composed of experts to be appointed by the Chief Minister shall review the policies, projects, and programs of the LLRDA and shall make recommendations, based on its findings, to the Chief Minister and to the Bangsamoro Parliament.

**Section 19. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The LLRDA Board, within one hundred twenty (120) days after the approval of this Act, shall issue the necessary rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act.

**Section 20. Interpretation and Construction.** – Any doubt in the interpretation of any provision of this Act shall be liberally construed in favor of the fulfillment of the objectives of this Act.

**Section 21. Separability Clause.** – If, for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 22. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 23. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
