**AN ACT**
**STRENGTHENING DISASTER PREPAREDNESS AND RESPONSE, THEREBY REQUIRING ALL LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION TO ESTABLISH A STANDARD EVACUATION CENTER IN EVERY MUNICIPALITY, PROVIDING MECHANISMS THEREOF, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:*

**Section 1. Title. -** This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro Evacuation Center Act of 2022”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy. -** It is a declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure the safety and general welfare of its constituents especially in crisis and disaster situations.

**Section 3. Objectives. -** This Act generally aims to strengthen disaster preparedness and response of the Bangsamoro Government. Specifically, it has the following objectives:

a) Come up with a standard design of evacuation centers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, based on the building code of the Philippines;
b) Set plans and policies for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the evacuation centers;
c) Establish standard staffing and capacity building measures in order to ensure operation and maintenance of the buildings;
d) Define the roles and responsibilities of the stakeholders in the use of the facilities;
e) Enable the local government units to have a designated center for prepositioning, stocking, repacking and piling of relief goods; and
f) Serve as the safe space for the communities affected by disaster especially within the area of jurisdiction.

**Section 4. Establishment of Evacuation Centers. -** The Bangsamoro Government shall establish in every municipality or city a standard evacuation center for disaster preparedness and response. The standard design and construction shall be prepared by the Ministry of Public Works (MPW) in Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Each evacuation center shall be located in safe and accessible spaces of the municipality or city.

The evacuation center shall accommodate at least 100 to 200 internally displaced persons (IDPs) with due consideration on the standard protocols for infectious diseases.

**Section 5. Facilities in the Evacuation Centers. -** The evacuation centers shall be equipped with proper ventilation, kitchen, water, plumbing, sanitation support structures, and facilities that will cater to women, especially breastfeeding mothers, as well as for the elderly and those with special needs.

**Section 6. Evacuation Center’s Management. -** The evacuation centers shall be under the direct supervision of the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) and shall be managed by the Municipal or City Social Welfare Officers (M/CSWDOs) in their respective areas.

The center shall have a designated office for the M/CSWDOs, which will serve as their municipal or city office.

**Section 7. Complementation for the Operation and Maintenance. -** The local government unit shall complement the manpower of the MSSD through collaboration with the Municipal Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Officer (MDRRMO) for the operation and maintenance of the evacuation center.

**Section 8. Coverage. -** This Act shall cover all municipalities and cities within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. In case of the Department of Social Welfare and Development in the province of Basilan and the city of Marawi, which services were already devolved to the local government units, the said LGUs are encouraged to implement this Act.

**Section 9. Appropriations. -** The funds necessary for the establishment of evacuation centers and the corresponding operation and maintenance shall be included in the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 10. Separability Clause. -** If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 11. Repealing Clause. -** All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 12. Effectivity. -** This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its full publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
