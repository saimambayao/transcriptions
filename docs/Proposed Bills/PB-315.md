**AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING THE USE OF “QUICK RESPONSE” (QR) CODES FOR ALL DOCUMENTS ISSUED BY BANGSAMORO MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES, AND RECOGNIZING ALL PHOTOCOPIED DOCUMENTS BEARING THE QR CODE AS CERTIFIED TRUE AND CORRECT**

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:*

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** This act shall be known as the “Quick Response (QR) Code Act of 2024.”

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy and Principles.** The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao government adopts the principle of Moral Governance in the discharge of its mandates. This is to provide a quality, honest, efficient, and credible service for the Bangsamoro people.

To provide a more efficient and effective service to the Bangsamoro people, the government shall align with existing national laws promoting the use of electronic documents and signatures, such as, but not limited to, Republic Act No. 8792, otherwise known as the Electronic Commerce Act of 2000.

The Bangsamoro government shall further align with the trend of technological advancement, ensuring that its services are efficient, accessible, and in line with global standards.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.** For purposes of this act, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

a. **Issuing Office** – refers to the ministries, offices, or agencies of the Bangsamoro government that issued the official documents.
b. **Official Documents** – refers to documents issued and/or released by the ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro government.
c. **Quick Response (QR) Code** – refers to codes that store significant data relative to the content of a document where it is printed and could allow the user to access such information when scanned.
d. **Receiving Office** – refers to the ministries, offices, or agencies of the Bangsamoro government where the official documents have been submitted.

**SECTION 4. Scope and Coverage.** The use of the QR code provided in this act shall be observed by all ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro government.

**SECTION 5. Use of Quick Response (QR) Code.** – All official documents issued by ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro government shall bear a quick response (QR) code.

The code shall have security features that shall be provided by the issuing office.

**SECTION 6. Recognition of Official Documents with QR Codes.** For purposes of authenticity, all official documents issued by the ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro government, bearing QR Codes, shall be recognized as true and correct: *Provided*, That before the receiving office accepts and considers the submitted official documents as genuine, the necessary security features on the QR codes must first be satisfied.

**SECTION 7. Training and Seminar.** All personnel who are assigned to the information and communication technology section of the Bangsamoro ministries, offices, and agencies shall undertake training and seminars on the safe and proper use of QR codes.

The training and seminars shall be conducted by the Bangsamoro Information and Communication Technology Office (BICTO). The program shall include, but not be limited to the following:

a. Creation of QR Codes;
b. Providing security features to the QR Codes; and
c. Determination of the authenticity of the documents bearing QR Codes;

**SECTION 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** The Bangsamoro Information and Communication Technology Office (BICTO) shall promulgate rules and regulations to be adopted by the Bangsamoro ministries, agencies, and offices, within 90 days from the effectivity of this Act.

**SECTION 9. Penalty.** Any person who violates this act shall be held liable under articles 171 or 172 of the Revised Penal Code and other applicable laws.

**SECTION 10. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 11. Repealing Clause.** All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 12. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in the Official Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
