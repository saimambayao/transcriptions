**AN ACT**
**ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO STATE UNIVERSITY IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro State University Act of 2025”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect and promote the right of all Bangsamoro people to quality and accessible education at all levels and to establish a regional university system that is committed to academic excellence and responsive to the development goals of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), particularly in the fields of science and technology, health, engineering, agriculture, education, peacebuilding, public governance, and other related fields.

**Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro State University.** – There is hereby established a public higher education institution to be known as the Bangsamoro State University (BSU), which shall be located in the Municipality of Kabuntalan, Maguindanao del Norte.

**Section 4. General Mandate.** – The BSU shall primarily offer professional training and advanced instruction in education, engineering, science and technology, arts and humanities, information and communication technology, liberal arts, health-related programs, and other relevant fields of study. It shall also conduct research, extension services, and production activities, and shall provide progressive leadership in its areas of specialization in support of the socioeconomic development of the BARMM.

**Section 5. Curricular Offerings.** – The BSU shall offer technical-vocational, undergraduate, and graduate programs in education, engineering, science and technology, arts and humanities, information and communication technology, liberal arts, legal management, law, public administration, health sciences, entrepreneurship, and other relevant fields consistent with the development priorities of the Bangsamoro region, as may be determined by the Board of Regents. Program offerings shall be regularly reviewed and updated to ensure responsiveness to evolving academic, technological, and labor market demands.

Subject to applicable agreements with other institutions, the BSU may establish new branches or extension campuses in collaboration with other academic institutions, as it deems appropriate, to improve access to high-quality education, foster academic innovation and advancement, and enhance its research capabilities.

**Section 6. Administration.** – The administration of the BSU and the exercise of its corporate powers shall be vested in the Board of Regents and the President of the BSU. The University shall have institutional autonomy as a chartered state university within the Bangsamoro education system.

**Section 7. Governing Board of the BSU.** – The governing body of the BSU shall be the Board of Regents, hereinafter referred to as the Board.

The Board shall be composed of the Minister of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education, who shall be the chairman of the Board, the President of the University as the Vice Chairman of the Board, and the following as members:

a) Chairperson of the Committee of Education – Bangsamoro Parliament;
b) Director General of the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA);
c) Minister of Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST);
d) Municipal Mayor of Kabuntalan, Maguindanao del Norte;
e) President of the Federation of Faculty Association of the BSU;
f) President of the Federation of Student Councils of the BSU; and
g) Representative of the non-teaching or personnel association of the BSU.

The representatives from the faculty, student body, and non-teaching personnel shall be duly elected in accordance with the guidelines set by the Board.

The President of the federation of faculty association, the President of the federation of student councils, and the Representative of the non-teaching personnel shall serve concurrently in the Board and their terms of office as provided under their respective associations’ constitutions and by-laws.

In case of vacancy among appointive positions in the Board, the vacancy shall be filled by the Board following guidelines set by it, and the appointee shall hold office only for the unexpired portion of the term.

**Section 8. Powers and Duties of the Board.** – In addition to its general powers of administration and the powers granted to the Board of Directors of a corporation under existing law, the Board shall have the following specific powers and duties:

a) Promulgate and implement policies in accordance with the declared State policies on education, other pertinent provisions of the Constitution on education, agriculture, science, and technology, and the policies, standards, and thrusts of the Bangsamoro Education Code or Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 18 of 2021;
b) Appoint, upon the recommendation of the President of BSU, the vice presidents, deans, directors, faculty members, and other officials and employees of BSU;
c) Confer the usual honorary degrees upon persons in recognition of learning, statesmanship, or eminence in literature, science, or art: *Provided, That* such degrees shall not be conferred in consideration of the payment of money or other valuable consideration;
d) Fix and adjust the salaries of faculty members, administrative officials, and employees, subject to the provisions of the Revised Compensation and Classification System and other pertinent budget and compensation laws governing hours of service, and such other duties and conditions as it may deem proper;
e) Hear and decide administrative cases or matters brought before it on appeal from the Office of the President involving BSU officials and employees, in accordance with the Civil Service Commission (CSC) rules on administrative cases;
f) Set the policies on the admission and graduation of students;
g) Establish research and extension centers to promote the development of BSU;
h) Establish and implement policy guidelines and procedures for participatory, transparent, and accountable decision-making within BSU, including the delegation of powers as may be appropriate;
i) Authorize the construction or repair of its buildings, machinery, equipment, and other facilities, and the purchase and acquisition of real property, including necessary supplies, materials, and equipment;
j) Develop consortia and other forms of linkages with local government units, institutions, and agencies, both public and private, local and foreign, in furtherance of the purposes and objectives of BSU;
k) Develop academic arrangements for institutional capability building with appropriate institutions and agencies, public and private, local and foreign, and appoint experts as consultants or visiting or exchange professors, scholars, or researchers, as the case may be; and
l) Promulgate rules and regulations as may be necessary to carry out the purposes and functions of BSU.

**Section 9. Board Meetings.** – The Board shall regularly convene once every three (3) months. The Chairman of the Board may, however, call a special meeting whenever necessary: *Provided, That* members are notified in writing at least three (3) days prior to the conduct of said meeting.

A quorum of the Board shall consist of a majority of all its members holding office at the time of the meeting: *Provided, however, That* the Chairman of the Board is among those present at the meeting.

In the absence of the Chairperson, the Director General for Higher Education, when duly designated, shall preside over the meeting. In all other cases, the Vice Chairperson shall act as presiding officer.

The members of the Board shall not receive any salary, but shall be entitled to reimbursements for actual and necessary expenses incurred, either for their attendance at the meetings of the Board or in connection with other official business authorized by resolutions of the Board, subject to existing laws and regulations.

**Section 10. The President.** – The BSU shall be headed by a President who shall be appointed by the Board, subject to its guidelines on qualifications and standards, and based on the recommendation of a search committee duly constituted for the purpose of selecting the President.

The President shall render full-time service, serve a term of five (5) years, and may be reappointed to another term: *Provided, That* this provision shall not adversely affect the term of the incumbent President.

Within six (6) months before the expiration of the term of office of the incumbent President of BSU, the Board shall constitute the Search Committee for the Presidency.

In case of a vacancy in the Office of the President by reason of death, compulsory retirement, resignation, removal for cause, or incapacity to perform the functions of the Office, the Board shall designate an Officer-in-Charge of BSU, pending the appointment of a new President: *Provided, That* the Officer-in-Charge shall serve only during the unexpired portion of the term.

The salary of the President of BSU shall be in accordance with the Revised Compensation and Position Classification System and shall be comparable to that received by the presidents of similar educational institutions.

The President shall be assisted by a Vice President for Academic Affairs, a Vice President for Administration, and a Vice President for Research, Development, and Extension, who shall be appointed by the Board upon the recommendation of the President of BSU.

The President shall be subject to periodic performance evaluation by the Board based on transparent and merit-based criteria.


**Section 11. The Secretary.** – There shall be a Secretary of the University who shall be appointed by the Board. The Secretary shall serve both the Board and the University, and shall be responsible for keeping all records and minutes of the meetings of the Board.

The Secretary shall also ensure that each member of the Board receives proper notice of board meetings.

**Section 12. The Treasurer.** – The Treasurer of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) shall serve as the ex-officio Treasurer of the BSU.

**Section 13. Position Structure and Staffing Pattern.** – The position structure and staffing pattern of the BSU shall be in accordance with the Civil Service Law, rules, and regulations. The positions created pursuant thereto shall be filled through regular appointments.

**Section 14. Faculty.** – No political belief, gender preference, cultural or community affiliation, ethnic origin, or religious opinion or affiliation shall be a matter of inquiry in the appointment of faculty members of the BSU: *Provided, That* such appointments shall be subject to the guidelines, qualifications, and standards set by the Board: *Provided, further*, That no member of the faculty shall teach for or against any particular church or religious sect.

**Section 15. Academic Freedom and Institutional Autonomy.** – The BSU shall enjoy academic freedom and institutional autonomy in accordance with paragraph (2), Section 5, Article XIV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution.

**Section 16. Report to the Parliament.** – The MBHTE shall submit to the Parliament an initial report on the implementation of this Act within six (6) months from its effectivity, and an annual report every year thereafter.

**Section 17. Accountability.** – All accounts and expenditures of the BSU shall be subject to audit by the Commission on Audit (COA).

**Section 18. Authority to Loan or Transfer Apparatus, Equipment, Supplies, and Detail of Personnel.** – The heads of ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro Government are hereby authorized to loan or transfer, upon the request of the President of the BSU, such apparatus, equipment, or supplies as may be needed by the University, and to detail employees for duty therein when, in their judgment, such apparatus, equipment, supplies, or services of the employees can be spared without serious detriment to the public service.

Employees so detailed shall perform such duties as may be required of them by the President of the BSU, and the time so employed shall be counted as part of their regular government service.

**Section 19. Development Plan, Management Audit, and Organizational, Administrative, and Academic Structure.** – Within one hundred twenty (120) days from the approval of this Act, the BSU shall undertake the following:

a) Submit a five (5)-year development plan, including its corresponding program budget, to the MBHTE for recommendation to the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management (MFBM);
b) Undergo a management audit in coordination with the MBHTE;
c) Establish its organizational, administrative, and academic structure, including the appointment of key officials; and
d) Initiate the establishment of at least four (4) separate academic degree-granting units, one of which shall be in the arts and sciences, and another unit dedicated to technical-vocational education and training programs duly recognized or accredited by the MBHTE or other competent authorities.

**Section 20. Tax Exemptions and Incentives.** – The Bangsamoro State University (BSU) shall enjoy the following tax exemptions and incentives:

a) The importation by the BSU, upon the recommendation of the MBHTE, of books, publications, and other educational materials intended for economic, technical, vocational, scientific, philosophical, historical, or cultural purposes shall be exempt from customs duties, in accordance with the provisions of Republic Act No. 10863, otherwise known as the *Customs Modernization and Tariff Act (CMTA)*;
b) All grants, bequests, endowments, donations, legacies, and contributions made to the BSU, which are actually, directly, and exclusively used for its educational, research, extension, or student support purposes, shall be exempt from donor’s tax. These shall likewise be allowed as deductions from the gross income of the donor for purposes of computing taxable income, in accordance with the *National Internal Revenue Code of 1997*, as amended, and the *Bangsamoro Revenue Code*; and
c) Provided, That such donations or contributions shall not be disposed of, transferred, or sold by the University for purposes other than those intended by the donor, unless otherwise allowed under existing laws and regulations.

**Section 21. Appropriations.** – The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the Contingent Fund. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual budget of the MBHTE under the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

**Section 22. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The MBHTE, in coordination with the BSU, the MFBM, and other concerned institutions, shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act.

**Section 23. Separability Clause.** – If, for any reason, any part or provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**Section 24. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules, and regulations inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 25. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
