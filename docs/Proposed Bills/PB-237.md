**AN ACT**
**PROMOTING ADEQUATE COMPENSATION AND ADDITIONAL BENEFITS TO PUBLIC HEALTH WORKERS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**BE IT ENACTED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** This act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Public Health Workers Compensation Ac of 2023t."

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure and support, among other things, the promotion of the health and safety of the people, and instill health consciousness among them. Cognizant of the crucial role of nurses and healthcare workers in providing accessible and acceptable health services at the grassroots level, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the promotion of their rights and welfare, and the faithful performance of their duties and functions, through the recognition of their rights as civil servants and the provision of remuneration, benefits, allowances, and other entitlements commensurate to the service they render to the public.

Towards this end, it shall enhance its healthcare system by attracting and retaining a competent healthcare workforce by providing a competitive salary package and benefits for Bangsamoro healthcare workers, who are at the forefront of the delivery of healthcare at the grassroots level, and which shall effectively harness their potential as partners in regional development.

**Section 3. Coverage.** This Act shall apply to all active and regularly performing public health workers employed within the BARMM. including physicians, nurses, nursing assistants, midwives, laboratory and medical technicians, and pharmacists employed in public hospitals, public sanitaria, public health infirmaries, government health centers, rural health units, barangay health stations, clinics, and other health-related establishments owned and operated by the Government or its political subdivisions with original charters.

**Section 4. Salary Enhancement for Public Health Workers.** All public health workers who are actively and regularly performing their duties shall be entitled to a monthly honorarium in the amount of not less than Three thousand pesos (Php 3,000.00).

The monthly honoraria shall be implemented in a phased manner over a period of three years, with annual increments to ensure a smooth transition and sustainable financial planning.

It shall be based on a comprehensive evaluation of existing salary scales, market standards, and regional economic factors to establish fair and competitive compensation for all public health workers. Special consideration shall be given to factors such as qualifications, years of service, and specialized skills in determining the appropriate monthly honoraria.

**Section 5. Additional Benefits for Public Health Workers.** The following incentives and benefits shall likewise be granted to all public health workers:

a. Hazard Allowance – Physicians shall receive an additional Ten thousand pesos (Php 10,000.00), while all other health workers shall receive an additional Five thousand pesos (Php 5,000.00) monthly as hazard pay while serving during an emergency health crisis or outbreak, as declared by the Bangsamoro Government;

b. Subsistence Allowance – All public health workers who render service within the premises of isolated barangay health stations shall be entitled to subsistence allowance, which in no case shall be less than one hundred pesos (Php 100.00) per day, subject to existing laws, rules and regulations;

c. Transportation Allowance – All public health workers shall be entitled to a monthly transportation allowance amounting to not less than One thousand pesos (Php 1,000.00);

d. Emergency Assistance – All public health workers shall be entitled to an emergency assistance amounting to Five thousand pesos (Php 5,000.00) for expenses incurred during health emergencies;

e. Death and Burial Benefits – In the event of the death of the public health worker during their incumbency, they shall be entitled to death and burial benefits amounting to Twenty thousand pesos (Php 20,000.00) to be paid to their respective legal beneficiaries.

**Section 6. Appropriations.** The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the budget of the Ministry of Health (MOH) in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act of the year following the enactment into law and thereafter.

**Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within ninety (90) days upon approval of this Act, the MOH, in coordination with relevant agencies and stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of the provisions of this Act.

**Section 8. Separability Clause.** If any section or part of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 9. Repealing Clause.** All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders, or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 10. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
