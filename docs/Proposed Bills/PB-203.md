**AN ACT**

**ESTABLISHING PEACE AND CULTURE CENTERS IN OTHER BANGSAMORO COMMUNITIES AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

**BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the "OBC Peace and Culture Centers Act."

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote sustainable development, protect the rights, and enhance the economic, social, and cultural development of Bangsamoro people residing in communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**SECTION 3. Establishment of the OBC Peace and Culture Centers.** – The Bangsamoro Government shall establish Peace and Culture Centers in the Bangsamoro communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. These Centers shall be strategically established in such provinces or cities with significant Bangsamoro constituents, as determined by the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC). They shall serve as hubs or channels for various programs and services aimed at enhancing the social, cultural, and economic development of the Other Bangsamoro Communities (OBCs). The objectives of these Centers shall be as follows:

(a) Enhance the social development of OBCs by improving their access to education, healthcare, social protection, and livelihood opportunities;

(b) Foster resilient, sustainable, equitable, and inclusive economic opportunities for OBCs by promoting community development and addressing their unique challenges;

(c) Preserve, celebrate, and integrate the unique cultural heritage, identity, and history of OBCs into mainstream society;

(d) Advocate for peace in OBCs and their surrounding communities through education, dialogue, understanding, peaceful co-existence, and harmony;

(e) Protect and promote the rights of Bangsamoro people residing in communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region through establishing and/or strengthening peace and other coordinating mechanisms and platforms;

(f) Improve the reach of the OOBC by channelling its consultation activities, coordination mechanisms, support and other services through the Peace and Culture Centers.


**SECTION 4. Programs and Services of the OBC Peace and Culture Centers.** – In coordination and collaboration with the appropriate government agencies and concerned local government units (LGUs), the OOBC shall develop the following programs and services to be implemented in the Peace and Culture Centers:

a) **Peace and Culture Program.** – This program aims at developing and implementing interventions that promote peace, understanding, and cultural preservation and appreciation within OBCs. It includes education and information initiatives, awareness campaigns, community fora, workshops, symposiums, and dialogues, sports for peace activities, language and cultural exchange, peace and cultural exhibitions, festivals, and fairs, and other events and activities that foster mutual respect, tolerance, and harmony among OBCs and their neighbouring communities;

b) **Livelihood Program.** – This program aims to create sustainable and inclusive economic opportunities for OBCs. It focuses on improving the organizational, management, and entrepreneurial skills of the OBC members, on developing their cooperatives, social enterprises, and other business organizations, and on promoting halal business and tourism. By supporting their enterprises and local industries, this program seeks to improve the economic resilience and inclusive and sustainable growth of OBCs;

c) **Women and Youth Development Program.** – This program is dedicated to empowering women and youth within OBCs through capacity building, leadership development, and increasing their access to resources and opportunities. It focuses on addressing gender and age-related disparities and promoting the active participation of women and youth in decision-making processes within their communities;

d) **Social Services.** – This aims to bridge the gap between OBCs and their access to critical services such as health, education, and social welfare. This is achieved through collaboration with relevant government agencies and partners, enhancing the social development and general well-being of OBCs; and

e) **Such Other Tailored Programs and Services for OBCs.** – This includes any other programs and services that cater specifically to the unique needs and contexts of the OBCs, as may be determined by the implementing ministries, offices, or agencies of the Bangsamoro Government based on the consultations and recommendations of the OOBC.

**SECTION 5. Program Guidelines.** – The OOBC, in consultation with relevant implementing agencies, shall establish clear guidelines for each program or service in Section 4, outlining objectives, expected outcomes, implementation processes, monitoring and evaluation mechanisms, and roles and responsibilities of stakeholders. These guidelines shall provide a roadmap for program execution, ensure consistency in operations, and provide a basis for assessing program performance and making necessary improvements.

**SECTION 6. Implementation.** – The Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), through the OOBC, shall be the lead agency responsible for the implementation of this Act. To ensure effective implementation, the OCM shall adopt a responsive staffing pattern for the OOBC, ensuring that it is equipped with the necessary manpower and expertise.

**SECTION 7. OBC Peace and Culture Center Development Plans.** – The OOBC, in collaboration with the appropriate government agencies and concerned LGUs and in consultation with the concerned OBCs and their organizations, shall formulate comprehensive development plans for each Peace and Culture Center. These plans shall identify the specific needs of the OBCs, propose other suitable programs and services, and outline a strategy for successful implementation. The development plans will ensure the Centers are responsive to the unique needs and contexts of the respective OBCs, fostering their sustainable growth and development. Each development plan shall be reviewed and updated every three (3) years or as deemed necessary to ensure its continued relevance and effectiveness.

**SECTION 8. Management of the OBC Peace and Culture Centers.** – The OOBC shall be the lead caretaker of the Peace and Culture Centers, which shall be co-managed by the recognized and accredited organizations of the OBCs utilizing them. As such, the OOBC shall facilitate the organization of the management committees of the Centers.

The OOBC shall also encourage and facilitate the management committees of the Centers in adopting their respective revenue raising and income generating strategies as well as financial management policies. The management committees shall have autonomy in allocating and utilizing the funds generated from the implementation of such strategies for the maintenance and operations of the Centers and for complementing and supporting their programs and services.

In managing the Peace and Culture Centers, the OOBC and the management committees of the Centers shall ensure their effective functioning as dynamic hubs for social, cultural, and economic development of OBCs, coordinating resources, implementing diverse programs, and conducting regular performance assessments.


**SECTION 9. Grievance and Dispute Resolution Mechanisms within the OBC Peace and Culture Centers.** – Robust grievance and dispute resolution mechanisms shall be established by the OOBC within the Peace and Culture Centers, in consulation with their management committees. These mechanisms shall address conflicts or issues related to the operations, programs, and services of the Centers arising among the members or organizations of the OBCs.

The mechanisms shall be designed to be impartial, transparent, and easily accessible, ensuring all members or organizations of the OBCs have the opportunity to express their grievances or conflicts in a safe and respectful environment. Confidentiality shall be maintained throughout the processes, and all issues shall be addressed in a timely and effective manner.

In cases of complex disputes or those involving external parties, the OOBC may engage the appropriate local government unit, and/or regional or national government agencies for mediation, arbitration, or legal recourse. The overarching goal is to ensure harmonious relationships and an environment of peace and mutual respect within the Peace and Culture Centers.

**SECTION 10. Intergovernmental and Inter-Agency Coordination and Collaboration.** – All relevant agencies of the Bangsamoro Government shall extend their full support, cooperation, and assistance to the OOBC in the implementation of this Act. For this purpose, a **Coordinating Council for OBCs** headed by the OCM shall be hereby established. Said Coordinating Council shall function as the primary body to facilitate cohesive inter-agency efforts for the successful implementation of this Act. It shall work on the coordination of programs and services, ensure optimal resource allocation, and promote the exchange of information and best practices among the agencies involved. It shall also work closely with the OOBC to identify areas of cooperation, address challenges, and review progress towards the objectives if the Act.

Furthermore, the OOBC shall establish partnerships and collaborations with concerned local government units, national government agencies, international organizations, non-government organizations, the private sector, and other stakeholders to ensure the effective implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 11. Monitoring and Evaluation.** – A comprehensive monitoring and evaluation system shall be established by OOBC to measure the progress and impact of this Act. Regular data collection, reporting, and analysis of key performance indicators related to each program or service under this Act shall be conducted. Findings from monitoring and evaluation activities shall be used to inform program or service improvements.

**SECTION 12. Appropriation and Funding.** – The necessary amount needed to implement the provisions of this Act shall be included in the Annual Appropriation of the Bangsamoro Government. The Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament shall provide sufficient and sustainable funding for the construction, management, and operation of the Peace and Culture Centers, as well as for the execution of their programs and services for OBCs. Furthermore, the OCM through the OOBC shall explore additional sources of funding such as grants, donations, and sponsorships from local and international organizations, public-private partnerships, and income-generating activities of the Centers.

**SECTION 13. Transparency.** – The OCM, through the OOBC, is committed to ensuring full transparency in the implementation of this Act. All financial transactions, including allocations, expenditures, income, and donations related to the Peace and Culture Centers, shall be recorded and reported in a timely and accurate manner. Annual financial and performance reports will be published and made accessible to the public. Furthermore, mechanisms will be put in place to encourage public participation in decision-making processes, fostering openness and accountability in the management and operation of the Centers. Any case of misuse or misappropriation of funds shall be strictly dealt with in accordance with existing laws and regulations.

**SECTION 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – Within ninety (90) days from the enactment of this Act, the OCM through the OOBC, in coordination with relevant agencies and partners, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 15. Separability Clause.** – If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 16. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof, inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 17. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Approved.
