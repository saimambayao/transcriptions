**AN ACT**
**PROVIDING FINANCIAL AID FOR MEDICATION MAINTENANCE OF SENIOR CITIZENS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**Section 1. Title.** This Act shall be known as the “*Financial Assistance for Senior Citizens Act.*”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** It shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to adopt an integrated and comprehensive approach to health and other social services available to all people at affordable cost, giving priority to the underprivileged, sick, elderly, disabled, women and children. It shall protect and promote the health and well-being of elderly citizens for their continued meaningful contribution and participation in the society.

Towards this end, it shall provide a monthly financial assistance to senior citizens, enabling them to access and afford necessary maintenance medications, thereby enhancing their quality of life and health outcomes.

**Section 3. Monthly Maintenance Medication Support.** The Bangsamoro Government, through the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) shall provide a monthly financial support amounting to Php 2,000.00 to all senior citizens in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, who are qualified and registered as such under Republic Act No. 9994, otherwise known as the Expanded Senior Citizens Act of 2010.

**Section 4. Eligibility and Registration.** To qualify for the monthly support, a senior citizen must meet the following criteria:

a. Be a bonafide resident of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;
b. Be a registered senior citizen, as defined in Republic Act No. 9994;
c. Submit an application to the MSSD or its designated agency, accompanied by a valid prescription for maintenance medications issued by a licensed medical practitioner within the last six (6) months from the date of application.

Once approved, beneficiaries shall be issued a monthly maintenance medication support card.

**Section 5. Use of the Monthly Support.** The monthly support shall be used exclusively to purchase prescribed maintenance medications, including but not limited to medicines for chronic illnesses, vitamins and supplements from all public or private pharmaceutical establishments, upon presentment of a prescription validly issued within the last six (6) months from the date of purchase. The monthly support shall not be transferrable to any person other than the person to which the same is provided as named in the monthly medication support card. Any unused portion of the support shall not be convertible to cash.

**Section 6. Implementation and Coordination.** The MSSD shall be responsible for the implementation of this Act and shall coordinate with other relevant agencies, including the Ministry of Interior and Local Government, to ensure efficient distribution of the monthly support.

**Section 7. Appropriations.** The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the budget of the MSSD in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act of the year following the enactment into law and thereafter.

**Section 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within sixty (60) days upon approval of this Act, the MSSD shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of the provisions of this Act.

**Section 9. Separability Clause.** If any section or part of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 10. Repealing Clause.** All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders, or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 11. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
