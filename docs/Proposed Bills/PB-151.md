**AN ACT IMPROVING CARE FOR SENIOR CITIZENS AND PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES (PWDs) IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION THROUGH THE CREATION OF SPECIAL HELP DESKS FOR THEM IN ALL GOVERNMENT OFFICES AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro Senior Citizen and PWDs’ Act of 2023”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** - It shall be the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to value the dignity of senior citizens and persons with disabilities (PWDs) and thereby guarantee full respect for human rights. Towards this end, the Bangsamoro government shall give full support and assistance to the improvement of their total well-being by enacting measures for their special considerations through the implementation of programs that can ensure easy access to government services and to fully integrate them into mainstream society.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms.** - For purposes of this Act, these terms are defined as follows:

a) Senior Citizen – This refers to any resident citizen of the Philippines at least sixty (60) years old as defined under Republic Act No. 9994, otherwise known as the “Expanded Senior Citizen Act of 2010.”

b) Persons with Disabilities (PWD) – This includes those who have long-term physical, mental, intellectual or sensory impairments which in interaction with various barriers may hinder their full and effective participation in society on an equal basis with others, as defined under Republic Act No. 10754.³

**Section 4. Establishment of Special Help Desks.** - There is hereby established special help desks for senior citizens and PWDs in all government offices and local government units (LGUs) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

It shall be designated as the desk where the senior citizens and PWDs may make inquiries, ask for any form of assistance, and lodge any complaints. It shall attend to, provide assistance, and address their concerns or problems.

**Section 5. The Role of Local Chief Executives.** – The Provincial Governors, City and Municipal Mayors as the guardian of their respective LGUs, must fill the gap in protecting and overseeing the welfare of the senior citizens and PWDs in their areas of responsibilities.

All LGUs shall advocate the rights and welfare of senior citizens and PWDs, and shall take a proactive stance in the dissemination of information regarding their benefits and privileges.

**Section 6. Coverage.** – This Act shall cover all the government offices and local government units within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulation.** - Within sixty (60) days from the promulgation of this Act, the necessary rules and regulations for the proper implementation of this Act shall be prepared by the Ministry of Social Services and Development in consultation with Ministry of Interior and Local Government.

**Section 8. Appropriations.** - The funds necessary for the establishment of special help desks for senior citizens and PWDs in all government offices and LGUs shall be included in the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and submitted to the Parliament for its approval.

**Section 9. Separability Clause.** - If, for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 10. Repealing Clause.** - All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 11. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
