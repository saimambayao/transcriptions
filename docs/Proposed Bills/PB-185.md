**AN ACT PROVIDING FOR BASIC DIGITAL LITERACY AND INTERMEDIATE INFORMATION AND COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGIES (ICT) SKILLS TRAINING IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**BE IT ENACTED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** This Act shall be known as the “*Bangsamoro Digital Literacy and ICT Skills Training Act of 2023.*”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** The Bangsamoro Government recognizes science and technology as essential to national and regional progress. It shall give priority to science, research, inventions, technology, education, and their development.

It is thus declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote the development of the region’s Information and Communications Technology (ICT) landscape by providing a comprehensive and sustainable digital literacy and intermediate ICT skills training to individuals and groups in the region.

The program will not only enhance the employability of the region’s workforce but will also improve its competitiveness in the global market. It will provide the Bangsamoro people the opportunity to improve their qualifications, increase their employment opportunities, and establish the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region as one of the investment destinations for global outsourced services.

**Section 3. Objectives.** The objectives of this Act are as follows:

A. To provide basic digital literacy skills training to BARMM residents, particularly those who are unemployed, underemployed, or belong to marginalized sectors.

B. To provide intermediate ICT skills training to BARMM residents who have basic digital literacy skills and who want to upgrade their qualifications for employment.

C. To promote the development of the ICT landscape in the BARMM region through institutional development, marketing, talent development, research, and policy development.

D. To establish the BARMM region as one of the investment destinations for global outsourced services.

**Section 4. Basic Digital Literacy and Intermediate ICT Skills Training Program.** - The Bangsamoro Government, through the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), in coordination with the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), shall establish a Basic Digital Literacy and Intermediate ICT Skills Training Program that shall provide free training to interested individuals, particularly those who lack the resources to attend paid training programs. The training program shall cover the following topics:

A. Basic computer operations, such as computer fundamentals and basic troubleshooting, operating systems, and software installation;

B. Basic digital literacy, such as internet and email usage, online communication and social media, cybersecurity and privacy protection, and ethical and legal issues in the use of ICT;

C. Intermediate ICT skills, such as basic programming concepts and languages, database management and SQL, web development and design, mobile application development, network administration and security, programming, graphic design, and video editing;

The training program shall be conducted in a manner that is accessible to all, using various platforms and delivery modes such as online, offline, and blended learning.

The training program shall be conducted by certified trainers and instructors, with the use of industry-standard software and tools, and shall be delivered using various platforms and delivery modes such as online, offline, and blended learning.

**Section 5. Program Components.** - The MBHTE shall institute a Basic Digital Literacy and Intermediate ICT Skills Training Program shall have the following components:

A. Institutional Development – It shall establish regional training centers for basic digital literacy and intermediate ICT skills training, which shall be equipped with modern digital tools and applications.

B. Marketing – It shall develop a marketing campaign to promote the training program and attract potential trainees, employers, and investors.

C. Talent Development - It shall provide scholarships, grants, and other forms of assistance to qualified trainees to enable them to pursue further studies and certification in ICT-related fields.

D. Research - It shall conduct research on the needs and opportunities in the ICT industry in BARMM, and provide policy recommendations to the BARMM government to promote the development of the industry.

E. Policy Development - It shall work with the relevant stakeholders, ministries, offices, and agencies to develop policies and regulations to support the development of the ICT industry in the region.


**Section 6. Appropriations.** The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the budget of the MBHTE’s current budget and shall thereafter be included in the annual Appropriations Act.

**Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** The MBHTE shall issue the necessary rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act.

**Section 8. Separability Clause.** If any section or part of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 9. Repealing Clause.** All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders, or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 10. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
