**AN ACT CREATING THE MINISTRY OF TOURISM, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session:*

**Section 1. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote tourism as a major socio-economic activity respecting, upholding, and maintaining the diverse cultural heritage and the moral and spiritual values of the people in the Bangsamoro Region.

**Section 2. Creation of the Ministry of Tourism (MOT).** – There is hereby created the Ministry of Tourism, hereinafter referred to as the Ministry.

**Section 3. Mandate.** - The Ministry of Tourism shall be the primary government agency charged with the responsibility of encouraging, promoting, and developing tourism as a major socio-economic activity to generate revenues and employment and to spread the benefits of tourism to a wider segment of the Bangsamoro population, with the support, assistance, and cooperation of both private and public sectors.

**Section 4. Powers and Functions.** – The Ministry shall have the following powers and functions:

a. Formulate tourism policies, plans, and projects for the development of tourism as an engine of socioeconomic and cultural growth;
b. Call upon all agencies of the Bangsamoro Government to properly carry out their programs in relation to and in coordination with the policies, plans, and projects of the Ministry and to assist in the implementation thereof;
c. Promulgate rules and regulations governing the operations and activities of all persons, firms, entities, and establishments that cater to tourists;
d. Communicate to the Parliament, and the heads of agencies of the Bangsamoro Government, the impact upon tourism and the economy of proposed governmental actions;
e. Provide an integrated market development program to attract people to visit the BARMM and enhance the prestige of the region and the Bangsamoro people in the national and international community;
f. Provide standards for accreditation of hotels, resorts, and tourist-oriented facilities for classification purposes consistent with national laws;
g. Develop programs to encourage private sector investment and participation in tourism activities and projects;
h. Promote the protection, maintenance, and preservation of historical, cultural, and natural assets;
i. Assist in the enforcement of all laws and regulations for the protection of tourists and other transients;
j. Coordinate with appropriate government agencies the development of infrastructure requirements supporting a tourist zone such as, but not limited to, access roads to the zone, electric power brought to the proper line of the zone, airports, harbors, and other support facilities;
k. Coordinate with concerned government agencies the provision of social infrastructure requirements supporting a tourist zone as educational facilities, health centers, social and recreational outlets, and other necessary amenities for the social upliftment of the populace and preservation of ecological balance;
l. Exercise such other powers and functions that may be devolved, necessary, proper, or incidental to the attainment of its mandate.

**Section 5. Structural Organization.** – The Ministry shall be headed by a Minister and shall be composed of the Ministry Proper, Offices, and their Divisions, and the Provincial and the City Tourism Offices.

**Section 6. The Minister.** – The Minister shall be appointed by the Chief Minister and shall exercise all powers and authority inherent to his/her office and responsibility.

**Section 7. The Deputy Minister.** – The Minister shall be assisted by a Deputy Minister in implementing the policies and in overseeing the operations and program developments of the Ministry. The Deputy Minister shall be appointed by the Chief Minister upon the recommendation of the Minister.

**Section 8. Ministry Services.** - Subject to the power of the Minister, with the approval of the Chief Minister, to reorganize, restructure, and redefine the functions of the Ministry for the effective discharge of its powers and functions under this Act, the Ministry shall have the following services:

(a) *Planning, Product Development, and Coordination Services.* – The Planning, Product Development, and Coordination Services, headed by a Director-General, shall formulate policies, plans, programs, rules, and regulations; review and evaluate the performance of the Tourism Master Plan and advise the Parliament on all matters affecting the tourism program of the region. It shall have the following offices, each headed by a Director II:

(1) *Planning and Policy Development Office.* – The Planning and Policy Development Office shall have the following divisions: Tourism Development Division, and Research and Information Management Division.

(2) *Product Development Office.* – The Product Development Office shall have the following divisions: Market Development Division, and Product Monitoring and Development Division.

(b) *Tourism Promotions Services.* – The Tourism Promotions Services, headed by a Director-General, shall promote the BARMM as a tourist destination domestically and internationally; and devise integrated marketing and promotional activities such as information dissemination, public relations, special events, and related tourist programs. It shall have the following offices, each headed by a Director II:

(1) *Public Affairs and Advocacy Office.* – The Public Affairs and Advocacy Office shall have the following divisions: Public Affairs and Communications Management Division, Advocacy and Creatives Design Division, and Promotions, Marketing, and Operations Division.

(2) *Tourism Standards and Regulation Office.* – The Tourism Standards and Regulation Office shall have the following divisions: Standards Development Division, Project and Investment Evaluation Division, Standards Monitoring and Enforcement Division, and Resource Generation Division.

(c) *Administrative Services Office.* – The Administrative Services Offices, headed by a Director-General, shall have the following offices, divisions, and units:

(1) *Administrative Office.* – The Administrative Office, headed by a Director II, shall be responsible for the administrative management operations of the Ministry on human resources, general services, and cash management.

(2) *Finance and Management Office.* – The Finance and Management Office, headed by a Director II, shall be responsible for all budgetary and financial management activities of the Ministry.

(3) *Legal Division.* – The Legal Division, headed by a Division Chief, shall be the main provider of the legal and advisory services of the Ministry.

(4) *Internal Audit Unit.* – The Internal Audit Unit shall conduct management and operations audit of the Ministry’s functions, programs, and projects; review and appraise systems and procedures; and advise the Minister on all matters relating to management control and operations audit by the Ministry.

**Section 9. Tourism Provincial Field Units.** - The Ministry is authorized to establish, operate and maintain a provincial field unit in each of the provinces of the region.

**Section 10. Transfer of Officers and Personnel.** – The Bureau of Tourism and all operating units of the MTIT with functions and responsibilities dealing with tourism are hereby abolished, and their powers and functions, applicable funds and appropriations, records, equipment, property, and personnel transferred to the Ministry.

**Section 11. Structure and Staffing Pattern.** – Subject to the approval of the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM), the Ministry shall determine its organizational framework and staffing pattern. The authorized positions created therein shall be filled by regular appointments by the Chief Minister or the Minister as the case may be: Provided, That, in the filling of positions created, preference shall be given to the personnel of the defunct Bureau of Tourism: Provided, however, That such individuals comply with the qualification standards set by the Civil Service Commission for the positions that they shall be appointed to: Provided, finally, That, if such individuals possess the same qualifications, seniority shall be given priority.

**Section 12. Transition Period.** - The transfer of functions, assets, funds, equipment, properties, transactions, and personnel of the office and units, and the formulation of the internal organic structure, staffing pattern, operating system, and revised budget of the Ministry, shall be completed within six (6) months from the effectivity of this Act, during which time, the existing personnel shall continue to assume their posts at MTIT in holdover capacities until new appointments are issued: *Provided*, That after the abolition of offices as specified in Section 12 of this Act, the Ministry, in coordination with the MFBM, shall determine and create new positions.

**Section 13. Appropriations.** - The amount needed for the initial implementation of this Act shall be taken from the current fiscal year’s appropriation for tourism under the MTIT. Henceforth, funds sufficient to fully carry out the functions and objectives of the Ministry shall be appropriated every fiscal year in the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

**Section 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The MTIT, the MFBM, and the Bangsamoro Attorney General’s Office shall issue within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**Section 15. Separability Clause.** - If any provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional, the other provisions not so declared shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 16. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations, and other issuances or parts thereof which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Section 17. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
