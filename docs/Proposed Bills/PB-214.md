**AN ACT STRENGTHENING THE SYSTEM OF MANAGEMENT AND ARCHIVAL RECORDS OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT, ESTABLISHING FOR THE PURPOSE THE OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO REGIONAL ARCHIVES, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** - This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Regional Archives Act of 2023".

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. -**

a. It is the policy of the State to conserve, promote, and popularize the Bangsamoro's historical and cultural heritage and resources, as well as artistic creations.

b. All the Bangsamoro's artistic and historic wealth constitutes the cultural treasure of the region and shall be under the protection of the Regional Government, which may regulate its disposition.

c. The Bangsamoro Government shall preserve the history, culture, art traditions, and rich cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their Sultanates, such as those of but not limited to, Sulu, Maguindanao, Kabuntalan, Buayan, the Royal House of Ranao and of the Iranun, and the non-Moro indigenous peoples of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

d. Further, the Bangsamoro Regional Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of the Bangsamoro heritage and culture.

e. Towards this end, all public records with the historical value held by Bangsamoro government offices shall be transferred to an official, permanent, and regional repository for proper management, control, regulation, and preservation of record disposition.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. -** For purposes of this Act, the following definition of terms are hereby adopted:

a) Archival Value - refers to the value attached to records that hold notoriety for the Bangsamoro Government, its people, and the general public because those records make the government accountable to its citizens; provide evidence about public policies and programs; protect or verify individuals' rights and entitlements; and provide information about the important people, issues, places, and events that make up the story of Bangsamoro;

b) Archives - shall refer to:

1. Public records, papers, periodicals, books or other items, articles, or materials, whether in the form of electronic, audiovisual, or print, which by their nature and characteristics have historical and cultural value, that have been selected for permanent preservation;

2. The place (building/room/storage area) where archival materials are kept and preserved; and

3. An organization (or part of an organization) whose main function is to select, collect and preserve archival records and make such records available for public use.

c) Disposal refers to the act of selling, burning, or any other way of discarding valueless records in accordance with the provisions of this Act and other existing laws.

d) Disposition - refers to the systematic sorting out of records in the office storage, whereby those classified as valuable records are permanently preserved, and those which are classified as valueless are disposed of.

e) Document - refers to recorded information regardless of medium or characteristics. Frequently used interchangeably with "records";

f) Inventory - refers to a descriptive listing of the records held by records series indicating its specific location, inclusive dates and volume in cubic meters; conduct of related activities to locate, identify, describe, count, and measure all records in the office and storage areas including all loose and bound papers, microforms, optical disks, and magnetic tapes and disks;

g) Private Archives/Collections - refers to records belonging to private individuals and/or entities that are of archival value;

h) Public Records - refers to records or classes of records, in any form, in whole or in part, created or received, whether before or after the effectivity of this Act, by a Bangsamoro government office or agency in the conduct of its affairs and have been retained by that government agency or its successors as evidence or because of the information contained therein;

i) Records - refers to information, whether in its original form or otherwise, including documents, signatures, seals, texts, images, sounds, speeches, or data compiled, recorded, or stored, as the case may be:

1. in written form on any material; or

2. on film, negative, tape, or other medium to be capable of being reproduced

j) Records Officer - refers to the employee responsible for overseeing the record's management program and providing guidance on adequate and proper record keeping.

**SECTION 4. Objectives.** This Act is primarily aimed at:

A. Organizing and establishing the system of management and administration of the Bangsamoro Government archival records;

B. Ensuring that full and accurate records of the affairs of the Bangsamoro Regional Government are maintained and preserved;

C. Increasing public confidence in the integrity of public records keeping and management;

D. Ensuring accessibility of public records relevant to the promotion and preservation of Bangsamoro cultural heritage; and

E. Supporting the safekeeping of private records.

**ARTICLE II**

**OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO REGIONAL ARCHIVES**

**SECTION 5. Office of the Bangsamoro Regional Archives. -** The Office of the Bangsamoro Regional Archives, hereinafter referred to as OBRA, is hereby created and organized, structurally and functionally, in accordance with the provisions of this Act. It shall be under the Office of the Chief Minister.

The OBRA shall be primarily responsible for the implementation of the objectives and provisions of this Act. It shall plan, develop, prescribe, disseminate, and enforce policies, rules, and regulations and coordinate programs governing the creation, general protection, use, storage, and disposition of public records, including the acquisition, storage, and preservation of public archives and providing facilities for reference, research or other purposes.

**SECTION 6. Powers and Functions. -** In pursuit of its mandate, the OBRA shall have the following powers and functions:

a) Act as the official repository of all agreements, laws, public records, bulletins, books, gazettes, thesis, publications, exhibitions, audio-recording, video-recording or images, and any other writings or documentation, relating to or involving the struggle of the Bangsamoro and the history, culture, tradition, and practices of the Bangsamoro, the Non-Moro Indigenous People, and the settler communities;

b) Conduct an inventory of the above writings and documentation that already exist and under the custody of the Bangsamoro Government;

c) Maintain a registry of all public records under the custody and control of the Bangsamoro Government, which shall be made available for public inspection;

d) Implement a records management and archival administration system for the efficient maintenance, preservation, retention, and utilization of public records of the Bangsamoro Government;

e) Adopt security measures and protection of the public records of the Bangsamoro Government;

f) Obtain, recover, and take custody and management of all public archives that are not in the custody of the Bangsamoro Government;

g) Accept, store, and preserve any public archive transferred or donated to the Bangsamoro Government; and

h) Facilitate the acquisition of public records and private archives that possess archival value through transfer, donation, purchase, or any other legal means.

**SECTION 7. Appropriations. -** The amount of ten million pesos (Php 10,000,000.00) shall be appropriated for the implementation of this Act and shall be sourced from the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act of the year following the approval of this act.

**SECTION 8. Separability Clause. -** If any portion or provision of the Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. -** All laws, parts of laws, issuances, proclamations, orders, ordinances, and administrative regulations in conflict with the provisions of this Act are hereby amended, modified, or repealed accordingly.

**SECTION 10. Effectivity. -** This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from its publication in a newspaper of regional circulation.
