**AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE SHARIFF KABUNSUAN SHRINE IN BARANGAY KALANGANAN 2, COTABATO CITY, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled, as follows:

**Section 1. Title.** *This Act shall be known as the “Shariff Kabunsuan Shrine Act”.*

**Section 2. Legal Bases.** This Act is enacted pursuant to the inherent power of the State to promote and preserve its cultural heritage, as enshrined in Section 2, Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution, and in accordance with Republic Act No. 10066 (National Cultural Heritage Act of 2009), particularly Section 2 thereof declaring the policy of the State to protect and preserve its cultural heritage, and Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law), which mandates the preservation, protection, and promotion of the Bangsamoro's cultural and historical heritage, recognizing the significant role of Islam and historical figures like Shariff Kabunsuan in the region's history.

**Section 3. Declaration of Policy.** It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to preserve, protect, and promote the rich cultural and historical heritage of the Bangsamoro people. Recognizing the pivotal role of Shariff Kabunsuan in the history of the region, the Bangsamoro Government shall establish a shrine in his honor to serve as a beacon of cultural understanding, historical awareness, and a symbol of unity.

**Section 4. Establishment of the Shrine.** There is hereby established a shrine in honor of Shariff Kabunsuan in Barangay Kalanganan 2, Cotabato City, to be known as the Shariff Kabunsuan Shrine.

**Section 5. Purpose.** The Shariff Kabunsuan Shrine shall serve as a historical and cultural landmark, a center for Islamic studies and research, and a tourist destination that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Muslim community in the Philippines.

**Section 6. Definition of Terms.** For the purpose of this Act, the following terms are hereby defined as follows:

a) “Shrine” — A memorial honoring a well-known person or historical event.
b) “Historical and Cultural landmark” — a place, structure, or site that holds significant historical, cultural, or aesthetic value.

**Section 7. Governance and Administration.** The Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) shall be responsible for the overall governance and administration of the Shrine. The specific structure and composition of the governing body, as well as its detailed powers and functions, shall be defined in the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.

**Section 8. Powers and Functions of the Shrine.** The Shrine, through its governing body, shall have the following powers and functions:

a) To manage and administer the shrine and its facilities;
b) To develop and implement plans and programs for the promotion and development of the shrine;
c) To enter into contracts and agreements with private entities and organizations for the benefit of the shrine;
d) To receive and administer donations and grants for the shrine;
e) To establish rules and regulations for the use and maintenance of the shrine.

**Section 9. Funding and Appropriation.** The necessary funds for the purpose of this Act shall be provided for in the annual appropriations for public works and disbursements shall be made by the Bangsamoro Commission on the Preservation of Cultural Heritage.

**Section 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).** Within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act, BCPCH, in coordination with the MTIT and other relevant concerned stakeholders shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**Section 11. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 12. Repealing Clause.** All laws, decrees, orders, and issuances inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**Section 13. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Official Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation within the BARMM.
