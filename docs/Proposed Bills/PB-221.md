**AN ACT CREATING THE BUREAU OF AGRICULTURAL AND FISHERIES ENGINEERING (BAFE) OF BARMM AND STRENGTHENING AND/OR ESTABLISHING THE AGRICULTURAL AND BIOSYSTEMS ENGINEERING SERVICES (ABES) IN THE BARMM LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS TO ACCELERATE AGRICULTURE AND FISHERIES MODERNIZATION AND RURAL AGRO-INDUSTRIALIZATION**

Be it enacted by the Parliament by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

**ARTICLE 1**
**Short Title and Declaration of Policies**

**SEC. 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bureau of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Services Act of 2020 of the BARMM.”

**SEC. 2. Declaration of Policies.** – It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Regional Government to provide timely, adequate, quality and responsive agricultural and biosystems engineering services to facilitate, support and accelerate agriculture and fisheries modernization and rural agro-industrialization.

To carry out the above declared policies, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority shall create the Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering of Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BAFE-BARMM) and strengthen and institutionalize its agricultural and biosystems engineering services at the Local Government Units consistent with policies and guidelines under Republic Act No. 10915 (Philippine Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Act of 2016), R.A. No. 8435 (Agricultural and Fisheries Modernization Act), Republic Act No. 10601 (Agriculture and Fisheries Mechanization Law), Republic Act No. 11054 (Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao) and RA No. 7160 (Local Government Code of 1991).

**SECTION 3. General Objectives.** – This Act seeks to:

a. Establish in the Bangsamoro Government a fast, reliable, and economical Local Area Network (LAN) system for efficient and effective delivery of services to the inhabitants of the region;
b. Ensure the security of data of the Bangsamoro Government;

**ARTICLE II**
**Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering**

**SEC. 3. Creation of a Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering (BAFE) of BARMM.** – A Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BAFE-BARMM) is hereby created as a line bureau under the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and shall have the following powers, functions and responsibilities:

(a) fisheries mechanization, farm-to-market roads, agri-fisheries infrastructure, soil and water conservation and management, rural agro-industrialization, food logistics infrastructures and other agricultural and biosystems engineering projects;

(b) Prepare, evaluate, validate and recommend engineering plans, designs and technical specifications on agricultural, fisheries and biosystems mechanization, irrigation and infrastructure projects;

(c) Formulate regional plans and programs on agricultural, fisheries and biosystems infrastructures, irrigation, agricultural and fishery mechanization, post harvest facilities Coordinate, oversee and monitor the regional planning and implementation of agri-and agro-processing development in coordination with the Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering of the Department of Agriculture (BAFE-DA) and concerned BARMM agencies and LGUs;

(d) Oversee and provide technical assistance to the operations of the agricultural and biosystems engineering units/offices of the Local Government Units of BARMM;

(e) Coordinate and monitor the enforcement of the Philippine Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Standards and other regulatory policies on agricultural and biosystems engineering at the BARMM territorial areas;

(f) Assist the BAFE-DA in the accreditation and registration scheme for agriculture and fishery machinery, tools and equipment in coordination and collaboration with BAFE-DA;

(g) Assist BAFE-DA in the issuance of permits to operate to agriculture and fishery tools and equipment manufacturers, fabricators, assemblers and importers; and accreditation for agricultural and fishery testing centers; and

(h) Undertake the registration, accreditation and regulation of agricultural, fisheries and biosystems facilities, buildings and structures in coordination and collaboration with BAFE-DA.


**SEC. 4. Composition and Structure of BAFE-BARMM.** – The BAFE-BARMM shall be headed byaBureau ExecutiveDirectorand Assistant Bureau Executive Director with equivalent ranks pursuant to existing laws, to be appointed by the Chief Minister and who must be registered and licensed agricultural and biosystems engineers.
The BAFE-BARMM shall initially have the following minimum divisions:

(1) Engineering Plans, Designs and Specifications; (2) Programs and Projects Management; (3) Standards Regulation and Enforcement; and (4) Administration and Finance.

Likewise, the BAFE-BARMM shall establish is Provincial Operation and Coordination Office in the provinces of Maguindanao, Lanao del Sur, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi and Basilan as well as its City and Municipal Operation Units in all 4th Class to 6th Class LGUs.

The BAFE-BARMM shall likewise create one Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer plantilla position in each of the cities and municipality of all provinces of BARMM which shall be augmented by the Local Government Units.

**ARTICLE III**
**LGU Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Units**

**SEC. 5. Strengthening and/or Establishment of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Units of the Local Government Units.** – TheAgricultural and Biosystems Engineering Groups/Units of the agriculture offices of the 1st Class to 3rd ClassProvincial, City and Municipal Local Government Units in BARMM are hereby strengthened, institutionalized and transformed into Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering (ABE) Office to carry out the following powers, functions and responsibilities:

a) Provide agricultural and biosystems engineering services which include engineering survey, preparation and evaluation of plans, designs, technical specifications, feasibility studies and cost estimates/program of work of irrigation, soil and water conservation and management, spring water census and survey, small water impounding, small farm reservoir, farm machinery, slaughterhouses, auction markets, poultry dressing plants, post-harvest facilities, auction markets, farm-to-market roads and other agricultural, fisheries and biosystems infrastructure projects of the LGUs;

b) Administer, supervise, and coordinate the construction, operation, maintenance, improvement and management of irrigation, soil and water conservation and management, small water impounding, small farm reservoir, farm machinery, slaughterhouses, auction markets, poultry dressing plants, post-harvest facilities, auction markets, farm-to-market roads and other agricultural, fisheries and biosystems infrastructure projects of the LGUs;

c) Undertake the registration of agricultural and fishery machinery and facilities, enforcement of the (PAES/PABES) and other agricultural, fishery and biosystems engineering regulatory activities in coordination and collaboration with the BAFE-BARMM, concerned national government agencies and other offices of the LGUs. In line with this, the ABE unit shall:

c.1. Provide assistance to BAFE and BAFE-BARMM on the validation and surveillance of applications relative to the issuance of Permit to Operate small and medium sized agricultural and fishery machinery and equipment manufacturer, fabricators, assemblers and importers;

c.2. Monitor the compliance on the provision of after-sales service and warranty of concerned agricultural and fisheries machinery assemblers, manufacturers, importers, suppliers, distributors and dealers in their respective locality in collaboration with BAFE-BARMM and BAFE-DA;

c.3. Maintain and regularly update the data bank on irrigation, post-harvest, agricultural machinery and agricultural, fishery and biosystems infrastructure projects at all local levels;

c.4. Conduct inspection, performance testing and evaluation of agricultural, fisheries and biosystems machineries and equipment procured by the LGUs in coordination and collaboration with the BAFE, BAFE-BARMM and AMTEC; Agricultural machinery testing and evaluation center.

c.5. Provide assistance to the City and Municipal Building Officials in the evaluation and processing of application permits and certificate of occupancy of agricultural, fishery and biosystems buildings and structures to ensure the enforcement of PAES/PABES and RA No. 10915; and

c.6. Coordinate with BAFE-BARMM and BFAR-DA on the registration of fish hatcheries, fish breeding facilities, private fishponds and post harvest facilities for fishing communities pursuant to Section 28 and 33 of RA No. 8550¹.

d) Coordinate with BAFE-BARMM and concerned national government agencies with regard to the implementation of national government programs and projects on irrigation, farm mechanization, post-harvest facilities, farm-to-market roads and agricultural, fisheries and biosystems infrastructure;

e) Be in the frontline of the delivery of basic agricultural and biosystems engineering services;

f) Provide training and extension activities to farmers and fisherfolk particularly in the installation, operation and maintenance of their irrigation, postharvest facilities, agricultural, fishery and biosystems machinery projects in coordination with the agriculture and fisheries extension workers. In line with this, the ABE units shall coordinate and collaborate with State and Local Colleges and Universities, TESDA, and other concerned government agencies for the competency assessment and certification of agricultural and biosystems engineering technicians and operators including the establishment and operation of accredited Technical-Vocational Education Training (TVET) and assessment centers, learning sites/farm schools, farm tourism sites for agricultural and biosystems engineering at the provincial, city and municipal levels;

g) Undertake and/or coordinate the pilot testing and commercialization of matured agricultural, fisheries and biosystems engineering technologies;

h) Provide assistance in the organization and operation of the Agricultural and Fishery Mechanization Committee (AFMeC) of the Provincial, City and Municipal AFCs and serve as Vice Chairperson and Secretariat of said Committee, as well as in providing technical assistance and support to the Local Chapters of the PSABE and ABEs in the local implementation of agricultural and fisheries mechanization and infrastructure projects;

i) Responsible in the planning, design and management of communal irrigation system devolved or to be devolved to the LGUs pursuant to Section 33 of RA No. 8435 (AFMA);

j) Formulate local (Provincial, City and Municipal) Agricultural and Fisheries Mechanization Plans for approval by the respective LGU Sanggunian pursuant to Section 33 of RA No. 10601;

k) Formulate local (Provincial, City and Municipal) Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Master Plan/Program for approval by the Sanggunian pursuant to Joint DA-DILG MC No. 11, series of 2004 in relation to RA No. 10915. The master plan/program which supports and complements to the Comprehensive Land Use Plans, Local Development Plans, Local Food Security and Agri-Fisheries Modernization Plans shall have the following sub-components: (1) agricultural, fishery and biosystems mechanization; (2) soil and water conservation and irrigation; (3) farm-to-market road or farm access road development; (4) post-harvest, agro-processing and agro-industrial development; (5) agricultural and biological waste utilization and management; and (6) farm electrification and bio-energy development.

l) Exercise such other powers and perform such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.


This also includes functions and responsibilities on agricultural and biosystems engineering under RA No. 10915, such as, but not limited to animal/livestock production engineering, aquaculture engineering, agricultural and biological waste management, and farm electrification and bio-energy development as part of the agricultural and fisheries modernization, environmental protection and food security program of the provincial, city and municipal LGUs.

The LGU Agricultural EngineeringOffice shall be headed by a Provincial, City or Municipal Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer who must be registered and licensed Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer and shall be appointed by the Provincial Governor, City Mayor or Municipal Mayor respectively.

In the case of provincial, city and municipal LGUs belonging to the fourth up to the sixth income classes which are not capable of establishing their agricultural and biosystems engineering office due to financial constraints, the BAFE-BARMM shall perform such responsibilities and, as deemed necessary, establish its provincial, city and municipal operation units, and shall be allocated and provided with necessary funding and manpower requirements for their operation.

**SEC. 6. Functional Coordination and Collaboration.** – The BAFE-BARMM and the LGU Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Units/Office shall establish and institute functional coordination and collaboration in program planning and implementation at the provincial, city and municipal levels. Primarily, the BAFE-BARMM shall exercise technical supervision to all Provincial LGU Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Units/Offices and provide agricultural and biosystems engineering services in all 4th to 6th Class LGUs.

**ARTICLE IV**
**Other Miscellaneous Provisions**

**SEC. 7. Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer Plantilla Position.** – The required number of various level of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer Plantilla Positions in BAFE-BARMM and Agricultural and BiosystemsEngineering Offices of the LGUs shall be created and funded by BARMM. Appointment to this positions shall be in accordance with the qualification standards prescribed by the Civil Service Commission to these positions, other existing civil service rules and regulations, and Republic Act No. 10915 and other relevant guidelines.

**SEC. 8. Reallocation/Reclassification of Positions.** – The Agriculturist, Agricultural Technologist and Other Position of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineers in the BAFE-BARMM and LGU Agricultural Engineering Units/Offices shall be reallocated and/or reclassified into various level of Agricultural Engineer positions pursuant to DBM Budget Circular No. 03,series of 2003 and other related DBM policies and guidelines.

**SEC. 9. Human Resources Development.** – The BAFE-BARMM together with the BARMM Commission on Higher Education, TESDA, BARMM Civil Service CommissionP (CSC), and BARMM Department of Labor and Employment, other concerned BARMMministries/agenciesand in coordination with the Professional Regulatory Board of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering of the Professional Regulation Commission, BAFE-DA and the Philippine Society of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineers shall formulate and implement an Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering Human Resource Development (HRD) Master Plan for BARMM to ensure the supply of adequate number of competent agricultural and biosystems engineers and agri-fisheries engineering technicians and operators necessary for the implementation of this BARMM Act, the Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act and the Agricultural and Fisheries Mechanization Law.

The Human Resource Development Master Plan shall include but not limited to, the following:

a) Continuing Professional Development Program and other capacity building, skills trainings and institutional strengthening for agricultural and biosystems engineers and agri-fisheries engineering technicians and operators;

b) Scholarship program for the underprivileged but deserving college and post graduate students who are taking up courses in state and private universities and colleges which have programs in agricultural and biosystems engineering; and vocational courses and skills development for agri-fisheries engineering technicians and operators;

d) Implementation of competency standards and training regulations for technical vocational education and training foragri-fisheries engineering technicians and operators;

e) Upgrading of facilities, faculty development and strengthening of the on-the-job training program of higher education institutions that offers agricultural and biosystems engineering courses;

f) Offering of Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering degree program in concerned state and private universities in the provinces of Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, and Basilan Maguindanao, Lanao del Sur and other concerned provinces/cities of ARMM.

**Section 10. Appropriations.** – For the initial organization and operation of the Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering (BAFE) of BARMM, the amount of One Hundred Fifty Million (P150,000,000) is hereby appropriated for the initial year of implementation which shall be sourced from any available funds in the BARMM Treasury not otherwise appropriated. Thereafter, its annual budget shall be included in the annual appropriations of the BARMM.

**SEC. 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The MAFAR withinsix (6) months upon the enactment of this Act and in coordination with BAFE-DA and consultation with thestakeholders shall promulgate the necessaryrules and regulations to implement this Act.
