**AN ACT**
**DECLARING BALOBOK CAVE IN BONGAO, TAWI-TAWI AS A REGIONAL HERITAGE ZONE, PROVIDING FOR ITS PROTECTION, PRESERVATION, AND PROMOTION, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “*Balobok Cave Preservation Act of 2022*”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy and Principles.** – It shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to recognize as an integral component of regional development the transcendental importance of collecting, preserving, reviving, protecting, and harnessing the cultural heritage of the people in the Autonomous Region.

Section 2, Article V of the *Bangsamoro Organic Law* states that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its authority on matters pertaining to libraries and museums, historical, cultural and archeological sites. Towards this end, it shall study and put forward the history, and promote the preservation of sites of cultural and archeological significance found within the region.

**Section 3. Balobok Cave.** – The Balobok Cave, located in Barangay Lakit-Lakit, Bongao, Tawi-Tawi is hereby declared as a regional heritage zone. A regional heritage zone shall refer to historical, anthropological, archeological, and artistic geographic areas that are culturally significant to the Bangsamoro region.

The Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) and the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) shall primarily be in charge of the actions necessary for the implementation of this Act, the site preservation, protection, development, and promotion.

**Section 4. Profile and natural boundary.** – Within six (6) months after the approval of this Act, the BCPCH, MENRE, in partnership with the Tawi-Tawi Provincial Government shall initiate the study of the site, not limited to physical inspection, research, measurements, coordinates, and all other interventions necessary to establish a verified account, precise profile and jurisdiction of the heritage zone.

**Section 5. Protection and management plan.** – The BCPCH, MENRE, Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT), and the Tawi-Tawi Provincial Government shall jointly formulate a comprehensive plan for the heritage zone’s preservation, protection, rehabilitation, infrastructure installation, development into ecotourism, management and maintenance.

**Section 6. Ancestral lands and rights.** – Ancestral lands and customary rights and interests arising from the declared jurisdiction of the heritage zone shall be accorded due recognition. The BCPCH and MENRE shall jointly prescribe rules and regulations to govern the ancestral lands within the heritage zone.

**Section 7. Coordination.** – The BCPCH shall coordinate with national agencies for the recognition of the Balobok Rock Shelter as a heritage zone. The BCPCH shall also facilitate the implementation of national programs supplemental to the development plans of the heritage zone by the region.

**Section 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – Within ninety (90) days from the passage of this Act, the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage, in close coordination with the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.

**Section 9. Appropriations.** – Such sums as may be necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be taken from the current appropriations of the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage. Thereafter, the fund necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

**Section 10. Separability Clause.** – If any provision of this Act shall be held unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 11. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, executive orders or issuances, or any parts thereof which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 12. Effectivity Clause.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
