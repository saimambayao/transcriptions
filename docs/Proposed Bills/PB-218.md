**AN ACT**
**DECLARING THE BUD TUKAY PEAK LOCATED IN BARANGAY PARANG, MUNICIPALITY OF PARANG, PROVINCE OF SULU AS A COMMUNITY-BASED ECOTOURISM ZONE AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

**BE IT ENACTED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **"Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Act."**

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – Pursuant to Section 35, Article XIII of the Bangsamoro Organic law, which provides, among others, that "The Bangsamoro Government shall promote tourism within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region," it is the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region of Muslim Mindanao to promote its tourism industry that is ecologically sustainable, responsible, participative, culturally sensitive, economically viable, and equitable for the local community. Towards this end, Bud Tukay has hereby declared an ecotourism zone. It shall be accorded priority development by the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT), the Ministry of Environmental, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), and the Ministry of Public Works (MPW).

**SECTION 3. Objectives.** – Pursuant to the above declaration of policy, the Bangsamoro Government shall endeavor to accomplish the following:

(a) Develop and promote community-based ecological tourism in the development of Bud Tukay Peak and its surrounding areas;

(b) Ensure the preservation of the environment and conservation of biodiversity within the Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Park;

(c) Develop ecotourism products and programs that will generate employment and livelihood for the local residents concerned, consistent with the preservation and maintenance of natural resources; and

(d) Guarantee that the financial and economic benefits of the tourism industry are equitably shared by and distributed among the local residents concerned by giving preference and support to community and home-based tourism enterprises, small businesses, and other similar tourism-oriented industries and businesses;

**SECTION 4. Definition of Terms.** – As used in this Act:

(a) Community-based ecotourism refers to a form of tourism where the local community and its residents have substantial control and involvement in the development and management of the local tourism industry, and a greater portion of the economic gains remain within the community;

(b) Ecotourism refers to a form of sustainable tourism within a natural area where community participation, protection and management of natural resources, cultural and indigenous knowledge and practices, environmental education, and ethics, as well as economic benefits, are fostered and pursued the enrichment of host communities and the satisfaction of visitors;

**SECTION 5. Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Zone.** – The Bud Tukay Peak, located in Barangay Parang, Municipality of Parang, Province of Sulu, is hereby declared as a community-based ecotourism zone. It shall be accorded priority development by the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT), the Ministry of Environmental, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), and the Ministry of Public Works (MPW).

**SECTION 6. Scope and Coverage.** – The Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Zone shall cover the entire area of Bud Tukay, which has an elevation of 1,998 feet, the actual area and coverage of which shall be determined by the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) in coordination with the local government unit concerned.

**SECTION 7. Establishment of a Buffer Zone.** – There shall also be established buffer zones surrounding the BTP for the purpose of providing extra protection around the area against and for the tourists or visitors as well as local people, where restrictions shall apply. Such buffer zones shall be managed according to the ecotourism development and management plan formulated pursuant to this Act. The Council shall exercise its authority over the designated buffer zone.


**SECTION 8. Formation of the Bud Tukay Peak Development Council and its composition.** – A multi-sectoral council to be known as the "Bud Tukay Peak Development Council," hereinafter referred to as the Council, is hereby created. It shall be composed of the following members:

a) The Mayor of the Municipality of Parang as Chairperson;

b) The Provincial Director of the MTIT or its representative as Vice-Chairperson;

c) The Provincial Director of the MENRE or its representative;

d) The Barangay Chairman of Barangay Tukay Parang;

e) One (1) representative from an academic institution; and

f) One (1) representative from among the residents of Bud Tukay Peak as a private sector representative; and

g) One (1) representative from the Women Sector of Brgy. Tukay, Parang, Sulu

All appointed and elected members of the Council shall serve only for the duration of their respective terms of office or appointment.

The members of the Council shall serve without compensation, except for the actual and necessary traveling and subsistence expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, either in their attendance at meetings of the Council or in connection with other official business authorized through a resolution of the Council, subject to existing rules and regulations. Each member shall have the full capacity and accountability for decisions binding to the member's sector.

The Secretary of the Sangguniang Bayan of the Municipality of Parang shall act as Secretariat for the Council during its meetings.

**SECTION 9. Functions and Responsibilities of the Council.** – The Council shall be responsible for the following:

(a) Formulation and implementation of the ecotourism development and management plan in consultation with the local residents, businesses, and other stakeholders;

(b) Formulation of the rules and regulations in relation to the maintenance and security of the ecotourism site;

(c) Implementation of a monitoring plan for the Forest area and to ensure the people's well-being in the BTP;

(d) Assessment and evaluation of the potential impact of the development on the environmental safety and well-being of the site, communities, and enterprises;

(e) Provision of technical and financial assistance to the communities within the site;

**SECTION 10. Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Development and Management Plan.** – The MENRE and the MTIT, in coordination with the MPW, shall jointly assist the Bud Tukay Peak Development Council in the formulation of the ecotourism development and management plan for the BTP. The ecotourism development and management plan shall ensure the preservation of the eco-park and shall include the following components:

a) Carrying capacity of the eco-park;

b) Management and maintenance of the Forest areas;

c) Product development for local income generation;

d) Capacity-building for the local community to allow residents therein to ably manage economically viable and responsible, community-based ecotourism business enterprises; and

e) Solid and liquid wastes management;

**SECTION 11. Coordination with other Ministries of the BARMM and other National Agencies.** – The MENRE and the MTIT shall closely coordinate with the Council, as well as the relevant regional offices of the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MLE), Ministry of Public Works (MPW), Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG), Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE), Philippine National Police (PNP) and other line agencies of the government in the implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – Within thirty (30) days from the effectivity of this Act, the MENRE, the MTIT, and the Council, in consultation with the Provincial Tourism Office of the Province of Sulu, other concerned government agencies and stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the proper and effective implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 13. Appropriations.** – An initial amount of Five Million Pesos (Php 5,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated and shall be made available to the Ministers of the MENRE, MTIT, MAFAR, and MPW, who shall include in their subsidy programs support for the capital outlay requirements of the BTP for the preliminary implementation of this Act and enhancement of the facilities of the ecotourism zone which shall be taken from the budget of their respective agencies for the current fiscal year. They shall also carry in their respective budget for the succeeding years an allocation for this program.

**SECTION 14. Separability Clause.** – If, for any reason, any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, other provisions hereof which are not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**SECTION 15. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, decrees, executive orders, proclamations, and administrative regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 16. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the *Official Gazette* or in at least two (2) newspapers of regional general circulation.
