**AN ACT**
**STRENGTHENING THE BANGSAMORO AGRICULTURAL SUPPORT SYSTEM, THEREBY CREATING THE BANGSAMORO PLANT AND ANIMAL INDUSTRIES OFFICE (BPAIO), DEFINING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:**

**CHAPTER I**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**Article I**
**Declaration of Principles and Policies**

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro Plant and Animal Industries Act of 2023”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It shall be the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature. 

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes its duty and obligation to protect and promote the well-being of the people, plant and animal population, environment and its respective ecosystems, in order to meet the requirement of the growing populace and guarantee the nation’s survival.

**Article II**
**Creation of Bureau of Plant and Animal Industries**

**Section 3. Creation.** – There is hereby created the Bangsamoro Plant and Animal Industries Office, herein referred to as **“the Office”**. The Office shall act as the primary Bangsamoro Government entity responsible for the comprehensive and integrated development of the plant and animal industries in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

For purposes of coordinating plans and policies, the Bangsamoro Office on Plant and Animal Industries shall be an attached agency of the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) – BARMM.

**CHAPTER 2**
**OBJECTIVES AND FUNCTIONS**

**Section 4. Purposes and Objectives.** – This Act generally aims to establish a Bangsamoro Plant and Animal Industries Office in the Bangsamoro Region. Specifically, it has the following purposes and objectives:

a) To promote the accelerated development of the plant and animal industries in all of their aspects;

b) To provide general directions for the continuing development of the industries;

c) To make the industries productive and profitable under sustainable environment through sound policies, programs, research, and services on production; and

d) To control environmental pollution by minimizing the use of pesticides, thereby promoting a healthy environment.

**Section 5. Powers and Functions.** – To carry out the purposes and objectives mentioned in the preceding section, the Office, is hereby vested with the following powers and functions:

a) Formulate and adopt general development plans and programs for plant and animal industries in the region;

b) Evaluate the existing policies, programs, and projects of all agencies and instrumentalities having to do with both plant and animal industries development and to integrate and coordinate the various facets of such activities into a general development program;

c) Coordinate, monitor and implement such policies, plans, and programs for the promotion of plant and animal industries;

d) Recommend specific policies and procedures that will promote the development of the plant and animal industries;

e) Exercise all the powers necessary to achieve the purposes and objectives for which it is organized.

**CHAPTER III**
**IMPLEMENTING STRUCTURE AND MECHANISM**

**Section 6. Chief Officials.** – The Office shall be headed by an Executive Director to be assisted by a Deputy Executive Director. The Office shall be composed of the following divisions:

a) Research, Planning, and Development Division;
b) Operations’ Division;
c) Monitoring and Evaluation Division; and
d) Finance and Administrative Support Division.

**Section 7. Office of the Executive Director.** – The Executive Director shall have the following duties and responsibilities:

a) Lead the planning and formulation of policy measures, as well as the benefit, protection, and welfare of the plant and animal industries in the region;

b) Provide direction and supervision over the full operation of the Office;

c) Provide policy guidelines and standards for the effective and efficient operation and function of the Office in accordance with its mandate;

d) Ensure that the mandate and functions of the Office and the provisions of this Act are faithfully executed and performed;

e) Represent the Office in all dealings with other offices, agencies, and instrumentalities of the Government and with all persons and entities, public or private, domestic, or foreign; and

f) Establish a recruitment system guided by civil service rules and regulations in the selection of its prospective staff and employees in the career service.

**Section 8. Functions and Responsibilities of the Divisions.** – The technical and administrative divisions shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

**1. Research, Planning, and Development Division.** – The Research, Planning, and Development Division shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

a) Conduct responsive studies for the development of the plant and animal industries in order to provide research outputs that will address the challenges facing the industries;

b) Develop and maintain a data and information system that will serve as a repository of processed data and statistical information related to plant and animal industries in the region;

c) Coordinate with other local researchers and other concerned offices for planning, policy formulation, and project development related to the industries;

d) Prepare the comprehensive plan of the plant and animal industry in the region;

e) Develop possible measures in support of the programs of the Office and to continuously contribute to the desired state of a well-managed Office responsive to the demands of the industries; and

f) Perform such other functions as may be directed by the Administrator and other competent authorities.

**2. Operations’ Division.** – The Operations’ Division shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

a) Coordinate the implementation of all programs of the Office in the Bangsamoro region in cooperation with concerned agencies at the national and regional levels;

b) Implement the programs and projects of the Office in the Bangsamoro region that are funded by the Bangsamoro Government;

c) Review and evaluate project proposals and make appropriate actions and recommendations for consideration of the Office;

d) Tap other appropriate Bangsamoro Government agencies to synchronize and coordinate the implementation of programs; and

e) Perform such other functions as may be directed by the Administrator and other competent authorities.

**3. Monitoring and Evaluation Division.** – The Monitoring and Evaluation Division shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

a) Conduct monitoring and evaluation activities relative to the implementation of projects in cooperation with the appropriate agency of the National and Bangsamoro Governments;

b) Identify and analyze policy issues and direct research studies to formulate appropriate policy alternatives and recommendations;

c) Submit regular monitoring and evaluation reports to higher authorities pertaining to the actual progress of programs and project implementation; and

d) Perform other functions as may be directed by the Administrator and other competent authorities.

**4. Finance and Administrative Support Division.** – The Finance and Administrative Support Division shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

a) Provide support services relating to personnel, staff development, records management, supplies and equipment, procurement, budgeting and accounting, cashiering, and general clerical and utility services; and

b) Perform other related functions as may be assigned by the Administrator and other competent authorities.

**Section 9. Staffing.** – The Office shall determine its staffing pattern in accordance with the Government Revised Compensation and Position Classification System.

**CHAPTER IV**
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 10. Rules and Regulations.** – The Office shall promulgate the required rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act and revise or amend the same, as may be necessary.

**Section 11. Periodic Reports.** – The Office shall submit an annual report to the Chief Minister indicating, among others, the list of programs being implemented, the stage of implementation, and the financial position of the Office. It shall likewise submit such periodic or other reports as may be required from time to time.

**Section 12. Mandatory Review on the Implementation of this Act.** – Every ten (10) years after the effectivity of this Act, an independent review panel composed of experts, to be appointed by the Chief Minister, shall review the policies and projects of the Office and shall make recommendations, based on its findings, to the Chief Minister and to the Parliament.

**Section 13. Implementation.** – Within sixty (60) days from the promulgation of this Act, the necessary rules and regulations for the proper implementation of its provision shall be formulated by the Ministry of Fisheries, Agrarian Reform (MAFAR).

**Section 14. Appropriations.** – The funds necessary for the establishment of the Bangsamoro Office on Plant and Animal Industries shall be included in the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and submitted to the Parliament for its approval.

**Section 15. Separability Clause.** – If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 16. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 17. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
