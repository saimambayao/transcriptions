**"AN ACT RENAMING THE MUNICIPALITY OF SOUTH UPI IN THE PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO DEL SUR TO 'TEDURAY MUNICIPALITY'"**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

**PREAMBLE**

Whereas, the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) and the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP) affirm the rights of Indigenous Peoples to preserve, promote, and develop their cultural identity, traditions, and self-governance;

Whereas, the Municipality of South Upi, created under Presidential Decree No. 1011 dated September 22, 1976, is predominantly inhabited by the Teduray Indigenous People, whose community identity and history are deeply rooted in the locality;

Whereas, the renaming of South Upi to **Teduray Municipality** reflects the aspirations of the Teduray people for cultural recognition, historical justice, and self-determination within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

Now, therefore, in pursuit of cultural preservation, social justice, and indigenous empowerment, this Act is hereby enacted.

**BOOK I**

**BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO**

**CHAPTER I**

**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**ARTICLE I**

**NAME AND PURPOSE**

**SECTION 1. Title.** This Act shall be known as the **"Teduray Municipality Renaming Act of 2025."**

**SECTION 2. Basis.** This Act is enacted pursuant to the Bangsamoro Organic Law, Republic Act No. 11054, and the applicable provisions of the Local Government Code, in recognition of the rights of Indigenous Peoples.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.** As used in this Act, the following terms shall mean:

	a. **Teduray** – Refers to the Indigenous Cultural Community predominantly residing in the Municipality of South Upi, recognized under Philippine law and international instruments.

	b. **Plebiscite** – Refers to the electoral process conducted to ratify the renaming of the municipality, to be administered by the Bangsamoro Electoral Office in coordination with the Commission on Elections (COMELEC).

	c. **Bangsamoro Government** – Refers to the regional government established under the Bangsamoro Organic Law.

**ARTICLE II**

**SCOPE**

**SECTION 1. Coverage.** This Act shall apply to the Municipality of South Upi in the Province of Maguindanao del Sur, Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**SECTION 2. Subject of Renaming.** All references to the Municipality of South Upi in official documents, maps, laws, and regional issuances shall be changed to reflect the new name "Teduray Municipality" upon the ratification of this Act.

**CHAPTER II**

**RENAMING AND IMPLEMENTATION**

**ARTICLE I**

**RENAMING AND PROCEDURES**

**SECTION 1. Renaming of the Municipality.** The Municipality of South Upi in the Province of Maguindanao del Sur is hereby officially renamed as **"Teduray Municipality."**

**SECTION 2. Conduct of Plebiscite.** The renaming shall be subject to ratification by a majority of the votes cast in a plebiscite to be conducted within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act by the Bangsamoro Electoral Office (BEO), in coordination with the Commission on Elections (COMELEC).

**SECTION 3. Funding Requirements.** The amount necessary to conduct the plebiscite shall be sourced from the Bangsamoro Government's available funds. The concerned local government unit may provide augmentation from its own resources, if necessary.

**SECTION 4. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within thirty (30) days from the effectivity of this Act, the Ministry of Local Government, in coordination with the concerned LGU and other stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations to implement this Act.

**ARTICLE II**

**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**SECTION 1. Effect of Renaming.** Upon ratification, all regional laws, executive issuances, rules, regulations, and documents referring to the Municipality of South Upi shall be deemed to refer to "Teduray Municipality."

**SECTION 2. Public Dissemination.** The Ministry of Interior and Local Government shall ensure the proper dissemination of this change to all relevant government agencies, civil society organizations, and the general public.

**SECTION 3. Separability Clause.** If any part or provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 4. Repealing Clause.** All laws, orders, rules, and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SECTION 5. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of general circulation within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region or in the Bangsamoro Gazette.
