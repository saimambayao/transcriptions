**AN ACT**
**ESTABLISHING A PERMANENT BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT CENTER OF BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM) PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Interim Parliament.

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT CENTER 2024”.**

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure efficient delivery of government services through continuous development of its infrastructure and innovative improvements in the delivery of its services. Thus, it is imperative for the government to make necessary measures to provide and ensure smooth operation of government processes and to ease congestions at Governor Gutierrez Avenue. Towards this goal, the Bangsamoro Government shall enact a measure for the establishment of a permanent Bangsamoro Government Center of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in some other place within Cotabato City. This shall also spur development in the other portion of the city and deliver more efficient and effective government services.

**SECTION 3. Objective.** – The objective of this Act is to establish a permanent Bangsamoro Government Center of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to other part within Cotabato City and separate it from the commercial, financial center and schools within the area of Governor Gutierrez Avenue to create opportunities for progress and spur development in the other portion of the city, while reducing traffic congestion within its current location.

**SECTION 4. Establishment of Bangsamoro Government Center.** – The Bangsamoro Government Center shall be fully established by 2024 in any place within Cotabato City. The Bangsamoro Government Center shall be established for the following offices:

a) Bangsamoro Parliament Session Hall;
b) BARMM Legislative Office of the eighty (80) Members of the Parliament;

xiii. Records Division;

xv. Accounting Division;

xvii. Cash Division;

d) BARMM Ministries;

These respective offices shall be prioritized in the transfer upon the completion of the establishment of the Bangsamoro Government Center by 2024.

**SECTION 5. Preparations Prior to Transfer.** – The Bangsamoro government through a Transitory Commission, shall immediately take action towards the development and implementation of masterplans, strategies and timetables that will provide the framework towards the establishment of Bangsamoro Government Center. It shall be developed as a green smart city equipped with the highest standard of modern technology and environment-friendly using energy-efficient and green infrastructure resolutions, fortified with digital information and communication technology infrastructure, and designed with effective and efficient transportation systems.

**SECTION 6. Other establishments and facilities.** – For a more convenient workplace and an opportunity for economic growth of the area, a commercial complex, business center, food hubs, fast food, convenience store, and other recreational activities and amenities are anticipated to be embraced therein. The facilities include the basic necessities such as, but not limited to, stable power and water supply, generator sets, telephone and internet connectivity, and drainage canal system.

**SECTION 7. Creation of a Transitory Commission.** – A Transitory Commission shall be responsible to provide necessary information and data such as but not limited to the identification of the location, cost of land and over all construction, and other pertinent statistics in the furtherance of the study and for the implementation of this Act. A Transitory Commission, chaired by the Chief Minister and composed of six (6) members representing the

1. Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management,
2. Ministry on Environment, Natural Resources and Energy
3. Ministry of Public Works,
4. Ministry of Transportation and Communication,
5. Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, and
6. Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office.

The Transitory Commission shall have the following powers and functions:

a) Lead and organize the preparation, development and implementation of masterplans, strategies, processes and timetable that will provide the framework towards the development of the Bangsamoro Government Center;
b) Serve as oversight committee for transferring and transitioning;
c) Determine capabilities of Offices to transfer to Bangsamoro Government Center and prescribe measures to continue or dismiss such transfer;
d) Develop and approve necessary transfer and transition requirements and conditions of Offices, including possible modes of transfer, safety measures and labor standards;
e) Determine the amount necessary to implement the transfer to Bangsamoro Government Center and recommend the same to the Chief Minister for incorporation to the proposed budget for the proper government agencies;
f) Oversee the immediate implementation of the provisions of this Act;
g) Perform functions mentioned under the other provisions of this Act;
h) Perform such other functions as may be necessary or implied from the provisions of this Act.

The Transitory Commission shall immediately convene upon approval of this Act and shall cease to exist upon completion and full implementation of the objectives of this Act.

**SECTION 8. Appropriations.** – The amount of Fifty Million Pesos (Php50,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated for the acquisition of land and initial appropriation for the use of the Transitory Commission to implement the provisions of this Act. For the succeeding fiscal years until its cessation, the Transitory Commission shall include the amounts necessary for its operations and the implementation of this Act in the annual General Appropriations Act Bangsamoro (GAAB).

**SECTION 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – Within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act, the Transitory Commission, in consultation with appropriate government agencies and other stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations and other issuances as may be necessary to ensure the effective implementation of this Act.

**SECTION 10. Separability Clause.** – If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain valid.

**SECTION 11. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, and regulations under the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) government including those passed under the defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) government regarding the construction and establishment of Bangsamoro Government Center not consistent with this Act are hereby repealed.

**SECTION 12. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the newspaper of general circulation.
