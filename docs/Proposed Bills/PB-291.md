**AN ACT INCORPORATING BASIC AGRI-FISHERY KNOWLEDGE IN THE ELEMENTARY AND SECONDARY SCHOOLS CURRICULUM IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES⁵**

*Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:*

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “***Basic Agri-Fishery Knowledge Act of 2024***”.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the vital role of the children and youth in nation-building and to encourage their involvement in public and civic affairs.

Towards this end, the children and youth must be included in advancing agri-fishery as key development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region by bringing awareness regarding basic farming and fishing in order to advance their knowledge on agri-fishery. This can be achieved through inclusion of a subject in the school curriculum in the region.

**Section 3. Objectives.** – This Act generally aims to create a mandatory subject that teaches all the basics of farming and fishing to pupils and students in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Specifically, it has the following objectives:

a) Impart knowledge to the children and youth on the importance of agri-fishery in the Bangsamoro region;
b) To make the children and youth responsible stewards of the Bangsamoro land and waters;
c) To prepare the children and youth and provide opportunities for them to explore variety of careers within the field of agri-fishery;
d) To develop an interest and to develop basic competencies to pupils and students to prepare them for their possible careers in the agri-fishery sector, and help make them valuable assets of the community and in the Bangsamoro region in the future; and
e) To dwell more in the agri-fishery sector and motivate research that will help advance agri-fishery in the Bangsamoro region.

**Section 4. Inclusion in the School Curriculum.** – It shall be mandatorily included in the curriculum of the elementary and secondary schools in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, a subject to be called Basic Agri-fishery Knowledge, which shall impart knowledge on the pupils and students about agri-fishery in general. It shall be an area of study that discusses the basic information or knowledge on the act or process of cultivating lands, planting seeds, and growing crops, and fishing activities in the region. This shall also include raising of animals and stock.

The Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) shall ensure the integration of this subject in the existing curriculum of elementary and secondary schools in the region.

**Section 5. Faculty.** – The faculty to be assigned in teaching the subject shall be an individual who has knowledge and expertise in agriculture and fishery. A continuing capacity-building program shall be afforded to the assigned teachers.

The MBHTE shall work closely with the Ministry of Agriculture, Fishery and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) in the implementation of this Act.

**Section 6. Continuing Research and Review of Agriculture and Fishery Curriculum in the Bangsamoro Region.** – There shall be a unit within the MBHTE which shall engage in a continuing research and review of the contents of the subject in agriculture and fishery’s curriculum of elementary and secondary schools in the Bangsamoro region.

**Section 7. Coverage.** – This Act shall cover all public and private elementary and secondary schools operating within the Bangsamoro Region.

**Section 8. Annual Report to the Parliament.** – The MBHTE shall submit a report to the Parliament within one year from the implementation of this Act, and every year thereafter, on the progress of the implementation of this Act.

**Section 9. Implementation.** – Within sixty (60) days from the promulgation of this Act, the necessary rules and regulations for the proper implementation of its provision shall be formulated by the MBHTE.

**Section 10. Mandatory Review on the Implementation of this Act.** – Every ten (10) years after the effectivity of this Act, an independent review panel composed of experts, to be appointed by the Chief Minister, shall review the implementation of this Act and shall make recommendations, based on its findings, to the Chief Minister and to the Parliament.

**Section 11. Appropriations.** – The funds necessary for the establishment of playgrounds shall be included in the General Appropriation Act of the Bangsamoro.

**Section 12. Separability Clause.** – If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 13. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 14. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
