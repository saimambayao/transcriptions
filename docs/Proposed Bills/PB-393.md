**AN ACT CONVERTING THE MATANOG WORLD ASSEMBLY OF MUSLIM YOUTH (WAMY) CLINIC IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF MATANOG, PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO DEL NORTE, INTO A LEVEL I HOSPITAL WITH TEN (10)-BED CAPACITY, TO BE KNOWN AS THE MATANOG DISTRICT HOSPITAL, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES.**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Interim Parliament, Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

**Section 1. Short Title.** This Act shall be known as the “Matanog District Hospital Act of 2025.”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government under Section 22, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” to provide for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents and establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellence and affordable medical services. In pursuit of this, the government shall prioritize the upgrading of existing health facilities to provide life-saving and basic medical care.

**Section 3. Conversion.** The existing Matanog World Assembly of Muslim Youth (WAMY) Clinic located in the Municipality of Matanog, Province of Maguindanao del Norte, is hereby converted into a Level I Hospital, to be known as the Matanog District Hospital.

**Section 4. Services and Bed Capacity.** The Matanog District Hospital shall operate as a Level I hospital with a ten (10)-bed capacity, and shall provide, at a minimum, the following services:

a) General medical and minor surgical services
b) Emergency and outpatient care
c) Maternal and child health services
d) Isolation services
e) Basic clinical laboratory and diagnostic services
f) Pharmacy services

**Section 5. Manpower Requirement.** The Ministry of Health (MOH) shall assign qualified medical and allied health personnel to the hospital. The minimum manpower shall include a medical officer, nurses, midwives, a Pharmacist, a medical and Radiologic technologist, and administrative support personnel, subject to existing civil service rules and MOH manpower standards.

**Section 6. Appropriations.** The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro of the year following its enactment into law and thereafter.

**Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** The Ministry of Health shall be the lead implementing agency. It shall promulgate the necessary guidelines and administrative orders for the full and effective implementation of this Act within sixty (60) days upon its effectivity.

**Section 8. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall not be affected and shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**Section 9. Repealing Clause.** All laws, orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 10. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation.
