**THE GOVERNMENT OF THE DAY**

**AN ACT ESTABLISHING A BANGSAMORO MEMORIAL MARKER AND ECO-PARK IN CAMP ABUBAKAR, MUNICIPALITY OF BARIRA, MAGUINDANAO TO HONOR THE SHUHADA/MARTYRS OF THE BANGSAMORO STRUGGLE WHO GAVE AND SACRIFICED THEIR LIVES FOR THE RIGHT TO SELF-DETERMINATION AND FREEDOM OF THE BANGSAMORO, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**SECTION 1. The Short Title.** This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bangsamoro Memorial Marker Act”.

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro:

(a) To promote unity, peace, justice, and goodwill among all people;

(b) To ensure every Filipino in its territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life. Social Justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

(c) To ensure that indigenous peoples shall have the freedom to retain their distinct indigenous and ethnic identity in addition to their Bangsamoro political identity; and

(d) To ensure and support the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology.

**SECTION 3. Bangsamoro Memorial Marker.** The Bangsamoro Memorial Marker shall include a list of names of the shuhada who were martyred as a result of their service in the protracted war for the right to self-determination and freedom of the Bangsamoro people.

**SECTION 4. Bangsamoro Eco-park.** The park shall be located in Camp Abubakar, Municipality of Barira. It shall be constructed, properly landscaped and designed with structures and ornaments that are reflective of Bangsamoro culture and art. It shall also be provided with complete facilities such as, but not limited to, multi-purpose hall, parking area, comfort rooms and wash areas, and access roads. The memorial marker shall be built within the said eco-park.

**SECTION 5. Maintenance of the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker and Eco-park.** The Bangsamoro Memorial Marker and Eco-park shall be maintained by the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH).

**SECTION 6. Bangsamoro Memorial Council (BMC).** It shall be created and composed of a Chairperson and five (5) members. It shall be chaired by the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) Chairperson.

**SECTION 7. Members of the Bangsamoro Memorial Council (BMC).** A council shall be composed of five (5) Provincial Focal Person representing the provinces of: (1) Maguindanao including Cotabato City and the 63 barangays; (2) Lanao del Sur; (3) Basilan including Lamitan City; (4) Sulu; and (5) Tawi-tawi, who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister under the recommendation of the Bangsamoro Memorial Council (BMC) Chair.

**SECTION 8. Functions and Responsibilities of the BMC.** — The BMC shall perform the following:

(a) Establish eligibility criteria for the inclusion of the names in the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker;

(b) Submit to the Office of the Chief Minister a final list of names for inclusion on the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker that meets the criteria in the preceding paragraph;

(c) Monitor the construction of the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker and Eco-Park and ensure that its structure conforms to the standards and designs set by them;

(d) Formulate plans, programs, policies, rules and regulations necessary for the discharge of its powers and functions as well as its internal organization and operations; and

(e) Such other functions as may be necessary or incidental to the proper implementation of the provisions of this Act.

**SECTION 9. Dissolution of the Bangsamoro Memorial Council (BMC).** The BMC shall dissolve automatically upon the completion and turn-over of the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker and Eco-park to the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH). The members thereof shall be released and discharged from the functions and responsibilities in the preceding section.

**SECTION 10. Appropriations.** — The funds necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act shall be charged against the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government.

Subsequent funding requirements necessary to carry out other provisions of this Act, which includes but not limited to the maintenance of the Bangsamoro Memorial Marker and Eco-park upon turn-over shall be included in the annual budget of the BCPCH.

**SECTION 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** — Within Ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act, the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) shall promulgate the Rules and Regulations necessary for the effective implementation of this Act notwithstanding the functions and responsibilities of the Bangsamoro Memorial Council (BMC) set forth in this Act.

**SECTION 12. Repealing Clause.** – Any law or issuance, executive order, or letter of instruction, administrative order, rules or regulation contrary to, or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 13. Separability Clause.** – Should any provisions of this Act or any part thereof be declared invalid, other provisions, so far as they are separable from the invalid ones, shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 14. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect immediately.
