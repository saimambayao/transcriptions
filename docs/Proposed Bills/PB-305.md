**AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE WASTE WORKERS’ HEALTH AND WELFARE PROGRAM FOR BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREOF**

**Section 1. Short Title.** This Act shall be known as the “*Bangsamoro Waste Workers’ Health and Welfare Act of 2024.*”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote the health, safety, and welfare of waste workers in the region through the establishment of a comprehensive program that provides benefits, education, and training, and ensures access to proper equipment and facilities, in recognition of the critical role they play in maintaining the country's cleanliness and sustainability.

**Section 3. Definition of “Waste Worker”.** For the purpose of this Act, "Waste worker" refers to any person engaged in the collection, transportation, processing, and disposal of solid waste, and includes, but is not limited to, garbage collectors, waste pickers, recyclers, and landfill and dumpsite workers.

**Section 4. Waste Workers’ Health and Welfare Program.** The Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE) and the Civil Service Commission (CSC) shall establish and administer the Waste Workers' Health and Welfare Program, designed to safeguard and promote the rights and welfare of waste workers in the Region, which shall include the following components:

a. Registration and Monitoring System – A system for the registration of waste workers, necessary for them to be covered under this Program.

b. Health Coverage and Benefits – A package of healthcare benefits, including free annual check-ups, vaccinations, and hospitalization assistance, in partnership with the Ministry of Health (MOH).

c. Occupational Safety Training – The provision of regular training programs on methods, techniques, and the proper use of personal protective equipment to minimize the risks associated with waste handling.

d. Personal Protective Equipment – The mandatory provision to waste workers of suitable and adequate personal protective equipment free of charge by their employers or agencies.

e. Educational Assistance – The facilitation of access to educational assistance programs and scholarship grants for waste workers and their dependents.

f. Financial Assistance – The institution of a loan program under the auspices of the MOLE, in collaboration with qualified financial institutions, to provide waste workers with low-interest loans for housing, emergency situations, or the establishment of sustainable livelihood businesses.

**Section 5. Funding.** The necessary funding for the implementation of the Waste Workers' Health and Welfare Program shall be allocated from the annual General Appropriations Act (GAA). The MOLE and CSC may also accept and utilize grants, donations, contributions, or other forms of assistance, subject to existing laws, rules, and regulations.

**Section 6. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** The MOLE and CSC, in consultation with MOH, relevant stakeholders and other government agencies, shall promulgate and publish the necessary implementing rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this law.

**Section 7. Separability Clause.** In the event that any provision of this Act is declared to be unconstitutional or invalid, other provisions thereof that are not affected thereby shall continue to be in force and effect.

**Section 8. Repealing Clause.** All other orders and related issuances inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed.

**Section 9. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
