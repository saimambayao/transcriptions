**AN ACT**
**STRENGTHENING VOLUNTEERISM IN THE STRENGTHENING BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM), PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:*

**Section 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the **“Bangsamoro Volunteerism Act of 2022”**.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to provide, maintain, and ensure delivery of basic and responsive services to the Bangsamoro people and other inhabitant of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. In pursuing this mandate, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure participation of all sectors, government and non-government, individual or groups, in order to ensure that no one is left behind.

**Section 3. Objectives.** – This Act generally aims to provide framework on volunteerism in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and to recognize and promote volunteerism as one of the tools for development of vulnerable and disadvantaged communities in the region.

**Section 4. Definition of Terms.** – The terms used in this Act shall mean as follows:

a. **Volunteerism** – refers to an act involving a wide range of activities, including traditional forms of mutual aid and development interventions that provides an enabling and empowering environment both on the part of the beneficiary receiving and the volunteer rendering the act, undertaken for reasons arising from socio-developmental, business or corporate orientation, commitment or conviction for the attainment of the public good and where monetary and other incentives or reward are not the primary motivating factors.¹⁹
b. **Volunteers** – refer to individuals or groups who, for reasons arising from their socio-developmental, business and corporate orientation, commitment or conviction, contribute time, service, and resources, whether on full-time or part-time basis, to a just and essential social development cause, mission, or endeavor in the belief that their activity is mutually meaningful and beneficial to public interest as well as to themselves.²⁰


**Section 5. Modalities of Volunteerism in the Bangsamoro.** – The modalities of volunteerism in the Bangsamoro, include but not limited to the following:

a. **Volunteerism in Disasters.** – This involves consistent individual or group participation in disaster preparedness and response activities undertaken either by the government agencies or non-government organizations;
b. **Volunteerism in Community Activities.** – This covers volunteerism of individuals or groups in activities that benefit the vulnerable and disadvantaged sectors, like women, children, elderly, differently-abled persons, orphans, and indigenous peoples;
c. **Volunteerism in Agriculture and Aquaculture Sectors.** – This involves volunteerism of individual or group activities and initiatives that help promote productivity and improve the income of farmers and fisherfolks;
d. **Volunteerism in Health Sector.** – This involves individual or group community volunteerism on health-related activities that help save lives, reduce malnutrition, and maternal and child mortality and morbidity;
e. **Volunteerism in Education Sector.** – This involves individual or group volunteerism in activities that help improve access to education of less fortunate children especially those in the geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDA);
f. **Volunteerism in the Non-Government Organizations.** – This covers individuals or groups who have been serving as volunteers in the non-profit organizations that are advancing the rights, welfare and protection of vulnerable and disadvantaged sectors; and
g. **Volunteerism in Volunteer Organizations.** – This pertains to non-profit organizations that promote and deliver volunteerism-related programs and activities among vulnerable and disadvantaged sectors of the community in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 6. Bangsamoro Volunteers Advisory Body (BVAB).** – There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Volunteers Advisory Body composed of the following composition:

a. Cabinet Secretary, Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) as Chair;
b. Director General, Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) as Co-Chair;
c. Director General, Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM);
d. Director General, Ministry of Education, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) as Member;
e. Director General, Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG) as Member;
f. Director General, Ministry of Social Welfare Service and Development (MSSD) as Member;
g. Director General, Ministry of Health (MOH);
h. Representative from Civil Society Organizations as Member;
i. Representative from the Academe as Member; and
j. Representative from the Business Sector as Member.

The representatives from the civil society organizations, the academe and the business sector shall be appointed by the Chief Minister and serve for the period of three (3) years. They shall be entitled for per diem in every meeting.

The Board shall meet at least twice a year, once each semester. It shall adopt its internal rules and procedures.

**Section 7. Functions of the Bangsamoro Volunteers Advisory Body.** – The Bangsamoro Volunteers Advisory Body has the following functions:

a. Adopt policies, plans, programs, and projects that will advance volunteerism in the Bangsamoro consistent with the national volunteerism policies;
b. Provide guidance and advisory services on volunteer matters;
c. Adopt guidelines for recognition and for a volunteerism awards system in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
d. Recommend volunteers from the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region for national and international recognition; and
e. Serve as a venue for enhancing policies and measures that will link volunteerism to the services of government.

**Section 8. Creation of Bangsamoro Volunteer's Coordinating Bureau (BVCB).** – There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Volunteer’s Coordinating Bureau as an attached Bureau under the BPDA.

**Section 9. Powers and Functions of the Bangsamoro Volunteer's Coordinating Bureau.** – The Bangsamoro Volunteer’s Coordinating Bureau has the following powers and functions:

a. Receive, manage and administer funds and donations through the BPDA, from all sources including foreign assistance, subject to usual accounting and auditing rules and regulations;
b. Enter in to contract and agreements through the BPDA relative to volunteerism;
c. Recommend policies, plans, programs and projects to the Bangsamoro Volunteers Advisory Body for the promotion and development of volunteerism in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
d. Recommend guidelines for a volunteerism recognition and awards system in the Bangsamoro;
e. Assess, review and recommend volunteers for recognition;
f. Coordinate and establish linkage and network with the Philippine National Volunteer Service Coordinating Agency (PNVSCA) and with international volunteerism organizations;
g. Provide technical assistance to volunteers and volunteer organizations in the region;
h. Promote volunteerism among women and youth and volunteerism as a useful mechanism in the development of communities;
i. Document, recognize, and promote the best volunteerism practices in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
j. Monitor and evaluate volunteerism activities in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
k. Perform such other functions as may be directed by higher authorities.

**Section 10. Organizational Structure of the Bangsamoro Volunteer's Coordinating Bureau.** – The Bangsamoro Volunteer’s Coordinating Bureau shall be composed of the following:

a. the Office of the Regional Coordinator,
b. the Research, Policy and Planning Division,
c. the Coordination, and Monitoring and Evaluation Division.

The Finance and Administrative works shall be under the BPDA.

The Bureaus shall be headed by a Regional Coordinator with a salary grade of 25.

**Section 11. Role of the Bangsamoro Government.** – The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the functionality of the structure herein created and the necessary support for the implementation of this Act. It shall also integrate in the its policies the measures that will help promote volunteerism in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 12. Appropriations.** – The funds necessary for the operationalization of this Act shall be included in the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 13. Separability Clause.** – If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

**Section 14. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, orders, rules and regulations, which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended accordingly.

**Section 15. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its full publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the BARMM.
