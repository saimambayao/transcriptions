**AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE TO BANGSAMORO PEOPLE AND COMMUNITIES OUTSIDE THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, DEFINING ITS IMPLEMENTING MECHANISM, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR**

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:*

**Section 1. Short Title** - This Act shall be known and cited as the **"Bangsamoro Assistance for Inclusive Development Act of 2025"** or **Bangsamoro Aid**, for brevity.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy** – It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure that the Bangsamoro people outside the Bangsamoro territory are granted sufficient assistance from this Government for their welfare and development.

The Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with concerned local government units where these Bangsamoro communities are situated, and appropriate national government agencies, shall ensure that the rights and benefits of the Bangsamoro communities outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region are protected and provided.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms** - For the purposes of this Act, the terms or words and phrases shall be construed as follows:

(a) **“Bangsamoro People and Communities Outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region”** refer to Bangsamoro people and communities located outside of the territorial jurisdiction of BARMM. These Bangsamoro areas can be individually or collectively referred to as Bangsamoro Populated Communities (BPCs), Bangsamoro Populated Area/s (BPAs) or that may be designated by the Council as “Specially Designated Area” (SDA).

(b) **“BDAMC”** or the **“Council”** refers to Bangsamoro Development Assistance and Management Council created under this Act.

(c) **“BDAP”** refers to Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan to be implemented under this Act.

(d) **“BPC”** refers to Bangsamoro populated community where at least two (2) contiguous or adjacent barangays in a municipality or across other municipality/ies are dominated by the Bangsamoro people.

(e) **“BPA”** refers to Bangsamoro populated area where a municipality or more in a province are dominated by the Bangsamoro people.

(f) **“SDA”** refers to Specially Designated Area (SDA) of a Bangsamoro community that may be designated by the Council through a Resolution.

(g) **“Al-madinat Al-shaqiqa”** or **“Sister City”** refers to a form of legal and social agreement between two municipalities to promote social, cultural and economic ties among the Bangsamoro Local Government Units.

(h) **Development assistance** – refers to regular assistance including, but not limited to, capacity-building, financial and technical assistance, and institutional support provided by the Council for the social, cultural, and economic development of the Bangsamoro people or communities outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region funded under this Act. Development assistance shall not include aid provided during disasters or calamities.

(i) **BDP** – refers to the Bangsamoro Development Plan of the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council.

(j) **Conflict Affected Area/s** – refers to an area or areas outside BARMM but dominated by Bangsamoro people that have experienced prolonged armed conflict, social unrest, and displacement due to historical, political and cultural tensions brought about by decades of conflict between the Philippine Government and various Moro revolutionary groups such as the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) and Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF).

(k) **Bangsamoro People** – refers to a group of people as defined by the Bangsamoro Organic Law or Republic Act 11054.

**Section 4. Scope and Coverage** – This Act shall apply to Bangsamoro people and communities outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region identified and designated by the Council as Bangsamoro Populated Area/s (BPAs) or Bangsamoro Populated Communities (BPCs), or other special designation that the Council may determine.

**Section 5. Criteria & Prioritization** – Subject to Section 13 hereof, underserved and conflict-affected areas and communities designated as BPA, BPC or SDA shall be prioritized to benefit from development assistance. *Provided that*, the Council shall establish a set of criteria, which shall include, but not be limited to, the following:

a) Preparedness of the designated BPA, BPC or SDA to collaborate with the BDAMC in a form of a resolution or petition;
b) The socio-economic condition of the concerned community/area necessitates an urgent intervention and support; and
c) The concerned area has received inadequate assistance from BARMM’s agencies or none at all.

**Section 6. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan** - There is hereby established a comprehensive framework for implementing development assistance to Bangsamoro people and communities in the identified BPAs /BPCs/SDAs. This framework shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan, hereinafter referred to as BDAP, and shall guide the Council, the concerned ministries/offices/agencies, development partners, and concerned local government units in implementing plans, programs, and services in the aforementioned areas.

The BDAP shall include, but not be limited to, the following programs and services:

a. Development of potable water systems and/or local electrification;
b. Small and medium-sized infrastructure projects such as community roads and bridges; irrigation, medical and dental clinics; *Madaris*, housing & resettlements; multi-purpose centers and barangay halls;
c. Social support services, including secular and Arabic education & scholarship; health and mental care; social welfare programs (e.g., burial assistance, marriage counselling, rido counselling, deportees’ assistance); human rights education, environmental protection activities; climate change adaptation and disaster preparedness;
d. Economic development initiatives, such as livelihood programs, aquaculture and agricultural support services, small business assistance, and skills training;
e. Cultural preservation and promotion activities; and,
f. Strengthening of local government institutions and capacities, subject to Section 10 (j) hereof.


**Section 7. Creation of the Management Council** - There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Development Assistance and Management Council (BDAMC) which shall serve as the implementing and management body to execute the BDAP and other issuances of the Chief Minister pertinent to this Act.

The Council shall be headed by the Chief Minister, who shall call and preside over the regular quarterly meeting of the Council. In the absence of the Chief Minister, the Vice Chair shall act as the Chairperson and Presiding Officer. The head of the Council shall approve projects and programs upon the recommendation of the Council and in close coordination with the concerned implementing ministry, office, or agency.

**Section 8. Quorum.** A simple majority of all members of the Council shall constitute a quorum for the conduct of its business. In the absence of a quorum, the members present shall constitute an Executive Committee and may proceed with the order of business.

**Section 9. Composition and Organizational Structure** – The Council shall be composed of *ex-officio* members from selected ministries/offices/agencies of the Bangsamoro Government and representatives of other development partners. The Council shall be chaired by the Chief Minister, and its members shall include:

1. Minister of Health (MOH);
2. Minister of Social Services and Development (MSSD);
3. Minister of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE);
4. Minister of Science and Technology (MOST);
5. Minister of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD);
6. Minister of Public Order and Safety MPOS;
7. Minister of Interior and Local Government (MILG);
8. Minister of Public Works (MPW);
9. Minister of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE);
10. Minister of Finance, and Budget and Management (MFBM);
11. Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR);
12. Minister of Labor and Employment (MOLE);
13. Minister of Indigenous People’s Affairs (MIPA);
14. Minister of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT);
15. Director General of Bangsamoro Planning and Development Agency (BPDA);
16. Chairman, Bangsamoro Commission for Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH);
17. Chair, Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC)
18. Chair, Bangsamoro Youth Commission (BYC)
19. One (1) Representative from the International donor agencies;
20. One (1) Representative from local or national NGO;
21. One (1) Representative from the concerned BPA/BP/SDA;

The Vice Chair shall be elected from among the members of the Council. The representatives from the international donor agencies, NGO and BPA/BPC/SDA shall be appointed by the Chief Minister. Provided that, the representative from the BPA/BPC/SDA shall be rotated from among the Bangsamoro areas that the Council may designate.

Concerned ministries, agencies and offices shall appoint their respective permanent representatives and permanent alternates to the Council.

**Section 10. Powers and Functions of the Council** – To successfully implement the provisions of this Act, the Council is hereby vested with the following powers and functions:

a. Adopt its Internal Rules of Procedures subject to the limitations set forth in this Act;
b. Formulate plans and policies for the effective implementation of the Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan (BDAP) and the provisions of this Act;
c. Ensure that all projects and programs are aligned with the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs);
d. Determine Bangsamoro areas and designate them as Bangsamoro Populated Area/s (BPA) or Bangsamoro Populated Community/ies (BPC) upon the recommendation of the Technical Secretariat or upon application of the concerned BPA/BPC to be identified as such;
e. It may also designate a particular Bangsamoro area as Specially Designated Area (SDA) through a Council Resolution;
f. Approve/disapprove proposed projects or programs to be implemented by the concerned BARMM ministry/office/agency in the identified BPA, BPC or SDA;
g. Determine staffing patterns and personnel complements, if necessary, subject to Section 11 hereof;
h. May create Technical Working Groups (TWGs) to assist the Technical Secretariat in the implementation of the Council’s decisions and action plans;
i. Establish partnerships with concerned local government units, development partners and appropriate national government agencies in the implementation of the BDAP and other undertakings within the identified BPA, BPC or SDA;
j. Encourage and facilitate BARMM LGUs to foster partnerships with LGUs located within the designated BPA through “sister city” arrangement (*Al-madinat Al-shaqiqa*);
k. Monitor and evaluate the implementation of BDAP to ensure compliance with the objectives of this Act; and,
l. Delegate some of its powers and functions to the Technical Secretariat, as provided herein.

**Section 11. Technical Secretariat** - To ensure the effective implementation of the BDAP, the existing Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC), created by virtue of Section 5, Paragraph “D”, Chapter 1, Title III of the BAA No.13, shall serve as the permanent technical secretariat of the Council. In addition to its existing functions, the OOBC shall:

a. Assist the Council in implementing the Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan;
b. Conduct research and surveys and recommend to the Council areas identified as BPA, BPC or SDA;
c. Receive proposed project or program and submit them to the Council for consideration;
d. Establish coordination with concerned LGUs, national government agencies and other stakeholders in the conduct of its activities and pursuant to the Council’s directives;
e. Coordinate with the Technical Working Group (TWG) that the Council may create to effectively implement the plans and programs set by the Council, and,
f. Perform such other delegated powers and functions as assigned by the Council.


**Section 12. Creation of Special Fund & Appropriation**- There shall be created a special fund known as the **“Bangsamoro Aid Fund”**. An initial amount of One Hundred Million Pesos (P100,000,000.00) shall be allocated for the initial implementation of this Act, to be sourced from the Office of the Chief Minister. Thereafter, a regular budget in the Bangsamoro General Appropriations shall be provided by the concerned ministries/offices/agencies relevant to the implementation of this Act. *Provided that*: not less than 5% of the approved budget of the concerned Ministry/Office/Agency shall be allocated for the implementation of the BDAP exclusively for the designated BPAs, BPCs and SDAs. *Provided further that*: Additional funds may be sourced from National government allocations, international development partners, and private sector contributions. These funds shall be directly managed by the Council, in coordination with the concerned national government agencies or funding partner.

**Section 13. Determination of Bangsamoro Communities and Areas** - Within one hundred twenty (120) days after the effectivity of this Act, the Technical Secretariat shall determine and recommend to the Council the Bangsamoro Populated Communities (BPCs) and Bangsamoro Populated Areas (BPAs) outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region that need development assistance from the Bangsamoro Government.

The determination and recommendation by the Technical Secretariat shall be without prejudice to the rights of Bangsamoro people outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to submit their applications for designation as Bangsamoro Populated Community or Area.

**Section 14. Oversight Function** – The Bangsamoro Parliament, through a special committee, shall exercise oversight function in the implementation of the Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan (BDAP) to ensure that the funds allocated are equitably provided to designated BPC, BPA or SDA, and its implementation is within the BDP framework.

**Section 15. Implementing Rules and Regulations** - Within one hundred twenty (120) days from the effectivity of this Act, the Office of the Chief Minister, together with the Ministers and heads of the concerned ministries and offices or their duly authorized representatives, shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.

**Section 16. Separability Clause** - If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 17. Repealing Clause** –Sub-paragraphs b, c and d, Section 8, Paragraph D, Chapter 1, Title III of BAA No. 13 are hereby expressly repealed. All other regional laws, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

**Section 18. Effectivity** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Official Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation in BARMM
