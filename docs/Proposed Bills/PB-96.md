**AN ACT AUTHORIZING THE ESTABLISHMENT OF AN ANNEX BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT CENTER (BGC) OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT WITHIN COTABATO CITY, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREOF, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

*Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:*

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as “*Annex Government Center Act of 2022.*”

**Section 2. Establishment of Annex Bangsamoro Government Center.** – There is hereby established an Annex Bangsamoro Government Center of the Bangsamoro Government within Cotabato City;

**Section 3. Objectives.** – This Act aims to decongest the existing Bangsamoro Government Center in order to provide more effective and efficient services to the constituents of the Bangsamoro Government.

**Section 4. Criteria for the Selection of the Area.** – The following are the criteria for the selection of the Annex Government Center of the Bangsamoro Government:

1)  **Sparsely populated.** – The proposed Annex BGC shall be located in a sparsely populated area and shall not be a cause of displacements of the population residing in the target area;
2)  **Proximate to the existing BGC.** – The proposed Annex BGC shall be within Cotabato City and shall be within a 10-kilometer radius from the existing BGC;
3)  **Minimal exposure to hazards.** – The proposed Annex BGC shall be located in an area where exposure to natural and human-induced hazards are minimal;
4)  **Accessibility.** – The proposed annex government center shall be located in an area that is connected to the main roads of the city and can be accessed by the general public once operational. It shall also be accessible to the banks and main commercial areas of the city;
5)  **Internet connectivity.** – The proposed annex government center shall be within the internet service providers existing towers or potential for expansion of setting-up of internet tower(s); and
6)  **Stable source of power and water supply.** – The proposed annex government center shall be within the service areas and can be supplied with stable power and water supply by the Cotabato Light and Power Company and the Metro Cotabato Water District respectively.

**Section 5. Area of the Government Center.** – The Annex BGC shall be not less than five (5) hectares located in Cotabato City.

**Section 6. Minimum Office Buildings and Facilities in the Government Center.** – The minimum office buildings consist of:

1)  the main building with an office space for the Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Ministers, and special programs;
2)  ministries and offices of the frontline services such as health, education, social welfare;
3)  offices without offices in the existing BGC;
4)  a grandstand with open space for recreational activities;
5)  a multi-purpose building;
6)  an employees’ cooperative cafeteria; and
7)  a staff house for transient employees.

The facilities shall include basic necessities such as stable power supply, generator sets, excellent internet connectivity, reliable water supply, closed-circuit television (CCTV) system, telephone system, drainage system, cemented road network, among others.

**Section 7. Target Users.** – The target users of the Annex BGC shall be ministries and offices offering frontline services such as health, education, social welfare services, and special programs, as well as ministries and offices without office buildings in the existing BGC.


**Section 8. Bangsamoro Parliament.** – The Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) shall construct a high rise building, as well as develop and expand its current land area to cover areas being occupied by offices that are adjacent to the existing BTA building, such as the Population Commission (POPCOM) Region XII, Ministry of Basic, Technical, and Higher Education (MBHTE), and Department of Agrarian Reform (DAR) Region XII.

**Section 9. Preparation of Architectural and Detailed Engineering Designs.** – The Ministry of Public Works (MPW) shall prepare the necessary documents, including, but not limited to, the architectural and detailed engineering designs of the proposed government center. It shall take into consideration an engineering design which represents the dignity of the Bangsamoro Parliament, signifies as a landmark for the Bangsamoro people, and consecrates the values of an independent and democratic parliamentary body.

**Section 10. Creation of Ad Hoc Body.** – There is hereby created an Ad Hoc Body tasked to conduct a feasibility study on the selection of the Annex Government Center of the Bangsamoro Government and oversee its eventual construction. The MPW, in close coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM), shall conduct the study.

The feasibility study shall contain a project road map for the selection of the Annex BGC and a proposed time table for its accomplishment. The project road map shall include the following:

a.  Project planning and land acquisition;
b.  Conceptual design and development;
c.  Procurement of design and building package;
d.  Construction; and
e.  Procurement and installation of fixtures.

**Section 11. Appropriations.** – An allocation of fifty million pesos (P50,000,000.00) shall be for the land acquisition and two hundred fifty million pesos (P250,000,000.00) for the land development, perimeter fencing, installation of water system, power supply, internet connection, telephone system, drainage system, road network within and connecting to the government center, guard houses, CCTV system, among others.

The Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) is hereby authorized to secure a parcel of land in Cotabato City and the MPW is likewise authorized to develop this parcel of land into an Annex BGC.

**Section 12. Repealing Clause.** – Any provision of law, decree, executive orders, ordinances, rules and regulations inconsistent herewith with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Section 13. Separability Clause.** – If any portion or provision of this Order is declared void or unconstitutional, the remaining portions or provisions thereof shall not be affected by such declaration.

**Section 14. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Region.
