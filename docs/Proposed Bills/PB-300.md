**AN ACT PROVIDING WELLNESS, THROUGH THE CONDUCT OF WEEKLY PHYSICAL CONDITIONING ACTIVITIES, FOR EMPLOYEES OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** This act shall be known as the “PHYSICAL FITNESS ACT OF 2024”

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy and Principles.** The Bangsamoro Government shall protect and promote the right to health of its employees and instill health consciousness among them.

To guarantee the delivery of efficient and effective public service for the Bangsamoro people, the health, safety, and welfare of the Bangsamoro officials and employees must be given paramount consideration. Thus, the Bangsamoro government must establish measures to ensure good physical condition and mitigate the risk of illness to its officials and employees.

The Bangsamoro ministries, offices, and agencies shall conduct weekly Physical Fitness Activities (PFA) that can lead to improved mental health, increased productivity, and enhanced overall quality of life.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.** For purposes of this act, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

a. **Mental Health** - refers to a state of well-being in which the individual realizes one's abilities and potentials, scopes adequately with the normal stresses of life, displays resilience in the face of extreme life events, works productively and fruitfully, and can make a positive contribution to the community.
b. **Physical Conditioning** – refers to any form of exercise that may develop physical fitness.
c. **Physical Fitness** – is a set of attributes that are either health – or skill-related.
d. **Skeleton workforce** – a minimum number of employees needed to maintain the operations of the ministries, offices, and agencies.
e. **Wellness** – the condition of good physical and mental health, especially when actively maintained by proper diet, exercise, and avoidance of risky behavior.

**SECTION 4. Scope and Coverage.** This Act shall cover all Bangsamoro government employees regardless of their status and nature of appointment.

**SECTION 5. Physical Conditioning Program.** All ministries, offices, and agencies in the Bangsamoro region are hereby enjoined to conduct weekly physical conditioning activities.

The activity shall not be less than thirty (30) minutes and shall be conducted in the afternoon of Friday, preferably at 4:30 p.m.: *Provided*, That the quality service of the respective ministries, offices, and agencies will not be interrupted or be prejudiced.

There shall be a skeleton workforce to remain in the office during the conduct of physical fitness activities. The workforce shall perform and provide the services needed to be discharged by the respective ministries, offices, and agencies.

Bangsamoro ministries, offices, and agencies unable to accommodate physical conditioning activities at the designated time and day may create a schedule that suits their office needs in order to adhere to this act.

**SECTION 6. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** All heads of Bangsamoro ministries, offices, agencies, and offices within the scope of this Act shall promulgate rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act within 90 days from the date of its approval.

**SECTION 7. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions not affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 8. Repealing Clause.** All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 9. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in the Official Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
