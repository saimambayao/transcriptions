## Appendix A: Enumerated Powers of the Bangsamoro Government (BOL Article V, Section 2)

The text below reproduces the enumeration verbatim from the BOL. When drafting a bill, your first task is to identify which of these powers provides legal authority for your proposed legislation. If the subject does not fall within any of these enumerated items — and does not fall within the General Welfare Clause (Section 3) — the Parliament likely lacks the power to legislate on it.

> **SECTION 2.** *Powers of the Bangsamoro Government.* — Subject to Section 20, Article X of the Constitution and this Organic Law, the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise its authority over the following matters without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the Philippines:

### The General Welfare Clause (Article V, Section 3)[^2]

The General Welfare Clause extends the Bangsamoro Government's authority beyond the 55 enumerated items above. It is the basis for legislation on subjects that are not explicitly listed but are necessary for effective governance and the promotion of public welfare. The full text reads:

> **SEC. 3.** *General Welfare.* — The Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the powers expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants.

**Practical note for drafters.** If you are relying on the General Welfare Clause rather than a specific enumerated power, say so explicitly in the Explanatory Note and the Declaration of Policy. A bill that rests on implied authority should make its legal reasoning transparent. Identify the express power the proposed legislation is "necessarily implied" from, or explain why the subject is "necessary, appropriate, or incidental" to efficient governance. The stronger you make this connection, the more likely the bill will withstand legal challenge.

## Appendix B: Sample Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (Annotated)

This appendix presents a complete, fictional bill — the "Bangsamoro Community Libraries Act" — with annotations explaining what each part does and why it is there. The bill is not real legislation. It exists solely to show how a well-structured BAA looks from title to effectivity clause.

Use this as a reference when drafting. The annotations are enclosed in brackets and set in bold so they stand out from the bill text.

---

Republic of the Philippines
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
**BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT**
Bangsamoro Government Center, Cotabato City

**BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY**
**(THIRD REGULAR SESSION)**

**BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. ___**

**[ANNOTATION: This is the Caption. It identifies the legislature, the seat of government, the session, and the bill number. The BAA number is left blank during drafting — it is assigned by the Secretary-General upon enactment. Always use the correct session designation.]**

---

Introduced by MP ____________

**[ANNOTATION: The authorship line identifies who filed the bill. If there are co-authors, list them all. This line appears before the long title.]**

---

**AN ACT ESTABLISHING A COMMUNITY LIBRARY SYSTEM IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION, PROVIDING FOR THE CREATION, MANAGEMENT, AND FUNDING OF MUNICIPAL AND BARANGAY LIBRARIES, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**[ANNOTATION: This is the Long Title. It describes the full scope of the bill in a single sentence. The long title must be broad enough to cover everything the bill contains but specific enough to give fair notice of its subject. Under the "one subject, one title" rule, the long title cannot be misleading — every substantive provision must fall within what the title announces. The phrase "and for other purposes" is a standard concluding phrase, but it does not excuse provisions that are genuinely outside the bill's scope.]**

---

*Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:*

**[ANNOTATION: This is the Enacting Clause. It is the formal statement of legislative authority. The standard form during the BTA period is "Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:" — used in approximately 50 of 89 enacted BAAs.[^3] Do not alter it. When an elected Parliament replaces the BTA, the formulation will change to: "Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament:"]**

---

### SECTION 1. *Short Title.* —

This Act shall be known as the *"Bangsamoro Community Libraries Act."*

**[ANNOTATION: The Short Title section gives the law a convenient name for citation. It always appears as Section 1. The short title should be concise and descriptive. Once enacted, people will refer to the law by this name — make it clear and memorable. The short title is set in italics and enclosed in quotation marks.]**

---

### SEC. 2. *Declaration of Policy.* —

The State recognizes the fundamental right of every Bangsamoro citizen to access information and knowledge. The Bangsamoro Government shall promote literacy, lifelong learning, and cultural enrichment by establishing a system of community libraries accessible to all residents of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region regardless of age, gender, religion, ethnicity, or economic status.

Toward this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall establish, fund, and maintain community libraries at the municipal and barangay levels, prioritizing communities with the least access to books, digital resources, and formal educational institutions.

**[ANNOTATION: The Declaration of Policy states the legislative intent and the public interest the bill serves. This section answers the question: why does this law exist? It should be specific enough to guide interpretation but not so detailed that it duplicates the substantive provisions. Avoid making promises in this section that the bill's operative provisions do not deliver. If the declaration says the government "shall" do something, the bill must contain a mechanism to make it happen.]**

---

### SEC. 3. *Definition of Terms.* —

As used in this Act, the following terms shall mean:

(a) *Bangsamoro Community Library* — a public library established under this Act and operated by the municipal or barangay government within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, open to all residents without charge for basic services;

(b) *Basic Library Services* — lending of books and other print materials, provision of reading space, access to reference materials, and digital access where available;

(c) *Digital Resource Center* — a designated area within a Bangsamoro Community Library equipped with computers and internet connectivity for public use;

(d) *Library Development Fund* — the fund established under Section 8 of this Act for the creation, maintenance, and improvement of Bangsamoro Community Libraries;

(e) *Ministry* — the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education of the Bangsamoro Government;

(f) *Municipal Library Board* — the body created under Section 6 of this Act that governs the operation of a Bangsamoro Community Library at the municipal level.

**[ANNOTATION: The Definition of Terms section assigns specific meanings to words the bill uses in a technical sense. Define only terms that need defining — do not define words that carry their ordinary meaning. Each definition should work as a direct substitute: wherever the term appears in the bill, the definition should fit in its place. Use "means" for exhaustive definitions and "includes" for open-ended ones. Cross-references to other sections (like "Section 8 of this Act") tie the definitions to the operative provisions.]**

---

### SEC. 4. *Establishment of Community Libraries.* —

Within two (2) years from the effectivity of this Act, every municipality in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall establish at least one (1) Bangsamoro Community Library located in or near the municipal hall or a similarly accessible public facility.

The Ministry shall identify priority municipalities based on the following criteria:

(a) absence of an existing public library;

(b) distance from the nearest university or college library;

(c) poverty incidence as determined by the most recent data from the Philippine Statistics Authority or the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority; and

(d) population of school-age children and out-of-school youth.

Municipalities already operating a public library that meets the minimum standards prescribed by the Ministry shall be deemed compliant with this section.

**[ANNOTATION: This is the first Substantive Provision. It creates the core obligation — every municipality must have at least one community library. Note how it sets a timeline ("within two years"), assigns responsibility to a specific body ("the Ministry"), establishes criteria for prioritization using a clear list, and provides a savings clause for municipalities that already comply. Each element is enforceable.]**

---

### SEC. 5. *Barangay Reading Centers.* —

The Ministry, in coordination with the municipal government and the Municipal Library Board, may establish Barangay Reading Centers in barangays that are geographically isolated or have populations exceeding ten thousand (10,000) residents.

A Barangay Reading Center shall be a satellite facility of the municipal Bangsamoro Community Library and shall provide, at minimum, a reading area, a rotating collection of books, and access to basic library services.

The Ministry shall issue guidelines for the establishment and operation of Barangay Reading Centers within six (6) months from the effectivity of this Act.

**[ANNOTATION: This is a second Substantive Provision. It extends the library system to the barangay level but uses permissive language ("may establish") because requiring every barangay to have a reading center would be impractical. The section defines what a Barangay Reading Center is, sets minimum standards, and delegates the details to Ministry guidelines with a clear deadline.]**

---

### SEC. 6. *Municipal Library Boards.* —

There is hereby created in each municipality a Municipal Library Board composed of:

(a) the municipal mayor, as chairperson;

(b) two (2) representatives of the local *sangguniang bayan*, to be designated by the vice-mayor;

(c) one (1) representative of the Ministry;

(d) one (1) representative of the local school division;

(e) one (1) representative from a people's organization or non-governmental organization operating in the municipality, to be selected by the municipal mayor; and

(f) the head librarian of the Bangsamoro Community Library, as secretary.

The Municipal Library Board shall:

(1) oversee the operations and development of the Bangsamoro Community Library;

(2) approve the annual acquisition plan for books and materials;

(3) set the rules for library use and borrowing;

(4) submit an annual report to the Ministry on the library's activities, collections, and usage; and

(5) recommend to the municipal government the annual budget allocation for the library.

Members of the Municipal Library Board shall serve without additional compensation.

**[ANNOTATION: This is a third Substantive Provision that creates a governance structure. When a bill creates a body, it must specify: (1) composition, (2) manner of selection, (3) powers and functions, and (4) compensation or the lack thereof. This section does all four. The composition is listed with enough specificity that there is no ambiguity about who sits on the board.]**

---

### SEC. 7. *Staffing and Training.* —

Each Bangsamoro Community Library shall have at least one (1) full-time librarian who meets the qualifications prescribed by the Civil Service Commission and the Ministry.

The Ministry, in coordination with the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government, shall develop and implement a training program for community librarians within one (1) year from the effectivity of this Act. The training program shall cover:

(a) library management and cataloguing;

(b) digital literacy and basic information technology;

(c) community engagement and literacy promotion; and

(d) preservation of Bangsamoro cultural materials.

**[ANNOTATION: This is an Administrative Provision. It addresses how the law will be implemented — who will staff the libraries and how they will be trained. Administrative provisions translate the bill's mandate into operational reality. Without this section, the bill creates libraries but provides no mechanism for ensuring they have qualified personnel.]**

---

### SEC. 8. *Library Development Fund.* —

There is hereby established a Library Development Fund to be administered by the Ministry. The fund shall consist of:

(a) annual appropriations from the Bangsamoro Expenditure Program as provided under Section 9 of this Act;

(b) grants, donations, and contributions from domestic and international sources accepted by the Bangsamoro Government for library purposes; and

(c) income derived from fees charged for non-basic library services, such as photocopying, printing, and room rental.

The Ministry shall promulgate rules and regulations governing the management, disbursement, and accounting of the Library Development Fund within six (6) months from the effectivity of this Act.

All expenditures from the Library Development Fund shall be subject to existing auditing laws and regulations.

**[ANNOTATION: The creation of a dedicated fund is a common mechanism in Bangsamoro legislation. This section identifies all funding sources, assigns a fund administrator, requires implementing rules with a deadline, and subjects the fund to audit. The last sentence is important — it ensures accountability and connects the bill to the broader legal framework for public finance.]**

---

### SEC. 9. *Appropriation.* —

The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of the Ministry. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for its continued implementation shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro Expenditure Program.

**[ANNOTATION: The Appropriation Clause identifies where the money will come from. There are two standard approaches: (1) charging initial costs to the implementing agency's current budget and providing for future appropriations in the annual BEP, or (2) specifying a lump-sum amount. Approach (1) is more common because specifying exact amounts in the bill can create problems if costs change. Never create obligations without identifying a funding source — unfunded mandates are a common drafting failure.]**

---

### SEC. 10. *Penalties.* —

Any public officer or employee who misappropriates, misuses, or diverts funds allocated for Bangsamoro Community Libraries or the Library Development Fund shall be subject to administrative and criminal penalties under existing laws, including Republic Act No. 3019 (Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act), Republic Act No. 6713 (Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees), and applicable Bangsamoro civil service laws.

Any person who willfully destroys, defaces, or steals library materials, equipment, or facilities shall be liable for the cost of replacement or repair and shall be subject to penalties under the Revised Penal Code and applicable Bangsamoro laws.

**[ANNOTATION: This is the Enforcement Provision. It specifies consequences for violations. Note that it references existing national laws rather than creating entirely new penalty schemes — this is good practice when national laws already cover the conduct. The section distinguishes between public officers (who face stricter accountability) and any person (a broader category). Be specific about which laws apply.]**

---

### SEC. 11. *Separability Clause.* —

If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**[ANNOTATION: The Separability Clause is standard in virtually every bill. It ensures that if a court strikes down one provision, the rest of the law survives. The language is formulaic — use this exact wording or its equivalent. Do not vary it without reason.]**

---

### SEC. 12. *Repealing Clause.* —

All laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof which are inconsistent with any provision of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**[ANNOTATION: The Repealing Clause removes conflicting provisions from other laws. This is the "general repealing clause" formulation. If the bill specifically repeals an identifiable law or section of a law, name it explicitly before the general clause. A specific repeal is always stronger than a general one. Courts disfavor implied repeals — if you intend to repeal something, say so by name.]**

---

### SEC. 13. *Effectivity.* —

This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in at least one (1) newspaper of general circulation within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**[ANNOTATION: The Effectivity Clause states when the law takes effect. Under the BOL,[^4] the standard rule is fifteen days after complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation within the BARMM. Some laws specify a different trigger — such as "upon approval" for urgent measures — but the fifteen-day publication rule is the default. Do not omit this section. A law without an effectivity clause creates confusion about when it becomes enforceable.]**

---

(Sgd.)

### EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region faces a severe shortage of public libraries. According to the National Library of the Philippines, there are fewer than ten registered public libraries serving the entire region's population of more than four million. Many municipalities have no library at all. Schools operate with limited book collections, and most residents have no access to reading materials outside the classroom.

This bill addresses that gap by mandating the creation of a Bangsamoro Community Library in every municipality, with satellite Barangay Reading Centers in geographically isolated or densely populated barangays. It creates a governance structure at the municipal level — the Municipal Library Board — to ensure local ownership and accountability. It establishes a dedicated Library Development Fund to sustain the system beyond annual appropriation cycles.

The legal authority for this measure rests on two enumerated powers under Article V, Section 2 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law: item (kk), "libraries and museums, and historical, cultural, and archaeological sites," and item (v), "education and skills training."[^5] The General Welfare Clause further supports the measure as one that promotes literacy, cultural enrichment, and access to information — all essential to the promotion of general welfare.

The bill draws on the experience of the Department of Education's school library standards, the National Library of the Philippines' guidelines for public libraries, and similar legislation in other ASEAN countries. It has been reviewed in consultation with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education and endorsed by the Philippine Librarians Association — Bangsamoro Chapter.

The immediate passage of this bill is earnestly requested.

(Sgd.)

**MP _______________**

**[ANNOTATION: The Explanatory Note is the drafter's argument for the bill. It is filed alongside the bill but is not part of the enacted law. A strong Explanatory Note answers three questions: (1) What problem does the bill solve? (2) Why is legislation the right response? (3) What does the bill do? It should cite the specific BOL authority, use data, and be concise — one to three pages is typical. The principal author signs it.]**

This appendix presents a complete, fictional resolution with annotations explaining each part. The resolution is a simple proposed resolution — the most common type — urging action on a matter within the Bangsamoro Parliament's concern.

Use this as a reference when drafting resolutions. Compare it with the bill structure in Appendix B to understand how resolutions differ.

**BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT**
Bangsamoro Government Center, Cotabato City

**[ANNOTATION: The header is identical to the header used for bills. It identifies the government, the autonomous region, and the parliamentary body. This institutional identification is required on all official legislative documents.]**

---

**PROPOSED RESOLUTION NO. ___**

**[ANNOTATION: The resolution number is assigned by the Bills and Index Division upon filing, just as with bills. "Proposed Resolution" indicates this is a simple resolution of the Parliament expressing its sense or will on a matter. If it were a joint resolution, it would read "Joint Resolution No. ___." If concurrent, "Concurrent Resolution No. ___." The type of resolution determines its procedural requirements and legal effect.]**

---

**A RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF BASIC, HIGHER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION TO DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A BANGSAMORO MOTHER TONGUE-BASED MULTILINGUAL EDUCATION PROGRAM THAT INCLUDES MERANAW, MAGUINDANAON, TAUSUG, SAMA, YAKAN, AND OTHER BANGSAMORO LANGUAGES AS MEDIA OF INSTRUCTION IN EARLY CHILDHOOD AND PRIMARY EDUCATION**

**[ANNOTATION: The title of a resolution serves the same function as the long title of a bill — it describes the full scope of what the resolution covers. Resolution titles typically begin with a gerund or descriptive phrase: "A Resolution Urging...," "A Resolution Commending...," "A Resolution Expressing...," or "A Resolution Directing...." The title should be complete enough that a reader understands the resolution's purpose without reading the body.]**

---

Introduced by MP _______________

---

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Organic Law recognizes the right of the Bangsamoro people to preserve and develop their culture and language, and Article V, Section 2(p) expressly grants the Bangsamoro Government authority over "culture and language";[^6]

**[ANNOTATION: The first WHEREAS clause establishes the legal foundation. In resolutions, the WHEREAS clauses follow a deductive order — they move from the broadest, most authoritative basis to the most specific and immediate concern. Starting with the BOL provision grounds the resolution in the Parliament's legislative authority. Note the exact citation of the BOL provision and the verbatim quotation of the enumerated power.]**

WHEREAS, the Republic Act No. 10523, known as the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013, mandates the use of the learner's mother tongue as the primary medium of instruction from Kindergarten to Grade 3, but the implementation of this policy in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region has been limited due to insufficient instructional materials and teacher training in Bangsamoro languages;

**[ANNOTATION: The second WHEREAS clause identifies the national law that applies to the subject and acknowledges the gap between what the law requires and what is actually happening on the ground. This clause narrows from the broad legal authority (first clause) to the specific policy context.]**

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is home to thirteen (13) ethnolinguistic groups, and the Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 identifies the preservation and promotion of Bangsamoro languages as a development priority under its socio-cultural agenda;

**[ANNOTATION: The third WHEREAS clause introduces supporting policy context — the BDP — and factual background about the region's linguistic diversity. Each successive clause gets closer to the specific action the resolution urges. This is the deductive ordering principle in practice: legal authority, then national policy context, then regional development priorities, then the specific problem and proposed response.]**

WHEREAS, the development of mother tongue-based instructional materials in Meranaw, Maguindanaon, Tausug, Sama, Yakan, and other Bangsamoro languages requires a coordinated effort between the Ministry, local linguistic experts, cultural institutions, and the communities themselves;

**[ANNOTATION: The fourth and final WHEREAS clause identifies the operational challenge and the need for coordination. It sets up the resolution's operative clause by making clear that the problem requires institutional action, not just goodwill. Four WHEREAS clauses is typical for a resolution of this type. Simple commendatory resolutions may have only two or three; complex policy resolutions may have more.]**

(2) develops culturally appropriate instructional materials in each identified language, in consultation with native speakers, local linguists, and cultural organizations;

(3) establishes a teacher training program for mother tongue-based multilingual instruction; and

(4) coordinates with the Department of Education and the National Commission for Culture and the Arts to ensure alignment with national education standards.

**[ANNOTATION: This is the Operative Clause — the heart of the resolution. It states the specific action the Parliament urges, directs, or resolves. The formulation "NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED BY THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT" is standard. The operative clause should be concrete and specific — it names the agency, sets a timeline, and lists what the action should include. Vague resolutions that urge an agency to "look into" a matter carry little weight.]**

## Appendix D: Pre-Drafting Checklist

This checklist is designed to be used before you start writing. Go through it after receiving a legislative directive and completing your research, but before you draft the first section of the bill. If you cannot check off an item, stop and address it. A gap at this stage becomes a structural problem in the draft.

Print this checklist. Keep it on your desk. Use it every time.

---

### 1. Legislative Directive

- [ ] 1.1 Has the MP provided a clear legislative direction?
- [ ] 1.2 Is the Initial Legislative Template documented?
- [ ] 1.3 Does the proposal align with the MP's legislative agenda?
- [ ] 1.4 Has the MP approved the Initial Legislative Template?
- [ ] 1.5 Has the MP identified the policy objectives the bill must achieve?
- [ ] 1.6 Has the MP indicated a desired timeline for filing?
- [ ] 1.7 Have potential co-authors been identified or discussed?

### 2. Legal Authority

- [ ] 2.1 Is the subject within the enumerated powers (BOL Article V, Section 2)?
- [ ] 2.2 If relying on the General Welfare Clause (Article V, Section 3), has the legal basis been documented and the connection to an express power articulated?
- [ ] 2.3 Does the 1987 Constitution permit this legislation?
- [ ] 2.4 Are there national laws that apply to or preempt the subject?
- [ ] 2.5 If national laws exist, has the relationship between the proposed BAA and those laws been analyzed?
- [ ] 2.6 Are there existing Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts on the same or a related subject?
- [ ] 2.7 If existing BAAs apply, has the proposed bill been checked for conflicts or overlaps?
- [ ] 2.8 Are there BOL provisions that specifically address the subject? If so, have they been quoted and cited?
- [ ] 2.9 Has the residual clause been considered — is there any aspect of this subject reserved to Congress?
- [ ] 2.10 Are there Shari'ah considerations relevant to the subject matter?

### 3. Research

- [ ] 3.1 Has the Policy Research and Legal Services (PRLS) been consulted?
- [ ] 3.2 Have all related national laws and their implementing rules and regulations been identified?
- [ ] 3.3 Have all related BAAs, resolutions, and executive issuances been identified?
- [ ] 3.4 Has a comparative review been conducted — how do other jurisdictions or regions handle this subject?
- [ ] 3.5 Is the Legislative Research Briefer complete?
- [ ] 3.6 Has the Legislative Research Briefer been reviewed and approved by the MP?
- [ ] 3.7 Have relevant data and statistics been gathered (enrollment figures, budget data, population data, incidence rates, etc.)?
- [ ] 3.8 Have relevant provisions of the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023-2028 been reviewed for alignment?
- [ ] 3.9 Have implementing rules and regulations of related laws been reviewed for practical insights?

### 4. Consultation

- [ ] 4.1 Have subject-matter experts been consulted?
- [ ] 4.2 Have the implementing agencies that will carry out the law been consulted?
- [ ] 4.3 Have the implementing agencies confirmed their capacity to implement the bill's requirements?
- [ ] 4.4 Have concerned sectors and constituencies been consulted?
- [ ] 4.5 Have affected local government units been consulted?
- [ ] 4.6 Has the gender impact of the proposed legislation been assessed?
- [ ] 4.7 Has the human rights impact been assessed?
- [ ] 4.8 If the bill affects indigenous peoples, have their representatives been consulted consistent with their customary processes?
- [ ] 4.9 Have consultation findings been documented and shared with the MP?

### 5. Drafting Readiness

- [ ] 5.1 Is the working outline prepared?
- [ ] 5.2 Has the working outline been reviewed by the MP or a senior staff member?
- [ ] 5.3 Has the comparative matrix been prepared (showing how the proposed bill relates to existing laws)?
- [ ] 5.4 Are all relevant BOL provisions identified, quoted, and ready for citation?
- [ ] 5.5 Has the bill's structure been determined (number of articles/chapters, if any, and the general sequence of sections)?
- [ ] 5.6 Have the terms that require definition been listed?
- [ ] 5.7 Has the implementing agency or mechanism been identified?
- [ ] 5.8 Has the funding source or appropriation mechanism been determined?
- [ ] 5.9 Has the penalty or enforcement scheme been determined?
- [ ] 5.10 Has a timeline for drafting been set, with milestones for first draft, MP review, and finalization?
- [ ] 5.11 Are all source materials organized and accessible for reference during drafting?

### 6. Compliance and Consistency

- [ ] 6.1 Has the proposed bill been checked against the BOL compliance requirements (Chapter 11)?
- [ ] 6.2 Is the terminology consistent with the BOL and existing BAAs?
- [ ] 6.3 Has the bill been checked for potential conflicts with the Constitution's bill of rights and other provisions?
- [ ] 6.4 If the bill creates a government body, does it specify composition, powers, functions, funding, and accountability?
- [ ] 6.5 If the bill imposes obligations, does every obligation have an accountable body and a consequence for non-compliance?
- [ ] 6.6 If the bill requires expenditure, does it include an appropriation clause?

---

**How to use this checklist.** Items 1 through 4 should be completed before drafting begins. Items 5 and 6 should be verified at the start of drafting and re-checked when the first draft is complete. If you discover during drafting that an item in sections 1 through 4 was not adequately addressed, pause the draft and go back. It is faster to fill a research gap now than to restructure a bill later.

This checklist does not replace the Quality Review Checklist (Appendix E), which is used after the draft is written. The Pre-Drafting Checklist ensures you are ready to write. The Quality Review Checklist ensures what you wrote is sound.

## Appendix F: Standard Clause Templates

This appendix provides the prescribed verbatim text for standard clauses used in Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts. These templates are derived from pattern analysis of all 89 enacted BAAs (BAA 1-89) and represent the most widely used and recommended formulations.[^7]

Use these templates as your starting point. Copy the exact wording unless the bill's subject matter requires a specific variation. Consistency in standard clauses reduces drafting errors, speeds committee review, and avoids unnecessary interpellation questions about clause wording.

---

### F.1 Separability Clause

**Recommended Standard (based on BAA 25, 26, 30 — used in general legislation):**

> If any provision of this Act or the application of such provision is declared invalid, the remainder of this Act shall not be affected by such declaration.

**Variant for Municipality Creation Bills (BAA 41-48 — includes BOL reference):**

> If, for any cause, any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest of the provisions hereof which are not affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Variant for Hospital Bills (BAA 78, 79 — "declared separable" formulation):**

> The provisions of this Act are hereby declared separable, and in the event one or more such provisions or parts thereof are declared unconstitutional, such declaration of unconstitutionality shall not affect the validity of the other provisions thereof.

---

### F.2 General Repealing Clause

**Recommended Standard (based on BAA 73 — comprehensive modern formulation):**

> All laws, decrees, executive orders, issuances, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof, that are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Variant for Municipality Creation Bills (BAA 41-48):**

> All laws, decrees, executive orders, or regulations or any part thereof which may be contrary or inconsistent to this Act are hereby repealed, amended, modified or altered accordingly.

**Variant with BOL Exception (BAA 53-55, 70-71 — later municipality creation bills):**

> All laws, decrees, executive orders or regulations or any part thereof which may be contrary or inconsistent to this Act, except those that declare any part of this Act unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Republic Act No. 11054, are hereby repealed, amended, modified, or altered accordingly.

---

### F.3 Specific Repealing Clause (Fill-in-the-Blank)

When the bill repeals identified provisions of existing law, use this pattern before the general repealing clause:

> [Section/s \_\_\_ of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. \_\_\_, otherwise known as the "\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_," is/are hereby repealed.]
>
> All laws, decrees, executive orders, issuances, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof, that are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

**Source:** Pattern derived from BAA 39 and BAA 50, which include specific named repeals. A specific repeal is always stronger than a general one. Courts disfavor implied repeals — if you intend to repeal something, name it explicitly.

---

### F.4 Effectivity Clause

**Recommended Standard (based on BAA 41-48, 53-55, 70-71 — Bangsamoro Gazette option, used in 13+ BAAs):**

> This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in one (1) newspaper of regional circulation.

**Variant — Newspaper Only (BAA 1, 2, 11, 36 — earlier BAAs):**

> This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**Variant — Official Gazette Option (BAA 76 — 2025 formulation):**

> This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**Variant — Complete Publication (BAA 73):**

> This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days following its complete publication in a newspaper of regional circulation.

---

### F.5 IRR Clause — 60-Day Variant

**Standard 60-Day (based on BAA 25 — most common timeframe for hospital and regulatory bills):**

> The [implementing agency] shall promulgate the necessary implementing rules and regulations within sixty (60) days after the effectivity of this Act.

**60-Day with Consultation (BAA 73 — recommended for bills with LGU involvement):**

> Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the [implementing agency] shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations, in consultation with [stakeholders], for the effective implementation of this Act.

**60-Day with Dual Agency (BAA 80):**

> Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the [primary agency] and [secondary agency] shall promulgate the appropriate Implementing Rules and Regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

---

### F.6 IRR Clause — 90-Day Variant

**Standard 90-Day (based on BAA 78, 79 — used for newer hospital bills):**

> The [implementing agency] shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this Act within ninety (90) days from its effectivity.

---

### F.7 Suppletory Clause

**Standard Formulation (based on BAA 82, Section 275 — Bangsamoro Labor and Employment Code):**

> In cases where this Act contains no applicable provision concerning [subject matter], the [national law] and other relevant [subject-area] legislation shall be applied suppletorily: *Provided*, That they are not contrary to existing Bangsamoro laws.

**Source:** BAA 82 (Bangsamoro Labor and Employment Code of 2024). Use this clause in bills that regulate a subject where comprehensive national legislation already exists and where gaps in the Bangsamoro law should be filled by the national law rather than creating a vacuum.

---

### F.8 Mandatory Review Clause

**Recommended Formulation (based on best practice — no single BAA uses this exact text, but elements appear in sunset/reporting provisions of BAA 78):**

> Within [three (3) / five (5)] years from the effectivity of this Act, the [implementing agency] shall submit to the Parliament, through the [relevant committee], a comprehensive evaluation report on the implementation and impact of this Act. The Parliament shall review this Act based on the evaluation report and determine whether amendments, extension, or repeal are warranted.

**Source:** Derived from the annual reporting and sunset provisions in BAA 78 (Tipo-Tipo General Hospital) and BAA 73 (Iranon Provincial Hospital). Although no enacted BAA uses a standalone "Mandatory Review Clause" by that name, the principle is embedded in the reporting requirements of newer legislation.

---

### Required Appendix: Map and Technical Description

Every municipality creation bill must include as an appendix:

   - Municipality name
   - Province (if applicable)
   - Island: Mindanao
   - Total area in hectares
   - Scale and datum reference
