Appendix C — Sample Resolution (Annotated)

This appendix presents a complete, fictional resolution with annotations explaining each part. The resolution is a simple proposed resolution — the most common type — urging action on a matter within the Bangsamoro Parliament’s concern.

Use this as a reference when drafting resolutions. Compare it with the bill structure in Appendix B to understand how resolutions differ.

Republic of the Philippines Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT Bangsamoro Government Center, Cotabato City

[ANNOTATION: The header is identical to the header used for bills. It identifies the government, the autonomous region, and the parliamentary body. This institutional identification is required on all official legislative documents.]

PROPOSED RESOLUTION NO. ___

[ANNOTATION: The resolution number is assigned by the Bills and Index Division upon filing, just as with bills. “Proposed Resolution” indicates this is a simple resolution of the Parliament expressing its sense or will on a matter. If it were a joint resolution, it would read “Joint Resolution No. .” If concurrent, “Concurrent Resolution No. .” The type of resolution determines its procedural requirements and legal effect.]

A RESOLUTION URGING THE MINISTRY OF BASIC, HIGHER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION TO DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A BANGSAMORO MOTHER TONGUE-BASED MULTILINGUAL EDUCATION PROGRAM THAT INCLUDES MERANAW, MAGUINDANAON, TAUSUG, SAMA, YAKAN, AND OTHER BANGSAMORO LANGUAGES AS MEDIA OF INSTRUCTION IN EARLY CHILDHOOD AND PRIMARY EDUCATION

[ANNOTATION: The title of a resolution serves the same function as the long title of a bill — it describes the full scope of what the resolution covers. Resolution titles typically begin with a gerund or descriptive phrase: “A Resolution Urging…,” “A Resolution Commending…,” “A Resolution Expressing…,” or “A Resolution Directing” The title should be complete enough that a reader understands the resolution’s purpose without reading the body.]

Introduced by MP ___

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Organic Law recognizes the right of the Bangsamoro people to preserve and develop their culture and language, and Article V, Section 2(p) expressly grants the Bangsamoro Government authority over “culture and language”;

[ANNOTATION: The first WHEREAS clause establishes the legal foundation. In resolutions, the WHEREAS clauses follow a deductive order — they move from the broadest, most authoritative basis to the most specific and immediate concern. Starting with the BOL provision grounds the resolution in the Parliament’s legislative authority. Note the exact citation of the BOL provision and the verbatim quotation of the enumerated power.]

WHEREAS, the Republic Act No. 10523, known as the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013, mandates the use of the learner’s mother tongue as the primary medium of instruction from Kindergarten to Grade 3, but the implementation of this policy in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region has been limited due to insufficient instructional materials and teacher training in Bangsamoro languages;

[ANNOTATION: The second WHEREAS clause identifies the national law that applies to the subject and acknowledges the gap between what the law requires and what is actually happening on the ground. This clause narrows from the broad legal authority (first clause) to the specific policy context.]

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is home to thirteen (13) ethnolinguistic groups, and the Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 identifies the preservation and promotion of Bangsamoro languages as a development priority under its socio-cultural agenda;

[ANNOTATION: The third WHEREAS clause introduces supporting policy context — the BDP — and factual background about the region’s linguistic diversity. Each successive clause gets closer to the specific action the resolution urges. This is the deductive ordering principle in practice: legal authority, then national policy context, then regional development priorities, then the specific problem and proposed response.]

WHEREAS, the development of mother tongue-based instructional materials in Meranaw, Maguindanaon, Tausug, Sama, Yakan, and other Bangsamoro languages requires a coordinated effort between the Ministry, local linguistic experts, cultural institutions, and the communities themselves;

[ANNOTATION: The fourth and final WHEREAS clause identifies the operational challenge and the need for coordination. It sets up the resolution’s operative clause by making clear that the problem requires institutional action, not just goodwill. Four WHEREAS clauses is typical for a resolution of this type. Simple commendatory resolutions may have only two or three; complex policy resolutions may have more.]

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED BY THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT, to urge the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education to develop and implement, within one (1) year from the adoption of this Resolution, a Bangsamoro Mother Tongue-Based Multilingual Education Program that:

(1) identifies and prioritizes the Bangsamoro languages to be used as media of instruction in early childhood and primary education;

(2) develops culturally appropriate instructional materials in each identified language, in consultation with native speakers, local linguists, and cultural organizations;

(3) establishes a teacher training program for mother tongue-based multilingual instruction; and

(4) coordinates with the Department of Education and the National Commission for Culture and the Arts to ensure alignment with national education standards.

[ANNOTATION: This is the Operative Clause — the heart of the resolution. It states the specific action the Parliament urges, directs, or resolves. The formulation “NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED BY THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT” is standard. The operative clause should be concrete and specific — it names the agency, sets a timeline, and lists what the action should include. Vague resolutions that urge an agency to “look into” a matter carry little weight.]

RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Ministry shall report to the Parliament, through the Committee on Basic, Higher and Technical Education, on the status of implementation of this Resolution within six (6) months from its adoption and every six (6) months thereafter until the program is fully operational.

[ANNOTATION: A RESOLVED FURTHER clause adds a supplementary directive. Here, it creates a reporting mechanism so the Parliament can track whether the Ministry acts on the resolution. This is good practice — a resolution without follow-up is easily ignored. Note that the report goes through the relevant committee, not directly to the plenary. This is consistent with the committee system’s oversight function.]

RESOLVED FINALLY, that copies of this Resolution shall be furnished to the Office of the Chief Minister, the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education, the Department of Education — Central Office, and the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority.

[ANNOTATION: The RESOLVED FINALLY clause directs distribution of the adopted resolution to the agencies and offices that need to know about it. This is a standard closing clause in most resolutions. It ensures the resolution reaches the relevant parties and becomes part of the official record.]

Adopted,

(Date of adoption)

[ANNOTATION: The adoption line records the date the Parliament adopted the resolution. This is filled in after the resolution passes, not during drafting.]

(Sgd.)

MP ___ Author

[ANNOTATION: The author signature block identifies who filed the resolution. If there are co-authors, each signs. The signature block typically appears at the end. In some formats, the author’s full name and title are printed below the signature line.]
