Appendix E — Quality Review Checklist (Detachable)

This checklist is designed to be printed and used after completing a bill draft, before submitting it for review. Work through every item. If you check “No” or cannot answer confidently, stop and fix the problem before proceeding.

The checklist is the companion to Appendix D (Pre-Drafting Checklist). Appendix D helps you prepare before writing. This appendix helps you verify after writing.

Print this. Keep a copy on your desk. Use it every time.

I. Legal Authority

2 No provision conflicts with the 1987 Philippine Constitution. ☐ ☐ ☐
3 No provision conflicts with the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054). ☐ ☐ ☐
4 Applicable national laws on the same subject have been identified and reviewed for conflicts. ☐ ☐ ☐
5 Existing Bangsamoro Autonomy Acts on the same subject have been reviewed for conflicts, overlaps, and gaps. ☐ ☐ ☐
6 No provision intrudes on powers reserved to Congress under the residual clause. ☐ ☐ ☐
7 Shari’ah considerations have been addressed where the bill affects personal, family, or property relations involving Muslims. ☐ ☐ ☐
8 Human rights implications have been assessed against Article III of the BOL and the Philippine Bill of Rights. ☐ ☐ ☐
9 If the bill imposes taxes or fees, it complies with the fiscal provisions of BOL Article XII. ☐ ☐ ☐

II. Structure and Completeness

No.ItemYesNoN/A
15 The long title accurately describes the bill’s content and scope. ☐ ☐ ☐
16 The one-subject, one-title rule is satisfied — the bill does not contain riders or unrelated provisions. ☐ ☐ ☐
17 The enacting clause uses the correct standard formulation for the current period. ☐ ☐ ☐
18 A short title is present in the preliminary provisions. ☐ ☐ ☐
19 A declaration of policy states the bill’s purpose, objectives, and legislative intent. ☐ ☐ ☐
20 All terms used in a technical, specialized, or restricted sense are defined in the definition of terms section. ☐ ☐ ☐
21 The definitions section does not define words in their ordinary meaning. ☐ ☐ ☐
22 Scope and coverage are clearly stated — who and what the bill applies to. ☐ ☐ ☐
23 Substantive provisions are organized in a logical sequence — general to specific, rights to obligations, prohibitions to penalties. ☐ ☐ ☐
24 Every duty or obligation identifies an accountable body or person. ☐ ☐ ☐
25 Every right identifies who may exercise it and who must respect it. ☐ ☐ ☐
26 Every prohibition has an enforcement mechanism — a penalty, sanction, or consequence. ☐ ☐ ☐
27 Every penalty corresponds to a clearly stated prohibition in the substantive provisions. ☐ ☐ ☐
28 The implementing agency or agencies are identified. ☐ ☐ ☐
29 The implementing agency has the capacity — or the bill provides for the capacity — to carry out the mandate. ☐ ☐ ☐
30 An IRR provision is included if the bill requires detailed rulemaking for implementation. ☐ ☐ ☐
31 The IRR provision includes a specific deadline for promulgation (not “within a reasonable time”). ☐ ☐ ☐
32 An appropriation or funding provision is included if the bill requires funding. ☐ ☐ ☐
33 The funding source is identified — annual BEP, special fund, or specific appropriation. ☐ ☐ ☐
34 A separability clause is present. ☐ ☐ ☐
35 A repealing clause identifies specific provisions of existing law that are repealed or modified. ☐ ☐ ☐
36 The repealing clause does not rely solely on a general repealing clause without specific repeals. ☐ ☐ ☐
37 An effectivity clause is present, following the standard formulation (fifteen days after publication). ☐ ☐ ☐
38 Transitory provisions address existing conditions — pending applications, current license holders, ongoing contracts — if applicable. ☐ ☐ ☐
39 If the bill creates a board or body, the composition, qualifications, terms, quorum, and voting requirements are all specified and internally consistent. ☐ ☐ ☐
40 If the bill creates a pilot or temporary program, a sunset clause with an evaluation mechanism is included. ☐ ☐ ☐
41 Reporting and oversight requirements are specified — who reports, to whom, how often, in what format. ☐ ☐ ☐
42 An explanatory note accompanies the bill (de facto required in the Bangsamoro Parliament). ☐ ☐ ☐

III. Language and Style

No.ItemYesNoN/A
43 “Shall” is used only for mandatory duties and obligations. ☐ ☐ ☐
44 “May” is used only for permissive authority or discretion. ☐ ☐ ☐
45 “Must” is used only for conditions or prerequisites. ☐ ☐ ☐
46 “Should,” “will,” “would,” and “ought” are not used for obligations or conditions. ☐ ☐ ☐
47 Active voice is used throughout — every obligation identifies who acts. ☐ ☐ ☐
48 Present tense is used for normative provisions (not future tense). ☐ ☐ ☐
49 No archaic legalisms appear — no “hereby,” “henceforth,” “hereinbefore,” “hereinafter,” “aforementioned,” “heretofore.” ☐ ☐ ☐
50 No redundant doublets appear — no “null and void,” “cease and desist,” “each and every,” “due and payable,” “fit and proper.” ☐ ☐ ☐
51 “And/or” does not appear anywhere in the bill. ☐ ☐ ☐
52 Lists use proper conjunctive (“and”) or disjunctive (“or”) format, with the conjunction placed before the final item. ☐ ☐ ☐
53 Each sentence expresses one idea. No sentence exceeds four lines of text. ☐ ☐ ☐
54 Long provisions are broken into numbered sub-items or lettered paragraphs for readability. ☐ ☐ ☐
55 Definitions use “means” for exhaustive definitions and “includes” for non-exhaustive definitions — not interchangeably. ☐ ☐ ☐
56 Every defined term passes the substitution test — the definition can replace the term in every instance without altering the provision’s meaning. ☐ ☐ ☐
57 Numbers are written as words with figures in parentheses — “thirty (30) days.” ☐ ☐ ☐
58 Monetary amounts are written in words and figures with the PhP abbreviation — “Two Hundred Thousand Pesos (PhP200,000.00).” ☐ ☐ ☐
59 No vague phrases appear without further specificity — no “as may be necessary,” “within a reasonable time,” “to the extent practicable,” “as appropriate.” ☐ ☐ ☐
60 Gender-neutral or gender-inclusive language is used throughout — “chairperson” not “chairman,” “fisherfolk” not “fishermen.” ☐ ☐ ☐
61 If pronouns are used, they are inclusive — “he or she,” “his or her” — or the provision is restructured to avoid pronouns. ☐ ☐ ☐
62 Section headings are present, descriptive, and formatted consistently (italicized for enrolled bills). ☐ ☐ ☐
63 Sections use “SEC.” followed by Arabic numerals. Articles use Roman numerals. ☐ ☐ ☐
64 Legal citations follow the established format — BOL provisions, national laws, BAAs, and resolutions are cited correctly. ☐ ☐ ☐
65 Abbreviations and acronyms are defined at first use and used consistently thereafter. ☐ ☐ ☐

IV. Internal Consistency

No.ItemYesNoN/A
66 The same term is used for the same concept throughout the bill — no synonyms, no alternating names. ☐ ☐ ☐
67 Section numbering is sequential with no gaps and no duplicates. ☐ ☐ ☐
68 Every cross-reference points to the correct section — each has been individually verified. ☐ ☐ ☐
69 Defined terms are used exactly as defined — no variation in form, no partial use. ☐ ☐ ☐
70 No contradictions exist between any two provisions in the bill. ☐ ☐ ☐
71 If the bill quotes the BOL or another law, the quotation is verbatim. ☐ ☐ ☐
72 Timelines are internally consistent — no provision gives a different deadline for the same action. ☐ ☐ ☐
73 Qualifications for any office or position are stated consistently across all provisions that reference them. ☐ ☐ ☐
74 The scope in the declaration of policy matches the scope in the substantive provisions. ☐ ☐ ☐
75 If the bill lists members of a body in the composition section, the quorum and voting provisions are mathematically consistent with the stated number. ☐ ☐ ☐
76 The bill’s table of contents (if included) matches the actual section headings and numbering in the body. ☐ ☐ ☐
77 Penalty provisions cover every prohibited act — no prohibition is missing a penalty, no penalty references a non-existent prohibition. ☐ ☐ ☐
78 The long title is still accurate after all revisions — it reflects the bill as it stands now, not an earlier draft. ☐ ☐ ☐

V. The Interpellation Test

No.ItemYesNoN/A
79 You have read the bill as a skeptical opposition MP and identified every weakness. ☐ ☐ ☐
80 Every provision can be implemented as written — no provision assumes resources, capacity, or conditions that do not exist. ☐ ☐ ☐
81 You can answer the question “what problem does this bill solve?” with specificity, not generalities. ☐ ☐ ☐
82 You can defend every policy choice in the bill — every threshold, every deadline, every penalty amount. ☐ ☐ ☐
83 You have identified all gaps — scenarios the bill should cover but does not. ☐ ☐ ☐
84 You have identified all unintended consequences — ways the bill could be misused, misinterpreted, or produce results contrary to its intent. ☐ ☐ ☐
85 The bill does not create an unfunded mandate — every financial obligation has a funding source. ☐ ☐ ☐
86 The bill does not delegate unlimited discretion to any single person or agency. ☐ ☐ ☐
87 The bill can be explained in plain language to a non-lawyer — a constituent, a journalist, an implementing officer — without confusion. ☐ ☐ ☐
88 At least one other person — a colleague, a PRLS researcher, a subject-matter expert — has reviewed the draft and provided feedback. ☐ ☐ ☐
