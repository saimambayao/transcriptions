# About This Guidebook

This guidebook was written for the people who draft the laws of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. It is designed for daily use — not as a textbook to be read once and shelved, but as a working reference to be consulted at every stage of the drafting process.

The guidebook covers the full lifecycle of a legislative measure in the Bangsamoro Parliament: from the moment a Member of Parliament issues a legislative directive, through research, consultation, drafting, review, co-authorship, and filing — and onward through the parliamentary stages of readings, committee scrutiny, deliberation, and passage into law. It also addresses the preparation of legislative briefers and budget briefers, the drafting of resolutions and amendments, and the standards of legislative style that govern how laws should be written.

Every provision cited from the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054) and Resolution No. 268 (the Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Parliament) has been quoted verbatim from the source text. The drafting principles and style rules reflect established legislative drafting practice adapted to the specific context of Bangsamoro governance, Shari'ah considerations, and the parliamentary system established under the BOL.

This guidebook is written to be timeless. It applies equally to the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament and to the elected Bangsamoro Parliament that will follow. Where procedures or conventions are specific to the transition period, the text says so. In all other respects, the guidance here is intended to serve any drafter working under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, regardless of when they read it.
