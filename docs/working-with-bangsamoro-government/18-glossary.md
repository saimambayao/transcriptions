# Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines the acronyms, legal terms, institutional terms, Islamic governance terms, and process terms used throughout this guidebook. Terms are listed alphabetically. Bold terms within definitions indicate cross-references to other glossary entries.

---

## A

**ACA (Additional Compensation Allowance)** -- A monthly allowance paid to government employees on top of their base salary. Amount varies by position and budget allocation.

**ACSCCRaPATFEE** -- The 12-competency framework for Complete Staff Work: Analytical and Critical Thinking, Creativity and Innovation, Strategic Thinking, Communication, Collaboration and Teamwork, Research and Analysis, Problem-Solving and Decision Making, Attention to Details and Quality, Time and Attention Management, Flexibility and Adaptability, Emotional Intelligence and Accountability, Ethics and Integrity. Applies to all BARMM government workers who produce **CSW**.

**ADDRESS IT** -- The 7-step **CSW** methodology: **A**nalyze the Context, **D**efine and **D**eliberate, **R**esearch, **E**xplore Options, **S**ubmit, **S**ustain, **I**terate and Improve. The standard process for producing decision-ready staff work in BARMM government.

**Adl** -- Arabic term meaning justice. One of the five core Islamic leadership principles applied in BARMM governance. Requires serving all people fairly, regardless of clan, tribe, religion, or political affiliation.

**Amanah** -- Arabic term meaning trustworthiness. The most fundamental Islamic governance value in BARMM. Treats public resources, information, and authority as sacred trusts that must not be misused.

**Appointing Authority** -- The official who has the power to issue appointments. In ministries, this is the Minister. In the Parliament, this is the Secretary General (for secretariat staff) or the Speaker. In LGUs, this is the local chief executive.

**ARTA (Anti-Red Tape Authority)** -- The national agency that monitors government compliance with service delivery standards. All government offices must have a **Citizen's Charter** specifying processing times and requirements for each service.

**AWA (Alternative Work Arrangement)** -- Flexible work arrangements in government, including work-from-home, compressed workweek, and skeleton workforce. Policies vary by office.

## B

**BAA (Bangsamoro Autonomy Act)** -- A law enacted by the **BTA** Parliament.[^1] BAAs are the Bangsamoro equivalent of national Republic Acts. Examples: BAA 13 (Administrative Code), BAA 17 (Civil Service Code), BAA 84 (Budget System Act).

**BAC (Bids and Awards Committee)** -- The committee in each government office responsible for procurement. Governed by RA 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act).[^2] BAC members must undergo procurement training.

**BARMM (Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao)** -- The autonomous political entity in Mindanao created by the **BOL** (RA 11054) and ratified through a plebiscite in 2019. Comprises 5 provinces (Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao del Norte, Maguindanao del Sur, Basilan, Tawi-Tawi), Cotabato City, and 63 barangays in North Cotabato organized into 8 SGA municipalities.

**BBOI (Bangsamoro Board of Investments)** -- The agency responsible for investment promotion and incentives in BARMM.

**BDP (Bangsamoro Development Plan)** -- The government's medium-term development roadmap. The current plan is the 2nd BDP (2023-2028), which has 6 development goals, 8 strategies, and the 12 Pillars of Moral Governance.

**BHRC (Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission)** -- Created by BAA 4. Investigates human rights violations within BARMM territory.

**BIAB (Bangsamoro Internal Auditing Body)** -- The internal audit unit that monitors financial compliance within BARMM government offices.

**BICTO (Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office)** -- The agency responsible for IT infrastructure, digital government, and technology services in BARMM.

**BOL (Bangsamoro Organic Law)** -- Republic Act 11054,[^3] the law that created BARMM. Ratified through a plebiscite on January 21, 2019 (mainland) and February 6, 2019 (island provinces). Establishes BARMM's powers, government structure, fiscal autonomy, and justice system.

**BPDA (Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority)** -- The agency responsible for development planning, monitoring and evaluation, and coordination of the **BDP** implementation.

**BTA (Bangsamoro Transition Authority)** -- The transitional government of BARMM, serving until regular elections are held. The BTA exercises both legislative (Parliament) and executive (Chief Minister and Cabinet) functions. Currently has 77 Members of Parliament.

**BWC (Bangsamoro Women Commission)** -- Created by BAA 8. Promotes gender equality and women's empowerment across BARMM.

**BYC (Bangsamoro Youth Commission)** -- Created by BAA 10. Promotes youth development and participation in governance.

## C

**CAB (Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro)** -- The peace agreement signed on March 27, 2014, between the Government of the Republic of the Philippines and the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF). The **BOL** is the legislative product of the CAB.

**CBI (Competency-Based Interview)** -- A structured interview format used in government hiring and promotion. Questions are designed to assess specific competencies through the **STAR** method. The interview component is 25% of the **Comparative Assessment**.

**Citizen's Charter** -- A document required by **ARTA** that specifies the services each government office provides, including steps, processing time, fees, and requirements. Must be publicly posted.

**COA (Commission on Audit)** -- The independent constitutional body that audits all government revenue, expenditures, and transactions. COA can **disallow** expenditures, requiring the responsible officials to refund from personal funds.

**COLA (Cost of Living Allowance)** -- An allowance for government employees, now integrated into base pay under recent **SSL** tranches.

**Comparative Assessment** -- The scoring system used by the **HRMPSB** to evaluate applicants for government positions. Five criteria: Education (25%), Experience (25%), Training (15%), Eligibility (10%), Interview (25%).

**COS (Contract of Service)** -- A project-based employment arrangement in government. No CSC eligibility required. Renewable. No security of tenure. Limited benefits (no GSIS, no leave credits). Common entry point for fresh graduates.

**Coterminous** -- An appointment type that ends when the appointing authority's term ends. Common for staff of elected officials, political appointees, and special assistants.

**CSC (Civil Service Commission)** -- The national constitutional body that administers the Philippine civil service, including examinations, qualification standards, personnel rules, and discipline.[^4] CSC oversight applies to BARMM through BAA 17.[^5]

**CSW (Complete Staff Work)** -- The doctrine that staff work should be so complete that the decision-maker only needs to approve or disapprove. In BARMM, CSW follows the **ADDRESS IT** methodology.

## D

**DAB (Development Academy of the Bangsamoro)** -- The government training institution for BARMM employees. Provides short courses, executive development programs, and specialized training.

**Detailing** -- Temporary assignment of a government employee to another office for 30-90 days (extendable). The employee remains on the payroll of the original office.

**DTR (Daily Time Record)** -- The form used to track daily attendance in government offices. Being replaced by biometric systems in many offices.

## E

**Eid al-Adha** -- The Festival of Sacrifice. One of the two major Islamic holidays observed as a national holiday in the Philippines and as an official holiday in BARMM.

**Eid al-Fitr** -- The Festival of Breaking the Fast, celebrated at the end of Ramadan. An official holiday in BARMM.

## F

**FOI (Freedom of Information)** -- The policy requiring government agencies to disclose public documents upon request, subject to exceptions for national security and personal privacy.

**Force Leave** -- The mandatory use of 5 days of vacation leave per year. Government employees must take these days off; they cannot be accumulated or monetized.

## G

**GAD (Gender and Development)** -- A government requirement that each office allocates budget and programs for gender-responsive initiatives. Guided by the GAD Planning and Budgeting Guidelines.

**GIDA (Geographically Isolated and Disadvantaged Area)** -- Remote, hard-to-reach areas with limited access to basic services. Includes island municipalities in Tawi-Tawi and Basilan, interior barangays in Lanao del Sur, and conflict-affected communities.

**GSIS (Government Service Insurance System)** -- The social insurance institution for government employees. Provides retirement pension, life insurance, disability benefits, and salary loan programs. Contributions are mandatory and deducted from salary.

## H

**HRMPSB (Human Resource Merit Promotion and Selection Board)** -- The board in each government office responsible for evaluating applicants and recommending selections for appointment or promotion. Uses the **Comparative Assessment** criteria.

## I

**IPCR (Individual Performance Commitment and Review)** -- Your individual performance contract under the **SPMS**. Sets specific targets at the beginning of the rating period and evaluates accomplishments at the end. Your IPCR rating determines PBB eligibility, promotion ranking, and training priority.

## J

**JO (Job Order)** -- A task-based employment arrangement in government. Hourly or daily rate. No benefits (no GSIS, PhilHealth, leave, PBB). No security of tenure. Used for short-term administrative, IT, and event support tasks.

## K

**Kalibugan** -- One of the 13 Bangsamoro ethnolinguistic groups. Located primarily in the Zamboanga Peninsula. Characterized by mixed ancestry and distinct cultural identity.

## L

**LGU (Local Government Unit)** -- Provincial, municipal, and barangay governments. In BARMM, LGUs are governed by **BAA 49** (Bangsamoro Local Governance Code).[^6]

**LSO (Legislative Staff Officer)** -- A staff position in the Parliament assigned to support committee work: research, policy analysis, legislative drafting, and briefer preparation.

## M

**MAFAR (Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for agricultural development, fisheries, and land reform.

**MBHTE (Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for education at all levels. Governed by **BAA 18** (Education Code).

**MENRE (Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for environmental protection, natural resource management, and energy development.

**MFBM (Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for fiscal management, budget preparation and execution, and financial management. Governed by **BAA 84** (Budget System Act).

**MILG (Ministry of the Interior and Local Government)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for LGU coordination, local governance, and disaster risk reduction.

**MIPA (Ministry of Indigenous Peoples' Affairs)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for indigenous peoples' rights, culture preservation, and ancestral domain management. Established by **BAA 64**.

**MOH (Ministry of Health)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for health service delivery.

**MOLE (Ministry of Labor and Employment)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for labor relations, employment, and labor standards. Governed by **BAA 82** (Labor Code).

**MOP (Manual of Procedures)** -- A document that describes the step-by-step processes for each function of a government office. Not all offices have one; new staff may be tasked to create one.

**MPOS (Ministry of Public Order and Safety)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for law enforcement and public safety.

**MPW (Ministry of Public Works)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for infrastructure development.

**MSSD (Ministry of Social Services and Development)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for social welfare and social protection programs.

**MOST (Ministry of Science and Technology)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for science, technology, and innovation.

**MOTC (Ministry of Transportation and Communications)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for transportation networks and communications infrastructure.

**MTIT (Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism)** -- The BARMM ministry responsible for trade promotion, investment facilitation, and tourism development.

## N

**Next-in-Rank** -- The principle that employees one level below a vacant position receive priority consideration for promotion. A preference, not a guarantee. The **HRMPSB** evaluates all qualified applicants.

## O

**OBC (Other Bangsamoro Communities)** -- Bangsamoro communities living outside the BARMM territory, particularly in other parts of Mindanao and Metro Manila. Protected under the **BOL** and served by the OOBC.

**ORAOHRA (Omnibus Rules on Appointments and Other Human Resource Actions)** -- The CSC rules governing the appointment, promotion, transfer, detailing, secondment, and reassignment of government employees. Specifies the **Comparative Assessment** criteria, **HRMPSB** process, next-in-rank principle, and vacancy posting requirements. The primary CSC regulatory framework for HR actions in government.

**OOBC (Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities)** -- The office responsible for serving Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM territory.

**OPCR (Office Performance Commitment and Review)** -- The office-level performance contract under the **SPMS**. Your office commits to specific outputs and outcomes. OPCR achievement determines whether the office qualifies for **PBB**.

## P

**Pag-IBIG (Home Development Mutual Fund)** -- The government fund that provides housing loans, savings programs, and calamity loans. Contributions are mandatory for all government employees.

**PAO (Political Affairs Officer)** -- A staff position in Parliament assigned to an MP's office, handling constituency concerns, stakeholder engagement, and representation.

**PBB (Performance-Based Bonus)** -- An annual cash bonus for government employees. Requires both the office (**OPCR**) and the individual (**IPCR**) to meet performance targets. Typical amount: PHP 10,000-35,000 depending on performance tier.

**PDS (Personal Data Sheet / CS Form 212)** -- The government's standard CV format. Required for all applications, promotions, and transfers. Must be kept updated throughout your career.

**PERA (Personnel Economic Relief Allowance)** -- A monthly allowance of PHP 2,000 paid to all government employees.

**PhilGEPS (Philippine Government Electronic Procurement System)** -- The online platform where government procurement opportunities are posted. Required for all procurement above certain thresholds.

**PhilHealth** -- The national health insurance corporation. Government employees are automatically enrolled. Monthly premiums are deducted from salary.

**PIMAO (Public Information and Media Affairs Officer)** -- A staff position in Parliament assigned to an MP's office, handling strategic communications, social media, media relations, and documentation.

**Plantilla** -- The list of authorized positions in a government office, including the position title, salary grade, and whether the position is filled or vacant. A "plantilla position" is a permanent position with security of tenure.

**PPR (Performance-Potential-Readiness)** -- A 3-step assessment framework used by supervisors to evaluate staff. Performance = current role effectiveness. Potential = capacity for a larger role. Readiness = skills for a specific new assignment.

**PRAISE (Program on Awards and Incentives for Service Excellence)** -- The government recognition framework. Each office has a PRAISE committee that identifies and recognizes outstanding performance.

## Q

**QS (Qualification Standards)** -- The minimum requirements for a government position: Education, Experience, Training, and Eligibility. Set by the CSC and specified in each vacancy posting.

## R

**RA 3019** -- The Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act.[^7] Defines corrupt acts by public officials and prescribes penalties including imprisonment and perpetual disqualification from public office.

**RA 6713** -- The Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees.[^8] Establishes the **8 norms of conduct** and requirements including **SALN** filing.

**RA 6770** -- The Ombudsman Act.[^9] Establishes the Office of the Ombudsman and its powers to investigate and prosecute government officials for graft, corruption, and misconduct.

**RA 6981** -- The Witness Protection, Security, and Benefit Act.[^10] Provides protection for witnesses, including government employees who report corruption.

**RA 9184** -- The Government Procurement Reform Act.[^11] Governs all government procurement, including bidding thresholds, procedures, and the **BAC**.

**RA 10524** -- The Expanded Positions for Persons with Disability Act.[^12] Requires government agencies to reserve at least **1% of positions** for persons with disabilities.

**RA 11054** -- See **BOL**.

**RA 11210** -- The Expanded Maternity Leave Act.[^13] Provides **105 days** of paid maternity leave for all female workers in government and private sector, with an additional 15 days for solo mothers.

**Rahmah** -- Arabic term meaning compassion or mercy. One of the five core Islamic leadership principles. In governance, it means applying rules with a human heart and prioritizing the relief of suffering.

**RRACCS (Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service)** -- The CSC rules governing disciplinary proceedings for government employees. Specifies grounds for disciplinary action, due process requirements, and penalties.

## S

**SALN (Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth)** -- A constitutional requirement[^14] for all government officials and employees to annually declare their assets, liabilities, net worth, business interests, financial connections, and relatives in government. Deadline: **April 30** of each year. Failure to file is grounds for dismissal.

**SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)** -- A feedback model used by supervisors. Describes the Situation (context), the Behavior (what was observed), and the Impact (the effect of the behavior).

**Secondment** -- Extended assignment of a government employee to another government entity for 6-12 months. Used for inter-agency projects and professional development.

**SGA (Special Geographic Area)** -- The 63 barangays in North Cotabato that voted to join BARMM in the 2019 plebiscite, organized into 8 municipalities (established through BAAs 41-48).

**Shura** -- Arabic term meaning consultation. One of the five core Islamic leadership principles. In governance, it means collective deliberation and decision-making, exemplified by the Parliament itself.

**Sidq** -- Arabic term meaning truthfulness. One of the five core Islamic leadership principles. In governance, it requires accurate reporting, honest communication, and refusal to hide problems.

**SPMS (Strategic Performance Management System)** -- The CSC's official performance management framework. Includes **IPCR** (individual) and **OPCR** (office) performance contracts. Uses a **5-point rating scale**: Poor (1), Unsatisfactory (2), Satisfactory (3), Very Satisfactory (4), Outstanding (5).

**SSL (Salary Standardization Law)** -- The national law that sets salary grades and compensation for all government positions.[^15] Updated periodically through "tranches" that adjust salary levels.

**STAR Method** -- The structured format for answering **CBI** questions: **S**ituation (context), **T**ask (your responsibility), **A**ction (what you did), **R**esult (the outcome). Essential for government interviews.

**Step Increment** -- An automatic salary increase within your current **SG** every **3 years** of continuous satisfactory service. Each SG has 8 steps.

## T

**Tausug (Bahasa Sug)** -- The language of the Tausug people. Also known as Bahasa Sug. The Tausug are one of the 13 Bangsamoro ethnolinguistic groups, historically centered in Sulu.

**Transfer** -- Permanent movement of a government employee from one office to another. Requires a vacancy, meeting the **QS**, and clearance from the releasing office.

**Transmittal Memorandum** -- A cover memo that accompanies a **CSW** output (briefer, policy recommendation, project proposal) as it moves from the staff member's desk to the decision-maker. Distinct from a regular memorandum.

## V

**VL (Vacation Leave)** -- 15 days per year for permanent government employees, earned monthly at 1.25 days. At least 5 days must be used as **force leave**.

## W

**Wali** -- The ceremonial head of the Bangsamoro government. Currently Sheikh Muslim M. Guiamaden.

---

*End of Glossary*
