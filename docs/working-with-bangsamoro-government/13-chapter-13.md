# Chapter 13: Government Communication: Writing That Gets Read

In government, writing IS working. If you cannot write a proper memo, you cannot do the job.

Your supervisor will judge your competence primarily through your written output. A staff member who writes well is trusted with bigger assignments. A staff member who writes poorly will spend their career doing data entry. This chapter teaches you the formats, conventions, and habits that make government writing effective.

---

## 13.1 The Memorandum: The Workhorse of Government

The memorandum (memo) is the most common document you will produce.[^1] Every request, report, recommendation, and notification moves through memos. You may write dozens in a single week. Learn this format first.

### Standard Memo Structure

```
REPUBLIC OF THE PHILIPPINES
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO
[MINISTRY/OFFICE NAME]
[Address]

MEMORANDUM

TO      : [Full name and title of recipient]
FROM    : [Full name and title of sender]
DATE    : [Date of memo]
SUBJECT : [Clear, specific subject]

---

1. [Purpose paragraph — why you are writing. State the action needed upfront.]

2. [Background paragraph — context the reader needs.]

3. [Details paragraph — specifics, data, findings, attachments referenced.]

4. [Action paragraph — what you need the reader to do, by when.]

[Signature block]
[Name]
[Position]
```

### Annotated Example

Here is a real-world example with annotations:

```
REPUBLIC OF THE PHILIPPINES
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO
MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT
Cotabato City

MEMORANDUM

TO      : HON. RAISSA H. JAJURIE
           Minister, MSSD
FROM    : AHMAD M. SANTOS
           Administrative Officer III, Social Protection Division
DATE    : 26 March 2026
SUBJECT : Request for Approval of BANGON Beneficiary List for
           Municipality of Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur

---

1. This is to request your approval of the attached BANGON beneficiary
   list for the Municipality of Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur, covering
   45 qualified beneficiaries for FY 2026 Batch 1.

2. Per MSSD Memorandum Order No. 2026-003, all BANGON beneficiary lists
   must be validated by the Municipal Social Welfare Office, endorsed
   by the Municipal Mayor, and approved by the Minister before
   disbursement.

3. The attached list has been validated against the following criteria:
   (a) residency in conflict-affected barangay, (b) income below
   poverty threshold, (c) no duplication with other livelihood programs.
   Supporting documents are attached as Annexes A-C.

4. In view of the above, approval of the attached beneficiary list is
   respectfully requested to enable disbursement before the end of
   Q1 FY 2026.

[Signature]
AHMAD M. SANTOS
Administrative Officer III
Social Protection Division
```

### Key Rules for Fresh Graduates[^14]

**The SUBJECT line is the most important line.** Your supervisor may receive 20 or more memos per day. A vague subject line ("Request") gets buried. A specific one ("Request for Approval of BANGON Beneficiary List for Municipality of Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur") gets read and acted on.

**One memo = one subject.**[^6] Do not combine unrelated requests in a single memo. If you need to request budget approval AND report on a field visit, write two separate memos.

**Number your paragraphs.**[^7] Government readers scan — they do not read every word. Numbered paragraphs allow them to reference specific points: *"See paragraph 3 of your memo dated 26 March 2026."* This also helps during discussions when your supervisor says, "I have a question about your third point."

**State the action requested in BOTH the first paragraph AND the last paragraph.** Decision-makers often read only the beginning and end. Your purpose should be clear whether they read the full memo or skim it.

**Attach supporting documents** rather than embedding lengthy data in the body. A memo with 10 pages of tables in the body is hard to read. A memo with 4 short paragraphs and 10 pages of tables in an annex is easy to read.

## 13.2 The Transmittal Memorandum

A transmittal memo accompanies a CSW output[^2] — a briefer, policy recommendation, project proposal, or report — as it moves from your desk to the decision-maker. It is NOT the CSW itself. It is the **cover page** that says: *"Here is the work. Here is what I need you to do with it."*

### Transmittal Memo Structure

```
MEMORANDUM

TO      : [Decision-maker]
FROM    : [You]
DATE    : [Date]
SUBJECT : Transmittal of [Name of Document] re: [Topic]

---

1. Transmitted herewith is the [Legislative Briefer / Policy Recommendation /
   Project Proposal / Budget Analysis] on [topic], prepared pursuant to
   [assignment source — e.g., "your directive during the March 15, 2026
   staff meeting" or "Committee Resolution No. 2026-001"].

2. [1-2 sentence summary of the key finding or recommendation.]

3. [List of attachments and how they are organized.]

4. For your [approval / information / appropriate action].

[Signature block]
```

### Annotated Example

```
MEMORANDUM

TO      : HON. MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT [Name]
           Chair, Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform
FROM    : [Your name]
           Legislative Staff Officer II
DATE    : 26 March 2026
SUBJECT : Transmittal of Legislative Briefer re: Proposed Bangsamoro
           Fisheries Code (BTA Bill No. 2026-0045)

---

1. Transmitted herewith is the Legislative Briefer on BTA Bill No. 2026-0045,
   "An Act Establishing the Bangsamoro Fisheries Code," prepared pursuant to
   your directive during the March 20, 2026 committee staff meeting.

2. The briefer recommends approval of the proposed bill with three amendments
   to align Section 12 with BOL Article V, Section 2, Item 14 (fisheries
   competence) and to harmonize penalty provisions with the national
   Fisheries Code (RA 8550 as amended by RA 10654).

3. The following documents are attached:
   (a) Legislative Briefer (10 pages)
   (b) Bill Comparison Matrix — Proposed vs. National Fisheries Code
   (c) Stakeholder Position Summary

4. For your review and appropriate action in preparation for the committee
   hearing scheduled on April 3, 2026.

[Signature]
[Name]
Legislative Staff Officer II
```

### When to Use a Transmittal Memo vs. a Regular Memo[^15]

| Use a regular memo when... | Use a transmittal memo when... |
|---|---|
| Your memo IS the document (a request, report, or notification) | Your memo ACCOMPANIES a more substantive document |
| The body of the memo contains all the information the reader needs | The attachments are more important than the memo itself |
| No attachments, or attachments are supporting documents | The main deliverable is the attachment |

**Rule of thumb:** If your memo has attachments that are more important than the memo itself, it is a transmittal.

## 13.3 The Endorsement Chain

Endorsements are how documents move between offices.[^3] When Office A sends a document to Office B for action, and Office B forwards it to Office C, each forwarding is an **endorsement**. Understanding endorsements is essential because you will both **write** them and **receive** them.

### How It Works

```
ORIGINAL MEMO: Municipality of Paglat --> MILG
(Requesting technical assistance for CDP update)

1st ENDORSEMENT: MILG --> BPDA
"Respectfully endorsed to the Bangsamoro Planning and Development
Authority for appropriate action. The Municipality of Paglat requests
technical assistance in preparing its Comprehensive Development Plan
update."
[Signed by MILG Director]

2nd ENDORSEMENT: BPDA Executive Director --> Planning Division
"Referred to the Planning Division for evaluation and recommendation."
[Signed by BPDA Executive Director]

3rd ENDORSEMENT: Planning Division --> BPDA Executive Director
"Respectfully returned with the recommendation that BPDA deploy a
planning team to Paglat municipality for a 5-day CDP workshop, with
an estimated cost of PHP 85,000 chargeable to the FY 2026 Technical
Assistance Fund."
[Signed by Division Chief]
```

Each endorsement adds a layer to the document's history.[^16] By the time you receive a document with three endorsements, you can trace its entire journey — who originated it, who reviewed it, what they recommended, and what is expected of you.

### Key Endorsement Phrases and What They Mean

These phrases are standard across Philippine government.[^4] Each one signals a specific action expectation:

| Phrase | What it means | Who uses it |
|---|---|---|
| **"Respectfully endorsed for appropriate action"** | "Please handle this — you have authority to decide" | Superior forwarding to subordinate office |
| **"Respectfully endorsed for information"** | "FYI only — no action needed from you" | Lateral or upward endorsement |
| **"Respectfully endorsed for approval"** | "I have reviewed this and recommend approval — please sign" | Subordinate forwarding to superior |
| **"Respectfully endorsed for comment"** | "Please review and give your input" | Any direction |
| **"Respectfully returned with recommendation"** | "I have done my review — here are my findings" | Subordinate returning to superior |

**Fresh graduate tip:** When you receive a document with endorsements, **read all the endorsements**. They tell you the document's journey — who sent it, who reviewed it, what they said, and what is expected of you. Many new staff skip the endorsements and miss critical context.

## 13.4 Email in Government

Government email is formal correspondence — not casual communication.[^17] Every email you send is potentially a **public record**. It can be subject to Freedom of Information (FOI) requests.[^5] It can be subpoenaed in administrative or legal proceedings. Write every email as if it will be read by someone other than the recipient.

### Email Template 1: Request Email

```
Subject: Request for FY 2026 BANGON Disbursement Data -
         Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur

Dear [Name / Sir / Ma'am],

Assalamu Alaikum.

I am writing to request the FY 2026 disbursement data for the BANGON
program in the Municipality of Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur. This data
is needed for the mid-year OPCR review of the Social Protection
Division, due on July 15, 2026.

Specifically, we need:
1. Total amount disbursed (January - June 2026)
2. Number of beneficiaries served per barangay
3. Liquidation status per tranche

May we request this data by [date — give at least 5 working days]?

Thank you and Wassalam.

[Your name]
[Your position]
[Your office]
[Contact number]
```

### Email Template 2: Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: Follow-Up: Request for FY 2026 BANGON Disbursement Data
         (sent [original date])

Dear [Name],

Assalamu Alaikum.

I am following up on my email dated [date] requesting the FY 2026
BANGON disbursement data for Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur. We have
not yet received the requested information.

As the mid-year OPCR review deadline is July 15, 2026, may we kindly
request the data by [new deadline — give 3 working days]?

Please let me know if there are any issues with the request. I am
happy to coordinate with your staff directly if that would help.

Thank you and Wassalam.

[Signature block]
```

### Email Template 3: Routing/Transmittal Email

```
Subject: FOR APPROPRIATE ACTION: Referral of Constituent Complaint -
         Water System, Brgy. Kalanganan, Marawi City

Dear [Recipient],

Assalamu Alaikum.

Please find attached the constituent complaint filed by [name],
Brgy. Captain of Kalanganan, Marawi City, regarding the non-functional
Level II water system in Sitio [name].

This was referred to our office by [source] on [date]. Per our office
mandate, this falls under your division's responsibility.

Recommended action: Site inspection and status assessment within
15 working days.

Please advise on next steps.

Thank you and Wassalam.

[Signature block]
```

### Email Rules in Government

**CC (Carbon Copy).**[^18] Copy those who need to know but do not need to act. **Always CC your immediate supervisor** on substantive emails. This prevents surprises and keeps your supervisor informed.

**BCC (Blind Carbon Copy).** Use sparingly. Appropriate only when sending to a large distribution list where recipients do not need to see each other's addresses.

**Key response words and what they mean:**

| Word | Meaning | Action required |
|---|---|---|
| **"Noted"** | "I have read and acknowledged" | NO — it does NOT mean "approved" |
| **"Approved"** | "I agree — this is an official action" | YES — proceed with implementation |
| **"For compliance"** | "You MUST do what this email says" | YES — it is a directive |

**Response time.** Acknowledge within 24 hours, even if you cannot fully respond yet. A quick reply — *"Received. I will provide the requested data by [date]"* — is professional. Silence is not.

**Never use:**
- Informal language or text speak ("pls," "thx," "ASAP" is acceptable but borderline)
- All caps (reads as shouting)
- Unprofessional tone ("I already sent this three times...")

## 13.5 Meeting Minutes

Every government meeting should produce minutes.[^19] As a new staff member, you will likely be assigned to take minutes. This is considered junior work — and it is also the **best way to learn what is happening in your office**. Embrace it.

### Meeting Minutes Template

```
MINUTES OF THE MEETING

Meeting Title : [e.g., "Weekly Staff Meeting, Social Protection Division"]
Date and Time : [e.g., "26 March 2026, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM"]
Venue         : [e.g., "MSSD Conference Room B, Cotabato City"
                 or "Via Google Meet"]
Presiding Officer : [e.g., "Division Chief Ahmad M. Santos"]

ATTENDEES:
1. [Name] — [Position]
2. [Name] — [Position]
3. [Name] — [Position]

ABSENT:
1. [Name] — [Reason if known]

---

AGENDA AND PROCEEDINGS:

I. Call to Order
   [Time called to order, by whom]

II. Approval of Previous Minutes
    [Status: approved / approved with corrections — specify corrections]

III. Matters Arising from Previous Minutes
     [Follow-up items from last meeting and their current status]

IV. [Agenda Item 1 — use a specific title, not "Item 1"]
    [Summary of discussion — key points, not verbatim]
    [Decision reached or action item assigned]

V. [Agenda Item 2]
   [Same format]

VI. Other Matters
    [Any items raised not on the original agenda]

VII. Schedule of Next Meeting
     [Date, time, venue]

---

ACTION ITEMS:

| # | Action Item | Person Responsible | Deadline | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] | Pending |
| 2 | [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] | Pending |
| 3 | [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] | Pending |

Prepared by  : [Your name, position]
Noted by     : [Presiding officer, position]
Date prepared: [Date]
```

### Meeting Minutes Rules[^20]

**Minutes are a RECORD, not a transcript.** Capture decisions and action items, not every word spoken. If a 30-minute discussion ends with a decision to deploy a team to Basilan, write the decision — not the 30 minutes of debate.

**Distribute within 48 hours.** Minutes lose value quickly. If people forget what was discussed, the minutes arrived too late.

**The action items table is the most important part.** Many readers skip the proceedings and go straight to the action items. Every action item must have a specific responsible person and a specific deadline — not "the team" and not "soon."

**Get the presiding officer to note/approve before distributing.** This ensures accuracy and gives the minutes official status.

**Keep a running file.** Maintain a folder (physical or digital) of all meeting minutes in chronological order. You will need to reference previous minutes regularly — *"Per the March 12 staff meeting minutes, Item IV..."*

## 13.6 Official Letters

Letters are for **external correspondence**[^21] — communicating with other government agencies, LGUs, development partners, CSOs, or the public. They are more formal than memos and follow a different format.

### Standard Letter Format

```
[Date]

[Recipient's full name and title]
[Position]
[Office/Organization]
[Address]

Dear [Title and Last Name / Sir / Ma'am]:

Assalamu Alaikum.

[Body — same structure as memo: purpose, background, details, action]

[Complimentary close]

Respectfully,

[Signature]
[Name]
[Position]
[Office]
```

### Key Differences Between Memos and Letters

| Feature | Memo | Letter |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | Internal (within your office or MOA) | External (other agencies, LGUs, partners, public) |
| **Header** | TO / FROM / DATE / SUBJECT | Full address block with recipient details |
| **Salutation** | None (the TO line serves this purpose) | "Dear [Title and Name]:" |
| **Tone** | Direct and functional | Formal and courteous |
| **Closing** | Signature block only | "Respectfully," or "Very truly yours," + signature |
| **When to use** | Routine internal communication | When communicating outside your immediate organization |

## 13.7 Action Phrases: The Five Phrases That Control What Happens

These five phrases determine what the recipient does with your document. Using the wrong one creates confusion, delays, or inaction. Master them.

| Action Phrase | What it means | When to use it | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **"For information"**[^8] | Read and note — no response or action needed | Sharing data, reports, or updates the recipient should know about but does not need to act on | "The attached FY 2025 Year-End Report is forwarded for your information." |
| **"For appropriate action"** | The recipient should decide what to do and act — you are NOT prescribing the action | Routing a complaint, referral, or request to the office responsible for handling it | "The attached constituent complaint is endorsed for your appropriate action." |
| **"For approval"** | You have done the work, you are recommending an action, and the decision-maker needs to sign off | Submitting a completed CSW (briefer, proposal, budget, beneficiary list) to a superior | "The attached project proposal is submitted for your approval." |
| **"For comment"** | You want the recipient's input before finalizing | Circulating a draft document for review | "The attached draft Administrative Order is forwarded for your comment." |
| **"For compliance"** | This is a directive — the recipient MUST do what is stated | Issuing instructions, citing regulations, or following up on unmet requirements | "Per CSC MC No. 14, s. 2018,[^9] compliance with posting requirements is directed." |

### Additional Routing Phrases[^22]

| Phrase | What it means |
|---|---|
| **"Respectfully submitted"** | This is my original work — I created this document |
| **"Respectfully transmitted"** | I am forwarding someone else's document |
| **"Respectfully endorsed"** | I am routing this to you with my recommendation |
| **"Respectfully returned"** | I am sending this back to you with my findings |

**Common mistake:**[^10] Using "submitted" and "transmitted" interchangeably. They mean different things. Use "submitted" for work you produced. Use "transmitted" for work someone else produced that you are forwarding.

## 13.8 Government Writing Voice: Rules for Fresh Graduates

Government writing is NOT academic writing.[^11] The transition from university to government requires you to **unlearn** several habits.[^23]

### Academic vs. Government Writing

| Academic writing | Government writing |
|---|---|
| Long sentences, complex syntax | Short sentences, simple structure |
| Passive voice ("It was determined that...") | Active voice ("The committee determined...") |
| Hedging ("It appears that there may be...") | Direct ("There is a shortage of...") |
| Citations in footnotes | Citations inline or in attachments |
| Builds to a conclusion at the end | States the conclusion FIRST, then supports it |
| Demonstrates the writer's knowledge | Enables the reader's decision |

### The Golden Rule of Government Writing

**Lead with the action.** Your reader is busy. They receive dozens of documents daily. Tell them what you need in the first paragraph — not the fourth.

| Before (academic habit) | After (government standard) |
|---|---|
| "In light of the recent developments in the implementation of the BANGON program, and considering the various challenges encountered by the implementing municipalities, particularly in terms of fund utilization and beneficiary validation, this memorandum seeks to..." | "This memorandum requests approval of the attached BANGON beneficiary list for Paglat, Maguindanao del Sur (45 beneficiaries, FY 2026 Batch 1)." |

The "before" version takes 40 words to say nothing. The "after" version takes 25 words to say everything the reader needs.

### Common Government Writing Mistakes

| Mistake | Example | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| **Using "the undersigned"** | "The undersigned respectfully requests..." | "I respectfully request..." (the CSC has explicitly discouraged "the undersigned")[^12] |
| **Confusing "submitted" and "transmitted"** | Using them interchangeably | "Submitted" = your original work. "Transmitted" = forwarding someone else's document. |
| **Using "thru"** | "Thru the Secretary General" | "Through the Secretary General" ("thru" is informal) |
| **Unnecessary preambles** | "In connection with the above-captioned subject matter and pursuant to..." | State the purpose directly in the first sentence |
| **Acronyms without definition** | "The HRMPSB reviewed the QS per CSC MC 14"[^13] | Spell out on first use: "The Human Resource Merit Promotion and Selection Board (HRMPSB)..." |
| **Run-on paragraphs** | One paragraph covering 3 different topics | One paragraph per point. Number them. |
| **Passive voice** | "It is recommended that the proposal be approved" | "I recommend approving the proposal" |
| **Burying the conclusion** | Main recommendation appears in paragraph 6 | Move the recommendation to paragraph 1 |

### Voice Principles for BARMM Writing[^24]

**Be direct.** Government readers want answers, not suspense.

**Be specific.** "Several municipalities" is vague. "Three municipalities in Basilan (Lamitan City, Tipo-Tipo, and Ungkaya Pukan)" is useful.

**Be concise.** If you can say it in 15 words, do not use 40.

**Be structured.** Number your paragraphs. Use headings in long documents. Include tables for comparative data. Bold key terms.

**Be courteous but not flowery.** "Respectfully" is appropriate. "I humbly beg your most esteemed consideration of this insignificant submission" is not.

## 13.9 Government Writing Quick Reference Card

Keep this at your desk until these conventions become habit.[^25]

### Side 1: Document Types Quick Reference

| Document | Use when | Key element |
|---|---|---|
| **Memo** | Internal communication | Subject line + action requested |
| **Transmittal** | Forwarding a CSW output | "Transmitted herewith..." + attachment list |
| **Endorsement** | Routing between offices | "Respectfully endorsed..." + action phrase |
| **Letter** | External correspondence | Formal salutation and complimentary close |
| **Minutes** | Recording meeting proceedings | Action items table with persons and deadlines |
| **Email** | Fast internal/external communication | Specific subject line + clear ask + professional tone |

### Side 2: Action Phrases Cheat Sheet

| Write this | When you mean |
|---|---|
| "For your approval" | "Please sign this — I have done the work" |
| "For your information" | "FYI — no action needed" |
| "For appropriate action" | "Please handle this — it is your responsibility" |
| "For your comment" | "Please review and give feedback" |
| "For compliance" | "You must do this — it is a requirement" |
| "Respectfully submitted" | "This is my original work" |
| "Respectfully transmitted" | "I am forwarding someone else's work" |
| "Respectfully endorsed" | "I am routing this to you with my recommendation" |
| "Respectfully returned" | "I am sending this back with my findings" |

## Key Takeaways

- **The memo is your workhorse.** Learn the format cold: TO, FROM, DATE, SUBJECT, numbered paragraphs, action requested in paragraphs 1 and 4.
- **The transmittal memo is your CSW's cover page.** It accompanies your substantive work and tells the decision-maker what to do with it.
- **Endorsements trace a document's journey.** Read all endorsements to understand context. Write endorsements with the correct action phrase.
- **Government email is formal correspondence.** CC your supervisor. Acknowledge within 24 hours. Write as if it will be FOI'd.
- **Meeting minutes are your learning tool.** Capture decisions and action items, not verbatim transcripts. Distribute within 48 hours.
- **Five action phrases control everything.** "For information," "for appropriate action," "for approval," "for comment," and "for compliance" each trigger different responses. Use the right one.
- **Government writing leads with the action.** State what you need in paragraph 1. Support it in paragraphs 2-3. Restate it in your closing paragraph.
- **Unlearn academic habits.** Short sentences. Active voice. Specific numbers. No hedging. No preambles. The reader needs a decision, not a literature review.

Your writing is your professional identity.[^26] In a government where most work is documented in writing, the quality of your memos, briefers, and reports is **the quality of your work**. Write well, and your career advances. Write poorly, and it stalls. Start practicing now.
