**AN ACT DECLARING SITIO MEKIR IN BARANGAY LAMUD, SOUTH UPI, MAGUINDANAO DEL SUR AS A PEACE SANCTUARY AND SAFE EVACUATION CENTER FOR NON-MORO INDIGENOUS PEOPLES DURING ARMED HOSTILITIES, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.**

This Act shall be known as the "Sitio Mekir Peace Sanctuary and Safe Evacuation Center Act."

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.**

It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote and protect the rights, welfare, and security of the Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples (NMIPs), in accordance with the 1987 Philippine Constitution, Republic Act No. 8371 (Indigenous Peoples’ Rights Act of 1997), and Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law). The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the ancestral domain of the Teduray and Lambangian tribes and affirms their right to peace, safety, and self-governance, particularly during armed conflicts and emergencies.

**SECTION 3. Objectives.**

This Act aims to:

a. Declare Sitio Mekir in Barangay Lamud, South Upi, Maguindanao del Sur, as a Peace Sanctuary and Safe Evacuation Center for Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples;

b. Ensure protection, humanitarian assistance, and cultural preservation of Indigenous communities during armed hostilities;

c. Strengthen coordination among ministries, local government units, and traditional leadership structures in sustaining peace and security mechanisms; and

d. Institutionalize Indigenous conflict resolution and peacekeeping systems consistent with their customs, traditions, and worldviews.

**SECTION 4. Definition of Terms.**

For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall mean:

a. **Peace Sanctuary** — a designated sacred space recognized by the Indigenous Peoples as neutral and protected during armed conflict, where civilians, particularly women, children, and elders, can seek safety and spiritual restoration.

b. **Safe Evacuation Center** — an area equipped and recognized for temporary shelter, humanitarian aid, and community safety during conflict or disaster.

c. **Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples (NMIPs)** — Indigenous Cultural Communities within the BARMM as defined under Article IX, Section 12 of Republic Act No. 11054.

**SECTION 5. Establishment of the Peace Sanctuary.**

Sitio Mekir in Barangay Lamud, South Upi, Maguindanao del Sur, is hereby declared as a Peace Sanctuary and Safe Evacuation Center for Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples during armed hostilities.

The site shall be respected as a demilitarized and neutral area, free from any armed operations, and shall serve as a humanitarian protection zone for displaced Indigenous populations.

**SECTION 6. Implementing Agencies.**

The implementation of this Act shall be carried out by the **Office of the Chief Minister (OCM)** **Ministry of Indigenous Peoples’ Affairs (MIPA)**, in coordination with the **Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS)**, **Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC)**, **Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD)**, and concerned **local government units (LGUs)**.

They shall coordinate with national agencies, civil society organizations, and traditional Indigenous structures (Këmamal Këadatan) to ensure effective management and protection of the peace sanctuary.

**SECTION 7. Appropriations.**

The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against available funds of the concerned ministries. Thereafter, such amounts as may be necessary for the continued operation, development, and maintenance of the peace sanctuary shall be included in the annual budget of the concerned ministries under the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

**SECTION 8. Monitoring and Evaluation.**

The MIPA, in coordination with the MPOS and BHRC, shall establish a monitoring and evaluation mechanism to ensure compliance with the humanitarian and cultural protection standards set forth in this Act.

**SECTION 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).**

Within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act, the MIPA, in consultation with relevant stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for its effective implementation.

**SECTION 10. Separability Clause.**

If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 11. Repealing Clause.**

All laws, decrees, orders, or regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 12. Effectivity.**

This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Government Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
