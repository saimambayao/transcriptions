**AN ACT PROVIDING FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE MEASURES AND ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS TO OTHER BANGSAMORO COMMUNITIES AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “Social Justice Measures and Assistance Programs to Other Bangsamoro Communities Act.”

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Organic Law that the National Government shall ensure the protection of the rights of the Bangsamoro people residing in communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, otherwise known as “Other Bangsamoro Communities (OBCs),” and undertake programs for their rehabilitation and development. This Act recognizes the marginalization and injustices faced by Bangsamoro communities outside the region, and is committed to enhancing their social, economic, and cultural development, thus:

(a) The Bangsamoro Government shall promote the social development of OBCs through various programs and services, including, but not limited to, education and scholarship programs, healthcare and wellness programs, women and youth development and empowerment programs, peacebuilding and conflict resolution programs, disaster-resilient housing programs, access to justice programs, interfaith dialogue and harmony programs, and advocacy and policy reform programs;

(b) The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the need to address the economic development needs of OBCs to promote sustainable economic growth, generate employment, and reduce poverty. The Bangsamoro Government shall implement livelihood and entrepreneurship programs, financial inclusion programs, agricultural development programs, tourism development programs, infrastructure development programs, environmental protection and conservation programs, disaster preparedness and response, and other economic programs, projects, and services to address these needs; and

(c) The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the importance of preserving and promoting the unique cultural heritage of OBCs to strengthen their sense of identity and promote intercultural understanding. The Bangsamoro Government shall implement, among others, cultural preservation and promotion programs, support to Bangsamoro arts and craft in OBCs, and promotion of Bangsamoro history in OBCs.

**SECTION 3. Social Justice Measures and Assistance Programs to OBCs.** – In pursuit of the policy stated in Section 2 of this Act, the Bangsamoro Government shall develop and implement comprehensive social justice measures and assistance programs aimed at promoting the social, economic, and cultural development of OBCs. These measures shall address the specific needs and challenges faced by OBCs, with the ultimate goal of achieving social justice, equity, inclusion, peace, and sustainable development for these communities.


**SECTION 4. Social Development Measures and Programs.** – The following projects and programs shall be implemented to promote the social development in OBCs:

a. Education Assistance. – This includes scholarship opportunities for qualified and marginalized students, adult education programs, support for public or private schools and teachers, and other programs that seek to improve access to quality education;

b. Healthcare and Wellness Programs. – This includes mobile clinics, equipment and provision, health education and awareness campaigns, capacity building for healthcare workers to improve access to quality healthcare services and promote healthy lifestyles, health support for senior citizens, retired individuals, and those without pensions, and other related health programs;

c. Women and Youth Development and Empowerment Programs. – This includes skills training and mentorship programs, access to financing and business development support, representation and participation in political and governance platforms, welfare and development programs, promotion of gender equity, youth entrepreneurship, and innovation, participation in decision-making processes for their welfare and development, and other related empowerment programs;

d. Peacebuilding and Conflict Resolution Programs. – This includes interfaith dialogue and mediation, capacity building on conflict resolution and mediation, peacebuilding initiatives, and other related activities to promote peaceful coexistence and prevent and resolve conflicts;

e. Disaster-Resilient Housing Programs. – This includes support for poor and low-income families to build or upgrade their homes to withstand natural disasters and reduce the impact of disasters;

f. Access to Justice Programs. – This includes legal aid and support services, community legal education and awareness campaigns, and promotion of alternative dispute resolution mechanisms to improve access to justice for marginalized communities; and

g. Advocacy and Policy Reform Programs. – This includes research and advocacy on issues affecting Bangsamoro communities, policy dialogue and engagement with policymakers, and promotion of evidence-based policy reform to promote social justice, human rights, and sustainable development.

**SECTION 5. Economic Development Measures and Programs.** – The following projects and programs shall be implemented to promote the economic development in OBCs:

a. Livelihood and entrepreneurship programs. – This includes skills training, access to capital, and business development support to help Bangsamoro communities start and grow their own businesses and generate sustainable income;

b. Financial inclusion programs. – This includes support for microfinance institutions, financial education and literacy training, and access to financial services to promote financial inclusion and reduce poverty;

c. Agricultural development programs. – This includes support for farmers and fisherfolk, irrigation systems, and agricultural research and extension services to improve agricultural productivity and promote sustainable livelihoods;

d. Tourism development programs. – This includes promotion of cultural and eco-tourism, development of tourist sites and attractions, and capacity building for local tour guides and operators to promote economic growth and cultural exchange;

e. Infrastructure development programs. – This includes road and bridge construction, water and sanitation projects, and rural electrification to improve access to basic services and infrastructure in underserved areas;

f. Environmental protection and conservation programs. – This includes training on environmental management and conservation, promotion of sustainable practices, and support for community-based conservation initiatives to promote environmental sustainability and resilience; and

g. Disaster preparedness and response programs. – This includes disaster risk reduction and management training, emergency response planning and capacity building, and support for disaster-resilient infrastructure and housing to reduce the impact of disasters and promote resilience.

**SECTION 6. Cultural Development Measures and Programs.** – The following projects and programs shall be implemented to promote the cultural development in OBCs:

a. Cultural preservation and promotion programs. – This includes support for cultural activities, promotion of faith-based and tradition practices, preservation of cultural sites and artifacts, and training for cultural workers and advocates to promote and protect the cultural heritage;

b. Support to Bangsamoro arts and crafts. – This includes capacity building for artisans, promotion of local crafts and products, and support for cultural entrepreneurship to promote the economic viability of traditional arts and crafts; and

c. Promotion of Bangsamoro history. – This includes development of educational materials, support for local historical research, and promotion of historical sites and landmarks to enhance awareness and understanding of the history of the Bangsamoro people.


**SECTION 7. Implementation.** – The Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), through the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC), shall be the lead agency in the planning, coordination, and implementation of this Act. As the lead agency, it shall:

(a) Set the strategic direction and ensure the alignment of the measures and programs with the broader objectives of social justice, fairness, inclusion, equity, and sustainable development. It shall develop and implement mechanisms to ensure all measures and programs under this Act are free from discrimination and that they provide equal opportunities for all, regardless of their socio-economic and political backgrounds or affiliations;

(b) Have the responsibility to identify and mobilize necessary resources, both human and financial, for the execution of the measures and programs;

(c) Be responsible for monitoring and evaluation, conducting regular reviews to assess the effectiveness of the measures and programs and making necessary adjustments based on feedback and changing needs of the communities;

(d) Foster an environment of transparency, ensuring all actions taken and progress made are accessible to the OBCs, thus promoting accountability and community engagement;

(e) Provide necessary training and capacity-building initiatives to equip involved personnel and stakeholders with the skills needed to effectively manage and implement the measures and programs;

(f) Develop a policy and procedures to identify and manage any potential conflicts of interest that may arise in the implementation of the Act;

(g) Perform any and all other acts incident to or required by virtue of this Act.

In light of this, all pertinent ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro Government are hereby mandated to extend their full support, cooperation, and assistance to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) and the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC) in the execution of this Act. They shall also be required to submit their program proposals complete with corresponding implementation guidelines, in order to effectively operationalize the provisions and implement the measures and programs outlined in this Act.

**SECTION 8. Collaboration with Concerned National Government Agencies and Local Government Units.** – The Bangsamoro Government shall collaborate with concerned national government agencies and local government units (LGUs) where there are OBCs in the implementation of the social, economic, and cultural development programs and activities provided in this Act, which may include the provision of technical expertise, financial and logistical support, and coordination with other government agencies and local communities

**SECTION 9. Annual Report.** – The OOBC shall prepare and submit an annual report to the Chief Minister and the Parliament on its performance related to the implementation of this Act. In the event of non-compliance or inadequate reporting, necessary remedial actions shall be taken by the Chief Minister and/or the relevant committees of the Parliament.

**SECTION 10. Funding.** – The funding for the measures and programs outlined in this Act shall be sourced from the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government. Given that the national government has the primary mandate to ensure the protection of the rights of the OBCs and undertake programs for their rehabilitation and development in accordance with Section 12, Article VI of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the OOBC shall actively engage in coordinating with the relevant agencies of the national government as well as the LGUs where these communities are located to secure additional resources and assistance and further enhance the economic, social, and cultural development of these communities.

Furthermore, the Bangsamoro Government shall also explore other funding avenues and supplementary sources, which include, but are not limited to, foreign aid, public-private partnerships, grants, donations, and other forms of financial support to ensure the sustainable and comprehensive development of the OBCs.

**SECTION 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The OCM, through the OOBC and in consultation with the OBCs and relevant ministries, offices, agencies, and other stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations within ninety (90) days after the approval of this Act.

**SECTION 12. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 13. Separability Clause.** – If, for any reason, any part or provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 14. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Approved.
