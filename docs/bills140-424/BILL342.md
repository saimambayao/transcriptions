**AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING THE BANGSAMORO ECONOMIC ZONE AUTHORITY AS AN INVESTMENT PROMOTION AGENCY, PROVIDING FOR ITS MANDATE, POWERS, AND FUNCTIONS, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**ARTICLE I**
**INTRODUCTORY PROVISION**

**Section 1. Title.** This Act shall be known as the “**Bangsamoro Economic Zone Act of 2025**”.

**Section 2. Basis.** Article XIII, Republic Act. No. 11054, otherwise known as Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, provides that:

The Bangsamoro Government may establish economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, including the establishment of a Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority which shall have similar powers as those of the Philippine Economic Zone Authority;

The Parliament may provide such additional powers and functions to the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority as may be necessary to meet the special circumstances of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

Once the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority is created, the Philippine Economic Zone Authority shall no longer create any other economic zone within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Any corporation, firm, or entity established within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region by the Philippine Economic Zone Authority shall be placed under the jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority, and shall continue to enjoy the benefits granted to it by the Philippine Economic Zone Authority;

Through the intergovernmental relations mechanism, the Bangsamoro Government and the National Government shall cooperate on customs, immigration, and quarantine services including the attendant international commitments thereto, in order to implement and make fully operational such economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports within one (1) year from their establishment. Business and other enterprises operating within the Bangsamoro economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports are entitled to the fiscal incentives and other benefits provided by the National Government to special economic zones;

The Bangsamoro Government shall provide fiscal incentives and other benefits to investors in economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports: Provided, That, for goods consumed and services rendered outside the established economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, all relevant national taxes shall be imposed;

Bangsamoro free ports shall be contiguous or adjacent to a seaport or airport. The area of coverage of a free port may be so much as may be necessary of that portion of the constituent local government units in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, subject to such criteria as the Parliament may provide in a law for that purpose;

The administration of existing free ports in the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao are hereby transferred to Bangsamoro Government; and,

Section 16, paragraph “L” of Republic Act 12066, otherwise known as “Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises to Maximize Opportunities for Reinvigorating the Economy (CREATE MORE Act), which amended Section 293 paragraph “H” of RA 11534 (CREATE Act), declares the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA) as one of the Investment Promotion Agencies (IPAs) in the country.

**Section 3. Purpose.** This Act seeks to achieve the following:

a. To institutionalize the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority as an Investment Promotion Agency (IPA), strengthen its mechanisms and processes for the integration, coordination, planning, and monitoring of economic zones, industrial estates, and free ports;
b. To encourage investments by implementing transparent and streamlined administrative processes and providing attractive fiscal and non-fiscal incentives to both local and foreign investors operating within the economic zones, industrial estates and free ports;
c. To promote regional integration and connectivity by developing infrastructure projects within the economic zones, industrial estates and free ports; incorporating local content development, fostering trade and investment linkages with neighboring regions and countries through cross-border trade, exchange of knowledge and expertise, and joint investment ventures, within the framework of a halal ecosystem; and,
d. To promote and incorporate sustainable development practices by implementing clear and stringent environmental standards and regulations and ensuring sustainable resource management in all the economic zones, industrial estates and free ports.

**Section 4. Declaration of Policy and Principles.** It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro government to translate into practical realities the following policies and mandates in Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law) and the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 (Bangsamoro Administrative Code), namely:

“The Bangsamoro Government shall promote the effective use of economic resources and endeavor to attain economic development that facilitates growth and full employment, human development, and social justice” (Paragraph 2, Section 2, Article XIII of RA 11054).

“The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the private sector as a mover of trade, commerce, and industry and shall encourage and support the building up of entrepreneurial capability in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region” (Section 24, Article XIII of RA 11054).

“The Bangsamoro Government shall also promote domestic trade preference for goods produced and materials sourced from within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and adopt measures to increase their competitiveness. It shall also ensure that Bangsamoro products and services gain considerable access to the markets of its trading partners. Particular attention shall also be given to the markets of its trading partners who have historic and cultural ties to the Bangsamoro people” (Paragraph 4, Section 24, Article XIII of RA 11054).

The Bangsamoro Government through the Parliament “may grant tax exemptions and incentives under this Organic Law upon a vote of majority of all its members: Provided, That these tax exemptions and incentives shall not diminish national revenues...” (Section 8, Article XII of RA 11054).

In pursuance of these policies, the Bangsamoro Government shall “actively encourage, promote and accelerate a sound and balanced industrial, economic and social development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in order to provide jobs to people, increase productivity and income, and improve the quality of living condition through the establishment of special economic zones and free ports in suitable and strategic locations in the Bangsamoro and through measures that shall effectively attract legitimate and productive foreign investments” and “promote, develop and regulate halal industry development, including the export and provision of halal products, processes and services within the Bangsamoro Economic Zone” (Section 22, Chapter 6, and Section 3, Chapter 1, Title XIV, BAA No. 13).

**Section 5. Definition of Terms.** For purposes of this Act, the following terms are hereby defined as follow:

“BEZA” or the “Authority” shall refer to the BANGSAMORO ECONOMIC ZONE AUTHORITY (BEZA) created pursuant to Article XIII, Republic Act. No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law) and Section 22, Chapter VI, Title XIV of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 or the Bangsamoro Administrative Code and duly recognized by Section 16, paragraph “L” of Republic Act 12066 (CREATE MORE Act).

BEZ or “Bangsamoro Economic Zone” (BEZ) refers to a selected area within the Bangsamoro region, both vertical and horizontal, which shall be operated and managed as an autonomous customs territory that is highly developed or has the potential to be developed into industrial, agri-industrial, manufacturing, eco-tourism, recreational, information technology, commercial, banking, investment, and financial centers whose metes and bounds are fixed or delimited by the proclamation of the Chief Minister, which includes the existing Polloc Freeport and Ecozone (PFEZ). A BEZ may contain any or all of the following: industrial estates (IEs), agri-industrial zone (AIZs), export processing zone (EPZ), halal hub, manufacturing, technology parks, eco-tourism zone, ICT parks and centers, free trade zone, tourist/recreational center and free port.

“Industrial Estate (IE)” refers to a tract of land subdivided and developed according to a comprehensive plan under a unified continuous management and with provisions for basic infrastructure and utilities, with or without pre-built standard factory buildings and community facilities for the use of a community of industries.

“Agri-Industrial Zone (AIZ)” refers to the specified area devoted for agricultural purposes and its allied or support industries to integrate the production and processing of agricultural products. The term shall likewise refer to aqua or marine areas devoted to production and processing of aqua and marine products. Further, the phrase agricultural products shall include livestock and poultry products.

“Export Processing Zone (EPZ)” refers to a specialized industrial estate located physically and / or administratively outside the customs territory and predominantly oriented to export production. Enterprises located in export processing zones are allowed to import capital equipment and raw materials free from duties, taxes and other import restrictions, as provided for by RA 12066 and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.

“Manufacturing Zone” refers to a specifically designated area where domestic and foreign investors are primarily engaged in industrial and manufacturing activities such as electronics, automobiles, textiles, consumer goods, pharmaceuticals, and more. Businesses in this zone focus on producing goods for export market.

“Technology Parks” refers to specialized zones that focus on research, technology, and innovation-driven industries. it often provides infrastructure, support services, and a collaborative environment to foster technological development and attract high-tech businesses.

“ICT Parks and Centers” refers to a dedicated area, facility or zone that focuses on hosting and supporting information and communications technology (ICT) companies and activities. It serves as a hub for IT-related businesses, startups, and organizations, providing them with the necessary infrastructure, resources, and services to operate and grow their operations.

“Free Trade Zone” refers to an isolated and policed area adjacent to a port of entry (such as a seaport) and / or airport where imported goods may be unloaded for immediate transshipments or stored, repacked, sorted, mixed, or otherwise manipulated. However, movement of these imported goods from the free-trade area to a non-free trade area in the country shall be subject to customs and internal revenue rules and regulations.

“Tourist / Recreational Center” refers to an area within the BEZ where tourist accommodation facilities such as hotels, apartelles, tourist inns, pension houses, resorts, sports and / or recreational facilities are provided to render tourism services for both local and foreign tourists, travellers and investors in accordance with the guidelines to be issued by the BEZA.

“Eco-Tourism Zone” or “eco-tourism park” is a designated area that focuses on promoting sustainable tourism practices and preserving the natural and cultural heritage of the area. It is established in ecologically sensitive regions, such as forests, mountains, coastal areas or wildlife habitats, with the aim of conserving natural resources, generating revenue through sustainable tourism activities. Its establishment shall be in accordance with Republic Act No. 9593, otherwise known as " The Tourism Act of 2009."

“Halal Hub” is a type of economic zone, declared by a competent authority in the BARMM, where developers and locators are engaged in the halal industry. It serves as a central hub for the production, processing, certification and distribution of halal products both for export and domestic markets, as well as in the provision of related and component logistics and services.

“PFEZ” refers to Polloc Freeport and Ecozone which was established by the defunct ARMM government through Proclamation No. 1 series of 2010 and operates as an ecozone and free port in the region.

“Autonomous Customs Territory” refers to BEZ established by the Bangsamoro Government where customs control and procedures, which include inspection, documentation, valuation, and payment of duties or taxes, are implemented in the region but recognizing the autonomous character of BARMM, and subject to provisions of Republic Act 10863 or the Customs Modernization and Tariffs Act (CMTA) and the bilateral agreement between the National government and Bangsamoro government through the intergovernmental relations mechanism pursuant to Section 28, Article XIII of RA 11054.

“Vertical BEZ” refers to a type of BEZ such as, but not limited to, IT center, manufacturing, commercial, banking; investment and financial center, and the like, where its establishment and operations are housed or situated in a vertical structure such as, but not limited to, buildings, selected floors within buildings, and selected areas on a floor, or in a complex.

“Horizontal BEZ” refers to a type of BEZ such as, but not limited to, agri-industrial zone, industrial estates, eco-tourism zone and the like where its establishment and operations are usually expansive and utilizes hectarage of terrestrial lands and/or bodies of water.

“Registered Business Enterprise (RBE)” refers to any individual, partnership, corporation, Philippine branch of a foreign corporation, or other entity organized and existing under the Philippine or regional autonomous laws and registered with the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA) or the Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI-BARMM) excluding service enterprises such as those engaged in customs brokerage, trucking or forwarding services, janitorial services, security services, insurance, banking, and other financial services, consumers' cooperatives, credit unions, consultancy services, retail enterprises, restaurants, or such other similar services, as may be determined by the Bangsamoro Government or the Fiscal Incentives Review Board, and as specifically defined by Section 16 of Republic Act 12066 (CREATE MORE Act);

**ARTICLE II**
**ESTABLISHMENT AND NATURE OF BANGSAMORO ECONOMIC ZONES**

**Section 6. Establishment of BEZ** - To ensure the viability and geographic dispersal of BEZ through a system of prioritization, the following areas, especially those identified within the Bangsamoro Development Plan’s Spatial Strategy as Primary Growth Centers and Secondary Growth Centers¹, are hereby identified:

1. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the City of Cotabato;
2. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the Province of Maguindanao del Norte;
3. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the Province of Maguindanao del Sur;
4. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the Province of Tawi-Tawi;
5. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the City of Lamitan and other potential areas in the Province of Basilan;
6. So much as may be necessary of that portion of the City of Marawi and other potential areas in the Province of Lanao del Sur;
7. So much as may be necessary of that portion within the municipalities of Pahamuddin, Kadayangan, Nabalawag, Old Kaabakan, Kapalawan, Malidegao, Tugunan and Ligawasan in the Special Geographic Areas (SGA) of BARMM;
8. Any private industrial estate which shall voluntarily apply for conversion into a BEZ.

These areas shall be developed through any of the following schemes:

a.) Private sector initiative;
b.) Local government initiative with the assistance of BARMM government; and,
c.) BARMM government initiative.

The metes and bounds and the nature of each BEZ, both vertical and horizontal, are to be delineated and more particularly described in a proclamation to be issued by the Chief Minister of the BARMM, upon the recommendation of the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA), in coordination with the provincial, municipal and/or city councils and/or the Regional Land Use Committee of the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council (BEDC).

**Section 7. Criteria for the Establishment of Bangsamoro Economic Zones** - Areas within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region may be established as Bangsamoro Economic Zones (BEZ) in a proclamation to be issued by the Chief Minister, based on the technical evaluation and recommendation of the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA), and must conform to the following criteria:

(a) The proposed area must be a primary or secondary growth center as identified in the Bangsamoro Development Plan of the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council;
(b) Adequate infrastructure, including transportation networks, power supply, water source and communication facilities should be available or planned to support the economic zone; it must be accessible;
(c) The availability of skilled, semi-skilled, and non-skilled trainable labor force in and around the Bangsamoro Economic Zone;
(d) The potential profitability of the area must be established to ensure that it generates employment opportunities, attracts investments, and promotes local industries;
(e) The area must be strategically located to leverage regional advantages, such as proximity to markets, transportation hubs, natural resources, or existing industrial clusters;
(f) For horizontal BEZ, the extent of vacant lands available for industrial/agro-industrial and commercial development and future expansion of the BEZ as well as of lands adjacent to the BEZ available for development of residential areas for the BEZ workers;
(g) The zone must be situated where security measures can easily be established to provide stability, ensure the safety of investments, curtail smuggling activities and create confidence among potential investors and businesses operating within the said zone;

Other areas which do not meet the foregoing criteria may be established as BEZ: Provided, That the said area shall be developed only through local government and/or private sector initiative under any of the schemes allowed in Republic Act No. 6957 (the build-operate-transfer law) or otherwise, and without any financial exposure on the part of the BARMM Government; Provided further, That the area can be easily secured to curtail smuggling activities; Provided further, That the area shall be one contiguous area, as far as practicable; Provided, finally, that after five (5) years the area must have attained a substantial degree of development, the indicators of which shall be formulated by the BEZA.

**Section 8. Special Criteria for the Establishment of Bangsamoro Free Ports**-The Bangsamoro free ports shall be established and developed based on, but not limited to, the following criteria:

(a) It must be contiguous or adjacent to an existing seaport or airport;
(b) It must be situated in an area with easy access to major road networks and have regional and international connectivity;
(c) Adequate infrastructure and port facilities like storage areas, customs services, transport networks, and other necessary amenities, are available; and,
(d) The area of coverage of a free port may be so much as may be necessary of that portion of the constituent's local government units in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 9. Bangsamoro Economic Zone to be a Decentralized Agro-Industrial, Industrial, ICT, Commercial/Trading, Eco-Tourism, Investment, and Financial Community** – Within the framework of the Constitution, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, and the interest of national sovereignty and territorial integrity of the Republic, Bangsamoro Economic Zones shall be developed as much as possible, into a decentralized, self-reliant and self-sustaining industrial, commercial/trading, agro-industrial, information and communications technology, eco-tourism, banking, financial, and investment center with minimum government intervention. Each Bangsamoro Economic Zone shall be provided with transportation, telecommunications, and other facilities needed to generate linkage with industries and employment opportunities for its own inhabitants and those of nearby towns and cities.

The Bangsamoro Economic Zones may establish mutually beneficial economic relations with other entities within the country, or subject to the administrative guidance of the BEZA Board with foreign entities or enterprises.

Foreign citizens and companies owned by non-Filipinos in whatever proportion may set up enterprises in the Bangsamoro Economic Zones, in joint venture with Bangsamoro people in any sector of industry, international trade and commerce within the Bangsamoro Economic Zone. Their assets, profits, and other legitimate interests shall be protected: Provided, That the BEZ through the BEZA may require a minimum investment for any BEZ enterprise in freely convertible currencies, without prejudice to the requirements provided by existing national laws and regulations: Provided, further, That the new investment shall fall under the priorities, thrusts and limits provided for in this Act.

**Section 10. Bangsamoro Economic Zones Operation and Management as an Autonomous Customs Territory.** All Bangsamoro Economic Zones, including the Polloc Freeport and Ecozone (PFEZ), shall be managed and operated by the BEZA as an autonomous customs territory.

Through the intergovernmental relations mechanism between the National Government and Bangsamoro Government, BEZA shall be hereby vested with the authority to issue Certificate of Origin for products manufactured or processed in each Bangsamoro Economic Zone in accordance with the Operational Certification Procedure for the Rules of Origin of the ASEAN Free Trade Area (AFTA) and the general guidelines that may be issued by the Intergovernmental Relations Body (IGRB).

For this purpose, BEZA shall engage the cooperation of relevant national government agencies to make such economic zones, industrial estates and free ports fully functional in accordance with paragraph 3, Section 28, Article XIII of Republic Act 11054.

**Section 11. Protection and Security.** - The protection of the Bangsamoro Economic Zones and the security of its perimeter fences shall be the responsibility of the Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with BEZA. The BEZA may provide and establish the BEZ’s internal security and firefighting forces.

It may, however, coordinate with military, police and other law enforcement agencies on law enforcement activities within the BEZ, industrial estates or free ports, if necessary. Provided, however, that military, police and other security forces sent for the purpose of security shall not interfere in the internal affairs of any of the BEZ, and expenditure for these military forces shall be borne by the national government or BARMM government.

**Section 12. Immigration Services Within the Bangsamoro Economic Zones.** Immigration services within the BEZ, industrial estates, and free ports shall be subject to intergovernmental relations mechanism between the National Government and the Bangsamoro Government.

The Bangsamoro Government, through the BEZA, shall ensure that necessary requirements and protocols for the ingress and egress of foreign investors to and from the BEZ shall have authorization from the Bureau of Immigration.

For this purpose, BEZA shall engage the cooperation of the Bureau of Immigration for the issuance of working visas to foreign executives and their spouses and dependent children under 21 years of age, and other aliens possessing highly technical skills which no Filipino or Bangsamoro within the BEZ possesses, as certified by the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE-BARMM).

**ARTICLE III**
**GOVERNING & IMPLEMENTING STRUCTURES**

**Section 13. The Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority.** The Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority as provided in Section 28, Article XIII of R.A. No. 11054 and Section 23, Chapter VI, Title XIV of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13, is created as a body corporate attached to the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism. In line with Section 16 (L) of Republic Act 12066 (CREATE MORE Act), the Authority is an investment promotion agency (IPA).

The Authority shall have Bangsamoro Director General with the rank of Director IV, who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister, to serve for a term of six (6) years, unless sooner removed for cause, with provision for reappointment. In case of removal for cause, the replacement shall serve only the unexpired portion of the term. The Bangsamoro Director-General shall be of proven probity and integrity, and a degree holder in any of the following fields: economics, business, public administration, law, accounting, management, or their equivalent, and with at least five (5) years relevant supervisory work experience, preferably in the field of financial and legal management or public administration and with civil service eligibility or its equivalent (R.A.1080).

The Bangsamoro Director-General shall be assisted by four (4) Deputy Directors, with the rank of Director II, each for Policy and Planning; Finance and Administration; Research & Development, and Operations, who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister, upon the recommendation of the BEZA Board, to serve for a term of six (6) years, unless sooner removed for a cause, on a staggered basis; such that on the first year, only one deputy director shall be appointed, on the second year another deputy director shall be appointed and so on until the positions of deputy directors shall be filled up: The deputy directors shall be of proven probity and integrity, and a degree holder in any of the following fields: economics, business, public administration, law, accounting, management or their equivalent, and with a five (5) -year relevant working experience and civil service eligibility. The Directors shall be at least thirty-five (35) years old, with proven probity and integrity and with a degree in economics, business, public administration, law, management, with master’s degree or its equivalent, with at least four (4) years relevant supervisory working experience, and with second-level civil service eligibility or its equivalent (R.A. 1080).

**Section 14. General Powers and Functions of the Authority.** The BEZA shall have the following powers and functions as exercised through its agents and the Bangsamoro Director-General:

a.) To operate, administer, manage, and develop the BEZ, including the Polloc Freeport and Ecozone (PFEZ), according to the principles and provisions set forth in this Act;
b.) To have its own fiscal autonomy particularly in terms of revenue generation, expenditure decision, and financial accountability, subject to the limitations set forth under this Act and other existing rules and regulations;
c.) To register, regulate and supervise the business enterprises in the BEZ in an efficient and decentralized manner;
d.) To implement its own fiscal policies tailored to the specific needs and priorities of the economic zone so that it can respond quickly to changes in the business environment and attract investments effectively;
e.) To coordinate with local government units and exercise general supervision over the development, plans, activities, and operations of the BEZ, industrial estates, export processing zones, free trade zones, and the like;
f.) In coordination with the local government units concerned and appropriate agencies, to construct, acquire, own, lease, operate and maintain on its own or through contract, franchise, license, bulk purchase from the private sector and build-operate-transfer scheme or joint venture, adequate facilities and infrastructure, such as light and power systems, water supply and distribution systems, telecommunication and transportation, print and media facilities, buildings, structures, warehouses, roads, bridges, ports and other facilities for the operation and development of the BEZ, including shipping and related business, stevedoring and port terminal services or concessions, incidental thereto and airport operations in coordination with the Civil Aviation Authority of the Bangsamoro (CAAB), and fix just and reasonable rates, fares, charges and other prices therefore;
g.) To create, operate and/or contract to operate such agencies and functional units or offices of the authority as it may deem necessary;
h.) To adopt, alter, and use a corporate seal; make contracts, lease, own or otherwise dispose of personal or real property; sue and be sued; and otherwise carry out its duties and functions as provided for in this Act;
i.) To coordinate the formulation and preparation of the development plans of the different entities mentioned above;
j.) To coordinate with the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT) and the local government units and appropriate government agencies for policy and program formulation and implementation;
k.) To monitor and evaluate the development and requirements of entities in sub-Section (a) and recommend to the local government units or other appropriate authorities the location, incentives, basic services, utilities and infrastructure required or to be made available for said entities;
l.) Within the limitation provided by law, to raise and/or borrow the necessary funds from local and international financial institutions and to issue bonds, promissory notes, and other securities for that purpose and to secure the same guarantee, pledge, mortgage, deed of trust, or assignment of its properties held by the BEZA for the purpose of financing its projects and programs within the framework and limitations of this Act, and;
m.) As an investment promotion agency, to promote investments, grant and administer fiscal and non-fiscal incentives to registered business enterprises; generate revenue from various sources, including fees, charges, and levies; and oversee the operations of the different economic zones, freeports, and industrial estates within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**Section 15. The Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA) Board.** The BEZA Board shall have the following functions and powers:

a. Set the general policies on the establishment and operations of the BEZ, industrial estates, export processing zones, free trade zones, and the like;
b. Review proposals for the establishment of BEZ based on the set criteria under Section 7 above and endorse to the Chief Minister the establishment of the BEZ, industrial estates, export processing zones, eco-tourism zone, IT center/parks, free trade zones and the like. Thereafter, it shall facilitate and assist in the organization of said entities;
c. Regulate and undertake the establishment, operation and maintenance of ports, airfields, utilities, other services and infrastructure in the BEZ, such as heat, light and power, water supply, telecommunication, transport, print and media facilities, toll roads and bridges, port services, and to fix just, reasonable and competitive rates, charges and fees therefor;
d. Approve the annual budget of the BEZA and the BEZ development plans, with concurrence of the Chief Minister, which budget shall be appropriated by the Bangsamoro Parliament. In case of delay in the approval of the annual budget of the BEZA, it may operate on the basis of the budget of the preceding year until such time as the annual budget is approved;
e. Approve or disapprove the grant of fiscal and non-fiscal Incentives to registered business enterprises operating within the BEZ, and may suspend the same, subject to provisions provided for by RA 12066 otherwise known as “CREATE MORE Act”, rules and regulations that may be provided by the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB) and such other applicable and relevant laws;
f. Exercise its powers and functions as provided for in this Act for the purpose of ensuring accountability and transparency through the implementation of robust governance mechanisms, including financial reporting, auditing, and monitoring systems;
g. Exercise authority over administrative cases or matters filed or brought before it by any person against any of their respective officials or employees, except those officials with salary grade 25 and above;
h. Render annual reports to the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro Parliament; and,
i. Issue rules and regulations to implement the relevant provisions of this Act in so far as its power and functions are concerned.

The BEZA Board shall exclusively be a policy-making body and shall not interfere in the day-to-day or administrative operations of the BEZA or in the formulation and enforcement of regulations implementing its policies, which operations and regulations are the primary responsibility of the Bangsamoro Director-General.

**Section 16. Composition of the BEZA Board.** – The Board shall be composed of ten (10) members, as follows:

1. Minister of Trade, Investment, and Tourism (MTIT) as Chairperson;
2. Bangsamoro Director-General of BEZA as Vice-Chairperson;
3. Minister of Labor and Employment (MOLE);
4. Minister of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy (MENRE);
5. Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR);
6. Minister of Public Works (MPW);
7. Minister of Transportation and Communications (MOTC);
8. Minister of Finance, and Budget and Management (MFBM);
9. Director-General of the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) and,
10. Representative of Business/Labor Sector within the BEZ.

In case of the unavailability of the Minister of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) to attend a particular board meeting, the Bangsamoro Director-General of the BEZA shall act as Chairperson.

**Section 17. Powers and Functions of the Bangsamoro Director-General.** - The Bangsamoro Director-General shall be the overall coordinator of the policies, plans and programs of the BEZs, including the existing Polloc Freeport and Ecozone (PFEZ). As such, s/he shall provide overall supervision over and general direction for the development and operations of these BEZs. In addition, s/he shall have the following specific powers and responsibilities:

a.) Safeguard all the lands, buildings, records, monies, credits and other properties and rights of the BEZ;
b.) Ensure that all revenues of the BEZ are collected and applied in accordance with its budget;
c.) Ensure that the investors/firms, industrial estates and employees of the BEZ are properly discharging their respective duties;
d.) Give such information and recommend such measures to the Board, as s/he shall deem advantageous to the BEZ;
e.) Submit to the Board, the ongoing and proposed projects, work and financial program, annual budget of receipts, and expenditures of the BEZ;
f.) Represent the BEZ in all its business matters and sign on its behalf after approval of the Board, all its bonds, borrowings, contracts, agreements and obligations made in accordance with this Act;
g.) Acquire jurisdiction, as s/he may deem proper, over the protests, complaints, and claims of the residents and enterprises in the BEZ concerning administrative matters;
h.) Subject to Article IV hereof, s/he may recommend the conduct of investigations and/or impose administrative disciplinary actions against erring firms or employees of BEZA or the Bangsamoro Economic Zones;
i.) Recommend to the Board the grant of fiscal and non-fiscal incentives to registered business enterprises; approval, refusal, amendment, or termination of the BEZ franchises, licenses, permits, contracts, and agreements in accordance with the policies set by the Board;
j.) Require owners of houses, buildings or other structures constructed without the necessary permit whether constructed on public or private lands, to remove or demolish such houses, buildings, structures within sixty (60) days after notice and upon failure of such owner to remove or demolish such house, building or structure within said period, the Bangsamoro Director-General or his authorized representative may summarily cause its removal or demolition at the expense of the owner, any existing law, decree, executive order and other issuances or part thereof to the contrary notwithstanding;
k.) Take such emergency measures as may be necessary to avoid fires, floods and mitigate the effects of storms and other natural or public calamities;
l.) Accept any local or foreign investment, business or enterprise in the BEZ, subject only to such rules and regulations to be promulgated by the BEZA without prejudice to the nationalization requirements provided for in the Constitution;
m.) Prepare and make out plans for the physical and economic development of the BEZ, including sustainable resource management, zoning and land subdivision, issue such rules and regulations which shall be submitted to the Board for its approval,
n.) To determine, if necessary, the structure and the staffing pattern and personnel complement of the BEZA, subject to the approval of the BEZA Board; and;
o.) Perform such other duties and exercises such powers as may be prescribed by the Board, and to implement the policies, rules and regulations set by the BEZA.

**Section 18. Structure and Staffing Patterns.** – Subject to preceding section and to support the functions of the Bangsamoro Director General, the Authority shall have at least four (4) divisions, namely, Policy and Planning Division, Administrative and Finance Division, Research and Development Division, and, Operations Division. Subject to Section 13 hereof, each Division shall be headed by a Deputy Director.

The Deputy Director of Policy and Planning Division shall be assisted by Planning Officer V and shall be supported by Policy Studies and Planning Unit, Management and Information Systems Unit, and Ecozone Development Unit.

The Deputy Director for Operations Division shall be assisted by Chief Economic Development Specialist and shall be supported by Enterprise Assistance Unit, Enterprise Regulations Unit, and Support Services Unit.

The Deputy Director for Administrative and Finance Division shall be assisted by Chief Finance Officer and shall be supported by Finance and Administrative Units.

The Deputy Director for Research and Development Division shall be assisted by Senior Research Specialist. The Division shall be supported by Ecozone Research Unit and Economics & Statistics Unit.

There shall be an Office of the Building Official who shall be responsible in the monitoring of compliance by the registered locators and developers on the National Building Code and its implementing rules as well as those contained in such other rules as the BEZA may adopt. He/she shall be supported by a pool of engineers (e.g. civil, mechanical, geodetic, electronics, electrical, sanitary) and architect/s.

Likewise, there shall be a separate Investment Promotions and Marketing Unit (IPMU) under the Office of the Bangsamoro Director General. The Unit shall at least be headed by a Chief Investment Promotion and Marketing Specialist and assisted by Senior Investment Promotion and Marketing Specialist, and Investment Promotion and Marketing Analyst.

Provided, That the Authority, through the Bangsamoro Director General, is authorized to determine the structure and the staffing pattern and personnel complement of the BEZA, and create such services, divisions, and units, as it may deem necessary in the future, subject to the approval of the BEZA Board.

**Section 19. Legal Counsel.** - The BEZA shall have its own internal legal counsel who shall be under the supervision of the Bangsamoro Director General. When necessary, BEZA shall engage the services of the Bangsamoro Attorney General’s Office (BAGO) or an outside counsel either on a case-by-case or on a fixed retainer basis.

**Section 20. Administration of each BEZ.** - Except for privately-owned, managed, or operated BEZs, each BEZ either established through BEZA or LGU, shall be organized, administered, managed by the BEZ Executive Committee (BEEC) composed of the following:

(a) The Bangsamoro Director-General of the BEZA or any of his/her representative;
(b) The Administrator who shall be appointed by the BEZA Board upon recommendation of the Bangsamoro Director-General; and
(c) One (1) Deputy Administrator to be appointed by the Board upon recommendation of the Bangsamoro Director-General.

The Administrator and the Deputy Administrator shall be of proven probity and integrity, and a degree holder in any of the following fields: economics, business, public administration, law, accounting, management, engineering or their equivalent, with at least four (4) years relevant working experience and with civil service eligibility or its equivalent (RA 1080).

The Administrator shall be responsible in overseeing and managing the day-to-day operations of the BEZ and as may be determined by the Bangsamoro Director General and upon the concurrence of the BEZA Board. S/he will be assisted by the Deputy Administrator in the execution of their duties and functions.

The residents of the area, where the BEZ is located and/or those with graduate level degrees, such as master’s in public administration, or professional board passers, shall be given priority in hiring.

For LGU-initiated BEZ, the concerned Provincial Local Executive shall be consulted in the hiring of the Administrator and Deputy Administrator.

Privately-owned BEZS shall retain autonomy and independence but shall be monitored by the BEZA for the implementation of incentives and to ensure that its operations are in adherence to this Act and other existing laws.

**Section 21. BEZ Advisory Council.** – A BEZ advisory body shall be created with the following members:

The Governor of the Province, where the BEZ is located, shall be the Chairperson of the Council;

The Vice Chairperson who shall be elected from among the members of the Council;

Members consisting of:

One (1) representative from the registered business enterprises (RBEs) in the BEZ;
One (1) of the Mayor/s of the municipality/ies or city/ies where the BEZ is located;
One (1) representative of the business sector in the periphery of the BEZ;
One (1) representative from the accredited labor union in the BEZ;
One (1) representative from Darul Ifta, and,
One (1) Representative of the BEZA.

The BEZ Advisory Council shall have the following functions:

(a) Advise the BEZ management on matters pertaining to policy initiatives; and
(b) Assist the BEZ management in dispute resolution between labor and any enterprise in the BEZ.

**ARTICLE IV**
**INVESTIGATIONS & DISCIPLINARY ACTION**

**Section 22. Investigation and Inquiries.** - Upon a written formal complaint made under oath, which on its face provides reasonable basis to believe that some anomaly or irregularity might have been committed, the BEZA shall have the power to inquire into the conduct of firms or employees of the Bangsamoro Economic Zone concerned and to conduct investigations, and for that purpose may subpoena witnesses, administer oaths, and compel the production of books, papers, and other evidences: Provided, That to arrive at the truth, the investigation (s) may grant immunity from prosecution to any person whose testimony or whose possessions of documents or other evidence is necessary or convenient to determine the truth in any investigation conducted by him/her or under the authority of the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority.

**Section 23. Disciplinary Action.** – Subject to Section 22 hereof, the BEZA shall have the authority to impose administrative disciplinary action against any official or employee with Salary Grade 25 and below. Provided, however, that administrative adjudication process shall be in accordance with Articles 189 and 190 and Book V (Procedure on Disciplinary Actions) of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 17 series of 2020.

**ARTICLE V**
**PROHIBITIONS & DISCLOSURES**

**Section 24. Prohibition Against Holding Any Other Office.** -The Bangsamoro director general, directors, administrators, officials and staff or assistants of the BEZA shall not hold any other office or employment within or outside the BEZA during their tenure. They shall not, during their tenure, directly or indirectly, practice any profession, participate in any business, or be financially interested in any contract with, or in any franchise, or special privilege granted by the BEZA or national government, or any subdivision, agency, or instrumentality thereof, including any government-owned-controlled corporation, or its subsidiary.

**Section 25. Full Disclosure of Financial and Business Interests.** – Every member of Board of BEZA, the director general, the directors, and their staff shall, upon assumption of office, make full disclosure of their financial and business interest.


**ARTICLE VI**
**OPERATIONS WITHIN THE BANGSAMORO ECONOMIC ZONE**

**Section 26. Sustainable Development Strategy of the BEZ.** – To ensure sustainability, the strategy and priority of development of each BEZ established pursuant to this Act shall be formulated by the BEZA, in coordination with the Ministry of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT), the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) and the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Agency (BPDA), and other concerned stakeholders: Provided, That such development strategy is consistent with the priorities of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) as outlined in the Bangsamoro Development Plan of the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council (BEDC). Provided further, that resource management, biodiversity conservation, climate change mitigation and adaptation; environmental education, environmental monitoring and reporting, and environmental enforcement are incorporated in the said strategy.

**Section 27. Fiscal Incentives.** — Business establishments registered and operating within the BEZs shall be entitled to the fiscal incentives as provided for under Presidential Decree No. 66, the law creating the Export Processing Zone Authority, or those provided under Book VI of Executive Order No. 226, otherwise known as the Omnibus Investment Act of 1987, or those provided under Republic Act No. 12066, otherwise known as the Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises to Maximize Opportunities for Reinvigorating the Economy (CREATE MORE ) Act, and any other related laws and regulations to be enacted by the National government and/or Bangsamoro Parliament.

Provided, that availment of fiscal incentives and its conditions shall be subject to the rules provided in Sections 17 and 18 of RA 12066, respectively.

Furthermore, tax credits for exporters using local materials as inputs shall enjoy the same benefits provided for in the Republic Act 7844 or the Export Development Act of 1994 and other applicable laws.

**Section 28. Implementation and Monitoring of Incentives.** – The BEZA Board shall be responsible for the administration and implementation of the incentives granted to registered business enterprises. The Board shall, likewise, submit to the Chief Minister and the Parliament the annual tax expenditures based on the computed costs in terms of revenue forgone on the tax incentives granted to the registered enterprises. For proper monitoring, the Bangsamoro Government through the Ministry of Finance, and Budget, and Management (MFBM), shall create a single database of all incentives provided by BEZA, in coordination with the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB).

**Section 29. Applicable National and Local Taxes.** All persons and service establishments in the BEZ shall be subject to national, regional, and local taxes under the National Internal Revenue Code of 1997, as amended, Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 (Bangsamoro Local Governance Code) and Bangsamoro Revenue Code of the BARMM. Provided, however, pending the passage of revenue code of BARMM, existing national laws and applicable regional issuances shall prevail.

**Section 30. Exemption from National and Local Taxes, Fees and Charges.** - Except for real property taxes on land owned by developers, no taxes, fees and charges, local and national, shall be imposed on business establishments operating within the BEZ. In lieu thereof, charges and fees amounting to three percent (3%) of the gross revenue earned by all businesses and enterprises within the BEZ shall be paid and remitted to the Bangsamoro autonomous government; another one percent (1%) of such gross revenue shall be paid and remitted to the municipalities and cities affected by the declaration of the zone in proportion to their population, area and other factors as determined by BEZA. In addition, there is hereby established a development fund of one percent (1%) of the gross revenue earned by all businesses and enterprises within the BEZ to be utilized for the development of the host province and municipalities outside the BEZ.

The one percent (1%) share of the municipalities and cities affected by the declaration of the BEZ shall be directly remitted by the business establishments to the treasurer's office of the municipalities or cities where the enterprise is located in proportion to their population, area and other factors as determined by the BEZA.

However, a portion of the revenues earned shall be retained by the BEZA in trust as development funds. The percentage of which shall be determined annually under the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act by the Bangsamoro Parliament. For the first five (5) years of operation of the BEZA wherein it has actual revenues to be remitted to the Bangsamoro autonomous government, the amount shall be 20% of the 5% of the gross revenues earned by BEZ locator.

In case of conflict between national, regional and local laws with respect to tax exemption privileges in the BEZ, the same shall be resolved in favor of the latter.

**Section 31. Disbursement of Funds.** - No money shall be paid out of the funds of any BEZ except in pursuance of the budget as formulated and approved by the BEZA.

**Section 32. Domestic Sales.** Goods manufactured by a BEZ enterprise shall be made available for immediate retail sales in the domestic market, subject to payment of corresponding taxes on the raw materials and other regulations that may be adopted by the BEZA Board.

However, in order to protect the domestic industry, there shall be a negative list of industries that will be drawn up by the BEZA as provided for by RA 7042 or the Foreign Investments Act of 1991, as amended. Enterprises engaged in the industries included in the negative list shall not be allowed to sell their products locally. Said negative list shall be regularly updated by the BEZA, in coordination with the concerned national and BARMM agencies.

The BEZA, in coordination with the Ministry of Trade, Investment, and Tourism, shall jointly issue the necessary implementing rules and guidelines for the effective implementation of this Section, pursuant to Executive Order 175 series of 2022.

**Section 33. Applicability of Banking Laws and Regulations.** Existing banking laws and Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) rules and regulations shall apply to banks and financial institutions to be established in the BEZ and to other BEZ registered enterprises. Among other pertinent regulations, these include those governing foreign exchange and other current account transactions (trade and non-trade) local and foreign borrowings, foreign investments, establishment and operation local and foreign banks, foreign currency deposit units, offshore banking units and other financial institutions under the supervision of the BSP: Provided, however, That banking and finance shall be liberalized with the establishment of foreign currency depository units of local commercial banks and offshore banking units of foreign banks with minimum Central Bank regulation.

**Section 34. After Tax Profits.** Without prior Bangko Sentral approval, after tax profits and other earnings of foreign investments in the enterprises within the BEZ may be remitted outward in the equivalent foreign exchange through any of the banks licensed by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas in the BEZ: Provided, however, that such foreign investments in said enterprises have been previously registered with the Bangko Sentral.

**Section 35. Eminent Domain.** The areas comprising BEZ may be expanded or reduced when necessary. For this purpose, the BEZA, subject to the concurrence of the Chief Minister, shall have the power to acquire, either by purchase, negotiation or condemnation proceedings, any private lands within or adjacent to the BEZ for:

(a) Consolidation of lands for zone development purposes;
(b) Acquisition of right of way to the BEZ; and
(c) The protection of watershed areas and natural assets valuable to the property of the BEZ.

If in the establishment of publicly owned BEZ, any person or group of persons who has been legally occupying a parcel of land within the Zone has to be evicted, the BEZA shall provide the person or group of persons concerned with proper disturbance compensation: Provided, however, That in the case of displaced agrarian reform beneficiaries, they shall be entitled to the benefits under the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law, including but not limited to Section 36 of Republic Act No. 3844, as amended by Republic Act 6389 (Code of Agrarian Reform in the Philippines) in addition to a home lot in the relocation site, and preferential employment in the project being undertaken.

**Section 36. Public Lands.** All existing public lands within the declared BEZ shall be under the exclusive jurisdiction and control of BEZA.

**Section 37. Leases of Lands and Buildings.** Lands and buildings in each BEZ may be leased to foreign investors for a period not exceeding fifty (50) years renewable once for a period of not more than twenty-five (25) years, as provided for under Republic Act No. 7652, otherwise known as the Investors' Lease Act, subject, however, to Republic Act 6657 or the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law as amended, and the Bangsamoro Local Governance Code. The leasehold right acquired under-long-term contracts may be sold, transferred or assigned, subject to conditions set forth under Republic Act No. 7652.

**Section 38. Land Conversion.** - Agricultural lands may be converted for residential, commercial, industrial and other non-industrial purposes, subject to the conditions set forth under Republic Act No. 6657 or the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law, as amended, and other existing national and BARMM regional laws and issuances.

**Section 39. Shipping and Shipping Register.** - Private shipping and related business including private container terminals may operate freely in the BEZ, subject only to such minimum reasonable regulations of local application which the BEZA may prescribe. The BEZA shall, in coordination with the Ministry of Transportation and Communications (MOTC) and the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT), maintain a shipping register for each BEZ as a business register of convenience for ocean-going vessels and issue related certification.

Ships of all sizes, descriptions and nationalities shall enjoy access to the ports of the BEZ, subject only to such reasonable requirement as may be prescribed by the BEZA in coordination with the appropriate agencies of the BARMM government.

**Section 40. Environmental Regulation.** The BEZA, in coordination with the appropriate agencies, shall take concrete and appropriate steps and enact proper measure for the protection of the local environment. It shall ensure that all proposed projects within the economic zones, industrial estate, and free ports have undertaken Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA). BEZA shall coordinate with MENRE for the latter’s issuance of Environmental Clearance Certificate (ECC) or Certificate of Non-Coverage (CNC) for projects, investments or locators in the BEZ.

Provided, BEZA shall assist MENRE in the processing of ECC applications of projects not classified as single project Environmentally Critical Projects (ECPs).

**Section 41. Termination of Business.** - Investors in the BEZ who desire to terminate business or operations shall comply with such requirements and procedures which the BEZA shall set, particularly those relating to the clearing of debts. The assets of the closed enterprise can be transferred and the funds can be remitted out of the BEZ subject to the existing rules, guidelines and procedures prescribed by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas and the Department of Finance.

**Section 42. Registration of Business Enterprises.** Business enterprises within a designated BEZ shall register with the BEZA to avail of all incentives and benefits provided for in this Act.

**Section 43. One Stop Shop Center.** The BEZA shall establish a one stop shop center for the purpose of facilitating the registration of new enterprises in the BEZ. Thus, all appropriate BARMM agencies that are involved in registering, licensing, or issuing permits to investors shall assign their representatives to the BEZ to attend to investors' requirements.

**Section 44. Halal Certifying Body.** The BEZA, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Halal Board shall establish a halal certifying body in every concerned BEZ, which shall be composed, as far as practicable, representatives coming from Ulama and ministries as defined in Section 35 of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13 and as provided under the Implementing Rules and Regulations that may be formulated by the BEZA.

**ARTICLE VII**
**LABOR RIGHTS AND INDUSTRIAL HARMONY IN THE BEZ**

**Section 45. Labor and Management Relations.** - Except as otherwise provided in this Act, labor and management relations in the BEZ shall be governed by the existing Labor Code of the Philippines, as amended and/or the BARMM’s Labor and Employment Code. Employees and personnel in the BEZ enterprises shall receive salaries and benefits, including disability coverage, and shall enjoy working conditions not less than those provided under the Philippine Labor Code and other relevant laws, issuances, rules and regulations of the Bangsamoro Parliament and the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE-BARMM).

**Section 46. Promotion of Industrial Peace.** - In the pursuit of industrial harmony in the BEZ, a multipartite body composed of one (1) representative each from the BEZA, Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC), Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE-BARMM); labor, business and industry sectors, shall be created in order to formulate an efficient and effective mechanism under a social pact for the enhancement and preservation of industrial peace in the BEZ within thirty (30) days after the effectivity of this Act.

The Body shall ensure that rights of labor and personnel to minimum wage standards; occupational health and safety standards, trade union organizing and collective bargaining; employment contracts, social security and benefits; child labor and forced labor prevention; training and skills development; grievance mechanisms and non-discriminatory clauses are incorporated in the said pact.

**ARTICLE VIII**
**BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS GOVERNMENT AND OTHER ENTITIES**

**Section 47. Relationship with the Bangsamoro Economic & Development Council (BEDC).** - The BEZA shall determine the development goals for the BEZ within the framework of the BARMM policies, goals and the Bangsamoro Development Plan. The Bangsamoro Director General shall, upon approval by the BEZA Board, submit the BEZ plans, programs and projects to the BEDC for inclusion in and as inputs to the overall regional development plan.

**Section 48. Relationship with the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB).** – Without prejudice to the power and authority as provided for by RA 11054 and this law, the BEZA shall maintain closer coordination with the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB) with respect to administration and granting of tax and duty incentives to registered export and domestic market enterprises as well as its withdrawal, cancellation or suspension thereof, as provided in the Implementing Rules and Regulations of RA 12066.

**Section 49. Relationship with the Local Government Units.** - Except as herein provided, the local government units comprising the BEZ shall retain their basic autonomy and identity. The cities shall be governed by their respective charters and the municipalities shall operate and function in accordance with Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 49 (Bangsamoro Local Governance Code).

**Section 50. Relationship of BEZA to Privately-Owned Industrial Estates.** - Privately-owned industrial estates shall retain their autonomy and independence and shall be monitored by the BEZA for the implementation of incentives.

**ARTICLE IX**
**MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS**

**Section 51. Appropriations.** –The initial amount of Twenty-Nine Million Pesos (P29,000,000.00) for Personal Services and Sixty-Three Million Pesos (P63,000,000.00) for the initial operation of the office shall be sourced from MTIT and Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), respectively. Thereafter, such amount as may be necessary for the continuous operation of BEZA and the implementation of its programs and services shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

**Section 52. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** - The BEZA, in consultation with MTIT, BEZ Advisory Council and other concerned stakeholders, shall issue the implementing rules and regulations of this Act within ninety (90) days starting from the effectivity of this Act.

**Section 53. Transitory Provision.** - Prior to effectivity of this Act, the Notice of Organization, Staffing and Compensation Action (NOSCA Serial No. 002021-01-034) and revised NOSCA with Serial No. 002021-02-034, which reflect the creation of two (2) positions (both are fixed in nature) and additional twenty (20) positions (18 permanent positions, 1 coterminous and 1 fixed term position), respectively, as approved by the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management (MFBM) and pursuant to the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 13, shall remain in force.

**Section 54. Transfer of Jurisdiction for Registered Business Enterprises (RBEs).** All business enterprises registered by the Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI-BARMM) and operating within the Bangsamoro Economic Zone prior to the enactment of this law are hereby transferred under the jurisdiction of BEZA, without prejudice to the rights and privileges that have been accorded to the said RBEs.

**Section 55. Separability Clause.** If any provision of this Act shall be held unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 56. Repealing Clause.** The Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 154 otherwise known as “The ARMM Special Economic Zone Act of 2003” is hereby expressly repealed, in accordance with the powers and authorities of the Bangsamoro Parliament under Republic Act 11054 otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL).

Likewise, Title XV, Chapter 3, Section 30, paragraph (a) of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No.13, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, which mandates the Bangsamoro Ports Management Authority (BPMA) to manage ports “*including those in special economic zones*”, is hereby expressly repealed.

All other Bangsamoro Government laws, executive orders, rules and regulations, issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**Section 57. Effectivity Clause.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in two (2) newspapers of regional or national circulation.
