**AN ACT**

**PROVIDING FOR THE PROTECTION, PRESERVATION, CONSERVATION, AND PROMOTION OF CULTURAL HERITAGE IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION, STRENGTHENING THE BANGSAMORO COMMISSION FOR THE PRESERVATION OF CULTURAL HERITAGE, ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO CULTURAL HERITAGE PROGRAM, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**

**BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** - This Act shall be known as the "Cultural Heritage Act of the Bangsamoro".

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** - The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is deeply committed to the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of its unique and diverse cultural heritage, encompassing both tangible and intangible elements. As such, the Bangsamoro Government shall embody and consolidate the historical narratives, socio-cultural practices, and varied traditions of the Bangsamoro people that contribute to the vibrant cultural mosaic of the region.

By fostering a profound cultural consciousness and appreciation, the Bangsamoro government shall strengthen the Bangsamoro people's distinctive cultural identity and promote intercultural dialogue, mutual respect, and peace within the region and beyond. It shall underscore the active participation of communities in the stewardship of cultural heritage sites and properties, ensuring their conservation and utilization for educational, scientific, cultural, and touristic purposes.

In furtherance of this commitment, the Bangsamoro Government pledges to allocate adequate resources, strategic planning, and continuous capacity-building for the effective implementation of its cultural heritage programs. It shall ensure the mainstreaming of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage, identity, and history in all levels of governance and development, integrating cultural heritage protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion principles and practices, including the use of innovative technology and tools, into local and regional planning strategies, infrastructure development, and environmental and other sustainability measures. Moreover, the Bangsamoro Government shall collaborate with local, national, and international entities to ensure the successful realization of this policy.

Furthermore, the Bangsamoro Government is committed to ensure that the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the region's cultural heritage be consistent with Islamic principles including stewardship (*Khilafah*), public interest (*Maslaha*), integrity (*Istiqamah*), respect for rights and property (*Huquq*), harmony with the environment (*Tawazun*), collaboration and cooperation (*Ta'awun*), avoiding waste (*Israf*), justice (*Adl*), and peace and tolerance (*Salam* and *Tasamuh*). The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the significance of culture in the Islamic perspective and shall adhere to these principles in its efforts to safeguard and showcase the Bangsamoro cultural heritage.

Finally, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes and prioritizes the need for a holistic approach to cultural heritage, embracing diversity, supporting artistic creativity, preserving cultural traditions, and promoting cultural expressions as fundamental components of the Bangsamoro's cultural vitality, communal identity, and historical continuity. It acknowledges the role of the Bangsamoro's cultural heritage as a bridge for understanding and cooperation, fostering social cohesion, mutual respect, and peaceful coexistence among its diverse inhabitants and with other communities and regions of the country.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.** The following terms, as used in this Act, shall mean:

a. "Bangsamoro Culture" refers to the sum total of ways of living built up by a group of human beings, which is transmitted from one generation to another, including but not limited to their beliefs, practices, arts, laws, customs, traditions, and institutions.

b. "Bangsamoro Identity" refers to the distinct collective attributes, characteristics, values, heritage, and cultural context that create a sense of belonging among the Bangsamoro people, including but not limited to their languages, traditions, customs, beliefs, and practices.

c. "Bangsamoro History" refers to the chronicle of significant events, experiences, and narratives of the Bangsamoro people, including their origins, struggles, and aspirations.

d. "Cultural Heritage" refers to the totality of cultural property preserved and developed through time and passed on to posterity.

e. "Tangible Cultural Heritage" refers to all natural and man-made, movable and immovable, and tangible objects of religious, artistic, historical, and cultural value.

f. "Intangible Cultural Heritage" refers to practices, expressions, knowledge, skills – as well as the instruments, objects, artifacts, and cultural spaces associated with them – that communities, groups, and in some cases, individuals, recognize as part of their cultural heritage.

g. "Cultural Properties and Sites" encompass both tangible and intangible properties and physical locations that hold cultural, artistic, historical, architectural, or archaeological significance to the Bangsamoro people. These may include tangible cultural properties such as artifacts, structures, artworks, and monuments, as well as intangible cultural heritage practices, expressions, knowledge, and skills associated with specific cultural sites.

h. "Cultural Protection" pertains to the measures and actions taken to safeguard and ensure the integrity, authenticity, and continued existence of cultural heritage, including the establishment of legal frameworks, regulations, and enforcement mechanisms to prevent unauthorized alteration, destruction, or illicit trade of cultural properties.

i. "Cultural Preservation" refers to activities and processes designed to safeguard cultural property from deterioration, damage, or loss. It includes conservation efforts, restoration work, maintenance, and preventive measures to ensure the physical and structural stability of cultural heritage objects, sites, or practices.

j. "Cultural Conservation" involves the comprehensive and sustainable management and care of cultural heritage, encompassing preservation, restoration, research, documentation, interpretation, and community engagement. It aims to retain the cultural, artistic, historical, and social significance of cultural properties while promoting their understanding and appreciation.

k. "Cultural Promotion" relates to initiatives and activities undertaken to raise awareness, enhance appreciation, and encourage active participation in cultural heritage. This may include educational programs, cultural events, exhibitions, publications, digital platforms, or other means of disseminating knowledge and showcasing the value and significance of cultural heritage to the Bangsamoro people and wider audiences.

**SECTION 4. Additional Powers and Functions of the BCPCH.** - In addition to its existing mandates, powers, and functions under the Bangsamoro Administrative Act (BAA) No. 13 otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, the BCPCH shall have the following additional powers and functions:

a) Exercise powers to enforce laws related to cultural heritage protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and deputize such powers to government agencies and local government units (LGUs) when necessary;

b) Exercise oversight powers over Local Culture and Arts Councils in local government units (LGUs) to ensure the effective implementation of cultural heritage programs, projects, and activities;

c) Require any person or entity intending to or is engaged in the sale, transfer, export, import, or any other form of transaction involving cultural property of the Bangsamoro to obtain a permit from the BCPCH, providing all necessary information and documentation as required by the IRR and guidelines promulgated by the BCPCH to this effect;

d) Revoke or suspend such permit if the person or entity is found to be in violation of the conditions for the issuance of the permit, any of the prohibited acts, engaged in illegal activities, or failing to comply with any provisions of this Act, and/or other applicable laws and regulations. Revocation or suspension shall be carried out in accordance with due process, IRR and guidelines promulgated to this effect;

e) Charge and collect fees for permits issued and undertook in connection with the implementation of this Act;

f) Mainstream Bangsamoro cultural heritage, identity, practices, and history in all levels of governance and development, including the integration of these aspects in the policies, programs, projects, and activities of ministries, agencies, and offices;

g) Recognize and support leaders, individuals, and organizations championing the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history, and provide incentives for their contributions to the preservation and promotion of the region's cultural heritage;

h) Review and endorse for approval all proposed programs, projects, and activities of ministries, agencies, and offices that may have an impact on the cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

i) Manage the Bangsamoro Endowment Fund for culture, identity, and history;

j) Coordinate with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education in providing scholarships related to Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history;

k) Adopt guidelines and issuances in the implementation of the provisions of this Act in alignment with Islamic principles;

l) Adopt a structure responsive to the implementation of its mandates, powers, and functions including but not limited to research, planning, and policy development, program management, monitoring and evaluation, cultural properties protection, regulation, and enforcement, quality management, administration and finance, and coordination and support services including granting awards and incentives;

m) Coordinate with national government agencies, local government units, international cultural entities, and other stakeholders in the implementation of this Act;

n) Perform such other duties and functions as may be provided by law.


**SECTION 5. Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program.** - The Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program is hereby established, guided by the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) in partnership with relevant agencies and local government units. The program incorporates and advances the following components:

a) *Culture-Sensitive Governance.* - This component prioritizes the integration of cultural considerations in governance and development. It includes the documentation, inventory, and registration of cultural properties and sites, and the creation of cultural heritage zones and cultural centers. It underscores the importance of active collaboration with government agencies and units in decision-making processes to ensure the promotion and respect of the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

b) *Cultural Development and Advocacy.* – This component is dedicated to enhancing cultural awareness and education, advocating for the significance Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history, and supporting local artists, artisans, and cultural workers. It focuses on fostering deeper societal integration of the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

c) *Cultural Preservation and Conservation.* – This component is devoted to maintaining and safeguarding the Bangsamoro cultural heritage. It entails efforts to preserve, conserve, and restore cultural properties and sites, ensuring their longevity for future generations to appreciate and learn from.

d) *Bangsamoro History and Development.* – This component illuminates the vibrant history and evolution of the Bangsamoro people. It involves documenting and studying the Bangsamoro history to deepen understanding and appreciation of the Bangsamoro culture and identity.

e) *Museum and Library Management and Development.* – This component involves establishing and effectively managing Bangsamoro museums and libraries as repositories of cultural artifacts. These institutions serve as venues for cultural education and catalysts for cultural tourism, encouraging greater appreciation of the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

f) *Development of Cultural Enterprises and Tourism.* – This component encourages and supports the development of cultural enterprises, including cooperatives and social enterprises, in their business planning, management, production, and service provision among others. It shall include provision of assistance to cultural enterprises in their registration of cultural products and services under the Intellectual Property Law. It shall also encourage tourism that respects and appreciates the cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro and involves promoting cultural sites, festivals, and local traditions, supporting local economies while promoting cultural understanding and appreciation. This shall be in coordination with the Cooperatives and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA), the Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT) and other relevant ministries, agencies, and offices.

g) *Such Other Components or Programs Adopted by the BCPCH and Other Implementing Agencies.* - This includes any other initiatives that BCPCH and other implementing ministries, agencies, and offices may undertake to fulfill their mandates, powers, and functions that will further protect, preserve, conserve, and promote the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

**SECTION 6. Master Plan on the Protection, Preservation, Conservation, and Promotion of the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage.** – The BCPCH shall develop and adopt, within six months after the effectivity of this law, a comprehensive Master Plan on the Protection, Preservation, Conservation, and Promotion of the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage. This Master Plan shall serve as a strategic framework and roadmap for the effective implementation of cultural heritage programs and initiatives in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**SECTION 7. Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Advocacy Network.** – A Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Advocacy Network shall be created to serve as a collaborative platform connecting the BCPCH with various cultural communities, academe, private sector, and other stakeholders. The network shall facilitate the exchange of information, ideas, and resources, provide policy recommendations, review and endorse projects, and assist in the implementation of the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program.

**SECTION 8. Preservation and Restoration of Cultural Properties and Sites.** - The Bangsamoro Government through the BCPCH shall provide financial and technical assistance to local government units, non-government organizations, and private individuals for the preservation and restoration of cultural properties, sites, history, art traditions, and rich cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their sultanates, including the non-moro indigenous peoples of BARMM, in accordance with the guidelines it shall adopt for the purpose.

**SECTION 9. Cultural Mapping by Local Government Units.** - Local Government Units, in coordination with the BCPCH, are hereby mandated to conduct a comprehensive cultural heritage mapping of their areas, including tangible and intangible heritage and natural and built heritage structures. The local government units shall mobilize and partner with concerned agencies to ensure prompt and effective implementation of this act. All findings and outputs shall be submitted and registered in the BCPCH.

**SECTION 10. Cultural Heritage Zones.** - The Bangsamoro Government, through the BCPCH, shall identify, establish, and manage Cultural Heritage Zones, which shall be areas of significant historical, cultural, or architectural value. The BCPCH shall develop guidelines for the preservation, conservation, and development of these zones.

**SECTION 11. Cultural Heritage Education and Awareness.** - The Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), in consultation with the BCPCH, shall integrate cultural heritage education in the basic and tertiary education curricula. The BCPCH shall coordinate with the MBHTE and other stakeholders to promote awareness and appreciation of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage.

**SECTION 12. Committee on Culture in the Bangsamoro Parliament.** – There shall be established a Committee on Culture in the Bangsamoro Parliament that shall have jurisdiction in the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

**SECTION 13. Funding.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall allocate necessary funds for the implementation of this Act. The BCPCH shall submit an annual budget proposal, including funding requirements for the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program to the Bangsamoro Parliament for approval.

**SECTION 14. Bangsamoro Endowment Fund (Awqaf) for Cultural Heritage.** - An Islamic endowment fund (Awqaf), referred to as the "Bangsamoro Endowment Fund for Cultural Heritage", shall hereby be established. This fund shall be devoted to the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage. It shall be sourced from donations, grants, contributions, endowments, legacies or gifts from various sources, both local and international, the annual budget allocation of the Bangsamoro Government from revenues it generates as determined in the Bangsamoro Revenue Code, and income from the development and utilization of cultural heritage properties.


**SECTIONS 15. Prohibited Acts.** - To the extent that the offense is not punishable by a higher punishment under another provision of law, violations of this Act may be made by whoever intentionally:

a) Destroys, demolishes, mutilates or damages any heritage site, cultural treasures, important cultural property and archaeological and anthropological sites within the Bangsamoro Region;

b) Modifies, alters, or destroys the original features of or undertakes construction or real estate development in any regional shrine, monument, landmark and other historic edifices and structures, declared, classified, and marked by the BCPCH without prior written permission from said Commission. This includes the designated security or buffer zone, extending five (5) meters from the visible perimeter of the monument or site;

c) Explores, excavates or undertakes diggings for the purpose of obtaining materials of cultural historical value without prior written authority from the BCPCH. No excavation or diggings shall be permitted without the supervision of a certified archaeologist;

d) Appropriates excavation finds contrary to the provisions of pertinent laws;

e) Imports, sells, distributes, procures, acquires, or exports cultural property stolen, or otherwise lost against the will of the lawful owner;

f) Illicitly exports cultural property listed in the BCPCH Registry of Cultural Property or those that may be categorized as such upon visitation or incorrectly declares the same during transit;

g) Deals in cultural property without proper registration and license issued by the cultural agency concerned; and

h) Such other prohibited acts as may be determined by law or regulation.

**SECTION 16. Penal Provisions.** – Any person who violates any provision of this Act shall be punished by a fine of not less than two hundred thousand pesos (P200,000.00) or by imprisonment for not less than ten (10) years, or both such fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court. In addition, the offender may be required to cover the cost of repair, restoration, or restitution of the affected cultural property or heritage site.

In case the violation is committed by a corporation, partnership, or any other entity, the head, directors, managers, or any officer who consented to or knowingly tolerated such violation shall be held liable. The juridical entity itself shall also be subjected to applicable fines, penalties, loss of licenses, or restrictions on operation as determined by law or regulation. If the liable individuals are aliens, they shall, after serving their sentence, be deported immediately without further proceedings. In case the individuals are public officials or employees, they shall, in addition to the penalties prescribed herein, be deemed automatically dismissed from the service and their benefits forfeited, after due proceedings.

The offender shall also be ordered to pay an indemnity for damages to the affected cultural properties or heritage sites at an amount determined by the court.

In the event that the violation involves endangering or damaging cultural property that has been declared as a national cultural treasure or important cultural property, the fine and prison term shall be doubled. The offender shall likewise be required to undertake restoration works and pay for any damage caused to the cultural property.

**SECTION 17. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** - The BCPCH, in consultation with relevant stakeholders, shall formulate and issue the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act within ninety (90) days from its effectivity.

**SECTION 18. Transitory Provisions.** - The BCPCH shall ensure the smooth transition and integration of the additional mandates, powers, and functions provided in this Act. Existing personnel, resources, and processes shall be reviewed and reorganized as necessary to fulfill the expanded roles and responsibilities of the BCPCH.

**SECTION 19. Separability Clause.** - If any part or provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional, the other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

**SECTION 20. Repealing Clause.** - All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations, or parts thereof, inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 21. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of general or regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

*Approved.*
