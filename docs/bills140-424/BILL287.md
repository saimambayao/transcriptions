**“AN ACT ESTABLISHING A POLITICAL PARTY SUBSIDY FUND IN THE BANGSAMORO, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES”**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, in Parliament assembled:

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “*Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Act.*”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro to guarantee equal access to opportunities for public service¹.

The Bangsamoro Government recognizes that political parties are the essence of participatory democracy. Thus, it is imperative to provide them public funding to reduce the influence of private money upon elections, and subsequently its influence on public policy. To this end, the Bangsamoro shall provide financial subsidies to regional political parties in the Bangsamoro and regulate campaign financing through effective and transparent mechanisms in order to level the playing field among all candidates and political parties.

**Section 3. Purpose.** - This Act aims to (1) provide financial subsidies to political parties, to augment b their expenditures for campaign purposes and party development (2) encourage and support continuing voter’s education and civic literacy programs through regional political parties.

**Section 4. Definition of Terms.** – For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall mean:

(a) “*Accredited Regional Political Parties*” – refers to a regional political party as defined under the Bangsamoro Electoral Code (BAA No. 35),² qualified to receive subsidy for party development and campaign purposes, and accredited for this purpose by the Bangsamoro Registration and Accreditation Committee based on a set of criteria provided by this Act. For purposes of this act, “accredited regional political parties” shall include accredited sectoral parties and organizations fielding candidates for the sectoral seats in Parliament;

(b) “*Bangsamoro Registration and Accreditation Committee (BRAC)*” – refers to the body tasked with monitoring the regional political party’s compliance with laws and issuances on the utilization of party subsidies and other assistance received from public funds, as provided in Article II, Section 4 and of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code;

(c) “*Bangsamoro Electoral Office (BEO)*” – refers to the office established by the COMELEC to supervise all national, regional, and local elections in the Bangsamoro, in accordance with the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the Bangsamoro Electoral Code;

(d) “*Campaign Contributions*” – refers to any form of donation to any candidate, political party, aggrupation or coalition thereof, given before, during or after the holding of elections. It includes any gift, donation, subscription, loan, advance, or deposit of money or anything of value, of those arising from a contract, pledge or agreement to contribute, made for the purpose of influencing the results of the elections, but shall not include services rendered without compensation by individuals volunteering a portion or all of their time in behalf of a candidate or regional political party. It also includes the use of office space, facilities, equipment, and office supplies and other materials and fixtures voluntarily donated by other persons, or allowed their use for free, the monetary value of which shall be assessed based on market rates prevailing in a particular area.”³

(e) “*Campaign expenditure*” – refers to any type of expense incurred, regardless of source, amount, and purpose, that relates, directly or indirectly, to the conduct of an electoral campaign. It includes all payments of money or anything of value, or a contract, promise, or agreement to spend, for the purpose of influencing the results of the election. It includes the use of office space and facilities personally owned by the candidate, the monetary value of the use of which shall be assessed based on the market rates prevailing in a particular area;⁴

(f) “*Candidate*” – refers to any person aspiring for, or seeking an elective public office, by him/herself or as nominee of an accredited regional political party, aggrupation or coalition of parties and who has filed a certificate of candidacy or certificate of nomination and acceptance with the BEO;

(g) “*Disclosure Requirement*” – refers to the duty of all candidates, political parties, aggrupations or coalitions thereof to reveal the details of campaign contributions received by them, and the expenditures made on account thereof. For accredited national political parties, it includes expenditures and destinations of party development and campaign monies given to them as their share in the Political Party Subsidy Fund established under this Act;

(h) “*Fund*” – refers to the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund established under this Act;

**Section 5. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund.** – There is hereby established a Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund, which shall be used to augment the operating funds of accredited regional political parties. The fund shall be used directly and exclusively for the following purpose:

a) Party development activities; and
b) Campaign expenditures.

**Section 6. Allowable Party Development Activities** – The following party development activities shall be allowed to be funded out of the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund:

a) General assemblies, party conventions, and voter’s education programs mandated under Art. III, Sec. 10 of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code;
b) Party administration, recruitment and civic education;
c) Research and policy development;
d) Education and training of members;
e) Institution building and constituent outreach program; and
f) Other reasonable logistical and operational expenses that is essential in strengthening the party.

**Section 7. Allowable Campaign Expenditures.** – Only the following campaign expenditures shall be allowed to be funded out of the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund:

a) Operating expenses of the party, which may include the hiring of personnel, professional secretariat, setting up of campaign headquarters, and other relevant electoral expenditures;
b) Travelling expenses of the candidates and support personnel in the course of the campaign, and for personal expenses incident thereto;
c) Information dissemination and advocacy campaigns of the regional political party;
d) Production and distribution of electoral paraphernalia and other propaganda materials; and
e) Other expenditures under Section 102 of the Omnibus Election Code.

**Section 8. Accreditation.** – A regional political party accredited in accordance with Section 9 hereof, and which seeks to be a recipient of the subsidy provided for under this Act, may apply for accreditation by the Commission, under such rules and regulations that the Commission shall prescribe, consistent with the provisions of this Act.

**Section 9. Criteria for Eligibility.** – The Commission, through the BRAC, shall accredit regional political parties eligible to receive subsidy from the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund, based on the following general criteria:

a) Political representation, consisting of the incumbent member of Parliament, governors, vice-governors, mayors, and vice-mayors;

b) Organizational strength and mobilization capacity, which may include the identifiable political organizations and strengths as evidenced by their organized chapters; the number of political chapters, organizations region-wide, the number of active and permanent members of the party; and the number of incumbent elective officials belonging to them ninety (90) days before the date of elections.

c) Performance and track record of the party, which may include the established record of the parties (including any previous parties that have merged or coalesced into the new party), their performance in past elections as well as the ability of the party to field a slate of candidates from the municipal level up to the level of member of Parliament;

**Section 10. Distribution of the Fund.** – The total amount of Political Party Subsidy Fund released annually shall be distributed as follows:

a) Ten percent (10%) of the Fund shall accrue to the COMELEC to be used exclusively for purposes of monitoring and the conduct of information dissemination campaigns and voter’s education in the Bangsamoro;

b) Sixty (60%) of the Fund shall be proportionately and ratably distributed to accredited regional political parties represented in the Parliament as party or district representative, based on the number of seats obtained in the most recent regional elections;

c) Twenty-five (25%) of the Fund shall be proportionately and ratably distributed to accredited regional political parties based on the number of seats obtained in local elective positions for governor, sangguniang panlalawigan, city mayor, city vice-mayor, sangguniang panlungsod, municipal mayor, municipal vice-mayor, sangguniang bayan in the most recent regional elections; and

d) Five (5%) of the Fund shall be proportionately and ratably distributed to accredited regional sectoral party or organizations represented in the Parliament through sectoral representatives.

**Section 11. Schedule of Releases from the Fund.** – Releases of the fund shall be made not later than the first quarter of the year. The Commission through the Bangsamoro Electoral Office shall inform the accredited regional political parties of the schedule of releases as well as the amount of the subsidy allocated at the start of every fiscal year.

**Section 12. Management of the Subsidy.** – For purposes of this Act, all releases from the Political Party Subsidy Fund during a non-election year shall be used exclusively for mandatory activities under the Bangsamoro Electoral Code and party development activities.

Funds released during an election year shall be divided as follows: seventy-five percent (75%) shall be used for campaign expenditures and twenty-five percent (25%) shall be used for mandatory and party development activities.

No accredited regional political party shall be allowed to use the subsidy for purposes other than those indicated in this Act.

**Section 13. Financial Reporting and Accounting.** – The accredited regional political party availing of the subsidy shall maintain a separate financial account for the funds used to finance campaign activities and party development, respectively.

**Section 14. Audit of the Fund.** – The Commission on Audit (COA) shall examine the financial reports of the accredited regional political parties on their use of the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund. Voluntary contributions to any accredited regional political party shall be accounted for separately under a different set of books of accounts, which shall be open to inspection by the COA.

**Section 15. Full Disclosure.** – The officials of every accredited regional political party shall submit a sworn statement of their assets and liabilities to the Commission which shall be made available to the public. All accredited regional political parties and their candidates shall also be required to make a public disclosure of all contributions as well as expenditures incurred using the Political Party Subsidy Fund.

**Section 16. Failure to Comply with Disclosure and Reporting Requirements.** – Failure of the accredited regional political party to comply with the provisions of this Act shall result in its disqualification from receiving its share in the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund., which shall revert to the Bangsamoro Treasury and other rights and privileges to which it would have been entitled under this Act.

**Section 17. Performance Monitoring and Reporting System.** – The Commission and the COA shall jointly design and implement in consultation with political parties, aggrupations or coalitions thereof, accredited citizen’s arms, the private sector and non-government organizations and government agencies, an integrated political party development and campaign subsidy performance monitoring and reporting system. The performance and monitoring system shall identify, define and operationalize a system of performance indicators and measures for party development and campaign subsidy deployment.

The Commission and the COA shall, based on the results of the system, publish and disseminate annual reports on the development of political parties that have received subsidies and on the distribution, use, and results of the campaign subsidies provided to political parties.

**Section 18. Publication and Dissemination of Political Party Development and Campaign Subsidy Performance Reports.** – The political party development and campaign subsidy performance reports shall be submitted to the Parliament not later than June 30 of every year, and shall be made available to the public via the internet and mass media as a guide to the Bangsamoro electors in deciding to participate in the activities and in supporting, affiliating, or joining them.

**Section 19. Appropriations.** – The amount of one hundred million pesos (Php100,000,000) is hereby appropriated from the funds of the Bangsamoro treasury not otherwise appropriated, effective immediately upon approval of this Act. Every year, thereafter, there shall be appropriated the amount one hundred million pesos (Php100,000,000). Such amounts appropriated pursuant to this Act shall go to the Bangsamoro Political Party Subsidy Fund, which shall be administered by the Commission through the Bangsamoro Electoral Office.

**Section 20. Rules, Regulations, and Guidelines.** – The Commission shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations to effectively implement the provisions of this Act.

The Bangsamoro Electoral Office, with the approval of the COMELEC, and the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, shall promulgate guidelines to facilitate the release of funds to every accredited regional political party.

**Section 21. Applicability.** – The provisions of the Bangsamoro Electoral Code and other regional election laws not inconsistent with this Act shall be applied suppletorily.

Nothing herein shall be construed to exempt any regional political party from campaign finance reporting requirements under existing national laws and regulations issued by the COMELEC.

**Section 22. Separability Clause.** – If any part or provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional, the other parts or provisions thereof shall remain valid and effectives.

**Section 23. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in at least one (1) newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).
