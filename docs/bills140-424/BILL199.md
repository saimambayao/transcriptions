**AN ACT**
**ESTABLISHING A FRAMEWORK FOR THE PROMOTION AND RECOGNITION OF THE 'BANGSAMORO' BRAND, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**EXPLANATORY NOTE**

This measure builds upon the foundations set forth in the Bangsamoro Organic Law and aims to further enhance the economic and cultural opportunities afforded to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

Building on the principles enshrined in the BOL, this bill focuses on leveraging the unique identity and potential of the Bangsamoro community by promoting and internationalizing the "Bangsamoro" brand across various goods, services, and creative products.

By capitalizing on the cultural heritage and creative talents of the Bangsamoro people, the act seeks to facilitate economic growth, cultural exchange, and increased recognition of the Bangsamoro community both nationally and internationally.

This bill aims to establish a comprehensive strategy that includes measures to support marketing initiatives, quality standards, and intellectual property protection. By formulating market research and analysis, marketing and promotion campaigns, and participation in trade fairs and exhibitions, the act intends to showcase and market "Bangsamoro" products and services to a wider audience.

To ensure the quality and integrity of Bangsamoro products and services, the act mandates the establishment and enforcement of quality standards covering production, packaging, labeling, and distribution processes. Furthermore, intellectual property protection is prioritized to safeguard the Bangsamoro brand. The act provides for the registration and protection of trademarks, copyrights, patents, and geographical indications associated with "Bangsamoro" products and services.

Consumer awareness and education programs are integral to the act, as they aim to build trust and confidence among consumers in the authenticity, craftsmanship, and cultural significance of Bangsamoro products and services. The act also provides assistance for intellectual property registration and enforcement, ensuring that the intellectual property rights associated with the Bangsamoro brand are respected and protected domestically and internationally.

This bill, through its comprehensive provisions, aims to create a conducive environment for the promotion, internationalization, and protection of the Bangsamoro brand. It is our fervent hope that this act will lead to increased economic opportunities, cultural exchange, and the preservation of the rich heritage and identity of the Bangsamoro people.

**AN ACT**
**ESTABLISHING A FRAMEWORK FOR THE PROMOTION AND RECOGNITION OF THE 'BANGSAMORO' BRAND, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

**BE IT ENACTED** by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

**Section 1. Title.** This act shall be known as the *"Bangsamoro Brand Promotion and Internationalization Act of 2023."*

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to recognize the Bangsamoro people's aspiration for self-governance, socio-economic development, and the promotion and preservation of their cultural heritage. It shall promote domestic trade preference for goods produced and materials sourced from within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and adopt measures to increase its competitiveness. It shall likewise ensure that Bangsamoro products and services gain considerable access to the markets of its trading partners.

Towards this end, it shall promote and internationalize the "Bangsamoro" brand across various goods, services, and creative products through the institutionalization of support marketing initiatives, establish quality standards, and provide intellectual property protection to enable the "Bangsamoro" brand to gain prominence and contribute to economic growth and cultural exchange.

**Section 3. Definitions.** For the purpose of this act, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

(a) Bangsamoro - refers to the collective identity and culture of the Moro people in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
(b) Accredited Bangsamoro goods and services - refer to goods, services, and creative products that are produced, developed, or originated within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). These products and services may carry distinct characteristics, cultural elements, and craftsmanship that reflect the identity and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people.
(c) Enterprises - refer to duly registered business entities engaged in production, processing, and manufacturing products in the BARMM.
(d) Bangsamoro Brand - refers to the use of the registered “Seal of Excellence” logo and design, and the “Choose Bangsamoro” slogan or tagline on the accredited product itself or in the packaging of the same as an indication and attestation of its high quality and standard after proper accreditation, as provided in this Act.
(e) Goods - refer to any tangible product, including but not limited to agricultural produce, handicrafts, textiles, garments, and other manufactured items.
(f) Services - refer to any intangible product, including but not limited to tourism, hospitality, education, healthcare, and professional services.
(g) Creative Products - refer to artistic and cultural works, including but not limited to visual arts, literature, music, film, and performing arts.

**Section 4. Bangsamoro Brand Promotion and Internationalization Strategy.** The Bangsamoro Government, through the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT), and in coordination with relevant agencies and stakeholders, shall develop and implement a comprehensive strategy to enhance the visibility and recognition of "Bangsamoro" products and services in both the national and international communities. The strategy shall include, but not be limited to, the following measures:

A. Market Research and Analysis: Conduct research to identify potential markets and consumer preferences for "Bangsamoro" products and services.
B. Marketing and Promotion: Develop marketing campaigns, including digital and traditional advertising, to promote the "Bangsamoro" brand locally and internationally.
C. Trade Fairs and Exhibitions: Organize or participate in trade fairs, exhibitions, and other similar events to showcase and market "Bangsamoro" products and services.
D. Product Development and Innovation: Encourage the development of new and innovative "Bangsamoro" products and services to meet the changing demands of the market.
E. Training and Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity-building programs to enhance the skills and capabilities of Bangsamoro entrepreneurs and professionals in marketing, branding, and product development.

**Section 5. Quality Standards and Certification.** To ensure the quality and integrity of "Bangsamoro" products and services, the MTIT shall establish and enforce quality standards. These standards shall cover production, packaging, labeling, and distribution processes. The government shall also develop a certification system to validate compliance with these standards. Such products that meet or exceed the criteria and standards set forth by the Ministry shall be accredited and bear a “Seal of Excellence” logo and design in their packaging, advertisements, marketing, and sales activities.

For this purpose, it shall support the development and licensing of the Bangsamoro brand, including the creation of a unified visual identity and brand guidelines. The guidelines shall also outline the procedures and requirements for interested individuals, businesses, and organizations to obtain licenses and permissions to use the Bangsamoro brand, ensuring that the brand is utilized appropriately and in alignment with the values and aspirations of the Bangsamoro community.

**Section 6. Intellectual Property Protection.** The MTIT shall strengthen intellectual property protection for the Bangsamoro brand. This includes the registration and protection of trademarks, copyrights, patents, industrial designs, and geographical indications associated with "Bangsamoro" products and services. The government shall establish mechanisms to enforce intellectual property rights and prevent infringement or unauthorized use of the Bangsamoro brand.

Any individual or entity, printing establishment, advertising, and sales agency shall not use the Bangsamoro brand for product packaging without the permission and consent of the Ministry.

**Section 7. Incentives.** The Region shall give incentives to enterprises that are granted a “Seal of Excellence” by virtue of this Act. The same shall also be granted to local business establishments and organizations which advocate, promote, and patronize Bangsamoro goods and services. The MTIT shall formulate guidelines for developing incentive programs.

**Section 8. Promotion of “Bangsamoro First Policy”.** All agencies of the Bangsamoro Government as well as the private business sector and consumers are hereby enjoined to advocate and patronize accredited Bangsamoro goods and services. The Bangsamoro Government shall, as far as practicable, make use of Bangsamoro goods and services in the basket of goods as official gifts, tokens or souvenirs of the government for official missions abroad, for visiting dignitaries, and for participants in meetings, conventions, events or contests sponsored or co-sponsored by the government.

**Section 9. Market Access and Trade Facilitation.** The MTIT shall actively work to promote market access for accredited Bangsamoro" goods and services in domestic and international markets. It shall collaborate with relevant government agencies to streamline trade processes, reduce trade barriers, and ensure a favorable business environment for accredited Bangsamoro entrepreneurs and exporters. Efforts shall be made to negotiate favorable trade agreements that facilitate the export and import of accredited Bangsamoro goods and services.

**Section 10. Cultural Preservation and Heritage Promotion.** Recognizing the importance of cultural preservation, the Bangsamoro Government shall incorporate initiatives to promote and protect the cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people. These initiatives shall include supporting accredited Bangsamoro goods such as traditional crafts, indigenous knowledge systems, and cultural events that showcase the unique identity of the Bangsamoro community. The promotion of cultural tourism shall also be encouraged as a means to enhance the recognition and understanding of the Bangsamoro brand.

**Section 11. Monitoring and Evaluation.** The MTIT shall establish a monitoring and evaluation mechanism to assess the effectiveness and impact of the initiatives implemented under this Act. Regular reviews and assessments shall be conducted to measure progress, identify challenges, and recommend improvements. The findings of these evaluations shall be used to refine strategies and adjust policies accordingly.

**Section 12. Public Awareness and Participation.** The MTIT shall initiate public awareness campaigns to inform the Bangsamoro community and the general public about the objectives and benefits of this Act. It shall actively encourage the participation and involvement of stakeholders, including Bangsamoro entrepreneurs, industry associations, civil society organizations, and the general public, in the implementation and promotion of the Bangsamoro brand.

It shall promote consumer awareness and education programs to highlight the value and quality of Bangsamoro goods and services. These programs shall aim to build trust and confidence among consumers in the authenticity, craftsmanship, and cultural significance of accredited products and services carrying the Bangsamoro brand.

It shall collaborate with cultural and creative industries, including artists, designers, musicians, filmmakers, and other creative professionals, to promote the Bangsamoro brand through various artistic and cultural expressions. Support programs shall be established to foster collaboration, training, and the production of creative works that embody the spirit and identity of the Bangsamoro community.

**Section 13. Appropriations.** The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the budget of the MTIT in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act of the year following the enactment into law and thereafter.

**Section 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within ninety (90) days upon approval of this Act, the MTIT, in coordination with relevant agencies and stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of the provisions of this Act.

**Section 15. Separability Clause.** If any section or part of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 16. Repealing Clause.** All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders, or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 17. Effectivity.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
