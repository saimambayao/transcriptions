**CHAPTER I**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**ARTICLE 1**
**Title and General Policies**

**Section 1. Short Title.** – This Act shall be known and cited as “*The Bangsamoro Gender and Development Code*”.

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote and protect the equal rights of women and men and guarantee the full participation of women in all spheres of political, economic, social, and cultural life. The Bangsamoro Government recognizes that achieving gender equality, gender equity, and women empowerment are vital for the attainment of sustainable development, peace, and security in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It acknowledges the historical and persistent marginalization of women and girls and the need for comprehensive and transformative measures to address gender and development issues.

To achieve this end, the Bangsamoro Government and the constituent units of BARMM shall:

a. Uphold and protect the fundamental rights and equal opportunities for all in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region regardless of religion, ethnic origin, parentage, sex, or gender, including the right of women to engage in lawful employment, and to be protected from exploitation, abuse, or discrimination, as embodied in the Convention on the Elimination of all Forms of Discrimination Against Women.¹

b. Observe, promote, and ensure gender-responsiveness in all aspects of peace building and security in BARMM.² Strengthen the commitment to Women, Peace, and Security (WPS) by adhering to international standards and frameworks such as the United Nations Security Council Resolutions (UNSCRs) 1325 and 1820.³ Furthermore, commit to Youth, Peace, and Security (YPS) principles, recognizing the significant role of young people, particularly young women and men, in the promotion of peace and security.⁴

c. Ensure the reduction of the vulnerability of women, girls, and marginalized groups to climate change and variability.⁵

d. Ensure and support preservation and enrichment of culture, promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment among its residents, maintain peace and order, and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants.⁶

e. Promote the important role of women in nation-building and regional development, ensuring their representation in decision-making and policy-determining bodies of the Bangsamoro Government.⁷

f. Recognize the role of women in governance, ensure their fundamental equality before the law, and guarantee their full and direct participation in governance and development process, ensuring that women shall benefit equally in the implementation of development programs and projects.⁸

g. Ensure gender responsiveness in the budgeting, allocation, and utilization of public funds.

h. Identify and implement special development programs and laws for women and establish mechanisms for consultation with women and international and local communities to further ensure the allocation and proper utilization of gender and development funds⁹, including those from Official Development Assistance (ODA).

**Section 3. Scope of Application.** – This Code shall apply to the Bangsamoro Government and all its ministries, agencies, and offices (MAOs), all local government units (LGUs) within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), and all private entities, including corporations, cooperatives, and other for-profit enterprises, not-for-profit organizations (NPOs), non-governmental organizations (NGOs), civil society organizations (CSOs), people’s organizations (POs), and educational institutions operating within the BARMM.

This Code shall also apply to individuals residing within the BARMM regardless of their gender, age, ethnicity, religion, socioeconomic status, or any other characteristic.

**ARTICLE 2**
**Guiding Principles**

**Section 4. Guiding Principles from the Qur’an and the Sunnah.** – In adherence to the rich and diverse teachings of Islam, this Code shall aim to integrate and put into practice the following guiding principles:

a. Men and women are created from a single soul, emphasizing their essential equality and shared humanity.¹⁰

b. Each individual deserves dignity and respect regardless of their gender. All people shall be treated with fairness and compassion¹¹.

c. The sanctity and value of human life, highlighting that taking a life unjustly is akin to killing all of humanity, while saving a life is as if saving all of humanity. The importance of protecting humanity, promoting peace and justice, and condemning violence and mischief underscores the responsibility of every individual to uphold the sanctity and value of human life and contribute to the well-being and safety of the community. Furthermore, harming an innocent life, including violence against women and children, is condemnable.¹²

d. Men and women are assured of equal value, they are equal in responsibility and accountability for their actions and contributions, and they are equally deserving of forgiveness and rewards.¹³

e. Men and women have equal opportunity to earn rewards through righteous deeds and they are encouraged to have active and equitable participation in all spheres of life¹⁴.

f. The sanctity of marriage and the significance of kindness in marital relations highlights the importance of compassion, respect, and affection, fostering a nurturing and supportive family environment.¹⁵

g. Marriage is a sacred union that should be entered into with mutual consent and understanding, emphasizing the importance of equality and respect between partners.¹⁶

h. Parents, particularly mothers, play a vital role in the upbringing and development of children. They deserve love, respect, and appreciation for their sacrifices and contributions.¹⁷

i. Men and women are partners in life, including in seeking lawful provisions.¹⁸ Mutual support, cooperation, protection, and respect between men and women are essential in promoting a righteous, God-centered, benevolent, law-abiding, harmonious, and just society¹⁹.

j. It is the duty of a husband to provide for the necessities of his wife, highlighting the importance of responsibility and care within the family structure.²⁰ However, women have the right to own and manage their property independently, underscoring their economic independence and empowerment²¹.

k. The diversity and cooperation among men and women in their duties and roles in nation-building and development are recognized and form part of the strength of human society²².

**Section 5. Guiding Principles from International Commitments.** – In alignment with the global commitments to gender equality, gender equity, and women’s rights and empowerment, this Code shall integrate and practice the following guiding principles, among others:

a. Elimination of all forms of discrimination against women, ensuring equal rights in political, economic, social, cultural, civil, and other fields.²³

b. Prevention and elimination of violence and abuse against women in all its forms, including human trafficking and domestic violence and abuse.²⁴

c. Recognition of unique vulnerabilities and protection at all times of the rights of children, especially girls, and ensuring an environment conducive to their development.²⁵

d. Increased women’s participation in conflict prevention and resolution, peace processes and negotiations, peacebuilding, peacekeeping, humanitarian response, and post-conflict processes.²⁶

e. Active pursuit of women’s advancement and empowerment, including but not limited to areas such as education, employment, health, and political participation.²⁷

f. Adherence to and integration of the principles enshrined in the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), especially those directly pertaining to gender and women’s empowerment such as, but not limited to the following:

1. Ensure women's full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership at all levels of decision-making in political, economic, and public life. End all forms of discrimination against all women and girls everywhere, eliminate all forms of violence against all women and girls in the public and private spheres, including trafficking and sexual and other types of exploitation, and recognize and value unpaid care and domestic work.²⁸

2. Ensure women's access to sexual and reproductive healthcare services and integrate the needs of women and girls in regional and local health plans, policies, and strategies.²⁹

3. Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all, paying special attention to the elimination of gender disparities in education and ensuring equal access to all levels of education and vocational training.³⁰

4. Promote policies that support productive activities, decent job creation, entrepreneurship, creativity, and innovation, and encourage the formalization and growth of micro-, small- and medium-sized enterprises, including through access to financial services, ensuring women’s full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership.³¹

5. Empower and promote the social, economic, and political inclusion of all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, economic, or other status, and ensure equal opportunity and reduce inequalities of outcome, including through the elimination of discriminatory laws, policies, and practices.³²

6. Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels, ensuring responsive, inclusive, participatory, and representative decision-making at all levels³³.

7. Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership of the region for sustainable development, recognizing the role of women as vital agents in development.³⁴

**Section 6. Guiding Principles from Statutes.** – This Code upholds and complies with the principles embodied in the applicable provisions of the Philippine Constitution and Special Laws, such as, the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054), Code of Muslim Personal Law (PD 1083), Magna Carta of Women (RA 9710), Women in Development and Nation Building Act (RA 7192), Anti-Rape Law of 1997 (RA 8353), Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2003 (RA 9208 and RA 10364), Anti-Violence Against Women and Children Act of 2004 (RA 9262), Safe Spaces Act (RA 11313), Mental Health Act (RA. 11036) Sustainable Forest Management Act (MMA Act 161), ARMM GAD Code (MMA Act 280) and applicable provisions of Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Law for 2012 (RA 10354).

**Section 7. Guiding Principles from Local Customs and Traditions.** – In recognition of the rich cultural heritage and diverse traditions in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, this Code integrates and respects the guiding principles derived from local customs and traditions, provided they align with the fundamental values of human dignity, equality, equity, and justice. These principles include:

a. Respect for the cultural identity and heritage of all indigenous peoples and local communities within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, acknowledging their unique practices, beliefs, and traditions related to gender and development.

b. Recognition and promotion of traditional conflict resolution mechanisms that are in harmony with the principles of justice and gender equity, ensuring that women have significant participation and representation in these processes.

c. Preservation and promotion of traditional arts, crafts, and industries, particularly those led or predominantly participated in by women, as a means of labor, employment, economic empowerment, and cultural preservation.

d. Respect for traditional knowledge and practices, including the role of women in environmental conservation and sustainable development efforts.

e. Encouragement of intergenerational transfer of knowledge and skills, ensuring that women and girls have equitable opportunities to learn, preserve, and propagate cultural heritage and traditional crafts.

f. Promotion of traditional practices that uphold the dignity and rights of women and girls, actively discarding any customs that may be discriminatory or harmful to them.

g. Respect for the diverse religious practices and beliefs within the region, including the roles and duties of women and girls in such practices and beliefs.

h. Engagement with community elders, leaders, and women’s groups in policy-making processes to ensure that local customs and traditions are respectfully integrated and aligned with the objectives of this Code.

i. Encouragement of dialogue and education about gender equality, gender equity, and women empowerment within the context of local customs and traditions, fostering a culture of respect, understanding, and shared responsibility for gender and development.

**ARTICLE 3**
**Definitions**

**Section 8. Definition of Terms.** – As used in this Act, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

1) **Amanah** - is an Arabic term for trust and it refers to man’s stewardship of Allah’s creations.

2) **Arranged Marriage** - is basically a marriage arranged by someone other than the couples getting married. This is usually decided by the parents or an older family member.

3) **ARMM** - refers to the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

4) **Bangsamoro** – refers to those who, at the advent of the Spanish colonization, were considered natives or original inhabitants of Mindanao and the Sulu archipelago and its adjacent islands, whether of mixed or full blood, including their spouses and descendants.

5) **Bangsamoro GAD Resource Pool (BGRP)** - is composed of technical assistance providers with expertise on gender and development (GAD) and gender and development (GAD) mainstreaming.

6) **Children** - refers to those below eighteen (18) years of age or older but are incapable of taking care of themselves as defined under Republic Act No. 7610.

7) **Civil Society Organization** - refers to the autonomous membership-based, cause-oriented, or service-oriented not-for-profit organizations which share a common defining identity – social solidarity with the people in the society it serves or represents.

8) **Darul-Ifta** - refers to the collegial body of ulama and other scholars that renders expert opinion on matters of Islamic jurisprudence, especially on issues pertaining to the life and religious practice of Muslims. The jurisprudential opinion (ifta’) must be based on the primary sources of law (i.e., the Glorious Qur’an, the Hadith, Ijma and Qiyas) and the accepted views of the classical jurists and legal luminaries and experts.

9) **Development** - is the improvement of the equality of life of all regardless of age, sex, gender, tribe, race, creed, religion, and disability. It is characterized by an enrichment of Filipino indigenous resources, sustainable utilization of the natural resources of the country and freedom from dependency. It is therefore, sustained, equitable growth and balanced ecology.

10) **Persons with Disabilities** - refers to a person suffering from restriction or different abilities, as a result of a mental, physical, or sensory impairment to perform an activity in the manner or within the range considered normal for a human being.

11) **Discrimination Against Women** – refers to any sex or gender-based distinction, exclusion, or restriction which has the effect or purpose of impairing or nullifying the recognition, enjoyment or exercise by women, irrespective of their marital status, on a basis of equality of men and women, of human rights and fundamental freedoms in the political, economic, social, cultural, civil, or any other field. It includes any act or omission, including by law, policy, administrative measure, or practice, that directly or indirectly excludes or restricts women in the recognition and promotion of their rights and their access to and enjoyment of opportunities, benefits, or privileges, and when a measure or practice of general application fails to provide for mechanisms to offset or address sex or gender-based disadvantages or limitations as a result of which women are denied or restricted in the recognition and protection of their rights and their access to and enjoyment of opportunities, benefits, or privileges:

12) **Early Marriage** – is defined as all marriages contracted where one or both parties are below eighteen (18) years of age.

13) **Fatwa** - refers to the opinion based on Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh) rendered by the Bangsamoro mufti, the body of ulama, and experts on a given issue.

14) **Forced Marriage** - refers to the kind of marriage where the consent of either or both parties was obtained through force, violence, threat, intimidation or other forms of coercion which leverage one’s power or authority over another resulting in the latter acting against their own personal will or judgment.

15) **Gender** - is a socially constructed difference between men and women. Differences are created, partly through socialization and partly through positive and negative discrimination in the various institutions and structures of society.

16) **GAD** - is the acronym for Gender and Development which refers to the development perspective and process that are participatory and empowering, equitable, sustainable, free from violence, respectful of human rights, supportive of self-determination and actualization of human potential. It seeks to achieve gender equality as a fundamental value that should be reflected in development choices; seeks to transform society’s social, economic, and political structures and questions the validity of the gender roles they ascribed to women and men, boys and girls; contends that women and girls are active agents of development and not just passive recipients of development assistance; and stresses the need of women and children to organize themselves and participate in political processes to strengthen their legal rights.

17) **Gender Equality** - refers to the principle asserting the equality of men and women to enjoy equal conditions for realizing their full potentials and benefit from the results of development and with the State recognizing that all human beings are free and equal in dignity and rights.

18) **Gender Equity** - refers to the policies, instruments, programs, services and actions that address the disadvantaged position of women in the society by providing preferential treatments and affirmative actions. Such temporary special measures aimed at accelerating de facto equality between men and women shall not be considered discriminatory but shall in no way entail as a consequence the maintenance of unequal or separate standards. These measures shall be discontinued when the objectives of equality of opportunity and treatment have been achieved.

19) **Gender-Fair** - refers to any practice, policy or procedure giving equal treatment to an individual or a group.

20) **Gender Identity** - refers to the personal sense of identity as characterized, among others, by the manner of clothing, inclinations, and behavior in relation to masculine or feminine conventions. A person may have a masculine or feminine identity with the physiological characteristics of the opposite sex.

21) **Gender Mainstreaming** - refers to the strategy for making women and girl’s as well as men and boy’s concerns and experiences an integral dimension of the design, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of policies and programs in all political, economic and societal spheres so that women and men benefit equally and inequality is not perpetuated. It is the process of assessing the implications for women and men, boys and girls of any planned action, including legislation, policies, or programs in all areas and at all levels undertaken by responsible officers themselves as defined under RA 9710.

22) **Gender Responsive** - refers to laws, policies and procedures made that should be accommodating to people regardless of race, class, sex, gender, religious affiliation and cultural status and ethnicity.

23) **Halal** - is an Arabic term which means permissible. It refers to something allowed by Islamic doctrines (Qur’an, Hadith and Fatwa).

24) **Halaw** - a Malay term which refers to Filipino refugees or illegal migrants deported by the Malaysian immigration authorities and made to board in any vessel bound for the Southern Philippine Islands regardless of their point of debarkation.

25) **Haram** – is an Arabic term which means anything that is strictly forbidden in Islam or something that is not allowed by Islamic doctrines (Qur’an, Hadith and Fatwa).

26) **Hijab** - refers to the dress code (not too tight to reveal the contours of the body, the clothing material should not be transparent, and not too attractive to catch the attention of onlookers) required for Muslim women and girls covering the whole body except the face, the hands and feet.

27) **‘Idda** - the period of waiting prescribed for a woman whose marriage has been dissolved by death or by divorce the completion of which shall enable her to contract a new marriage.

28) **Indigenous People (IPs)** - refers to a group of people or homogenous societies identified by self-ascription and ascription by others, who have continuously lived as organized community on communally bounded and defined territory, and who have, under claims of ownership since time immemorial, occupied, possessed customs, traditions and other distinctive cultural traits, or who have, through resistance to political, social, and cultural inroads of colonization, non- indigenous religions and culture, became historically differentiated from the majority of Filipinos. IPs shall likewise include peoples who are regarded as indigenous on account of their descent from the populations which inhabited the country, at the time of conquest or colonization, or at the time of inroads of non-indigenous religions and cultures, or the establishment of present state boundaries, who retain some or all of their own social, economic, cultural and political institutions, but who may have been displaced from their traditional domains or who may have resettled outside their ancestral domains;

29) **Mahr** - is an Arabic term which refers to any gift or present which a groom is required to give to the bride. It is a requirement under Islamic law considered as a personal right of the bride. The bride’s right over the Mahr is imprescriptible and inalienable.

30) **Marginalization** - refers to a condition where a whole category of people is excluded from useful and meaningful participation in political, economic, social, and cultural life.

31) **Mental Health** - refers to a state of well-being in which the individual realizes one’s own abilities and potentials, scopes adequately with the normal stresses of life, displays resilience in the face of extreme life events, works productively and fruitfully, and is able to make a positive contribution to the community;

32) **Mental Health Service** - refer to psychosocial psychiatric or neurologic activities and programs along the whole range of the mental health support services including promotion, prevention, treatment, and aftercare, which are provided by mental health facilities and mental health professionals;

33) **Moro** - refers to native people who have historically inhabited Mindanao, Palawan, and Sulu, and who are largely of Islamic faith.

34) **Non-Government Organization** - refers to an intermediate agency or institution that tends to operate with a full-time staff complement and provide a wide range of services and programs and are non-profit.

35) **Overseas Bangsamoro Worker (OBW)** - refers to a person who is to be engaged, is engaged or has been engaged in a remunerated activity in a state of which he or she is not a citizen or on board a vessel navigating the foreign seas other than a government ship used for military or non-commercial purposes or on an installation located offshore or on the high seas; to be used interchangeably with Migrant Worker.

36) **Proxy Marriage** - is a marital union that follows after the approval of offer and acceptance to marry between parents and elders of the contracting parties wherein one or both the parties to be married are not present during the marriage ceremony.

37) **Psychosocial Program** - is an intervention using a system approach to the conditions of women as it views differentiated needs in a social context. Relevant programs, projects, and activities of partner agencies that involve the provision of psychosocial services, investigatory services or procedures, medical or hospital-based services, legal prosecution services, and other anti-VAWC services at the barangay, municipal, city, and provincial levels shall be further enhanced, strengthened and supported. Concerned LGUs shall document, monitor, and establish data banks on cases of child, early, and forced marriages and abuses against women and their effects.

38) **Reproductive Health** - refers to a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease and infirmity in all matters relating to the reproductive system and to its functions and processes. This implies that people are able to have a responsible, safe, consensual and satisfying sex life, that they have the capability to reproduce and the freedom to decide if, when, and how often to do so. This further implies that women and men attain equal relationships in matters related to sexual relations and reproduction.

39) **Senior Citizen** - refers to a person sixty (60) years of age and above.

40) **Sexual Harassment** - refers to a form of misconduct involving an act or series of unwelcomed sexual advances, requests for sexual favors or other verbal or physical behavior of a sexual nature which is made directly or indirectly. It is a form of misconduct involving an act or a series of unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical behavior of a sexual nature, made directly, indirectly or impliedly.

41) **Sexual Orientation** - refers to the direction of emotional sexual attraction or conduct. This can be towards people of the same sex (homosexual orientation) or towards people of both sexes (bisexual orientation) or towards people of the opposite sex (heterosexual orientation).

42) **Shari’a** - refers to all the ordinances and regulations governing Muslims as found principally in the Qur’an and the Hadith.

43) **Social Protection** - refers to policies and programs that seek to reduce poverty and vulnerability to risks and enhance the social status and rights of women, especially the marginalized by promoting and protecting livelihood and employment, protecting against hazards and sudden loss of income, and improving people’s capacity to manage risks. Its components are labor market programs, social insurance, social welfare, and social safety nets.

44) **Solo Parent** - refers to any person who falls under the category of a solo parent defined under Republic Act No. 8972, otherwise known as the “Solo Parents Welfare Act of 2000.”

45) **Stigma** - refers to the dynamic devaluation and dehumanization of an individual in the eyes of others which may be based on attributes that are arbitrarily defined by others as discreditable or unworthy and which result in discrimination when acted upon.

46) **Substantive Equality** - refers to the full and equal enjoyment of rights and freedoms contemplated under this Act which takes cognizance of existing inequalities with the view to achieving equitable outcomes for disadvantaged or marginalized peoples and groups.

47) **Sunnah** - refers to the oral traditions relating to the words and deeds of Prophet Mohammad (Peace be Upon Him) and is one of the main sources of Islamic Law (second in authority to the Qur’an). It is also a collection of writings that documented the sayings and actions of the Prophet Mohammad (PBUH).

48) **Survivor’s Support** - is an organized group of women to whom a woman-survivor of violence voluntarily agrees to establish a professional helping process. Survivor support groups shall offer a safe space for women to express emotions and find understanding after experiencing violence. They shall provide resources, peer counseling, and education on violence dynamics. These groups foster community, empowerment, and healing, guiding survivors towards reclaiming their lives and building resilience.

49) **Temporary Special Measure** - refers to a variety of legislative, executive, administrative, and regulatory instruments, policies, and practices aimed at accelerating this de facto equality of women in specific areas. These measures shall not be considered discriminatory but shall in no way entail as a consequence the maintenance of unequal or separate standards. They shall be discontinued when their objectives have been achieved.

50) **Voyeurism** - means the act of taking photo or video coverage of a person or group of persons performing sexual act or any similar activity or of capturing an image of the private area of a person or persons without the latter's consent, under circumstances in which such person/s has/have a reasonable expectation of privacy, or the act of selling, copying, reproducing, broadcasting, sharing, showing or exhibiting the photo or video coverage or recordings of such sexual act or similar activity through VCD/DVD, Internet, cellular phones and similar means or device without the written consent of the person/s involved, notwithstanding that consent to record or take photo or video coverage of same was given by such person/s.

51) **Wives in Subsequent Marriages** - refer to those married women whose marriage had been contracted after the first or succeeding marriages of the husband.

52) **Women in Especially Difficult Circumstances (WEDC)** - refer to victims and survivors of sexual and physical abuse, illegal recruitment, prostitution, trafficking, armed conflict, women in detention, victims and survivors of rape and incest, and such other related circumstances which have incapacitated them functionally.

53) **Women Empowerment** – refers to the provision, availability, and accessibility of opportunities, services and observance of human rights which enable women to actively participate and contribute to the political, economic, social, and cultural development of the nation as well as those which shall provide them equal access to ownership, management, and control of production, and of material and informational resources and benefits in the family, community, and society.

54) **Women in the Security Sector** - refers to women employed in the security sector, both in the major and technical services, who are performing combat or non-combat functions, providing security to the state, and protecting the people from various forms of threat. It also includes women trainees in all military training institutions.

55) **Young Adult** - refers to man or woman regardless of marital status whose age ranges from 18 to 35 years old.

**ARTICLE 4**
**Rights of Women**

**Section 9. Rights of Women.** – In addition to the rights of women already provided in Republic Act No. 9710 or the Magna Carta of Women, Presidential Decree (PD) 1083, and other Philippine laws and statutes, as well as those in international commitments where the Philippines is a signatory of, the following rights shall be upheld:

a. Women shall be recognized as equals of men in worth, dignity, and humanity, and shall be accorded respect, fair treatment, and equitable opportunities in all aspects of life. ³⁵

b. The protection of women’s life and dignity shall be paramount. The Bangsamoro Government and all constituent political units of BARMM shall safeguard them from all forms of violence and ensure their active involvement in fostering community peace, justice, and overall well-being.

**CHAPTER II**
**PROTECTION AND PREVENTION**

**ARTICLE 5**
**Prevention of Harm**

**Section 10. Orientation on Gender-Based Violence.** All ministries, offices, agencies (MAOs) and establishments or entities, government and private, in the BARMM shall conduct or sponsor, in coordination with the Bangsamoro Women Commission, the orientation on gender-based violence to their respective personnel. These orientations shall be comprehensive, regularly updated, and emphasize the promotion of gender equality and respect. Certificate of Compliance shall be submitted to the Bangsamoro Women Commission - Gender and Development (GAD) Focal Point System (GFPS).

**Section 11. Empowering Women in Peacebuilding.** The Bangsamoro Government shall build the capacities of women and girls to participate and lead in mediation, conflict prevention and resolution, and other peacebuilding-related skills.³⁶

**Section 12. Strengthening Local Safety Initiatives.** The Bangsamoro Government shall strengthen local protection and community policing mechanisms to respond to and prevent all forms of harm and threats coming from disasters, violent conflicts, gender-based violence, cyber-attacks, drug-related operations, and gender and race-related stigma and discrimination.³⁷

**Section 13. Strengthening Women-led Early Warning and Early Response (EWER) Systems³⁸.** The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the following:

a. Establishment of women-led, disaster-related community-based EWER systems;
b. Develop local models with EWER functionality checklist; and
c. Support installation of evacuation signage to households indicating presence of the elderly and persons with disabilities.

**ARTICLE 6**
**Protection Against Violence and Abuse**

**Section 14. Violence Against Women.** The Bangsamoro Government adheres to the provisions of RA 9262 and declares as unlawful the violence against women acts as enumerated therein.

In addition, violence against women shall include but is not limited to the following:

a. Physical, sexual, and psychological violence occurring in the family and other close relationships, including battering, sexual abuse of female children in the household, marital rape, and other traditional practices harmful to women, non-spousal violence and violence related to exploitation;

b. Physical, sexual, and psychological violence occurring within the general community, including rape, sexual abuse, sexual harassment and intimidation at work, in educational institutions, and elsewhere, trafficking in women, and prostitution;

c. Physical, sexual, and psychological violence perpetrated against women and condoned by the State, wherever it occurs;

d. Violations of the human rights of women in situations of armed conflict, in particular murder, physical and psychological torture, systematic rape, sexual slavery and forced pregnancy;

e. Forced sterilization and forced abortion, coercive/forced use of contraceptives, prenatal sex selection and female infanticide;

f. To buy or sell a woman or any of her body parts for profit.

**Section 15. Sexual Violence.** The Bangsamoro Government declares the following acts as unlawful. Sexual abuse shall include, but is not limited to, the following:

a. Rape as defined and penalized under Article 266-A of the Revised Penal Code (RPC), further amended by Republic Act 11648.

b. When a person subjects a woman to have sexual intercourse with an animal under any of the circumstances stated in paragraph I or through any abnormal, unusual or ignominious sexual act; and

c. Any intentional contact with use of force, threat, intimidation, and coercion, however slight, of one’s penis to the genitals, mouth, breast or anus of a woman or intentional contact thereof, however slight, to any part of her body other than her sexual organ, or the use of any instrument or object which intentionally touches the genitals, breast, or anus of a woman.

**Section 16. Reporting cases of VAW to Relevant Government Agencies by Traditional Leaders.** All cases of VAW reported/referred to the Traditional leaders, such as Ulama (or Imam), Sultan, Bai-a-labi, Datus, and other community elders, shall report cases of violence against women and children to relevant government agencies.

**Section 17. Trafficking in Women Defined.** It refers to a covert or overt recruitment of women into the sex trade industry. It includes new forms of sexual exploitation such as sex tourism, the recruitment of domestic labor to work in developed countries, and organized marriages between women from developing countries and foreign nationals and promoting or initiating a system in which women become movable properties and objects of exchange.

Trafficking in women shall include the following, but is not limited to:

a. For any person’s association, cult, religion or organization or similar entities to commit the following acts:
i. Establish or carry on a business for the purpose of matching women for marriage to foreigner either on a mail order basis or through personal introduction or cyberspace;
ii. Advertise, publish, print or distribute, or cause the advertisement, publication, printing or distribution of any brochure, flyer or propaganda material calculated to promote the above-mentioned prohibited acts for profit or advantage;
iii. Solicit, enlist or attack/induce any woman to join any club, association or organization whose objective is to match women for marriage to foreigners either on a mail-order basis or through personal introduction or cyberspace or any forms which facilitate the act of solicitation;
iv. Use the postal services, cyberspace or satellite TV to promote the above-mentioned prohibited acts;
v. Inclusion of massage parlors and videoke rooms in the list of entertainment facilities that allow, attract or entice prostitution are prohibited.

b. To buy or sell a woman, or any of her body parts for profit;

c. To use her body by any pretext; including to be used for experiments, research or the like without her consent;

d. To act as a procurer of a sex worker; and

e. To threaten or use violence and force a woman to become a mail-order bride.

Trafficking in women shall be unlawful and punishable as prescribed in Republic Act No. 9208 and 10364.

**Section 18. Mail Order Bride Defined.** It is a practice where a woman establishes personal relations with a male and female foreign national via mail or similar means upon recruitment by an individual or agency for the purpose of exploiting women in the guise of marriage.

This practice is a form of violence against women and shall be unlawful and punishable under Republic Act No. 6955³⁹.

**Section 19. Sexual Harassment Defined.** It is a form of misconduct involving an act or a series of unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical behavior of a sexual nature, made directly, indirectly or impliedly.

Sexual harassment shall be unlawful in public spaces, online, employment, education, or training environment as prescribed in Republic Act No. 7877 and RA 11313.

**Section 20. Other Forms of Sexual Violence.** Other than the forms of violence described above, sexual violence also include those covered by the Revised Penal Code such as Acts of Lasciviousness.

**Section 21. Pedophilia Defined.** It is a form of sexual perversion where children are the preferred victims under the following circumstances:

a) When the offender shall engage in sexual activity, or groom her with the aim of having sexual activity, including for example sexual intercourse with a girl; or

b) When the offender shall engage in sexual activity or groom him or her with the aim of having sexual activity, including for example anal intercourse with a boy or girl.

**Section 22. Forced Marriage.** No person shall be forced to marry on the basis of vitiated consent. The solemnization of marriage shall require the free and prior informed consent of both parties to the marriage. A marriage that is solemnized through the use of coercion, force or intimidation or without the permission of one or both parties, shall be deemed as a form of violence against women and children, especially if one or both of the parties involved are minors.

The free and voluntary consent of both parties to a marriage, who reached the age of majority, shall be deemed as part and parcel of the Islamic principle of *al-ijab wal qabul* (offer and acceptance) which is an essential requisite to all legal and legitimate *nikah* (marriage).

Furthermore, this Code defines parties to a marriage as referring to the individuals to be wed. While the importance of the role of the *wali* to a valid *nikah* under Islamic law is recognized, the approval of the *wali* or legal guardian to a marriage does not preclude the need to ascertain that the individuals themselves who are to be wed have given their free and informed consent prior to the solemnization of a marriage.

Any person, agency, or representative committing fraudulent or coercive acts to cause and effect a forced marriage shall be penalized following this Code and other pertinent laws. Furthermore, such marriage solemnized through vitiated consent of one or both parties shall be deemed null and void.

**Section 23. Advocacy on Protection from and Prevention of Child or Early Marriage.** Child or early marriages shall be discouraged. The Bangsamoro Government agencies, and local government units shall conduct strong advocacy campaign on the protection from and prevention of child marriage.

Non-government, and civil society organizations are encouraged to develop programs and advocacies that will support the protection against child and early marriages.

**Section 24. VAW Survivor Support Group.** The Bangsamoro Government through the BWC shall coordinate and partner with an organized group of women to provide resources, peer counseling, and education on violence dynamics. These groups foster community, empowerment, and healing, guiding survivors towards reclaiming their lives and building resilience.

**Section 25. Women and Children Protection Desks.** The Bangsamoro Government through the Police Regional Office in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall maintain and strengthen Women and Children Protection Desks (WCPDs) to specifically handle concerns of women and children and investigate their cases. They shall be staffed by gender-sensitive women police officers. Toward this end, the PNP Police Regional Office - BAR in coordination with the LGUs and BWC shall ensure that the police personnel shall be provided with gender-responsive trainings and services. Moreover, LGUs shall support the operations of WCPDs.

There shall be a separate WCPD area in all police stations to safeguard the confidentiality of the identity and information divulged by the survivors and to ensure the latter’s protection from any harm or danger.

**Section 26. Barangay VAW Desks.** The Bangsamoro Government, through the MILG shall mandate all LGUs to establish a functional VAW Desk in every barangay to ensure that VAW cases are fully addressed in a gender-responsive manner.⁴⁰

The VAW desk shall have a separate room where the intake interview can be conducted (if no separate room is available, an enclosed area within the vicinity where the barangay hall is located).

**Section 27. Protection of Service Providers in Violence Against Women and their Children and Trafficking Cases.** Service providers assisting violence against women and their children (VAWC) and trafficking cases shall be accorded protection from imminent threat and danger while in the exercise of their official functions.

**Section 28. Establishment of Rape Crisis Center.** – The Bangsamoro Government through MSSD, MILG, MOH, and BHRC shall establish a Rape Crisis Center in every province and city located within their government hospitals, health clinics, or other suitable facilities to provide comprehensive support for the purpose pursuant to Republic Act 8505.

**ARTICLE 8**
**Protection Against Exploitation and Derogatory Acts**

**Section 29. Online Sexual Harassment Defined**– Online sexual harassment as defined in Republic Act 11313 shall be unlawful.

All employment, educational and training institutions are mandated to enact its Code of Conduct and constitute its committee on decorum and investigation.

Local Government Units are mandated to pass ordinances that will localize the Safe Spaces Act.

**Section 30. Prohibition of Printing, Publication, Display, and Distribution of Pornographic Scenes on Movie/TV Trailers/Shows, Posters, Billboards and Other Materials and Similar Literature.** The Bangsamoro Government and LGUs shall exercise vigilance in the unwarranted printing, publication and display of pornographic and sensationalized scenes offensive to human sensitivities. It shall be unlawful for any person to possess, print, publish, display or distribute or cause the printing, publication, display, or distribution of obscene or pornographic scenes on movie/TV trailers/shows, posters, billboards, literature and other visual materials which treat women as sex objects and commodities, including the following derogatory portrayals that debase or tends to debase the value and integrity of women and is considered contrary to public morals:

1. **Pornographic and Indecent Shows.** Indecent shows as used in this Code include nude or other provocative gestures which further project and exhibit women as sex objects. Any person or agency who organizes, funds, or markets shows depicting women as sex objects in private or in public places shall violate women’s rights;

2. **Live shows.** Nude shows and other provocative gestures that further project and exhibit individuals as sex objects are considered haram. It shall be a violation of women’s rights to influence or force a woman or girl-child to dance or do naked shows in public or private places for commercial or entertainment purposes; and

3. **Protection of Women Deprived of Liberty (WDL).** In recognition of the vulnerability of WDLs, the Bangsamoro Government in coordination with Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP) and other government agencies concerned shall ensure that WDLs are not used, exploited, or exposed to any form of obscene or pornographic contents, displays, and publications. Any act of voyeurism and other acts that violate the privacy of vulnerable women deprived of liberty in detention facilities shall also be prohibited.

Any person, who produces, organizes, funds, markets, or promotes shows, motion pictures, television programs and publicity materials depicting individuals as sex objects for distribution, television broadcast, or viewing in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be penalized following this Code and other applicable laws.

The Bangsamoro Government, through its Intergovernmental Relations Body, shall coordinate with the National Government for its representation in the Movie and Television Review and Classification Board (MTRCB) for purposes of compliance with the provisions of this Code.

**Section 31. Gender-Sensitive Physical Plan.** A physical plan appropriate for a gender-sensitive environment shall be adopted by all ministries, offices, agencies and establishments or companies which shall help prevent sexual harassment, sexual abuse and other forms of maltreatment in the workplaces. These plans should incorporate features that address the different needs and vulnerabilities of all genders.

**Section 32. Prohibition of Objectification and Indecent Exposure of Women in Showcases and Competitions.** Any form of showcasing, pageantry, and competitions that have the potential to commodify, abuse, humiliate, or treat women as objects of sexualization is expressly prohibited within schools, communities, and barangays in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Moreover, contests that involve the indecent exposure of a woman's body, deemed offensive to morals and societal decorum, are identified as acts of violence against women and are explicitly prohibited. Specifically:

a) The wearing of costumes or attires that expose sensitive areas of a woman’s body to public view (awrat), including but not limited to parts of the breasts, thighs, back, or other revealing clothing such as swimsuits, gowns with plunging necklines, or high slits, is strictly prohibited.

b) Engaging in indecent activities is likewise prohibited, aiming to maintain a standard of decency and respect within the cultural and social context of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Additionally, institutions within BARMM are expressly forbidden from engaging in or endorsing any manner of showcasing, pageantry, and/or competitions beyond the prohibitions of this Code, and beyond the territorial jurisdiction of BARMM, unless the participant is a non-Muslim and the sponsoring institution is a Catholic school, in the context of educational institutions.
On the other hand, the projection of women's strengths and potentials, especially with regard to indigenous and culturally relevant forms which are deemed appropriate shall be encouraged. The following may be allowed but strictly regulated:

a) Contests showcasing only the participant’s talent, intelligence, local culture and traditions; and
b) Contests showcasing local traditional costumes as part of promoting local culture and tourism.

**ARTICLE 9**
**Women Friendly Spaces**

**Section 33. Facilities and Support Systems for all Employees.** The Bangsamoro Government shall establish policy standards for gender-specific health and occupational safety that will ensure the security, safety, and health of workers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. In appropriate cases, employers shall:

a) Establish separate toilet rooms, lavatories, and lounges for men and women and provide at least a dressing room for women;

b) All government and private establishments in the BARMM shall provide a private area or room designated and equipped for the purpose of breastfeeding or expressing milk for infants. The lactation stations must have all the amenities and equipment required, the specifications of which will be established by the MOH. The MOLE shall include the establishment of a lactation facility in the evaluation of the labor standard facilities of all government and private establishments in the BARMM;

c) Establish homecare centers in the workplace to temporarily cater to breastfeeding, child-rearing, and early childhood care for children of working parents while on their respective jobs; and

d) Establish a prayer room for women in all offices both at the Regional, Provincial, and Municipal levels to perform religious obligations.

**Section 34. Wearing of Hijab Among Muslim Women.** Public and private institutions such as schools, hospitals, offices, correctional facilities, prisons and other institutions within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall respect and allow Muslim women to wear hijab, including Muslim women uniformed personnel in the Philippine National Police (PNP), the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP), Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP), Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP), Philippine Coast Guard (PCG), and private security services, among others. A resolution or memorandum shall be issued by the Bangsamoro Parliament or the Office of the Chief Minister to ensure that institutions, entities, establishments, and organizations do not discriminate against the use of hijab and shall be seen as part of every citizen’s freedom of religion and expression.

**ARTICLE 10**
**Women, Peace and Security**

**Section 35. Protection of Women and Children Affected by Armed Conflict, and Non-Traditional Security Issues.** During conflict and post-conflict settings. The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the protection and security of women and girls; men and boys; persons with disabilities; and the elderly in situations of armed conflict, militarization, and non-traditional security issues, adopting strategies of the NAPWPS and BRAPWPS.

**Section 36. Post-Conflict Rehabilitation and Reconstruction.** The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that women, children, senior citizens, and persons with disability, are properly addressed and prioritized during post-conflict rehabilitation. It shall provide programs, projects, and support systems that may enable women to speedily recover from debilitating effects of conflicts. Ensuring that all responses and strategies are gender-responsive considering the specific needs, priorities, and capacities of women and children.

**Section 37. Psychosocial Support for Women.** The Bangsamoro Government shall establish a mental health and psychosocial support team that will cater to the needs of women experiencing critical incidents such as armed conflict and natural disasters, strengthened in consultation with the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC), Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD), Ministry of Health (MOH), and the Bangsamoro Darul Ifta’ (BDI).

Psychosocial sessions shall also be initiated and spearheaded by the MOH and MSSD in partnership with trained debriefers coming from non-government organizations (NGOs) within the BARMM.

Early recovery plans shall be implemented in conflict affected communities. The MOH shall regularly submit a post debriefing report to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM) to determine indicators of program implementation.

**ARTICLE 11**
**Other Measures on the Protection of Special Sectoral Concerns**

**Section 38. Promoting Respect and Unity.** The Bangsamoro Government shall acknowledge the importance of respecting individual identity in line with Bangsamoro traditions and values. It shall emphasize the collective responsibility to maintain a respectful and dignified environment, ensuring every community member is valued and respected. By upholding these principles, we reinforce our commitment to social harmony and unity, which are central to our cultural ethos.

**Section 39. Assistance to Individuals in Need of Special Protection.** LGUs shall provide necessary assistance to individuals in BARMM. This includes access to prayer facilities, religious disability, orphans, mentally-ill patients, substance dependents, alcoholic, rehabilitated hessonites, released prisoners, orphans, and victims of armed conflict and war.

**Section 40. Support for Women Deprived of Liberty.** Women inmates and detainees and their rights shall be protected. It shall be the responsibility of the Bangsamoro Government, through Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC), to ensure the following:

1. Establish an independent oversight body that is responsible for monitoring and ensuring the compliance of correctional facilities in BARMM. This oversight body should have the authority to investigate complaints, conduct regular inspections, and impose sanctions for violations of the rights of WDL.

2. Collaborate with relevant BARMM Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (MAOs) to develop and implement gender-responsive policies and programs specifically tailored to the needs and situation of WDL in BARMM. This includes:

a) Ensuring that Bangsamoro women have the freedom and resources to practice their religion while in detention. This includes access to prayer facilities, religious materials, and the ability to observe religious rituals, including holding regular Naseehat (symposiums).

b) Providing specialized healthcare services tailored to the unique health needs of women. This encompasses gynecological care, prenatal and postnatal care, family planning, and other gender-specific health services. Each provincial jail within BARMM shall hire a health worker to ensure the provision of adequate healthcare services for WDL.

c) Offering comprehensive mental health support, including counseling, therapy, and access to psychiatric care. Addressing mental health needs is crucial for the overall well-being and rehabilitation of women in detention.

d) Implementing structured rehabilitation programs that focus on addressing the underlying causes of their detention. This can include addiction treatment, anger management, vocational training, and educational programs.

e) To facilitate the speedy disposition of cases of women inmates and detainees, the Bangsamoro government shall ensure that women have access to legal representation and assistance. This includes legal advice, assistance with court proceedings, and help with any legal issues they may face during their time in detention.

f) Creating a safe and secure environment within the detention center to prevent violence, abuse, and harassment, ensuring the safety of both WDL and women staff. This includes:
i) Providing separate facilities/rooms for males and females for their protection while on rehabilitation.
ii) Coordinating with the PNP in ensuring the separate structure and friendly spaces for detention and rehabilitation of women inmates and detainees;
iii) Assigning of women jail guards to women inmates and detainees; and
iv) Ensuring that women inmates and detainees have a separate detention cell at municipal police stations.

g) Providing assistance in finding access to basic necessities such as food, modest clothing (e.g. Islamic clothing for Muslims), and hygiene supplies to ensure a smooth transition into the community.

h) Ensuring that WDL shall have the right to vote and actively participate in the political process. Their entitlement to engage in elections, express their political views, and contribute to decisions affecting their lives shall be safeguarded without prejudice. The correctional authority authorities shall facilitate and ensure the exercise of their voting rights, promoting inclusive political participation within the confines of the law.

i) Providing comprehensive and specialized training for correctional staff on gender sensitivity, women's rights, and the unique needs of WDL.

j) Designating of gender-focused social workers from the Bangsamoro Women Commission to regularly monitor the conditions and treatment of WDL.

In Preparation for Re-Integration, the following programs may be implemented:

a) Provide comprehensive programs to assist women in finding sustainable employment or developing skills for self-employment. This includes job placement services, vocational training, and entrepreneurship development.

b) Facilitate the process of reconnecting women with their families and rebuilding healthy relationships. This may involve family visitation, family counseling, communication workshops, and assistance in creating a stable home environment.

c) Offer opportunities for further education, skills upgrading, or vocational training to enhance qualifications for employment and economic stability upon release.

d) Establish connections with community organizations, support groups, and mentors who can provide ongoing assistance, guidance, and a sense of belonging in the community;

The Bangsamoro Government shall collaborate with the National Government on various fronts, including the Inter-Governmental Relations Body (IGRB) discussions concerning the situation of Women Deprived of Liberty (WDL), encompassing health, infrastructure, policies, among others. Additionally, the Bangsamoro government shall adopt existing policies of the BARMM for WDL outside the Regional Territory.

**Section 41. Reporting of Cases of Harassment Committed Against Persons with Disability.** The Bangsamoro Government, through the Ministry of Interior and Local Government, shall require all LGUs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to monitor and report cases of harassment committed against persons with disability.

**CHAPTER III**
**WELFARE PROVISIONS**

**ARTICLE 12**
**Education of Women**

**Section 42. Provision of Quality Education.** The Bangsamoro Government shall enact laws to establish, maintain, and support quality and balanced education with an institutionalized set of standards of curricula, structure, and secured environment responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations, as well as the support for the professional development and continuous monitoring and evaluation of teachers and schools.

All State Universities and Colleges (SUCs) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall, including schools and institutions such as the Madaris and Madrasahs, be deemed integral components of the regional educational system. The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the accessibility of children, adolescents, and young adults to quality and balanced education.

The Bangsamoro Government shall promote the use of gender-sensitive and gender-fair language in school textbooks, contexts, and materials at all levels.

**Section 43. Promotion of Gender-Responsive School Infrastructure.** The Bangsamoro Government ensures that the school infrastructure, such as bathrooms and classrooms, promotes a gender-responsive, conducive, and positive learning environment in the education system.

**Section 44. Gender-responsive Distance Learning Program.** The Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) and the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) in coordination with the BWC, shall institutionalize gender-responsive distance learning programs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 45. Integration of Women, Adolescent, and Youth Health and Nutrition in the Curriculum.** All educational institutions in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao shall integrate a comprehensive Women, Adolescent, and Youth Health and Nutrition program into their curricula.

The educational institutions shall develop gender-sensitive curricula and policies as allowed.

This program should address the specific physical, mental, and social health needs of women, adolescents, and youth within the context of Islam, adhering to Halal guidelines.

**Section 46. Monitoring and Reporting of Stereotyped Portrayal of Gender Roles in Educational Materials.** The MBHTE in partnership with other educational institutions in the BARMM shall develop, formulate, and provide effective mechanisms for monitoring and reporting of educational materials that stereotype and discriminate against any individual or group of people. Any educational materials included in the teaching curricula that unfairly discriminate or stereotype individuals or groups of people on account of their sex, gender, ethnicity, disability, religion, or creed or those assign stereotypical gender roles that demonstrate social constructs that limit possibilities for young learners into a gendered gap, shall be promptly recalled and its distribution halted.

MBHTE shall periodically review the educational materials to ensure the non-discriminatory or stereotype portrayal of any group of people, especially women.

**Section 47. Scholarship Programs.** Educational institutions through their respective extension service programs shall forge partnerships with the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), Ministry of Health (MOH), and other stakeholders for the provision of equal access to scholarship grants especially for technical education and livelihood skills development training for women, out-of-school youths, and other marginalized women sectors including women with disabilities, indigenous people (IP), solo parents, women former combatants, and widows of war. A specific quota shall be provided in the implementation of the scholarship program.

Ministries, Agencies, and Offices (M/A/Os), LGUs, and the BARMM Security Sectors are encouraged to provide scholarship grants annually to its personnel and Women, Peace, and Security (WPS) advocates to the diploma course on WPS as part of their career development.

**Section 48. Promotion of Gender-Responsive Counseling.** The schools in the BARMM shall actively promote gender-responsive professional school counseling and career education programs to encourage individuals to pursue academic and technical courses to widen their future career opportunities.

**Section 49. Access to Science and Women-Friendly Alternative Technology Education.** The Bangsamoro Government in coordination with State Universities and Colleges (SUCs) and private Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) shall strengthen courses on technology development education. Towards this end, these institutions shall establish a science and women-friendly alternative technology center for rural women in each municipality or city in support to strengthen technology development education. These centers shall promote gender equality, women’s empowerment, and access to technologies that are responsive to the needs of women.

**ARTICLE 13**
**Health of Women**

**Section 50. Right to Health.** The Bangsamoro Government shall respect and promote a rights-based approach to women and men’s health byconsidering their respective specific realities and health needs at every stage in their lives. It shall also promote the couple’s and/or spouse’s shared obligation for childbirth and childcare.⁴¹

**Section 51. Budget For Women’s Health.** A substantial portion of the health budget determined by the Bangsamoro Government shall be allocated for women’s health, services, facilities and staffing.

**Section 52. Upgrading of the Health Care Delivery System.** Quality health care and services that are not discriminatory on account of their gender, age, sex, religion, and ethnicity shall be implemented by the Bangsamoro Government, especially in the geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDA).

**Section 53. Reproductive Health and Reproductive Health Care Services.** The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the availability and accessibility of methods, techniques, and services that contribute to reproductive health and individual well-being. The elements of reproductive health care shall include the following:

a. Reproductive Health and perspectives from Islam, IP traditions, and Community Settlers’ norms;
b. Family planning programs and services of Muslim, IP, and community settlers’ societies;
c. Maternal and Child Health and Nutrition, including breastfeeding;
d. Proscription of abortion and management of abortion complications;
e. Adolescent and youth reproductive health guidance and counseling;
f. Prevention, treatment, and management of reproductive tract infections (RTIs), HIV and AIDS, and other sexually transmittable infections (STIs);
g. Elimination of violence against women and children and other forms of sexual and gender-based violence;
h. Culturally-sensitive and appropriate education and counseling on sexuality and reproductive health;
i. Treatment of breast and reproductive tract cancers and other gynecological conditions and disorders;
j. Male responsibility and involvement and men’s reproductive health;
k. Prevention, treatment, and management of infertility and sexual dysfunction;
l. Mental health aspect of reproductive health care.

**Section 54. Women’s Right Over Their Bodies.** Women’s decision to attain healthy sexual development and achieve their reproductive intention shall be given appropriate support and guidance by all health professionals. Provision of accurate and appropriate information on these matters and corresponding services shall be made publicly available.

**Section 55. Community-Based Primary Health Care Service.** Primary health care is a comprehensive approach to health, focusing on prevention, treatment, and empowerment across all ages. It strengthens healthcare systems by providing essential services closer to home, promoting healthy lifestyles, and ensuring smooth referrals to specialists when needed. This goes beyond just building hospitals and encompasses broader social factors that impact health, ultimately aiming to empower individuals and communities to live healthier lives.

The Bangsamoro Government shall provide, at all times, equitable, accessible, and community-based comprehensive primary health care services. These health programs and services shall be culture-sensitive and gender-responsive covering all stages of the human life cycle. The programs shall also address the significant causes of mortality and morbidity among men and women of all ages, including that of the unborn child. The provision of comprehensive health services must be parallel to the Islamic beliefs and cultural practices that are non-discriminatory to women. The rights of the spouses to establish a family following their convictions and demands of responsible parenthood, as well as the rights of women to protection from unsafe practices, drugs, devices, interventions, and substances, shall be thereto respected.⁴²

The Bangsamoro government shall strengthen and ensure availability and access to primary health care services already existing in the BARMM LGUs. It shall ensure support to the availability of personnel, capacitating personnel, and the provision of funds therefor.

**Section 56. Health Care Financing Mechanisms.** The Bangsamoro Government shall set up appropriate healthcare financing mechanisms through health insurance coverage. It shall enter into an agreement with the provincial and municipal governments, the Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth), and the MOH for the enrolment of indigents especially women and children to any available healthcare packages such as hospitalization, maternal and child-care, tuberculosis and other contagious diseases treatment packages. Where the head of household has not made appropriate arrangements, a safety net for his or her dependents will be available to provide basic and emergency health care.

**Section 57. Partnership in Family Planning Services.** The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage and promote partnership on family planning and reproductive health program implementation with relevant stakeholders.

**Section 58. Institutionalization of the Fatwa on Health.** The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage the inclusion of fatwa on health programs and projects implemented in the region issued by the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’.

**Section 59. Mobile Health Care Delivery Services.** There shall be a monthly mobile maternal and health care delivery service through a mobile health clinic in all cities and municipalities of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region for their constituents, especially women and those in the marginalized sectors. Towards this end, each municipality on the mainland shall provide a van and motorized boat for the island municipality to promptly attend to the needy patients.

The MOH, in coordination with the Integrated Provincial Health Offices (IPHOs), shall provide the human resource requirement of the program. The concerned LGUs shall be responsible for the implementation, maintenance and sustainability of the program in their respective jurisdictions. It shall also be responsible for ensuring proper information dissemination to all residents, particularly the indigenous people’s communities.

**Section 60. Conduct of Maternal and Neonatal Mortality and Morbidity Review.** All LGUs shall formulate their respective ordinances for the regular reporting of maternal and neonatal mortality and morbidity indices. Annual maternal and infant death reviews shall be conducted which shall be attended by the concerned agencies such as the Ministry of Health (MOH), Bangsamoro Population and Development Office (BPDO), Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD), the Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG), Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) - BARMM, the Barangay Local Government Units (BLGUs), Municipal Local Government Units (MLGUs), and provincial government with the purpose of formulating plans, programs, and projects to target a ten percent (10%) yearly decrease in maternal and infant mortality and morbidity.

**Section 61. Leave Benefits.** In addition to those provided under existing laws, women employees who have rendered continuous aggregate employment service of at least six (6) months for the last twelve (12) months shall be entitled to a special leave benefit of a maximum of two (2) months leave with full pay based on her gross monthly compensation following surgery caused by gynecological disorders as provided under Republic Act No. 9710 otherwise known as the Magna Carta of Women.

**Section 62. Comprehensive Information, Education, and Campaign Materials on Health.** The Bangsamoro Government shall provide inclusive, appropriate, and accurate information and education on all aspects of women’s health.

**Section 63. Integration of Gender-Inclusive Mental Health and Mental Health Services.** The Bangsamoro Government shall provide and ensure accessible, efficient and gender-inclusive mental health programs and services, including psychological interventions and support through the MOH, MSSD, and other concerned institutions.

In addition, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that mental health and psychosocial support (MHPSS) policies are integrated into the broader workforce and GAD policies. These policies shall not only address mental health but also promote gender equality.

**ARTICLE 14**
**Social Services for Women**

**Section 64. Comprehensive Support to Women in Especially Difficult Circumstances.** All Provinces in the Bangsamoro Government shall provide comprehensive support to survivors of violence irrespective of gender, age, religion, affiliation, or ethnicity. Women in Especially Difficult Circumstances (WEDCs) shall be provided with the necessary services and interventions stipulated under Chapter V, Section 31 of RA 9710.

Facilities providing necessary interventions to survivors of violence as well as programs directly benefiting the clients shall be actively supported by the Bangsamoro Government. These facilities will include Crisis Intervention Units (CIUs) by the MSSD, and VAWC centers by the MSSD and LGUs, the Women and Children Protection Desk (WCPD’s) by the PNP, Legal Assistance Desk (LAD) by the DOJ and the Women and Children Protection Units (WCPUs) by the MOH, and the Women Peace Centers (WPCs) in the Provinces of Lanao del Sur, Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, Maguindanao, and the City of Cotabato.

**ARTICLE 15**
**Labor and Employment of Women**

**Section 67. Equal Access to Employment, Training, and Promotion.** The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that no employer shall discriminate against anyone in terms of employment opportunities, trainings, and promotions on account of gender, age, ethnicity, religion, and civil status as prescribed by the Bangsamoro Labor Code.

**Section 68. Employment of Children.** No child below eighteen (18) years of age shall be employed except when working under the direct supervision of the parents or guardian, involving activities that are not hazardous in nature and which do not in any way interfere with his/her schooling, subject to the conditions and requirements prescribed by the Bangsamoro Labor Code.

**Section 69. Salaries, Wages, and Financial Benefits.** All salaries, wages, and financial benefits of employees shall be given in the form of money, a valid check, or an electronic payroll system. No employer shall be allowed to pay utilizing notes, bonds, vouchers, coupons, tokens, tickets, chits, or any similar objects, even when expressly requested by the employee.

Every employer shall comply with the minimum wage set by the Bangsamoro Tripartite Wages and Productivity Board (BTWPB). In the same manner, all employers shall be required to grant benefits to all workers such as, but not limited to, maternity or paternity leave, sick leave, vacation leave, medical services, and other similar services, subject to the limitations provided under the applicable laws.

If the wife is an employee or has an income-generating enterprise, her income shall be disposed of at her discretion. Utilization of her income for family purposes is voluntary and not compulsory.

**Section 70. Technical, Livelihood, and Employment Assistance Program.** The Bangsamoro Government shall enact a law to strengthen strategic, responsive, and gender-sensitive policies and programs that will ensure and facilitate the provisions of technical, livelihood, and employment assistance to all workers regardless of gender.

**Section 71. Prohibited Acts.** It shall be unlawful for any employer to terminate a worker on the account of her pregnancy, or while on leave or in confinement due to her pregnancy, or to discharge or refuse the admission of the worker upon returning to work for fear that she may again be pregnant. Any officer, supervisor, head or authorized employer who commits such unlawful acts shall be liable in accordance with the Bangsamoro Labor Code/under this code.

**Section 72. Occupational Safety and Health Standards.** The Bangsamoro Government shall set and enforce mandatory occupational safety and health standards to eliminate or reduce occupational safety and health hazards in all workplaces. Special considerations shall be integrated to address the unique needs and conditions of women, including aspects related to pregnancy and motherhood. It shall institute comprehensive programs to ensure safe and healthful working conditions in all places of work.

**Section 73. Protection and Welfare of Domestic Workers.** The Bangsamoro Government shall formulate appropriate policies, strategies, and programs as provided in the existing laws on domestic workers which shall ensure the protection from gender-based violence and promotion of their social rights and privileges out of their employment. These policies and programs shall be developed in consultation with domestic workers and their organizations.

**Section 74. Monitoring System for Labor Standards.** MOLE shall monitor compliance to labor standards of private business establishments.–The monitoring system shall utilize gender-disaggregated data and gender-sensitive indicators to effectively assess compliance.

**Section 75. Municipal-Based Registration of House helpers.** The MOLE, in coordination with the MILG, shall undertake a registration system and annual survey of house helpers in their barangays to monitor working conditions, cases of sexual harassment, sexual abuse, and other forms of maltreatment. The results of the surveys shall be collated and presented annually to Parliament with recommendations for legal and other changes to address the problems identified.

Households shall be required to enlist their house helpers for identification and for other purposes. Employers shall provide their house helpers recreation/day-off at terms and conditions pursuant to minimum standards set forth in pertinent national laws.

**Section 76. Deployment of Women Overseas Domestic Workers.** The Bangsamoro government through MOLE shall handle the deployment of women as domestic workers abroad. Regulations and guidelines shall be established with a strong cultural and gender perspective, prioritizing the safety and well-being of women overseas domestic workers.

Notwithstanding the above stated provision, MOLE shall endeavor to develop, promote, and implement suitable programs such as skills training and upgrading, micro-credit financing, and educational programs, and ultimately, generate employment and provide alternative economic opportunities for Bangsamoro women domestic workers to squarely address the root cause of women deployment overseas.

MOLE shall report annually to the Bangsamoro Parliament all its work on women overseas domestic workers.

**Section 77. Special Course on Overseas Bangsamoro Workers.** The Bangsamoro Government through MOLE and OWWA shall ensure a special course on overseas contract work primarily to orient women on the issues and concerns relative to migration shall be systematically introduced to all women especially at the barangay level.

**ARTICLE 16**
**Marriage and Family Relations**

**Section 78. Marriage and Family Relations of Muslims.** Marriages and family relations among Muslims, including property relations, succession, and settlement of the estate of a deceased person shall be governed by the Shari’ah, Presidential Decree No. 1083 or the Code of Muslim Personal Laws, Executive Order No. 209 or the Family Code of the Philippines, as applicable, and this Code.

Presidential Decree No. 1083 recognizes the legal system of Muslims in the Philippines as part of the law of the land, codifies Muslim personal laws, and provides for the administration and enforcement of the Code through the establishment of Shariah courts.

**Section 79. Marriages and Family Relations of Non-Muslims.** Marriages and family relations of non-Muslims shall be governed by existing laws.

**Section 80. Mixed Marriages.** For marriages between Muslims and non-Muslims, respect for each culture shall be observed. Mixed marriages shall be governed by the law under which the marriage is celebrated. In case of dual ceremony, the marriage where it is first registered shall be the governing law of the marriage.

**Section 81. Solemnization of Marriage.** Notwithstanding the applicable provisions of Presidential Decree No. 1083, it shall be the duty of the solemnizing officer or *wali* to obtain and declare, in any appropriate way, the consent of the woman to the marriage. However, should the woman, for any reason, refuse to give her consent, the solemnizing officer shall not solemnize the marriage.

**Section 82. Legal Age of Marriage.** The legal age of marriage shall be in accordance with the established laws.

**Section 83. Specification of Mahr (customary dower).** The amount or value of the dower may be fixed by the contracting parties (mahr-musamma) before, during or after the marriage ceremony. If the amount or the value thereof has not been so fixed, a proper dower (mahr-mithl) shall, upon petition of the woman, be determined by the court according to the social standing of the parties.

**Section 84. Prenuptial Agreement.** A prenuptial agreement shall be highly encouraged as part of the marriage settlement of the contracting parties. It must be in writing and attached to the certificate of marriage, and duly registered with the Shari’ah Registry Office. It is encouraged that the document shall include the following agreement on (1) subsequent marriage, (2) power to divorce, and (3) property regime of husband and wife.

**Section 85. Pre-Marriage Counseling.** The Bangsamoro Government shall nurture strong marriage and family bonds through providing free and accessible pre-marriage counseling in the Bangsamoro Region. A pre-marriage counseling shall be conducted to provide would-be-couples with a basis for making an informed and responsible decision about marriage.

The provisions of this Act shall be fully and promptly implemented by the MSSD, MILG, Bangsamoro Darul Ifta’ (BDI), Ministry of Indigenous Peoples Affairs (MIPA), and MOH.

All LGUs in all levels, through the MILG and in coordination with MSSD, shall establish a pre-marriage counseling desk.

The Bangsamoro Government shall also support the conduct of Pre-Marriage Orientation and Counseling sessions required to all contracting parties or would-be-couples applying for a marriage license in compliance to Family Code of the Philippines.

**Section 86. Divorce.** With the exception of cases involving domestic violence, divorce shall be valid and effective only after the exhaustion of all possible means of reconciliation according to the provisions of PD 1083.

**Section 87. Financial Support.** Any divorced woman shall be entitled to financial support as provided for by the PD. 1083. The support must also be qualified based on *‘idda*. The support herein provided shall be based on the means of the divorced husband. Children of divorced parents shall continually be given support.

**Section 88. Subsequent Marriages.** Notwithstanding the rule on polygamy, a Muslim man shall only be allowed to contract subsequent marriage based on Shariah and upon complying to the requirements with the satisfaction of the court pursuant to PD 1083.

**Section 89. Protection of Wives in Subsequent Marriages.** No wife shall be deprived of the benefits due to her and her children upon the death or retirement of her husband or after subsequent marriage.

**ARTICLE 17**
**Other Measures on the Promotion of Welfare of Women**
**(Special Sectoral Concerns)**

**Section 90. Rights of Women with Disabilities.** The Bangsamoro Government shall give full support to the improvement of the total well-being of women with disabilities and their integration into the mainstream of society. It shall adopt policies ensuring the rehabilitation, self-development and self-reliance of women with disabilities which will develop their skills and potentials to enable them to compete favorably for available opportunities. These opportunities are inclusive, accessible, and tailored to the unique needs and capabilities of women with disability in collaboration with appropriate MAOs as embodied in Republic Act 7277 as amended by Republic Act 10754.

**Section 91. Organization of Persons with Disabilities.** Each barangay shall establish a committee for persons with disability within the barangay development councils, incorporating traditional leaders to ensure effective representation and promotion of their interests.

**Section 92. Formation of Committee for Elderly Women.** Cities and municipalities are required to establish a Committee for Elderly Women under Office of Senior Citizens Affairs (OSCA), focusing on promoting their welfare and addressing their unique needs. This committee will engage in policy advice, community awareness, and intergenerational initiatives, aiming to improve healthcare, social inclusion, and economic security for elderly women. The committee will collaborate with various stakeholders and regularly evaluate the effectiveness of its programs.

**Section 93. Organization of Senior Citizen Associations.** The Bangsamoro Government shall support the organizations and strengthen senior citizen associations to advance the interests of these particular groups.

**Section 94. Senior Citizens Support Fund Allocation.** The necessary appropriations for the operation and maintenance of the OSCA shall be appropriated based on the provision stated under the RA No. 9994 or otherwise known as the Expanded Senior Citizens Act of 2010.

**Section 95. Support for Solo Parents.** The Bangsamoro Government, through MSSD shall support the natural and primary rights and duty of solo parents in rearing their children by providing for their basic needs, and extending to them assistance in social services and welfare benefits, with the end in view of uplifting their status and circumstances and adhere to international agreements and national laws on families.

**CHAPTER IV**
**DEVELOPMENT AND EMPOWERMENT PROVISIONS**

**Article 18**
**Gender and Development Mainstreaming**

**Section 96. Utilization of Gender and Development (GAD) Funds and Budget.** MAOs shall allocate a minimum of five percent (5%) of their total budgets to GAD fund utilization. This is to ensure that resources are dedicated to addressing gender disparities, promoting women's empowerment, and integrating gender perspectives into government programs and projects.

**Section 97. Gender and Development Advocacy.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall formulate a gender and development plan to encourage all MAOs to affirm the role of women and ensure the espousal of gender and development principles and objectives, as well as mobilization of advocacy activities to widen the participation and empowerment of women of Bangsamoro.⁴³

**Section 98. Creation of the Bangsamoro GAD Resource Pool (BGRP).** The BWC shall create, capacitate and accredit a Bangsamoro GAD Resource Pool (BGRP) based on the guidelines developed by the Commission. The BGRP serves as its support group in the provision of GAD-related technical assistance, conduct of gender-related research and studies, and GAD monitoring & evaluation.

**Section 99. Capacity Building for Gender Mainstreaming.** The Bangsamoro Government through the Bangsamoro Women Commission shall ensure the provision of capacity-building programs on gender mainstreaming for all government officials and employees. The BWC shall create GAD learning materials to standardize the core messages in delivering GAD technical assistance in the context of Bangsamoro.

**Section 100. Active Support to Gender Studies.** The Bangsamoro Women’s Commission (BWC) shall allocate a significant fund for scholarship and research grants dedicated to gender studies which shall aim to support documentation and research that focus on gender-related issues.⁴⁴

**Section 101. Integration and Promotion of the Women, Peace and Security Agenda.** The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall ensure that gender perspectives in initiatives are geared towards peacebuilding, conflict-transformation, and post-conflict reconstruction, and with a ‘gender-relational’ approach to peacebuilding practice.

**Article 19**
**Representation of Women in Peace, Governance, and Development Platforms**

**Section 102. Representation of Women in the Political and Public Sphere.** The Bangsamoro Government shall institute the following affirmative action mechanisms so that women can participate meaningfully in the formulation, implementation, and evaluation of policies, plans, and programs for national, regional, and local development.

a) Empowerment within the Civil Service. – Within the next five (5) years from the approval of this code, the number of women in third (3rd) level positions in government shall be incrementally increased to achieve a fifty-fifty (50-50) gender balance;

b) Development Councils and Planning Bodies. – To ensure the participation of women in all levels of development planning and program implementation, at least forty percent (40%) of membership of all development councils from the regional, provincial, city, municipal, and barangay levels shall be composed of women;

c) Other Policy and Decision-Making Bodies. – Women’s groups shall also be represented in international, national, and local special and decision-making bodies;

d) International Bodies. – The Bangsamoro Government shall take all appropriate measures to ensure the opportunity of women, on equal terms with men and without any discrimination to represent their government at the international level and to participate in the work of international organizations;

e) Integration of Women in Political Parties. – The Bangsamoro Government shall provide incentives to political parties with women’s agenda. It shall likewise encourage the integration of women in their leadership hierarchy internal policy-making structures, appointive, and electoral nominating processes; and

f) Private Sector. – The Bangsamoro Government shall take measures to encourage women leadership in the private sector in the form of incentives.⁴⁵

**Section 103. Women’s Involvement and Representation in Regional and Local Special Bodies.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall institute affirmative action mechanisms so that women can participate in the formulation, implementation and evaluation of policies, plans, and programs for regional and local developments, such as the following:

a) Local Special Bodies. To reduce gender gaps, a voluntary gender quota of at least forty percent (40%) of the membership of all local special bodies at the regional, provincial, municipal and barangay level must constitute women;

b) Other Policy and Decision-Making Bodies. Women’s groups shall also be represented in international, national and local decision-making or special bodies, peace negotiating panels; and

c) Private Sector. The Bangsamoro Government through the Bangsamoro Women Commission shall adopt measures to encourage and monitor women’s participation in the private sector, such as encouraging women’s leadership in the private sector in the form of incentives as stipulated in RA 9710 or the Magna Carta of Women.⁴⁶

**Section 104. Observance of Special Events.** - The Bangsamoro Government shall promote the observance of the celebration of special events relative to the promotion and protection of women and children’s rights, such as, but not limited to, the following:

A. Declaration of the month of March as Women’s Month with the inclusion of Women’s Summit and Bangsamoro Women’s Day;
B. Every March 8 as International Women’s Day;
C. Every February 1 as World Hijab Day;
D. Declaring 25 November to 12 December as the 18-Day Campaign to End Violence Against Women (VAW);
E. Declaring the Last Monday of March of Every Year as Women with Disabilities Day.

The conduct of critical consciousness raising activities relevant to the events by the MAOs of BARMM and LGUs shall also be encouraged. A report on the implementation of this Act shall be presented during these events.

**Article 20**
**Political Participation and Empowerment of Women**

**Section 105. Women Development Council.** Under Title IX, Sec. 130 of the Bangsamoro Local Government Code provides for the Creation of a Women Development Council in their respective locality. It shall be composed of Women’s Organization which serve as a vehicle in mainstreaming Gender and Development (GAD) in pursuit of women empowerment, gender equality, social justice, lasting peace, fulfillment and protection of human rights and sustainable development.

**Section 106. Accreditation and Certification of Women Organizations.** The Bangsamoro Women Commission shall accredit and certify women and people’s organizations (including women serving organizations) in Bangsamoro region engaged in women’s rights and protection to ensure standards of inclusivity, transparency, accountability, and commitment to principles promoting gender equality and equity, and fostering an effective network of entities working towards the advancement of women’s rights and protection in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Article 21**
**Economic Empowerment of Women**

**Section 107. Increase Capital Assistance for Women.** All MAOs and LGUs engaged in socio-economic programs shall coordinate, align and increase their capital assistance and/or subsidy to women on a yearly basis until it reaches fifty per cent (50%) within the next five (5) years, especially on land-based projects, using a community bank approach.

To advance women economic empowerment, forty percent (40%) of the GAD fund of all MAOs and LGUs shall be appropriated for the implementation and sustenance of women’s livelihood programs or enterprise programs. Such allocation shall include but not be limited to provision for an Islamic Microfinance program.

**Article 22**
**Women and Cultural Development**

**Section 108. Recognition and Preservation of Cultural Identity and Integrity.** The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall recognize and respect the rights of the people to practice, promote, protect, and preserve their own culture, traditions, and institutions and to consider these rights in the formulation and implementation of regional policies and programs.

The Bangsamoro Government shall actively promote and support initiatives that empower women in their cultural roles and endeavors, ensuring their equal participation in activities that contribute to the preservation, promotion, and enrichment of Bangsamoro cultural diversity.⁴⁷

**Section 109. Integrated Development Program for Indigenous Women.** The Bangsamoro Government shall develop a program which shall facilitate empowerment of the indigenous women. Active support for the preservation of the indigenous women’s knowledge of the environment shall be incorporated in the program.⁴⁸

**Article 23**
**Women and the Environment**

**Section 110. Women’s Participation in the Implementation of Programs and Projects on Environment and Natural Resources Management.** The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage the participation of women in the implementation of programs and projects on environment and natural resources management. This includes creating specific programs or initiatives aimed at empowering women in areas affected by climate change and conflict. The government shall teach the principles of Al-Khilafah (stewardship) and Al-Amanah (trust) in the direction of the environment and natural resources.

It shall likewise recognize the active role of women in the conservation, protection, constructive utilization, management and development of environment and natural resources.⁴⁹

**Article 24**
**Women and Other Sustainable Development Concerns**

**Section 111. Integration of Women in Digital Inclusion and Technological Advancements.** The Bangsamoro Government shall foster women’s access to digital technologies and support their participation in the digital economy. This includes initiatives to bridge the digital divide through digital literacy programs and equitable access to information and communication technologies.

**Section 112. Women’s Participation in Sustainable Agriculture and Responsive Security.** The Bangsamoro Government shall endorse women's pivotal role in sustainable agriculture and food security. Policies shall be tailored to strengthen women's land rights, enhance their agricultural productivity, and support their roles in household and community nutrition.

**Section 113. Gender-Sensitive Disaster Risk Reduction and Management.** Disaster risk reduction and management policies shall integrate gender perspectives, focusing on the unique vulnerabilities and capacities of women. This includes women’s active participation in community-based disaster risk management and climate change adaptation initiatives.

**Section 114. Women’s Empowerment in Sustainable Urban Development.** The Bangsamoro Government shall advocate for women’s involvement in urban planning and development, ensuring safe, accessible, and gender-responsive urban environments. Efforts shall be made to address women's specific needs in housing and urban policies.

**Section 115. Promoting Women in Sustainable Tourism and Cultural Heritage.** The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage women's participation in sustainable tourism and cultural heritage preservation. Support shall be provided to women artisans, performers, and entrepreneurs in sustainable tourism markets.

**Section 116. Fostering Research and Development in Sustainable Solutions.** The Bangsamoro Government shall promote women’s involvement in research and development for sustainable solutions, particularly in renewable energy, water conservation, and waste management. Partnerships with academic institutions and research organizations shall be encouraged.

**Section 117. Collaboration for Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).** The Bangsamoro Government shall foster strategic partnerships to support programs addressing the intersection of gender and sustainable development, in alignment with the SDGs. Knowledge exchange and best practice sharing shall be encouraged at regional and international forums.

**CHAPTER V**
**INSTITUTIONAL ROLES AND MECHANISMS**

**ARTICLE 26**
**Role of the Bangsamoro Government and its Ministries, Agencies, and Offices (MAOs)**

**Section 118. Role of the Bangsamoro Government.** The Bangsamoro Government shall uphold and protect the fundamental rights of women, men, and children in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region regardless of religion, ethnic origin, parentage, or gender including the right of women to be protected from all forms of gender-based violence, exploitation, discrimination, and abuse. It shall also pursue the empowerment of women and their role in governance, regional development, and nation-building and ensure the substantive equality of every individual before the law.

**Section 119. Role of the Shari'ah Courts.** The Shari'ah Courts shall, through the Agama Arbitration Councils within the areas of the Autonomous Region, take direct role to ensure the following:

1. The protection of women and children against abuses in the practice of polygyny, unwarranted divorces, violence and discrimination;
2. The consent of the parties to marriage, especially that of the woman, must be freely given;
3. The parties entering into a contract of marriage, regardless of age, shall undergo a pre-marriage counseling;
4. The divorcees awarded their support in accordance with the Qur'an and Presidential Decree No. 1083; and
5. The establishment of clear procedural guidelines, community engagement, and continuous training of judicial personnel on gender-sensitive practices.⁵¹

**Section 120. Role of the Local Government Units (LGUs).** The local government units (LGUs) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall provide political and institutional support, through mainstreaming of gender and development, in the preservation and enrichment of culture, promotion of health and safety, enhancement of the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encouragement of the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improvement of public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment, maintain peace and order and preserve the comfort and convenience among its inhabitants. Local officials and employees shall be responsible for promoting and ensuring that gender and development are mainstreamed in local policymaking, planning, programming, budgeting, implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

In this regard, it shall be the responsibility of the LGUs to:

a) Deepen their knowledge and build their competencies on GAD-related laws and international commitments, gender mainstreaming, gender analysis, gender-responsive planning and budgeting, and GAD-related tools;

b) Ensure active participation and representation of women at the local committees and mechanisms such as:
a. Peace and order councils; and
b. Development councils.

c) Ensure active participation of local committees/councils in partnership with national/regional government agencies, academe, private sector and civil society organizations (CSOs) operating at the local level for effective gender mainstreaming;

d) Establish and/or strengthen their monitoring and evaluation systems to assess the effectiveness and efficiency of their gender mainstreaming efforts;⁵²

**ARTICLE 26**
**Roles of the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (as Gender Ombud) and the Commission on Audit (COA) on the Judicious Utilization of GAD Budgets**

**Section 121. Role of Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission as the Gender and Development Ombud.** The Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC) as the Gender and Development Ombud shall:

1. Monitor the compliance with this Code, in partnership with BWC and other concerned agencies within the BARMM;
2. Establish guidelines and mechanisms that will facilitate access of women to legal remedies under this Code and related laws, and enhance the protection and promotion of the rights of women, especially marginalized women;
3. Assist in the filing of cases against individuals, agencies, institutions or establishments that violate the provision of this Code; and
4. Regularly submit to the Office of the President, Office of the Chief Minister, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, and furnish the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC), an assessment report on the compliance of this Code.⁵³

**Section 122. The Commission on Audit (COA).** The Commission on Audit (COA), with the assistance of the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC), shall conduct an annual audit of Gender and Development (GAD) funds. This audit aims to assess the prudent utilization of the GAD budget and the efficiency and effectiveness of interventions aimed at addressing gender issues. The purpose is to ensure these measures contribute to the fulfillment of the objectives of the Bangsamoro’s commitments, plans, and policies on GAD, gender equality, and women’s empowerment.

The COA shall furnish the BWC with any assessment reports and audit findings on the use of GAD budget of the BARMM MAOs.⁵⁴

**ARTICLE 27**
**Role of LGUs and the Local Women Development Councils**

**Section 123. Role of the Local Government Units.** The LGUs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall provide political and institutional support, through mainstreaming of gender and development, in the preservation and enrichment of culture, promotion of health and safety, enhancement of the right of the people to a balanced ecology, encouragement of the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, improvement of public morals, enhance economic prosperity and social justice, promote full employment, maintain peace and order and preserve the comfort and convenience among its inhabitants. Local officials and employees shall be responsible for promoting and ensuring that gender and development are mainstreamed in local policymaking, planning, programming, budgeting, implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

**ARTICLE 28**
**GAD Focal Point System**

**Section 124. Creation of the Gender and Development Focal Point System.** The Bangsamoro Government shall, through the Bangsamoro Women Commission, ensure that every ministry, agency, and office have its GAD Focal Point System (GFPS) constituted and ensure that this mechanism shall be strengthened.

**Section 125. Function of the Gender and Development Focal Point System (GFPS).** The GFPS shall establish and maintain a robust Gender and Development (GAD) framework, led by the GAD Focal Point, to mainstream gender perspectives into all levels of policy-making, planning, and programming. This includes regular assessment and enhancement of gender-responsiveness, development and oversight of annual GAD plans and budgets and accomplishment reports, and fostering collaboration with external entities on women's rights and gender development. The GFPS shall also engage and incorporate contributions from women, gender advocates, and marginalized groups throughout the development planning process. They shall ensure that all agency personnel, especially finance officers, receive targeted training on GAD principles to effectively support and implement the GAD objectives as part of the agency's broader human resource development initiatives.

**Section 126. Structure and Composition of the Gender and Development Focal Point System.** The Gender and Development (GAD) Focal Point System (GFPS) shall be an integral structure within the agency, consisting of the GAD Focal Point which is the Head of Ministry/Office, a designated Deputy as the Head of the Executive Committee (ExeCom), supported by a Technical Working Group (TWG) and a dedicated Secretariat. The ExeCom will be formed from the existing management committee or its equivalent, with the authority to appoint an alternate chair if necessary. The TWG, potentially chaired by an ExeCom member or an appointed chair, will include diverse representatives from various divisions and offices, ensuring cross-departmental collaboration. The GFPS Secretariat shall consist of designated personnel from regular plantilla positions or contract of service employees across various divisions and units of the agency. Their primary responsibilities include coordinating GAD initiatives, consolidating GAD reports and plans, maintaining a comprehensive GAD database, and collecting necessary verification documents to substantiate and facilitate GAD-related activities and reports.⁵⁵

**Section 127. Honorarium.** The Gender and Development Focal Point System (GFPS) plays a crucial role in advancing gender equality and promoting the rights of all genders. To recognize and encourage the active participation of GFPS members, including the ExeCom, TWG, and Secretariat in fulfilling their responsibilities, an honorarium shall be granted to respective members of the GFPS. The Bangsamoro Women Commission in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management shall issue guidelines for the implementation of this provision subject to the existing budgeting, accounting, and auditing rules and regulations.

**ARTICLE 29**
**Role of Development Partners**

**Section 128. Role of the Security Sector.** The security sector shall promote a gender-balanced recruitment policy, continuous gender sensitivity training, and strong inter-agency coordination for addressing gender-based violence and discrimination. The role of the security sector in gender and development in the Bangsamoro shall include but not be limited to the following:

1. Ensure that all laws protecting women and children are appropriately enforced and undertake proactive measures to prevent and address the recruitment of children and young people into extremist groups;

2. Ensure that WCPDs, Family and Juvenile Gender and Development – Regional Community Affairs Development Division (FJGAD-RCADD), and Office of Army Gender and Development (OAGAD) are fully functional;

3. Uphold gender representation in the recruitment process, at the very least, by complying with a relevant quota of women recruits as defined by law;

4. Provide gender-responsive services and mechanisms in handling, investigating, and responding to cases;

5. Ensure that all programmed activities are gender-responsive;

6. Ensure that all enforcers and service providers are gender-sensitive and adequately trained to handle gender-based issues/cases;

7. Establish and enforce protocols/guidelines on proper handling of persons and cases involving gender-based issues;

8. Maintain separate, accessible and user-friendly data banks of cases involving gender-based violations;

9. Maintain files of cases of gender-based violence and ensure confidentiality of said case files;

10. Adhere to established referral pathways;

11. Formulate tool and establish a mechanism for clients to evaluate/assess front desk service providers; and

12. Maintain offices and mechanisms for redress of grievance in case of violations or complaints filed against service providers.⁵⁶

**Section 129. Role of Peoples, Non-Governmental and Civil Society Organizations.** The Bangsamoro Government and local government units (LGUs) shall promote the establishment of peoples’ non-governmental and civil society organizations to serve as active partners for gender-responsive development.

Such organizations shall carry-out the following:

1. Mobilization of grassroots communities, poor and marginalized peoples, particularly women, to assert and exercise their rights;

2. Monitoring of government and donor policies and practices;

3. Providing of knowledge, research, advocacy and alternative policies;

4. Delivery of services and innovative development programming;

5. Building coalitions and networks for greater civil society coordination and impact;

6. Mobilization of financial and human resources in partnership with funding agencies;

7. Building of stable, viable and autonomous organizations;

8. Organization of disadvantaged sectors and committees toward greater self-reliance and empowerment;

9. Providing support functions and professional services;

10. Providing access to resources (organizations, technical, logistic and managerial);

11. Conduct of advocacy campaign against discrimination of women;

12. Delivery of certain essential services;

13. Conduct of capability-building and livelihood services;

14. Development of local enterprises designed to improve productivity and income;

15. Encouragement of industrialization; and

16. Promotion of ecological balance and enhancement of the economic and social well-being of the people.⁵⁷

**Section 130. Role of the Private Sector.** The participation of the private sector shall be encouraged to drive economic growth, generate income by creating new jobs that may lead to sustained poverty reduction, awareness and advocacy campaigns, widened women’s participation in economic activities, and the promotion of religious and culturally-appropriate gender and development programs with the end-goal of achieving protection and promotion of women’s rights and empowerment.⁵⁸

**Section 131. Role of the Traditional Leaders.** The Bangsamoro Government shall maintain a strong relationship with traditional leaders in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. As custodians and protectors of their own culture, customs and values, traditional leaders shall promote positive values within their own community and advocate respect for women and girls.

The role of traditional leaders shall be as provided under Republic Act No. 11054, or the Organic Act for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, Republic Act No. 8371 or the Indigenous Peoples’ Rights Act of 1997, and Presidential Decree No. 1083 or the Code of Muslim Personal Laws.

**Section 132. Role of Educational Institutions.** The Bangsamoro Government through the MBHTE and other concerned agencies and other educational bodies shall monitor the compliance of the regional educational subsystem with the Bangsamoro Education Code as well as the minimum standards set by the National Government.

The Madaris and public schools in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be strengthened and developed to become the primary vehicles for the preservation, protection and promotion of non-discriminatory cultures, norms, customs and traditions.

**Section 133. Role of the Religious Sector.** The Bangsamoro Government and the local government units (LGUs) shall promote and ensure the active participation of the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ of the Bangsamoro Government, jurisconsult and other recognized religious groups in the rendition of *fatwa* on contentious issues such as, but not limited to, the following:

1. Gender justice in the context of Islam;
2. Leadership and political rights of women;
3. Gender equity in relation to women’s rights to inheritance and economic rights;
4. Early and arranged marriages of persons below eighteen (18) years of age;
5. Determination of appropriate marrying age for men and women;
6. Forced marriages and proxy marriages;
7. Pre-marriage counseling as requirement for solemnization of marriage;
8. Rights to alimony and support to dependent wife and children;
9. Women’s rights to pursue and practice profession;
10. Abuses in the practice of polygyny and their impact on women and children; and
11. Violence against women and children

**Mechanisms for Implementation and Coordination**

**Section 134. Creation of GAD Offices in the Ministries, Agencies, and Offices (MAOs).** All Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (MAOs) shall create a GAD Office to be used by the Gender and Development Focal Point System (GFPS) Secretariat to ensure the integration of gender equality and women’s empowerment principles into their policies, programs, and projects. This includes providing adequate facilities for the GFPS Secretariat.

**Section 135. Bangsamoro Gender and Development Committee (BGADC).** The Bangsamoro Government, through the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council (BEDC), shall create a mechanism that will monitor the implementation of the GAD plans and programs. This mechanism shall be headed by the Bangsamoro Women Commission as the chair, MILG as the co-chair, and other ministries, offices, provincial LGUs, and civil society organizations as members.

The BGADC under the Bangsamoro Economic Development Council (BEDC) shall be the coordinating mechanism for gender and development in the BARMM.

The BGADC shall also be the platform for the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC) to conduct monitoring and oversight of the proper implementation of this Code by all accountable ministries and offices.

**Section 136. Creation of the Bangsamoro Women Commission Accreditation and Certification Unit (ACU).** The Bangsamoro Government through the BWC shall establish an Accreditation and Certification Unit tasked with certifying and accrediting women’s sectoral organizations for participation in sectoral representative elections, as well as accrediting partnerships with women’s associations and women-serving agencies for the implementation of programs, projects, and activities focused on women, gender, and development within the BARMM.

**Section 137. Establishment of Gender-Sensitive Management Information Database.** The Bangsamoro Government shall mandate all MAOs and LGUs to submit annual gender statistics to BWC. This central repository shall organize and safeguard sex-disaggregated information, with annual data templates available for download on the Commission’s website. The accessible data shall support informed decision-making for local planners and policymakers, promoting sustainable development and gender equality in the region.⁵⁹

The Bangsamoro Government, through the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC) in coordination with the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), shall also institutionalize the establishment of a management information database on women and gender statistics from sex-disaggregated data submitted by ministries, agencies, and offices and local government units in the region. The database shall serve as the data repository for institutional mechanisms for gender mainstreaming, planning, and policy or decision-making purposes prescribed by the law. The data gathered shall also be accessible to local planners for development purposes.

**Section 138. Preparation of Annual Gender and Development Plan and Budget.** The Bangsamoro Government through its ministries, offices, SUCs, and LGUs shall annually formulate their respective GAD Plan and Budget and submit the same along with their accomplishment reports to the Bangsamoro Women Commission for review.

All concerned ministries and agencies shall prepare their GAD Plan and Budget using the reporting format and as provided under the rules and regulations prescribed by the Bangsamoro Women Commission. At least five to thirty percent (5%-30%) of overseas development assistance (ODA) and at least five percent (5%) of the total annual budget of each ministry, office and LGU shall be programmed for Gender and Development.

**Section 139. Mainstreaming of Gender and Development (GAD) Programs in the Local Development Plan.** The Bangsamoro Government through Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC) shall issue a circular directing ministries and offices as well as LGUs to integrate gender and development programs in their year-round local development plans and activities.

The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that ministries, offices and local government units take full responsibility towards the effective and efficient mainstreaming of GAD in their respective plans and budget.

**Section 140. Formulation and Implementation of this Code.** The Bangsamoro Government shall, not limited to GADTIMPALA Women Commission, formulate plans and programs for the effective and efficient implementation of the Code.

**Section 141. Scholarship Grants and Research.** A sufficient amount shall be allotted by the Bangsamoro Government to support gender-related studies, research, and scholarship grants to its GFPS and other qualified employees to support the Bangsamoro Government’s GAD initiatives.

**Section 142. Incentives and Awards.** There is hereby established an award for GAD such as but not limited to GADTIMPALA (Gender and Development Transformation and Institutionalization through Mainstreaming of Programs, Agenda, Linkages and Advocacies) Awards within the BARMM GAD framework. The purpose of these awards is to recognize and incentivize individuals and organizations, MAOs and LGUs within the BARMM that demonstrate outstanding commitment and achievements in addressing gender issues through innovative approaches and judicious utilization of the GAD Budget.

The Bangsamoro Government, through the Bangsamoro Women Commission shall prepare detailed guidelines and procedures for the evaluation of the nominated ministries, offices, and agencies for the GADTIMPALA Awards. The initial amount of one million pesos (P1,000,000.00) for the Miscellaneous and Other Operating Expenses shall be allocated subject to budgeting and accounting existing rules and regulations. Subsequent funding requirements shall be included in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

**Section 143. Mainstreaming of Gender and Development (GAD) in LGUs.** The MILG shall, in coordination with BWC, ensure the operationalization of the principles and policies provided under this code.

**Section 144. Gender-fair Language.** The Bangsamoro Government through the Bangsamoro Women Commission ensures that all ministries, offices, and local government units institutionalize their GAD mandates for effective and efficient implementation of their programs, projects and activities. All ministries, offices, local government units, educational institutions, scholarship programs, and training initiatives, hereby commit to employing gender-fair language in all official documents, publications, and communications for fostering a gender-sensitive societal ethos.

**Section 145. Collective Responsibility.** Following the overall policy framework on GAD, the operationalization of this Code shall be a joint responsibility of the Bangsamoro Government including its provinces, cities, municipalities, and barangays. All stakeholders shall collaborate successfully in the implementation of this Code.

Further, the Chief Minister, through the BWC, shall report regularly to the Bangsamoro Parliament on the status of the implementation of this Code and other laws pertaining to the welfare of women and girls and the promotion of gender justice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 146. Bangsamoro Women Commission.** The BWC shall be the primary policymaking and coordinating body for women and gender equality concerns in the BARMM Region. It shall be the principal women development agency of the BARMM that shall promote women’s economic empowerment, protect and advance women’s rights through strengthened services and justice delivery system, promote gender-responsive governance through promotion of equal representation of women in decision-making bodies and promote gender and development programs. Notably, its function shall be, but not limited to, the following:

1. Facilitate the monitoring of the effective implementation of this Code;
2. Review and update this Code to amend or revise the same;
3. Collect and consolidate reports on women and Gender and Development concerns as well as make recommendations thereon;
4. Coordinate with law enforcement agencies for the protection of women and their children and provision of free legal assistance, security and safety of gender-based violence survivors;
5. Serve as a repository of data on gender and development for the formulation of programs, projects, proposals and strategies;
6. Spearhead the enhancement, updating of and provision of technical assistance relative to existing guidelines and tools on gender-responsive planning and budgeting, gender analysis, GAD Code formulation, gender mainstreaming, gender audit and/or gender assessment at the local level in partnership with MILG, MFBM, BPDA, civil society organizations, academe and other stakeholders;
7. Take the lead in advocating the use of the HGDG for the program and/or project design, development, management, implementation, monitoring and evaluation, in partnership with BPDA and MILG;
8. Lead the development of a monitoring and evaluation system to determine progress and results of implementing the MCW in partnership with MILG, BPDA, MFBM and concerned agencies;
9. Provide technical assistance and as necessary, support the capacity building of MILG, BPDA and MFBM to enable them to perform their roles as stipulated in this Code; and
10. Develop a mentorship program or workshops to guide and support ministries, offices, and agencies, and local government units in the GAD fund utilization and documentation and reporting processes for such purpose.

**Section 147. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** Within one hundred eighty (180) days following the effectivity of this Code, the Bangsamoro Gender and Development Committee (BGADC), chaired by the Bangsamoro Women Commission, and in consultation with the concerned regional line agencies and LGUs shall have completed the formulation of the implementing rules and regulations necessary for the effective implementation of this Code.

**Section 148. Compliance Report.** Within one year from the enactment of this Code and annually thereafter, the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC) will collaborate with ministries, offices, and local government units to provide the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), via the Bangsamoro Gender and Development Committee (BGADC), detailed reports on the implementation progress of this Code, emphasizing its impact on women’s status. These reports will include evaluations of the Code's effectiveness. Additionally, the BWC is to supply the Philippine Commission on Women a copy of the compliance report regarding the Code's execution.

**Section 149. Appropriations.** The Bangsamoro Government shall appropriate the necessary funds to the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC) for the effective implementation of this Code.

Ministries, agencies, offices and LGUs shall allocate at least five percent (5%) of their total annual budget to GAD and for the implementation of the provisions of this Code and other GAD-related laws and development plans.

**ARTICLE 33**
**Monitoring and Evaluation**

**Section 150. Monitoring and Evaluation System.** All MAOs and LGUs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall establish, strengthen and maintain a gender-responsive monitoring and evaluation (M&E) system. In establishing the M&E system, the head of MAOs and local chief executive of LGUs shall issue an administrative order or executive order creating their M&E team.

The GFPS of ministries, agencies, offices and LGUs shall monitor the implementation of their annual GPB and assess the status of their institutional mechanism on gender mainstreaming annually.

The BWC shall act as the primary oversight of the implementation of the consolidated annual Gender and Development Plans of the Bangsamoro Government.

**Section 151. Submission of Accomplishment Report.** All MAOs at the regional level shall submit their GAD Accomplishment Report based on their BWC-endorsed GAD Plan and Budget to the BWC for review, assessment, and consolidation.

LGUs at the city/municipal, and provincial levels must submit their Gender and Development Accomplishment Report based on their GAD Plan and Budget to the MILG for review and consolidation. The consolidated report shall be sent to the BWC for submission to the Philippine Commission on Women.

**CHAPTER VI**
**PENAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 152. Penalties.** Should MAOs, or LGU in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region violate any provision of this Code and its implementing rules and regulations, the sanctions under administrative law, civil service, or other appropriate laws shall be recommended to the Bangsamoro Civil Service Commission and/or the MILG. The BHRC shall stand as the Gender Ombud in coordination with the BWC. The person(s) directly responsible for the violation as well as the head of the agency or local chief executive shall be held liable under this Code.

Specifically, the following shall be punishable under this Code:

**A. Institutional Mechanisms**

1. Non-submission of GAD plan;
2. Failure to implement the GAD plan;
3. Failure to submit accomplishment report;
4. Non-establishment of Gender Focal Point System (GFPS);
5. Non-deployment of qualified and trained GFP officers;
6. Non-submission of gender-disaggregated data;
7. Failure to comply or non-allocation of GAD Budget;
8. Insufficient GAD fund allocation;
9. Misuse of GAD Budget/Irregular implementation/use of GAD budget; and
10. Non-implementation of Bangsamoro GAD Evaluation and Monitoring System.

The Bangsamoro Gender and Development Committee (BGADC) shall serve as the investigative body to bolster transparency, accountability, and oversight in the GAD fund allocation, disbursement, and utilization.

**B. Women Empowerment**

1. Failure to put up barangay VAW desks;
2. Failure to provide services to Women in Especially Difficult Circumstances;
3. Failure to enhance policies, programs, facilities, and services to ensure protection and security of women in times of disaster, calamities and other crises;
4. Failure to address the needs of women from a gender perspective;
5. The discriminatory, demeaning and derogatory portrayal of women in an advertisement, shows and programs in various media;
6. Discrimination concerning social protection.

**C. Anti-Violence Against Women and their Children Act of 2004 (R.A. 9262);**

**D. Safe Spaces Act (R.A. No. 11313);**

**E. Special Protection of Children Against Child Abuse, Exploitation and Discrimination Act (Republic Act No. 7610);**

**F. Anti-Sexual Harassment Act of 1995 (Republic Act No. 7877);**

**G. Anti-Rape Law of 1997 (Republic Act No. 8353);**

**H. Rape Victim Assistance and Protection Act of 1998 (Republic Act No. 8505);**

**I. Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2003 (Republic Act No. 9208);**

**J. Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 (R.A. 10175);**

**K. Solo Parent Act (R.A. 8972);**

**L. Expanded Maternity Leave Law (RA 11210);**

**M. Breastfeeding Promotion Act (RA 10028).**

**N. Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Act (RA 11861)**

Filing a complaint under this Code shall not preclude the offended party from pursuing other remedies available under the law and invoking any of the provisions of existing statutes, especially those recently enacted laws on protecting women and children.

**CHAPTER VII**
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 153. Repealing Clause.** All laws, proclamations, executive orders, rules and regulations inconsistent with this Code are hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

**Section 154. Separability Clause.** Should any section, provision, part or portion of this Code be declared unconstitutional or invalid by a competent court, the other sections, provisions, parts or portions not affected thereby shall remain valid and in full force and effect.

**Section 155. Suppletory Clause.** On matters not provided for in this Code, any existing applicable laws, executive orders, rules and regulations shall apply suppletorily.

**Section 156. Effectivity Clause.** This Code shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following the completion of its publication in any newspaper of regional circulation.⁶⁰
