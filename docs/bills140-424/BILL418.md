**Parliament Bill No. 418**

Authored by: MPs Suharto S. Esmael

**AN ACT GRANTING ZERO HOSPITALIZATION BILLING FOR EXPIRED PATIENTS IN GOVERNMENT HOSPITALS OF THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

**Section 1. Short Title.** - This Act shall be known as the “Zero Billing Hospitalization for Expired Patients Act”

**Section 2. Declaration of Policy and Legal Basis.** – It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to enact laws to promote, protect, and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro people and other inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Consistent with the principles of moral governance and the Bangsamoro Organic Law, to ensure that social justice, compassion, and dignity guide all public health services.

The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure every Filipino citizen in its territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life. Social justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**Section 3. Purpose.** The purpose of this Act is to uphold the dignity of every person, especially in moments of loss and grief. The Bangsamoro government shall ensure that no financial burden shall be imposed on the families of patients who die while admitted in government hospitals within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

In the Bangsamoro spirit of compassion, dignity, and moral governance, this Act seeks to ensure that no grieving family shall bear the financial weight of hospitalization when a loved one passes away in a government hospital. It affirms that care does not end at death and that mercy must extend to the final moment.

In recognition of the sanctity of life and the dignity of every person, the Bangsamoro Government affirms its duty to extend compassion to families in grief.

This Act operationalizes moral governance in healthcare by institutionalizing zero hospital billing for deceased patients.

**Section 4. Definition of Terms.** For purposes of this Act:

a. **Government Hospital** refers to any hospital owned, managed, or operated by the Bangsamoro Government through the Ministry of Health (MOH-BARMM), including provincial, district, municipal, and specialty hospitals.

b. **Deceased Patient** refers to any person who dies while admitted or undergoing emergency treatment in a government hospital.

c. **Zero Billing Certificate (ZBC)** refers to the document issued by the hospital certifying that all hospital fees and professional charges are waived in accordance with this Act.

d. **Bereaved Family** refers to the immediate family members or legal next of kin of the deceased patient.

**Section 5. Coverage.** - This Act shall apply to all patients who die while admitted in any government-owned and operated hospital within BARMM, regardless of cause of death, duration of confinement or nature of socioeconomic status.

**Section 6. Zero Billing Guarantee.** -Upon the death of a patient in a government hospital, the hospital administrator or its authorized representative shall immediately issue a Zero Billing Certificate. This certificate shall waive all fees, including room and board, professional fees, laboratory, radiologic, and diagnostic services, medication and supplies, emergency, intensive care, and surgical services, and any other medical or ancillary charges incurred.

Release of the deceased patient or medical records shall not be withheld pending payment of any charges.

**Section 7. Verification and Documentation Mechanism.**

The hospital’s Patient Care and Records Office, in coordination with the Finance and Billing Section, shall:

a. Verify the patient’s death through proper medical certification;
b. Prepare the Zero Billing Certificate within twenty-four (24) hours of death; and
c. Submit a monthly consolidated report to MOH-BARMM for audit and reimbursement purposes.

**Section 8. Role of the Ministry of Health.**

The **MOH-BARMM** shall:

a. Implement and oversee compliance with this Act;
b. Conduct periodic audits and submit compliance reports to the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM);
c. Formulate policies for hospital reimbursement, sustainability, and capacity development; and
d. Develop public awareness campaigns emphasizing the right of families to zero billing for deceased patients.

**Section 9. Institutional Incentives.**

Government hospitals that fully comply with this Act shall receive:

a. **Priority access** to regional health subsidies and capital outlay programs;
b. **Performance-Based Grants** from the Bangsamoro Treasury;
c. **Recognition and Accreditation Incentives**, including eligibility for awards, certifications, and inclusion in regional health development plans; and
d. **Capacity-building support** from MOH-BARMM and partner institutions to improve service delivery and financial sustainability.

**Section 10. Accountability and Penalties.**

Any hospital personnel who, directly or indirectly, withholds the release of a deceased patient or demands payment contrary to this Act shall be subject to:

a. Administrative sanctions under Bangsamoro Civil Service Rules; and
b. Possible suspension or dismissal after due process.

Hospitals found repeatedly violating this Act may face reduction or suspension of performance-based incentives under the preceding Section of this Act.

**Section 11. Appropriations.** - The funds necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act shall be charged against the annual appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government.

The amount of Two Hundred Fifty Million (PHP 250,000,000.00) shall be appropriated to support the initial implementation of this Act upon its effectivity. Thereafter, such sums as may be needed for its continued operation shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro Government under the MOH BARMM.

**Section 12. - Implementing Rules and Regulations.** - The Ministry of Health, in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management shall create a Technical Working Group to formulate the necessary implementing rules and regulations within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act.

**Section 13. Separability Clause.** - If any provision or part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 10. Repealing Clause.** - All regional laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**Section 11. Effectivity.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation within BARMM.
