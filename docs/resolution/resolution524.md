**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 524**

**RESOLUTION URGING THE BANGSAMORO ECONOMIC AND DEVELOPMENT COUNCIL, THROUGH THE BANGSAMORO PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY, TO PREPARE AND SUBMIT A COMPREHENSIVE PERFORMANCE REPORT OF THE BANGSAMORO GOVERNMENT TO THE NATIONAL GOVERNMENT, COVERING THE PERIODS 2019 TO 2022 AND 2023 TO 2024**

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Article XVI of the Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (OLBARMM)”, provides for the dissolution of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) and the submission of its final report and recommendations on the status of government during the transition period to the Parliament, as well as to the House of Representatives, the Senate of the Philippines, and the Office of the President;

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Article VI of the OLBARMM provides that:

*“The President shall exercise general supervision over the Bangsamoro Government to ensure that laws are faithfully executed;”*

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article XVI of the same law provides for the first regular election for the Bangsamoro Government under this Organic Law shall be held and synchronized with the 2022 national elections. However, this was superseded with the passage of Republic Act No. 11593, otherwise known as *“An Act Resetting the First Regular Elections in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao”*, to be held and synchronized with the 2025 national elections;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article XVI of the same law provides that the legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during transition shall be vested in the BTA and that all powers and functions of the Bangsamoro Government as provided in this Organic Law are vested in the BTA during the transition period;

**WHEREAS**, the first BTA has served office from February 19, 2019 to August 10, 2022 following the appointment of the eighty (80) members of the BTA by then President, His Excellency Rodrigo Roa Duterte;

**WHEREAS**, His Excellency President Ferdinand *“Bongbong”* Romualdez Marcos, Jr. appointed the second set of eighty (80) members of the BTA last August 11, 2022;

**WHEREAS**, there is a need to prepare the performance report of the first BTA to document its accomplishments and inform both the national government and the general public of what has been achieved during the first transition period;

**WHEREAS**, the governance of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao is guided by the principles of transparency, accountability, and moral governance, as enunciated by the Chief Minister Ahod Balawag Ebrahim;

**WHEREAS**, ministries, agencies, and offices were involved in implementing development programs, projects, and activities funded through the block grant and other sources provided by the National Government to the Bangsamoro Government;

**WHEREAS**, the implementation of programs and projects covers major sectors, including economic, social, infrastructure, environment, and climate change, as well as development administration and governance;

**WHEREAS**, significant gains and achievements of these major sectors shall be highlighted in the performance report, including laws and legislative measures passed and approved by the first BTA Parliament. Moreover, major implementation issues, concerns, and recommendations shall also be highlighted in the report;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council, as the planning and policy-making body of the Bangsamoro Government, shall oversee the preparation and submission of the performance report; and the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, as the Technical Secretariat of the Council, shall coordinate the preparation of the performance report in cooperation with implementing ministries, offices, and agencies;

**WHEREAS**, the performance report of the Bangsamoro Government shall be submitted by the Office of the Chief Minister to the Office of the President, the Senate President, and the Speaker of the House of Representatives:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To urge the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council through the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority to prepare and submit a comprehensive performance report of the Bangsamoro Government to the National Government, covering the periods 2019 to 2022 and 2023 to 2024.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To forward this Resolution to the Office of the Chief Minister, for information and guidance of the ministries, agencies, and offices of the Bangsamoro Government.

**ADOPTED**, *Rajab* 23, 1446/January 23, 2025.
