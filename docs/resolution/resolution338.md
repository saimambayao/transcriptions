SECOND REGULAR SESSION

RESOLUTION NO. 338

RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING ALNAKRAN ABDILLA, A NATIVE OF TAWI-TAWI, AND HIS TEAM FOR SECURING A BRONZE MEDAL FOR MEN’S BEACH VOLLEYBALL IN THE 32ND SOUTHEAST ASIAN GAMES IN PHNOM PENH, CAMBODIA, AND FOR GIVING HONOR AND PRIDE TO THE BANGSAMORO REGION

WHEREAS, on May 5, 2023, the 32nd Southeast Asian (SEA) Games held its opening ceremony hosted by Cambodia in its capital, Phnom Penh. This biennial multi-sports event is held from May 5 to 17, hosting a total of 584 games in 36 sports;

WHEREAS, the SEA Games is participated by eleven (11) Southeast Asian Nations: Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Timor-Leste, and Vietnam. Wherein, the Philippines debuted its participation in the year 1977;

WHEREAS, Alnakran “Ranran” Abdilla, hailing from the Province of Tawi-Tawi, and his team won against their Vietnamese rival in the men’s beach volleyball competition on May 16, held in Otres Beach, Sihanoukville, Cambodia;

WHEREAS, securing the score of 2-0 game victories, the men’s national team settled for a bronze medal, maintaining the position for a third consecutive time in the SEA Games. This bronze medal is the only medal obtained by the Philippines in the volleyball sport category;

WHEREAS, the Philippine men’s team for the beach volleyball category is composed of athletes Abdilla, Jaron Requinton, James Buytrago, and Jude Garcia;

WHEREAS, playing in the professional scene for more than a decade, Abdilla is renowned to be a proficient spiker in the sport. Now, he is a member of the Philippine Airforce, and has consistently delivered victory for their volleyball club. His sports career is embellished by several Most Valuable Player (MVP) awards;

WHEREAS, Abdilla’s prowess in the sport is cemented for being a consistent medalist in the SEA Games: winning a silver medal for indoor volleyball in the 2019 SEA Games, and a bronze for beach volleyball in the 2021 Sea Games;

WHEREAS, the Bangsamoro is immensely proud of the victory obtained by the son of Tawi-Tawi Province. His achievement signifies the province’s enduring capacity in nurturing talents in the field of sports;

WHEREAS, Alnakran Abdilla’s achievement is a testament to his hard work, dedication, and perseverance in representing the Bangsamoro and the Philippines in an international sporting event;

WHEREAS, Alnakran Abdilla’s accomplishment has brought pride and honor to the region and the country and has inspired many Bangsamoro, especially the youth, to pursue their dreams and strive for excellence;

WHEREAS, this resolution signifies the Bangsamoro Government’s eagerness to support and recognize its athletes. The competitiveness and athleticism exemplified by Abdilla and his team serve as an inspiration, not only to the Bangsamoro but also to the country as a whole;

WHEREAS, it is appropriate to provide commensurate incentives from the appropriate office in the Bangsamoro:

NOW, THEREFORE, be it

RESOLVED, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, To commend and congratulate Alnakran Abdilla, a native of Tawi-Tawi, and his team for securing a bronze medal for men’s beach volleyball in the 32nd Southeast Asian Games in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, and for giving honor and pride to the Bangsamoro region.

RESOLVED, FURTHER, To direct the Bangsamoro Sports Commission to provide incentives and continued support for Abdilla’s athletic career.

RESOLVED, FINALLY, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the family of Alnakran Abdilla, and the Bangsamoro Sports Commission, for information.

ADOPTED, Dhul Qa’dah 04, 1444/May 25, 2023.
