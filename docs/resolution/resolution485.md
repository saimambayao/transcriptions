**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 485**

**RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING THE 1ST INTERNATIONAL NUCLEAR SCIENCE OLYMPIAD GOLD MEDALIST AND HIGHEST SCORER AWARDEE AND NAMED NUCLEAR AMBASSADOR MOHAMMAD NUR CASIB OF PHILIPPINE SCIENCE HIGH SCHOOL-CENTRAL MINDANAO CAMPUS THEREBY BRINGING PRIDE AND HONOR TO THE BANGSAMORO PEOPLE**

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article II of the 1987 Constitution provides that, “*The State recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building and shall promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It shall inculcate in the youth patriotism and nationalism, and encourage their involvement in public and civic affairs;*”

**WHEREAS**, Section 17, Article II of the same Constitution states that, “*The State shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture, and sports to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development;*”

**WHEREAS**, Section 13, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the “*Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao*” provides that “*The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promote and protect their physical, moral, spiritual, and nationalism, encourage involvement in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through sports;*”

**WHEREAS**, the Philippines greeted the world’s 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) with a blast as the Department of Science and Technology (DOST) welcomed the Olympiad participants in the opening ceremonies held at the SMX Convention Center in New Clark City, Pampanga, marking a significant milestone in nuclear education;

**WHEREAS**, the historical international event ran from August 1-6, 2024, featured fifty-five (55) top contenders, twenty-seven (27) team leaders, and fourteen (14) observers from fourteen (14) Asia-Pacific countries;

**WHEREAS**, the International Nuclear Science Olympiad (INSO) aims to increase awareness of the peaceful applications of Nuclear Science and Technology (NST), to popularize knowledge and understanding of NST to improve the nuclear scientific approach towards its uses and applications, and to enhance the interest in NST among secondary school students;

**WHEREAS**, the competition aims to highlight the Philippines’ commitment to advancing nuclear education and fostering international collaboration in the peaceful use of nuclear science;

**WHEREAS**, the event was organized by the Philippine Nuclear Science Institute, in collaboration with the DOST, Philippine Nuclear Science Foundation Incorporated, Department of Education (DepEd), and the National Research Council of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, Marina Binti Mishar from the Department of Technical Cooperation of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), describes the Olympiad as “*a global community united by a common goal: to explore, innovate, and push the boundaries of what is possible in the field of nuclear science;*”

**WHEREAS**, Ms. Helena Zhivistkaya, an expert in the Nuclear Knowledge Management Section of the IAEA Department of Nuclear Energy, reminded the contenders that, “*The Olympiad is not just a competition but building long-lasting cooperation for future activities;*”

**WHEREAS**, Mohammad Nur G. Casib of Bangsamoro Region, a gold medalist member of the Philippine team, displayed brilliance and excellence in the Olympiad;

**WHEREAS**, special awards were also conferred to Mr. Casib for garnering the highest points in the experimental and theoretical exams, respectively. The contenders competed as individuals and were ranked according to the points they obtained in the two exams, in which Mr. Casib emerged on top;

**WHEREAS**, he secured the highest points in overall scores, earning him the title “*Nuclear Ambassador;*”

**WHEREAS**, his success has brought pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people, and serves as an inspiration to our Bangsamoro youth;

**WHEREAS**, his remarkable achievements have not only demonstrated unparalleled dedication and excellence in his field but have also made him an inspiration for the Bangsamoro youth, serving as a powerful testament to the limitless potential within the Bangsamoro community and motivating others to strive for greatness;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government takes immense pride in his outstanding accomplishments, recognizing them not only as personal victories but as a collective triumph that reflects the success and progress of the entire Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, further solidifying our commitment to fostering excellence and empowering our youth;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government congratulates and commends Mr. Casib for his continued success, outstanding performance, and contributions in the national and international competitions that brought pride, honor, and glory to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To congratulate and commend the 1st International Nuclear Science Olympiad gold medalist and highest scorer awardee and named Nuclear Ambassador Mohammad Nur Casib of Philippine Science High School-Central Mindanao Campus thereby bringing pride and honor to the Bangsamoro people.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To present this Resolution to the family of Mohammad Nur Casib and the Philippine Science High School-Central Mindanao, for information.

**ADOPTED**, *Safar* 15, 1446/August 19, 2024.
