**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 477**

**RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE NEW SHARI'AH COUNSELORS-AT-LAW WHO SUCCESSFULLY PASSED THE 2024 SHARI'AH BAR EXAMINATIONS**

**WHEREAS**, Section 5 (5) of Article VIII of the 1987 Philippine Constitution grants the Supreme Court the authority to promulgate rules regarding the protection and enforcement of constitutional rights, the practice and procedure in all courts, the admission to the practice of law, the integrated bar, and legal assistance to the underprivileged, ensuring that such rules provide a simplified and inexpensive procedure for the speedy disposition of cases, are uniform for all courts of the same grade, and do not modify substantive rights, with the rules of procedure of special courts and quasi-judicial bodies remaining effective unless disapproved by the Supreme Court;

**WHEREAS**, the *Shari'ah* or Islamic law forms part of the Islamic tradition derived from the religious precepts of Islam, particularly the *Qurâ€™an* and *Sunnah*, and is a special field of law based on Presidential Decree No. 1083 also known as the "*Code of Muslim Personal Laws in the Philippines*;"

**WHEREAS**, *Shari'ah* is one of the pillars of the Bangsamoro Tri-Justice System, which upholds the unique cultural and historical heritage of the Bangsamoro in the administration of justice, as stated in Section 1, Article X of Republic Act No. 11054, or the "*Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao*;"

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Article X of the aforementioned Republic Act provides that "*A successful examinee who has qualified for special membership in the Philippine Bar shall be duly conferred the title of counselor-at-law;*"

**WHEREAS**, on April 28, 2024, and May 2, 2024, the Supreme Court conducted the first digitalized and regionalized *Shari'ah* Bar Examinations at the University of the Philippines, Diliman, Quezon City, and at the Ateneo de Davao University, Davao City;

**WHEREAS**, a total of eight hundred fifty-three (853) individuals aspiring to practice law before *Shari'ah* courts took the examinations;

**WHEREAS**, on July 16, 2024, the Supreme Court, through Associate Justice Maria Filomena Singh, the 2024 *Shari'ah* Bar chairperson, announced that one hundred eighty-three (183) examinees, or twenty-one point forty-five percent (21.45%) of the total number of examinees, successfully passed the 2024 *Shari'ah* Bar Examinations;

**WHEREAS**, the same was affirmed by the Supreme Court in an *en banc* resolution, B.M. No. 4248, which approves the usage of the formal title *Shari'ah* Counselor-at-Law (SCL) by all successful passers of the *Shari'ah* Bar Examinations;

**WHEREAS**, the new *Shari'ah* counselors-at-law have hurdled a rigorous two-day examination, which tested not only their legal knowledge and stamina but also their deep commitment to upholding the rich traditions and principles of *Shari'ah* within the Philippine legal landscape;

**WHEREAS**, the new *Shari'ah* counselors-at-law hold great potential in raising the standards of the legal profession and improving the administration of justice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, the people in the Bangsamoro have high hopes that our new *Shari'ah* counselors-at-law will help provide the much-needed *Shari'ah* legal services and demonstrate a strong commitment to justice, ethics, and the moral values we collectively uphold as Bangsamoro:

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To commend the new *Shari'ah* counselors-at-law who successfully passed the 2024 *Shari'ah* Bar Examinations.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To present this Resolution to the 2024 *Shari'ah* Bar Examination passers, for information.

**ADOPTED**, Muharram 17, 1446/July 23, 2024.
