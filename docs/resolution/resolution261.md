**RESOLUTION NO. 261**

**RESOLUTION EXPRESSING COMMENDATION TO THE TWELVE (12) YOUNG BANGSAMORO ATHLETES WHO BROUGHT PRESTIGE AND RECOGNITION TO THE BANGSAMORO BY EXHIBITING THEIR SKILLS AND TALENT AND BAGGING OVERALL CHAMPION WITH TWENTY-FIVE (25) MEDALS WITH ELEVEN (11) GOLDS, SIX (6) SILVERS, AND EIGHT (8) BRONZES DURING THE PAN ASIAN INTERNATIONAL JIU JITSU COMPETITION HELD AT AYALA MALLS CIRCUIT, CIRCUIT MAKATI, METRO MANILA LAST JUNE 26, 2022**

**WHEREAS**, twelve (12) young Bangsamoro athletes representing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, guided by Coach Abdul Aziz Talusan, participated in the Pan Asian International Jiu Jitsu Competition held at Ayala Malls Circuit, Circuit Makati, Metro Manila last June 26, 2022;

**WHEREAS**, the names of the Bangsamoro athletes who participated in the said competition are the following:

1. Abdul Aziz Talusan – 1 Bronze
2. Sittie Farhana Ebrahim – 2 Golds
3. Ruhullah Enampadan – 1 Gold, 1 Silver, 1 Bronze
4. Hakim Midtimbang – 1 Gold, 1 Silver, 1 Bronze
5. Chris Balbotin – 1 Gold, 1 Silver
6. Muiz Abobakar – 1 Gold, 1 Silver
7. Ashzahir Abobakar – 1 Gold, 1 Silver
8. Asharook Abobakar – 1 Gold, 1 Silver, 2 Bronze
9. Jaheed Sandigan – 1 Gold, 1 Bronze
10. Mohannad Biruar – 1 Gold, 1 Bronze
11. Prince Rastafari Mohammad – 1 Gold
12. Fahima Mohammad – 1 Bronze

**WHEREAS**, Jiu Jitsu is an unarmed ground-based fighting sport that is focused on skills and the ability to gain a dominant position over the opponent and the use of techniques until the opponent is down and submission;

**WHEREAS**, Jiu Jitsu is also considered a discipline as it follows a systematic approach to mastering the execution of various techniques;

**WHEREAS**, similar to other sports, Jiu Jitsu requires adequate training, mental focus, and self-discipline — characteristics that modern-day competitive young Bangsamoro athletes should possess and enhance;

**WHEREAS**, despite challenges and limited financial support, this group of young Bangsamoro Jiu Jitsu athletes has excelled in the competition by exhibiting their talents and skills in the modern martial arts discipline and bagging twenty-five (25) medals with eleven (11) golds, six (6) silvers, and eight (8) bronzes, bringing prestige and recognition not only for themselves but also for the Bangsamoro, in general;

**WHEREAS**, this extraordinary event which recognizes the skills and talents of young Bangsamoro athletes is worth commendable;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Government encourages the promotion of mental and physical fitness through sports:

**NOW, THEREFORE**, be it

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, To express commendation to the twelve (12) young Bangsamoro athletes who brought prestige and recognition to the Bangsamoro by exhibiting their skills and talent and bagging twenty-five (25) medals with eleven (11) golds, six (6) silvers, and eight (8) bronzes during the Pan Asian International Jiu Jitsu competition held at Ayala Malls Circuit, Circuit Makati, Metro Manila last June 26, 2022.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, that this Resolution be presented to the twelve (12) Bangsamoro athletes concerned, for information.

**ADOPTED**, Dhu Al-Hijjah 29, 1443/July 28, 2022.
