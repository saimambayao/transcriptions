**RESOLUTION EXPRESSING PROFOUND GRATITUDE TO HIS EXCELLENCY PRESIDENT RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE AND THE ADMINISTRATION’S SECURITY, JUSTICE AND PEACE CABINET CLUSTER (SJPCC) MEMBERS, DEFENSE SECRETARY DELFIN N. LORENZANA, NATIONAL SECURITY COUNCIL SECRETARY HERMOGENES C. ESPERON, JR., INTERIOR SECRETARY EDUARDO M. AÑO, AND PRESIDENTIAL ADVISER ON PEACE, RECONCILIATION AND UNITY (PAPRU) SECRETARY CARLITO G. GALVEZ, JR., FOR MAINTAINING THE STATUS QUO ON THE CURRENT COMPOSITION OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY (BTA) IN THE AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO**

**WHEREAS,** as stated under Section 2, Article I of Republic Act No. 11054, the purpose of the Organic Law is to establish a political entity, provide for its basic structure of government in recognition of the justness and legitimacy of the cause of the Bangsamoro people and the aspirations of Muslim Filipinos and all indigenous cultural communities in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to secure their identity and posterity, allowing for meaningful self-governance within the framework of the Constitution and the national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS,** to realize the magnificent purpose of this Organic Law for the alleviation of the living condition of the Bangsamoro people, President Rodrigo Roa Duterte appointed the 80 BTA Members in 2019 and placed upon them the task of initiating and forging legislative measures and other related responsibilities that would bring peace, development and progress to the Bangsamoro Region and its constituents;

**WHEREAS,** in three years, the BTA Members have proved themselves worthy of the President’s appointment by dedicatedly and vigorously fulfilling their task of passing important bills, resolutions, and doing other related legislative functions, and undertaking various programs and projects for vulnerable communities in their respective constituencies;

**WHEREAS,** in the aforementioned span of time that the BTA Members have spent in the Parliament, there are still many legislative jobs, programs and projects unfinished due to the Covid-19 pandemic, and need another year or more to be done;

**WHEREAS,** on October 12, 2021, the BTA Members adopted Resolution No. 173 requesting President Duterte to allow the incumbent BTA Members to continue carrying out their mandate in order to preserve the gains achieved under the Body’s various programs, projects, and legislative measures;
