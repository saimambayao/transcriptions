**BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT**

**THIRD REGULAR SESSION**

**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 494**

**RESOLUTION PROVIDING SPECIFIC TIME FOR MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT DURING PLENARY SESSIONS TO GIVE RECOGNITION, COMMENDATION, AND EXTEND CONDOLENCES, IF ANY, THEREBY AMENDING RULE VIII OF ADOPTED RESOLUTION NO. 268 OR “RESOLUTION PROVIDING FOR RULES, PROCEDURES, AND PRACTICES OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT”**

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, or the “*Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao*”, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise powers expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental to its efficient and effective governance, and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, Sections 2 and 3 of Article XVI of the preceding law provide that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) is the interim government in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition period, and all powers and functions of the Bangsamoro Government, as provided in the Organic Law are vested in the BTA, which shall exercise legislative authority;

**WHEREAS**, Section 25, Article VII of the same law provides that the BTA Parliament shall adopt its own rules for the conduct of its business;

**WHEREAS**, the BTA approved in its plenary session, Adopted Resolution No. 268, or the “*Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament*”, which guides the conduct of all sessions, committee hearings and meetings, and other similar activities;

**WHEREAS**, Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268 provides for the “*Parliamentary Order of Business*”, wherein all the matters to be addressed during a specific session are laid down;

**WHEREAS**, many of the resolutions filed by the Members of the Parliament (MPs) give recognition and commendation to acknowledge the various kinds of achievements, may it be a Bangsamoro or not, while others are filed to extend condolences to the bereaved families;

**WHEREAS**, all these measures, when included in the Order of Business of the Parliament, are tackled in the plenary, going through the legislative process, such as the period of interpellation and the period of amendment which may be time-consuming;

**WHEREAS**, it is better to allocate a specific time for each of the MPs who wish to give recognition, commendation, or extend their condolences, rather than having the members file it as a resolution:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Parliament, To provide specific time for Members of Parliament during plenary sessions to give recognition, commendation, and extend condolences, if any, thereby amending Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268 or “*Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.*”

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To amend Rule VIII of Adopted Resolution No. 268, to read as follows:

**Rule VIII. – Parliamentary Order of Business**

**Section 1. Order of Business.** – On any session day of the Parliament, each Member of the Parliament shall be furnished with a printed copy of the Order of Business.

The daily Order of Business shall be as follows:

(a) Call to Order;

(b) Invocation or Prayer;

(c) National Anthem;

(d) Bangsamoro Hymn;

(e) Roll Call;

(f) Reading and Approval of Journal of the Previous Session;

(g) Recognitions, Commendations, Memorials, and Messages and Communications;

(h) Reference of Business:

(1) Bills on First Reading; and

(2) Resolutions.

(i) Bills on Second Reading:

(1) Committee Reports on Bills;

(2) Period of Interpellations;

(3) Period of Amendments; and

(4) Approval on Second Reading.

(j) Resolutions Referred to the Committee:

(1) Committee Reports;

(2) Period of Interpellations;

(3) Period of Amendments; and

(4) Approval.

(k) Unfinished Business;

(l) Privilege Hour;

(m) Question Hours; and

(n) Bills on Third Reading.

On the first Wednesday of every quarter, the Order of Business shall include the Chief Minister’s Hour immediately after the Roll Call.

In case of a Special Session, the Order of Business, as communicated, shall be strictly in accordance with the purpose of the request made by the Chief Minister or the order of the Speaker.

**Section 2. Giving Recognition, Commendation, and Extending of Condolences.** – Members of Parliament (MP) who wish to give recognition and commendation shall notify the Office of the Floor Leader at least three (3) calendar days before the commencement of the session, which shall be included in the Order of Business.

Each MP then be given a maximum of three (3) minutes to deliver a speech during the plenary session.

**Section 3. Manner of Dissemination.** – The daily Order of Business shall be posted on the Parliament’s website and sent via electronic mail to the Members. As far as practicable, such Order of Business shall be sent at least three (3) calendar days before the commencement of the session.

**Section 4. Amendment of the Order of Business.** – Any MP may, prior to the start of the session, register his or her desire to avail of Section 2. The Floor Leader may, at his or her discretion, move for an amendment of the Order of Business to accommodate such request.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, That this Resolution shall take effect immediately upon its approval.
