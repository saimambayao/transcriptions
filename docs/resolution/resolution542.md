**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 542**

**RESOLUTION CONDEMNING THE RAID THAT LED TO THE KILLING OF SEVEN MEMBERS OF THE MORO ISLAMIC LIBERATION FRONT, CALLING FOR FAIR, IMPARTIAL, AND SPEEDY INVESTIGATION, AND REQUESTING THE RETURN OF THE INTERNATIONAL MONITORING TEAM IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION**

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament is committed to upholding the rights, welfare, and security of the Bangsamoro people;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament has a duty to ensure that the human rights of all Filipino citizens, including members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF), are upheld, respected and protected;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament recognizes the necessity for law enforcement agencies to apprehend individuals, who are suspected of criminal activities; however, such actions must be conducted in strict accordance with established legal procedures and in full compliance with human rights standards;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament has taken notice of a raid conducted on June 18, 2023, in Barangay Damawato, Datu Paglas, Maguindanao del Sur, which resulted in the tragic killing of seven (7) members of the MILF;

**WHEREAS**, the victims, all members of the MILF, namely: Nasser Yousef M. Husain, 34; Norjihad M. Husain, 29; Nasrullah Mamay Singkala, 38; Ivan Pumpugay, 18; Izrael Laguiab, 41; Morsid Madidis, 50; and Mama Kakim, 53, succumbed to fatal gunshot wounds;

**WHEREAS**, reports indicate that the victims were handcuffed and killed in various locations, including a mosque, raising serious concerns about the use of excessive force and the violation of their human rights;

**WHEREAS**, the victims had already filed their applications for amnesty and were anticipating the grant thereof;

**WHEREAS**, the raid took place during the ongoing implementation of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB), particularly the decommissioning and normalization track, in which the MILF is a key stakeholder committed to the pursuit of lasting peace;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Parliament acknowledges the vital role of the International Monitoring Team (IMT) in fostering confidence-building measures and in maintaining peace and security in the region:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To condemn the raid that led to the killing of seven members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front, calling for fair, impartial, and speedy investigation, and requesting the return of the International Monitoring Team in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To strongly condemn the raid that led to the killing of seven members of the MILF and express deep concern over the reported use of excessive force and the violation of the victims’ rights, and call upon the higher authorities to conduct a fair and impartial inquiry and speedy investigation into the circumstances surrounding the raid, with the aim of holding accountable those responsible.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE**, To urge the concerned authorities to conduct the investigation in a transparent manner and ensure that its findings are made public, as well as to request the immediate return of the IMT in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to facilitate the ongoing peace process and to provide independent monitoring considering the prevailing situation on the ground.

**RESOLVED, FURTHERMORE**, To affirm the commitment of the Bangsamoro Parliament to the peace process and call on all parties to uphold the established mechanisms, including the Coordinating Committee on the Cessation of Hostilities, the Ad Hoc Joint Action Group, and the peace panel, in apprehending MILF members suspected of involvement in criminal activities.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, To furnish a copy of this resolution to the Office of the Chief Minister, the OPAPRU, the Commission on Human Rights, and other relevant government agencies for their immediate attention and appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, Sha’ban 19, 1446/February 18, 2025.
