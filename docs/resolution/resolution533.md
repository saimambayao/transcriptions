**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 533**

**RESOLUTION REQUESTING THE MINISTRY OF BASIC, HIGHER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION TO RENDER A COMPREHENSIVE REPORT ON THE CURRENT STATUS OF TEXTBOOK AVAILABILITY, SCHOOL BUILDING AND CLASSROOM CONDITIONS, AND TEACHER SUFFICIENCY TO THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Article XIV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that:

*“The State shall protect and promote the right of all citizens to quality education at all levels, and shall take appropriate steps to make such education accessible to all;”*

**WHEREAS**, Section 16, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the *“Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao”*, provides that:

*“It shall be a top priority of the Bangsamoro Government to establish, maintain, and support a complete and integrated system of quality education, which shall be a subsystem of the national education system. The Bangsamoro Government shall develop an educational framework relevant and responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people;”*

**WHEREAS**, quality education is essential to the growth and development of our society; therefore, the availability of necessary resources, infrastructure, and personnel plays a critical role in ensuring that our students receive the best possible education;

**WHEREAS**, the availability of textbooks, the condition of school buildings and classrooms, and the sufficiency of teachers in educational institutions under the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) are paramount matters that the Bangsamoro Government must consider;

**WHEREAS**, these challenges, if left unaddressed, could severely impact the quality of education and the overall learning environment for students across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

**WHEREAS**, there is a need for the MBHTE to provide timely and accurate updates on the enrollment figures, challenges faced, and measures taken to address any issues during the enrollment process;

**WHEREAS**, there is a need to determine the condition of the school buildings, including whether they require condemnation, demolition, repair, or replacement, as well as access to water sources for sanitation, toilet facilities, and electricity;

**WHEREAS**, this data, including the classroom-student ratio, needs to be segregated geographically and by education level;

**WHEREAS**, the equitable distribution of resources and educational opportunities across all geographical areas within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is crucial for achieving inclusive and sustainable education development;

**WHEREAS**, the information provided by the Ministry will assist in making informed decisions and policy adjustments that will benefit students, educators, and the education system as a whole;

**WHEREAS**, the MBHTE is hereby requested to submit a comprehensive report detailing the current issues and challenges related to the following:

a) Availability of Textbooks: A detailed account of the current state of textbook distribution and availability in all educational institutions, including any shortages or delays in their provision.

b) Status of School Buildings and Classrooms: A thorough assessment of the condition of school buildings and classrooms, including any infrastructure deficiencies, ongoing repairs, or planned construction projects.

c) Sufficiency of Teachers: An analysis of the current teacher workforce, highlighting any shortages, the teacher-student ratio, and the adequacy of staffing levels in meeting educational needs:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To request the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education to render a comprehensive report on the current status of textbook availability, school building and classroom conditions, and teacher sufficiency to the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To forward this Resolution to the MBHTE, for information and appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, *Sha’ban* 13, 1446/February 12, 2025.
