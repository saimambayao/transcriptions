**RESOLUTION NO. 241**

**RESOLUTION EXPRESSING THE SINCEREST APPRECIATION AND GRATITUDE OF THE BTA PARLIAMENT TO THE ORGANIZATION OF ISLAMIC COOPERATION - COUNCIL OF FOREIGN MINISTERS (OIC-CFM) FOR ITS UTMOST CONCERN ON THE PLIGHT OF THE MUSLIM COUNTRIES, INCLUDING NON-MEMBER MUSLIM COMMUNITIES LIKE THE BANGSAMORO, DURING THE OIC-CFM'S 48TH SESSION HELD ON MARCH 22-23, 2022 IN ISLAMABAD, PAKISTAN**

**WHEREAS**, the theme “Partnering for Unity, Justice, and Development” of the 48th Session of the Organization Of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) - Council of Foreign Ministers in Islamabad, Pakistan on March 22-23, 2022 seeks to “build partnerships across the Islamic world, and build bridges of cooperation to address the myriad of challenges confronting the Muslim Ummah”;

**WHEREAS**, the 48ᵗʰ Session tackled broad range of issues on matters of “peace and security, economic development, cultural and scientific cooperation, and revitalizing the role of the OIC in the Muslim Ummah”;

**WHEREAS**, the conference endeavors to promote unity amongst the member states, advance the cause of justice for Islamic peoples, and achieve the mutually reinforcing goals of prosperity and development for all OIC member states;

**WHEREAS**, resolutions were adopted during the Session that sought to build ‘partnership’ across the Islamic world, and build bridges of cooperation that will articulate OIC’s perspective on major contemporary issues involving the Muslim Ummah that will address the myriad of challenges confronting them;

**WHEREAS**, the OIC has played a major role in forging milestones of peace agreements: The 1976 Tripoli Agreement and the 1996 Final Peace Agreement between the Government of the Republic of the Philippines (GRP) and the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) that led to the creation of Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM); and the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) between the GRP and the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) that eventually led to the passage of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL). Both peace agreements sought to establish in Southern Philippines a meaningful self-rule with greater fiscal autonomy and justice system;

**WHEREAS**, the MNLF, as a party to the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao and a holder of permanent observer status in the OIC, strongly supports the call of the pan-Islamic body for unity, justice, and development of the Muslim States including the non-member Muslim communities:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, to express the sincerest appreciation and gratitude of the BTA Parliament to the Organization of Islamic Cooperation - Council of Foreign Ministers (OIC-CFM) for its utmost concern on the plight of the Muslim countries, including non-member Muslim communities like the Bangsamoro, during the OIC-CFM'S 48ᵗʰ Session held on March 22- 23, 2022 in Islamabad, Pakistan.
