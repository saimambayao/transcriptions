**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 498**

**RESOLUTION URGING ALL GOVERNMENT MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES, INCLUDING THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO TO ESTABLISH LACTATION STATIONS IN THEIR RESPECTIVE OFFICES FOR THEIR WOMEN EMPLOYEES AND CLIENTS, PURSUANT TO REPUBLIC ACT NO. 10028 OR “THE EXPANDED BREASTFEEDING PROMOTION ACT OF 2009”**

**WHEREAS**, the Shari’ah recommends a two-year lactation period for infants;

**WHEREAS**, according to the World Health Organization, breastfeeding is one of the most effective ways to ensure a newborn’s health and survival, providing antibodies that protect infants against illnesses;

**WHEREAS**, government data shows that as of 2023, the Bangsamoro Region has one of the highest prevalence of stunting in the country, affecting 45% of children under 5 years old;

**WHEREAS**, breastfeeding has been scientifically proven to provide numerous health benefits to both infants and mothers such as stronger immune systems, lower risk of certain diseases, and improved bonding between mother and child, and others;

**WHEREAS**, according to the Department of Health and Department of Social Welfare and Development, breastfeeding is a very common practice in the Philippines, with around 93% of children having been breastfed;

**WHEREAS**, Republic Act No. 10028, also known as *“The Expanded Breastfeeding Promotion Act of 2009”*, mandates the establishment of lactation stations in government offices, establishments, and institutions to promote and support breastfeeding among working mothers;

**WHEREAS**, Section 11, Chapter III of Republic Act No. 10028 stipulates that health and non-health facilities, establishments, or institutions, including public places, shall establish lactation stations as appropriate, and these lactation stations are mandated to have adequate facilities that enable nursing mothers to collect milk or breastfeed their children in a conducive and private environment;

**WHEREAS**, the Philippines’ Gender and Development budget policy of 5% may be allocated for the establishment, repair, and maintenance of lactation stations;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) government is committed to upholding the rights and welfare of its constituents, including women and children:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To urge all government ministries, offices, and agencies, including the local government units in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to establish lactation stations in their respective offices for their women employees and clients, pursuant to Republic Act No. 10028 or *“The Expanded Breastfeeding Promotion Act of 2009.”*

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To urge the government ministries, agencies, offices, and instrumentalities to establish lactation stations and to encourage local government units to do the same.

**RESOLVED, FURHTERMORE**, To call upon the Ministry of Health to provide technical assistance and guidance to government agencies and offices in establishing and maintaining of lactation stations, ensuring that they meet the standards set forth in Republic Act No. 10028.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, To furnish a copy of this Resolution to all government agencies and offices in the BARMM for their information, compliance, and necessary action.

**ADOPTED**, *Safar* 24, 1446/August 28, 2024.
