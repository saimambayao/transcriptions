**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 500**

**RESOLUTION ENCOURAGING THE MINISTRIES, AGENCIES, AND OFFICES IN THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO TO ESTABLISH CHILD-MINDING CENTERS WITHIN THEIR WORKPLACES OR IN LOCATIONS ACCESSIBLE FROM THE WORKPLACES**

**WHEREAS**, Section 12, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State recognizes the sanctity of family life and shall protect and strengthen the family as a basic autonomous social institution, it shall equally protect the life of the mother and the life of the unborn from conception. The natural and primary right and duty of parents in the rearing of the youth for civic efficiency and the development of moral character shall receive the support of the government;

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article V of the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao states that the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general welfare;

**WHEREAS**, Republic Act No. 11861, also known as the *“Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Act”*, recognizes the challenges faced by solo parents in balancing work responsibilities with parental duties, thereby providing additional benefits and privileges to solo parents and their children, amending Republic Act No. 8972;

**WHEREAS**, Section 30, Article V of the Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Act’s Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) provides that the Department of Labor and Employment and the Civil Service Commission shall promote and encourage the establishment of appropriate child-minding centers within the workplace, or in accessible locations to the workplace or residence of the solo parent;

**WHEREAS**, the Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Act’s Revised IRR further provides that national government agencies with more than three hundred (300) employees, including government-owned and controlled corporations, and private employers with two hundred (200) or more employees, may establish child-minding centers to accommodate their employees’ children who are aged seven (7) years and below;

**WHEREAS**, child-minding centers are facilities provided by the employer where the solo parent employee’s child, children, or dependents who are aged seven (7) years old and below are habitually received for purposes of care and supervision during working hours;

**WHEREAS**, the child-minding centers shall be available free of charge to their employees, with priority given to those who are solo parents as defined under the Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Act;

**WHEREAS**, the establishment of child-minding centers will not only be beneficial to the children and their parents but also to the government, as it will lead to improved performance and minimal absenteeism of employees related to child care;

**WHEREAS**, the child-minding center can also provide assistance to constituents who need a safe place for their children while they conduct business in the offices:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To encourage the ministries, agencies, and offices in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to establish child-minding centers within their workplaces or in locations accessible from the workplaces.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To forward the foregoing Resolution to all concerned ministries, agencies, and offices including local government units in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, for information.
