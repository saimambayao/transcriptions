**FOURTH REGULAR SESSION**

**RESOLUTION NO. 259**

**RESOLUTION EXPRESSING DEEPEST CONDOLENCES AND SYMPATHIES TO THE FAMILY OF THE LATE FORMER PRIME MINISTER OF JAPAN SHINZO ABE AND EXTENDING PROFOUND APPRECIATION AND GRATITUDE FOR HIS CONTRIBUTION TO PEACE AND DEVELOPMENT IN THE BANGSAMORO REGION**

**WHEREAS**, former Japan Prime Minister Shinzo Abe died on Friday, July 8, 2022, at the age of 67;

**WHEREAS**, former Prime Minister Abe was shot twice while campaigning for a parliamentary election on a street in the City of Nara, Japan, on Friday morning;

**WHEREAS**, former Prime Minister Abe was a Japanese politician who served as Prime Minister of Japan and President of the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) from 2006 to 2007, and from 2012 to 2020. He was the longest-serving prime minister in Japanese history;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) expresses its gratitude for his selfless contributions to peace and development in the Bangsamoro Region;

**WHEREAS**, former Prime Minister Abe has always been there to support the peace process in Mindanao. On his official visit to the Philippines on December 9, 2006, he said that the peace process in Mindanao is important for the stability and development of the Philippines and that Japan will continue to provide support as much as possible;

**WHEREAS**, under his leadership, Japan gave a financial grant to the Bangsamoro Region. The grant is for the improvement of socio-economic infrastructure and the construction of Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) Centers in the Bangsamoro Region;

**WHEREAS**, also during his tenure, he supported the needed development assistance in the Bangsamoro Region as a way to signify his country's support for the ongoing peace process;

**WHEREAS**, former Prime Minister Abe said in his message at the Philippines Development Forum 2014 that he hoped that all the people in the Philippines, including people in Mindanao, would be able to enjoy "*the dividends of peace*";

**WHEREAS**, Japan has been one of the staunch supporters of the Mindanao peace process. The Government of Japan supported the holding of the first meeting of former Philippine President Benigno S. Aquino III with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) Chairman Al Haj Ahod “Murad” Ebrahim on August 4, 2011. The meeting was the first step that led to the signing of the peace agreement. Under the administration of former Prime Minister Abe, Japan vowed to continue supporting the political transition and capacity-building of the BTA. Japan is making numerous efforts aimed at active contribution to regional and international peace, stability, and prosperity;

**WHEREAS**, the BTA offers its deepest sympathies to the family of the former Prime Minister of Japan, Shinzo Abe. The BTA Parliament joins the people of Japan and the rest of the world in mourning a global political giant who contributed so much to world peace:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the BTA Parliament, To express its deepest condolences and sympathies to the family of the late former Prime Minister of Japan Shinzo Abe and extend profound appreciation and gratitude for his contribution to peace and development in the Bangsamoro Region.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, That a copy of this Resolution be forwarded to the bereaved family of former Prime Minister of Japan Shinzo Abe, for information.

**ADOPTED**, Dhu Al-Hijjah 20, 1443/July 19, 2022.
