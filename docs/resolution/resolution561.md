**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 561**

**RESOLUTION COMMENDING HIS EXCELLENCY PRESIDENT FERDINAND ROMUALDEZ MARCOS JR. ON THE ISSUANCE OF MEMORANDUM ORDER NO. 36, SERIES OF 2025 AND RESPECTFULLY REQUESTING HIS EXCELLENCY THE PRESIDENT TO DIRECT THE NATIONAL AMNESTY COMMISSION FOR THE EXPEDITED ISSUANCE OF SAFE CONDUCT PASSES TO QUALIFIED MORO ISLAMIC LIBERATION FRONT AND MORO NATIONAL LIBERATION FRONT AMNESTY APPLICANTS**

**WHEREAS**, part of the peace policy of the Philippine Government is to nurture a climate conducive to peace and to implement programs for reconciliation and reintegration of rebels into mainstream society;

**WHEREAS**, an amnesty program is an integral component of the national government’s comprehensive peace efforts to achieve long-term peace and stability of the country;

**WHEREAS**, after seventeen (17) years of negotiations, the Government of the Philippines reached a landmark peace agreement with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) through signing the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) in Manila on March 27, 2014;

**WHEREAS**, pursuant to paragraph 2, Section 19, Article VII of the Philippine Constitution, the President of the Philippines has the power to grant amnesty with the concurrence of a majority of all the Members of Congress;

**WHEREAS**, His Excellency President Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr. signed Proclamations No. 405 and 406 on November 22, 2023, granting amnesty to members of the MILF and the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) respectively;

**WHEREAS**, Executive Order (EO) No. 125, series of 2021, entitled Creation of the National Amnesty Commission, created the National Amnesty Commission (NAC) with the mandate to receive and process applications for amnesty under Proclamation No. 1090 and 1091, both series of 2021;

**WHEREAS**, His Excellency President Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr. signed EO No. 47 on November 24, 2023, amending EO No. 125, series of 2021, which, under Section 1, mandates the Commission to receive and process applications for amnesty pursuant to Proclamations Nos. 405 and 406, and under Section 2 provides that the Commission shall continue to exist until the completion of its mandate or as may be determined by the President;

**WHEREAS**, some leaders and members of the MILF and the MNLF covered by Proclamations Nos. 405 and 406, have applied for amnesty before the NAC pursuant to EO No. 47, series of 2023;

**WHEREAS**, the NAC on June 11, 2025 held in Cotabato City a ceremonial issuance of Safe Conduct Passes (SCPs) for qualified amnesty applicants, during which a total of twenty-five (25) SCPs were issued, including seven (7) to MILF members and two (2) to MNLF members;

**WHEREAS**, the NAC reported that approximately three thousand (3,000) individuals have applied for amnesty, with ninety percent (90%) requesting an SCP;

**WHEREAS**, despite the total number of applications received, only one hundred forty (140) are currently under review, of which twenty-seven (27) have already been approved and issued, indicating that the processing and evaluation of applications remain ongoing;

**WHEREAS**, considering the significant disparity between the volume of applications received and the limited number of SCPs issued to date, there is an urgent need to expedite their processing and issuance to ensure that qualified applicants from the MILF and the MNLF can promptly benefit from the protections afforded under the amnesty program;

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, to express its profound gratitude to His Excellency President Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr. for issuing Memorandum Order No. 36, thereby affirming the government’s commitment to protect the rights and security of qualified members of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) under the amnesty program and existing peace agreements.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, to respectfully request His Excellency President Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr. to direct the National Amnesty Commission (NAC) to expedite the issuance of Safe Conduct Passes (SCPs) under Memorandum Order No. 36 for qualified Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) amnesty applicants.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, to forward a copy of this Resolution to His Excellency President Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr., through the Office of the President at the Malacañang Palace, Metro Manila, Philippines for his information and favorable consideration.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, to furnish a copy of this Resolution to the National Amnesty Commission (NAC) for appropriate action.

**ADOPTED**, Jumada Al-Awwal 22, 1447/November 13, 2025.
