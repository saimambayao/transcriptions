**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 451**

**RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING AND COMMENDING THE HONORABLE DEPUTY FLOOR LEADER OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT AND MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT MINISTER ATTORNEY RAISSA HERRADURA JAJURIE AND MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT FROILYN TENORIO MENDOZA FOR BEING RECIPIENTS OF THE 2024 INQUIRER WOMEN OF POWER AWARD GIVEN BY THE PHILIPPINE DAILY INQUIRER LAST MARCH 11, 2024**

**WHEREAS,** it is enshrined in Section 14, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution that, “*The State recognizes the role of women in nation-building, and shall ensure the fundamental equality before the law of women and men;*”

**WHEREAS,** Section 5, Article XIII of the Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the “*Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao*” provides that “*The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the role of women in governance and ensure their fundamental equality before the law. It shall guarantee full and direct participation of women in governance and development process, ensuring that women shall benefit equally in the implementation of development programs and projects;*”

**WHEREAS,** the Philippine Daily Inquirer – one of the country’s most trusted newspapers with more than a million daily readers nationwide – has been giving the Women of Power Award to honor exceptional women from diverse sectors for their remarkable achievements in their profession, business, civic mission, or creative endeavors, through which they have become a source of inspiration and made a positive impact on the community;

**WHEREAS,** on March 11, 2024, Honorable Member of Parliament (MP) and Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) Minister Atty. Raissa H. Jajurie and MP Froilyn T. Mendoza were chosen as among the recipients of the 2024 Women of Power Award during a ceremony at Seda Hotel in Bonifacio Global City, Taguig by The Philippine Daily Inquirer;

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Raissa Herradura Jajurie is a Bangsamoro lawyer who is currently serving the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao as a Deputy Floor Leader of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, the current Minister of the MSSD, the Committee on Local Government Chairperson and Committee on Rules Vice Chairperson;

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Jajurie is a Tausug who was born in the southern part of the Philippines – Jolo Sulu, a graduate of Ateneo de Manila University with a degree in Political Science. She finished her Law degree at the University of the Philippines and was admitted to the Philippine Bar in the year 1995;

**WHEREAS,** prior to her appointment as a Member of the Parliament and MSSD Minister, Atty. Jajurie held several key positions in different organizations where she displayed extraordinary leadership, service, and competence, which include the following:

a.  Co-founder of Nisa Ul Haqq Fi Bangsamoro (Women for Justice in the Bangsamoro);
b.  Co-founder of Bangsamoro Lawyers Network;
c.  Member of Moro Islamic Liberation Front Peace Implementing Panel; and
d.  Member of the Bangsamoro Transition Commission (tasked to create a draft for the Bangsamoro Organic Law)

**WHEREAS,** for over two decades, Atty. Jajurie also served in the Sentro ng Alternatibong Lingap Panlegal (SALIGAN) where she was active in human rights advocacy as a lawyer, researcher, and trainer while working with Moro lawyers, Muslim women, and the Moro communities;

**WHEREAS,** Atty. Jajurie’s career is indeed an exceptional and exemplary one befitting of commendation and noteworthy for taking the role of setting a benchmark for excellence;

**WHEREAS,** MP Froilyn T. Mendoza is a Teduray woman from South Upi, Maguindanao del Sur, a Bachelor of Science in Agriculture degree holder from the University of Southern Mindanao, a registered midwife, and a pioneering alumna from the Women’s Peace Leadership And Security Program at the Center for Peace and Conflict Studies in Siem Reap, Cambodia. She is renowned for her dedication to advancing and popularizing the rights of Indigenous Peoples (IPs), with a particular focus on indigenous women;

**WHEREAS,** MP Mendoza’s significant contributions include co-founding the Teduray Lambangian Women Organization, receiving the 2019 N-Peace Award, and participating in numerous international gatherings representing her tribe, with her work being featured in the ASEAN launch of the Regional Action Plan on Women, Peace, and Security, and in Cambodia’s National Peace Gallery, highlighting her advocacy for gender equality, women peace builders, and the rights and roles of indigenous women in political structures;

**WHEREAS,** MP Mendoza recently completed the Manaaki New Zealand Short Term Training Scholarship for Senior Level Public Sector Leadership in Aotearoa, New Zealand. She played a crucial role in crafting the Implementing Rules and Regulations of the Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 290 (the GAD Code of ARMM) and the Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 241. She also made history as the first IP woman leader to join the first transition commission that drafted the Basic Law for the Bangsamoro in 2013;

**WHEREAS,** this Resolution intends to give due credit to the efforts of our fellow Bangsamoro who have truly exhibited the prowess that a Bangsamoro woman is capable of, in the Philippines and around the world;

**WHEREAS,** to recognize and celebrate these excellent and historic accomplishments of our very own colleagues in this Honorable Parliament, it is therefore fitting and proper that we should extend our congratulations and commendation for these extraordinary women of power:

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To congratulate and commend the Honorable Deputy Floor Leader of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament and Ministry of Social Services and Development Minister Attorney Raissa Herradura Jajurie and Member of Parliament Froilyn Tenorio Mendoza for being recipients of the 2024 Inquirer Women of Power Award given by the Philippine Daily Inquirer last March 11, 2024.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** To furnish a copy of this Resolution to the Office of Minister Raissa H. Jajurie and Office of MP Froilyn T. Mendoza, for information.

**ADOPTED,** Dhu Al-Qa’dah 14, 1445/May 22, 2024.
