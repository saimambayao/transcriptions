**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 487**

**RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING THE CONTRIBUTIONS OF THE LATE COMMISSIONER ATTORNEY NASSER A. MAROHOMSALIC FOR THE BANGSAMORO PEOPLE AND EXPRESSING DEEPEST CONDOLENCES AND SYMPATHIES TO HIS FAMILY**

**WHEREAS**, Commissioner Atty. Nasser A. Marohomsalic, born on July 21, 1955, was a distinguished native of Lumba-Bayabao of the province of Lanao del Sur, who made significant contributions to the Bangsamoro and the country;

**WHEREAS**, he peacefully passed away and returned to the Almighty Allah (S.W.T.) on July 6, 2024, at the Philippine Heart Center, marking a great loss to the Bangsamoro;

**WHEREAS**, Commissioner Marohomsalic, was a devoted husband to Hadja Omensalam Benito Adap Cotigal. Their union was blessed with six (6) children, one of them being Atty. Nashmyleen A. Marohomsalic, the first Filipina lawyer to be admitted as counsel in the International Criminal Court and a member of the International Criminal Court Bar Association;

**WHEREAS**, Commissioner Marohomsalic was a human rights champion, peace advocate, writer, novelist, historian, lawyer, and an exemplary civil servant;

**WHEREAS**, he passed the Bar Examinations in 1981 after completing his Bachelor of Laws from the University of the Philippines in the same year. He took his Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science in 1977 at Far Eastern University and was an honorary alumnus of Mindanao State University-Main Campus, where he was enrolled in Liberal Arts for two years. He was granted a certificate as Fellow Doctor of History and Management by the Royal Institution of Singapore;

**WHEREAS**, in 1988, at the very young age of 33, he was appointed as Commissioner for the Regional Consultative Commission for Muslim Mindanao that drafted the first Organic Act of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao legislated by the Congress, the Republic Act No. 6734;

**WHEREAS**, in 1994, he was appointed as Commissioner of the Commission on Human Rights;

**WHEREAS**, he served as Conservator and Independent Trustee of the Philippine Public School Teachers Association from 2013 to 2018. He held various posts in the Integrated Bar of the Philippines, including the positions of National Secretary and Governor for Western Mindanao from 2007 to 2017. In 2005, he became the Founding Chairperson of the Muslim Legal Assistance Foundation, Inc., and Incorporator/Founding Member of the Executive Committee of the Legal Network for Truthful Elections in 2007;

**WHEREAS**, he was the Founding Deputy Legal Counsel and Ex-Officio Member of the Executive Committee of Lakas Christian-Muslim Democrats of the Philippines, formerly the National Union of Christian Democrats-United Muslim Democrats of the Philippines. He was the Founding National Spokesman of the United Muslim Democrats of the Philippines;

**WHEREAS**, he was the Founding President of Suara Bangsa Moro Party List in 2005. He was also a Convenor of the White Ribbon Movement;

**WHEREAS**, Commissioner Marohomsalic was a Member of the Panel of Independent Lawyers that assisted the Government and the Moro Islamic Liberation Front Peace Panels in the drafting of some of the provisions of the Bangsamoro Basic Law in 2014;

**WHEREAS**, he was a Convenor and Vice President of the Philippine Center for Islam and Democracy;

**WHEREAS**, in 2014, as a dedicated peace advocate, he worked as the publicity ambassador for Heavenly Culture, World Peace, Restoration of Light Philippines, a non-government organization associated with the United Nations Department of Global Communications and dedicated to achieving world peace through heavenly culture and global peace activities;

**WHEREAS**, on February 20, 2023, he took an oath as Commissioner of the National Amnesty Commission, demonstrating his importance in the government’s amnesty program in promoting peace in the country;

**WHEREAS**, his contributions to the Bangsamoro Region encompass his work as a legal professional, author, and advocate for human rights, as evidenced by the 1999 publication of his speeches as Commissioner of Human Rights by the Institute of Foreign Service under the title “*Towards Peace, Autonomy and Human Rights*”, and by the 2001 book “*Aristocrats of the Malay Race: A History of the Bangsa Moro in the Philippines*”, written under a research project funded by the Mindanao State University and the Ramos Peace and Development Foundation. He also recently published “*Maratabat: A Novel on Rido and Revenge Justice;*”

**WHEREAS**, he intervened and defended Bangsamoro peace agreements before the Supreme Court twice: first, the Memorandum of Agreement on Ancestral Domain in August 2008, and second, the Bangsamoro Organic Law in January 2019;

**WHEREAS**, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament expresses its profound condolences on the passing of Commissioner Nasser A. Marohomsalic. It also expresses its sincere gratitude for the invaluable contributions to the Bangsamoro Region and the country of the late Commissioner:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To recognize the contributions of the late Commissioner Attorney Nasser A. Marohomsalic for the Bangsamoro people and to express its deepest condolences and sympathies to the family.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To present this Resolution to the bereaved family of the late Commissioner Atty. Nasser A. Marohomsalic, for information.

**ADOPTED**, *Safar* 16, 1446/August 20, 2024.
