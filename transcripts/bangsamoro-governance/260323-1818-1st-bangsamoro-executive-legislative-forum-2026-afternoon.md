## Organized Notes

**Opening and Moderation**
- **Floor Leader John Anthony L. Lim** (Parliament Floor Leader/Spokesperson) and **Cabinet Secretary Mohd Asnin Pendatun** co-moderated the afternoon open forum session
- The session continued the Q&A from the morning's 1st Bangsamoro Government Executive-Legislative Forum 2026

**Constitutionality of the BARMM Budget (Supreme Court Petition)**
- **Deputy Speaker Jose I. Lorena** (Deputy Speaker and Vice Chair, Committee on Finance) confirmed a **petition exists in the Supreme Court** questioning the constitutionality of the BARMM budget
- The Supreme Court has **not issued any order** to stop budget implementation; no **restraining order** has been received
- Lorena argued the budget was designed to fund **social services** and **education**, making a TRO unlikely
- He asserted that when **budget discretion** is granted to a legislative committee, the Supreme Court substituting its own judgment would constitute **judicial overreach**

*Quotable Quotes:*
> "I don't believe that the Supreme Court will at this point in time issue a restraining order to restrain us from implementing programs and projects, especially those priority areas identified in the budget." [18:41]
> "When matters are given to a committee of the Congress of the Philippines or the Senate, they will not stop it and substitute it by their discretion. It will be an overreach." [20:27]

**Safeguarding Transition Gains for the Regular Government**
- Cabinet Secretary Pendatun stated that **policies and safeguards** have been implemented within Parliament and the Cabinet to ensure the **momentum of the transition period** continues
- He emphasized that the **results of the upcoming elections** will be a major factor in guaranteeing that progress is carried over to the regular government

**Non-IRA Barangay Subsidy Increase**
- **Minister Ubaida C. Pacasem** (Minister of Finance, Budget, and Management) announced the **subsidy for non-IRA barangays increased from PHP 100,000 to PHP 120,000** effective 2025
- The increase applies to **47 validated barangays** across the region, not just Maguindanao del Sur
- Guidelines are available at the **MFBM website** (mfbm.bangsamoro.gov.ph)

**Independent Ministry of Energy for BARMM**
- **Deputy Speaker Baintan A. Ampatuan** reported the bill to **split the MENRE** (Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy) into separate ministries is in **advanced stage**
- A **focus group discussion** has been completed; the bill will next go to the **Committee on Amendments** before elevation to the plenary
- She noted the **Bangsamoro Organic Law** already provides for a dedicated energy ministry and urged fellow MPs to support the bill

**National Roads and Infrastructure Under National Government Competence**
- **Chief Minister Abdulraof A. Macacua** raised the problem of **national highways** within BARMM territory that remain under the **Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH)** jurisdiction
- The public **blames the BARMM government** for poor road conditions even though these roads are **not under regional competence**
- He proposed that BARMM **justify using its own funds** to repair national roads, since the people affected are Bangsamoro constituents
- Deputy Speaker Lorena cited **Republic Act 11054** (BOL), which allows BARMM to **identify critical highways** and submit proposals to DPWH for implementation
- He referenced **Governor Alvarez of Palawan**, who advanced highway construction costs and had DPWH **reimburse** the province
- **MP Jaafar Apollo Mikhail L. Matalam** (Chair, Committee on Public Works) was asked to take cognizance of the issue

*Quotable Quotes (Chief Minister Macacua):*
> "We need to justify the use of the money of the BARM even for national government competence, because we can do it. We have money. Our people are being victimized." [29:40]

**Peace, Order, and Security Crisis**
- **Deputy Floor Leader Randolph C. Parcasio** delivered a passionate appeal about the **peace and order crisis** in BARMM, citing **brutal and anomalous killings** in the region
- He stressed that **Bangsamoro people are now fighting each other**, not external enemies, and questioned how the region can develop its **economy** without addressing ground-level insecurity
- He called for a **joint effort** between the regional government, local government units, and the MILF to stop the violence
- Chief Minister Macacua responded that **security is not the sole concern of government** but requires collaboration with the **AFP, PNP**, and former combatant groups (MILF, MNLF)
- He pointed to the **Regional Peace and Order Council** and **Provincial Peace and Order Councils** as existing mechanisms and offered to invite Parcasio to participate

*Quotable Quotes (Deputy Floor Leader Parcasio):*
> "How can we develop our economy with this kind of situation on the ground? Our enemy is no longer our enemy -- we are fighting each other." [38:49]
> "If we don't stop all this killing, nothing will happen to us. What is this autonomous government for if it's only political?" [43:01]

**Education Crisis: Struggling Readers and EDCOM Report**
- The forum addressed the **EDCOM report** showing BARMM provinces, especially **Tawi-Tawi**, ranking lowest in **reading performance** nationwide
- **Mayor Bruce Dela Cruz Matabalao** (Mayor, Cotabato City) shared Cotabato City's intervention: redirecting **Special Education Fund (SEF)** collections toward **reading programs** rather than athletics or uniform purchases
- He noted only **three LGUs** in BARMM collect SEF: **Cotabato City, Upi, and Datu Paglas**
- Cotabato City is redirecting **50% of its PHP 40 million SEF** collection to improve reading outcomes
- **Deputy Speaker Benjamin T. Loong** mentioned plans to meet with the **Schools Division Superintendent of Tawi-Tawi** to address challenges caused by the province's **island geography and logistics**
- Multiple speakers championed the **Synergeia Foundation**, an NGO with proven track records in **education upliftment**, including the **Tarabasa Program** (reading skills intervention in partnership with DSWD)
- A concern was raised that **MBHTE** (Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education) has been **closed to coordination** with NGOs like Synergeia Foundation
- **Board Member Paglas** (Maguindanao del Norte) seconded the call to partner with Synergeia, citing successful meetings with mayors from across the region

**Waste-to-Energy Technology**
- Chief Minister Macacua mentioned that some **BARMM hospitals** have procured technology to **convert hospital waste into useful output**
- The question on **waste-to-energy for jobs, reduced electric bills, and environmental benefits** was noted for further study by the relevant ministries

**Legislative Updates**
- **Stray Animal Sanctuary Bill**: noted for referral to appropriate committee at the upcoming **Committee on Rules meeting on March 28**
- **GAD Code** (Gender and Development Code): confirmed to be in **last stage of committee deliberations**, as earlier reported by **MP Abrar J. Hataman** (Chair, Committee on Youth, Women, and Family)
- **Agricultural Productivity**: the question on **40% wastage in rural areas** was referred to the **Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform**

**Possibility of Sulu Returning to BARMM**
- Deputy Speaker Lorena stated the **door remains open** for Sulu's return, noting the BARMM's **PHP 114 billion budget** can accommodate Sulu's needs
- He said a **bill in Congress** could restore the province, and emphasized the **historical narrative of the Bangsamoro is incomplete without Sulu**
- **Deputy Speaker Nabil A. Tan** affirmed the possibility, noting BARMM continues to pay **Sulu employee salaries** (MBHTE, MAPAR, MENRE) and that people may change their minds once they see BARMM's contributions

*Quotable Quotes (Deputy Speaker Lorena):*
> "The historical narrative of the Bangsamoro cannot be complete without the participation of the people of Sulu. That cultural affiliation cannot be destroyed by any act of Congress or decision of the Supreme Court." [1:05:00]

**Bangsamoro Women's Commission Advocacy**
- BWC Chairperson **Hadja Bainon G. Karon** presented three legislative/executive priorities:
  1. **Joint Memorandum Circular (JMC No. 2026-01)**: guidelines for **localizing Republic Act 9710** (Magna Carta of Women) in BARMM, already designed and pending signatures from MFBM, BPDA, BWC, and the Office of the Chief Minister
  2. **Bangsamoro GAD Code**: urged **urgent passage** to institutionalize gender-responsive governance as mandatory, not optional
  3. **Executive Order on Women, Peace, and Security**: mandating integration of the **Regional Action Plan on WPS** into all BARMM ministries' programs and activities
- She framed these as **moral imperatives** for inclusive governance and lasting peace

**Salary Release Inconsistencies**
- A question was raised about **inconsistent salary releases** across BARMM offices, with some receiving on time while others face delays
- Cabinet Secretary Pendatun acknowledged that **first quarter releases** are typically delayed and committed to looking into the disparities

**Tourism and Trade Initiatives**
- **Minister Farserina A. Mohammad Muhammad** (Minister of Trade, Investment, and Tourism) outlined the BARMM tourism agenda centered on **culture-based and experience-driven tourism**:
  - **Community-based and community-led tourism** development
  - **Heritage experiences**: weaving, culinary tourism, camp-to-tourism-site conversion
  - **Halal and Muslim-friendly tourism** promotion
  - Strengthened **promotional campaigns and partnerships**

**Barter Trade Progress and Challenges**
- Minister Farserina A. Mohammad reported that **Barter Trade Operational Guidelines** were adopted in **September 2025**
- The **national government through Customs** officially recognized BARMM's guidelines via **CMC Series of 2026**
- The guidelines were included in the **BIMP-EAGA agenda** during the Philippines' chairmanship in 2025
- **Minister Termisi** (Ministry of Transportation and Communications) discussed the **non-conventional size ship (NCSS)** framework for cross-border trade with **Malaysia (Sabah), Brunei, and Indonesia**
- Key challenge: vessels registered in **Region 9** need to be re-registered as **BARMM home-ported** for better operational control
- **Deputy Speaker Benjamin T. Loong** recounted efforts to establish a trade route through **Latato** (nearest port to Bongao, Tawi-Tawi) to Sabah, and called for organizing accredited barter traders
- **Deputy Floor Leader Suharto M. Ambolodto** raised the critical issue of **counter-trade** -- BARMM must produce goods to export, not just import, noting that Malaysia, Brunei, and Indonesia are all **halal net importers** of products available in the Bangsamoro
- Minister Farserina A. Mohammad emphasized that the operational guidelines are a **starting point** and the regulatory framework must come first before trading can scale

**Supreme Court and BARMM Elections**
- Deputy Speaker Lorena stated the forum **cannot pre-empt the Supreme Court** on petitions regarding **Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) 86 and 88**
- The **Commission on Elections** is preparing for the **September election** but awaits the **signing of the law** and the enrolled joint bicameral report

**Interagency Accountability**
- Cabinet Secretary Pendatun confirmed that ministries missing **crucial interagency meetings** are asked for **explanations** through his office, and continuing absences trigger formal attention from leadership

**Provincial Impressions and Commitments**
- **Tawi-Tawi** (via Deputy Speaker Loong): reiterated concerns about **rising fuel costs and commodity prices**
- **Basilan**: representative committed to relaying all concerns to the provincial government and pursuing **relevant legislation**
- **Lanao del Sur** (Provincial Development Officer): highlighted critical issues of **conflicting data between agencies**, **duplicity of programs**, **overlapping functions**, and **waste of resources**; proposed creating a **single unified program per province** to rally all line agencies around, and requested a **regular tactical platform** for technical coordination
- **Maguindanao del Norte** (Board Member Abasquita): submitted two resolutions -- (1) establishing a **BARMM district collector for customs** (currently a sub-port of Davao/Region 9), and (2) **expanding the Freeport of Polloc** to serve as a regional trade hub including barter trade
- **Cotabato City** (Mayor Matabalao): thanked the Chief Minister for the forum and for the commitment to recover the **lost PHP 1.6 billion NTA**; appealed for approval of a **PHP 180 million sanitary landfill** project pending with MFBM for over three years, which would be the **first high-tech sanitary landfill in BARMM**
- **Maguindanao del Sur**: commended the forum as **timely and meaningful**, pledged to **harmonize local legislative agenda** with regional priorities
- **Special Geographic Areas** (via MP Mohammad Kelie U. Antao): expressed gratitude to BARMM line agencies and affirmed good organizational coordination despite rural challenges

**Chief Minister Macacua's Closing Response**
- Directed the forum to become a **regular quarterly activity**, with province-by-province reviews
- Instructed Minister Pacasem to **approve Cotabato City's sanitary landfill** application if there are no issues
- Called for a **special meeting to harmonize conflicting data** across agencies
- Asked Maguindanao del Norte to **submit the customs resolution** for consideration
- Committed to addressing the **Freeport of Polloc** issue through coordination with the national government
- Affirmed all remaining concerns would be **tackled in follow-up sessions**
