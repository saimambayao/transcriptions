### V. PROCEEDINGS

#### A. Welcome Remarks and Rationale

Deputy Speaker Atty. Laisa M. Alamia delivered the welcome remarks and presented the rationale of the forum. She described it as a strategic, results-oriented platform designed to bring together Bangsamoro government leadership and constituent local government units. The forum, she explained, would give local chief executives the opportunity to present priority concerns and development challenges directly to the Chief Minister and Speaker. It would also ensure governance issues are communicated for coordinated, timely, and policy-driven responses. Deputy Speaker Alamia further outlined the forum's role in strengthening institutional linkages between different levels of government, aligning cabinet legislative priorities with LGU-level development needs, and reinforcing the principle of participatory and inclusive governance under the Bangsamoro Organic Law.

---

#### B. Message of Unity — Wali of the Bangsamoro

His Eminence Sheikh Muslim M. Guiamaden, Wali of the Bangsamoro, delivered his message entirely in Arabic with a Filipino paraphrase. The Wali centered his address on unity as both a divine command and a practical governance principle.

He declared that unity is a Quranic mandate, a prophetic method, and a historical lesson — not merely a political stance. The Bangsamoro's institutions and achievements, he said, were built through collective unity around one goal, overcoming past disagreements. Managing diversity within the framework of unity is the true source of strength; uniformity is not required.

> "Our call for unity is not merely a political position — it is a divine command, a prophetic method, and a clear historical lesson. Allah says: 'Hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together and do not become divided.'"

The greatest danger, the Wali warned, is not difference of opinion but its transformation into internal division that weakens institutions. He called for continued cooperation with the national government under President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. on the basis of mutual respect and partnership. He urged that unity be made a permanent method, cooperation a work style, and the interest of the Bangsamoro the highest goal.

> "The strength of a nation lies not in the differences of its components, but in its ability to manage those differences within the framework of unity."

#### C. Parliamentary Report — Speaker of the BTA Parliament

BTA Parliament Secretary General Prof. Raby B. Angkal introduced Speaker Mohammad S. Yacob, who then delivered a detailed report on parliamentary operations.

The Speaker began by outlining the governance structure. The BTA Parliament operates as a unicameral parliamentary body with 77 members exercising three functions: legislation, representation, and oversight. The Wali serves as ceremonial head, while executive authority is vested in the Chief Minister endorsed by the parliamentary majority.

On transparency, Speaker Yacob reported that Parliament now livestreams plenary sessions on social media. Live streaming of public hearings and committee meetings is also being initiated. All resolutions and session summaries are published on the BTA Parliament website. He announced that Atty. John Anthony L. Lim had been appointed as official BTA Parliament Spokesperson.

The Speaker identified seven priority bills mandated by the Bangsamoro Organic Law: the Bangsamoro Revenue Code, the Bangsamoro Investment Energy Development Code, the Magna Carta for PWDs in BARMM, the Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority, the Bangsamoro Gender and Development Code, the Bangsamoro Code of Muslim Personal Laws, and the Institutionalization of Darul Ifta in the Bangsamoro.

Since October 21, 2025, Parliament enacted seven laws: the Bangsamoro Labor Code, the Bangsamoro Nutrition Commission Act, the Bangsamoro Budget System Act, the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro 2026, the Bangsamoro Parliamentary District Act of 2025, the removal of "None of the Above" from the official ballot (amending BAA No. 35/Electoral Code), and the Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Program. Six fundamental codes had already been passed to support LGUs: the Administrative Code, Civil Service Code, Electoral Code, Education Code, Indigenous Peoples Code, and Local Governance Code.

Speaker Yacob then reported on coordination activities. Coordination meetings had been conducted across all five provinces — Maguindanao del Sur, Maguindanao del Norte, Lanao del Sur, Tawi-Tawi, and Basilan — culminating in this forum. The Camp and Community Visitations Program reached six MILF camps: Camp Abubakar, Camp Badar, Camp Umar, and Camp Rajamuda in Maguindanao, as well as Camp Bilal and Camp Busra in Lanao del Sur. Parliament satellite offices now operate in the provinces, supplemented by a Manila Liaison Office in BGC, Taguig. The Speaker also noted engagement with foreign dignitaries and international platforms, coordination with Presidential Adviser on Peace, Reconciliation and Unity (OPAPRU) Secretary Carlito Galvez Jr., an official courtesy to the leadership of the Philippine Senate and House of Representatives on November 11, participation in the GPH-MILF Transitional Justice Technical Working Group on December 9, and the activation of the Philippine Congress-Bangsamoro Parliament (PCBP) with Asia Foundation on January 15.

> "We pass laws, you bring them to life. We define policies, you translate them into services."

> "The success of our laws is not measured by the number of laws we pass, but by their impact in your communities. You are not just implementers — you are co-leaders of the Bangsamoro people."

#### D. Executive Agenda — Interim Chief Minister

Cabinet Secretary Mohd Asnin Pendatun introduced Interim Chief Minister Abdulraof A. Macacua, who presented the Five Pillars of Governance under the "Mas Matatag" framework.

The first pillar, **Mas Matatag na Gobyerno (Stronger Government)**, calls for professional institutions built on a merit system, fiscal responsibility, digital governance, and accountability mechanisms, with local ordinances and standards aligned to the regional government.

The second pillar, **Mas Matatag na Kabuhayan (Stronger Livelihood)**, aims for tangible economic progress for farmers, fisherfolk, youth, entrepreneurs, and workers. This entails a strengthened investment framework, MSME support, agricultural modernization, and Islamic finance mechanisms.

The third pillar, **Mas Matatag na Pamayanan (Stronger Communities)**, focuses on development visible at the barangay level — in schools, health services, infrastructure, water systems, and social protection.

The fourth pillar, **Mas Matatag na Seguridad (Stronger Security)**, treats peace as the foundation of governance and prioritizes implementation of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro. The Chief Minister stressed the need to institutionalize peace through systems, not personalities.

> "Security is not about enforcement. It is about mediation, prevention, and trust. We must institutionalize peace so it does not depend on personality, but on system."

The fifth pillar, **Mas Matatag na Pananampalataya (Stronger Faith)**, advances faith-guided governance with support for Madrasah education, the Sharia system, interfaith harmony, and responsible engagement with religious institutions.

The Chief Minister emphasized alignment across all levels of government.

> "If we pass budget without coordination, we delay progress. If we implement without alignment, we waste resources. We cannot afford either. We cannot afford duplication. We cannot afford contradiction. We cannot afford institutional competition."

He also raised contractor issues, noting complaints about substandard projects. He cautioned against unfairly blaming LGUs when contractors may also be at fault.

> "We inherited autonomy from sacrifice. We must secure it through responsible leadership."

#### E. Open Forum — Morning Session

The morning open forum was moderated by BTA Parliament Floor Leader and Spokesperson Atty. John Anthony L. Lim and Cabinet Secretary Mohd Asnin Pendatun. Questions were submitted via Slido (QR code) and from the floor.

**1. Fuel Crisis Response.** The Tawi-Tawi delegation raised the fuel crisis as an urgent cross-cutting concern. Cabinet Secretary Pendatun reported that an emergency cabinet meeting had already been conducted and a special task force created under the Office of the Chief Minister, with BARMM RREDY serving as secretariat. In the short term, the government would deploy a targeted social amelioration program and fuel subsidies for the transport sector. For the long term, the Attorney General was studying the legality of direct fuel importation from Malaysia or Brunei. Atty. Lim added that MTIT had deployed price monitoring teams and was cracking down on fuel hoarding by gasoline stations. Diesel was expected to increase by Php13 the following day, and some stations were already limiting fuel to Php500 per vehicle. Chief Minister Macacua stressed that the government must prepare for a worst-case scenario in which fuel for vehicles runs out entirely.

**2. BTA Bill 387 — Fiscal Autonomy for OCM-Attached Agencies.** A Slido question raised the status of BTA Bill 387, which would grant fiscal autonomy to agencies attached to the Office of the Chief Minister. Deputy Speaker Baintan A. Ampatuan and Committee on Amendments Chair MP Sittie Fahanie S. Uy-Oyod confirmed that a round table discussion is scheduled for April 6, after three prior reschedulings. The agencies affected include the Bangsamoro Information Office, Settlers Office, Darul Ifta, and others. Chief Minister Macacua expressed support for the bill, noting that it would address criticism that too much of the budget is concentrated in the OCM.

**3. BTA Bill 393 — Matanog District Hospital.** In response to a Slido question, Deputy Speaker Atty. Laisa Alamia reported that the Committee on Health had scheduled meetings for the following two days. She noted that a hospital framework requires guidance from the Ministry of Health on prioritization, given that multiple MPs have filed bills to create or upgrade hospitals across the region.

**4. Quality Control of Public Works.** An anonymous Slido question raised concerns about the quality of public works projects. Deputy Speaker Ampatuan reported that she had filed a resolution to create a Quality Assurance Unit within the Ministry of Public Works, modeled after the DPWH's QAU. The resolution had already been approved at committee level and awaits elevation to plenary. Chief Minister Macacua confirmed that he had received many complaints about substandard projects via text. He cautioned that investigations should cover all sides, noting that contractors sometimes unfairly blame LGUs.

**5. Revenue Code Status.** Deputy Speaker Atty. Nabil A. Tan responded to a Slido question on the Revenue Code's status. The code is nearly complete, with amendments finished. Only one MP reservation remains — from MP Naguib G. Sinarimbo on certain provisions. It is the only remaining priority code mandated by the BOL.

**6. Natural Resource Exploration and Fuel Self-Sufficiency.** MENRE Minister Akmad A. Brahim addressed questions on natural resource exploration. The ministry is coordinating with the Department of Energy on guidance and collaboration. On Sulu oil exploration, four companies have bid on a service contract, though exploration takes seven to ten years. Kapaay exploration is ongoing with an estimated timeline of five to seven years. At Liguasan Marsh, a previous investor (DML) withdrew, and the area is now open for new bidding. Minister Brahim clarified that fuel pricing is controlled by the national government under the Oil Deregulation Law (Republic Act 8479), and MENRE's role is limited to monitoring and anti-hoarding enforcement.

**7. Gender and Development Code (BTA Bill 336).** MP Abrar J. Hataman, Chair of the Committee on Youth, Women, and Family, reported that a new technical working group convened on March 10–11 in Davao. Contentious issues were finalized with Darul Ifta guidance. The amendment period would begin within days, and the committee report to plenary was expected soon.

**8. Cotabato City Budget Crisis.** Mayor Bruce Dela Cruz Matabalao of Cotabato City raised an urgent budget shortfall. The city's NTA was certified at Php3.4 billion in October but was reduced to Php1.8 billion in February — a Php1.6 billion gap caused by a DBM error. The DBM offered partial assistance through the LGSF, but it was insufficient to cover the shortfall. The Mayor requested regional government assistance for big-ticket projects, including two bridges connecting Cotabato City to Datu Odin Sinsuat and Sultan Kudarat. Committee on Finance Chair MP Kitem D. Kadatuan Jr. deferred to the MFBM, and Minister Ubaida C. Pacasem referenced the IMPACT program for high-impact projects as a possible funding source. Chief Minister Macacua stated that the government is proactively identifying LGUs to assist, rather than waiting for individual requests.

**9. Maguindanao Province Split in Commissions.** BYC Chairperson Nasserudin D. Dunding raised the issue that commissioners still represent both Maguindanao del Norte and del Sur despite the province's split. Deputy Speaker Ampatuan explained that the Chief Minister has continuing power to restructure the bureaucracy via executive order under the Administrative Code — no legislation is needed. Cabinet Secretary Pendatun confirmed that a cabinet memorandum is being prepared to formalize the split in commission representation. The Chief Minister gave initial instructions to prioritize this matter.

#### F. Open Forum — Afternoon Session

The afternoon session resumed at approximately 1:30 PM, continuing with Slido and floor questions.

**1. Constitutionality of the BARMM Budget (Supreme Court Petition).** Deputy Speaker Atty. Jose I. Lorena addressed a previously submitted question about a petition in the Supreme Court questioning the constitutionality of the BARMM budget. He reported that the Supreme Court has not issued any order stopping budget implementation; no restraining order has been received. The budget was designed for social services and education, making a TRO unlikely. Deputy Speaker Lorena argued that when budget discretion is granted to a legislative committee, the Supreme Court substituting its own judgment would amount to judicial overreach.

> "I don't believe that the Supreme Court will at this point in time issue a restraining order to restrain us from implementing programs and projects, especially those priority areas identified in the budget."

**2. Safeguarding Transition Gains for Regular Government.** Cabinet Secretary Pendatun addressed how transition gains would be preserved for the incoming regular government. Policies and safeguards have been implemented within both Parliament and Cabinet to maintain momentum. The results of the upcoming elections, he noted, will be a major factor in guaranteeing continuity. A final transition report — now on its second draft — is being prepared as required by the BOL and will be submitted to Parliament, both houses of Congress, and the Office of the President. He also announced that the Philippine Congress-Bangsamoro Parliament Forum was scheduled for the following day, March 24. The BARMM election is expected in September or October 2026.

**3. Non-IRA Barangay Subsidy Increase.** A question was raised regarding 36 barangays in Maguindanao del Sur receiving the non-IRA subsidy. MFBM Minister Pacasem confirmed that the subsidy had been increased from Php100,000 to Php120,000 effective 2025. The increase applies to 47 validated barangays across the region, not limited to Maguindanao del Sur. Guidelines are available at mfbm.bangsamoro.gov.ph.

**4. Independent Ministry of Energy for BARMM.** Deputy Speaker Ampatuan responded to a question about establishing a separate Ministry of Energy. A bill to split the MENRE into separate ministries is in an advanced stage. A focus group discussion has been completed, and the next step is deliberation by the Committee on Amendments before elevation to plenary. She noted that the Bangsamoro Organic Law already provides for a dedicated energy ministry, and she urged fellow MPs to support the bill.

**5. National Roads and Infrastructure Under National Government Competence.** Chief Minister Macacua himself raised the issue of national highways within BARMM remaining under DPWH jurisdiction while the public blames the BARMM government for their poor conditions. He proposed that BARMM justify using its own funds to repair these roads since constituents are directly affected. Deputy Speaker Lorena cited Republic Act 11054 (BOL), which allows BARMM to identify critical highways and submit proposals to DPWH. He referenced Governor Alvarez of Palawan, who advanced highway costs and had DPWH reimburse the province. The matter was referred to MP Jaafar Apollo Mikhail L. Matalam, Chair of the Committee on Public Works, for further action.

> "We need to justify the use of the money of the BARM even for national government competence, because we can do it. We have money. Our people are being victimized." — Chief Minister Macacua

**6. Peace, Order, and Security Crisis.** Deputy Floor Leader Randolph C. Parcasio delivered a passionate appeal about brutal and anomalous killings across BARMM. He stressed that Bangsamoro people are fighting each other, not external enemies. He questioned how the region can develop its economy without ground-level security and called for a joint effort between the regional government, LGUs, and MILF.

> "How can we develop our economy with this kind of situation on the ground? Our enemy is no longer our enemy — we are fighting each other." — Deputy Floor Leader Parcasio

> "If we don't stop all this killing, nothing will happen to us. What is this autonomous government for if it's only political?" — Deputy Floor Leader Parcasio

Chief Minister Macacua responded that security is not the sole concern of government but requires collaboration with the AFP, PNP, and former combatant groups (MILF, MNLF). He pointed to the Regional Peace and Order Council and Provincial Peace and Order Councils as existing mechanisms. He offered to invite Parcasio to participate in the Regional Peace and Order Council.

**7. Education Crisis: Struggling Readers and EDCOM Report.** A Slido question and floor discussion turned to the education crisis. Mayor Matabalao cited the EDCOM report showing BARMM provinces — especially Tawi-Tawi — ranking lowest in reading performance nationwide. He noted that only three LGUs in BARMM collect the Special Education Fund: Cotabato City, Upi, and Datu Paglas. Cotabato City is redirecting 50% of its Php40 million SEF collection toward reading programs instead of athletics or uniforms. Deputy Speaker Benjamin T. Loong announced plans to meet with the Schools Division Superintendent of Tawi-Tawi to address challenges posed by island geography and logistics. Multiple speakers championed the Synergeia Foundation, an NGO with a proven track record that includes the Tara, Basa! Program — a reading skills intervention in partnership with DSWD. A concern was raised that MBHTE has been closed to coordination with NGOs like Synergeia. A provincial board member from Maguindanao del Norte seconded the call to partner with the foundation, citing successful meetings with mayors across the region. The matter was referred for further coordination with MBHTE.

**8. Waste-to-Energy Technology.** Chief Minister Macacua responded to a Slido question by noting that some BARMM hospitals have procured technology to convert hospital waste into useful output. The topic was noted for further study by relevant ministries.

**9. Possibility of Sulu Returning to BARMM.** Deputy Speaker Lorena and Deputy Speaker Atty. Nabil Tan addressed a question about Sulu's potential return to BARMM. The door remains open — BARMM's Php114 billion budget can accommodate Sulu, and a bill in Congress could restore the province. BARMM continues to pay Sulu employee salaries under MBHTE, MAPAR, and MENRE.

> "The historical narrative of the Bangsamoro cannot be complete without the participation of the people of Sulu. That cultural affiliation cannot be destroyed by any act of Congress or decision of the Supreme Court." — Deputy Speaker Lorena

**10. Bangsamoro Women's Commission Advocacy.** BWC Chairperson Hadja Bainon G. Karon presented three legislative and executive priorities. First, she discussed Joint Memorandum Circular No. 2026-01, which provides guidelines for localizing Republic Act 9710 (Magna Carta of Women) in BARMM; the circular has been designed and is pending signatures from MFBM, BPDA, BWC, and the Office of the Chief Minister. Second, she urged urgent passage of the Bangsamoro GAD Code to institutionalize gender-responsive governance as mandatory, not optional. Third, she called for an Executive Order on Women, Peace, and Security that would mandate integration of the Regional Action Plan on WPS into all BARMM ministries' programs and activities.

**11. Salary Release Inconsistencies.** Cabinet Secretary Pendatun responded to a Slido question about salary release delays. He acknowledged that first quarter releases are typically delayed and committed to looking into disparities between offices.

**12. Tourism and Trade Initiatives.** MTIT Minister Farserina A. Mohammad outlined the tourism agenda, centered on culture-based and experience-driven tourism. The strategy emphasizes community-based and community-led tourism development, heritage experiences such as weaving and culinary tourism, camp-to-tourism-site conversion, and halal and Muslim-friendly tourism promotion.

**13. Barter Trade Progress and Challenges.** Minister Mohammad reported that Barter Trade Operational Guidelines were adopted in September 2025. The national government, through Customs, officially recognized BARMM's guidelines via CMC Series of 2026, and these guidelines were included in the BIMP-EAGA agenda during the Philippines' 2025 chairmanship. MOTC Minister Termizie G. Masahud discussed the non-conventional size ship (NCSS) framework for cross-border trade with Malaysia (Sabah), Brunei, and Indonesia. A key challenge is that vessels registered in Region 9 need re-registration as BARMM home-ported. Deputy Speaker Loong recounted efforts to establish a trade route through Latato — the nearest port to Bongao, Tawi-Tawi — to Sabah. Deputy Floor Leader Suharto M. Ambolodto raised the issue of counter-trade: BARMM must produce goods to export, not just import. He noted that Malaysia, Brunei, and Indonesia are halal net importers of products available in the Bangsamoro. Minister Mohammad emphasized that the operational guidelines are a starting point and that the regulatory framework must come first.

**14. Supreme Court and BARMM Elections.** Deputy Speaker Lorena stated that the forum cannot pre-empt the Supreme Court on petitions regarding BAA 86 and 88. COMELEC is preparing for the September election but awaits the signing of the law and the enrolled joint bicameral report.

**15. Interagency Accountability.** Cabinet Secretary Pendatun addressed a Slido question about interagency coordination. Ministries missing key meetings are asked for explanations, and continuing absences trigger formal attention from leadership.

**16. Legislative Updates (Brief).** The Stray Animal Sanctuary Bill was noted for referral at the Committee on Rules meeting on March 28. A question on agricultural productivity — specifically the 40% wastage rate — was referred to the Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform.

#### G. Provincial Impressions

Representatives from across the BARMM provinces shared their impressions of the forum and highlighted concerns specific to their areas.

Deputy Speaker Benjamin Loong spoke for Tawi-Tawi, reiterating concerns about rising fuel costs and commodity prices. He expressed gratitude for the government's fire relief response in Bongao and for the Ramadan rice distribution under Project Tabang.

The MP representative for Basilan committed to relaying all concerns raised at the forum to the provincial government and pledged to pursue relevant legislation addressing the issues discussed.

Lanao del Sur's Provincial Development Officer reported that the Governor was absent because he was convening the Provincial Economic Crisis Management Committee on fuel prices. The officer raised several technical concerns: conflicting data between agencies, duplicity of programs and overlapping functions leading to wasted resources. She proposed creating a single unified program per province around which all line agencies could rally, and she requested a regular tactical platform for technical coordination between regional and provincial levels.

A provincial board member from Maguindanao del Norte submitted two resolutions from the Office of the Governor, Vice Governor, and Sangguniang Panlalawigan. The first calls for establishing a BARMM district collector for customs, given that the province currently operates as a sub-port of Davao/Region 9. The second seeks to expand the Freeport of Polloc to serve as a regional trade hub, including for barter trade.

Mayor Bruce Dela Cruz Matabalao of Cotabato City thanked the Chief Minister for the forum and for the commitment to recover the lost Php1.6 billion NTA. He appealed for approval of a Php180 million sanitary landfill project that has been pending with MFBM for over three years — the Land Bank loan expired twice during that period. The project would be the first high-tech sanitary landfill in BARMM. The Mayor also noted that Cotabato City recently received recognition as an independent component city by MILG and is pursuing status as the first highly urbanized city in BARMM.

The official representative of Maguindanao del Sur commended the forum as timely and meaningful. She pledged to harmonize the local legislative agenda with regional priorities and affirmed the province's commitment to inclusive development, peace building, and good governance.

MP Mohammad Kelie U. Antao spoke for the Special Geographic Areas, expressing gratitude to BARMM line agencies and affirming good organizational coordination despite rural challenges.

---

#### H. Closing Directives — Chief Minister

Interim Chief Minister Macacua issued the following directives before closing the forum. First, the Executive-Legislative Forum shall become a regular quarterly activity with province-by-province reviews. Second, MFBM Minister Pacasem was directed to approve Cotabato City's sanitary landfill application if there are no outstanding issues. Third, a special meeting will be convened to harmonize conflicting data across agencies. Fourth, Maguindanao del Norte is to submit the customs resolution for consideration. Fifth, the Freeport of Polloc expansion will be addressed through coordination with the national government. Sixth, all remaining concerns will be tackled in follow-up sessions.
