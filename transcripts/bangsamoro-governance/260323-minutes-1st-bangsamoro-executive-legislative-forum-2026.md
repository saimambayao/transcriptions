### V. PROCEEDINGS

#### A. Welcome Remarks and Rationale

**Deputy Speaker Atty. Laisa M. Alamia** presented the rationale of the forum, describing it as a **strategic and results-oriented platform** that brings together the Bangsamoro government leadership and constituent local government units. She outlined the forum's objectives:

- Provide LCEs the opportunity to **present priority concerns and development challenges** directly to the Chief Minister and Speaker
- Ensure governance issues are communicated for **coordinated, timely, and policy-driven responses**
- Strengthen **institutional synergy** between different levels of government
- Serve as a venue for **policy alignment** between cabinet legislative priorities and development needs at the LGU level
- Reinforce the principle of **participatory and inclusive governance** under the Bangsamoro Organic Law

---

#### B. Message of Unity -- Wali of the Bangsamoro

**His Eminence Sheikh Muslim M. Guiamaden**, Wali of the Bangsamoro, delivered his message entirely in Arabic (with Filipino paraphrase). His message centered on **unity as both a divine command and practical governance principle**. Key points:

- **Unity is a Quranic mandate, a prophetic method, and a historical lesson** -- not merely a political stance
- The Bangsamoro's institutions and achievements were built through **collective unity around one goal**, overcoming past disagreements
- **Managing diversity within the framework of unity** is the true source of strength; uniformity is not required

> "Our call for unity is not merely a political position -- it is a divine command, a prophetic method, and a clear historical lesson. Allah says: 'Hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together and do not become divided.'"

- The greatest danger is not difference of opinion but its **transformation into internal division** that weakens institutions
- Called for **continued cooperation with the national government** under President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. on the basis of mutual respect and partnership
- Urged that **unity be made a permanent method**, cooperation a work style, and the interest of the Bangsamoro the highest goal

> "The strength of a nation lies not in the differences of its components, but in its ability to manage those differences within the framework of unity."

#### C. Parliamentary Report -- Speaker of the BTA Parliament

**BTA Parliament Secretary General Prof. Raby B. Angkal** introduced **Speaker Mohammad S. Yacob**, who delivered a comprehensive report on parliamentary operations. Key points:

**Governance Structure**
- The BTA Parliament operates as a **unicameral parliamentary body** with 80 members exercising three functions: **legislation, representation, and oversight**
- The Wali serves as ceremonial head; executive authority is vested in the Chief Minister endorsed by the parliamentary majority

**Transparency Measures**
- Parliament now **livestreams plenary sessions** on social media
- Live streaming of **public hearings and committee meetings** is being initiated
- All **resolutions, session summaries** are published on the BTA Parliament website
- **Atty. John Anthony L. Lim** was appointed as official BTA Parliament Spokesperson

**Priority Legislation** (mandated by the Bangsamoro Organic Law):
1. Bangsamoro Revenue Code
2. Bangsamoro Investment Energy Development Code
3. Magna Carta for PWDs in BARMM
4. Lake Lanao Rehabilitation and Development Authority
5. Bangsamoro Gender and Development Code
6. Bangsamoro Code of Muslim Personal Laws
7. Institutionalization of Darul Ifta in the Bangsamoro

**Laws Enacted** (October 21, 2025 to date -- 7 bills):
1. Bangsamoro Labor Code
2. Bangsamoro Nutrition Commission Act
3. Bangsamoro Budget System Act
4. General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro 2026
5. Bangsamoro Parliamentary District Act of 2025
6. Removal of "None of the Above" from official ballot (amending BAA No. 35/Electoral Code)
7. Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Program

**Six Fundamental Codes** already passed to support LGUs:
- Administrative Code, Civil Service Code, Electoral Code, Education Code, Indigenous Peoples Code, Local Governance Code

**Coordination Activities**
- Coordination meetings conducted across **all 5 provinces** (Maguindanao del Sur, Maguindanao del Norte, Lanao del Sur, Tawi-Tawi, Basilan), culminating in this forum
- **Camp and Community Visitations Program** at 6 MILF camps: Camp Abubakar, Camp Badar, Camp Umar, Camp Rajamuda (Maguindanao), Camp Bilal and Camp Busra (Lanao del Sur)
- Parliament satellite offices in provinces and a **Manila Liaison Office** in BGC, Taguig
- Engagement with foreign dignitaries and international platforms
- Coordination with OPAPRU Secretary Carlito Galvez
- Official courtesy to leadership of Philippine Senate and House of Representatives (November 11)
- Participation in GPH-MILF Transitional Justice Technical Working Group (December 9)
- Philippine Congress-Bangsamoro Parliament (PCBP) activation with Asia Foundation (January 15)

> "We pass laws, you bring them to life. We define policies, you translate them into services."

> "The success of our laws is not measured by the number of laws we pass, but by their impact in your communities. You are not just implementers -- you are co-leaders of the Bangsamoro people."

#### D. Executive Agenda -- Interim Chief Minister

**Cabinet Secretary Mohd Asnin Pendatun** introduced **Interim Chief Minister Abdulraof A. Macacua**, who presented the **Five Pillars of Governance** (Mas Matatag):

**1. Mas Matatag na Gobyerno (Stronger Government)**
- Professional institutions, **merit system**, fiscal responsibility
- **Digital governance** and accountability mechanisms
- Alignment of local ordinances and standards with regional government

**2. Mas Matatag na Kabuhayan (Stronger Livelihood)**
- Tangible economic progress for **farmers, fisherfolk, youth, entrepreneurs, workers**
- Strengthened **investment framework, MSME support, agricultural modernization**
- **Islamic finance** mechanisms

**3. Mas Matatag na Pamayanan (Stronger Communities)**
- Development visible at the **barangay level**
- Focus on schools, health services, infrastructure, water systems, social protection

**4. Mas Matatag na Seguridad (Stronger Security)**
- Peace as the foundation; implementation of the **Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro**
- **Institutionalize peace through systems, not personalities**

> "Security is not about enforcement. It is about mediation, prevention, and trust. We must institutionalize peace so it does not depend on personality, but on system."

**5. Mas Matatag na Pananampalataya (Stronger Faith)**
- Faith-guided governance; support for **Madrasah education, Sharia system**
- **Interfaith harmony** and responsible engagement with religious institutions

The Chief Minister emphasized **alignment** across all levels:

> "If we pass budget without coordination, we delay progress. If we implement without alignment, we waste resources. We cannot afford either. We cannot afford duplication. We cannot afford contradiction. We cannot afford institutional competition."

He also raised **contractor issues**, noting complaints about substandard projects, and cautioned against unfairly blaming LGUs when contractors may also be at fault.

> "We inherited autonomy from sacrifice. We must secure it through responsible leadership."
