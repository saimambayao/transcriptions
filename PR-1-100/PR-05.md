**A RESOLUTION**
**DEFINING THE REPRESENTATION OF OTHER SECTORS IN THE COUNCIL OF LEADERS AS AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL RELATIONS BODY, AS PROVIDED UNDER THE BANGSAMORO ORGANIC LAW (BOL)**

**WHEREAS,** Section 9, Article VI of the Bangsamoro Organic Law defines the composition of the Council of Leaders as an intergovernmental relations body. It advises the Chief Minister on matters of governance in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);

**WHEREAS,** specifically enumerated under the provision are the following members which will compose the Council of Leaders:

(a) Chief Minister as head of the council;
(b) Members of the Congress of the Philippines from the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
(c) Provincial governors and mayors of chartered cities in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;
(d) Representatives of traditional leaders, non-Moro indigenous communities, women, settler communities, the Ulama, youth, and Bangsamoro communities outside of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region; and
(e) Representatives of other sectors;

**WHEREAS,** the same provision provides that [t]he mechanism of representation and number of representatives in paragraphs (d) and (e) shall be determined by the Parliament. The representation of the non-Moro indigenous communities shall be pursuant to their customary laws and indigenous processes;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) hereby defines the mechanism and number of representatives in paragraphs (d) and (e) of the mentioned provision;

**WHEREAS,** the representatives of the traditional leaders, non-Moro indigenous communities, women, settler communities, the Ulama, youth, and Bangsamoro communities outside of the BARMM are as follows:

(a) One (1) from the traditional leaders - Member of Parliament from the MILF nominees;
(b) One (1) from the non-Moro indigenous communities – Minister of Indigenous Peoples’ Affairs (MIPA);
(c) One (1) from the women - Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Women Commission (BWC);
(d) One (1) from settler communities – Executive Director of the Office on Settler Communities;
(e) One from the Ulama - Member of Parliament from the MILF nominees who is an Ulama;
(f) One from the youth – Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Youth Commission;
(g) One from the Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM – Executive Director of the Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities under the Office of the Chief Minister; and
(h) One from the non-government organizations (NGOs) – to be identified by the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (BEDC) from among its accredited civil society organizations.

**NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED** by the BTA Parliament, to pass and approve this Resolution Defining the Representation of Other Sectors in the Council of Leaders as an Intergovernmental Relations Body, as Provided by the Bangsamoro Organic Law.

**BE IT RESOLVED FURTHER,** that a copy of this resolution be furnished to the concerned bodies and agencies, for information and appropriate action.
