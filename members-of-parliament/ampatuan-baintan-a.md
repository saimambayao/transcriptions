## Biosketch

**CAREER SUMMARY**

Engr. Baintan Adil-Ampatuan is a visionary leader whose career spans governance, legislation, and development planning. Currently serving as a Member of Parliament in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) **since 2019,**she has played a pivotal role in the legislative and oversight functions of the Bangsamoro government. As **one of the Deputy Speakers** of the second BTA and the **Deputy Minority Leader** during the first BTA, she **has demonstrated** exceptional leadership in navigating complex legislative processes.

In her legislative role, she has been instrumental in authoring transformative laws. Engr. Ampatuan is the principal author of the **Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board Act of 2022,** the **Pura Municipal Hospital Act of 2024,** and the **Maguindanao Provincial Hospital Upgrading Act of 2025**, and **is**a co-principal author of **15** other critical legislative measures. These include the **Bangsamoro Socio-Economic Development Planning Act,** the **Bangsamoro Administrative Code,** and several acts focused on upgrading and establishing healthcare facilities. Throughout her tenure, she has maintained an impressive legislative record, authoring and co-authoring over **320** bills across two parliamentary terms. Her leadership extends to the introduction of almost **250 **resolutions,** **85 of which have been adopted**** by the Bangsamoro Parliament.

Beyond legislation, Deputy Speaker Ampatuan actively participates in oversight and governance. As a Deputy Speaker, she **currently serves on** **17** Statutory Committees and seven (**7**) Parliamentary Committees of the Bangsamoro Parliament. She was the Vice-Chairperson of the Committee on Finance, Budget, and Management for more than **six (6)** years, and her contributions span various committees focused on rules, health, education, agriculture, public works, and social development. Her commitment to normalization and peacebuilding is reflected in her role as **Co-Chairperson** **of the Joint Task Forces on Camps Transformation (JTFCT)**, a key mechanism supporting the region’s peace and development goals.

Deputy Speaker Ampatuan’s administrative expertise is rooted in her time as **Executive Director of the Regional Planning and Development Office (RPDO-ARMM)**. In this capacity, she led the preparation of the ARMM Transition Report, a comprehensive document that served as a cornerstone for the governance shift to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Her leadership extended to managing infrastructure projects as **OIC–District Engineer** at the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH-ARMM).

 Academically, she holds a **Senior Executive Master’s** in National Security Administration (SE-MNSA) from the National Defense College of the Philippines, graduating with distinction and receiving **academic and thesis awards**. She also earned a **Master’s** in Professional Studies in Development Management and a **Bachelor of Science**in Civil Engineering from Notre Dame University, where she became a licensed Civil Engineer.

 Throughout her career, Deputy Speaker Ampatuan has been recognized for her outstanding contributions. Among her accolades are the **Civil Service Commission–ARMM Regional Outstanding Lady Executive (ROLE) Award**, the **Sharief Kabungsuan Merit Award**, and the **Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas Stakeholders Award**. Her commitment to excellence extends beyond the Bangsamoro region, as she has represented the Bangsamoro government in international forums and training programs in Europe, North America, and Asia. These opportunities have allowed her to integrate global best practices into her work, further enhancing her contributions to the region.

She is the principal author of ***The MP’s Little Green Book: A Personal Guide for Members of the Bangsamoro Parliament***, published in 2024 by the Institute of Autonomy and Governance (IAG) and supported by the Australian Government.

Her work stands as a powerful testament to her unwavering commitment to fostering a peaceful, progressive, and resilient Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
