## Biosketch

**BIOSKETCH of MP ABDULRAOF A. MACACUA**  
**Name:** Abdulraof A. Macacua (also known as Sammy Gambar)  
**Birthdate:** September 13, 1957  
**Birthplace:** Gambar, Maguindanao, Philippines  
**Parents:**Macacua W. Tubo-Tubo and Salma B. Macacua  
**Position:** Interim Chief Minister (Present)  
**Affiliation:** Bangsamoro Transition Commission, Member of MILF Peace Negotiating Panel, Chief-of-Staff of the Bangsamoro Islamic Armed Forces (BIAF)

**Early Life and Education:**

Born in the serene town of Gambar, Maguindanao, Abdulraof Macacua’s early years were steeped in the historical and cultural struggles of the Bangsamoro people. His formative years were marked by witnessing the tumultuous fight for self-determination, which ignited his dedication to the cause. Macacua’s early exposure to political and social issues fueled his commitment to education as a tool for empowerment.

**Career and Military Experience:**

Minister Macacua’s career began in 1971 when, motivated by a strong sense of duty toward the Bangsamoro people, he entered the military. His involvement in the revolutionary movement was driven by the pursuit of independence for the Bangsamoro, a vision that guided his actions through the years. Through rigorous training in both local and international military settings, CM Abdulraof rose through the ranks of the Bangsamoro Islamic Armed Forces (BIAF) and eventually became its Chief-of-Staff.

As a key member of the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) Peace Negotiating Panel, he played a pivotal role in shaping the future of the Bangsamoro. His efforts helped pave the way for key advancements in the peace process and the eventual passage of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, which laid the foundation for the establishment of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

**Role in the Bangsamoro Transition:**

* Abdulraof Macacua played a crucial role in the implementation of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL). He was Instrumental in drafting and authoring key legislation, including the Bangsamoro Electoral Code and the Bangsamoro Local Governance Code, which sought to address issues of autonomy, governance, and representation for the Moro people.
* Served as the Minister of the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy within the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, Abdulraof “Sammy Gambar” Macacua’s leadership focuses on environmental sustainability and energy. Macacua is determined to make the Bangsamoro a greener, more climate-resilient region, focusing on the protection of natural resources, environmental conservation, and energy sustainability.
* His role as a Member of Parliament has been marked by his dedication to representing his constituents with integrity and passion. His legislative work is rooted in advocacy for equitable policies that support the marginalized and underserved sectors of society. With his deep understanding of the issues facing both his province and the country, Gambar has become a strong voice for reform, working tirelessly to enact laws that promote sustainable development, social justice, and national unity.
* As Senior Minister, Abdulraof Macacua exemplifies strong leadership and an unwavering commitment to public service. His role in shaping national policy and driving key initiatives has been instrumental in fostering growth, stability, and prosperity. Gambar’s visionary approach emphasizes the importance of collaboration across sectors to address the diverse needs of the Bangsamoro people, particularly in areas of governance, economic development, and social welfare. He has significantly influenced the development and governance of the Bangsamoro  
  Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) through his roles as Senior Minister.
* As 1 ST Governor of the newly created Maguindanao del Norte, Macacua has been a beacon of progress for the province. His leadership has focused on empowering local communities, improving infrastructure, and boosting the region’s agricultural and educational sectors. Under his governance, Maguindanao del Norte has witnessed growth in various sectors, with a focus on peace-building and creating an inclusive environment for all citizens, especially in historically underserved areas. His tenure is marked by a series of strategic initiatives and legislative contributions aimed at fostering economic growth, infrastructural development, and political stability in the region.​

Abdulraof A. Macacua’s life has been dedicated to the Bangsamoro people. Despite the adversity he faced from an early age and throughout his military career, his vision for a free and self-determined Bangsamoro has always been a driving force. Now, in his role as a minister and public servant, he seeks to make a lasting difference by integrating environmental stewardship with his historical commitment to the well-being and prosperity of the Bangsamoro.
