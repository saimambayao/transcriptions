# About This Guidebook

## Purpose and Scope

This guidebook bridges two worlds that rarely speak to each other in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao: **artificial intelligence** and **government operations**.

The 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (2023-2028) dedicates Chapter 8 to technology and innovation — but its focus is on science labs, research scholarships, and metrology facilities. It says nothing about how AI can transform the daily work of government staff. This guidebook fills that gap.

You will learn how AI tools can assist you at every stage of the governance cycle — from strategic planning and budgeting, through program implementation and monitoring, to legislative oversight and codification. Not as a replacement for your judgment, but as a force multiplier for your expertise.

Every chapter is grounded in real BARMM governance work. The examples come from actual BAAs, resolutions, budget documents, and institutional processes. The case studies draw from platforms already built for Bangsamoro institutions. This is not a theoretical manual about what AI might do someday. It is a practical guide about what AI can do for you right now.

---

## Who This Guidebook Is For

This guidebook serves **all BARMM government staff** — from administrative aides to ministers, from committee secretariat to the Office of the Chief Minister. AI is not an IT concern. It is a productivity tool for anyone who reads, writes, analyzes, or decides.

You do not need technical skills to use this guidebook. If you can write a memorandum, you can write an AI prompt. Chapter 2 starts from zero.

---

## How to Use This Guidebook

You have two options:

**Sequential reading** — Start with Part I (Chapters 1-4) to build your AI literacy, then work through Part II (Chapters 5-11) for your governance function, and finish with Part III (Chapters 12-15) for ethics and implementation. This is recommended for first-time readers.

**Chapter-pick reading** — Go directly to the chapter that matches your current work. Each chapter is self-contained, with cross-references to related chapters when you need deeper context.

## What This Guidebook Is Not

This guidebook does **not** teach you how to build AI systems, write code, or train machine learning models. It teaches you how to **use** AI tools to do your existing government work better, faster, and with higher quality.

It does **not** replace your professional judgment. Every AI output must pass through your expertise before it becomes an official government document. Chapter 3 explains this in detail.

It does **not** endorse any single AI vendor or platform. The patterns you learn here work across tools. Specific tools appear only in boxed case studies and appendices, and they will evolve. The principles will not.
