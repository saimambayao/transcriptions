# About the Author

**Saidamen "Sayeed" R. Mambayao** has worked in governance, legislation, technology, and development across the Bangsamoro region for over a decade. He is currently a Consultant at the Office of the Chief Minister — Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC), where he leads policy development, strategic planning, and institutional strengthening for Bangsamoro communities living outside BARMM.

He previously served as OIC-Legislative Affairs at the Bangsamoro Parliament (2025), where he drafted the Bangsamoro Early Childhood Care and Development Bill, created a 39,000-word Bill-Drafting Manual for parliamentary staff, and built the Parliamentarian app — a constitutional research platform with AI agents for legal analysis and BOL interpretation. Before that, he served as Supervising Political Affairs Officer III / Chief of Staff (SG 25) for MP Diamila Disimban-Ramos (2022-2025), leading the drafting of bills on cultural heritage, cooperative development, social justice for Other Bangsamoro Communities, and OBC peace and culture centers.

His government career spans the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), where he directed the Access to Higher and Modern Education Scholarship Portal, and the Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG), where he managed the ESKEY program and served as lead drafter and secretariat head of the Bangsamoro Local Governance Code.

Saidamen is also a full-stack software developer who has built eight government-scale platforms for Bangsamoro institutions, including e-Bangsamoro (a four-portal e-governance system), BangsamoroHR, MoroMarket (a digital marketplace for cooperatives and social enterprises), and Tarbiyyah-MS (a madrasah management system). He maintains 129 AI skills for Claude Code — a library that powers the AI-augmented workflows described throughout this guidebook.

He is the Founder of MoroTech and MoroAcademy (social enterprises empowering Bangsamoro communities through technology and knowledge), the Chairperson of the Moro Developers Community, and the Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Scholars Association. He is the author of seven BARMM guidebooks covering bill drafting, complete staff work, supervision, speech writing, policy recommendations, manual of operations formulation, and strategic planning.

This guidebook is written from the intersection of all three domains — governance, technology, and development — that have defined his career. It is the guidebook he wishes existed when he first started building AI tools for Bangsamoro institutions.
