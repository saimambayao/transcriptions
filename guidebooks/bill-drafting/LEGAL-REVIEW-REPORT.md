## Major Errors

### MAJOR-1: Glossary — Shari'ah entry cites "BOL, Article X, Sections 7-10" but jurisdiction is broader

**Location:** 13-glossary.md, line 137

**Guidebook text:**
> Shari'ah governs personal, family, and property relations involving Muslims in the Bangsamoro. The Parliament has authority to legislate on matters involving Shari'ah within the scope of the BOL, including the jurisdiction and procedures of Shari'ah courts. (BOL, Article X, Sections 7-10)

**Issue:** The citation "Article X, Sections 7-10" is too narrow. The Shari'ah High Court is created in Section 7 and its composition is in Section 9, its compensation in Section 10 — these are accurate. However, the Parliament's authority to enact Shari'ah laws is found in **Section 4** of Article X (not Sections 7-10). The Shari'ah Circuit Courts (Section 5) and District Courts (Section 6) are also relevant to the description. The glossary definition describes the Parliament's Shari'ah legislative power but cites only the sections on the High Court.

**Correct citation:** (BOL, Article X, Sections 1-10) — or more precisely, "Article X, Section 4" for the Parliament's legislative power, and "Sections 5-10" for court jurisdiction and structure.

**Severity:** MAJOR — wrong section numbers for a key concept (Parliament's Shari'ah legislative authority).

---

### MAJOR-2: BOL Key Provisions reference file — Article structure table has incorrect subject labels

**Location:** Not in the guidebook itself, but in the reference file (`bol-key-provisions.md`) that the guidebook relies on.

**Issue in reference file:** The Article Structure table in `bol-key-provisions.md` lists:
- Article VIII as "Transition" — but in the actual BOL, **Article VIII is "Wali"** (the ceremonial head). The Transition provisions are in **Article XVI** (Bangsamoro Transition Authority).
- Article XI as "Economy, Trade, Investment, Tourism" — but in the actual BOL, **Article XI is "National Defense and Security, Public Order and Safety, and Coast Guard Services."** Economy provisions are in **Article XIII** (Regional Economy and Patrimony).
- Article XII as "Fiscal Autonomy" — this is **correct** in the BOL.
- Article XIII as "Transitory Provisions" — but in the BOL, **Article XIII is "Regional Economy and Patrimony."** Transitory provisions are not a separate article; **Article XVI** covers the BTA transition, and **Article XVII** covers amendments/revisions/repeal.

**Impact on guidebook:** The guidebook does not reproduce this table directly. Chapter 1 and Chapter 2 cite BOL articles by number correctly (e.g., "Article XII (Fiscal Autonomy)" and "Article X (Bangsamoro Justice System)" are always correct in the guidebook text). However, drafters who consult the reference file may be misled.

**Severity:** MAJOR — the reference file has incorrect article-to-subject mappings, though the guidebook chapters themselves cite correctly.

### MAJOR-3: Chapter 9 — BDP development goals wording not fully verifiable

**Location:** 10-chapter-09.md, lines 91-96

**Guidebook text lists six BDP goals:**
1. Stable, just, and accountable government
2. Equitable, competitive, and robust economy
3. Peaceful, safe, and resilient Bangsamoro communities
4. Inclusive, responsive, and quality social services
5. Rich and diverse Bangsamoro culture and identity preserved and recognized
6. Strategic, adequate, and climate-resilient infrastructure

**Issue:** These are stated as the BDP 2023-2028 development goals. The BOL itself does not enumerate these specific six goals — they come from the Bangsamoro Development Plan document. The wording cannot be verified against the BOL or Resolution 268 (neither source contains them). The guidebook correctly attributes them to the BDP rather than the BOL, but a reader consulting this as a legal reference should note that these goals are policy-level targets, not statutory provisions.

**Severity:** MAJOR (downgraded from critical because the attribution is correct — the guidebook says "BDP 2023-2028" not "BOL"). However, the exact wording of the six goals should be verified against the actual BDP document, which was not available as an authoritative source for this review.
