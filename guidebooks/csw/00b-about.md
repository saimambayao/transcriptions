# About This Guidebook

## Purpose

This guidebook provides a comprehensive, practical framework for Complete Staff Work (CSW) within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It translates the principles of CSW — as mandated by Memorandum Circular No. 72, s. 2019 and MC No. 2, s. 2022 — into actionable guidance tailored for Bangsamoro governance.

The guidebook introduces the **ADDRESS IT** framework, a seven-step methodology that structures the CSW process from initial analysis through implementation and continuous improvement:

| Step | Mnemonic | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1 | **A** | Analyze the Context |
| 2 | **DD** | Define and Deliberate the Problem or Need |
| 3 | **R** | Research Needed Information |
| 4 | **E** | Explore Options |
| 5 | **S** | Submit Proposal or Report |
| 6 | **S** | Sustain Efforts to Implement |
| 7 | **IT** | Iterate and Improve |

## Intended Audience

This guidebook is written for three primary audiences:

1. **Parliament Staff** — Committee secretariat members, legislative analysts, and research officers who prepare briefers, reports, and recommendations for Members of Parliament
2. **Ministry Personnel** — Staff of Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (MOAs) who prepare proposals, budget requests, project designs, and administrative actions for decision-makers
3. **Government Officers** — Supervisors, directors, and senior staff who review, approve, and champion CSW practices within their organizations

The material is designed for professionals at all experience levels, from newly hired staff learning CSW for the first time to seasoned officers seeking to formalize and improve their existing practices.

## How to Use This Guidebook

### For New Staff
Start with **Chapter 1** (Philosophy) and **Chapter 2** (Bangsamoro Context) to understand why CSW matters. Then read **Chapter 3** to identify which CSW type applies to your work. Follow with **Chapters 4-10** to learn the ADDRESS IT steps sequentially.

### For Experienced Staff
Use **Chapter 3** to identify your CSW type, then jump to the relevant ADDRESS IT step chapters (4-10) as needed. Refer to **Chapters 11-12** for context-specific guidance (Parliament or MOA). Use the appendix templates to structure your outputs.

### For Supervisors and Reviewers
Focus on **Chapter 13** (Writing Standards and Quality Assurance) and **Chapter 14** (Building a CSW Culture). Use **Appendix F** (Quality Review Checklist) and **Appendix J** (Competency Assessment Tool) to evaluate staff work and develop capacity.

### For Training Facilitators
Each ADDRESS IT step chapter (4-10) includes a "Why This Step Matters — Consequences of Skipping" section that can be used as discussion material. The appendices provide ready-to-use templates for workshops and exercises. **Appendix K** offers annotated sample documents for classroom analysis.

### Quick Reference
**Appendix A** (ADDRESS IT Quick Reference Card) is designed to be detached and kept at your desk as a daily reference. **Appendix F** (Quality Review Checklist) serves the same purpose for reviewers.

## Conventions Used in This Guidebook

- **Bold text** highlights key terms, framework names, and important concepts
- *Italic text* indicates document titles, template names, and technical terms on first use
- Boxed callouts marked **TIP** provide practical advice from experienced practitioners
- Boxed callouts marked **CAUTION** warn about common mistakes and their consequences
- Boxed callouts marked **BANGSAMORO NOTE** highlight adaptations specific to BARMM governance
- Section references use the format "Chapter X, Section X.X" for cross-referencing
- All templates in the appendices include annotations explaining each field's purpose and how it maps to the ADDRESS IT framework

## Relationship to Other BARMM Guidebooks

This CSW Guidebook complements the **Bill Drafting Guidebook for the Bangsamoro Parliament**, which provides detailed guidance on legislative drafting. Where the Bill Drafting Guidebook focuses on the technical craft of writing legislation, this guidebook covers the analytical and decision-support work that precedes, accompanies, and follows legislative action.

Staff preparing legislative briefers should use both guidebooks together: this guidebook for the CSW process and analytical framework, and the Bill Drafting Guidebook for drafting conventions and legal formatting standards.
