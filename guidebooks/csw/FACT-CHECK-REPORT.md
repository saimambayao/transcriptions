## Category 1: BOL (RA 11054) Article/Section References

### ERROR-01 — Article IX cited as "Shari'ah justice system" [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 2 (03-chapter-02.md), Section 2.3; Chapter 1 (02-chapter-01.md), Section 1.2.2; Chapter 4 (05-chapter-04.md), Section 4.5.1
**Claim**: "Article IX, Section 1. The Bangsamoro Government shall have competence over the Shari'ah justice system."
**Actual**: Article IX is titled **"Basic Rights"** and covers transitional justice, reparation for unjust dispossession, indigenous peoples' rights, customary rights, religious freedom, and human rights. The Shari'ah justice system is in **Article X — The Justice System in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region**.
**Correction**: All references to "Article IX" for Shari'ah matters should be changed to **Article X**.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — wrong legal citation

### ERROR-02 — Article V, Section 2 revenue quote is misattributed [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 2 (03-chapter-02.md), Section 2.3
**Claim**: "Article V, Section 2. The Bangsamoro Government shall have the authority to create its own sources of revenue, levy taxes, fees, and charges..."
**Actual**: The provision about revenue/taxation is found in **Article XII (Fiscal Autonomy), Section 6**: "The Bangsamoro Government shall have the power to create its own sources of revenues and to levy taxes, fees, and charges, subject to the provisions of this Organic Law..." The quoted text also adds "and to issue bonds, subject to such guidelines and limitations as the Congress may impose" which does not appear verbatim in Section 6.
**Correction**: Change to **Article XII, Section 6** and use the verbatim text from the BOL.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — wrong article and section number, paraphrased as verbatim quote

### ERROR-03 — Article VII, Section 1 cited for creating/altering LGUs [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 2 (03-chapter-02.md), Section 2.3
**Claim**: "Article VII, Section 1. The Bangsamoro Government shall have the power to create, divide, merge, abolish, or substantially alter boundaries of municipalities and barangays."
**Actual**: Article VII, Section 1 is about the **Seat of Government** ("The Parliament shall fix by law the permanent seat of the Bangsamoro Government..."). The power to create/alter LGU boundaries is in **Article VI (Intergovernmental Relations), Section 10**.
**Correction**: Change to **Article VI, Section 10**.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — wrong article and section number

### ERROR-04 — Article VIII cited as "Indigenous peoples' rights" [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 2 (03-chapter-02.md), Section 2.3; Chapter 4 (05-chapter-04.md), Section 4.5.1
**Claim**: "indigenous peoples' rights (Article VIII)"
**Actual**: Article VIII is titled **"Wali"** (the ceremonial head of the Bangsamoro). Indigenous peoples' rights are covered in **Article IX (Basic Rights), Section 3**.
**Correction**: Change "Article VIII" to **Article IX, Section 3**.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — wrong article

### ERROR-05 — Article XII cited as "Intergovernmental relations" [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 2 (03-chapter-02.md), Section 2.3; Chapter 4 (05-chapter-04.md), Section 4.5.1
**Claim**: "Intergovernmental relations (Article XII)"
**Actual**: Article XII is titled **"Fiscal Autonomy."** Intergovernmental relations are covered in **Article VI**.
**Correction**: Change to **Article VI**.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — wrong article

### ERROR-06 — Article XIII cited as "Natural resources" [MINOR]

**Location**: Chapter 4 (05-chapter-04.md), Section 4.5.1
**Claim**: "Natural resources | Article XIII"
**Actual**: Article XIII is titled **"Regional Economy and Patrimony"** — it does cover natural resources extensively but the formal title differs. This is acceptable shorthand but should ideally use the correct title.
**Correction**: Consider using "Article XIII (Regional Economy and Patrimony)" for precision.
**Severity**: MINOR — substantively correct but imprecise title

### ERROR-07 — Article VI, Section 13 cited for education authority [CRITICAL]

**Location**: Chapter 5 (06-chapter-05.md), Section 5.1.1; multiple ECCD examples throughout
**Claim**: "BOL Article VI, Section 13" as the basis for BARMM education authority
**Actual**: Article VI, Section 13 is about **"National Programs and Projects"** (Pantawid, School Building Program, etc. continuing to be funded by the National Government). It does not establish BARMM education authority. Education competencies of the Bangsamoro Government are established through the general powers provisions (Article V/VII) and implemented through BAA 18 (Education Code). The Section 13 text reads: "National programs and projects such as the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program... shall continue to be funded by the National Government..."
**Correction**: Remove or change the BOL citation. Education authority derives from the general powers of the Bangsamoro Government under Article V and Article VII, and is operationalized through BAA 18.
**Severity**: CRITICAL — cites the wrong provision for a claim repeated throughout the guidebook

**Note**: The exact scope of Article IV vs. V needs precise checking; the general reference is reasonable.

### VERIFIED-02 — Article III cited for "Territory and jurisdiction"
