# How to Use This Guidebook

Your decision-maker just assigned you a task. You have a week. The output must be something they can approve or disapprove without rewriting your work. Where do you start?

This guidebook answers that question. It gives you a structured, repeatable process for producing **Complete Staff Work** (CSW) in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Whether you draft legislative briefers in Parliament, prepare budget requests in a ministry, or write project proposals in an LGU, the process is the same.

## The Problem This Guidebook Solves

Most government staff know *what* to produce. Few know *how* to get there systematically. Without a process, you waste time guessing what your supervisor wants. You miss stakeholders. You skip research. You present one option when three existed. You submit work that comes back covered in red ink.

CSW is the discipline that eliminates this waste. When done right, your superior should only need to do one thing: **approve or disapprove**.

## What You Will Find Inside

This guidebook has four parts:

**Part I: Foundations (Chapters 1-3)** explains the philosophy of CSW, its place in Bangsamoro governance, and the nine types of CSW documents you will encounter.

**Part II: The ADDRESS IT Process (Chapters 4-10)** walks you through the seven steps of the ADDRESS IT framework, from analyzing the context to iterating on your solution. Each chapter covers one step with practical tools, templates, and BARMM examples.

**Part III: Applications (Chapters 11-12)** applies the ADDRESS IT framework to two specific contexts: Parliamentary committee work and Ministry/Office/Agency (MOA) decision-making.

**Part IV: Quality and Culture (Chapters 13-14)** covers writing standards, quality assurance, and how to build CSW as an institutional practice rather than a one-time training exercise.

The **Glossary** defines key terms. The **Appendices** provide detachable reference cards, templates, checklists, and sample documents.

## The ADDRESS IT Framework at a Glance

ADDRESS IT is a seven-step methodology for Complete Staff Work. Each step builds on the one before it. The process is cyclical: Step 7 loops back to Step 1 when conditions change.

| Step | Mnemonic | Action | Key Question |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **A** | Analyze the Context | What is the assignment, who are the stakeholders, and what laws apply? |
| 2 | **DD** | Define and Deliberate the Problem or Need | What exactly is the problem, and what causes it? |
| 3 | **R** | Research Needed Information | What evidence do you need, and where do you find it? |
| 4 | **E** | Explore Options | What are the viable solutions, and which is best? |
| 5 | **S** | Submit Proposal or Report | How do you package your work for decision? |
| 6 | **S** | Sustain Efforts to Implement | How do you keep momentum after approval? |
| 7 | **IT** | Iterate and Improve | How do you learn from results and improve? |

The mnemonic spells **ADDRESS IT** because that is exactly what CSW does: it addresses the problem so the decision-maker does not have to.

## How to Read This Guidebook

### If You Are New to CSW

Read sequentially. Start with Chapter 1 to understand why CSW matters. Move through Chapters 2-3 to see where CSW fits in BARMM and which type applies to your work. Then follow Chapters 4-10 step by step.

### If You Are Experienced but Want Structure

Go directly to Chapter 3 (Types of CSW) and identify which type matches your current assignment. Then read the relevant ADDRESS IT step chapters (4-10) for that type. Use the appendix templates to structure your output.

### If You Supervise Staff Who Produce CSW

Start with Chapter 13 (Writing Standards and Quality Assurance) and Chapter 14 (Building a CSW Culture). Use Appendix F (Quality Review Checklist) to evaluate staff work. Use Appendix J (Competency Assessment Tool) to identify training needs.

### If You Design or Facilitate CSW Training

Each ADDRESS IT step chapter (4-10) ends with a "Consequences of Skipping" section you can use as case-based discussion material. Appendix K provides three annotated sample CSW documents for classroom analysis.

## Conventions Used in This Guidebook

Throughout this guidebook, you will encounter the following formatting conventions:

- **Bold text** highlights key terms, framework names, and definitions
- *Italic text* marks document titles and template names on first use
- **TIP** callouts provide practical advice from experienced practitioners
- **CAUTION** callouts warn about common mistakes and their real consequences
- **BANGSAMORO NOTE** callouts highlight adaptations specific to BARMM governance
- Blockquoted text presents verbatim legal provisions from the BOL, BAAs, or national laws
- Section references use "Chapter X, Section X.X" for cross-referencing
