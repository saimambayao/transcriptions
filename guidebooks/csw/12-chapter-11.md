# Chapter 11 — CSW for Parliamentary Committees

If you work in the Bangsamoro Parliament, your CSW directly shapes legislation. A committee deliberation is only as good as the briefer that informs it. An oversight inquiry is only as incisive as the research behind it. A budget review is only as thorough as the analysis your staff produces.

This chapter applies the ADDRESS IT framework to the four main CSW outputs of parliamentary committee work: legislative briefers, committee reports, oversight CSW, and budget deliberation briefers.

## 11.1 How the 13-Section Legislative Briefer Maps to ADDRESS IT

The 13-section legislative briefer is the standard CSW output for bill analysis. Each section maps directly to an ADDRESS IT step.

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step | What the Step Requires |
|---------|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| **I. Overview** | Bill title, author, committee referral, status | **Step 1** (Analyze) | Identify the assignment: what bill, who authored it, where it stands |
| **II. Brief Description** | Summary of key provisions | **Step 1** (Analyze) | Understand what the bill does |
| **III. Previous Proceedings** | Legislative history, related measures | **Step 2** (Define) | Define what problem the bill addresses; what has been tried before |
| **IV. Implementing Agency** | Agency responsible for implementation | **Step 1** (Analyze) | Identify stakeholders responsible for execution |
| **V. Appropriations Needed** | Estimated budget impact | **Step 3** (Research) | Research the fiscal cost of the bill |
| **VI. Objects of Expenditure** | Specific budget line items | **Step 3** (Research) | Research where the money will be spent |
| **VII. Source of Funds** | Where funding comes from | **Step 3** (Research) | Research available funding mechanisms |
| **VIII. Sectoral Analysis** | Impact on specific sectors | **Step 3** (Research) | Research who benefits and who is affected |
| **IX. BOL Alignment** | Constitutional and BOL consistency | **Steps 1+3** | Review legal framework; verify constitutional basis |
| **X. Parliamentary Strategy** | Recommended approach for committee action | **Step 4** (Explore) | Explore options for how the committee should proceed |
| **XI. Proposed Amendments** | Specific textual amendments | **Step 4** (Explore) | Explore options for improving the bill's text |
| **XII. Recommended Vote** | Approval, deferral, or disapproval | **Step 5** (Submit) | Submit your recommendation to the committee |
| **XIII. Strategic Intelligence** | Broader political and policy context | **Steps 4+5** | Provide context for decision-making |

### How to Complete Each Section

**Sections I-III (ADDRESS IT Steps 1-2):** Establish the context and define the problem. Do not analyze yet. Describe the bill, its history, and the problem it aims to solve.

**Sections IV-IX (ADDRESS IT Step 3):** Research. This is the analytical core. Investigate the budget impact, sectoral effects, legal consistency, and implementation feasibility. Cite every data point.

**Sections X-XII (ADDRESS IT Steps 4-5):** Evaluate and recommend. What are the committee's options? What amendments improve the bill? Should the committee approve, defer, or disapprove?

**Section XIII (Context for decision):** Provide the intelligence the committee needs to act wisely. This includes stakeholder positions, political dynamics, timing considerations, media attention, and potential controversies.

> **TIP:** Spend 50% of your time on Sections IV-IX (research). Spend 30% on Sections X-XIII (analysis and recommendation). Spend 20% on Sections I-III (context). The research sections carry the analytical weight.

## 11.2 Committee-Specific CSW

Each standing committee has its own policy domain. Your CSW must reflect the committee's jurisdiction and expertise.

### Committee Context Factors

| Factor | What to Consider |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Committee mandate** | What is the committee's subject matter jurisdiction under the Rules of the BTA Parliament? |
| **Committee composition** | Who are the members? What are their policy interests and district constituencies? |
| **Pending legislation** | What bills are referred to the committee? What is the hearing calendar? |
| **Previous committee actions** | What positions has the committee taken on similar issues? |
| **Inter-committee dynamics** | Does the bill require referral to another committee (e.g., finance, legal)? |

### Adapting CSW for Different Committee Types

**Legislative committees** (Education, Health, etc.) — Focus on policy substance, sectoral impact, implementation feasibility, and alignment with BDP goals.

**Committee on Finance and Budget** — Focus on fiscal impact, budget adequacy, fund sourcing, and COA compliance. Use the Budget Briefer (BB) format.

**Committee on Rules** — Focus on procedural matters: order of business, priority legislation, session calendar.

**Committee on Legal Affairs** — Focus on constitutional consistency, BOL alignment, legal drafting quality, and potential judicial challenges.

**Oversight committees** — Focus on implementation status, performance against targets, agency compliance, and corrective actions needed.

## 11.3 Committee Reports and Oversight CSW

When a committee completes its deliberation on a bill or conducts an oversight investigation, it produces a **Committee Report** for plenary consideration.

### Committee Report Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **I. Introduction** | The referral, committee jurisdiction, and scope of the report |
| **II. Proceedings** | Summary of committee hearings, resource persons who testified, key issues raised |
| **III. Analysis** | The committee's assessment of the bill or issue (draws from the legislative briefer) |
| **IV. Amendments Adopted** | Specific textual amendments agreed upon during committee deliberation |
| **V. Recommendation** | The committee's recommendation to plenary: approve, approve with amendments, defer, disapprove |
| **VI. Signatures** | Committee Chairperson and members who concur |

**The committee report is the committee's CSW to plenary.** It must be decision-ready. Members of Parliament who are not on the committee will read this report to decide their vote. Give them everything they need.

### Oversight CSW

Oversight is the Parliament's power to monitor how laws and programs are implemented. Oversight CSW differs from legislative CSW:

| Legislative CSW | Oversight CSW |
|----------------|--------------|
| Analyzes proposed legislation | Analyzes implementation of existing legislation |
| Forward-looking: what should we enact? | Backward-looking: did the law achieve its purpose? |
| Primary output: legislative briefer | Primary output: oversight report |
| Decision: approve/amend/disapprove the bill | Decision: commend/require corrective action/legislate amendments |

**Oversight CSW follows ADDRESS IT:**

1. **Analyze** — What law or program are you overseeing? What is the implementation status?
2. **Define** — What implementation problems have emerged?
3. **Research** — What do the data show? What are the COA findings? What do implementing agencies report?
4. **Explore** — What corrective options exist? Legislative amendment? Budget adjustment? Administrative reform?
5. **Submit** — Present oversight findings and recommendations to the committee
6. **Sustain** — Track whether corrective actions are implemented
7. **Iterate** — Schedule follow-up oversight to verify improvement

## 11.4 Interpellation Preparation as CSW

When an MP prepares to interpellate (question a minister or agency head during plenary or committee session), the staff must produce a specific type of CSW: **interpellation preparation material**.

### Interpellation Prep Structure

| Component | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| **Background brief** | Issue summary, relevant data, legal context |
| **Key questions** | 10-15 prepared questions, sequenced to build a line of inquiry |
| **Expected answers** | Anticipated responses from the agency head |
| **Follow-up questions** | Questions to ask based on expected answers |
| **Documentary evidence** | Exhibits, data tables, COA findings to cite during interpellation |
| **Desired outcome** | What the MP wants to achieve through the interpellation |

> **TIP:** Good interpellation questions are specific, evidence-based, and sequenced. Start with factual questions the respondent must answer ("What was your agency's utilization rate in FY 2025?"). Build toward analytical questions ("Why is 47% acceptable when the national average is 78%?"). End with action-oriented questions ("What corrective measures will you implement, and by when?").

## 11.5 Budget Deliberation Briefers

During BARMM's annual appropriation process, each agency's budget is reviewed by the Committee on Finance and Budget. Staff produce **Budget Briefers** (BB01-BB13) for each agency under the committee's review calendar.

### Budget Deliberation Timeline

| Phase | Activity | CSW Required |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Pre-deliberation** | Agency submits BEP to Parliament | Staff prepares BB01-BB13 for assigned agencies |
| **Committee hearing** | Agency presents budget; committee questions | Staff provides real-time research support |
| **Post-hearing analysis** | Committee reviews testimony and data | Staff updates briefer with hearing findings |
| **Committee markup** | Committee adjusts budget figures | Staff prepares options for budget adjustments |
| **Report to plenary** | Committee reports recommended budget to plenary | Staff prepares committee report on budget |

### Budget Deliberation CSW Best Practices

1. **Start with BB06 (Budget Comparison).** The multi-year comparison reveals trends, variances, and anomalies that drive the rest of the analysis.
2. **Flag utilization rates.** If an agency consistently underspends, question why before recommending the same or higher allocation.
3. **Connect budget to BDP.** Show which BDP goals each PPAS advances. Flag budget items that do not connect to any BDP priority.
4. **Prepare questions for the MP.** Based on your analysis, draft specific questions the committee member should ask during the hearing. Include the expected answer and the follow-up.
5. **Update after the hearing.** The agency's testimony may change your analysis. Update the briefer with new information before the markup session.

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** BARMM budget deliberation occurs under BAA 84 (Budget System Act). Familiarize yourself with the budget cycle, timeline requirements, and classification system (Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 PPAS) before preparing budget briefers. Cross-reference with the latest Budget Call memorandum from MFBM.

**Chapter Summary**

- The 13-section legislative briefer maps directly to ADDRESS IT steps 1-5
- Committee CSW must reflect the specific committee's jurisdiction, composition, and policy domain
- Committee reports are the committee's CSW to plenary — they must be decision-ready
- Oversight CSW follows ADDRESS IT but focuses on implementation assessment rather than legislative analysis
- Interpellation preparation is a specialized CSW product requiring sequenced questions and evidence
- Budget deliberation briefers (BB01-BB13) follow a structured timeline from pre-deliberation through plenary report
- All parliamentary CSW exists to serve one purpose: enabling informed collective decision-making by Members of Parliament
