# Chapter 12 — CSW for MOA Decision-Making

If you work in a Ministry, Office, or Agency (MOA), your CSW supports executive decision-making. Ministers, directors, and division chiefs need staff work that is accurate, actionable, and audit-ready. The stakes are different from Parliament — you are dealing with budgets, procurement, personnel, and program delivery where every decision has a COA trail.

This chapter covers the five most common MOA CSW contexts: budget requests, project proposals, personnel actions, procurement justifications, and the approval hierarchies that govern them.

## 12.1 Budget Requests and Financial Proposals

Every peso your agency spends starts with a budget request. CSW for budget requests must justify the allocation with data, connect it to mandates, and survive both MFBM review and parliamentary scrutiny.

### Budget Request CSW Structure

| Section | Content | ADDRESS IT Step |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Agency mandate and function** | Legal basis for the agency's existence and operations | Step 1 |
| **Program/project description** | What the budget will fund (PPAs) | Steps 1-2 |
| **Performance data** | Accomplishments against prior-year targets | Step 3 |
| **Budget breakdown** | PS, MOOE, CO by line item | Step 3 |
| **Multi-year comparison** | Current vs. previous years with variance analysis | Step 3 |
| **BDP alignment** | Which development goals the budget advances | Step 4 |
| **Justification** | Why this amount, why now, what happens without it | Steps 4-5 |
| **Utilization plan** | How funds will be obligated and disbursed by quarter | Step 6 |

### Common Budget CSW Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Requesting more than prior year without justification | MFBM flags for reduction | Explain every variance exceeding 10% |
| Ignoring prior-year utilization rates | Committee reduces allocation based on underspending | Address utilization proactively with improvement plan |
| Vague PPAS descriptions | Budget cannot be attributed to specific outcomes | Write measurable PPAS with clear KPIs |
| Missing legal basis | Budget items appear unauthorized | Cite the BOL provision, BAA, or administrative order |
| No BDP alignment | Budget appears disconnected from regional priorities | Map every major PPAS to a BDP goal |

> **CAUTION:** COA audits start with the budget. Every peso must be traceable from appropriation to obligation to disbursement to output. Your budget CSW creates the paper trail. If the CSW is sloppy, the audit trail is compromised.

## 12.2 Project Proposals and Program Design

MOA staff produce project proposals (PP template, Chapter 3) for new initiatives. These proposals undergo multiple approval layers before implementation.

### Project Proposal Approval Chain

| Level | Reviewer | What They Check |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Division Chief | Technical accuracy, feasibility |
| 2 | Director/Undersecretary | Alignment with agency priorities |
| 3 | Minister | Strategic fit, resource availability |
| 4 | MFBM (for budget implications) | Fiscal impact, fund availability |
| 5 | Parliament (for legislative appropriation) | BDP alignment, public value |

Your CSW must survive every level. Each reviewer looks at different things. The Division Chief cares about whether the numbers are right. The Minister cares about whether this advances their priorities. Parliament cares about whether this serves the Bangsamoro people.

### Program Design Elements

Good project proposals include:

| Element | Standard |
|---------|---------|
| **Theory of change** | Clear logic: If we do [intervention], then [output] will lead to [outcome] because [evidence] |
| **Beneficiary identification** | Specific target population with selection criteria |
| **Geographic scope** | Where the project will operate (municipalities, barangays) |
| **Implementation structure** | Who does what, reporting lines, coordination mechanisms |
| **Sustainability plan** | What happens when project funding ends |
| **Risk register** | Identified risks with probability, impact, and mitigation |
| **M&E plan** | How outcomes will be measured and reported |

> **TIP:** Decision-makers in BARMM approve dozens of project proposals each year. Make yours stand out by leading with the problem and the evidence, not the solution. A proposal that demonstrates a deep understanding of the problem is more convincing than one that jumps straight to activities and budgets.

## 12.3 Personnel Actions and Administrative Matters

Personnel CSW is highly regulated. The Civil Service Commission (CSC), BARMM HR guidelines, and BAA 17 (Bangsamoro Civil Service Code) set strict requirements for hiring, promotion, reassignment, and disciplinary actions.

### Personnel Action CSW Requirements

| Action | CSW Must Include | Key Regulations |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **New hiring** | Position vacancy, qualification standards, comparative assessment, selection board recommendation | CSC MC 14/2018, BAA 17 |
| **Promotion** | Performance evaluation, qualification match, selection board recommendation | CSC promotion rules, BARMM HR guidelines |
| **Reassignment/detail** | Justification, duration, receiving office consent, no diminution of rank or pay | CSC rules on reassignment |
| **Reorganization** | Rationalization study, staffing pattern, transition plan, affected personnel list | DBM/MFBM guidelines |
| **Disciplinary action** | Statement of charges, evidence, due process documentation, recommended penalty | CSC Revised Rules on Administrative Cases |

### Critical Personnel CSW Rules

1. **Document everything.** Every step of the personnel action must be on paper. Missing documentation is the number one cause of COA disallowance for personnel expenses.
2. **Follow the selection board process.** Hiring and promotion decisions must go through the Personnel Selection Board. Shortcuts invite appeals and legal challenges.
3. **Verify qualification standards.** Do not recommend someone who does not meet CSC minimum qualification standards. This includes education, experience, training, and eligibility.
4. **Calculate financial impact.** Every personnel action has a cost: salary differential, benefits, allowances. Include these calculations in your CSW.

> **CAUTION:** Personnel CSW errors have personal liability implications. If a hiring is disallowed by COA, the approving officer and the certifying officer may be required to refund the salary paid. Get the CSW right.

## 12.4 Procurement Justifications

BARMM procurement follows RA 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act) as adapted for autonomous region operations. Procurement CSW justifies why the agency needs to buy something, what specifications are required, and how the procurement will be conducted.

### Procurement CSW Elements

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Purchase request** | What is being procured, quantity, specifications |
| **Market survey** | Price comparison from at least three suppliers |
| **Budget certification** | Confirmation that funds are available |
| **Technical specifications** | Detailed specs that are non-restrictive (do not favor a single supplier) |
| **Procurement method** | Why the chosen method (competitive bidding, shopping, direct contracting) is appropriate |
| **Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC)** | Maximum price the agency will pay |
| **End-user certification** | Confirmation from the requesting unit |

### Procurement CSW Common Errors

| Error | COA/BAC Response |
|-------|-----------------|
| Technical specs written to favor one supplier | BAC rejects; potential administrative charges |
| No market survey or outdated prices | ABC may be inflated; audit finding |
| Wrong procurement method for the amount | Procurement is void; items must be returned or regularized |
| Missing budget certification | Procurement cannot proceed; delays implementation |
| Splitting purchases to avoid competitive bidding | Violation of RA 9184; subject to sanctions |

> **BANGSAMORO NOTE:** BARMM procurement can involve additional coordination with the BARMM Bids and Awards Committee (BAC) structure. Ensure your CSW identifies the correct BAC (agency-level or centralized) and follows the approved procurement plan. Late procurement requests that fall outside the Annual Procurement Plan (APP) require justification and amendment.

## 12.5 Approval Hierarchies and Transmittal Procedures

MOA CSW flows through a defined hierarchy. Understanding this chain is critical for correct transmittal.

### Typical MOA Approval Chain

| Level | Position | Authority | Transmittal Format |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Preparing officer** | Analyst/Staff | Drafts the CSW | Internal draft with initials |
| **Reviewing officer** | Section/Unit Head | Checks technical accuracy | Review notation on draft |
| **Recommending officer** | Division Chief | Recommends approval | Signed endorsement memo |
| **Approving authority** | Director/Minister | Makes the decision | Signed approval on transmittal memo |

### Transmittal Rules

1. **Use the transmittal memorandum.** Every CSW reaching the approving authority must have a transmittal memo (Chapter 8, Section 8.11).
2. **Include all endorsements.** The reviewing and recommending officers' signatures must appear on the transmittal chain.
3. **Route through the correct channels.** Do not skip levels. A CSW that goes directly from the analyst to the minister bypasses quality gates and may be returned.
4. **Track transmittal dates.** Log when the CSW was submitted, to whom, and the expected decision date. Follow up if no action is taken within the standard period.

### When to Use Different Transmittal Channels

| Urgency | Channel | Appropriate For |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Routine** | Standard memo with physical routing | Regular CSW, reports, recommendations |
| **Urgent** | Expedited routing with "URGENT" marking | Time-sensitive decisions, deadlines |
| **Confidential** | Sealed envelope, hand-delivered | Personnel actions, legal opinions, sensitive matters |
| **For information** | Email or electronic copy | Progress reports, data updates, status reports |

## 12.6 Working with COA Requirements

The Commission on Audit (COA) reviews all government expenditures. Your CSW must anticipate COA requirements and build compliance into the documentation.

### COA Documentation Standards

| COA Requirement | How Your CSW Addresses It |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Legal basis for expenditure** | Cite the BOL provision, BAA, or administrative order authorizing the action |
| **Approved budget** | Reference the allotment and its SARO/NCA number |
| **Proper authorization** | Include all required signatures in the approval chain |
| **Supporting documents** | Attach all evidence: quotations, contracts, performance data, accomplishment reports |
| **Value for money** | Demonstrate that the expenditure achieves results proportionate to cost |
| **Compliance with procurement law** | Document the procurement method, bidding process, and BAC resolution |

### Proactive Audit Readiness

Do not wait for COA to ask for documents. Organize them from the start.

**Audit-ready file structure:**

1. Transmittal memorandum with approval
2. CSW document (complete, with all sections filled)
3. Legal basis documentation
4. Budget certification and allotment documents
5. Procurement documents (if applicable)
6. Implementation records (progress reports, accomplishment reports)
7. Financial documents (obligation requests, disbursement vouchers, liquidation reports)

> **TIP:** Create this file structure at the start of implementation, not when the auditor arrives. If your CSW file is organized from Day 1, audit preparation takes hours instead of weeks.

**Chapter Summary**

- Budget request CSW must justify allocations with data, address utilization rates, and connect to BDP goals
- Project proposals must survive multiple approval layers by addressing each reviewer's priorities
- Personnel CSW is heavily regulated by CSC rules and BAA 17; documentation errors create personal liability
- Procurement CSW must follow RA 9184, include market surveys, and avoid restrictive specifications
- All MOA CSW flows through defined approval hierarchies with proper transmittal procedures
- COA compliance starts with the CSW — build audit-ready documentation from Day 1
