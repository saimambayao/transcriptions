### MINOR

**m1. Voice compliance — Chapter 5 opens with a statement, not a WHY-oriented hook**

Chapter 5 opens: "Every memo you write, every briefer you draft, every program you implement serves one plan. Know it." While engaging, the second paragraph begins with "If you have ever wondered why the Bangsamoro government exists..." -- the WHY is deferred. Most other chapters lead with WHY in the very first sentence.

---

**m2. Voice compliance — Chapter 10 opens with a WHAT statement instead of WHY**

Chapter 10 opens: "Every assignment you receive in government follows a process. That process has a name: Complete Staff Work (CSW)." This opens with WHAT (defining CSW) rather than WHY (why CSW matters to the reader). Compare with Chapter 1's strong WHY opening.

---

**m3. Chapter 4 — Temporary appointment duration stated as "12 months" in text but "up to 2 years" in Chapter 9**

In Chapter 4 (Section 4.3, Temporary): "The appointment expires after the specified period (typically 12 months)." In Chapter 9 (Section 9.8): "Given up to 2 years to pass the CSC exam." These are not necessarily contradictory (12 months renewable vs. 2-year maximum) but the inconsistency may confuse readers. A clarifying note in Chapter 4 would resolve this.
