## Notes

### On the BOL Article Numbering Errors (Errors #1-4, #8-9)

The most systematic error in the guidebook is the misidentification of BOL article numbers in Chapter 2 (the research methodology section). The correct BOL article-to-title mapping is:

| Article | Correct Title |
|---------|--------------|
| I | Name and Purpose |
| II | Bangsamoro Identity |
| III | Territorial Jurisdiction |
| IV | General Principles and Policies |
| **V** | **Powers of Government** |
| **VI** | **Intergovernmental Relations** |
| **VII** | **Bangsamoro Government** (includes Parliament) |
| **VIII** | **Wali** |
| **IX** | **Basic Rights** |
| **X** | **Bangsamoro Justice System** (includes Shari'ah) |
| XI | National Defense, Security, Public Order |
| **XII** | **Fiscal Autonomy** |
| **XIII** | **Regional Economy and Patrimony** |
| XIV | [not in local source] |
| XV | [not in local source] |
| XVI | Bangsamoro Transition Authority |

Chapter 2, Section 2.3.3 ("Step 2: Republic Act No. 11054") contains 4 out of 5 incorrect article labels. This appears to be a systematic misremembering of the BOL structure rather than isolated typos. The rest of the guidebook (particularly Chapter 1, which quotes the BOL extensively and correctly) uses the correct article numbers.

### On the Priority Codes Discrepancy

Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 contain conflicting information about the status of the Local Government Code and the number/source of priority codes. Chapter 4's treatment is more accurate. Chapter 1 appears to reflect an earlier draft state that was not updated when the LGC was enacted.

### On the Enacting Clause Variations

The guidebook uses three different formulations of the enacting clause across different chapters. This was already flagged in the QA-REPORT.md file. It is an internal consistency issue rather than a factual error -- Resolution No. 268 provides "Be it enacted by the Parliament in session assembled" as an example, and actual BAAs have used slightly different wordings.
