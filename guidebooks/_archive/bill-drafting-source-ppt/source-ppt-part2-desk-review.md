# Part 2: Desk Review on BTA Parliament's Legislative Development

---

## A PRESENTATION OF THE DESK REVIEW ON BTA PARLIAMENT'S LEGISLATIVE DEVELOPMENT

---

## BASIC FUNCTIONS OF THE PARLIAMENT

- **Lawmaking**
- **Representation**
- **Oversight**

---

## LAWMAKING FUNCTION

- It makes and remakes laws, amend, or repeal them.
- Has "authority to enact laws on matters that are within the powers and competencies of the Bangsamoro Government." (BOL)
- "[e]nact laws to promote, protect, and ensure the general welfare." (BOL)

---

## REPRESENTATION FUNCTION

- The political system of Bangsamoro "allows its people to freely participate in the political processes," (BOL)
- The government shall "ensure people's participation." (BOL)

(Parliament is the institution in which the citizens participate in the governance. The representative principle is based on the joint notions of responsiveness to public or partisan demands and accountability to elected officials)

---

## REPRESENTATION CONSISTS OF:

- To establish regular, direct **linkages** with their constituents.
  - **listen** to their grievances and demands,
  - **report** to them.
- To provide their constituents with a **voice** at the higher, more visible levels of government — **to make their thoughts and concerns known to the powers that be**.
- To **secure new resources** for their constituents, prevent existing resources from being taken away, and more generally, provide a wide array of services.

---

## OVERSIGHT FUNCTION

- "to see to it that laws, policies, and programs addressing specific concerns within their respective jurisdictions have been carried out" (BTA Rules)
- "Public office is a public trust. Public officers and employees shall at all times be accountable to the people" (BOL, Constitution)
- Checks and Balances does not apply only Separation of Powers

---

## INSTITUTIONALIZATION OF PARLIAMENT

The process by which a body acquires a definite way of performing its **functions** through which its members and permanent staff of a parliament learn, maintain, and adapt to the procedures, mechanisms, and rules of the institution.

## CLASSIFICATION OF RESOLUTION

| BASELINE | FINDINGS/RECOMMENDATIONS |
|----------|--------------------------|
| **Simple Resolution** — A simple resolution is a measure used by the BTA to take action affecting its own procedures, urgent resolution requiring immediate action by the BTA, or to express an opinion, sympathy or commendation. | - Concurrent Resolution is applicable only to bicameral legislative system. But the Parliament is a unicameral |
| **Concurrent Resolution** — A concurrent resolution is a resolution issued concurrently by both the Executive and the BTA expressing their sentiments on certain matters. | - Joint Committee Resolution: the Parliament should act thru Plenary its committee cannot act by its committee to validly issue a resolution unless it is not issued behalf of the Parliament. |
| **Joint Committee Resolution** — A joint committee resolution is a resolution of two or more committees expressing the will or action of the Members on certain matters. This requires the majority vote of all the committees concerned. | |
