## MP INTERVIEWS

---

## Sustainable Bill-Drafting Capacity

- bill-drafting is not something that can be taught in a classroom.
- practical training and mentoring
- It takes time, according to him for a bill drafter to possess that expertise.
- Capacity building is short-term and not sustainable.
- Focus to train experts in bill-drafting
- 18 months to two years before the actual bill drafting work.

---

## Advantage of Bill Drafting Manual

- provide uniformity of style and format,
- assist in the training of bill-drafter,
- promote greater stylistic uniformity, to ensure accuracy, clarity, and uniformity legislation

---

## Challenges in Bill-Drafting

- Lack of permanent technical staff that
- No technical knowledge on how to prepare bills and resolutions.
- Lack of knowledge in writing a bill or resolution.
- Reliance on staff and on experts
- Majority of lawyers would not even accept to be their Chief of Staff and Legal Consultants
- Gathering data is a problem for some MPs for an evidence-based legislation
- BARMM is still organizing research offices
- Not yet established relationship with research institutions
- proper understanding of the problem
- Precision and clarity of language

---

## Addressing the Challenges in Drafting Bills and Resolutions

- hiring of legal consultants and experts.
- conduct a bill drafting seminar to empower the ordinary stakeholders in translating their advocacies into a legislative draft. The MPs also receive technical support in this aspect from several INGOs through trainings.
- MPs have their legal officers to do the research, to talk to some institution and to ask some technical expertise.
- bill or a resolution is based on the reliable and accurate facts backed by research.
- avoid legal jargons.
- Personal Interview with Member of the Parliament D.

---

## Importance of Language and Style in the Application of Legislation

-
- language and style is very important
- should not be written in sophisticated language or double meaning.
- one wrong word will spell a difference.
- simplest as possible will make common people understand.
- time for the BARMM to create a bill drafting unit

---

## Consistency of Legislative Bills and Resolutions

- many of the bills and resolutions in the Parliament are still subject to interpretation.
- general uniformity as regard to the title,
- used too many compound sentences and the MPs are seeking clarification for just one specific word in the debate which causes delays in the deliberation.
- file in different format and in varying paper sizes, although the passed bills and resolution are consistent

## EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

- not a mandatory requirement
- sets out the purposes or reasons, salient features,, and policy considerations
- no prescribed particular form, structure, and content
- advantage to being **DETAILED** because it will give substantive information to the members of the Parliament making them more informed on the bill and helpful in the deliberation.

## USING "AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES"

- USE if a bill has more than two significant purposes
- DESCRIBE the most important purposes and finish with "and for other purposes".
- DO NOT USE if the bill clearly has a single purpose

## SHORT TITLE

- short title is the first section of the body of a bill
- to abbreviate the long title of the bill. quick reference
- concise citation of the bill
- If the bill is short or it is not expected that it will be frequently cited in legal documents, then it should be dispensed with.
- Not "Title"
- Not "Name".

## DECLARATION OF POLICY

- preliminary to the substantive portions of the law
- guiding principle, purpose and the necessity for the enactment
- useful in statutory construction as an aid in interpreting the meaning of its substantive provisions.
- The declaration of policy is not an operative provision and is not a source of right or duties.
- declaration of policy is dispense because it is considered as surplusage because the policy of a bill is evident from its operative provisions.

---

## PURPOSE OR OBJECTIVE

- states the general objectives of the act to provide guidance and understand the legislation.
- strengthen a law against a constitutional challenge
- courts relied thereon to construe unclear and ambiguous language
- helpful in a short bill if the operative provisions do not clearly indicate what the bill is intended to accomplish.

---

## Legislative Sentence

- A bill is a series of legislative sentences containing legal rules.
- The legal rule is the main component of a legal sentence and it is comprised of a **legal subject** and a **legal action**.
- The former is a person while the latter is a **predicate** which states the **legal effect** on the subject.
- The legal rule may be **modified** by **a case, condition, or exception**. These modifiers provide certain circumstances in the applicability of the legal rule.

---

## LEGAL RULE

- **DIRECTIVES**
  - what to do
  - how to do it
  - what not to do
- **DECLARATIVE**
- **CREATES ENTITY**
- **IMPOSE DUTIES**
- **CONFER** rights, powers, privileges, immunities, and jurisdiction
- **PROVIDES** for the prescriptions, prohibitions, or regulations or combinations thereof which can usually be thought of in terms of requiring, forbidding, or allowing some action.

---

## LEGAL ACTION

- Legal action directs the legal subject to act :
  - in a particular manner,
  - describing the particular act permitted, required, limited or prohibited.
  - verb directs, limits, or permits action or omission.
- Operative verb:
  - "shall" "may" or "must" "is"
  - negative form "shall not" "may not" or "must not"

## Modifiers

- The legal rules may not apply to certain circumstances or to subjects. When legislative sentence becomes more complex, legal rules may contain **cases, conditions, and exceptions** which modify the applicability of the legal rules.

- The modifiers limit the applicability of a legal rule by further qualifying its legal subject or legal action.
