# Introduction

Out of 12,214 cooperatives registered in the Bangsamoro region between 2010 and 2020, only 1,424 — **11.66 percent** — secured their certificates of compliance.[^1] That means nearly nine out of every ten cooperatives in BARMM cannot prove they meet minimum legal requirements. They cannot access government programs. They cannot partner with international donors. They cannot fully serve the communities that formed them.

This is not a minor administrative gap. The Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028 identifies the **low functionality and unsustainable profitability of cooperatives** as the priority challenge for the cooperative and social enterprise sub-sector.[^2] The BDP sets a target of raising compliance to **50 percent** by 2028 — more than quadrupling the current rate.[^3] Reaching that target requires more than goodwill. It requires that cooperative organizers, officers, and supporting agencies understand exactly what the law demands and how to meet those demands in the Bangsamoro context.

That context is unlike any other in the Philippines. Cooperatives here must navigate **RA 9520** (the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008), align with the **twelve pillars of moral governance**, respect the requirements of **Shari'ah compliance**, and prepare for a new legislative framework under **BTA Bill No. 210** (the proposed Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code).[^4] Many cooperatives — especially those organized by former combatants under the normalization process — face policies carried over from the defunct Cooperative Development Authority, making compliance even harder.[^5]

This guidebook exists because that situation is unacceptable and fixable.

## Why This Guidebook Exists

No BARMM-specific cooperative development guidebook exists today. The materials available to cooperative organizers in the Bangsamoro are generic national references designed for a context that does not account for Shari'ah-compliant transactions, moral governance standards, or the unique institutional landscape of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority. A cooperative officer in Cotabato City or Jolo faces questions that a manual written for Metro Manila cannot answer: How do you structure bylaws that comply with both RA 9520 and Islamic prohibitions on *riba* (usury)? What role does the Cooperative and Social Enterprise Authority (CSEA) play versus the national CDA? How should your cooperative prepare for the provisions of BTA Bill No. 210 once it becomes law?

This guidebook fills that gap. It provides a **single, practical, Bangsamoro-grounded reference** that walks you through every stage of cooperative development — from deciding whether a cooperative is the right vehicle for your community, through registration, governance, operations, compliance, and growth. Every chapter draws on the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054), the BDP 2023-2028, the pending Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code (BTA Bill No. 210), and the regulatory framework of CSEA.

Where national law applies, we tell you. Where Bangsamoro-specific rules add requirements or open new possibilities, we tell you that too. The goal is not to replace legal counsel but to give you the knowledge you need to ask the right questions, make informed decisions, and build cooperatives that actually work.

## Who This Guidebook Is For

This guidebook serves three audiences. Find yours and start where it makes the most sense for you.

**If you are a community organizer or prospective cooperative founder** — someone gathering fifteen or more people to form a cooperative — start with **Chapter 1** (why cooperatives matter in the Bangsamoro context) and read straight through **Chapter 4** (registration). These chapters give you the foundations and the step-by-step process to get your cooperative legally established. Return to the later chapters as your cooperative grows.

**If you are a sitting cooperative officer** — a board director, committee chair, manager, or treasurer of an existing cooperative — you likely need help with governance, financial management, or compliance. Start with **Chapter 5** (governance), **Chapter 7** (financial management), or **Chapter 9** (compliance). Use the table of contents and cross-references to navigate directly to your concern.

**If you are a government worker, LGU official, or development partner** supporting cooperatives — a CSEA field officer, a Municipal Cooperative Officer, a MAFAR agrarian reform specialist, or an NGO project manager — **Chapter 13** (the CSEA ecosystem) and **Chapter 14** (the future of cooperatives under BTA Bill No. 210) give you the policy and institutional framework. The appendices provide templates and checklists you can distribute to the cooperatives you assist.

## How This Guidebook Is Organized

The guidebook has **14 chapters** organized in **four parts**, designed to follow the lifecycle of a cooperative from concept to maturity.

**Part I: Foundations (Chapters 1-2)** answers the "why" and "what" questions. Chapter 1 explains why cooperatives matter for Bangsamoro communities — the economic case, the development mandate, and the alignment with moral governance. Chapter 2 helps you choose the right cooperative type for your community's needs, covering the categories recognized under RA 9520 and the additional classifications anticipated under BTA Bill No. 210.

**Part II: Building (Chapters 3-4)** covers everything from the first community meeting to the moment you receive your Certificate of Registration from CSEA. Chapter 3 walks you through the pre-formation process — the economic survey, the Pre-Registration Seminar, drafting your Articles of Cooperation and bylaws. Chapter 4 details the registration process, documentary requirements, and common reasons applications get returned.

**Part III: Running (Chapters 5-10)** is the operational core. Chapter 5 covers governance — your board, committees, and the general assembly. Chapter 6 addresses membership rights and responsibilities. Chapter 7 handles financial management, including Shari'ah-compliant alternatives to interest-based lending. Chapter 8 covers day-to-day operations and business planning. Chapter 9 explains compliance requirements — what you must file, when, and how to avoid losing your certificate. Chapter 10 addresses the education and training obligations that every cooperative must fulfill under the law.

**Part IV: Growing (Chapters 11-14)** looks beyond survival toward sustainability and scale. Chapter 11 covers growth strategies, federation, and inter-cooperative partnerships. Chapter 12 addresses dispute resolution — internal conflicts, mediation, and the adjudicatory role of CSEA. Chapter 13 maps the full CSEA ecosystem, including partnerships with MAFAR, MTIT, and international development agencies. Chapter 14 looks ahead to the Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code (BTA Bill No. 210) and how your cooperative can prepare for the transition.

## A Note on BTA Bill No. 210

**BTA Bill No. 210** — the proposed Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code — is pending before the Bangsamoro Parliament as of this writing.[^6] Once enacted, it will establish the first comprehensive legal framework governing cooperatives and social enterprises specifically in BARMM, replacing reliance on national legislation for many provisions.

This guidebook covers **both** the current legal framework under **RA 9520** and the anticipated framework under **BTA Bill No. 210**. Where the two diverge — on definitions, registration requirements, governance standards, or Shari'ah compliance obligations — we note the difference clearly. **Chapter 14** provides a full analysis of the transition and what your cooperative should do now to prepare. Until BTA Bill No. 210 is enacted, **RA 9520 remains the governing law**.

## Conventions Used in This Guidebook

The following conventions appear throughout the text to help you find what you need quickly:

- **Bold terms** mark key concepts, legal terminology, and important names on first use. When you see a bold term, it signals something worth understanding and remembering.

- **Footnotes** (marked with superscript numbers like this[^7]) cite the specific legal provision, BDP section, or data source behind every claim. Check the footnotes when you need to verify a statement or locate the original source.

- **Cross-references** point you to related chapters and sections (e.g., "see Chapter 7, Section 7.3"). Follow these when a topic connects to material covered elsewhere in the guidebook.

- **Blockquotes** set off verbatim legal text from the BOL, RA 9520, or BTA Bill No. 210:

  > Verbatim provisions appear in this format so you can distinguish exact legal language from our explanation of it.

- **Appendix templates** at the back of the guidebook provide ready-to-use documents — sample Articles of Cooperation, bylaws, economic survey forms, board resolutions, and compliance checklists. These are referenced by chapter and designed for you to adapt to your cooperative's specific needs.

- Where BTA Bill No. 210 changes or adds to the current framework under RA 9520, we mark the relevant passage with **[BTA Bill 210]** so you can distinguish current law from anticipated law.
