# Appendix A -- Articles of Cooperation Templates by Type

This appendix provides a complete, ready-to-use Articles of Cooperation (AOC) template for **multi-purpose cooperatives** -- the most common type formed in Bangsamoro communities. Following the template, you will find adaptation notes for credit, producer, consumer, agriculture, and worker cooperatives. Every fill-in field is marked with [___]. Replace each blank with the information specific to your cooperative.

**Before you begin**, make sure you have:
- Your approved **Name Reservation Slip** from CSEA
- The results of your **Economic Survey** (Chapter 3)
- The list of at least **15 cooperators** with complete names and postal addresses
- Your **capitalization plan** with subscribed and paid-up amounts computed
- Your elected **Board of Directors** (odd number, 5 to 15 members)
- Your elected **Treasurer**

**Document specifications per CDA MC 2015-01:**
- Paper size: 8.5" x 13" (legal) or 8.5" x 14" (long)
- Font: Bookman Old Style, size 12
- Prepare 4 original copies -- no photocopies accepted
- All cooperators must sign on the margin of every page
- Use non-metallic fasteners only (no staples, no paper clips)

## Article II -- Purposes

That the purposes for which this Cooperative is organized are to engage in:

1. [___specify primary business activity, e.g., "Lending and savings mobilization"___];

2. Facilitating the procurement of [___specify inputs/implements, e.g., "farm equipment, seeds, and fertilizers"___] for the members;

3. Processing and marketing of the members' [___specify products/produce, e.g., "agricultural products including copra, abaca, and cassava"___];

4. Storing and transporting of members' [___specify products/produce___];

5. Providing credit facility for [___specify what production/services, e.g., "livelihood, agricultural production, and micro-enterprise development"___];

6. Promoting and advancing the economic and social status of the members;

7. Coordinating and facilitating the activities of cooperatives;

8. Advocating for the cause of the Cooperative movements;

9. Ensuring the viability of Cooperatives through the utilization of new technologies;

10. Encouraging and promoting self-help or self-employment as an engine for economic growth and poverty alleviation; and

11. Any other activities that lead to the reduction of cost and/or addition in value of outputs.

> **Fill-up note:** Items 1-5 are your cooperative's specific business activities. Items 6-11 are standard purposes from the CDA template. For multi-purpose cooperatives, list at least two different types of business activities (e.g., credit + consumer store, or agriculture + marketing). Be specific -- vague purposes are a common reason for registration delays.

---

## Article III -- Goals

That the goals of this Cooperative are to help improve the quality of life of its members and thereby contribute to inclusive growth, enterprise development and employment. In furtherance thereto, it shall aim:

1. To attain increased income, savings, investments, productivity, and purchasing power, and promote among themselves equitable distribution of net surplus through maximum utilization of economies of scale, cost-sharing and risk-sharing;

2. To provide optimum social and economic benefits to its members;

3. To teach members efficient ways of doing things in a Cooperative manner;

4. To propagate Cooperative practices and innovative ideas in business undertakings and management;

5. To empower through provision of access, ownership, control and opportunities to the poor, vulnerable, lower income and less privileged groups to increase their ownership in the wealth of the nation;

6. To actively support the government, other Cooperatives and people oriented organizations, both local and foreign, in promoting Cooperatives as a practical means towards sustainable socio-economic development under a truly just and democratic society;

7. To develop a dynamic savings mobilization and capital build-up schemes to sustain its developmental activities and long-term investments, thereby ensuring optimum economic benefits to the members, their families and the communities;

8. To adopt membership expansion mechanism/scheme, thereby ensuring growth of the Cooperative movement;

9. To implement policy guidelines that will ensure transparency, accountability and equitable access to its resources and services, and promote the interests of the members;

10. To adopt such other plans as may help foster the welfare of the members, their families and the community;

11. To advance the competitiveness and innovativeness of the industry;

12. To coordinate with other Cooperatives on learning exchanges, coop trade, and information exchanges in fostering sustainable development;

13. To advocate legal framework and enabling policies appropriate for the development of Cooperatives; and

14. To be the voice and the institution of the poor and the excluded in resisting the growth-centered development aggression and instead promote people-centered development.

> **Fill-up note:** Article III goals are standard across all cooperative types. You may adopt them as written. The General Assembly may add goals specific to your community's needs, but do not delete any of the 14 standard goals.

## Article IV -- Powers and Capacities

That the powers, rights and capacities of this Cooperative are those prescribed under Article 9 of Republic Act 9520:

1. To the exclusive use of its registered name;

2. To sue and be sued;

3. Of succession;

4. To amend its Articles of Cooperation in accordance with the provisions of RA 9520;

5. To adopt by-laws not contrary to law, morals or public policy, and to amend and repeal the same in accordance with RA 9520;

6. To purchase, receive, take or grant, hold, convey, sell, lease, pledge, mortgage, and otherwise deal with such real and personal property as the transaction of the lawful affairs of the Cooperative may reasonably and necessarily require, subject to the limitations prescribed by law and the Constitution;

7. To enter into division, merger, or consolidation, as provided under RA 9520;

8. To form subsidiary Cooperatives and join federations or unions, as provided in this Code;

9. To avail of loans, be entitled to credit and to accept and receive grants, donations and assistance from foreign and domestic sources subject to the conditions of said loans, credits, grants, donations or assistance that will not undermine the autonomy of the cooperative. The Authority, upon written request, shall provide necessary assistance in the documentary requirements for the loans, credit, grants, donations and other financial support;

10. To avail preferential rights granted to Cooperatives under RA 7160, otherwise known as the Local Government Code, and other laws, particularly those in the grant of franchises to establish, construct, operate and maintain ferries, wharves, markets or slaughterhouses and to lease public utilities, including access to extension and on-site research services and facilities related to agriculture and fishery activities;

11. To exercise such other powers granted under RA 9520 or necessary to carry out its purposes as stated in this Articles of Cooperation; and

12. To act as Guardian Cooperative and accept the responsibilities of supervising and monitoring the activities of the Laboratory Cooperative and act in its behalf in dealings with third parties when capacity to contract is required. *(applicable to Guardian Cooperative only -- delete if not applicable)*

> **Fill-up note:** Article IV is standard across all cooperative types. Adopt as written. Delete item 12 only if your cooperative is not serving as a Guardian Cooperative.

## Article V -- Term of Existence

That the term for which this Cooperative shall exist is **[___term in words, e.g., "Fifty"___] ([___number___]) years** from the date of its registration with the Cooperative Development Authority.

> **Fill-up note:** The maximum term is fifty (50) years. Most cooperatives choose the full fifty years. You may choose a shorter term, but there is no practical advantage to doing so.

---

## Article VI -- Common Bond and Field of Membership

That the common bond of membership of this Cooperative is **[___choose one: Residential / Institutional / Associational / Occupational___]** and the field of membership shall be open to all **[___describe specific field, e.g., "residents of Barangay Kalanganan, Marawi City" or "employees of the Bangsamoro Regional Government" or "members of the Lanao del Sur Farmers Association" or "fishermen and aquaculture workers"___]** who are natural persons, Filipino citizens, of legal age, with the capacity to contract and possess all the qualifications and none of the disqualifications provided for in the By-laws and this Articles of Cooperation.

> **Fill-up note:** Choose only ONE common bond. The four types are:
> - **Residential** -- members working and/or residing in the same place
> - **Institutional** -- members are employees, workers, or officers of a particular institution
> - **Associational** -- members come from a registered or recognized association, group, or club
> - **Occupational** -- members come from the same or allied profession or occupation
>
> Your field of membership must be specific enough to clearly identify who is eligible. "Residents of BARMM" is too broad. "Residents of Barangay Kalanganan, Marawi City" is appropriately specific.

## Article VII -- Area of Operation

That the membership of this Cooperative shall come from **[___geographic area, e.g., "Barangay Kalanganan, Marawi City, Lanao del Sur"___]**.

Its principal office shall be located at **[___complete address: Number/Unit/Floor, Street, Barangay, City/Municipality, Province, Zip Code___]**.

> **Fill-up note:** The principal office must be within the area of operation. If your area of operation is "Marawi City," your principal office must be in Marawi City. A mismatch between area of operation and principal office address is a common cause of registration denial.

## Adapting This Template for Other Cooperative Types

The template above works for **multi-purpose cooperatives**. For other types, the primary difference is in **Article II -- Purposes**. Here is how to adapt each type:

### Credit Cooperative

Replace Article II purposes 1-5 with:

1. Generating savings among members through thrift and savings mobilization;
2. Extending credit to members for provident and productive purposes;
3. Providing skills development, livelihood, and entrepreneurship training;
4. Providing loan protection and life insurance benefits to members;
5. Providing other financial services as may be allowed by law;

### Agriculture Cooperative

Replace Article II purposes 1-5 with:

1. Raising, culturing, and caring of [___specify: livestock / poultry / fish / crops___];
2. Facilitating the procurement of farm inputs and implements for the members;
3. Processing and marketing of the members' agricultural produce;
4. Storing and transporting of members' agricultural produce;
5. Providing credit facility for agricultural production and related activities;

### Producer Cooperative

Replace Article II purposes 1-5 with:

1. Manufacturing and processing of [___specify raw materials___] into [___specify finished products___];
2. Selling processed products to wholesale and retail markets;
3. Facilitating the procurement of raw materials and production equipment for members;
4. Providing quality control and product development services;
5. Providing credit facility for members' production activities;

### Consumer Cooperative

Replace Article II purposes 1-5 with:

1. Procurement and distribution of commodities to members and non-members through [___specify: retail store / wholesale outlet / canteen / water refilling station___];
2. Operating a [___specify business type___] for the benefit of members and the community;
3. Sourcing quality products at competitive prices for members;
4. Promoting consumer education and responsible consumption;
5. Providing credit facility for members' consumer needs;

### Worker Cooperative

Replace Article II purposes 1-5 with:

1. Providing employment to members through joint labor and production of [___specify products/services___];
2. Managing and operating [___specify business___] owned collectively by the worker-members;
3. Ensuring equitable distribution of income from collective work;
4. Providing skills development and training for worker-members;
5. Providing social protection and welfare benefits to worker-members;

> **Important:** For all types, Articles III through XII remain the same. The adaptation is limited to Article II. For the complete type-specific templates, visit the CDA website at https://cda.gov.ph/downloads/cooperative-pro-forma-registration-documents/
