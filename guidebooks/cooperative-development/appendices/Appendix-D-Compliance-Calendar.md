# Appendix D -- Compliance Calendar and Reporting Templates

This appendix provides a month-by-month compliance calendar for cooperatives operating on a **January-to-December fiscal year** (the standard for most cooperatives). It also includes overviews of the key reporting templates you will need throughout the year. Post this calendar in your cooperative office. Assign a responsible officer to each deadline.

---

## Key Reporting Templates Overview

### 1. CAPR -- Cooperative Annual Progress Report

The CAPR is submitted online via the CAIS system. It contains six modules:

| Module | Contents |
|--------|----------|
| **General Information** | Cooperative name, registration number, COR date, type, principal office, area of operation, contact details |
| **Business Activities** | Types of operations, services offered, revenue sources, transaction volumes |
| **Membership** | Total members by type (regular/associate), new members, terminated members, active vs. inactive count |
| **Officers** | Current BOD members, committee members, management staff, training compliance status for each officer |
| **Transactions and Finances** | Deposit liabilities, loans outstanding, interest/service charge income, taxes paid |
| **Other Information** | Type-specific data, community development activities, awards/recognition, federation/union affiliation |

**Submission process:**
1. Log in to CAIS (https://cs.cda.gov.ph/cais/)
2. Complete all six modules
3. Upload financial statements
4. Validate data entry
5. Submit electronically
6. Print acknowledgment receipt
7. Submit hard copy AFS to CSEA/CDA Provincial/Extension Office

### 2. CAFS -- Cooperative Audited Financial Statements

The CAFS must include:

| Statement | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Statement of Financial Position** (Balance Sheet) | Shows the cooperative's assets, liabilities, and members' equity as of fiscal year end |
| **Statement of Operations** (Income Statement) | Shows revenues, expenses, and net surplus or loss for the fiscal year |
| **Statement of Changes in Members' Equity** | Shows movements in share capital, reserve fund, and other equity accounts during the year |
| **Statement of Cash Flows** | Shows cash inflows and outflows from operating, investing, and financing activities |
| **Notes to Financial Statements** | Explains accounting policies, significant transactions, and additional details for each statement |

**Audit requirements by cooperative size:**

| Total Assets | Auditor Requirement |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Less than PHP 3 million | Internal audit acceptable (Audit Committee) |
| PHP 3 million to PHP 15 million | Must be signed by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) |
| More than PHP 15 million | Must be audited by an external auditor accredited by CDA/CSEA |

### 3. Certificate of Compliance (COC)

The COC is issued by CSEA/CDA after verifying that the cooperative has met all compliance requirements:

| Requirement for COC Issuance | Status |
|-----------------------------|--------|
| CAPR submitted (complete and accurate) | [ ] |
| CAFS submitted (audited as required) | [ ] |
| Surety bonds current and adequate | [ ] |
| Officer training compliance met | [ ] |

**COC validity:** One (1) year -- must be renewed annually.

**Uses of COC:**

- Required for government transactions and accreditations
- Required for partnership agreements with government agencies and NGOs

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

| Violation | Consequence | Escalation |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Late CAPR/CAFS submission | Penalty fee + COC delay | Continued non-compliance leads to suspension |
| Failure to hold General Assembly | Serious violation -- warning letter from CSEA/CDA | Repeated failure leads to dissolution proceedings |
| BOD not meeting monthly | Warning | Continued failure may result in compliance order |
| No proper books of accounts | Serious violation | May result in suspension of operations |
| Fund misuse | Criminal liability for responsible officers | Dissolution of cooperative |
| Failure to operate for 2 consecutive years | Ground for dissolution | Registration cancellation |
| Failure to maintain minimum 15 members | Ground for dissolution | Registration cancellation |
| Operating beyond scope of registration | Warning to cancellation | Depending on severity |
