# Appendix B -- Bylaws Template with Shari'ah Provisions

This appendix provides a complete bylaws template based on the CDA model, enhanced with provisions for Shari'ah compliance relevant to Bangsamoro cooperatives. The Shari'ah provisions appear in **Article XII** (a new article added to the standard CDA template) and are cross-referenced throughout. Every fill-in field is marked with [___].

**Before you begin**, make sure you have:
- Your completed **Articles of Cooperation** (Appendix A)
- The **cooperative name** exactly as it appears on your AOC and Name Reservation Slip
- The **common bond and field of membership** exactly as stated in Article VI of your AOC
- Agreement on **capitalization details** consistent with Articles X and XI of your AOC
- Agreement on **statutory fund allocation percentages** (minimum percentages set by RA 9520)

**Document specifications per CDA MC 2015-01:**
- Paper size: 8.5" x 13" (legal) or 8.5" x 14" (long)
- Font: Bookman Old Style, size 12
- Prepare 4 original copies
- At least a majority of members (including cooperators) must sign on the adoption page
- Use non-metallic fasteners only

## Article II -- Membership

**Section 1. Kinds of Membership.** This Cooperative shall have regular and associate members.

*Regular Members* are those who have complied with all the membership requirements and are entitled to all the rights and privileges of membership.

*Associate Members* are those who have no right to vote nor be voted upon and are entitled only to limited rights, privileges and membership duration as provided in the By-laws of the Cooperative, the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008, and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.

An associate member who meets the minimum requirements of regular membership and continues to patronize the Cooperative for two (2) years, and signifies his/her intention to remain a member shall be considered a regular member.

   - b. [___]
   - c. [___]

2. **Associate Members** *(if applicable)*
   - a. [___]
   - b. [___]
   - c. [___]

Example for a residential common bond: "Residents of Barangay Kalanganan, Marawi City, Lanao del Sur who are at least 18 years of age." - b. Certificate of completion of the prescribed Pre-Membership Education Seminar (PMES);
- c. Subscribed and paid the required minimum share capital and membership fee; and

**Section 4. Application for Membership.** An applicant for membership shall file a duly accomplished form to the Board of Directors who shall act upon the application within **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) days** from the date of filing. The Board of Directors shall devise a form for the purpose which shall, aside from the personal data of the applicant, include the duties of a member to participate in all programs including but not limited to capital build-up and savings mobilization of the Cooperative and, such other information as may be deemed necessary.

The application form for membership shall include an undertaking to uphold the By-laws, policies, guidelines, rules and regulations promulgated by the Board of Directors and the general assembly. No application for membership shall be given due course if not accompanied with a membership fee of **[___amount in words___] (PHP [___amount___])**, which shall be refunded to the applicant in case of rejection.

**Section 5. Appeal.** An applicant whose application was denied by the Board of Directors may appeal to the General Assembly and the latter's decision shall be final. For this purpose, the General Assembly may opt to create an Appeal and Grievance Committee/Membership Committee. The Appeal and Grievance Committee/Membership Committee shall decide appeals on membership application within thirty (30) days upon receipt thereof.

**Section 6. Minimum Share Capital Requirement.** An applicant for **regular membership** shall subscribe at least **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) shares** and pay the value of at least **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) shares** upon approval of his/her membership.

An applicant for **associate membership** shall subscribe at least **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) shares** and pay the value of at least **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) shares** upon approval of his/her membership.

However, no member shall own or hold more than ten percent (10%) of the total subscribed share capital of the Cooperative.

> **Fill-up note:** The minimum subscription and payment amounts here must be consistent with your capitalization plan in the AOC. For example, if your par value is PHP 100 per share, a minimum subscription of 5 shares = PHP 500, with at least 2 shares paid up = PHP 200.

**Section 7. Duties and Responsibilities of a Member.** Every member shall have the following duties:

- a) Pay the installment of his/her share capital subscription as it falls due and to participate in the capital build-up and savings mobilization activities of the Cooperative;
- b) Patronize the Cooperative's business(es) and services;
- c) Participate in the membership education programs and other activities and affairs of the Cooperative;
- d) Attend and participate in the deliberation of all matters taken during General Assembly meetings;
- e) Observe and obey all lawful orders, decisions, rules and regulations adopted by the Board of Directors and the General Assembly.

**Section 8. Rights and Privileges of Members.**

**Regular Members**

- a. Attend general membership meetings;
- b. Avail himself of the services of the Cooperative, subject to certain conditions as may be prescribed by the Board of Directors;
- c. Inspect and examine the books of accounts, the audited financial statements, the minutes books, the share register, and other records of the Cooperative during reasonable office hours;
- d. Secure copies of Cooperative records/documents pertaining to the account information of the concerned member;
- e. Participate in the continuing education and other training programs of the Cooperative; and
- f. Such other rights and privileges as may be granted by the General Assembly.

**Associate Members** *(if applicable)*

- a. Attend general membership meetings;
- b. Avail themselves of the services of the Cooperative, subject to certain conditions as may be prescribed by the Board of Directors;
- c. Inspect and examine the books of accounts, the audited financial statements, the minutes books, the share register, and other records of the Cooperative during reasonable office hours;
- d. Secure copies of Cooperative records/documents pertaining to the account information of the concerned member;
- e. Has preference in the payment of interest on share capital and in the distribution of the net assets of the Cooperative in case of liquidation;
- f. Such other rights and privileges as may be granted by the General Assembly.

**Section 9. Members Entitled to Vote.** Any regular member who meets the following conditions is a member entitled to vote:

- a. Paid the membership fee and the value of the minimum shares required for membership;
- b. Not delinquent in the payment of his/her share capital subscriptions and other accounts or obligations;
- c. Has completed the continuing education program prescribed by the Board of Directors;
- d. Has participated in the affairs of the Cooperative and patronized its businesses in accordance with cooperative's policies and guidelines; and
- e. [___other conditions, or delete this line___].

Failure of the member to meet any of the above conditions shall mean suspension of voting until the same have been lifted upon the determination of the Board of Directors.

**Section 10. Liability of Members.** A member shall be liable for the debts of the Cooperative only to the extent of his/her subscribed share capital.

**Section 11. Termination of Membership.** Termination of membership may be automatic, voluntary or involuntary, which shall have the effect of extinguishing all rights of a member in the Cooperative, subject to refund of share capital contributions under Section 13 hereof.

- a. **Automatic Termination of Membership.** The death or insanity of a member shall be considered an automatic termination of his/her membership in the Cooperative: Provided, however, that in case of death or insanity of a member, the next-of-kin shall assume the duties and responsibilities of the original member.

- b. **Voluntary Termination.** A member may, for any valid reason, withdraw his/her membership from the Cooperative by giving a sixty (60) day notice to the Board of Directors.

- c. **Involuntary Termination.** A member may be terminated by a vote of the majority of all the members of the Board of Directors for any of the following causes:
  - i. Has not patronized the service(s)/business(es) of the Cooperative;
  - ii. Has continuously failed to comply with his/her obligations;
  - iii. Has violated any provision of this By-laws and the policies of the Cooperative; and
  - iv. For any act or omission injurious or prejudicial to the interest or the welfare of the Cooperative, as defined by the General Assembly.

**Section 12. Manner of Involuntary Termination.** The Board of Directors shall notify in writing the member who is being considered for termination and shall give him/her the opportunity to be heard. The written decision of the board of directors shall be communicated in person or by registered mail to said member and is appealable within thirty (30) days from receipt thereof to the General Assembly or Appeal and Grievance Committee/Membership Committee, as the case may be, whose decision shall be final.

**Section 13. Refund of Share Capital Contribution.** A member whose membership is terminated shall be entitled to a refund of his/her share capital contribution and all other interests in the Cooperative. However, such refund shall not be made if upon payment the value of the assets of the Cooperative would be less than the aggregate amount of its debts and liabilities exclusive of his/her share capital contribution.

## Article III -- Administration

**Section 1. The General Assembly (GA).** The General Assembly is composed of all the members entitled to vote, duly assembled and constituting a quorum and is the highest policy-making body of the Cooperative.

**Section 2. Powers of the General Assembly.** Subject to the pertinent provisions of the Cooperative Code and the rules issued thereunder, the General Assembly shall have the following exclusive powers which cannot be delegated:

- a) To determine and approve amendments to the Cooperative Articles of Cooperation and By-laws;
- b) To elect or appoint the members of the Board of Directors, and to remove them for cause; and
- c) To approve developmental plans of the Cooperative.

**Section 3. Meetings.** Meetings of the General Assembly may be regular or special. All proceedings and business(es) undertaken at any meeting of the General Assembly, if within the powers or authority of the Cooperative, there being a quorum, shall be valid.

**Section 4. Regular General Assembly Meeting.** The General Assembly shall hold its annual regular meeting every **[___month, e.g., "March"___]** at the principal office of the Cooperative or at any place as may be determined by the Board.

> **Fill-up note:** The annual regular meeting must be held within ninety (90) days after the close of the calendar year. If your fiscal year ends December 31, the meeting must be held by March 31. Most cooperatives hold their annual GA in February or March.

**Section 5. Special General Assembly Meeting.** The Board of Directors may, by a majority vote of all its members, call a Special General Assembly meeting at any time to consider urgent matters. The Board must likewise call a Special GA meeting within one (1) month from receipt of a written request from:

- a. at least ten percent (10%) of the total number of members entitled to vote;
- b. the Audit Committee; or
- c. the Federation or Union to which the Cooperative is a member; or
- d. upon Order of the Cooperative Development Authority.

**Section 6. Notice of Meeting.** All notices of meetings shall be in writing and shall include the date, time, place, and agenda thereof.

- a. **Regular General Assembly Meeting.** Notice shall be served by the Secretary personally, by registered mail, or by electronic means to all members at least one (1) week before the meeting. It shall be accompanied with an agenda, minutes of the last GA meeting, consolidated reports, audited financial statements, and other relevant papers.

- b. **Special General Assembly Meeting.** Notice shall be served at least one (1) week before the meeting. It shall state the purpose, and except for related issues, no other business shall be considered.

- c. **Waiver of Notice.** Notice of any meeting may be waived, expressly or impliedly, by the member concerned.

**Section 7. Order of Business.** The order of business of a Regular General Assembly meeting shall be:

- a. Call to Order;
- b. Proof of due notice;
- c. Roll Call;
- d. Reading, consideration and approval of the minutes of the previous meeting;
- e. Presentation and approval of reports of the Board of Directors, officers, and committees, including CAPR and all other required reports;
- f. Unfinished business;
- g. New business (including election of directors and committee members, approval of development plan and budget, hiring of external auditor, and other matters);
- h. Announcements; and
- i. Adjournment.

**Section 8. Quorum for General Assembly Meeting.** During Regular or Special General Assembly meeting, **[___percentage, at least twenty-five percent___]** of the total number of members entitled to vote shall constitute a quorum.

> **Fill-up note:** The law requires at least twenty-five percent (25%). You may set a higher quorum but not lower.

**Section 9. Voting System.** Only members entitled to vote shall participate and vote. A member is entitled to one vote only regardless of the number of shares owned. Election or removal of Directors and Committee members shall be by secret ballot. No proxy and/or cumulative voting shall be allowed.

## Article IV -- Board of Directors

**Section 1. Composition of the Board of Directors (BOD).** The Board of Directors shall be composed of **[___number in words___] ([___number___])** members.

> **Fill-up note:** Must be an odd number, not less than 5 nor more than 15. Must be consistent with Article IX of the AOC.

**Section 2. Functions and Responsibilities.** The Board of Directors shall:

- a. Provide over-all policy direction;
- b. Formulate development plan;
- c. Review the annual plan and budget and recommend for the approval of the General Assembly;
- d. Evaluate the capability and qualification of external auditor candidates;
- e. Appoint and terminate the General Manager or CEO;
- f. Review, monitor and evaluate program effectiveness;
- g. Formulate and review the vision, mission and goals;
- h. Establish risk management system;
- i. Establish performance evaluation system at all levels;
- j. Review and approve organizational and operational structures;
- k. Establish policies and procedures for effective operation;
- l. Appoint committee members as specified in the Code and By-laws;
- m. Decide election-related cases involving the Election Committee;
- n. Act on Ethics Committee recommendations on governance violations;
- o. Ensure compliance with CSEA/CDA regulations and other statutory requirements;
- p. Report to the General Assembly on performance and achievements;
- q. Present policies requiring confirmation to the General Assembly;
- r. Present financial, social and performance reports to the General Assembly; and
- s. Perform such other functions as authorized by the General Assembly.

**Section 3. Qualifications.** Any member who is entitled to vote and has the following qualifications can be elected or continue as member of the Board of Directors:

- a. Has paid the minimum capital requirement;
- b. Has no delinquent account with the Cooperative;
- c. Has continuously patronized the Cooperative services;
- d. A member in good standing for the last two (2) years;
- e. Completed or willingness to complete within the prescribed period the required education and training; and
- f. [___other qualifications, or delete this line___]

**Section 4. Disqualifications.** Any member under any of the following circumstances shall be disqualified:

- a. Holding any elective position in the government, except party-list representatives who are cooperative officers;
- b. Holding any position directly involved in day-to-day cooperative operations;
- c. Having direct or indirect personal interest with the business of the Cooperative;
- d. Having been absent for **[___number___]** consecutive meetings or more than **[___percentage___]** of all meetings within twelve months, unless with valid excuse approved by the Board;
- e. Being an official or employee of CSEA/CDA, except in cooperatives organized among themselves;
- f. Having been convicted by final judgment involving financial and/or property accountability; and
- g. Having been disqualified by law.

**Section 5. Procedure for Disqualifications.** The procedure for disqualification shall be provided in the election guidelines or policy of the Cooperative.

**Section 6. Election of Directors.** The members of the Board shall be elected by secret ballot during the annual Regular General Assembly meeting. Unless earlier removed, they shall hold office for a term of **[___number in words___] ([___number___]) years** or until their successors shall have been elected and qualified. Majority of the elected directors obtaining the highest number of votes during the first election after registration shall serve for two (2) years, and the remaining directors for one (1) year. Thereafter, all directors shall serve for a term of two (2) years.

**Section 7. Election of Officers within the Board.** The Board shall convene within ten (10) days after the General Assembly meeting to elect from among themselves the Chairperson and the Vice-Chairperson, and to elect or appoint the Secretary and Treasurer from outside the Board.

**Section 8. Meeting of the Board of Directors.** The regular meeting of the Board shall be held at least once a month. The Chairperson or majority of directors may call a Special Board meeting at any time. Notice of special meetings shall be served in writing at least one (1) week before the meeting. Majority of the total number of Directors constitutes a quorum.

**Section 9. Vacancies.** Any vacancy in the Board may be filled within thirty (30) days by a majority vote of the remaining directors, if still constituting a quorum; otherwise, the General Assembly shall fill the vacancy in a regular or special meeting. The elected director shall serve only for the unexpired term.

**Section 10. Removal of Members of the Board and Committees.** All complaints for removal shall be filed with the Board, and the officer shall be given the opportunity to be heard. An elective officer may be removed by three-fourths (3/4) of the regular members present and constituting a quorum, in a Regular or Special General Assembly meeting called for the purpose.

**Section 11. Prohibitions.** Any member of the Board shall not hold any other position directly involved in day-to-day operation and management of the Cooperative nor engage in any business similar to that of the Cooperative or that creates a conflict of interest.

## Article V -- Committees

**Section 1. Audit Committee.** An Audit Committee shall be composed of three (3) members elected during a General Assembly meeting, serving a term of one (1) year. No member of the committee shall hold any other position within the Cooperative. The Committee shall provide internal audit service, maintain complete records of its examination and inventory, and submit audit reports quarterly or as required.

**Section 2. Functions of the Audit Committee.** The Audit Committee shall:

- a. Audit the performance of the cooperative and its responsibility centers;
- b. Monitor adequacy and effectiveness of management and internal control systems;
- c. Review books of accounts, financial records, and policies on internal control, accounting and risk management;
- d. Review internal audit reports;
- e. Follow up on internal and external audit recommendations;
- f. Discuss internal audit results with the Board;
- g. Submit reports and recommend policy changes to the General Assembly;
- h. Review, approve or amend Ethics Committee reports when the Board fails to act within 30 days; and
- i. Perform such other functions as prescribed in the By-laws.

**Section 3. Election Committee.** An Election Committee shall be composed of three (3) members elected during a General Assembly meeting, serving a term of one (1) year. No member of the committee shall hold any other position within the Cooperative.

**Section 4. Functions of the Election Committee.** The Election Committee shall:

- a. Formulate election rules and guidelines for GA approval;
- b. Recommend necessary amendments to election rules;
- c. Implement duly approved election rules;
- d. Supervise the conduct and proceedings of elections;
- e. Canvass and certify election results;
- f. Proclaim winning candidates;
- g. Decide election-related cases except those involving the Committee itself; and
- h. Perform such other functions as prescribed in the By-laws.

**Section 5. Education and Training Committee.** An Education and Training Committee shall be composed of three (3) members appointed by the Board, serving a term of one (1) year. The Vice-Chairperson of the Board shall act as Committee Chairperson.

**Section 6. Functions of the Education and Training Committee.** The Education and Training Committee shall:

- a. Keep members, officers, and staff informed regarding cooperative goals, policies, procedures, and services;
- b. Plan and implement educational programs for members, officers and staff;
- c. Develop promotional and training materials; and
- d. Conduct and coordinate training activities.

**Section 7. Mediation and Conciliation Committee.** A Mediation and Conciliation Committee shall be composed of three (3) members appointed by the Board, serving a term of one (1) year. No member shall hold any other position in the Cooperative during his/her term.

**Section 8. Functions of the Mediation and Conciliation Committee.** The Mediation and Conciliation Committee shall:

- a. Conduct mediation-conciliation proceedings and services;
- b. Formulate and develop conciliation-mediation policies and guidelines;
- c. Monitor conciliation-mediation programs and processes;
- d. Submit semi-annual reports of cooperative cases to CSEA/CDA;
- e. Accept and file evaluation reports;
- f. Submit recommendations for improvement to the Board;
- g. Recommend members for conciliation-mediation training;
- h. Issue Certificates of Non-Settlement (CNS); and
- i. Act as conciliator-mediator during their term.

**Section 9. Ethics Committee.** An Ethics Committee shall be composed of three (3) members appointed by the Board, serving a term of one (1) year. No member shall hold any other position in the Cooperative during his/her term.

**Section 10. Functions of the Ethics Committee.** The Ethics Committee shall:

- a. Formulate, develop, implement and monitor the Code of Governance and Ethical Standards (CGES);
- b. Conduct initial investigation on complaints involving violations of the CGES;
- c. Submit reports with recommended sanctions to the Board; and
- d. Perform such other functions as prescribed in the By-laws.

**Section 11. Gender and Development (GAD) Committee.** A GAD Committee shall be composed of three (3) members appointed by the Board, with at least one member from the Board. The Committee shall develop and recommend GAD and Gender Equality policies, monitor GAD program implementation, and submit reports to the Board.

**Section 12. Other Committees.** The Board of Directors may create such other committees as may be deemed necessary for the operation of the Cooperative, including but not limited to a Credit Committee for cooperatives engaged in lending activities.

**Section 13. Qualification and Disqualification of Committee Members.** The qualifications and disqualifications of the Board of Directors shall also apply to all members of the committees.

## Article VI -- Officers and Management Staff

**Section 1. Officers and their Duties.** The officers of the cooperative shall include the Members of the Board of Directors, Members of the Different Committees, General Manager/Chief Executive Officer, Secretary and Treasurer.

**a. Chairperson** -- shall set and prepare the agenda for board meetings, preside all meetings of the Board and General Assembly, sign contracts and documents as authorized, and perform such other functions as authorized by the Board.

**b. Vice-Chairperson** -- shall perform all duties of the Chairperson in his/her absence and perform such other duties as delegated by the Board.

**c. Treasurer** -- shall ensure all cash collections are deposited per Board policy, have custody of funds, securities and related documentation, monitor financial management operations, ensure maintenance of complete cash transaction records, maintain a Petty Cash Fund, maintain a Daily Cash Position Report, and perform such other functions as prescribed.

**d. Secretary** -- shall keep an updated and complete member registry, record and maintain minutes of all meetings, ensure Board decisions are transmitted to management, issue and certify the list of members entitled to vote, prepare and issue Share Certificates, serve notice of all meetings, certify quorum, keep copies of Treasurer's reports, serve as custodian of the Cooperative seal, and perform such other functions as prescribed.

**e. General Manager** -- shall oversee day-to-day business operations, assist the Board in formulating development plans, provide systems and procedures for policy implementation, implement approved plans and programs, submit monthly status reports to the Board, represent the Cooperative in authorized transactions, ensure regulatory compliance, and perform such other functions as delegated.

**Section 2. Liabilities of Directors, Officers and Committee Members.** Directors, officers and committee members who willfully and knowingly vote for or assent to patently unlawful acts, or who are guilty of gross negligence or bad faith, shall be liable jointly and severally for all damages resulting therefrom.

**Section 3. Management Staff.** The core management team shall take charge of day-to-day operations. The Board shall appoint, fix compensation, and prescribe functions and responsibilities.

**Section 4. Compensation.** Subject to the approval of the General Assembly, members of the Board and Committees may receive per diems for actual attendance and reimbursement of actual and necessary expenses. Directors and officers shall not be entitled to any per diem when, in the preceding calendar year, the Cooperative reported a net loss or had a dividend rate less than the official inflation rate.

## Article VII -- Capital Structure

**Section 1. Source of Funds.** The Cooperative may derive its funds from:

- a. Member's share capital contributions;
- b. Loans and borrowings including deposits;
- c. Revolving capital build-up (deferred patronage refund or interest on share capital);
- d. Subsidies, grants, legacies, aids, donations, awards and assistance from local or foreign institutions;
- e. Retentions from proceeds of services acquired/goods procured by members; and
- f. Other sources authorized by law.

**Section 2. Continuous Capital Build-Up.** Every member shall have invested in any or all of the following:

- a. At least **[___amount___] Pesos (PHP [___])** per month;
- b. At least **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** of his/her annual interest on capital and patronage refund; and
- c. At least **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** of each good procured/service acquired from the Cooperative.

**Section 3. Borrowing.** The Board of Directors, upon approval of the General Assembly, may borrow funds from any source under terms that best serve the Cooperative's interest. All borrowings shall comply with the Shari'ah compliance provisions in Article XII of this By-laws, where applicable.

**Section 4. Revolving Capital.** The General Assembly may authorize the Board to raise revolving capital by deferring payment of patronage refunds and interest on share capital, or such other legally adopted schemes.

**Section 5. Share Capital Certificate.** The Board shall issue a Share Capital Certificate only to a member who has fully paid his/her subscription. No member shall own or hold more than ten percent (10%) of the total subscribed share capital.

**Section 6. Assignment of Share Capital.** No member shall transfer shares unless: (1) held for not less than one year, (2) assigned to the Cooperative or an eligible member, and (3) approved by the Board.

## Article VIII -- Allocation and Distribution of Net Surplus

**Section 1. Allocation.** At the end of its calendar year, the Cooperative shall allocate and distribute its net surplus as follows:

**a. Reserve Fund.** **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** shall be set aside for the Reserve Fund. In the first five (5) years after registration, this amount shall not be less than fifty percent (50%) of the net surplus. The reserve fund shall be used for the stability of the Cooperative and to meet net losses. It shall not be distributed among members. Upon dissolution, the reserve fund shall be disposed of per resolution of the General Assembly in accordance with RA 9520.

**b. Education and Training Fund (ETF).** **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** shall be set aside for the ETF. Half may be spent by the Cooperative for its own education and training purposes; the other half may be remitted to a union or federation chosen by the Cooperative.

**c. Community Development Fund (CDF).** **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** shall be used for projects and activities benefiting the community where the Cooperative operates.

**d. Optional Fund.** **[___percentage___] percent ([___]%)** shall be set aside for Land and Building Fund, or any other necessary fund as determined by the General Assembly.

> **Fill-up note:** Minimum statutory percentages per RA 9520 Article 86:
> - Reserve Fund: 10% (50% in first 5 years)
> - ETF: up to 10%
> - CDF: 3%
> - You may set higher percentages. The remainder after statutory allocations goes to interest on share capital and patronage refund.

**Section 2. Interest on Share Capital and Patronage Refund.** The remaining net surplus shall be made available to members in the form of interest on share capital (not to exceed the normal rate of return on investment) and patronage refunds, distributed in proportion to individual patronage.

## Article IX -- Settlement of Disputes

**Section 1. Mediation and Conciliation.** All inter and intra-cooperative disputes shall be settled within the cooperative in accordance with CDA guidelines, Article 137 of RA 9520, and the Alternative Dispute Resolution Act of 2004.

**Section 2. Voluntary Arbitration.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this By-laws shall be exclusively referred to voluntary arbitration under CDA institutional rules, after compliance with the conciliation or mediation mechanisms in this By-laws.

---

## Article X -- Miscellaneous

**Section 1. Investment of Capital.** The Cooperative may invest its capital in securities of secondary cooperatives, reputable banks, government securities, real estate for cooperative use, or any other manner approved by the General Assembly. All investments shall comply with the Shari'ah compliance provisions in Article XII of this By-laws, where applicable.

**Section 2. Accounting System.** The Cooperative shall keep all books of accounts in accordance with the Standard Charts of Accounts (SCA) for Cooperatives and the Philippine Financial Reporting Framework (PFRF) for Cooperatives.

**Section 3. Financial Audit, Performance Audit, and Social Audit.** At least once a year, the Board shall cause the audit of the Cooperative's books in accordance with CDA/CSEA guidelines.

**Section 4. Annual Report.** During the annual Regular Assembly meeting, the Officers shall submit a report of operations together with audited financial statements, performance audit, social audit reports, and list of officers and trainings completed. The Cooperative shall submit the web-based CAPR together with the CAFS and attachments to CSEA/CDA within 120 days from the end of the calendar year.

---

## Article XI -- Amendments

**Section 1. Amendment of Articles of Cooperation and By-laws.** Amendments may be adopted by at least two-thirds (2/3) votes of all members with voting rights, present and constituting a quorum. Amendments shall take effect upon approval by CSEA/CDA.

## Article XII -- Shari'ah Compliance Provisions

> This article is specific to Bangsamoro cooperatives that choose to operate in accordance with Islamic principles. It is an addition to the standard CDA bylaws template, authorized under BTA Bill No. 210 which requires cooperatives in BARMM to align with moral governance and Shari'ah principles. Cooperatives that do not wish to adopt Shari'ah provisions may delete this article entirely.

**Section 1. Establishment of the Shari'ah Advisory Committee.** The Board of Directors shall establish a **Shari'ah Advisory Committee** composed of at least three (3) members, of whom at least one (1) shall possess formal education or recognized expertise in Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh al-mu'amalat* -- Islamic commercial law). The Committee shall be appointed by the Board and shall serve for a term of one (1) year, renewable upon reappointment.

The Shari'ah Advisory Committee shall:

- a. Review all business activities, financial products, and investment decisions of the Cooperative for compliance with Shari'ah principles;
- b. Issue written opinions (*fatwa*) on whether proposed transactions, contracts, or business arrangements comply with Islamic law;
- c. Conduct an annual Shari'ah audit of the Cooperative's operations and submit a compliance report to the Board and the General Assembly;
- d. Advise the Board on the design of Shari'ah-compliant financial products, including but not limited to *murabaha* (cost-plus financing), *musharakah* (partnership), *mudarabah* (profit-sharing), and *ijarah* (leasing);
- e. Provide guidance on the proper distribution of profits and surpluses in accordance with Islamic principles;
- f. Train officers and members on the fundamentals of Islamic finance and Shari'ah-compliant cooperative operations; and
- g. Coordinate with the BARMM Darul Ifta' or recognized Islamic scholars for guidance on complex matters.

**Section 2. Prohibition on Riba (Interest-Based Transactions).** The Cooperative shall not engage in any transaction involving *riba* (usury or excessive interest). All lending, borrowing, and financing activities shall be structured using Shari'ah-compliant instruments.

Specifically:

- a. **Lending to members** shall be conducted through Shari'ah-compliant modes such as *murabaha* (the Cooperative purchases an asset and sells it to the member at a disclosed markup), *musharakah* (joint venture where both the Cooperative and the member share in profits and losses), or *qard hasan* (benevolent loan with no interest, where only the principal is repaid);
- b. **Borrowing by the Cooperative** shall be structured to avoid interest-based debt. The Cooperative may enter into partnership arrangements, seek grants, or use other Shari'ah-compliant financing instruments;
- c. **Savings and deposits** by members shall not earn interest (*riba*). Returns on member deposits, if any, shall be based on profit-sharing arrangements where the member shares in the actual profits generated by the Cooperative's Shari'ah-compliant activities;
- d. **Service charges and administrative fees** that are genuinely cost-based and do not constitute *riba* are permissible. The Shari'ah Advisory Committee shall review all fee structures.

> **Transition provision:** Cooperatives transitioning from conventional to Shari'ah-compliant operations may maintain existing interest-based arrangements until their contractual maturity, provided no new interest-based transactions are entered into after the adoption of this By-laws amendment.

**Section 3. Halal Business Activities Requirement.** The Cooperative shall engage only in business activities that are *halal* (permissible under Islamic law). The following activities are strictly prohibited:

- a. Production, trade, or distribution of alcoholic beverages;
- b. Production, trade, or distribution of pork and pork-derived products;
- c. Gambling (*maysir*) in any form, including lotteries, raffles with gambling elements, and speculative trading;
- d. Production or distribution of materials deemed immoral or harmful under Islamic principles;
- e. Investment in or financing of any enterprise engaged in the above prohibited activities; and
- f. Any transaction involving excessive uncertainty (*gharar*) that renders the contract void under Islamic commercial law.

The Shari'ah Advisory Committee shall maintain a list of permissible and prohibited activities specific to the Cooperative's line of business and shall update this list as needed.

**Section 4. Moral Governance Code.** In addition to the Code of Governance and Ethical Standards (CGES) required under RA 9520, the Cooperative shall adopt a **Moral Governance Code** that incorporates the following principles:

- a. **Amanah (Trustworthiness)** -- All officers, directors, committee members, and employees shall conduct their duties with the highest degree of trust and integrity. Funds and property of the Cooperative are held in trust (*amanah*) and shall be managed with utmost care and accountability;
- b. **Adalah (Justice)** -- All dealings with members, non-members, partners, and the community shall be conducted with fairness and justice. No member shall be discriminated against on the basis of tribe, clan, gender, or economic status;
- c. **Shura (Consultation)** -- Major decisions affecting the Cooperative shall be made through genuine consultation with members, consistent with the cooperative principle of democratic member control. The General Assembly embodies this principle;
- d. **Ihsan (Excellence)** -- Officers and employees shall strive for excellence in service delivery, financial management, and member relations. Mediocrity in the management of collective resources is a betrayal of the community's trust;
- e. **Transparency and Disclosure** -- Financial records, business decisions, and officer compensation shall be disclosed to members in a timely and accessible manner. Hidden charges, undisclosed conflicts of interest, and concealment of material information are prohibited; and
- f. **Social Responsibility** -- The Cooperative shall allocate resources for the welfare of the community, particularly the poor, orphans, and marginalized groups, consistent with the Islamic principles of *zakat* (obligatory charity) and *sadaqah* (voluntary charity). The Community Development Fund shall prioritize projects that serve these groups.

**Section 5. Shari'ah Compliance Reporting.** The Shari'ah Advisory Committee shall submit an annual **Shari'ah Compliance Report** to the General Assembly alongside the regular financial and performance reports. This report shall:

- a. Certify whether the Cooperative's operations during the reporting period were in compliance with Shari'ah principles;
- b. Identify any transactions or activities that were found to be non-compliant and the corrective actions taken;
- c. Report on the disposition of any income derived from non-compliant activities (which shall be channeled to charitable purposes and not distributed as surplus); and
- d. Recommend improvements to the Cooperative's Shari'ah compliance framework.

**Section 6. Relationship to Philippine Cooperative Law.** The Shari'ah compliance provisions in this Article supplement but do not replace the requirements of Republic Act No. 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations. In the event of any conflict between this Article and the provisions of RA 9520, the provisions of RA 9520 shall prevail. The Cooperative shall comply with all statutory requirements of CSEA/CDA regardless of Shari'ah compliance considerations.
