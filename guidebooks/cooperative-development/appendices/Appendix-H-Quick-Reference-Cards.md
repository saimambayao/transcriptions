## Card 1: ICA 7 Cooperative Principles + Moral Governance

---

### THE 7 COOPERATIVE PRINCIPLES

*Adopted by the International Cooperative Alliance (ICA), 1995 | Codified in RA 9520, Article 4*

| No. | Principle | What It Means |
|:---:|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Voluntary and Open Membership** | Anyone can join who can use the cooperative's services and accepts membership responsibilities -- no discrimination by gender, social class, race, politics, or religion. |
| 2 | **Democratic Member Control** | One member, one vote. Members set policies and make decisions. Elected officers are accountable to the membership, not to themselves. |
| 3 | **Member Economic Participation** | Members contribute capital equitably. Surplus belongs to all members collectively -- distributed through reserves, patronage refunds, or member-approved activities. |
| 4 | **Autonomy and Independence** | The cooperative governs itself. Agreements with government, banks, or other organizations must never surrender democratic member control. |
| 5 | **Education, Training, and Information** | The cooperative trains its members, officers, managers, and staff. It informs the public -- especially the youth -- about what cooperatives are and why they matter. |
| 6 | **Cooperation Among Cooperatives** | Cooperatives grow stronger by working together -- through federations, unions, and alliances at the local, national, and international level. |
| 7 | **Concern for Community** | The cooperative works for sustainable community development through policies approved by its members. Profit is not the only goal. |

---

### MORAL GOVERNANCE: THE BANGSAMORO ADDITION

BTA Bill 210 requires Bangsamoro cooperatives to align with Moral Governance. These 12 pillars complement the 7 ICA Principles:

| Pillar | Application to Cooperatives |
|--------|-----------------------------|
| **Integrity** | Officers handle funds without personal gain |
| **Accountability** | Every peso is documented and reported to members |
| **Transparency** | Financial statements and decisions are open to all members |
| **Patriotism** | The cooperative invests in Bangsamoro communities |
| **Excellence** | The cooperative strives for quality in products and services |
| **Cultural Tolerance** | The cooperative respects diverse traditions within the membership |
| **Loyalty to God** | Operations follow Shari'ah principles and ethical standards |
| **Justice** | All members are treated fairly regardless of clan, tribe, or status |
| **Inclusivity** | Women, youth, persons with disabilities, and indigenous peoples are welcome |
| **Objectivity** | Decisions are based on merit and facts, not personal relationships |
| **Dialogue** | The cooperative consults its members and community before major decisions |
| **Moral Balance** | Business success is balanced with ethical and spiritual responsibility |

---

*"A cooperative is an autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise." -- ICA Definition*

## Card 2: Cooperative Lifecycle Roadmap

---

### FROM IDEA TO GROWTH: YOUR COOPERATIVE JOURNEY

Follow these steps in order. Each step builds on the one before it. Do not skip steps.

---

**PHASE 1: EXPLORE AND ORGANIZE**

| Step | Action | Key Output | Reference |
|:----:|--------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Identify the common need** | Shared economic problem that a cooperative can solve | Chapter 1 |
| 2 | **Build the organizing group** | At least 15 committed individuals with a common bond of interest | Chapter 3 |
| 3 | **Conduct the Economic Survey** | Written assessment of viability: market, members, capital, area of operation | Chapter 3 |
| 4 | **Attend the Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS)** | PRS Certificate from a CDA-accredited provider for all organizers | Chapter 3 |

---

**PHASE 2: REGISTER**

| Step | Action | Key Output | Reference |
|:----:|--------|------------|-----------|
| 5 | **Reserve your cooperative name** | Name Reservation Slip from CSEA or CDA | Chapter 4 |
| 6 | **Draft Articles of Cooperation and Bylaws** | Notarized AOC (4 copies) + signed Bylaws (4 copies) | Chapter 4, Appendix B |
| 7 | **Prepare documentary requirements** | Economic Survey, Treasurer's Affidavit, Surety Bond, PRS Certificate | Chapter 4, Appendix A |
| 8 | **Submit to CSEA-BARMM or CDA** | Filed application; wait up to 60 days for processing | Chapter 4 |
| 9 | **Receive Certificate of Registration** | You are now a registered cooperative with juridical personality | Chapter 4 |

---

**PHASE 3: SET UP AND LAUNCH**

| Step | Action | Key Output | Reference |
|:----:|--------|------------|-----------|
| 10 | **Organize the first General Assembly** | Elect BOD, Audit Committee, Election Committee | Chapter 5 |
| 11 | **Hold the first Board meeting** | Elect Chairperson, appoint Secretary and Treasurer, open bank account | Chapter 5, Appendix E |
| 12 | **Set up books of accounts** | Chart of Accounts, journals, ledgers | Chapter 7, Appendix F |
| 13 | **Officers complete mandatory training** | Training certificates within first half of term | Chapter 10 |
| 14 | **Begin operations** | First transaction with members | Chapter 8 |

---

**PHASE 4: OPERATE AND COMPLY**

| Step | Action | Key Output | Reference |
|:----:|--------|------------|-----------|
| 15 | **Hold monthly Board meetings** | Minutes for every meeting | Chapter 5, Appendix E |
| 16 | **Prepare monthly financial reports** | Treasurer's Report to the Board | Chapter 7, Appendix F |
| 17 | **Hold Annual General Assembly** | Within 90 days after fiscal year end | Chapter 5 |
| 18 | **Submit CAPR and CAFS** | Within 120 days after fiscal year end via CAIS | Chapter 9 |
| 19 | **Distribute net surplus** | Per GA resolution, following statutory fund requirements | Chapter 7, Appendix F |
| 20 | **Obtain Certificate of Compliance** | Annual renewal from CSEA/CDA | Chapter 9 |

---

**PHASE 5: GROW AND STRENGTHEN**

| Step | Action | Key Output | Reference |
|:----:|--------|------------|-----------|
| 21 | **Expand membership and services** | New members, new products/services, increased capital | Chapter 11 |
| 22 | **Join a federation or union** | Network, shared services, collective bargaining power | Chapter 11 |
| 23 | **Develop leaders** | Succession planning, advanced training for officers | Chapter 10 |
| 24 | **Invest in the community** | CDF projects aligned with BDP 2023-2028 | Chapter 14 |

---

*Timeline: Steps 1-9 typically take 3-6 months. Steps 10-14 take 1-3 months. Steps 15-20 are ongoing annually. Steps 21-24 begin after Year 1.*

## Card 3: Shari'ah Compliance Checklist

---

### SHARI'AH COMPLIANCE FOR BANGSAMORO COOPERATIVES

*Based on BTA Bill 210 Governing Principles and Maqasid al-Shari'ah*

---

### PERMISSIBLE (HALAL) -- Do These

| Category | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Transactions** | Conduct only halal (permissible) business activities |
| **Trade** | Use cost-plus financing (*Murabaha*) instead of interest-based loans |
| **Savings** | Offer safekeeping deposits (*Wadiah*) instead of interest-bearing savings |
| **Partnerships** | Use equity partnerships (*Musharakah*) for joint ventures |
| **Investment** | Use profit-sharing (*Mudarabah*) where one party provides capital and the other provides labor |
| **Insurance** | Use mutual guarantee (*Takaful*) instead of conventional insurance |
| **Contracts** | Ensure all contracts have clear terms, known prices, and specified delivery |
| **Products** | Trade only in halal goods and services |
| **Risk** | Share risks equitably among parties -- do not shift all risk to one side |
| **Wealth** | Earn through lawful, productive economic activity |

---

### PROHIBITED (HARAM) -- Avoid These

| Prohibition | Arabic Term | What It Means in Practice |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Usury / Interest** | *Riba* | Do not charge or pay interest on loans or deposits. Use profit-sharing or cost-plus alternatives. |
| **Gambling** | *Maysir* | Do not conduct raffles, lotteries, or games of chance -- even for fundraising. |
| **Excessive Uncertainty** | *Gharar* | Do not enter contracts where the subject matter, price, or delivery date is unknown or ambiguous. |
| **Forbidden Goods** | *Haram* | Do not trade in alcohol, pork, tobacco, or other goods prohibited under Islamic law. |
| **Speculation** | *Gharar* | Do not engage in speculative trading without actual goods or real economic activity. |
| **Exploitation** | *Dhulm* | Do not exploit members, employees, suppliers, or customers through unfair terms. |
| **Deception** | *Ghish* | Do not misrepresent products, services, or financial conditions. |

---

### PRACTICAL CHECKLIST FOR OFFICERS

Before any major transaction, ask these questions:

- [ ] Is the product or service halal?
- [ ] Are the contract terms clear and understood by all parties?
- [ ] Is there real economic activity behind this transaction (not just paper)?
- [ ] Is risk shared fairly, or is one party bearing all the risk?
- [ ] Are we charging interest? If yes, convert to a Shari'ah-compliant alternative.
- [ ] Does this transaction benefit members and community, or only a few?
- [ ] Would we be comfortable explaining this transaction at the General Assembly?
- [ ] Would we be comfortable explaining this transaction to our *Ulama* (religious scholars)?

---

### SHARI'AH-COMPLIANT ALTERNATIVES AT A GLANCE

| Instead of... | Use... | How It Works |
|---------------|--------|--------------|
| Interest-bearing loan | **Murabaha** | Cooperative buys the item, sells to member at cost + agreed markup, payable in installments |
| Savings with interest | **Wadiah** | Member deposits for safekeeping; cooperative may give voluntary gift (*hibah*), not guaranteed return |
| Conventional insurance | **Takaful** | Members contribute to a mutual pool; claims paid from the pool; surplus shared |
| Joint venture with interest | **Musharakah** | Both parties contribute capital, share profits by agreement and losses by capital ratio |
| Silent partnership | **Mudarabah** | One party provides capital, the other provides management; profit shared by agreement, loss borne by capital provider |

---

*When in doubt, consult with the CSEA-BARMM or a qualified Shari'ah advisor. BTA Bill 210 requires alignment with Shari'ah objectives -- this is not optional for Bangsamoro cooperatives.*
