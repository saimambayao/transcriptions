# Introduction

## How to Use This Guidebook

Open this book when you need it. Not before. Not all at once.

Each chapter stands on its own. If you have a feedback conversation tomorrow, read Chapter 3. If you are writing performance evaluations next week, read Chapter 13. If you just became a supervisor and everything feels different, start with Chapter 1.

**The structure follows a logic:**

- **Chapter 1** grounds you in what supervision means in the Bangsamoro context — the values, the transition from doer to leader, the management cycle
- **Chapters 2 through 13** each cover one supervisory competency — assessing performance, giving feedback, coaching, setting goals, conducting check-ins, managing underperformance, developing talent, leading teams, managing change, delegating, building accountability, and evaluating performance
- **Chapter 14** connects supervision to Complete Staff Work (CSW) — the work product your staff produces using the ADDRESS IT methodology
- **The Glossary** defines the terms used throughout
- **The Appendices** give you detachable tools — templates, checklists, and forms you can photocopy and use in real conversations

**Three ways to read this guidebook:**

1. **As a reference** — Look up the chapter for the skill you need right now. Read it. Apply it. Come back when you need the next one.
2. **As a development map** — Use Appendix K (the 12 Supervisory Competencies Quick Reference) to assess yourself. Read the chapters where you scored lowest first.
3. **As a training companion** — If you are attending a Supervisory Development Course, each chapter maps to a training module. Read the chapter before the session. Bring your questions.

Every chapter follows the same pattern:

- **Why this matters** — what happens when this competency is missing
- **The framework** — the model or protocol you will use
- **How to do it** — step-by-step guidance with BARMM examples
- **Common mistakes** — what to avoid
- **Tools and templates** — practical instruments in the appendices

## Who This Guidebook Is For

This guidebook is written for **you** — the supervisor.

You might be a **Director** overseeing an entire office. You might be a **Division Chief** managing a team of ten. You might be a **Section Head** with three staff members and a stack of deliverables. You might be a **Unit Supervisor** who just got promoted last month and is not entirely sure what changed.

What you all share is this: **people report to you**. Their performance is your responsibility. Their development is your mandate. Their output reflects your leadership.

This guidebook speaks to you as a peer being coached. It does not lecture you. It does not assume you are failing. It assumes you want to get better at the hardest part of your job — supervising other people.

**This guidebook is not for staff members.**

Staff members have their own tools — the Complete Staff Work methodology, the ADDRESS IT framework, the ACSCCRaPATFEE competencies. If you supervise staff who use those tools, Chapter 14 shows you how your supervision role connects to their CSW process.

But every chapter in this book is written from **your** seat. The supervisor's seat. The person who sets expectations, gives feedback, conducts check-ins, writes evaluations, coaches struggling performers, develops rising talent, and holds the team together when everything is changing.

That is the work this guidebook helps you do well.
