## PART A: POWER-BY-POWER SHARI'AH ANALYSIS

### 1. Islamic Banking (Power ii) — Art. XIII, Sec. 31-34

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes — the most intensive of all 55 powers.** Islamic banking IS Shari'ah-based finance. The entire subject is grounded in Islamic jurisprudence. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 31 (Islamic banking system); Sec. 32 (Shari'ah-compliant financial institutions, Shari'ah Supervisory Board, tax neutrality); Sec. 34 (Board functions: monitoring, *fatwa* issuance); Art. X, Sec. 4 (commercial law authority) |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) Prohibition of *riba* (interest); (2) Prohibition of *gharar* (excessive uncertainty); (3) Prohibition of *maysir* (gambling/speculation); (4) Permissible contracts: *mudarabah*, *musharakah*, *murabaha*, *ijarah*, *istisnaa*, *sukuk*; (5) *Amanah* (trust/stewardship); (6) *Maslahah* (public interest); (7) *Zakat* (obligatory alms — RA 6848, Sec. 3) |
| **Existing legislation** | RA 11439 (Islamic Banking Act); RA 6848 (AAIIBP Charter); PD 1083 (recognizes Shari'ah, does not cover banking); Art. X, Sec. 4 (parliamentary authority) |
| **Legislative gaps** | (1) No tax incentives or tax neutrality despite express BOL mandate; (2) No Bangsamoro Islamic banking and finance framework; (3) No Bangsamoro Shari'ah Supervisory Board established; (4) No Halal Development Act |
| **How to legislate** | Bangsamoro Islamic Finance Tax Incentives and Neutrality Act; Bangsamoro Islamic Banking and Finance Framework Act (including Shari'ah Supervisory Board, non-bank Islamic finance regulation, AAIIBP participation) |
| **BAA 13 mandates** | Bk. VI, Tit. IV, Sec. 1-3 (MFBM); Bk. V, Tit. II, Ch. 3, Sec. 52-55 (Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta') |

### 2. Agriculture, Livestock, and Food Security (Power c) — Art. V, Sec. 2(c); Art. XIII, Sec. 23

### 4. Environment, Parks, Forest Management (Power x) — Art. XIII, Sec. 8-9

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes — extensively addressed in Islamic law.** Environmental stewardship is a sacred trust. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 8 (protected areas — Parliament shall legislate); Sec. 9 (management transfer from DENR); Sec. 3 (comprehensive sustainable development framework) |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) *Khalifah* (stewardship/vicegerency — sacred duty); (2) *La darar wa la dirar* (no harm — one of five maxims of Islamic jurisprudence); (3) *Hima* (protected reserve — oldest documented protected area system, 14 centuries); (4) *Harim* (inviolable zone — buffer zones around water sources); (5) *Ihya al-mawat* (reviving dead land — reforestation); (6) *Maslahah* (public interest) |
| **Existing legislation** | No Shari'ah legislation on environment. PD 1083 does not address natural resources. |
| **Legislative gaps** | No Bangsamoro Protected Areas Act (expressly mandated by BOL); no Bangsamoro Forestry Code; no Bangsamoro EIA law |
| **How to legislate** | Integration, not separate track: (a) formally recognize *hima* as a protected area category alongside NIPAS; (b) incorporate *harim* principles into watershed/buffer zone regulations; (c) *khalifah* stewardship language in preamble; (d) community-managed protected areas modeled on classical *hima* with ulama participation |
| **BAA 13 mandates** | MENRE (Tit. IX, Sec. 1-12) |

### 9. Trade and Industry (Power zz) — Art. XIII, Sec. 24-28, 30

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes — profound.** Islamic commercial law (*fiqh al-mu'amalat*) is one of the most developed bodies of jurisprudence. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 30 (halal accreditation — **the BOL DOES mention halal explicitly**); Art. XIII, Sec. 31-34 (Islamic banking); Art. X, Sec. 4 (commercial law); BAA 13, Sec. 3(w) (Shari'ah-compliant lending) |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) Halal/*haram* distinction; (2) Prohibition of *riba*; (3) *Mudarabah*/*musharakah* (profit-sharing/joint venture); (4) Prohibition of *gharar* (clear contracts — consumer protection); (5) *Hisbah* (market supervision — the Islamic concept of market regulation by *muhtasib*); (6) *Maslahah* (public interest) |
| **Existing legislation** | BAA 13 Halal Board (Sec. 33-36); BAA 13, Sec. 3(w) (Shari'ah-compliant lending mandate); RA 10817 (national halal framework) |

| **BAA 13 mandates** | MTIT (Bk. VI, Tit. XIV) including Halal Board, Bureau of Product Standards, Trade Regulation and Consumer Protection Division |

**CORRECTION to Old Ch 12**: Old Chapter 12 states "The BOL does not mention 'halal' explicitly." This is **WRONG**. Art. XIII, Sec. 30 expressly provides for halal accreditation and mandates Parliament to ***"enact laws to further strengthen its policy and programs on halal development."***

### 10. Barter Trade and Countertrade (Power e) — Art. XIII, Sec. 26

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes.** Traditional barter (*bay' al-muqayadah*) is a Shari'ah-compliant form of commerce with centuries of history in the Bangsamoro homeland. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 26 (regulate barter trade); Art. XIII, Sec. 28 (customs cooperation) |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) *Bay' al-muqayadah* (barter sale — permissible with specified goods, completed exchange, no coercion); (2) Prohibition of *gharar* (clear contract terms); (3) Prohibition of *riba* (countertrade financing must avoid interest) |
| **Existing legislation** | None specific to barter. EO 313 (1996) and EO 64 (2011) are national. |
| **Legislative gaps** | No BAA regulates barter trade despite detailed BOL mandate — completely dormant legislatively |
| **How to legislate** | Recognize *bay' al-muqayadah* historical legitimacy in preamble; ensure registration requirements do not criminalize traditional Islamic commerce; provide Shari'ah-compliant countertrade financing (*murabaha* trade credit) |
| **Note** | Sulu's exclusion from BARMM (EO 91, s. 2025) reduces barter infrastructure — Jolo was primary barter center. New infrastructure needed in Tawi-Tawi and Basilan. |

### 12. Inland Waters (Power gg) — Art. XIII, Sec. 22

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes — directly applicable.** Water holds special status in Islamic law as a communal resource. Prophetic tradition: Muslims share water, pasture, and fire. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 22 ("exclusive powers" — strongest language in BOL); Art. X, Sec. 4 (property law — water rights) |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) *Maslahah* (public welfare); (2) *Amanah* (stewardship); (3) Communal water rights (*shirkah* — access to drinking water is a fundamental right); (4) Prohibition of *israf* (conservation obligation); (5) Prohibition of *darar* (harm — pollution, upstream harm) |
| **Existing legislation** | PD 1083 does not address water resources. No enacted Shari'ah water legislation. |
| **Legislative gaps** | No Bangsamoro Inland Waters Code; NWRB continues issuing water permits in BARMM; no intergovernmental framework for Rio Grande de Mindanao |
| **How to legislate** | Principled approach: guarantee public access (no privatization); require community consultation (*shura*) before water extraction permits; conservation requirements reflecting *israf* prohibition; environmental liability for pollution grounded in *darar* prohibition; host community benefit sharing reflecting communal water nature |

### 14. Cooperatives and Social Enterprises (Power k) — Art. XIII, Sec. 24, 27

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Shari'ah dimension?** | **Yes — significant.** Cooperative model is deeply consistent with Islamic economic principles, BUT conventional cooperative financing (interest-bearing loans, fixed returns) conflicts with *riba* prohibition. |
| **BOL basis** | Art. XIII, Sec. 24 (***"Bangsamoro cooperative code"*** — named by title); Art. XIII, Sec. 27 (social enterprise legislation); Art. X, Sec. 4 (commercial law authority). NOTE: cooperative code is NOT a priority code under Art. XVI, Sec. 4(a) — it is a separate named legislative mandate. |
| **Key Shari'ah principles** | (1) *Mudarabah* (trust financing/profit-sharing — cooperative pools savings, provides capital to entrepreneurs); (2) *Musharakah* (joint venture — most natural Shari'ah cooperative structure); (3) *Murabaha* (cost-plus — cooperative purchases goods, resells at disclosed markup); (4) Prohibition of *riba*; (5) *Maslahah*; (6) *Amanah* (cooperative officers as trustees) |
| **Existing legislation** | RA 9520 (national Cooperative Code — assumes interest-based financing); EO 316 (1996 — recognized Islamic cooperative principles for ARMM); PD 1083 (limited — does not address cooperatives); BAA 13, Sec. 57-65 (CSEA framework) |
| **Legislative gaps** | No Bangsamoro Cooperative Code; no Shari'ah-compliant cooperative models codified; CSEA cannot independently register cooperatives; social enterprise ecosystem lacks legal definition |
| **How to legislate** | Optional dual-track system: conventional AND Shari'ah-compliant cooperative models as distinct legal categories at registration. Shari'ah track: financing limited to *mudarabah*/*musharakah*/*murabaha*; prohibition of *riba*-bearing transactions; Shari'ah Advisory Board or *Darul Ifta'* certification. Compliance voluntary, not mandatory. |
| **BAA 13 mandates** | CSEA (Sec. 57-65 — attached to OCM) |
| **Pending PB** | PB No. 210 (Bangsamoro Cooperative and Social Enterprise Code) |

|-----------|--------|

## PART B: CROSS-CUTTING SHARI'AH THEMES

### Recurring Principles Across All 15 Powers

| Principle | Arabic | Frequency | Application |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| *Maslahah* | Public interest/welfare | 14 of 15 powers | Justifies all economic regulation |
| *Amanah* | Trust/stewardship | 12 of 15 powers | Government as trustee of resources and funds |
| Prohibition of *riba* | Interest/usury | 7 powers | Islamic banking, cooperatives, trade financing, agricultural credit, barter, economic zones, grants |
| *Khalifah* | Stewardship/vicegerency | 4 powers | Environment, ancestral domain, natural resources |
| Prohibition of *israf* | Waste/excess | 4 powers | Fisheries (overfishing), environment (degradation), inland waters (conservation), land classification |
| *Hima* | Protected reserve | 4 powers | Environment, ancestral domain, land classification, inland waters |
| Halal compliance | Permissible goods/services | 5 powers | Agriculture, fisheries, tourism, trade/industry, economic zones |
| Prohibition of *gharar* | Excessive uncertainty | 3 powers | Islamic banking, trade, barter |
| *Waqf* | Endowment | 3 powers | Agriculture (*waqf*-CARP conflict), grants (endowments as revenue), cooperatives |
| Prohibition of *darar* | Harm | 2 powers | Environment (*la darar wa la dirar*), inland waters (pollution) |

### Three Integration Models

1. **Mandatory Shari'ah compliance** — Islamic banking only (definitional characteristic)
2. **Optional dual-track** — Cooperatives (conventional and Shari'ah-compliant models at registration)
3. **Principled integration** — All other powers (Shari'ah values inform legislation without mandating specific compliance mechanisms)

## PART E: ERROR LOG WARNINGS FOR THIS CHAPTER

From the fact-check error log, these patterns are most relevant to Ch 13 content:

1. **BOL Article swaps (Error Pattern #1)**: Art. XII = Fiscal Autonomy; Art. XIII = Regional Economy and Patrimony. Do NOT confuse. Art. X = Justice System (Shari'ah courts), NOT Art. IX.
2. **BAA number fabrication (Error Pattern #2)**: Always verify against index. Key: BAA 13 = Administrative Code, BAA 17 = Civil Service Code, BAA 18 = Education Code.

1. **Islamic Finance and Banking** (powers ii, z) — the core: *riba* prohibition, permissible contracts, institutional architecture

7. **Agriculture and Fisheries** (powers c, y) — *muzara'ah*, *waqf*-CARP tension, *salam* financing, halal fishery products
