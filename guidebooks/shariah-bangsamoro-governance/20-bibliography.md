# Bibliography and References

### Primary Legal Sources

**Republic Act No. 11054** (Bangsamoro Organic Law). Signed July 27, 2018; ratified by plebiscite January-February 2019. Establishing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

**Presidential Decree No. 1083** (Code of Muslim Personal Laws of the Philippines). Signed February 4, 1977. Codifying Muslim personal laws and providing for their administration.

**BAA No. 13** (Bangsamoro Administrative Code). Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament. Prescribing the structural, functional, and procedural principles of governance of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

**BAA No. 17** (Bangsamoro Civil Service Code). Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**BAA No. 18** (Bangsamoro Education Code). Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**BAA No. 20** (Dual Use of Hijri and Gregorian Calendars). Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**Republic Act No. 9520** (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008). Congress of the Philippines. Signed February 17, 2009.

**Republic Act No. 10817** (Philippine Halal Export Development and Promotion Act of 2016). Congress of the Philippines.

**1987 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines.** Article X (Local Government), Sections 15-21 (Autonomous Regions).

**OCM Executive Order No. 2, Series of 2026** (Mas Matatag na Bangsamoro Agenda). Office of the Chief Minister, BARMM.

### Citation Standards

Feliciano, Myrna S. *Philippine Manual of Legal Citations.* 10th ed. Quezon City: U.P. Law Complex, 2019.

### Islamic Jurisprudence Sources

**Al-Qur'an.** Sahih International translation. Accessed via quran.com.

**Sahih al-Bukhari.** Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Isma'il al-Bukhari (d. 870 CE / 256 AH). 97 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Sahih Muslim.** Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (d. 875 CE / 261 AH). 57 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Sunan Abu Dawud.** Compiled by Imam Abu Dawud al-Sijistani (d. 889 CE / 275 AH). 43 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Jami' at-Tirmidhi.** Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn 'Isa at-Tirmidhi (d. 892 CE / 279 AH). 49 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Sunan an-Nasa'i.** Compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb an-Nasa'i (d. 915 CE / 303 AH). 51 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Sunan Ibn Majah.** Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Yazid Ibn Majah (d. 887 CE / 273 AH). 38 books. Accessed via sunnah.com.

**Tafsir Ibn Kathir (Abridged).** Imam Isma'il ibn 'Umar ibn Kathir (d. 1373 CE / 774 AH). Classical Qur'anic commentary. Accessed via quran.com.

### Islamic Governance and Jurisprudence

Al-Ghazali, Abu Hamid. *Al-Mustasfa min 'Ilm al-Usul* (The Essentials of the Principles of Jurisprudence). c. 1109 CE.

Al-Shatibi, Abu Ishaq. *Al-Muwafaqat fi Usul al-Shari'ah* (Concordance of the Fundamentals of Shari'ah). c. 1388 CE.
