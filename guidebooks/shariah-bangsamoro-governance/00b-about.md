# About This Guidebook

## Purpose

This guidebook teaches you how to research, legislate, and apply Shari'ah in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). It is a practitioner's guide — written for the people who draft bills, analyze legislation, manage cooperatives, write policy, and advise government agencies.

The Bangsamoro Organic Law recognizes Shari'ah as part of the Bangsamoro justice system. BOL Article X dedicates 20 sections to the Shari'ah court system, the sources of Islamic law, Parliament's legislative power over Shari'ah matters, and the relationship between traditional justice and the formal court system. PD 1083 (the Code of Muslim Personal Laws) provides 190 articles governing marriage, divorce, inheritance, family relations, and property for Muslims. The complete Kutub al-Sittah (the six major Hadith collections) and the Qur'an are publicly accessible in digital form.

These are powerful tools. But they are underused — not because practitioners lack competence, but because no guidebook has shown them how to connect scriptural sources to legislative drafting, how to navigate PD 1083 alongside the BOL, or how to produce Shari'ah legal analysis with the same rigor applied to secular law.

This guidebook fills that gap.

## What This Guidebook Is

- A **research methodology** for finding relevant Qur'anic verses, Hadith narrations, and enacted law on any Shari'ah topic
- A **legislative strategy framework** for deciding how Parliament should legislate Shari'ah — as standalone BAAs, as mainstreamed provisions, or as a hybrid approach
- A **legal analysis toolkit** for producing Shari'ah legal opinions using IRAC methodology grounded in scriptural and enacted sources
- A **compliance guide** for checking draft legislation against Shari'ah principles and constitutional constraints
- A **development practitioner's reference** for Islamic finance, halal economy, and Maqasid al-Shari'ah as a policy framework

## What This Guidebook Is Not

This guidebook does not issue *fatawa* (binding religious rulings). That is the institutional mandate of the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta (BDI), established under BAA 13, Secs. 52-56. The distinction is the same one that applies to all legal work: a legal researcher can analyze constitutional provisions without being a Supreme Court justice. They read, analyze, and advise — but the authoritative ruling comes from the court.

This guidebook produces legal analysis. The BDI issues binding religious rulings. Both are necessary. They serve different functions.

## Intended Audience

- **Legislative staff** — committee analysts, bill drafters, parliamentary researchers
- **MOA legal officers** — ministry counsel, legal division staff
- **Development practitioners** — cooperative organizers, social enterprise consultants, program managers
- **Policy analysts** — PRLS staff, BPDA planners, OOBC consultants
- **Governance consultants** — external advisors working with BARMM entities

## How to Use This Guidebook

**Part I (Foundations)** gives you the constitutional, legal, and philosophical framework. Read it first — everything else builds on it.

**Part II (Researching Shari'ah)** teaches you how to find and analyze primary Islamic sources. Use it when you need to research a Shari'ah topic from scratch.

**Part III (Legislating Shari'ah)** addresses the strategic and practical questions of how Parliament should enact Shari'ah-related legislation. Use it when drafting, reviewing, or planning legislation.

**Part IV (Applying Shari'ah in Development)** covers Islamic finance and halal economy. Use it when your development work has a Shari'ah dimension.

**Part V (Practical Tools)** provides detachable checklists, worksheets, and quick references. Use them on the job.

Each chapter ends with a **Worked Example**, **Common Pitfalls**, and a **Quality Checklist** you can use before submitting your work.

## Acknowledgments

This guidebook draws on the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054), PD 1083 (Code of Muslim Personal Laws), the Bangsamoro Administrative Code (BAA 13), and the 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028. Scriptural citations reference the Qur'an (Sahih International translation) and the Kutub al-Sittah (six major Hadith collections). All legal citations follow the Philippine Manual of Legal Citations (Feliciano, 10th Ed., 2019), with Bangsamoro extensions for Islamic jurisprudence.
