# Chapter 16 — The Practitioner's Toolkit

This chapter provides the detachable tools you need on the job. Print the ones relevant to your work and keep them at your desk. Each tool is designed to be used independently — you do not need to read the preceding chapters to use a checklist, though the chapters provide the reasoning behind each item.

## 16.1 Shari'ah Checkpoint Checklist

Use this when screening any bill, policy, or program for Shari'ah dimensions.

**Does the subject involve any of the following?**

- [ ] Personal law for Muslims (marriage, divorce, inheritance, custody, guardianship)
- [ ] Property relations between Muslims
- [ ] Minor criminal offenses under Shari'ah court jurisdiction (*ta'zir*)
- [ ] Islamic finance (cooperative bylaws, banking, investment, insurance)
- [ ] Halal certification or regulation
- [ ] *Waqf* (endowment) or *zakat* (alms)
- [ ] Islamic education or *madaris*
- [ ] The BDI or religious authorities
- [ ] Any reference to "Shari'ah," "Islamic law," or "Muslim personal law"

**If YES to any:** Proceed with the full compliance review (Chapter 9, Sec. 9.2).

**If NO to all:** Document "No Shari'ah dimension identified" and proceed with the standard process.

## 16.2 Jurisdictional Determination Worksheet

Use this when determining which Shari'ah court has jurisdiction over a case.

**Step 1: Party Check**
- Are all parties Muslim? [ ] Yes [ ] No
- If non-Muslim party: Has voluntary submission been confirmed? [ ] Yes [ ] No [ ] N/A

**Step 2: Subject Matter**
- [ ] Marriage/divorce/mahr/property upon divorce → **Circuit Court** (Art. X, Sec. 5)
- [ ] Custody/guardianship/legitimacy/paternity → **District Court** (Art. X, Sec. 6)
- [ ] Estate settlement → **District Court** (Art. X, Sec. 6)
- [ ] Communal property → **Circuit Court** (Art. X, Sec. 5)
- [ ] *Ta'zir* (*arresto menor*) → **Circuit Court** (Art. X, Sec. 5)
- [ ] Shari'ah-compliant contracts → **District Court** (Art. X, Sec. 6)

**Step 3: Monetary Threshold (civil cases)**
- Real property assessed value:
  - [ ] ≤ P400,000 → **Circuit Court**
  - [ ] > P400,000 → **District Court**
- Other civil claim:
  - [ ] ≤ P200,000 → **Circuit Court**
  - [ ] > P200,000 → **District Court**

**Step 4: Appellate Pathway**
Circuit Court → District Court → High Court → Supreme Court (questions of law only)

**Note:** Verify Shari'ah High Court operational status before planning appellate strategy.

## 16.4 Islamic Finance Instrument Quick Reference

| Instrument | Arabic | How It Works | Status in BARMM |
|-----------|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Mudarabah** | مضاربة | One party provides capital, other provides labor. Profits shared by ratio; losses borne by capital provider. | *Fiqh* concept — no BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |
| **Musharakah** | مشاركة | All parties contribute capital and management. Profits shared per agreement; losses per capital ratio. | *Fiqh* concept — no BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |
| **Murabaha** | مرابحة | Seller discloses cost, adds agreed markup. Buyer knows exact cost and profit. Sale, not loan. | *Fiqh* concept — no BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |
| **Waqf** | وقف | Property dedicated for charitable/religious purposes in perpetuity. Cannot be sold, gifted, or inherited. | *Fiqh* concept — no dedicated BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |
| **Zakat** | زكاة | Obligatory alms — percentage of qualifying wealth. One of the Five Pillars. 8 recipient categories (Qur'an 9:60). | Not in PD 1083. No BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |
| **Sukuk** | صكوك | Islamic bonds — asset-backed certificates. Returns from underlying asset, not interest. | No BAA. Art. XII, Sec. 24 authorizes borrowing. |
| **Takaful** | تكافل | Islamic insurance — mutual risk-sharing. Contributions pooled; surplus returned to participants. | No BAA. Art. X, Sec. 4 authorizes. |

## 16.8 Qur'an and Hadith Citation Format Quick Reference

### Qur'an Format
```
[n] Qur'an, Surah [Name] ([Number]):[Ayah].
    Arabic: "[exact Arabic text]".
    Translation: "[English translation]".
```

**Example:**
```
1 Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2):282.
  Arabic: "يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا تَدَايَنتُم بِدَيْنٍ إِلَىٰ أَجَلٍ مُّسَمًّى فَاكْتُبُوهُ".
  Translation: "O you who believe, when you contract a debt for a specified term, write it down."
```

### Hadith Format
```
[n] [Collection Name], Book [Number], Hadith [Number].
    "[Arabic text]".
    Translation: "[English translation]".
```

**Rule:** General principle in legislative text. Exact source in footnote. Never inline scriptural text in operative provisions.

## 16.9 When to Refer to BDI (Decision Tree)

1. **Is the question answered by enacted law (BOL, PD 1083, BAA)?**
   - Yes → Proceed with legal analysis. No BDI referral needed.
   - No → Continue to question 2.

2. **Is the *fiqh* position well-established and uncontested?**
   - Yes → Proceed with analysis, citing the established position.
   - No → Continue to question 3.

3. **Do the *madhahib* disagree on this point?**
   - No → Proceed with the consensus position.
   - Yes → Continue to question 4.

4. **Does the disagreement materially affect the legal outcome?**
   - No → Present both positions, proceed with analysis.
   - Yes → **Refer to BDI.** Frame the referral with: the specific question, the enacted law context, the scriptural evidence, the *madhhab* positions, and why a ruling is needed.

5. **Does the output create binding policy affecting Muslim religious practice?**
   - Yes → **Refer to BDI** regardless of scholarly consensus.
   - No → Proceed with analysis.

## 16.10 AI in Practice: AI-Assisted Shari'ah Legal Analysis

Artificial intelligence tools can assist with Shari'ah legal research and analysis. When used properly, they accelerate the research process — searching scriptural sources by keyword, cross-referencing Hadith across collections, identifying relevant enacted law provisions, and structuring legal opinions.

**What AI can do well:**
- Search large text collections (Qur'an, 6 Hadith collections, BOL, PD 1083, 89 BAAs) by keyword
- Cross-reference narrations across Hadith collections to identify *muttafaq 'alayh* status
- Structure legal analysis using IRAC methodology
- Format citations per the Feliciano protocol with Bangsamoro extensions
- Identify potential constitutional issues in draft legislation

**What AI cannot do:**
- Issue *fatawa* — binding religious rulings are the BDI's institutional mandate
- Determine the "correct" *madhhab* position when scholars disagree
- Replace human judgment on matters of religious practice
- Guarantee accuracy of scriptural citations — every AI-generated citation must be verified against the source text

**The verification rule:** Every Qur'anic verse and Hadith citation produced by AI must be verified against the source text before inclusion in any official document. AI tools can fabricate plausible-sounding but incorrect citations. The practitioner is responsible for verification.

**Building your source library:** Chapter 6 teaches you how to build a searchable Islamic source library using publicly available digital sources. This library serves as the verification baseline for AI-generated citations and as the primary research tool for manual analysis.
