# Chapter 3 — PD 1083: The Code of Muslim Personal Laws

Presidential Decree No. 1083, signed on February 4, 1977, is the foundational law governing Muslim personal, family, and property relations in the Philippines.[^1] It has 190 articles organized across 5 Books. It predates the BOL by four decades — and it remains in force.

Every practitioner working with Shari'ah in BARMM must understand PD 1083 for one critical reason: **Parliament can only legislate on matters "not provided for" under PD 1083.**[^2] If PD 1083 already covers a subject, PD 1083 governs. Parliament's legislative power under BOL Art. X, Sec. 4 is supplementary — it fills gaps, it does not override.

This chapter maps PD 1083 so you know what is already covered and where Parliament can act.

---

## 3.1 What PD 1083 Covers (5 Books, 190 Articles)

PD 1083 is organized into five Books:

| Book | Title | Articles | Key Subjects |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| **One** | General Provisions | 1-12 | Purpose, construction, definitions, applicability, conflict-of-law rules |
| **Two** | Persons and Family Relations | 13-88 | Marriage, divorce, *mahr* (dower), parental authority, legitimacy, support, Muslim Registry |
| **Three** | Succession | 89-136 | Intestate succession, order of successors, exclusion rules, shares, legacies, debts |
| **Four** | Adjudication and Settlement of Disputes | 137-168 | Shari'ah court jurisdiction, *agama* arbitration council, Jurisconsult of Islamic Law |
| **Five** | Miscellaneous and Transitory Provisions | 169-190 | Applicability rules, repealing clause, effectivity |

**Key structural insight:** Books Two and Three (marriage through inheritance) are the substantive personal law provisions. Book Four establishes the court system and dispute resolution mechanisms. Book Five handles transitions and applicability.[^3]

---

## 3.2 PD 1083 and the BOL: The Supplementary Relationship

The relationship between PD 1083 and the BOL is the single most misunderstood aspect of Shari'ah legislation in BARMM.

BOL Art. X, Sec. 4 grants Parliament power to enact laws on:
- **Personal, family, and property law** jurisdiction
- **Commercial and other civil actions** not provided for under PD 1083
- **Criminal jurisdiction** on minor offenses punishable by *arresto menor* or *ta'zir* equivalent to *arresto menor*[^4]

The operative phrase is **"not provided for under Presidential Decree No. 1083."** This means:

1. **If PD 1083 covers it** — PD 1083 governs. Parliament cannot enact a conflicting BAA on the same subject.
2. **If PD 1083 does not cover it** — Parliament can legislate. This is where the legislative opportunity lies.
3. **Commercial and civil matters** — Parliament has explicit power here, because PD 1083 focuses on personal law, not commercial law.

**What PD 1083 covers** (Parliament cannot override):
- Marriage requirements and formalities (Art. 13-26)
- Effects of marriage, rights of spouses (Art. 27-44)
- Divorce: by *talaq*, *khul'*, *faskh*, and *li'an* (Art. 45-54)
- *Iddah* (waiting period), *mahr* (dower) (Art. 55-61)
- Parental authority, legitimacy, support (Art. 62-83)
- Succession and inheritance rules (Art. 89-136)

**What PD 1083 does NOT cover** (Parliament can legislate):
- Islamic finance instruments (*mudarabah*, *musharakah*, *murabaha*)
- *Waqf* (Islamic endowment) — beyond basic property provisions
- *Zakat* (obligatory alms) administration
- Halal regulation and certification
- Islamic banking and insurance (*takaful*)
- Criminal offenses beyond *ta'zir* equivalent to *arresto menor*

## 3.3 Navigating PD 1083 by Subject

When a Shari'ah question arises, use this quick-reference to find the relevant PD 1083 provisions:

**Marriage:**
| Question | PD 1083 Provision |
|----------|------------------|
| What are the requirements for a valid Muslim marriage? | Art. 13-16 (requisites, capacity, consent, solemnization) |
| Who can perform a Muslim marriage? | Art. 17-18 (solemnizing officers) |
| What are the effects of marriage? | Art. 27-44 (property, support, obligations) |
| What is *mahr* (dower)? | Art. 55-61 |

**Divorce:**
| Question | PD 1083 Provision |
|----------|------------------|
| What kinds of divorce are recognized? | Art. 45 (*talaq*), Art. 46 (*khul'*), Art. 47-54 (judicial decree) |
| What is the *iddah* waiting period? | Art. 55-57 |
| What are the effects of divorce? | Art. 54 |

**Inheritance:**
| Question | PD 1083 Provision |
|----------|------------------|
| Who inherits under Muslim law? | Art. 89-109 (classes of heirs) |

| What is the *agama* arbitration council? | Art. 157-163 |
| Who is the Jurisconsult of Islamic Law? | Art. 164-168 |

## 3.4 When PD 1083 Governs vs. When Parliament Can Legislate

**The decision tree:**

1. **Identify the subject matter.** Is it personal law (marriage, divorce, inheritance, family relations, property between spouses)? Or is it commercial/civil?

2. **If personal law:** Check PD 1083. If PD 1083 has provisions on this subject, PD 1083 governs. Parliament cannot enact a conflicting BAA.

3. **If commercial/civil not in PD 1083:** Parliament can legislate under BOL Art. X, Sec. 4.

4. **If criminal (*ta'zir*):** Parliament can legislate, but the offense must be punishable by *arresto menor* or its equivalent — and fines must be commensurate to the offense.[^5]

5. **If the subject falls in a gap** — PD 1083 touches the area but does not fully address it — Parliament may enact supplementary legislation. The key is that the new BAA must not conflict with existing PD 1083 provisions.

**Example:** PD 1083 covers succession and inheritance. But it does not comprehensively address *waqf* (Islamic endowment) as a separate institution. Parliament could enact a "Bangsamoro Waqf Act" to establish a waqf registry, administration framework, and regulatory body — as long as it does not conflict with PD 1083's property and succession provisions.

## 3.5 Worked Example

> **Scenario:** A Member of Parliament wants to file a bill requiring mandatory pre-marriage counseling for all Muslim marriages in BARMM.
>
> **Analysis using this chapter:**
>
> 1. **Subject identification:** This is personal law — marriage.
>
> 2. **PD 1083 check:** PD 1083, Art. 13-26, covers marriage requirements and formalities. Does it require pre-marriage counseling? No — it specifies requisites (capacity, consent, *mahr*), solemnization, and registration, but does not address counseling.
>
> 3. **Supplementary or conflicting?** A mandatory counseling requirement does not conflict with PD 1083's existing requirements — it adds a new procedural step that PD 1083 does not address.
>
> 4. **Conclusion:** Parliament can legislate on this under BOL Art. X, Sec. 4 (personal and family law jurisdiction). The bill is supplementary to PD 1083, not conflicting. However, the bill should explicitly state that it applies "in addition to the requirements under Presidential Decree No. 1083" to make the supplementary relationship clear.
>
> 5. **Constitutional check (Chapter 2):** Since the bill applies only to Muslim marriages, the "exclusively Muslim" scope is satisfied. No voluntary submission issue arises.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Saying PD 1083 has "4 Books."** It has **5 Books**. Book Five (Miscellaneous and Transitory Provisions) is often overlooked but contains important applicability rules.
- **Assuming Parliament can amend PD 1083.** Parliament cannot amend a national law. It can enact supplementary BAAs on matters not provided for — but it cannot change PD 1083's text. Only Congress can amend PD 1083.
- **Confusing PD 1083's court jurisdiction with BOL Art. X.** PD 1083, Book Four establishes the original Shari'ah court jurisdiction. BOL Art. X, Secs. 5-7 update and expand it. When the two overlap, check which is more recent and more specific.
- **Overlooking Art. 3(3).** PD 1083, Art. 3(3) states: "The provisions of this Code shall be applicable only to Muslims and nothing herein shall be construed to operate to the prejudice of a non-Muslim." This mirrors BOL Art. X, Sec. 1 — both establish the "exclusively Muslim" boundary.
- **Presenting PD 1083 as outdated.** PD 1083 was enacted in 1977, but it remains good law. The BOL supplements it — it does not replace it.

## Quality Checklist

Before submitting any work product that involves PD 1083:

- [ ] Have you identified the specific PD 1083 article(s) that apply?
- [ ] Have you determined whether the subject is "provided for" under PD 1083?
- [ ] If drafting a bill, have you confirmed it supplements rather than conflicts with PD 1083?
- [ ] Have you cited the correct Book and Title — not just the article number?
- [ ] Have you checked whether BOL Art. X, Secs. 5-7 update PD 1083's court jurisdiction provisions?
- [ ] Have you used "5 Books" — not "4 Books"?
