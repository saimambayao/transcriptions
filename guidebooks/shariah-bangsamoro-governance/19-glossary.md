# Glossary of Shari'ah and Islamic Legal Terms

***'Aqidah*** — Islamic creed; the foundational beliefs of Islam (oneness of God, prophethood, afterlife). Matters of *'aqidah* are outside the scope of legislative analysis.

***Agama* Arbitration Council** — A mediation body established under PD 1083 (Art. 157-163) to settle family disputes before they reach Shari'ah courts.

***Arresto Menor*** — A penalty under Philippine law for minor offenses, with imprisonment of one day to thirty days. BOL Art. X, Sec. 4 limits Parliament's *ta'zir* criminal jurisdiction to offenses equivalent to *arresto menor*.

**BAA** — Bangsamoro Autonomy Act. Regional legislation enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament. As of February 2026, 89 BAAs have been enacted.

**BARMM** — Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. Established by the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054), ratified by plebiscite in January-February 2019.

**BDI** — Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta. The consultative body for policy-making through Islamic jurisprudence in BARMM. Established under BAA 13, Book III, Title III, Chapter 1, Section G, Secs. 52-56. Headed by the Bangsamoro Mufti.

**BOL** — Bangsamoro Organic Law. Republic Act No. 11054, signed July 27, 2018. The foundational law establishing BARMM.

**BTA** — Bangsamoro Transition Authority. The interim government of BARMM pending elections.

***Dalil*** — Evidence or proof. In Islamic jurisprudence, the scriptural or rational basis for a legal ruling.

***Daruriyyat*** — Necessities. The highest level of the Maqasid hierarchy — what is essential for survival and social order.

***Faskh*** — Judicial dissolution of marriage. One of the forms of divorce recognized under PD 1083 (Art. 47-54).

***Fatwa*** (pl. *fatawa*) — A binding religious ruling issued by a qualified Islamic legal scholar (*mufti*) in response to a specific question. In BARMM, the BDI is the institutional authority for issuing *fatawa*.

***Fiqh*** — Islamic jurisprudence. The human understanding and application of Shari'ah, developed through centuries of scholarly tradition. Distinct from Shari'ah itself (divine law).

***Hajiyyat*** — Needs. The second level of the Maqasid hierarchy — what removes hardship and facilitates life.

***Hifz al-'Aql*** — Protection of intellect. One of the five Maqasid objectives.

***Hifz al-Din*** — Protection of faith. One of the five Maqasid objectives.

***Hifz al-Mal*** — Protection of property. One of the five Maqasid objectives.

***Hifz al-Nafs*** — Protection of life. One of the five Maqasid objectives.

***Hifz al-Nasl*** — Protection of lineage. One of the five Maqasid objectives.

***'Iddah*** — The waiting period following divorce or the death of a husband, during which a woman may not remarry. Governed by PD 1083, Art. 55-57.

***Ijma'*** — Scholarly consensus. One of the four sources of Shari'ah recognized by BOL Art. X, Sec. 3.

***Ikhtilaf*** — Scholarly disagreement. When the schools of jurisprudence hold different positions on a legal question.

***Istidlal*** — Legal reasoning or argumentation. The process of deriving legal rulings from scriptural and rational evidence.

***Khalifah*** — Stewardship. The concept that humans are trustees of God's creation, with responsibility for its preservation and just management.

***Khul'*** — Divorce initiated by the wife, typically involving return of the *mahr* (dower). Recognized under PD 1083 (Art. 46).

***Kutub al-Sittah*** — The Six Books. The six most authoritative Hadith collections in Sunni Islam: Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Sunan Abu Dawud, Jami' at-Tirmidhi, Sunan an-Nasa'i, and Sunan Ibn Majah.

***Li'an*** — Mutual imprecation. A form of divorce involving sworn accusations of adultery between spouses. Recognized under PD 1083.

***Madhhab*** (pl. *madhahib*) — School of Islamic jurisprudence. The four Sunni schools are Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali. PD 1083 predominantly reflects Hanafi and Shafi'i traditions.

***Mahr*** — Dower. A mandatory gift from the groom to the bride in a Muslim marriage. Governed by PD 1083, Art. 55-61.

**Maqasid al-Shari'ah** — The higher objectives or purposes of Islamic law. Five objectives: protection of faith, life, intellect, lineage, and property. A scholarly framework, not enacted law.

***Mudarabah*** — Profit-sharing partnership. One party provides capital, the other provides labor. A *fiqh* concept not yet codified by any BARMM BAA.

***Mufti*** — A qualified Islamic legal scholar authorized to issue *fatawa*. The Bangsamoro Mufti heads the BDI.

***Murabaha*** — Cost-plus financing. The seller discloses cost and adds an agreed markup. A *fiqh* concept not yet codified by any BARMM BAA.

***Musharakah*** — Joint venture. All parties contribute capital and share management, profits, and losses. A *fiqh* concept not yet codified by any BARMM BAA.

***Muttafaq 'Alayh*** — Agreed upon. A Hadith narration found in both Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim, carrying the highest Hadith authority.

***Nawazil*** — Novel circumstances. New situations not directly addressed by classical Islamic scholarship, requiring fresh juristic reasoning.

**PD 1083** — Presidential Decree No. 1083, the Code of Muslim Personal Laws of the Philippines. Signed February 4, 1977. Contains 190 articles across 5 Books governing Muslim personal, family, and property law.

***Qiyas*** — Analogical reasoning. One of the four sources of Shari'ah recognized by BOL Art. X, Sec. 3.

***Riba*** — Interest or usury. Prohibited in Islamic finance. The prohibition is grounded in Qur'anic verses (notably Surah Al-Baqarah 2:275) and Hadith narrations.

***Sadaqah*** — Voluntary charity, as distinguished from *zakat* (obligatory charity).

***Sukuk*** — Islamic bonds. Asset-backed certificates that provide returns based on an underlying asset rather than interest payments. A *fiqh* concept; no BARMM BAA codifies it.

***Sunnah*** — The traditions, sayings, and practices of Prophet Muhammad (SAW). One of the two principal sources of Shari'ah recognized by BOL Art. X, Sec. 3. Preserved in Hadith collections.

***Tabarru'*** — Charitable donation. Used in Islamic social finance. Referenced in PB 379.

***Tahsiniyyat*** — Enhancements. The third level of the Maqasid hierarchy — what improves quality of life.

***Takaful*** — Islamic insurance. Mutual risk-sharing arrangement among participants. A *fiqh* concept not yet codified by any BARMM BAA.

***Talaq*** — Divorce initiated by the husband. Recognized under PD 1083 (Art. 45).

***Ta'zir*** — Discretionary punishment for offenses not specified in the Qur'an or Sunnah. Under BOL Art. X, Sec. 4, Parliament's criminal jurisdiction is limited to *ta'zir* equivalent to *arresto menor*.

***'Ulama*** (sing. *'alim*) — Islamic scholars. Those with formal training in Islamic sciences and jurisprudence.

***Waqf*** — Islamic endowment. Property dedicated for charitable or religious purposes in perpetuity. PD 1083 includes basic property provisions; no comprehensive BARMM BAA exists.

***Zakat*** — Obligatory alms. One of the Five Pillars of Islam, calculated as a percentage of qualifying wealth. Distributed to eight categories of recipients specified in the Qur'an (Surah At-Tawbah 9:60). No BARMM BAA codifies zakat administration.
