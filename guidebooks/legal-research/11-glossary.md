# Glossary of Legal Research Terms

**Administrative Code** — BAA No. 13, the Bangsamoro Administrative Code. Prescribes the structural, functional, and procedural principles of the Bangsamoro Government. Establishes all ministries, the OCM, and attached agencies. See Tier 3 in the legal hierarchy.

**BAA** — Bangsamoro Autonomy Act. Regional legislation enacted by the BTA Parliament on matters within the Bangsamoro Government's legislative authority. As of February 2026, 89 BAAs have been enacted.

**BARMM** — Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. The political entity established by Republic Act No. 11054 (the BOL).

**Block Grant** — The annual fiscal transfer from the National Government to the Bangsamoro Government, equivalent to 5% of net national internal revenue. Governed by BOL Article XII, Sections 15-16 (not Section 9).

**BOL** — Bangsamoro Organic Law. Republic Act No. 11054, signed July 27, 2018, ratified by plebiscite January-February 2019. The foundational law creating BARMM and defining its powers, structure, and fiscal autonomy.

**BTA** — Bangsamoro Transition Authority. The interim government exercising both legislative and executive powers during the transition period.

**Chain Trace** — See Provision Chain Trace.

**Comparative Matrix** — A structured table comparing how different legal sources treat the same subject. Two types: (1) tier-by-tier matrix comparing provisions across the legal hierarchy, and (2) verbatim section-by-section matrix comparing multiple bills or laws provision by provision. See Chapter 2, Section 2.4.3.

**National Law Consistency Required** — See entry under "Power Requiring National Law Consistency."

**Creating Law** — The specific legislation that established an MOA and defined its initial mandate, structure, and functions. May be a BOL provision, a provision of BAA 13, or a standalone BAA.

**Enumerated Powers** — The 55 subjects listed in BOL Article V, Section 2, over which the Bangsamoro Government exercises authority. Where the BOL does not require consistency with national law, these powers are exercised without national law constraint.

**Enumerated Power (No National Law Consistency Required)** — A subject within the 55 enumerated powers (Art. V, Sec. 2) where the BOL does not require consistency with national law. In these areas, BAAs govern and national laws generally do not apply. See Chapter 3, Section 3.2.1.

**Explicit Authority** — Authority directly stated in enacted legislation. Example: BAA 13, Book IV, Sec. 5 explicitly creates OOBC.

**Feliciano Format** — The citation standard from the Philippine Manual of Legal Citations (Feliciano, Myrna S., 10th ed., Quezon City: U.P. Law Complex, 2019). The mandatory citation format for all legal research outputs in the Bangsamoro Government.

**General Welfare Clause** — BOL Article V, Section 3. Extends the Bangsamoro Government's authority beyond the 55 enumerated powers to include "powers expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and effective governance."

**Good Law** — A provision that is currently in force, has not been repealed or amended, and has not been declared unconstitutional. Confirming good law is the final verification step. See Chapter 13, Section 13.2.

**Ijma** — Consensus of Islamic scholars. One of the secondary sources of Shari'ah recognized by BOL Article X, Section 3.

**Implied Authority** — Authority not directly stated in legislation but reasoned from an explicit grant through the general welfare clause (Art. V, Sec. 3). Must be documented with a chain of reasoning linking the implied function to the express grant.

**IRAC** — Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion. The standard structure for legal analysis memos. See Chapter 12, Section 12.2.2.

**IRR** — Implementing Rules and Regulations. Detailed implementation guidelines for specific laws, issued by the implementing agency.

**Jurisprudence** — Supreme Court decisions interpreting legal provisions. Tier 7 in the BARMM legal hierarchy. Provides binding interpretation of all other sources.

**Legal Mandate Matrix** — A structured document mapping every legal provision granting authority to a specific MOA. Used during strategic planning. See Chapter 12, Section 12.2.4.

**Legislative Gap** — A situation where the BOL grants an enumerated power to the Bangsamoro Government, but Parliament has not enacted a BAA to exercise it. National laws may apply suppletorily until Parliament acts.

**Lex Posterior** — The principle that later legislation prevails over earlier legislation on the same subject. See Chapter 3, Conflict Resolution Rule 5.

**Lex Specialis** — The principle that specific legislation prevails over general legislation on overlapping topics. See Chapter 3, Conflict Resolution Rule 6.

**Maqasid al-Shari'ah** — The higher objectives of Islamic law: protection of faith (din), life (nafs), intellect (aql), lineage (nasl), and property (mal). A framework for assessing whether legislation aligns with Islamic principles. See Chapter 9, Section 9.6.

**MOA** — Ministries, Offices, and Agencies. The collective term for all executive entities of the Bangsamoro Government.

**Nested Sovereignty** — The model describing BARMM's unique constitutional position: the region exercises culturally-specific sovereignty that is nested within, and ultimately subordinate to, the national sovereignty of the Philippine Republic. See Chapter 9, Section 9.7.

**OOBC** — Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities. An attached office under the OCM created by BAA 13, Book IV, Sections 5-6. Mandated to provide assistance to Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM.

**National Law Relationship** — The three-step test that determines which tier of the legal hierarchy controls for a given subject: enumerated power (no national law consistency required), power requiring national law consistency, or residual national power. See Chapter 3, Section 3.3.

**PRLS** — Parliamentary Research and Legislative Support. The Parliament's institutional research arm.

**Provision Chain Trace** — The technique of following what one legal provision references until all related authority is found. Step 4 of the five-step research methodology. See Chapter 11.

**Qiyas** — Analogical reasoning. One of the secondary sources of Shari'ah recognized by BOL Article X, Section 3.

**Research Memorandum** — The standard output of legal research: a comprehensive inventory of all applicable legal authority organized by tier, with verbatim provisions and verification status. See Chapter 12, Section 12.2.1.

**Residual National Power** — A subject not granted to the Bangsamoro Government by the BOL. Under Article V, Section 1, all residual powers are vested in the National Government. See Chapter 3, Section 3.2.3.

**Search Record** — The documented record of how a research task was conducted: keywords used, tiers searched, sources consulted, date, and researcher. Provides an audit trail for reproducibility. See Chapter 2, Section 2.7.

**SDF** — Special Development Fund. PHP 5 billion per year for 10 years, provided by the National Government. Governed by BOL Article XIV, Section 2 (not Article XII, Section 10).

**Power Requiring National Law Consistency** — A subject where both the Bangsamoro Government and the National Government exercise authority, and the BOL requires consistency with national law. Examples: civil service (Art. VII, Sec. 39), budget (Art. VII, Sec. 28), elections (Art. IV, Sec. 4). See Chapter 3, Section 3.2.2.

**Shari'ah** — Islamic law. In the BARMM context, a parallel legal dimension that intersects all tiers of the hierarchy. Applies exclusively to cases involving Muslims (unless a non-Muslim voluntarily submits). See Chapter 9.

**Suppletory Application** — When a national law fills a gap where BARMM legislation is silent. The national law applies until Parliament enacts a BAA on the subject.

**Ta'zir** — Discretionary punishment in Islamic law. Under BOL Art. X, Sec. 4, Parliament can legislate on criminal offenses punishable by *arresto menor* or *ta'zir* equivalent to *arresto menor*. Hard limit: Parliament cannot expand Shari'ah criminal jurisdiction beyond minor offenses.

**Ultra Vires** — Beyond the authority. An act or provision that exceeds the legal authority of the entity that issued it. An executive issuance that contradicts its enabling BAA is *ultra vires*.

**Verbatim** — Word-for-word. Legal provisions must be quoted verbatim from source text, not paraphrased from memory. See Chapter 13, Section 13.3.
