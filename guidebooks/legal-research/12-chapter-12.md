# Chapter 12 — Producing Legal Research Outputs

Your research is complete. You have searched all seven tiers, traced the provision chains, built a Comparative Matrix, and verified every citation. Now you need to deliver. The person who asked the question — an MP, a Minister, a committee chair, a planning officer — needs your findings in a format they can act on.

This chapter covers the six standard output formats for legal research in the Bangsamoro Government. Each serves a different purpose and audience. The first — the Research Memorandum — is the foundation that feeds all others.

---

## 12.1 Purpose

Legal research has no value until it reaches a decision-maker in a usable format. A researcher who finds every applicable provision but delivers them as an unstructured list has not completed the job. The output format determines whether your research actually informs the decision.

Six formats exist because six different situations require six different structures. A committee chair preparing for a hearing needs a different format than a planning officer building a strategic plan. An MP deciding whether to sponsor a bill needs a different format than a legal officer reviewing a contract. Match the format to the need.

---

## 12.2 The Six Standard Output Formats

### 12.2.1 Research Memorandum

**Purpose:** The raw research output — a comprehensive inventory of all applicable legal authority organized by tier.

**Audience:** The researcher (as working document), downstream consumers (analysts, drafters, reviewers).

**When to use:** After every research task. This is the default output.

**Structure:**

```markdown
## Legal Research Memorandum

**Question:** [The formulated research question from Step 1]
**Jurisdiction:** [BARMM / National / Both]
**National Law Relationship:** [Enumerated (no consistency required) / National law consistency required / Residual national power / Gap]
**Date:** [Date completed]
**Researcher:** [Name and position]

### Applicable Authorities

| # | Tier | Source | Provision | Authority/Mandate | Actor | Status |
|---|------|--------|-----------|-------------------|-------|--------|
| 1 | 1 | Constitution | Art. X, Sec. 15 | Constitutional basis for autonomous regions | Congress | In force |
| 2 | 2 | BOL (RA 11054) | Art. V, Sec. 2(v) | Education and skills training — enumerated power | BG | In force |

### Key Provisions (Verbatim)

**[Citation]:**
> [Exact verbatim text copied from source]
— Source: [file path or document reference]

### Provision Chain Map

| Source Provision | Traceable Concept | Related Provisions Found | Relevance |
|-----------------|-------------------|------------------------|-----------|

### Comparative Matrix

[If multiple tiers address the same subject — include the tier-by-tier matrix from Chapter 2]

### Conflict Analysis
[Any conflicts between tiers, with resolution per national law relationship test]

### Gaps Identified
[Areas where authority is implied but not explicit, or where no authority exists]

### Search Record
[Keywords, tiers searched, sources consulted, date, researcher]
```

**Rules:**
- Every claim in the Applicable Authorities table must have a source path
- Verbatim text must be copied from the source, not paraphrased
- Status must be verified (in force / amended / repealed)
- The Search Record must be complete and honest

### 12.2.2 Legal Analysis Memo (IRAC)

**Purpose:** A structured legal argument that answers a specific question with a conclusion.

**Audience:** MPs, Ministers, legal officers who need an answer, not just an inventory.

**When to use:** When the research question requires interpretation — not just "what does the law say?" but "what does it mean for this situation?"

**Structure (IRAC):**

```markdown
## Legal Analysis Memorandum

**Question:** [Specific legal question]
**Date:** [Date]
**Prepared for:** [Recipient]
**Prepared by:** [Name and position]

### I. Issue
[One paragraph framing the legal issue precisely]

### II. Rule
[The applicable legal provisions, organized by tier, with verbatim quotations.
This section draws directly from the Research Memorandum.]

### III. Application
[Apply the rules to the specific facts. This is where legal reasoning happens —
how do the provisions interact with the particular situation?]

### IV. Conclusion
[Clear answer to the question. State the legal basis for the conclusion.
If the answer is uncertain, state the uncertainty and what additional
information would resolve it.]

### V. Recommendation (optional)
[If the recipient asked for a recommendation, provide one.
Distinguish between the legal conclusion and the policy recommendation.]
```

**The difference from a Research Memorandum:** The memorandum says "here are all the applicable laws." The IRAC memo says "here is what they mean for your specific question, and here is my conclusion." The memorandum is descriptive. The IRAC memo is analytical.

### 12.2.4 Legal Mandate Matrix

**Purpose:** Maps every legal provision granting authority to a specific MOA.

**Audience:** Planning officers, legal officers, Ministers, Executive Directors.

**When to use:** During strategic planning (Chapter 2 of the Strategic Planning Guidebook), mandate verification, or institutional assessment.

**Structure:**

<table>
<colgroup><col style="width:20%"><col style="width:40%"><col style="width:40%"></colgroup>
<thead><tr><th>Legal Basis</th><th>Authority Granted</th><th>Justification</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td>[Full citation: law, article, section]</td><td>[Verbatim text of what the provision authorizes or requires]</td><td>[Why this provision is relevant to this MOA: direct basis, implied from Art. V Sec. 3, national law suppletory, etc.]</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

This format comes from the Strategic Planning Guidebook, Chapter 2, Section 2.7.[^2] The three-column structure (Legal Basis, Authority Granted, Justification) is simpler than the Research Memorandum because it serves a different purpose — not comprehensive research, but institutional mandate mapping.

**Gap documentation:** The matrix should include two types of gaps:
- **Type A:** Functions the MOA currently performs without clear legal basis
- **Type B:** Mandates in the law that the MOA is not currently addressing

### 12.2.5 Legal Opinion

**Purpose:** Formal interpretation of a legal question with a recommendation, issued by a legal officer or the Attorney-General's Office.

**Audience:** The requesting official or institution.

**When to use:** When an institutional decision requires formal legal guidance — not just research or analysis, but an authoritative interpretation that the institution can rely on.

**Structure:**

```markdown
## Legal Opinion

**Opinion No.:** [Sequential number per issuing office]
**Date:** [Date issued]
**Requested by:** [Name and position of requester]
**Issued by:** [Name and position of legal officer]
**Subject:** [Brief description of the legal question]

### Statement of Facts
[The factual background that prompted the request]

### Legal Issue(s)
[The specific legal question(s) to be resolved]

### Discussion
[Analysis of applicable legal provisions, organized by tier.
Application of provisions to the facts.
Resolution of any conflicts between provisions.]

### Opinion
[The legal officer's formal interpretation.
Clear, unambiguous statement of what the law requires, permits, or prohibits.]

### Recommendation
[If applicable — what the requesting official should do based on this opinion]

### Caveats
[Any limitations on this opinion — e.g., "This opinion is based on the laws
in force as of [date]. Any subsequent legislation may alter the analysis."]
```

**The Legal Opinion is the most authoritative research output.** It carries institutional weight. It can be cited in official proceedings. It can be relied upon for decision-making. Because of this, it demands the highest standard of research — every provision must be verified, every citation must be accurate, and every conclusion must be supported by the legal text.

### 12.2.6 Legislative Research Briefer

**Purpose:** Captures the legal and policy research foundation for a bill being drafted. Gives the MP a complete picture of the legal terrain before reviewing the first draft.

**Audience:** The sponsoring MP, the drafter, committee members.

**When to use:** During Stage 2 of the bill drafting process (Bill Drafting Guidebook, Chapter 2, Section 2.3.4).

**Structure (from the Bill Drafting Guidebook):**

```markdown
## Legislative Research Briefer

**Bill:** [Working title]
**Prepared for:** [MP name]
**Date:** [Date]
**Prepared by:** [Name]

### 1. Brief Description of the Proposed Measure
### 2. Related Laws and Issuances
### 3. Policy and Legal Analysis
### 4. Impact Assessment
### 5. Shari'ah and Gender Considerations
### 6. Recommendations
```

The Legislative Research Briefer is a specialized output format for the bill drafting context. It combines elements of the Research Memorandum (Section 2: related laws) and the IRAC Memo (Section 3: analysis) into a format designed for MP consumption.[^3]

## 12.3 The Pipeline: Research Memorandum Feeds Everything

The Research Memorandum is not just one of six outputs — it is the **source** for all other outputs. The pipeline flows in one direction:

```
Research Memorandum
    ├── → Legal Analysis Memo (add interpretation + conclusion)
    ├── → Comparative Matrix (extract from the tier-by-tier comparison)
    ├── → Legal Mandate Matrix (filter to a specific MOA's provisions)
    ├── → Legal Opinion (add formal interpretation + recommendation)
    └── → Legislative Research Briefer (reformat for MP consumption)
```

**Never produce an IRAC Memo, a Legal Opinion, or a Mandate Matrix without first producing a Research Memorandum.** The downstream formats are only as reliable as the underlying research. If the memorandum is incomplete, every downstream product inherits the gaps.

## 12.4 Citation Format: Feliciano 10th Edition

All legal research outputs in the Bangsamoro Government use the **Philippine Manual of Legal Citations** (Feliciano, Myrna S., 10th edition, Quezon City: U.P. Law Complex, 2019) as the citation standard.

**Key formats:**

| Source Type | Format | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Republic Act | `Rep. Act No. [N], sec. [S], art. [A].` | Rep. Act No. 11054, sec. 2, art. V. |
| BAA | `BAA No. [N], sec. [S].` | BAA No. 13, sec. 5, Book IV. |
| Resolution | `Adopted Res. No. [N], BTA Parliament.` | Adopted Res. No. 268, BTA Parliament. |
| Constitution | `Const. art. [A], sec. [S].` | Const. art. X, sec. 15. |
| Subsequent reference | `*Id.*, sec. [S].` | *Id.*, sec. 3. |
| Book | `[Author], *[Title]*, [ed.] ([Place: Publisher, Year]), [page].` | Feliciano, Myrna S., *Philippine Manual of Legal Citations*, 10th ed. (Quezon City: U.P. Law Complex, 2019), Rule 4.6, p. 19. |

**Rules:**
- Every factual claim must have a footnote citation
- Verbatim quotations must cite the exact section and article
- Subsequent references to the same source use *Id.* (italicized)
- When citing the same source but a different section: *Id.*, sec. [new section]

## 12.5 The Seven Never-Fabricate Rules

These rules apply to every output format. Violating any of them produces unreliable research that can damage institutional credibility.

1. **Never expand an acronym** without reading the source document. "MBHTE" is "Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education" — not "Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Tertiary Education."

2. **Never cite a statistic** without a source file path. "The poverty rate is 29.80%" must cite PSA data with the year and source.

3. **Never cite a BOL article** without verifying against the verbatim text. The BOL has 18 articles, not 13. Art. IX is Basic Rights, not Shari'ah. Art. X is Justice System, not Basic Rights.

4. **Never cite a BAA number** without verifying against the index. BAA 25 is a hospital upgrade, not the Education Code. BAA 60 is a memorial site, not the BEZA Act. Any BAA > 89 does not exist as of February 2026.

5. **Never invent a program name** — if unsure, write `[verify: name]` and check.

6. **Never present data without temporal context** — always include the year and source.

7. **If a claim is not in your Research Memorandum, do not include it in a downstream output.** The memorandum is the single source of truth. If you discover a new claim while writing an IRAC memo, add it to the memorandum first, verify it, then include it.

## 12.6 Worked Example

**Research question:** *What is the legal basis for BARMM's authority over cooperatives?*

### Research Memorandum (excerpt)

| # | Tier | Source | Provision | Authority/Mandate | Status |
|---|------|--------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | 2 | BOL | Art. V, Sec. 2(k) | "Cooperatives and social entrepreneurship" — enumerated power (no national law consistency required) | In force |
| 2 | 2 | BOL | Art. V, Sec. 3 | General welfare clause — implied powers for cooperative regulation | In force |
| 3 | 2 | BOL | Art. XIII, Sec. 24 | "The Bangsamoro Government shall create a favorable environment for cooperatives" | In force |
| 4 | 3 | BAA 13 | MTIT mandate | Trade, investments, and tourism — cooperatives may fall under MTIT | In force |
| 5 | 5 | RA 9520 | Philippine Cooperative Code | National cooperative framework — applies suppletorily (no BAA enacted) | In force |
| 6 | 5 | RA 6938 | Original Cooperative Code (1990) | Superseded by RA 9520 (2008) | Repealed |

**National law relationship:** Enumerated power (BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(k)), no national law consistency required. **Legislative gap** — no BAA specifically codifying cooperative law.

### Same Question as IRAC Memo (excerpt)

**Issue:** Does the Bangsamoro Government have enumerated authority to regulate cooperatives, and what law currently governs cooperatives within BARMM?

**Rule:** BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(k) grants enumerated power over "cooperatives and social entrepreneurship" (no national law consistency required). Art. XIII, Sec. 24 mandates a "favorable environment for cooperatives."

**Application:** The BOL does not require national law consistency for cooperatives. However, Parliament has not enacted a Bangsamoro Cooperative Code. In the absence of a BAA, RA 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code) applies suppletorily.

**Conclusion:** BARMM has enumerated authority over cooperatives (no national law consistency required) but has not yet exercised it through legislation. RA 9520 governs suppletorily. A future Bangsamoro Cooperative Code would supersede RA 9520 within BARMM.

### Same Question as Legal Mandate Matrix (for MTIT)

| Legal Basis | Authority Granted | Justification |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| BOL Art. V, Sec. 2(k) | Cooperatives and social entrepreneurship — enumerated power | Direct basis — enumerated Bangsamoro power (no national law consistency required) |
| BOL Art. XIII, Sec. 24 | Create favorable environment for cooperatives | Direct basis — affirmative mandate |
| BAA 13, MTIT sections | Trade, investments, tourism mandate | MTIT may cover cooperative regulation pending sector BAA |
| RA 9520 | Philippine Cooperative Code | Suppletory — applies until Parliament enacts a Bangsamoro Cooperative Code |

## 12.7 Common Pitfalls

**Pitfall 1: Producing a downstream output without a Research Memorandum.** An IRAC memo written directly from memory — without first compiling a verified Research Memorandum — will have gaps. Always produce the memorandum first.

**Pitfall 2: Using the wrong format for the audience.** An MP does not need a Research Memorandum — they need an IRAC Memo or a Legislative Research Briefer. A planning officer does not need an IRAC Memo — they need a Legal Mandate Matrix. Match the format to the recipient.

**Pitfall 3: Citing provisions in downstream outputs that are not in the memorandum.** If a provision appears in your IRAC memo but not in your Research Memorandum, it was not researched and verified. Add it to the memorandum first.

**Pitfall 4: Omitting the Search Record.** The Search Record is not bureaucratic overhead — it is the audit trail that proves your research was thorough. Without it, no one can verify that you searched all relevant tiers.

**Pitfall 5: Confusing the Research Memorandum with the IRAC Memo.** The memorandum lists authority. The memo interprets it. Do not blend the two. Keep the descriptive (memorandum) separate from the analytical (memo).

## 12.8 Quality Checklist

Before delivering any research output, confirm:

- [ ] A Research Memorandum has been produced as the foundation
- [ ] The output format matches the audience and purpose
- [ ] Every provision cited in the output appears in the Research Memorandum (verified)
- [ ] All citations follow Feliciano 10th Edition format
- [ ] The Seven Never-Fabricate Rules have been applied
- [ ] Verbatim text is copied from source, not paraphrased
- [ ] The Search Record is complete and attached (or referenced)
- [ ] The Shari'ah dimension has been addressed (included or confirmed absent)
- [ ] The national law relationship is documented (Enumerated — no consistency required / National law consistency required / Residual national power / Gap)
- [ ] All Comparative Matrix conflicts have been resolved with the national law relationship test
