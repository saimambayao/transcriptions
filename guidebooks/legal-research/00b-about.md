# About This Guidebook

## Purpose

This guidebook teaches you how to conduct systematic legal research within the Bangsamoro legal system. Whether you are preparing a legislative briefer, building a legal mandate matrix, drafting a bill, or analyzing a policy proposal, the quality of your output depends on the quality of your research. This guidebook gives you the methodology to get it right.

## Who This Guidebook Is For

- **Parliament staff** — legislative researchers at the Parliamentary Research and Legislative Support (PRLS), committee secretariat officers, and legislative staff in MP offices who prepare research memoranda, briefers, and legal analyses
- **MOA legal units** — legal officers and planning staff in Ministries, Offices, and Agencies who need to establish legal mandates, verify authority, and ensure compliance
- **Consultants and advisors** — policy consultants, legislative consultants, and governance practitioners working with any branch of the Bangsamoro Government
- **Anyone** who needs to answer the question: *What does the law say about this?*

## What You Will Learn

By the end of this guidebook, you will be able to:

1. **Formulate** a legal research question that is answerable, specific, and tied to a legal outcome
2. **Navigate** the seven-tier BARMM legal hierarchy and determine which tier controls for any given subject
3. **Search** systematically through the Constitution, the BOL, BAAs, national laws, executive issuances, and jurisprudence
4. **Trace** provision chains — following what one provision references to find all related authority
5. **Verify** that every provision you cite is current, correctly attributed, and accurately quoted
6. **Produce** five types of legal research output: Research Memoranda, Legal Analysis Memos, Comparative Matrices, Legal Mandate Matrices, and Legal Opinions
7. **Apply** the Shari'ah research dimension whenever a question crosses the Shari'ah-secular boundary

## How This Guidebook Relates to Other BARMM Guidebooks

This guidebook is the **foundation**. Legal research is the skill that feeds every other competency:

- The **Bill Drafting Guidebook** teaches you how to draft legislation. Its Stage 2 (Legislative Research) is the starting point — this guidebook expands that stage into a complete discipline.
- The **Strategic Planning Guidebook** teaches you how to build a strategic plan. Its Chapter 2 (Legal Alignment) uses the legal hierarchy to establish mandates — this guidebook teaches you how to navigate that hierarchy thoroughly.
- The **CSW Guidebook** teaches you how to prepare Complete Staff Work. Its Step 3 (Research) depends on the methodology this guidebook provides.

If those guidebooks are the house, this guidebook is the foundation inspection manual.

## How to Use This Guidebook

**Read Chapters 1-3 first.** They establish the methodology and the legal hierarchy that every other chapter builds on.

**Then go to the chapter you need.** If you are researching a BOL provision, go to Chapter 5. If you are searching for relevant BAAs, go to Chapters 6-7. If you need to trace a provision chain, go to Chapter 11. If you need to produce a specific output format, go to Chapter 12.

**Use the appendices as working tools.** The templates and checklists are designed to be detached, photocopied, or adapted for your office. The National Law Relationship Test (Appendix B) belongs on every researcher's desk.
