# Glossary of Planning Terms

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Strategic Planning Guidebook. Each entry provides the full form (if applicable) and a BARMM-specific definition.

<table>
<colgroup><col style="width:12%"><col style="width:20%"><col style="width:68%"></colgroup>
<thead><tr><th>Term</th><th>Full Form</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td>AFP</td><td>Annual Financial Plan</td><td>The MOA's detailed budget allocation by expense class (Personal Services, Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses, Capital Outlay) for a single fiscal year, linked to specific programs, projects, and activities.</td></tr>
<tr><td>APP</td><td>Annual Procurement Plan</td><td>A yearly schedule of all goods, services, and infrastructure procurements an MOA intends to undertake, certified compliant with BAA No. 38 (Procurement Code) and aligned with the Annual Work Plan.</td></tr>
<tr><td>AWP</td><td>Annual Work Plan</td><td>The one-year operational breakdown of the MOA's multi-year strategic plan, detailing quarterly targets, milestones, responsible units, and expected deliverables for each program, project, and activity.</td></tr>
<tr><td>BAA</td><td>Bangsamoro Autonomy Act</td><td>A law enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament on matters within the BARMM's legislative authority, including appropriations, sector policies, and administrative mandates; numbered sequentially (e.g., BAA No. 13 is the Administrative Code).</td></tr>
<tr><td>BDP</td><td>Bangsamoro Development Plan</td><td>The medium-term (usually five-year) development framework adopted by Parliament that sets regional development goals, cross-cutting strategies, and sector-specific targets to which all MOA strategic plans must align.</td></tr>
<tr><td>BEDC</td><td>Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council</td><td>The chief policy-making body for regional development, chaired by the Chief Minister, responsible for directing plan formulation and recommending the Bangsamoro Development Plan for parliamentary approval.</td></tr>
<tr><td>BOL</td><td>Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054)</td><td>The constitutional organic law that created the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, defined its powers, established the parliamentary form of government, and granted authority over 55 enumerated areas including administration, budgeting, health, education, and environment.</td></tr>
<tr><td>BPDA</td><td>Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority</td><td>The technical secretariat to the BEDC and the central planning, coordinating, and monitoring agency for all BARMM development plans, policies, programs, and projects; serves as the primary reviewer of MOA strategic plans.</td></tr>
<tr><td>BTA</td><td>Bangsamoro Transition Authority</td><td>The interim parliament (2019-2025) established under the BOL to transition the BARMM from provisional to permanent governance structures; enacted foundational laws and conducted the 2025 elections for the permanent Bangsamoro Parliament.</td></tr>
<tr><td>CO</td><td>Capital Outlay</td><td>The budget expense class for procurement of equipment, infrastructure, and assets with a useful life exceeding one year; tracked separately from operational expenses under the Unified Accounts Code Structure (UACS).</td></tr>
<tr><td>CSO</td><td>Civil Society Organization</td><td>A non-governmental organization, community-based organization, faith-based group, or professional association that represents constituencies and stakeholder interests outside government; engaged in MOA planning through stakeholder analysis and consultation processes.</td></tr>
<tr><td>FTE</td><td>Full-Time Equivalent</td><td>A unit of measurement representing one employee working at full capacity for one fiscal year; used in resource planning to calculate staffing requirements, salary allocations, and organizational plantilla compliance.</td></tr>
<tr><td>GAD</td><td>Gender and Development</td><td>The BARMM's mainstreaming approach to ensure that all MOA programs, policies, and budgets explicitly address gender equality, women's empowerment, and social inclusion; requires at least 5% budget allocation for gender-responsive programs.</td></tr>
<tr><td>LGU</td><td>Local Government Unit</td><td>A municipality, city, or barangay within the BARMM that implements regional and local development programs; significant stakeholder and implementation partner in MOA strategic plans for service delivery and community-level execution.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Legal Mandate Matrix</td><td>—</td><td>A planning tool that systematically maps each functional area or program of an MOA to its legal basis across the 7-tier BARMM legal hierarchy (Constitution, BOL, Administrative Code, BAAs, national laws, executive orders, jurisprudence).</td></tr>
<tr><td>M&E</td><td>Monitoring and Evaluation</td><td>The systematic process of tracking program implementation progress (monitoring) and assessing whether programs achieved their intended results and impact (evaluation); distinct from routine reporting and essential to Results-Based M&E.</td></tr>
<tr><td>MFBM</td><td>Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management</td><td>The MOA responsible for preparing the General Appropriations Act, managing public funds, managing state finances, and evaluating whether MOA budget proposals align with strategic plans and fiscal priorities.</td></tr>
<tr><td>MOA</td><td>Ministry, Office, or Agency</td><td>Any organizational unit of the Bangsamoro Government that exercises defined mandate, operates a budget, and files a strategic plan; includes 15 primary ministries, independent offices, attached agencies, and autonomous bodies.</td></tr>
<tr><td>MOOE</td><td>Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses</td><td>The budget expense class for routine operational costs such as utilities, repairs, supplies, contracted services, and travel; does not include personnel salaries or capital purchases.</td></tr>
<tr><td>OCM</td><td>Office of the Chief Minister</td><td>The principal staff office of the Chief Minister responsible for policy coordination, inter-agency liaison, special projects, and overall executive administration of the Bangsamoro Government.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Outcome</td><td>—</td><td>The medium-term result of a program or project, typically achieved within 1-3 years, representing a change in knowledge, attitude, behavior, practice, or condition among target beneficiaries; sits between outputs and impact in the Results-Based M&E framework.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Output</td><td>—</td><td>The immediate, tangible product or service delivered through a program or project (e.g., number of training participants, curriculum developed, health clinics established); measured at the completion of implementation.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Plantilla</td><td>—</td><td>The official staffing pattern and organizational structure of an MOA, specifying the number and position titles authorized by law; determines salary obligations, FTE calculations, and human resource planning constraints.</td></tr>
<tr><td>PPA</td><td>Program, Project, Activity</td><td>The three-level classification of all MOA work: Programs are ongoing sectoral initiatives (e.g., vocational training), Projects are time-bounded deliverables with defined output (e.g., construction of a school), and Activities are routine tasks supporting program/project delivery.</td></tr>
<tr><td>PS</td><td>Personal Services</td><td>The budget expense class for employee salaries, wages, benefits, and compensation; the largest operating cost for most MOAs and subject to plantilla authorization.</td></tr>
<tr><td>RBME</td><td>Results-Based Monitoring and Evaluation</td><td>A systematic approach to monitoring and evaluation that tracks inputs, processes, outputs, outcomes, and impact using specific performance indicators aligned with the Theory of Change; BARMM's required standard for MOA performance measurement.</td></tr>
<tr><td>SMART</td><td>Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound</td><td>The criteria for designing strategic objectives and performance targets; Specific (clear and unambiguous), Measurable (quantifiable), Achievable (realistic), Relevant (aligned with MOA mandate and BDP), and Time-bound (with a defined deadline).</td></tr>
<tr><td>Stakeholder</td><td>—</td><td>Any individual, group, or organization with a legitimate interest in or power to influence an MOA's strategic plan; includes primary beneficiaries, implementing partners, oversight bodies, funding agencies, and affected communities.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Strategic Objective</td><td>—</td><td>A SMART-formatted statement of what an MOA intends to achieve in support of its mission and in alignment with the BDP goals; sits at the midpoint between broad vision and specific programs, and is the foundation for Results-Based M&E.</td></tr>
<tr><td>SWOT</td><td>Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats</td><td>A strategic analysis tool used in Point 5 (Strategy Analysis) to assess the MOA's internal capacity (strengths, weaknesses) and external context (opportunities, threats); informs the definition of strategic objectives and competitive positioning.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Theory of Change</td><td>ToC</td><td>A visual and narrative model that explains the causal pathway by which an MOA's activities will produce outputs, then outcomes, then impact; explicitly states assumptions and preconditions at each stage; foundation for Results-Based M&E design.</td></tr>
<tr><td>UACS</td><td>Unified Accounts Code Structure</td><td>The standardized classification system for government budget accounts, used to code all MOA expenditures (Personal Services, MOOE, Capital Outlay) and link spending to specific programs, projects, and activities for audit and reporting.</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>
