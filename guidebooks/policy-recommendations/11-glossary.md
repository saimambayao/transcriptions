# Glossary of Policy Terms

**Absorption Rate.** The percentage of an approved budget that an agency actually obligates and disburses within a fiscal year. A low absorption rate signals implementation bottlenecks. Calculated as: (Total Disbursements / Total Appropriations) x 100.

**Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA).** A legislative measure enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament. A BAA carries the force of law within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and is the primary legislative output of the Parliament.

**Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP).** The medium-term strategic plan of the Bangsamoro government. The BDP 2023-2028 is the current plan, organized around three goals: moral governance, meaningful self-determination, and genuine socioeconomic development.

**Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL).** Republic Act No. 11054, signed on July 27, 2018, and ratified in January-February 2019. The charter of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region that establishes its government structure, powers, and fiscal framework.

**Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA).** The government body responsible for coordinating development planning, monitoring BDP implementation, and reviewing programs for alignment with the development plan.

**Bardach's Eightfold Path.** An eight-step policy analysis methodology developed by Eugene Bardach. The steps are: define the problem, assemble evidence, construct alternatives, select criteria, project outcomes, confront trade-offs, decide, and tell your story.

**Baseline.** The starting measurement of a condition before a policy intervention is applied. Baselines establish the "before" against which policy outcomes are measured.

**Benchmark.** A standard of performance used for comparison. Benchmarks may come from BDP targets, national averages, international standards, or evidence-based best practices.

**Block Grant.** The annual fiscal transfer from the national government to the Bangsamoro government, established under BOL Article XII, Section 8. The Bangsamoro government's largest funding source.

**Capital Outlay (CO).** Budget expenditure category covering one-time or long-term investments — equipment, vehicles, infrastructure, IT systems, and construction.

**Comparative Analysis.** A research method that examines how other jurisdictions or programs have addressed a similar policy problem. Used to identify best practices, cost benchmarks, and design features.

**Concurrent Powers.** Authority shared between the Bangsamoro government and the national government. Both levels can act, subject to coordination through mechanisms like the Intergovernmental Relations Body.

**Cost-Benefit Analysis.** A method of evaluating a policy by comparing its total costs to its total benefits, usually expressed in monetary terms. Used to assess whether a policy intervention produces net positive value.

**Enumerated Powers.** The fifty-five subject areas listed in BOL Article V, Section 2, over which the Bangsamoro government exercises exclusive legislative authority.

**Evidence Registry.** A document that lists every data point used in a policy recommendation, along with its source, date, and access location. Ensures traceability and supports the review process.

**Fiscal Feasibility.** An assessment of whether the government can afford a proposed policy recommendation given its current and projected fiscal capacity.

**Focus Group Discussion (FGD).** A qualitative research method in which a small group of stakeholders discusses a policy issue under the guidance of a facilitator. Used to gather community perspectives and test policy proposals.

**General Welfare Clause.** BOL Article V, Section 3. Grants the Bangsamoro government authority over matters not explicitly enumerated but necessary for the general welfare of the Bangsamoro people.

**Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).** Detailed rules issued by an executive agency to operationalize a law. IRRs specify procedures, standards, and requirements that the law itself does not detail.

**Intergovernmental Relations Body (IGRB).** The body established under the BOL to coordinate between the Bangsamoro government and the national government on shared powers, disputes, and BOL implementation.

**Internally Generated Revenue (IGR).** Revenue that the Bangsamoro government collects directly through taxes, fees, charges, and other sources within its jurisdiction, as distinct from transfers from the national government.

**Key Informant Interview (KII).** A qualitative research method in which a researcher conducts a structured or semi-structured interview with a person who has specialized knowledge of the policy issue.

**Key Performance Indicator (KPI).** A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively an objective is being achieved. KPIs are classified as input, output, or outcome indicators.

**Maintenance and Other Operating Expenses (MOOE).** Budget expenditure category covering recurring operational costs — travel, supplies, training, communications, utilities, and professional services.

**Monitoring.** The systematic tracking of implementation progress against planned activities, timelines, and performance targets. Monitoring is ongoing and occurs during implementation.

**Needs Assessment.** A structured analysis of the gap between current conditions and desired conditions for an affected population. Answers: what do people need, and how far are they from having it?

**Other Bangsamoro Communities (OBCs).** Communities outside the Bangsamoro geographic area that share cultural, linguistic, and historical ties with the Bangsamoro people, as defined in BOL Article III.

**Outcome Indicator.** A measure of the change in conditions resulting from a policy intervention — e.g., enrollment rate, income level, disease prevalence. Outcome indicators assess whether the policy is working.

**Output Indicator.** A measure of the products or services delivered by a policy intervention — e.g., beneficiaries served, facilities built, permits issued. Output indicators assess whether the implementation is producing.

**Personnel Services (PS).** Budget expenditure category covering staff costs — salaries, wages, benefits, allowances, and personnel-related expenses.

**Policy Brief.** A condensed document (typically 4-6 pages) that summarizes a policy recommendation for wider circulation. Designed as a gateway document that leads readers to the full recommendation.

**Policy Cycle.** The six-stage process through which policy moves: problem identification, analysis, formulation, adoption, implementation, and evaluation. The cycle is iterative — evaluation feeds back into new problem identification.

**Policy Recommendation.** A structured document that defines a policy problem, presents evidence, evaluates alternatives, and recommends a specific course of action to a decision-maker.

**Programs, Projects, Activities, and Services (PPAS).** The classification system for government expenditure under BAA 84. Budget items are organized as PPAS and categorized as Tier 1 (existing) or Tier 2 (new and expanded).

**PSA-BARMM.** The Philippine Statistics Authority regional office for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. The primary source of official statistics — population, poverty, employment, agriculture, and vital statistics.

**Reserved Powers.** Authority retained exclusively by the national government — defense, foreign affairs, monetary policy, customs, citizenship, and postal service. The Bangsamoro government cannot act on reserved subjects.

**Shari'ah.** Islamic law. The Bangsamoro government has authority over the Shari'ah justice system and related matters, including personal status law, Islamic banking, waqf, and zakat.

**Special Development Fund (SDF).** A ten-year fund established under BOL Article XII, Section 11, for post-conflict rehabilitation, reconstruction, and development in areas affected by conflict.

**Stakeholder.** Any person, group, or institution that is affected by, has influence over, or has an interest in a policy issue. Stakeholder mapping identifies these actors and their positions.

**Status Quo.** The current state of affairs — what happens if the government takes no new action. The status quo is always included as one of the policy alternatives in a recommendation.

**Transmittal Memorandum.** A formal communication that accompanies the approved policy recommendation when it is sent to the implementing agency or other recipient. Specifies the action required.

**Ultra Vires.** Latin for "beyond the powers." An ultra vires action is one that exceeds the legal authority of the government body taking it. Ultra vires recommendations are legally void and should be identified and prevented during the review process.
