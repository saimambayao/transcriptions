# Chapter 4 — Drafting the Manual

You have planned your effort, formed your team, and gathered your information. Now you write. This chapter guides you through the nine standard chapters that every MOP should contain, the format standards that make your MOP professional and usable, and the visual aids that bring procedures to life.

## 4.1 The Nine Standard Chapters

Every MOP for a BARMM Ministry, Office, or Agency should follow a nine-chapter structure. This structure ensures completeness and allows readers to find information predictably across agencies. When an auditor reviews the MILG MOP and then picks up the MBHTE MOP, they should find the same chapter structure with agency-specific content.

### 4.1.1 Chapter I: Introduction

The Introduction chapter provides context. It answers: What does this agency do? Why does this MOP exist? How should readers use it?

**Required content:**

- **Agency background.** Brief history, creation, and evolution. Reference the enabling BAA.
- **Vision, mission, and mandate.** Taken verbatim from the agency's official documents and BAA 13.
- **Purpose of the MOP.** State clearly why this MOP was developed and what it replaces.
- **Scope and coverage.** Define what the MOP covers and what it does not cover.
- **Legal basis.** List all laws, BAAs, executive orders, and regulations that authorize the agency's operations and govern the procedures in the MOP.
- **How to use this MOP.** Brief instructions for readers.
- **Amendment procedures.** How users can request changes to the MOP.
- **Definitions of terms.** Key terms used throughout the MOP, defined once and used consistently.

**Length guide:** 5-10 pages.

### 4.1.2 Chapter II: Organizational Structure

This chapter documents the agency's structure from the highest position to the lowest. It makes reporting relationships, functional responsibilities, and staffing patterns explicit.

**Required content:**

- **Organizational chart.** A visual diagram showing every division, section, and unit with reporting lines.
- **Functional chart.** A chart showing which functions belong to which organizational unit.
- **Position descriptions.** For each position, state the title, salary grade, reporting relationship, and primary duties. Align with BAA 17 (Bangsamoro Civil Service Code) requirements.
- **Staffing pattern.** The authorized number of positions per unit, distinguishing between filled and vacant positions.
- **Delegation of authority.** Who acts when the primary authority is absent, for each key approval function.

**Length guide:** 8-15 pages.

> **BAA 13 connection.** BAA 13 prescribes the organizational structure for each Ministry. Your organizational chart must be consistent with BAA 13. If your actual structure differs from what BAA 13 prescribes, document the variance and cite the authority for the deviation (e.g., an executive order or reorganization plan).

### 4.1.3 Chapter III: Human Resource Management

This chapter documents all HR procedures: recruitment, appointment, onboarding, leave management, performance evaluation, training, discipline, and separation.

**Required content:**

- **Recruitment and selection procedures.** How positions are advertised, applications screened, interviews conducted, and appointments processed. Reference BAA 17 (Bangsamoro Civil Service Code).
- **Onboarding procedures.** What new employees receive, learn, and complete in their first week.
- **Leave management.** Types of leave, application procedures, approval authorities, and tracking.
- **Performance evaluation.** Performance appraisal cycle, forms, rating system, and feedback procedures.
- **Training and development.** How training needs are identified, programs selected, and participation tracked.
- **Discipline.** Administrative disciplinary procedures. Reference BAA 17, Book V (Accountability of Public Officers and Employees).
- **Separation procedures.** Resignation, retirement, transfer, and termination processing.

**Length guide:** 15-25 pages.

### 4.1.4 Chapter IV: Financial Management

This chapter documents all financial procedures: budget preparation, fund management, disbursement, collections, and financial reporting.

**Required content:**

- **Budget preparation.** Annual budget cycle, roles, timelines, and forms. Reference BAA 84 (Budget System Act).
- **Fund management.** How funds are received, recorded, and managed.
- **Disbursement procedures.** How payments are processed, including voucher preparation, pre-audit, approval, and release.
- **Cash advance and liquidation.** Procedures for cash advances and their timely liquidation.
- **Revenue collection.** If applicable, how your agency collects and records revenue.
- **Financial reporting.** Monthly, quarterly, and annual financial reports: who prepares them, who reviews them, who submits them, and to whom.

**Length guide:** 10-20 pages.

### 4.1.5 Chapter V: Planning and Programming

This chapter documents how the agency plans its work, sets targets, and programs its activities.

**Required content:**

- **Annual work planning.** How the agency develops its annual operational plan, aligned with the Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP 2023-2028).
- **Program/project development.** How new programs, projects, activities, and services (PPAS) are conceived, designed, and approved.
- **Performance targeting.** How KPIs are set, aligned with organizational outcomes, and tracked.
- **Monitoring and reporting.** How the agency monitors progress against plans and reports performance.

**Length guide:** 8-12 pages.

### 4.1.6 Chapter VI: Service Delivery

This is the core of your MOP. This chapter documents every procedure your agency uses to deliver its mandated services. Each service or output gets its own workflow table using the PRLS four-column format (Chapter 5).

**Required content:**

- **List of services and outputs.** A complete catalog of what your agency produces.
- **Workflow tables for each service.** Step-by-step procedures in the four-column format: step number, responsible person, action/procedure, and output/form used.
- **Turnaround time standards.** Expected processing time for each service, categorized as simple, routine, or complex.
- **Quality standards.** What constitutes an acceptable output for each service.
- **Escalation procedures.** What happens when a service cannot be delivered within the standard turnaround time.

**Length guide:** 20-40 pages (the largest chapter).

> **This chapter is where the PRLS MOP Model (Chapter 5) applies most directly.** Organize your service delivery procedures by output category, use four-column workflow tables, and follow the hierarchical approval flow: Reception, Evaluation/Delegation, Execution, Review, Approval, Delivery.

### 4.1.7 Chapter VII: Procurement and Property Management

This chapter documents procurement procedures, property management, and supply management.

**Required content:**

- **Procurement procedures.** Small value procurement, competitive bidding, alternative methods of procurement. Reference RA 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act) and BAA 84.
- **Property management.** Receiving, recording, issuing, and disposing of government property.
- **Supply management.** Requesting, purchasing, storing, and distributing supplies.
- **Inventory procedures.** Physical inventory schedules, reconciliation, and reporting.
- **Vehicle management.** If applicable, vehicle assignment, use, maintenance, and fueling procedures.

**Length guide:** 10-15 pages.

### 4.1.8 Chapter VIII: Reporting and Accountability

This chapter documents all reporting obligations and accountability mechanisms.

**Required content:**

- **Internal reports.** Weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual reports that divisions must submit to the agency head.
- **External reports.** Reports submitted to the Chief Minister's Office, Parliament, COA, CSC, DBM, and other oversight bodies.
- **Compliance reports.** Gender and Development (GAD), Persons with Disabilities (PWD), and other mandated compliance reports.
- **Records management.** How records are created, filed, maintained, retrieved, and disposed of.
- **Document control.** How official documents are numbered, tracked, and archived.

**Length guide:** 8-12 pages.

### 4.1.9 Chapter IX: Annexes

The Annexes chapter contains all forms, templates, checklists, and reference documents mentioned in the MOP.

**Required content:**

- **All forms referenced in Chapters I-VIII.** Each form should be labeled, numbered, and include instructions for completion.
- **Organizational chart.** Full-size version.
- **Legal references.** Relevant excerpts from BAA 13, the enabling BAA, and other governing legislation.
- **Sample completed forms.** At least one example of each major form, filled out correctly.
- **Glossary.** If not already included in Chapter I.

**Length guide:** Variable (15-50 pages depending on the number of forms).

## 4.2 Format Standards

Consistent formatting makes your MOP professional and easy to navigate. Apply these standards throughout.

### 4.2.1 Page Layout and Typography

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| **Paper size** | A4 (210 x 297 mm) |
| **Margins** | Top: 25mm, Bottom: 30mm, Left: 25mm, Right: 22mm |
| **Body font** | Inter, Calibri, or Arial, 11pt |
| **Heading 1 (Chapter)** | 22pt, bold, navy (#1B365D) |
| **Heading 2 (Section)** | 16pt, bold, navy |
| **Heading 3 (Subsection)** | 13pt, bold, navy |
| **Heading 4 (Sub-subsection)** | 11.5pt, bold, slate (#2C5F7C) |
| **Line spacing** | 1.15 |
| **Paragraph spacing** | 6pt after |
| **Page numbers** | Bottom center, "Page X of Y" format |

### 4.2.2 Numbering Systems

Use a hierarchical decimal numbering system:

- **Chapter:** Chapter I, Chapter II, etc. (Roman numerals for MOP chapters, to distinguish from this guidebook's Arabic numerals)
- **Section:** 1.0, 2.0, 3.0
- **Subsection:** 1.1, 1.2, 1.3
- **Sub-subsection:** 1.1.1, 1.1.2, 1.1.3
- **Procedure steps:** 1, 2, 3 (Arabic numerals within workflow tables)

Number all forms in the Annexes: Form 1, Form 2, etc. Cross-reference forms by number throughout the MOP.

### 4.2.3 Header and Footer Requirements

Every page of the MOP (except the cover page) should include:

- **Header:** Agency name and MOP title, centered, 8pt italic
- **Footer left:** "MOP -- [Agency Acronym] -- [Effective Date]"
- **Footer right:** "Page X of Y"

## 4.3 Visual Aids and Diagrams

Visual aids transform dense text into scannable information. Include at least three types of visual aids in your MOP.

### 4.3.1 Workflow Diagrams

Create a workflow diagram for each major procedure. Use standard flowchart notation:

- **Start/End:** Rounded rectangle
- **Process step:** Rectangle
- **Decision:** Diamond
- **Document:** Rectangle with wavy bottom edge
- **Direction:** Arrows

Place workflow diagrams at the beginning of each procedure section, before the detailed workflow table. The diagram gives readers a quick overview; the table provides the step-by-step detail.

### 4.3.2 Organizational Charts

Include organizational charts at three levels:

1. **Agency-wide chart** in Chapter II showing all divisions and their reporting relationships
2. **Division-level charts** in Chapter VI showing the internal structure of each division
3. **Functional responsibility matrix** showing which positions are responsible for which services

### 4.3.3 Decision Trees

Use decision trees for processes that involve multiple branching paths. Decision trees are especially useful for:

- Determining which procurement method to use based on estimated cost
- Determining which approval authority applies based on transaction amount
- Routing incoming documents to the correct division based on subject matter
- Determining which leave type applies based on the employee's situation

## 4.4 Ensuring Consistency Across Chapters

Consistency is the hallmark of a professional MOP. Apply these checks before finalizing:

**Terminology consistency.** Use the same term for the same thing throughout. If you call it "Referral and Action Slip" in Chapter VI, do not call it "Action Slip" in Chapter VIII or "Routing Form" in the Annexes. Create a terminology list and enforce it.

**Cross-reference accuracy.** Every reference to a form, section, or chapter must point to the correct location. Verify each cross-reference after the final draft is assembled.

**Role title consistency.** Use official position titles from the staffing pattern. Do not alternate between "Division Chief," "Chief of Division," and "Division Head" for the same position.

**Numbering continuity.** Verify that no numbers are skipped or duplicated in the chapter, section, and form numbering systems.

**Format consistency.** Check that heading levels, table formats, bullet styles, and spacing are uniform throughout. A QA Specialist (Section 2.2.3) should conduct this check.

> **Quality check.** Before submitting the draft for review, the Technical Writer and QA Specialist should independently verify all cross-references, terminology, and formatting. Use the Quality Review Checklist in Chapter 7 as your guide.
