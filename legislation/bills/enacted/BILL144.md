**An Act Creating the Bangsamoro Convention Center and Appropriating Funds Therefor**

**SECTION 1. Short Title.** – This act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Convention Center Act".

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** – It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to provide a venue for local and international conventions, meetings, fairs, and social events in the Bangsamoro Region in order to promote economic growth, cultural exchange, and establish the Bangsamoro Region as a leading destination for events and gatherings.

**SECTION 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Convention Center.** – There is hereby created the Bangsamoro Convention Center, which shall be located in the Municipality of Parang, Maguindanao del Norte Province. The Center shall be a government-owned and controlled corporation with a separate legal personality, under the management and supervision of the Bangsamoro Government.

**SECTION 4. Powers and Functions of the Bangsamoro Convention Center.** – The Bangsamoro Convention Center shall have the following powers and functions:

a) To host local and international conventions, meetings, fairs, and social events;
b) To provide facilities and services for conventions, meetings, fairs, and social events, including but not limited to: meeting rooms, exhibition halls, auditoriums, and accommodation;
c) To establish and collect fees and charges for the use of its facilities and services;
d) To enter into contracts and agreements necessary for the proper and efficient discharge of its functions;
e) To promote and market the Center as a premier venue for events in the Bangsamoro Region;
f) To establish partnerships and collaborations with private and public entities for the promotion and development of the Center;
g) To exercise such other powers and perform such other functions as may be necessary for the attainment of its objectives.

**SECTION 5. Administration and Management of the Bangsamoro Convention Center.** – The Bangsamoro Convention Center shall be administered and managed by a board of trustees, which shall be composed of the following:

a) The Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government, as the chairman of the board;
b) The Minister of Trade, Investments and Tourism;
c) The Minister of Basic, Higher and Technical Education;
d) The Chairman of the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage;
e) The Governor of Maguindanao del Norte Province;
f) Two (2) representatives from the private sector, to be appointed by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government.

The board shall be responsible for the overall management and direction of the Center and shall have the power to formulate policies and guidelines for the operations of the Center. The board shall appoint a chief executive officer who shall be responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Center.

**SECTION 6. Appropriations.** – The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this act shall be included in the annual general appropriations act. The Bangsamoro Convention Center shall also have the power to generate funds through fees and charges for the use of its facilities and services, as well as through partnerships and collaborations with private and public entities.

**SECTION 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** – The board of trustees, in consultation with the relevant government agencies, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the implementation of this act, within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this act.

**Section 8. Annual Report.** – The Bangsamoro Convention Center shall submit an annual report to the Bangsamoro Government and the relevant committees of the Bangsamoro Assembly, detailing its financial and operational performance, as well as its plans and programs for the coming year.

**SECTION 9. Auditing.** – The Bangsamoro Convention Center’s financial and operational performance shall be subject to regular auditing by the Bangsamoro Government’s Commission on Audit.

**SECTION 10. Separability Clause.** – If any provision of this act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**SECTION 11. Repeating Clause.** – All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with this act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 12. Effectivity.** – This act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Region.
