**AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO SPORTS INSTITUTE (BSI) UNDER THE BANGSAMORO SPORTS COMMISSION, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES**

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

**SECTION 1. Title.** – This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Sports Institute Act of 2025”.

**SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.** - It is the policy of the State to promote and develop sports in the country, to achieve excellence in international sports competitions, to ensure success in the country's quest to achieve competitiveness in the Olympic Games and to promote international amity among nations. Pursuant thereto, the establishment and construction of a national sports training facility shall be a primary strategy to achieve the foregoing objectives.

Consistent with the Bangsamoro Government’s commitment to youth development, social cohesion, and regional autonomy under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, this Act affirms the vital role of sports in promoting regional identity, unity, and peace.

**SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.**

*   Sports - refer to all forms of physical activity which, through casual or organized participation, aim at expressing or improving physical fitness and mental well-being, forming social relationships, or obtaining results in competition at all levels.

*   Athlete - a person who is proficient in sports and other forms of physical exercise.

*   Bangsamoro Sports Institute (BSI) - A school, training center, and organization in the Bangsamoro Region that helps young people learn, develop, and improve their mental and physical skills through sports.

*   Bangsamoro Sports Development Fund - refers to a special fund allocated by the Bangsamoro Government and sourced from annual appropriations, grants, or donations, intended solely for the planning, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of sports development programs and related infrastructure projects within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. This Fund shall support grassroots sports, talent identification and training, capacity-building of sports personnel, procurement of equipment, and the maintenance and improvement of sports facilities.

**SECTION 4. Basis.** – This Act is enacted pursuant to:

(a) Section 19, Article XIV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states: “The State shall promote physical education and encourage sports programs, league competitions, and amateur sports, including training for international competitions, to foster self-discipline, teamwork, and excellence for the development of a healthy and alert citizenry.”.

(b) Section 20, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law: "The Bangsamoro Government shall encourage and support programs, league competitions, indigenous games, martial arts, and amateur sports including training for regional, national, and international competitions.".

(c) Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 10 or the Bangsamoro Youth Commission Act which mandates the promotion of youth participation in sports and physical development programs in BARMM.

**SECTION 5. Coverage.** This Act shall cover all current and aspiring athletes residents of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao who are competing for regional, national and international competitions.

**SECTION 6. Objectives.** – The objectives of the BSI shall be:

(a) To serve as the primary regional hub for sports training, education, and athlete development in BARMM;

(b) To produce world-class athletes through scientific training, coaching, and sports research;

(c) To provide training and certification programs for coaches, referees, and sports officials;

(d) To serve as a venue for local, national, and international sports events;

(e) To support peacebuilding and social inclusion through sports and physical activities across diverse communities in BARMM; and

(f) To promote Islamic values, discipline, and character development through sports.

(g) To coordinate with the Philippines Sports Institute of the Philippine Sports Commission for supporting Bangsamoro athletes competing internationally.

**SECTION 7. Legal Mandate and Interagency Coordination.** - The Bangsamoro Sports Institute shall operate under the policy guidance and supervision of the Bangsamoro Sports Commission and shall coordinate with the Philippine Sports Commission, Department of Education, Commission on Higher Education, and other relevant national sports bodies to ensure conformity with national training, competitions, and accreditation standards.

**SECTION 8. Accreditation and Certification.**- The BSI shall develop and implement standards for accreditation of sports coaches, trainers, officials, and partner institutions, subject to minimum national standards and guidelines issued by relevant government agencies.

**SECTION 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations.** - The BSI shall, in consultation and coordination with the the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, the Ministry of Transportation and Communications, the Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Basic Higher and Technical Education, Bangsamoro Youth Commission, formulate and promulgate the necessary rules and regulations within thirty (30) days upon the effectivity of this Act.

**SECTION 10. Administration and Management.** The Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall have the authority to administer, manage, and maintain the BSI. The Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall have the power to determine and appoint officials of the BSI.

**SECTION 11. Ownership of Property.** The land, facilities, and other physical properties of the Bangsamoro Sports Institute shall be owned by the Bangsamoro Sports Commission on behalf of the Bangsamoro Government.

Titles and legal ownership shall be processed in accordance with Indigenous People’s Rights Act (RA 8371), the Bangsamoro Land Laws, and other applicable national and regional policies on public lands and government property.

**SECTION 12. Appropriation.** To implement this Act, a Bangsamoro Sports Development Fund shall be created and included in the annual appropriation of the Bangsamoro Sports Institute. This fund shall cover:

(a) Operational costs of the BSI;
(b) Construction and maintenance of sports facilities;
(c) Scholarships for sports trainees and students;
(d) Compensation of personnel and trainers;
(e) Equipment, materials, and research initiatives.

Funding for the BSI shall be sourced from:

1. Annual appropriations under the General Appropriations Act (GAA) through the Philippine Sports Commission;
2. Special development assistance funds intended for BARMM;
3. Internal appropriations from the Bangsamoro Government under its Block Grant and Special Development Fund as provided under the Bangsamoro Organic Law;
4. Grants, endowments, and donations from public or private entities, international organizations, and development partners, subject to existing rules and regulations.

**SECTION 13. Monitoring and Evaluation.** - The Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall develop an annual performance evaluation mechanism for BSI operations, sports programs, athlete and infrastructure utilization, and submit an annual report to the Bangsamoro Parliament and the Office of the Chief Minister.

**SECTION 14. Promotion of Indigenous Sports.** - The BSI shall include in its programs the preservation and promotion of indigenous sports and traditional martial arts of the Bangsamoro people as part of cultural heritage and identity.

**SECTION 15. Separability Clause.** – If any provision of this Act is held unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

**SECTION 16. Repealing Clause.** – All laws, rules, regulations, or issues inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

**SECTION 17. Effectivity.** – This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
