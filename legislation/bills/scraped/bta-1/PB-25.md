AN ACT CREATING A BANGSAMORO VETERAN MUJAHIDEEN/
MUJAHIDAT AFFAIRS OFFICE, DEFINING ITS BASIC

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bangsamoro
Veteran Mujahideen Act of 2019”.

Section 2. Name. — The office to be created under this Act shall be known as
the Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office (BVMAO).

Section 3. Purpose. — The purpose of this Act is to establish an office that will
provide financial, medical, social and other assistance to the veterans of the Moro
Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) and Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) who
are underprivileged and have no opportunity for employment because of their old age,
lack of the required education, or other requirements for employment.

Section 4. Declaration of Policy — It is the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to protect the interests of its people and ensure every Bangsamoro in its
territorial jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in
life. It is also the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government to establish an
economic mechanisms that are responsive to the needs of its people.

Section 5, Definition of Terms — As used in this Act, the following terms shall
be understood to mean:

a. "Bangsamoro Government” refers to the corporate parliament entity with
jurisdiction over all matters devolved to it by the Constitution, the Bangsamoro
Organic Law, and other pertinent laws, through which the functions of
autonomous governance are exercised throughout the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, including the various
instrumentalities through which political authority is made effective;

b. “Employment” refers to the state of being employed in any public or private
enterprise or organization with expectation of remuneration for the services
rendered.

c. “National Government” refers to the entire machinery of the central
government, as distinguished from the different forms of local governments;

d. “Qualified Beneficiaries” refer to those who may avail of the benefits,
privileges and other assistance granted under this Act;

e. “Underprivileged” refers to those Veteran Mujahideen and other qualified
beneficiaries who have no opportunity for employment because of their old age,
lack of the required education, or other requirements for employment, and thus,
needs financial, medical, social, and other assistance from the Bangsamoro
Government.

f. “Veteran Mujahideen” refer to those combatants of the MILF and MNLF who
actually took part in the protracted wars for self-determination between the
Bangsamoro people and the National Government and who are at least sixty
(60) years of age from the time of the effectivity of this Act.

Section 6. Creation. — There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Veteran
Mujahideen Affairs Office which shall be placed under the operational supervision
and control of the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) as one of its
component divisions.

Section 7. Composition and Organizational Structure. — The Bangsamoro
Veteran Mujahideen Affairs Office shall be composed of a Director for Bangsamoro
Veteran Mujahideen Affairs, as head of the office, and his/her immediate staff, three
(3) Development Management Officers V, as the respective heads of the three (3)
component sections of the office, and their immediate staff, and other necessary
personnel as may be determined under the implementing rules of this Act.

Section 8. Appointment. - The Director for Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen
Affairs shall be appointed by the Interim Chief Minister for a term of three (3) years
without prejudice for reappointment. Thereafter, the Director shall be appointed by
the Chief Minister for a term of six (6) years without reappointment. All other
employees and personnel of the office shall be appointed by the Minister of the MPOS
subject to qualification standards prescribed under the implementing rules of this Act
which shall not be below the minimum standards provided under civil service laws,
rules, and regulations.

Section 9. Duties and Functions. — The Bangsamoro Veteran Mujahideen
Affairs Office shall perform the following:

a. Create a functional and effective interagency working group composed of
representatives from the Ministry of Social Services; the Ministry of Health;
the Ministry of Trade, Investment, and Tourism; the Ministry of Agriculture,
Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform; the Ministry of, Basic, Higher, and
Technical Education; Ministry of Human Settlement and Development; and

Section 10. Scope. — The benefits and privileges provided under this Act shall
be available to the underprivileged Veteran Mujahideen as defined in Section 4 of this
Act. The two (2) Thousand cash allowance as provided in paragraph (a) of Section 12
shall be granted to the spouse and children of Veteran Mujahideen within five (5)
years from the time of his death.

The same benefits and privileges shall not be available to those Veteran
Mujahideen who receive benefits and privileges under the decommissioning process
as part of the normalization.

Section 11. Other Qualified Beneficiaries. — The interagency working group
may identify other qualified beneficiaries who are not official members of the MILF
or MNLF as may be recommended by the Minister of the MPOS and subject to the
approval of the Chief Minister, provided that they possess the following requirements:

a. They are at least seventy (70) years of age from the time of the effectivity
of this Act;

b. They are underprivileged and have no sufficient means of support;
c. They had actually took part in any of the encounters between the MILF or
MNLF and the forces of the National Government in connection with the

struggle for right to self-determination; and

d. They can provide sufficient proof of such participation.

Section 12. Benefits and Privileges. — The underprivileged Veteran
Mujahideen and other qualified beneficiaries shall be entitled to receive or enjoy the
following benefits and privileges:

a. Six (6) Thousand Pesos (PhP 6,000) monthly cash allowance;

b. Free medical assistance from any of the BARMM-Ministry of Health
supervised facilities, health centers, and hospitals;

c. [Twenty percent (20%) discount on the sale of goods and services from any
establishments within BARMM, as identified in the implementing rules of
this Act, for their exclusive use and enjoyment or availment;

d. Exemption from taxes, fees, and other charges imposed by the
Bangsamoro Government;

e. Ten percent (10) discount from electric, water, and other similar utilities
computed proportionately based on the numbers of actual occupants of the
household they are actually residing;

f. Exemption from fees for skills, socioeconomic, and educational trainings
or seminars conducted by any public or private institutions within
BARMM;; and

g. Such other benefits and privileges as may thereafter be determined by law.

Section 13. Appropriation — The initial amount of thirty (30) Million
necessary to finance the implementation of this Act is hereby appropriated and shall
be charged to the available funds of the Bangsamoro Treasury. Thereafter, the
annual budget of the office shall be provided in the Annual Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act as part of the annual budget appropriated for the MPOS.

Section 14. /mplementing Rules and Regulations. — The Ministry of Public
Order and Safety and the Attorney General’s Office shall formulate the necessary
implementing rules and regulations within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of
this Act in order to effectively implement the provisions of this Act.

Section 15. Separability Clause — Should any provision of this Act be
subsequently declared unconstitutional or illegal, the other provisions not so affected
shall remain in full force and effect.

Section 16. Repealing Clause — All regional laws, decrees, orders, rules and
regulations, or any part thereof, which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed
or amended accordingly.

Section 17. Effectivity — This Act shall take effect fifteen days after its
publication in a newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.
