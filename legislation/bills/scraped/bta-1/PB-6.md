EXPLANATORY NOTE

In the exercise of its right to self-determination and self-governance, the Bangsamoro political
entity is free to pursue its spiritual, political, economic, social, and cultural development.
Hence, the enactment into law of this proposed guiding religious principle by the Bangsamoro
Transition Authority is not only a milestone but a landmark in the history of the Bangsamoro
people in general.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transitional Authority (BTA) assembled:

(RCMAA) of 2019”.

ARTICLE II
DECLARATION OF POLICY

Section 1. Constitutional Recognition, Protection and Development - Sections 17 to 19, Article
XIV, of the 1987 Constitution, cultural recognition through the principle of “unity in diversity’,
recognition and protection of cultural communities, preservation and development of their
cultures, traditions and institutions, including access to cultural opportunities.

Section 2. Distinct Heritage and Way of Life, Respect for Diversity - The Bangsamoro society
and government reflective of its heritage and distinct way of life, while respecting the rights of
its non-Muslim minorities in its midst, recognizes the democratic system in the country as
influenced by the Madinah model of governance in achieving the people’s full aspirations and
potentials.

Section 3. General Welfare and Public Morals - As provided for in Section 3, Article V Powers of
Government of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the Bangsamoro government shall exercise
powers essentials for the general welfare, including preservation and enrichment of culture,
improve public morals, enhance social justice and preserve the comfort and convenience of its
inhabitants.

Section 4. Equitable Representation - As provided for in Section 11, Article VI Inter-
governmental Relations of Government of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the Bangsamoro
government shall be represented in the commissions and agencies of the National Government
that implement and enforce policies, programs and projects in the autonomous region.

Section 5. Jurisdiction - As provided for in Section 42, Article Vi] Bangsamoro Government of
the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the parliament may create other offices, including jurisdiction
over hajj and umrah matters for its residents.

Section 6. Transitional Justice - As provided for in Section 1, Article IX Basic Rights of the
Bangsamoro Organic Law, the parliament shall enact transitional justice mechanism to address
the legitimate grievances such as historical injustices, human rights violations, and
marginalization.

Section 7. System of Beliefs and Traditions - As provided for in Section 4, Article IX Basic Rights
of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the customs, beliefs and traditions of the Bangsamoro people
are hereby recognized, protected and guaranteed.

Section 8. Transfer of Power - As provided for in Section 11, Article XVI, the Bangsamoro
Transition Authority of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, provides for the disposition of personnel
of national government whose mandate and functions are referred to or now vested in the
Bangsamoro Government by virtue of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, without prejudice to the
power of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority to organize the bureaucracy.

Section 9. Rights and Wellbeing of Muslims - As a matter of policy, Republic Act 9997
recognizes the rights and well-being of Muslim Filipinos with due regards to their beliefs,
customs, traditions and institutions, and their active participation in nation-building.

Section 10. Roles of the Religious Sector — The religious sector in the BARMM includes the
ulama, alimat, asatidz, ustazat, mashaykh or murshidin, du’at, huffaz, a’7immah, muballighin or
jama’at-tabligh and muhtadin. As critical sector, especially in the current fight against
extremism and terrorism, the Bangsamoro government shall explore ways and means to
provide spaces and opportunities for their collective participation and engagement.

Section 11. International Consensus — As part of the global Muslim ummah (community), the
BARMM fully subscribes to international consensus that promotes inter-religious dialogue such
as ‘A Common Word’ and Muslim unity in diversity such as the ‘Amman Message’; that respects
religious minorities in Muslim-majority areas, such as the ‘Marrakesh Declaration’; and the
promotion of moderation and tolerance such as the ‘New Mardin Declaration’.

ARTICLE III
CREATION AND GENERAL ORGANIZATION

Section 1. Creation — In pursuance of the above stated policies, the Regional Commission on
Muslim Affairs (RCMA) is hereby created in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMMM).

Section 2. Mandate — The RCMA shall represent and perform the powers and functions of the
National Commission on Muslim Filipinos (NCMF) in the BARMM.

Section 3. Organizational Structure — The RCMA shall have a commission en banc, regional
office and provincial offices;
(a) For fair representation, the Commission En Banc shall be composed of
commissioners, one for each BARMM province, and shall be headed by a chairman, who
shall be chosen from among themselves and all of them shall be appointed by the Chief

agency.

(b) There shall be one Regional Support Office (RSO) located at the BARMM

sustainable decentralization of office operations and services shall be pursued in favor

(c) Provincial Service Offices {PSOs) shall be conspicuously located at the provincial

Bangsamoro Government, a Regional Coordination Board on Muslim Affairs (RCBMA) is
hereby created composite of all ministries, agencies, bureaus and offices performing powers
and functions and responsibilities stated in Section 2, Article IV, of this Act.

ARTICLE IV
POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

Section 1. Core Powers and Functions - The RCMA shall in coordination, collaboration and
cooperation with the National Coommission on Muslim Filipinos perform the following powers
and functions within the territorry of the Bangsamoro Autonous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

(a) Provide advice and assistance to the Regional Government in the formulation,
coordination, implementation and monitoring of policies, plans, programs and projects
affecting Muslim residents; when so authorized, represent the Chief Minister or the
Regional government on matters concerning Muslims.

(b) Undertake and coordinate development programs and projects for the advancement
of Muslim beliefs, traditions and institutions.

(c) Serve as a government link to the religious communities in the BARMM.

(d) Serve as lead regional agency in peaceful resolution and in influencing actors of
remaining armed conflicts and local feuds (rido, pagbanta) through active non-violent
means; and in redressing the human security contexts upon which with feuds and
conflicts exist.

(e) Enter, subject to existing laws, policies and guidelines, into contracts, agreements or
arrangements with government or private agencies/entities as may be necessary to
attain the objectives of the Commission;

(f) Monitor and evaluate the performance of all existing policies and development
programs of the government that seek to strengthen and uplift the Muslim conditions

(g) Solicit and accept grants, donations and gifts, in cash or in kind, in whatever source,
in coordination with the appropriate agency for the benefit of the Muslim Filipinos, and

(h) Administer all programs, projects and activities, formulate the necessary rules and

(i) Prescribe rules and regulations for the establishment and administration of waaf

principles of Shari'ah (Islamic Law). Additionally, administer and hold in trust waqf
(endowment) properties, and receive by way of grant, donations or gifts, awgaf

(j) Promulgate such rules and regulations and exercise such powers and functions as
may be necessary to carry out the purposes and objectives outlined in this Act.

(k) Perform such other functions as may be necessary for its effective operations and
continued enhancement as a government entity.

Section 2. Ministerial Complementation — For program efficiency and effectiveness, program

relevance and complementation across the regional cabinet, the following powers and

functions are hereby referred:
(a) The power and function on madrasah education, from ibtida’i (primary), I’dadi
(elementary) and thanawi (secondary) to kulliyah (collegiate) and graduate programs,

Jawi orthography; and the promotion and development of traditional and prophetic
sports and games shall be headed by a competent Ulama member are hereby referred
to the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education.

(b) The powers and functions on taqweem (calendar) and halal testing are hereby
referred to the Ministry of Science and Technology; however, the power and function
over taharri al-Hilal, the declaration of the start and end of annual sawm (fast) in the
month of Ramadhan, including declaration over the two Ejds (feasts) shall continue to
be exercised by the Regional Darul-Ifta’ of the Bangsamoro. In the case of halal testing,
the power and function on halal certification shall continue to reside with recognized
halal certifying body and shall be headed by a competent Ulama member.

{c) The rights and responsibilities on the promotion, development, regulation and
supervision of Aalal agriculture and fisheries shall be headed by a competent Ulama
member are hereby referred to the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian
Reform.

ARTICLE V
LEADERSHIP, MANAGEMENT AND OPERATIONS

Section 1. Leadership - The RCMA shall be headed by a Minister-Chairman to be appointed by
the Chief Minister as part of the regional cabinet. The Minister-Chairman shall be assisted by
the commission en banc in providing strategic policy direction and program investment for the
agency. The highest career officer within the commission, who shall assist the Minister-
Chairman and be responsble for daily operations and smooth transition in between
governments, shall be the Director-General.

Sestion 2. Leadership Qualification — Consistent with the stated policy on recognition and
mainstreaming of the religious sector in the BARMM and with due consideration to the
principle of meritocracy, all MMA appointments shall be based on dual qualifications: (a) meet
the minimum standards set by the Civil Service, and (b) with at least Islamic education
credentials. The same general considerations shall apply to bureaus and offices across the
regional bureaucracy with the tasks specified under Section 2, Article IV of this Act.

Section 3. Program Management — Within the regional office, there shall be organized bureaus,
each one in-charge of specific or cluster of powers and or functions of the commision. The
initial set of program bureaus to be organized shall be as follows: (a) hajj and umra regulations
and supervision bureau (b) awqaf and sadakah regulations and supervision bureau, (c) zakat
regulations and supervision bureau, (d) masajid regulations and supervision bureau, (e) amanat
al madhalim bureau, {e) al-hisbah bureau, (g) traditional madrasa regulations and supervision
bureau, (h) peacemaking and conflict transformation bureau, and (i) muslim studies, research
and training academy bureau.

Section 4. Operations System — For sustainable, efficient and effective operations and
management, the commission shall put in place technical and operational standards and
systems, such as but not limited to the following - management information system, human
resources management and development system, financial management system, and policy
planning and programming system; as manifested in the agency's approved operations’
manual, citizen’s charter, online transparency seal and similar government mechanisms on
transparency and accountability.

Section 5. Civil Service Law Coverage - Hiring, appointment of employees, promotion,
disciplinary action, separation and other terms and conditions of the service of employees of
the commission and such other matter affecting its employees shall be consistent with the
provisions of the Civil Service Commission rules and regulations in the BARMM. Further, given
the nature of Muslim affairs, as part of the minimum qualification standards for all
commission’s officials and employees shall include Arabic proficiency and kulliyah credential for
the minister-chairman, all commissioners, regional officials down to provincial and section
heads. Furthermore, Arabic proficiency and thanawi credential shall be additionally required

ARTICLE VI
TRANSITORY PROVISIONS

Section 1. Transfer to BARMM -— All projects, programs and activities not covered within the
powers and functions of the NCMF offices in the BARMM shall be exercised and performed by
the Regional Commission on Muslim Affairs (RCMA) within the Autonomous region.

Section 2. Transition Phase — The first three (3) years of the existence of the commission from
the date of approval of this Act shall be considered its transition phase. This 3-year transition
phase shall be utilized by the commission to build up qualifiers from the religious sector to
comply with the dual qualification standard stated in this Act.

Section 3. Implementing Rules and Regulations - The commission shall issue the implementing
rules and regulations within the first year of its organization.

ARTICLE VII
FINAL PROVISIONS

Section 1. Amendments and Revisions- All proposals to amend or revise the provisions hereof
shall be endorsed by the commission en banc.

Section 2. Separability Clause- Any section or provision of this Act shall be declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the other sections or provisions hereof which are not affected
thereby shall continue to be binding and operational.

Section 3. Repealing Clause- All laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations, and other
issuances or parts thereof, which are inconsistent with this statute, are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.

Section 4. Effectivity Clause-This Act shall take effect fifteen days after its publication in two (2)
regional newspapers of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM).
