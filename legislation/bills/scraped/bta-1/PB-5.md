EXPLANATORY NOTE

The bill seeks to establish an Office of the Bangsamoro Director-General for
Madaris Education in the Ministry of Education for Basic, Higher, and
Technical Education in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM).

Section 2 paragraph 2 of Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution states that the
“State shall establish, maintain and support a complete, adequate, and
integrated system of education relevant to the needs of the people and society.”

Likewise, Section 16 of Republic Act No. 11054 known as Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao states, that “the
Bangsamoro Government shall develop an educational framework relevant and
responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people.”

Section 18 of RA 11054 further provides that, “In coordination with the
Department of Education, Commission on Higher Education, and Technical
Education and Skills Development Authority, the Bangsamoro Government
shall establish, maintain and supervise Madaris Education in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region. The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the integration
in the elementary and high school education curricula the teaching of Islamic
and Arabic studies for Muslim pupils and students in public school.” For this
purpose, “The Parliament shall enact legislation to develop and strengthen the
Madaris educational system in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.”

Pursuant to this, the BARMM government is mandated to establish, maintain,
strengthen and supervise the madrasah education system in the Bangsamoro
region.

According to International Religious Freedom Report 2003 released by the
Bureau of Democracy, Human Rights and Labor of the United States, there are
at least 1,569 Islamic schools (madrasahs) across the Philippines. Of these, 53
percent are located in the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM).

The said Report likewise reveal that there are 1,140 madrasahs seeking
financial assistance from local and foreign donors that are registered with the
Office on Muslim Affairs, while only 35 are registered with the Department of
Education. This situation is due primarily to the inability of the madrasah to
meet the Department of Education’s accreditation standards for curricula and
adequate facilities.

The Bureau of Madaris Education (BME) of the defunct ARMM in cooperation
with the Bangsamoro Development Authority (BDA) and Tarbiyyah of the MILF
with the assistance of Basic Education Assistance for Mindanao (BEAM -
ARMM) consolidated a madrasah data from said offices in 2018. The result
reveals that there are 1, 914 madaris (plural of madrasah) in the entire region.
Of these, only 135 madaris are regulated by the BME and the bureau has no
authority to monitor, supervise, and support them as they are not recognized.

That is why this urgent bill seeks the immediate creation of the Office of the
Bangsamoro Director-General for Madaris Education in the Ministry of
Education for Basic, Higher, and Technical Education in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) to administer, supervise,
and regulate the madrasah educational system and to provide assistance and
mechanisms in order to improve the quality of Madrasah education in the
entire region.

In view of the foregoing immediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

“AN ACT CREATING THE OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO
DIRECTOR-GENERAL FOR MADARIS EDUCATION IN THE MINISTRY OF
EDUCATION AND TO STRENGTHEN AND INSTITUTIONALIZE MADARIS
EDUCATION AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER
PURPOSES.”

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session
assembled:

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE. This Act shall be known as the “Madrasah
Education Director-General Office Act of 2019” of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.

SEC. 2. DECLARATION OF POLICY. It is hereby declared a policy of the
. Bangsamoro Autonomous Government under Section 16, Republic Act No.
11054 otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) that the
Bangsamoro Government shall develop an educational framework relevant and
responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people.

Section 18 of the said law further provides that, the Bangsamoro
Government shall establish, maintain and supervise Madaris Education in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. In the case of Madaris teachers (also called
Asatidz), the Civil Service Commission shall promulgate rules and regulations
to set the standards for their qualifications, appointments, and promotions.

As mandated further in the BOL, the Bangsamoro Government shall
ensure the integration in the elementary and high school education curricula
the teaching of Islamic and Arabic studies for Muslim pupils and students in
public schools. For this purpose, the Bangsamoro Parliament shall enact
legislations to develop and strengthen the Madaris educational system in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

SEC. 3. OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO DIRECTOR-GENERAL FOR
MADARIS EDUCATION (OBDGME). There is hereby created an Office of the
Bangsamoro Director-General for Madaris Education who shall be responsible
_ for the maintenance and supervision of Madaris education in the Bangsamoro.

It shall also be vested with authority, accountability and responsibility
for ensuring access to, promoting equity in, and improving the quality of
Madaris Education in the Bangsamoro as provided in Section 2 hereof.

SEC. 4. COMPOSITION OF THE OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO
DIRECTOR-GENERAL FOR MADARIS EDUCATION. The Office of the
Bangsamoro Director-General shall be composed of two sub-offices: Director
for Technical Services (DTS) and Director for Administrative and Finance
Services (DAFS).

Each of the sub-offices shall be headed by a director that shall be a
career executive officer chosen from among the Ministry of Education
personnel. The purpose of which is to maintain stability of the office in case of
leadership changes, continuity and smooth operation of the office and effective
delivery of its powers, functions and duties.

DTS shall have five (5) divisions: Curriculum and Instruction Division,
Technical Support Division, Teaching, Learning and Learner Support Services
Division, Planning, Policy and Research Division, and Accreditation and
Quality Assurance Division.

DAFS shall have two (2) divisions: The Administrative and Finance
Divisions.

Each division shall have its own programs, activities, and projects (PAPs).

SEC. 5. POWERS, DUTIES AND FUNCTIONS OF THE OFFICE OF THE
BANGSAMORO DIRECTOR-GENERAL FOR MADARIS EDUCATION. The
Office of the Bangsamoro Director-General for Madaris Education shall have
the following functions:

a.) Maintain and support a complete and integrated system of quality
education;

b.) Develop and strengthen the Madaris Educational System in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

c.) Define and develop an educational Madaris policy framework relevant
and responsive to the needs, ideas and aspirations of the Bangsamoro
people and the communities thery serve;

d.) Develop a comprehensive Madaris education plan;

e.) Develop a Madaris regional educational standards with a view towards
benchmarking for international competitiveness;

f.) Monitor, evaluate and assess regional learning outcomes for Madaris
education;

g.) Formulate, adopt and evaluate plans, programs, activities, projects,
educational standards and curricular designs related to madaris
education;

h.)Establish linkages and cooperation with Islamic educational
organizations, academic institutions, and cultural centers both local
and abroad;

i.) Establish a system of recognition, accreditation and standardization
of madaris and professionalization of asatidz (plural of ustadz);

j.) Establish capacity development programs for asatidz for their
continuing professional development;

k.) Coordinate and work closely with the Civil Service Commission to

Madaris in the region;

o.) Carry out infrastructure development programs for duly accredited

p.) Perform such other functions as may be necessary to ensure efficient
implementation of Madrasah educational programs in the region.

SEC. 6. FUNDING. Education being the highest priority in the
Bangsamoro, the funds necessary for the implementation of this Act shall not
be less than 2% of the Annual Block Grant provided by the National
Government to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. This
is to ensure that development of Madaris Education will be given top priority by
the Bangsamoro Government.

SEC. 7. IMPLEMENTING RULES AND REGULATIONS. The Ministry of
Education for Basic, Higher, and Technical Education is hereby mandated to
formulate within 60 days from the passage of this act, the Implementing Rules
and Regulations (IRR) in coordination with the appropriate committees for its
effective and immediate implementation.

SEC. 8. REPEALING CLAUSE. Any provision of regional laws, orders
and administrative issuances which are in conflict or inconsistent with the
provisions and/or purposes of this Act are hereby repealed, revoked or
modified accordingly.

SEC. 9. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE. In the event that any part or parts of
this Act is declared unconstitutional, other parts not so declared shall remain
valid and effective.

SEC. 10. EFFECTIVITY. This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days
from the date of its publication in at least two (2) newspapers of general
circulation in the Bangsamoro region.
