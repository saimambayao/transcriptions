EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Philippines is a nation blessed with great potential in sports, as it is an integral
element of our culture. Many Filipino athletes triumphed and bagged various awards
and medals in amateur, Olympic and professional competitions in individual and team
sports. With the common goal to contribute to national pride, sports development
promotes intensive sports training and high quality education at the local level through
our local government units.

Through Executive Order (EO) No. 01 dated August 20, 2001, the Regional Sports
Coordinating Office (RSCO) in the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, was
created, pursuant to Republic Act (RA) No. 6734 (as amended by RA No. 9054).
Furthermore, Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 287 expanded and strengthened the
powers and functions of the RSCO, consistent with then Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao’s declaration of policy “to develop and promote sports to foster physical and
mental prowess and inculcate values of sportsmanship, understanding, cooperation,
teamwork, camaraderie and goodwill.”

With the successful enactment of RA No. 11054 otherwise known as “An Act Providing
for the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao,
Article IX , Section 20 of the same allows the Bangsamoro Parliament to pass a law
creating a Bangsamoro Sports Commission (BSC) which shall continue to uphold sports
development in the newly created autonomous region. To further operationalize this
goal and manage its transition, the Interim Chief Minister issued Executive Order No.
004, s. 2019 amending EO No. 01, s.2001 allowing the transition of the RSCO into the
Bangsamoro Sports Commission (BSC).

However, despite the transformation of the RSCO into the BSC, it could not fully operate
as it should during the period of transition due to the limited funding and personnel
restrictions. The BSC had to make do with the meager appropriation for the RSCO in the
previous administration that eventually hindered the implementation of programs and
projects that could have immediately impacted on the success of the newly-installed
Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA).

This bill does not only comply with the directive above-mentioned, but also seeks to
reiterate the BSC’s mandate to sustain an integrated regional sports promotion and
development program in the BARMM. It also strengthens the agency by further defining
its functions of the BSC to coordinate with non-governmental organizations and the
private sector in pursuit of its mandate.

This bill endeavors to give the BSC a more active role in the Bangsamoro bureaucracy,
which is no longer merely limited to sports coordination. It likewise addresses the
limited funding for the plans, programs and sports projects to be implemented by the
BSC.

It should be noted that this bill was forged through revisiting the relevant provisions of
past and existing laws on sports policies in the country and in the region.

With the aforementioned, the approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

AN ACT PROMOTING THE DEVELOPMENT OF SPORTS IN THE
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION, ESTABLISHING THE
BANGSAMORO SPORTS COMMISSION, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR
AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament
assembled, as follows:

Section 1. Title of the Act. - This Act shall be known as “The Bangsamoro Sports
Commission Act of 2019”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the policy of the Bangsamoro government
to promote physical education, encourage and sustain the development of sports in the
country to foster physical fitness, self-discipline, teamwork and excellence for the
development of a healthy and alert citizenry.

Attuned with this, the Bangsamoro Government aims to develop and promote sports to
foster physical and mental prowess and inculcate values of sportsmanship,
understanding, cooperation, teamwork, camaraderie and goodwill. As such, it shall
encourage and support sports programs, league competitions, indigenous games,
martial arts, and amateur sports, including training for regional, national, and
international competitions.

Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Sports Commission (BSC). — In
pursuance of the above mentioned policy, the Bangsamoro Sports Commission,
hereinafter referred to as the Commission, is hereby created which shall be the lead
agency to implement the provisions of this Act. For purposes of general policy and
program coordination, supervision and direction, the BSC shall remain a regional
agency attached to the Office of the Chief Minister.

Section 4. Objectives of the Commission.- The Commission shall have the
following objectives:

(a) To provide leadership, formulate the policies and set the priorities and direction
of BARMM’s sports promotion and development, particularly giving emphasis on
grassroots participation and sports excellence;

(b) To encourage wide participation of all sectors, government and private, in sports
promotion and development;

(c) To support Bangsamoro athletes in their quest to bring honor and glory to the
Bangsamoro;

(d) To conduct basic and applied research relevant to physical education and sports;

(e) To establish and maintain linkages with international and national sports
development institutions and organizations for continuing upgrading of sports
manpower of the Bangsamoro people; and

(f) To perform such other functions and exercise such other powers necessary to

Section 5. Mandate of the Commission. - The Commission is mandated to sustain
an integrated regional sports promotion and development program in the BARMM.?

The Commission shall design and assist in the implementation of programs such as but
not limited to seminars and congresses for the purpose of promoting sports and
maintaining and supporting a pool of athletes in the Bangsamoro region.

Section 6. Powers and Functions of the Commission. — The Commission shall
have the following powers and functions:

(a) Plan, implement and oversee the integrated sports promotion and development
program for the region;

(b) Coordinate and maintain linkages with the Philippine Sports Commission,
Manila, National Sports Commission or organizations of other countries, and
other non- governmental organizations, local or foreign, whose main objective is
sports promotion and development;

(c) Establish and maintain fully-equipped sports facilities and centers in strategic
places in the area of autonomy and supervise the management and maintenance
thereof;

(d) Formulate rules and regulations for the different sports competition defining and
delineating areas of responsibilities of all sectors involved in sports promotion
and development;

(e) Provide such incentives, recognition and awards to deserving athletes and other
persons and entities involved in or supporting sports development as may be
permissible under the rules of amateurism;

(f) Undertake basic and applied research on sports development, particularly the
promotion and preservation of indigenous sports;

(g) Assist in the establishment of regional, provincial, municipal and barangay or
school district sports promotion and development councils, which shall initiate,
conduct and coordinate sports activities in their respective jurisdictions;

(h) Assist LGUs in promoting sports at the community level, especially those
included in the calendar of events of regional, national and international
competitions; and

(i) Perform such other functions as may be provided by law.

Section 7. Composition of the Commission and Term of Office.— The
Commission En Banc shall be composed of a Chairperson and two (2) Commissioners,
all of whom shall be appointed by the Chief Minister.

The term of office of the Chairperson and the two (2) Commissioners shall be co-
terminus with the appointing Chief Minister: Provided, however, That they shall remain
in office until their replacements shall have been appointed or an Officer-in-Charge has
been designated.

Section 8. Qualifications of the Chairperson and the Commissioners. — The
Chairperson and the Commissioners must be citizens of the Philippines, residents of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, bonafide members of associations or an advocacy
group pushing for the rights, welfare and development of the field of sports. They must

Section 9. The Executive Director, Functions Term of Office. - There shall be
an Executive Director, appointed by the Chief Minister, who shall be responsible for
implementing the policies, rules, regulations and directives of the Commission and shall
direct and supervise its day-to-day operation. The Executive Director shall have
supervision and control over the Coordination and Support Services Unit, and
Administrative and Finance Services Unit.

The specific functions of the Executive Director are as follows:

a. Execute the policies and measures approved by the Commission En Banc and
be responsible for the efficient and effective day-to-day management of the
operations of the BSC.

b. Prepare the annual budget for the operations of BSC for submission to the
Commission En Banc;

c. Prepare an annual report on the accomplishments of BSC for submission to
the Commission En Banc, the Parliament and other appropriate agencies of
the Bangsamoro Government;

d. Perform such other duties as may be assigned to him by the Commission En
Banc.

The Executive Director shall serve for a fixed term of four (4) years, subject to
reappointment.

Section 10. Qualifications of Executive Director. — No person shall be appointed
as Executive Director of the Commission unless a citizen of the Philippines, a esident of
the Bangsamoro autonomous region, at least 30 years of age, of good moral character a
college graduate, and has at least three (3) years of relevant experience in any of the
following fields: sports development, law; economics; public administration,
commerce; management; social services; or finance.

Section 11. Functions and Responsibilities of Officers and Staff.-— The
Administrative and Finance Services Unit shall be primarily responsible for providing
services related to financial management, personnel, records, supplies and equipment,
while the Coordination and Support Services Unit shall be primarily responsible for
plans and programs formulation, research, public information and production,
transportation, communications and such other functions and responsibilities as may be
necessary and required by the Commission.

Section 12. Appointment of Staff and Employees of the Commission. — The
staff, officers and employees of the Commission shall be appointed by the Chairperson
of the Commission.

Section 13.Assistance by Bangsamoro Government Entities.-— The
Commission may call upon any Bangsamoro Government entity for assistance in the
performance of its functions and duties. All heads of ministries, agencies, and offices of
the Bangsamoro Government are hereby enjoined to render full assistance and
cooperation to the Commission to ensure the attainment of its objectives and the
success of the regional sports development program.

Section 14. Appropriations. — The financial requirements to implement this Act and
to support the Commission’s operations, in pursuit of its sports development programs,
shall be covered by an annual appropriation taken from the Annual Block Grant, passed
by the Bangsamoro Parliament.

Section 15. Transitory Provision. — Upon approval of this Act, the incumbent
Chairperson and the two (2) Commissioners, as well as the Executive Director,
appointed by the interim Chief Minister shall continue to office until the expiration of
their term as provided herein.

During the transition period under the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, appointed
officials and employees of the Commission who do not have the required eligibility shall
be granted a reasonable period within which to meet the eligibility requirements,
pending which they shall be allowed to continue discharging their duties.

Section 16. Repealing Clause. — All previous laws, Executive Orders, other decrees,
and rules and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.

Section 17. Separability Clause. — Should any provision of this Act be found
unconstitutional by a court of law, such provision shall be severed from the remainder of
this Act, and such action shall not affect the enforceability of the remaining provisions of
this Act.

Section 19. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Official Gazette or in two (2) newspapers of general circulation in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
