EXPLANATORY NOTE

Planning and_ policy-making are crucial in the socio-economic
development of a region. The defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(ARMM) has its Regional Economic and Development Planning Board (REDPB)
which is the highest planning and policy-making body. During the transition
phase of the Bangsamoro Government, it is very important to put in place a
structure like the REDPB that will provide direction to its overall development.

It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to protect and improve the
quality of life of its inhabitants. In view of this policy, the Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (hereinafter “BOL”)
emphasized the importance of careful planning in the development of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Hence, Section 4, Article XIII thereof provides
for the establishment of the Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council
(hereinafter “Council”) which shall serve as the planning, monitoring, and
coordinating agency for all development plans, programs, and projects of the
Bangsamoro Government. This bill seeks to establish said Council.

With this bill, the Council shall serve as a primary institution that sets and
guides the direction of comprehensive socio-economic development effort and
other development concerns and priorities in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. To ensure people’s participation, the Council shall take into account the
development plans of the provinces, cities, municipalities, and barangays as
approved by their respective local development councils.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of the bill is earnestly requested.

Be it enacted by the members of the BTA Parliament of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “The
Bangsamoro Planning Act of 2019.”

Sec. 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to protect and improve the quality of life of its inhabitants
through careful planning of the development of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. It is hereby also declared that the Bangsamoro Government shall
provide equitable opportunities for the development of constituent local
government units and shall strengthen governance systems to ensure

inclusivity and people's participation.

Sec. 3. Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council. — There is
hereby created and established the Bangsamoro Economic and Development
Council, hereinafter referred to as the “Council’, which shall serve as the

primary institution that sets and guides the direction of comprehensive socio-

Sec. 4. Composition. — The Council shall be composed of the following:

a) The Chief Minister;

in accordance with its rules and regulations to be prescribed by it,
and to be appointed by the Chairman upon selection.

Provided that, the Chief Minister shall act as Chairman of the Council,
preside over its meetings, and perform such other duties and functions as
may be necessary in the conduct of the Council affairs.

Provided further that, the Vice-Chairman shall be selected by the
majority of the members of the Council from among themselves to act as
Chairman in the absence of the latter and to perform such other duties as may
be assigned to him by the Council.

Provided further that, membership in the Council shall be coterminous
with the appointing authority.

Provided finally that, the members mentioned in subparagraph (i) shall
comprise one fourth (1/4) of the membership in the Council, and that the

youth, women, business, and the academe are represented therein.

Sec. 6. Council Meeting. - The Council shall meet as often as may be
necessary but not less than once every quarter. It shall meet at such time,
place and date as may be determined and fixed by the Council; Provided that,
there shall be, in all its meetings, prior notices served to all members thereof.

Special meetings may be called by the Chairman or at the request of
the majority of the members of the Council; Provided that, notices specifying
the agenda to be taken up shall be served at least three (3) days before the
special meeting.

A simple majority of the members of the Council shall constitute a
quorum to do business. In no case shall proxy be allowed in any meeting of
the Council be it regular or special. The Council shall adopt its own internal
rules and regulations in the conduct of its business. Every Council member
shall be entitled to traveling expenses and honorarium for every meeting

attended in an amount to be fixed by the Council.

Sec. 7. Executive Committee. - The Council shall constitute an
Executive Committee, hereinafter referred to as the “Execom’”, from among its
members to act for and on its behalf when the Council is not in session on
urgent matters as may be deemed necessary by the Chairman. To ensure
equitable representation and participation the following shall comprise the

Execom:

a) The Chairman of the Council

b) One (1) Deputy Chief Minister

c) The Speaker

d) Chairmen of the Sectoral Committees of the Council
e) One (1) Provincial Governor

f) One (1) City Mayor

g) One (1) Private Sector Representative

Provided that, the members of the Execom mentioned under
subparagraphs (b), (e), (f), and (g) shall be designated by the Chairman.

Sec. 8. Sectoral Committees. - To assist the Council in the
performance of its functions, it shall create its Sectoral Committees with
members from the Bangsamoro government ministries, agencies and offices,
and private sector representatives. The Sectoral Committees shall include the

following:

a) Economic Development Committee
b) Social Development Committee
c) Infrastructure Development Committee

d) Governance and Development Administration Committee

The Council may create such other Committees as it may deem
necessary for the effective operation and functioning thereof. The Sectoral

Committees shall have the following functions:

a) Assist the Council in the analysis and translation of the national
development goals and policies into specific sectoral objectives of
development plan and programs of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region;

b) Coordinate all sectoral planning and programming activities of sectoral
ministries, regional agencies and offices and the local government
units;

c) Provide updated sectoral data and statistical information and assist in
the preparation of annual Bangsamoro regional development report
and accomplishment; and

d) Perform other functions as may be directed by the Council.

Sec. 9. Technical Support Office. — There is hereby created a
Technical Support Office, hereinafter referred to as “TSO”, that shall assist the

Council in the discharge of its duties and responsibilities.

The TSO shall be headed by an Executive Director who shall be
assisted by a Deputy Executive Director, both of whom shall be appointed by
the Chief Minister. The TSO shall be composed of four (4) technical divisions
and one (1) administrative and finance division.

Sec. 10. Appropriations. - The amount necessary to finance the initial
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current year’s (2019)
appropriations of former ARMM. Thereafter, the regular budget of the Council
shall be included in the Bangsamoro General Appropriations Act (BGAA) for

its continued operations and functioning.

Sec.11. Separability. - If for any cause any part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of R.A. 11054, the remainder of

this Act or any provision not affected thereby shall remain in force and effect.

Sec. 12. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days
following the completion of its publication in a newspaper of general

circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
