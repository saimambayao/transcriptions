EXPLANATORY NOTES:

The Subject of this Bill is an “Act requiring all students in Public and Private Schools, from
Grade six (6) to Grade Twelve (12) AND GRADUATING COLLEGE STUDENTS to plant at least two
(2) trees each before officially considered enrolled and another two (2) trees before it shall be
certified candidates for graduation, respectively, and; APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREOF.

SHORT TITLE OF THE BILL: “ BARMM Tree Planting Act”.

Between 1990 and 2005, the Philippines lost 32.3 percent of its forest cover. Illegal logging has
historically been a huge issue for the country,including in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region,
but now the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), and the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao, is taking from its shoulder that moral duty to include that policy direction
as an integral component in the pursuit of the Chief Minister’s Moral Governance.

The BTA through this representation is putting forward a new way of dealing with the climate
change problem, through a comprehensive Tree Planting law/ Act, a bill that requires students
from Grade 9 to Grade 12 to plant two (2) trees each every time they register for enrolment
and before they are officially considered entrolled and as a requirements for graduation,
respectively.

This proposed BTA Parliament Bill, entitled the BARMM Tree Planting Act', has been proposed
by this representation to strengthen the climate change policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Government, through Tree Planting Program. The bill is now submitted for deliberation before
this august body and approval.

"This initiative, if properly implemented, will ensure that at least 22 million new trees would be
planted each year. In the course of one generation, no less than 110 million can be planted
under this initiative,"

“Even with a survival rate of only 10 percent, this would mean an additional 110,000 trees
would be available for the youth to enjoy, when they assume the mantle of leadership in the
future."

Since 2015, we have actually seen a slight increase in forested area in the Philippines thanks to
government Anti-Logging initiatives and increased law enforcement tackling the country's
widespread illegal logging.

Introduced by MP RASUL ENDEREZ ISMAEL

An Act
requiring all Students in Public and Private Schools, from Grade Six (6) to Grade Twelve (12) and
graduating College Students within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to plant
at least Two (2) Trees each towards environmental preservation and appropriating funds thereof.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, Parliament, of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao, assembled;

Section 1.- This Act shall be known as the “BARMM Tree Planting Act”

Section 2 — It is the declared policy of the State to protect and advance the right of the Filipino people
to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature. Towards this end,
the State shall promote and pursue reforestration programs and encourage tree planting as a means of
involving the youth in public and civic affairs.

Section 3 — Tree Planting as a requirement for Enrolment and Graduation.- instilled that moral treats of
Social Responsibility, all students from Grade Six (6) to Grade Twelve (12), whether in private or public
schools shall be required to plan at least two (2) trees per student before officially considered enrolled
in their respective Grade Level. In the same manner, graduating college students, whether in public or
private educational institutions, shall be required to plan at least two (2) trees before it shall be certified
candidates for graduation. Every year thereafter, students from Grade six (6) to Grade twelve (12), and
all graduating college students in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall participate in a Tree
Planting program, as a responsible participating citizens.

Section 4. Tree Planting Sites — the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education, Ministry of
Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) and the concerned local Government Units (LGUs)
shall determine the appropriate tree planting sites within the Local Government ‘s respective areas, in
coordination with schools, colleges and universities. It is unlawful for any person, organization or
corporation to remove or cut trees in the designated Tree Planting Sites.

Section 5. — Preference for Indigenous Species. — The species of trees to be planted should be
appropriate to the location, climate and topography of the area, with preference for the planting of
indigenous species. Provided, in areas such as: Coastal area, mangroves; riverbanks, bamboo trees; in
plain and hilly areas, Narra, mahogany and, acacia trees are suggested.

Section 6. — Partner agencies and Stakeholders.- The Ministry of Basic Higher and Technical Education
(MBHTE) shall be the lead implementing Agency pertinent to the provisions of this Act, in partnership
with the following BARMM Agencies:

(a) Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE)
(b) Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform ( MAFAR)
(c) Ministry of Interior, and Local Local Government (MILG)

(d) Ministry of Science and technology (MOST)

(e) Ministry of Indigenous People’s Affairs (MIPA)

The responsibilities of these agencies shall include:

1.) Establishment of nursery and seedling preparation
2.) Site identification and preparation

3.) ServicesTechnical support and extension services
4.) Monitoring and Evaluation

Section 7 Provisions of Seedlings - The Community Environment and Natural Resources (CENRO) of the
Ministry of Environment, Natural resource and Energy (MENRE) and the Ministry of Agriculture,
Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), in partnership with LGUs throughout the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region shall maintain a seedling bank that will serve as a ready source of seedling to be
used in the implementation of this Act.

Section 8 Implementating Rules and Regulations — The Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical
Education (MBHTE) in coordination with MENRE and MILG, and in consultation with other agencies, shall
promulgate and issue the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) to Implement this Act within (60)
Days after its effectivity.

Section 9. Any person who violates any provision of this Act or any rule or regulation promulgated
thereunder shall be punished with a fine of not more than Ten Thousand pesos or, in appropriate cases,
with disqualification to acquire or enjoy any privilege granted exclusively to citizens of the Philippines,
such as the acquisition either through sale, free patent, hemestead, or lease of public lands, enjoyment
of a franchise to own or operate a public utility or the grant of lease, license or a privilege involving
natural resource; and for a period of five years be disqualified to hold public office, to graduate from
any educational institutition at all level, to take any bar, board or civil service examination, and to
practice any profession licensed and regulated by the Supreme Court or the Professional Regulation
Commission.

The persons hereinabove required to plant trees shall take good care of them, and from time to time,
remove any tree planted by them in their respective areas which has grown very old, is diseased, or
defective, and replant with trees their respective areas whenever necessary.
