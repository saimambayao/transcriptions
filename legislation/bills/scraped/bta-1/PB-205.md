EXPLANATORY NOTE

It is no less than Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Constitution that guarantees the
protection and promotion of “the right to health of the people” and mandates the State to “Instill
health consciousness among them”.

In the same tenor, Section 22, Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, provides that “the
Bangsamoro Government shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill
health consciousness among them. it shall also uphold the peoples’ right to have access to essential
goods health, and other social services that promote their well-being”;

Further, section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the
Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), provides that “the Bangsamoro Government shall exercise the
power expressly granted, those necessarily implied therefrom, as well as powers necessary,

The Betel Nut (“Moma” or “Mama”) is a betel nut and leaf concoction or quid with
tobacco, the chewing of which produces blood-red spittle. The practice of betel chewing used to
be prevalent throughout the Philippines from the mountains in the north to the Muslim
communities in the south. The tribal people of the Cordillera, commonly called Igorots, carry their
containers around their waists or in little baskets specially made to parcel betel. Most of the men,
especially the older ones, chew betel constantly.

A research article made in Zamboanga City which was published in 2019 informs us of the
health risk in the use of betel nut among frequent chewers. Accordingly, this practice leads to oral
submucous fibrosis which is attributed to the presence of the active alkaloid arecoline present in
the betel nut. Individuals with oral submucous fibrosis are at high-risk for precancerous conditions
which may develop into malignancy at a rate of 7.6%.

A study made by the World Health Organization (WHO) shows that under The
International Agency for Cancer Research classifies betel nut as a Group | carcinogen. Smokeless
tobacco use is associated with cancers of the oral cavity, pancreas and esophagus. The nicotine in
tobacco also leads to addiction, making it more difficult to give up the betel nut chewing when
mixed with tobacco. In view of this, a technical report which was published by WHO in 2012,
which establishes a platform of action in the control of betel nut and tobacco use was created.

Up to this date there are no existing laws that regulate the use of betel nut or mama within
the BARMM area. Even though one of the components in betel nut use is tobacco which is being
regulated under R.A. 9211;

In view of the practice of Betel Nut (“Moma or Mama”) use and consumption within the
localities and provinces, the government has to take necessary measures - such as regulation of
using betel nut, in preventing higher health risks in frequent consumption of betel nut within the
region.

This bill is being presented as a solution to an existing health risk which haunts our
constituents without them getting informed of the risks of said practice.

In view of the foregoing, this representation is seeking for the early passage of this bill into law.

SECTION 2. Purposes. -

a. To preserve the cultural and tribal practice of consuming or chewing mama in all its
forms, with or without tobacco

b. To protect the corals and seashells and other environmental resources from unnecessary
and irresponsible utilization and destruction

c. To protect the people of the BAR from the ill-effects of the betel nut use or consumption
and the irresponsible and unregulated disposal of their residues;

d. To regulate the use, consumption, sale and trade of mamah, tobacco and its other
components

SECTION 3. Definitions. - As used in this Act, the following shall be understood as follows:

(a) “Act” shall mean the Mamah Control Act of 2022

(b) “Bangkit” or lime, either in orange, yellow or red color, use as one of the ingredients in betel
nut chewing or “mamah”.

(c) “Betel Nut” shall refer either to the husk, fruit or seed, of an immature nut of the areca palm,
locally known as “bunga” that is used in betel nut chewing.

(d) “Business” includes every trade, occupation, profession, and other lawful purpose, whether or
not carried on for profit.

(e)“Buyuh” refers to the leaf of the betel pepper vine used to wrap the other ingredients of the betel
nut chew.

(f)“Employee” means any individual, who under the usual common law rules applicable in
determining the employer-employee relationship, has the status of an employee.

(g) “Employer” includes any person acting directly or indirectly in the interest of an employer in
relation to an employee.

(h) “Entity” includes corporation and foreign corporation; nonprofit corporation; profit and
nonprofit unincorporated associations; business, trust, estate, partnership, trust, and two or more
persons having a joint or common economic interest; and state, United States and foreign
government.

(1) “MTIT” means the Ministry of Trade Investment and Tourism.

(j)“Person” shall mean any natural person, corporation, firm, partnership, association, institution,
joint-stock company, trust, other organizations of persons, or any other legal entity.

(k) “Minister” shall mean the Minister of MTIT.

(1)“minor” shall mean any natural person under 18 years of age.

(m) “senior” shall mean any person under 70 years of age.

(n) “tobacco consumption area” refers to open and or properly ventilated areas designated for the
common purpose of tobacco and betel nut consumption

(o)“spittoon” refers to any recyclable and non-transparent receptacle required by this law to be
used by consumers, chewers or users of mama’ or betel nut, with or without tobacco, as personal
disposal unit of their saliva or spits and other waste.

SECTION 4. Ban on Betel Nut Consumption in Public Places —Consumption, chewing of betel
nut and any of its components, with or without tobacco, shall be absolutely prohibited in the
following public places:

a. Centers of youth activity such as playschools, preparatory schools, elementary schools, high
schools, colleges and universities, youth hostels and recreational facilities for persons under
eighteen (18) years old;

b. Elevators and stairwells;

c. Within the buildings and premises of public and private hospitals, medical, dental, and optical
clinics, health centers, nursing homes, dispensaries and laboratories;

d. Public conveyances and public facilities including airport and ship terminals and train and bus
stations, restaurants and conference halls, except for separate smoking areas;

e. Public office, center or building, regardless of its ownership and use, including other
infrastructures, except those that are open and without roofing;

SECTION 5. Designated Tobacco Consumption Areas. — Designated smoking areas pursuant
to Republic Act No. 9211 are hereby additionally designated as betel nut consumption areas and
shall be hereinafter referred to as tobacco consumption area. Provided, that in general public,
private workplaces and other places where no such place has been designated, the owner,
proprietor, operator, possessor, manager or administrator of such places shall establish a tobacco
consumption area. Such area may be within the building, but must be an open space or separate
area with proper ventilation and disposal facility for tobacco and betel nut waste.

All designated tobacco consumption areas shall have at least one (1) legible and visible sign posted,
namely “TOBACCO CONSUMPTION AREA” for the information and guidance of all concerned.
In addition, the sign or notice posted shall include a warning about the health effects of direct or
secondhand exposure to tobacco smoke and betel-nut consumption.

All other areas, not so designated are hereby considered as NON-SMOKING, NON BETEL NUT
CONSUMPTION AREAS.

SECTION 6. Minimum Age Sales — It shall be unlawful:

a. For any licensed retailer of betel nut products to sell or distribute betel nuts and its component
ingredient to any minor;

b. For any person to purchase betel nuts and its component ingredient from a minor;

c. For a minor to sell or buy betel nuts and its component ingredient t; and

d. For a minor to use or consume betel nuts and its component ingredient, with or without tobacco.

Prior to the sale, the licensee shall determine the actual age of the buyer based on valid
government identification card presented by the latter.

SECTION 7. Condition of Premises: Betel Nut Display and Required Sign. —

(1) A person or business entity shall ensure that betel nut and its component ingredients for
consumption, like lime and tobacco, are not openly displayed or in any manner displayed in
any part of the business premises which are generally open to the public. Such packages or
bags of betel nut and/or its components shall be secured underneath or behind the cashier
counter at the point of sale, to be accessed only by the owner or an authorized agent (cashier)
of the retail establishment upon request from a person of legal age to purchase such item or
product.

(2) Any person or business entity which sells or permits to be sold on its premises betel nut shall

SECTION 8. Sale of Betel Nut Products Within School Perimeters.—The sale or distribution of
betel nuts and any of the component ingredient for betel nut consumption, including tobacco, is
prohibited within one hundred (100) meters from any point of the perimeter of a school, public
playground or other facility frequented particularly by minors.

SECTION 9. Consumption. — Any person who consumes or chews betel-nut and any of its
component ingredients, with or without tobacco, shall do so only in the designated “TOBACCO
CONSUMPTION AREA” and shall keep within himself a personal spittoon, which shall be non-
transparent. The spittoon shall be tightly capped or closed at all times, except during its actual
usage.

It shall be unlawful for any person chewing betel-nut or any of its component to spit-out
the same, the juice thereof or its residue or waste to anything other than his personal spittoon.

SECTION 10. Transportation of Betel Nut. — The transportation or bringing into the BAR of
betel-nut or mamah, tobacco and/or any of its components from outside of the region is prohibited.
Provided, that licensed trader may secure an additional permit to bring betel-nut or mamah and
tobacco into the BAR from the MTIT, subject to such rules and regulations that it may formulate
pursuant to this Act.

SECTION 11. Disposal of Betel-nut, its components and spittoon. — The betel-nut, any of its
component, tobacco, spittoon shall be disposed of in the trash bins located in the designated
TOBACCO DISPOSAL AREA. It shall be unlawful to dispose of the betel-nut, its components,
tobacco, or any of its waste and residue, spittoon or its contents in public places, roads, streets,
alleys, lagoons, rivers, canals and the like.

SECTION 12. Licensing requirement. - A person or business entity shall be required to obtain a
Betel Nut Control License issued by the Ministry of Trade Industry and Tourism prior to engaging
in the importation and/or selling of betel nut in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

SECTION 13. Duration of license - Upon payment of required license fees, each license shall be
for a period of one (1) year commencing on the date of its approval by the Director of MTIT in the
province where said business operates.

Director considering an application for a Betel Nut Control License, a thorough investigation into
an applicant's background will be conducted. The Minister or his/her designee may inquire into an
applicant’s:

(1) moral character;

(b) Not to openly display the betel-nut and any of the component ingredient for its

SECTION 18. Fines, Penalties, and Suspension and Revocation of Licenses. - (a) Any person
or business entity that offers, gives, or permits to be given or sold any betel nut to an individual
under the age of 18 (a minor) shall, upon the issuance of a notice of violation to the owner, agent,
or employee in charge at the time of violation by any representative of the MTIT, law enforcement
officer, or any member of the Sangguniang Barangay, who has properly identified himself or
herself, shall be fined and penalized within a period of two years, in the following manner:

(1) First Offense: A fine of not less than 100 but not more than 300.

(2) Second Offense: A fine of not less than 300 but not more than 500.

(3) Third Offense: A maximum fine of 500 and revocation of license up to one year.

(4) Any person, parent or legal guardian, of any minor who suffers or permits to allow by either
willfully or negligently, such persons under the age of 18 years old to carry, possess, or consume
betel nut, shall be penalized and pay a maximum fine of 300.

(5) Any person(s) or business establishment found to be operating and selling betel nut without a
valid Betel Nut Control License issued by the MTIT, in violation of Section 1 shall pay a maximum
fine of 500.

(6) Any person or business entity who violates any other provisions of this Act shall be penalized
and pay a maximum fine of 500.

(b) Suspension and Revocation of Licenses. A Betel Nut Control License may be suspended
and/or revoked for any of the following reasons:
(1) Where the continuation of a license would be contrary to the public interest;
(2) Violation of, causing or permitting a violation of, or failure or refusal by a licensee to
comply with any provision of this Act and/or these regulations;
(3) Misrepresentation of a material fact by an applicant in obtaining or renewing a license;
(4) A plea, verdict, or judgment of guilty to any public offense involving moral turpitude.

SECTION 18. Confiscation and disposal of confiscated Betel Nut “Areca Nut”. - In all cases of
violation of this Act, the Betel Nut and its component ingredient for consumption, including
tobacco shall be confiscated and immediately destroyed and disposed of.

SECTION 19. IRR. - Within Sixty days from the effectivity of this Act, the MTIT, in coordination
with the MOH and the BAGO is hereby mandated to promulgate the Implementing Rules and
Regulations necessary for the proper implementation of this Act.

SECTION 20. Separability Clause. - If any of the sections or provisions of this Act is adjudged
invalid, all its other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in force.
