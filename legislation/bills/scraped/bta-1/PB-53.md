EXPLANATORY NOTE

Sustainable Development is a worldwide concern and so the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. The concept of sustainable development
has undergone various development phases since its introduction. The
history of sustainable development has started as early as in 1962 up to
date. On September 27, 2015, the UN member-states adopted a new set
of goals for the next 15 years. These goals, known as the Sustainable
Development Goals (SDGs) 2030, aim to build on the gaps of the MDGs,
fill in the gaps, and address new challenges. Thereafter, the National
Government has subsequently crafted Philippine Development Plan (PDP)
2017-2022 and recognized that it needed to transform the world by
ensuring sustainable development and leaving no one behind in order to
live “THE LIFE WE WANT”.

In line with this and by virtue of “Inclusivity Principle”, the right of
the Bangsamoro people to healthful and ecological balance recognized by
no less than the 1987 Philippine Constitution and R.A. 11054 must be
protected more particularly, the rights of the next generations. !n fact, the
future generations are the concerns to be taken care of not only by the
people but also by the Bangsamoro Government to beh and sustain the
environment. alicia ie Tas

Republic Act 11054 has mandated the parliament to create
Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board composed of representatives
from the National Government and the Bangsamoro Government. The
Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board shall ensure the integration
and harmonization of economic, social and environmental considerations
as vital dimensions of sustainable development policy and practice in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Hence, this bill seeks to establish the Bangsamoro Sustainable
Development Board, defining its powers and functions, providing funds
therefor and for other purposes.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of the bill is earnestly requested.

Sec. 3. Bangsamoro Sustainable Development Board. - There is
hereby created and established the Bangsamoro Sustainable Development
Board, hereinafter referred to as the “Board”, which shall ensure the
integration and harmonization of economic, social, and environmental
considerations as vital dimensions of sustainable development policy and

practice in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

b) Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform;
c) Minister of Interior and Local Government;

d) Minister of Science and Technology;

Sec. 6. Technical Support Staff: - There is hereby created a Technical
Support Staff, hereinafter referred to as “TSS” that shall assist the Board

in the discharge of its duties and responsibilities.

The TSS shall be under the Ministry of Environment, Natural
Resources and Energy and shal! be headed by a Director. The TSS shall

be composed of technical, administrative and finance division.

Sec. 7. Board Meeting. — The Board shall meet at least once every
quarter at such time, place and date as may be determined and fixed by
it. Provided that, there shall be, in all its meetings, prior notices served to

all the members thereof.

A simple majority of the members of the Board shall constitute a
quorum to do business. The Board shall adopt its own internal rules and
regulations in the conduct of its business. Every member of the Board
shall be entitled to traveling expenses and honorarium for every meeting

attended in an amount to be fixed by the Board.

Sec. 8. Appropriations. — The funding support for the operation of the
Board shall be included in the annual budget of the Bangsamoro
Government. Further, an amount equivalent to ____—s per cent of the
revenues collected from the exploration, development, and utilization of
any natural resources within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall
be allocated for the operation of the Board. Provided that, the said amount
allocated shall not be included in the amount to be deducted from the
block grant under Section 21, Article XII of the Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, hereinafter

referred to as “Organic Law”.

Sec. 9. Separability. - If for any cause any part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Organic Law, the
remainder of this Act or any provision not affected thereby shall remain in

force and effect.
