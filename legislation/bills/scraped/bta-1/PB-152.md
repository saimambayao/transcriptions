SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “THE BANGSAMORO DRUG ABUSE TREATMENT
AND REHABILITATION CENTERS ESTABLISHMENT ACT OF 2021”.

Center. - Any of the treatment and rehabilitation centers for drug dependents referred to in
this Act.

Dangerous Drugs. — those provided under Republic Act No. 9165 otherwise known as the
“Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002" repealing Republic Act No. 6425, otherwise
known as the “Dangerous Drugs Act of 1972”, which include those listed in the Schedules
annexed to the United Nations 1961 Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs, as amended by
the 1972 Protocol, and in the Schedules annexed to the 1971 Single Convention on
Psychotropic Substances.

Drug — as used in this Act, a general term for any substance, stimulating or depressing, that
can be habituating or addictive.

Drug Abuse — also referred to as substance abuse, is any inappropriate, illegal or excessive
use of a drug or substance including but not limited to those defined under Section 4,
paragraph b of this Act.

Drug Dependence. — based on the World Health Organization definition, it is a cluster of
physiological, behavioral and cognitive phenomena of variable intensity, in which the use of
psychoactive drug takes on a high priority thereby involving, among others, a strong desire
or a sense of compulsion to take the substance and the difficulties in controlling substance-
taking behavior in terms of its onset, termination, or levels of use.

Island provinces - refer to the provinces and cities located within the political and/or
geographical areas of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao consisting
of the provinces of Basilan, Sulu, Tawi-tawi and the cities of Isabela and Lamitan in Basilan.

Mainland provinces - refer to the provinces and cities located within the political and/or
geographical areas of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao consisting
of the provinces of Lanao del Sur and Maguindanao, the cities of Cotabato and Marawi and
the 63 Special Geographic Area barangays of Cotabato province.

Manual of Operations — as used in this Act refers to the Manual of Operations for Drug
Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers prescribed by the Department of Health and
hereto attached as Annex “A” which shall be an integral part of this Act, which, to quote:
“shall serve as standard reference material to aid administrators and practitioners in the
management and operations of the different Drug Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers in
the Philippines. This Manual presents the minimum standards for facilities, services and
resources recommended by the Department of Health (DOH) for this type of health care
facility. These same standards will be uniformly applied by the DOH as basis for
accreditation of Drug Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers in the country”.

Non-residential Treatment and Rehabilitation Center (Out-patient Center) — a health facility
that provides diagnosis, treatment and management of drug dependents on an outpatient
basis. It may be a drop-in/walk-in Center or any other office/facility used by support groups
that provide consultation or counseling. From time to time, it may provide temporary
shelter for patients in crisis for not more than twenty- four (24) hours.

Residential Treatment and Rehabilitation Center (In-patient Center) — a health facility that
provides comprehensive rehabilitation services utilizing, among others, any of the accepted
modalities: Multidisciplinary Team Approach, Therapeutic Community Approach and/or
Spiritual Services towards the rehabilitation of a drug dependent.

SECTION 4. Functions and Objectives. The centers shall function as government residential (In-patient)
and non-residential (Out-patient) drug abuse treatment and rehabilitation health facilities with the main
objective of providing efficient, effective and highly accessible, affordable and comprehensive total drug
abuse treatment and rehabilitation care and services to drug dependents or patients suffering from the
effects of dangerous drugs, alcohol and related substances due to abuse or addiction in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The centers shall have the following specific functions and objectives:

a) Provide medical diagnosis, treatment and management of drug dependents;

b) Provide comprehensive rehabilitation services utilizing, among others, any of the accepted
modalities: Multidisciplinary Team Approach, Therapeutic Community Approach and/or
Spiritual Services towards the rehabilitation of a drug dependent;

c) Provide temporary shelter for patients in crisis for not more than twenty-four (24) hours;

d) Provide consultation and counseling to drop-in/walk-in patients undergoing treatment at
home or other community-based centers; and,

e) Provide assistance to other drug abuse treatment and rehabilitation services support groups.

f) Perform other functions prescribed and mandated under the Manual of Operations issued
by the Department of Health for this type of facility for accreditation and hereto attached as
Annex “A”.

SECTION 5. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao Drug Abuse Treatment and
Rehabilitation Centers — To implement the above-mentioned policies, there shall be established Drug
Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao. Two such centers shall initially be established each to cover both the mainland provinces and
island provinces of the region as defined under Section 3 of this Act.

One such center which shall hereinafter be referred to as the Maguindanao Drug Abuse Treatment and
Rehabilitation Center shall be located at the Maguindanao Provincial Hospital compound in Datu Hoffer
Municipality, Maguindanao Province. The second such center which shall hereinafter be referred to as
the Sulu Drug Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation Center shall be located at the Sulu Provincial Hospital
compound in Jolo, Sulu. Additional centers to be located in the other areas of the region may be
established thereafter upon the evaluation and recommendation by the Minister of Health.

Each of the herein-mentioned centers shall be constructed, initially, with a 10-30 bed capacity in
conformity with the structural and operational standards prescribed under the Manual of Operations
issued by the Department of Health for this type of facility for accreditation and hereto attached as
Annex “A”. Each of the herein-mentioned centers may be upgraded thereafter into a higher facility with
a larger capacity upon the evaluation and recommendation by the Minister of Health.

SECTION 6. Catchment area/coverage. — The Maguindanao Drug Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation
Center shall serve primarily, but not limited to, the patients coming from the various parts of the
mainland provinces and cities of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao specifically
the Maguindanao and Lanao Del Sur provinces and the cities of Cotabato and Marawi including the 63
special geographic areas in North Cotabato.

The Sulu Drug Abuse Treatment and Rehabilitation Center shall serve primarily, but not limited to, the
patients coming from the various parts of the island provinces of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao specifically the Basilan, Sulu and Tawi-tawi provinces including the cities of Isabela
and Lamitan in Basilan province.

SECTION 7. Administration, Management and Organization. - Each of the herein-mentioned center
shall be headed by a Center Chief who shall be designated or appointed by the Minister of Health with a
rank not higher than the rank of the Provincial Health Officer Il.

The herein-mentioned centers shall be under the administrative supervision of the Minister of Health
who shall be assisted by the respective Provincial Health Officer Il of the Provincial Health Office where
the center is established. The respective Provincial Health Officer II shall have operational supervision
over the corresponding center.

The organizational structure and all other personnel requirements of the Center shall be determined by
the Ministry of Health through the Minister of Health in accordance with the minimum personnel or
staffing requirements as prescribed under the Department of Health - Manual of Operations for the
accreditation of such facility hereto attached as Annex “A”.

SECTION 8. Treatment and Rehabilitation Procedure. The Manual of Operations prescribed by the
Department of Health, hereto attached as Annex “A” shall be adopted as the primary guidelines for the
treatment and rehabilitation systems and procedures in the operation of the herein-mentioned centers
and without prejudice thereof, the Ministry of Health, in consultation with the Darul Iftah of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao shall specifically include {slamic/Religious
teachings and approaches in the rehabilitation procedures to cater to Muslim patients including the
provision of facilities for Islamic religious practices.

SECTION 9. Appropriation. — The total amount of Two Hundred Million Pesos (Php 200 million} or One
Hundred Million Pesos (Php 100 million) for each of the center is hereby appropriated for the
establishment of these facilities as described under Section 5 of this Act which shall cover Capital Outlay
{CO) to include construction of the center structures, land development and procurement of equipment;
Pre-operating expenses; Personnel Services (PS); and, Maintenance and Other Operating expenses
{IMOOE) in accordance with the minimum requirements prescribed under the Department of Health -
Manual of Operations for the accreditation of such facility hereto attached as Annex “A”. The Ministry of
Health shall incorporate in its succeeding annual appropriations the amount required for the continued
operations of the herein-mentioned center and its upgrading as it may deem necessary.

SECTION 10. Separability Clause. - |n the event that any of the provisions of this Act is declared
unconstitutional, the validity of the other provisions shall not be affected by such declaration.

SECTION 11. Amendments or Revisions. — Any amendment to, revision, or repeal of this Act shall be
made by law enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament.

SECTION 12. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a Newspaper
of Regional Circulation.
