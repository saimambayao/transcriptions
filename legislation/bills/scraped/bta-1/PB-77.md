Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, Bangsamoro Transition
Authority (BTA) interim Parliament

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “MMOQH-BARMM BUFFER REPLENISHABLE
FUND ACT OF 2020”.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy and Principle. - It is a policy of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao as provided under Section 22, Article IX of
Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” to provide for a comprehensive and integrated
health service delivery for its constituents and establish by law a general hospital system to
serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life
shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical
services.

It is also a declared policy to establish and follow the principle of moral governance in the
affairs of the BARMM regional government.

SECTION 3. Establishment of the MOH-BARMM Buffer Replenishable Fund. — There shall be
established a Buffer Replenishable Fund to defray the timely payment of authorized salaries, wages
and other remuneration of the nationally-funded health workers deployed in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous region under the Human Resources for Health Deployment Program of the Department
of Health (DOH) but directly supervised by the BARMM- Ministry of Health (BARMM-MOH). The
Fund shall be managed and utilized by the BARMM-MOH in the event of the delayed transfer or
downloading of the funds from the national government to the BARMM-MOH for the afore-
mentioned purpose. The utilized amount of the Buffer Replenishable Fund shall be replenished
immediately upon receipt of the transferred equivalent amount of funds from the national
government and/or the Department of Health.

SECTION 4. Administration and management of the Fund. - The BARMN- Ministry of Health shall
administer and manage the Buffer Replenishable Fund in accordance with its cash management and
disbursement system and procedures subject to all existing government accounting and auditing
rules and regulations. In no way shall it be utilized or disbursed for purposes other than that which
is prescribed under Section 3 of this Act. Specifically, the Fund shall be used to temporarily cover the
eventual delayed transfer and/or downloading of funds to defray the salaries and other
remuneration of the existing 1,963 health workers allocated and deployed to the BARMM by the
DOH including but not limited to other additional nationally funded health workers that may be
deployed in the succeeding years.

SECTION 5. Termination of the Fund. The Buffer Replenishable Fund shall automatically be
terminated upon the cessation or cancellation of the herein-mentioned nationally funded DOH-
Human Resource for Health Deployment Program and/or stoppage of hiring or renewal of the
appointment or contract of services of the health workers paid under the same program. In such
eventuality, the fund shall immediately be liquidated and restored to the BARMM-MOH budget and
appropriations as savings.

SECTION 6. Appropriation. - The amount of One Hundred Eighty Million Pesos (Php180,000,000.00)
is hereby appropriated for the herein described Buffer Replenishable Fund as augmentation to the
approved budget and fund allocation of the Ministry of Health for the year 2021 equivalent to two
(2) months salaries of the nationally funded human resources for health cadres deployed in the
BARMM and directly supervised by the BARMM-MOH including any additional allocation or
deployment of other health workers in the succeeding years. Thereafter, the amount of the Buffer
Replenishable Fund shall be calculated annually to correspond to two (2) months salaries of the
deployed nationally funded cadres or health workers. Whenever there is a deficit the Ministry of
Health shall propose it in the annual budget of the Ministry of Health as reflected in the Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act.

SECTION 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The existing BARMM government implementing
rules and regulations regarding the utilization and disbursement of funds shall be applied in the
implementation of this Act subject to all government accounting and auditing rules and procedures.

SECTION 8. Separability Clause. - In the event any provision of this Act or the application of such
provision is declared invalid, the remainder of this Act shall not be affected by such declaration.

SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. — All laws and regulations under the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) government including those passed under the defunct Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) government regarding the establishment of a Buffer
Replenishable fund not consistent with this Act are hereby repealed.

SECTION 10. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in at least
two (2) national newspapers of general circulation.

RATIONALE

For the proposed “MOH-BARMM BUFFER REPLENISHABLE FUND ACT OF 2020”.

The national government through the Department of Health has re-formulated the
Philippine Health Agenda to provide focus on improving access to responsive health care system.
The redirection underscores the idea of improving human resources for health to increase the
availability of a highly motivated and skilled health workforce and the quality of health services in
rural, remote, and underserved areas of the country which was adopted as one of the primary
strategies to meet the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) targets and in consonance with the
intentions of the Universal Health Care law. One of the initial strategies taken by the Department of
Health to meet the objectives of the new Philippine Health Agenda is the Human Resources for
Health Deployment program (HRHDP).

The HRHDP is a response to the limited capacity of some LGUs to provide the recommended
human resources for Health and the Department of Health recognizes the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) formerly ARMM as one of the regions with the lowest
average of available human resources for Health. This is particularly expressed under the DOH
Secretary of Health Administrative Order No. 2014-0025 dated September 01, 2014, to quote:

“Most of our HRH are primarily urban centered with 28% of them in the National Capital
Region while other regions only have an average of 4% of the total HRH in health facilities.
Autonomous Region of Muslim Mindanao has less than 1% share of these HRH (DOH
NDHRHIS, June 2013). While there is no universal norm or standard for a minimum HRH
density in any given country or region recommended by the World Health Organization, it
was estimated that a density of fewer than 2.28 physicians, nurses and midwives per 1000
population generally fail to achieve a targeted 80% coverage rate for skilled birth attendance
and child immunization (World Health Report, 2006). With the commitment to attain
universal health care, HRH must be responsive to the needs of our country and support the
achievement of our Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) targets. This would require the
right quantity, quality and relevance of HRH to our health system needs. Therefore, there is
a need to harmonize the policies and processes for the placement and distribution of health
professionals through the deployment programs”

in response thereof, the DOH allocated and deployed a total of 1,963 cadres or health
workers to the BARMM consisting of 29 DTTB; 14 MOINV; 20 Dentists; 1,413 Nurses; 19 Medical
Technologists; 5 Pharmacists; 25 Nutritionist Dietitians; 157 Midwives; and, 5 Physical Therapists
who will serve at least until 2021, including but not limited to other additional nationally funded
health workers that may be deployed in the succeeding years.

The DOH shall provide for the payment of the salaries and other remuneration for the
deployed health workers to the BARMM through sub-allotment or transfer of funds as provided
under Section VI, Paragraph D of the DOH Administrative Order No. 2014-0025 dated September 01,
2014 which states that “all direct expenses related to deployment shall be charged against DOH
funds and the Health Human Resource Development Bureau (HHRDB) shall sub-allot/ transfer funds
to DOH Regional Offices/ DOH hospitals including the DOH ARMM (now MOH-BARMM)”.
Unfortunately, the sub-allotment or transfer of funds from the Department of Health (DOH) to the
BARMM-Ministry of Health (BARMM-MOH) for the salaries and remuneration of the afore-
mentioned cadres or human resources for Health deployed to the BARMM takes one to two months

This bill therefore seeks to establish a Buffer Replenishable Fund to defray the timely
payment of two (2) months equivalent of salaries and other remuneration of the DOH- nationally
funded health workers deployed in the Bangsamoro autonomous region in the total amount of
one hundred eighty million pesos (php 180,000,000.00) to be appropriated as augmentation to the
approved budget and fund allocation of the Ministry of Health for the year 2021. Thereafter, the
amount of the Buffer Replenishable Fund shall be calculated annually to correspond to two (2)
months salaries of the deployed nationally funded cadres or health workers for the period which
shall be included in the annual budget of the Ministry of Health and reflected in the Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act.

It is incumbent upon the BARMM-BTA Government in general and this august body in
particular to give fair and just treatment to all workers serving the constituents of this region
through this government and in this regard- to cushion the effects of delayed payment of salaries
and other remuneration as a result of the delayed sub-allotment or transfer of funds from the
national government which is inevitable due to the required official procedures. This is in adherence
to the teachings of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W.) that states- “the workers must be paid their wages
before their sweat dry”, and, as advocacy to the principle of moral governance adopted by the
BARMM-BTA government.
