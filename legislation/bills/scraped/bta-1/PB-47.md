AN ACT CREATING THE BARMM BUREAU OF QUARANTINE UNDER THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND
ADOPTING THE POWERS AND FUNCTIONS OF THE NATIONAL BUREAU OF QUARANTINE UNDER
REPUBLIC ACT NO. 9271 AND PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, Bangsamoro Transition
Authority (BTA) Interim Parliament

SECTION 1. Title. — This Act shall be known as the “BARMM Quarantine Act of 2020.”

SECTION 2. The BARMM Bureau of Quarantine (BARMM-BOQ). - The BARMIV- Bureau of Quarantine

shall be an agency under the BARMM Ministry of Health operating within the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao and shall be headed by a Bureau Director with the rank of Director IV to be
appointed by the BARMM Chief Minister.

SECTION 3. BARMM- BOQ Organizational Structure and Personnel complement. - The BARMM
Ministry of Health is hereby authorized to determine the Organizational Structure and personnel
complement of the BARMM- BOQ subject to the approval of the Minister of Health. It may adopt in
principle the organizational structure of the national BOQ and its basic personnel and staffing pattern
which is composed of the Office of the Bureau Director, Medical Services Division, Inspection Division,
Administrative Division and Field Operation Offices including a special International Disease Surveillance
Unit.

SECTION 4. POWERS AND FUNCTIONS. - Consistent with the provisions under Section 22, Article IX of
Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.”, to read as follows:

Government in the provision of quarantine services for the prevention and control of
epidemic and other communicable diseases.”

the BARMM- BOQ shall adopt the powers and functions of the national DOH-Bureau of Quarantine as
provided under R.A. No. 9271 also known as the Quarantine Act of 2004 with a BARMM-wide scope
following the national government international commitment in accord with the International Health
Regulations (IHR) of the World Health Organization (WHO). For the purpose of this Act, these adopted
powers and functions shall read as follows:

a) Jurisdiction and Functions of the Bureau. The examination at ports of entry and exit in the
BARMM of incoming and outgoing vessels and aircraft, the necessary surveillance over their
sanitary conditions, as well as over their cargoes, passengers, crews, and all personal effects,
and the issuance of quarantine certificates, bills of health, or other equivalent documents
shall be vested in and be conducted by the Bureau. This Bureau shall have authority over
incoming and outgoing vessels both domestic and foreign, including those of the army and

navy, their wharfage and anchorage, and over aircraft and airports, insofar as it is necessary
for the proper enforcement of the provisions of this Act.

b) Authority to Promulgate and Enforce Rules and Regulations and Provide Penalties for
Their Violations. — (b1). The Director of the BARMM- BOQ with the approval of the BARMM-
Minister of Health, is authorized to promulgate and enforce rules and regulations as in his
judgment are necessary to prevent the introduction, transmission or spread of "public
health emergencies of international concern" from foreign countries into the Philippines or
from one (1) domestic seaport/airport to another. For purposes of implementing these
regulations, the Director of the BARMM- BOQ may provide intervention strategies such as
health education and advisories, inspections, fumigation, disinfection, pest extermination,
vaccination for international travel, medical examination of aliens/foreigners for
immigration purposes and destruction of animals or articles found to be infected or
contaminated as to be sources of infection to human beings in coordination with other
concerned quarantine agencies such as the national DOH-BOQ, veterinary quarantine, plant
quarantine, etc. and other measures as in his judgment may be necessary.

(b2). Regulations prescribed under this section shall provide for the apprehension, detention
or surveillance for the purpose of preventing the introduction, transmission or spread of
such public health emergencies of international concern as may be specified from time to
time in Ministry Orders by the Minister of Health upon the recommendation or direction of
the international health surveillance through the national Department of Health.

(b3). Whenever it is deemed necessary for the protection of the public health of the
Bangsamoro from public health emergencies of international concern, immunization and
other preventable measures against these diseases shall be mandatory on all persons
arriving at any seaport/airport of entry in the BARMM.

(b4). The BARMM Minister of Health upon the recommendation of the BARMM- BOQ
Director may prescribe examination of any individual believed to be infected with a disease
of international concern on board vessels and aircraft entering any seaport or airport in the
BARMM. Such rules and regulations may provide that if upon examination, any such
individual is found to be infected or has been exposed to infection considered as dangerous
contact, he may be isolated aboard a vessel, in a hospital with facilities for infectious
diseases, at a quarantine station, or at any isolation facility, and in such a manner as may be
proscribed by the said regulations.

SECTION 5. Prohibition of Entry of Hazardous Cargo and Materials. - Whenever the Director of the
BARMM- BOQ determines that there is an existence of any public health emergency of international
concern in a foreign country and that there is imminent danger of the introduction of hazardous cargoes
or materials into the BARMM areas, he/she, in coordination with the DOH and its Bureau of Quarantine,
Bureau of Customs and other concerned agencies, may recommend to the BARMM Chief Minister
through the BARMM Minister of Health, the prohibition of its entry to the BARMM areas for public
health interest.

SECTION 6. Quarantine Documents/Clearances of Vessels and Aircraft. - (a) All vessels or aircraft from
foreign ports arriving at any port of the BARMM shall be required to submit the required maritime
declaration of health/general declaration and other documents as prescribed by the regulations under
the national DOH-Bureau of Quarantine and other such regulations as may be prescribed and imposed
by the BARMIV- BOQ.

(b) All ocean-going vessels or international aircraft from foreign seaports/airports calling at any seaport
or airport in the BARMM to disembark its passengers or discharge its cargo, or for other purposes must
secure a quarantine clearance in compliance with the health regulations imposed by the BARMM- BOQ.
This quarantine certificate/clearance (Pratique) shall be a prerequisite to customs clearance.

(c) After compliance with the quarantine laws and regulations, all vessels or aircraft leaving any seaport
or airport of the BARMM shall secure quarantine outgoing clearance. This certificate/clearance shall also
be a prerequisite to customs clearance of outgoing vessels and aircraft.

SECTION 7. Quarantine Stations. - The Director of the BARMM MOH-BOQ shall control, direct and
manage all quarantine stations, grounds and anchorages, and designate their boundaries. With the
approval of BARMM Minister of Health, he/she shall establish such additional quarantine station,

grounds and anchorages if, in his judgment, these are necessary to prevent the introduction of diseases
of international concern into the country. The BARMM- BOQ Director, in the same manner may also
order the closure of non-functional quarantine stations, grounds and anchorages.

SECTION 8. Penalties. - (a) Any person who violates any regulation prescribed in this Act shall forfeit to
the BARMM- Bureau of Quarantine a fine of not less than Ten thousand pesos (P10,000.00) but not
more than Fifty thousand pesos (P50,000.00) or be imprisoned for not more than one (1) year or both at
the discretion of the court of competent jurisdiction.

(b) Any vessel or aircraft that violates any provision of this Act shall forfeit to the BARMM- Bureau of
Quarantine a fine of not less than One Hundred Thousand pesos (P100,000.00) but not more than Five
Hundred thousand pesos (P500,000.00).

SECTION 9. Authority to Utilize Income. - The BARMM- Bureau of Quarantine shall be authorized to use
at least fifty percent (50%) of the income generated subject to accounting and auditing rules and
regulations.

SECTION 10. Appropriations. - The BARMM- Ministry of Health shall include in their annual budget the
appropriations for the implementation of this Act.

SECTION 11. Intergovernmental coordination. - The BARMM Minister of Health and other designated
representatives to the Intergovernmental Relations Body (IRB) as provided under Section 2, Article VI of
Republic Act No. 11054 are hereby granted authority to coordinate and make the necessary
arrangements with the national government for the implementation of this Act.

SEC. 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The BARMM- MOH shall promulgate the implementing
rules and regulations of this Act within sixty (60) days after the enactment of this Act.

SEC. 13. Separability Clause. - |n the event any provision of this Act or the application of such provision
to any person or circumstances is declared invalid, the remainder of this Act or the application of said
provisions to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected by such declaration.

SEC. 14. Repealing Clause. — All laws and regulations under the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) government including those passed under the defunct Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) government regarding foreign and local quarantine not consistent with
this Act are hereby repealed.

SEC. 15. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in at least two (2)
national newspapers of general circulation.
