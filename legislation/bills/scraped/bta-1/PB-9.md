Section 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “BANGSAMORO POPULATION
_ MANAGEMENT AND DEVELOPMENT ACT OF 2019”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. The Bangsamoro government subscribes to the
country’s international commitments as a signatory to the international conference on
population and development, national laws like the Responsible Parenthood and

Reproductive Health Care Law, and the national policy on population management. It
guarantees the sustainable approach to human development by adopting the ideals of
sustainable and inclusive development towards poverty reduction and socio-economic
development, consistent and fully cognizant of the respect to cultural as well as Islamic and
other religious practices and rites.

The Bangsamoro government hereby declares that for the purpose of achieving
better health outcomes, furthering social, economic, and political reform for its
development; increasing the share of each constituent in the fruits of economic progress
and meeting the standing socio-demographic and economic challenges, a policy and
program on population and sustainable development integration shall be undertaken.

The Bangsamoro government shall also protect and promote the right to health of
the people and instill health consciousness among them. It shall uphold the people’s rights
to have access to health and other social services, including family planning, that will
promote their well-being.

The Bangsamoro government recognizes and guarantees the promotion of gender
equality, gender equity, and women empowerment and dignity-- as a health, human right
concerns, and as a social responsibility without prejudice to Shariah and other codes ot
ethics.

Section 3. Guiding Principles for Implementation. This act declares the following
as guiding principles:

a) Human beings are at the center of concerns for sustainable development. They
are entitled to a healthy and productive life.

b) Population-related goals and policies are integral parts of Bangsamoro cultural,
economic and social development. The principal aim of which is to improve the
quality of life of all people.

c) People are the most important and valuable resource of any nation. Countries
should ensure that all individuals are given the opportunity to make the most of
their potential. They have the right to an adequate standard of living for
themselves and their families, including adequate food, clothing, housing,
education, water, and sanitation.

d) The right to development is a universal inalienable right and an integral part of
fundamental human rights. The human person is the central subject of
deveiopment.

e) Investment in healthcare and education that would prepare the younger
population to be productive members of the workforce will facilitate economic
growth, thus, resulting to demographic dividend or demographic bonus.

f) Advancing gender equality, equity, empowerment of women, elimination of all
kinds of violence against women and ensuring women’s ability to control their
own fertility, are cornerstones of population and development-related programs.

g) Sustainable development as a means to ensure human well-being equitably

shared by all people today and in the future, requires that the interrelationships

Section 6.1. Population Management and Development Programs. In

order to ensure sustainable human, socio-economic development and better health
outcomes in BARMM, there shall be a Population Management and Development
Program (PMDP) in the context of human rights, gender equality and existing
cultural norms and religious convictions of the people. The PMDP shall have the
following components:

a)

b)

Reproductive Health, Responsible Parenthood and Family Planning
Program. This program component shall assist parents /couples to achieve their
desired number and spacing of children within the demand of responsible
parenthood through effective family planning. it specifically aims to attain zero
unmet need for modern family planning through increase access to family
planning information and services. this program shall be consistent with the
provisions of armm rh code and regional gad code.

Population and Sustainable Development Integration. This program
component shall ensure the integration of population and demographic
variables and dimensions in sectoral development concerns: health, agriculture,
employment, economic including poverty reduction, environment and
education. This program shall also include the strategies that can address
population issues including unmanaged urbanization, high consumption rate
and other issues related to demographic processes as they relate to
development.

For this purpose, the Local Government Units shall endeavor to adopt and
institutionalize approaches and mechanisms for evidence-based and population-
centered planning including the strengthening of demographic and socio-
economic database and situational analysis as basis for identifying local policy
and program interventions. In coordination with the Commission On Population
Management And Development and Bangsamoro Planning and Development
Office, the Local Government Units shall conduct capability building strategies
for this purpose.

Personnel, Power and Duties. The Bangsamoro Regional Commission on
Population Management and Development shall be headed by an Executive Director
and assisted by a Deputy Executive Director both of which are duly appointed by the

However, the incumbent regional director of the interim office of the

Commission On Population And Development shall continue to conduct the affairs of
the commission until a new Executive Director and/or a Deputy Executive Director
is appointed and qualified.

Section 7.3. Other Personnel. The Bangsamoro Regional Commission on
Population Management and Development shall have such personnel as may be
necessary for the performance of its basic functions and such other personnel as
may be assigned or detailed from other agencies of the Bangsamoro government. All
positions, except technical and professional staff, and such other positions declared
to be highly technical, policy determining, or primarily confidential shall be subject
to the Civil Service rules and regulations and shall be consistent with the existing
budgeting, organization, staffing, position classification and compensation policies,
guidelines and standards; provided further, that all personnel shall be entitled to the
benefits and privileges normally accorded to government employees such as
retirements, GSIS insurance, including the benefits accorded to public health
workers under the Republic Act no. 7305, known as the Magna Carta of Public
Heulin Workers and iis revised implementing rules and regulations (IRR); provided
finally, that in the appointment and promotion of employees, merit and efficiency
shall serve as basis, consider gender and ethnic balance and no political test or
qualification shall be prescribed and considered for such appointments or

promotion.

Section 8. Donations and Grants. The Bangsamoro Regional Commission on
Population Management and Development shall receive donations, contributions,
grants, bequests or gifts and in whatever form in accordance with the provisions
and policies of the Bangsamoro parliament.

Section 9. Appropriation and Source of Funding. The amount necessary for
the implementation of this act shall be sourced from the annual Bangsamoro
government budget. For this purpose, an amount of ten million (10) pesos per
annum shall be appronriated by the Bangsamoro parliament. Provided, that the
National Population Commission shall provide funding assistance to the
Bangsamoro Regional Commission on Population Management and Development

during its initial five (5) years of operation.

For its supplemental budget, unon approval of the creation of this
commission, the funding requirements of the Bangsamoro Regional Commission on
Population Management and Development shall be included in its budget proposal
to be submitted to the Bangsamoro parliament, subject to the regular budgetary
process, chargeable to the block grant.

Section 10. Financial Assistance to Population Management and
Development Program. After appointment, the Executive Director shall coordinate
with the National Office of the Commission on Population and Development and
work for the release of fund primarily intended to finance programs and projects of
the Bangsamoro Regional Commission On Population Management And
Development. The allocation of funds shall be for the initial period of five (5) years
and in accordance with the National Population Commission scheduled program of

assistance.

Thereafter, upon mutual agreement, the national office of the commission on
population and development and the Bangsamoro Commission on Population
Management and Development may enter into other appropriate technical and/or
financial assistance agreements.
