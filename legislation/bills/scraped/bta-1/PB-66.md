EXPLANATORY NOTES BA OG: =
This bill establishes the Occupational Health and Safety Standards of the private
establishments within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The economic growth of a certain region depends largely to the business established
within the area which contributes revenues and income to the treasury of the
government. It is proper that the Bangsamoro Government is supportive of this
sector by legislating good and fair labor laws beneficial to the employer and the
labor-force.

This bill ensures both the employer and the labor-force that the working environment
is under a humane workplace. An environment that is pro-health, comfortable and
free from hazard. When the labor-force is being cared by the employer it is
expectedly that they are more productive as compare to those who are not. They are
more motivated to come to work and perform well when they feel good for
themselves and working within a comfortable environment. This Standards help
maintain the orderliness of the establishment which is regularly inspected as
provided for herein.

This bill is emanated from the Visitorial power of the Secretary of Labor and
Employment as provided for in the Labor Code of the Philippines. The Minister of
the Ministry of Labor and Employment being given the power to oversee the general
welfare and performance of the Labor, Employment and Occupation within its
territorial jurisdiction as provided for in Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known
as Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL). Pending the passage of the Bangsamoro Labor
Code and for the uniformity of the implementation of the labor laws in the
Philippines, the Minister takes this symmetric Visitorial power within its territorial
jurisdiction.

Further, the Bill discusses both the duties of the employer and duties and rights of
employees in the private establishments with respect to Health and Safety Standards
within the working environment. The rests of the provisions detail the needed
mechanism to establish the said Standards.

Finally, I appeal to my fellow Parliament Members to enjoin me in this cause to
enact more beneficial and fair labor laws to encourage more business investors to
come to our region, hence create more employment.

In view of this, the approval of this bill is humbly requested.

a
MP ROMEO K SEMA, DPA

CHAPTER I
TITLE AND DECLARATION OF POLICY

Section 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as “Bangsamoro Occupational
Safety and Health Standards of 2020”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy- The State affirms labor as a primary social and
economic force, and that a safe and healthy workforce is an integral aspect of
national building.

The State shall ensure a safe and healthful workplace for all working people by
affording them full protection against all hazards in their work environment. It shall
ensure that the provisions of the Labor Code of the Philippines, all domestic laws,
and internationally-recognized standards on occupational safety and health are being
fully enforced and complied with by the employers, and it shall provide penalties for
any violation thereof.

The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure the Bangsamoro people in its

jurisdiction the provision of the basic necessities and equal opportunities in life.
Social justice shall be promoted in all aspects of life and phases of development in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

It is the general welfare policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure and
support, among other things, promotes health and safety of its inhabitants.

It is also the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to provide, maintain and ensure
the delivery of basic and responsive health programs and guarantee the right of
workers to humane condition of work.

The Bangsamoro Government shall also promote health and safety and preserve the
comfort and convenience of the workers in all places of employment.

CHAPTER II
GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 3. Coverage. - This Act shall apply to all private establishments, projects,
sites, including Regional Economic Zone Authority (REZA) establishments, and all
other places where work is being undertaken in all branches of economic activity
within the BARMM core territories, except in the public sector.

Section 4. Definition of Terms. - As used in this Act:

CHAPTER III

DUTIES AND RIGHTS OF EMPLOYERS, WORKERS AND OTHER
PERSONS

Section 5. Duties of Employers. - Employers shall have the following duties and
rights:

(a) Provide the workers a place of employment free from hazardous conditions that
are causing or are likely to cause death, illness or physical harm to the workers.

Sanitize or disinfect area of activities regularly in compliance with Health protocols.

(b) Give complete job safety instructions and health preventive measures
orientation to all the workers especially to those entering the job for the first time,
including those relating to familiarization with their work environment;

(c) Use only inspected and approved devices and equipment, for the workplace, by
the authorized representatives of Ministry of Labor and Employment;

(d) Comply with OSH standards including training, medical examination and where
necessary, provision of protective and safety devices and supplies such as personal
protective equipment (PPE), facemask, alcohol or sanitizer, machine guards and
others as may be necessary for workers safety;

(ec) Encourage and allow workers and their safety and health representatives to
participate actively in the process of organizing, planning, implementing and
evaluating the safety and health program to improve safety and convenience in the
workplace;

(f) Provide, where necessary, for measures to deal with emergencies and accidents
including first-aid arrangements;

(g) Establish a work plan scheme whenever it is necessary by reason of public health
and safety and religious belief; and

(h) Provide the Bangsamoro workers of prayer room for their obligatory five (5)
times a day prayer as part of their spiritual health.

Section 6. Duties and Rights of Workers and other persons. - The following are the
rights and duties of workers:

(a) Bangsamoro workers may avail of work plan scheme during the month of
Ramadhan;

(b) All workers may opt to work based on the work plan scheme in consideration of
public health and safety;

(c) Every worker shall participate and cooperate in ensuring compliance with OSH
Standards in the workplace. The worker shall make proper use of all safeguards and
safety devices furnished for the worker's protection and that of others, and shall
observe measures and instructions to prevent accidents or imminent danger situation

The worker shall report to the supervisor immediately any work hazard that may be

(d) It shall be the duty of any person, including the builder or contractor who visits,

(c)Whenever two (2) or more undertakings are engaged in activities simultaneously

(f) The Worker’s right to safety and health at work shall be guaranteed. All workers

hazards in the workplace, provided access to training and education on health
protocols, chemical safety, electrical safety mechanical safety, and ergonomical

safety;

(g) The worker has the right of refusal to work without threat or reprisal from the
employer if, as determined by the MOLE, an imminent danger situation exists in the
workplace that may result in illness, injury or death, and corrective actions to
eliminate the danger have not been undertaken by the employer;

(h) Workers and their representatives shall have the right to report accidents,
dangerous occurrences, and hazards to the employer, to the MOLE and other
concerned government agencies exercising jurisdiction as the competent authority
in the specific industry or economic activity;

(i) Workers' Right to Personal Protective Equipment (PPE). - Every employer,
contractor or subcontructor, if any, shall provide his workers, free of charge,
protective equipment for their eyes, face, hands and feet, and free, and lifeline, safety
belt or harness, gas or dust respirators or masks, protective shields whenever
necessary by reason of the hazardous work process or environment, chemical,
radiological, mechanical and other irritants or hazards and health crisis capable of
causing injury or impairment in the function of any part of the body through
absorption, inhalation or physical contact. The cost of the PPE shall be part of the
safety and health program which is a separate pay item pursuant to Section 20 of this
Act;

(j) All PPE shall be of the appropriate type as tested and approved by the MOLE
based on OSH standards. The usage of PPE in all establishments, projects, sites and
all other places where work is being undertaken shall be based on the evaluation and
recommendation of the safety officer;

Section 7. Safety Signage and Devices. - All establishments, projects, sites and all
other places where work is being undertaken shall have safety signage and devices

Section 8. Safety in the Use of Equipment. In relation to the use of equipment, the

Section 9. Occupational Safety and Health Information. - Workers in all

appropriate measures, including the probable location of workers, for the prevention,
control and protection against those hazards.

Section 11. Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) Committee. - To ensure that the

(a) Employer or representative as the chairperson ex officio;

(b) President of Worker’s Union if the workers are organized or elected by the

applicable; and

(f) Additional two (2) workers representatives who shall come from the union if the
workers are organized or elected by the workers through a simple majority vote if
they are unorganized, as members.

The committee shall effectively plan, develop, establish, oversee and monitor the
implementation of the safety and health program.

Section 11. Safety Officer. - To ensure that a safety and health program is duly
followed and enforced, covered workplaces shall have safety officers who shall:

(b) Frequently monitor and inspect any health or safety aspect of the operation being

Section 12. Occupational Health Personnel and Facilities. - Covered workplaces
shall have qualified occupational health personnel such as physicians, nurses,
certified first-aiders, and dentists duly complemented with the required medical
supplies, equipment and facilities. The number of health personnel, equipment and
facilities. The number of health personnel, equipment and facilities, and the amount
of supplies shall be proportionate to the total number of workers and the risk of
hazard involved, the ideal ratio of which shall be prescribed by the MOLE.

Section 13. Safety and Health Training. -

(a) All safety and health personnel shall undergo the mandatory training on basic
occupational safety and health for safety officers as prescribed by MOLE.

(b) All workers shall undergo the mandatory eight (8) hours safety and health
seminar as required by the MOLE which shall include a portion on joint employer-
employee orientation.

(c) All personnel engaged in the operation, erection and dismantling of equipment
and scaffolds, structural erections, excavations, blasting operations, demolition,
confined spaces hazardous chemicals, welding, and flame cutting shall undergo
specialized instruction and training on the said activities.

Section 14. Occupational Safety and Health Reports. - All employers, contractors
or subcontractors, if any, shall submit all safety health reports verified by Safety and
Health Committee of respective establishment as prescribed by the MOLE.

Section 15. Workers' Competency Certification. - In order to professionalize,

CHAPTER VI

ENFORCEMENT OF OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH
STANDARDS

Section 19. Authority to Enforce - The authority to enforce mandatory OSH
standards is vested to the Minister of Labor and Employment within its territorial
jurisdiction pursuant to its mandate letter (jj), Section 2, Article V of Republic Act
No. 11054 otherwise known as Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) or in some cases
may be delegated by the same through a valid document to a competent government
authority.

Pending the passage of Bangsamoro Labor Code and for uniformity of the
implementation of Labor Laws in the Philippines, the Minister of Labor and
Employment within its jurisdiction is with symmetric powers with the Secretary of
the Labor and Employment, without prejudice to the latter’s exclusive powers, in
pursuing its mandate concerning Labor, Employment and Occupation provided for
in the BOL, Pursuant to Article 128 of the Labor Code of the Philippines and other
applicable laws, the Secretary of Labor and Employment or the Secretary's
authorized representatives shall have the authority to enforce the mandatory
occupational safety and health standards in all establishments and conduct, together
with representatives from the labor and the employer sectors, an annual spot audit
on compliance with OSH standards. The Secretary or the Secretary's duly authorized
representatives can enter workplaces at anytime of the day or night where work is
being performed to examine records and investigate facts, conditions or matters
necessary to determine compliance with the provisions of this Act.

No person or entity shall obstruct, impede, delay or otherwise render ineffective the
orders of the Secretary of Labor and Employment or the Secretary's duly authorized
representatives issued pursuant to the authority granted under Article 128 of the
Labor Code of the Philippines, and no lower court or entity shall issue temporary or
permanent injunction or restraining order or otherwise assume jurisdiction over any
case involving the enforcement orders.

The Secretary of Labor and Employment may likewise order stoppage of work or
suspension of operations of any unit or department of an establishment when
noncompliance with law or implementing rules and regulations poses grave and
imminent danger to the health and safety of workers in the workplace.

The procedure for inspecting work premises, notifying employers of violations, and
issuing compliance or stoppage orders shall be pursuant to the procedure laid down
in Article 128 of the Labor Code of the Philippines as implemented through relevant
regulations issued by the DOLE on administration and enforcement of labor laws.
The inspector or person authorized by the DOLE to enforce compliance with OSH
standards shall present proper identification upon request, and such inspector or
person shall only act within the authority or direction given by the Secretary of Labor
and Employment.

The Secretary of Labor and Employment or the Secretary duly authorized
representatives shall inspect establishments and workplaces regardless of the size
and nature of operation. Any kind of self-assessment shall not take the place of labor

Section 20. Payment of Workers During Work Stoppage Due to Imminent Danger
or Contagious disease classified as Public Emergency Health Crisis. - If stoppage
of work due to imminent danger occurs as a result of the employer's violation or
fault, the employer shall pay the workers concerned their wages during the period
of such stoppage of work or suspension of operation.

Section 20. Payment of Workers During Work Stoppage Due to Imminent Danger
or Contagious disease classified as Public Emergency Health Crisis. - If stoppage
of work due to imminent danger occurs as a result of the employer's violation or
fault, the employer shall pay the workers concerned their wages during the period of
such stoppage of work or suspension of operation.

Further, if stoppage of work due to risk of public health which has been declared by
World Health Organization (WHO), National Government and the Bangsamoro
Government, the employer shall adopt a work plan scheme that workers may opt to
avail and they may be given assistance or may be paid the concerned workers their
wages, during the period of such stoppage of work or suspension of operation, as the
case maybe.

For purposes of payment of wages and any other liabilities arising from a work
stoppage order, the employer is presumed a party at fault if the work stoppage order
is issued secondary to an imminent danger situation which would imperil the lives
of the workers.

Section 21. Standards Setting Power of the Minister of Labor and Employment. -
The Minister of Labor and Employment shall, in consultation with the other
concerned government agencies and relevant stakeholders, by appropriate orders, set
and enforce mandatory OSH standards to eliminate or reduce occupational safety
and health hazards depending on the number of employees of the establishment, the
nature of its business operations, and the risk or hazard involved.

The Minister shall also institute new, and update existing programs to ensure safe
and healthy working conditions in all workplaces especially in hazardous industries
such as a mining, fishing, construction, and the maritime industry.

Section 22. Employee's Compensation Claim. - A worker may file claims for
compensation benefit arising out of work-related disability or death. Such claims
shall be processed independently of the finding of fault, gross negligence or bad faith
of the employer in a proceeding instituted for the purpose.

Section 23. Incentives to Employers. - There shall be an established package of
incentives under such rules and regulations as may be promulgated by the MOLE to
qualified employers to recognize their efforts toward ensuring compliance with OSH
and general labor standards such as OSH training packages, additional protective
equipment, technical guidance, recognition awards and other similar incentives.

Section 24. Prohibited Acts. - The following are considered as prohibited acts:

(a) Willful failure or refusal of an employer, contructor or subcontructor to comply
with the required OSH standards or with a compliance order issued by
the Ministerof Labor and Employment or by the Minister's authorized
representative shall make such employer, contructor or subcontructor liable for an
administrative fine not exceeding One hundred thousand pesos (P100,000.00) per
day until the violation is corrected, counted from the date the employer or contructor
is notified of the violation or the date the compliance order is duly served on the
employer. The amount of fine imposed shall depend on the frequency or gravity of
the violation committed or the damage caused: Provide, however, That the
maximum amount shall be imposed only when the violation exposes the workers to
a risk of death, serious injury or serious illness.

(b) An employer, contractor or sub-contractor who willfully fails or refuses to
comply with the required OSH standards or with a duly issued compliance order,
and engages in any of the following acts to aid, conceal or facilitate such
noncompliance shall be liable for a maximum of One hundred thousand pesos
(P100,000.00) administrative fine separate from the daily fine imposed above:

(1) Repeated obstruction, delay or refusal to provide the Minister of Labor and
Employment or any of its authorized representatives access to the covered
workplace or refusal to provide or allow access to relevant records and
documents or obstruct the conduct of investigation of any fact necessary in
determining compliance with OSH standards;

(2) Misrepresentation in relation to adherence to OSH standards, knowing
such statement, report or record submitted to the MOLE to be false in any
material aspect;

(3) Making retaliatory measures such as termination of employment, refusal
to pay, reducing wages and benefits or in any manner discriminates against
any workers who has given information relative to the inspection being
conducted.

For the purpose, the MOLE, in consultation with relevant stakeholders, shall issue a
list of offenses with corresponding reasonable administrative fines depending on the
severity, frequency and damage caused without prejudice to the filing of a criminal
or a civil case in the regular courts, as the case may be.

The fine collected shall be used for the operation of occupational safety and health
initiatives, including occupational safety and health training and education and other
occupational safety and health programs.

CHAPTER VII
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

Section 25. Updated MOLE Computerized Labor Law Compliance System. -
The Minister of Labor and Employment shall establish an updated labor inspection
system of computerized gathering and generation of real time data on compliances,
monitoring of enforcement, and a system of notification on workplace accidents and
injuries.

Section 26. Applicability to Micro and Small Enterprises (MSEs). - Specific to
MSEs, the MOLE shall develop OSH core compliance standards to ensure safe and
healthy workplaces. All MSEs shall be required to implement the prescribed
standards for housekeeping, materials handling and storage, electrical and
mechanical safety and PPE, and to monitor hazards regularly.

Section 27. Inter-Governmental Coordination and Cooperation. - The MOLE shall
institute a mechanism for coordination with the Department of Labor and
Employment, Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy, Ministry of
Transportation and Communication, Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and
Agrarian, Ministry of Public Works, Ministry of Trade, Industry and
Tourism, Ministry of Interior and Local Government, Ministry of Health, REZA
and all other government agencies, including local government units, within sixty
(60) days from the issuance of the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.
They shall regularly convene to monitor the effective implementation of this Act as
well as the related programs and projects that are established to prevent and eliminate
the incidence of injury, sickness or death in all workplaces.

Section 28. /mplementing Rules and Regulations.—The Minister of Labor and
Employment, in coordination with agencies concerned, shall promulgate the rules
and regulations within ninety (90) days after the effectivity of this Act.

The MOLE shall issue the appropriate standards of occupational safety and health
based on the number of employees, risk or hazard involved.

Section 29. Separability Clause. - If any part, section or provision of this Act shall
