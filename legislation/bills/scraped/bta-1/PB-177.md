Explanatory Note

Section 1, Article XII of the 1987 Constitution provides that, “[t]he State shall promote
industrialization and full employment based on sound agricultural development and agrarian
reform, through industries that make full and efficient use of human and natural resources, and
which are competitive in both domestic and foreign markets.” The Constitution further provides
in Section 5, Article XIII that, “[t]he State shall recognize the right of farmers, farmworkers, and
landowners, as well as cooperatives, and other independent farmers’ organizations to participate
in the planning, organization, and management of the program, and shall provide support to
agriculture through appropriate technology and research, and adequate financial, production,
marketing, and other support services.”

In the case of Philippine Coconut Producers Federation, Inc. (COCOFED), et. al. v.
Presidential Commission on Good Government (PCGG), G.R. No. 75713, October 2, 1989,
the Supreme Court held that “[i]t cannot be denied that the coconut industry is one of the major
industries supporting the national economy. It is, therefore, the State’s concern to make it a strong
and secure source not only of the livelihood of a significant segment of the population, but also of
export earnings the sustained growth of which is one of the imperatives of economic stability”.

The coconut industry is currently progressing. The economic well-being of a major part of
the population depends a large extent on the viability of the coconut industry and its improvement
in the areas of production, processing and marketing.

Section 23, Article XIII of R.A. No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL)
provides that with the policies and laws of the Bangsamoro Government on agriculture as a key
development strategy, it shall promote productivity measures, and provide support for small
farmers, landholders and marginal fisherfolk.

Based on the Crops Production Survey (CrPS) released by the Philippine Statistics
Authority (PSA), the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is the 6"
region with the most coconut production from July to September 2021 with 352,100 metric tons
of total coconut production. Also, on another survey released by PSA entitled “2019 BARMM
Other Crops Production”, Coconut (with husk) is recognized as the region’s number | Non-Food
and Industrial Crop with 1,331,804.97 metric tons of total production.

The coconut industry is an important pillar of the region’s agriculture as it is among the
major industries in the BARMM. Its development would mean significant improvement in the
lives of the people of the region. The industry needs an intensive and extensive effort in order to
promote integrated development and growth of the industry in all its aspects, in order to reach the
full potential of the industry and to ensure that coconut farmers are the direct participants in, and
beneficiaries of, such development and growth. In addition, the issues surrounding the industry
should also be acted upon by the appropriate agency in order to achieve inclusive development.

The Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA) is the government agency that is tasked to develop
the coconut industry to its full potential in line with the new vision of a united, globally
competitive, and efficient coconut industry. Currently, the PCA has no counterpart in Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

It is also worth noting that more than half of the coconut trees in Basilan have been plagued
by coconut scale insects also known as cocolisap. A cocolisap attack is manifested by the
yellowish color of the leaves of the coconut trees thereby reducing the coconut fruits and gradually
killing the trees. Time and enough funding are needed to mass-produce the biological control
agents that are used minimize the population of the cocolisap. The BARMM has no means to assist
the farmers affected by the said calamity.

The creation of the Bangsamoro Coconut Authority (BCA), as proposed in this measure,
will promote and protect the coconut industry in the region considering the importance of the
industry in nation-building, it being one of the principal industries in the BARMM. The BCA will
serve the Bangsamoro Region, thereby promoting and maintaining the development of the industry
in the region.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this measure is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao assembled:

Section I. Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Coconut Authority Act
of 2022”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. —\t is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government
to advance agriculture as a key development strategy, promote productivity measures, and provide
support for small farmers, landholders and marginal fisherfolks. The coconut industry is an
important pillar of the region’s agriculture and has a huge potential to contribute to the economy
of the region. The Bangsamoro Government shall promote the accelerated growth and
development of the coconut and other palm oils industry so that the benefits of such growth accrue
to the greatest number. It shall prioritize the welfare of the coconut farmers who are among the
poorest and most vulnerable sectors in the rural areas and provide continued leadership and support
in the integrated development of the industry.

Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Coconut Authority. — There is hereby created the
Bangsamoro Coconut Authority, herein referred to as “the Authority”. It will serve as the agency
for the comprehensive and integrated development of the coconut industry in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), to the benefit of the Bangsamoro farmers.

Section 8. Board Meetings. — The Board shall meet regularly at least once every quarter
or as often as it deems necessary upon written request signed by a majority of the members. A
majority of all shall constitute a quorum.

Section 9. Periodic Reports. — The Board shall submit to the Bangsamoro Parliament
periodic reports on its findings and make appropriate policy recommendations on actions to be
taken by the Bangsamoro Parliament/Government through the appropriate Committee/s or
Ministries, in order to carry out the objectives and mandates of this Act.

Section 10. Organizational Structure of the Authority. — Under the policy supervision of
the Board, the Authority shall be headed by an Administrator, to be assisted by a Deputy
Administrator. The Office of the Administrator shall be assisted by three (3) technical divisions
and one (1) support finance and administrative division to wit:

1) Research & Development Division;

2) Operations’ Division;

3) Monitoring and Evaluation Division; and

4) Finance and Administrative Support Division.

Section 11. Powers and Duties of the Administrator’. — The management of the Authority
shall be vested in an Administrator, to be appointed by the Chief Minister upon the
recommendation of the Board, who shall have the following powers and duties:

a) Direct and manage the affairs and business of the Authority, in accordance with policies
and plans enunciated by the Board;

b) Provide policy guidelines and standards for the effective and efficient operation and
function of the Authority in accordance with its mandate and responsibilities;

c) Lead the formulation of policy measures as well as development plans and programs for
the benefit, protection, and welfare of the coconut industry;

d) Appoint, with the approval of the Board, such officials and employees of the Authority as
may be necessary for the proper discharge of its functions and responsibilities, and with
the approval likewise of the Board, to remove, suspend and otherwise discipline, for just
cause, any subordinate personnel thereof;

e) Represent the Authority in all dealings with other offices, agencies, and instrumentalities
of the government and with all persons and entities, public or private, domestic or foreign;

f) Prepare and submit thirty days after the close of each fiscal year an annual report to the

Chief Minister, and such periodic reports of activities as may be required from time to time;
