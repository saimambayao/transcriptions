EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is the model definition of a melting-pot of cultures that
encompasses the Philippines. In it, several unique Indigenous Communities and Indigenous people exist
each with their unique identity, religion and ethnicity all of which are protected by no less than the
constitution as Filipinos and by the Bangsamoro Organic Law as enshrined in its general principles and
policies (Section 10, Article IV). These Indigenous Communities have their own inimitable ways,
traditions and needs which are best served by local heads within their communities. Right now, there
exists an opportunity for the Bangsamoro Government to better serve these Indigenous Communities in
order to ensure that they are involved in the decision-making process which would greatly impact their
communities.

This Bill aims to bridge the gap that exists between the Indigenous People within our region and
the Bangsamoro Government. Through the establishment of these community centers, our constituents
belonging to the Indigenous Communities will have unabated access to government amenities without the
tension of being discriminated against by reason of their belief, ethnicity, race, custom, sex and any other
reason as these centers will be run by the Indigenous communities themselves. These centers will also be
the gateway for our indigenous communities to voice out their concerns and needs so that the

AN ACT ESTABLISHING INDIGENOUS PEOPLE’S COMMUNITY CENTERS IN
APPROPRIATE BARANGAYS AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Title. This Act shall be known as the /ndigenous People's Community Centers (IPCC) Act
of 2022;

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. — Pursuant to Section 10, Article 4 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law,
it is the policy of the Bangsamoro government that Indigenous peoples shall have the freedom to retain
their distinct indigenous and ethnic identity in addition to their Bangsamoro political identity. And as
such, the Bangsamoro Government shall:

1. Recognize and promote the rights of Indigenous Cultural Communities (ICCs)/Indigenous
Peoples (IPs) within the framework of regional unity and development;

2. Protect the rights and preserve the unique traditions, culture, and identity of the ICCs/IPs in the
Bangsamoro to ensure their economic, social and cultural well-being;

4. Encourage ICCs/IPs’ participation in decision making matters affecting t their welfare and to

SECTION 3. Establishment/Construction of Community Centers. -There shall be an established
indigenous people’s community center (IPCC) in the barangay, to be determined by the Ministry of
Indigenous People’s Affairs (MIPA), which shall serve as the multi-purpose center for the indigenous
peoples located therein.

SECTION 4. IP Community Center Requirements. - The following shall be the minimum requirements
for every IPCC that will be established under this Act:

a) Location/Designation - each location shall be determined by the MIPA in close coordination
with the LGUs concerned and in consultation with the IP beneficiaries but shall in all cases be centrally

located in the IP community.

b) Design and materials - The IPCC’s design shall reflect the unique IP identity of the beneficiary
IP community and utilize at least twenty percent (20%) indigenous or local materials.

c) Structural/Building Capacity - The IP Community Centers shall be constructed and designed to
be calamity-resilient, made of sturdy materials, built in accordance with the specifications of Republic Act
No. 6541, other known as the “National Building Code of the Philippines”, and should be at par with the
standards set by the International Building Code.

The Ministry of Public Works (MPW) shall be in charge of the construction of IP community
centers and formulating the building specifications therefor based on standards, issuances, and other
guidelines set by the MPW. The design of which shall be reflective of the identity of the IP for whom the
center is to be constructed. It shall also ensure that the construction of such IPCC is in accordance with the
above-mentioned requirements during its construction.

d) Amenities —The IPCC shall be well-ventilated and shall have the capacity to accommodate a
large number of people. The centers shall have the following minimum amenities:

a. Assembly or meeting room;

b. Training and learning room;

c. Display kiosk or store;

d. Shower and toilet facilities;

e. Access thereto by and accommodation of the disabled:
f. Provisions for emergency/exit doors;

g. Food preparation areas with adequate ventilation;

SECTION 5. Operations and Management of the IPCC. - The municipal government, through the
barangay concerned shall be primarily responsible for the operation, supervision, and management of IPCC
established under this Act.

The mandatory audit of infrastructures used as IPCC shall be conducted by the Municipal Engineer
and/or the building official at least once every year and immediately after the occurrence of a hazard or
disaster event.

SECTION 6. Role of the MIPA. - The MIPA shall be the primary lead agency which shall oversee the
implementation of this act.

SECTION 7. Appropriations. - The amount necessary to fund the construction and maintenance of IPCCs
under this Act shall be included in the annual program of MIPA for the next fiscal year. Every year
thereafter, the amounts necessary for the construction and maintenance of IPCC shall be included in the list
of programs as part of annual budget in the General Appropriations Act.

The appropriations necessary for the operation and management of the evacuation centers shall be
charged against the funds of the Municipal Government concerned.

SECTION 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within ninety days (90) after the effectivity of this
Act, the MIPA, Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) and the MPW, in
coordination with the appropriate government agencies concerned, shall formulate the necessary rules and
regulations necessary to carry out the objectives of this Act.

SECTION 8. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional, other
provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, decrees, executive orders, or parts thereof inconsistent
with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.
