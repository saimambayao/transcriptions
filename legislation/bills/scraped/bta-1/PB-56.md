EXPLANATORY NOTE

One of the peculiar features of the Bangsamoro Government is the
inclusion of the Wali as an integral part of the acts of autonomous
governance. This is consistent with its parliamentary form as enshrined in
Republic Act No. 11054 or the “Bangsamoro Organic Law”. Wali is a
ceremonial head that performs ministerial functions without powers relating
to government. However, while his functions are merely symbolic, his duties
are essential parts in the exercise of governmental powers.

The Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (hereinafter referred to as “BOL”) clearly emphasized this role of
the Wali. For this purpose, Section 3, Article VIII of R.A No. 11054 provides
that the Wali shall only perform ceremonial duties such as opening the
session of the Parliament, administering the oath of office, dissolving the
Parliament upon the advice of the Chief Minister after a vote of no confidence
against the government of the day, calling for election of a new Parliament,
and attending to public ceremonies. Consistent with the Bangsamoro
Government’s principles and policies, the Wali shall also encourage and

promote programs intended for social, cultural, and moral recovery of the

Section 10. Functions and Duties of the Wali -—

(1) The Wali shall only perform ceremonial duties such as:

(a) Opening the session of the Parliament;

(b) Administering the Oath of Office;

(c) Dissolving the Parliament upon the advice of the Chief
Minister after the vote of no confidence against the
government of the day;

(d) Calling for the election of new Parliament; and,

(e) Attending to public ceremonies.

(2) The Wali shall encourage the promotion of social, cultural, and

moral recovery programs for the Bangsamoro Government.

Section 11. Per Diem of the Wali - The Wali shall receive per diem,

subject to existing rules and regulations on the grant of per diem.

The per diem shall be sourced from the funds of the Bangsamoro

Government and shall be provided for in its annual appropriations law.

a) The Wali may be removed from office by the Parliament on any of the

following grounds:

PART 4
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

Section 17. Protection from Personal Liability — No Criminal actions or
civil suit shall be brought personally against the Wali in any court of law in the
Philippines with respect to any proceedings done in the regular discharge of the

functions of the Wali under this Act.

Section 18. Facilities —

(a) The Bangsamoro Government shall provide adequate facilities for
the efficient functioning of the Office of the Wali based on its
identified plans and programs.

(b) The Bangsamoro Government shall also provide the Wali an official
residence during his tenure with necessary supplies and equipment

deserving of his stature as the ceremonial head.

Section 19. Implementing Guidelines - The Office of the Wali shall
prepare detailed implementing guidelines defining the specific functions of its
divisions and support services, and provide rules and regulations to streamline

the Office’s processes and procedures.

Section 20. Funding - The Bangsamoro Government must ensure that
sufficient funds of the Office are appropriated by the Parliament against the

Bangsamoro Expenditure Program.
Section 21. Amendments or Revisions - Consistent with the provisions

of the Organic Law of the Bangsamoro Government, this Act may be amended or

revised by the Bangsamoro Parliament by a majority vote of all its members.
