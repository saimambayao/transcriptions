(c)“BARMM” refers to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim

(d)“Constitution” refers to the 1987 Constitution unless otherwise provided:

(e)““Mujahidens” refers to the members of the MNLF and MILF combatants
from the start of Martial Law upto the ratification of the Bangsamoro
Organic Law.

Section 3. Qualification. Only those members of the “Mujahiden and
Mujahedat” who were members of the MNLF and MILF combatants since the
declaration of Martial Law in the year 1972 up to the ratification of the
Bangsamoro Organic Law in the year 2018 shall be qualified under this Bull.

Section 4. Beneficiaries. Beneficiaries of the Mujahidens are categorized as
follows, to wit:

a. Parents —1f the Mujahiden died single.

b. Surviving Spouse/s- if the Mujahiden died married.

¢. Surviving Children — the Dependents of the married Mujahiden.
d. Nearest of Kin

Section 5.Financial Assistance. Financial Assistance for the Benefictaries
shall be allocated as follows.

a. Parents- Livelihood project

b. Surviving Spouse/s- Livelihood project

c. Surviving Children- Education Assistance (Elementary, Secondary and
Tertiary)

d. Nearest of Kin.

Section 6. Validation. The Bangsamoro Parliament shall formulate a system
that would complete the listing of the qualified Mujahidens and _ their
beneficiaries who should receive the socio-economic assistance.

Section 7. Funding — The Bangsamoro Parliament shall have the authority
to allocate funds for the purpose of this Bill.

Section 8. Amendments and Revisions — Consistent with the provisions of
the BOL, this Act may be amended and can be revised by the Bangsamoro
Parliament.

Section 9. Repealing Clause — All laws, decrees, orders, rules and
regulations which are inconsistent with this Act, are hereby repealed or amended
accordingly.

Section 10. Separability Clause — The provisions of this Act are hereby
declared to be separate and in the event one or more of such provisions are held
unconstitutional, the validity of other provisions shall not be affected thereby.

Section 11. Effectivity Clause — This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15)
days following the completion of the publication in a newspaper of general

circulation within BARMM jurisdiction.
