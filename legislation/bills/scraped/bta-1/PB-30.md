EXPLANATORY NOTE

The importance of autonomy among local governments instituted through
a system of decentralization is provided and guaranteed by the 1987 Constitution.
Article X, Section 2 thereof provides “[t]he territorial and political subdivisions
shall enjoy local autonomy. Furthermore, Section 1 of the same article also edicts
that [t]here shall be autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and the

Cordilleras as hereinafter provided.

The historic signing of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro
(CAB) between the government of the Philippines (GPH) and the Moro Islamic
Liberation Front (MILF), and the enactment of Republic Act 11054, also known
as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), paved the way for the replacement of the
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao with the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao. This law is also expected to empower the
Bangsamoro community to address and end poverty, establish lasting peace,
avert violence and extremism and achieve genuine development and prosperity in

their region.

In order to attain this, harmonized regional development is a very
important target in economic policy-making. It is important to promote a good
balance between regions to secure political and economic stability and to
stimulate the active participation of the people. But harmonized economic growth
necessitates focused planning and policy-making which decentralized agency
must promote in close coordination with national long-term development plans.
The defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) has its Regional

Economic and Development Planning Board (REDPB) which is the highest

Section 4, Article XIII of the BOL provides for the establishment of the
Bangsamoro Economic and Development Council (hereinafter “Council”) which
shall serve as the planning, monitoring, and coordinating agency for all

development plans, programs, and projects of the Bangsamoro Government.

With this bill, we aim to create a Bangsamoro Economic and
Development Council (BEDC) that will serve as a primary institution that
sets and guides the direction of comprehensive socio-economic development
effort and other development concerns and priorities in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region. To ensure people’s participation, the Council shall take into
account the development plans of the provinces, cities, municipalities, and

barangays as approved by their respective local development councils.

In view of the foregoing, I seek the expeditious consideration and approval

of this measure in the Parliament.

- Muslim Mindanao assembled:

SECTION 1. Name - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro
Economic and Development Council Act of 2019.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policies — It is the declared policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to pursue the acceleration of social, economic, and
political development and promote the welfare of its constituents through a
holistic, effective and responsive planning, coordination, and monitoring and
evaluation of the implementation of plans, policies programs and projects in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that socio-
economic development programs and activities in the region are well coordinated
and responsive to the needs of its constituents and consistently aligned with
national development plans such as the Philippine Development Plan. It shall
encourage the active participation of various government agencies, Local
Government Units (LGUs), Non-Government Organizations (NGOs) as well as
private sector at the regional and local levels in the formulation of development
plans and other planning and development activities in the BARMM.

SECTION 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Economic and
Development Council — There shall be created a Bangsamoro Economic and
Development Council (BEDC) which shall serve as the highest governing and
socio-economic development policy-making body of the Bangsamoro
Government. It shall serve as the planning, coordinating, and monitoring agency
for all development plans, policies, programs and projects of the Bangsamoro
Government including its constituent LGUs.

For the effective performance of the Council’s functions, the composition
of the BEDC can be reconstituted by the Chief Minister upon recommendation of
the majority of the members of the Council.

It shall select the private sector representatives to the Council, subject to
the guidelines and qualification criteria to be formulated by the BPDA and

Additional members from other sectors may be considered upon

members shall be determined in the internal rules and regulations to be
formulated by the BEDC.

SECTION 5. BEDC Chairman — The Chief Minister shall act as the
Chairman of the BEDC, preside over its meetings and performs such other duties
and functions as may be necessary in the conduct of the affairs of the Council.

SECTION 6. BEDC Vice Chairman —- The Director General of the
Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA), in addition to its
functions as such, shall serve as the Vice Chairman of the BEDC. The Vice-Chair
shall preside the BEDC meeting in the absence of the Chairman and perform such
other duties as may be assigned by the Council.

SECTION 7. Functions of the BEDC — The BEDC shall have the
following functions and responsibilities:

1. Direct the plan formulation and recommend for approval by the
Parliament the short, medium and long-term development plans of the
BARMM;

2. Review, prioritize, and endorse the annual and multi-year investment
program of BARMM to the Parliament for approval, and possible funding
by the national government and other partners;

3. Ensure that the approved development plans of provinces, cities
municipalities and barangays are taken into consideration in the
Bangsamoro regional development plan;

4. Promote and direct the inflow and allocation of private investments in the
region to support regional development goals, policies and strategies;

5. Coordinate the development, funding and implementation of programs
and projects of various ministries or LGUs in the BARMM to insure their
effectiveness and responsiveness to the needs of the Bangsamoro;

6. Monitor and evaluate all development programs and _ projects
implemented by the national government, Bangsamoro ministries/line
agencies/offices, local government units, and government owned and/or
controlled corporations in the BARMM;

7. Call upon any representative of the concerned national agencies,
ministries/line agencies/offices, LGUs, and government owned and/or
controlled corporations on issues and concerns affecting the
implementation of their programs and projects in the BARMM; and

SECTION 8. Meeting of the BEDC — The BEDC shall meet regularly
once every quarter at such time, place and date as may be determined and fixed
by the BEDC. The Chairman or the majority of its member may call a special
meeting for a specific purpose with a compelling reason at least five (5) days
before the special meeting. A majority of the member of the Council shall
constitute a quorum to do business. In no case a proxy shall be allowed in any
meeting of the Council be it regular or special meeting.

SECTION 9. BEDC Technical Secretariat — The BPDA shall serve as
the Technical Secretariat of the BEDC. The BEDC Technical Secretariat shall be
headed by the BPDA Director General to ensure the technical capability and
competence of the Council.

SECTION 10. Functions of the Technical Secretariat — The BPDA
as the Technical Secretariat of the Council and shall perform the following
functions:

1. Facilitate and coordinate the formulation and implementation of
development plan and investment program of the BARMM;;

2. Provide technical and administrative support to the Council and its
comunittees;

3. Upon request, provide technical assistance on planning to BARMM
ministries, line agencies, LGUs, NGOs, and other government agencies
in the BARMM;

4. Develop and maintain a comprehensive management information
system;

5. Develop a comprehensive program for capability building for the
members of the Council in areas of regional and local planning, project
development, investment programming, monitoring and evaluation
and other necessary concerns crucial to the functions of the Council;

6. Perform such other functions as may be assigned by the Council.

SECTION 11. Executive Committee — The BEDC shall create an
Executive Committee to act on matters that require immediate attention for and
on behalf of the BEDC, when it is not in session. The Executive Committee shall
be composed of the following:

a) Chief Minister, Chairman

b) BPDA Director General, Vice Chairman

c) Executive Secretary, Member

d) Minister of Finance, Budget and Management, Member
e) All Sectoral Committee Chairpersons, Member

Subject to the internal rules and regulations of the BEDC, the Executive
Committee shall be responsible in overseeing the execution of the economic and
development plans as may be directed by the BEDC.

Other members of the Executive Committee may be determined by the internal
rules and regulations with the appropriate proportion of voting and ex-officio
non-voting members.

Section 15. Transitory Provision. — Upon approval of this Act, the incumbent
Chairperson and the two (2) Commissioners, as well as the Executive Director,
appointed by the interim Chief Minister shall continue to office until the expiration of
their term as provided herein.

During the transition period under the Bangsamoro Transition Authority, appointed
officials and employees of the Commission who do not have the required eligibility shall
be granted a reasonable period within which to meet the eligibility requirements,
pending which they shall be allowed to continue discharging their duties.

Section 16. Repealing Clause. — All previous laws, Executive Orders, other decrees,
and rules and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.

Section 17. Separability Clause. — Should any provision of this Act be found
unconstitutional by a court of law, such provision shall be severed from the remainder of
this Act, and such action shall not affect the enforceability of the remaining provisions of
this Act.

Section 19. Effectivity.— This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Official Gazette or in two (2) newspapers of general circulation in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
