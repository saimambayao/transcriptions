South Ubian is a 3rd class coastal municipality in the province of Tawi-Tawi. It has a population
of more or less 29,583 people in a land area of around 27,204 hectares. Its most recently
published poverty incidence is 25.27% per Philippine Statistics Authority. Presently, their most
proximate access to medical services is a Rural Health Clinic in the neighboring Municipality of
Tandubas which is 22.8 km away by sea. However, the Doctor of that Rural Health Clinic has
now retired, and it is now run only by nurses and midwives. Those who need to avail of proper
medical services have to travel all the way to Bongao, the capital of Tawi-Tawi, where the
closest hospital is situated. The only mode of public transportation is by motor launch or
“lantsa” which is only scheduled to depart three times a week. They have to desperately
traverse around 96 km of the perils ofthe sea for 8 to 10 hours usually while in a condition
not fit for travel. There were many instances of fatalities while in transit and upon arrival in
Bongao because of hardly accessible health services. It is the legal and moral responsibility of
the Bangsamoro Government to alleviate the plight of these people and provide them their
basic right to life, health, and medical services. It is high time that the dividends of peace reach
even our most remote and isolated islands.

Section 8, Article IX of the Bangsamoro Organic Law states that “the Bangsamoro Government
shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs...
tothe Bangsamoro people and other inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.”
Section 22, Article IX of the same law further enunciates that “the Bangsamoro Government
shall adopt a policy on health that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service
delivery for its constituents. It shall establish by law a general hospital system to serve the
health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual's basic right to life shall be
attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.” The
Bangsamoro Organic law aptly emphasizes the importance and priority of providing health
services by statingin Section 19, Article XIl on Fiscal Autonomy that “the Parliament shall assign
the highest budgetary priority to education, health, and social services as may be provided in
its appropriations law.”

“AN ACT ESTABLISHING A LEVEL 1 GENERAL HOSPITAL WITH TWENTY-FIVE BED CAPACITY IN
THEMUNIPALITY OF SOUTH UBIAN, PROVINCE OF TAWI-TAWI TO BE KNOWN AS THE SHEIKH
SALAMAT HASHIM MEMORIAL HOSPITAL AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR”

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Creation. — There is hereby established the “Sheikh Salamat Hashim Memorial
Hospital” in the Municipality of South Ubian, Province of Tawi-Tawi to address the health
service delivery for its constituents.

SECTION 2. Purpose. — The purpose of this act is to establish a level 1 general hospital with
twenty-five bed capacity to provide for a comprehensive and integrated health services to
the people of the Municipality of South Ubian and its neighboring islands. It is an essential
institution in the effective delivery of the health system making health services accessible at
any time to the people in need.

SECTION 4. Services. — The establishment of the hospital shall include required units and
services as the Ministry of Health may deem necessary to carry out the purpose of this Act
integrating into its system of operations the appropriate provisions of Republic Act No. 11223
otherwise known as the Universal Health Care Act.

SECTION 5. Organization; Administrative Supervision — The Sheikh Salamat Hashim
Memorial Hospital shall be under the direct supervision of the Ministry of Health which shall

determine and approve its appropriate organizational structure and plantilla positions.

SECTION 6. Appropriations. — The Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management shall
provide annual funding for the approved plantilla positions and the maintenance and other
operations for the hospital. All its personnel shall be appointed pursuant to the provisions of
the Bangsamoro Administrative Code and the Civil Service Law and other pertinent laws.

The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be charged against
available funds of the Bangsamoro Government. Thereafter, the amount necessary for the

maintenance and operations of the hospital shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act.

SECTION 7. Effectivity — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the

Official Gazette or in two (2) newspapers of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Approved.
