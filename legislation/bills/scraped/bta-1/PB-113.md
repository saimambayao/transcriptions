EXPLANATORY NOTE

This bill aims to make it a legacy of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority under the
leadership of Chief Minister Ahod “Al Haj Murad” Ebrahim and Parliament Speaker Ali Pangalian
Balindong to institutionalize Moral Governance through legislation of a geo-tagging system for all
infrastructure projects.

During his January 21, 2021 speech, Chief Minister Ahod Ebrahim stated in his report:

“The level of transparency in our infrastructure work has been manifested
with the Expanded Bangsamoro Advanced Road Mapping and Management
(E-BARMM) System — an online repository of the Ministry of Public Works
~ BARMM projects. Using geotagged photos for data validation, the core
goals of the E-BARMM System are transparency and efficiency in project
monitoring.”

While geo-tagging is currently in place for the Ministry of Public Works, the system may
in the future no longer be continued. Thus, the best practice may in the future be lost. In order to
safeguard the best practice as being done by the present BARMM,, its institutionalization through
a law is sought. Moreover, this will institutionalize geo-tagging not only in the MPW but in all the
various agencies and instrumentalities of the Bangsamoro that are implementing infrastructure
projects. This will allow for a development reference that include all sectors and improve the
planning and programming for an integrated long term anti-poverty and improved access to social
services program.

The geo-tagging of all infrastructure projects in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region will
allow for more effective policymaking by the regional government agencies as well as National
and local government units, improve the planning and programming of development projects,
determine the location of geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDA) based on the

The Updated Philippine Development Plan 2017-2022 acknowledges geo-tagging as a
means for transparency and better planning and programming. The President Duterte “Build Build
Build” programs have specific implementation reforms which include the mandatory geo-tagging
of projects.

Geo-tagging is an efficient way of tracking the existence and progress of government
projects as it allows government officials and the citizenry alike to check and follow up projects
as they are being carried out in real time.

With the use of a GPS-enabled phone, anyone can easily learn to take geo-tagged photos
and upload to the web-based platforms of the various government agencies.

Geo-tagging is a revolutionary and inexpensive approach to using Information and
Communication Technology (ICT) and Global Positioning System (GPS) applications for accurate
visualization of projects.

One of the benefits of geo-tagging is that it is very cost-effective and safe as it allows for
easier monitoring of progress on the ground and communities without having to undergo the
security risks as well as the logistics costs. This will also provide easy reference for development
partners and implementing agencies as to the location of current development works in order to
better plan for other development interventions. This also serves as a transparency mechanism that
allows the citizens to monitor development in their communities, avoid duplication and
overlapping of infrastructure projects, and eliminate fictitious projects as well as false reporting of
data. This will also allow the government agencies to determine which areas are receiving
development projects and which areas are lagging in terms of infrastructural development.

Moreover, this will become a regional integrated spatial development database that will be
vital regional development planning.

As early as 2013, the National Economic Development Authority hailed and awarded the
Mindanao Rural Development Authority for the ‘Good Practice Award’ for applying geo-tagging
technology for good governance and transparency.

In 2020, National DPWH has required mandatory geo-tagging of all infrastructure projects
regardless of cost, infrastructure type or fund source, in the four stages of the project: before project
implementation, upon mobilization, during the monthly progress and upon completion. This is to
improve efficiency in planning, budgeting, project monitoring, and contract management
processes.

In the National Government, the geo-tagging technology has already been institutionalized
in several executive agencies. Geo-tagging is already institutionalized in the Department of Public
Works and Highways, Department of Agriculture and the Department of Environment and Natural
Resources. The National agencies have used geo-tagging to build up their database and strengthen
development planning and implementation.

In April 2019, RA 11315 was signed by President Rodrigo Duterte which establishes
community-based monitoring system (CBMS). It is “an organized technology-based system of
collecting, processing, and validating necessary disaggregated data for monitoring at the local level
while empowering communities to participate in the process.” The CBMS utilizes geo-tagging
technology together with other data collection methodologies of the National Government and
local governments. The lead agency is the Philippine Statistics Authority.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Regional Infrastructure Geo-
lagging Act of 2021”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the policy of the State to adopt and implement a
policy of full public disclosure, subject to reasonable conditions prescribed by law, and to
recognize the vital role of communication and information in nation-building. Further, the
Bangsamoro Government adopts Moral Governance as its guiding principle in the pursuit of
genuine and meaningful autonomy and ensures the necessary conditions for sustained socio-
economic development by improving accountability of physical and financial outcomes of
government infrastructure projects, eliminating corruption, and ensuring multi-stakeholder
participation.

Pursuant to these policies, the Regional Government recognizes the importance of
transparency and efficiency project monitoring and data-based planning and programming for
equity in development.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. — As used in this Act, the following terms shall mean:

(a) Geo-tagging — is the process of adding metadata and geographical information, usually
in the form of latitude and longitude, to the physical site or location of government
infrastructure projects and of uploading them to a web-based application. It shall also
include the name of the project, the location and the cost of the project to allow the
government, the citizenry, and other stakeholders to check the progress of projects in
real time.

(b) Online Portal —is a specially designed website where relevant information, images, and
map visualization on all government infrastructure projects collected before, during

Section 5. Geo-tagging in the various stages of the Project. — In line with the thrust of the
Bangsamoro Government to improve the efficiency in planning, budgeting, and project
monitoring, geo-tagged photos shall be taken in the following four (4) stages of the project:

Before Project Implementation;
Upon Mobilization;

During the Monthly Progress; and
Upon the Completion of the Project.

ot Se

The uploaded data shall provide information of the name of the project, its cost, the
location, the name of the contractor or implementing agency, and such other relevant information
as required in the Implementing Guidelines provided in the succeeding section.

Section 6. /mplementing Guidelines. — Each concerned Head of the Agency or Office shall
develop the corresponding guidelines within ninety (90) days from the effectivity of this Act which
may be updated annually to ensure that the geotagging procedure and processes, rules and
standards are kept abreast with recent progress and technology. The guidelines define the
responsibilities of each of the units and divisions involved in the implementation of the
infrastructure projects in the Agency.

Section 7. Regionwide Geo-tagging. — Upon the effectivity of this Act, all infrastructure
projects to be implemented by the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be geo-tagged in all
stages of the project implementation by means of a Global Positioning System (GPS)-enabled
phone or other GPS and image capturing devices, which shall be uploaded to the regional online
portal created by virtue of this Act.

Section 8. Agency Online Portal. — All the Implementing Agencies shall include in their
official website an online portal where information pertinent to the specified location of a project
can be accessed. The geo-tagged projects shall be reflected in the respective official websites of
the Implementing Agencies along with the status of each project.

Section 9. Jnformation and I:ducation Campaign. - The Implementing Agencies shall also
conduct information dissemination programs that will inform the public of the existence,
availability, and online accessibility of such transparency mechanisms.

Section 10. Access, Website and Geo-Tagging Regional Portal— The Office of the Chief
Minister, Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office (BICTO), in
coordination with the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) and other
Implementing Agencies, shall set up an online portal to create a Regional Geo-tagging Databank
of the geo-tagged projects to show all the infrastructure projects in geographical areas in the
BARMM. The BICTO shall ensure that the data is updated and synchronized with the
Implementing Agency updates. It shall also regularly coordinate and monitor the Regional Portal
as well as the Agency Online Portals to ensure that these are accessible to the public. It shall also
facilitate institutional arrangements on data sharing.

Section 11. Periodicity of Data Update and Back up of Data. — Regular and synchronized
updating of data shall be conducted by all the Implementing Agencies including BICTO every
quarter of the year. The Implementing Agencies and the BICTO shall ensure that back-up data is
stored to ensure the cumulative data of geo-tagged projects remain available cumulatively every
year.

Section 13. Capacity-building. — The Implementing Agency shall undertake and establish
capacity-building measures for its concerned personnel from the regional level to its provincial
and district levels to develop their respective skills in uploading and generating data and
information on various infrastructure projects, and skills necessary in maintaining an online portal.

Section 14. Appropriations. — An initial amount of Ten Million Pesos (Php 10,000,000.00)
is hereby appropriated and shall be made available to the BICTO to establish the online Regional
Infrastructure Portal for public access as well as assist and conduct capacity building for the
establishment of the online portal in the respective official websites of the Implementing Agencies.
Thereafter, each Implementing Agency shall include in their regular budget the needed funds for
the implementation of this Act.

Section 15. Separability Clause. — If any portion or provision of the Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain in
full force and effect.

Section 16. Repealing Clause. — All laws, parts of laws, issuances, proclamations, orders,
ordinances, and administrative regulations in conflict with the provisions of this Act are hereby
amended, modified, or repealed accordingly.

Section 17. [ffectivity. — This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from its

publication in a newspaper of regional circulation.
