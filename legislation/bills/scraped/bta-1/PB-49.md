EXPLANATORY NOTE

Energy as a basic commodity continually evolves with the pace of the economic
development in any economies in the world. Energy, for it to become a product or a
commodity for commercial use, revenue generation, and economic development
requires exploration, utilization and development.

The Bangsamoro is blessed, that it has within territorial jurisdiction, natural
energy sources that can be explored, utilized, and developed.

It is for the purpose of undertaking the development of energy sources
utilization that this bill is submitted, creating a public corporation which shall be
known as the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro (EDCB). It is
through this, that the Bangsamoro Government with the National Government, shall
identify and select prospective contract areas to be offered for exploration and
development, as embodied in Article XIII Section 10 of republic Act 11054.!

It is recognized that energy related activities and projects contribute substantial
amount to the coffers of governments. With the initiatives, and programs that our
Bangsamoro Government seeks to pursue, diligent efforts must be had to support and
ensure that they are materialized during our time. It is with the EDCB, that we supplant
what we already have. The creation of the EDCB not a mere creation of an income
generating company, it is a move towards improving the quality of lives of the
Bangsamoro and funding the programs that Bangsamoro Government envisions to
pursue.

We are all living witnesses of how we waste resources by allowing idleness. It is
thus a shame if we continually allow to defer development at our time.

We need this vehicle to bring us where we want to be in energy development,
and it is within the authority of the Bangsamoro Government to create the Energy
Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of the bill is earnestly sought.

Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the "Charter of the Bangsamoro
Energy Development Corporation."

Declaration of Policy . — It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to promote economic development through the effective
and efficient utilization of energy sources, for power generation to
achieve sustainable development goals. In line with this policy, the
establishment of an organized entity is necessary in order to supplant
power and energy sources and consequently enable the unimpeded and
efficient growth of the economy.

Creation of the Energy Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro. —
For the purpose of undertaking the development of energy sources
utilization and for other purposes specified in this Act, there is hereby
created a public corporation which shall be known as the as the Energy
Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro. The words "EDCB" and
"Board" appearing in this Act shall respectively refer to the Energy
Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro and the Energy
Development Corporation of the Bangsamoro Board, hereinafter
provided. For aforesaid purpose, the EDCB shall have capacity to sue
and be sued.

Supervision. - The EDCB under the supervision of the Ministry of
Energy and Natural Resources and Economy.

The Minister of Energy and Natural Resources and Economy shall, in a
concurrent capacity, be the ex officio chairman of the EDCB Board, unless
otherwise directed by the Chief Minister: Provided, That in no case shall
the Minister be the chief executive officer or chief operating officer of the
EDCB, any law to the contrary notwithstanding.

Office and Term. - The principal office of the EBDC shall be determined
by its Board of Directors. Provided that it be within the Bangsamoro
Government Territory. It may establish such offices, agencies,
subsidiaries, branches or correspondents in the Philippines or abroad as
its business operations would require.

The EBDC shall have a term of fifty (50) years from approval of this Act,
which shall be deemed renewed for an equal period unless sooner
dissolved by law.

Registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission. - The EDCB
through its Board shall register with the Securities Exchange
Commission within one (1) year from the constitution of the Board.

Definition of Terms. -
"Energy projects" shall mean activities or projects relative to the

exploration, extraction, production, —_importation-exportation,
processing, transportation, marketing, distribution, utilization,

"Energy resources utilization” shall include exploration, discovery,
development, extraction, utilization, refining, processing, transport, and
marketing of all forms of energy resources.

"Energy resources" means any substance, mineral or otherwise, which by
itself or in combination with other substance or after processing or
refining or the application to it of technology emanates, gives off,
generates or causes, the emanation or generation of heat or power or
energy such as, but not limited to, petroleum or oil, coal, marsh gas,
methane gas, geothermal sources of heat and power, uranium and other
minerals and fossils deposits.

Purposes . — The EDCB shall have the following purposes:

a. Toengage in the exploration, utilization, and development of energy
resources within the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro;

b. To engage in the transportation, storage, importation, exportation,
refining, supply, sale and distribution of crude oil, refined
petroleum and petroleum based products, whether imported or
produced by local refineries;

c. To foster conditions relating to energy resources utilization
conducive to a balanced and sustainable growth of the economy; and

d. To undertake, by itself or otherwise, exploration, utilization, and
development of all energy resources of the territorial jurisdiction of
the Bangsamoro, including surveys and activities related thereto.

Powers and Functions of the EDCB . — The EDCB shall have the

following powers and functions:

a. To adopt a code of by-laws to complement this charter;

b. To adopt and use a corporate seal which shall be judicially noticed;

c. To undertake, by itself or otherwise, exploration, utilization and
development of all energy resources within the in the territorial
jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro including surveys and activities
related thereto;

d. To establish, maintain, control and direct in any area within the in
the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro as it may deem
appropriate, and construct, install or maintain therein ports
adequate for the use of vessels engaged in offshore oil drilling
operations, airports sufficient for direct service flights,
telecommunications center and _ ship-to-shore communications
facilities, provide electric power and fresh water supply, and
perform such other acts as it may deem necessary and advantageous
or convenient to such operations;

e. To lease, at reasonable rates, to private domestic entities or persons
within the in the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro such
portion or portions of the petroleum and energy base, including
facilities necessary for warehousing, logistical centers for the storage
of oil drilling and oil well supplies, fabrication of off-shore drilling
components and structures, mechanical repair facilities and the like;
spaces for the office, habitation and recreational requirements of
personnel directly engaged in offshore oil drilling and in manning
the various logistical support operations and their immediate
dependents;

f. To undertake all other forms other energy resources exploitations;

g. Toenter into contracts, with any domestic person or entity, National
Governments agencies, or foreign entities following the conditions

Governing Body . — The EDCB shall be governed by the EDCB Board of
Directors. The Board which shall be composed of nine (9) members, to
be appointed by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government of
the Philippines. The members of the Board shall serve for a term of three
(3) years or until their successors shall have been appointed and
qualified. In case of any vacancy in the Board, the same shall be filled by
the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro Government for the unexpired
term.

No person shall be appointed as member of the Board unless he is a
natural born citizen of the Philippines, at least thirty-five (35) years of
age and of established integrity. The Chairman of the Board, who shall
be the chief executive officer of the EDCB as well as the President of the
EDCB, shall be appointed by the Chief Minister of the Bangsamoro
Government. The other officers of the EDCB shall be appointed by the
Board.

Capital Stock . - The EDCB shall have a capital stock divided into ten

million no-par shares to be subscribed, paid for the voted as follows:

a. One million shares of stock shall be originally subscribed and paid
for by the Bangsamoro Government at an original issue value of P50
per share.

b. The remaining five million shares of stock may be subscribed and
paid for by the Bangsamoro Government or by a Bangsamoro
Government financial institutions at values to be determined by the
Board, but in no case less than the original issued value above stated
to the Bangsamoro Government.

c. The voting power pertaining to shares of stock subscribed by the
government of the Bangsamoro Government shall be vested in the
Chief Minister or in such person or persons as the Parliament may
designate.

d. The voting power pertaining to shares of stock subscribed by the
government financial institutions shall be vested in them.

Duties and Responsibilities of the Chairman of the Board and President
— The Chairman of the Board and the President, shall exercise such
powers and perform such duties as may be provided in the By-Laws or
as may be vested in them by the Board.

Issuance of Bonds. — The EDCB, upon the recommendation of the
Minister of Finance and Budget and Management and with the approval
of the Chief Minister, is hereby authorized to issue bonds or other
securities, which may be guaranteed by the Bangsamoro Government,
to finance its operations.

General Counsel. - The EDCB shall establish a legal division which shall
perform the duties of General Counsel until the creation of the office of
the Solicitor General of the Bangsamoro Government. Any provision of
law to the contrary notwithstanding, the General Counsel and such
personnel as may be necessary to assist him in the performance of his
duties and responsibilities shall receive such allowances as shall be fixed
by the Board.

Appointment, Control and Discipline of Personnel - Provision of law to
the contrary not withstanding, the "Board", upon recommendation of the
President of the EDCB, shall appoint the officers and employees of the
EDCB and its subsidiaries; fix their compensation, allowances and
benefits, their working hours and such other conditions of employment
as it may deem proper; grant them leaves of absence under such
regulations as it may promulgate, discipline and or remove them for
cause; and establish and maintain a recruitment and merit system for the
EDCB and its affiliates and subsidiaries.

The organizational framework and staffing pattern of the EDCB shall be
prescribed and approved by the Chief Minister within sixty (60) days
after the approval of this Act and the authorized positions created
therein shall be filled by regular appointments by the Chief Minister,
subject to existing Bangsamoro Government laws, rules and regulation.

Loans . —The Bangsamoro Government, through the Chief Minister of
the Bangsamoro Government, or his duly authorized representative,
may guarantee, absolutely and unconditionally, as primary obligor, the
payment of the loans, credits and indebtedness secured by the EDCB or
any of its affiliate corporations, for the amount which the Chief Minister
of the Bangsamoro Government has been authorized by its Parliament
subject to limitations under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, as well as the
performance of all or any of the obligations undertaken by the EDCB or
its affiliate corporations in the territory of the Bangsamoro Government
pursuant to loan agreements entered into with foreign governments or
any international financial institutions or fund sources.

Bangsamoro Government Financial Institutions Guarantees. — The
provision of any law to the contrary notwithstanding, any financial
institution owned or controlled by the Bangsamoro Government other
than social insurance institutions, if any, are empowered to guarantee
acceptance credits, loans, transactions, undertakings, or obligations of
any kind which may be incurred by the EDCB, whether directly or
indirectly, in favor of any person, association or entity, whether
domestic or foreign.

Privileges and Incentives . — The EDCB shall be entitled to all the
incentives and privileges granted by existing laws within the within the
territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro, to private enterprises engaged
in energy operations.

or-controlled corporations created under the Bangsamoro Government
shall extend whatever assistance may be needed by the EDCB or any of
its affiliates or subsidiaries, including the detail of its officials and
employees to the EDCB or any of its subsidiaries on full time or part-
time basis under arrangement satisfactory to the EDCB and its affiliates
and subsidiaries and the other office or corporation concerned. Said
