AN ACT ESTABLISHING A PERMANENT SEAT OF GOVERNMENT IN AREAS OF THE
FORMER CAMP ABUBAKAR, PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO, APPROPRIATING
FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority

SECTION 1. Seat of Government. In consonance with the mandate of the Bangsamoro
Organic Law or Republic Act Number 11054, the permanent seat of government in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao is hereby established in the areas
covered by former Camp Abubakar, which border the towns of Matanog, Barira and Buldon,
Province of Maguindanao.

SECTION 2. Funding. The funding for the construction, development and improvement of
the areas where the seat of government shall be established as mentioned in the preceding
section may be taken from the appropriation law to be enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition
Authority, from the appropriation act as enacted by the Congress of the Philippines and/or
from any other sources which is legally viable and feasible.

SECTION 3. Separability Clause. If any part of this law is held to be unconstitutional or
invalid, other parts which are not affected thereby shall remain valid.

SECTION 4. Repealing Clause. All Regional Laws, Rules or Regulations within the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao which are inconsistent herewith are
deemed repealed, modified or amended accordingly.

SECTION 5. Effectivity. This law shall be effective within fifteen (15) from its publication in
a newspaper of regional or local circulation.

EXPLANATORY NOTE

Article X Section 15 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that:

“There shall be created autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and
in the Cordilleras consisting of provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical
areas sharing common and distinctive historical and cultural heritage, economic
and social structures, and other relevant characteristics within the framework of
this Constitution and the national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the
Republic of the Philippines.”

Furthermore, Article Vil Section 1 of Republic Act 11054 otherwise known as the

Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, provides that:

“Seat of Government - The Parliament shall fix by law the permanent
seat of the Bangsamoro Government anywhere within the territorial
jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region taking into consideration
accessibility and efficiency in which its mandate may be carried out under this

Organic Law.”

Relative thereto, this Bill seeks to establish the permanent seat of the newly formed
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in the former Camp Abubakar and

appropriate funds for the development thereof.

Significantly, the former Camp Abubakar, used to be the main and biggest camp of the
Moro Islamic Liberation Front. The former Camp Abubakar is a place of strong symbolism of the
Bangsamoro struggle. With the establishment of the BARMM, which came as a result of the
victory of the long time struggle for self-determination of the Bangsamoro people, it is but proper
to make the former Camp Abubakar, the permanent seat of the Bangsamoro Government.

Geographically, establishing the permanent seat of the Bangsamoro Government in the
former Camp Abubakar, being spread over the towns of Matanog, Barira, and Buldon, will
promote an increase economic activity in the area, which will further spread in the other nearby
towns. Further, the former Camp Abubakar is accessible to some BARMM areas such as the
Province of Lanao del Sur and the Barangays in North Cotabato that joined BARMM. It is also
nearer to Zamboanga Peninsula, which is the gateway of our Muslim Brothers and Sisters
coming from the Island Provinces (Part of BARMM) Sulu, Basilan and Tawi-Tawi. Not to
mention also its proximity to Davao Peninsula and Region Ten (X), which is composed of
Lanao del Norte, the two Misamis (Occidental and Oriental) Provinces, Bukidnon and Camiguin.

Moreover, as to efficiency, the former Camp has its size of approximately 7,000
hectares, hence, it is capable of accommodating the growing size of the Bangsamoro
Government and the offices within the BARMM. If the area will be chosen as the permanent
seat of the Bangsamoro Government, economic development will necessarily boom in the
neighboring areas and thus, peace and order problem will be contained. Nearest to the
proposed seat of government are several sea ports, the Polloc Port in Parang, Maguindanao
and the coastal towns of Lanao del Sur. Airport can also be established in the area such as in
the municipality of Malabang and in other neighboring areas. The areas can become the biggest
economic hub in Mindanao due to the potential of its vast and pristine land and sea. As one of
the political economist said: “ If there is economic development, necessarily the unstable peace
and order condition will be contained as the people will be satisfied on their economic standing.”
Because of its vast area, it can be turn to another Putra Jaya in Malaysia, where all government
offices including Building for the Parliament were situated. Hence, on this note, | am earnestly
appealing to my fellow members of the Parliament for the immediate approval of this proposed
bill.
