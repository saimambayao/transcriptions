PROPOSED ACI/BILL TO ADDRESS THE DECADES OF SOCIAL
INEQUALITY, NEGLECT AND POVERTY IN THE BANGSAMORO
AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO

RATIONALITY OF THE PROPOSED ACT /BILL

The Bangsamoro region always lag behind in terms of development. Government
neglect, corruption, inadequate programs for social and economic development
including environment protection are the perennial causes of poverty in the
Bangsamoro. A very shameful statistic shows the poorest provinces in the Philippines
come from Bangsamoro. They top the list despite huge fiscal support and allocation
from national government and foreign support programs. Reality wise, the current
socio-economic demographics of the Bangsamoro spawns corruption in all levels of
government, influences the unstable peace and order, and reinforces the climate of

injustice and social disorder in this part of the country.

Upon these premises, there is a need to fuse and converge National and Sub-National
Government effort to address these decades old problem and there is the urgent need
of legislating policies and programs to address and eradicate this problem of poverty

and neglect.

Nonetheless, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, being at the
center of this perennial problem of poverty and neglect, must assert and take action
without waiting for the national government. Under the general welfare clause of
Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (OLBARMM) or the Bangsamoro Organic
Law (BOL), the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament must establish short to
medium term policies that target the reduction of social inequalities and poverty
incidence in the region to acceptable levels. The BTA must provide for the creation of
a Social Action Commission spearheaded by the Interim Chief Minister that will
formulate strategies and programs to implement such policies.

At the national level, while the President’s priorities are to eliminate social problems
on drugs, criminalities, and corruption happening in different parts of the country, the
economic situation in the region must be recognized as a push factor in the context of
another political-social phenomenon, Violent Extremism, and this compels a whole of
nation approach in fighting it.

At the end of the day, an impoverished Bangsamoro generates a whole lot of other
problems impacting not only the BARMM but the whole Philippine Archipelago and
the South East Asian region as well. As such, The BARMM Government without
waiting for national government action must look after its own no matter the amount
of resources available. It must lead the way in the fight to end poverty in the
Bangsamoro through meaningful legislation and direct executive action.

AN ACT CREATING THE BANGSAMORO SOCIAL ACTION COMMISSION

SECTION 1. Declaration of Policy. — It shall be the declared policy of the
government of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)
+o address the root causes of social inequality and poverty in the region, formulate its
own solutions, to promote and uplift the dignity and welfare of the constituents
coming from all walks of life, and liberate them from the bondage of poverty and
neglect. Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Regional Government shall give its full
support to all national programs and cooperate with the national government in the
formulation and implementation of high impact programs directed towards the
eradication of social inequality, neglect and poverty in the BARMM

SECTION 2. Creation. — To realize this policy, there is hereby created a
Bangsamoro Social Action Commission (BASAC), hereinafter referred to as the
“COMMISSION” which shall be under the Office of the Chief Minister of the
Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 3. Aims and Purposes. — The Aims and Purposes of this Commission
are the following:

1. Motivate and encourage the poor to stand and assert their right to live and to
lead in a decent life through active involvement and participation in community
development programs and activities;

2, Eradicate exploitation and discrimination through comprehensive
development and poverty alleviation programs;

3. To help fast-tract government projects and programs geared towards
combating, eradicating or alleviating poverty;

4. To encourage and train BARMM constituents to fight poverty by providing
them the jobs and livelihood opportunities and to properly utilize their own
basic indigenous knowledge, labor and time;

5. To coordinate and collaborate with the national government in the
implementation of its programs on poverty alleviation in the territory of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

SECTION 4. Composition. — The Commission shall be composed of one (1)
Chairman and four (4) Commissioners who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister,
provided however, that in the appointment process the chief minister shall endeavor
to ensure representation of all the major tribes in the Commission.

SECTION 5. Qualifications and Appointment. — No person shall be appointed
as Chairman or Members of the Commission unless he or she possess the following
qualifications:

1. Acitizen of the Philippines and an inhabitant, resident and registered voter of
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao for at least five (5)
years prior to appointment;

2, At least twenty — five (25) years of age on the day of his/her appointment;

3. The Chairman and Commissioners must be able to read and write and have
wide-ranging knowledge and understanding on the current issues about
poverty and other socio-economic issues in the Bangsamoro.

4. The appointment of the chairman and members of the Commission shall be
subject to the rules of Civil Service Commission and other pertinent laws,

ON 6. Compensation. - The Chairperson shall receive an annual compensation of
Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Pesos (P750,000.00), while the Commissioners shall
each receive an annual compensation of Six Hundred Sixty Thousand Pesos
(P660,000.00). Such salaries are subject to accross-the-board increase and
adjustment as may be allowed by law and existing rules and regulations. In addition,
each shall be entitled to all benefits and privileges corresponding to their respective
positions and may be authorized by law.

SECTION 7. Powers and Functions. — The Chairman shall be the administrative
head of the Commission and shall be assisted by the Commissioners as members in
the performance of his duties. Specifically, the Chairman shall exercise the following
powers and functions:

1. Approve and sign voucher, payroll, check, reinvestment, and other
disbursement as may be authorized by law;

2. Recommend the appointment, termination, suspension or discipline of
personnel of the Commission to the Chief Minister;

3. Lead the Commission in the formulation and adoption of a strategic plan to for
poverty alleviation and reduction with the participation and involvement of the
government and non-government organizations, and people’s organizations;

4. Organize non-governmental organizations, such as: foundations, cooperatives,

5. Represent the poor sector of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim

SECTION 8. Staff. — The Commission shall have and expert man power for
planning, education and trainings, administrative and finance divisions with allowable
extended staff of at least twenty (20) members each, without prejudice to employment
of additional personnel when the need may arise.

SECTION 9. Coordination. — The Commission shall coordinate closely with the
regional line agencies, such as: Ministry of Trade and Industry, Ministry of Social
Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture and Fisheries, Ministry of Agrarian Reform,
Cooperative Development Authority, and to all other concern Government Agencies,
NGO’s and People’s Organizations for all programs and strategies it may undertake
and design to uplift and advance the welfare of people in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.

SECTION 10. Sources of Funds. — For the initial organization and operation of
the Commission, the sum of One Hundred Million Pesos (PHP 100, 000,000.00) shall
be funded out of the unappropriated portion of the local funds of the Bangsamoro
Regional Government of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
is hereby appropriated for the purpose, and/or the Office the BARMM Chief Minister
shall incorporate the same for subsequent budgeting from the national government.
The Commission shall likewise be authorized to solicit and generate any form of
assistance from philanthropic organizations of both national and international donors
to augment its funding initial requirement.

SECTION 11. Audit, — All financial transactions accounts and expenditures of the
Commission shall be subjected to existing accounting and auditing rules as provided
in the R.A. No. 11054 otherwise known as the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao and other existing laws.

SECTION 12. Separability Clause. — If any part or section or provision hereof

shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, the other provisions thereof not affected
by such declaration shall remain in full force and effect.
