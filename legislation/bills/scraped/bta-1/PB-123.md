EXPLANATORY NOTE

Section 22, Article IX of R.A. No. 11054, otherwise known as the Organic Law for
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao provides that the
Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy on health that provides for a
comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents. It shall
establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people
and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt
intervention of excellent and affordance medical services. The Bangsamoro Government
shall protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health
consciousness among them. It shall also uphold the people’s right to have access to
essential goods, health, and other social services that promote their well-being.

Hospitals play a vital role in the health care system. Hospitals are health care
institutions that have an organized medical and other qualified staff to deliver medical,
nursing, radiology, laboratory and related services every day and all the time. Hospitals
offer a variable range of acute, convalescent and terminal care using diagnostic,
preventive and curative services in response to acute and chronic conditions arising from
diseases as well as injuries and genetic abnormalities. With all these various services,
hospitals have been able to generate essential information for research, expertise,
education and management. Hospitals are important to treat minor and serious diseases,
illnesses and disorders of the body function of varying types and severity. It also helps
promote health, give information on prevention of illnesses and as well offer curative
services. It provides a safe environment for people who are not safe in their homed or safe
in the community because of either their infectious state or because of abuse of any type.
Hospitals also create jobs for personnel like the nurses, medical doctors and other related
health personnel. Hospital environment also expand medical and nursing knowledge
thereby promoting research in various fields.

The Municipality of Butig is among the isolated political subdivisions of Lanao del
Sur. Considering its geographic location, establishing a hospital thereat will also benefit
Butig’s neighbouring municipalities. A recent visit by the Members of the Parliament in
the area revealed the need for public infrastructure that will serve its constituents. One of
the recommendations was the establishment of a municipal hospital that will answer the
medical needs of the political subdivision’s constituents.

In view of the foregoing, it becomes a matter of utmost urgency and necessity for
the Parliament to enact a law that will adequately and effectively address the concerns of
the Bangsamoro constituents.

The proposed measure seeks to establish a Butig Municipal Hospital thus
increasing the reach of the medical services and assistance of the regional government.

It is for these reasons that the early approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

ary, MMGRERE DANDY -LATIPH

Member of Parliament

AN ACT ESTABLISHING A TWENTY-FIVE-BED MUNICIPAL HOSPITAL IN
MUNICIPALITY OF BUTIG, PROVINCE OF LANAO DEL SUR AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Creation. - There shall be established, under the supervision of the
Ministry of Health, a twenty-five-bed municipal hospital in the Municipality of Butig, to
be known as the Butig Municipal Hospital.

SECTION 2. Hospital Organization; Bed Capacity. — The organization of the
hospital shall include such units and services as the Ministry of Health may deem
essential to carry on with the purpose of this Act but shall in no case be contrary to the
basic organizational plans prescribed by the Ministry of Health, the Department of
Health nor with the principles of sound hospital administration.

It shall likewise maintain facilities and wards for emergency cases as well as those for
out-patients and domiciliary services. The hospital shall have a bed capacity of twenty-
five (25).

All personnel and employees of the hospital shall be appointed in accordance with the
powers vested by the Bangsamoro Administrative Code. In coordination with the
Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management and the Civil Service Commission, the
Ministry of Health shall determine the plantilla positions to be created and filled up.

SECTION 3. Supervision by the Ministry of Health. — The Butig Municipal
Hospital shall be under the supervision of the Ministry of Health.

SECTION 4. Appropriation. - The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of
this Act shall be charged against the annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act passed by
the Bangsamoro Parliament. Thereafter, the amount necessary for the continued
operation of the hospital shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.

SECTION 5. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its

publication in the Official Gazette or in two (2) newspapers of general circulation in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
