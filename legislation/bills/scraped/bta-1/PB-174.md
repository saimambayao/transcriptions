EXPLANATORY NOTE

Kutawatu Cave, located in Brgy. Rosary Heights 1 in Cotabato City, is among
the numerous attractions that can be found in the Bangsamoro Region that is rich in
historical and cultural values.

During the Spanish subjugation, the cave became the refuge of our forefathers to
avoid being forcibly converted to Christianity. At the height of the Japanese invasion,
the same cave served the many purposes of our guerillas.

Among its unique features is the fact that it is the only cave in the entire country
that is located in the heart of the city and has many hidden underground passageways,
one of which connects to the oldest church in the city, the Tamontaka Church. It is also
the source of the city’s name.

Yet, aside from the modest efforts of the City Government of Cotabato to secure
the same, no importance was given to it by the national agencies.

Fortunately, our Mujahideen, some of whom are members of this august body,
had succeeded in securing for the Bangsamoro, our rights to self-governance and the
freedom to pursue our own political, economic, social, and cultural development. For
this purpose, Section 24 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law mandates the Bangsamoro
Government to “preserve the history, culture, arts traditions, and the rich cultural
heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their Sultanates, such as those of Sulu,
Maguindanao, Kabuntalan, Buayan, the Royal House of Ranao and of the Iranun, and
the non-Moro indigenous peoples of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.”

It, therefore, behooves on us that we take steps to protect and preserve this
monument as a source of our pride and part of our history of struggle. This proposed

bill is among our small steps to preserve our history, culture, and heritage.

I, therefore, seek your support for the passage of this bill.

HON. MP ENGR. D HA ARB am MPSA, CESE

126 Section 1. The “Kutawato Cave," located in Barangay Rosary Heights 1,

127. Cotabato City, is hereby declared as a historical heritage site and an ecotourism
128 destination. As such, its preservation, development, and promotion shall be jointly
129 prioritized by the Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Tourism (MTIT) and the
130 Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH).

concerned agencies of government, shall prepare a historical heritage site conservation
plan and an ecotourism development plan containing, among others, the conservation,
preservation, rehabilitation, construction, installation and maintenance of such
appropriate facilities and infrastructure that shall enhance tourism in the area; Provided,

Section 3. The MTIT and the BCPCH, in coordination with the Ministry of
Public Works (MPW), the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy
(MENRE), and the City Government of Cotabato shall take immediate steps to
implement the site preservation and ecotourism development plan for “Kutawato
Cave.” It shall incorporate the same in the Regional Tourism Development Plan that the
MTIT shall hereafter develop.

Section 4. The MTIT, BCPCH, Ministry of Public Works (MPW), and MENRE
shall include the implementation of this Act in their respective programs, the funding
of which shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act (BAA).

Section 5. Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the MTIT and
the BCPCH shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the proper
implementation of this Act.

Section 6. All regional laws, rules, regulations inconsistent with any of the
provisions of this Act are hereby deemed repealed and amended accordingly.

Section 7. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the
Bangsamoro Gazette or a newspaper of regional circulation.
