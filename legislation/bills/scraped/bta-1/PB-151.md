EXPLANATORY NOTE

The healthcare workers are at the forefront of the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic
which puts them at high risk for|infection. The sacrifice of our doctors, nurses and other health
workers to serve the sick and those afflicted with the fatal novel coronavirus in order to protect the
Bangsamoro Region should not be forgotten and deserves the highest commendation.

Article II, Section 15 of |the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the State shall
protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them.
It is also the policy of the State to promote social justice in all aspects of life, and promote the
general welfare of the people.

The Bangsamoro Government, for its part, recognizes the efforts and the paramount
importance of both public and private health workers for their continuous delivery of health
services, disease prevention and promotion of health in general.

These frontliners who made the ultimate sacrifice to fight against the pandemic by
rendering service to the Bangsamoro Region are entitled to be provided with financial assistance.
No amount will ever be enough to repay them for their significant contribution to the fight against
COVID-19, but through this initiative, the families they left behind are supported and taken care
of.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Region shall ensure the welfare and adequate support
for our health workers and guarantee that all benefits owing to them shall be provided to them with
utmost efficiency especially during the existence of a pandemic.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as “Compensation for Health Workers
Act of 2021.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. —Itis the policy of the State to instill health consciousness
among our people, promote social justice in all aspects of life, encourage community services and
volunteerism, and entice trained health professionals to render services during this period of the
pandemic, by granting adequate compensation and benefits commensurate with their services.

The Regional Government recognizes the paramount importance of public and private
health workers for the continuous delivery of health services, disease prevention and promotion of
health in general.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. — For purposes of this Act, the following definition of terms
is hereby adopted:

(a) Allied Medical Personnel - are allied health workers such as medical technologists,
respiratory therapists, dentists, radiographers, pharmacists, dietitians, and others regardless of the
nature of employment in hospitals and health facilities in the BARMM who are attending or
exposed to persons who are classified as either suspected, probable, or confirmed COVID-19
cases.

(b) Compensation - shall refer to the amount of Thirty Thousand Pesos (P30,000.00) given
to the public and private healthcare worker who dies as a result of COVID-19 infection while in
the performance of duty in this time of the pandemic.

(c) Healthcare Workers) - shall refer to all public and private medical personnel who
regardless of the nature of employment in hospitals and health facilities, medical or temporary
treatment, and monitoring facilities or vaccination sites situated within the BARMM.

(d) Work in the performance of duty - includes all acts performed by the public and private
health workers in relation to their legal duty as such, or as required by their immediate supervisor.
It shall include the time spent traveling from home to the place of work and vice versa.

Section 4. Coverage. - This Act shall apply to all public and private health workers
regardless of the nature of employment in hospitals and health facilities in the BARMM who may
die as a result of COVID-19 disease while in the performance of duty in this time of the pandemic.

Section 5. Grant of COVID-19 Compensation. - The Bangsamoro Government shall grant
compensation in the amount of Thirty Thousand Pesos (P30,000.00) to heirs of health workers
who may die as a result of COVID-19 disease contracted while in the performance of duty in this
time of the pandemic.

Provided, That compensation to these health workers pursuant to this Act is without
prejudice to other existing benefits provided under existing laws, the applicable government
insurance systems and their governing laws. Provided, further, that the compensation herein shall
be given to the beneficiaries not/later than three (3) weeks following the death of health worker
and upon submission of complete and compliant documentary requirements.

Section 6. Appropriations. — The amount necessary for the implementation of this Act
shall be charged against the current year available appropriations of the Ministry of Health (MOB),
and any sources available as may be identified by the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and
Management (MFBM). Thereafter, the amount necessary for its continuous implementation shall
be included in the succeeding Bangsamoro Appropriations Act (BAA).

Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The MOH and MFBM, in consultation
with other ministries and concerned stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary implementing
rules and regulations within thirty (30) days from the effectivity of this Act: Provided, That the
non-promulgation of the rules and regulations provided under this section shall not prevent the
immediate implementation of this Act upon effectivity.

Section 8. Separability Clause. - If any of the sections or provisions of this Act is adjudged
invalid, all its other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in force.

Section 9. Repealing Clause. — All laws, parts of laws, issuances, proclamations, orders,
ordinances, and administrative regulations in conflict with the provisions of this Act are hereby
amended, modified, or repealed accordingly.

Section 10. Effectivity. -|This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following its

complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.
