EXPLANATORY NOTE

Established in November 5, 1916, the Sulu Provincial Hospital is the main referral center with
patients coming from all 19 municipalities in Sulu catering to more than 900,000 people. The
century-old provincial hospital began with 30 hospital beds and after several decades was
augmented to merely 100 beds despite the significant increase in population and demand. To
highlight its importance, before the COVID-19 pandemic, the 5-year average bed occupancy rate
of the Sulu Provincial Hospital was at a staggering 121%.

However, pursuant to the 2013 Department of Health (DOH) Classification of Hospitals, there is
no Level 1 Hospital with a 100-bed capacity. According to the DOH guidelines, a hospital with a
100-bed capacity should be classified as a Level II Hospital. Hence, the Sulu Provincial Hospital
is severely under-staffed because of the lack of a significant number of plantilla positions which
are only accorded for Level II Hospitals. :

In addition, as a Level I Hospital, the Sulu Provincial Hospital does not have a provision for
intensive management of patients needing critical care. Patients needing Intensive Care Unit
(ICU) admissions are only treated in the wards, which makes it difficult for the hospital staff to
provide the required care for such patients — and have sufficient resources — they cannot offer
more than what is authorized. More importantly, the inadequacy can lead to poor patient
outcomes.

Lastly, there is only one Level II Hospital in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region — the
Maguindanao Provincial Hospital, which is distant from those residing in the BARMM Island
Provinces.

Section 22, Article IX of the Bangsamoro Organic Law mandates the Bangsamoro Government
to adopt a policy on health that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service
delivery for its constituents. It requires the enactment of legislation of a general hospital system
to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life
shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.

The Organic Law further directs the protection and promotion of the right to health of the people,
upholding their right to have access to essential goods, health, and other social services that
promote their well-being.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

Section 1. The Sulu Provincial Hospital is hereby converted into a Level II Hospital,
under the direct supervision and control of the Ministry of Health (MOH), Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The authorized bed capacity of the provincial hospital is hereby increased from one
hundred (100) to two hundred (200) beds.

Section 2. The existing service facilities and other services of the hospital are hereby
upgraded to conform with the bed capacity increase as provided for in Section 1 hereof.

The existing medical personnel shall also be correspondingly increased. The Ministry of
Health, in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management and the Civil
Service Commission, shall determine the additional plantilla positions to be created and filled up
in coordination with the management of the Sulu Provincial Hospital.

Section 3. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Government shall provide the necessary funds
to support the increase in bed capacity of the Sulu Provincial Hospital and the corresponding
upgrading of its facilities and services.

The Minister of the Ministry of Health (MOH), in consultation with the management of
the Sulu Provincial Hospital, shall issue orders, rules and circulars as may be necessary to carry
out the implementation of this Act. The Minister of the MOH shall immediately include in the
Ministry’s programs the implementation of this Act, the funding of which shall be included in
the General Appropriations Act.

Section 4. The provisions of this Act are hereby declared separable, and in the event one
or more such provisions or part thereof are declared unconstitutional, such declaration of
unconstitutionality shall not affect the validity of the other provisions thereof.
