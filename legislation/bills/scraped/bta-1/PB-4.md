Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority assembled:

SECTION 1. TITLE. - This Act shall be known as
the "BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM
MINDANAO ACT OF 2019”

SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION AND USE OF FUNDS. — The
sum of Ten Billion One Hundred Three Million Eight Hundred Sixteen
Thousand Pesos (PhP 10,103,816,000.00) is hereby appropriated to fund the
public works, infrastructure and development projects in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), as listed in the List
of Projects attached hereto as Annex and made an integral part of this Act.

PROVIDED, thatthe release and use of the fund shall be subject to the
provisions of Republic Act No. 11054 or the Bangsamoro Organic Law
(BOL), and other pertinent laws and official issuances.

SECTION 3.VALIDITY OF APPROPRIATIONS. — _ The
Appropriations authorized in this Act shall be valid and effective for the
fiscal year 2019 and beyond, until fully released, obligated and disbursed,
but subject to law, rules and regulations.

SECTION 4. ALLOCATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — The
amount appropriated in this Act shall be allocated and distributed to the
provinces, cities, municipalities and barangays constituting the BARMM,
according to the List of Projects attached hereto.

SECTION 5. PROGRAM OF WORKS. Before the actual
implementation of the identified projects, there shall be a Program of Works
duly approved by the Minster of Public Works or his duly authorized
representative, which Program of Works shall serve as basis for
disbursement and expenditure of the funds.

SECTION 6. PROJECT IMPLEMENTATION. - All projects
covered by this Act shall be implemented by the Ministry of Public Works —
BARMM or may be delegated by the latter to the District Engineering
Offices (DEOs).

Implementation and disbursement of funds shall be subject to
appropriate laws, rules and regulations.

SECTION 7. RELEASE OF FUNDS. - The funds shall be released
to the Ministry of Public Works - BARMM.

The Ministry of Public Works - BARMM is authorized to deduct a
maximum of three percent (3%) from the appropriated amount of the
infrastructure projects as Engineering and Administrative Overhead (EAO)
Expenses, to be used in the following:

1. Administrative overhead including the hiring of individuals
but only under Job Orders or Contracts of Service or such
other form of engagements where there is no employer-
employee relationship;

2. Pre-construction activities after detailed engineering;
3. Construction project management;
4. Testing and quality control;

5. Acquisition, rehabilitation and repair of heavy equipment,
motor vehicles, laboratory apparatus, survey instruments and
other equipment, machineries and implements of the
Ministry;

6. Contingencies in relation to pre-construction activities,
PROVIDED THAT, the EAO expenses shall be treated /
booked as capitalized expenditures and shall form part of the
project cost PROVIDED FURTHER, that EAO deduction
shall not be authorized for projects costing below One

SECTION 8. MONITORING OF PROJECTS. — The Ministry of
Public Works — BARMM< shall closely monitor and conduct periodic
inspection and validation of the status of project implementation based on
the approved Program of Work, project specifications, and time frame of
completion, among others.

SECTION 9. PROJECT REALIGNMENT AND CONVERSION.
— The Regional Planning and Development Office (RPDO — BARMM) may
recommend the realignment or conversion of projects to the Bangsamoro
Transition Authority for appropriate action or legislation subject to the
approval of the Chief Minister, when the conversion or realignment thereof
warrants, PROVIDED, that any realignment or conversion shall not exceed
the original amount of the project, PROVIDED FURTHER, that splitting or
clustering of projects may be allowed and funded out of such funds within
the legislative district, PROVIDED FINALLY, that the following grounds
are present:

1. The peace and order situation in the proposed project site
prevents the orderly implementation of the project;

2. The project is not feasible as shown by a feasibility study
conducted thereon;

3. A fortuitous event renders the implementation of the project
impractical at the proposed site;

4. There is duplicity in the funding of the project and/or
overlapping of identification as when a proposed project is
already adequately funded from other sources; and

5. Abandonment for just, reasonable and valid grounds.

SECTION 10. COORDINATION. — The Ministry of Public Works
— BARMM shall closely coordinate with project proponents, contractors,
and all stakeholders in order to ensure the smooth and _ timely
implementation of the projects.

SECTION 11. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE. - If any provision of
this Act is declared invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the other
remaining unaffected provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION 12. REPEALING CLAUSE. - All laws, rules and
regulations, orders and issuances, which are inconsistent with this Act are
hereby revoked, amended or modified accordingly.

SECTION 13. EFFECTIVITY. - This Act shall take effect fifteen
(15) days following its complete publication in the Official Gazette a
newspaper of general circulation.
