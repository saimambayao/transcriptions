Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the Bangsamoro Irrigation Act of 2020.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — Under Section 37, Article XIII. Public Works and
Infrastructure. - The National Government shall fund and implement the construction and
maintenance of the national roads, bridges, water supply and services, and flood control and
irrigation systems and for the maintenance of the existing airports, seaports, and wharves in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region: Provided, That with regard to water supply and services, flood
control and irrigation systems that connect or from facilities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region, there shall be cooperation and coordination between the Bangsamoro Government and the
appropriate national or local government bodies. All national roads and bridges in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region shall be included in the National Road Network Information System.
Nationally-funded infrastructure projects shall be implemented by the National Government.

The Bangsamoro Government shall submit proposals to the appropriate national
government agency for the inclusion of the cost of such maintenance in the latters' budget that
shall be submitted to the Congress of the Philippines for inclusion in the General Appropriations
Act. Funding for national roads, bridges, and irrigation systems shall be regularly released to the
relevant departments of the National Government’.

TITLE I
BANGSAMORO IRRIGATION OFFICE

Section 3. Bangsamoro Irrigation Office Creation. — There is hereby created Bangsamoro
Irrigation Office, herein after referred to as the “Office”. The Chief Minister shall exercise

Section 4. Mandate. — To serve as the primary office to formulate, plan, recommend, coordinate,
administer and implement policies and undertake systematic development program for managing
and monitoring the condition and status of the irrigation projects implemented and to be
implemented.

Section 5. Composition. — The Office shall be composed of one (1) Regional Irrigation Manager
and four (4) Area Managers representing the territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro regions
namely, the Province of Basilan including Lamitan City, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, Lanao del Sur including
Marawi City, and Maguindanao including Cotabato City and the 63 barangays of North Cotabato.

Section 6. Qualification. — No person shall be appointed as Regional Manager or an Area
Manager, unless he/she possesses the following qualifications:

(1) A citizen of the Philippines;
(2) At least 35 years of age at the time of appointment;

(3) A resident and a registered voter of Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao;

(4) Holder of a Bachelor’s degree;
(5) Have occupied positions with irrigation management responsibility; and
(6) Have not been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude.

Section 7. Appointment, Term of Office and Removal from Office. - In constituting this Act,
the Chief Minister shall appoint one (1) Regional Manager and four (4) Area Managers.

Appointment, Term of Office and Removal from Office shall be in conformity with Civil
Service law, rules and regulations.

Section 8. Compensation. - The Regional Manager and the four (4) Area Managers shall be
entitled to compensation with Salary Grade 26 and 25 respectively.

TITLE If
POWERS AND FUNCTIONS
Section 9. Powers and Functions. - The Office shall have the following powers and functions:

(a) To investigate, study, improve, construct and administer all irrigation systems within
the Bangsamoro regions;

(b) To investigate all available and possible water resources in the region for the purpose
of utilizing the same for irrigation, and to plan, design and construct the necessary projects
to make farming convenient to Bangsamoro populace;

(c ) To do such other things, to transact all businesses as are directly or indirectly
necessary, incidental or conducive to the attainment of the above objectives’; and

(e) To render annual report to the Office of the Chief Minister on its activities, projects and
programs.

TITLE Hil
ORGANIZATION AND STRUCTURE

Section 9. The Office as a Collegial Body. - The Bangsamoro Irrigation Office is composed of
one (1) Regional Manager and four Area Managers. As a collegial body, the Office shall
promulgate rules, orders, and resolutions necessary for the efficient conduct of its business and
administration, towards the performance of its mandates.

Section 10. Structural Organization of the Office. — The Office shall have the following offices
and units;

1. Office of the Regional Manager;
2. Offices of the Area Managers;

3. Office of the Executive Director which shall be the head Secretariat of the Office and
shall be in charged with the over-all supervision of its operations;

4. Administrative and Finance Services Division which shall provide administrative
support to the Office and to its division and offices;

5. Engineering and Technical Services Division which shall be responsible for providing
the Office with efficient and effective services in terms of designing, planning,
implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

Section11. Offices of the Regional Manager and Four (4) Area Managers. — Regional manager
shall have his/her office at Cotabato City and each Area Manager shall have an office in the
province that he/she represents.

Section 12. Access to funds. — The budget allocated for the Office shall be directly released to it,
for efficiency of operation, subject to usual accounting and auditing rules and regulations.

TITLE IV
APPROPRIATION

Section 13. Appropriations. - The initial amount of Twenty Million Pesos (PhP 20,000,000.00)
for Personnel Services, Five Million Pesos (PhP 5,000,000.00) for the Miscellaneous and Other
Operating Expenses, and One Million Pesos (PhP 1,000,000.00) for the Capital Outlay of the
Office shall be sourced from the Miscellaneous Personnel Benefits Fund (MPBF) and the
Contingent Fund respectively in accordance to Bangsamoro Act No. 3, subject to existing rules
and regulations. Additional funding for personnel services, maintenance and other operating
expenses and capital outlay maybe requested from the MPBF and Contingent Fund. Subsequent
funding requirements shall be included in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

TITLE V
FINAL PROVISIONS

Section 14. Post legislative scrutiny. - Five years after the implementation of this Act there shall
be a post legislative scrutiny to review, examine, overlook and evaluate the performance of the
Office to be conducted by the Office of the Chief Minister and the latter shall advise, recommend
_ and suggest necessary measures for more efficient execution of its mandate.

Section 15. Separability Clause. - In case any clause, sentence, or provision of these rules and
regulations or any portion hereof is held or declared unconstitutional or invalid by a competent
