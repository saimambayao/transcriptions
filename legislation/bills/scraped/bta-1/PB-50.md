Explanatory Note

Consistent with the provision of RA 6734 that established the first
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, one of the important legislations
enacted by the first Regional Legislative Assembly is Muslim Mindanao Act No.
10-An Act creating the Office of the Regional Treasury to:

a) Act as principal custodian of all funds of the Autonomous Regional
Government;

b) Formulate annual projections of revenue needs, cash position and
borrowing capacity of the Regional Government, and

c) Adopt implementing strategies for efficient collections of revenues,
taxes, fees and charges.

Muslim Mindanao Act No. 287 or the ARMM Administrative Code
underscored that it is a declared policy of the Autonomous Regional
Government that the Regional Treasury shall be responsible for the evaluation
of a system of sound and efficient management of financial resources and to
ensure that resources are generated and managed in accordance with regional
law and applicable existing national policies.

The role of this revenue agency in the revenue administration and fiscal
management functions of the Bangsamoro Government has been emphasized
by the Organic Law that established the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao.

Specifically, Section 4, Article XII on Fiscal Autonomy stipulates that
there is hereby created a Bangsamoro Treasury Office whose primary function
is to receive and safeguard all the revenues generated and collected by the
Bangsamoro Government. The Office shall be headed by a Regional Treasurer
who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister and whose term shall be
determined by a law to be passed by the Parliament.

Given the foregoing statements, the early passage of this important
legislation is respectfully sought.

Be it enacted by the members of the BTA Parliament of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as “Bangsamoro
Treasury Act of 2020”.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.- It is a declared policy of the
Bangsamoro Government that it shall have the power to create its own sources
of revenues and to levy taxes, fees and charges, subject to the provisions of
the Organic Law consistent with the principles of equalization, equity,
accountability, administrative simplicity, harmonization and economic
efficiency, and fiscal autonomy. Such taxes, fees and charges shall accrue
exclusively to the Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 3. Establishment of the Office. - To carry out the
aforementioned policy as stated in Section 6, Article XII, there is hereby
established a Bangsamoro Treasury Office for the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao whose primary function is to receive and
safeguard all revenues generated and collected by the Bangsamoro
Government.

SECTION 5. Organizational Structure and Staffing. - The Bangsamoro
Treasury Office shall be headed by a Regional Treasurer who shall act and
exercise the powers and functions of a Chief Executive Officer. He/she shall be
assisted by Two (2) Assistant Regional Treasurers: a) An Assistant Regional
Treasurer for administration who shall assist the Regional Treasurer in the day-
to-day management of the office, more specifically those relating to
administrative matters: personnel, legal assistance, information, records
management, supplies and equipment, security and custodial works and such
other works, b) An Assistant Regional Treasurer for operations who shall assist
the Regional Treasurer in terms of operations such as but not limited to
collection system, research and planning on the creation of sources of
revenues and other forms of income, and such other functions as may be
assigned by the Regional Treasurer.

The divisions under the Administration Service shall be the following: 1)
Administrative; 2) Budget; 3) Accounting; and A) Cash. The divisions placed
under the Operations Service are: 1) Revenue/Non-Tax Revenue; 2) Regional
Accounting; 3) Asset Management, A) Fiscal; 5) Local Operations; and 6)
Planning.

Consistent with its mandate to receive, safeguard and raise revenues for
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, the Regional
Treasurer shall design the organizational structure of the Office and its detailed
staffing requirement in accordance with the civil service rules and regulations.

SECTION 6. Qualification and Appointment. - No person shall be
appointed by the Chief Minister to the position of Regional Treasurer unless
he/she has the following qualifications:

a) A Bangsamoro, resident and registered voter of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region;
b) At least 35 years of age upon appointment;

c) A Certified Public Accountant, or a member of the Philippine BAR or
Masters in Business, Finance, Economics, Accounting, or any related
master degree, with at least second level of eligibility;

d) With at least five years of supervisory experience in the treasury
operations or related service;

e) Of proven competence, honesty and integrity; and

f) Qualifications (c) and (d) shall take effect in the appointment of the
Regional Treasurer after the Bangsamoro Transition period. Said
exemption however, does not extend to the Assistant Regional
Treasurers.

These qualifications shall also apply to the Assistant Regional Treasurers.

The Regional Treasurer who shall have the rank equivalent to a Cabinet
Minister shall be appointed by the Chief Minister for a term of five (5) years
without prejudice to one reappointment, unless he/she is sooner resigned,
retired, permanently incapacitated or remove from Office. In case of vacancy,
the appointee succeeding him/her shall serve only the unexpired portion of
his/her term.

The Regional Treasurer shall appoint the rank and file employees of the
Office from division chief and below subject to civil service rules and
regulations.

The two (2) Assistant Regional Treasurer who shall belong to the Career
Service Appointment shall be appointed by the Chief Minister upon the
recommendation of the Regional Treasurer.

Subject to the Salary Standardization Law, the Regional Treasurer shall
initially receive an annual compensation equivalent to that of a Cabinet
Minister.

The Two (2) Assistant Regional Treasurers shall receive annual
compensation one grade below the salary of the Regional Treasurer.

SECTION 7. Working with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and
Management. - The Bangsmoro Treasury Office shall coordinate and
collaborate with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM)-
BARMM for finance policy and programs.

SECTION 8. Relationship with Local Treasurers.- The relationship
between the Regional Treasurer and Treasurers of constituent local
government units shall be in accordance with the provisions of applicable
national and Bangsamoro laws, rules and regulations.

The Regional Treasurer may deputize the Provincial, City and Municipal
Treasurers for the exclusive purpose of exercising any of his powers provided
for in Section 4 of this Act.

SECTION 9. Appropriations. - The budget of Fifty-four Million Pesos
(P54,000,000.00) necessary to finance the initial implementation of this Act
shall be charged against the current year’s (2020) appropriations of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Thereafter, the regular budget of the Office
shall be included in the succeeding Bangsamoro Regional Appropriations Act
for its continued operations and functioning.

SECTION 10. Separability. - If for any cause any part of this Act is
declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of RA 11054, the
remainder of this Act or any provision not affected thereby shall remain in
force and effect.

SECTION 11. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days
following the completion of its publication in a newspaper of general
circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
