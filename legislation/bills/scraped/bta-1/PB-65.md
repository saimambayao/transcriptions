the novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19) a global health emergency. On 9 March 2020,

government units (LGUs) to render full assistance and cooperation, and implement
appropriate response and measures to curtail the threat of COVID-19. On 16 March 2020,
Presidential Proclamation No. 929 was issued, declaring a State of Calamity throughout the
country.

of deaths, 628 recovered and with a total of 869 positive cases.” Additionally, per DOH report

AN ACT PROHIBITING DISCRIMINATION AGAINST PERSONS WHO ARE
DECLARED CONFIRMED, SUSPECTED, PROBABLE, AND RECOVERED CASES OF
COVID-19, INCLUDING HEALTH WORKERS, OTHER FRONTLINERS, SERVICE
WROKERS, REPATRIATED OFWs, RETURNING OFs AND LOCALLY STRANDED
INDIVISUALS (LSIs), AND PROVIDING PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS THEREOF

Be it ordained by the Bangsamoro Parliament, in session assembled:

Section 1. SHORT TITLE - This Act shall be known as “BANGSAMORO COVID-19
RELATED ANTI-DISCRIMINATION ACT OF 2020”.

Section 2. DECLARATION OF POLICY - It is hereby declared policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to promote and protect the health and safety, maintain peace and
order, instill health consciousness among the Bangsamoro people.

It shall likewise be the policy of the Bangsamoro government to enact laws to
regulate, promote, protect, and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro people and
other inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Towards this end, it shall combat the prevalent stigmatization, humiliation and
harassment on the basis of one’s medical condition or being a confirmed, suspect or probable
case, and or exposed to areas with history of COVID-19 contamination. It also aims to bring
normalcy and order in the Bangsamoro region amidst the COVID-19 pandemic and protect

Section 3. DEFINITION OF TERMS - per Department of Health Administrative Order
No. 2020-0013 dated 09 April 202015 which amends and defines the transition from
reporting individuals as Patients under Investigation (PUI) and Persons Under Monitoring
to Suspect, Probable and Confirmed COVID-19 cases. As such, the terms “Persons Infected,
PUI and PUMs” under the definition of terms shall be replaced with Suspect, Probable and
Confirmed case, and these terms shall be used in this Act and define as follows:

a.)

“Discrimination” refers to any act or omission which unduly curtails or impairs
aright, or excludes and withholds access to services, goods, benefits, or privileges,
where the right or access to which is not otherwise withheld, prohibited, or
restricted by relevant protocols, guidelines, and rules issued by the State through
the Department of Health, the Inter-Agency Task Force for Emerging Infectious
Diseases (IATF-EID), the Ministry of Health, the Bangsamoro Inter-Agency Task
Force (BIATF) and such other authorized agencies of the government relative to
measures against the COVID-19 pandemic. It also includes any act that causes or
tends to cause any stigma, disgrace, shame, humiliation, harassment, or
discrimination against persons who are confirmed, suspect, probable cases of
COVID-19, or who have recovered from such including their families, household
members, health workers, other frontliners.

b.) Confirmed Case- any individual, irrespective of presence or absence of clinical

“Suspect Case - is a person who show Influenza-like Illness (ILI) such as fever,

“Probable Case - A person whose testing for COVID-19 is inconclusive or those
tested positive for COVID-19 but whose test was not done in the national or sub-
national reference laboratory or officially accredited laboratory for COVID-19
confirmatory testing.

“Close contact” refers to a person who may have come into contact with the
probable or confirmed case two days prior to the onset of illness of the confirmed
COVID-19 case until the time the said cases test negative on laboratory
confirmation or other approved laboratory test through: (a) face-to-face contact
with probable or confirmed case within 1 meter and for more than 15 minutes; or
(b) direct physical contact with probable or confirmed case; or (c) direct care for
a patient with probable or confirmed COVID-19 without using proper personal
protective equipment; or (d) other situations as indicated by local risk
assessments.

“Health workers” refer to those who deliver care and services to the sick and

Section 4. PROHIBITED ACTS - It shall be unlawful for any person, whether natural
or juridical, to commit any act or make utterances which causes or tend to causes stigma,
disgrace, humiliation, embarrassment, threat, harassment or otherwise discriminate a
person declared confirmed, suspected, probable, or recovered from COVID-19, including
health workers, frontliners, service workers, ROFWs, ROFs or even LSIs as defined under this
Act.

Any public officer or employee who refuses or fails, without justifiable reason, to give
assistance to a person declared confirmed, suspected, probable, or has recovered from
COVID-19, health workers, frontliners, ROFW, ROFs or even LSIs who intends to return to
his place of residence or domicile, after obtaining clearance of the COVID-19 Infection from
the proper health officials, shall be equally liable under Section 5 of this Act.

Any person who unreasonably refused to honor valid and existing contracts on the

Any person who shall publicly claim, post on social media, spread or announce that a

Section 5. PENALTIES - any person who commits the prohibited acts described in
Section 4 of this Act shall, upon conviction, be penalized by a fine of be fine of Five Thousand
Pesos (Php 5,000.00) or imprisonment not exceeding six (6) months or both, at the
discretion of the court.

In case where the offender is a corporation, association, partnership or any other
juridical person, the penalty shall be imposed upon the president, directors, managers,
managing partners , as the case may be, who participated in the commission of the offense
or who shall have knowingly permitted or failed to prevent the commission of the same.

If the offender is a public officer, the maximum penalty prescribed herein shall be
imposed without prejudice to the administrative sanctions that may be charged.

Nothing herein in this shall preclude the victim from instituting a separate and
independent action for damages and other affirmative reliefs.

Section 6. ROLE OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNITS - The constituent local
government units of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao are
encouraged to enact and enforce their own anti-discrimination ordinances or executive
orders that will prohibit and penalize acts of discrimination against persons who are
confirmed, suspect, probable cases and recovered from COVID-19, health workers,
frontliners, service workers, ROFWs, ROFs or even LSIs within their respective jurisdiction.

Section 7. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE - If, for any reason, any part or provision of this
Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, other parts or provisions hereof, which are not
affected thereby, shall remain in full force and effect.

Section 8. REPEALING CLAUSE - All regional acts, rules or regulations in conflict
with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, provided, that the rights already vested
under those acts, rules or regulations shall not be impaired.

Section 9. EFFECTIVITY CLAUSE - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days

after its publication in at least two (2) newspapers of general circulation.
