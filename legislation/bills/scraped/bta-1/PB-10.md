A. Explanatory Statement:

WHEREAS, Sec. 15, Article IX Basic Rights of the Bangsamoro Organic Law
provides:

The Bangsamoro Government shall ensure that Settlers enjoy the rights
guaranteed in this Organic Law. For this purpose, the Parliament shall create an Office
for Settler Communities to promote the welfare and address issues and concerns of
settlers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

WHEREAS, the proposed bill implements for the purpose the provision of Sec 15,
Article IX of the Bangsamoro Organic Law for the creation of Office for Settler
Communities as a fundamental and Basic Right afforded to them by law

F. Body of the Bill:

ARTICLE I. NAME AND PURPOSE

Section 1.Short Title. —This act shall be known as Creation of an Office for Settler
Communities Act.

Section 2.Name. —The government agency to be created by this act shall be known as
the Office for Settler Communities.

Section 3.Purpose. — The purpose of this Act is to ensure that Settler Communities have
full enjoyment of their rights and privileges guaranteed to them by the Bangsamoro
Organic Law, the Constitution and all other laws as may be enacted by the Bangsamoro
Parliament. Ensuring further fair and impartial access to employment, and due respect be
afforded to them on account of their religious and cultural beliefs, practices and customary
traditions.

ARTICLE Il. SETTLERS IDENTITIY

Section 1.Settler Communities. — Those who reside under the territorial jurisdiction of
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao and who are excluded from
the definition of Article Il Section 1 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law nor considered to be
a member of Indigenous Cultural Communities / Indigenous People as defined under the
provisions of Republic Act No. 8371 or Indigenous People’s Rights Act.

Section 2.Office for Settler Communities Symbol. —The Office shall within ninety (90)
days upon its approval pass through a resolution for the creation of its Official seal, logo
and emblem.

ARTICLE Ill. GENERAL PRINCIPLES AND POLICIES
Section 1. Freedom in the Exercise of Religion. —The Office for Settler Communities
shall uphold and safeguard the right of every person especially the Settlers in the free

exercise of their religious beliefs, practices and customary rights and traditions.

Section 2. Cultural and Religious Sensitivity.— The Office for Settler Communities shall
promote Cultural and Religious Sensitivity and shall ensure that Settlers be afforded due

ARTICLE IV. POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

Section 1. Nature of Office. -The Office for Settler Communities is an integral component
of the Bangsamoro Government. It shall be directly under the Office of the Chief Minister.

Section 2. Powers of the Office. -The Office for Settler Communities enjoys powers and
authority on the following matters as provided for under the Bangsamoro Organic Law
and sanctioned by existing laws and regulations:

a.) Social Services/Assistance to Settlers

b.) Administration of its internal operations and internal policy making

c.) Allocation of funds for livelihood, skills development trainings, creation of
cooperatives and other businesses for the Settlers

d.) Coordination to other line agencies for efficient execution of its mandate

e.) Creation of special projects in providing basic services to Settler Communities

f.) Provision of legal assistance to all Settlers in the region

g.) Referrals to proper government agencies for Settlers with other concerns

i.) Enter into contracts, agreements or arrangement with government or

private agencies or entities as may be necessary to attain the objectives of

this Act.

j.) Serve as the primary government agency through which Settlers can seek

government assistance and as the medium through which such assistance

may be extended.

k.) Review and assess the conditions of the Settlers including existing laws and

policies pertinent thereto and to propose relevant laws and policies to address

their role in regional development.

ARTICLE VI - INTER-AGENCY COUNCIL

SECTION 1. INTER-AGENCY MECHANISM. There is hereby created an Inter-
Agency Council to coordinate and resolve issues between Settlers, Moros and |.P’s
through regular consultation and continuing negotiation in a non-adversarial manner
oreserving further the peace and harmonious relationship between and among them.

SECTION 2. MEMBERSHIP. — The members of the Inter-Agency Council are
the following: the Office for Settler Communities, Ulama, Ministry of Indigenous Peoples,
Office for the Youth and Office for Women. The representatives of the Parliament on the
reserve seats for I.P’s, Settler Communities, Youth, Women and Ulama shall likewise be
members of the Inter-Agency Council

SECTION 3. PURPOSE. = The primary purpose of this Council is to resolve
intercultural and interfaith conflicts and differences in peaceful consultations and
negotiations and to create resolutions in the preservation of peace and harmonious
relations between and among the Settlers, Moro’s and the |.P’s.

SECTION 4. POWERS AND FUNCTIONS. — The Inter-Agency Board enjoys the
following powers without prejudice to the supervision of the Chief Minister to viz:

1. To make policy, rules and regulations to be followed in handling and

conflicts arising from cultural and religious differences through the
advancement of various conflict resolution mechanisms.

ARTICLE Vil - BUDGETARY REQUIREMENTS
SECTION 1. CAPITAL OUTLAY FOR OFFICE

The Office of Settler Communities in Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao shall secure a leased building on which necessary changes on the structure
shall be allowed to define a more suitable and convenient place for work. And upon
concurrence of the majority vote of all the members of the Parliament or the Ministers the
office may purchase land to construct permanent building for Settler Communities. Please
see “ANNEX C” for a proposed budget for minor renovation and land purchase and
building construction.

SECTION 2. CAPITAL OUTLAY FOR VEHICLES AND OFFICE EQUIPMENT.
The main thrusts of the Bangsamoro Government is the provision and delivery of basic
services to the Settler Communities in pursuit of its mandate under the Bangsamoro
Organic Law. In consonance with these, the need to purchase office vehicles and office
equipment is imperative in order to provide an effective and efficient delivery of services,
for purposes of mobility to cater the needs of the Settlers who are geographically distant,
provide a smooth flow of work, a well-organized, effective, accurate and presentable
working system in the Office. Please see “ANNEX D” for the proposed budget for
Vehicles and Office Equipment.

ARTICLE Vill — FINAL PROVISIONS

SECTION 1. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE. Any provision or portion of this
Act found to be in violation of the Constitution or declared to be invalid by a competent
court, shall not impair other provisions or part thereof and which shall continue to be in
full force and effect.

SECTION 2. REPEALING CLAUSE. Laws, rules and regulations or parts
thereof, which are inconsistent or in conflict with the provisions of this Act, are hereby
repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 3. EFFECTIVITY. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days
following its publication in a newspaper of regional publication.
