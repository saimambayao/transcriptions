AN ACT CREATING AN OFFICE FOR TRADITIONAL OR
TRIBAL JUSTICE SYSTEM OF THE NON-MORO INDIGENOUS
PEOPLES, PROVIDING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREOF

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

ARTITLE I. PRIMARY PROVISIONS

Section i. Title. - This Act shall be known as an OFFICE FOR
TRADITIONAL OR TRIBAL JUSTICE SYSTEM OF THE NON-MORO
INDIGENOUS PEOPLES

Section 2. Purpose — To create an office that shall recognize, protect,
preserve, promote and support the traditional justice systems of the Non-Moro
Indigenous Peoples in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM).

Section 3. Statutory Basis — Section 17, Article X of RA 11054 otherwise
known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides “Traditional or Tribal
Systems. — The Parliament shall enact laws to promote and support the
traditional justice systems that are appropriate for the indigenous peoples.

The indigenous peoples shall have the right to use their own commonly accepted
justice system, conflict resolution institutions, peace building mechanisms, and
other customary laws and practices within their respective communities and as
may be compatible with the national legal system and within the internationally
recognized human rights. The traditional justice systems are the mechanisms
to determine, settle, and decide controversies and enforce decisions involving

disputes between members of the indigenous peoples concerned in accordance
with the customary laws of these communities.”

Further, Section 18, Article X of the same law which provides for the “Office
for Traditional or Tribal Justice Systems. There is hereby created an Office for
the Tribal Justice System that shall responsible for overseeing the study,
preservation, and development of the tribal justice system within Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region. The powers and functions of the Office for Tribal Justice
System shall be defined by the Parliament.

The Office for Tribal Justice System shall ensure the full participation of
indigenous peoples in the formulation, implementation, and evaluation of
policies related to the strengthening tribal justice system. Provided, that such
systems shall maintain their indigenous character in accordance with the
respective practices of each tribe”.

Section 4. Definition of Terms:

a. Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples — refers to a group of Non-Moro people
or homogenous societies identified by self-ascription by others, who have
continuously lived as organized community on communally bounded and
defined territory, and who have, under claims of ownership since time
immemorial, occupied, possessed, and utilized such territories; sharing
common bonds of language, customs, traditions, and other distinctive
cultural traits, and who have, through resistance to political, social and
cultural inroads of colonization, non-indigenous religions and cultures,
become historically differentiated from the majority of Filipinos. Non-Moro
Indigenous Peoples shall likewise include peoples who are regarded as
indigenous on account of their decent from the populations which inhabited
the country, at the time of the conquest or colonization, or at the time of
inroads non-indigenous religions and cultures, or the establishment of
present state boundaries, who retain some or all of their own social,
economic, cultural and political institutions, but who may have been
displaced from their own their tradition domains or who may have resettled
outside their ancestral domains. It shall include, but not limited to, the
Teduray, Lambangian, Dulangan Manobo, B’laan, Erumanen ne Menuvu,
Bandjao, Sama, Yakan, Blaan and Higaonon within Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;

b. Traditional Justice Systems — are the mechanisms to determine, settle,
and decide controversies and enforce decisions involving disputes between
members of the indigenous peoples concerned in accordance with the
customary laws of these communities.

c. Office for Traditional or Tribal Justice Systems — refers an office that
is primarily responsible for overseeing the study, preservation, and
development of the tribal justice system within Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region.

d. Indigenous Political Structure — refers to organizational and cultural
leadership systems, institution, relationships, patterns and processes for
decision-making and participation, identified and accepted by the non-Moro
Indigenous Peoples.

e. Full participation of Indigenous Peoples — refers to the process
wherein the Office for Tribal Justice System shall ensure that all Non-Moro
Indigenous Peoples within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao shall be informed to the importance of their active participation
in the formulation, implementation, and evaluation of policies related to the
strengthening tribal justice system.

Section 5. Declaration of Policy. It is the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to:

a. Recognize and promote the rights of the Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples
within the framework of the constitution and national laws (Section 9, Art.
IV of RA 11054); and

b. Recognize the rights of the Indigenous Peoples and shall adopt measures
for the promotion and protection of the right to justice systems and
indigenous political structure (Section 3(c) of Art. IX RA 11054).

Section 6. Coverage - This Act shall have force and effect amongthe Non-
Moro Indigenous Peoples (NMIPs) within the territories of Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

ARTICLE II. OFFICE FOR TRIBAL JUSTICE SYSTEM

Section 7. Powers and Functions. The powers and functions of the Office
for Tribal Justice System are as follows:

a. Oversee the study, preservation, and development of the tribal justice
system within Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

b. Formulate, implement, and evaluate policies related to the strengthening
tribal justice system;


============================================================

============================================================
c. Ensure the full participation of indigenous peoples in the formulation,
implementation, and evaluation of policies related to the strengthening
of their tribal justice systems;

d. Document the tribal justice systems of Non-Moro IPs within Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region;

e. Document the indigenous political structures of non-Moro IPs within
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

f. Repository of all documents related to the Trial Justice System of the
Indigenous Peoples.

Section 8. Office Organizational Set Up - The Office shall be under the
direct supervision and control of the Office of the Executive Director. The rank
of the head of office shall be Executive Director II.

There shall be two (2) Divisions and Sections under these, to wit: Technical
Management Services Division (TMSD) and Administrative and Finance
Division (AFD). The Technical Management Services Division shall perform the
technical powers and functions of the office as herein provided. It shall be
headed by a Development Management Officer V.

Sections under the TMSD are: (a) Policy and Planning Section, which shall be
headed by a Planning Officer III (PO III), (b) Research and Documentation
Section, which shall be headed by a Development Management Officer III
(DMO III), (c) Legal Section, which shall be headed by a Legal Officer IV (LO
IV), and (d) Human Resource Development Section, which shall be headed by
a Development Management Officer III (DMO III).

The Administrative and Finance Division (AFD) that shall perform general
administrative supervision of personnel, efficient utilization of funds, supplies
and equipment, security of office premises and general services function. It
shall be headed by an Administrative Officer V (AO V).

The Sections under the Administrative and Finance Division (AFD) are as
follows, to wit: (a) Human Resource Management Section, which shall be
headed by a Human Resource Management Officer III (HRMO III), (b)
Accounting Section, which shall be headed by an Accountant III, (c) Cash
Section, which shall be headed by a Cashier III, (d) Bookkeeping Section, which
shall be headed by a Bookkeeper III, (e) Supply Section, which shall be headed
by a Supply Officer III and General Services Section, which shall be headed by
an Administrative Officer III.

Section 9. Appointment — the Chief Minister shall select and appoint all
employees of the Office for Tribal Justice System from a short list submitted by
each Indigenous Political Structure of every tribe.

Section 10. Qualification — the employees of the Office for Tribal Justice
System must be a member of non-Moro Indigenous People within BARMM.

Section 11. Funds — The funds amounting to Fifty Million Pesos
(P50,000,00) per annum shall be taken from the Contingency Fund of the
BTA.

ARTICLE If. FINAL PROVISIONS

Section 12. Construction and Interpretation. This act shall be
interpreted in favor of the non-Moro Indigenous Peoples.

Section 13. Separability Clause. - Should any provision of this Act is
declared unconstitutional by a competent court, other provisions shall not be
affected thereby shall remain in full force and effort.

Section 14. Effectivity Clause. - This Act shall take effect within fifteen
(15) days following the completion of its publication in at least two (2)
newspapers of regional circulation.
