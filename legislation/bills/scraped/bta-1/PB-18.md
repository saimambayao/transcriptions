“An Act creating a Bureau to institutionalize Halal Industry Development and Accreditation
Authority in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and for other purposes.”

EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro people by the Bangsamoro Organic Law states that the Parliament shall enact laws
to further strengthen its policy and programs on Halal’. .

Whereas, the Halal Industry food and non-food is expected to be a trillion-dollar industry by 2030.
More importantly it is the religious obligation of each and every followers of Islam to consume Halal

products;

whereas, Halal food and products is an expanding industry for Muslims and Non-Muslims alike due
to the assurance of quality and integrity of Halal;

Whereas, under Republic Act 10817 known as the “Philippine Halal Export Development and
Promotion Act of 2016” it was declared a policy of the State to promote the growth and ensure the
integrity and quality of Philippine Halal exports”, to this end the development and promotion of the
Halal industry was sought as a mode of achieving equity and justice for farmers and producers and
increasing employment opportunities.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this bill is urgently sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Title. This act shall be known as the “An Act establishing the Halal Industry Development
and Accreditation Authority in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. \t shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Government
to strengthen the Halal industry within the Bangsamoro Territory including its surrounding areas.
The Halal Industry includes Food and Non-Food products, services and the Islamic Banking
Institutions. The Halal Industry shall be recognized as a means to bring employment opportunities
and increase business trade as a primarily Muslim enterprise.

Finally, it shall be the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Government to uphold the
integrity of Halal products, and its logos in order to ensure quality assurance in the domestic and
export market. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Government shall protect consumers and users of
Halal products and services.

Section 3. Definition of Terms — As used in this Act, the following terms shall have the following
definition:

Accreditation — Refers to the procedure by which the Bureau on Halal Industry
Development and Accreditation Authority in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to formally attest
to the competence of a certification body to provide inspection and certification services;

mt Certification — Refers to an attestation by a third party Certification Body to conformance of
standards and guidelines related to products, processes, systems or persons;

Halal — refers to lawful or permissible under Sharia’h (Islamic Law).

Section 4. The Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Program -There shall be created a
masterplan for the development of the Halal Industry within the Bangsamoro territory, in cooperation
with the National Agencies and partner countries. The Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development
Program shall be created in order to implement the plan with specific focus on increasing the
consumers trust and integrity of products and services bearing the Halal Logo.

It shall include all related areas in the Halal ecosystem such as Food and non-Food Products, Islamic
financing, Tourism services, and transportation, logistics and other services.

Section 5. The Creation of the Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development and Accreditation Board

— In order to ensure cooperation between and among the Ministries for the proper implementation of

the “Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Program”, there is hereby created the Bangsamoro

Halal Industry Development and Accreditation Board (“Bangsamoro Halal Industry Board” for
‘evity).

Section 8. Creation of the Bureau on Halal Industry Development and Accreditation Authority in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region — In order to ensure the proper implementation of the
Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Program, there is hereby created the Bureau on Halal
Industry Development and Accreditation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Section 9. Composition of the Bureau- The Bureau on Halal Industry Development and
Accreditation Authority shall be headed by the Executive Director to be appointed by the Chief
Minister to serve for a period of thee (3) years. The Bureau shall be divided into two divisions each
headed by a Deputy Director; one (1) for Religious and Accreditation affairs which shall include the
functions for accreditation, standard setting, benchmarking, and registration, and one (1) for
Technical and Administrative affairs which shall include marketing, planning, budget and research.

The Bureau on Halal Industry Development and Accreditation Authority shall ensure that experts
from the field of Medical Science, Food Technology, Chemistry, Veterinary Medicine and other
relevant expertise shall be well represented.

Section 10. Functions of the Bureau—tThe following are the functions of the Bureau on Halal
Industry Development and Accreditation Authority:

1. Grant or deny accreditation of Halal Certification Bodies operating within the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region, and suspend or withdraw such accreditation in accordance with
established policies and guidelines as approved by the Bangsamoro Halal Board. In case of
an unfavorable decision, the party seeking Halal Accreditation may appeal to the
Bangsamoro Halal Board;

2. Create a system of recognition for Halal Certification Bodies granted authority from the
National Government;

3. Act as an interagency Secretariat for the Bangsamoro Halal Industry Board. The ministries
under the Bangsamoro Halal Board may assign personnel to the Bureau for this purpose;

4. Formulate accreditation policies and guidelines to govern the process of accreditation for
Halal Certifying bodies;

5. Facilitate conduct of various Halal Capacity Building and training in partnership with the
relevant ministries;

6. Investigate violations and receive complaints regarding the integrity of Halal products, and its
logos and violations under this Act;

Section 11. Local Standards for Halal. — The development of Local Standards for Halal shall be the
mandate of the following agencies:

1. Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reforms — for standards on Halal quality of
feeds and fertilizers including the examination of primary and post-harvest foods;

2. Ministry of Health—for standards on processed and prepackaged foods, drugs and cosmetics;

3. Ministry of Trade, Investment and Tourism — for standards on tourism, services including
logistics, and other non-food products;

4. Other Ministries as allowed by the Bangsamoro Halal Industry Board;

The local Halal standards shall be created in consultation with the private stakeholders within the
Bangsamoro Territory.

_Section 12. Mandatory use of Halal Logo.—No establishments shall be permitted to use the words
Jalal in any place of their establishment, and the Halal Logo unless they are certified by a
Certification Body accredited by the Bureau on Halal Industry Development and Accreditation
Authority. A business establishment using the words Halal or any logo, or misrepresent that their
products and services are Halal without accreditation shall be subject to the following penalties:

First Offense — A fine of P 5,000.00
Second Offense — A fine of P 30,000.00
Third Offense - A fine of P 50,000.00 and Business Closure

coordination with the relevant ministry shall establish and implement a capacity building
development program for the Halal Industry which shall include the following:

Capacity building for Halal Certifying bodies;

Skills training for Halal food and non-food producers including farmers and fisherfolk;
Scholarship programs for exchange of technology with partner countries.

Section 15. Islamic Banking.—lslamic banking shall be recognized as an invaluable part of the
Halal ecosystem. To this end, the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management shall have its own
office geared towards promotion of Islamic Finance in close coordination with the Shariah

Section 16. Halal Ecozone Territories—The Bangsamoro Halal Board together with the Halal
Export Board, Philippine Economic Zone Authority or the Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority
once constituted, Mindanao Development Authority and the Southern Philippines Development
Authority shall work together to create Halal Ecozone Territories which shall specialize in creating a
functional Halal ecosystem within a local unit and provide for benefits for those establishing Halal
related businesses within the zone. The territory shall be equipped for Halal export for partner
countries. The areas of Tawi-Tawi and Polloc Parang shall be given special consideration for the
development of the Economic Zone.

Section 17. Private participation—The Bangsamoro Government shall recognize the invaluable
participation of the private sector for the establishment and promotion of the Halal Industry within
the Bangsamoro territory. Thus, the Executive Director of Bureau on Halal Industry Development
and Accreditation Authority shall encourage the participation of the private stakeholders in the
creation of the Bangsamoro Halal Industry Development Program.

Section 18. Coordination with National Agencies——The Executive Director of Bureau on Halal
Industry Development and Accreditation Authority thru the Office of Chief Minister shall ensure
cooperation with the Halal Export Board and its member agencies. The Executive Director shall
ensure that the Bangsamoro Halal Board is well represented in matters of foreign trade. The
Bangsamoro Halal Board shall create a system of recognition for Certifying bodies granted
accreditation by the Philippine Accreditation Board (PAB) of the Department of Trade and Industry
(DTI).

Section 19. Enforcement Policy—The Halal Export Board may seek the aid of the Local
Government Agencies, Philippine National Police and other enforcement agencies to enforce laws
against slaughtering in unregistered establishments branded as Halal.

‘ection 20. Halal Facilities —Funds shall be allocated for the creation of Halal Facilities such as the
aecessary common service facilities such as but not limited to slaughterhouses, landing facilities,
warehouses, and laboratories and testing facilities.
