EXPLANATORY NOTE

Section 12, Article VI of the Bangsamoro Organic Law provides that “The National
Government shall ensure the protection of the rights of the Bangsamoro people residing in
communities outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and undertake programs for their
rehabilitation and development. The Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with the local
government units where these communities are located and the appropriate national
government agencies, shall provide assistance to enhance their economic, social, and cultural
development”

In pursuance of this mandate, there is a need to create an office that shall be the primary
agency of the Bangsamoro government which will ensure the welfare and development of the
Bangsamoro communities situated outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM). The proposed bill will create institutional arrangements and modalities
between the Bangsamoro government and the Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM.

The Bangsamoro parliament must recognize its fundamental role in the process of
bringing just and lasting peace not only in Mindanao but throughout the Philippines by enacting
the proposed bill in order to be of service to Bangsamoro people living outside the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in implementation of the mandates of BOL.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

“AN ACT CREATING THE OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO COMMUNITIES
OUTSIDE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION DEFINING ITS POWERS,
FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR
AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES.”

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority assembled:

Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Communities Outside
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) Act of 2019”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — As stated in Section 12, Article VI of Republic Act No.
11054, otherwise known as Bangsamoro Organic Law that it shall be the policy of the
‘Bangsamoro Government to provide assistance to communities outside BARMM and enhance
their economic, social and cultural development in coordination with the local government units
where these communities are located and the national government agencies providing services to
these communities.

Section 3. Creation of the Office of the Bangsamoro Communities Outside the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region. — There shall be created an Office of the Bangsamoro Communities
Outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, hereinafter referred to as the BCOBAR Office,
which shall be organized structurally and functionally, in accordance with the provisions of this
Act.

Section 4. Mandate. - The BCOBAR Office shall be the primary administrative entity of the
Bangsamoro government in giving assistance to Bangsamoro people residing outside the
Bangsamoro region that includes the development and implementation of their economic, social,
and cultural well-being. It shall formulate and undertake a systematic program for promoting
their welfare in coordination with local government units as well as appropriate national
government agencies.

Section 5. Powers and Functions. - To accomplish its mandate, the BCOBAR Office shall have
the following powers and functions:

1) Implement policies on Bangsamoro Communities Outside BARMM;

2) Recommend to the Chief Minister and other appropriate Ministries policies for the effective
implementation of its mandate and functions;

3) Monitor and evaluate existing policies and development programs of the Bangsamoro
government that seeks to develop and uplift the social, cultural, and economic conditions of
Bangsamoro people outside the BARMM;

4) Propose relevant and vital legislations to the Bangsamoro Parliament on the social, cultural,
and economic development of Bangsamoro communities outside BARMM;

5) Represent the Bangsamoro Communities Outside BARMM in the Council of Leaders headed.
by the Chief Minister; and

6) To perform such other functions as maybe needed to achieve its goals and objectives to the
fullest and as maybe provided by law.

Section 6. Hierarchy of the BCOBAR Office - The Office shall be administered under the
control and supervision of the Director General who shall be appointed by the Chief Minister.

The Director General shall have the following powers:
1) Provide executive direction and supervision over the entire operations of the BCOBAR office;

2) Sit as member in the Council of Leaders representing the Bangsamoro communities outside
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region pursuant to Section 9, Article VI of the Bangsamoro
Organic Law;

3) Establish policies and standards for the effective, efficient and economical operation of the
BCOBAR office in accordance with the policies and programs of the Bangsamoro government;

4) Coordinate with local government units, national government agencies, public and private
interest groups, including non-government organizations and people’s organizations on
BCOBAR policies and initiatives;

5) Undertake consultations with ministers and officers of the Bangsamoro government relating
to the concerns of the Bangsamoro people residing outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region; and

6) Perform such other tasks as maybe necessary for effective implementation of the mandates of
its office.

There shall also be two (2) Deputy Director Generals who shall be recommended by the
Director General to the Chief Minister for appointment.

The Deputy Director Generals shall assist the Director General in the performance of his
functions and mandates.

One Deputy Director General shall be tasked to formulate and implement policies and pertinent
laws, as well as plan and monitor programs and projects of the BCOBAR Office, while the other
Deputy Director General shall be responsible for administration and finance.

Both Deputy Director Generals shall perform such other functions and duties as maybe assigned
by the Director General.

All other officers and employees of the BCOBAR Office shall be appointed by the Director
General in accordance with the Bangsamoro Civil Service laws, rules and regulations.

Section 7. Structure and Staffing Pattern of the BCOBAR Office - The organizational and
staffing pattern shall be determined by the implementing rules and regulations that shall be
promulgated within 60 days from the effectivity of this Act.

Section 8. Sectoral and Industry Task Forces - The BCOBAR Office may create sectoral and
industry taskforces, technical working group, advisory bodies or committees for the furtherance
of its objectives. Additional private sector representatives, such as from the academe, including
non-government organizations (NGOs) and people’s organizations (POs), as well as
representative of other National Government Agencies (NGAs), Local Government Units
(LGUs) and Government Owned or Controlled Corporations (GOCCs) may be appointed to
these working groups.

Section 9. Appropriations. - The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act
shall be charged against the current appropriations of the BARMM and to carry out the functions
and powers of the BCOBAR Office including the acquisition of its office facilities and
equipment as provided in this Act, an amount of Two Hundred Million pesos (PhP
200,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated. Thereafter, such sums as may be needed for the

_continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations

Act.

Section 10. Record of Transaction. - The BCOBAR Office shall keep proper accounts and
records of all its transactions and affairs, and shall exert all efforts to ensure that all payments —
made out of its moneys are correctly made and properly authorized, and that adequate control is
maintained over the assets of, or in the custody of the BCOBAR Office and the expenditures it
may incur. The BCOBAR Office shall be subject to audit by the Commission on Audit.

Section 11. Periodic Performance Evaluation. — The Director General is hereby required to
formulate and enforce a system of measuring and evaluating periodically and objectively the
performance of the BCOBAR Office and submit the same annually to the Chief Minister and to
the BARMM Parliament.

Section 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The Office of the Bangsamoro
Communities Outside of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region or the BCOBAR Office, Ministry
of Finance, Budget and Management and Ministry of Social Welfare shall issue the
implementing rules and regulations sixty (60) days after the effectivity of this Act.

Section 13. Separability Clause. - If for any reason, any section or provision of this Act is
declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other sections or provisions hereof which are not

affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

Section 14. Effectivity Clause. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication
in two (2) regional newspapers of general circulation.
