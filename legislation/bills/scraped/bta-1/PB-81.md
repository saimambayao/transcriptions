AN ACT PRIORITIZING ESTABLISHMENT OF THE BANGSAMORO
AGRICULTURE AND FISHERIES TRAINING INSTITUTE

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. - This act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Agriculture

and Fisheries Training Institute”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous State to promote agriculture as a means to alleviate poverty and food
sufficiency in the Bangsamoro regions. To this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall give
priority on promotion of science and technology training and innovation for all
Bangsamoro Farmers and Fisherfolks. It shall develop an extensive information campaign
to actively promote and disseminate available modern agriculture and fisheries techniques,

innovations and knowledge.

The trainings conducted shall be those that are appropriate and relevant in

accordance with the heritage and beliefs of the Bangsamoro farmers and fisherfolk.

Section 3. Mandate. - The Bangsamoro Agricultural and Fisheries Training
Institute shall spearhead the planning, monitoring and implementation of the provision of
training and other extension services to Bangsamoro farmers and fisherfolks. The provision
of training shall be publicly funded and shall include the training of State Universities and
Colleges and Local Government Unit to assist them in providing technical assistance, and
research, development of Training Modules and Information, Education and

Communication (IEC) materials.

Section 4. Extension Service. - Extension service refers to the provision of
training, information, and support services by the government and non-government
organizations to the agriculture and fisheries sectors to improve the technical, business and

social capabilities of farmers and fisherfolk.

Extension services shall include post-harvest training, market analysis and

capability building for cooperatives.

Section 5. Bangsamoro Training Institute Office- There is hereby established
three (3) Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute Office in key areas of the

Bangsamoro territory which shall be headed by a Director with Salary Grade 24.

The Bangsamoro Agriculture and Fisheries Training Institute Office shall be an
attached agency of the Ministry on Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform and shall

directly report, and submit reports required by the Ministry.

Section 6. Partnership with the Bangsamoro Cooperative Development
Authority. Farmer’s cooperatives shall be the cornerstone for training development and
dissemination, as such the Bangsamoro Cooperative Development Authority shall be a key
partner in the dissemination of information relating to training and extension services, as

well as assessment of needs of farmers and fisherfolk.

Section 7. Research, innovation, and science and technology. - The BAFTI shall
give priority to research and keep itself updated with modern agriculture and fisheries
trends, including the use of machineries. Training modules shall be created and updated

regularly in partnership with the State Universities and Colleges.

Section 8. Training Needs Assessment and Information, Education and
Communication Materials. - The BAFTI, together with the MAFAR and the LGU
concerned, shall conduct Training Needs Assessment for the Bangsamoro Farmers and
Fisherfolks in order to deliver the correct, culturally appropriate, and relevant training to
the Farmers and Fisherfolks. The BAFTI shall also deliver culturally appropriate IEC in the

language most appropriate to the end-user

Section 9. Halal Development. - The BAFTI shall prioritize the development of
the Halal industry and shall include the relevance and incentives of Halal in all training

modules.

Section 10. Agriculture short courses. - The BAFTI shall cooperate with the
Technical Education and Skills Authority- BARMM for the creation of Agriculture and
Fisheries short courses, which shall include short term training on agriculture and fisheries,

and related marketing and enterprise.

Section u1. Private participation. —The BAFTI shall recognize the invaluable

participation of the private sector for the development of Irrigation services all throughout

Section 12. Additional Staffing. —The MAFAR may hire additional field officers
