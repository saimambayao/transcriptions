EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Organic Law states that the Bangsamoro Government shall
exercise its authority over Water Supply and Services, Flood Control and Irrigation
Services. It is known to all that the Bangsamoro that we have a great reliance in the
industry of Agriculture, and it is to this end that we must exert all efforts to remove all
obstacle to produce greater yield in agriculture. Our lack of irrigation service is one such
obstacle which must be eliminated with great urgency.

Our country possess about 10.3million hectares of agricultural lands, out of which
only 9.3% are considered to be irrigable. While our country is blessed with having one of
the highest rainfall in the world at 2.200 millimeters per year, only 6% of the figure is being
harvested. In comparison, India which harvests and collects 60% of their rainfall despite
not having much. This is due to the lack of our proper investment in our irrigation system.

Irrigation remains to be a key factor in agriculture and rice production and it is time
that we give it due attention. The farmers are relying only during the rainy season and their
produce is limited to only a period of almost six months in one year. Irrigated areas are the
only areas where produce can be made consistently in a year, doubling rice production and
in turn alleviating poverty. Irrigation can be seen as an immediate solution for poverty
alleviation in poverty-stricken areas which are either often flooded for a whole year or
farmers were unable to harvest due to a drough.

There is an urgent need to address this bottleneck in agriculture, and the bottleneck
can only be alleviated thru a convergent approach in irrigation. The assembly and
cooperation of the member agencies in this bill is seen as an immediate and long-term

solution for irrigation in agriculture.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this bill is urgently sought.

MP MOHAMM ACOB, PH.D

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:
Section 1. Short Title. — This act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Irrigation Act”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It shall be the policy of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous State to promote agriculture as a means to alleviate poverty and food
sufficiency in the Bangsamoro regions. To this end, reliable and efficient system of
irrigations shall be established to complement existing agricultural infrastructure and to
increase the yield of our agriculture produce.

An integrated approach is vital to the adequate supply of irrigation in both upstream
and downstream areas, thus the policy of convergence and cooperation between national
and local agencies, is recognized as a vital means of securing adequate irrigation.

Section 3. Scope. - This Act shall apply to all areas of irrigable agricultural land
except those which are reserved as a National Project which shall be under the sole
authority of the National Irrigation Authority. Priority shall be given to Small Scale
Irrigation Projects (SSIP) and farmers with landholding of eight (8) hectare or belows.

Section 4. Free Irrigation. - Under the Republic Act No. 10969 or the “Free
Irrigation Act”, all farmers with landholdings of eight (8) hectares and below are hereby
exempted from paying irrigation service fees (ISF) for water derived from national
irrigation systems (NIS) and communal irrigation systems (CIS) that were, or are to be,
funded, constructed, maintained and administered by the National Irrigation
Administration (NIA) and other government agencies, including those that have been
turned over to irrigators associations (IAs). All unpaid ISF and the corresponding penalties
of farmers with eight (8) hectares and below to NIA, and all loans, past due accounts and
the corresponding interests and penalties of [As to NIA, are hereby condoned and written
off from the books of NIA.

Section 5. Composition of the “Bangsamoro Irrigation Board” - There is hereby
established three (3) Regional Bangsamoro Irrigation Board within the same allocated
jurisdiction of the National Irrigation Offices of Pagadian City, Cagayan De Oro and
Midsayap City. They shall be composed of the following Ministries and their appointed

Ministers:

(a) Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reforms, as Chairman of the
Board;

(b) Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources;
(c) Ministry of Public Works

(d) Ministry of Interior and Local Government

(e) Appropriate regional representative of the National Irrigation Board

(f) Representatives of the Local Government Unit involved

(g) Provincial/Municipal Agriculture Officer involved

The Regional Bangsamoro Irrigation Board shall convene at least once every quarter.

Section 6. Powers and Functions. - The Regional Bangsamoro Irrigation Board
shall have the following functions:

(a) The Board shall identify and assess areas which are in need of irrigation service,
and to develop, operate and maintain the irrigation system to be established. A
Bangsamoro Agriculture-Irrigation Development Plan shall be established from
the data gathered and requested from the farmers, irrigation association and the
Local Government Units, in coordination with all member agencies;

(b) Ensure the implementation of Republic Act No. 10969, or the “Free Irrigation
Service Act”. To this end, the Regional Bangsamoro Irrigation Board shall keep a
registry of all entitled beneficiaries farmers and identify challenges in the
implementation of this law;

(c) Coordinate with the projects relating to irrigation between all member agencies,
including but not limited to: National Irrigation System led by the NIA, Small
Scale Irrigation Projects by the Department of Agriculture and the Watershed
Management by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources,
projects of Irrigation Associations and other Community Irrigation System;

(d) Identify existing irrigation system resources in need of further funding, repair
and maintenance

(e) Establish a collaboration with Irrigation Associations and provide Training
Needs Assessment (TNA) in order to ensure the full utility of irrigation systems;

Section 7. Local Government Participation. — The Local Government Units
shall, together with the National Irrigation Office and the Municipal Agriculture Officer,
submit periodic reports on the status of irrigation or lack of irrigation of agricultural lands
within their area.

Section 8. Coordination and Convergence.—The Ministry of Agriculture and
Agrarian Reforms as chair of the Regional Bangsamoro Irrigation Board shall ensure
cooperation and convergence between all government agencies and projects affecting the
supply of water and irrigation in agricultural lands. To this end, the ministry shall be
consulted in all projects within the Bangsamoro Area affecting irrigation and water
supplies.

Section 9. Irrigation map. — There shall be created an Irrigation Map detailing
the total areas equipped for irrigation and the manner of irrigation. Data shall be collated
among all member agencies and shall be used to establish the long term plans of irrigation
within the Bangsamoro Areas.

Section 9. Research and Modernization. — The Ministries involved shall
intensify and recognize research and technologies to support farm irrigation and water
supply, including the development, supply and operation of Solar Irrigation.

Section 10. Private participation. —The members of the Regional Irrigation Board
shall recognize the invaluable participation of the private sector for the development of
Irrigation services all throughout the Bangsamoro area. To this end, public-private
corporations and partnerships shall be actively sought and encouraged.

Section 11. Additional Staffing. —The Ministry of Agriculture may hire additional
field officers to implement this Act. The Minister may authorize secondment of his staff to
the Regional Irrigation Offices for efficient communication.

Section 12. Appropriations. —The funds necessary to carry out the initial
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the respective budget of concerned
agencies for the current year. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued
implementation of this Act shall be included in the General Appropriation.

Section 13. Separability Clause -- Should any provision of this Act be subsequently
declared unconstitutional, the other provisions not so declared shall remain in full force
and effect.

Section 14. Repealing Clause —A\I laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations, or
any part thereof, which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended

accordingly.

Section 15. Effectivity.—This Act shall take effect immediately.

So Approved.
