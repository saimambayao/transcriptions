“AN ACT CREATING THE BANGSAMORO PILGRIMAGE AUTHORITY IN THE
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO DEFINING ITS
POWERS, FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR
AND FOR OTHER RELEVANT PURPOSES.”

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority assembled:

Section 1. Title.—This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao Act of 2019”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy.—As stated in Section 42, Article VII of Republic Act
11054, otherwise known as Bangsamoro Organic Law that “The Parliament may create
other offices such as for the youth, women, settler communities, disaster risk reduction
and management, and planning and development, including a Bangsamoro Pilgrimage
Authority which shall exercise jurisdiction over Haj and Umrah matters affecting pilgrims
from within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Pilgrimage Authority.—There is hereby created,
under the Office of the Chief Minister, an agency to be called as Bangsamoro Pilgrimage
Authority, hereinafter referred to as Authority, which shall be organized, staffed,
operated, and maintained, structurally and functionally, in accordance with the provisions
of this Act and the general principles led down in Republic Act 11054, otherwise known
as “Bangsamoro Organic Law.”

Section 4. Composition of the Authority.—The Authority herein created shall be
composed of a Chairman whose appointment, tenure and qualifications shall be
determined by the Chief Minister, and four (4) members consisting of the representatives
of the Ministers of Finance, Social Work and Development, Interior .and., Local

Section 5. Powers, Functions and Duties.—The Authority shall have the following
powers, functions and duties:

(a) To initiate, plan, integrate, execute, implement, coordinate, manage, supervise,
and administer all aspects and phases of programs, projects and activities, relevant to the
annual Muslim pilgrimage to Mecca, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, of pilgrims coming from
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, including, but not limited to the following:

1. Devising ways, means, methods and measures to encourage all Muslims from
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to save money to enable them to undertake the
pilgrimage;

2. Pre-departure processing of pilgrim applicants at convenient designated places;

3. Ferry of pilgrim participants from home provinces to designated point of
departure;

4. Arrangement for transport from the Philippines to Mecca, Kingdom of Saudi
Arabia and return, including charter or contract, whether by ship or airplane;

5. Administration and assistance to the pilgrims abroad or airplane to and fro and
while at Mecca, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia;

6. Deprocessing of pilgrims upon return to the Philippines at designated point of
arrival;

7. Ferry of pilgrims from point of arrival to home provinces; and

8. General administration, to include funding of the programs, projects and
activities necessary for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Government participation in the
pilgrimage;

(b) To coordinate with and obtain assistance, support and cooperation from other
offices, agencies, branches, or instrumentalities of National Government, as well as from
the private sectors whenever necessary to ensure the success of every pilgrimage;

(c) To formulate and submit programs, plans and recommendations for carrying
out the pilgrimage, including funding requirements to the Chief Minister, not later than
January 15 of each year; and to submit to the Chief Minister reports on the progress of
the pilgrimage programs, plans, projects, and activities, including a final report on the
completion of the pilgrimage;

(e) To appoint and administer, subject to pertinent budgetary, accounting and
auditing and civil service rules and regulations, such numbers of subordinate officials and
employees necessary for the efficient and effective functioning of the Authority;

(f) To accept and receive grants, donations or gifts in whatever form and from
whatever sources, Provided, That, said grants, donations or gifts shall be administered,
obligated and disbursed in accordance with the terms thereof, or in the absence of such
terms, in such a manner at the Authority may, in the exercise of sound discretion,
determine, Provided, further, That, said grants, donations or gifts shall be subject only to
such limitations as the grantor, donor or giver may impose; Provided, Finally, That the

Section 5. Executive Director.—The Authority shall be administered, operated and
maintained by the Executive Director, to be appointed by the Chief Minister, upon
recommendation of the Chairman of the Authority, as the chief executive officer of the
Authority.

Section 6. Hajj Attaché.--The Chief Minister shall appoint a Hajj Attache. The Hajj
Attache shall possess related academic degree and must be able to read, write and speak
fluently the Arabic language. The Hajj Attache shall coordinate with his counterpart in the
National Commission on Muslim Filipinos and the Ministry of Hajj of the Kingdom of Saudi
Arabia on all matters pertaining to the conduct of the annual Hajj. The Hajj Attache shall
enjoy the same rank, salary, and privileges comparable to those Attaches of the national
government.

Section 7. Amirul Hajj. The Chief Minister shall appoint the Executive Director of
Bureau Pilgrimage Authority as the Amirul Hajj and head of Bangsamoro pilgrimage
delegation to Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and shall serve as the representative of the Chief
Minister in the annual haijj.

Section 8. Salaries, benefits, bonuses, and remunerations.—The salaries, benefits
and remunerations of the Chairman, executive director and other subordinate officials
and employees of the Authority shall be determined by the Authority, subject to the

Section 9. Coordination with NCMF.--The Authority shall actively cooperate and

Section 11. Repealing Clause.—All regional laws, rules and regulations or parts
thereof which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

Section 12. Effectivity.—This Act shall take effect upon approval and publication in
a newspaper of general circulation in accordance with existing laws.
