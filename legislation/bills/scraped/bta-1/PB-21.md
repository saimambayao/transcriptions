Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament,
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao

Section 1. Title: This Law shall be known as Barter Trade Center Act of
2019 of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Section 2. Mandate: There shall be established an office to manage and
administer the trade operating in barter system and named as Barter Trade Center,
BTC-BARMM for brevity. Its mandate is to serve and cater into the
entrepreneurial activities of the traders operating within the territory of the
BARMM Region vis-a-vis other neighboring countries of the Philippines,
specifically, with the Brunei Darussalam, Indonesia and Malaysia, and East
ASEAN Growth Area (BIMP-EAGA) Member States and countertrade with the
Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) Member States. It shall render
basic services and responds to needs for a quality services to the public specifically
the economic sector, called the barter traders. It shall take appropriate measures
and actions to foster strong economic relationship of domestic traders with their
business counterpart in Brunei, Malaysia and Indonesia through available means of

by air, land or water transportation.

Section 3. Vision and Mission: As a Center, it shall endeavor to perform and
deliver quality and holistic responsive services and take actions calling for the real-

time attention about operation of the barter trading in the BARMM.

Section 4. The following terms used in the law shall be defined as follows:

A. Barter Trade is the traditional exchanges of goods, products or merchandise
between persons or business cooperatives of a countries or state usually

carried by sea or air transport;

B. Barter Ports relate to the entry and exit points of barterable goods and duly
authorized by law to admit or deny entrance of any goods in accordance with

the existing Philippine and Bangsamoro Autonomous Government laws.

C. Domestic Local Traders refer to those duly accredited and register business
operators whose line of business involve buy and sell of the merchandises,
items and goods within and outside the country; these may include sole
proprietor, partnership, cooperatives and company or corporation duly

register with proper authorities and accredited by the BTC.

D. Foreign Traders pertain to the non-citizen traders engaged in barter trading
of their goods with the Philippines as their markets as well as source of

barterable goods and items they may sell in their country of origin;

Section 5. Objective: The establishment of the BTC shall aim at the

following objectives:

A. To foster an economic environment conducive for business growth of
small and medium scale trades and entrepreneurs in the region;

B. To nurture the entrepreneur skills of the traders by providing them basic
services and facilities enabling them to operate their business
productively;

C. To regulate the operation of local barter traders operating in the
BARMM;

D. To develop linkages between the barter traders and Bangsamoro

Autonomous Government as well as create business relationship between

Sec. 6. Organizational structure of BTC-BARMM -— The office of the
BTC-BARMM Offfice shall be under the direct administrative and technical
supervision of the Chief Minister, Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao. As provided under R.A. 11054, the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Government have the power over the barter trade and
countertrade.

As an Office, it shall be headed by a General Administrator, who will
be assisted by a two (2) Assistant Administrators, and three (3) division
heads. It shall have its own manpower complement as itemized in Annex A
of this law.

The General Administrator must be:

a. Resident of the BARMM for at least five years prior to his/her
appointment;

b. College Graduate; and

c. Atleast twenty five (25) years of age, preferably with vast experience in
commercial transactions.

In no case that the General Administrator and the two assistant
administrators should come from the same tribe. The assistant administrator
must be one in charge of domestic and local traders and the other for foreign

traders. The division chiefs must come at least from two tribes. In hiring of

SEC. 8. Functions - The BTC shall exercise the following functions
to wit:

A. To conduct research on policies designed to improve the operation of the
Office;

B. To formulate the necessary guidelines for its operation as a Center;

C. To register accredit traders, individual, cooperatives or business company
doing barter trading within the region;

D. To collect necessary fees for engaging and availing the programs,
services and projects of the BTC; the collections shall be remitted to the
Bureau of Treasury Office and shall form part of the revenues of the
BARMM;

E. To identify allowable goods to be subject of barter trade, such ready to
wear goods, (RTWs) having in view the existing laws, taxation, barters
and customs of the National Government;

F. To establish linkages with the other government agencies national,
regional and local to carry out its mandate

G. To establish and identify barter ports as entry and exit points of
barterable goods and continue operating those already established within
the BARMM jurisdiction.

H. To extend necessary assistance to barter traders and protect their interest
as economic force.

Section 9. Barter Ports. The BTC shall establish its frontline services
in the Barter Ports in Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, Polloc of Maguindanao, Port of
Lamitan, Basilan and Picong of Lanao del Sur. The BTCC shall have the
exclusive authorities on functions relative to the barterable goods unloaded
in these barter ports. The ports the Regional Port Management Authority of
the Ministry of Transportations and Communication (RPMA-MOTC),
BARMM through its designated personnel may continue managing the ports
on domestic vessels on non-barter related matters.

In these ports, the Department of Finance through the Bureau of
Customs may deploy its representative to ensure that necessary fees
prescribed by law when applicable should paid prior to release of barterable
goods to the concerned trader for circulations.

Section 10. Collections — The collections accruing from registrations
and accreditations of the barter traders shall be remitted according to the
revenue code enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament. In no instance that
these collections be expended prior to its remittance to the proper authorities
unless otherwise provided by laws.

Section 11. Miscellaneous Services. The BTC shall endeavour to
ensure that the barter traders would export the endemic or native products of
the Philippines in general and those locally manufactured by the residents of
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region to their destination when they intend

to procure goods from their foreign suppliers. In addition, BTC may

SEC. 12. Funds- The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of
this Act shall be included in the Budget Proposal of the Office of the Chief
Minister for its integration in the Appropriations Act of the year following
its enactment into law and thereafter. The funds shall include the salaries of
its personnel, the capital outlay, and maintenance and other operating
expenses. Part of financial requirements shall be chargeable against the
development fund of the BARMM pursuant to Section 2, Article XIV on
Rehabilitation and Development, of RA 11054.

The funds of ten million pesos (Php10,000,000.00) for its initial
operation for current year may be charged against available funds released to
the BTA for transitional operation as provided under Section 14 of Article
XVI of RA 11054 providing among others funds necessary to carry out the
requirements of transition, including the organizational activities of the
Bangsamoro Transition Authority, organization, bureaucracy, hiring of
personnel, and the exercise of functions and powers of the Bangsamoro
Transition Authority as provided in this Organic Law, shall be charge

against available funds in the current General Appropriations Act.

Section 13. Applicability. This law shall apply to trades which fall
under Barter Trade System. In particular, the law shall govern the business
transactions transported through air, land and water transportation as the
case may be which uses Bangsamoro Territory as its entry point for their

barterable goods and exit point when buying local products for sale in their
