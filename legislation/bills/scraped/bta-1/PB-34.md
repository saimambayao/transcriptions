Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the Bangsamoro Women
Commission Act of 2019.

section 2. Declaration of Policy. The Bangsamoro Government reaffirms the
important role of women in nation building and regional development, as
enshrined in Republic Act 11054, otherwise known as the Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

lt also recognizes, promotes, protects, and upholds the fundamental rights of
women including the right to engage in lawful employment and be protected from
exploitation, abuse or discrimination.

lt also ensures that women are at the forefront of all peace-building efforts,
including conflict prevention, conflict resolution and conflict management, in
keeping with its commitment under United Nation Security Council Resolution
(UNSCR) 1325.

It is designed to create the right conditions and environment for the harmonious

development of the Bangsamoro women.

A. Nature of the Commission

Section 3. Bangsamoro Women Commission: Creation, Nature and Status. There
is hereby created a Bangsamoro Women Commission, hereinafter referred to as
the ‘Commission’. The Chief Minister shall exercise supervision and control over
the Commission.

The Commission shall be the primary policy-making, coordinating, and monitoring
body on women, gender and development in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

The Commission shail exercise corporate powers. It shall have a seal and may sue
and be sued.

B. The Chairperson and Members

Section 4. Composition. The Commission shall be composed of one (1) Chairperson
and four (4) Commissioners representing the territorial jurisdiction of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region namely, the Province of Basilan including
Lamitan City, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, Lanao del Sur including Marawi City, and
Maguindanao including Cotabato City and the 63 barangays of North Cotabato.

Section 5. Qualification. No person shall be appointed as Chairperson or a
Commissioner, unless she possesses the following qualifications:

(1) Natural-born citizen of the Philippines;

(2) Resident of the Autonomous Region at ieast five (5) years prior to the date
of appointment;

(3) Holder of a bachelor’s degree;

(4) With a good track record in holding positions of responsibility and leadership
in the government, duly registered women’s organizations, or women-
serving organizations and/or institutions;

(5) Possesses good moral character;

(6) Have not been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude.

Section 6. Appointment and Term of Office. The Chief Minister shali appoint the
Chairperson and the four Commissioners of the Bangsamoro Women Commission.

Registered women’s organizations, peopie’s organizations serving women in the

Bangsamoro Autonomous Region may submit list of nominees for the Chairperson
and the four (4) Commissioners to the Chief Minister for his/her consideration.

The Chairperson and Commissioners shall be appointed for a term of three (3) years
and without prejudice to reappointment for another term.

TITLE tl: POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

Section 7. Powers of the Commission. The following powers shall be exercised by
the following:

A. Chairperson

(1) To recommend to the Chief Minister the approval of appointment of the
officers and other personnel of the Commission and fix their compensation,
allowances and other emoluments, subject to civil service and other existing
applicable laws, rules and regulations;

B. Commission as Collegial Body

(2) To suspend, dismiss, or otherwise discipline for cause, any employee, and/or
to recommend the approval or disapproval of the appointment, transfer or
detail of employees, subject to the provisions of existing laws and
regulations;

(3) To enter into contracts subject to the approval of the Chief Minister;

(4) To purchase, own, possess and dispose of properties, real or personal,
including use and control of any land, building, facilities, equipment,
instruments, tools and rights required or otherwise necessary for the
accomplishment of the objectives of the Commission.

(5) To accept donations, gifts, bequests, and grants from benevolent individuals,
entity, corporations, organizations, businesses, establishments and
foundations, whether domestic or foreign;

(6) To monitor and ensure the implementation by various ministries and
agencies of the Bangsamoro Government of their women development
projects and activities as indicated in their respective annual budget,
including its GAD plan;

(7) To issue the rules and regulations in pursuance of the provisions of this Act;
and

(8) To perform any and all other acts incident to or required by virtue of its
creation.

TITLE tii: ORGANIZATION AND STRUCTURE

Section 9. The Commission as a Collegial Body. The Bangsamoro Women
Commission composed of the Chairperson and four (4) Commissioners, as a
collegial body, shall promulgate policies, orders, and resolutions necessary for the
efficient administration of the Commission and for proper execution of its mandate
under this Act.

In the exercise of its powers and functions, the Commission shall sit and render its
order and resoiution en banc. Every such order or resolution of the Commission
must bear the concurrence and signature of majority of Members thereof.

The Commission shall meet regularly. A majority of the Members constitutes a
quorum needed in any en banc meeting of the Commission.

Section i0. Structural Organization of the Commission. The Commission shali have
the following offices and units:

1. Office of the Chairperson;

2. Office of the Commissioners;

3. Office of the Executive Director in charged with the over-all supervision of

Section 11. Provincial Office. The Commission shall be authorized to establish,
operate and maintain a provincial office in each of the major provinces of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Each provincial office shall be supervised by a
commissioner.

The provincial offices shall have the following powers and functions:

1) Implement plans, policies, programs, projects, rules and regulations of the
Commission, in coordination with other relevant provincial offices of
ministries, agencies and offices of the BARMM;

2) Provide efficient and effective service to women and families;

3) Coordinate with other provincial line agencies and other ministries, agencies
and offices, as well as with the local government units, for programs on
women;

4) Perform such other functions as may be provided by this Act or assigned by
the Commission.

Section 12. Access to Funds. The budget allocated for the Commission shall be
directly transferred to the Commission, for efficiency of operation, subject to usual
accounting and auditing rules and regulations.

Section 16. Separability Clause. if any part, section or provision of this Act shall be

Section 17. Repealing Clause. All regional laws, orders, rules and regulations or
parts thereof which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified

Section 18. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
