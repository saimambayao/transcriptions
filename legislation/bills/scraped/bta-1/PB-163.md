EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Government has been steadfast in recognizing the achievements of its
outstanding constituents in their respective fields of profession. Bangsamoro topnotchers bring
pride, honor, and serve as an inspiration to the youth. However, the government understands
the difficulties endured and the efforts exerted by every board or bar candidate.

Every board, bar, or any other national licensure examination candidate has
experienced, in one way or another, a staggering test of mettle through a gauntlet of hardships,
adversities, and pressures, much more now amid the concerns brought about by the COVID-
19 pandemic.

Not only do they experience these burdens but it takes a heavy toll on them mentally,
economically, and spiritually. Consequently, the accomplishment of these candidates to place
on top of the rankings is an impressive feat worthy of emulation and public acknowledgment.

Thus, the enactment of this measure for the due recognition of topnotchers. as well as
to provide them with benefits, privileges, and a respectable amount which will serve as a cash
incentive for topnotchers is proper and reasonable.

The granting of recognition and incentives to the Bangsamoro people and other
inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is one way of inspiring individuals to strive
for greater heights of excellence.

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Excellence and
Incentives Act of 2021”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy and Principles. — It is hereby declared to be the policy
of the Bangsamoro Government to encourage and support the development of excellence in
the autonomous region, promote the self-reliant capabilities of the Bangsamoro to aim for
optimum proficiency and improved aptitude in various fields in education, and recognize their
contributions in bringing honor and distinction.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall promote excellence in all fields
of academic human endeavor by institutionalizing the grant of benefits and incentives to
exceptional Bangsamoro individuals who have successfully placed in the national board, bar
and other national licensure examinations.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. — For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall
be defined as follows:

a. Board, Bar, and other National Licensure Examinations — refers to examinations
conducted nationwide to determine the qualifications of graduates of various
professions who seek licensure to practice their respective professions or areas of
specialization, sanctioned by the appropriate government agencies, such as, but not
limited to, the Professional Regulation Commission, and the Supreme Court of the
Philippines;

b. Topnotchers — refers to individuals who officially ranked from top 1 to top 10 of
national licensure examinations, with the corresponding certification from the
appropriate administering agency.

Section 4. Scope and Coverage. — This Act shall include all Bangsamoro individuals
and other inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao who shall
rank from top 1 to top 10 in any national licensure examination in the academic/educational
field, including professional licensure examinations given by the Professional Regulatory
Commission, and the Philippine Bar Examination exclusively administered by the Supreme
Court of the Philippines.

Section 5. Creation of a Committee. — To fully implement the goal of this program, a
Committee shall be created under the Office of the Chief Minister which shall be responsible
for the following:

a. The formulation of a procedural manual as a guide in the granting of recognition and
incentives to topnotchers;

b. The assessment of documents required to verify their authenticity and the rank of the
grantee; and

c. The amount of incentives that the topnotchers will receive.

Section 6. Appropriations. — The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this
Act shall be included in the budget of the Office of the Chief Minister in the annual
Bangsamoro General Appropriations Act. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the
continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual GAA for BARMM.

Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within nincty (90) days from the
approval of this Act, the MBHTE shall formulate and promulgate the necessary rules and
regulations to implement the provisions of this Act. The implementing rules and regulations
issued pursuant to this section shall take effect thirty (30) days after its publication in at least
two (2) newspapers of general circulation.

Section 8. Separability Clause. — If any provision of this Act shall be held

unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force
and effect.

Section 9. Repealing Clause. — All laws, executive orders or issuances, or any parts
thereof which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

Section 10. Effectivity Clause. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
publication.
