EXPLANATORY NOTE

Upon ratification of the Republic Act No. 11054 or also known as Bangsamoro Organic
Law, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) was created to serve as the interim
government in the BARMM during its transition period. The BTA ensures the
accomplishment of the priority legislations, such as the enactment of laws creating
various offices under the BARMM, among others. In fact, the BTA is mandated to
organize the bureaucracy during the transition period. But this is without prejudice to the
authority of the Bangsamoro Government to reorganize the bureaucracy upon its
constitution.

Under Sec. 8, Article XVI of the BOL, the BTA is specifically mandated to create the
Office on Youth. Sec. 2, Article V, also provides powers to Bangsamoro Government to
develop programs for the youth sector. Moreover, the Bangsamoro Government
recognizes the vital role of the youth in nation-building, promote and protect their
ohysical, moral, spiritual, and natioanlism. The youth sector are even encouraged to
participate in public and civic affairs, and promote mental and physical fitness through
sports.

For this purpose, the Bill is hereby submitted for the creation of the Bangsamoro Youth
Commission, defining its powers, functions, and composition.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of the Bill is earnestly requested.

Section 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Youth Commission of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao Act of 2019.”

Section 2. Creation of the Commission. Pursuant to Article IX, Sec 13 of the
Bangsamoro Organic Law which recognizes the vital role of youth, there is hereby
created a Bangsamoro Youth Commission of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao, hereinafter referred to as the “Commission”. The Commission shall
be administered in accordance with the provisions of this Act, under the supervision and
direction of a Chairperson.

Section 3. Declaration of Policy. The Bangsamoro Government recognizes the vital
role of the youth in nation-building. Similarly, it is the policy of the State to promote and
protect the physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual and social well-being of the youth, and
to inculcate in them patriotism, nationalism, and encourage their involvement in public
and civic affairs. Towards this end, the Commission shall:

(a) promote and protect the physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-
being of the youth;

(b) provide opportunity for the involvement and participation in social challenges,
economic entrepreneurship and political leadership training of the Youth;

(c) imbue upon the youth the values of freedom, justice, and equality and empower
them to create a dignified, just and humane society;

(d) inculcate upon the youth the ideals of patriotism and nationalism, human rights
and other positive values; infuse and cultivate in them humane life and uphold
the sanctity and inviolability of the institution of a family;

(e) establish intervention mechanism for youth activities for civic duty, environmental
protection, agricultural and industrial productivity, reduction of the incidence of
poverty, and acceleration of socio-economic development;

(f) mobilize potentials, skills, abilities and talents of the Youth in the area of
autonomy and redirect their industry, creativity, inventive genius and enthusiasm

Section 4. Definition of Terms. As used in this Act, the following terms are defined as
follows:

(a) “Youth” shall refer to the critical period in a person's growth and development
from the onset of adolescence towards the peak of mature, self-reliant and
responsible adulthood comprising the considerable sector of the population from
the age of fifteen (15) to thirty (30) years.

(b) “Youth organizations” refer to those organizations and institutions whose
members are youth; or any organization or institution whose primary advocacies
and programs address youth development issues and concerns.

(c) “Youth Serving Organizations” shall refer to those registered organizations or
institutions whose principal programs, projects and activities are youth-oriented
and youth-related.

(d) “Commission” shall refer to the Bangsamoro Youth Commission.

Section 5. Status and Nature of the Commission. The Commission shall have the
same status as the other agencies/offices of the Bangsamoro Regional Government
under the Office of the Chief Minister.

The Commission shall exercise corporate powers. It shall have a seal.

The Commission shall have the power to determine its budget in accordance with the
applicable rules and regulations.

Section 6. Mandate and Functions. The Commission shall be the primary policy-
making and coordinating body of all institutions that have youth-related, programs,
projects, and activiites of the Bangsamoro Government.

Its functions include policy determining, planning, coordinating, implementing, regulating
and administering youth programs consistent to the declared policies of this Act.

The Commission shall likewise be responsible for overseeing the accredited youth
organizations and youth serving organizations within the jurisdiction of Bangsamoro
Government.

In fulfilling its mandate, the Commission shall be guided by the declared policies and
principles of this Act and the following strategic objectives:

(a) To provide the leadership in the formulation of policies and in the setting of
priorities and direction of youth promotion and development programs and
activities;

(b) To encourage wide and active participation of the youth in governmental and
non-governmental programs, projects and activities affecting them;

Section 7. Composition and Appointment. The Commission shall be composed of
five (5) Commissioners coming from each of the major provinces in the BARMM. It shall
be headed by a Chairperson appointed by the Chief Minister from the Commissioners.
In no case shall more than one Commissioner come from the same province. This is
without prejudice to additional seats in the event the Parliament creates new province in
the BARMM.

The Chairperson and the Commissioners shall be appointed by the Chief Minister.

Section 8. Term of Office. —Of those first appointed under this Act, the Chairperson
shall hold office for Five (5) years, two (2) Member for Four (4) years and Two (2)
Member for three (3) years, all without reappointment. In no case shall any member be
appointed, re-appointed or designated in a temporary or acting capacity. The
succeeding appointment after the expiration of the term of the first appointment under
this Act shall serve the term of Three (3) years with reappointment for another term;
provided that they should not be more than the age limit at the time of their
reappointment.

The term of the Chairperson and Members of the Commission shall commence on the
day immediately after the end of the previous term, regardless of the date of
appointment. Appointment to any vacancy position occurring during the term shall serve
only for the remaining portion of said term. The successor appointed for the unexpired
term shall ikewise be ineligible for reappointment.

The Chairperson shall, among others, supervise the formulation and implementation of
policies, plans, and programs on youth development.

Section 9. Qualifications. The Chairperson and the Commissioners shall possess the
following qualifications:

(a) a natural-born citizen of the Philippines,

(b) a resident of the BARMM for a period of five (5) years,

(c) must have exercised positions of responsibility and have shown leadership in any
youth and/ or youth-serving organizations or institutions,

(d) a college degree holder, of good moral character and not have been convicted of
a crime involving moral turpitude, and

(e) must have at least one (1) year exposure to youth affairs, projects and programs
management.

In addition to the qualifications stated above, the Chairperson shall not be more than
forty-five (45) years of age and Commissioners shall not be more than forty (40) years
of age, at any time during their incumbency, but both shall not be less than twenty-five
(25) years of age.

shall exercise the following powers and functions:

Section 12. Commission Secretariat. The Commission shall be supported by a
Secretariat headed by the Executive Director. The Executive Director shall be appointed
by the Chief Minister upon the recommendation of the Commission. He shall head the
administrative and finance division and the technical service division.

The Executive Director must not be more than forty-five (45) years of age during his
incumbency, and must possess executive and management experience of at least three
(3) years and with experience in managing youth affairs, projects and programs.

The staffing pattern and compensation schedule of the secretariat shall be in
accordinace with the existing laws, rules and regulations.

responsible for:

Section 14. Provincial Offices. The Commission shall be authorized to establish,
operate, and maintain a provincial office in each of the major provinces of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous in Muslim Mindanao. Each provincial office shall be
supervised by a Commissioner.

Section 15. Powers and Functions of the Provincial Offices. The provincial offices
shall have the powers and functions:

(a) Implement laws, policies, plans, programs, projects, rules and regulations of the
Commission;

(b) Provide efficient and effective service to the youth sector;

(c) Coordinate with provincial offices of other departments, offices and agencies for
youth programs;

(d) Coordinate with LGUs concerning youth affairs; and

(e) Perform such other functions as may be provided by law or assigned by the
Chairperson.

Section 16. Administrative and Finance Division. There shall be created an
Administrative and Finance Division.

The Administrative Unit shall perform the Human Resource and Management, Supplies
and Records functions. While the Finance Unit shall ensure that rules and regulations
regarding accounting, budget management, and cash disbursement are followed.

Section 17. Technical Service Division. There shall be created under the Commission
a Technical Service Division which shall be composed of the following sub-units: 1.
Planning and Development, 2. Research Service Unit, 3. Project Management,
Monitoring, and Evaluation, and 4. Public Relations Office.

Section 18. Youth Coordinating Council. There shall be created a Youth
Coordinating Council which shall be composed of the Chairperson of the Commission,
and Youth Representatives coming from the Ministry of Basic, Technical and Higher
Education, Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, the Ministry of Social
Services, Ministry of Local Government, the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources
and Energy, the Ministry of Labor and Employment, the Ministry of Agriculture,
Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform, and the Ministry of Health, Ministry of Indigenous
Peoples’ Affairs, Regional Commission on Bangsamoro Women, and Ministry of Trade
and Investment and Tourism.

The Council shall assist the Commission in the establishment of policies, standards,
rules and guidelines and other initiatives for its provisions.

The Council shall meet once every three (3) months, or as often as maybe necessary,
upon call of its chairperson on important matters relating to youth affairs, welfare and
development.

Section 19. Abolition and Transfer of Assets, Properties and Funds of the Office
on Bangsamoro Youth Affairs. The Office on Bangsamoro Youth Affairs is hereby
abolished. The powers and functions, applicable funds and appropriations, records,
assets and equipment and properties acquired, owned and possessed by the Office on
Bangsamoro Youth Affairs is hereby transferred to the Bangsamoro Youth Commission.

Section 20. Effect of Separation from Service as a Result of this Act. Any official or
employee of the Office on Bangsamoro Youth Affairs or any other personnel of the
regional or local government separated from the service as a result of the operation and
effect of this Act maybe absorbed, if qualified, by the Commission for the good of the
service, or where qualified therefor, may opt to transfer to another office or elect to
apply for separation pay or retirement benefits: Provided, That the official or employee
who may be absorbed by the Commission shall not suffer any loss or diminution of pay,
seniority or rank.

Section 21. Transitory Provisions. Within sixty (60) to ninety (90) days after the
approval of this Act, the organizational structure, including the creation of new offices,
divisions or units as may be deemed necessary for the Commission to carry out its
powers and functions under this Act, shall be determined by the Chairperson through a
committee that shall be established for the purpose.

The Chief Minister, upon the approval of this act and during the transition period, shall
appoint the commissioners and the chairperson.

Section 22. Tax Deduction and Exemption of Donations and Contributions. - Any
donation, contribution, bequest and grant which may be made to the Commission shall
constitute as allowable deduction from the income of the donor for income tax purposes
and shall be exempt from donor's tax, subject to such conditions as provided under the
National Internal Revenue Code, as amended.

Section 23. Implementing Rules and Regulations. The Commission shall promulgate
and issue such rules and regulations and other issuances as may be necessary to
ensure the effective implementation of this Act within one hundred twenty (120) days
from the approval thereof.

Section 24. Repealing Clause. All laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and
regulations and other issuances or parts thereof which are inconsistent with this Act are
hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

Section 25. Separability Clause. lf any provision of this Act shall be declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full
force and effect.

Section 26. Effectivity Clause. This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from its
publication in at least two (2) newspapers of general circulation.

Approved.
