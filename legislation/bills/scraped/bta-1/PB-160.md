EXPLANATORY NOTE

Section 15, Article 11 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that “the state
shall protect and promote the right to health of the People and instill health
consciousness on them. It is likewise enshrined under Section 11 of Article 12 states
“the state shall adopt an integrated and comprehensive approach to health and
development which shall endeavor to make essential goods, health and other social
services available to the people at affordable costs”

Republic Act 11054, “The Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao" mandates this government to adopt a policy on health that provides for a
comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents. It shall establish by
law a general hospital system to serve the requirements of its people to ensure that the individual
basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable
medical services.

The Ediborah P. Yap Memorial Hospital, otherwise known as the Lamitan District Hospital
was established in 1976 and formerly known as the Basilan City Emergency Hospital but later in
1984 when Lamitan was still a Municipality in the Province of Basilan was changed to Lamitan

District Hospital by virtue of Batas Pambansa Bilang 713, and is operating as level 1 with a 25-Bed
Capacity. It is located at Barangay Limook, Lamitan City, Basilan, with the distance of 30
kilometers from Isabela City, the seat of the Provincial Government.

As the only government hospital operating in Lamitan City, it covers the western half of
the Province as it’s catchment area, addressing the hospitalization requirements of Lamitan City
with the Population based on the 2020 Census has 100,150 and the 8 Municipalities such as;
Tuburan 24,742, Akbar 23,098, Muhammad Ajul 24,625, Tipo-Tipo 25, 531 , Al-Barka 23,736 ,
Ungkaya Pukan 24,016, Sumisip 47,345 and the Island Municipality of Tabuan Lasa 29, 327 with

To date, the Hospital is served by 4 permanent Physicians (including the Chief of Hospital)
and 6 Job Order Doctors as compensation. It caters and provide quality healthcare needs and
services and serves as referral hospital of Rural Health Units within the City and the Municipalities
mentioned- above hereof. The Physicians are backed by 7 Nurses (including the Chief Nurse) and
3Nursing attendants. With the constant increase of the number of patients, 17 Nursing
Attendants were hired, and another 17 Registered Nurses are being requested to compensate
the disparity between nurse-patient ratio.

Other than the Nursing service, the hospital is also served by 2 regular employees, aided
by 2 Job Order employees, assigned in the X-ray department which is servicing 24/7. The Clinical
Laboratory on the other hand is manned by 1 Medical Technologist and 1 Medical Laboratory
Aide. They, too, operate the Laboratory 24 hours a day. The Hospital Pharmacy is manned by 1
regular License Pharmacist. To keep the Pharmacy open 24/7, additional 2 registered pharmacists
and 2 Pharmacy assistants were assigned. Other support services in the hospital include
Nutrition, Dietetics, and Administrative Services also manned by limited number of work force.

In 2014, the hospital recorded a total admission of 2,127 which is 62.46% HIGHER
THAN ITS RATED CAPACITY. In 2018, it dramatically rose to 4,258 or 172.1% and constantly
increased to 199.57% with a total admission of 4,601 in 2019. Due to the existence of pandemic
since 2020, our admission drops to 2,041 at 76.3%.

The hospital is now a PHILHEALTH ACCREDITED HOSPITAL and is 100% No Balance
Billing compliant. Currently Reality Check of the Lamitan District Hospital would reveal that the
need for its capability and capacity enhancement is necessary and expedient for it efficiently and
effectively serve the patients in its catchment area. The rising trend of admitted patients from
2014 up to the first quarter of 2019 increased fourfold of its rated capacity. Daily admissions
alone placed the number of patients from 60-70 patients which is way too high of its holding and
serving capacity. The increase in the number of patients is attributed to the dengue, pneumonia

Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Interim Parliament.

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “LAMITAN DISTRICT HOSPITAL,
UPGRADE ACT OF 2021”.

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. -- It is a policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao as provided under Section 22, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054,
otherwise known as the "Organic Law of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao" to provide for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its
constituents and establish by law and general hospital system to serve the health requirements
of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the
prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.

SEC. 3. Upgrading of the Lamitan District Hospital. -- The Lamitan District Hospital is
hereby upgraded from its present level with 25 bed capacity into Level 2 with 100 Authorized
Bed Capacity in accordance with it set by the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services under the
Department of Health (DOH). The existing building including its landscaping, medical and office
equipment and other facilities, its operating funds and personnel are also hereby upgraded to
include the needed construction and procurement thereof in order to meet the prescribed
standards to the upgraded level.

SEC 4. Integration of the Universal Health Care (UHC) Program. ---The upgrading of the
Lamitan District Hospital shall integrate into its system of operations all pertinent and relevant
provisions under Republic Act No. 11223 otherwise known as the Universal Health Care (UHC)
Act.

SEC 5. Administration, Management, and Organization. -- The Lamitan District Hospital
shall be under the administrative supervision of the Ministry of Health (MOH) who is hereby
authorized to determine and approve the appropriate organizational structure for the
management and operations of the facility, and if necessary, shall include the creation of the
Hospital Board of Directors, defining its powers and functions and the appointment of the officers
and members thereof.

SEC. 6. Appropriations - The amount of Two Hundred Six Million Pesos (Php.
210,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated from the BARMM General Appropriations for the
upgrading of the Lamitan District Hospital, including the construction of infrastructures,
acquisition of medical and office equipment and Annual budget needed for personal services,
maintenance, operations and other expenses for the implementation of this act, and thereafter,
such amount as may be needed for the continued operations of the hospital.

SEC. 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations - The BARMM-MOH shall promulgate the
implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of this Act within sixty (60) days after the enactment
of this act.

SEC. 8. Separability Clause. -- In the event any provision of this act or the application of
such provision is declared invalid, the remainder of this act shall not be affected by such
declaration.

SEC. 9. Repealing Clause. -- All laws and regulations under the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) government including those passed under the defunct
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) government regarding the operations of the
Lamitan District Hospital not consistent with this Act are hereby repealed.

SEC. 10. Effectivity - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a
newspaper of regional circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
