the broad spectrum of interest in preserving, protecting, and promoting various sites
located in the Bangsamoro Region.

This is in line with the provisions of the 1987 Constitution that provides that the

State shall foster the preservation, enrichment, and dynamic evolution of a Filipino
culture based on the principle of unity in diversity in a climate of free artistic and

popularize the nation's historical and cultural heritage and resources, as well as artistic

Consequently, Section 24, Article [X of the Bangsamoro Organic Law mandates

cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their Sultanates, such as those of Sulu,
Maguindanao, Kabuntalan, Buayan, the Royal House of Ranao and of the Iranum, and
the non-Moro indigenous peoples of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

The Sultanate of Sulu was founded by Sultan Sharieful Hashim, who introduced
Islamic political institutions and made Islam as the state religion. Thus, it is only
befitting that we take steps to promote his invaluable contribution to our political
consciousness and aspiration, by preserving, conserving and developing his tomb to
serve as a constant reminder of our origin and illustrious past.

Furthermore, it will enhance and promote Tausug Culture and will contribute to
the prestige of Sulu as the cradle of Bangsamoro government.

To be declared as a historical heritage site and an ecotourism destination, the
Bangsamoro people will have the chance to experience heritage tourism that includes
authentically representing the stories and people of the past, particularly the rich culture
of the Province of Sulu and its people.

Hence, this proposal is an Act Declaring the Tomb of Sultan Sharieful Hashim
as a historical heritage site and ecotourism destination.

I, therefore, humbly request my esteemed colleagues to help me pass this bill.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament assembled:

Section 1. The “TOMB OF SULTAN SHARIEFUL HASHIM" located in Barangay
Buansa, Municipality of Indanan, Province of Sulu, is hereby declared as a historical heritage
site and an ecotourism destination. As such, its preservation and development shall be jointly
prioritized by the Ministry of Trade, Investments and Tourism (MTIT) and the Bangsamoro
Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH).

Section 2. Within six (6) months after the approval of this Act, the MTIT, in
coordination with the BCPCH, the Provincial Tourism Office of the Province of Sulu and the
Municipal Tourism Office of the Municipality of Indanan and other concerned agencies of

plan, and an ecotourism development plan containing, among others, the rehabilitation,

Section 3. The MTIT and the BCPCH, in coordination with the Ministry of Public
Works (MPW), the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), the
Province of Sulu, and the Municipality of Indanan, shall take immediate steps to implement
the historical heritage conservation, development, and promotion plan, and ecotourism
development plan for the “Tomb of Sultan Sharieful Hashim,” and shall incorporate the same
in the Regional Tourism Development Plan that the MTIT shall hereafter develop.

Section 4. The BCPCH, MTIT, MPW, and MENRE shall include the implementation
of this Act in their respective programs, the funding of which shall be included in the annual
Bangsamoro Appropriations Act for the incoming fiscal year.

Section 5. Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the MTIT and the

BCPCH shall jointly promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the proper
implementation of this Act.

Section 6. All regional laws, rules, regulations inconsistent with any of the provisions
of this Act are hereby deemed repealed and amended accordingly.

Section 7. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the

Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation.
