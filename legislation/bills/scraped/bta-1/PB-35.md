EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao declared that: “The Bangsamoro Government
shall fully respect human rights.”!

In the Preamble it pronounced that it “accepted principles of
human rights” while it also provided that one of Bangsamoro
Government power is human rights.* We can also refer to the creation
of a transitional justice mechanism violations as among the legitimate
grievances of the Bangsamoro people that needs to be addressed.3

The Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission was created under
Section 7, Article IX of RA 11054 which is intended to give life to
human rights provisions in the RA 11054, thus:

Section 7. Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission. - There is
hereby created a Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission. The
chairperson and two (2) commissioners of the Commission shall be
appointed by the President upon recommendation of the Chief
Minister. The composition of the Commission shall reflect the ethnic
distribution of the population of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. The chairperson shall be a member of the Philippine Bar and
a resident of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. A commissioner

Chapter IV
MANDATE

Sec. 21. Mandate. —As human rights institution of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, the mandate of the Commission is
to promote and protect human rights including protection of
civilians, during armed conflict, under the framework of
international humanitarian law.

Sec. 22. Human Rights Promotion—Human rights promotion are
programs, activities, projects, services, and initiatives that empower
person, group or people to assert and claim their human rights and to
enable the government to comply with its human rights duties

Sec. 23. Human Rights Protection—There is a human rights
protection when the government complies with its duty to respect,
duty to protect, and duty to fulfill human rights.

Sec. 24. Duty to Respect. —The duty to respect exists when the
government refrain from interfering with or curtailing the enjoyment
of human rights.

Sec. 25. Duty to Protect. —The duty to protect exists when the
government protect person, group, and people from acts or
omissions from government or non-government actors that would
constitute human rights violations or abuses.

Sec. 26. Duly To Fuifill. —There is a duty to fulfill when the

government takes positive steps in facilitating the enjoyment of
human rights.

Sec. 27. Government. —Unless otherwise indicated, the term
“government” shall refer to national government, Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region, local government, agency, security sector of the
government, department, bureau, office, instrumentality, or
government-owned or controlled corporations, government financial
institution, bureau, office, and other government entities. It also
includes person, who is acting on behalf of the government, such as
military officials and personnel, law enforcers, jail and correctional
wardens, other law enforces, and all officials and employees of the
civil service. They may be also be indicated as “government-actor.”

For purposes of international reporting, the “government”
referred to in this section may be indicated as “State” or “state-actor”’
as the case may be.

Sec. 28. International Humanitarian Law—The Commission shall
protect civilians during armed conflict and exercise jurisdiction on
complaint arising from violations of International Humanitarian Law
and its implementing statute Republic Act No. Republic Act No. 9851
Philippine Act on Crimes Against International Humanitarian Law,
Genocide, and Other Crimes Against Humanity.

Chapter VII
HUMAN RIGHTS PROMOTION

Sec. 34. Education and Training. —The Commission shall
conduct regular education and training for the promotion of human
rights with the objectives of making those who are conferred with
human rights know their rights including the redress mechanisms in
case of violation and abuse, and inculcate upon them the human
rights of others and their corresponding responsibilities in the
promotion and protection of those rights.

Sec. 35. Public Awareness. —The Commission shall undertake
human rights public awareness campaigns, events, seminars,
workshops, fora, and summits; engage the mass media, isstuie of press
statements, conduct press conferences, and interviews; utilize social
media and internet; and employ other means of communication in
promoting human rights in public spaces.

Sec. 36. Publication. —The Commission shall publish, in print or
on-line, newsletter, information, education and communication
materials, training materials and such other publication necessary for
the promotion of human rights.

Sec. 37. Investigation—The Commission shall exercise
jurisdiction over complaints on human rights violations and abuse
that occurred in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Investigation
conducted by any other tribunal shall not be a bar to the exercise of

accessible all its mechanisms for redress of human rights violations

order to perform its mandate. These personnel shall be given the
additional and necessary compensation for those working days.

Sec. 39. Commencement of Investigation. — Investigation may
commence upon filing of a complaint, in any form, by any person,
group or people with the Commission free from docket and legal
fees. The Commission, at its own instance, may commence an
investigation.

Sec. 116. Franking Privilege. - All official mail matters and
telegrams of the Commission addressed for delivery within the
Philippines shall be received, transmitted and delivered free of
charge. Provided, that such mail matters when addressed to private
persons or non-government offices shall not exceed One Thousand
and Twenty (1,020) grams. All mail matters sent through government

Chapter XVII
TRANSITORY

Sec. 117. Transfer of Properties, —All assets, capital, records,
accounts, contracts funds, receivables, equipment, and facilities of the
RHRC shall be transferred to the Commission. No liabilities shall be
absorbed by the Commission.

Sec. 118. Hold-Over Capacity—The chairperson, commissioners,
officials and employees of the RHRC holding appointive positions
shall continue to perform their functions in a hold-over capacity
unless they are replaced by new appointees. The chairperson of the
REIRC shall not issue appointments created under this Act during the
transition period.

Sec. 119. Transition Committee. —Upon the appointments of the
members of the Commission Proper, it shall immediately create a
Transition Committee for purposes of transitioning from the RHRC
to BHRC.

Sec. 120. Retirement of the RHRC Personnel.—The retirement of
RHRC personnel shall be subject to the applicable incentives
provided by Section 10, Article XVI of RA 11054.

Act, shall receive the same retirement benefits and privileges at a
