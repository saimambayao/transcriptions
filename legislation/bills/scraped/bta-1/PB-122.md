EXPLANATORY NOTE

Civil Service Commission (CSC) Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 14, s.
2018 or the 2017 Omnibus Rules on Appointments and Other Human Resource
Actions (ORAOHRA), Revised 2018 defines provisional appointment as:

“Provisional - an appointment issued to an appointee who meets all the requirements of the position
except the eligibility but only in the absence of a qualified eligible actually available who is willing
to accept the appointment, as certified by the Schools Division Superintendent. The appointment
may be subject to reappointment (renewal), Scholarship-graduates of the Merit Selection Program
of the Department of Science and Technology — Science Education Institute (DOST-SE]) shall be
allowed to teach on provisional status, which appointment may be renewed every school year but
not to exceed four (4) years.

The issue of provisional/temporary teachers continues to haunt the education
sector since the passage of Republic Act No. 7836, otherwise known as the
“Philippine Teachers Professionalization Act of 1994,” which provides for the
standardization of professional teaching at the level of Basic Education. While
measures were legislated to resolve this issue and provide mechanisms that would
allow the transition of provisional teachers to regular teachers, the problem continues
to persist.

In the case of the defunct Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(ARMM), Muslim Autonomy Act No. 303, otherwise known as the “Strengthened
Basic Education Act in ARMM of 2012” provides for mechanisms in which
provisional teachers may be given permanent status either through their years of
service, or a moratorium for which they may take the Licensure Exam for Teachers
(LET). Unfortunately, the problem still persisted and is now inherited by the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

On May 31, 2020, the Ministry of Basic, Technical, Higher Education
(MBTHE) released Memorandum Order No. 154 and No. 194-B with the subject
Cease and Desist Order, affecting about 828 provisional teachers of the region. The
Civil Service Commission - BARMM and the Bangsamoro Attorney General’s
Office both affirmed that the memoranda were consistent with Rule IV of the
ORAOHRA. Under this rule, provisional or temporary appointment is good only for
one year. However, the affected provisional teachers have stayed in their positions
for almost or more than two decades.

As a compromise and in view of the COVID-19 pandemic, the MBTHE
allocated humanitarian assistance amounting to Php 190,980,000.00 intended for the
affected teachers who were willing to re-apply and work for one (1) year under
contract of service. Part of the budget included Php 10-Million LET Review
program, an appeal for the PRC to conduct special LET exam, Php 15,000.00
monthly salary, and some additional funds for administrative assessment activity. |

With the passage of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 18, otherwise known as
the “Bangsamoro Education Code of 2021,” expectations were high that this would
once and for all resolve the issue, unfortunately, the law remains silent and offers no
remedy or mechanisms for the integration of provisional teachers to the professional
service.

In consideration of the challenges brought upon by the ongoing COVID-19
pandemic and the transition from ARMM to BARMM, this measure aims to
recognize the expertise and contributions of provisional teachers who have been
rendering continuous service for at least ten (10) years to the region. By granting
them permanent status under certain conditions, the authors believe that this would
be the most just recourse to resolving this issue and ensure that they continue to
contribute to the development of the Bangsamoro. Furthermore, this proposed
measure also provides for other mechanisms that would facilitate the integration of
provisional teachers not meeting the 10-year of continuous service criteria to the
professional teaching services.

In view of the foregoing, this representation is seeking the support of his
colleague for the immediate passage of this Bill into law.

AN ACT GRANTING PERMANENT STATUS FOR NON-LICENSURE EXAM
FOR TEACHERS (LET) PASSERS UNDER CERTAIN CONDITIONS, WHO
HAVE RENDERED AT LEAST TEN (10) YEARS OF EFFICIENT SERVICE

TO THE REGION, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:
Section 1. This act shall be known as the “Provisional Teachers Act of 2021.”

Section 2. All non-Licensure Exam for Teachers (LET) passers who have
served the defunct DepED-ARMM for at least ten (10) years shall be given
permanent status in recognition of their expertise and years of service, Provided
that the following conditions be satisfied for them to be qualified for the position in
plantilla:

1. Teacher must have rendered a continuous service to the defunct DepED-
ARMM of at least 10 years in teaching prior to the effectivity of this act;

2. He/She must pass the qualifying exam provided the Ministry of Basic,
Technical and Higher Education (MBTHE) - BARMM:

3. Other conditions as may be deemed necessary.

Granting that an applicant has satisfied all the above conditions, this will only
grant him/her a permanent position but cannot be promoted unless he/she pass the
LET Exam.

Section 3. All non-holders of Professional Teachers License employed under
the defunct DepEd-ARMM who have rendered continuous service for less than ten
(10) years shall be required to take the LET. Those who fail to pass such
examination within two (2) years upon the promulgation of this Act shall be
replaced with LET passers.

Section 4. Graduates of science, mathematics, Statistics, engineering, music,
and other degree courses with shortages of in qualified LET applicants to teach in
their specialized subjects in the elementary and secondary education may be hired:
Provided, that they pass the LET within five (5) years after their date of hiring;

Section 5. The MBTHE shall promulgate the Implementing Rules and

Section 6. All laws, decrees, and executive orders inconsistent with this Act

Section 8. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days from the day of its
