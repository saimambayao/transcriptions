Explanatory Note

Section 2, Article V of the Organic Law provides that the Bangsamoro
Government shall exercise its authority over matters including agriculture,
food security, inland waters, public works, and infrastructure water supply
and services, flood control and irrigation systems, among others.

In close collaboration with the National Irrigation Administration (NIA)
that takes the lead in the development of irrigation projects and facilities in
the country under R.A. 3601, the Bangsamoro Government shall fully
harness its irrigation potentials to increase production and productivity of
the region’s agricultural resources.

The current state of irrigation development in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region is indicated by these statistics. In 2018, the potential
irrigable areas in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM) were about 160,150 hectares, while the area served

was only about 50,096 hectares or the equivalent of a development rate of
31.28 percent of the region’s irrigable areas.’

This implies that the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region still has vast
irrigation potentials that, if properly developed, will address food security
and rice sufficiency in the region.

Presently, the National Irrigation Administration does not have a
regional office in BARMM. The autonomous region is presently served by
NIA’s provincial offices in the provinces. Maguindanao is served by NIA
Region XII, through its provincial office in Maguindanao. Lanao del Sur is
served by NIA Region X, while the Island provinces of Sulu, Tawi-Tawi, and
Basilan are served by the NIA Regional Office IX.

With the establishment of the irrigation coordinating office in BARMM,
the Bangsamoro Government would be able to address the irrigation gaps
and it would be able to work directly on irrigation system development in
the autonomous region.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this measure is earnestly
sought.

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao assembled:

Section 1. Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro
Irrigation Coordinating Act of 2027”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — |t is the declared policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to advance agriculture as a key development
strategy, promote productivity measures, and provide support for small
farmers, land holders and marginal fisherfolks. Irrigation is a major
component of agricultural modernization in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. The prospective expansion of irrigated lands in the BARMM will
contribute significantly to the agricultural progress and economic growth of
the autonomous region.

Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Irrigation Coordinating
Office. — In the pursuit of the foregoing policies, the Bangsamoro Irrigation
Coordinating Office is hereby created to serve as the coordinating agency
for the comprehensive development of all types of irrigation, in close
cooperation with the National Irrigation Administration, with priority to the
national, communal, and individual system of irrigation.

The Bangsamoro Irrigation Coordinating Office shall be under the
Office of the Chief Minister.

Section 4. Functions and Responsibilities of the Office. — The
Bangsamoro Irrigation Coordinating Office shall have the following
functions and responsibilities:

1. To coordinate with the National Irrigation Administration to
study available and possible water resources in the BARMM for the
purpose of utilizing the same for irrigation purposes;

2. To assess and inventory all inland waters such as lakes, rivers,
river systems, and streams within BARMM territorial jurisdiction in
coordination with the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources
(MENRE) as part of the region’s inland water systems for agricultural
production purposes;

3. To coordinate with appropriate agencies of government in the
preservation and management of the inland waters within the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

4. To conduct regular monitoring and evaluation of the progress of
the implementation of regional irrigation programs and _ projects,
including implementation problems and issues, and recommend
solutions for the purpose;

9. To participate in the planning and policy formulation for the
development, utilization, and maintenance of irrigation systems in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

6. To assess and inventory all existing irrigation systems in the
BARMM and their present status and conditions, and make
appropriate recommendations for the purpose;

7. To establish and maintain a database management system for
irrigation systems in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

8. To represent the Bangsamoro region in all activities pertinent to
irrigation development plans and programs;

9. To lead the formulation of a roadmap to further develop the
irrigation systems of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

10. To perform other tasks as directed by the Chief Minister or
other competent authority which includes the NIA Administrator.

Section 5. Organizational Structure. — The Office shall be headed
by an Executive Director to be assisted by a Deputy Executive Director.
The Office shall be composed of the following divisions:

a) Planning and Policy Division;

b) Coordination, Monitoring, and Evaluation Division;

c) Research, Information, and Communication Division;
d) Finance and Administrative Support Division.

Section 6. Functions and Responsibilities of the Executive
Director. — The Executive Director shall have the following functions and
responsibilities:

a) Provide direction and supervision over the full operation of the
Bangsamoro Irrigation Coordinating Office;

b) Provide policy guidelines and standards for the effective and
efficient operation and function of the Office in accordance with its
mandate and responsibilities;

c) Establish a recruitment system guided by civil service rules and
regulations in the selection of its prospective staff and employees in
the career service;

d) Ensure that the mandate and functions of the Office and the
provisions of this Act are faithfully executed and performed;

e) Actively participate in the meetings, conferences and other
functions of the cabinet;

f) Lead the formulation of policy measures as well as
development plans and programs for the benefit, protection, and
welfare of the beneficiaries of irrigation facilities and infrastructure.

Section 7. Functions and Responsibilities of the Division. — The
technical and administrative divisions shall have the following functions and
responsibilities:

1. Planning and Policy Division. — The Planning and Policy
Division shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

a) Coordinate with the Office for comprehensive policy formulation,
planning, and investment programming for irrigation-related
infrastructure facilities and utilities;

b) Coordinate the preparation of the socio-economic profile of
irrigation project service areas;

c) Review and evaluate area irrigation project proposals and make
appropriate actions and recommendations to the appropriate
agency;

d) Coordinate with the implementing agencies relative to the
Irrigators’ Association and farmers’ organization issues and
concerns in the maintenance and operation of their irrigation
systems;

e) Perform such other functions as may be directed by the
Executive Director and other competent authority.

Coordination, Monitoring and Evaluation Division. — The
Coordination, Monitoring and Evaluation Division shall have the
following functions and responsibilities:

a) Coordinate comprehensive monitoring and evaluation activities
related to planning and implementation of irrigation development
projects in cooperation with the appropriate agency of the National
and Bangsamoro Government;

b) Monitor and evaluate the progress of privately-owned irrigation
project implementation or private systems initiated by individuals
or group of farmers;

c) Submit regular monitoring and evaluation reports to higher
authorities pertaining to the actual progress of programs and
project implementation;

d) Coordinate with the appropriate National and Bangsamoro
Government agencies in the proper management and
maintenance of projects related to water supply, drainage control,
and irrigation systems;

e) Perform other functions as may be directed by the Executive
Director and other competent authority.

Research, Information and Communication Division. — The
Research, Information and Communication Division shall have the
following functions and responsibilities:

a) Develop and maintain a data and information system that will
serve as a repository of processed data and statistical information
related to water supply and irrigation systems;

b) Coordinate with local researchers based on statistical data, as

Section 8. Staffing. — The Bangsamoro Irrigation Coordinating Office
shall determine its staffing pattern in accordance with the Government
Revised Compensation and Position Classification System.

Section 9. Appropriations. — The funds necessary for the
operations and functioning of the Office shall be included in the annual
appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and submitted to
the Parliament for its approval.

Section 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within sixty
(60) days from the promulgation of this Act, the necessary rules and
regulations for the proper implementation of its provision shall be
formulated.
