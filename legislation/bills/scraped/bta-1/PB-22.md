Parliament Bill No.: _22
Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao BTA-BARMM:

Section1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as “Recruitment Agency
Accreditation Act of 2019”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the policy of Bangsamoro Government to

_ promote, protect and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro people

particularly the Bangsamoro migrant workers from illegal recruitment, trafficking in
person and violation of rights, of any forms. Towards this end, it shall maximize the
implementation and enforcement of labor laws to protect and ensure that the rights
of Bangsamoro migrant workers shall not be compromised or violated by mandatory
accreditation of all licensed Local and Foreign Recruitment and Employment
Agencies operating within BARMM to register for accreditation.

Section 3. Mandatory Accreditation of Recruitment Agency. - All licensed Local and
Foreign Recruitment and Employment Agencies operating whether main office or
branch office within BARMM shall register for accreditation at Ministry of Labor and
Employment. For this purpose, a certificate of accreditation shall be issued upon
proper compliance hereof.

Section 4. Implementation. — The Ministry of Labor and Employment is mandated to
issue policies and guidelines necessary to implement the provision of this Act.

The MOLE shall conduct inspections to ensure compliance hereof and shall issue
periodically a list of accredited recruitment agencies to be published in a newspaper
of general circulation or in its website.

Within thirty (30) days after the effectivity of this act, only those recruitment agencies
who secure a certificate of accreditation in accordance with this Act and its
Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) shall be allowed to operate in
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Section 5. Penalties. — (a) Any recruitment agency who violates any provision of this
Act shall be punished with a fine of not less than twenty thousand pesos
(Php20,000.00) but not more than fifty thousand pesos (Php 50,000.00) and shall not
be allowed to operate within BARMM, unless, until they registered for accreditation.

(b) Tampering, alteration and forgery of certificate of accreditation shall be punished
with the fine of not less than seventy five thousand pesos (Php 75,000.00) but not
more than one hundred fifty thousand pesos (Php 150,000.00), without prejudice to
the criminal action that may be filed.

Section 6. Separability Clause. — If any provision or part hereof is held invalid or
unconstitutional, the remainder of this Act or the provision not otherwise affected
thereof shall remain valid and subsisting.

Section 7. Repealing Clause. — All regional laws, decrees, orders, rules and
regulation, or parts thereof inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified
accordingly.

Section 8. Effectivity Clause. — This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from
its publication in at least two (2) newspaper of general circulation.

EXPLANATORY NOTES

Recruitment Agency Accreditation Act of 2019 has been crafted specifically to
regulate licensed recruitment agencies operating within Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region for the protection of Bangsamoro migrant workers.

Many of our Bangsamoro migrant workers are victims of illegal recruitment, and
human trafficking that exploit and abuse their vulnerabilities and assaults their
human dignity which cause so many untold sufferings, including their families.

Now, it’s about time for the Bangsamoro Government to give protection and ensure
that the licensed recruitment agencies are compliant with the rules and regulations of
our labor laws.

There is a need for mandatory registration for accreditation of all licensed both local
and foreign recruitment agencies operating within the Bansangmoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) at the Ministry of Labor and Employment
(MOLE) for us to maximize the implementation and enforcement of our labor laws; to
protect and ensure that rights of Bangsamoro migrant workers shall not be
compromised or violated; and to secure the best possible terms and condition for
them.

| appeal to my colleagues to join me in the immediate passage of this bill.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this bill is earnestly requested.
