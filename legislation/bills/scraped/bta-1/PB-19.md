Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the “INDIGENOUS PEOPLES
DAY ACT”.

Section 2. Purpose - To recognize, respect and promote the identity, cultural
integrity and right to self-determination of the Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples within the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Section 3. Declaration of Policies - The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region of
Muslim Mindanao [BARMM] reaffirms the policies embodied in the 1987 Philippine
Constitution, Republic Act 8371 [Indigenous Peoples Rights Act of 1997 (IPRA) ], Republic
Act 9054 [Organic Act], and international treaties and agreements concerning indigenous
people, such as, but not limited to the UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples
(UNDRIP) as adopted by the General Assembly on September 13, 2007. It is hereby
declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to:

a. Affirms that indigenous peoples are equal to all other peoples, while
recognizing the right of all peoples to be different, to consider themselves
different, and to be respected as such, and also that all peoples contribute
to the diversity and richness of civilizations and cultures, which constitute

the common heritage of humankind; (United Nations Declaration of Rights
of the Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP)

. Recognize, respect, and promote all rights of the Non-Moro Indigenous

Section 4. Definition of Terms:

a. Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples — As mentioned in Section 8, Article 7
of RA11054 refers to homogenous societies identified by self-ascription and
ascription by others, who have continuously lived as organized community
on communally bounded and defined territory, and who have, under claims
of ownership since time immemorial, occupied, possessed, and utilized such
territories; sharing common bonds of language, customs, traditions, and
other distinctive cultural traits, and who have, through resistance to
political, social and cultural inroads of colonization, non-indigenous
religions and cultures, become historically differentiated from the majority
of Filipinos. Non-Moro Indigenous Peoples shall likewise include peoples
who are regarded as indigenous on account of their decent from the
populations which inhabited the country, at the time of the conquest or
colonization, or at the time of inroads non-indigenous religions and
cultures, or the establishment of present state boundaries, who retain some
or all of their own social, economic, cultural and political institutions, but
who may have been displaced from their own tradition domains or who may
have resettled outside their ancestral domains. It shall include, but not
limited to the, Teduray, Lambangian, Dulangan Manobo, B’laan, Erumanen
ne Menuvu, and Higaonon within BARMM;

b. Indigenous Peoples (IP) Day - a special non-working holiday day to
express the Bangsamoro Governments’ recognition, respect, and promotion
of the identity, cultural integrity and right to self-determination of the Non-
Moro Indigenous Peoples within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao.

Section 5. Coverage - This Act shall have force and effect within the territories
of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Section 6. Indigenous Peoples (IP) Day - It is hereby declared that October
29 of every calendar year as Indigenous Peoples (IP) Day and further declaring it as
special Non-Working Holiday within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao.

Section 7. Funds for Support — There shall be funds to be allotted for the
celebration of the Indigenous Peoples (IP) Day in the amount of ten Million Pesos to be
taken from the regular budget of the MIPA.

Section 8. Implementing Ministry - This Act and the funds allotted for

~ celebration of Indigenous Peoples (IP) Day shall be implemented by the Ministry of

Indigenous Peoples Affairs (MIPA).

Section 9. Saving Clause. — This Act will not in any manner adversely affect
the rights and benefits of the Indigenous Peoples under Republic Act 8371, otherwise
known as the Indigenous Peoples Rights Act of 1997, other conventions,
recommendations, international treaties, regional and national laws, awards, customs
and agreements.

Section 10. Separability Clause. - Should any provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional by a competent court, other provisions shall not be affected thereby shall
remain in full force and effect.

Section 11. Repealing Clause. - All laws, issuances, decrees, or any part or
parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended
accordingly.

Section 12. Effectivity Clause. - This Act shall take effect within fifteen (15)
days following the completion of its publication in at least two (2) newspapers of regional
circulation.
