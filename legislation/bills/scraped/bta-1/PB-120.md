EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro is a nation with rich culture and very long history, enough
to consider us among the First Nation of this country. Unfortunately, until the advent
of social media and the internet, our very limited way of preserving our cultural
memory and history is through story telling. In Tausog, we call it “kissa”, an Arabic
word that literary means “story”. It’s no wonder that except for recent documents
and records, what we only have are references to the original.

Lately, some of our writers and intellectuals succeeded in reducing into
writing our history and traditions. These writings, nevertheless, are scattered in
various libraries and personal collections.

The maintenance of our own archival records is one of the important tasks of
the Bangsamoro Regional Government that should be advanced to preserve our
collective identity and history. The initiative to strengthen the system of
management and archival records of the Bangsamoro Government through the
establishment of the Office of the Bangsamoro Regional Archives complements the

ARTICLE I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Regional
Archives Act of 2021%.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. —

a. It is the policy of the State to conserve, promote, and popularize the nation’s
historical and cultural heritage and resources, as well as artistic creations.

b. All the country’s artistic and historic wealth constitutes the cultural treasure
of the nation and shall be under the protection of the State which may regulate
its disposition.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. — For purposes of this Act, the following definition
of terms are hereby adopted:

(a) Archival Value — refers to the value attached to records that remain useful
for the Bangsamoro Government, its people, and the general public
because those records make government accountable to its citizens;
provide evidence about public policies and programs; protect or verify
individuals’ rights and entitlements; and provide information about the
important people, issues, places, and events that make up the story of
Bangsamoro history;

(b) Archives — shall refer to:

1. Public records, papers, periodicals, books or other items, articles or
materials, whether in the form of electronic, audio-visual or print,
which by their nature and characteristics have enduring value, that have
been selected for permanent preservation;

2. The place (building/room/storage area) where archival materials are
kept and preserved; and

3. An organization (or part of an organization) whose main function is to
select, collect and preserve archival records and make such records
available for public use.

(c) Disposal — refers to the act of selling, burning, or any other way of
discarding valueless records in accordance with the provisions of this Act
and other existing laws.

(d) Disposition — refers to the systematic sorting out of records in the office
storage whereby those classified as valuable records are permanently
preserved and those which are classified as valueless are disposed of.

Section 4. Objectives. This Act is primarily aimed at:

(a) Organizing and establishing the system of management and administration
of the Bangsamoro Government archival records;

(b) Ensuring that full and accurate records of the affairs of the Bangsamoro
Regional Government are maintained and preserved;

(c) Increasing public confidence in the integrity of public records keeping and
management;

(d)Ensuring accessibility of public records relevant to the promotion and
preservation of Bangsamoro cultural heritage;

(e) Supporting the safekeeping of private records.

ARTICLE II
OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO REGIONAL ARCHIVES

Section 5. Office of the Bangsamoro Regional Archives. — The Office of the
Bangsamoro Regional Archives, hereinafter referred to as OBRA, is hereby created
and organized, structurally and functionally, in accordance with the provisions of
this Act. It shall be under the Office of the Chief Minister.

The OBRA shall be primarily responsible for the implementation of the
objectives and provisions of this Act. It shall plan, develop, prescribe, disseminate
and enforce policies, rules and regulations, and coordinate programs governing the
creation, general protection, use, storage and disposition of public records including
the acquisition, storage and preservation of public archives and providing facilities
for reference, research or other purposes.

Section 6. Powers and Functions. — In pursuit of its mandate, the OBRA shall have
the following powers and functions:

(a) Act as the official repository of all agreements, laws, public records,
bulletin, books, gazettes, thesis, publications, exhibitions, audio-recording,
video-recording or images and any other writings or documentations,
relating to or involving the struggle of the Bangsamoro and the history,
culture, tradition and practices of the Bangsamoro, the Non-Moro
Indigenous People, and the settler communities;

(b) Conduct an inventory of the above writings and documentations that are
already existing and under the custody of the Bangsamoro Government;

(c) Maintain a registry of all public records under the custody and control of
the Bangsamoro Government which shall be made available for public
inspection;

(d) Implement a records management and archival administration system for
the efficient maintenance, preservation, retention, and utilization of public
records of the Bangsamoro Government;

(e) Adopt security measures and protection of the public records of the
Bangsamoro Government;

(f) Obtain, recover, and take custody and management of all public archives
that are not in the custody of the Bangsamoro Government;

(g) Accept, store, and preserve any public archive transferred or donated to the
Bangsamoro Government;

(h) Facilitate the acquisition of public records and private archives that possess
archival value through transfer, donation, purchase, or any other legal
means;
