38 Article II. RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS OF BANGSAMORO OVERSEAS WORKERS
39
40 Section 5. Every OBW shall have the following rights:
41
42 a. Right to equality. There shall be equal treatment on workers based on race, color,
43 sex, language, religion, political beliefs, and age.
44
45 b. Right to equal Pay. Every OBW shall always get the right amount of pay for the
46 work he/she renders.
47
48 c. Right to work. Every OBW is entitled to the choice of working for any company,
49 good working environment, and protection from unemployment.
50
51 d. Right to Rest and Leisure. Each OBW must be assigned a fair amount of working
52 hours per week including periodic holidays with pay.

1 e. Right to Standard Treatment. The employer or any member of the company shall
2 not subject an OBW to any kind of abuse nor inflict any form of physical violence
3 or harassment or any act tending to degrade the dignity of BOWs.
4
5 f. Guarantee of Privacy. Respect for the privacy of the OBW shall be guaranteed at
6 all times and shall extend to all forms of communication and personal effects. This
7 guarantee equally recognizes that OBW is obliged to render satisfactory service at
8 all times.
9
10 The preceding enumerated rights shall include all the rights and privileges recognized
11 under the national laws and international laws, declaration and agreement on the rights
12 and privileges of overseas workers as well as those rights and privileges granted to them
13 by the host country.
14
15 Section 6. Obligations of OBW. Consistent with the existing rules and regulations
16 governing the Recruitment and Employment of Land based Overseas Filipino Workers, it
17 is the duty of all Bangsamoro Overseas Filipino Workers to conduct themselves in the
18 most professional, responsible and ethical manner in the performance of their duties and
19 fulfill the following obligations:
20
21 a. As to their family. Every OBW is obliged to provide the family ample financial and
22 moral support; to communicate regularly; and to preserve the integrity of the family;
23
24 b. As to their fellow workers. Every OBW shall assist, support, and cooperate with
25 fellow workers; and refrain from committing acts which are detrimental to the
26 interest of fellow workers;
27
28 c. As to the country. Every OBW shall obey the laws of the Republic of the Philippines
29 at all times; abide by this rules and regulations; and be an ambassador of goodwill
30 of the country;
31
32 d. As to their Licensed Recruitment Agency and Principal/Employer. Every OBW
33 shall provide the licensed recruitment agency and principal/employer with accurate
34 statements/certifications regarding his/her skill, experience and other
35 qualifications; understand and abide with the terms and conditions of the
36 employment contract as well as the company rules and regulations; and maintain
37 a high level of professionalism and productivity in the performance of contractual
38 obligations; and
39
40 e. As to the host country. Every OBW shall respect the customs, traditions and
41 religious practices of the host country, and obey the laws of the host country.

26 Article VI. PENALTIES
27
28 Section 31. Any person found guilty after final conviction of illegal recruitment shall suffer
29 the penalty of imprisonment of not less than twelve (12) years and one (1) day but not
30 more than twenty (20) years and a fine of not less than One million pesos (P1,000,000.00)
31 nor more than Two million pesos (P2,000,000.00).
32
33 Section 32. The penalty of life imprisonment and a fine of not less than Two million pesos
34 (P2, 000,000.00) nor more than Five million pesos (P5, 000,000.00) shall be imposed if
35 illegal recruitment constitutes economic sabotage as defined therein.
36
37 Provided, however, That the maximum penalty shall be imposed if the person illegally
38 recruited is less than eighteen (18) years of age or committed by a non-licensee or non-
39 holder of authority.
40
41 Section 33. Any person found guilty of any of the prohibited acts shall suffer the penalty
42 of imprisonment of not less than six (6) years and one (1) day but not more than twelve
43 (12) years and a fine of not less than Five hundred thousand pesos (P500,000.00) nor
44 more than One million pesos (P1,000,000.00).
45
46 If the offender is an alien, he or she shall, in addition to the penalties herein prescribed,
47 be deported without further proceedings.

15 subsistence allowance benefit, money claims, compassionate visit, medical evacuation
16 and medical repatriation.
17

20 officers, employees or agents with relatives, within the fourth civil degree of
21 consanguinity or affinity, who work or have interest in any of the licensed
22 recruitment/manning agencies or in any of the government agencies involved in the
23 overseas employment program shall be disqualified from providing this workers'
24 insurance coverage.
25
