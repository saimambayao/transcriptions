EXPLANATORY NOTE

The former war-torn area of Parang, Sulu is an eye-catcher of a destination boasting pristine
beaches and other untapped potential tourist spots that cannot be described into words by even the most
virtuoso of writers. One of these potential tourist spots is Bud Tukay Peak.

Section 35, Article XIII of Republic Act 11054 otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law
provides that “The Bangsamoro Government shall promote tourism within the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. It may recommend the designation of tourism enterprise zones to the Tourism Infrastructure and
Enterprise Zone Authority, in accordance with Republic Act No. 9593, otherwise known as “The Tourism
Act of 2009.”” Pursuant to the aforesaid provision of law, this bill is being introduced in order to present
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region with another jewel that it can pride over its neighboring regions while
also providing our constituents with a source of income.

With the declaration of Bud Tukay as an official community-based ecotourism zone and the
possible developments which would come following this declaration, opportunities will open for the local
residents in terms of employment. This would also take advantage of the current efforts of the National

Section 1. Short Title. -This Act shall be known as the "Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Act".

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — Pursuant to Section 35, Article XIII of the Bangsamoro Organic law
which provides, among others, that “The Bangsamoro Government shall promote tourism within the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region“, it is the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region of Muslim
Mindanao to promote its tourism industry that is ecologically sustainable, responsible, participative,
culturally sensitive, economically viable, and equitable for the local community. Towards this end, the Bud
Tukay is hereby declared an ecotourism zone. It shall be accorded priority development by the Ministry of
Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT), Ministry of Environmental, Natural Resources and Energy
(MENRE), and the Ministry of Public Works (MPW)

Section 3. Objectives. - Pursuant to the above declaration of policy, the Bangasamoro Government shall
endeavor to accomplish the following:

(a) Develop and promote community-based ecological tourism in the development of the Bud
Tukay Peak and its surrounding areas;

(b) Ensure the preservation of the environment and conservation of the biodiversity within the Bud
Tukay Peak Ecotourism Park.

(d) Guarantee that the financial and economic benefits of the tourism industry are equitably shared

Section 4. Definition of Terms. -As used in this Act:

(a) Community-based ecotourism refers to a form of tourism where the local community and its
residents have substantial control and involvement in the development and management of the local
tourism industry and a greater portion of the economic gains remain within the community;

(b) Ecotourism refers to a form of sustainable tourism within a natural area where community
participation, protection and management of natural resources, cultural and indigenous knowledge
and practices, environmental education, and ethics, as well as economic benefits are fostered and
pursued the enrichment of host communities and the satisfaction of visitors;

Section 5. Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Zone. -The Bud Tukay Peak located in Barangay Parang,
Municipality of Parang, Province of Sulu is hereby declared as a community-based ecotourism zone. It
shall be accorded priority development by the Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT),
Ministry of Environmental, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), and the Ministry of Public Works
(MPW).

Section 6. Scope and Coverage. - The Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Zone shall cover the entire area of Bud
Tukay, which has an elevation of 1,998 feet, the actual area and coverage of which shall be determined by
the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), in coordination with the local
government unit concermed.

Section 7. Establishment of a Buffer Zone. - There shall also be established buffer zones surrounding the
BTP for the purpose of providing extra protection around the area against and for the tourist or visitors as
well as local people where restrictions shall Apply. Such buffer zones shall be managed according to the
ecotourism development and management plan formulated pursuant to this Act. The Council shall exercise
its authority over the designated buffer zone.

Section 8 Formation of the Bud Tukay Peak Development Council and its composition. - A multi-
sectoral council to be known as the "Bud Tukay Peak Development Council", hereinafter referred to as the
Council, is hereby created. It shall be composed of the following members:

(a) The Mayor of the Municipality of Parang as Chairperson;

(b) The Provincial Director of the MTIT or its representative as Vice-Chairperson;
(c) The Provincial Director of the MENRE or its representative;

(d) The Barangay Chairman of Barangay Parang;

(e) One (1) representative from an academic institution; and,

(f) One (1) representative from among the residents of Bud Tukay Peak, as private sector
representative.

All appointed and elected members of the Council shall serve only for the duration of their
respective terms of office or appointment.

The members of the Council shall serve without compensation, except for the actual and necessary
traveling and subsistence expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, either in their attendance in
meetings of the Council or in connection with other official business authorized through a resolution of the
Council, subject to existing rules and regulations. Each member shall have the full capacity and

The Secretary of the Sangguniang Bayan of the Municipality of Parang shall act as Secretariat for

Section 9. Functions and Responsibilities of the Council. -The Council shall be responsible for the
following:

(a) Formulation and implementation of the ecotourism development and management plan in
consultation with the local residents, businesses, and other stakeholders;

(b) Formulation of the rules and regulations in relation to the maintenance and security of the
ecotourism site;

(c) Implementation of a monitoring plan for the Forest area and to ensure the peoples well-being in
the BTP;

(d) Assessment and evaluation of the potential impact of the development to the environmental
safety and well-being of the site, communities, and enterprises

(e) Provision of technical and financial assistance to the communities within the site;

Section 10. Bud Tukay Peak Ecotourism Development and Management Plan. -The MENRE and the
MTIT, in coordination with the MPW, shall jointly assist the Bud Tukay Peak Development Council in the
formulation of the ecotourism development and management plan for the BTP. The ecotourism
development and management plan shall ensure the preservation of the eco-park and shall include the
following components:

(a) Carrying capacity of the eco-park;

(b) Management and maintenance of the Forest areas;

(c) Product development for local income generation;

(d) Capacity-building for the local community to allow residents therein to ably manage
economically viable and responsible, community-based ecotourism business enterprises and

(1) Solid and liquid wastes management;

Section 11. Coordination with other Ministries of the BARMM and other National Agencies. -The
MENRE and the MTIT shall closely coordinate with the Council, as well as the relevant regional offices of
the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MLE), Ministry of Public Works (MPW), Ministry of Interior and
local Government (MILG), Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR), Ministry
of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE), Philippine National Police (PNP) and other line
agencies of the government in the implementation of this Act.

Section 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations. -Within thirty (30) days from the effectivity of this Act,
the MENRE, the MTIT, and the Council, in consultation with the Provincial Tourism Office of the Province
of Sulu, other concerned government agencies and stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and
regulations for the proper and effective implementation of this Act.
