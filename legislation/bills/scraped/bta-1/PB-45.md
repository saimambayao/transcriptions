Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in plenary
session:

Section 1. Title - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Sultanate
Act of 2020”.

Section 2. Definition of Terms  - For purposes of this Act, the following
terms are hereby defined as follows:

(a) “Act” refers to this document unless otherwise stated;
(bo) “Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL)” refers to Republic Act 11054;

(c) “BARMM’ refers to the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao;

(d) “Constitution” refers to the 1987 Constitution unless otherwise
provided;

(e) “Commission” refers to the Bangsamoro Sultanate Commission;
(f) “Sultanate’refers to the territory and/or area ruled by the sultan;

(g) “Sultan” refers to the royal title of a leader.

Section 3. Declaration of Policy - It is the declared policy of the
Bangsamoro government under Section 4, Article 5 of the Republic Act No.
11054 to recognize, protect, and guarantee the customs, beliefs, and
traditions of the Bangsamoro people. They shall adopt measures to ensure
mutual respect and protection of the distinct beliefs, customs, and
traditions of the Bangsamoro people. Republic of the Philippines

Section 8, Article 5 provides that the Bangsamoro government shall ensure
that settlers enjoy the rights guaranteed in this Organic Law. For this
purpose, the Parliament shall create an office for settler communities to
promote the welfare and address issues and concerns of settlers in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Section 1, Article X also ensures that the Bangsamoro justice system will
be administered in accordance with the unique cultural and _ historical
heritage of the Bangsamoro. The dispensation of justice in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall be in consonance with the
Constitution, Shari'ah, traditional or tribal laws, and other relevant laws.
The traditional or tribal laws shall be applicable to disputes of indigenous
peoples within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Section 17, Article X further provides that the Parliament shall enact laws to
promote and support the traditional or tribal justice systems that are
appropriate for the indigenous peoples.The indigenous peoples shall have
the right to use their own commonly accepted justice systems, conflict
resolution institutions, peace building processes or mechanisms, and
customary laws and practices within their respective communities and as
may be compatible with the national legal system and with internationally
recognized human rights. The traditional justice systems are the
mechanisms to determine, settle, and decide controversies and enforce
decisions involving disputes between members of the indigenous peoples
concerned in accordance with the customary laws of these communities.

Section 4. Organizational Structure - The Commission shall have
sub-offices in each BARMM provinces. It shall be composed of five (5)
sub-offices:

(a) Basilan Office of the Bangsamoro Sultanate

(b) Lanao Office of the Bangsamoro Sultanate

(c) Maguindanao Office of the Bangsamoro Sultanate
(d) Sulu Office of the Bangsamoro Sultanate

(e) Tawi-tawi Office of the Bangsamoro Sultanate

Section 5. Composition - The Commission shall have a chairperson and
a commissioners. The chairperson and commissioners shall be elected
from the sultans of each of the five (5) BARMM provinces. The chairperson
shall be chosen from among the commissioners themselves. To avoid
monopoly, there shall be rotation of the chairmanship among the
commissioners. Further, each chairperson can serve for one full-term of
three years only.

The chairperson and the commissioners shall be appointed by the Chief
Minister, provided however, that in the appointment process the chief
minister shall ensure representation of the sultans in the different provinces
of BARMM.

Section 6. Qualifications and Appointments - No person shall be
appointed as chairperson and commissioners unless he or she possess
the following qualifications:

(a) Resident and registered voter of the BARMM for at least five (5)
years prior to appointment;

(b) At least thirty-five (35) years of age on the day of his/her
appointment;

(c) Must be able to read and write;

(d) Must have a wide-ranging knowledge and understanding on the
current issues and problems of the sultan in the BARMM;

(e) The appointment of the chairman and commissioners shall be
subject to the rules of Civil Service Commission and other pertinent laws.

Section 7. Power, Duties and Functions - The Commission shall have
the following powers and functions:

(a) Act as a leader or arbiter of disputes;

(b) Enforce decisions involving disputes in the community in
accordance with customary laws that are not inconsistent with the national
legal system;

(c) Adopt laws that promote and support traditional and tribal justice
system that are appropriate;

(d) Adopt programs that will strengthen the principle of conciliation and
mediation as mandated in the BOL;

(e) Provide assistance and advise to the BARMM government in the
implementation and maintenance of peace and order;

(f) Ensure the full participation of the traditional leaders in the
formulation, implementation and evaluation process related to the
strengthening of the tribal justice system;

(g) Make policies which address the needs of the Bangsamoro taking
into consideration the dynamics of their culture and traditional leadership;

(h) Develop leadership style suitable for the Bangsamoro to address
their wide-ranging social problems;

(i) Make recommendations that will exhibit the objectives of their
leadership of becoming a meaningful, dynamic, productive and functional
leaders;

(j) Make policies that will promote goodness, justice, peace and
harmony;

(k) Undertake to develop programs and projects for the preservation of
the royal traditions of the Bangsamoro;

Section 8. Funding - For the initial organization and operation of the
Commission, the sum of One Hundred Million Pesos (PHP 100,000,000)
shall be funded from the unappropriated portion of the block grant for the
year 2020. Thereafter, the subsequent funding shall be included in the
annual General Appropriation Act (GAA) of the BARMM.

Section 9. Amendments and Revisions - Consistent with the provisions
of the BOL, this Act may be amended and revised by the Bangsamoro
Parliament.

Section 10. Repealing Clause - All laws, decrees, orders, rules and
regulations which are not inconsistent with this Act, are hereby repealed or
amended accordingly.

Section 11. Separability Clause - The provisions of this Act are hereby
declared to be separate and in the event one or more of such provisions
are held unconstitutional, the validity of other provisions shall not be
affected thereby.

Section 12. Effectivity Clause - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15)
days following the completion of the publication in a newspaper of general
circulation in the BARMM.

So Approved.
