Cotabato City had witnessed more history than any other place in Mindanao. Its history
dates back to the fifteenth (15") century when Shariff Kabunsuan, an Arab missionary,
landed along the banks of the Rio Grande de Mindanao and introduced Islam to the
natives.

Those that did not accept the arrival of the Muslims went into higher ground or the

interior of the island. During the same year, the Sultanate of Maguindanao was formally
established, with Kota Wato as its capital.

Islam was the faith that moved the early settlers to communal life, and to establish the
Sultanate of Maguindanao with its golden age ushered in by Sultan Dipatuan Qudarat
during the 17" century the time when Cotabato City developed as the Capital town of
Maguindanao.

In the nineteenth (19") century, when Sultan Makakua ruled, roads and wharfs were

constructed which gave rise to the birth of modern day of Cotabato. However, the then

Municipality of Cotabato was first organized at the later part of the 19" century when the

Spaniards established a military post at what is now Barangay Tamontaka, one of the

earliest Christian settlements founded south of the Philippines. Cotabato was then

eficially founded in 1862 when the Pueblo de Cotabato was established: Christianity
as introduced in the area in around the year 1870.

However, the city broke off administratively from Maguindanao as it
rejoined Soccsksargen in the 1990s. Now many sources consider the city as part of the
present Cotabato province, although geographically it is stil] considered part of
Maguindanao.

Traditionally resisting efforts for inclusion to the Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanap despite serving as the government center of the ARMM, the January 21,
2019 Bangsamoro Autonomous Region creation plebiscite resulted in the ratification of
the Bangsamoro Organ i

This means Cotabato City may serve as the Capital of the region and the Offices of the
former ARMM may be retained for use by the Bangsamoro Regional Government.

AN ACT FIXING COTABATO CITY As PERMANENT SEAT OF THE GOVERNMENT FOR
THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)

Be it enacted by the Parliament in session assembled:

Section 1. Seat of Bangsamoro Government. The permanent seat of the
government for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)
is hereby fixed in the City of Cotabato pursuant to Section 1, Article VII of the
Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao also
known as Republic Act (R.A.) 11054, which provides that the Parliament shall fix by
law the permanent seat of the Bangsamoro Government anywhere within the
territorial jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region taking into
consideration accessibility and efficiency in which its mandate may be carried out
under this Organic Law.

Section 2. Territorial Jurisdiction. As ratified through a plebiscite on January 21,
2019, Cotabato City has been included as part of the BARMM area with an
overwhelming number of votes of YES to include the same as part of the BARMM
area.

Section 3. According to Accessibility. Cotabato City can be reached via air
through Awang airport which is located in the neighboring municipality Datu Odin
_4nsuat (D.O.S.), Maguindanao, BARMM. The city is also accessible by land from
almost all parts of the Mindanao and combination of by land and by sea from some
Parts of Visayas, Luzon and National Capital Region.
