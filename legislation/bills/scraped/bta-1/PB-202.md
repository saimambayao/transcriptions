A social perspective on development requires addressing poverty in all its
dimensions. It promotes people-centered approach to poverty elimination advocating
the empowerment of people living in poverty through their full participation in all
aspects of political, economic and social life, especially in the design and
implementation of policies that affect the poorest and most vulnerable groups of society.
An integrated strategy towards poverty elimination necessitates implementing policies
geared to more equitable distribution of wealth and income and social protection
coverage.

Hence, the urgency to focus on the elimination of hunger and extreme poverty in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao is impelled by the impending
fiscal crisis and the state of poverty of at least one third of the regions population.

Poverty elimination is the responsibility not only of the Bangsamoro government
but must also be the concern of all sectors of society, and, therefore, who should all
link arms and unite in the fight against poverty; that, pursuant to this Act, the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region should create the Bangsamoro Anti-Poverty
Commission for purposes of poverty data collection of all poverty alleviation programs
of the Ministries and agencies as the over-arching strategy in poverty reduction.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

AN ACT CREATING THE BANGSAMORO ANTI-POVERTY COMMISSION,
DEFINING ITS POWERS AND FUNCTIONS, APPROPRIATING FUNDS
THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

SECTION 1. 7it/e. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Anti-Poverty Act
of 2022”.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the policy of the Bangsamoro government
to alleviate poverty through policies that will provide the delivery of adequate social
services to improve the quality of life of the Bangsamoro people.

Pursuant to this Act, the Bangsamoro government recognizes the need to adopt focused
and specific measures that will ensure poverty reduction through stringent monitoring
of poverty alleviation program implementations.

SECTION 3. Coverage. — This Act shall cover impact monitoring of all poverty
alleviation programs within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

SECTION 4. Creation. — There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Anti-Poverty
Commission (BAPC), hereinafter referred to as the “Commission”.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro government shall create a “Bangsamoro Anti-
Poverty Commission” which generates updated and disaggregated data necessary in
targeting beneficiaries, conducting more comprehensive poverty analysis and needs
prioritization, designing appropriate policies and interventions, and monitoring impact
over time.

This data collection shall respect the fundamental human right of privacy, ensure data
quality, and uphold data protection principles of legitimate purpose, transparency, and
proportionality.

SECTION 5. General Objective. —The Bangsamoro Anti-Poverty Commission aims to
assess the success of poverty alleviation programs in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.

SECTION 6. Purpose. — This Act enables the Bangsamoro government to have an
economic and social tool to measure the success of implementation of poverty
alleviation and development programs.

SECTION 7. Definition of Terms, — As used in this Act:

a. Data refers to the information to be collected by the BAPC which includes the
compendium of facts, figures, and maps in relation with the identified target
indicators of poverty such as health, nutrition, water, sanitation, shelter,
education, income, employment, security, and community participation; and

b. Minimum Basic Needs refers to the services programs of the Bangsamoro
government Ministries and agencies that addresses person's basic rights. These
are, but not limited to, the right to adequate food, right to decent work, right to
relevant and quality education, right to adequate housing and the right to the
highest attainable standard of health.

SECTION 9. Functions of the Commission. — The Commission shall exercise the
following, to wit:

a. Develop and review data collection forms;
b.

Monitor, oversee, and coordinate the impact of poverty alleviation programs in
the region;

Ensure the success of the implementation of poverty alleviation programs of the
Bangsamoro government in the provinces, cities, municipalities and barangays;
Process the data generated and submitted by the ministries and other agencies;
Submit semi-annual report to the Office of the Chief Minister and to the
Bangsamoro Parliament;

Analyze, study and conduct research on the poverty alleviation programs in order
to meet the minimum basic needs of the people;

Recommend measures on poverty alleviation programs of the Ministries and
other agencies; and

Perform such other functions as may be necessary or incidental to the proper
implementation of this Act.

Every bureau and services shall have at least two divisions which shall be headed by a
director and division chief, respectively.

SECTION 14. Data Collection. — Each Ministry and other agencies are the primary
data collecting authority within its office. The Commission shall be copy furnished
thereof.

SECTION 15. Periodicity of Data Collection. — Regular and synchronized data
collection shall be in accordance with the existing guidelines implemented by the said
ministries or agencies.

SECTION 16. Storage and Access of Data. — The Commission and the Ministries or
agencies are allowed to maintain their own database for use in local level planning and
program implementation.

The Commission shall receive and store all aggregated data gathered by the Ministries
to create databank of collated information. It shall undertake measures to ensure the
integrity and safety of the gathered information against unnecessary leakage and
access by unauthorized persons.

SECTION 17. Social Protection. — The appropriate Bangsamoro government
ministries and agencies shall use the data generated by the Commission in prioritizing
timely, relevant and much-needed social protection programs of government in areas
identified to have the highest incidence of poverty.

SECTION 18. Appropriations. — The amount necessary for the initial implementation
of this Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of the BARMM and to
carry out the functions and powers of the BAPC including the acquisition of its office
facilities and equipment as provided in this Act, an amount of Two Hundred Million
pesos (PhP 200,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated. Thereafter, such sums as may be
needed for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual
General Appropriations Act.

SECTION 19. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within sixty (60) days from
the effectivity of this Act, the Bangsamoro Anti-Poverty Commission, the Ministry of
Social Services and Development, the Ministry of Labor and Employment, the
Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries
and Agrarian Reform, and the Ministry of Finance, and Budget Management, in
coordination with other concerned agencies implementing services programs shall
promulgate the rules and regulations necessary for the effective implementation of this
Act.

SECTION 20. Separability Clause. — If any provision or part of this Act is held
unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in
full force and effect.

SECTION 21. Repealing Clause. — Any \aw, presidential decree, executive order,
letter of instruction, administrative order, rule or regulation contrary to or inconsistent
with the provisions of this Act is hereby repealed, modified, or amended accordingly.

SECTION 22. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
