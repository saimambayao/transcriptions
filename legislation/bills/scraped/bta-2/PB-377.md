13
14
15 SECTION 1. Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Sports Institute Act of
16 2025”.
17
18 SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the policy of the State to promote and develop
19 sports in the country, to achieve excellence in international sports competitions, to ensure success in
20 the country's quest to achieve competitiveness in the Olympic Games and to promote international
21 amity among nations. Pursuant thereto, the establishment and construction of a national sports
22 training facility shall be a primary strategy to achieve the foregoing objectives.
23
24 Consistent with the Bangsamoro Government’s commitment to youth development, social
25 cohesion, and regional autonomy under the Bangsamoro Organic Law, this Act affirms the vital role
26 of sports in promoting regional identity, unity, and peace.

27

28 SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.

29

30 e Sports - refer to all forms of physical activity which, through casual or organized
31 participation, aim at expressing or improving physical fitness and mental well-being,
32 forming social relationships, or obtaining results in competition at all levels.

33

34 e Athlete - a person who is proficient in sports and other forms of physical exercise.

35

36 e Bangsamoro Sports Institute (BSI) - A school, training center, and organization in the
37 Bangsamoro Region that helps young people learn, develop, and improve their
38 mental and physical skills through sports.

39

40 e Bangsamoro Sports Development Fund - refers to a special fund allocated by the
41 Bangsamoro Government and sourced from annual appropriations, grants, or
42 donations, intended solely for the planning, implementation, monitoring, and
43 evaluation of sports development programs and related infrastructure projects within
44 the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. This Fund shall support grassroots sports,
45 talent identification and training, capacity-building of sports personnel, procurement
46 of equipment, and the maintenance and improvement of sports facilities.

47

48

49 SECTION 4. Basis. — This Act is enacted pursuant to:

50

51 (a) Section 19, Article XTV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states: “The State shall
52 promote physical education and encourage sports programs, league competitions, and
53 amateur sports, including training for international competitions, to foster

13 SECTION 5. Coverage. This Act shall cover all current and aspiring athletes residents of

14 the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao who are competing for regional,
15 national and international competitions.

16

17 SECTION 6. Objectives. — The objectives of the BSI shall be:

18

19 (a) To serve as the primary regional hub for sports training, education, and athlete
20 development in BARMM;

21

22 (b) To produce world-class athletes through scientific training, coaching, and sports
23 research;

24 (c) To provide training and certification programs for coaches, referees, and sports
25 officials;

26

27 (d) To serve as a venue for local, national, and international sports events;

28

29 (e) To support peacebuilding and social inclusion through sports and physical
30 activities across diverse communities in BARMM; and

31 |

32 (f) To promote Islamic values, discipline, and character development through sports.
33 (g) To coordinate with the Philippines Sports Institute of the Philippine Sports
34 Commission for supporting Bangsamoro athletes competing internationally.

35

36 SECTION 7. Legal Mandate and Interagency Coordination. - The Bangsamoro Sports

37 Institute shall operate under the policy guidance and supervision of the Bangsamoro Sports
38 Commission and shall coordinate with the Philippine Sports Commission, Department of Education,
39 Commission on Higher Education, and other relevant national sports bodies to ensure conformity
40 with national training, competitions, and accreditation standards.

41

42 SECTION 8. Accreditation and Certification.- The BSI shall develop and implement
43 standards for accreditation of sports coaches, trainers, officials, and partner institutions, subject to
44 minimum national standards and guidelines issued by relevant government agencies.

45

46 SECTION 9. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The BSI shall, in consultation and
47 coordination with the the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, the Ministry of
48 Transportation and Communications, the Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Basic Higher and
49 Technical Education, Bangsamoro Youth Commission, formulate and promulge the necessary rules
5@ and regulations within thirty (30) days upon the effectivity of this Act.

51

52 SECTION 10. Administration and Management. The Bangsamoro Sports Commission
53 shall have the authority to administer, manage, and maintain the BSI. The Chairperson of the
54 Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall have the power to determine and appoint officials of the BSI.

55

56 SECTION 11. Ownership of Property. The land, facilities, and other physical properties of
57 the Bangsamoro Sports Institute shall be owned by the Bangsamoro Sports Commission on behalf
58 of the Bangsamoro Government.

59

60 Titles and legal ownership shall be processed in accordance with Indigenous People’s Rights Act
61 (RA 8371), the Bangsamoro Land Laws, and other applicable national and regional policies on
62 public lands and government property.

21 SECTION 13. Monitoring and Evaluation. - The Bangsamoro Sports Commission shall

22 develop an annual performance evaluation mechanism for BSI operations, sports programs, athlete
23 development, and infrastructure utilization, and submit an annual report to the Bangsamoro
24 Parliament and the Office of the Chief Minister.

25

26 SECTION 14. Promotion of Indigenous Sports. - The BSI shall include in its programs the
27 preservation and promotion of indigenous sports and traditional martial arts of the Bangsamoro
28 people as part of cultural heritage and identity.

29

30 SECTION 15. Separability Clause. — If any provision of this Act is held unconstitutional or
31 invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

32

33 SECTION 16. Repealing Clause. — All laws, rules, regulations, or issues inconsistent with
34 this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

35

36 SECTION 17. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a

37 newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
