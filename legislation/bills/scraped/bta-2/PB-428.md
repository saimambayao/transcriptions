EXPLANATORY NOTE

Teachers play a central role in improving the quality of education in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. As the education system continues to develop,
there is a growing need to support teachers who wish to pursue advanced studies
in fields related to their teaching and academic work.

The Bangsamoro Education Code already recognizes the importance of providing
assistance, including scholarships and other forms of support, to teachers and
learning facilitators. However, there is still no specific law that puts this policy into
operation through a structured graduate scholarship program for teachers under
the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE).

This proposed measure provides a clear framework for granting scholarships for
master’s and doctorate studies to qualified MBHTE teachers. It allows for financial
assistance and study support, while requiring scholars to render return-service so
that the benefits of advanced training are returned to the Bangsamoro education
system.

Consistent with the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the Bangsamoro Education Code,
and existing national laws, this measure aims to strengthen teaching quality,
address gaps in specialized fields, and help build a more capable and responsive
education sector in the region.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING A GRADUATE SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM
FOR TEACHERS UNDER THE MINISTRY OF BASIC, HIGHER AND
TECHNICAL EDUCATION (MBHTE), PROVIDING FOR MASTER’S AND
DOCTORATE DEGREE GRANTS, RETURN-SERVICE OBLIGATIONS, AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament
assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “MBHTE Teachers
Graduate Scholarship Act”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is hereby declared the policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to promote quality, accessible, and relevant education
by strengthening the academic qualifications and professional competence of
teachers through graduate and postgraduate education.

Pursuant to Article IX of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the
Bangsamoro Organic Law, and Chapter 5 (Sections 185 to 187) of Bangsamoro
Autonomy Act No. 18, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Education Code, the
Bangsamoro Government shall provide institutional support, including scholarships
and financial assistance, to teachers and learning facilitators as part of continuing
professional development.

This Act seeks to operationalize and institutionalize such policy by establishing a
Structured graduate scholarship program for teachers under the Ministry of Basic,
Higher and Technical Education, subject to accountability and return-service
obligations.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. For purposes of this Act, the following terms
or words shall mean or be understood as follows:

a) MBHTE refers to the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education
of the Bangsamoro Government;

b) Teacher refers to a permanent or regular teaching personnel under
MBHTE, including those in basic education, higher education, technical-vocational
education, or madaris education;

c) Graduate Studies refer to master’s or doctorate degree programs duly
recognized and accredited by competent authorities;

d) Scholar refers to a teacher granted benefits under this Act; and

e) Return-Service refers to the mandatory service rendered to MBHTE
after completion of graduate studies.

SECTION 4. Establishment of the Graduate Scholarship Program. There is
hereby established a Graduate Scholarship Program for MBHTE Teachers, which
Shall provide financial and academic assistance to qualified teachers pursuing
master’s or doctorate degrees in fields relevant to their teaching assignments or
institutional functions.

SECTION 5. Objectives of the Program. The Program shall aim to:

a) Improve the quality of instruction and academic leadership in MBHTE
institutions;

b) Address shortages in specialized and critical subject areas;

c) Enhance research, curriculum development, and innovation in education;
and

d) Ensure that public investment in graduate education directly benefits the
Bangsamoro educational system.

SECTION 6. Eligibility Requirements. To qualify for the scholarship, an
applicant must:

a) Be a permanent or regular teacher under MBHTE;

b) Have rendered at least two (2) years of satisfactory service, unless
otherwise determined by MBHTE;

c) Be admitted to or enrolled in a recognized and accredited higher
education institution;

d) Pursue a field of study directly related to the subject, specialization, or
academic discipline currently taught or performed;

e) Have no pending administrative or criminal case; and
f) Execute a Scholarship and Return-Service Agreement.

SECTION 7. Priority Fields of Study. MBHTE shall determine priority fields
based on regional and sectoral needs, which may include but are not limited to:

a) Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM);
b) Teacher Education and Educational Leadership;

c) Madaris and Islamic Education;

d) Special and Inclusive Education;

e) Technical-Vocational and Skills Education;

f) Information and Communication Technology; and

g) Other fields critical to the development of the Bangsamoro educational
system.

SECTION 8. Scholarship Benefits. The scholarship granted under this Act shall,
subject to applicable laws, rules, and regulations, include the following benefits,
as may be determined by the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education:

a) Tuition and other school fees;
b) Monthly or semestral allowance to support living and academic expenses;
c) Book, research, thesis, or dissertation assistance; and

d) Such other reasonable educational expenses necessary for the completion
of graduate studies.

SECTION 9. Selection and Administration. MBHTE shall establish a
transparent, merit-based selection process through a duly constituted scholarship
committee and shall issue guidelines governing application, evaluation, approval,
and monitoring of scholars.

SECTION 10. Return-Service Obligation. As a condition for the grant of
scholarship under this Act and in recognition of the public investment in the
professional development of teachers, every scholar shall be required to render
return-service to the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education, subject to
the following:

a) Every scholar shall render one (1) year of return-service for every year of
scholarship availed, unless otherwise provided in the Implementing Rules and
Regulations;

b) Return-service shall be rendered in any MBHTE-supervised institution,
program, or assignment, as may be determined by the Ministry; and

c) Scholars may be deployed to priority or underserved areas consistent with
institutional needs and regional development goals.

SECTION 11. Breach of Obligation. Failure to complete the approved program
or to comply with the return-service obligation, without justifiable cause, shall
result in:

a) Refund of all scholarship benefits received, subject to reasonable terms
and conditions; and

b) Such administrative sanctions as may be provided under applicable civil
service rules.

SECTION 12. Monitoring and Evaluation. MBHTE shall monitor the academic
progress of scholars and evaluate the impact of the Program on institutional
performance and educational outcomes.

SECTION 13. Appropriations. The amount necessary for the _ initial
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of
the Bangsamoro Government, subject to the availability of funds. Thereafter, such
sums aS may be necessary for its continued implementation shall be included in
the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations. Within ninety (90) days
from the effectivity of this Act, MBHTE shall promulgate the necessary
Implementing Rules and Regulations.

SECTION 15. Separability Clause. If any provision of this Act is declared
invalid, the other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and
effect.

SECTION 16. Repealing Clause. All laws, rules, and regulations inconsistent
with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 17. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its

publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao.
