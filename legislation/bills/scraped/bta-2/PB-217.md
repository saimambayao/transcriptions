AN ACT
DECLARING “KUTAWATO CAVE” LOCATED IN BARANGAY ROSARY
HEIGHTS 1, CITY OF COTABATO, A HISTORICAL HERITAGE SITE AND AN
ECOTOURISM DESTINATION, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Declaration of Historical Site and Eco-tourism Destination. - The
“Kutawato Cave," located in Barangay Rosary Heights 1, Cotabato City, is hereby
declared a historical heritage site and an ecotourism destination. As such, its
preservation, development, and promotion shall be jointly prioritized by the Ministry of
Trade, Industry, and Tourism (MTIT) and the Bangsamoro Commission for the
Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH).

SECTION 2. Development Plan. - Within six (6) months after the approval of this Act,
the MTIT and the BCPCH, in coordination with the Cotabato City Tourism Office (CCTO)
and other concerned agencies of government, shall prepare a historical heritage site
conservation plan and an ecotourism development plan containing, among others, the
conservation, preservation, rehabilitation, construction, installation, and maintenance of
such appropriate facilities and infrastructure that shall enhance tourism in the area;

Provided, That said conservation and development plan shall ensure the preservation of

SECTION 3. Implementing Agencies. - The MTIT and the BCPCH, in coordination with
the Ministry of Public Works (MPW), the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources
and Energy (MENRE), and the City Government of Cotabato, shall take immediate steps
to implement the site preservation and ecotourism development plan for “Kutawato
Cave.” It shall incorporate the same in the Regional Tourism Development Plan that the
MTIT shall hereafter develop.

SECTION 4. Appropriations. - An initial amount of Three Million Pesos (Php
3,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated and shall be made available to the implementing
agencies referred to in Section 3 of this Act. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary
for the continued implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act.

SECTION 5. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within sixty (60) days from the
effectivity of this Act, the MTIT and the BCPCH shall promulgate the necessary rules and
regulations for the proper implementation of this Act.

SECTION 6. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, rules, and regulations inconsistent
with any of the provisions of this Act are hereby deegned repealed and amended
accordingly.

SECTION 7. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication
in the Bangsamoro Gazette or a newspaper of regional circulation.
