Explanatory Note

In the context of contemporary struggles for self-determination and
national identity, the sacrifices made by mujahideen and mujahidat—those
who have fought and continue to fight for their people's rights—cannot be
overlooked. This Act is designed as a critical step toward addressing the
profound injustices faced by the families of non-decommissioned martyred
mujahideen and mujahidat. Recognizing their sacrifices through the
provision of assistance, benefits, and privileges acknowledges the gravity
of their plight and ensures that their families do not suffer further as a
consequence of their loved ones' martyrdom in the struggle for justice.

Martyred mujahideen and mujahidat refer to those combatants who have
lost their lives in the pursuit of ideological and nationalistic causes, and
whose contributions are integral to the historical narrative of resistance
and resilience within their communities. However, it is crucial to
acknowledge that many of these brave individuals died during the struggle
and thus were not decommissioned, meaning they have not formally laid
down arms or transitioned to civilian life. Consequently, their families are

An Act Providing for Assistance, Benefits and Privileges to Families of
Non-Decommissioned Martyred Mujahideen and Mujahidat Providing
Funds Therefore and for Other Purposes

Be it enacted by the legislative authority assembled in the Bangsamoro
Parliament that the following provisions be made for the assistance, benefits,
and privileges to families of non-decommissioned martyred mujahideen and
mujahidat.

Section 1: Short Title

This Act may be cited as the "Martyred Mujahideen Act."
Section 2: Definitions

For the purposes of this Act:

a) "Martyred Mujahid" refers to a member of the freedom-fighting force in the
Bangsamoro Region who has died in the line of duty.

b) "Mujahidat" refers to female counterparts engaged in similar efforts.

c) "Family" encompasses the spouse, children, and dependent relatives up to the
3'4 Civil Degree of the martyred individuals.

Section 3: Establishment of the Martyred Mujahideen Assistance Fund

A fund shall be established known as the Martyred Mujahideen Assistance Fund,
with provisions for appropriations to support the initiatives outlined in this Act.

Section 4: Financial Assistance

The families of non-decommissioned martyred mujahideen and mujahidat shall
receive monthly financial aid during their lifetime, with provisions to increase
assistance as per the economic conditions and inflation rates, ensuring adequate
support for basic needs.

Section 5: Educational Benefits

The Act shall mandate the establishment of an Educational Scholarship Program
for children of martyred mujahideen and mujahidat to ensure uninterrupted
access to quality education. Scholarships shall cover tuition fees, school
supplies, and ancillary costs through recognized educational institutions.

Section 6: Healthcare Privileges

Families of martyred mujahideen and mujahidat shall receive comprehensive
healthcare benefits, including access to public hospitals and specialized
treatment facilities, free of charge, coupled with psychological counseling
services to address the emotional trauma arising from their loss.

Section 7: Employment Opportunities

The government shall create a quota of employment opportunities within public
sectors for qualified dependents of martyred mujahideen and mujahidat.
Incentives shall be offered to private entities to hire members of these families to
foster economic stability and independence.

Section 8: Housing and Land Grants

Eligibility criteria outlined under this provision shall allow for housing assistance
in the form of grants, subsidized loans, or outright land grants for the
construction of homes to ensure the families have a secure environment.

Section 9: Legal Assistance

The Act shall provide for legal support services for the families of martyred
individuals to navigate issues such as property rights, welfare services, and any
administrative processes they may encounter, ensuring they receive all entitled
benefits.

Section 10: Commemoration Activities

This Act establishes a mandate for annual commemorative events, honoring the
sacrifices of martyred mujahideen and mujahidat, promoting national unity and

awareness of their contributions, thereby instilling a sense of respect and
gratitude among the citizenry.

Section 11: Implementation Agency

The Office of the Chief Minister shall oversee the proper implementation of this
Act through the creation of a dedicated task force specifically focused on the
welfare of families of martyred mujahideen and mujahidat.

The Office of the Chief Minister shall promulgate the necessary implementing
rules and regulations for the proper implementation of this act.

Section 12: Monitoring and Evaluation

A rigorous framework shall be established to monitor and evaluate the proper
implementation of this Act. An independent body shall be constituted to assess
the efficacy of the assistance programs and recommend necessary
enhancements or reforms.

Section 13: Funding Provisions

All finances required for implementing this Act shall be appropriated from the
national budget. Additional funds may be sourced from donations, grants, and
international aid, ensuring the Act remains sustainable and transparent.

Section 14: Penalty Clause

Any individual or entity found to be fraudulently obtaining assistance under this
Act shall be penalized in accordance with the Revised Penal Code and other
applicable laws.

Section 15: Effectivity

This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of
general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
