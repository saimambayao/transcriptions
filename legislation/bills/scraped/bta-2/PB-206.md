BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO AND FOR

EXPLANATORY NOTE

Roads play a significant role as part of essential and basic social infrastructure for
livelihood and economic activity. Indubitably, roads facilitate better travel experiences
for people, making their daily life activities even more active and competitive, which may
open more opportunities for trade and commerce, tourism and make travel easy for
people going to work or school and other personal transactions.

According to the 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan 2023-2028, BARMM
accounts for only 3% of the national roads of the country. As of 2021, out of the entire
national road network of 1,038 kilometers in BARMM, only 10.09 kilometers are left
unpaved. However, about 24% of the region’s national roads are in poor condition.

Meanwhile, the consolidated length of the local road network in BARMM is
10,912.58 kilometers, consisting of provincial, municipal, and barangay roads. This data
was collected from all LGUs of BARMM municipalities and provinces, including the
recently added Special Geographic Area. More than 40% of BARMM’s local roads are
unpaved. The pavement rates of Local roads by province are as follows: Maguindanao,
39.36%; Lanao Del Sur, 48.58; Basilan, 68.15; Sulu, 64.47; Tawi-Tawi, 47.03; Special
Geographic Area, 26.19.

Under the present institutional setup, local roads are maintained by the local
government. Pursuant to the Local Government Code of 1991, province-funded roads are
maintained by the local Provincial Engineer’s Office (PEO), city/municipal roads are
maintained by the respective Engineering services, and the Barangay Roads by the local
Barangay council through the Barangay Roads maintenance committee. However, the
Ministry of Public Works shall have a concurrent role with the LGUs pertaining to road
maintenance.

The lack of proper maintenance of local roads within the BARMM region is
apparent, as shown by potholes, cracked pavements, collapsing shoulders, and faded
road markings, among others.

Local governments, by law - particularly through the Local Government Code, are
expected to address the deterioration of public assets, like roads, within their respective
jurisdictions. Due to the nature of the country’s budgeting system and the dynamics of
local decision-making processes, needed action to stall the deterioration of infrastructure
usually comes late or not at all. As a result, this often leads to more expensive
rehabilitation or reconstruction. Moreover, under current government procurement laws
and practices, construction of infrastructure projects entails bidding that is most often
participated in by contractors from outside the community. In reality, routine
maintenance does not require big contractors. For all types of roads, the budget for
operations and maintenance comes from three primary sources: the General Fund
(General Appropriations Act); 20 percent of the Internal Revenue Allotments to Local
Governments, or the Development Fund; and the Special Local Roads Fund (SLRF), as
provided for under Republic Act (RA) 8794 or the Motor Vehicles Users’ Charge (MVUC)
Law.

Despite the existing provisions under the Local Government Code of 1991
mandating the LGUs to undertake maintenance of their respective local roads, in the
enactment of the annual budget, the amount to be allocated for road maintenance shall
be subject to the discretion of the LGUs, which may not even provide the substantial
difference for the purpose if not listed as its top priorities. With the meager amount given
by the LGUs for road maintenance, it may not attain the purpose of ensuring that roads
under their responsibility and jurisdiction are properly maintained. It is, therefore, not
unusual for road users to see a lack of maintenance of the local roads in the whole of the
Bangsamoro Region. The absence of a sense of urgency for the I.GUs pertaining to their

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Local Roads
Maintenance Act.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to ensure the maintenance of local roads in all of its constituent local
government units to accelerate the socio-economic-cultural development of the region.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. - As used in this Act, the term “Road Maintenance”
shall mean preserving and restoring existing road infrastructure facilities in good
operating condition to lengthen their useful life and avoid premature deterioration and
expensive rehabilitation or reconstruction. It includes any road work specifically related
to maintaining water control or road safety and visibility, such as:

d. Ditch cleanout - means manual cleaning and desilting of clogged, silted, and unclean
ditches, including open canals.

e. Replacing or installing relief culverts - refers to installing concrete culverts in areas

road surface.

f. Clearing culvert inlets and outlets on existing forest roads - means manual cleaning of
culvert inlets and outlets that have been clogged with naturally occurring silt and
vegetation on forest roads.

h. Road Safety devices and warning signs maintenance - means repainting, repairing,
reinstallation, and/or replacement of road safety devices such as, but not limited

i. Reblocking - refers to the replacement of damaged concrete pavements.

j. Cracks and Joints Sealing - refers to the use of industrial hot-mix asphalt, cracks and
unfilled joints in the concrete pavement are filled to prevent further damage to the
road surface due to water seepage into the concrete blocks.

k. Regravelling - means spreading gravel on unpaved road surfaces to make them
more conducive for the riding populace.

SECTION 5. Local Road Maintenance Fund. - The constituent provincial, city,
municipal, and barangay local government units of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao are hereby mandated to apportion at least one percent (1%) of their
respective total budget appropriations for the maintenance of their respective local roads.

twelve (12) members with the Chief Minister as chairman and the following as
members: the Minister of Public Works; the Minister of Interior and Local
Government; the Minister of Labor and Employment; the Head of the Bangsamoro
Planning and Development Authority; the Minister of Health; the Minister of
Social Services and Development; Regional Director of the DPWH-PMO; City and
Provincial Engineers; Philippine Institute of Civil Engineer’s Representative;
Representative from the Business Sector; and, Representative from the Women’s
sector.

The Council shall perform the following functions:

1. To assess the situation of road maintenance in the Region;

2. To evaluate the local roads maintenance plans and activities of the
Provinces and Highly Urbanized and Independent Cities;

3. To determine the gaps in the maintenance and safety of road connectivity
and provide necessary technical and other assistance to the constituent local
government units in the Region;

4. To coordinate road maintenance plans, programs, and activities of the
Regional Government with the constituent local government units;

5. To regularly update the national DPWH on the road maintenance of the
Region; and

6. Other functions as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.

following members with the Local Chief Executive of the Provincial LGU as its

SECTION 7. Classification of Provincial, City, Municipal, and Barangay Roadways. -
For the purpose of delineating the scope of responsibility of each local government unit,
roads are classified as follows:

1. Regional Roads

a. Roads that connect provinces, cities, and municipalities that are not city,
provincial, municipal, barangay, or national roads;

b. Roads that are vital for regional interconnectivity and economy economic
development;

c. Roads that support tourism, agricultural development, peace and security,
and urban expansion are not included in the local roads of the LGUs and
are declared as regional roads by the Bangsamoro Regional Development
Planning Board.

2. Provincial Roads

a. Roads that connect cities and municipalities without traversing National
Roads;

b. Roads that connect National Roads to barangays through rural areas; and
c. Roads that connect to major provincial government infrastructure.
3. Municipal and City Roads:
a. Roads within the Poblacion;
b. Roads that connect to provincial and national roads; and

c. Roads that provide inter-barangay connections to major municipal and city
infrastructure without traversing provincial roads.

4. Barangay Roads:

a. Other public roads (officially turned over) within the barangay and not
covered in the above definitions.

The authority and responsibility to maintain roads shall be subject to subsequent
conversions.

SECTION 8. Types of Maintenance. - All funds allocated pursuant to the provision of
this Act shall be primarily utilized for routine, preventive, and periodic road
maintenance, which shall include, but not be limited to, pavement maintenance,
regravelling, shoulder maintenance, vegetation control, bridge and _ structure
maintenance, drainage improvement and maintenance, traffic service maintenance,
concrete reblocking, seal widening, erosion control, and safety device installation and
maintenance.

SECTION 9. Local Roads Inventory and Road Map. - Local government units shall
conduct an annual inventory of the existing Local Road to update the provincial, city,
municipal, and barangay road assets.

SECTION 10. Identification of Local Roads. - Upon recommendation of the local
development board of each constituent local government unit, a local government unit
may, pursuant to a resolution, identify and acknowledge their respective local roads.

However, identification and acknowledgment of the Regional Roads shall be pursuant to
an executive order duly issued by the Chief Minister upon recommendation from the
Bangsamoro Regional Economic Development Planning Board.

SECTION 11. Role of the Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE), the Ministry of
Social Services (MSSD), and the Technical Education Skill and Development (TESD). -
A Memorandum of Agreement shall be entered into among MOLE, MSSD, TESD, and
the LGUs for purposes of promoting and facilitating local employment, ensuring
livelihood in the community, and the development of human resource skills and technical
knowledge of the locals on the different road maintenance activities that may be
undertaken in their localities.

SECTION 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within sixty (90) days from the
approval of this Act, the Ministry of Finance and Budget and Management and the
Ministry of Interior and Local Government shall, in consultation with other concerned
government agencies, promulgate rules and regulations necessary to implement this Act.

SECTION 13. Appropriations. - The necessary appropriations for the operation and
maintenance of the Bangsamoro Local Roads Maintenance Program shall be
appropriated and approved by the constituent local government units concerned, as well
as the Regional Government for regional roads.

SECTION 14. Separability Clause. - If any portion or provision of this Act is

subsequently declared invalid or unconstitutional, other provisions hereof which are not
affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.
