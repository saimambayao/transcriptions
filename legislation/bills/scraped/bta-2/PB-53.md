Explanatory Note

"Wherever the art of Medicine is loved, there is also a love of Humanity.”

— Hippocrates

Under Sec. 16 of Article IX, of Republic Act No. 11054, it shall be a top
priority of the Bangsamoro Government to establish, maintain, and support a
complete and integrated system of quality education, which shall be a
subsystem of the national education system. The Bangsamoro Government
shall develop an educational framework relevant and responsive to the needs,
ideals, and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people.

This Parliament body have approved several bills establishing
numerous hospitals in the region, adopted health standards and upgraded
facilities of existing hospitals in observance of the policy on health of the
Bangsamoro government. In relation to that, this representation created this
bill to further implement the aforementioned policy by creating a medical
school to encourage and open the opportunity to the Bangsamoro people to
study medicine.

The Mindanao State University — College of Medicine is the only
government medical school in Mindanao and the only medical school in the
region, started in the school year 1984-1985. The college aims to provide

This representation is of the opinion that it is high time for this

SETION 3. The Establishment of Bangsamoro Medical School. — There is
hereby established in the Province of Maguindanao with a central site and
administration office at Cotabato City a medical school which shall be known
as the “Bangsamoro Medical School”, the same being organized as a body
corporate under that name.

The Bangsamoro Medical School shall be constructed and start its operations
under capacity and capability as prescribed by the licensing standards and
regulation of the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) of the Philippines.
The Bangsamoro Medical School may be upgraded thereafter into a higher
facility for advance medical studies upon the evaluation and recommendation
by the Commission on Higher Education

SECTION 4. Purpose. - To provide the Bangsamoro young people with highly
accessible, affordable, comprehensive, and rewarding medical education and
integrating in its system of operations all pertinent and relevant provision
under Republic Act No. 2382 otherwise known as the Medical Act of 1959 as
amended and CHED Memorandum Order No. 18, Series of 2016.

The Bangsamoro Medical School, hereinafter referred to as “School”, shall
provide advanced instruction and professional training in scientific,
technological, and special instructions in the field of medicine by offering
Doctor of Medicine program and other related academic programs. It shall
also undertake research and extension services and provide progressive
leadership in response to the needs of the Bangsamoro region for quality
health services.

The Bangsamoro Medical School shall serve primarily, but not limited to the
Bangsamoro young people from different territories of BARMM and may
extend its services as additional to other existing medical schools.

SECTION 5. Powers of the School. - The School shall have the general
powers of a corporation set forth in the Corporation Law. The administration
of the School and the exercise of its powers shall be vested exclusively in the
Board of Trustees and in the President in so far as authorized by the Board.

It shall have the power to acquire public lands for its expansion and /or
beneficial use; and it shall be authorized to undertake the construction of its
buildings and other permanent improvements and such sums as may be
necessary for the purpose shall be included in the Annual General
Appropriations Act for the School.

Section 6. Governing Body. - The governing body of the School shall be the
Board of Trustees, hereinafter referred to as the Board, which shall be
composed of the following:

(a) The Minister of the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education
(hereinafter referred to as MBHTE), chairman;
(b) The President of the School, vice chairman;

(c) The Minister of the Ministry of Health, member;

(d) Chairman of the Committee on Basic, Higher and Technical Education
in the Bangsamoro Parliament, member: Provided, that if he is the same
person with the Minister of the Basic Higher, and Technical Education, he
shall assume the position of chairman;

(e) Chairman of the Committee on Health in the Bangsamoro Parliament,
member;

(f} Minister of the Ministry of the Science and Technology, member;
(g) Director of the Cotabato Regional Medical Center (CRMC), member;
(h) President of the faculty assembly of the School, member;

(i) President of the student governing body representing the entire
population of students of the School; and

(j}) Two (2) prominent medical doctors who have distinguished themselves
in their respective fields, chosen from among a list of at least five (5) qualified
persons in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, as recommended by the
search committee constituted by the President in consultation with the
Minister of Basic, Higher and Technical Education based on the normal
standards and qualifications of the position, members.

The term of office of the president of faculty assembly and the president of the
student governing body shall be coterminous with their respective terms of
office.

For the purpose of the provisions of this section, the faculty and the students
shall organize themselves to form their respective assembly or governing body,
as the case may be.

The members of the Board referred to in paragraph (j) of this section shall
serve for a term of two years.

In case of vacancy in the office of the president, the officer-in-charge of the
University designated by the Board shall serve for the unexpired term only.

Section 7. The Board as the policy-making body. - The Board shall
promulgate and implement policies in accordance with the declared National
and Bangsamoro Government policies on education and other pertinent
provisions of the Constitution and the Bangsamoro Organic Law on
education, science and technology and health as well as the policies,
standards, and thrusts of a complete and integrated system of quality
education in the Bangsamoro provided under the Bangsamoro Education
Code of 2021.

Section 8. Powers of the Board of Trustees. - The Board shall have the
following specific powers and duties in addition to its general powers of
administration and the exercise of all the powers granted to the Board of
Directors of a corporation under Revised Corporation Code of the Philippines
and other related laws promulgated by the Bangsamoro Parliament:

(a) To enact rules and regulations not contrary to law, as may be
necessary to carry out the purposes and functions of the School;

(b} To receive and appropriate all sums as may be provided, for the
support of the School in the manner it may determine, in its discretion, to
carry out the purposes and functions of the School,

(c}) To receive in trust legacies, gifts and donations of real and personal
properties of all kinds, to administer and dispose the same when necessary
for the benefit of the School, subject to limitations, directions and instructions
of donors, if any. Such donations shall be exempt from all taxes that may be
imposed by the Bangsamoro government;

(d) To fix the tuition fees and other school fees and charges, such as,
but not limited to, matriculation fees, graduation fees and laboratory fees, as
it may deem proper and reasonable to impose after due consultations with
the involved sectors.

Such fees and charges, including government subsidies and other income
generated by the School, shall constitute special trust funds and shall be
deposited in any authorized government depository bank, and all interests
that shall accrue therefrom shall form part of the same fund for the use of the
School.

Any provision of existing laws, rules and regulations promulgated by the
Bangsamoro Parliament to the contrary notwithstanding, any income
generated by the School from tuition fees and other charges, as well as from
the operation of auxiliary services and land grants, shall be retained by the
School, and may be disbursed by the Board for instruction, research,
extension or other programs/projects of the School: Provided, That all
fiduciary fees shall be disbursed for the specific purposes for which they are
collected.

Section 9. Board Meetings. - The Board shall regularly convene at least once
every quarter. The Chairman of the Board may call a special meeting
whenever necessary: Provided, That members are notified in writing at least
three (3) days prior to said meeting.

A majority of all members holding office shall constitute a quorum for board
meetings: Provided, That the Minister of the MBHTE who is the chairman of
the Board or the president of the School is among those present in the
meeting. In the absence of the Minister of the MBHTE, a deputy minister of
the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education, duly designated by
him, shall represent him in the meeting with all the rights and responsibilities
of a regular member: Provided, however, That in the said meeting, the
president of the School as the vice chairman shall be the presiding officer:
Provided, further, That the preceding proviso notwithstanding, the Minister of
Basic, Higher and Technical Education is hereby authorized to designate a
deputy minister to be the regular Chair to the Board, in which case said
deputy minister of the MBHTE shall act as the presiding officer.

The members shall serve without compensation, but they shall be reimbursed
for necessary expenses incurred in their attendance of meetings of the Board
subject to pertinent existing laws and regulations or in connection with their
official business authorized by resolution of the Board.

Section. 10. The President. - The administration of the School shall be
vested in the president of the School who shall render full-time service. He
shall be appointed by the Board, upon recommendation of a duly constituted
search committee. He shall have a term of four (4) years and shall be eligible
for reappointment for another term.

In case of vacancy by reason of death, resignation, removal for cause or
incapacity of the president to perform the functions of his office, the Board
shall have the authority to designate an officer-in-charge pending the
appointment of a new president.

In case of vacancy in the office of the president as mentioned in the
immediately preceding paragraph, his successor shall hold office for the
unexpired term. If the successor shall serve for a period of more than two (2)
years, then such shall be considered as one full term for the successor.

The powers and duties of the president of the School, in addition to those
specifically provided in this Act, shall be those usually pertaining to the office
of the president of a similar School, and those delegated by the Board.

The salary of the president of the School shall be in accordance with the
Compensation and Position Classification System adopted by the Bangsamoro
Parliament and shall be comparable to that being received by other presidents
of similar educational institutions of like standing.

Section. 11. Board Secretary. - The Board shall appoint a secretary who
shall serve as such for both the Board and the School and shall keep all
records and proceedings of the Board. He shall communicate to each member
of the Board notice of meetings.

Section 12. School Treasurer. - The Bangsamoro Treasurer shall be the ex-
officio treasurer of the School.

Section. 13. Non-discrimination. - No religious opinion or affiliation shall
be a matter of inquiry in the appointment of faculty members of the School:
Provided, however, That no member of faculty shall teach against any
particular church or religious sect.

Section 14. Authority to acquire loans and initiate transfer. - Heads of
ministries and offices of the Bangsamoro Government are hereby authorized
to loan or transfer, upon request of the president of the School, such
apparatus, equipment or supplies as may be needed by the School, and to
detail employees for duty therein when, in the judgment of the head of the
ministry or office, such apparatus, equipment, supplies or services of such
employees can be spared without detriment to the public service. The
employees so detailed shall perform such duties as required of them by the
president of the School, and the time so employed shall be counted as part of
their official service.

Section 15. Scholarship Program. - The School shall provide a scholarship
program and other affirmative action programs to assist poor but deserving
students to qualify for admission to the School.

Section 16. Academic Freedom. - The School shall enjoy academic freedom.

Section 17. Progress Report. - On or before the fifteenth (15) of the second
month after the opening of the regular classes each year, the Board shall file
with the Bansamoro Parliament a detailed report on the progress, conditions

Section 18. Development Plan. - Within a period of one hundred twenty
(120) days after the approval of this Act, the School shall accomplish the
following:

faculty of the School shall be exempt from any civil service examination as a
requisite for appointment, without prejudice to the right of the Board to
impose other professional examination requirements intended to maintain
high academic standards.

Section 20. Bangsamoro Education Code. - The provisions of Bangsamoro
Autonomy Act No. 18, otherwise known as the "Bangsamoro Education Code
of 2021" shall form an integral part of this Act and together shall serve as the
governing charter of the School.

Section 21. Administration, Management and Organization. — The
Bangsamoro Medical School shall be under the administrative supervision of
the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) who are hereby authorized to
determine and approve the appropriate organizational structure for the
management and operations of the facility pursuant to the “POLICIES,
STANDARDS AND GUIDELINES FOR THE DOCTOR OF MEDICINE (M.D.)
PROGRAM” of CHED Memorandum Order No. 18, Series of 2016 and Medical
Act of 1959 as amended.

Section 23. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The Ministry on Basic,
