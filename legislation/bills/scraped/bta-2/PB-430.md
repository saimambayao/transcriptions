EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is continuously navigating the
complex journey from years of armed conflict towards lasting peace, stability, and development.

Nevertheless, in spite of considerable progress in governance, infrastructure, and social services in the
region, the remnants of war continue to be profoundly ingrained in the lives of numerous individuals, especially the
children who have lost one or both parents who -- whether they were Bangsamoro combatants or innocent
civilians -- backed the Bangsamoro quest for self-determination and perished as a result of the enduring conflict in
the region that has persisted for decades.

The orphaned children, who were sacrificially yet unintentionally left by their martyred mujahideen parents
in the name of “jihad fi sabilillah”, face unique and intricate challenges. Among the most urgent concerns are the
lack of parental guidance and support, rendering them susceptible to poverty, neglect, and exploitation; interrupted
education, as displacement and financial difficulties often compel them to cease their studies; and psychosocial
trauma, stemming from exposure to violence, loss, and instability.

Addressing the needs of these impoverished orphaned children is not merely an act of kindness — it
represents both a legal and moral obligation and a strategic investment in the peace and development of the
Bangsamoro. By providing these disadvantaged orphans the opportunity to development themselves
educationally and skillfully in income generation, we disrupt the cycle of poverty, avert the resurgence of violence,
and cultivate a generation that will uphold the achievements of the peace process.

From a policy perspective, the BARMM government is tasked with implementing programs that promote
inclusive development, social justice, and the realization of the rights of every Bangsamoro child. These objectives
are in accordance with the principles established in the Philippine Constitution and other pertinent legislation,
including the Bangsamoro Organic Law (Republic Act No. 11054) and the United Nations Convention on the
Rights of the Child.

In light of the foregoing, this legislative initiative has been proposed to create the Orphanage Village and

Learning Center (OVLC) in the Municipality of Nabalawag, in the Special Geographic Area (SGA) of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

a) Institutionalize both formal and informal education tailored to address the unique requirements of the orphan-

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. —This Act shall be referred to as the "Mujahideen Orphaned Development and
Empowerment (MODE) Act of 2026."

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. —The Bangsamoro Government hereby declares its policy to safeguard, assist,
and empower the disadvantaged children of fallen mujahideen, along with dependents of collateral family victims
of armed conflict within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and beyond the region’s core Bangsamoro
communities.

To fulfill these responsibilities, the government further declares its support policy aimed at providing these
orphans with a secure, nurturing, and culturally appropriate environment that fosters their education, skill
development, and moral guidance aligned with Islamic principles and Bangsamoro traditions.

SEC. 3. Operational Definition of Terms. —For the purpose of this Act:

a) Learning center - Denotes a specialized facility or location where orphaned dependents of fallen mujahideen
receive formal education aimed at their professional development, alongside non-formal training in practical skills
tailored to meet their immediate social and economic requirements.

b) Mujahideen parent - Denotes a Bangsamoro father or mother affliliated with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front
(MILF) or the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) who has participated in the armed struggle, either as a
combatant or as a supporter, and has given his or her life in the quest for self-determination for the Bangsamoro.
This also includes any other Bangsamoro civilian parent who is not a member of the MILF or MNLF but similarly
sympathizes with the Bangsamoro cause and has encountered comparable situations.

c) Orphaned children - Refers to underprivileged individuals from mujahideen parents, aged between 6 and 30
years, categorized as either minors or young adults.

i) Minor - Describes an orphaned child from a mujahideen family, regardless of gender, who is under 18 years
of age, as defined by Republic Act No. 6809.

ii) Young adult - Refers to any orphaned member of a mujahideen family, irrespective of gender, who is aged
between 18 and 30 years. This specifically modifies the definition of youth as stated in R.A. 8044, s. 1995, which
defines youth as being between 15 and 30 years of age, with the intention of clarifying the age overlaps between
this MODE Act and R.A. 6809.

SEC. 4. Establishment. — An Orphanage Village and Learning Center, abbreviated as OVLC, or simply known
as the Center, is hereby established in the Municipality of Nabalawag, Special Geographic Area (SGA),
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMI).

SEC. 5. Goal and Objectives. —The OVLC primarily aims to empower orphaned children of martyred
mujahideen from disadvantaged backgrounds, enabling them to recognize and utilize their full potential for their
overall development and for the benefit of the society they will be part of as they mature.

Specifically, the Center aims to:

a. Ensure that orphaned learners receive shelter, food, clothing, and healthcare assistance for a period
determined by their program agreement with the MOVTC management;

b. Deliver both formal and non-formal education, along with vocational and technical training, tailored to their
needs and interests;

c. Offer psychological assistance and counseling services to aid them in overcoming their traumatic experiences
and to facilitate their return to a normal life;

d. Support their reintegration into the community by instilling positivity and confidence in their ability to coexist;
and

e. Aid them in generating income by developing livelihood opportunities following the conclusion of their contract
with the Center management; and

e. Protect and promote Bangsamoro cultural heritage and Islamic values among them.

SEC. 6. Support Mechanisms. —In keeping with its goal and objectives, the Center is dedicated to provide
resources that include, but are not limited to:

a) Shelter for eligible orphaned children, along with facilities for their educational, vocational, and technical
training;

b) Opportunities for activities, particularly with young adult learners, designed to help them generate income for
themselves;

c) An Islamic unit that motivates them to move past their sorrowful experiences, confront current challenges,
develop resilience in navigating life, and engage in constructive competition within their local communities; and

d) A psychosocial unit dedicated to offering a variety of services intended to assist them in managing stress,
trauma, and challenges, as well as their community involvement and the development of relationships with others.

By leveraging the aforementioned resources, the Center offers services to eligible orphans, aimed at:

(a) Fostering cultural preservation and spiritual growth among them;

(b) Supporting their psychosocial well-being;

(c) Improving their livelihood skills and community-driven livelihood projects;

(d) Creating employment opportunities for and alongside them; and

(e) Guaranteeing their access to an educational program that encompasses both basic and secondary education.

All of these services shall be carried out through the joint efforts of the Ministry of Social Service and
Development (MSSD) and the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE), in partnership with
other pertinent ministries and offices of the Bangsamoro government, as well as accessible non-governmental
organizations that share similar responsibilities to those of the Center.

SEC. 7. Administration. —The Center shall operate under the joint oversight of the Ministry of Social Services
and Development (MSSD) and the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE), in collaboration
with other concerned Ministries and Offices, and selected nongovernment organizations and sectors in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

A Board of Trustees shail be formed to oversee policy direction, comprising representatives from:

a) The Ministry of Social Service and Development (MSSD) serving as the Chairperson;
b) The Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBHTE) acting as the Associate Chairperson; and

Other governmental and non-governmental entities as members, which include
a) The Ministry of Public Works (MPW),

b) The Ministry of Human Settlement and Development (MHSD),

c) The Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR),

d) The Ministry of Labor and Employment (MOLE),

e) The Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST),

f) Two (2) representatives from accredited civil society organizations,

g) Two (2) representative from the Ulama Council, and

h) Other individuals or groups as may be deemed necessary.

Operationally, the Center shall primarily be managed by an Executive Director alongside specialized personnel
and administrative staff, as outlined below:

a) Educators or Learning Facilitators - Tasked with facilitating teaching and learning processes, ensuring that
learners receive a high standard of education and training, in addition to other developmentally appropriate
learning opportunities;

b) Child Welfare Specialists - Deliver expertise on issues related to learners’ welfare, guaranteeing the safety and
well-being of the children;

Cc) Social Workers - Provide support with case management, address the emotional and social needs of the
learners, and advocate for their rights.

d) Administrative Staff - Manage daily operations, which include budgeting, logistics, and various administrative
duties; and

e) Leadership Team - Responsible for the overall management and strategic direction of the Center, ensuring
alignment with its mission and objectives.

SEC. 8. Eligibility. —The beneficiaries of the program shall include:
a) Indigent orphaned children aged between 6 and 30 years old

b) Individuals residing in any Bangsamoro community, irrespective of whether the community is located within the
BARMM or in other areas of Mindanao;

c) Orphans of a mujahideen parent, either the father or mother, who was either a combatant or a civilian
supporter, and who lost his or her life in armed conflict while pursuing the Bangsamoro quest for self-
determination; and

d) Any other individuals who satisfy the criteria in items a to c, and are acknowledged by the MSSD as requiring
residential care.

SEC. 9. Funding. —The funds required for the initial execution of this Act will be drawn from the current
appropriations of the MSSD and other collaborating Ministries and Offices of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region. Subsequently, any necessary amounts for its ongoing operation will be incorporated into the annual
budget of the Bangsamoro Government.

Through the Bangsamoro Government, the Center may also accept grants, donations, and endowments from
both local and international partners, in accordance with existing laws and auditing regulations.

SEC. 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). —Within ninety (90) days following the enactment of this
Act, the MSSD and MBHTE, in collaboration with relevant ministries and stakeholders, will issue the necessary
IRR to ensure the effective implementation of this Act.

remaining provisions will remain unaffected.

SEC. 12. Repealing Clause. —All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and regulations that are inconsistent with this Act
