EXPLANATORY NOTE

The proposed legislative measure aims to establish a schedule for the submission of the
proposed expenditure program for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(BARMM), not later than thirty (30) days from the fourth Monday of July of every year. The
submission of the proposed expenditure program is crucial for the effective governance and
fiscal planning of the region. By implementing a standardized schedule, this measure seeks to
enhance transparency, accountability, and efficiency in the budgetary processes of the
BARMM.

Since its inception, the BARMM has faced challenges in the timely submission of its
proposed expenditure program. The absence of a specific schedule has led to inconsistencies
in the budgetary process, hindering effective governance. To address these issues, this
legislative measure seeks to establish a specific schedule for the submission of the proposed
expenditure program.

The legislative measure aims to achieve the following objectives:

A. Standardize the schedule: The proposed legislation sets a fixed and consistent timetable
for the submission of the proposed expenditure program, ensuring that it is prepared
and submitted in a timely manner.

B. Enhance transparency and accountability: By instituting a schedule, the measure

governance, improves service delivery, and ultimately contributes to the overall development
and well-being of the people in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament
assembled:

Section 1. Title. This act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Budget Reform Act of
2023."

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. \t hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to formulate and implement a Regional Budget Expenditure Program that is
reflective of regional objectives, strategies, and plans.

Towards this end, it shall establish a regional budget expenditure program oriented
towards the achievement of explicit objectives and expected results to ensure that funds are
utilized and operations are conducted effectively, economically, and efficiently. It shall pursue
to enforce the power of the Parliament to ensure the responsibility of all ministries, bureaus,
offices, and agencies to propose, execute, and deliver committed results in the appropriations
in a timely, predictable, and economic manner.

Section 3. The Proposed Bangsamoro Expenditure Program. The Ministry of
Finance, Budget, and Management shall submit to the Bangsamoro Parliament, not later than
thirty (30) days from the fourth Monday of July of every year, the Proposed Bangsamoro
Expenditure Program (BEP), which shall be the basis of the General Appropriations Bill for
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The Minister of Finance, Budget, and Management may, from time to time, transmit to
the Bangsamoro Parliament such proposed supplemental or deficiency appropriations as are,
in their judgment, (a) necessary on account of laws enacted after the transmission of the
Proposed BEP, or (b) otherwise needed in the public interest.

Section 4. Form and Content. The Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management shall
determine the form and content of the Proposed BEP which shall include, but not limited to,
the following:

a. Minister’s Budget Message — provides a preview of the Proposed BEP, explaining
the principles, objectives, and policy framework adopted and the spending priorities
for the Fiscal Year;

b. Budget of Expenditures and Sources of Financing — contains the macroeconomic
parameters; dimensions of three-year (3-year) expenditure program, revenue,
financing, and outstanding debt; overview of the financial positions of BARMM
GOCCs, and public-private partnership projects; list of new multi-year projects,
including the project duration; the total project cost and contractual authorities
issued;

c. Bangsamoro Expenditure Program — includes strategic objectives, details of the
three-year (3-year) expenditure program, performance information, such as key
strategies, outputs, and outcomes for agencies in relation to their budgets, and
staffing summary.

Section 5. Implementing Rules and Regulations. Within ninety (90) days upon
approval of this Act, the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management, in coordination with
relevant agencies and stakeholders, shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the
effective implementation of the provisions of this Act that the Regional Budget Expenditure
Program reflects regional objectives, strategies, and plans.

Section 6. Separability Clause. If any section or part of this Act is held unconstitutional
or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and
effect.

Section 7. Repealing Clause. All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders,
or other rules and regulations contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are
hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

Section 8. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in a
newspaper of general circulation in the region.
