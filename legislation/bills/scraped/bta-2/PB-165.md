EXPLANATORY NOTE

In the Philippines, the power of the people to directly initiate legislation is
enshrined in Article VI, Sections 1 of the Philippine Constitution, which provides:

“the legislative power shall be vested in the Congress of the Philippines
which shall consist of a Senate and a House of Representatives, except to
the extent reserved to the people by the provision on initiative and
referendum. (emphasis supplied)”

as well as in Section 32, which states:

“The Congress shall, as early as possible, provide for a system of
initiative and referendum, and the exceptions therefrom, whereby the
people can directly propose or enact laws or approve or reject any
act or law or part thereof passed by the Congress or local legislative
body after the registration of a petition thereof signed by at least ten per
centum of the total number of registered voters, of which every legislative
district must be represented by at least three per centum of the registered

voters thereof. (emphasis supplied)”

This Constitutional provision is operationalized at the national level through
Republic Act No. 6735, also known as The Initiative and Referendum Act. Section 4
thereof states: “The power of initiative and referendum may be exercised by all
registered voters of the country, autonomous regions, provinces, cities,
municipalities and barangays. (emphasis supplied)”

Meanwhile, Republic Act No 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic
Law, mandates that the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region shall have a democratic
political system that allows its people to freely participate in the political processes

within its territorial jurisdiction.?

The Organic Law further reinforces this principle by expressly providing that
the Bangsamoro Parliament has the power, among others, to enact a law on
initiatives? as well as call for a referendum? on important issues affecting the

Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

To give life to the above-declared principles in the Constitution and in the
Organic Act of the region, it is imperative that the Parliament, within this transition
period, enact a law that will define and set the parameters for the exercise of the
power of initiative in the Bangsamoro, in so far as regional laws are concerned.
Such a law will operationalize and give flesh to the spirit of the law that sovereignty

resides in the people and that all power emanates from them. IT recognizes the |

importance of citizen’s direct participation in the democratic processes of our region

and provides a mechanism for the people to propose, amend or repeal existing laws.

This proposed bill reinforces and safeguards this power by ensuring that
direct initiatives that comply with the requirements shall be directly approved by
the people through a plebiscite, with no need for Parliamentary concurrence or
approval. However, should the Parliament recognize the wisdom and necessity of

said proposed measure, it likewise has the power to adopt in toto such proposition.

Further, this bill, much like its counterpart in national law (RA No. 6735)
distinguishes between direct initiative measures -- which is the heart and soul of
this proposed enactment -- and indirect initiative measures, herein called people-
initiated bills. The former only needs to be approved by electors in a plebiscite,
while the latter is a petition of the people to the members of the Parliament for the

enactment through the regular legislative process.

This proposed bill focuses itself only to provide for a system of initiative on
laws that may be passed by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro. It does not cover
initiatives on ordinances at the provincial, municipal or barangay level, which may
be the subject of another parliamentary act, or otherwise remain to be within the
coverage of RA No. 6735.

In view of some concerns regarding the cost of mounting a plebiscite, this bill
proposes to synchronize all plebiscites for initiative measures with regular elections
in the Bangsamoro. Thus the plebiscite questions on initiative, called the ballot
measure, is voted on using the same ballot as the one to be used for the election
of elected officials in the Parliament. Aside from minimizing costs, this will also
have the effect of normalizing plebiscites for important matters affecting our

constituency, and thus strengthening our democracy.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of this bill is earnestly sought. #

Section 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro

People’s Initiative Act.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. -— The following principles and policy

of the Bangsamoro are hereby affirmed and guaranteed:

(a) The Bangsamoro people have the right to chart their political future
through a democratic process that will allow genuine and meaningful self-

governance.

(b) The Bangsamoro people have the right to directly initiate and enact
legislation through the power of initiative, expressly guaranteed in Article VII,
Sections 1 and 32 of the Philippine Constitution. In this regard, the Bangsamoro
government shall take all necessary measures to facilitate the exercise of this

right;

(c) The voters in the Bangsamoro region continue to retain their power to
directly propose, enact, approve or reject, in whole or in part, laws, ordinances
or resolutions passed by any local legislative body other than the Bangsamoro
Parliament as provided for under the Constitution and Republic Act No. 6735

(also known as “The Initiative and Referendum Act.”);

(d) The voters of the Bangsamoro have the right to be informed of the
nature, proponents and funders of initiative petitions that may be circulated to

obtain their signatures;

(e) Signatories to initiative petitions have the right to have their personal
data protected from unauthorized use, or use beyond the purpose of supporting

a proposition to qualify for the ballot;

(f) Plebiscites on initiatives and referenda, shall be synchronized with

regular elections in the Bangsamoro.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. - For purposes of this Act, the following

terms shall mean:

(a) Initiative is the power as well as the process engaged by the people

of the Bangsamoro to directly enact regional legislation through a plebiscite;

(b) People-initiated Bill is a bill filed by the voters of the Bangsamoro

(c) Proposition, also referred to as the initiative measure, is the measure

(g) Referendum - is the power of the people to approve or reject statutes

Section 4. Coverage. - This Act shall only cover propositions to enact,
amend or repeal regional legislation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao. Initiatives for all other legislative acts shall be covered by

existing legislation on the matter.

Section 5. Prohibited Measures. — The following cannot be the subject
of a regional initiative:

(a) propositions embracing more than one subject;

(b) propositions that are not within the powers and competencies of the
Bangsamoro Government or are within the reserved powers of the national
government;

(c) notwithstanding the immediately preceding section, propositions that

refer to annual appropriations, revenues, or emergency measures.

Section 6. Who may exercise. — The power of initiative may be
exercised by registered voters of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim

Mindanao.

Section 7. Preliminary Procedure. — (a) prior to the filing of a petition
with the Bangsamoro Electoral Office, proponents of an initiative measure
(proposition), shall submit the proposed text of the proposition sought to be
enacted to the Bangsamoro Attorney General, with a request for the issuance of
a circulating title and summary and a unique numerical identifier for the
proposition sought to be enacted.

(b) No submissions and requests to the Bangsamoro Attorney General
relating to initiatives shall be entertained six (6) months prior to the conduct of

a regular election in the Bangsamoro region.

Section 8. Review Period. - (a) Upon receipt of the proposition, the
Bangsamoro Attorney General shall determine whether or not the proposition
falls among the measures that cannot be the subject of a regional initiative as

provided for by this Act.

(b) The Attorney General shall publish in its website the proposed text of
the initiative measure and facilitate a thirty (30)-day review process, during
which any member of the public may submit written public comments via the
same website. The Attorney General shall provide any written comments

received during this period to the proponents.

(c) The proponents may introduce amendments to the initial text within
the review period. Such amendments must be germane to the theme, purpose
or subject of the initiative measure as originally proposed. An amendment shall

not be accepted more than five days after the public review period is concluded.

(d) Within the same period, the Bangsamoro Attorney General shall
request the Ministry of Finance and Budget Management (MFBM) to prepare a
report of estimated fiscal impact of the proposed measure to be submitted to

the Attorney General.

Section 10. Initiatives Coordinator. — There shall be created within
the Bangsamoro Attorney General’s Office a unit to be known as the Initiatives
Coordination Office, to coordinate all initiative measures as provided for in this
Act, as well as to provide the necessary guidance and support to proponents in

the procedure provided for under this law.

Section 11. Circulating the Petition and Gathering Signatures. -
Proponents shall have one hundred and eighty (180) days from receipt of the
circulating title and summary, mentioned in Section 9 paragraph b hereof, to

collect the required number of signatures.

A proposition supported by the required number of signatures within the

Section 15. Date and Conduct of Plebiscite on Initiative. -— The
plebiscite for all propositions able to garner the required signatures within the
prescribed period shall be synchronized with the regular elections in the
Bangsamoro, which shall be under the supervision of the Commission on

Elections through the Bangsamoro Electoral Office.

Section 16. Effectivity of Laws Passed through Initiative. —
Propositions approved a majority of the votes cast in the plebiscite shall take
effect fifteen (15) days after certification by the Commission that such

proposition has obtained the required number of votes.

Section 17. Adoption by the Parliament. — If at any time before the
plebiscite is held, the Bangsamoro Parliament adopts jn toto the proposition
presented, the initiative shall be cancelled and the law shall be deemed as if it

has been enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament.

Section 18. Limitation upon the Bangsamoro Parliament. — Any
proposition approved through the system of initiative as herein provided shall
not be repealed, modified, or amended within six (6) months from the date
therefrom and may only be repealed by the Parliament within three years
thereafter. Laws seeking to repeal laws enacted through initiative shall be

subject to a referendum.

Section 19. Procedure in a People-initiated Bill. — (a) Registered
voters in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region may, through a petition, file a bill
with the Bangsamoro Parliament proposing the adoption, enactment, repeal, or ,

amendment of any regional law or resolution.
