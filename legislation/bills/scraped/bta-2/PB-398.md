EXPLANATORY NOTE

For the past three (3) consecutive years, the Bangsamoro Government has
granted an additional allowance to its officials and employees as assistance
for the observance of the Holy Month of Ramadhan.! This support has
provided financial relief to government workers amid inflationary pressures,
while also honoring the spiritual and cultural importance of Ramadhan to the
Bangsamoro people.

This bill seeks to institutionalize the grant of the Ramadhan Bonus which
shall be considered as one of the as an additional yearly benefit to all qualified
officials and employees of the Bangsamoro Government. Under this measure,

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament assembled:
ARTICLE I.
GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Ramadhan Bonus
Act of 2025.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy - It is hereby declared the policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to promote the general welfare of its officials and
employees by exercising the powers expressly granted to it, those necessarily
implied therefrom, and those essential to the attainment of efficient and
effective governance. In pursuance of this policy, the grant of a Ramadhan
Bonus as an annual benefit is hereby institutionalized as a recognition of their

ARTICLE II.
GRANT OF RAMADHAN BONUS

Section 3. Grant of Ramadhan Bonus. - A Ramadhan Bonus, as an
additional yearly benefit, shall be granted to all qualified officials and
employees of the Bangsamoro Government in an amount not less than Ten
Thousand Pesos (P10,000.00) for each qualified employee.

Section 4. Coverage. — This Act shall cover all officials and employees of the
ministries, offices, and agencies of the Bangsamoro Government, including
those engaged through Contract of Service (COS) or Job Order (JO), subject
to the conditions under the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).

Section 5. Exclusions. — The following personnel shall not be entitled to the
Ramadhan Bonus:

a. Those who are absent without official leave (AWOL);

b. Those engaged as consultants, experts, apprentices, student laborers,
or through institutional COS arrangements not considered part of the
organic manpower of the Bangsamoro Government.

ARTICLE III.
FUNDING SOURCE AND IMPLEMENTATION

Section 6. Funding Source and Release. — The amount necessary for the
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the appropriate funds
under the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro Government.

Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within thirty (30) days
from the effectivity of this Act, the Ministry of Finance, and Budget and
Management (MFBM), in consultation with concerned ministries, offices, and
agencies, shall promulgate the IRR for the effective implementation of this Act.

The MFBM may issue supplemental guidelines on an annual basis as may be
necessary to ensure the orderly release of the Ramadhan Bonus.

ARTICLE IV.
FINAL PROVISIONS

Section 9. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the provisions not affected thereby shall remain
in full force and effect.
