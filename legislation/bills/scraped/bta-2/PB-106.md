EXPLANATORY NOTE

Republic Act 11054, or the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao, mandates the Bangsamoro Government to adopt a policy on health that
provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents. It shall
establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people and
ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention
of excellent and affordable medical services.

In addition, Republic Act No. 11223, or the Universal Health Care Act of 2018 declares
that the State shall adopt a health care model that provides all Filipinos access to a
comprehensive set of quality and cost-effective, promotive, preventive, curative, rehabilitative,
and palliative health services without causing financial hardship, and prioritizes the needs of
the population who cannot afford such services.

Consistent with the above provisions, this proposed measure aims to upgrade the apex
or end-referral hospital to protect and promote the right to health of the citizens of the City of
Lamitan and its surrounding areas.

The Ediborah P. Yap Memorial Hospital, otherwise known as the Lamitan District
Hospital, was established in 1976. In 1984, when Lamitan was still a municipality in the

Province of Basilan, the hospital was renamed as the Lamitan District Hospital by virtue of
Batas Pambansa Blg. 713, and is operating as level-1 medical facility, with a 25-bed capacity.

As the only government hospital operating in Lamitan City, the catchment area of
Lamitan District Hospital covers the western half of the Province of Basilan, which has a
population of 100,150 individuals across eight municipalities.

Currently, the Lamitan District Hospital is staffed by four permanent physicians,
including the Chief of Hospital, and six job order doctors. The physicians are supported by
seven nurses, including the Chief Nurse, and twenty nursing attendants.

However, there is a clear disparity in nurse-to-patient ratio and a sizeable lack of
medical facility, equipment, and support staff to effectively cater and treat the hospital’s
admitted patients. In 2014, the hospital recorded a total admission of 2,127 patients, which is
62.46% higher than its rated capacity. In 2018, hospital admissions rose dramatically to 4,258,
or 172.1% and recorded constant increases up to 199.57% of its capacity, with a total admission
of 4,601 in 2019.

The rising trend of admitted patients from 2014 to the first quarter of 2019 was recorded
at four times the hospital’s rated capacity. Daily admissions alone placed the number of
patients from 60 to 70 patients, which significantly surpasses its holding and serving capacity.
Under these existing conditions, the hospital’s ability to provide much-needed medical care for
the people within its zone of operations is in jeopardy of failing from lack of capacity, resources
and manpower.

Hence, the present state of the hospital has affected its capability to effectively
and efficiently attend to the needs of the patients: It severely lacks the required medical
facilities, equipment and supplies, and medical and support staff to address the needs of its
patients. The foregoing circumstances surrounding the Lamitan District Hospital reveal
the urgent need to upgrade its capability and capacity for it to effectively serve the patients in
its catchment area at a level of efficiency that will save more lives.

With the intent of keeping the hospital prepared amidst increasing rates of
patient admissions and through the continuous increases in demand for its healthcare services,
we propose enhancing the capacity and capability of the Lamitan District Hospital, upgrading
it from its present 25 bed-capacity to a 100 bed-capacity. Hence, this proposal.

In view of the stated policy of the BARMM government to provide for comprehensive
and integrated health service delivery to its constituents, it is a matter of utmost consideration
and urgency for this government to enact a law that will address these health concerns and
challenges.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session:

Section 1. The Lamitan District Hospital is hereby converted into a Level II Hospital,
under the direct supervision and control of the Ministry of Health (MOH), Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The authorized bed capacity of the provincial hospital is hereby increased from twenty-
five (25) to one hundred (100) beds.

Section 2. The existing service facilities and other services of the hospital are hereby
upgraded to conform with the bed capacity increase as provided for in Section 1 hereof.

The existing medical personnel shall also be correspondingly increased. The Ministry
of Health, in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, and the Civil
Service Commission, shall determine the additional plantilla positions be created and filled up
in coordination with the management of the Lamitan District Hospital.

Section 3. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Government shall provide the necessary
funds to support the increase in bed capacity of the Lamitan District Hospital and the
corresponding upgrading of its facilities and services.

administrative regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby
