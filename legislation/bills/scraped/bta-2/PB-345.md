EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Organic Law provides for the establishment of a civil service system
in the Bangsamoro region, ensuring that recruitment, selection, and promotion within
the region’s government are based on merit, fitness, and equal opportunity. This
provision underlines the importance of improving employment conditions and career
prospects for public servants, which aligns with the objectives of the proposed bill to
provide civil service eligibility to long-serving employees in non-permanent positions.

This bill seeks to provide civil service eligibility to employees of the Bangsamoro
government who are currently appointed under non-permanent employment statuses
such as job order, contract of service, casual, or contractual positions. Specifically, the

bill grants civil service eligibility to those who have rendered a total of ten (10) years of
efficient service in any of these positions.

This bill recognizes the long-serving employees and their contributions to public service
by providing them with more job security and career advancement within the
government. Further, by granting eligibility, the bill seeks to strengthen the workforce in
the Bangsamoro region, helping the Bangsamoro government to retain skilled and
experienced personnel who are familiar with their roles and the specific needs of their
community.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

HAVE RENDERED A TOTAL OF TEN (10) YEARS OF EFFICIENT SERVICE, AND
FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Section 1. All government employees, who are holding career civil service positions
appointed under job order, contract of service, casual or contractual status who have
rendered at least a total of ten (10) years of continuous and efficient service are hereby
granted civil service eligibility. Thereafter, they are entitled to permanent appointments
to their present positions or to other positions to which the eligibility conferred herein
qualifies them as determined by the Civil Service Commission.

Section 2. The Bangsamoro Civil Service Commission shall promulgate the rules and
regulations to implement this Act consistent with merit and fitness principle within ninety
(90) days after its effectivity.
