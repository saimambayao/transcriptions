AN ACT CREATING THE TAWI-TAWI MARITIME SCHOOL AND FOR OTHER
PURPOSES

SECTION 1. Title. This Act shall be known as the “Tawi-Tawi Maritime School Act of 2025.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) to promote maritime education and training,
especially in the island provinces, to enhance economic opportunities, support the maritime
industry, and uplift the socio-economic condition of the people of Tawi-Tawi and the neighboring
island communities.

SECTION 3. Creation of the Tawi-Tawi Maritime School. There is hereby established the
Tawi-Tawi Maritime School, located in the Province of Tawi-Tawi, under the supervision and
control of the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) in coordination with
the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and relevant maritime
authorities.

SECTION 4. Objectives. The Tawi-Tawi Maritime School shall have the following objectives:

a. To provide quality maritime education and training to students from Tawi-Tawi and
neighboring areas.

b. To develop skilled maritime professionals to support the maritime industry in BARMM
and the Philippines.

c. To offer short-term and long-term courses focusing on navigation, seamanship, marine
engineering, fisheries, and other related fields.

d. To facilitate employment opportunities for graduates in the local and international maritime
industry.

SECTION 5. Courses and Programs. The school shall offer the following programs:

a. Bachelor of Science in Marine Transportation (BSMT)
b. Bachelor of Science in Marine Engineering (BSMarE)
c. Certificate and short courses in:
i. Basic Safety Training (BST)
il. Seafarers Rating Course
iii. Fishery Technology
iv. Vessel Operations and Maintenance
v. Other related maritime courses

SECTION 6. School Administration. The school shall be headed by a Director, appointed by the
MBHTE, who shall have the following duties:

1. Oversee the management and operation of the school.

2. Implement policies, standards, and guidelines set by the MBHTE and other regulatory
agencies.

3. Promote partnerships with local and international maritime institutions.

SECTION 7. Personnel and Staffing

A. Teaching Personnel
a. The Tawi-Tawi Maritime School shall employ qualified maritime instructors who
meet the standards set by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and the
Maritime Industry Authority (MARINA).
b. Instructors must possess:
i. A valid certificate of competency issued by MARINA.
ii. Relevant professional experience in maritime operations.
iii. A bachelor’s degree in maritime education or a related field.

B. Administrative and Support Personnel
a. The school shall hire non-teaching staff necessary for the efficient administration
and operation of the school, including:
i. Registrar
ii. Administrative Officer
iii. Finance Officer
iv. Librarian
v. | Maintenance Personnel
b. Personnel selection shall follow the Civil Service Commission (CSC) rules and
regulations.

C. Employment Terms
a. All personnel shall receive compensation, allowances, and benefits in accordance
with the applicable laws and regulations of the Bangsamoro Government.
b. Continuing professional development shall be provided to all personnel to enhance
skills and competencies.

SECTION 8. Funding and Appropriations. The amount necessary for the establishment and
operation of the Tawi-Tawi Maritime School shall be included in the annual budget of the
Bangsamoro Government under the MBHTE. Additional funding may be sourced from grants,
donations, and income generated by the school.

SECTION 9. Partnerships and Collaboration. The school shall establish linkages with maritime
industries, local government units, international maritime organizations, and educational
institutions for internship, employment, and curriculum enhancement.

SECTION 10. Monitoring and Evaluation. The MBHTE shall conduct periodic monitoring and
evaluation of the school’s programs and performance to ensure adherence to educational and
maritime standards.

SECTION 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). Within ninety (90) days from the
effectivity of this Act, the MBHTE, in coordination with the MAFAR and relevant stakeholders,
shall promulgate the necessary rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

SECTION 12. Separability Clause. If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or
invalid, the other provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 13. Repealing Clause. All laws, decrees, orders, and issuances inconsistent with this

Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 14. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the
Official Gazette of the Bangsamoro Government or in a newspaper of general circulation in
BARMM.
