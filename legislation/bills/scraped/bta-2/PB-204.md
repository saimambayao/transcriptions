AN ACT

PROVIDING FOR THE PROTECTION, PRESERVATION, CONSERVATION,
AND PROMOTION OF CULTURAL HERITAGE IN THE BANGSAMORO
AUTONOMOUS REGION, STRENGTHENING THE BANGSAMORO
COMMISSION FOR THE PRESERVATION OF CULTURAL HERITAGE,
ESTABLISHING THE BANGSAMORO CULTURAL HERITAGE PROGRAM,
AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in
session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the "Cultural Heritage
Act of the Bangsamoro".

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is
deeply committed to the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of its unique
and diverse cultural heritage, encompassing both tangible and intangible elements. As
such, the Bangsamoro Government shall embody and consolidate the historical narratives,

socio-cultural practices, and varied traditions of the Bangsamoro people that contribute to
the vibrant cultural mosaic of the region.

By fostering a profound cultural consciousness and appreciation, the Bangsamoro
government shall strengthen the Bangsamoro people's distinctive cultural identity and
promote intercultural dialogue, mutual respect, and peace within the region and beyond. It
shall underscore the active participation of communities in the stewardship of cultural
heritage sites and properties, ensuring their conservation and utilization for educational,
scientific, cultural, and touristic purposes.

In furtherance of this commitment, the Bangsamoro Government pledges to
allocate adequate resources, strategic planning, and continuous capacity-building for the
effective implementation of its cultural heritage programs. It shall ensure the
mainstreaming of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage, identity, and history in all levels of
governance and development, integrating cultural heritage protection, preservation,
conservation, and promotion principles and practices, including the use of innovative
technology and tools, into local and regional planning strategies, infrastructure
development, and environmental and other sustainability measures. Moreover, the
Bangsamoro Government shall collaborate with local, national, and international entities
to ensure the successful realization of this policy.

Furthermore, the Bangsamoro Government is committed to ensure that the
protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the region’s cultural heritage be
consistent with Islamic principles including stewardship (Khilafah), public interest
(Maslaha), integrity (Istigamah), respect for rights and property (Huguq), harmony with
the environment (Jawazun), collaboration and cooperation (Ta‘awun), avoiding waste
(Israf), justice (Adl), and peace and tolerance (Salam and Tasamuh). The Bangsamoro
Government recognizes the significance of culture in the Islamic perspective and shall
adhere to these principles in its efforts to safeguard and showcase the Bangsamoro cultural
heritage.

Finally, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes and prioritizes the need for a
holistic approach to cultural heritage, embracing diversity, supporting artistic creativity,
preserving cultural traditions, and promoting cultural expressions as fundamental
components of the Bangsamoro's cultural vitality, communal identity, and historical
continuity. It acknowledges the role of the Bangsamoro's cultural heritage as a bridge for
understanding and cooperation, fostering social cohesion, mutual respect, and peaceful co-
existence among its diverse inhabitants and with other communities and regions of the
country.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. The following terms, as used in this Act, shall
mean:

"Bangsamoro Culture" refers to the sum total of ways of living built up by a group of
human beings, which is transmitted from one generation to another, including but not
limited to their beliefs, practices, arts, laws, customs, traditions, and institutions.

"Bangsamoro Identity" refers to the distinct collective attributes, characteristics, values,
heritage, and cultural context that create a sense of belonging among the Bangsamoro
people, including but not limited to their languages, traditions, customs, beliefs, and
practices.

“Bangsamoro History” refers to the chronicle of significant events, experiences, and
narratives of the Bangsamoro people, including their origins, struggles, and aspirations.

"Cultural Heritage" refers to the totality of cultural property preserved and developed
through time and passed on to posterity.

"Tangible Cultural Heritage" refers to all natural and man-made, movable and immovable,
and tangible objects of religious, artistic, historical, and cultural value.

"Intangible Cultural Heritage" refers to practices, expressions, knowledge, skills — as well
as the instruments, objects, artifacts, and cultural spaces associated with them — that
communities, groups, and in some cases, individuals, recognize as part of their cultural
heritage.

"Cultural Properties and Sites" encompass both tangible and intangible properties and
physical locations that hold cultural, artistic, historical, architectural, or archaeological
significance to the Bangsamoro people. These may include tangible cultural properties
such as artifacts, structures, artworks, and monuments, as well as intangible cultural
heritage practices, expressions, knowledge, and skills associated with specific cultural
sites.

"Cultural Protection" pertains to the measures and actions taken to safeguard and ensure
the integrity, authenticity, and continued existence of cultural heritage, including the
establishment of legal frameworks, regulations, and enforcement mechanisms to prevent
unauthorized alteration, destruction, or illicit trade of cultural properties.

"Cultural Preservation" refers to activities and processes designed to safeguard cultural
property from deterioration, damage, or loss. It includes conservation efforts, restoration
work, maintenance, and preventive measures to ensure the physical and structural stability
of cultural heritage objects, sites, or practices.

"Cultural Conservation" involves the comprehensive and sustainable management and care
of cultural heritage, encompassing preservation, restoration, research, documentation,
interpretation, and community engagement. It aims to retain the cultural, artistic, historical,
and social significance of cultural properties while promoting their understanding and
appreciation.

"Cultural Promotion" relates to initiatives and activities undertaken to raise awareness,
enhance appreciation, and encourage active participation in cultural heritage. This may
include educational programs, cultural events, exhibitions, publications, digital platforms,

SECTION 4. Additional Powers and Functions of the BCPCH. - \n addition to
its existing mandates, powers, and functions under the Bangsamoro Administrative Act
(BAA) No. 13 otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Administrative Code, the BCPCH shall
have the following additional powers and functions:

Exercise powers to enforce laws related to cultural heritage protection, preservation,
conservation, and promotion in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and deputize such
powers to government agencies and local government units (LGUs) when necessary;

Exercise oversight powers over Local Culture and Arts Councils in local government units
(LGUs) to ensure the effective implementation of cultural heritage programs, projects, and
activities;

Require any person or entity intending to or is engaged in the sale, transfer, export, import,
or any other form of transaction involving cultural property of the Bangsamoro to obtain a
permit from the BCPCH, providing all necessary information and documentation as
required by the IRR and guidelines promulgated by the BCPCH to this effect;

Revoke or suspend such permit if the person or entity is found to be in violation of the
conditions for the issuance of the permit, any of the prohibited acts, engaged in illegal
activities, or failing to comply with any provisions of this Act, and/or other applicable laws
and regulations. Revocation or suspension shall be carried out in accordance with due
process, IRR and guidelines promulgated to this effect;

Charge and collect fees for permits issued and undertook in connection with the
implementation of this Act;

Mainstream Bangsamoro cultural heritage, identity, practices, and history in all levels of
governance and development, including the integration of these aspects in the policies,
programs, projects, and activities of ministries, agencies, and offices;

Recognize and support leaders, individuals, and organizations championing the
Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history, and provide incentives for their contributions to
the preservation and promotion of the region's cultural heritage;

Review and endorse for approval all proposed programs, projects, and activities of

ministries, agencies, and offices that may have an impact on the cultural heritage of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region;

Manage the Bangsamoro Endowment Fund for culture, identity, and history;

Coordinate with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education in providing
scholarships related to Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history;

SECTION 5. Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program. — The Bangsamoro
Cultural Heritage Program is hereby established, guided by the Bangsamoro Commission
for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) in partnership with relevant agencies
and local government units. The program incorporates and advances the following
components:

Culture-Sensitive Governance. — This component prioritizes the integration of cultural
considerations in governance and development. It includes the documentation, inventory,
and registration of cultural properties and sites, and the creation of cultural heritage zones
and cultural centers. It underscores the importance of active collaboration with government
agencies and units in decision-making processes to ensure the promotion and respect of the
Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

Cultural Development and Advocacy. — This component is dedicated to enhancing cultural
awareness and education, advocating for the significance Bangsamoro culture, identity,
and history, and supporting local artists, artisans, and cultural workers. It focuses on
fostering deeper societal integration of the Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

Cultural Preservation and Conservation. — This component is devoted to maintaining and
safeguarding the Bangsamoro cultural heritage. It entails efforts to preserve, conserve, and
restore cultural properties and sites, ensuring their longevity for future generations to
appreciate and learn from.

d) Bangsamoro History and Development. — This component illuminates the vibrant history

and evolution of the Bangsamoro people. It involves documenting and studying the
Bangsamoro history to deepen understanding and appreciation of the Bangsamoro culture
and identity.

e) Museum and Library Management and Development. — This component involves

establishing and effectively managing Bangsamoro museums and libraries as repositories
of cultural artifacts. These institutions serve as venues for cultural education and catalysts

Development of Cultural Enterprises and Tourism. — This component encourages and

among others. It shall include provision of assistance to cultural enterprises in their

Such Other Components or Programs Adopted by the BCPCH and Other Implementing

SECTION 6. Master Plan on the Protection, Preservation, Conservation, and
Promotion of the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage. — The BCPCH shall develop and adopt,
within six months after the effectivity of this law, a comprehensive Master Plan on the
Protection, Preservation, Conservation, and Promotion of the Bangsamoro Cultural
Heritage. This Master Plan shall serve as a strategic framework and roadmap for the
effective implementation of cultural heritage programs and initiatives in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region.

SECTION 7. Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Advocacy Network. — A
Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Advocacy Network shall be created to serve as a
collaborative platform connecting the BCPCH with various cultural communities,
academe, private sector, and other stakeholders. The network shall facilitate the exchange
of information, ideas, and resources, provide policy recommendations, review and endorse
projects, and assist in the implementation of the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program.

SECTION 8. Preservation and Restoration of Cultural Properties and Sites. -
The Bangsamoro Government through the BCPCH shall provide financial and technical
assistance to local government units, non-government organizations, and private
individuals for the preservation and restoration of cultural properties, sites, history, art
traditions, and rich cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their sultanates,
including the non-moro indigenous peoples of BARMM, in accordance with the guidelines
it shall adopt for the purpose.

SECTION 10. Cultural Heritage Zones. - The Bangsamoro Government, through
the BCPCH, shall identify, establish, and manage Cultural Heritage Zones, which shall be
areas of significant historical, cultural, or architectural value. The BCPCH shall develop
guidelines for the preservation, conservation, and development of these zones.

SECTION 11. Cultural Heritage Education and Awareness. - The Ministry of
Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE), in consultation with the BCPCH, shall
integrate cultural heritage education in the basic and tertiary education curricula. The
BCPCH shall coordinate with the MBHTE and other stakeholders to promote awareness
and appreciation of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage.

SECTION 12. Committee on Culture in the Bangsamoro Parliament. — There
shall be established a Committee on Culture in the Bangsamoro Parliament that shall have
jurisdiction in the protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the
Bangsamoro culture, identity, and history.

SECTION 13. Funding. - The Bangsamoro Government shall allocate necessary
funds for the implementation of this Act. The BCPCH shall submit an annual budget
proposal, including funding requirements for the Bangsamoro Cultural Heritage Program
to the Bangsamoro Parliament for approval.

SECTION 14. Bangsamoro Endowment Fund (Awgqaf) for Cultural Heritage. -
An Islamic endowment fund (Awgaf), referred to as the "Bangsamoro Endowment Fund
for Cultural Heritage", shall hereby be established. This fund shall be devoted to the
protection, preservation, conservation, and promotion of the Bangsamoro cultural heritage.
It shall be sourced from donations, grants, contributions, endowments, legacies or gifts
from various sources, both local and international, the annual budget allocation of the
Bangsamoro Government from revenues it generates as determined in the Bangsamoro
Revenue Code, and income from the development and utilization of cultural heritage
properties.

cultural property and archaeological and anthropological sites within the Bangsamoro
Region;

Modifies, alters, or destroys the original features of or undertakes construction or real estate
development in any regional shrine, monument, landmark and other historic edifices and

SECTION 16. Penal Provisions. — Any person who violates any provision of this
Act shall be punished by a fine of not less than two hundred thousand pesos (P200,000.00)
or by imprisonment for not less than ten (10) years, or both such fine and imprisonment at
the discretion of the court. In addition, the offender may be required to cover the cost of
repair, restoration, or restitution of the affected cultural property or heritage site.

In case the violation is committed by a corporation, partnership, or any other entity,
the head, directors, managers, or any officer who consented to or knowingly tolerated such
violation shall be held liable. The juridical entity itself shall also be subjected to applicable
fines, penalties, loss of licenses, or restrictions on operation as determined by law or
regulation. If the liable individuals are aliens, they shall, after serving their sentence, be
deported immediately without further proceedings. In case the individuals are public
officials or employees, they shall, in addition to the penalties prescribed herein, be deemed
automatically dismissed from the service and their benefits forfeited, after due proceedings.

The offender shall also be ordered to pay an indemnity for damages to the affected
cultural properties or heritage sites at an amount determined by the court.

In the event that the violation involves endangering or damaging cultural property
that has been declared as a national cultural treasure or important cultural property, the fine
and prison term shall be doubled. The offender shall likewise be required to undertake
restoration works and pay for any damage caused to the cultural property.

SECTION 17. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The BCPCH, in
consultation with relevant stakeholders, shall formulate and issue the necessary rules and
regulations for the effective implementation of this Act within ninety (90) days from its
effectivity.

SECTION 18. Transitory Provisions. - The BCPCH shall ensure the smooth
transition and integration of the additional mandates, powers, and functions provided in
this Act. Existing personnel, resources, and processes shall be reviewed and reorganized
as necessary to fulfill the expanded roles and responsibilities of the BCPCH.

SECTION 19. Separability Clause. - If any part or provision of this Act is held
invalid or unconstitutional, the other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain
in full force and effect.

SECTION 20. Repealing Clause. - All laws, decrees, orders, rules, and
regulations, or parts thereof, inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed
or modified accordingly.

Section 21. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of general or regional circulation
in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Approved.
