AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING THE CONDUCT OF MONTHLY VALUES
FORMATION SESSIONS IN THE HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT PLANS
OF ALL MINISTRIES, OFFICES, AND AGENCIES OF THE BANGSAMORO
GOVERNMENT, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Values
Formation Act of 2025.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is hereby declared the policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to promote moral governance and strengthen the ethical
foundations of public service. In accordance with Article IX, Section 3 of the
Bangsamoro Organic Law and Article 101 of the Bangsamoro Civil Service Code (BAA
No. 17), the Bangsamoro Government recognizes the importance of integrating
Islamic values and ethical guidance in the development of its workforce.

SECTION 3. Institutionalization of Monthly Values Formation Sessions. All
Ministries, Offices, and Agencies (MOAs) of the Bangsamoro Government shall
conduct Islamic Values Formation Sessions once every month, to be held for at least
two (2) hours, scheduled preferably on a preferred day and time by each MOA, without
prejudice to its operational exigencies.

Each MOA shall have the authority to select the specific day of the month that
best aligns with its internal workflow and service delivery requirements.

SECTION 4. Role of the Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ (BDI). The Bangsamoro
Darul-lfta’ shall:

a. Designate qualified personnel to serve as facilitators or resource persons
for the sessions across all MOAs;

b. Coordinate with the focal persons of each MOA to ensure orderly
scheduling, implementation, and documentation;

c. Develop and maintain a Values Formation Module, adaptable to the unique
needs of each MOA;

d. Ensure that all sessions are aligned with the principles of Shari'ah, moral
governance, ethical public service, and the spirit of the Bangsamoro Civil
Service Code.

SECTION 5. Survey and Needs Assessment. To contextualize topics and ensure
relevance to the mandates of the BARMM MOAs, the BDI shall administer a Values
Formation Needs Assessment Survey prior to the conduct of sessions. Each MOA
shall ensure that the survey is accomplished by not less than ten percent (10%) of its
workforce, or more when deemed necessary.

The results of the survey shall serve as the basis for setting the thematic focus
of each session.

SECTION 6. Integration into HRD Plans. The highest ranking Human
Resource Management Officer, or each MOA’s Personnel Development Committee,
shall ensure that the implementation of this Act is fully integrated into the Annual
Human Resource Development (HRD) Plans, and that attendance to such sessions is
included in the employees’ HRD records.

Attendance to at least one (1) islamic Values Formation Session annually shall
constitute compliance with the mandatory HRD intervention required under the
Bangsamoro Civil Service Code.

SECTION 7. Monitoring, Reporting, and Compliance. All MOAs shall submit a
Quarterly Report of Compliance to the BDI and BHRO, containing:

Date and topic of the session;
Name of the facilitator;

Number of attendees;

Challenges and recommendations.

The BDI shall consolidate all reports and submit an Annual Values Formation
Report to the Chief Minister.

SECTION 8. Separability Clause. If any provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. All laws, rules, executive issuances, and
memoranda inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 10. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette or any newspaper of general circulation in the

Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
