Explanatory Note

Section 2, Article IV of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law
(BOL), states that in exercising its right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region is free to pursue its political, economic, social, and cultural
development as provided in the Organic Law.

As mentioned in Section 2 (qq), Article V of the BOL, subject to Section 20,
Article X of the Constitution and the Organic Law, the Bangsamoro Government shall
exercise its authority over public works and infrastructure in the Bangsamoro region
without prejudice to the general supervision of the President of the Republic of the
Philippines.

Furthermore, Section 3 of the same article provides that the Bangsamoro
Government shall exercise the power expressly granted, those necessarily implied
therefrom, as well as powers necessary, appropriate, or incidental for its efficient and
effective governance and those which are essential to the promotion of general

welfare. Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure
and support, among other things, the preservation and enrichment ‘of culture,
promote health and safety, enhance the right of the people to a balanced ecology,
encourage and support the development of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and
technological capabilities, improve public morals, enhance economic prosperity and

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)
represents a significant milestone in the history of the Philippines, embodying the
aspirations for self-governance, peace, and prosperity for the Bangsamoro people.

Despite its rich natural resources, cultural heritage, and strategic location, the
Bangsamoro region has faced decades of underdevelopment and conflict, resulting in
limited access to basic services, inadequate infrastructure, and weak connectivity.
These challenges have hampered the region’s potential for economic growth, poverty
reduction, and improved quality of life for its residents. There is an urgent need to
develop infrastructure that supports transportation, communication, energy, water,
and sanitation systems to drive inclusive growth and bridge the development gap
between BARMM and the rest of the Philippines.

To realize this vision, it is essential to address the long-standing issues of
inadequate infrastructure, limited connectivity, and underdeveloped transportation
networks that have constrained the region’s socio-economic growth.

The promotion of infrastructure and connectivity development in the
Bangsamoro region, as proposed in this bill, aims to address these challenges by
establishing a robust infrastructure development strategy aligned with the
Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) and the priorities of the national government.
By investing in infrastructure and enhancing connectivity, this measure seeks to create
an enabling environment for economic activities, facilitate access to essential services,
and improve living conditions in BARMM.

The Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), as the interim government of the
Bangsamoro autonomous region, has the mandate to legislate measures that promote
the general welfare of its constituents.

The Bangsamoro Infrastructure and Connectivity Development Act of 2024 is
a transformative piece of legislation designed to address the pressing need for
infrastructure development and improved connectivity in BARMM. By providing a
legal framework for coordinated infrastructure planning, investment, and
implementation, this Act will contribute significantly to the region’s socio-economic
progress, peacebuilding efforts, and sustainable development.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this measure is earnestly sought.

THE BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO
(BARMM) TO SUPPORT ECONOMIC GROWTH, EMPLOYMENT, AND
SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT, APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND
FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao assembled:

Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Infrastructure
and Connectivity Development Act of 2024”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to promote inclusive economic growth, enhance connectivity, and build
sustainable infrastructure in the Bangsamoro region to ensure the well-being and
prosperity of the Bangsamoro people. This policy seeks to address the long-standing
challenges of inadequate infrastructure, limited connectivity, and underdeveloped
transportation networks.

Section 3. Objectives. - This Act aims to promote infrastructure and
connectivity development in the Bangsamoro region. Specifically, it has the following
objectives:

a) Develop and improve transportation networks, including roads, bridges,
ports, and airports, to facilitate the movement of goods, services, and people
within BARMM and to other regions;

b) Enhance digital connectivity by establishing reliable and affordable internet
access across BARMM;

c) Expand and upgrade energy infrastructure to provide a stable and
sustainable electricity supply;

d) Strengthen water supply, sanitation, and waste management infrastructure,
particularly in underserved communities;

e) Promote climate-resilient infrastructure development to ensure long-term
sustainability and disaster risk reduction; and

f) Encourage public-private partnerships (PPPs) to support infrastructure
projects and attract investments in BARMM.

Section 4. Creation of the Bangsamoro Infrastructure and Connectivity
Development Authority (BICDA). - An independent body known as the Bangsamoro
Infrastructure and Connectivity Development Authority (BICDA) is hereby created.
It shall be responsible for planning, coordinating, implementing, and monitoring
infrastructure projects in BARMM.

The BICDA shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of
representatives from the Bangsamoro Government, the private sector, and relevant
national government agencies. The Chief Minister shall appoint the members of the
Board, subject to the approval of the Bangsamoro Parliament.

Section 5. Powers and Functions. - To carry out the objectives mentioned in
Section 4 of this Act, the BICDA, is hereby vested with the following powers and
functions:

a) Develop and implement the Bangsamoro Infrastructure Development Plan
(BIDP);

b) Oversee the planning, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure
projects;

c) Enter into contracts, agreements, and partnerships for the implementation
of infrastructure projects;

d) Coordinate with relevant BARMM ministries, agencies, local government
units (LGUs), and national government agencies; and

e) Engage in resource mobilization activities, including securing funding from

development partners and private investors.

Section 6. Bangsamoro Infrastructure Development Plan (BIDP). - The BICDA
shall prepare and implement the Bangsamoro Infrastructure Development Plan
(BIDP) as the comprehensive infrastructure strategy for BARMM. The BIDP shall
define the priority infrastructure projects, timelines, funding sources, and responsible
agencies for implementation. It shall be developed in consultation with relevant
stakeholders, including relevant national agencies, BARMM ministries, LGUs, civil
society organizations, and the private sector.

The national agencies may implement programs and projects in the BARMM
based on the BIDP.

Section 7. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Infrastructure Fund (BIF). -
The Bangsamoro Infrastructure Fund (BIF) is hereby’ established to
finance infrastructure and connectivity projects in BARMM. The BIF shall be sourced
from the following:

1) Annual appropriations from the BARMM budget;

2) Grants, donations, and contributions from international and
local development partners;

3) Proceeds from PPP arrangements; and

4) Other lawful sources of funds.

The BICDA shail manage the BIF in accordance with the principles of
transparency, accountability, and efficiency.

Section 8. Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs). - The Bangsamoro
Government, through the BICDA, shall promote PPPs as a mechanism for financing,
developing, and maintaining infrastructure projects in BARMM. The BICDA shall
create a framework to facilitate the participation of private sector entities in
infrastructure development, providing incentives, tax benefits, and simplified
regulatory processes.

Section 9. Climate-Resilient and Sustainable Infrastructure Development. -
All infrastructure projects shall incorporate climate change adaptation, disaster risk
reduction, and sustainable development principles. The BICDA, in coordination with
the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources, and Energy (MENREB), shall ensure
that infrastructure projects comply with environmental laws and regulations.

Section 10. Priority Infrastructure Projects. - The following infrastructure
projects shall be prioritized under this Act:

a) Road Networks and Bridges - construction, upgrading, and maintenance
of roads, highways, and bridges to connect municipalities, provinces,
and economic zones in BARMM;

seaports and airports to enhance trade, tourism, and transportation;

c) Digital Connectivity - development of internet infrastructure, digital hubs,

online services;

e) Energy Infrastructure - promotion of renewable energy projects

Section 11. Monitoring and Evaluation, - The BICDA shall establish a
monitoring and evaluation (M&E) framework to track the progress, impact, and
sustainability of infrastructure projects. Regular progress reports shall be submitted
to the Bangsamoro Parliament, the Chief Minister, and relevant stakeholders to
ensure transparency and accountability.

Section 12. Reporting to the Bangsamoro Parliament. - The BICDA shall
submit an annual report to the Bangsamoro Parliament, outlining the progress
of infrastructure projects, financial status, challenges faced, and recommendations for
future actions.

Section 13. Role of Local Government Units (LGUs). - LGUs within BARMM
shall actively participate in the planning, implementation, and monitoring
of infrastructure projects in their respective areas, in coordination with the BICDA.

Section 14, Penalties for Non-Compliance. - Any person or entity found
violating the provisions of this Act, including engaging in corrupt practices or misuse
of funds, shall be subject to penalties as provided under existing laws and regulations.

Section 15. Mandatory Review on the Implementation of this Act. - Every five
(5) years after the effectivity of this Act, an independent review panel composed of
experts, to be appointed by the Chief Minister, shall review its implementation and
provide recommendations to the Chief Minister and Parliament.

Section 16. Appropriations. - The amount necessary for the initial
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the Contingent Fund. Thereafter,
funds for its continued implementation shall be included in the General
Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

Section 17. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The Ministry of Public
Works (MPW) shall issue the implementing rules and regulations within sixty (60)
days from the effectivity of this Act.

Section 18. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the
remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

Section 19. Repealing Clause. - All laws, orders, rules, and regulations
inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, modified, superseded, or amended
accordingly.

Section 20. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
