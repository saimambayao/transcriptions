EXPLANATORY NOTE
Taxes are the lifeblood of the government.

This doctrine cannot be truer in the case of the Bangsamoro Region
whose block grant is to cease in a few years’ time. Hence, it is deemed a
priority to encourage investments and improve the existing sources of income
in the region. One of the primary sources of the local government units’
income, which will eventually endow to the benefit of the Bangsamoro region,
is the collection on business permits and licenses. However, there are various
micro-scale and small-scale businesses within the Bangsamoro region that are
unregistered. These are loss of income and opportunity for the LGUs and of
the Bangsamoro region.

The Bangsamoro government understands that one of the reasons why
they are unregistered is the tedious process of acquiring one. This costs
business owners time and resources. Hence, to encourage the registration of
businesses and to enable the Bangsmoro government to regulate them, this
bill is being proposed to simplify and streamline the registration process of
business establishments. This is not only intended for unregistered businesses
but to also encourage business investments in the region.

The proposed Single Window Transaction - Modified Business
One-Stop Shop (SWiT-M-BOSS) is a laudable innovation of the City of
Muntinlupa and other cities. The new simplified process encouraged more
registrants and more investments in their city due to the ease of conducting
business. Under the old process, there are various offices involved such as
BPLO, Zoning, City Buidling, City Health, Fire Department, PESO, Treasury,
Mayor's Office, and others. In this proposed bill, the business owner or its
representative will only deal with one person who shall receive all necessary

SECTION 1. Title. This law shall be known as the “SWiT-M-BOSS of
BARMM.”

SEC. 2. Purpose. This law is being promulgated in pursuant to Republic Act
No. 9485, otherwise known as the “ Anti-Red Tape Act of 2007”, as amended,
which requires all government agencies or instrumentalities to streamline
procedures for the issuance of local business licenses, clearances, permits,
certifications or authorizations.

The law seeks not only to ease the burden of collecting local revenues through
business registration but also to facilitate easy access to essential public
services.

SEC. 3. Coverage. This bill governs all cities and municipalities within the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. This bill shall

SEC. 6. Compliance Period. All cities and municipalities in BARMM shall be
given a period of three (3) months to reorganize their respective BPLO and to
make necessary arrangements in their BPLS, including the organization of
awareness efforts to encourage registration among business enterprises
within their jurisdictions.

SEC. 7. Enacting Ordinances/Resolutions. If deemed necessary by the local
government units concerned, an ordinance or a resolution adopting this law
and detailing the manner by which to implement this law may be passed
within the aforecited compliance period.

SEC. 8. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in a newspaper of general circulation.
