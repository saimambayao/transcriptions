EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: “The strong (active) believer
is better and more beloved to Allah than the weak believer, while there is good in both.”
The Prophet himself participated in swimming, archery, running, and horseback riding
and was said to have encouraged parents to involve their children in these sports.

In 1928, the ninth Olympiad was held in Amsterdam, Netherlands, where the first
Muslim Olympian by the name of Tuburan Kontong Tamse, a 17-year-old Sulu native,
represented and brought honor and glory not only to the Bangsamoro people but to every
tongue chanting and praising the Philippine flag, in the swimming competition. This feat
of a young Tausog is a testament to how sports-loving the Bangsamoro people were.

With the development in sports both locally and internationally, many of our
Bangsamoro athletes are inspired to follow in the footsteps of Mr. Tamse. Many have

During the recent conduct of the Southeast Asian Games at Phnom Penh,

and Mr. Denmark Abdurasad, also grabbed the bronze medal in the Men’s Tanding class
D (60-65 kilograms.

However, before any medal or trophies these athletes receive in every sport they
participate in, they have undergone intense, nerve-wracking, grueling, and thorough
training for years. The dedication and determination they pour in during their training
to give us honor and glory are certainly astounding and extraordinary. Perhaps there are
more ways to acknowledge and reward their tears and sweat than to give them mere
applause and medals. It is important to provide our athletes proper recognition through
a system of rewards for their outstanding achievements by including the giving of
financial assistance to them, which is the subject matter of this bill.

According to research, Money has frequently been used as an extrinsic motivator
since it is assumed that humans are willing to invest more effort for financial reward. The
level and amount of effort in a person will either increase or decrease on the basis of the
quality of incentives offered to the person.

Hence, to better provide due and proper recognition to our Bangsamoro athletes,
it is earnestly sought that this bill granting cash incentives be passed and approved.

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Athletes
Incentives Act of 2023”.

SECTION 2. Statement of Policy. — It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to promote physical education and encourage sports programs, league
competitions, and amateur sports, including training for regional, national, and
international competitions, to foster self-discipline, teamwork, and excellence for the
development of a healthy and alert citizenry. It shall develop innovative and productive
individuals, promote sportsmanship, and cooperation, encourage and support
indigenous games, martial arts, and amateur sports.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall ensure to look after the welfare of
the Bangsamoro athietes and coaches for the Bangsamoro Region by providing benefits

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. — For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall
be defined as follows:

a. Athletes with disabilities shall refer to persons with disabilities, as defined under
Republic Act No. 7277, otherwise known as the “Magna Carta for Disabled
Persons,” as amended, who are Filipino citizens and a resident of Bangsamoro
Region, recognized and accredited by the Bangsamoro Sports Commission and the
Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (TESDA) for those who have
represented the region in a national athletic sports competition, and by the
Philippine Sports Commission (PSC) and the National Paralympic Committee of
the Philippines (NPC PHIL) for those who have participated in an international
sports competitions, and;

b. International sports competitions shall refer to international sports competitions
under the following categories:

1. Regular major competitions such as the Summer Olympic Games,
Winter Olympic Games, Asian Games, Asian Winter Games, Southeast
Asian (SEA) Games, Youth Olympic Games, Paralympic Games, Asian
Park Games, Asian Indoor and Martial Arts Games, Asian Beach Games
and Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) Para Games;

2. World-level championships are held at least every two (2) years, with at
least forty-five (45) countries participating by sport;

3. Asian-level competitions are held at least every two (2) years, with at
least twenty-five (25) countries participating by sport; and

4. Qualifying competitions for World-level games with at least ten (10)
countries participating.

Competitions granting prize money shall not be included.

c. National Sports Competition shall refer to the National Sports Competition under
the following:

1. The Palarong Pambansa, an annual interregional competition staged by
the Department of Education among the 17 different regions in the

country;

SECTION 4. Cash Incentives for Athletes. — In addition to the incentives and benefits
that Bangsamoro athletes will receive from the national government in an international
sports competition, the Bangsamoro athletes and other athletes who are not officially or
currently members of the Bangsamoro training pool but who have otherwise represented
the region in national and international sports competitions, who won gold, silver and
bronze medals in national and international sports competitions shall be entitled to cash
incentives in the following amounts:

Winter Games, Asian Indoor and Martial Arts Games, Asian Para
Games, and World-level competitions held at least every two years
with at least forty-five (45) countries participating;

b. Team Events

For competitions with less than five (5) participants per team, the team shall receive the
same cash incentives for individual medal winners as herein provided, to be divided
equally among the team members present during the competition.

For competitions with five (5) or more participants per team, each team member shall
receive twenty-five percent (25%) of the cash incentives for individual medal winners as
herein provided.

Provided, That for demonstration and exhibition sports events in the Olympics, the
winning athlete shall receive twenty-five percent (25% ) of the cash incentives for Olympic
medalists as herein provided.

Provided, further, That Bangsamoro athletes and other athletes who surpass Bangsamoro
or Philippine records or ranking in any measurable national and international sports
competition shall be given cash incentives, the amount of which may be determined by
the BSC.

SECTION 5. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). — The BSC, in consultation
with the Ministry of Health (MOH), the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical
Education (MBHTE), the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM), among
others, and other concerned agencies shall promulgate and issue the implementing rules
and regulations within sixty (60) days upon approval of this Act: Provided, That the failure
of the BSC to promulgate the rules and regulations shall not prevent the implementation
of this Act upon its effectivity.

SECTION 6. Monitoring and Reportorial Requirements. - The BSC and MBHTE shall
submit a report on the benefits, privileges, and incentives granted under this Act to
Bangsamoro athletes to the MFBM for the purpose of monitoring the implementation of

SECTION 8. Separability Clause. - If any portion or provision of this Act is subsequently

SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, decrees, executive orders,
