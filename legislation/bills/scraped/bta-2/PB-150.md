Explanatory Note

Article II, Section 4 of the 1987 Philippine Constitution specified that it is the prime
duty of the Government to serve and protect the people'. In congruence with this, the
other principles and state policies stated in the said Constitution are geared toward the
promotion of general welfare, significantly, the protection of the people, the promotion
of the people's right to health and the right to a clean and healthy environment. Therefore,
it is the State’s duty and obligation to ensure the health, welfare, and safety of the present
and future generation of the Filipinos.

response to a Business World query on May 2019 that the Bangsamoro’s “potential for
food production is immense. In five years the Bangsamoro area will definitely be a major

The Bureau of Animal Industry and the Bureau of Plant Industry was created in
1930 by virtue of Republic Act No. 3639. Since its creation, the Bureaus have gone
through several organizational changes mandated by various Republic Acts, Presidential
Decrees, and Executive Orders. Both are attached agencies under the purview of the
Department of Agriculture, where both play a significant role in the over-all protection
and development of the Plant and Animal Industries in the country.

The Bangsamoro, a newly created autonomous region in the part of Mindanao, is
heavily dependent on agriculture as one of its sources of livelihood. The region covers
11,608 km? or 4.0 percent of the country’s land area, with a population of 4.12 million‘.
The agriculture industry in the region should be protected to biosecurity threats like
attacks from invasive species, pests, and diseases that can cause damage to the
agricultural production. The region is also not spared from other possible threats or risks
to its animals, plants, human population and its environment.

The Plant and Animal Industries may be affected with the outbreaks of
communicable diseases coming from other parts of the world. Such vulnerabilities may
threaten economic losses with far reaching effects. To minimize the effects of these threats
and prevent the potential downfall of these plant and animal industries, the Bangsamoro
Office of Plant and Animal Industries should be established in the region.

To ensure efficiency in planning and delivery of the programs for the industries in
the region, there is a pressing need to establish an office that concentrates in the
development of the industries in the region. The proposed Bangsamoro Plant and Animal
Industries Office shall provide the overall policy and program directions of the industries
in the region and shall coordinate the activities of government bodies to ensure the
success of these industries. It shall also be responsible in promoting and maintaining the
development of the said industries in the region.

The creation of the Plant and Animal Industries Office in the Bangsamoro Region

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao assembled:

CHAPTER I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article I
Declaration of Principles and Policies
Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Plant and Animal
Industries Act of 2023”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It shall be the declared policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced
and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government recognizes its duty and obligation
to protect and promote the well-being of the people, plant and animal population,
environment and its respective ecosystems, in order to meet the requirement of the
growing populace and guarantee the nation’s survival.

Article II
Creation of Bureau of Plant and Animal Industries

Section 3. Creation. - There is hereby created the Bangsamoro Plant and Animal
Industries Office, herein referred to as “the Office”. The Office shall act as the primary
Bangsamoro Government entity responsible for the comprehensive and integrated
development of the plant and animal industries in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

For purposes of coordinating plans and policies, the Bangsamoro Office on Plant

and Animal Industries shall be an attached agency of the Ministry of Agriculture,
Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) - BARMM.

CHAPTER 2
OBJECTIVES AND FUNCTIONS

Section 4. Purposes and Objectives. - This Act generally aims to establish a
oro Plant and Animal Industries Office in the Bangsamoro Region. Specifically,
it has the following purposes and objectives:

a) To promote the accelerated development of the plant and animal industries in
all of their aspects;

b) To provide general directions for the continuing development of the industries;

c) To make the industries productive and profitable under sustainable
environment through sound policies, programs, research, and services on
production; and

d) To control environmental pollution by minimizing the use of pesticides,
thereby promoting a healthy environment.

Section 5. Powers and Functions. - To carry out the purposes and objectives
mentioned in the preceding section, the Office, is hereby vested with the following
powers and functions:

a) Formulate and adopt general development plans and programs for plant and
animal industries in the region;

CHAPTER III
IMPLEMENTING STRUCTURE AND MECHANISM

Section 6. Chief Officials. - The Office shall be headed by an Executive Director to
be assisted by a Deputy Executive Director. The Office shall be composed of the following
divisions:

a) Research, Planning, and Development Division;
b) Operations’ Division;

c) Monitoring and Evaluation Division; and

d) Finance and Administrative Support Division.

Section 7. Office of the Executive Director. - The Executive Director shall have the
following duties and responsibilities:

a) Lead the planning and formulation of policy measures, as well as the benefit,
protection, and welfare of the plant and animal industries in the region;

b) Provide direction and supervision over the full operation of the Office;

c) Provide policy guidelines and standards for the effective and efficient
operation and function of the Office in accordance with its mandate;

d) Ensure that the mandate and functions of the Office and the provisions of this
Act are faithfully executed and performed;

CHAPTER IV
FINAL PROVISIONS

Section 10. Rules and Regulations. - The Office shall promulgate the required
rules and regulations for the effective implementation of this Act and revise or amend
the same, as may be necessary.

Section 11. Periodic Reports. - The Office shall submit an annual report to the
Chief Minister indicating, among others, the list of programs being implemented, the
stage of implementation, and the financial position of the Office. It shall likewise submit
such periodic or other reports as may be required from time to time.

Section 12. Mandatory Review on the Implementation of this Act. - Every ten
(10) years after the effectivity of this Act, an independent review panel composed of
experts, to be appointed by the Chief Minister, shall review the policies and projects of
the Office and shall make recommendations, based on its findings, to the Chief Minister
and to the Parliament.

Section 13. Implementation. - Within sixty (60) days from the promulgation of this
Act, the necessary rules and regulations for the proper implementation of its provision
shall be formulated by the Ministry of Fisheries, Agrarian Reform (MAF AR).

Section 14. Appropriations. - The funds necessary for the establishment of the
Bangsamoro Office on Plant and Animal Industries shall be included in the annual
appropriations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region and submitted to the Parliament
for its approval.

Section 15. Separability Clause. - If for any cause, any part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the rest
of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

Section 16. Repealing Clause. - All laws, orders, rules, and regulations which are
inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified, superseded,
or amended accordingly.

Section 17. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following

its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
