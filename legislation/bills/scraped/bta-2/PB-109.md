EXPLANATORY NOTE

Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government is mandated by law to
ensure and support, among other things, the promotion of the health and safety of its
constituents. As provided in Article IX, Section 22 of Republic Act No. 11054, or the Organic
Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

“The Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy of health that provides for a
comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents. It shall
establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its
people and ensure that the individual’s basic right to life shall be attainable through
the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.”

Lantawan is a coastal and a third-class municipality in the island province of Basilan,
created and politically subdivided into 25 barangays. According to the Population Census of
2020, it has a total population of 31,040 which represents 7.28% of the total population of
Basilan Province across an area of 405.048241 square kilometers, which constitutes 11.73%
of Basilan' s total area.

There is only the Matarling Rural Health Unit in Lantawan that provides basic health
services to the entire municipality, and it is primarily a maternity clinic. For emergency and
other health services, the patients need to be transported to the nearest hospital, or the nearest
health center in neighboring municipalities. This inconveniences Lantawan’s constituents and,

in some cases, the cost paid is in the lives of patients who expire without receiving the urgent
medical care they need.

Under these conditions, the State is bound by law to support the highest attainable

standard of health to uphold the fundamental right of every human being, as is the state’s
obligation.

Hence, to give substance and spirit to the provision of the Bangsamoro Organic Law,
there is an urgent need for the construction and establishment of the Lantawan General
Hospital, a measure that provides the municipality’s constituents’ societal rights, and ensure
that their individual basic rights to life are upheld.

The current principle that has been recently adopted in the 2030 Agenda for
Sustainable Development and Universal Health Coverage is a rights-based approach to health

that requires health policy and programs to prioritize the needs of those furthest behind the
first.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session:

Section 1. There is hereby established a Level I general hospital with twenty-five (25)
bed capacity in the Municipality of Lantawa, Province of Basilan, under the direct
administrative and technical supervision of the Basilan Integrated Provincial Health Office
(IPHO).

Section 2. The BARMM Ministry of Health (MOH) shall formulate the necessary
guidelines for its operations as a level I hospital and ensure funding for the construction of the
hospital building and other complementary infrastructure, facilities, and medical equipment, as
well as the costs for the necessary maintenance and personnel services.

Section 3. The Chief of Hospital of Lantawan General Hospital, in consultation and
coordination with the Basilan IPHO, shall submit to the MOH an annual development plan to
reflect the developmental goals of the hospital every year. The medical personnel, capital
outlays and budgetary needs for the operation of Lantawan General Hospital shall be based on
the annual hospital development plan.

Any future increase in bed capacity and upgrading of health care services and the
facility shall be consistent with the annual hospital development plan.
