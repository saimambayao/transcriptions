AN ACT PROVIDING FOR BEREAVEMENT LEAVE OF FIVE (5) DAYS WITH
HALF-PAY TO REGULAR AND CONTRACTUAL EMPLOYEES IN PUBLIC
AND PRIVATE SECTOR

EXPLANATORY NOTE

While death of a loved one and grief is an inevitable experience, a bereavement leave to allow
an employee or a worker mourn their loss is a leave that is overlooked and unrecognized by
employers, both in the public and private sector.

This puts an employee in a prejudiced condition as they have to make use and exhaust all
their leaves for the year inorder to attend to their grief and to the rites that are associated with
the funeral of a loved one. In cases where there are no leaves available to an
employee/ worker, they are constrained to absent without leave and risk their employment
records or to miss their attendance in the funeral of immediate family member. This is a
situation that is not fair and highly unfavorable to the employee or a worker and a
predicament not sanctioned under our constitutional laws on family being the foundation of
nation (Section 1, Article XV, 1987 Constitution) and on labor as primary social economic force
(Section 18, Article II, 1987 Constitution).

Further, the grant of bereavement leave is much more essential and vital in the context of
Bangsamoro people where customary services are performed as part of their customary
culture and practice. It is an integral part of the Moro culture to immediately arrange and
conduct the burial of their loved and to perform Janazah. Thereafter, it is customary to gather
for an average of four (4) days after the death of a family member or a kin. The gathering
allows the family to mourn and to be consoled by the visiting relatives and friends.

Thus, more than the fair consideration that is afforded by this proposal to the labor, this also
seeks to put value into the belief, customs, and practices of the Bangsamoro people by
allowing them to grief for their loved ones and to be present during the performance of our
customary services.

This bill is being proposed to provide remedy for the gap under our labor laws in relation to
the lack of recognition of an employee/worker’s bereavement and the preservation of our
customs as people. Hence, its passage is earnestly sought.

AN ACT PROVIDING FOR BEREAVEMENT LEAVE OF FIVE (5) DAYS WITH
HALF-PAY TO REGULAR AND CONTRACTUAL EMPLOYEES IN PUBLIC
AND PRIVATE SECTOR

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao Assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Bereavement
Leave Act”.

SEC. 2. Bereavement Leave. An employee, whether regular, casual, or contractual, in
a public office or a private establishment, shall be granted a bereavement leave of five
(5) days with half pay following the death of his or her immediate family member.

SEC. 3. Immediate Family, defined. An immediate family member of an employee
shall be his or her:

a) biological/adopted parent/s;

b) biological/ adopted child/ren;

c) sibling/s.

SEC. 4. Period of Availment. The employee shall avail of the bereavement leave
within two (2) days from the death of the immediate family member. The failure to
immediately avail of the bereavement leave within the said period shall be considered
as a waiver thereof.

SEC. 5. Non-Commutation. The bereavement leave not availed of by an
employee/worker shall neither be regarded as leave credits nor be commuted into
corresponding monetary value.

SEC. 6. Non-Diminution of Benefits. The benefit under this law shall be without
prejudice to existing bereavement leave or similar benefits under a company policy or
a company practice. In cases where a bereavement leave or a similar benefit granted
by the public office or private establishment through a company policy, a CBA, law,
or a similar document is greater or more beneficial to the employee, the latter shall
take precedence over this law.

SEC. 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. The Ministry of Labor and
Employment shall coordinate with the Civil Service Commission for BARMM, the
private sector, and the public sector, for the crafting and issuance of the necessary
rules and regulations within thirty (30) days from the effectivity of this act.

SEC. 8. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in
the Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation within BARMM.
