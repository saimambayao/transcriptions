32 The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is the model definition of a melting pot of

33 cultures that encompasses the Philippines. In it, several unique Indigenous Communities
34 and Indigenous people exist, each with their unique identity, religion, and ethnicity, all

38 and needs which are best served by local heads within their communities. Currently,
39 there exists an opportunity for the Bangsamoro Government to better serve these
40 Indigenous Communities in order to ensure that they are involved in the decision-
41 making process, which would greatly impact their communities.

AN ACT
ESTABLISHING INDIGENOUS PEOPLE’S COMMUNITY CENTERS IN THE
BANGSAMORO REGION AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Title. This Act shall be known as the “Indigenous People's Community
Centers (IPCC) Act of 2023”.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - Pursuant to Section 10, Article 4 of the Bangsamoro
Organic Law, it is the policy of the Bangsamoro government that Indigenous peoples
shall have the freedom to retain their distinct indigenous and ethnic identity in addition
to their Bangsamoro political identity. And as such, the Bangsamoro Government shall:

1. Recognize and promote the rights of Indigenous Cultural Communities
(ICCs)/Indigenous Peoples (IPs) within the framework of regional unity and
development;

2. Protect the rights and preserve the unique traditions, culture, and identity of the
ICCs/IPs in the Bangsamoro to ensure their economic, social, and cultural well-

3. Guarantee the freedom of ICCs/IPs from non-discrimination and equal protection

4. Encourage ICCs/IPs’ participation in decision-making matters affecting their
welfare and to freely express their grievances to the regional government; and

5. Encourage participation in education and health services, as well as other services
of ICCs/IPs, in order to render such services more responsive to the needs and
desires of these communities.

SECTION 3. Establishment/Construction of Community Centers. -There shall be an
established indigenous people’s community center (IPCC) in selected communities
within the Bangsamoro Region, to be determined by the Ministry of Indigenous People’s
Affairs (MIPA), which shall serve as the multipurpose center for the Indigenous Peoples
located therein.

As far as practicable, there shall be at least one IPCC per province in the Bangsamoro
Region.

SECTION 4. IP Community Center Requirements. - The following shall be the minimum
requirements for every IPCC that will be established under this Act:

a) Location/ Designation - each location shall be determined by the MIPA in close
coordination with the LGUs concerned and in consultation with the IP
beneficiaries but shall, in all cases, be centrally located in the IP community.

b) Design and materials - The IPCC’s design shall reflect the unique IP identity of the
beneficiary IP community and utilize at least twenty percent (20%) of indigenous
or local materials.

c) Structural/ Building Capacity - The IP Community Centers shall be constructed
and designed to be calamity-resilient, made of sturdy materials, and shall be built
in accordance with the specifications of Republic Act No. 6541, other known as the
“National Building Code of the Philippines.”

The Ministry of Public Works (MPW) shall be in charge of the construction of IP
community centers and formulating the building specifications therefore based on
standards, issuances, and other guidelines set by the MPW. The design of which shall be
reflective of the identity of the IP for whom the center is to be constructed. It shall also

SECTION 5. Operations and Management of the IPCC. - The municipal/city
government, through the organized indigenous people’s council/ community concerned,
shall be primarily responsible for the operation, supervision, and management of the
IPCC established under this Act.

The mandatory audit of infrastructures used as IPCC shall be conducted by the
Municipal/City Engineer and/or the building official at least once every year and
immediately after the occurrence of a hazard or disaster event.

SECTION 6. Role of the MIPA. - The MIPA shall be the primary lead agency which shall
oversee the implementation of this act.

SECTION 7. Appropriations. - The amount necessary to fund the construction and
maintenance of IPCCs under this Act shall be included in the annual program of MIPA
for the next fiscal year. Every year thereafter, the amounts necessary for the construction
and maintenance of IPCC shall be included in the list of programs as part of the annual
budget in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

The appropriations necessary for the operation and management of the Indigenous
Community centers shall be charged against the funds of the Municipal/City
Government concerned.

effectivity of this Act, the MIPA and the MPW, in coordination with the appropriate
