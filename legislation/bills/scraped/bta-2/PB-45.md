EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Province of Tawi-Tawi comprises eleven island municipalities, all encompassed
by two legislative districts and further subdivided into 203 barangays. Hence, the Datu Halun
Sakilan Memorial Hospital was established to ensure the accessible delivery of an integrated,
quality, and sustainable health service to all its residents in the province.

Since its creation in 1974, it remains to be a Level 1 hospital with a limited budget for
operations and severely deficient health manpower status. Positions for nurses, doctors, and
other vital positions are significantly below the WHO standards for human resources for health.

In 1982, Batas Pambansa Blg. 257 converted the Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial
Hospital to a provincial hospital with a seventy-five (75) bed capacity.

Despite its conversion, it is still difficult for the hospital to attend to all of the residents
due to an inadequate amount of medical equipment, medicine, and healthcare staff. First, the
only way for most of the populace to visit the hospital is by riding a boat, since the
municipalities are scattered on islands. To travel to the nearest Level II medical facility in
Zamboanga City, it takes the average Tawi-Tawi resident 16 hours to travel by boat, and taking
a flight remains a non-economical alternative.

To further highlight the urgency, the Level I 75-bed capacity hospital presently has a
150-200% bed occupancy rate. This overloaded capacity, coupled with the insufficient budget
which amount is shared concomitantly by | 1 Rural Health Units in the province, makes it more
difficult for field health workers to implement the health programs and deliver basic health
services in remote areas.

On behalf of our constituents from the island provinces, the doctors and allied health
professionals of Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital, and as a native of the Province of
Tawi-Tawi, this representation is sponsoring this bill with urgency and without reservation, in
fulfillment of our obligations under the Bangsamoro Organic Law to adopt a policy on health
that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for our constituents.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

AMILBAHAR S. MAWALLIL
Member of the Parliament

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session:

Section 1. The Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital is hereby converted into a Level
II Hospital, under the direct supervision and control of the Ministry of Health (MOH),
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

The authorized bed capacity of the provincial hospital is hereby increased from seventy-
five (75) to one hundred (100) beds.

Section 2. The existing service facilities and other services of the hospital are hereby
upgraded to conform with the bed capacity increase as provided for in Section | hereof.

The existing medical personnel shall also be correspondingly increased. The Ministry
of Health, in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management, and the Civil
Service Commission, shall determine the additional plantilla positions be created and filled up
in coordination with the management of the Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital.

Section 3. The Bangsamoro Autonomous Government shall provide the necessary
funds to support the increase in bed capacity of the Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital and
the corresponding upgrading of its facilities and services.

The Minister of the Ministry of Health (MOH), in consultation with the management
of the Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital, shall issue orders, rules, and circulars as may be
necessary to carry out the implementation of this Act. The Minister of the MOH shall
immediately include in the Ministry’s programs the implementation of this Act, the funding of
which shall be included in the General Appropriations Act.

Section 4. The provisions of this Act are hereby declared separable, and in the event,
one or more such provisions or part thereof are declared unconstitutional, such declaration of
unconstitutionality shall not affect the validity of the other provisions thereof.

Section 5. All regional laws, acts, decrees, executive orders, proclamations, and/or
administrative regulations which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby
amended, modified, superseded, or repealed accordingly.

Section 6. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper
of general circulation in the region.
