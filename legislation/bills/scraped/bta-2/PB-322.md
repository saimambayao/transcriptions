EXPLANATORY NOTE

Paragraph 2, Article [IX of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro
Organic Law, provides that the Bangsamoro Government shall protect and promote the right
to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them. It shall also uphold the
peoples' right to have access to essential goods, health, and other social services that promote
their well-being.

In response to the growing demand for sustainable urban mobility and recognizing the
benefits of promoting cycling as an eco-friendly mode of transportation, this bill proposes the
establishment of a comprehensive bike route network that will interconnect various local
government units (LGUs) within the Bangsamoro Region.

Cycling has gained significant traction as a viable means of transportation, offering
numerous environmental, health, and economic benefits. It reduces greenhouse gas emissions,
alleviates traffic congestion, promotes physical activity, and enhances overall public health.
However, the lack of safe designated path and a coherent and interconnected bike route network
hinders its widespread adoption and safety.

The primary objective of this bill is to create a unified and comprehensive bike route
network that links urban and rural areas across the Bangsamoro Region. By doing so, we aim
to improve safety for cyclists by providing designated routes separate from motor vehicles. By
providing safe and interconnected bike routes across local government units (LGUs), it can
encourage more people to use bicycles as a mode of transportation for commuting and
recreation.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Comprehensive Bike Route Network
Act of 2024.”

Section 2. Definition of Terms. — For purposes of this Act, the following terms are defined:

a. "Bike Route" refers to a designated path or off — road paths specifically designed
for bicycles, providing safe passage for cyclists.

b. "Comprehensive Bike Route Network" refers to an interconnected system of bike
routes that connect various LGUs within a region or jurisdiction.

c. “Non-motorized vehicles” includes all forms of travel that do not rely on an engine
or motor for movement. This includes, but is not limited to bicycles, and small —
wheeled non — motorized transport such as skates, skateboards, and push scooters.

Section 3. Declaration of Policy. — The Bangsamoro Organic Law mandates the Bangsamoro
Government to protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health
consciousness among them. It shall also uphold the peoples' right to have access to essential
goods, health, and other social services that promote their well-being.

The Bangsamoro Government acknowledges the need for infrastructure and a change
in mindset to encourage and accommodate alternative modes of transportation such as bicycles,
and other non-motorized vehicles, and to provide safe and convenient pathways for commuters.

Section 4. Establishment of a Comprehensive Bike Route Network. — Immediately upon the
effectivity of this Act, the Ministry of Transportation and Communication with the Ministry of
Public Works, and in coordination with Local Government Units (LGUs), shall identify and
create a Comprehensive Bike Route Network connecting each local government unit within
the Bangsamoro Jurisdiction.

The said ministries shall also be responsible for the planning and construction of the
bike routes, ensuring safety standards, and overseeing the implementation of the network.

The design for bike routes shall prioritize safety, accessibility, and connectivity, taking
into account the needs of cyclists of all ages and abilities.

Section 5. Promotion and Awareness. — The Bangsamoro government specifically the
Ministry of Transportation and Communication shall conduct public awareness campaigns to
promote the use of the bike route network and encourage cycling as a sustainable mode of
transportation. Educational programs shall be implemented to raise awareness about path
safety, cycling etiquette, and the benefits of cycling for individuals and communities.

Section 6. Monitoring and Evaluation, — The Ministry of Transportation and Communication
shall establish mechanisms for monitoring the usage, safety, and effectiveness of the bike route
network. Regular evaluations shall be conducted to assess the impact of the bike routes on
reducing traffic congestion, promoting health and fitness, and enhancing connectivity between
LGUs.

Section 7. Jmplementing Rules and Regulations. — Within sixty (60) days immediately after
the effectivity of this Act, the Ministry of Transportation and Communication, in coordination
with the Ministry of Public Works, Ministry of Interior and Local Government and other
appropriate agencies, shall promulgate and issue the necessary guidelines for the effective
implementation of this Act. The implementing rules and regulations issued pursuant to this
section shall take effect thirty (30) days after its publication in a newspaper of regional
circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
