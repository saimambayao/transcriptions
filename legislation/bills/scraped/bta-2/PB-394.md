EXPLANATORY NOTE

The ratification of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) in 2019 marked a historic
milestone for the Bangsamoro people and the entire Philippine nation. Across the
archipelago, the event was met with jubilation, hope, and a renewed sense of identity
among the Bangsamoro and other Muslim Filipinos, as well as indigenous communities
who had long aspired for meaningful self-governance and recognition.

When it was ratified, celebrations erupted not only in the core territories of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) but also in
communities across the country where Bangsamoro people reside. The Bangsamoro
people found new hope ignited by the ratification of this law.

While we are grateful of the developments we introduced so far during this
transition period, we are nonetheless not unaware that there is so much more to be
done — especially for our brothers and sisters living beyond the jurisdiction of the
Bangsamoro territory.

Thus, this proposed measure seeks to institutionalize development assistance to
Bangsamoro people and communities residing outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). This initiative is anchored on the State’s
commitment to social justice, national unity, and inclusive development, as enshrined in
the 1987 Philippine Constitution and reinforced by subsequent legislations and peace
agreements.

Section 9, Article Il of the 1987 Constitution mandates the State to “promote a
just and dynamic social order that will ensure the prosperity and independence of the
nation and free the people from poverty through policies that provide adequate social
services, promote full employment, a rising standard of living, and an improved quality
of life for all.”

Meanwhile, the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (RA 11054), recognizes the need to support Bangsamoro communities

“The National Government shall ensure the protection of the rights of

Autonomous Region and undertake programs for their rehabilitation and

Furthermore, the Bangsamoro Administrative Code (BAA No.13) underscores the

advancement for all Bangsamoro communities.

Ultimately, this proposed measure is also consistent with the spirit and objectives
of all signed peace agreements entered into by the Philippine Government and the
Moro liberation fronts, including the Framework Agreement on the Bangsamoro and the
Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro. These agreements, as recognized by
the Supreme Court, aim to address the historical injustices suffered by the Bangsamoro
people and to promote their meaningful participation in national life, not only within but
also outside the autonomous region.

By institutionalizing development assistance to Bangsamoro people and
communities outside BARMM, we fulfill the constitutional mandate of social justice,
uphold the commitments of the peace process, and ensure that all Bangsamoro are
empowered to contribute to and benefit from the nation’s progress.

In view of the foregoing, approval of this measure is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

Section 1: Short Title - This Act shall be known and cited as the "Bangsamoro
Assistance for Inclusive Development Act of 2025” or Bangsamoro Aid, for brevity.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy — \t is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to
ensure that the Bangsamoro people outside the Bangsamoro territory are granted
sufficient assistance from this Government for their welfare and development.

The Bangsamoro Government, in coordination with concerned local government units
where these Bangsamoro communities are situated, and appropriate national
government agencies, shall ensure that the rights and benefits of the Bangsamoro
communities outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region are protected and provided.

Section 3. Definition of Terms - For the purposes of this Act, the terms or words and
phrases shall be construed as follows:

(a) “Bangsamoro People and Communities Outside the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region’ refer to Bangsamoro people and communities located
outside of the territorial jurisdiction of BARMM. These Bangsamoro areas can
be individually or collectively referred to as Bangsamoro Populated
Communities (BPCs), Bangsamoro Populated Area/s (BPAs) or that may be
designated by the Council as “Specially Designated Area” (SDA).

(b) “BDAMC” or the “Council” refers to Bangsamoro Development Assistance
and Management Council created under this Act.

(c) “BDAP” refers to Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan to be
implemented under this Act.

(d) “BPC” refers to Bangsamoro populated community where at least two (2)
contiguous or adjacent barangays in a municipality or across other
municipality/ies are dominated by the Bangsamoro people.

(e) “BPA” refers to Bangsamoro populated area where a municipality or more in
a province are dominated by the Bangsamoro people.

(f) “SDA” refers to Specially Designated Area (SDA) of a Bangsamoro
community that may be designated by the Council through a Resolution.

(g) “Al-madinat Al-shaqiqa” or “Sister City” refers to a form of legal and social
agreement between two municipalities to promote social, cultural and
economic ties among the Bangsamoro Local Government Units.

(h) Development assistance — refers to regular assistance including, but not
limited to, capacity-building, financial and technical assistance, and
institutional support provided by the Council for the social, cultural, and
economic development of the Bangsamoro people or communities outside
the Bangsamoro autonomous region funded under this Act. Development
assistance shall not include aid provided during disasters or calamities.

(i) BDP — refers to the Bangsamoro Development Plan of the Bangsamoro
Economic Development Council.

(j) Conflict Affected Area/s — refers to an area or areas outside BARMM but
dominated by Bangsamoro people that have experienced prolonged armed
conflict, social unrest, and displacement due to historical, political and cultural
tensions brought about by decades of conflict between the Philippine
Government and various Moro revolutionary groups such as the Moro
National Liberation Front (MNLF) and Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF).

(k) Bangsamoro People— refers to a group of people as defined by the
Bangsamoro Organic Law or Republic Act 11054.

Section 4. Scope and Coverage — This Act shall apply to Bangsamoro people and
communities outside the Bangsamoro autonomous region identified and designated by
the Council as Bangsamoro Populated Area/s (BPAs) or Bangsamoro Populated
Communities (BPCs), or other special designation that the Council may determine.

Section 5. Criteria & Prioritization — Subject to Section 13 hereof, underserved and
conflict-affected areas and communities designated as BPA, BPC or SDA shall be
prioritized to benefit from development assistance. Provided that, the Council shall
establish a set of criteria, which shall include, but not be limited to, the following:

a) Preparedness of the designated BPA, BPC or SDA to collaborate with the
BDAMC in a form of a resolution or petition;

b) The socio-economic condition of the concerned community/area necessitates
an urgent intervention and support; and

c) The concerned area has received inadequate assistance from BARMM’s
agencies or none at all.

Section 6. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan -
There is hereby established a comprehensive framework for implementing development
assistance to Bangsamoro people and communities in the identified BPAs /BPCs/SDAs.
This framework shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Development Assistance Plan,
hereinafter referred to as BDAP, and shall guide the Council, the concerned
ministries/offices/agencies, development partners, and concerned local government
units in implementing plans, programs, and services in the aforementioned areas.

The BDAP shall include, but not be limited to, the following programs and services:

a. Development of potable water systems and/or local electrification;

b. Small and medium-sized infrastructure projects such as community roads and
bridges; irrigation, medical and dental clinics; Madaris, housing & resettlements;
multi-purpose centers and barangay halls;

c. Social support services, including secular and Arabic education & scholarship;
health and mental care; social welfare programs (e.g., burial assistance,

Section 7. Creation of the Management Council - There is hereby created a

Bangsamoro Development Assistance and Management Council (BDAMC) which shall
serve as the implementing and management body to execute the BDAP and other
issuances of the Chief Minister pertinent to this Act.

The Council shall be headed by the Chief Minister, who shall call and preside over the
regular quarterly meeting of the Council. In the absence of the Chief Minister, the Vice
Chair shall act as the Chairperson and Presiding Officer. The head of the Council shall
approve projects and programs upon the recommendation of the Council and in close
coordination with the concerned implementing ministry, office, or agency.

Section 8. Quorum. A simple majority of all members of the Council shall constitute a
quorum for the conduct of its business. In the absence of a quorum, the members
present shall constitute an Executive Committee and may proceed with the order of
business.

Section 10. Powers and Functions of the Council — To successfully implement the
provisions of this Act, the Council is hereby vested with the following powers and
functions:

a. Adopt its Internal Rules of Procedures subject to the limitations set forth in this
Act;

b. Formulate plans and policies for the effective implementation of the Bangsamoro
Development Assistance Plan (BDAP) and the provisions of this Act;

c. Ensure that all projects and programs are aligned with the Bangsamoro
Development Plan (BDP) and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs);

d. Determine Bangsamoro areas and designate them as Bangsamoro Populated
Area/s (BPA) or Bangsamoro Populated Community/ies (BPC) upon the
recommendation of the Technical Secretariat or upon application of the
concerned BPA/BPC to be identified as such;

e. It may also designate a particular Bangsamoro area as Specially Designated
Area (SDA) through a Council Resolution;

f. Approve/disapprove proposed projects or programs to be implemented by the
concerned BARMM ministry/office/agency in the identified BPA, BPC or SDA:

g. Determine staffing patterns and personnel complements, if necessary, subject to
Section 11 hereof;

h. May create Technical Working Groups (TWGs) to assist the Technical
Secretariat in the implementation of the Council’s decisions and action plans;

i. Establish partnerships with concerned local government units, development
partners and appropriate national government agencies in the implementation of
the BDAP and other undertakings within the identified BPA, BPC or SDA;

j. Encourage and facilitate BARMM LGUs to foster partnerships with LGUs located
within the designated BPA through “sister city” arrangement (A/-madinat Al-
shagiqa);

k. Monitor and evaluate the implementation of BDAP to ensure compliance with the
objectives of this Act; and,

|. Delegate some of its powers and functions to the Technical Secretariat, as
provided herein.

Section 11. Technical Secretariat — To ensure the effective implementation of the
BDAP, the existing Office for Other Bangsamoro Communities (OOBC), created by
virtue of Section 5, Paragraph “D’, Chapter 1, Title Ill of the BAA No.13, shall serve as
the permanent technical secretariat of the Council. In addition to its existing functions,
the OOBC shall:

a. Assist the Council in implementing the Bangsamoro Development Assistance
Plan;

b. Conduct research and surveys and recommend to the Council areas identified as
BPA, BPC or SDA;

c. Receive proposed project or program and submit them to the Council for
consideration;

d. Establish coordination with concerned LGUs, national government agencies and
other stakeholders in the conduct of its activities and pursuant to the Council’s
directives;

e. Coordinate with the Technical Working Group (TWG) that the Council may create
to effectively implement the plans and programs set by the Council, and,

f. Perform such other delegated powers and functions as assigned by the Council.

Section 12. Creation of Special Fund & Appropriation- There shall be created a
special fund known as the “Bangsamoro Aid Fund”. An initial amount of One Hundred
Million Pesos (P100,000,000.00) shall be allocated for the initial implementation of this
Act, to be sourced from the Office of the Chief Minister. Thereafter, a regular budget in
the Bangsamoro General Appropriations shall be provided by the concerned
ministries/offices/agencies relevant to the implementation of this Act. Provided that: not
less than 5% of the approved budget of the concerned Ministry/Office/Agency shall be
allocated for the implementation of the BDAP exclusively for the designated BPAs,
BPCs and SDAs. Provided further that: Additional funds may be sourced from National
government allocations, international development partners, and private sector

Section 13. Determination of Bangsamoro Communities and Areas - Within one
hundred twenty (120) days after the effectivity of this Act, the Technical Secretariat shall
determine and recommend to the Council the Bangsamoro Populated Communities
(BPCs) and Bangsamoro Populated Areas (BPAs) outside the Bangsamoro
autonomous region that need development assistance from the Bangsamoro
Government.

The determination and recommendation by the Technical Secretariat shall be without
prejudice to the rights of Bangsamoro people outside the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region to submit their applications for designation as Bangsamoro Populated
Community or Area.

Section 14. Oversight Function — The Bangsamoro Parliament, through a special
committee, shall exercise oversight function in the implementation of the Bangsamoro
Development Assistance Plan (BDAP) to ensure that the funds allocated are equitably
provided to designated BPC, BPA or SDA, and its implementation is within the BDP
framework.

Section 15. Implementing Rules and Regulations - Within one hundred twenty (120)
days from the effectivity of this Act, the Office of the Chief Minister, together with the
Ministers and heads of the concerned ministries and offices or their duly authorized
representatives, shall promulgate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.

Section 16. Separability Clause - |f any provision of this Act is declared invalid or
unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

Section 17. Repealing Clause —Sub-paragraphs b, c and d, Section 8, Paragraph D,
Chapter 1, Title Ill of BAA No. 13 are hereby expressly repealed. All other regional laws,
rules, and regulations or parts thereof inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed,
amended or modified accordingly.

Section 18. Effectivity - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication
in the Bangsamoro Official Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation in BARMM
