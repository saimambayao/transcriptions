EXPLANATORY NOTE

Section 2 (3), Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution mandates the establishment and
maintenance of an incentive system, among others, which shall be available to deserving
students in both public and private schools, especially to the underprivileged.

The Bangsamoro Government has consistently honored the achievements of its
exceptional constituents in their respective fields of work and professions. And the
Bangsamoro Topnotcher Act of 2023 is one way of expressing gratitude for the honor and
pride that the Bangsamoro topnotchers bring to the Bangsamoro through their
remarkable performance in their academic pursuits.

The Bangsamoro Parliament approved and adopted Proposed Resolution Nos.
260, 261, 203, 218, 234, 257, 295, and 309 commending and congratulating Nishrien
Amatonding Barua, Al-Zaheer Adil Abubakar, Hamid Simpal Salik, Benjamin Rinabor,

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Topnotcher Act
of 2023”.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to promote excellence in academic pursuits by providing incentives to
Bangsamoro individuals who have brought honor and recognition to the region by
topping national licensure examinations. Towards this end, it shall establish and
maintain an incentive system which shall be available to deserving students in both
public and private schools.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.- As used in this Act, the term “Bangsamoro
Topnotcher/s” refers to Bangsamoro individuals who earned a spot in the Top ten (10) lists
of their respective professional licensure examinations given by the Professionai

SECTION 4. Cash Incentives for Bangsamoro Topnotcher. - Bangsamoro Topnotchers shall
be entitled to cash awards in the following amounts:

1. Top One (1) to Three (3) - One Hundred Thousand Pesos (P100,000.00) each;
2. Top Four (4) to Six (6) - Seventy-Five Thousand Pesos (P75,000.00) each; and
3. Top Seven (7) to Ten (10) - Fifty Thousand Pesos (50,000.00) each.

Provided, That these amounts may be increased whenever circumstances so warrant, and
the recipient shall be invited to regional events that recognize and acknowledge the
importance of excellence in academic pursuits.

SECTION 5. Prohibition Against Diminution of Incentives - No memorandum, order,
or issuance shall diminish the prescribed schedule of incentives. Donations and gifts from
individuals, institutions, or groups shall be construed as distinct and separate from the
cash incentives provided herein.

SECTION 6. Implementing Agency. - Within ninety (90) days from the approval of this
Act, the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education shall develop and adopt a
program to effectuate the provisions of this Act and promulgate such rules and
regulations that will govern the grant of awards and incentives.

SECTION 7. Appropriations. - The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this
Act shall be included in the budget of the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical
Education. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary for the continued implementation
of this Act shall be included in the annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

SECTION 8. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, decrees, orders, rules and regulations,
other issuances, or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby
repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 9. Separability Clause.- If any provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, all other parts or
provisions not affected thereby shall continue to remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 10. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen days (15) after its complete
publication in at least two (2) regional newspapers of general circulation.
