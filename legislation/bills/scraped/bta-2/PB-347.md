Explanatory Note

Section 15, Article X of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that there
shall be created autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao and in the Cordilleras,
consisting of provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical areas sharing common
and distinctive historical and cultural heritage, economic and social structures, and
other relevant characteristics within the framework of this Constitution and the
national sovereignty as well as territorial integrity of the Republic of the Philippines.

The Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB), the final peace
accord, was signed on March 27, 2014, thus concluding the 17 years of negotiations
and armed struggle between the Government of the Philippines and the Moro Islamic
Liberation Front (MILF). This agreement paved the way for the establishment of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

Moreover, Republic Act (R.A.) No. 11054, or the Bangsamoro Organic Law
(BOL), was signed into law on July 27, 2018, by Former President Rodrigo R. Duterte,
repealing R.A. No. 6734 as amended by R.A. No. 9054.

Section 2 (a), Article III of the BOL provides that the territorial jurisdiction of
the BARMM, subject to the indispensable plebiscite, shall be composed, inter alia, of
the present geographical area known as the Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao, created under R.A. No. 6734, as amended by R.A. No. 9054, which includes
the Province of Sulu.

Additionally, Section 2, Article IV of the BOL states that in the exercise of its
right to self-governance, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region is free to pursue its
political, economic, social, and cultural development as provided for in the Organic
Law.

The BOL was ratified through a plebiscite on January 21, 2019, for the core
territories, namely the provinces of Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, Basilan, and
Tawi-Tawi, the component cities of Marawi and Lamitan, as well as the City of
Cotabato.!

On February 6, 2019, another plebiscite was held in Lanao del Norte, as well as
in the municipalities of Aleosan, Carmen, Kabacan, Midsayap, Pikit, and Pigkawayan
in North Cotabato, and other areas that sought inclusion in the proposed Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), leading to the inclusion of 63
Barangays from North Cotabato.*

However, during the said plebiscite, the residents of Sulu were not in favor of
ratifying the BOL. Based on Sulu’s Certificate of Canvass, signed by the Commission
on Elections (COMELEC) Provincial Plebiscite Board of Canvassers, the “YES” vote
lost at 137,630 as against 163,526 “NO” votes. It is to be noted that Sulu is composed
of 19 municipalities and had 375,137 registered voters. Of the over 375,000 registered
voters, the province registered a 301,196-voter turnout during the said plebiscite.°

Pursuant to the plebiscite, unfortunately, on September 9, 2024, the Supreme
Court promulgated its En Banc decision on the case of Province of Sulu, et al. v.
Medialdea, et al., G.R. Nos. 242255, 243246, and 243693. The Court ruled that the BOL
is constitutional but excluded the Province of Sulu from the jurisdiction of the
BARMM after the province rejected the law’s ratification in the plebiscite.

The Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA), as the interim government of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, has the mandate of protecting the general welfare

Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 58 or the Bangsamoro Parliamentary
Districts Act of 2024 was signed into law on February 28, 2024.4

The said law creates 32-single-member parliamentary districts in the BARMM,
which are as follows: eight (8) in Lanao del Sur, seven (7) in Sulu, four (4) each in
Maguindanao del Norte and Maguindanao del Sur, three (3) each in Basilan and Tawi-
Tawi, two (2) in Cotabato City, and one (1) in the Special Geographic Area comprising
63 villages in six (6) North Cotabato towns that voted for inclusion in the BARMM in
the 2019 plebiscite, and now legislated into eight (8) municipalities.

In view of the above-mentioned Supreme Court decision, the Province of Sulu
shall be excluded from the jurisdiction of BARMM. Its exclusion requires recalibration
of the parliamentary seat allocations. There is a compelling need to reapportion the

remaining seven (7) vacant parliamentary seats allocated to the said province.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this measure is earnestly sought.
