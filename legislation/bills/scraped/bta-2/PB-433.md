EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) faces the intricate challenge of "rido," a
term derived from the Maranao language that denotes violent clan conflicts. This term is acknowledged not only by
the Bangsamoro ethnic groups but also by non-Bangsamoro communities, both within the region and throughout
the country, as well as on an international scale.

Historically, the term "rido” has been understood to denote conflicts that result in loss of life, community
displacement, and disruptions to peace and development. Over time, however, the application of this term has
evolved to encompass a broader spectrum of interpersonal or community disputes, including even minor
disagreements that do not involve violence or threats.

Consequently, the indiscriminate application of the term "rido" has led to various negative outcomes, such as:

a) The inflation of certain non-violent disagreements, which creates unnecessary anxiety and undermines the
credibility of conflict reporting, ultimately leading to a misrepresentation of the conflict's severity;

b) Government agencies, peacebuilders, and humanitarian organizations occasionally find it necessary to redirect
resources that were originally allocated for significant security threats to address issues that could still be
effectively managed through traditional mediation or barangay-level processes — a situation that ultimately
distorts policy and resource allocation due to the perception of the conflict as "rido", despite it not accurately
reflecting its true context; and

c) The inappropriate application of the term “rido" risks diminishing its historical and cultural significance, which
may reduce the urgency in addressing genuine violent clan conflicts, resulting in detrimental cultural and social
repercussions.

This proposed measure aims to:

a) Establish clear and culturally sensitive criteria for categorizing local conflicts in BARMM;

b) Prohibit the use of the term "rido" for disputes that do not fulfill the criteria of violent clan feuds; and

c) Encourage accurate conflict documentation and reporting to bolster effective peace and development initiatives.

By regulating the classification of local conflicts, this Act aims to improve peacebuilding efforts, ensure the prudent
use of resources, and preserve the integrity of cultural terminology. It also aligns with the broader goal of fostering

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be referred to as the “Responsible Conflict Characterization Act of
the Bangsamoro.”

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. — The Bangsamoro Government is committed to fostering peace, preventing the
escalation of disputes, and ensuring the accurate and culturally appropriate use of terminology in the description of
local conflicts. The inappropriate application of the term rido to trivial disagreements undermines peacebuilding
initiatives and may provoke unnecessary concern or tension.

SEC. 3. Definition of Terms. — For the purposes of this Act:

a. Rido — A violent, prolonged clan feud characterized by retaliatory actions, frequently resulting in loss of life,
property destruction, or community displacement.

b. Simple Dispute — A disagreement or misunderstanding among individuals, families, or groups that does not
involve violence, threats of violence, or enduring retaliatory actions.

c. Mischaracterization — The act of inaccurately labeling a simple dispute as rido or any similar term without a
factual basis.

SEC. 4. Prohibition. — No individual, public official, media organization, or entity shall mischaracterize a simple
dispute as rido or any similar term in official documents, public declarations, or media reports.

SEC. 5. Responsibilities of Individuals, Institution Officials, and Media.

a) Individuals must refrain from using the term "rido" when it is not applicable, particularly in public forums, during
interviews with mass media, or on any occasions that reach a large audience.

b) .Officials of institutions, including but not limited to public service representatives, uniformed officers from all law
enforcement agencies, and leaders of non-governmental organizations, are obligated to guarantee the precise
classification of conflicts in their reports and communications.

c) Media organizations, encompassing radio, print, and broadcast, are required to verify the nature of disputes
before utilizing culturally sensitive conflict terminology like “rido".

SEC. 6. Penalties. — Any individual or entity determined to have engaged in willful mischaracterization of conflict
shall face the following penalties:

a) For individuals — a monetary fine ranging from 10,000 to 50,000, or a requirement to complete at least 32
hours of community service.

b) For both government and non-government officials — a monetary fine ranging from 750,000 to #100,000,
along with a public apology through mass media with BARMM-wide coverage.

c) For media organizations or entities — a monetary fine ranging from 50,000 to 100,000, accompanied by a
public apology disseminated through mass media with coverage across the region...

SEC. 7. Education and Awareness. — A comprehensive six-month awareness initiative across BARMM,
focusing on appropriate conflict classification and cultural sensitivity, shall be executed by the Ministry of Public
Order and Safety (MPOS) in collaboration with the Ministry of Basic, Higher and Technical Education (MBSTE),
immediately after the legislation is enacted.

SEC. 8. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). — The Chief Minister is required to issue the Implementing
Rules and Regulations (IRR) within ninety (90) days following the enactment of this Act, through a committee
chaired by the MPOS and comprising other pertinent ministries as members.

SEC. 9. Separability Clause. — Should any section of this Act be deemed invalid, the remaining sections shall
continue to be in full effect.

SEC. 10. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Bangsamoro Gazette
or in a widely circulated newspaper within the Region.

The imposition of penalties on violators shall commence immediately following the BARMM-wide intensive six-
month awareness initiatives as outlined in Section 5 of this Act.
