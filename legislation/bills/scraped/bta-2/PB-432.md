EXPLANATORY NOTE

Agriculture remains the comerstone of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(BARMM), where the great majority of the region’s population relies on small-scale farming for both income and
sustenance. Nevertheless, the sector faces persistent challenges—such as fragmented land use, low productivity,
limited access to markets and post-harvest facilities, susceptibility to climate change, a disorganized community
value chain for agricultural production, and inadequate sustainability support services, among others—as
evidenced by numerous research findings’.

The aforementioned challenges impede the region's ability to attain competitive food security, foster
sustainable economic development, and cultivate truly empowered agricultural communities. These issues are
implied, if not explicitly articulated, in the remarks made by Agriculture Secretary Francisco P. Tiu Laurel, Jr.
during the recent Sustainable Agriculture Forum held at Dusit Thani, Manila in September 2024. He stressed that
"while agriculture is crucial to the Philippine economy (including BARMM), it encounters numerous challenges
such as climate change, resource degradation, and socio-economic pressures.”

This proposed legislation presents a hopeful worldview that the challenges facing the agriculture sector,
particularly in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, can be mitigated through the establishment of mechanisms
that promote the efficient utilization of agricultural land and the grouping of crop commodities, in addition to value-
chain farming systems customized for the Bangsamoro farming communities — both of which are anticipated to
lead to sustainable agricultural production and enterprises in the region.

This proposed legislative initiative also outlines a clear path aimed at establishing "community-based,
commodity-clustered and value-chained farming enterprise” frameworks that will strategically align
agricultural crop production with the region's agro-climatic conditions, market demands, and community resources.

From the perspective of this proposed legislation, crop zoning or clustering will guarantee that agricultural
lands are allocated for the most appropriate and high-value crops, thus enhancing yields and reducing losses. At
the same time, the value-chain farming strategy will unify production, processing, storage, and marketing systems.

By institutionalizing the aforementioned mechanisms, the bill specifically seeks to:

a) Enhance agricultural crop production through the adoption of culturally relevant, scientifically informed crop
distribution and sustainable farming practices;

b) Strengthen market connections by establishing direct connections between organized group of farmers and
buyers who offer fair prices, as well as processors and exporters;

c) Foster inclusive growth by enabling cooperatives, farmers’ associations, and local government units to

d) Ensure resilience against climate change and market fluctuations through diversified and well-coordinated

e) Provide the necessary funding, capacity-building initiatives, and technical support to ensure effective

This initiative aligns with the goals set forth in the Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act (Republic
Act No. 8435) as well as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (R.A. No. 11054). Both of these legislative measures
highlight the significance of sustainable agricultural development and fair distribution of resources.

ARTICLE I: - INTRODUCTORY PROVISIONS

SECTION 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be referred to as the “Community-based Agricultural Crop Enterprise
Development Act of 2026.”

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to foster
inclusive, community-based, commodity-clustered and value-chained agricultural development by providing
technical and financial support to small farmers, enabling them to engage in sustainable and profitable agricultural
crop enterprises, thereby ensuring food security, alleviating poverty, and encouraging rural economic development.

It is further established as a policy that the Bangsamoro government mandates the simultaneous and coordinated
implementation of strategic crop clustering, value-chain integration, and community-managed enterprises to
guarantee food security, create livelihoods, and foster climate-resilient agricultural systems.

All the aforementioned policies are consistent with the goals set forth in Republic Act No. 8435, otherwise known
as the Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act (AFMA) of 1997, as well as in Republic Act No. 11054, referred
to as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) of 2018.

SEC. 3. Objectives. — The objectives of this Act are to:

a) Enhance agricultural crop production through the adoption of culturally relevant, scientifically informed crop
distribution and sustainable farming practices;

b) Strengthen market connections by directly linking farmers to buyers, processors, and exporters;

c) Foster inclusive growth by enabling cooperatives, farmers’ associations, and local government units to
collaboratively manage agricultural crop enterprises;

d) Ensure resilience against climate change and market fluctuations through diversified and well-coordinated
production systems; and

e) Provide the necessary funding, capacity-building initiatives, and technical support to ensure effective
implementation.

' SEC. 4. Scope and Coverage. — This Act shall apply to all agricultural enterprises engaged in crop-based
production within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region -- a farming initiative that incorporates the principles of
community participation, commodity clustering, and value chain development. Additionally, it shall extend to all

ARTICLE Il: - OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS

SEC. 5. Definition of Terms. — For the purposes of this Act:

a) Community-based, Commodity-clustered, and Value-chained agricultural enterprise —Refers to a framework
where agricultural production is aligned with community participation and the grouping of commodities to enhance
value creation, aimed ultimately at establishing sustainable agricultural systems that benefit not only the farming
households but also the community and the broader economy.

i) Community-based enterprises entail cooperation among local farmers and stakeholders to enhance
agricultural methods and improve market accessibility.

ii) Commodity-clustered enterprises emphasize the cultivation of particular agricultural products in clusters,
which can improve market competitiveness and resource availability.

iii) Value-chained enterprises scrutinize the complete value utilization, from input provision to product
development and consumption, to maximize efficiency and profitability.

b) Farming Enterprise — Any activities related to the production, processing, or marketing of agricultural products
conducted by small-scale farmers who are members of cooperatives or community organizations established
around particular agricultural commodities.

c) Holistic Farming — Refers to agricultural methods that prioritize collaboration among farmers, producers, and
consumers to increase the value of agricultural goods. It is characterized as community-based, commodity-
clustered, and value chain-focused.

d) Operational conceptual framework — Encompasses the transition from theoretical definitions to a variety of
specific programs, projects and activities (PPAs) or measures within small-scale agricultural enterprises, engaging
specific human groups to execute the PPAs or measures.

e) Small Farmers — Refers to individuals or households from marginalized backgrounds who have been affected
by conflict in BARMM, who are engaged in farming on not more than (3) hectares, and are members of a farmers’
association or cooperatives, within the specified zones or clusters in the region.

Refers to individuals or families from marginalized backgrounds impacted by conflict, who engage in agriculture on
a maximum of three (3) hectares, and are members of a farmers' association or cooperatives, situated within the
specified zones or clusters in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

f) Support Mechanisms — Refers to the programs, services, and resources offered by governmental bodies,
private organizations, or a combination of both, aimed at supporting farmers in their farming enterprises. These
support mechanisms may include, but are not limited to, financial grants, agricultural subsidies, accessible credit
options, crop insurance, farm equipment, and post-harvest infra structure.

SEC. 6. Conceptual Paradigm. — Community engagement, commodity clustering, and value chain integration
shall be the unified small-scale agricultural crop enterprise development conceptual framework to enhance the
growth of agricultural enterprises among small-scale farmers in the region.

The framework, which endeavors to empower rural agricultural communities by grouping smallholder farmers into
clusters according to their commodities and incorporating them into sustainable value chains, aims to:

a) Enhance efficiency and quality by facilitating collective access to contemporary agricultural technologies,
superior inputs, and training, while also promoting the adoption of climate-resilient and sustainable farming
methods;

b) Boost market competitiveness by enabling collective marketing initiatives to achieve better prices and stable
buyers, while also reinforcing partnerships with agribusinesses, cooperatives, and institutional markets;

c) Improve farmers’ incomes and livelihoods by reducing production costs through bulk purchasing and shared
services, as well as enhancing value through post-harvest processing, packaging, and branding; and

d) Encourage inclusive and participatory development by ensuring the active involvement of farmers, women, and
youth in decision-making processes, along with building local capacity for enterprise management and governance.

The allocation of human resources, encompassing the active engagement of diverse sectors in the advancement
of small-scale agricultural crop enterprises within the region, is a crucial component of the framework.
Consequently, collaboration among concerned ministries and agencies of the regional government, the Local
Government Units (LGUs), along with the active engagement of various nongovernmental organizations and

ARTICLE Ill: —- INSTITUTIONAL COUNCIL SUPPORT

SEC. 7. Creation. - The Regional Agricultural Enterprise Development Council (RAEDC), hereinafter referred to
as the Council, is hereby established. This Council will act as the principal body for policy-making, coordination,
and monitoring of agricultural crop enterprise development initiatives aimed at supporting small crop farmers in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

SEC. 8. Membership. — The Council shall consist of:
a) The Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform — Chairperson;
b) The Minister of Trade, Investments, and Tourism — Vice Chairperson;

c) One (1) Representative each from the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM), Ministry of
Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE), Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST),
Bangsamoro Cooperatives Authority (BCA) and other government instrumentalities having concerned with
agribusiness development;

d) Two (2) representatives from accredited small farmers’ organizations from each province;

e) One (1) representative from successful agricultural cooperatives;

f) One (1) representative from the academic sector with expertise in agriculture or agribusiness; and

g) One (1) representative from the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) who is a planning
expert shall serve as a member of the Council, tasked specifically with reviewing the Council's development plans
and related matters.

SEC. 9. Authority and Responsibilities. - The Council shall:

a) Determine the essential commodities for municipalities in each province by working in partnership with the Local
Government Units (LGUs) of these municipalities and the province, as part of the Council's benchmarking initiative.

b) Develop and execute a Regional Agricultural Enterprise Development Plan;

a. Facilitate small farmers’ access to financing, technology, and training;

b. Create market connections both within and outside the region;

c. Collaborate with local government units and relevant agencies to ensure program alignment; and
d. Oversee and assess the outcomes of agricultural enterprise initiatives.

SEC. 10. Secretariat. - The Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries, and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) will function as the
Secretariat of the Council, providing both administrative and technical assistance.

ARTICLE IV: - PROGRAMS AND SUPPORT MECHANISMS

SEC. 11. Establishment of the Bangsamoro Farming Enterprise Support Program (BFESP). - A
comprehensive program is hereby established to provide technical, financial, and market support to small farmers.

The BFESP is hereby established to provide:

a. Capacity Building — Training in modem, climate-resilient, and halal-compliant farming practices;

b. Access to Credit and Grants — Provision of low-interest loans and start-up grants for agricultural enterprise
initiatives;

c. Infrastructure Support - Development of farm-to-market roads, irrigation systems, and post-harvest facilities;

d. Market Linkages — Establishment of partnerships with local and national buyers, cooperatives, and agribusiness
firms; and

e. Research and Innovation — Collaboration with agricultural research institutions for the transfer of technology.

SEC. 12. Priority Beneficiaries. - The primary beneficiaries will consist of marginalized farmers, women,
indigenous communities, and individuals impacted by conflict in BARMM; provided that these beneficiaries are
actively involved in agricultural activities, either as owners or long-term tenants of no more than three (3) hectares,
but with a minimum of half a hectare of cultivated land, and are members of a farmers’ association or cooperatives,
within the designated zones or clusters in the region.

SEC. 13. Funding. — The funds required for the initial implementation of this Act shall be drawn from the current
appropriations of MAFAR and other cooperating ministries and offices having regular budget especially for
agricultural development and sustainability. Subsequently, any additional amounts deemed necessary shall be
incorporated into the annual General Appropriations Act for the Bangsamoro (GAAB).

SEC. 14. Monitoring and Evaluation. - The MAFAR shall be responsible for monitoring and evaluating the
programs, projects, and activities of recipient farmers’ associations or cooperatives. It shall also be required to
provide an annual report to the Office of the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro Parliament, detailing the status of
implementation, which encompasses the number of beneficiaries, project results, and financial expenditures.

SEC. 15. Penalties. — Any individual or organization found to be misappropriating funds or participating in
fraudulent activities under this Act shall face administrative, civil, and criminal penalties as per existing laws.

ARTICLE IV: - FINAL PROVISIONS
SEC. 16. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). — Within ninety (90) days after the enactment of this Act,
the MAFAR, in collaboration with other stakeholders, shall form a Technical Working Group (TWG) responsible for
developing the IRR. The outputs generated by this group will be reviewed and approved by the Regional
Agricultural Enterprise Development Council (RAEDC) before their final submission to the Chief Minister of the
Bangsamoro government for approval.

SEC. 17. Separability Clause. — Should any provision of this Act be found unconstitutional, the remaining
provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SEC. 18. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days following its publication in the Official Gazette
or in a widely circulated newspaper within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.
