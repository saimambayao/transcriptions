E NATORY NOT

Waaf is one of the great acts of charity in Islam. Literally meaning “to
preserve, stand and/or to hold”, it is a continuous, ongoing philanthropy. It has
been mentioned in the Hadith of Allah’s Messenger (peace be upon him) that “When
the son of Adam dies, his deeds come to an end, except for three things: ongoing
charity (Sadagatul jariya), Knowledge (by which people benefit), and a righteous
child who prays for him.” Indeed, in the Islamic world, ongoing or perpetual charity,
has found its manifestation on what we now know as wadf.

In Islam, waqf means a religious endowment or a voluntary and irrevocable
dedication of a portion of one’s wealth and its disbursements for religious or
charitable purposes that are aligned with the Shari’ah. Waqf property covers both

fund pious and charitable activities, including, but not limited to construction of

Historically, wagqf have played a major role in providing sustainable finance
for many social services, including but not limited to education and health. In fact,
it has been estimated that during the Umayyad and Abbasid Caliphates, more than
60 percent of public services were carried out via the institution of waqf. First, waqf
financed the basic social services (and infrastructure) of education and health as
well as sustenance or social security for the needy. Second, beyond these basic
needs, waaf played an important role in the development of cultural and scientific
life as they financed the production of books, the construction of libraries, as well
as supporting scholars of various religious and secular sciences.

The Bangsamoro is not a stranger on the different waqf arrangements.
Anecdotal evidence points to mosques, madrasahs, and other structures that are
standing on waaf land. However, these arrangements are informal in nature. We
have no data on the actual number of waaf that has been established in the BARMM
and in the Philippines and the properties covered by waqf. And we do not know
oftentimes to what extent the waaf founder’s intentions have been realized in the
length of time that the waqf has been in operation. There is no central governing
body that monitors and provides support to the waafs. This is quite understandable,
given that there is no public policy framework that governs waaf in the Bangsamoro
or in the Philippines.

A legislative and policy framework on waqf would formalize the various wacaf
arrangements in the Bangsamoro, and provide the much-needed support to parties
involved in waaf - such as the founder (waqif), the wagf managers and trustee/s
(mutawalli or nadhir); ensure accountability especially in the realization of the aims
of the waqf; and protect the interests of the beneficiaries - whether an identified
group of people or the general public. This in turn, is envisioned to encourage
individuals in the Bangsamoro to establish wacqf.

Under Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic
Law, the Bangsamoro Parliament shall have the power to enact laws to promote,
protect and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro and other inhabitants in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region. Similarly, under Article V, Section 2 (ii)
thereof, the Bangsamoro Government is empowered to exercise authority over
Islamic banking and finance. This is not only limited to the Islamic equivalents of
conventiona! financial products, but also social finance such as waqf, zakat and
others.

Thus, this proposed bill seeks to encourage the establishment of waqf to
benefit the widest number of our constituents, especially those who are poor,
vulnerable and disadvantaged, and those who seek a more robust spiritual life. A
Bangsamoro Waqf Commission in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim

Chapter II. Nature of Waqf

Section 4. Purpose of Waqf. Wacaf shall be established for religious, pious, or
charitable purposes for the benefit of the Muslim community, the general public, or
the family of the waqif.

When constituted to defraud creditors or to deprive heirs of their inheritance,
the waaf shall be considered as having no force and effect.

Section 5. Wagf, How Established. Waaf may be created by an act of a living
person (inter vivos) or by will (waif bil wasiyat). A living waqf may also be made
by a person in a condition of death-illness (marj-al-maut), provided the property
endowed does not exceed one-third of the decedent’s estate.

Section 6. Requirements for the Establishment of Wagqf. No wadf shali be
deemed valid without the concurrence of the following essential requisites:

a) The waqif, who must be of legal age and sound mind

b) The waqf asset or property (mawquf), which must be under the
ownership of the waqif at the time of the endowment,

c) The Mutawalli, trustee or manager appointed by the Wagqif;

d) The pious, charitable educational! purpose for which the wadf is
constituted (maqasid); and

e) The beneficiaries (al mawquf alaihim).

Section 7. The Wagqjif. The founder of the waqf, must be:

a) a person of legal age, of sound mind having the right to dispose his
property;

b) the lawful owner of the property at the moment of the endowment;

c) not subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings at the time of
the endowment.

Section 8. The Property Subject of the Waqf (Mawgquf). Properties subject
of waaqf must be:

a) An immovable property;

b) located within the territory of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao;

c) be a thing that is in existence, known and specified at the time of the
endowment;

d) of vaiue and capable of generating income (rei) or providing social or
community benefits for the welfare of the public;

e) not subject to any liens or encumbrances;

f) only used for purposes and activities in accordance with the shari’ah.

g) in case of rental properties, the rent must be in accordance with
market rate (ujrat al-mithl), to be re-assessed on an annual basis.

Section 9. Annotation in the Certificate of Titie of the Properties. The
registration of the waqf with the Commission shall constitute an encumbrance on
the mauguf or waaf property. This shall be duly annotated in the Certificate of Title
together with the pertinent details of the waqf registration.

Section 10. Exemption from Taxes. Wadaf properties registered with the
Commission shall be exempt from the payment of applicable taxes that are within
the taxing powers of the Bangsamoro Parliament under Article XII, Section 9 of the
Bangsamoro Organic Law, provided such properties are exclusively used for
religious, charitable or educational purposes.

The waaf property must comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

Section 11. Disposition of Waqf Properties. No waaf property shail be
sold, mortgaged, or otherwise disposed of except upon approval of the Commission,
in which case, shall be in consideration of the maqasid from which it was endowed
and the intents of the wagif, is still living.

Section 12. Replacement, Conversion, Substitution or Exchange of
Waof Properties. If in the opinion of the mutawalli, the original waqf property is
no longer fit to be the subject of the waaf, or that the maqasid of the waaf will be
better served by utilizing another property, the mutawalli may opt for the
replacement (taghyir), conversion (tabdil), substitution (istibdal) or exchange
(ibdal) of the property subject to the approval of the Bangsamoro Waqf Commission.

Section 13. The Maqasid. The waqf may be constituted for the following
purposes:

a) For pious, charitable or educational purposes (waqf khayri);

b) For the construction and maintenance of a public utility (waqf al-sabil);
and

c) For the specific needs of a disadvantaged sector of society (waqf al-
awaridh).

d) For the benefit of the wagif’s family, relatives and descendants (waqf
al-ahli or waqf al-dhurri)

Section 14. Who may be a Mutawalli. A mutawalli may either be a natural or
juridical person. A Muslim of legal age residing in the province where the waaf
property is located, possessing sound mind, and not having been accused or
convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude, may be appointed as a mutawaili.

A waqif may not be a mutawalli over his or her own property.

Section 15. Wagf Corporate Entities. Waqf properties valued above five
million pesos (Php 5,000,000) at the time of the endowment shall be administered
by Waaf corporate entities registered as such with the Bangsamoro Waaf
Commission.

Section 16. Duties of Mutawallis. The following are the duties, responsibilities
and functions of mutawallis:

a) Manage and administer the waqf properties in accordance with the

beneficial interest of any kind in the waqf property.

Section 17. Appointment and Removal of the Mutawalli. The appointment
of a mutawalli shall be made in the same deed constituting the waqf. Once
appointed, the mutawalli may only be removed through an order of the Commission,
upon a request by the wadif or by his heirs, if deceased.

Section 18. Grounds for the Removal of a Mutawailii. The wagif may request
the Commission for the removal of the mutawalli, on the following grounds:

a) The mutawalli denies the character of the property as a waqf property
and sets up an adverse title to it in himself;

b) Misappropriation of the waqf property;

c) Having sufficient funds, allows the property to fall into disrepair;

d) Causes damage or loss to the property or commits an act that could
be considered a breach of trust, knowingly and intentionally;

Chapter III. The Bangsamoro Waqf Commission

Section 24. The Bangsamoro Wagqf Commission. There is hereby
established a Bangsamoro Waaf Commission, attached for budgetary purposes to
the Office of the Chief Minister, which shall be responsible for overall supervision of
management of awgaf in the Bangsamoro.

Section 25. Powers and Functions of the Commission. The Commission
shall have the following powers and functions:

a) Register and or accredit waqf entities and mutawallis in the
Bangsamoro and provide for the standards and procedure for
registration and renewal thereof;

b) Establish and maintain a registry of waqf properties in the
Bangsamoro, which shall include a list of all wagf properties, including
their location, size, value, type (whether residential, commercial,
agricultural) and other relevant information to facilitate their
development;

c) Ensure that the maqasid of waqf properties are being fulfilled in
accordance with the intent of the waqif;

d) Ensure that the process of waqf are applied as to its Islamic purpose
and subject to the conditions prescribed by the waqif;

e) Issue advisory opinions (fatwa) regarding waqf and surrounding issues
for purpose of clarifying and providing guidance to all concerned
parties;

Chapter IV. Finai Provisions

Section 30. Construction and Interpretation. Rules on the establishment,
management and disposition of waqf properties shall be construed liberally in favor
of the full realization of the desire of the wagqif, in so far as it does not contradict
the religious or charitable purpose of the wacf.

Section 31. Suppietory Application of Existing Laws. Provisions of existing
laws on trusts and fiduciary entities as well as existing and guidelines set by the
Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas on the matter shall apply suppletorily as long as they
are not in contravention of this Act or the general principles of the Shari’ah;

Section 32. Annual Report. The Bangsamoro Waqf Commission shall submit
an annual report to the Bangsamoro Parliament on the status of waaf in the
Bangsamoro, including pertinent information on waqf entities and waqf properties
in its registry. The annual report shall likewise include an assessment of the impact
of waaf on the development of the Bangsamoro communities.

Section 33. Appropriations. The amount necessary for the implementation of
this Act shall be sourced from the Bangsamoro Special Development Fund until such
Fund is no ionger avaiiabie to the Bangsamoro. Thereafter, such amount necessary
for the implementation of this Act shall be included in the annual General
Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

Section 34. Implementing Rules and Regulations. the Bangsamoro Waaf
Commission shall draft such rules and regulations as may be necessary to carry out
the provisions of this Act within 60 days from appointment of the members of the
first Commission.

Section 35. Separability. If any part or provision of this Act is held invalid or
unconstitutional, the other parts or provisions thereof shall remain valid and
effective.

Section 36. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
publication in at least one (1) newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).
