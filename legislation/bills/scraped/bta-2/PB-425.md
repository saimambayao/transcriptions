Explanatory Note

Article 26 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights affirms that “Everyone
has the right to education.”

Echoing this principle, Section 1, Article XIV of the 1987 Philippine Constitution
mandates that “The State shall protect and promote the right of all citizens to quality
education at all levels and shall take appropriate steps to make such education
accessible to all.”

In the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), this
commitment is reinforced by Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the
Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL). Section 13, Article IX of the BOL provides that “The
Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic
and responsive health programs, quality education, appropriate services, livelihood
opportunities, affordable and progressive housing projects, power and electricity, and
water inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.”

Also enshrined in Section 16, Article IX of the BOL is the priority of the
Bangsamoro Government to establish, maintain, and support a complete and
integrated system of quality education, which shall be a subsystem of the national
education system. The Bangsamoro Government shall develop an educational

framework that is relevant and responsive to the needs, ideals, and aspirations of the
Bangsamoro people. For this purpose, the Bangsamoro Government shall conform to
the minimum standards set by the National Government.

For decades, the Bangsamoro people have endured conflict, marginalization,
and limited access to opportunities. With the establishment of the BARMM, the BOL
entrusts to the Bangsamoro Government both the mandate and the moral
responsibility to uplift the lives of its constituents through meaningful education,
human development, and empowerment.

One of the vital elements for the continued growth and stability of the
Bangsamoro Region is the presence of competent and service-oriented professionals.
Professionals such as teachers, nurses, engineers, social workers, agriculturists,
lawyers, and other licensed practitioners are essential in strengthening institutions
and delivering public services that directly improve the lives of Bangsamoro
communities. Empowering more Bangsamoro individuals to enter professional fields
builds a stronger workforce, enhances government service delivery, and advances a
more prosperous and self-sustaining region.

A growing number of Bangsamoro students and graduates aspire to become
licensed teachers, nurses, engineers, social workers, lawyers, and other professionals
who can contribute to the continued peace, progress, and long-term development of
the region. However, the high cost of review programs, learning materials,
transportation, and accommodation required for licensure and Bar examinations
poses a significant barrier to many qualified but financially disadvantaged examinees.

Recognizing these realities, this proposed measure seeks to establish the
Bangsamoro Professional Examination Review Subsidy Program (Program), which
shall provide financial assistance to qualified and eligible Bangsamoro residents
preparing for Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) licensure examinations and
the Supreme Court Bar Examinations.

The Bangsamoro Transition Authority, as the interim government of the
Bangsamoro Region, carries the mandate to promote equitable access to education and
professional development. By removing economic barriers, this measure ensures that
poverty is no longer a disqualifier and a hindrance in attaining the professional
aspirations of the Bangsamoro. It is an investment in our human capital that will
return dividends in the form of a more skilled workforce and a more self-sustaining
region.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this measure is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao assembled:

Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Licensure
Examination Assistance Act of 2025”.

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to promote the dignity, aspirations, and long-standing struggle of the
Bangsamoro people by expanding access to education, professional growth, and
socio-economic opportunities.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall assist its constituents in
overcoming barriers to learning and career development. It shall ensure that every
Bangsamoro, especially the youth, the marginalized, and those striving to uplift their
families and communities, has fair access to the knowledge, training, and professional
qualifications needed to contribute to a peaceful, progressive, and self-reliant
Bangsamoro homeland.

Section 3. Creation of the Bangsamoro Professional Examination Review
Subsidy Program. - There is hereby established the Bangsamoro Professional
Examination Review Subsidy Program, hereinafter referred to as the “Program,”
under the Office of the Chief Minister (OCM), which shall provide financial and
institutional support and assistance for BARMM examinees preparing for Professional

Section 4. Forms of Subsidy. - Subject to existing budgeting, accounting, and
auditing rules and regulations, the Program shall provide financial, material, or
institutional support to qualified examinees in any or all of the following:

a) Professional Review Fee Support - Full or partial subsidy of fees charged
by review centers, whether conducted on-site or online. This shall cover
costs directly associated with preparation for the examinations;

b) Transportation and Living Allowance - Provision of financial assistance
for transportation, accommodation, and meals during review sessions and
examination periods, with the specific amount or ceiling of such allowance
to be established in the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of this
Act;

c) Study Materials Assistance - Provision of financial assistance for the
acquisition of review books and other materials necessary for examination
preparation; and

d) Priority Assistance for Marginalized Examinees - Additional support for
examinees who are orphans, persons with disabilities (PWDs), indigenous
peoples, and members of low-income households.

Section 5. Coverage. - The Program shall be made available to all qualified
BARMM residents who are preparing for PRC licensure examinations or the Bar
Examinations. For purposes of this Act, coverage shall include:

a) All BARMM residents who are qualified to take any PRC licensure
examination;

b) BARMM residents who are duly qualified to take the Philippine Bar
Examinations as administered by the Supreme Court; and

c) Bangsamoro students and professionals who are currently studying or
working outside the BARMM, provided that they can present valid proof
of permanent residency in the region.

Section 6. Eligibility Requirements. - To qualify for assistance under the
Program, applicants must submit the following requirements to the Board:

a) Proof of Residency - Valid documents showing permanent residency
within the BARMM;

b) Proof of Examination Eligibility - Official certification or other documents
showing that the applicant is duly qualified to take the corresponding PRC
licensure examination or the Bar Examination;

Section 9. Board Meetings. - The Board shall convene regularly once every
three (3) months. The Chairperson may, however, call a special meeting whenever
necessary: Provided, that members are notified in writing at least three (3) days prior
to the conduct of such meeting.

A quorum of the Board shall consist of a majority of all its members holding
office at the time of meeting: Provided, however, That the Chairperson is among those
present.

Section 10. Compensation. - Members of the Board shall not receive additional
compensation, except for honoraria, per diems, or allowances allowed under existing
Bangsamoro laws, subject to accounting and auditing rules.

Section 11. Voluntary Community Service for Subsidy Recipients. - Successful
examinees who received subsidies shall be encouraged to participate in voluntary
community service within BARMM for a minimum of sixty (60) hours within one (1)
year following their examination. Such service shall be entirely voluntary and shall
not be a requirement for receiving the subsidy, but is strongly promoted as a means
of contributing to the development and welfare of the Bangsamoro community.

Section 12. Annual Report to the Parliament. - The OCM shall submit a report
to the Parliament within one year from the implementation of this Act, and every year
thereafter, on the progress of the implementation of this Act.

Section 13. Appropriations. - The amount necessary for the initial
implementation of this Act shall be charged against the Contingent Fund. Thereafter,
such amounts as may be necessary for the continued implementation of this Act shall
be included in the annual budget of the OCM under the General Appropriations Act
of the Bangsamoro.

Section 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The OCM shall issue the
implementing rules and regulations within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this
Act.

Section 15. Separability Clause. - If for any cause, any part of this Act is
declared unconstitutional or contrary to the provisions of Bangsamoro Organic Law,
the rest of the provisions shall remain in force and effect.

Section 16. Repealing Clause. - All laws, orders, rules, and regulations that are
inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, are hereby repealed, modified,
superseded, or amended accordingly.

Section 17. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its
complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
