EXPLANATORY NOTE

Whereas, Section 15, Article XII of Republic Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law)
guarantees the Bangsamoro Government’s fiscal autonomy, including the right to receive an
annual block grant and other development assistance from the national government;

Whereas, due to bureaucratic delays, procurement bottlenecks, and absorptive capacity
limitations, significant portions of development allocations in BARMM remain unutilized by
year-end, resulting in funds being reverted to the national treasury, undermining development
continuity;

Whereas, to safeguard these funds and ensure project implementation continuity, it is
imperative to create a dedicated Trust Fund that allows retention, carry-over, and multi-year
programming of unspent yet committed development funds;

Now therefore, this Bill seeks to institutionalize the Bangsamoro Trust Fund for
Development Continuity (BTFDC), define its legal structure and governance, and enable
effective, accountable, and transparent use of developrént resources across fiscal years.

AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING THE BANGSAMORO TRUST FUND FOR
DEVELOPMENT CONTINUITY (BTFDC), PROVIDING FOR ITS STRUCTURE,
MANAGEMENT, UTILIZATION, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bangsamoro Trust
Fund for Development Continuity Act of 2025,” hereinafter referred to as the BTFDC Act.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to uphold and advance the principles of fiscal autonomy, good governance, and
development continuity by ensuring the efficient, accountable, and timely implementation of
development programs and projects across the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

To this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall institutionalize a Bangsamoro Trust Fund
for Development Continuity (BTFDC) as a fiscal mechanism that enables the retention,
carryover, and programmed utilization of obligated but unutilized development funds. This
shall apply to appropriations sourced from the Annual Block Grant, Special Development
Fund, national government subsidies, official development assistance (ODA), and other lawful
sources.

(BTFDC). There is hereby established the Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development
Continuity (BTFDC), a special fiduciary account under the stewardship of the Bangsamoro

The BTFDC shall possess a distinct legal personality for fiduciary purposes, administered
under the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM), and shall operate under its
direct administrative supervision, without prejudice to constitutional oversight mechanisms.

The BTFDC shall function as a mechanism for fiscal continuity and programmatic
implementation, ensuring that budgetary allocations earmarked for development projects and
public services are not forfeited due to technical, procurement, or administrative delays.
Instead, such funds shall be retained, safeguarded, and deployed in accordance with approved
work and financial plans, subject to appropriate audit and transparency safeguards.

SECTION 4. Objectives. The Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development Continuity
(BTFDC) is hereby established to pursue the following core objectives, consistent with the
Bangsamoro Government’s mandate of fiscal autonomy, inclusive development, and
responsive governance:

a. To prevent the automatic reversion of obligated but unutilized development funds to
the National Treasury or other lapsing mechanisms, thereby safeguarding regional
development allocations;

b. To enable the timely and full completion of development projects that encounter
justifiable delays in procurement, implementation, or administrative processes, without
forfeiting their corresponding budgets;

c. To institutionalize a legal and operational mechanism for the retention, carryover, and
multi-year programming of development funds in accordance with approved work and
financial plans and within legally defined retention periods;

d. To support strategic, long-term, and high-impact investments that align with the
Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP), the Bangsamoro Regional Spatial Development
Framework (RSDF), and other sectoral master plans;

e. To enhance the efficiency, transparency, and accountability of public financial
management in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region through improved fund utilization,
stronger internal controls, and citizen-participatory oversight mechanisms.

These objectives shall guide the governance, operationalization, and oversight of the
BTFDC and shall be integrated into the policies, implementing rules, and monitoring
frameworks governing the use of public resources in the region.

In case of competing fund utilization proposals, priority shall be given to programs
addressing post-conflict rehabilitation, basic social services, and critical infrastructure, in
alignment with the Bangsamoro Development Plan and subject to impact-based evaluation.

SECTION 5. Fund Sources. The Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development Continuity
(BTFDC) shall be composed of financial resources derived from the following lawful sources:

a. Unutilized balances of obligated funds from the Bangsamoro Government’s share in
the Annual Block Grant, the Special Development Fund (SDF), and national government
subsidies, which remain unspent at the end of the fiscal year, subject to validation and
accounting reconciliation;

c. Reflows, earnings, and interest income derived from fiduciary placements or trust fund

SECTION 6. Eligible Uses. The Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development Continuity
(BTFDC) shall be utilized exclusively to support development undertakings that are consistent
with the principles of good governance, inclusive growth, and strategic resilience. The
following shall constitute eligible uses of the Fund:

a. The continuing implementation, completion, or recommencement of delayed, ongoing,
or partially implemented development projects, including those hindered by
procurement, logistical, or administrative constraints;

b. The funding of priority programs and projects in key sectors such as infrastructure,
public health, education, sustainable livelihood, digital transformation, energy access,
research, and innovation, as identified in the Bangsamoro Development Plan and sectoral
master plans;

c. The establishment of contingency reserves, adaptive measures, and risk mitigation
mechanisms to support critical or high-value development initiatives affected by natural
disasters, conflict, fiscal shocks, or force majeure events;

d. The implementation of capacity-building programs, community-driven development
projects, and peacebuilding initiatives, including those aligned with the principles of
transitional justice and reconciliation, particularly in conflict-affected, marginalized, and
underserved areas.

All fund disbursements shall be governed by approved work and financial plans, subject
to performance-based monitoring, and aligned with the public investment priorities of the
Bangsamoro Government. Utilization shall be in accordance with relevant financial
accountability standards, Shari’ah-compliant fiduciary rules, and oversight mechanisms
established by law.

The BTFDC shall not be used for personal services expenditures, debt servicing, political
activities, or any other purpose not directly related to project implementation and development
outcomes as certified by the BTFDC Management Board.

Development Continuity (BTFDC) is hereby authorized to retain and carry over unutilized but
obligated development funds beyond the close of the fiscal year, for a maximum period of two

Such retention and carryover shall be subject to the execution of a Memorandum of

accordance with the rules set by the BTFDC Governing Board and the relevant provisions of
this Act.

SECTION 8. Governance Structure. The Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development
Continuity (BTFDC) shall be administered and governed by a BTFDC Management Board,
which shall exercise policy direction, fiduciary oversight, and strategic guidance over the
utilization, retention, and monitoring of the Fund.

The BTFDC Management Board shall be composed of the following:

1. Minister of Finance, Budget, and Management — Chairperson

2. Director General of the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) —
Vice Chairperson

3. Chairperson of the Bangsamoro Parliament Committee on Finance, Budget, and
Management — Member

4. Duly designated representative from the Commission on Audit - BARMM — Ex
Officio Member, Non-Voting

5. One (1) representative from an accredited Civil Society Organization (CSO) engaged
in development planning or public financial management — Member, appointed by the
Chief Minister upon recommendation of the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and
Management

The Board shall be assisted by a Technical Secretariat, which shall serve as the
administrative and operational arm of the Fund and shall be based within the Ministry of
Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM). The Secretariat shall be responsible for
coordinating project evaluation, fund release, documentation, records management, and inter-
agency liaison.

The BTFDC shall also be supported by:

ensure adherence to financial controls and risk management policies;

b. A Monitoring and Evaluation Unit, responsible for tracking fund utilization, project
outcomes, and performance indicators, and for preparing consolidated reports for
submission to the Management Board, the Bangsamoro Parliament, and oversight bodies.

Members of the Management Board shall serve for a term of three (3) years, renewable

The specific powers, functions, quorum requirements, and operational rules of the

SECTION 9. Monitoring, Reporting, and Transparency. To uphold the principles of
accountability, good governance, and participatory oversight, the Bangsamoro Trust Fund for
Development Continuity (BTFDC) shall institutionalize robust mechanisms for monitoring,
reporting, and public transparency. For this purpose, the BTFDC shall adopt and implement
the following:

a. A digital fund tracking and reporting system, fully integrated with the Bangsamoro
Budget and Accountability Management System (BBAMS), to enable real-time visibility
of fund flows, disbursements, project statuses, and implementing agency performance,
accessible to both internal oversight and external stakeholders;
b. The preparation and submission of annual performance, financial, and audit reports to
the Bangsamoro Parliament, including:

o Statements of receipts and disbursements;

0 Project completion and output reports;

o Internal and external audit findings;

o Compliance and risk assessments;
c. The operationalization of citizen feedback, grievance redress, and community- based
monitoring tools, with data regularly published through the official BARMM
transparency portal, in accordance with open government standards and applicable data
privacy laws.

All reports, fund releases, project progress, and feedback mechanisms shall be made
publicly available in formats understandable to civil society, development partners, and the
general public, thereby strengthening public confidence and participatory oversight.

The BTFDC shall be subject to independent third-party performance audits every three
(3) years, commissioned by the Parliament and conducted by qualified institutions accredited
by COA.

consultation with the Commission on Audit (COA), Department of Budget and Management

SECTION 11. Appropriations. An initial amount of Five Hundred Million Pesos
(P500,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated for the establishment and operationalization of the
Bangsamoro Trust Fund for Development Continuity (BTFDC). This amount shall be charged
against the Continuing Appropriation Fund of the Bangsamoro Government under the current
fiscal year.

Thereafter, such amounts as may be necessary for the continued implementation,
management, and expansion of the BTFDC shall be appropriated annually through the
Bangsamoro Appropriations Act (BAA), based on the Fund’s projected requirements,
utilization performance, and strategic development priorities.

The appropriated funds shall be released in accordance with the budgeting, accounting,
and auditing rules prescribed by the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM)
and the Commission on Audit (COA), and shall be subject to the fiduciary guidelines of the
BTFDC Management Board.

The total amount retained in the BTFDC shall not exceed five percent (5%) of the total
regional budget for the current fiscal year unless expressly authorized by the Bangsamoro
Parliament via special appropriation law. This limitation is imposed to ensure fiscal discipline,
prevent fund hoarding, and maintain the integrity of the annual budgeting process, without
undermining long-term development programming.

SECTION 12. Separability Clause. If any provision or part of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, such declaration shall not affect
the validity and enforceability of the other provisions hereof, which shall remain in full force
and effect.
