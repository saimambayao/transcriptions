Explanatory Note

The Bangsamoro Islamic Armed Forces (BIAF) played a pivotal role in the decades-long
armed struggle for self-determination, justice, and peace in the Bangsamoro. Many of BIAF
fighters made the ultimate sacrifice in pursuit of these aspirations. Their legacy is etched into the
very foundations of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), and
their families continue to bear the weight of that sacrifice.

This proposed measure seeks to institutionalize a one-time death benefit for the families
of BIAF Shaheed, as a tangible expression of the Bangsamoro Government’s recognition and
gratitude. It also establishes the BIAF Shaheed Benefit Investment Fund (BSBIF), a sustainable
mechanism to ensure the long-term viability of the program through Shariah-compliant
investments.

By providing structured financial assistance and honoring the memory of those who gave
their lives for the Bangsamoro cause, this bill affirms our collective commitment to justice,

historical reconciliation, and inclusive development.

Approval of this measure is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) in Parliament assembled:

Section 1. Title. - This Act shall be known as the “BIAF Shaheed Benefit Act of
2025.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to
recognize the sacrifices of the Bangsamoro Islamic Armed Forces (BIAF) Shaheed who
dedicated their lives to the struggle for self-determination and peace in the Bangsamoro. In
recognition of their sacrifice and service, the Bangsamoro Government shall extend financial
assistance to the families of Shaheed BIAF fighters who died in combat during the armed
struggle.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. - For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall be
defined as follows:

a) BIAF Shaheed — Refers to a BIAF fighter who died in combat or during war-related
operations in the pursuit of the Bangsamoro right to self-determination, prior to the
signing of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB), as verified by
the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS).

b) Beneficiary — Refers to the surviving spouse, children, or parents of the BIAF
Shaheed.

c) BIAF Shaheed Benefit Investment Fund (BSBIF) — the dedicated investment
account established under this Act to support the grant of benefits.

Section 4. Coverage. - This Act shall apply to the beneficiaries of BIAF Shaheed who
died in combat or during war-related operations prior to the signing of the Comprehensive
Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB).

The MPOS, in coordination with the Office of the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro
Human Rights Commission (BHRC), shall create and maintain an official list of recognized
Shaheed, which shall be updated annually. The recognition as Shaheed by the MPOS shall be
based on verified records, organizational documentation, Certification by the Moro Islamic
Liberation Front, and community validation.

Section 5. Eligibility. — The beneficiary of a BIAF Shaheed, as verified by the MPOS,
shall be entitled to financial assistance under this Act.

Section 6. Benefit Amount. — The one-time financial assistance shall range from Twenty
Thousand Pesos (PhP20,000.00) to One Hundred Thousand Pesos (PhP100,000.00), based on
the rank or position held by the Shaheed at the time of death, as follows:

The MPOS, in coordination with the Office of the Chief Minister, shall validate the rank

or position of each Shaheed based on official records, testimonies, and organizational

Section 7. Claim Process. — Eligible beneficiaries shall submit documentary proof of
service and casualty to the MPOS. Upon verification, the MPOS shall endorse the claim to the
BTO for processing and payment.

All claims must be filed within ten (10) years from the effectivity of the Implementing
Rules and Regulations (IRR), unless extended by the Bangsamoro Parliament for compelling
reasons. Claims filed beyond this period may be entertained upon approval of the MPOS,
provided that the delay is due to compelling humanitarian or administrative circumstances.

The MPOS shall undertake public information campaigns to ensure that eligible
beneficiaries are informed of the claims process.

Section 8. Establishment of the BSBIF. — The Ministry of Finance, and Budget, and
Management (MFBM) shall establish and maintain the BIAF Shaheed Benefit Investment Fund
(BSBIF), with an initial seed fund of One Hundred Million Pesos (PhP100,000,000.00) sourced
from the Bangsamoro Government’s annual budget. Additional contributions may be allocated
annually as determined by the MFBM.

Section 9. Investment Reserve. — Fifty percent (50%) of the initial seed fund shall be
reserved exclusively for investment purposes, in accordance with this Act and subject to
guidelines issued by the Bangsamoro Treasury Office (BTO).

Section 10. Permissible Investments. — The BTO shall invest the BSBIF in Shariah-
compliant instruments, including but not limited to Sukuk (Islamic bonds), Islamic microfinance
ventures, and Shariah-compliant securities listed under the Philippine Stock Exchange.

Section 11. Duration of the BSBIF. — The BSBIF shall remain active for fifteen (15)
years from the effectivity of this Act, unless extended by the Bangsamoro Parliament upon
recommendation of the MFBM.

Section 12. Disposition of Remaining Funds. — Upon expiration of the BSBIF’s
duration, any remaining balance shall be returned to the Bangsamoro Treasury unless reallocated
by the Bangsamoro Parliament for programs aligned with the objectives of this Act. Disposition
shall be determined through a public audit and consultation process, with a final report submitted
to the Parliament.

Section 13. Implementing Agency. — The MFBM and the MPOS shall jointly implement
and enforce the provisions of this Act.

Within three (3) months from its effectivity, the MFBM and MPOS shall promulgate the
necessary Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) in consultation with relevant stakeholders,
and shall establish an inter-agency coordination mechanism to ensure consistent implementation,
policy harmonization, and compliance monitoring.

Section 14. Separability Clause. — If any part or provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full
force and effect.

Section 15. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication

in the Bangsamoro Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation within the BARMM.
