Explanatory Note

As a Muslim region, it is a policy of the Bangsamoro Government to regulate the
operations and maintenance of business establishments in the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region.

Among these business establishments is the operation and maintenance of

Muslim restaurants and other food establishments within the jurisdiction of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.

Halal foods are foods that Muslims are allowed to eat, drink, and consume.
Shariah or Islamic Law specifies what foods are allowed and how these foods must be

prepared.

Halal certification gives assurance to the consumers that the food products are
produced according to the Shariah Law, and that they are also clean, hygienic,
environmentally-friendly, and respects animal welfare.!

Halal food is now a growing business industry in the BARMM economy,
particularly in the urban centers of the autonomous region. To protect the health and
well-being of BARMM constituents, it is the intention of this proposed bill to require
Muslim restaurants and other food establishments within BARMM to secure Halal
certification from appropriate agency of the government and penalties should be
provided for the absence and/or violation thereof.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of this legislative measure is earnestly

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in session assembled:

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as “ Halal Food Certification Act
of 2022.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the declared policy of Bangsamoro
Government to promote, protect, and ensure the general welfare of the Bangsamoro
people and other inhabitants in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall require halal food
certification from every Muslim restaurant and food establishment within the BARMM.

Section 4. Persons Covered. - Any sole proprietor, partnership, cooperative,
association, corporation or any juridical entity, either by franchise or not, engaged in
Muslim restaurant or eatery business within the Bangsamoro Region.

Section 5. Securing Halal Certification. - Any person covered by this Act shall
secure a halal certification from any Halal accrediting body before the end of January
of every current year.

The accrediting body having jurisdiction to provide inspection and
certification services shall evaluate the application received in accordance with the
prescribed procedure within fifteen (15) days upon receipt. A passed or a failed
certification will be issued to the applicant and shall be valid for one (1) year.

Further, this certification shall be posted in an area visible to customers and
consumers and shall be indicated in the menu of the establishment.

Section 6. Application Fee. - Upon application, a fee shall be collected from
any person covered by this Act in the amount not exceeding five hundred pesos
(P500.00).

This collection shall be monitored by the Ministry of Trade Investments and
Tourism - Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (MITI-

BARMM).

Section 7. General Guidelines. - For the purpose of this Act, the following
guidelines in the preparation, handling, storage, and verification of halal products
shall serve as basic requirements for certification are hereby established:


============================================================

a) The food or product or their ingredients must not contain any component
of animals that are prohibited under Shariah or animals that are not
slaughtered according to Shariah;

b) The food or product must not contain ingredients that are
considered haram by Shariah;

c) The food or product must not be prepared, processed, or manufactured
using equipment or facilities that have been contaminated with haram
(forbidden) ingredients; and

d) During its preparation, processing, storage, or transportation, the food or
product must be fully separated from and must not come in contact with any
other food or product that has been decreed haram by Shariah or does not
meet the requirements stated in items (a), (b), or (c) above.

These guidelines shall be observed together with the existing Philippine
Standards for halal certification, without prejudice to accredited bodies adopting
stricter and more comprehensive guidelines or requirements for the issuance of
certifications to applicants.

Section 8. Prohibited Acts. - It shall be unlawful for any sole proprietor,
partnership, or corporation, either by franchise or not, engaged in the operation of a
Muslim restaurant or any food business, to:

a) Knowingly use, indicate, or post a halal passed certification in any place
visible to the public or to do such acts as to make it appear or convey that
the food establishment is halal-compliant or that it is serving halal-
compliant food, without the required certification from any halal
accredited body; and

b) _Falsely represent any food sold, prepared, served, or offered for sale to be
a halal without the proper halal certification.

Section 9. Administration. - This Act shall be administered by the MTTT-
BARMM as part of the promotion of halal awareness in the entire Bangsamoro Region.

Section 10. Penalties. - Any violation of Section 7 or 8 of this Act committed by
any establishment shall be a ground for the cancellation of its halal certification. The
suspension of operations of the violator(s) for a period not exceeding six (6) months
or cancellation of their licenses or permits to conduct business, may also be
recommended by the appropriate government agency, depending on the gravity of
the infractions;

Violation of Section 7 hereof shall also constitute the crime of false description
or representation punishable under the provisions of paragraph 2, Article 189 of the
Revised Penal Code of the Philippines.

A violation of Section 8 hereof shall constitute the crime of falsification by
a private individual and use of falsified documents, punishable under Article 172
of the Revised Penal Code of the Philippines with a punishment of prision coreccional

The criminal penalty shall be imposed upon the owner of the establishment

cooperative or other juridical entities, the penalty provided for by law shall be
imposed upon the directors, officers, employees or other persons therein responsible

Section 11. Sanctions. Any covered person or entity that failed to secure
a certification, whether a passed or a failed, shall pay a fine of five hundred pesos
(P500.00) per day, until this Act has been complied with.

Section 12. Transitory Provision. - Those covered persons having an existing
certification from accredited bodies as halal-compliant before the effectivity of this
Act may continue to use such certification until December of its effectivity. Thereafter,
those concerned shall be obliged to secure a certification on or before the last day
of January of the current year.

Section 13. Appropriations. - Initially, the funds needed to carry out the
provisions of this Act shall be at least five percent (5%) of the funds allotted to the
MITTT- BARMM which are intended for the said purpose.

Section 14. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, executive orders, rules or
regulations, or any part thereof, which are inconsistent with this Act, are hereby
repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

Section 15. Separability Clause. - If any provision or part of this Act is held
invalid or unconstitutional by a competent court, the remainder of this Act or the
provisions not otherwise affected shall remain valid and subsisting.

Section 16. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days

following the completion of its publication in a newspaper of general circulation in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
