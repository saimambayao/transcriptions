SEC. 3. Definition of Terms. — As used in this Act, the following terms will be taken to mean as:

a.

Transitional justice and reconciliation is the full range of processes and mechanisms
associated with a society’s attempt to come to terms with a past of violent conflict
characterized by a legacy of large-scale repression, human rights violation, and other
abuses, with the end view of ensuring accountability, serving justice, and achieving
reconciliation. These may include both judicial and non-judicial mechanisms, with or
without international involvement, such as individual prosecutions, reparations, truth-

seeking, institutional reform, vetting and dismissals, or a combination thereof.

Dealing with the past is a framework of conflict transformation, for societies undergoing
political and social transitions following a past of gross human rights violations, that is
centered on the right to know or truth-seeking, right to justice or accountability, right to
reparations or socio-economic justice, and guarantee of non-recurrence or institutional

reforms that dismantle impunity and strengthen the rule of law.

Legitimate grievances are those social, economic, political, cultural, and other forms of
harm suffered, collectively or individually, by the Bangsamoro people and other
indigenous peoples in the region throughout the different points of their history as a
result of systematic and wide-scale state-sponsored or state-sanctioned commission of
gross human rights violation, repression, subjugation, discrimination, and other forms of
injustices as part of the imposition of a monolithic Filipino identity and Philippine State,

and which led to the marginalization of the peoples in their own homeland.

Human rights violations are transgressions, whether state-sponsored or -sanctioned or

independently committed by non-state actors, of internationally and constitutionally

SEC. 5. Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Program. — There shall be a
comprehensive regional program on transitional justice and reconciliation to be called the
Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Program, hereinafter “the Program,” that
will, as much as practicable and to the maximum extent allowed by the authorities and
jurisdiction of the Bangsamoro Government under the Constitution and the BOL, serve as the
regional mechanism to aid in addressing the legitimate grievances, correcting historical
injustices, redressing human rights violations, and setting to right the marginalization through
land dispossession suffered by the Bangsamoro people and other indigenous peoples in the

region, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the CAB.

The Program shall build on the recommendations of the TJRC in their 2016 report and shall form
part of but not supplant the national program for transitional justice and reconciliation referred
to in the CAB. Provided, That, consistent with the TJRC report, the Program shall be guided by
the dealing with the past framework and shall ensure that the right to know, right to justice, right
to reparations, and the guarantee of non-recurrence shall be the hallmarks of the policies,
programs, and initiatives thereunder, ultimately towards healing the wounds of conflict and

realizing reconciliation for all.

Human rights-based. The Program shall be guided by the human rights principles of
universality, indivisibility, equality and non-discrimination, participation, and
accountability and shall focus on developing at the individual, community, and
institutional levels the capacities of the duty-bearers to meet their obligations and the

rights-holders to claim their rights.

Victim-centered. The Program shall place, at the forefront of all efforts and policies
thereunder, the rights and dignity of the victims or survivors, their well-being and safety,
and meeting their needs and wishes. As such, the Program shall ensure that the victims
or survivors are protected from harm, that no further harm is inflicted upon them as a
result of the Program, and that all is done to help the victims or survivors as regards issues
and challenges affecting them that have come to light in the course of the implementation

of the Program.

Future-oriented. The attainment of the objectives of the Program shall be done in a
manner that ensures non-recurrence, introduces institutional reforms, builds trust and
fosters harmony, and, as far as practicable, avoids breeding and rekindling hatred and
animosities but focuses on healing and reconciliation among peoples and communities,
without compromising on the full recognition and formal documentation of the events of

the past.

Sensitive to Bangsamoro and Filipino contexts. The Program shall prioritize the use of
indigenous methodologies and processes for knowledge gathering, conflict resolution,
and healing and reconciliation, and shall ensure recognition and respect for the cultural

and religious sensibilities of the Bangsamoro and other indigenous communities.

People-owned. The Program shall employ the bottom-up approach in policy- and decision-
making, allowing for the active participation of the communities, doing away with the
usual formalities, when necessary, thereby ensuring that the accomplishments of the

Program emanate and enjoy wide acceptance from the stakeholders.

SEC. 8. Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Commission. — There is hereby
created an independent Bangsamoro Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Commission,
hereinafter “BTJRC’. The BTJRC shall design and formulate the plans and mechanisms to
implement and supervise the Program. For this purpose, the BTJRC shall adopt its own
institutional guidelines and rules of procedure, which shall ensure the active participation of

stakeholders without being restricted by the usual formalities of law and judicial processes.

SEC. 9. Fiscal Autonomy. - The BTJRC shall have fiscal autonomy. It shall be provided with
adequate funding, which shall be regularly and automatically released to it, to finance its
operations. Annual appropriation of funds of the BTJRC shall not be reduced from the amount
received in the preceding fiscal year. The compensation, emoluments, privileges, or any other
benefits enjoyed by the Chair, Commissioners, and all its officials and employees under this Act

shall not be decreased.

SEC. 10. Composition. - The BTJRC shall be composed of five (5) regular members who are all
descendants of native inhabitants of Mindanao and the Sulu archipelago, or their spouses. Upon
the recommendation of relevant agencies, non-government organizations, and/or academic

institutions, the following shall be appointed by the Chief Minister:

a. Chair, who is a recognized expert in the field of transitional justice;

b. Commissioner on Human Rights Violations, who has a track record in human rights
promotion and protection, peace advocacy, or advancement of the rule of law;

c. Commissioner on Land Dispossession, who has extensive experience in working on
land issues;

d. Commissioner on History, Memory, and Truth, who is an expert on Bangsamoro

history;

There shall also be a Commissioner from Civil Society Organizations (CSO), who shall be appointed

from among the members of the CSO Forum as provided under Section 15 of this Act.

SEC. 15. CSO Forum. — There shall be created a Civil Society Organization Forum, hereinafter
“Forum,” that will be the platform for regular engagement by the BTJRC with civil society and
grassroots organizations in all matters pertaining to transitional justice and reconciliation for the
Bangsamoro.

The Forum shall ensure the representation of all relevant civil society, peoples, and other non-
governmental organizations and shall promulgate its own internal rules of procedure to foster
the widest participation of the stakeholders in all official acts of the Forum.

SEC. 16. Meetings; Regular and Special. - The BTJRC shall regularly meet at least once every
three (3) months. Upon determination of a majority of the members of the BTJRC, such meetings
may be made open to the public. A notice of the meeting clearly indicating the matters for
discussion shall be sent to the members at least three (3) working days before the regular

meeting.

Upon call of the Chair, at least three Commissioners, or a resolution of the CSO Forum, special
meetings may likewise be held to discuss specific issues which shall be clearly identified in the
request to call for such a special meeting sent by the Commissioners or the CSO Forum to the

Chair.

SEC. 17. Duration of the BTJRC. — The BTJRC shall operate for a period of seven (7) years from its
constitution. It shall be deemed constituted upon the appointment and qualification of its first
set of Chair and Commissioners. It may continue to operate for an additional three (3) years upon
the determination of the Chief Minister, on the last year of the seven years, that such extension
is necessary for the fulfillment of the mandate of the BTJRC and to serve the ends of transitional

justice and reconciliation for the Bangsamoro.

SEC. 18. Role of the BLA. — The BTJRC shall turn over to the Bangsamoro Library and Archives

all records and official documents/reports it has gathered prior to its dissolution.

SEC. 19. Role of the BCPCH. - The Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural

Heritage shall maintain the museums established as a result of the implementation of this Act.

SEC. 20. Appropriations. — For the initial operating expenses of the BTJRC to carry out the
mandate of this Act, the amount of Fifty Million Pesos (P50,000,000.00) is hereby appropriated
from the Contingent Fund. Thereafter, its regular budget shall be included in the General

Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

SEC. 21. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — Within sixty (60) days from the approval of this
Act, the Office of the Chief Minister shall promulgate rules and regulations implementing the

provisions of this Act.

SEC. 22. National Transitional Justice and Reconciliation Program for the Bangsamoro. - This
Act shall neither substitute nor supersede the national government’s obligation to create and
implement a program for transitional justice and reconciliation in conjunction with the
Bangsamoro Government as provided in the BOL and the CAB. Upon the creation of the
transitional justice and reconciliation program for the Bangsamoro at the national level, the
official output of the BTJRC shall become part of the former’s official records and documentation,
and shall be treated as official recommendations of the Bangsamoro thereto, without prejudice
to additional recommendations, decisions, and action points that the latter may thereafter make

and submit.

In the event that such a program at the national level is not created and implemented by the
national government for the duration of the BTJRC’s existence, the latter shall grant the former
full rights to adopt all its official findings and recommendations for implementation. This
adoption by the national government of the BTJRC’s official output, is further encouraged, and
shall undergo a formalization process with the Bangsamoro, to be guided by the CAB, particularly

its Annex on Normalization, with transitional justice and reconciliation as its primary goal.

SEC. 23. Transitory Provision. - Upon the constitution of the BTJRC, the Bangsamoro Human
Rights Commission shall turn over to the former all records and output emanating from the
exercise of its function as provided in Section 51, of Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 4, otherwise

known as the Bangsamoro Human Rights Act of 2019.
