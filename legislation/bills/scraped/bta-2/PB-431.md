AN ACT

EXPANDING THE SCOPE OF AUTHORITY AND SERVICES OF SPECIAL
GEOGRAPHIC AREA DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY AMENDING
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMY ACT NO. 13 OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE

PURPOSES

Section 1. Declaration of Policy. - It is hereby declared to be the policy of
the Bangsamoro Government to treat Special Geographic Area (SGA) as a special
development and administrative area and certain basic services affecting or
involving Special Geographic Area as SGA-wide services more efficiently and
effectively planned, supervised and coordinated by a development authority as
created herein, without prejudice to the autonomy of the affected local
government units.

Pursuant to this policy, Special Geographic Area, as an authority created under
Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 13, embracing the municipalities of Kadayangan,
Kapalawan, Ligawasan, Malidegao, Nabalawag, Old Kaabakan, Pahamuddin
and Tugunan is hereby constituted into a _ special development and
administrative area subject to direct supervision of the Chief Minister.

Section 2. Special Geographic Area Development Authority. - The affairs of
Special Geographic Area shall be administered by the Special Geographic Area
Development Authority, hereinafter referred to as the SGADA. The SGADA shall

Section 4. Special Geographic Area Board. - The governing board and
policy making body of the SGADA shall be the Special Geographic Area Board,
composed of the following:

a) Chairperson of SGADA;

b) Minister, Ministry of the Interior and Local Government;
c) Minister, Ministry of Finance, and Budget and Management;

d) Bangsamoro Director General, Bangsamoro Planning and Development
Authority; and

e) The President of the League of Mayors in the Special Geographic Area.

The Board shall be headed by the chairperson, who shall be appointed by
the Chief Minister and who shall continue to hold office at the discretion of the
appointing authority. He shall be vested with the rank, rights, privileges,
disqualifications, and prohibitions of a cabinet member.

The chairperson shall be assisted by an Executive Director as the head of the
implementing arm of the Authority with (a) a Deputy Executive Director for
finance and administration, (b) a Deputy Executive Director for planning and (c)
Deputy Executive Director for operations, all of whom shall be appointed by the
Chief Minister, subject to civil service laws, rules and regulations. They shall
enjoy security of tenure and may be removed for cause in accordance with law.

The Deputy Executive Director for planning must have not less than five (5)
years extensive experience in development and planning or must hold a master's
degree in rural development or similar disciplines.

The chairperson and members of the Board shall be entitled to allowance and per
diems in accordance with existing policies, rules and regulations on the matter.

Section 5. Functions and Powers of the Special Geographic Area
Development Authority. — The SGADA shall:

a. Formulate, coordinate and regulate the implementation of medium and
long-term plans and programs for the delivery of SGA-wide services,
land use and physical development within Special Geographic Area,
consistent with Bangsamoro Development Plan objectives and
priorities;

b. Prepare, coordinate and regulate the implementation of medium-term
investment programs for SGA-wide services which shall indicate
sources and uses of funds for priority programs and projects, and which
shall include the packaging of projects and presentation to funding
institutions;

the delivery of specific services under its jurisdiction, subject to the

Section 6. Functions of the Chairperson. - The Chairperson shall:

a.

Appoint, subject to civil service laws, rules and regulations, all
subordinate officers and employees, with Salary Grade not higher than
24, who shall enjoy security of tenure and may be removed only for
cause in accordance with law. The Chairperson is hereby authorized to
engage the services of experts/consultants either on full time or part-
time basis, as may be required in the performance of his functions and
duties as may be determined by him;

submission to the Board:

Submit for consideration of the Board such other policies and measures
as he may deem necessary to carry out the purposes and provisions of

staffing pattern and fix the number of subordinate and employees of
the SGADA; and exercise the power to discipline subordinate officials
and employees under the provisions of law;

Prepare an annual report on the accomplishments of the SGADA at the
close of each calendar year for submission to the Board and to the Chief
Minister; and

Perform such other duties as may be assigned to him by the Chief
Minister or by the Board.

Section 7. Functions of the Executive Director. - The Executive Director
shall:

a. Assist the Chairperson in the administration of the SGADA and
supervision of subordinate personnel;

b. Assist the Chairperson in the supervision of the operation of the
various operating centers and units of SGADA:;

c. Assist the Chairperson in the review of plans and programs for the
SGADA and for Special Geographic Area in the preparation of the
annual report of activities and accomplishments of the SGADA; and

d. Perform such other duties and functions as may be lawfully delegated
or assigned by the Chairperson from time to time.

Section 8. Jnstitutional Linkages of the SGADA. - The SGADA shall, in
carrying out its functions, consult, coordinate and work closely with the LGUS,
the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, the Bangsamoro
government ministries, offices and agencies, and accredited people's
organizations (POs), nongovernmental organizations (NGOs), and the private
sector operating in Special Geographic Area.

The SGADA shall have a master plan that shall serve as the framework for the
Bangsamoro ministries, offices and agencies operation and strategic plan, and
local development plans of the component LGUs.

The SGADA shall submit its development plans and investment programs to the
BPDA for integration into the Bangsamoro Development Plan and public
investment program.

The implementation of the SGADA's plans, programs, and projects shall be
undertaken by the LGUs, the concerned Bangsamoro government ministries,
offices and agencies, the POs, NGOs and the SGADA itself where appropriate.
For this purpose, the SGADA may enter into contracts, memoranda of agreement
and other cooperative arrangements with these bodies for the delivery of the
required services within Special Geographic Area.

The SGADA shall, in coordination with the BPDA and the Ministry of Finance,
and Budget and Management, interface with the foreign assistance agencies for
purposes of obtaining financing support, grants and donations in support of its
programs and projects.

Section 11. Zransitory Provisions. - To prevent disruption in the delivery of
basic services pending the full implementation of the SGADA's organizational
structure and staffing pattern, all officials and employees of the interim SGADA
shall continue to exercise their duties and functions and receive their salaries
and allowances until they shall have been given notice of change of duties and
functions, and of being transferred to another office or position.

All assets and properties presently in use or under the accountability of the
interim SGADA and all its obligations, indebtedness, or liabilities shall be
transferred to and assumed by the SGADA expanded under this Act, subject to
the conditions that may be established by the Ministry of Finance, and Budget
and Management, Office of the Chief Minister, and Commission on Audit.

The civil service laws, rules and regulations pertinent to the displacement of
personnel affected by this Act shall be strictly enforced. The Bangsamoro
government shall provide such amounts as may be necessary to pay the benefits
accruing to displaced employees at the rate of one and one-fourth (1 1/4) month's
salary for every year of service: Provided, That, if qualified for retirement under
existing retirement laws, said employees may opt to receive the benefits
thereunder.

Section 12. Repealing Clause. — All other laws, decrees, executive orders,
rules and regulations, or parts thereof inconsistent with or contrary to the
provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

Section 13. Separability Clause. - If any part or provision of this Act is
held unconstitutional or invalid, other parts or provisions thereof which are not
affected shall continue to remain in full force and effect.

Section 14. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days
following completion of its publication in at least two (2) newspapers of regional

circulation.
