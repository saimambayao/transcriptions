EXPLANATORY NOTE

Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL),
provides that it shall be the top priority of the Bangsamoro Government to establish,
maintain, and support a complete, quality, and culturally grounded educational system
that responds to the needs and aspirations of the Bangsamoro people. Section 16,
Article IX of the BOL explicitly emphasizes the government's responsibility to maintain
an integrated system of education that upholds both academic excellence and
relevance to the moral ideals of the region.

In harmony with this mandate, the Bangsamoro Education Code (BAA No. 18) requires
the integration of Islamic education into the regional curriculum, strengthening Arabic
proficiency, and institutionalizing support for Madaris and faith-based learning
institutions. The Bangsamoro Development Plan (BDP) 2023—2028 further identifies

youth formation, faith-based learning, and culturally responsive education as
instrumental pillars for peacebuilding, community development, and moral

The Salamat Hashim Boarding Madrasah Act of 2025 seeks to establish a public
boarding madrasah in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(BARMM) that integrates faith-based and culturally responsive education with the K—
12 curriculum. This initiative aligns with the BOL, Bangsamoro Education Code (BAA
18), and the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE) Public
Madrasah Framework, all of which mandate the Bangsamoro Government to provide
inclusive, quality education that reflects the region’s Islamic and cultural values. The
institution aims to develop morally upright, academically competent, and community-
rooted youth capable of contributing to peacebuilding, civic responsibility, and moral
governance.

The SHBM will offer full-time residential education for Grades 7-12, combining secular
subjects with advanced Islamic studies, Quranic and Arabic language proficiency, and
leadership formation. It prioritizes access for high-performing learners from
underserved communities while accommodating non-Muslim students with alternative
values education. Governance will be overseen by a Board of Trustees representing
government, academia, civil society, and religious authorities, with MBHTE
maintaining regulatory and budgetary authority.

The Act also provides for faculty development, scholarships through the Salamat
Hashim Scholarship Fund, safe and gender-sensitive boarding facilities, and
compliance with child protection and disaster resilience standards. Its implementation
strengthens moral governance, promotes peacebuilding, and honors the legacy of
Salamat Hashim by nurturing morally upright, academically competent, and
community-rooted youth capable of contributing to the development and stability of
BARMM.

In view of the foregoing, the jmmediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

SECTION 1. Title. This Act shall be known as the “Salamat Hashim Boarding
Madrasah Act of 2025.

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is hereby declared the policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to establish and support an integrated, inclusive, and faith-
responsive educational system consistent with the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the
Bangsamoro Education Code (BAA 18), and the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and
Technical Education (MBHTE) Public Madrasah Education Framework. The State
shall promote the development of morally upright, academically competent, and
community-rooted youth capable of contributing to peacebuilding and moral
governance.

SECTION 3. Establishment. There is hereby established a public educational
institution to be known as the Salamat Hashim Boarding Madrasah (SHBM), which
shall operate under the Public Madrasah System of the Bangsamoro Autonomous
Region pursuant to Title VII of the Bangsamoro Education Code (BAA No. 18). The

It shall include core academic subjects such as English, Mathematics, Science,

SECTION 7. Admission and Inclusivity.

a) The SHBM may admit Muslim and non-Muslim students who respect the
school's cultural educational environment.

b) At least 60% of admitted students, belonging to the top 10 of their class and
passed the competitive admission tests, shall come from underserved areas.

c) Non-Muslim students shall not be compelled to participate in religious rites and
shall be provided appropriate alternative activities.

d) Admission shall comply with MBHTE Child Protection Policies and Gender and
Development (GAD) standards.

SECTION 8. Faculty and Personnel Development.

a) Teachers of secular subjects shall meet DepEd and MBHTE standards, while
teachers of Islamic and Arabic Studies shall meet MBHTE accreditation for
Islamic education specialists. Faculty shall not be required to adhere to any
particular religious affiliation, except for teachers of Islamic subjects.

b) MBHTE, in coordination with the Board, shall provide capacity-building,
training, scholarships, and exchange programs with local and international
boarding madaris.

SECTION 9. Scholarship and Support. A Salamat Hashim Scholarship Fund is
hereby created and shall be administered by the MBHTE. The Fund shall provide
financial assistance to graduates of the Salamat Hashim Boarding Madrasah for their
further studies or continued pursuit of higher education. Support may include tuition,
lodging, meals, books, uniforms, and other basic educational needs, subject to COA
auditing rules and MBHTE scholarship guidelines.

SECTION 10. infrastructure, Boarding, and Safety Standards. The SHBM shail

a) School safety and disaster resilience
b) Boarding school facility requirements

SECTION 11. Funding. The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this
Act shall be charged against the current appropriations of the MBHTE. Thereafter,
such sums as may be necessary for its continued operation shall be included in the
annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

SECTION 12. Monitoring, Evaluation, and Accreditation. MBHTE shall conduct
annual Quality Assurance and Regulation (QAR) evaluations, accreditation, and
performance audits consistent with the Public Madrasah Education Framework.
Reports shall be submitted annually to the Parliament.

SECTION 13. Constitutional Compliance Clause. Nothing in this Act shall be
construed to establish a religion, favor or discriminate against any religious
denomination, or permit the use of public funds for sectarian activities. Identity-based
Islamic and Arabic studies under this Act shall be implemented solely within the
authorized Public Madrasah Framework of RA 11054 and BAA 18.

SECTION 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations. Within ninety (90) days from
effectivity, MBHTE shall promulgate the IRR, in consultation with the Board of Trustees
or other relevant stakeholders, necessary for the effective implementation of this Acct.

SECTION 15. Separability Clause. If any provision of this Act is declared invalid or
unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION 16. Repealing Clause. All inconsistent laws, rules, or regulations are
hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

SECTION 17. Effectivity. This Act shail take effect fifteen (15) days after publication
in the Bangsamoro Gazette or a newspaper of regional circulation.
