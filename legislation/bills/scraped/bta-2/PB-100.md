EXPLANATORY NOTE

Sibutu is a municipality in the province of Tawi-Tawi with a land area of 109 square kilometers anda
population of approximately 34,243 who are mostly boat builders and seaweed farmers. It lies about
14 k.m. east of the coast of Sabah, Malaysia. The municipality was created out of Sitangkai, Tawi-
Tawi, by virtue of Muslim Mindanao Autonomy Act No. 197, which was subsequently ratified in a
piebiscite heid on October Zi, Z006. it has a poverty incidence of 26.26% per Philippine Statistics
Authority. The only hospital in the municipality is Datu Alawaddin T. Bandon, Sr. Municipal Hospital
which is a level | hospital with ten (10) bed capacity. The management of the hospital reported that
the average bed occupancy rate was 95.89% in 2019; 85.44% in 2020; and 73% in 2021; and the
existing facilities are gravely not up to standards and needs of its people. The next closest medical
facility is the Datu Sakilan Memorial Hospital in the Municipality of Bongao which is around 51.7

week. In case of serious medical emergency, our constituents in that remote area not only have to
wait for a scheduled trip to Bongao, but would also have to endure the distress and perils of traveling
by sea for 3 hours. Since Datu Halun Sakilan Memorial Hospital is also a level | hospital with bed
occupancy rate of 150 to 200 percent, in most cases, they would have to travel to Zamboanga City for
proper medical attention. This has caused tremendous hardships and numerous fatalities for the

Section 8, Article IX of the Bangsamoro Organic Law states that “the Bangsamoro Government shall
provide, maintain, and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs... to the
Bangsamoro people and other inhabitants of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.” Section 22,
Article IX of the same law further enunciates that “the Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy
on heaith that provides for a comprehensive and integrated heaith service delivery for its
constituents. It shall establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of
its people and ensure that the individual's basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt

intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.”

Therefore, it is incumbent upon the Bangsamoro Government as part of its core mandate to afford
the people of Sibutu quality medical facilities and services by upgrading the only hospital in that
municipaiity from ievei i of ten (iG) bed capacity to ievei ii of (50) bed capacity.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

“AN ACT UPGRADING THE DATU ALAWADDIN T. BANDON, SR. MEMORIAL HOSPITAL IN
THE MUNICIPALITY OF SIBUTU, PROVINCE OF TAWI-TAWI FROM ITS PRESENT LEVEL | OF
TEN (10) BED CAPACITY TO LEVEL II GENERAL HOSPITAL OF FIFTY (50) BED CAPACITY AND
APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES”

SECTION 1. Creation. — The Datu Alawaddin T. Bandon Sr. Municipal Hospital was created by
Republic Act No. 6853 establishing a ten (10) bed municipal hospital in the Municipality of
Sitangkai, Province of Tawi-Tawi on February 24, 1990.

SECTION 2. Change of Location. — Republic Act No. 6853 provides that Datu Alawaddin T.
Bandon, Sr. Municipal Hospital is located in the Municipality of Sitangkai. However, Muslim
Mindanao Act 197 created the Municipality of Sibutu out of the Municipality of Sitangkai.
Hence, the location of the hospital shall be in the Municipality of Sibutu.

SECTION 3. Upgrading of the Hospital. —- The upgrading of the Datu Alawaddin T. Bandon, Sr.
Municipal Hospital from its existing ten (10) bed capacity municipal hospital to a Level II
general hospital with fifty (50) bed capacity shall be consistent with the prescribed standards
set by the Ministry of Health (MOH) of the Bangsamore Government. It shall integrate into
its system of operations and services the relevant provisions of Republic Act No. 11223,
otherwise known as the Universal Health Care Act.

SECTION 4. Services. The existing health service facilities and professional manpower of the
Datu Alawaddin T. Bandon, Sr. Municipal Hospital are hereby upgraded and increased to
conform with the standards of Level Il general hospital and to ensure that the operating
systems of the hospital keep up with the advancement in science and technology.

The information system of the hospital shall be streamlined to create a complete patients’
information in a simplified and easily comprehensible format. It can syne and share the
inputs of doctors and authorized personnel among departments and offices seamlessly,
generating clear and instantaneous communication. This will help the doctors and other
medical personnel to confidently do their iob and serve the patients better.

SECTION 5. Organization; Administrative Supervision. - The MOH, in coordination with the
Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management (MFBM) shall determine and approve its
appropriate organizational structure and plantilla positions to meet the prescribed standards
of the upgraded level of the hospital. It shall supervise and monitor the administration and
management of the hospital.

SECTION 6. Appropriations. — The Ministry of Finance, Budget and Management shall
provide annual funding for the plantilla positions and the maintenance and other operating
expenses of the hospital in the annual General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro.

SECTION 7. Separability Clause. — \f any provision or part of this act is declared invalid, the
remaining provisions or parts not affected shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 8. Repealing Clause. — All regional laws, executive orders, issuances, rules, and
regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified
accordingly.
