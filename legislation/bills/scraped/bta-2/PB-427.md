EXPLANATORY NOTE

Sound public financial management is fundamental to effective governance. In
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM), the
growing scale and complexity of public expenditures require systems that ensure
transparency, accountability, and timely access to accurate financial information.
Strengthening mechanisms for monitoring the allocation, release, and utilization
of public funds is essential to sustaining public trust in autonomous governance.

The establishment of the Bangsamoro Government embodies a higher moral
standard of leadership rooted in Islamic principles. Central to these is Amanah,
the trust placed upon public officials to manage resources on behalf of the people.
This trust imposes both a legal responsibility and a moral obligation to safeguard
public funds and ensure their proper use.

To uphold this obligation, financial governance must move beyond conventional
reporting frameworks and adopt systems that are verifiable, traceable, and
resistant to manipulation. A public financial ledger anchored on blockchain
technology offers a secure and immutable mechanism for recording government

financial transactions. Through this system, the Bangsamoro Government will be
able to: (a) track every peso from appropriation to audit; (b) record

The proposed Amanah Chain (A-CHAIN) system embodies the belief that
public funds are a trust that must be guarded with utmost integrity. It
strengthens transparency, reinforces accountability, and ensures that the
financial governance of BARMM lives up to its moral commitment to the people.

This measure is not a copy of any national proposal. While inspired by the
broader national demanc to confront corruption and protect public funds,
A-CHAIN is designed specifically for the political, cultural, and administrative
realities of the Bangsamoro, consistent with its autonomy and its vision of Moral

Governance.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session
assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Amanah Chain Act of
2025” or the “A-CHAIN Act.”

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy.

The Bangsamoro Government, guided by the Bangsamoro Organic Law and the
doctrine of Moral Governance, recognizes its obligation to uphold Amanah—the
principle of trust, integrity, and accountability —in all dealings involving public
funds.

To address recurring issues in financial management, including fragmented
systems, delayed reporting, and weak public access to information, the
Bangsamoro Government shall adopt secure and transparent technologies that
ensure verifiable and tamper-resistant financial records.

For this purpose, the Amanah Chain (A-CHAIN) is hereby established as the
official blockchain-based public ledger for recording and tracking budget

allocations, releases, procurement activities, project milestones, and
disbursements across all ministries and local government units within BARMM.

SEC. 3. Definition of Terms.

3.1 Amanah Chain (A-CHAIN) - the official blockchain-based public ledger of
BARMM for recording and verifying financial and procurement
transactions.

3.2 Blockchain — a decentralized and cryptographically secured ledger that
permanently records transactions in immutable blocks.

3.3 Digital Public Finance Asset (DPFA) — any budget, financial, audit,
or procurement record uploaded into A-CHAIN.

3.4 Smart Amanah Contract (SAC) — automated protocols in A CHAIN that
trigger fund releases or procurement actions upon compliance with set
requirements.

3.5 Validator Nodes — institutions or servers authorized to verify and secure
transactions, such as the Bangsamoro Treasury Office, COA-BARMM, and
accredited universities and civil society organizations.

3.6 Participating Entities — ministries, offices, agencies, and LGUs required to
integrate their systems with A-CHAIN.

SEC. 4. Establishment of the A-CHAIN System.

A-CHAIN is hereby established as the decentralized blockchain-based
platform of the Bangsamoro Government. It shall:

4.1 Record all financial transactions,

4.2 Track fund utilization,

4.3 Document procurement activities,

4.4 Provide public access to non-confidential entries, and 4.5
Ensure immutability and traceability of all records.

A public A-CHAIN Transparency Portal shall be created for access by
citizens, researchers, and civil society groups.

SEC. 5. Mandatory Integration.

All ministries, offices, agencies, provinces, cities, municipalities, and
barangays in BARMM shall integrate their financial and procurement
systems with A-CHAIN, including:

5.1 Budget preparation,
5.2 Allotments and cash releases,
5.3 Disbursement reports,

5.4 Procurement processes,
5.5 Project milestones, and
5.6 Audit observations.

Failure to upload required records shall constitute administrative
liability, without prejudice to applicable civil or criminal actions.

SEC. 6. Features and Standards.

A-CHAIN shall have at least the following:
6.1 Immutable records,
6.2 Decentralized validation,
6.3 Full audit trails,
6.4 Interoperability with regional and national platforms, 6.5
Public access to non-confidential data,
6.6 Cybersecurity safeguards, and
6.7 Compliance with Islamic governance principles.

SEC. 7. Duties of Implementing Bodies.

7.1 Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM)

7.1.1 Lead implementor,
7.1.2 Ensure integration and accuracy,
7.1.3 Issue compliance guidelines.

8.1 Bangsamoro Information and Communications Technology Office
(BICTO)

8.1.1 Develop and maintain A-CHAIN,
8.1.2 Manage validator nodes,
8.1.3 Ensure security and interoperability.

8.2 Commission on Audit - BARMM

8.2.1 Use A-CHAIN for audit trails,
8.2.2 Enforce audit rules,
8.2.3 Recommend improvements.

8.3 All Ministries and LGUs

8.3.1 Upload required records,
8.3.2 Use Smart Amanah Contracts,

8.4 Transparency and Public Oversight

The A-CHAIN Transparency Portal shall publish real-time budget use,
procurement information, project locations, and citizen monitoring
reports.

SEC. 9. Pilot Implementation.
A-CHAIN shall initially be piloted in:

9.1 One infrastructure-intensive ministry,
9.2 One social service ministry,

9.3 One province, and

9.4 One city or municipality.

SEC. 10. Appropriations.

Funds needed for A-CHAIN shall be sourced from the Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act, the block grant, the Special Development Fund, and other
lawful sources.

SEC. 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations.

Within ninety (90) days, MFBM and BICTO, in coordination with COA BARMM,
shall issue the IRR.

SEC. 12. Separability Clause.

If any provision of this Act is declared invalid, the remaining provisions shall
remain in full force.

SEC. 13. Effectivity.

This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in the Bangsamoro
Gazette or a newspaper of regional circulation.
