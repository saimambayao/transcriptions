Explanatory Note

The 1987 Philippine Constitution, in Section 15, Article II, declares that the state shall
protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among
them. Section 11, Article XIII declares that the State shall adopt an integrated and
comprehensive approach to health development, making essential goods and services accessible
to all at an affordable cost.

In the Bangsamoro Region, It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government under Section
22, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” to provide for a comprehensive and
integrated health service delivery for its constituents and establish by law a general hospital
system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to
life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellence and affordable medical
services. In pursuit of this, the government shall prioritize the upgrading of existing health
facilities to provide life-saving and basic medical care.

In the heart of the Bangsamoro Region, the Municipality of Matanog in Maguindanao
Del Norte stands as a resilient community striving for peace, stability, and inclusive
development. With a previous total population of 36,034 residents as of the 2020 census of the
Philippine Statistics Authority, Matanog has long faced systemic challenges in accessing reliable
and adequate healthcare. Its rugged terrain, distance from major urban centers, and limited
government infrastructure have all contributed to the persistent health disparities experienced by
its people. The Municipality has a total of 8 Barangays, most of which are classified as
geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas.

In response to this need, the World Assembly of Muslim Youth (WAMY) established a
clinic in Matanog as part of its humanitarian outreach. This facility provided basic medical care
to residents who would otherwise have had to travel long distances to seek treatment at Dr.

Serapio B. Montaner, Jr. Al-Haj Memorial Hospital, located in Malabang, Iranun District
Hospital, located in Parang, or Cotabato Regional and Medical Center in Cotabato City. The
WAMY Clinic evolved into more than just a health outpost; it became a symbol of hope and
partnership in public health service delivery.

However, temporary solutions are no longer enough. According to Department of Health
(DOH) standards, a Level I hospital is typically established to serve a population of at least
10,000 to 30,000 people, depending on geographic isolation and health service accessibility.
With over 41,146 residents and growing as of the latest census, which is the 2024 census of the
Philippine Statistics Authority, Matanog exceeds the minimum population requirement and fully
qualifies for its own Level I hospital. This becomes even more urgent when we consider the
growing population from neighboring municipalities and the upland barangays that rely on
Matanog as their nearest service hub.

This proposed measure seeks to convert the WAMY Clinic into a Level I government
hospital under the Ministry of Health, BARMM. As a Level I hospital, the facility will be staffed
with qualified medical professionals and equipped with basic emergency, surgical, maternal, and
pediatric services, essential components of primary healthcare delivery. More importantly, it will
be accessible to all, particularly the poor and vulnerable, under the government’s health
insurance and social protection programs.

The conversion will not only strengthen the health system in Matanog; it will also: Spur
local employment and professional opportunities for health workers, reduce maternal and child
mortality through improved services, ease the burden on distant hospitals in Malabang, Parang,
or Cotabato City, and contribute to long-term peace and development by investing in human
dignity and well-being.

In view of the above, ensuring access to basic, quality, and affordable medical services
for the Bangsamoro People must be given priority by the BARMM Government. Essentially, this
can be realized by establishing a general hospital in identified strategic areas of the BARMM,
such as in Matanog, Maguindanao Del Norte.

Thus, in view of the foregoing, the immediate approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Interim Parliament, Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao:

Section 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Matanog District Hospital Act of 2025.”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government under Section
22, Article [IX of the Republic Act No. 11054 otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao” to provide for a comprehensive and
integrated health service delivery for its constituents and establish by law a general hospital
system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic right to
life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellence and affordable medical
services. In pursuit of this, the government shall prioritize the upgrading of existing health
facilities to provide life-saving and basic medical care.

Section 3. Conversion. The existing Matanog World Assembly of Muslim Youth (WAMY)
Clinic located in the Municipality of Matanog, Province of Maguindanao del Norte, is hereby
converted into a Level I Hospital, to be known as the Matanog District Hospital.

Section 4. Services and Bed Capacity. The Matanog District Hospital shall operate as a Level I
hospital with a ten (10)-bed capacity, and shall provide, at a minimum, the following services:

a) General medical and minor surgical services

b) Emergency and outpatient care

c) Maternal and child health services

d) Isolation services

e) Basic clinical laboratory and diagnostic services
f) Pharmacy services

Section 5. Manpower Requirement. The Ministry of Health (MOH) shall assign qualified
medical and allied health personnel to the hospital. The minimum manpower shall include a
medical officer, nurses, midwives, a Pharmacist, a medical and Radiologic technologist, and
administrative support personnel, subject to existing civil service rules and MOH manpower
standards.

Section 6. Appropriations. The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be
included in the General Appropriations Act of the Bangsamoro of the year following its
enactment into law and thereafter.

Section 7. Implementing Rules and Regulations. The Ministry of Health shall be the lead
implementing agency. It shall promulgate the necessary guidelines and administrative orders for

the full and effective implementation of this Act within sixty (60) days upon its effectivity.

Section 8. Separability Clause. If any provision of this Act is held invalid or unconstitutional,
the remaining provisions shall not be affected and shall continue to be in full force and effect.

Section 9. Repealing Clause. All laws, orders, rules, and regulations or parts thereof
inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

Section 10. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the
Bangsamoro Gazette or in a newspaper of regional circulation.
