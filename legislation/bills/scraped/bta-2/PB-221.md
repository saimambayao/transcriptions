EXPLANATORY NOTE

Agriculture hunting, forestry and fishing (AHFF) remains to have the largest share in the
region’s total economic performance at 55.6 percent contribution out of its 7.2 percent
growth rate in its Gross Regional Domestic Product (PSA-BARMM, 2018). Cassava, palay,
coconut, corn and seaweed were the top five commodities produced in the region which
contributed to the region’s agricultural output.

Maximizing the potentials of these agri-fisheries resources for regional food security and
economic prosperity, the sector needs to be modernized, mechanized and agro-
industrialized. This involve the establishment and/or provision of adequate irrigation, agri-
fisheries, machineries and equipment, post harvest facilities, farm-to-market roads and
other agri-fisheries infrastructure and facilities in all the Bangsamoro territorial areas at the
barangay levels. Over-all, this will result for inclusive growth and greatly help in the over-all
objective for a genuine and lasting peace in Bangsamoro and the whole of Mindanao. This
operationalizes Section 23 of RA No. 11054, otherwise known as the Organic Law for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao which states: “Consistent with the
provisions of Republic Act. No. 8550, otherwise known as “The Philippine Fisheries Code of
1998” as amended, and other relevant national laws, the policies and laws of the
Bangsamoro Government on agriculture as a key development strategy, promote
productivity measures, and provide support for small farmers, landholders, and marginal
fisher folk.”

The aforecited endeavours principally require the creation of the Bureau of Agricultural and
Fisheries Engineering of BARMM (BAFE-BARMM) and strengthening or establishment of
Agricultural and Biosystems Engineering (ABE) unit and the creation of corresponding
Agricultural and Biosystems Engineer positions in every province, city and municipality of
the Bangsamoro Local Government Units. This mandated under Section 46 of RA No. 8435
(Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act) Section 29 of RA No. 10601 (Agricutural and
Fisheries Mechanization Law), and Section 34 of RA No. 10915 (Philippine Agricultural and
Biosystems Engineering Act of 2016).

ARTICLE Il
Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering

SEC. 3. Creation of a Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering (BAFE) of
BARMM. - A Bureau of Agricultural and Fisheries Engineering of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BAFE-BARMM) is hereby created as a line
bureau under the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Agrarian Reform (MAFAR) and
shall have the following powers, functions and responsibilities:

(a) fisheries mechanization, farm-to-market roads, agri-fisheries infrastructure, soil and
water conservation and management, rural agro-industrialization, food logistics
infrastructures and other agricultural and biosystems engineering projects;

(b) Prepare, evaluate, validate and recommend engineering plans, designs and technical
specifications on agricultural, fisheries and biosystems mechanization, irrigation and
infrastructure projects;

(c) Formulate regional plans and programs on agricultural, fisheries and biosystems
infrastructures, irrigation, agricultural and fishery mechanization, post harvest facilities
Coordinate, oversee and monitor the regional planning and implementation of agri-
and agro-processing development in coordination with the Bureau of Agricultural and
Fisheries Engineering of the Department of Agriculture (BAFE-DA) and concerned
BARMM agencies and LGUs;

(d) Oversee and provide technical assistance to the operations of the agricultural and
biosystems engineering units/offices of the Local Government Units of BARMM;

(e) Coordinate and monitor the enforcement of the Philippine Agricultural and Biosystems
Engineering Standards and other regulatory policies on agricultural and biosystems
engineering at the BARMM territorial areas;

(f) Assist the BAFE-DA in the accreditation and registration scheme for agriculture and
fishery machinery, tools and equipment in coordination and collaboration with BAFE-
DA;

(g) Assist BAFE-DA in the issuance of permits to operate to agriculture and fishery tools
and equipment manufacturers, fabricators, assemblers and importers; and
accreditation for agricultural and fishery testing centers; and

(h) Undertake the registration, accreditation and regulation of agricultural, fisheries and
biosystems facilities, buildings and structures in coordination and collaboration with
BAFE-DA.
