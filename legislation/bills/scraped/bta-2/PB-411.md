AN ACT

PROVIDING FOR THE CREATION OF PARLIAMENTARY DISTRICTS IN THE
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO
(BARMM).

Explanatory Note

Section 15, Article X of the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides for the
creation of autonomous regions in Muslim Mindanao composed of provinces, cities,
municipalities, and geographical areas sharing common and distinctive historical and
cultural heritage, economic and social structures, and other relevant characteristics,
within the framework of the Constitution and the national sovereignty and territorial
integrity of the Republic of the Philippines.

Further, Congress enacted Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the
Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), establishing the autonomous political entity now
known as the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM). The
BOL was signed into law on July 27, 2018 by former President Rodrigo Roa Duterte,
repealing R.A. No. 6734 as amended by R.A. No. 9054.

Section 2(a), Article III of the BOL provides that the territorial jurisdiction of
the BARMM, subject to a mandatory plebiscite, shall be composed, among others, of
the then Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM) created under R.A. No.
6734, as amended by R.A. No. 9054, which includes the Province of Sulu.

A two-part plebiscite was held on January 21, 2019 for the ARMM territories,
where all provinces voted to join the new autonomous region, and on February 5-6,
2019 for the City of Cotabato and the 16 barangays of North Cotabato seeking

inclusion in the BARMM. Cotabato City and the 63 barangays of Cotabato province
voted favorably to join the BARMM.

However, on September 9, 2024, the Supreme Court promulgated its En Banc
Decision in Province of Sulu, et al. v. Medialdea, et al., G.R. Nos. 242255, 243276, and
243693. While upholding the validity of the BOL, the Court declared the Province of
Sulu not part of the BARMM.

Subsequently, the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act (BAA) No. 58, or the
Bangsamoro Parliamentary District Act of 2024, was enacted on February 28, 2024,
establishing 32 single-member parliamentary districts in the BARMM, including
seven (7) for the Province of Sulu. Pursuant to the Supreme Court decision, the BTA
enacted BAA No. 77, or the Bangsamoro Redistricting Act of 2025, amending BAA No.
58 to exclude Sulu.

Section 7(b), Article VII of the BOL provides that the Parliament may undertake
new redistricting by law to ensure a more equitable representation of constituencies
in the Parliament. Section 10 of the same Article further states:

Section 10. Redistricting for Parliamentary Membership. - The Parliament shall
have the power to reconstitute by law the parliamentary districts apportioned among
the provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographical areas of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region to ensure equitable representation in Parliament. The
redistricting, merging, or creation of parliamentary districts shall be based on the
number of inhabitants and additional provinces, cities, munictpalities, and
geographical areas, which shall become part of the Bangsamoro territorial jurisdiction.

For the purpose of redistricting, parliamentary districts shall be apportioned based on
the population and geographical area: Provided, That each district shall comprise, as
far as practicable, contiguous, compact, and adjacent territorial jurisdiction: Provided,
further, That each district shall have a population of at least one hundred thousand
(100,000).

Republic Act No. 12123 reset the first regular BARMM elections to October 13,
2025. However, in its En Banc Decision in G.R. Nos. E-02219 and E-02235 promulgated
on September 30, 2025, the Supreme Court declared BAA 77 unconstitutional for the
following reasons:

1. It altered and/or created new precincts during the election period in violation
of the Voter's Registration Act;

2. It violated the BOL requirement that each district shall comprise, as far as
practicable, contiguous, compact, and adjacent areas;

3. It improperly vested powers in Congress that the BOL assigns to the
Bangsamoro Parliament, while constraining the Parliament by imposing a fixed
minimum of seven seats;

4. It unlawfully expanded the President’s appointment powers beyond what the
BOL permits; and

5. Section 5 of BAA 77 severed the constitutional link between residence, voter
registration, and representation.

The Court likewise declared BAA 58 unconstitutional and incapable of serving
as the framework for the first parliamentary elections, noting that the exclusion of Sulu
created a structural flaw resulting in disenfranchisement and disproportional
representation inconsistent with the BOL mandate of fixed and proportionate
representation.

As a consequence, the October 13, 2025 Parliamentary Elections cannot proceed
absent a valid districting law. The Supreme Court ordered the BTA to determine
parliamentary districts by October 30, 2025, in strict compliance with the BOL and the
criteria set by the Court, and directed the Commission on Elections (COMELEC) to
hold the elections not later than March 31, 2026.

This decision underscores the urgent necessity of enacting a new districting
law. As the interim government of the BARMM, the BTA has the mandate to enact a
law determining parliamentary districts and to ensure fair and equitable
representation of all Bangsamoro communities in the Parliament.

This measure seeks to create parliamentary districts in the BARMM in full and
faithful compliance with the Constitution, the Bangsamoro Organic Law, and the
directives of the Supreme Court. It ensures that all constituencies are represented
fairly, thereby safeguarding the democratic rights of the Bangsamoro people and
enabling the conduct of the first regular parliamentary elections in accordance with
law.

The Province of Basilan has a recorded population of 541,947 as of 2024, which,
under an ideal demographic distribution, would qualify it for five (5) parliamentary
district seats, consistent with the population benchmark of approximately 100,000
inhabitants per district. However, owing to its unique island geography — wherein one
municipality is physically separated from the main landmass by several nautical
miles — strict compliance with the criteria of contiguity and population distribution
proves impracticable. In this regard, it must be emphasized that the Bangsamoro
Organic Law (BOL) expressly provides that the apportionment standards shall apply
“as far as practicable,” recognizing the diversity of territorial and demographic
conditions in the region.

Similarly, in the case of Maguindanao del Norte, the creation of the proposed
Fifth District with a population of 81,945 constitutes a justified exception to the general
population requirement of 100,000 inhabitants. This exception is warranted in
consideration of the principles of contiguity, compactness, and adjacency, as well as
the distinct cultural and historical identity of the communities comprising the
proposed district.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro
Parliamentary Redistricting Act of 2025”

SECTION 2. Guiding Principles in the Creation of Parliamentary Districts. -
The creation and apportionment of parliamentary districts in the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) shall be guided by the following
principles:

a) Adherence to Constitutional and Legal Mandates. — The districting process
shall strictly comply with the Bangsamoro Organic Law, the 1987 Philippine
Constitution, relevant jurisprudence, and other applicable national and
regional laws, rules, and regulations;

b) Equitable Representation. — Parliamentary districts shall be apportioned
to ensure fair and proportional representation based on population and
geographic distribution, guaranteeing meaningful participation for all
communities, including indigenous peoples, linguistic minorities, and
residents of geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas;

c) Contiguity, Compactness, and Adjacency. — In accordance with Article VII,
Section 10 of the Bangsamoro Organic Law, districts shall, as far as
practicable, consist of contiguous, compact, and adjacent territory, with due

regard to natural features, cultural and historical ties, transportation
networks, and established political and administrative boundaries;

d) Transparency and Participatory Deliberation. — The formulation of
districts shall be undertaken through an open, transparent, and accountable
process, with due consultations and meaningful participation from local
government units, civil society, traditional and religious leaders, sectoral
representatives, and affected communities; and

e) Inclusivity and Social Justice. — The drawing of districts shall promote the
equitable representation of marginalized and underrepresented groups,
ensure respect for cultural diversity, and facilitate fair access to political
participation, basic services, and development opportunities.

SECTION 3. Criteria in the Apportionment of Parliamentary Districts. —
Each parliamentary district shall, as far as practicable, be apportioned among the
provinces, cities, municipalities, and geographic areas of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region based on the following criteria: (1) it shall consist of contiguous,
compact, and adjacent territorial jurisdiction; and (2) it shall have a population of at
least one hundred thousand (100,000).
