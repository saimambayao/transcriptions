EXPLANATORY NOTE

The COVID-19 pandemic has presented unprecedented challenges to the education system in
the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, with far-reaching consequences for the learning
outcomes and academic progress of the students. The sudden closure of schools, the shift to
remote learning, and the disruption of traditional educational routines have created substantial
learning gaps, exacerbating existing disparities in our educational landscape.

This measure is a legislative response to this critical need. It establishes a dedicated Learning
Recovery Fund to channel resources specifically toward bridging the learning gaps that have
emerged during the pandemic.

The primary objective of this legislation is to empower the Ministry of Basic, Higher, and
Technical Education (MBHTE) to deploy the Learning Recovery Fund for a range of targeted
initiatives. These initiatives include additional tutoring and academic support for students who
have fallen behind, the expansion of accessible and high-quality educational resources, specific
interventions for students with special education needs or disabilities, opportunities for
professional development among educators to enhance their capacity to address these learning
gaps, and the promotion of community engagement and parental involvement in education.

It is important to emphasize that this legislation prioritizes those who have been most affected
by the learning gaps and seeks to address the unique challenges faced by disadvantaged and
vulnerable students. The Act is designed to be in effect until a specified date, after which it will

Section 1. Title. — This act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Learning Recovery Act
of 2023."

Section 2. Declaration of Policy. — \t hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to ensure the right to quality education for all students in the region, with particular
attention to those who have been most adversely impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. It
recognizes the recent crisis as an exceptional circumstance, leading to learning gaps for
students who have been disproportionately affected by the disruptions in their education.

Toward this end, it shall take proactive measures to address these gaps and prioritize
the recovery of lost knowledge and skills of students through the allocation and utilization of
resources for learning recovery initiatives.

Section 3. Learning Recovery Fund. — The Bangsamoro Government shall establish a
Learning Recovery Fund (Fund) to serve as a dedicated financial resource for students in the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to address the learning gaps that have
emerged due to the COVID-19 pandemic. This fund shall be administered by the Ministry of
Basic, Higher, and Technical Education (MBHTE).

Section 4. Accountability and Transparency. — The MBHTE shall submit regular
progress reports to the Parliament on the implementation of learning recovery initiatives which
shall be made publicly available, including information on the allocation and expenditure of
funds.

Section 5. Sunset Clause. — This Act shall remain in effect for three (3) years, at which
point it shall be subject to review and renewal as necessary.

Section 6. Implementing Rules and Regulations. — The MBHTE shall issue the rules
and regulations necessary for the proper implementation of this Act within six (6) months from
its effectivity.

Section 7. Separability Clause. — If any section or part of this Act is held

unconstitutional or invalid, all other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain
in full force and effect.

Section 8. Repealing Clause. — All ordinances, executive orders, administrative orders,

Section 9. Effectivity. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in
a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
