EXPLANATORY NOTE

The protection of consumers is a fundamental responsibility of government, anchored on the
constitutional mandate to promote the general welfare and ensure the rights of the people to
health, safety, and a balanced economy. In the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM), the protection of consumer rights and welfare assumes greater
significance, given the distinct socio-cultural, economic, and religious context of the region.

At present, consumers in BARMM remain vulnerable to a wide range of exploitative
practices. Experiences from local markets and communities reveal instances of deceptive
sales and advertising, such as false product claims and misleading discounts; the sale of
hazardous or substandard products, including expired food, counterfeit medicines,
uncertified electrical devices, and non-halal goods; price manipulation, particularly
hoarding and profiteering in rice, fuel, and other basic commodities; and abuses in digital
commerce, such as online fraud, undelivered purchases, and misuse of personal data. These
practices mirror the consumer vulnerabilities already recognized under Republic Act No.
7394, otherwise known as the Consumer Act of the Philippines, but are aggravated in
BARMM by the absence of regional mechanisms for enforcement, education, and redress.

Such practices not only erode consumer confidence but also distort market competition,
burden households with unjust costs, and endanger public health and safety, thereby
impairing the stability of the regional economy and the overall welfare of the Bangsamoro
people. The need for a localized and culturally responsive framework becomes even more
urgent in view of the growing reliance on digital platforms for trade and the increasing
demand for halal-certified, eco-friendly, and locally produced goods.

establish a comprehensive legal framework for the protection of consumers within the region.
Specifically, it aims to:

2.

confidence, and contributing to regional economic growth consistent with Islamic values,

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Title.

This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Consumers Act of 2025.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.

It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to promote the rights, welfare, and
empowerment of consumers within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (BARMM). Towards this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall:

L.

Protect consumers against products, production processes, and services that are
hazardous to health, safety, and the environment.

Shield consumers against deceptive, unfair, abusive, and unconscionable sales
practices.

Guarantee the availability of safe, quality, and reasonably priced goods, services, and
credit facilities.

Promote a healthy and fair marketplace by preventing monopolies, profiteering,
hoarding, and other market manipulations.

Encourage sustainable consumption and the use of halal, eco-friendly, and locally

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms.

For purposes of this Act, the following terms are defined:

i.

Consumer — any individual, group, household, community, or juridical entity that
buys, uses, or avails of goods, services, or credit for personal, family, household, or
community purposes, and not for commercial gain.

Goods — all tangible products, whether manufactured, produced, grown, or processed,
offered for sale in the market, including food, clothing, electronics, medicine, and
agricultural produce.

Services — any commercial activity offered to the public, covering both essential
services (healthcare, education, utilities) and commercial services (transportation,
telecommunications, digital platforms, and financial services).

Hazardous Products — foods, substances, or services that are unsafe or harmful to
health, safety, or the environment under normal use, including expired food,
counterfeit drugs, defective appliances, and toxic chemicals, which must be
proactively removed from the market.

. Labeling — any written, printed, or graphic information on or attached to a product,

ensuring truth in packaging by providing clear, accurate, and necessary details for
consumers.

Misleading Advertisement — any false, deceptive, or misleading promotion of a
product or service, intended to create false expectations or misrepresent facts, such as
“miracle cure” claims or fake discounts.

Price Manipulation — unfair practices such as hoarding, profiteering, or cartelization
that create artificial shortages or unreasonably inflate prices, including collusion
among sellers or cartels.

Digital Commerce — the buying, selling, marketing, or distribution of goods and
services through electronic networks such as online platforms, marketplaces, and
social media, ensuring consumer protections extend to e-commerce transactions.

SECTION 4. Rights of Consumers.

Consumers within BARMM shall enjoy the following rights:

1.

Right to Safety — the right of consumers to be protected from hazardous, harmful, or
defective goods, services, and production processes, ensuring they are not exposed to
risks that may endanger health or life.

Right to Information — the right of consumers to receive accurate, sufficient, and
clear details on the quality, quantity, composition, price, and risks of goods and
services, including truthful labeling, halal certification, expiry dates, and honest
advertising.

Right to Choose — the right of consumers to access affordable, diverse, and
competitively priced goods and services, with options that respect cultural and
religious practices, including halal-certified and locally produced products.

Right to Representation — the right of consumers to be heard and included in
government decision-making through consultations, hearings, and representation in
the Bangsamoro Consumer Protection Council (BCPC) on matters affecting their
welfare and interests.

Right to Redress — the right of consumers to fair settlement and remedies for just
claims, including compensation, replacement, or legal action in cases of harm caused
by misrepresentation, defective goods, or substandard services.

Right to Consumer Education — the right of consumers to be informed and educated
about their rights, responsibilities, and available remedies through formal, informal,
and non-formal means, including programs on financial literacy, product standards,
and consumer protection laws.

Right to a Healthy Environment — the right of consumers to live and consume in a
safe, clean, and sustainable environment, including access to safe water and air,
proper waste management, and eco-friendly production practices.

Right to Privacy and Data Protection — the right of consumers to be protected from
misuse, unauthorized access, or exploitation of their personal and_ financial
information in both physical and digital transactions, including those involving online
commerce.

SECTION 5. Duties of Consumers.

Consumers shall have the following responsibilities:

. Duty to Be Critical and Responsible — consumers must exercise vigilance and

responsibility in purchasing and using goods and services, avoiding panic buying,
counterfeit products, and wasteful consumption.

Duty to Assert Rights Responsibly — consumers must uphold and defend their rights
when violated, while exercising fairness and responsibility in doing so.

Duty to Support Fair Trade — consumers must promote and engage in fair trade by
patronizing ethical businesses and rejecting exploitative or abusive practices.

Duty to Practice Sustainable Consumption - consumers must observe
environmentally responsible practices by reducing waste, recycling, and choosing
eco-friendly products to support sustainability.

. Duty to Respect Cultural and Religious Sensitivities — consumers must honor

religious, halal, and cultural practices in all consumer transactions to promote
inclusivity and mutual respect.

Duty to Report Violations — consumers must report breaches of consumer protection
laws and unethical practices to the proper authorities in order to strengthen
enforcement and safeguard public welfare.

SECTION 6. Bangsamoro Consumer Protection Council (BCPC).

There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Consumer Protection Council (BCPC) under the
Ministry of Trade, Investments, and Tourism (MTIT) which shall serve as the primary
agency responsible for the protection of consumers in the BARMM.

Composition:

Minister of Health (Co-Chairperson)

Minister of Science and Technology (Member)

Minister of Finance, and Budget and Management (Member)

One (1) representative each from the Bangsamoro Women Commission, Bangsamoro
Youth Commission, and Bangsamoro Darul ‘Ifta (Member)

Two (2) representatives from accredited consumer organizations (Members)

One (1) representative from the business sector (Member)

SECTION 7. Functions of the BCPC.

The functions are preventive, corrective, and educational it shall:

Formulate policies, receive complaints, conduct investigations, enforce labeling and packing
standards, and promote consumer education.

The BCPC serves as the “watchdog” of consumers in BARMM.

Towards this end the Council shall:

1.

Formulate, coordinate, and implement consumer protection policies and programs in
BARMM.

Establish consumer assistance and complaint desks in every province and city of the
BARMM.

Investigate complaints, conduct hearings, and recommend sanctions to proper
authorities.

Develop and enforce standards for labeling, packaging, weights and measures, price
tagging, and advertising.

Promote consumer education programs in partnership with schools, LGUs, religious
institutions, and civil society organizations.

Publish regular reports on consumer issues and market conditions.

Coordinate with national government agencies such as the DTI, FDA, and NTC to
harmonize consumer protection efforts.

Recommend the filing of civil, criminal, or administrative cases against violators.

SECTION 8. Prohibited Acts.
The following are declared unlawful:

L.

Deceptive Sales Practices — misrepresentation, false advertising, or fraudulent
marketing.

Sale of Hazardous Products — distribution of goods that are unsafe, expired, or unfit
for use.

Price Manipulation — hoarding, profiteering, or cartel practices that distort the
market.

Unfair Trade Practices — including tie-in sales, bait-and-switch tactics, or
unreasonable contract terms.

5. Violation of Warranties — refusal to honor repair, replacement, or refund obligations.
6. Digital Fraud — scams, phishing, identity theft, or fake online sales.

7. Tampering with Weights and Measures — altering weighing scales, meters, or

SECTION 9. Penalties.

1. Administrative Penalties — fines ranging from 5,000.00 to P500,000.00 depending
on the severity and frequency of the offense, suspension or revocation of business
licenses, and confiscation of goods.

2. Civil Liability — restitution, replacement, or refund of the goods or services, and
damages to the aggrieved consumer.

3. Criminal Liability — imprisonment of not less than one (1) month but not more
than five (5) years for willful and repeated violations, without prejudice to higher
penalties under national laws.

SECTION 10. Appropriations.

The amount necessary for the initial implementation of this Act shall be charged against the
current appropriations of the MTIT. Thereafter, such sums as may be necessary shall be
included in the annual budget of the Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 11. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).

Within six (6) months from the effectivity of this Act, the MTIT, in consultation with the
BCPC and relevant stakeholders, shall promulgate the IRR.

SECTION 12. Separability Clause.

If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not
affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 13. Repealing Clause.

All regional laws, rules, and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or
modified accordingly.
