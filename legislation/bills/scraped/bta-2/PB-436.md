EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) faces complex challenges in maintaining
peace, public order, and security, which are linked to its unique socio-political context, historical conflicts, and new
security threats.

Despite the existence of multiple agencies and mechanisms aimed at addressing these concerns, the absence of
a centralized, integrated, and coordinated data and information management system hampers the effectiveness,
precision, and promptness of decision-making and response.

The proposed initiative aims to establish the Bangsamoro Integrated Public Order and Safety, and Security Data
and Information Management (BIPOSSDIM) Coordinating Body as the principal entity tasked with the collection,
consolidation, analysis, and dissemination of pertinent data and information regarding peace and order, law
enforcement, disaster response, and security issues within BARMM.

This Coordinating Body will:

(1) Serve as a central point for the sharing and coordination of data and information among various ministries,
agencies, offices, law enforcement groups, local government units, and pertinent stakeholders;

(2) Create and maintain a secure, real-time database to facilitate evidence-based policy-making and operational
planning;

(3) Strengthen the capacity of BARMM institutions to respond swiftly to security incidents, natural disasters, and
other threats to public safety; and

(4) Foster transparency, accountability, and community trust through accurate and accessible public safety
information.

By institutionalizing BIPOSSDIM, the BARMM Government will be better positioned to address peace and security
issues in a comprehensive, data-driven, and collaborative manner, ensuring that policies and interventions are
based on reliable information.

ARTICLE |
GENERAL PROVISIONS

SECTION. 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known and cited as the “Bangsamoro Integrated Public Order and
Security Data and Information Management Act’ or the “BIPOSSDIM Act of 2026.”

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. — It is the declared policy of the Bangsamoro Government, pursuant to Republic
Act No. 11054 (Bangsamoro Organic Law), to maintain peace and order, and ensure public safety and security,
recognizing that effective governance, particularly in security matters, relies on timely, accurate and integrated
data and information. Toward this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall establish a secure, efficient and robust
data and information management system aimed at facilitating coordinated and evidence-based planning and
response to public order and safety, and security, while upholding the principles of moral governance,
transparency, accountability and data privacy rights of every individual.

SEC. 3. Definition of Terms. — As used in this Act:

Authorized User — refers to a member of the BISPOSSDIM Coordinating Body, and authorized representatives
from other relevant government agencies, local government units, and additional entities, in alignment with the
policies, rules, and regulations established by the Body for accessing data and information it holds concerning
public order, safety, and security.

Authorized User — denotes to a person who is a member of the BISPOSSDIM Coordinating Body, together with
authorized representatives from relevant government agencies, Local Government Units (LGUs), and other
governmental and non-governmental entities that possess the authority to access data and information related to
public order, safety, and security, in compliance with the policies, rules, and regulations established by the Body.

BIPOSSDIM also called as Coordinating Body or simply Body — refers to the Bangsamoro Integrated Public Order
and Safety, and Security Data and Information Management Coordinating Body.

Data — denotes "quantitative or statistical facts" relevant to public order, safety, and security, which includes
incidents, trends, and demographics within the BARMM.

Information - pertains to “qualitative or anecdotal facts" concerning public order, safety, and security,
encompassing incidents, trends, and demographics within the region.

Interoperationality — refers to the ability of the data and information system, the Body and its members, as well
as other authorized end-users, to access, exchange, integrate, and effectively utilize these data and information in
a coordinated manner.

Personal Data and Information — relates to both quantitative (statistical) and qualitative (anecdotal) facts about
an individual that is protected under Republic Act No. 10173, known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012, and must be
handled in compliance with strict regulations.

Public Order and Safety Data and Information - includes statistics and systematically collected anecdotes
regarding crime, disaster risk, emergency responses, and various related datasets.

Security Data and Information — involves intelligence, threat assessments, and other sensitive statistical and
anecdotal facts relevant to the maintenance of peace and order.

SEC. 4. Purpose. — This Act endeavors to establish the BIPOSSDIM Coordinating Body as the principal entity
for planning, coordinating, and monitoring all activities related to public order and safety, and security data and
information management within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

SEC. 5. Data and Information Classification and Authorized Users. — Data and information in this Act shall
be classified into public and exclusive where:

Public Data and Information (PDI) are legally accessible, freely usable, reusable and redistributable to the
stakeholders and other individuals, groups or institutions recognized by the DIPOSSDIM Coordinating Body as
legitimate end-users; while

Exclusive Data and Information (EDI) are regarded as sensitive, proprietary, or confidential information and
knowledge, and are available solely to the members of the Body and other exceptionally acknowledged individuals,
groups, or institutions.

SEC. 6. Classification of Data and Information and Authorized Users. — The data and information defined in
this Act shall be categorized as either public or exclusive:

Public data and information are legally available, freely usable, reusable, and redistributable to stakeholders and
other individuals, groups, or institutions deemed legitimate end-users by the DIPOSSDIM Coordinating Body;
whereas

Exclusive Data and Information, which encompass sensitive, proprietary, or confidential facts and knowledge, are
strictly reserved for the use of the members of the Body and other specially recognized individuals, groups, or
institutions.

ARTICLE Il
THE BIPOSSDIM COORDINATING BODY

SEC. 7. Creation of the Body. — There is hereby created the Bangsamoro Integrated Public Order and Safety,
and Security Data and Information Management Coordinating Body, hereinafter referred to as the “Coordinating
Body”, or simply as the “Body. It shall be an attached group to the Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS).

SEC. 8. Structure and Core Responsibilities of Members. — The Body shall consist of competent
representatives from relevant ministries, agencies and offices, and may include but is not restricted to:

(a) The Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) as the Chairperson, and for the dissemination and
administration of data and information that predominantly revolves around community-oriented peacebuilding and
conflict resolution;

(b) The Ministry of the Interior and Local Government (MILG) as the Associate Chairperson, and for the
dissemination and/or management of data and information that enhances public safety and bolsters the
capabilities of Local Government Units (LGUs) in overseeing peace and order, as well as disaster-related
emergencies;

(c) The Ministry of Health (MOH) for emergency coordination data and information, particularly concerning health-
related security issues;

(d) The Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) for coordinating and utilizing data related to disaster
response;

(e) The National Intelligence Coordinating Agency (NICA)-BARMM is in charge of coordinating intelligence data
and information;

(f) The Philippine National Police (PNP)-BARMM for coordinating data and information related to public order,
safety, and crime;

(g) The Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP)-BARMM for coordinating data and information related to regional
security;

(h) The Philippine Statistics Office (PSO)-BARMM for facilitating the sharing of pertinent data and information
essential for policy-making, planning, and program implementation by the mandated public order, safety, and
security agencies;

(i) The Bangsamoro Information and Communication Technology Office (BICTO) coordinating and publishing data
and information intended for public access;

(j) The Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority (BPDA) for integrating relevant data and information into
regional development plans; and

(k) The Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission (BHRC) for ensuring the the promotion and protection of human
rights in the management and dissemination of data and information.

SEC. 9. Powers and Functions of the Body. — The Body shall have the following powers and functions:

(a) Provide a framework for coordination and collaboration in data and information sharing and management
among various ministries and agencies having concern in public order, safety and security in the BARMM;

(b) Develop a secure, integrated database system accessible to authorized personnel from member agencies for
legitimate public safety and security purposes;

(c) Formulate and recommend policies, plans, programs, and guidelines for the unified and secure management of
public order and safety, and security data and information in the BARMM

(d) Harmonize and coordinate all data collection and information-sharing initiatives among member agencies to
ensure interoperationality and avoid redundancy;

(e) Prescribe rules and regulations for the establishment, operation, and maintenance of secure data infrastructure;

(f) Standardize the collection, processing, and storage of data and information related to public order and safety,
and security concerns, including conflict sensitivity and crime incidence;

(g) Ensure that all data and information management protocols comply with national and regional data privacy and
cybersecurity laws, rules, and regulations;

(h) Monitor and evaluate the implementation of the integrated data and information system and report to the Office
of the Chief Minister.

(i) Guarantee the protection and responsible utilization of sensitive data, in compliance with national laws and
international standards regarding data privacy and human rights; and

(j) Furnish the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro Parliament with dependable data for evidence-based policy-
making, planning, and resource allocation for peace and secutiry initiatives.

SEC. 10. Secretariat. — The MPOS shall serve as the Secretariat of the Taskforce and shall provide
administrative and technical support.

CHAPTER IIl
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

SEC. 11. Appropriations. - The funds required to implement the provisions of this Act shall be drawn from the
current year's appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government. Subsequently, any amounts deemed necessary for
its ongoing operation shall be incorporated into the annual Bangsamoro Appropriations Act (BAA).

SEC. 12. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - The BIPOSSDIM Coordinating Body, in collaboration with
pertinent agencies and the National Privacy Commission, shall establish the Implementing Rules and Regulations
(IRR) of this Act within sixty (60) days following its effectivity.

SEC. 13. Separability Clause. - Should any provision of this Act be found unconstitutional or invalid, the
remaining provisions that are not affected shail continue to be valid and in force.

SEC. 14. Repealing Clause. - All laws, Bangsamoro autonomy acts, ordinances, rules, regulations, and other
issuances or parts thereof that are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, modified, or amended as
necessary.

SEC. 15. Effectivity. - This Act shall become effective fifteen (15) days after its full publication in at least one (1)
newspaper of general circulation within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao.
