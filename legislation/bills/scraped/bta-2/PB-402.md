Explanatory Note

The 1987 Philippine Constitution, in Section 15, Article II, declares that the state shall
protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among
them. Section 11, Article XIII declares that the State shall adopt an integrated and
comprehensive approach to health development, making essential goods and services
accessible to all at an affordable cost.

In the Bangsamoro Region, Section 22, Article IX of the Republic Act No. 11054,
otherwise known as the “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao” also declares that the Bangsamoro Government shall protect and promote the right
to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them, and it shall also uphold
the people’s right to have access to essential goods, health, and other social services that
promote their well-being.

The prevalence of drug dependence remains a public health and social concern in the
BARMM. While the national government has established rehabilitation facilities in other
regions, BARMM lacks fully operational, government-owned treatment and rehabilitation
centers in all its provinces. Existing initiatives, such as Balay Silangan, are primarily
reformation facilities and do not fully address the medical, psychological, and reintegration
needs of drug dependents.

This Bill seeks to establish one fully operational Drug Dependent Treatment and Rehabilitation
Center in every BARMM province to provide comprehensive, accessible, and sustainable
interventions. It aligns with the principles of moral governance, peacebuilding, and human
development, and ensures that the Bangsamoro Government fulfills its responsibility to protect
and rehabilitate its people.

Thus, in view of the foregoing, the immediate approval of this bill is earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Drug Dependent
Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers Act of 2025.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. It is the policy of the State to protect and promote the
right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them, as enshrined in
Section 15, Article II of the 1987 Philippine Constitution. Furthermore, pursuant to Section 11,
Article XIII of the Constitution, the State shall adopt an integrated and comprehensive
approach to health development, which seeks to make essential goods, health, and other social
services accessible and affordable to all, with particular priority to the needs of the
underprivileged, sick, elderly, disabled, women, and children.

The State shall likewise endeavor to provide free medical care to paupers. In accordance
with Section 8 of the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao (Republic Act No. 11054), the Bangsamoro Government shall provide, maintain,
and ensure the delivery of basic and responsive health programs. Moreover, under Section 22
of the same law, the Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy on health that ensures a
comprehensive and integrated health service delivery system for its constituents.

In line with these constitutional and legal mandates, the Bangsamoro Government

recognizes drug dependency as a public health concern requiring compassionate, evidence-

based,

and community-oriented intervention. It shall, therefore, endeavor to establish and

maintain drug treatment and rehabilitation centers in every province of the Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region to ensure access to holistic and culturally appropriate rehabilitation
services for drug dependents.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall be
defined as:

a)

b)

c)

d)

e)

Dangerous Drugs — Those provided under Republic Act No. 9165, otherwise known as
the “Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002,” which include those listed in the
schedules annexed to the United Nations 1961 Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs,
as amended by the 1972 Protocol, and in the schedules annexed to the 1971 Single
Convention on Psychotropic Substances;

Drug Dependents — as defined under Republic Act 9165, are the persons of psychic or
physical dependence upon dangerous drugs;

Drug Rehabilitation — a dynamic process involving medical, psychological, spiritual,
and social interventions designed to enable an individual to live a drug-free, healthy,
and productive life;

Treatment and Rehabilitation Center (TRC) — a facility, whether residential or non-
residential, operated and maintained for the treatment, care, aftercare, follow-up, and
social reintegration of drug dependents; and

Community-Based Rehabilitation Program (CBRP) — interventions, treatments, and
recovery support services provided at the community level for persons with mild or
moderate dangerous drug use.

SECTION 4. Establishment of Drug Dependent Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers.

a)

b)

c)

There is hereby established one (1) Drug Dependent Treatment and Rehabilitation
Center in every province of BARMM;

Each Treatment and Rehabilitation Center shall provide, at a minimum:
1) Residential and out-patient treatment services;
2) Detoxification and medical care;
3) Psychological, psychiatric, and spiritual counseling;
4) Skills training, livelihood, and reintegration programs; and
5) Family support and aftercare programs;

The Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers shall be government-owned and operated,
under the supervision of the Ministry of Health (MOH) in coordination with the
Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS), the Ministry of Social Services and
Development (MSSD), the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP), the
Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA), and local government units.

SECTION 5. Mandate of the Ministries.

a) Ministry of Health (MOH) — shall have primary responsibility for the operation,
management, staffing, and regulation of the Centers;

b) Ministry of Public Order and Safety (MPOS) — shall provide peace and order support, and
assist in referral systems in cooperation with law enforcement agencies;

c) Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) -— shall provide aftercare,
livelihood, psychosocial support, and community reintegration programs;

d) Ministry of Interior and Local Government (MILG) - shall coordinate with LGUs,
Provincial Anti-Drug Abuse Councils (PADACs), and Barangay Anti-Drug Abuse
Councils (BADACs) for referral and monitoring;

e) Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA) and the Bureau of Jail Management and
Penology (BJMP) BARMM - Shall provide the list and coordinate the persons arrested
and tested positive for using illegal drugs;

f) Bangsamoro Darul-Ifta’ and the Bangsamoro Endowment, Call and Guidance Center-
may provide spiritual and moral guidance programs consistent with Islamic values and
moral governance;

SECTION 6. Admission and Referral.

a) The Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers shall accept voluntary admissions, referrals
by courts, law enforcement, LGUs, and community-based programs; and

b) Priority shall be given to residents of the BARMM provinces where the Centers are
located.

SECTION 7. Funding.

a) The initial funding for the establishment and construction of the Centers shall be
charged against the appropriations of the Bangsamoro Government under the Annual
Appropriations Act.

b) The amount necessary for the continuous operation and maintenance of the Centers
shall be included in the annual budget of the Ministry of Health.

c) Additional funding sources may include national government assistance, international
donors, and public-private partnerships, provided that operations remain under
government supervision.

SECTION 8. Personnel and Staffing.

a) Each Treatment and Rehabilitation Center shall employ qualified physicians,
psychiatrists, psychologists, nurses, social workers, guidance counselors, and support
staff; and

b) The MOH shall ensure continuous training and accreditation of staff in partnership with
the Dangerous Drugs Board (DDB) and DOH.

SECTION 9. Community-Based Rehabilitation. The MOH, MILG, MPOS, and MSSD, with
the support of PDEA, shall jointly develop and strengthen Community-Based Drug
Rehabilitation Programs (CBDRPs) in every province, city, and municipality in BARMM to
complement the services of the Treatment and Rehabilitation Centers.

SECTION 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations. Within ninety (90) days from the
effectivity of this Act, the MOH, MILG, MPOS, and MSSD, and all other concerned ministries,
in consultation with LGUs, civil society organizations, and faith-based groups, shall
promulgate the necessary Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR).

SECTION 11. Separability Clause. If any part or provision of this Act is declared
unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be
in force and effect.

SECTION 12. Repealing Clause. All laws, decrees, orders, or issuances inconsistent with the
provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

SECTION 13. Effectivity. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in
the Bangsamoro Government Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
