EXPLANATORY NOTE

The Bangsamoro has a rich and flourishing culture with a very long history,
enough to consider it among the primordial communes of this country. Unfortunately,
until the advent of social media and the internet, our limited way of preserving our
cultural memory and history is through word of mouth and story-telling from our elders.
In Tausug, we call it "kissa," an Arabic word that literally means "story". It's no wonder
that, except for recent documents and records, we only have verbal references to serve as
our historical data.

Lately, some of our writers and intellectuals succeeded in reducing into writing
our history and traditions. These writings, nevertheless, are scattered in various libraries
and personal collections.

The maintenance of our own archival records is one of the important tasks of the
Bangsamoro Regional Government that should be advanced to preserve our collective
identity and history. The initiative to strengthen the system of management and archival
records of the Bangsamoro Government through the establishment of the Office of the
Bangsamoro Regional Archives complements the existing and noble purposes of the
Bangsamoro Museum, Bangsamoro Library and Archives, and the Bangsamoro
Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage.

Through these efforts, we could help preserve our history, educate and allow
others to understand our diversity, and provide tangible reminders of the sacrifices of
our martyrs, the ingenuity of our intellectuals, the determination of our leaders, and our
resiliency and unwavering commitment to the dream of governing our own territory.

We are at the height of making a difference for the Bangsamoro People and at the
most appropriate time to preserve and make known our identity and history. Our timing
is right. Let us, therefore, build a home for our narratives to bridge us to our posterity.
Let us create the OBRA.

BE IT ENACTED by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro Regional Archives
Act of 2023".

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. -

a. Itis the policy of the State to conserve, promote, and popularize the Bangsamoro’s
historical and cultural heritage and resources, as well as artistic creations.

b. All the Bangsamoro’s artistic and historic wealth constitutes the cultural treasure
of the region and shall be under the protection of the Regional Government, which
may regulate its disposition.

c. The Bangsamoro Government shall preserve the history, culture, art traditions,

and rich cultural heritage of the Bangsamoro people and their Sultanates, such as
those of but not limited to, Sulu, Maguindanao, Kabuntalan, Buayan, the Royal
House of Ranao and of the Iranun, and the non-Moro indigenous peoples of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region.

. Further, the Bangsamoro Regional Government shall ensure and support, among

other things, the preservation and enrichment of the Bangsamoro heritage and
culture.

. Towards this end, all public records with the historical value held by Bangsamoro

government offices shall be transferred to an official, permanent, and regional
repository for proper management, control, regulation, and preservation of record
disposition.

SECTION 3. Definition of Terms. - For purposes of this Act, the following definition of
terms are hereby adopted:

a) Archival Value - refers to the value attached to records that hold notoriety for the

Bangsamoro Government, its people, and the general public because those records
make the government accountable to its citizens; provide evidence about public
policies and programs; protect or verify individuals' rights and entitlements; and
provide information about the important people, issues, places, and events that
make up the story of Bangsamoro;

b) Archives - shall refer to:

1. Public records, papers, periodicals, books or other items, articles, or
materials, whether in the form of electronic, audiovisual, or print, which by
their nature and characteristics have historical and cultural value, that have
been selected for permanent preservation;

2. The place (building/room/ storage area) where archival materials are kept
and preserved; and

3. An organization (or part of an organization) whose main function is to
select, collect and preserve archival records and make such records
available for public use.

c) Disposal refers to the act of selling, burning, or any other way of discarding

valueless records in accordance with the provisions of this Act and other existing
laws.

ARTICLE II
OFFICE OF THE BANGSAMORO REGIONAL ARCHIVES

SECTION 5. Office of the Bangsamoro Regional Archives. - The Office of the
Bangsamoro Regional Archives, hereinafter referred to as OBRA, is hereby created and
organized, structurally and functionally, in accordance with the provisions of this Act. It
shall be under the Office of the Chief Minister.

The OBRA shall be primarily responsible for the implementation of the objectives and
provisions of this Act. It shall plan, develop, prescribe, disseminate, and enforce policies,
rules, and regulations and coordinate programs governing the creation, general
protection, use, storage, and disposition of public records, including the acquisition,
storage, and preservation of public archives and providing facilities for reference,
research or other purposes.

SECTION 6. Powers and Functions. - In pursuit of its mandate, the OBRA shall have the
following powers and functions:

a) Act as the official repository of all agreements, laws, public records, bulletins,
books, gazettes, thesis, publications, exhibitions, audio-recording, video-
recording or images, and any other writings or documentation, relating to or
involving the struggle of the Bangsamoro and the history, culture, tradition, and
practices of the Bangsamoro, the Non-Moro Indigenous People, and the settler
communities;

b) Conduct an inventory of the above writings and documentation that already exist
and under the custody of the Bangsamoro Government;

c) Maintain a registry of all public records under the custody and control of the
Bangsamoro Government, which shall be made available for public inspection;

d) Implement a records management and archival administration system for the
efficient maintenance, preservation, retention, and utilization of public records of
the Bangsamoro Government;
