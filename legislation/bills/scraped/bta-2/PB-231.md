EXPLANATORY NOTE

This era of technology is a blessing as it makes works easier and services accessible. The
internet has now become indispensable to almost every transaction especially in the aspect of
delivering public services. However, the convenience brought by technology is not without
burden. To preserve the convenience of technology especially internet, substantial amount of
funds is needed for its long-term sustainability. Second, government data stored in the internet are
prone to hacking compared to those locally stored in computers not shared in the internet. Third,
Local Area Network system does not require internet connection. Therefore, even in cases if
calamity where internet is unserviceable, government services can still continue if LAN is
established in the Bangsamoro Government. And these are just some of the examples why we
need to establish LAN in our government. It will save us more funds.

HUSSEIN, UNOZ

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament of the BARMM:
SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the "Bangsamoro LAN Act."

SECTION 2. Policy. — It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to support the development
of appropriate and self-reliant scientific and technological capabilities, enhance economic
prosperity and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants. It is also the policy of the
Bangsamoro Government to deliver services to the inhabitants of the region in speedy, efficient,
and effective manner.

SECTION 3. General Objectives. -- This Act seeks to:

a. Establish in the Bangsamoro Government a fast, reliable, and economical Local Area
Network (LAN) system for efficient and effective delivery of services to the inhabitants
of the region;

b. Ensure the security of data of the Bangsamoro Government;

SECTION 4. Definition of Terms. — When used in this Act, the following term shall have the
following meaning assigned to them:

(a) “LAN - is a computer network established within a small geographic area, such as a house,
office or buildings and allow resource sharing, including printers, apps and other shared
services; allow multiple devices on a LAN to share a single internet connection; and.
protect LAN-connected devices using network security tools.

A local area network (LAN) is a communications system that allows the interconnection
and sharing of resources between independent devices within a moderately sized
geographic area

(b) “Wired LAN is also known as Ethernet LAN connection. In this type of connection, wires
are used to connect the computers. It is a fast connection and latency is low in a wired
connection. Latency means when you transfer data then in how much time it is received

SECTION 5. Establishment of LAN — The Bangsamoro Government shall design and establish its
Local Area Network (LAN) system that shall connect and serve all its offices including the Office
of the Chief Minister, the Ministries, the Parliament, and all other agencies attached to them.

SECTION 6. Design — The Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST) and the Bangsamoro
Information and Communication Technology (BICTO) shall provide the most appropriate LAN
type or structure for the Bangsamoro Government within thirty (30) days upon approval of this
act.

SECTION 7. Implementing Rules and Regulation. — The Ministry of Science and Technology,
and Bangsamoro Information and Communication Technology shall provide the Implementing
Rules and Regulation of this Act within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act.

SECTION 8. Separability Clause. —\f any part of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid,
the other provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION 9. Repealing Clause. - Any law, issuances, orders, letter of instruction, administrative
order, rules or regulations contrary to or is inconsistent with the provision of this Act is hereby
repealed, modified, or amended accordingly.

SECTION 10. Effectivity Clause. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication
in at least two (2) newspapers of general circulation in the Region.
