AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING THE PANGLIMA SUGALA COMMUNITY
HOSPITAL, A TEN (10)-BED INFIRMARY HOSPITAL IN PANGLIMA SUGALA,
TAWI-TAWI, PROVIDING FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament in session assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. This Act shall be known as the “Panglima Sugala emma Hospital
Institutionalization Act.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - It is the policy of the Bangsamoro Government to ensure
that accessible, responsive, and quality health care is available to all, especially in underserved and
remote communities. Toward this end, the Bangsamoro Government shall recognize and sustain
existing local health facilities that meet community needs and promote equitable health access.

SECTION 3. Institutionalization of Panglima Sugala Community Hospital. - The existing
Panglima Sugala Community Hospital, located in the Municipality of Panglima Sugala, Province
of Tawi-Tawi, is hereby institutionalized as a ten (10)-bed infirmary hospital under the Ministry
of Health - BARMM.

SECTION 4. Scope of Services. - The Panglima Sugala Community Hospital shall provide the
following basic health services: General outpatient care and medical consultation, Emergency and
first-response services, Minor surgical procedures, Maternal and newborn care, Laboratory and
diagnostic services, Basic pharmacy and medicines access and Community-based health education
and disease prevention

SECTION 5. Administrative Supervision and Management. - The hospital shall be under the full
administrative and technical supervision of the Ministry of Health - BARMM, which shall ensure
the provision of adequate personnel, medical supplies, and operational systems. A Medical
Officer-in-Charge shall be appointed to oversee day-to-day operations.

SECTION 6. Staffing and Human Resources. - The Ministry of Health, in coordination with the
Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management (MFBM), shall establish the appropriate staffing
pattern, subject to Civil Service and health regulations. Preference shall be given to qualified health
professionals from Tawi-Tawi.

SECTION 7. Funding. - The amount necessary for the continued operation, maintenance,
staffing, and upgrading of the Panglima Sugala Community Hospital shall be included in the
annual budget of the Ministry of Health under the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act.

SECTION 8. Upgrading and Development. - The Ministry of Health is authorized to enhance the
services, facilities, and bed capacity of the Panglima Sugala Community Hospital as warranted by
population demand, public health needs, and available resources.

SECTION 9. Monitoring and Evaluation. - The Ministry of Health shall conduct regular
monitoring and evaluation of the hospital’s performance and compliance with BARMM health
standards. Results shall inform policy decisions and resource allocation.

SECTION 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity
of this Act, the Ministry of Health shall issue the necessary rules and guidelines for its
implementation.

SECTION 11. Separability Clause. - \f any provision of this Act is declared invalid or
unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION 12. Repealing Clause. - All laws, rules, and regulations inconsistent with this Act are
hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

SECTION 13. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the
Bangsamoro Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation in the BARMM.
