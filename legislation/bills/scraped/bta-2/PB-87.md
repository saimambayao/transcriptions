EXPLANATORY NOTE

Tawi-Tawi, the far-south islands of the Philippines is known to have been blessed in numerous
aspects.

The islands of the Philippines were first introduced to Islam during the 14" century through
the seas and straits of Tawi-Tawi. In recent years, when the region was afflicted with an almost
perpetual cycle of violent conflicts, the province of Tawi-Tawi remained peaceful. More
peaceful even compared to other provinces in the country.

Despite having been isolated from the greater lands in the country, surrounded by its vast
coastline, the province thrived through its bountiful aquatic resources. Tawi-Tawi boasts of its
ecological balance. The iconic Bud Bongao remains inhabited by wildlife unique to the
province. The islands are home to several flora and fauna species.

Its history, culture, natural resources, scenery, and pristine beaches, all of these together make
up a cornucopia of a tourist haven. Yet, there is still more to explore. In Barangay Lakit-Lakit,
east of Bongao Municipality lies a rock formation and part of a cave system situated along the
coast, named by the locals as the Balobok.

The Balobok Cave is found to be one of the earliest human settlements in Southeast Asia dating
back to 8,000 years ago. Recent efforts to excavate the area led to the discovery of ancient
artifacts such as earthenware shards, polished stones, and bone tools. Also, some accounts point

Government to preserve, protect, promote and develop the Balobok Cave in Bongao, Tawi-

Tawi. This bill seeks to declare the Balobok Cave as a regional heritage zone.

In view of the foregoing, the passage of this bill is earnestly sought.

AN ACT

HERITAGE ZONE, PROVIDING FOR ITS PROTECTION, PRESERVATION, AND
PROMOTION, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

Section 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “Balobok Cave Preservation
Act of 2022”

Section 2. Declaration of Policy and Principles. — \t shall be the policy of the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to recognize as an integral
component of regional development the transcendental importance of collecting,
preserving, reviving, protecting, and harnessing the cultural heritage of the people in the
Autonomous Region.

Section 2, Article V of the Bangsamoro Organic Law states that the Bangsamoro
Government shall exercise its authority on matters pertaining to libraries and museums,
historical, cultural and archeological sites. Towards this end, it shall study and put forward
the history, and promote the preservation of sites of cultural and archeological significance
found within the region.

Section 3. Balobok Cave. — The Balobok Cave, located in Barangay Lakit-Lakit,
Bongao, Tawi-Tawi is hereby declared as a regional heritage zone. A regional heritage zone
shall refer to historical, anthropological, archeological, and artistic geographic areas that are
culturally significant to the Bangsamoro region.

The Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage (BCPCH) and
the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy (MENRE) shall primarily be in

charge of the actions necessary for the implementation of this Act, the site preservation,
protection, development, and promotion.

Section 4. Profile and natural boundary. — Within six (6) months after the approval of
this Act, the BCPCH, MENRE, in partnership with the Tawi-Tawi Provincial Government shall
initiate the study of the site, not limited to physical inspection, research, measurements,
coordinates, and all other interventions necessary to establish a verified account, precise profile
and jurisdiction of the heritage zone.

Section 5. Protection and management plan. - The BCPCH, MENRE, Ministry of
Trade, Investment and Tourism (MTIT), and the Tawi-Tawi Provincial Government shall
jointly formulate a comprehensive plan for the heritage zone’s preservation, protection,
rehabilitation, infrastructure installation, development into ecotourism, management and
maintenance.

Section 6. Ancestral lands and rights. — Ancestral lands and customary rights and
interests arising from the declared jurisdiction of the heritage zone shall be accorded due
recognition. The BCPCH and MENRE shall jointly prescribe rules and regulations to govern
the ancestral lands within the heritage zone.

Section 7. Coordination. — The BCPCH shall coordinate with national agencies for the
recognition of the Balobok Rock Shelter as a heritage zone. The BCPCH shall also facilitate
the implementation of national programs supplemental to the development plans of the heritage
zone by the region.

Section 8. Jmplementing Rules and Regulations. — Within ninety (90) days from the
passage of this Act, the Bangsamoro Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage, in
close coordination with the Ministry of Environment, Natural Resources and Energy shall
promulgate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act.

Section 9. Appropriations. — Such sums as may be necessary for the initial
implementation of this Act shall be taken from the current appropriations of the Bangsamoro
Commission for the Preservation of Cultural Heritage. Thereafter, the fund necessary to carry
out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act of the
Bangsamoro.

Section 10. Separability Clause. — If any provision of this Act shall be held

unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force
and effect.

Section 11. Repealing Clause. — All laws, executive orders or issuances, or any parts
thereof which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

Section 12. Effectivity Clause. — This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after
publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the region.
