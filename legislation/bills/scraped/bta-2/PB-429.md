EXPLANATORY NOTE

Housing is a central pillar of the Government's poverty alleviation program. It
provides security of tenure, which is as essential to Bangsamoro families as food and
clothing. Without tenurial security, communities are constrained from undertaking
meaningful development activities, and families are unable to plan for a stable and
dignified future.

Access to safe, decent, and affordable housing is a fundamental component of
human dignity, social stability, and sustainable development. The Bangsamoro
Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) continues to face persistent housing
challenges arising from armed conflict-related displacement, rapid urbanization, disaster
vulnerability, and unregulated residential development.

At present, housing and human settlement initiatives in the Bangsamoro Region
remain fragmented, resulting in inconsistent standards, inefficient allocation of resources,
and heightened risks to public safety and community welfare.

Article IX of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (RA 11054) on Basic Rights - Social
Justice states that:

Section 9. Housing and Human Settlements. - The Parliament shall pass a
law creating a housing and human settlements agency to address the lack of
shelters, settlements, and livelihood for the disadvantaged and homeless,
especially those victims of conflicts and atrocities. The agency may directly solicit
and receive assistance, donations, aids, and grants from donors for its housing,
settlements, and livelihood programs and development.

This proposed measure seeks to establish a comprehensive housing and human
settlements framework for BARMM by strengthening the institutional mandate of the
Ministry of Human Settlements and Development, setting clear building, safety, and
occupancy standards, regulating housing use and allocation, and _ institutionalizing
transparency, accountability, and participatory governance mechanisms.

The bill draws from international best practices in housing governance while
remaining firmly anchored on Bangsamoro autonomy, Islamic values, and existing
national laws. It aims to promote orderly and sustainable development, safeguard public
safety, prevent the misuse of housing resources, and ensure equitable and inclusive
access to housing for present and future generations of the Bangsamoro people.

In view of the foregoing, and in fulfillment of the Bangsamoro Organic Law's
mandate, the immediate passage of this bill is earnestly so

Be it enacted by the Parliament of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
Mindanao Assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title - This Act shall be known as the “Bangsamoro Housing and
Human Settlement Development Act of 2026.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - \t is hereby declared the policy of the Bangsamoro
Government to:

a. Uphold the right of every Bangsamoro to safe, decent, affordable, culturally
appropriate housing;
b. Formulate and implement housing and settlement policies and plans;

while fully respecting Bangsamoro autonomy, Islamic values, and national

Ensure public safety, structural integrity, accessibility, and orderly urban and

Strengthen the Ministry of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD) as
the primary housing authority of the Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 3. Purpose or Objectives. - This Act aims to establish a comprehensive,
coherent, and sustainable housing and human settlements framework for the
Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. It seeks to regulate housing
development, ownership, use, and occupancy; ensure compliance with building and
safety standards; and promote transparent, equitable, and accountable housing
allocation. It further aims to strengthen the institutional capacity of the Ministry of Human
Settlements and Development as the housing arm of the Bangsamoro Government.

SECTION 4. Definition of Terms. - For purposes of this Act:

a.

b.

Housing Agency refers to the Ministry of Human Settlements and Development
(MHSD).

Human Settlements and Urban Development shall be understood as the
integrated planning, development, and management of residential areas and
their surrounding environment. This includes not only the construction of
housing units but also the development of resilient communities through the
provision of road networks, public infrastructure, livelihood opportunities,
educational and religious facilities, and access to electricity,
telecommunications, and other essential services. In short, the Housing
Agency is responsible for holistic community development, ensuring that
housing projects contribute to safe, sustainable, and livable human
settlements.

Human Settlements refer to residential communities or developments provided
with adequate housing, basic services, infrastructure, and access to livelihood
opportunities.

Public Housing refers to housing projects that are developed, financed,
assisted, regulated, owned, and/or managed by the Bangsamoro Government,
whether implemented directly or through its ministries, offices, agencies, or
instrumentalities, for the purpose of providing safe, decent, and affordable
housing to underserved and disadvantaged families.

Private Housing refers to housing developed by private individuals,
cooperatives, associations, or juridical entities, subject to regulation under this
Act.

Socialized housing refers to housing programs and projects covering houses
and lots or home lots only undertaken by the Bangsamoro Government or the
private sector for the underprivileged and homeless citizens which shall include
sites and services development, long-term financing, liberalized terms on
interest payments, and such other benefits in accordance with the provisions
of this Act.

SECTION 5. Scope and Application. - This Act shall apply to all subdivision housing and
human settlements within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao
(BARMM), whether public or private, and regardless of funding source, including but not
limited to:

Socialized, economic, and public housing projects;

residential buildings and dwelling units, including residential components of
mixed-use developments.

SECTION 6. Designation of Regulatory Authority. - The Ministry of Human Settlements
and Development (MHSD) is the central housing and human settlements authority of the
Bangsamoro Government. In this capacity, it shall have the power to:

a. Issue, directly or in coordination with appropriate local government units,
permits for housing construction, renovation, and occupancy;

b. Require, as a pre-condition to the issuance of permits and approvals, the
submission of certifications, clearances, or other documentary requirements
from appropriate authorities demonstrating compliance with applicable
building, fire, accessibility, habitability, environmental, and related standards;

c. Conduct inspections, audits, and compliance monitoring of housing projects
and residential buildings;

d. Regulate the use, leasing, and occupancy of public housing projects and
government-assisted housing units; and

e. Impose administrative sanctions, including fines, suspension, or revocation of
permits, for violations of this Act and its implementing rules and regulations.

f. Formulate, adopt, and update housing price ceilings and thresholds for
economic and socialized housing, consistent with Batas Pambansa Blg. 220,
as may be applicable within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region, and in
accordance with standards, guidelines, or policies issued by the Ministry.

g. May directly solicit and receive assistance, donations, aids, and grants from
donors for its housing, settlements, and livelihood programs and development.

h. Enter into partnerships or collaborative arrangements with private sector
developers and other stakeholders to expand housing supply, promote
affordable housing development, and improve access to housing, subject to
applicable laws and regulations.

i. Establish, implement, and enforce programs for affordable housing financing,
including in-house loans or other financial instruments, for eligible Bangsamoro
individuals, families, and communities for the acquisition of housing units,
subject to existing banking laws, applicable provisions on Islamic financing, and

and conditions of the Ministry’s own loan programs.

j. Exercise such other powers and perform such other acts as may be necessary
and incidental to the effective implementation of this Act, consistent with its
mandate, this Act, and existing Bangsamoro laws and policies.

SECTION 7. Housing Planning and Allocation System. - The Ministry of Human
Settlements and Development (MHSD) shall establish a transparent, equitable, and rules-
based housing planning and allocation system for public and government-assisted
housing, which shall ensure:

a. The prioritization of low-income families, internally displaced persons, and
other vulnerable or marginalized sectors, in accordance with established
criteria;

b. Clear, objective, and publicly documented eligibility requirements and selection
procedures for public housing beneficiaries;

c. The prohibition of misuse, unauthorized transfer, subleasing, or illegal leasing
of public housing units, except as may be expressly allowed under MHSD
guidelines; and

d. Regulated resale, transfer, or succession mechanisms, where permitted by law
and subject to conditions prescribed by the MHSD.

SECTION 8. Building and Safety Standards. - All residential buildings and dwelling units
within the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) shall comply
with minimum building and safety standards, as may be prescribed by the Ministry of
Human Settlements and Development (MHSD), including but not limited to:

a. Structural integrity, stability, and durability appropriate to intended residential
use;

b. Fire prevention measures, emergency access, and means of egress;

c. Adequate ventilation, sanitation, lighting, and overall habitability;

d. Accessibility and safety features for persons with disabilities, senior citizens,
women, and children; and

e. Disaster-resilient, climate-responsive, and hazard-appropriate design, taking
into account local environmental and geographic conditions.

The Ministry of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD) shall issue and
periodically update technical standards and guidelines, harmonized with existing national
laws and regulations and adapted from relevant international best practices.

SECTION 9. Regulation of Renovation and Alteration. - No major renovation, structural
alteration, or modification of residential buildings and dwelling units shall be undertaken
without prior approval from the Ministry of Human Settlements and Development (MHSD),
in accordance with its technical standards and guidelines.

The following acts are prohibited:

a. Alterations affecting structural integrity or safety;

b. Unauthorized extensions encroaching on public, common, or easement areas;
and

c. Construction works that pose danger to occupants, neighbors, or the
surrounding community.

SECTION 10. Ownership, Tenure, and Use of Housing Units.

a. Ownership and tenancy of housing units shall be governed by applicable
Bangsamoro and national laws;

b. Residential units shall not be used for commercial, industrial, or illegal purposes
without proper authorization; and

c. The MHSD may impose occupancy limits or conditions in accordance with its
technical standards and guidelines to prevent overcrowding and protect public
health.

SECTION 11. Enforcement and Penalties. - The MHSD is hereby authorized, in
accordance with its technical standards, rules, and applicable administrative procedures,
to:

a. Issue notices of violation and compliance orders;

b. Suspend, revoke, or deny permits and approvals issued by the Ministry, for
failure to comply with the requirements of this Act and its implementing rules
and regulations, without prejudice to permits and clearances issued by other
competent authorities;

c. Impose administrative fines and penalties; and.

d. Order the correction, removal, or demolition of illegal works at the expense of
the violator.

SECTION 12. Community Participation and Cultural Sensitivity. - Housing and settlement
development under this Act shall:

a. Respect Islamic principles of justice (‘adl), equity, and community welfare
(maslahah), consistent with Bangsamoro identity and values;

b. Encourage meaningful participation of Bangsamoro local communities in the
planning, design, and implementation of housing and settlement projects;

c. Recognize extended-family living arrangements, traditional land-use patterns,
and cultural practices prevalent in Bangsamoro communities; and

d. Promote culturally appropriate housing design, including, where practicable, the
provision of prayer spaces and consideration of the gibla orientation in
residential units and community facilities.

SECTION 13. Bangsamoro Housing and Settlement Registry. - The MHSD shall
establish, maintain, and regularly update a Bangsamoro Housing and Settlement
Registry, in electronic or other accessible form, to record:

a. Housing and settlement projects;
b. Ownership and occupancy information; and
c. Permits, compliance status, and enforcement actions.

SECTION 14. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - Within six (6) months from the
effectivity of this Act, the MHSD shall promulgate the Implementing Rules and
Regulations (IRR) in consultation with relevant local government units, stakeholders, and
affected sectors.

SECTION 15. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional
or invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION 16. Repealing Clause. - All Bangsamoro laws, rules, regulations, and
issuances inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed or modified

in a newspaper of general circulation in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim
