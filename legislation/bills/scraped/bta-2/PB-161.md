EXPLANATORY NOTE

The 1987 Philippine Constitution, in Section 15 Article III, declares that the State shall
protect and promote the right to health of the people and instill health consciousness among them.
Section 11 Article XIII declares that the State shall adopt an integrated and comprehensive
approach to health development that shall endeavor to make essential goods, health and other
social services available to all the people at affordable cost!.

Similarly, Section 22, Article IX of Republic Act 11054 otherwise known as the
“Bangsamoro Organic Law” provides that “The Bangsamoro Government shall adopt a policy on
health that provides for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its constituents.
It shall establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its
people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the
prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical services”.

The island province of Sulu is home to more than 1,000,108 people as per the latest census
of the Philippine statistics authority. 7 According to the standards set out by the National

Government under AO 2006-0004 of the Department of Health, the hospital bed-to-population
ratio should not be more than 1 bed per 1000 population.° In the island province however, only
320 beds are available to service the 1 million population of Sulu which leaves us at a deficit of
680 beds from the ideal standards of a set-up by the National Government. Even with the additional
200 beds to be added which would come by virtue of the upgrade of Sulu Provincial Hospital and
the establishment of the Maimbung District Hospital, a total of 480 beds would still be needed in
the province. Moreso in the eastern portion of the island which is only currently being serviced by
a 25-bed capacity infirmary and a 25-bed capacity Level 1 district Hospital for the 4 municipalities
of Luuk, Old Panamao, Kalingalan Caluang, and the coastal Municipality of Omar which
collectively house a population of more than 155,000 people in a land area of more than 900 square
kilometers.*

To put things into perspective, a patient has to travel for more than an hour to reach the
Provincial Hospital in Jolo in case the Luuk District Hospital (LDH) is overwhelmed by patients
and cannot be transferred to the Panamao infirmary just like what happened during the onslaught
of the COVID-19 Pandemic. That travel time can mean the life and death of a person in emergent
situations.

Through this bill, we would be able to greatly improve the service level of healthcare in
the eastern part of the island province by allocating more funds necessary to upgrade Luuk District
Hospital’s bed capacity. As a level 1 district Hospital, LDH can already cater to Major and Minor
Surgeries, Emergency care, Obstetrics and Neonatal care, Laboratory specimen testing, Out Patient
Services and even Dental services. It is opportune that we already have a district hospital that can
offer a wide array of services with only the need for an upgrade in order to serve the people of
Sulu better. With the additional beds, staff and equipment that come with this hospital upgrade,
we should be one step closer to bridging the gap between our service level of health care to the
ideal set-up and becoming the model of government service that the Bangsamoro have always
aspired for our people. In the same manner, we in the Parliament would also be a leap closer to
fulfilling our mandate given by the Bangsamoro Organic Law to “...establish by law a general
hospital system to serve the health requirements of its people and ensure that the individual basic
right to life shall be attainable through the prompt intervention of excellent and affordable medical
services>”’.

It is with these sentiments that I present this bill for the approval of my colleagues in the
Parliament.

Be it enacted by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority in Parliament assembled:

SEC. 1. Short Title. — This Act shall be known as the “UPGRADING OF LUUK HOSPITAL
ACT OF 2023.”

SEC. 2. Declaration of Policy. — \t is the policy of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in
Muslim Mindanao to provide for a comprehensive and integrated health service delivery for its
constituents and establish by law a general hospital system to serve the health requirements of its
people and ensure that the individual basic right to life shall be attainable through the prompt
intervention of excellent and affordable medical services.

SEC. 3. Increase of Bed Capacity of the Luuk District Hospital. — The authorized bed capacity
of the Luuk District Hospital is hereby increased from Twenty-Five (25) to Fifty (50) beds. Any
future increase in bed capacity shall be consistent with its hospital development plan.

SEC. 4. Health Services, Facilities, and Workforce. - The existing professional health care
services and facilities of the Luuk District Hospital are also hereby upgraded to conform with and
be commensurate to the increase in bed capacity.

The existing workforce of the Luuk Hospital, including doctors, nurses and support personnel shall
be correspondingly increased to conform to the upgrade.

The existing medical personnel shall also be correspondingly increased. The Ministry of Health,
in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and Management and the Civil Service
Commission, shall determine the additional plantilla positions to be created and filled up in
coordination with the management of the Luuk District Hospital.

The complementary infrastructure, facilities and medical equipment shall also be correspondingly
constructed and procured in conformity with the increased bed capacity.

SEC. 5. Appropriations. — The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be
included in the Bangsamoro Appropriations Act. Thereafter, funds necessary for the maintenance
and operation of the hospital shall be provided in accordance with the provision of the Bangsamoro
Appropriations Act and other pertinent laws.

SEC. 6. Repealing Clause. - All regional laws, decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations
which are inconsistent with the provisions of this Act are hereby repealed, amended or modified
accordingly.

SEC. 7. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional or invalid
by acourt of competent jurisdiction, all other parts or provisions not affected thereby shall continue

to remain in full force and effect.

SEC. 8. Effectivity. - This act shall take effect fifteen days (15) after its complete publication in at
least two (2) regional newspapers of general circulation.
