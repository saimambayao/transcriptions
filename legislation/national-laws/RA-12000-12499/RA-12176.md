> [ REPUBLIC ACT NO. 12176, April 15, 2025 ]
>
> AN ACT INCREASING THE BED CAPACITY OF THE TOMAS LACHICA DISTRICT HOSPITAL IN THE MUNICIPALITY OF JOSE ABAD SANTOS, PROVINCE OF DAVAO OCCIDENTAL, FROM TWENTY-FIVE (25) TO FIFTY (50) BEDS, AND APPROPRIATING FUND THEREFOR
>
> Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:
>
> **Section 1.** The authorized bed capacity of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital in the Municipality of Jose Abad Santos, Province of Davao Occidental, shall be increased from twenty-five (25) to fifty (50) beds. Any future increase in bed capacity shall be consistent with the hospital development plan that shall be prepared by the Chief of Hospital of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital and submitted to the Department of Health.
>
> **Section 2.** The existing professional healthcare services and facilities of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital shall also be upgraded to conform with and be commensurate to the increase in bed capacity as provided in Section 1 hereof.
>
> **Section 3.** The existing workforce of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital, which includes doctors, nurses, and support personnel, shall be increased correspondingly.ℒαwρhi৷
>
> **Section 4.** The Provincial Government of Davao Occidental shall provide for the necessary funds to cover the maintenance and other operating expenses of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital. It shall also appropriate funds for the additional human resources complement to support the increase in bed capacity and the corresponding upgrading of its service facilities and professional healthcare services.
>
> **Section 5.** The Secretary of Health shall include in the Department's subsidy program, support for the capital outlay requirements of the Tomas Lachica District Hospital.
>
> **Section 6.** If any provision of this Act is declared unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in full force and effect.
>
> **Section 7.** All laws, executive orders, issuances, and rules and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.
>
> **Section 8.** This Act shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following the completion of its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
>
