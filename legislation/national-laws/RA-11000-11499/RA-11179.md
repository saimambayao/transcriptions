# Republic Act No. 11179

**An Act Separating the Cabangahan National High School - Cordova National High School Extension in Barangay Day-As, Municipality of Cordova, Province of Cebu From the Cabangahan National High School, Converting It into An Independent National High School to Be Known as Cordova National High School and Appropriating Funds Therefor**

> **Seventeenth Congress

> *Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-third day of July, two thousand eighteen.*

> **REPUBLIC ACT No. 11179**
>
> **An Act Separating the Cabangahan National High School - Cordova National High School Extension in Barangay Day-As, Municipality of Cordova, Province of Cebu From the Cabangahan National High School, Converting It into An Independent National High School to Be Known as Cordova National High School and Appropriating Funds Therefor**
>
> *Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippine Congress Assembled:*
>
> **Section 1.** - The Cabangahan National High School - Cordova National High School Extension in Barangay Day-as, Municipality of Cordova, Province of Cebu is hereby separated from the Cabangahan National High School and converted into an independent national high school to be known as Cordova National High School.1âwphi1
>
> **Section 2.** - All personnel, assets, liabilities, and records of the Cabangahan National High School - Cordova National High School Extension are hereby transferred to and absorbed by the Cordova National High School.
>
> **Section 3.** - The Secretary of Education shall immediately include in the Department's program the operationalization of the Cordova National High School, the initial funding of which shall be charged against the current year's appropriation of the Cabangahan National High School - Cordova National High School Extension, Thereafter, the amount necessary for the continued operation of the school shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
>
> **Section 4.** - The Secretary of Education shall formulate the rules and regulations necessary to carry out the purpose of this Act within the one hundred twenty (120) days after its effectivity.
>
> **Section 5.** - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the *Official Gazette* or in any newspaper of general circulation.
