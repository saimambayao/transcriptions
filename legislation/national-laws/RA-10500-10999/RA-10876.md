# Republic Act No. 10876

**An Act Separating the Kalinga National High School-Ambato Tinglayan Annex in Barangay Ambato-Legleg, Municipality of Tinglayan, Province of Kalinga from the Kalinga National High School, Converting it into an Independent National High School to be Known as Tinglayan National High School and Appropriating Funds Therefor**

> Sixteenth Congress
> Third Regular Session
>
> *Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-seventh day of July, two thousand fifteen.*

> **REPUBLIC ACT No. 10876**
>
> **An Act Separating the Kalinga National High School-Ambato Tinglayan Annex in Barangay Ambato-Legleg, Municipality of Tinglayan, Province of Kalinga from the Kalinga National High School, Converting it into an Independent National High School to be Known as Tinglayan National High School and Appropriating Funds Therefor**
>
> *Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippine Congress Assembled:*
>
> **Section 1.** The Kalinga National High School-Ambato Tinglayan Annex in Barangay Ambato-Legleg, Municipality of Tinglayan, Province of Kalinga is hereby separated from the Kalinga National High School and converted into an independent national high school to be known as Tinglayan National High School.1âwphi1
>
> **Section 2.** All personnel, assets, liabilities and records of the Kalinga National High School-Ambato Tinglayan Annex are hereby transferred to and absorbed by the Tinglayan National High School.
>
> **Section 3.** The Secretary of Education shall immediately include in the Departments program the operationalization of the Tinglayan National High School, the initial funding of which shall be charged against the current years appropriation of the Kalinga National High School-Ambato Tinglayan Annex. Thereafter, the amount necessary for the continued operation of the school shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
>
> **Section 4.** The Secretary of Education shall issue rules and regulations necessary to carry out the purpose of this Act.
>
> **Section 5.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
