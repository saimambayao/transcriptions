> Eighteenth Congress
> Third Regular Session

> *Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-sixth day of July, two thousand twenty one.*
>
> **[ REPUBLIC ACT NO. 11715, April 29, 2022 ]**
>
> **AN ACT COTABATO SANITARIUM IN THE MUNICIPALITY OFSULTAN KUDARAT, PROVINCE OF MAGUINDANAO INTO A GENERAL HOSPITAL TO BE KNOWN AS THE COTABATO SANITARIUM AND GENERAL HOSPITAL, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR**
>
> **Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:**
>
> **Section 1.** The Cotabato Sanitarium in the Municipality of Sultan Kudarat, Province of Maguindanao, is hereby converted into a general hospital to be known as the Cotabato Sanitarium and General Hospital (CSGH), which shall be under the direct control and supervision of the Department of Health (DOH).
>
> **Section 2.** The bed capacity of the CSGH shall be increased from forty (40) to two hundred fifty (250) beds. Any future increase in bed capacity shall be inconsistent with the hospital development plan which shall be prepared by the DOH.
>
> **Section 3.** The existing professional health care services and facilities of the CSGH shall also be upgraded to conform with and be commensurate to the increase in bed capacity as provided for in Section 2 hereof.
>
> **Section 4.** The existing workforce of the CSGH, which includes doctors, nurses, and support personnel, shall also be increased correspondingly.ℒαwρhi৷ The Secretary of the DOH shall, after consultation with the Chief of the CSGH, and in coordination with the Secretary of the Department of Budget and Management and the Commissioner of the Civil Service Commission, determine the additional new positions to be created and filled up.
>
> **Section 5.** The Secretary of Health shall immediately included in the DOH's program the implementation of this Act, the funding of which shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.
>
> **Section 6.** If any provision or part of this Act is declared unconstitutional, the remaining parts or provisions not affected shall remain in full force and effect.
>
> **Section 7.** All laws, executive orders, issuances, rules and regulations inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, or modified accordingly.
>
> **Section 8.** This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
