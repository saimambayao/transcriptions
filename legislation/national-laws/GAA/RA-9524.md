## Title

AN ACT APPROPRIATING FUNDS FOR THE OPERATION OF THE GOVERNMENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF THE PHILIPPINES FROM JANUARY ONE TO DECEMBER THIRTY-ONE, TWO THOUSAND AND NINE, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES.

## Enacting Clause

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

The following sums, or so much thereof as may be necessary, are hereby appropriated from the National Treasury of the Philippines not otherwise appropriated, for the operation of the Government of the Republic of the Philippines from January one to December thirty-one, two thousand and nine.

---

## Section I: Congress of the Philippines

### A. Senate

**Total Appropriation: P2,290,522,000**

Programs:
- General Administration and Support Services: P1,116,836,000
- Legislative Services: P936,012,000

Locally-Funded Projects included operational requirements for various congressional oversight committees addressing:
- Agricultural Modernization
- Labor and Employment
- E-Commerce
- The RP-US Visiting Forces Agreement
- Environmental matters (Clean Air Act, Ecological Solid Waste Management)
- Dangerous Drugs oversight
- Anti-Money Laundering initiatives

### A.1 Senate Electoral Tribunal

**Appropriation: P119,798,000**

Adjudication of electoral contests involving Members of the Senate.
- Personal Services: P51,981,000
- Operating Expenses: P67,817,000

### B. Commission on Appointments

**Appropriation: P289,810,000**

Review or confirmation of appointments submitted to the Commission: P142,922,000

### C. House of Representatives

**Total Appropriation: P4,578,747,000**

The House budget structure mirrored the Senate, with general administration, legislative services, and numerous locally-funded oversight committee projects including those addressing civil aviation, automated election systems, human security, credit information systems, and universal access to medicines.

### C.1 House Electoral Tribunal

**Appropriation: P90,413,000**

Adjudication of electoral contests involving House members.

### Congressional Special Provisions

1. **Organizational Flexibility**: The Senate President and House Speaker, along with their respective electoral tribunal chairs, were authorized to reorganize the organizational structures of their respective offices, to fix and determine the rights of their respective members, employees and consultants. This included authority to create new positions and adjust existing ones, with affected personnel receiving retirement gratuities and separation pay.

2. **Automatic Release**: Appropriations authorized for the Congress shall be automatically and regularly released.

3. **Operational Realignment**: Individual members could realign their operational expense allocations across categories without exceeding total amounts.

### Congress of the Philippines Total

- Personal Services: P3,339,453,000
- Operating Expenses: P3,840,012,000
- Capital Outlays: P189,825,000
- **Total: P7,369,290,000**

## Section IV: Department of Agrarian Reform

**Appropriation: P7,868,521,000** (P7,866,164,000 from regular appropriations; P2,357,000 from the Fiduciary Fund)

### A. Office of the Secretary

**Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program (CARP):**

Allocated P5,931,146,000 specifically for land acquisition and distribution, targeting lands under the Voluntary Offer to Sell (VOS) and Voluntary Land Transfer programs as of December 31, 2008. Distribution:

- Department of Agrarian Reform (DAR): P4,103,460,000
- Bureau of Rehabilitation of Ecological Damage and Rehabilitation (BREDE): P446,720,000
- Department of Justice Land Registration Authority: P102,059,000
- Department of Finance Land Bank of the Philippines: P1,278,907,000

Program Beneficiaries Development Component under the Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Program: P5,288,607,000

**Suspension of Land Acquisition and Distribution**: No amount shall be appropriated/used for land acquisition or distribution except for those commenced as of December 31, 2008.
