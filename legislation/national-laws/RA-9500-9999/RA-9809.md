# Republic Act No. 9809

**An Act Separating the Manga National High School - Mansasa Annex in Barangay Mansasa, City of Tagbilaran, Province of Bohol from the Manga National High School, Converting it into an Independent National High School to Be Known as Mansasa National High School and Appropriating Funds Therefor**

> Fourteenth Congress
> Third Regular Session
>
> *Begun and held in Metro Manila, on Monday, the twenty-seventh day of July, two thousand nine.*
>
> **REPUBLIC ACT No. 9809               November 26, 2009**
>
> AN ACT SEPARATING THE MANGA NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL - MANSASA ANNEX IN BARANGAY MANSASA, CITY OF TAGBILARAN, PROVINCE OF BOHOL FROM THE MANGA NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL, CONVERTING IT INTO AN INDEPENDENT NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL TO BE KNOWN AS MANSASA NATIONAL HIGH SCHOOL AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR
>
> *Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representative of the Philippines in Congress assembled:*
>
> **Section 1.** *Separation and Conversion into a National High School.* - The Manga National High School - Mansasa Annex in Barangay Mansasa, City of Tagbilaran, Province of Bohol is hereby separated from the Manga National High School and converted into an independent national high school to be known as Mansasa National High School.
>
> **Section 2.** *Transfer of Assets and Liabilities.* - All personnel, assets, liabilities and records of the Manga National High School - Mansasa Annex are hereby transferred to and absorbed by the Mansasa National High School.
>
> **Section 3.** *Appropriations.* The Secretary of Education shall immediately include in the Department's program the operationalization of the Mansasa National High School, the initial funding of which shall be charged against the current year's appropriations of the Manga National High School - Mansasa Annex. Thereafter, the amount necessary for the continued operation of the school shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.1awphil
>
> **Section 4.** *Implementation.* - The Secretary of Education shall issue rules and regulations that may be necessary to carry out the purpose of this Act.
>
> **Section 5.** *Effectivity.* - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the *Official Gazette.*
>
