WHEREAS, Executive Order No. 292, otherwise known as the Administrative
“
Code of 1987”, Book IV, Title III, Chapter 10, Section 31 provides for the authority of
the Secretary of Justice to review, revise and/or promulgate new rules and regulations
to govern the conduct of proceedings in the Board of Commissioners and the Boards
of Special Inquiry, including the determination of the size and number of the support
staff to be assigned thereat;
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Immigration (Bl) issued on 04 July 2016 Immigration
Administrative Order No. JHM-2016-001 establishing the Office of the Secretary of the
BI Board of Commissioners (BOC) and defining its powers, duties and functions;
WHEREAS, Department Order No. 786 and BI Personnel Order JHM No. 2016-
644 were issued on 10 November 2016 and 14 November 2016, respectively,
designating a Secretary of the BI Board of Commissioners;
WHEREAS, the BOC Secretary performs multifarious functions, including: (i)
preparation of the agenda for the deliberations of the BOC on visa applications,
deportation cases, and all other matters requiring BOC’s action; (ii) implementation of
the BOC’s decision on visa applications, including revalidation, and deportation cases;
and (iii) preparation, keeping and managing of the minutes and all records of the BOC,
among others;
WHEREAS, in order to further enhance the overall administrative and
operational efficiency in the performance of its duties and functions, delivery of
immigration services, and to promote and maintain integrity, transparency and
accountability in public service pursuant to Republic Act No. 11032 (Ease of Doing
Business and Efficient Government Service Delivery Act of 2018) and Republic Act
No. 6713 (Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees) ,
there is a need to rationalize and delineate the duties and functions of the BOC
Secretary;

NOW, THEREFORE, and consistent with the provisions of existing laws and
rules, the following are hereby promulgated, as follows:
1. REVOCATION OF IMMIGRATION ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER NO. JHM-2016-
001
A
Immigration Administrative Order No. JHM-2016-001 issued on 04 July
2016 establishing the Office of the Secretary of the Board of Commissioners
in the Bureau of Immigration and defining its powers, duties and functions
is hereby revoked. Consequently, Department Order No. 786 and Personnel
Order JHM No. 2016-644 dated 10 November 2016 and 14 November 2016,
respectively, are likewise revoked and the position of the Secretary of the
BI Board of Commissioners is hereby abolished.
B Within fifteen (15) working days from the effectivity of this Circular, the
incumbent BOC Secretary shall complete the: (i) conduct an inventory of all
properties, materials, passports, visa implementation stamps, documents
and records; (ii) prepare a terminal report of the activities and affairs of the
Office of the BOC Secretary; and (iii) proper turn-over of the said inventory
and report to the Transition Team composed of the respective Chiefs of Staff
of the Offices of the Commissioner and two (2) Deputy Commissioners.
C Upon completion of the turn-over, all BI organic and contractual personnel
assigned at the Office of the Board Secretary shall report to their respective
mother offices while Job Order employees shall report to the Personnel
Section for assignment of duties.
11. ESTABLISHMENT OF AGENDA AND RECORDS MANAGEMENT UNIT
(BOC-ARMU) UNDER THE BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS
A
There is hereby created a unit under the BOC to be named as Agenda and
Records Management Unit (BOC-ARMU) to be headed by a Unit Head
designated by the Commissioner and concurred by the Deputy
Commissioners.
B. The BOC-ARMU Head shall:
1 Prepare the agenda on visa application, deportation cases and other
matters for the deliberation of the BOC:
2 Attend in and take the minutes of all meetings of the BOG;
3 Keep and manage all BOC records; and
4. Perform other related functions as may be directed and assigned by
the BOC.
111 ESTABLISHMENT OF IMPLEMENTATION UNIT (BOC-lU) UNDER THE
BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS
A
There is hereby created a unit under the BOC to be named as
Implementation Unit (BOC-lU) to be headed by a Unit Head designated by
the Commissioner and concurred by the Deputy Commissioners.

B. The BOC-lU Head shall:
1. Implement all BOC Orders approving or denying visa applications,
including applications for visa revalidation;
2 Disseminate to the proper offices, for implementation, all BOC
decisions, resolutions and orders on deportation cases and all other
deportation-related matters; and
3. Perform other related functions as may be directed and assigned by
the BOC.
IV. PERSONNEL COMPLEMENT AND BUDGET ALLOCATION FOR THE BOC-
ARMU AND BOC-lU
A. The Commissioner, through appropriate Personnel Orders, shall assign
sufficient support personnel/staff and provide budgetary allocation for the
operations of the BOC-ARMU and BOC-lU sourced from the BI existing
plantilla and budget. The Commissioner may likewise engage the services
of Job Order employees, as may be necessary, for assignment to the said
Units to augment its personnel complement.
All issuances, rules and regulations, or any part thereof, that are inconsistent with
this Circular are hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.
This Circular shall take effect immediately.
