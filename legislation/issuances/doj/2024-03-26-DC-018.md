1. RATIONALE
Sections 30, 31, and 34 of Chapter 5, Title I-A, Book V of Executive Order No.
292, s. 1987, or the Administrative Code of 1987, provides that the development
and retention of a competent and efficient work force in the public service is a
primary concern of the government. Every department is directed to establish
a continuing program and plan for career and personnel development of all
government employees, which shall include, among others, in-service training,
overseas and local scholarships, and training grants.
In line with its commitment to good governance and service excellence, the
Department of Justice (DOJ) recognizes that enhancing human resource
management and workforce productivity is a powerful driver in achieving
organizational objectives and upholding excellence in public service. This is
achieved through strengthened personnel learning and development programs,
alongside streamlined processes and robust internal controls.
In 2022, Department Circular (DC) No. 025 was issued, establishing the
Scholarship and Personnel Development Program (SPDP) to provide a more
efficient, responsive, comprehensive, and strategic development system aimed
at empowering employees and fostering organizational growth.
During the implementation of various DOJ in-house training programs, it was
determined that there is a need to develop a framework for implementing all
Learning and Development Intervention (LDI) programs to aid officials and
supervisors in applying the appropriate strategies and interventions to address
gaps in the performance of service, enhance competencies, improve job
performance, increase productivity, and strengthen the DOJ workforce. Such a
framework shall also cover monitoring and evaluation in all stages of the LDI to
ensure quality in the delivery of processes and outputs, as well as inputs for
continuous improvement.
In view of the foregoing and pursuant to the standards set forth by the Civil
Service Commission (CSC) – Program to Institutionalize Meritocracy and

11. OBJECTIVES
A. General Objectives
These Guidelines aim to establish standards for planning, assessing,
designing, delivering, and evaluating L&D programs and activities, including the
results and expected outputs, thereby providing equal opportunities for L&D to
all DOJ employees.
B. Specific Objectives
1. Identify step-by-step processes and procedures that will guide the Training
Section and supervisors in the development, provision, coordination,
delivery, and evaluation of L&D activities of the DOJ;
2. Identify and implement appropriate LDls that will address existing
competencies or skill gaps; and
3. Ensure that each employee is given the opportunity to reach his/her full
potential through the development of knowledge, skills, behavior, and
abilities acquired during trainings.
111. SCOPE AND COVERAGE
These Guidelines shall apply to all permanent, coterminous, and contractual
(with appointment) officials and employees, who shall undergo formal and non-
formal LDls identified in the Learning Needs Assessment (LNA), Individual
Development Plan (IDP), and Individual Performance Commitment and Review
(IPCFR), upon the recommendation of the concerned immediate supervisors.
All formal and non-formal educational programs and LDls requested to be
attended by employee/s that are not included or identified in the LNA, IDP, or
IPCR shall continue to be governed by DC No. 25, s. 2022, subject to the
provisions of Items V and VI below.
IV DEFINITION OF TERMS
A. External or Specialized Training Program – refers to Formal LDI that is
conducted outside the organization, usually by an external learning service
provider, that focuses on specific skills, knowledge, or competencies not
typically covered by internal training programs. These programs are often
highly specialized and tailored to particular industries, professions, or
advanced technical areas.
B Formal Educational Program – refers to a structured and systematic
educational experience provided by accredited institutions like universities,
colleges, or professional schools, funded by the Department or by
sponsoring partner agencies or scholarship-granting bodies. It typically
leads to a recognized certification, diploma, or degree and follows a set
curriculum approved by educational authorities.

C. Formal Learning and Development Intervention (Formal LDI) – refers to
a structured training program designed to enhance the employee’s
knowledge, skills, and attitudes, This is typically delivered through organized
sessions, such as classroom-based training or online courses, and is
aligned with the agency's strategic objectives and the individual
development needs of employees.
D. Individual Development Plan (IDP) – refers to a tool that assists
employees in developing their knowledge, skills, and attitude to achieve
performance goals. It is a two-way commitment between the employee and
the immediate supervisor on the specific interventions that will be
undertaken to meet the current and future competency needs.
E. Internal or In-House Training Program – refers to Formal LDI conducted
within the organization, typically by internal experts or specialized trainers,
aimed at enhancing the skills, knowledge, and competencies of employees
in alignment with the organization’s specific needs.
F. Learning and Development Implementation Monitoring Matrix
(Training Program Monitoring Report) – refers to a structured document
that outlines the details necessary for the execution of the L&D Plan. It
includes information such as the title of the intervention, target participants,
objectives, schedule, budget requirements, responsible units or persons,
and expected outputs. The matrix serves as a planning and monitoring tool
to ensure the organized, timely, and effective delivery of L&D interventions
in alignment with organizational goals.
G. Learning and Development Intervention (LDI) – refers to any organized
activity or program designed to improve employees' knowledge, skills, and
attitudes, bridge competency gaps, and align individual capabilities with
organizational goals, ensuring an effective and ethical delivery of public
service
H. Learning Needs Assessment (LNA) or Training Needs Assessment
(TNA) – refers to the systematic process used to identify the knowledge,
skills, and abilities that employees or individuals need to acquire in order to
perform effectively in their roles or meet organizational goals. This process
helps in determining gaps between current competencies and the desired
level of performance, which in turn guides the design and delivery of training
programs.
I Non-Formal Educational Program – refers to all forms of local or foreign
training, short-course programs, internships, study visits or observations,
conventions, conferences, or forums intended to attain or improve skills and
competencies. This shall not cover the attendance or participation in
meetings, negotiations, dialogues, conventions, conferences, congressional
hearings, court proceedings, technical working group sessions, or
consultations, in which the official/employee represents his/her office/unit or
the Department.

J. Non-Formal Learning and Development Intervention (Non-Formal LDI)
– refers to structured programs or activities that occur outside traditional
classroom settings or formal training structures. This intervention is
organized to enhance employees’ knowledge, skills and attitudes, but is
typically more experiential, hands-on, and immediately applicable to daily
work which is highly adaptable to the immediate needs of the learner or the
organization
.
K. Study Leave – refers to the study leave privilege under the CSC Omnibus
Rule on Leave, as amended, allowing officials and employees a time-off
from work with pay in preparation for their bar/board examinations or in their
completion of master's degree.

VIII. LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT INTERVENTIONS (LDls)
A. Qualification and Selection of Learners.
The selection of learners/participants in LDls shall be based on the IDPs,
LNA results, and IPCR of officials and employees, and shall not in any case
be left to the discretion of an employee, as some programs may pre-identify
learners based on technical requirements.
All employees whose IDP indicates performance gaps that are crucial in the
performance of their functions or in the next higher position shall be
prioritized in the LDls.
For internal or in-house training programs that require registration or
submission of an application, a first-come, first-served basis shall be
implemented. Registration shall be closed once the target number of
learners is reached or upon the end of the set deadline, whichever comes
first. Confirmation of participation shall be sent to the interested learners
thereafter.
