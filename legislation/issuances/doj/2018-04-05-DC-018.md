SUBJECT :(cid:9) Rationalization of Reportorial Requirements on Cases

1.0(cid:9) For purposes of efficiency and protection of personal information, policy
guidance on the above-captioned subject are hereby set forth, particularly
phasing out specific reportorial requirements and prescribing procedures on
reports that will be maintained or required.
Periodic/Regular Case Reports Phased Out
2.0(cid:9) All periodic/regular reports on cases handled by the National Prosecution
Service including the following requirements under previous issuances shall no
longer be submitted to the Department, with exceptions in item number 3.0:
2.1(cid:9) Monthly report of convictions and copies of court decisions;
2.2(cid:9) Reports on child abuse and other child-related cases;
2.3(cid:9) Reports on violence against women and their children;
2.4(cid:9) Inventory and status report of environmental cases;
2.5(cid:9) Report of Ombudsman/corruption cases; and
2.6(cid:9) Monthly report on DOJ Jail Decongestion Program.
Case Reports to be Maintained
3.0(cid:9) The following reports on cases shall be maintained:
3.1(cid:9) Quarterly Report of Operations per Department Circular No. 051,s. 2015;
3.2(cid:9) Quarterly report for the Dangerous Drugs Board (DDB) on illegal drugs
cases filed in court (i.e. directly to DDB without need to furnish the D0J)
in line with its Integrated Drug Abuse Data Information Network and
pursuant to Department Order No. 211, s. 2008;
3.3(cid:9) Established regular case report/s specifically required for submission to
special programs/inter-agency bodies, including the Inter-Agency
Council Against Trafficking (IACAT) and AO 35 Task Force;
3.4(cid:9) Monthly inventory report of cybercrime and cyber-related cases pursuant
to Department Circular No. 002, s. 2018; and
3.5(cid:9) Case registry and reports that may be required as needed, in relation to
specific offenses such as dangerous drugs (i.e. per separate issuance/s);

4.0(cid:9) While not required for regular submission to the DOJ, prosecution offices are
advised to have an internal registry or record of Ombudsman/corruption cases
pursuant to DOJ-OMB Memorandum of Agreement dated 29 March 2012. NPS
regional/office heads may require or maintain other reports for internal
documentation, monitoring and evaluation subject to legitimate specific
purpose and personal data protection, but not for submission to the DOJ.
Policies/Procedure on Reportorial Requirements on NPS Cases
5.0(cid:9) All offices including Offices of the Secretary and other senior officials shall refer
to the Planning and Management Service any data/statistical requirement on
NPS-handled cases prior to directing or requesting prosecution offices to submit
reports thereon. This is to avoid redundant/overlapping/inconsistent reports
on NPS cases and to ensure harmonized requirements, procedures and forms.
6.0(cid:9) For purposes of efficiency and data privacy, such reports shall be required on
need basis only, subject to approval, authorization and/or issuance of the
Secretary of Justice. Reports for regular submission shall only be for
exceptional and justifiable reasons, subject to policy/oversight/regulatory
guidance or requirements. Case reports involving personal information covered
by the Data Privacy Act of 2012 shall be required by the Department and
released by the office/unit concerned in accordance with the specific purpose
and control procedure including the appropriate processing unit.
7.0(cid:9) DOJ and prosecution offices concerned shall appropriately/adequately secure
and control such case reports including all physical/electronic records and
personal information therein, in accordance with the Data Privacy Act, its
Implementing Rules and Regulations, and relevant issuances of the National
Privacy Commission and this Department. DOJ offices concerned shall
appropriately dispose or return to prosecution offices, as applicable, submitted
case reports that are unnecessary and not required.
8.0(cid:9) The Department shall continually evaluate and improve the monitoring and
statistical systems relating to criminal cases handled by the NPS/DOJ in line
with management, policy and oversight requirements, and facilitate the
development and implementation of electronic system/s for efficient and secure
recording, monitoring and reporting of said cases using available information
and communications technology resources.
9.0(cid:9) The Prosecutor General and Regional Prosecutors are advised to immediately
disseminate this Circular to all constituent provincial/city prosecution offices
within respective regional jurisdictions, and facilitate/ensure compliance.
10.0 This Circular takes effect immediately and supersedes all previous issuances/
directives inconsistent therewith.
