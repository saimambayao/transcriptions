SUBJECT COMPLIANCE WITH ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER No. 30, S.
2020
DATE 28 April 2020
Attached is a copy of Administrative Order (AO) No. 30 dated 21 April 2020
entitled, Directing all Government Offices to Formulate and Issue Guidelines on the
Interruption of Periods for the Filing of Documents, Payment of Taxes, Charges and
Other Fees, and Cancellation of Proceedings Before Their Respective Offices, and
on the Movement of Timelines for the Release of Benefits.
Pursuant thereto, heads of Bureaus, Commissions and Offices attached to the
DOJ are hereby enjoined to comply with the directive to immediately formulate and
issue rules or guidelines, consistent with the directives of the Inter-Agency Task
Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (lATF), on the: (i)
interruption of reglementary periods for the commencement of actions/claims, filing
of pleadings and all other papers, and rendition of judgments, resolutions, and orders
for the duration of the community quarantine; (ii) cancellation of proceedings and
rescheduling thereof after the lifting of the community quarantine; (iii) acceptable
justifications for failure of litigants and claimants to comply with reglementary periods
or to appear at legal proceedings if they are coming from jurisdictions where
quarantine protocols restrict their movements; (iv) suspension of deadlines for the
payment of monetary obligations and/or submission of documents for the duration of
the community quarantine; and (v) movement of timelines for the grant of any
benefit, to allow the release thereof for the duration of the community quarantine.
In connection therewith and pursuant to the Memorandum from the Executive
Secretary dated 31 March 2020 on the Submission of Report to Congress Pursuant
to Republic Act No. 11469 of the Bayanihan to Heal as One Act, you are hereby
requested to immediately submit your respective agency's report on the rules and
guidelines you may have implemented to comply with the said AO.
For information, guidance and compliance.
MENARDO I. GUEVARRA
