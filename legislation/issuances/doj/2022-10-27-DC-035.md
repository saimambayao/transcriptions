Scope
2.0 This Circular covers all Regional, Provincial and City Prosecution offices of the
National Prosecution Service (NPS), except for the DOJ Prosecution Staff
which is under the DOJ main office cluster and covered by separate issuance/s.
Nevertheless, the Prosecutor General shall discharge the herein regional
performance management over prosecution offices in the National Capital
Region (NCR).
Office Performance Evaluation Measures and Requirements
3.0 Prosecution offices shall be grouped according to the following prosecutor-case
ratio categories, covering cases handled for both investigation/resolution and
prosecution in court
3.1 Light caseload – Less than 250 cases per prosecutor;
3.2 Moderate caseload – At least 250 per prosecutor;
3.3 Average caseload – At least 500 cases per prosecutor;
3.4 Heavy caseload – At least 1,000 per prosecutor; and
3-5 Extremely heavy caseload – At least 1500 per prosecutor.
4.0 Rating of each office and regional cluster shall be based on the performance
indicators, targets and rating/ranking system implemented under D.C. No. 049,
s. 2014, as detailed/illustrated in Annex A:
4.1 As agreed during the Midyear Assessment with the Regional
Prosecutors on 09 September 2022, the following performance weight
shall be adopted for the office performance evaluation:
4.1.1 Case Disposition Rate (resolved cases as percentage of all cases
handled for investigation/resolution) – 40% weight;
4.1.2
Case Aging (percentage of criminal complaints for preliminary
investigation pending within 120 days) – 40% weight; and
4.1.3 Prosecution Success Rate (proportion of convictions against
acquittals) – 10% weight;
4.1.4 Timeliness on the submission of reportorial requirement – 10%
weight.
4.2 Two (2) scores shall be given for every indicator referring to the following
criteria/rating scales, the average of which shall serve as the final
performance rating for the indicator.
4.2.1 Existing rating scale under D.C. No. 049, s. 2014, which assigns
scores for particular decrease or increase in performance

5.0 The following are prescribed in the preparation, review and submission of
Quarterly Reports of Operations (QRO) by all prosecution offices:
5.1 Provincial/city Prosecution offices in Regions 1 to 14, and Prosecution
offices in the NCR shall prepare and submit/encode their QRO on the
reporting template provided to their respective Regional Prosecution
Office and the Office of the Prosecutor General, respectively, within
seven (7) days after the end of the quarter covered;
5.2 Regional Prosecution Offices and the Office of the Prosecutor General
shall review and ensure the correctness/completeness of data/reports
encoded by their constituent offices, and submit/encode their own
report, within fifteen (15) days after the end of the subject quarter.
5.3 The Regional Prosecution Offices and Office of the Prosecutor General
shall ensure timely compliance on the submission of consolidated
QROs (excel copy and signed copy) to the Planning and Management
Service - Evaluation and Statistics Division (PMS-ESD) every 15th day
after each quarter.
5.4 For purposes of performance management and accountability, and
reporting to oversight agencies, the information/data encoded in the
said electronic facility as reviewed/endorsed by the Regional Heads will
be deemed by the Department as official, subject to validation based
on the Means of Verification (MOV).

7.0
Prosecution Offices which failed to submit the required performance reports by
the end of the performance year shall automatically be given a score of zero (0)
in the three Performance indicators (Case disposition, Case aging and
Prosecution Success Rate) unless a valid justification be provided not more
than five (5) days after the end of the submission period.
Late submission of the reportorial requirements shall affect the score of the
concerned Prosecution Office in the timeliness indicator.
8.0 The PMS-ESD shall evaluate the reports submitted. A report with
evaluation/findings on the data consistencies/correctness shall be prepared
and coordinated with the concerned Prosecution Office for clarification and
further amendments/corrections.
The timeliness of the submission of the reportorial requirements shall also be
rated using the set standards, as prescribed in this guideline.
9.0 Provincial and City Prosecution Offices with calculated final average score of 2
or 1 may be given “satisfactory” adjectival rating instead of “unsatisfactory or
poor”, respectively, by Regional Heads if there were factors beyond their control
that led to the low final scores. For this, approved explanation/justification shall
be attached on the vetted summary of rating of the concerned Region and
indicated on the remarks.
10.0 The rating of each regional cluster (i.e. average of final weighted scores of all
offices therein) and every prosecution office shall serve as the rating of the
regional and office head, respectively. For office with more than one (1) chief
of office during the year, all chiefs who served as office head for at least three
(3) months shall receive the said office rating.
Requirements for Individuals/Employees
11.0 The performance of the heads of regional, provincial and city prosecution
offices shall be rated semi-annually based on the evaluated performance of
their respective offices within the region for the period January to June, and
July to December to conform with the policy of the CSC. Hence, the calculated
performance rating approved by the Prosecutor General and Regional
Prosecutors, as the case may be, shall serve as the final performance rating of
the heads of city and provincial prosecution offices. The performance ratings
of Regional Prosecutors shall be approved by the Secretary upon
determination/ recommendation of the PMT.
12.0 First and second level employees/officers shall be rated through the SPMS
under CSC M.C. No. 6, s. 2012, and D.C. No. 049, s. 2014, particularly using
the prescribed Individual Performance Commitment and Review (IPCR) form
as approved by respective supervisors and heads of offices.

13.0 For Regions 1 to 14, Individual Performance Commitment and Review (IP(;R)
forms of employees shall be securely retained by the regional offices as part of
official employee records and among the basis for professional and career
development interventions/programs therein. Only the Summary List of
Employee Ratings prescribed under D.C. 049, s. 2014 (Annex “B”), shall be
submitted to the Personnel Division.
14.0 For NCR prosecution offices, approved IPCR forms and Summary List of
Employee Ratings shall be submitted to the Personnel Division through the
DOJ Prosecution Staff.
15.0 Pursuant to CSC MC No. 6, s. 2012, the average rating of all individual
performance assessments shall not be higher than the adjectival performance
rating of the office based on evaluation and rating by the PMT. If otherwise, the
head of office concerned shall be required to adjust the individual employee
ratings to conform with the said policy.
Implementation Procedures
16.0 The PMT shall oversee the implementation of these guidelines and provide
further guidance or management/policy recommendations on this matter.
17.0 The ESD shall also provide the PMT the result of the evaluation of office
performance based on Quarterly Reports of Operations (QRO) with attached
Means of Verification vis-a-vis the herein adopted indicators, targets and rating
scale, as well as compliance with all other herein requirements.
18.0 The final performance ratings based on the result of evaluation, as laid down
by these guidelines, shall be the basis of all Performance-Based Incentives and
other entitlements of the employees.
19.0 The Personnel Division shall enforce the requirements and rating/ranking
criteria at the individual/employee level, including compliance with the SPMS,
policy stated in item 16.0, and other pertinent Civil Service and compensation
rules and regulations.
20.0 The Prosecutor General and Regional Prosecutors shall undertake necessary
measures to manage the performance of constituent offices within respective
jurisdictions, including the timely and proper coordination, reporting, monitoring,
documentation and validation.
21.0 The PMT shall duly inform the Prosecutor General and Regional Prosecutors
concerned on compliance with the deficiencies and calculated office
performance and rating based on the reports received and processed.
22.0 The PMT shall conduct periodic validation/review of regional and office
performance ratings especially those that will have significant
implications/impact on personnel actions such as promotion, incentives and
sanctions.
