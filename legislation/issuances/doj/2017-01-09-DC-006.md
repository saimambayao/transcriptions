SUBJECT : SUBMISSION OF MONTHLY ASSESSMENT AND

SERVICES, PROGRAMS AND COMMITTEES
In the interest of the service, pursuant to existing laws, and in line with the
supervision and control of the Secretary of Justice over the Department Proper, the
following offices in the Department are DIRECTED to submit a MONTHLY
ASSESSMENT AND ACCOMPLISHMENT REPORT, viz:
Planning and Management Service
Administrative Service
Financial Service
Internal Audit Service
Technical Staff
Legal Staff
Board of Pardons and Parole
Office of Cybercrime
Office for Competition
Witness Protection, Benefit and Security Program
Board of Claims/Victims Compensation Program
12.A0 35 Task Force
Inter-Agency Council Against Trafficking
Committee on Women and Children
15.D03 Action Center
The Monthly Report shall be brief and contain only the following:
1. Accomplishment
Monthly progress on annual targets;
Actual accomplishment/s (0/0 of performance targets);
Explanation on variance between targets and outputs, if any.
2. Assessment

### Page 2

Problems and issues in their respective offices, with identified
responsibility/responsible officer, if any;
Actions taken or recommended solutions;
Proposals to take a matter on the agenda.
The Monthly Report shall be submitted to the Planning and Management
Service, Department of Justice, not later than three (3) working days after the
close of every month. The PMS shall compile the same and provide copies thereof
for the Office of the Secretary and the Undersecretary-in-charge of the office
concerned within one (1) working day from receipt thereof.
The Head of a Service, Program or Committee who shall fail to submit a
Monthly Report shall be deemed liable for simple neglect of duty, as prescribed
under Section 46(D)(1) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in
the Civil Service, punishable as follows:
First Offense Suspension of one (1) month
and one (1) day to six (6)
months
Second Offense - Dismissal from the service
Provided, that in case the element of corruption is present, or that the duty
was neglected in exchange for valuable consideration, the person concerned shall be
liable for gross neglect of duty, which is a grave offense, as prescribed under Section
46(A)(2) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service,
punishable with dismissal from the service.
Provided further, that in case any officer in the agency concerned is
responsible for preparing the report, or is responsible for transmitting the same to
the Department, and he or she fails to do so, he or she shall likewise be liable for
simple neglect of duty or gross neglect of duty, as the case may be.
All previous issuance are hereby amended or repealed as far as these are
inconsistent herewith.
This Circular shall take effect immediately.
For the Department's information and guidance.
