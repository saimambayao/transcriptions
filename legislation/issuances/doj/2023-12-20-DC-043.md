I .O. Purpose
This Circular is being issued to facilitate compliance by all offices in the Department
of Justice (DOJ) with prescribed requirements for the grant of Performance-Based
Bonus (PBB) for FY 2023.
The guidelines contained herein shall cover: a) all offices in the DOJ Proper,
including the Office of the Secretary, Office of the UndersecretaHes and Office of
the Assistant Secretaries, b) all regional/provincial/city prosecution offices
nationwide, including the National Capital Region (NCR), and (c) all personnel of
agencies holding regular, contractual, and casual positions.

3.0. Definition of Terms
3.1 . Accountable/Responsible Office - refers to the office in-charge of the
achievement of a specific PBB target or implementation of and
compliance with a specific PBB requirement.
3.2. Delivery Unit - refers to the primary offices in the Department performing
substantive line functions, technical services, administrative support, as
reflected in the Department’s organizational structure and functional
chart
3.3. Digitalization - refers to the process of using technologies to enhance
the operations of the Department, and provide new revenue and value-
producing opportunities;
3.4. External Service -' refers to government services applied for or
requested by external citizens or clients or those who do not form part or
belong to the government agency or office providing service.
3.5. Head of Office - refers to Director IV or equivalent, or the highest person
in-charge of the achievement of a specific PBB target or implementation
and compliance with a specific PBB requirement.

3.6. Internal Service - refers to government services applied for or
requested by citizens or clients who are within the respective government
agency or office such as, but not limited to, its personnel or employees,
whether regular or contractual. Internal Service include services such as,
but not limited to, back-end/support services and regulatory functions
related to permitting, licensing, and issuance of privilege, right, reward,
clearance, authorization, or concession.
3.7. Interoperability system - refers to the ability of different operating and
software systems, applications and services to communicate and
exchange data in an accurate, effective, and consistent manner to
different platforms and agencies.
3.8. Government Services - refers to the process or transaction between
applicants or requesting parties and government offices or agencies,
involving applications for any privilege, right, reward, license, clearance,
permit or authorization, concession, or for any modification, renewal,
extension of the enumerated applications or requests, which are acted
upon in the ordinary course of business of the agency or office
concerned.
3.9. Government-to-Business (G2B) - refers to government services
whose client is a business entity.
3.10. Government-to-Citizen (G2C) – refers to government services whose
client is the transacting public.
3.11. Government-to-Government (G2G) - refers to government services
whose client is government employee or another government agency.
4.0. Eligibility of Criteria
The criteria for the grant of FY 2023 PBB are categorized into four (4) dimensions
of accountability, namely: Performance Results, Process Results, Financial
Results, and Citizen/Client Satisfaction Results as defined hereunder. The
accomplishment of offices for each of the criteria shall be rated using a scale of 1-
5 (5 as the highest). Each criterion has an assigned weight as shown in the table
below. The maximum score that may be obtained is 100 points.
To be eligible for the FY 2023 PBB, the DOJ the following specific criteria must be
met
4.1 The DOJ must attain a total score of at least 70 points; and
4.2. The DOJ must achieve at least a rating of 4 in at least three {3) of
these four (4) dimensions of accountability.

7.0. Eligibility of Delivery Units and Individuals
7.1. For FY 2023 PBB, the delivery units of eligible agencies shall no longer
be ranked . However, the unit/s most responsible for deficiencies shall be
isolated.
7.1.1. Based on Item 4.0, to be eligible for the FY 2023 PBB, the agency
must attain a total score of at least 70 points and achieve at least a
rating of 4 for at least three (3) in the four (4) accountability
dimensions.
To be able to attain at least 70 points, the agency should achieve a
performance rating of 4 in at least three (3) criteria. In case the agency
fails to meet a rating of 4 in at least three (3) criteria, the unit/s most
responsible (including its head) for the criteria stated in Section 5.0,
with a performance rating of below 4 will be isolated from the grant of
FY 2023 PBB.
7.1.2. The unit/s most responsible (including its head) for the non-
compliance with the Department Accountabilities provided in Section
6.0 will also be isolated from the grant of the FY 2023 PBB.
7.2. Eligible delivery units shall be granted FY 2023 PBB at uniform rates across
the agency, including its officials and employees. The corresponding rates of
