WHEREAS, the Commission on the Settlement of Land Problems (COSLAP)
is an administrative body under the Department of Justice (DOJ) vested with quasi-
judicial powers and functions to act on, decide, and resolve land disputes involving
small settlers and members of indigenous communities;
WHEREAS, the COSLAP is primarily created to extend assistance to small
settlers, tillers, indigenous cultural minorities, and the underprivileged in settling their
land problems and/or disputes in an expedi0ous and inexpensive investigation,
conciliation, and adjudication;
WHEREAS, pursuant to Executive Order No. 366, s. 2004, the Department of
Budget and Management (DBM) approved the DOJ Rationalization Plan (DOJ
RatPlan) on 28 June 2010, thereby deactivating the COSLAP;
WHEREAS, the records of cases previously under the jurisdiction of COSLAP
were turned-over and remain within the custody of the Records Management Section
(RMS) of the DOJ (RMS-DOJ);
WHEREAS, there is a need to update and inventory the records of COSLAP
cases within the custody of the RMS to ensure proper, comprehensive, and updated
filing and recording of COSLAP cases;
WHEREAS, the completion of a proper and updated case records inventory is
crucial to ensure accurate and appropriate responses to inquiries regarding COSLAP
cases;
NOW, THEREFORE, in the interest of the service and pursuant to existing laws,
rules, and regulations and to provide a clear and standardized procedure for the
inventory and custody of records related to cases previously under the jurisdiction of
the COSLAP, these Guidelines are hereby adopted:
Section 1. Purpose. These Guidelines cover the records management and
protocols for responding to inquiries from individuals, government agencies, and other
stakeholders regarding COSLAP cases.

Section 2. Scope. These Guidelines shall only apply to records management
and protocols for responding to inquiries regarding COSLAP cases, excluding case
resolution, disposition, and execution.
Section 3. Inventory of COSLAP Case Records. The RMS shall prepare a
master list of all cases previously under COSLAP. This list must include, but is not
limited to:
3.1 Case Number:
3.2 Parties involved;
3.3 Case status prior to deactivation of COSLAP; and
3.4 Condition/content of the case record/file folder upon receipt by the RMS
(complete/incomplete documents)
Section 4. Documentation and Digitization. All COSLAP records must be
accurately documented, cataloged, and, where feasible, digitized for secure electronic
storage. Physical records shall be maintained in a secure, organized, and accessible
storage to facilitate future reference and minimize the risk of misplacement or damage.
Section 5. Request for Copy ofCOSLAP Case Records. Requests for a copy
of COSLAP case records may only be made by the parties in the case, their legal
representatives or authorized agents, upon submission of appropriate authorization,
and other parties authorized by law to access such records.
Section 6. Submission of Request. The authorized requesting party must
submit a formal written request indicating, as much as possible, details that may
include the case number, title, and/or specifying the document(s) needed. Written
requests should be submitted to the Head of the Administrative Service, through the
RMS
Section 7. Assessment and Payment of Fees. Upon receipt of the request,
the RMS shall evaluate the request and assess/determine the applicable fees, if any.
The Accounting Division shall then issue an order of payment to the requesting party,
who shall pay the fees to the DOJ Cashier Section. The RMS shall only issue/release
the documents or certification being requested upon the presentation of the official
receipt by the requesting party.
Section 8. Issuance of Certified Reproduced Copy. Where no original copy
exists in the records, a Certified Reproduced Copy shall be issued. This is a machine-
or reproduced copy of a document held within the RMS, duly marked and certified as
accurate to the best extent possible.
Section 9. Issuance of Certification of No Available Records. If the request
pertains to a document that cannot be found within the records, the requesting party
will be provided with a Certification of No Available Records.
Section 10. Verification of Original Document. Only the head of the RMS or
his/her duly authorized representatives shall verify and sign the certification stamp
certifying that the issued document copy accurately reflects the content of the original
