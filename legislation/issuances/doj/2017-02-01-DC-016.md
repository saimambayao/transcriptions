PURPOSE
This Circular is being issued to provide the revised implementing
guidelines in the Department of Justice to effectively implement the Civil Service
Commission (CSC) and Department of Budget and Management (DBM) Joint
Circular No. 02, s. 2015 dated 25 November 2015.
RATIONALE
Rendition of overtime services shall be authorized only when extremely
necessary, such as when a particular work or activity cannot be completed
within the regular work hours and that non-completion of the same will a) cause
financial loss to the government or its instrumentalities; b) embarrass the
government due to its inability to meet its commitments; or c) negate the
purposes for which the work or activity was conceived.

4. APPROVING AUTHORITY
4.1(cid:9) Request for authority to render overtime service shall be made on a
monthly basis, filed not later than the fifth day of the month, and using the
enclosed form (Annex A). In order to facilitate request for funding from the
Department of Budget and Management, the monthly request for authority
should state the following:
names of personnel who are authorized to render overtime services;
the work or activity to be undertaken/accomplished to justify the
rendition of overtime services;
the specific days and periods of overtime services to be rendered;
budget estimate for overtime services covering the period being
requested.
4.2 Overtime during Sundays and holidays shall be resorted to only when

Undersecretary for Finance and Administration.
4.3 Handwritten entries in DTRs which cannot be supported by the data from
the DO] Gate Access Control System may be considered overtime service upon
approval by the Supervising Undersecretary for Finance and Administration.
4.4 Notwithstanding the approval to render overtime service for a particular
month, the Chiefs of Divisions may allow or disallow their respective staff to
render overtime services with pay taking into consideration the day to day
operation of their respective offices. Further, they shall monitor the daily
individual accomplishments of their respective staff who are rendering overtime
services.

beyond the normal eight (8) work hours, exclusive of the one-hour breaks. For
purposes of availing overtime pay under this Circular, such hours shall be from
8:00am to 5:00pm, or 7:00am to 4:00pm, or 9:00am to 6:00pm (for those
allowed on flexi-time), except Saturdays, Sundays and holidays. Hence, period

8. LIMITATIONS
8.1(cid:9) One-hour breaks shall be observed for breakfast, lunch, or supper and
rest, and every three (3) hours of continuous overtime service, or as may be
necessary.
8.2(cid:9) Rendering overtime service shall be resorted to only when extremely
necessary. No employee shall be allowed to render overnight service for more
than 2 consecutive nights, for health reasons and to ensure employee
productivity.
8.3(cid:9) Overtime services rendered should not be used to offset undertime.
8.4(cid:9) Handwritten entries in the Daily Time Record (DTR) will not be
counted/considered as overtime services rendered. In case of biometrics
failure, the print-out of the DOJ Gate Access Control System may support the
handwritten entries to prove the unreflected time-in or time-out.
8.5(cid:9) Drivers and confidential staff of officials who are required to keep the
same work hours as these officials which are beyond eight hours of work are
allowed to render overtime services, provided that their attendance are
captured by the biometrics system. In case an official is in out-of-office
meetings such that his/her driver or confidential staff will not be able to record
the attendance through the biometrics system, the claim for overtime pay is
subject to the following conditions:
submission of certification from the official concerned that overtime
services are actually rendered for hours indicated in the DTRs of the driver
and/or confidential staff;
confirmation from the Office of the Secretary that the out-of-office
meeting attended by the official is reported and/or included in the OSEC
Locator Chart a day prior to said meeting; and
approval of the Undersecretary for Finance and Administration.
8.6(cid:9) The total overtime pay of an employee in a year shall not exceed 50%
of his/her total basic salary for the year.
8.7(cid:9) No overtime pay or other allowances shall be paid nor any Compensatory
Time-Off (CTO) shall be granted to the same personnel receiving honoraria for
the period honoraria are granted pursuant to DBM Circular No. 2001-2 dated 1
October 2001.
9. FUNDING
Payment of overtime services rendered is chargeable against available
appropriation for overtime or savings under the Personnel Service (PS) Fund of
