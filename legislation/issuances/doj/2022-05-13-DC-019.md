ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER NO. 33
CONSTITUTING THE INTER-AGENCY TASK FORCE TO SUPPORT
THE HOSTING OF THE 33RD ASIA-PACIFIC REGIONAL SCOUT
JAMBOREE
WHEREAS, under Section 13, Article II of the Constitution, the State recognizes
the vital role of the youth in nation-building, and shall promote and protect their
physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being, inculcate in them
patriotism and nationalism, and encourage their involvement in public and civic affairs;
WHEREAS, Commonwealth Act No. 111, as amended by Presidential Decree
No. 460 and Republic Act No. 7278, mandates the Boy Scouts of the Philippines (BSP)
to: (i) promote, through organization and cooperation with other agencies, the ability
of boys to do things for themselves and others; (ii) train them in scoutcraft; and
(iii) teach them patriotism, courage, self-reliance, kindred virtues, and moral values,
using methods commonly used by boy scouts;
WHEREAS, the Asia-Pacific Regional Scout Jamboree (APRSJ), organized by
the Asia-Pacific Regional Scout Committee of the World Organization of the Scout
Movement, is a major scouting event in the Asia-Pacific region which aims to provide
opportunity for scouts aged 12 to 17 years old to experience fellowship of world
scouting in a fun and learning environment;
WHEREAS, the Philippines, through the BSP, is set to host the 33'd APRSJ on
14-21 December 2025 at Kainomayan Scout Camp, Botolan, Zambales, with the
theme, "Be Prepared: Scouts for Peace and Sustainable Development',” and
WHEREAS, the successful hosting of the 33’d APRSJ requires the involvement
and support of all concerned government agencies, including government-owned
or -controlled corporations (GOCCs), state universities and colleges (SUCs), local
government units (LGUs), and the private sector;
NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby order:

Section 1. Creation of an Inter-Agency Task Force. The Inter-Agency Task
Force (“Task Force”) to support the preparation, organization, and hosting of the 33rd
APRS J is hereby created. The Task Force shall be composed of the heads of the
following
:
Chairperson BSP
Members Department of Education
Department of Foreign Affairs
Department of Health
Department of Information and Communications Technology
Department of the Interior and Local Government
Department of Public Works and Highways
Department of Tourism
Department of Transportation
Bureau of Immigration /
Philippine National Police
Philippine Information Agency
The BSP shall serve as the Secretariat of the Task Force, and shall provide
administrative and technical support to the Task Force.
The Chairperson and members of the Task Force may designate their respective
alternates, with ranks not lower than Assistant Secretary or its equivalent, who shall
be fully authorized to decide for or on their behalf.
The Task Force may invite other concerned government agencies, as well as
non-government organizations (NGOs), civil society groups, the academe, and other
stakeholders as observers and/or resource persons to aid in the performance of the
functions of the Task Force, subject to existing laws, rules and regulations.
Section 2. Duties and Functions. The Task Force shall perform the following
duties and functions, subject to existing laws, rules and regulations:
a. Support the BSP in the formulation and implementation of programs, activities and
projects, as well as in the organization and management of the requirements for
the hosting of the 33’d APRSJ;
b. Ensure coordination and collaboration with other departments, bureaus, offices,
agencies and instrumentalities of the government, including GOCCs and SUCs,
regarding programs, activities and projects for the preparation, organization and
hosting of the 33rd APRSJ; and
c. Perform such other functions as may be directed by the President and/or the
Executive Secretary.
Section 3. Agency Support. All national government agencies and
instrumentalities, including GOCCs and SUCs, are hereby directed, and all LGUs,
NGOs, and the private sector are hereby encouraged, to render support and
assistance for the effective and efficient implementation of this Order.
Section 4. Funding. The amounts necessary for the implementation of this
Order shall be charged against available appropriations of concerned agencies,
subject to existing budgeting, accounting, and auditing laws, rules and regulations.

BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES
EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 87
UPDATING THE LIST OF DESIGNATED STATISTICAL ACTIVITIES
THAT WILL GENERATE CRITICAL DATA FOR DECISION-MAKING
OF THE GOVERNMENT AND PRIVATE SECTORS
WHEREAS, Executive Order (EO) No. 352 (s. 1996) established the System
of Designated Statistics as a framework used by the national government to identify,
prioritize and organize the collection, production and dissemination of key statistical
data through the conduct of designated statistical activities;
WHEREAS, designated statistical activities pertain to the specific tasks or
activities, such as censuses and surveys, to be conducted by government agencies
to generate critical data that are essential for economic planning, program
development, and policy-making by both the government and private sectors;
WHEREAS, Republic Act (RA) No. 10625 or the “Philippine Statistical Act of
2013” created the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) as the central statistical
authority of the national government mandated to develop and maintain appropriate
frameworks and standards for the collection, processing, analysis and dissemination
of data
WHEREAS, RA No. 10625 further established the PSA Board as the highest
policy-making body on matters related to government statistical operations, data
quality standards, and classification of collected data;
WHEREAS, consistent with its duties and functions under RA No. 10625, the
PSA Board, through Resolution No. 16 (s. 2024), resolved to approve and adopt the
updated list of designated statistical activities under EO No. 352;
WHEREAS, updating the list of designated statistical activities under EO No.
352 aims to ensure the availability of reliable data that reflect the current state of the
Philippine economy and socioeconomic growth, enabling data-driven policy-making
that better address the nation’s evolving needs and challenges; and
WHEREAS, Section 17, Article VII of the Constitution mandates the President
to ensure the faithful execution of laws;
THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES

NOW, THEREFORE, I, FERDINAND R. MARCOS, JR., President of the
Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws,
do hereby order:
Section 1. Adoption. The updated list of designated statistical activities
attached as Annex A hereof, is hereby adopted. The updated list of designated
statistical activities shall support the generation of critical and essential data for
administrators, planners and policy-makers in both the government and private
sectors
Any modification, addition, or deletion in Annex A hereof shall be approved by
the PSA Board.
Section 2. Reporting and Dissemination. To ensure timely dissemination of
statistical data to the public, all concerned government agencies are hereby directed
to submit the results of the conduct of the designated statistical activities and
administrative reports to the PSA within the timeframes designated and approved by
the PSA Board.
Section 3. Adherence to Official Statistical Standards. All designated
statistical activities shall conform to official statistical standards, including
classifications, frameworks, methodologies, concepts, and definitions, as approved by
the PSA Board. Any improvements or revisions to these standards shall be adopted
by implementing agencies only upon the approval of the PSA Board.
Section 4. Agency Support. All concerned government departments,
agencies, bureaus, offices and instrumentalities, including government-owned or
-controlled corporations, are hereby directed, and local government units and the
private sector are hereby encouraged, to undertake efforts in support of the
implementation of this Order.
Section 5. Funding. The amounts necessary to carry out the implementation
of this Order shall be charged against the current and available appropriations of the
PSA and the Implementing Agencies under Annex A hereof, subject to pertinent
budgeting, accounting, and auditing laws, rules and regulations. Thereafter, the
funding requirements necessary for the continued implementation of this Order shall
be included in the budget proposals of the PSA and the Implementing Agencies,
subject to the usual budget preparation process.
Section 6. Separability. If any part or provision of this Order is declared invalid
or unconstitutional, the provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and
effect
Section 7. Repeal. EO No. 352 (s. 1996) is hereby repealed. All other
issuances, orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof, that are inconsistent with
this Order, are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.
Section 8. Effectivity. This Order shall take effect immediately upon its
publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.

MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 84
APPROVING AND ADOPTING THE COCONUT FARMERS AND
INDUSTRY DEVELOPMENT PLAN 2024-2028, AND DIRECTING
THE IMPLEMENTATION THEREOF
WHEREAS , Section 2 of Republic Act (RA) No. 1 1524 or the “Coconut Farmers
and Industry Trust Fund Act” declares it as a policy of the State to consolidate and
expedite the delivery of benefits due to coconut farmers to increase their incomes,
alleviate poverty, and achieve social equality;
WHEREAS, Section 4 of RA No. 11524 mandates the Philippine Coconut
Authority (PCA), in consultation with relevant stakeholders to prepare and submit for
the President’s approval a Coconut Farmers and Industry Development Plan (CFIDP),
which shall set the direction and policies for the development and rehabilitation of the
Philippine coconut industry;
WHEREAS, Section 1 of Executive Order No. 172 (s. 2022) approved the
CFIDP 2022-2026 and directed the PCA, in consultation with relevant stakeholders,
to review the CFIDP annually, and update the same every five (5) years to ensure that
the strategies and policies therein are effective and responsive to evolving industry
needs, subject to the approval of the President;
WHEREAS, the PCA, in collaboration with coconut farmers, local communities,
and concerned government agencies, facilitated the development of the revised
CFIDP, which aims to streamline operations and strengthen agency collaborations, to
further expedite the delivery of benefits and services to coconut farmers;
WHEREAS, the PCA Board, through Resolution No. 029-2024, resolved to
endorse the CFIDP 2024-2028 for the President’s approval;
NOW THEREFORE, the following are hereby ordered:
The CFIDP 2024-2028, which is annexed to this Circular, is hereby
APPROVED and ADOPTED.
The PCA, as the lead agency responsible for the promotion and development
of the Philippine coconut industry, shall lead the implementation of the CFIDP
2024-2028.
In accordance with their respective mandates, all departments, agencies,
bureaus, and offices of the National Government, including state universities and
colleges, government-owned or -controlled corporations, and government financial
