SUBJECT :(cid:9) Standards for Gender-Sensitive and Child-Friendly Public
Assistance Desks and Interview/Investigation Rooms
1.0(cid:9) This is to provide general guidelines on the establishment and operation of
gender sensitive and child friendly public assistance desks and investigation
rooms in prosecution offices nationwide, in relation to the relevant laws,
policies, issuances and standards including the following:
1.1(cid:9) RA 9710, the Magna Carta of Women, and other laws relating to the
legal protection of women and children;
1.2(cid:9) RA 9485, the Anti-Red Tape Act and its implementing Rules and
Regulations under CSC Memorandum Circular No. 12, s. 2008;
1.3(cid:9) Revised Manual for Prosecutors (2008);
1.4 Performance Standards and Assessment Tool on Violence Against
Women for DO] Prosecution Services (2008);
1.5(cid:9) Manual on Law Enforcement and Prosecution of Trafficking in Persons
Cases (2008);
1.6(cid:9) Prosecutors' Manual on Handling Child-Related Cases (2007); and
1.7(cid:9) Protocol for Case Management of Child Victims of Abuse, Neglect and
Exploitation (2013).
Operational Definitions
2.0(cid:9) For this purpose, the following operational definitions are adopted:
2.1(cid:9) Public assistance desk — frontline service facility of an office manned by
at least one (1) employee authorized to receive and immediately
address any form of communication such as queries, complaints, and
feedback from the public;
2.2(cid:9) Interview/investigation room — enclosed area dedicated and/or used
for the conduct of interviews and investigations with parties and
witnesses (i.e. clarificatory hearings and inquest proceedings);

2.3(cid:9) Gender sensitive — fair, neutral and non-discriminatory in relation to
gender status or preference, and responsive to the special needs of
women including privacy and protection from abuse, neglect and
maltreatment; and
2.4(cid:9) Child friendly — sensitive and responsive to circumstances of children
including privacy, preferential attention, language/dialect understood
by the child, protection from abuse/neglect/maltreatment, and overall
consideration of the best interests of the child.
Guiding Principles
3.0(cid:9) The following principles shall be observed at all times in the implementation
of the herein standards:
3.1(cid:9) Accessibility — availability of appropriate facilities in the most suitable
location;
3.2(cid:9) Responsiveness — gender sensitive, child friendly and responsive to
special needs; and
3.3(cid:9) Confidentiality — privacy or protection of personal information from
misuse and undue disclosure.
Facility Standards
4.0(cid:9) Public assistance desks and interview/investigation rooms shall be situated at
the ground floor and/or near the main entrance of the building/office, to
facilitate access by the public including pregnant women, senior citizens and
persons with disabilities.
5.0(cid:9) While public assistance desks will be in accessible and open locations, they
shall have adequate distance from other facilities (e.g. pathways, waiting
areas and other office/service facilities) to ensure confidentiality of sensitive
information of clients especially if gender or child-related.
6.0(cid:9) An interview/investigation room shall, as much as possible, be dedicated for
conduct of interviews, clarificatory hearings, inquest proceedings as well as
case briefing with parties and witnesses. It shall be separate and properly
secured to ensure privacy and facilitate the proceedings.
7.0(cid:9) The interview/investigation room shall contain gender-neutral furnishings,
comfortable chairs and table, and documentation equipment (e.g. computer,
recorder). If possible, the room shall include a small play area for children to
facilitate proceedings for women/solo parents accompanied by young children
and child-victims/survivors/witnesses. It shall also be structured and/or
furnished appropriate for pregnant women and persons with disabilities, and
as much as possible, in close proximity to a comfort room.
8.0(cid:9) Prosecution offices may partner or coordinate with the hall of justice building
administrators, other national agencies, local government units, local social
welfare offices and/or non-government organizations for provision or use of
space/facility for public assistance desks and interview/investigation rooms.

Procedural Standards
9.0(cid:9) Confidentiality of personal/case information shall be strictly observed in the
conduct of public assistance, interviews, clarificatory hearings, inquest
proceedings and receiving/routing/safekeeping of documents relevant
thereto.
10.0 Gender-sensitive and child-friendly language and proceedings shall be used
during all the said proceedings, with sensitivity to the ordeal faced especially
by women/children-victims, and avoiding discriminatory and offensive words/
statements/gestures/methods as well as legal and technical jargon.
11.0 Assistance of and/or intervention from appropriate individuals and trained
professionals may be sought in instances of communication constraints
involving foreigners, locals with other dialects, persons with special needs, or
those exhibiting trauma, extreme emotions or unstable/violent behavior.
12.0 Interviews/investigations involving women and child victims/survivors/
witnesses shall, as necessary and available, be in the presence of trained and
duly authorized social workers.
13.0 Prosecution offices shall ensure referral mechanisms are in place for purposes
of assistance, investigation, case buildup/evidence gathering and prosecution
in court. These include police/law enforcement assistance, legal counseling
and representation by public attorneys, medical/neuro-psychological
examination and treatment, social welfare support/services, rehabilitation and
relevant interventions, application for Department's witness protection and
victims compensation programs.
Implementation
14.0 Implementation of said standards shall be in accordance with relevant laws,
policies, rules, regulations and standards including those in Item 1.0, and
shall be considered and/or integrated into management/service improvement
programs and initiatives of the Department.
15.0 The DOJ Committee on Women and Children and Regional Gender and
Development Committees shall facilitate implementation including the
identification of pilot/priority prosecution offices in the NCR and Regions 1 to
14, based on availability of facilities and appropriate personnel.
16.0 The said DOJ Committee shall provide further guidance and capacity building,
facilitate funding, and monitor/evaluate implementation.
