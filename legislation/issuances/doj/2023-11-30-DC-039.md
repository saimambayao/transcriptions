I. Rationale
Under Republic Act (RA) No. 10071, also known as the - Prosecution Service
Act of 201 C7’, the National Prosecution Service (NPS) is mandated to assist the
Secretary of Justice in the performance of his powers and functions in the Department,
relative to its role as the prosecution arm of the government, particularly the
investigation and prosecution of criminal offenses.
The NPS consists of the Regional Prosecution Offices (RPOs), including the
National Capital Region (NCR), and the Provincial Prosecution Offices (PPOs) and
City Prosecution Offices (CPOs) nationwide. With the nationwide operation of the NPS,
it was able to provide a very satisfactory performance in the Calendar Year (CY) 2022,
wherein they were able to accomplish a resolution rate of 92.95% for criminal
complaints filed before the Prosecution Offices, exceeding the target set in the General
Appropriations Act (GAA) CY 2022 of 91.50%.
CY 2022’s performance also marked the lowest backlog of cases at 23.20%
over the period between CYs 2017-2022. As to the outcome indicator of successful
prosecution, the NPS was able to secure a total of 160,427 mnvictions in CY 2022,
rooted on better case build-up. This is an improved performanoe vis-a-vis CY 2021
with 133,928 convictions.
1
Taking into account the critical functions of the NPS, it is vital to monitor its
performance to ensure efficient and effective delivery of services. Measures that will
gauge continuous improvement in its performanm should therefore be established.
Further, the results of the performance evaluations of the NPS will greatly contribute
in identifying gaps in the delivery of services. Hence, these guidelines.
