ANTI-COUNTERFEIT AND ANTI.PIRACY (ACAP)
POLICY OF THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
WHEREAS, Section 13, Article XIV of the 1987 Constitution provides that the State
shall protect and secure the exclusive rights of scientists, inventors, artists and other
gifted citizens to their intellectual property and creations;
WHEREAS, Section 2 of Republic Act No. 8293, otherwise known as the Intellectual
Property Code of the Philippines has declared that an effective intellectual and
industrial property system is vital to the development of domestic and creative activity,
facilitates the transfer of technology, attracts foreign investments, and ensures market
access for our products;
WHEREAS, the same section recognizes that the use of intellectual property bears a
social function and the State shall promote the dissemination of knowledge and
information for the promotion of national development and progress and the common
good
WHEREAS, the Department of Justice is the principal law agency of the government,
acting as both its legal counsel and its prosecution arm under Executive Order No.
292, otherwise known as the Administrative Code of 1987;
WHEREAS, the National Committee on Intellectual Property Rights (N('IPR) is an
inter-agency body created by Executive Order No. 736, s. 2008, to formulate and
implement plans and policies and to strengthen and ensure the promotion1 protection1
and enforcement of intellectual property rights in the country;
WHEREAS, all member-agencies of the NCIPR agreed to insdtutionalize and adopt
an Intellectual Property (IP)/Anti-Counterfeit and Anti-Piracy Policy (ACAP) to carry
out its mandate;
WHEREAS, this Department, as one of the member-agencies of the NCIPR and in
recognition of the need to secure the exclusive rights of scientists1 artists, and other
gifted citizens to their creations;
NOW THEREFORE, the following Anti-Counterfeit and Anti-Piracy policies and
guidelines are hereby adopted:
I. SCOPE
The ACAP Policy shall apply to all officials and personnel employed by this
Department, its offices, programs, staff, services, and attached agencies, including
those engaged through institutional or individual contract of service, job order, and
consultancy.

II. PURPOSE
This Policy aims to strengthen the protection and enforcement of intellectual
property rights in the country and to promote the policy of the State to protect and
secure the exclusive rights of scientists, inventors, artists, and other gifted citizens to
their intellectual property and creations.
III. OBJECTIVE
This Policy seeks to immediately ban the use, purchase, rental or lease,
transfer and promotion of counterfeit and pirated products, equipment, goods, or
services in the Department.
IV. DEFINITION OFTERMS
The following terms in this Memorandum Circular shall mean, as follows:
1. Department – refers to the Department of Justice.
2. Intellectual Property Rights – rights protected under the Intellectual Property Code
of the Philippines and intellectual property related laws.
3. Intellectual Property (IP) – refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions,
literary and artistic works, designs, and symbols, names and images used in
commerce1
.
4. Copyright (or author’s right) – is a legal term used to describe the rights of creators
over their literary and artistic works. Works covered by copyrights include books,
music, paintings, sculptures, films, computer programs, databases,
advertisements, maps and technical writings2
5. Pirated copyright goods – refers to any goods which are copies made without the
consent of the right holder or person duly authorized by the right holder in the
country of production and which are made directly or indirectly from an article
where the making of that copy would have constituted an infringement of a
copyright or a related right under the law of the country of importation3.
6. Trademark counterfeit goods – refers to any goods, including its packaging,
bearing without authorization a trademark which is identical to the trademark
validly registered in respect of such goods, or which cannot be distinguished in its
essential aspects from such a trademark, and which thereby infringes the rights of
the owner of the trademark in question under the law of the country of importation4.

V. POLICIES AND GUIDELINES
A. ANTI-COUNTERFEITING AND ANTI-PIRACY CONDUCT OF OFFICIALS
AND PERSONNEL OF THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
1. Officials and personnel shall exemplify respect and ensure the promotion
of intellectual property rights at all times.
2
Officials and personnel are prohibited from promoting, patronizing, selling,
or offering for sale counterfeit and pirated goods and services in the
Department premises.
3. Officials and personnel are prohibited to bring counterfeit and pirated
products, goods, or materials in their respective office premises, except if
its presence is in relation to an investigation relating to a complaint for
intellectual property rights filed before the Department or in any of its
prosecution offices.
4. Officials and personnel are directed to use only legitimate and/or licensed
software in all office-issued equipment or gadgets pursuant to Executive
Order No. 115, s. 1995, and the DOJ ICT Policy (Department Circular No.
041, s. 2013).
5. Officials and personnel are prohibited from utilizing office-issued computer
devices and office internet in accessing websites and/or online-platforms
offering for sale or otherwise, pirated materials or counterfeit goods,
except, if such access is pursuant to an investigation relating to a
complaint for violation of intellectual property rights filed before the
Department or in any of its prosecution offices.
6 The information technology offices or management information system
offices of the Department shall ensure that pirated materials, applications,
or programs utilizing office internet are not downloaded using office-issued
equipment and gadgets, except if the same is in relation to an investigation
to a complaint for violation of intellectual property rights filed before the
Department or any of its prosecution office. For this purpose, the pirated
materials include, but are not limited to:
(i) Pirated software downloaded in office-issued devices;
(ii) Pirated movies, games, and music files downloaded in office-issued
computer devices;
(iii) Pirated e-books, e-comics, e-manga in office-issued personal
computer devices;
(iv) Streaming of pirated content on movies, shows, music on different
platforms; and
(V) Pirated learning and other educational materials in office-issued
computer devices.

7. The information technology offices or management information system
offices shall take appropriate actions in accordance with the existing
DOJ ICT Policy, civil service rules, and applicable office policies in
instances where pirated materials are found to be saved or installed in
office-issued computer devices utilizing the Department’s information and
communication infrastructure.
8. All Heads of Offices and Attached Agencies of the Department shall exert
efforts to educate personnel on this policy to respect intellectual property
rights and prohibition on patronizing, promoting, or offering for sale
counterfeit and pirated goods.
9. The security guards on duty shall ensure that vendors or peddlers of
counterfeit and pirated products will not be allowed entry into the
Department premises.
B. POSTING OF NOTICES IN CONSPICUOUS PLACES AND IN WEBSITE
The Heads of Offices, Administrative Services. and the Information and
Communication Division shall ensure that notices prohibiting counterfeit and pirated
goods in the premises of the Department are posted in conspicuous places and in the
official websites.
C. DISCIPLINARY ACTION
Any violation of the ACAP Policy shall be dealt with in accordance with the
existing civil service laws, rules and regulations and applicable office policies and
guidelines.
D. EFFECTIVIW
This policy shall take effect immediately upon approval of the Secretary of
Justice and shall be valid and in force until revoked, amended or modified.
