DEPARTMENT CIRCULAR NO.0 08-C
TO THE PROSECUTOR GENERAL
ALL REGIONAL PROSECUTORS
ALL PROVINCIAL AND CITY PROSECUTORS
SUBJECT : CLOSURE OF OFFICES AND SUSPENSION OF WORK IN
THE NATIONAL PROSECUTION SERVICE FOR THE
PERIOD OF PUBLIC HEALTH EMERGENCY
DATE 22 March 2020
Pursuant to Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging
Infectious Diseases Resolution Nos. 11 and 12, in view of the physical closure of
all courts nationwide effective 23 March 2020 in accordance with Supreme Court
Administrative Circular No. 32-2020, and to safeguard the health and safety of all
concerned, all prosecution offices nationwide shall likewise be physically closed
effective 23 March 2020 and for the duration of the public health emergency.
Everyone is directed to stay in their respective residences.
Prosecutors and the necessary support staff shall go to their respective
offices only if there is a need to conduct inquest proceedings and for very
urgent matters that require immediate action as may be determined by the
heads of prosecution offices concerned.
For purposes of inquest proceedings and very urgent court duties, all
prosecution offices shall immediately coordinate with the trial courts, the local
Philippine National Police unit, and other law enforcement units. All prosecution
offices shall immediately make available to all relevant persons and offices,,
through traditional or digital means, the names of their designated contact/point
persons with all relevant contact details.
All prosecution offices are directed to ensure the availability at all times of
inquest prosecutors who shall be on-call. The conduct of all inquest proceedings
shall proceed in accordance with the Rules of Court and existing rules and
regulations of the Department of Justice, including guidelines on the designation
of inquest prosecutors on duty and the necessary support staff therefor, where
applicable. Any previous designation of the members of all skeletal workforces for
the duration of the public health emergency shall stand unless otherwise modified
by the heads of offices.

Preliminary investigations and all other non-crucial services as may be
determined by the heads of offices are suspended. Preliminary investigation
hearings, case conferences, meetings, and trainings during the period of the public
health emergency are deemed cancelled and shall be rescheduled after the lifting of
the public health emergency.
Unless absolutely necessary and upon the determination of the Office of the
Prosecutor General or the heads of the concerned prosecution offices, no travel or
leave applications shall be allowed/granted during the period of the public health
emergency.
The foregoing notwithstanding, all prosecutors are directed to attend to and reduce
their pre-existing work backlog during their downtime period.
These guidelines shall supersede all previous guidelines inconsistent
herewith, shall take effect immediately, and shall remain in force for the duration of
the public health emergency unless sooner revoked.
