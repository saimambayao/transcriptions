WHEREAS, the Department of Justice (DOJ) and the Office of the
Ombudsman (OMB) have concurrent jurisdiction over complaints for
crimes involving public officers and employees falling outside the exclusive
jurisdiction of the Sandiganbayan;
WHEREAS, Section 31 of the Ombudsman Act of 1989 authorizes
the Ombudsman to designate or deputize any prosecutor to act as a
special investigator or prosecutor to assist in the investigation and
prosecution of certain cases;
WHEREAS, in the Memorandum of Agreement entered between the
DOJ and the OMB on 29 March 2012, they agreed on the matters involving
jurisdiction of the offices, including the endorsement or referral of cases
for investigation and/or prosecution, and the coordination, reporting, and
monitoring of these cases;
WHEREFORE, in the interest of justice and pursuant to the
provisions of existing laws, the following guidelines for the handling of the
cases endorsed by the OMB are hereby adopted:
1. Resolution of referred/endorsed complaints. – All complaints
referred/endorsed by the OMB to any prosecution office of the DOJ
having jurisdiction over the complaint shall be resolved by the said
prosecution office in accordance with the existing laws and rules,
without the need for approval from the OMB.

The prosecution office shall provide the Office of the Ombudsman
with a copy of its resolution.
Further, if the prosecution office resolves to indict the respondent(s)
before the appropriate court, it shall have full control over the
prosecution of the said case.
2. Prosecution of referred/endorsed cases. – The prosecution of a
case, which was investigated by the OMB and was subsequently
endorsed to the DOJ for prosecution, shall be under the control and
supervision prosecution office which has jurisdiction over the case.
Provided, however, if the trial court orders a reinvestigation of the
case, the said prosecution office shall immediately refer the
reinvestigation of the case to the OMB for proper
disposition/resolution.
3. Reporting and Monitoring of Cases. – Within thirty (30) days from
receipt of the endorsed complaint or case, the prosecution office
must submit a report of said endorsement to the Regional
Prosecution Office that has jurisdiction over it. The report shall
contain the details of the complaint or case, including, among
others, the name(s) of the complainant(s), name(s) of the
respondent(s) or accused, nature of the complaint or case, trial
court hearing the case, and date of endorsement by the OMB.
Thereafter, the prosecution office shall submit a comprehensive
quarterly report of the status of the complaint(s) or case(s) to the
appropriate Regional Prosecution Office.
The Regional Prosecution Office shall monitor the status of the
complaints for investigation or cases for prosecution endorsed by
the OMB to the prosecution offices within its jurisdiction. It shall
maintain a recording of all the complaints and cases endorsed by
the OMB to its prosecution offices.
The Regional Prosecution Offices shall further provide an annual
inventory report of all the endorsed complaints and cases, including
the status thereof, to the Secretary of Justice and the Ombudsman.
