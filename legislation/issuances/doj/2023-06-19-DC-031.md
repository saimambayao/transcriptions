In the interest of service and pursuant to existing laws, the 2023 Rules on
Mediation in the National Prosecution Service (Rules on Mediation), copy attached
as Annex “A”, is hereby issued for the guidance and pilot implementation in the City
Prosecution Offices within the National Capital Region (NCR). The Rules on
Mediation shall be observed in all cases falling under its scope and referred for
mediation before said prosecution offices. The Office for Alternative Dispute
Resolution shall conduct the appropriate training for the select group of prosecutors
within every city prosecution office in the NCR.
This Circular takes effect simultaneously with the effectivity of the 2023 Rules
on Mediation.
For strict compliance.

Rule 1 - Title, Policy and Scope
Section 1. Title - These Rules shall be known as the "2023 Rules on Mediation
in the National Prosecution Service"
Section 2. Declaration of Policy - it is the policy of the State to:
a) Uphold the right of all persons to speedy disposition of their complaint
before all judicial, quasi-judicial, and administrative bodies;
b) Promote, develop and expand the use of Alternative Dispute Resolution
(ADR) such as mediation as a tool for speedy and impartial resolution of
disputes; and
c) Encourage the use and promotion of ADR to decongest the dockets of
the Prosecution Service and the Courts.
Section 3. Coverage - These Rules shall apply exclusively to the mediation of
the civil aspect of the following offenses:
1. Cyber-Libel as defined under Title 13, Chapter 1, Section 1 of the
Revised Penal Code in relation to RA 10175 or the Cybercrime
Prevention Act of 2012:
2. Libel as defined under Title 13, Chapter 1, Section 1 of the Revised
Penal Code; and
3. Violations of Batas Pambansa BIg. 22, or the Bouncing Checks Law
provided that the amount involved does not exceed Php 200l000.00;
4. All cases cognizable by the first level courts i.e. Metropolitan Trial
Court (MeTC), Municipal Circuit Trial Court (MCTC), Municipal Trial
Court (MTC) and Municipal Trial Court in Cities (MTCC) as defined
under Batas Pambansa BIg. 129, as amended by Republic Act No.
11576, EXCEPT those (a) already covered by the Katarungang
Pambarangay Law; (b) criminal cases subject to inquest proceedings
except in cases where the respondent signs a waiver of his rights
under Art. 125 of the Revised Penal Code; (c) victimless crimes; (d)
violation of city or municipal ordinances
These Rules shall not apply in instances where the abovementioned
offense are accompanied by offenses not covered by these Rules. In no
instance shall the Secretary of Justice be deprived of his authority to terminate
mediation and proceed with the prosecution of the offense to protect public,
interest and avoid any possible miscarriage of justice.

Rule II - Definition of Terms
Section 1. Definition of Terms. - As used in these Rules, the following shall
mean
a) Failure to Settle - a situation where no settlement is reached within the
mediation period;
b) Investigating Prosecutor - the public prosecutor assigned to conduct the
preliminary investigation of a complaint;
c) Mediation – a process whereby a designated mediator facilitates the
communication and negotiation between the parties and assists them in
reaching a settlement agreement in a complaint falling under Section 3,
Rule 1 of these Rules.
d) Mediation Coordinator – the prosecutor designated by the Provincial or
City Prosecutor to perform functions stated under Rule III, Section 3
hereof
e) Mediator - a trained public prosecutor identified by the National
Prosecution Service and recognized by the OADR as qualified to provide
mediation under these Rules;
f) Mediator's Report - a document submitted by the Mediator indicating
the outcome of the mediation proceedings;
g) Roster of Mediators - a list of OADR trained and recognized prosecutors
from the National Prosecution Service who shall facilitate the resolution
of the dispute;
h) Settlement Agreement - the final document signed by the parties and
attested to by the Mediator detailing the terms of settlement;

Section 2. Provincial and City Mediation Secretariat - The Local Mediation
Secretariat, established at the Provincial or City Prosecution Office, shall be
headed by the Provincial or City Prosecutor, who shall be assisted by the
Mediation Coordinator, and support staff. The Local Mediation Secretariat shall:
a) Oversee and direct the mediation office within the prosecution office;
b) Monitor the mediation operations from the identification of a complaint
to be referred for mediation to the termination of the mediation
proceedings;
c) Maintain a local roster of mediators of twenty-five percent (25%) or its
prosecutors but in all cases shall not be less than ten (10) prosecutors
and ensure the availability of said mediators;
d) Design and implement a local advocacy program for mediation;
e) Submit reports to the National Mediation Secretariat at the end of each
month;
f) Evaluate the performance of mediators included in the local roster;
g) Submit to the National Mediation Secretariat recommendations for the
improvement of the mediation program in the NPS; and
h) Perform such other functions as may be necessary in the fulfillment of
the foregoing
Section 3. Mediation Coordinator - The Provincial or City Prosecutor shall
designate a Mediation Coordinator who shall have the following duties and
responsibilities:
a) Help oversee and direct the mediation office within the prosecution
office
b) Immediately assign a case to a mediator within the period prescribed
in these Rules;
c) Ensure that the timelines set under these Rules are observed;
d) Receive and safekeep Mediator's Reports, Settlement Agreements
and other documents related to or arising from mediation
proceedings;
e) Provide the logistical requirements of the mediation office;
f) Assist in the evaluation of the performance of mediators;
g) Submit recommendations to the National Secretariat for the
improvement of the NPS Mediation Program;
h) Approve Settlement Awards as recommended by the Mediator; and
i) Perform such other duties and responsibilities as may be necessary
in the implementation of the NPS Mediation Program.

Section 4. Regional Mediation Coordinator – The Regional Prosecutor
acting as the Regional Mediation Coordinator, shall have the power to
designate and authorize the detail of additional Prosecutors as Mediators to
other prosecution offices within the region, in the interest of service and
pursuant to existing laws.
Rule IV - Mediation Process
Section 1. Referral to Mediation - Immediately upon receipt of a complaint,
the Prosecutor of the Day/Evaluator shall determine whether or not the same
is subject to these rules. Should it be covered, the Prosecutor of the
Day/Evaluator shall refer it within twenty-four (24) hours to the Mediation
Coordinator for assignment. The Prosecutor of the Day/Evaluator can
summarily dismiss the complaint in case of any apparent ground for dismissal.
Section 2. Issuance of Notice - The dates for the schedule of mediation shall
be issued by the Mediator and sent to the parties, which shall not be less than
two (2) settings.
Section 3. Assignment of Mediator – The Mediation Coordinator shall assign
within twenty-four (24) hours from receipt of referral from the Prosecutor of the
DaY/Evaluator, a mediator from the local roster of duly accredited mediators,
who shall assist the parties in settling the case.
Section 4. Mediation Period - The mediation shall be automatically terminated
within fortY-five (45) calendar days from date of assignment to the Mediator,
which may be extended with the conformity of both parties. The Mediation
Process shall be considered terminated upon the execution of a Settlement
Agreement between the parties, or when there is a failure to settle as defined
under these Rules. In case of failure to settle, the complaint shall be formally
docketed, and the conduct of Preliminary Investigation shall commence
.
Section 5' Mediation Outcome - When the mediation results in an agreement
to settle the civil aspect of the complaint, the Mediator shall assist the parties in
drafting and finalizing the terms and conditions of their Settlement Agreement
and Affidavit of Desistance, as well as prepare the Mediator's Repilrt. such
affidavit and other records shall be sworn to before the Mediator and forwarded
to the Investigating Prosecutor within forty-eight (48) hours.
The Investigating Prosecutor, within forty-eight (48) hours from the date
of signing of the Settlement Agreement, shall then recommend the dismissal of
the complaint and any counter-charge, with the approval of the Provincial or
CitY Prosecutor. The said dismissal shall be without prejudice to its refiling,
subject to the limitation on prescriptions.

Upon termination of the mediation period, the Mediation Coordinator
shall retain a copy of the Settlement Agreement and Affidavit of Desistance, as
well as the Mediator's Report. In case of failure to settle, the Mediation Process
shall be terminated and the formal conduct of preliminary investigation shall
commence. In no instance shall the mediator be included in the
raffle/assignment of cases previously mediated by him/her.
Section 6. Settlement Agreement - Every settlement agreement shall contain:
a) The terms and conditions agreed upon by the parties; and
b) A clause providing for the dismissal without prejudice of the complaint
Section 7. Enforcement of Settlement Agreement – in case of breach or
violation of the terms and conditions of the Settlement Agreement, the
Complainant shall seek the enforcement of the same pursuant to Rule 15 of
A.M. No. 07-1 1-08-SC or the Special Rules on Alternative Dispute Resolution
without prejudice to other available remedies. Should the parties refile the same
case with the concerned prosecution office, it shall no longer be subject to
mediation .
Rule V - The Parties
Section 1. Appearance of Parties - The parties shall personally appear during
the mediation proceedings.
Representatives of juridical persons, such as partnerships and
corporations, must be fully authorized to enter into mediation and sign
agreements as shown by the applicable partners' resolution, board resolution
or secretary's certificate .
To empower the parties in crafting their own solution to their dispute,
lawYers shall onIY assist their clients before and after the mediation process.

Rule VII – Confidentiality
Section 1. Confidentiality of Proceedings - To encourage the spontaneity
conducive to effective communication which enhances the possibility of
successful mediation efforts, the mediation proceedings and all its incidents
shall be kept strictly confidential. No transcript or formal record or any audio-
visual recording shall be made of the proceedings. The mediator, nevertheless,
may take down personal notes which shall not form part of the records of the
case and which shall, upon the termination of the proceedings, be immediately
disposed of.
Section 2. Confidentiality Issues to be Discussed - The mediator shall
discuss issues of confidentiality with the parties during the mediation
proceedings, particularly its scope and extent with reference to any private
caucus that the mediator may hold with a party.
Section 3. Privilege of Confidentiality - Information obtained through
mediation proceedings shall be subject to the following principles and
guidelines:
a) Information obtained through mediation shall be privileged and
confidential :
b) A party or a mediator may refuse to disclose and may prohibit any
other person from disclosing confidential information obtained during
the mediation ;
c) Confidential information shall not be subject to discovery and shall be
inadmissible in any proceeding, whether judicial or quasi-judicial.
However, evidence or information that is otherwise admissible or
subject to discovery does not become inadmissible or protected from
discovery solely by reason of its use in mediation;
d) in any proceeding, the following persons involved or previously
involved in mediation may not be compelled to disclose confidential
information obtained during the mediation:
i The parties to the dispute; and
ii. The Mediator;
e) A mediator cannot testify on confidential information gathered in
mediation .

Section 4. Exceptions to the Privilege of Confidentiality - There is no
privilege against disclosure under Section 3 of this Rule if the mediation
communication information is:
a) in an agreement evidenced by a record authenticated by all parties
to the agreement;
b) Available to the public;
c) A threat or statement of a plan to inflict bodily injury or to commit a
crime of violence;
d) Intentionally used to plan a crime, attempt to commit, or commit a
crime, or conceal an ongoing crime or criminal activity;
e) Sought or offered to prove or disprove a claim or complaint of
professional misconduct or malpractice filed against a mediator in a
proceeding; or
O Sought or offered to prove or disprove a claim or complaint of
professional misconduct or malpractice filed against a party, non-
party participant, or representative of a party based on conduct
occurring during mediation.
Section 5. Prohibition; Exceptions - A Mediator may not make a report,
assessment, evaluation, recommendation, finding, or other communication
regarding a mediation, except:
a) To state that the mediation occurred and that a settlement was
reached or it has failed; and
b) As permitted to be disclosed under Section 4 hereof.
Rule VIII - Awards and Funding
Section 1. Awards - The Mediation Secretariat in the national level shall
conduct an annual performance evaluation in each prosecution office. The
prosecution office that obtains the highest success in mediation shall receive
recognition from the Secretary of Justice.
Section 2. Compensation – The mediators and members of the Mediation
Secretariat shall received due compensation as may be allowed by law, subject
to the availability of funds, and to the usual accounting and auditing rules and
regulations.
Section 3. Funding - The DOJ shall include in its annual appropriation the
funding necessary to implement these rules.

Rule IX - Construction, Separability and Effectivity
Section 1. Construction - These Rules shall be liberally construed in order to
promote the objective of assisting disputants obtain a just, speedy, amicable
and durable settlement of disputes.
Section 2. Repealing Clause - All rules, circulars, and other issuances
inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed.
Section 3. Separability - if, for any reason, any section or provision of these
Rules is declared unconstitutional or invalid by the Supreme Court, the other
sections or provisions hereof not affected by such declaration shall remain in
full force and effect.
Section 4. Effectivity - These Rules shall take effect on 1 January 2024 and
after fifteen (15) calendar days following its publication in at least two (2)
national newspapers of general circulation.
