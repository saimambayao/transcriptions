Subject: WlPO Philippines Summer School
Dear Secretary Guevarra:
We are pleased to announce that the WIPO-Philippines Summer School on
Intellectual Property will be held from 18 to 29 June 2018 in Taguig City, Metro Manila.
The Summer School is a two-week program jointly organized by the World
Intellectual Property Organization (WlPO) and the Intellectual Property Office of the
Philippines (IPOPHL) in order to provide an opportunity for senior college students and
young professionals from various disciplines to acquire deeper knowledge of each domain
of intellectual property, how the international IP system functions, and its intersection with
other policy areas such as health and innovation.
The Program consists of lectures, case studies and group discussion on selected
topics, with orientation towards the interface between IP and other disciplines.
Only 50 slots are open so early applications are encouraged. Tuition waivers will
be given for up to 10 applicants with excellent credentials. Certificates of participation will
be awarded to those who successfully complete all requirements of the program.
Detailed information on the course including registration procedure, requirements,
and fees can be found at:

Eligibility
The Summer School Is open to senior students (graduate and postgraduate
students) and young professionals from any field of study or discipline. The program
is designed for candidates who wish to obtain deeper knowledge of IP. Only fifty (50)
slots are open so early applications are encouraged.
All applicants are required to have successfully completed the WlPO distance
learning General Course on Intellectual Property before the registration deadline.
Please note that priority will be given to those who have completed the said course
in the selection of participants.
Participants are responsible for their own travel to and from Manila, accommodation
and living expenses during their participation of the program.
Insurance and Medical Examination
Non-resident participants will be provided with insurance coverage for the duration of
their stay in connection with the Summer School program.
Each participant will be required to present a medical certificate upon arrival.
Online Registration
Applications must be submitted online with resumes, letters of motivation, and copies
of passports (or identity cards).
Selected applicants will be notified two weeks after registration deadline.
Registration Fees for Summer Schools on IP
Registration fees are payable upon notification of acceptance. They fall into the
following categories:

This course builds on the concepts presented in DL-001 - intellectual Property
Primer. It provides a more in-depth view of the fundamentals of IP law, and is
considered as indispensable to pursue more advanced courses of study on specific
areas of IP law.

Self-assessment tools are strategically placed throughout each module to assist
participants with gauging their respective levels of knowledge and progress, as well
as their ability to apply the concepts and facts presented within the course.
Academic support is provided throughout each module by experienced tutors who
are expert practitioners in the field of IP.
Final Exam and Certificate
The final exam for this course is comprised of a series of multiple choice questions.
A fixed amount of time is allocated for participants to complete and submit the exam
on-line. Participants are contacted regarding modaiities for accessing the final exam
approximately one week prior to the deadline for completion of the DL-101 course.
Participants who pass the DL-101 final exam are awarded an electronic certificate of
course completion.
Eligibility
This course is open to individuals who are/will be employed in the field of
administration of intellectual property rights; those seeking general knowledge of IP;
and those who generate various forms of IP and seek a deeper understanding of the
effective management and protection of IPRs.
