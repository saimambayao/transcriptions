Scope
2.0 This Circular covers all Services/Offices and Program units in the DOJ Proper
(main office) including the DOJ Prosecution Staff, excluding Regional,
Provincial and City Prosecution offices of the National Prosecution Service
(NPS) which is covered by a separate issuance.

and updated based on indicators and targets in FY 2020 GAA. Office
performance shall be measured and rated based thereon.
6.0 Officials and offices concerned may submit to the PMT their OPCR forms with
proposed revised indicators and targets to align with their CY 2020 performance
commitments and/or reprogrammed activities, without prejudice to applicable
indicators/targets in the FY 2020 GAA and subject to review by the PMS and
PMT.

reports, which will also contribute to the reduction of possible transmission of
COVlD-19 amidst the current pandemic situation, an electronic submission
facility shall be developed and implemented by the DOJ Evaluation and

8.0 Offices shall submit the following accomplishment reports on or before the
prescribed deadlines and must be fully complied with by 15 January 2021:
8.1 CY 2020 Quarterly Accomplishment Report (Annex "B"), as
prescribed in Department Circular No. 017, s. 2014, every 15*^ day of the

8.2 CY 2020 Yearend Accomplishment Report on Priority

9.0 Reports submitted with deficient and/or incorrect information shall only be
processed upon completion or correction of entries.
10.0 Based on the submitted accomplishment reports on the prescribed performance
measures cited in item 5.0 above, the CSC-prescribed 5-point rating scale shall
be used in determining office performance ratings.
11.0 In case of equal adjectival ratings and calculated weighted scores among two
or more delivery units, forced ranking shall be based on assessment of
performance aspects such as service/output quality and responsiveness not
quantified and included in the prescribed indicators, as determined by the PMT.
Yearend Report
12.0 Apart from reports prescribed in Item No. 8.0, all offices (except offices of the
Secretary, Undersecretaries and Assistant Secretaries) shall submit a Yearend
Report for CY 2020 to the Evaluation and Statistics Division on or before 31
Januarv 2021. This report shall be in narrative form to be considered in the DOJ
Annual Report pursuant to Section 43, Book t of Executive Order No. 292, and for
public information and other oversight compliance. The report shall briefly contain
the following information (template with sample content attached as Annex "D"):
12.1 Office functions, mandates, strategic framework and program priorities;
12.2 Summation of and/or explanations on CY 2020 accomplishments, as well
as challenges and constraints; and
12.3 Strategic direction and key action plans for CY 2021 including priority
programs/project/activities under the DOJ Development Plan and other
government plans and policies.
Requirements for Individuals/Employees
13.0 Third level officials shall be rated and ranked using the Career Executive
Service Performance Evaluation System (CESPES), while first and second level
employees shall be rated through the SPMS under CSC M.C. No. 6, s. 2012,
and D.C. No. 017, s. 2014.

14.0 The IPCR forms with approved accomplishments and ratings for CY 2020 by
heads of offices, as well as the Summary List of Employee Ratings( Annex "E"),
shall be submitted to the Personnel Division on or before 31 January 2021.
15.0 Pursuant to CSC MC No. 6, s. 2012, the average rating of all individual
performance assessments shall not be higher than the adjectival performance
rating of the office based on evaluation and rating by the PMT. If otherwise, the
head of office concerned shall be required to adjust the individual employee
ratings to conform with the said policy.
Performance-Based Incentives
16.0 To qualify for PBB, an officer or employee should receive at least "satisfactory"
rating under the CESPES or SPMS, as the case may be. The PBB of individual
employees shall be based on performance ranking of the individual's delivery
unit, and subject to the eligibility requirements and corresponding PBB
amount/rates to be prescribed by the AO 25 Inter-Agency Task Force for the
current year.
17.0 Step increments due to meritorious performance pursuant to CSC-DBM Joint
Circulars No. 1, s. 2012, may be granted to qualified officials/employees within
the 5% of the total number of personnel in every office, as follows:
17.1 Two( 2) steps for two (2) ratings of "outstanding" during the two (2) rating
periods within a calendar year; and
17.2 One (1) step for one (1) rating of "outstanding" and one (1) rating of "very
satisfactory", or two (2) ratings of "very satisfactory" during the two (2)
rating periods within a calendar year.
18.0 Based on the said percentage criteria, the Personnel Division shall
proportionately determine the maximum number of personnel in each office to
be qualified for the said step increment, based on the plantilla and actual
deployment of personnel. Heads of the said offices shall then determine the
subordinate personnel who will be granted such benefit, subject to the cited
required performance rating.
19.0 Eligibility for the FY 2020 PBB and step increment for meritorious performance
requires compliance to the SPMS. Non-compliance to the following
requirements shall warrant disqualification of offices/employees concerned:
19.1 Reports required under Items 8.0 and 12.0;
19.2 Duly accomplished/submitted CESPES rating of third-level officials; and
19.3 Duly accomplished/approved IPCR forms with success indicators,
accomplishments and ratings for individual employees.
20.0 Individual performance evaluation/rating and/or grant of the subject incentives
may be withheld pending compliance with the AO 25 Task Force requirements,
and others that may be further prescribed thereof, other oversight agencies and
the Department.

Implementation Procedures
21.0 The PMT shall oversee SPMS and PBB implementation in accordance with the
herein guidelines and provide further guidance or management/policy
recommendations on the matter.
22.0 The ESD shall organize an orientation session with the offices/delivery units
with regard to the implementation of an electronic reporting facility, as stated in
item 7.0. The ESD shall also provide the PMT the results of the evaluation of
office performance based on quarterly accomplishment reports vis-a-vis the
herein adopted indicators, targets and rating scale, as well as compliance to all
other herein requirements.
23.0 The Personnel Division shall enforce the requirements and rating/ranking
criteria at the individual/employee level and step increment for meritorious
performance, including compliance to CESPES and SPMS, policy stated in item
15.0, and other pertinent Civil Service and compensation rules and regulations.
24.0 The PMT shall duly inform offices concerned on compliance deficiencies and
calculated office performance and rating based on the reports received and
processed.
25.0 The PMT may, on its own initiative and upon higher management directive,
review/validate office performance ratings especially those that will have
significant implications/impact on personnel actions such as promotion,
incentives and sanctions.
26.0 Concerns/complaints in relation to this guidelines and implementation thereof
shall be formally submitted/referred to the PMT Chairperson or raised during
performance assessment and planning session.
27.0 The Planning and Management Service shall organize assessment and
planning session/s to facilitate the review of accomplishments based on the
reports submitted, and update/calibrate indicators and targets for the
succeeding year onwards.
28.0 Heads of offices shall ensure that this is disseminated and explained to
constituent employees immediately upon receipt.
29.0 This Circular takes effect immediately and supersedes provisions of previous
policy issuances/directives inconsistent herewith.
For guidance and compliance of all concerned.

CY 2019 YEAREND REPORT
Office Functions, Mandates, Strategic Framew/ork and Program Priorities
The Planning and Statistics Division (PSD) under the Planning and Management Service
is mandated to provide planning, programming, project development and evaluation,
and research and statistical services to help ensure the efficient and effective operations
of the Department pursuant to Department Circular No. 014, s. 2011.
In 2019, as part of its mandate to ensure the alignment of the Department's strategic
agenda with the development framework of the national government, the PSD updated
its plans and programs as committed under the Philippine Development Plan, Security,
Justice and Peace Cabinet Cluster, Justice Sector Convergence Program, Gender and
Development Agenda, Philippine Anti-Illegal Drugs Strategy and other national strategic
framework/agenda of the government.
Summary of Accomplishments
The Division organized and facilitated three (3) major planning and programming
activities in 2019. These are the DOJ Assessment and Planning Session in January, NPS
Management Conference in February, and Operations, Expenditure and Procurement
Planning in August. PSD complied with the pertinent agency budget requirements, such
as performance targets, program and project profiles during the budget preparations as
well as other budget legislation requirements and budget execution documents.
In line with its role in project development, the PSD facilitated the updating of the
Department's Public Investment Program, engagements with development partners (i.e.
European Union and UN Office of Drugs and Crime) and handled and/or contributed in
the planning, development and financing of the following projects:
• P hilippine Crime Index
• National Justice Information System
• P ublic Financial Management System improvement; and
• C ompetency-based human resource management framework.
The PSD formulated policies and issuances, and provided guidance on matters related
to the implementation of Strategic Performance Management System and Performance-
Based Incentive System for CY2019. Office performance commitments and
accomplishments of all delivery units were evaluated and report validations were
conducted in prosecution offices in NPS Regions IV and XII, and Financial Service in
DOJ Proper. The Division also drafted the CY 2018 DOJ Annual Report and prepared
performance accountability reports submitted to the Department of Budget and
Management and Commission on Audit.
In terms of its research and statistical services function, the PSD was able to generate
various statistical reports/data based on the more than 1,700 statistical reports
processed as received from prosecution offices nationwide within the year. These
reports include quarterly report of operations, yearend report of resources, court
