2.0 Policy Statement
2.1 Accreditation by the OADR is a quality assurance mechanism wherein
acnredited ADR providers in the private and public sectors are
evaluated and certified by the agency to have conformed with the
minimum standards on qualification, competence and performance for
the delivery of ADR services.
2.2 While there is no prejudice to the rights of parties to choose non-
accredited institutions and individuals, accreditation intends to
reasonably ensure that ADR services from external providers for or with
government agencies are delivered by institutions and professionals
with integrity and competency on ADR systems as neutrals, training
providers, consultants and program managers, among others.
2.3 The accreditation system serves as accountability mechanism for
sustained conformance of ADR providers with the established
institutional and professional service standards including competence,
quality, ethical practice, impartiality, transparency, protection of
confidential information, and training programs.
2.4 Training standards are anchored on professional competencies
necessary for general ADR services. The herein guidelines provide for
the minimum competency requirements and basic accreditation levels.
The OADR shall develop further competency and training standards in
relation to specialized ADR services.

5.0 Statement of Principles for Accredited APOs
An ADR Provider Organization (APO) accredited by the OADR to render ADR
services to the public shall commit to adhere to the following principles:
ETgEL
5. 1 Quality and Competence – An APO shall reasonably ensure that the
neutrals it accredits to provide ADR services are qualified and
competent to render such services through a system of training,
accreditation, performance monitoring and evaluation, and continuing
education. The APO shall take measures to comply with or exceed the
training standards set by the C)ADR, and will continuously strive to
improve its training programs or curricula with the aim of providing the
i
highest standards of training for its neutrals. The APO shall likewise

5.2 Confidentiality of ADR Process – An APO shall preserve and protect
the integrity and confidentiality of the ADR process. In this regard, it
shall take measures to ensure that no information or communication
obtained in the course of an ADR proceeding shall be disclosed without
the express written consent of the parties, except in those cases
allowed or required by law. Such measures shall include the protection
of personal information in accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012,
its Implementing Rules and Regulations, issuances of the National
Privacy Commission, and other applicable laws, rules and regulations.
5.3 Neutrality, Fairness, Independence and Impartiality – An APO shall
reasonably ensure that the rules of procedure governing ADR
proceedings conducted under its auspices are fundamentally fair and
impartial, and that the neutrals performing ADR services are made
aware of their obligation to remain neutral, fair, independent and
impartial at all times.

These Standards shall apply to the training of ADR practitioners to enable
them to serve as neutrals in ADR proceedings, such as arbitration, mediation,
conciliation, negotiation, early neutral evaluation, and mini-trial, and provide

training of ADR practitioners to ensure that accredited and certified ADR
neutrals and such other service providers possess the basic knowledge and
skills to serve as such.
