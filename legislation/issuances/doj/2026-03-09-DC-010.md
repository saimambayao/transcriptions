In the interest of service and pursuant to Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 114
dated 06 March 2026 issued by the Office of the President, mandating the strict
adoption of energy conservation protocols across all government agencies and
instrumentalities, the Department hereby adopts and implements the following
guidelines to institutionalize energy conservation protocols and optimize the use of the
Department’s resources:
1. ENERGY CONSERVATION MEASURES
1. All offices shall maintain a standard thermostat setting of 24'C for all air-
conditioned spaces. Furthermore, sleep settings shall be activated on all office
equipment, and all non-essential lights and electronic equipment must be
strictly turned off during lunch breaks and after office hours.
2. Virtual meetings shall be the default mode for all inter-agency coordination,
consultations, conferences, and similar engagements. All meetings, seminars,
and workshops must be conducted through secure digital platforms, except in
cases where physical presence is legally required or operationally
indispensable.
3 All official travel shall be restricted to essential and indispensable functions that
cannot be fulfilled through virtual meetings or electronic communication. Travel
undertaken without a clear justification of urgency, operational necessity, or
direct programmatic relevance shall likewise be considered unnecessary.
Accordingly, the provisions of Executive Order No. 77, s. 2019, regarding the
grant of travel authorities and entitlements, shall be strictly observed.
Nonetheless, the provisions of Department Circular No. 36 dated 02 November
2022 entitled: Omnibus Guidelines on Foreign and Local Travel of Officials and
Employees of the Department and its attached agencies shall still be
applicable and construed in conformity with EO No. 77 and prevailing
issuances on the matter.

4. All concerned units shall implement strict fuel efficiency measures, including
the consolidation of trips and the optimization of travel routes. Strict
minimization of idling shall be observed, and engines shall be switched off
during waiting periods except when operationally necessary. The "full-tank
method" for fuel monitoring and a rigid preventive maintenance schedule for
all service vehicles shall be strictly followed.
5. All offices shall adopt and implement such other appropriate energy
conservation measures to ensure the efficient, judicious, and responsible use
of energy resources in all government operations, consistent with applicable
laws, regulations, and national policies on energy efficiency and conservation.
11. ADOPTION OF A FOUR (4)-DAY ONSITE WORK ARRANGEMENT
6. Pursuant to the mandate under Section 3 of MC No. 114. s. 2026, all offices
shall transition to a four (4)-day onsite work arrangement.
7. This arrangement may be implemented through a Compressed Workweek,
where employees complete the mandatory 40-hour workweek in four days, or
through a designated common day for Work-From-Home (WFH)
arrangements, in accordance with Civil Service Commission (CSC),
Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 06, s. 20221, as amended by CSC MC No.
01, s. 20252
For the Department, a Combination of Flexible Work Arrangement3 may be
adapted . Hence, government officials and employees may render work from 8
o’clock AM until 5 o’clock in the afternoon, including lunchbreaks, from
Mondays to Thursdays, while a Work From Home (WFH) scheme may be
implemented for 8 hours on Fridays, in order to comply with the forty (40)-hour
workweek requirement.
8. In compliance with the second paragraph of the preceding number, each
office/unit is hereby directed to formulate its own internal guidelines governing
the specific work arrangements of its respective personnel. These office-
specific guidelines must be consistent with the office’ mandate as well as the
operational needs of the Department, and comply with the provisions of MC
No. 114, s. 2026, as well as CSC MC No. 06, s. 2022, as amended by CSC
MC No. 01, s. 2025.
9. All office-specific guidelines shall be submitted to the Office of the Secretary,
thru the Assistant Secretary for Administrative Service, for review and
approval. Such submission must be made within three (3) days from the
issuance of this Department Circular.

III. NON-INTERRUPTION OF GOVERNMENT SERVICES
10. The adoption of these energy conservation and work protocols shall not
impede the delivery of services. All offices, particularly those involved in public
safety and emergency response, shall ensure that their mandated functions
remain fully operational and that frontline services are available to the public
without interruption.
11. Prosecutors and their support staff shall continue to conduct inquest
proceedings in person and/or online, and attend to urgent matters that require
immediate action, as may be determined by the heads of the concerned
prosecution offices. The conduct of inquest proceedings shall proceed in
accordance with the Rules of Court and existing rules and regulations of the
Department. All prosecution offices are directed to ensure the availability at all
times of inquest prosecutors who shall be on-call.
12. Notwithstanding the work arrangements implemented by each office, all
officials and employees shall likewise be on-call as may be determined by the
Secretary of Justice.
IV. MONITORING
The Administrative Service shall monitor the strict compliance and
implementation of these guidelines, and may recommend further measures to improve
the same, if the need arises.
V. EFFECTIVITY
This Memorandum Circular shall take effect immediately and shall remain in
force until further orders.
For compliance.
