In the interest of the service and pursuant to Department of Budget and
Management (DBM) Budget Circular No. 2024-6, s. 2024 entitled, Rules and
Regulations on the Grant of Medical Allowance to Civilian Government Personnel, and
other relevant laws, rules, and regulations, the following guidelines on the grant of
medical allowance to eligible Department of Justice (DOJ) officials and employees are
hereby adopted:
1,
COVERAGE
This Circular shall cover all officials and employees in the DOJ, regardless of
appointment status, whether regular, casual, or contractual, and on full-time or
part-time basis.
EXCLUSION
11.
This Circular shall exclude consultants, workers employed through contract of
service/job order arrangement, and other individuals hired without employer-
employee relationship.
111 DEFINITION OF TERMS
a. Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) provider - refers to a juridical
entity legally organized to provide or arrange for the provision of pre-agreed
or designated health care services to its enrolled members for a fixed pre-
paid fee for a specific period of time.
b. HMO-type product - refers to an agreement issued on individual/family or
group basis approved pursuant to Insurance Commission (IC) Circular
Letter No. 2017-19 and issued by duly licensed HMO companies by the IC.

IV. RATES OF THE MEDICAL ALLOWANCE
For Fiscal Year 2025, the Medical Allowance for full-time service shall not
exceed Php7,000 per annum.
For each subsequent year, the Medical Allowance shall not exceed the amount
authorized under the pertinent general provisions in the annual General
Appropriations Act (GAA).
V. FORMS AND OTHER DETAILS OF THE MEDICAL ALLOWANCE
a. The Medical Allowance may be granted in the following forms:
1. HMO-type product coverage – could be availed of by either (i) the DOJ;
or (ii) recognized employees' organizations in the DOJ.
Such arrangement is without prejudice to the preference of employees
to opt out from such group purchase and individually avail of another
HMO product.
2. Cash – for those who will: (i) avail their own; or (ii) pay/renew their
existing HMO-type benefit, subject to the submission of reportorial
requirements provided in Item VIII of this Circular.
b. In case an official or employee opts to continue his/her existing HMO
product, the Medical Allowance shall be spent thereto provided that it will
cover the fiscal year, in whole or in part, when the allowance was granted.
c. In cases where the HMO-type product availed is below the rate of Php7,000
Medical Allowance, the official or employee shall not be obligated to refund
the excess amount to the DOJ.
VI. CONDITIONS AND GUIDELINES ON THE GRANT OF MEDICAL
ALLOWANCE
The grant of the Medical Allowance shall be subject to the following conditions
and guidelines:
a. The official or employee is already employed in the DOJ and is to render
services for at least a total or an aggregate of six (6) months of service in a
particular fiscal year, including leaves of absence with pay, as well as those
services rendered under any of the alternative work arrangements
prescribed by the Civil Service Commission.
b. A newly-hired employee may qualify for the grant of the Medical Allowance
after rendering six (6) months of service.
c. An official or employee, who transferred from another government agency
to the DOJ and was not granted Medical Allowance by the former agency,

shall be granted Medical Allowance after the submission of a certification
from the former agency that said official or employee was not granted
Medical Allowance for the current fiscal year.
d. The Medical Allowance of a DOJ official or employee on detail to another
government agency shall be granted by the DOJ, while those on
secondment shall be paid by the recipient agency.
e. A compulsory retiree, whose services have been extended, may be granted
the Medical Allowance, subject to the pertinent conditions and guidelines
under this Circular
f. Those who are formally charged with administrative and/or criminal cases,
which are still pending for resolution, shall be entitled to Medical Allowance
until found guilty by final and executory judgment, provided:
1. Those found guilty shall not be entitled to Medical Allowance in the year
of finality of the decision. The personnel shall refund the Medical
Allowance received for that year.
2. If the penalty imposed is only a reprimand, the personnel concerned
shall be entitled to Medical Allowance.
g. For employees on Study Leave or Study/Training/Scholarship Grant
1 An employee on study leave or on study/training/scholarship grant,
whether locally or abroad, shall be entitled to the grant of Medical
Allowance for the year if he/she renders at least six (6) months of
service in the same year, including leaves of absence with pay, prior to
and/or after the study leave or study/training/scholarship grant.
2. If an employee is on study/training/scholarship grant for the entire year,
whether locally or abroad, he/she is not entitled to the Medical
Allowance.
h Pursuant to Revenue Memorandum Circular No. 107-2024 of the
Department of Finance-Bureau of Internal Revenue, the authorized Medical
Allowance granted under Executive Order No. 64, s. 2024 falls under the
de minimis benefit contemplated in Section 2.78.1(A)(3) of Revenue
Regulations (RR) No. 2-98, as amended.
Such being the case, the Medical Allowance and/or the actual premium paid
to HMO providers in compliance with EO No. 64, s. 2024 is exempt from
income tax and, consequently, to withholding tax pursuant to Section
2.78.1 (A)(3) of RR No. 2-98, as amended.
VII. FUND SOURCES
The grant of the Medical Allowance shall be charged against the available PS
allotments of the DOJ.

VIII. REPORTORIAL REQUIREMENTS
a. All officials and employees of the DOJ, who have an existing HMO provider
prior to the grant of Medical Allowance by the DOJ and availed of the cash
option mentioned in Item V(a)(2) hereof, shall submit a proof of enrollment
with an HMO provider, such as but not limited to:
1 Certified copy of the HMO agreement and/or identification card issued
by the HMO provider;
2. Certificate of membership issued by a duly registered/certified HMO
provider; and/or
3 Official receipt for the payment of membership fee for the HMO product
acquired .
b. The DOJ Financial Service shall submit to the DBM Budget and
Management Bureau not later than one (1) month after every fiscal year,
an annual report on the grant of the Medical Allowance using the template
in Annex "A” hereof.
IX. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE
If any section or part of this Circular is declared contrary to law or
unconstitutional by a competent authority, the remaining sections or parts
hereof shall remain in full force and effect.
X. EFFECTIVITY
This Circular takes effect immediately and shall remain in full force until further
orders.
