VI. The DOJ Strategic Performance Management System (SPMS)
The DOJ envisions providing the highest quality of human resource to make
the Department an institution worthy of national pride. This is concretized by
the acquisition of and retention of human capital that is supportive of a dynamic
work environment.
The human resource, considered as the most significant asset in the
organization, should be kept effective and efficient to be able to contribute to
the organizational growth. The creation of a work environment in which
employees are made aware of their role vis-a-vis the DOJ’s expectations are
crucial in the establishment of a system that would objectively gauge
performance against required standards. Performance evaluation, as part of
the performance management system, is an indispensable tool to measure the
contribution of department persQnnel to the attainment of the organizatiQn
goals
The SPMIS of the DOJ focuses on the linking of the individual performance vis-
a-vis the agency’s organizational vision, mission and strategic goals. It
comprises the strategies, methods and tools to ensure fulfillment of functions
of the offices and its personnel, as well as to assess their accomplishments. It
provides a periodic opportunity for the supervisor (rater) and the employee
(ratee) to discuss expectations and evaluate them, as well as provide
interventions when expectations are not met.
The system follows the performance management cycle which has four phases:
(a) Performance Planning and Commitment; (b) Performance Monitoring and
Coaching; (c) Performance Review and Evaluation; and (d) Performance
Rewarding and Development Planning.
The DOJ abides with the existing general policies of no discrimination based
on age, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, civil status, disabilities, religion,
ethnicity, political affiliation and among others in the implementation of its
performance management system.
