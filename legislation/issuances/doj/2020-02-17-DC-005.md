In the interest of the service and pursuant to the Department of Budget and
Management's( DBM) approved organization and staffing modifications of the Planning and
Management Service (RMS), this Department, the PMS Is restructured to be composed of
the following Divisions:
1. Planning Division;
2. Evaluation and Statistics Division; and
3. Management Division;
The Management Information Systems Division (MISD) is elevated to an Information
and Communications Technology Service (ICTS) to be composed of the following Divisions:
1. ICT Application Systems Division; and
2. ICT Infrastructure Division.
The two (2) ICT Divisions shall remain an integral part of the PMS until the President
of the Philippines approves the DBM-endorsed creation of a separate ICTS and its Director
position.
The functions of the five (5) divisions in accordance with applicable laws and policy
issuances, are prescribed in Annex "A" hereof.
The Director of the PMS and the concerned Heads of Divisions, in coordination with
other offices, shall ensure the seamless transition of the restructured PMS, including
appropriate procedural adjustments and necessary human resource actions. The selection
and recruitment shall be strictly based on functional competency requirements of the PMS
Divisions.
Other offices and attached agencies of the Department are advised to observe the
herein prescribed structure in any direct working/functional arrangements with the PMS
Divisions.
This Circular amends relevant provisions of all previous issuances inconsistent
herewith, takes effect immediately, and remains in force until further issuances.

B. Evaluation and Statistics Division
1. Monitoring and Evaluation^
a. Monitors, evaluates and reports agency performance and implementation
of programs and projects in accordance with laws, policies, guidelines,
established/adopted standards, and approved plans/targets;
b. Submits to the implementing agency head of the agency's rolling six-year
evaluation agenda;
c. Reviews evaluation plans contained in project/program proposals;
d. Manages the agency's evaluation budget and related activities;
e. Conducts/manages evaluations ensuring that these are undertaken with
due regard for impartiality and in line with evaluation best practices;
f. Submit to the implementing agency head the findings and
recommendations of evaluation activities;
g. Publishes evaluation reports in the agency's public website;
h. Submits evaluation reports to oversight agencies/bodies concerned in
accordance with prescribed guidelines;
i. Serve as repository of all evaluation studies conducted/commissioned;
2. Statistics and Research**
a. Compiles, analyzes and integrated statistical data, including operational
statistics;
b. Assists in the formulation of policy proposals and general socio-economic/
sector guidelines requiring research and statistics of the agency;
c. Develops projections/forecasts, and prepares socio-economic/sector
reports and reviews based on conducted research;
d. Undertakes continuing analysis of socio-economic conditions and trends
relating to the sectoral area in which the Department is concerned;
C. Management Division^
1. Develops plans and programs relative to management improvement in the
Department;
2. Examines the administrative organization of the Department and provide
recommendations for improvement;
3. Maintains and updates the Department organization and other manuals;
4. Conducts regular management surveys of the organizational structure,
manpower and operations, and studies on special problems as assigned;
5. Reviews existing methods, systems and procedures/processes, and provide
recommendations for improvement;
6. Develops new and improved management systems, and provides staff
supervision over the implementation of such systems improvements, as well
as conduct of trainings on the application of the system/s;
7. Develops staffing standards and manpower requirements of the Department;

D. ICT Application Systems Division
1. Plans the development of applications systems in accordance with strategic
ICT agenda;
2. Undertakes the development, management, monitoring and/or evaluation of
all application systems of the Department, as well as inter-agency systems
where the DOJ is involved or part of;
3. Recommends ICT budget and procurement relative to application systems
development, administration, maintenance, security and improvement;
4. Provides technical services relating to systems development including
programming, installation, configuration, testing and quality control;
5. Administers and maintains the agency's various application systems,
including cybersecurity and protection/preservation of electronic data; and
6. Incorporates new technology and continually improves application systems;
7. Provides or facilitates training relative to application systems;
E. ICT Infrastructure Division
1. Plans the ICT infrastructure (hardware and network) of the agency in
accordance with strategic ICT agenda of government and the Department;
2. Undertakes the development, management, monitoring and/or evaluation of
the Departments ICT infrastructure:
3. Recommends budget and procurement relative to ICT infrastructure
development, administration, maintenance, security and improvement;
4. Provides technical services on ICT infrastructure including design, installation,
configuration, testing, quality control, assessment and support;
5. Administers and maintains the agency's ICT infrastructure to ensure sustained
cyber security, connectivity, and protection/preservation/ availability of
application systems and electronic data;
6. Incorporates new technology in improving ICT infrastructure and supporting
office productivity and application systems;
7. Provides training relative to use and preservation of ICT infrastructure.
