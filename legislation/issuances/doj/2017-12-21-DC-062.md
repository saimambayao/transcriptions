Pursuant to Sections 14 and 16 of Republic Act No. 10071 (Prosecution Service
Act of 2010), Prosecutors in the National Prosecution Service (NPS) with the ranks of
Prosecutor General, Prosecutors V, IV, III, II and I shall have the same qualifications
for appointment, rank, category, prerogatives, salary grade, and salaries, allowances,
and emoluments and other orivileaes. and shall enjoy the same retirement and other
benefits as those of the Presiding Justice of the Court of Appeals (CA), Associate
Justices of CA, Judges of the Regional Trial Court,( RTC), Judges of the Metropolitan
Trial Court (MetTC), Judges of the Municipal Trial Court in Cities (MTCC), Judges of
the Municipal Trial Court in Municipalities (MTCM), respectively.
Under existing laws, the Presiding Justice and Associate Justices of the Court of
Appeals (CA) as well as Judges of the RTC, MetTC, MTCC and MTCM are entitled to
annual thirty (30) days forfeitable leave benefit with pay, in addition to the fifteen
(15) days vacation leave and fifteen (15) days sick leave privileges. As such.
Prosecutors in the NPS with the same judicial ranks shall also enjoy the same annual
thirty-day forfeitable leave privilege consistent with Sections 14 and 16 of R.A. No.
10071.
Wherefore, the following guidelines shall be observed in availing of the annual
30 days forfeitable leave benefits in the NPS, viz:
1. The annual 30 days forfeitable leave shall apply to the Prosecutors in the
NPS with the ranks of Prosecutor General, and Prosecutors V, IV, III, II
and I.
2. In addition to the 15 days vacation leave and fifteen 15 days sick leave
privileges provided by law. Prosecutors with the ranks of Prosecutor

General and Prosecutors V, IV, III, II and I shall earn thirty (30) days
forfeitable leave credits with pay starting January 2018.
3. The forfeitable leave can be availed of from January 1 up to December 31
exclusive of Saturdays, Sundays and Holidays.
4. The newly appointed Prosecutors should render service for at least three
(3) months before they can avail of the forfeitable leave.
5. Newly appointed Prosecutors and who assumed their duties before
January of the year shall be entitled to thirty (30) days forfeitable leave;
those appointed in February shall be entitled to 27.5 days forfeitable
leave; those appointed in March shall be entitled to 25 days forfeitable
leave; those appointed in April shall be entitled to 22.5 forfeitable leave;
and so on, with the decrease of 2.5 days every month until the end of the
year.
6. The respective heads of prosecution offices concerned shall submit the
Schedule of Leave of their prosecutors to the Leave Section within fifteen
(15) days before the beginning of each year.
7. Prosecutors with the ranks of Prosecutor General and Prosecutors V, IV
III, II and I shall no longer be entitled to Special Leave Privileges
pursuant to Section 21, Rule XVI of the Omnibus Rules on Leave; and
8. This forfeitable leave privilege given annually to the Prosecutor General
and Prosecutors V, IV, III, II and I is non-cumulative and non-convertible
to cash.
Please be guided accordingly.
