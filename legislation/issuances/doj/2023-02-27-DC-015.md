WHEREAS, Republic Act (R.A.) No. 10071 or the “Prosecution Service Act of
2010” was enacted with the aim of strengthening and rationalizing the National
Prosecution Service (NPS);
WHEREAS, in order to ensure the effective and efficient administration of justice,
R.A. No. 10071 created and established a NPS composed of prosecution offices and
prosecutor positions which may be classified as (1) those initially mandated under the said
Act, (2) those mandated by virtue of the creation of new/administrative regions, (3) those
mandated by virtue of the creation of new provinces and cities, and (4) those mandated
by virtue of the creation of new courts or branches thereof;
WHEREAS, at present, the NPS has a total of sixteen (16) Regional Prosecution
Offices (RPOs) nationwide that are established to promote efficiency in the government
and improve the administration of justice;
WHEREAS, R.A. No. 10071 prescribed an initial number of prosecutor positions
for the Regional, Provincial, and City Prosecution Offices nationwide based on the
prosecutor to court ratio provided in Sec. 8 of the Act;
WHEREAS, taking into consideration the authorized number of prosecutor
positions to be appointed by the President under R.A. No. 10071, and the creation of new
administrative regions, new provinces and cities, and new courts, there is a need to
rationalize the Department’s prosecution offices in order to establish a clear baseline of
workforce as required under the Act;
WHEREAS, R.A. No. 10071 provided for a baseline number of prosecutor
positions to be appointed by the President;
WHEREAS, R.A. No. 10071 also provided for the creation of additional prosecutor
positions upon the establishment of new provinces or cities (Sec. 8 of the Act) and new
courts (Sec. 13 of the Act);
WHEREAS, R.A. No. 10071 1ikewise provided that, aside from the prosecutors who
shall be appointed by the President, there shall also be Prosecution Attorneys who shall
be appointed by the Secretary of Justice;

WHEREAS, the increase in the number of prosecutor positions would also
necessitate an increase in the number of support staff positions in the NPS;
WHEREAS, Executive Order (EO) No. 910, series of 1983, requires one (1)
stenographic reporter for every two (2) prosecutors;
WHEREAS, in order to ensure effective and efficient delivery of public service by
the NPS, especially those involving the resolution of complex cases, and in order to ensure
a high disposition rate in every NPS office, the necessity of appointing additional
Prosecution Attorneys and other support staff has become more evident;
WHEREAS, given the foregoing mandates, there is a need to immediately create
new prosecutor positions to be appointed by the President, Prosecution Attorneys and
support staff positions to avoid disruption in the delivery of public service;
NOW, THEREFORE, in the interest of the service, in view of the need to establish
a concrete set of guidelines for the creation and provision of adequate manpower
requirements in the Regional, Provincial, and City Prosecution Offices for prosecutors,
Prosecution Attorneys and their support staff, and for the guidance of the Department and
all its prosecution offices, the following guidelines are hereby promulgated:
SECTION 1. PURPOSE.
This Circular shall provide for the staffing standards and guidelines for all Regional,
Provincial, and City Prosecution Offices of the Department.
SECTION 2. SCOPE.
The constituent offices herein covered shall include the Office of the Secretary of
Justice Prosecution Staff (OSJPS) and the Regional, Provincial, and City Prosecution
Offices of the NPS.
Specifically, these guidelines shall apply to all prosecutor positions as regards the
creation, transfer, and upgrading of, and appointments to the same.

SECTION 8. RESPONSIBILITY CLAUSE.
a. Creation of Positions by Virtue of Newly Created Courts.
It shall be the responsibility of the heads of Provincial/City Prosecution
Offices to directly report to the Planning and Management Service (PMS), this
Department, through the Office of the Regional Prosecutor concerned, the creation
of court branch/es in their respective provinces/cities using the attached template
(Annex A).
All reports received by the PMS shall be evaluated on a quarterly basis. If
found to be in order, the PMS shall submit the corresponding Department action
document to the Undersecretary-in-Charge of Personnel, this Department, for
approval. Thereafter, the Department action document shall then be transmitted to
the Department of Budget and Management (DBM), for their appropriate action.
b. Creation of Positions by Virtue of Newly Created Regions/Provinces/Cities.
The Department Legislative Liaison Office (DLLO), this Department, shall
be primarily responsible for reporting the enactment of laws providing for the
creation of the new administrative regions, provinces, or cities to the PMS, this
Department, copy furnished the NPS.
Thereafter, the PMS shall request from the DBM, on a quarterly basis, the
creation of additional plantilla positions, based on the newly established
administrative regions, provinces, or cities.
SECTION 9. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE.
In any section or part of this Circular is declared contrary to law or unconstitutional
by a competent authority, the remaining sections or parts hereof shall remain in full force
and effect.
SECTION 10. RETROACTIVITY CLAUSE.
As far as practicable, this Circular shall retroactively apply to the date of effectivity
of R.A. No. 10071, without prejudice to the positions created and filled thereafter. This
shall include, among others, the benchmarking of positions using the initially identified
ratios in the Act, and the existing positions that were based on the same.
