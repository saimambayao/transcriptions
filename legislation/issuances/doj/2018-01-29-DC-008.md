Dear Secretary Esperon:
It is my privilege to inform the Honorable Secretary that the National Defense College of the
Philippines (NDCP) is now accepting nominees for its Master in National Security
Administration (MNSA)R egular Course No. 54forAcademic Year2018-19 which will convene
on 01 October 2018 and end on 30 September 2019. It is a one-year full-time government-
funded scholarship consisting of thirty-nine (39) units earned through various forms of
lectures, thesis writing, as well as local and foreign field studies. Guided by a holistic approach
to national security and development, with emphasis on resource management, the NDCP
follows a curriculum that integrates the political, economic, socio-cultural, techno-scientific.
environmental, and military dimensions of national security. It also includes modules on
leadership, national security administration, and global security and cooperation.
The MNSA program aims to develop the competencies, values, and skills of key officials and
national security leaders in the formulation and implementation of policies related to national
security and development. As such, nominees are selected in their capacities as future
national security leaders with Salary Grade 18 and above or its equivalent. The civilian
graduates have the privilege of being commissioned as Lieutenant Colonels or Commanders
in the Reserve Force, AFP. In addition, the Career Executive Service (CES)e ligibility process
required for the CES eligibility has been integrated within the MNSA program.
All expenses for the course will be borne by the College except for the costs to be incurred
for board and lodging, thesis writing, and the tv\.'o-week Global Security and Development
Studies. These shall be shouldered by the respective sending agencies of the candidates.
