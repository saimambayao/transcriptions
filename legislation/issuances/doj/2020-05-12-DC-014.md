DEPARTMENT CIRCULAR NO.
TO ALL CONCERNED
SUBJECT DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE OPERATIONAL
GUIDELINES DURING THE PERIOD OF PUBLIC HEALTH
EMERGENCY IN THE PHILIPPINES
DATE 12 May 2020
In the exigency of the service, pursuant to Executive Order No. 112 s. 2020
adopting the Omnibus Guidelines for the Implementation of Community Quarantine
in the Philippines, and in relation to the relevant Resolutions of the Inter-Agency
Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (lATF), the
following operational guidelines are hereby imposed:
I. Scope
This Circular covers work in the Department of Justice Main Office and in all
Prosecution Offices nationwide which have been removed from
Enhanced Community Quarantine (or any modified version thereof).
II. Period Covered
This Circular takes effect immediately and shall remain in force, unless
sooner revoked or modified.
III. Workforce Limitation on Rotation Basis
1. Without prejudice to any requirements that may be imposed by the
Department of Health (DOH)o r the lATF, and subject to the actual demand
of the respective offices or programs, a maximum of up to 50% of the
workforce of any office or program may be physically present to report for
work at any given time. The remaining personnel not required to physically
report for work shall be given assignments that can be done at home.
2. DOJ personnel residing in critical areas under Enhanced Community
Quarantine shall not be required to physically report for work.
3. The Department shall revert to a five (5)-day workweek during the covered
period. Personnel of each office or program required to physically report for
work shall be rotated on a weekly basis.

4. The head of each office or program shall submit the rotational schedule of
their workforce to the Office of the Undersecretary for Administration and
Finance IMMEDIATELY.
5. All officials, directors and heads of offices and programs are strictly
mandated to oversee and account for their respective offices and personnel
at all times.
IV. Alternative Working Arrangements
1. Members of the Legal Staff, Technical Service, Prosecutors not performing
Inquest Duties or Preliminary Investigation, other lawyers of the
Department, and other personnel whose work may be performed from
home, may adopt work-from-home arrangements in accordance with
existing rules and regulations of the Civil Service Commission.
2. The Undersecretaries shall devise work-from-home arrangements for their
respective offices and programs under their charge. Such work-from-home
arrangements shall adopt a clear output-based system in lieu of physical
attendance at work. Personnel should be given tasks to be performed to the
full extent possible in terms of man-days per work week.
3. Confidential information shall be protected and secured at all times.
4. All officials and personnel with immunodeficiency, comorbidities, or health
risks, as well as pregnant women, shall be prioritized in work-from-home
arrangements.
5. Supervising Undersecretaries are directed to immediately submit to the
Office of the Secretary the proposed work arrangements for the offices,
services or programs under their charge.
V. All Personnel On-Call
The foregoing notwithstanding, all DOJ officials and personnel are required
to be on stand-by and be on-call, wherever they may be, as anyone may be
asked to report for work should the exigencies of the service so require.
VI. Work Activities
During the covered period, all mass gatherings and activities in the
Department are prohibited. All teambuilding activities, seminars (except if
done via video- or tele-conferencing), as well as non-essential local and
foreign travel, are likewise cancelled.
VII. Constituent/Attached Agencies of the Department
The constituent/attached agencies of the Department are hereby
encouraged to adopt these rules for their own operations, or adopt and
implement their own rules as may be appropriate for the continuous delivery
of service and efficient discharge of official functions.
