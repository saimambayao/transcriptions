SUBJECT : GUIDELINES IN THE IMPLEMENTATION OF
REPUBLIC ACT NO. 9003, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS
THE ECOLOGICAL SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT ACT
OF 2000
Among the State policies provided for under Section 2 of R.A. No. 9003,
otherwise known as the Ecological Solid Waste Management Act of 2000, is the
policy to adopt a systematic, comprehensive and ecological solid waste
management program which shall ensure the proper segregation, collection,
transport, storage, treatment and disposal of solid waste through the formulation
and adoption of the best environmental practices in ecological waste
management excluding incineration, among others.
In this regard, Section 21 thereof imposes a mandatory segregation of
solid wastes primarily conducted at the source (i.e. the Department).
Accordingly, pursuant to the provisions of the said Act and its
Implementing Rules and Regulations, a MANDATORY SEGREGATION
POLICY is hereby imposed in the Department of Justice, whereby solid wastes
shall be separated at source (i.e. offices in the Department) and placed in
separate bins for BIODEGRADABLE, NON-BIODEGRADABLE (NON-
RECYCLABLE), and NON-BIODEGRADABLE (RECYCLABLE).
In accordance with Section 22 thereof, the Department shall provide for
separate containers for each type of waste from all sources, provided that, sets
of containers for the three categories, clearly and legibly labelled accordingly,
shall be strategically placed in various places in the Department. A central and
separate collection area, likewise segregated, shall also be placed for collection
of segregated wastes.
The Administrative Service, together with the Janitorial Services, is hereby
DIRECTED to implement this Circular, including the designation of the strategic

All officials and employees of the Department are hereby DIRECTED to

Non-segregation or improper segregation of solid wastes in violation of
R.A. 9003, its implementing rules and regulation, and of this Circular shall
constitute a violation of reasonable office rules and regulations, which is a light
offense, as prescribed under Section 46(F)(3) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on
Administrative Cases in the Civil Service, punishable as follows:
First Offense(cid:9) -(cid:9) Reprimand
Second Offense -(cid:9) Suspension of one (1) to thirty (30) days
Third Offense -(cid:9) Dismissal from the service
For this purpose, Janitorial Personnel of this Department are hereby
DIRECTED to inform the concerned official or employee that he or she is in
violation of this section and that the instance of his or her violation shall be
recorded. The Janitorial Personnel shall record all violations of this section and
include the same in their progress report to the Administrative Service. The
Administrative Service shall transmit a report of violations of the foregoing to the
Technical Service for appropriate disciplinary action, if any.
In case any officer or employee of the Administrative Service who is
responsible for preparing a progress report or report of violations, or is
responsible for transmitting the same to an appropriate office for appropriate
action, fails to perform his or her responsibility herein provided, he or she shall
be liable for simple neglect of duty, which is a less grave offense, as prescribed
under Section 46(D)(1) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on Administrative Cases
in the Civil Service, punishable as follows:

First Offense Suspension of one (1) month and one
(1) day to six (6) months
(cid:9)
Second Offense - Dismissal from the service
Provided, that in case the element of corruption is present, or that the
duty was neglected in exchange for valuable consideration, the person
concerned shall be liable for gross neglect of duty, which is a grave offense, as
prescribed under Section 46(A)(2) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on
Administrative Cases in the Civil Service, punishable with dismissal from the
service.
In case a Janitorial Personnel fails to keep segregate the solid waste he
or she collected, he or she shall be immediately removed from the Department's
detail. Continuous violation by the Janitorial Personnel to segregate the solid
waste shall be a ground for termination of contract between the Department and
the private janitorial agency.
