performance of offices/units in the DOJ Proper and ranking of offices and

of FY 2016 Performance-based Bonus (PBB); and
1.3(cid:9) CSC-DBM Joint Circular No. 1, s. 2012, re: grant of step increment/s
due to meritorious service and length of service.

4.0 Ratings of the said offices/clusters shall be based on calculated
accomplishment rates and the rating system under SPMS. Thereafter
office/cluster forced ranking will be based on the said ratings, on which the
following PBB percentage distribution prescribed by the AO 25 Task Force will
be applied subject to accomplishment of agency targets under major final
outputs (MFO) in FY 2016 General Appropriations Act, general administration
and support services and support to operations:
4.1(cid:9) Best delivery units — Top 10%;
4.2(cid:9) Better delivery units — Next 25%;
4.3(cid:9) Good delivery units — Next 65%.
5.0(cid:9) In case of equal point ratings, forced ranking of offices concerned shall be
based on calculated average accomplishment rates and/or the Secretary's
assessment of performance aspects such as service/output quality and
responsiveness not quantified and included in the OPCR forms.

7.0 Other third level officials, particularly the Undersecretaries, Assistant
Secretaries, Chief State Counsel, Assistant Chief State Counsels, and Office/
Service/Staff Directors shall be rated using the Career Executive Service
Performance Evaluation System (CESPES) and should receive a rating of at
least "satisfactory".
8.0(cid:9) First and second level employees/officers, including lawyers under second
level technical/managerial positions, shall be rated and ranked through the
SPMS under CSC M.C. No. 6, s. 2012, and D.C. No. 017, S. 2014. The said
employees/officers should receive a rating of at least "satisfactory" in the
individual performance commitment and review (IPCR) forms duly approved
by the Head of Office and submitted to the Personnel Division.
9.0 The PBB rates of individual employees shall depend on the performance
ranking of the regional cluster where they belong, based on the individual's
monthly basic salary as of December 31, 2016, as follows, but not lower than
P5,000.00:
PBB as % of Monthly
Performance Category
Basic Salary
"Best" Regional Cluster 65%
"Better" Regional Cluster 57.5%
"Good" Regional Cluster 50%
10.0 Step increments due to meritorious performance may be granted to qualified
officials/employees within the top 5% in every office or cluster, as follows:
10.1 Two (2) steps for two (2) ratings of "outstanding" during the two (2)
rating periods within a calendar year; and
10.2 One (1) step for one (1) rating of "outstanding" and one (1) rating of
"very satisfactory", or two (2) ratings of "very satisfactory" during the
two (2) rating periods within a calendar year.
11.0 Individuals/employees under the following occupational groups in each office
or cluster may be grouped and force ranked separately, especially for large
offices with at least 20 personnel per group (i.e. the person ranked on top,
which is 5% of 20, will be entitled to step increment for meritorious
performance):
11.1 Second/professional-level (including lawyers); and
11.2 First/sub-professional-level, (e.g. administrative assistants/aides).
12.0 In case of same ratings, the head/s of office/s concerned shall force rank
employees and officials concerned based on performance aspects such as
service/output quality and responsiveness not quantified and/or included in
the IPCR forms, in consultation with respective supervisors.

requires compliance to the SPMS. Non-compliance to the following SPMS

14.0 The PMT, shall serve as the implementing and oversight group for PBB
implementation to harmonize with the SPMS, and shall provide necessary

15.0 The Planning and Statistics Division (Planning and Management Service) shall

16.0 The Personnel Division (Administrative Service) shall enforce the prescribed
eligibility requirements and rating/ranking criteria at the individual/employee
level for the PBB eligibility and step increment for meritorious performance,
including compliance to CESPES and SPMS as well as other pertinent Civil
Service and compensation rules and regulations.
17.0 The PMT shall duly advise offices concerned on SPMS compliance/
deficiencies, calculated office performance and rating based on quarterly
accomplishment reports, and eligibility of individual officials/officers/
employees based on submitted IPCR forms and PBB requirements.
Validation/audit of reported accomplishments may also be conducted by the
PMT, as necessary.
18.0 Disputes/complaints arising from implementation hereof shall be formally
submitted/referred to the PMT Chairperson for appropriate action, mainly in
accordance with SPMS guidelines/procedures.
19.0 As prescribed under AO 25 Task Force M.C. 2016-1, the herein guidelines and
other relevant issuances/documents/information shall be posted in the
Transparency Seal page of the D03 Website. Heads of offices shall ensure
that this is disseminated to constituent employees immediately upon receipt.
The PMT may organize/participate in forums to facilitate information
dissemination, communication and feedback.
