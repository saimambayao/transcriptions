SUBJECT ESTABLISHMENT OF A DOJ HUMAN RIGHTS OFFICE
(HRO)
JUL 1 4 2023
DATE
WHEREAS, Section 11, Article II of the 1987 Constitution declares as a policy
of the State to value the dignity of every human person and guarantee full respect for
human rights;
WHEREAS, the Philippines is a contributor and signatory to the 1948
Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHFR), and has always been steadfast in
upholding, protecting, and promoting human rights at the national and international
levels, as shown by its vibrant democracy “anchored on the dignity of the human
person and the sanctity of human life”;1
WHEREAS, the Philippines is a Party to various United Nations (UN) human
rights treaties and conventions;
WHEREAS, the Philippines formally joined in May 2023 the Group of Friends
of the Nelson Mandela Rules, an international organization under the auspices of the
United Nations to create awareness and promote the practical application of the
United Standard Minimum Rules for the Treatment of Prisoners, otherwise known as
the Mandela Rules;
WHEREAS, Administrative Order (AO) No. 163, series of 2006, appointed the
Secretary of Justice as Vice-Chairperson of the Presidential Human Rights
Committee (PHRC), the primary advisory body to the President on all human rights
concerns/issues, and designated the Department of Justice (DOJ) as Lead Agency
for the Working Group on International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
(ICCPFR) as well as member of the various Working Groups on other international
human rights treaties and conventions;
WHEREAS, AO No. 01, s. 2016, created the Presidential Task Force on
Violence against Media Workers mandated to ensure a safe environment for Media
Workers, and whereby the Secretary of Justice is appointed as Chairperson;
WHEREAS, the DOJ, as the government’s principal law agency responsible
for the effective, efficient, and equitable administration of justice, is one with the

WHEREFORE, in the interest of the service and pursuant to the provisions of

commitments and obligations, including but not limited to the following:

4. Liaise with relevant government agencies or offices, such as the Presidential
Human Rights Committee Secretariat, among others, in the preparation and
consolidation of necessary reports, positions, or statements in relation to said
commitments/obligations
;
5. Coordinate with the Department’s specialized units and committees for groups
in need of special protection (e.g., women, children, differently-abted persons,
migrants, LGBTI,3 senior citizens, indigenous peoples, and such other
marginalized sectors or vulnerable groups), to ensure that their programs,
activities, and projects are aligned with the State’s human rights policies and
commitments;
6. Assess and monitor the human rights mechanism standards in the Department
and its attached agencies, and recommend policies, including legislative
measures, to effectively implement human rights laws, rules, and regulations;
7. Keep and maintain a repository of records and data relating to human rights
programs, activities and projects of the Department and its attached agencies;
8. Develop and publish relevant data analytics and advocacy materials on human
rights;
9. Implement and extend relevant capacity building activities and technical
assistance necessary for the attainment of work plan and commitments; and
10. Perform such other functions and duties as may be directed by the Secretary of
Justice.
The operations of the DOJ-HRO shall be administered by a Program Director,
to be assisted by technical officers consisting of prosecutors, state counsels,
lawyers, statisticians, and ICT Officers, as well as administrative staff, under the
supervision of the Undersecretary and/or Assistant Secretary for Legal Services and
Liaison Cluster.
Henceforth, in the efficient implementation and discharge of its duties and
functions, the DOJ-HRO shall be allocated with necessary budgetary appropriations
and provided with office space, equipment, and fixtures.
All concerned units, committees, offices, programs, and services in the
Department and its attached agencies with existing and ongoing human rights-
related programs are hereby directed to work closely and coordinate with the said
Office, and provide needed assistance and information or relevant data to enable the
same to fulfill its mandates.
