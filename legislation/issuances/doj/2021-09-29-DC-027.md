1.0. PURPOSE
These guidelines are being issued in relation to the prescribed requirements
that must be complied with by all offices in the Department ofJustice (DOJ) for
the grant of Performance-Based Bonus (PBB) for FY 2021, to be given in FY
2022.
These guidelines shall cover a) all offices in the DOJ Proper, including the
Office of the Secretary, Office of the Undersecretaries and Office of the
Assistant Secretaries; and b) all regional/provincial/city prosecution offices
nationwide, including the National Capital Region (NCR).
2.0. LEGAL FRAMEWORK
Pursuant to Memorandum Circular No. 2021-1, s. of2021 ofthe Inter-Agency
Task Force on the Harmonization of National Government Performance
Monitoring, Information and Reporting Systems (accessible online at
https://www.doj.gov.ph/doj performance management.html), the guidelines
for the grant of PBB for FY 2021 are hereby adopted.
3.0. ELIGBILITY CRITERIA
The FY 2021 PBB criteria and conditions are categorized into four (4)
dimensions of accountability, namely: Performance Results, Process
Results, Financial Results, and Citizen/Client Satisfaction Results, which
are defined hereunder. The accomplishment of offices for each of the criteria
shall be rated using a scale of 1-5 (where 5 is the highest). Each criterion has
an assigned weight as shown in the table below. The maximum score that may
be obtained is 100 points. To be eligible for the FY 2021 PBB, the agency must
attain a total score of at least 70 points.

The targets of each prosecution office shall be based on the prosecutor-case
ratio categories as provided for under Department Circular No. 18, s. 2020. It
covers cases handled for both investigation/resolution and prosecution in
Court. The following are the case ratio categories:

2. Moderate caseload - At least 250 per prosecutor;
3. Average caseload - At least 500 cases per prosecutor;
4. Heavy caseload - At least 1,000 per prosecutor; and
5. Extremely heavy caseload - At least 1500 per prosecutor.

5.0. ELIGIBILITY OF DELIVERY UNITS AND INDIVIDUALS
5.1. For FY 2021 PBB, the delivery units ofeligible agencies shall no longer
be ranked. However, the unit/s most responsible fordeficiencies shall be
isolated.
5.1.1. Based on Item 3.0, to be eligible forthe FY 2021 PBB, the agency
must attain a total score ofat least 70 points. To be able to attain
at least 70 points, the agency should achieve a performance
rating of 4 in at least three (3) criteria. In such case, while the
agency will be eligible, the unit/s most responsible (including
its head) for the criteriawith a performance rating of below4
will be isolated from the grant of the FY 2021 PBB.
5.1.2. The unit/s most responsible (including its head) for the non
compliance with the Department Accountabilities provided in
Section 4.0 will also be isolated from the grant of the FY 2021
PBB.
5.2. Eligible delivery units shall be granted FY 2021 PBB at uniform rates
across the agency, including its officials and employees. The
