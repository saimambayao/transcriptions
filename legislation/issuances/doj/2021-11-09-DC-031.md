In addition to the directives set forth in Department Circular No. 026 dated
October 9, 2020 entitled "Review and Compliance Procedure in the Filing and
Submission of Statement of Assets, Liabilities and Net Worth and Disclosure of
Business Interests and Financial Connections", the following supplemental guidelines
shall be observed in the submission of the SALNs and review to be done by the Review
and Compliance Committees, both in the DOJ Proper and in the Regional Prosecution
Offices:
Employees are strictly required to fill in all applicable information and/or make
a true and detailed statement in their SALNs. Items not applicable should be
marked N/A (not applicable). Relative to this, employees are enjoined to use
the SALN Form as provided in the portal at https://my.doj.gov.ph.
All SALNs shall be submitted in triplicate copies, all originally signed by the
declarant/s. In case of joint filing and the declarant's spouse is also with the
government, additional copies shall be submitted to be stamped "received" by
the RCC Secretariat.
Employees who are detailed or reassigned to an office outside of their mother
office/unit shall submit their SALNs to said office where they are detailed or
reassigned. The review of their SALNs shall be done by the RCC having
territorial jurisdiction over said offices.
To facilitate the timely filing and submission of the SALNs, the following shall
be the officers delegated with authority limited only to administration of oaths
of SALN filers:

Station Designated Officer
Offices in the DOJ Proper Director IV, Administrative Service
Local Prosecution Offices Chief of prosecution office where the

The oath for the SALNs of employees who are on study leave abroad shall be
done at the Philippine Foreign Post nearest them and their SALNs shall likewise
be submitted on or before the deadline of submission of SALNs.
To aid the RCCs in their review of the SALNs of officials and employees, the
Chief of Personnel Division shall submit to the RCC an alphabetical list of
employees as of December 31 of every year. Said list shall be submitted not
later than January 31 of the following year and shall include the name, position,
and Tax Identification Number (TIN) of officials and employees. Those who are
separated from the service (due to retirement, resignation, death, etc.) during
the inclusive year shall be indicated in the remarks.
This circular shall be posted as part of the Transparency Seal on the DOJ
website.
This Circular takes effect immediately and shall remain in force until further
orders.
