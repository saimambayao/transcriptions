I.(cid:9) Policy Statement
WHEREAS, the Department's compound is a vital installation of
government, given the important work done by its officials and employees
pursuant to its mandate;
WHEREAS, as part of security protocols in the Department, there is a need
to monitor and control the ingress and egress of people, vehicles,
equipment, and others;
NOW, THEREFORE, the following rules are promulgated for consistency
and uniformity in the implementation of the DO) Gate Access Control
System:
H. Coverage
This circular shall apply to all officials and employees of this
Department, including contractuals, job orders, and other
employees of the government, as well as to the members of the
media and the general public.
This circular shall likewise apply to all vehicles, government or
privately owned, whether of officials or employees of the
Department, or of visitors, that will seek to enter or exit the
Department's compound.

### Page 2

C. For purposes of this Circular, unless otherwise stated, the
Department's officials and employees shall include its plantilla
and non-plantilla personnel.
Members of the media shall mean duly-accredited persons by
recognized media or news outfits, provided, that they shall be
required to conspicuously wear their respective media IDs at all
times while inside the Department.
Visitors and other persons shall mean the general public with an
official business with the Department, or persons who have a
concern or transaction with the Department or any of its official
or employee, provided, that they shall be required to
conspicuously wear their respective IDs at all times while inside
the Department, and provided further, that employees are
enjoined not to entertain social visits from friends inside the
Department.
III.(cid:9) System of Access (Entry and Exit)
A. There shall be two modes of access to the Department:
Walkway for Pedestrians
All persons entering or exiting the Department on foot shall
do so through the Pedestrian Walkway located at the Security
Outpost in the Main Gate.
There shall be two turnstile gates installed in the walkway
A dedicated turnstile gate with conspicuous markings "For
Officials and Employees" shall be located at the left-
side of the Security Outpost where officials and employees
in the DOJ plantilla shall tap their DO] ID Access Card
for verification, identification and authentication.
A separately dedicated turnstile gate with conspicuous
markings "For Visitors and Others" shall be located at
the right-side of the Security Outpost.
Job Orders, Contractuals and Non-DOJ personnel shall be
issued regular (non-Access Card) DOJ ID. They shall be
required to wear their respective DOJ IDs at all times while
inside the Department premises.
Members of the media shall be required to wear their
respective Media ID at all times while inside the
Department premises.

For this purpose, officials of other government offices shall
mean those occupying the rank of at least Assistant Secretary
or their equivalent in the executive branch, Associate Justice
or their equivalent in the judicial branch, Members of Congress
or their equivalent in the legislative branch, or Members of
Constitutional Commissions, provided that, their vehicles shall
be subjected to an appropriate identification of passengers
and inspection by DOJ security personnel.
Likewise, VIPs shall mean persons who are to be given
preferential access, as authorized by the Office of the
Secretary, provided, that such authority is properly
communicated with the Officer-in-Charge in the Security
Outpost.
Armored vans, delivery vans and other commercial vehicles
transacting business with the Department may be allowed
access inside the Department subject to reasonable security
measures, as may be deemed necessary by the Security Unit.
The main gate shall be manned at all times by at least two (2)
security personnel, with visible identification, preferable in the
proper uniform and sufficiently armed with an appropriate
weapon or sidearm.
Unauthorized vehicles shall be barred from entering the main
gate. Their passengers shall be allowed access to the
Department's compound only through the walkway.
No pedestrian shall be allowed to pass through the main gate.
Nothing in this item shall be construed as to deny access to
senior citizens and other people with special needs. For this
purpose, their respective vehicles may be allowed access
inside the Department for them to be dropped off at the
carport located at the flagpole (behind Delas Alas Hall/Main
Building). One (1) VIP parking slot shall at all times be
reserved for senior citizen/PWD parking.
IV.(cid:9) Access on Non-working Days
A. No person shall be allowed access inside the Department premises on
Saturdays, Sundays, Holidays, or days without work, except for Officials
of the Department together with their confidential staff, Security
Personnel on duty, and employees of the Department duly-authorized
and required by their respective supervisors to report to work on the
foregoing days, provided, that in all instances, the Officer-in-Charge at

the Security Outpost shall be duly-informed of their presence and he or
she shall log the time of their entrance and exit, provided further, that
access during non-working days shall be allowed only for official
purposes.
B. No privately-owned vehicle shall be allowed access inside the
Department premises on Saturdays, Sundays, Holidays, or days without
work, except in those instances similar to item IV(A) above.
V.(cid:9) Policy on Vendors
In pursuit of delivering services to the general public, the Department
shall be accessible to the visitors and other persons with official business,
transaction or concern with the Department and/or its officials and
employees, subject to reasonable security measure as provided above.
Nonetheless, for security purposes, vendors and other wanderers are
strictly prohibited from entering the Department premises.
On special of occasions like anniversary, bazaars, and other events and
activities especially sanctioned by the Department, the prohibition on
vendors may be relaxed, provided, that a space shall be identified where
outsider are allowed access inside the Department, and, that the
Security Personnel shall be on heightened alert as to prohibit loitering of
unauthorized persons.
VI.(cid:9) DO] ID Access Card
All officials and employees listed in the DOJ plantilla shall be issued new
Identification Cards equipped with an access pass. Accordingly, the ID
Access Card shall be worn conspicuously at all times.
All officials and employees listed in the DOJ plantilla are hereby
DIRECTED to proceed to the Communications Division to apply for their
respective DO] ID Access Passes. The Communications Division shall
process the dissemination and collection of an appropriate form to be
filled-up for this purpose. Likewise, the Communications Division shall
supervise the enrolment to the system.
All officials and employees shall be responsible for their respective DOJ
ID Access Passes. In case of loss or need for replacement, a request for
the purpose shall be accompanied by a duly notarized Affidavit.
Appropriate fees shall be collected, through the DO] cashier and with an
appropriate official receipt, to cover replacement costs.

The Administrative Service, through the Security Unit, shall have primary
responsibility in the implementation of this Circular, except as to the

All officials and employees, as well as all those who seek access to the
Department, shall comply with this Circular.
VIII. Sanctions
A. Repeated failure to bring DO] ID Access Card or DO] ID (non-Access
Card). — any person issued a DO] Access Card or any DOJ non-plantilla
personnel issued a DO] ID (non-Access Card), who fails to bring his or
her ID shall only be permitted access to the DO] premises by registering
as a visitor at the Security Outpost, wearing a Visitor's pass issued in
exchange for his or her valid ID, and entering through the visitors' gate,
otherwise, he or she shall be barred from entering the DO] premises.
Repeated failure to bring his or her ID for five (5) consecutive days
within a single month or five (5) non-consecutive days within a year shall
constitute a violation of reasonable office rules and regulations, which is
a light offense, as prescribed under Section 46(F)(3) of Rule 10 of the
Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service, punishable as
follows:
First Offense(cid:9) Reprimand
Second Offense -(cid:9) Suspension of one (1) to thirty (30) days
Third Offense -(cid:9) Dismissal from the service
Except that in case of loss or destruction of the DO] ID Access Card or
DO] ID (non-Access Card), the submission of an appropriate request for
replacement together with the notarized affidavit shall exempt the
concerned official or employee from this provision, until he or she has
been issued a replacement ID.

IX.(cid:9) Other exclusions
The provisions of this Circular shall be without prejudice to the power of
the Secretary to promulgate emergency rules and issue directives for the
security within the Department premises during times of crisis or alarm,
such as, but not limited to, calamity, disaster, rally, riot, violence,
rebellion, insurrection, coup d'etat, and other similar instances of general
and widespread disorder and chaos outside the Department premises.
Nothing in this Circular shall be interpreted to prohibit the access of
police, fire, medical, and other emergency personnel during times of
need.
X.(cid:9) Effectivity

5, 2017 together with a "NO ID, NO ENTRY" policy.
The prohibition on the access of unauthorized vehicles and parking
inside the DO) Compound shall commence on January 5, 2017.
