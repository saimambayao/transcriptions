II. Unless otherwise provided by law, executive issuance, or otherwise directed herein
or by the Secretary:
a. The Undersecretaries, Assistant Secretaries, Prosecutor General, and Chief
State Counsel shall retain their respective authority, pursuant to existing laws
and issuances, to sign all communications and action documents pertaining to
their respective areas of responsibilities under this Circular.
b. The Assistant Secretaries, as deputies, shall have authority to sign all
communications and action documents pertaining to the units and programs
falling under their respective special assignments, subject to the supervision of
the Undersecretary-in-charge, if applicable. The assistance of Assistant
Secretaries may be required by the Secretary or any of the Undersecretaries as
the exigencies of the service may require.
c. All documents, papers, letters, communications, information and other matters
pertaining to the offices and proceedings for the attention and/or action by the
Secretary of Justice shall be coursed through the Undersecretary to whom the
specific subject matter is delegated as provided in this Circular.
III.In the implementation of the foregoing delineation of areas of duties and
responsibilities, all the above-mentioned personnel are enjoined to keep the
Secretary of Justice advised of developments falling under the areas assigned to
them, such as through the submission of reports (e.g., Monthly Accomplishment
Reports, Post-Event Reports, periodic budgetary/financial reports of
offices/committees/councils that have their own separate funding, etc.) or
memoranda on particular issues or matters.
To further ensure that the Secretary of Justice is informed of critical developments
within the Department, there shall be regular Executive Committee Meetings held
immediately after the flag ceremony on Mondays, to be presided over by the
Secretary of Justice or the Senior Undersecretary, as the case may be.
