WHEREAS, pursuant to Executive Order (E.O.) No. 292, otherwise known as the
Administrative Code of 1987, the Secretary of Justice has the power to promulgate
rules and regulations necessary to carry out department objectives, policies, functions,
plans, programs and projects and in relation thereto, promulgate administrative
issuances necessary for the efficient administration of the offices under the Secretary
and for proper execution of the laws;
WHEREAS, the Secretary of Justice has the power to exercise control and supervision
over the National Prosecution Service, under Republic Act No. 10071 or the National
Prosecution Service Act,
WHEREAS, pursuant to Republic Act No. 11643 otherwise known as AN ACT
GRANTING SURVIVORSHIP BENEFITS TO THE SURVIVING LEGITIMATE
SPOUSE AND DEPENDENT CHILDREN OF A DECEASED RETIRED MEMBER OF
THE NATIONAL PROSECUTION SERVICE AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS
THEREFOR, in case of death of a retired member of the National Prosecution Service
(NPS) or a member thereof who is eligible to retire optionally at the time of death, the
surviving legitimate spouse and dependent children of said deceased member shall
be entitled to receive all the retirement benefits that the deceased was receiving or
entitled to receive;
NOW, THEREFORE, in the interest of the service and pursuant to existing laws, rules
and regulations, and in order to rationalize the procedure in the filing, evaluation and
payment of survivorship benefits, the following Rules are hereby adopted:

Section 3. DEFINITION OF TERMS. As used in this IRFI, unless the context otherwise

Section 7. ACCRUAL. Unless otherwise terminated, discontinued or suspended for
causes set forth in this IRR, the Qualified Beneficiaries shall be entitled to the

a. Upon the death of a Qualified Prosecutor, who was receiving or was entitled

Section 8. AUTOMATIC INCREASE. The Survivorship Benefits as defined in Section
3(a) hereof shall be automatically increased whenever there is an increase in the
salary and allowance of the same position from which the Qualified Prosecutor retired
or died, as the case may be.6
Section 9. DEDUCTIONS AGAINST MONTHLY SURVIVORSHIP BENEFIT. In case
there is any amount paid or to be paid by other government offices/agencies from their
own funds, such as but not limited to the Government Insurance Service System
(GSIS) and the Philippine National Police (PNP), for payment of similar benefits, the
said amount/s shall be deducted from the amount payable by the DOJ.
Section 10. SEMI-ANNUAL RENEWAL OF ACTIVE STATUS (SARAS). All Qualified
Beneficiaries are required to renew their active status twice a year, every January and
July of each calendar year, from the time of receipt of their initial Survivorship Benefits,
by complying with the following:
a. Show proof that the Qualified Beneficiary/ies is/are alive; and
b. Submit a sworn undertaking that they possess no disqualifications by
reason of the grounds for termination or discontinuance provided in this
IRR
The Committee shall adopt a procedure to ensure that the beneficiaries may
conveniently and accessibly comply with this provision, such as but not limited to,
communication via Zoom, FaceTime, or via any personal appearance before any
prosecution office in the country.

Section 12. DISCONTINUANCE OF SURVIVORSHIP BENEFITS.
a. Survivorship benefits shall be discontinued upon the occurrence of any of
the following circumstances:
(1 ) Gainful employment of Dependent Child/ren; or
(2) Cessation of incapability for self-support due to a mental or
physical defect or condition of the Dependent Child/ren.
b. The share of the Qualified Beneficiary, which was discontinued, shall be
equally distributed to the other Qualified Beneficiaries, if any.
C Upon cessation of the ground for discontinuance and submission of
adequate proof thereof, the payment of Survivorship Benefits to the
adversely affected Qualified Beneficiary shall resume from the time of such
cessation, and without any retroactive effect.
Section 13. TERMINATION OF SURVIVORSHIP BENEFITS.
a. Survivorship benefits shall be terminated permanently upon the occurrence
of any of the following circumstances:
(1) Death of a Qualified Beneficiary;
(2) Remarriage of the Surviving Legitimate Spouse/s (SLS);
(3) Attainment of the age of twenty-two (22) years by a Dependent Child
who is not incapable of self-support due to a mental or physical
defect or condition; or
(4) Marriage of a dependent child.
b The termination of the Survivorship Benefits of a Qualified Beneficiary shall
be permanent and will no longer entitle him/her to such benefits. His/her
share shall be equally distributed to the other Qualified Beneficiaries, if any.

RULE III
MULTIPLE BENEFICIARIES
Section 14. DIVISION OF SHARES IN CASE OF MULTIPLE BENEFICIARIES. The
survivorship benefit shall be equally divided among two or more Qualified Beneficiaries
as may be determined by the Committee.
Section 15. MULTIPLE VALID AND EXISTING MARRIAGES. The claim for
Survivorship Benefits arising from the death of a male Muslim Qualified Prosecutor,
who is permitted to have more than one wife but not more than four at a time pursuant
to Presidential Decree No. 1083 or the Code of Muslim Personal Laws, may be filed
by more than one Surviving Legitimate Spouse and several Dependent Children of the
Qualified Prosecutor with different legal wives.
Section 16. APPEARANCE OF NEW CLAIMANTS. If additional claimants of the
Survivorship Benefits appear, they shall promptly submit the necessary documents
establishing their qualifications.
The Committee may issue an order suspending the processing and/or release of
the Survivorship Benefits.
The Committee will issue a Resolution deciding on this matter including the
following if necessary:
a. The correct shares of all the Qualified Beneficiaries, which will be followed
in distributing the Survivorship Benefits; and
b. How the unpaid share of the Preterited Qualified Beneficiary, without
interest, will be paid in case the other beneficiaries have previously received
the monthly Survivorship Benefits.

The Committee shall adopt and promulgate issuances or guidelines to ease the

documentary requirements.
No survivorship benefit shall be released until receipt and verification of the SBAF
and other documentary requirements, and the issuance of a Resolution promulgated
by the Committee.
Section 23. DENIAL OF CLAll 1. The survivorship benefit claim shall be:
a. Denied entirely if the deceased prosecutor is not qualified; or
b. Denied only insofar as to the disqualified beneficiary/ies, without affecting
the claim of the other Qualified Beneficiary/ies of the Qualified Prosecutor.
Any claim for Survivorship Benefits, which is attended by fraud,
misrepresentation, falsification or collusion, shall be denied and may be subject to the
filing of any criminal action which the Committee may deem appropriate.
Section 24. APPEAL OF THE COMMITTEE RESOLUTION. The claimant may file an

FINAL PROVISIONS
Section 25. FUNDING SOURCE. The funding requirement for the payment of

Section 27. AMENDMENT. The IRR may be modified by the Department of Justice
when necessary for the effective implementation and enforcement of R.A. No. 11643.
Section 28. SEPARABILITY CLAUSE. If for any reason, any part or provision of
these Rules is declared unconstitutional, the other parts or provisions hereof which
are not affected shall remain and continue to be in full force and effect.
Section 29. REPEALING CLAUSE. All rules and regulations inconsistent with these
Rules are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.
Section 30. EFFECTIVITY. These Rules shall take effect after fifteen (15) days from
the date of publication thereof in the Official Gazette; provided that three (3) certified
copies of these Rules shall have likewise been filed with the Office of the National
Administrative Register (ONAFR) of the University of the Philippines Law Center,
University of the Philippines, Diliman, Quezon City.
