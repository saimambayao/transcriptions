In view of the implementation of the COVID-19 Alert Level System in the
National Capital Region (NCR) and pursuant to the Guidelines on the Pilot
Implementation of Alert Levels System for COVID-19 Response in the NCR dated 13
September 2021, Department of Justice Offices and City Prosecution Offices in the
NCR shall adopt the following work arrangements beginning 16 September 2021,
depending on the Alert Level declared in the said area:
COVID-19 Alert Level Work Arran .ement
Alert Level 5 Offices shall be fully operational, through an on-site
skeletal workforce of at least twenty percent (20%)
and the rest under a work-from-home arrangement'
Alert Level 4 Offices shall be fully operational, through an on-site
skeletal workforce of at least twenty percent (20%)
and the rest under a work-from-home arrangement
Alert Level 3 Offices shall be fully operational, through an on-site
skeletal workforce of at least thirty percent (30%)
and the rest under a work-from-home arrangement
Alert Level 2 Offices shall be fully operational, through an on-site
skeletal workforce of at least fifty percent (50%)
and the rest under a work-from-home arrangement

The heads of offices/units/programs are further directed to submit to Director
IV Liza B. De Leon, Administrative Service, the list of personnel who will be
unable to physically report for work due to the imposition of granular lockdowns
in their respective residences.
Frontline Services
All officials, directors, and heads of offices/units/programs are hereby reminded
that frontline services shall be fully operational regardless of the alert level.
Accordingly, all heads of offices shall ensure the availability of personnel at the
Frontline Service who will receive documents, mail, and other papers.
Work-From-Home Arrangement
All officials, directors, and heads of offices/units/programs shall strictly observe
the relevant rules and guidelines of the Civil Service Commission (CSC),
particularly CSC Memorandum Circular No. 18, s. 2020,2 on the implementation
of work-from-home and/or other alternative work arrangements.
Department personnel under work-from-home arrangements are directed to
make themselves readily available during regular work hours for any urgent
work that may need their on-site presence.
Inquest Proceedings
Prosecutors and their support staff shall continue to conduct inquest
proceedings online and/or in person, and attend to urgent matters that require
immediate action, as may be determined by the heads of the prosecution offices
concerned. Accordingly, all prosecution offices are directed to ensure the
availability at all times of inquest prosecutors who shall be on-call. The conduct
of inquest proceedings shall proceed in accordance with the Rules of Court and
existing rules and regulations of the Department.
This Circular shall take effect immediately and shall remain in force until further
orders, subject to any such amendment as may be declared or issued by the IATF-
MEID or any other competent authority.
For strict compliance.
