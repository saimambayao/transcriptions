1 .0 Rationale
One of the core functions of the Department of Justice (DOJ) is the administration of the
Criminal Justice System. The said system includes the Prosecution Pillar, the core of
which is the National Prosecution Service (NPS). The main task of the NPS is to
investigate criminal complaints from the general public and law enforcement agencies,
and prosecute such complaints that are filed in court for judicial proceedings.
Through the years, there are national developments and major breakthroughs of the
Department that led to the enhancement of the reporting, monitoring and evaluation of
this core function. This includes the signing of a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)
between the Department of Justice (DOJ), Department of Interior and Local Government
(DILG), Dangerous Drugs Board (DDB) and Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA)
in 2015, which aims to establish an integrated criminal justice information called the
National Justice Infbrmation System (NJIS).
The NJIS is an information and communications technology (ICT)-based tool envisioned
to facilitate the management and exchange of vital information across law enforcement
and justice sector agencies in the discharge of their statutorily mandated public functions.
One of its Project Components is the Prosecution Case Management System (PCMS).
With the system development in 2017, the PCMS has been plot-tested to different
Prosecution Offices particularly in the National Capital Region (NCR).
In the interest of the service, all Prosecution Offices shall be guided by the following policy
guidelines on the Implementation of the Prosecution Case Management System (PCMS)
in the National Prosecution Service.

This issuance aims to ensure the full implementation of the Prosecution Case

workstations or laptops using any web browser connected to the
