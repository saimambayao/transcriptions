1.0 BACKGROUND
Budget Circular (BC) No. 2022-1 dated 11 February 20221 was issued to
harmonize the various circulars issued, and to provide the omnibus guidelines
on the acquisition, use, rental, and replacement of government motor vehicles
as a single reference document.
Annex A (Updated Motor Vehicle Description, Classifications, and
Specifications Guide) of said BC provides the motor vehicle types, vehicle
description, and allowed specifications for the intended uses and/or users of
the same
2.0
PURPOSE
Ibis Circular is being issued to provide additional motor vehicle type options
for Department Secretary-level and Undersecretary-level positions and other
officials of equivalent rank, as well as the Chief of Staff of the Armed Forces
of the Philippines (AFP), that are necessary and appropriate in the exercise of
their executive functions, and would create economical value in reducing the
number of motor vehicles that may be needed to transport the accompanying
immediate support staff and/or security personnel.

3.0 POLICY STATEMENT
Consistent with BC No. 2022-1, it is the policy of the government to ensure
the prudent and judicious acquisition, use, rental, and replacement of
government motor vehicles, thus, the motor vehicles to be acquired and/or
rented by the government shall be:
a. Limited to those necessary and appropriate for the officials authorized to
use official transport vehicles;
b. For the performance by government personnel of official functions or
activities requiring transport mobility; and
c. Cost-effective, fuel efficient, environment-friendly, and at par with the
improvements and developments in the automotive industry and relevant
technology.
4.0
COVERAGE
4.1 This Circular shall apply to all National Government Agencies (NGAs)
under the Executive Branch, including SUCs, GOCCs, GFIs, LWDs, and
the LGUs.
4.2 The Legislature, Judiciary, Constitutional Commissions, and Office of
the Ombudsman are encouraged to adopt the provisions of this
Circular, as may be necessary and applicable.
4.3 As provided under Administrative Order (AO) No. 14, s. 2018,2 the
acquisition of the following motor vehicles shall not be covered by this
Circular:
a. Vehicles used for security purposes for the President and the
Vice-President;
b. Vehicles donated in kind to the Philippine Government or any of
its agencies and instrumentalities by foreign governments, and
bilateral and multilateral institutions;
C. Vehicles used for visIting foreign dignitaries maintained by the
Office of the President (OP)-Proper and the Department of Foreign
Affairs; and
d. Vehicles acquired using funds from existing official development
assistance programs according to the terms thereof.

6.0 PRUDENCE IN ACQUIRING MOTOR VEHICLES FOR THE EXERCISE OF
EXECUTIVE FUNcrioNS
Notwithstanding the inclusion of a passenger van and an SUV as among the
motor vehicle types that the Department Secretary-level officials, and
Undersecretary-level officials, as well as the Chief of Staff of the AFP,
respectively, may use in the exercise of their executive functions, the agencies
concerned shall determine which motor vehicle type is the most appropriate
and beneficial for the government for the purpose, taking into consideration
the cost of acquisition and maintenance, among others.
7.0 RErrERATiON ON THE PROHiBrrION ON THE ACQUISITION OF
LUXURY VEHICLES
All government entities covered under this Circular are prohibited from
acquiring and/or using luxury vehicles for their operations, consistent with the
description/definition of a luxury vehicle under AO No. 14, s. 2018 and BC No.
2022-1. Agencies are prohibited from acquiring or using the following
passenger vans and SUVs:
a. Passenger van that has an engine displacement exceeding 2500 cc, if
gasoline-fed; or 3000 cc, if diesel-fed; and/or with an engine exceeding
four (4) cylinders; and
b. SUV that has an engine displacement exceeding 2700 cc, if gasoline-fed;
or 3000 cc, if diesel-fed; and/or with an engine exceeding four (4)
cylinders.
8.0 TRANSITORY GUIDELINES
These guidelines shall not apply to all requests for authority to purchase or
rent motor vehicles which were authorized by the appropriate approving
authority prior to the effectivity of this Circular, unless the agency concerned
requests for a revised Authority to Purchase Motor Vehicles/Authority to Rent
Motor Vehicles, supported by valid justification as may be accepted by the
approving authority.

9.0 RESOLUTION OF ISSUES
Issues and concerns that may arise in the implementation of this Circular shall
be referred to the Department of Budget and Management for resolution.
10.0 SEPARABILITY
If any provision of this Circular is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the other
provisions not affected thereby shall remain valid and subsisting.
Other provisions of BC No. 2022-1 that are not affected by these supplemental
guidelines shall remain in full force and effect.
11.0 EFFECTiVrrY
