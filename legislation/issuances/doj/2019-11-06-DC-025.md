In the interest of justice, pursuant to Section 1.1.2 of Department Circular No.
034 dated 31 August 2018 ("Guidelines on the Disposition of Petitions for Review and
Cases Under Automatic Review") and Department Order No.2 86 dated 30 May 2019
("Petition for Review Decongestion Task Force") , as amended by Department Order
No. 574 dated 25 October 2019 , and the provisions of existing laws, the following
guidelines on the disposition of petitions for review and cases under automatic review
covered by the Decongestion Project ("Guidelines") are hereby issued for the
disposition of cases filed before 01 July 2016:
1. COVERAGE AND GENERAL PROVISIONS
1.1. These Guidelines shall cover all petitions for review and cases under
automatic review, including the corresponding motions for reconsideration,
filed with the Office of the Secretary of Justice before 01 July 2016 except
those (1) already pending with the offices of the Undersecretaries and
Assistant Secretaries; and (2) cases subject of follow-up request or any
similar motion from party-litigants that had been or may be processed in
accordance with Section 1.1. 1 of Department Circular No. 034 dated 31
August 2018.
1.2. Undersecretaries and Assistant Secretaries authorized to resolve Petitions
for Review and Cases on Automatic Review under Department Circular No.
034 dated 31 August 2018 and Department Order No. 509 dated 26
September 2019 are hereby authorized to sign and/or approve resolutions
on petitions for review and cases under automatic review filed prior to 1 July
2016, including the corresponding motions for reconsideration, for and on

### Page 2

behalf of the Secretary of Justice subject to the provisions of these
Guidelines.
1.3. Nothing in these Guidelines shall be construed to deprive the Secretary of
Justice of the power to directly act upon any case at any time or level of
review at his discretion.
1.4. The foregoing notwithstanding, existing department orders and other
assignments made by the Secretary of Justice shall remain applicable
insofar as these are not inconsistent with these Guidelines.
2. INVENTORY OF CASES AND CLASSIFICATION OF RECORDS
2.1. The Inventory Team created under D.O. No. 286 dated 30 May 2019, as
amended, shall conduct a physical inventory and maintain a database of all
cases filed before 01 July 2016 that had remained unresolved upon the
effectivity of D.C. No. 034 dated 31 August 2018.
2.1.1 The said Inventory Team shall have the custody of, be responsible
for, and ensure the integrity of the record of said cases. The Inventory Team
shall also submit a list of all such cases to the Office of the Secretary
Appeals Management Office (SAMO).
2.2. The Inventory Team shall be assisted by a team of legal researchers that
have been or may be hired by the Governance in Justice Programme
(GOJUST Programme), which is the Department's development partner in
implementing this decongestion project, pursuant to mutually agreed Terms
of Reference.
2.3. The Inventory Team and the team of legal researchers shall classify and tag
case records, as follows:
2.3.1. Red for case records with draft resolutions that outrightly dismiss
the petition pursuant to the applicable provisions of Department
Circular No. 70 dated 3 July 2000.
2.3.2. Green for case records with draft resolutions that affirm, reverse,
and/or otherwise modify the findings and recommendations of the
investigating prosecutor or prosecution office concerned.
2.3.3. Yellow for case records without draft resolutions.
2.4. Upon classification of the case records, legal researchers shall fill up the
corresponding forms ^ and attach the same to the corresponding case
folders.
2.5. The Inventory Team shall enter the classification made in its database.
See attached forms.

3. WRITESHOPS
3.1. The five (5)-day writeshops, wherein authorized Undersecretaries and
Assistant Secretaries, together with a sufficient number of Prosecutors of
the Prosecution Staff, shall resolve the Petitions for Review and Cases on
Automatic Review subject of these Guidelines, shall be conducted
according to a rotation schedule to be determined by the Chairman of the
Steering Committee.
3.2. The GOJUST Programme shall provide the venue, meals, and other
necessary resources for these writeshops.
3.3. The Steering Committee, upon the recommendation of the Technical
Working Group (TWG) and Secretariat created under the D.O. No. 286
dated 30 May 2019, as amended, shall ensure that at least one team
conducts a writeshop weekly at the venue that may be provided by the
GOJUST Programme. The TWG and Secretariat shall likewise ensure that
the corresponding office orders authorizing the attendance of writeshop
team members shall be issued accordingly.
3.4. During a team's scheduled writeshop week, the Inventory Team shall
randomly assign case records to the writeshop team's prosecutors for their
evaluation of the merits of the case and the drafting of the appropriate
resolution. It shall ensure that; (i) the assignment of cases is equitable; (ii)
the case assignment and receipt of records are entered in its database; and
(iii) such assignment of cases remain confidential.
The undersecretary or assistant secretary, as the writeshop team leader,
shall review and approve resolutions drafted by the prosecutors. The
approved resolutions, together with the case records, shall be sent to the
Mailing Team created under D.O. No. 286 dated 30 May 2019, as amended,
and shall be processed in accordance with D.C. No. 034 dated 31 August
2018.
4 AUTHORITY TO RESOLVE CASES
4.1. If, in the course of the evaluation of a petition for review that was initially
tagged as red, it is determined that the same is not for outright dismissal
and should be resolved on the merits, the writeshop team member to whom
the case was assigned may proceed to draft the corresponding resolution.
4.2. If, in the course of the evaluation of a petition for review that was initially
tagged as green, it is determined that the same should be dismissed outright
under D.C. No. 70 dated 3 July 2000, or that the same should be resolved
instead by the appropriate Office of the Regional Prosecutor pursuant to
D C. No. 70-A dated 10 July 2000, the writeshop team member to whom the
case was assigned may proceed to draft the corresponding resolution.

4 3 In both cases, the Undersecretary or Assistant Secretary, as the
corresponding writeshop team leader, shall be authorized to approve for
and on behalf of the Secretary of Justice the subject resolution.
5. RECORDING AND TRANSMITTAL OF APPROVED RESOLUTIONS
5.1. The writeshop team shall transmit sufficient copies of signed resolutions
together with a mailing list, return cards, as well as the case records of
resolved and recorded cases, to the Mailing Team of the Office of the
Secretary Appeals Management Office.
6. PROMULGATION AND MAILING
6 1 After receipt of the approved resolution, mailing list, return cards, and the
complete case records from the writeshop team, the Mailing Team shall
encode the necessary information in the Petition for Review Inform^ic^
System pursuant to Paragraph 11 and sub-paragraphs 11.1 to 11.3 of D C.
No. 034 dated 31 August 2018 (Promulgation and Mailing).
7. MOTIONS FOR RECONSIDERATION
7.1. Paragraph 12 and sub-paragraphs 12.1 to 12.6 of D.G. No. 034 dated 31
August 2018 (Motions for Reconsideration) shall be applicable to motions
for reconsideration emanating from resolutions promulgated pursuant to this
Circular.
8. This Circular shall take effect immediately.
