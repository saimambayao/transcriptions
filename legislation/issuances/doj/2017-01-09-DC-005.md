Among the State policies provided for under Section 2 of R.A. No. 9211,
otherwise known as the Tobacco Regulation Act of 2003, is the policy to protect the
populace from hazardous products and promote the right to health and instil health
consciousness among them, among others.
For which purpose the government shall regulate the use of tobacco products
in order to promote a healthful environment and protect the citizens from the hazards
of tobacco smoke.
Accordingly, Section 5 thereof imposes a smoking ban in public places, which
includes under subsection (e) public facilities such as the premises of the Department
of Justice, except for separate smoking areas.
Moreover, Section 6 thereof provides for the designation of smoking and non-
smoking areas in public workplaces, among others.
Accordingly, pursuant to the provisions of the said Act and its Implementing
Rules and Regulations, a 100% NO SMOKING POLICY is hereby adopted within
the facilities of the Department of Justice, except for the area near the DOJ fence in
the parking spaces fronting Padre Faura Street, which is designated as a smoking
area. For this purpose, refer to Annex 'A' hereof.
Except for the designated smoking area, smoking shall be absolutely prohibited
in the Department. Smoking outside the designated area shall constitute as Conduct
Prejudicial to the Best Interest of the Service, which is a grave offense, as prescribed
under Section 46(13)(8) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the
Civil Service, punishable as follows:
(cid:9)
First Offense(cid:9) - Suspension of six (6) months and one (1)
day to one (1) year
(cid:9)
Second Offense - Dismissal from the service

For this purpose, Security Personnel of this Department are hereby DIRECTED
to inform the concerned official or employee that he or she is in violation of this section
and that the instance of his or her violation shall be recorded. The Security Personnel
shall record all violations of this section and include the same in their progress report
to the Administrative Service. The Administrative Service shall transmit a report of
violations of the foregoing to the Technical Staff for appropriate disciplinary action, if
any. The Security Personnel are also further DIRECTED to perform roving or rounds
in the Department to ensure compliance with this Circular.
Any Security Personnel of this Department who fails to record any instance of
violation of this Circular shall be liable for simple neglect of duty, which is a less grave
offense, as prescribed under Section 46(D)(1) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on
Administrative Cases in the Civil Service, punishable as follows:
(cid:9)
First Offense Suspension of one (1) month and one (1)
day to six (6) months
(cid:9)
Second Offense - Dismissal from the service
Provided, that in case the element of corruption is present, or that the duty was
neglected in exchange for valuable consideration, the person concerned shall be liable
for gross neglect of duty, which is a grave offense, as prescribed under Section
46(A)(2) of Rule 10 of the Revised Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service,
punishable with dismissal from the service.
Provided further, that in case any officer in the Department is responsible for
preparing a progress report or report of violations, or is responsible for transmitting
the same to an appropriate office for appropriate action, and he or she fails to do so,
he or she shall likewise be liable for simple neglect of duty or gross neglect of duty,
as the case may be.
Provided further, that in case the person involved is from the security agency
contracted by the Department, he or she shall be immediately removed from the
Department's security detail.
Nothing in this Circular shall preclude the filing of appropriate criminal cases as
provided under Section 32 of the law, or other applicable administrative, civil or
criminal liability against the erring official or employee.
For strict compliance.
