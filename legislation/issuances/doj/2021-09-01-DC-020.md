In view ofthe approval by President Rodrigo R. Duterte ofthe recommendation
ofthe Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases
(IATF-MEID) to extend the Modified Enhanced Community Quarantine (MECQ) with
additional restrictions in the National Capital Region (NCR), and pursuant to the
Omnibus Guidelines on the Implementation of Community Quarantine in the
Philippines, as amended, Department ofJustice Offices and City Prosecution Offices
in the NCR shall remain fully operational during the period 01 to 07 September 2021,
through an onsite skeletal workforce of at least twenty percent (20%) and the rest
under a work-from-home arrangement.
I. Skeletal Workforce on Rotation Basis
All officials, directors, and heads ofoffices/units/programs are hereby directed
to oversee and account for their respective personnel at all times.
The heads of offices/units/programs are further directed to submit to Director
IV Liza B. De Leon, Administrative Service, the list of personnel who will
physically report for work during the MECQ period on rotation basis.
II. Frontline Services
All officials, directors, and heads ofoffices/units/programs are hereby reminded
that frontline services shall be fully operational during the period of MECQ.
Accordingly, all heads ofoffices shall ensure the availability ofpersonnel at the
Frontline Service who will receive documents, mail, and other papers.
Ill. Work-From-Home Arrangement
All officials, directors, and heads ofoffices/units/programs shall strictly observe
the relevant rules and guidelines of the Civil Service Commission (CSC),

particularly CSC Memorandum Circular No. 18, s. 2020,1 on the implementation
ofwork-from-home and/or other alternative work arrangements.
Department personnel under work-from-home arrangements are directed to

IV. Inquest Proceedings
Prosecutors and their support staff shall continue to conduct inquest
proceedings online and/or in person, and attend to urgent matters that require
immediate action, as may be determined bythe heads ofthe prosecution offices
concerned. Accordingly, all prosecution offices are directed to ensure the
availability at all times of inquest prosecutors who shall be on-call for the
duration of the MECQ. The conduct of inquest proceedings shall proceed in
accordance with the Rules of Court and existing rules and regulations of the
Department.
Further, in accordance with the policies of the Department of Health on the
managementand prevention ofCOVID-19 in the workplace and in orderto reduce the
transmission of COVID-19, all personnel are hereby reminded to strictly observe the
following health protocols as the MECQ remains in effect:
I. Safe Workplace
a. Before entering the workplace, wearface masks and face shields. Ifworking
in a shared work space or room, face masks should be worn at all times.
b. Stay in your own workstations whenever possible. Limit movements
between buildings and offices.
c. Clean frequently touched surfaces in personal workspaces.
d. Adequate ventilation should be strictly enforced inside the workplace.
Natural air flow exchange (opening windows, opening doors, turning off air
conditioning units to reduce air recirculation) is highly encouraged.
II. Reporting of COVID-19 Symptoms and Cases
a. Any personnel who is experiencing any symptom ofCOVID-19 should self
isolate or stay at home and should inform his/her supervisor immediately.
b. Any personnel who tests positive for COVID-19 should immediately report
such fact to Director IV Liza B. De Leon, Administrative Service for
monitoring and the conduct of contact tracing, in accordance with relevant
rules and regulations.

Ill. Non-DOJ Personnel and Visitors
c. Non-DOJ personnel/visitors shall not be allowed to enter DOJ
buildings/offices. All transactions with non-DOJ personnel/visitors shall be
conducted at the Frontline Service or at the DOJ gate.
IV. Meetings and Gatherings
a. Any social gathering that involves close and prolonged contact among
participants shall be prohibited. Accordingly, in-person meetings/gatherings
shall not be allowed, except when absolutely necessary and with the
approval ofthe undersigned.
b. Communal eating shall be strictly prohibited.
V. Shuttle Services
a. Minimum public health standards should be enforced in the shuttle services,
i.e., use offace shields and face masks, observance of physical distancing,
and frequent disinfection.
b. Employees inside the vehicles should avoid talking with each other, making
or taking phone calls, eating, and removing their face masks and shields.
c. Adequate ventilation should be strictly enforced inside the shuttle service.
The opening ofwindows, with at least three (3) inches of opening, while in
transit should be practiced whenever possible.
This Circular shall take effect immediately and shall remain in force for the
duration of the MECQ, subject to any such extension or amendment as may be
declared or issued by the IATF-MEID or any other competent authority.
