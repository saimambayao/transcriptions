WHEREAS,t he Secretary of Justice, pursuant to his authority under Administrative Order
No. 142, s. 1994, issued Department Circular (D.C.) 041 on 14 August 2017 which made
available the Temporary Visitor's Visa Upon Arrival( TV-VUA) to nationals of the People's
Republic of China (PROC);
WHEREAS, the TV-VUA has encouraged tourism arrivals which translate to tangible
economic benefits for the country, but risks relating to its misuse, including its use by
nationals intending to ultimately obtain work in the country, need to be addressed;
WHEREAS, promoting tourism must be balanced with other public interests, including the
guarantee of preference for work for Filipinos;
WHEREAS, simplifying procedures related to the processing of TV-VUAs is necessary to
ensure greater efficiency and accountability;
WHEREFORE, in furtherance of the foregoing, D.C. No. 041 is hereby amended to read
as follows:
"Section 1. Coverage. This Circular shall govern the issuance of TV-VUA to qualified
nationals of the People's Republic of China^ (PROC) who visit the Philippines for
temporary and short stays not exceeding thirty (30) days at any given time.
Section 2. Eiigibiiity and Quaiifications. (a) PROC nationals falling under any of the
following categories are eligible to avail of the TV-VUA:
1. Foreign Investors and businessmen duly endorsed by the Board of
Investments (BOI), Philippine Retirement Authority (PRA), Subic Bay
Metropolitan Authority (SBMA), Cagayan Economic Zone Authority (CE2A),
^ Including Chinese nationals from Taiw^, as well as the Special Administrative Regions of Hong Kong
and Macau.

2. Athletes and delegates to sports competitions, conventions, or exhibitions

1. If he or she is not included in the Bl Blacklist or the subject of an existing

3. Upon payment of all the appropriate fees.
Section 3. EffectMty and Restrictions,( a) A TV-VUA shall be effective for a maximum
authorized stay of thirty (30) days from the applicant's date of arrival in the Philippines,
and shall automatically expire either:

1. at the end of this period; or
2. even before such period, upon its holder's departure from the Philippines.
(b) No extension or renewal of a TV-VUA shall be allowed. Moreover, a holder of a
TV-VUA shall be automatically disqualified from applying for: (i) the conversion thereof to
any other visa; (ii) change of status to any other non-immigrant or immigrant without first
departing from the Philippines; or (iii) any permit, including work or study permits, issued
by the Bl or the Department of Labor and Employment.
Section 4. Condrtions and Limitations, (a) Notwithstanding the validity of a TV-VUA
Order, a PROG national may be excluded from entering the Philippines under any of the
grounds provided by law, immigration regulations, or this Circular. Without limiting the
foregoing, entry may be denied under any of the following circumstances:
1. There exist other ground/s to deny entry pursuant to OA No. 613 or
immigration regulations, including Bl Memorandum Order No. ADD 001-
005; and
2. Non-payment of VUA fees prior to the subject's arrival in the Philippines.
(b) All TV-VUA holders, including members of a tour group, must arrive at and depart
from the Philippines in accordance with the terms specified in the Order.
(c) After issuance of a VUA Order, a change of tour itinerary may only be permitted
for justifiable reasons and after being coordinated with the Office of the Immigration
Commissioner at least one (1) day prior to the scheduled arrival and subject to payment
of appropriate fees.
(d) No extension of stay shall be allowed except in cases of force majeure affecting,
or medical emergency concerning, the TV-VUA holder; Provided, that an extension of
stay, when authorized, shall only be for the duration of the force majeure or medical
emergency.

Section 11. Duties of the Port Operations Division (POD), Seaport Operations
Section (SOS),( a) The POD, through the Special Operations Communications Unit

(c) The POD shall submit to the Office of the Commissioner a monthly report of all

Section 13. Violations and Sanctions,( a) A TV-VUA holder who arrives earlier/later
and/or in a flight or vessel different from that specified in the VUA Order shall be
automatically excluded as an improperly documented alien under Section 29(a)(17) of OA
No. 613.
(b) A TV-VUA holder who changes a tour itinerary without obtaining the prior approval
of the Immigration Commissioner, stays beyond the period specified in the Order, or
othenwise violates any condition of the TV-VUA shall be subject to deportation pursuant
to Section 37(a)(7) of CA No. 613.
(c) Any TV-VUA holder who commits any of the foregoing infractions shall be
proceeded against accordingly and, in addition, shall be automatically included in the Bl
Blacklist. Request for lifting of a blacklist order may only be given due course twelve (12)
months from date of actual departure unless the Secretary of Justice, upon the
recommendation of the Immigration Commissioner, waives this period for humanitarian,
economic, political, or other special considerations.
(d) The Bl and DoT-accredited tour operator shall be liable for administrative fines and
such other sanctions as may be imposed by the Bl for violation of any immigration law,
rule, or regulation by a TV-VUA holder/member of its tour group. To further ensure
compliance with the VUA Order and as a guarantee for its assumption of liability, a Bl and
DoT-accredited tour operator shall be required to post a non-interest earning cash bond
in such amount as may deemed appropriate by the Commissioner, and approved by the
Secretary of Justice, which amount shall not be less than Five Hundred Thousand Pesos
(Php 500,000.00).
(e) In addition to the foregoing, the following sanctions may be imposed by the Bl for
any violation of the terms and conditions as stated in the application, grant/issuance
and/or implementation of the VUA Order under this Circular, to wit:
1. Exclusion or deportation of any group tour member or individual traveler
without prejudice to the imposition of other applicable penalties under CA
No. 613, other Philippine laws, and existing Bl rules and regulations;
2. Inclusion in the Blacklist Order of the responsible officers of the concemed
tour operator;
3. Imposition of administrative penalties, including but not limited to forfeiture
of cash bond and/or loss of accreditation, upon a Bl and DoT-accredited
tour operator for failure to comply with, and/or for violating the terms and
conditions of, its Affidavit of Undertaking.
