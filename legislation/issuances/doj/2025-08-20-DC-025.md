SUBJECT: INVITATION TO APPLY FOR VACANCIES IN THE UNITED NATIONS
SPECIAL PROCEDURES MANDATE-HOLDERS (UNSPMH) SYSTEM
Dear Secretary Remulla:
We respectfully wish to share the following information with your agency regarding
the ongoing call for applications to the UNSPMH system.
The Secretariat of the Human Rights Council (HRC) is currently accepting
applications for 16 mandate-holder positions. The selection and confirmation of
appointees to the UNSPMH are expected to be completed during the sixty-first session of
the HRC, which is provisionally scheduled to take place between 23 February and 3 April
2026
The vacancies are as follows:
• Expert Mechanism on the Right to Development – Four members: representing
African States, Asia-Pacific States, Eastern European States, and Western
European and Other States;
• Expert Mechanism on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples – Two members:
representing Asia and the Arctic;
• Independent Expert on the enjoyment of all human rights by older persons;
• Independent Expert on the situation of human rights in Somalia;
• Special Rapporteur on adequate housing as a component of the right to an
adequate standard of living, and on the right to non-discrimination in this
context;
• Special Rapporteur on contemporary forms of slavery, including its causes and
consequences;
• Special Rapporteur on extreme poverty and human rights;
• Special Rapporteur on the negative impact of unilateral coercive measures on
the enjoyment of human rights;
• Special Rapporteur on the right to food;
• Special Rapporteur on the sale, sexual exploitation, and sexual abuse of
children;
• Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights defenders; and
Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights in Myanmar.
•
Please note that some of these positions may only be filled if the HRC decides to
extend the corresponding mandates.

Applicants are expected to possess a well-established record of professional
experience and recognized expertise in human rights, particularly in relation to the
thematic or country-specific focus of the mandate to which they are applying. Mandate
holders serve in their personal capacity and are not considered United Nations (UN) staff
members. While the role is not remunerated, travel-related expenses may be reimbursed
in accordance with applicable UN rules and regulations when undertaking official
mISSIons
Given the scope and significance of the role, selected mandate holders will be
required to dedicate substantial time and effort to the effective discharge of their
responsibilities. These may include participation in sessions of the HRC and the UN
General Assembly; conducting country visits; preparing thematic and country-specific
reports; convening stakeholder consultations; engaging with governments and civil
society; responding to allegations of human rights violations; and advising States and
other relevant actors on issues within the scope of the mandate. In this regard , candidates
should be in a position to commit approximately four to six months per year, depending
on the workload associated with the mandate.
Interested individuals must complete both of the following steps by 28 August
2025 at 12:00 noon (Geneva time). Early submissions are highly encouraged.
1. Complete the online survey: https://survev.ohchr.org/337342
2. Submit a mandate-specific Word application form:
Application instructions and downloadable forms are available at:
https://www.ohchr.org/en/hr-bodies/hrc/sp/hrc61
For further information and guidance on the application process, applicants may
refer to the following resources:
• Application guidelines: https://www.ohchr.org/en/hr-bodies/hrc/sp/application-
guidelines
• Information on the selection process and frequently asked questions:
https://www.ohchr.org/en/hr-bodies/hrc/sp/basic-information-selection-
independent-experts
For technical assistance, the HRC Secretariat may be reached at: ohchr-
