WHEREAS, pursuant to Section 5, Chapter 2, Book IV of Executive Order (E.O.) No.
292, otherwise known as the Administrative Code of 1987, the Secretary of Justice
shall exercise disciplinary powers over officers and employees in the Department, in
accordance with law, including their investigation and the designation of a committee
or officer to conduct such investigation;
WHEREAS, corollary thereto, Section 47(2) and (3), Chapter 6, Title I-A, Book V of
E.O. No. 292 provides that the Secretaries and heads of agencies shall have
jurisdiction to investigate and decide matters involving disciplinary action against
officers and employees under their jurisdiction. Such decisions shall be final in case
the penalty imposed is suspension for not more than thirty (30) days or fine in an
amount not exceeding thirty (30) days' salary;
WHEREAS, Republic Act (R.A.) No. 10071, otherwise known as the National
Prosecution Service Act, vests the Secretary of Justice with the power of control and
supervision over the National Prosecution Service (NPS) which is composed of the
prosecution staff in the Office of the Secretary and such number of regional
prosecution offices, provincial prosecution offices, and city prosecution offices as
provided by law;
WHEREAS, Department Order (D.O.) No. 305 dated 18 May 2023 constituted the
Prosecution Integrity Board (PIB) which shall have the jurisdiction to take cognizance
of all administrative complaints pertaining to prosecutors and prosecution attorneys of
the NPS, and to investigate and recommended to the Secretary of Justice appropriate
actions in accordance with pertinent Civil Servce laws, rules and regulations, such as
but not limited to: the 2017 Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service (2017)
W\CCS), the Deparment’s rules and regulations, and the Codes of Codnuct for
Prosecutors and Support Staff; \
NOW, THEREFORE, in the interest of the service and pursuant to existing laws, rules
and regulations, and in order to rationalize the procedure in the handling of
administrative complaints, the following Rules are hereby adopted:
Section 1. Title. - These Rules shall be known as 2023 Rules of Procedure of the
ProsecutIon IntegrIty Board (PIB) of the Department Justice (DOJ).

Section 2, Coverage. - These Rules shall cover all prosecutors who are presidential
appointees (Prosecutor I to Prosecutor V) and prosecution attorneys who are
appointed by the Secretary of Justice. These shall likewise apply to any employee of
the Department, acting in conspiracy with the prosecutor charged under these Rules.
Section 3. Interpretation and Construction. - These Rules shall be liberally
construed to promote the objective of ascertaining the truth through a just, speedy,
simple, non-technical and inexpensive proceeding. Technical rules on evidence and
procedure shall not be binding in these proceedings.
Section 4. Form of Complaint. - A complaint may be initiated by the Secretary of
Justice, by the head of office of the concerned prosecutor, or by any other person.
The complaint shall be written in clear, simple and concise language that is sufficient
to inform the prosecutor of the acts and/or omissions complained of to enable him/her
to prepare his/her defense.
It shall be subscribed and sworn to by the complainant or the head of office and must
contain the following:
a. Full name and address of the complainant;
b. Full name and address of the person complained of, or any appellation by
which he/she is known, as well as his/her position and place of station. For
this purpose, the office address of the person complained of will suffice;
c. Statement of ultimate, relevant and material facts relating to the acts or
omissions complained of;
d. Certification of non-forum shopping;
e. Specific reference to the laws, rules and regulations, codes or policies
violated, as far as practicable; and
f
Affidavits of witnesses and other evidence, if any.
Section 5. Treatment of Anonymous Complaints. - Anonymous complaints shall
only be acted upon when the allegations contained therein are accompanied with
verifiable facts or documentary evidence to support the same and may be a basis to
initiate a fact-finding investigation by the case evaluator to determine the truth or falsity
thereof
Section 6. Where to File Complaint. - The complaint shall be addressed to the PIB
at the Office of the Secretary and may be filed at any regional, provincial or city office
of the Department through personal or postal service, or by electronic mail to the
official e-mail address of the concerned prosecution office.
Any official or personnel of the Department who receives a complaint shall be under
obligation to treat the same with utmost confidentiality. It shall be incumbent upon the

Section 8. Assignment and Evaluation. – Within seven (7) working days upon
receipt of the complaint, the PIB Executive Director shall assign the same to a Case
evaluator. Within ten (10) working days from assignment of the complaint, the Case
evaluator shall assess the same and:
a If the complaint is insufficient in form, send a notice of compliance to the
complainant, giving the latter a period of ten (10) days from receipt thereof
to execute the proper form. Failure to comply despite due notice shall
cause the dismissal of the complaint, without prejudice to treating the
same as an anonymous complaint under Section 5 above;
b. If the complaint insufficient in substance, draft an Evaluation Report
recommending dismissal thereof for the consideration of the PIB; or
c, if the complaint sufficient in form and substance, issue an Order requiring
the respondent to submit an answer to the Complaint within ten (10) days
from receipt thereof. No motion for extension to file answer shall be
allowed, except for meritorious grounds.

Such Order shall be served on the respondent in such manner as the case

Section 9. Assessment of Complaint. - After the receipt of the answer or lapse of
the period given to the respondent to submit the same, the case evaluator shall, within
thirty (30) days, prepare a Memorandum Report addressed to the Secretary, with any
of the following recommendations:
a. Dismissal of the complaint;
b. Filing of a formal charge against the respondent and with a draft formal
charge for recommending approval of the PIB, and approval of the
Secretary; or
c. Filing of a formal charge with issuance of preventive suspension against
the respondent, with a draft formal hharge for the recommending approval
of the PIB, and approval of the Secretary.
The Memorandum Report to the Secretary of Justice shall include: a) a summary of
the facts; b) the evaluation and legal basis for the dismissal of the complaint or the
issuance of a formal charge; c) if applicable, the proposed formal charge for the
signature of the Secretary; and d) if warranted, the proposed issuance of preventive
suspension for the signature of the Secretary.
Section 10. Action of the PIB. - The PIB shall act on the Memorandum Report within
fifteen (15) days from its receipt, either approving or disapproving the recommendation
of the case evaluator.
Thereafter, the PIB shall elevate all its actions, with the corresponding action
document/s, to the Secretary of Justice for final approval.
Section 11. Formal Charge. – After a finding of prima facie case, the Secretary, as
the disciplining authory shall formally charge the prosecutor complained of, who shall
now be called respondent. The formal charge against the respondent shall contain a
specification of the charge and identification of the acts and/or omissions complained
of, a brief statement of material or relevant facts, and the legal basis for the issuance
of the formal charge.
An administrative case against the respondent is deemed pending once the formal
charge is issued.
Section 12. Preventive Suspension. - When a complaint is given due course
pursuant to Section 11, the respondent may be placed under preventive suspension
by the Secretary of Justice for a period of not more than ninety (90) days, without pay,
pending the termination of the proceedings, based on the following grounds:
a. The acts or offenses complained of involve dishonesty, oppression, grave
misconduct, gross neglect of duty, or other offenses punishable by
dismissal from the service: and

Section 15. Action of Case Officer/s if No Answer is Filed. - Failure of the
respondent to submit an answer within the fifteen (15)-day reglementary period

Section. 16. Formal Charge Hearing. - A hearing shall only be conducted if justified
and requested by the respondent in his/her answer, or if there are facts and issues to
be clarified from either or both of the parties or from their respective witness/es, as
may be determined by the case officer/s. If the respondent is charged for a case that
would have a penalty of dismissal from the service, a hearing shall be mandatory.
The direct testimonial evidence for the complainant, the respondent, and their
respective witnesses, if any, shall consist of the affidavits submitted, together with the
complaint or answer, as the case may be. No additional evidence shall be presented
during the formal hearing unless the same was unavailable at the time of filing.
Section 17. Conduct of Hearing. - The parties may conduct cross-examination, re-
direct and re-cross examination, and avail of the service of counsel during the hearing.
The hearing shall be conducted within fifteen (15) days from the filing of the answer or
reply, if one has been filed, and shall be completed within thirty (30) days.
The case officer/s shall submit his/her or their findings and recommendations, together
with the case records and appropriate action documents, to the PIB within fifteen (15)
days from the termination of the hearing. The findings and recommendations shall be
contained in a Memorandum Report, setting forth clearly and distinctly the facts of the
case, the arguments of the parties, the issues, the facts and law upon which the
findings, conclusions and recommendations are based, for the consideration of the
PIB
Section 18. Withdrawal of Complaint. - The withdrawal of the complaint shall not
result in its automatic dismissal or prevent the PIB from continuing to act thereon.
Section 19. PIB Review and Recommendation. - The PIB shall evaluate and review
the findings and recommendations contained in the submitted Memorandum Report
of the case officer/s and within fifteen (15) days from receipt thereof, shall approve or
disapprove the recommendation therein or submit its own recommendation, with the
draft Decision to the Secretary of Justice, for his consideration.
Section 20. Jurisdiction of the PIB. - While recognizing the supervisory powers of
the heads of regional, provincial and city prosecution offices over their personnel under
R.A. 10071, such powers are still under the control and supervision of the Secretary
of Justice, who, through the PIB, shall have the authority to take cognizance and
assume jurisdiction of matters falling under the Board’s mandate, in the exigency of
service
Section 21. Transitory Provisions. - Complaints filed and reports submitted against
prosecutors prior to the effectivity of these Rules and which have not been evaluated,
or the preliminary investigations of which have not commenced, shall be governed by
these Rules.
Complaints filed and reports submitted against members of support staff prior to the
effectivity of these Rules and which have not been evaluated, or the preliminary
investigations of which have already commenced, shall be governed by the PIB
subject to the rules of procedure of the Internal Affairs Unit (IAU).
