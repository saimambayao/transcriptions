WHEREAS, the Department of Justice (DO)) issued Department Circular
No. 001 (DC 001) dated 05 January 2015, with the subject "Procedure for
Commencing Administrative Proceedings Against Bureau of Immigration

more particularly the 2017 RACCS, the conduct of administrative

RULE I
APPLICABILITY AND CONSTRUCTION
SECTION 1. Applicability. These rules and procedure (Rules) shall
apply to all administrative disciplinary proceedings within the DOJ and BI
against officials and employees of the BI, who are not Presidential
appointees.
SECTION 2. Construction. These Rules shall be liberally construed
in order to promote just, speedy and inexpensive disposition of
administrative disciplinary proceedings without violating administrative due
process and the substantive rights of the parties.
Administrative disciplinary proceedings shall be conducted without
strict recourse to technical rules of procedure and evidence applicable to
judicial proceedings.
RULE II
COMMENCEMENT OF
ADMINISTRATIVE DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDINGS
SECTION 3. Commencement of Administrative Disciplinary
Proceedings. Pursuant to Section 48(1), Chapter 7, Subtitle A, Title I,
Book V of the Administrative Code, and Section 10, Rule 3 of the 2017
RACCS, administrative disciplinary proceedings under these Rules may be
commenced by any of the following:
Secretary, or his/her representative expressly authorized/designated
to commence administrative disciplinary proceedings subsequent to
the effectivity of these Rules (Authorized Representative);
Commissioner of the Bureau of Immigration (Commissioner); or
Any other person, upon valid complaint.
SECTION 4. Administrative(cid:9) Disciplinary(cid:9) Proceedings
Commenced Motu Proprio by the Secretary, his/her Authorized
Representative, or Commissioner.
a. The Secretary, his/her Authorized Representative, or the
Commissioner may commence motu proprio administrative
disciplinary proceedings under these Rules by issuing a Show Cause
Order (SCO) pursuant to Section 14, Rule IV hereof.
The SCO shall require the concerned BI official or employee to submit,
within a period of five (5) days from receipt thereof, his/her counter-
affidavit or sworn comment/explanation as to why he/she should not

intelligently prepare his/her defense.
RULE III
COMPLAINT
SECTION 6. Requisites of a Valid Complaint. The complaint
mentioned in Section 5 hereof must be in writing, duly subscribed and
sworn to by the complainant/s, and contain the following:
the full name and address, and if available, electronic mail (e-mail)
address and contact number, of the complainant;
the full name and address, and if available, e-mail address and
contact number, of complainant's counsel, if any;
the full name, position, office, and address which may be the office
address of the BI official/s and/or employee/s complained of;
a narration of the relevant and material facts which shows the acts
or omissions allegedly committed;

certified true copies of documentary evidence and affidavits of
complainant's witnesses, if any; and
a certification or statement of non-forum shopping.
Should there be more than one BI official or employee complained of,
the complainant/s must submit such number of copies of the complaint
corresponding to the number of persons complained of.
SECTION 7. Where to File Complaint.
The sworn written complaint may be filed with either the Office of
the Secretary or Office of the Commissioner.
The sworn written complaint may likewise be filed before the Board
of Discipline (BOD) of the BI in its capacity as the primary
investigating body of the D03 and BI with respect to administrative
disciplinary proceedings.
SECTION 8. Action on the Complaint. In complaints filed before the
Offices of the Secretary or Commissioner, both shall have the discretion to
either:
motu proprio, determine the sufficiency in form and substance of
the complaint in accordance with Section 6 hereof; or
forward the complaint to the BOD within a period of three (3) days
from receipt thereof, for such determination of sufficiency.
In case the complaint is determined to be non-compliant with any of
the requisites of a valid complaint under Section 6 hereof, the same may
be dismissed by the office which made such determination, without
prejudice to the refiling of the complaint and compliance with or submission
of the lacking requirements.
Such order of dismissal shall be sent by mail and/or e-mail to the
complainant's given or last known address, copy furnished the Secretary,
whenever applicable.
On the other hand, if the complaint is determined to be sufficient in
form and substance, the same shall be referred for preliminary
investigation in accordance with Rule IV hereof.
SECTION 9. Anonymous Complaint. No anonymous complaint
shall be entertained unless the act complained of is of public knowledge or
the allegations can be verified or supported by documentary or direct
evidence.

warrant the issuance of formal charge against any BI official or employee.

Secretary and Commissioner, the decision of the Secretary shall prevail.

SECTION 16. Compliance with the SCO or NTE. Within five (5)
days from receipt of the SCO/NTE, the BI official or employee shall submit
his/her subscribed and sworn counter-affidavit/comment/explanation,
together with affidavits of his/her witnesses and other supporting
documents relied upon for his/her defense.

If the BI official or employee fails or refuses to submit his/her
subscribed and sworn counter-affidavit/comment/explanation within the
period given in the SCO/NTE, he/she shall be considered to have waived
his/her right to submit the same and the preliminary investigation shall
proceed based on available records.
SECTION 17. Duration of the Preliminary Investigation. The
preliminary investigation shall commence within a period of five (5) days
from receipt by the BOD or the duly-designated preliminary investigating
body of the: a.) complaint which has been determined to be sufficient in
form and substance; or b.) SCO, together with the counter-affidavit or
sworn comment/explanation, if any, and other pertinent records, whichever
is applicable.
The preliminary investigation shall be terminated within a period of
twenty (20) days from commencement.
In meritorious cases however, such periods may be extended upon
approval by the Secretary.

RULE V
FORMAL CHARGE AND
ORDER OF PREVENTIVE SUSPENSION
SECTION 20. Issuance and Contents of the Formal Charge.
After a finding of prima facie case, the Secretary shall formally charge the
BI official or employee, who shall now be called as respondent.
The formal charge shall contain the following:
specification of the charge;
brief statement of material or relevant facts, which may be
accompanied by certified true copies of the documentary evidence
and sworn statements covering the testimony of witnesses, if any;
directive to answer the charge in writing, under oath, in not less
than three (3) days but not more than ten (10) days from receipt
thereof;
an advice for the respondent to indicate in the answer whether or
not he/she elects for the conduct of a formal investigation; and
notice that respondent may opt to be assisted by a counsel of
his/her choice.
SECTION 21. Incomplete Attachments of the Formal Charge.
If the respondent receives a Formal Charge with incomplete attachments
mentioned in paragraph b, Section 20, Rule V, the respondent may request
for the lacking documents within ten (10) days from receipt of the Formal
Charge. The period of the respondent to file his/her answer under Section
23, Rule VI will not run until the requested documents or a letter-reply has
been received by the respondent.
SECTION 22. Preventive Suspension. Upon issuance of the
Formal Charge or anytime thereafter, the Secretary, motu proprio or upon
motion, may issue an order placing respondent on preventive suspension
for a maximum period of ninety (90) days.
RULE VI
ANSWER
SECTION 23. Requisites of an Answer. The answer, which is
in writing and under oath, shall be specific and shall contain material facts
and applicable laws, including original or certified copies of documentary
evidence, sworn statements covering testimonies of witnesses, if any.

SECTION 24. Prohibited Pleadings. Requests for clarification, bills
of particulars, motions to dismiss, motions to quash, motions for
reconsideration and motion for extension of time to file answer shall not be
entertained. Pleadings and motions of this nature shall be noted without
action and attached to the records of the case.
SECTION 25.(cid:9) Failure to File an Answer. If the respondent fails
or refuses to file an answer within the period given in the Formal Charge,
the respondent shall be considered to have waived his/her right to submit
the same and the case shall be decided based on available records.
SECTION 26. Action on the Answer. When the Secretary
determines that the answer sufficiently exculpates the respondent from the
charge against him/her, the case shall be dismissed. Otherwise, the
investigation shall proceed.
RULE VII
FORMAL INVESTIGATION
SECTION 27. Formal Investigation; When Conducted. A formal
investigation shall be conducted where the merits of the case cannot be
decided judiciously without conducting such investigation, or when the
respondent elects to have one.
Such formal investigation shall be held not earlier than five (5) days
or later than ten (10) days from receipt of the respondent's answer or upon
the expiration of the period to file the same without the respondent having
filed an answer.
SECTION 28. Formal Investigation; When Not Necessary. If the
respondent did not elect for the conduct of a formal investigation in his/her
answer, or when the Secretary determines that the merits of the case can
be judiciously decided without conducting such investigation:
An order shall be issued directing the parties to file their respective
Position Papers/Memoranda, which may be accompanied by certified
true copies of their documentary evidence, and sworn statements of
their witnesses, within a period of thirty (30) days from receipt of
such order.
Upon receipt of the parties' Position Papers/Memoranda, if any, or
upon the expiration of the period to file the same, the case shall be
submitted for resolution, and decided based on the documents and
evidence on record.

SECTION 29. Submission of Position Paper/Memorandum.
Notwithstanding the election by respondent and/or conduct of formal
investigation, the parties may, based on their express and/or mutual
consent, move for the submission of position paper/memorandum and
consider the investigation submitted for decision without any need for
further hearings.
SECTION 30. Hearing Officers/Panel. The Technical Staff of the
DOJ shall have the authority to conduct and preside over any and all formal
investigations under these Rules without need of another issuance, unless
the Secretary designates another personnel or office within the DOJ or any
of its attached agencies.
SECTION 31. Power to Issue Subpoena. The Technical Staff or
duly-designated hearing officer/panel shall have the power to issue
subpoena ad testificandum to compel the attendance of witnesses and
subpoena duces tecum to compel the production of documents or things.
SECTION 32. Prosecuting Attorneys. The Secretary shall designate
and constitute a pool of lawyer-personnel within the DOJ and/or any of its
attached agencies who will have the authority to act as prosecuting
attorneys in any and all formal investigations under these Rules, unless the
Secretary decides to designate other personnel or office within the DOJ or
any of its attached agencies outside of the said pool of lawyer-personnel.
In cases where there is a complainant who has his/her own private
prosecuting attorney/s, such private prosecuting attorney/s shall be
considered under the direct control and supervision of the duly-designated
prosecuting attorney by the Secretary.
SECTION 33. How conducted. Option to conduct via
videoconferencing. The appearance of parties and/or their counsel, if
any, before the Technical Staff or duly-designated hearing officer/panel
shall be required during the conduct of formal investigation, including pre-
hearing conference and hearing.
However, the Technical Staff or duly-designated hearing officer/panel,
motu proprio or upon motion, may issue an order directing the conduct of
the pre-hearing conference and/or hearing through videoconferencing or
the use of video, audio, and data transmission devices to allow participants
in different physical locations to simultaneously communicate by seeing and
hearing each other.
Videoconferencing during formal investigations shall be in accordance
with the Civil Service Commission Resolution No. 2100420 dated 02 June
2021, or the "Guidelines on the Use of Videoconferencing Platform for
Hearings and Pre-Hearing Conferences in Civil Service Commission," or any
of its subsequent amendments/modifications. In case there are matters not

SECTION 34. Pre-hearing briefs, conference and order. The
conduct of the pre-hearing conference is mandatory, and failure of any
party to attend the same may, upon appropriate motion of the present
party, cause the immediate submission of the case for decision based on
available records.
The parties may file their respective pre-hearing briefs, copy furnished
the other party, at least three (3) days before the date set for the pre-
hearing conference.
At the commencement of the formal investigation, the Technical Staff
or duly-designated hearing officer/panel shall conduct a pre-hearing
conference for the parties to appear, consider, and agree on any of the
following:
Stipulation of facts;
Simplification of issues;
Identification and marking of evidence of the parties;
Waiver of objections to admissibility of evidence;
Limiting the number of witnesses, and their names;
Dates of subsequent hearings; and
Such other matters as may aid in the prompt and just disposition
of the case.
The agreement/s entered into during the pre-hearing conference
shall be contained in a pre-hearing order which shall be binding on the
parties, unless, in the interest of justice, the Technical Staff or duly-
designated hearing officer/panel allows a deviation therefrom.
SECTION 35. Continuous Hearings. Postponement. Hearings
shall be conducted on the hearing dates set by the Technical Staff or duly-
designated hearing officer/panel or as agreed upon during the pre-hearing
conference.
Each party may be granted only one (1) postponement upon oral or
written and meritorious request.
Without prejudice to the immediately preceding paragraph, the failure
of the respondent and/or his counsel to appear in any hearing despite due
notice shall be deemed a waiver of respondent's right to be present during
such hearing and present his/her evidence thereon.

SECTION 36. Order of Hearing. Unless the Technical Staff or duly-
designated hearing officer/panel directs otherwise, the order of hearing
shall be as follows:
The prosecution shall present its evidence;
The respondent shall present evidence in support of his/her
defense;
Upon motion, the prosecution may present rebuttal evidence; and
Upon motion, the respondent may present sur-rebuttal evidence.
SECTION 37. Formal Offer of Evidence. Memoranda. The
prosecution and respondent shall formally offer, either orally or in writing,
their evidence immediately upon termination of their presentation of
evidence. Objections by the other party to such formal offer of evidence
may likewise be done either orally or in writing.
Thereafter, the parties, upon motion, may be given a maximum
period of five (5) days from receipt of the order granting such motion within
which to submit their respective memoranda.
SECTION 38. Duration of Formal Investigation. The formal
investigation shall be terminated within a period of thirty (30) days from
commencement thereof. In meritorious cases however, such period may be
extended upon approval by the Secretary.
SECTION 39. Formal Investigation Report.
Within fifteen (15) days after the conclusion of the formal
investigation, a Formal Investigation Report (FIR) containing a
narration of the: material facts established during the formal
investigation; findings and the evidence supporting said findings; and
recommendations, shall be submitted by the Technical Staff or duly-
designated hearing officer/panel to the Secretary.
The complete records of the case shall be attached to the FIR,
which shall be treated with strict confidentiality. The complete records
with Table of Contents shall be systematically and chronologically
arranged, paged, and securely bound to prevent loss and ensure
integrity of the records.
RULE VIII
DECISION
SECTION 40. When Case is Decided. The Secretary shall decide
the case within thirty (30) days from receipt of the FIR and complete case
records.

SECTION 41. Finality of Decisions. A decision rendered by the
Secretary whereby a penalty of reprimand, suspension for not more than
thirty (30) days, or a fine in an amount not exceeding thirty (30) days'
salary is imposed, shall not be appealable. It shall be final and executory
unless a motion for reconsideration is seasonably filed. However, the
respondent may file an appeal or petition for review when the issue raised
is violation of due process.
If the penalty imposed is suspension exceeding thirty (30) days, or
fine in an amount exceeding thirty (30) days' salary, the decision shall be
final and executory after the lapse of the reglementary period for filing a
motion for reconsideration or an appeal and no such motion or pleading has
been filed.
SECTION 42.(cid:9) Execution of Decision/Resolution. The final and
executory decision or resolution of the Secretary shall be implemented
through the Commissioner, unless the Secretary designates another official
or employee; and notwithstanding the pendency of any appeal or petition
for review filed by the aggrieved party.
RULE IX
MOTION FOR RECONSIDERATION
SECTION 43.(cid:9) Motion for Reconsideration. The party adversely
affected by the decision or resolution may file a motion for reconsideration
with the Secretary within a period of fifteen (15) days from receipt of said
decision or resolution.
A motion for extension of time to file motion for reconsideration shall
not be allowed.
Only one (1) motion for reconsideration shall be entertained. If a
second motion for reconsideration from the same party is filed
notwithstanding the proscription under these Rules, the finality of action
shall be reckoned from the date of denial of the first motion for
reconsideration.
SECTION 44.(cid:9) Grounds. The motion for reconsideration shall be
based only on any the following grounds:
New evidence has been discovered which materially affects the
decision or resolution;
The decision or resolution is not supported by the evidence on
record; or
Errors of law or irregularities have been committed which are
prejudicial to the interest of the movant.

SECTION 45.(cid:9) Effect of Filing. The filing of a motion for
reconsideration within the reglementary period shall stay the execution of
the decision or resolution sought to be reconsidered.
RULE X
APPEAL
SECTION 46. Appeal. For decisions or resolutions of the Secretary
falling under the second paragraph, Section 41, Rule VIII hereof, the party
adversely affected may appeal said decision or resolution to the Civil
Service Commission, within a period of fifteen (15) days from receipt
thereof, and in accordance with Rule 13 of the 2017 RACCS on Appeal in
Disciplinary Cases, as may further be amended.
RULE XI
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
SECTION 47. Transitory provision. Administrative disciplinary
proceedings already commenced and pending under DC 001 dated 05
January 2015 are hereby recognized and shall continue regardless of the
stage thereof.
The authority given to any personnel or employee of the DO] and BI
to conduct preliminary investigation and/or formal investigations prior to
the effectivity of these Rules, shall likewise continue and are hereby

Without prejudice to the immediately preceding paragraph however,
upon the effectivity of these Rules, all administrative disciplinary
proceedings commenced and pending under DC 001 shall then be continued
in accordance with these Rules.
SECTION 48. Inventory. The Technical Staff and the BI shall each
submit to the Secretary an inventory of all pending or unresolved
administrative cases in their respective dockets, within seven (7) days from
the effectivity of these Rules.
SECTION 49. Suppletory Application of the 2017 RACCS. In
circumstances or stages of administrative disciplinary proceedings where
these Rules do not provide therefor, the 2017 RACCS and its subsequent
amendments, insofar as they are not contrary to law, public policy,
customs, traditions, and morals, shall have suppletory application.
SECTION 50. Separability. If any portion of these Rules is declared
unconstitutional or invalid by competent authority, the other provisions not
otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.
