**THIRD REGULAR SESSION**

**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 450**

**RESOLUTION STRONGLY CONDEMNING THE SENSELESS KILLING OF MS. CHRISTHIA "THIA" ANGELA O. ARAGONCILLO, A LOGISTICS MANAGEMENT OFFICER AT THE MINISTRY OF SOCIAL SERVICES AND DEVELOPMENT—MAGUINDANAO PROVINCIAL OFFICE**

**WHEREAS,** the 1987 Philippine Constitution provides that the “*prime duty of the Government is to serve and protect the people*” (Section 4, Article II) and that “*no person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law*” (Section 1, Article III);

**WHEREAS,** the Constitution further states that it is the policy of the State to maintain “*peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy*” (Section 5, Article II) and that the State “*values the dignity of every human person and guarantees full respect for human rights*” (Section 11, Article II);

**WHEREAS,** the right to life is enshrined in Article III of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and Article VI of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, which provides that “*Every human being has the inherent right to life. This right shall be protected by law. No one shall be arbitrarily deprived of his life;*”

**WHEREAS,** Section 3, Article V of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, mandates that “*Within its territorial jurisdiction, the Bangsamoro Government shall maintain peace and order and preserve the comfort and convenience of its inhabitants;*”

**WHEREAS,** Section 6, Article IX of the same Organic Law states that “*The Bangsamoro Government shall fully respect human rights;*”

**WHEREAS,** Section 27 (b) of the Bangsamoro Autonomy Act No. 4 or the “*Bangsamoro Human Rights Act of 2019*” mandates the Bangsamoro Human Rights Commission to “*protect human rights and uphold international humanitarian law through conducting investigations, monitoring, fact-finding missions, and public inquiries, reporting the results of the same, and proceeding with prosecution when warranted;*”

**WHEREAS,** Adopted Resolution No. 268, or the Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures, and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, includes a Committee on Bangsamoro Justice System, which has jurisdiction on “*all matters relating to the plural justice systems in the BARMM, including but not limited to... criminal jurisdiction which the Parliament has the power to legislate on as provided under Republic Act No. 11054*” (Section 3, Rule XII). It also provides for the Parliament to create special or ad hoc committees “*to consider specific issues which necessitate immediate and prompt action by the Parliament*” (Sections 11 and 12, Rule XII);

**WHEREAS,** on April 17, 2024, the Ministry of Social Services and Development (MSSD) issued an official statement strongly condemning the fatal shooting of Ms. Christhia "Thia" Angela O. Aragoncillo, a dedicated Logistics Management Officer at the MSSD – Maguindanao Provincial Office, which occurred moments after she left her workplace;

**WHEREAS,** the statement further reported that another colleague was injured in the incident and is currently recovering in a medical facility;

**WHEREAS,** the MSSD added that the perpetrator of this cowardly act remains unidentified and that they are cooperating closely with the authorities in their investigation to serve justice;

**WHEREAS,** the fatal shooting of Ms. Aragoncillo, a public servant of the Bangsamoro Government, is a deplorable act of violence against an individual who is serving and improving the lives of the people in the Bangsamoro Region;

**WHEREAS,** this act of violence against government employees is unacceptable and has no place in a society that values the rule of law, human rights, and the safety and well-being of its citizens;

**WHEREAS,** the Bangsamoro Transition Authority extends its deepest condolences to the family of Ms. Aragoncillo and expresses sincere sympathy to her colleague who was injured in the incident:

**NOW, THEREFORE,** be it

**RESOLVED,** as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To strongly condemn the senseless killing of Ms. Christhia "Thia" Angela O. Aragoncillo, a Logistics Management Officer at the Ministry of Social Services and Development–Maguindanao Provincial Office.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER,** To express sincere sympathy and wish for the speedy recovery of Ms. Aragoncillo’s colleague who was injured in the incident.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY,** To furnish this Resolution to the family of Ms. Aragoncillo, her injured colleague, as well as to the MSSD, for information and its necessary effects.

**ADOPTED,** *Dhu Al-Qa’dah 13, 1445/May 21, 2024.*
