**THIRD REGULAR SESSION**

**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 523**

**RESOLUTION REQUIRING THE OFFICE OF THE CHIEF MINISTER, THROUGH THE BANGSAMORO PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY, TO UPDATE THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION PLAN 2019-2022, FOR CONSIDERATION AND APPROVAL OF THE BANGSAMORO TRANSITION AUTHORITY PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Section 2, Article XVI of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as the *“Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (OLBARMM)”*, states that:

*“There is hereby created a Bangsamoro Transition Authority which shall be the interim government in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition period.”*

**WHEREAS**, Section 3, Article XVI of the same law provides that:

*“Legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition shall be vested in the Bangsamoro Transition Authority.”*

**WHEREAS**, Article XVI of the same law provides for the following provisions:

**Sec. 4. *Functions and Priorities*.** – The Bangsamoro Transition Authority shall ensure the accomplishment of the following priorities during the transition period:

xxx

(c) Organization of the bureaucracy of the Bangsamoro Government during transition, including the approval and implementation of a transition plan, and institution of a placement process for hiring of personnel during transition.

xxx

**Sec. 6. *Transition Plan*.** – Within the first sixty (60) days of the transition period, the interim Chief Minister shall submit to the Bangsamoro Transition Authority a transitory plan that shall contain the proposed organizational plan, as well as, the schedule for implementation therefor. The Bangsamoro Transition Authority shall, by a majority vote of all its members, approve or otherwise act on the proposed transition plan within ten (10) days upon submission by the interim Chief Minister. If the transition plan is not acted upon within sixty (60) days, it shall be deemed approved. The transition plan shall be implemented within fifteen (15) days from its approval;

**WHEREAS**, on June 17, 2019, in a special session called for the purpose, the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament approved and adopted Resolution No. 12, entitled *“Resolution approving the Transition Plan of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM);”*

**WHEREAS**, according to Chief Minister Ahod Balawag Ebrahim, *“the transition plan aims to identify the new structural framework of the BARMM, based on the provisions of Republic Act No. 11054, also known as the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL), taking into consideration the existing structure, budget, and other relevant features of the ARMM;”*

**WHEREAS**, the transition period was extended until June 30, 2025, under Republic Act No. 11593, otherwise known as *“An Act Resetting the First Regular Elections in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao;”*

**WHEREAS**, His Excellency President Ferdinand *“Bongbong”* Romualdez Marcos, Jr., appointed the eighty (80) Members of Parliament of the BTA last August 12, 2022, and the BTA held its inaugural session on September 15, 2022;

**WHEREAS**, given the extension of the transition period and with the new composition of the BTA Parliament, it is necessary to revisit and update the Bangsamoro Transition Plan 2019-2022 to define the road map and deliverables of the BTA, as an interim government, during the extended period until June 30, 2025:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved by the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament, To require the Office of the Chief Minister, through the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, to update the Bangsamoro Transition Plan 2019-2022, for consideration and approval of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority Parliament.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To furnish this Resolution to the Office of the Chief Minister and the Bangsamoro Planning and Development Authority, for information and appropriate consideration.

**ADOPTED**, *Rajab* 23, 1446/January 23, 2025.
