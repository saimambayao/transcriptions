**PARLIAMENT RESOLUTION NO. 489**

**RESOLUTION ISSUING DRESS CODE GUIDELINES FOR MEMBERS OF THE PARLIAMENT**

**WHEREAS**, Sections 2 and 3, Article XVI of Republic Act No. 11054, otherwise known as “Organic Law for the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao”, provide that the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) shall serve as the interim government in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition period and it shall exercise legislative and executive powers in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region during the transition period;

**WHEREAS**, Section 25, Article VII of the abovementioned law provides that the Parliament shall adopt its own rules for the conduct of its business;

**WHEREAS**, BTA Adopted Resolution No. 268 or the “*Resolution Providing for Rules, Procedures and Practices of the Bangsamoro Transition Authority (BTA) Parliament*”, otherwise known as “*the Rules*”, guides the conduct of all sessions, committee hearings and meetings, and other similar activities of the BTA;

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Rule XX of the Rules on “*Decorum, Parliamentary Acts and Language*” defines “*Decorum*” as “*the proper etiquette and conduct of Members during the session of the Parliament*”, while Section 2 defines “*unparliamentary speeches, acts, and language*” as those acts that are offensive or improper;

**WHEREAS**, Section 1, Rule XXVIII of the Rules on “*Discipline, Ethics, and Accountability*” provides that “*All Members present in sessions, meetings, hearings and public consultations shall have the obligation to obey the legitimate orders of the Speaker. The Speaker or a Member can call to order any Member for simple breach of conduct or this Rules. The Speaker shall warn an offending Member for his/her disorderly behavior during the sessions, meetings, hearings, and public consultations for grave breach of order. The Parliament shall have the power to impose a penalty upon the offending Member by majority vote to reprimand, censure, or be removed from the plenary;*”

**WHEREAS**, under Section 3 (a), Rule XXVIII of the Rules on “*Discipline, Ethics, and Accountability*” a Member of Parliament shall “*Act at all times and in all places in a manner that shall reflect the honor, virtue and uprightness of the Parliament;*”

**WHEREAS**, Section 3 (h) of Republic Act No. 6713 or the “Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees” provides that “*public officials and employees and their families shall lead modest lives appropriate to their positions and income. They shall not indulge in extravagant or ostentatious display of wealth in any form;*”

**WHEREAS**, in upholding the dignity, honor, virtue, and integrity of the Parliament, it is imperative for every Member to adhere to appropriate attire standard;

**WHEREAS**, there is a necessity to establish clear dress code guidelines that best reflect the Parliament’s dignity and are also in keeping with applicable laws;

**WHEREAS**, the dress code guidelines shall provide for the type of attire that Members of the Parliament (MPs) are prescribed to wear, the types of attire that are considered inappropriate, and the corresponding sanctions in case of violation thereof:

**NOW, THEREFORE, be it**

**RESOLVED**, as it is hereby resolved, by the Bangsamoro Parliament, To issue a Dress Code Guidelines for Members of the Parliament.

**RESOLVED, FURTHER**, To adopt the following Dress Code Guidelines for Members of the Parliament:

**DRESS CODE GUIDELINES FOR MEMBERS OF THE PARLIAMENT**

In keeping with the dignity, honor, virtue, and integrity of the Parliament, Members shall adhere to the following dress code guidelines:

**SECTION 1.** During regular or special sessions of the Parliament, the Members shall wear the following:

(a) For Gentlemen: “*Barong Tagalog*” or Coat and Tie;

(b) For Ladies: “*Filipina*” Dress or Modest Business Attire; and

(c) Traditional Filipino dress, including modest traditional attire of the Bangsamoro.

**SECTION 2.** Parliamentary robes shall be provided by the Parliament and mandatorily be worn during plenary sessions in accordance with the specified dress code.

**SECTION 3.** Members shall wear their issued Parliament pins during parliamentary sessions.

**SECTION 4.** Members shall observe these guidelines, except when the Speaker of Parliament issues an order requiring the MPs to wear a different dress code for a particular occasion.

**SECTION 5.** Members who violate or do not comply with these guidelines shall be meted with the following:

(a) 1st Offense: Erring Members shall receive a written notice of violation by the Office of the Parliament Speaker;

(b) 2nd Offense: Erring Members shall be considered absent. Consequently, any action they raise before the plenary shall produce no effect and shall not be recorded *except* when the Member disrupts the said sessions/meetings; and

(c) 3rd Offense: Referral to the Committee on Ethics and Privileges for appropriate disciplinary action, without prejudice to the application of Section 1, Rule XXVIII of the Rules.

**SECTION 6.** The Committee on Ethics and Privileges of the Bangsamoro Parliament shall oversee the implementation of the dress code guidelines, resolve any issues or disputes, and conduct periodic reviews to recommend necessary updates to the dress code.

**RESOLVED, FINALLY**, that this Resolution shall take effect immediately upon its approval and shall remain in force perpetually unless a new set of guidelines on the same subject matter is issued.

**ADOPTED**, *Safar* 17, 1446/August 21, 2024.
