SUBJECT: SUPPLEMENTAL GUIDELINES ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF FINANCIAL
CONSUMER PROTECTION ASSISTANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
(FCPAMS) BY THE CDA-REGULATED ENTITIES (CDARES)

This Memorandum Circular is hereby issued to provide guidance on complaint handling
by CDA-Regulated Entities offering financial products and services, except Cooperative Banks
and Insurance Cooperatives, pending the full establishment and operationalization of their
respective Financial Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS).

Pursuant to Memorandum Circular No. 2025-08, or the “Guidelines on the Establishment
of Financial Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS) by the CDA-
Regulated Entities (CDARES)”, CDAREs must complete and operationalize their respective
FCPAMS by the following deadlines:

e For Medium and Large Cooperatives: on or before 30 June 2026; and
e For Micro and Small Cooperatives; on or before 31 December 2026

While the establishment and operationalization of FCPAMS remain pending, all CDAREs
are directed to continue observing the existing procedures for the handling and resolution of
complaints.

Complaint handling shall continue to be governed by, and pyceree in accordance with,
the following:

e Republic Act No. 9520, otherwise known as the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008,
and its Implementing Rules and Regulations, particularly on the settlement of disputes
and the conduct of conciliation, mediation, and other alternative dispute resolution
mechanisms at the cooperative level;

e The CDA Omnibus Rules of Procedure, which prescribe the procedures for mediation-
conciliation, voluntary arbitration, investigation, adjudication, and administrative
proceedings before the Authority; and

e Other applicable CDA laws, rules, regulations, and issuances governing the filing,
handling, and resolution of complaints.

CDAREs shall prioritize the exhaustion of internal remedies and alternative dispute
resolution mechanisms in accordance with their respective by-laws and existing rules. They
shall ensure that complaints are addressed promptly and fairly and that proper records of
complaints received and actions taken thereon are duly maintained.

This interim arrangement is without prejudice to the rights of financial consumers under
Republic Act No. 11765, otherwise known as the Financial Products and Services Consumer
Protection Act (FCPA).
