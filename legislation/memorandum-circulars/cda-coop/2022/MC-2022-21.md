SECTION 1. TITLE.

This Memorandum Circular shall be known as the Guidelines Implementing the
Cooperative Development Authority’s (CDA) Handholding Program for Micro and Small
Cooperatives.

SECTION 2. LEGAL BASES
This Guidelines is anchored on the following provisions of law:

a. RA 11364 or the Cooperative Development Authority Charter of 2019, Section 4 (0)
Develop and conduct management and training programs that will provide members
of cooperatives with the entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, technical
skills required for efficient operation of their cooperatives and inculcate in them the
true spirit of cooperativism and provide, when necessary, technical and professional
assistance to ensure the viability of cooperatives with special concern for agrarian
reform, agriculture, fishery, and the economically depressed sectors.

b. RA 11364, Section 4 (dd) Collaborate with concerned agencies that can provide
technical, professional, and marketing assistance including trainings on transfer of
technology to ensure the viability, growth, competitiveness and innovativeness of
cooperatives giving special concern to agrarian reform, agriculture, fishery and the
economically depressed sectors.

c. RA 11364 Section 4 (ee) Provide assistance on cooperatives product development
and facilitate their marketing both in the domestic and international market in
coordination with the concerned agencies.

d. Rule V, Section 2, IRR of RA 11364, Development and conduct of training programs.
The Authority shall develop and conduct training programs for officers and members
of cooperatives which shall focus on entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial
expertise and technical skills required for the efficient operation of their cooperatives.

e. Section 3 (d) RA 10744 or the Credit Surety Fund Cooperative Act of 2015, Build up
the capability of cooperatives and NGOs in the areas of credit evaluation, loan and
risk management and good governance principle.

SECTION 3. DESCRIPTION OF THE PROGRAM

The Cooperative Development Authority's Handholding Program for Micro and Small
Cooperatives was born out of the need to help the micro' and small* registered cooperatives
across the country to achieve inclusive growth and bring shared prosperity for all. Micro and small
cooperatives compose 82% of the total registered cooperatives. 3

Through this program, the Authority puts together initiatives that can help these
cooperatives with entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, and technical skills required
for the efficient operation of their cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of
cooperativism. These shall include, but not be limited to trainings, technical assistance, coaching,
and networking with other relevant government agencies, the academe, or private institutions.

SECTION 4. OBJECTIVES OF THE PROGRAM

a. To provide free mandatory trainings for members and officers of micro and small
cooperatives prescribed by the Cooperative Development Authority;

b. To provide other trainings for members and officers of cooperatives on cooperative
principles and values, good governance, business development, business and
entrepreneurial management, organizational development, conflict management, risk
management, financial literacy, financial management, basic computer literacy and
other trainings necessary for the development of micro and small cooperatives;

c. To provide technical assistance to micro and small cooperatives to help them comply
with regulatory requirements prescribed by the Authority and other regulatory
Agencies;

d. To provide technical assistance to micro and small cooperatives to help them avail of
services of other government agencies, private institutions, and international
institutions;

e. To link micro and small cooperatives with other government agencies or other entities
for training, technical or financial assistance; and

f. Undertake activities that will-create a conducive environment for the growth and
development of cooperatives.

SECTION 5. COVERAGE

This Guidelines shall cover all micro and small cooperatives registered with the
Cooperative Development Authority.

Regional Extension Offices shall provide for free the mandatory trainings
prescribed by the Authority to micro and small cooperatives. This does not,
however, preclude micro and small cooperatives to avail of the services of
accredited Cooperative Training Providers (CTPROs) and Cooperative ’
Development Offices. In case they choose to avail of the services of accredited
CTPROs and Cooperative Development Offices, logistical requirements for the
conduct of the trainings shall be in accordance with the agreement of both parties.

A Quarterly Schedule of the Mandatory and Other Trainings to be conducted in
the Regional and field offices shall be posted in their social media accounts and
on the CDA Website or other venues to ensure extensive dissemination.

Regional Offices and the Head Office shall constitute their respective Trainers
Bureau composed of subject matter experts from the Regional Offices and Head
Office, respectively.

The Cooperative Research, Information, and Training Section (CRITS) and the
Cooperative Research, Information, and Training Division (CRITD) shall maintain
an electronic copy of all training materials used by the respective pool of trainers
as well as a record of all the trainings conducted under this program and a
summary of the Training Evaluation Forms.

The Authority shall ensure that their trainers are equipped to deliver the trainings,
both in terms of technical capability, training materials and equipment. It shall
include in its annual program a refresher training for all personnel assigned to
conduct the trainings.

Mandatory Trainings attended by the members and officers of micro and small
cooperatives under this program shall be considered as compliance to the
required training for cooperatives prescribed under Rule 7 of the Revised IRR of
RA 9520.

A Certificate of Completion shall be issued to participants who completed the

training. The Certificate shall bear a distinct code for control and monitoring
purposes.

Coaching

Coaching activities are to be provided for free by personnel of the Authority.

Coaching can be delivered thru one-on-one consultation or small group
facilitation. It can be provided by phone, other on-line methods, or face to face as
agreed upon by the requesting cooperative/client and CDA.

SECTION 8.

Micro and small cooperatives may be referred to other government agencies or
private institutions that can best respond to their specific needs. Referrals should
always be covered by a referral letter. Regional Offices shall maintain a record of
all referrals made and if possible, the status of the referrals. Micro and small
cooperatives may be referred to medium or large cooperatives for possible
managerial, financial, and technical assistance.

A monitoring report of the assistance provided based on the referral shall be |
submitted by the CDS in the area using a prescribed form.

EVALUATION OF ASSISTANCE PROVIDED

For trainings and coaching conducted, duly-accomplished Training Evaluation
Form and Coaching Evaluation Forms shall be required from the participants
which shall form part of the report of the personnel conducting the training or
coaching.

For referral/ networking service, a duly-accomplished client feedback form shall
be required from the requesting cooperative/client.

The Regional Extension Offices shall conduct an_ effectiveness

monitoring/assessment activity every two (2) years on the handholding activities
conducted by their respective Regions.

REPEALING CLAUSE

Memorandum Circular No. 2017-01, series of 2017, Capability Building Program for Micro
Cooperatives and other prior issuances found inconsistent herewith are deem repealed.

SECTION 9.

EFFECTIVITY

This Memorandum Circular shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following the
completion of its publication in the Office of the National Administrative Register (ONAR) and
submission of copy hereof to the Official Gazette.
