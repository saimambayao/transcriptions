The CDA Board of Directors hereby promulgates the following guidelines governing
the creation of the National Coordinating Committee for Cooperative Development (NCC-CD):

Section 1. Title

This Circular shall be known as the Guidelines of the National Coordinating Committee
for Cooperative Development (NCC-CD).

Section 2. Rationale

Before the enactment of RA 11364 the National Coordinating Committee on
Cooperative Promotion and Development (NCC-CD) was operating pursuant to Executive
Order 95 designating the Cooperative Development Authority as the lead government agency
in the promotion and development of cooperatives in the country. The RA 11364 expressly
mandates the creation of the National Coordinating Committee.

This Circular is issued to prescribe guidelines governing the creation, composition,
functions, representations, and meetings of NCC-CD towards mutual collaboration, inclusive
formulation, and enforcement of cooperative development initiatives.

Section 3. Legal Bases

The legal bases of this Guidelines are anchored on Section 4 (b), (aa), (dd), (ee), (ii),
and Sec. 6 (f) of Republic Act No. 11364, and Sections 2 and 3 of Rule IV Implementing the
Rules and Regulations of the said law, quoted below:

Section 2. CDA as the lead agency in cooperative development. The CDA, as the lead agency
of the government in the promotion and development of cooperatives, shall synchronize the
efforts of other relevant government institutions toward the common goal of empowering the
cooperatives in achieving their growth as instruments of equity, social justice and economic
development.

Section 3. National Coordinating Committee. All government branches, instrumentalities,
subdivisions: and agencies with programs and projects concerning cooperatives, shall
coordinate such programs and projects with the Authority, with a view to providing a
comprehensive technical and financial program for cooperatives, through the creation of a
National Coordinating Committee, chaired by the Authority. The Committee shall be composed
of representatives from said government branches, instrumentalities, subdivisions and
agencies whose ranks should not be lower than the Director level in charge of cooperative
programs.

Section 5. Functions of NCC-CD
The NCC-CD shall have the following functions:

a. Coordinate the formulation and implementation of programs, projects, and activities
with the CDA, with the view to providing comprehensive technical and financial
programs for cooperatives;

b. Harmonize the formulation and implementation of policies, regulations,’and reforms as
well as the pursuit of development initiatives that are mutually important for the
promotion of the respective mandates of the member government agencies;

c. Assess current cooperative programs, projects, and activities of national govemment
agencies and the provision of policy recommendations and technical guidance of the
NCC-CD for these programs and initiatives, if deemed necessary.

Section 6. Creation of Sub-Committees

The NCC-CD may create sub-committees to address specific concerns relative to the
agency programs and projects for the cooperatives.

The Sub-Committees’ member-agencies shall submit reports to the Secretariat the
respective programs, projects, and services on cooperative development including the status
of implementation for information and dissemination.

The presiding officer of each sub-committee shall be designated by the CDA
Chairperson from among the CDA Board of Directors. For the respective Sub-Committees,
the presiding officers shall have the same role as that of the NCC-CD Chairperson as provided
under Section 8 of this Memorandum Circular.

Section 7. NCC-CD Chairperson

The CDA shall be the Head Convenor. During meetings, the CDA Chairperson shall
be the presiding officer. The NCC-CD Chairperson may designate an alternate presiding
officer which shall come from the CDA Board of Directors.

Section 8. Role of the NCC-CD Chairperson

a. Preside in all meetings;

b. Determines the agenda of the meetings;

c. Exercises general supervision and control over the affairs of the NCC-CD in
accordance with the policies set forth by the same;

d. Invites representatives of agencies not members of the NCC-CD to attend any
meetings of the Committee to discuss issues of interest to the NCC-CD;

e. Call special meetings, as may be necessary; and

f. Performs such other duties as may be required.

Section 9. Frequency of Meetings

The NCC-CD shall have regular quarterly meetings, to discuss, among others,
programs and projects for cooperatives to be implemented by the respective government
instrumentalities, subdivisions, and agencies and to provide updates on the implementation.

A special meeting may be called by the NCC-CD Chairperson or upon request of at
least five (5) of NCC-CD.

Section 10. Role of CDA Institutional Development Services (IDS)

The IDS, through the Cooperative Project Development Assistance Division (CPDAD),
designated as Secretariat shall be responsible for ensuring that NCC-CD activities are
coordinated, facilitated, and documented.

As such, the Division shall be responsible for coordinating, facilitating, documenting,
and maintaining all records and documents related to the operation and coordination activities
of the NCC-CD.

Section 11. Resources

The logistics, funds, and Secretariat services to the Committee shall be provided by
the CDA.

Section 12. Reports

The Committee, through the Secretariat, shall prepare the NCC-CD activity report
within thirty (30) days after the conduct of each meeting.

Section 13. Amendment/ Repeal

All Memorandum Circulars or rules issued by CDA that are inconsistent with this
issuance are repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

Section 13. Effectivity

This Memorandum Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days following the completion
of its publication in the Official Gazette and the filing of the copy thereof with the Office of
National Administrative Register (ONAR).
