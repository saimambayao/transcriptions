# MC 2025-07 | STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP FOR INCREASED PARTICIPATION IN THE CREDIT SURETY FUND (CSF) PROGRAM

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-07**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: GUIDELINES FOR EMPOWERING MICRO AND SMALL COOPERATIVES THROUGH STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP WITH MEDIUM AND LARGE COOPERATIVES AND COOPERATIVE BANKS FOR INCREASED PARTICIPATION IN THE CREDIT SURETY FUND (CSF) PROGRAM**

**I. BACKGROUND**
Micro and Small Cooperatives (MSCs) play a pivotal role in local economic growth but often face challenges in accessing traditional financing due to limited credit histories and lack of collateral. The Credit Surety Fund (CSF) addresses these challenges but has a high initial contribution requirement (Php100,000) that can be prohibitive for MSCs. 

In response, the Cooperative Development Authority introduces a strategic partnership where Medium and Large Cooperatives (MLCs) and Cooperative Banks (CBs) act as "Big Brothers" to support MSCs in joining CSF Cooperatives through financial assistance/donations.

**II. TITLE**
This Guidelines shall govern the **"Empowering Micro and Small Cooperatives Through Strategic Partnership with the Medium and Large Cooperatives and Cooperative Banks for Increased Participation in the Credit Surety Fund (CSF) Program”**.

**III. LEGAL BASES**
1. **1987 Philippine Constitution (Art. II, Sec. 9)** — State promotion of a just and dynamic social order.
2. **RA No. 11364 (CDA Charter)** — Development of cooperatives through partnership and specific use of statutory funds.
3. **RA No. 9520 (Cooperative Code)** — Principles of "Cooperation among Cooperatives" and allocation of Community Development Funds (CDF) and Optional Funds (OF).
4. **RA No. 10744 (CSF Act of 2015)** — Creation and organization of Credit Surety Fund Cooperatives.

**IV. OBJECTIVES**
1. Align with regulatory frameworks for CDF and OF utilization.
2. Establish clear eligibility criteria to ensure funds reach needy MSCs.
3. Foster transparency and accountability in fund management.
4. Set clear roles for MLCs, CBs, MSCs, and CSFCs.

**V. SCOPE AND COVERAGE**
Applies to participating MLCs, Cooperative Banks (as fund managers), MSCs seeking to join CSFCs, and existing CSF Cooperatives.

**VI. DEFINITION OF TERMS**
*   **Community Development Fund (CDF)** — Refers to the amount (at least 3% of net surplus) used for community projects.
*   **Cooperative Bank (CB)** — Organized for the purpose of providing financial services to cooperatives.
*   **Medium and Large Cooperatives (MLCs)** — Contributors to the program.
*   **Micro and Small Cooperatives (MSCs)** — Beneficiaries of the program.
*   **Koop Kapatid Program** — A CDA program where partner-cooperatives extend assistance to beneficiary-cooperatives.

**VII. PROGRAM MECHANICS**

**1. Creation of the Fund**
*   MLCs must submit a General Assembly Resolution authorizing donation.
*   Collaboration is formalized via a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA).
*   Funds are placed in a special earmarked account managed by the CB.

**2. Purpose and Sources**
*   **Purpose**: Exclusively to cover initial contribution for MSCs to join CSFCs.
*   **Sources**: CDF, Optional Fund (OF), and Social and Community Service Expenses.
*   **Initial Contribution**: At least Five Hundred Thousand Pesos (Php500,000.00) from participating MLCs.

**3. Management of the Fund**
*   CBs act as conduits; funds are revolving (replenished as MSCs repay loans).
*   CBs must have a SAFr rating of at least "3" from the BSP.

**4. Participation Criteria for MSCs**
*   Must have a valid COC and belong to Micro/Small category.
*   Minimum operational history of one (1) year and must be earning.
*   Must submit: Letter of intent, GA Resolution, AFS, and List of Officers.

**VIII. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES**

| Entity | Primary Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **MLCs** | Allocate CDF/OF, execute Deed of Donation, and enter MOA. |
| **Cooperative Banks** | Act as conduit, provide interest-free loans to MSCs, and evaluate applications. |
| **MSCs** | Use proceeds strictly for CSFC contribution and adhere to repayment terms. |
| **CSFCs** | admit MSCs, provide training/workshops, and monitor MSC performance. |
| **CDA** | Promote the program, oversee implementation, and conduct periodic audits. |

**IX. MONITORING AND REPORTING**
*   CBs report fund status, disbursements, and repayments to CDA Extension Offices.
*   Periodic site visits and audits are conducted to verify fund utilization and business performance.

**XI. EFFECTIVITY**
This Guidelines shall take effect fifteen (15) days after filing with ONAR and the Official Gazette.
