# MC 2025-12 | FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE STANDARDS FOR COOPERATIVES ENGAGED IN LENDING THRU ENHANCED PESOS

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-12**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE STANDARDS FOR COOPERATIVES ENGAGED IN LENDING THRU ENHANCED PESOS**

Pursuant to Section 4(a) and (f) of Republic Act No. 11364, otherwise known as the Cooperative Development Authority Charter of 2019, the following Guidelines is hereby issued to prescribe the use of the Enhanced PESOS as financial performance standards for Cooperatives after a series of workshops, consultations with the cooperative sector and other concerned agencies.

**Section 1. Title:** This Guidelines shall be known as the **“Financial Performance Standards for Cooperatives Engaged in Lending thru Enhanced PESOS”**.

**Section 2. Coverage.** This Guidelines shall govern and cover all registered Credit Cooperatives and Multipurpose Cooperatives whose main business is lending.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms**

*   **AVERAGE MEMBERS’ SHARE** — refers to the average of the amount of capital that members have contributed to the cooperative between two periods.
*   **AVERAGE TOTAL LOANS OUTSTANDING** — refers to the average of the amount of outstanding loans to members over a specific period of time.
*   **LOANS RESTRUCTURED** — refer to loans where the original terms have been modified to make repayment easier for the borrower. This can include changes such as extended loan terms, lower interest rates, partial or full condonation of interest and penalties, or modified installment amounts.
*   **MAIN BUSINESS** — refers to the business with the highest percentage in terms of volume of business.
*   **NON-EARNING ASSETS** — are assets that do not give direct revenues to the cooperative, i.e., Cash on Hand, Cash in Bank which are non-interest bearing, Accounts Receivable, Assets in Liquidation, Fixed Assets (Land, Building, Equipment, etc.), Prepaid Expenses and other deferrals, and problem assets.
*   **ENHANCED PESOS** — refers to a set of indicators and standards that measures the financial performance of cooperatives engaged in lending.
*   **PORTFOLIO AT RISK (PAR 30)** — is the outstanding principal balance of all loans with one or more amortization past due for over 30 days.
*   **PROBLEM ASSETS** — refers to a resource or financial claim whose value has significantly decreased or become unlikely to be recovered often due to non-payment, damage or a lack of market.

**Section 4. Purpose of the Enhanced PESOS.**
The purpose of these standards is to serve as a management tool for the cooperative, enabling the identification of operational problem areas, the determination of areas for improvement, and the assessment of the cooperative's overall health.

This will also serve as a supervisory tool for the Authority to evaluate the performance of cooperatives engaged in lending. Results of the evaluation and assessment of the cooperative's performance against certain standards will allow the Authority and the designated supervisor/regulator to compare the performance of various cooperatives over time and across peers.

**Section 5. Indicators of Enhanced PESOS**

This component is comprised of indicators and standards that look at the financial performance of the cooperatives engaged in lending. It has the following major components:

*   **A. Portfolio Quality (P)** — The ratios/indicators under this group provide the cooperatives appropriate tools in monitoring the quality of the loan. This should be monitored closely inasmuch as the loan portfolio accounts constitute the bulk of the cooperative's assets. Protection is measured by comparing the adequacy of the provisions for loan losses against the amount of delinquent loans.
*   **B. Efficiency (E)** — The ratios under this group focus on the operation and administrative efficiency on the delivery of financial services, i.e., loans and savings products to its members. Indicators under this component determine the ability of the cooperative to generate sufficient income to cover expenses on operations.
*   **C. Stability (S)** — The stability of the cooperative is important to ensure that financial services are delivered to its members in a sustained manner. Important to this component is the increase of the institutional capital of the cooperative instead of purely relying on the share capital of members.
*   **D. Operation (O)** — The indicators under this group emphasize the effectiveness of the cooperative to generate income on its operations and return to members. These are key metrics used to evaluate the efficiency and overall performance.
*   **E. Structure of Assets (S)** — This component will help ascertain the quality and the structure of the assets of the cooperative. The indicators determine the extent of the share of the cooperative’s various assets to its total assets.

**Section 6. Overall Rating System**

After getting all the points under each component, an overall rating will be obtained, accompanied by an adjectival rating as follows:

*   **Rating 1. Very Satisfactory.** Strong performance that consistently provides safe and sound operations. Resistant to external shocks.
*   **Rating 2. Satisfactory.** Satisfactory performance that consistently provides safe and sound operations. Some areas of concern require attention.
*   **Rating 3. Fair.** Performance is flawed to some degree and is a supervisory concern. Only nominally resistant to adverse conditions.
*   **Rating 4. Needs Improvement.** Falling short in meeting key performance standards. Requires very close supervisory attention.
*   **Rating 5. Poor.** Unsatisfactory performance and is in critical condition, requiring immediate and comprehensive remedial action.

**Section 7. Reporting of Enhanced PESOS Rating**
The Enhanced PESOS rating shall be incorporated in the Audited Financial Statement as part of the Notes to the Financial Statement.

**Section 8. Regulatory Measures and Enforcement Actions**

| Description / Nature of Violation / Non-Compliance | Enforcement / Regulatory Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Failure to attain a “Fair” Rating on Enhanced PESOS | **3rd Year** — Requirement to Submit a Corrective Action Plan (CAP)<br>**5th Year** — Warning<br>**7th Year** — Merger/Consolidation or Revocation of Registration |

**Section 8. Repealing Clause**
All circulars, regulations, issuances or parts thereof, inconsistent with any of the provisions of this Circular are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

**Section 9. Effectivity**
This Memorandum Circular shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following the completion of its publication in the Official Gazette and the Office of National Administrative Register (ONAR).
