# MC 2025-06 | UNIFORM PROCEDURE FOR THE FILING OF SURETY BOND IN APPLICATIONS FOR CDO AND WRIT OF EXECUTION

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-06**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: UNIFORM PROCEDURE FOR THE FILING OF SURETY BOND IN APPLICATIONS FOR CEASE AND DESIST ORDER (CDO) AND IN MOTIONS FOR THE ISSUANCE OF WRIT OF EXECUTION PENDING APPEAL**

Pursuant to Section 5, Article X, Rule VI of the CDA Omnibus Rules of Procedure and the CDA Implementing Rules and Regulations of the Financial Products and Services Consumer Protection Act (FCPA IRR), the following guidelines are hereby promulgated:

**Section 1. Legal Bases.**
a. **RA 11364 (CDA Charter)** — Section 4(a), (t), and (v) regarding the power to formulate regulations, hear/decide disputes, and issue cease and desist orders.
b. **CDA Omnibus Rules of Procedure** — Rule VI, Article X (Section 5) and Article XV (Section 2) regarding the requirement of a bond for CDO issuance and execution pending appeal.
c. **MC No. 2023-14 (FCPA IRR)** — Rule VII, Section 1(d) on enforcement actions and the issuance of CDOs.

**Section 2. Purpose.**
To establish a standardized process for the submission of applications for Cease and Desist Orders (CDO) and motions for the issuance of Writ of Execution Pending Appeal.

**Section 3. Definition of Terms.**
*   **Movant** — The party filing an application for the issuance of a CDO.
*   **Applicant** — The winning party who filed a motion for execution pending appeal.
*   **Cease and Desist Order (CDO)** — A written order directing a party to refrain from a particular act pending case resolution.
*   **Surety Bond** — A promise by a surety to be answerable for the debt or default of another (the principal) to protect the other party from damages.

**Section 4. Scope and Coverage.**
Applies to docketed cases for adjudication where a party applies for a CDO or a Writ of Execution Pending Appeal.

**Section 5. Filing of Bond.**
The applicant/movant must secure a surety bond from a bonding company accredited by the Insurance Commission (IC). The bond must be filed with the proper CDA office where the proceeding is pending. The bond serves to pay the adverse party for any damages sustained if it is later decided the applicant was not entitled to the order.

**Section 6. Filing of Surety Bond Requirements.**
A bond must be equivalent to the monetary award (if any) plus potential damages and attorney's fees. It must be accompanied by original or certified true copies of:
a. **Declaration under Oath** from the bonding company (genuine and effective until case resolution).
b. **Indemnity Agreement** between the party and the bonding company.
c. **Certificate of Authority** from the Insurance Commission.
d. **Proof of Security Deposit** (Checks are NOT acceptable).
e. **Notarized Board Resolution/Secretary’s Certificate** with specimen signatures.
f. **Administrative Order** granting authority to the bonding company to participate in government projects.

*Note: Bonds from blacklisted or delinquent companies will not be accepted.*

**Section 7. Validity and Liability.**
*   **Validity**: Effective from the date of posting until the case is finally decided or award satisfied. If a bond expires during pendency, it must be renewed or the CDO will be lifted.
*   **Liability**: The bonding company is solidarily liable to pay the premium amount upon establishment of default.
*   **Non-refundability**: The bond is a security; filing fees are non-refundable regardless of the application outcome.

**Section 8. Exemption from Filing of Bond.**
*   Motu proprio applications by the Authority.
*   Applications filed by government agencies.

**Section 9. Effectivity.**
This Circular shall take effect immediately after filing with ONAR and publication.
