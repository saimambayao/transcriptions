Section 5. Coverage.

This Guideline shall cover the organization and registration of the proposed
Memorial Service Cooperative as a special type of cooperative, including those currently
engaged in memorial service activities as defined herein.

Section 6. Definition.

The following terms shall be defined as:

a.

Area of Operation —- shall refer to the place/area where the cooperative
members come from and operate their authorized purpose or business as
provided for in their articles of cooperation and by-laws, which may either be
regional or provincial.

Authority — shall refer to the Cooperative Development Authority.

Cooperative — shall refer to the autonomous and duly registered association of
persons with a common interest who have voluntarily joined together to achieve
their social, economic, and cultural needs and aspirations by making equitable
contributions to the capital required, patronizing their products and services and
accepting a fair share of the risks and benefits of the undertaking in accordance
with universally accepted cooperative principles.

Memorial Service — an activity or range of activities related to honoring and
paying tribute to the deceased. These activities include but are not limited to
providing interment, i.e., burial and funeral rites, cremation, and other related
services except pre-need plans.

Memorial Service Cooperative - a special type of cooperative engaged in
memorial service activities.

Registration — shall refer to the operative act by which the CDA grants judicial
personality to a prospective lagistics service cooperative, as evidenced by the
Certificate of Registration.

Section 7. Registration.
7.1 Name of the Cooperative

The name of the cooperative shall include the type “Memorial Service” and the word
“Cooperative,” “Kooperatiba,” “Coop,” or “Koop,” e.g., United Memorial Service Cooperative.

No cooperative shall be allowed to use a name that is identical, deceptively, or
confusingly similar to any existing cooperative or contrary to public policy, morals, or existing
laws.

The use of the words development, integrated, incorporated, corporation, company,
incorporation, partnership, or other similar connotations, or the use of federation, union,
Philippines, and Pilipinas in the name of a proposed Memorial service cooperative is likewise
prohibited except if it is a part of the registered name of the association or institution where
the members of the proposed cooperative come from.

The name of a cooperative shall not be written in all capital letters except if it is an
acronym or the name of a registered corporation, partnership, association, or foundation.
The use of special characters (e.g.! @ # $ % &#39; A &amp; *) in the proposed cooperative
name is not allowed, and the number of characters should not exceed 100, including the
acronym if using one.

7.2. Membership.

A Memorial Service Cooperative may be organized and registered with at least
fifteen (15) member cooperatives of all types and categories except cooperative unions.

7.3. Area of operation.

A memorial service cooperative's area of operation may be municipal/city, regional,
provincial, or national.

7.4. Capital Requirements.

The authorized share capital of the Memorial Service Cooperative shall be provided
for in its Articles of Cooperation. At least twenty-five percent (25%) of the authorized share
capital shall be subscribed by the members, and at least twenty-five percent (25%) of the
subscribed share capital shall be paid by the members before registration. The minimum

7.5. Bonding Requirements of Accountable Officers/Employees of the Cooperative.

To meet any contingency in the cooperative's operations, accountable
officers/employees of cooperatives shall submit a surety bond for the faithful performance of
their functions per Article 14(5) and Article 56 of the Code.

For purposes of registration of the memorial service cooperative, the following
persons shall be bonded:

1) Chairperson;
2) Treasurer; and
3) Manager

The board of directors shall determine the adequacy of such bonds. For this purpose,
the Board shall be guided by the cooperative's initial net worth, which shall include the paid-
up capital, membership fees, and other assets at the time of registration.

7.6 Registration Requirements.

The following documents shall be submitted to the Authority as part of the registration
requirements:

1) Duly notarized Articles of Cooperation;

2) By-laws;

3) Economic Survey;

4) Business Plan;

5) Treasurer's Affidavit;

6) Surety Bond of Accountable Officers;

7) A General Assembly Resolution from each cooperative approving its membership
and

8) A Board Resolution appointing a representative to the Memorial Service Cooperative

7.7. Registration Fee.
The registration fee to be paid by the proposed cooperative shall be one-tenth (1/10)
of one (1%) percent of the paid-up share capital or the amounts prescribed in the CDA

schedule of fees, whichever is higher.

The proposed cooperative shall likewise pay the Legal and Research fee (LRF) in an
amount equivalent to one (1%) of the fee imposed but in no case lower than ten (10) pesos.

7.8. Registration Procedure.

The registration procedure shall be in accordance with the approved Citizens’
Charter of the Authority.

Section 8. Jurisdiction,

The Memorial Service Cooperative's application for registration shall be filed with the
CDA Regional Office, which has jurisdiction over the cooperative's principal office.

Section 9. Voting system.

Each member of a memorial service cooperative shall have one (1) basic vote and as
many incentive votes as provided for in the bylaws, but not more than five (5) votes. The
votes cast by the delegates shall be deemed votes cast by the members thereof.

Section 10. Compliance with Requirements of Other Agencies.

The Memorial Service Cooperative shall comply with licenses/ permits and other
requirements from other regulatory agencies/LGUs prior to commencing operation.

Section 11. Prohibitions.

The Memorial Service Cooperative shall be prohibited from engaging in any business
activity unrelated to memorial services and any activities contrary to law, morals, public
order, or public policy.

Section 12. Cooperatives Engaged in Memorial Services.

Primary cooperatives with existing memorial services as part of their business
activities are exempt from the provisions of this circular, provided they extend their services
exclusively to their members and immediate family members.

Section 13. Primary Cooperatives Planning to Engage in Memorial Services

Primary Cooperatives planning to hold memorial services as part of their services
shall amend the Articles of Cooperation and Bylaws (ACBL), provided they extend their
services exclusively to their members and their immediate family members.

Section 14. Monitoring and Inspection.
The Extension Office concerned shall monitor and inspect the Memorial Service

Cooperative to ensure compliance with this Guideline, laws, rules, regulations, and other
administrative issuances.

Section 15. Separability Clause.

If any provision is declared null or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions shall
remain valid and enforceable.

Section 16. Transitory Provision.

Existing federations of cooperatives presently engaged in Memorial Services are
hereby given one (1) year from the effectivity of this circular to separate their memorial
business activities and register as a Memorial Service Cooperative per this Memorandum
Circular.

The remaining business activities shall continue to be undertaken by the federation.
They will be regulated strictly following Memorandum Circular No. 2020-24, Guidelines
Governing the Registration and Operation of the Federation of Cooperatives.

Section 17. Repealing Clause.

All other rules, regulations, and issuances contrary or inconsistent with the provisions
of these rules are deemed repealed or modified accordingly.

Section 18. Effectivity.

This Memorandum Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days after it is published in the
Official Gazette and filed with the Office of the National Administrative Register.
