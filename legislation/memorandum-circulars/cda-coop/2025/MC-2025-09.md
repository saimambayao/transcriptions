Section I. Title

This Memorandum Circular shall be known as the Required and Recommended
Trainings for Members, Officers, and Staff of Cooperatives.

Section Il. Legal Bases.

1. Section 4 (0), RA 11364. Develop and conduct management and training programs
that will provide members of cooperatives with the entrepreneurial capabilities,
managerial expertise, and technical skills required for efficient operation of their
cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of cooperativism and provide, when
necessary technical and professional assistance to ensure the viability and growth of
cooperatives with special concern for agrarian reform, agriculture, fishery and the
economically depressed sectors;

2. Section 4 (ii), RA 11364. Formulate, adopt and implement, in a manner consistent
with Section 17 of this Act, a standard training for cooperative officers or members
which shall focus on basic cooperative principles and values, good governance,
business and entrepreneurial management, human resource management, risk
management, conflict management, and technical skills_ required for the efficient and
effective operations of the cooperatives; and

3. Section 17, RA 11364. The Authority, in partnership with learning and training
institutions with the cooperative sector, shall formulate standards of training
requirements for cooperative officers and members to ensure compliance thereof.

4. Article 4 (5) RA 9520, The Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008. Education, Training
and Information- cooperatives shall provide education and training for their members,
elected and appointed representatives, managers, and employees, so that they can
contribute effectively and efficiently to the development of their cooperatives.

5. Article 44 RA 9520 Functions, Responsibilities and Training Requirements of
Directors, Officers and Committee Members. The functions and responsibilities of
directors, officers and committee members, as well as their training requirements,
shall be in accordance with the rules and regulations issued by the Authority.

areas of credit evaluation, loan and risk management, and good governance

7. Section 5 Rule 7 of the Revised IRR of RA 9520. Training Requirements for the
Officers of the Cooperative. The following officers of the cooperative regardless of
type and size, such as: Board of Directors, Secretary, Treasurer, Election Committee,
Audit Committee, Ethics Committee, Mediation and Conciliation Committee, other
committees created by the General/Representative Assembly and General Manager
or Chief Executive Officer, shall be required to undergo a minimum of sixteen (16)
hours of training on the fundamentals of cooperatives and another sixteen (16) hours
on governance and management of cooperatives within the first half of their term.
Provided, That, in case of micro-cooperatives, they shall only undergo sixteen (16)
hours of training on the fundamentals, governance and management of cooperatives.
The content of the training program shall be prescribed by the Authority in
consultation with the accredited training providers and the cooperative sector.

In addition to the above-mentioned trainings, the officers of cooperatives engaged in
savings and credit operations shall be required to undergo an additional sixteen (16)
hours of specialized training on financial management, risk management and credit
management, provided that these cooperatives have at least Five Million Pesos
(Php5,000,000.00) worth of deposit liabilities based on the latest Audited Financial
Statement of the cooperative.

Cooperative officers shall likewise endeavor to undergo additional appropriate
trainings.

Section Ill. Scope.
This Memorandum Circular covers the required and recommended trainings for all members,
officers and staff of cooperatives registered with the Cooperative Development Authority
(CDA).
Section IV. Purposes.

This Memorandum Circular is being issued for the following purposes:

1. Prescribe the required and recommended trainings for members, elected and

appointed officers, managers, and employees of cooperatives per category

including a standard course outline for the required trainings;

2. Identify the authorized providers of the required trainings prescribed in this
Circular.

3. Prescribe the sanctions for non-compliance to this Circular.
Section V. Definition of Terms
Required Trainings. Refer to courses covering the fundamentals, governance and

management of cooperatives and specialized training on financial management, risk
management and credit management, as stipulated in Section 5 of the Revised

Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of Republic Act 9520 and any additional training
required by the CDA.

Recommended Trainings. These pertain to trainings and courses that are recommended
for members, officers, and staff of cooperatives. These trainings are voluntary in nature and
are highly recommended for those seeking to enhance their expertise, insights, and
proficiencies in specific areas relevant to cooperative management, governance, or
operational aspects.

Accredited Cooperative Training Providers (CTPros). This refers to institutions or
organizations that have been accredited by the Cooperative Development Authority to
conduct required and recommended trainings for cooperatives in accordance with the
provisions of RA 9520 and its IRR, RA 11364 and its IRR, Memorandum Circular 2023-11,
and their subsequent amendments.

Cooperative Development Officers (CDOs). This refers to the officer in the Municipal,
City, and Provincial levels who takes charge of the office for the development of
cooperatives registered with the Cooperative Development Authority, in accordance with RA
No. 11535’,

Section VI. Required Trainings

V1.1 Members. All cooperatives are required to ensure that all their members undertake the
Fundamentals of Cooperatives Course, Financial Literacy? and Leadership and Values
Formation within one (1) year from the date of acceptance of their membership. For existing
members, the cooperative shall prepare an action plan to comply with this requirement within
one (1) year from the effectivity of this Circular.

V1.1.1 Continuing Education for Members. All cooperatives shall adopt an annual
continuing education program for members to be reflected in their annual
development plan.

Section VIIl. Sanctions for Non-Compliance with the Required Trainings
The following will be imposed in accordance with due process:
a. Willful failure of the cooperative to comply with the training requirements for its
officers despite due notice from the Authority shall be a ground for the non-issuance

of Certificate of Compliance for the succeeding year.

b. Failure of any officer/s of a cooperative to undergo the prescribed trainings shall
disqualify said officer/s to hold any elective or appointive position.

Section IX. Report on Trainings Undertaken by Officers, Members, and Staff of
Cooperatives

All cooperatives shall submit a list of trainings undertaken by officers to the Regional Office
which has jurisdiction over them in accordance with the form prescribed by the Supervision
and Examination Division (SED). Insurance and CSF Cooperatives shall submit their list to
the Head Office.

Records of trainings attended by members and staff shall be kept by the cooperatives and
made available during the inspection.

Section X. Miscellaneous Provisions

Regional Extension Offices shall prepare their respective quarterly training calendars for
micro and small cooperatives to be posted on their websites and social media platforms

Trainings may be conducted through the traditional method, hybrid, and virtual or online
means.

CDA personnel are not allowed to act as resource persons in trainings conducted by
Accredited Cooperative Training Providers.

Certified Cooperative Development Officers are still required to undergo trainings on
Financial Management, Risk Management and Credit Management in accordance with

All training providers shall adopt the Standard Training Curricula for the Required Trainings
herein provided as Annex A.

Section XI. Transitory Provisions.

Required trainings undertaken prior to the effectivity of this Circular shall be considered as
compliance to the same.
