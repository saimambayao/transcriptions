Section l. Title.

This Memorandum Circular shall be titled as, “Guidelines for Equivalency Certification for
Former CDA Officials, Officers, Technical Personnel, and Incumbent and Past
Cooperative Officers, Leaders, and Advocates, with Five (5) or More Years of
Experience in Cooperative Training”.

Section Il. | Program Description.

The Equivalency Certification Program by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA)
acknowledges the expertise of former CDA officials, Officers, Technical Personnel, and
incumbent and past cooperative leaders and advocates with over 5 years of experience in
cooperative training. It validates their competency in cooperative education, offering formal
recognition for their contributions to cooperative development.

Through a meticulous evaluation process, the program assesses applicants’ knowledge and
skills, granting certification to those meeting established criteria. This initiative promotes
professionalism, enhances the quality of cooperative education, and fosters capacity-building
within the cooperative sector, ensuring the continued advancement and sustainability of
cooperatives nationwide.

Section lil. Legal Basis.

1. Section 4(0) of RA No. 11364 mandates the Authority to “develop and conduct
management and training programs that will provide members of cooperatives with the
entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, and technical skills required for
efficient operation of the cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of
cooperativism and provide, when necessary, technical and professional assistance to
ensure the viability and growth of cooperatives with special concern for agrarian
reform, agriculture, fishery and the economically depressed sectors”.

2. Section 4(a) of RA No. 11364, the Authority is mandated to develop and formulate, in
consultation with the cooperative sector and other concerned institutions, appropriate
regulations, standards, rules, orders, guidelines, and/or circulars to implement RA No.
11364 and RA No. 9520 to ensure the effective and sound operation of cooperatives.

Equivalency Certification is the formal recognition granted to individuals who have
acquired the required knowledge, competencies, and relevant experience in
cooperative development—whether through formal education, professional practice,
or sustained leadership and advocacy. It serves as an alternative pathway for
recognizing the qualifications of trainers, including cooperative advocates, leaders,
former CDA officials, officers, and technical personnel, who demonstrate expertise

This Guidelines aims to achieve the following:

1.

Formal Recognition of Expertise. To formally acknowledge and certify the expertise

years) in cooperative training, thereby validating their knowledge and skills in

cooperative sector by ensuring that certified individuals meet established criteria of
competency, thereby enhancing the overall quality and ‘credibility of cooperative
education and training programs.

Promotion of Sustainable Cooperative Development. To foster capacity-building
and continuous improvement within the cooperative sector, ensuring that certified

Section VI. Coverage of their Equivalency

The Equivalency Certification, once granted, shall be valid and recognized nationwide and
may be used for the following purposes:

Accreditation as Trainer under a CDA-Accredited Cooperative Training Provider
(CTPro). The certification shall serve as proof of qualification in lieu of the required
Training of Trainers (TOT) or other prescribed courses and at least two (2) years of
experience as a resource person of cooperatives, for purposes of being listed as a
trainer under any CDA-Accredited CTPro across all regions in the Philippines.

After being listed as Trainer of CDA-Accredited CTPro, inclusion in CDA’s Pool of
Recognized Cooperative Trainers. Equivalency-certified individuals may be included
in the CDA’s national database or pool of cooperative trainers, subject to applicable
guidelines and performance standards.

The certification is portable and shall not be restricted by geographic location or cooperative
type, allowing the certified individual to offer services to cooperatives and CTPros anywhere
in the country.

a. Eligible Applicants— The following individuals may apply for Equivalency

Certification, provided they have accumulated at least five (5) years of relevant

e Former CDA officials, officers, and technicai personnel

Competency Requirement — Applicants must demonstrate in-depth
knowledge, practical experience, and proficiency in relevant areas of cooperative
education and training. This may be established through documented outputs, prior
training engagements, or other verifiable contributions to cooperative development.

Section Vill. Application Process

a. Submission of Application

Application Submission and Data Privacy Waiver — Applicants shall submit a signed
formal application to the Regional Director together with supporting documents,
including a detailed résumé outlining their training experience, certifications, and
relevant accomplishments. For former CDA officials, officers, and Head Office (HO)
technical personnel, applications shall be submitted directly to the Office of the
Administrator at the Head Office. Formal application shall include Data Privacy
Waiver authorizing the use and verification of personal information and documents
provided as part of the evaluation process.

Processing Fee— Anon-refundable processing fee of Three Thousand Pesos
(3,000.00) shall be paid upon filing of the application.

Evaluation

Evaluation and Assessment Procedure for Equivalency Certification

An Evaluation Panel shall be constituted to assess all applications for Equivalency
Certification. The panel will be composed of experienced professionals from the
Cooperative Development Authority (CDA), the academe, development partners, and
representatives from the cooperative sector.

A Regional Panel shall evaluate applications submitted at the regional level.

A Head Office Panel shall evaluate applications from former CDA officials, officers,
and national/institutional cooperative leaders and advocates.

The panel shall conduct a comprehensive review of each applicant's training record,
demonstrated expertise, and contributions to cooperative development, based on the
submitted portfolio and documentary requirements.

Former CDA officials, officers, and national or institutional cooperative leaders shall be

exempted from the assessment process and may be issued a Certificate of
Equivalency upon evaluation of their credentials and supporting documents.

Section IX. Grant and Effect of Equivalency Certification

a.

Applicants who meet the eligibility criteria and successfully pass the evaluation process
shall be awarded an Equivalency Certification in Cooperative Training.

For the Regional level, the Regional Director, by Authority of the Chairperson, shall
issue an Equivalency Certification. For the Head Office level, the Administrator, by
Authority of the Chairperson, shall issue an Equivalency Certification.

The certification formally recognizes the applicant's expertise and practical experience
as equivalent to the standard qualifications required for cooperative trainers.

The Equivalency Certification shall be valid for five (5) years from the date of issuance.
It may be used to support an application as a member of the pool of trainers of an
accredited Cooperative Training Provider (CTPro), and shall be deemed equivalent to
the trainer qualification requirements outlined in Section 6.3-5 of CDA Memorandum
Circular No. 2023-11 and any subsequent amendments thereto.

Certified individuals may conduct trainings on a national scope upon engagement as
part of the pool of trainers of accredited CTPros.

Once engaged as members of the pool of trainers of accredited CTPros, they shall be
governed by the policies and guidelines on trainer accreditation as prescribed in MC
2023-11 and any amendments thereafter

In no case, the certification shall be used to conduct independent practice outside of
the accredited CTPro.

Section XI. Grounds for Revocation and/or Disapproval of Renewal of the
Certification

The Equivalency Certification may be disapproved or revoked on any of the following grounds,
after due process:

a. Misrepresentation of Qualifications

— Submission of false, misleading, or fraudulent information or documents during
the application process.

b. Ethical Misconduct
— Involvement in unethical behavior that undermines the integrity of the
certification, including but not limited to:
¢ Plagiarism
*- Academic dishonesty
¢ Exploitation or abuse of authority
* Acts prejudicial to the interest of cooperative stakeholders

c. Violation of Laws or Regulations
— Breach of any law, regulation, or rule related to cooperative training or
professional conduct.

d. Incompetence or Substandard Performance
— Demonstrated inability to conduct cooperative training at a professional
standard, based on consistent negative feedback or results of performance
evaluation by the assessment panel.

e. Failure to Maintain Good Standing
— Conduct or behavior that negatively affects the credibility or professional
reputation of the certification holder within the cooperative sector, including
dismissal for cause from previous posts.

f. Non-Compliance with CDA Policies and Directives
— Failure to adhere to CDA policies, guidelines, or directives related to training
and certification. (Shall be governed by CTPro accreditation guidelines. )

g. Non-Compliance with Continuous Professional Development Requirements
— Failure to participate in required professional development activities to maintain
competency. (Also covered under CTPro accreditation guidelines. )

h. Final Judgment in a Civil, Criminal, or Administrative Case Involving Moral
Turpitude
— A final judgment by a competent court or authority that establishes the
commission of acts involving moral turpitude.

i. Other Justifiable Grounds
— Any other act or omission deemed valid and justitiable by the CDA that
compromises the integrity, objectives, or effectiveness of the certification
program.

Section XII. Appeal and Reapplication

a. An applicant whose application is disapproved may reapply, provided that the
grounds for disapproval have been sufficiently addressed or no longer exist.

b. An appeal mechanism and or motion for reconsideration shall be established for
applicants whose certifications are denied or revoked. The appeal or motion for
reconsideration must be filed within fifteen (15) working days from receipt of the
written decision, and shall be reviewed by the CDA Appeals Committee or
designated authority.
