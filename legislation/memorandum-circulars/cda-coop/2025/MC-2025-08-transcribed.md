# MC 2025-08 | GUIDELINES ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF FINANCIAL CONSUMER PROTECTION ASSISTANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (FCPAMS)

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-08**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: GUIDELINES ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF FINANCIAL CONSUMER PROTECTION ASSISTANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (FCPAMS) BY THE CDA REGULATED ENTITIES (CDAREs)**

The Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) recognizes the vital role of financial consumers in ensuring a strong and stable cooperative system. Pursuant to Republic Act (RA) No. 11765 or the Financial Products and Services Consumer Protection Act and its Implementing Rules and Regulations under Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 2023-14, Series of 2023, financial consumers have the right to protection at all stages of their financial transactions with CDA-Regulated Entities (CDAREs).

**Section 1. Title**
This guidelines shall be known as the **“Guidelines on the Establishment of Financial Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS) by the CDA Regulated Entities (CDARES)”**.

**Section 2. Legal Bases**
a. **Section 4(a) of RA 11364** — Power to formulate regulations to ensure the effective and sound operation of cooperatives.
b. **Section 6(a) of RA 11765** — Authority to formulate standards and rules for financial products or services.
c. **Section 8(f) of RA 11765** — Requirement for financial service providers to establish a single consumer assistance mechanism.
d. **CDA MC 2023-14, Series of 2023** — Mandate for CDAREs to establish FCPAMS for handling complaints, inquiries, or requests.

**Section 3. Scope and Applicability**
Governs all CDAREs as defined under Section 4(b), Rule I of MC 2023-14, regarding financial products and/or services (excluding insurance cooperatives and cooperative banks).

**Section 4. Objectives**
a. Provide a framework for institutionalizing FCPAMS;
b. Ensure easily accessible channels for assistance and redress;
c. Implement clear procedures for addressing consumer complaints;
d. Reinforce trust through compliance with consumer protection regulations.

**Section 5. Definition of Terms**
*   **CDA-Regulated Entities (CDAREs)** — Cooperatives registered/regulated by the Authority that offer financial products/services.
*   **Complex complaint or request** — Concerns requiring thorough investigation, legal review, or multi-level coordination (e.g., legal or technical issues).
*   **Financial Consumer** — A person or entity who is a purchaser or recipient of financial products or services.
*   **Financial Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS)** — First-level recourse mechanism for consumers to resolve dissatisfactions for free.
*   **Inquiry** — Request for information or clarification on products, fees, or terms.
*   **Request** — Formal appeal for action/assistance (e.g., document retrieval, account adjustments).
*   **Simple complaint or request** — Straightforward concerns resolvable without in-depth investigation.

**Section 6. Establishment of the CDAREs’ FCPAMS**
FCPAMS must be established as a first-level recourse mechanism, implemented by a **Consumer Assistance Team (CAT)**. Procedural steps from receiving to reporting must be adopted.

**Section 7. Role of the Board of Directors and Senior Management**
The BOD is responsible for effective recourse, approving policies, and ensuring resource adequacy. Senior Management implements and monitors the system and submits required reports.

**Section 8. Minimum Requirements**

**a. Consumer Assistance Team (CAT)**
CDAREs shall designate a CAT (officer, unit, or department) independent from claims handling. CAT roles include logging, investigating, resolving, and reporting complaints.

**b. Capacity Building**
CAT personnel must undergo periodic training in:
*   Interpersonal/Customer service skills
*   Handling financial consumer feedback
*   Problem solving and conflict resolution

**c. Consumer Assistance Mechanisms**
Channels must include walk-ins, web portals, mobile apps, email, social media, etc. A dedicated help desk or hotline is required.

**d. Process and Timelines**
| Action Type | Timeline |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Acknowledgment** | Within 24 hours of receipt |
| **Inquiry Resolution** | Next business day |
| **Simple Complaint Resolution** | Within 7 days |
| **Complex Complaint Resolution** | Within 45 days |
| **Communication of Results** | Within 2 days after resolution |

**Section 9. Interface with the Authority**
Consumers dissatisfied with the CDARE's resolution may elevate concerns to the Authority within seven (7) days from receipt of the final communication.

**Section 10. Period of Compliance**
*   **Medium and Large Cooperatives**: On or before June 30, 2026.
*   **Micro and Small Cooperatives**: On or before December 31, 2026.
