MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-11
Series of 2025

SUBJECT  : REVISED GUIDELINES FOR CONDUCTING PRE-REGISTRATION
SEMINARS FOR PROSPECTIVE COOPERATIVES

Section 1. Title.

This Guidelines shall be known as the Revised Guidelines for Conducting Pre-
Registration Seminars (PRS) for Prospective Cooperatives.

Section ll. Legal Basis.

1. RA11364, Section 4.c, Register all cooperatives including amendments to the Articles
of Cooperation and By-laws (ACBL), division, merger and consolidation.

2. CDA MC 2020-20, Guidelines Governing the Registration of
Cooperatives Through the Electronic Cooperative Registration Information System (E-
Coopris)

3. RA 11535 ARTICLE XVII, SEC. 487, 3.ii. In collaboration with the Cooperative
Development Authority (CDA), provide assistance to prospective cooperatives in the
conduct of the required pre-registration seminar and/or pre-membership education
seminar and in the preparation of required documents for registration.

Section Ill. Purpose

This Memorandum Circular is hereby issued to establish guidelines governing the
conduct of the PRS. This guideline covers the primary objectives of conducting a PRS, course
outline, implementation mechanics, how to avail of the PRS, and the necessary documented
information.

Section IV. Definition of Terms

1. Pre-Registration Seminar — This refers to the seminar conducted by the Cooperative
Development Authority (CDA) for prospective cooperatives. A Certificate of Attendance
to a PRS issued by the Regional Director of the CDA Regional Extension Office where
the PRS was conducted is one of the documentary requirements for registering a
primary cooperative.

Section V. Implementation Mechanics

1. The Pre-Registration Seminar (PRS) shall be administered by the Regional Extension
Offices through the Cooperative Development Specialists (CDS), assisted by
personnel from the Registration Section and the Cooperative Research Information
and Training Section (CRITS).

Section VI. Assistance of Local Government Units (LGUs) to Cooperatives

1.

The Local Government Units (LGUs), through their Cooperative Development Officers
(CDOs), in collaboration with the CDA, provide assistance to prospective cooperatives
in the conduct of the required pre-registration seminar and/or pre-membership
education seminar and in the preparation of required documents for registration.

LGUs through their CDOs may provide the following assistance to groups signifying to
be organized as cooperatives:
a. Provide venue, meals, and other facilities during the conduct of PRS
b. Coordinate with CDA to conduct a PRS
c. Conduct an orientation on cooperative development programs of the LGU,
including situational analysis, and social preparation

Section Vill. Documented Information

The Regional Extension Office shall maintain the following documented information
related to the PRS:

1. Attendance Sheet containing the names of participants, organization/group, address,
gender, place where PRS was conducted and the names of the Resource Person/s
during the PRS.

2. Digital Copy of the presentation materials used.

3. Action photos of the PRS conducted.

Section IX. Data Privacy and Protection

In compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA No. 10173), CDA Regional
Extension Offices, and other training organizers shall ensure the proper handling, storage,
and protection of personal information collected during training activities. This includes, but is
not limited to, attendance sheets, registration forms, digital presentations containing
participant details, and training documentation with identifying information.

Collected data shall be used solely for purposes of training documentation, monitoring,
and reporting as required under this Circular, and shall not be shared with third parties without
the explicit consent of the data subjects, except as required by law. Proper safeguards must
be in place to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse of personal data. All
personnel handling such information must be trained on data privacy protocols and
responsibilities.

Section X. Repealing Clause.

CDA Memorandum Circular 2017-02, Guidelines on the Conduct of Pre-Registration
Seminar (PRS) for Prospective Members of Would-Be Primary Cooperatives and other
issuances inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

Section XI. Separability Clause

If for any reason, any provision of this Guidelines is declared null and void or

unconstitutional, the other provisions hereof which are not affected thereby shall continue to

be in full force and effect. The Doctrine of Operative Fact shall also be applicable to provisions
that were later declared to be unconstitutional.
