SUBJECT: GUIDELINES ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF FINANCIAL CONSUMER
PROTECTION ASSISTANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (FCPAMS) BY THE
CDA REGULATED ENTITIES (CDARES)

The Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) recognizes the vital role of financial consumers in
ensuring a strong and stable cooperative system

. Pursuant to Republic Act (RA) No. 11765 or the Financial Products and Services Consumer
Protection Act and its Implementing Rules and Regulations under Memorandum Circular (MC)
No. 2023-14, Series of 2023, financial consumers have the right to protection at all stages of their
financial transactions with CDA-Regulated Entities (CDAREs). This protection includes access to
an effective mechanism to express concerns, file complaints, and seek redress related to
financial products and services.

Consumer protection is integral to the CDA’s mandate, supporting prudential regulation and
supervision, financial stability, financial inclusion, and financial education. To uphold this, RA
11765 mandates CDAREs to observe fair, transparent, and sound market conduct to enhance
consumer confidence in financial transactions. To this end, the CDA sets forth minimum
guidelines to institutionalize Financial Products and Services Consumer Assistance Management
System (FCPAMS) within the CDAREs, ensuring that financial consumers receive accessible,
affordable, independent, fair, accountable, timely, and efficient means for resolving complaints
regarding their financial transactions.

The FCPAMS serves as the primary platform for addressing consumer concerns at the
cooperative level.

Section 1. Title

This guidelines shall be known as the “Guidelines on the Establishment of Financial
Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS) by the CDA Regulated
Entities (CDARES)”.

Section 3. Scope and Applicability

This guidelines governs the establishment of FCPAMS relative to the handling of consumer
complaints, inquiries, or requests involving financial products and/or services offered or marketed
by all CDAREs as defined under Section 4 (b), Rule | of MC 2023-14.

Section 4. Objectives

The guidelines shall aim to:

a. provide a framework for the establishment and institutionalization of FCPAMS by the
CDAREs;

b. provide financial consumers with easily accessible channels to seek assistance and
redress, ensuring timely and appropriate resolutions to their concerns regarding financial
products and services;

c. require CDAREs to implement clear procedures for addressing consumer complaints,
maintaining proper documentation, and adhering to fair business practices; and

d. reinforce trust in CDAREs by ensuring compliance with consumer protection regulations
and upholding the principles of fair and responsible market conduct.
Section 5. Definition of Terms
For this purpose, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

a. CDA-Regulated Entities (CDAREs) refer to cooperatives registered, regulated, and/or
supervised by the Authority, as provided under existing laws, rules, and regulations, which

offer or market financial products and/or services, excluding insurance cooperatives and
cooperative banks.

Complex complaint or request refers to a concern that requires thorough investigation,
legal review, or multi-level coordination with internal units or external institutions.
Complaints may involve legal, financial, or technical concerns that take longer to resolve.

Financial consumer refers to a person or entity, or their duly appointed representative,
who is a purchaser, lessee, recipient, or prospective purchaser, lessee or recipient of
financial products or services. It shall also refer to any person, natural or juridical, who had
or has a current or prospective financial transaction with a CDARE regulated by the
Authority.

Financial Consumer Protection Assistance Management System (FCPAMS) is a first-
level recourse mechanism for financial consumers who are dissatisfied with a financial
product and/or service. It is aimed at providing free assistance to financial consumers on
their concerns with the financial products, services, and/or transactions with the CDAREs
and/or their third party service providers.

Financial product or service refers to financial products or services which are offered,
developed, and/or marketed by CDAREs which may include, but are not limited to credit,
savings, investment, and other similar products and services. This also includes digital
financial products or services which pertain to the broad range of financial services
accessed and delivered through digital channels.

Inquiry refers to a financial consumer's request for information or clarification regarding
financial products, services, or transactions with a CDARE. Inquiries may include, among
others, understanding financial products and services offered by CDAREs, clarifications
on fees, charges, terms, and conditions, and information on consumer rights and
protection measures.

Request refers to a financial consumer's formal appeal for assistance or action regarding
financial products, services, or transactions with a CDARE. Requests may involve, among
others, guidance on procedures for transactions, complaints, or dispute resolution,
document issuance or retrieval, account-related adjustments, modifications or corrections,
such as updating personal information or revising loan terms, reversal or refund requests
for erroneous transactions, and reconsideration of a financial decision made by a CDARE.

Senior Management refers to the management involved in the financial and/or credit
operations of the cooperative, such as Chief Executive Officer, Chief Operating Officer,
General Manager, Branch Manager, Department Head of Loan
Administration/Operations, or its equivalent.

Simple complaint or request refers to a straightforward concern that can be easily
resolved without requiring in-depth investigation, coordination with external parties, or

product-related inquiries that do not require escalation or technical investigation.
Section 6. Establishment of the CDAREs’ FCPAMS
The FCPAMS must be established by all CDAREs as the first-level recourse mechanism, to
provide free assistance to financial consumers on their concerns about the financial products,
services and/or transactions of the CDAREs and/or their service providers. It shall be
implemented by a Consumer Assistance Team (CAT) in accordance with Section 8(a) of this
Guidelines which shall be commensurate with the size, structure, nature of products and services,
and complexity of operations of the CDAREs, ensuring at all times that there is no conflict of
interest.
In addition, the CDARE shall adopt requirements and procedures for the availment of the
FCPAMS by the financial consumers from receiving, recording, evaluating, resolving, monitoring
and reporting of consumer complaints, requests or inquiries.
Section 7. Role of the Board of Directors and Senior Management

The Board of Directors (BOD) shall be responsible for the delivery of effective recourse to its
financial consumers. Pursuant thereto, the BOD shall:

a. Approve the consumer assistance policies and procedures:

b. Approve risk assessment strategies relating to effective recourse by the consumer:

c. Ensure compliance with consumer assistance policies and procedures:

d. Provide adequate resources devoted to consumer assistance: and

e. Review the consumer assistance policies and procedures at least annually.
The CDARE’s senior management shall be responsible for the implementation and monitoring of
the consumer assistance policies and procedures, including on the submission of Complaints
Report to CDA.
Section 8. Minimum Requirements
The FCPAMS shall have the following minimum requirements:

a. Creation of Consumer Assistance Team


--- PAGE 5 ---

The CDARE shall create or designate a Consumer Assistance Team (CAT) which shall
implement the FCPAMS. The CAT may be an officer, a unit, a group, or a department.
The CAT shall be independent from the CDAREs’ claims handling units.

In case of conflict of interest, the CAT shall be immediately excused from the complaint-
handling process, and the matter shall be reassigned to an independent officer or
committee to ensure fair and unbiased resolution.

The CAT serves as the primary point of contact between the CDARE and financial
consumer regarding financial products and services-related concerns. The CDARE shall
clearly define its roles and responsibilities, which may include the following:

a.1. Receives and logs consumer complaints and request;

a.2. Classifies complaints and requests as Simple or Complex based on their nature;
a.3. Investigates and resolves complaints within the prescribed period:

a.4. Escalates unresolved or complex complaints to higher authorities, if necessary;

a.5. Ensures the confidentiality of consumer information;

a.6. Guides members on the complaint resolution process;

a.7. Documents complaints and prepares reports for regulatory submission;

a.8, Processes consumers’ feedbacks;

a.9. Identifies recurring issues and recommends process improvements;

a.10. Assists members with inquiries on products and services; and

a.11. Ensures compliance with consumer protection laws and regulations.

. Capacity Building for the Consumer Assistance Team

The CAT and third-party agents, if any, must be equipped with knowledge on the structure
and implementation of the CDARE’s FCPAMS. As a minimum, they shall be provided with
periodic trainings on the following:

b.1. CDARE’s corporate structure and financial products and services;

b.2. Interpersonal skills / customer service;

b.3. Basic and advanced listening skills;

b.4. Written and verbal communication skills;

Publication of the FCPAMS

The key features of the FCPAMS shall be published in conspicuous places within the

c.1. Terms and conditions of the CDARE’s products and services:

. Consumer Assistance Mechanisms or Channels

The CDARE shail have a wide range of consumer assistance channels for submitting
complaints, requests or inquiries, including but not limited to walk-in or personal visit,
centralized web portal, mobile application, social media, letter, email, telephone, fax, and
such other channels as may be deemed necessary by the CDARE, or as may be required
by the Authority, to address consumer needs.

The CDARE shall ensure that there is a consumer assistance help desk or hotline
dedicated to addressing consumer inquiries and concerns manned by the CAT.

A sample Complaint, Inquiry, and Request (CIR) Form is attached as Annex A, which may

Consumer Assistance Process and Timelines

The CDARE shall adopt a Consumer Assistance Process which outlines the step-by-step
procedure for receiving, acknowledging, evaluating, and resolving financial consumer
concerns related to financial products and services provided by the CDARE. This ensures
that financial consumers receive fair, transparent, and timely responses to their inquiries,
complaints, and requests.

To ensure efficiency and accountability, the CDARE shall define clear timelines for each
Stage of the process — from the initial acknowledgment to the final resolution — including
the communication of the results to the financial consumer who raised the inquiry,
complaint, or request.

The CDARE must adopt a systematic approach for evaluating the Financial Consumer

|. Manual of Consumer Assistance Policies and Procedures

The CDARE shall develop, maintain, and implement a Manual of Consumer Assistance
Policies and Procedures which contain the internal policies, processes, and practices they
follow in handling complaints, inquiries, and requests from financial consumers.

Section 9. Interface with the Authority

The CDARE shall exhaust all internal remedies available to address the issues raised by the
financial consumers as mentioned in Section 8e. Financial consumers who are dissatisfied with
the CDARE's handling of their complaints, inquiries, and requests may elevate their concerns to
the Authority within seven (7) days from receipt of the communication of the result of the
assessment, investigation, and resolution of their complaint, inquiry, or request by the
cooperative, through the mechanisms provided in MC 2023-14.

The complainant may opt to avail the complaints mechanism or the Consumer Assistance
Management System of the CDA provided in Rule V or may opt to directly proceed to the
adjudication process in Rule VI.

Section 10. Period of Compliance

Pursuant to the Memorandum to CDAREs dated January 8, 2025, full Compliance with RA 11765
and its IRR, which include the establishment of FCPAMS, shall be:

a. Onor before June 30, 2026 for Medium and Large Cooperatives; and
b. On or before December 31, 2026 for Micro and Small Cooperatives

Section 11. Sanction

Failure or non-compliance with this guideline may result in regulatory actions or penalties, after
due process, in accordance with the Rule VII of MC No. 2023-14 or the FCPA IRR.

Sample Confidentiality Clause

All information obtained, processed, and documented during the handling of financial
consumer complaints and requests under the Financial Consumer Protection Assistance
Management System (FCPAMS) shall be treated with strict confidentiality. The CDA-
Regulated Entity (CDARE) shall ensure that the identity, personal information, and transaction
details of the financial consumer are safeguarded and used solely for the purpose of resolving
the complaint or request.

Access to confidential information shall be limited to authorized personnel directly involved in
the assessment, investigation, and resolution of the complaint. Any disclosure of information
to third parties shall require the explicit consent of the financial consumer, except when
disclosure is necessary to comply with legal or regulatory requirements.

The CDARE shall adopt appropriate data privacy and security measures in accordance with
the RA 10173 or the Data Privacy Act of 2012 and its implementing rules and regulations.
Unauthorized disclosure, misuse, or mishandling of confidential information shall be subject
to disciplinary action and applicable penalties under existing laws and cooperative policies.

Sample Statement of No Conflict of Interest

The CDA-Regulated Entity (CDARE) hereby affirms its commitment to uphold the principles

Any personnel found to have a conflict of interest shail be immediately excused from the

committee to ensure fair and unbiased resolution.

The CDARE shall adopt internal policies and procedures to prevent, identify, and manage
conflicts of interest, consistent with consumer protection laws and cooperative governance
standards.
