# MC 2025-10 | Guidelines for Equivalency Certification for Former CDA Officials, Officers, Technical Personnel, and Incumbent and Past Cooperative Officers, Leaders, and Advocates

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-10**
**Series of 2025**

**TO: ALL CONCERNED**

**SUBJECT: GUIDELINES FOR EQUIVALENCY CERTIFICATION FOR FORMER CDA OFFICIALS, OFFICERS, TECHNICAL PERSONNEL, AND INCUMBENT AND PAST COOPERATIVE OFFICERS, LEADERS, AND ADVOCATES, WITH FIVE (5) OR MORE YEARS OF EXPERIENCE IN COOPERATIVE TRAINING**

**Section I. Title.**
This Memorandum Circular shall be titled as, **“Guidelines for Equivalency Certification for Former CDA Officials, Officers, Technical Personnel, and Incumbent and Past Cooperative Officers, Leaders, and Advocates, with Five (5) or More Years of Experience in Cooperative Training”**.

**Section II. Program Description.**
The Equivalency Certification Program by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) acknowledges the expertise of former CDA officials, Officers, Technical Personnel, and incumbent and past cooperative leaders and advocates with over 5 years of experience in cooperative training. It validates their competency in cooperative education, offering formal recognition for their contributions to cooperative development.

Through a meticulous evaluation process, the program assesses applicants’ knowledge and skills, granting certification to those meeting established criteria. This initiative promotes professionalism, enhances the quality of cooperative education, and fosters capacity-building within the cooperative sector, ensuring the continued advancement and sustainability of cooperatives nationwide.

**Section III. Legal Basis.**
1. **Section 4(o) of RA No. 11364** mandates the Authority to “develop and conduct management and training programs that will provide members of cooperatives with the entrepreneurial capabilities, managerial expertise, and technical skills required for efficient operation of the cooperatives and inculcate in them the true spirit of cooperativism...”
2. **Section 4(a) of RA No. 11364**, the Authority is mandated to develop and formulate, in consultation with the cooperative sector and other concerned institutions, appropriate regulations, standards, rules, orders, guidelines, and/or circulars to implement RA No. 11364 and RA No. 9520.

**Section IV. Definition of Terms**
*   **Officers** — The Officers of the cooperative shall include the members of the Board of Directors, members of the different committees created by the General Assembly, General Manager or Chief Executive Officer, Secretary, Treasurer and Members holding other positions as maybe provided for in their By-laws.
*   **Cooperative leaders and advocates** — Individuals who are past and incumbent cooperative leaders and cooperative advocates (except Cooperative Development Officers under MC 2023-12) who guide, support, and promote the cooperative movement.
*   **CDA Officials** — Chairperson, Board Member, Administrator, and Deputy Administrators.
*   **CDA Officers** — Service Directors and Regional Directors.
*   **Technical Personnel** — Division Chief and below from the Head Office and the Regional Offices.
*   **Equivalency Certification** — The formal recognition granted to individuals who have acquired the required knowledge, competencies, and relevant experience in cooperative development as an alternative pathway for recognizing qualifications.

**Section V. Objectives.**
1. **Formal Recognition of Expertise** — To formally acknowledge and certify the expertise of persons covered by this Memorandum who have extensive experience (over 5 years) in cooperative training.
2. **Enhancement of Professional Standards** — To elevate professionalism within the cooperative sector by ensuring that certified individuals meet established criteria of competency.
3. **Promotion of Sustainable Cooperative Development** — To foster capacity-building and continuous improvement within the cooperative sector.

**Section VI. Coverage of Equivalency**
The Equivalency Certification, once granted, shall be valid and recognized nationwide for:
1. **Accreditation as Trainer under a CDA-Accredited Cooperative Training Provider (CTPro)** — Serves as proof of qualification in lieu of required TOT or other prescribed courses.
2. **Inclusion in CDA’s Pool of Recognized Cooperative Trainers** — For individuals engaged with a CDA-Accredited CTPro.

**Section VII. Eligibility Criteria**
*   **Eligible Applicants** — Former CDA officials, officers, and technical personnel; and Cooperative officers, leaders, and advocates with at least five (5) years of relevant experience in conducting required trainings for cooperatives.
*   **Competency Requirement** — Applicants must demonstrate in-depth knowledge, practical experience, and proficiency through documented outputs or verifiable contributions.

**Section VIII. Application Process**
1. **Submission of Application** — Submit a signed formal application and supporting documents to the Regional Director (or Administrator for Head Office personnel).
2. **Processing Fee** — A non-refundable processing fee of Three Thousand Pesos (P3,000.00).
3. **Documentation** — Portfolio of training sessions, instructional materials, certifications of service, and certificates of appreciation as Resource Person.

**Section IX. Grant and Effect of Equivalency Certification**
Awarded certifications are valid for five (5) years. They recognize the holder's expertise as equivalent to standard trainer qualifications outlined in MC No. 2023-11.

**Section X. Continuous Professional Development**
Holders are required to pursue continuous professional development to remain abreast of current legal frameworks and best practices.

**Section XI. Grounds for Revocation**
1. Misrepresentation of Qualifications.
2. Ethical Misconduct (Plagiarism, abuse of authority, etc.).
3. Violation of Laws or Regulations.
4. Incompetence or Substandard Performance.
5. Final Judgment in a case involving Moral Turpitude.

**Section XII. Appeal and Reapplication**
Applicants may reapply or file an appeal/motion for reconsideration within fifteen (15) working days from receipt of the decision.

**Section XIII. Oversight and Monitoring**
The program is overseen by the CDA through its REOs. Certified trainers must submit an annual report online summarizing all cooperative trainings conducted.

**Section XIV. Effectivity**
This Circular shall be effective fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or any newspaper of general circulation and registration with ONAR.
