# MC 2025-05 | GUIDELINES FOR THE ORGANIZATION AND REGISTRATION OF A MEMORIAL SERVICE COOPERATIVE

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2025-05**
**Series of 2025**

**SUBJECT: GUIDELINES FOR THE ORGANIZATION AND REGISTRATION OF A MEMORIAL SERVICE COOPERATIVE AS A SPECIAL TYPE OF COOPERATIVE ORGANIZED AMONG REGISTERED COOPERATIVES**

Pursuant to the powers vested by Republic Act No. 11364 (CDA Charter of 2019) and Republic Act No. 9520 (Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008), the Authority hereby issues these guidelines.

**Section 1. Title.**
This Memorandum Circular shall be known as the **“GUIDELINES FOR THE ORGANIZATION AND REGISTRATION OF A MEMORIAL SERVICE COOPERATIVE AS A SPECIAL TYPE OF COOPERATIVE ORGANIZED AMONG REGISTERED COOPERATIVES.”**

**Section 2. Policy.**
It is the declared policy of the State to foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as a practical vehicle for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people's power towards the attainment of economic development and social justice.

**Section 3. Purpose.**
To provide for the orderly organization and registration of Memorial Service Cooperatives as a special type of cooperative.

**Section 4. Legal Bases.**
*   **Art. 23, RA 9520** — Defines types of cooperatives, including service cooperatives and "other types as may be determined by the Authority."
*   **Sec. 4(a), RA 11364** — Authority to develop and formulate regulations, standards, and guidelines.

**Section 5. Coverage.**
Covers the organization and registration of proposed Memorial Service Cooperatives, including those currently engaged in memorial service activities.

**Section 6. Definition.**
*   **Memorial Service** — Activities related to honoring the deceased, including interment (burial/funeral rites), cremation, and related services (excluding pre-need plans).
*   **Memorial Service Cooperative** — A special type of cooperative engaged in memorial service activities organized among registered cooperatives.

**Section 7. Registration.**
1. **Name** — Must include the type “Memorial Service” and the word “Cooperative” or its equivalent. Prohibited terms include "integrated," "incorporated," "federation," or "union" (unless part of the parent association's name).
2. **Membership** — Organized with at least **fifteen (15) member cooperatives** of all types/categories (except unions).
3. **Area of Operation** — May be municipal/city, provincial, regional, or national.
4. **Capital Requirements** — The minimum paid-up share capital requirement shall be **Php5,000,000** or as provided in the Business Plan, whichever is higher.
5. **Bonding Requirements** — Accountable officers (Chairperson, Treasurer, and Manager) must be bonded.

**Section 7.6 Registration Requirements.**
Applicants must submit:
1. Notarized Articles of Cooperation;
2. By-laws;
3. Economic Survey;
4. Business Plan;
5. Treasurer's Affidavit;
6. Surety Bond of Accountable Officers;
7. GA Resolution from each member cooperative;
8. Board Resolution appointing a representative.

**Section 9. Voting System.**
Each member cooperative shall have one (1) basic vote and as many incentive votes as provided in the by-laws, but not more than **five (5) votes**.

**Section 11. Prohibitions.**
Memorial Service Cooperatives are prohibited from engaging in any business activity unrelated to memorial services.

**Section 12 & 13. Primary Cooperatives.**
Primary cooperatives with existing or planned memorial services are exempt if they extend services **exclusively to their members and immediate family members**, subject to ACBL amendments.

**Section 16. Transitory Provision.**
Existing federations engaged in memorial services are given **one (1) year** to separate and register their memorial business activities as a separate Memorial Service Cooperative.

**Section 18. Effectivity.**
This Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in the Official Gazette and filing with ONAR.
