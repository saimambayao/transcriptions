Section 1. LEGAL BASIS

1.1. The Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008 (RA 9520), Article 2 declares the policy of
the state to foster the creation and growth of cooperatives as practical vehicle for
promoting self-reliance and harnessing people power towards the attainment of
economic development, equity and social justice;

1.2 RANo. 11364 or the Cooperative Development Charter of 2019

a. Section 2, paragraph 4, Declaration of Policy - The government and all its
branches, subdivisions, instrumentalities and agencies shall continue to provide
technical guidance, financial assistance and other services to enable the
cooperatives to develop into viable and responsive economic enterprises towards
a strong cooperative movement, free from condition which infringes upon the
objective and character of cooperatives.

b. Section 4. Powers, Functions and Responsibilities. -The Authority shall have,
among others, the following powers and functions:

b.1.Formulate, adopt and implement integrated and comprehensive plans and
programs on cooperative development consistent with the national policy on
cooperatives and establish an integrated framework on cooperative development
for all government agencies (Section 4 (b)); and

b.2. Administer all grants and donations exclusively intended for cooperatives
coursed through the Authority for cooperative development, without prejudice to the
right of cooperatives to directly receive and administer such grants and donations
upon agreement with the grantors and donors thereof (Section 4 (m)).

1.3. Section 2 of Commission on Audit (COA) Circular No. 2012-001 dated June 14, 2012.

Section 2. WHO MAY AVAIL THE PROGRAM

Only duly registered cooperatives with the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA)
and cooperatives accredited by the CDA may avail the program without prejudice to other
documentary requirements prescribed by existing government auditing and accounting rules
and regulations designed for a project design/proposal or its equivalent as may thereafter be
approved by the CDA Board of Directors (BODs). Provided that, these cooperatives have no
unliquidated financial assistance with the CDA at the time of application.

Section 3. PURPOSE OF THE FUND ASSISTANCE

All funds, grants or other financial assistance granted pursuant to this Guidelines shall
only be utilized for purposes of maintenance and expansion of projects, development or start-
up capital of the receiving cooperative. No portion of which shall be used for any of the
prohibited items enumerated under Section 9 of this Guidelines.

6.3. Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)?

The MOA shall incorporate the following terms and provisions:

Project name, intended beneficiaries, benefits to be delivered, project cost
estimates, brief description of the project and its site or location;

Systems and procedures to implement the project such as, but not limited to,
procurement of goods and services by the NGO/PO and their distribution
which should be documented and coordinated with the GO's authorized
officials and the respective barangays;

Time schedules for the release of funds, periodic inspection/evaluation,
reporting monitoring requirements, date of commencement and date of
completion;

Submission of the required periodic financial and physical status reports;
Specific period to liquidate the funds granted;

In case of construction projects, the cooperative shall conduct simple bidding
or canvass to ensure the best terms and quality of purchase;

Monitoring and inspection of project implementation and verification of
financial records and reports by CDA;

Visitorial audit by the officials and personnel of COA authorized to perform
the audit;

Institution of legal action by CDA against the defaulting cooperative which
fails to complete a project covered by the MOA;

In case of the dissolution of the recipient cooperative, voluntary or involuntary,
the lien of CDA on its assets, in accordance with existing laws, to the extent
of the unexpended or unutilized portion of the fund;

Maintenance of the cooperative of a separate savings/current account for
each fund received from CDA; and

The return by the cooperative to CDA of any amount not utilized to complete
the project, including interest, if any.

6.3.1. On the requirement for separate savings/current account, this shall be

based on the amount of financial assistance received, to wit:

- For financial assistance of PhP100,000.00 and below, the cooperative
can deposit the amount to their existing bank account and no need to
open a separate savings/current account, however a separate
subsidiary ledger for the fund shall be maintained;

* For financial assistance of more than PhP100,000.00, a separate
savings/current account and subsidiary ledger shall be maintained;

Any additional requirements shall be reduced into writing either through a
Memorandum or similar CDA issuances.

Section 8. MONITORING

For effective and efficient monitoring of the project, the CDA Regional Extension Office
shall undertake the-following to ensure proper utilization of funds and completion of projects
that are the subject of financial assistance/grant:

8.1.

8.2.

8.3.

8.4.

Require the cooperative to submit periodic progress reports until the project
completion, as indicated in the MOA, to the CDA Regional Office having
jurisdiction over them.

The Cooperative Development Specialist (CDS), or a holder of an equivalent
position, assigned in the area shall be responsible in collating and validating the
veracity of the progress reports submitted by the cooperative vis-a-vis the
approved project proposal and provisions of the MOA. Any deviation in the use
of funds of the project shall be reported immediately.

The cooperative shall submit a terminal report and Certificate of Completion after
the completion of the project. After receiving proof of the completion of the project,
the CDA Regional Extension Office having jurisdiction over the cooperative shall
issue a Certificate of Acceptance to the cooperative beneficiary; and

The CDA Regional Extension Office shall submit the status of fund releases and
monitoring report/s to the CDA Head Office.

Any additional requirements shall be reduced into writing either through a

Memorandum or similar issuances.

Section 10. SANCTIONS

The following sanctions may be imposed for any violation of the provisions of this
Guidelines and the MOA:

10.1.

10.2.

10.3.

10.4.

as disallowed amounts after financial audits:

initiate appropriate actions against the cooperative for material violations of the
provisions of the MOA;

Reserves the right to initiate administrative/civil/criminal actions against any
Director, Officer, or employee of the cooperative for any violation of any
provision of the MOA.

All cooperatives found to have violated these Guidelines and/or other similar
issuances (or may thereafter be issued) by the Authority, pertaining to grant of
financial assistance, shall be disqualified from any future grants or financial
assistance coursed through the CDA.

Section 11. EFFECTIVITY

This Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days following the completion of its publication
in the Official Gazette or a newspaper in general circulation, and the filing of the copy thereof
with the Office of National Administrative Register (ONAR).

Section 12. REPEALING CLAUSE

Financial Assistance/Grant to Micro Cooperatives Affected by Calamities and Disasters, and
Guidelines on the Release of Financial Assistance/Grant to Micro and Small Cooperatives for
Recovery and Alleviation, respectively, are hereby repealed.
