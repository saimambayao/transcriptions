Pursuant to Section 6, Article XII, of the 1987 Constitution, Article 26 (3) of RA 9520
and Rule 6 of the Revised Rules and Regulations Implementing Certain and Special
Provisions of RA 9520 and Sec. 4 (e) of RA 11364, the Authority hereby prescribes
the following amended Guidelines governing laboratory cooperatives.

Section 1. Title.

*

This Memorandum shall be known as the "Amended Guidelines for the Creation, _
Organization, Supervision, and Monitoring of Laboratory Cooperatives."

Section 2. Statement of Policy and Objectives

The Cooperative Development Authority (CDA) is statutorily mandated to promote the
viability and growth of cooperatives as an instrument of social justice and economic
development in pursuance of Section 15, Article XII of the 1987 Philippine Constitution.

In line with the foregoing policy, the Authority recognizes the valuable contributions of
Guardian Cooperatives in the socio-economic development of minors through the
organization of Laboratory Cooperatives and to prepare them for membership in
regular cooperatives; hence, this Guidelines.

Section 3. Scope and Coverage

This Guidelines shall apply to all Laboratory Cooperatives recognized by the Authority
under RA 9520 and its Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations.

Section 5. Jurisdiction

All applications for the recognition of laboratory cooperatives shall be filed with the
Extension Office, which has jurisdiction over the Guardian Cooperative.

Section 6. Organization of Laboratory Cooperative

A guardian cooperative may assist in organizing a laboratory cooperative of fifteen (15)
or more minors, including students or out-of-school minors, who are Filipino Citizens.
The prospective laboratory cooperative shall be affiliated with a guardian cooperative
within or nearest their area of operation.

Section 7. Purposes of Laboratory Cooperatives

A laboratory cooperative shall be organized for any or all of the following purposes:

1. To serve as the training ground for its members to prepare them for membership
in regular cooperatives through activities such as but not limited to the following:

a) Holding of parliamentary affairs; |
b) Visiting credit or financial service cooperatives, banks, and other
educational trips.

2. To teach the values of thrift and savings mobilization among its members.

3. To instill cooperative values, principles, financial discipline,
business/entrepreneurial skills, and leadership skills among its members.

4. To promote and advocate Filipino social and cultural values, financial, education,
ecological awareness, and sustainable deveiopment.

Section 8. Affiliation

A Laboratory Cooperative shall! be affiliated with a duly registered cooperative, the

Guardian Cooperative, whose articles of cooperation and by-laws shall provide

acceptance of the responsibilities as a guardian cooperative.

A Laboratory Cooperative primarily composed of students from a particular school may .

affiliate with the school's cooperative, if any, or select a cooperative within its area of
operation.

If the laboratory cooperative is composed primarily of out-of-school minors, it shall be
affiliated with a cooperative within or nearest its area of operation.

Section 9. Documentary Requirements for the Recognition of Laboratory
Cooperative :

The Guardian Cooperative shall submit the following documentary requirements to the
Authority for the issuance of a Certificate of Recognition:

1. Resolution of the Board of Directors of the Guardian Cooperative accepting its
responsibility and liability as Guardian of the Laboratory Cooperative.

2. Manual of Operations as specified in Section 11.

Section 12. Name of Laboratory Cooperative

The name of the Laboratory Cooperative shall include “Laboratory Cooperative of
(Name of Guardian Cooperative)."

Section 13. Issuance of Certificate of Recognition

The Authority shall issue a Certificate of Recognition within the period prescribed in
the citizen's charter upon compliance with all the requirements set forth in these
Guidelines. It serves as conclusive evidence that the Authority duly recognizes the
Laboratory Cooperative as an affiliate of the Guardian Cooperative, which shall remain
valid unless such recognition has been revoked or canceled earlier.

Issuing the Certificate of Recognition does not bestow upon a Laboratory Cooperative
a juridical personality. |

Section 14. Duties and Responsibilities of the Guardian Cooperative

The following shall be the duties and responsibilities of the Guardian Cooperative over
the Laboratory Cooperative:

1. Formulate, adopt, implement, and monitor the programs, plans, and activities
appropriate for the education and training of the Laboratory Cooperative
members concerning the Laboratory Cooperative’s purposes, as specified
under Section 7:

2. Supervise the conduct of Officers and General Assembly meetings of the
Laboratory Cooperative;

3. Represent the Laboratory Cooperative in its dealings and transactions with third
parties when the capacity to contract is required;

4, Formulate, adopt, and implement internal control policies and procedures on
the funds, savings, and deposits of the Laboratory Cooperative;

5. Prepare, keep, and maintain the list of members of the Laboratory Cooperative;

6. Submit an annual report on the activities and economic operations of the
Laboratory Cooperative;

7. Maintain separate subsidiary ledgers, passbooks of members, financial
statements, and other accountable forms; and

8. Adopt and implement a systematic procedure in the collection of savings and
deposits.

Section 15. Liability of the Guardian Cooperative

The Guardian Cooperative shall be liable for any violations committed in the operation
of the Laboratory Cooperative.

Section 16. Election of Officers of the Laboratory Cooperative

The set of officers of the Laboratory Cooperative shall be in accordance with the
organizational structure of the Laboratory Cooperative, as prescribed by the Guardian
Cooperative. The Guardian Cooperative shall prescribe the election guidelines
governing the election of officers.

Section 17. Termination of Membership

The following shall be the causes for termination of membership in a laboratory -

cooperative:

1. Upon reaching the age of eighteen (18) years old;

2. Such other causes may be provided for in the By-Laws of the Guardian

Cooperative and the Manual of Operations for the Laboratory Cooperative;

Section 18. Option of Member who Reaches the Age of Majority
Any member who reaches the age of eighteen years (18) old has the option to join
the Guardian Cooperative by signifying their intention to become a member upon
compliance with all the requirements for membership.
Section 19. Supervision by a Guardian Cooperative
A Guardian Cooperative may supervise one (1) or more laboratory cooperatives.

Section 20. Effect of Dissolution of Guardian Cooperative.

The dissolution of the Guardian Cooperative shall result in the revocation of the
Certificate of Recognition of the Laboratory Cooperative. However, in such a case, the

Section 21. Separability Clause

If any provisions of these guidelines are declared null and void or unconstitutional, the
other provisions not affected shall continue to be in force and effect.

Section 22. Repeal

All previous circulars and guidelines issued by the Authority which are inconsistent

These amended guidelines shall take effect after fifteen (15) days following the
