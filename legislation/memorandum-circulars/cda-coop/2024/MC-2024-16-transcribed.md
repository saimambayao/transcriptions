# MC 2024-16 | REITERATION OF PROHIBITIONS FOR COOPERATIVE OFFICERS

**MEMORANDUM CIRCULAR NO. 2024-16**
**Series of 2024**

**SUBJECT: REITERATION OF THE PROHIBITIONS UNDER ARTICLES 45, 48 AND 49 OF REPUBLIC ACT NO. 9520**

This Circular serves as guidance for cooperative officers to uphold the best interests of their members and refrain from using cooperative resources or their positions for personal gain or to benefit competing business interests.

**SECTION 2. LEGAL BASES (RA 9520)**
*   **Article 45 (Liability)**: Officers who willfully assent to unlawful acts or act in bad faith are **jointly and severally liable** for damages. They are accountable for **double the profits** obtained in violation of their duty.
*   **Article 48 (Disloyalty)**: A director who grabs an opportunity belonging to the cooperative is liable for **double the profits**, unless ratified by a 3/4 vote of the general assembly.
*   **Article 49 (Confidential Information)**: Illegal use of confidential info for benefit makes the officer liable for direct losses and benefits received.
*   **Article 140 (Penal Provisions)**: Violations of Arts. 45, 48, and 49 carry a fine of **P5,000.00 to P500,000.00** and/or **imprisonment of 5 to 10 years**.

**SECTION 4. PROHIBITIONS**
1. **Competing Businesses**: Officers and members are NOT allowed to own or be a party to another company that engages in the same business as the cooperative.
2. **Resource Use**: Cooperative money, personnel, vehicles, equipment, and software CANNOT be used to support a competing business entity.
3. **Grave Offense**: These acts are classified as grave offenses under the CDA Omnibus Rules, leading to administrative removal or suspension.

**SECTION 6. MONITORING AND ANNUAL OATH**
*   The CDA will maintain a database to monitor implementation.
*   **Mandatory Requirement**: All Board Directors and Officers must submit a **Sworn Statement (Annex A)** along with the Cooperative Annual Progress Report (CAPR) affirming their adherence to these provisions.
