A CLARIFICATORY STATEMENT ON THE APEX ORGANIZATIONS

In response to a call from the representatives of the PFCCO,
NATCCO, FFFCI, NAMVESCCO, KKPPI, TUCP and the PHILRECA for the
recall of CDA Board Resolution No: 310, S-1995, the Cooperative
Development Authority has remained Gonsistent with its mandate
for the development and growth of cooperatives, of whatever type
and nature. Such is buttressed by the fact that the said Board
Resolution is well within the bounds of the Cooperative Code of
the Philippines.

.

1. The Board Resolution was initiated through a
request from the Cooperative Union of the
Philippines (CUP) to be named as the apex
organization of the cooperative unions. Citing
the fact that no other union of cooperatives has
been registered with the Authority, such
Reselution serves only to further clarify and
amplify the provisions of law, specifically
Article €&7 (2) (a), wherein it is stated that
“fCajn apex organization maybe a federation or a
union." such as it was worded, the Resolution
called it as the National Apex Union of

Cooperatives, implying that it is only the apex

organization of cooperative unions and its
members.
2. The. Authority is not and has not violated the

principle of subsidiarity as enshrined in the
Cooperative Code neither of the constitutional
freedom of association. Nowhere within the
context »- of the Resolution are cooperatives
mandated to become members of the Cooperative
Union of the Philippines (CUP). The authority has
also stood pat on its stand that cooperatives,
mnowing best how to handle their affairs, shoauld
decide for themselves whether or not to goin a
particular cooperative organization apr not. it
dust so happens that the members of the CUP have
decided on its being their apex organization and
have decided to remit the fifty per cent (50 %) to
-the CUP. Other cooperatives, whether primary or
secondary, are free to organize their own apex
organizations without»any restrictions from the
Authority - so long as the proper requirements for
registration are met.

The collection of the CETF is a matter which
is primarily within the purview of the apex
organization and the Authority has not mandated
any turn-over of CETF to the CUP. The use of the
statutory reserves has already been pravided in
the Cooperative Code. The Authcority has merely
clarified the fact that such should be remmitted
to the proper apex organization, whether a union
or a federation. This is mere implementation of
the provisions of law. -° .

The Authority has accepted the fact that other
numerous organizations have sprung up —- each being
responsible for the collection of CETF from its
members. The CUP, as with any other highest level
cooperative oarganization, is responsible for the
collections and use of CETF funds from its
members. A member should and always will defer to
the rules adopted by its own organization on the
use and collection of the CETF. Thus, for each
apex organization of cooperatives, the Authority
may also certify as toa their being apex
organizations for this purpose as to their members
only in the same manner as that of CUP’ s
certifications, albeit under Article 24 of the
Coaperative Code. .

The Authority has never refused its services to
any cooperative organization. The Agency stands
well within its mandate as to the promotion = and
growth of coopertaives. The Authority firmly
recognizes that numérours apex organizations have
sprung up through disaffiliation. Through several
consultationsa with the national federations, the
