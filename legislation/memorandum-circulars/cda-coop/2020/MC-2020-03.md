SUBJECT : REGULATORY RELIEF FOR COOPERATIVES IN VIEW OF
PROCLAMATION NO. 922

Pursuant to Article 53 of R.A. No. 9520 and Rule 8, Section 6 of its
Implementing Rules and Regulations and considering the issuance by
President Rodrigo R. Duterte, on March 8, 2020, of Proclamation No. 922
declaring a State of Public Health Emergency Throughout the Philippines

due to the outbreak of Covid-19, the following guidelines are issued to

govern the availment of regulatory relief by cooperatives:

Section 1. TITLE. This Circular shall be known as the “REGULATORY
RELIEF FOR COOPERATIVES IN VIEW OF PROCLAMATION NO. 922”.

Section 2. COVERAGE. This Circular covers all cooperatives registered
with the Authority.

Section 3. REGULATORY RELIEF.

In the light of the above-stated Proclamation No. 922, cooperatives are
hereby allowed to postpone their scheduled general assembly meeting and
election of officers for a period of three (3) months from the effectivity of this
Circular or until such time that State of Public Health Emergency is lifted,
whichever comes first. Incumbent officers shall be on hold-over capacity
until such time that a general assembly meeting and election of officers are
conducted. Pending the conduct of general assembly meeting, the
cooperative shall operate on a reenacted budget.

Cooperatives which opt to postpone their general assembly meeting and
election of officers under this Circular shall be relieved from the following:

1. Conduct of general assembly meeting and election;
2. Conduct of community programs or activities involving a large crowd;
3. Findings of CDA for the following violations:

a. Non-presentation to the general assembly of the required
reports;
b. Non-approval of the development/annual plan.

Section 4. REQUIREMENTS. The following are the requirements to be
submitted to the Authority for the availment of regulatory relief: ,

1. Letter informing CDA Central Office/Extension Office concerned of the
postponement of the General Assembly, signed by the Chairperson
and/or the Chief Executive Officer/General Manager of the
cooperative; '

2. Board Resolution requesting availment of the regulatory relief.

Section 5. SUBMISSION OF REQUIRED REPORTS AND ISSUANCE OF
CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE (COC).

The required reports shall still be submitted to the Authority within one
hundred twenty (120) days after the close of the calendar year for the
issuance of Certificate of Compliance (COC). The reports as submitted shall
be posted in a conspicuous place in the premises of the cooperative.

In case the postponed general assembly meeting will later on be conducted,
the Authority shall be informed of any changes in the submitted reports
within five (5) days after the conduct of the said general assembly.

Section 6. APPLICABILITY PERIOD. The availment of regulatory relief
shall cover ONLY the operations of the cooperatives for calendar year 2019.

Section 7. INFORMATION DISSEMINATION. All concerned CDA
Extension Offices must endeavor to inform and disseminate this Circular to
cooperatives within their jurisdiction.

Section 8. EFFECTIVITY. This Memorandum Circular shall take effect .
immediately after its publication with the Office of National Administrative
Register (ONAR).
