i

Pursuant to Resolution No. 324, S-2020, dated November 5, 2020 of the CDA
Board of Directors, in connection with the recent calamities brought about by
Typhoon Quinta and Super Typhoon Rolly which is considered as the
strongest tropical cyclone observed worldwide in 2020. These typhoons left
devastating effects in the different areas including the province of
Catanduanes, Bicol Region and Southern Luzon.

In this connection, all capable and able cooperatives are strongly encouraged

to show the spirit of compassion and the practice of the 7 Cooperative

Principle which is showing their utmost Concern for the Community through

the conduct of the following:

1. Utilize their Community Development Fund (CDF), which under Article 86
(3) of R.A. No. 9520, shall not be less than three per centum (3%) of their
net surplus, for projects or activities that will benefit the community where
the cooperative operates, or any other allowable funds of the cooperative
that can be used to provide assistance to their beneficiaries;

2. Provide the necessary assistance including but not limited to provision of
food packs water, vitamins, clothes and blankets, alcohol, soap and other
basic necessities that may be distributed to those community members .
who are in dire need of help but with adherence to safety protocols, and
ensuring that the procurement of these goods will not shortchange the
members; and

3. Coordinate with other cooperatives to pool in resources to increase the
scope of assistance to be provided to the highly affected areas.

All CDA Regional Offices must endeavor to inform and disseminate this
Circular to cooperatives within their jurisdiction.
