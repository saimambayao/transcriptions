3. Videoconferencing shall refer to a conference between two or more participants
at different sites by using computer networks to transmit audio and video data.
For example, a point-to-point (two-person) video conferencing system works
much like a video telephone. Each participant has a video camera, microphone,
and speakers mounted on his or her computer. As the two participants speak
to one another, their voices are carried over the network and delivered to the
other's speakers, and whatever images appear in front of the video camera
appear in a window on the other participant's monitor.1
