SUBJECT : POLICY GUIDELINES MANDATING COOPERATIVES
TO PUT-UP SIGNAGE IN THEIR RESPECTIVE
OFFICES

DATE : June 11, 2012

Pursuant to the powers vested to the Authority by Republic Act No. 6939 and the
provision of Article 5lof RA 9520, the Cooperative Development Authority hereby
promulgates the following guidelines to facilitate the in identification of the
locations of cooperatives.

Section 1. Title.

This guideline shall be known as “Policy Guidelines Mandating
Cooperatives to put-up Signage in their Respective Offices’.

Section 2. Policy.

It is the declared policy of the State to foster the creation and growth of
cooperatives as practical vehicle for promoting self-reliance and harnessing people
power towards the attainment of economic development and social justice.

Section 3. Purpose

These guidelines are issued for the purpose of helping the public in locating
the cooperative. —

Section 4. Legal Basis.

The legal framework of this guideline is anchored on the following provisions
of law:

Article 14 (2) (d)
The articles of cooperation shall set forth:

(d) The area of operation and the postal address of its principal office.

Article 51 Address

“Every cooperative shall have an official address to which all notices and
communications shall be sent. Such address and every change thereof shall be
registered with the Authority.”

Section 5. Coverage.

This circular shall apply to all cooperatives registered with the Authority.
Section 6. Definition of Terms.

As used in this circular, the following terms shall be defined as:

(a) Cooperative - shall refer to an autonomous and duly registered association
of persons with common bond of interest , who have voluntarily joined
together to achieved their social, economic and cultural needs and
aspirations by making equitable contribution to the capital required,
patronizing their products and services and accepting a fair share of the
risk and benefits of the undertaking in accordance with universally
accepted cooperative principles.

(b) Area of operation — shall refer to the place/area where the cooperative
members come from as provided for in their articles of cooperation and
bylaws; ;

(c) Signage — shall refer to a prescribed design used for identification or as
means of giving direction;

(d) Authority — refers to the Cooperative Development Authority;

(ec) Registration -— is the operative act of the Authority granting juridical
personality to a proposed cooperative and is evidenced by the Certificate of
Registration;

Section 7. SIGNAGE SPECIFICATION

Cooperatives are required to put up a signage at the place of its official
address which shall be posted in front of the cooperative’s principal office, branch,
and satellite office.

It shall be printed on a panaflex/tarpaulin/billboard on a landscape mode,
with a minimum size of 6ft wide x 3ft long, on a white background, containing at
least the following information:

a) Name of the Cooperative;
b) Address of the Cooperative;

Section 8. Transitory Provisions.

All registered cooperatives are given one (1) year to comply upon the
effectivity of these guidelines.

For new cooperatives registered after the effectivity of these guidelines shall
adhere to the signage requirement before the commencement of their business
operation.

Section 9. Effectivity.

These guidelines shall take effect upon the approval of the CDA Board of
Administrators and fifteen (15) days after filing with the Office of National
Administrative Registry (ONAR).

Section 10. Separability Clause.
if any provision of these guidelines is declared null and void or

unconstitutional, the other provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in
force and effect.
