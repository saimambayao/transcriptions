SUBJECT : NEW GUIDELINES IN THE ACCREDITATION OF COOPERATIVE
EXTERNAL AUDITORS

Pursuant to the rule-making power of the Authority as prescribed under Section 4(a) of RA No.
11364 otherwise known as the Cooperative Development Authority Charter of 2019 and Article 80 of
Republic Act 9520, otherwise known as the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008, the following
guidelines are issued to govern the accreditation of cooperative external auditors:

SECTION 1. Definition of Terms

ACCREDITATION- refers to the act of giving official authorization or approval to a qualified
auditor to conduct financial audits to cooperatives.

ACCOUNTING SERVICES - refers to the bookkeeping of cooperative’s financial transactions
and preparation of financial statement

AUDIT SERVICES - refers to the audit of annual financial statements to be able to form an
opinion on the Cooperative's financial statements and issue a report

AUDITING FIRM - refers to an entity managed either by a sole practitioner or a partnership
providing audit services to cooperatives.

AUDITOR-IN-CHARGE - refers to the team leader of the audit engagement.

AUDIT ENGAGEMENT LETTER - refers to a letter that informs the cooperative of an upcoming
audit. It details the audit objectives, the timeline, and the audit team members. It also
covers the pre-audit meeting, expected deliverables, and the audit team’s mission.

AUTHORITY -— refers to the Cooperative Development Authority, the only government agency
granting juridical personality to cooperatives.

COOPERATIVE EXTERNAL AUDITOR (CEA) — refers to an independent Certified Public
Accountant (CPA) and partnership firm accredited by the CDA, whose relationship to
the client is to express an opinion on the financial statements.

FRAUD -— refers to an intentional act by one or more individuals among management,
employees, or third parties that result in a misrepresentation of financial statements. It
may involve:

* Manipulation, falsification or alteration of records or documents.

* Misappropriation of assets.

. Suppression or omission of the effects of transactions from records or documents.
* Recording of transactions without substance.

* Intentional misapplication of accounting policies.

« Omission of material information

* — And any other instance/factor which may be classified as fraud.

PARTNER - refers to all members of a partnership but not necessarily conducting audits to
cooperatives.

SECTION 4. Accreditation of Signing Partner

The signing partner of a firm/partnership shall be separately accredited by the Authority. In case
the signing partner has an existing accreditation as an individual /sole practitioner, the certificate of
accreditation shall be surrendered and be replaced by a certificate of accreditation as a signing partner.
The validity of accreditation of the new certificate shall be the remaining validity period of the certificate
of the partnership firm. In no case shall a signing partner be allowed to sign an audit report when the
firm's accreditation is not valid.

In case a signing partner resigns in the firm, he/she can be accredited as an individual CEA
after submission of a valid PRC BOA accreditation and the original CDA Certificate of Accreditation
issued as a signing partner. In such case, a new certificate will be issued and the validity period of such
certificate shall be the remaining period of the certificate of accreditation as a signing partner.

SECTION 5. Training Requirements

The training requirements for the Accreditation of CEAs shall be equivalent to a minimum of 24
hours of training, both for initial and renewal.

These trainings shall be conducted by the PICPA in coordination with the Authority in
accordance with the prescribed training course outlines attached in this Circular (Annex 2.1 and 2.2).

The PICPA Chapter in coordination with the PICPA National may conduct the training for the

The Certificate of Training shall be valid for five (5) years from the date of issue and can only
be used once by the applicant. It cannot be used in the succeeding applications.

SECTION 6. Accrediting Body
The application for the accreditation of CEA shall be approved by the CDA Board of Directors
(CDA-BOD) upon recommendation of the Deputy Administrator - Registration, Supervision and
Examination Services (RSES).
SECTION 7. Validity of Accreditation
The accreditation of a cooperative external auditor shall be valid for a period of five (5) years
from the date of issuance unless earlier revoked and/or cancelled, provided that the validity of the PRC
ID, PRC-BOA Accreditation and Certificate of Good Standing with the PICPA are still valid.
The 5-year validity shall apply only to all succeeding applications for both initial and renewal
after the effectivity of this guidelines.

Partnership or Firm — Six Thousand Pesos (P6,000.00)

No application shall be acted upon without corresponding payment of application fee. All
application fees paid shall be non-refundable.
SECTION 9. Audit Coverage

CEAs may accept audit engagement from cooperatives duly registered with the Authority.

SECTION 10. Procedures for Accreditation
Step 1. Application for Accreditation
The applicant shall submit the complete documentary requirements as prescribed in Section 3

and Section 4 of this Guidelines through the Accreditation Facility Information System (AFIS) which can
be accessed at the CDA website (www.cda.gov.ph) and shall pay the application fee.

Step 2. Evaluation of Application/Renewal

The CDA Extension Office shall initially evaluate the submitted documents. If found complete,
the CDA Extension Office will endorse the application to the CDA Head Office. In case of findings, the
applicant shall be notified through email to comply with the findings.

The CDA-Head Office shall review the submitted documents. In case there are findings, the
applicant shall be notified through email to comply with the findings. In case of denial or disapproval of
the application, the applicant shall likewise be notified through email.

If found complete, the Registration, Supervision and Examination Services shall recommend for
the approval of the accreditation to the CDA Board of Directors.

Step 3. Oath Taking and Issuance of CEA Accreditation Certificate

The applicant shall be informed of the approval of its/his/her application and the schedule of the
Oath Taking. The applicant shall take his/her oath to be administered by the Authority. The Certificate
of accreditation shall be released upon submission of the signed Pledge of Commitment.

SECTION 11. Conditions for the Engagement of a Cooperative External Auditor

a) No external auditor shall conduct audit to a cooperative if he/she or any member of his/her
immediate family is related to the officers or management staff of the cooperative up to 4th
degree of consanguinity or affinity, or if his/her independence is considered impaired under
the circumstances specified in the Code of Professional Ethics for Certified Public
Accountants. In the case of a partnership, this limitation shall apply to all the partners,
associates, and the auditor-in-charge including the members of their immediate family up to
Ath degree of consanguinity or affinity.

b) There shall be no outstanding loans or any credit accommodation of the external auditor,
his/her immediate family and the members of the audit team with the cooperative at the time
of the signing of the engagement and during the engagement. In the case of partnership, this
prohibition shall apply to all the partners and the auditor-in-charge.

c) The external auditor must not be currently engaged nor was engaged during the past 3 years
in providing the following services to the Cooperative:

i. Internal audit functions;

ii. Information systems design, implementation, and assessment;

iii. accounting service; and

iv. Such other services, which could affect his/her independence as, may be determined
by the CDA.

d) The external auditor, auditor-in-charge, and members of the audit team must adhere to the
highest standards of professional conduct and shall carry out services in accordance with
relevant ethical, auditing and reporting standards, such as the Code of Professional Ethics for
Certified Public Accountants; Philippine Standards on Auditing (PSA); and the Philippine
Financial Reporting Framework for Cooperatives;

e) For the immediate past three (3) years, the external auditor/signing partners must not have

held a position relevant to the conduct of audit such as, but not limited to, Chief Executive
Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Comptroller, Accountant, Bookkeeper, Audit Committee, etc;

f) For the immediate past three (3) years, the external auditor/signing partners must not have
been a member/officer of the cooperative or employee/officer of a federation/union to which
the cooperative being audited is affiliated;

g) The external auditor shall undertake the audit of the concerned cooperative for a maximum of
five (5) consecutive years. In case of partnership, the five (5) year limitation shall be applicable
to the signing partner/s. Rotation among the signing partners is allowed, provided the firm's
accreditation and his/her accreditation as signing partner are valid. Provided that he/she
may be allowed to audit the same client after a rest period or cooling off of two (2) years.

SECTION 12. Audit or Accounting Services To Micro Cooperatives

The external auditor, including all signing partners, shall provide audit or accounting
services which includes bookkeeping and preparation of the financial statements free of charge to
at least one (1) micro cooperative during the validity of his/her accreditation. An external auditor
cannot audit a cooperative which was previously provided with any accounting services.

For this purpose, the Authority shall provide all accredited cooperative external auditors
with the list of micro cooperatives.

SECTION 13. Preparation of Audit Reports and Disclosure of Adverse Findings
a) The external auditor shall prepare the independent audit report with the following attachments:

a.1) Standard presentation of the audited financial statements with comparative figures of
the immediately preceding year, which will include the following:

Statements of Financial Condition

Statements of Operations

Statements of Cash Flows

Statements of Changes in Equity (to include changes in Statutory Funds)
Notes to Financial Statements

a.2) Statement of Management Responsibility
a.3) Statement of representation of the external auditor to the Authority
b) Disclosure of external auditor's adverse findings
b.1. To enable timely and appropriate remedial action, the accredited auditing firm must
report to the CDA Extension Office having jurisdiction over the cooperative being

audited, within thirty (30) calendar days after discovery, the following cases:

b.1.1. Any material findings involving fraud or dishonesty including cases that were
resolved during the period of audit;

b.1.2. Any potential losses the aggregate of which amounts to at least five percent
(5%) of the total asset; and

b.1.3. Insufficiency of the cooperative’s assets to cover claims of creditors.

b.2. The accredited external auditor shall report directly to the CDA EO where the
cooperative being audited is registered, within fifteen (15) calendar days after the
occurrence of the following:

b.2.1._ Termination or resignation as external auditor, prior to the completion of audit
engagement stating the reason thereof;

b.2.2. Discovery of a material breach of cooperative laws or these rules and
regulations such as, but not limited to;

b.2.2.1. Net worth to risk assets ratio; Loans and other risk assets review
and classification using portfolio at risk; and
b.2.2.2. Findings on matters of cooperative governance that may require

urgent action by the Authority.

b.3. The external auditor shall discuss with the management of the cooperative details of
the adverse findings with the corresponding corrective measures.

SECTION 14. Signing of Audit Report

All audit reports shall be signed by the external auditor. In case of a partnership, only the
accredited signing partner/s shall sign the audit report.

A partnership firm cannot engage in the audit of the cooperatives in case of expired accreditation
even if the accreditation of the signing partner is still valid.

SECTION 15. Working Paper Retention

The external auditor shall retain audit documentations and audit working papers for a minimum
period of ten (10) years.
SECTION 16. Updating of Records

The external auditor shall update his /her records with the Authority. The external auditor is
required to submit valid documents to the CDA Extension Office in case of expiration of the validity
period of the previously submitted documents.
SECTION 17. Sanctions. The external auditor shall be sanctioned in any of the following cases:

1. Non-conduct of audit or accounting service to at least one (1) micro cooperative free of charge
as part of his/her social responsibility;

2. Non-observance of the five (5) year limitation of audit engagement to a particular cooperative
as provided in Section 11 of this Guidelines;

3. Conduct of audit with invalid accreditation with CDA and PRC BOA, and expired PRC ID of
the sole practitioner and the signing partner who signed the audit report;

4. Failure to comply with the provisions of the Guidelines on the Standard Chart of Accounts,
Philippine Financial Reporting Framework for Cooperatives, and other standards for
cooperatives;

5. Failure to update the records with the CDA pursuant to Section 16 of this guidelines.

SECTION 18. Revocation/Cancellation of Accreditation

The following shall be a ground for the revocation and/or cancellation, after due process, of the
accreditation:

1. Misrepresentation of | Financial Statements which are used by the cooperatives in
fraudulent acts;

2. Non-disclosure of adverse findings to cooperatives as provided in Section 13 (b) of this
Guidelines and

3. Allowing the use of Accreditation number by a non-accredited external auditor;

4. Non-adherence to any of the conditions for engagement of a cooperative external auditor;
and

5. Other acts analogous to the foregoing.

An external auditor who is found to have committed the above-mentioned violations which
resulted to the detriment of the cooperatives shall be barred from conducting audits to cooperatives.
SECTION 19. List of Cooperative External Auditors

The list of Cooperative External Auditors shall be posted in the CDA Website and shall be
regularly updated.

SECTION 20. Transitory Provision

All applications previously denied prior to the effectivity of this guidelines may reapply for
renewal.
