SUBJECT: GUIDELINES GOVERNING THE ALTERNATIVE MODE OF SUBMISSION OF
REQUIRED REPORTS

Pursuant to R.A. No. 11364, the Authority hereby issues the following guidelines for the proper
and accurate encoding, submission, and generation of cooperative data.

SECTION 1. PURPOSE

This Memorandum Circular serves as a policy guidelines governing the alternative mode of
submission of required reports and issuance of Certificate of Compliance (COC) in case of
difficulty in accessing/submitting the required reports through the Cooperative Assessment
Information System (CAIS).

SECTION 2. COVERAGE

This guidelines shall apply to all types and categories of cooperatives registered with the
Authority, except Credit Surety Fund (CSF) cooperatives.

SECTION 3. DEFINITION OF TERMS
As used in this guidelines, the operational definition of the following terms shall be:
AFS- shall refer to the Audited Financial Statements of the cooperative

Authorized representative — refers to the person duly authorized by the Board of
Directors of the cooperative to submit the required reports to the Cooperative
Development Authority (CDA).

CAIS - refers to Cooperative Assessment Information System
COC- shall refer to the Certificate of Compliance issued to cooperatives

Compliant Cooperative- refers to a cooperative issued with a valid Certificate of
Compliance at the time of the submission of reports.

Offline CAPR — refers to the tool used in the offline submission of required reports
using the prescribed excel forms which shall be submitted through the link address
provided.

SECTION 4. GENERAL GUIDELINES

The submission of required reports shall be done online using the CAIS. The authorized
person shall ensure a stable internet connection and that the computer or device used meets
the following minimum system requirements for accessing the CAIS platform :

a. Operating System: Windows 10 or higher or MacOS

b. Processor: Intel Core i3 or AMD Ryzen 3 Processor (or Higher)

c. Ethernet connection (LAN) OR a wireless adapter (Wi-Fi) for Internet Connectivity

d. Memory (RAM): 4 GB or above

In case there is difficulty in the online submission due to internet connectivity and/or systems
issues, the cooperative may submit the required reports using offline CAPR following the
procedures in the succeeding section. The cooperative shall show proof of such difficulty by
attaching a screenshot or picture.

Only compliant cooperatives and cooperatives who submitted complete prior year/s' reports
after December 31 are allowed to submit reports.

Only authorized representatives of cooperatives are allowed to encode the cooperative data.
The cooperative shall use its official email address in the submission of reports.

5.2. In the main page, enter the valid registration number, name of compliance officer or

SECTION 6. NOTICE OF DEFICIENCY.

In case of findings in the evaluation of submitted reports, a Notice of Deficiency shall be sent
to the cooperative’s official email address for correction/compliance. To comply with the
findings, the cooperative shall edit the previously submitted OFFLINE CAPR to correct the
deficiencies. After complying with the findings, click SUBMIT and follow the same
procedures as stated in 5.4 to 5.6 above. In 5.5 above, the cooperative shall likewise upload
the Notice of Deficiency in the Google Form. The cooperative shall inform the CDA-
Head/Extension Office of the resubmission through their respective email addresses.

The cooperative shall comply with the findings within fifteen (15) calendar days from the
issuance of the notice. Noncompliance within the said period shall mean non-submission of

SECTION 7. NON-COMPLIANT AND DISSOLVED COOPERATIVES

Cooperatives issued with Notice of Non-Submission of Required Reports, Show Cause Order
(SCO), and those under dissolution cannot proceed with the submission of reports unless
complied with the submission of previous required reports and/or the SCO is lifted.

Cooperatives issued with Order of Dissolution cannot proceed with the submission, unless the
Order of Dissolution is lifted.

SECTION 8. INFORMATION DISSEMINATION.

The Authority, through its Extension Offices, shall be responsible for the information
dissemination on the proper use of the Offline CAPR.

Section 9. EFFECTIVITY

This Memorandum Circular shall take effect upon its publication in the newspapers of general
circulation and the filing of the copy thereof with the Office of the National Administrative
Register (ONAR).
