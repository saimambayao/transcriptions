SUBJECT : SUPPLEMENTAL GUIDELINES TO MC NO. 2021-03 TITLED
“REGULATORY RELIEF IN THE CONDUCT OF GENERAL
ASSEMBLY MEETINGS FOR 2021 DUE TO THE COVID-19
PANDEMIC” .

This Circular is being issued to clarify and supplement certain relief measures for
cooperatives in order to facilitate the conduct of general assembly meetings and cause
the approval of their needed policies, guidelines, internal rules and regulations.

Section I. Conduct of Annual General Assembly Meetings (AGA) in
Accordance with the By-Laws

Cooperatives who intend and are able to conduct their AGA in the manner provided in
_ their by-laws are not covered by this Guidelines. Hence, those cooperatives need not
submit a request for relief as required under MC No. 2021-03.

Section III. Use of Facebook Live as an Extension of Videoconferencing
Platform

Use of Facebook Live as an extension for conducting videoconferencing during the
general assembly meeting is allowed, provided that the following conditions are met:

1. The cooperative shall create a separate facebook closed group for members in
good standing (MIGS)/entitled to vote and non-MIGS/not entitled to vote. In the
MIGS/METV group, a poll survey shall be conducted to determine the number of
members who will attend the videoconferencing and the facebook live.

2. If the number of MIGS/METV members who want to attend the

videoconferencing exceeds the maximum subscription, sequential general assembly
may be adopted by the cooperative.

Section IV. Election of Officers

In order to retain the one-half plus one of the set of the members of the board of
directors’ term of office as set forth in its By-laws, the cooperative shall have the
following options, subject to the approval of the general assembly in a referendum:

1. All elected officers whose original term of office ends on CY 2021 shall have an
extension of one (1) year of service; or

2. _ All elected officers whose original term of office ends in CY2021 shall step down.
The one-half plus one set of the members of the board who shall obtain the highest
number of votes in the election to be conducted for CY 2021 shall serve for two (2)
years and the remaining half of the elected directors shall serve for one (1) year.

Section V. Cancellation of the Conduct of the Annual General Assembly
Meeting

1 The following are the valid grounds for the cancellation of the AGA:

a. Security concerns;

b. Majority of members are not knowledgeable or have no access in the
internet, as shown by a survey conducted or any other similar documents;

c. Majority of members cannot participate in the general assembly meeting as
evidenced by a survey conducted;

. The cooperative has a pending case in court relative to eadership dispute;
e. Cooperatives where majority of the members are frontliners; and
f. All other circumstances analogous to the foregoing.

Section VI. Miscellaneous Provisions

1 Any violation of this Circular shall be subject to sanctions provided in CDA MC
No. 2021-03. This is intended to punish officers who violate the provisions of the same,
especially on the cancellation of general assembly meetings when the majority of
members actually desire otherwise.

2 Cooperatives are encouraged to wait for the opportunity to conduct their
annual/regular general assembly meetings since the relief extends up to September 30,
2021. This will avoid the officers being sanctioned for failure to submit a request for
cancellation by June 30, 2021.

3 All CDA regional offices must endeavor to inform and disseminate this Circular
to cooperatives of all types and categories within their jurisdiction.

Section VII. Separability Clause

- Whenever any part of this Circular is declared unconstitutional or inconsistent with

existing laws by a competent court, the remaining provisions of this Circular not
affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

Section VIII. Effectivity

This Circular shall take effect immediately after filing with the Office of the National
Administrative Register (ONAR).
