Act 9520, otherwise known as Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008 and

name prior to the registration of new cooperatives and amendment of name
for existing cooperatives with the Authority.

Section 5. Extension of Reservation Period. A thirty day extension of
reservation period may be allowed provided that the applicant pays the One
hundred Pesos (P100) fee for every extension. However, any single or
multiple extensions shall not exceed a total of ninety (90) days reservation
period from date of original reservation. Hence, no extension shall be
allowed for approved ninety (90) days reservation.

An application for extension of cooperative name reservation shall be
filed on or before the expiration of the valid reservation.

Section 6. Preferential Right over Reserved Cooperative Name- The
applicant shall have the right over the reserved name during the approved
reservation period including its extension. Otherwise, the reserved name
shall be made available to other applicants.

Section 7. Validity Period — All reserved names shall be valid in accordance
with the period specified in the CNRN.

Section 8. Cooperative Name Reservation Request Form (CNRRF) —
CNRRF can be downloaded from the CDA website and shall be made
available in all CDA Offices nationwide.

Section 9. Cooperative Names - No cooperative name shall be allowed by
the Authority if the proposed name is identical or deceptively or confusingly
similar to that of any existing cooperative, contrary to public policy, moral
and existing laws.

The proposed cooperative name shall incorporate the word
“cooperative” and specifying the type in accordance with the economic
activity.

The use of “development”, “integrated”, “Incorporated”, or other
similar connotation shall not be allowed.

Section 10. Cooperative Name Reservation System — The Authority shall
develop and implement a computerized system to prevent duplication of
cooperative names and to facilitate an effective and efficient process of
Cooperative Name Reservation.

Section 12. Effectivity — This Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days upon
approval of the Board of Administrators and submission of the copy hereof to
the Office of the Administrative Register (ONAR).
