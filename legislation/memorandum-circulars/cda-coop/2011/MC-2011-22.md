Section I, Legal Basis. Pursuant te Articles 127, 128, Chapter XVII of Republic Act No. 9520, otherwise
known as the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008, and CDA MC No. 2010-10, the following are the
“Guidelines on Post Referendum Activities in the Registration of Electric Cooperatives with the

Cooperative Development Authority (CDA).”

Section 2. Coverage. This guidelines shall cover the post referendum activities in the registration of EC's
which seek to register with CDA through referendum in accordance with RA 9520, its implementing rules
and regulations (Rule 6), and Memorandum Circular No. 2010-10.

Section 3. Definition of Terms. The following terms shall be defined as follows in order to govern and
have a uniform interpretation as mentioned in these guidelines:

Ad-Hoc Election Committee — refers to a body created by the member-consumers
petitioners for the purpose of conducting a Member-Initiated Referendum composed of not
less than 3 but not more than S designated member-consumers in good standing in every
district, whose functions shall be limited to the distribution of survey forms, conduct of
referendum, canvass and reporting the result of the referendum to the General Assembly or
to the Board of Directors (BOD), whichever is applicable.

Certificate of Registration - refers to the documents issued by the Authority under its
official seal evidencing the registration of granting juridical personality to an Electric
Cooperative and entitlement to the rights and privileges under the Code.

Code - refers to Republic Act No. 9520 otherwise known as the Philippine Cooperative Code
of 2008.

Cooperative Develapment Authority (CDA) or Authority - refers to government agency
created under Republic Act No. 6939, mandated to undertake development, registration,
regulation and supervision of all cooperatives under the Code.

Electric Cooperative or “EC”’- refers to one organized for the primary purpose of
undertaking power generation, utilizing renewable energy sources, including hybrid

electric cooperatives which shall be submitted in four (4) copies to the Authority:

Section 4. Jurisdiction. Application for registration of electric cooperative shall be filed in the CDA
Extension Office which has jurisdiction over said electric cooperative however, if said electric
cooperative opted to register with the CDA Central Office and the same has been processed and
appraved by the CDA Central Office such registration shall be farwarded to the CDA Extension Office
where the cooperative is located and the same extension office shall assume jurisdiction over said

cooperative.
Section 5. Procedure for Registration.

Upon approval of the member-consumers by way of referendum for the registration of the
electric cooperative to the CDA, the following shall be undertaken:

a. Board initiated. The requirements for the registration and supporting documents
including the result of the referendum shall be submitted by the incumbent BOD in four
(4) copies in accordance with the jurisdiction for registration of electric cooperatives as
prescribed in Section 4 herein. However, in the event that the incumbent BOD fails or
refuses to submit the requirements within thirty (30) days from the conduct of the
referendum, any member-consumer/s of the electric cooperative may submit the same

to the CDA.

b. Member-consumer initiated referendum. The incumbent BOD shall submit the
requirements for registration to the CDA. In case of the failure of the incumbent BOD to
submit such requirements within thirty (30) days from the conduct of the referendum,
the same, including the resolution, shall be made and submitted by the interim BOD
which shall facilitate the registration of the electric cooperative with the CDA.

Section 6. Creation of a Transition Committee. Upon the issuance of the certificate of registration , a
transition committee is hereby created composed of the members of the Interim Board of Directors,
which may include any member of the incumbent BOD who express/es their written manifestation to
join said committee within thirty days (30) from the registration of the electric cooperative.

Section 7. Powers of the Transition Committee.

1. To convene, conduct, facilitate and ensure that all activities related to the smooth
transition and turn-over of the operations and management of the electric cooperative
shall be undertaken, including the mandatory calling of an organizational
meeting/general assembly within three (3) months from the issuance of the certificate

of registration.

3 To conduct election in accordance with the CDA prescribed election guidelines for
electric cooperatives within ninety (90) days from the issuance of the certificate of

registration, if applicable

3. To perform any and all powers necessary for the effective implementation of their
mandate.
