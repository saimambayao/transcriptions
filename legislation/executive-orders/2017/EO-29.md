# EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 29

**RENAMING THE NATIONAL DISASTER RESILIENCE MONTH AND SHIFTING ITS FOCUS FROM DISASTER AWARENESS BUILDING TO DISASTER RESILIENCE**

> **MALACAÑAN PALACE**
> MANILA
>
> **BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES**
>
> [ EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 29, June 28, 2017 ]
>
> RENAMING THE NATIONAL DISASTER RESILIENCE MONTH AND SHIFTING ITS FOCUS FROM DISASTER AWARENESS BUILDING TO DISASTER RESILIENCE
>
> WHEREAS, Executive Order No. 137 (s.1999) mandates the observance of National Disaster Consciousness Month (NDCM) during the month of July of every year and the implementation of a Civil Defense Deputization Program at the local government level;
>
> **WHEREAS,** the purpose of the NDCM is to provide national government agencies and local government units ample time to implement their disaster awareness campaigns with longer focus and on a comprehensive basis;
>
> **WHEREAS,** the objective of the Civil Defense Deputization Program is to designate Civil Defense Deputized Coordinators (Tanggol Patrols) whose primary role is to initiate and coordinate programs, projects and activities on civil protection and disaster management;
>
> **WHEREAS,** government efforts and previous emergencies, hazards, and disasters have increased the awareness of the public on the importance of disaster risk reduction and management (DRRM);
>
> **WHEREAS,** there is aneed to promote the significance of disaster resillience which pertains to the ability of individuals, communities, organizations, and states to adapt to and recover from the effects of hazards and disasters through effective ris management;
>
> **WHEREAS,** Republic Act (RA) No. 10121 or the Philippine Disaster risk Reduction and Management Act of 2010 mandates the National Disaster Risk Reduction and Mangaement Council (NDRRMC) to develop a National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Framework (NDRRMF) which shall provide for a comprehensive, all-hazards, multi-sectoral, inter-agency and community-based appropach to DRRM;
>
> **WHEREAS,** the approved NDRRMF envisions "safer, adaptive and disaster resilient Filipino communities towards sustainable development" and conveys a paradigm shift from a reactive to proactive approach to increase people's resilience and decrease their vulnerabilities;
>
> **WHEREAS,** RA No. 10121 provides for the establishment of Local DRRM Offices and Barangay DRRM Committees which shall be responsible for setting the direction, development, implementation and coordination of disaster risk management programs within their territorial jurisdiction;
>
> **NOW, THEREFORE, I RODRIGO ROA DUTERTE**, President of the Philippines by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution and existing laws, do hereby order.
>
> **Section 1.** *Title.* The NDCM shall be renamed as National Disaster Resilience Month NDRM to be observed for the whole month of July of every year.
>
> **Section 2.** *Disaster Resilience.* The NDRM shall be collectively observed by all Filipinos throughout the country through the conduct of activities relative to building disaster resilience covering the four thematic areas of disasters, namely, disaster prevention and mitigation, disaster preparedness, disaster response, and disaster rehabilitation and recovery.
>
> **Section 3.** *Lead Agencies.* The NDRRMC shall take the lead in the preparation, coordination, organization and implementation of the programs and activities under this Order. The Regional DRRM Councils, Local DRRM Councils and Barangay DRRM Committees shall ensure the implementation of all programs and activities. >
> **Section 4.** *Separability.* If anysection or part of this Order is held unconstitutional or invalid, the other sections or provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.
>
> **Section 5.** *Repeal*ing Clause. Executive Order no. 137 (s. 1999) and all orders, rules and regulations, issuances or any part thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Executive Order are hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.
>
> **Section 6.** *Effectivity.* This Order shall take effect immediately upon publication in a newspaper of general circulation.
>
