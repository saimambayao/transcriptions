> **MALACAÑAN PALACE**
> MANILA
>
> **BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINES**
>
> [ Executive Order No. 880, May 18, 2010 ]
>
> PLACING THE MALACAÑANG MUSEUM IN THE OFFICE OF THE PRESIDENT UNDER THE SUPERVISION OF THE NATIONAL MUSEUM, CREATING THE PRESIDENTIAL SPECIAL COLLECTION AND PROVIDING FOR ITS ADMINISTRATION, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES
>
> WHEREAS, there is a need to institutionalize and enhance further, and carry out to the highest possible standard, the administration, management and promotion of the built heritage and the artistic, historical and cultural collection of the Office of the President, which is currently the mandate and responsibility of the Malacañang Museum, an organic office of the Office of the President;
>
> **WHEREAS,** the National Museum, by virtue of Republic Act No. 8492, is tasked with the objective of taking the lead, as a cultural center, in the study and preservation of the nations rich artistic and cultural heritage, in the reconstruction and rebuilding of our past, and the development of the national cultural wealth (**Section 6.**3), and has the duties and functions to collect, preserve, restore and exhibit to the public objects of arts (**Section 7.**10);
>
> **WHEREAS,** the President of the Philippines has the continuing authority to reorganize the National Government as provided by EO No. 292, otherwise known as the Administrative Code of 1987.
>
> **NOW, THEREFORE, I, GLORIA MACAPAGAL-ARROYO,** President of the Republic of the Philippines, by virtue of the power vested in me by law, do hereby order:
>
> **Section 1.** *The Malacañang Museum*
>
> a.) The Malacañang Museum shall be placed under the supervision of the National Museum for museological and related policy purposes, but shall remain under the Office of the President for administrative and budgetary purposes;
>
> b.) The Malacañang Museum shall administer for, museum and other purposes approved by the President of the Philippines, the following structures and their associated grounds: Kalayaan Hall, the old cafeteria building, the Boat House, the Goldenberg Mansion and Teus Mansion, the Laperal Mansion (formerly Arlegui Guest House), the Valdes Mansion, Bahay Uganayan and the Watson Building, the secondfloor of which shall be made available to the National Museum for purposes of perpetuating the memory and work of Dr. Henry Otley Beyer, as well as any heritage or other structure or space designated by the President;
>
> c.) The Malacañang Museum shall continue to carry out its mandate as stated in the Reorganization Plan of the Office of the President, as well as any other duties and responsibilities designated by the President;
>
> d.) The Malacañang Museum, in the pursuit of its mandate shall, subject to available resources, establish branch museum at the mansion House in Baguio City, Malacañang sa Sugbu in Cebu City and curate the Presidential Yacht BRP Pag-Asa in coordination with the Philippine Navy.
>
> **Section 2.** *The Presidential Special Collection*
>
> a) All properties, defined here as movable objects and items, of the Office of the President, possessing significant artistic, historical and cultural value, shall collectively be known as the Presidential Special Collection, hereafter referred to as the Collection;
>
> b) The content and scope of the collection shall be studied, and recommended by the Malacañang Museum and National Museum and submitted by the Board of Trustees of the National Museum for the approval of the President;
>
> c) After the approval by the President, the properties recommended for inclusion in the Collection shall cease to be properties of the Office of the President and, in line with pertinent regulations and procedures, become properties of the National Museum, upon which the National Museum shall have the primary responsibility of conserving, managing and administering the Collection in conformity with this Executive Order;
>
> d) The Office of the President, through the Malacañang Museum, shall have priority in accessing any item of the collection through temporary, extended or permanent loan for appropriate display and use in any structure officially used by the President, or for official purposes, subject to the appropriate guidelines of the NationalMuseum, and likewise, any items of the Collection, not in use by the Office of the President, at any time, shall be appropriately displayed, stored of otherwise managed by the National Museum;
>
> e) The National Museum may add any other of its properties as may appropriately belong in the Collection for historical, thematic, or other reasons, for purposes of enhancement and rationalization, and likewise the Office of the President, through the Malacañang Museum, may add further properties to the Collection as these are required or identified, upon the recommendation of the Board of Trustees of the National Museum.1aшphi1
>
> **Section 3.** This Executive Order shall take effect immediately.
>
> DONE in the City of Manila, this 18th day of May, in the year of our Lord, Two Thousand and Ten.
>

> President of the Philippines
