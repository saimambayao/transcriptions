> An ex post facto law has been defined as one-(a) which makes an action done before the passing of the law and which was innocent when done criminal, and punishes such action; or (b) which aggravates a crime or makes it greater than it was when committed; or (c ) which changes the punishment and inflicts a greater punishment than the law annexed to the crime when it was committed; or (d ) which alters the legal rules of evidence and receives less or different testimony than the law required at the time of the commission of the offense in order to convict the defendant.
