> WHEREAS, pursuant to Republic Act No. 11054, Article X, Section
> 12, the Court is empowered to administer the SSBE for admission of
> applicants to the Philippine Bar as special members to practice law in Shari’ah
> courts;
>
> _ WHEREAS, pursuant to the Strategic Plan for Judicial Innovations
> (SPJT) 2022-2027, reforms on the conduct of the SSBE must be pursued to
> bolster Shari'ah practice and to effectively administer and enforce Shari’ah
> in the country; :
>
> WHEREAS, the reforms for the conduct of the SSBE strengthen the
> foundations of the justice system and aid this Court in its aim of having an
>
> inclusive legal framework that acknowledges all identities and amplifies the
>
> voices of the minority;
>
> WHEREAS, the Court implemented several reforms in the conduct of
> the 2024 SSBE with roaring success, which include the following: (a) the
> conduct of two batches of Shari'ah Training Seminars (STS), qualifying more
> candidates to take the SSBE; and (b) the digitalization and regionalization of
> the SSBE, similar to the regular Bar examinations. Furthermore, the Court
> retained the PHP 2,000.00 SSBE application fee for indigent applicants and
> allowed an installment scheme for applicants who demonstrated meritorious
> reasons warranting leniency in the payment of the increased SSBE fees 2
>
> WHEREAS, the first regionalized and digitalized SSBE, chaired by
> Associate Justice Maria Filomena D. Singh, was successfully held on April
> 28 and May 2, 2024 at the University of the Philippines-Diliman and Ateneo
> de Davao University, with 853 examinees completing it. Of these, 77 took the
> SSBE manually in Arabic using pen and paper. A total of 183 examinees or
> 21.45% passed the 2024 SSBE and remarkably, 62.3% of whom are women;?
>
> NOW, THEREFORE, the Court resolves, upon the recommendations
> of Senior Associate Justice Marvic M.V.F. Leonen and Associate Justice
> Japar B. Dimaampao of the Committee on Access to Justice/ Underserved
> Areas, and Associate Justices Maria Filomena D. Singh and Antonio T. Kho,
> Jr., Chairpersons of the 18% and 19% SSBEs, respectively, to APPROVE and
> ADOPT the Qualifications for the 2025 and Succeeding SSBEs.
