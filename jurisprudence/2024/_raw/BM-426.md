> In a January 23, 2025 Letter,'' the IBP-BOG noted that individuals
> holding positions as directors or higher in the national government, as well
> as those in equivalent roles within LGUs and GOCCs, occupy highly
> influential positions. Given that a Director I position corresponds to Salary
> Grade 25, the IBP-BOG reached a consensus that individuals occupying
> positions with Salary Grade 25 and above should no longer be eligible to
> serve as chapter officers. In its February 3, 2025 Letter,'? the IBP-BOG
> further clarified its decision to specify the prohibition on government
> lawyers with certain salary grades from serving as chapter officers by
> distinguishing those employed in the national government and GOCCs from
> those working in LGUs. Specifically, under the revised policy, the
> prohibition applies to those holding positions in the national government or
> GOCCs with Salary Grade 26 and above, while those in LGUs are covered
> by the restriction if they hold positions with Salary Grade 25 and above.
>
> Regarding the 50% limitation on government lawyers serving as
> chapter officers, the IBP-BOG proposed that government lawyers should not
> hold more than half of the total officer positions in a chapter. However, the
> IBP-BOG recognized that some smaller chapters, such as the IBP Apayao
> Chapter (Chapter), have fewer active members in good standing—
> specifically, no more than 100. Due to this unique circumstance, the IBP-
> BOG argued that the 50% limitation should not apply to these smaller
> chapters. .
>
> To bolster this position, the IBP-BOG submitted a January 22, 2025
> Letter’? from Atty. Minerva R. Magtanao-Taguinod, Governor of the IBP
> Northern Luzon. In her Letter, Atty. Magtanao-Taguinod expressed concern
> that the implementation of the revised IBP By-Laws, particularly Section 4,
> would render it impossible for the Chapter to elect a complete set of
> qualified officers. As an example, out of the Chapter’s 49 members, only six
> are not employed in the government and are thus eligible to run for office.
> This leaves the Chapter unable to fulfill the necessary number of officers for
> its leadership.
>
> In response to this dilemma, the Chapter passed Resolution No. 9-
> 2024'* seeking an exemption from the prohibition. To substantiate its
> request, the Chapter submitted supporting documents, including appointment
> papers and certifications verifying the employment of its members in
> government service. Emphasizing the potential for new lawyers to join the
> Chapter—which could alleviate the shortage of eligible candidates in future
> elections—the Chapter specifically requested that the exemption apply only
> to the upcoming elections of chapter officers on February 22, 2025.

> paragraph of Section 3, which grants the IBP the prerogative to undertake all

>
> We partially grant the requests.
>
> After careful consideration, this Court finds merit in granting the
> proposed modifications to the election guidelines for the upcoming IBP
> Chapter Elections on February 22, 2025, subject to certain qualifications.
>
> With respect to online voting, this Court recognizes the IBP-BOG’s
> effort to establish a reasonable threshold for its implementation. The
> requirement that at least 10% of a chapter’s total members in good standing
> must formally request online voting before it is allowed ensures that this
> mechanism is adopted based on actual demand. This approach aligns with
> this Court’s Strategic Plan for Judicial Innovations 2022-2027 (SPJ),
> which seeks to modernize legal processes and improve accessibility while
> maintaining the integrity of elections.
>
> With regard to the 50% limitation on government lawyers serving as
> chapter officers, this Court recognizes the unique situation faced by the
> Chapter and similarly situated small chapters. The JBP-BOG has
> demonstrated that the strict application of this rule would make it difficult
> for certain chapters to elect a complete set of officers. However, this Court
> emphasizes that the situation in one chapter should not automatically result
> in a blanket exemption. Membership compositions vary, and any exemption
> should be justified based on necessity and supporting data. This Court, thus,
> grants the IBP-BOG’s request to lift the 50% limitation for chapters with
> 100 or fewer members while underscoring that future adjustments to this
> threshold must be based on a comprehensive assessment of IBP chapters
> nationwide.

> This Court notes that the IBP-BOG has revised its position on the
> prohibition of government lawyers from holding certain IBP chapter officer
> positions. Specifically, the IBP-BOG has agreed that no government lawyer
> shall be: allowed to occupy the position of chapter president by way of
> election, appointment, or succession. This adjustment renders any additional
> restrictions, such as prohibiting government lawyers from being elected as
> vice president, unnecessary. This Court finds this revision reasonable, as it
> effectively preserves the IBP’s nonpolitical nature while still allowing
> qualified government lawyers to serve in other leadership capacities within
> their respective chapters.
>
> The IBP-BOG has also refined its position regarding the prohibition
> based on salary grade by differentiating between those employed in the
> national government or GOCCs versus those employed in LGUs. Under the
> revised policy, government lawyers holding positions in the national
> government or GOCCs with Salary Grade 26 and above, as well as those
> holding positions in LGUs with Salary Grade 25 and above, shall be
> prohibited from serving as chapter officers. This distinction provides a more
> nuanced approach to the concern that higher-ranking government lawyers
> may wield considerable influence over IBP affairs.
>
> Nevertheless, it must be emphasized that the grant of these requests
> applies only to the upcoming IBP Chapter Elections on February 22, 2025
> and shall not, in any way, be construed as a revision or amendment of the
> IBP’s Revised By-Laws, which this Court approved in its March 8, 2023
> Resolution. This Court’s approval is an exceptional measure in view of the
> limited time before the scheduled elections and the urgent necessity to
> ensure that all IBP chapters can validly elect their officers. Any permanent
> changes to the IBP’s electoral policies must go through the proper
> amendment process in accordance with the IBP By-Laws and be submitted
> to this Court for final approval. Further, such amendments must be
> grounded on well-supported arguments, backed by empirical data, and
> reflective of the needs of IBP members nationwide to ensure that any policy
> adjustments are fair, reasonable, and consistent with the IBP’s mandate.
