### 10. "Pain Alone" Is Not Enough — GR 264870

**The law:** GR 264870 (Leonen, April 2025): "The effect on the victim alone, which is the second element, is not enough to sustain a conviction."

**What prosecution must prove (and can't):**
- MEANS: Acts of public ridicule, humiliation, verbal abuse, denial of support — done with intent to tilt power against the woman. **Andy did nothing directed at Boony.** He was in his own apartment. She came uninvited. He blocked the door.
- EFFECT: Boony experienced anguish. **Probably yes — but pain alone is insufficient.**

**The pain was self-inflicted:** Boony went to Andy's office, brought officemates, went to the apartment, and created the confrontation. The law does not criminalize causing emotional pain — it criminalizes causing pain THROUGH specific acts of psychological violence. Boony's pain is real but generated by her own actions.

### 21. Right Not to Testify and Charlie State Witness Risk

**Constitutional basis:**
- **1987 Constitution, Art. III, Sec. 17:** "No person shall be compelled to be a witness against himself."
- **Rule 115, Sec. 1(e):** The accused has the right "to be exempt from being compelled to be a witness against himself."

**Andy's right:** Absolute. Cannot be compelled to testify. Prosecution cannot comment on silence or draw adverse inferences. This is the foundation of the "Andy should NOT testify" strategy.

**Charlie's right:** Same as Andy's. As co-accused, Charlie is also protected by the right against self-incrimination. He cannot be compelled to testify against himself or against Andy.

**THE CHARLIE STATE WITNESS RISK:**

Under **Rule 119, Section 17**, the prosecution can move to discharge Charlie as accused and convert him to a state witness. Requirements:
1. Absolute necessity for testimony
2. No other direct evidence available
3. Testimony can be substantially corroborated
4. He is not the most guilty
5. No conviction for moral turpitude

**If discharged:** Charlie LOSES his right to remain silent and CAN be compelled to testify against Andy. He becomes a prosecution witness testifying from inside the relationship itself.

**Defense strategy against discharge:**
- **Object on grounds (a) and (b):** Prosecution has other evidence — Boony's affidavit, CCTV, former partners, brothers. Charlie's testimony is not "absolutely necessary."
- Even if Charlie testifies, the legal shields survive: Information scope, GR 264870 means requirement, Boony created the public dimension, talaq dissolved the marriage.

**Charlie must retain independent counsel.** His interests may diverge from Andy's. If prosecution offers a deal, Andy's counsel cannot advise Charlie — conflict of interest.

**If Charlie flips:** He provides direct testimony about the pre-talaq relationship from inside the relationship itself. The defense's "never deny or confirm" strategy becomes harder. Pivot to: the relationship's existence does not establish statutory elements on the charged date.

### 22. Boony's Menstruation Claim

**The attack:** Boony claims she was menstruating on January 23, 2025. PD 1083 Art. 46(1) requires talaq "during her non-menstrual period (tuhr)." If she was menstruating, the talaq arguably was not during *tuhr*.

**Five defense layers:**

| # | Layer | Argument |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | **Evidentiary** | How does Boony prove menstruation 14+ months ago? No medical records. Her word — from a witness with a provably false sworn statement (S15). |
| 2 | **Islamic law** | All four Sunni schools (Hanafi, Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali) hold talaq during menstruation is *haram* (sinful) but **VALID** (effective). This is *talaq al-bid'ah*. Hadith of Abdullah ibn Umar: Prophet commanded him to take wife back, but the divorce COUNTED. PD 1083 Art. 5 directs interpretation consistent with Islamic schools. |
| 3 | **PD 1083 textual** | Art. 46(1) says talaq "**may be** affected... during tuhr" — "may be" is permissive, not mandatory. No provision declares talaq during menstruation VOID. Compare with Art. 161 "**shall**" (mandatory) — yet non-filing doesn't void the talaq. |
| 4 | **Estoppel** | Boony accepted the talaq, left the apartment, sent Jan 30 SMS, negotiated settlement for 7 months. She cannot claim invalidity for the first time in a criminal proceeding after 14 months of post-talaq conduct. |
| 5 | **Jurisdictional** | Talaq validity is for the Shari'ah Circuit Court (PD 1083 Art. 143), not the RTC. Neither Boony nor prosecution has challenged the talaq in the proper forum. |

**Impact on probability:** If menstruation claim is raised and credited, acquittal probability drops by ~5 percentage points.

### 23. Former Male Partners and Brothers as Witnesses

**Former male partners:**
- **Character evidence** under Rule 130, Sec. 54 — generally inadmissible to prove conduct on a specific occasion.
- Defense should object. Even if admitted as "similar acts" evidence (Rule 130, Sec. 34): establishes orientation, not Sec. 5(i) elements on February 12.
- Orientation is not a crime, not an element of any RA 9262 offense, not relevant to the charged date.

**Boony's brothers — five types of testimony:**
