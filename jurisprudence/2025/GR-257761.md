> Unlike in inferior courts where costs of suit are assessed by the judge,
> in superior courts, the payment of costs of suit requires that an assessment be
> made by the clerk of court, and the appeal, if any, from that assessment to the
> court. The assessment or so-called "taxation of costs" is a condition precedent

> items, or to the inclusion of costs in an execution. Thus, the court cannot pass
