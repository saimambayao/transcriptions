Accordingly, We find that the CA erred in reckoning the reglementary period for filing a motion for reconsideration from the petitioner's receipt of the assailed CA Decision. The said period should have been counted from the OSG' s receipt of the same decision.

> Anent the crux of the appeal, We find in favor of the petitioner.
>
> The 0MB was correct. •T he respondent may still be found guilty of simple misconduct. In Civil Sei'vice Commission v. We defined Ledesma,25 the concept of "misconduct" and its different pennutations as such:
>
> Misconduct is "a transgression ofs ome established and definite rule of action, more particularly, unlawfitl behavior or gross negligence by a public officer." The misconduct is grave if it involves any of the additional elements of corruption, willful intent to violate the law or to disregard established rules, which must be established by substantial evidence. Otherwise, the misconduct is only simple. A person charged with grave .misconduct may be held liable for simple misconduct ift he misconduct does not involve any of the additional elements to qualify the misconduct as grave. 26 (Emphasis supplied, citations omitted)
>
> Verily, the plain unlawful behavior of a public officer-even if not motivated by bad faith, malice or a corrupt consideration-already gives rise to the administrative offense of simple misconduct.
>
