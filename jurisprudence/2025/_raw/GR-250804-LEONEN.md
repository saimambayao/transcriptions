> Article II, Section 5 of the Constitution provides that the maintenance
> of peace and order; the protection of life, liberty, and property; and the
> promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the
> people of the blessings of democracy.1 The Philippine National Police plays
> an important role in giving life to this mandate. Thus, it is necessary to ensure
> that there is an effective disciplinary mechanism in place to preserve the
> integrity of the police force.
> The pivotal question in this case is the appellate process of disciplinary
> actions of the Philippine National Police.
> At the center of the issue is petitioner Police Major Alfred Arturo, who
> was assigned parade and flag-raising duties for the Philippine National

> January 21, 2016 rehearsal. Consequently, an administrative action was filed
> against him for two counts of less grave neglect of duty for failing to attend
> the rehearsal and performing his parade duties.

> mother's medical reports from the Philippine National Police General

> day but did not march because he was merely a reserve or filler.2
