> In Macutay, We held that notwithstanding the caption of the complaint,
> it did not include a prayer for recovery of ownership or annulment of title.
> Further, We found that the allegations therein indicate that it was essentially
> an action for recovery of possession, or an accion publiciana.
>
> Similarly, the present Complaint does not include a prayer for recovery
> of ownership or annulment of title. Although respondent alleged in her MR
> before the RTC and Petition before the CA that she sought to recover
> ownership and possession as an attribute thereof, nowhere in her Complaint —
> does she seek recovery of ownership. What determines the nature of the
> action, as well as the court which has jurisdiction over the case, are the
> allegations in the Complaint,!* not the allegations in a subsequent pleading or
> motion. Further, contrary to the CA’s ruling, there was no boundary dispute
> here and not all cases of encroachment bring the case within the ambit of
> accion reivindicatoria, as in this case where recovery of ownership is not
> sought. Respondent did not even allege that petitioners disputed her title.
> Hence, there is no reason for the ownership of the property to be conclusively
> determined through an accion reivindicatoria.
