> DISSENTING OPINION
>
> LEONEN, J.:
>
> At issue here is the deceased Pacita Javier (Javier)'s inclusion as an incorporator for respondent AZ 17/31 Realty, Inc.'s incorporation. The majority did not deem this as fraud, saying that even without Javier's name, there were six other incorporators and the minimum paid-up capitalization followed.1 It requirements were declined to consider Securities and Exchange Commission Resolution No. 359, series of 2010 (SEC Resolution No. 359), saying that this did "not ripen to a doctrine of practical construction, sans judicial acquiescence."2
>
> For the majority, then, Javier's inclusion may be a ground for criminal liability, but it cannot be a ground to dissolve the incorporation.3 Instead, the corporation supposedly should be given a reasonable time of at least six months to correct the erroneous inclusion.4
>
> I dissent. Deliberately including a deceased incorporator who died over three years before the incorporation is a misrepresentation and a violation of the Corporation Code. It is a fraudulent act that should be dealt with by the Securities and Exchange Commission.
